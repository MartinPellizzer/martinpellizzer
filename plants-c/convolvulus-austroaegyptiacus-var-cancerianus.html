Convolvulus austroaegyptiacus var. cancerianus

Convolvulus austroaegyptiacus var. cancerianus, commonly known as the Egyptian bindweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its climbing or trailing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, facilitating its spread in disturbed soils. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to harsh environments.

Its botanical features include hairy stems, alternate leaves, and vibrant yellow or orange flowers that bloom throughout the growing season.
