Corylus avellana var. acuta

Corylus avellana var. acuta, commonly known as the sharp-horned hazel, is a variant of the common hazel.

It is characterized by its elongated, sharp-tipped nuts and distinctive leaf morphology. Native to parts of Europe and western Asia, this plant thrives in temperate climates. The leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and the plant produces catkins in early spring.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible nuts.
