Canna × hybrida

Canna × hybrida, commonly known as the giant cannas, are herbaceous perennials native to the Americas.

They are characterized by their large, colorful, strap-like leaves and showy, spadix-like flowers that come in a variety of hues. These plants typically grow in moist, sunny environments and are often found in wetlands or near water features. They have a rhizomatous root system that allows them to spread and form dense colonies.

Canna × hybrida is valued in ornamental horticulture for its striking appearance and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
