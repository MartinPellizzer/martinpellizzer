Coilonox glandulosum

Coilonox glandulosum, commonly known as the sticky glandular plant, is a species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which produce a sticky substance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical or subtropical regions. Its flowers are small and clustered, often with a distinctive coloration that aids in pollination.

Coilonox glandulosum is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and potential ecological roles.
