Cassia pycnophylla

Cassia pycnophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, flat pods containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cassia pycnophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Leguminosae family.
