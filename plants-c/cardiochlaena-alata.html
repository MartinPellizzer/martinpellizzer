Cardiochlaena alata

Cardiochlaena alata, commonly known as the winged sun-cup, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features distinctive, winged bracts that give it its common name and aid in wind dispersal of its seeds. It typically grows in grasslands and open habitats, often forming dense colonies.

Its yellow-orange flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
