Carex gracilis lusus dioica

Carex gracilis lusus dioica, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a dioecious species, meaning that male and female flowers are found on separate plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its slender, grass-like leaves are usually dark green and arranged in a dense, grass-like cluster.

The small, inconspicuous flowers appear in spikes and are often wind-pollinated, contributing to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
