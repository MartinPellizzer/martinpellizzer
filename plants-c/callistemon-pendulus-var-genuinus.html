Callistemon pendulus var. genuinus

Callistemon pendulus var. genuinus, commonly known as the weeping bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bottlebrush-like inflorescences that are typically bright red or orange in color. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are evergreen and arranged in whorls around the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers attract nectar-feeding birds and insects, contributing to its ecological value.
