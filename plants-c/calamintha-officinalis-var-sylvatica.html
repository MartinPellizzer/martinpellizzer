Calamintha officinalis var. sylvatica

Calamintha officinalis var. sylvatica, commonly known as sweetwood or mountain thyme, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and square stems. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and traditional medicine for their calming and digestive properties.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
