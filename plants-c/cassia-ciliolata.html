Cassia ciliolata

Cassia ciliolata, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces pods containing flat, seed-filled seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. Cassia ciliolata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its leaves are compound, with small, hairy leaflets that give it a distinctive appearance.
