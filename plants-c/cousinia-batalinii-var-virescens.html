Cousinia batalinii var. virescens

Cousinia batalinii var. virescens, commonly known as the green-flowered cousinia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow to yellow flowers. It is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in steppes and desert margins.

Cousinia batalinii var. virescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid landscapes.
