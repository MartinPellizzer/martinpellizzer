Cybianthus grandifolius

Cybianthus grandifolius, commonly known as the grandifolius cybianthus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its foliage is evergreen, providing visual interest throughout the year.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
