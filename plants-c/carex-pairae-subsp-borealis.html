Carex pairae subsp. borealis

Carex pairae subsp.

borealis, commonly known as the northern sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of North America. It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. Its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous, producing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is adapted to cold climates and plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.
