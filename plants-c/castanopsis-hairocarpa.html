Castanopsis hairocarpa

Castanopsis hairocarpa, commonly known as the hairocarpa chestnut, is a species of evergreen tree belonging to the Fagaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, featuring a broad, rounded crown and thick, leathery leaves. Its fruit is a hard, spiny nut enclosed in a woody capsule, which typically matures over several months.

The plant is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes used in reforestation efforts due to its ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
