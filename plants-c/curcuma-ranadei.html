Curcuma ranadei

Curcuma ranadei, commonly known as the orange ginger, is a rhizomatous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its vibrant orange-colored rhizomes, which are rich in curcuminoids, similar to those found in turmeric. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a reddish hue on the underside.

Curcuma ranadei is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its potential medicinal properties.
