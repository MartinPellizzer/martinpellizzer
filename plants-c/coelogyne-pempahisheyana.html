Coelogyne pempahisheyana

Coelogyne pempahisheyana, commonly known as the Pempahisheyan orchid, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, slender stem and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming during the summer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
