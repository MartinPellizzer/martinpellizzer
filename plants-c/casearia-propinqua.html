Casearia propinqua

Casearia propinqua, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits that mature in the fall. These fruits are edible for some bird species but are generally not consumed by humans.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and vibrant fruit display.
