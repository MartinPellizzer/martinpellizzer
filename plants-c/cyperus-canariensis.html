Cyperus canariensis

Cyperus canariensis, commonly known as spiny flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are usually inconspicuous. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread aggressively, making it a common weed in aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.

The plant's botanical features make it adaptable to a variety of moist soil conditions, contributing to its widespread distribution.
