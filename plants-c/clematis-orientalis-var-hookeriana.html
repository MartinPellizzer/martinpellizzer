Clematis orientalis var. hookeriana

Clematis orientalis var. hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's Clematis, is a climbing vine native to the Himalayas.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pale blue, often with a pinkish tinge. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its historical significance in botanical horticulture.
