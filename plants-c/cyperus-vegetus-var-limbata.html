Cyperus vegetus var. limbata

Cyperus vegetus var. limbata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically brown or pale in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus vegetus var. limbata is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
