Clausena san-ki

Clausena san-ki, commonly known as the wild mangosteen, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits with a thick, purple rind and a sweet, aromatic pulp. Its botanical features make it distinct from the common mangosteen (Garcinia mangostana).

The plant is often found in moist, tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
