Campanula ermenekensis

Campanula ermenekensis, commonly known as the Ermeneke bellflower, is a rare and endemic species native to Turkey.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of blue to violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine pubescence, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Campanula ermenekensis is adapted to grow in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
