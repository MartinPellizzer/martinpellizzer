Calamagrostis porteri subsp. perplexa

Calamagrostis porteri subsp.

perplexa, commonly known as the "Mexican bluegrass," is a perennial grass native to Mexico. It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and culms that are often tinged with purple.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-like flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
