Chrysanthemum santoanum

Chrysanthemum santoanum, commonly known as the Santoanum chrysanthemum, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to white and sometimes pink. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
