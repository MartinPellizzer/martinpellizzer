Convolvulus bidrensis

Convolvulus bidrensis, commonly known as the Bidrensian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive funnel-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant blue or purple blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often twining.

Convolvulus bidrensis thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
