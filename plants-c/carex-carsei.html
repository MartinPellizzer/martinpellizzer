Carex carsei

Carex carsei, commonly known as the Sierra Carex, is a perennial sedge native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-yellow flowers. It is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Carex carsei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
