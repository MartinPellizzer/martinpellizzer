Carphochaete grahamii

Carphochaete grahamii, commonly known as Graham's goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its yellow flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is named in honor of the botanist John Graham, who contributed significantly to the study of North American flora.
