Carelia conyzoides

Carelia conyzoides, commonly known as the African blueweed, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Carelia conyzoides is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
