Calamus acanthochlamys

Calamus acanthochlamys, commonly known as the spiny-chlamys arrowroot, is a member of the Costaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its thick, rhizomatous growth and large, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, which are followed by triangular, spiny fruits. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their starchy roots, which are used in traditional culinary and medicinal practices.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in its native habitats.
