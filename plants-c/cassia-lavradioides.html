Cassia lavradioides

Cassia lavradioides, commonly known as the yellow cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Cassia lavradioides thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
