Colutea frutescens

Colutea frutescens, commonly known as wild buckwheat, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and rocky soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant has a short, branched stem.

Colutea frutescens is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
