Calathea jagoriana

Calathea jagoriana, commonly known as the jagoriana calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with intricate patterns of green, silver, and pink. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, enhancing its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and is often used as a decorative plant.
