Coreopsis emarcida

Coreopsis emarcida, commonly known as the narrowleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces bright yellow blooms from late spring to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, drought-tolerant plants.

Coreopsis emarcida is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
