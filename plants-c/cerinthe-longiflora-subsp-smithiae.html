Cerinthe longiflora subsp. smithiae

Cerinthe longiflora subsp.

smithiae, commonly known as the Smith's long-flowered cerinthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins.

The plant produces clusters of tubular, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soils.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and historical use in herbal medicine.
