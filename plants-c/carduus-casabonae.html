Carduus casabonae

Carduus casabonae, commonly known as Casablanca thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed areas. It has been cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its distinctive appearance.

The plant's flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
