Cyanus cheiranthifolius subsp. willdenowii

Cyanus cheiranthifolius subsp.

willdenowii, commonly known as the willdenow's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces striking blue to violet flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
