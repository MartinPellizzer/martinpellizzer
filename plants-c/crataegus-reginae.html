Crataegus reginae

Crataegus reginae, commonly known as the Queen's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches, oval leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries known as haws, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

Crataegus reginae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
