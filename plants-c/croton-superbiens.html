Croton superbiens

Croton superbiens, commonly known as the golden croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that display shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Croton superbiens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.
