Corymbia setosa

Corymbia setosa, commonly known as the slender gum tree, is a species of eucalyptus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark that peels in thin, papery layers. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy green upper surface and a pale underside.

The flowers are small and clustered in umbels, producing a distinctive reddish or pinkish hue.
