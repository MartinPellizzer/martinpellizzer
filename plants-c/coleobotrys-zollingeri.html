Coleobotrys zollingeri

Coleobotrys zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or pale green spines. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for dry environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptations, it is a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
