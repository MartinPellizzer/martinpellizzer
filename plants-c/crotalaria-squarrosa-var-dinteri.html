Crotalaria squarrosa var. dinteri

Crotalaria squarrosa var. dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, often with a pubescent surface.

The species is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
