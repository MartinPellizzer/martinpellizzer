Cirsium tweedyi

Cirsium tweedyi, commonly known as Tweedy's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands. It produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Cirsium tweedyi is often found in regions with Mediterranean climates, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
