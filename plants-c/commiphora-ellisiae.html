Commiphora ellisiae

Commiphora ellisiae, commonly known as the Elysian myrrh, is a rare and critically endangered species native to the rainforests of Ecuador.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that belongs to the Burseraceae family, known for its aromatic resinous properties. The plant produces a dark, aromatic gum resin, similar to myrrh, which has been historically used in traditional medicine. Its limited distribution and habitat specificity make it highly vulnerable to environmental changes and deforestation.
