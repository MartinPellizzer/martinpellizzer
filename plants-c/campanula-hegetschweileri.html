Campanula hegetschweileri

Campanula hegetschweileri, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features slender, hairy stems that grow up to 30 centimeters tall, often forming dense clusters. The plant produces small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, typically arranged in a basal rosette.

Campanula hegetschweileri thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.
