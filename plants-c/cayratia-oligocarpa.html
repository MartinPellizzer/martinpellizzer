Cayratia oligocarpa

Cayratia oligocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese maypop, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round fruits that are often edible and have a mild, sweet flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Cayratia oligocarpa is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its potential use in traditional medicine.
