Calla oblongifolia

Calla oblongifolia, commonly known as the oblong-leaf calla, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which is characterized by its unique inflorescence known as a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, with the spathe often displaying a contrasting color.

Calla oblongifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
