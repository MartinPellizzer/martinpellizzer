Crotalaria obliqua

Crotalaria obliqua, commonly known as the cowpea shrub or devil's bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes, followed by bean-like pods containing elliptical seeds. It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.

Crotalaria obliqua is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain areas.
