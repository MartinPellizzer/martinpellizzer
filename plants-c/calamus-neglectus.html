Calamus neglectus

Calamus neglectus, commonly known as the slender sweet flag, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet, marshy environments, often near water bodies. The plant features a rhizomatous root system and long, narrow leaves that are usually green with a slight sheen. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Aroid family.

Calamus neglectus is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.
