Coleus coeruleus

Coleus coeruleus, commonly known as bluecoleus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet foliage, which is often variegated with white or silver markings. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or purple. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor settings.
