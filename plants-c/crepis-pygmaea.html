Crepis pygmaea

Crepis pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf hawk's-beard, is a small annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with slender stems and small, yellow flower heads. The plant produces numerous tiny, bristly seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves.
