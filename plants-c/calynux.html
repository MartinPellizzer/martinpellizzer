Calynux

Calynux, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and covered in fine pubescence. Calynux prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for wetland gardens.

Its botanical name, Calynux, reflects its ornamental value and distinctive floral structure.
