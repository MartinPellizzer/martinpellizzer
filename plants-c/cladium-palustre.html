Cladium palustre

Cladium palustre, commonly known as sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like blades that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense stands in shallow water or moist soil. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize the soil and prevent erosion in marshy areas. Sawgrass is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

It is also notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor, acidic conditions.
