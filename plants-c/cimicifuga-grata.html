Cimicifuga grata

Cimicifuga grata, commonly known as American bugbane, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features tall, slender stems that bear clusters of white, lace-like flowers in late summer. The plant has large, pinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its roots contain compounds that have been historically used for their potential therapeutic properties.

Cimicifuga grata thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland habitats.
