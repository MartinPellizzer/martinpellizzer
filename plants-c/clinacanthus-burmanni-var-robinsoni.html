Clinacanthus burmanni var. robinsoni

Clinacanthus burmanni var. robinsoni, commonly known as the red-flowered clinacanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking red or pink tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with opposite, ovate leaves that have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are primarily pollinated by birds, particularly sunbirds, due to their shape and color.

The plant is also noted for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
