Crucifera pollichii

Crucifera pollichii, commonly known as the Pollich's mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows or along stream banks. The plant has serrated leaves and produces yellow or white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are small and can be dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across different habitats.

This species is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity and as a potential source of bioactive compounds.
