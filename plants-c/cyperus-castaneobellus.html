Cyperus castaneobellus

Cyperus castaneobellus, commonly known as the red-veined sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its slender, triangular stems are often adorned with reddish veins, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cyperus castaneobellus produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, contributing to its ecological role in wetland habitats.
