Crucifera armoracia

Crucifera armoracia, commonly known as horseradish, is a biennial plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous vegetables like cabbage and mustard. The plant features thick, fleshy roots that store its characteristic pungent compounds. Its leaves are alternate, lobed, and have a rough texture.

Horseradish is known for its strong aroma and is often used as a spice or condiment.
