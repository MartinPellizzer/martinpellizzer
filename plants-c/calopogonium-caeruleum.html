Calopogonium caeruleum

Calopogonium caeruleum, commonly known as blue crown clover, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a low-growing legume characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces cylindrical, woody pods that contain several elliptical seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Calopogonium caeruleum is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment and as a forage crop.
