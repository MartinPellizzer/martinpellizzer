Cleome houstonii

Cleome houstonii, commonly known as Texas spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, spindly stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are simple, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Cleome houstonii is an annual plant that is relatively easy to grow in gardens, making it a popular choice for pollinators.
