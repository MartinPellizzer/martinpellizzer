Catharanthus roseus var. angustus

Catharanthus roseus var. angustus, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Madagascar periwinkle, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of tubular, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is a member of the Apocynaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though it contains toxic alkaloids that require careful handling.
