Chlora imperfoliata var. lanceolata

Chlora imperfoliata var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf chlora, is a perennial herb native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often in shades of white or pale yellow. Its foliage is noted for its glossy texture and distinct venation patterns.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
