Coleus batianoffii

Coleus batianoffii, commonly known as the Batianoff's coleus, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 feet, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often velvety in texture.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens and indoor cultivation.
