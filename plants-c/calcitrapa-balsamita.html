Calcitrapa balsamita

Calcitrapa balsamita, commonly known as sweetroot or balsamroot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its tall, leafy stems and showy yellow flowers. The plant has a taproot that is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically arranged in a composite head.

Calcitrapa balsamita thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides.
