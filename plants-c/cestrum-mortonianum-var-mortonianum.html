Cestrum mortonianum var. mortonianum

Cestrum mortonianum var. mortonianum, commonly known as the white nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale purple flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and ovate, with a smooth texture.

The fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature, containing several seeds.
