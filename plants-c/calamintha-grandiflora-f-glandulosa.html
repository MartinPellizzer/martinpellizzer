Calamintha grandiflora f. glandulosa

Calamintha grandiflora f. glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular calamintha, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, hairy leaves and produces small, showy blue or violet flowers. The plant is characterized by its glandular structures on the leaves and stems, which contribute to its distinctive scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
