Cassia cultrifolia

Cassia cultrifolia, commonly known as the African candle bush, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny branches and bipinnate leaves that are divided into numerous small leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in compact spikes, followed by long, slender pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various arid and semi-arid environments.

Cassia cultrifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in some regions.
