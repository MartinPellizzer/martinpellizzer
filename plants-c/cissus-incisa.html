Cissus incisa

Cissus incisa, commonly known as the rock fig or African snowball, is a climbing plant native to Africa.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in small, white, waxy bumps. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
