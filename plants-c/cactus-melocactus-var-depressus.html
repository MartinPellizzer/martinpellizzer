Cactus melocactus var. depressus

Cactus melocactus var. depressus, commonly known as the Mexican hat cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and thick, ribbed stems that store water efficiently. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its fleshy stems are covered in spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss.

This cactus is well adapted to harsh desert environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil to thrive.
