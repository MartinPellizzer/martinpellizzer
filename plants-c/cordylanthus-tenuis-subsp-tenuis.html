Cordylanthus tenuis subsp. tenuis

Cordylanthus tenuis subsp.

tenuis, commonly known as slenderleaf filaree, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features slender, erect stems and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

It is adapted to dry environments and has a deep root system to access moisture.
