Cissus sexangularis

Cissus sexangularis, commonly known as the diamond vine, is a climbing plant native to Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, four-angled stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into bright red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance.

Cissus sexangularis is also valued for its ability to climb and trail, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and trellises.
