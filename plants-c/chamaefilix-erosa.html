Chamaefilix erosa

Chamaefilix erosa, commonly known as rock rose, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically features small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with fleshy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic foliage and showy flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking hardy, ornamental plants.
