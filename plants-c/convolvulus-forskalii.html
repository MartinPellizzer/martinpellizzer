Convolvulus forskalii

Convolvulus forskalii, commonly known as the Persian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, violet or blue flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant is a vigorous climber, often using its tendrils to attach to supports. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a decorative vine.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its rapid growth and ability to produce abundant blooms throughout the growing season.
