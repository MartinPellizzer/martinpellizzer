Cnicus creticus

Cnicus creticus, commonly known as the Crete thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is well adapted to arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in coastal areas and on limestone slopes.

Cnicus creticus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
