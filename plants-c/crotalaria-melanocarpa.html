Crotalaria melanocarpa

Crotalaria melanocarpa, commonly known as black-seed crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive dark-colored seeds. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its leaves are typically trifoliate. It is often found in dry, disturbed soils and can thrive in arid environments.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and antifungal properties.
