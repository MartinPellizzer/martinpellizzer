Cyanus acmophyllus

Cyanus acmophyllus, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are blue to violet in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

Cyanus acmophyllus is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
