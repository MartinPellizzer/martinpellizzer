Cucubalus floccosus

Cucubalus floccosus, commonly known as the woolly cucumber, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, fleshy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fleshy fruits resembling cucumbers. Its botanical features make it a unique species within the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is often grown for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
