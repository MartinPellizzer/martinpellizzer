Carlina subdecurrens

Carlina subdecurrens, commonly known as the narrowleaf carline thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features a rosette of basal, narrow, and often hairy leaves that are arranged in a decurrent pattern along the stem. Its flowers are typically blue to purple and are borne in a single, showy terminal head.

Carlina subdecurrens is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
