Campanula speciosa subsp. oliveri

Campanula speciosa subsp.

oliveri, commonly known as the Oliver's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in summer.

The plant has lanceolate to ovate leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
