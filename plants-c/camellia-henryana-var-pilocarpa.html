Camellia henryana var. pilocarpa

Camellia henryana var. pilocarpa, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a species native to China and is characterized by its distinctive, large, glossy dark green leaves.

It produces showy, camellia-like flowers that range in color from white to deep red, depending on the variety. The plant is an evergreen shrub or small tree, typically growing to a height of 3 to 5 meters. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, making it a valuable ornamental plant in gardens.

The plant prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, thriving in temperate climates.
