Cucumis pedatus

Cucumis pedatus, commonly known as the horned cucumber, is a unique member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, horn-like fruit that resembles a small horn. The plant produces bright yellow flowers and has heart-shaped, lobed leaves. Its fruit is typically harvested when immature, as it becomes fibrous and bitter when fully mature.

The botanical features of Cucumis pedatus make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ornamental gardening.
