Campanula bononiensis subvar. ruthenica

Campanula bononiensis subvar. ruthenica, commonly known as the Ruthenian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is valued for its attractive blooms and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
