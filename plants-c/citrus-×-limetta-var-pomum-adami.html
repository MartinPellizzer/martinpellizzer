Citrus × limetta var. pomum-adami

Citrus × limetta var. pomum-adami, commonly known as the pomelo or Chinese grapefruit, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, thick-skinned fruit with a pale yellow to greenish-white color. The fruit contains a high water content and is known for its mild, slightly sweet flavor. Botanically, it belongs to the Rutaceae family and is closely related to other citrus species such as grapefruit and oranges.

The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with fragrant leaves and white flowers that bloom in spring.
