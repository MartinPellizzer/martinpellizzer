Cuphea longipedunculata

Cuphea longipedunculata, commonly known as the red hot poker or firecracker plant, is a striking member of the Lythraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red flowers that resemble tiny torches. The plant has hairy, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its inflorescence consists of long, slender peduncles bearing clusters of showy, two-lipped flowers.

Cuphea longipedunculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower due to its long-lasting blooms.
