Centaurea adjarica

Centaurea adjarica, commonly known as the Adjara centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces vibrant blue to purple flowers. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine or subalpine areas.

Centaurea adjarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
