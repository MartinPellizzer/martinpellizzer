Carex scita var. brevisquama

Carex scita var. brevisquama, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This variety is typically found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a single, narrow spike of small, three-ranked flowers.

The plant plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
