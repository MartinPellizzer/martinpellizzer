Codonanthemum puberulum

Codonanthemum puberulum, commonly known as the woolly codonanthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, pubescent leaves and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and shrublands. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with a Mediterranean climate.
