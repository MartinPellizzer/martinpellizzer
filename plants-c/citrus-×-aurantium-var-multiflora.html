Citrus × aurantium var. multiflora

Citrus × aurantium var. multiflora, commonly known as the sweet orange.

It is a hybrid citrus species originating from Southeast Asia, characterized by its rounded, green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces round, orange-colored fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. The fruit contains a juicy, segmented pulp with a high concentration of vitamin C and essential oils.

It is widely cultivated for both its fruit and its use in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
