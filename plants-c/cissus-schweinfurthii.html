Cissus schweinfurthii

Cissus schweinfurthii, commonly known as the rock fig or elephant's foot, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in rocky or dry soil conditions.

Cissus schweinfurthii is also known for its ability to climb using its tendrils, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or trellises.
