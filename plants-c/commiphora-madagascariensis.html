Commiphora madagascariensis

Commiphora madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascan myrrh tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine. The plant features smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves with a leathery texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

The tree thrives in tropical dry forests and is valued for its ecological and cultural significance.
