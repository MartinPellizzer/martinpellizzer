Chondrosea rosularis

Chondrosea rosularis, commonly known as the rosette sea star, is a unique marine plant found in shallow coastal waters.

It exhibits a distinctive rosette-like structure composed of fleshy, leaf-like structures called phyllodes. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in high-salinity environments, which is an adaptation to its marine habitat. Its growth pattern is slow, and it often forms dense clusters that provide habitat for small aquatic organisms.

Due to its specialized adaptations, Chondrosea rosularis plays a minor but ecologically significant role in coastal ecosystems.
