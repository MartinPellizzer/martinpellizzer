Carpesium nepalense var. griffithii

Carpesium nepalense var. griffithii, commonly known as the Nepal thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
