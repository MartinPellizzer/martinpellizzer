Cyathea bettinae

Cyathea bettinae, commonly known as the Betts' tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to several meters. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its tall stem, while its fronds are used for photosynthesis.

Cyathea bettinae plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
