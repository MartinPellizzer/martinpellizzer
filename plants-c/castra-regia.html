Castra regia

Castra regia, commonly known as the royal fortress, is a rare and striking plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its harsh environment. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Castra regia is often found growing on rocky outcrops, where it thrives in well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
