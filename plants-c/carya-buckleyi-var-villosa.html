Carya buckleyi var. villosa

Carya buckleyi var. villosa, commonly known as the velvet chestnut, is a variant of the chestnut genus native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy or velvety texture on its leaves and young shoots, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, sweet-tasting nuts encased in a spiny husk. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in the region's flora.
