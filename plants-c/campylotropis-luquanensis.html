Campylotropis luquanensis

Campylotropis luquanensis, commonly known as the Luquan hairy beggar-ticks, is a flowering plant species native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately compound, while its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
