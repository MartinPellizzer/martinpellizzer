Carex gembolensis

Carex gembolensis, commonly known as the Gembol sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and swampy areas. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Carex gembolensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and small fauna.
