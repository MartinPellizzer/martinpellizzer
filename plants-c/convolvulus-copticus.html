Convolvulus copticus

Convolvulus copticus, commonly known as the Egyptian bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing along roadsides and in dry areas.

Convolvulus copticus is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
