Cucumis alba

Cucumis alba, commonly known as the white cucumber, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant produces small, white, spherical fruits that are typically harvested when immature. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and it has a trailing growth habit.

The plant is often grown for its edible fruit, which has a mild, slightly sweet flavor.
