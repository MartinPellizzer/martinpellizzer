Cousinia qaradaghensis

Cousinia qaradaghensis, commonly known as the Qaradagh hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It features narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in summer.

Cousinia qaradaghensis is often found in alpine meadows and is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations.
