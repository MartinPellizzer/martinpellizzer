Crataegus alpestris

Crataegus alpestris, commonly known as the alpine hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges, and it is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Crataegus alpestris is also valued for its ecological role, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
