Calamintha albiflora

Calamintha albiflora, commonly known as whiteflower germander, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features aromatic, hairy leaves that are typically opposite and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, white, tubular flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Calamintha albiflora is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
