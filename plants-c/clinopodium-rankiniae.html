Clinopodium rankiniae

Clinopodium rankiniae, commonly known as Rankin's Mint, is a perennial herb native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, square stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Clinopodium rankiniae is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
