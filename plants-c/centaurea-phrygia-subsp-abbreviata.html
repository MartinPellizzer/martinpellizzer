Centaurea phrygia subsp. abbreviata

Centaurea phrygia subsp.

abbreviata, commonly known as the short-stemmed centaurea, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features erect, branching stems that are typically shorter than the typical species, with hairy surfaces.

The plant produces vibrant blue to purple composite flowers, which are arranged in dense, conical inflorescences. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
