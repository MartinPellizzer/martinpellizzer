Cuphea subuligera

Cuphea subuligera, commonly known as the spiny cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, spiny bracts that encase its small, tubular flowers. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens in warm climates.

Cuphea subuligera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
