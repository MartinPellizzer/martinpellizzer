Cistanche violacea

Cistanche violacea, commonly known as the purple cistanche, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients, typically growing on the roots of various shrubs and trees. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia. Its root system is thick and fleshy, which stores nutrients and water.

Due to its unique growth habit and medicinal properties, it is of interest in both botanical research and traditional medicine.
