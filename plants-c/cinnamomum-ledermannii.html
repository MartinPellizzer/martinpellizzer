Cinnamomum ledermannii

Cinnamomum ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the laurel family ( Lauraceae ) and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces dark purple berries that contain kidney-shaped seeds, which are used in traditional medicine.

Its essential oils, derived from the leaves and bark, are valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.
