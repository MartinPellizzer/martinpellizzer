Calamagrostis pooides

Calamagrostis pooides, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-blue foliage and tall, erect flowering stems that produce small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive silvery sheen that gives it its common name.

Calamagrostis pooides is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
