Cinchona officinalis var. crispa

Cinchona officinalis var. crispa, commonly known as the wavy bark cinchona, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and often peeling bark, which contains high concentrations of alkaloids such as quinine. The plant is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is typically found in humid, mountainous forests. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers.

This variety is particularly valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in the treatment of malaria.
