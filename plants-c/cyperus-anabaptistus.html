Cyperus anabaptistus

Cyperus anabaptistus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and riparian zones, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, linear leaves, and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated. Its root system helps in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in aquatic habitats.

Cyperus anabaptistus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and adaptability to various water conditions.
