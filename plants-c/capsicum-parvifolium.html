Capsicum parvifolium

Capsicum parvifolium, commonly known as the dwarf pepper or wild pepper, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family.

It is native to the Andes mountains of South America and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a mild, peppery flavor.

Due to its compact size and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for gardeners looking to add visual interest to their landscapes.
