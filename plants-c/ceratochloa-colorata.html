Ceratochloa colorata

Ceratochloa colorata, commonly known as the blue-green grass, is a member of the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wet meadows. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinct waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Ceratochloa colorata is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive color and texture.
