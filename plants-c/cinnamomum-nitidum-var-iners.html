Cinnamomum nitidum var. iners

Cinnamomum nitidum var. iners, commonly known as the smooth-leaved cinnamon tree, is a variant of the cinnamon tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, reddish-brown fruits that contain aromatic seeds. It is often cultivated for its essential oils and medicinal properties.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both traditional medicine and horticulture.
