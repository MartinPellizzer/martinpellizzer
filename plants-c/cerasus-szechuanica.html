Cerasus szechuanica

Cerasus szechuanica, commonly known as the Sichuan cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Cerasus szechuanica is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine for its potential health benefits.
