Crinum doriae

Crinum doriae, commonly known as the Doria's glory-of-the-snow, is a striking bulbous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Crinum doriae is often grown for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in well-drained soil with partial shade.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
