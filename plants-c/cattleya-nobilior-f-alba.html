Cattleya nobilior f. alba

Cattleya nobilior f. alba, commonly known as the white noble cattleya, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a fragrant scent, which are typically borne on sturdy, upright stems. The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are dark green and arranged in a fan shape, contributing to its lush appearance. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light and well-draining soil.

This cultivar is highly valued in horticulture for its beauty and is often cultivated for floral arrangements and ornamental purposes.
