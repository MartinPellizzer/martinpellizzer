Correa macrocalyx

Correa macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered correa, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive red or orange throat. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Correa macrocalyx is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is well-suited to coastal climates.
