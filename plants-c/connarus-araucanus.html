Connarus araucanus

Connarus araucanus, commonly known as the Araucaria tree, is a species of coniferous evergreen native to South America.

It belongs to the family Araucariaceae and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its foliage consists of scale-like leaves that are closely pressed to the branches.

This plant is notable for its ecological importance and historical significance in indigenous cultures.
