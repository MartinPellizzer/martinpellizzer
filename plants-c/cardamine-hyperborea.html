Cardamine hyperborea

Cardamine hyperborea, commonly known as the alpine buttercup, is a perennial herb native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in rocky or moist, shaded environments such as mountain meadows and forested areas. The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive veined pattern. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and are arranged in a short raceme.

Cardamine hyperborea is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in alpine and subalpine ecosystems.
