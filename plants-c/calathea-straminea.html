Calathea straminea

Calathea straminea, commonly known as the pink diamond plant, is a tropical perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display a mix of pink, white, and green hues. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is prized for its ornamental value in both indoor and outdoor settings. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to maintain its vibrant foliage.

Calathea straminea is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for houseplants.
