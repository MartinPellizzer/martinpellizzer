Cucurbita pepo var. phaeotaenia

Cucurbita pepo var. phaeotaenia, commonly known as the black-seed pumpkin, is a variant of the common gourd species.

It is characterized by its dark green, elongated fruits that often have a rough, warty texture. The plant produces small yellow flowers and thrives in warm, sunny conditions. Its seeds are dark and numerous, typically enclosed within the thick, fibrous rind.

This variety is valued for both ornamental purposes and as a source of edible seeds and squash.
