Carex vesicaria subsp. virens

Carex vesicaria subsp.

virens, commonly known as green bulblet sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and cylindrical inflorescence.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive mid-green color.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often greenish or reddish, adding to its ornamental value in gardens.
