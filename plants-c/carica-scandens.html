Carica scandens

Carica scandens, commonly known as the climbing papaya, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Caricaceae family and is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit, with tendrils that allow it to ascend trees or other structures. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers, followed by edible fruits similar to those of the common papaya. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit production, it is cultivated in various tropical regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
