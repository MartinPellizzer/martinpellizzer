Croton caudatus var. malaccanus

Croton caudatus var. malaccanus, commonly known as the Malacca croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which often features shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has alternate, simple leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental leaves and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in warm climates.
