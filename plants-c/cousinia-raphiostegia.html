Cousinia raphiostegia

Cousinia raphiostegia, commonly known as the Kashmir copper grass, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky, alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climates. The plant features slender, grass-like stems and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink and purple. Its leaves are narrow and covered in a fine, white waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Cousinia raphiostegia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and full sun.
