Cynoglossum heratense

Cynoglossum heratense, commonly known as Herat's tongue, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and distinctive, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cynoglossum heratense is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
