Carex mohriana

Carex mohriana, commonly known as Mohri's sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Mohri's sedge produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are pollinated by wind.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
