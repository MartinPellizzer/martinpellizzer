Cymbidium angustifolium

Cymbidium angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf orchid, is a species native to eastern Australia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and upright growth habit. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and relatively easy-to-care-for ornamental plant.
