Cliffortia concavifolia

Cliffortia concavifolia, commonly known as the Cape sugarbush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are leathery and have a distinctive concave base, giving it its scientific name.

Cliffortia concavifolia is often used in ornamental gardening due to its hardiness and attractive appearance.
