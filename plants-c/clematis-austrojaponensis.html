Clematis austrojaponensis

Clematis austrojaponensis, commonly known as the Japanese Clematis, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its graceful growth habit.

Clematis austrojaponensis is relatively hardy and can be grown in USDA hardiness zones 6 to 8.
