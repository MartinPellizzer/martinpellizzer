Cinnamomum pseudomelastoma

Cinnamomum pseudomelastoma, commonly known as the Java cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown bark that resembles true cinnamon. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, dark purple berries. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy surface with a distinct aromatic scent.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its bark in traditional medicine and as a spice.
