Cryptochilus sanguineus

Cryptochilus sanguineus, commonly known as the red-stemmed orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking red stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
