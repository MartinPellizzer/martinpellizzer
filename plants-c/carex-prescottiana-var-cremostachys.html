Carex prescottiana var. cremostachys

Carex prescottiana var. cremostachys, commonly known as the Prescott sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their fibrous roots and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or wetland habitats. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
