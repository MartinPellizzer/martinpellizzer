Capsicum regale

Capsicum regale, commonly known as the Christmas bell pepper, is a striking ornamental and edible plant native to South America.

It produces large, heart-shaped fruits that can grow up to 12 inches in diameter, often with a vibrant red or orange hue when mature. The plant is a member of the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, and it is cultivated for both its ornamental value and its sweet, mild flavor.

Capsicum regale requires a long growing season and is often grown as an annual in cooler regions.
