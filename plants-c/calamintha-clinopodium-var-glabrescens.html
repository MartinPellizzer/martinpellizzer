Calamintha clinopodium var. glabrescens

Calamintha clinopodium var. glabrescens, commonly known as smooth catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a strong, mint-like fragrance.

This variety is distinguished by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves, setting it apart from other calamintha species.
