Carum atlanticum

Carum atlanticum, commonly known as Atlantic caraway, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant features slender, branching stems and small white to greenish flowers that bloom in umbrella-like clusters. Its leaves are finely divided and have a mild, caraway-like scent.
