Coelogyne micrantha

Coelogyne micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments. The plant features a pseudobulb with fleshy leaves and produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow. Its blooming period occurs during the cooler months, often in winter or early spring.

The orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
