Croton rhamnifolius var. caudatus

Croton rhamnifolius var. caudatus, commonly known as the long-leaved croton, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often serrated leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by bright red or orange berries. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
