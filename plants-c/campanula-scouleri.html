Campanula scouleri

Campanula scouleri, commonly known as the Pacific bluebell, is a perennial herb native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, sky-blue to lavender flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in a fine, hairy texture, providing a soft appearance.

Campanula scouleri is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in cool, humid climates.
