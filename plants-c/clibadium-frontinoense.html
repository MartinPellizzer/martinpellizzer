Clibadium frontinoense

Clibadium frontinoense, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in vibrant shades of purple and blue. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
