Centaurea nigra f. radiata

Centaurea nigra f. radiata, commonly known as the radiate nightshade, is a flowering plant characterized by its striking dark purple to black flowers.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant features slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are arranged in a radiate head, with both ray and disk florets, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ability to thrive in challenging environments.
