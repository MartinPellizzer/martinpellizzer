Callicarpa cinnamomea

Callicarpa cinnamomea, commonly known as the cinnamon beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright orange-red berries in the fall, which are edible but often considered too tart for consumption. Callicarpa cinnamomea thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
