Cynorkis cinnabarina

Cynorkis cinnabarina, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, vivid red petals and sepals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are small but numerous, often arranged in a dense inflorescence that enhances its visual appeal.

Due to its unique coloration and habitat preferences, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
