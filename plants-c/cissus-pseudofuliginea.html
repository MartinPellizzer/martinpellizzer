Cissus pseudofuliginea

Cissus pseudofuliginea, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray leaves that have a waxy texture and a pleasant, sweet scent. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are followed by green, berry-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Cissus pseudofuliginea is also known for its ability to climb using tendrils and its potential use in traditional medicine.
