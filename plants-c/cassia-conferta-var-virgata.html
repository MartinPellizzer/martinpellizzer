Cassia conferta var. virgata

Cassia conferta var. virgata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves composed of several narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
