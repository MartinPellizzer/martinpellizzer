Cleome noeana var. persepolitana

Cleome noeana var. persepolitana, commonly known as the Persian spider flower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while the stems are erect and branching.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
