Cyperus dussianus

Cyperus dussianus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and riparian zones. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Cyperus dussianus is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and its ability to thrive in water-saturated soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
