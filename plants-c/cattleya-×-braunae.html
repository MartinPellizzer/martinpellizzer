Cattleya × braunae

Cattleya × braunae, commonly known as the Brauns Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid prized for its vibrant, fragrant blooms.

It originates from a cross between Cattleya bicolor and Cattleya mossiae, resulting in a plant with striking orange and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its native tropical habitats. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light and well-draining potting mix.

Its flowers can last several weeks, making it a popular choice for both collectors and gardeners.
