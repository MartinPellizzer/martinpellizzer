Carex cavaleriensis

Carex cavaleriensis, commonly known as the Cavalerien's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex cavaleriensis plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
