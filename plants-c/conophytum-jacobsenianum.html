Conophytum jacobsenianum

Conophytum jacobsenianum, commonly known as the Jacobsen's conophytum, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, triangular leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its unique morphology and low maintenance make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
