Chlorophytum burundiense

Chlorophytum burundiense, commonly known as the Burundi spider plant, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Burundi.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces delicate, pendulous inflorescences with small white flowers that bloom in spring. It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often growing in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
