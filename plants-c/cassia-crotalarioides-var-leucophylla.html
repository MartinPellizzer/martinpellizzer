Cassia crotalarioides var. leucophylla

Cassia crotalarioides var. leucophylla, commonly known as the white-leaf cassia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features compound leaves with elongated, pale green leaflets that give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are followed by flat, elongated pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes in suitable climates.
