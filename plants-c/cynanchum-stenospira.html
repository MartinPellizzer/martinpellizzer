Cynanchum stenospira

Cynanchum stenospira, commonly known as the narrow-spurred milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes other milkweeds, and is characterized by its milky sap, a common trait in the genus Cynanchum. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive spurred structure that aids in pollination by insects.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
