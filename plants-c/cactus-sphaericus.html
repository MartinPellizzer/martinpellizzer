Cactus sphaericus

Cactus sphaericus, commonly known as the globe cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. Its spines are short and dense, providing protection from herbivores and reducing water loss through transpiration.

Cactus sphaericus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
