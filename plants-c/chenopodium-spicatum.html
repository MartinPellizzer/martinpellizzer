Chenopodium spicatum

Chenopodium spicatum, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in disturbed soils and arid environments. The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated edges and a rough texture. Its small, greenish flowers are arranged in clusters, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

Chenopodium spicatum is often found in regions with temperate to semi-arid climates.
