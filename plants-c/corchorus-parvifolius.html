Corchorus parvifolius

Corchorus parvifolius, commonly known as slenderleaf dock, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its alternate, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory and detoxifying properties.

The plant is also valued for its fibrous stems, which have been historically used in textile production.
