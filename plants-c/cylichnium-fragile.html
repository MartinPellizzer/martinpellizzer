Cylichnium fragile

Cylichnium fragile, commonly known as the brittle cylichnium, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, branching stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its fragile nature, it is often found in disturbed or marginal habitats.
