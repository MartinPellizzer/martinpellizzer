Carex tagawana

Carex tagawana, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grasses, and reeds. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and usually have a bluish-green hue.

Carex tagawana produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.
