Commicarpus stellatus

Commicarpus stellatus, commonly known as the starfruit or carambola, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped fruit, which turns yellow when ripe. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is not only flavorful but also rich in vitamin C and antioxidants.
