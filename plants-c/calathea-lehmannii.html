Calathea lehmannii

Calathea lehmannii, commonly known as the leopard calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, mottled leaves that display a mix of green, silver, and reddish hues. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its unique leaf patterns. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Calathea lehmannii is a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
