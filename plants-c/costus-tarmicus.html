Costus tarmicus

Costus tarmicus, commonly known as the Tarmic costus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and related plants. The plant features tall, slender stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. Its flowers are clustered in spadices, typical of the Zingiberaceae family, and are usually yellow or orange in color.

Costus tarmicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
