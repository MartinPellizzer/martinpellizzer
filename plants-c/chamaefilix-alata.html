Chamaefilix alata

Chamaefilix alata, commonly known as the rock rose, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a low-growing, spreading habit with fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for xeriscaping.
