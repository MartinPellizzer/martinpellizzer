Caryophyllus propinquus

Caryophyllus propinquus, commonly known as the close relative of the clove, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural studies.
