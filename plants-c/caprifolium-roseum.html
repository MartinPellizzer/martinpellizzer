Caprifolium roseum

Caprifolium roseum, commonly known as the pink honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated margin, and small, fragrant flowers that range from pale pink to deep red. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Caprifolium roseum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
