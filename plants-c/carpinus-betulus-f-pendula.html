Carpinus betulus f. pendula

Carpinus betulus f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping hornbeam, is a unique variant of the common hornbeam.

It is characterized by its graceful, cascading branches that droop gracefully from the main trunk. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by edible, chestnut-like fruits. Its foliage is dark green in summer and turns to a striking coppery hue in autumn.

This ornamental tree is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
