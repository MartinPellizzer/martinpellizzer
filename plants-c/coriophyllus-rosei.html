Coriophyllus rosei

Coriophyllus rosei, commonly known as the roseleaf coralplant, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its succulent, spiny stems and clusters of small, pinkish flowers. The plant exhibits a unique adaptation to arid environments, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water. Its flowers typically bloom in the spring and are pollinated by insects such as bees and butterflies.

Coriophyllus rosei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
