Cornus canadensis f. connatifolia

Cornus canadensis f. connatifolia, commonly known as the Canadian dogwood or red dogwood, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cornaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red stems and small white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
