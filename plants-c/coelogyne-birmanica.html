Coelogyne birmanica

Coelogyne birmanica, commonly known as the Burmese orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant, and colorful flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, enabling it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically long and strap-like, and it prefers bright, indirect light.

Coelogyne birmanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a suitable environment.
