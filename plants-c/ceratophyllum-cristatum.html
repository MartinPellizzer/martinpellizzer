Ceratophyllum cristatum

Ceratophyllum cristatum, commonly known as the spiked water-milfoil, is a freshwater plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, scale-like leaves. The plant lacks true roots and instead absorbs nutrients through its stems and leaves. It typically grows in shallow waters and is often found in ponds and lakes.

Ceratophyllum cristatum is an important part of aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
