Calceolaria penlandii subsp. penlandii

Calceolaria penlandii subsp.

penlandii, commonly known as Penland's slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its striking, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically orange or yellow in color.

The plant produces showy, bilaterally symmetrical blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
