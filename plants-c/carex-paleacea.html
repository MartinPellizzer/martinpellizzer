Carex paleacea

Carex paleacea, commonly known as paleleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to brown, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex paleacea plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
