Clomenocoma squarrosa

Clomenocoma squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, silver-green foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its unique appearance makes it a striking addition to native gardens and xeriscapes.

Clomenocoma squarrosa is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
