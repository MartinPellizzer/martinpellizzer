Clethra bracteata

Clethra bracteata, commonly known as the white clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 feet and features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in cylindrical clusters during the summer months. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Clethra bracteata is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
