Chrysochlamys tenuis

Chrysochlamys tenuis, commonly known as the narrow-leafed goldenbush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, typically blooming in spring. It is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Chrysochlamys tenuis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native plant landscaping.
