Chevreulia nivea

Chevreulia nivea, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking white, flame-like flowers that bloom in spring, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant has compound leaves that are alternate and pinnate, with small, ovate leaflets. Its bark is smooth and light gray, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds.

Chevreulia nivea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
