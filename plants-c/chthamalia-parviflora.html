Chthamalia parviflora

Chthamalia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered chthamalium, is a species of red algae found primarily in coastal areas.

It thrives in intertidal zones, attaching itself to rocks and other substrates. The plant has a delicate, branching structure with small, pinkish to reddish fronds. It plays an important role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various organisms.

Its reproductive structures are typically located at the base of the fronds, facilitating spore dispersal in the aquatic environment.
