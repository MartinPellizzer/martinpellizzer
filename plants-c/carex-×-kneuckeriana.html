Carex × kneuckeriana

Carex × kneuckeriana, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It belongs to the Carex genus, which includes over 2,000 species of sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces viable seeds that aid in its propagation.

Carex × kneuckeriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
