Cytisus leucanthus var. pallidus

Cytisus leucanthus var. pallidus, commonly known as the pale-flowered broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and coastal areas.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
