Crinum × ceciliae

Crinum × ceciliae, commonly known as the hybrid Crinum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in shades of white, pink, and yellow. The plant produces a bulbous stem that stores nutrients and water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. Its leaves are strap-like, long, and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

Crinum × ceciliae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
