Carex ensifolia subsp. lugens

Carex ensifolia subsp.

lugens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with linear, sword-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
