Crocus antalyensis subsp. striatus

Crocus antalyensis subsp.

striatus, commonly known as the striped crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family. It is native to Turkey and is characterized by its distinctive, narrow leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers with a yellow center.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats and is known for its early blooming period in spring. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
