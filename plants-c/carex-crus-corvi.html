Carex crus-corvi

Carex crus-corvi, commonly known as the crowfoot sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, pond edges, and along stream banks.

Carex crus-corvi plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
