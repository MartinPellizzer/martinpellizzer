Cereus houlletii

Cereus houlletii, commonly known as the night bloomer or moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is typically found in rocky or arid environments. The plant produces striking white or pale pink flowers at night, which emit a sweet fragrance. Its growth is slow, and it thrives in full sun with well-draining soil.

Cereus houlletii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
