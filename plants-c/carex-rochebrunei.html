Carex rochebrunei

Carex rochebrunei, commonly known as the Rochebrune sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. The plant features grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and narrow, with a dense, tufted growth habit. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex rochebrunei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
