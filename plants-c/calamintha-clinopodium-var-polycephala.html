Calamintha clinopodium var. polycephala

Calamintha clinopodium var. polycephala, commonly known as narrow-leaved summer savory, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, aromatic stems covered in small, opposite leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is valued for its culinary uses and historical role in herbal medicine.
