Crotalaria pedunculosa

Crotalaria pedunculosa, commonly known as the clustered crotalaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant features erect, branching stems and produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers arranged in clusters. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
