Cliffortia laricina var. longifolia

Cliffortia laricina var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved starling bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, five-petaled flowers in shades of white and pink. The leaves are typically long and slender, giving the plant its varietal name, and they are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found growing on cliffs or slopes.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
