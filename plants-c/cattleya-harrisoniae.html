Cattleya harrisoniae

Cattleya harrisoniae, commonly known as the Harrison's Cattleya, is a tropical orchid native to the rainforests of Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically feature a vibrant pink or purple coloration with contrasting white or yellow markings. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences, and its pseudobulbs are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its native humid environment. The flowers are highly fragrant, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
