Convolvulus sabatius var. mauritanicus

Convolvulus sabatius var. mauritanicus, commonly known as the Mauritania bindweed, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, pale yellow or white flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and thrives in well-drained soils.

This species is known for its ability to tolerate drought conditions and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
