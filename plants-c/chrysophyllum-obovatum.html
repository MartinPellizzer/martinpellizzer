Chrysophyllum obovatum

Chrysophyllum obovatum, commonly known as the African cherry or yellow bell, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are rich in nutrients and often consumed fresh or processed into beverages. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

Chrysophyllum obovatum is also valued for its timber, which is used in furniture and construction.
