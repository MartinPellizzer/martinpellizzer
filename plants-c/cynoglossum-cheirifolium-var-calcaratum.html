Cynoglossum cheirifolium var. calcaratum

Cynoglossum cheirifolium var. calcaratum, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in a white, powdery coating. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers with five petals, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
