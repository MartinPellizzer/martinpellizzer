Cecropia boliviana

Cecropia boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian cecropia, is a fast-growing tree native to the Amazon rainforest of South America.

It belongs to the Cecropiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, hollow stems. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria in its root nodules, enhancing soil fertility. Its rapid growth rate makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.

The plant also provides habitat and food for various animals, including insects and birds.
