Convolvulus bractiflorus

Convolvulus bractiflorus, commonly known as the bract-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant produces small, leaf-like bracts that enclose the flowers, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Convolvulus bractiflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
