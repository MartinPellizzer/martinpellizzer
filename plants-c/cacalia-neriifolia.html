Cacalia neriifolia

Cacalia neriifolia, commonly known as the blackleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowers bloom in the spring and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cacalia neriifolia is a perennial herb that prefers well-drained soil and can be found in the southeastern United States.
