Crocus × paulineae

Crocus × paulineae, commonly known as the Paulineae crocus, is a hybrid species within the Crocus genus.

It is characterized by its vibrant, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This ornamental plant is valued for its early blooming habit and is commonly cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
