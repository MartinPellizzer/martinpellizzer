Chamaemelum suaveolens

Chamaemelum suaveolens, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

Sweet everlasting is often used in herbal remedies and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
