Copernicia glabrescens var. havanensis

Copernicia glabrescens var. havanensis, commonly known as the Cuban palm or Havanna palm, is a species of palm native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It is a medium-sized tree with a slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are often used for thatching. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, fibrous fruits. It thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soil and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and utility, it is cultivated in various regions for both horticultural and practical purposes.
