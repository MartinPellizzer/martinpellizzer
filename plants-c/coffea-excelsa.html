Coffea excelsa

Coffea excelsa, commonly known as the West African coffee or kopi luwak, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to tropical Africa, particularly the western and central regions, and grows at elevations between 1,000 and 2,000 meters. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that contain the seeds used for coffee production. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a strong aromatic scent.

The plant is valued not only for its coffee beans but also for its ornamental qualities.
