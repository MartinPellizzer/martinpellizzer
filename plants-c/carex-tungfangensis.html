Carex tungfangensis

Carex tungfangensis, commonly known as the Tungfang sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to China and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. It is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wet conditions.

Carex tungfangensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
