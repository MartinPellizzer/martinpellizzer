Chenopodium album var. candicans

Chenopodium album var. candicans, commonly known as lamb's quarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are followed by triangular, edible seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Chenopodium album var. candicans is a common weed but also has historical uses in traditional medicine and as a food source.
