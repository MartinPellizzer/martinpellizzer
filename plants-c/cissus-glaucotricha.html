Cissus glaucotricha

Cissus glaucotricha, commonly known as the silver-leafed cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive glaucous leaves, which have a waxy, bluish-gray coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its stems are succulent and can develop aerial roots, allowing it to climb and attach to surfaces.

Cissus glaucotricha thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage.
