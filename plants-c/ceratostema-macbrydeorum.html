Ceratostema macbrydeorum

Ceratostema macbrydeorum, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a distinctive greenish throat. The plant has a creeping rhizome and produces small, oval leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a prized addition to botanical collections.
