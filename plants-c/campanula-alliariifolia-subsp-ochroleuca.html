Campanula alliariifolia subsp. ochroleuca

Campanula alliariifolia subsp.

ochroleuca, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly garlic-scented aroma. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
