Convolvulus farinosus

Convolvulus farinosus, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, trailing stems and small, pale blue or pink flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
