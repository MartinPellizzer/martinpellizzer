Cochlidium repandum

Cochlidium repandum, commonly known as the common cocklebur, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to North America and is characterized by its spiny, spherical fruits that encase the seeds. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family.
