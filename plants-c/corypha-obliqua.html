Corypha obliqua

Corypha obliqua, commonly known as the oblique palm, is a species of palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces distinctive inflorescences with clustered, spadix-like flowers, which are often surrounded by brightly colored bracts. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The oblique palm is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in some regions.
