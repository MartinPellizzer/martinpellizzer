Cassia minutiflora

Cassia minutiflora, commonly known as the small-flowered cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features compound leaves with numerous small, narrow leaflets, and produces clusters of yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Cassia minutiflora is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
