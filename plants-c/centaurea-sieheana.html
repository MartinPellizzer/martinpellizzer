Centaurea sieheana

Centaurea sieheana, commonly known as Siehe's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its bright blue or purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Centaurea sieheana is often found in open areas with well-drained soil, and it can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
