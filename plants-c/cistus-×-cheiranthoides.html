Cistus × cheiranthoides

Cistus × cheiranthoides, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the genus Cistus, which includes several species of evergreen shrubs characterized by their aromatic foliage and showy flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are leathery and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Cistus × cheiranthoides is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a hardy plant suitable for xeriscaping.
