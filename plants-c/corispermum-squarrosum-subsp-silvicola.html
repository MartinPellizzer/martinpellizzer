Corispermum squarrosum subsp. silvicola

Corispermum squarrosum subsp.

silvicola, commonly known as the forest corispermum, is a low-growing annual plant native to temperate regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow flowers and bract-like leaves.

The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its seeds are enclosed in a capsule and are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
