Combretum auratum

Combretum auratum, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and turn reddish in color when mature. Its bark is smooth and light brown, often peeling in thin layers.

Combretum auratum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
