Celsia rupicola

Celsia rupicola, commonly known as the rock celsia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Due to its ornamental value and unique habitat, it is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
