Carex secalina

Carex secalina, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, ponds, and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Carex secalina is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
