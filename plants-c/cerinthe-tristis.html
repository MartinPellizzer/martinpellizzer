Cerinthe tristis

Cerinthe tristis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cerinthe, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, tubular blue or violet flowers. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
