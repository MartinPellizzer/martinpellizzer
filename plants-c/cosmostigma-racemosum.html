Cosmostigma racemosum

Cosmostigma racemosum, commonly known as the clustered starflower, is a member of the Geraniaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, racemose inflorescence composed of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant, colorful blooms that appear in late spring to early summer.
