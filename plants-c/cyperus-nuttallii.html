Cyperus nuttallii

Cyperus nuttallii, commonly known as the Nuttall flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of ponds. It has small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense spikes.

Cyperus nuttallii is often found in disturbed or wet soils and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
