Chrysothamnus greenei subsp. filifolius

Chrysothamnus greenei subsp.

filifolius, commonly known as the narrowleaf goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and silvery-green foliage, which give it a striking appearance.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and slopes. It has small, linear leaves and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in harsh, dry conditions.
