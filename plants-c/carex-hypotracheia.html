Carex hypotracheia

Carex hypotracheia, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct midrib, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes.

Carex hypotracheia plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
