Cereus tetragonus var. ramosior

Cereus tetragonus var. ramosior, commonly known as the clustered night blooming cereus, is a cacti species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its striking appearance and nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
