Chorizanthe pungens var. hartwegiana

Chorizanthe pungens var. hartwegiana, commonly known as Hartweg's spiny tumbleweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its woody, branched stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. Its unique morphology allows it to scatter its seeds easily, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and animals.
