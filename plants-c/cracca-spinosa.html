Cracca spinosa

Cracca spinosa, commonly known as the spiny cracca, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features spiny stems and leaves, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cracca spinosa is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
