Calamus saltuensis

Calamus saltuensis, commonly known as the saltuensis canes, is a member of the Arecaceae family, characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnately arranged leaves.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, typically found in wetlands and marshy areas where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or green, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits containing numerous seeds. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Calamus saltuensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
