Colocasia esculenta var. acris

Colocasia esculenta var. acris, commonly known as the elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water, which helps it survive in humid climates.

The plant is also valued for its edible corms, which are used in various cuisines.
