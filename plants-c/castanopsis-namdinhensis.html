Castanopsis namdinhensis

Castanopsis namdinhensis, commonly known as the Nam Dinh tallow tree, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and chestnuts, and is characterized by its hard, thick-shelled nuts. The fruit, which matures in the fall, is an important food source for various wildlife species.
