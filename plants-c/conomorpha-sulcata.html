Conomorpha sulcata

Conomorpha sulcata, commonly known as the African giant pouched rat, is a rodent native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its long, slender body, large ears, and a distinctive pouched tail. The plant-like appearance of its fur and the way it moves through dense vegetation have led to its name. While not a plant, it is often mistaken for one due to its botanical-looking features.

This species plays an important role in its ecosystem as a seed disperser and prey animal.
