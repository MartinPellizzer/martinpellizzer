Cynanchum hieronymi

Cynanchum hieronymi, commonly known as the Hieronymi milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by follicles that contain seeds with silky hairs. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and poor soil conditions.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
