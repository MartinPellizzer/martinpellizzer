Cistanche calotropidis var. tomentosa

Cistanche calotropidis var. tomentosa, commonly known as the tomentose Cistanche, is a parasitic plant belonging to the Orobanchaceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of China, where it attaches to the roots of host plants such as Cistanche tubulosa. The plant lacks true leaves and chlorophyll, relying entirely on its host for nutrients. It produces yellowish to reddish flowers and has a fleshy, branched stem.

This variety is valued in traditional medicine for its purported ability to enhance vitality and treat various ailments.
