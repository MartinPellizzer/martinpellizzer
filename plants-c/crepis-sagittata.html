Crepis sagittata

Crepis sagittata, commonly known as the arrowhead groundsel, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, grassy areas and has narrow, arrow-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Crepis sagittata is often found in meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
