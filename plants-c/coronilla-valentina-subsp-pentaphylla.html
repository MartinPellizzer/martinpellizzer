Coronilla valentina subsp. pentaphylla

Coronilla valentina subsp.

pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leafed vetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate or pentaphyllate leaves, which give it its scientific name.

The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it an attractive plant for ecological gardens and restoration projects.
