Chiococca alba subsp. parvifolia

Chiococca alba subsp.

parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered white chiococca, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America. It is a shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within cloud forests.

The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in inflorescences, often with a faintly fragrant scent.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in botanical gardens.
