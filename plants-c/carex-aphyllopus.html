Carex aphyllopus

Carex aphyllopus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Carex aphyllopus plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
