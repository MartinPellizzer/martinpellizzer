Centaurea orphanidea subsp. thessala

Centaurea orphanidea subsp.

thessala, commonly known as the Thessalian star-thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features spiny, branched stems and is characterized by its distinctive blue to purple flowers.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a rough texture, and it produces small, achene-like seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as steppes and slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
