Carex dentata

Carex dentata, commonly known as the toothed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, with small, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

Carex dentata plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
