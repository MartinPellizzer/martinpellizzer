Carex montana f. albescens

Carex montana f. albescens, commonly known as the white-flowered sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species Carex montana.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America. The plant features dense, grass-like foliage with narrow, strap-shaped leaves that are typically bright green. Its most distinguishing characteristic is the presence of white or pale blue flower spikes that appear in late summer.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
