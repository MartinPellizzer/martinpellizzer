Carex rochebrunei var. remotispicula

Carex rochebrunei var. remotispicula, commonly known as the slender spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a narrow, spike-like arrangement of small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is notable for its delicate foliage and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
