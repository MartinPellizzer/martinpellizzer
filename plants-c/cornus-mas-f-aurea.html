Cornus mas f. aurea

Cornus mas f. aurea, commonly known as the golden cornel.

This ornamental shrub is a compact variant of the common cornel, characterized by its striking golden-yellow leaves that contrast beautifully with its red stems. It produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that attract wildlife. The plant is native to Europe and thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is valued for its seasonal color changes and is often used in gardens for its visual interest throughout the year.
