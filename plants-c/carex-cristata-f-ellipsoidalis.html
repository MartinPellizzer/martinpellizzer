Carex cristata f. ellipsoidalis

Carex cristata f. ellipsoidalis, commonly known as the elliptical sedge, is a variant of the common sedge, characterized by its unique elliptical-shaped leaves.

This plant is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks. It has a dense, grass-like appearance with narrow, grassy foliage that forms clumps. The inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-lobed flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

It is a hardy perennial that thrives in temperate climates and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
