Chrysophyllum millenianum

Chrysophyllum millenianum, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or millenary cherry, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cherries, with a sweet, juicy pulp. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The plant is also valued for its timber, which is used in furniture and construction.
