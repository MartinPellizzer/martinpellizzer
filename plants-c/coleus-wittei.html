Coleus wittei

Coleus wittei, commonly known as the African violet or velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often features striking patterns of red, purple, and green. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are velvety to the touch, giving it its common name. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, blooming in clusters.

Coleus wittei thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
