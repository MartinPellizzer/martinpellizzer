Caruelia ochroleuca

Caruelia ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow-leafed broom.

This plant is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in regions of southern Africa. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its ecological role includes providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivores.
