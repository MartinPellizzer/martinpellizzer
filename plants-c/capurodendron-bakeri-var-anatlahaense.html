Capurodendron bakeri var. anatlahaense

Capurodendron bakeri var. anatlahaense, commonly known as the Anatlaha endemic.

It is a rare shrub native to the humid forests of western Madagascar. The plant features small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface and a distinctive, fragrant white flower. Its botanical significance lies in its unique adaptations to the tropical environment.

It is of particular interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential medicinal properties.
