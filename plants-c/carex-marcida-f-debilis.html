Carex marcida f. debilis

Carex marcida f. debilis, commonly known as the slender marsh sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, contributing to soil stabilization.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its ornamental appeal in water gardens.
