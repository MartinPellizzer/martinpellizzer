Cevallia sinuata

Cevallia sinuata, commonly known as the sinuous cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered in spines, which help to reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat. Its root system is adapted to absorb moisture efficiently, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky soils.

Cevallia sinuata is also known for its slow growth rate and ability to survive in extreme desert conditions.
