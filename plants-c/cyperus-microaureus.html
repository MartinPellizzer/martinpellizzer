Cyperus microaureus

Cyperus microaureus, commonly known as the golden flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in wetlands, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus microaureus is also known for its ability to tolerate a wide range of soil conditions and is sometimes used in phytoremediation efforts.
