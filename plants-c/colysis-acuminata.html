Colysis acuminata

Colysis acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-leaved colysis, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves with pointed tips. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
