Cuscuta approximata var. macranthera

Cuscuta approximata var. macranthera, commonly known as the giant dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has slender, thread-like stems that wrap around its host. The plant is native to North America and can be found growing on a variety of plants, including agricultural crops. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, animals, and human activity, making it an invasive species in some regions.

The plant's rapid growth and ability to cause significant damage to crops make it a challenging weed to manage.
