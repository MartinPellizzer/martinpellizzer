Cenia nudicaulis

Cenia nudicaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed cenia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has a slender, unbranched stem that is often leafless, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically small and yellow, arranged in a dense cluster at the top of the stem.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
