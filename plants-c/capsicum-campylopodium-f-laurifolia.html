Capsicum campylopodium f. laurifolia

Capsicum campylopodium f. laurifolia, commonly known as the laurel-leaf chili, is a botanical variant of the species Capsicum campylopodium.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate, and slightly waxy leaves that resemble those of laurel plants, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It is native to Central America and thrives in warm, humid climates.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, sweet flavor, making it suitable for both culinary and garden use.
