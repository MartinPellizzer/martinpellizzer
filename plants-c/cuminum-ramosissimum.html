Cuminum ramosissimum

Cuminum ramosissimum, commonly known as wild cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branched stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds, which are harvested for their aromatic properties, are used in various culinary and medicinal applications.

Cuminum ramosissimum thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for Mediterranean climates.
