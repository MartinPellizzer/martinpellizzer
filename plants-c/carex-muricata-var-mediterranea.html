Carex muricata var. mediterranea

Carex muricata var. mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown to beige flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is adapted to temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
