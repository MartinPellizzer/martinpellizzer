Commiphora sambesiaca

Commiphora sambesiaca, commonly known as the Sambesian myrrh, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest of South America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces a reddish-brown resin that has been traditionally used for its aromatic properties. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland and mountainous regions.

Due to its unique resin and ecological significance, it is of interest to both botanists and conservationists.
