Carica hastifolia

Carica hastifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf papaya, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a fruit similar to the common papaya, though it is less commercially cultivated. Botanically, it belongs to the Caricaceae family and is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in moist, tropical climates.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their potential health benefits.
