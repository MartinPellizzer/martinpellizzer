Cavendishia lactiviscida

Cavendishia lactiviscida, commonly known as the sticky leaf orchid, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family and is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves that produce a sticky substance. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
