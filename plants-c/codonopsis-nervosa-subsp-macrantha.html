Codonopsis nervosa subsp. macrantha

Codonopsis nervosa subsp.

macrantha, commonly known as the broad-flowered codonopsis, is a herbaceous perennial native to China. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and produces white or pale blue flowers in late summer. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, while its roots are fibrous and used in traditional medicine.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
