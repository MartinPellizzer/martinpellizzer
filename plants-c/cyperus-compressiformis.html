Cyperus compressiformis

Cyperus compressiformis, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a dense cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a compact rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and pond edges, and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Cyperus compressiformis is also known for its ability to tolerate waterlogged soil, making it a hardy and adaptable species.
