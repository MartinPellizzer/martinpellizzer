Callista tridentifera

Callista tridentifera, commonly known as the three-toothed callista, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive tridentate (three-toothed) leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or forested areas.

Callista tridentifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
