Celastrus paniculatus var. poilanei

Celastrus paniculatus var. poilanei, commonly known as the African star apple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and showy yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, aromatic flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
