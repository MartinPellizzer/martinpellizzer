Campanula macrocalyx

Campanula macrocalyx, commonly known as the large bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
