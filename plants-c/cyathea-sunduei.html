Cyathea sunduei

Cyathea sunduei, commonly known as the Sundue's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, providing structural support and storing nutrients.

Cyathea sunduei plays an important ecological role in its native forests, contributing to biodiversity and soil stability.
