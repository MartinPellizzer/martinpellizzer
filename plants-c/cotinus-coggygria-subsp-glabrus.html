Cotinus coggygria subsp. glabrus

Cotinus coggygria subsp.

glabrus, commonly known as the smooth velvet tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its striking, fuzzy, pink to red flowers that appear in late spring to early summer.

The plant has smooth, glabrous bark that becomes fissured with age, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are typically oval to lance-shaped and turn vibrant red or orange in the fall.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
