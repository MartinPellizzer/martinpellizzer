Celosia globosa

Celosia globosa, commonly known as the firecracker plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, spiky bracts that resemble small fireworks, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by the colorful, papery bracts. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Celosia globosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and long-lasting blooms.
