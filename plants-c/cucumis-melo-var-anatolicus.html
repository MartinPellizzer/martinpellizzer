Cucumis melo var. anatolicus

Cucumis melo var. anatolicus, commonly known as the Armenian cucumber, is a botanical variant of the melon genus.

It is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical shape and thin, edible skin. The plant produces yellow flowers and fruits that typically measure between 15 to 30 centimeters in length. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its crisp, mild flavor and is often cultivated for both fresh consumption and culinary use.
