Carex planifolia

Carex planifolia, commonly known as flatleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its leaves are flat and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters.

Carex planifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
