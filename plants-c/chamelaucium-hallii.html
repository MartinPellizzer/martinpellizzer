Chamelaucium hallii

Chamelaucium hallii, commonly known as the coastal tea tree, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. Chamelaucium hallii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.

Its essential oils are also utilized in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
