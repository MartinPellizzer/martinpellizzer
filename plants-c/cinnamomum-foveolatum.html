Cinnamomum foveolatum

Cinnamomum foveolatum, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum foveolatum, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces dark brown, aromatic bark that is used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and emit a strong cinnamon scent when crushed.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.
