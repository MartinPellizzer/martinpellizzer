Campanula calycina

Campanula calycina, commonly known as the bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and woodlands. The plant produces striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and hairy.

Campanula calycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ease of care.
