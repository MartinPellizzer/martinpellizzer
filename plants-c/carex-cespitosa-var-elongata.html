Carex cespitosa var. elongata

Carex cespitosa var. elongata, commonly known as the tall sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves and produces spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is often used in wetland restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
