Chrysion

Chrysion, commonly known as the goldenweed, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow or golden-colored flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plants typically have narrow, grass-like leaves and are often found in moist or wetland habitats. Chrysion species are native to various regions in Europe, Asia, and Africa.

They are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
