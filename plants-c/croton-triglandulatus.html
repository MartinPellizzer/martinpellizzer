Croton triglandulatus

Croton triglandulatus, commonly known as the three-toothed croton, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves with three lobes. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters. Its bark and leaves contain toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful if ingested.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.
