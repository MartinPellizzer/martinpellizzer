Callicarpa pentandra f. celebica

Callicarpa pentandra f. celebica, commonly known as the Java beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with slightly serrated edges and emits a subtle, sweet fragrance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The berries are edible but are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife rather than humans.
