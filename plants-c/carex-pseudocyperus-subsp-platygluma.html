Carex pseudocyperus subsp. platygluma

Carex pseudocyperus subsp.

platygluma, commonly known as the broadleaf sedges, is a species of sedge characterized by its grass-like appearance and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along water edges, where it thrives in moist to wet soil conditions.

The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in two rows, and its flowering structures consist of small, three-ranked spikelets. It is a perennial herb that plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
