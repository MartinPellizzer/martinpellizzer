Coleus dazo

Coleus dazo, commonly known as the velvetleaf coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a unique texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Coleus dazo is typically grown as a houseplant or in shaded outdoor gardens due to its sensitivity to direct sunlight.
