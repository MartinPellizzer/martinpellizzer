Cyperus haspan var. firmicaulis

Cyperus haspan var. firmicaulis, commonly known as the firm-stemmed flatsedge, is a grass-like plant native to wetland and aquatic environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It is often found in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Its structure and growth habits make it useful for stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
