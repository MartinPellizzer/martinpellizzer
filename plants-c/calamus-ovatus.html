Calamus ovatus

Calamus ovatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, elongated leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically pollinated by wind. Its rhizomes are frequently harvested for their aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Calamus ovatus thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
