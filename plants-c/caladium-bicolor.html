Caladium bicolor

Caladium bicolor, commonly known as the flameleaf elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, white, and variegated patterns. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-draining soil. It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage.

Caladium bicolor is typically propagated through tuberous roots and can be grown in containers or in garden beds during the growing season.
