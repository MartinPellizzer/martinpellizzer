Clematis tenuiflora

Clematis tenuiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Clematis tenuiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
