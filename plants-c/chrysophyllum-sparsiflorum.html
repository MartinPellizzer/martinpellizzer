Chrysophyllum sparsiflorum

Chrysophyllum sparsiflorum, commonly known as the sparseflower chrysophyllum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, edible fruits with a sweet, creamy pulp and a single seed. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruit, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
