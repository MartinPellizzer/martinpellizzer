Cassia cathartica

Cassia cathartica, commonly known as the sicklepod or Indian cassia, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Cassia cathartica is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
