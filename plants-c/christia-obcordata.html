Christia obcordata

Christia obcordata, commonly known as the heartleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant is an annual vine that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full sun. Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, summer-flowering plants.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
