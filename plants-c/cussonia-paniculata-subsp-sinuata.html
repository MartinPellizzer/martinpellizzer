Cussonia paniculata subsp. sinuata

Cussonia paniculata subsp.

sinuata, commonly known as the Sinuata arrowroot, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its tall, bamboo-like stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often prominently veined.

The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, starchy tubers that are used in traditional cuisines. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal.

The species is well adapted to humid, shaded environments and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.
