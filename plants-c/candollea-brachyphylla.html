Candollea brachyphylla

Candollea brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-stemmed orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are clustered in inflorescences with vibrant colors ranging from yellow to orange.

Candollea brachyphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare plants.
