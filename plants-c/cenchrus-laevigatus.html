Cenchrus laevigatus

Cenchrus laevigatus, commonly known as silky grass or slender sand couch, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, making it suitable for erosion control. Its leaves are typically bluish-green and have a silky texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is often used in landscaping and as a forage plant for livestock.
