Cyphopsis volubilis var. parviflora

Cyphopsis volubilis var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered cyphopsis, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value. Its leaves are finely divided and emit a mild, spicy scent when crushed.

This variant is distinguished by its smaller flower size compared to other varieties within the species.
