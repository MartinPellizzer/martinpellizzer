Calliandra abbreviata

Calliandra abbreviata, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences composed of numerous small, clustered flowers in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant has compound leaves with pairs of small, oval leaflets and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring moderate water and good air circulation.

Calliandra abbreviata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it a useful plant in agroforestry systems.
