Carex paniculata var. simplex

Carex paniculata var. simplex, commonly known as the smooth sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, flat leaves that are smooth to the touch. The plant produces slender, branching spikes of small, greenish-yellow flowers in the summer months. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of wetland conditions.
