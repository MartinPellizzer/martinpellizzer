Callisia meiandra

Callisia meiandra, commonly known as the Mexican spider plant, is a member of the Commelinaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its striking, cascading foliage with variegated leaves that display shades of green, white, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers indirect sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Callisia meiandra is also known for its ability to propagate easily through runners, which allows it to spread quickly in suitable conditions.
