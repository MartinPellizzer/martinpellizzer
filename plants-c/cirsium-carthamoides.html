Cirsium carthamoides

Cirsium carthamoides, commonly known as yellow cockle, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in dry, disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. The plant features spiny, leafy stems and produces yellow to orange composite flower heads that bloom in summer. Its leaves are typically lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

The plant is often considered a weed but has historical uses in traditional medicine.
