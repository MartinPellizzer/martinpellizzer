Coelogyne corymbosa

Coelogyne corymbosa, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its clustered pseudobulbs and long, arching stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and yellow, often with a distinctive red or orange lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Coelogyne corymbosa is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its use in traditional medicine.
