Coleus amiculatus

Coleus amiculatus, commonly known as the silver leaf coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with silver and green hues. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant leaves.

Due to its attractive appearance, it is popular in gardens and as a houseplant.
