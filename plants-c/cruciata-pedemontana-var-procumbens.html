Cruciata pedemontana var. procumbens

Cruciata pedemontana var. procumbens, commonly known as the creeping cruciata, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features prostrate stems that spread along the ground, forming a dense mat of foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
