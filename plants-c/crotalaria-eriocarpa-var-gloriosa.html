Crotalaria eriocarpa var. gloriosa

Crotalaria eriocarpa var. gloriosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf glory flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmately divided leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is an annual or biennial herb, often found in disturbed soils and agricultural fields. Its seeds are encased in a hard, ridged capsule, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

The species is also noted for its potential use in phytoremediation due to its tolerance of heavy metals.
