Cyathea toppingii

Cyathea toppingii, commonly known as the Topping's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, compound fronds. The plant has a distinctive crown of spirally arranged, leathery leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting the plant's vertical growth.

Cyathea toppingii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in shaded, humid environments with well-drained soil.
