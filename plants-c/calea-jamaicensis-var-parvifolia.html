Calea jamaicensis var. parvifolia

Calea jamaicensis var. parvifolia, commonly known as the "flower of the evening," is a herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, white, tubular flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its unique flowering behavior and ornamental value make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
