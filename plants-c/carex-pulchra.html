Carex pulchra

Carex pulchra, commonly known as the pretty sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its grass-like appearance features narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters.

Carex pulchra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
