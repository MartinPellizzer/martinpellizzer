Cyathea merrillii

Cyathea merrillii, commonly known as the Merrill's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a slow growth rate and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests. Its rhizome system supports the development of its towering structure, and it plays a role in the ecological balance of its habitat.

The fronds are used in traditional medicine in some cultures, highlighting its cultural and ecological significance.
