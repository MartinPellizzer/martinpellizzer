Crataegus mackenzii var. bracteata

Crataegus mackenzii var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features simple, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated margins and produces white or pinkish flowers in early spring. Its fruit is a small, red-pink berry that ripens in late summer.

The species is characterized by its distinctive bracteate inflorescence, which is a key feature in its botanical identification.
