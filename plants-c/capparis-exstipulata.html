Capparis exstipulata

Capparis exstipulata, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis peruviana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow, fleshy fruit that is encased in a papery calyx. The plant produces small, white flowers with five petals and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have a rough texture.

Capparis exstipulata is valued for its edible fruit, which is used in various culinary applications.
