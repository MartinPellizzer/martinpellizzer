Cylindropuntia × congesta

Cylindropuntia × congesta, commonly known as the Fishhook cactus, is a hybrid species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are often covered in dense clusters of spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its spiny structure. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sunlight.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in desert gardens.
