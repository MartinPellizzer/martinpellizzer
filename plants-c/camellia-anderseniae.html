Camellia anderseniae

Camellia anderseniae, commonly known as the Andes camellia, is a rare and ornamental evergreen shrub native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a contrasting central stripe. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking backdrop to its blooms. It thrives in cool, humid climates with well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for botanical gardens and specialized horticulture.

Due to its limited natural range and habitat specificity, Camellia anderseniae is considered a valuable species for conservation efforts.
