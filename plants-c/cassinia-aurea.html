Cassinia aurea

Cassinia aurea, commonly known as the golden cassinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a silvery-green hue, and the flowers are arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

Cassinia aurea is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ecological role in supporting native pollinators.
