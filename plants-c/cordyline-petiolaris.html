Cordyline petiolaris

Cordyline petiolaris, commonly known as the white cordyline, is a striking evergreen plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features tall, slender stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Cordyline petiolaris is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
