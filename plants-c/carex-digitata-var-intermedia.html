Carex digitata var. intermedia

Carex digitata var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown, depending on the stage of development. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
