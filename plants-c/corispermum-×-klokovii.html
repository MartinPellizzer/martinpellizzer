Corispermum × klokovii

Corispermum × klokovii, commonly known as the hybrid bladderwort, is a rare and intriguing plant species belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its small, succulent, bladder-like structures that aid in water retention, a feature typical of many halophytic plants. This hybrid is often found in saline or brackish environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are small and yellow, typically arranged in clusters, and it exhibits a short growth season.

Due to its limited distribution and unique ecological niche, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical and ecological studies.
