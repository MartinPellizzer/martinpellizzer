Convallaria hirta

Convallaria hirta, commonly known as hairy lily of the valley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Convallariaceae and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant has slender, arching stems and produces dark blue berries that contain toxic seeds. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its foliage consists of broad, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it its common name.
