Centaurea cuspidata

Centaurea cuspidata, commonly known as the needlehead centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spiny leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats like grasslands and coastal areas.
