Crataegus darlingtoniana

Crataegus darlingtoniana, commonly known as the Darlington hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
