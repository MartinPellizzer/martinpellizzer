Cirsium straminispinum

Cirsium straminispinum, commonly known as the slender thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lance-shaped leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

The species is often found in disturbed areas and is considered a common weed in some regions.
