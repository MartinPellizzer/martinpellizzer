Carduus lusitanicus subsp. broteroi

Carduus lusitanicus subsp.

broteroi, commonly known as the Portuguese thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

The plant features a robust stem with spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers in late summer. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinct, serrated edge.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
