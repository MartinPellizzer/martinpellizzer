Caladenia carnea var. fuscata

Caladenia carnea var. fuscata, commonly known as the dusky spider-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid endemic to southern Australia.

It features a single, slender stem with a single flower that is typically pink to purple in color, with a distinctive, intricate pattern on the labellum. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single, showy orchid flower during the spring season. It is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

This variety is part of the Caladenia genus, known for its specialized pollination mechanisms involving specific insect species.
