Calamagrostis viridiflavescens var. montevidensis

Calamagrostis viridiflavescens var. montevidensis, commonly known as the Montevidense green-yellow reed, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and fine, lance-shaped leaves that exhibit a distinctive greenish-yellow hue. This variety thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with temperate climates.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that add visual interest during the growing season.
