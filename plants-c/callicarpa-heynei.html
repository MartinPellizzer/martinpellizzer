Callicarpa heynei

Callicarpa heynei, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various birds and small mammals.
