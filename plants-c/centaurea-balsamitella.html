Centaurea balsamitella

Centaurea balsamitella, commonly known as the balsam thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy leaves and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

The plant's resilience to harsh conditions makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
