Clerodendrum hexangulatum

Clerodendrum hexangulatum, commonly known as the six-angled clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs.
