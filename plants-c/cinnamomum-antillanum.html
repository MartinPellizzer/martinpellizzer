Cinnamomum antillanum

Cinnamomum antillanum, commonly known as the Cuban cinnamon or cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its aromatic bark, which contains essential oils and is used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries containing one or two seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
