Crataegus oxyacantha f. alboplena

Crataegus oxyacantha f. alboplena, commonly known as the white-flowered hawthorn, is a cultivar of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its pale white to cream-colored flowers, which contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its spring blossoms and autumn fruit.
