Campylocentrum calostachyum

Campylocentrum calostachyum, commonly known as the golden bell plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a velvety texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
