Cardamine nuttallii var. gemmata

Cardamine nuttallii var. gemmata, commonly known as Nuttall's buttercup variant gemmata, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is often described as being arranged in a basal rosette with trifoliate leaves.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-rich soils and its role in supporting local insect populations.
