Carex flacca f. castriferrei

Carex flacca f. castriferrei, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a variant of the common sedge, characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit.

It is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, typically found in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that bears small, three-lobed flowers, which are usually wind-pollinated. Its rhizomatous growth allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies that stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
