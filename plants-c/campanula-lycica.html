Campanula lycica

Campanula lycica, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula lycica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
