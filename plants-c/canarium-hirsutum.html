Canarium hirsutum

Canarium hirsutum, commonly known as the hairy candlenut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Sapotaceae and is characterized by its rough, hairy bark and broad, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, spiny capsules containing oily seeds. These seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood and its role in local ecosystems.
