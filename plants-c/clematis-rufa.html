Clematis rufa

Clematis rufa, commonly known as the red clematis, is a deciduous vine native to Europe and western Asia.

It features climbing stems with hooked tendrils that allow it to ascend supports. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five oval leaflets, and are often hairy beneath.

Clematis rufa thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
