Crataegus pallescens

Crataegus pallescens, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is commonly found in woodland edges and open woods.

Crataegus pallescens plays a role in supporting local wildlife, including birds and insects.
