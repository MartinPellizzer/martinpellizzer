Conyza umbrosa

Conyza umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow fleabane, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a distinct serrated edge, while its flowers are small and yellowish-white.

Conyza umbrosa is often found in regions with moderate to high rainfall and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
