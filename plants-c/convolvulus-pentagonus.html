Convolvulus pentagonus

Convolvulus pentagonus, commonly known as the five-angled convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-angled stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Convolvulus pentagonus is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
