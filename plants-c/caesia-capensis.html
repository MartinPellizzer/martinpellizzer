Caesia capensis

Caesia capensis, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Caesia capensis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
