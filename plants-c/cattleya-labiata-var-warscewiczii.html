Cattleya labiata var. warscewiczii

Cattleya labiata var. warscewiczii, commonly known as the Warscewicz's Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, and showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant produces pseudobulbs and has long, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a fan shape. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and ease of cultivation.
