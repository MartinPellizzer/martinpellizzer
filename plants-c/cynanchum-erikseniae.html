Cynanchum erikseniae

Cynanchum erikseniae, commonly known as the Eriksen's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its milky sap is a distinguishing feature. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
