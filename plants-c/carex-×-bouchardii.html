Carex × bouchardii

Carex × bouchardii, commonly known as the Bouchard's sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It has narrow, linear leaves that are usually dark green in color.

Carex × bouchardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of wetland environments.
