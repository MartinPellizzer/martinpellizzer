Clusia flava var. brownei

Clusia flava var. brownei, commonly known as the golden glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a bright yellow-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

The plant is also known for its air-purifying properties and resistance to pests and diseases.
