Carex divisa var. longiculmis

Carex divisa var. longiculmis, commonly known as the divided sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, grass-green leaves that are often divided into segments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
