Centaurea paniculata var. cossoniana

Centaurea paniculata var. cossoniana, commonly known as the Cosson's knapweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features erect, branching stems that are hairy and often reddish in color. The plant produces showy, pink to purple composite flowers arranged in panicle-like inflorescences, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often found in dry grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in full sun conditions.
