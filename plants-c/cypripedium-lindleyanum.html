Cypripedium lindleyanum

Cypripedium lindleyanum, commonly known as the Lindley's lady's slipper, is a striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that resemble a slipper, with a distinctive pouch-like structure at the base. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forests and thrives in cool, humid climates. Its leaves are broad and leathery, and it produces a single, fragrant bloom each year.

Cypripedium lindleyanum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also of interest to botanists due to its unique floral morphology and ecological adaptations.
