Cirsium foliosum

Cirsium foliosum, commonly known as the leafy thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a rough, serrated texture.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
