Campomanesia guazumifolia

Campomanesia guazumifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It produces small, orange-yellow fruits that are edible and have a sweet, honey-like flavor. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and culinary uses in traditional Brazilian cuisine.
