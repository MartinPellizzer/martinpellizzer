Cyclophyllum cymosum

Cyclophyllum cymosum, commonly known as the clustered cyclophyllum, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive growth pattern.

It features a compact, bushy structure with tightly clustered leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually broad and have a waxy texture, which helps in reducing water loss.

The botanical significance of Cyclophyllum cymosum lies in its unique morphology and potential ecological role in its native habitats.
