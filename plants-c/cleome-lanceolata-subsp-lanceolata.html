Cleome lanceolata subsp. lanceolata

Cleome lanceolata subsp.

lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf cleome, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southern United States. It features alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers with five petals. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in fields.

It is a member of the Cleomaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
