Carex exserta

Carex exserta, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-parted flowers, which are wind-pollinated.
