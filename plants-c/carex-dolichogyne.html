Carex dolichogyne

Carex dolichogyne, commonly known as the long-beaked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green to brown in color, and it thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and swamps.

Carex dolichogyne is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
