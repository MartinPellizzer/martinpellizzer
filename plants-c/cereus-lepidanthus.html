Cereus lepidanthus

Cereus lepidanthus, commonly known as the silver crown cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a tall, columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance characterized by its white, woolly spines and a crown of small, white flowers. The plant thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it well-suited for desert environments. Its flowers, which bloom at night, are pollinated by bats and moths, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant's structure and adaptations reflect its evolutionary response to harsh, dry conditions.
