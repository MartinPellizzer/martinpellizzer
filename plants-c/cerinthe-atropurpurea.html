Cerinthe atropurpurea

Cerinthe atropurpurea, commonly known as purple toadflax, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features striking violet-blue flowers with distinctive spurred petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often hairy to the touch. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
