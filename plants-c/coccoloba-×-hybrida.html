Coccoloba × hybrida

Coccoloba × hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are a key feature of its ornamental value. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a glossy texture and a tendency to turn red in the fall.

Coccoloba × hybrida is well-suited for tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
