Crataegus invicta

Crataegus invicta, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It features dense, thorny branches and produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and red berries that ripen in the fall. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Crataegus invicta is relatively drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.
