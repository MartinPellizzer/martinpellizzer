Cattleya vasconcelosiana

Cattleya vasconcelosiana, commonly known as the Veludo or Velvet Cattleya, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, velvety-textured flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow with dark red or purple markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs are robust, and it produces multiple racemes of fragrant blooms during the flowering season.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
