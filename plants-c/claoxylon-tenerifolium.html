Claoxylon tenerifolium

Claoxylon tenerifolium, commonly known as the Canary Island mahogany, is a tropical tree native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, aromatic bark. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical in shape. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
