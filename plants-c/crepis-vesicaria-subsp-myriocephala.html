Crepis vesicaria subsp. myriocephala

Crepis vesicaria subsp.

myriocephala, commonly known as the broad-headed mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a single terminal head.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in disturbed or marginal areas.

The subspecies is distinguished by its broad, rounded flower heads compared to other crepis species.
