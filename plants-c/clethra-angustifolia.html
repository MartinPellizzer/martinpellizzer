Clethra angustifolia

Clethra angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically 2-4 inches long and have a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale pink flowers in cylindrical clusters during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy blooms and attractive autumn foliage.
