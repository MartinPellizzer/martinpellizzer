Cinnamomum perrottetii var. nilgiriense

Cinnamomum perrottetii var. nilgiriense, commonly known as the Nilgiri cinnamon, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical regions.
