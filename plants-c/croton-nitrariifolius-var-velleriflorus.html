Croton nitrariifolius var. velleriflorus

Croton nitrariifolius var. velleriflorus, commonly known as the velvetflower croton, is a tropical shrub characterized by its striking, velvety-textured flowers.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and floral display.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types, making it a versatile choice for horticultural use.
