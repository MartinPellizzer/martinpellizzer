Clethra johnstonii

Clethra johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are opposite in arrangement and have a serrated margin. Its fruit is a dry, capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Clethra johnstonii is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
