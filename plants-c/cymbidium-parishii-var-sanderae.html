Cymbidium parishii var. sanderae

Cymbidium parishii var. sanderae, commonly known as the Sanderae Cymbidium, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its tall, erect inflorescence bearing clusters of large, showy flowers with vibrant pink and white hues. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are elongated and leathery, providing a striking contrast to the delicate blooms.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique floral structure.
