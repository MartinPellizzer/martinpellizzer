Chromolaena rojasii

Chromolaena rojasii, commonly known as the Mexican fireweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and a strong aromatic scent.
