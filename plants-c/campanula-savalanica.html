Campanula savalanica

Campanula savalanica, commonly known as the Savalan bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Campanula savalanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, showy blooms.
