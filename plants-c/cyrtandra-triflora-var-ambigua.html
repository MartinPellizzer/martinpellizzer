Cyrtandra triflora var. ambigua

Cyrtandra triflora var. ambigua, commonly known as the ambiguous three-flowered cyrtandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in clusters. Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
