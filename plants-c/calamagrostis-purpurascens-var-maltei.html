Calamagrostis purpurascens var. maltei

Calamagrostis purpurascens var. maltei, commonly known as the Maltei false panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive purple-tinted foliage, which adds ornamental value to gardens. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, making it suitable for wetland or bog environments. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish-brown flowers.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
