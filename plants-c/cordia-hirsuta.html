Cordia hirsuta

Cordia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces fleshy, orange-red berries that are often eaten by birds and other animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

Cordia hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
