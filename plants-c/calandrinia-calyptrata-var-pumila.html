Calandrinia calyptrata var. pumila

Calandrinia calyptrata var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf pink, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing annual species characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant nature.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
