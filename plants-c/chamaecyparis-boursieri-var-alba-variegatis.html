Chamaecyparis boursieri var. alba-variegatis

Chamaecyparis boursieri var. alba-variegatis, commonly known as the Variegated Hinoki cypress, is a rare and ornamental conifer native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage, featuring white and green streaks on its needle-like leaves. The plant has a compact, pyramidal shape with a slow growth rate, making it suitable for small garden spaces. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, and is often used in rock gardens or as a specimen plant.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued by horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
