Campanula rotundifolia var. stenophylla

Campanula rotundifolia var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features rounded, broad leaves that are often covered in a fine downy layer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
