Carex viridula subsp. oedocarpa

Carex viridula subsp.

oedocarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its flowers are usually small and greenish, with three stamens and a single pistil.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
