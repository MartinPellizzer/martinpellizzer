Centaurea scabiosa f. dumetorum

Centaurea scabiosa f. dumetorum, commonly known as the sticky centaury, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny bracts and small, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in dry, rocky soils. It has hairy leaves and stems, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often found in meadows and along roadsides, where it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
