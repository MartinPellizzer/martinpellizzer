Ceratostylis latifolia

Ceratostylis latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved corydalis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its slender, erect stem and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that are often borne on short pedicels. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental value make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
