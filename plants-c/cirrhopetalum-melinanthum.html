Cirrhopetalum melinanthum

Cirrhopetalum melinanthum, commonly known as the silky petal orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its unique, elongated petals that resemble streamers. It thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
