Carex × germanica

Carex × germanica, commonly known as the German sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces dense clusters of spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands.

Carex × germanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
