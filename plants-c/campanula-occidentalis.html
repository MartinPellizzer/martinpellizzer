Campanula occidentalis

Campanula occidentalis, commonly known as the western bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces showy blue to violet bell-shaped flowers in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Campanula occidentalis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
