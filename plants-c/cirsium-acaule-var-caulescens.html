Cirsium acaule var. caulescens

Cirsium acaule var. caulescens, commonly known as the tall thistle, is a variant of the genus Cirsium characterized by its upright growth habit and spiny leaves.

It is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in meadows and grasslands. The plant produces large, showy purple or pink flower heads that are surrounded by bracts. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture and prominent spines.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
