Cardamine castellana

Cardamine castellana, commonly known as Castilian watercress, is a small perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three small, oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces tiny white or greenish flowers in early spring.

The plant is valued for its peppery flavor and has been used in traditional cuisine and herbal remedies.
