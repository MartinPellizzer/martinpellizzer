Cyperus Diaphanus Subsp. Setiformis

Cyperus diaphanus subsp. setiformis is a unique member of the sedge family known for its striking, feathery appearance. This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in wetland environments. Its slender, hair-like leaves give it a delicate and elegant look, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. It is often used in water features and bog gardens due to its adaptability to moist soil conditions. Despite its beauty, it remains a relatively lesser-known species within the Cyperus genus.
