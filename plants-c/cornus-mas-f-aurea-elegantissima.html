Cornus mas f. aurea-elegantissima

Cornus mas f. aurea-elegantissima, commonly known as the golden dogwood, is a striking variation of the cornelian cherry.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow foliage, which displays a brilliant hue in both spring and autumn. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, preceding its leaves, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

This cultivar is prized for its seasonal color changes and ability to add visual interest throughout the year.
