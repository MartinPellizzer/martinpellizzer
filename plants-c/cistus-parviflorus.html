Cistus parviflorus

Cistus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, evergreen leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant.

Cistus parviflorus is often used in xeriscaping due to its hardy nature and ornamental value.
