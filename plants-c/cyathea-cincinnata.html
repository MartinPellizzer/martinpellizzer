Cyathea cincinnata

Cyathea cincinnata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a robust root system that helps it anchor in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically dark green and can grow up to several meters in length.

Cyathea cincinnata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates.
