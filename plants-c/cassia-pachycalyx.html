Cassia pachycalyx

Cassia pachycalyx, commonly known as the flame tree, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking, bright orange to red inflorescences that appear in clusters, making it a visually striking ornamental plant. The leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets that give the plant a feathery appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
