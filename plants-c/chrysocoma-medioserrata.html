Chrysocoma medioserrata

Chrysocoma medioserrata, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow to greenish in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

The plant is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested.
