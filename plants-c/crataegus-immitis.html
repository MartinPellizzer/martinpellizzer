Crataegus immitis

Crataegus immitis, commonly known as the canyon thimbleberry, is a species of hawthorn native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as streamside areas and forest understories. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for wildlife. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn bright red in the fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
