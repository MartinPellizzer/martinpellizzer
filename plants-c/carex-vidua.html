Carex vidua

Carex vidua, commonly known as slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a single pistil.

Carex vidua plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
