Carex microptera

Carex microptera, commonly known as the dwarf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and forest floors. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces tiny flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex microptera is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting wildlife.
