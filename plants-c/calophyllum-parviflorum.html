Calophyllum parviflorum

Calophyllum parviflorum, commonly known as the small-flowered calophyllum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces oval, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
