Calamus sekundurensis

Calamus sekundurensis, commonly known as the Bornean calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the tropical regions of Borneo and Sumatra. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, yellowish flowers, which are typically borne at the base of the plant.

Calamus sekundurensis is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appearance in horticulture.
