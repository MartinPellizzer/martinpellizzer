Carex rigida subsp. intricata

Carex rigida subsp.

intricata, commonly known as the intricate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are often borne on slender, upright stems.

The subspecies intricata is distinguished by its complex, branching inflorescence and finely textured foliage.
