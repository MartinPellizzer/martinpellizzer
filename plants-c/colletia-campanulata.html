Colletia campanulata

Colletia campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spiny areoles and its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its succulent structure. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Colletia campanulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
