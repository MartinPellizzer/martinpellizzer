Chamaecrista niqueroensis

Chamaecrista niqueroensis, commonly known as the Niquero burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are bipinnate, and the flowers are clustered in racemes, often displaying shades of yellow or orange.

The species is valued for its ecological role in soil enrichment and as a forage plant for livestock.
