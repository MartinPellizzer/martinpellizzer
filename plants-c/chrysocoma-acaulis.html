Chrysocoma acaulis

Chrysocoma acaulis, commonly known as the rock fig, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, succulent stems and dense, evergreen foliage that helps it retain moisture in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by edible, fig-like fruits. Its ability to thrive in rocky soils makes it well adapted to dry, nutrient-poor habitats.

Chrysocoma acaulis plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
