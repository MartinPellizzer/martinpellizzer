Corydalis fabacea var. integrata

Corydalis fabacea var. integrata, commonly known as the pink corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pink to lavender-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10 to 15 inches and features slender, hairy stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early-blooming flowers.
