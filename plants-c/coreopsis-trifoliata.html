Coreopsis trifoliata

Coreopsis trifoliata, commonly known as the fragrant tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring hairy stems and alternate leaves with three leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive black center, blooming from late spring to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.

Coreopsis trifoliata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
