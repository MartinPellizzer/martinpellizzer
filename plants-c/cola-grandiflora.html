Cola grandiflora

Cola grandiflora, commonly known as the African cola tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces berries that contain stimulant compounds, primarily caffeine and theobromine, which are harvested for commercial use. It thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Cola grandiflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in many African communities.
