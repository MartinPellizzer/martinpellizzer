Chirita wangiana

Chirita wangiana, commonly known as the Wangian chirita, is a species of flowering plant in the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its trailing habit, heart-shaped leaves, and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a houseplant in some parts of the world.
