Cypripedium × barbeyi

Cypripedium × barbeyi, commonly known as the hybrid lady's slipper orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cypripedium calceolus and Cypripedium pubescens.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a distinctive, showy flower that resembles a slipper, typically pink or white in color. The plant features a single, erect stem that bears a single, large, pouch-shaped flower with intricate patterns. It is native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe, where it thrives in cool, moist habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and protected areas.
