Cassebeera atropurpurea var. minima

Cassebeera atropurpurea var. minima, commonly known as the dwarf purple cassava, is a compact shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features striking purple foliage with a velvety texture, and its leaves are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and home landscapes.
