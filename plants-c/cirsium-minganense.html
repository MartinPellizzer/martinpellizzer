Cirsium minganense

Cirsium minganense, commonly known as Mingan thistle, is a perennial plant native to the boreal forests of eastern Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forested areas near lakes and rivers. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in a cylindrical shape.

Cirsium minganense plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various insects and small animals.
