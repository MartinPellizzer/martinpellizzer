Craspedorhachis perrieri

Craspedorhachis perrieri, commonly known as the Perrier's grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is a small, tufted grass that typically grows in wet or marshy environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in swampy areas.

Due to its habitat preferences, it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.
