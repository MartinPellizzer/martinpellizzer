Cyphomandra elliptica

Cyphomandra elliptica, commonly known as the golden apple guava, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and clusters of small, yellow-orange fruits that resemble apples. The plant produces edible fruits with a sweet, acidic flavor and a firm, yellow flesh. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and agricultural settings.
