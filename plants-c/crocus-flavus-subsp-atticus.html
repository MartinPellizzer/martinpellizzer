Crocus flavus subsp. atticus

Crocus flavus subsp.

atticus, commonly known as the Attic crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky soils and open woodlands, preferring well-drained, sunny to partially shaded environments.

The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during late winter to early spring. Its bulbs are adapted for storing nutrients, allowing it to survive harsh seasonal conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in ancient Greek culture.
