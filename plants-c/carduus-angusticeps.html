Carduus angusticeps

Carduus angusticeps, commonly known as narrowleaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in a dense, spherical head.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
