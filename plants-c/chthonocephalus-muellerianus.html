Chthonocephalus muellerianus

Chthonocephalus muellerianus, commonly known as the Mueller's ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the rainforests of northeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, pale green to yellowish inflorescence and its lack of visible leaves, relying instead on its roots for nutrient absorption. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on the trunks of rainforest trees. Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is highly sensitive to environmental changes.

Conservation efforts are crucial to protect this enigmatic and botanically significant species.
