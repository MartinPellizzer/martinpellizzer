Cirsium eriophorum var. polonicum

Cirsium eriophorum var. polonicum, commonly known as the Polish thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and grasslands. It is often found in open habitats with good drainage.

Its presence is an indicator of nutrient-rich, well-drained soils in temperate climates.
