Calpidia macrophylla

Calpidia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved calpidia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its large, glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Calpidia macrophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
