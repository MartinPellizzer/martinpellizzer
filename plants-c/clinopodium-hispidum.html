Clinopodium hispidum

Clinopodium hispidum, commonly known as rough hedgenettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers with purple or pink petals. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Clinopodium hispidum is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
