Castanopsis catappifolia

Castanopsis catappifolia, commonly known as the Philippine chestnut, is a deciduous evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and beeches, and is characterized by its hard, woody fruit capsules. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading crown and lanceolate leaves that are glossy green on top and pale underneath.

It produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which are an important food source for wildlife and have been traditionally used by indigenous communities.
