Carthamus boissieri

Carthamus boissieri, commonly known as the Persian saffron crocus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble those of the saffron crocus. The plant produces small, spiky seed heads that contain numerous tiny, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rocky or arid landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
