Crotalaria schliebenii

Crotalaria schliebenii, commonly known as Schlieben's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Crotalaria schliebenii is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural practices.

However, it can be toxic to livestock if ingested in large quantities.
