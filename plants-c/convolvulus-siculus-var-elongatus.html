Convolvulus siculus var. elongatus

Convolvulus siculus var. elongatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, winding stems that often climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a hairy texture, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variant is often found in disturbed soils and can be considered a weed in agricultural settings.
