Curcuma ecomata

Curcuma ecomata, commonly known as the black turmeric, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its dark purple to black rhizomes, which are rich in curcuminoids and other bioactive compounds. Its leaves are large, broad, and lance-shaped, often reaching up to 1 meter in length.

The flowers are typically yellow or orange, emerging from a spike that rises above the foliage.
