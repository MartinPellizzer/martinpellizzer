Clematis occidentalis var. occidentalis

Clematis occidentalis var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and its flowers are typically blue or violet, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can climb trellises or fences with its long, flexible stems.
