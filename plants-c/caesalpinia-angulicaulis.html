Caesalpinia angulicaulis

Caesalpinia angulicaulis, commonly known as the angular crown flower, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive angular, branching structure. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical inflorescences, which are often visited by birds and insects. Its leaves are bipinnate, with small, feather-like leaflets, and it has a woody, thorny stem.

Caesalpinia angulicaulis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
