Chrysocoma dinteri

Chrysocoma dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's chrysocoma, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are adapted to store water, enabling it to survive in drought conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It is often found growing on rocky outcrops and in sandy soils, thriving in full sun.

Due to its unique appearance and resilience, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping in dry climates.
