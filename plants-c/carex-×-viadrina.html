Carex × viadrina

Carex × viadrina, commonly known as the marsh sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and spikelets arranged in clusters. Native to wetlands and marshy areas, it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
