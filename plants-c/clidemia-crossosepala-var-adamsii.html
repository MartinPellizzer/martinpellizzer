Clidemia crossosepala var. adamsii

Clidemia crossosepala var. adamsii, commonly known as Adams' stinging nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, heart-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a milky sap that can cause skin irritation, similar to other members of the Urticaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands.
