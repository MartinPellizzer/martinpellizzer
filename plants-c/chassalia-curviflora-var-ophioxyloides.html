Chassalia curviflora var. ophioxyloides

Chassalia curviflora var. ophioxyloides, commonly known as the golden chassalia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is native to southern Africa and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
