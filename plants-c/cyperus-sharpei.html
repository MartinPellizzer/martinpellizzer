Cyperus sharpei

Cyperus sharpei, commonly known as the spiked flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as along riverbanks or in swampy areas.

Cyperus sharpei is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
