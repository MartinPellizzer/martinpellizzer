Carex heterosperma

Carex heterosperma, commonly known as the variable sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in the summer, typically from June to August. Its seeds are variable in size and shape, contributing to its common name.

Carex heterosperma thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
