Convallaria pubescens

Convallaria pubescens, commonly known as the hairy lily of the valley, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Convallariaceae and is characterized by its slender, arching stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant produces red berries that contain toxic seeds, making it important to handle with care. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and rocky areas.
