Cynanchica oetaea

Cynanchica oetaea, commonly known as the Oetaea milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus Cynanchica. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, often growing at high altitudes.

Its seeds are adapted for dispersal by wind, contributing to its distribution across rugged terrains.
