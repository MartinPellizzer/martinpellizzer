Crataegus × heterodonta

Crataegus × heterodonta, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for hedgerows and ornamental gardens.
