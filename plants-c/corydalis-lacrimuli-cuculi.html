Corydalis lacrimuli-cuculi

Corydalis lacrimuli-cuculi, commonly known as the weeping cuckoo flower, is a member of the Fumariaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that resemble tiny cuckoo birds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight pubescence, while its roots are tuberous and used in traditional medicine.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a subtle beauty to its natural habitat.
