Centaurea serratula

Centaurea serratula, commonly known as the saw-toothed centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, serrated leaves that give it its common name, and it produces bristly, spiny seed heads. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands.

Centaurea serratula is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
