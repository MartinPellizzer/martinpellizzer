Cirsium tracyi

Cirsium tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiny leaves and showy purple or pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
