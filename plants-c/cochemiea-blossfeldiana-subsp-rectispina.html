Cochemiea blossfeldiana subsp. rectispina

Cochemiea blossfeldiana subsp.

rectispina, commonly known as the Mexican snowball cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and clusters of white, ball-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant has thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in dry conditions. Its leaves are typically blue-gray in color, providing a striking contrast to its white blooms.

It is well-suited for rock gardens or as a container plant due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
