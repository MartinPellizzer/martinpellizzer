Ceanothus cuneatus var. submontanus

Ceanothus cuneatus var. submontanus, commonly known as the Sierra ceanothus, is a flowering shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils in open woodland and chaparral habitats. It is adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its foliage and flowers provide nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
