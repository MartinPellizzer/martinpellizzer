Convolvulus gemellus

Convolvulus gemellus, commonly known as the lesser bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is a trailing or climbing herb that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pale purple or white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its rapid growth, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
