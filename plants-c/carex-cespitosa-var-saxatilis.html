Carex cespitosa var. saxatilis

Carex cespitosa var. saxatilis, commonly known as the rock sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and is often found growing in rocky or gravelly soils. This variety has compact, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to harsh environments and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

The plant is valued in landscaping for its ability to stabilize soil and its ornamental appeal.
