Crepis hirsuta

Crepis hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It produces yellow ray florets surrounded by a series of disk florets.

Crepis hirsuta typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it is often found in temperate regions.
