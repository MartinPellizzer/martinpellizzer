Capsella bursa-pastoris obliqua

Capsella bursa-pastoris obliqua, commonly known as the narrow-leaf shepherd's purse, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are usually white or pale purple.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
