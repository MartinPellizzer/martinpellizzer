Centaurea × donatiana

Centaurea × donatiana, commonly known as the hybrid blueweed, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are composed of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and often branched.

Centaurea × donatiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
