Crataegus tomentosa var. structilis

Crataegus tomentosa var. structilis, commonly known as the woolly crabapple.

This plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States. It features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red or yellow berries in the fall. The leaves are typically oval with a fuzzy, grayish underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open woods.
