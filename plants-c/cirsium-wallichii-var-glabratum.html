Cirsium wallichii var. glabratum

Cirsium wallichii var. glabratum, commonly known as the smooth thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually hairless, giving it the "glabratum" designation, which means smooth.

This variant is often found at high altitudes and is adapted to cold, harsh climates.
