Claoxylon papuae

Claoxylon papuae, commonly known as the Papuan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the highland regions of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has a straight trunk and can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad canopy of dark green leaves. Its flowers are small and yellowish, appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Claoxylon papuae plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
