Catapodium siculum

Catapodium siculum, commonly known as the Sicilian catapodium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale green to brown flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slight pubescence.

Catapodium siculum is typically found in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in coastal and semi-arid environments.
