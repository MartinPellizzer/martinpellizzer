Cucumis sativus var. falcatus

Cucumis sativus var. falcatus, commonly known as the horned cucumber, is a unique variant of the common cucumber.

It is characterized by its elongated, curved shape and spiky, warty surface, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested when they are still green. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes as well as for its unique flavor and texture in culinary applications.
