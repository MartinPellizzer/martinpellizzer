Crambe tataria var. buschii

Crambe tataria var. buschii, commonly known as Siberian mustard, is a herbaceous plant native to eastern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds, which are used in various traditional and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and agricultural studies.
