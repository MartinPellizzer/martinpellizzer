Carthamus hispanicus subsp. hispanicus

Carthamus hispanicus subsp.

hispanicus, commonly known as Spanish safflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with prominent central disks.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments and has hairy, linear leaves. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dye production.
