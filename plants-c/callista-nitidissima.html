Callista nitidissima

Callista nitidissima, commonly known as the glossy bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Callista nitidissima is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
