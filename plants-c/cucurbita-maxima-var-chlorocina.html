Cucurbita maxima var. chlorocina

Cucurbita maxima var. chlorocina, commonly known as the warty pumpkin, is a variant of the giant pumpkin species characterized by its distinctive warty skin.

This plant belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to North America. It produces large, typically orange, elongated fruits that can weigh several kilograms. The leaves are broad and lobed, with a rough texture, and the vines can spread extensively.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and in some cases for its edible seeds and flesh.
