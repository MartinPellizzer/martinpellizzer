Campomanesia cavalcantina

Campomanesia cavalcantina, commonly known as the Brazilian persimmon, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, orange-red berries that are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, honey-like flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its potential in ecological restoration due to its adaptability and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
