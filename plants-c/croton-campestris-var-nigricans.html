Croton campestris var. nigricans

Croton campestris var. nigricans, commonly known as black croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking dark green to blackish leaves, which are often glossy and have a unique, almost metallic sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and landscapes.
