Chaptalia malcabalensis

Chaptalia malcabalensis, commonly known as the Malcabal mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers bloom in spring, producing small yellow to orange blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
