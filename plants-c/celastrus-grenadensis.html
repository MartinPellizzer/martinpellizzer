Celastrus grenadensis

Celastrus grenadensis, commonly known as the Grenada staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright orange berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
