Cereus variabilis var. laetevirens

Cereus variabilis var. laetevirens, commonly known as the bright green cereus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are often a vibrant green color, especially when exposed to adequate sunlight. The plant produces showy, white flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water.

Its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming make it a popular choice for collectors and cactus enthusiasts.
