Clinopodium nepeta var. glandulosum

Clinopodium nepeta var. glandulosum, commonly known as woolly mint or catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features square, hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves with a fragrant, minty aroma. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically lavender to purple in color, arranged in whorled clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for herbal teas and as a natural pest repellent.
