Cynanchica abbreviata

Cynanchica abbreviata, commonly known as the short-stemmed milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with limited water availability.

Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.
