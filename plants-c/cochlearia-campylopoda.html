Cochlearia campylopoda

Cochlearia campylopoda, commonly known as the narrowleaf scurvygrass, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, rounded leaves and produces yellow flowers in clusters. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often found in the British Isles and along the Atlantic coast.

Cochlearia campylopoda is adapted to salt-laden winds and is often found in maritime climates.
