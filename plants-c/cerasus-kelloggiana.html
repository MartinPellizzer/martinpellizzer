Cerasus kelloggiana

Cerasus kelloggiana, commonly known as the Kellogg cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark red fruits that are typically edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use.

It is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
