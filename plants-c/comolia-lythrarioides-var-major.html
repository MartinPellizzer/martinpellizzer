Comolia lythrarioides var. major

Comolia lythrarioides var. major, commonly known as the purpleleaf gooseberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark purple foliage that turns reddish in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant fall color and compact growth habit.
