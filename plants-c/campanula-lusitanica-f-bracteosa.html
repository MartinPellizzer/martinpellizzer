Campanula lusitanica f. bracteosa

Campanula lusitanica f. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteate bellflower, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or limestone soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

This variety is distinguished by its prominent bracts, which are modified leaves that surround the flower cluster.
