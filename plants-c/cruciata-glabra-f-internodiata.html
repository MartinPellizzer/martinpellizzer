Cruciata glabra f. internodiata

Cruciata glabra f. internodiata, commonly known as the smooth cruciata, is a variant of the cruciata plant characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is native to certain regions of South America. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous annual with upright, branched stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, typical of the genus.
