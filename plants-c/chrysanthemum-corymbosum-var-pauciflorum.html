Chrysanthemum corymbosum var. pauciflorum

Chrysanthemum corymbosum var. pauciflorum, commonly known as the New England aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the fall, typically in shades of purple, pink, and white. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.

Its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to ecological and ornamental plantings.
