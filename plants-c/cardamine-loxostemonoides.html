Cardamine loxostemonoides

Cardamine loxostemonoides, commonly known as the slender water crowfoot, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils along stream banks and wet meadows. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.
