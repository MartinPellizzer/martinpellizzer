Croton glandulosus var. schomburgkianus

Croton glandulosus var. schomburgkianus, commonly known as the Schomburgk's croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy leaves with a broad, ovate shape and prominent veins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Croton family, Euphorbiaceae.
