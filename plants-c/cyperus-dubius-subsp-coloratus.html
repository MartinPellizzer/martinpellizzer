Cyperus dubius subsp. coloratus

Cyperus dubius subsp.

coloratus, commonly known as the purple flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia. It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and dense, grass-like foliage.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its adaptability makes it a common species in both natural and disturbed habitats.
