Cracca pineta

Cracca pineta, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads surrounded by spiny, leaf-like bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in saline and well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs.

Cracca pineta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
