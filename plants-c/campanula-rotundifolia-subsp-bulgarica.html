Campanula rotundifolia subsp. bulgarica

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

bulgarica, commonly known as the Balkan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its rounded, lobed leaves and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in mountainous regions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly larger leaves and more robust growth compared to the typical form of Campanula rotundifolia.
