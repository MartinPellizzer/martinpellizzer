Conanthera parvula

Conanthera parvula, commonly known as the small-flowered coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features a basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink to red in color. The plant blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

Conanthera parvula is a valuable addition to native gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
