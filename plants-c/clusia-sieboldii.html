Clusia sieboldii

Clusia sieboldii, commonly known as the wax plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Clusia sieboldii is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often used as a houseplant.

Its foliage remains lush and vibrant throughout the year, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
