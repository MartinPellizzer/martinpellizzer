Carex pensylvanica f. glumabunda

Carex pensylvanica f. glumabunda, commonly known as the Pennsylvania sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, greenish flowers in summer.

This variety is notable for its compact growth habit and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
