Cheilanthes semiglabra

Cheilanthes semiglabra, commonly known as the rock fern or cliff brake, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a perennial, evergreen fern that typically grows on rock faces or in crevices, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features a short, rhizomatous stem with lance-shaped, leathery leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its fronds are usually dark green and have a distinctive, compact appearance.

This species is well-suited to arid and semi-arid climates, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
