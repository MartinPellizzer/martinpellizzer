Carduus personata subsp. personata

Carduus personata subsp.

personata, commonly known as the common hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a tall, branching stem that bears yellow composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually spiny and hairy, while its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is known for its ornamental value and use in traditional medicine.
