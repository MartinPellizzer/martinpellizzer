Croton caudatus var. klotzschianus

Croton caudatus var. klotzschianus, commonly known as the Klotzschian croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with leaves that often display vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and its flowers are small, clustered, and typically green or yellowish. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

This ornamental plant is popular in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to add visual interest to gardens.
