Canna variabilis

Canna variabilis, commonly known as the giant cannas, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, colorful, arrowhead-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces showy, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by vibrant bracts, often in shades of red, yellow, or orange. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Canna variabilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in water features.
