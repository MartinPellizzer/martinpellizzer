Chamaerops bilaminata

Chamaerops bilaminata, commonly known as the two-layered palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Canary Islands and is characterized by its distinctive double-layered leaves. The plant typically grows as a solitary tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are long and pinnate, with a prominent midrib and a dense layer of leaflets on both sides.

Chamaerops bilaminata is well adapted to coastal environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
