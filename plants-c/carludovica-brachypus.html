Carludovica brachypus

Carludovica brachypus, commonly known as the short-stalked cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, spiny stems that store water. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to poor soil conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by cactus enthusiasts.
