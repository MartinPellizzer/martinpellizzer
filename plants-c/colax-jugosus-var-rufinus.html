Colax jugosus var. rufinus

Colax jugosus var. rufinus, commonly known as the red-stemmed colax, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-brown stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
