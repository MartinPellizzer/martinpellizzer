Cyperus humboldtianus

Cyperus humboldtianus, commonly known as the Humboldt flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in areas with standing water. Its flowering structures, known as spikelets, produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Cyperus humboldtianus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
