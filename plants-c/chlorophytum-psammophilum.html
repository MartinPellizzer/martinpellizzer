Chlorophytum psammophilum

Chlorophytum psammophilum, commonly known as the sand-loving spider plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers on slender stems, which are typically followed by clusters of red berries. It is well adapted to sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it ideal for xeriscaping.

Its resilience and low maintenance make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.
