Cyperus laevigatus subsp. albidus

Cyperus laevigatus subsp.

albidus, commonly known as the white flatsedge, is a grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in spikelets.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
