Calea platylepis var. scabra

Calea platylepis var. scabra, commonly known as the rough-leaved calea, is a species native to Mexico.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white, tubular flowers that bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners interested in native flora.
