Clematis sieboldii

Clematis sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features palmately divided, dark green leaves that are typically 5-9 cm in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and blue, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis sieboldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
