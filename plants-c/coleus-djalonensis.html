Coleus djalonensis

Coleus djalonensis, commonly known as the African coleus, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are often colored in shades of red, purple, and green, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
