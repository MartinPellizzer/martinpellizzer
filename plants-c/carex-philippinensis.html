Carex philippinensis

Carex philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex philippinensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and small fauna in its native habitat.
