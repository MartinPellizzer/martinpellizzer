Cyathea gregaria

Cyathea gregaria, commonly known as the clustered tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments, often in shaded forest understories. Its rhizomes are clustered, giving it the common name, and it can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

Cyathea gregaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
