Colchicum bulbocodioides subsp. hungaricum

Colchicum bulbocodioides subsp.

hungaricum, commonly known as the Hungarian autumn crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It features striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant produces a single, showy flower per stem, with reflexed petals and a spurred tepal at the base. Its bulbous underground storage organs are adapted for survival in temperate climates.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
