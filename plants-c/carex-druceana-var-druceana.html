Carex druceana var. druceana

Carex druceana var. druceana, commonly known as the Druce's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to brown, and the plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
