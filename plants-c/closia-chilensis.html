Closia chilensis

Closia chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean clover, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is often found in coastal areas and arid regions, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its adaptability makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
