Cucurbita maxima var. badagarensis

Cucurbita maxima var. badagarensis, commonly known as the African pumpkin or bitter gourd, is a variant of the common squash native to Africa.

It is characterized by its elongated, ridged fruit with a pale green to yellowish skin. The plant produces yellow flowers and has large, heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is often cultivated for both culinary and medicinal purposes.
