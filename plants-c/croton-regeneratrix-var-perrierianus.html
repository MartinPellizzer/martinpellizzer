Croton regeneratrix var. perrierianus

Croton regeneratrix var. perrierianus, commonly known as the Perrier's croton, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
