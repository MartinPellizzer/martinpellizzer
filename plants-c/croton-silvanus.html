Croton silvanus

Croton silvanus, commonly known as the mountain croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is characterized by its diverse morphology and often milky sap. The plant features alternate, simple, and often hairy leaves that can vary in shape from ovate to elliptic. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically greenish-yellow, and it produces woody capsules as fruit.

Croton silvanus is valued for its ornamental potential and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
