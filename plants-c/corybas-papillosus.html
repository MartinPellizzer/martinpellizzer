Corybas papillosus

Corybas papillosus, commonly known as the silky hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like flower that is often covered in a dense, silky covering of hairs. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as rainforests and swampy areas. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive, spurred lip.

Corybas papillosus is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its specialized pollination mechanisms involving small flies.
