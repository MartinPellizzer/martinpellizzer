Chuquiraga floribunda

Chuquiraga floribunda, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, thriving in cool, temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are often hairy and lance-shaped, and it produces small, nutlet-like fruits.

Chuquiraga floribunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
