Cytisus ferox

Cytisus ferox, commonly known as the red broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Cytisus ferox is also known for its sharp, thorn-like stems, which can make handling the plant difficult.
