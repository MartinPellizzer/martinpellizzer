Chamaemelum ruthenicum

Chamaemelum ruthenicum, commonly known as the Ruthenian fennel, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are aromatic and finely divided, while its flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer.

Chamaemelum ruthenicum is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.
