Cuphea epilobiifolia f. tonduzii

Cuphea epilobiifolia f. tonduzii, commonly known as the tonduzii form of the flame flower, is a variant of the species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in dense clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-draining soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
