Crepis viscidula

Crepis viscidula, commonly known as sticky hawk's-beard, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are arranged in a single, showy head. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows and grassy areas.

Crepis viscidula is adapted to a variety of soil types and is relatively drought-tolerant.
