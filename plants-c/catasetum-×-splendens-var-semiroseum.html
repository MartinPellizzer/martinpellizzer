Catasetum × splendens var. semiroseum

Catasetum × splendens var. semiroseum, commonly known as the semiroseum orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, semi-rosy pink to white flowers with a distinctive yellow lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its inflorescence consists of multiple flowers arranged in a spike, with showy, spurred petals.

The botanical features of this variety make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
