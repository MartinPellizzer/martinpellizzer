Calanthe unifolia

Calanthe unifolia, commonly known as the single-flower calanthe, is a flowering plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of China, India, and Indonesia. This species is characterized by its compact, evergreen rhizomatous growth habit and its showy, solitary flowers that typically bloom in spring. The flowers are usually white or pale pink, with a distinctive, spurred lip that aids in pollination.

Calanthe unifolia prefers cool, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden settings.
