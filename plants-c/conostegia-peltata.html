Conostegia peltata

Conostegia peltata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round, woody fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
