Caccinia

Caccinia, commonly known as the sandstone cactus, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Cactaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plants are characterized by their spiny stems and clusters of small, colorful flowers that bloom in spring. Caccinia species are typically shrubs or small trees, adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Their unique botanical features make them an important part of the desert flora and a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
