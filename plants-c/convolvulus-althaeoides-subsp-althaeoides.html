Convolvulus althaeoides subsp. althaeoides

Convolvulus althaeoides subsp.

althaeoides, commonly known as the lesser bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, and it has heart-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It is known for its climbing habit, using tendrils to ascend supports, and it blooms from spring to early summer.

This species is often considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and ability to spread aggressively.
