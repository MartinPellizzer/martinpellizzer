Cola brevipes var. hirsuta

Cola brevipes var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy cola, is a species native to western Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of caffeine.

The hairy texture of its stems and leaves is a distinguishing botanical feature of this variety.
