Carex decumbens

Carex decumbens, commonly known as the prostrate sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually flat and arranged in a dense, spreading rosette. Its flowering stems are shorter than the leaves and bear small, clustered spikelets.

Carex decumbens plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
