Clematis ochroleuca var. fremontii

Clematis ochroleuca var. fremontii, commonly known as the Fremont's clematis, is a flowering vine native to the western United States.

It features showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in arid regions.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various climate conditions.
