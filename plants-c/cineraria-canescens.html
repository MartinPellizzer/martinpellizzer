Cineraria canescens

Cineraria canescens, commonly known as the silver cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Cineraria canescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
