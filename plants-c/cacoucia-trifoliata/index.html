Cacoucia Trifoliata

Three-leaf cacoucia, scientifically known as Cacoucia trifoliata, is a unique and intriguing plant native to parts of Africa and Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically around a central stem. This plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks, where it thrives in the humid climate. Its delicate foliage and subtle blossoms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. Despite its beauty, it remains relatively underappreciated in the broader horticultural community.
