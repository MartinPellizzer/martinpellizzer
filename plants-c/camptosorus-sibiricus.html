Camptosorus sibiricus

Camptosorus sibiricus, commonly known as Siberian mountain avens, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Siberia and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early summer, which are clustered in a terminal umbel. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Camptosorus sibiricus thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
