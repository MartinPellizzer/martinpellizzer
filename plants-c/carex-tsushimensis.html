Carex tsushimensis

Carex tsushimensis, commonly known as the Tsushimen sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Carex tsushimensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
