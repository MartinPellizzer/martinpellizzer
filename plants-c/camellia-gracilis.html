Camellia gracilis

Camellia gracilis, commonly known as the slender camellia, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers come in shades of red, pink, and white, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Camellia gracilis prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, thriving in temperate climates.
