Cercidiphyllum ovale

Cercidiphyllum ovale, commonly known as the Chinese handkerchief tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn bright red in the fall, making it a striking ornamental plant. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring, followed by oval, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, preferring a temperate climate.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and parks.
