Convolvulus biflorus

Convolvulus biflorus, commonly known as the two-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers that bloom in the early morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus biflorus is part of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other familiar vines and flowering plants.
