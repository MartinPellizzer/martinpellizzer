Chrysothamnus bigelovii

Chrysothamnus bigelovii, commonly known as the yellow rabbitbrush, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-green foliage and produces clusters of yellow flowers in the fall. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that are adapted to arid environments, allowing it to conserve water efficiently. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert and sagebrush ecosystems.

The yellow rabbitbrush plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
