Cathartocarpus speciosus

Cathartocarpus speciosus, commonly known as the showy cat's-tail, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence that resembles a cat's tail. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in dense clusters along the spike. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue when mature.

Cathartocarpus speciosus is often found in moist, open habitats such as wetlands and prairies.
