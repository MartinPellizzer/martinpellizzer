Cymbalaria pilosa

Cymbalaria pilosa, commonly known as the common figwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.

Cymbalaria pilosa is also known for its ability to spread through both seeds and its rhizomatous root system.
