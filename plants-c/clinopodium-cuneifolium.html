Clinopodium cuneifolium

Clinopodium cuneifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved oregano, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its small, pale blue or purple flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees.

Clinopodium cuneifolium is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
