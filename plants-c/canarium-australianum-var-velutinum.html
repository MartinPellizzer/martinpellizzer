Canarium australianum var. velutinum

Canarium australianum var. velutinum, commonly known as the velvet nut tree, is a species native to Australia, particularly found in Queensland.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody capsules containing edible nuts. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subtropical regions.

The velvet nut tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and its nuts, which are sometimes used in traditional culinary practices.
