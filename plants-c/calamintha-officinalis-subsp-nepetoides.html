Calamintha officinalis subsp. nepetoides

Calamintha officinalis subsp.

nepetoides, commonly known as catnip, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves that are usually gray-green in color.

The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers arranged in whorls along the stems. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, known for its aromatic and often medicinal properties. Calamintha officinalis subsp.

nepetoides is valued for its essential oils, which contain compounds like nepetalactone, responsible for its characteristic effects on animals and humans.
