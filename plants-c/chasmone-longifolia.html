Chasmone longifolia

Chasmone longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf chasmone, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
