Convolvulus sepium var. catesbeianus

Convolvulus sepium var. catesbeianus, commonly known as the Catesby's bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing habit, with opposite leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The flowers are typically pink to purple, with five petals and a tubular corolla, blooming in summer. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds.

This species is native to the southeastern United States and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
