Caladenia decora

Caladenia decora, commonly known as the pink fairy orchid, is a striking plant native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and a single, showy pink or mauve flower. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.

Caladenia decora is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its elegant appearance and ecological significance.
