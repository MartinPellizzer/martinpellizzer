Calendula amplexicaulis

Calendula amplexicaulis, commonly known as the embracing caldron, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, overlapping leaves that wrap around the stem. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers with a prominent central disk, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Calendula amplexicaulis is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.

Its growth habit and vibrant blooms make it a notable species in botanical gardens and wildflower meadows.
