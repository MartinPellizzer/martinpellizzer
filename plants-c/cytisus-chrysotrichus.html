Cytisus chrysotrichus

Cytisus chrysotrichus, commonly known as golden broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a striking visual element to its habitat. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
