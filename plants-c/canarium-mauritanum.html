Canarium mauritanum

Canarium mauritanum, commonly known as the Mauritania canarium, is a species of flowering plant in the family Burseraceae.

It is native to parts of Africa, particularly the arid regions of Mauritania and the Sahel. The plant is characterized by its small, leathery leaves and produces yellowish flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and limited water availability.

The tree is often used in traditional medicine and for its durable wood.
