Campanula lazica

Campanula lazica, commonly known as the Lazic bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate, with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine environments, where it blooms in late summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
