Conospermum coerulescens subsp. adpressum

Conospermum coerulescens subsp.

adpressum, commonly known as the Grey-leaved Waratah, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, grey-green foliage and striking, pinkish-red floral spikes.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring, attracting nectar-feeding birds and insects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and heathland environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a valued species in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
