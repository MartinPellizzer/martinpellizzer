Crataegus laevicalyx

Crataegus laevicalyx, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional Chinese medicine.

Crataegus laevicalyx thrives in well-drained soils and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
