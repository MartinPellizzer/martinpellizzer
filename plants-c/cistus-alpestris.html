Cistus alpestris

Cistus alpestris, commonly known as the alpine rockrose, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. It is often found in open habitats such as heaths, slopes, and cliffs.

Cistus alpestris is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
