Comanthosphace japonica var. japonica

Comanthosphace japonica var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese glasswort, is a halophyte plant that thrives in saline environments.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in coastal and marshy areas. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. It is native to East Asia, particularly Japan, and is often found in tidal zones and salt marshes.

Its unique adaptations make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
