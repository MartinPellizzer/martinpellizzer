Coelogyne singalangensis

Coelogyne singalangensis, commonly known as the Singalang orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it prefers bright, indirect light with high humidity.

Coelogyne singalangensis is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
