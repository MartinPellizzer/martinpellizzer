Centaurea setabensis

Centaurea setabensis, commonly known as the Setubal thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant has a robust root system that helps it survive in arid conditions. Its flowering period occurs from late spring to early summer.

Centaurea setabensis is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline soils.
