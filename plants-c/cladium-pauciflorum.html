Cladium pauciflorum

Cladium pauciflorum, commonly known as the slender spikerush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence that bears small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense stands in marshes and swamps. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a distinct midrib, and it plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems.

Cladium pauciflorum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to improve water quality and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
