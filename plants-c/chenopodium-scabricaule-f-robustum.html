Chenopodium scabricaule f. robustum

Chenopodium scabricaule f. robustum, commonly known as roughleaf goosefoot, is a variant of the Chenopodium genus characterized by its robust growth habit and rough, textured foliage.

It is a herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats. The leaves are typically lance-shaped with a distinctive, sandpapery texture, and the plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It is adapted to thrive in poor soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

This plant is also valued for its nutritional content and has been utilized in traditional cuisines.
