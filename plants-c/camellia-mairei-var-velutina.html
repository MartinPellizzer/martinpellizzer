Camellia mairei var. velutina

Camellia mairei var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, velvety leaves and produces striking red or pink camellia flowers in late winter to early spring. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal. It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

The flowers are also used in traditional Chinese medicine.
