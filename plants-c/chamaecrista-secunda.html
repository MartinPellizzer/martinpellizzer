Chamaecrista secunda

Chamaecrista secunda, commonly known as the narrow-leaf burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and herbal practices.
