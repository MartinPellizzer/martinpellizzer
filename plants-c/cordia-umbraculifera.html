Cordia umbraculifera

Cordia umbraculifera, commonly known as the umbrella tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-like leaves that can reach up to 30 inches in diameter. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Cordia umbraculifera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and unique appearance.
