Chloris mossambicensis

Chloris mossambicensis, commonly known as the African lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Chloris mossambicensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
