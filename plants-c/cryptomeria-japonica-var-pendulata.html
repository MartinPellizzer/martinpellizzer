Cryptomeria japonica var. pendulata

Cryptomeria japonica var. pendulata, commonly known as the weeping cedar, is a variant of the Japanese cedar characterized by its distinctive cascading foliage.

This evergreen conifer features long, pendulous branches that droop gracefully, giving it a unique, ethereal appearance. The leaves are needle-like, dark green, and arranged in spirals along the branches. It is native to Japan and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental tree in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
