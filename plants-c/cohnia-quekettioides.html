Cohnia quekettioides

Cohnia quekettioides, commonly known as the smoothleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pinkish to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and wet meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cohnia quekettioides is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
