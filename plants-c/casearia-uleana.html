Casearia uleana

Casearia uleana, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet to purple berries that appear in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various bird species.
