Cubanthus linearifolius

Cubanthus linearifolius, commonly known as the slenderleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue or purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its vines can reach lengths of several meters, making it a climbing plant often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
