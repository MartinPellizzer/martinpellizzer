Cypripedium taibaiense

Cypripedium taibaiense, commonly known as the Taibai monkshood, is a rare and striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive purple and green coloration, and it typically grows in cool, shaded forest environments. The plant has a long, fleshy rhizome and features a single, erect stem that bears a single, hood-like flower. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its complex pollination mechanisms involving specific insects.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered an endangered species, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
