Carex unciniiformis var. elachista

Carex unciniiformis var. elachista, commonly known as the slender hook sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its narrow leaves and compact growth habit make it a distinctive and ecologically important component of wetland ecosystems.
