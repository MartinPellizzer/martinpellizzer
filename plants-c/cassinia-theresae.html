Cassinia theresae

Cassinia theresae, commonly known as the Tasmanian cassinia, is a flowering plant native to Tasmania.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as heathlands and grassy slopes. Its flowers are yellow and produce small, dry seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Cassinia theresae is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
