Chromatolepis magna

Chromatolepis magna, commonly known as the giant chromatolepis, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its massive, segmented trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The leaves are typically bright green, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss in its humid environment. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish flowers, followed by edible, round fruits that are a food source for various wildlife.

Due to its size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
