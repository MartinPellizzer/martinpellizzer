Cartiera multiceps

Cartiera multiceps, commonly known as the multiceps orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in its natural habitat.

The flowers are usually a mix of white and purple, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insect species.
