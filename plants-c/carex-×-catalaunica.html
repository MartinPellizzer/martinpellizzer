Carex × catalaunica

Carex × catalaunica, commonly known as the Catalaunica sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense spikes, typical of the sedge family.

Carex × catalaunica is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
