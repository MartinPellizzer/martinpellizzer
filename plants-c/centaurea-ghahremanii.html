Centaurea ghahremanii

Centaurea ghahremanii, commonly known as the Iranian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. It features bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its foliage consists of narrow, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in its native climate.
