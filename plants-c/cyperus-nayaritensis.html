Cyperus nayaritensis

Cyperus nayaritensis, commonly known as the Nayarit flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist to aquatic conditions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Cyperus nayaritensis is often found in regions with seasonal flooding, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
