Commelina rufociliata

Commelina rufociliata, commonly known as the red-tipped spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers with red-tipped petals. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a waxy surface, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for nutrient absorption in poor soils.

Commelina rufociliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
