Cylindropuntia leptocaulis f. longispina

Cylindropuntia leptocaulis f. longispina, commonly known as the long-spined hedgehog cactus, is a succulent native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with a waxy coating, adapted to store water in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a vibrant touch to its desert habitat. Its spines are long and sharp, serving as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

This variety is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ability to thrive in full sun.
