Cynanchum villosum

Cynanchum villosum, commonly known as the hairy milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate and have a distinctive hairy texture, contributing to its common name.

Cynanchum villosum is also known for its milky sap, which can be toxic to livestock.
