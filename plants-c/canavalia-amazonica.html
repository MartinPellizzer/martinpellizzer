Canavalia amazonica

Canavalia amazonica, commonly known as the Amazon bean or wild peanut, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its long, serpentine stems and can grow up to several meters in length. Its pods contain edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients.

Canavalia amazonica is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.
