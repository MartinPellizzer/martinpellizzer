Chironia baccata

Chironia baccata, commonly known as the red-fruited balm, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and clusters of small, pink to white flowers. The plant produces small, red, berry-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Chironia baccata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
