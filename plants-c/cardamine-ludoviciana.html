Cardamine ludoviciana

Cardamine ludoviciana, commonly known as the Louisiana watercress, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodlands. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately lobed, and have a peppery flavor, similar to watercress.

The small white or pink flowers appear in clusters and are followed by pods that contain several seeds.
