Carex mertensii

Carex mertensii, commonly known as Mertens' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes.

Carex mertensii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
