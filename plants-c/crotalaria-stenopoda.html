Crotalaria stenopoda

Crotalaria stenopoda, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and palmate leaves. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it a valuable crop for soil enrichment. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Crotalaria stenopoda is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
