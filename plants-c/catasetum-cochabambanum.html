Catasetum cochabambanum

Catasetum cochabambanum, commonly known as the Cochabamba Catasetum, is a tropical orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often fragrant flowers that come in a range of colors, including yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence is a raceme of showy, spurred flowers that attract specific pollinators.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens.
