Convolvulus lineatus var. gerardi

Convolvulus lineatus var. gerardi, commonly known as the scarlet bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, green, spherical fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is considered a weed in many regions.
