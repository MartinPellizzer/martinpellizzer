Crataegus prunellifolia

Crataegus prunellifolia, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it typically grows in moist, woodland habitats.

Crataegus prunellifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
