Chirocalyx pictus

Chirocalyx pictus, commonly known as the painted calyx plant, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful calyx that remains showy even after the flowers have faded. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and produces clusters of tubular, often brightly colored flowers. Its unique calyx is often adorned with intricate patterns, giving it its common name.

Chirocalyx pictus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid environments.
