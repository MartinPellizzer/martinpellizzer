Cyclanthera dioscoreoides

Cyclanthera dioscoreoides, commonly known as the arrowleaf cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like pumpkins and gourds. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into elongated, thin fruits. Its botanical structure is characterized by tendrils that aid in climbing, and the fruit is typically edible, though it may have a mild, cucumber-like flavor.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and sometimes for its edible fruit in local cuisines.
