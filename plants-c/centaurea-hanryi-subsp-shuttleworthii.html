Centaurea hanryi subsp. shuttleworthii

Centaurea hanryi subsp.

shuttleworthii, commonly known as Shuttleworth's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has narrow, linear leaves. It produces small, ray florets that are usually sterile, with the fertile disc florets at the center.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
