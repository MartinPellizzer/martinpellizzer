Cortaderia trianae

Cortaderia trianae, commonly known as the blue grass tree, is a striking ornamental grass native to South America.

It features tall, arching blades that range in color from blue-gray to silver-green, creating a dramatic visual effect in gardens. The plant produces fluffy, feathery plumes during the flowering season, typically in late winter to early spring. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates.

Its architectural form and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for landscaping and botanical collections.
