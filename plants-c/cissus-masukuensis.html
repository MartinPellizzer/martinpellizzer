Cissus masukuensis

Cissus masukuensis, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
