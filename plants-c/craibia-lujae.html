Craibia lujae

Craibia lujae, commonly known as the Lujan's crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the summer months. Craibia lujae thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
