Clompanus scaphigera

Clompanus scaphigera, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of purple or pink, which bloom in late summer. Clompanus scaphigera thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its habitat.
