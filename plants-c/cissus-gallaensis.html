Cissus gallaensis

Cissus gallaensis, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a climbing plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tendrils that help it climb. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in forest understories.

Cissus gallaensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
