Cissus acutissima

Cissus acutissima, commonly known as the rock fig or heartleaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor spaces.
