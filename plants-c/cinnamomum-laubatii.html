Cinnamomum laubatii

Cinnamomum laubatii, commonly known as the Laubatii cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces dark brown, aromatic bark that is used in traditional medicine and as a spice. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Cinnamomum laubatii is valued for its essential oils and has been used in various cultural and medicinal practices.
