Centaurea kotschyana

Centaurea kotschyana, commonly known as the Kotschy's bluemink, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and spiny, bristly foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowering period occurs in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea kotschyana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry climates.
