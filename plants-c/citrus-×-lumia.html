Citrus × lumia

Citrus × lumia, commonly known as the kumquat, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that turn orange when mature. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, while the flowers are fragrant and white or pale yellow.

The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often consumed whole, including the peel.
