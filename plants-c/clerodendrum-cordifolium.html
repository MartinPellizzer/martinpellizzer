Clerodendrum cordifolium

Clerodendrum cordifolium, commonly known as the heart-leaved clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

Clerodendrum cordifolium is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
