Crinum jagus

Crinum jagus, commonly known as the Jamaican lily, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that grow from a bulbous stem, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. Crinum jagus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
