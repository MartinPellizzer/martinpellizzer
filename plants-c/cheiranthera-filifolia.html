Cheiranthera filifolia

Cheiranthera filifolia, commonly known as the thread-leaved orchid, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, slender leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink and bloom during the wet season.
