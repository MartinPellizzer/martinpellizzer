Cenchrus ciliaris var. nubicus

Cenchrus ciliaris var. nubicus, commonly known as the Sudan grass, is a drought-tolerant grass native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its coarse, wiry leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous pale green to brown florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used for forage and soil stabilization.

Its adaptability makes it a valuable species in arid and semi-arid regions.
