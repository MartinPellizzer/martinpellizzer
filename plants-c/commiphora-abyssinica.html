Commiphora abyssinica

Commiphora abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian myrrh tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and aromatic resinous exudates. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and elliptical, leathery leaves. Its resin, known as myrrh, has been historically used in incense, medicine, and religious ceremonies.

The tree thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.
