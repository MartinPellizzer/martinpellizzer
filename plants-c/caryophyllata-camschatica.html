Caryophyllata camschatica

Caryophyllata camschatica, commonly known as the Kamchatka carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also valued for its adaptability to cooler climates and its ability to attract pollinators.
