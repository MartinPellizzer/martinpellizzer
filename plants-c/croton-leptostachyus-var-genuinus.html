Croton leptostachyus var. genuinus

Croton leptostachyus var. genuinus, commonly known as the narrow-stalked croton, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its distinctive, often variegated foliage with a mix of green, yellow, and red hues. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in tropical and subtropical gardens.
