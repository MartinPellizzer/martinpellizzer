Carludovica trigona

Carludovica trigona, commonly known as the spiny pear, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to Mexico.

It is a shrub-like cactus characterized by its thick, spiny stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant produces edible, pear-shaped fruits that are often used in traditional cuisine. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it thrives in arid environments with well-drained soil.

Carludovica trigona is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
