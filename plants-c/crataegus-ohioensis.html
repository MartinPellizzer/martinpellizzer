Crataegus ohioensis

Crataegus ohioensis, commonly known as the Ohio hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the central and eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet and features thorny branches, oval to elliptic leaves, and showy white or pink flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and pome fruits.
