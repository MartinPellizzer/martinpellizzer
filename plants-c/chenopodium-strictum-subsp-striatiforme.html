Chenopodium strictum subsp. striatiforme

Chenopodium strictum subsp.

striatiforme, commonly known as the narrow-leaf goosefoot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and alternate leaves that are typically narrow and ribbed. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
