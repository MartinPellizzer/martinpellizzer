Carex × leptoblasta

Carex × leptoblasta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a hybrid plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-green leaves that are often slightly curved. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of sedges. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands.

Carex × leptoblasta is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
