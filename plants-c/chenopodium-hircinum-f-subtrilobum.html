Chenopodium hircinum f. subtrilobum

Chenopodium hircinum f. subtrilobum, commonly known as the three-lobed goosefoot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, typically in late summer. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
