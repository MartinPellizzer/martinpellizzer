Catasetum pallidum

Catasetum pallidum, commonly known as the pale orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, pendulous inflorescence and showy, pale yellow to white flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial plant, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers have a unique structure with a specialized lip that aids in pollination by specific insect species.

The genus Catasetum is known for its intricate floral morphology and association with certain pollinators.
