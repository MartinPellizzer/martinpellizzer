Capsicum annuum f. nigrum

Capsicum annuum f. nigrum, commonly known as black pepper plant, is a variety of the common chili pepper.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black fruits that develop from the flowers. The plant is native to South America but is widely cultivated in tropical regions for its peppercorns. The fruits are used in various culinary and medicinal applications due to their pungent flavor.

Its botanical features include hairy stems, lobed leaves, and small, clustered flowers.
