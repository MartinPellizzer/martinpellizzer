Cavendishia martii

Cavendishia martii, commonly known as the Amazonian orchid, is a flowering plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in its natural habitat.

Cavendishia martii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique and vibrant blooms.
