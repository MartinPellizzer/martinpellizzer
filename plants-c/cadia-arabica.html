Cadia arabica

Cadia arabica, commonly known as Arabica coffee.

It is a species of evergreen shrub native to the highlands of Ethiopia and Sudan. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that contain two seeds, which are roasted and ground to make coffee. Arabica plants thrive in cool, high-altitude climates with consistent rainfall.

They are known for their complex flavor profiles and are one of the most widely cultivated coffee species globally.
