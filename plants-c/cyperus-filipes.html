Cyperus filipes

Cyperus filipes, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along waterways. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered and bear small, inconspicuous flowers.

Cyperus filipes is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
