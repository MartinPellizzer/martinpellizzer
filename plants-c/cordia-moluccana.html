Cordia moluccana

Cordia moluccana, commonly known as the Moluccan cordia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, fleshy fruits containing several seeds. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
