Cyrtanthus brachysiphon

Cyrtanthus brachysiphon, commonly known as the short-siphon bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers typically bloom in spring and are usually pink or purple in color. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is cultivated in various regions with a Mediterranean climate.
