Cotoneaster floccosus

Cotoneaster floccosus, commonly known as the white cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven oval leaflets, and it produces a woolly covering on its young stems. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Cotoneaster floccosus is relatively hardy and adaptable to various soil types.
