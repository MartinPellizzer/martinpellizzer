Chelone albida

Chelone albida, commonly known as white hellebore, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Chelone albida is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
