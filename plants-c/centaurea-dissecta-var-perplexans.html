Centaurea dissecta var. perplexans

Centaurea dissecta var. perplexans, commonly known as the dissected centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is a perennial species that can reach heights of up to 60 cm.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and drought-tolerant landscaping.
