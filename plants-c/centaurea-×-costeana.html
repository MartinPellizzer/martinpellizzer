Centaurea × costeana

Centaurea × costeana, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowering heads, which are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a composite structure. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lanceolate leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Native to Europe, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
