Calamintha clipeata

Calamintha clipeata, commonly known as round-leaved germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic foliage. The plant features opposite, ovate to round leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically pink or purple and bloom in clusters during the summer months.

Calamintha clipeata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
