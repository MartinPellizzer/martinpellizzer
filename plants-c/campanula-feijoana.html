Campanula feijoana

Campanula feijoana, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a silvery or grayish underside, which helps reduce water loss.

Campanula feijoana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
