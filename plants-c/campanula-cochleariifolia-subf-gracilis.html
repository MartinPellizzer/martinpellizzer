Campanula cochleariifolia subf. gracilis

Campanula cochleariifolia subf.

gracilis, commonly known as slender bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to violet in color.

The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
