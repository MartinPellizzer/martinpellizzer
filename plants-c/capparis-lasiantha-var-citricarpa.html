Capparis lasiantha var. citricarpa

Capparis lasiantha var. citricarpa, commonly known as the sour apple caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its edible, sour-tasting fruits, which are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.
