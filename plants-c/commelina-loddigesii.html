Commelina loddigesii

Commelina loddigesii, commonly known as the red-veined dayflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Commelinales order and is part of the Commelinaceae family, which includes many ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves with distinctive red veins and small, blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
