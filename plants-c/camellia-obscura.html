Camellia obscura

Camellia obscura, commonly known as the dark camellia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is a flowering plant in the Theaceae family, characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring, are usually deep red or purple, with a waxy texture.

Camellia obscura is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms and hardy nature.
