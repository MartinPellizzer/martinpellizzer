Cryptocoryne pontederiifolia

Cryptocoryne pontederiifolia, commonly known as the water wisteria, is a semi-aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, pale blue flowers that emerge above the water surface. It thrives in shallow, still water with moderate light and nutrient-rich substrates.

Cryptocoryne pontederiifolia is popular in aquariums and wetland gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
