Clematis inclinata

Clematis inclinata, commonly known as the leaning clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, green berries that mature to a dark blue or black color. It thrives in partial to full shade and is often found in woodland areas, where it climbs along trees or shrubs.

Clematis inclinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and insects.
