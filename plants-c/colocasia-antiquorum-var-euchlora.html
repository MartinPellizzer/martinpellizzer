Colocasia antiquorum var. euchlora

Colocasia antiquorum var. euchlora, commonly known as the giant elephant ear, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length.

The foliage is typically a deep green with a velvety texture and often has a slight sheen. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires rich, well-draining soil to flourish. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

It is also known for its ability to produce striking purple or red spathes that enclose its yellowish-white spadix.
