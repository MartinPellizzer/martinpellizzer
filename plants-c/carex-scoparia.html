Carex scoparia

Carex scoparia, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wetlands, and stream banks. Carex scoparia has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
