Crocus sublimis

Crocus sublimis, commonly known as the alpine crocus, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Western Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bulbous underground stem. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, narrow shape during late winter to early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge after the flowers.

Crocus sublimis is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
