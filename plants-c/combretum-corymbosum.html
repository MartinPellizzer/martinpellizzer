Combretum corymbosum

Combretum corymbosum, commonly known as the red bushwillow, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that appear in dense clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, rough texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The tree is also known for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
