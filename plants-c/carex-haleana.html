Carex haleana

Carex haleana, commonly known as Hale's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This species is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in swampy areas or along streambanks.

Carex haleana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
