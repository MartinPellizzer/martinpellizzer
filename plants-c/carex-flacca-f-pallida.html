Carex flacca f. pallida

Carex flacca f. pallida, commonly known as the pale flat sedge, is a variant of the flat sedge species native to North America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often pale green, giving it its distinguishing appearance. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

The pale flat sedge produces small, clustered spikes of pale to greenish flowers, which are wind-pollinated and contribute to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
