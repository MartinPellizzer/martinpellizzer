Chamaesciadium gracile

Chamaesciadium gracile, commonly known as slender sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically features small, succulent leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, helping to conserve moisture in dry environments. The plant produces delicate, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its drought tolerance, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
