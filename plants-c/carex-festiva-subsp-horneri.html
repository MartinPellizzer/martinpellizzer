Carex festiva subsp. horneri

Carex festiva subsp.

horneri, commonly known as the Horner's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

This subspecies is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
