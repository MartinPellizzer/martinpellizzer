Carlina lyrata

Carlina lyrata, commonly known as the lyrical carline thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy blue to purple composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, gray-green leaves with a fuzzy texture and a distinctive basal rosette. Carlina lyrata thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
