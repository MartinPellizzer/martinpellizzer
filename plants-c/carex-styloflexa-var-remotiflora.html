Carex styloflexa var. remotiflora

Carex styloflexa var. remotiflora, commonly known as the remote-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated, a common reproductive strategy among sedges. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks, and is often found in temperate climates.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a relatively rare and ecologically significant species.
