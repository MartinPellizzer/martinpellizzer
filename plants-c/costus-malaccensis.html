Costus malaccensis

Costus malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca costus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Costaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy inflorescences. The plant produces striking orange to red bracts that surround small, tubular flowers. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
