Coleus reticulatus

Coleus reticulatus, commonly known as the net-veined coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with intricate patterns. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and display vibrant hues of red, purple, and green. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale purple or white, arranged in spikes.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
