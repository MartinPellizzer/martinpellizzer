Crocus kosaninii

Crocus kosaninii, commonly known as the Kosanin's crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It produces striking, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils. It is often found in open woodlands and meadows, where it thrives in partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
