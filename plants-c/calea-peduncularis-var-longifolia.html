Calea peduncularis var. longifolia

Calea peduncularis var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-stemmed candleplant, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, showy white to pale blue flowers that resemble candles. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stem. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
