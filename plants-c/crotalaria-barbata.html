Crotalaria barbata

Crotalaria barbata, commonly known as the hairy cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces elongated pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Crotalaria barbata is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.

However, some species within the genus are toxic, so caution is advised when handling or consuming any part of the plant.
