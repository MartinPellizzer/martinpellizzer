Crocus lageniflorus subvar. lutescens

Crocus lageniflorus subvar. lutescens, commonly known as the yellow bulbous crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces striking, cup-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and forms a bulbous corm as its storage organ. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and early bloom time, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
