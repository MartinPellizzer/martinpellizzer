Cyathea melanophlebia

Cyathea melanophlebia, commonly known as the black-stem tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories. Its leaves are bioluminescent, emitting a faint blue glow at night, a rare trait among ferns.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
