Cassia siamea

Cassia siamea, commonly known as the Siamese cassia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically odd-pinnate with elliptical leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in spikes, followed by elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. Cassia siamea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and foliage make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
