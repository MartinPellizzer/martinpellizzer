Callitriche glareosa

Callitriche glareosa, commonly known as shining water star, is a small aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in North America.

It typically grows in slow-moving streams, ponds, and wetlands, often forming dense mats on the water surface. The plant has tiny, star-shaped leaves that are usually less than 1 centimeter in diameter, arranged in whorls around the stems. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-white in color, and appear in clusters.

Callitriche glareosa plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
