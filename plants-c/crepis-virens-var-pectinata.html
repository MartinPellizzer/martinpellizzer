Crepis virens var. pectinata

Crepis virens var. pectinata, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and fibrous root system. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and is often found in disturbed soils. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The variety pectinata is distinguished by its broader, more pectinate leaves compared to the typical form.
