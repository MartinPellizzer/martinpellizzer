Cinnamomum tavoyanum

Cinnamomum tavoyanum, commonly known as Tavoy cinnamon or Burmese cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces reddish-brown, fragrant bark that is harvested for its essential oils and medicinal properties. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and aromatic qualities, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
