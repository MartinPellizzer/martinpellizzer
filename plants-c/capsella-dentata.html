Capsella dentata

Capsella dentata, commonly known as the toothed knotweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped with serrated edges, and the stems are hairy and often reddish in color.

Capsella dentata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and woodland edges.
