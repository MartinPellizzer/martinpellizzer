Columbea imbricata var. striata

Columbea imbricata var. striata, commonly known as the streaked ivy leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves with distinct streaking patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It is typically grown as a climbing vine and thrives in warm, humid climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
