Calandrinia minuscula

Calandrinia minuscula, commonly known as the dwarf portulaca, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing annual herb with fleshy, succulent leaves and vibrant yellow or orange flowers. The plant thrives in arid and coastal environments, exhibiting drought tolerance due to its succulent structure. Its small size and rapid growth make it suitable for rock gardens and as a ground cover.

Calandrinia minuscula is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
