Calamus macgregorii

Calamus macgregorii, commonly known as the MacGregor's canescent reed, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that are often covered in a whitish, powdery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color and can grow up to several meters in length. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, yellowish flowers, which are followed by elongated, spadix-like fruits.

Calamus macgregorii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional basket-making in its native regions.
