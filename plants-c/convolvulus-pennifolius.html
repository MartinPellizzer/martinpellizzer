Convolvulus pennifolius

Convolvulus pennifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia, where it thrives in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in the early morning. Its leaves are narrow and alternate, with a hairy texture that helps reduce water loss.

This species is often found in disturbed habitats and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.
