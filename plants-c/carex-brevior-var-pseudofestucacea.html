Carex brevior var. pseudofestucacea

Carex brevior var. pseudofestucacea, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in three rows. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated, a common reproductive strategy among sedges. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization in aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
