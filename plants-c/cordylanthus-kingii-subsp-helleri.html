Cordylanthus kingii subsp. helleri

Cordylanthus kingii subsp.

helleri, commonly known as Heller's tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features upright stems with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences, producing small, yellowish blooms.

It is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often found in association with other drought-tolerant flora.
