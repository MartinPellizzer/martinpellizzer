Calophyllum saigonense

Calophyllum saigonense, commonly known as the Saigon flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its large, showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces elliptical, leathery leaves and has a straight, cylindrical trunk with a smooth, grayish bark. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its wood, which is used in traditional crafts and furniture making.
