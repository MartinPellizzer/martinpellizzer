Coreopsis tinctoria var. imminuata

Coreopsis tinctoria var. imminuata, commonly known as the dwarf coreopsis, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring minimal water once established.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
