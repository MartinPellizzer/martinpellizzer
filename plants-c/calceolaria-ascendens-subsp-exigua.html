Calceolaria ascendens subsp. exigua

Calceolaria ascendens subsp.

exigua, commonly known as the slender cupflower, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America. It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that are typically violet or purple in color.

The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Its unique floral structure and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
