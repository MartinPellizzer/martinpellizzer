Cleome juncea

Cleome juncea, commonly known as spider flower, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cleome juncea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
