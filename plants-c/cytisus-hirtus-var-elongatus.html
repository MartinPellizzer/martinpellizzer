Cytisus hirtus var. elongatus

Cytisus hirtus var. elongatus, commonly known as the long-stemmed broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being hairy and lance-shaped. This variety is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or dry habitats.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
