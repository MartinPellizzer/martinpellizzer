Campanula scheuchzeri f. oblongifolia

Campanula scheuchzeri f. oblongifolia, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and adapted to cold, rocky environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine meadows and mountainous areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Campanulaceae family.
