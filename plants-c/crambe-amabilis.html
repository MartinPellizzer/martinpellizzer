Crambe amabilis

Crambe amabilis, commonly known as the sweet crambe, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are alternate, oval-shaped, and slightly hairy. It is known for its edible seeds, which can be pressed to extract oil.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and potential agricultural uses.
