Calandrinia axilliflora

Calandrinia axilliflora, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual plant with hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces vibrant purple or pink flowers that open in the morning and close by midday, adapting to conserve moisture. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the leaf axils, giving it its scientific name.

Calandrinia axilliflora is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
