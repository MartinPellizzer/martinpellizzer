Catharanthus roseus var. nanus

Catharanthus roseus var. nanus, commonly known as the dwarf Madagascar plume, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of pink, white, and red. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade conditions. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and long blooming period.

Additionally, it contains alkaloids that have medicinal uses, though it is toxic if ingested.
