Crotalaria agatiflora subsp. engleri

Crotalaria agatiflora subsp.

engleri, commonly known as the Engler's spurred crotalaria, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the legume family Fabaceae. It is characterized by its distinctive spurred petals and compound leaves, which are typical of the genus Crotalaria.

The plant produces yellow to orange flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences, and its seeds are encased in a hard, ridged pod. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

The plant has been studied for its potential medicinal properties, although it contains toxic alkaloids that require careful handling.
