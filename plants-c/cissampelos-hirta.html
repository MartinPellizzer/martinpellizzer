Cissampelos hirta

Cissampelos hirta, commonly known as the hairy arrowhead, is a climbing plant belonging to the family Menispermaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and often climbs using its tendrils. Its leaves are usually hairy, giving it the common name, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
