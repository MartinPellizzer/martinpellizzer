Catha fasciculata var. parvifolia

Catha fasciculata var. parvifolia, commonly known as African khat, is a shrub native to East Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into three-winged fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, preferring well-drained soils.

The leaves contain psychoactive alkaloids, which have been traditionally used for their stimulating effects.
