Carludovica acuminata

Carludovica acuminata, commonly known as the Mexican giant nightshade, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in the evening. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
