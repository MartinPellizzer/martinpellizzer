Caperonia cordata var. mollis

Caperonia cordata var. mollis, commonly known as the soft-heart heather, is a perennial evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant features leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and rockeries.
