Cyperus roestelii

Cyperus roestelii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and streambanks. The plant has fibrous roots and exhibits a clump-forming growth habit.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
