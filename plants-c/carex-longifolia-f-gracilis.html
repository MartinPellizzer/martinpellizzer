Carex longifolia f. gracilis

Carex longifolia f. gracilis, commonly known as slender spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized inflorescence structure. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with elongated, cylindrical spikelets that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
