Clinopodium graveolens subsp. rotundifolium

Clinopodium graveolens subsp.

rotundifolium, commonly known as roundleaf hedge nettle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features opposite, ovate to round leaves with a hairy surface, and its stems are often reddish and branched.

The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shady habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its aromatic leaves and flowering structure make it a notable species within the Lamiaceae family.
