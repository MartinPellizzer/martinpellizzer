Cassia fockeana

Cassia fockeana, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. Its bark is smooth and grayish, while the roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia fockeana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
