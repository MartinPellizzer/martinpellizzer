Clerodendrum splendens var. longicuspe

Clerodendrum splendens var. longicuspe, commonly known as the long-pointed flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and can grow as a shrub or small tree. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
