Cochemiea swinglei

Cochemiea swinglei, commonly known as the Swingle's cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the Sonoran Desert in the United States and Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in white to grayish spines. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its arid environment. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Due to its compact size and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid gardens.
