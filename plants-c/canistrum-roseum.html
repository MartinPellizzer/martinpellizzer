Canistrum roseum

Canistrum roseum, commonly known as the red-leafed candelabra, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family and is native to Central and South America.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and striking red or pinkish leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by green, spiny capsules that mature into orange or red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
