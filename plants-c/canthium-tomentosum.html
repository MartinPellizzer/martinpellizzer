Canthium tomentosum

Canthium tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly spurge.

It is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small yellow flowers that appear in clusters. It grows as an annual herb, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural applications.
