Cyanus matthiolifolius

Cyanus matthiolifolius, commonly known as the Matthiola leafed sunflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue and purple flowers, which bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
