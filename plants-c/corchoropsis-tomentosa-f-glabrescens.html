Corchoropsis tomentosa f. glabrescens

Corchoropsis tomentosa f. glabrescens, commonly known as the silky cotton or velvet abutilon, is a variant of the abutilon plant characterized by its soft, hairy foliage.

It is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions, often found in coastal and disturbed soils. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a dense, velvety texture, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange, resembling those of the hibiscus. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires moderate to high sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and seasonal blooms.
