Clematis uniflora

Clematis uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered clematis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces showy, single flowers in shades of white to pink. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is typically found in woodland areas. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for gardeners seeking native plants.

Clematis uniflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
