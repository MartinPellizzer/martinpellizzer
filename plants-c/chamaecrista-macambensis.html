Chamaecrista macambensis

Chamaecrista macambensis, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in sandy or disturbed soils. It is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects.
