Cymbidium calceolaria

Cymbidium calceolaria, commonly known as the shoe flower, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spurred petals that resemble a shoe, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually a vibrant red or orange, with a contrasting yellow lip.

Cymbidium calceolaria is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
