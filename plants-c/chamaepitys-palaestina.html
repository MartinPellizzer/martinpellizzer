Chamaepitys palaestina

Chamaepitys palaestina, commonly known as the Palestinian juniper, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or arid soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features needle-like leaves and produces small, dark blue berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and resilience to drought.

Chamaepitys palaestina plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
