Cymbidium koran

Cymbidium koran, commonly known as the Korean orchid, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family, characterized by its upright growth habit and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces tall spikes of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Cymbidium koran is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
