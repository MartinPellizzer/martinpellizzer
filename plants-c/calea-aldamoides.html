Calea aldamoides

Calea aldamoides, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that open at night. The plant has velvety, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. Its flowers are typically pale blue to white, with a distinctive central disk.

Calea aldamoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique nocturnal blooming habit.
