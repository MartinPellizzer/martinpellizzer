Cyphostemma pachyanthum

Cyphostemma pachyanthum, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant.
