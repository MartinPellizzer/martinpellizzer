Carex provotii

Carex provotii, commonly known as the Armenian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically flowering in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and forested areas.

Carex provotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
