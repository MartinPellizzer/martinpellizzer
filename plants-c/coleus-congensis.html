Coleus congensis

Coleus congensis, commonly known as the African coleus, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with leaves that display vibrant shades of red, purple, and green. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pink, which bloom during the warmer months.

Coleus congensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its ornate leaf patterns and adaptability to various growing conditions.
