Cirsium eatonii var. peckii

Cirsium eatonii var. peckii, commonly known as Peck's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as grasslands and open woodlands. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
