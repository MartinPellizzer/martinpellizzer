Cassia marksiana

Cassia marksiana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that resemble small showers, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value. Its leaves are compound, with multiple oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, pod-like fruits.

Cassia marksiana is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
