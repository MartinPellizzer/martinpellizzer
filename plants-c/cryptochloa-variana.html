Cryptochloa variana

Cryptochloa variana, commonly known as the variable cryptochloa, is a rare and delicate grass species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.

Botanically, it is of interest for its unique morphological traits and potential ecological roles in its native environment.
