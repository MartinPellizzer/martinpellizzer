Calopogon parviflorus

Calopogon parviflorus, commonly known as the grass pink, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and moist woodlands, preferring acidic soils. Its flowers are borne on tall, slender stems and bloom in early summer.

Calopogon parviflorus is an important component of wetland ecosystems and is often found in association with other moisture-loving plants.
