Clerodendrum hexagonum

Clerodendrum hexagonum, commonly known as the purple glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from purple to pink, often with a distinctive yellow center. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, clustered fruits that contain several seeds.

Clerodendrum hexagonum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
