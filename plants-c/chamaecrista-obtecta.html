Chamaecrista obtecta

Chamaecrista obtecta, commonly known as the narrowleaf burr legume, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters. The plant is known for its distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and native plant landscaping.
