Creochiton bibracteata

Creochiton bibracteata, commonly known as the two-bracted creosote beetle, is a species of leaf beetle native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dark, glossy exoskeleton and the presence of two prominent bracts on its body. The plant it feeds on, creosote bush (Larrea tridentata), is a drought-tolerant shrub with small, resinous leaves. Creochiton bibracteata plays a role in the ecological dynamics of its habitat by contributing to nutrient cycling through its feeding and life cycle.

Its botanical associations highlight the intricate relationships between insects and their host plants in arid environments.
