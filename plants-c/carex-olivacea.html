Carex olivacea

Carex olivacea, commonly known as the olive sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of greenish-yellow flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex olivacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
