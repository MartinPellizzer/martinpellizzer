Camphoromoea rhamnoides

Camphoromoea rhamnoides, commonly known as the camphor tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowers, which emit a strong camphor scent. The plant belongs to the family Lauraceae and is known for its dense, evergreen foliage. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

The tree is valued for its wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.
