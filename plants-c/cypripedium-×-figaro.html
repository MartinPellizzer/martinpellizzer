Cypripedium × figaro

Cypripedium × figaro, commonly known as the Figaro lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch, which is typically deep pink or red. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, often arranged in a fan shape.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
