Cyperus benadirensis

Cyperus benadirensis, commonly known as the Benadire sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus benadirensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
