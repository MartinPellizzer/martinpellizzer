Chaetochloa imberbis var. streptobotrys

Chaetochloa imberbis var. streptobotrys, commonly known as the threadgrass, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets.
