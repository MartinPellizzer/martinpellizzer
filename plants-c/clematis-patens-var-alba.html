Clematis patens var. alba

Clematis patens var. alba, commonly known as the white clematis, is a climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, green berries that mature to a dark blue color. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vigorous growth and long-lasting blooms.
