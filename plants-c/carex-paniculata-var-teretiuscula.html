Carex paniculata var. teretiuscula

Carex paniculata var. teretiuscula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, making it a common sight in marshes and along the edges of ponds. It plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its botanical structure reflects adaptations to its aquatic or semi-aquatic habitat, with rhizomes that aid in vegetative propagation.
