Campuloclinium megacephalum

Campuloclinium megacephalum, commonly known as the giant-headed cuphea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with thick, woody stems and opposite leaves. Its botanical features include glandular hairs on the foliage and a unique, clustered inflorescence that gives it its common name.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
