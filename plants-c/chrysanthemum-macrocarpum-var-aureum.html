Chrysanthemum macrocarpum var. aureum

Chrysanthemum macrocarpum var. aureum, commonly known as the golden chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and is often cultivated for its vibrant color and long-lasting blooms.
