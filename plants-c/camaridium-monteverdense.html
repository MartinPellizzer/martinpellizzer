Camaridium monteverdense

Camaridium monteverdense, commonly known as the Monteverde camara, is a flowering plant native to the cloud forests of Costa Rica.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often on the forest floor or on tree trunks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a glossy surface, while its stems are often covered in fine, white hairs.

Camaridium monteverdense plays an important role in the local ecosystem by providing nectar for various pollinators.
