Commersonia herbacea

Commersonia herbacea, commonly known as the hairy commersonia, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hairy structures, giving it its common name. Its flowers are usually white or pale green and are arranged in clusters, making it visually distinct.

Commersonia herbacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
