Carex nivicola

Carex nivicola, commonly known as the snow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It thrives in moist, often cold environments such as alpine meadows and tundra regions. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a dense, fibrous root system.

Carex nivicola plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
