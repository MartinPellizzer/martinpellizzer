Combretum punctatum subsp. squamosum

Combretum punctatum subsp.

squamosum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, scale-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its adaptability and striking foliage make it a notable species in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
