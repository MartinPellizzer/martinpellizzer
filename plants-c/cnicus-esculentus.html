Cnicus esculentus

Cnicus esculentus, commonly known as the edible thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow flower heads. The plant is known for its edible leaves, which are often used in traditional cuisine after being blanched to remove bitterness. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Cnicus esculentus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
