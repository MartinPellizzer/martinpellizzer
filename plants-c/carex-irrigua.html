Carex irrigua

Carex irrigua, commonly known as the slender swamp sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Carex irrigua thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
