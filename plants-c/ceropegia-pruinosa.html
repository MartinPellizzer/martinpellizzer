Ceropegia pruinosa

Ceropegia pruinosa, commonly known as the velvet leaf or silver leaf, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves covered in a fine, silvery pubescence that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Ceropegia pruinosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
