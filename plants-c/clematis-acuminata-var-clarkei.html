Clematis acuminata var. clarkei

Clematis acuminata var. clarkei, commonly known as Clarke's Clematis, is a flowering vine native to China.

It features slender, twining stems that can climb to heights of several meters. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are compound, with three to five oval-shaped leaflets.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
