Campium angustipinnum

Campium angustipinnum, commonly known as narrow-leafed campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, often with a slightly hairy surface.

Campium angustipinnum is an annual or biennial herb that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.
