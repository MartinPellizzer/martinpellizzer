Crocus suaveolens subsp. imperati

Crocus suaveolens subsp.

imperati, commonly known as the sweet-scented crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It produces delicate, cup-shaped flowers with a fragrant, honey-scented aroma, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a member of the Iridaceae family.

This subspecies is particularly noted for its early blooming habit, often flowering in late winter or early spring.
