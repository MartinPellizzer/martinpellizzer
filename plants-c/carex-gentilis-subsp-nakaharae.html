Carex gentilis subsp. nakaharae

Carex gentilis subsp.

nakaharae, commonly known as the Nakahara sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or wetlands. It produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are often green or brown in color.
