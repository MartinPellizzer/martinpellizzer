Catasetum trifidum

Catasetum trifidum, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers with a unique three-lobed lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are known for their intricate structure, including specialized pollination mechanisms.

Catasetum trifidum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
