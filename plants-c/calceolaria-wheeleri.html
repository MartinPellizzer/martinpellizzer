Calceolaria wheeleri

Calceolaria wheeleri, commonly known as the Wheeler's slipper orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive calyx-like structure that resembles a slipper, which is a key feature of the genus Calceolaria. The plant produces delicate, colorful flowers in shades of purple, pink, and white, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in rocky or mountainous regions.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
