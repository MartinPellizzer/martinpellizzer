Convolvulus hastatus var. multiflorus

Convolvulus hastatus var. multiflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a sprawling perennial that produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy areas and can be invasive in some regions.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
