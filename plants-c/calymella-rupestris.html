Calymella rupestris

Calymella rupestris, commonly known as the rock daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, succulent growth habit and is often found growing on rocky outcrops. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are fleshy and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to conserve water in arid environments.

Calymella rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in rock gardens and xeriscapes.
