Cremanthodium humile

Cremanthodium humile, commonly known as the dwarf crownbeard, is a small annual herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish to reddish flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
