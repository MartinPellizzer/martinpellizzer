Combretum auriculatum var. longispicatum

Combretum auriculatum var. longispicatum, commonly known as the long-spined flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like inflorescences that appear in late summer, consisting of numerous small, red or orange flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy surface, while the bark is smooth and reddish-brown. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract birds and insects, contributing to local biodiversity.
