Citrus × aurantium subf. asahikan

Citrus × aurantium subf.

asahikan, commonly known as the Japanese mandarin or kurobuta mandarin, is a hybrid citrus plant native to East Asia. It is characterized by its small, round, and often fragrant fruits with a thin, smooth peel.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is valued for its sweet, juicy pulp and is often used in culinary applications. This subsp.

is well adapted to subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
