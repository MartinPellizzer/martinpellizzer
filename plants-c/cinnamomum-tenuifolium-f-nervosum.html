Cinnamomum tenuifolium f. nervosum

Cinnamomum tenuifolium f. nervosum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cinnamon or a variant of the Chinese cinnamon, is a botanical variant characterized by its slender, elongated leaves.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is native to southern China and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, with a compact, bushy form. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a distinctive veining pattern that gives it its "nervosum" designation.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
