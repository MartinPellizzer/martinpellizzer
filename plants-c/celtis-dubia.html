Celtis dubia

Celtis dubia, commonly known as the Mexican hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the southern United States and Mexico.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits that are edible for some animals. Celtis dubia is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in riparian zones and woodland areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and shelter for wildlife.
