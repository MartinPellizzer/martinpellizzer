Clinopodium ashei

Clinopodium ashei, commonly known as Ashe's mint, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, minty fragrance.

Ashe's mint produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale blue or purple, attracting various pollinators.
