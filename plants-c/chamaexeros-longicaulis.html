Chamaexeros longicaulis

Chamaexeros longicaulis, commonly known as the long-stemmed chamaexeros, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is occasionally cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
