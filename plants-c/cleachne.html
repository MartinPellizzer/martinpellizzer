Cleachne

Cleachne, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are herbaceous perennials native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plants typically have alternate, lance-shaped leaves and produce small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. Cleachne species are often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of growing conditions.
