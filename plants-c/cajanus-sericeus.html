Cajanus sericeus

Cajanus sericeus, commonly known as silky cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often grown as a cover crop or for its ornamental value due to its delicate appearance.

Cajanus sericeus has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility.
