Chamaecrista fuscescens

Chamaecrista fuscescens, commonly known as the purple crown flower, is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and compound leaves.

It is native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico, typically found in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to purple, often arranged in dense clusters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being narrow and pointed.

Chamaecrista fuscescens is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
