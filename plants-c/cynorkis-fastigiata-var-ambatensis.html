Cynorkis fastigiata var. ambatensis

Cynorkis fastigiata var. ambatensis, commonly known as the Madagascar bamboo, is a unique plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that form a dense, upright growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is adapted to grow in dry, rocky environments.

Due to its distinctive appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
