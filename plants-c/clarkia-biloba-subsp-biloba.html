Clarkia biloba subsp. biloba

Clarkia biloba subsp.

biloba, commonly known as the Biloba Clarkia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a herbaceous annual with striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a terminal cluster.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
