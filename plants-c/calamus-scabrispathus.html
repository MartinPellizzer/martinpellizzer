Calamus scabrispathus

Calamus scabrispathus, commonly known as the rough-pathed calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and broad, sword-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with spadices, typical of the Araceae family.

Calamus scabrispathus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
