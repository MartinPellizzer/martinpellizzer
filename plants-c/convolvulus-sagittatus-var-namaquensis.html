Convolvulus sagittatus var. namaquensis

Convolvulus sagittatus var. namaquensis, commonly known as theNamaqua morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky and sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
