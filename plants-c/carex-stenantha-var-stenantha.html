Carex stenantha var. stenantha

Carex stenantha var. stenantha, commonly known as narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

The leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a distinct midrib, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
