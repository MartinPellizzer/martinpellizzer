Coleocarya gracilis

The plant typically grows on the trunks of trees, relying on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, which is a key aspect of its botanical biology. Its leaves are narrow and strap-like, adapted to minimize water loss in its humid, shaded habitat.
