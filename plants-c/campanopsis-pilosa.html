Campanopsis pilosa

Campanopsis pilosa, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue or white flowers. The plant typically grows as a herb or shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Campanopsis pilosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.
