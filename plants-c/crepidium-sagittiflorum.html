Crepidium sagittiflorum

Crepidium sagittiflorum, commonly known as the arrowflower orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant features a slender stem with a single, showy spike of flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, or purple. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The orchid's unique floral structure and ability to thrive in humid climates make it a notable species in botanical studies.
