Croton lundianus var. microphyllus

Croton lundianus var. microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaved croton, is a shrub native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
