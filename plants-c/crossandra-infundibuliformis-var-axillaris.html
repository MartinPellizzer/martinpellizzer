Crossandra infundibuliformis var. axillaris

Crossandra infundibuliformis var. axillaris, commonly known as the axillary crossandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in warm, humid environments. It is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its vibrant colors and compact growth habit.

The varietal form 'axillaris' is distinguished by its flowers emerging from the leaf axils, giving it a unique appearance.
