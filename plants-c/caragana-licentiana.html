Caragana licentiana

Caragana licentiana, commonly known as the Licentian shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access water from deeper soil layers.

The flowers are usually yellow or reddish, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
