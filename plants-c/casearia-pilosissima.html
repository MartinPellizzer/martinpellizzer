Casearia pilosissima

Casearia pilosissima, commonly known as the silky casearia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elliptical, woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The silky casearia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
