Cordia llanense

Cordia llanense, commonly known as the yellow cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina, Bolivia, and Paraguay.

It belongs to the genus Cordia, which includes over 200 species of flowering plants. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Cordia llanense is often used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
