Ceropegia haagnerae

Ceropegia haagnerae, commonly known as the Ghost Plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, tubular stems that are often tinged with purple and have a unique, ghost-like appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale green. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Ceropegia haagnerae is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
