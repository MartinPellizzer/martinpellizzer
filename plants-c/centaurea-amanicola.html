Centaurea amanicola

Centaurea amanicola, commonly known as the Ammanica knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It features upright stems with narrow, gray-green leaves and produces bright yellow to orange flowers.

Centaurea amanicola is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
