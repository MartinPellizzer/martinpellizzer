Chamaecrista calycioides var. calycioides

Chamaecrista calycioides var. calycioides, commonly known as the yellow burr legume, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces distinctive yellow pods that resemble burrs. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites. It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

The flowers are typically small and yellow, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
