Centaurea croatica

Centaurea croatica, commonly known as the Croatian centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its bright blue or purple flowers are surrounded by pale yellow ray florets, creating a striking visual appearance.

Centaurea croatica is also valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
