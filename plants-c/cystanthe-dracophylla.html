Cystanthe dracophylla

Cystanthe dracophylla, commonly known as the draco's leaf, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the rainforest understory. The plant features small, leathery leaves that are arranged oppositely along its stems. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are pollinated by insects.

Cystanthe dracophylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
