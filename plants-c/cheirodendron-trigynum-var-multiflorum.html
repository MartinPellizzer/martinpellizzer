Cheirodendron trigynum var. multiflorum

Cheirodendron trigynum var. multiflorum, commonly known as the multi-flowered cheirodendron, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the rainy season. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
