Crataegus parvula

Crataegus parvula, commonly known as the dwarf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 6 meters and features thorned branches, which are a distinctive botanical characteristic. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have serrated edges.

Crataegus parvula is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various habitats.
