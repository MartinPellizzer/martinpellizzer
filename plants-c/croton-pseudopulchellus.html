Croton pseudopulchellus

Croton pseudopulchellus, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that often have a velvety texture and are typically green with prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental leaves and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
