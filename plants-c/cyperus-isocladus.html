Cyperus isocladus

Cyperus isocladus, commonly known as the narrowleaf paperplant, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green or brown, arranged in spikelets. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cyperus isocladus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to moist conditions.
