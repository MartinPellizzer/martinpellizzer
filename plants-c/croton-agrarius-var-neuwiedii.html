Croton agrarius var. neuwiedii

Croton agrarius var. neuwiedii, commonly known as the Neuwied croton, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which can range from bright yellow to deep red, depending on the season and environmental conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses in tropical and subtropical regions.
