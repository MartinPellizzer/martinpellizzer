Convolvulus lasiospermus

Convolvulus lasiospermus, commonly known as the silky morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a trailing perennial that produces small, pale blue to purple flowers with five petals. The leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is part of the Convolvulaceae family, known for its climbing or trailing growth habits.
