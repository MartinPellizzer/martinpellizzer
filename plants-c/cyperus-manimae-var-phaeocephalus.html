Cyperus manimae var. phaeocephalus

Cyperus manimae var. phaeocephalus, commonly known as the dark-headed flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in dense clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is distinguished by its dark purple to blackish flower heads, which give it its common name.
