Crataegus latiflora

Crataegus latiflora, commonly known as the broadleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet and features compound leaves with 5 to 9 ovate leaflets. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers in spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in woodlands, stream banks, and disturbed areas.

Crataegus latiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and supporting local biodiversity.
