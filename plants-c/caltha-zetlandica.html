Caltha zetlandica

Caltha zetlandica, commonly known as the Zetland buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream sides. Its flowers bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
