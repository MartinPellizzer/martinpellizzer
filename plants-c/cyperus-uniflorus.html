Cyperus uniflorus

Cyperus uniflorus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, and thrives in moist, poorly drained soils. Its slender, grass-like stems are often covered in fine, hairy structures, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Cyperus uniflorus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
