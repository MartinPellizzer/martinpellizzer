Centropogon affinis

Centropogon affinis, commonly known as the narrowleaf false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in wetlands and disturbed soils, exhibiting a low, sprawling growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are yellow and dandelion-like in appearance.

Centropogon affinis is often found in swampy areas and is adapted to moist, nutrient-rich environments.
