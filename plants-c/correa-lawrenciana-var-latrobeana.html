Correa lawrenciana var. latrobeana

Correa lawrenciana var. latrobeana, commonly known as the coastal correa, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a red or purple throat. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, preferring sandy or gravelly soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, evergreen leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
