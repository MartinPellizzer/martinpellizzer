Coronilla parviflora

Coronilla parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered crown vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems and small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Coronilla parviflora plays a role in nitrogen fixation, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
