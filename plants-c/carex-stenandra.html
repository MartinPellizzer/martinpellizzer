Carex stenandra

Carex stenandra, commonly known as narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.

Carex stenandra is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and environmental benefits.
