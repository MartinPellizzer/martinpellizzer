Carex monile var. raeana

Carex monile var. raeana, commonly known as the slender basket sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are often green or brown in color.

Carex monile var. raeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
