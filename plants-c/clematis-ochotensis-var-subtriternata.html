Clematis ochotensis var. subtriternata

Clematis ochotensis var. subtriternata, commonly known as the Ochotensis Clematis, is a perennial vine native to the Far East.

It features climbing stems with prominent, palmately compound leaves that are trifoliate or subtrifoliate in structure. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
