Cypholophus pachycarpus

Cypholophus pachycarpus, commonly known as the broad-fruited cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces distinctive, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs. Its seeds are enclosed in thick, fleshy pods, which are an important food source for various wildlife.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
