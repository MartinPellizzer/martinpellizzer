Convolvulus pluricaulis

Convolvulus pluricaulis, commonly known as the many-stemmed morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often covered with fine hairs.

Convolvulus pluricaulis is adapted to arid environments and is often found growing along roadsides and in disturbed soils.
