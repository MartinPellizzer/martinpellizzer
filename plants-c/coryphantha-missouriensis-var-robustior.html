Coryphantha missouriensis var. robustior

Coryphantha missouriensis var. robustior, commonly known as the Missouri giant hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the United States, particularly in Missouri and surrounding regions.

It is a perennial cactus that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open woodlands or along stream banks. The plant features a cylindrical stem with prominent, spiny areoles and produces yellow flowers during the summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a notable species in xerophytic plant communities.
