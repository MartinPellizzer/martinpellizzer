Clitoria annua

Clitoria annua, commonly known as the butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive violet-blue flowers that resemble butterflies, which bloom in clusters. The plant is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and has compound leaves with three leaflets. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds and insects, and it produces small, flat seed pods.

Clitoria annua is also valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
