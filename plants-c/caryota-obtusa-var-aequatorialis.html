Caryota obtusa var. aequatorialis

Caryota obtusa var. aequatorialis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian fishtail palm, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that resemble a fish's tail, giving it its common name. The plant has a slender, unbranched trunk that can grow up to several meters in height. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.
