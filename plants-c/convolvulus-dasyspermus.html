Convolvulus dasyspermus

Convolvulus dasyspermus, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces distinctive, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to violet in color. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers open in the morning, hence its common name. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other climbing and trailing plants.

The species is adapted to dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
