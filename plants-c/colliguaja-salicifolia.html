Colliguaja salicifolia

Colliguaja salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf colliguaja, is a member of the Apocynaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to Central and South America, particularly in regions with tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its lanceolate, willow-like leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its fruits are follicles that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
