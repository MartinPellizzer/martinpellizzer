Calytrix depressa

Calytrix depressa, commonly known as the low calytrix, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Australia.

It features small, glossy, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers during the spring and summer months. It is typically found in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and coastal landscapes.
