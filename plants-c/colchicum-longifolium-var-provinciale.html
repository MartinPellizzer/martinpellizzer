Colchicum longifolium var. provinciale

Colchicum longifolium var. provinciale, commonly known as the Alpine colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features striking, pale purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are typically shorter and more compact compared to the typical variety. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and alpine plant collections.
