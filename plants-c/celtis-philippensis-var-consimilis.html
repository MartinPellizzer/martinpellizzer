Celtis philippensis var. consimilis

Celtis philippensis var. consimilis, commonly known as the Philippine hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, yellowish fruits that are important for local wildlife. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
