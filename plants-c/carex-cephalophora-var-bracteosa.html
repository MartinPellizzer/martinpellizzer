Carex cephalophora var. bracteosa

Carex cephalophora var. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, three-angled berries.

This variety is distinguished by its distinctive bract-like structures that enclose the inflorescence, contributing to its ornamental value in wetland gardens.
