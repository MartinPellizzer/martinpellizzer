Cerasus obliqua

Cerasus obliqua, commonly known as the Japanese apricot, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its early spring blooming habit, producing clusters of fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant's leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a serrated margin, while its fruits are small, yellow or orange drupes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Cerasus obliqua is also valued for its edible fruit, which has a tart flavor and is used in various culinary applications.
