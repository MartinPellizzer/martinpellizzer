Chamaeranthemum

Chamaeranthemum, commonly known as the Christmas chrysanthemum, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are native to South Africa and are characterized by their compact, bushy growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, while the flowers come in a range of colors including red, orange, yellow, and white. Chamaeranthemum species are often cultivated as ornamental plants due to their vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.

They are also popular for their use in floral arrangements and as potted plants during the holiday season.
