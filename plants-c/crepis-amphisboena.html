Crepis amphisboena

Crepis amphisboena, commonly known as the two-lobed hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It features basal rosettes of narrow, gray-green leaves and produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by a series of tubular florets.

Crepis amphisboena is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is often found in disturbed or marginal areas.
