Cornus plicata

Cornus plicata, commonly known as the Chinese dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, white or pink bract-like flowers that appear in clusters, often mistaken for petals. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans.

Cornus plicata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
