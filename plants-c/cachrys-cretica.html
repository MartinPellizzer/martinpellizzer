Cachrys cretica

Cachrys cretica, commonly known as the creeping cinquefoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from May to June. The plant has narrow, waxy leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Cachrys cretica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
