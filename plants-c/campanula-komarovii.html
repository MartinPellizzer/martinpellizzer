Campanula komarovii

Campanula komarovii, commonly known as the Komarov's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
