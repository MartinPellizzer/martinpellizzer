Clytostoma binatum

Clytostoma binatum, commonly known as the sweet-scented orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its leaves are usually broad and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique fragrance and visual beauty.
