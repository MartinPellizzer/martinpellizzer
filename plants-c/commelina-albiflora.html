Commelina albiflora

Commelina albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family, which includes many ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. Its most distinctive feature is its clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Commelina albiflora is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.
