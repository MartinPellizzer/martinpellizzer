Chirita auriculata

Chirita auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate chirita, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, tubular flowers. The leaves are often heart-shaped with a distinctive auricle, or ear-like projection, at the base. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.

The plant produces small, colorful flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
