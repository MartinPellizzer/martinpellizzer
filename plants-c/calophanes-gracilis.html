Calophanes gracilis

Calophanes gracilis, commonly known as the slender calophanes, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its botanical features include a slender stem and opposite leaf arrangement.

Calophanes gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
