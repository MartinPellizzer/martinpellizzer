Cecropia diguensis

Cecropia diguensis, commonly known as the giant ceiba, is a fast-growing tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and a massive, umbrella-shaped crown. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and it produces small, greenish flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits. It plays a significant ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The tree is also valued for its durable wood and is often found in disturbed or secondary forest areas.
