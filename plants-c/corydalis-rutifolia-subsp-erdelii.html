Corydalis rutifolia subsp. erdelii

Corydalis rutifolia subsp.

erdelii, commonly known as the Erdel's spring corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats.

The plant produces delicate, pinkish-purple flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
