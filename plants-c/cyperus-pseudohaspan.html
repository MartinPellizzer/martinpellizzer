Cyperus pseudohaspan

Cyperus pseudohaspan, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist, poorly drained soils.

Cyperus pseudohaspan plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
