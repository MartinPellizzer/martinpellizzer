Cassia apoucouita var. obtusifolia

Cassia apoucouita var. obtusifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with broad, ovate leaflets and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
