Chamaescilla corymbosa var. latifolia

Chamaescilla corymbosa var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf chamaescilla, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
