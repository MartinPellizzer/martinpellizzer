Cortaderia fulvida

Cortaderia fulvida, commonly known as the silver grass or silver reed, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, silver-blue plumes that give it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in panicles, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves are long, narrow, and have a metallic sheen, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cortaderia fulvida is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
