Carex filicina var. microgyna

Carex filicina var. microgyna, commonly known as the small-fruit sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering structures consist of male and female flowers, with the female flowers being smaller and more clustered.

This variety is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
