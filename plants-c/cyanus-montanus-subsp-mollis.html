Cyanus montanus subsp. mollis

Cyanus montanus subsp.

mollis, commonly known as the velvetblue coneflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flower heads, which are surrounded by a series of pale blue ray flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are hairy and slightly lobed, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
