Clintonia glomerata

Clintonia glomerata, commonly known as the clustered bluebead, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Melanthiaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue-black berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. Clintonia glomerata thrives in shaded, moist woodland habitats and is often found in rich, acidic soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of forest ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
