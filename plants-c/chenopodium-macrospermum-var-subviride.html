Chenopodium macrospermum var. subviride

Chenopodium macrospermum var. subviride, commonly known as green goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically covered with fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or yellowish in color. It is native to the western United States and thrives in disturbed soils and arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
