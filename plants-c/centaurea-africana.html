Centaurea africana

Centaurea africana, commonly known as the African star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, typically from July to September. The plant has upright, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-gray hue. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in grasslands and rocky soils.

Centaurea africana is known for its ability to spread through seeds that are dispersed by wind and animals.
