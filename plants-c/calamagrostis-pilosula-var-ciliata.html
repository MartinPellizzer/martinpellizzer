Calamagrostis pilosula var. ciliata

Calamagrostis pilosula var. ciliata, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It features slender, upright culms with a blue-green hue, and its leaves are finely textured with a silvery sheen. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with purple or pink, adding ornamental value. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its adaptability makes it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and wetland landscaping.
