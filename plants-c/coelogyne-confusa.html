Coelogyne confusa

Coelogyne confusa, commonly known as the white-lipped orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendant, white or pale yellow flowers with prominent red or purple lips. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte on trees or rocks, and its pseudobulbs are short and stout. Its leaves are elliptic and arranged in two rows, and it blooms during the summer months.

Coelogyne confusa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
