Cocos nucifera var. rubescens

Cocos nucifera var. rubescens, commonly known as the red-fruited coconut, is a variant of the coconut palm characterized by its distinctive reddish hues in both its fruit and foliage.

This variety is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soils. The fruit of Cocos nucifera var. rubescens is typically smaller than the standard coconut, with a thicker, more fibrous husk. The plant produces both male and female flowers, with the female flowers developing into the edible fruit.

Its unique appearance and potential for ornamental use make it a valuable addition to tropical gardens and landscapes.
