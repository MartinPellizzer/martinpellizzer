Casearia varians var. obovata

Casearia varians var. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf casearia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered white or yellowish flowers, which are followed by distinctive orange to red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural gardens in warm climates.
