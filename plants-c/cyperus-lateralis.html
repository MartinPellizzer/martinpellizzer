Cyperus lateralis

Cyperus lateralis, commonly known as the flatleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, clumping growth habit. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets.

Cyperus lateralis is often found in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.
