Caladenia caesarea subsp. caesarea

Caladenia caesarea subsp.

caesarea, commonly known as the blue-tongued orchid, is an orchid species native to southeastern Australia. It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive, elongated lip that resembles a tongue.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands. It is a terrestrial orchid that produces a single, erect stem bearing a few flowers.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty.
