Chamaesaracha physaloides

Chamaesaracha physaloides, commonly known as the balloon vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its unique, inflated, balloon-like fruits that contain seeds. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, yellow flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
