Carex hakonensis var. macrocarpa

Carex hakonensis var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikes of small, usually green or brownish flowers, which are arranged in a distinctive inflorescence. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
