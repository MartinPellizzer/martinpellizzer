Chrysanthemum chanetii

Chrysanthemum chanetii, commonly known as the African chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows as a shrub with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange, with a central disc surrounded by ray florets.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sunlight.
