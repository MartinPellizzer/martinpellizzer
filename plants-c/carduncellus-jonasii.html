Carduncellus jonasii

Carduncellus jonasii, commonly known as the Jonas's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant features a robust, upright stem with spiny leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. Its flowers are yellow and appear in dense clusters, often blooming in the summer months.

Carduncellus jonasii is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils.
