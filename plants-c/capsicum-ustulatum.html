Capsicum ustulatum

Capsicum ustulatum, commonly known as the scorched pepper, is a rare and unique species of chili pepper native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wrinkled, and often scorched appearance, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that range in color from yellow to red, depending on maturity. It is a perennial shrub that thrives in warm, arid environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, sharing genetic relatives with other peppers and tomatoes.
