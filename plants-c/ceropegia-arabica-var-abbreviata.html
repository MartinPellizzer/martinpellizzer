Ceropegia arabica var. abbreviata

Ceropegia arabica var. abbreviata, commonly known as the African violet or more specifically the Cape violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, which are adapted for specific pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its compact growth habit, it is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
