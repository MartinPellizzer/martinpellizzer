Cydonia japonica var. genuina

Cydonia japonica var. genuina, commonly known as the Japanese quince.

It is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and belongs to the Rosaceae family. The plant produces yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by fruit that ripens in autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The fruit is typically used in traditional Japanese cuisine and herbal remedies.
