Cardiospermum corindum f. loxense

Cardiospermum corindum f. loxense, commonly known as the hairy heartleaf.

This variant of the heartleaf is characterized by its distinctive hairy foliage and showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. It is a tropical plant native to regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture due to the dense pubescence.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
