Comarostaphylis polifolia subsp. polifolia

Comarostaphylis polifolia subsp.

polifolia, commonly known as the coastal dune lichen, is a unique plant that thrives in coastal environments. It is a lichen, which is a symbiotic organism composed of a fungus and an alga or cyanobacterium.

This subshrub is typically found on sandy soils and is adapted to withstand harsh maritime conditions. Its morphology includes a crustose thallus with a dark green to brown coloration.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion in coastal areas.
