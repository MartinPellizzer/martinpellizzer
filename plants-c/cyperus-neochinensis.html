Cyperus neochinensis

Cyperus neochinensis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and pond margins, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Cyperus neochinensis produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the base of the plant.

It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive, upright growth habit and adaptability to various water conditions.
