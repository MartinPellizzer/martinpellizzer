Carex oblita

Carex oblita, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, making it a common component of wetland ecosystems.

Carex oblita is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various insect and bird species.
