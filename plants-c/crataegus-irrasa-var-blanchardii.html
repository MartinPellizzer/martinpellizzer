Crataegus irrasa var. blanchardii

Crataegus irrasa var. blanchardii, commonly known as the Blanchard's hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptic leaves, and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is distinguished by its unique leaf shape and floral structure.
