Chaetostachys versteegii

Chaetostachys versteegii, commonly known as the African grass or Versteeg's grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ornamental gardening or for erosion control.
