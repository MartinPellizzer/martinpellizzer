Centaurea boissieri subsp. funkii

Centaurea boissieri subsp.

funkii, commonly known as the Funk's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, bristly foliage.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, composite flowers, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
