Cephalocereus apicicephalium

Cephalocereus apicicephalium, commonly known as the Queen of the Night, is a species of cactus native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that bloom only at night, making it a striking nocturnal display. Its flowers are typically followed by bright red fruits that contain edible seeds.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
