Cuphea salicifolia

Cuphea salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that resemble willow leaves, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cuphea salicifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
