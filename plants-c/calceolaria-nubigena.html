Calceolaria nubigena

Calceolaria nubigena, commonly known as the cloud crown, is a species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-like flowers that resemble small cups, which are a distinctive feature of the Calceolaria genus. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in cloud forests and alpine meadows. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, adapted to withstand harsh mountain conditions.

Calceolaria nubigena is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
