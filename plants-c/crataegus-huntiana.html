Crataegus huntiana

Crataegus huntiana, commonly known as the Hunt's hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in areas of Georgia and South Carolina. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that appear in the spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy pome that ripens in the fall, attracting various birds and small mammals.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
