Crataegus tortuosa

Crataegus tortuosa, commonly known as the twisted hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The twisted hawthorn is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
