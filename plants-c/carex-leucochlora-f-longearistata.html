Carex leucochlora f. longearistata

Carex leucochlora f. longearistata, commonly known as the long-awned white sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, which are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in dense clusters, typical of the sedge family. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is distinguished by its elongated, hairy awns on the bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance.
