Cinnamomum rupestre

Cinnamomum rupestre, commonly known as the rock cinnamon, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces dark brown, aromatic berries that contain several seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its medicinal and aromatic properties, it is valued in traditional herbal practices.
