Chromolucuma baehniana

Chromolucuma baehniana, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has elongated, strap-like leaves that are adapted to capture moisture from the humid air. It thrives in well-drained, nutrient-rich substrates and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Chromolucuma baehniana is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
