Cuspidia araneosa

Cuspidia araneosa, commonly known as the spider cushion, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a compact, rounded growth habit with dense clusters of small, fleshy leaves that resemble spider legs, giving it its common name. The leaves are typically blue-gray in color and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss. It produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the warmer months.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
