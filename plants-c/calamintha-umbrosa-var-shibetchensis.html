Calamintha umbrosa var. shibetchensis

Calamintha umbrosa var. shibetchensis, commonly known as Japanese calamint, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It features aromatic, narrow leaves with a strong mint-like scent, which are often used in traditional herbal remedies. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
