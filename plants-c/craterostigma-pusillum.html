Craterostigma pusillum

Craterostigma pusillum, commonly known as the small-leaf rockcress, is a small, perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring season. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Craterostigma pusillum is also known for its ability to survive in arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
