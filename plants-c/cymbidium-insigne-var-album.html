Cymbidium insigne var. album

Cymbidium insigne var. album, commonly known as the white orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers with a subtle pinkish tinge, which bloom during the winter months. The plant has long, narrow leaves that grow in a fan shape, and it typically thrives in cool, humid environments. It is a sympodial orchid, meaning it grows in pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its elegance and fragrance.
