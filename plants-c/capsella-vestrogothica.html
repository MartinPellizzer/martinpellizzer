Capsella vestrogothica

Capsella vestrogothica, commonly known as the northern shepherd's purse, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mustard family (Brassicaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive triangular seed pods. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. It produces small white or purple flowers and has a basal rosette of leaves.

Capsella vestrogothica is an annual plant that completes its life cycle within one growing season.
