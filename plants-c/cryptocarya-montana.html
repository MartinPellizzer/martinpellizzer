Cryptocarya montana

Cryptocarya montana, commonly known as the mountain cryptocarya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and mountainous forests.

Cryptocarya montana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
