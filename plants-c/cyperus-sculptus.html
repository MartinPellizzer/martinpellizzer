Cyperus sculptus

Cyperus sculptus, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, zigzag-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its slender, triangular stems are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Cyperus sculptus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist environments.
