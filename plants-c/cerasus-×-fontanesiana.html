Cerasus × fontanesiana

Cerasus × fontanesiana, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Yoshino cherry, is a hybrid species of the Prunus genus.

It is native to Japan and is renowned for its ornamental value, particularly its showy pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small deciduous tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a serrated edge, and the fruit is a small, dark red cherry that is edible but less commonly consumed.

The species is widely cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal and cultural significance.
