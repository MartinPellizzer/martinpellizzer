Cyrtopera poiformis

Cyrtopera poiformis, commonly known as the smoothleaf pincushion.

It is a flowering plant native to southern Africa, characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and clusters of small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cyrtopera poiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
