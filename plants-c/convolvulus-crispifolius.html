Convolvulus crispifolius

Convolvulus crispifolius, commonly known as the curlyleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, curling foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and bloom in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Convolvulus crispifolius is a member of the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
