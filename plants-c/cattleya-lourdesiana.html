Cattleya lourdesiana

Cattleya lourdesiana, commonly known as the Lourdes orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid that thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with intricate patterns. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in seasonal dry periods.

Cattleya lourdesiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its long-lasting blooms.
