Catha inermis

Catha inermis, commonly known as khat, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula and East Africa.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 2 meters tall, featuring small, elliptical leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant contains psychoactive alkaloids, particularly cathinone and cathine, which are responsible for its stimulating effects. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in coastal regions.

The plant is cultivated for both traditional use and as an ornamental species.
