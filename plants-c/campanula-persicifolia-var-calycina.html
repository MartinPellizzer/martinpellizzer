Campanula persicifolia var. calycina

Campanula persicifolia var. calycina, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators.
