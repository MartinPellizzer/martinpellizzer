Crowea exalata subsp. obcordata

Crowea exalata subsp.

obcordata, commonly known as the broad-leaf crowea, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red throat.

The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often broader than those of the typical species. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.

The flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a vibrant touch to the landscape during the cooler months.
