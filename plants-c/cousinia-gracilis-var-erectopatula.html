Cousinia gracilis var. erectopatula

Cousinia gracilis var. erectopatula, commonly known as the upright cousinia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and dense clusters of small, pink to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun, exhibiting drought tolerance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered with a fine layer of white hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
