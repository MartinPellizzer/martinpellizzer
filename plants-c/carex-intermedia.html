Carex intermedia

Carex intermedia, commonly known as intermediate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, which contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant produces triangular, three-ranked leaves and has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
