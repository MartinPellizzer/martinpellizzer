Coleus maculatus

Coleus maculatus, commonly known as the spotted coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that display a variety of patterns, including red, purple, and white. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with serrated edges, and it produces small, tubular flowers in shades of blue or purple.

Coleus maculatus thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
