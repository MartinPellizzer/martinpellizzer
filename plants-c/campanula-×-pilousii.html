Campanula × pilousii

Campanula × pilousii, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and blooms in late spring to early summer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
