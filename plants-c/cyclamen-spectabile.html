Cyclamen spectabile

Cyclamen spectabile, commonly known as the Persian cyclamen, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountains of Turkey and the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, velvety leaves and showy, bowl-shaped flowers that come in a range of colors including pink, white, and purple. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage and long-lasting blooms.

Cyclamen spectabile is typically grown as a houseplant or in shaded garden beds during the spring and early summer.
