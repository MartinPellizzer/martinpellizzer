Campanula cochleariifolia var. paniculta

Campanula cochleariifolia var. paniculta, commonly known as the panicle bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue to violet. The plant produces panicles of small, clustered flowers, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and extended blooming period.
