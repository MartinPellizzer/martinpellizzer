Campanula dichotoma var. peltieri

Campanula dichotoma var. peltieri, commonly known as the Peltier's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems. This perennial species thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
