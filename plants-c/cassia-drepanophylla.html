Cassia drepanophylla

Cassia drepanophylla, commonly known as the golden cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain small, hard seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Cassia drepanophylla is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
