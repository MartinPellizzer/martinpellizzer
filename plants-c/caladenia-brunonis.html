Caladenia brunonis

Caladenia brunonis, commonly known as the Black Hill Spider-orchid, is an orchid species native to the south-western region of Western Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, pale green to brownish flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as heaths and woodlands. Its flowering period occurs during the autumn season, and it is known for its low-growing habit and delicate appearance.

Caladenia brunonis is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific environmental conditions for successful growth and reproduction.
