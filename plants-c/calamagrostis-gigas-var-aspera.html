Calamagrostis gigas var. aspera

Calamagrostis gigas var. aspera, commonly known as the rough bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It features tall, slender stalks with narrow, bluish-green leaves that are covered in fine, rough hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers that bloom in late summer, adding a soft, cloud-like appearance to the landscape. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
