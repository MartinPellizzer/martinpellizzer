Clarkia concinna subsp. automixa

Clarkia concinna subsp.

automixa, commonly known as the Sierra clarkia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its delicate, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has slender stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens.
