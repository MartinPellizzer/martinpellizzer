Cymbidium babae

Cymbidium babae, commonly known as the Babae orchid, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments within forest understories. The plant features long, slender pseudobulbs that support its linear, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually clustered in racemes, with petals and sepals that are often white or pale pink, adorned with intricate patterns.

Cymbidium babae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its elegant, fragrant blooms.
