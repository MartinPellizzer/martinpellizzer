Cephalomanes crassum

Cephalomanes crassum, commonly known as the broad-leafed sea lettuce, is a marine green alga found in coastal areas.

It has a flattened, ribbon-like thallus that can grow up to several centimeters in length. The plant exhibits a branching structure with a central axis and lateral fronds. It thrives in shallow, tide-pool environments and is often found attached to rocks.

Its simple morphology and rapid growth make it an important component of intertidal ecosystems.
