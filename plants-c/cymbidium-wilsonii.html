Cymbidium wilsonii

Cymbidium wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's orchid, is a species of orchid native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in winter or early spring. The plant produces pendulous, fragrant blooms in shades of white, pink, and red, often with a yellow or orange lip. It prefers cool, humid environments and is often cultivated in gardens or greenhouses.

Cymbidium wilsonii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a popular choice for floral arrangements.
