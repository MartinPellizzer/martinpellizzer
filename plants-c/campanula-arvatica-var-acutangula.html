Campanula arvatica var. acutangula

Campanula arvatica var. acutangula, commonly known as the sharp-angled bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lanceolate and often have a slightly serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
