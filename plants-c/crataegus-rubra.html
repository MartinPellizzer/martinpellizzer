Crataegus rubra

Crataegus rubra, commonly known as the red hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and showy pink to white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
