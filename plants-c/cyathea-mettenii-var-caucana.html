Cyathea mettenii var. caucana

Cyathea mettenii var. caucana, commonly known as the Caucana tree fern, is a tropical plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in persistent, fibrous roots. The fronds are large, typically reaching several meters in length, and are pinnately compound with a distinctive, elongated shape. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
