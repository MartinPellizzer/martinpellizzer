Couma

Couma, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a tropical tree belonging to the Leguminosae family.

It is native to West Africa and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that resemble small bread loaves, which are typically cooked and consumed. The tree is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agroforestry systems.

Its seeds can be processed into a flour used in traditional cuisines.
