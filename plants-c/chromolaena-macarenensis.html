Chromolaena macarenensis

Chromolaena macarenensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian chromolaena, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a rough texture and often have a reddish or purplish underside.

It produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, making it a distinctive and sometimes invasive species in its native habitat.
