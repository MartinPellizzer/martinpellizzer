Conophytum uviforme var. subincanum

Conophytum uviforme var. subincanum, commonly known as the "lizard's tail" or "false stone plant," is a succulent native to southern Africa.

It features compact, fleshy leaves that form a rosette, often covered in a powdery white coating called farina. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
