Calliandra dendroidea

Calliandra dendroidea, commonly known as the weeping calliandra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its cascading, feathery inflorescences. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into smaller leaflets, giving it a delicate appearance.

Calliandra dendroidea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic value and is also used in some regions for soil stabilization.
