Crotalaria siamica

Crotalaria siamica, commonly known as the Siam crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Crotalaria siamica is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
