Cachrys abyssinica

Cachrys abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian heath, is a shrub native to the high-altitude regions of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many plants adapted to acidic soils. The plant features dense, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves that help reduce water loss in its arid environment. It produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique adaptations, it plays an important role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
