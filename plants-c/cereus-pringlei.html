Cereus pringlei

Cereus pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that bloom at night, a trait common among many cactus species. Its succulent stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

The plant's structure and adaptations make it well-suited for desert conditions, with minimal leaf surface to reduce water loss.
