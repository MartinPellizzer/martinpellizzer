Chrysopogon gryllus f. flavescens

Chrysopogon gryllus f. flavescens, commonly known as the golden grass or yellow bluestem, is a variant of the species Chrysopogon gryllus, which belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to yellowish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of grasses. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils, often in regions with a warm climate.

Its yellowish hue is particularly noticeable in its foliage, making it a distinctive ornamental and ecological plant.
