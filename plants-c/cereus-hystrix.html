Cereus hystrix

Cereus hystrix, commonly known as the Christ's Thorn or the Old Man of the South, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and clusters of white flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its thick, ribbed trunk and dense, white spines. It thrives in arid environments and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert conditions.

Its unique morphology and nocturnal blooming habit make it a notable species in the Cactaceae family.
