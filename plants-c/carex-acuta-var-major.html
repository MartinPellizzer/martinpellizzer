Carex acuta var. major

Carex acuta var. major, commonly known as the sharp-tongued sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces spikelets that contain small, three-lobed flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ecological importance, it serves as a habitat and food source for various insects and small animals.
