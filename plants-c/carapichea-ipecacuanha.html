Carapichea ipecacuanha

Carapichea ipecacuanha, commonly known as Brazilian ipecacuanha, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces red berries that contain a milky latex, which has been historically used in traditional medicine. Its botanical significance lies in its unique chemical composition, including compounds with emetic properties.
