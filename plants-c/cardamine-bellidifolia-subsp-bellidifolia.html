Cardamine bellidifolia subsp. bellidifolia

Cardamine bellidifolia subsp.

bellidifolia, commonly known as the bellidifolia watercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant has slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and have a peppery aroma when crushed.

The flowers are small, white or pale green, and arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
