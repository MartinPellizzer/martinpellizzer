Chamaesyce drummondii

Chamaesyce drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of less than 10 centimeters. The plant features small, fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in dense, branched inflorescences, blooming from late spring to early summer.

It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
