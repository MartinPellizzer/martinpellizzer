Clerodendrum luteopunctatum

Clerodendrum luteopunctatum, commonly known as the golden dot flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences that consist of clusters of small, tubular flowers surrounded by bright yellow bracts. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture with a waxy coating.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
