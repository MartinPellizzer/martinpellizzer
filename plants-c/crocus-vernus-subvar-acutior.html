Crocus vernus subvar. acutior

Crocus vernus subvar. acutior, commonly known as the sharp-corned crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, early spring flowers with bright yellow petals and a distinctive, three-lobed style. The plant's leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a bulbous underground stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.
