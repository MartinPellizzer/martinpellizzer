Cicca albizzioides

Cicca albizzioides, commonly known as the Indian velvet tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
