Cirsium × czepeliense

Cirsium × czepeliense, commonly known as the Polish thistle, is a hybrid plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, silvery-green leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces purple to pink flowers in composite flower heads, typical of the daisy family. It is native to Central and Eastern Europe and thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
