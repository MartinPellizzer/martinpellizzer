Cocculus laurifolius var. triplinervis

Cocculus laurifolius var. triplinervis, commonly known as the three-veined coral tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features palmately compound leaves with three to five lobes, each leaflet having a distinct midrib and three secondary veins, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and shade-providing qualities.

The species is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in warm climates.
