Centropogon oaxacanus

Centropogon oaxacanus, commonly known as the Oaxacan flame flower, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to Mexico, particularly the state of Oaxaca, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red and orange tubular flowers, which are adapted for pollination by hummingbirds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Centropogon oaxacanus is valued in horticulture for its showy blooms and ability to grow in well-drained soils.
