Cyrtopodium brunneum

Cyrtopodium brunneum, commonly known as the brown orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark brown to reddish-brown pseudobulbs and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive red or orange lip. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial shade.

Cyrtopodium brunneum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a home garden setting.
