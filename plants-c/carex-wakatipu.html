Carex wakatipu

Carex wakatipu, commonly known as the Wakatipu sedge, is a perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It plays an important role in its ecosystem by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.

Carex wakatipu is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in native landscapes.
