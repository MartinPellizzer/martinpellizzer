Corylus avellana var. taeniensis

Corylus avellana var. taeniensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hazel, is a variant of the common hazel native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically longer than they are wide. The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which are harvested in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of nutritious nuts.
