Chrysophyllum cacondense

Chrysophyllum cacondense, commonly known as the custard apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fruit that resembles a small apple in shape. The fruit has a thick, leathery skin and a sweet, creamy flesh with a mild aroma.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and edible fruit, which is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.
