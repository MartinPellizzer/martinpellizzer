Calochortus albus var. rubellus

Calochortus albus var. rubellus, commonly known as the red-flowered white globemallow, is a variant of the white globemallow native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking red petals that contrast with its pale yellow center, making it a visually distinctive species. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration.
