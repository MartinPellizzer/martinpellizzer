Croton urticifolius var. accedens

Croton urticifolius var. accedens, commonly known as the stinging croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
