Cynanchum phillipsonianum

Cynanchum phillipsonianum, commonly known as the Philippine milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly the Philippines.

It is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its milky sap, which contains latex. The plant typically grows in forested areas and has opposite, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellowish-green with a distinctive horn-shaped structure, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Cynanchum phillipsonianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
