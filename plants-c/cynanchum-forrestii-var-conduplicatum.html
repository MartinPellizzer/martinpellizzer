Cynanchum forrestii var. conduplicatum

Cynanchum forrestii var. conduplicatum, commonly known as the Chinese milkweed or forest milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its milky sap, which is a common feature among milkweeds. The plant typically grows in forested areas and has opposite, lanceolate leaves with a hairy surface. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in umbels, and are pollinated by insects.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
