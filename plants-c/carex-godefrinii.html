Carex godefrinii

Carex godefrinii, commonly known as the Godefrin's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to eastern North America and typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy, with a dense cluster of spikelets at the top. Its flowering season occurs in late spring to early summer, producing small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in cylindrical inflorescences.

Carex godefrinii plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
