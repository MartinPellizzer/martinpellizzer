Citrus × aurantium var. hierochuntica

Citrus × aurantium var. hierochuntica, commonly known as the bitter orange, is a citrus species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, peels that contain a high concentration of essential oils and bitter compounds. The plant typically grows as a small evergreen tree or shrub, with dark green, glossy leaves. Its flowers are fragrant and white, often giving way to small, round, green fruits that turn orange when mature.

The fruit is primarily used for its zest and peel in culinary and medicinal applications.
