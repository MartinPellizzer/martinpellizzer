Crataegus columbiana var. piperi

Crataegus columbiana var. piperi, commonly known as the California hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
