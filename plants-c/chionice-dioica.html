Chionice dioica

Chionice dioica, commonly known as the dioecious snowdrop, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Colchicaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a bulbous base.

Chionice dioica is notable for its separate male and female flowers on different plants, a feature known as dioecy.
