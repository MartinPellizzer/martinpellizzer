Correa calycina

Correa calycina, commonly known as the pink fairy fan, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking pink to white bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery-grey pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Correa calycina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is well-suited to coastal environments.
