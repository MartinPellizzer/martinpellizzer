Cannabis intersita

Cannabis intersita, commonly known as the "Mexican hemp," is a species of flowering plant in the Cannabaceae family.

It is native to Central America and parts of the southern United States. The plant is characterized by its slender, dioecious structure, with separate male and female plants. Its leaves are typically narrow and serrated, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers.

Cannabis intersita contains varying levels of cannabinoids, though it is generally lower in THC compared to other cannabis species.
