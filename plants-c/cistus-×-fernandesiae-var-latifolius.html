Cistus × fernandesiae var. latifolius

Cistus × fernandesiae var. latifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, alkaline soils and full sun, making it well-suited for Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are typically broad and leathery, contributing to its drought tolerance.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in rocky or coastal environments.
