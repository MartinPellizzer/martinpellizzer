Calliandra vaupesiana

Calliandra vaupesiana, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Venezuela and Colombia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive pink or red flower clusters. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and has a woody stem with thorny branches. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Calliandra vaupesiana is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
