Chimonocalamus elegans

Chimonocalamus elegans, commonly known as the elegant bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

Due to its slow growth and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
