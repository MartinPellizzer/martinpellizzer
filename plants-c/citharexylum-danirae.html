Citharexylum danirae

Citharexylum danirae, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its attractive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with opposite, simple, and often aromatic leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are not only visually appealing but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
