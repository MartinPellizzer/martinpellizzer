Cyclopogon laxiflorus

Cyclopogon laxiflorus, commonly known as the broad-flowered cyclopogon, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly found in Cuba and the Bahamas. This herbaceous plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are characterized by their broad, lance-shaped petals and a distinctive, spurred lip.

The plant is noted for its delicate appearance and is of interest to botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
