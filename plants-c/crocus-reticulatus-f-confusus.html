Crocus reticulatus f. confusus

Crocus reticulatus f. confusus, commonly known as the confused crocus, is a botanical variant of the reticulated crocus.

It is characterized by its delicate, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive reticulated pattern on the petals. This subspecies is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and thrives in alpine environments. The plant produces a bulbous corm and has narrow, grass-like leaves.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
