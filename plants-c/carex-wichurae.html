Carex wichurae

Carex wichurae, commonly known as the Wichura's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as wetlands and forest floors. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late spring to early summer, produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

Carex wichurae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
