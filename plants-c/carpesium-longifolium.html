Carpesium longifolium

Carpesium longifolium, commonly known as the narrowleaf thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It is often found in dry, disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun.

Carpesium longifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
