Clausena odorata

Clausena odorata, commonly known as the fragrant satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The tree produces yellow-orange berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Its wood is valued for its fine grain and is used in furniture and carving.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
