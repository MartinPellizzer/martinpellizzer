Crucifera cardamine

Crucifera cardamine, commonly known as white sweet clover.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, fertile soils and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets, and it produces a strong, sweet scent.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in herbal remedies.
