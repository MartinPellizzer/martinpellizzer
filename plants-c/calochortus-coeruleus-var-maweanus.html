Calochortus coeruleus var. maweanus

Calochortus coeruleus var. maweanus, commonly known as the blue glory lily, is a striking member of the Liliaceae family native to the western United States.

This perennial plant features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to deep violet, often with a contrasting yellow or white throat. It typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, such as meadows and grasslands, preferring full sun to partial shade. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a basal rosette of foliage.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant collections.
