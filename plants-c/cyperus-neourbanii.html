Cyperus neourbanii

Cyperus neourbanii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, linear leaves and small, clustered flower heads. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Cyperus neourbanii is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
