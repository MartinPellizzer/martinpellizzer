Commelina setosa

Commelina setosa, commonly known as the small blue grass or slender spiderwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Commelinales order.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and features small, tubular blue or violet flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces seeds in a capsule-like structure. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina setosa is notable for its ability to spread vegetatively through rhizomes, making it a common weed in disturbed soils.
