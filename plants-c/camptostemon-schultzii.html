Camptostemon schultzii

Camptostemon schultzii, commonly known as Schultze's blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Camptostemon schultzii is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
