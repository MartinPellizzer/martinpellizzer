Campylotropis harmsii

Campylotropis harmsii, commonly known as the Harms' bottlebrush.

It is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States, particularly in the Appalachian region. The plant is characterized by its dense, cylindrical clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
