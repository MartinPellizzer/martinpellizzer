Chenopodium benthamii subsp. rectum

Chenopodium benthamii subsp.

rectum, commonly known as the narrowleaf goosefoot, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and alternate, narrow leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

The species is notable for its potential as a drought-tolerant plant with possible ecological and agricultural applications.
