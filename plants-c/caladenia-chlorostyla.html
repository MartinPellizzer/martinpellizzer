Caladenia chlorostyla

Caladenia chlorostyla, commonly known as the blue-tongued orchid, is an Australian native orchid species characterized by its distinctive blue-violet petals and a prominent, tongue-like lip.

It belongs to the orchid family Orchidaceae and is endemic to the southeastern regions of Australia. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, often in sandy or rocky soils. Its flowers are usually pale blue to white, with a striking contrast provided by the deep blue throat.

Caladenia chlorostyla is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in clusters, making it a popular subject for botanists and plant enthusiasts.
