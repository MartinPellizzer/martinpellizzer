Clematis douglasii var. scottii

Clematis douglasii var. scottii, commonly known as the Scott's Clematis, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous vine that produces showy, blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden settings.

Its botanical characteristics include a climbing habit and a long blooming period that extends into early autumn.
