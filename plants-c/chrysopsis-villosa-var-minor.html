Chrysopsis villosa var. minor

Chrysopsis villosa var. minor, commonly known as the slender goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features slender, erect stems covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming in late summer. Its leaves are linear and covered with a similar dense pubescence, contributing to its distinctive look.

It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.
