Crotalaria biflora

Crotalaria biflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive two-flowered inflorescences. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with prominent, spurred petals, and its leaves are typically trifoliate. It grows as an annual herb, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Crotalaria biflora is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
