Calanthe flavicans

Calanthe flavicans, commonly known as the yellow calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow and green floral colors, which are particularly vivid in its native habitat. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial shade. Its leaves are usually broad and ovate, with a smooth texture, and it produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers.

Calanthe flavicans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
