Carex loncholepis

Carex loncholepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are usually arranged in a tight spiral, and it produces small, clustered flower spikes during the summer months.

Carex loncholepis is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
