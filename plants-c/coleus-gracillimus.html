Coleus gracillimus

Coleus gracillimus, commonly known as the slender coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a soft texture. Its flowers are typically tubular and come in shades of red, pink, and purple, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Coleus gracillimus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and colorful blooms.
