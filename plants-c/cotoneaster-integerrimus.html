Cotoneaster integerrimus

Cotoneaster integerrimus, commonly known as the Sierra cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It is often found in rocky slopes and woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.

Cotoneaster integerrimus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
