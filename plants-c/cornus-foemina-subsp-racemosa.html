Cornus foemina subsp. racemosa

Cornus foemina subsp.

racemosa, commonly known as the racemose cornel, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly toothed margin.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
