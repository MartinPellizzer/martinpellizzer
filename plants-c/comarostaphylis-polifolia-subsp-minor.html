Comarostaphylis polifolia subsp. minor

Comarostaphylis polifolia subsp.

minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaved stonecress, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in open, sunny habitats.

The plant features small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
