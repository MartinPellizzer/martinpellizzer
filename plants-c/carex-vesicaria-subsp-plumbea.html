Carex vesicaria subsp. plumbea

Carex vesicaria subsp.

plumbea, commonly known as the plumbea sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are usually bluish-green in color, which distinguishes it from other sedges in the genus.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support wetland biodiversity.
