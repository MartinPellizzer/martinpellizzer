Cynara sibthorpiana

Cynara sibthorpiana, commonly known as the African artichoke, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and purple flowers. The plant produces edible artichoke-like buds that are used in various cuisines. It thrives in warm, dry climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary value.

Cynara sibthorpiana is also valued for its ability to withstand drought conditions.
