Carthamus ambiguus

Carthamus ambiguus, commonly known as the ambiguous thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features spiny leaves and yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical structure supports its role in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
