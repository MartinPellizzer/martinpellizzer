Caladenia longicauda subsp. calcigena

Caladenia longicauda subsp.

calcigena, commonly known as the rock-crevice spider-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stem and a single, lance-shaped leaf.

The flower spike bears one or two small, spider-like flowers with a distinctive greenish-brown hue and intricate markings. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

This subspecies is noted for its specialized pollination mechanism, attracting specific insect pollinators.
