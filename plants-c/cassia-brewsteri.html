Cassia brewsteri

Cassia brewsteri, commonly known as the desert cassia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy species for xeriscaping.

The leaves and flowers are often used in traditional medicine by indigenous communities.
