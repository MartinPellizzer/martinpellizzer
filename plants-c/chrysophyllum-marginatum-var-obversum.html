Chrysophyllum marginatum var. obversum

Chrysophyllum marginatum var. obversum, commonly known as the white-leafed starfruit, is a tropical plant belonging to the Sapotaceae family.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically white or pale green on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into star-shaped fruits with a distinctive waxy coating. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
