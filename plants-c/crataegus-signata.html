Crataegus signata

Crataegus signata, commonly known as the showy crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated margins, contributing to its ornamental value.

Crataegus signata is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits to wildlife.
