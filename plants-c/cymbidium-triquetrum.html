Cymbidium triquetrum

Cymbidium triquetrum, commonly known as the three-lobed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with tall, upright stems that bear clusters of fragrant, lance-shaped flowers. The leaves are broad and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern. The flowers typically bloom in late winter to early spring, adding a striking visual element to gardens.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its elegant, long-lasting blooms.
