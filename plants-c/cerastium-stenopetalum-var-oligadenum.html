Cerastium stenopetalum var. oligadenum

Cerastium stenopetalum var. oligadenum, commonly known as the narrow-petaled mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate habit and small, white flowers. The leaves are typically fleshy and succulent, adapted to survive in dry or rocky environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.
