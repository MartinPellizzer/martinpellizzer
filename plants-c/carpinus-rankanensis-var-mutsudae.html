Carpinus rankanensis var. mutsudae

Carpinus rankanensis var. mutsudae, commonly known as the Japanese hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, winged samaras that are dispersed by wind. It typically grows to a height of 5-10 meters and has a compact, rounded crown.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
