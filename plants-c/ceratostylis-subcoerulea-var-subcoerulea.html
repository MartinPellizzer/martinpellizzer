Ceratostylis subcoerulea var. subcoerulea

Ceratostylis subcoerulea var. subcoerulea, commonly known as the blue-flowered orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial orchid, often found in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

The flowers are small, fragrant, and bloom in clusters, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
