Costus gabonensis

Costus gabonensis, commonly known as the Gabon costus, is a perennial herb native to Central and Southern Africa.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its aromatic rhizomes. The plant features tall, narrow leaves and produces showy, spike-like inflorescences with pale yellow to orange flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
