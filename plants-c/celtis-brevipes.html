Celtis brevipes

Celtis brevipes, commonly known as the Mexican hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers followed by small, round berries that are edible for some animals but not typically for humans.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, making it a resilient and adaptable species.
