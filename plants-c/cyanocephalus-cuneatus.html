Cyanocephalus cuneatus

Cyanocephalus cuneatus, commonly known as the blue-headed cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its short, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments and possesses a deep root system to access water. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Cyanocephalus cuneatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with suitable climates.
