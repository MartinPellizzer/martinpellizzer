Cissus quadrialata

Cissus quadrialata, commonly known as the four-leafed cissus, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that often exhibit a striking variegation of green and white. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to trail or climb make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor decoration.
