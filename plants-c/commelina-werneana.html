Commelina werneana

Commelina werneana, commonly known as the purple arum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelina family, which includes many species with showy, often colorful inflorescences. The plant features a striking violet or purple spathe that encloses a cluster of small, white to yellowish flowers. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a waxy surface that aids in water retention.

Commelina werneana is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.
