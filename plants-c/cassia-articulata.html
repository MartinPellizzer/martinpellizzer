Cassia articulata

Cassia articulata, commonly known as the slender cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant has a slender, branching stem and is often found in wetlands and along waterways. Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods, and it is known for its ability to thrive in moist environments.

Cassia articulata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
