Carduncellus monspelliensium var. caulescens

Carduncellus monspelliensium var. caulescens, commonly known as the prickly lettuce, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, ribbed stems and broad, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, prickly hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a cluster, which are followed by spherical, spiky seed heads. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural settings.
