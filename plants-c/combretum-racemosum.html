Combretum racemosum

Combretum racemosum, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, racemose inflorescences that bear small, white, fragrant flowers. The leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain one or two seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
