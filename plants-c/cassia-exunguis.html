Cassia exunguis

Cassia exunguis, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia exunguis is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
