Carex bromoides

Carex bromoides, commonly known as flatleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically dark green and densely packed. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks.

Carex bromoides is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
