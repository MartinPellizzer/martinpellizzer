Carduus hymenolepis

Carduus hymenolepis, commonly known as the hairy cardoon, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its robust growth habit and distinctive appearance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
