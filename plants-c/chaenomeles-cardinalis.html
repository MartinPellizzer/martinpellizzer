Chaenomeles cardinalis

Chaenomeles cardinalis, commonly known as the Chinese flowering quince, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, single or double pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, yellow, edible fruits in the fall, though they are typically not consumed by humans. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly serrated margin, while its bark is smooth and reddish-brown.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
