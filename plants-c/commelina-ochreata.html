Commelina ochreata

Commelina ochreata, commonly known as the yellow arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with white veins. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often near water bodies. Its leaves are arrow-shaped, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract various pollinators.

Commelina ochreata is often used in ornamental gardening due to its vibrant color and adaptability to moist environments.
