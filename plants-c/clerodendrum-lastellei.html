Clerodendrum lastellei

Clerodendrum lastellei, commonly known as the red-flowered clerodendrum, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, dark berries that contain the seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and diverse floral structures.
