Centaurea jacea subsp. lusitanica

Centaurea jacea subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian knapweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features erect, branching stems that can reach up to 1 meter in height. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are bright red with yellow centers.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive floral structure and is often found in Mediterranean climates.
