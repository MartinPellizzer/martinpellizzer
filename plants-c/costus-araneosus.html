Costus araneosus

Costus araneosus, commonly known as the spider costus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking, feather-like leaves that resemble spider legs, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often with a reddish or orange hue.

Costus araneosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
