Calanthe laciniata

Calanthe laciniata, commonly known as the slit-leaf calanthe, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features lanceolate, serrated leaves that are often dark green in color. Its flowers are typically pale green to white, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in hue.

Calanthe laciniata thrives in shaded, moist environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
