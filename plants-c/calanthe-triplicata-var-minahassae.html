Calanthe triplicata var. minahassae

Calanthe triplicata var. minahassae, commonly known as the Minahassa calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, upright growth habit and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant typically features three to five lance-shaped leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are borne on terminal racemes and are known for their intricate, spurred structure.

This orchid thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil for optimal growth.
