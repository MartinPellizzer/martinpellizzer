Chenopodina splendens

Chenopodina splendens, commonly known as the shining chenopod, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows from a short, thick rootstock and has a branching structure that allows it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are often coated with a waxy layer, which helps reduce water loss and adapt to extreme conditions.

Chenopodina splendens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with suitable climates.
