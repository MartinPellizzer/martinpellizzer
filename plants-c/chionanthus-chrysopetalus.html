Chionanthus chrysopetalus

Chionanthus chrysopetalus, commonly known as the goldenflower viburnum, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white to yellowish flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and turn red or purple in the fall. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

Chionanthus chrysopetalus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate floral display.
