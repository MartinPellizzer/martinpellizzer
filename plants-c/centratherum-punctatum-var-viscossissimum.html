Centratherum punctatum var. viscossissimum

Centratherum punctatum var. viscossissimum, commonly known as the sticky centatherum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that are densely covered with a sticky, resinous substance, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance and resilience.
