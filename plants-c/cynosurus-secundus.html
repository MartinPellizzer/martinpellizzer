Cynosurus secundus

Cynosurus secundus, commonly known as slender wood sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tufted at the base, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Cynosurus secundus is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various invertebrates.
