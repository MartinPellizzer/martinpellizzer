Cordia splendida

Cordia splendida, commonly known as the shining cordia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. Its fruit is a distinctive, fleshy capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Cordia splendida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in various cultures.
