Centaurea × spuria

Centaurea × spuria, commonly known as the common centaury, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and has linear, hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Centaurea × spuria is known for its ornamental value and has been cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
