Carex fumosa

Carex fumosa, commonly known as smoky sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its dark, almost blackish stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments.

Carex fumosa produces small, clustered flower heads that are often hidden among the foliage.
