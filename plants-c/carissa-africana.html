Carissa africana

Carissa africana, commonly known as the yellow bell bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces clusters of yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The plant is notable for its fragrant blooms and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and arid regions.

Carissa africana is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
