Convolvulus cynanchifolius

Convolvulus cynanchifolius, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a climbing vine with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. The flowers are typically pale blue or purple, with five petals and a distinctive spurred structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
