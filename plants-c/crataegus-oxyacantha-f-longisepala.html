Crataegus oxyacantha f. longisepala

Crataegus oxyacantha f. longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its elongated sepals that persist through flowering, giving it a distinctive appearance. This form is typically found in woodland areas and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
