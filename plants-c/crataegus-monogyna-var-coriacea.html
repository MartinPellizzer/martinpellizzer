Crataegus monogyna var. coriacea

Crataegus monogyna var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough-fruited hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves with serrated edges, and small, white or pinkish flowers that appear in spring. The fruit is a red, fleshy berry that matures in late summer, providing food for birds and other wildlife.

This variety is often found in hedgerows, woodlands, and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
