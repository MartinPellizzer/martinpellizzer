Casearia hypoleuca

Casearia hypoleuca, commonly known as the white casearia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
