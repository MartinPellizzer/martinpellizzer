Convolvulus erosus

Convolvulus erosus, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it often grows along the ground, forming dense mats. It is native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and sunny areas.

The flowers bloom in the early morning, hence its common name, and the plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
