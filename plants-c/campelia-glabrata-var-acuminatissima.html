Campelia glabrata var. acuminatissima

Campelia glabrata var. acuminatissima, commonly known as the Acuminata Campelia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

The varietal name 'acuminatissima' refers to its sharply pointed leaves, a distinctive feature of this botanical variant.
