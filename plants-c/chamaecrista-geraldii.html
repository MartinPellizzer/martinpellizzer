Chamaecrista geraldii

Chamaecrista geraldii, commonly known as the narrowleaf burr legume, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves, which are typically narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, followed by distinctive, bean-like pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.

Chamaecrista geraldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
