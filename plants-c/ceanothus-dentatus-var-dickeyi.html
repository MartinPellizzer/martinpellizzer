Ceanothus dentatus var. dickeyi

Ceanothus dentatus var. dickeyi, commonly known as the Dickey's ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats. It has small, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its structure is compact with multiple stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
