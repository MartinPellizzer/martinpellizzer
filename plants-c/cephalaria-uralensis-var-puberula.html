Cephalaria uralensis var. puberula

Cephalaria uralensis var. puberula, commonly known as the hairy cephalaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.
