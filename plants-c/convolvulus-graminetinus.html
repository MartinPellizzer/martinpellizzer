Convolvulus graminetinus

Convolvulus graminetinus, commonly known as the grass-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that resemble grass blades. The plant produces vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas or along roadsides.

Convolvulus graminetinus is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar climbing or trailing growth habits.
