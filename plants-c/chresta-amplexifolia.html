Chresta amplexifolia

Chresta amplexifolia, commonly known as the clasping leaf plant, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaves that wrap around the stems, giving it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Chresta amplexifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blossoms.
