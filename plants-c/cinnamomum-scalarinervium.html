Cinnamomum scalarinervium

Cinnamomum scalarinervium, commonly known as the Philippine cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, fragrant leaves and bark. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by elliptical, dark green berries. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a distinctive venation pattern, and the bark is thick and cinnamon-scented.

This species is valued for its essential oils and has traditional uses in medicine and perfumery.
