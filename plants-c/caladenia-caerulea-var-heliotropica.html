Caladenia caerulea var. heliotropica

Caladenia caerulea var. heliotropica, commonly known as the blue-veined spider orchid, is a striking orchid species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, violet-blue flowers with intricate veining. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils in open woodland or heathland environments. It blooms during the autumn season, producing a single or occasionally two flowers per stem.

This variety is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
