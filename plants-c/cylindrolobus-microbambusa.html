Cylindrolobus microbambusa

Cylindrolobus microbambusa, commonly known as the narrow bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems that grow in dense clumps, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and arranged in a whorled pattern around the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or wetland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
