Costus linearis

Costus linearis, commonly known as the slender costus, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers, and is characterized by its upright, narrow leaves and tall, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in dense clusters. Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.
