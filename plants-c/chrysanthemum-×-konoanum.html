Chrysanthemum × konoanum

Chrysanthemum × konoanum, commonly known as the Japanese chrysanthemum, is a hybrid species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its diverse range of flower colors, including white, yellow, red, and purple, and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of 30 to 100 centimeters. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are composed of ray and disc florets.

This species is well adapted to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
