Calocephalus lacteus

Calocephalus lacteus, commonly known as the milkhead plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that exude a milky sap when damaged, giving it its common name. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
