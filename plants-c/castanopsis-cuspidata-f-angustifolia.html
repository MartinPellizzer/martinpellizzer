Castanopsis cuspidata f. angustifolia

Castanopsis cuspidata f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved tanoak, is a variant of the tanoak tree native to the temperate rainforests of western North America.

It is a deciduous evergreen shrub or small tree characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing one or two seeds. Its leaves are typically arranged alternately along the stems, and the plant exhibits a slow growth rate.

Castanopsis cuspidata f. angustifolia plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
