Crucifera erophila

Crucifera erophila, commonly known as the slender mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and pinnately compound leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its seeds are small and contained within siliques, which are dry, dehiscent fruits.

Crucifera erophila plays a role in supporting local pollinators and contributes to the biodiversity of its habitat.
