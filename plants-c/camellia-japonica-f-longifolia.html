Camellia japonica f. longifolia

Camellia japonica f. longifolia, commonly known as the Japanese camellia with long petals, is a botanical variant of the Japanese camellia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow petals that give it a distinctive, elegant appearance. This evergreen shrub produces large, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial shade to full sun.

The plant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
