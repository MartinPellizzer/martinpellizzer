Cerasus rhexii

Cerasus rhexii, commonly known as the Japanese cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, tart cherries that are typically not suitable for eating but are valued for ornamental purposes. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Cerasus rhexii is widely cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and parks.
