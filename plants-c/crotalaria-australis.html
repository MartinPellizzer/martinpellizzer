Crotalaria australis

Crotalaria australis, commonly known as the Australian crownflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that resemble a crown. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal regions. Its leaves are palmately compound, and it produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds.

Crotalaria australis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
