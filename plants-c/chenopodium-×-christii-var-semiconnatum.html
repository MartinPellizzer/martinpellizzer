Chenopodium × christii var. semiconnatum

Chenopodium × christii var. semiconnatum, commonly known as the desert spinach, is a hybrid plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial with succulent, fleshy leaves that are edible and rich in nutrients. The plant features small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, and it is well adapted to hot, dry environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional cuisines for their mild, spinach-like flavor.

The plant's ability to thrive in poor soil conditions makes it a valuable resource in xeriscaping and sustainable agriculture.
