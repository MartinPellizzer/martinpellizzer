Cucurbita lagenaria var. oblonga

Cucurbita lagenaria var. oblonga, commonly known as the bottle gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is characterized by its elongated, cylindrical shape.

The plant produces large, smooth, green fruits that can grow up to several feet in length. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil. The bottle gourd is typically cultivated for its edible young fruits, which are used in various culinary applications.

Its rapid growth and vine-like habit make it a popular choice for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
