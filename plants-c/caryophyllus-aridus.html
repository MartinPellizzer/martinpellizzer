Caryophyllus aridus

Caryophyllus aridus, commonly known as the desert pink, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to access groundwater in dry conditions.

Caryophyllus aridus is often used in traditional medicine by Indigenous Australian communities for its purported healing properties.
