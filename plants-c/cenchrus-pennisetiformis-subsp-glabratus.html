Cenchrus pennisetiformis subsp. glabratus

Cenchrus pennisetiformis subsp.

glabratus, commonly known as the smooth false panicgrass, is a grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is characterized by its erect, cylindrical stems and narrow, linear leaves that are typically hairless.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to various environmental conditions, contributing to its widespread distribution.
