Centaurea aplolepa var. subciliata

Centaurea aplolepa var. subciliata, commonly known as the subciliate centaurea, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils.

This variety is distinguished by its finely ciliate (fringed) margins on the leaves, giving it its specific epithet.
