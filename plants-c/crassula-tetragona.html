Crassula tetragona

Crassula tetragona, commonly known as the Four-angled Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, four-sided stems that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with plenty of sunlight.

Crassula tetragona is often grown as a decorative plant due to its compact size and attractive appearance.
