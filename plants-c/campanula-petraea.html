Campanula petraea

Campanula petraea, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to rocky and alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, hairy leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh, windy conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in mountainous and rocky habitats.

Campanula petraea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings.
