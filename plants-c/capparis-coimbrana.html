Capparis coimbrana

Capparis coimbrana, commonly known as the Coimbra caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and rocky soils, thriving in Mediterranean climates. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fleshy, edible fruits. Its leaves are broad and succulent, adapted to conserve water in arid conditions.

Capparis coimbrana is part of the Capparaceae family, which includes other caper species known for their distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.
