Carex paludosa f. depauperata

Carex paludosa f. depauperata, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a variant of the common sedge found in wetland habitats.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
