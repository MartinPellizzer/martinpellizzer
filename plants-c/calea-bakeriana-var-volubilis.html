Calea bakeriana var. volubilis

Calea bakeriana var. volubilis, commonly known as the creeping calea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow ray florets and dark purple disk florets. The plant has a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for ground cover in shaded areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
