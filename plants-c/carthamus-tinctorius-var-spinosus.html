Carthamus tinctorius var. spinosus

Carthamus tinctorius var. spinosus, commonly known as the thorned safflower, is a variant of the safflower plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and leaves, which distinguish it from other varieties of Carthamus. The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its botanical features include a taproot system and hairy leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and resilience.
