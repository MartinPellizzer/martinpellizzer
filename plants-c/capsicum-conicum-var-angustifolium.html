Capsicum conicum var. angustifolium

Capsicum conicum var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chili, is a variant of the bird's eye chili pepper.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves and small, fiery red or orange fruits. This plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in well-drained soil and full sun. Its botanical features include small, white flowers and a compact growth habit.

It is often cultivated for its pungent, spicy flavor used in various culinary applications.
