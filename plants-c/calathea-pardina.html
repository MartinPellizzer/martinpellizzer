Calathea pardina

Calathea pardina, commonly known as the leopard calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display a mix of green, silver, and pink hues. The foliage is often adorned with intricate patterns resembling a leopard's spots, giving it its common name. This plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

It is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
