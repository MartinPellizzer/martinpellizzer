Clematis bathiei

Clematis bathiei, commonly known as the Yellow Clematis, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, actinomorphic flowers with five sepals and five petals. Clematis bathiei is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its vibrant blooms and relatively easy care requirements.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun exposure for optimal growth.
