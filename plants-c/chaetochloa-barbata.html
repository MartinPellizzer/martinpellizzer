Chaetochloa barbata

Chaetochloa barbata, commonly known as the hairy grass or barbata grass, is a perennial plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves that are often covered in short, stiff hairs, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, cylindrical flower clusters.

Chaetochloa barbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
