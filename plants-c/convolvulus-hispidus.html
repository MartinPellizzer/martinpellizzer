Convolvulus hispidus

Convolvulus hispidus, commonly known as the hairy bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with slender stems that can twine around other plants or structures. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale purple to white in color, often with a distinct veining pattern. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture.

Convolvulus hispidus is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, and it thrives in a variety of soil types, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.
