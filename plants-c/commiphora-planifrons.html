Commiphora planifrons

Commiphora planifrons, commonly known as the flat-leaved myrrh tree, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a fragrance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland evergreen forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and commercial purposes.
