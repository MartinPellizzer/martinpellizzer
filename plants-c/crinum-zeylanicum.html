Crinum zeylanicum

Crinum zeylanicum, commonly known as the Ceylon lily, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces large, showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. The plant has strap-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

Crinum zeylanicum is also known for its ability to tolerate partial shade and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
