Convolvulus pubescens

Convolvulus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves covered in fine, hairy textures. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of purple, pink, or white. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Convolvulus pubescens is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb or trail along structures.
