Camellia multisepala

Camellia multisepala, commonly known as the many-sepaled camellia, is a species native to China and is part of the Theaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and can grow as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with multiple petals and sepals that give it its common name.

Camellia multisepala is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its winter blooms.
