Cynanchum pachylepis

Cynanchum pachylepis, commonly known as the broad-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing seeds with silky hairs. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for various ailments.
