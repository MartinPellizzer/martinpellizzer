Chrysodracon aurea

Chrysodracon aurea, commonly known as the golden dragon tree, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble dragon scales, giving it its common name. The plant features a woody stem with a smooth, pale bark that becomes more textured with age. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a glossy, dark green surface.

Chrysodracon aurea thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
