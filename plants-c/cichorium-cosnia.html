Cichorium cosnia

Cichorium cosnia, commonly known as the Armenian chicory, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright, hairy stems and basal leaves that are spoon-shaped with wavy margins. The plant produces blue to violet flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their bitter compounds.

Cichorium cosnia is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
