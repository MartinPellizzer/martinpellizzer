Capparis grandiflora var. annamensis

Capparis grandiflora var. annamensis, commonly known as the Annam mountain caper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly succulent texture.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.
