Crocodilodes microcephala

Crocodilodes microcephala, commonly known as the dwarf crocodile orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, compact growth habit and unique, intricate floral structures that resemble miniature crocodiles, giving it its distinctive common name. The plant produces fragrant, violet-colored flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting specific pollinators such as certain species of beetles and moths. Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is classified as endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and ecological role within its native ecosystem.
