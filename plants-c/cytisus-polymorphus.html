Cytisus polymorphus

Cytisus polymorphus, commonly known as the variable broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its leaves are small, narrow, and usually have a slightly hairy texture.

Cytisus polymorphus is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
