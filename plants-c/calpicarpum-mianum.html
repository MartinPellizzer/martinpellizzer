Calpicarpum mianum

Calpicarpum mianum, commonly known as the Chinese sand pear, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by yellowish-green fruits that mature in late summer. Calpicarpum mianum is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun exposure for optimal growth.
