Chasmone cuneifolia

Chasmone cuneifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chasmone, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It is a perennial herb that thrives in full sun and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
