Cestrum pittieri

Cestrum pittieri, commonly known as the red cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet, with opposite, simple, ovate leaves. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Cestrum pittieri is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil and full sun.
