Crataegus pruinosa f. mecocantha

Crataegus pruinosa f. mecocantha, commonly known as the white-flowered hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its white flowers and smooth, often glabrous leaves. The plant is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. It produces small, red berries that are edible but not particularly palatable.

This form is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
