Capparis bequaertii

Capparis bequaertii, commonly known as the Madagascar caper, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and thorny branches. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically followed by green, spherical fruits. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Capparis bequaertii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
