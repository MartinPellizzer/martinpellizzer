Centaurea cyanus subsp. coa

Centaurea cyanus subsp.

coa, commonly known as the blue cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, conical clusters.

The plant has hairy, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas. Centaurea cyanus subsp.

coa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
