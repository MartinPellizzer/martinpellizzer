Campanula adscendens

Campanula adscendens, commonly known as the ascending bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy as well.

Campanula adscendens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
