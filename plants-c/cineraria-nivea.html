Cineraria nivea

Cineraria nivea, commonly known as the white cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale blue, bloom in spring and summer.

Cineraria nivea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
