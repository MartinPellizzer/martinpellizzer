Cestrum cobanense

Cestrum cobanense, commonly known as the Coban nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and fragrant, tubular flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Cestrum cobanense is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
