Carex laevigata var. welwitschii

Carex laevigata var. welwitschii, commonly known as the Welwitsch's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth, waxy surface, and it produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various aquatic and semi-aquatic environments.
