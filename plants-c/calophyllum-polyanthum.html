Calophyllum polyanthum

Calophyllum polyanthum, commonly known as the fragrant evergreen or golden flame tree, is a tropical plant belonging to the Clusiaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking, fragrant yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round, orange-red fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Calophyllum polyanthum is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
