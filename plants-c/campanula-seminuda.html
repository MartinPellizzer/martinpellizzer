Campanula seminuda

Campanula seminuda, commonly known as the naked bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
