Carex gunniana var. brevior

Carex gunniana var. brevior, commonly known as the short-stemmed sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically forms dense clumps of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland and mountain ecosystems.
