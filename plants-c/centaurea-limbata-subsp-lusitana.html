Centaurea limbata subsp. lusitana

Centaurea limbata subsp.

lusitana, commonly known as the Lusitanian cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is a herbaceous perennial with upright stems and bright yellow to orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a dense, composite head.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
