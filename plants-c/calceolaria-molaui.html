Calceolaria molaui

Calceolaria molaui, commonly known as the Molaui slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates, thriving in rocky soils. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and moths.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
