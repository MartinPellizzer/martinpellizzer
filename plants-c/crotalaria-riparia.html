Crotalaria riparia

Crotalaria riparia, commonly known as the river cowpea, is a herbaceous annual plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume features such as trifoliate leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils, thriving in wetland environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a distinctive spurred structure at the base of the petals.

The seeds are enclosed in slender, elongated pods and are dispersed by water, contributing to its natural distribution.
