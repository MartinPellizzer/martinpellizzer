Cestrum frutescens

Cestrum frutescens, commonly known as night blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its fruits are capsules that split open to release seeds, and the plant is known for its ornamental value and medicinal uses.

Cestrum frutescens thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its showy, night-blooming flowers.
