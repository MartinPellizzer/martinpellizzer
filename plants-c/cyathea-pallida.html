Cyathea pallida

Cyathea pallida, commonly known as the pale tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and fronds that are usually bipinnate. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests. Its spores are produced on the undersides of its fronds, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

Cyathea pallida is valued for its ornamental appearance and its role in tropical ecosystems.
