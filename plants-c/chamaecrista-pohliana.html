Chamaecrista pohliana

Chamaecrista pohliana, commonly known as the yellow burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in open woods or along roadsides.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
