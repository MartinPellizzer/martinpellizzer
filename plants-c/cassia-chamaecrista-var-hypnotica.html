Cassia chamaecrista var. hypnotica

Cassia chamaecrista var. hypnotica, commonly known as the hypnotic cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and clusters of bright yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a distinctive addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
