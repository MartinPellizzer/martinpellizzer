Cyathea elegans

Cyathea elegans, commonly known as the elegant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth rate and longevity. Its fronds are typically a deep green, with a slightly waxy texture, and it produces spores on the undersides of its fronds.

Cyathea elegans is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
