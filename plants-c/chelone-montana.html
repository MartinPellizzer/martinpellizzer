Chelone montana

Chelone montana, commonly known as turtlehead, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular flowers that resemble a turtle's head, typically blooming in shades of purple and pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland areas.

Chelone montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing nectar for pollinators.
