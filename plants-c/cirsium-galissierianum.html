Cirsium galissierianum

Cirsium galissierianum, commonly known as the Galissard thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in Mediterranean climates. It produces achenes as its fruit, which are dispersed by wind.

Due to its habitat and appearance, it is often considered a characteristic species of its native region.
