Chabraea serpyllifolia

Chabraea serpyllifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf chabraea, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage due to a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of white and yellow, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Chabraea serpyllifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
