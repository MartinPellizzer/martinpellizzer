Ceanothus papillosus var. floribundus

Ceanothus papillosus var. floribundus, commonly known as the showy ceanothus, is a flowering shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and Mediterranean climates. Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant blossoms and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is also valued for its drought tolerance and use in landscaping and restoration projects.
