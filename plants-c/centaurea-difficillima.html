Centaurea difficillima

Centaurea difficillima, commonly known as the purple centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and spiny, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Centaurea difficillima is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
