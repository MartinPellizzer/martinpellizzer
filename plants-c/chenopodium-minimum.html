Chenopodium minimum

Chenopodium minimum, commonly known as purslane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, fleshy leaves, and small yellow flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and gardens, and is highly adaptable to various environmental conditions. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly tart flavor, making it a popular ingredient in some cuisines.

Additionally, purslane is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions due to its succulent nature.
