Castanea sloanea

Castanea sloanea, commonly known as the Sloane's chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and other chestnut species. The tree produces small, spiny capsules that contain one to three chestnut-like seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
