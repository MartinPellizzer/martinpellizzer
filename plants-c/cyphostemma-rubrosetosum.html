Cyphostemma rubrosetosum

Cyphostemma rubrosetosum, commonly known as the red-hairy cyphostemma, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and distinctive red, hairy tendrils that aid in its climbing habit. The leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a rough texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are adapted for animal dispersal.

This plant is often found in rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
