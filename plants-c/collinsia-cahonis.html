Collinsia cahonis

Collinsia cahonis, commonly known as the Sierra Valley bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branching.

Collinsia cahonis is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
