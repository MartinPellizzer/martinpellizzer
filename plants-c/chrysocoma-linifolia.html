Chrysocoma linifolia

Chrysocoma linifolia, commonly known as the whitebark tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, white bark and elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by woody, cone-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a notable species in its native region.
