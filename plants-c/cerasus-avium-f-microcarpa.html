Cerasus avium f. microcarpa

Cerasus avium f. microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or miniature cherry, is a compact, ornamental variant of the common cherry.

It features small, rounded leaves and produces diminutive, fragrant flowers in spring. The plant is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This form is prized for its unique size and ability to add visual interest to smaller garden spaces.
