Clematis indivisa

Clematis indivisa, commonly known as the smooth clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically in late spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

Clematis indivisa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens for its climbing habit and seasonal blooms.
