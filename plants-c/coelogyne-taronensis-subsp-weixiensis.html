Coelogyne taronensis subsp. weixiensis

Coelogyne taronensis subsp.

weixiensis, commonly known as the Weixian coelogyne, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to southern China. It is characterized by its striking, fragrant orchid flowers that bloom in clusters on long, arching inflorescences.

The plant typically grows as an epiphyte on trees or rocks, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to conserve moisture in its natural habitat.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
