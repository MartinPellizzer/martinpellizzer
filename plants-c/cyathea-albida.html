Cyathea albida

Cyathea albida, commonly known as the white tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a fibrous, white to pale brown rind. The plant has a large, compound frond that can grow up to several meters in length, with a feathery appearance. Cyathea albida thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique structure and slow growth make it a popular ornamental plant in horticulture.
