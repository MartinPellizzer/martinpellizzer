Crinum longifolium

Crinum longifolium, commonly known as the giant swamp lily, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer, often with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically bluish-green in color and can grow up to several feet in length. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as swamps and marshes, and is often found near water bodies.

Crinum longifolium is a perennial species that can reach heights of up to 6 feet, making it a notable presence in its native habitat.
