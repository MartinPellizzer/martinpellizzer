Chionochloa flavicans f. temata

Chionochloa flavicans f. temata, commonly known as the Temate grass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage. The plant typically forms dense mats on rocky or alpine slopes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often tinged with reddish hues.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
