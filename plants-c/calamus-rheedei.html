Calamus rheedei

Calamus rheedei, commonly known as the red sandalwood bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnate leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine. Calamus rheedei is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in the production of essential oils.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a habitat for various wildlife species and its contribution to forest biodiversity.
