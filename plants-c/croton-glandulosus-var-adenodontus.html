Croton glandulosus var. adenodontus

Croton glandulosus var. adenodontus, commonly known as the hairy croton, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves that are typically oval to lance-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forest areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in horticulture.
