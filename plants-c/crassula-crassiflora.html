Crassula crassiflora

Crassula crassiflora, commonly known as the fat leaf plant or rock jade, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a bluish or reddish tinge, which help it store water. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or indoor cultivation.

Crassula crassiflora is prized for its compact growth habit and low maintenance requirements.
