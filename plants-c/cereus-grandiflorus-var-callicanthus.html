Cereus grandiflorus var. callicanthus

Cereus grandiflorus var. callicanthus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and spiny, ribbed structure. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance. These flowers typically bloom during the summer months and are followed by small, red fruits.

It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
