Ceanothus integerrimus var. parryi

Ceanothus integerrimus var. parryi, commonly known as Parry's ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Nevada.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and sunny habitats, such as chaparral and coastal scrub. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a dense cover that supports local wildlife.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
