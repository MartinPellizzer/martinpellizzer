Chiococca racemosa var. coriacea

Chiococca racemosa var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough-stemmed chiococca, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a climbing shrub that typically grows in humid, shaded environments within cloud forests. The plant features elongated, opposite leaves that are often covered in a rough, textured surface. Its flowers are small, clustered in racemes, and are usually white or pale green.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage structure.
