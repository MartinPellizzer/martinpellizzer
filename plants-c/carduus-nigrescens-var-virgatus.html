Carduus nigrescens var. virgatus

Carduus nigrescens var. virgatus, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. It has a deep taproot system, which helps it survive in arid conditions.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses in herbal remedies.
