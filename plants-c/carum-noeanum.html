Carum noeanum

Carum noeanum, commonly known as Persian cumin, is an aromatic herb native to the mountains of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white or greenish flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are highly valued for their distinct flavor and aroma. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Carum noeanum thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for temperate climates.
