Cecropia amphichlora

Cecropia amphichlora, commonly known as the two-leaved ceiba, is a fast-growing tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, three-lobed fruits containing several seeds.
