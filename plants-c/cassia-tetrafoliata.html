Cassia tetrafoliata

Cassia tetrafoliata, commonly known as the four-leaf cassia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which typically have four leaflets, giving it its common name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia tetrafoliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some pharmacological applications.
