Convolvulus elegans

Convolvulus elegans, commonly known as the elegant bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar climbing or trailing growth patterns.

Convolvulus elegans is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
