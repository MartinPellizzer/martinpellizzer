Caesalpinia dasyrhachis

Caesalpinia dasyrhachis, commonly known as the woolly ticktree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often covered in a dense, woolly pubescence. The plant produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruits are woody pods that contain several seeds.

Caesalpinia dasyrhachis is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
