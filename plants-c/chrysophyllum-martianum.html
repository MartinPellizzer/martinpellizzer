Chrysophyllum martianum

Chrysophyllum martianum, commonly known as the Martian cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, sweet fruits resembling cherries. Its fruit is often consumed fresh or used in local cuisines, and the tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential economic value.

Chrysophyllum martianum thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate for optimal growth.
