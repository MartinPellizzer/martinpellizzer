Cerastium kilimandscharicum

Cerastium kilimandscharicum, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and hairy, succulent leaves. The plant thrives in rocky and alpine environments, often found in the mountainous regions of Tanzania, Kenya, and Uganda.
