Croton condorensis

Croton condorensis, commonly known as the Peruvian croton, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often spiny plants. The plant features striking, palmate leaves with a vibrant green color and a rough texture. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Croton condorensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and resilience in various environmental conditions.
