Capparis oligandra

Capparis oligandra, commonly known as the rock caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its leaves are usually small and succulent. The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
