Crotalaria decasperma

Crotalaria decasperma, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used as a protein source in some parts of the world.

Crotalaria decasperma is also known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
