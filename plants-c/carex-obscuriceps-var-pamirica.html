Carex obscuriceps var. pamirica

Carex obscuriceps var. pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized growth habits. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky or alpine environments, often in high-altitude meadows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dense, compact growth form that helps it withstand harsh climatic conditions.

The inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, inconspicuous flowers, typical of many sedges.
