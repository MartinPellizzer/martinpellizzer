Cistus creticus var. morisii

Cistus creticus var. morisii, commonly known as the Cretan rockrose, is a flowering shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It is often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions of Crete.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
