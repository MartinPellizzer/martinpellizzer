Codonopsis ovata

Codonopsis ovata, commonly known as the oval bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to white flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and has lanceolate, hairy leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. Its fruit is a capsule that releases small, smooth seeds when mature.
