Convolvulus japonicus

Convolvulus japonicus, commonly known as Japanese morning glory, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial vine that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its seeds are small and often dispersed by wind, allowing it to spread easily in cultivated and wild areas.
