Claytonia peirsonii subsp. californacis

Claytonia peirsonii subsp.

californacis, commonly known as the California mountain sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the western United States. It features rosettes of fleshy, oval leaves that are typically reddish or purple in color, with a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, white to pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native plant gardens.
