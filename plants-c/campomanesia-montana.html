Campomanesia montana

Campomanesia montana, commonly known as the Brazilian wax apple or wax jambul, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, red, wax-coated fruits that are rich in nutrients and have a tart, sweet flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
