Croton cordatus

Croton cordatus, commonly known as the heartleaf croton, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically green with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, often blooming in the summer months. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Croton cordatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
