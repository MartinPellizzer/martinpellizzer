Cynanchum bekinolense

Cynanchum bekinolense, commonly known as the Bikanol or Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia and parts of China.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing numerous seeds. It is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in arid to semi-arid climates.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
