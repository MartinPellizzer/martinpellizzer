Cyperus zollingerioides

Cyperus zollingerioides, commonly known as the Zollinger's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, often forming dense stands. Its flowering spikes are usually short and clustered, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

Cyperus zollingerioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
