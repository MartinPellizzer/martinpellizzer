Carex atractodes

Carex atractodes, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and are wind-pollinated.

Carex atractodes plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
