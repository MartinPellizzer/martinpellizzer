Carex vesicaria f. pendulina

Carex vesicaria f. pendulina, commonly known as the hanging sedge, is a flowering plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its pendulous, cylindrical inflorescences that hang from the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, thriving in areas with consistent moisture. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

This ornamental plant is valued for its unique form and adaptability to moist environments.
