Chamaecrista choriophylla var. rossicorum

Chamaecrista choriophylla var. rossicorum, commonly known as the Russian crown vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Caucasus region.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers arranged in racemes. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It has a deep root system, which helps it thrive in poor soil conditions.

This species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil restoration efforts.
