Chamaepentas

Chamaepentas, commonly known as the mountain maple, is a genus of deciduous shrubs or small trees native to the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in temperate regions, often in mountainous or forested areas, and is characterized by its opposite, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring, followed by samaras (winged seeds) that aid in wind dispersal. Chamaepentas species are valued for their ornamental appeal and ecological role in providing habitat and food for wildlife.

They are also important in soil stabilization and contribute to the biodiversity of their native ecosystems.
