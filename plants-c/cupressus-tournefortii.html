Cupressus tournefortii

Cupressus tournefortii, commonly known as the Mediterranean cypress, is a tall, evergreen conifer native to the Mediterranean region.

It has a narrow, columnar shape with scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by cylindrical cones. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and drought tolerance.

The species is also valued for its aromatic wood and historical use in traditional medicine.
