Catostemma milanezii

Catostemma milanezii, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its unique nocturnal blooming habit makes it a notable species in the nightshade family.
