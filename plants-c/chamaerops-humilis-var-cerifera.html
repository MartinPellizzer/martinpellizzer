Chamaerops humilis var. cerifera

Chamaerops humilis var. cerifera, commonly known as the dwarf date palm, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small, cold-hardy palm that typically grows to a height of about 3 to 5 meters. The plant features a slender trunk with a fibrous, bark-like covering and produces small, clustered inflorescences. Its leaves are fan-shaped, with a silvery-blue coloration on the underside, and it bears small, edible fruits similar to dates.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various climates.
