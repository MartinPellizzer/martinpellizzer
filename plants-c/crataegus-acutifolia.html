Crataegus acutifolia

Crataegus acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its sharp, pointed leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife. Crataegus acutifolia typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or thickets.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
