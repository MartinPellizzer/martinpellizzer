Calothamnus quadrifidus f. obtusus

Calothamnus quadrifidus f. obtusus, commonly known as the broad-leaved stork's bill, is a distinctive shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its lanceolate, often broad and rounded leaves. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is typically found in coastal and sandy habitats.

The f. obtusus form is distinguished by its more rounded leaf tips compared to the typical form.
