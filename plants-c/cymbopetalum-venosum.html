Cymbopetalum venosum

Cymbopetalum venosum, commonly known as the red-veined hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking, velvety red leaves with prominent veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires plenty of sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
