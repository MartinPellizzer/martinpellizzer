Crinum asiaticum var. procerum

Crinum asiaticum var. procerum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved crinum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with a strong, fragrant scent. The plant has strap-like, lanceolate leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
