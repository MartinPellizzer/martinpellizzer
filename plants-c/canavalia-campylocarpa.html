Canavalia campylocarpa

Canavalia campylocarpa, commonly known as the hairy jackbean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, hairy pods that contain edible seeds. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.

The seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
