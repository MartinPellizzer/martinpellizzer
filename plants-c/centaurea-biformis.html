Centaurea biformis

Centaurea biformis, commonly known as the two-lobed knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

Centaurea biformis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
