Celastrus pringlei

Celastrus pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
