Centaurea uniflora subsp. nervosa

Centaurea uniflora subsp.

nervosa, commonly known as the narrow-veined centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers with prominent central disks, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and relatively rare member of the Asteraceae family.
