Cactus capillaris

Cactus capillaris, commonly known as the feathered cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, branched stems that resemble feathers, which help reduce water loss through transpiration. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a vibrant touch to its desert habitat. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Cactus capillaris plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
