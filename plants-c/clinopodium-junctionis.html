Clinopodium junctionis

Clinopodium junctionis, commonly known as mountain savory, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or purple, and it produces tiny, four-angled fruits.

Clinopodium junctionis is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
