Chenopodium dissectum

Chenopodium dissectum, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its lobed, fern-like leaves that are edible and often used in cooking. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often grown as a leafy vegetable due to its mild flavor and nutritional value.

Its botanical features make it both a useful crop and an ornamental plant in gardens.
