Carex × sumikawaensis

Carex × sumikawaensis, commonly known as the Sumikawa sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-green leaves and dense, cylindrical flower clusters. Native to Japan, this species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade. It is often found in forest understories and wetland margins, contributing to ecological diversity in these habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
