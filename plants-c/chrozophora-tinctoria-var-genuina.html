Chrozophora tinctoria var. genuina

Chrozophora tinctoria var. genuina, commonly known as the yellow rocket, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and feathery leaves, which are arranged in a distinctive rosette pattern. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in dye production.
