Cojoba whitefoordiae

Cojoba whitefoordiae, commonly known as the Whitefoord's cojoba, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is adapted to prevent erosion, making it valuable for stabilizing sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
