Clerodendrum mabesae

Clerodendrum mabesae, commonly known as the Mabesae Flame Creeper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and cascading clusters of bright red or orange tubular flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, white pubescence. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative vine due to its striking floral display.

Clerodendrum mabesae is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and as a houseplant.
