Convallaria dichotoma

Convallaria dichotoma, commonly known as the red baneberry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features a striking appearance with its bright red berries that contrast with its dark green, palmately lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, blooming in early summer.

The plant contains toxic compounds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
