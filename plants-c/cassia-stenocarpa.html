Cassia stenocarpa

Cassia stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and often nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cassia stenocarpa is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
