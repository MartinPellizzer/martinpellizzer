Cyperus fuscus f. calidus

Cyperus fuscus f. calidus, commonly known as the warm-colored flatsedge, is a variant of the flat sedge species.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and wetlands. Its botanical features include small, three-lobed flowers that are usually pale green to brown in color.

This form is notable for its distinct coloration and habitat preferences, making it an interesting subject for ecological and botanical study.
