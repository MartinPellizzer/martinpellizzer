Crocus pestalozzae subsp. violaceus

Crocus pestalozzae subsp.

violaceus, commonly known as the violet-flowered crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It produces showy, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to April.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or limestone soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate beauty.

The subspecies is closely related to other crocus species but is distinguished by its unique floral coloration.
