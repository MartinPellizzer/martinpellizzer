Colchicum turcicum

Colchicum turcicum, commonly known as the Turkish meadow saffron, is a perennial bulbous plant native to regions of Turkey and the Caucasus.

It produces striking, violet-blue flowers with reflexed petals, typically blooming in late spring. The plant has lance-shaped, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous corm. It is notable for its toxic properties, containing colchicine, a compound used in medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
