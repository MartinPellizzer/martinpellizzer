Corybas vitreus

Corybas vitreus, commonly known as the glassy hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, hood-like flower structure that encloses the reproductive organs. The plant produces a single, strikingly shaped flower with a bright yellow or orange coloration. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is noted for its specialized pollination mechanisms, attracting specific insect pollinators.
