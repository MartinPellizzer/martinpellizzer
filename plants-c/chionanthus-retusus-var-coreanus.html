Chionanthus retusus var. coreanus

Chionanthus retusus var. coreanus, commonly known as the Korean Fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting, clustered flowers and attractive foliage.
