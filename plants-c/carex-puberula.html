Carex puberula

Carex puberula, commonly known as the velvet sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features grass-like leaves that are densely packed and often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex puberula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
