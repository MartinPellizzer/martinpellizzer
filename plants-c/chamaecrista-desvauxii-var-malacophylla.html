Chamaecrista desvauxii var. malacophylla

Chamaecrista desvauxii var. malacophylla, commonly known as the Malacophylla Cassia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its attractive foliage and floral display.
