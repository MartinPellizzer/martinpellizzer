Campanula rapunculus var. cymosospicata

Campanula rapunculus var. cymosospicata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for garden cultivation.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
