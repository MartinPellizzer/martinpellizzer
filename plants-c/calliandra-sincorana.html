Calliandra sincorana

Calliandra sincorana, commonly known as the pink shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to red inflorescences that resemble shower-like structures, which bloom throughout the year. The plant is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.

Its showy flowers also attract pollinators such as bees and birds, contributing to its ecological value.
