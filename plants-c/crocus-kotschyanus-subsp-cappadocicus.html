Crocus kotschyanus subsp. cappadocicus

Crocus kotschyanus subsp.

cappadocicus, commonly known as the Cappadocian crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East. It produces showy, early spring blooms with violet-blue petals and a distinctive yellow center.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or semi-arid environments. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and can remain dormant for extended periods.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early-flowering display.
