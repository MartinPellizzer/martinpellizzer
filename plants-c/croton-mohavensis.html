Croton mohavensis

Croton mohavensis, commonly known as the Mohave croton, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves that can range from green to red, orange, or yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to poor soils and high temperatures.

The Mohave croton is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
