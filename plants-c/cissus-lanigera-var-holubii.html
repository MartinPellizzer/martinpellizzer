Cissus lanigera var. holubii

Cissus lanigera var. holubii, commonly known as the velvet leaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its stems are succulent and capable of trailing or climbing, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trellises.

It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
