Carum neurophyllum

Carum neurophyllum, commonly known as western parsley or mountain parsley, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It features finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds are small and oblong, with a mild, spicy flavor.

Carum neurophyllum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
