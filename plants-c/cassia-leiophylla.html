Cassia leiophylla

Cassia leiophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, waxy powder. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes.

Cassia leiophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
