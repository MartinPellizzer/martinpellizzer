Chamaele decumbens var. gracillima

Chamaele decumbens var. gracillima, commonly known as the slender chamaeleon plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, facilitating its dispersal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
