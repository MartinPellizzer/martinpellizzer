Clymenum tenuifolium

Clymenum tenuifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clymenum, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 2 centimeters wide. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks.

Clymenum tenuifolium is often found in humid tropical and subtropical climates.
