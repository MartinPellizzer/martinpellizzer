Calocarpum procerum var. cuspidatum

Calocarpum procerum var. cuspidatum, commonly known as the pointed calocarpum, is a variant of the calocarpum species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small, deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its elongated, pointed leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, round fruits that are encased in a fleshy, red or orange outer covering. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it thrives in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
