Coronilla aculeata

Coronilla aculeata, commonly known as the prickly coronilla, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its spiny stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Coronilla aculeata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
