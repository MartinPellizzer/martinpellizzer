Cicer acanthophyllum

Cicer acanthophyllum, commonly known as the desert pea, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the Mediterranean and Middle East.

It is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and small, compound leaves adapted to conserve water. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Cicer acanthophyllum is valued for its resilience to drought and its role in stabilizing sandy soils.
