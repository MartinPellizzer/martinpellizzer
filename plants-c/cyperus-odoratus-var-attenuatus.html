Cyperus odoratus var. attenuatus

Cyperus odoratus var. attenuatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in poorly drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge family.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various forms of wildlife.
