Clerodendrum fragrans f. pleniflorum

Clerodendrum fragrans f. pleniflorum, commonly known as the fragrant clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of pink and white. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a mild scent. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as a parent species in hybridization efforts within the Clerodendrum genus.
