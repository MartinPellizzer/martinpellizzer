Crematomia andrieuxii

Crematomia andrieuxii, commonly known as the Madagascar star cactus, is a unique succulent native to Madagascar.

It features a compact, globular stem with a star-like pattern of tubercles and spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the dry season, which are highly attractive to pollinators. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Due to its striking appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
