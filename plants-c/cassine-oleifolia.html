Cassine oleifolia

Cassine oleifolia, commonly known as the African snowball, is a flowering plant native to tropical and southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Cassine oleifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
