Campylospermum walkeri

Campylospermum walkeri, commonly known as the white-flowered sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained sandy soils and full sun, making it adaptable to arid environments. Its flowers are typically borne on erect stems and bloom during the cooler months.
