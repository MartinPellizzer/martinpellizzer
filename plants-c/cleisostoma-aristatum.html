Cleisostoma aristatum

Cleisostoma aristatum, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, hood-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange with a contrasting lip. The plant has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, and its leaves are strap-like and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting mix for optimal growth.

Cleisostoma aristatum is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
