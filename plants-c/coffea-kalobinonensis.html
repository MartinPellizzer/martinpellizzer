Coffea kalobinonensis

Coffea kalobinonensis, commonly known as the Kalobinonensis coffee, is a rare species native to Madagascar.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in the humid, shaded understory of tropical forests. The plant produces small, oval-shaped red berries that contain the coffee beans. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, with a slightly serrated edge.

This species is valued for its unique flavor profile and is considered important for genetic diversity in the Coffea genus.
