Celosia aurea

Celosia aurea, commonly known as the cockscomb, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, plume-like inflorescences that resemble a rooster's comb, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its flowers come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and pink, and it thrives in full sunlight.

Celosia aurea is relatively drought-tolerant and can be grown in well-drained soil.
