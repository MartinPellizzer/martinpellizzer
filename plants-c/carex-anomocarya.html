Carex anomocarya

Carex anomocarya, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow, linear leaves. Native to North America, it thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along streambanks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
