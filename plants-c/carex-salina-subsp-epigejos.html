Carex salina subsp. epigejos

Carex salina subsp.

epigejos, commonly known as salt marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions. It thrives in saline and brackish wetlands, often found in tidal marshes and estuaries.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are often surrounded by bracts.

This subspecies is adapted to withstand high salt concentrations and fluctuating water levels.
