Calochortus selwayensis

Calochortus selwayensis, commonly known as the Selway's mariposa lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in open, rocky habitats, such as meadows and grasslands. It produces a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, stout stem.

Calochortus selwayensis is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant blooms.
