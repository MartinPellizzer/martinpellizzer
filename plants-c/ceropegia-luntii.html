Ceropegia luntii

Ceropegia luntii, commonly known as the Lunt's African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and tubular flowers. The plant produces small, pale pink to white flowers that are often clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ceropegia luntii is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
