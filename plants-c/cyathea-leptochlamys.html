Cyathea leptochlamys

Cyathea leptochlamys, commonly known as the slender-veined tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as tropical rainforests. Its fronds are bi-directionally pinnate, with a distinctive pattern of veins that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Cyathea leptochlamys plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
