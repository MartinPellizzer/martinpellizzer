Comocladia glabra var. acuminata

Comocladia glabra var. acuminata, commonly known as the smooth bladder nut, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged oppositely. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds. Its leaves and stems are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is adapted to grow in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
