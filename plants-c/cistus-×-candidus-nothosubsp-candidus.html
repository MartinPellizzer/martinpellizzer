Cistus × candidus nothosubsp. candidus

Cistus × candidus nothosubsp.

candidus, commonly known as the white rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, preferring full sun and well-drained conditions. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, often covered in a fine layer of white or greyish hairs.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in coastal and Mediterranean climates.
