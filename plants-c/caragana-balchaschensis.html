Caragana balchaschensis

Caragana balchaschensis, commonly known as the Balchaschen caragana, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

Caragana balchaschensis is often used for soil stabilization and as a source of forage for livestock.
