Calystegia soldanella var. australis

Calystegia soldanella var. australis, commonly known as the Australian morning glory, is a climbing vine native to Australia.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range from white to pink. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various soil types. It is often found in coastal regions and open woodlands.

The flowers bloom in spring and are pollinated by insects, making it an important species in local ecosystems.
