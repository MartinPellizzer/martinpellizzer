Crocus vernus var. sabinei

Crocus vernus var. sabinei, commonly known as the Sabine crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in woodland areas and meadows. It is a member of the Iridaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

The variety is distinguished by its slightly different flower coloration and habitat preferences compared to the typical Crocus vernus.
