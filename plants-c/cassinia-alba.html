Cassinia alba

Cassinia alba, commonly known as white corymbia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are arranged in corymbs, giving it a fluffy appearance. Cassinia alba is typically found in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
