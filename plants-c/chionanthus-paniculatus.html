Chionanthus paniculatus

Chionanthus paniculatus, commonly known as the fragrant chionanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. Its flowering panicles are usually terminal and can reach up to 12 inches in length.

It prefers moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
