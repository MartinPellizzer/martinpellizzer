Carex kelvingtoniana

Carex kelvingtoniana, commonly known as Kelvington's sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Oregon. This perennial plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the sedge family.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
