Chrysophyllum bonplandii

Chrysophyllum bonplandii, commonly known as the African cherry or bonplandii cherry, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
