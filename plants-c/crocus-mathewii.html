Crocus mathewii

Crocus mathewii, commonly known as the Mathew's crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the eastern Mediterranean, particularly in Turkey and Cyprus. The plant produces striking, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem.

Crocus mathewii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and early blooming flowers.
