Cissampelos monoica

Cissampelos monoica, commonly known as the single-flowered moon vine, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains alkaloids, which can be toxic to humans and animals. Its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its rapid growth habit.
