Chamaesyce sachetiana

Chamaesyce sachetiana, commonly known as the sachet weed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, green, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are yellow and clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, which give it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, roadsides, and other sunny, dry habitats.
