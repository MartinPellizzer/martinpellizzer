Celosia baccata

Celosia baccata, commonly known as the flame flower or crimson cup, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spiky bracts that resemble flames. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are surrounded by colorful, papery bracts. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Celosia baccata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
