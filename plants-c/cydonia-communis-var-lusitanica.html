Cydonia communis var. lusitanica

Cydonia communis var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Lusitanian quince, is a cultivar of the quince species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its thick, rough, pale yellow skin and its aromatic, golden-yellow flesh. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by the characteristic oval-shaped fruit. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its culinary uses and ornamental appeal in gardens.
