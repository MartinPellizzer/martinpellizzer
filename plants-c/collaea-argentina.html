Collaea argentina

Collaea argentina, commonly known as the Argentine collaea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes caraway and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and features slender stems with finely divided leaves. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in umbels, a common floral arrangement in the Apiaceae family.

Collaea argentina is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
