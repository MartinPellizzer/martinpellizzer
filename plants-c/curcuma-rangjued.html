Curcuma rangjued

Curcuma rangjued, commonly known as golden turmeric, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit and vibrant yellow-orange rhizomes. The plant produces tall, leafy stems with narrow, sword-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, orange-red flowers. Its rhizomes are widely used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice due to their bioactive compounds.

The botanical features of Curcuma rangjued make it an important plant in both horticulture and ethnobotanical practices.
