Crocus laevigatus subsp. boryi

Crocus laevigatus subsp.

boryi, commonly known as the Boryi crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family. It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and prefers well-drained conditions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bulbous corms that store nutrients for the next growing season.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
