Calamintha alpina var. elatior

Calamintha alpina var. elatior, commonly known as alpine pennyroyal, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in mountainous areas. Its small, white or pale blue flowers are clustered in whorls along the stem, and it produces tiny, aromatic seeds.

This variety is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
