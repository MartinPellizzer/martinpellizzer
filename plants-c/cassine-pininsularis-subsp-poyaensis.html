Cassine pininsularis subsp. poyaensis

Cassine pininsularis subsp.

poyaensis, commonly known as the Poyas Island cassine, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean islands. It typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.

The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by fleshy, red berries.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
