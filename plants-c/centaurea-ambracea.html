Centaurea ambracea

Centaurea ambracea, commonly known as the Greek star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its blue to purple composite flowers and spiny, bristly leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its inflorescence consists of a single, showy flower head surrounded by bracts.

Centaurea ambracea is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered an invasive species in some areas outside its native range.
