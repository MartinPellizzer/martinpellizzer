Copaiva

Copaiva, commonly known as copaiba, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Burseraceae, native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic resins, which are often tapped from the bark of its trees. The most well-known species, Copaifera officinalis, is valued for its medicinal and commercial uses. The plant typically grows as a medium to large evergreen tree, with smooth, pale bark and compound leaves.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and contributing to the biodiversity of its native forests.
