Cissus edulis

Cissus edulis, commonly known as the devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in tiny, white, waxy dots. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular houseplant. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color.

Cissus edulis is also notable for its ability to climb using its tendrils, which help it reach sunlight in its natural habitat.
