Crypsinus lagunensis

Crypsinus lagunensis, commonly known as the Guanacaste tree, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. The tree produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, round fruits containing winged seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth, glossy surface.

The plant is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and dry forests.
