Costus eburneus

Costus eburneus, commonly known as the white costus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers and other aromatic plants. The plant features tall, narrow leaves and produces clusters of white to pale yellow flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Botanically, it is characterized by its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
