Cirsium grahamii

Cirsium grahamii, commonly known as Graham's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows. Its leaves are covered in stiff, bristly hairs, and its flowers are arranged in a spherical shape.

Cirsium grahamii is often found in arid environments and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
