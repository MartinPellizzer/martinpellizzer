Chloris retusa

Chloris retusa, commonly known as slender grass or slender bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to greenish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Chloris retusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
