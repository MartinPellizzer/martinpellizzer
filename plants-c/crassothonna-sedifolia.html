Crassothonna sedifolia

Crassothonna sedifolia, commonly known as the woolly leaf cycad, is a unique plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that are covered in dense, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces cones rather than flowers, typical of cycads, and has a slow growth rate. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
