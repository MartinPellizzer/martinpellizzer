Caladenia toxochila

Caladenia toxochila, commonly known as the red-tongued spider-orchid, is a striking orchid species native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance. The plant produces a single, erect stem with a single flower that has a dark red or purple lip and a white to pale yellow dorsal sepal. Caladenia toxochila typically grows in heathlands and open forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is often protected in its natural habitat.
