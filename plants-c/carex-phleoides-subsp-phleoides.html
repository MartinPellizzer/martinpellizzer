Carex phleoides subsp. phleoides

Carex phleoides subsp.

phleoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and dense clusters of spikelets.

This subspecies typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually wind-pollinated.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
