Centaurea tenuifolia var. tenuiloba

Centaurea tenuifolia var. tenuiloba, commonly known as slenderleaf knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry meadows and rocky habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
