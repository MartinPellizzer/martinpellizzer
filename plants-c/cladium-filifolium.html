Cladium filifolium

Cladium filifolium, commonly known as the slender sawgrass, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and aquatic habitat.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and shallow waters, where it thrives in saturated soil conditions. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family.

Cladium filifolium plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
