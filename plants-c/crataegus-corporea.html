Crataegus corporea

Crataegus corporea, commonly known as the corporeal hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Crataegus corporea is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
