Cyrtosperma cuspidispathum

Cyrtosperma cuspidispathum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed arrowhead, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its arrow-shaped leaves and is native to Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows in wet, swampy areas and has a rhizomatous root system. Its flowers are small and clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, which is enclosed by a modified leaf called a spathe.

It is often used in traditional medicine and ornamental horticulture due to its unique appearance and adaptability.
