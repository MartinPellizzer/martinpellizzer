Cyclanthera biglandulifera

Cyclanthera biglandulifera, commonly known as the large-fruited bitter gourd, is a tropical climbing vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often spiny fruits, which are typically green and turn yellow or orange when mature. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are borne in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are heart-shaped with prominent veins and are often covered in fine pubescence.

Cyclanthera biglandulifera is valued for its edible fruits, which have a bitter taste and are used in various traditional cuisines.
