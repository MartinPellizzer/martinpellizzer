Capraria integrifolia

Capraria integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf capraria, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Violaceae and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, violet-blue flowers. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have smooth margins, giving it its common name. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrublands.

This plant is well adapted to salt spray and can tolerate periods of drought.
