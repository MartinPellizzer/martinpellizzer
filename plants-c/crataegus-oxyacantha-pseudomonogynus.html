Crataegus oxyacantha pseudomonogynus

Crataegus oxyacantha pseudomonogynus, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is notable for its ornamental value and medicinal uses.
