Comolia smithii

Comolia smithii, commonly known as the Smith's comolia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Comolia smithii is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats, contributing to the ecological diversity of these regions.
