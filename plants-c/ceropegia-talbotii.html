Ceropegia talbotii

Ceropegia talbotii, commonly known as the silver leaf or talbot's ceropegia, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its fleshy, tuberous roots and trailing stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which attract specific pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia talbotii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
