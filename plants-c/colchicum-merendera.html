Colchicum merendera

Colchicum merendera, commonly known as the Merendera colchicum, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant produces a single, erect stem that bears a cluster of waxy, purple to pink flowers. Its leaves are basal, forming a rosette, and the plant is adapted to grow in dry, rocky habitats.

Colchicum merendera is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
