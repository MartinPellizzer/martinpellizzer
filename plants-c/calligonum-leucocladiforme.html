Calligonum leucocladiforme

Calligonum leucocladiforme, commonly known as the white-leaved goosefoot, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically grayish-white in color, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are often consumed by birds and small mammals. It has a deep root system that allows it to access groundwater, making it highly drought-tolerant.

Due to its hardiness, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in degraded landscapes.
