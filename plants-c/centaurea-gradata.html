Centaurea gradata

Centaurea gradata, commonly known as the graduated centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea gradata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant color and unique appearance.
