Cirsium leptophyllum

Cirsium leptophyllum, commonly known as the slender thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leafy structures. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, and its flowers are blue to purple in color. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
