Colutea tomentosa

Colutea tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy balsam or velvet vetch, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are covered in dense, soft hairs, giving the plant a velvety appearance. The flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and can often be found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

This plant is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
