Chenopodium benthamii subsp. benthamii

Chenopodium benthamii subsp.

benthamii, commonly known as desert spinach, is a species native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America. It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves, which are adapted to store water.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Due to its nutritional value, it is sometimes used as a food source by both wildlife and humans.
