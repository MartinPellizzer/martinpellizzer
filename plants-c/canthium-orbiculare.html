Canthium orbiculare

Canthium orbiculare, commonly known as the globe senna, is a tropical shrub native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive orbicular (rounded) leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
