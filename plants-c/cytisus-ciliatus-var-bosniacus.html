Cytisus ciliatus var. bosniacus

Cytisus ciliatus var. bosniacus, commonly known as the Bosnian broom, is a shrub native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and pairs of small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
