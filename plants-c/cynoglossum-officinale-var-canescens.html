Cynoglossum officinale var. canescens

Cynoglossum officinale var. canescens, commonly known as hoary bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features hairy, grayish-blue leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered with fine, white pubescence. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
