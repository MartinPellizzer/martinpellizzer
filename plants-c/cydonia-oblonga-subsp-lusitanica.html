Cydonia oblonga subsp. lusitanica

Cydonia oblonga subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese quince, is a shrub or small tree native to the Iberian Peninsula. It produces fragrant, yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange to red, pear-shaped fruits.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges. The fruit is typically harvested in late autumn and is used in traditional culinary preparations.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
