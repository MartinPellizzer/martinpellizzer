Cyrtopera ramosa

Cyrtopera ramosa, commonly known as the hairy leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, hairy leaves and small, fragrant orchid-like flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

Cyrtopera ramosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
