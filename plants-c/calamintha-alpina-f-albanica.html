Calamintha alpina f. albanica

Calamintha alpina f. albanica, commonly known as the Albanian alpine germander, is a subspecies of the alpine germander native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with aromatic, narrow leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of pink to violet. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. It is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its calming and digestive effects.

Its compact growth habit and fragrant foliage make it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine collections.
