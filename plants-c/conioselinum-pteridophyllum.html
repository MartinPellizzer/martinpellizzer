Conioselinum pteridophyllum

Conioselinum pteridophyllum, commonly known as the fern-leaved wormwood, is a flowering plant species belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of linear, fern-like leaves that are often hairy and aromatic. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters called capitula, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to regions of Europe and Asia, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Conioselinum pteridophyllum is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
