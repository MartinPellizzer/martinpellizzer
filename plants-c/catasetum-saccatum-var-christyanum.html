Catasetum saccatum var. christyanum

Catasetum saccatum var. christyanum, commonly known as the Christyan's catasetum, is a rare orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often hairy pseudobulbs, which store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming during the dry season. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soil and requires high humidity and indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
