Calliandra nogalensis

Calliandra nogalensis, commonly known as the Nogalensis calliandra, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. It is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in semi-arid environments.

Calliandra nogalensis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
