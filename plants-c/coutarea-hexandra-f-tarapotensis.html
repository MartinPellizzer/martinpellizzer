Coutarea hexandra f. tarapotensis

Coutarea hexandra f. tarapotensis, commonly known as the Andean trumpet tree, is a tropical plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that resemble trumpets, which bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

The bark and leaves of this species have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
