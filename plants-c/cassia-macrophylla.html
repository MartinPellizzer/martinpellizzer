Cassia macrophylla

Cassia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf cassia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, narrow pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia macrophylla is also valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine.
