Cardamine flaccida proles depressa

Cardamine flaccida proles depressa, commonly known as the depressed fern, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that are typically borne on long, slender stems. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in a raceme, often appearing in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of diverse, temperate ecosystems.
