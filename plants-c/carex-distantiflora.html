Carex distantiflora

Carex distantiflora, commonly known as the distant-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and pond margins. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the ends of its stems.

Carex distantiflora plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
