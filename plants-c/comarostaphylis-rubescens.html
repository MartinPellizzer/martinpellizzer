Comarostaphylis rubescens

Comarostaphylis rubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to dry, sunny environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Comarostaphylis rubescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
