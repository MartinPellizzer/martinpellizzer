Crassula diabolica

Crassula diabolica, commonly known as the Devil's Ivy, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a compact and striking appearance. The plant produces small, pale green flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements contribute to its popularity among plant enthusiasts.
