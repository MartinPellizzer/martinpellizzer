Cleome oxalidea

Cleome oxalidea, commonly known as sweet shower or Mexican spider flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the southern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spindly stems and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers have five petals with prominent, elongated stamens. Cleome oxalidea is a perennial that thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

It is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
