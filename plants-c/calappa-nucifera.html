Calappa nucifera

Calappa nucifera, commonly known as the betel nut palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a straight trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces clustered inflorescences with small, white flowers that develop into hard, woody fruits containing a single seed. The seeds, known as betel nuts, are the primary product of the plant and are used in traditional practices.

Botanically, it belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its robust root system and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
