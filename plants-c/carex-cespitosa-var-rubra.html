Carex cespitosa var. rubra

Carex cespitosa var. rubra, commonly known as redstem sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It features dense, grass-like foliage with a distinctive reddish hue in its stems, particularly in the autumn. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, three-lobed flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
