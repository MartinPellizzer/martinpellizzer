Cyathea strigillosa

Cyathea strigillosa, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a fibrous, brownish layer that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. Cyathea strigillosa is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
