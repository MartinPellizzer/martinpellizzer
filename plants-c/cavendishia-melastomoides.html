Cavendishia melastomoides

Cavendishia melastomoides, commonly known as the red-flowered sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Melastomataceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to store water. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in xerophytic plant adaptations.
