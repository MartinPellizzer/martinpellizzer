Cracca barbigera var. bakeriana

Cracca barbigera var. bakeriana, commonly known as the Baker's cracca, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems that are covered in soft, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly serrated margin and a pale underside.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
