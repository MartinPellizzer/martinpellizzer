Cyathea uleana var. abitaguensis

Cyathea uleana var. abitaguensis, commonly known as the Abitagua tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately divided fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in tropical rainforests. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting the growth of its massive fronds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in botanical gardens.
