Copaifera demeusei

Copaifera demeusei, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a species of tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Leguminosae and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap, which is often used in traditional medicine. The plant typically grows in humid lowland forests and can reach heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and oval-shaped, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

The tree is valued for its latex, which contains compounds with potential medicinal properties.
