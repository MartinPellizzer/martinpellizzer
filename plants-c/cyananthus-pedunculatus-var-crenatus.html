Cyananthus pedunculatus var. crenatus

Cyananthus pedunculatus var. crenatus, commonly known as the crested cyananthus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly wavy or serrated edge, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
