Chayota

Chayota, commonly known as the "chayote" or "Spanish pear," is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It produces a large, pear-shaped fruit with a thick, green rind and a white, edible flesh that is often eaten raw or cooked. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can be used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The fruit is rich in vitamins and minerals, making it a nutritious addition to various dishes.
