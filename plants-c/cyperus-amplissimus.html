Cyperus amplissimus

Cyperus amplissimus, commonly known as the broadleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and flooded meadows, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its leaves are slender and grass-like, with a triangular cross-section characteristic of many sedges.

Cyperus amplissimus produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the ends of its stems.
