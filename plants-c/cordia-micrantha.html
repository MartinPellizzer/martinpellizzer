Cordia micrantha

Cordia micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered cordia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are often followed by small, round fruits. Its bark is thick and corky, and it is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Cordia micrantha is adapted to dry environments and can tolerate seasonal drought.
