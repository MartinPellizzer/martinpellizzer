Cardiospermum pilosum

Cardiospermum pilosum, commonly known as the hairy heartleaf or sticky nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, sticky hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by distinctive, inflated fruit that resembles a heart. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in humid environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a notable species in botanical studies.
