Cardamine montana

Cardamine montana, commonly known as mountain watercress, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The small white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.
