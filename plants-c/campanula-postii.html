Campanula postii

Campanula postii, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette, while its stems are often hairy.

Campanula postii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
