Cliffortia scandens

Cliffortia scandens, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in mountainous regions. The plant features a trailing or climbing habit, with slender stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in length. Its flowers are small and yellow, with a distinctive ray floret structure, and it produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.
