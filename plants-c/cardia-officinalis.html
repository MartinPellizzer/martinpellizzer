Cardia officinalis

Cardia officinalis, commonly known as wild thyme, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and its aromatic, woody stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small white or pink flowers in the summer.

Cardia officinalis has been used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and carminative properties.
