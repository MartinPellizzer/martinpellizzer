Carex eleocharis

Carex eleocharis, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and cylindrical spikes of small, usually bisexual flowers. This plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as pond margins and saturated soils. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Carex eleocharis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
