Cousinia leiocephala

Cousinia leiocephala, commonly known as the pale-headed cousinia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features dense, woolly foliage and produces small, clustered flowers that are often pink or purple in color. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to survive in low-water environments.

Cousinia leiocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in dry gardens.
