Cistus scorpioides

Cistus scorpioides, commonly known as the creeping rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with woody stems and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow to reddish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and resistance to coastal conditions.

Its aromatic foliage and ability to thrive in poor soils make it a valuable plant in xeriscaping and ecological restoration.
