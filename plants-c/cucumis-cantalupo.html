Cucumis cantalupo

Cucumis cantalupo, commonly known as the cantaloupe melon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces large, spherical fruits with a netted rind and sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms on the same plant.

The fruit is typically harvested when the skin turns from green to a golden-orange hue.
