Cassia bacillus

Cassia bacillus, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its long, drooping panicles of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year in warm climates. The leaves are pinnately compound, with each leaflet being elliptical and slightly serrated. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in medicine and as a source of natural dye.
