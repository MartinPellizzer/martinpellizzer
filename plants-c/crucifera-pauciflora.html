Crucifera pauciflora

Crucifera pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to survive in harsh conditions. The plant has reduced floral structures, which is reflected in its common name, and often has hairy stems and leaves. It is found in regions such as southern Africa and parts of the Mediterranean.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
