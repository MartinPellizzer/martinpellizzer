Clematis hirsutissima var. scottii

Clematis hirsutissima var. scottii, commonly known as the Scotch clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of Scotland.

It features large, showy flowers that range from white to pink, often with a distinctive purple or red throat. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces long, wiry stems that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
