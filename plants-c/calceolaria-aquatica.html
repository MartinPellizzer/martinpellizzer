Calceolaria aquatica

Calceolaria aquatica, commonly known as the water calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-like flowers that resemble small cups, which are typically violet or purple in color. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found near streams or in wetlands. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Calceolaria aquatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with high humidity.
