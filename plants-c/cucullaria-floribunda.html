Cucullaria floribunda

Cucullaria floribunda, commonly known as the flowering bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Caprifoliaceae and is characterized by its showy floral display and ecological importance for pollinators.
