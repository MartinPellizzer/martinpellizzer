Calea hypoleuca

Calea hypoleuca, commonly known as the "flower of the evening," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, violet-blue flowers that open in the late afternoon and close at dawn. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported calming effects.

Calea hypoleuca thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
