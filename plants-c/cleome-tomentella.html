Cleome tomentella

Cleome tomentella, commonly known as woolly cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late summer. It is a member of the Cleomaceae family, also known as the spiderplant family.

Cleome tomentella is typically found in disturbed soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique foliage.
