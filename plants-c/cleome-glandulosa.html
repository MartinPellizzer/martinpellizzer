Cleome glandulosa

Cleome glandulosa, commonly known as the stinkcup, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its glandular leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a sprawling growth habit and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its foliage is often covered with small, sticky glands, which give it a distinctive appearance.

Cleome glandulosa is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an annual species.
