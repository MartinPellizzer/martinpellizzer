Copaifera aromatica

Copaifera aromatica, commonly known as Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap, which has been traditionally used for its medicinal properties. The plant belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is known for its large, palmate leaves and yellowish flowers. Its bark and resin are harvested for their therapeutic and aromatic qualities.

The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in the Amazon rainforest.
