Calamus sessilifolius

Calamus sessilifolius, commonly known as the sessile-leaf calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and narrow, sessile leaves that are often clustered at the base. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Calamus sessilifolius is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental foliage.
