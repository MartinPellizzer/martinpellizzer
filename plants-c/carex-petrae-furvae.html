Carex petrae-furvae

Carex petrae-furvae, commonly known as the alpine sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to high-altitude regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical flower spikes. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous and alpine environments. It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms in its native habitat.

Carex petrae-furvae is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
