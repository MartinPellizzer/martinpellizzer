Calamintha hungarica

Calamintha hungarica, commonly known as Hungarian catnip, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal and culinary uses. The plant features square stems, opposite leaves, and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to purple. Its leaves and flowers are often used in herbal teas and traditional remedies due to their aromatic properties.

Calamintha hungarica is valued for its fragrance and potential calming effects.
