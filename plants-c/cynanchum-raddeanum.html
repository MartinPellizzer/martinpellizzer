Cynanchum raddeanum

Cynanchum raddeanum, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Cynanchum raddeanum is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional Chinese medicine.
