Camphorosma glabra

Camphorosma glabra, commonly known as smooth camphorosma, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Camphorosma glabra is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
