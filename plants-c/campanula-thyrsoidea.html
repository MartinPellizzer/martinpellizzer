Campanula thyrsoidea

Campanula thyrsoidea, commonly known as the tall bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its showy blooms.
