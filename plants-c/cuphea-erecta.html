Cuphea erecta

Cuphea erecta, commonly known as the wax plant, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features glandular hairs on its stems and leaves, which give it a waxy appearance and contribute to its common name. Cuphea erecta thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
