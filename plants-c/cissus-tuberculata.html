Cissus tuberculata

Cissus tuberculata, commonly known as the rock fig or African ice plant, is a trailing vine native to southern Africa.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in small, raised bumps or tubercles, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its ability to thrive in rocky, arid environments makes it well adapted to drought conditions.

Cissus tuberculata is also popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
