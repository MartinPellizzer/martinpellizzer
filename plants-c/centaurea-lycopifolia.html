Centaurea lycopifolia

Centaurea lycopifolia, commonly known as the lycopod centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and bright yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
