Calogyne raphanoides

Calogyne raphanoides, commonly known as the African violet, is a striking ornamental plant native to southern Africa.

It features dense, velvety foliage and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of violet, pink, and white. The plant belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light. Its unique leaf structure, with a rosette formation, helps it conserve moisture in its native arid environment.

Calogyne raphanoides is popular among gardeners for its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
