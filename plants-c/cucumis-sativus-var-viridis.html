Cucumis sativus var. viridis

Cucumis sativus var. viridis, commonly known as the green cucumber, is a variant of the common cucumber plant.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated fruits that are typically green in color and have a mild, refreshing flavor. The plant is a herbaceous vine that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are broad and lobed, and it produces yellow flowers that develop into the edible fruit.

This variety is widely cultivated for both fresh consumption and culinary uses.
