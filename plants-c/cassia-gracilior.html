Cassia gracilior

Cassia gracilior, commonly known as the slender cassia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, compound leaves. The plant produces yellow, fragrant flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by elongated, slender pods. Cassia gracilior is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit and botanical features make it a distinctive member of the cassia genus.
