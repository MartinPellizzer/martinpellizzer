Cistus brasiliensis

Cistus brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian rockrose, is a shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cistus brasiliensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in arid environments.
