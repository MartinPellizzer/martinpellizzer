Centaurea × paredensis

Centaurea × paredensis, commonly known as the hybrid centaury, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Centaurea × paredensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
