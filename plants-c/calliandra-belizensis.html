Calliandra belizensis

Calliandra belizensis, commonly known as the Belize calliandra, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink or red inflorescences. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Calliandra belizensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
