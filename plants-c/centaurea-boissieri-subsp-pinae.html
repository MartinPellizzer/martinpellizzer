Centaurea boissieri subsp. pinae

Centaurea boissieri subsp.

pinae, commonly known as the Pina centaury, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and bright yellow to orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in dense, composite heads.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
