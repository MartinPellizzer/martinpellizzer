Ceratostylis phaeochlamys

Ceratostylis phaeochlamys, commonly known as the dark-chalmed finger orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky crevices. The plant features a slender, wiry stem with several elongated, narrow leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and have a distinctive, finger-like spur.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid conditions.
