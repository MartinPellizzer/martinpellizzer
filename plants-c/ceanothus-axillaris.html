Ceanothus axillaris

Ceanothus axillaris, commonly known as the coastal ceanothus, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California and southern Oregon.

It features small, fragrant, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild, wet winters.

Ceanothus axillaris is also an important nectar source for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
