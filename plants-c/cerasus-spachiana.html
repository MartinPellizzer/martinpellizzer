Cerasus spachiana

Cerasus spachiana, commonly known as the Spachian cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus within the Rosaceae family, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or black in color, similar to other cherry species. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive spring blossoms and is also valued for its fruit in certain regions.

The plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun, thriving in temperate climates.
