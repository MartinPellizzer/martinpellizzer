Chloris neglecta

Chloris neglecta, commonly known as common grassvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, pale blue or purple flowers.

Chloris neglecta is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various insects.
