Cistus salviifolius var. occidentalis

Cistus salviifolius var. occidentalis, commonly known as western woolly cistus, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, silver-gray leaves that are glandular and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers in late summer, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant suitable for xeriscaping and coastal gardens.
