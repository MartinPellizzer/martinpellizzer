Combretum laxum var. viridulum

Combretum laxum var. viridulum, commonly known as green flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It features compound leaves that are often bright green and may have a reddish or yellowish tinge, contributing to its ornamental value. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its attractive foliage and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a street tree in warmer climates.
