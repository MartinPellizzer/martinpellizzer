Cattleya loddigesii

Cattleya loddigesii, commonly known as the Loddiges' orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a sympodial orchid with thick, fleshy pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a yellow lip. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Cattleya loddigesii is prized for its beauty and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
