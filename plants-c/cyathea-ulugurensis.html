Cyathea ulugurensis

Cyathea ulugurensis, commonly known as the Uluguru mountain tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Uluguru Mountains in Tanzania.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant exhibits a distinctive trunk that is covered with a fibrous, spongy layer called the rachis. Cyathea ulugurensis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in montane rainforests.

Its unique morphology and habitat make it an important component of the region's biodiversity.
