Carex leporina f. robusta

Carex leporina f. robusta, commonly known as the robust marsh sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its foliage is often dense and can form thick mats, contributing to soil stabilization in wetland environments.

The species is notable for its adaptability to varying water levels and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
