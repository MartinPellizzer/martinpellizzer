Cyperus monophyllus

Cyperus monophyllus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and a distinctive, umbrella-shaped inflorescence that gives it its common name. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Cyperus monophyllus is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various water conditions.
