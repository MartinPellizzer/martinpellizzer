Ceropegia duplicata

Ceropegia duplicata, commonly known as the "Lion's Tail," is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features cascading clusters of bright yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has fleshy, trailing stems with small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its unique floral structure attracts specific pollinators, such as certain moths and flies.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.
