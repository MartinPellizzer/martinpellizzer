Cupania flagelliformis

Cupania flagelliformis, commonly known as the creeping cupania, is a woody vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems that can grow along the ground or climb other structures. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, and its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate in shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains a single seed.
