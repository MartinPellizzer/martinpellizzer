Curcuma pedicellata

Curcuma pedicellata, commonly known as the ginger flower or edible ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic rhizomes and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with sword-shaped leaves and clusters of orange-red or yellow flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in cooking and traditional medicine for their flavor and potential health benefits.

The botanical features of Curcuma pedicellata make it both ornamental and economically valuable.
