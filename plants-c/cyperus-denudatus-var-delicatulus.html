Cyperus denudatus var. delicatulus

Cyperus denudatus var. delicatulus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually clustered in dense spikes, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
