Crepis incrassata

Crepis incrassata, commonly known as the thick-leaved cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are thick and leathery, often with a rough texture, and its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a cluster.

Crepis incrassata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage.
