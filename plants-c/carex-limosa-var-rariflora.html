Carex limosa var. rariflora

Carex limosa var. rariflora, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a variant of the slender sedge species native to wetland and marsh environments.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical leaves and dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems. Its reproductive structures are adapted for wind pollination, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.

The varietal form, rariflora, is distinguished by its reduced flowering structure compared to the typical species.
