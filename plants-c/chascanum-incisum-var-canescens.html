Chascanum incisum var. canescens

Chascanum incisum var. canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf gourd, is a variant of the chascanum species native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silvery-green leaves and pale yellow to white flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical fruits that range in color from pale green to yellow. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its edible fruits and ornamental value.
