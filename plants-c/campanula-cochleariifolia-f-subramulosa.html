Campanula cochleariifolia f. subramulosa

Campanula cochleariifolia f. subramulosa, commonly known as the subbranching bellflower, is a variant of the common bellflower.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette at the base, while the stems are often branched, giving the plant its subramulosa designation. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate flowers and compact growth form.
