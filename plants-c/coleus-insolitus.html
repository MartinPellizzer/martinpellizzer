Coleus insolitus

Coleus insolitus, commonly known as the strange coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which can range from deep reds to bright yellows and purples. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

This plant is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden areas due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
