Carica × fructifragrans

Carica × fructifragrans, commonly known as the hybrid papaya, is a naturally occurring hybrid between *Carica papaya* and *Carica pubescens*.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, orange-colored fruit, which is typically more robust than that of *C. papaya*. The plant is a tropical perennial, often cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamins and enzymes. It prefers warm, humid climates and thrives in well-drained, nutrient-rich soils.

The hybrid is valued for its adaptability and potential for improved fruit quality and disease resistance.
