Chamaemelum gerberi

Chamaemelum gerberi, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, aromatic leaves. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in dry, sunny environments. The plant has a long history of use in traditional medicine, though its toxicity requires caution.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the daisy family.
