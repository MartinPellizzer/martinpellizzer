Cyperus granadinus

Cyperus granadinus, commonly known as the Florida marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, and its flowering spikes are usually green or reddish in color.

Cyperus granadinus is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
