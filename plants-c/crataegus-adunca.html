Crataegus adunca

Crataegus adunca, commonly known as the slender hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5 to 9 leaflets, and its showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Crataegus adunca is often found in moist woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
