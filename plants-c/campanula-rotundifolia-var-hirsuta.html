Campanula rotundifolia var. hirsuta

Campanula rotundifolia var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name. The plant produces bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
