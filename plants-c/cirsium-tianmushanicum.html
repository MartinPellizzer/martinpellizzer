Cirsium tianmushanicum

Cirsium tianmushanicum, commonly known as the Tianmu thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in stiff, bristly hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

Cirsium tianmushanicum is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
