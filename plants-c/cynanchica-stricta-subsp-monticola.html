Cynanchica stricta subsp. monticola

Cynanchica stricta subsp.

monticola, commonly known as the mountain milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in racemes, followed by legume-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
