Crataegus pseudoheterophylla subsp. turkestanica

Crataegus pseudoheterophylla subsp.

turkestanica, commonly known as the Turkestan hawthorn, is a shrub native to Central Asia. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and variable leaf shapes.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are white or pink, and it produces small, red berries that are edible.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphological features and ecological adaptations.
