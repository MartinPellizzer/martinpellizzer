Caryochloa montevidensis var. brasiliensis

Caryochloa montevidensis var. brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian needlegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
