Cyananthus chungdienensis

Cyananthus chungdienensis, commonly known as the Chungdien bluebell, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. Its leaves are lanceolate and arranged alternately along the stem.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
