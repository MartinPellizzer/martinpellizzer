Cryptocarya mucronata

Cryptocarya mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-pointed laurel, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurel, avocado, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Cryptocarya mucronata is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
