Cicercula sativa

Cicercula sativa, commonly known as the common vetch, is an annual legume native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, lens-shaped pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Cicercula sativa is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in agricultural settings.
