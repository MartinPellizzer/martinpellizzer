Campanula rotundifolia f. dubia

Campanula rotundifolia f. dubia, commonly known as the "dubious round-leaved bellflower," is a botanical variant of the common bellflower.

It is characterized by its distinctive round, waxy leaves and bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. This plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. It is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it a notable species within the Campanulaceae family.
