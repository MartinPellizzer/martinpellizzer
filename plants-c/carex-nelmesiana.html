Carex nelmesiana

Carex nelmesiana, commonly known as the Nelmes' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils in forested areas, often in shaded environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge family.

Carex nelmesiana is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its aesthetic appeal in natural landscapes.
