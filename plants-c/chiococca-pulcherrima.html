Chiococca pulcherrima

Chiococca pulcherrima, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that appear in clusters, often resembling small bells. The plant has opposite, simple, and leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds.

Chiococca pulcherrima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
