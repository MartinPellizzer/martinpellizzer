Carex scirpoidea var. gigas

Carex scirpoidea var. gigas, commonly known as the giant sedges, is a tall, robust sedge native to wetland habitats in North America.

It forms dense clumps of grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces tall, cylindrical spikelets that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its height and dense growth, it is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
