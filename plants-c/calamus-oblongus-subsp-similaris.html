Calamus oblongus subsp. similaris

Calamus oblongus subsp.

similaris, commonly known as the broadleaf calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia. It is a rhizomatous plant characterized by its tall, slender stems and elongated, narrow leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, typical of many palms. Its rhizomes are often harvested for use in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.

The subspecies similaris is distinguished by its broader leaves compared to the typical form of Calamus oblongus.
