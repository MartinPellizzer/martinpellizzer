Camellia purpurea

Camellia purpurea, commonly known as the purple camellia, is a species of flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is characterized by its showy, double flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant is evergreen, with glossy, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Camellia purpurea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its winter blooming period.
