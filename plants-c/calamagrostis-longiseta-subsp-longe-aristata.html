Calamagrostis longiseta subsp. longe-aristata

Calamagrostis longiseta subsp.

longe-aristata, commonly known as the tall-haired bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color.

The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that are typically green to purplish in hue. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
