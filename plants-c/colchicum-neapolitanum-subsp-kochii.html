Colchicum neapolitanum subsp. kochii

Colchicum neapolitanum subsp.

kochii, commonly known as the Naples autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces distinctive, helmet-shaped seed capsules. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
