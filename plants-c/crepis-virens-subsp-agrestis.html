Crepis virens subsp. agrestis

Crepis virens subsp.

agrestis, commonly known as the rough hawk's-beard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides.

The plant features erect, hairy stems and basal leaves that are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers are arranged in a single head, with ray florets that are usually pale yellow or cream-colored.

The plant is characterized by its small, oblong achenes, which are often covered in bristles and dispersed by wind.
