Coffea brachyphylla

Coffea brachyphylla, commonly known as the dwarf coffee plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to the highlands of Ethiopia and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, glossy leaves. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers followed by red or orange berries containing seeds used for coffee production. It thrives in cool, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its unique characteristics, it is of interest to both botanists and coffee enthusiasts.
