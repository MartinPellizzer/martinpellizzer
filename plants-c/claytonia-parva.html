Claytonia parva

Claytonia parva, commonly known as the dwarf spring beauty, is a small perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of only a few inches, making it a low-growing groundcover. It prefers moist, shaded habitats such as woodland floors and rocky areas.

Claytonia parva is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting early pollinators.
