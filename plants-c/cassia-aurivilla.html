Cassia aurivilla

Cassia aurivilla, commonly known as golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is smooth and greyish in color. Cassia aurivilla is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical climates.
