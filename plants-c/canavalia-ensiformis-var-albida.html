Canavalia ensiformis var. albida

Canavalia ensiformis var. albida, commonly known as the white cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, fibrous pods that contain edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen makes it a valuable crop for soil enrichment.
