Carex deflexa var. deanei

Carex deflexa var. deanei, commonly known as the Deane's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in temperate regions.

Carex deflexa var. deanei plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
