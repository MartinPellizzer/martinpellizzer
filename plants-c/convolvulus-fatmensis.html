Convolvulus fatmensis

Convolvulus fatmensis, commonly known as the Fatmen's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has climbing stems with tendrils that allow it to grow along fences or other structures. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
