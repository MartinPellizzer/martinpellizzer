Centaurea candolleana

Centaurea candolleana, commonly known as the Candolle's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stem and produces showy, purple to pink composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It blooms from late spring to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea candolleana is typically found in dry, sunny habitats like grasslands and rocky soils.
