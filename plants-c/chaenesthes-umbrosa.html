Chaenesthes umbrosa

Chaenesthes umbrosa, commonly known as the shadowy mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of coniferous trees.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that often forms a thick, mat-like structure on its host. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white or pale green flowers, followed by small, fleshy berries that are a food source for certain bird species. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in temperate forest regions.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly affect the health of its host trees if left unchecked.
