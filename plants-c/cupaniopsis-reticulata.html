Cupaniopsis reticulata

Cupaniopsis reticulata, commonly known as the diamondleaf hakea, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded foliage and small, clustered white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces woody, spine-tipped fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in local ecosystems.
