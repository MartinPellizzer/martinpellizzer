Casearia murceana

Casearia murceana, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered, violet to blue-black berries. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The berries are an important food source for birds and other wildlife, contributing to its ecological significance.
