Corallodendron spathaceum

Corallodendron spathaceum, commonly known as the lace corallodendron, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that are often yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
