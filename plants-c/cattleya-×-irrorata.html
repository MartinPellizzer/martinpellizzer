Cattleya × irrorata

Cattleya × irrorata, commonly known as the iridescent cattleya, is a hybrid orchid originating from crosses between Cattleya mossiae and Cattleya trianaei.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant produces large, showy flowers with intricate patterns of purple, pink, and white, often with a metallic sheen. It is prized for its vibrant colors and long-lasting blooms, making it a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.

Cattleya × irrorata typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture from the air and rain.
