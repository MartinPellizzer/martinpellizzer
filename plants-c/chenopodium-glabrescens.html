Chenopodium glabrescens

Chenopodium glabrescens, commonly known as pigweed, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and roadsides.

Chenopodium glabrescens is a common weed that can compete with crops for nutrients and water.
