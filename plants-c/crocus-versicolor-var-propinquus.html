Crocus versicolor var. propinquus

Crocus versicolor var. propinquus, commonly known as the variable crocus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful blooms that range from white to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or grassy habitats and prefers well-drained soil. Its flowers usually appear in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
