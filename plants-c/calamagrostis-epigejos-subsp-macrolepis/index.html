Calamagrostis Epigejos Subsp. Macrolepis

Calamagrostis epigejos subsp. macrolepis is a lesser-known grass species that thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. It is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks, where it forms dense, attractive clumps. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, silver-green leaves that shimmer in the sunlight. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, add a subtle yet elegant touch to the landscape. Despite its beauty, this subspecies remains relatively obscure in horticultural circles.
