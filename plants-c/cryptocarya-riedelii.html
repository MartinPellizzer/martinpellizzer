Cryptocarya riedelii

Cryptocarya riedelii, commonly known as the Riedel's cryptocarya, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy upper surface. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in panicles, and it produces dark, woody fruits containing seeds.

Cryptocarya riedelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and ecological significance.
