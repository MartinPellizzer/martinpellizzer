Cyperus caucasicus

Cyperus caucasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian flatsedge, is a member of the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in temperate regions. The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and slightly pointed, with a fibrous root system. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, three-ranked flowers.

Cyperus caucasicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
