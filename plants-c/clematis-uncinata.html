Clematis uncinata

Clematis uncinata, commonly known as the hooked clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five ovate leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer. The flowers are typically blue to purple and have a distinctive spurred structure at the base of the petals. The plant thrives in partial to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or structures.
