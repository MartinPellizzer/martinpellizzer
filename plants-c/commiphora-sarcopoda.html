Commiphora sarcopoda

Commiphora sarcopoda, commonly known as the African frankincense tree, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and small, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been traditionally used for incense and medicinal purposes. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-yellow in color, and it is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its aromatic properties and has cultural significance in various regions.
