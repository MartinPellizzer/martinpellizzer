Coccinia cordifolia var. wightiana

Coccinia cordifolia var. wightiana, commonly known as the heartleaf cucumber or Indian butterfruit, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellow, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to cucumbers. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species within the Cucurbitaceae family.
