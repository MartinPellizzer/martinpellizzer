Chassalia grandistipula

Chassalia grandistipula, commonly known as the giant featherflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, feathery inflorescence. The plant produces showy yellow to orange ray florets surrounded by tubular disc florets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Chassalia grandistipula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
