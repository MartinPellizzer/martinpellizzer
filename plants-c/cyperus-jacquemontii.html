Cyperus jacquemontii

Cyperus jacquemontii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and cylindrical flower spikes. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along water bodies. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
