Caragana grandiflora var. balchaschensis

Caragana grandiflora var. balchaschensis, commonly known as the Balchaschenskaya caragana, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought. It produces small, woody pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and erosion control.
