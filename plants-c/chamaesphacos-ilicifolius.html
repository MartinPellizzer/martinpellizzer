Chamaesphacos ilicifolius

Chamaesphacos ilicifolius, commonly known as the silverleaf chamaesphacos, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery, fuzzy leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, fleshy fruits. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand long periods of drought.

Its unique morphology makes it an interesting subject for botanical study in xerophytic ecosystems.
