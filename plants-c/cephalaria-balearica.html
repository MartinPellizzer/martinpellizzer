Cephalaria balearica

Cephalaria balearica, commonly known as the Balearic cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is found in coastal areas and mountainous regions. It has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and often have a bluish-green hue.

Cephalaria balearica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
