Chrysothamnus affinis

Chrysothamnus affinis, commonly known as the common goldenbush, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and produces small, yellow flowers in the spring. The plant has a woody stem and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a slightly hairy texture.

Chrysothamnus affinis is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native range.
