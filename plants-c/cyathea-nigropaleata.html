Cyathea nigropaleata

Cyathea nigropaleata, commonly known as the black-pale leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a slow growth rate and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and swamps. Its rhizome system is robust, allowing it to anchor firmly in the soil and absorb nutrients efficiently.

The fronds are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
