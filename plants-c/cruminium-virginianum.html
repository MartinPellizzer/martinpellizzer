Cruminium virginianum

Cruminium virginianum, commonly known as the Virginia crinum, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces a tall, slender stem with clusters of white or pink flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
