Coryphantha bella

Coryphantha bella, commonly known as the beautiful coryphantha, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a small, solitary plant with a short, cylindrical stem that often branches at the base. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, spherical fruits. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted to arid environments.

Coryphantha bella is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in rock gardens and desert landscapes.
