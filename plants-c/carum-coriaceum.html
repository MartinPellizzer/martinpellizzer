Carum coriaceum

Carum coriaceum, commonly known as Persian cumin or wild cumin, is an annual herb native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Central Asia.

It features slender, branching stems and small, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine and cooking. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into cylindrical, dark brown seed pods containing numerous tiny seeds. Its botanical name reflects its leathery (coriaceum) leaves, which are distinctive and contribute to its aromatic qualities.

Carum coriaceum is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in herbal remedies for digestive and respiratory ailments.
