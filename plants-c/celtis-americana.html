Celtis americana

Celtis americana, commonly known as the American hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers that give way to spherical, pale yellow fruits. Its leaves and fruits are often used in traditional medicine, and the tree is valued for its adaptability to various soil types.

Celtis americana is also a host plant for several butterfly species, contributing to local biodiversity.
