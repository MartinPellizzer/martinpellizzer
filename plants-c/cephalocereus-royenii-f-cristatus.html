Cephalocereus royenii f. cristatus

Cephalocereus royenii f. cristatus, commonly known as the Cristate Hedgehog Cactus, is a unique variant of the standard Cephalocereus royenii.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flattened, crest-like growth pattern that forms a rosette of fleshy, spiny segments. This form results from a natural mutation known as "cristation," which causes the plant to grow in a fan-like shape rather than the typical upright structure. The plant is native to Mexico and thrives in arid environments with well-draining soil.

It produces yellow flowers in the evening, adding an ornamental value to its already striking appearance.
