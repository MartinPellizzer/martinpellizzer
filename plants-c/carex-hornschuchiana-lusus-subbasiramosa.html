Carex hornschuchiana lusus subbasiramosa

Carex hornschuchiana lusus subbasiramosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to various wetland habitats and its ecological importance in supporting aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
