Cryptocarya infectoria var. acuminulata

Cryptocarya infectoria var. acuminulata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cryptocarya, is a species native to eastern Australia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant features lanceolate to ovate leaves that are usually glossy and have a distinct aromatic scent. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, arranged in clusters, and it produces small, round berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and resilience.
