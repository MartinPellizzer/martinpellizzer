Cucurbita lundelliana

Cucurbita lundelliana, commonly known as the Lundell's pumpkin, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and squash. The plant produces large, elongated fruits with a rough, dark green skin. Its flowers are typically yellow, and it is primarily cultivated for its edible seeds and ornamental value.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
