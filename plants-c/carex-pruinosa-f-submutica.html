Carex pruinosa f. submutica

Carex pruinosa f. submutica, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a variant of the pruinosa subspecies within the Carex genus.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The leaves are typically covered in a fine, silky pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

This plant plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
