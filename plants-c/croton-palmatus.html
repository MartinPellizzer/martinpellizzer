Croton palmatus

Croton palmatus, commonly known as the fan palm, is a tropical plant characterized by its distinctive fan-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is native to Southeast Asia. The plant features a slender, unbranched trunk that is often covered in a fibrous sheath. Its leaves are large, broad, and arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a unique appearance.

Croton palmatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in warm, humid climates.
