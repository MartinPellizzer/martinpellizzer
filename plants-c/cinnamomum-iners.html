Cinnamomum iners

Cinnamomum iners, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Ceylon cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the樟科 (Lauraceae) family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and oval-shaped berries that contain dark brown seeds. Its bark is used in traditional medicine and as a spice, while its leaves are often used in perfumery.
