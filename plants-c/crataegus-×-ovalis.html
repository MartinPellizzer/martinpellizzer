Crataegus × ovalis

Crataegus × ovalis, commonly known as the oval hawthorn, is a hybrid shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers in spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are a food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and horticultural use.
