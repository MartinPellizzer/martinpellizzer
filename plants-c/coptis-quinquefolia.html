Coptis quinquefolia

Coptis quinquefolia, commonly known as the eastern goldthread, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant contains alkaloids, particularly berberine, which has medicinal properties. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its rhizomes are often harvested for use in traditional medicine and herbal remedies.
