Cuphea ramosa

Cuphea ramosa, commonly known as the whorled cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Cuphea ramosa is also known for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
