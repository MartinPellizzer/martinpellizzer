Campanula alliariifolia var. silvatica

Campanula alliariifolia var. silvatica, commonly known as the forest bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features basal rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
