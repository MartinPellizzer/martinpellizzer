Clematis viorna var. flavida

Clematis viorna var. flavida, commonly known as the yellow clematis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, usually yellow or white flowers. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically borne in clusters along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its botanical features make it both aesthetically pleasing and ecologically valuable in suitable climates.
