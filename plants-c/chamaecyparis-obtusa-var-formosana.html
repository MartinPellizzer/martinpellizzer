Chamaecyparis obtusa var. formosana

Chamaecyparis obtusa var. formosana, commonly known as the Formosan cedar, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer native to Taiwan.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and white underneath. The tree produces small, round cones that are typically retained on the branches for several years. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental tree in subtropical climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and its ability to provide a unique texture in landscape design.
