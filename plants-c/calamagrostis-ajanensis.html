Calamagrostis ajanensis

Calamagrostis ajanensis, commonly known as the Ajan grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia, including China and Korea.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often adorned with silky hairs. This plant thrives in moist or wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks, and is often found in riparian zones. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are typically green to purplish in color.

Calamagrostis ajanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
