Cleisostoma expansum

Cleisostoma expansum, commonly known as the broadleaf night bloomer, is a tropical climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, showy flowers that open at night. The plant produces pale yellow to white petals with a distinctive, recurved lip that attracts nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its leaves are broad and ovate, often with a waxy texture, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Cleisostoma expansum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and unique blooming habit.
