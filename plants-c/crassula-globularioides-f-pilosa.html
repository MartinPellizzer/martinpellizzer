Crassula globularioides f. pilosa

Crassula globularioides f. pilosa, commonly known as the woolly crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded rosette of fleshy, bluish-green leaves covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The leaves are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water, making it well-suited for arid environments. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the summer months.

This cultivar is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
