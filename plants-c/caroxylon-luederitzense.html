Caroxylon luederitzense

Caroxylon luederitzense, commonly known as the Luederitz date palm, is a rare and ancient plant native to the Namib Desert in Namibia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of long, pinnate leaves that are adapted to withstand extreme aridity. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear edible dates, although its fruit is not as commonly consumed as that of other date palms. Due to its unique adaptations, it plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its harsh environment.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this endangered species from habitat loss and climate change.
