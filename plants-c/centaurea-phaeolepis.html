Centaurea phaeolepis

Centaurea phaeolepis, commonly known as the purple centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, often found in Mediterranean climates. Its striking purple-blue flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant has been historically used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
