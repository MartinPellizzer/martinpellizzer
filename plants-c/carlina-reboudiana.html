Carlina reboudiana

Carlina reboudiana, commonly known as the Italian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust stem structure. The plant features spiny, gray-green leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. Its showy purple-blue flower heads appear in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Carlina reboudiana is often found in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments.
