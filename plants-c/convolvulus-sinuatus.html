Convolvulus sinuatus

Convolvulus sinuatus, commonly known as the sinuous morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a vigorous, flowering vine.
