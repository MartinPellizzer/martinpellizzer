Chenopodium ovalifolium

Chenopodium ovalifolium, commonly known as pigweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its alternate, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Chenopodium ovalifolium is an annual species that can become invasive in certain regions.
