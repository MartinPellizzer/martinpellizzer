Carex gunniana

Carex gunniana, commonly known as Gunn's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open forested or alpine environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex gunniana is often found in subalpine and alpine zones, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
