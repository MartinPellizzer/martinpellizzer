Caralluma adscendens var. carinata

Caralluma adscendens var. carinata, commonly known as the spineless devil's tongue, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to reddish in color, with small, spiny leaves that are often reduced or modified. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is adapted to withstand extreme drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This species is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
