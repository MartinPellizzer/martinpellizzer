Caladenia transitoria subsp. transitoria

Caladenia transitoria subsp.

transitoria, commonly known as the Transient Spider-orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and delicate, spider-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as heathlands and woodlands. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive lip that resembles a spider's abdomen.

This subspecies is known for its ephemeral blooming period, often flowering after rainfall in spring.
