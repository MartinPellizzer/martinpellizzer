Cynoglossum cristulatum

Cynoglossum cristulatum, commonly known as the crested bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often with a white or yellow throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

Cynoglossum cristulatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
