Carduus longifolius

Carduus longifolius, commonly known as the greater centaury, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust, upright stem covered in coarse, hairy texture. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom from summer to autumn, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are deeply lobed and have a distinctive blue-green color with a rough texture.

Carduus longifolius is often found in grasslands, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils.
