Cymbidium fragrans

Cymbidium fragrans, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and dense clusters of fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in winter or early spring, producing pendulous flowers in shades of white, pink, and red. Its leaves are elongated, strap-shaped, and arranged in two rows along the stem.

Cymbidium fragrans is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower.
