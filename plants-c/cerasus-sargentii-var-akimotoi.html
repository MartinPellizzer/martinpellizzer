Cerasus sargentii var. akimotoi

Cerasus sargentii var. akimotoi, commonly known as the Japanese cherry plum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly during the spring when it produces clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that turn yellow in autumn, adding to its seasonal appeal. Its fruit is a small plum that is edible but not typically cultivated for commercial use.

The species is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic beauty and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
