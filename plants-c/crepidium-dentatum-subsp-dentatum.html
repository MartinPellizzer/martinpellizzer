Crepidium dentatum subsp. dentatum

Crepidium dentatum subsp.

dentatum, commonly known as the toothed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to parts of Asia and the Pacific. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands.

The plant features a slender, erect stem with a few leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers with a distinct tooth-like appearance on the petals. It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique morphological features.

The subspecies is notable for its specialized habitat requirements and limited distribution.
