Cheiranthus villosus

Cheiranthus villosus, commonly known as the hairy cheiranthus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow or white flowers that bloom in early spring. It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Cheiranthus villosus is typically perennial and thrives in temperate climates.
