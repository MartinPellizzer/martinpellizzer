Cryptanthus colnagoi

Cryptanthus colnagoi, commonly known as the "Rainbow Cranesbill," is a striking member of the Geraniaceae family native to Brazil.

This plant is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The flowers are typically small and inconspicuous, with the showy bracts serving as the primary ornamental feature. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its vivid coloration.

Cryptanthus colnagoi is a popular choice for rock gardens and containers due to its compact size and long-lasting display.
