Copiapoa cinerea var. krainziana

Copiapoa cinerea var. krainziana, commonly known as the Krainziana Pencil Cactus, is a species of cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often covered in a white, powdery coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, adapting to the arid environment.

It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-draining soil and full sunlight.
