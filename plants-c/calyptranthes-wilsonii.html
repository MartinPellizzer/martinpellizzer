Calyptranthes wilsonii

Calyptranthes wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's calyptranthes, is a species of flowering plant in the family Euphorbiaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly Costa Rica and Panama. The plant is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy dark green upper surface.

The flowers are small and clustered in umbels, often surrounded by specialized structures called cyathia, which are characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family.
