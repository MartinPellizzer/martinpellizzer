Caricina approximata

Caricina approximata, commonly known as the African marsh hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in wet or marshy habitats. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are often pink or red in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture.

Caricina approximata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
