Calycera andina

Calycera andina, commonly known as the Andean calycera, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the family Calyceraceae and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to the cool, arid conditions of its native habitat. It is often found growing in rocky soils at elevations ranging from 3,000 to 5,000 meters.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
