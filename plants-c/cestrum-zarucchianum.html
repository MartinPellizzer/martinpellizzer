Cestrum zarucchianum

Cestrum zarucchianum, commonly known as the red-flowered cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a rough texture, while its fruits are small berries that turn red when mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant scent.
