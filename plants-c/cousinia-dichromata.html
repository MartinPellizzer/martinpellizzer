Cousinia dichromata

Cousinia dichromata, commonly known as the two-colored cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Cousinia dichromata is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
