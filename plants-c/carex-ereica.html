Carex ereica

Carex ereica, commonly known as slender bog sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Carex ereica thrives in poorly drained soils and is often found in association with other wetland plants.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
