Cattleya × eximioides

Cattleya × eximioides, commonly known as the orchid with the "flamingo pink" flowers, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy blooms that typically range in color from pink to white, often with a distinctive red or orange lip. The plant is an epiphyte, meaning it grows on the surface of trees or other structures rather than in soil. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Cattleya × eximioides is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
