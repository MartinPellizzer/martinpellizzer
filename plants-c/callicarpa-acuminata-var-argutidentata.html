Callicarpa acuminata var. argutidentata

Callicarpa acuminata var. argutidentata, commonly known as the rough-leaved beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that appear in late summer. The leaves are ovate with a rough, serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
