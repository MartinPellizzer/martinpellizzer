Cylindropuntia caerulescens

Cylindropuntia caerulescens, commonly known as the blue prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are typically blue-green in color, covered with dense clusters of spines. The plant produces showy yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tunas, is a fleshy, edible pear-shaped structure that ripens to a red or purple color.

This species is adapted to arid environments and plays an important role in desert ecosystems.
