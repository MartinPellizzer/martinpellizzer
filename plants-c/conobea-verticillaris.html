Conobea verticillaris

Conobea verticillaris, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue and purple. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Conobea verticillaris prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for garden settings.
