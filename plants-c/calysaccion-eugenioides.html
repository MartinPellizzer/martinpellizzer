Calysaccion eugenioides

Calysaccion eugenioides, commonly known as the white-flowered bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
