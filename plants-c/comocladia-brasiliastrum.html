Comocladia brasiliastrum

Comocladia brasiliastrum, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped fruit, which is typically yellow or orange when mature. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are simple and alternate, often with a glossy appearance.

The fruit is not only ornamental but also has some culinary and medicinal uses in traditional practices.
