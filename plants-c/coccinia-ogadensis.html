Coccinia ogadensis

Coccinia ogadensis, commonly known as the African butterfruit, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. The plant belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers and squash. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

The plant is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses in local cuisines.
