Coumarouna crassifolia

Coumarouna crassifolia, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to Central and Eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to edible, round, green fruits. The fruit has a thick, inedible rind and a starchy, white pulp that is used in various traditional dishes. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The plant has cultural and economic significance in many African communities.
