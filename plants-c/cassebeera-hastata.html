Cassebeera hastata

Cassebeera hastata, commonly known as the red-veined dayflower, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces showy, two-lipped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
