Centaurea mariana

Centaurea mariana, commonly known as the American centaury, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, bristly leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea mariana is often found in the eastern and central regions of North America.
