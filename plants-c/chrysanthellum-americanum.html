Chrysanthellum americanum

Chrysanthellum americanum, commonly known as the American chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with ray florets surrounding a central disk.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
