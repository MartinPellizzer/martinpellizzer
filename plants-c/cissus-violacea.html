Cissus violacea

Cissus violacea, commonly known as the purple leaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, purple-tinged leaves that give it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Cissus violacea has a vigorous growth habit and can be trained to grow on trellises or walls.
