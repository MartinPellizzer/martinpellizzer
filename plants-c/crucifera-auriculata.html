Crucifera auriculata

Crucifera auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive auriculate (ear-shaped) leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Native to temperate regions, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological studies and horticultural practices.
