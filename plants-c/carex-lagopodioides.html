Carex lagopodioides

Carex lagopodioides, commonly known as the hare's foot sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are usually green or brown in color.

Carex lagopodioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
