Cynoglossum nebrodense subsp. pustulatum

Cynoglossum nebrodense subsp.

pustulatum, commonly known as the Italian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features basal leaves that are heart-shaped and covered with fine hairs, and it produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy stems and the presence of glandular dots on the leaves.
