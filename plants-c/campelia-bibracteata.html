Campelia bibracteata

Campelia bibracteata, commonly known as the yellow bell.

It is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its showy, yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring. It has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a rough texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.
