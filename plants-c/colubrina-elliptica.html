Colubrina elliptica

Colubrina elliptica, commonly known as the narrowleaf colubrina, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features elliptical, dark green leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often attracting pollinators like butterflies and bees. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

Colubrina elliptica is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
