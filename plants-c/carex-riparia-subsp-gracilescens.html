Carex riparia subsp. gracilescens

Carex riparia subsp.

gracilescens, commonly known as slender river sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and riparian habitats. It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found along streams, rivers, and marshes.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
