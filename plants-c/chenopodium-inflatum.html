Chenopodium inflatum

Chenopodium inflatum, commonly known as the inflated goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, saccate fruits that resemble small, balloon-like structures. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides. Its leaves are usually alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in a fine layer of white, powdery bloom.

The species is sometimes cultivated as a forage crop due to its nutritional value and hardy nature.
