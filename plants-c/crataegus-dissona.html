Crataegus dissona

Crataegus dissona, commonly known as the discordant hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its distinctive, often irregularly shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It typically grows in moist woodlands and is often found in riparian habitats.

Crataegus dissona is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
