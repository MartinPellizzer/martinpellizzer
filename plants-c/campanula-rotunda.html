Campanula rotunda

Campanula rotunda, commonly known as the round bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy stems and leaves that are rounded and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically lobed and may have a slightly waxy texture.

Campanula rotunda is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
