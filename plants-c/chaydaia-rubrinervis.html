Chaydaia rubrinervis

Chaydaia rubrinervis, commonly known as the red-veined chaydaia, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored leaves with prominent red veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
