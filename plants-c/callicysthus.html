Callicysthus

Callicysthus, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. Its leaves are broad and glossy, often with a slight waxy coating.

Callicysthus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
