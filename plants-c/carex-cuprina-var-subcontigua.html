Carex cuprina var. subcontigua

Carex cuprina var. subcontigua, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in wet or moist habitats. This variety is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist environments where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
