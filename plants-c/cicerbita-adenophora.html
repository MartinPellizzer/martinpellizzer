Cicerbita adenophora

Cicerbita adenophora, commonly known as the hairy bittercress, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.

Cicerbita adenophora is an annual plant that completes its life cycle within a single growing season.
