Centaurea paniculata subsp. leucophaea

Centaurea paniculata subsp.

leucophaea, commonly known as the white-headed thistle, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features upright, branching stems covered in soft, grayish-green foliage.

The plant produces showy, purple to pink flower heads that are surrounded by white, fluffy pappus hairs, giving it its common name. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats like meadows and woodland edges.
