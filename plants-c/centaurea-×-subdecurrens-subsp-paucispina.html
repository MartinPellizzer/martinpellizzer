Centaurea × subdecurrens subsp. paucispina

Centaurea × subdecurrens subsp.

paucispina, commonly known as the narrowleaf star-thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features spiny, hairy stems and lanceolate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has a taproot system and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and is part of the Asteraceae family.

Due to its invasive potential in some regions, it is sometimes considered a noxious weed.
