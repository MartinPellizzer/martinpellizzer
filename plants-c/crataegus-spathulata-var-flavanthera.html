Crataegus spathulata var. flavanthera

Crataegus spathulata var. flavanthera, commonly known as the yellow-flowered hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically smooth or slightly hairy on the upper surface. The flowers are showy, ranging from white to yellow, and appear in clusters during the spring. The fruit is a small, red to orange berry that ripens in the fall.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant seasonal display.
