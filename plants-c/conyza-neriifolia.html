Conyza neriifolia

Conyza neriifolia, commonly known as blackleaf fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often dark green. Its flowers are yellow with white margins, and it produces small, dry fruits called achenes.

Conyza neriifolia is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
