Caltha sibirica

Caltha sibirica, commonly known as the Siberian buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northern regions of Asia.

It features bright yellow flowers with a distinctive cup-shaped structure, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are alternate and covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and stream banks.

Caltha sibirica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
