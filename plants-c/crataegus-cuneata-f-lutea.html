Crataegus cuneata f. lutea

Crataegus cuneata f. lutea, commonly known as the yellow thornapple, is a variant of the common hawthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its yellowish-orange fruit, which contrasts with the typical red or pink fruits of the species. The plant features thorny branches, simple leaves with serrated edges, and is native to the eastern United States. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental plant in horticulture.
