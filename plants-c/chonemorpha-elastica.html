Chonemorpha elastica

Chonemorpha elastica, commonly known as the rubber plant, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that can grow quite large. The plant is noted for its ability to store water in its leaves, making it drought-tolerant. Its stems are flexible and can be used for propagation.

The rubber plant is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
