Cineraria clusiana

Cineraria clusiana, commonly known as the blue cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small, daisy-like blooms.

Cineraria clusiana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and hardiness.
