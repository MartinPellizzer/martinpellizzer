Calycanthus floridus var. laevigatus

Calycanthus floridus var. laevigatus, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and provide dense foliage. Its root system is fibrous and helps it thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

The sweetshrub is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
