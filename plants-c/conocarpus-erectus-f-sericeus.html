Conocarpus erectus f. sericeus

Conocarpus erectus f. sericeus, commonly known as the silver mangrove, is a variant of the mangrove species native to coastal regions of the Indo-Pacific.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white pubescent leaves, which give it its common name. The plant thrives in saline and brackish water environments, often forming dense mangrove forests. Its pneumatophores, or breathing roots, help it extract oxygen from waterlogged soil.

This species plays a vital role in coastal ecosystems by stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
