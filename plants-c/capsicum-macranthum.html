Capsicum macranthum

Capsicum macranthum, commonly known as the giant capsicum or large-fruited pepper, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with large, broad leaves and showy, yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, red or orange fruits that can reach lengths of up to 20 centimeters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to gardens and a subject of interest in botanical research.
