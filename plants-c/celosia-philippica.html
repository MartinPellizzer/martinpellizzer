Celosia philippica

Celosia philippica, commonly known as the flame flower or churchwarden, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, spiky bracts that resemble flames. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in dense, colorful inflorescences.

Celosia philippica is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in warm climates.
