Crataegus tersa

Crataegus tersa, commonly known as the smooth thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed areas.

Crataegus tersa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
