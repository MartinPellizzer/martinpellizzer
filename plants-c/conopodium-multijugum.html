Conopodium multijugum

Conopodium multijugum, commonly known as the many-lobed bladderwort, is a carnivorous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It is characterized by its small, bladder-like structures that trap tiny aquatic organisms. The plant typically grows in shallow water and has slender, branching stems. Its leaves are often divided into multiple lobes, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Conopodium multijugum plays an important ecological role in nutrient cycling within its habitat.
