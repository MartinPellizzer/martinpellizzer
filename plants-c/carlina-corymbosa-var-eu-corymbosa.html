Carlina corymbosa var. eu-corymbosa

Carlina corymbosa var. eu-corymbosa, commonly known as the European garden carline thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe.

It features a rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves that are spoon-shaped and covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces showy, yellow-centered composite flower heads in late summer to early autumn. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
