Cuscuta japonica f. thyrsoidea

Cuscuta japonica f. thyrsoidea, commonly known as Japanese dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has slender, thread-like stems that wrap around its host. The plant is native to East Asia and is often found growing on various herbaceous plants. Its life cycle involves germination from seeds, attachment to a host, and the formation of haustoria to extract water and nutrients.

It is considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its ability to spread rapidly and reduce crop yields.
