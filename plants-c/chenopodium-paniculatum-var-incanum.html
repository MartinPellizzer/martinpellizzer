Chenopodium paniculatum var. incanum

Chenopodium paniculatum var. incanum, commonly known as silverleaf goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in branched clusters, typical of the Chenopodiaceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a potential forage plant.
