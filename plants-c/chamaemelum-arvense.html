Chamaemelum arvense

Chamaemelum arvense, commonly known as field chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces yellow flowers with white petals, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its aromatic leaves and flowers contain compounds like chamazulene, which contribute to its medicinal properties.

Chamaemelum arvense is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory effects.
