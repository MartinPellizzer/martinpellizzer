Cousinia ugamensis

Cousinia ugamensis, commonly known as the Ugam dwarf gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that attract pollinators.

Cousinia ugamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
