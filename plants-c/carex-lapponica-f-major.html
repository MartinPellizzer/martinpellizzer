Carex lapponica f. major

Carex lapponica f. major, commonly known as the large Lapland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with similar environmental conditions.
