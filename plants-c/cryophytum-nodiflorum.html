Cryophytum nodiflorum

Cryophytum nodiflorum, commonly known as the snowbell plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and exhibits a rosette formation of lanceolate, hairy leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to withstand cold temperatures.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
