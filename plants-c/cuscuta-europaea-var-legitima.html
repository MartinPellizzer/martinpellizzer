Cuscuta europaea var. legitima

Cuscuta europaea var. legitima, commonly known as the European dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has slender, thread-like stems that wrap around its host to establish a connection. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often found growing on crops such as wheat and barley. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, animals, or through human activity, allowing it to spread to new areas.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant damage to agricultural crops.
