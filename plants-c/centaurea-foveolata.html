Centaurea foveolata

Centaurea foveolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

Centaurea foveolata is known for its slender, upright stems and narrow, linear leaves.
