Cerasus dielsiana var. abbreviata

Cerasus dielsiana var. abbreviata, commonly known as the dwarf cherry or Chinese dwarf cherry, is a shrub native to China.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red or pink berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its compact size and seasonal color.

This variety is relatively hardy and adaptable to a range of soil types, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
