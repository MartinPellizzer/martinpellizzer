Cyphostemma princeae

Cyphostemma princeae, commonly known as the Prince's moonvine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, simple, elliptic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellow berry that contains several seeds.

Cyphostemma princeae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
