Chamaecytisus supinus var. lupinifolius

Chamaecytisus supinus var. lupinifolius, commonly known as the purple broom or lupin-leaved broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and profuse flowering with bright orange-red tubular flowers. The leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of oval, leathery leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and rocky garden environments.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
