Coccoloba caracasana f. glabra

Coccoloba caracasana f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth coccoloba, is a shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or near freshwater sources.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and seasonal fruit display.
