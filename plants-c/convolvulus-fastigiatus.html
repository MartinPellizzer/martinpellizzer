Convolvulus fastigiatus

Convolvulus fastigiatus, commonly known as the upright bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a trailing or climbing herb with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, blooming from spring to autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Due to its vigorous growth, it can become invasive if not managed properly.
