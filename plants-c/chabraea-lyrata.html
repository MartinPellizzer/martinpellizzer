Chabraea lyrata

Chabraea lyrata, commonly known as the lyreleaf chabraea, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive lyre-shaped leaves, which are often broad and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
