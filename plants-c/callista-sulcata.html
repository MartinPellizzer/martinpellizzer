Callista sulcata

Callista sulcata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Callista sulcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
