Caesalpinia domingensis

Caesalpinia domingensis, commonly known as the Dominican glory flower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly the Dominican Republic.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces bright red or orange flowers that are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are bipinnate, meaning they are divided into small, feather-like leaflets.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
