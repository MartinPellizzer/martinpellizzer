Conopodium majus subsp. marizianum

Conopodium majus subsp.

marizianum, commonly known as the alpine sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically succulent and arranged in a rosette pattern, adapted to harsh alpine conditions.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.
