Cnicothamnus lorentzii var. azafran

Cnicothamnus lorentzii var. azafran, commonly known as the bluebells of the Andes, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climatic conditions.
