Chamaecrista azulana

Chamaecrista azulana, commonly known as blue crown bean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive blue-green hue. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are followed by elongated, narrow pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Chamaecrista azulana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its nitrogen-fixing properties.
