Castanea crenata var. pleiocarpa

Castanea crenata var. pleiocarpa, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut or Kintoki chestnut, is a variant of the chestnut tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its deciduous nature, producing oval-shaped nuts enclosed in spiny husks. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and serrated, typically appearing in a dense canopy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional Japanese culture.
