Cousinia lamprophyllaria

Cousinia lamprophyllaria, commonly known as the silverleaf cousinia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, yellowish flowers in late summer. The plant has silver-gray foliage due to dense covering of fine hairs, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Cousinia lamprophyllaria is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
