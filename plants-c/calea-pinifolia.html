Calea pinifolia

Calea pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf calea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, pine-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Calea pinifolia is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
