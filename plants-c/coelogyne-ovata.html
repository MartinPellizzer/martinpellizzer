Coelogyne ovata

Coelogyne ovata, commonly known as the oval orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendant, showy flowers that typically bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has long, strap-like leaves and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright indirect light.

Coelogyne ovata is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its fragrant, colorful blooms.
