Carex comans f. stricta

Carex comans f. stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a variant of the Carex comans species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks.

Its botanical structure makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
