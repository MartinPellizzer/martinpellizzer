Cyperus arenarius

Cyperus arenarius, commonly known as the sand flats sedge, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or saline soils, often found in coastal dunes and salt marshes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is adapted to withstand high salinity and drought conditions.

Cyperus arenarius plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
