Cyanospermum calycinum

Cyanospermum calycinum, commonly known as the blue-green alga, is a type of cyanobacteria that thrives in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its ability to perform photosynthesis and produce oxygen, contributing to aquatic ecosystems. The organism is capable of forming dense blooms, which can impact water quality and aquatic life. It contains various pigments, including phycocyanin, which gives it its distinctive blue-green color.

Cyanospermum calycinum plays a significant role in nutrient cycling and is often used in biotechnological applications.
