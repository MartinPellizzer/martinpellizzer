Calamus billitonensis

Calamus billitonensis, commonly known as the Billiton reed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, where it grows in tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, fibrous fruits.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, Calamus billitonensis is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
