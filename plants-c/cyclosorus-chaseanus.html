Cyclosorus chaseanus

Cyclosorus chaseanus, commonly known as the Chase's cyclosorus, is a rare aquatic fern native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has finely divided, delicate fronds that form a rosette shape, often floating just below the water's surface. Its reproductive structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.
