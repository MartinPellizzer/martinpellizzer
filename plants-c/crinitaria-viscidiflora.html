Crinitaria viscidiflora

Crinitaria viscidiflora, commonly known as the sticky-flowered crinitaria, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, often hairy foliage and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
