Chrysochlamys multiflora var. pachypoda

Chrysochlamys multiflora var. pachypoda, commonly known as the broad-leaved chrysochlamys, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
