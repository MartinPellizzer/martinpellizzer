Costia caucasica

Costia caucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian costia, is a species of aquatic plant found in freshwater environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, branching structure and small, ribbon-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow waters and is often found in regions with temperate climates. Costia caucasica plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.

Its presence is an indicator of water quality and ecological health in its native habitats.
