Cananga sellowiana

Cananga sellowiana, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and its distinctive, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Anacardiaceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its showy blossoms. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, often with a glossy appearance.

The tree is also valued for its potential medicinal properties, though further research is needed to confirm its therapeutic uses.
