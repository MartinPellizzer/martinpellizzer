Capsella agrestis-rubella

Capsella agrestis-rubella, commonly known as the red weed or field pennycress, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and has a low-growing habit with trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by triangular pods containing several seeds. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive reddish tinge, especially on the underside.

Capsella agrestis-rubella is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas, making it a common weed in agricultural settings.
