Convolvulus munitus

Convolvulus munitus, commonly known as the rock dodder, is a flowering plant belonging to the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae).

It is a small, annual herb that typically grows in rocky or arid environments, often found in Mediterranean regions. The plant has thin, trailing stems with small, pale green leaves and produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers. Its flowers are usually five-petaled and bloom in the spring.

Convolvulus munitus is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
