Carduus valentinus

Carduus valentinus, commonly known as the Valentine's thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads that resemble small thistle blooms. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in dry, sunny environments such as grasslands and wastelands. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into bristly, spiky seed heads.

Its botanical features make it both a distinctive ornamental plant and a potential weed in certain regions.
