Crataegus karadaghensis

Crataegus karadaghensis, commonly known as the Karadagh hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the Caucasus region, specifically found in parts of Azerbaijan and northern Iran. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that appear in spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that ripen in late summer and are edible.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
