Cousinia ecbatanensis

Cousinia ecbatanensis, commonly known as the Ecbatana thistle, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its robust, spiny stems and serrated leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Cousinia ecbatanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
