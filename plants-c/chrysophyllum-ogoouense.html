Chrysophyllum ogoouense

Chrysophyllum ogoouense, commonly known as the African star apple, is a small evergreen tree native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces a pear-shaped fruit with a thick, edible pulp surrounding a single seed. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
