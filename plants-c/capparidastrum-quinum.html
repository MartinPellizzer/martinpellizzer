Capparidastrum quinum

Capparidastrum quinum, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Capparaceae and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
