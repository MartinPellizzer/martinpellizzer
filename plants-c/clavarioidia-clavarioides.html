Clavarioidia clavarioides

Clavarioidia clavarioides, commonly known as the club fungus, is a type of basidiomycete fungus characterized by its club-shaped or branched fruiting body.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and grasslands, often on decaying wood or soil. The spore-bearing structures, called basidia, are found on the surface of the fruiting body, which can vary in color from yellow to reddish-brown. This species is often found in association with certain tree species, particularly oaks and pines, and plays a role in decomposing organic matter.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for mycological study.
