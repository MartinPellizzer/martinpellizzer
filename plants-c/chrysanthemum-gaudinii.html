Chrysanthemum gaudinii

Chrysanthemum gaudinii, commonly known as the Gaudin's chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically produces vibrant yellow to orange blooms, often with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Chrysanthemum gaudinii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
