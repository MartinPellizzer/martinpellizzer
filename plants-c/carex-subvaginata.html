Carex subvaginata

Carex subvaginata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in clusters at the top of the stems. Carex subvaginata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
