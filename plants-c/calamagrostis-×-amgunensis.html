Calamagrostis × amgunensis

Calamagrostis × amgunensis, commonly known as the silvergrass or giant reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the wetlands of eastern Asia.

It features narrow, silver-gray blades that give it a distinctive appearance, and it often grows in dense clumps. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist or wet conditions, making it suitable for riparian zones and wetland restoration projects. Its flowering spikes are usually pinkish or purplish, adding ornamental value to gardens and natural landscapes.

Calamagrostis × amgunensis is also valued for its erosion control capabilities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
