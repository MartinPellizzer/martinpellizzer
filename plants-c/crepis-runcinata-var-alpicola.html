Crepis runcinata var. alpicola

Crepis runcinata var. alpicola, commonly known as the alpine buttonweed, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, providing a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient member of alpine flora.
