Clusia botryoidea

Clusia botryoidea, commonly known as the wax plant or Brazilian waxleaf, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss in its native habitat. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is a member of the Clusiaceae family, also known as the guttiferae family, and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Clusia botryoidea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and resilience to poor soil conditions.
