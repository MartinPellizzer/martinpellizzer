Ceropegia retrospiciens

Ceropegia retrospiciens, commonly known as the Cape raffia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and small, waxy leaves. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are often pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ceropegia retrospiciens is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
