Casearia piscidia

Casearia piscidia, commonly known as the yellow casearia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature, attracting various bird species.

Casearia piscidia is often found in moist forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
