Cochlearia armoracia var. macrocarpa

Cochlearia armoracia var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant yellow horned-poppy, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its bright yellow flowers and distinctive, inflated seed pods. The plant typically grows in coastal and maritime environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The variety macrocarpa is notable for its larger, more robust seed pods compared to other varieties of Cochlearia.
