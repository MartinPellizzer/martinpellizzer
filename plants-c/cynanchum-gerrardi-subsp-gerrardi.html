Cynanchum gerrardi subsp. gerrardi

Cynanchum gerrardi subsp.

gerrardi, commonly known as the Carolina snakeplant, is a member of the Asclepiadaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its erect, slender stems and opposite, lanceolate leaves that are typically gray-green in color.

The plant produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers in umbels, which are followed by follicles containing seeds. It is native to the southeastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate a variety of growing conditions.
