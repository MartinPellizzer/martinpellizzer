Chloris elongata

Chloris elongata, commonly known as slender grass or narrowleaf grass, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

Chloris elongata is a hardy perennial that plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
