Chesneya acaulis

Chesneya acaulis, commonly known as the leafless chesneya, is a unique succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves, with photosynthesis occurring through its thick, fleshy stems. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its specialized adaptations, it is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a decorative succulent in arid climates.
