Capparidastrum petiolare

Capparidastrum petiolare, commonly known as the slender caper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Capparidaceae family, characterized by its distinctive, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring compound leaves with serrated margins. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish-yellow, with prominent stamens that attract pollinators.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, flat seeds.
