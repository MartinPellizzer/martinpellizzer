Cranocarpus

Cranocarpus, commonly known as the sea holly, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads, which are surrounded by spiny, leaf-like bracts. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and saline soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Cranocarpus species are often used in ornamental gardening due to their unique appearance and hardy nature.
