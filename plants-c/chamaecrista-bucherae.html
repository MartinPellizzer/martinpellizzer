Chamaecrista bucherae

Chamaecrista bucherae, commonly known as Buchera's burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny pods. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
