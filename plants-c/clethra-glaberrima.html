Clethra glaberrima

Clethra glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth clethra, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and has slender, reddish stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are clustered in cylindrical inflorescences and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Clethra glaberrima prefers moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland areas.
