Calceolaria ascendens

Calceolaria ascendens, commonly known as the ascending hooded orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, hood-like flowers that resemble small orchids, with intricate patterns and vibrant colors. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, thriving in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh conditions of its habitat.

Calceolaria ascendens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
