Coelogyne cruciformis var. cruciformis

Coelogyne cruciformis var. cruciformis, commonly known as the cruciform coelogyne, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with a distinctive cross-shaped pattern on the petals. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat with minimal rainfall. Its leaves are sword-shaped and often grow in a rosette formation, contributing to its unique appearance.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and fragrance.
