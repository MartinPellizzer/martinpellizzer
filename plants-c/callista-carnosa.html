Callista carnosa

Callista carnosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are often a deep green with a soft texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow, typically blooming during the warmer months. Callista carnosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
