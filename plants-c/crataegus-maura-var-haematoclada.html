Crataegus maura var. haematoclada

Crataegus maura var. haematoclada, commonly known as the blood-red hawthorn, is a variant of the hawthorn genus characterized by its distinctive red-pink flowers and fruit.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and Asia, often found in woodland edges and rocky soils. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but typically have a tart flavor. Its ornamental value is due to its showy spring blossoms and vibrant autumn foliage.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential cardiovascular benefits.
