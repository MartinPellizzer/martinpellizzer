Carex misandra f. flavida

Carex misandra f. flavida, commonly known as the yellow sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

The yellow sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
