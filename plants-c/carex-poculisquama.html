Carex poculisquama

Carex poculisquama, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex poculisquama is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
