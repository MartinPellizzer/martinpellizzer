Cestrum ovalifolium

Cestrum ovalifolium, commonly known as the night-blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom at night. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cestrum ovalifolium is also known for its strong, pleasant scent that attracts nocturnal pollinators.
