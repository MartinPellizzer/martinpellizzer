Cardamine macrophylla var. dasyloba

Cardamine macrophylla var. dasyloba, commonly known as the broad-leaf water crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive basal leaves that are broadly ovate to heart-shaped with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
