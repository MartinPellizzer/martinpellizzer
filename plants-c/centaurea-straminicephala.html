Centaurea straminicephala

Centaurea straminicephala, commonly known as the redhead clover, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer, typically from July to September. The plant has upright, branching stems and hairy leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas.

Centaurea straminicephala is a perennial species that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters.
