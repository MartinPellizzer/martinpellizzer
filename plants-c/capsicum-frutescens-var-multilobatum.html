Capsicum frutescens var. multilobatum

Capsicum frutescens var. multilobatum, commonly known as the bird's eye chili, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub-like plant characterized by its small, pungent fruits that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and the plant produces yellow flowers that develop into the characteristic red or orange fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its culinary and medicinal uses.

The plant's botanical features make it well-suited for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
