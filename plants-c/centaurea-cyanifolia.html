Centaurea cyanifolia

Centaurea cyanifolia, commonly known as the blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Centaurea cyanifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
