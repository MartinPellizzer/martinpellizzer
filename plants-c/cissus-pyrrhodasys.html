Cissus pyrrhodasys

Cissus pyrrhodasys, commonly known as the red-leaved cissus, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or purple leaves, which contrast with its pale green stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, yellowish fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Cissus pyrrhodasys is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.
