Chloranthus brachystachys var. flavus

Chloranthus brachystachys var. flavus, commonly known as the yellow-flowered boxwood, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Chloranthaceae.

It is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It is native to parts of East Asia, including Japan and China, and thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely used in horticulture.
