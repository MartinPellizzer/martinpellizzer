Chamaelinum paniculatum

Chamaelinum paniculatum, commonly known as the slender panicled bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
