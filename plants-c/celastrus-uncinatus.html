Celastrus uncinatus

Celastrus uncinatus, commonly known as the hooked staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. Its unique hooked thorns on the stems help it to grasp and climb on trees and shrubs.

Celastrus uncinatus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets.
