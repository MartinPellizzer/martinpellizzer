Chaetospora aurata

Chaetospora aurata, commonly known as the golden coral moss, is a unique liverwort species characterized by its intricate, branching structure that resembles coral.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found on rocks or decaying wood in temperate regions. The plant's leaves are small, overlapping, and often have a waxy coating that helps retain moisture. Its reproductive structures, called capsules, are borne on stalks and release spores into the air.

Due to its delicate nature, it is often overlooked but plays a vital role in forest ecosystems.
