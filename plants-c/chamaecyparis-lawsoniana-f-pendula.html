Chamaecyparis lawsoniana f. pendula

Chamaecyparis lawsoniana f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping cedar, is a unique cultivar of the Lawson cypress.

It is characterized by its cascading, drooping branches that create a distinctive, weeping appearance. This ornamental conifer has scale-like leaves that are blue-green in color, adding visual interest to the landscape. The plant is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its elegant form and seasonal color changes.
