Cineraria amelloides

Cineraria amelloides, commonly known as the blue cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features silvery-green, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Cineraria amelloides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
