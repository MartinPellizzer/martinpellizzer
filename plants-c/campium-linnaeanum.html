Campium linnaeanum

Campium linnaeanum, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branched.

The yellow bellflower is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
