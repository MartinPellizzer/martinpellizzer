Chorizema diversifolium

Chorizema diversifolium, commonly known as the desert sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in desert ecosystems.
