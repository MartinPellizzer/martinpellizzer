Calibrachoa

Calibrachoa, commonly known as "million bells," is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family, which also includes tomatoes and potatoes.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and dense clusters of small, vibrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in shades of red, orange, yellow, and white, making it a popular choice for container gardening and hanging baskets. Calibrachoa thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its compact size and continuous blooming make it a versatile addition to gardens and landscapes.
