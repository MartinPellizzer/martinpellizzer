Clusia brachystyla var. brachystyla

Clusia brachystyla var. brachystyla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically lance-shaped and have a dark green upper surface with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by orange to red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Clusia brachystyla var. brachystyla is also known for its air-purifying qualities and resistance to pests and diseases.
