Cassia italica subsp. arachoides

Cassia italica subsp.

arachoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces long, slender pods containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
