Convolvulus sericophyllus

Convolvulus sericophyllus, commonly known as the silkyleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The leaves are velvety and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered invasive in certain regions.
