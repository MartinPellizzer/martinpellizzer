Camunium heptaphylla

Camunium heptaphylla, commonly known as the seven-leafed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branching.

Camunium heptaphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
