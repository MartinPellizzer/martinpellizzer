Crambe persica

Crambe persica, commonly known as Persian crambe, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbage and mustard. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy in texture. Its seeds are edible and have a mild, nutty flavor, making them a valuable crop in some regions.

Crambe persica is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
