Crotalaria africana

Crotalaria africana, commonly known as the African cowpea or devil's bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces elliptical pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Crotalaria africana is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is sometimes used as a green manure crop.

However, some species within the genus are toxic, so caution is advised when handling or consuming any part of the plant.
