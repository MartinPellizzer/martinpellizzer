Chenopodium strictum subsp. microphyllum

Chenopodium strictum subsp.

microphyllum, commonly known as dwarf goosefoot, is a low-growing annual plant native to North America. It typically reaches heights of less than 10 centimeters and has small, fleshy, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

This species is known for its adaptability and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
