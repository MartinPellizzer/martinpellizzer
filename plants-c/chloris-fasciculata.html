Chloris fasciculata

Chloris fasciculata, commonly known as slender grassvetch, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue or purple flowers. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Chloris fasciculata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
