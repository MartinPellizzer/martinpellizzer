Cycas canalis

Cycas canalis, commonly known as the channel cycas, is a species of cycad native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of pinnately compound leaves. The leaves are typically dark green and can reach lengths of up to 2 meters. The plant produces cones, with male cones clustered at the base of the plant and female cones emerging from the crown.

Cycas canalis is a slow-growing plant that thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
