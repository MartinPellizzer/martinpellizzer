Celastrus sieberi

Celastrus sieberi, commonly known as the Sieber's staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellow-green flowers that are followed by bright orange berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
