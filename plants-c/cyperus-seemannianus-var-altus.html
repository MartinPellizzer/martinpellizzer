Cyperus seemannianus var. altus

Cyperus seemannianus var. altus, commonly known as the tall flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. The plant features slender, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth surface and a slight waxy coating.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family.
