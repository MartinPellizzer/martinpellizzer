Cortaderia columbiana

Cortaderia columbiana, commonly known as the Chilean feather reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to South America.

It features slender, arching leaves that are typically silver-white in color, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, feathery flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Cortaderia columbiana prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy choice for temperate climates.
