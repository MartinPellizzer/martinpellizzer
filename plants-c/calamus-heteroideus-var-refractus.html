Calamus heteroideus var. refractus

Calamus heteroideus var. refractus, commonly known as the slender calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. This plant is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional basket weaving. The flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in the axils of the leaves.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
