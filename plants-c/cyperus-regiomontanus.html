Cyperus regiomontanus

Cyperus regiomontanus, commonly known as the spike rush, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical spikelets that are often green or reddish in color. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, making it a common sight in marshy areas and along waterways. Cyperus regiomontanus has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in aquatic environments.
