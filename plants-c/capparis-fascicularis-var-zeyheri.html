Capparis fascicularis var. zeyheri

Capparis fascicularis var. zeyheri, commonly known as the Zeyher's caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with woody stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-white flowers that are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is relatively drought-tolerant.
