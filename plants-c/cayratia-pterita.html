Cayratia pterita

Cayratia pterita, commonly known as the silverleaf vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in loose inflorescences. Its fruit is a slender, fleshy pod that splits open to release tiny, black seeds.

Cayratia pterita is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and vigorous climbing habit.
