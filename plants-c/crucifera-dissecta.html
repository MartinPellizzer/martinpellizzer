Crucifera dissecta

Crucifera dissecta, commonly known as the dissected crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, almost fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, four-petaled flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, typical of the crucifer family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological niche, it is considered a rare and specialized species in its native range.
