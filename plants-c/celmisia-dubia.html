Celmisia dubia

Celmisia dubia, commonly known as the mountain celery.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand, characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are often hairy and have a distinctive, aromatic scent.

Celmisia dubia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
