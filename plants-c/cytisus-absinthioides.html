Cytisus absinthioides

Cytisus absinthioides, commonly known as the silver bush broom, is a shrub native to the western Mediterranean region.

It features slender, arching stems covered in dense clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, airy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a valuable addition to wildlife gardens.
