Calceolaria elliptica

Calceolaria elliptica, commonly known as the elliptic slipperwort, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive slipper-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are elliptical and often have a velvety texture, while the flowers range in color from pale yellow to deep orange.

Calceolaria elliptica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cool, humid conditions.
