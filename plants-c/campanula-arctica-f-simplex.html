Campanula arctica f. simplex

Campanula arctica f. simplex, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It features small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy, slender stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

This variety is notable for its simplicity in form and hardiness in extreme environments.
