Crinum pedicellatum

Crinum pedicellatum, commonly known as the pink crinum, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle purple hue. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette at the base. It produces long, slender stems that bear clusters of flowers, making it a striking addition to gardens.

Crinum pedicellatum is adapted to full sun to partial shade and thrives in well-drained soil.
