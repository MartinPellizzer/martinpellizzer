Castilleja rhexiifolia

Castilleja rhexiifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Indian paintbrush, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and relies on a host plant for some of its nutrients. Its striking red-orange flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, leafless stems and small, scale-like leaves.
