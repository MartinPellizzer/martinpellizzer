Cupania mutabilis var. coriacea

Cupania mutabilis var. coriacea, commonly known as the roughcup plant, is a perennial shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
