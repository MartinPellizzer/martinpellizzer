Chianthemum olgae

Chianthemum olgae, commonly known as the Galician thyme, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of Galicia in Spain.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fragrant, small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
