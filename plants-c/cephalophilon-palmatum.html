Cephalophilon palmatum

Cephalophilon palmatum, commonly known as the golden bamboo palm, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a tall, slender trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers at the top of the plant. The plant has feathery, palm-like leaves that give it a tropical appearance, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique foliage and showy blooms make it a desirable addition to botanical collections.
