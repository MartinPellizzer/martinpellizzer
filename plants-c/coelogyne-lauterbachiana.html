Coelogyne lauterbachiana

Coelogyne lauterbachiana, commonly known as the Lauterbach's orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, branched stems and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in its natural habitat. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink with a yellow lip, and it prefers shaded, humid environments.

Coelogyne lauterbachiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
