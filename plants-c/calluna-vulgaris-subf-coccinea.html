Calluna vulgaris subf. coccinea

Calluna vulgaris subf.

coccinea, commonly known as the red heather, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, depending on the variety. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant floral display.

This subshrub thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands.
