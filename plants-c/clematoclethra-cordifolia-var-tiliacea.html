Clematoclethra cordifolia var. tiliacea

Clematoclethra cordifolia var. tiliacea, commonly known as the Tiliaceous arrowwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the honeysuckle family (Caprifoliaceae) and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its berries are dark blue to black and serve as a food source for various birds and small mammals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
