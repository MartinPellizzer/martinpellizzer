Cedrela rosmarinus

Cedrela rosmarinus, commonly known as the rosemary cedar, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its aromatic leaves that emit a scent reminiscent of rosemary. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a dense canopy of lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, clustered, and typically yellow or greenish-yellow, while the fruit is a capsule containing numerous seeds.

The wood is valued for its durability and fine grain, making it useful in construction and furniture making.
