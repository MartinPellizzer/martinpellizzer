Crataegus okanaganensis var. okanaganensis

Crataegus okanaganensis var. okanaganensis, commonly known as the Okanagan hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Okanagan Valley in British Columbia, Canada.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along streambanks.
