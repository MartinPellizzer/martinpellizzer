Camellia campanisepala

Camellia campanisepala, commonly known as the bell-shaped camellia, is a species native to China and is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers.

It belongs to the Theaceae family and is known for its showy, often pink or red blooms that appear in winter and early spring. The plant is evergreen, with glossy, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Camellia campanisepala is also valued for its cold hardiness and ability to add color to the landscape during the colder months.
