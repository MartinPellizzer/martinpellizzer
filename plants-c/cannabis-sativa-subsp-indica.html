Cannabis sativa subsp. indica

Cannabis sativa subsp.

indica, commonly known as Indian hemp, is a flowering plant native to the Hindu Kush region of South Asia. It is characterized by its compact stature, broad leaves, and high concentration of cannabinoids such as THC and CBD.

The plant typically flowers within 60 to 90 days, depending on environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually dark green with a slightly serrated edge, and it produces small, fluffy seeds.

This subspecies is widely cultivated for both medicinal and recreational purposes due to its psychoactive and therapeutic properties.
