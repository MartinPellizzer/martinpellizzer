Carapa megistocarpa

Carapa megistocarpa, commonly known as Brazilian rubberwood, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its dense, heavy wood used in furniture and construction. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruit is a capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The tree is also valued for its latex, which has been historically used in the production of rubber.
