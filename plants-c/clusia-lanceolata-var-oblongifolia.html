Clusia lanceolata var. oblongifolia

Clusia lanceolata var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf glorybark, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically lanceolate to oblong in shape, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
