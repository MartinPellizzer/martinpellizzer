Calligonum polygonoides subsp. laristanicum

Calligonum polygonoides subsp.

laristanicum, commonly known as the Laristan dwarf tamarisk, is a low-growing shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in harsh environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during the summer months, which are adapted for pollination by wind or insects. It is well-suited to sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry riverbeds and coastal dunes.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
