Cyphorima

Cyphorima, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosettes of velvety, waxy leaves and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in a variety of colors. Cyphorima species are typically herbaceous perennials, thriving in warm, humid environments. They are popular houseplants due to their attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

The flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to their ecological significance.
