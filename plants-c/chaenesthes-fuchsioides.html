Chaenesthes fuchsioides

Chaenesthes fuchsioides, commonly known as the scarlet pimpernel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing herb with slender, hairy stems and small, bright red flowers that open in the morning and close in the evening. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through self-seeding make it a common weed in gardens and disturbed areas.
