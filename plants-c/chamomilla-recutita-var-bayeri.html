Chamomilla recutita var. bayeri

Chamomilla recutita var. bayeri, commonly known as German chamomile, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, which bloom in the summer months. The plant grows up to 30 cm tall, with finely divided, aromatic leaves that contain essential oils. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for herbal gardens.

The flowers are harvested for their medicinal properties, particularly for their calming and anti-inflammatory effects.
