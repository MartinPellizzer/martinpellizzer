Colubrina angustior

Colubrina angustior, commonly known as narrowleaf colubrina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and riverbanks. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and opposite, with a slightly rough texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by water or wind.
