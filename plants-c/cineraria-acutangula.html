Cineraria acutangula

Cineraria acutangula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, while its flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of stems.

Cineraria acutangula is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
