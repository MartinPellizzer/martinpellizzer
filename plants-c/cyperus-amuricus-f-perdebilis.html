Cyperus amuricus f. perdebilis

Cyperus amuricus f. perdebilis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its stems are often reddish in color, and it is known for its ability to thrive in moist to wet conditions.

This subspecies is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in wetland ecosystems.
