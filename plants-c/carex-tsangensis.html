Carex tsangensis

Carex tsangensis, commonly known as the Tsang's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or wetlands. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces capsule fruits that contain seeds.

Carex tsangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.
