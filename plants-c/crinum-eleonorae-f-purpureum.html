Crinum eleonorae f. purpureum

Crinum eleonorae f. purpureum, commonly known as the Purple Crinum, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear large, showy flowers in shades of purple and white. The plant produces strap-shaped, evergreen leaves that are often used in ornamental gardening. Native to South Africa, it thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its dramatic floral displays.
