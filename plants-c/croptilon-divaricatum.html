Croptilon divaricatum

Croptilon divaricatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf tansy.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America, typically found in wetland habitats. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine, hairy hairs. Its leaves are narrow and slightly serrated, with a distinctive aromatic scent.

Croptilon divaricatum produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer.
