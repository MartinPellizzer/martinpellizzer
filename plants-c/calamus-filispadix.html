Calamus filispadix

Calamus filispadix, commonly known as the thread-leaf calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and narrow, linear leaves that are often densely packed. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage structure.
