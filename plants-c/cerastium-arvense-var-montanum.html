Cerastium arvense var. montanum

Cerastium arvense var. montanum, commonly known as mountain mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, hairy, ovate leaves and produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers in early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil.
