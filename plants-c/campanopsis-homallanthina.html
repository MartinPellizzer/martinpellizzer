Campanopsis homallanthina

Campanopsis homallanthina, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and attractive foliage.
