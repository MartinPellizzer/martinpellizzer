Calamagrostis llanganatensis

Calamagrostis llanganatensis, commonly known as the Llanganatensis reed, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fine, silver-green foliage that reflects its adaptation to harsh, cold environments. This plant typically grows in wet meadows and marshy areas, where it forms dense clumps. Its flowering spikes are often adorned with delicate, feathery bracts, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Calamagrostis llanganatensis plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
