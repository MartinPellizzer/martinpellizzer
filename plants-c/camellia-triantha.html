Camellia triantha

Camellia triantha, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a flowering plant native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Theaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color, with a distinctive waxy texture, and bloom in late winter to early spring.

Camellia triantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
