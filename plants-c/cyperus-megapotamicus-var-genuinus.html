Cyperus megapotamicus var. genuinus

Cyperus megapotamicus var. genuinus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. It has rhizomes that help it spread and establish in moist environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
