Carex planostachys

Carex planostachys, commonly known as the flat-stalked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in a dense, cylindrical cluster. Its flowering structure consists of small, inconspicuous spikes that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex planostachys plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
