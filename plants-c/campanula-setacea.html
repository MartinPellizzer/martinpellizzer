Campanula setacea

Campanula setacea, commonly known as the spiked bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Europe and western Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white throat. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Campanula setacea is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
