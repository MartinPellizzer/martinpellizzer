Crepis chirindica

Crepis chirindica, commonly known as the African mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Crepis chirindica is adapted to arid conditions and has a low-growing, compact form.
