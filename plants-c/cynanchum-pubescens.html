Cynanchum pubescens

Cynanchum pubescens, commonly known as the hairy milkweed, is a flowering plant native to various regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in umbels. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Cynanchum pubescens is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic and is a defense mechanism against herbivores.
