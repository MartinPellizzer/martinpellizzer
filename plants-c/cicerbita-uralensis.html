Cicerbita uralensis

Cicerbita uralensis, commonly known as the Ural bittercress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and Eastern Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and edible leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a slightly bitter taste, while its flowers are yellow or white.

Cicerbita uralensis is often found in alpine environments and is adapted to withstand cold and harsh weather conditions.
