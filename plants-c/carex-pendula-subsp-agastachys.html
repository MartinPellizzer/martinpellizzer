Carex pendula subsp. agastachys

Carex pendula subsp.

agastachys, commonly known as the weeping sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It features long, arching inflorescences that give it a graceful, weeping appearance.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
