Clematis loureiroana var. peltata

Clematis loureiroana var. peltata, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to Japan.

It features large, showy flowers that range from white to pale blue, often with a distinctive violet hue at the base. The foliage is palmately divided, with broad, lobed leaves that are typically a deep green. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its dramatic floral displays.
