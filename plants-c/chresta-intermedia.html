Chresta intermedia

Chresta intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate chresta, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically blooms in the summer months, producing white or pale yellow petals. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Chresta intermedia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
