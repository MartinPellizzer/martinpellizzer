Cytisus jankae var. pseudopygmaeus

Cytisus jankae var. pseudopygmaeus, commonly known as the dwarf broom, is a small shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features narrow, trifoliate leaves and produces bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to coastal and rocky habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
