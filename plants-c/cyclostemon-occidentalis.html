Cyclostemon occidentalis

Cyclostemon occidentalis, commonly known as the western cyclostemon, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky, arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and extreme temperatures. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are often pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping to conserve water in its natural habitat.

Cyclostemon occidentalis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
