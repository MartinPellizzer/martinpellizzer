Cinchona peruviana var. micrantha

Cinchona peruviana var. micrantha, commonly known as the Peruvian quinine tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that contain the seeds, and it is well adapted to high-altitude environments. It is a member of the Rubiaceae family, which includes many medicinal and ornamental plants.
