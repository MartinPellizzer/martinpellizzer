Callistemon megalongensis

Callistemon megalongensis, commonly known as the Long Island bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract birds and bees.
