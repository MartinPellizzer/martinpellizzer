Croton myriadenus lusus abortivus

Croton myriadenus lusus abortivus, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features large, palmate leaves with multiple lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
