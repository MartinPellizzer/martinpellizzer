Cyphostemma masukuense subsp. ulugurense

Cyphostemma masukuense subsp.

ulugurense, commonly known as the Uluguru moonvine, is a climbing plant native to the Uluguru Mountains in Tanzania. It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves.

The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by distinctive, horn-like fruits. It is adapted to grow in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forest habitats.
