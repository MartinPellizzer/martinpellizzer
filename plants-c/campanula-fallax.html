Campanula fallax

Campanula fallax, commonly known as the nodding bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue, violet, or white. The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This plant is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators.
