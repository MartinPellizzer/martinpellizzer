Caulinia prorepens

Caulinia prorepens, commonly known as the creeping caulinia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with slender stems that spread along the ground. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Caulinia prorepens is often found in grassy areas and can thrive in well-drained soils.
