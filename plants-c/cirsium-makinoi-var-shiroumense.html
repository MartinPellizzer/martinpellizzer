Cirsium makinoi var. shiroumense

Cirsium makinoi var. shiroumense, commonly known as the white flowering thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and pale purple to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its flowering stems are often branched and bear multiple flower heads.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
