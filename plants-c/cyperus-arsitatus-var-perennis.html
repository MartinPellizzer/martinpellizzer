Cyperus arsitatus var. perennis

Cyperus arsitatus var. perennis, commonly known as the everlasting sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense colonies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its adaptability makes it a common ornamental and ecological plant in various landscapes.
