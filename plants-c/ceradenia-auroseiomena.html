Ceradenia auroseiomena

Ceradenia auroseiomena, commonly known as the golden-hued orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the cloud forests of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green foliage. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers with a unique coloration pattern that sets it apart from other orchid species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is often protected in conservation efforts.
