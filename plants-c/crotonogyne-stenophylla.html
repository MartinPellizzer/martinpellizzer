Crotonogyne stenophylla

Crotonogyne stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or green, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and traditional medicine practices.
