Cocculus petiolaris

Cocculus petiolaris, commonly known as the spurred cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its distinctive spurred stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red berries. Its berries contain a single seed and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant's unique morphology and medicinal properties make it an interesting subject in botanical studies.
