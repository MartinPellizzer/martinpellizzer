Cynoglossum officinale var. angustifolium

Cynoglossum officinale var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaf hound's tongue, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer, typically from July to September. The plant has a distinctive heart-shaped leaf with a rough, hairy surface, and its stem is often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally for its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
