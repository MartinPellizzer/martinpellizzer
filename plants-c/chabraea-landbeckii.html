Chabraea landbeckii

Chabraea landbeckii, commonly known as the yellow sandalwood, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves with a leathery texture. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in inflorescences, while its fruit is a woody capsule containing numerous seeds.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
