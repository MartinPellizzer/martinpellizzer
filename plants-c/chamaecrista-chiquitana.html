Chamaecrista chiquitana

Chamaecrista chiquitana, commonly known as the Chiquitana burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to South America, particularly found in regions of Bolivia and Paraguay.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive, spiny seed pods. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and efficient water use.

Its small, yellow flowers are typically clustered in dense inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value in some horticultural settings.
