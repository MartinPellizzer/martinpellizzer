Cyperus heptaphyllus

Cyperus heptaphyllus, commonly known as the swamp cyperus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Cyperus heptaphyllus produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in panicles.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
