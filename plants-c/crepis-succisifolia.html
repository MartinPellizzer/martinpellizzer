Crepis succisifolia

Crepis succisifolia, commonly known as the succulent thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Crepis succisifolia is known for its drought tolerance and ability to store water in its thick, fleshy leaves.
