Calytrix brachyphylla

Calytrix brachyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a small evergreen shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical red flowers that appear in spring, resembling a bottlebrush. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Calytrix brachyphylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
