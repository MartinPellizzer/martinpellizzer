Crassula chlorifolia

Crassula chlorifolia, commonly known as the Jade Plant or Money Plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green with a bluish tinge, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red during the spring and summer months. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with bright, indirect sunlight.

Crassula chlorifolia is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and attractive appearance.
