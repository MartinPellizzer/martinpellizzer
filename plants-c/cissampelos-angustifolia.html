Cissampelos angustifolia

Cissampelos angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed moon vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed, and its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in racemes. The plant produces fleshy, orange or yellow fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
