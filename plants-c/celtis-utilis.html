Celtis utilis

Celtis utilis, commonly known as the European hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by round, fuzzy fruits. Celtis utilis is typically found in woodland areas and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
