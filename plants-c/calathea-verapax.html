Calathea verapax

Calathea verapax, commonly known as the prayer plant, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that often feature intricate patterns in shades of green, silver, and pink. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its ability to close its leaves at night, a behavior that gives it its common name. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.

Calathea verapax is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
