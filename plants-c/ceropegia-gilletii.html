Ceropegia gilletii

Ceropegia gilletii, commonly known as the silver leaf ceropegia, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, silver-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellow, with a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xerophytic gardens.

Ceropegia gilletii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
