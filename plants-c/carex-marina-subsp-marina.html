Carex marina subsp. marina

Carex marina subsp.

marina, commonly known as salt meadow cordgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal areas. It thrives in brackish or saline wetlands, often found in tidal marshes and salt marshes.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. It plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.

Its adaptability to saltwater environments makes it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
