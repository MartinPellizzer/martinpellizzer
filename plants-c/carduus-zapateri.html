Carduus zapateri

Carduus zapateri, commonly known as the Zapater's thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple to violet flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are typically broad, deeply lobed, and covered in fine hairs.

Carduus zapateri is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
