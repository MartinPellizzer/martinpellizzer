Campanula pilosa var. pontica

Campanula pilosa var. pontica, commonly known as the Pontic bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
