Cyrtandra × ellipticifolia

Cyrtandra × ellipticifolia, commonly known as the hybrid blue ginger, is a member of the Gesneriaceae family.

It is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Cyrtandra caroliniana and Cyrtandra elliptica. This species is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are typically tubular with two lips. The leaves are usually elliptical and glossy, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
