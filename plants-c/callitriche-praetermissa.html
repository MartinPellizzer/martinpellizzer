Callitriche praetermissa

Callitriche praetermissa, commonly known as the lesser water starwort, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water, wet meadows, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has small, floating or submerged leaves that are often oval to lance-shaped, and it produces tiny, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink. It is an annual or short-lived perennial, often found in still or slow-moving water bodies.

The species is known for its delicate appearance and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
