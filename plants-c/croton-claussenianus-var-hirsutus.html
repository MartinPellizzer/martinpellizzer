Croton claussenianus var. hirsutus

Croton claussenianus var. hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often hairy, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates.

The hairy croton is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
