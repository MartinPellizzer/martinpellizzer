Caragana reticulata

Caragana reticulata, commonly known as the Chinese sandthorn, is a shrub native to temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, hard pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Caragana reticulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
