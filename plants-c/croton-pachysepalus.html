Croton pachysepalus

Croton pachysepalus, commonly known as the thick-sepal croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are often brightly colored. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from yellow to red, followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some parts of the world for its unique foliage.
