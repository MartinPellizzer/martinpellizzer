Chaenactis douglasii var. typica

Chaenactis douglasii var. typica, commonly known as the Douglas's skyrocket, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in alpine and arid environments. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh conditions.
