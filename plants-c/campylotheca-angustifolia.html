Campylotheca angustifolia

Campylotheca angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf campylotheca, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glabrous and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
