Copiapoa angustiflora

Copiapoa angustiflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cactus, is a species native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny, ribbed stems and narrow, elongated leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, typically during the rainy season. It is adapted to extreme arid conditions, storing water in its thick stems and having a shallow root system.

Copiapoa angustiflora is a popular ornamental cactus due to its unique appearance and resilience.
