Citrus medica

Citrus medica, commonly known as the citron, is a small evergreen tree native to South Asia.

It produces a large, thick-skinned fruit that is typically yellow or green in color. The fruit is characterized by its high content of essential oils, sugars, and citric acid. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a rough texture and a strong citrus scent.

The plant is widely cultivated for its fruit, which is used in culinary, medicinal, and religious contexts.
