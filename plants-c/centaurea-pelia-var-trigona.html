Centaurea pelia var. trigona

Centaurea pelia var. trigona, commonly known as the three-lobed cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the inflorescence consists of a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
