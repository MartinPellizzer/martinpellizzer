Cytisus lamarckii

Cytisus lamarckii, commonly known as the yellow broom, is a flowering shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, woody stems and bipinnate leaves that are finely divided. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Cytisus lamarckii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display.
