Cymbopogon schmidianus

Cymbopogon schmidianus, commonly known as Schmidian grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with long, narrow leaves. This plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and forest edges. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

While not widely cultivated for commercial purposes, it plays a role in local ecosystems and may have traditional uses in some cultures.
