Carex japonica variegata

Carex japonica variegata, commonly known as Japanese sedge, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, variegated leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
