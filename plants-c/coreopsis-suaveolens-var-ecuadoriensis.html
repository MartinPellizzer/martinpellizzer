Coreopsis suaveolens var. ecuadoriensis

Coreopsis suaveolens var. ecuadoriensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
