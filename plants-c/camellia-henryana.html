Camellia henryana

Camellia henryana, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter. The plant produces reddish-brown, woody fruits that contain seeds. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Camellia henryana is also valued for its cold hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.
