Chrysophyllum pubipetalum

Chrysophyllum pubipetalum, commonly known as the African cherry or yellow bell, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries containing a single seed. It is valued for its ornamental value and has some traditional uses in local medicine.

The fruit is also sometimes used in culinary applications.
