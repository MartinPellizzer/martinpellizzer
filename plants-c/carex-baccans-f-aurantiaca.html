Carex baccans f. aurantiaca

Carex baccans f. aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden spike rush, is a variant of the spike rush plant characterized by its striking golden-yellow inflorescence.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forests or wetlands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

This ornamental plant is valued for its unique coloration and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
