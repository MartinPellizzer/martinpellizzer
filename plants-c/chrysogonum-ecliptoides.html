Chrysogonum ecliptoides

Chrysogonum ecliptoides, commonly known as the yellow coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces clusters of yellow, tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by composite flower heads and a showy appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its botanical structure supports pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
