Cnicus pascuarensis

Cnicus pascuarensis, commonly known as the meadow thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Cnicus pascuarensis is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
