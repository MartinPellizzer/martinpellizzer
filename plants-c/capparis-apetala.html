Capparis apetala

Capparis apetala, commonly known as the pale-flowered caper, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are often clustered in racemes, and it is characterized by its thorny stems and aromatic foliage. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is known for its resilience in harsh environmental conditions.

Capparis apetala is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations and ecological significance in its native habitat.
