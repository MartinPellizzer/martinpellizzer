Crepis × hybrida

Crepis × hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and disturbed soils. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the seeds are dispersed by wind.

Crepis × hybrida is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
