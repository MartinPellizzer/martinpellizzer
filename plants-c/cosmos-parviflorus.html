Cosmos parviflorus

Cosmos parviflorus, commonly known as the smallflower cosmos, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features delicate, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a prominent yellow center, blooming from summer to early fall. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, hairy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.

Cosmos parviflorus is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
