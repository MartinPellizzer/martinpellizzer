Carex flacca var. glauca

Carex flacca var. glauca, commonly known as blue waxy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-brown flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
