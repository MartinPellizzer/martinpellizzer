Capparis grandipetala

Capparis grandipetala, commonly known as the large-petalled caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fleshy, edible fruits. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Capparis grandipetala is adapted to arid and coastal environments, showcasing resilience to salt spray and drought conditions.
