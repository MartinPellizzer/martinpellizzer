Carludovica rhodocephala

Carludovica rhodocephala, commonly known as the red-headed cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and bright red to orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its foliage consists of spiny, fleshy leaves that reduce water loss.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
