Crucifera tenella

Crucifera tenella, commonly known as the slender mustard, is a small annual herb belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, alternate leaves that are usually oval or lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the cruciferous family. Its fruits are short, pod-like silique that contain several seeds.

Crucifera tenella is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
