Chenopodium fremontii var. fremontii

Chenopodium fremontii var. fremontii, commonly known as desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in dense clusters, typically during the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in sandy or gravelly soils.

Its edible leaves are used in traditional Native American cuisine and are valued for their nutritional content.
