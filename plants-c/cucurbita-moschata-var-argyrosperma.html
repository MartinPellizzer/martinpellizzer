Cucurbita moschata var. argyrosperma

Cucurbita moschata var. argyrosperma, commonly known as the Mexican pumpkin or casaba pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, round, yellow to orange-colored fruit with a smooth, slightly ribbed surface. The plant produces both male and female flowers, with the female flowers developing into the edible fruit. The seeds are typically white or pale yellow and are encased in a hard, fibrous shell.

This variety is valued for its use in both culinary and ornamental applications.
