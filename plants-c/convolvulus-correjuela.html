Convolvulus correjuela

Convolvulus correjuela, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its rapid growth and ornamental flowers make it both a popular garden plant and a potential weed.
