Collomia linearis var. congesta

Collomia linearis var. congesta, commonly known as the clustered fairybells, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is a perennial species that blooms in late spring to early summer.
