Cardamine bocconei

Cardamine bocconei, commonly known as the Boccone's watercress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and economically important plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately divided, and often have a peppery aroma similar to other members of the family.

The flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in racemes, attracting various pollinators.
