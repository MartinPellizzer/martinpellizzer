Cyathea × sessilifolia

Cyathea × sessilifolia, commonly known as the hybrid tree fern, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cyathea cooperi and Cyathea lepifera.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in persistent, fibrous roots and a crown of large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are often used in horticulture for their aesthetic value and ability to thrive in controlled indoor settings.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to adapt to various growing conditions.
