Clematis edentata

Clematis edentata, commonly known as the toothless clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, with five to seven petals and a distinctive spurred structure. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Clematis edentata is relatively hardy and can be grown in gardens with proper support for its climbing habit.
