Carex salina f. stricta

Carex salina f. stricta, commonly known as the salt meadow sedge.

This plant is a variant of the salt meadow sedge, characterized by its upright growth habit and narrower leaves compared to the typical form. It thrives in saline and brackish wetlands, often found along coastal areas and estuaries. The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are typically green to reddish in color.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
