Cylindropuntia ciribe

Cylindropuntia ciribe, commonly known as the ciribe cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a spineless, columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. It is well adapted to harsh desert environments, storing water in its thick stems and minimizing water loss through its waxy surface.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is sometimes cultivated in dry gardens and arid landscapes.
