Cusparia magdalenensis

Cusparia magdalenensis, commonly known as the Magdalen thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lance-shaped leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. Its botanical features include a robust root system and a tall, upright stem.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
