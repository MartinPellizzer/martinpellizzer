Camellia puhoatensis

Camellia puhoatensis, commonly known as the Puhoatensis camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red or pink flowers with a distinctive, waxy texture, which are highly ornamental. Camellia puhoatensis is adapted to cool, humid climates and prefers well-drained, acidic soils.

It is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
