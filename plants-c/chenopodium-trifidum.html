Chenopodium trifidum

Chenopodium trifidum, commonly known as wild spinach, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and finely divided leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its seeds are edible and have been used by indigenous peoples for food and medicinal purposes.

Chenopodium trifidum thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in other open areas.
