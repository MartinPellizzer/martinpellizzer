Conchidium jerdonianum

Conchidium jerdonianum, commonly known as the Jerdon's conchidium, is a rare and specialized plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spirally arranged leaves that form a compact, rosette-like structure. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale in color, often blooming during the wet season. Its botanical features are adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
