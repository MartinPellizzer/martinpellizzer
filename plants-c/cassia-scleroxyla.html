Cassia scleroxyla

Cassia scleroxyla, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, woody pods containing flat, elliptical seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia scleroxyla is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture.
