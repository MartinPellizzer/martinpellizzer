Chassalia lanceolata subsp. lanceolata

Chassalia lanceolata subsp.

lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf chassalia, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairless. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, yellowish flowers arranged in a spike.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
