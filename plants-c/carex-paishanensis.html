Carex paishanensis

Carex paishanensis, commonly known as the Paishan sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to China and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are usually inconspicuous. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
