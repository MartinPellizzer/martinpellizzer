Croton lanceolatus

Croton lanceolatus, commonly known as the lanceleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves, which are typically green with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.
