Cereus pentlandii var. salmoneus

Cereus pentlandii var. salmoneus, commonly known as the salmon cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces striking, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom at night. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun exposure.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
