Clathrospermum mannii

Clathrospermum mannii, commonly known as the African blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the morning. Its flowers are characterized by a distinctive five-lobed corolla and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
