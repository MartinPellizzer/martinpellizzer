Camissonia claviformis subsp. aurantiaca

Camissonia claviformis subsp.

aurantiaca, commonly known as the yellow stalked camissonia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is notable for its bright yellow floral display, which attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
