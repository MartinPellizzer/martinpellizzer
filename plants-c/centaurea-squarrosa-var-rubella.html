Centaurea squarrosa var. rubella

Centaurea squarrosa var. rubella, commonly known as the red centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its bright red to orange-yellow flowers, which bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas and open woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
