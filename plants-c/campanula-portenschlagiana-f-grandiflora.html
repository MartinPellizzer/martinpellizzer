Campanula portenschlagiana f. grandiflora

Campanula portenschlagiana f. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora bellflower, is a cultivar of the Campanula genus native to Europe.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 18 inches and features dark green, hairy leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
