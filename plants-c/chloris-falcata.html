Chloris falcata

Chloris falcata, commonly known as the sicklegrass, is a perennial grass native to Australia and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive sickle-shaped leaves and produces small, fluffy seed heads. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and grasslands, thriving in full sun. Its fibrous root system helps prevent soil erosion, making it ecologically significant in certain habitats.

Chloris falcata is also used in some regions for forage and erosion control.
