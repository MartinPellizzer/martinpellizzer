Carum holopetalum

Carum holopetalum, commonly known as the white caraway, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It features a stout, upright stem that can grow up to 1 meter in height. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are pinnately lobed and have a strong, aromatic scent.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
