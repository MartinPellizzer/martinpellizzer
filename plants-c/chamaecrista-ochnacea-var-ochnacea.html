Chamaecrista ochnacea var. ochnacea

Chamaecrista ochnacea var. ochnacea, commonly known as the yellow cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers in clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a well-developed root system that helps in nitrogen fixation. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

The seeds are encased in hairy pods and are dispersed by wind or animals.
