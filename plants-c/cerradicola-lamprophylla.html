Cerradicola lamprophylla

Cerradicola lamprophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It possesses small, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces white, fleshy berries that are a food source for birds and other animals. Its roots form specialized structures called haustoria, which penetrate the host tree's vascular system to extract water and nutrients.

Cerradicola lamprophylla is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions where its host trees thrive.
