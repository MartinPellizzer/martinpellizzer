Cucurbita perennis

Cucurbita perennis, commonly known as the marsh pumpkin, is a perennial vine native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucumbers. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that give way to orange, spherical fruits. Its fruit is edible and has a sweet, nutty flavor, often used in traditional cuisines.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for both its ornamental and culinary uses.
