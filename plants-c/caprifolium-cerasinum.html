Caprifolium cerasinum

Caprifolium cerasinum, commonly known as the common honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually yellow or orange-red, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its berries, which are red and fleshy, mature in autumn and are an important food source for birds.

The plant is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.
