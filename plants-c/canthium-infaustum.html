Canthium infaustum

Canthium infaustum, commonly known as the swamp cowrie or blackwood.

It is a tropical shrub belonging to the Malvaceae family, characterized by its woody stems and large, palmate leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in wet, swampy habitats and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
