Cirsium kirbense

Cirsium kirbense, commonly known as Kirb's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in stiff hairs, contributing to its defensive mechanisms against herbivores.

Cirsium kirbense plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain insects and wildlife.
