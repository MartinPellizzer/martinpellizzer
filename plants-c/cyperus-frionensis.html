Cyperus frionensis

Cyperus frionensis, commonly known as the Mexican flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus frionensis is primarily used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can also serve as a habitat for various aquatic organisms.
