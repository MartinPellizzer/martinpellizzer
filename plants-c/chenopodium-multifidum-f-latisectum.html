Chenopodium multifidum f. latisectum

Chenopodium multifidum f. latisectum, commonly known as the broad-leafed goosefoot, is a variant of the common goosefoot plant.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are often deeply divided. The leaves are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. It produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This plant is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.
