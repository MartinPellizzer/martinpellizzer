Campanula cochleariifolia f. anagalloides

Campanula cochleariifolia f. anagalloides, commonly known as the pink bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in woodland or open areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its vibrant color and unique floral structure.
