Carum crinitum

Carum crinitum, commonly known as the curly fennel or wild fennel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant features feathery, finely divided leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in umbrella-like clusters. Its seeds are small, aromatic, and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Carum crinitum is known for its sharp, anise-like scent and is often found growing in dry, rocky soils.
