Calamagrostis aculeolata

Calamagrostis aculeolata, commonly known as slender false panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis aculeolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
