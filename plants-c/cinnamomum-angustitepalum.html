Cinnamomum angustitepalum

Cinnamomum angustitepalum, commonly known as narrow-leaf cinnamon, is a species of tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for aromatic purposes.

The tree is valued for its essential oils and is often cultivated for its ornamental and economic value.
