Clerodendrum tomentosum

Clerodendrum tomentosum, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces elongated, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves and flowers are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
