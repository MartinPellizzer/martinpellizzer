Convolvulus bicuspidatus

Convolvulus bicuspidatus, commonly known as the two-lobed morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped, pale blue to purple flowers that open in the morning. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Its rapid growth and climbing habit make it an adaptable species in various ecological settings.
