Celmisia intermedia

Celmisia intermedia, commonly known as the silver tufted bellflower, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, silver-hairy foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

Celmisia intermedia is often found in rocky or tussock grassland habitats, where it contributes to the unique biodiversity of New Zealand's mountainous regions.
