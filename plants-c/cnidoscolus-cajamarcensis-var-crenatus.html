Cnidoscolus cajamarcensis var. crenatus

Cnidoscolus cajamarcensis var. crenatus, commonly known as the Andean arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and serrated edges. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, spiny fruits. The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.
