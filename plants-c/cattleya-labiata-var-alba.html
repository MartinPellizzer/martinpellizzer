Cattleya labiata var. alba

Cattleya labiata var. alba, commonly known as the white cattleya, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, white flowers that often have a slight pinkish tinge at the base. The plant typically features broad, dark green leaves and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is prized for its elegant appearance and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
