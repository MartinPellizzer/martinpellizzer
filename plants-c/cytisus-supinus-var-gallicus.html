Cytisus supinus var. gallicus

Cytisus supinus var. gallicus, commonly known as the French broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
