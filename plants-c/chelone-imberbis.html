Chelone imberbis

Chelone imberbis, commonly known as the tawny crownbeard, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, spire-like inflorescences composed of numerous small, pale yellow to white ray flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, woodland habitats and has heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Chelone imberbis is valued in gardens for its ornamental appearance and ecological benefits.
