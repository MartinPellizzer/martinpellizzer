Chloris nashii

Chloris nashii, commonly known as Nash's grass, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodlands.

Chloris nashii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
