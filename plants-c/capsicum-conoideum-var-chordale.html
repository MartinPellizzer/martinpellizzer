Capsicum conoideum var. chordale

Capsicum conoideum var. chordale, commonly known as the threadleaf chili, is a variant of the horn pepper species native to South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and small, elongated fruits that range in color from green to red when mature. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its botanical features include hairy stems and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in regional cuisines.
