Carya leiodermis var. callicoma

Carya leiodermis var. callicoma, commonly known as the smooth-shelled hickory, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the walnut family (Juglandaceae) and is characterized by its smooth, light-gray bark and alternate, pinnately compound leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green catkins and female flowers, followed by edible, sweet nuts enclosed in a thick, leathery husk.

Its wood is valued for its strength and durability, making it useful in furniture and tool-making.
