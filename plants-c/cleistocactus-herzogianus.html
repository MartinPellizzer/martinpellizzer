Cleistocactus herzogianus

Cleistocactus herzogianus, commonly known as the Herzog's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It is a small, globular cactus with a ribbed stem that typically grows to a height of about 10 centimeters. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, a unique adaptation to its desert environment. Its spines are short and yellowish, providing protection from herbivores.

Cleistocactus herzogianus is well adapted to extreme drought conditions, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
