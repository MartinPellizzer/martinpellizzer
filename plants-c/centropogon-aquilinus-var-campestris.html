Centropogon aquilinus var. campestris

Centropogon aquilinus var. campestris, commonly known as the coastal bluemouth, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant features slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually blue or violet in color, arranged in dense spikes.

It is adapted to saline environments and plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes.
