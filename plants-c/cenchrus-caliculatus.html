Cenchrus caliculatus

Cenchrus caliculatus, commonly known as the needle grass, is a perennial grass native to the warmer regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its sharp, needle-like leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a common sight in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration and wildlife habitat.
