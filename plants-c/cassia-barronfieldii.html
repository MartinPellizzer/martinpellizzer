Cassia barronfieldii

Cassia barronfieldii, commonly known as the Barronfield's cassia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Cassia barronfieldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
