Carlina simplex var. ramosa

Carlina simplex var. ramosa, commonly known as the ramosa carline thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and large, purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant features stiff, spiny leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in rocky or disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

The showy composite flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
