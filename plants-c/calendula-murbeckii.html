Calendula murbeckii

Calendula murbeckii, commonly known as the desert marigold, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellow to orange flowers and hairy, gray-green leaves. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to dry conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Calendula murbeckii is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
