Calea membranacea

Calea membranacea, commonly known as the velvetleaf or sacred plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Campanulaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to violet. The plant has hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and it thrives in moist, shady environments. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in humid climates with well-drained soil.

Calea membranacea is also noted for its use in traditional medicine and spiritual practices among indigenous communities.
