Crocus hadriaticus subsp. parnassicus

Crocus hadriaticus subsp.

parnassicus, commonly known as the Parnassian crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the alpine regions of the Balkans and the Carpathian Mountains. It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color, with a distinctive dark central blotch.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its bulbs are often covered in a thin, fibrous coating that helps protect them during winter dormancy.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
