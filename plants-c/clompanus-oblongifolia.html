Clompanus oblongifolia

Clompanus oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf clompanus, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, often blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a temperate climate with adequate sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
