Columnea filifera

Columnea filifera, commonly known as the thread-leaved flame flower, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a contrasting throat. The plant has slender, hanging stems that produce clusters of small, showy blooms. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine, silky hairs.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often grown as a hanging plant or in containers.
