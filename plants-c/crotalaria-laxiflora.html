Crotalaria laxiflora

Crotalaria laxiflora, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features small, yellow flowers clustered in racemes, with distinctive spurred petals that attract pollinators. Its leaves are alternate and trifoliate, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Crotalaria laxiflora is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.
