Cerastium vulgatum var. americanum

Cerastium vulgatum var. americanum, commonly known as American prostrate mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to North America.

It features small, hairy, oval leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern at the base of the plant. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its compact size and dense foliage make it a popular choice for ground cover in gardens.
