Cytisus ratisbonensis var. subspinescens

Cytisus ratisbonensis var. subspinescens, commonly known as the prickly broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny growth and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, oval leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are typically covered in sharp, recurved thorns. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed sites.

This variety is closely related to other cytisus species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
