Catha flexuosa

Catha flexuosa, commonly known as the African coffee plant, is a shrub native to the Horn of Africa.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its branched stem and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. It is known for its psychoactive properties due to the presence of cathinone and cathine.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used traditionally in various cultural practices.
