Ceratostylis baliensis

Ceratostylis baliensis, commonly known as the Bali spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Indonesian island of Bali.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance due to its elongated, narrow leaves and delicate inflorescence. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers, which are usually pale green or white, are small and clustered, and they bloom during the wet season.

Ceratostylis baliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
