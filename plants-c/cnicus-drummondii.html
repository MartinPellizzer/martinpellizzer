Cnicus drummondii

Cnicus drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's sowthistle, is an annual plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and basal leaves that are usually lobed or toothed. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by small, spiny fruits. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is considered a common weed in many regions.
