Cerasus campanulata

Cerasus campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its fragrant flowers and attractive foliage, making it a popular choice for ornamental horticulture.
