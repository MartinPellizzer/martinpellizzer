Calamagrostis tashiroi subsp. sikokiana

Calamagrostis tashiroi subsp.

sikokiana, commonly known as the Japanese silver grass, is a perennial grass native to Japan. It features slender, silver-blue foliage that turns a striking coppery color in the fall.

The plant produces delicate, feathery plumes that add ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its upright growth habit and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for landscaping and native plant gardens.
