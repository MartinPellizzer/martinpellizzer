Cestichis cyperifolia

Cestichis cyperifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical flower clusters. This plant typically grows in moist or wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense stands.

Cestichis cyperifolia plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
