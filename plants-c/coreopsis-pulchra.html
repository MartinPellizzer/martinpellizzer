Coreopsis pulchra

Coreopsis pulchra, commonly known as the showy coreopsis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bright yellow to orange flowers with radiating petals. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its flowering period extends from late spring to early fall, adding vibrant color to gardens throughout the growing season.
