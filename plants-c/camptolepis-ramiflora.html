Camptolepis ramiflora

Camptolepis ramiflora, commonly known as the branching-flowered camptolepis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Camptolepis ramiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
