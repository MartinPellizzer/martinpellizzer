Columella simplicifolia

Columella simplicifolia, commonly known as the simple-leaf columella, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. It is native to regions of South America, particularly in the Andean foothills.

Columella simplicifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
