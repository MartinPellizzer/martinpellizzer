Connaropsis varians

Connaropsis varians, commonly known as the variable connaropsis, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Connaraceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It is typically found in moist, tropical forests and is adapted to thrive in shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
