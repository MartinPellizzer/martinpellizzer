Campanula glomerata f. canescens

Campanula glomerata f. canescens, commonly known as the downy bellflower, is a flowering plant characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers.

This cultivar features hairy, grayish-green foliage that gives it a soft, textured appearance. It produces clusters of blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is typically compact, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
