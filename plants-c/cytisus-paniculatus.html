Cytisus paniculatus

Cytisus paniculatus, commonly known as the sweet woodruff, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in late spring. The plant has slender, woody stems and compound leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cytisus paniculatus is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
