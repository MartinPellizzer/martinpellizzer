Cucurbita maxima var. boliviana

Cucurbita maxima var. boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian squash, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, elongated fruits that can vary in color from green to orange or red depending on the variety. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. Its fruit is often used in local cuisines for its sweet and nutty flavor.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal as well as its culinary and medicinal uses.
