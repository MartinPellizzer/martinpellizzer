Capparis spinosa var. mucronifolia

Capparis spinosa var. mucronifolia, commonly known as the Mediterranean caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, thorny stems and produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant's leaves are pinnately compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets. Its fruit is a capsule that contains small, black seeds.

This variety is closely related to the common caper and is valued for its edible flower buds and ornamental qualities.
