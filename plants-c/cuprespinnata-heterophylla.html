Cuprespinnata heterophylla

Cuprespinnata heterophylla, commonly known as the variable cupressus, is a unique species of coniferous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which varies in shape and size depending on its growth conditions. The plant exhibits a morphological variation known as heterophylly, where leaves differ in form between different parts of the plant. Its cones are small and typically contain two seeds, and it thrives in moist, temperate climates.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is often of interest to botanists and conservationists.
