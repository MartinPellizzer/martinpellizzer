Conchograecum umbrosum

Conchograecum umbrosum, commonly known as the shadow orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the cloud forests of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated flowers that resemble elongated orchid blooms, often with a velvety texture. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions. Its leaves are typically broad and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its colorful flowers.

Due to its habitat specificity and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
