Cyanus angelescui

Cyanus angelescui, commonly known as the angel's flower, is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

It features showy blue or violet petals that surround a central disk of smaller florets, typical of the genus Cyanus. The plant is native to high-altitude regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in rocky and arid environments. Its flowers are adapted to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies with their vibrant colors.

Cyanus angelescui is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
