Chenopodium microspermum

Chenopodium microspermum, commonly known as dwarf goosefoot, is a small annual plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are usually alternate and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The species is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
