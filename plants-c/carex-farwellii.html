Carex farwellii

Carex farwellii, commonly known as Farwell's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, streambanks, and forested areas. Its slender, triangular stems are often reddish or purple at the base, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Carex farwellii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
