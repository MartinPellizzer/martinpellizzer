Cyperus solidus var. elatior

Cyperus solidus var. elatior, commonly known as the broadleaf paperplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in dense, tufted clusters at the base. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along water bodies.

Its rapid growth and ability to form dense stands make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
