Cocos nucifera var. viridis

Cocos nucifera var. viridis, commonly known as the green coconut, is a variant of the coconut palm characterized by its distinctively green, unripe fruit.

This plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. The leaves are large, feather-like, and provide shade, while the trunk is straight and sturdy. The fruit develops in clusters and contains a clear liquid known as coconut water, which is rich in nutrients.

This variety is often cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value in tropical landscapes.
