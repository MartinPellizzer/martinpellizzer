Carex pityophila

Carex pityophila, commonly known as the slender flat-sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-gray hue.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color, and it plays a role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
