Campanopsis etbaica

Campanopsis etbaica, commonly known as the Ethiopian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Campanopsis etbaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
