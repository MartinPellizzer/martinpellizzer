Corydalis claviculata var. picta

Corydalis claviculata var. picta, commonly known as the painted corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to violet, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or woodland habitats and prefers well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Corydalis claviculata var. picta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
