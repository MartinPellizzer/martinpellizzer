Crocus lacteus var. penicillatus

Crocus lacteus var. penicillatus, commonly known as the Penicillate Crown Violet, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces a bulbous underground stem, typical of many members of the Crocus genus. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from the base of the plant.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
