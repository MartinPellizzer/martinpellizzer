Centaurea psephelloides

Centaurea psephelloides, commonly known as the Greek star-thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, sparsely branched stems covered in grayish-white hairs. The plant produces purple to pink flowers arranged in dense, spherical heads, typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a bluish-gray hue.

It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
