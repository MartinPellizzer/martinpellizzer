Capparis cynophallophora f. mollis

Capparis cynophallophora f. mollis, commonly known as the soft-fruited caper, is a cultivar of the caper plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible, green buds known as capers, which are widely used in culinary applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary versatility.
