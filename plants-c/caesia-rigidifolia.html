Caesia rigidifolia

Caesia rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid stalked caesia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, white to pale green flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Caesia rigidifolia is often found in pine flatwoods and wetlands, where it plays a role in the local ecosystem.
