Carex oederi f. intermedia

Carex oederi f. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in a compact spike, and are usually wind-pollinated.
