Cerasus prostrata var. latifolia

Cerasus prostrata var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cherry shrub, is a deciduous plant native to China.

It is characterized by its compact, spreading growth habit and dark green, ovate leaves that are broader than those of the typical Cerasus prostrata. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardens.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
