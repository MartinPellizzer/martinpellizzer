Chamaeraphis spinescens var. depauperata

Chamaeraphis spinescens var. depauperata, commonly known as the spiny glassworts, is a halophytic plant adapted to saline environments.

It features succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, a characteristic common in coastal plants. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in salt marshes and other coastal areas where it helps stabilize soil and filter water.

Its morphological adaptations make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
