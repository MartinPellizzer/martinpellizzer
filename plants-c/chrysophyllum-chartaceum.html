Chrysophyllum chartaceum

Chrysophyllum chartaceum, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that ripen to a bright red or orange color. The fruit's pulp is juicy and sweet, often consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.
