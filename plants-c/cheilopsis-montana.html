Cheilopsis montana

Cheilopsis montana, commonly known as the mountain chalice, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually broad and slightly hairy, adapted to the harsh mountain climate.

Cheilopsis montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
