Crambe reniformis var. hispanica

Crambe reniformis var. hispanica, commonly known as Spanish crambe, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop adds to its botanical significance.
