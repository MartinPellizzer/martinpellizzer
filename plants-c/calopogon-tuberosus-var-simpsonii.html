Calopogon tuberosus var. simpsonii

Calopogon tuberosus var. simpsonii, commonly known as the Simpson's bog orchid, is a rare and unique species native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in acidic, swampy environments, often in association with pine or cypress forests. The plant features a short, rhizomatous stem with a single, showy, pale yellow or orange flower that blooms in late spring. Its leaves are grass-like and arranged in a rosette, and the plant relies on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.
