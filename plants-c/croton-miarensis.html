Croton miarensis

Croton miarensis, commonly known as the Miarensis Croton, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental species. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, colorful leaves that vary in shape and hue depending on the variety. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in cyathia, a unique floral structure typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Croton miarensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant due to its vibrant foliage.
