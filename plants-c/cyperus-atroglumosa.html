Cyperus atroglumosa

Cyperus atroglumosa, commonly known as black-flowered flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that often have a dark purple or black hue. It typically grows in wet or moist soils, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Cyperus atroglumosa is often found in disturbed habitats and can be an indicator species for wetland environments.
