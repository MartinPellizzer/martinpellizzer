Culcasia falcifolia

Culcasia falcifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf arrowleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length. The plant thrives in humid, shady environments and requires well-draining soil. Its leaves are typically deep green with a slightly textured surface.

Culcasia falcifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.
