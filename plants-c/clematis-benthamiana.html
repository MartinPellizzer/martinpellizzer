Clematis benthamiana

Clematis benthamiana, commonly known as the Benthams' clematis, is a climbing vine native to Australia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires support as it climbs.

Clematis benthamiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
