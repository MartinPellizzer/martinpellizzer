Carduus coloradensis

Carduus coloradensis, commonly known as the Colorado thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

The Colorado thistle plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
