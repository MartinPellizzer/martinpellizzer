Cucumis argenteus

Cucumis argenteus, commonly known as the silver cucumber, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its slender, pale green fruits that have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its fruit is typically harvested when immature, as it becomes fibrous and bitter when fully mature.

The silver cucumber is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has limited use in culinary applications.
