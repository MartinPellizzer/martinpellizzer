Carex pubicarpa

Carex pubicarpa, commonly known as the pubescent sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive pubescent (hairy) texture on the lower surface.

The flowering structure consists of spikelets, which produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.
