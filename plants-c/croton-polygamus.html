Croton polygamus

Croton polygamus, commonly known as the red croton, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which can range from deep red to orange and yellow hues. The leaves are typically alternate, simple, and often have a rough texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody, spiny capsules containing seeds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
