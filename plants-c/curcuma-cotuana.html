Curcuma cotuana

Curcuma cotuana, commonly known as the pink ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other gingers and turmeric plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pink or purple flowers. Its leaves are large, broad, and have a slightly serrated edge.

The rhizomes of Curcuma cotuana are often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant in gardens.
