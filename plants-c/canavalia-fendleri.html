Canavalia fendleri

Canavalia fendleri, commonly known as the Mexican bean or wild pigeon pea, is a leguminous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Canavalia fendleri thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and agricultural systems.
