Carduus × stangii subsp. vindobonensis

Carduus × stangii subsp.

vindobonensis, commonly known as the Vienna thistle, is a hybrid plant native to Central Europe. It is a perennial herb characterized by its robust, spiny stems and large, violet-blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a single, showy head.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological adaptability and role in supporting pollinators.
