Cynodon parodii

Cynodon parodii, commonly known as the silvergrass or parodii grass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and silver-gray foliage due to the presence of fine, hair-like structures on its leaves. The plant produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers in panicles, which are typically visible in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens and landscapes.

Cynodon parodii is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to form dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.
