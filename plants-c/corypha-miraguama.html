Corypha miraguama

Corypha miraguama, commonly known as the Miraguama palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves and tall, slender trunk. The plant produces large, spadix-like inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers. It thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.

The Miraguama palm is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
