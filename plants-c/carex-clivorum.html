Carex clivorum

Carex clivorum, commonly known as the cliff sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This species typically grows in moist, rocky or gravelly soils, often found in mountainous or coastal areas. Its slender, grass-like leaves are arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters called spikes.

Carex clivorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
