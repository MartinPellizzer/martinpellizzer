Chassalia christineae

Chassalia christineae, commonly known as the Christine's chassalia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and ray florets. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cool, temperate climate.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
