Capnophyllum africanum var. leiocarpon

Capnophyllum africanum var. leiocarpon, commonly known as the African capillary plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, hairless stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are borne in compact inflorescences, which are typical of the Compositae family. It is native to southern Africa and thrives in dry, sandy soils.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
