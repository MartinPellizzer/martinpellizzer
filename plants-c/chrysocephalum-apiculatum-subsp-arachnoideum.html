Chrysocephalum apiculatum subsp. arachnoideum

Chrysocephalum apiculatum subsp.

arachnoideum, commonly known as the silky everlasting or silky chrysocephalum, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is characterized by its dense, silver-blue foliage and compact, fluffy flower heads that resemble small clouds.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its silvery leaves. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
