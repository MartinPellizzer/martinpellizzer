Chrysanthemum grigorjewi

Chrysanthemum grigorjewi, commonly known as the Siberian chrysanthemum, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white. The plant is adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in rocky habitats at high altitudes.

Due to its ornamental value and resilience, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in cooler regions.
