Crossandra fruticulosa

Crossandra fruticulosa, commonly known as the flame flower, is a shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Crossandra fruticulosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and bees.
