Chingia horridipes

Chingia horridipes, commonly known as the spiny leaf cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, spiny stems and narrow, fleshy leaves that help it store water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its adaptations to desert conditions include a slow growth rate and the ability to survive prolonged droughts.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
