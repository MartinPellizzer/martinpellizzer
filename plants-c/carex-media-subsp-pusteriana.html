Carex media subsp. pusteriana

Carex media subsp.

pusteriana, commonly known as the Alpine sedge, is a perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in alpine and subalpine habitats. Its leaves are usually dark green and narrow, with a prominent midrib.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
