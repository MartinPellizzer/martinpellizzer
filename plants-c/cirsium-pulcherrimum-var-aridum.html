Cirsium pulcherrimum var. aridum

Cirsium pulcherrimum var. aridum, commonly known as the desert thistle, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green foliage and showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with coarse, rigid hairs, and the flowers are arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in disturbed or rocky habitats.
