Carpesium racemosum

Carpesium racemosum, commonly known as the yellow ragwort, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and branched inflorescences. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in prairies and open woodlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often reddish in color.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
