Crepidium rajanum

Crepidium rajanum, commonly known as the Indian orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the Indian subcontinent and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It is a terrestrial orchid that grows in shaded, humid environments, often near forests or along riverbanks.

Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.
