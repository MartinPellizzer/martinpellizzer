Cylindropuntia imbricata var. spinotecta

Cylindropuntia imbricata var. spinotecta, commonly known as the desert hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a spineless or sparsely spined cactus with cylindrical stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are often followed by red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh desert conditions.
