Carex spectabilis var. elegantula

Carex spectabilis var. elegantula, commonly known as the elegant sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, with a dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spikelets. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
