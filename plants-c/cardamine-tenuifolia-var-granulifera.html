Cardamine tenuifolia var. granulifera

Cardamine tenuifolia var. granulifera, commonly known as the granulate watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to moist, shaded environments in temperate regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive, serrated margin and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, grass-like foliage and produces tiny, edible seeds that are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in rich, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
