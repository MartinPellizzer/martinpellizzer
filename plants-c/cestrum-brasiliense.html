Cestrum brasiliense

Cestrum brasiliense, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale pink, blooming at night. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, hard seeds.
