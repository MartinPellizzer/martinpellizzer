Crataegus monstrata

Crataegus monstrata, commonly known as the monstrous hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. Native to certain regions of Asia, it is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to gardens and natural habitats.
