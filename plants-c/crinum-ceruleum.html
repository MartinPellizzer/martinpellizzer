Crinum ceruleum

Crinum ceruleum, commonly known as the blue crinum, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features strap-like, grassy leaves and a robust rhizomatous root system. It thrives in wetlands and moist soils, often growing along the edges of ponds and streams.

Crinum ceruleum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
