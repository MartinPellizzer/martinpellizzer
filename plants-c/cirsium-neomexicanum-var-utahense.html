Cirsium neomexicanum var. utahense

Cirsium neomexicanum var. utahense, commonly known as Utah thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and plains. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Its foliage and flowers are often covered in fine hairs, which provide some protection against herbivores.
