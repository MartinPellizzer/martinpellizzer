Cyperus manilensis

Cyperus manilensis, commonly known as the Manila flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered and have narrow, linear leaves.

Cyperus manilensis produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the sedge family.
