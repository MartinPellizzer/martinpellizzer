Chasmanthium laxum

Chasmanthium laxum, commonly known as eastern switchgrass, is a native North American grass species that thrives in moist to wet environments.

It is characterized by its clumping growth habit and its distinctive, arching, silver-green foliage. The plant produces fluffy, plume-like seed heads that are particularly noticeable in the fall. It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and tolerate standing water.

Chasmanthium laxum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
