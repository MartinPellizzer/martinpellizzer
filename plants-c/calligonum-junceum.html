Calligonum junceum

Calligonum junceum, commonly known as the narrowleaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, scale-like leaves that reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It is adapted to saline and alkaline soils, making it a hardy species in challenging conditions.

Its root system is extensive, helping to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in its native habitat.
