Centaurea samothracica

Centaurea samothracica, commonly known as the Samothrace centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the Aegean region of Greece.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, bristly leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Centaurea samothracica is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies for its anti-inflammatory effects.
