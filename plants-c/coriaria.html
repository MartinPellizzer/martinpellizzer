Coriaria

Coriaria, commonly known as sweet germander, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Coriariaceae.

It consists of about 100 species, primarily found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plants are shrubs or small trees, often with aromatic, sometimes prickly leaves. They produce small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

The fruits are dry, three-lobed berries that contain several seeds.
