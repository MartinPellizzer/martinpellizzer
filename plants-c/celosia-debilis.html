Celosia debilis

Celosia debilis, commonly known as the sickle celosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and vibrant, spiky bracts that resemble sickles. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often overshadowed by the showy bracts. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Celosia debilis is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
