Carex eremopyroides

Carex eremopyroides, commonly known as the desert sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant is adapted to dry conditions, with a fibrous root system that helps it retain moisture. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Carex eremopyroides plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
