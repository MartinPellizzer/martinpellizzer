Calceolaria viscosissima

Calceolaria viscosissima, commonly known as the sticky calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hood-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has velvety, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in its native high-altitude environment. It thrives in cool, moist conditions and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Calceolaria viscosissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
