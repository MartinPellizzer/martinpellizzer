Capsella autumnalis

Capsella autumnalis, commonly known as autumn cress, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cabbages and mustard. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the fall, followed by kidney-shaped pods containing seeds. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, similar to that of watercress.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.
