Carex matsudae

Carex matsudae, commonly known as the Matsuda's sedge, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its foliage is usually dark green, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

Carex matsudae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
