Cicer fedtschenkoi

Cicer fedtschenkoi, commonly known as the Armenian pea or mountain pea, is a leguminous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. The plant typically grows in rocky, mountainous areas and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and slightly hairy.

Cicer fedtschenkoi is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional medicine and as a food source in its native habitat.
