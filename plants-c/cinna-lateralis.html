Cinna lateralis

Cinna lateralis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features alternate, narrow leaves that are often hairy and have a distinct yellowish-green color. Its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in dense, flat-topped inflorescences.

Cinna lateralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
