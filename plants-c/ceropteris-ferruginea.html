Ceropteris ferruginea

Ceropteris ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery fronds with a distinctive reddish-brown coloration on the underside. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often covered in a thick, rusty-colored coating, giving it its common name.

Ceropteris ferruginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticulture.
