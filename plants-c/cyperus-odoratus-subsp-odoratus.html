Cyperus odoratus subsp. odoratus

Cyperus odoratus subsp.

odoratus, commonly known as the sweetgrass or fragrant sedgeland, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along water bodies.

Its aromatic leaves have been used traditionally in various cultural and medicinal practices.
