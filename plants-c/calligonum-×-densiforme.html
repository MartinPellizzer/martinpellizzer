Calligonum × densiforme

Calligonum × densiforme, commonly known as the dense-flowered tamarisk, is a hybrid shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to thrive in saline and alkaline soils. The plant produces small, pink to reddish flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in harsh environments.

Calligonum × densiforme is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and adaptability.
