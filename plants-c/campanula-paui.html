Campanula paui

Campanula paui, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Campanula paui has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, providing a distinctive appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh mountain conditions.
