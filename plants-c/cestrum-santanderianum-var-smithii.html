Cestrum santanderianum var. smithii

Cestrum santanderianum var. smithii, commonly known as the Santa Fe nightshade, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
