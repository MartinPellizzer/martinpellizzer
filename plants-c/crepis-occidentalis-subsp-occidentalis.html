Crepis occidentalis subsp. occidentalis

Crepis occidentalis subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as western mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and parts of Canada. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate, hairy leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.
