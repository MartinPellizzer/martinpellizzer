Clematis dioica subsp. catesbyana

Clematis dioica subsp.

catesbyana, commonly known as the Catesby's Clematis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a climbing vine characterized by its twining stems and showy, white to pale blue flowers.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive flowers and vigorous growth.
