Cassia serpens var. grandiflora

Cassia serpens var. grandiflora, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of several elliptical leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and can be used in traditional medicine.
