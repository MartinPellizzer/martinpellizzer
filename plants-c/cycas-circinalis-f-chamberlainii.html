Cycas circinalis f. chamberlainii

Cycas circinalis f. chamberlainii, commonly known as the Chamberlain's cycad, is a rare and distinctive variant of the cycad genus Cycas.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a tight, fan-like pattern. The plant produces large, cone-like structures, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its rarity and unique morphology, it is highly valued in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
