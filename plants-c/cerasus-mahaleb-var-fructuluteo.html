Cerasus mahaleb var. fructuluteo

Cerasus mahaleb var. fructuluteo, commonly known as the sweet cherry or sour cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The flowers are typically pink or white, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge. The fruit is a small, round cherry that can be either sweet or sour, depending on the variety.

This plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and orchards.
