Coelogyne integerrima

Coelogyne integerrima, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy flowers. The plant produces fragrant, white or pale yellow blossoms with distinctive dark veining. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Coelogyne integerrima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
