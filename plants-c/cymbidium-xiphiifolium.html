Cymbidium xiphiifolium

Cymbidium xiphiifolium, commonly known as the Chinese orchid, is a species native to East Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring upright, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces clustered spikes of fragrant, showy flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often with a distinctive red or purple lip. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Cymbidium xiphiifolium is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and cultural significance in traditional Chinese horticulture.
