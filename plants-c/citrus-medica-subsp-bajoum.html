Citrus medica subsp. bajoum

Citrus medica subsp.

bajoum, commonly known as the Bajau lime, is a citrus species native to Southeast Asia. It is a small evergreen tree that produces small, round, and highly aromatic citrus fruits.

The fruit has a thick, leathery rind and a juicy, acidic pulp, making it popular in local cuisines. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.

It is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and traditional medicine.
