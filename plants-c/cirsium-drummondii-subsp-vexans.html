Cirsium drummondii subsp. vexans

Cirsium drummondii subsp.

vexans, commonly known as Drummond's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple or pink composite flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and sagebrush steppe habitats. It has a rosette of basal leaves and produces a tall flowering stem with numerous flower heads.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various pollinators and wildlife.
