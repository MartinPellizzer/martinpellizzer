Camellia nakaii

Camellia nakaii, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It produces showy, double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has dark green, elliptical leaves that provide a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Camellia nakaii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
