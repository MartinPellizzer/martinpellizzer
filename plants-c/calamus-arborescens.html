Calamus arborescens

Calamus arborescens, commonly known as the tree-like reed, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height, often resembling a tree. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green inflorescences, which are typical of the genus Calamus. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a distinctive waxy coating.

Calamus arborescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
