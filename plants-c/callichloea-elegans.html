Callichloea elegans

Callichloea elegans, commonly known as the silver grass, is a member of the Poaceae family, characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance.

It is native to South America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant features narrow, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences.

Due to its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil, Callichloea elegans is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
