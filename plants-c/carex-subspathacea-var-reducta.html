Carex subspathacea var. reducta

Carex subspathacea var. reducta, commonly known as the reduced sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes during the summer months. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value.
