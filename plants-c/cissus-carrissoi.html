Cissus carrissoi

Cissus carrissoi, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often covered in tiny, white, felt-like hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil with partial to full sunlight.

Cissus carrissoi is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or walls.
