Cyperus princeae

Cyperus princeae, commonly known as the Prince's flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, this plant thrives in wetland environments such as marshes and swamps. Cyperus princeae typically produces small, clustered flowers that are often inconspicuous, and it is often found in moist soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
