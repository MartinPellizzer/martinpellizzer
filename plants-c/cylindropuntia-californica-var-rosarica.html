Cylindropuntia californica var. rosarica

Cylindropuntia californica var. rosarica, commonly known as the Rosary cactus, is a species native to California and Baja California.

It is a perennial cactus with a cylindrical stem that is typically segmented and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it well adapted to arid environments.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in dry climates.
