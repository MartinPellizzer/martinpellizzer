Carex oederi

Carex oederi, commonly known as the Oeder's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Carex oederi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in diverse soil conditions.
