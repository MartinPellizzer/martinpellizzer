Crocus olivieri f. balcanicus

Crocus olivieri f. balcanicus, commonly known as the Balkan crocus, is a subspecies of the olive crocus native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a bulbous perennial plant characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
