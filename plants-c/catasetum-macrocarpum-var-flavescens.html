Catasetum macrocarpum var. flavescens

Catasetum macrocarpum var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered catasetum, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive yellow coloration. The plant produces a unique structure called a galea, which is a specialized appendage on the column. It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

This orchid prefers warm, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.
