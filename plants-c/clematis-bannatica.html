Clematis bannatica

Clematis bannatica, commonly known as the Bannat's clematis, is a climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, with five petals. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Clematis bannatica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
