Campanula cephalaria var. cardiophylla

Campanula cephalaria var. cardiophylla, commonly known as the heart-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late summer. The leaves are distinctive, often heart-shaped, and are covered in a fine layer of white hairs. This variety thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
