Conamomum utriculosum

Conamomum utriculosum, commonly known as the bladderpod cress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other mustard family plants. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pungent odor, typical of the family. Its leaves are alternate and lobed, often covered in fine hairs.

Conamomum utriculosum is often found in moist, rocky habitats and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
