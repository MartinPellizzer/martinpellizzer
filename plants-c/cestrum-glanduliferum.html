Cestrum glanduliferum

Cestrum glanduliferum, commonly known as the glandular cestrum, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in the evening. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous small, dark seeds.

The plant is characterized by its glandular hairs on the stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name.
