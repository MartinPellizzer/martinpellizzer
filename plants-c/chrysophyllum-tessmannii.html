Chrysophyllum tessmannii

Chrysophyllum tessmannii, commonly known as the African cherry or yellow bell, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces fragrant, yellow flowers that are followed by small, round fruits with a sweet, edible pulp. Its fruit is often used in local cuisines and has a mild, sweet flavor.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its timber.
