Crataegus pyracantha lalandei

Crataegus pyracantha lalandei, commonly known as the Laland's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, bright red berries, and clusters of white flowers in spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
