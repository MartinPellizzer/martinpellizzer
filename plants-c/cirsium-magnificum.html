Cirsium magnificum

Cirsium magnificum, commonly known as the magnificent thistle, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its robust structure and ornamental value make it a notable species in horticulture.

However, its prickly nature and invasive tendencies require careful management in cultivated settings.
