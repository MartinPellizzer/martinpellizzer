Camellia euonymifolia

Camellia euonymifolia, commonly known as the flame camellia, is a flowering plant native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from red to orange, with a distinctive yellow center. The plant has elliptical, glossy leaves that are dark green and provide a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of climates.

Camellia euonymifolia prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
