Crataegus advena

Crataegus advena, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red or orange berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. Crataegus advena is often found in woodlands, hedgerows, and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
