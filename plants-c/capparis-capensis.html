Capparis capensis

Capparis capensis, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or yellow bush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and fleshy, yellow-orange fruits that are edible. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fragrant white flowers. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often found in the Western and Eastern Cape provinces.

Capparis capensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
