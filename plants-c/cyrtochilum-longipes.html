Cyrtochilum longipes

Cyrtochilum longipes, commonly known as the long-lobed orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has a compact growth habit with pseudobulbs and is often found in shaded, humid environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the forest floor, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

Cyrtochilum longipes is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique floral structure.
