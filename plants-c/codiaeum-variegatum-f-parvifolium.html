Codiaeum variegatum f. parvifolium

Codiaeum variegatum f. parvifolium, commonly known as the variegated croton, is a popular ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored leaves that display shades of green, yellow, red, and white, often with intricate patterns. The plant is a shrub or small tree, typically growing up to 3 meters in height, and thrives in warm, humid environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

Codiaeum variegatum f. parvifolium requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to flourish.
