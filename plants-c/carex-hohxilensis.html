Carex hohxilensis

Carex hohxilensis, commonly known as the Hohxil sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to high-altitude regions of China, particularly in the Hohxil area of Qinghai Province. The plant features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and linear, with a dense rosette growth habit. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-lobed flowers, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex hohxilensis is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
