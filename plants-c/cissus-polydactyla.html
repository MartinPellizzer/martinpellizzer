Cissus polydactyla

Cissus polydactyla, commonly known as the devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly glossy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is known for its adaptability to various light conditions and its ability to thrive in humid environments.

Cissus polydactyla is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of care.
