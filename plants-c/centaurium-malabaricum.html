Centaurium malabaricum

Centaurium malabaricum, commonly known as the Malabar chickweed, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its small, blue or purple flowers and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrubs. It has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

Centaurium malabaricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
