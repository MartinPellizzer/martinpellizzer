Coccothrinax miraguama var. arenicola

Coccothrinax miraguama var. arenicola, commonly known as the sand palm, is a small, slow-growing palm native to the Caribbean.

It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in dune environments where it helps stabilize sand dunes. The plant has a slender trunk with a crown of feather-like leaves, and it produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible fruits. It is well adapted to salt spray and wind, making it a resilient species in harsh coastal conditions.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
