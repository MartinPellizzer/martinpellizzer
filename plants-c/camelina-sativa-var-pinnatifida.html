Camelina sativa var. pinnatifida

Camelina sativa var. pinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leafed camelina, is a variant of the camelina plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, pinnately divided leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. This plant is often found in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It is valued for its oilseed production and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

The species is also noted for its adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
