Croton bifurcatus var. emeliae

Croton bifurcatus var. emeliae, commonly known as Emelia's croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features vibrant, colorful leaves that range from green to red and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its striking foliage.
