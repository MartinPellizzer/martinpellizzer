Celtis caucasica var. caudata

Celtis caucasica var. caudata, commonly known as the Caucasian hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by small, round berries that ripen to a dark blue or black color. It is often found in dry, rocky soils and can tolerate moderate drought conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
