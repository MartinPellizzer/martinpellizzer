Crotalaria lasiocarpa

Crotalaria lasiocarpa, commonly known as the hairy crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive spiny pods and yellow flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, hairy seeds within its elongated, woody pods. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry, sandy environments.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a weed in some agricultural areas.
