Cissus pruinata

Cissus pruinata, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that are followed by bright red berries. It is a climbing or trailing plant that thrives in warm, humid environments.

Cissus pruinata is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and easy care requirements.
