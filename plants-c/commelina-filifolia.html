Commelina filifolia

Commelina filifolia, commonly known as threadleaf commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It produces small, blue-violet flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences.

Commelina filifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
