Cymbopogon glaucus

Cymbopogon glaucus, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green or pale blue in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Cymbopogon glaucus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
