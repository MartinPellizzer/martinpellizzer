Carex gentiliana

Carex gentiliana, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Carex gentiliana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
