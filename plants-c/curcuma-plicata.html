Curcuma plicata

Curcuma plicata, commonly known as the zigzag ginger, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, pleated, and often zigzag-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, and its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Curcuma plicata thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
