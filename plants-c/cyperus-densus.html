Cyperus densus

Cyperus densus, commonly known as the dense flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically wind-pollinated. Cyperus densus thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for various invertebrates and contributing to soil stabilization in aquatic ecosystems.
