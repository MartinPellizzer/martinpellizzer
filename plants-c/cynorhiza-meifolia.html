Cynorhiza meifolia

Cynorhiza meifolia, commonly known as the mei flower vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and a fibrous root system that aids in its climbing growth. Its botanical name reflects its historical use in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
