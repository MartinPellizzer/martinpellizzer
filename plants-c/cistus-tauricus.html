Cistus tauricus

Cistus tauricus, commonly known as the Turkish rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is drought-tolerant. Its aromatic foliage contains essential oils, which contribute to its use in traditional herbal medicine.

Cistus tauricus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
