Callicarpa rubella var. crenata

Callicarpa rubella var. crenata, commonly known as the red-berried beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by vibrant red berries in the fall. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with slightly serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The plant thrives in partial to full sunlight and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its striking fruit color and is a popular choice in native plant gardens.
