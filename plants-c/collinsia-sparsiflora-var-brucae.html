Collinsia sparsiflora var. brucae

Collinsia sparsiflora var. brucae, commonly known as Bruce's fairybell, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and dunes. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
