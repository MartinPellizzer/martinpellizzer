Calophyllum hexapetalum

Calophyllum hexapetalum, commonly known as the white mangosteen, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces striking white flowers with six petals, which are followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits containing a single, large seed. The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local medicine.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
