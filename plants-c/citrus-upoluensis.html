Citrus upoluensis

Citrus upoluensis, commonly known as the Upolu citrus or Pulu citrus, is a species of citrus native to the island of Upolu in Samoa.

It is a small evergreen tree with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit is generally small, with a thick peel and a juicy, acidic pulp. It is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The plant is adapted to tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.
