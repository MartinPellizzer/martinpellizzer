Cecropia philipsonii

Cecropia philipsonii, commonly known as the giant ceiba, is a fast-growing tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and distinctive, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release numerous small, winged seeds.

Cecropia philipsonii plays an important role in tropical ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
