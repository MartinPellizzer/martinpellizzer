Crocus banaticus var. pictus

Crocus banaticus var. pictus, commonly known as the painted crocus, is a striking bulbous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red, often with intricate patterns resembling paint splashes. The plant typically blooms in early spring, emerging from its underground corms. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that appear after the flowers.

The painted crocus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early bloom and colorful display.
