Chrysocoryne angianthoides

Chrysocoryne angianthoides, commonly known as the golden crown or angel's crown, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, while its bark is smooth and grayish.

The flowers bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it an important species in its native ecosystem.
