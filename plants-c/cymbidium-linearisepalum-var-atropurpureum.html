Cymbidium linearisepalum var. atropurpureum

Cymbidium linearisepalum var. atropurpureum, commonly known as the purple-veined orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow petals and sepals that often display a striking purple or reddish hue. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually borne in clusters and can last for several weeks, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The leaves are elongated and grass-like, contributing to its overall elegant appearance.
