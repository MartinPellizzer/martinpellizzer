Canavalia rosea

Canavalia rosea, commonly known as the pink bean or rose bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, pink to red flowers. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Canavalia rosea is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
