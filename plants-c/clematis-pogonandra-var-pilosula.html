Clematis pogonandra var. pilosula

Clematis pogonandra var. pilosula, commonly known as the silky clematis, is a climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that are covered in a soft, velvety down. Its stems are wiry and capable of clinging to supports, making it a popular choice for trellises and arbors.

It thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
