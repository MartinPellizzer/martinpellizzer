Carex jackiana f. ochrolepis

Carex jackiana f. ochrolepis, commonly known as the golden sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces spikes of pale yellow to golden flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a smooth texture, while the inflorescence consists of clustered spikes.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of aquatic and semi-aquatic environments.
