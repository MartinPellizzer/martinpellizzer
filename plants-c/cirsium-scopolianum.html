Cirsium scopolianum

Cirsium scopolianum, commonly known as the scopolian thistle, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, and the flowers are usually arranged in a compact cluster.

Cirsium scopolianum is often found in the southeastern states, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils.
