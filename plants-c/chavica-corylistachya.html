Chavica corylistachya

Chavica corylistachya, commonly known as the Persian ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It features upright, branched stems covered with stiff, gray-green leaves that are lance-shaped and often hairy. The plant produces showy, spiky flower heads in shades of purple to pink, which bloom in late summer. Its flowers are arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Chavica corylistachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
