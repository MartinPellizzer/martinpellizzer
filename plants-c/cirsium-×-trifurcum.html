Cirsium × trifurcum

Cirsium × trifurcum, commonly known as the thistle bush, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 2 meters. It has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in various environmental conditions.

Due to its invasive nature in some regions, it is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural settings.
