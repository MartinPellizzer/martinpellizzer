Chamaefistula apiculata

Chamaefistula apiculata, commonly known as the narrowleaf boxwood, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage composed of small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardiness.

Chamaefistula apiculata is also valued for its ability to tolerate pruning, making it suitable for topiary and hedging.
