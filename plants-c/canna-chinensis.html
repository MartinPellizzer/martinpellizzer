Canna chinensis

Canna chinensis, commonly known as Chinese canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, paddle-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces showy, spadix-like flowers surrounded by colorful bracts, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Canna chinensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and water features.
