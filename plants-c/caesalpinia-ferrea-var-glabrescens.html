Caesalpinia ferrea var. glabrescens

Caesalpinia ferrea var. glabrescens, commonly known as ironwood or Brazilian ironwood, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its dense, hard wood and compound leaves. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, sandy soils.

Due to its durability and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
