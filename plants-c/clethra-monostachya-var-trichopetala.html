Clethra monostachya var. trichopetala

Clethra monostachya var. trichopetala, commonly known as the hairy-petioled sweet pepper tree, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, which bloom in late summer. Its fruits are small, capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators and providing habitat for wildlife.
