Carex macrothyrsa

Carex macrothyrsa, commonly known as the large-bract sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers, typically in late spring to early summer.

Carex macrothyrsa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
