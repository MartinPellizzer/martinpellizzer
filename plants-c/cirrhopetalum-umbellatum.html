Cirrhopetalum umbellatum

Cirrhopetalum umbellatum, commonly known as the umbrella bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of blue and purple. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it has a compact, low-growing habit.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
