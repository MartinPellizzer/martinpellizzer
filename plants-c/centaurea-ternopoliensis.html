Centaurea ternopoliensis

Centaurea ternopoliensis, commonly known as the Ternopil centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Carpathian Basin.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features bright blue to purple composite flowers arranged in a compact head, with bracts that are often red or reddish. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Centaurea ternopoliensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
