Carex setina

Carex setina, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curved, with a distinct midrib. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often hidden among the foliage, making it less showy than other sedges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex setina plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
