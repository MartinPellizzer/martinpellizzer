Centaurea melanoptilon

Centaurea melanoptilon, commonly known as the black-centred cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It features slender, hairy stems and small, blue to purple flowers arranged in a composite head.

The leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
