Chlorophytum cauliferum

Chlorophytum cauliferum, commonly known as the tree-leafed spider plant, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette of long, narrow leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers on a slender stem, which can reach up to 1 meter in height. It is a succulent that thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Due to its ornamental value, it is popular as a houseplant and in rock gardens.
