Crataegus aurantia

Crataegus aurantia, commonly known as the orange hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright orange-red fruits, which are typically harvested in the fall. The plant has simple, ovate leaves that are usually serrated along the edges. Its flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in clusters and blooming in early spring.

The orange hawthorn is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
