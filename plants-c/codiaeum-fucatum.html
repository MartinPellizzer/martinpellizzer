Codiaeum fucatum

Codiaeum fucatum, commonly known as the Variegated Flameplant, is a striking tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often features shades of red, yellow, and green in intricate patterns. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and can grow quite large, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
