Capsicum richardii

Capsicum richardii, commonly known as the red capsicum or chili pepper, is a species native to South America.

It is characterized by its small to medium-sized fruits, which are typically red when mature. The plant is a herbaceous shrub with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are yellow and bilaterally symmetrical, typical of the Solanaceae family.

The fruit contains numerous seeds enclosed in a fleshy, aromatic pulp.
