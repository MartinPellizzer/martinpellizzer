Carex wallichiana

Carex wallichiana, commonly known as the Himalayan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

Carex wallichiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage.
