Centaurea pergamacea

Centaurea pergamacea, commonly known as the parchment centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils. The plant features upright stems with narrow, gray-green leaves that have a distinctive waxy coating. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, arranged in dense clusters at the top of the stems.

Centaurea pergamacea is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
