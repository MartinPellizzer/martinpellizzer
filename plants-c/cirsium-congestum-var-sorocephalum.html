Cirsium congestum var. sorocephalum

Cirsium congestum var. sorocephalum, commonly known as the clustered thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and meadows. Its leaves are broadly lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture, and the stems are often reddish in color.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
