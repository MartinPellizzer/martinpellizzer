Calamagrostis rubra

Calamagrostis rubra, commonly known as the red wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking reddish-purple foliage that becomes more intense in the fall. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers.

Calamagrostis rubra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
