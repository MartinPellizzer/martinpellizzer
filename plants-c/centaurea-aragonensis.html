Centaurea aragonensis

Centaurea aragonensis, commonly known as Aragon's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in grasslands and shrublands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green hue.

Centaurea aragonensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
