Chaetanthera moenchioides

Chaetanthera moenchioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved moonflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its showy, white, night-blooming flowers. The plant has slender, ascending stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.
