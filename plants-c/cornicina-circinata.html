Cornicina circinata

Cornicina circinata, commonly known as the spiral leaf fern, is a unique plant characterized by its intricate, spiraling fronds.

Native to tropical regions, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's leaves are arranged in a distinctive helical pattern, giving it a visually striking appearance. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, typically producing spores rather than seeds.

Cornicina circinata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
