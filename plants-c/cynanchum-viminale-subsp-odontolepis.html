Cynanchum viminale subsp. odontolepis

Cynanchum viminale subsp.

odontolepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf milkvetch, is a perennial climbing plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and opposite, while the fruit is a follicle containing toxic milky sap.

This subspecies is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
