Calendula chrysantha

Calendula chrysantha, commonly known as the common marigold, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange petals and is part of the Asteraceae family. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces daisy-like flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often branched.

Calendula chrysantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
