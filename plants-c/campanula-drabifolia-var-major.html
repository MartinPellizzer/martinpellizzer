Campanula drabifolia var. major

Campanula drabifolia var. major, commonly known as the Great Campanula, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, supporting clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate, with a hairy texture, and the plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It blooms from late summer to early autumn, producing showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
