Commicarpus ramosissimus

Commicarpus ramosissimus, commonly known as the hairy mallow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in arid and semi-arid environments.
