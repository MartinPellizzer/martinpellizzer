Chabertia umbellata

Chabertia umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in an umbel, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Chabertia umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
