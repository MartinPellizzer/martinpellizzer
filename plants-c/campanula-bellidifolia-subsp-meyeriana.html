Campanula bellidifolia subsp. meyeriana

Campanula bellidifolia subsp.

meyeriana, commonly known as the Meyer's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
