Ceiba ventricosa

Ceiba ventricosa, commonly known as the ceiba tree, is a large tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its massive, buttressed roots and a wide, spreading crown. The tree can grow up to 50 meters tall, with a thick, hollow trunk that provides habitat for various animals. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

The ceiba tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting biodiversity and contributing to forest structure.
