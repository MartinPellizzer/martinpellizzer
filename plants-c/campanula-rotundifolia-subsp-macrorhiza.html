Campanula rotundifolia subsp. macrorhiza

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

macrorhiza, commonly known as the large-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers.

The leaves are rounded and often hairy, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
