Chenopodium lobodontum

Chenopodium lobodontum, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, edible leaves that are often used in cooking. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture.

Chenopodium lobodontum is also valued for its nutritional content, containing high levels of vitamins and minerals.
