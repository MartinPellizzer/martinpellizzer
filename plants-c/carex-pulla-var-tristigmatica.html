Carex pulla var. tristigmatica

Carex pulla var. tristigmatica, commonly known as the dusky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting wildlife.
