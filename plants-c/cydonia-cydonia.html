Cydonia cydonia

Cydonia cydonia, commonly known as the quince, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces fragrant, yellowish flowers in the spring, which are followed by edible, citrus-scented fruits that ripen in the fall. The fruit has a tough, inedible outer skin and a sweet, aromatic pulp inside. The plant is valued for its ornamental value and its use in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.
