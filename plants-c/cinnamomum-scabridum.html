Cinnamomum scabridum

Cinnamomum scabridum, commonly known as rough-corked cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, bark-like texture on its stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by dark blue berries. It is valued for its aromatic wood and has been used in traditional medicine.

The leaves and bark contain essential oils that contribute to its distinctive scent and medicinal properties.
