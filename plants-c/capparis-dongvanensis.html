Capparis dongvanensis

Capparis dongvanensis, commonly known as the Dong Van prickly custard apple, is a flowering plant species native to Vietnam.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Its botanical features include compound leaves and a unique growth habit adapted to its mountainous habitat.

Capparis dongvanensis is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and ecological significance in its region.
