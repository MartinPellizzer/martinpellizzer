Cornus obliqua

Cornus obliqua, commonly known as redosier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, multi-stemmed growth habit and showy clusters of white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces bright red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and turn a striking red or purple in the autumn.

Cornus obliqua is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
