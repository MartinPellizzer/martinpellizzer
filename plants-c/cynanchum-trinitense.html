Cynanchum trinitense

Cynanchum trinitense, commonly known as the Trinidad milkvine, is a climbing vine native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny fruits. It is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and often grows in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
