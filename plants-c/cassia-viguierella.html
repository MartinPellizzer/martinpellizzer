Cassia viguierella

Cassia viguierella, commonly known as the golden cassia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their anti-inflammatory properties.

The bark and seeds contain compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal applications.
