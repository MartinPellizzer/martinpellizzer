Catostemma spruceanum

Catostemma spruceanum, commonly known as the African moonflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, emitting a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough, hairy texture.

Catostemma spruceanum thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or riverine habitats.
