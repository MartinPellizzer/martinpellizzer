Cuphea swartziana

Cuphea swartziana, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange bracts that resemble flames, which are not petals but modified leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant appearance.

Cuphea swartziana is also valued for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
