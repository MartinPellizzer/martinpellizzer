Camellia fragilis

Camellia fragilis, commonly known as the fragile camellia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Theaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Camellia fragilis is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
