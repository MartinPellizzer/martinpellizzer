Cestrum nutans

Cestrum nutans, commonly known as the night-blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that open in the evening and close by dawn. The plant typically grows as a shrub or vine, with opposite, simple leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are usually white or pale yellow, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

Cestrum nutans is valued for its ornamental beauty and nocturnal blooming habit.
