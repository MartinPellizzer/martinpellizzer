Cassebeeria versicolor

Cassebeeria versicolor, commonly known as the variable cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately compound leaves and showy yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a woody structure with smooth, green bark. Its flowers attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, due to their vibrant colors and nectar-rich blossoms.

Cassebeeria versicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a source of natural dye.
