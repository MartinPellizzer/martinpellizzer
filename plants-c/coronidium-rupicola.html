Coronidium rupicola

Coronidium rupicola, commonly known as the rock coronidium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, preferring well-drained environments. The plant features upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small, clustered in terminal spikes, and are usually white or pale pink in color.

Coronidium rupicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a drought-tolerant plant.
