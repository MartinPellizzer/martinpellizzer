Cecropia virgusa

Cecropia virgusa, commonly known as the giant water willow, is a fast-growing tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous leaves and distinctive hollow stems that often contain water. The plant thrives in wetland environments, such as swamps and riverbanks, where it plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms. Its seeds are dispersed by water, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas rapidly.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in its native ecosystems.
