Crepis montana

Crepis montana, commonly known as mountain hoary oat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, silver-green foliage and small, dandelion-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sunlight. Its seed heads are distinctive, consisting of a cluster of bracts that resemble a small, spiky crown.

Crepis montana is often found in meadows, slopes, and grasslands at high altitudes.
