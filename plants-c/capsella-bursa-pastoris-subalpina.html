Capsella bursa-pastoris subalpina

Capsella bursa-pastoris subalpina, commonly known as the alpine chickweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its small, white or pink flowers and hairy, alternate leaves. The plant typically thrives in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its seed pods, which resemble a shepherd's pouch, are a distinctive feature and contain numerous small, round seeds.

This species is often found in mountainous regions across Europe and parts of Asia.
