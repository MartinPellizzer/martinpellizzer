Convolvulus oblongatus

Convolvulus oblongatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, typically blooming in the early morning. It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Convolvulus oblongatus is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
