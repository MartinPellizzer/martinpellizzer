Centaurea amara

Centaurea amara, commonly known as bitterweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, bristly stems and bright red or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, achene-like seeds. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in grassy areas.

Centaurea amara is known for its bitter taste, which has historically been used in herbal medicine.
