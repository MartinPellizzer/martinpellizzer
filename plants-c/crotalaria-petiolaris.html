Crotalaria petiolaris

Crotalaria petiolaris, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features palmate leaves with three to five leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, and the plant is often grown as a forage crop or for ornamental purposes.

Crotalaria petiolaris is also noted for its potential medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
