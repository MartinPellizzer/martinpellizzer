Cucumis melo var. dumeri

Cucumis melo var. dumeri, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a cultivar of the melon species characterized by its large, elongated fruit.

The plant is a herbaceous vine with trailing stems and hairy leaves that are typically broad and lobed. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, a feature common in cucurbit species. The fruit develops from the female flowers and matures to a pale green or yellowish color, often with a textured rind.

It is cultivated for its sweet, juicy flesh and is adapted to warm climates with well-drained soil.
