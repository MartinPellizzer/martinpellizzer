Crotalaria catatii

Crotalaria catatii, commonly known as the catatonic cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features distinctive yellow flowers with two lobes, and its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets. Crotalaria catatii is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

It is also known for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
