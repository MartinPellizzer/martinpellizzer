Clutia polygonoides var. grandifolia

Clutia polygonoides var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered clutia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that are typically borne in axillary or terminal inflorescences. Its fruits are capsules that dehisce to release small, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
