Cocos oleracea

Cocos oleracea, commonly known as the edible cocoyam, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves and a swollen, starchy underground stem called a corm. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its nutrient-rich tubers. Its flowers are typically borne in a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

The plant is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses in various parts of the world.
