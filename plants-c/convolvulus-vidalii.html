Convolvulus vidalii

Convolvulus vidalii, commonly known as the Vidal's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. It thrives in warm climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
