Caltha bisma

Caltha bisma, commonly known as the white marsh marigold, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are palmately lobed and covered in a fine layer of hairs.
