Cerastium latifolium var. nigrescens

Cerastium latifolium var. nigrescens, commonly known as the black-leaved mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green to almost black in color, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their mild diuretic properties.
