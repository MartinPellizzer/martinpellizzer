Casearia grewiifolia var. grewiifolia

Casearia grewiifolia var. grewiifolia, commonly known as the Whitebeard tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features lanceolate, glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers clustered in panicles, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.
