Campanula velata subsp. serpylliformis

Campanula velata subsp.

serpylliformis, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes.

The plant produces bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette or along the stems.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.
