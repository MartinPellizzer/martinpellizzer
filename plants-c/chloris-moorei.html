Chloris moorei

Chloris moorei, commonly known as Moore's grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often pale green or brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Chloris moorei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its fine texture and drought tolerance.
