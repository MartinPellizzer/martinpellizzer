Campanula parvula

Campanula parvula, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches and features small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and basal leaves that are kidney-shaped with toothed edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Campanula parvula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
