Cota dumetorum

Cota dumetorum, commonly known as "chamomile" or "wild chamomile," is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers surrounded by white, petal-like structures. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and thrives in open habitats such as fields and roadsides. Its leaves are hairy and serrated, while its stems are upright and branching.

Cota dumetorum is known for its aromatic properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
