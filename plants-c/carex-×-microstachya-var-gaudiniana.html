Carex × microstachya var. gaudiniana

Carex × microstachya var. gaudiniana, commonly known as the Gaudin's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist, temperate regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized reproductive structures. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wet or damp environments such as marshes, stream banks, and pond edges.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat creation for various wildlife.
