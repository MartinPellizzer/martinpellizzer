Camptosema pedicellatum

Camptosema pedicellatum, commonly known as the slender leafed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various host trees.

It possesses small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, fleshy berries that are dispersed by birds. Its root system includes haustoria, which penetrate the host tree's vascular system to extract water and nutrients.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.
