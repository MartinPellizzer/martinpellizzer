Cyperus congestus f. giganteus

Cyperus congestus f. giganteus, commonly known as the giant flatsedge, is a tall, grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescences that often appear in clusters at the top of the plant. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette-like formation. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a common weed in aquatic habitats.
