Crataegus tenuirama

Crataegus tenuirama, commonly known as the slender thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade in well-drained soils.

Crataegus tenuirama is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
