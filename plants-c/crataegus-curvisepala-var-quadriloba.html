Crataegus curvisepala var. quadriloba

Crataegus curvisepala var. quadriloba, commonly known as the four-lobed hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-lobed leaves, which give it its botanical name. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
