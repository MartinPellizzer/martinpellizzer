Cercanthemum amplexicaule

Cercanthemum amplexicaule, commonly known as the clasping thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive rosette of basal leaves that clasp the stem, giving it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and it has a fibrous root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
