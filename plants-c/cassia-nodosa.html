Cassia nodosa

Cassia nodosa, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces elongated pods that contain flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cassia nodosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
