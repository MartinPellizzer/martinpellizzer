Cardamine raphanifolia var. pilosa

Cardamine raphanifolia var. pilosa, commonly known as hairy watercress, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in soft, hairy glands. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its flowers are small and white, with five sepals and numerous stamens.

The species is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its edible leaves, though it can be toxic in large quantities.
