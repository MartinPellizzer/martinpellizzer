Carex spicigera f. rostrata

Carex spicigera f. rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, with male and female flowers often occurring on separate stems.

This variant is distinguished by its elongated, pointed bracts at the base of the inflorescence, giving it its specific epithet "rostrata.".
