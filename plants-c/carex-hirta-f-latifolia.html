Carex hirta f. latifolia

Carex hirta f. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sedges, is a variant of the common sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically broader than the standard form, giving it a distinctive look. It thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, greenish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated.
