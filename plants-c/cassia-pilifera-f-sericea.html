Cassia pilifera f. sericea

Cassia pilifera f. sericea, commonly known as silver cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, fern-like leaves and clusters of yellow, fragrant flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain flat, dark brown seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its attractive foliage and floral display.
