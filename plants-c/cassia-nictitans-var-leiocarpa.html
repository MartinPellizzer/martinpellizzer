Cassia nictitans var. leiocarpa

Cassia nictitans var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, drooping clusters of yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are compound, consisting of multiple oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its adaptability to different climatic conditions.
