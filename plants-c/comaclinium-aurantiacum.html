Comaclinium aurantiacum

Comaclinium aurantiacum, commonly known as the golden comaclinium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking orange-yellow flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for dry garden environments.

Due to its unique color and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
