Carex dietrichiae

Carex dietrichiae, commonly known as the Dietrich's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and swampy areas. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in early summer.

Carex dietrichiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
