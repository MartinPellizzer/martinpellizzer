Curcuma aeruginosa

Curcuma aeruginosa, commonly known as blue ginger, is a rhizomatous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental blue-green leaves and striking purple or violet flowers. The plant belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a food additive.
