Convolvulus arvensis var. parvifolius

Convolvulus arvensis var. parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaf field bindweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically pale purple or white, with five petals and a distinct spur. This plant is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in agricultural fields.

It is considered a weed in many regions due to its aggressive growth habit and ability to spread through extensive root systems.
