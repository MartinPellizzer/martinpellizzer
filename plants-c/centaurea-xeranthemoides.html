Centaurea xeranthemoides

Centaurea xeranthemoides, commonly known as the desert star, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, grass-like leaves. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping. It produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Centaurea xeranthemoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in arid landscapes.
