Crataegus oxyacanthoidea

Crataegus oxyacanthoidea, commonly known as the red-fruited hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
