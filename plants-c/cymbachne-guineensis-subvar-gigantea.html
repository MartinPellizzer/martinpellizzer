Cymbachne guineensis subvar. gigantea

Cymbachne guineensis subvar. gigantea, commonly known as giant Guinea grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its robust growth, with tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy on the underside. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers.

This grass is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it forms dense stands and plays a role in soil stabilization.
