Cnicus mexicanus

Cnicus mexicanus, commonly known as Mexican cowplant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can be found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and has ornamental value due to its delicate appearance.
