Carex quadrifida var. lenis

Carex quadrifida var. lenis, commonly known as the soft-bristled sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly curled at the tips. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and contain small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is distinguished by its more lax, softer appearance compared to the typical form of Carex quadrifida.
