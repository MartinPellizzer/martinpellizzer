Cestrum candidum

Cestrum candidum, commonly known as white jessamine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer. The plant is typically deciduous, shedding its leaves in the winter, and grows as a shrub or small tree. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a smooth texture with a slightly glossy appearance.

Cestrum candidum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
