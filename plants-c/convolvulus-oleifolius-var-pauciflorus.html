Convolvulus oleifolius var. pauciflorus

Convolvulus oleifolius var. pauciflorus, commonly known as the lesser crownflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, funnel-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, while its stems are hairy and branching.

This species is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to thrive in Mediterranean climates.
