Crataegus oxyacantha f. gireoudii

Crataegus oxyacantha f. gireoudii, commonly known as the Gireoud's hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its distinctive floral and foliar features, including small, white flowers and oval-shaped leaves. This form is often found in specific geographic regions, typically in temperate climates. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential ecological role.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
