Cardamine hirsuta var. stylosa

Cardamine hirsuta var. stylosa, commonly known as the hairy watercress, is a variant of the common watercress species.

It is a herbaceous plant native to moist, temperate regions of Europe and Asia. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves with a peppery aroma, and its stems are often hairy, giving it its common name. It thrives in wet soils near streams, ponds, and marshes.

Botanically, it is characterized by its small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by heart-shaped, edible seeds.
