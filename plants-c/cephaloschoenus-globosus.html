Cephaloschoenus globosus

Cephaloschoenus globosus, commonly known as the globe sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in spring. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, rounded inflorescence, which gives it its common name. It thrives in shallow water or moist soils and is often found in swampy or marshy areas.

Cephaloschoenus globosus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats and supporting local biodiversity.
