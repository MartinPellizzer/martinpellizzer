Camellia indochinensis var. tunghinensis

Camellia indochinensis var. tunghinensis, commonly known as the Tunghin camellia, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam and Cambodia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beautiful blooms and foliage.
