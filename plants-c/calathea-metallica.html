Calathea metallica

Calathea metallica, commonly known as the metallic calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, iridescent leaves that display a metallic sheen, often with intricate patterns in shades of green, silver, and bronze. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, and they have a velvety texture that enhances their visual appeal. This plant thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect light to maintain its vibrant appearance.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and decorative purposes.
