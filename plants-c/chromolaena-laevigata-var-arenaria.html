Chromolaena laevigata var. arenaria

Chromolaena laevigata var. arenaria, commonly known as sand chromolaena, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or coastal habitats.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain environments.
