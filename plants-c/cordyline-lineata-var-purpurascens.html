Cordyline lineata var. purpurascens

Cordyline lineata var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-stemmed cordyline, is a striking ornamental plant native to New Zealand.

It features tall, upright stems that are distinctly purple in color, often with a reddish hue, and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that appear in clusters at the base of the leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical gardens.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a valuable addition to both landscape design and indoor horticulture.
