Cyperus reflexus var. fraternus

Cyperus reflexus var. fraternus, commonly known as the fraternus flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense clumps with slender, triangular stems. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
