Cranichis mandonii

Cranichis mandonii, commonly known as the Mandon's orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, intricate flowers that typically display a combination of white, green, and purple hues. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single, slender stem that bears a few flowers.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Cranichis mandonii is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
