Celastrus sinuatodentatus

Celastrus sinuatodentatus, commonly known as the wavy-toothed staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with wavy, toothed margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
