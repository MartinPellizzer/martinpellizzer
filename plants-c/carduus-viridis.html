Carduus viridis

Carduus viridis, commonly known as the common sowthistle, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, spiny stems and rosettes of broadly ovate, hairy, light green leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in a single head, followed by pale green to brown seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties.
