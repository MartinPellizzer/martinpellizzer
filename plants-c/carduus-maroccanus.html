Carduus maroccanus

Carduus maroccanus, commonly known as Moroccan thistle, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are broad, lobed, and covered in stiff hairs, while its flowers are yellow and arranged in a circular pattern.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
