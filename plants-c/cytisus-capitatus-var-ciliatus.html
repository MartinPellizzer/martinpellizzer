Cytisus capitatus var. ciliatus

Cytisus capitatus var. ciliatus, commonly known as the hairy broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
