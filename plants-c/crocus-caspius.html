Crocus caspius

Crocus caspius, commonly known as the Caspian crocus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its delicate, early-blooming flowers that typically appear in spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, cup-shaped blooms in shades of white, yellow, or pale purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
