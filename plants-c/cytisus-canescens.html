Cytisus canescens

Cytisus canescens, commonly known as the silver broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are small, alternate, and often have a hairy texture.

Cytisus canescens is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for coastal and rocky habitats.
