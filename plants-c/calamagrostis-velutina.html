Calamagrostis velutina

Calamagrostis velutina, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-based flowers.

Calamagrostis velutina is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
