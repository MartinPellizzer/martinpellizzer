Cochlospermum insigne var. mattogrossense

Cochlospermum insigne var. mattogrossense, commonly known as the Brazilian glory flower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy red and orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of this species make it a notable addition to horticultural collections.
