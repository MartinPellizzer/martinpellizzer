Cirsium canescens

Cirsium canescens, commonly known as the hoary thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its grayish-blue foliage covered in fine, white hairs, which give it a distinctive, silvery appearance. The plant produces spiny, purple to pinkish flower heads that bloom in late summer. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Cirsium canescens is part of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower structures and medicinal properties in some species.
