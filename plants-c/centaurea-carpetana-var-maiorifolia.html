Centaurea carpetana var. maiorifolia

Centaurea carpetana var. maiorifolia, commonly known as the greater carpetweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats such as roadsides and fields.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
