Carex trichocarpa var. laeviconica

Carex trichocarpa var. laeviconica, commonly known as the smooth-fruited sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grassy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of sedges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value comes from its dense, upright growth habit and ability to form attractive clumps.
