Catasetum fernandezii

Catasetum fernandezii, commonly known as the Fernandez's catasetum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that can range from yellow to red, with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence is unique, featuring a specialized structure called a "labellum" that aids in pollination by specific wasps.

The genus Catasetum is notable for its specialized pollination mechanisms, making Catasetum fernandezii a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
