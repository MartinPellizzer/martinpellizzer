Cassia kirkii var. microphylla

Cassia kirkii var. microphylla, commonly known as the little-leaf cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some medicinal uses in local communities.
