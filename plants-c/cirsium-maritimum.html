Cirsium maritimum

Cirsium maritimum, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green leaves and striking blue to purple thistle-like flowers. The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dunes and coastal cliffs. Its flowering period typically occurs from June to August, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cirsium maritimum plays an important role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.
