Centrosema virginianum var. angustifolium

Centrosema virginianum var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaf false indigo, is a perennial legume native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with narrow, elliptical leaflets and produces small, clustered white or pale blue flowers. The plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and is often found in moist, woodland habitats. It has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing capabilities in ecological restoration projects.
