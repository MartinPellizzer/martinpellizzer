Conophytum wettsteinii subsp. francoiseae

Conophytum wettsteinii subsp.

francoiseae, commonly known as the "Cape rainflower," is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical or spherical leaves that store water, typical of succulents adapted to arid environments.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal watering, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts and conservationists.
