Carex ternaria

Carex ternaria, commonly known as the three-branched sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in three rows. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks.

Carex ternaria plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
