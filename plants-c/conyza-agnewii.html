Conyza agnewii

Conyza agnewii, commonly known as Agnew's weed, is an annual flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, and it produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Conyza agnewii is often considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
