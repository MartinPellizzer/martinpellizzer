Crucifera erucoides

Crucifera erucoides, commonly known as the wild mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and elongated, slender pods that contain seeds. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in various habitats such as roadsides and fields. It has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces a strong, pungent odor when crushed.

This species is often considered a weed but plays a role in supporting pollinators and soil health.
