Cyrtochilum corniculatum

Cyrtochilum corniculatum, commonly known as the horned orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically has a pseudobulb and linear leaves, and its flowers are characterized by their distinctive, horn-like shape. Cyrtochilum corniculatum thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

It is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
