Crataegus phaneroneura

Crataegus phaneroneura, commonly known as the showy crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its showy, single or clustered flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant produces small, red or yellow fruits that are edible for birds and small mammals. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic with serrated edges, and it can grow up to 6 meters in height.

Crataegus phaneroneura is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
