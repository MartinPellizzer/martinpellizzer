Chrysanthemum indicum var. lushanense

Chrysanthemum indicum var. lushanense, commonly known as the Lushan chrysanthemum, is a variant native to China, specifically the Lushan region.

This plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, white, and orange. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil with partial to full sunlight. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are sturdy and branching.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has historical significance in traditional Chinese horticulture.
