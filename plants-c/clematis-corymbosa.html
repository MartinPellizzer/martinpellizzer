Clematis corymbosa

Clematis corymbosa, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features palmately compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, arranged in corymbs. The plant produces small, round, yellow berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis corymbosa is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
