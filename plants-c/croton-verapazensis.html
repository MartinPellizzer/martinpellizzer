Croton verapazensis

Croton verapazensis, commonly known as the Verapaz croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Central America, particularly found in the tropical regions of Guatemala and Honduras. The plant is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that often display a mix of red, orange, and yellow hues. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Croton verapazensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage.
