Correa × viridiflora-alba

Correa × viridiflora-alba, commonly known as the Silverflower Correa, is a hybrid shrub native to southern Australia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, depending on the variety. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
