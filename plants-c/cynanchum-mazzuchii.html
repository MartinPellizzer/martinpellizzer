Cynanchum mazzuchii

Cynanchum mazzuchii, commonly known as Mazzini's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers, which are followed by distinctive, spiny fruits. It thrives in rocky or arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
