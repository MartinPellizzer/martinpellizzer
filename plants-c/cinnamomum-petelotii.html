Cinnamomum petelotii

Cinnamomum petelotii, commonly known as the Petelot's cinnamon, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

However, it is less commonly cultivated compared to other cinnamon species due to its specific environmental requirements.
