Carex enanderi

Carex enanderi, commonly known as the Enander's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, thriving in swampy or marshy environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

Carex enanderi plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
