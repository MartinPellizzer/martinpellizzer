Cytisus subidaeus

Cytisus subidaeus, commonly known as the Spanish broom, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is a shrub that typically grows in open, sunny areas such as coastal dunes and rocky soils. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets.

Cytisus subidaeus is adapted to dry and saline environments, making it a hardy species with ecological and ornamental value.
