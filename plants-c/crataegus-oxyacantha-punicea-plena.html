Crataegus oxyacantha punicea-plena

Crataegus oxyacantha punicea-plena, commonly known as the red flowering hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its showy, double pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are edible but often used in jams and wines. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and aesthetic purposes.
