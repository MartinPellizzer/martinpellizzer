Cassia silvestris f. olivacea

Cassia silvestris f. olivacea, commonly known as the olive cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and produces bright yellow flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that contains several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
