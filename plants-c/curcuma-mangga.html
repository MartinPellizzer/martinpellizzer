Curcuma mangga

Curcuma mangga, commonly known as golden ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other ginger and turmeric species. The plant produces bright yellow to orange rhizomes that are rich in curcumin, a compound known for its potential health benefits. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, while its flowers are typically orange or red in color.

Curcuma mangga is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
