Convolvulus trigonus

Convolvulus trigonus, commonly known as the three-angled morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-angled stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, blooming in the early morning. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus trigonus is a climbing plant that can spread rapidly, making it both ornamental and potentially invasive in certain regions.
