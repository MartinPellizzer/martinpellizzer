Centaurium suffruticosum subsp. apertum

Centaurium suffruticosum subsp.

apertum, commonly known as the open-centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, bright yellow flowers.

The plant typically thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in dry, well-drained soils.
