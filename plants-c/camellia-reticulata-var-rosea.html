Camellia reticulata var. rosea

Camellia reticulata var. rosea, commonly known as the rose camellia, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, double pink flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is an evergreen shrub with glossy, dark green foliage that provides a striking contrast to its blooms. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Camellia reticulata var. rosea is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
