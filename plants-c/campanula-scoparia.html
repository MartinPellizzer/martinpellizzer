Campanula scoparia

Campanula scoparia, commonly known as the rock bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. The plant produces bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense mat.

Campanula scoparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in rocky environments.
