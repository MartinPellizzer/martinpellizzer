Cleistocalyx myrtoides

Cleistocalyx myrtoides, commonly known as the white myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, white, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a glossy green appearance. This plant is often found in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

Its aromatic properties make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and traditional uses.
