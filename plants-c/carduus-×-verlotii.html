Carduus × verlotii

Carduus × verlotii, commonly known as the Everlasting flower or Centaurea × verlotii, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, fluffy flower heads that remain intact even after the plant has dried, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It features spiny leaves and a compact, bushy growth habit.

Due to its ornamental value and longevity, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in floral arrangements.
