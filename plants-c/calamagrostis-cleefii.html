Calamagrostis cleefii

Calamagrostis cleefii, commonly known as Cleef's reedgrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding a subtle ornamental value to the landscape. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a background plant in native plant gardens.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is less commonly cultivated but appreciated for its ecological and aesthetic qualities.
