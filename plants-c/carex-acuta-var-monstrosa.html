Carex acuta var. monstrosa

Carex acuta var. monstrosa, commonly known as the spiked sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species Carex acuta.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and distinctive, spiky inflorescence that resembles a bottle brush. This plant is native to wetland and marshy environments in North America. It has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish hue.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by three-sided, three-rowed seed heads.
