Ceresia elegans

Ceresia elegans, commonly known as the silverleaf ceresia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pale yellow or greenish-white, arranged in clusters.

Ceresia elegans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
