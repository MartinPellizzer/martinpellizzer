Cyathea patellifera

Cyathea patellifera, commonly known as the broad-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting the growth of the trunk and roots.

The fronds are evergreen and contribute to the plant's ornamental value in gardens and conservation areas.
