Calophyllum lanigerum

Calophyllum lanigerum, commonly known as the silky tallow tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, glossy leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in various cultures.

The tree is also valued for its timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
