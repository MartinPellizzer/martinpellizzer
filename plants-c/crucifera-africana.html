Crucifera africana

Crucifera africana, commonly known as African mustard, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and economically important plants. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnately divided leaves. It thrives in dry, arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some African cuisines.
