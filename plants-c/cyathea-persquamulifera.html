Cyathea persquamulifera

Cyathea persquamulifera, commonly known as the broad-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in persistent, fibrous roots. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Cyathea persquamulifera plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
