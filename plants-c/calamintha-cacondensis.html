Calamintha cacondensis

Calamintha cacondensis, commonly known as Mexican catnip, is a perennial herb native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their fragrant leaves and flowers. The plant features square, hairy stems and opposite leaves with a slightly serrated margin. Its small, tubular flowers are typically blue to purple in color and bloom in clusters.

Calamintha cacondensis is often grown for its medicinal properties and as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and fragrance.
