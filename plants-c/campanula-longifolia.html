Campanula longifolia

Campanula longifolia, commonly known as the long-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its ability to attract pollinators.
