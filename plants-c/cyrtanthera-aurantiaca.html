Cyrtanthera aurantiaca

Cyrtanthera aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant orange-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are tubular and often have a strong fragrance, attracting specific pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
