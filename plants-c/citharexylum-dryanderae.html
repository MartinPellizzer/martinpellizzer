Citharexylum dryanderae

Citharexylum dryanderae, commonly known as the pink woolly bush, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features small, white to pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a woolly texture to its foliage. The plant has aromatic leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry woodland areas.

Citharexylum dryanderae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in gardens and as a hedging plant.
