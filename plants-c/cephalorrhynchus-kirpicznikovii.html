Cephalorrhynchus kirpicznikovii

Cephalorrhynchus kirpicznikovii, commonly known as the Kirpicznikov's corymbose orchid, is a rare and specialized orchid species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique corymbose inflorescence, which consists of tightly clustered flowers. The plant produces small, brightly colored flowers that are typically white or pale pink, with intricate patterns on the petals and sepals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
