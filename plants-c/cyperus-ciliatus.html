Cyperus ciliatus

Cyperus ciliatus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its slender, grass-like leaves are often covered with fine hairs, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense clusters.

Cyperus ciliatus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
