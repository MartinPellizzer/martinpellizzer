Cassinia comorensis

Cassinia comorensis, commonly known as the Comoros globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the Comoros Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats, characterized by its erect stem and dense foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in globular clusters, which are typical of the Asteraceae family. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

Cassinia comorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
