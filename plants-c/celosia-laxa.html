Celosia laxa

Celosia laxa, commonly known as the cockscomb, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its distinctive, plume-like inflorescences that resemble a rooster's comb, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its vibrant, long-lasting blooms in shades of red, orange, and pink. Celosia laxa is a herbaceous annual or perennial, depending on the climate, and thrives in full sun with well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ease of care.
