Commiphora samharensis subsp. terebinthina

Commiphora samharensis subsp.

terebinthina, commonly known as the Somali frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the Horn of Africa. It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and incense.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and exhibits a distinctive branching pattern with dense foliage. Its leaves are opposite, leathery, and have a glossy appearance, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The subspecies is notable for its unique chemical composition, contributing to its value in both cultural and commercial contexts.
