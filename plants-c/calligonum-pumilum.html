Calligonum pumilum

Calligonum pumilum, commonly known as the dwarf saltbush, is a low-growing shrub native to arid and saline environments in central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh conditions. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It is well adapted to saline soils and can thrive in areas with high salt content.

Calligonum pumilum plays an important role in stabilizing soils and preventing erosion in its native habitats.
