Cineraria pulchella

Cineraria pulchella, commonly known as the velvet cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its velvety, silver-green leaves and showy blue to violet flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Cineraria pulchella is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
