Colobanthus apetalus

Colobanthus apetalus, commonly known as the Antarctic pearlwort, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Antarctica and subantarctic regions.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming plant with small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often hidden among its foliage. It is adapted to survive in extreme conditions with minimal sunlight and nutrient-poor soil.

Colobanthus apetalus plays a vital role in the fragile ecosystems of its native habitats.
