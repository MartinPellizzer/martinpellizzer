Centaurea seridis var. subferox

Centaurea seridis var. subferox, commonly known as the desert knapweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its sparsely leafy, upright stems and bright yellow to orange-red composite flowers. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in poor, sandy soils. It is often found in rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its blooming period typically occurs in the summer months, adding vibrant color to its natural environment.
