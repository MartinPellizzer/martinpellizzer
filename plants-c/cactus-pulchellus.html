Cactus pulchellus

Cactus pulchellus, commonly known as the blue agave, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped stem, which is thick and fleshy, designed to store water. The plant produces a tall flowering stalk that bears clusters of small, white flowers. Its leaves are blue-gray in color, with sharp spines along the edges.

Cactus pulchellus is adapted to arid environments and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of agave nectar.
