Calyptromyrcia paniculata var. opaca

Calyptromyrcia paniculata var. opaca, commonly known as the opaca paniculate myrtle, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in panicle-like clusters. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
