Chamaepentas graniticola

Chamaepentas graniticola, commonly known as the granite rock fig, is a unique plant species native to the rocky, arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the summer months. Its fruit is a small, round fig-like structure that contains edible seeds.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
