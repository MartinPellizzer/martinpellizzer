Cyperus ascopinguis

Cyperus ascopinguis, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its slender, grass-like leaves are arranged in three rows, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Cyperus ascopinguis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its ability to thrive in moist conditions and its aesthetic appeal.
