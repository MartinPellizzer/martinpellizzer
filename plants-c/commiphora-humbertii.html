Commiphora humbertii

Commiphora humbertii, commonly known as the Humbert's myrrh tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic resinous exudates, which are used in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often glandular and emit a strong, spicy scent. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, smooth, yellowish fruits.

The species is valued for its unique chemical composition, including various terpenoids and sesquiterpenes.
