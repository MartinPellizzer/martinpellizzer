Ceropegia papillata

Ceropegia papillata, commonly known as the African violet or velvet plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, tuberous roots and produces delicate, pale purple flowers with a unique, waxy texture. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are typically green with a slight sheen. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia papillata is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
