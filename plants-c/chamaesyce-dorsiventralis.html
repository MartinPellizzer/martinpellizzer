Chamaesyce dorsiventralis

Chamaesyce dorsiventralis, commonly known as the waxyleaf spurge, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, erect stems that are often covered in a waxy coating. The leaves are typically opposite, elliptic in shape, and have a distinct upper and lower surface, giving the plant its species name "dorsiventralis.". It produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom in the spring.

This plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
