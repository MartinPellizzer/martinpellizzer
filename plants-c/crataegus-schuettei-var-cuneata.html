Crataegus schuettei var. cuneata

Crataegus schuettei var. cuneata, commonly known as the Schuette hawthorn, is a variant of the hawthorn genus characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral features.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to specific regions of Central Europe, particularly Germany. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with serrated margins and a glossy dark green surface. Its flowers, which appear in spring, are white or pale pink and are arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value.

The fruit is a small, red-pink berry that ripens in the fall, providing food for various wildlife species.
