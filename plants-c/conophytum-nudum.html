Conophytum nudum

Conophytum nudum, commonly known as the "naked conophytum," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Conophytum nudum is prized for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements in succulent gardens.
