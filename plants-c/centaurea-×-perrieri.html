Centaurea × perrieri

Centaurea × perrieri, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves and showy composite flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
