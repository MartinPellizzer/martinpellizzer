Castanea vesca var. affinis

Castanea vesca var. affinis, commonly known as the Persian chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Fagaceae, which includes oaks, beeches, and other chestnut species. The plant is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with serrated margins and produces small, spiny fruits known as nuts. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its astringent properties.

The species is closely related to the common chestnut and shares similar ecological preferences.
