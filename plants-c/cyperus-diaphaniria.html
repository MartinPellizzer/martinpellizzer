Cyperus diaphaniria

Cyperus diaphaniria, commonly known as the glassy flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or aquatic environments, thriving in moist soils and shallow water. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often submerged or floating, adapted for water dispersal.

Cyperus diaphaniria is notable for its translucent, waxy leaves that give it a unique appearance in its natural habitat.
