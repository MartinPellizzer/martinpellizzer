Carex ferruspiculata

Carex ferruspiculata, commonly known as the ironwood sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are often dark green and slightly hairy, with a dense, compact growth habit. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex ferruspiculata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
