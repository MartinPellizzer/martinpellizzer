Coursetia guatemalensis

Coursetia guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan coursetia, is a shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its seeds are encased in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
