Clusia acuminata

Clusia acuminata, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

Clusia acuminata is also known for its ability to thrive in low-light environments and its tolerance to drought.
