Cryptocarya rubra

Cryptocarya rubra, commonly known as the red myrtle, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy red flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for native wildlife. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, often with a distinctive reddish hue on the underside.

Cryptocarya rubra is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
