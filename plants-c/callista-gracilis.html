Callista gracilis

Callista gracilis, commonly known as the slender callista, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its slender stems and small, daisy-like flowers make it a distinctive and attractive species.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its delicate appearance and adaptability.
