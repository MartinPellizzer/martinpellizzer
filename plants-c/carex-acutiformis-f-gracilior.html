Carex acutiformis f. gracilior

Carex acutiformis f. gracilior, commonly known as the slender spike sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in dense clusters along the central axis. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
