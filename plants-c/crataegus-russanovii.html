Crataegus russanovii

Crataegus russanovii, commonly known as the Russian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters and features thorny branches covered with a layer of grayish bark. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in autumn.

Crataegus russanovii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and seasonal interest.
