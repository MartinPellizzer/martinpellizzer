Carex × marshallii

Carex × marshallii, commonly known as the marsh sedge.

It is a hybrid plant in the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers, typically green to brown in color, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex × marshallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
