Cardamine magnifica

Cardamine magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent water shield, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are often submerged or floating, adapted to aquatic conditions. Its small, white flowers bloom in clusters and are pollinated by insects.

Cardamine magnifica plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various aquatic organisms.
