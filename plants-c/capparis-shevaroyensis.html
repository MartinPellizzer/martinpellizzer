Capparis shevaroyensis

Capparis shevaroyensis, commonly known as the Shevaroy hills caper, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and dry habitats, often found in mountainous regions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by fleshy, edible berries. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
