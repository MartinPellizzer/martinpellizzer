Chamaecrista cotinifolia

Chamaecrista cotinifolia, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red bean tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree with compound leaves that are often trifoliate or pinnate. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are arranged in dense, racemose inflorescences. Its fruits are pod-like structures that contain several seeds, and the plant is known for its ornamental value and medicinal uses.

Chamaecrista cotinifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.
