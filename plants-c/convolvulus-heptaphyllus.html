Convolvulus heptaphyllus

Convolvulus heptaphyllus, commonly known as the seven-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It is a member of the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or spread along the ground.
