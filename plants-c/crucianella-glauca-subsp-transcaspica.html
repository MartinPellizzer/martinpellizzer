Crucianella glauca subsp. transcaspica

Crucianella glauca subsp.

transcaspica, commonly known as the Transcaspian blueberry, is a perennial shrub native to Central Asia. It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, glaucous leaves and clusters of white flowers.

The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically blue or black in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

This subspecies is of interest for its potential use in horticulture and as a source of natural antioxidants.
