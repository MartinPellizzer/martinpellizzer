Capparis lucens

Capparis lucens, commonly known as the sour fig or white caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that produces small, yellowish-white flowers with a distinctive, pungent odor. The plant's fruit, known as the sour fig, is edible and has a tart, acidic flavor. Capparis lucens thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Its unique appearance and aromatic qualities make it a notable species in botanical studies and horticulture.
