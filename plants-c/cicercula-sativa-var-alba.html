Cicercula sativa var. alba

Cicercula sativa var. alba, commonly known as the white flax, is a variant of the flax plant native to temperate regions.

It is characterized by its white, silky fibers that are harvested for textile production. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender stems and narrow leaves. Its flowers are small and usually pale blue or white, arranged in loose spikes.

The botanical significance of this variety lies in its fibrous structure and historical use in textile manufacturing.
