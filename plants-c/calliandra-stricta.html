Calliandra stricta

Calliandra stricta, commonly known as the straight acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and compound leaves that are often feather-like in appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which are highly attractive to pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Calliandra stricta is well adapted to dry and arid environments, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.

Its rapid growth and nitrogen-fixing ability make it useful for soil stabilization and as a forage plant.
