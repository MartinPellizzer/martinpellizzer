Chaenomeles maulei f. grandiflora-perfecta

Chaenomeles maulei f. grandiflora-perfecta, commonly known as the Maule cherry.

It is a compact shrub native to South America, characterized by its showy, double pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, red berries in the fall. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its floral display.

The foliage and flowers provide food for pollinators, making it ecologically beneficial.
