Campanula rotundifolia var. breynina

Campanula rotundifolia var. breynina, commonly known as the roundleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, velvety leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
