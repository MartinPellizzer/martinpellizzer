Cissus figariana

Cissus figariana, commonly known as the heartleaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by berries that change color from green to red as they mature. It thrives in humid environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage.

Cissus figariana is also valued for its ability to climb and its air-purifying properties.
