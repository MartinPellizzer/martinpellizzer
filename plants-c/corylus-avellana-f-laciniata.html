Corylus avellana f. laciniata

Corylus avellana f. laciniata, commonly known as the ornamental horse chestnut, is a decorative cultivar of the horse chestnut tree.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a distinctive, fern-like appearance. The plant produces showy, clustered white flowers in spring, followed by spiny, chestnut-like fruits. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and seasonal interest.
