Clematis taiwaniana var. ryukiuensis

Clematis taiwaniana var. ryukiuensis, commonly known as the Ryukyu clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for woodland gardens. Its climbing habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both beauty and ecological interest.

The species is also valued for its role in local biodiversity and traditional horticulture.
