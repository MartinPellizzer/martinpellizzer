Cucumis melo var. salmoneus

Cucumis melo var. salmoneus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a variety of melon characterized by its elongated, slender fruits.

The plant typically grows as a vine with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. The fruit has a thin, smooth skin and a mild, sweet flavor.

It is cultivated for its edible fruit, which is often used in fresh consumption or as a decorative element in gardens.
