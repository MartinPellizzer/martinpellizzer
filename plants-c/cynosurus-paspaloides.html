Cynosurus paspaloides

Cynosurus paspaloides, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, fertile soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to blue flowers.

Cynosurus paspaloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
