Conyza brasiliensis

Conyza brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian ironweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

Conyza brasiliensis is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed soils, making it an invasive species in some regions.
