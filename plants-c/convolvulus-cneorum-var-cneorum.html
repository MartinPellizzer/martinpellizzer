Convolvulus cneorum var. cneorum

Convolvulus cneorum var. cneorum, commonly known as the sweet woodruff, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has a climbing or trailing habit. Its leaves and flowers are used in herbal medicine and as a flavoring agent in food and beverages.

The species is known for its pleasant, sweet scent, which is often described as reminiscent of vanilla.
