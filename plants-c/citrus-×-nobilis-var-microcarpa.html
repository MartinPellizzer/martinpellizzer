Citrus × nobilis var. microcarpa

Citrus × nobilis var. microcarpa, commonly known as kaffir lime, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, rough, and pebbled green fruit peel, which is used extensively in culinary and aromatic applications. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits with a high oil content in the rind, contributing to its distinctive scent. Its leaves are broadly elliptical and have a strong citrus aroma, often used in traditional medicine and cooking.

The tree thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
