Citrus × aurantium var. lusitanica

Citrus × aurantium var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese lemon, is a hybrid citrus plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its small, yellow, and slightly tart fruits that are often used in cooking and traditional medicine. The plant typically grows as a small shrub or tree with glossy, dark green leaves. It prefers warm, sunny climates and well-drained soil, making it suitable for Mediterranean regions.

Its flowers are fragrant and white, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
