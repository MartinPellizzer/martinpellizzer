Carex haematostoma

Carex haematostoma, commonly known as the blood-stem sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest floors. Its flowering spikes produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are often wind-pollinated.

Carex haematostoma is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
