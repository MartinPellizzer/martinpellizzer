Cineraria elongata

Cineraria elongata, commonly known as the longleaf cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in a fine, powdery coating.

Cineraria elongata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
