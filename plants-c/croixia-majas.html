Croixia majas

Croixia majas, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, featuring woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
