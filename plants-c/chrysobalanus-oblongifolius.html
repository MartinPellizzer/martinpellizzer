Chrysobalanus oblongifolius

Chrysobalanus oblongifolius, commonly known as the Brazilian nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody pods containing edible seeds. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall and thrives in humid, well-drained soils. Its fruits are an important food source for various wildlife species.

The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood and is often found in rainforest ecosystems.
