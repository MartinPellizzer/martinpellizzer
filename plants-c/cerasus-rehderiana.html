Cerasus rehderiana

Cerasus rehderiana, commonly known as the Chinese cherry or Rehder's cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Cerasus.

It is native to China and is characterized by its small, sweet, and edible fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The leaves are ovate with a pointed tip and are dark green on the upper surface.
