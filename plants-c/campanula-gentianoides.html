Campanula gentianoides

Campanula gentianoides, commonly known as the gentian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its blue or violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Campanula gentianoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a wildflower in natural habitats.
