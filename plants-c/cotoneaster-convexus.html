Cotoneaster convexus

Cotoneaster convexus, commonly known as the convex cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, orange-red berries that are typically consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive form and seasonal color changes.

Cotoneaster convexus is also valued for its ability to tolerate various environmental conditions, making it adaptable to different garden settings.
