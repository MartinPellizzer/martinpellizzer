Cyphostemma hypoleucum

Cyphostemma hypoleucum, commonly known as the white-stemmed moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, pale green stems and heart-shaped, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellowish-orange fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in rocky or wooded areas.

Cyphostemma hypoleucum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
