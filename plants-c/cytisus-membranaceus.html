Cytisus membranaceus

Cytisus membranaceus, commonly known as the silky acacia, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in a fine, silky down, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

This plant is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in certain ecological contexts.
