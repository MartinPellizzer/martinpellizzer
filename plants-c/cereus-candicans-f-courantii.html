Cereus candicans f. courantii

Cereus candicans f. courantii, commonly known as the Christmas star cactus, is a hybrid succulent plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically produces bright pink or red flowers during the winter months, which is why it is often associated with the holiday season. Its fleshy, ribbed stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

It requires bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to flourish.
