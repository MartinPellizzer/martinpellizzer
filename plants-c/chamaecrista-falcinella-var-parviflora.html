Chamaecrista falcinella var. parviflora

Chamaecrista falcinella var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered crown vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and pairs of opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its seeds are enclosed in slender, curved pods that split open when mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
