Carex johnstonii var. angustifolia

Carex johnstonii var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in wet or moist environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, or along streambanks, where it thrives in saturated soil conditions.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
