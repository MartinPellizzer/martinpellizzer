Connarus pentaphyllus

Connarus pentaphyllus, commonly known as the five-leafed connarus, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by small, woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

Connarus pentaphyllus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
