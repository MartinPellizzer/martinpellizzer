Cytisus filiformis

Cytisus filiformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf broom, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, often twining stems that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, typically with a slight twist, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cytisus filiformis is adapted to dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
