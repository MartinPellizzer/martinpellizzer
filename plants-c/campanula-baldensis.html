Campanula baldensis

Campanula baldensis, commonly known as the Bald Mountain bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Appalachian Mountains.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats at high elevations. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Campanula baldensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native range.
