Campanula gracilis var. capillaris

Campanula gracilis var. capillaris, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, often covered in fine hairs. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
