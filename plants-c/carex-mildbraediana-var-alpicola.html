Carex mildbraediana var. alpicola

Carex mildbraediana var. alpicola, commonly known as the alpine sedge, is a perennial plant native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. This variety is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, typically growing in moist, rocky soils. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in alpine meadows and mountainous areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is a common indicator species of high-altitude ecosystems.
