Cirrhopetalum trisetum

Cirrhopetalum trisetum, commonly known as the three-bristled bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cool, moist environment to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
