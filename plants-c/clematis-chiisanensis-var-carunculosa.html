Clematis chiisanensis var. carunculosa

Clematis chiisanensis var. carunculosa, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to Japan.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a velvety texture and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in early summer. The plant is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to climb using its twining stems. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
