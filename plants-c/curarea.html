Curarea

Curarea, commonly known as arrow poison, is a plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Erythroxylaceae and is known for its toxic properties. The plant contains potent alkaloids, particularly curare, which have been traditionally used by indigenous peoples for hunting. Its leaves and roots are the primary sources of these alkaloids.

Due to its toxic nature, curarea is not suitable for cultivation or use without proper caution and expertise.
