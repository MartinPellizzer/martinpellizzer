Croton glandulosodentatus

Croton glandulosodentatus, commonly known as the glandular-toothed croton, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries such as Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its large, palmate leaves with serrated margins and glandular dots on the underside. Its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking foliage.
