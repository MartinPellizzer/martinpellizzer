Cluytiandra engleri

Cluytiandra engleri, commonly known as the Engler's cluytiandra, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its slender stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and potential medicinal properties.

It is often found in humid, shaded environments within tropical forests.
