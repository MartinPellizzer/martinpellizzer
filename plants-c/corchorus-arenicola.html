Corchorus arenicola

Corchorus arenicola, commonly known as desert dock, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and southern Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer to reduce water loss. Its small, greenish flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the warmer months.

Corchorus arenicola is also valued for its edible leaves, which are used in traditional cuisines across its native range.
