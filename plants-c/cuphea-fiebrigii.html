Cuphea fiebrigii

Cuphea fiebrigii, commonly known as the flame flower, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red bracts that resemble flames. The plant produces small, white, five-petaled flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Cuphea fiebrigii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a container plant.
