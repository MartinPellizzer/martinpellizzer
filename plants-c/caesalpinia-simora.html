Caesalpinia simora

Caesalpinia simora, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright red, feather-like flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has compound leaves composed of several narrow, elongated leaflets. Its seeds are encased in elongated, woody pods that split open when mature.

Caesalpinia simora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
