Cordia elliptica

Cordia elliptica, commonly known as the oval cordia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, the Caribbean, and the Pacific Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features simple, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and typically have five petals, ranging in color from white to pink.

The fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature, often containing a single seed.
