Cotoneaster wilsonii f. typicus

Cotoneaster wilsonii f. typicus, commonly known as Wilson's cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to various soil types.

This species is often used in landscaping due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
