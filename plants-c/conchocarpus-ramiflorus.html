Conchocarpus ramiflorus

Conchocarpus ramiflorus, commonly known as the branched-flower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typically appearing in yellow or greenish-yellow hues.

The fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
