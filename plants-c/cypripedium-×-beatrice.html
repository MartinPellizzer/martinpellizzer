Cypripedium × beatrice

Cypripedium × beatrice, commonly known as the Beatrice lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch, which is a specialized structure for attracting pollinators. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments and has thick, fleshy roots adapted for nutrient absorption. Its flowers are usually pink or white with a contrasting yellow or orange lip.

Cypripedium × beatrice is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
