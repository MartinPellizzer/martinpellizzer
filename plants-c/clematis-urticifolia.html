Clematis urticifolia

Clematis urticifolia, commonly known as stinging clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive, fragrant, white to light blue flower cluster. The plant is known for its stinging hairs on the stems and leaves, which can cause skin irritation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Clematis urticifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
