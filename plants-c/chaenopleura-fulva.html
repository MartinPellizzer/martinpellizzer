Chaenopleura fulva

Chaenopleura fulva, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, and it is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
