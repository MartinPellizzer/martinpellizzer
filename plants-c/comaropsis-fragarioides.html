Comaropsis fragarioides

Comaropsis fragarioides, commonly known as the sweetroot or wild carrot, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its root is edible and has been used by indigenous peoples for food and medicinal purposes.

It plays an important role in supporting pollinators and maintaining ecological balance in its native environment.
