Cichorium callosum

Cichorium callosum, commonly known as the woolly chicory, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its rosette of thick, woolly, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Its distinctive appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
