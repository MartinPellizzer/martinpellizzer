Chrysophtalmum dichotomum

Chrysophtalmum dichotomum, commonly known as the two-branched sunflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with a dark central disk, and its leaves are typically alternate and lance-shaped. The plant grows in a bushy form, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical features make it an attractive and hardy addition to the flora of its native region.
