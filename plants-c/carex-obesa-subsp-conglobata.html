Carex obesa subsp. conglobata

Carex obesa subsp.

conglobata, commonly known as the broad-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green to bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, compact cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish-brown in hue.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
