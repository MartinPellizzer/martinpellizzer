Crataegus loddigesiana

Crataegus loddigesiana, commonly known as the Loddiges hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart, and they serve as a food source for various wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural cultivation.
