Cyperus spinulifer

Cyperus spinulifer, commonly known as the spinylifter sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family.

Cyperus spinulifer is often found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments.
