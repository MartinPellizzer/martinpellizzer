Convolvulus euxinus

Convolvulus euxinus, commonly known as the Crimean bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky areas.

Convolvulus euxinus is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
