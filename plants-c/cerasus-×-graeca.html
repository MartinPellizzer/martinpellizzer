Cerasus × graeca

Cerasus × graeca, commonly known as the cherry plum, is a hybrid deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows to a height of 3–8 meters and features oval to elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in spring, followed by edible drupes that range in color from yellow to deep red. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental and fruit cultivation.

The cherry plum is also valued for its hardiness and ability to adapt to various climatic conditions.
