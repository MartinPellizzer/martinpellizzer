Carex acuta var. panormitana

Carex acuta var. panormitana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets of flowers. This variety is native to specific regions in the Mediterranean, including parts of southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along water edges.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
