Cyperus raphiostachys

Cyperus raphiostachys, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Cyperus raphiostachys produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, which are typically wind-pollinated.
