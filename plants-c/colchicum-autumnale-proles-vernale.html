Colchicum autumnale proles vernale

Colchicum autumnale proles vernale, commonly known as the spring death-cup, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its striking purple to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces a toxic bulb that contains colchicine, a compound used in medicine and research. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows.

Due to its toxic properties, it should be handled with care.
