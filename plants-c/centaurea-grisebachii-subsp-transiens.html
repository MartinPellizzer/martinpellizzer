Centaurea grisebachii subsp. transiens

Centaurea grisebachii subsp.

transiens, commonly known as the Transylvanian bluemink, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces striking blue to purple flower heads composed of numerous small florets, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
