Cinnamomum camphora var. nominale

Cinnamomum camphora var. nominale, commonly known as the Chinese camphor tree, is a deciduous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils used in traditional medicine and perfumery. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, featuring a broad, spreading crown and smooth, grayish-brown bark. Its flowers are small and white, followed by dark purple berries that contain three winged seeds.

The plant is valued for its timber, which is durable and resistant to decay.
