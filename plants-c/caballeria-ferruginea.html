Caballeria ferruginea

Caballeria ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty caballeria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, bract-like structures that surround its flower heads. It thrives in dry, open habitats and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
