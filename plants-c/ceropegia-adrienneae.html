Ceropegia adrienneae

Ceropegia adrienneae, commonly known as the Adrienne's African violet, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, trailing stems and small, fleshy leaves. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are often pink or white, attracting specific pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
