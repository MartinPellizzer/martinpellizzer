Capparis malmeana

Capparis malmeana, commonly known as the Malme's caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are usually succulent and have a distinctive, somewhat bitter taste.

Capparis malmeana is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
