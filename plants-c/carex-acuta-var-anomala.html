Carex acuta var. anomala

Carex acuta var. anomala, commonly known as the sharp-sedge variant, is a sedge species native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swales, and along streambanks. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

This variety is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its adaptability and ecological benefits.
