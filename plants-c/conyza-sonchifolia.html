Conyza sonchifolia

Conyza sonchifolia, commonly known as common ironweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring hairy stems and lanceolate leaves with serrated edges. Its yellow flowers appear in clusters, and it produces numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Conyza sonchifolia is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.
