Cirsium botrys

Cirsium botrys, commonly known as the clustered thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy leaves and its distinctive, spherical flower heads composed of numerous small purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Cirsium botrys is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
