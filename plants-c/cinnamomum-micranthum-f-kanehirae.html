Cinnamomum micranthum f. kanehirae

Cinnamomum micranthum f. kanehirae, commonly known as the Kaneshirae cinnamon, is a rare and lesser-known variant of the cinnamon plant.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Japan, specifically Honshu and Shikoku, where it thrives in humid, cool climates. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers, which contribute to its unique botanical profile. It produces small, dark reddish-brown fruits that contain aromatic seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable but endangered species in its native range.
