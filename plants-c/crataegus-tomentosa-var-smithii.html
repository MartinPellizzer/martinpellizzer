Crataegus tomentosa var. smithii

Crataegus tomentosa var. smithii, commonly known as the Smith's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval with a hairy texture, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
