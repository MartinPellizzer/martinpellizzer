Chamaecrista selleana

Chamaecrista selleana, commonly known as the silky senna, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces elongated, narrow pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are hairy and have a soft, silky texture.

Chamaecrista selleana is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
