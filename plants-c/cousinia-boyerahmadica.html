Cousinia boyerahmadica

Cousinia boyerahmadica, commonly known as the Persian cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It features slender stems and small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Cousinia boyerahmadica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
