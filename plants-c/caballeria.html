Caballeria

Caballeria, commonly known as the "horse chestnut," is a deciduous tree native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and showy, white or pink spathes that enclose clusters of yellow flowers. The tree produces edible seeds enclosed in spiny husks, which are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it can grow up to 25 meters tall.

Caballeria is valued for its ornamental beauty and is commonly planted in parks and gardens.
