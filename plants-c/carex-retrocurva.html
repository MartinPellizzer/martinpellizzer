Carex retrocurva

Carex retrocurva, commonly known as the bent-sheath sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces three-sided, three-rowed fruits.

Carex retrocurva is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
