Clavija serratifolia

Clavija serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated leaf clavija, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in arid or semi-arid climates.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally grown in botanical gardens and traditional herbal practices.
