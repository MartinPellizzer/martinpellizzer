Cleome hullettii

Cleome hullettii, commonly known as the yellow cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woods.

Cleome hullettii is a perennial species that is relatively easy to grow in gardens and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
