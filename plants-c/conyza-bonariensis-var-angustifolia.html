Conyza bonariensis var. angustifolia

Conyza bonariensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf horseweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features slender, branching stems that grow from 30 to 100 cm in height, often with a hairy texture. The leaves are lanceolate to linear, typically narrow and pointed, with a serrated margin. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a composite head, which are wind-pollinated.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
