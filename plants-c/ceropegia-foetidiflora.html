Ceropegia foetidiflora

Ceropegia foetidiflora, commonly known as the stinking cucumber plant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent with fleshy, often swollen stems and leaves that are typically green and somewhat hairy. The plant produces unique, tubular flowers that are often green or yellowish with a strong, unpleasant odor. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to store water in its stems.

The flowers are pollinated by flies, which are attracted by the plant's foul scent.
