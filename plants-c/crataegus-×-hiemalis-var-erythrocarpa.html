Crataegus × hiemalis var. erythrocarpa

Crataegus × hiemalis var. erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited hawthorn, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, fleshy fruits that contrast with its small, ovate leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant autumn foliage and showy flowers.
