Clavinodum globinodum

Clavinodum globinodum, commonly known as the globular nodding orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique globular inflorescence, which resembles a small ball or globe, and its nodding flowers that droop gracefully. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in tropical or subtropical regions. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, providing a lush backdrop to its delicate blossoms.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists alike.
