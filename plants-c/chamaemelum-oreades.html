Chamaemelum oreades

Chamaemelum oreades, commonly known as mountain sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, adapted to thrive in rocky and well-drained soils.

Chamaemelum oreades is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
