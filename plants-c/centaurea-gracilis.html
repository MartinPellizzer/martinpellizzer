Centaurea gracilis

Centaurea gracilis, commonly known as slender centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial with slender, branching stems and small, bright blue to purple flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are typically arranged in loose clusters and bloom from late spring to early summer.

Centaurea gracilis is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
