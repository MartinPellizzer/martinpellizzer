Carex notha

Carex notha, commonly known as narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Native to North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled, three-rowed berries.

Carex notha plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
