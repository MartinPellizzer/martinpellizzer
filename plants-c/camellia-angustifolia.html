Camellia angustifolia

Camellia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf camellia, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is cultivated for its ornamental value and is often grown in gardens and as a houseplant. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, preferring a humid climate.

Camellia angustifolia is also valued for its resilience and ability to withstand mild frost.
