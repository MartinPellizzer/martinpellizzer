Crataegus lassa var. recurva

Crataegus lassa var. recurva, commonly known as the recurved hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are typically oval or elliptic, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It is often used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported cardiovascular benefits.
