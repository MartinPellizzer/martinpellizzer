Citrosma thea

Citrosma thea, commonly known as the Chinese citron tree, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its fragrant leaves and yellowish flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits with a thick, aromatic rind that is often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are used in herbal remedies for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
