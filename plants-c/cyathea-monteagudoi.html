Cyathea monteagudoi

Cyathea monteagudoi, commonly known as the Monteagudo's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the cloud forests of Central America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and thrives in areas with well-drained, acidic soils. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, supporting its upright growth form.

Cyathea monteagudoi plays a significant role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and contributing to forest structure.
