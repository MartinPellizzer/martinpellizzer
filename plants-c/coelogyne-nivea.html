Coelogyne nivea

Coelogyne nivea, commonly known as the white coelogyne, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale yellow flowers with prominent green veins. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to tree trunks or branches in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The orchid blooms during the winter months, producing fragrant flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and moths.
