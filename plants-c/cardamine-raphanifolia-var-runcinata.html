Cardamine raphanifolia var. runcinata

Cardamine raphanifolia var. runcinata, commonly known as the slender water crowfoot, is a herbaceous plant native to moist, often calcareous habitats.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves with elongated, serrated lobes and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. The plant thrives in shallow water or damp soil, often found in stream margins and wet meadows. Its flowers are typically arranged in a loose cluster and are pollinated by insects.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting aquatic and terrestrial invertebrates.
