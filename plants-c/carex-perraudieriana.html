Carex perraudieriana

Carex perraudieriana, commonly known as the Perraudier's sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly found in regions of Argentina and Chile. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Carex perraudieriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
