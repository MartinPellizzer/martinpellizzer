Clausena anisata var. multijuga

Clausena anisata var. multijuga, commonly known as the Indian blackberry, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often trifoliate or pinnate, and its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit is a drupe, typically black when ripe, and contains a single seed. This plant is often found in moist, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
