Cestrum amelanchier

Cestrum amelanchier, commonly known as the American hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible nuts enclosed in a spiky husk, which ripen in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

Cestrum amelanchier is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
