Capsella bursa-pastoris monasterialis

Capsella bursa-pastoris monasterialis, commonly known as the common chickweed, is a low-growing annual herb belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems and small, alternate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers with four petals, which are typically arranged in a raceme. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds, aiding in its widespread distribution.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a common weed in agricultural and urban settings.
