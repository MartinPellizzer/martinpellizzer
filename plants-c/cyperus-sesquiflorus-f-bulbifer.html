Cyperus sesquiflorus f. bulbifer

Cyperus sesquiflorus f. bulbifer, commonly known as the bulbiferous flatsedge, is a variant of the flatsedge genus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like foliage and distinctive, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces bulb-like structures called bulbils, which are used for vegetative propagation. These bulbils are often found at the base of the plant and can sprout into new individuals.

The botanical features of this plant make it adaptable to wetland and marshy environments.
