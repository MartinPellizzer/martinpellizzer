Cullumia ciliata

Cullumia ciliata, commonly known as the fringed cullumia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

The plant is known for its delicate, hairy leaves and showy, yellow to orange disk flowers. It thrives in rocky, arid environments and is often found in scrublands and chaparral habitats.

Cullumia ciliata is adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
