Cistus × florentinus var. apriciformis

Cistus × florentinus var. apriciformis, commonly known as the apricot-shaped rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is a popular choice among gardeners in Mediterranean climates.
