Chaenactis santolinoides var. indurata

Chaenactis santolinoides var. indurata, commonly known as the rough tansy, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features gray-green, woolly foliage and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

Its fibrous root system helps it survive in poor soil conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.
