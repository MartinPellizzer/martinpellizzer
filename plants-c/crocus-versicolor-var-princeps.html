Crocus versicolor var. princeps

Crocus versicolor var. princeps, commonly known as the Prince's crocus, is a bulbous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features striking, showy flowers with petals that range from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves emerge, and thrives in well-drained soil. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant colors.

The variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to naturalize over time.
