Carex trichocarpa var. deweyi

Carex trichocarpa var. deweyi, commonly known as Dewey's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas or along streambanks.

Dewey's sedge is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
