Carthamus brasiliensis

Carthamus brasiliensis, commonly known as Brazilian safflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, spiky seeds that are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and somewhat hairy.

The botanical features of Carthamus brasiliensis make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
