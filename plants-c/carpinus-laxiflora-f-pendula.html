Carpinus laxiflora f. pendula

Carpinus laxiflora f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping hornbeam, is a unique cultivar of the hornbeam tree characterized by its pendulous branches that create a cascading effect.

It is a deciduous tree with small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by winged fruits. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This ornamental tree is often used in landscaping for its distinctive appearance and hardy nature.
