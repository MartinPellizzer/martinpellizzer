Chelonanthus alatus

Chelonanthus alatus, commonly known as the winged buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems that are covered with distinctive, wing-like flaps of tissue. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by dark blue berries in the fall. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Chelonanthus alatus is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in partial to full sun, making it a hardy plant in woodland and open habitats.
