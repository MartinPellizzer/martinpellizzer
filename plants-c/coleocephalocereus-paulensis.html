Coleocephalocereus paulensis

Coleocephalocereus paulensis, commonly known as the Paul's night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and is known for its nocturnal flowering habit. The plant produces large, white, fragrant flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its structure is adapted to arid environments, with spines that provide protection and reduce water loss.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in warm climates.
