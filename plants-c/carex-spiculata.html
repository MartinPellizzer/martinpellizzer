Carex spiculata

Carex spiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex spiculata is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
