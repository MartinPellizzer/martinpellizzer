Commiphora caryifolia

Commiphora caryifolia, commonly known as the Indian myrrh tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to parts of India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing plants. The plant is characterized by its aromatic resinous exudate, which has been used traditionally for medicinal and incense purposes. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, rocky soils.
