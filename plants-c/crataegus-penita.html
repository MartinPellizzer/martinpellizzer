Crataegus penita

Crataegus penita, commonly known as the bitter hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife. Crataegus penita typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks, preferring partial to full sun.

Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
