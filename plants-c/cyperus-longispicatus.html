Cyperus longispicatus

Cyperus longispicatus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Cyperus longispicatus thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, and is often found in shallow water or moist soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
