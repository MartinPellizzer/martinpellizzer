Cheiroglossa malgassica

Cheiroglossa malgassica, commonly known as the Madagascar moon vine, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, moon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
