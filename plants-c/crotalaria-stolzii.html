Crotalaria stolzii

Crotalaria stolzii, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive rattle-like structure at the base of the petals. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, branched stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites.

Crotalaria stolzii is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in certain ecological contexts.
