Carex austromontana

Carex austromontana, commonly known as the southern mountain sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered along the upper stems.

Carex austromontana is valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
