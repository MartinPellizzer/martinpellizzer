Crataegus laciniata var. hispanica

Crataegus laciniata var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its finely serrated leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, juicy berries that are edible but often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food and shelter for various wildlife.
