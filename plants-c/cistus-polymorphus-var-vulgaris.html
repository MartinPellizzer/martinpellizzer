Cistus polymorphus var. vulgaris

Cistus polymorphus var. vulgaris, commonly known as the rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, evergreen leaves and produces showy, pink or white flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

The plant is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
