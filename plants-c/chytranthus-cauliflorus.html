Chytranthus cauliflorus

Chytranthus cauliflorus, commonly known as the purple bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters at the base of the stem, a feature known as cauliflorous growth. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, adapting well to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical studies.
