Cucumis persicodorus

Cucumis persicodorus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, elongated fruits with a pale green skin. The plant is native to the Middle East and has been cultivated for its edible cucumbers. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is typically harvested when immature, as it becomes bitter when fully mature.
