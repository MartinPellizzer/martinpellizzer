Chenopodium album var. cymigerum

Chenopodium album var. cymigerum, commonly known as common lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This species is also known for its nutritional value and has been used in traditional cuisines.
