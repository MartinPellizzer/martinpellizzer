Cirsium × austriacum

Cirsium × austriacum, commonly known as the Austrian thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, purple to pinkish composite flowers. Native to Europe, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats such as meadows and roadsides. The plant has lance-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal, aiding in its propagation.
