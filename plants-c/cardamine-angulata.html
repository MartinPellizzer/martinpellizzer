Cardamine angulata

Cardamine angulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf watercress, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. Its leaves are edible and have a peppery flavor, similar to other members of the mustard family.

Cardamine angulata is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.
