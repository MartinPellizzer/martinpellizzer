Cassia picta

Cassia picta, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in spring or early summer. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance. Its bark is smooth and grayish, while the stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs.

Cassia picta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
