Cestrum strigilatum var. calycinum

Cestrum strigilatum var. calycinum, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its attractive, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often blooming at night. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but should be consumed with caution. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.
