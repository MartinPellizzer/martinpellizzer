Chrysopogon wightianus var. leucanthus

Chrysopogon wightianus var. leucanthus, commonly known as the white-headed grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense spikelets, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
