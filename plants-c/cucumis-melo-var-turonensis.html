Cucumis melo var. turonensis

Cucumis melo var. turonensis, commonly known as the Turk's turban melon, is a cultivated variety of the melon plant characterized by its distinctive, thick, turban-shaped fruit.

The fruit typically has a rough, netted rind that can range in color from pale green to yellow or orange, depending on the variety. It is native to Central Asia and has been cultivated for centuries for its sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant is a vigorous vine that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Its botanical features include lobed leaves and yellow flowers, which are essential for its reproductive cycle.
