Carex atrata var. pullata

Carex atrata var. pullata, commonly known as the black sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features dark green, strap-shaped leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
