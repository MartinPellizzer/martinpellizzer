Capparis trichopetala

Capparis trichopetala, commonly known as the hairy caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and rocky habitats, characterized by its prickly stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by fleshy, red berries. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh environments.
