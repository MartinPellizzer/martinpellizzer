Carex tolmiei var. denticulata

Carex tolmiei var. denticulata, commonly known as the toothed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences that bear small, inconspicuous flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks.

The varietal name "denticulata" refers to the finely toothed margins of its leaves, a distinguishing feature of this plant.
