Campylotropis albopubescens

Campylotropis albopubescens, commonly known as the white-hairy milkvetch, is a perennial legume native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems covered in white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its trifoliate leaves are pinnately compound, with each leaflet having a hairy texture.

It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats.
