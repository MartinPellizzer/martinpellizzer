Carex porteri

Carex porteri, commonly known as the Porters sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It has grass-like leaves that are often dark green and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Carex porteri produces small, clustered flower spikes that are usually brown or reddish in color, contributing to its ornamental value in wetland gardens.
