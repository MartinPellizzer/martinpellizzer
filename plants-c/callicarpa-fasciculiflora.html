Callicarpa fasciculiflora

Callicarpa fasciculiflora, commonly known as the clustered beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, vibrant purple to red berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers in the spring. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

The berries are not only ornamental but also attract birds and other wildlife.
