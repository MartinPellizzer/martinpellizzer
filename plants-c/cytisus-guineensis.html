Cytisus guineensis

Cytisus guineensis, commonly known as African broom, is a flowering shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is deep and drought-resistant, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
