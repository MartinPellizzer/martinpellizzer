Camarea sericea

Camarea sericea, commonly known as the silky camarea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, which includes plants such as philodendrons and peace lilies. The plant is characterized by its striking, waxy, white spathes that enclose a dense cluster of tiny, yellowish flowers. Its leaves are typically broad and smooth, with a silvery sheen that gives it its common name.

Camarea sericea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens and as a houseplant.
