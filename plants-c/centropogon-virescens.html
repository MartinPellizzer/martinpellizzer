Centropogon virescens

Centropogon virescens, commonly known as the green centropogon, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Acanthaceae family, characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, orange, or yellow. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

Centropogon virescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
