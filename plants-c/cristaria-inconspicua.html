Cristaria inconspicua

Cristaria inconspicua, commonly known as the slender marsh orchid, is a small flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It typically grows in moist soil along the edges of ponds, marshes, and swamps. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It is part of the Orchidaceae family and is often found in association with other wetland flora.

Due to its inconspicuous appearance, it is often overlooked despite its ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
