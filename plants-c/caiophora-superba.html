Caiophora superba

Caiophora superba, commonly known as the giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to two meters in diameter, floating on the water's surface. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, blooming at night. Its roots anchor it in the muddy substrate, while its rhizomes spread horizontally to form dense colonies.

Caiophora superba plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various aquatic organisms.
