Catasetum tigrinum

Catasetum tigrinum, commonly known as the tiger orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that often feature contrasting patterns resembling a tiger's stripes. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are known for their intricate structure, with specialized pollination mechanisms that attract specific insects.

Catasetum tigrinum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and unique floral characteristics.
