Cerasus fimbriata

Cerasus fimbriata, commonly known as the fringed cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fringed petals that give it its common name. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
