Castilleja angustifolia var. flavescens

Castilleja angustifolia var. flavescens, commonly known as the narrowleaf Indian paintbrush, is a hemiparasitic plant that belongs to the Orobanchaceae family.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange-red bracts that resemble paintbrushes, which are used for attracting pollinators. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and relies on host plants for some of its nutrients, making it a partial parasite. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and sagebrush steppe.

Its flowering period occurs in the spring and early summer, adding a vibrant splash of color to its native environment.
