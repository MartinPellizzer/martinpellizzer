Conyza setosa

Conyza setosa, commonly known as hispid cottonweed, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small clusters of flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. Its leaves are hairy and grayish-green, while its flowers are small and yellow, often surrounded by white or pale green bracts.

Conyza setosa is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.
