Cistus petiolatus

Cistus petiolatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves and showy, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. It is often found in scrublands and coastal areas, where it contributes to the local flora.

Cistus petiolatus is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
