Calytrix merrelliana

Calytrix merrelliana, commonly known as the mountain bottlebrush, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a leathery texture, and it produces small, clustered fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Calytrix merrelliana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
