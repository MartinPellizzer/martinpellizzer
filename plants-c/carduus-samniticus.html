Carduus samniticus

Carduus samniticus, commonly known as the Southern Tuscan thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are spiny and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, while its purple flowers bloom in late summer.

Carduus samniticus is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
