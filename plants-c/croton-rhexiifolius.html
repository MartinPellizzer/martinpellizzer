Croton rhexiifolius

Croton rhexiifolius, commonly known as the jagged-leaf croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves with deeply lobed margins that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.

Croton rhexiifolius is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
