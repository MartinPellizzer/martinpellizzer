Cirsium casabonae

Cirsium casabonae, commonly known as the Casabonan thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained conditions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a notable species in Mediterranean flora.
