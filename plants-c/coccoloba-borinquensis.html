Coccoloba borinquensis

Coccoloba borinquensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican beautyberry, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red fruits that appear in clusters, which are a key feature of its botanical profile. The plant has compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to salt spray, making it a resilient species in tropical environments.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various bird species.
