Comesperma calymega var. strictum

Comesperma calymega var. strictum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved comesperma, is a shrub native to the eastern coast of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, which appear in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its compact growth habit.
