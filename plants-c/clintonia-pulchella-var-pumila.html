Clintonia pulchella var. pumila

Clintonia pulchella var. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf white bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features a low-growing habit with clustered, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has strap-like, dark green leaves that form a dense rosette near the ground. It thrives in moist, shaded woodlands and is often found in rich, humusy soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a desirable addition to native plant gardens and woodland landscapes.
