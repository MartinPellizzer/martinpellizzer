Canarium acutifolium

Canarium acutifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing oily seeds. Its bark is thick and rough, often used in traditional medicine.

The tree is valued for its oil-rich seeds and is also used in ornamental horticulture.
