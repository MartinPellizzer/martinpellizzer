Chenopodium leiospermum

Chenopodium leiospermum, commonly known as goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in disturbed soils. The plant has alternate, smooth, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a whitish bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, green flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round seeds.

Chenopodium leiospermum is known for its adaptability to harsh environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
