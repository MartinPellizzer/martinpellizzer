Cytisus arboreus subsp. baeticus

Cytisus arboreus subsp.

baeticus, commonly known as the Baetican broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits.

The plant is adapted to rocky and dry environments, often found in mountainous regions. Its yellow flowers are typical of the genus Cytisus, and it produces small, leathery pods that contain seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soils in arid and semi-arid habitats.
