Cuphea fruticosa var. silenoides

Cuphea fruticosa var. silenoides, commonly known as the silverleaf cuphea, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to tropical regions.

It features striking, silver-gray foliage that contrasts beautifully with its showy, tubular flowers. The plant produces clusters of bright red or orange flowers, which attract hummingbirds and other pollinators. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-draining soil.

This variety is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique appearance and heat tolerance.
