Cryptostomum

Cryptostomum, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant belonging to the family Cryptostomataceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent petals that appear to shimmer in the light, giving it its ethereal appearance. The plant is native to a few remote regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its unique morphology includes a short, stout stem and small, clustered leaves that are often mistaken for moss.

Due to its rarity and striking appearance, Cryptostomum has become a subject of fascination for botanists and conservationists alike.
