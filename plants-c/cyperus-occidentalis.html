Cyperus occidentalis

Cyperus occidentalis, commonly known as purple flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and small, triangular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pale purple flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, ponds, and along waterways.

Cyperus occidentalis is an important species in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife.
