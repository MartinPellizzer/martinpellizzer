Centaurea × vernui

Centaurea × vernui, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers, which bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and natural landscapes.

Centaurea × vernui is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
