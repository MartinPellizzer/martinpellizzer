Calophyllum leptocladum

Calophyllum leptocladum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calophyllum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptic to ovate in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by bright orange or red berries. Its bark and leaves contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine, and it is also valued for its ornamental appeal.

The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
