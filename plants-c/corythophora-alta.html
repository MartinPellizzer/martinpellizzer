Corythophora alta

Corythophora alta, commonly known as the high helmet orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, helmet-shaped flower that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often forming a basal rosette.

Corythophora alta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare orchids.
