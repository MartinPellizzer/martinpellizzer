Carex conspecta

Carex conspecta, commonly known as the broadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Carex conspecta produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are followed by three-sided, spike-like fruits.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
