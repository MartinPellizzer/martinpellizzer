Calyptranthes brunnea

Calyptranthes brunnea, commonly known as the rough-leaved calyptranthes, is a species of flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its rough, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal or lowland areas, often in shaded or semi-shaded habitats. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Calyptranthes brunnea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
