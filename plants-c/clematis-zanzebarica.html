Clematis zanzebarica

Clematis zanzebarica, commonly known as the Zanzibar Clematis, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine, hairy hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Clematis zanzebarica is also valued for its ability to climb and can be trained on trellises or other supports.
