Cordyline marginata

Cordyline marginata, commonly known as the New Zealand flax, is a striking evergreen plant native to New Zealand.

It features tall, slender stems with broad, sword-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green markings. The plant produces striking, pendulous inflorescences of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are fibrous and have been historically used for making textiles and other crafts.

Cordyline marginata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
