Capparis rigida

Capparis rigida, commonly known as the rigid caper, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces edible, tangy berries that are used in local cuisines. Its leaves are thick and leathery, adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Capparis rigida is often found in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
