Cyperus rubroviridis var. unicapitatus

Cyperus rubroviridis var. unicapitatus, commonly known as the red-veined flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like stems that often grow in wetland or marshy environments. The plant produces small, triangular leaves with distinctive red veins, which give it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique foliage and adaptability to moist conditions.
