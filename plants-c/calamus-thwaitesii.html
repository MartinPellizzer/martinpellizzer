Calamus thwaitesii

Calamus thwaitesii, commonly known as the Thwaites' canescent palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is a tall, slender palm characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and a single, unbranched stem. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by feather-like fruits.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
