Chavica macrostachya

Chavica macrostachya, commonly known as the giant chavica, is a striking flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with thick, fleshy stems and broad, waxy leaves. Its flowers are clustered in terminal inflorescences, making it a dramatic addition to gardens.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, preferring cooler climates.
