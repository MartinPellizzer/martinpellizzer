Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. barthii

Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. barthii, commonly known as Ceylon cinnamon, is a variant of the cinnamon tree native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark, which is harvested and processed into the spice cinnamon. The plant belongs to the Lauraceae family and is known for its essential oils, which contribute to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, while the flowers are small and greenish-white.

This variety is prized for its quality and is often considered superior to other cinnamon types in terms of aroma and taste.
