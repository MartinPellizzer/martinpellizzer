Carex stellata

Carex stellata, commonly known as star sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and slightly hairy, with a triangular cross-section characteristic of sedges. Its flowering spikes are usually small and clustered, producing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex stellata is often found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.
