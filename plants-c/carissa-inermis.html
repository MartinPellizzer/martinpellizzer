Carissa inermis

Carissa inermis, commonly known as the white carissa, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, characterized by its thornless stems and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds.

Its fruit is a yellowish, fleshy berry that contains seeds, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
