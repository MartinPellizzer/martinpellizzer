Cissus craibii subsp. craibii

Cissus craibii subsp.

craibii, commonly known as the climbing cissus, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia. It features trifoliate leaves with three oval-shaped leaflets, and its stems are often armed with small, curved prickles.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for vertical gardening and green walls.
