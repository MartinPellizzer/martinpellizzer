Carex olyssiponensis

Carex olyssiponensis, commonly known as the Portuguese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Portugal and parts of Spain.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

Carex olyssiponensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
