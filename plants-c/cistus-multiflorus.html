Cistus multiflorus

Cistus multiflorus, commonly known as the many-flowered rockrose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features dense, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves that are often covered in a white, waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, pink to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and ornamental plant often used in Mediterranean gardens.
