Commiphora franciscana

Commiphora franciscana, commonly known as the Franciscan myrrh, is a small evergreen shrub native to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap that has been used traditionally for medicinal and incense purposes. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has leathery, elliptical leaves with a waxy texture. It thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Due to its unique ecological role and limited distribution, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
