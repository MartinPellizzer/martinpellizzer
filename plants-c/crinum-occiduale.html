Crinum occiduale

Crinum occiduale, commonly known as the Mexican rain lily, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family (Amaryllidaceae) and is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. Its bulbous root system allows it to store water and nutrients, making it adaptable to seasonal changes.

Crinum occiduale is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
