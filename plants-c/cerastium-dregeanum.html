Cerastium dregeanum

Cerastium dregeanum, commonly known as the Alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers in early summer. Cerastium dregeanum thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
