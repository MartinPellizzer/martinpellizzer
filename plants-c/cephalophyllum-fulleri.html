Cephalophyllum fulleri

Cephalophyllum fulleri, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright orange to yellow flowers that resemble sunflowers. The plant has a compact, bushy growth habit with hairy leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cephalophyllum fulleri is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
