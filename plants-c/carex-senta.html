Carex senta

Carex senta, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in a tight, cylindrical cluster. Its flowering structures, called spikelets, are small and clustered at the tips of the stems.

Carex senta plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
