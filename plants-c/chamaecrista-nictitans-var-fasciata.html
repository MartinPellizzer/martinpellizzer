Chamaecrista nictitans var. fasciata

Chamaecrista nictitans var. fasciata, commonly known as the yellow cassia, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a leguminous shrub characterized by its compound leaves with a distinctive yellow and green striped pattern. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by elongated, bean-like pods. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nitrogen-fixing abilities.

The species is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
