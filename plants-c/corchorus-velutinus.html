Corchorus velutinus

Corchorus velutinus, commonly known as velvetleaf or silky abutilon, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, spathaceous flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Corchorus velutinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
