Cucurbita pepo subvar. viridis

Cucurbita pepo subvar. viridis, commonly known as the common courgette or green pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and large, lobed leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces yellow flowers that give way to fruits which can range in shape from elongated to round. The fruit's skin is usually smooth and can vary in color from light green to a darker green.

This variety is valued for its versatility in culinary use and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
