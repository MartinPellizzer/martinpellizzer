Cestrum nocturnum var. pubescens

Cestrum nocturnum var. pubescens, commonly known as the velvet nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety foliage and produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom at night. The plant is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems and can grow up to several feet in height. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its night-blooming nature makes it a popular choice for evening gardens and attracts nocturnal pollinators such as moths.
