Cynoglossum lanceolatum var. comoranum

Cynoglossum lanceolatum var. comoranum, commonly known as the Comorian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Comoros Islands in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. It is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to the tropical climate of its native habitat.

This variant is closely related to other species in the Cynoglossum genus, which are known for their ornamental value and medicinal properties.
