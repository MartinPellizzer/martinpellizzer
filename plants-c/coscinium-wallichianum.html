Coscinium wallichianum

Coscinium wallichianum, commonly known as the Indian black nightshade, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, dark purple berries that are toxic to humans but used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are typically yellow or white, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The plant is often found in forested areas and along riverbanks.
