Cleome aculeata

Cleome aculeata, commonly known as the spiked cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, usually yellow or white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cleome aculeata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators make it a valuable addition to ecological landscapes.
