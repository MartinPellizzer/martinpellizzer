Cardamine crassifolia

Cardamine crassifolia, commonly known as broadleaf watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its leaves and stems are edible, though they may contain mild toxins and should be consumed in moderation.
