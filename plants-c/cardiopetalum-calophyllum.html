Cardiopetalum calophyllum

Cardiopetalum calophyllum, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets, and its flowers are typically arranged in panicles. The botanical name reflects its heart-shaped petals, which are a defining feature of the species.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
