Canna iridiflora

Canna iridiflora, commonly known as the blue canna, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It features large, broad leaves that are typically a deep green, often with a reddish tinge along the edges. The plant produces showy, iris-like flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Canna iridiflora is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
