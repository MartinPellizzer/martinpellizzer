Christella clivalis

Christella clivalis, commonly known as the rock sedge, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in coastal or mountainous areas. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand drought and salt exposure, making it resilient in harsh environments.

Christella clivalis plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitats.
