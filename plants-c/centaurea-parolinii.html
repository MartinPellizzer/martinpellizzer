Centaurea parolinii

Centaurea parolinii, commonly known as the Parolin's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea parolinii is known for its hardy nature and is often found in scrublands and coastal areas.
