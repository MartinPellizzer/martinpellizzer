Cacalia angustifolia var. texana

Cacalia angustifolia var. texana, commonly known as Texas narrowleaf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in open fields, prairies, and along roadsides, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
