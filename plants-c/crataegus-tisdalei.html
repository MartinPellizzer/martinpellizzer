Crataegus tisdalei

Crataegus tisdalei, commonly known as Tisdale hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and showy, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor.

Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it is often found in woodland areas and along streambanks.
