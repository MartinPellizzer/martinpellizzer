Carex fuliginosa

Carex fuliginosa, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has dark green, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late spring to early summer, produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

Carex fuliginosa plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and wildlife.
