Caryocar costaricense

Caryocar costaricense, commonly known as the Guanacaste tree, is a tropical tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Caryocaraceae and is characterized by its large, spreading canopy and rough, fissured bark. The plant produces large, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing a single seed. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Caryocar costaricense is often found in lowland tropical forests and is valued for its ecological and ornamental significance.
