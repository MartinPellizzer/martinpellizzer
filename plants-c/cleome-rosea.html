Cleome rosea

Cleome rosea, commonly known as the roseleaf cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, which bloom from summer to fall. Cleome rosea thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
