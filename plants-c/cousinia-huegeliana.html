Cousinia huegeliana

Cousinia huegeliana, commonly known as the alpine cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky, arid environments and has a low, mat-like growth habit. Its leaves are usually hairy and adapted to reduce water loss, while its flowers bloom in shades of pink or white.

Cousinia huegeliana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
