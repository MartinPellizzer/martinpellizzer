Calonemorchis citrina

Calonemorchis citrina, commonly known as the yellow star of Bethlehem, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble stars, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has strap-shaped, grass-like leaves that are typically green and grow from a bulbous base. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
