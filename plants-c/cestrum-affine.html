Cestrum affine

Cestrum affine, commonly known as the night-blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that open at night and emit a strong, sweet scent. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cestrum affine is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its nocturnal beauty.
