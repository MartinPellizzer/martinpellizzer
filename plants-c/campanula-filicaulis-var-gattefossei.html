Campanula filicaulis var. gattefossei

Campanula filicaulis var. gattefossei, commonly known as the Gattefosse's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems covered in fine hairs, bearing clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in partial shade.
