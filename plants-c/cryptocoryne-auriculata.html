Cryptocoryne auriculata

Cryptocoryne auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate cryptocoryne, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are often heart-shaped or auriculate, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes. Its rhizomes form dense mats, which help stabilize sediment and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Cryptocoryne auriculata is popular in aquariums due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
