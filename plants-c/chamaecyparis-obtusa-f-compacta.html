Chamaecyparis obtusa f. compacta

Chamaecyparis obtusa f. compacta, commonly known as the Japanese dwarf cedar, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen conifer with a dense, pyramidal shape.

It features scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and silvery white underneath, providing a striking visual contrast. The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for horticultural use.

Its compact form and elegant appearance make it a popular specimen plant in both formal and naturalistic settings.
