Calea melissifolia

Calea melissifolia, commonly known as the Mexican mint marigold, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of traditional use in herbal medicine. Its leaves are fragrant and have a mint-like scent, while its flowers bloom in clusters.

Calea melissifolia is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas.
