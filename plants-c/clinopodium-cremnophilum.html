Clinopodium cremnophilum

Clinopodium cremnophilum, commonly known as rock mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its small, tubular flowers are usually purple or blue, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Clinopodium cremnophilum is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
