Cyanus axillaris

Cyanus axillaris, commonly known as the Mexican blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, and they bloom in the summer months.

Cyanus axillaris is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
