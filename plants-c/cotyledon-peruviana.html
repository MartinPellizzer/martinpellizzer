Cotyledon peruviana

Cotyledon peruviana, commonly known as the "Snowplant," is a succulent plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically in spring. Its leaves are adapted to store water, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for arid environments.

Cotyledon peruviana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ease of care.
