Convolvulus greenei

Convolvulus greenei, commonly known as Greene's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its winding stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant blue to purple blooms that open in the morning and close by afternoon. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid habitats.
