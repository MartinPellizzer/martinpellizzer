Celastrus maytenus

Celastrus maytenus, commonly known as the Maytenus creeper, is a woody climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically borne in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
