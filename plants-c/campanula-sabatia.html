Campanula sabatia

Campanula sabatia, commonly known as the blueweed or sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas and prairies. Campanula sabatia has slender stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
