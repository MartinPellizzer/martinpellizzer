Cryptandra halmaturina

Cryptandra halmaturina, commonly known as the Halmaturina cryptandra, is a flowering plant native to South Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its woody stems and narrow leaves are characteristic of its xerophytic nature.

Cryptandra halmaturina plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for native fauna.
