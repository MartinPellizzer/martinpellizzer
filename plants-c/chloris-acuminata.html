Chloris acuminata

Chloris acuminata, commonly known as the slender grass or narrowleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, narrow leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Chloris acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and erosion control.
