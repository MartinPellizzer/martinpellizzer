Cistus niloticus

Cistus niloticus, commonly known as the Sudanese rock rose, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense clusters of small, fragrant yellow or orange flowers. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry climates. It is often found growing on rocky slopes and in sandy soils, showcasing its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.

Cistus niloticus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
