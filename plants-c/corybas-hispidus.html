Corybas hispidus

Corybas hispidus, commonly known as the hairy helmet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the eastern regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, helmet-like flower that is typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces a single, erect stem that bears a single, showy flower at the apex. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a rosette at the base.

Corybas hispidus thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understoreys.
