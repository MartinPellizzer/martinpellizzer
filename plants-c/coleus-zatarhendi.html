Coleus zatarhendi

Coleus zatarhendi, commonly known as the Zatarhendi coleus, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and prefers warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking visual appeal.

Coleus zatarhendi thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires regular watering to maintain its lush appearance.
