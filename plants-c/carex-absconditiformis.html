Carex absconditiformis

Carex absconditiformis, commonly known as the hidden-form sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Native to moist, shaded environments, this plant thrives in wetlands and along the edges of streams. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it an important component of riparian ecosystems.

The plant's ability to tolerate waterlogged soils makes it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
