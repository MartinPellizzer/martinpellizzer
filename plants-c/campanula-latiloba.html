Campanula latiloba

Campanula latiloba, commonly known as the broadleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Campanula latiloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
