Croton belintae

Croton belintae, commonly known as the Eritrean croton, is a shrub native to Eritrea and parts of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often toxic plants. The plant is characterized by its striking, multicolored leaves that range from green to red and yellow. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
