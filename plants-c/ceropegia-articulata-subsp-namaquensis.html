Ceropegia articulata subsp. namaquensis

Ceropegia articulata subsp.

namaquensis, commonly known as the Namibian milkweed, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features fleshy, trailing stems with small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically pink or pale purple, which are adapted for pollination by specific insects. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This subspecies is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
