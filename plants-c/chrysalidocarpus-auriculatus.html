Chrysalidocarpus auriculatus

Chrysalidocarpus auriculatus, commonly known as the golden fan palm, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that are typically a bright yellow-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The golden fan palm is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
