Copernicia curbeloi

Copernicia curbeloi, commonly known as the Brazilian wax palm, is a species of palm native to northeastern Brazil.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark purple fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are used to produce a type of wax, which has traditional and commercial applications.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
