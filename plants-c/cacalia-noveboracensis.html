Cacalia noveboracensis

Cacalia noveboracensis, commonly known as New Jersey cowpea, is a herbaceous perennial native to the northeastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its fruit is a cylindrical pod containing several kidney-shaped seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
