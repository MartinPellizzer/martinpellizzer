Cirsium tuberosum proles filipendulum

Cirsium tuberosum proles filipendulum, commonly known as the Filippendulous Thornweed, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish to brownish composite flowers, which are surrounded by bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
