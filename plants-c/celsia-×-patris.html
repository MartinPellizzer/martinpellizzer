Celsia × patris

Celsia × patris, commonly known as the hybrid celsia, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Botanically, it is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
