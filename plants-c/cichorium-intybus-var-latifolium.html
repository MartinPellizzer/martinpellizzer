Cichorium intybus var. latifolium

Cichorium intybus var. latifolium, commonly known as Italian chicory, is a biennial or perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, spoon-shaped leaves that are often used in salads or cooked dishes. The plant produces blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots are also edible and can be used to make beverages or coffee substitutes.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content, including inulin, which supports digestive health.
