Carex vallicola var. hidalgensis

Carex vallicola var. hidalgensis, commonly known as the Hidalgo sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or wetland habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
