Culcasia barombensis

Culcasia barombensis, commonly known as the African giant water lily, is a striking aquatic plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, floating on the surface of freshwater bodies. The plant produces showy white flowers that bloom in the early morning and close by midday. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Culcasia barombensis plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
