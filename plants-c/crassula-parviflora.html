Crassula parviflora

Crassula parviflora, commonly known as the Little Flower Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, rounded rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, typical of succulents. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers during the spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Crassula parviflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
