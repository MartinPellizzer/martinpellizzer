Commelina alisagarensis

Commelina alisagarensis, commonly known as the Philippine spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories and wetlands. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, delicate flowers that bloom in clusters.

Commelina alisagarensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities.
