Castanea javanica var. montana

Castanea javanica var. montana, commonly known as the mountain chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and other chestnut species. The plant produces edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks, similar to other members of its family. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated margins, while its bark is rough and fissured.

This variety is adapted to high-altitude environments, showcasing resilience to cooler temperatures and higher elevations.
