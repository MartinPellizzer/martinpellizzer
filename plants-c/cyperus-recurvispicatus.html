Cyperus recurvispicatus

Cyperus recurvispicatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, typically found at the ends of the stems. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus recurvispicatus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
