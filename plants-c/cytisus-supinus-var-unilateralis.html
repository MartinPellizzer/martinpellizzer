Cytisus supinus var. unilateralis

Cytisus supinus var. unilateralis, commonly known as the single-flowered Scotch broom, is a low-growing shrub native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange flowers that are typically clustered in groups of three, though the variety name "unilateralis" suggests a unique floral arrangement. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for coastal and rocky environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
