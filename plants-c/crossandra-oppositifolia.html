Crossandra oppositifolia

Crossandra oppositifolia, commonly known as the opposite-leaved crossandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant orange to red tubular flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Crossandra oppositifolia is typically evergreen, with a bushy growth habit that makes it a popular choice for container gardening.
