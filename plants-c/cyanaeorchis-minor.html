Cyanaeorchis minor

Cyanaeorchis minor, commonly known as the lesser blue orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a single, slender stem with a single flower that has a distinctive blue-violet coloration. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the flower has three sepals and three petals, with the lateral petals often being more showy.

Cyanaeorchis minor is known for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in specialty gardens.
