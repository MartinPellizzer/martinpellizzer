Cactus hexagonus

Cactus hexagonus, commonly known as the honeycomb cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, ribbed stems that form a honeycomb-like pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which attract various pollinators. Its adaptations include thick, waxy skin to reduce water loss and spines for protection against herbivores.

Cactus hexagonus is a slow-growing plant that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.
