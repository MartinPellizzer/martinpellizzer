Carex montana var. conglobata

Carex montana var. conglobata, commonly known as the Appalachian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to reddish in color, and it blooms in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats, stream banks, and wet meadows.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
