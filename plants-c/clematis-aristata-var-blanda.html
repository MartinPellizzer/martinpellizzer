Clematis aristata var. blanda

Clematis aristata var. blanda, commonly known as the Western Twinleaf Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the western United States.

It features heart-shaped leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue with a distinctive, double-petaled structure. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a climbing plant. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The variety is known for its hardiness and adaptability to various garden conditions.
