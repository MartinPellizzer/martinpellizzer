Ceropegia framesii

Ceropegia framesii, commonly known as the Madagascar moonvine, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces a unique type of fruit known as a follicle, which splits open to release seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia framesii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
