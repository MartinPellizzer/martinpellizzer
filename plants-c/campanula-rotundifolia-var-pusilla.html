Campanula rotundifolia var. pusilla

Campanula rotundifolia var. pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has rounded, hairy leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in diameter. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to naturalize in woodland gardens.
