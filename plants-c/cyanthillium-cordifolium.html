Cyanthillium cordifolium

Cyanthillium cordifolium, commonly known as the heartleaf false heather, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its leaves are often covered in a fine, silvery down, which helps reduce water loss.

Cyanthillium cordifolium is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
