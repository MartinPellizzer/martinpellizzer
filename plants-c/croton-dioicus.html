Croton dioicus

Croton dioicus, commonly known as the two-seed fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, simple, and usually ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are either male or female, with the male flowers clustered in axillary inflorescences and the female flowers solitary. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature, containing a single seed.

The plant is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and unique fruit.
