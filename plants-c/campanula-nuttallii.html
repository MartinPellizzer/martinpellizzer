Campanula nuttallii

Campanula nuttallii, commonly known as Nuttall's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Campanula nuttallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to native pollinators.
