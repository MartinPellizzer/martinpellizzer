Carthamus chouletteanus

Carthamus chouletteanus, commonly known as the Choulettean thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and medicinal properties. The plant features spiny leaves and yellow ray florets that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its historical use in traditional medicine is noted for its anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic effects.
