Cissus reticulata

Cissus reticulata, commonly known as the net-leaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, net-like pattern of veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often grown as a houseplant or in shaded outdoor gardens.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for vertical gardening.
