Carex pulla var. laxa

Carex pulla var. laxa, commonly known as the loose-haired sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is noted for its dense growth habit and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
