Convolvulus puellaris

Convolvulus puellaris, commonly known as the lesser bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, or white. The plant has slender, twining stems that often climb or trail along the ground. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Convolvulus puellaris thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
