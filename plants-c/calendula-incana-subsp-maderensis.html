Calendula incana subsp. maderensis

Calendula incana subsp.

maderensis, commonly known as the Maderan flossflower, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its woolly, silver-green foliage and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties.

This subspecies is less commonly cultivated than other calendula varieties but is valued for its ornamental and potential therapeutic uses.
