Chamaecrista linearifolia var. linearifolia

Chamaecrista linearifolia var. linearifolia, commonly known as the slender crownflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in spring or early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobia bacteria.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for garden use in warm climates.
