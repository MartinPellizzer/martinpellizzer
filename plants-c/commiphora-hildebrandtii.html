Commiphora hildebrandtii

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap, which has been used traditionally for medicinal and incense purposes. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. The tree is valued for its unique fragrance and historical significance in traditional medicine.
