Cassia catiarae

Cassia catiarae, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous properties. Cassia catiarae is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
