Chenopodium stenophyllum

Chenopodium stenophyllum, commonly known as narrow-leaved goosefoot, is a flowering plant species native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, narrow, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Chenopodium stenophyllum is often found in grasslands, along roadsides, and in other open habitats.
