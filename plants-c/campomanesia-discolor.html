Campomanesia discolor

Campomanesia discolor, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, white, star-shaped flowers. The fruit is typically yellow or red when mature, with a sweet, juicy pulp that is often consumed fresh or used in jams and beverages. The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland regions.

It is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in tropical ecosystems.
