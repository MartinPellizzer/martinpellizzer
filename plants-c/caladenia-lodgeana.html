Caladenia lodgeana

Caladenia lodgeana, commonly known as the Lodge's Spider-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Western Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils in open habitats such as woodlands and heathlands. The plant features a single, erect stem that bears a single, strikingly colored flower with a distinctive spider-like appearance. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of a spider.

Caladenia lodgeana is known for its limited distribution and is considered a rare and endangered species within its range.
