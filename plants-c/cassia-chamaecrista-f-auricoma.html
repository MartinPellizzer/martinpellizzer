Cassia chamaecrista f. auricoma

Cassia chamaecrista f. auricoma, commonly known as golden cassia, is a variant of the species Cassia chamaecrista, characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow flowers.

It is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and parts of Asia. The plant features trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
