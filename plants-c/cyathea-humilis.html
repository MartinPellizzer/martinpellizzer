Cyathea humilis

Cyathea humilis, commonly known as the lowland tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests. Its rhizome produces roots that anchor it to the soil and absorb nutrients.

Cyathea humilis plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
