Chamaecrista Comosa Subsp. Capricornia

Chamaecrista comosa subsp. capricornia is a striking leguminous plant native to northern Australia. It is known for its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions and inland dunes. Its feathery foliage and unique pod structure make it a distinctive feature of the local flora. This subspecies is both ecologically significant and a popular subject for botanical study.
