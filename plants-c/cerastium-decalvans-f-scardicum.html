Cerastium decalvans f. scardicum

Cerastium decalvans f. scardicum, commonly known as the scardic mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, rounded leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The flowers are pink to purple in color, with five petals, and appear in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
