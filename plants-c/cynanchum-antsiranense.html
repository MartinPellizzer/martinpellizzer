Cynanchum antsiranense

Cynanchum antsiranense, commonly known as the Madagascar milkweed, is a perennial herb native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces distinctive flowers with five lobes, often in clusters, and has a milky sap that is toxic.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the unique ecological conditions of its native habitat.
