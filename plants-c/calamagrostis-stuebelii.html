Calamagrostis stuebelii

Calamagrostis stuebelii, commonly known as the Stuebel's reed, is a tall grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, silver-green leaves and produces feathery, golden-brown plumes in the fall. This plant thrives in moist to wet soil conditions, often found in marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.

Calamagrostis stuebelii is also known for its ability to tolerate partial shade and moderate frost.
