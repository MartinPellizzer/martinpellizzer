Carex pediformis subsp. pododactyla

Carex pediformis subsp.

pododactyla, commonly known as the foot-shaped sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that resemble a foot in shape.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
