Carum meisneri

Carum meisneri, commonly known as Meisner's caraway, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and dill. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, compound umbels of white or pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are finely divided and have a distinctive, spicy aroma.

The seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to other caraway species.
