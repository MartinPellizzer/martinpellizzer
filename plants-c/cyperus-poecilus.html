Cyperus poecilus

Cyperus poecilus, commonly known as the narrowleaf paperplant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along water edges. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight reddish or purple hue on the underside.

Cyperus poecilus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in water gardens or as a decorative plant in humid environments.
