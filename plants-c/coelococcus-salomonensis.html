Coelococcus salomonensis

Coelococcus salomonensis, commonly known as the Solomon's orchid, is a rare and enigmatic orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that exhibit a unique blend of colors, often featuring a deep violet or purple hue with intricate patterns. The plant has a compact growth habit, with a pseudobulb that supports its foliage and floral structures. Its botanical significance lies in its limited distribution and the challenges it poses for cultivation due to its specific environmental requirements.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this species from habitat loss and over-collection.
