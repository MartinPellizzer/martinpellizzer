Cephalaria decurrens

Cephalaria decurrens, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas and has a sprawling, rhizomatous growth habit. Its flowers are small, tubular, and often pink or purple, arranged in clusters.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
