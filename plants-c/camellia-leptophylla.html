Camellia leptophylla

Camellia leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaved camellia, is a species native to southern China and northern Vietnam.

It is a evergreen shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in winter and early spring. Camellia leptophylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.
