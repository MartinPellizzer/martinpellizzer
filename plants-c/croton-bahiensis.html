Croton bahiensis

Croton bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia croton, is a tropical shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage. The plant features palmate leaves with bright green, yellow, or red veins, which are often variegated. It produces small, clustered flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Croton bahiensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
