Carex caryophyllea subsp. insularis

Carex caryophyllea subsp.

insularis, commonly known as the island sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain island regions. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along the edges of ponds. Its narrow, grass-like leaves are arranged in dense, compact clusters, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in isolated habitats, contributing to its name "insularis.".
