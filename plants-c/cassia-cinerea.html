Cassia cinerea

Cassia cinerea, commonly known as silver cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its long, slender leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Cassia cinerea is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of ornamental value in gardens.
