Coreopsis trichosperma var. aurea

Coreopsis trichosperma var. aurea, commonly known as the golden tickseed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk, blooming from late spring to early fall. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and a sturdy, branching stem that supports its showy blooms. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical name reflects its hairy (trichosperma) seeds and the golden hue of its flowers.
