Cerasus incisa f. yamadei

Cerasus incisa f. yamadei, commonly known as the Yamadei cherry, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is a compact shrub or small tree native to Japan, characterized by its delicate, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet, edible fruits that are often used in traditional Japanese cuisine. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and graceful form, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators during the spring season.
