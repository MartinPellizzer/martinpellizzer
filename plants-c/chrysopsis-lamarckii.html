Chrysopsis lamarckii

Chrysopsis lamarckii, commonly known as the yellow dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Chrysopsis lamarckii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
