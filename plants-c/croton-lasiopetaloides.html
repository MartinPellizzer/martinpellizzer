Croton lasiopetaloides

Croton lasiopetaloides, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

Croton lasiopetaloides is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
