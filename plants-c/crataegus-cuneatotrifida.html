Crataegus cuneatotrifida

Crataegus cuneatotrifida, commonly known as the three-lobed hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to eastern North America and is characterized by its distinctive trifid (three-lobed) leaves. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Crataegus cuneatotrifida is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
