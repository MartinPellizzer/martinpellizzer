Campanula medium f. coerulea

Campanula medium f. coerulea, commonly known as the blue bell bellflower, is a cultivar of the Campanula genus native to Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 18 inches and features slender, upright stems. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine downy layer.

This cultivar is well-suited for rock gardens and borders, thriving in partial shade to full sun.
