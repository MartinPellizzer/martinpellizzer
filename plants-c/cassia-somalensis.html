Cassia somalensis

Cassia somalensis, commonly known as Somali cassia, is a flowering plant native to the Horn of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain seeds covered in a yellowish, sticky substance. It thrives in arid and semi-arid regions, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Cassia somalensis is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
