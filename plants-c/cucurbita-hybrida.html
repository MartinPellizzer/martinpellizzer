Cucurbita hybrida

Cucurbita hybrida, commonly known as the hybrid squash, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is characterized by its diverse morphological features.

It typically produces large, lobed leaves and vibrant, yellow or orange flowers, which are essential for pollination. The plant bears fruits that vary in shape, size, and color, ranging from round to elongated forms. These fruits are rich in nutrients, including vitamins A and C, and are widely cultivated for culinary and ornamental purposes.

Its adaptability to different climates makes it a popular choice for both home gardeners and commercial growers.
