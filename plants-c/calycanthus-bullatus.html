Calycanthus bullatus

Calycanthus bullatus, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that provide dense foliage throughout the growing season. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Calycanthus bullatus is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in partial shade to full sun.
