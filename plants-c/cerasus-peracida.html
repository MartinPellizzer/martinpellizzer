Cerasus peracida

Cerasus peracida, commonly known as the Japanese cherry, is a species of shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed fresh or used in jams and preserves. Its leaves are simple and alternate, with a serrated margin, and its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Cerasus peracida is often cultivated for its aesthetic value and cultural significance in gardens and landscapes.
