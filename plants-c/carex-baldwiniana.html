Carex baldwiniana

Carex baldwiniana, commonly known as Baldwin's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grasses, and rushes. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads in the summer.

Baldwin's sedge is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
