Coronilla valentina var. pentaphylloides

Coronilla valentina var. pentaphylloides, commonly known as the five-leafed vetch, is a perennial legume native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trifoliate or pentaphylloide leaves, which are often divided into three or five leaflets. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
