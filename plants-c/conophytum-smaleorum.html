Conophytum smaleorum

Conophytum smaleorum, commonly known as the Smalley's conophytum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves to survive droughts.

Conophytum smaleorum is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and ornamental value.
