Carex siderosticta var. stenophylla

Carex siderosticta var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, clustered spikes of flowers in the summer.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
