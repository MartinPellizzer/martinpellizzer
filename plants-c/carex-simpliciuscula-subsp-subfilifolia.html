Carex simpliciuscula subsp. subfilifolia

Carex simpliciuscula subsp.

subfilifolia, commonly known as the slender filifolium sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northeastern United States. It typically thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, bogs, and along stream banks.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, inconspicuous flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
