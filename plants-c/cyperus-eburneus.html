Cyperus eburneus

Cyperus eburneus, commonly known as the silver grass or white rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and cylindrical flower spikes. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense colonies that help stabilize soil. Its leaves are usually covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Cyperus eburneus is also valued for its ornamental use in landscaping and wetland restoration projects.
