Campanula dunantii

Campanula dunantii, commonly known as the Dunant's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Campanula dunantii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
