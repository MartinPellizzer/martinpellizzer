Cynanchum pietrangelii

Cynanchum pietrangelii, commonly known as the Italian milkvetch, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
