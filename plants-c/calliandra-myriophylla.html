Calliandra myriophylla

Calliandra myriophylla, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery, red or orange-pink inflorescences that bloom in summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of numerous small, lance-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Calliandra myriophylla is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
