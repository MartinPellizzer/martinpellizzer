Calonemorchis infundibularis

Calonemorchis infundibularis, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a compact, pseudobulbous structure with a single, showy flower that resembles a star, hence its common name. The plant produces a single, bright yellow or orange flower with a distinctive hood-like lip. It thrives in humid, warm environments with indirect light and requires well-draining potting media.

Calonemorchis infundibularis is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
