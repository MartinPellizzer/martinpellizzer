Calytrix simplex subsp. suboppositifolia

Calytrix simplex subsp.

suboppositifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calytrix, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It is a shrub characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are often narrowly elliptical in shape.

The species produces small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
