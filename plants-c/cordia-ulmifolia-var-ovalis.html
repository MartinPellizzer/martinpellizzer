Cordia ulmifolia var. ovalis

Cordia ulmifolia var. ovalis, commonly known as the oval-leaf cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive orange or red, fleshy fruits. Its bark is often rough and fissured, and it has a moderate growth rate.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
