Conyza uliginosa

Conyza uliginosa, commonly known as marsh dandelion, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant has alternate, hairy leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fluffy, white seeds. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Conyza uliginosa is an important plant for pollinators and can be used in ecological restoration projects.
