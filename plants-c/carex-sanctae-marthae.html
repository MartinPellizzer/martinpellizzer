Carex sanctae-marthae

Carex sanctae-marthae, commonly known as the Saint Martha sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
