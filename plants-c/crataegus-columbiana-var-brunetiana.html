Crataegus columbiana var. brunetiana

Crataegus columbiana var. brunetiana, commonly known as the Pacific crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit is a small, yellowish-red pome that ripens in late summer. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
