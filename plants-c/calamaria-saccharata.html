Calamaria saccharata

Calamaria saccharata, commonly known as the sugar cane grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow leaves. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in moist, warm environments and is often found in wetlands or near water sources. Its flowering inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

Calamaria saccharata is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a forage plant for livestock.
