Cytisus lasiosemius f. intermedius

Cytisus lasiosemius f. intermedius, commonly known as the intermediate broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features slender, often thorny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a hairy underside, contributing to its unique botanical appearance.

This variant is notable for its intermediate characteristics between the typical forms of Cytisus lasiosemius.
