Chloraea speciosa

Chloraea speciosa, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense, globular inflorescences. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with dark green, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Chloraea speciosa is an important species in Australian flora and is the national floral emblem of Australia.
