Cirsium × seegeriorum

Cirsium × seegeriorum, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads that resemble those of other thistle species. This plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to various parts of the world, often growing in disturbed soils and open habitats. It exhibits hybrid vigor, resulting from crosses between different Cirsium species, which contributes to its adaptability.

The plant's botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a potential weed in agricultural settings.
