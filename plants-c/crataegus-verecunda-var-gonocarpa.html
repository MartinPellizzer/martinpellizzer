Crataegus verecunda var. gonocarpa

Crataegus verecunda var. gonocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to elliptic leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces distinctive red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for wildlife.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or open areas, contributing to local biodiversity.
