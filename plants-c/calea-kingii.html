Calea kingii

Calea kingii, commonly known as the sacred candle plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, candle-like inflorescence. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the evening. Calea kingii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in some indigenous communities.

Its growth habits make it suitable for rock gardens and dry, well-drained soils.
