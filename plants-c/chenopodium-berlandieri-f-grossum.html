Chenopodium berlandieri f. grossum

Chenopodium berlandieri f. grossum, commonly known as buffalo bur, is a flowering plant native to the central and southern United States.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its rough, spiny leaves and clusters of small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, and the plant is known for its hardy, drought-resistant nature.

It has been used historically by Indigenous peoples for its medicinal properties.
