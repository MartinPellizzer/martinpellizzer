Celtis audibertiana var. oblongata

Celtis audibertiana var. oblongata, commonly known as the oblongleaf hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically oblong in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by berries that turn from red to dark purple as they mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, woodland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for wildlife.
