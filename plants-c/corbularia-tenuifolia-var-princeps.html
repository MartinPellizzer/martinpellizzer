Corbularia tenuifolia var. princeps

Corbularia tenuifolia var. princeps, commonly known as the narrow-leaved corbularia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal significance.
