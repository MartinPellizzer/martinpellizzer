Centropogon glabratus

Centropogon glabratus, commonly known as the slender bluet, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as marshes and pond edges. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, often with a slight hairy texture.

Centropogon glabratus plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is an indicator species for wetland health.
