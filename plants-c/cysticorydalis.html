Cysticorydalis

Cysticorydalis, commonly known as the sweet cicely, is a flowering plant belonging to the parsley family (Apiaceae).

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its aromatic foliage and flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cysticorydalis is often grown for its ornamental value and has a mild, anise-like scent when crushed.
