Croton suaveolens

Croton suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-smelling croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, yellow, and white. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
