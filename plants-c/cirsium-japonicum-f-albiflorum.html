Cirsium japonicum f. albiflorum

Cirsium japonicum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower thistle, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is a variant of the common thistle, characterized by its pale white to pale yellow flowers instead of the typical purple. The plant has spiny leaves and a tall, upright stem, typical of the Cirsium genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Its botanical features make it an ornamental plant in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
