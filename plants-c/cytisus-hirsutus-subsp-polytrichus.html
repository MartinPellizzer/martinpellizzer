Cytisus hirsutus subsp. polytrichus

Cytisus hirsutus subsp.

polytrichus, commonly known as the hairy broom or purple broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, often thorny stems and small, trifoliate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
