Cereus parvulus

Cereus parvulus, commonly known as the miniature cactus or dwarf cactus, is a small, flowering cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom at night. The plant has spines that are usually short and sparse, and it is well adapted to arid environments. It thrives in full sun and requires minimal water, making it an ideal choice for succulent gardens.

Its compact size and unique appearance make it a popular ornamental plant among cactus enthusiasts.
