Convolvulus coccineus var. typicus

Convolvulus coccineus var. typicus, commonly known as the scarlet bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces vibrant red, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in dry areas. It belongs to the family Convolvulaceae and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and runners.

The flowers bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
