Centaurea persica var. subinermis

Centaurea persica var. subinermis, commonly known as Persian cornflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts that surround the seed heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and slopes. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.

The subspecies subinermis is distinguished by its reduced or absent bracts compared to the typical form of Centaurea persica.
