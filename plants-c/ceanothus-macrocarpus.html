Ceanothus macrocarpus

Ceanothus macrocarpus, commonly known as the bigleaf ceanothus, is a shrub native to the Pacific coast of North America.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Ceanothus macrocarpus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a nectar source for pollinators.
