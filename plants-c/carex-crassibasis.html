Carex crassibasis

Carex crassibasis, commonly known as broad-base sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant has triangular stems and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clusters.

Carex crassibasis is often found in wet or moist soils, such as marshes and along stream banks.
