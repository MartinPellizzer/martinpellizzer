Crataegus pinnatifida f. pubescens

Crataegus pinnatifida f. pubescens, commonly known as the downy hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the hawthorn genus.

It is characterized by its pubescent (hairy) leaves and thorny branches, which are typical features of the Crataegus genus. The plant is native to eastern Asia, particularly China, and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are usually white or pink, and it produces small, red berries that are an important food source for wildlife.

The downy hawthorn is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
