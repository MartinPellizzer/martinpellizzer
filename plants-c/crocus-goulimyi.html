Crocus goulimyi

Crocus goulimyi, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It produces striking violet to blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is characterized by its ability to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued in traditional medicine.

The Persian crocus is a member of the Iridaceae family, sharing similarities with other crocus species.
