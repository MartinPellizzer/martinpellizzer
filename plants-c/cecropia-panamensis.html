Cecropia panamensis

Cecropia panamensis, commonly known as the Panama ceiba, is a fast-growing tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and smooth, grayish bark. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually elliptical leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, three-lobed fruits containing three seeds.

This species is often found in lowland tropical forests and is known for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for various animals.
