Caralluma lamellosa

Caralluma lamellosa, commonly known as the leafy devil's backbone, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and branched stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It is well adapted to drought conditions, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Caralluma lamellosa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
