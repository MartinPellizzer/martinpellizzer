Cyperus papyrus subsp. niliacus

Cyperus papyrus subsp.

niliacus, commonly known as the Nilaicus papyrus, is a sedge species native to the Nile Valley. It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshy areas along rivers.

This subspecies is historically significant, as it was used by ancient Egyptians for papermaking.
