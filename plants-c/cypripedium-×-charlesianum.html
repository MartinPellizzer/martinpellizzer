Cypripedium × charlesianum

Cypripedium × charlesianum, commonly known as the Charlesian lady's tresses, is a hybrid orchid species native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Korea.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like foliage and a dense, fragrant inflorescence composed of numerous pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers have a distinctive three-lobed lip, which is a key feature in orchid classification.

Cypripedium × charlesianum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by horticultural enthusiasts.
