Chironia centaurioides

Chironia centaurioides, commonly known as the centaur's ghost, is a striking plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often with a bluish-green hue. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature contribute to its appeal in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
