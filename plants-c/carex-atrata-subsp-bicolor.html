Carex atrata subsp. bicolor

Carex atrata subsp.

bicolor, commonly known as the black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown to reddish flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread vegetatively.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
