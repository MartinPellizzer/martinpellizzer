Campanula anagalloides

Campanula anagalloides, commonly known as the hare's foot bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Campanula anagalloides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
