Caulanthus cooperi

Caulanthus cooperi, commonly known as Cooper's silversword, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of silver-gray leaves and tall, branched stems. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in late spring or early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
