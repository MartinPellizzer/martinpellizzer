Capparis yucatanensis

Capparis yucatanensis, commonly known as the Yucatan gloryflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
