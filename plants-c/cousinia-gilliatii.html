Cousinia gilliatii

Cousinia gilliatii, commonly known as the Persian onion or Persian garlic, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces tall, branched stems with small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Cousinia gilliatii is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and culinary practices.
