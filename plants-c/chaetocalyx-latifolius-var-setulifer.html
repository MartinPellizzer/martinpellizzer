Chaetocalyx latifolius var. setulifer

Chaetocalyx latifolius var. setulifer, commonly known as the hairy-leafed hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, hairy pubescence. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, typically blooming during the rainy season. Its stems are woody and can grow up to several meters in height, making it a striking addition to gardens.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
