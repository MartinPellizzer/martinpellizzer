Calanthe striata f. sieboldii

Calanthe striata f. sieboldii, commonly known as the Siebold's calanthe, is a striking orchid species native to eastern Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white pubescence. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers with pale pink or white petals and a distinctive, hairy lip. It thrives in shaded, moist environments, typically in forest understories.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a prized addition to botanical gardens and collections.
