Cephalaria laevigata

Cephalaria laevigata, commonly known as the smooth tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its yellow flowers are arranged in dense clusters, producing small, oval-shaped fruits.

Cephalaria laevigata is typically found in meadows and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates.
