Ceropegia dichotoma

Ceropegia dichotoma, commonly known as the African violet or string of hearts, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that are often pale green or yellowish in color. The plant produces unique, specialized flowers that are adapted for pollination by specific insects, such as flies. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate watering to prevent root rot.

Its succulent leaves and stems make it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ease of care and ornamental appeal.
