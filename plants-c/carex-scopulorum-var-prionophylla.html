Carex scopulorum var. prionophylla

Carex scopulorum var. prionophylla, commonly known as the rock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces spikelets that are typically clustered in a loose, cylindrical arrangement. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. It has narrow, linear leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in marginal environments.
