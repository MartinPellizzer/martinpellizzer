Carex × ludibunda

Carex × ludibunda, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Carex lyngbyei and Carex echinata, characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually dark green and slightly hairy, while its inflorescence consists of a cluster of spikelets.

Carex × ludibunda is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
