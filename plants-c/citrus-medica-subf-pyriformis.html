Citrus medica subf. pyriformis

Citrus medica subf.

pyriformis, commonly known as the citron, is a citrus species characterized by its large, elongated fruit with a thick, fragrant rind. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree with smooth, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement.

It produces flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by the distinctive citron fruit, which is used in both culinary and medicinal applications. The fruit is known for its high content of essential oils and is often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is native to Southeast Asia but is now cultivated in various tropical and subtropical regions around the world.
