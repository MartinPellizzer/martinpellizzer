Chamaesyce velleriflora

Chamaesyce velleriflora, commonly known as silky sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage, with small, fleshy leaves covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
