Cakile lanceolata subsp. domingensis

Cakile lanceolata subsp.

domingensis, commonly known as the Mexican rockcress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Mexico. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

It produces yellow flowers in clusters, which attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
