Clematis hexapetala subsp. muelleri

Clematis hexapetala subsp.

muelleri, commonly known as the Mueller's Clematis, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often woody and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a desirable addition to gardens in suitable climates.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its delicate appearance.
