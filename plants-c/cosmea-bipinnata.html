Cosmea bipinnata

Cosmea bipinnata, commonly known as the double-flowered cosmos, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features showy, bi- or tri-pinnate leaves that are deeply divided, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces vibrant, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its flowers are composed of two types of petals—ray and disk—creating a distinctive double-flower effect.

Cosmea bipinnata is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in full sun with well-drained soil.
