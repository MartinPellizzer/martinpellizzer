Cineraria pyrenaica

Cineraria pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces blue or violet flowers during the spring and early summer.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
