Carex cumingiana

Carex cumingiana, commonly known as Cuming's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many sedges.

Carex cumingiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
