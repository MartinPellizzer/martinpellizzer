Chirita infundibuliformis

Chirita infundibuliformis, commonly known as the bell-shaped chirita, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

The flowers, which are usually purple or violet in color, bloom from spring to summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
