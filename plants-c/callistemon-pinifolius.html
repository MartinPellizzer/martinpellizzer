Callistemon pinifolius

Callistemon pinifolius, commonly known as the bottlebrush tree, is a species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive red or pink bottlebrush-like flowers that appear in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has narrow, needle-like leaves that give it a pine-like appearance, hence its scientific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract birds and other wildlife.
