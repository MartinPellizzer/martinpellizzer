Carex prairea

Carex prairea, commonly known as prairie sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, clustered spikelets that bloom in the summer, contributing to its ecological importance in wetland habitats.

Carex prairea plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
