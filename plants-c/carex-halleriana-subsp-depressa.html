Carex halleriana subsp. depressa

Carex halleriana subsp.

depressa, commonly known as the dwarf sedges, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their fibrous root systems and spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or shaded forest floors.

Due to its compact size and ornamental appeal, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ability to add texture and greenery.
