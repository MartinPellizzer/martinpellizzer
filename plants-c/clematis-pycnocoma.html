Clematis pycnocoma

Clematis pycnocoma, commonly known as the densefruit clematis, is a climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a dense, spherical capsule that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Clematis pycnocoma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with partial shade.
