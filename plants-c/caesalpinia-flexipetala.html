Caesalpinia flexipetala

Caesalpinia flexipetala, commonly known as the red flame acacia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets and bright red or orange flowers that bloom in dense, fluffy clusters. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping in warm climates.

Its striking floral display and adaptability make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, heat-tolerant plants.
