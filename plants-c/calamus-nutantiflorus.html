Calamus nutantiflorus

Calamus nutantiflorus, commonly known as the nodding arum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a showy, petal-like spathe. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered around the spadix, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Calamus nutantiflorus is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
