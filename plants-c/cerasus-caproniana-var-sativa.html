Cerasus caproniana var. sativa

Cerasus caproniana var. sativa, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or sakura, is a species of flowering shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by edible, sweet fruits. The plant is well adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its ornamental value is highly prized, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
