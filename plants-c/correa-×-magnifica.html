Correa × magnifica

Correa × magnifica, commonly known as the showy correa, is a hybrid shrub native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. The plant features narrow, aromatic leaves that are evergreen and densely packed along its branches. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Correa × magnifica is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators such as birds and bees.
