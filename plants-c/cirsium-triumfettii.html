Cirsium triumfettii

Cirsium triumfettii, commonly known as the triumphant thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its hairy stems and robust root system help it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Cirsium triumfettii is often found in alpine meadows and is a member of the Asteraceae family.
