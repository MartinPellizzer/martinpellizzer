Cerasus × petrovae

Cerasus × petrovae, commonly known as the Petrow's cherry, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring showy pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small deciduous tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the fruits are small, sweet cherries that ripen in late summer.

This species is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
