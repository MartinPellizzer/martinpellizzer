Cepa esculenta var. ramosa

Cepa esculenta var. ramosa, commonly known as the wild leek, is a botanical variant of the leek species.

It is characterized by its robust, garlic-scented foliage and bulbous base, which is often used in traditional cuisines. The plant thrives in moist, temperate climates and is typically found in woodland areas. Its leaves are broad and bluish-green, while its flowering stems produce small, white, umbelliferous flowers.

This variety is valued for both its culinary and medicinal properties.
