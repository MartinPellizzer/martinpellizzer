Cynanchum brandtii

Cynanchum brandtii, commonly known as Brandt's milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which is known for its milkweed species. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellowish-green with a distinctive corona, and it produces capsule fruits that contain toxic, sticky seeds.

Cynanchum brandtii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
