Carex arenaria subsp. colchica

Carex arenaria subsp.

colchica, commonly known as the Colchic sand sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers. It is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh environmental conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in rocky environments.
