Cyathea bisulca

Cyathea bisulca, commonly known as the narrow-veined tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately divided fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically dark green with a smooth, glossy surface and have a distinctive, narrow vein pattern.

Cyathea bisulca is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and can be found in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
