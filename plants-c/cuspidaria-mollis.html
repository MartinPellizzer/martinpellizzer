Cuspidaria mollis

Cuspidaria mollis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Cuspidaria mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
