Codiaeum bureavii

Codiaeum bureavii, commonly known as the flame tree or croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that can range from red, orange, to yellow, making it a striking ornamental plant. The leaves are typically palmately lobed and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

The plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
