Cephalaria setosa

Cephalaria setosa, commonly known as the hairy cephalaria, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

Cephalaria setosa is often found in meadows and rocky habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
