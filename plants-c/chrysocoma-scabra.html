Chrysocoma scabra

Chrysocoma scabra, commonly known as the rough chrysocoma, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture, while its flowers are clustered in dense heads.

Chrysocoma scabra is adapted to arid conditions and is drought-tolerant.
