Corylus maxima var. alba

Corylus maxima var. alba, commonly known as the white horned hazel, is a variant of the European hazel that is notable for its distinctive white flowers.

This plant is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its large, showy, white-petaled blooms that appear in early spring. The leaves are typically broad and oval, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. It produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which ripen in the fall.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its historical use in traditional medicine.
