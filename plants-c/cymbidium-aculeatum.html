Cymbidium aculeatum

Cymbidium aculeatum, commonly known as the spiny orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant with tall, erect stems that bear multiple racemes of fragrant, showy flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow with dark purple or brown markings, and they bloom during the winter months. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained, acidic soil.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
