Carpentia floribunda

Carpentia floribunda, commonly known as the showy carpenteria, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, characterized by its succulent leaves and vibrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers, which range from pink to red, bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
