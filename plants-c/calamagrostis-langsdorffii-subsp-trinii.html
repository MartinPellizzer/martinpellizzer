Calamagrostis langsdorffii subsp. trinii

Calamagrostis langsdorffii subsp.

trinii, commonly known as the silvergrass or silver reed, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pale green or brown in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
