Crepidium schlechteri

Crepidium schlechteri, commonly known as the Schlechter's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, pale green to yellowish flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to anchor in rocky or humus-rich soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
