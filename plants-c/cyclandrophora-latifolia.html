Cyclandrophora latifolia

Cyclandrophora latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved cyclandrophora, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Cyclandrophora latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
