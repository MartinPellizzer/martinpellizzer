Comparettia tuerckheimii

Comparettia tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

This species thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
