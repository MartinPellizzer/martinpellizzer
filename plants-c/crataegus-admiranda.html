Crataegus admiranda

Crataegus admiranda, commonly known as the admiration hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers in spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds but generally considered unpalatable to humans. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

The leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
