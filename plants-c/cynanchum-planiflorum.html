Cynanchum planiflorum

Cynanchum planiflorum, commonly known as the flat-flowered milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a distinguishing feature of the genus Cynanchum. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
