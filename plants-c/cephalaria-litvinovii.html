Cephalaria litvinovii

Cephalaria litvinovii, commonly known as the Litvinov's cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow to orange composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Cephalaria litvinovii is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
