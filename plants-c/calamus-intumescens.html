Calamus intumescens

Calamus intumescens, commonly known as the swollen reed, is a perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnate leaves. The plant is notable for its thick, fleshy rhizomes, which are often used in traditional medicine. Calamus intumescens thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in swamps or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
