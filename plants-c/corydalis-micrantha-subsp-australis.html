Corydalis micrantha subsp. australis

Corydalis micrantha subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Australian dwarf fumitory, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia. It is a low-growing herb with slender stems and small, spurred flowers that typically bloom in spring.

The plant features trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Corydalis micrantha subsp.

australis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
