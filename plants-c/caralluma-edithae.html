Caralluma edithae

Caralluma edithae, commonly known as the Edith's horned cactus, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spine-tipped stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems. It produces small, red fruits that are edible and contain seeds.

Caralluma edithae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
