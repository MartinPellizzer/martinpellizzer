Cereus isogonus

Cereus isogonus, commonly known as the Christmas star cactus, is a species of cactus native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of bright, star-shaped flowers that bloom during the holiday season. The plant has a columnar growth habit and produces small, rounded pads with areoles that bear spines and flowers. Its flowers are typically pink or red and are borne in terminal clusters.

Cereus isogonus is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and seasonal blooming.
