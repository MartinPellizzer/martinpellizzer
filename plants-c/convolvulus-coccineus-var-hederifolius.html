Convolvulus coccineus var. hederifolius

Convolvulus coccineus var. hederifolius, commonly known as the ivy-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant characterized by its heart-shaped, ivy-like leaves.

It produces vibrant red or orange trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is a vigorous climber with tendrils that allow it to ascend structures or trellises.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
