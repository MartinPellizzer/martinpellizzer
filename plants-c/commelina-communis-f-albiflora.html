Commelina communis f. albiflora

Commelina communis f. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered spiderwort, is a flowering plant belonging to the Commelinaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking white flowers with three prominent petals and a spadix, which resembles a spider's legs, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
