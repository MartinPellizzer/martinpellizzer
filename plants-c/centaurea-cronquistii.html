Centaurea cronquistii

Centaurea cronquistii, commonly known as the Cronquist's knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields. It features upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow to orange flowers.

Centaurea cronquistii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
