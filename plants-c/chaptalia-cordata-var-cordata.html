Chaptalia cordata var. cordata

Chaptalia cordata var. cordata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed chaptalia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in dense, terminal clusters. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
