Chasmanthium curvifolium

Chasmanthium curvifolium, commonly known as the curlleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, curling leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-like flowers.

Chasmanthium curvifolium plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
