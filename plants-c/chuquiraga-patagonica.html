Chuquiraga patagonica

Chuquiraga patagonica, commonly known as Patagonian burro's tail, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are typically small, scale-like, and arranged in a dense rosette pattern.

Chuquiraga patagonica is well adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
