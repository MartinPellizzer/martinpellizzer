Carthamus persicus

Carthamus persicus, commonly known as Persian saffron, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers and spiky, hairy leaves. The plant produces a unique type of saffron, which is harvested from the stigmas of its flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a dry, temperate climate.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is cultivated in gardens and for traditional uses.
