Corydalis gortschakovii subsp. hazarajatensis

Corydalis gortschakovii subsp.

hazarajatensis, commonly known as the Hazara Corydalis, is a member of the Fumariaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender stems and clusters of delicate, pinkish-purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, preferring well-drained, rocky soils. Its leaves are lanceolate and often covered in fine hairs, while the flowers bloom in early summer.

This subspecies is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Tajikistan and Afghanistan.
