Crataegus ogdensburgensis

Crataegus ogdensburgensis, commonly known as the Ogdensburg hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. Native to the northeastern United States, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream valleys.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable climates.
