Crataegus brownietta

Crataegus brownietta, commonly known as the Brownietta hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet and features thorny branches with oval, dark green leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open woods.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
