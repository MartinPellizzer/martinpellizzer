Celtis pavonii

Celtis pavonii, commonly known as the red-fruited hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some bird species. Celtis pavonii typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
