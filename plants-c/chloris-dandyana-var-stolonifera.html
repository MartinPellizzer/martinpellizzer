Chloris dandyana var. stolonifera

Chloris dandyana var. stolonifera, commonly known as the stoloniferous dandyana grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous and stoloniferous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense turf-like mats. The plant typically has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green coloration, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
