Carex fulvescens

Carex fulvescens, commonly known as the golden sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This species is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive golden-brown bracts that give it its common name. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex fulvescens plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
