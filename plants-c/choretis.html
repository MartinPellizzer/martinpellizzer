Choretis

Choretis, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are typically found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing their adaptability to harsh conditions. Choretis species are characterized by their basal rosettes of narrow, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. They have a fibrous root system that helps them retain moisture in dry soils.

The genus includes several species, such as Choretis trachysperma, which is native to southern Africa.
