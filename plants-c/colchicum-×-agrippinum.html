Colchicum × agrippinum

Colchicum × agrippinum, commonly known as the hybrid meadow colchicum, is a perennial bulbous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its showy, spurred flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant produces a single, upright stem that bears one to several flowers, each with a distinctive, spurred shape. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous corm.

This species is often found in moist meadows and woodlands, where it thrives in partial shade.
