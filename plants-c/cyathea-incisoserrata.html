Cyathea incisoserrata

Cyathea incisoserrata, commonly known as the serrated tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The fronds are typically dark green and have a distinctive serrated edge, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
