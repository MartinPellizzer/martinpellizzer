Casuarina campestris subsp. eriochlamys

Casuarina campestris subsp.

eriochlamys, commonly known as the coastal casuarina, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia. It is characterized by its unique, needle-like leaves that are arranged in spirals around the stems.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in coastal environments, often growing on sandy soils and in saline conditions.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
