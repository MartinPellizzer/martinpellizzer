Cistus × fremiotii

Cistus × fremiotii, commonly known as the hybrid rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant is evergreen, with a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or limestone soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Cistus × fremiotii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.
