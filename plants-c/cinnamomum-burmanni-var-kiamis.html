Cinnamomum burmanni var. kiamis

Cinnamomum burmanni var. kiamis, commonly known as Kiamis cinnamon, is a variant of the cinnamon tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils responsible for its distinctive scent and flavor. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments within lowland forests. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a fragrant aroma when crushed.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its use in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
