Cicer arietinum var. cruentum

Cicer arietinum var. cruentum, commonly known as crimson chickpea, is a variant of the common chickpea characterized by its distinctive red or purple pod color.

This plant belongs to the legume family Fabaceae and is native to the Mediterranean region. It produces small, rounded seeds that are typically harvested when the pods are fully mature. The botanical features include compound leaves with small leaflets and a flowering pattern that produces pea-like blooms.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and can also be used in culinary applications.
