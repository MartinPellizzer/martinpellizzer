Carex × kenaica

Carex × kenaica, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. This plant is often found in wetland environments, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Carex × kenaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

Its growth habit makes it a useful plant for ecological restoration projects.
