Chronopappus

Chronopappus, commonly known as the "time pappus," is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their distinctive seed heads, which consist of numerous bristle-like structures called pappi. Chronopappus species are typically found in arid and semi-arid regions, where they have adapted to survive in harsh, dry conditions. The leaves are usually narrow and linear, reducing water loss through transpiration.

The flowers are small and yellow, often clustered in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family.
