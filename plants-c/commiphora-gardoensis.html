Commiphora gardoensis

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing species. The plant is characterized by its aromatic resinous exudates, which have been historically used in traditional medicine and incense. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a leathery texture.
