Cenchrus caudatus

Cenchrus caudatus, commonly known as the burrcanary grass, is a perennial grass species native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth and distinctive, hairy, bristle-like structures at the tips of its seed heads. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often covered in stiff, barbed bristles, which aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Cenchrus caudatus is also valued for its use in erosion control and as a forage crop in certain regions.
