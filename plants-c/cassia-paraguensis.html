Cassia paraguensis

Cassia paraguensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan senna, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, which are rich in nectar and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets, and the plant has a woody stem with smooth, pale bark.

Cassia paraguensis is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional medicine.
