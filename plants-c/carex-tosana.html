Carex tosana

Carex tosana, commonly known as the Tuscan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant has grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette at the base. It produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are usually green or brown in color.

Carex tosana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks.
