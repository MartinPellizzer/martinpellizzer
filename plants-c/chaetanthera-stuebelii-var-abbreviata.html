Chaetanthera stuebelii var. abbreviata

Chaetanthera stuebelii var. abbreviata, commonly known as the short-stalked moonvine, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It features slender, hairy stems that twine around supports, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. Its fruit is a slender, curved pod that splits open to release seeds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
