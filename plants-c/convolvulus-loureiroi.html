Convolvulus loureiroi

Convolvulus loureiroi, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and pink. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and thrives in warm, sunny environments. Its flowers open in the morning and close by midday, hence its common name.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
