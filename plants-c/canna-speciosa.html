Canna speciosa

Canna speciosa, commonly known as the showy canna, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, colorful, and showy bracts that resemble petals, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Canna speciosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
