Cyperus confertus

Cyperus confertus, commonly known as the tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in color.

Cyperus confertus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
