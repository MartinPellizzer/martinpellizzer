Cardamomum masticatorum

Cardamomum masticatorum, commonly known as the wild cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which also includes ginger and turmeric. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers and has aromatic, thread-like rhizomes that are used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its medicinal and culinary properties.
