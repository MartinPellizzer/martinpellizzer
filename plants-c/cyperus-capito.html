Cyperus capito

Cyperus capito, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and distinctive umbrella-shaped inflorescence, which consists of numerous small, clustered spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along waterways. Cyperus capito has narrow, linear leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern at the base.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and water garden settings.
