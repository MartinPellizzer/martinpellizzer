Carex nagasakensis

Carex nagasakensis, commonly known as the Nagasaki sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Carex nagasakensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.
