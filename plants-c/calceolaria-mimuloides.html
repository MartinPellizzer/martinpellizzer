Calceolaria mimuloides

Calceolaria mimuloides, commonly known as the mimic orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like flowers that resemble small orchids. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in high-altitude regions. Its flowers are usually violet or purple and are known for their intricate structure and ornamental appeal.

Calceolaria mimuloides is often cultivated for its beauty and is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance.
