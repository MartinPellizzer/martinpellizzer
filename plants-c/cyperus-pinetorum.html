Cyperus pinetorum

Cyperus pinetorum, commonly known as the pinegrass sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and lakes. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in color. Cyperus pinetorum has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
