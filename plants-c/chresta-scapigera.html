Chresta scapigera

Chresta scapigera, commonly known as the scarlet flax or red flax, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces striking red or orange flowers with a distinctive, elongated shape. The plant features slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Chresta scapigera is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
