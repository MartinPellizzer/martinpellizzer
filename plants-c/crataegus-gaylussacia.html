Crataegus gaylussacia

Crataegus gaylussacia, commonly known as the Gaylussacia hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
