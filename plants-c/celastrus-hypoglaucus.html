Celastrus hypoglaucus

Celastrus hypoglaucus, commonly known as the smooth staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, elliptical leaves with smooth margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Celastraceae and is noted for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
