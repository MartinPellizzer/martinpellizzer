Calispepla aegacanthoides

Calispepla aegacanthoides, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found near the shoreline.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens along coastal areas.
