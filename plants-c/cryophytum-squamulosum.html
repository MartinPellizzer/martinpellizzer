Cryophytum squamulosum

Cryophytum squamulosum, commonly known as the snowflake plant, is a rare and delicate succulent native to the cold, high-altitude regions of South America.

It features a compact, rosette-shaped growth pattern with thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink during the short growing season. It thrives in cool, dry conditions with minimal water, making it a challenging but rewarding plant for experienced growers.

Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
