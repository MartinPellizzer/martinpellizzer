Clematis cylindrica var. hendersonii

Clematis cylindrica var. hendersonii, commonly known as the Henderson's clematis, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features slender, wiry stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
