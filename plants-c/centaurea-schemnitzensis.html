Centaurea schemnitzensis

Centaurea schemnitzensis, commonly known as the Schemnitz thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It has a compact growth habit with a rosette of basal leaves and a single upright flowering stem.

Centaurea schemnitzensis is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
