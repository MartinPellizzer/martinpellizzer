Capsicum brachypodum

Capsicum brachypodum, commonly known as the dwarf capsicum or small-fruited chili pepper, is a species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous plant with small, upright stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces small, round to oval-shaped fruits that range in color from green to red when mature. It thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often grown as an ornamental or for its spicy fruits.

Due to its compact size, it is suitable for container gardening and small spaces.
