Convolvulus parviflorus

Convolvulus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a trailing herb with slender, hairy stems that often grow along the ground. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, blooming in the morning. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Convolvulus parviflorus is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.
