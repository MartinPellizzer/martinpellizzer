Cornus hongkongensis subsp. elegans

Cornus hongkongensis subsp.

elegans, commonly known as the Hong Kong cornel. It is a flowering plant native to southeastern China, particularly Hong Kong.

The species is characterized by its small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

It prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.
