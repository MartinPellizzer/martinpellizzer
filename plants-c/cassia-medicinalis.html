Cassia medicinalis

Cassia medicinalis, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves and produces capsule fruits containing flat, winged seeds. Cassia medicinalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in various cultures.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
