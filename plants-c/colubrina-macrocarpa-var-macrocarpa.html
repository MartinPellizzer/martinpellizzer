Colubrina macrocarpa var. macrocarpa

Colubrina macrocarpa var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the Florida colubrina, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in clusters, often in shades of pink or white, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

This species is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
