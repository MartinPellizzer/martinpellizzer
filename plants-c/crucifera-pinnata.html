Crucifera pinnata

Crucifera pinnata, commonly known as the featherleaf crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the crucifer family. It is native to temperate regions and often found in moist, disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
