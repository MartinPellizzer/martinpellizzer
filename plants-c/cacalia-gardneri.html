Cacalia gardneri

Cacalia gardneri, commonly known as the California bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the aster family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cacalia gardneri is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
