Colax viridis var. trimaculatus

Colax viridis var. trimaculatus, commonly known as the green colax with three spots, is a variant of the colax plant characterized by its distinct leaf patterns.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green with three distinct light green or yellow spots along the midrib. The plant is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
