Ctenitis brevipetiolulata

Ctenitis brevipetiolulata, commonly known as the short-petioled tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its fronds are typically bright green and have a leathery texture, making it a striking addition to shaded gardens.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for healthy growth.
