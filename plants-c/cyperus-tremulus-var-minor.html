Cyperus tremulus var. minor

Cyperus tremulus var. minor, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in low-lying areas with poor drainage. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are often pale green or brown.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetlands and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
