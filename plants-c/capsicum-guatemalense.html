Capsicum guatemalense

Capsicum guatemalense, commonly known as the Guatemalan pepper, is a species native to Central America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. This plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and funnel-shaped, and it produces small, round fruits that range in color from green to red.

The plant is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a spice in local cuisines.
