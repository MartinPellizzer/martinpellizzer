Croton lobatus var. riparius

Croton lobatus var. riparius, commonly known as the river croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that vary in shape and color depending on the environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from yellow to red, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine in some regions.
