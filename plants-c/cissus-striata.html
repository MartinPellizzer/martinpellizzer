Cissus striata

Cissus striata, commonly known as the creeping jade or silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, waxy, heart-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically a bright green with a silvery underside, giving it a striking appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, berry-like fruits.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in a variety of indoor and outdoor settings.
