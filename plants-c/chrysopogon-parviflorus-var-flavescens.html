Chrysopogon parviflorus var. flavescens

Chrysopogon parviflorus var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered chrysopogon, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and is adapted to dry, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight bluish tint, and it is often found in disturbed habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
