Convolvulus bona-nox

Convolvulus bona-nox, commonly known as nightshade convolvulus, is a flowering plant belonging to the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in warm, sunny environments. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often hairy.

Convolvulus bona-nox is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is known for its ability to spread rapidly through its creeping vines.
