Cordia asterophora

Cordia asterophora, commonly known as the starflower or false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
