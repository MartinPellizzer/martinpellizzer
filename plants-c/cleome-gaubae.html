Cleome gaubae

Cleome gaubae, commonly known as spider flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, spider-like blooms that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and branching.

Cleome gaubae thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
