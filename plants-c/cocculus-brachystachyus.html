Cocculus brachystachyus

Cocculus brachystachyus, commonly known as the short-stalked devil's backbone, is a climbing plant native to parts of Africa and southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, twisting stems and palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by kidney-shaped, spiny fruits. Its seeds are enclosed in these fruits and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The plant's unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
