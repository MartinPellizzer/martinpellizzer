Costus oblitterans

Costus oblitterans, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a member of the Costaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, slender stems and small, white or pale pink flowers. Native to tropical regions of Central and South America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is notable for its unique, almost translucent leaves that give it its common name.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for shaded garden settings.
