Cerasus lusitanica

Cerasus lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian cherry or Spanish cherry, is a small deciduous tree native to southwestern Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The tree produces small, red or black fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It is often found in rocky or calcareous soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
