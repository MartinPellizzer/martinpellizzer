Cyathea scabriseta

Cyathea scabriseta, commonly known as the scaly tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of feathery, compound fronds. The fronds are typically dark green and have a distinctive scaly texture on their undersides, which gives the plant its common name. Cyathea scabriseta thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular specimen plant in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
