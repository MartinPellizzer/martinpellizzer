Crataegus × chersonensis

Crataegus × chersonensis, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a hybrid species within the genus Crataegus, belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, oval to ovate leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is widely cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
