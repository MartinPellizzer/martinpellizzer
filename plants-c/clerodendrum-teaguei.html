Clerodendrum teaguei

Clerodendrum teaguei, commonly known as the Florida beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of violet to purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly serrated edge and can grow up to 6 feet tall. It thrives in partial to full sun and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various bird species.
