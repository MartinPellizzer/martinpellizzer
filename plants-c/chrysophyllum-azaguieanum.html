Chrysophyllum azaguieanum

Chrysophyllum azaguieanum, commonly known as the Azaguiean starfruit, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The tree is also valued for its timber and medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
