Commelina obliqua f. mathewii

Commelina obliqua f. mathewii, commonly known as the Mathew's spiderwort, is a rare and unique variant of the spiderwort genus.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. Native to specific regions in India, this plant thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its botanical features include a short, rhizomatous stem and small, clustered inflorescences.

Due to its limited distribution and distinctive morphology, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
