Cassia uniflora var. parvifolia

Cassia uniflora var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
