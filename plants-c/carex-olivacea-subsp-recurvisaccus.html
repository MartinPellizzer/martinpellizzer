Carex olivacea subsp. recurvisaccus

Carex olivacea subsp.

recurvisaccus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a glossy appearance.

The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown to yellowish flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas, pond margins, and along streams.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments.
