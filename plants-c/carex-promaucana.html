Carex promaucana

Carex promaucana, commonly known as the purple-fringed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Carex promaucana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
