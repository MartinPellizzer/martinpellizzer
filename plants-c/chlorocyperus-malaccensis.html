Chlorocyperus malaccensis

Chlorocyperus malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca green sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its cylindrical, grass-like stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in moist soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
