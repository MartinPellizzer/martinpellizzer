Coleus laetus

Coleus laetus, commonly known as the spreading coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that often resemble flowers. The plant features opposite, ovate to lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Coleus laetus produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually clustered in spikes.
