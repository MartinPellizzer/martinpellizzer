Centaurea phyllocephala var. persica

Centaurea phyllocephala var. persica, commonly known as Persian crownflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright, sparsely branched stems that bear narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a silvery-white underside. The plant produces striking, bright red to orange flowers with a distinctive yellow center, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and unique foliage structure.
