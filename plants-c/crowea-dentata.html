Crowea dentata

Crowea dentata, commonly known as the Sydney flame or flame flower, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring broad, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces striking clusters of bright orange-red flowers during the spring and summer months. These flowers are borne on long, slender stalks and are composed of numerous small, tubular petals.

Crowea dentata is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy and ornamental plant in suitable climates.
