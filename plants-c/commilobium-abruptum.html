Commilobium abruptum

Commilobium abruptum, commonly known as the sharp-lobed wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and distinctive, spurred flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant prefers moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a short, upright stem.

It is part of the Oxalidaceae family, known for its calcium oxalate crystals in the leaves.
