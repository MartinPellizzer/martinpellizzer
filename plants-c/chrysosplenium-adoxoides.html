Chrysosplenium adoxoides

Chrysosplenium adoxoides, commonly known as the silver cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Saxifragaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, five-petaled white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are usually basal, arranged in a rosette, and have a silvery sheen due to dense pubescence.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
