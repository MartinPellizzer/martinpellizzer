Carduus filipendulus

Carduus filipendulus, commonly known as the lesser thistle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features spiny, silver-green leaves with a rough texture, and its flowers are typically purple or pink, arranged in a spherical cluster. The plant grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun.

It is often found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed areas, and is known for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
