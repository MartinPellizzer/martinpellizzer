Cracca rigidula

Cracca rigidula, commonly known as rigid dandelion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate leaves. The plant has hairy, ribbed stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow, forming a single head with many small florets, and it produces a distinctive, elongated seed head.

Cracca rigidula is typically found in meadows, roadsides, and disturbed soils, and it is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
