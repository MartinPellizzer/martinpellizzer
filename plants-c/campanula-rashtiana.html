Campanula rashtiana

Campanula rashtiana, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Campanula rashtiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
