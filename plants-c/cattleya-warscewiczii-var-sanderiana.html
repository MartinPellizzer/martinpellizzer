Cattleya warscewiczii var. sanderiana

Cattleya warscewiczii var. sanderiana, commonly known as the Sander's cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink, often with intricate patterns and a distinct lip. The plant has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that support its foliage and flowering stems. Its leaves are broad, fleshy, and arranged in two rows, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and fragrance.
