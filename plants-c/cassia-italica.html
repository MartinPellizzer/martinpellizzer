Cassia italica

Cassia italica, commonly known as the Italian cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain small, dark brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Cassia italica is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
