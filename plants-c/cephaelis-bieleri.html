Cephaelis bieleri

Cephaelis bieleri, commonly known as the Blue Poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
