Chenopodium hircinum subsp. euhircinum

Chenopodium hircinum subsp.

euhircinum, commonly known as the common lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically glaucous with a waxy coating.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This species is known for its rapid growth and is considered a weed in many regions.
