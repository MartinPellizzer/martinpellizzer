Cirsium smithianum

Cirsium smithianum, commonly known as Smith's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and grasslands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Its structure features a stout stem with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in stiff, bristly hairs.
