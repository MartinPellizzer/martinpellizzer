Capparis polyantha

Capparis polyantha, commonly known as the Chinese date or the Indian caper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant's leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. Its fruit is a fleshy drupe that contains a single seed, and it is often used in traditional medicine.

Capparis polyantha thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas.
