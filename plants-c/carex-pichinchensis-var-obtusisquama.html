Carex pichinchensis var. obtusisquama

Carex pichinchensis var. obtusisquama, commonly known as the obtuse-squama sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and specialized reproductive structures. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers, typical of sedges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or forested habitats.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
