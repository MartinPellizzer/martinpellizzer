Crataegus × pilosa

Crataegus × pilosa, commonly known as the nodding hawthorn, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of small, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for hedging and landscaping.
