Carex glauca f. bulbosa

Carex glauca f. bulbosa, commonly known as the blue sedge, is a variant of the common blue sedge, characterized by its striking blue-gray foliage.

It is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale brown flowers in the summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of wetland environments.
