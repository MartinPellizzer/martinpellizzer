Cieca nigra

Cieca nigra, commonly known as the black nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, and is characterized by its small, dark purple berries. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and alternate, ovate leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale purple, and they bloom in clusters.

The berries, which are toxic when ripe, contain alkaloids and are often used in traditional medicine.
