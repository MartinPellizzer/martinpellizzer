Carex pontica

Carex pontica, commonly known as the Pontic sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. The plant features slender, triangular stems that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, soft hairs. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, typically growing in dense clusters at the base of the plant.

Carex pontica produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are usually wind-pollinated.
