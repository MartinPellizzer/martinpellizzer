Cicercula

Cicercula, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, many of which are native to southern Africa. The plants typically have basal leaves that are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. They produce small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which are followed by fluffy, seed-bearing structures.

Cicercula species are often found in grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.
