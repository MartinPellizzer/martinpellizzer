Chironia ovata

Chironia ovata, commonly known as the oval leaf chironia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, ovate leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Chironia ovata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and greenhouses.
