Campylospermum angustifolium

Campylospermum angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf campylospermum, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features small, narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies of arid and semi-arid regions.
