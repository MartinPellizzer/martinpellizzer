Cyphia tricuspis

Cyphia tricuspis, commonly known as the three-lobed cyphia, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, which give it its common name. Its showy, tubular flowers are typically violet or purple, with a distinctive three-lobed corolla that attracts pollinators like hummingbirds.

Cyphia tricuspis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and tropical landscapes.
