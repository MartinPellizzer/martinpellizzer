Carapa guianensis

Carapa guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese copal tree, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly found in Guyana, Suriname, and French Guiana.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae, which includes mahogany and other valuable timber trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, oval fruits containing a single seed.

The tree is valued for its durable wood and has traditional uses in local communities.
