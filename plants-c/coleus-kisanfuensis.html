Coleus kisanfuensis

Coleus kisanfuensis, commonly known as the Kisanfu coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The plant typically grows as an upright herb with opposite leaves that vary in shape and color. It produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale pink to white in color.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
