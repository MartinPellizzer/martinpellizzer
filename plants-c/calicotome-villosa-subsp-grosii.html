Calicotome villosa subsp. grosii

Calicotome villosa subsp.

grosii, commonly known as the Portuguese broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, yellowish-green foliage and produces small, yellow flowers in clusters.

The plant has a woody stem covered in fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally used in landscaping for erosion control.
