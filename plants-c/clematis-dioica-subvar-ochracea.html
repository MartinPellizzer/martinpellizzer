Clematis dioica subvar. ochracea

Clematis dioica subvar. ochracea, commonly known as the yellow clematis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens.

This subvariety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, showy blooms.
