Cyperus absconditicoronatus

Cyperus absconditicoronatus, commonly known as the hidden crown sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, which often form dense clumps. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Cyperus absconditicoronatus is often found in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of water bodies.
