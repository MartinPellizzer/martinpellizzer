Corydalis curviflora subsp. altecristata

Corydalis curviflora subsp.

altecristata, commonly known as the tall-crowned corydalis, is a member of the Fumariaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions.

The plant features slender stems and clusters of small, spurred flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
