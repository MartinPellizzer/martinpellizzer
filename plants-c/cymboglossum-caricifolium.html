Cymboglossum caricifolium

Cymboglossum caricifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf cymboglossum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern China.

It features slender, upright stems that bear lance-shaped leaves with a slightly wavy margin. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple to pink in color, arranged in loose clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Cymboglossum caricifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a foliage plant.
