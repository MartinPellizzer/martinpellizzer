Calceolaria neeana

Calceolaria neeana, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive calyx-like hood that encloses the flower, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in cool and moist environments. Its flowers are usually violet or blue in color, with intricate petal structures that attract specific pollinators.

Calceolaria neeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
