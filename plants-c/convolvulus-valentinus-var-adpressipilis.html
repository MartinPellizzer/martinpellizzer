Convolvulus valentinus var. adpressipilis

Convolvulus valentinus var. adpressipilis, commonly known as the Valentine's convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a ground cover.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a versatile choice for coastal and Mediterranean climates.
