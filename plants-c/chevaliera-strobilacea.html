Chevaliera strobilacea

Chevaliera strobilacea, commonly known as the horned lily, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of bright yellow flowers that resemble small horns. The plant produces small, greenish-brown capsules as fruit, which contain numerous tiny seeds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Chevaliera strobilacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained, rocky soils.
