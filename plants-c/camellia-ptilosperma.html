Camellia ptilosperma

Camellia ptilosperma, commonly known as the hairy-seed camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, hairy seeds.

Camellia ptilosperma is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
