Caltha grosseserrata

Caltha grosseserrata, commonly known as the broad-toothed crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges, giving it its scientific name. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
