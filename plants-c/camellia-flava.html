Camellia flava

Camellia flava, commonly known as the yellow camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its bright blossoms. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Camellia flava is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
