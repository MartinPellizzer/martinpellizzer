Carex perdentata

Carex perdentata, commonly known as the toothed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers form spikelets that are arranged in a dense, cylindrical inflorescence.

Carex perdentata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
