Cymbopogon connatus

Cymbopogon connatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its essential oils, particularly citral, are used in various industries including perfumery and food flavoring.

Cymbopogon connatus thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in suitable climates.
