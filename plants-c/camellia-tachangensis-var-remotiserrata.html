Camellia tachangensis var. remotiserrata

Camellia tachangensis var. remotiserrata, commonly known as the remote serrated camellia, is a rare and ornamental evergreen shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves with finely serrated margins. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers that range in color from deep red to pink, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation in temperate climates.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often sought after by botanists and horticulturists alike.
