Cucurbita martinezii

Cucurbita martinezii, commonly known as the Chihuahuan gourd, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers, squash, and melons. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers, with the fruit typically being a rough, elongated gourd that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used by indigenous peoples for food and traditional crafts.
