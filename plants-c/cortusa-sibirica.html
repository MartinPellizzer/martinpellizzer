Cortusa sibirica

Cortusa sibirica, commonly known as Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cortusa sibirica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
