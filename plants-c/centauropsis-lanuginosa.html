Centauropsis lanuginosa

Centauropsis lanuginosa, commonly known as the woolly centauropsis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. The plant features soft, hairy leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, clustered flowers with showy petals. Its root system is fibrous, and it is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
