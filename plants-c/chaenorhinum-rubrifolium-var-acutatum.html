Chaenorhinum rubrifolium var. acutatum

Chaenorhinum rubrifolium var. acutatum, commonly known as the acute-leaved flaxflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a reddish hue, giving the plant its species name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
