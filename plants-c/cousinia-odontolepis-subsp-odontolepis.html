Cousinia odontolepis subsp. odontolepis

Cousinia odontolepis subsp.

odontolepis, commonly known as the toothed cousinia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish to reddish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual appeal.
