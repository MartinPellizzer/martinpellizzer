Cocculus cordifolius

Cocculus cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, blooming in the early morning. Its fruit is a distinctive, spiny capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Convolvulaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
