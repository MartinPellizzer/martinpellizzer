Cestrum oblongifolium

Cestrum oblongifolium, commonly known as the night-blooming jasmine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, opening at night and closing by morning. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and pleasant scent.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by moths and bats, making it an important species in nocturnal ecosystems.
