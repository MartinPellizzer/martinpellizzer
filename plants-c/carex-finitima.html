Carex finitima

Carex finitima, commonly known as the small-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This species typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue, while its spikelets are small and clustered.

Carex finitima plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
