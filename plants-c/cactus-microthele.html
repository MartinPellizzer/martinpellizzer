Cactus microthele

Cactus microthele, commonly known as the desert rose, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It features a short, thick stem with clusters of fleshy, spiny leaves that help store water. The plant produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and white, typically during the rainy season. Its adaptations include a shallow root system and a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Cactus microthele is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
