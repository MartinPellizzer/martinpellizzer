Cyperus auricomus var. minor

Cyperus auricomus var. minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swampy areas and along water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, often with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are typically inconspicuous.

Cyperus auricomus var. minor is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and improve water quality.
