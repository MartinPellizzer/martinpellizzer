Calophyllum verticillatum

Calophyllum verticillatum, commonly known as the mountain ebony, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood and distinctive, compound leaves with three to five leaflets. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

Calophyllum verticillatum is valued for its hard wood and has traditional uses in various cultures for medicinal and ceremonial purposes.
