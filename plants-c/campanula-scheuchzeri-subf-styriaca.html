Campanula scheuchzeri subf. styriaca

Campanula scheuchzeri subf.

styriaca, commonly known as the Styrian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Alps. It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from blue to violet in color, typically blooming in late summer.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and grows in rocky or gravelly habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.

This subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and adaptability to high-altitude environments.
