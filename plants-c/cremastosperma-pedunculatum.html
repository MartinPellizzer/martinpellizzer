Cremastosperma pedunculatum

Cremastosperma pedunculatum, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It is often found in forested areas and is valued for its hard, durable wood.

Due to overharvesting, it is considered an endangered species in many regions.
