Crocus annulatus var. estriatus

Crocus annulatus var. estriatus, commonly known as the striped crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate, early spring blooms with strikingly colored, often purple or violet, petals that have distinctive striped patterns. The plant produces small, rounded bulbs that are adapted to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge after the flowers.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early blooming display.
