Clematis liuzhouensis

Clematis liuzhouensis, commonly known as the Liuzhou clematis, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, typically white or pale yellow flowers. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
