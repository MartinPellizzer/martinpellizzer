Capparis racemifera

Capparis racemifera, commonly known as the clustered caper, is a flowering plant belonging to the Capparaceae family.

It is native to regions of Africa, the Middle East, and southern Europe, typically growing in rocky or coastal areas. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by edible, greenish fruits. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Capparis racemifera is notable for its ability to thrive in arid and saline environments.
