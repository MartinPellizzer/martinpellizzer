Cineraria pyroglossa

Cineraria pyroglossa, commonly known as the flameleaf cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that resemble flames, hence its common name. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice among gardeners seeking unique foliage.
