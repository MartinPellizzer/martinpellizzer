Calappa campestris

Calappa campestris, commonly known as the field poppy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers with a distinctive black spot at the base. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as fields and roadsides. It has a taproot system and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

Calappa campestris is an annual plant that blooms from late spring to early summer.
