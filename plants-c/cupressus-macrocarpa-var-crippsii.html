Cupressus macrocarpa var. crippsii

Cupressus macrocarpa var. crippsii, commonly known as the 'Crispii' or 'Crisp' Monterey cypress, is a cultivar of the Monterey cypress native to California.

It is characterized by its narrow, columnar growth habit and scale-like leaves that are a distinctive bluish-green color. The cones are small and woody, typically containing two to three seeds. This variety is often used in landscaping for its elegant appearance and tolerance to urban conditions.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental planting.
