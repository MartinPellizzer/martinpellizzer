Clinopodium menthifolium subsp. ascendens

Clinopodium menthifolium subsp.

ascendens, commonly known as mountain mint or wild mint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a strongly aromatic scent when crushed. Its flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in spikes, often blooming in late summer.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
