Caladium bicolor var. albomaculatum

Caladium bicolor var. albomaculatum, commonly known as the "White Spotted Dieback," is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are predominantly green with distinctive white or cream-colored spots. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant and contrasting leaf patterns.

It is typically grown as an annual in regions with colder winters.
