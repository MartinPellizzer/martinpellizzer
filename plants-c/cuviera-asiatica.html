Cuviera asiatica

Cuviera asiatica, commonly known as the Asian cuviera, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, showy bracts that resemble flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, often with a velvety texture.

Cuviera asiatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
