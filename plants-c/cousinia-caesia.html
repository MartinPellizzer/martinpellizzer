Cousinia caesia

Cousinia caesia, commonly known as the blue cousinia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, adding a striking visual element to its natural habitat.

Cousinia caesia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
