Clematis pensylvanica

Clematis pensylvanica, commonly known as the Pennsylvania clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and can grow up to 20 feet in length. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Clematis pensylvanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in USDA zones 4 through 8.
