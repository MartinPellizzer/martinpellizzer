Crepidium fimbriatum

Crepidium fimbriatum, commonly known as the fringed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features a slender, upright stem with a single, lance-shaped leaf. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, often with a faintly colored lip, and are arranged in a loose cluster.

Crepidium fimbriatum is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in association with other orchid species in its habitat.
