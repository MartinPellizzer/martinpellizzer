Cacalia baptizanda

Cacalia baptizanda, commonly known as the sacred bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often with a distinct central disc. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and cultural significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
