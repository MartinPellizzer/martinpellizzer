Cucurbita pepo var. coronalis

Cucurbita pepo var. coronalis, commonly known as the Crown of Thorns gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, and often lobed leaves that give it a rugged appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is typically elongated, with a rough, warty surface, and can vary in color from green to orange.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique foliage and hardy nature.
