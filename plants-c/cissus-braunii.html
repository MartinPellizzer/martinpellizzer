Cissus braunii

Cissus braunii, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a tropical climbing plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid environments and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for vertical gardening and green walls.
