Comarum palustre var. stipulaceum

Comarum palustre var. stipulaceum, commonly known as the slender water clover, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It features small, delicate, heart-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a floating or submerged manner. The plant produces tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from June to August. It thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems.

Its presence indicates good water quality and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
