Camellia crassiphylla

Camellia crassiphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved camellia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.

Camellia crassiphylla is also known for its ability to produce edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines.
