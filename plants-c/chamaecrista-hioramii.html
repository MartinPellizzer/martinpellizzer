Chamaecrista hioramii

Chamaecrista hioramii, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens in warm climates.

Chamaecrista hioramii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
