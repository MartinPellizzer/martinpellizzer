Carex morrowii var. yesinensis

Carex morrowii var. yesinensis, commonly known as the Yesinensis sedge, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
