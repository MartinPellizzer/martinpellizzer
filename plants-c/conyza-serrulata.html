Conyza serrulata

Conyza serrulata, commonly known as roughleaf fleabane, is an annual flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides. Its leaves are serrated and covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture.

Conyza serrulata produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
