Clusia alata

Clusia alata, commonly known as the Brazilian shield fern, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Clusia alata is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive, evergreen appearance.
