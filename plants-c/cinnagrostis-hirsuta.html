Cinnagrostis hirsuta

Cinnagrostis hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, sunny to partially shaded environments such as meadows and stream banks. The plant features a basal rosette of hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow flowers in early summer. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are arranged in a cluster at the end of each stem.

Cinnagrostis hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
