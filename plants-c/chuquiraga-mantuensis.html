Chuquiraga mantuensis

Chuquiraga mantuensis, commonly known as the Andean burweed, is a flowering plant native to the high Andes of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh, high-altitude environment.

Chuquiraga mantuensis is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in regions with cool, dry climates.
