Crepidomanes aphlebioides

Crepidomanes aphlebioides, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its delicate structure and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
