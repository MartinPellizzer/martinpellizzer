Cytisus heterophyllus

Cytisus heterophyllus, commonly known as the variable broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which vary in shape and size along the stem, giving it its species name "heterophyllus.". The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
