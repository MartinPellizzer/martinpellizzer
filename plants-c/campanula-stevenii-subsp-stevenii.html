Campanula stevenii subsp. stevenii

Campanula stevenii subsp.

stevenii, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and grasslands.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
