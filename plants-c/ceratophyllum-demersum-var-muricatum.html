Ceratophyllum demersum var. muricatum

Ceratophyllum demersum var. muricatum, commonly known as the spiked water chicken, is a submerged aquatic plant native to South America.

It features dense, branching stems with small, rounded leaves that are often covered in fine, spiny projections. The plant produces small, white flowers that are typically borne in clusters near the water's surface. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in freshwater ecosystems.
