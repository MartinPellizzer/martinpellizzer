Crataegus regalis var. paradoxa

Crataegus regalis var. paradoxa, commonly known as the Paradoxical Hawthorn, is a rare and unique variant of the hawthorn genus.

It is native to the southeastern United States, specifically found in Georgia and Florida. This plant is characterized by its distinctive white flowers and red, fuzzy fruits that appear in the fall. The leaves are typically ovate with serrated edges, and the plant often grows as a shrub or small tree.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to humid, subtropical climates.
