Campe vulgaris

Campe vulgaris, commonly known as common chickweed, is a low-growing annual herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces tiny white or pink flowers. The plant thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, fields, and along paths. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through seeds and runners.

Campe vulgaris is also valued for its culinary and medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
