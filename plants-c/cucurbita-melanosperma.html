Cucurbita melanosperma

Cucurbita melanosperma, commonly known as the black-seed pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its dark, wrinkled skin and small, black seeds. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers, typical of many squash species. Its fruits are often used in traditional cooking and have a slightly sweet, nutty flavor.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
