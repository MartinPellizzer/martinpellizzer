Cyathea irregularis

Cyathea irregularis, commonly known as the irregular tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in persistent, fibrous roots. The plant has large, pinnate fronds that can grow up to several meters in length, with a distinctive, rough texture on the underside. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Cyathea irregularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological structure of its native habitats.
