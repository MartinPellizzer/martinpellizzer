Cineraria cordata

Cineraria cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety blue or purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped, thick, and hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid native environment. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in various regions with a Mediterranean climate.
