Campanula mirabilis

Campanula mirabilis, commonly known as the wonder bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and eastern Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late summer. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves and upright stems that support its clusters of tubular flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula mirabilis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
