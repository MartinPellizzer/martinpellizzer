Carex simulans var. densiflora

Carex simulans var. densiflora, commonly known as dense-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its adaptability and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in garden settings.
