Cissus sagittifer

Cissus sagittifer, commonly known as the arrowleaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in small, white hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by edible, berry-like fruits. Its stems are succulent and can root at the nodes, allowing it to spread easily.

It is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
