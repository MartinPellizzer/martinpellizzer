Cousinia stereoneura

Cousinia stereoneura, commonly known as the mountain cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a compact cluster, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Cousinia stereoneura is adapted to harsh, arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an important species in alpine and steppe ecosystems.
