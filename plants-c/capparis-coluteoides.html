Capparis coluteoides

Capparis coluteoides, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has woody stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Capparis coluteoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
