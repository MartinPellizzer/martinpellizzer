Cerasus hippophaeoides

Cerasus hippophaeoides, commonly known as the Chinese cherry or Japanese cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and medicinal properties.
