Cucurbita pepo subsp. gumala

Cucurbita pepo subsp.

gumala, commonly known as the Turk's turban gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia. This species is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, turban-shaped fruit, which can vary in color from green to yellow or orange.

The plant produces large, lobed leaves and typically grows as a vine with tendrils that aid in climbing. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can also be used in some traditional culinary applications.
