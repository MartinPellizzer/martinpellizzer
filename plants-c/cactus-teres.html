Cactus teres

Cactus teres, commonly known as the spiny pear, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are covered in sharp, spiny tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by small, fleshy fruits that are edible. Its succulent structure allows it to store water efficiently, adapting to its harsh environment.

The plant's unique morphology makes it an interesting subject for both botanical study and horticultural cultivation.
