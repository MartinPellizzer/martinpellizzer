Cistus quinquevulnerus

Cistus quinquevulnerus, commonly known as the five-wound rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to rocky and dry soils, thriving in coastal and mountainous environments. Its leaves are covered in glandular hairs that secrete essential oils, contributing to its strong scent.

Cistus quinquevulnerus is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a source of honey.
