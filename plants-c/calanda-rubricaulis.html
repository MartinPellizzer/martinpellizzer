Calanda rubricaulis

Calanda rubricaulis, commonly known as the red-stemmed calanda, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, reddish stems and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, silvery down, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Calanda rubricaulis is valued for its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms, making it a popular choice among gardeners.
