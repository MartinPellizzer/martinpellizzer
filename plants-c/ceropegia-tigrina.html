Ceropegia tigrina

Ceropegia tigrina, commonly known as the tiger's claw, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features distinctive, fleshy, opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a waxy coating. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a unique, intricate structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
