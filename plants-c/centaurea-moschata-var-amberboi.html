Centaurea moschata var. amberboi

Centaurea moschata var. amberboi, commonly known as the Amberbloom Scotch thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking yellow-orange flower heads that are surrounded by purple to brown bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are often glandular and emit a mild, aromatic scent. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to full sun conditions.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
