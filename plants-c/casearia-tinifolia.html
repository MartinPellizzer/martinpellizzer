Casearia tinifolia

Casearia tinifolia, commonly known as the yellow casearia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year, followed by small, round fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
