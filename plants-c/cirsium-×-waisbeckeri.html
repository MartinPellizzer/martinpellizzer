Cirsium × waisbeckeri

Cirsium × waisbeckeri, commonly known as the Waisbecker's thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads that resemble those of other thistle species. Native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other riparian habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
