Calamagrostis halleriana var. langsdorffii

Calamagrostis halleriana var. langsdorffii, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-blue blades that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often pale green or brown in color.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
