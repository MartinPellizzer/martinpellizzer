Carthamus glaucus subsp. anatolicus

Carthamus glaucus subsp.

anatolicus, commonly known as Anatolian safflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and robust, branching stems.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of showy ray florets surrounded by tubular disc florets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used historically in traditional medicine and dye production.
