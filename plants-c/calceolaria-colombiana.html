Calceolaria colombiana

Calceolaria colombiana, commonly known as the Colombian slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of Colombia.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, slipper-shaped flowers that resemble tiny cups. The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and shrublands, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its flowers are usually violet or purple, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

Calceolaria colombiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
