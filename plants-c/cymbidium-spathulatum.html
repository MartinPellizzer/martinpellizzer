Cymbidium spathulatum

Cymbidium spathulatum, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a sympodial orchid with a pseudobulb structure that supports its growth and storage of nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, typically during the winter months. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Cymbidium spathulatum is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a cut flower.
