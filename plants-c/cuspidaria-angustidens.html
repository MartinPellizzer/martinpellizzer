Cuspidaria angustidens

Cuspidaria angustidens, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cuspidaria, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in swampy or marshy environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
