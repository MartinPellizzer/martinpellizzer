Crudia sparei

Crudia sparei, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and showy, pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
