Castanopsis carlesii var. sessilis

Castanopsis carlesii var. sessilis, commonly known as the Chinese tallow tree or *Sapium sebiferum*, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers followed by orange to red berry-like fruits that contain toxic seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to various parts of the world, though it can become invasive in some regions.
