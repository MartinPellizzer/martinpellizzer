Clinogyne leucantha

Clinogyne leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered clinogyne, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its attractive, showy white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It typically grows in well-drained soils in open woodland or grassland habitats.

Clinogyne leucantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
