Calceolaria pilosa

Calceolaria pilosa, commonly known as the hairy slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, slipper-shaped flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Calceolaria pilosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique and colorful blooms.
