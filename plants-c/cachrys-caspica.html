Cachrys caspica

Cachrys caspica, commonly known as the Caspian thyme, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Caspian Sea region.

It features small, aromatic leaves and produces pink to purple flowers in the summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. Its essential oils contain thymol, which gives it medicinal and culinary uses.

Cachrys caspica is valued for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
