Cerastium viscosum var. elongatum

Cerastium viscosum var. elongatum, commonly known as sticky chickweed or elongated mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has hairy, succulent leaves that are often sticky to the touch, a characteristic shared with its species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ground cover.
