Chytranthus carneus var. carneus

Chytranthus carneus var. carneus, commonly known as the red-flowered chytranthus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
