Caesalpinia lucida

Caesalpinia lucida, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange floral bracts that resemble flames, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Caesalpinia lucida is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
