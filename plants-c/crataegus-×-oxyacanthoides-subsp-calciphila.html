Crataegus × oxyacanthoides subsp. calciphila

Crataegus × oxyacanthoides subsp.

calciphila, commonly known as the limestone hawthorn, is a shrub native to calcareous soils in Europe. It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in alkaline conditions and is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
