Crassula arborea

Crassula arborea, commonly known as the tree crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or red during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Crassula arborea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
