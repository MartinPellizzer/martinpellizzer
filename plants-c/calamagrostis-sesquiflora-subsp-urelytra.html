Calamagrostis sesquiflora subsp. urelytra

Calamagrostis sesquiflora subsp.

urelytra, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluejoint, is a perennial grass native to North America. It typically grows in wet to moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of compact, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
