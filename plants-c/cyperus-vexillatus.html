Cyperus vexillatus

Cyperus vexillatus, commonly known as the flag iris or slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along water bodies. Its grass-like leaves are usually narrow and arranged in a triangular cross-section, a characteristic feature of many sedges.

Cyperus vexillatus produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, often found in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.
