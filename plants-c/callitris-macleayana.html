Callitris macleayana

Callitris macleayana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf mountain pine, is a species of conifer native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and needle-like leaves that are arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, reddish-brown cones that contain seeds, and it is typically found in dry, sandy soils. It has a slow growth rate and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

This species is also valued for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
