Cleome perrieri

Cleome perrieri, commonly known as the red cleome, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems, and they attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cleome perrieri is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
