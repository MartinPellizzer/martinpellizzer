Condalia buxifolia

Condalia buxifolia, commonly known as the African snowbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are arranged in a compact, bushy form. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Condalia buxifolia is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and salty air, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.
