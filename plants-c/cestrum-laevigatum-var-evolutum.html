Cestrum laevigatum var. evolutum

Cestrum laevigatum var. evolutum, commonly known as the white cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a slightly hairy underside. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrant blooms.
