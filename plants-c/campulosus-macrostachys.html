Campulosus macrostachys

Campulosus macrostachys, commonly known as the tall milkweed, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It features a robust, upright stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with five petals, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its milky sap is a characteristic feature, often used in traditional medicine.

Campulosus macrostachys is adapted to survive in harsh environments, making it an important species in its native ecosystems.
