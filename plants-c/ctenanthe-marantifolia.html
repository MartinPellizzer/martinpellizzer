Ctenanthe marantifolia

Ctenanthe marantifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf maranta, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, silver, and pink. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family, known for its ornamental foliage rather than its flowers. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires indirect light to maintain its vibrant appearance.

Ctenanthe marantifolia is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
