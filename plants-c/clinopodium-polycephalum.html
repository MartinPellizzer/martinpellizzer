Clinopodium polycephalum

Clinopodium polycephalum, commonly known as narrowleaf mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the central and eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils in open woods or along stream banks. Its small, white to pale blue flowers are arranged in dense, terminal clusters and bloom in late summer.

The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a minty fragrance when crushed.
