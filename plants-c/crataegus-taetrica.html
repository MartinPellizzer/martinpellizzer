Crataegus taetrica

Crataegus taetrica, commonly known as the Carpathian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest edges and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
