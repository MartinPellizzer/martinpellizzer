Crossandra infundibuliformis

Crossandra infundibuliformis, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Crossandra infundibuliformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
