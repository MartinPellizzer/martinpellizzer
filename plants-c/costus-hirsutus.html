Costus hirsutus

Costus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy costus, is a tropical perennial plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its aromatic rhizomes. The plant features tall, narrow leaves and produces showy, spadix-like inflorescences that are often yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Costus hirsutus is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
