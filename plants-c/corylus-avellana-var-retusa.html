Corylus avellana var. retusa

Corylus avellana var. retusa, commonly known as the horned hazel, is a variant of the hazelnut tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, horn-like projections on the nuts, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, deciduous leaves that are alternate and ovate in shape. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers a temperate climate.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible nuts.
