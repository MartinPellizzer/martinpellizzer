Cassia mimosoides var. wallichiana

Cassia mimosoides var. wallichiana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers, which bloom throughout the year in some regions. The leaves are bipinnate, consisting of numerous small, oval leaflets arranged in feathery patterns. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
