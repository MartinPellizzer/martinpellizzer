Cyperus tomaiophyllus var. crassistolon

Cyperus tomaiophyllus var. crassistolon, commonly known as the broad-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences. It thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along waterways.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
