Cornus controversa f. variegata

Cornus controversa f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated dogwood, is a unique cultivar of the Cornus controversa species.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, which features white and green patterns on its leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of about 3 to 6 feet. It produces small white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall.

This cultivar thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
