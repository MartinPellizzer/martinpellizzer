Cleretum patersonjonesii

Cleretum patersonjonesii, commonly known as the Paterson's jonesii clethra, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Clethraceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It has simple, opposite leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
