Clinopodium bolivianum subsp. bolivianum

Clinopodium bolivianum subsp.

bolivianum, commonly known as Bolivian mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the Andean regions of South America. It typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky slopes, thriving in cool, temperate climates.

The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a hairy surface and a strong mint-like aroma. Its flowers are small, clustered in whorls, and are usually pink to purple in color. Clinopodium bolivianum subsp.

bolivianum is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses.
