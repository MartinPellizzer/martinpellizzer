Chamaecrista desvauxii var. triumviralis

Chamaecrista desvauxii var. triumviralis, commonly known as the three-toothed cassia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring compound leaves with three leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers in dense, racemose inflorescences, which bloom during the dry season. Its small, pod-like fruits contain several seeds, and the plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

It is often found in rocky soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
