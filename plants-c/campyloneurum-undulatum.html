Campyloneurum undulatum

Campyloneurum undulatum, commonly known as the undulating fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often curved and undulating in appearance. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually light green and have a leathery texture.

It plays a role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to nutrient cycling.
