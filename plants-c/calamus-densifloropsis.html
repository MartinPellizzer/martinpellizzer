Calamus densifloropsis

Calamus densifloropsis, commonly known as the dense-flowered calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and broad, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white spadices, which are typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and wetland restoration projects.
