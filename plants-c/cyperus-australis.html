Cyperus australis

Cyperus australis, commonly known as the Australian flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and wetlands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences.

Cyperus australis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
