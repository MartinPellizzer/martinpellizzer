Chonemorpha grandieriana

Chonemorpha grandieriana, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family native to the dry forests of Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair, which helps to reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a vibrant touch to its otherwise muted appearance. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture efficiently, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
