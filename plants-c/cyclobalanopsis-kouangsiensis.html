Cyclobalanopsis kouangsiensis

Cyclobalanopsis kouangsiensis, commonly known as the Khamti oak, is a species of evergreen tree belonging to the Fagaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Laos, Vietnam, and Cambodia. The plant is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and produces small, chestnut-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in mixed forests.

Due to its ecological importance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable but threatened species in its native habitat.
