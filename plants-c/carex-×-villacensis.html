Carex × villacensis

Carex × villacensis, commonly known as the hybrid sedges, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Carex sylvatica and Carex riparia, characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-sided flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex × villacensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
