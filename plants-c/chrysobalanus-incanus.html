Chrysobalanus incanus

Chrysobalanus incanus, commonly known as the white spondias, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into fleshy, red berries. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
