Cestrum fragile var. grandiflorum

Cestrum fragile var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the fragrant cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains several seeds.
