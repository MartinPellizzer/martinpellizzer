Campomanesia ilhoensis

Campomanesia ilhoensis, commonly known as the Ilhoense Myrtle, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like eucalyptus and clove. The plant is characterized by its fragrant, white to pink flowers and small, edible berries that are often used in local cuisine. Its leaves are glossy and have a distinctive aromatic scent, while its bark contains compounds with potential medicinal properties.

Campomanesia ilhoensis plays a vital role in the biodiversity of its native habitat and is a subject of interest for botanical research.
