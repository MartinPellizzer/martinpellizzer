Carex deweyana var. leptopoda

Carex deweyana var. leptopoda, commonly known as the slender flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters. It is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic insects.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
