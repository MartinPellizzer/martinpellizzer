Calamintha × subnuda

Calamintha × subnuda, commonly known as narrowleaf germander, is a hybrid perennial plant belonging to the Lamiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. The plant is often used in herbal medicine for its purported soothing properties.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
