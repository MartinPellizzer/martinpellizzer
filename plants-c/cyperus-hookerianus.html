Cyperus hookerianus

Cyperus hookerianus, commonly known as the swamp cyperus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually pale green to brown in color. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in saturated soils and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Cyperus hookerianus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and environmental benefits.
