Calamagrostis effusiflora

Calamagrostis effusiflora, commonly known as the silky wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Asia and North America.

It features slender, arching stems that produce panicles of soft, silky florets, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Calamagrostis effusiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
