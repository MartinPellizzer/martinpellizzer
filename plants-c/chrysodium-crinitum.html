Chrysodium crinitum

Chrysodium crinitum, commonly known as the curly hair lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its delicate, curling thallus that resembles hair, giving it its common name. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae, which provide nutrients through photosynthesis. It typically grows on tree trunks, rocks, or soil in temperate regions.

Due to its slow growth and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered an indicator species for ecological health.
