Campanula bertolae f. rosinae

Campanula bertolae f. rosinae, commonly known as the Rosina bellflower, is a rare and striking plant native to the Alps.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine meadows, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
