Caesalpinia punctata

Caesalpinia punctata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange feathery flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces long, flat pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological and horticultural purposes.
