Cryptanthus minarum

Cryptanthus minarum, commonly known as the miniature bell flower, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.

Cryptanthus minarum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soil with moderate watering.
