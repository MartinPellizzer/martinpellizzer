Cyperus eragrostis var. pauperita

Cyperus eragrostis var. pauperita, commonly known as slender flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and pond edges. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a slightly fibrous texture.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are usually pale green to brown, contributing to its ecological role in wetland habitats.
