Cordyline kaspar

Cordyline kaspar, commonly known as the red-veined cordyline, is a striking tropical plant native to New Zealand.

It features tall, upright stems with broad, lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive red-veined pattern. The leaves are typically a deep green with prominent red or purple veins, giving it a dramatic appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its vibrant coloration.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
