Clerodendrum villosum var. macrocalyx

Clerodendrum villosum var. macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and lavender. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and prominent veins. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The flowers are typically clustered in terminal panicles, adding to its visual appeal.
