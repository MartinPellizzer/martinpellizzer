Carex holostoma

Carex holostoma, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and clustered spikelets that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. Native to wetland and marshy habitats, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions. The plant plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its ecological importance makes it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
