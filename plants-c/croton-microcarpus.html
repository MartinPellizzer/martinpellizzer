Croton microcarpus

Croton microcarpus, commonly known as the dwarf croton, is a small shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features distinctive, palmate leaves with bright yellow and green veins, which contribute to its ornamental value. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
