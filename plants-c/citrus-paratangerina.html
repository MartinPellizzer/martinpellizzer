Citrus paratangerina

Citrus paratangerina, commonly known as the Mexican lime or kaffir lime, is a small evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It produces small, round, green fruits with a thick, aromatic rind and a juicy, acidic pulp. The plant thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its leaves are dark green, fragrant, and often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

The fruit is valued for its high vitamin C content and distinctive flavor.
