Coleus punctatus

Coleus punctatus, commonly known as the spotted coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that display a variety of shades of red, purple, and green. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptic in shape and often have distinctive spots or streaks. It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

This plant prefers warm, humid conditions and well-draining soil to thrive.
