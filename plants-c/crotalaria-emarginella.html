Crotalaria emarginella

Crotalaria emarginella, commonly known as the narrow-leaf false pigeon pea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with a distinctive shape. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces small, bean-like pods that contain several seeds.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and is also used in some traditional medicinal practices.
