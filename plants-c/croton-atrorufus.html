Croton atrorufus

Croton atrorufus, commonly known as the red-leaved croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red leaves that contrast with its green stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
