Crataegus aminii

Crataegus aminii, commonly known as the Afghan hawthorn, is a species of hawthorn native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant is characterized by its thorny branches.

Crataegus aminii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
