Cinchona pubescens var. pelletieriana

Cinchona pubescens var. pelletieriana, commonly known as the Peruvian bark or quina, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its evergreen, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers and red, fleshy berries that contain the alkaloid quinine, historically used in the treatment of malaria. Its leaves are often covered with fine, soft hairs, contributing to its common name "pubescens.".

This variety is particularly valued for its medicinal properties and historical significance in pharmaceutical history.
