Centaurea napulifera subsp. velenovskyi

Centaurea napulifera subsp.

velenovskyi, commonly known as the Velenovsky's Centaury, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spiny bracts around its flower heads.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
