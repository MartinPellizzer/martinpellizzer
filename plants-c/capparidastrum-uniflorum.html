Capparidastrum uniflorum

Capparidastrum uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flowered caper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family, which includes various species with distinctive yellow or white flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with simple, alternate leaves. Its flowers are small and clustered, often borne on short pedicels, and are followed by small, capsule-like fruits.

The plant is adapted to dry conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
