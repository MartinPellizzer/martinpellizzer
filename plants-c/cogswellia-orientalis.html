Cogswellia orientalis

Cogswellia orientalis, commonly known as the oriental cogswellia, is a rare and unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rounded shape and thick, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the cooler months, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. Its slow growth rate makes it a prized specimen among succulent enthusiasts.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
