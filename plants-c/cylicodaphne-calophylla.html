Cylicodaphne calophylla

Cylicodaphne calophylla, commonly known as the golden sago palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark red fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
