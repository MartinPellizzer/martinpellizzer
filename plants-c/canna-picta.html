Canna picta

Canna picta, commonly known as the painted canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features large, lance-shaped leaves that are often a vibrant green with a reddish or purple tint along the veins. The plant produces showy, spadix-like flowers surrounded by colorful bracts that can range from red to orange or yellow. Canna picta thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wetland landscapes.
