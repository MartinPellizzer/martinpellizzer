Chenopodium benthamii

Chenopodium benthamii, commonly known as desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are adapted to store water. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs. Chenopodium benthamii is valued for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients and used in traditional diets.

It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is well-suited to harsh, dry environments.
