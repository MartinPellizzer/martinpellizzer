Calea acoma

Calea acoma, commonly known as the sacred candle plant, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its striking, candle-like inflorescences that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features lance-shaped, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.

Calea acoma is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used by indigenous communities for its purported health benefits.
