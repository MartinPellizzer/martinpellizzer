Cheirostylis calcarata

Cheirostylis calcarata, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, showcasing its adaptability to nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are usually fleshy and succulent, helping it retain moisture in arid conditions.

The species is noted for its unique floral structure, which plays a role in its pollination strategy.
