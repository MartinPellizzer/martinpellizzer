Chrysophyllum sebertii

Chrysophyllum sebertii, commonly known as the African cherry or West African cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and economic importance.

Chrysophyllum sebertii is also noted for its ability to withstand drought conditions once established.
