Convolvulus sphaerostigma var. depauperatus

Convolvulus sphaerostigma var. depauperatus, commonly known as the sparse-flowered convolvulus, is a flowering plant belonging to the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it a notable species for ecological studies and garden cultivation.
