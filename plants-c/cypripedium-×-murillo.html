Cypripedium × murillo

Cypripedium × murillo, commonly known as the hybrid lady's slipper orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cypripedium calceolus and Cypripedium pubescens.

It is characterized by its large, showy, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. Its orchidaceous flowers are adorned with intricate patterns and are often fragrant.

Cypripedium × murillo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
