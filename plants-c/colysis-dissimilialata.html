Colysis dissimilialata

Colysis dissimilialata, commonly known as the silverleaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are often clustered together, followed by round, fuzzy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive if not managed properly.
