Carex orthostemon

Carex orthostemon, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spikes of small, usually greenish or brownish flowers. Native to North America, it thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize wetland areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
