Crepis globifera

Crepis globifera, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spherical, blue or purple flower heads that resemble small globes, surrounded by spiny leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower structure. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Crepis globifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be found in gardens and naturalized areas.
