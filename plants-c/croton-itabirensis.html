Croton itabirensis

Croton itabirensis, commonly known as the Itabira croton, is a species of flowering plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that range from green to red and yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in cyathia, which are cup-like structures typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine, though the plant contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
