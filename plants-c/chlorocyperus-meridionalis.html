Chlorocyperus meridionalis

Chlorocyperus meridionalis, commonly known as the southern bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, blue-green colored leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas and along riverbanks.

Chlorocyperus meridionalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in natural landscapes.
