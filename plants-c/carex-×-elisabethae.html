Carex × elisabethae

Carex × elisabethae, commonly known as the Elizabeth's sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

It is valued in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
