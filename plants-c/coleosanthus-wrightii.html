Coleosanthus wrightii

Coleosanthus wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's coleus, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
