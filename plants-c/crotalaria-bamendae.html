Crotalaria bamendae

Crotalaria bamendae, commonly known as the Cameroon crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces distinctive yellow or orange flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in a pod and are often used in traditional medicine.

Crotalaria bamendae is also noted for its ecological role in soil enrichment and as a potential source of bioactive compounds.
