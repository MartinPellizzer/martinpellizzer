Collinsia parviflora f. rosea

Collinsia parviflora f. rosea, commonly known as the roseleaf fairybell, is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Plantaginaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep rose. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or open areas, preferring partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a soft, fuzzy texture.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in garden settings for its vibrant, showy blooms.
