Casearia banquitana

Casearia banquitana, commonly known as the black mangrove, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are clustered in catkin-like inflorescences, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in saline and brackish water environments, often found in mangrove ecosystems.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
