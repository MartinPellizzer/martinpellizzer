Cornus kousa subsp. kousa

Cornus kousa subsp.

kousa, commonly known as the Kousa dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia. It features showy, white bracts that surround small yellowish flowers, which bloom in early spring.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that turn vibrant red or orange in the fall. It produces red or purple berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. Cornus kousa subsp.

kousa is valued for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
