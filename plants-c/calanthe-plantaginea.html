Calanthe plantaginea

Calanthe plantaginea, commonly known as the pink calanthe, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that are clustered in a raceme, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often arranged in a rosette pattern around the base.

Calanthe plantaginea thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
