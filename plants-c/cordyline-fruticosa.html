Cordyline fruticosa

Cordyline fruticosa, commonly known as the cabbage tree, is a striking evergreen plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Pacific.

It features a tall, unbranched trunk with a swollen base, often covered in a fibrous, spongy material. Its leaves are large, lance-shaped, and typically have a waxy texture with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by bright red berries.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
