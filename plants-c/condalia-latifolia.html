Condalia latifolia

Condalia latifolia, commonly known as the African glorybower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often appearing in clusters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with prominent veins.

Condalia latifolia is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in dry climates.
