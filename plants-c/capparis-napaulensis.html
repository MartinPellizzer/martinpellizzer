Capparis napaulensis

Capparis napaulensis, commonly known as the Himalayan caper, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has elliptical leaves with a rough texture and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it is adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Capparis napaulensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
