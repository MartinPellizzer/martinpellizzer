Cayratia timoriensis var. mekongensis

Cayratia timoriensis var. mekongensis, commonly known as the Mekong Cayratia, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often trifoliate or pinnate, and its small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments, often in forest understories or along riverbanks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.

The species is also noted for its potential in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
