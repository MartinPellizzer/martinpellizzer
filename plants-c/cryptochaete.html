Cryptochaete

Cryptochaete, commonly known as the "false dandelion," is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their basal rosettes of leaves and inconspicuous, small flowers that often go unnoticed. The genus includes several species, many of which are native to Africa, with a few found in the Americas. Cryptochaete species typically thrive in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions.

Their ecological role includes providing habitat and nectar for pollinators, despite their modest appearance.
