Chamaelinum sativum

Chamaelinum sativum, commonly known as the garden chamaelion, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, colorful flowers that range from blue to purple. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color.

Chamaelinum sativum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively low maintenance once established.
