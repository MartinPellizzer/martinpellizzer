Campanula cilicia

Campanula cilicia, commonly known as the Cilician bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are usually upright and branched.

Campanula cilicia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained, dry conditions.
