Camellia gilbertii

Camellia gilbertii, commonly known as the Gilbert's camellia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its flowers are usually pink or red, with a waxy texture and a strong, spicy fragrance.

Camellia gilbertii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
