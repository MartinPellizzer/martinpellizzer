Crepinella brachypoda

Crepinella brachypoda, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandmat, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant features small, narrow leaves and produces clusters of pale yellow to greenish flowers. It is often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Crepinella brachypoda plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small insects and animals.
