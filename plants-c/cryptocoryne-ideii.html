Cryptocoryne ideii

Cryptocoryne ideii, commonly known as the Java crypt, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length, often displaying a striking green to bluish-green coloration. The plant thrives in soft, acidic water with moderate to high light conditions. It is often used in aquariums for its aesthetic appeal and ability to provide habitat for small aquatic life.

Cryptocoryne ideii is also valued for its hardy nature and adaptability to various water conditions.
