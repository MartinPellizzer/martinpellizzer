Capsella rubella var. sabulosa

Capsella rubella var. sabulosa, commonly known as sand catchpole, is a flowering plant native to sandy soils in the United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and distinctive seed pods. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and dunes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in early spring.

The species is often found in coastal regions and is adapted to tolerate saline and well-drained soils.
