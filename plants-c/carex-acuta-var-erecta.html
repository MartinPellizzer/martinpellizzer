Carex acuta var. erecta

Carex acuta var. erecta, commonly known as the upright spike sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage and produces dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. It has narrow, linear leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly rough texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
