Cinnamomum malabatrum

Cinnamomum malabatrum, commonly known as the Malabar cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces berries that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its bark and leaves are also valued for their essential oils, which have antimicrobial properties.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and economic importance in local markets.
