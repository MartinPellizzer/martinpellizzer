Crataegus seclusa

Crataegus seclusa, commonly known as the hidden hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces red or orange berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Crataegus seclusa is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional medicine.
