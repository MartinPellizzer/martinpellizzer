Casearia samoensis

Casearia samoensis, commonly known as the Samoan beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the islands of Samoa and nearby regions in the South Pacific.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub that typically grows in coastal and lowland areas. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom throughout the year, followed by bright blue, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly serrated edge, while its bark is smooth and pale.

Casearia samoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and fruit.
