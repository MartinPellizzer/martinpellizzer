Cymbidium canaliculatum f. purpurascens

Cymbidium canaliculatum f. purpurascens, commonly known as the Purple-veined Cymbidium, is a hybrid orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that bear clusters of elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with distinctive purple veins. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil with moderate humidity.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
