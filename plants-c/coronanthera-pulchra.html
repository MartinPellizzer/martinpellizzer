Coronanthera pulchra

Coronanthera pulchra, commonly known as the beauty bell orchid, is a species of orchid native to Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

The orchid is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
