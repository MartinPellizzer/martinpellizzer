Crataegus albicans var. tatnalliana

Crataegus albicans var. tatnalliana, commonly known as the Tatnall hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins, and it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
