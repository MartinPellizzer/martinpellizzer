Catasetum × obrienianum

Catasetum × obrienianum, commonly known as the O'Brien's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers with a unique structure that includes a specialized lip used for pollination. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees and deriving moisture and nutrients from the air. Its flowers are known for their strong fragrance, which attracts specific pollinators such as bees and wasps.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and adaptability to various growing conditions.
