Crocus boryi subsp. tournefortii

Crocus boryi subsp.

tournefortii, commonly known as the Algerian crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils. It is often found in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its early spring blooms.
