Campanula odontosepala

Campanula odontosepala, commonly known as the toothed bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, often with a white or yellow throat. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula odontosepala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
