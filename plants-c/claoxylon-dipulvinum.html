Claoxylon dipulvinum

Claoxylon dipulvinum, commonly known as the African blackwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its dark, dense wood and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by legume-like pods containing flattened seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its valuable timber, it is also considered an important species for ecological and economic purposes.
