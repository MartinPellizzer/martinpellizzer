Cytisus heuffelii

Cytisus heuffelii, commonly known as the Heuffel's broom, is a flowering shrub native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in early summer, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a hedge.

Cytisus heuffelii is known for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought conditions.
