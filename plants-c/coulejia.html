Coulejia

Coulejia, commonly known as the rock cress, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Brassicaceae.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plants are characterized by their small, elongated leaves and clusters of tiny, four-petaled flowers. They often have a strong, peppery scent, similar to that of mustard plants.

Coulejia species are generally perennial and can be found in various regions across Europe, Asia, and North America.
