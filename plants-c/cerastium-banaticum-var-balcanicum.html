Cerastium banaticum var. balcanicum

Cerastium banaticum var. balcanicum, commonly known as the Balkan mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered with fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers with five petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
