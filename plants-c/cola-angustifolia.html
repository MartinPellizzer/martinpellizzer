Cola angustifolia

Cola angustifolia, commonly known as African cola, is a species of flowering plant in the family Malvaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces oblong, woody pods that contain seeds surrounded by a thick, aromatic pulp. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and swamps.

Cola angustifolia is valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a stimulant.
