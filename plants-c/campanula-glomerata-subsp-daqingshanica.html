Campanula glomerata subsp. daqingshanica

Campanula glomerata subsp.

daqingshanica, commonly known as the Daqingshan bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of northern China. It is characterized by its dense clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
