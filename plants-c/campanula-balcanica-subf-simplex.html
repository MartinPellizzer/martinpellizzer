Campanula balcanica subf. simplex

Campanula balcanica subf.

simplex, commonly known as the Balkan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
