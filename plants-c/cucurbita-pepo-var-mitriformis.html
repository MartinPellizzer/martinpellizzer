Cucurbita pepo var. mitriformis

Cucurbita pepo var. mitriformis, commonly known as the Turk's Turban gourd, is a unique variant of the common gourd species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bulbous shape with a wide, flattened base and a narrow, elongated top. The plant produces large, pale green to yellowish fruits that mature to a deep orange or red color. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes or as a edible vegetable.

The leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are typically yellow with a prominent central stigma.
