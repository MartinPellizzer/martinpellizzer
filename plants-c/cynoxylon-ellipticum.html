Cynoxylon ellipticum

Cynoxylon ellipticum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ironwood, is a rare and hardy shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and dense, woody structure, which provide it with exceptional durability. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color, followed by hard, woody fruits. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and poor soil conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
