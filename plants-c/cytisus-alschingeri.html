Cytisus alschingeri

Cytisus alschingeri, commonly known as the Alsinger's broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has slender, woody stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Cytisus alschingeri is known for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate coastal conditions.
