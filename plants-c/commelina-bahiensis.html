Commelina bahiensis

Commelina bahiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian dayflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its roots are fibrous and adaptive to waterlogged soils.

Commelina bahiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
