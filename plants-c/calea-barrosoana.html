Calea barrosoana

Calea barrosoana, commonly known as the "Cape Cod flower," is a striking tropical plant native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pinkish flowers that resemble those of the orchid family. The plant features a short, thick stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Calea barrosoana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique floral appearance.
