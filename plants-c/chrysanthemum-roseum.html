Chrysanthemum roseum

Chrysanthemum roseum, commonly known as the roseum chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and red. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in the fall and are often used in ornamental gardening.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
