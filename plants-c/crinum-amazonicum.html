Crinum amazonicum

Crinum amazonicum, commonly known as the Amazonian lily, is a striking herbaceous plant native to South America.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter, typically in shades of white, yellow, or pink. The plant has strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Crinum amazonicum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its dramatic floral displays.
