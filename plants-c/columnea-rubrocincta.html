Columnea rubrocincta

Columnea rubrocincta, commonly known as the red-belted flame flower, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround small, tubular flowers, creating a dramatic visual display. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green.

Columnea rubrocincta thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
