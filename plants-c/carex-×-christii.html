Carex × christii

Carex × christii, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves. The plant produces spikes of small, usually green or brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex × christii is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
