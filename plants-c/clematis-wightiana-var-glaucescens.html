Clematis wightiana var. glaucescens

Clematis wightiana var. glaucescens, commonly known as the Wight's Clematis, is a climbing vine native to the tropical regions of South America.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a distinctive violet or purple hue at the base. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves that are typically glaucous, giving them a dusty blue or grayish appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and support for its vigorous growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
