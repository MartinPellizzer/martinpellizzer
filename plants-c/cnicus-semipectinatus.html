Cnicus semipectinatus

Cnicus semipectinatus, commonly known as the lesser sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are often lobed and may have a bluish or purplish tint, while the flowers are small and yellow.

It is generally considered a weed but has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
