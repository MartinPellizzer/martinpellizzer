Crassula albicaulis

Crassula albicaulis, commonly known as the White-stemmed Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
