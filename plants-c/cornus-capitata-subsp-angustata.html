Cornus capitata subsp. angustata

Cornus capitata subsp.

angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf cornel. It is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

The plant is characterized by its slender, angular leaves and clusters of small, white or pinkish flowers. It produces red or orange berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor.

This subspecies is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.
