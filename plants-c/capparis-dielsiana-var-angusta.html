Capparis dielsiana var. angusta

Capparis dielsiana var. angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Capparidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, angular stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, round, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and unique foliage.
