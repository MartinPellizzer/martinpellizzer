Carex vixdentata

Carex vixdentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America, typically found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and wet meadows. The plant has grass-like leaves that are narrow and often have a slightly rough texture. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, three-ranked structures.

Carex vixdentata plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
