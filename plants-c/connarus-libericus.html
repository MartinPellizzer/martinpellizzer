Connarus libericus

Connarus libericus, commonly known as the European connarus, is a rare and ancient plant species native to parts of Europe.

It is a small shrub characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red or black in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Due to habitat loss, Connarus libericus is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
