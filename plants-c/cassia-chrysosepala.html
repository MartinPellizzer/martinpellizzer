Cassia chrysosepala

Cassia chrysosepala, commonly known as golden-sepaled cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow sepals and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Cassia chrysosepala is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
