Camissonia parryi

Camissonia parryi, commonly known as Parry's camissonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. This species is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers and slender, hairy stems. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Camissonia parryi is often found in open areas such as grasslands and slopes, where it thrives in full sun.
