Calendula maroccana subsp. murbeckii

Calendula maroccana subsp.

murbeckii, commonly known as Moroccan marigold, is a flowering plant native to North Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are hairy and gray-green, while the flowers are often borne in clusters.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
