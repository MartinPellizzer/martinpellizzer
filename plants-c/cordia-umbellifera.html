Cordia umbellifera

Cordia umbellifera, commonly known as the Mexican allspice tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually dark green on top and pale underneath. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers arranged in umbels, which are followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. These berries are edible and have a spicy, aromatic flavor, often used in culinary applications.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
