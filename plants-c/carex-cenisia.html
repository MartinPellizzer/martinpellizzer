Carex cenisia

Carex cenisia, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its slender, grass-like leaves are usually bright green and can reach heights of up to 60 cm.

Carex cenisia produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are followed by triangular, three-sided seed heads.
