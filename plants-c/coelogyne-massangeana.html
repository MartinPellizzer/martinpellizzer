Coelogyne massangeana

Coelogyne massangeana, commonly known as the Orchid of the Gods, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters of 10 to 20, often in shades of white, yellow, and pink. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Coelogyne massangeana is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
