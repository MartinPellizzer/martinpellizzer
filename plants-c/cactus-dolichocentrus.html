Cactus dolichocentrus

Cactus dolichocentrus, commonly known as the long-spined cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its spines are long and straight, providing protection from herbivores.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.
