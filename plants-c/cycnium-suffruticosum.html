Cycnium suffruticosum

Cycnium suffruticosum, commonly known as the common nightshade, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in umbels, while the fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains several seeds.

Cycnium suffruticosum is characterized by its toxic properties, with all parts of the plant containing alkaloids that can be harmful to humans and animals.
