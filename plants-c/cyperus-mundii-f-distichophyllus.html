Cyperus mundii f. distichophyllus

Cyperus mundii f. distichophyllus, commonly known as the two-ranked flatleaf sedgeland, is a variant of the Cyperus mundii species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in two rows, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, poorly drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
