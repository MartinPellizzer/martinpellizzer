Caesalpinia tortuosa

Caesalpinia tortuosa, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical leguminous shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bright red, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in summer. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are typically followed by long, slender pods that contain several seeds.

Caesalpinia tortuosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
