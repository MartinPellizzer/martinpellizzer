Campanula crystallocalyx

Campanula crystallocalyx, commonly known as the crystal bellflower, is a species of flowering plant in the Campanulaceae family.

It is native to specific regions in Central Asia and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is often covered in a fine, waxy coating, giving it a distinct appearance.

Campanula crystallocalyx is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and resilience.
