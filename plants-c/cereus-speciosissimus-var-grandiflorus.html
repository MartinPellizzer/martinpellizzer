Cereus speciosissimus var. grandiflorus

Cereus speciosissimus var. grandiflorus, commonly known as the giant night-blooming cereus, is a cactus species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and sparse, spiny foliage. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night and emit a fragrant scent. These flowers are typically short-lived, lasting only a single night.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
