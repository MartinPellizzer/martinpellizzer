Cornulaca tragacanthoides

Cornulaca tragacanthoides, commonly known as desert smartweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous annual that thrives in sandy or rocky soils, often found in dry, disturbed habitats. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, and its leaves are typically narrow and hairy. It is known for its ability to withstand drought conditions due to its deep root system and reduced leaf surface area.

The plant also has a historical use in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
