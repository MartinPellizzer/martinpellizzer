Cyperus herndoniae

Cyperus herndoniae, commonly known as the Herndonia flatsedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is often found in wetland and marshy environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically pale green to brown in color. It plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems and can serve as habitat for small invertebrates.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
