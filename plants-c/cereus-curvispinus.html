Cereus curvispinus

Cereus curvispinus, commonly known as the hooked spine cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces large, white, night-blooming flowers that are typically fragrant. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and well-draining soil.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to desert conditions, with thick stems that store water and spines that provide protection.
