Cicer korshinskyi

Cicer korshinskyi, commonly known as the Korshinsky vetch, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used for forage or as a source of protein.

It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
