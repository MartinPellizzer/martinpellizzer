Chenopodium opulifolium subsp. orientale

Chenopodium opulifolium subsp.

orientale, commonly known as Chinese spinach or *Amaranthus tricolor*, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Asia. It features alternate, broadly ovate to triangular leaves with a slightly pubescent surface.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in dense, spike-like inflorescences. Its seeds are small, smooth, and often used as a grain in traditional cuisines.

It is cultivated for its edible leaves and seeds, which are rich in nutrients.
