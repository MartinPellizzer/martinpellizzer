Crataegus azarolus var. dulcis

Crataegus azarolus var. dulcis, commonly known as the sweet thorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring and its red, edible fruits that ripen in autumn. The plant has thorny branches and is often found in rocky or dry habitats. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the fruit contains several small seeds.

The sweet thorn is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
