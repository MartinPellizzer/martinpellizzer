Campyloneurum cubense

Campyloneurum cubense, commonly known as the Cuban shield fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features a short, creeping rhizome with pinnately compound leaves that are leathery and dark green. Its spore-producing structures, called sorus, are located on the underside of the leaves.

Campyloneurum cubense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
