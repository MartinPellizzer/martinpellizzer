Clerodendrum aculeatum f. lanceolatum

Clerodendrum aculeatum f. lanceolatum, commonly known as the lanceleaf synandra, is a compact shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces striking clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for gardens and container planting.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
