Cucumis melo var. vulgaris

Cucumis melo var. vulgaris, commonly known as the cantaloupe, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a fruit-bearing vine that produces large, netted, orange-colored fruits. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and yellow flowers that are typically unisexual. The fruit develops from the female flowers and is characterized by its sweet, aromatic flesh.

It is cultivated for its edible fruit, which is rich in vitamins A and C.
