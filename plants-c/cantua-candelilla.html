Cantua candelilla

Cantua candelilla, commonly known as the candleplant, is a flowering plant native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, waxy substance called candelilla wax, which is used in various industrial applications. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs.

Cantua candelilla thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine and desert environments.
