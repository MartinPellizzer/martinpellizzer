Cyrtandra paludosa var. microcarpa

Cyrtandra paludosa var. microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited swamp cyrtandra, is a species of flowering plant in the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in wetland and swampy habitats. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tubular, white to pale yellow flowers. Its fruits are small capsules that contain numerous tiny seeds.

This variant is notable for its adaptation to moist, low-light environments and is often found in mangrove and coastal forest ecosystems.
