Celtis wightii var. consimilis

Celtis wightii var. consimilis, commonly known as the Indian hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to parts of South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and oval-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated margin. The tree produces small, white to pale yellow flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

It is often found in dry to moist deciduous forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
