Callistemon pachyphyllus var. viridis

Callistemon pachyphyllus var. viridis, commonly known as the green bottlebrush, is a variant of the bottlebrush plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, bright green foliage and striking red or orange floral spikes that appear in late spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are adapted to withstand dry conditions, making it drought-tolerant. Its flowers are rich in nectar and attract a variety of pollinators, including birds and insects.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
