Colubrina nicholsonii

Colubrina nicholsonii, commonly known as the Florida colubrina, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. These flowers are followed by elongated, narrow fruits that contain several seeds.

Colubrina nicholsonii is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to coastal and dune environments.
