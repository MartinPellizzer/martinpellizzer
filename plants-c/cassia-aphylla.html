Cassia aphylla

Cassia aphylla, commonly known as the smooth cassia, is a shrub native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous stems and broad, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by elongated, flat pods containing numerous seeds. Cassia aphylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Leguminosae family.
