Convolvulus pilosellifolius var. linearifolius

Convolvulus pilosellifolius var. linearifolius, commonly known as the slenderleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and trumpet-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant grows as a trailing vine, often found in rocky or sandy soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
