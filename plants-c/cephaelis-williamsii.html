Cephaelis williamsii

Cephaelis williamsii, commonly known as the Williams' cephaelis, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or red in color, blooming during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Cephaelis williamsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
