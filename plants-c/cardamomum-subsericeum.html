Cardamomum subsericeum

Cardamomum subsericeum, commonly known as the silky cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow. Its rhizomes, which are the primary part used, have a distinct, spicy aroma and are used in both culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
