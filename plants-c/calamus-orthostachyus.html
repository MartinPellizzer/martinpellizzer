Calamus orthostachyus

Calamus orthostachyus, commonly known as the straight-stemmed arrowroot, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender, and erect stems that can grow up to several meters in height. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a waxy texture, while its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe.

The plant is valued for its edible rhizomes, which are used in traditional cuisines and as a source of starch.
