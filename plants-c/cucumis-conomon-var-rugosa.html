Cucumis conomon var. rugosa

Cucumis conomon var. rugosa, commonly known as the warty cucumber, is a variant of the common cucumber characterized by its rough, bumpy skin.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plant is a vine that produces elongated, edible fruits with a mild, slightly bitter taste. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Its unique texture and flavor make it a distinctive choice for culinary and ornamental purposes.
