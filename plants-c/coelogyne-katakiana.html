Coelogyne katakiana

Coelogyne katakiana, commonly known as the Katakian orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. The plant typically blooms during the dry season, and its pseudobulbs are cylindrical, often covered in a fibrous sheath. Coelogyne katakiana prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for cultivation in shaded gardens or as a potted plant.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
