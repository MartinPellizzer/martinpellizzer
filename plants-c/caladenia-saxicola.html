Caladenia saxicola

Caladenia saxicola, commonly known as the rock orchid, is a striking terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features a single, erect stem with a single or few flowers that are usually pale pink or white. Its flowers have a distinctive lip that is often darker in color, and the species is known for its delicate and elegant appearance.

Caladenia saxicola is part of the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance.
