Crocus × koritnicus

Crocus × koritnicus, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a hybrid plant that belongs to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking violet or blue flowers, which typically bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a corm as its underground storage organ. It is native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and early blooming flowers.
