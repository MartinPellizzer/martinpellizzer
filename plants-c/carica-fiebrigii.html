Carica fiebrigii

Carica fiebrigii, commonly known as the Fiebrig's papaya, is a tropical plant belonging to the Caricaceae family.

It is native to Central and South America and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow-orange fruit. The plant produces a fleshy, aromatic fruit that is similar to the common papaya but typically smaller and less sweet. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is edible and often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
