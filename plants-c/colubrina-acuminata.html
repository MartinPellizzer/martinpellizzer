Colubrina acuminata

Colubrina acuminata, commonly known as the Florida colubrina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually blue to purple in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a rough texture, while its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Colubrina acuminata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
