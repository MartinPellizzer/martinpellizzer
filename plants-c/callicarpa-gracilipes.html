Callicarpa gracilipes

Callicarpa gracilipes, commonly known as the slender beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces striking violet-purple berries that ripen in the fall, attracting birds and other wildlife. Callicarpa gracilipes typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters and thrives in partial shade to full sun.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy fruit display.
