Cousinia pulcherantha

Cousinia pulcherantha, commonly known as the Kashmiri gentian, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, alpine environments and has a compact, cushion-like growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Cousinia pulcherantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
