Callitropsis macrocarpa

Callitropsis macrocarpa, commonly known as the Alaska cedar, is a species of coniferous tree native to the western coast of North America.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and small, cone-shaped fruits. The tree typically grows in coastal rainforests, where it thrives in moist, temperate climates. Its bark is thick and scaly, providing protection against harsh weather conditions.

Callitropsis macrocarpa plays an important ecological role in its habitat, offering shelter and food to various wildlife species.
