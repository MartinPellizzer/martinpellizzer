Cajanus acutifolius

Cajanus acutifolius, commonly known as the sickle bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods containing several seeds, which are used as a food source in some parts of the world. Cajanus acutifolius is adapted to grow in poor soils and can thrive in arid conditions.

It also plays a role in nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility.
