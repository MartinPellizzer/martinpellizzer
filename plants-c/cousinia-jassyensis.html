Cousinia jassyensis

Cousinia jassyensis, commonly known as the Jassy's cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and clustered, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive silver-gray appearance due to dense pubescence.

Cousinia jassyensis is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
