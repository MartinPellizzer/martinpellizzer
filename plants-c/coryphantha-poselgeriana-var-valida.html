Coryphantha poselgeriana var. valida

Coryphantha poselgeriana var. valida, commonly known as the valid coryphantha, is a species of cacti native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spherical or slightly elongated stem, which is typically covered in spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a vibrant touch to its desert habitat. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun exposure to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
