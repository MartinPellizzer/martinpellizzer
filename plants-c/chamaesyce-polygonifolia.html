Chamaesyce polygonifolia

Chamaesyce polygonifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf sandwort, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It has small, fleshy, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils, thriving in full sun.

Chamaesyce polygonifolia is well adapted to harsh conditions, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
