Crataegus monogyna subsp. maritima

Crataegus monogyna subsp.

maritima, commonly known as the sea hawthorn, is a shrub native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its small, white to pink flowers and red berries that mature in the autumn.

The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found along sea cliffs and dunes. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a serrated edge, while its stems are covered in thorns.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize coastal soils.
