Carex arisanensis var. tremula

Carex arisanensis var. tremula, commonly known as the trembling sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces three-sided, spiky fruits.

This variety is distinguished by its delicate, trembling appearance, which gives it its common name.
