Camellia sinensis f. rosea

Camellia sinensis f. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered camellia, is a unique cultivar of the tea plant characterized by its striking pink and white petals.

It is a hybrid species that exhibits both ornamental and potential tea-making qualities. The plant is native to China and thrives in subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil. Its flowers are often used in floral arrangements due to their beauty, while the leaves can be processed into a delicate tea.

This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal and its role in both horticulture and specialty tea production.
