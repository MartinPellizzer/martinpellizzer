Cissus grossedentata

Cissus grossedentata, commonly known as the African snowflake, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, white to pale green leaves that resemble snowflakes. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by green, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Cissus grossedentata is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and ease of care.
