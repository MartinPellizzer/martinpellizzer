Carex winkleri

Carex winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Native to specific regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, which develop into three-angled berries.

Carex winkleri is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, contributing to the ecological diversity of these habitats.
