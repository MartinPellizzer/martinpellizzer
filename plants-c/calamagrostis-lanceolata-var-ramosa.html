Calamagrostis lanceolata var. ramosa

Calamagrostis lanceolata var. ramosa, commonly known as the silver feather reed grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, silver-gray plumes that add ornamental value to gardens. The plant prefers moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that contrasts beautifully with its silvery inflorescences.

It is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife in wetland ecosystems.
