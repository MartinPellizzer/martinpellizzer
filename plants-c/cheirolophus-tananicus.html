Cheirolophus tananicus

Cheirolophus tananicus, commonly known as the Madagascan sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces numerous small, daisy-like flowers arranged in a composite head. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
