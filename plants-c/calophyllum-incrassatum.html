Calophyllum incrassatum

Calophyllum incrassatum, commonly known as the African sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to regions across Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark and large, glossy leaves. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers that are often fragrant, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. Its wood is highly valued for its fragrance and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

The plant thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.
