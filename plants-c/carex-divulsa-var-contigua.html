Carex divulsa var. contigua

Carex divulsa var. contigua, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually green to bluish-green in color. Its flowering spikes are usually tall and cylindrical, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
