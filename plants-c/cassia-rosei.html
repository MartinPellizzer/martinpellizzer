Cassia rosei

Cassia rosei, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
