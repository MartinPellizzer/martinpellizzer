Ceropegia abinsica

Ceropegia abinsica, commonly known as the Madagascar moonvine, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, succulent stems. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ceropegia abinsica is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
