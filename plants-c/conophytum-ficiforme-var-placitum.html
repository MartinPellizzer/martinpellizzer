Conophytum ficiforme var. placitum

Conophytum ficiforme var. placitum, commonly known as the "Coral Cactus," is a member of the Aizoaceae family native to South Africa.

It is a compact, succulent plant with dense clusters of fleshy, ribbed stems that resemble coral or branched structures. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
