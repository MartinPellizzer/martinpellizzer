Cyperus virens var. scaberrimus

Cyperus virens var. scaberrimus, commonly known as the rough flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and have a rough texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

Cyperus virens var. scaberrimus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.
