Conanthera trimaculata var. concomillana

Conanthera trimaculata var. concomillana, commonly known as the Texas spiderflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features slender, hairy stems that often grow in clusters, with small, white, spider-like flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it adaptable to arid environments.
