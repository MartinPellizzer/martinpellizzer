Cuphocarpus commersonii

Cuphocarpus commersonii, commonly known as the commerson's cup-horned tree, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive cup-shaped fruit, which is covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a rough texture with a bluish-green hue.

It produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators.
