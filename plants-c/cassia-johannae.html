Cassia johannae

Cassia johannae, commonly known as the yellow cassia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, compound leaves that are often feather-shaped. The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences, which are composed of numerous small, clustered flowers. Cassia johannae is typically found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
