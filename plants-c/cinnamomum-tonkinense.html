Cinnamomum tonkinense

Cinnamomum tonkinense, commonly known as Vietnamese cinnamon or tonkin cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that contain numerous seeds. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
