Crepidium elmeri

Crepidium elmeri, commonly known as the Elmer's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and along stream banks. The plant features a slender, often branched stem with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in a loose inflorescence, often with a distinctive hood-like structure.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with proper moisture and light conditions.
