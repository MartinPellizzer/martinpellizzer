Clerodendrum myrmecophilum

Clerodendrum myrmecophilum, commonly known as the ant-plant, is a flowering shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant is notable for its unique relationship with ants, providing them with shelter and nectar in exchange for protection. Its growth habit includes a woody stem with a rough, bark-like surface.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in garden ecosystems.
