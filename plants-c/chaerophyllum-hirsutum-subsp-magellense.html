Chaerophyllum hirsutum subsp. magellense

Chaerophyllum hirsutum subsp.

magellense, commonly known as the Magellanic cow parsley, is a perennial herb native to South America. It is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and small white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and riverbanks. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
