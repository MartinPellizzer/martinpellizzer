Cassia javanica subsp. nodosa

Cassia javanica subsp.

nodosa, commonly known as the nodding cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
