Chamaecrista falcifoliolata

Chamaecrista falcifoliolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring pinnately compound leaves with narrow, elongated leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in dense, racemose inflorescences, which are characteristic of the Fabaceae family. Its small, elliptical pods contain several seeds, and the plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The botanical features of Chamaecrista falcifoliolata make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
