Chloraster

Chloraster, commonly known as the green alga, is a unicellular, photosynthetic organism belonging to the kingdom Protista.

It contains chlorophyll, which allows it to perform photosynthesis in aquatic environments. Chloraster typically inhabits freshwater habitats such as ponds and lakes. Its structure is simple, consisting of a single cell with a nucleus and chloroplasts.

It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by producing oxygen and serving as a food source for other organisms.
