Citrus × bigaradia var. bigamia

Citrus × bigaradia var. bigamia, commonly known as the common mandarin.

This hybrid citrus plant is characterized by its small, sweet, and segmented fruits with a thin, peels. It is a cross between the mandarin and the pummelo, resulting in a plant that exhibits traits of both parent species. The leaves are glossy and oval-shaped, while the flowers are fragrant and white.

It thrives in subtropical climates and is widely cultivated for its edible fruit.
