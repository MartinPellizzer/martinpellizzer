Cyperus longespicatus

Cyperus longespicatus, commonly known as the long-spiked flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, triangular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikes of tiny, pale green to brown flowers, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in wetland habitats such as marshes and swamps.

Cyperus longespicatus is an important component of aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
