Crinum australe var. rubricaule

Crinum australe var. rubricaule, commonly known as the red-flowered swamp lily, is a striking tropical plant native to Australia.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a contrasting yellow throat. The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically a deep green, and it thrives in wet, swampy environments. It is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family, known for its ornamental value and sometimes toxic properties.

This variety is particularly noted for its vibrant floral display and adaptability to moist habitats.
