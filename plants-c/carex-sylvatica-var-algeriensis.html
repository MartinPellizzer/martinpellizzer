Carex sylvatica var. algeriensis

Carex sylvatica var. algeriensis, commonly known as Algerian woodland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to North Africa, particularly Algeria, and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth, slightly waxy surface. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that bears small, inconspicuous flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
