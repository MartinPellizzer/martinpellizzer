Cedrela serrata

Cedrela serrata, commonly known as the Brazilian walnut or cedro, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-gray bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing edible seeds. Cedrela serrata is valued for its durable, aromatic wood and is often used in furniture-making.

It also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
