Clusia pseudochina

Clusia pseudochina, commonly known as Chinese evergreen, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.

Clusia pseudochina is relatively drought-tolerant and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
