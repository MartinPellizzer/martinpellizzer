Carlina curetum var. montana

Carlina curetum var. montana, commonly known as mountain carline thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant features a robust stem with large, silver-green, rough-textured leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy composite flower heads consist of blue to purple ray florets surrounding a central disk of yellow tubular flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
