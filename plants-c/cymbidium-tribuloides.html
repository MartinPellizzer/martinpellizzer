Cymbidium tribuloides

Cymbidium tribuloides, commonly known as the woolly cymbidium, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, woolly coating. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, spurred orchid flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cymbidium tribuloides is a perennial evergreen that requires well-draining soil and partial shade for optimal growth.
