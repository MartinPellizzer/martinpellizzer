Canarium carapifolium

Canarium carapifolium, commonly known as the white-leafed candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually white or pale green on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spherical fruits containing oily seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its oil-rich seeds and has traditional uses in various cultures.
