Cypripedium cothurnum

Cypripedium cothurnum, commonly known as the showy lady's slipper, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, large, slipper-shaped flower that is typically pink or white in color. The plant has a thick, fleshy rhizome and is often found in moist, shaded woodlands. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces showy, fragrant blossoms in late spring.

Cypripedium cothurnum is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance.
