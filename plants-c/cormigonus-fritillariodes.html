Cormigonus fritillariodes

Cormigonus fritillariodes, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive golden hue. The plant grows from a corm, a swollen stem base that serves as a storage organ for nutrients. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, while the stems are upright and branching.

Cormigonus fritillariodes is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
