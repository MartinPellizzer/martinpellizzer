Cassia bifida

Cassia bifida, commonly known as split cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves that are divided into two parts, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Cassia bifida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its seeds are sometimes used as a spice, similar to cinnamon.
