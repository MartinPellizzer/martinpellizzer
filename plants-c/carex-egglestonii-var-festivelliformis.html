Carex egglestonii var. festivelliformis

Carex egglestonii var. festivelliformis, commonly known as the festive sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces clusters of small, greenish-brown flowers in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It has narrow, strap-like leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
