Chromolaena polyantha

Chromolaena polyantha, commonly known as the white chromolaena, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small white flowers. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings due to its adaptability and rapid growth.

Chromolaena polyantha is also known for its potential medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
