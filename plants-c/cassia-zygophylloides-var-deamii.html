Cassia zygophylloides var. deamii

Cassia zygophylloides var. deamii, commonly known as the Deam's cassia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its seed pods are elongated and contain numerous small, brown seeds.

This species is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
