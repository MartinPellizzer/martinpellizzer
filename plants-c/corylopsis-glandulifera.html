Corylopsis glandulifera

Corylopsis glandulifera, commonly known as the sticky cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and covered in small, glandular dots. Its fruit is a persistent, fleshy berry that ripens to a dark red color.

Corylopsis glandulifera is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
