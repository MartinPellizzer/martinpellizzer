Camellia petelotii var. petelotii

Camellia petelotii var. petelotii, commonly known as the Petelot's camellia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces striking, large, double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil. Its leaves are dark green and elliptical, often with a slight waxy coating.

Camellia petelotii var. petelotii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant in subtropical climates.
