Crocus reticulatus lusus striatus

Crocus reticulatus lusus striatus, commonly known as the striped crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers that typically feature a mix of purple, white, and yellow hues. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped blooms that are borne on short, sturdy stems above the foliage. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

This perennial plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
