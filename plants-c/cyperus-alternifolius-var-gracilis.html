Cyperus alternifolius var. gracilis

Cyperus alternifolius var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, grassy cluster. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Its botanical structure makes it adaptable to a variety of aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
