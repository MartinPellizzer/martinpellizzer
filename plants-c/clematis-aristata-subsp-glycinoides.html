Clematis aristata subsp. glycinoides

Clematis aristata subsp.

glycinoides, commonly known as the yellow clematis, is a perennial vine native to the western United States. It features slender, twining stems that can climb to heights of several meters.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with three to five leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
