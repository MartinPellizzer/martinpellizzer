Crotalaria diversifolia

Crotalaria diversifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its palmately lobed leaves and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter.

Crotalaria diversifolia is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in agricultural settings for its nitrogen-fixing properties.
