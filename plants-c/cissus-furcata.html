Cissus furcata

Cissus furcata, commonly known as the devil's ivy or creeping fig, is a climbing vine native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Cissus furcata is often used in landscaping for its ability to climb and cover structures effectively.
