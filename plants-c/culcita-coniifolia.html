Culcita coniifolia

Culcita coniifolia, commonly known as the common marsh woundwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, fens, and along stream banks. The plant has a creeping rootstock and produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Culcita coniifolia is characterized by its ability to thrive in poorly drained soils and its role in wetland ecosystems.
