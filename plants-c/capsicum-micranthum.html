Capsicum micranthum

Capsicum micranthum, commonly known as the small-flowered chili pepper, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. This plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its small, yellow or orange flowers are followed by fruits that range in color from yellow to red.

The plant is valued for its edible fruits, which have a mild to spicy flavor depending on the variety.
