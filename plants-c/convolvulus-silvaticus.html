Convolvulus silvaticus

Convolvulus silvaticus, commonly known as the woodland bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in woodland and shrubland habitats, often thriving in partial shade. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are usually pale purple or pink, with five lobes. Its vines can climb or trail along the ground, and its leaves are heart-shaped with a hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several small, dry seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
