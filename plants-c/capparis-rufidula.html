Capparis rufidula

Capparis rufidula, commonly known as the red-stemmed caper, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features woody stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fleshy, red berries. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
