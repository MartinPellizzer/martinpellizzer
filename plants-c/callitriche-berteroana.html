Callitriche berteroana

Callitriche berteroana, commonly known as the Mexican water starwort, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to Central and South America.

It typically grows in shallow water, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has tiny, star-shaped leaves that are often floating or submerged, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers. It is an annual or short-lived perennial, with a compact growth habit.

Its presence indicates healthy wetland ecosystems and it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
