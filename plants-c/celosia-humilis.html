Celosia humilis

Celosia humilis, commonly known as the dwarf crown-of-thorns, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branched stems and dense clusters of small, colorful bracts that resemble a crown. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by the showy bracts. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements contribute to its ornamental value.
