Calliandra xylocarpa

Calliandra xylocarpa, commonly known as the white calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery inflorescences that produce small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Additionally, it is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agroforestry systems.
