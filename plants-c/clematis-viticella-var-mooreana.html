Clematis viticella var. mooreana

Clematis viticella var. mooreana, commonly known as the Moore's Clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to Europe.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is known for its strong growth habit and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be trained on trellises or fences.

This variety is also relatively disease-resistant and easy to maintain in gardens.
