Cynodendron auratum

Cynodendron auratum, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The golden flame tree is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
