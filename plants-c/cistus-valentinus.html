Cistus valentinus

Cistus valentinus, commonly known as Valentine's cistus, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves and produces showy, pink or white flowers in the spring. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often found in rocky or dry soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.

Cistus valentinus is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought.
