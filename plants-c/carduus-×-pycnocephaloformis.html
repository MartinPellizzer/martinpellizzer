Carduus × pycnocephaloformis

Carduus × pycnocephaloformis, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Carduus pycnocephalus and Carduus crispus.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and large, spiny heads of purple to pink flowers. The plant is native to Europe and has been introduced to various regions around the world. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in fields, roadsides, and along stream banks.

Botanically, it is notable for its complex inflorescence structure and its role in ecological succession.
