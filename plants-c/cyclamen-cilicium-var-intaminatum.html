Cyclamen cilicium var. intaminatum

Cyclamen cilicium var. intaminatum, commonly known as the Cilician cyclamen, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and Syria.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinctive silver-pink underside, and produces clusters of pink to purple flowers in spring. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, humus-rich soil. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.

Cyclamen cilicium var. intaminatum is a member of the Primulaceae family, sharing characteristics with other cyclamen species.
