Cantua pungens

Cantua pungens, commonly known as the spiked gentian, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and clusters of striking blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, downy layer.

Cantua pungens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
