Consolida Cornuta

Consolida cornuta, also known as the horned poppy, is a striking member of the Papaveraceae family. Native to Europe and western Asia, this plant is known for its distinctive, horn-like seed pods that resemble tiny horns. Its vibrant orange to red flowers bloom in late spring and early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners. With its unique appearance and easy care requirements, Consolida cornuta is both an ornamental and ecological asset in the landscape.
