Cynoglossospermum concavum, commonly known as the concave tongue plant, is a member of the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tongue-shaped leaves that are often concave in appearance. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
