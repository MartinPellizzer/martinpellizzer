Carex obliqua

Carex obliqua, commonly known as the slough sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. Carex obliqua thrives in moist, poorly drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and wet meadows.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
