Crataegus prunifolia var. splendens

Crataegus prunifolia var. splendens, commonly known as the showy hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its ornamental white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges and a glossy green surface. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
