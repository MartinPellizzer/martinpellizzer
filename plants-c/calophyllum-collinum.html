Calophyllum collinum

Calophyllum collinum, commonly known as the African mahogany, is a tropical tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in furniture and construction, and for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is also known for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and maintaining soil stability in tropical regions.
