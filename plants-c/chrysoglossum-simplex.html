Chrysoglossum simplex

Chrysoglossum simplex, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its drought tolerance and ornamental value contribute to its appeal in both horticultural and ecological settings.
