Cascarilla riveroana

Cascarilla riveroana, commonly known as the Rio Negro cinnamon tree, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow as they mature. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils, which have been used in traditional medicine for their aromatic and antiseptic properties.

The plant is adapted to humid, shaded environments and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its habitat.
