Cineraria bipinnata

Cineraria bipinnata, commonly known as the doubleleaf cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a rosette of large, hairy, lobed leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid native environment. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Cineraria bipinnata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
