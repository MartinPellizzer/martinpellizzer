Crocus vernus f. niveus

Crocus vernus f. niveus, commonly known as the white crocus, is a botanical variant of the common crocus.

It is characterized by its pale white to almost pure white flowers, which contrast with the typical purple or yellow hues of the species. This cultivar is prized for its elegant appearance and is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes. The plant is a member of the Iridaceae family and produces showy, cup-shaped blooms in early spring.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.
