Coussapoa asperifolia subsp. magnifolia

Coussapoa asperifolia subsp.

magnifolia, commonly known as the magnolia-like tree, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil. It is characterized by its large, showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant has a woody stem with rough, bark-like texture, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a leathery texture. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
