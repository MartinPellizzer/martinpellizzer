Clethra esquirolii

Clethra esquirolii, commonly known as the Mexican sweet pepper tree, is a deciduous shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the plant family Clethraceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
