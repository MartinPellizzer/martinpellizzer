Carex vansteenisii

Carex vansteenisii, commonly known as the Van Steenis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

Carex vansteenisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
