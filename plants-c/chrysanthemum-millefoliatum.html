Chrysanthemum millefoliatum

Chrysanthemum millefoliatum, commonly known as the common chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and showy flower heads that range from yellow to red. The plant is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Chrysanthemum millefoliatum is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural pigments.
