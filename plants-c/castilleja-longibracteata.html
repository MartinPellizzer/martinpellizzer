Castilleja longibracteata

Castilleja longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract Indian paintbrush, is a hemiparasitic plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Orobanchaceae family and is characterized by its showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. This species is often found in open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
