Convolvulus subsericeus subsp. hamadae

Convolvulus subsericeus subsp.

hamadae, commonly known as the Hamada morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa. It is characterized by its delicate, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to white, typically blooming in the early morning.

The plant has slender, winding stems that often trail along the ground, and its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in suitable climates.
