Cerastium grandiflorum var. albanicum

Cerastium grandiflorum var. albanicum, commonly known as the Albanian mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its frost-resistant nature.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
