Crataegus sororia var. segnis

Crataegus sororia var. segnis, commonly known as the slender hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched structure and small, white to pinkish flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are oval to elliptic, with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
