Carissa sessiliflora var. grandiflora

Carissa sessiliflora var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Natal plum or yellow carissa, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant features broad, ovate leaves with a glossy green surface and produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. These flowers are followed by yellow or orange berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also cultivated for its fruit in some regions.
