Cassia dimidiata

Cassia dimidiata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and striking clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia dimidiata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
