Calonemorchis caesarea subsp. maritima

Calonemorchis caesarea subsp.

maritima, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silver-gray foliage, which help it thrive in saline and well-drained soils.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy habitats along the shorelines. Its flowers are borne on tall, branching stems and bloom from late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in coastal gardens.
