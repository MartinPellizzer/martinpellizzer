Cynoglossum tomentosum

Cynoglossum tomentosum, commonly known as the fuzzy mouse ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features hairy, velvety leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological landscapes.
