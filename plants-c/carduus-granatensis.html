Carduus granatensis

Carduus granatensis, commonly known as the Granada thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.

The plant's robust structure and adaptability make it a notable species in its native environment.
