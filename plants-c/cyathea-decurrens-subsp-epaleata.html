Cyathea decurrens subsp. epaleata

Cyathea decurrens subsp.

epaleata, commonly known as the broad-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length.

The plant has a distinctive trunk covered in a fibrous, spongy material called the rachis. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The fronds are typically bright green and can be used for ornamental purposes in horticulture.
