Cryptantha grayi var. cryptochaeta

Cryptantha grayi var. cryptochaeta, commonly known as the Sierra buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.
