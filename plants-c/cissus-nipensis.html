Cissus nipensis

Cissus nipensis, commonly known as the rock fig or stonecress, is a climbing plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits. Cissus nipensis is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in shaded areas, showcasing its adaptability to various environments.

Its rapid growth and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
