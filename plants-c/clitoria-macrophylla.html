Clitoria macrophylla

Clitoria macrophylla, commonly known as the winged bean or Himalayan balsam, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive butterfly-shaped blue or purple flowers and heart-shaped leaves. The plant is a climbing vine that can grow up to several meters in length. Its flowers are hermaphroditic, containing both male and female reproductive organs.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been introduced to other regions, sometimes becoming invasive.
