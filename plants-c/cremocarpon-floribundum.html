Cremocarpon floribundum

Cremocarpon floribundum, commonly known as the flowering rockcress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments. Its flowers are hermaphroditic, containing both male and female reproductive structures.

Cremocarpon floribundum is adapted to cold climates and can survive in harsh, nutrient-poor conditions.
