Carex cruciata

Carex cruciata, commonly known as the cross-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in early summer. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex cruciata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
