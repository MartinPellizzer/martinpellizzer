Cineraria salicifolia

Cineraria salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that resemble willow leaves, hence its common name. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers a sunny to partially shaded location.

Cineraria salicifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
