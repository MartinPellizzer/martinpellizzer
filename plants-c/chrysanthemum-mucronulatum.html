Chrysanthemum mucronulatum

Chrysanthemum mucronulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chrysanthemum, is a species native to China.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its flowering period occurs in the late summer to early autumn, adding vibrant color to gardens during this time.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in traditional Chinese horticulture.
