Convolvulus breweraceus

Convolvulus breweraceus, commonly known as the Brewer's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its rapid growth and spreading nature make it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed in certain environments.
