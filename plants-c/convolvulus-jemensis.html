Convolvulus jemensis

Convolvulus jemensis, commonly known as the Jemensis bindweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain environments if not managed properly.
