Convolvulus arvensis f. notatus

Convolvulus arvensis f. notatus, commonly known as the spotted field bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive spotted or mottled appearance. The flowers are typically pale purple or white, with five petals and a tubular structure. This subspecies is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along field edges.

It is considered a weed in agricultural settings due to its rapid growth and extensive root system.
