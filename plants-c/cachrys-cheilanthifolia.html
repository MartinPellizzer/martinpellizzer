Cachrys cheilanthifolia

Cachrys cheilanthifolia, commonly known as the golden saxifrage, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, typically in clusters at the ends of its stems. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Cachrys cheilanthifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
