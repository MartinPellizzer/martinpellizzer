Cladium laxum

Cladium laxum, commonly known as the marsh grass or sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used for thatching and basket weaving by indigenous communities. The plant thrives in shallow, freshwater habitats such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of lakes. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, consist of small, greenish flowers surrounded by bracts.

Cladium laxum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
