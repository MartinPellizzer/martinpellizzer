Camellia jiuyishanica

Camellia jiuyishanica, commonly known as the Jiuyishan camellia, is a species native to China, specifically the Jiuyishan region in Fujian Province.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces striking, large, double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its showy blooms.

Camellia jiuyishanica prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for temperate climates.
