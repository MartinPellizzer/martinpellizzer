Calceolaria dichotoma

Calceolaria dichotoma, commonly known as the two-branched slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive bilaterally symmetrical flowers that resemble small slippers, with a unique pouch-like structure at the base. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are linear and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Calceolaria dichotoma is valued in horticulture for its vibrant colors and unusual floral morphology.
