Cistus scabrosus

Cistus scabrosus, commonly known as rough rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink or red, depending on the species. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun.

Cistus scabrosus is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
