Cerastium lanigerum var. durmitoreum

Cerastium lanigerum var. durmitoreum, commonly known as the white mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, rounded leaves that are densely covered with fine, soft hairs, giving it a fluffy appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and garden beds.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a ground cover in horticulture.
