Ceratosanthes tomentosa var. subnuda

Ceratosanthes tomentosa var. subnuda, commonly known as the hairy false mallow, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features a slender, branching stem covered in dense, white, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and also bear a fine pubescence. Its showy, pale yellow to orange flowers are clustered in terminal spikes and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is adapted to moist, rich soils and is often found in woodland edges and stream banks.
