Crotalaria linaria

Crotalaria linaria, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features palmate leaves with three to five leaflets and produces yellow or orange flowers that attract pollinators. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source for livestock.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soils, making it a valuable species in ecological restoration projects.
