Chionanthus velutinus

Chionanthus velutinus, commonly known as the silky fringe tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant, white to cream-colored flowers that appear in clusters along the branches in early spring. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a velvety texture, giving the plant its common name. It typically grows to a height of 6 to 15 feet and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

The plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
