Cyathea aquilina var. maxonii

Cyathea aquilina var. maxonii, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan. Its fronds are typically evergreen, providing a lush, tropical appearance.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
