Carex iraqensis

Carex iraqensis, commonly known as the Iraqi sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Middle East.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water bodies. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, clustered flowers in early summer.

Carex iraqensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
