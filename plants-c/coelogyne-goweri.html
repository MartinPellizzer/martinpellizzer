Coelogyne goweri

Coelogyne goweri, commonly known as the Gower's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant typically blooms during the dry season, with flowers emerging from the base of the pseudobulb. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Coelogyne goweri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
