Carex badilloi

Carex badilloi, commonly known as the Mexican sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant has slender, triangular stems and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. Its leaves are grass-like, with a rough texture, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Carex badilloi is often found in wetlands and riparian areas, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
