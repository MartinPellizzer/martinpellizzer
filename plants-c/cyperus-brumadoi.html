Cyperus brumadoi

Cyperus brumadoi, commonly known as the Brazilian flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus brumadoi is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
