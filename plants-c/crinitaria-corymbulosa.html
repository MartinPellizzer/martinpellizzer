Crinitaria corymbulosa

Crinitaria corymbulosa, commonly known as the clustered sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive blue or violet flowers that bloom in dense, branched clusters. The plant thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in maritime climates. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to reduce water loss in saline environments.

Crinitaria corymbulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in coastal gardens.
