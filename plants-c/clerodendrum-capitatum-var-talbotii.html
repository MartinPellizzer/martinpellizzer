Clerodendrum capitatum var. talbotii

Clerodendrum capitatum var. talbotii, commonly known as the Talbot's Clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
