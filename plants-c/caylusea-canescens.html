Caylusea canescens

Caylusea canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf caylusea, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for xeriscaping.

Caylusea canescens is often used in ornamental gardening for its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
