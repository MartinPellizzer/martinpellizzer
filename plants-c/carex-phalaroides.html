Carex phalaroides

Carex phalaroides, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular, often glaucous leaves that are narrow and linear. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in dense, cylindrical clusters. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds.

Carex phalaroides is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
