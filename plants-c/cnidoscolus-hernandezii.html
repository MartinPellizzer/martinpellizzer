Cnidoscolus hernandezii

Cnidoscolus hernandezii, commonly known as the Mexican hog peanut, is a perennial shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves with long, slender leaflets and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, hard seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed soils and along forest edges.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
