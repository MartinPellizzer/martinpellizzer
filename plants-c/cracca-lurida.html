Cracca lurida

Cracca lurida, commonly known as the pale cranesbill, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Geraniaceae family and is characterized by its upright, branching stems and distinctive violet to pink flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in woodland edges and rocky soils. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Cracca lurida is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
