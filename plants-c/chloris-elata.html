Chloris elata

Chloris elata, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-green blades and tufted flowering heads that produce pale blue or greenish flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassy areas, heaths, and along coastal regions. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive, feathery appearance.

Chloris elata is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various insects and small animals.
