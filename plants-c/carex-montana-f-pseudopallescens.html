Carex montana f. pseudopallescens

Carex montana f. pseudopallescens, commonly known as the gray-silver sedge, is a variant of the montana sedge, characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, typically found in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.

This cultivar is particularly noted for its dense growth habit and ability to stabilize soil in riparian areas.
