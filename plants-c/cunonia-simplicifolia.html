Cunonia simplicifolia

Cunonia simplicifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved cunonia, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Rhamnaceae and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and scrub habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
