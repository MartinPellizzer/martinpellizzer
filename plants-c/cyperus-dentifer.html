Cyperus dentifer

Cyperus dentifer, commonly known as the toothed flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its root system helps stabilize soil in wetland areas, contributing to ecosystem health.

Cyperus dentifer is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber in some cultures.
