Carpinus tientaiensis

Carpinus tientaiensis, commonly known as the Tientai hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, spiky fruits that are dispersed by wind. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
