Carpodinus lactea

Carpodinus lactea, commonly known as the white milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its milky sap, which is a distinguishing botanical feature. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually white or pale pink, attracting various pollinators.

The plant's latex contains cardiac glycosides, which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
