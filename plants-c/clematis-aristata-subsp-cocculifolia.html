Clematis aristata subsp. cocculifolia

Clematis aristata subsp.

cocculifolia, commonly known as the mountain clematis, is a perennial vine native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It features climbing stems with small, palmately lobed leaves that are typically hairy.

The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers in late spring to early summer. Its botanical characteristics include a robust root system and a preference for well-drained, rocky soils.

It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cooler, higher elevation environments.
