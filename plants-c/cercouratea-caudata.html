Cercouratea caudata

Cercouratea caudata, commonly known as the long-tailed fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, arching fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant features a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread and form dense mats. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, with a feathery appearance that gives it a lush, tropical look.

It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.
