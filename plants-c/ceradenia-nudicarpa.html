Ceradenia nudicarpa

Ceradenia nudicarpa, commonly known as the naked-fruited ceradenia, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, violet-colored flowers with a distinctive spurred lip. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, often covered in a fine pubescence.

The fruit is a capsule that remains attached to the plant for an extended period, contributing to its common name.
