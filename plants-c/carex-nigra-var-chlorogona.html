Carex nigra var. chlorogona

Carex nigra var. chlorogona, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grasses, and related plants. This variety is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and produces clusters of small, spike-like flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
