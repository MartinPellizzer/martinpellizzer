Cyperus usitatus var. macrobulbosus

Cyperus usitatus var. macrobulbosus, commonly known as the giant umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences that resemble umbrellas, consisting of dense clusters of small, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas, pond edges, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and landscaping in humid climates.
