Centaurea napulifera subsp. nissana

Centaurea napulifera subsp.

nissana, commonly known as the Mediterranean knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open grasslands. It has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance, supporting various pollinators and contributing to local biodiversity.
