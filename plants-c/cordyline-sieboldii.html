Cordyline sieboldii

Cordyline sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese threadleaf cordyline, is a striking evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It features tall, slender stems with elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that appear in terminal spikes during the spring. Its foliage is highly ornamental, making it a popular choice for landscaping and indoor cultivation.

Cordyline sieboldii thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, requiring moderate watering to prevent root rot.
