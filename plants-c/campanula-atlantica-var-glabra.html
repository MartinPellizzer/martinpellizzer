Campanula atlantica var. glabra

Campanula atlantica var. glabra, commonly known as the Atlantic bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of the Atlantic coast.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, smooth texture, giving it the variety name "glabra," which means "smooth" in Latin. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate salty air and coastal conditions.
