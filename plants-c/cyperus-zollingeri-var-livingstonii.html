Cyperus zollingeri var. livingstonii

Cyperus zollingeri var. livingstonii, commonly known as the Livingston flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, with a distinctive pale green to yellowish hue. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are often hidden among the foliage, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
