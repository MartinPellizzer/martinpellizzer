Crassocephalum flatmense

Crassocephalum flatmense, commonly known as the flat-headed sunflower, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features large, showy yellow flowers with a flat, disc-like center, which gives it its common name. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
