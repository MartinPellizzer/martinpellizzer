Cotoneaster bilokonii

Cotoneaster bilokonii, commonly known as the Armenian cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows to a height of 1-3 meters and features small, oval leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers in spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Cotoneaster bilokonii is also known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
