Cyrtochilum linguiforme

Cyrtochilum linguiforme, commonly known as the tongue-shaped orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a compact, low-growing habit, featuring dark green, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, delicate flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, with a distinctive, elongated shape that resembles a tongue. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish.

Cyrtochilum linguiforme is appreciated for its unique morphology and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value.
