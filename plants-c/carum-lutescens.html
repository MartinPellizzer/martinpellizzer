Carum lutescens

Carum lutescens, commonly known as yellow caraway, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in umbels, and its leaves are finely divided, giving it a delicate appearance. The seeds, which are small and aromatic, are often used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
