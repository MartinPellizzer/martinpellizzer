Catasetum × santo-antoniense

Catasetum × santo-antoniense, commonly known as the Santo Antoniense orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often orange or red flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, tropical environments. Its flowers have a unique structure with specialized pollinia that are transferred by specific insect pollinators.

The botanical interest in this species lies in its hybrid origin and its role in orchid horticulture.
