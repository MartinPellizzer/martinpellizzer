Croton brasiliensis

Croton brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian croton, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that display a variety of hues, including red, yellow, and green. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental species. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in outdoor gardens.

The leaves are typically thick and leathery, providing a unique texture and visual appeal.
