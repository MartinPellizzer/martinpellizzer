Ctenopteris macra

Ctenopteris macra, commonly known as the giant chain fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its fronds are composed of numerous small, lance-shaped leaflets arranged in a distinctive, cascading pattern.

Ctenopteris macra plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
