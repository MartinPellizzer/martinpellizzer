Cephalophyllum hallii

Cephalophyllum hallii, commonly known as the Hall's headplant, is a unique member of the Portulacaceae family.

It is a small, perennial succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa. The plant is characterized by its fleshy, stem-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. Due to its specialized adaptations, it is well-suited to survive in harsh, dry environments.
