Callerya nitida var. minor

Callerya nitida var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf callerya, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
