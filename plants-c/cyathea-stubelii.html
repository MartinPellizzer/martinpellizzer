Cyathea stubelii

Cyathea stubelii, commonly known as the Stuble's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for many decades, making it a long-lived species. Its rhizomes are fibrous and contribute to the structural support of the plant.

Cyathea stubelii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
