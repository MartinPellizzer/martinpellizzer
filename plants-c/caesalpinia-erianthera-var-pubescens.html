Caesalpinia erianthera var. pubescens

Caesalpinia erianthera var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy glorybower, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, hairy stipules and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique appearance and adaptability contribute to its appeal in both ecological and horticultural settings.
