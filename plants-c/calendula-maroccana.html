Calendula maroccana

Calendula maroccana, commonly known as Moroccan marigold, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Calendula maroccana is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value.
