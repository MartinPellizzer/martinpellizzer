Corynelobos

Corynelobos, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood, is a member of the Rutaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil. The plant is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood and compound leaves with ovate leaflets. It produces small, white flowers that are followed by orange-yellow fruits.

Due to overharvesting, it is now classified as critically endangered by the IUCN.
