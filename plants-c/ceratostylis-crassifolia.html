Ceratostylis crassifolia

Ceratostylis crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed helmet orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its robust, fleshy leaves and distinctive, helmet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest undergrowth. Its flowers are usually green or yellowish-green with a prominent, spurred lip.

Ceratostylis crassifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in warm climates for its unique appearance.
