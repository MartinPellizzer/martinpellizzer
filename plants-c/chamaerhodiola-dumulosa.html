Chamaerhodiola dumulosa

Chamaerhodiola dumulosa, commonly known as the Siberian geranium, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of Asia, particularly Siberia and the Russian Far East.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, pink or white flowers that bloom in the summer months, and its leaves are usually covered in a fine layer of white hair. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

Chamaerhodiola dumulosa is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
