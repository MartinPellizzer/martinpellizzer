Colignonia parviflora subsp. biumbellata

Colignonia parviflora subsp.

biumbellata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved colignonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, bell-shaped inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in coastal fynbos vegetation, thriving in well-drained, sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
