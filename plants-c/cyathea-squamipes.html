Cyathea squamipes

Cyathea squamipes, commonly known as the scaly tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant has a distinctive trunk covered in a layer of brown, scaly tissue, which gives it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Cyathea squamipes plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various forest organisms.
