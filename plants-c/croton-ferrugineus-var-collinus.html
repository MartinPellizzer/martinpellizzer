Croton ferrugineus var. collinus

Croton ferrugineus var. collinus, commonly known as the red-leaf croton, is a tropical shrub characterized by its striking, brightly colored foliage.

It features thick, waxy leaves that often display shades of red, orange, and yellow, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.
