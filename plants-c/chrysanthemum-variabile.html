Chrysanthemum variabile

Chrysanthemum variabile, commonly known as the variable chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its diverse range of flower colors, including white, yellow, and purple. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are composite, consisting of ray florets and disc florets.

Chrysanthemum variabile is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for centuries in traditional Chinese horticulture.
