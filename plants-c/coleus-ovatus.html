Coleus ovatus

Coleus ovatus, commonly known as the oval-leaved coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are often brightly colored in shades of red, purple, and green. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale blue or violet, arranged in whorled clusters.

Coleus ovatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and is relatively easy to grow in warm, humid conditions.
