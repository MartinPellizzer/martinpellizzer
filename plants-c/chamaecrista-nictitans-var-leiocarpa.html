Chamaecrista nictitans var. leiocarpa

Chamaecrista nictitans var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the yellow cassia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
