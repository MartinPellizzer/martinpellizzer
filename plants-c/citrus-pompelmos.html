Citrus pompelmos

Citrus pompelmos, commonly known as the grapefruit.

It is a citrus tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America, characterized by its thick, rough, and often peeling bark. The plant produces large, oval-shaped fruits with a thick, segmented rind and a juicy, segmented pulp that can be either sweet or sour depending on the variety. The leaves are dark green, oval, and slightly serrated, while the flowers are white and fragrant.

The fruit is rich in vitamin C and is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.
