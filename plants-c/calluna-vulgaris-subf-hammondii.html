Calluna vulgaris subf. hammondii

Calluna vulgaris subf.

hammondii, commonly known as the Hammond's heather, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Scottish Highlands. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathland and moorland environments. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its floral display.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
