Chamaesyce garberi

Chamaesyce garberi, commonly known as Garber's sandmat, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed stems and small, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a hardy species suitable for xeriscaping.
