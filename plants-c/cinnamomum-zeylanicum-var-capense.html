Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. capense

Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. capense, commonly known as the West African cinnamon or Ceylon cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of West Africa.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, aromatic bark that is used to produce a type of cinnamon. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 15 meters. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a fragrant, slightly hairy surface.

The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy berries containing a single seed.
