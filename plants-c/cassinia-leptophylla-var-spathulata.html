Cassinia leptophylla var. spathulata

Cassinia leptophylla var. spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed cassinia, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, often with a slightly hairy surface.

The flowers bloom in summer, attracting a variety of pollinators including bees and birds.
