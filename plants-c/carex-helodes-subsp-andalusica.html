Carex helodes subsp. andalusica

Carex helodes subsp.

andalusica, commonly known as the Andalusian sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants.

This subspecies is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a vital role in supporting local biodiversity and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
