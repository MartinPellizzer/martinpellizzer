Carex floresiana

Carex floresiana, commonly known as the Flores sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to the island of Flores in Indonesia and is adapted to moist, tropical environments. The plant has grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other waterlogged habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, Carex floresiana is considered a rare and endemic species.
