Ceratostylis acutifolia

Ceratostylis acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a slender, wiry stem with a single, erect, hooded flower that is typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are acute at the tips, contributing to its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The orchid's unique floral structure and ornamental appearance make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
