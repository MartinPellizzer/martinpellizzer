Camellia parvimuricata var. hupehensis

Camellia parvimuricata var. hupehensis, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a species native to China.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy, camellia-like flowers that bloom in winter or early spring. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, with a leathery texture, and the flowers are usually red or pink, sometimes with white petals.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
