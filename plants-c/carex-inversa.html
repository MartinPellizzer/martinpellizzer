Carex inversa

Carex inversa, commonly known as the inverted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive inverted inflorescence, where the spikes are oriented downward, a unique feature among sedges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, bogs, and along stream edges.

Carex inversa plays a vital role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
