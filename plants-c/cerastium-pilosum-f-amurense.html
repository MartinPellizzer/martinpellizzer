Cerastium pilosum f. amurense

Cerastium pilosum f. amurense, commonly known as the Amur mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its flowers are typically white or pink, with five petals, and bloom in early spring.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
