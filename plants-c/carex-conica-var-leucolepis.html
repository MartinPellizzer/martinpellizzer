Carex conica var. leucolepis

Carex conica var. leucolepis, commonly known as the white-leaved tussock sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often white or pale green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a tall, cylindrical spike that bears small, three-ranked flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland environments.
