Cynosurus echinatus subvar. giganteus

Cynosurus echinatus subvar. giganteus, commonly known as the giant hairy fescue, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, tufted growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces spikelets that are covered in short, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in certain habitats.
