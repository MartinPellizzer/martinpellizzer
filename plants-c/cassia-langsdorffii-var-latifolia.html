Cassia langsdorffii var. latifolia

Cassia langsdorffii var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved cassia, is a species native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, which are typical of the legume family. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.
