Crataegus emersoniana

Crataegus emersoniana, commonly known as the Emerson hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches, simple leaves, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant communities and ecological restoration projects.
