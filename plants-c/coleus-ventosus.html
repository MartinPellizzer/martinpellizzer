Coleus ventosus

Coleus ventosus, commonly known as the wind coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, purple, and green. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, arranged in spikes at the ends of the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage.

Coleus ventosus prefers well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
