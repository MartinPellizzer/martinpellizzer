Carex tolmiei var. subsessilis

Carex tolmiei var. subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and wet meadows. Its flowering stems bear small, three-lobed flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

This variety is notable for its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
