Cyperus sesquiflorus

Cyperus sesquiflorus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and swampy areas. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
