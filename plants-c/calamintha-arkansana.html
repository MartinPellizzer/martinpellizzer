Calamintha arkansana

Calamintha arkansana, commonly known as Arkansas mountain betony, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces small, pale blue to violet flowers.

Its fragrance and ornamental value make it a desirable plant for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
