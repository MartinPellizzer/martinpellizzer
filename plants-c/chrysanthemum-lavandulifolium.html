Chrysanthemum lavandulifolium

Chrysanthemum lavandulifolium, commonly known as the lavenderleaf chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of lavender. The plant produces daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the autumn. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its foliage and flowers are used in aromatherapy and herbal remedies due to their soothing properties.
