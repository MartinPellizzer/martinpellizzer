Centropogon hartwegii var. parvus

Centropogon hartwegii var. parvus, commonly known as the dwarf marsh orchid, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it often grows in dense clusters near water sources.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
