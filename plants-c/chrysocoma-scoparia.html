Chrysocoma scoparia

Chrysocoma scoparia, commonly known as the African sand thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Chrysocoma scoparia is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often used in traditional medicine.
