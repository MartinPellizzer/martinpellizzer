Coelogyne guibertiae

Coelogyne guibertiae, commonly known as the Guibert's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb with a single, fleshy leaf and produces a tall spike bearing numerous fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warmer months, and it is appreciated for its ornamental value.

Coelogyne guibertiae is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
