Campanula bordesiana subsp. tibestica

Campanula bordesiana subsp.

tibestica, commonly known as the Tibesti bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibesti Mountains in Chad. It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers, which typically range in color from blue to purple, and its upright, herbaceous growth habit.

The plant thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand harsh climatic conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss in its dry habitat.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and potential medicinal properties.
