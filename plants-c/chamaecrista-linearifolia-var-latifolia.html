Chamaecrista linearifolia var. latifolia

Chamaecrista linearifolia var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved poinsettia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and clusters of small, bright red bracts that resemble flowers. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its unique foliage and seasonal color changes make it a notable species in botanical gardens and landscapes.
