Campanula carpatica var. hendersonii

Campanula carpatica var. hendersonii, commonly known as the Henderson's bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it a versatile choice for rock gardens or borders.
