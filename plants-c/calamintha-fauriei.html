Calamintha fauriei

Calamintha fauriei, commonly known as Faurie's catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are typically blue to purple, arranged in whorls along the stems, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Calamintha fauriei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
