Ceanothus mogollonicus

Ceanothus mogollonicus, commonly known as the Mogollon ceanothus, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays an important role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Ceanothus mogollonicus is also valued for its ornamental appeal in landscaping.
