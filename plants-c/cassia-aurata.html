Cassia aurata

Cassia aurata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that resemble small showers, hence its common name. The plant produces oval, dark green leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia aurata is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.
