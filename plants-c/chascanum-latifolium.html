Chascanum latifolium

Chascanum latifolium, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Chascanum latifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
