Centaurea cariensis subsp. cariensis

Centaurea cariensis subsp.

cariensis, commonly known as the Carian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and open habitats.

The plant features slender, branching stems and produces bright yellow to orange flowers arranged in composite heads. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy surface.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in supporting local pollinator populations.
