Cirsium townsendii

Cirsium townsendii, commonly known as Townsend's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as meadows and slopes. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered with stiff, bristly hairs.

The flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
