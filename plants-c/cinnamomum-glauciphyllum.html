Cinnamomum glauciphyllum

Cinnamomum glauciphyllum, commonly known as the white camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils used in traditional medicine and perfumery. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a waxy, pale green upper surface and a darker underside. It produces small, fragrant flowers and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
