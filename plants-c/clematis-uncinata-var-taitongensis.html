Clematis uncinata var. taitongensis

Clematis uncinata var. taitongensis, commonly known as the Taitong Clematis, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, hooked hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
