Calathea grandis

Calathea grandis, commonly known as the giant calathea, is a striking tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length, often with intricate patterns and vibrant coloration. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is prized for its ornamental value in shaded indoor environments. Its foliage displays a unique ability to change color in response to light, a phenomenon known as nyctinasty.

Calathea grandis requires high humidity and indirect light to thrive, making it a popular choice for tropical plant enthusiasts.
