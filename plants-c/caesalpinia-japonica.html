Caesalpinia japonica

Caesalpinia japonica, commonly known as the Japanese flame tree, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red, flame-like flowers that appear in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves composed of multiple oval-shaped leaflets. It produces long, flat pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Caesalpinia japonica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
