Corynandra

Corynandra, commonly known as the African daisy, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Africa and consists of several species, including Corynandra corymbosa and Corynandra paniculata. These plants are characterized by their showy, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white or pale pink petals. They typically grow in open, sunny habitats and are often found in grasslands and along riverbanks.

Corynandra species are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens.
