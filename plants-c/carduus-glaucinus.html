Carduus glaucinus

Carduus glaucinus, commonly known as the blue thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features a robust, upright stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, covered in dense, blue-gray hairs. Its leaves are deeply lobed, with spiny margins, and the plant produces purple to blue flowers in late summer. The plant is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
