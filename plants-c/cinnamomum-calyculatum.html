Cinnamomum calyculatum

Cinnamomum calyculatum, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or small-leaved cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, cinnamon-scented bark and elliptical, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that contain smooth, oval seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its aromatic properties, it is valued in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
