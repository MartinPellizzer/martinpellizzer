Capparis sclerophylla

Capparis sclerophylla, commonly known as the coastal caper, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, fleshy leaves that are adapted to withstand salt spray and sandy soils. The plant produces clusters of white or pink flowers, which are followed by small, edible berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments.

Capparis sclerophylla is an important component of coastal ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various native wildlife.
