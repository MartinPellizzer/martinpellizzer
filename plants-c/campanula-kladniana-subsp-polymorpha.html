Campanula kladniana subsp. polymorpha

Campanula kladniana subsp.

polymorpha, commonly known as the Kladnica bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Central Europe. It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and upright.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
