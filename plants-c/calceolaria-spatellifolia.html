Calceolaria spatellifolia

Calceolaria spatellifolia, commonly known as the Spatulate Calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, pouch-like flowers that resemble small cups. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves, and it produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow.

Calceolaria spatellifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
