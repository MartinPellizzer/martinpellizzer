Camellia bolovenensis

Camellia bolovenensis, commonly known as the Boloven camellia, is a rare species native to the mountainous regions of Laos.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments within tropical and subtropical forests. Its leaves are broad and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms.

Camellia bolovenensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
