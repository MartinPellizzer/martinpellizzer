Calliprena cernua

Calliprena cernua, commonly known as the drooping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that hang downward. The flowers are typically pale blue or violet in color, and they bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
