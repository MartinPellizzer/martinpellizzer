Cerasus × tschonoskii var. pseudoverecunda

Cerasus × tschonoskii var. pseudoverecunda, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or sakura, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, preceding the foliage. The plant has simple, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a key species in the Prunus genus, valued for both its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
