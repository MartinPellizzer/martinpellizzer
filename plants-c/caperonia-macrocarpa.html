Caperonia macrocarpa

Caperonia macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited caper, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces distinctive, fleshy, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
