Crataegus coccinea var. rotundifolia

Crataegus coccinea var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the red-flowering crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, apple-like fruits and showy pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically oval or rounded, with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, red berries in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display.
