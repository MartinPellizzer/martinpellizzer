Cyathea elongata

Cyathea elongata, commonly known as the long-stemmed tree fern, is a member of the Cyatheaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, pinnate fronds. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Pacific, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a distinctive root system that helps anchor it in moist soil.

Its slow growth rate makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
