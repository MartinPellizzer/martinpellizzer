Chrysanthemum integrifolium

Chrysanthemum integrifolium, commonly known as the smoothleaf chrysanthemum, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, serrated leaves and vibrant yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal. It produces small, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Chrysanthemum integrifolium is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic value and historical use in traditional medicine.
