Cinnamosma macrocarpa

Cinnamosma macrocarpa, commonly known as the broad-leafed cinnamosma, is a tropical tree native to the wetlands of Queensland, Australia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by woody, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or riparian habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
