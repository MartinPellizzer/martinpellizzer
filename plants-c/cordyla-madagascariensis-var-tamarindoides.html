Cordyla madagascariensis var. tamarindoides

Cordyla madagascariensis var. tamarindoides, commonly known as the Madagascar tamarind tree, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, palmate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, woody pods containing edible seeds.
