Crataegus neobushii

Crataegus neobushii, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but typically used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along riverbanks.

Crataegus neobushii is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical medicinal uses.
