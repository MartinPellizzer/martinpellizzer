Carex nutans var. major

Carex nutans var. major, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features grass-like leaves that are typically wider and more robust compared to other Carex species. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered in a loose panicle.

This variety is often found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
