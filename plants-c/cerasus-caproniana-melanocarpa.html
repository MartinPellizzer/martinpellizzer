Cerasus caproniana melanocarpa

Cerasus caproniana melanocarpa, commonly known as the black cherry or Prunus caproniana, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern United States and is characterized by its small, dark purple to black fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. Its flowers are white to pink and appear in clusters, followed by the development of its edible drupes.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
