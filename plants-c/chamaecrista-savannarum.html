Chamaecrista savannarum

Chamaecrista savannarum, commonly known as the Carolina goldenrod, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, often found in open habitats such as roadsides and disturbed areas. The plant features opposite, linear leaves and produces small, yellow to orange flowers in clusters. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds.

It is adapted to thrive in full sun and is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
