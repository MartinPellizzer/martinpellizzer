Cirsium sairamense

Cirsium sairamense, commonly known as the Sairam thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid habitats, showcasing adaptations to harsh environmental conditions. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for certain insect species.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
