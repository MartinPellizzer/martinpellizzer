Chrysopsis decumbens

Chrysopsis decumbens, commonly known as the decumbent goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial that typically reaches heights of less than 10 centimeters. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are usually gray-green, covered in fine hairs, and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Chrysopsis decumbens is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
