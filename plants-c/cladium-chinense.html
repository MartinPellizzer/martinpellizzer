Cladium chinense

Cladium chinense, commonly known as the Chinese sawgrass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and broad, blade-like leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting aquatic ecosystems. Its flowering spikes produce small, greenish-white flowers that are pollinated by wind.

Cladium chinense is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to various parts of the world for landscaping purposes.
