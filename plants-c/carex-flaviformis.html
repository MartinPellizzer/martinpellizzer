Carex flaviformis

Carex flaviformis, commonly known as the yellow-flowered sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on tall, slender inflorescences. Carex flaviformis thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
