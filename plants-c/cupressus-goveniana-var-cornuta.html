Cupressus goveniana var. cornuta

Cupressus goveniana var. cornuta, commonly known as the Sierra Madre cypress, is a gymnosperm native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, columnar form and scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the Cupressaceae family. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing protection against wildfires and drought.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in arid and semi-arid landscapes.
