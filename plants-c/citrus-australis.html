Citrus australis

Citrus australis, commonly known as the Australian lemon or finger lime, is a small evergreen shrub native to Australia.

It produces unique, elongated fruits that resemble fingers, containing translucent, jelly-like seeds. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits. Its leaves are dark green and glossy, while the flowers are fragrant and white.

The fruit's juice is highly acidic and has a tart, citrusy flavor, making it popular in culinary applications.
