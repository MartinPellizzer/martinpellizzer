Cactus foersteri

Cactus foersteri, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its globular shape and bright yellow spines that give it its common name. The plant has a thick, water-storing stem that helps it survive in arid environments. Its flowers, which are typically yellow or orange, bloom in the spring and are relatively short-lived.

Cactus foersteri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
