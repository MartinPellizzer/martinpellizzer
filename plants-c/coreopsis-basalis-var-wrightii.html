Coreopsis basalis var. wrightii

Coreopsis basalis var. wrightii, commonly known as Wright's coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are composed of ray florets and disk florets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
