Carex lemmonii

Carex lemmonii, commonly known as Lemmon's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This species typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or mountainous areas. Its leaves are grass-like, slender, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Carex lemmonii produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.
