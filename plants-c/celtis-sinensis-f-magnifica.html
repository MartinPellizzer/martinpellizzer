Celtis sinensis f. magnifica

Celtis sinensis f. magnifica, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry or large-fruited hackberry, is a cultivar of the hackberry tree native to China.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are typically smooth and dark green on top with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by edible, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and fruit.

This cultivar is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and resistance to urban environmental conditions.
