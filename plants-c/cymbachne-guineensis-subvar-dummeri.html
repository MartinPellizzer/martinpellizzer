Cymbachne guineensis subvar. dummeri

Cymbachne guineensis subvar. dummeri, commonly known as the African grass or Guinea grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions.

This species is often used for forage and soil stabilization in agroecosystems.
