Cineraria taraxacifolia

Cineraria taraxacifolia, commonly known as the dandelion cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its rosette of basal leaves that are hairy and lobed, resembling those of the dandelion. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy seed heads that are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in rocky or disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
