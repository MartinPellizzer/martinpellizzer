Commelina tuberosa var. nana

Commelina tuberosa var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf dayflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, bright blue or violet flowers with white markings, typically appearing in clusters. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces tubers as a storage organ, which can be used for propagation.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
