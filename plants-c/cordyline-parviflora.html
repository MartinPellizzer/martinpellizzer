Cordyline parviflora

Cordyline parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered cordyline, is a striking tropical plant native to New Zealand.

It features a tall, unbranched stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers at the top of the stem, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and indoor spaces.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its unique foliage and seasonal flowering.
