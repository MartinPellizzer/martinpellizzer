Carex contigua f. submonostachya

Carex contigua f. submonostachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a variant of the Carex genus characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit.

It is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, often found in wet or moist soils such as marshes, stream banks, and woodland edges. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated, a common reproductive strategy among sedges. Its rhizomatous growth allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies that stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.

The botanical features of Carex contigua f. submonostachya make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
