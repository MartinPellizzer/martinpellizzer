Corydalis ochotensis var. pedunculata

Corydalis ochotensis var. pedunculata, commonly known as the Siberian Corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant produces capsule fruits containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
