Cinchona calisaya var. microcarpa

Cinchona calisaya var. microcarpa, commonly known as the Yungas cinchona, is a species native to the humid forests of the Yungas region in Bolivia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits. It is part of the Rubiaceae family and is known for its historical use in the production of quinine.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
