Chusquea bambusoides subsp. oxylepis

Chusquea bambusoides subsp.

oxylepis, commonly known as the Andean bamboo, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is native to the Andes region of South America. It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that resemble bamboo, often reaching heights of up to 6 meters.

The plant produces inflorescences that are typically panicle-shaped, with small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in moist, temperate climates and is often found in montane forests.

Its structure and growth habit make it an important component of the Andean ecosystem.
