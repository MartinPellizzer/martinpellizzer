Chamaecytisus leiocarpus var. subleiocarpus

Chamaecytisus leiocarpus var. subleiocarpus, commonly known as the silverbark broom, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, wiry stems that are covered in a fine, silvery-gray pubescence. The leaves are small, elliptical, and have a leathery texture with a bluish-green hue. It produces yellow flowers in terminal clusters, which bloom in early spring.

The plant is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
