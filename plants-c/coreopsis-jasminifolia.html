Coreopsis jasminifolia

Coreopsis jasminifolia, commonly known as the jasmine coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and daisy-like flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces bright yellow to orange blooms that are often tinged with red or brown at the base. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, ornamental plants.

Coreopsis jasminifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
