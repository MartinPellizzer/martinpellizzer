Camellia japonica subsp. hortensis

Camellia japonica subsp.

hortensis, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan. It is characterized by its broad, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath.

The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It prefers well-drained, acidic soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This subspecies is valued for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal in garden settings.
