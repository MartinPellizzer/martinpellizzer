Chenopodium ambrosioides var. integrifolium

Chenopodium ambrosioides var. integrifolium, commonly known as wild spinach or lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually smooth or slightly hairy. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas such as fields and roadsides.

This plant is edible, with young leaves that can be cooked and consumed like spinach.
