Crataegus sanguinea var. schroederi

Crataegus sanguinea var. schroederi, commonly known as the Schröder’s hawthorn, is a variant of the common hawthorn native to parts of Europe.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 8 meters. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and showy pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that ripen in the fall and are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

This variety is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and along streams, thriving in well-drained soils.
