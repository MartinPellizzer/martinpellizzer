Carex rara var. biwensis

Carex rara var. biwensis, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological restoration and wetland conservation efforts.
