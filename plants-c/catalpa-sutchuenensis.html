Catalpa sutchuenensis

Catalpa sutchuenensis, commonly known as the Chinese catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The tree produces long, bean-like pods that contain sticky, inedible seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

Catalpa sutchuenensis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
