Cephalaria humilis

Cephalaria humilis, commonly known as the common cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges. The plant features a slender, erect stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are small, clustered in loose panicles, and are typically pale blue to purple in color.

Cephalaria humilis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and modest floral display.
