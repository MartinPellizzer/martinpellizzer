Centaurea bornmuelleri

Centaurea bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Bornmueller's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and branching.

Centaurea bornmuelleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
