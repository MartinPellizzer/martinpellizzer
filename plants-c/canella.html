Canella

Canella, commonly known as allspice, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown berries that contain numerous seeds. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that attract pollinators like bees and birds. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and have a subtle spicy scent.

The dried berries are widely used as a spice in cooking, particularly in Caribbean and Latin American cuisines.
