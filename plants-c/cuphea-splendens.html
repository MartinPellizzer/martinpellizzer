Cuphea splendens

Cuphea splendens, commonly known as the flame flower, is a strikingly colorful plant native to Central America.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-draining soil.

Cuphea splendens is often grown as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
