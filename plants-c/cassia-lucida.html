Cassia lucida

Cassia lucida, commonly known as golden cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain flat, winged seeds. Cassia lucida thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical features make it both visually striking and ecologically beneficial in its native habitats.
