Convolvulus arvensis f. auriculatus

Convolvulus arvensis f. auriculatus, commonly known as the auriculate field bindweed, is a variant of the common field bindweed.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant is perennial and typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. Its stems are twining and can become invasive, often forming dense mats.

The auriculate form is distinguished by the presence of auricles—small ear-like lobes—at the base of the leaves.
