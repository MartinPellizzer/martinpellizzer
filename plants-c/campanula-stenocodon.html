Campanula stenocodon

Campanula stenocodon, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
