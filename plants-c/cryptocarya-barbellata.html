Cryptocarya barbellata

Cryptocarya barbellata, commonly known as the smooth-leafed sandstone fig, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Australia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocados, and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, fleshy berries that are an important food source for native wildlife. It typically grows in coastal heathlands and sclerophyll forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
