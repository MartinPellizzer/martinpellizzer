Cordia lasiocalyx

Cordia lasiocalyx, commonly known as the rough-leaved cordia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, dark green, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Cordia lasiocalyx is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
