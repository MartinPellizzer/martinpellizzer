Cucurbita pepo var. medullosa

Cucurbita pepo var. medullosa, commonly known as the giant pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is characterized by its large, round, orange-colored fruits.

This variety is native to Central and South America and is cultivated for its impressive size and high sugar content. The plant produces broad, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers, with both male and female flowers present on the same plant. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires a long growing season to reach its full potential.

The fruit's thick, rugged rind and sweet, dense flesh make it a popular choice for carving and culinary use.
