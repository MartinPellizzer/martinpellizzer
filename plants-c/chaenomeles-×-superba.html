Chaenomeles × superba

Chaenomeles × superba, commonly known as Japanese flowering quince, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring, typically in shades of pink, red, or white. The plant's fruit is a fleshy, yellow or orange pome that ripens in late summer. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its winter bark and spring blooms.

The species is a hybrid between Chaenomeles speciosa and Chaenomeles cathayensis.
