Carex obtusata subf. cuspidata

Carex obtusata subf.

cuspidata, commonly known as the blunt-scaled sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of small, three-ranked flowers, and it produces triangular, three-sided berries.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
