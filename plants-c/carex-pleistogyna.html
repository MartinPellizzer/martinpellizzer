Carex pleistogyna

Carex pleistogyna, commonly known as the pleistogyna sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Native to certain regions of Europe, it typically grows in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, which are followed by triangular, three-sided berries.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
