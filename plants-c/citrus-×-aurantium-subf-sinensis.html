Citrus × aurantium subf. sinensis

Citrus × aurantium subf.

sinensis, commonly known as the mandarin orange, is a hybrid citrus species native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, sweet, and segmented fruits with a thin, loose rind.

The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, featuring glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is rich in vitamin C and is widely cultivated for both culinary and medicinal uses. The subsp.

sinensis is particularly noted for its cold hardiness compared to other citrus varieties.
