Cissus crinita

Cissus crinita, commonly known as the curly ivy or hairleaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and distinctive, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that often have a curling edge. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Cissus crinita is relatively low maintenance and suitable for indoor cultivation.
