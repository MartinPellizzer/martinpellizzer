Clusia cajamarcensis

Clusia cajamarcensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian glorybower, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family, which includes plants known for their showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Clusia cajamarcensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
