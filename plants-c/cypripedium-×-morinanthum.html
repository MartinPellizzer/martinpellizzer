Cypripedium × morinanthum

Cypripedium × morinanthum, commonly known as the Morin's lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers with a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch, which is a key feature of the Cypripedium genus. The plant typically grows in cool, shaded forest environments, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink and yellow, are pollinated by specific insect species, primarily bees.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
