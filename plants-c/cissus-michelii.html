Cissus michelii

Cissus michelii, commonly known as the rock fig or heartleaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and ability to adhere to surfaces using aerial roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for vertical gardening and indoor plants.
