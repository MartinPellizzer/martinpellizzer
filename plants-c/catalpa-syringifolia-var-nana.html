Catalpa syringifolia var. nana

Catalpa syringifolia var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf catalpa, is a compact variety of the catalpa tree.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in late spring, often in shades of white or pale yellow. It is native to eastern North America and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
