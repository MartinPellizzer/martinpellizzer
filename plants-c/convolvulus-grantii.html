Convolvulus grantii

Convolvulus grantii, commonly known as the Grant's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, colorful flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has slender, trailing stems that often climb or sprawl along the ground. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers come in shades of red, yellow, and orange.

Convolvulus grantii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
