Centaurea epirota

Centaurea epirota, commonly known as the Epirus centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its bright blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea epirota is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
