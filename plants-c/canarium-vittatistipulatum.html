Canarium vittatistipulatum

Canarium vittatistipulatum, commonly known as the golden apple tree, is a tropical plant belonging to the Burseraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Malaysia, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The tree is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green hue. Its most notable feature is the production of small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic resin, which has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of incense.
