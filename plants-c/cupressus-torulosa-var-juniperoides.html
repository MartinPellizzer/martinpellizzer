Cupressus torulosa var. juniperoides

Cupressus torulosa var. juniperoides, commonly known as the Mexican cypress, is a variant of the Torulosa cypress.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Mexico, characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and scale-like foliage. The plant has small, round cones that mature to a reddish-brown color, and its leaves are dark green and needle-like. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

This variety is also valued for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and air pollution.
