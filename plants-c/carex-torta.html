Carex torta

Carex torta, commonly known as the twisted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often twisted or spiraled, giving it its common name. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to reddish and appear in late summer.

Carex torta plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
