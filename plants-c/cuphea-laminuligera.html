Cuphea laminuligera

Cuphea laminuligera, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America and northern South America.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by hummingbirds and bees, making it an important species in pollinator habitats.

Cuphea laminuligera is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
