Capparis murrayana

Capparis murrayana, commonly known as the Murray's caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climates. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems.
