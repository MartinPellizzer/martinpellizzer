Carex spicatopaniculata

Carex spicatopaniculata, commonly known as the panicled sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with red or purple. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade. The plant is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native, low-maintenance plants.
