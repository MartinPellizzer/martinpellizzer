Cousinia elymaea

Cousinia elymaea, commonly known as the Elymian cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as steppes and rocky soils, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, while the flowers are small and yellowish-white in color.

Cousinia elymaea is often found in open habitats and can thrive in poor soil conditions.
