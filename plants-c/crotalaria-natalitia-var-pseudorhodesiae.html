Crotalaria natalitia var. pseudorhodesiae

Crotalaria natalitia var. pseudorhodesiae, commonly known as the Natal crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and produces clusters of yellow, spurred flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and its pods are elongated and contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
