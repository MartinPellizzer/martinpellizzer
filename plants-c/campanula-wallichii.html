Campanula wallichii

Campanula wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

This species is valued for its beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
