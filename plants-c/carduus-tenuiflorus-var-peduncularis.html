Carduus tenuiflorus var. peduncularis

Carduus tenuiflorus var. peduncularis, commonly known as the slender-flowered thistle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. It has a deep taproot system, allowing it to survive in poor soil conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
