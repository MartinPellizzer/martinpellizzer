Cyphia subscandens

Cyphia subscandens, commonly known as the climbing cyphia, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a vine-like plant characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The flowers are typically small and clustered, often in inflorescences that are either axillary or terminal. It produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

This plant is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests.
