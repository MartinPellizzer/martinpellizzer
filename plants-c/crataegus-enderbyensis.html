Crataegus enderbyensis

Crataegus enderbyensis, commonly known as Enderby's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pale pink flowers in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in native habitats.
