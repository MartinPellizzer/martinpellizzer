Cinnamomum chana

Cinnamomum chana, commonly known as Indian cinnamon or Ceylon cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to southern India.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The bark, which is the source of its essential oil, contains high levels of cinnamic acid and essential oils. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

The plant's botanical features make it valuable in both traditional medicine and the fragrance industry.
