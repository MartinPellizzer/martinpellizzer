Chordifex leucoblepharus

Chordifex leucoblepharus, commonly known as the white-eyed feather grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It features slender, arching stems that produce fluffy, white plumes, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its delicate aesthetic.

Chordifex leucoblepharus is drought-tolerant and prefers full sun to partial shade.
