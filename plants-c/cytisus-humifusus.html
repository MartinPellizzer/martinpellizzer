Cytisus humifusus

Cytisus humifusus, commonly known as the creeping broom, is a low-growing shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to its habitat. The plant has slender, woody stems that often lie close to the ground, forming dense mats. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Cytisus humifusus thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
