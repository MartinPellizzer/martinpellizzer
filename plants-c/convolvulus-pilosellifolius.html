Convolvulus pilosellifolius

Convolvulus pilosellifolius, commonly known as the hairyleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The leaves are hairy and ovate, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.
