Cerastium ciliatulum

Cerastium ciliatulum, commonly known as the hairy mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate stems and small, hairy leaves. The flowers are typically white or pink, with five petals and numerous stamens, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and rocky areas.

This plant is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for small invertebrates.
