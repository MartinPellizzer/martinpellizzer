Chrysanthemum zawadzkii subsp. zawadzkii

Chrysanthemum zawadzkii subsp.

zawadzkii, commonly known as the Zawadzki's chrysanthemum, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and a variety of flower forms, including single, semi-coupe, and full pompon types.

The plant typically produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in the autumn season. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in floral arrangements and landscape design.
