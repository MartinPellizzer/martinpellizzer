Carduus dentatus

Carduus dentatus, commonly known as the serrated thistle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a serrated edge, which serves as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
