Coccoceras muticum var. pedicellatum

Coccoceras muticum var. pedicellatum, commonly known as the red seaweed or *Gracilaria pedicellata*, is a marine algae belonging to the family Gracilariaceae.

It is characterized by its branching, filamentous structure and a reddish to purplish coloration due to the presence of phycoerythrin. The plant grows in shallow coastal waters, often attached to rocky substrates or other marine vegetation. It plays an important role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.

Its gelatinous texture and high agar content make it valuable for industrial and commercial applications.
