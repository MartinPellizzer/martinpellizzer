Convolvulus lineatus var. pentapetaloides

Convolvulus lineatus var. pentapetaloides, commonly known as the five-petaled morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy margin. The flowers are typically pale blue to violet, with five petals, giving it its common name. It blooms during the warmer months, producing showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil.
