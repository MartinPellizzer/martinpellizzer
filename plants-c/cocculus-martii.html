Cocculus martii

Cocculus martii, commonly known as the Indian devil's-bit, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that contain one or two seeds. Its roots and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Cocculus martii is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and shrublands.
