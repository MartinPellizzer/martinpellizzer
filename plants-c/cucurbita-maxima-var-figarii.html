Cucurbita maxima var. figarii

Cucurbita maxima var. figarii, commonly known as the figleaf gourd, is a cultivated variety of squash native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble fig leaves, hence its common name. The plant produces elongated, ribbed fruits that can grow up to 30 inches in length. It thrives in warm climates and requires full sun and well-drained soil.

This variety is often grown for ornamental purposes and can also be used in culinary applications.
