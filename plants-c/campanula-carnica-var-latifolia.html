Campanula carnica var. latifolia

Campanula carnica var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It features basal rosettes of broad, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically hairy and have a bluish-green hue. The plant produces upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
