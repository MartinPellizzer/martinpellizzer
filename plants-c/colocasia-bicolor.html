Colocasia bicolor

Colocasia bicolor, commonly known as the Variegated Elephant Ear, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that display a striking pattern of green, white, and sometimes pink or purple hues. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires rich, well-draining soil to flourish. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its dramatic visual impact.

Colocasia bicolor is a member of the Araceae family, which includes other plants like taro and philodendrons.
