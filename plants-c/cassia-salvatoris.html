Cassia salvatoris

Cassia salvatoris, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in dense, panicle-like clusters. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia salvatoris is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
