Castilleja applegatei subsp. pinetorum

Castilleja applegatei subsp.

pinetorum, commonly known as the Applegate's paintbrush, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is a hemiparasitic species, meaning it partially relies on other plants for water and nutrients through its root system.

The plant features showy, bright red to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It typically grows in coniferous forests and is often found in association with lodgepole pine.

Its botanical structure includes slender, erect stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves.
