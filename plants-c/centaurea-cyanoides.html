Centaurea cyanoides

Centaurea cyanoides, commonly known as blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Centaurea cyanoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
