Centaurea pseudosinaica

Centaurea pseudosinaica, commonly known as the Eastern Centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in sunny environments. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disc.

Centaurea pseudosinaica is often found in scrublands and grassy slopes, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions.
