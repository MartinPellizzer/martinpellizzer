Calystegia macrostegia subsp. arida

Calystegia macrostegia subsp.

arida, commonly known as the desert bindweed, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a trailing perennial vine with hairy, slender stems that often root at the nodes.

The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming from summer to early fall. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often pubescent, adapting to the harsh conditions of its environment.

The species is known for its resilience, thriving in poor soils and with minimal water.
