Cucurbita bononiensis

Cucurbita bononiensis, commonly known as the Italian pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a herbaceous vine that produces large, round, orange-colored fruits with a thick, ribbed rind. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and typically grows in warm climates. It is native to southern Europe and has been cultivated for its edible fruits.

The botanical features of Cucurbita bononiensis make it well-suited for both ornamental and culinary use.
