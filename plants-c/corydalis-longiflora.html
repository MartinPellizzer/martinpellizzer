Corydalis longiflora

Corydalis longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered corydalis, is a flowering plant in the family Fumariaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Asia, including China, Korea, and Japan. The plant features slender, hairy stems and produces clusters of delicate, pinkish-purple flowers with two prominent upper petals. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight reddish tinge.

Corydalis longiflora is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
