Coleus crispipilus

Coleus crispipilus, commonly known as the crinkled coleus, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, crinkled leaves that come in a variety of colors, including red, purple, and green. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.

Its leaves are also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
