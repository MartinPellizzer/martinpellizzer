Cuviera africana

Cuviera africana, commonly known as African cup-of-sun, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
