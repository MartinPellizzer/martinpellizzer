Cleistanthus polystachyus subsp. milleri

Cleistanthus polystachyus subsp.

milleri, commonly known as the miller's cleistanthus, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which are typically borne in clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
