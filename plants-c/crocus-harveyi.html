Crocus harveyi

Crocus harveyi, commonly known as the Harvey's crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its striking violet or blue flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, with showy, cup-shaped flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it grows from corms, which are underground storage organs.

Crocus harveyi is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively easy to care for.
