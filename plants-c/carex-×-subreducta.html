Carex × subreducta

Carex × subreducta, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Carex rosea and Carex subulata, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are grass-like, with a dense, tufted growth habit, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

Carex × subreducta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
