Coffea canephora var. nganda

Coffea canephora var. nganda, commonly known as Nganda coffee, is a variety of robusta coffee native to Central Africa.

It is characterized by its medium-sized, oval-shaped beans and a moderate level of caffeine compared to other robusta varieties. The plant typically grows in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil. It produces dark green, serrated leaves and small white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries containing the seeds.

This variety is valued for its strong flavor and is often used in blends to enhance body and aroma.
