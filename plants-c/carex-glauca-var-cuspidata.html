Carex glauca var. cuspidata

Carex glauca var. cuspidata, commonly known as blue-green sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically found in dense clusters. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in water management and habitat support.
