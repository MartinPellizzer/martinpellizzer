Chrysanthemum monspeliense

Chrysanthemum monspeliense, commonly known as the French chrysanthemum, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing yellow to white flowers. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value and hardiness make it a popular choice among gardeners in temperate climates.
