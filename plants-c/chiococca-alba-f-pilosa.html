Chiococca alba f. pilosa

Chiococca alba f. pilosa, commonly known as the woolly white coca, is a variant of the white coca plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments with cool, humid conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and have psychoactive properties due to the presence of alkaloids.

The botanical features of this plant make it unique within the Erythroxylaceae family.
