Campanula cochleariifolia var. foudrasii

Campanula cochleariifolia var. foudrasii, commonly known as the Foudras' bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
