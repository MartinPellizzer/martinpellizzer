Cirsium horridulum f. atropurpureum

Cirsium horridulum f. atropurpureum, commonly known as the purplestem thistle, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and large, showy purple to reddish-purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped with coarse teeth and are covered in fine, whitish hairs.

The inflorescence consists of a single, terminal purple flower head surrounded by bracts, which are often tinged with red or purple.
