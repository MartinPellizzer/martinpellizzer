Condalia megacarpa

Condalia megacarpa, commonly known as the giant false dandelion, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that are clustered in spherical heads, followed by distinctive, inflated seed pods. Its leaves and stems are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
