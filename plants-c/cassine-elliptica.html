Cassine elliptica

Cassine elliptica, commonly known as the narrow-leaf cassine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on peduncles. Cassine elliptica is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

Its foliage is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
