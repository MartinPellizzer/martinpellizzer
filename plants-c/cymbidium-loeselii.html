Cymbidium loeselii

Cymbidium loeselii, commonly known as the Loesel's orchid, is a species of orchid native to the tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elegant, long-lasting flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant features strap-like leaves and produces inflorescences with multiple blooms, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Cymbidium loeselii is also valued for its fragrance and is often cultivated for both its aesthetic and aromatic qualities.
