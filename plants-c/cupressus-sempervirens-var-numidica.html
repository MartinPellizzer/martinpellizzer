Cupressus sempervirens var. numidica

Cupressus sempervirens var. numidica, commonly known as the Moroccan cypress, is a compact, evergreen conifer native to the Atlas Mountains of North Africa.

It features a narrow, pyramid-shaped crown with scale-like leaves that are a rich, dark green. The plant produces small, spherical cones that are typically reddish-brown in color. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and can tolerate coastal conditions.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
