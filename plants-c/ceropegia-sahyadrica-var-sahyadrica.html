Ceropegia sahyadrica var. sahyadrica

Ceropegia sahyadrica var. sahyadrica, commonly known as the Sahyadri moonvine, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a climbing plant with thin, wiry stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. The flowers are small, tubular, and typically white or pale green, with a unique structure that resembles a moon or a small lantern. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests and rocky areas.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage and delicate blooms.
