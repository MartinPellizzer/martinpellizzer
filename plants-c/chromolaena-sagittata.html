Chromolaena sagittata

Chromolaena sagittata, commonly known as arrowleaf false hibiscus, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, arrow-shaped leaves that are typically green with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters during the warm seasons. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

Chromolaena sagittata is also known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
