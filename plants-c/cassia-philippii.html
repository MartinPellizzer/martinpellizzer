Cassia philippii

Cassia philippii, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain flat, seed-filled pods. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Cassia philippii is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
