Canarium tomentosum var. flavum

Canarium tomentosum var. flavum, commonly known as the yellow candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark brown bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, oval-shaped fruits containing a single seed. The fruit's outer layer is covered with a thick, yellowish pulp that is rich in oils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the oil extracted from its seeds.
