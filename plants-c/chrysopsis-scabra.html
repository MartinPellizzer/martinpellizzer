Chrysopsis scabra

Chrysopsis scabra, commonly known as the roughleaf chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features grayish-green, rough-textured leaves and produces yellow to orange ray flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

Chrysopsis scabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
