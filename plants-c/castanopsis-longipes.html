Castanopsis longipes

Castanopsis longipes, commonly known as the long-fruited talaud, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and beeches, and is characterized by its hard, woody fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical forests, reaching heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside.

The tree produces small, clustered flowers followed by elongated, spiny fruits that contain one or two seeds.
