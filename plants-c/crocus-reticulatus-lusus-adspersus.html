Crocus reticulatus lusus adspersus

Crocus reticulatus lusus adspersus, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive reticulated pattern on its petals. The plant typically grows to a height of about 10-15 centimeters and blooms in early spring. Its bulbs are adapted to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun conditions.

It is valued for both its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
