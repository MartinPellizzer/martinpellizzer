Cosmanthus pectinatus

Cosmanthus pectinatus, commonly known as the silky cosmanthus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in a fine, silky hair.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
