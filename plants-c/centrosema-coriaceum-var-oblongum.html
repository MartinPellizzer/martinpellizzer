Centrosema coriaceum var. oblongum

Centrosema coriaceum var. oblongum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf velvet bean, is a perennial legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features trifoliate leaves with ovate to elliptic leaflets and produces small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and oblong, stipitate pods that contain several seeds. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

This species is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and use in forage and soil improvement.
