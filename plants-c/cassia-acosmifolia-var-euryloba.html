Cassia acosmifolia var. euryloba

Cassia acosmifolia var. euryloba, commonly known as the golden cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are oval and slightly serrated.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
