Cupressus disticha var. imbricaria

Cupressus disticha var. imbricaria, commonly known as the Japanese false cypress, is a slow-growing evergreen conifer with a distinctive, fan-shaped crown.

It features scale-like leaves that are tightly packed, giving it a dense, compact appearance. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of the Cupressaceae family. It is native to Japan and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
