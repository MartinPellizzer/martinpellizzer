Commelina communis var. ciliata

Commelina communis var. ciliata, commonly known as the creeping spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped, lanceolate leaves with ciliated margins, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, blue-violet flowers arranged in umbels, which are typically borne on slender stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread via rhizomes make it an effective ground cover in suitable habitats.
