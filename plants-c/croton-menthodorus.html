Croton menthodorus

Croton menthodorus, commonly known as the Indian croton, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by orange or red seed pods. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and unique appearance.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
