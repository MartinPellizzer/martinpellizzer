Chondrostylis bancana

Chondrostylis bancana, commonly known as the banana-leaf fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its large, strap-like fronds that resemble banana leaves. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. The leaves are often used in traditional medicine and horticulture due to their aesthetic appeal and medicinal properties.
