Clematis patens var. louisae

Clematis patens var. louisae, commonly known as the Louisiana clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis patens var. louisae is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
