Canna siamensis

Canna siamensis, commonly known as the Siam canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, colorful, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces tall, upright stems with showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Canna siamensis is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and water features.
