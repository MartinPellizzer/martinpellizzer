Caprifolium dumosum

Caprifolium dumosum, commonly known as the shrub rose, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, thorny growth habit, which provides structural support and protection. The plant produces fragrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

Caprifolium dumosum is well adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade conditions.
