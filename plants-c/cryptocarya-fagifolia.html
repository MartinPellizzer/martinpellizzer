Cryptocarya fagifolia

Cryptocarya fagifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf myrtle, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are often aromatic. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits.

Cryptocarya fagifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
