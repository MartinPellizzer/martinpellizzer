Cephalocroton discolor

Cephalocroton discolor, commonly known as the variable-headed croton, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that vary in color from green to reddish-brown. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive, elongated seed pods. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
