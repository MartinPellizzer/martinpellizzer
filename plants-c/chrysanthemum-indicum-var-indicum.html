Chrysanthemum indicum var. indicum

Chrysanthemum indicum var. indicum, commonly known as the common chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its bushy growth habit and diverse range of flower colors, including yellow, white, and purple. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs.
