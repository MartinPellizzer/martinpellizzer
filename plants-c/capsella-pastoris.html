Capsella pastoris

Capsella pastoris, commonly known as the shepherd's cabbage, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and fields. Its fruit is a short, cylindrical silique that contains several seeds.

Capsella pastoris is an annual plant that is relatively easy to cultivate and is sometimes grown as a wildflower in gardens.
