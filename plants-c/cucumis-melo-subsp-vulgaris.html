Cucumis melo subsp. vulgaris

Cucumis melo subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as the cantaloupe, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family. It is characterized by its rough, netted rind and sweet, fragrant flesh.

The plant is a climbing vine that produces yellow flowers and has palmate leaves. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is typically harvested when the rind turns from green to a characteristic orange or yellow color.
