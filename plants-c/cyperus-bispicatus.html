Cyperus bispicatus

Cyperus bispicatus, commonly known as the two-awned flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Cyperus bispicatus is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
