Cotoneaster wilsonii

Cotoneaster wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth margin and a glossy upper surface.

Cotoneaster wilsonii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
