Cucurbita pyxidaris var. flava

Cucurbita pyxidaris var. flava, commonly known as the yellow hubbard squash, is a variant of the bottle gourd native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its elongated, bottle-shaped fruits that mature to a bright yellow color. The plant is a vigorous vine with large, heart-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its culinary uses and ornamental appeal in gardens.
