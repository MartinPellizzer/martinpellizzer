Cyathea hypotricha

Cyathea hypotricha, commonly known as the mountain tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically in mountainous forests. Its roots form a network that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Cyathea hypotricha plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
