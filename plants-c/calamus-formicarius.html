Calamus formicarius

Calamus formicarius, commonly known as the ant plant, is a climbing monocot native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant is notable for its unique relationship with ants, as it provides shelter and food for various ant species. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color.

Calamus formicarius is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.
