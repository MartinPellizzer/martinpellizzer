Cynoglossum borbonicum

Cynoglossum borbonicum, commonly known as the Sicilian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and showy blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a bluish-gray hue due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

Cynoglossum borbonicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.
