Caralluma caudata var. milleri

Caralluma caudata var. milleri, commonly known as the Indian Fig or Crown of Thorns, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of India and Australia.

It features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are typically spiny and arranged in a rosette pattern, providing protection from herbivores.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its resilience and unique appearance.
