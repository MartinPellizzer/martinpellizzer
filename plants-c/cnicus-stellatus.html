Cnicus stellatus

Cnicus stellatus, commonly known as the starthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and wastelands. It has a compact growth habit with a rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that rises above them.

Cnicus stellatus is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
