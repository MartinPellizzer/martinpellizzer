Campanula mollis var. microphylla

Campanula mollis var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaf bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically ranging in color from blue to purple. The leaves are small, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
