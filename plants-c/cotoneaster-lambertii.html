Cotoneaster lambertii

Cotoneaster lambertii, commonly known as Lambert's cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Cotoneaster lambertii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value, particularly its attractive fall foliage and winter berries.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to a variety of garden settings.
