Conostephium planifolium

Conostephium planifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It features compound leaves with broad, flat leaflets that give it its scientific name. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, which are arranged in cylindrical spikes. It is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to dry environments.

Conostephium planifolium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
