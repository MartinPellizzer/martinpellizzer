Chione sylvicola

Chione sylvicola, commonly known as the mountain snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth texture.

Chione sylvicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
