Columella trifolia

Columella trifolia, commonly known as the three-leaf columella, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Columella trifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
