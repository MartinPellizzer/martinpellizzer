Clematis virginiana f. missouriensis

Clematis virginiana f. missouriensis, commonly known as the Missouri Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to 15 feet in length, often climbing or trailing along fences, trellises, or other structures. The plant produces showy, white to pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer, which are typically borne in clusters at the base of the stems. Its foliage consists of alternate, trifoliate leaves with finely toothed margins, providing a lush green appearance throughout the growing season.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
