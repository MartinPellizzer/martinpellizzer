Capparis callosa

Capparis callosa, commonly known as the rough caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody shrub form and has distinctive, thick, fleshy leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in umbels. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous small, black seeds.

Capparis callosa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
