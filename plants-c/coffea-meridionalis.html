Coffea meridionalis

Coffea meridionalis, commonly known as the Southern coffee plant, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 2 to 3 meters. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries containing two seeds, which are the coffee beans. These beans are used to make coffee and are known for their unique flavor profile.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.
