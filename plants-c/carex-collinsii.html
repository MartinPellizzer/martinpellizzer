Carex collinsii

Carex collinsii, commonly known as Collins' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

Carex collinsii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
