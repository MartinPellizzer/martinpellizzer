Conyza boelckei

Conyza boelckei, commonly known as goatweed, is an annual flowering plant native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in grasslands, roadsides, and agricultural fields. It has small, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its seeds are dispersed by wind.

Conyza boelckei is considered a weed in some regions but also has potential medicinal uses in traditional medicine.
