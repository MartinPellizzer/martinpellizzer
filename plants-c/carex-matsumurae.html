Carex matsumurae

Carex matsumurae, commonly known as the Matsumura's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to East Asia, particularly found in regions of Japan and China. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Carex matsumurae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
