Calantica decaryana

Calantica decaryana, commonly known as the mountain snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of silvery hair.

Calantica decaryana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
