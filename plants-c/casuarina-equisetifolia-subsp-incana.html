Casuarina equisetifolia subsp. incana

Casuarina equisetifolia subsp.

incana, commonly known as the silky casuarina, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its unique feathery foliage, consisting of needle-like leaves arranged in clusters.

The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain woody seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and is often used for dune stabilization due to its salt tolerance.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ornamental contexts.
