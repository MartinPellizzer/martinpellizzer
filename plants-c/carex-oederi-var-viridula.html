Carex oederi var. viridula

Carex oederi var. viridula, commonly known as the Emerald Sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the eastern United States.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
