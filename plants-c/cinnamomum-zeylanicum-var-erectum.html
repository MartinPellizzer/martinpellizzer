Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. erectum

Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. erectum, commonly known as Ceylon cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its aromatic bark, which is harvested for its inner layer, known as the cinnamon bark. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers and oval-shaped leaves with a smooth, dark green surface. The bark contains essential oils and compounds like cinnamaldehyde, which contribute to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for both commercial and traditional uses.
