Cayaponia oppositifolia

Cayaponia oppositifolia, commonly known as the Cayaponia tree, is a species native to Central America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. The leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, often with a leathery texture. It produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, fleshy berries.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional medicinal uses.
