Cassine laevigata

Cassine laevigata, commonly known as the silky hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It has smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that are often slightly hairy underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by spherical, blue-black berries. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
