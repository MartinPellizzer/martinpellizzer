Cyperus dactyliformis var. involutus

Cyperus dactyliformis var. involutus, commonly known as the curled flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often rolled or curled, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wet or muddy habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical structure makes it adaptable to various wetland environments, contributing to its ecological significance.
