Cephaelis botryocephala

Cephaelis botryocephala, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments and is often found in rocky or alpine regions. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by insects, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Cephaelis botryocephala is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
