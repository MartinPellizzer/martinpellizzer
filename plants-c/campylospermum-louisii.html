Campylospermum louisii

Campylospermum louisii, commonly known as the Louisiana flame flower, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a woody stem that becomes more branched with age. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
