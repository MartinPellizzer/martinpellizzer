Carex flava subsp. alpina

Carex flava subsp.

alpina, commonly known as the alpine yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

This subspecies is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous habitats.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
