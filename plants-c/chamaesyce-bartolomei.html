Chamaesyce bartolomei

Chamaesyce bartolomei, commonly known as the Bartolomei's sand mat, is a low-growing annual herb native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features dense, sprawling foliage composed of small, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Its botanical structure reflects its evolutionary adaptations to survive in harsh, desert-like environments.
