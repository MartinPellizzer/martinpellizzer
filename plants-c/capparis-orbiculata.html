Capparis orbiculata

Capparis orbiculata, commonly known as the globe caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, characterized by its thick, woody stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by edible, round, green fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Capparis orbiculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and fruit.
