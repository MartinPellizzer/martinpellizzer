Calanthe rajana

Calanthe rajana, commonly known as the Rajan's calanthe, is a rare and endemic orchid found primarily in the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact, sympodial growth habit and possesses showy, fragrant flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant has elliptical to ovate leaves that are usually arranged in two rows along the stem. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, moist soils of its native habitat.

Calanthe rajana is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a significant role in the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
