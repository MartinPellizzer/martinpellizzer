Cavendishia mexiae

Cavendishia mexiae, commonly known as the Mexican Cavendish, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring spiny structures that provide protection against herbivores and reduce water loss. Its flowers bloom in the spring and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological role in its native habitat.

Cavendishia mexiae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with well-draining soil and full sun exposure.
