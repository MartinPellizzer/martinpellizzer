Crataegus gemmosa

Crataegus gemmosa, commonly known as the gemmosa hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Crataegus gemmosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
