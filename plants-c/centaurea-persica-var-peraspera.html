Centaurea persica var. peraspera

Centaurea persica var. peraspera, commonly known as Persian centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces striking blue to purple flower heads composed of numerous small, ray-like florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its historical use in traditional medicine.
