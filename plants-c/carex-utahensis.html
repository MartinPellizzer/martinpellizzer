Carex utahensis

Carex utahensis, commonly known as Utah sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. Utah sedge typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has triangular stems and produces spike-like inflorescences with small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.
