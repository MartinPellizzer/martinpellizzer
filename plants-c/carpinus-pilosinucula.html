Carpinus pilosinucula

Carpinus pilosinucula, commonly known as the silky chinquapin, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the genus Carpinus, which includes hornbeams and chinkapins, and is characterized by its small, elongated leaves with a silvery underside. The tree produces small, spiny fruits that contain single seeds, a feature typical of the genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

The silky chinquapin is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
