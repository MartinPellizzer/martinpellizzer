Campanula rotundifolia subsp. ruscinonensis

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

ruscinonensis, commonly known as the Ruscinonensis bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its rounded, slightly hairy leaves and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
