Cactus hemisphaericus

Cactus hemisphaericus, commonly known as the globe cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its otherwise desolate appearance. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

This cactus is well adapted to extreme conditions, making it a resilient and iconic symbol of desert ecosystems.
