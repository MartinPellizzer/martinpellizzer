Clidemia candolleana var. schwackeana

Clidemia candolleana var. schwackeana, commonly known as the white mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom throughout the year. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs, giving the plant a velvety texture. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate a range of soil conditions.
