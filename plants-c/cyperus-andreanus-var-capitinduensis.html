Cyperus andreanus var. capitinduensis

Cyperus andreanus var. capitinduensis, commonly known as the Cape grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its tall, grass-like appearance with cylindrical stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes and along riverbanks.

Its botanical structure makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
