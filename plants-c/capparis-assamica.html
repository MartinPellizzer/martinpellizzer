Capparis assamica

Capparis assamica, commonly known as the Assam cucumber or wild cucumber, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Nepal.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, cucumber-like fruits. Capparis assamica thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Capparaceae family.
