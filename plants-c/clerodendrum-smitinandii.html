Clerodendrum smitinandii

Clerodendrum smitinandii, commonly known as the Smitinandii glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
