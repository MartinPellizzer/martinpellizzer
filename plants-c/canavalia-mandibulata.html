Canavalia mandibulata

Canavalia mandibulata, commonly known as the sword bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically pink or purple, resembling those of other Phaseolus species. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are edible when cooked. It is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Canavalia mandibulata is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable crop in sustainable agriculture.
