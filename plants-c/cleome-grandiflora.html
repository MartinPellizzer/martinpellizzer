Cleome grandiflora

Cleome grandiflora, commonly known as the "Spider Flower," is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, branching stems. The plant produces clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Cleome grandiflora thrives in full sun to partial shade and is known for its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
