Catasetum cernuum var. umbrosum

Catasetum cernuum var. umbrosum, commonly known as the dark-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments with well-drained, acidic soil. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its unique pollination mechanism involving specific insect pollinators.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for collectors and botanical gardens.
