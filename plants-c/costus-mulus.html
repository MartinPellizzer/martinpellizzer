Costus mulus

Costus mulus, commonly known as the white costus, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric, and is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features long, narrow leaves and produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Costus mulus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.
