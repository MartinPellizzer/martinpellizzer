Cassia falcinella var. intermedia

Cassia falcinella var. intermedia, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that appear in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets, and it produces long, slender pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate various soil types and its use in traditional medicine.
