Cyathea subglandulosa

Cyathea subglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments. Its rhizome is often covered with small, glandular structures, giving it its scientific name.

Cyathea subglandulosa plays an important role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
