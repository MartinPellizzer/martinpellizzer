Crotalaria oxalidifolia

Crotalaria oxalidifolia, commonly known as the kidneyleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are often mistaken for oxalis species due to their similar appearance. It produces bean-like pods containing several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.

Crotalaria oxalidifolia is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural practices.
