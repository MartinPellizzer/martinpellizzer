Casearia maynacarpa

Casearia maynacarpa, commonly known as the Brazilian casearia, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forest understories and riverbanks.

Its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties make it of interest in horticulture and phytochemistry.
