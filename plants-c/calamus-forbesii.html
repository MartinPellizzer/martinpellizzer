Calamus forbesii

Calamus forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' canescent reed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems that are often covered with a whitish, powdery coating. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth texture and a distinct midrib.

Calamus forbesii produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, typical of the Araceae family.
