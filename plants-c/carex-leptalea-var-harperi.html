Carex leptalea var. harperi

Carex leptalea var. harperi, commonly known as the Harpers' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
