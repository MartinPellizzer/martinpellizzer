Chenopodium × mendelii

Chenopodium × mendelii, commonly known as the hybrid goosefoot, is a species resulting from the cross between Chenopodium album and Chenopodium berlandieri.

It is an annual herbaceous plant characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides. Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines, while its seeds can be processed into a flour-like substance.

The species is notable for its genetic diversity and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
