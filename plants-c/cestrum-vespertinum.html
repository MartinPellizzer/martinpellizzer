Cestrum vespertinum

Cestrum vespertinum, commonly known as the evening glory, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, blooming in the evening. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds, often dispersing them by wind or animals.
