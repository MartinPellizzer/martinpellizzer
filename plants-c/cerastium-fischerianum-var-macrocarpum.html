Cerastium fischerianum var. macrocarpum

Cerastium fischerianum var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-fruited mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features small, spoon-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny white flowers with five petals, which bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a distinctive, inflated capsule that contains numerous small seeds.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.
