Carduus × rechingeri

Carduus × rechingeri, commonly known as the Hungarian thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and large, showy purple flower heads that resemble those of its parent species. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to southern Europe. It is often found in grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it can become invasive in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
