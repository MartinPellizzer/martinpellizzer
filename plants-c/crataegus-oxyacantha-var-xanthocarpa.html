Crataegus oxyacantha var. xanthocarpa

Crataegus oxyacantha var. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited hawthorn, is a variant of the common hawthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, reddish-bark and thorny branches. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with serrated edges, and it produces small, white flowers in spring.

The fruit is distinctive for its bright yellow color, which contrasts with the typical red or orange hues of other hawthorn varieties.
