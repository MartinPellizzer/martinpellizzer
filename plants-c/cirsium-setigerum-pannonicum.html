Cirsium setigerum-pannonicum

Cirsium setigerum-pannonicum, commonly known as the Pannonian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are usually deeply lobed and covered with stiff, prickly hairs, which help deter herbivores.

The Pannonian thistle is known for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various insects and wildlife.
