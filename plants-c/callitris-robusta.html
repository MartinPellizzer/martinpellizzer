Callitris robusta

Callitris robusta, commonly known as the coastal tea tree, is a hardy evergreen conifer native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and compact, cone-like fruits. The plant thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in dune environments. It has a slow growth rate and is well adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
