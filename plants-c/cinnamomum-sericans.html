Cinnamomum sericans

Cinnamomum sericans, commonly known as the Chinese cinnamon or Cinnamomum aromaticum, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by oval-shaped berries. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a smooth surface and a strong, spicy aroma.

The bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cinnamon.
