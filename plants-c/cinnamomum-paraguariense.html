Cinnamomum paraguariense

Cinnamomum paraguariense, commonly known as Paraguayan cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to South America, particularly Paraguay and Brazil.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries containing kidney-shaped seeds. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine for their antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.

The essential oils extracted from the plant are valued for their aromatic and therapeutic uses.
