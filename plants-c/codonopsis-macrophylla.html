Codonopsis macrophylla

Codonopsis macrophylla, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has large, broad leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period and ability to attract pollinators.
