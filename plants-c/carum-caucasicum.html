Carum caucasicum

Carum caucasicum, commonly known as Caucasian cumin, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes plants like parsley and dill, and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its seeds, which are used as a spice, have a warm, slightly sweet, and aromatic flavor.

Carum caucasicum is often grown for its culinary and medicinal properties.
