Cedrela paraguariensis var. brachystachya

Cedrela paraguariensis var. brachystachya, commonly known as the Paraguayan rosewood, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil, Paraguay, and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Meliaceae and is characterized by its dense, aromatic wood that is highly valued for furniture and musical instruments. The plant has compound leaves with alternate arrangement and produces small, yellowish-green flowers clustered in catkins. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its natural propagation in the wild.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
