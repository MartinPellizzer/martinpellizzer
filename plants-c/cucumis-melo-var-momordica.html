Cucumis melo var. momordica

Cucumis melo var. momordica, commonly known as the bitter melon, is a tropical vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, ridged fruits that are typically green to orange in color and contain a bitter, seedy pulp. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its stems are hairy and trailing.

Bitter melon is cultivated for its edible fruit, which is used in various cuisines and traditional medicine.
