Cryptomeria japonica var. pungens

Cryptomeria japonica var. pungens, commonly known as the Japanese fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The foliage consists of needle-like leaves that are blue-green in color and have a slightly prickly texture. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of conifers.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
