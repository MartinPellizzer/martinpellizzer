Croton caudatus var. tomentosus

Croton caudatus var. tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage.
