Cnestis longiflora

Cnestis longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered cnestis, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

While not widely cultivated, it is of interest to botanists due to its unique floral structure and ecological adaptations.
