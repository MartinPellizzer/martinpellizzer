Camissonia pusilla

Camissonia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf camissonia, is a small annual herb native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features delicate, hairy leaves and small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in early spring. It has a compact growth habit and often forms dense clusters.

Camissonia pusilla is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in disturbed or sandy soils.
