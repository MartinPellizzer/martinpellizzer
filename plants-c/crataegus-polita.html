Crataegus polita

Crataegus polita, commonly known as the smooth hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. Crataegus polita typically grows in moist woodlands and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
