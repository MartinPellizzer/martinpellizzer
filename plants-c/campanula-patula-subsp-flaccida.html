Campanula patula subsp. flaccida

Campanula patula subsp.

flaccida, commonly known as the drooping bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are lanceolate and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
