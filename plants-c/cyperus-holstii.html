Cyperus holstii

Cyperus holstii, commonly known as the spike grass or narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters at the base of the plant.

Cyperus holstii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
