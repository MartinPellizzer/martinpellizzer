Convolvulus scaber

Convolvulus scaber, commonly known as rough knotweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy, often reddish stems. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine, stiff hairs.

Convolvulus scaber is native to southern Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in waste areas.
