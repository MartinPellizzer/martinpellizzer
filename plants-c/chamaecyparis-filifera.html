Chamaecyparis filifera

Chamaecyparis filifera, commonly known as the Japanese cedar or Japanese white cedar, is a coniferous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its scale-like leaves that are closely pressed to the branches. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its wood is highly valued for its durability and aromatic properties.
