Chamaecytisus hirsutus subsp. polytrichus

Chamaecytisus hirsutus subsp.

polytrichus, commonly known as the hairy broom or sweet broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has compound leaves with multiple pairs of oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value and hardy nature make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
