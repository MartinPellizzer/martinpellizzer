Cassia kolabensis

Cassia kolabensis, commonly known as the African cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain numerous seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Cassia kolabensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
