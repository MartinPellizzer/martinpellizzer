Clarkia

Clarkia, commonly known as the "California poppy," is a genus of flowering plants in the evening primrose family, Onagraceae.

These plants are native to western North America, particularly California, Oregon, and Washington. Clarkia species are herbaceous annuals or perennials, characterized by their showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to red and pink. They typically grow in well-drained soils and thrive in full sun, making them popular in native plant gardens.

The genus includes over 20 species, many of which are valued for their ornamental beauty and ecological benefits.
