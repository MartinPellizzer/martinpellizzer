Campanula rotundifolia var. dilecta

Campanula rotundifolia var. dilecta, commonly known as the round-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of rounded, velvety leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, sky-blue to violet flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and is often used in gardens for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
