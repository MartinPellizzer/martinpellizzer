Cnicus schimperi

Cnicus schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and distinctive foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its native habitat.
