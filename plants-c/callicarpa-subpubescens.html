Callicarpa subpubescens

Callicarpa subpubescens, commonly known as the red-hair tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, bright red berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Callicarpa subpubescens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
