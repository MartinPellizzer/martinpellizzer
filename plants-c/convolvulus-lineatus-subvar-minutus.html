Convolvulus lineatus subvar. minutus

Convolvulus lineatus subvar. minutus, commonly known as the dwarf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The flowers are typically pale blue or white, with five petals and a distinctive spurred shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing along rocky outcrops or in dry habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
