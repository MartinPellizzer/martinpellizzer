Convolvulus tricolor var. quadricolor

Convolvulus tricolor var. quadricolor, commonly known as the four-color morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored flowers that typically display shades of red, orange, yellow, and white. The plant is a vigorous climber with heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Native to southern Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and as a decorative plant in containers.
