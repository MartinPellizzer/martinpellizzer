Commelina lunata

Commelina lunata, commonly known as the moonflower or white arum, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and produces striking white to pale blue flowers with a distinctive spathe and spadix. The plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks, thriving in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Commelina lunata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
