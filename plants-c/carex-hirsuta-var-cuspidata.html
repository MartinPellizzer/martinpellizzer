Carex hirsuta var. cuspidata

Carex hirsuta var. cuspidata, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-green leaves. The plant produces cylindrical spikes of small, inconspicuous flowers, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and woodland edges.

This variety is distinguished by its hairy leaf surfaces and pointed leaf tips, contributing to its unique botanical identity.
