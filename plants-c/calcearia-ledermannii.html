Calcearia ledermannii

Calcearia ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, spiny fruits called achenes, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
