Calonemorchis corynepetala

Calonemorchis corynepetala, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in cool and moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in a spike-like inflorescence.

Calonemorchis corynepetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
