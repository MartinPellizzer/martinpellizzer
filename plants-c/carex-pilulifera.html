Carex pilulifera

Carex pilulifera, commonly known as the pill-like sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States and southeastern Canada.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-yellow flowers in the summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Carex pilulifera plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
