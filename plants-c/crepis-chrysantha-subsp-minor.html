Crepis chrysantha subsp. minor

Crepis chrysantha subsp.

minor, commonly known as the lesser yellow hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower.

The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, and the plant produces small, cylindrical achenes as its seed structure.

It is relatively low-maintenance and often used in wildflower gardens for its ornamental value.
