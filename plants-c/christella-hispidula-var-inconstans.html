Christella hispidula var. inconstans

Christella hispidula var. inconstans, commonly known as the roughleaf christella, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, oval leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, wooded areas.
