Cardaria fenestrata

Cardaria fenestrata, commonly known as the fenestrated cress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its small, white or pale yellow flowers and its distinctive, lobed leaves. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Cardaria fenestrata is a short-lived perennial that produces a capsule fruit containing numerous seeds.
