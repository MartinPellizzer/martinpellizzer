Chaetoseris beesiana

Chaetoseris beesiana, commonly known as the narrowleaf dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in full sun. Its flowers are yellow and bloom in the spring, followed by the formation of a crown-like seed head.

The species is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
