Cytisus albus var. incarnatus

Cytisus albus var. incarnatus, commonly known as the pale broom or white broom, is a flowering shrub native to southern Europe.

It features slender, erect stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a range of soil types.
