Cardamine trichocarpa subsp. elegans

Cardamine trichocarpa subsp.

elegans, commonly known as the elegant watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family. It is a perennial herb characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small white flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland areas. Its leaves are often submerged or floating, adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
