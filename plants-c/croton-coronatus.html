Croton coronatus

Croton coronatus, commonly known as the crowned croton, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, palmate leaves that often display a mix of green, yellow, and red hues. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Croton coronatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
