Convolvulus batata

Convolvulus batata, commonly known as the sweet potato, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trailing stems that often produce vibrant yellow or red flowers. The plant is native to Central and South America but is now cultivated worldwide for its edible tuberous roots. Its roots are rich in carbohydrates, fiber, and various vitamins, making it a nutritious food source.

The plant's rapid growth and ability to thrive in diverse soil conditions contribute to its widespread agricultural importance.
