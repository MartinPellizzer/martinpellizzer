Campanula Scheuchzeri Var. Ficarioides

Campanula scheuchzeri var. ficarioides is a rare and striking plant native to the high altitudes of the Swiss Alps. Known for its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers, it thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil. This variant of Campanula is particularly notable for its unique foliage, which resembles that of the ficus plant, giving it its common name. It is often found in rocky crevices and among mountainous vegetation, making it a hardy specimen for alpine gardens. Due to its limited distribution and ecological sensitivity, it is considered a valuable and endangered species in its native habitat.
