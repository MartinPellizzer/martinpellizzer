Calea asclepiifolia

Calea asclepiifolia, commonly known as the sacred herb or Mexican ghostflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, violet-colored bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often hidden beneath the showy bracts. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Calea asclepiifolia is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional healing practices for centuries.
