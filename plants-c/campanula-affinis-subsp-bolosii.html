Campanula affinis subsp. bolosii

Campanula affinis subsp.

bolosii, commonly known as Bolosii bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or open habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of small, showy blooms.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
