Carduus trichocephalus

Carduus trichocephalus, commonly known as the hairy cardue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and milky sap. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces purple to pink composite flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wastelands.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
