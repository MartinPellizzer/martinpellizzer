Chrysocoma obtusata

Chrysocoma obtusata, commonly known as the African bluebell, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. These flowers bloom in spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
