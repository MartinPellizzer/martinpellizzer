Carex × kuekenthaliana

Carex × kuekenthaliana, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a sterile hybrid resulting from the cross between Carex breviculmis and Carex morrowii, characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically forms compact clumps with narrow, linear leaves that are often a deep green color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, which are typically borne on separate stems from the leaves.

Carex × kuekenthaliana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
