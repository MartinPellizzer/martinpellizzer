Coccoloba acuminata var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy coccoloba, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its hairy, pubescent leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America, often found in moist, shaded environments.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage.
