Convolvulus pulchellus

Convolvulus pulchellus, commonly known as the pretty bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a trailing or climbing herb with opposite, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pinkish flowers. The plant has a fibrous root system and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is adapted to full sun.

Convolvulus pulchellus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
