Carex garberi

Carex garberi, commonly known as Garber's sedge, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This species typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and usually have a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many sedges.
