Campylospermum dependens var. paniculatum

Campylospermum dependens var. paniculatum, commonly known as the weeping campylospermum, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its pendulous, drooping branches that give it a distinctive weeping appearance. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in panicles, which are often fragrant and attract pollinators. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a glossy surface.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
