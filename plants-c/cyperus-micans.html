Cyperus micans

Cyperus micans, commonly known as the silver sprout sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Cyperus micans typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of water bodies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
