Ceropegia karulensis

Ceropegia karulensis, commonly known as the Karul's arrowhead, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and tubular flowers. The plant produces small, fragrant blooms that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ceropegia karulensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and delicate flowers.
