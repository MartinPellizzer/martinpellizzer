Carex aequivoca

Carex aequivoca, commonly known as the common sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in temperate regions across North America.

Carex aequivoca plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
