Coeloglossum viride f. purpureum

Coeloglossum viride f. purpureum, commonly known as the purple-stemmed green orchid, is a rare and distinctive orchid species characterized by its unique foliage and floral structure.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a subtle purple tinge, giving it its varietal name. The plant produces striking, pendulous inflorescences bearing greenish-white flowers with a faint purplish hue. It is native to specific regions in Europe and thrives in moist, shaded habitats such as woodland floors.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is often considered a conservation concern.
