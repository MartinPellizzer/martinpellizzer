Cinnamomum australe

Cinnamomum australe, commonly known as the Australian cinnamon tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree produces berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds and other animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforests or coastal areas.

The bark and leaves of Cinnamomum australe have been used traditionally for their aromatic properties and medicinal value.
