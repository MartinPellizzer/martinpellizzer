Convolvulus mammosus

Convolvulus mammosus, commonly known as the milkweed vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its stems are often covered in fine hairs, and it has a milky sap that can be toxic if ingested.

Convolvulus mammosus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
