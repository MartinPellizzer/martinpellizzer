Conomorpha multipunctata

Conomorpha multipunctata, commonly known as the speckled orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers with distinct markings. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are often pollinated by specific insect species, showcasing a unique ecological relationship.
