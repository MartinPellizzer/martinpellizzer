Callicarpa horsfieldii

Callicarpa horsfieldii, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright purple berries that appear in the fall, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

The berries are not only visually appealing but also attract birds and other wildlife, contributing to its ecological value.
