Crinum erubescens var. viridifolium

Crinum erubescens var. viridifolium, commonly known as the green-leaved crinum, is a striking bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, contrasting with its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers are usually white or pale pink and are borne on tall, sturdy stems. This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its bold foliage and fragrant blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for temperate climates.
