Campylotropis huberi

Campylotropis huberi, commonly known as the silverleaf brier, is a shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, thorny stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces pink to purple flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several elliptical seeds.

Campylotropis huberi is often found in moist, shaded woodlands and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
