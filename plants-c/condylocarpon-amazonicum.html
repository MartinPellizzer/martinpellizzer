Condylocarpon amazonicum

Condylocarpon amazonicum, commonly known as the Amazonian lichen, is a species of lichen that thrives in the humid tropical environments of the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Caloplacaceae and is characterized by its dense, crust-like growth form. The lichen is composed of a symbiotic relationship between a green alga and a fungus, which allows it to survive in nutrient-poor conditions. It plays a crucial role in the ecological dynamics of the rainforest by contributing to soil formation and nutrient cycling.

Its presence is often an indicator of environmental health and stability in the region.
