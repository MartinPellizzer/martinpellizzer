Capnorchis chrysantha

Capnorchis chrysantha, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Capnorchis chrysantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
