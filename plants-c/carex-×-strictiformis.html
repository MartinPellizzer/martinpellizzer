Carex × strictiformis

Carex × strictiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes or along stream banks. It is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
