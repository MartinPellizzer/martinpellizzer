Chrysanthemum indicum var. tsurugisanense

Chrysanthemum indicum var. tsurugisanense, commonly known as the Tsurugisanense chrysanthemum, is a rare and ornamental variety native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically blooms in late summer to early autumn, producing vibrant yellow to orange petals. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or container cultivation.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in horticultural collections.
