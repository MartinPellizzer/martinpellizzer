Cerasus × koshiensis

Cerasus × koshiensis, commonly known as the Koshi cherry, is a hybrid species of cherry native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and edible fruits that are typically harvested in late spring. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and a dark green color, while its flowers are white or pale pink, appearing in early spring.

The Koshi cherry is valued for both its ornamental beauty and its culinary uses in traditional Japanese cuisine.
