Cactus pretiosus

Cactus pretiosus, commonly known as the rose-thorn cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a spineless cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a cylindrical stem covered in red or pink flowers. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and requires minimal water once established.

Its vibrant flowers bloom in spring, making it a striking addition to arid landscapes.
