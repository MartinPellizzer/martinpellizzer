Citrus × aurantium

Citrus × aurantium, commonly known as the sweet orange.

It is a hybrid citrus species native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its thick, peely rind and juicy, segmented fruit. The plant is typically evergreen, with broad, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is rich in vitamin C and is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant is also cultivated for its essential oils and is an important crop in tropical and subtropical regions.
