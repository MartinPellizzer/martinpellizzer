Cyathea albifrons

Cyathea albifrons, commonly known as the white-veined tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for many decades, making it a long-lived species. Its fronds are typically bright green with white veins, giving it its common name.

Cyathea albifrons is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its elegant appearance and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
