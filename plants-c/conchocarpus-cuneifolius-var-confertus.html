Conchocarpus cuneifolius var. confertus

Conchocarpus cuneifolius var. confertus, commonly known as the hairy hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, hairy leaves that are usually heart-shaped or ovate in form. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red, attracting various pollinators. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that split open to release seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
