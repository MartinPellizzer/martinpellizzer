Cestrum glaucophyllum

Cestrum glaucophyllum, commonly known as the white cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its fruit is a small, capsule-like structure that contains several seeds.

Cestrum glaucophyllum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
