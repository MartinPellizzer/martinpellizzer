Cyperus densicaespitosus var. major

Cyperus densicaespitosus var. major, commonly known as the dense hairy flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features dense, hair-like structures on its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and wetlands.

Its botanical structure includes narrow, linear leaves and small, three-lobed flowers arranged in spikelets.
