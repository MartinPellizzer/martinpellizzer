Chaenostoma huillanum

Chaenostoma huillanum, commonly known as the Huillanum water plant, is a species of aquatic plant native to South America.

It typically grows in freshwater environments such as lakes and streams, where it forms dense underwater vegetation. The plant has long, narrow leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in light absorption and water flow. Its flowers are small and white, often floating on the water surface, which attracts pollinators like insects.

Chaenostoma huillanum plays a role in stabilizing aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
