Chrysopteris araneosa

Chrysopteris araneosa, commonly known as the spider-veined fern, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Davalliaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that feature intricate, web-like vein patterns, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth, where it thrives in high humidity. Its leaves are bioluminescent, emitting a faint glow in the dark, which aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

This unique botanical feature makes it a fascinating subject for ecological and botanical study.
