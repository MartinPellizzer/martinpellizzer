Carex turrita

Carex turrita, commonly known as the tall sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, cylindrical leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, with three stamens and a three-lobed pistil.

Carex turrita is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
