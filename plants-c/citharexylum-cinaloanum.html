Citharexylum cinaloanum

Citharexylum cinaloanum, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in late winter or early spring. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly rough texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal blooms.
