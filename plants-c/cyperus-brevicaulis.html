Cyperus brevicaulis

Cyperus brevicaulis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist to saturated soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 10 cm in length, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. Cyperus brevicaulis is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread vegetatively and form dense stands.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
