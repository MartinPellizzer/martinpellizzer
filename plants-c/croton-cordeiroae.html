Croton cordeiroae

Croton cordeiroae, commonly known as the Brazilian croton, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful, and often variegated leaves that display shades of green, yellow, and red. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique foliage.
