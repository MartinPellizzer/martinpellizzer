Cissus strigosa

Cissus strigosa, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in shaded areas.

Cissus strigosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
