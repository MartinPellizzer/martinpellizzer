Camassia angusta

Camassia angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf camas, is a herbaceous perennial native to western North America.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its bulbs are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Camassia angusta is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and native plant landscapes.
