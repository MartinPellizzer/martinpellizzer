Campanula pentagonia

Campanula pentagonia, commonly known as the five-angled bellflower, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-lobed, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula pentagonia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
