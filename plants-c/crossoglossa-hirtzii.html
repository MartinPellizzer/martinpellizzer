Crossoglossa hirtzii

Crossoglossa hirtzii, commonly known as the hairy crosslip orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant features showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers with a distinctive cross-shaped lip, which is a defining trait of the genus Crossoglossa. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it the "hairy" descriptor in its common name.

Crossoglossa hirtzii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing in forest understories.
