Carex pseudopunctata

Carex pseudopunctata, commonly known as the spotted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly found in wet or moist environments. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in consistently damp conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive pattern of green and silver markings that give it its common name.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the sedge family.
