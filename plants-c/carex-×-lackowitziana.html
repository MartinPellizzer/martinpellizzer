Carex × lackowitziana

Carex × lackowitziana, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. This plant is typically found in wetland or moist soil environments, thriving in areas with consistent moisture. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually green or brown, and it produces small, three-angled berries.

Carex × lackowitziana is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
