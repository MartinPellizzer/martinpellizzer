Cistus pruinosus

Cistus pruinosus, commonly known as the sticky rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, glandular leaves that are often covered in a waxy, sticky substance. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

Cistus pruinosus is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in its native habitat.
