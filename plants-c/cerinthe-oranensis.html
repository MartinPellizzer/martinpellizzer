Cerinthe oranensis

Cerinthe oranensis, commonly known as the Algerian toadflax, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Cerinthe oranensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
