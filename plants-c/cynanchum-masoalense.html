Cynanchum masoalense

Cynanchum masoalense, commonly known as the Madagascar milkweed, is a climbing herbaceous plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its milky sap, which is a common feature among plants in this family. The plant has opposite leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape, and it produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow and orange. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
