Carex loliacea

Carex loliacea, commonly known as slender marsh sedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Carex loliacea plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
