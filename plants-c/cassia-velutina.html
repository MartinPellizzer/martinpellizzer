Cassia velutina

Cassia velutina, commonly known as velvet cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, velvety leaves and bright yellow, clustered flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces elongated, flat pods containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cassia velutina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
