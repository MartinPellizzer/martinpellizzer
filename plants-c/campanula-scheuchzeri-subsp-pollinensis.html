Campanula scheuchzeri subsp. pollinensis

Campanula scheuchzeri subsp.

pollinensis, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, adapted to withstand cold and windy conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
