Cubeba arborescens

Cubeba arborescens, commonly known as wild cubeba or Indian cubeba, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Piperaceae and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Cubeba arborescens thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.
