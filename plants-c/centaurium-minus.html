Centaurium minus

Centaurium minus, commonly known as lesser centaury, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender stems that are often hairy and bear small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of delicate, violet-blue flowers with five petals, typically blooming from June to August. Its flowers are arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences, and it is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Centaurium minus is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
