Cynanchum magdalenicum

Cynanchum magdalenicum, commonly known as Magdalena's milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its opposite, lanceolate leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that contains latex, a common feature among milkweed species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
