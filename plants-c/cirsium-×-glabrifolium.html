Cirsium × glabrifolium

Cirsium × glabrifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a hybrid plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green foliage and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to the western United States, it thrives in dry, rocky habitats. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with soft, fuzzy hairs and produces small, globe-shaped seed heads.

It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
