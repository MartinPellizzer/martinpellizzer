Carduus italicus

Carduus italicus, commonly known as Italian thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

The plant is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental due to its distinctive appearance and hardy nature.
