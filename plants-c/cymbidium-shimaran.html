Cymbidium shimaran

Cymbidium shimaran, commonly known as the Shimaran orchid, is a species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, lance-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, producing showy blooms in shades of white, pink, and red. It prefers cool, humid environments and is often cultivated in shaded gardens or as a potted plant.

Cymbidium shimaran is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
