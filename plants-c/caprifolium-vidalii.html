Caprifolium vidalii

Caprifolium vidalii, commonly known as the Vidal's honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, typically blooming in late spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that ripen in early autumn and are edible.

Caprifolium vidalii is often found in rocky habitats and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils.
