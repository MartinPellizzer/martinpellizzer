Carum carvifolium

Carum carvifolium, commonly known as Persian cumin or wild cumin, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It features slender, branched stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate, lacey appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its seeds, which are harvested when mature, are used in various culinary and medicinal applications.

Carum carvifolium thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and borders.
