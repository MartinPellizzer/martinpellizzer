Carex alsia

Carex alsia, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like flower clusters. Native to North America, it thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and streambanks. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has a fibrous root system.

Its botanical structure supports its role in wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
