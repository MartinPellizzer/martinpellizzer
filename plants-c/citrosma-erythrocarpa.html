Citrosma erythrocarpa

Citrosma erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited citrosma, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and distinctive red berries. The plant typically grows in lowland rainforests and thrives in humid, warm climates. Its fruits are small, round, and have a thick, leathery skin that turns red when mature.

The botanical features of Citrosma erythrocarpa make it an important species in both ecological and potential medicinal contexts.
