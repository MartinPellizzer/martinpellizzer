Congea pentandra

Congea pentandra, commonly known as the African nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with simple, opposite, and ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by berries that turn from green to red as they mature. Its botanical features include a hairy stem, glandular leaves, and a distinctive, pungent odor.

The plant is part of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers.
