Chrysosplenium geoides

Chrysosplenium geoides, commonly known as the mountain spleenwort, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the family Saxifragaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and features small, kidney-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
