Carex appressa var. typica

Carex appressa var. typica, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curved, with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that are usually greenish or brownish in color, typical of sedges. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
