Conospermum coerulescens subsp. dorrienii

Conospermum coerulescens subsp.

dorrienii, commonly known as the Blueberry Bush, is a shrub native to Western Australia. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that displays a striking blue-green hue.

The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers during the winter months, followed by edible, blue-black berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
