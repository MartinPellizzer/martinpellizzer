Catostemma clarkii

Catostemma clarkii, commonly known as the Clark's catostemma, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is often found in shaded to partially shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
