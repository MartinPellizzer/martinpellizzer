Cyrtochilum leopoldianum

Cyrtochilum leopoldianum, commonly known as the Leopold's orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a pseudobulb and linear leaves, typical of many orchid genera. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often with a yellow or greenish throat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Cyrtochilum leopoldianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
