Convolvulus luteolus var. saxicola

Convolvulus luteolus var. saxicola, commonly known as the yellow field bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
