Cassia grandiflora

Cassia grandiflora, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer. The plant has compound leaves composed of several narrow, lance-shaped leaflets. It produces small, flat pods that contain numerous seeds.

Cassia grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
