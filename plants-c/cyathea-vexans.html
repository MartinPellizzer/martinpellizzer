Cyathea vexans

Cyathea vexans, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests. Its fronds are typically dark green and have a velvety texture, giving it its common name.

Cyathea vexans plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various forest organisms.
