Callicarpa inaequalis

Callicarpa inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright red berries that appear in the fall, making it a striking ornamental plant. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a slightly serrated edge, and the plant produces fragrant, white flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, often found in woodland areas.

Due to its attractive berries and ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
