Campanula cespitosa f. austriaca

Campanula cespitosa f. austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It features dense clusters of bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a compact, mat-forming growth habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
