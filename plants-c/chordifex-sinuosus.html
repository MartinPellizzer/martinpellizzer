Chordifex sinuosus

Chordifex sinuosus, commonly known as the sinuous thorned vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and thorn-like structures that provide mechanical support as it climbs. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptic, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss. Its flowers are small and clustered, often in greenish-white or pale yellow hues, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
