Condea thyrsiflora

Condea thyrsiflora, commonly known as the African milk bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white to yellowish flowers, which are followed by follicle fruits containing seeds. It is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic and is often used in traditional medicine.

Condea thyrsiflora thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
