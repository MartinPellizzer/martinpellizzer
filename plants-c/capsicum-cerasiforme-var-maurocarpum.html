Capsicum cerasiforme var. maurocarpum

Capsicum cerasiforme var. maurocarpum, commonly known as the black bell pepper.

This variety is characterized by its dark, almost black, elongated fruits that resemble small peppers. The plant produces medium-sized, pointed fruits with a glossy skin that transitions from dark green to black as it matures. It is a compact shrub with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The botanical features of this variety make it unique among Capsicum species, particularly due to its distinctive coloration and fruit morphology.
