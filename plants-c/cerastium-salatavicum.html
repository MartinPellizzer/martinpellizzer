Cerastium salatavicum

Cerastium salatavicum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers in early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Cerastium salatavicum is often found in mountain meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
