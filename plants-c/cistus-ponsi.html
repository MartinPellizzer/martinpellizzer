Cistus ponsi

Cistus ponsi, commonly known as the Ponsi rockrose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience to drought. It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

Cistus ponsi is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
