Carex acuta var. sphaerocarpa

Carex acuta var. sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the spherical sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for wetland restoration and garden landscapes.
