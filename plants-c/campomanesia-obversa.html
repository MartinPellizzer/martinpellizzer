Campomanesia obversa

Campomanesia obversa, commonly known as the pitanga or Brazilian cherry, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. The berries are edible, with a sweet, tangy flavor, and are often used in local cuisine and traditional medicine.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental and culinary uses.
