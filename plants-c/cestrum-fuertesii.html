Cestrum fuertesii

Cestrum fuertesii, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a member of the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom at night. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture.

Cestrum fuertesii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to attract nocturnal pollinators.
