Chamaechaenactis scaposa var. parva

Chamaechaenactis scaposa var. parva, commonly known as the small desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is a low-growing perennial with a rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its stems are usually unbranched and rise from the center of the rosette, supporting the flower heads.

It is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, showcasing resilience to drought and high temperatures.
