Crepis costata

Crepis costata, commonly known as the serrated hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features narrow, serrated leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Crepis costata is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
