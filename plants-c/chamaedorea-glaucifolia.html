Chamaedorea glaucifolia

Chamaedorea glaucifolia, commonly known as the blue-leafed bamboo palm, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Central America.

It features slender, upright stems that are covered in a distinctive bluish-gray sheen, giving it its common name. The plant has pinnately compound leaves that are dark green with a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate humidity and well-draining soil.

Chamaedorea glaucifolia is popular as a houseplant due to its air-purifying qualities and low maintenance requirements.
