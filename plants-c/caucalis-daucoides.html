Caucalis daucoides

Caucalis daucoides, commonly known as the lesser carrot or mouse-ear parsley, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, fern-like leaves that are often clustered at the base. The plant produces tiny white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in moist, calcareous soils.

Caucalis daucoides is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.
