Castanea vesca var. strictifolia

Castanea vesca var. strictifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved horse chestnut.

It is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers in spring. Its fruit is a spiny capsule containing one or two chestnuts, which are edible after careful preparation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
