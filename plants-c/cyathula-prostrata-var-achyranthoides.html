Cyathula prostrata var. achyranthoides

Cyathula prostrata var. achyranthoides, commonly known as the prostrate cyathula, is a herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features a low-growing, sprawling habit with slender, hairy stems that often lie flat on the ground. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters called cyathia, which are characteristic of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a slightly rough texture.
