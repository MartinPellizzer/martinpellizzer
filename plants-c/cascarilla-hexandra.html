Cascarilla hexandra

Cascarilla hexandra, commonly known as the yellow sandalwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish wood that is highly valued for its fragrance and medicinal properties. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy texture. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, typically clustered in spikes, and it produces small, round fruits containing a single seed.

The tree is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
