Curcuma karnatakensis

Curcuma karnatakensis, commonly known as the Karnataka turmeric, is a perennial herb native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces strikingly colorful, sword-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange flowers. Its rhizomes are valued for their medicinal properties, similar to other turmeric species.

The botanical features of C. karnatakensis make it a notable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
