Clinopodium nepeta subsp. conillii

Clinopodium nepeta subsp.

conillii, commonly known as mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or open woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, four-lobed fruits called nutlets, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming and antiseptic properties.
