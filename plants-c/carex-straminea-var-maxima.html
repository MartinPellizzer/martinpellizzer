Carex straminea var. maxima

Carex straminea var. maxima, commonly known as the giant marsh sedge, is a tall, robust sedge species native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically light green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces large, showy spikelets that are often pinkish or reddish, adding visual interest to its appearance. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in riparian zones and pond margins.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
