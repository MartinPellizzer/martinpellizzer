Callicarpa yakusimensis

Callicarpa yakusimensis, commonly known as the Japanese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright purple berries that appear in the fall, which are both ornamental and edible. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The berries attract birds and other wildlife, contributing to its ecological value.
