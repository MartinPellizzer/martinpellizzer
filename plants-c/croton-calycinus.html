Croton calycinus

Croton calycinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a garden plant.
