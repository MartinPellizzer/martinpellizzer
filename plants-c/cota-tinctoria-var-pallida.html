Cota tinctoria var. pallida

Cota tinctoria var. pallida, commonly known as the pale dye plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is notable for its historical use in natural dyeing processes, particularly for producing yellow and orange dyes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and historical contexts.
