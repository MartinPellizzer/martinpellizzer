Christella × varievenulosa

Christella × varievenulosa, commonly known as the hybrid Christella, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Ericaceae.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Christella monogyna and Christella tetrandra, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and thrives in shady to partially shaded environments. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to form compact, shrub-like growth.

Due to its hybrid nature, it exhibits a range of morphological variations in leaf shape and flower color.
