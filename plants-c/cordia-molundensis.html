Cordia molundensis

Cordia molundensis, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by spherical, red or orange fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in warm climates.
