Cousinia immitantiformis

Cousinia immitantiformis, commonly known as the wrinkled flax, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and yellow flower heads. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its flowers are typically arranged in a flat-topped cluster, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cousinia immitantiformis is often found in alpine or steppe environments, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
