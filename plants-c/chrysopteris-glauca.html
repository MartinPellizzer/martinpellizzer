Chrysopteris glauca

Chrysopteris glauca, commonly known as the silverleaf chrysopteris, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
