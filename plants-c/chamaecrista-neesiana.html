Chamaecrista neesiana

Chamaecrista neesiana, commonly known as the sicklepod or coast mallow, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in pairs. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often growing along shorelines and dunes.

Chamaecrista neesiana is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important plant in ecological restoration efforts.
