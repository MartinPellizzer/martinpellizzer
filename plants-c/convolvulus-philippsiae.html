Convolvulus philippsiae

Convolvulus philippsiae, commonly known as the Philippine morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its trumpet-shaped, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in the morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or forest edges.

Convolvulus philippsiae is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
