Calamus thysanolepis

Calamus thysanolepis, commonly known as the hairy calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in wet, tropical environments.

Calamus thysanolepis is often found in marshy areas and along the edges of water bodies.
