Caryocar

Caryocar, commonly known as the "pequi" tree, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the family Caryocaraceae and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The fruit is a woody, spiky capsule that contains a single large seed, which is often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. The plant is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
