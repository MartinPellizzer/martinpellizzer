Celtis japonica f. purpurascens

Celtis japonica f. purpurascens, commonly known as the Japanese hackberry variegated form, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, featuring variegated leaves with white and yellow margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by small, round, green fruits. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The variegation in its leaves makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and landscapes.
