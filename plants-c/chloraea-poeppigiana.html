Chloraea poeppigiana

Chloraea poeppigiana, commonly known as the silver wattle, is a flowering plant native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its slender, zigzagging stems and clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish flowers. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
