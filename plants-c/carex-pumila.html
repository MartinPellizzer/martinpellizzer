Carex pumila

Carex pumila, commonly known as dwarf sedges, is a low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically forms dense mats of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp environments.

Carex pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
