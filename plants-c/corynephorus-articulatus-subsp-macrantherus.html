Corynephorus articulatus subsp. macrantherus

Corynephorus articulatus subsp.

macrantherus, commonly known as the broad-panicle grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, open panicles that are often adorned with long, drooping branches.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that becomes more pronounced in dry conditions. This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and coastal areas.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
