Cestrum diurnum var. fastigiatum

Cestrum diurnum var. fastigiatum, commonly known as the silverleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has narrow, silver-green leaves that give it its common name, and it often grows as a shrub or small tree. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes in suitable climates.
