Carex pubigluma

Carex pubigluma, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered with fine, silky hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex pubigluma produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are often followed by distinctive three-sided, triangular seed heads.
