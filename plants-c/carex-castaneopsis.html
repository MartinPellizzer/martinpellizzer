Carex castaneopsis

Carex castaneopsis, commonly known as the chestnut sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces spikes of small, brownish flowers in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, which can help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The leaves are often dark green and have a slightly rough texture, making it a distinctive species within the Carex genus.
