Ceropegia fimbriifera

Ceropegia fimbriifera, commonly known as the laceleaf ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fimbriated (fringed) leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate watering to prevent root rot.

This species is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
