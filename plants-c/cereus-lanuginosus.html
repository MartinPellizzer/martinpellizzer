Cereus lanuginosus

Cereus lanuginosus, commonly known as the woolly night bloomer, is a cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its sparsely branched stems and dense, woolly covering of white hairs. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, typically in shades of white and pink. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cereus lanuginosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
