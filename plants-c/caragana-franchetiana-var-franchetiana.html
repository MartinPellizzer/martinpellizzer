Caragana franchetiana var. franchetiana

Caragana franchetiana var. franchetiana, commonly known as the Chinese pea shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species for dry climates. It produces small, edible pods that contain seeds used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The shrub is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
