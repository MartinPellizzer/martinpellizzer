Conomorpha roraimae

Conomorpha roraimae, commonly known as the Roraima fern, is a unique species of polypodioid fern native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically green with a subtle sheen. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky substrates or in crevices. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, contributing to its low profile in the dense forest undergrowth.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
