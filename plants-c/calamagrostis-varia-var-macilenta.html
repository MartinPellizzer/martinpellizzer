Calamagrostis varia var. macilenta

Calamagrostis varia var. macilenta, commonly known as the slender threadgrass, is a grass species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps and marshes, and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, fluffy flowers that appear in late summer.

Calamagrostis varia var. macilenta is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological value and ornamental appeal.
