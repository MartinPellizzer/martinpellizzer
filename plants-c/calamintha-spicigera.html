Calamintha spicigera

Calamintha spicigera, commonly known as spiked caraway, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, square stems and small, white to pale blue flowers. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. Its leaves are fragrant and have been historically used for their digestive and calming properties.

Calamintha spicigera thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
