Coffea paniculata

Coffea paniculata, commonly known as the paniculate coffee plant, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub that typically grows up to 3 meters in height. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves with a glossy green surface and a slightly hairy underside. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in panicles, which give the plant its scientific name.

The fruit is a small, red drupe containing a single seed, which is used for producing coffee.
