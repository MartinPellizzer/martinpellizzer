Carex × schuetzeana f. supercanecens

Carex × schuetzeana f. supercanecens, commonly known as the silver-haired sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with a distinctive silvery hue on the leaf surfaces. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
