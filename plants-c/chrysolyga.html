Chrysolyga

Chrysolyga, commonly known as the golden leaf orchid, is a striking plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow foliage and intricate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Native to tropical regions, this plant thrives in warm, humid environments with well-draining soil. The leaves are typically broad and glossy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
