Cymburus urticifolius

Cymburus urticifolius, commonly known as the stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Urticaceae family.

It is characterized by its hollow, hairless stems and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant contains fine, venomous hairs that cause skin irritation upon contact. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Cymburus urticifolius is a perennial plant that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height.
