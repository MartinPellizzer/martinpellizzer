Callicarpa bodinieri var. bodinieri

Callicarpa bodinieri var. bodinieri, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant violet to purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy and turn red in autumn. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil.

The berries are not only ornamental but also attract birds and are edible for humans when ripe.
