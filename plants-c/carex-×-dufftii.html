Carex × dufftii

Carex × dufftii, commonly known as the Dufft's sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Carex genus in the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish or greenish flowers. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in partial shade and its preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

Carex × dufftii is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological benefits and adaptability.
