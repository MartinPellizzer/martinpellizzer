Catasetum cernuum var. rodigasianum

Catasetum cernuum var. rodigasianum, commonly known as the Rodigasian Spider Orchid, is a striking orchid species native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, spider-like flowers that are typically yellow or orange with intricate patterns. The plant produces specialized structures called resupinate flowers, which are adapted for pollination by specific wasps. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Its unique morphology and pollination strategy make it a fascinating subject in botanical studies.
