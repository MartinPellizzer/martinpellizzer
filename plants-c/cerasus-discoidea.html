Cerasus discoidea

Cerasus discoidea, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or yubari melon, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe. The plant produces white or pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by the development of its edible fruits. Cerasus discoidea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a fruit-bearing plant in temperate climates.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in both horticulture and botanical research.
