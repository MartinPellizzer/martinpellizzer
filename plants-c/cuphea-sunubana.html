Cuphea sunubana

Cuphea sunubana, commonly known as the red hot poker or firecracker plant, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular red bracts that resemble flames. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered within the showy bracts. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, drought-tolerant plants.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which can last from summer into fall.
