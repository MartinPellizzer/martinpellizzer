Cuphea aequipetala var. hispida

Cuphea aequipetala var. hispida, commonly known as the rough-hairy cuphea, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and has a compact, bushy growth habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
