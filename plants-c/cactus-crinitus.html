Cactus crinitus

Cactus crinitus, commonly known as the golden hair cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cylindrical stems that are often covered in yellow or golden spines. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

The plant's unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and succulent collections.
