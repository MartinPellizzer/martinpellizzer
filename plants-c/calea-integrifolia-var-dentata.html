Calea integrifolia var. dentata

Calea integrifolia var. dentata, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and have a velvety texture, often with serrated edges in this variety. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

This plant is also noted for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
