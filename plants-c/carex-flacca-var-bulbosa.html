Carex flacca var. bulbosa

Carex flacca var. bulbosa, commonly known as the bulbous sedges, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features slender, grass-like stems with whorled leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in hue.

This variety is often found in moist, well-drained soils in meadows or along streambanks.
