Cystacanthus punctatus

Cystacanthus punctatus, commonly known as the spotted bladder nut, is a unique plant species characterized by its distinctive, bladder-like fruits.

It belongs to the family Cystacanthaceae and is native to specific regions in Africa. The plant's leaves are typically small and succulent, adapted to arid environments. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, and it relies on insect pollination.

The fruit, which matures into a hard, spiky capsule, contains seeds that are dispersed by wind or animals.
