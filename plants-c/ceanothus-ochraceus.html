Ceanothus ochraceus

Ceanothus ochraceus, commonly known as the yellow ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, gray-green leaves and clusters of showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats. It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
