Commiphora eminii subsp. zimmermannii

Commiphora eminii subsp.

zimmermannii, commonly known as the African frankincense tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to eastern Africa. It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces a fragrant resin, which is harvested from the bark and used in traditional medicine and incense. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
