Cyperus micranthus

Cyperus micranthus, commonly known as narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is a perennial herb characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus micranthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in wetland ecosystems.
