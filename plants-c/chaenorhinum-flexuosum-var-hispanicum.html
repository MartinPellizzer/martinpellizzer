Chaenorhinum flexuosum var. hispanicum

Chaenorhinum flexuosum var. hispanicum, commonly known as the Spanish horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and striking red to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and wildflower displays.
