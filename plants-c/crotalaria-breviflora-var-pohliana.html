Crotalaria breviflora var. pohliana

Crotalaria breviflora var. pohliana, commonly known as the Pohliana Crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features distinctive yellow-orange flowers with a spurred shape, and its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal applications.
