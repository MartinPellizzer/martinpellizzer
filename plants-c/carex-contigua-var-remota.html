Carex contigua var. remota

Carex contigua var. remota, commonly known as the remote sedge, is a perennial plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and clustered growth habit. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
