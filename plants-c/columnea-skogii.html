Columnea skogii

Columnea skogii, commonly known as the Skog's flame flower, is a tropical shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that are often adorned with a contrasting yellow or white throat. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Columnea skogii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
