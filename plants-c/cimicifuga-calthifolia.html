Cimicifuga calthifolia

Cimicifuga calthifolia, commonly known as white bugbane, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, white, lace-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and has compound leaves with serrated edges. Its roots contain compounds that have been used in traditional medicine, though it is toxic if ingested.

Cimicifuga calthifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
