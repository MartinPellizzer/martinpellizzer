Cyperus strigosus

Cyperus strigosus, commonly known as the hairy flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Cyperus strigosus is an important component of wetland ecosystems, providing habitat and contributing to soil stabilization.
