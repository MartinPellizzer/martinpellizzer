Clerodendrum ingratum

Clerodendrum ingratum, commonly known as the "Chinese lantern vine," is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, papery bracts that resemble small lanterns, which are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, surrounded by the showy bracts. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its decorative foliage and unique inflorescences.

Clerodendrum ingratum is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and sage.
