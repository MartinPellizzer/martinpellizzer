Carex thorelii

Carex thorelii, commonly known as the Thorell's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Carex thorelii produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are often surrounded by bracts.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland habitats by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
