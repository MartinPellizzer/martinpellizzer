Clematis orientalis var. glabrescens

Clematis orientalis var. glabrescens, commonly known as the Chinese Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a slightly hairy texture when young. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements in garden settings.
