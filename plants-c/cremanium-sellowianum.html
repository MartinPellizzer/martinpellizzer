Cremanium sellowianum

Cremanium sellowianum, commonly known as the Sellow's cremanium, is a climbing plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
