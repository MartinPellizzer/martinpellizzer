Cassia hedysaroides

Cassia hedysaroides, commonly known as the golden cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Cassia hedysaroides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
