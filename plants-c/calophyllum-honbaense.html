Calophyllum honbaense

Calophyllum honbaense, commonly known as the Honbaense Whitewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, spherical fruits. Its seeds are encased in a hard, spiny shell, and the tree is valued for its durable wood used in traditional crafts and construction.

Calophyllum honbaense thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
