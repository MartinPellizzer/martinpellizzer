Commelina forskaolii var. ramulosa

Commelina forskaolii var. ramulosa, commonly known as the slender commelina, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features striking blue-violet flowers with three petals and three sepals, typical of the Commelina genus. The leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in its native habitat. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
