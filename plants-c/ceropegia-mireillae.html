Ceropegia mireillae

Ceropegia mireillae, commonly known as the Madagascar starfish plant, is a succulent species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, tuberous stems and clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its unique floral structure resembles a star, giving it its common name.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ease of care.
