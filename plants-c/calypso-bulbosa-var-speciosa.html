Calypso bulbosa var. speciosa

Calypso bulbosa var. speciosa, commonly known as the showy calypso, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers with white petals and a distinctive violet lip. It is a herbaceous perennial that blooms in the spring, often in shaded, wetland habitats.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native environment.
