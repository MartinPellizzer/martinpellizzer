Corchorus hirsutus

Corchorus hirsutus, commonly known as the hairy knotweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its roots are fibrous and have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Corchorus hirsutus is also valued for its ornamental appeal and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
