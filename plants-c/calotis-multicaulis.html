Calotis multicaulis

Calotis multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered calotis, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a branched stem and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats.

Calotis multicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
