Chamaesenna velutina

Chamaesenna velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf chamaesenna, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several seeds.

Chamaesenna velutina is often found in disturbed habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
