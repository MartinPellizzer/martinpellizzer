Commelina nudiflora var. glabrata

Commelina nudiflora var. glabrata, commonly known as the naked-flowered spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features striking blue-violet to white flowers that are typically borne on slender, leafless stems. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

The plant is notable for its ability to spread through rhizomes, making it both a useful groundcover and a potential weed in certain environments.
