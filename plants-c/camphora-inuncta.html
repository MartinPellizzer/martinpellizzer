Camphora inuncta

Camphora inuncta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf camphor tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. This evergreen tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic when crushed. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by dark, berry-like fruits.

Its essential oil, derived from the bark and leaves, is valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
