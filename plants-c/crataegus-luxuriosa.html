Crataegus luxuriosa

Crataegus luxuriosa, commonly known as the lush hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Crataegus luxuriosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
