Camellia pilosperma

Camellia pilosperma, commonly known as the woolly camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Korea.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine, woolly pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers that range in color from white to deep red, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release seeds when mature.

Camellia pilosperma is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.
