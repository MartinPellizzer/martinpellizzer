Crataegus pedicellata f. assurgens

Crataegus pedicellata f. assurgens, commonly known as the pink-flowered hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the hawthorn genus characterized by its distinctive floral morphology.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America, typically found in moist, wooded areas. The plant is notable for its showy, pink to rose-colored flowers that bloom in early spring, preceding the foliage. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins, and the fruit is a small, red pome that ripens in the fall.

This cultivar is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
