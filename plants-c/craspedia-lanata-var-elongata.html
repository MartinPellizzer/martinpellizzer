Craspedia lanata var. elongata

Craspedia lanata var. elongata, commonly known as the hairy false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its hairy, elongated leaves and bright yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
