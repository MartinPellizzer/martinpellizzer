Crocus dispathaceus

Crocus dispathaceus, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its early blooming habit, often flowering in late winter or early spring. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, with a distinctive, often fragrant scent. Its leaves are grass-like and basal, forming a rosette near the ground.

Crocus dispathaceus is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
