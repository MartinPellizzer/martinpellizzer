Convolvulus siculus var. major

Convolvulus siculus var. major, commonly known as the Sicilian bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its trailing habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain environments.
