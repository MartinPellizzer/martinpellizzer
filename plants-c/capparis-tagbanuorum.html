Capparis tagbanuorum

Capparis tagbanuorum, commonly known as the Tagbanua caper, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered together. Its leaves are typically ovate and have a slightly rough texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical environments.
