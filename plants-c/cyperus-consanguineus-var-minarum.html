Cyperus consanguineus var. minarum

Cyperus consanguineus var. minarum, commonly known as the dwarf sedges, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known for their rhizomatous growth habits. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flower heads. Its botanical features include a compact stature, with leaves arranged in three rows, and it often forms dense mats in its natural habitat.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
