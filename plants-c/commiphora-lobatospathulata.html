Commiphora lobatospathulata

Commiphora lobatospathulata, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing plants. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. It produces a resinous sap that has been used traditionally for its aromatic and medicinal properties.
