Coussarea ampla

Coussarea ampla, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting a variety of pollinators. The plant has a woody stem with smooth, greyish bark, and its leaves are compound, with several leaflets arranged in a feather-like pattern. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Coussarea ampla is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
