Crotalaria giessii

Crotalaria giessii, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow, spurred flowers. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in grassland habitats and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Crotalaria giessii is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its role in improving soil fertility.
