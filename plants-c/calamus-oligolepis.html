Calamus oligolepis

Calamus oligolepis, commonly known as the slender calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Calamus oligolepis is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its role in local ecosystems and traditional uses.
