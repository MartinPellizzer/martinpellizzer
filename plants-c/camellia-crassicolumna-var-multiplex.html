Camellia crassicolumna var. multiplex

Camellia crassicolumna var. multiplex, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a species native to China and characterized by its large, showy flowers.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring broad, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces waxy, pink to red flowers with prominent yellow stamens, typically blooming in winter and early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial shade to full sun.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
