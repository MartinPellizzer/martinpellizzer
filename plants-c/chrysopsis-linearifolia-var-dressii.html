Chrysopsis linearifolia var. dressii

Chrysopsis linearifolia var. dressii, commonly known as the Desert Sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it well adapted to desert environments. Its botanical features include a taproot system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
