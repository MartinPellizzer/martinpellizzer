Calamintha tauricola

Calamintha tauricola, commonly known as Tauric mint, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants known for their medicinal properties. The plant features square stems and opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically blue or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems.

Calamintha tauricola is valued for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and antiseptic effects.
