Cestrum tubulosum

Cestrum tubulosum, commonly known as the tubular jessamine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, tubular, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring and summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fragrant blossoms.

Cestrum tubulosum is adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
