Calceolaria pinnata

Calceolaria pinnata, commonly known as the winged slipper orchid, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Calceolaria pinnata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
