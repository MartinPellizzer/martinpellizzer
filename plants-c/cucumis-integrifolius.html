Cucumis integrifolius

Cucumis integrifolius, commonly known as the Mexican bitter gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Central America and parts of the southern United States. The plant is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers. Its fruit is typically elongated, with a rough, bitter skin and a pale green interior.

It is often cultivated for its edible, though bitter, fruit.
