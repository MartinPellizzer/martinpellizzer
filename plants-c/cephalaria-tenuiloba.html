Cephalaria tenuiloba

Cephalaria tenuiloba, commonly known as slender cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and clusters of small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale yellow, bloom in spring and attract various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
