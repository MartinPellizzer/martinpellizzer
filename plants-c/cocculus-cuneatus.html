Cocculus cuneatus

Cocculus cuneatus, commonly known as the spiny cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiny stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that give way to green, spiny fruits. Its botanical features include a hairy stem and alternate leaves with a pointed shape.

Cocculus cuneatus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.
