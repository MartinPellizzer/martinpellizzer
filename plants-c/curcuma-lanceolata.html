Curcuma lanceolata

Curcuma lanceolata, commonly known as ginger lily or wild ginger, is a rhizomatous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other economically important plants like ginger and turmeric. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces fragrant, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in terminal clusters. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to turmeric.

The botanical characteristics of Curcuma lanceolata make it both ornamental and valuable for its medicinal properties.
