Carapa granatum

Carapa granatum, commonly known as the red cedar or Brazilian cedar, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its reddish-brown bark and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into woody capsules containing numerous seeds. Carapa granatum is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for various wildlife species in its native regions.
