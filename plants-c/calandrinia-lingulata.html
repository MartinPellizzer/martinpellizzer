Calandrinia lingulata

Calandrinia lingulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved portulaca, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing annual herb that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water, while its stems are often hairy.

Calandrinia lingulata is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
