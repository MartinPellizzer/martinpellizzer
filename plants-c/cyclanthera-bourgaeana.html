Cyclanthera bourgaeana

Cyclanthera bourgaeana, commonly known as the Bourganeau cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It produces long, slender fruits that resemble cucumbers, typically green when mature. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Cyclanthera bourgaeana is valued for its edible fruits, which have a mild, slightly sweet flavor.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
