Chaetanthera pungens

Chaetanthera pungens, commonly known as the prickly nightshade, is a flowering plant belonging to the Solanaceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, thriving in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often blooming in the evening. Its leaves are typically hairy and have a distinctive, sometimes prickly texture.

Despite its attractive appearance, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with care.
