Calamagrostis × uralensis

Calamagrostis × uralensis, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, grass-like perennial plant native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It features slender, arching stems that can grow up to 6 feet in height, with narrow, blade-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers in late summer, which are typically pale green to golden in color. It thrives in moist soils and is often found near wetlands, stream banks, and other water-rich environments.

Its dense growth habit makes it an effective erosion control plant and a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
