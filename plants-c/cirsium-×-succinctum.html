Cirsium × succinctum

Cirsium × succinctum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lance-shaped leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to Europe, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a common weed in agricultural settings.
