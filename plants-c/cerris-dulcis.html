Cerris dulcis

Cerris dulcis, commonly known as the sweet chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the beech family (Fagaceae) and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, ovate leaves. The tree produces large, spiny capsules that contain edible, sweet nuts enclosed in a hard shell. Its flowers are inconspicuous, with male and female flowers occurring on separate trees, and it relies on wind pollination.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in timber and food production.
