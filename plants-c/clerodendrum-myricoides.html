Clerodendrum myricoides

Clerodendrum myricoides, commonly known as the sweet flag or red-rooted clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in dense inflorescences. Its roots are often reddish in color, which contributes to its common name, and the plant is known for its aromatic properties.

Clerodendrum myricoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
