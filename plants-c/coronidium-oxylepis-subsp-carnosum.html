Coronidium oxylepis subsp. carnosum

Coronidium oxylepis subsp.

carnosum, commonly known as the Woolly Crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its woolly, gray-green foliage and showy, yellow-orange flower heads that resemble daisies.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. Its flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, and it is often found in open grasslands and along rocky outcrops.

The subspecies carnosum is distinguished by its dense, hairy leaves and compact growth habit.
