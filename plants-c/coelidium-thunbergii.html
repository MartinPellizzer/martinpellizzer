Coelidium thunbergii

Coelidium thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese yew, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, reddish-brown cones that mature over several years. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a slow growth rate.

Coelidium thunbergii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
