Cola nigerica

Cola nigerica, commonly known as African cola, is a species of evergreen shrub native to tropical West Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces hard, woody pods that contain stimulant-rich seeds, which are harvested for their caffeine content. These seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in beverages.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
