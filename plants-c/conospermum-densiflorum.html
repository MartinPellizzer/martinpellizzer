Conospermum densiflorum

Conospermum densiflorum, commonly known as the denseflower honeysuckle, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and is often found in heathlands and dry forests. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which attract nectar-feeding birds and insects. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, providing a drought-resistant adaptation.

Conospermum densiflorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
