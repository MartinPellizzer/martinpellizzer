Camellia triquetra

Camellia triquetra, commonly known as the three-lobed camellia, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive triquetrous (three-lobed) petals and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces showy, waxy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Camellia triquetra is also valued for its resistance to pests and diseases, contributing to its horticultural appeal.
