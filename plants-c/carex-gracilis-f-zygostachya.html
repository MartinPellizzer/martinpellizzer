Carex gracilis f. zygostachya

Carex gracilis f. zygostachya, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a variant of the slender sedge species native to wetland environments.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically forms dense clumps and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in marshes or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, greenish-brown flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
