Crataegus laxa

Crataegus laxa, commonly known as the slender hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters, featuring thorny branches and oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, juicy berries in the autumn.

Crataegus laxa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature and seasonal interest.
