Cyrtanthus speciosus f. albus

Cyrtanthus speciosus f. albus, commonly known as the white-flowered glory-of-the-snow, is a variant of the evergreen perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking white, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features strap-like, dark green leaves that are narrow and evergreen. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
