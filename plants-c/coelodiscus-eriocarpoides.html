Coelodiscus eriocarpoides

Coelodiscus eriocarpoides, commonly known as the velvetpod cissus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by distinctive, hairy, velvety pods. Its botanical features include woody stems and alternate leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitats.
