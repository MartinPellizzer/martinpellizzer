Centaurea doddsii

Centaurea doddsii, commonly known as Dodds' coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which are typically arranged in a conical shape, giving it its common name. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils. It is often found in dune environments and is considered a rare and endangered species.

Centaurea doddsii plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat.
