Camellia lipingensis

Camellia lipingensis, commonly known as the Chinese camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves and produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its edible seeds, which are used to produce oil. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, often growing in mountainous regions.

Camellia lipingensis is a popular choice for gardens and landscapes due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
