Cupania sylvatica

Cupania sylvatica, commonly known as the American persimmon, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by bright orange to red, fleshy fruits. Its fruit is edible, with a sweet, honey-like flavor when fully ripe, and is often consumed by both humans and wildlife.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
