Crepis subglabrescens

Crepis subglabrescens, commonly known as the smooth hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture that distinguishes it from other Crepis species.

The flowers are small and yellowish, often arranged in clusters that attract various pollinators.
