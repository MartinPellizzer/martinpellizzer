Clematis canadensis

Clematis canadensis, commonly known as Canadian vine, is a deciduous climbing plant native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to seven oval leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer. The flowers are typically blue or purple and are borne in clusters, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and can reach lengths of up to 15 meters.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along streambanks.
