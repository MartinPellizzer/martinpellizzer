Cissus meyeri-johannis

Cissus meyeri-johannis, commonly known as the silver dollar plant, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves with a distinctive silver or white variegation pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its trailing habit makes it a popular choice for hanging baskets or as a decorative indoor plant.
