Conoclinium album

Conoclinium album, commonly known as white honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial vine that produces showy, tubular white to pale yellow flowers. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and is often found in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant's botanical name reflects its resemblance to honeysuckle and its association with the genus Conoclinium.
