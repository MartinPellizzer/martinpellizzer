Carex pachyneura

Carex pachyneura, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are often wind-pollinated.

Carex pachyneura plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
