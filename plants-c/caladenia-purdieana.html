Caladenia purdieana

Caladenia purdieana, commonly known as the Purple Sun-orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Western Australia.

It typically grows in sandy soils and is found in open habitats such as heathlands and woodlands. The plant produces a single erect stem with a single, strikingly colored flower that ranges from purple to pink. Its floral structure is characteristic of the orchid family, featuring a labellum that distinguishes it from other species.

Caladenia purdieana is a rare and protected species, highlighting its ecological significance in its native environment.
