Conyza ulmifolia

Conyza ulmifolia, commonly known as smallflower ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and along riverbanks. It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers.

Conyza ulmifolia is often found in wetland ecosystems and can serve as a food source for various insects and wildlife.
