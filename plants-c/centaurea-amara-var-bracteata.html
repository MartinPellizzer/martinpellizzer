Centaurea amara var. bracteata

Centaurea amara var. bracteata, commonly known as bitter centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces bracts, which are modified leaves that surround the flower heads, giving it its specific variety name. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in dry, rocky soils.

This species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
