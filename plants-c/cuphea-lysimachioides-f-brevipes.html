Cuphea lysimachioides f. brevipes

Cuphea lysimachioides f. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed flame flower, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to tropical regions.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with contrasting white or yellow markings. The plant has small, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are typically short and branching. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders or container planting.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
