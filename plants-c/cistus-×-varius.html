Cistus × varius

Cistus × varius, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cistus ladaniferus and Cistus monspeliensis. The plant is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, appearing in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Cistus × varius is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional herbal medicine.
