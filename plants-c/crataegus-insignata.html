Crataegus insignata

Crataegus insignata, commonly known as the California hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. Its leaves are oval-shaped with serrated edges and turn yellow in the fall.

Crataegus insignata thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, sandy soils and full sun.
