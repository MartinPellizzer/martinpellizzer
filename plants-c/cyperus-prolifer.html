Cyperus prolifer

Cyperus prolifer, commonly known as the lovegrass, is a perennial grass native to Africa, Australia, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, spreading growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. Cyperus prolifer thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its ability to form dense mats and its tolerance to various soil conditions.
