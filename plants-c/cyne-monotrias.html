Cyne monotrias

Cyne monotrias, commonly known as the three-lobed fern, is a unique plant characterized by its intricate frond structure.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant features delicate, feather-like leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. Cyne monotrias thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Its botanical significance lies in its role as an indicator species for tropical forest ecosystems.
