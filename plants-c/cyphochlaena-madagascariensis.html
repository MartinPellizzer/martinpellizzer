Cyphochlaena madagascariensis

Cyphochlaena madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and grass-like, while its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences.

Due to its restricted habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
