Cephalostachyum peclardii

Cephalostachyum peclardii, commonly known as the Peclard's bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its clumping growth habit and tall, slender stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers.

This ornamental bamboo is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
