Cephalophyllum apiculatum

Cephalophyllum apiculatum, commonly known as the apiculate headplant, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped growth pattern and fleshy, bluish-green leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are clustered at the top of the stem, giving it its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
