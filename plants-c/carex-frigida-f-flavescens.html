Carex frigida f. flavescens

Carex frigida f. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered sedge, is a variant of the Carex frigida species, characterized by its distinctive yellowish inflorescence.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, typically found in moist to wet habitats such as bogs, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green, and its inflorescence consists of a single, slender spike that bears yellowish to brownish flowers. It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
