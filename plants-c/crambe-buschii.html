Crambe buschii

Crambe buschii, commonly known as the yellow crambe, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants. The plant features alternate, pinnately lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its seeds are small and oval, often used for oil extraction due to their high oil content.

Crambe buschii is valued in agriculture for its potential as a biodiesel crop and as a green manure.
