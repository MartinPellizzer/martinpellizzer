Centaurea paniculata subsp. corymbosa

Centaurea paniculata subsp.

corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features wiry stems and produces clusters of bright blue to purple flowers with distinctive ray florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
