Covellia tuberculata

Covellia tuberculata, commonly known as the rough-leaved sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically features succulent, fleshy leaves that are covered in small, raised bumps, giving it a textured appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Covellia tuberculata is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
