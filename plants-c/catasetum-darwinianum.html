Catasetum darwinianum

Catasetum darwinianum, commonly known as the Darwin's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers. The plant produces a unique structure called a "column," which is a fused stamen and pistil, typical of orchids. Its flowers are typically spurred and can vary in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety.

Catasetum darwinianum is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and ease of cultivation under the right conditions.
