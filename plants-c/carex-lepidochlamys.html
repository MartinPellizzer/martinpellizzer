Carex lepidochlamys

Carex lepidochlamys, commonly known as the silver-spike sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often silver- or grayish-hued, giving it its common name.

Carex lepidochlamys plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
