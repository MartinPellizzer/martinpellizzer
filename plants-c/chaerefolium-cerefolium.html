Chaerefolium cerefolium

Chaerefolium cerefolium, commonly known as the alpine lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that resemble a hand or a fan. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in early summer, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
