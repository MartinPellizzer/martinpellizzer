Columnea jamaicensis

Columnea jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican flame flower, is a climbing perennial plant native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and emits a mild fragrance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Columnea jamaicensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
