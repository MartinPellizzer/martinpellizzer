Clianthus dampieri var. marginatus

Clianthus dampieri var. marginatus, commonly known as the red kauri, is a striking flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that appear in clusters. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is known for its ability to thrive in sandy soils. It has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces distinctive, pea-like flowers that attract pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and public spaces.
