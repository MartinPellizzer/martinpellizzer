Calamus zollingeri subsp. merrillii

Calamus zollingeri subsp.

merrillii, commonly known as the Zollinger's cane palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Thailand and Malaysia.

The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stem and pinnate leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike that bears small, fragrant flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
