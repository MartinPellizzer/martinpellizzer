Calamintha parviflora

Calamintha parviflora, commonly known as narrowleaf catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive scent. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers in whorled clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for garden landscapes.

Calamintha parviflora is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used to treat digestive ailments and as a calming herb.
