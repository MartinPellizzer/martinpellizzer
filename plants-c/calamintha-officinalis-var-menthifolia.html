Calamintha officinalis var. menthifolia

Calamintha officinalis var. menthifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaved germander, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that are narrow and lance-shaped, with a strong mint-like scent. The plant produces small, pale blue or purple flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom in late summer. Its root system is fibrous and contains volatile oils, contributing to its historical use in herbal medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
