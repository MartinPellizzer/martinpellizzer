Centaurea scripczinskyi

Centaurea scripczinskyi, commonly known as the Scripczinsky's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts, which are typical of the Centaurea genus. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Centaurea scripczinskyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
