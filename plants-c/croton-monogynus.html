Croton monogynus

Croton monogynus, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful foliage. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are small and clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Croton monogynus is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

It has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
