Calea ovalis

Calea ovalis, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, white, tubular flowers that open at night and close during the day. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its aromatic, sweet scent. Its leaves are oval-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

Calea ovalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
