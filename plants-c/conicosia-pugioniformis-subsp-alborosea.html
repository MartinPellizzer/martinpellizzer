Conicosia pugioniformis subsp. alborosea

Conicosia pugioniformis subsp.

alborosea, commonly known as the white-flowered ironwood, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, white flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its hardy nature, it is relatively low maintenance and suitable for coastal areas.
