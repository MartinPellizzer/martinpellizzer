Chaetanthera serrata var. argentea

Chaetanthera serrata var. argentea, commonly known as silverleaf chaetanthera, is a variant of the species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-white undersides on the leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub with narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage.
