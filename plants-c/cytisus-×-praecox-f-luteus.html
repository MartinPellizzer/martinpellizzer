Cytisus × praecox f. luteus

Cytisus × praecox f. luteus, commonly known as the golden broom, is a hybrid shrub native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which bloom in early spring, giving it its common name. The plant has slender, wiry stems and small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.
