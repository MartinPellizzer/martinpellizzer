Campanula rochelii

Campanula rochelii, commonly known as Rochel's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

Campanula rochelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, bell-like blooms.
