Caragana pendula

Caragana pendula, commonly known as the weeping shrub or Siberian pea shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that bear small, pea-like flowers in early spring. The plant produces yellow to orange pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or sandy landscapes.

Its ornamental value comes from its distinctive, drooping form and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
