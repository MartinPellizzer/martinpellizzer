Cephalophora doniana

Cephalophora doniana, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has a smooth, greyish-bark trunk and lance-shaped, opposite leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and savannas.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade.
