Chorizanthe douglasii var. albens

Chorizanthe douglasii var. albens, commonly known as silverleaf jointweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland desert regions. It produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are adapted for wind pollination.
