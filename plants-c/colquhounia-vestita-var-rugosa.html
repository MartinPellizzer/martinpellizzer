Colquhounia vestita var. rugosa

Colquhounia vestita var. rugosa, commonly known as the rough-hairy kowhai, is a deciduous shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in spring, attracting native pollinators. Its bark is smooth and grey, and it typically grows in forest margins and rocky areas.

The variety rugosa is distinguished by its more pronounced hairy foliage compared to the typical form.
