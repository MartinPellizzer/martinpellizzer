Cistus rosmarinifolius var. sedjera

Cistus rosmarinifolius var. sedjera, commonly known as the Moroccan rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Morocco.

It features aromatic, silver-gray foliage with a dense, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to rocky and coastal environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils.

Its essential oils are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.
