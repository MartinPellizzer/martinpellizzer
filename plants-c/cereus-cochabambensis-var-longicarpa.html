Cereus cochabambensis var. longicarpa

Cereus cochabambensis var. longicarpa, commonly known as the long-fruited night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to Bolivia.

It is characterized by its spiny, columnar stems and produces large, showy flowers that open at night. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its fruit is elongated and fleshy, containing edible seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
