Carex conica

Carex conica, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgewort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers.

Carex conica is often used in native plant gardens for its ecological value and ornamental appeal.
