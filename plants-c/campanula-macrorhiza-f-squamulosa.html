Campanula macrorhiza f. squamulosa

Campanula macrorhiza f. squamulosa, commonly known as the squamulose bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a common sight in woodland habitats. The plant features basal leaves that are often covered in fine, scale-like hairs, giving it its "squamulosa" designation. Its flowers, which range in color from blue to purple, bloom in late spring to early summer.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded garden environments.
