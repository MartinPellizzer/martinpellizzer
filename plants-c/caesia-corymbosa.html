Caesia corymbosa

Caesia corymbosa, commonly known as the clustered false indigo, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. Caesia corymbosa is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
