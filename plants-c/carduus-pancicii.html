Carduus pancicii

Carduus pancicii, commonly known as Pancici's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, purple flowers and silver-gray, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, bitter taste, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

Carduus pancicii is valued for its ornamental appearance and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
