Caltha rostrata

Caltha rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated crowfoot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family, characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, and the flowers feature five to eight sepals that are often mistaken for petals.

Caltha rostrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
