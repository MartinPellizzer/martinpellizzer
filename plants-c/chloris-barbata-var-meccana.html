Chloris barbata var. meccana

Chloris barbata var. meccana, commonly known as the meccana grass, is a variant of the slender grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and compact growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental and turfgrass applications. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue that adds visual appeal.

It is often used in landscaping for its durability and aesthetic value in garden settings.
