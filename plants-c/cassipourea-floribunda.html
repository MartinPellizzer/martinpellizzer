Cassipourea floribunda

Cassipourea floribunda, commonly known as the pink flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded canopy and clusters of showy, pinkish-red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are typically borne in inflorescences at the ends of branches, and the plant produces small, woody fruits.

Cassipourea floribunda is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
