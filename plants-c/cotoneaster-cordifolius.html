Cotoneaster cordifolius

Cotoneaster cordifolius, commonly known as the heart-leaved cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Asia.

It features ovate, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pink flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a versatile plant for landscaping.
