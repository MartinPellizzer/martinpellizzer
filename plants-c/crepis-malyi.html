Crepis malyi

Crepis malyi, commonly known as the Malaya mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish.

Crepis malyi is often found in high-altitude meadows and is adapted to cold, arid conditions.
