Cleonia punica

Cleonia punica, commonly known as the Mediterranean cleonia, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and it produces small, round berries that contain seeds.

Cleonia punica is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
