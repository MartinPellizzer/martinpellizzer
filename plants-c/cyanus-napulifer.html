Cyanus napulifer

Cyanus napulifer, commonly known as the Italian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

Cyanus napulifer prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
