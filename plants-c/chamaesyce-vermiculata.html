Chamaesyce vermiculata

Chamaesyce vermiculata, commonly known as the silverleaf spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features succulent, fleshy leaves that are covered in a fine, white coating, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in disturbed habitats.

Due to its invasive tendencies, it can outcompete native vegetation in certain regions.
