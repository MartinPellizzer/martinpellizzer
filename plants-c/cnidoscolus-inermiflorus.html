Cnidoscolus inermiflorus

Cnidoscolus inermiflorus, commonly known as the woolly spurge, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and covered in fine, soft hairs.

Cnidoscolus inermiflorus is often found in dry, disturbed habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.
