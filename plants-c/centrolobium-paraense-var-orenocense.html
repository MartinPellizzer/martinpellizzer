Centrolobium paraense var. orenocense

Centrolobium paraense var. orenocense, commonly known as the Orenoco centrolobium, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest region of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically alternate and pinnately arranged. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forest growth.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a role in supporting local biodiversity and maintaining soil stability.
