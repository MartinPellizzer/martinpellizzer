Cryptogramma acrostichoides var. sitchensis

Cryptogramma acrostichoides var. sitchensis, commonly known as Sitka cryptogramma, is a variety of the common cryptogramma found in coastal regions.

It is a fern that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near the coast or in rocky areas. The plant has delicate, feather-like fronds that are usually dark green and finely divided. It produces spores in small, round clusters on the underside of its leaves.

This variety is well adapted to the cool, humid climates of the Pacific Northwest.
