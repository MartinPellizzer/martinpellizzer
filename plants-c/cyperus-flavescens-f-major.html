Cyperus flavescens f. major

Cyperus flavescens f. major, commonly known as the giant flatsedge, is a variant of the flatsedge genus native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that are typically pale yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in wetland environments, including marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration efforts.
