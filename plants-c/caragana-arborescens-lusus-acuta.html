Caragana arborescens lusus acuta

Caragana arborescens lusus acuta, commonly known as the narrowleaf shrub or Siberian peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of Central Asia and Eastern Europe.

It features slender, multi-branched stems that often grow in dense clusters, with small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, followed by pods that contain several seeds. It is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in poor soils and extreme temperatures.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient plant often used in ecological restoration projects.
