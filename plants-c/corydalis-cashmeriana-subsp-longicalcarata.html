Corydalis cashmeriana subsp. longicalcarata

Corydalis cashmeriana subsp.

longicalcarata, commonly known as the Kashmir corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas. It is a perennial herb with slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces delicate, pinkish-purple flowers in early spring, which are clustered in loose racemes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a valued species in alpine botany and horticulture.
