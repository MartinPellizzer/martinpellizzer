Caletia hexandra

Caletia hexandra, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow-orange flowers that resemble small bells. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface depending on the variety.

Caletia hexandra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
