Chamaecrista benthamii

Chamaecrista benthamii, commonly known as the slender crownflower, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate leaves. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers in dense clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It has adapted to survive in dry conditions with deep roots and a waxy leaf coating to reduce water loss.

Chamaecrista benthamii is often found in sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
