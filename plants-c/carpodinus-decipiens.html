Carpodinus decipiens

Carpodinus decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, inconspicuous flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-gray hue due to dense pubescence.

Carpodinus decipiens is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.
