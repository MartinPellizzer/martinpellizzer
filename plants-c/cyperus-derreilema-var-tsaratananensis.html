Cyperus derreilema var. tsaratananensis

Cyperus derreilema var. tsaratananensis, commonly known as the Madagascar sedge, is a perennial herb native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, showcasing narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. It is notable for its ecological significance in supporting local biodiversity and its potential use in wetland restoration projects.

Its unique morphology and habitat preferences make it an important species in its native ecosystem.
