Crataegus antimima

Crataegus antimima, commonly known as the Antimina hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is distinguished by its pinnate leaves and persistent, red fruit.

Its hardy nature makes it adaptable to various soil types and light conditions.
