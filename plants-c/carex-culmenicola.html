Carex culmenicola

Carex culmenicola, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands, bogs, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct triangular cross-section, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Carex culmenicola plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
