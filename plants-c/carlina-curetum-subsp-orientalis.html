Carlina curetum subsp. orientalis

Carlina curetum subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern cudweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features basal rosettes of narrow, hairy leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers in composite inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn.

The species is characterized by its robust root system and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
