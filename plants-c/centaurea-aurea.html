Centaurea aurea

Centaurea aurea, commonly known as the golden centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in dense, spherical clusters. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas. It blooms from late spring to early summer, producing numerous small flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea aurea is a perennial herb that can grow up to 30 cm tall, with a compact and upright growth habit.
