Croton escathos

Croton escathos, commonly known as the scarlet croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that range from yellow to red and orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody capsules containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
