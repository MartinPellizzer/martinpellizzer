Calamus densiflorus

Calamus densiflorus, commonly known as the denseflower arrowroot, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric.

It is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, characterized by its tall, upright stems and clusters of small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces rhizomes that are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, waxy surface that helps it retain moisture in its native habitat.

Calamus densiflorus is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its practical uses in various cultural contexts.
