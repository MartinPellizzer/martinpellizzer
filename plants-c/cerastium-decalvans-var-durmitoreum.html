Cerastium decalvans var. durmitoreum

Cerastium decalvans var. durmitoreum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to alpine regions of Europe.

It features small, hairy leaves that are typically opposite and ovate in shape. The plant produces clusters of white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its delicate structure and specific habitat requirements, it is often found in mountain meadows and scree slopes.
