Corymbia tumescens

Corymbia tumescens, commonly known as the red blood gum, is a species of eucalyptus native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark that often appears reddish or brownish in color. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, which bloom in summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a leathery texture.

Corymbia tumescens is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient and drought-tolerant species.
