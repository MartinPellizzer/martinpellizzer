Chaetochloa chondrachne

Chaetochloa chondrachne, commonly known as the tufted hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves that form dense tufts, often growing in moist soils along the edges of ponds and marshes. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomes help stabilize soil and prevent erosion in wetland habitats.

Chaetochloa chondrachne plays an important ecological role in supporting aquatic and terrestrial wildlife.
