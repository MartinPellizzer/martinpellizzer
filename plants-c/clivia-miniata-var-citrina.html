Clivia miniata var. citrina

Clivia miniata var. citrina, commonly known as the yellow clivia, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, strap-like leaves that are a vibrant green, often with a slight yellowish tinge. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are highly attractive to pollinators. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
