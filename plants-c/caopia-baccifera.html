Caopia baccifera

Caopia baccifera, commonly known as the red-fruited acanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a distinct reddish hue. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, fleshy seeds.

Caopia baccifera is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant colors and unique foliage.
