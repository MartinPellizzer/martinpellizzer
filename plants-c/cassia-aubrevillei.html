Cassia aubrevillei

Cassia aubrevillei, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with multiple leaflets and produces small, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Cassia aubrevillei is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
