Cyperus cristatus var. nigritanus

Cyperus cristatus var. nigritanus, commonly known as the black rush, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, dark green leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense mats. It is characterized by its dark, almost blackish coloration, which gives it its common name.

This variety is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its striking appearance and adaptability to moist conditions.
