Crocus hadriaticus subsp. peloponnesiacus

Crocus hadriaticus subsp.

peloponnesiacus, commonly known as the Peloponnesian crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky soils and dry habitats, often found in southern Europe.

The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during late winter to early spring. Its leaves are basal, narrow, and grass-like, emerging after the flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and floral characteristics.
