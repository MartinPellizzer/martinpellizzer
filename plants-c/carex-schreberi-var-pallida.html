Carex schreberi var. pallida

Carex schreberi var. pallida, commonly known as the pale sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of water bodies. Its slender, grass-like leaves are usually pale green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters.

The pale sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland environments.
