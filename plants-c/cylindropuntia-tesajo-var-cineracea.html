Cylindropuntia tesajo var. cineracea

Cylindropuntia tesajo var. cineracea, commonly known as the silver cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, segmented stems that are covered in a dense layer of white, powdery bloom, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are pollinated by various insects and birds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to extreme temperatures and low precipitation.

Due to its spiny structure, it is often used as a natural barrier in desert landscapes.
