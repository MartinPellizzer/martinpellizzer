Calandrinia ciliata

Calandrinia ciliata, commonly known as the pink fairy lantern, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant pink to red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling small lanterns. The plant has erect, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are opposite, ovate, and also hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.

Calandrinia ciliata is a popular choice for rock gardens and containers due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
