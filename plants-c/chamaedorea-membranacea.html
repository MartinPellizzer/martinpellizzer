Chamaedorea membranacea

Chamaedorea membranacea, commonly known as the velvet palm or Mexican bamboo, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Central America.

It features slender, arching stems with narrow, dark green leaves that have a leathery texture. The plant is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low light requirements and minimal maintenance needs. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Its compact size and elegant appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental purposes.
