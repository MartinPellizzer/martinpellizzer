Chenopodium subpalmatum

Chenopodium subpalmatum, commonly known as New Mexico goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are lobed and resemble a hand, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Chenopodium subpalmatum is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in dry conditions.
