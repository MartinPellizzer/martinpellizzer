Corylus rostrata var. fargesii

Corylus rostrata var. fargesii, commonly known as the Farges' hornbeam, is a variant of the hornbeam species native to China.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its distinctive, elongated nuts with a narrow beak-like projection. The leaves are typically oval with serrated edges and a dark green upper surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This plant is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
