Calendula aegyptiaca var. microcephala

Calendula aegyptiaca var. microcephala, commonly known as the small-headed African calendula, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow to orange flowers. The plant produces small, rounded flower heads that are typically less than 2 cm in diameter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and branching.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
