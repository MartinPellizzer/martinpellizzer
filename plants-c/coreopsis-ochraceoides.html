Coreopsis ochraceoides

Coreopsis ochraceoides, commonly known as the yellow coreopsis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers with a distinctive orange center, blooming from late spring to early fall. The plant has slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for wildflower gardens.

Its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies enhances its ecological value.
