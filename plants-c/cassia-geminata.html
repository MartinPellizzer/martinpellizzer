Cassia geminata

Cassia geminata, commonly known as the twinleaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Cassia geminata is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
