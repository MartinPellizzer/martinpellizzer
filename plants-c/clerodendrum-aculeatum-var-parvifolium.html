Clerodendrum aculeatum var. parvifolium

Clerodendrum aculeatum var. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved flame bush, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, typically blooming in summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display.
