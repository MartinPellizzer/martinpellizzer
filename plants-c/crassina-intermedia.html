Crassina intermedia

Crassina intermedia, commonly known as the common spiny holly.

It is a shrub native to the eastern United States, characterized by its spiny leaves and small, red berries. The plant has a woody stem with thorn-like projections, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have a glossy surface.

Crassina intermedia thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.
