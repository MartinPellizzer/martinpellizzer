Centradenia grandifolia

Centradenia grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered centradenia, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and large, glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, attracting various pollinators. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind and animals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
