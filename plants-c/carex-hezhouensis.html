Carex hezhouensis

Carex hezhouensis, commonly known as the Hezhou sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
