Cyperus ascopusillus

Cyperus ascopusillus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often found in disturbed soils near water bodies. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces three-sided, fibrous seeds.

Cyperus ascopusillus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
