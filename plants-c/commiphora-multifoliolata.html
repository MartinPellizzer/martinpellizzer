Commiphora multifoliolata

Commiphora multifoliolata, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often lobed leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and is known for its aromatic resin, which has been used traditionally for medicinal and incense purposes. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
