Corydalis ternatifolia

Corydalis ternatifolia, commonly known as the three-lobed corydalis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant features trifoliate leaves, which give it its specific epithet "ternatifolia," meaning three-lobed. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland habitats.

Corydalis ternatifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ease of cultivation.
