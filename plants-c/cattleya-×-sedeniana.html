Cattleya × sedeniana

Cattleya × sedeniana, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cattleya mossiae and Cattleya bicolor.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, showy flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, pink, and purple. It has pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in less fertile soils.

Cattleya × sedeniana is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
