Cytisus nigricans var. sericeus

Cytisus nigricans var. sericeus, commonly known as the silverleaf broom, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-haired stems and small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The leaves are alternate, pinnate, and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, rocky habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
