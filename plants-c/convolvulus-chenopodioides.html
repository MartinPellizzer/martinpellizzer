Convolvulus chenopodioides

Convolvulus chenopodioides, commonly known as desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb with trailing stems that produce small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to red. The plant has small, scale-like leaves and thrives in sandy or rocky soils, often found in disturbed or dry environments. It is adapted to survive in low-water conditions, with deep roots that allow it to access moisture from deeper soil layers.

The flowers typically bloom in the morning, giving the plant its common name.
