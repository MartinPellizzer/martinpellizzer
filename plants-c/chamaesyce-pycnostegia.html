Chamaesyce pycnostegia

Chamaesyce pycnostegia, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
