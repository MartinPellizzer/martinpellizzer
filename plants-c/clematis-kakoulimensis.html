Clematis kakoulimensis

Clematis kakoulimensis, commonly known as the Cyprus clematis, is a flowering plant native to the island of Cyprus.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, usually blue or purple flowers. The plant features trifoliate leaves with three lance-shaped leaflets and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
