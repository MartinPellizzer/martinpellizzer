Commiphora leandriana

Commiphora leandriana, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing plants. The plant is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap, which exudes from the bark and is used in traditional medicine and incense. Its leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a leathery texture, while its flowers are small and clustered in panicles.

Commiphora leandriana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
