Cinnamomum burmanni var. linearifolium

Cinnamomum burmanni var. linearifolium, commonly known as the linear-leaved cinnamon tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by oval-shaped berries containing a single seed. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in lowland forests or secondary vegetation.

This variety is valued for its aromatic bark and leaves, which have been used in traditional medicine and as a spice.
