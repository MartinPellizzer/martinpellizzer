Copaifera martii

Copaifera martii, commonly known as Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its thick, aromatic bark and can grow up to 30 meters tall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers and heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its most notable feature is the resin it exudes, which is used in traditional medicine and perfumery.

The botanical significance of Copaifera martii lies in its role as a source of valuable natural compounds and its ecological importance in the rainforest ecosystem.
