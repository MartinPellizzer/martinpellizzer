Casuarina campestris subsp. tessellata

Casuarina campestris subsp.

tessellata, commonly known as the tessellated she-oak, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its distinctive, scale-like leaves that form dense, cylindrical structures.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by woody, cylindrical cones. It is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, often found in dune environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and stabilizing coastal dunes.
