Cassia viscosopilosa

Cassia viscosopilosa, commonly known as sticky pod cassia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and produces yellow flowers that are often visited by pollinators. The plant is notable for its distinctive, sticky, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
