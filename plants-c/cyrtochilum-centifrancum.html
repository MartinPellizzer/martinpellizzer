Cyrtochilum centifrancum

Cyrtochilum centifrancum, commonly known as the Centifrant Orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small, terrestrial orchid with a compact, leafy growth habit and produces delicate, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink. The plant typically blooms during the rainy season, showcasing a unique floral structure with lip-like petals. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Cyrtochilum centifrancum is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
