Cattleya forbesii f. rosea

Cattleya forbesii f. rosea, commonly known as the Rosea Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid prized for its vibrant, pinkish-white flowers with a striking rose-colored lip.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its foliage consists of broad, elliptical leaves that are often dark green in color.

The bloom period usually occurs in the spring, producing clusters of fragrant, showy flowers that attract pollinators.
