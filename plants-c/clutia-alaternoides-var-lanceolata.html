Clutia alaternoides var. lanceolata

Clutia alaternoides var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf clutia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often blooming during the wet season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or near water bodies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some local cultures.
