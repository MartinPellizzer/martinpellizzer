Canna fuchsina

Canna fuchsina, commonly known as the red canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Cannaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy leaves and vibrant, fiery red flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet, with broad, arrow-shaped leaves that are a deep green. Its inflorescence consists of a cluster of three to five bracts, each bearing a single, tubular flower that opens in a spiral pattern.

Canna fuchsina thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
