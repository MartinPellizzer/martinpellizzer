Cactus rosea

Cactus rosea, commonly known as the roseate cactus, is a unique hybrid plant resulting from the cross between a prickly pear cactus and a rose.

It features a compact, rounded shape with a thick, fleshy stem that can store water, typical of desert plants. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual element to its appearance. Its foliage is covered in spines, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

Cactus rosea is well adapted to arid environments, making it a resilient and ornamental choice for xeriscaping.
