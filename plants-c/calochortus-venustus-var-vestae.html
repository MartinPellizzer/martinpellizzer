Calochortus venustus var. vestae

Calochortus venustus var. vestae, commonly known as the golden cupcup, is a striking member of the Liliaceae family native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a contrasting purple or red throat. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting flowers.
