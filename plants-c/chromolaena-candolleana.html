Chromolaena candolleana

Chromolaena candolleana, commonly known as the false chinchona, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by elongated, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings due to its adaptability.

Chromolaena candolleana is also known for its potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
