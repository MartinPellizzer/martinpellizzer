Cinchona calisaya

Cinchona calisaya, commonly known as the Calisaya quinine tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its ornamental leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant is notable for its medicinal properties, particularly the presence of alkaloids such as quinine, which have historically been used in the treatment of malaria. The tree can reach heights of up to 15 meters and is valued both for its ecological role and its historical significance in pharmaceutical science.
