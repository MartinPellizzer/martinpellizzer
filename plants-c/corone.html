Corone

Corone, commonly known as the crown flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers with bright colors such as yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically has a compact growth habit with a central stem and multiple flowering heads. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern.

Corone species are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
