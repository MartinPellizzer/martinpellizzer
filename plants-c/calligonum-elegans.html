Calligonum elegans

Calligonum elegans, commonly known as the elegant goosefoot, is a perennial shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically alternate and have a distinctive heart-shaped form. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is well adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and the ability to thrive in poor soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological restoration projects in its native habitat.
