Calanthe brevicornu var. megalophora

Calanthe brevicornu var. megalophora, commonly known as the giant-headed calanthe, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically white or pale pink with a prominent, elongated lip. The plant features broad, strap-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-draining, rich soil to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts for its beauty and rarity.
