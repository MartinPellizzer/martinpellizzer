Carex collina

Carex collina, commonly known as slender marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. Native to wetland environments, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
