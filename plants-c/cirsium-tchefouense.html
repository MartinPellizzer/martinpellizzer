Cirsium tchefouense

Cirsium tchefouense, commonly known as the Tchefou thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as hillsides and wastelands. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Cirsium tchefouense is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
