Cestrum elegans var. longiflorum

Cestrum elegans var. longiflorum, commonly known as the long-flowered nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom at night, attracting moths and other nocturnal pollinators. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters, with glossy, dark green leaves. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, orange-red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
