Campuloclinium crenatum

Campuloclinium crenatum, commonly known as the hairy leaved blueweed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers arranged in loose clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a desirable plant for native gardens and ecological restoration projects.
