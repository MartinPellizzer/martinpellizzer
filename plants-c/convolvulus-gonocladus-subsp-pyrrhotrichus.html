Convolvulus gonocladus subsp. pyrrhotrichus

Convolvulus gonocladus subsp.

pyrrhotrichus, commonly known as the redstem morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The flowers are typically pale yellow to orange, with five petals and a distinctive funnel shape. It produces small, dark brown seeds enclosed in a capsule, which are dispersed by wind.

This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.
