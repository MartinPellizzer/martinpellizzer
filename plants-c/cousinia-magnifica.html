Cousinia magnifica

Cousinia magnifica, commonly known as the magnificent cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and branched. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Cousinia magnifica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
