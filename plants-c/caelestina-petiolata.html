Caelestina petiolata

Caelestina petiolata, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, sky-blue to white flowers. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Its delicate appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and native plant landscapes.
