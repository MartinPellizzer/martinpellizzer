Campanula ciscaucasica

Campanula ciscaucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, adapting to its alpine environment.

Campanula ciscaucasica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
