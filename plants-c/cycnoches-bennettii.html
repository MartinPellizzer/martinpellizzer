Cycnoches bennettii

Cycnoches bennettii, commonly known as Bennett's cycnoches, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in mountainous or forested areas. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Cycnoches bennettii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
