Cyperus sesquiflorus var. cylindricus

Cyperus sesquiflorus var. cylindricus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences are adapted to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
