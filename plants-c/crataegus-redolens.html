Crataegus redolens

Crataegus redolens, commonly known as the sweetbriar rose, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart in flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

Crataegus redolens is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in hedgerow construction.
