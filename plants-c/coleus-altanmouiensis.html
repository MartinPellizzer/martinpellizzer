Coleus altanmouiensis

Coleus altanmouiensis, commonly known as the Altanmouensis coleus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are often brightly colored, displaying shades of red, orange, and yellow, which make it a popular ornamental plant.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, thriving in warm, humid climates.
