Cerasus opaca

Cerasus opaca, commonly known as the black cherry, is a species of Prunus native to eastern North America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by dark red, edible berries in the fall.

Its fruits are an important food source for various wildlife species, including birds and mammals.
