Carex juncea

Carex juncea, commonly known as slender flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, with a distinct triangular cross-section characteristic of sedges.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown, and it plays an important role in wetland ecosystems.
