Chlorophytum recurvifolium

Chlorophytum recurvifolium, commonly known as the curly-leaf spider plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically narrow and have a wavy, curling edge. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers on slender stalks during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Chlorophytum recurvifolium is popular as a houseplant due to its low maintenance and ability to propagate easily through offsets.
