Croton polymorphus var. glabrescens

Croton polymorphus var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leaved croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically smooth and glossy, with a variety of leaf shapes and colors. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
