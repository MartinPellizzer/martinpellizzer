Calliandra tweediei var. sancti-pauli

Calliandra tweediei var. sancti-pauli, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, pink to red inflorescences that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it a beneficial addition to gardens.
