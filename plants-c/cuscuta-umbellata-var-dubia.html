Cuscuta umbellata var. dubia

Cuscuta umbellata var. dubia, commonly known as the slender dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies entirely on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a complex system of haustoria to attach to and extract resources from its host. This variety is typically found in arid or semi-arid regions, often growing on grasses and other herbaceous plants. Its life cycle involves seed dispersal through the wind or by adhering to animals.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can significantly impact the growth and health of its host plants.
