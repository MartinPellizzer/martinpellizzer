Clerodendrum macrostachyum

Clerodendrum macrostachyum, commonly known as the giant flame bush, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom in summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
