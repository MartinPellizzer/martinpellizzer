Ceanothus sorediatus var. glaber

Ceanothus sorediatus var. glaber, commonly known as the smooth ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has smooth, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of wax. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various wildlife species.
