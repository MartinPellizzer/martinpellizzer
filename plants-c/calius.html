Calius

Calius, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinct orange or red throat. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Calius has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
