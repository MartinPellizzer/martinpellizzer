Carex ligerina

Carex ligerina, commonly known as the silverhair sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Carex ligerina thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
