Centaurea schouwii

Centaurea schouwii, commonly known as Schouw's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its bright blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, and the plant has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces.

Centaurea schouwii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
