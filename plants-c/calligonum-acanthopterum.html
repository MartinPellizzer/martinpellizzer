Calligonum acanthopterum

Calligonum acanthopterum, commonly known as the spiny tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and is often found in saline or alkaline soils. The plant has small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. Its flowers are typically pink or reddish and appear in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Calligonum acanthopterum is well adapted to drought conditions and plays a role in stabilizing sandy or eroded soils.
