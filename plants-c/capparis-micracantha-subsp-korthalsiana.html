Capparis micracantha subsp. korthalsiana

Capparis micracantha subsp.

korthalsiana, commonly known as the Korthals's caper, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It features woody stems with thorny branches and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels.

The plant's leaves are simple, opposite, and have a slightly succulent texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
