Calamintha patavina var. rotundifolia

Calamintha patavina var. rotundifolia, commonly known as roundleaf caraway, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that are round or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in umbels, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a globular, three-lobed capsule containing several seeds.

This variety is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
