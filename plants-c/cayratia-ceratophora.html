Cayratia ceratophora

Cayratia ceratophora, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery down. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by small, round berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.
