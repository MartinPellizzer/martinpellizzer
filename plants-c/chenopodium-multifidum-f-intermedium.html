Chenopodium multifidum f. intermedium

Chenopodium multifidum f. intermedium, commonly known as the intermediate goosefoot, is a variant of the goosefoot plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often lobed or deeply divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
