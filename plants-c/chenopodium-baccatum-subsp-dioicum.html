Chenopodium baccatum subsp. dioicum

Chenopodium baccatum subsp.

dioicum, commonly known as red goosefoot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America. It is characterized by its alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often reddish or purplish in color.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense, branched clusters called cymes. Its fruit is a dry, three-angled capsule that contains three seeds.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.
