Crotalaria nana var. umbellata

Crotalaria nana var. umbellata, commonly known as the dwarf rattlebox, is a small herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 30 cm and features trifoliate leaves with small, rounded lobes. Its flowers are clustered in umbel-like inflorescences, giving it its scientific name.

The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in certain agricultural contexts.
