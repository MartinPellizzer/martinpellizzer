Croton guaraniticus f. intermedius

Croton guaraniticus f. intermedius, commonly known as the Brazilian croton, is a shrub or small tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, tropical conditions and is often found in secondary forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and collectors.
