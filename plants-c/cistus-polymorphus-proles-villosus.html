Cistus polymorphus proles villosus

Cistus polymorphus proles villosus, commonly known as the woolly rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features gray-green, hairy leaves that help reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its woody stems are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

This species is well adapted to rocky soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.
