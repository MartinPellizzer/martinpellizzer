Calamus crinitus

Calamus crinitus, commonly known as the hairy sweet flag, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet, swampy areas and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in spadices, which are typical of the Araceae family, though it is classified in Arecaceae. Calamus crinitus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

Its rhizomes are sometimes harvested for use in herbal remedies and as a source of aromatic compounds.
