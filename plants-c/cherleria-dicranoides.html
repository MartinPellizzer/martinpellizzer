Cherleria dicranoides

Cherleria dicranoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed grass, is a species of grass found in various parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and along roadsides. It has a fibrous root system and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its ecological role includes providing forage for livestock and contributing to soil stabilization.
