Carex melanolepis

Carex melanolepis, commonly known as black-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its leaves are dark green to blackish in color, giving it its common name, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters.

Carex melanolepis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
