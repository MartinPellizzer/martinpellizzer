Centaurea subarmata

Centaurea subarmata, commonly known as the Armenian star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces striking purple to blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or dry habitats. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Centaurea subarmata is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
