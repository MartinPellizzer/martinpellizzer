Clinacanthus siamensis

Clinacanthus siamensis, commonly known as the Siam devil's backbone, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking, spiny stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Clinacanthus siamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
