Carex varia f. colorata

Carex varia f. colorata, commonly known as the colorful sedge, is a striking ornamental plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant is notable for its vibrant, colorful inflorescences that display shades of red, purple, and pink, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes. It thrives in moist to wet soils and prefers partial to full shade, often found in swampy areas or along stream banks.

Its unique foliage and seasonal color changes add visual interest throughout the growing season.
