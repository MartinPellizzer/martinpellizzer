Capparis decidua

Capparis decidua, commonly known as the desert fig or Indian date tree, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia and Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, fleshy fruits resembling small figs. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
