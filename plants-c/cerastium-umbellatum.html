Cerastium umbellatum

Cerastium umbellatum, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features small, white flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and thrives in moist, shaded environments. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Cerastium umbellatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wildflower gardens.
