Commelina salicifolia var. angustata

Commelina salicifolia var. angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf Commelina, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small, blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are often narrow and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are clustered in inflorescences and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
