Cotinus arenarius

Cotinus arenarius, commonly known as the velvetleaf tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Anacardiaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, fuzzy, green leaves that turn red or orange in the fall. It produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits.

Cotinus arenarius is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
