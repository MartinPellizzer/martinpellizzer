Cyclea polypetala

Cyclea polypetala, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features showy, pinkish-purple flowers that are lip-shaped and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

Cyclea polypetala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
