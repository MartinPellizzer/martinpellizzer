Ceroxylon utile

Ceroxylon utile, commonly known as the wax palm, is a striking tropical plant native to the Caribbean, particularly found in regions like Cuba, Hispaniola, and Jamaica.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a thick layer of wax-like substance, which provides protection against environmental stressors. The plant produces large, feather-like leaves that can reach several meters in length, making it a distinctive feature of its habitat. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing at the top of the plant, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by various wildlife.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, Ceroxylon utile is also considered a symbol of national identity in some Caribbean countries.
