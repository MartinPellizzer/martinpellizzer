Calea montana

Calea montana, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a striking plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white or pink flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant has a distinct, often hairy stem and heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers emit a sweet, fragrant scent that attracts nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Calea montana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
