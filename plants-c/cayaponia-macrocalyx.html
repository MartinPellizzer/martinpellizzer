Cayaponia macrocalyx

Cayaponia macrocalyx, commonly known as the giant cayaponia, is a climbing shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 10 centimeters in diameter, typically in shades of white and pink. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and prominent veins. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.
