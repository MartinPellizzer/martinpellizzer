Calamagrostis emirnensis

Calamagrostis emirnensis, commonly known as the Emirnensis reed, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver-gray in color. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments, such as marshes and streambanks. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

Calamagrostis emirnensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
