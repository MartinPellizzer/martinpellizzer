Cerastium triviale subsp. fontanum

Cerastium triviale subsp.

fontanum, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its mat-forming growth habit and small, clustered flowers.

The plant features hairy stems and leaves that are typically rounded and lobed, adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions. Its flowers, which are usually pink or white, bloom in early summer and are pollinated by insects.

This subspecies is often found in rocky soils and open habitats, contributing to the biodiversity of alpine ecosystems.
