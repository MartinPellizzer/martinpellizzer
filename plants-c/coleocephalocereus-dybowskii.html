Coleocephalocereus dybowskii

Coleocephalocereus dybowskii, commonly known as the Dybowskii candelabra cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that often branch out into a tree-like structure, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically nocturnal in bloom. Its thick, ribbed stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh desert conditions.

This cactus is also noted for its slow growth rate and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique form and resilience.
