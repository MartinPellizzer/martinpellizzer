Cissus bryophyllum

Cissus bryophyllum, commonly known as the silver vine, is a trailing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by translucent, waxy berries. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions.

Cissus bryophyllum is also known for its ability to climb and trail, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and shelves.
