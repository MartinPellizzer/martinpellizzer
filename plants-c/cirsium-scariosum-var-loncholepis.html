Cirsium scariosum var. loncholepis

Cirsium scariosum var. loncholepis, commonly known as the slender thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a deep taproot system for water absorption. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family.

This variety is often found in open woodlands and grasslands at higher elevations.
