Coreopsis breviligulata

Coreopsis breviligulata, commonly known as the narrowleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring lance-shaped leaves and bright yellow to orange flowers. The plant thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and natural habitats. Its botanical features include showy ray flowers and a central disk, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Coreopsis breviligulata is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
