Crocus vernus var. lusitanicus

Crocus vernus var. lusitanicus, commonly known as the Portuguese crocus, is a bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, early spring flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.

This variety is closely related to other crocus species but is distinguished by its specific geographic origin and flower characteristics.
