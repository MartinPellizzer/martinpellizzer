Carex pseudofoetida subsp. acrifolia

Carex pseudofoetida subsp.

acrifolia, commonly known as the red-stemmed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, particularly China. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value comes from its striking reddish stems and foliage, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plants.
