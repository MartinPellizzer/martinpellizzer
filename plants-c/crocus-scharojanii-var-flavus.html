Crocus scharojanii var. flavus

Crocus scharojanii var. flavus, commonly known as the Persian yellow crocus, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically between February and April. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, cup-shaped flowers with three petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its vibrant, early-blooming flowers.
