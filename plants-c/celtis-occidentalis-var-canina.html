Celtis occidentalis var. canina

Celtis occidentalis var. canina, commonly known as the dogwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its small, inconspicuous flowers and distinctive, rounded leaves. The tree produces small, red drupes that are an important food source for birds and small mammals. It typically grows in a variety of soil types and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its slow growth rate and ornamental value make it a popular choice for urban landscapes.
