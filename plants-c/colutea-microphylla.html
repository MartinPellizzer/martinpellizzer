Colutea microphylla

Colutea microphylla, commonly known as the lesser corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its compound leaves and legume pods. The plant produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink or purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet being narrow and pointed.

Colutea microphylla is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cooler, high-altitude environments.
