Carex albolutescens var. glomerata

Carex albolutescens var. glomerata, commonly known as the white-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of white to pale yellow flowers, which are typical of the sedge genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water gardens and bog landscapes.
