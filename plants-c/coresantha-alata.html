Coresantha alata

Coresantha alata, commonly known as the winged coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in the summer months. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its distinctive feature is the presence of wing-like structures on the stems, which give it its scientific name.

Coresantha alata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and along roadsides.
