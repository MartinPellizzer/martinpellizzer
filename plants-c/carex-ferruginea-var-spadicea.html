Carex ferruginea var. spadicea

Carex ferruginea var. spadicea, commonly known as the rusty sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually dark green with reddish-brown markings, and the flowers are small and clustered in spikes.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
