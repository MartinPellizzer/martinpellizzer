Cochlearia henryi

Cochlearia henryi, commonly known as Henry's cochlearia, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of central Asia, particularly in China. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early summer. Its leaves are typically basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine, white hairs.

Cochlearia henryi is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.
