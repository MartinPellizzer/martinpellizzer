Cynanchum hemsleyanum

Cynanchum hemsleyanum, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.

Cynanchum hemsleyanum is also used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and resilience.
