Ceropegia chipiaensis

Ceropegia chipiaensis, commonly known as the Chipiaensis African Violet, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and tubular flowers. The plant produces small, fragrant blooms that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ceropegia chipiaensis is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
