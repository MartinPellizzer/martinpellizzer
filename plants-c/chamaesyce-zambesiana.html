Chamaesyce zambesiana

Chamaesyce zambesiana, commonly known as the Zambesian sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with similar climates.
