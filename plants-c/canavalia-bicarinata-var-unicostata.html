Canavalia bicarinata var. unicostata

Canavalia bicarinata var. unicostata, commonly known as the single-costate cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a single prominent vein on each leaflet. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing several seeds. It is adapted to warm climates and is often grown as a forage crop or for its edible seeds.

Its botanical features make it valuable in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
