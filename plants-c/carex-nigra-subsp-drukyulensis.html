Carex nigra subsp. drukyulensis

Carex nigra subsp.

drukyulensis, commonly known as the Druk Yul sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Himalayan region. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests and stream sides. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
