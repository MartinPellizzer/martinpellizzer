Carex cespitosa var. intricata

Carex cespitosa var. intricata, commonly known as the hooked sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly curled or twisted appearance, giving it its varietal name.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typically found in clusters at the tips of the stems.
