Cistus salviifolius proles rhodanensis

Cistus salviifolius proles rhodanensis, commonly known as the Rhodanian salvia-leaved rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and clusters of pink to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance and heat resistance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a soft, fuzzy texture, while its stems are woody and sprawling.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
