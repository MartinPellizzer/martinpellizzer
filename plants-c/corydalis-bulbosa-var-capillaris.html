Corydalis bulbosa var. capillaris

Corydalis bulbosa var. capillaris, commonly known as the slender corydalis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces tuberous roots that serve as storage organs, allowing it to survive in harsh environmental conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial shade, often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
