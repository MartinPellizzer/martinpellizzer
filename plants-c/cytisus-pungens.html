Cytisus pungens

Cytisus pungens, commonly known as the spiked broom, is a flowering shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry landscapes.

Cytisus pungens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
