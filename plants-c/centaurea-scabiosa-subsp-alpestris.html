Centaurea scabiosa subsp. alpestris

Centaurea scabiosa subsp.

alpestris, commonly known as the alpine cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk florets.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. Its flowers are usually pink to purple in color, with a distinctive scabious appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators.
