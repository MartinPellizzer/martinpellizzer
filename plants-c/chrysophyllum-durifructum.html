Chrysophyllum durifructum

Chrysophyllum durifructum, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are typically round or egg-shaped with a smooth, yellowish skin. The fruit's interior contains a white, juicy pulp surrounding numerous small, hard seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and its fruit, which is consumed fresh or used in culinary applications.
