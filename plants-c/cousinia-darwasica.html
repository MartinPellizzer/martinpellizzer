Cousinia darwasica

Cousinia darwasica, commonly known as the Darwasica cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a dense cluster at the end of each stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments.

Cousinia darwasica is often found in rocky or sandy habitats, showcasing its resilience to extreme conditions.
