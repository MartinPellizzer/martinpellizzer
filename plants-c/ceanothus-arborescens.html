Ceanothus arborescens

Ceanothus arborescens, commonly known as the California lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features fragrant, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.

Its ecological importance includes providing nectar for pollinators and supporting local wildlife.
