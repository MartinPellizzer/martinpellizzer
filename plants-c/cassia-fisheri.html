Cassia fisheri

Cassia fisheri, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in pendulous clusters. The plant produces elongated pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Cassia fisheri thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
