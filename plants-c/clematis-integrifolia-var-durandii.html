Clematis integrifolia var. durandii

Clematis integrifolia var. durandii, commonly known as the Durand's Clematis, is a climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and its stems are covered in fine, silky hairs. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

This variety is known for its vigorous growth and ability to climb trellises or walls with ease.
