Chrysanthemum dissectum

Chrysanthemum dissectum, commonly known as the Japanese chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and vibrant, pompon-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun. It is a popular ornamental plant used in gardens and floral arrangements.

Chrysanthemum dissectum is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
