Campanula candida

Campanula candida, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are oval and slightly hairy, with a rough texture.

Campanula candida is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and easy care requirements.
