Crotalaria becquetii subsp. turgida

Crotalaria becquetii subsp.

turgida, commonly known as the African turgid cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces bright yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes.

The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and is known for its distinctive, inflated seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fallow fields.

The species is also valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
