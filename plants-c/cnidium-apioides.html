Cnidium apioides

Cnidium apioides, commonly known as the Chinese pepper plant, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is native to regions of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features a tall, slender stem with finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine. Its small, yellowish-green flowers are clustered in umbels, a characteristic feature of the Apiaceae family.

The roots and rhizomes of Cnidium apioides are the primary parts used in herbal remedies, valued for their purported medicinal properties.
