Chionanthus tenuiflorus

Chionanthus tenuiflorus, commonly known as the slenderflower chionanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, pendant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. Its flowering period attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
