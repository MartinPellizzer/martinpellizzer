Chrysanthemum shiwogiku

Chrysanthemum shiwogiku, commonly known as Japanese chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant is typically grown as an ornamental and is prized for its compact growth habit and long blooming period. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

Chrysanthemum shiwogiku is also used in traditional medicine and as a decorative element in Japanese culture.
