Carex tentaculata var. gracilis

Carex tentaculata var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender tussock sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh habitats.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in bogs, fens, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ecological importance lies in providing habitat and food for various wetland organisms.
