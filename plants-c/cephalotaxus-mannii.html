Cephalotaxus mannii

Cephalotaxus mannii, commonly known as the Himalayan yew, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Taxaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, scale-like leaves arranged in spirals. The plant produces small, reddish berries that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy aril. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in traditional medicine.

Cephalotaxus mannii is adapted to grow in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates.
