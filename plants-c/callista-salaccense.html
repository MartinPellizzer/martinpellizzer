Callista salaccense

Callista salaccense, commonly known as the Salacca palm, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that develop into elongated, edible fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Callista salaccense plays a significant role in both ecological and agricultural systems in its native habitat.
