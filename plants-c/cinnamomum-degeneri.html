Cinnamomum degeneri

Cinnamomum degeneri, commonly known as the Degener's cinnamon tree, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a smooth margin and emit a distinctive cinnamon-like scent when crushed.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
