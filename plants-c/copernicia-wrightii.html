Copernicia wrightii

Copernicia wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's copernicia, is a species of palm native to Mexico.

It is a small, slender tree that typically grows to a height of about 5 meters. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, coconut-like fruits. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with long, narrow leaflets that are arranged in a feather-like pattern.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
