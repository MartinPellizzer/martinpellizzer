Cirsium tauricola

Cirsium tauricola, commonly known as the Tauric thistle, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in mountainous or coastal areas. Its leaves are usually spiny and lobed, with a rough texture, and the flowers are typically purple or blue in color.

Cirsium tauricola is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
