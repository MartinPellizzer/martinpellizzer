Cyperus costaricensis

Cyperus costaricensis, commonly known as the Costa Rican flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, thriving in moist to submerged soil conditions. Its slender, triangular stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Cyperus costaricensis plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
