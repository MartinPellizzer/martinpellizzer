Cirsium sahendi

Cirsium sahendi, commonly known as Sahend's thistle, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, leafy stems and large, purple to pinkish flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are usually deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

Cirsium sahendi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
