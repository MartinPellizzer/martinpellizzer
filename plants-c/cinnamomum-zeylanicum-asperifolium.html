Cinnamomum zeylanicum asperifolium

Cinnamomum zeylanicum asperifolium, commonly known as the rough-leaved cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Sri Lanka.

It is characterized by its dark green, rough-textured leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a distinctive, slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by oval, red or black berries. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds.

The species is valued for its essential oils and has ecological significance in its native habitat.
