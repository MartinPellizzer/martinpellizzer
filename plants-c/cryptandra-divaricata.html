Cryptandra divaricata

Cryptandra divaricata, commonly known as the variable cryptandra, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its woody structure and small, aromatic leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
