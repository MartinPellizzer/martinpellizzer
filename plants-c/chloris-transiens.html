Chloris transiens

Chloris transiens, commonly known as the transient grass, is a species of flowering plant in the Poaceae family.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and small, spikelet-based inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Chloris transiens is well adapted to survive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
