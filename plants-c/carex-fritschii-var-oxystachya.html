Carex fritschii var. oxystachya

Carex fritschii var. oxystachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a single, elongated spike that bears small, greenish-brown flowers.

Carex fritschii var. oxystachya is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its ornamental appeal in water gardens.
