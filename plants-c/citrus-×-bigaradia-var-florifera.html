Citrus × bigaradia var. florifera

Citrus × bigaradia var. florifera, commonly known as the sweet lime or Persian lime, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from a cross between Citrus limon (lemon) and Citrus aurantifolia (sweet lime).

It is characterized by its small, yellow, juicy fruits with a thin rind and a high juice content. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is widely used in culinary applications and is valued for its tart flavor and aromatic properties.
