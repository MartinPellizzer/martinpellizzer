Cathestecum stoloniferum

Cathestecum stoloniferum, commonly known as the creeping cathestecum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features a creeping growth habit with stolons that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The plant has small, opposite leaves that are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, often displaying a range of colors from white to pink.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
