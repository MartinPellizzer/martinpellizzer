Caiophora dederichiorum

Caiophora dederichiorum, commonly known as the Dederich's caiophora, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, featuring elongated, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface that aids in water retention. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale green to white in color, adapted for pollination by specific insect species. Its growth habit is predominantly epiphytic, meaning it thrives by growing on the surfaces of trees without deriving nutrients from them.

Due to habitat loss and limited study, much about its ecological role and reproductive strategies remains unknown.
