Cynoglossum officinale f. canescens

Cynoglossum officinale f. canescens, commonly known as the hairy hound's tongue, is a variant of the hound's tongue plant.

It is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its distinctive, tongue-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
