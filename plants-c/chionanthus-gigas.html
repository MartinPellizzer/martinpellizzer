Chionanthus gigas

Chionanthus gigas, commonly known as the Chinese fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its showy, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Chionanthus gigas is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
