Champaca michelia

Champaca michelia, commonly known as the frangipani or champak, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. The plant has simple, opposite, and ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its bark is smooth and pale, with a distinct fragrance when crushed.

Champaca michelia is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
