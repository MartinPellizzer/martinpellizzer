Cyperus pilosus

Cyperus pilosus, commonly known as the hairy flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. Cyperus pilosus typically grows in moist or wet soils, often found in marshy areas or along water bodies.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
