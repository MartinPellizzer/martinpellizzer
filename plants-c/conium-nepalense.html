Conium nepalense

Conium nepalense, commonly known as Nepal poison hemlock, is a toxic plant native to the Himalayan region.

It belongs to theApiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. It contains toxic alkaloids, particularly coniine, which can be lethal to humans and animals.

Due to its toxicity, it is often avoided in gardens and requires careful handling.
