Carex nervata

Carex nervata, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and are often wind-pollinated.

Carex nervata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
