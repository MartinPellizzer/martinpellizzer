Crotalaria tigrensis

Crotalaria tigrensis, commonly known as the tiger bean plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers with prominent black veins. The plant produces elongated pods containing several bean-like seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a food source. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are often hairy and oval in shape.

Crotalaria tigrensis is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
