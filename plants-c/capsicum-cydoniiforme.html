Capsicum cydoniiforme

Capsicum cydoniiforme, commonly known as the golden apple pepper, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, yellow-orange fruits that resemble small apples, hence its common name. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers and has a bushy growth habit with broad, ovate leaves. Its fruits are typically harvested when they reach full color, indicating maturity.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, sweet flavor, making it suitable for both culinary and decorative uses.
