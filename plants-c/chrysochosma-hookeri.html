Chrysochosma hookeri

Chrysochosma hookeri, commonly known as the golden sandwort, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in desert environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically succulent, adapted to store water in its harsh habitat.

The species is valued for its resilience and is often used in xeriscaping due to its ability to thrive with minimal water.
