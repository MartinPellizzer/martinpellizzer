Cardamine nuttallii var. covilleana

Cardamine nuttallii var. covilleana, commonly known as the Coville's watercress, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a delicate, peppery aroma, and its small white flowers are borne in clusters. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in wetland habitats and is often found in association with other aquatic or semi-aquatic flora.
