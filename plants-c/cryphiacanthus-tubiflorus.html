Cryphiacanthus tubiflorus

Cryphiacanthus tubiflorus, commonly known as the swamp hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a reddish or orange hue at the base. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
