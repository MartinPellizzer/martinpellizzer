Caesalpinia falcaria var. rusbyi

Caesalpinia falcaria var. rusbyi, commonly known as the Rusby's ticktree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often feathery and arranged in a unique, zigzag pattern. The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its habitat. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that splits open to release its seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
