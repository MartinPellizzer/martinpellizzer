Callicarpa sessilifolia

Callicarpa sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaf beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet-blue berries that appear in late summer. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its attractive foliage and showy fruit make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
