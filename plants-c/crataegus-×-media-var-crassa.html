Crataegus × media var. crassa

Crataegus × media var. crassa, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping and hedgerows.
