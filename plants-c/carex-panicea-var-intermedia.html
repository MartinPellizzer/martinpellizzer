Carex panicea var. intermedia

Carex panicea var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate tufted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds. The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly glossy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical cluster of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
