Crotalaria natalitia

Crotalaria natalitia, commonly known as Natal crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows in grasslands and disturbed soils, thriving in warm, sunny conditions. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil fertility.

However, some species within the genus are known to be toxic to livestock, so caution is advised when handling or consuming any part of the plant.
