Cousinia mindshelkensis

Cousinia mindshelkensis, commonly known as the Mindshelk cushion plant, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, cushion-like growth form, which helps it withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
