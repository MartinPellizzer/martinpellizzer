Corycium magnum

Corycium magnum, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb nutrients from the forest floor, and it often grows as an epiphyte on trees.

The plant's unique morphology and vibrant blooms make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
