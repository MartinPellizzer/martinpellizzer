Crotalaria ovata

Crotalaria ovata, commonly known as the oval cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces pods containing kidney-shaped seeds, which are often used as a food source in some cultures. Crotalaria ovata is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a valuable plant in agricultural settings.

Its botanical features include hairy stems, trifoliate leaves, and a bushy growth habit.
