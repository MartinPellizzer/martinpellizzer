Camissoniopsis

Camissoniopsis, commonly known as the narrowleaf yarrow, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to North America and typically grows in dry, rocky soils in open habitats. The plant features slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in dense clusters, and it blooms from spring to summer.

Camissoniopsis is often found in disturbed areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
