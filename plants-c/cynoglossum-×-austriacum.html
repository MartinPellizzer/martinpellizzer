Cynoglossum × austriacum

Cynoglossum × austriacum, commonly known as the Austrian bellflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that resemble small bells. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which extends from summer into early autumn.
