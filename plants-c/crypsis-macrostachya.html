Crypsis macrostachya

Crypsis macrostachya, commonly known as the giant flame flower, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, showy inflorescences.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia. The plant features elongated, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color, with a distinctive, spurred shape. Its foliage consists of broad, ovate leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle.

Crypsis macrostachya thrives in high-light environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.
