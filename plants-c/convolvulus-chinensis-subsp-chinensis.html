Convolvulus chinensis subsp. chinensis

Convolvulus chinensis subsp.

chinensis, commonly known as Chinese morning glory, is a flowering plant native to China and other parts of East Asia. It is a herbaceous annual with trailing stems that produce trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail.

The flowers bloom in the early morning, giving the plant its common name.
