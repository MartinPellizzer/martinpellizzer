Carthamus hispanicus subsp. macrocephalus

Carthamus hispanicus subsp.

macrocephalus, commonly known as the Spanish safflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces distinctive, elongated seed pods that contain numerous small, dark seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for cultivation in Mediterranean climates.

This subspecies is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.
