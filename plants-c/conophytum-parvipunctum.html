Conophytum parvipunctum

Conophytum parvipunctum, commonly known as the small-punctured conophytum, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that are often covered in small, white tubercles. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne on short, erect stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
