Crataegus geneseensis

Crataegus geneseensis, commonly known as the Geneseo hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the genus Crataegus.

It is native to the eastern United States, specifically found in the Appalachian region. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 10 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have serrated margins.

The flowers are white or pink, appearing in clusters, and are followed by red or orange berries that are an important food source for wildlife.
