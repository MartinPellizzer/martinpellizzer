Condylago furculifera

Condylago furculifera, commonly known as the forked leaf orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive forked leaves, which are usually narrow and lance-shaped. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
