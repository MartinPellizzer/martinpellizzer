Centaurea spathulata

Centaurea spathulata, commonly known as the broadleaf cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive, flattened shape.
