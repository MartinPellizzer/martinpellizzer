Chenopodium × bontei var. cristatiforme

Chenopodium × bontei var. cristatiforme, commonly known as the cushion chenopod, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit, forming a low, mat-like structure that helps it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, green to reddish leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating, which reduces water loss. Its flowers are typically small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters at the base of the stems.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
