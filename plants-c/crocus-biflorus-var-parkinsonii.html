Crocus biflorus var. parkinsonii

Crocus biflorus var. parkinsonii, commonly known as the Parkinson's crocus, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces two to three large, cup-shaped flowers per stem. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, early-flowering blooms.
