Carex ignota var. fusiformis

Carex ignota var. fusiformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a triangular cross-section. This variety is native to certain regions of North America and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, three-ranked flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
