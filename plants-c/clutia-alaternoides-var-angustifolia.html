Clutia alaternoides var. angustifolia

Clutia alaternoides var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf clutia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It features small, elliptic to ovate leaves that are typically glossy and arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that release tiny seeds when mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
