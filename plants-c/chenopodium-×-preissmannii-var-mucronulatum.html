Chenopodium × preissmannii var. mucronulatum

Chenopodium × preissmannii var. mucronulatum, commonly known as the narrowleaf goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

This variety is often cultivated as a forage plant due to its nutritional value for livestock.
