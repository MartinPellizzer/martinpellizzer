Convolvulus tricornutus

Convolvulus tricornutus, commonly known as the three-angled morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple. The plant produces small, three-lobed fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus tricornutus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
