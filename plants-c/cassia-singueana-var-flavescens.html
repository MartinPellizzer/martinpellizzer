Cassia singueana var. flavescens

Cassia singueana var. flavescens, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow inflorescences that appear in clusters during the dry season. The plant has pinnate leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pod-like fruits that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
