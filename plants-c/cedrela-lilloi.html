Cedrela lilloi

Cedrela lilloi, commonly known as the Lillo's ceiba, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and broad, palmate leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing flat, winged seeds. It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

Cedrela lilloi also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
