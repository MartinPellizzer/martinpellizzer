Cyperus salzmannianus

Cyperus salzmannianus, commonly known as the Salzmann's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, and is often found in disturbed soils.

Cyperus salzmannianus is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is sometimes used in ornamental horticulture.
