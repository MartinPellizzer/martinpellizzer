Croton pusilliflorus

Croton pusilliflorus, commonly known as the dwarf croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in cyathia, typical of the genus Croton. Its leaves are usually palmately lobed and can vary in color from green to shades of red and yellow.

Croton pusilliflorus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
