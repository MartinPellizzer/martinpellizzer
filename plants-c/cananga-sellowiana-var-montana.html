Cananga sellowiana var. montana

Cananga sellowiana var. montana, commonly known as the mountain torch tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant white flowers that bloom in terminal clusters, often emitting a sweet, vanilla-like scent. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, round, yellow fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

Due to its unique appearance and aromatic properties, it is occasionally cultivated for landscaping and as a source of natural fragrance.
