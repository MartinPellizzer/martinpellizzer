Chenopodium melanocarpum f. leucocarpum

Chenopodium melanocarpum f. leucocarpum, commonly known as white-seeded goosefoot, is a variant of the common goosefoot species.

It is characterized by its pale, almost white seeds, which distinguish it from the typical dark-seeded form. This plant is an annual herb that thrives in disturbed soils and arid environments. It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to regions of the southwestern United States.

Its botanical features include succulent leaves and a prostrate or erect growth habit.
