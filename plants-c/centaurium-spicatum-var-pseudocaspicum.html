Centaurium spicatum var. pseudocaspicum

Centaurium spicatum var. pseudocaspicum, commonly known as the Caspian centaury, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are arranged in spike-like clusters and are usually a vivid red or orange color.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported tonic and healing properties.
