Cynosurus uniolae

Cynosurus uniolae, commonly known as the narrowleaf hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, dunes, and wetlands. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a clump-forming growth habit.

Cynosurus uniolae is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
