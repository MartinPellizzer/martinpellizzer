Chionochloa pallens subsp. pilosa

Chionochloa pallens subsp.

pilosa, commonly known as the silky chionochloa, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, silky hairs covering its leaves and stems.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, often in rocky or gravelly soils. Its foliage is usually a bright green, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
