Cyperus laxespicatus var. testui

Cyperus laxespicatus var. testui, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-lobed flowers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
