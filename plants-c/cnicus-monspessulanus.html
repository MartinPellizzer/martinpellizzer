Cnicus monspessulanus

Cnicus monspessulanus, commonly known as the Spanish Broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, woody fruits called cypselae. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
