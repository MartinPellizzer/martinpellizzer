Crepidospermum multijugum

Crepidospermum multijugum, commonly known as the silverleaf vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. Its rapid growth habit makes it both a popular ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in some regions.

The leaves and stems are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
