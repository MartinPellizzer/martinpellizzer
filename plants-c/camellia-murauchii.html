Camellia murauchii

Camellia murauchii, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, double pink or white flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in bonsai and landscape design.

Camellia murauchii is also noted for its cold hardiness, able to withstand light frosts in its native habitat.
