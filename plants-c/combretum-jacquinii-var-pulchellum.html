Combretum jacquinii var. pulchellum

Combretum jacquinii var. pulchellum, commonly known as the red-flowered combretum, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
