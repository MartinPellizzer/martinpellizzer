Commiphora campestris subsp. glabrata

Commiphora campestris subsp.

glabrata, commonly known as the smooth Commiphora, is a small shrub native to tropical regions of Africa. It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and small, elliptical leaves.

The plant produces a resinous sap, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic compounds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests and disturbed soils.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small vertebrates.
