Carlina gorterioides

Carlina gorterioides, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Its showy yellow-orange flowers bloom in spring and are followed by spherical, brownish seed heads.

Carlina gorterioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
