Cinnamomum formicarium

Cinnamomum formicarium, commonly known as the African cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown bark, which is used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elliptical, dark green leaves. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule containing aromatic seeds.

Due to its unique fragrance and medicinal properties, it is valued in both culinary and herbal applications.
