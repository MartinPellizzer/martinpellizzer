Cymbopogon hirtus f. pubescens

Cymbopogon hirtus f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy lemon grass, is a variant of the lemon grass species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that emit a strong citrus scent when crushed. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its inflorescence consists of dense, cylindrical clusters of small, pale-green flowers.

This variety is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and culinary applications.
