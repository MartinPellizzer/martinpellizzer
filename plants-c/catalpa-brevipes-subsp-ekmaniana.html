Catalpa brevipes subsp. ekmaniana

Catalpa brevipes subsp.

ekmaniana, commonly known as the Mexican catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico. It is characterized by its elongated, heart-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces long, slender seed pods that split open to release its distinctive, helmet-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry, open habitats.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
