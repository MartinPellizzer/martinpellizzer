Cyperus cyperoides var. evolutior

Cyperus cyperoides var. evolutior, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas, along stream banks, or in flooded fields. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, with small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
