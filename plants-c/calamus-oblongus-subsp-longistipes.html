Calamus oblongus subsp. longistipes

Calamus oblongus subsp.

longistipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed calamus, is a member of the Arecaceae family, characterized by its robust, arched rhizomes. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and the Philippines.

The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of aromatic materials. Its flowering stems are slender and can reach several meters in length, bearing small, clustered inflorescences.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in both cultural and medicinal contexts.
