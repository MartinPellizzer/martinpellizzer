Citrus × aurantium var. lunata

Citrus × aurantium var. lunata, commonly known as the pomelo or sometimes the sweet orange, is a citrus species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, thick-skinned fruit, which can weigh up to 10 pounds and has a mild, sweet flavor compared to other citrus fruits. The plant is a small evergreen tree with dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is used in both culinary and medicinal applications, and it is valued for its high vitamin C content.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.
