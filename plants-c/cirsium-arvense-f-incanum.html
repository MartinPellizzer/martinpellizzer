Cirsium arvense f. incanum

Cirsium arvense f. incanum, commonly known as the hoary thistle, is a variant of the common knapweed.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its spiny, gray-green foliage and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and waste areas, and is native to Europe. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

The inflorescence consists of a cluster of small, showy flowers that bloom in late summer.
