Crocus suaveolens

Crocus suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-scented crocus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Iridaceae family and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. Its bulbs are perennial, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

The flowers emit a subtle, sweet fragrance, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
