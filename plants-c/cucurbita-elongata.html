Cucurbita elongata

Cucurbita elongata, commonly known as the elongated gourd, is a species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces large, elongated fruits that can grow up to 3 meters in length, with a smooth, pale green to yellowish exterior. The leaves are broad and lobed, and the plant is characterized by its vigorous vine growth habit.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can also be used in traditional culinary practices.
