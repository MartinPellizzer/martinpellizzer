Convolvulus boissieri subsp. compactus

Convolvulus boissieri subsp.

compactus, commonly known as the compact convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial herb with prostrate or ascending stems that bear small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple.

The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.
