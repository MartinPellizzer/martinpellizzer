Carex polytrichoides

Carex polytrichoides, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spikes of small, three-ranked flowers. This species typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization.

The plant is often found in temperate regions of North America and is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
