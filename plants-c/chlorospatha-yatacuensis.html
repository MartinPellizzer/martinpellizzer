Chlorospatha yatacuensis

Chlorospatha yatacuensis, commonly known as the Yatacu Valley sunspire, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in Peru.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that are coated with a waxy, reflective surface, which helps it retain moisture in its arid environment. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom during the brief wet season, attracting specific pollinators. Its unique morphology and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists studying adaptation to extreme conditions.

Due to habitat destruction, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
