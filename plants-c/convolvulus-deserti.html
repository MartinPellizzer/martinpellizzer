Convolvulus deserti

Convolvulus deserti, commonly known as the desert bindweed, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial herb that thrives in sandy or gravelly soils, often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its heart-shaped leaves are arranged alternately along the stems, and the plant has a trailing or climbing growth habit.

Convolvulus deserti is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
