Cirsium dissimile

Cirsium dissimile, commonly known as the slender thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and spiny leaves. The plant features robust, upright stems covered in sharp, stiff hairs. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges and a woolly texture.

Cirsium dissimile produces purple to pinkish flower heads that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
