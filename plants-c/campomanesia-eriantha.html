Campomanesia eriantha

Campomanesia eriantha, commonly known as the Brazilian star-apple or pitanga, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible and have a sweet, tropical flavor.

Its fruit is often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine, while the tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and potential in agroforestry systems.
