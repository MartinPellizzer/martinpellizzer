Crocanthemum domingense

Crocanthemum domingense, commonly known as the Dominican chamomile, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Hispaniola.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny environments and has aromatic leaves that contain essential oils. Its botanical features include hairy stems and linear, lance-shaped leaves.

It is often used in traditional medicine for its calming and anti-inflammatory properties.
