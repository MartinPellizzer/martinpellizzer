Chondrilla nudicaulis

Chondrilla nudicaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed false flax, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, unbranched stem and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, which help minimize water loss.

Chondrilla nudicaulis is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.
