Crotalaria orixensis

Crotalaria orixensis, commonly known as the Orixon's crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow-orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets arranged in a palmate pattern.

Crotalaria orixensis is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
