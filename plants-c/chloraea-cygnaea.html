Chloraea cygnaea

Chloraea cygnaea, commonly known as the white kowhai, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

The white kowhai is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
