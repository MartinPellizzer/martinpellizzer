Chamaeraphis schiedeana

Chamaeraphis schiedeana, commonly known as the sandspurry, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or coastal environments, showcasing adaptations to arid conditions. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, aiding in moisture retention.

The seeds are encased in a hard, dry fruit known as a capsule, which aids in wind dispersal.
