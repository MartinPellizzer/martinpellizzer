Cirsium uninervium

Cirsium uninervium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and have a single vein, giving it its scientific name.

Cirsium uninervium is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.
