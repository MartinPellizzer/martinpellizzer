Clematis aristata f. oblongisepala

Clematis aristata f. oblongisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled clematis, is a rare and distinctive cultivar of the clematis genus.

It is characterized by its elongated, ovate sepals that give it a unique appearance compared to typical clematis species. This plant typically produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

The variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and floral display.
