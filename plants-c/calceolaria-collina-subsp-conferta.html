Calceolaria collina subsp. conferta

Calceolaria collina subsp.

conferta, commonly known as the clustered calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often with a distinctive hood-like structure.

The plant typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky soils, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, arranged in a rosette pattern.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral displays.
