Cucumis melo var. adana

Cucumis melo var. adana, commonly known as the Adana melon, is a variety of the cantaloupe species native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its netted rind and sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant is a trailing vine with palmate leaves and yellow flowers that are pollinated by insects. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is typically harvested when the rind turns a deep orange or yellow color.
