Convolvulus guianensis

Convolvulus guianensis, commonly known as the Guianese morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to twine around supports. Its flowers open in the morning and close by evening, hence its common name.

The plant is often found in moist, tropical environments and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
