Coccinia variifolia

Coccinia variifolia, commonly known as the variable leaf cucumber, is a tropical vine native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, yellow, edible fruits that resemble miniature cucumbers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible produce.

Coccinia variifolia is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
