Caryota horrida

Caryota horrida, commonly known as the rough-leaf palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that are covered in small, spiny projections. The plant typically grows as a tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its trunk is usually slender and often has a fibrous, spongy texture.

Caryota horrida is valued for its ornamental leaves and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for landscaping purposes.
