Cerinthe major subsp. elegans

Cerinthe major subsp.

elegans, commonly known as the elegant toadflax, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces striking, two-lipped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
