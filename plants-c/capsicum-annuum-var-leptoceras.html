Capsicum annuum var. leptoceras

Capsicum annuum var. leptoceras, commonly known as the bird's eye chili, is a compact, bushy plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It produces small, pointed, and often red or yellow fruits that are highly valued for their intense heat and flavor. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is widely cultivated for culinary and medicinal purposes due to its unique properties.
