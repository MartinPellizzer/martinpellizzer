Castalia alba

Castalia alba, commonly known as the white castalia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves with a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Castalia alba is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
