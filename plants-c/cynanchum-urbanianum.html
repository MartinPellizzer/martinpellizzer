Cynanchum urbanianum

Cynanchum urbanianum, commonly known as the European milkvetch, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, branched stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound with small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in loose racemes, while the fruit is a cylindrical, ribbed capsule containing several seeds.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
