Chrysophyllum taiense

Chrysophyllum taiense, commonly known as the Taiwan starfruit, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and fruit yield, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and for local consumption.
