Croixia calophylla

Croixia calophylla, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically golden yellow in color, with a distinctive corolla that curves inward. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Croixia calophylla is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its striking floral display.
