Coelogyne ramosii

Coelogyne ramosii, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters along the stem. The plant produces long, slender pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, aiding in its survival in its natural habitat. Its leaves are strap-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.

Coelogyne ramosii thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
