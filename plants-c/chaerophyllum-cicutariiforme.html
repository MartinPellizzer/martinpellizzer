Chaerophyllum cicutariiforme

Chaerophyllum cicutariiforme, commonly known as the common cow parsley, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables. The plant features a basal rosette of finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in umbels. Its medicinal properties have been historically used in traditional remedies.
