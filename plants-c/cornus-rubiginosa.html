Cornus rubiginosa

Cornus rubiginosa, commonly known as red dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red stems, which are most prominent in the winter months. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers in clusters during early spring, followed by red berries that attract birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Cornus rubiginosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
