Cassia purpurea

Cassia purpurea, commonly known as the purple cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant has bipinnate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its seeds are encased in long, slender pods. Cassia purpurea is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for gardens in warm climates.
