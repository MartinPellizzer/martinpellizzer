Cyperus nipponicus

Cyperus nipponicus, commonly known as Japanese flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers that are typically brown or purplish in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and pond edges, and is often found in temperate regions.

Cyperus nipponicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
