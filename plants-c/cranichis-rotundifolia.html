Cranichis rotundifolia

Cranichis rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf twayblade, is a small, herbaceous orchid native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its unique, grass-like leaves and its small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant is a perennial, often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and meadows. It has a specialized root system that allows it to absorb nutrients from the soil efficiently.

The species is part of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, ephemeral blooms.
