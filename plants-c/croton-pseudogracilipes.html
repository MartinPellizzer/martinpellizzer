Croton pseudogracilipes

Croton pseudogracilipes, commonly known as the slender croton, is a tropical plant belonging to the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia. Its bark and leaves contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.
