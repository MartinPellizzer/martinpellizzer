Campylocentrum minutum

Campylocentrum minutum, commonly known as the dwarf starfruit, is a small shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, star-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is often used in culinary applications due to its mild, sweet flavor.
