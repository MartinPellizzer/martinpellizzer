Capparis paradoxa

Capparis paradoxa, commonly known as the Paradoxical Caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and limestone habitats, characterized by its spiny stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces striking yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in spring and summer. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hard seeds.

Capparis paradoxa is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
