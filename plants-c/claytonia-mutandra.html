Claytonia mutandra

Claytonia mutandra, commonly known as the pink mountain sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and produces small, showy pink or purple flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Claytonia mutandra is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky slopes.

It is a member of the Portulacaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in native plant gardens.
