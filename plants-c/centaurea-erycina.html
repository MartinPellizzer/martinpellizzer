Centaurea erycina

Centaurea erycina, commonly known as the Sicilian star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, bristly leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.
