Convolvulus calvertii

Convolvulus calvertii, commonly known as Calvert's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often twining.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid environments.
