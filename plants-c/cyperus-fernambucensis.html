Cyperus fernambucensis

Cyperus fernambucensis, commonly known as the Fernambucan flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered and produce small, inconspicuous flowers.

Cyperus fernambucensis is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wetland organisms.
