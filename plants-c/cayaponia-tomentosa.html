Cayaponia tomentosa

Cayaponia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly cayaponia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.
