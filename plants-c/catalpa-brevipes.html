Catalpa brevipes

Catalpa brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The tree produces long, cylindrical seed pods that split open to release its winged seeds. Catalpa brevipes typically grows to a height of 10 to 20 feet, making it a compact choice for smaller landscapes.

Its foliage and floral displays make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural habitats.
