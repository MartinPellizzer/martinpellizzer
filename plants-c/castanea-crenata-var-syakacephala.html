Castanea crenata var. syakacephala

Castanea crenata var. syakacephala, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut.

This variety is native to Japan and is characterized by its small, spiny fruits that mature in autumn. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It is a deciduous tree that thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in Japanese horticulture.
