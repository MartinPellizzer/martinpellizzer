Cistanthe cachinalensis

Cistanthe cachinalensis, commonly known as the Chilean sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Chile.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branched stems and small, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Cistanthe cachinalensis is often found in coastal and desert environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
