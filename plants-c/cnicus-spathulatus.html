Cnicus spathulatus

Cnicus spathulatus, commonly known as the broad-leaved sow thistle, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, wavy-edged leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. It has a hairy stem and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
