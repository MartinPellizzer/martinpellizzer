Cyperus heldreichianus

Cyperus heldreichianus, commonly known as the Heldreich's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus heldreichianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
