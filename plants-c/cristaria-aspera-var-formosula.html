Cristaria aspera var. formosula

Cristaria aspera var. formosula, commonly known as the rough-surfaced water plant, is a variant of the species Cristaria aspera.

It is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in wetland and aquatic environments. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, rough-textured leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in shallow water and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and ecologists studying wetland ecosystems.
