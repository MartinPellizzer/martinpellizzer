Callicore crocata

Callicore crocata, commonly known as the purple hairstreak, is a small, delicate butterfly species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

Its larvae feed on plants in the family Polygonaceae, such as knotweed and buckthorn, which are essential for its development. The adult butterfly is characterized by its striking purple and blue wing patterns, which serve as camouflage in its natural habitat. The plant's role in the butterfly's life cycle highlights the intricate relationships between flora and fauna in ecosystems.

Callicore crocata's presence often indicates a healthy, diverse environment with abundant host plants.
