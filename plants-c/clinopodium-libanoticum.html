Clinopodium libanoticum

Clinopodium libanoticum, commonly known as Lebanon savory, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its fragrant, slightly minty scent.

Clinopodium libanoticum is often grown as a ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and culinary uses in some traditional cuisines.
