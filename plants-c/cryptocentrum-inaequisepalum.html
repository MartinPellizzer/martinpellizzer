Cryptocentrum inaequisepalum

Cryptocentrum inaequisepalum, commonly known as the unequal-sepal cryptocentrum, is a tropical flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive inflorescence known as a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. The plant features showy, often colorful bracts that serve to attract pollinators. Its leaves are typically broad and arrow-shaped, with a smooth or slightly textured surface.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
