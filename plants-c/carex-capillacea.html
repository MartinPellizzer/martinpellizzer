Carex capillacea

Carex capillacea, commonly known as slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically found in the leaf axils. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Carex capillacea plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
