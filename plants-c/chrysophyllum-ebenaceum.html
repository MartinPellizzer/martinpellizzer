Chrysophyllum ebenaceum

Chrysophyllum ebenaceum, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are small, yellow, and covered in a sticky substance. It is valued for its hard, dense wood used in furniture and musical instruments.

The plant also has medicinal uses in traditional African medicine.
