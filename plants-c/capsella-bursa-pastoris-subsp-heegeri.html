Capsella bursa-pastoris subsp. heegeri

Capsella bursa-pastoris subsp.

heegeri, commonly known as the "blessed milkweed" or "lady's purse," is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is often found in temperate climates. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are pale yellow or greenish-white.

This subspecies is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its role in supporting pollinators.
