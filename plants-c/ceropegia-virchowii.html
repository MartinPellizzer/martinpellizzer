Ceropegia virchowii

Ceropegia virchowii, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, waxy leaves that often have a silvery or blue-gray sheen. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia virchowii is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appearance and ease of care.
