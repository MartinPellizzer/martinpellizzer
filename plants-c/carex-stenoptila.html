Carex stenoptila

Carex stenoptila, commonly known as narrow-leaved sedgeland sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to North America and is typically found in wetland habitats such as marshes and swales. The plant has grass-like leaves that are narrow and usually have a bluish-green hue. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Carex stenoptila plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
