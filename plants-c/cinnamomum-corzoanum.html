Cinnamomum corzoanum

Cinnamomum corzoanum, commonly known as the Mexican cinnamon tree, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces berries that contain seeds, which are used for propagation. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.

The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.
