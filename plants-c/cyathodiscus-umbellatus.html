Cyathodiscus umbellatus

Cyathodiscus umbellatus, commonly known as the umbrella-shaped diatom, is a unicellular algae belonging to the class Bacillariophyceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, symmetrical, and often ornate valve structure, which resembles an umbrella in shape. The species is found in freshwater environments, where it thrives in nutrient-rich waters. Its silica-based cell wall, or frustule, is intricately patterned and plays a crucial role in its ecological function.

Cyathodiscus umbellatus is an important indicator species for water quality and ecosystem health.
