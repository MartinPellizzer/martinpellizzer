Coreopsis flammula

Coreopsis flammula, commonly known as the yellow coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches. The plant features slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its most striking feature is its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with a central disk and ray florets.

Coreopsis flammula is known for its ability to thrive in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for wildflower gardens.
