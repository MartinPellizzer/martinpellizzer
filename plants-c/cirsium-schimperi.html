Cirsium schimperi

Cirsium schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian thistle, is a perennial herb native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, hairy stems and purple to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Cirsium schimperi is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
