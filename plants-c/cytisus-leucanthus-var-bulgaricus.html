Cytisus leucanthus var. bulgaricus

Cytisus leucanthus var. bulgaricus, commonly known as the Bulgarian whitethorn, is a shrub native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its slender, spiny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This variety is closely related to other cytisus species and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
