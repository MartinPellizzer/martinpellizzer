Coreopsis connata

Coreopsis connata, commonly known as the prairie coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the central and eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open habitats such as prairies, meadows, and woodland edges. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually hairy and somewhat woody at the base.

Coreopsis connata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
