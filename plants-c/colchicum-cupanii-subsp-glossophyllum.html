Colchicum cupanii subsp. glossophyllum

Colchicum cupanii subsp.

glossophyllum, commonly known as the Glossy Autumn Crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, waxy coating. It produces toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.

This subspecies is often found in rocky soils and open woodland habitats.
