Cynanchum savannarum

Cynanchum savannarum, commonly known as the eastern ironweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and stream banks. The plant features upright stems that are often hairy and bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. Its showy purple to blue flowers appear in late summer, forming dense clusters at the top of the stems.

Cynanchum savannarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
