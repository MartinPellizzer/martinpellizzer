Cocos petraea var. alpina

Cocos petraea var. alpina, commonly known as the Alpine coconut, is a unique variant of the rock coconut plant.

It is a shrub-like plant adapted to grow in rocky, alpine environments. The plant features small, clustered leaves and produces edible, coconut-like fruits. It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is considered a rare and resilient species.
