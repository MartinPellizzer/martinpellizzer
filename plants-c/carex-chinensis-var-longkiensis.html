Carex chinensis var. longkiensis

Carex chinensis var. longkiensis, commonly known as the Chinese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and cylindrical inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a smooth texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
