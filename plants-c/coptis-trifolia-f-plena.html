Coptis trifolia f. plena

Coptis trifolia f. plena, commonly known as the full-flowered three-leaf goldthread, is a variant of the Coptis trifolia species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in China. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties due to the presence of alkaloids.

The f. plena form is distinguished by its more abundant and showy flowers compared to the typical form.
