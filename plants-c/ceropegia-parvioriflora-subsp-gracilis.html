Ceropegia parvioriflora subsp. gracilis

Ceropegia parvioriflora subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as the slender-stemmed ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa. It is a succulent shrub characterized by its trailing stems and small, fleshy leaves.

The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand periods of drought.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in rock gardens.
