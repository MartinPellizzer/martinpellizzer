Cornus walteri var. confertiflora

Cornus walteri var. confertiflora, commonly known as the Walter's dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically medium green with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, berry-like drupes. Its botanical characteristics include a compact growth habit and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
