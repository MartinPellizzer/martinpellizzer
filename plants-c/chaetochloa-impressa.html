Chaetochloa impressa

Chaetochloa impressa, commonly known as the smooth meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in wet meadows, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist and nutrient-rich soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 60 centimeters, and its leaves are narrow and slightly rolled. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle, bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in saturated conditions, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
