Cochlearia officinalis var. alpina

Cochlearia officinalis var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine cochlearia, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant features basal leaves that are kidney-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white or pinkish flowers clustered in racemes. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
