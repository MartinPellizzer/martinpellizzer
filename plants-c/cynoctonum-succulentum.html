Cynoctonum succulentum

Cynoctonum succulentum, commonly known as the ghost plant, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features a compact, cushion-like growth form with dense clusters of fleshy, blue-green leaves that give it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers during the cooler months, which are followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are covered in fine, white hairs that help reduce water loss in dry conditions.

Cynoctonum succulentum is well adapted to harsh environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil to thrive.
