Callistachya virginica var. lanceolata

Callistachya virginica var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf false indigo, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces striking spikes of blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
