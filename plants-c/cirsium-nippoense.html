Cirsium nippoense

Cirsium nippoense, commonly known as the Japanese thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its growth habit is upright, with a height ranging from 60 to 120 centimeters.

Cirsium nippoense is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
