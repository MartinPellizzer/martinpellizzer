Cleistopholis lucens

Cleistopholis lucens, commonly known as the shining flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-bearing fruits. The plant is notable for its striking, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in spring, producing clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
