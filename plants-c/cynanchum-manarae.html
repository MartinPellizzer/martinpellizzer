Cynanchum manarae

Cynanchum manarae, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer, followed by follicles containing seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.

Its root contains alkaloids and is used to treat various ailments.
