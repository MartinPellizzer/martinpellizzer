Cacalia maximowicziana var. alata

Cacalia maximowicziana var. alata, commonly known as the winged bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Campanulaceae and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant features slender, branching stems and hairy leaves that are often tinged with silver. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.
