Crinum scabrum

Crinum scabrum, commonly known as the rough crinum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are long, strap-like, and often have a rough texture, giving it its common name.

Crinum scabrum is valued for its ornamental flowers and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
