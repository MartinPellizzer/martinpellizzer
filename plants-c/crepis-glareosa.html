Crepis glareosa

Crepis glareosa, commonly known as the shining hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

Crepis glareosa is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
