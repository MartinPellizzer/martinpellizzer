Celtis bungeana var. lanceolata

Celtis bungeana var. lanceolata, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It features smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age, and its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a pointed tip. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by spherical, greenish fruits that mature to a dark blue or black color. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in urban environments.
