Carallia suffruticosa

Carallia suffruticosa, commonly known as the red sandalwood or red cedar, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are often clustered together. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and have a glossy appearance.

The fruit is a small, red drupe that contains a single seed, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
