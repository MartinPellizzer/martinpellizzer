Clematis clarkeana

Clematis clarkeana, commonly known as the Clarke's Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the Himalayas.

It features compound leaves with three to five ovate leaflets and produces striking, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has a long flowering season, typically from late spring to early autumn. Its stems are covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, fertile soil.

Clematis clarkeana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a climbing plant.
