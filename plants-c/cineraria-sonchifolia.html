Cineraria sonchifolia

Cineraria sonchifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features silvery, fuzzy leaves that give it its common name, and it produces showy yellow flowers with a central disk. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cineraria sonchifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
