Clinopodium menthifolium

Clinopodium menthifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved wild mint, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic plants. The plant has slender, upright stems and small, ovate leaves with a distinctive mint-like fragrance. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in whorls along the stems.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
