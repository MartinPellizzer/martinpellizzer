Chaenostoma impeditum

Chaenostoma impeditum, commonly known as the clustered water-starwort, is a small aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and lakes. The plant has finely divided, floating leaves that are often clustered at the water's surface. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, appearing in clusters, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to water quality maintenance.
