Carduus quinquefolius

Carduus quinquefolius, commonly known as the common thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and is often found in disturbed soils, roadsides, and fields. Its leaves are covered in fine, prickly hairs, and the flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional medicine.
