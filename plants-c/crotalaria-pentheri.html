Crotalaria pentheri

Crotalaria pentheri, commonly known as the purple cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces vibrant purple or yellow flowers, which are clustered in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the pods contain several seeds that are often used in traditional medicine.

Crotalaria pentheri is also noted for its ornamental value and its role in agroforestry systems.
