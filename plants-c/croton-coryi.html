Croton coryi

Croton coryi, commonly known as the African croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and Eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often strikingly colored foliage. The plant is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are typically green with prominent veins and can vary in shape and size. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Croton coryi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens due to its vibrant leaf patterns.
