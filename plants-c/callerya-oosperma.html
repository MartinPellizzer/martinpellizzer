Callerya oosperma

Callerya oosperma, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a vibrant and showy appearance. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil through symbiotic relationships with bacteria. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, meaning they are divided into smaller leaflets, and the plant can grow as a shrub or small tree.

Callerya oosperma is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
