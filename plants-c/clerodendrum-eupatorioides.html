Clerodendrum eupatorioides

Clerodendrum eupatorioides, commonly known as the purple verbena or wild betony, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, visually appealing plant.
