Cryptotaenia canadensis var. japonica

Cryptotaenia canadensis var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese cowparsley, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
