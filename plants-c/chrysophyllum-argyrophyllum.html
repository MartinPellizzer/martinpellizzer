Chrysophyllum argyrophyllum

Chrysophyllum argyrophyllum, commonly known as the silverleaf cherimoya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that have a silvery underside. The tree produces fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, custard-like fruits. Its fruit is typically pear-shaped and has a sweet, creamy texture with a mild, tropical flavor.

The plant is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
