Callista stratiotes

Callista stratiotes, commonly known as the water hibiscus, is a tropical aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central stamen. The plant thrives in shallow, still water and is often found in ponds, lakes, and wetlands. Its leaves are broad, heart-shaped, and float on the water's surface, while its roots anchor it to the substrate.

Callista stratiotes is also known for its ability to purify water and provide habitat for aquatic life.
