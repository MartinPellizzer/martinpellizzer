Clinopodium micranthum var. yakusimense

Clinopodium micranthum var. yakusimense, commonly known as Yakusim mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its small, tubular flowers are usually violet or blue, and it produces small, hairy fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional medicinal uses in local cultures.
