Clerodendrum acerbianum

Clerodendrum acerbianum, commonly known as the Indian bladdernut, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers, followed by distinctive bladder-like seed pods. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional medicinal uses in some cultures.
