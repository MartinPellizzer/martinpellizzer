Calligonum acanthopterum var. setosum

Calligonum acanthopterum var. setosum, commonly known as the hairy calligonum, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, often spiny foliage. The leaves are typically small, linear, and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of yellow or green.

This plant is well-suited for revegetation projects in degraded landscapes due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
