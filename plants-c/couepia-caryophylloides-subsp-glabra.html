Couepia caryophylloides subsp. glabra

Couepia caryophylloides subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved couepia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that are typically elliptical in shape.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruits are capsules that release seeds when mature, contributing to its propagation.

This species is often found in dry to semi-arid habitats, showcasing its adaptability to varying environmental conditions.
