Cucurbita asperata

Cucurbita asperata, commonly known as the rough gourd, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its rough, warty skin. The plant produces large, typically green, oval-shaped fruits that can vary in size. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun for optimal growth.

The rough gourd is often cultivated for its edible fruits, which can be used in various culinary applications.
