Chamaecytisus ponomarjovii

Chamaecytisus ponomarjovii, commonly known as the Ponomarjov's buckthorn, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features small, dark green leaves that are leathery in texture and arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
