Commiphora rangeana

Commiphora rangeana, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to parts of eastern and southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing trees. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap, which has been used traditionally for medicinal and incense purposes. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or scrub habitats.
