Crotalaria tappenbeckiana

Crotalaria tappenbeckiana, commonly known as the Tappenbeck's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in soil enrichment.

However, some species within the genus Crotalaria are toxic to livestock, so caution is advised when handling this plant.
