Combretum marginatum

Combretum marginatum, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow to orange flowers that appear in clusters, often giving the plant a fiery appearance. The leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy texture with a distinctive margin that may appear slightly hairy. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The plant is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
