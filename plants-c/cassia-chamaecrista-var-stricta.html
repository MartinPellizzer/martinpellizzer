Cassia chamaecrista var. stricta

Cassia chamaecrista var. stricta, commonly known as yellow cassia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy and adaptable species.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable botanical subject in horticulture and pharmacology.
