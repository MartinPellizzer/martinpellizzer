Campanopsis ecklonii

Campanopsis ecklonii, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Campanopsis ecklonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
