Clematis hexapetala subsp. microphylla

Clematis hexapetala subsp.

microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved sweet Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale blue flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories. It has a climbing habit and produces showy blooms in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
