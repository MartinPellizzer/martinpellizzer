Citrus articulata

Citrus articulata, commonly known as the Chinese citrus or the fragrant citrus, is a species of flowering plant in the Rutaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or orange when mature. Its botanical features include a woody stem, simple leaves with a smooth surface, and a distinctive aroma.

Citrus articulata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of essential oils.
