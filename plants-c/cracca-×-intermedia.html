Cracca × intermedia

Cracca × intermedia, commonly known as the hybrid yellow rocket, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its yellow flowers, which appear in late spring to early summer, attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cracca × intermedia is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in full sun to partial shade.
