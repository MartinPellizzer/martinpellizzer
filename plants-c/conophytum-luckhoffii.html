Conophytum luckhoffii

Conophytum luckhoffii, commonly known as the "Stemless Stonecrop," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that form a rosette shape. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative succulent.
