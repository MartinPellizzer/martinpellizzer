Clerodendrum mimicum

Clerodendrum mimicum, commonly known as the mimic trumpet bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet, often resembling other flowers to attract pollinators. The plant produces small, green, egg-shaped fruits that turn yellow when mature. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Clerodendrum mimicum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a hedge plant.
