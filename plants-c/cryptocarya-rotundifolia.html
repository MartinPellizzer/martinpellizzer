Cryptocarya rotundifolia

Cryptocarya rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leafed cryptocarya, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, round-leafed foliage and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The fruit is a small, dark berry that contains several seeds, contributing to its ecological role in forest ecosystems.
