Crotalaria rothii

Crotalaria rothii, commonly known as the tropical crownflower, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant features distinctive yellow-orange flowers clustered in racemes, often surrounded by leaf-like bracts. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often pubescent, while the pods are cylindrical and dehiscent.

Crotalaria rothii is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it valuable in agricultural settings.
