Caladium zamiifolium

Caladium zamiifolium, commonly known as the Mexican flame flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety red bracts that surround its small, yellow flowers. The plant produces showy, papery bracts that are often mistaken for petals, while the true flowers are small and inconspicuous. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Caladium zamiifolium is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
