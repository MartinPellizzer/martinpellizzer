Cyperus kuntzei

Cyperus kuntzei, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist to aquatic conditions. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by water.

Cyperus kuntzei plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
