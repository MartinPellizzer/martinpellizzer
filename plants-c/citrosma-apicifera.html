Citrosma apicifera

Citrosma apicifera, commonly known as the mountain citrus or wild citron, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, leathery leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature, with a thick, citrus-like rind. Its botanical features make it valuable in traditional medicine and horticulture.

Citrosma apicifera is also noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
