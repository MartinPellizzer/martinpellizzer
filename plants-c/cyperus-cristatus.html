Cyperus cristatus

Cyperus cristatus, commonly known as the crested grass or spiky sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and distinctive, spike-like inflorescences that give it its common name. It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in wetlands and along water bodies.

Cyperus cristatus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
