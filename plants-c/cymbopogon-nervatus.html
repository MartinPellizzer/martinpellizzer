Cymbopogon nervatus

Cymbopogon nervatus, commonly known as narrowleaf lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often aromatic and used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typical of the Poaceae family. Its essential oils, particularly citral, are valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

Cymbopogon nervatus is often cultivated for its fragrance and potential therapeutic uses.
