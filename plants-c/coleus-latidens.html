Coleus latidens

Coleus latidens, commonly known as the broadleaf coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, purple, and green. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is known for its square, hairy stems. Its leaves are typically large and broad, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Coleus latidens thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
