Cryptocarya merrillii

Cryptocarya merrillii, commonly known as the Malabar ironwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves. The tree is known for its hard, dense wood, which is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. It typically grows in moist, shaded habitats and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Cryptocarya merrillii plays an important ecological role in its native environment, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
