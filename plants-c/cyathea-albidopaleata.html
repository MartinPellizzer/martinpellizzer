Cyathea albidopaleata

Cyathea albidopaleata, commonly known as the white-veined tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The fronds are typically light green with distinct white veins, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
