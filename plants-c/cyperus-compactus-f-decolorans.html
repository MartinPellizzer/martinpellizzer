Cyperus compactus f. decolorans

Cyperus compactus f. decolorans, commonly known as the variable flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, clustered inflorescences. The plant exhibits a unique color variation, with the inflorescences often changing from green to reddish or brown as they mature. It thrives in wetland environments, including marshes and pond edges, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland management.
