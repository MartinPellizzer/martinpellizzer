Canscorinella

Canscorinella, commonly known as the rock saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Canscorinella is well adapted to saline soils and can thrive in areas with low rainfall.

Its hardy nature makes it an important component of coastal and inland desert ecosystems.
