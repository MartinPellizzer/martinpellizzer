Colquhounia seguinii var. pilosa

Colquhounia seguinii var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy colquhounia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green foliage and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is often found in woodland areas and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
