Castela jacquiniifolia

Castela jacquiniifolia, commonly known as the golden bower or wild sweet pepper.

It is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States, particularly Florida and Georgia. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal racemes. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that contains several seeds.

The plant is often found in wetlands and along the edges of swamps, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils.
