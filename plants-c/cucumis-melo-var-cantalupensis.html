Cucumis melo var. cantalupensis

Cucumis melo var. cantalupensis, commonly known as the Cantaloupe, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to Southwestern Asia.

It is a trailing, annual vine that produces large, oblate, net-veined fruits with a sweet, aromatic flavor. The fruit typically has a yellow to orange rind and a pale green to yellow flesh. It is prized for its high water content and is widely cultivated for both fresh consumption and processing.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
