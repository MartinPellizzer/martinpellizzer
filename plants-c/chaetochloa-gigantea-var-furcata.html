Chaetochloa gigantea var. furcata

Chaetochloa gigantea var. furcata, commonly known as the giant hairgrass, is a unique grass species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of long, thread-like leaves that give it a fluffy appearance. The plant thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its foliage is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to tolerate moist conditions.

The varietal form *furcata* distinguishes it from other varieties through its distinctive branching and growth patterns.
