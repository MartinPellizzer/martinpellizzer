Cyperus aureobrunneus

Cyperus aureobrunneus, commonly known as the golden rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically brown or golden in color, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist or wet environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

Cyperus aureobrunneus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
