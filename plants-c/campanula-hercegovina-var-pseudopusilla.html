Campanula hercegovina var. pseudopusilla

Campanula hercegovina var. pseudopusilla, commonly known as the Hercegovaian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
