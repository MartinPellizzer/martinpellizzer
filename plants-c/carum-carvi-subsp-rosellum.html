Carum carvi subsp. rosellum

Carum carvi subsp.

rosellum, commonly known as wild caraway, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges.

The plant features slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The seeds, which are small and dark, are harvested for their essential oils and culinary uses.
