Cosmos sessilis

Cosmos sessilis, commonly known as the sessile cosmos, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for dry gardens.

The plant is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
