Chusquea wettsteinii

Chusquea wettsteinii, commonly known as the Wettstein's bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the subfamily Bambusoideae and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features long, slender culms that are often used in traditional crafts by local communities. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.

Chusquea wettsteinii thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.
