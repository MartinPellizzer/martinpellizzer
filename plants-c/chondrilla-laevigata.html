Chondrilla laevigata

Chondrilla laevigata, commonly known as the smooth chondrilla, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and yellow flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are usually lance-shaped. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
