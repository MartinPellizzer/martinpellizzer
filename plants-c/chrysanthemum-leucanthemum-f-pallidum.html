Chrysanthemum leucanthemum f. pallidum

Chrysanthemum leucanthemum f. pallidum, commonly known as the pale marguerite, is a cultivar of the common marguerite plant.

It is characterized by its pale yellow to white flowers, which are typically smaller and more delicate than those of the typical species. The plant features a compact growth habit with hairy stems and basal leaves that are usually spoon-shaped. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
