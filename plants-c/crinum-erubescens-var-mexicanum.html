Crinum erubescens var. mexicanum

Crinum erubescens var. mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican red lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink to red in color, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. The plant has strap-like, grassy leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical characteristics include a bulbous root system and a distinct, fragrant floral scent.
