Calea coriacea

Calea coriacea, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and its unique, showy flowers that open at night. The flowers are typically white or pale pink and have a distinctive, waxy appearance. Calea coriacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to bloom in low-light conditions.

It prefers well-drained soil and moderate humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
