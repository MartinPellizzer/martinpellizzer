Catatia cordata

Catatia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf catatia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are typically thick and succulent, adapted to retain water in arid conditions.

The flowers bloom in spring and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
