Cepa schoenoprasa

Cepa schoenoprasa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved onion, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-like, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and linear. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers in umbels, which bloom in the spring. Its bulbous root system is similar to that of other Allium species, and it is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

The plant is noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
