Chamomilla meridionalis

Chamomilla meridionalis, commonly known as the southern chamomile, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, while the flowers are used in traditional medicine for their calming properties.

Chamomilla meridionalis is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and herbal remedies.
