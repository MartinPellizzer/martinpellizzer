Cheiridopsis excavata

Cheiridopsis excavata, commonly known as the burrowing beauty, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
