Cyclanthera stenura

Cyclanthera stenura, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bitter gourd, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and pumpkins. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and elongated, bitter-tasting fruits that are typically harvested when immature. Cyclanthera stenura is often cultivated for its edible, albeit bitter, fruits, which are used in various traditional cuisines.
