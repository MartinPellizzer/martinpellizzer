Campomanesia kiaerskoviana var. impressovenosa

Campomanesia kiaerskoviana var. impressovenosa, commonly known as the impressovenosa persimmon, is a rare and lesser-known species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a small, yellowish berry that ripens in late summer and is edible, though not widely cultivated.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and potential in horticulture due to its unique appearance and adaptability to tropical climates.
