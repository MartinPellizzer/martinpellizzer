Cyperus flavescens var. rubromarginatus

Cyperus flavescens var. rubromarginatus, commonly known as the red-margin flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are usually narrow and have a distinct reddish margin, which gives it its common name.

The flowering spikes are usually small and clustered, producing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
