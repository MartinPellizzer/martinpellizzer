Cistus ladanifer subvar. guttatus

Cistus ladanifer subvar. guttatus, commonly known as the purple rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, violet to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces a distinctive, sticky resin known as ladanum, which has been historically used in perfumery and traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing on rocky or limestone substrates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
