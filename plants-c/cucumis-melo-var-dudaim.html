Cucumis melo var. dudaim

Cucumis melo var. dudaim, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a vine that produces elongated, thin fruits with a mild, sweet flavor. The plant typically grows in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are broad and lobed, while its flowers are yellow and borne in clusters.

This variety is often cultivated for its crisp, edible cucumbers, which are used in various culinary applications.
