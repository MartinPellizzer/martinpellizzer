Cuphea villosa

Cuphea villosa, commonly known as the fuzzy cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and foliage, which give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and orange, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cuphea villosa is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its vibrant blooms and drought tolerance.
