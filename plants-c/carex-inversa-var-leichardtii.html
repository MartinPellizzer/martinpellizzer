Carex inversa var. leichardtii

Carex inversa var. leichardtii, commonly known as the Leichardt's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along water bodies.

This variety is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
