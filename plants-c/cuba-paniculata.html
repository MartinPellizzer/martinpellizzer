Cuba paniculata

Cuba paniculata, commonly known as the Cuban panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and panicles of small, fluffy flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.

Cuba paniculata is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its light, airy appearance.
