Capparis pachaca

Capparis pachaca, commonly known as the Andean crossberry, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family, which is characterized by its often thorny stems and showy flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture with a strong, pungent odor.

Capparis pachaca thrives in humid, mountainous environments and is often found in cloud forests at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.
