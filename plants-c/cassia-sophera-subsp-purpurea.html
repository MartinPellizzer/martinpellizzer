Cassia sophera subsp. purpurea

Cassia sophera subsp.

purpurea, commonly known as purple flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking purple or red bracts that surround its small yellow flowers, which bloom in summer.

The plant produces long, slender pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern.
