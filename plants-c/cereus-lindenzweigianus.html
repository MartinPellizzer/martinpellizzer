Cereus lindenzweigianus

Cereus lindenzweigianus, commonly known as the Lindenzweig's cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring a tall, spiny stem and clusters of white flowers that bloom at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water and reduce transpiration. Its flowering period typically occurs during the warmer months, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant's structure and growth habits reflect its evolutionary adaptations to survive in harsh, dry climates.
