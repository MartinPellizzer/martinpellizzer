Carex echinochloe

Carex echinochloe, commonly known as beak sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, clustered in dense spikes, and it produces three-sided, triangular seeds.

Carex echinochloe plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
