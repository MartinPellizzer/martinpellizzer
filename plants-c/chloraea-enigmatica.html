Chloraea enigmatica

Chloraea enigmatica, commonly known as the enigmatic greenbloom, is a rare flowering plant native to the misty highlands of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its striking, pale green petals that shimmer under sunlight, giving it a unique and almost ethereal appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom during the cooler months, typically between May and August. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its humid, tropical environment.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, Chloraea enigmatica remains a subject of botanical fascination and study.
