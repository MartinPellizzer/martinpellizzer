Caragana hopeiensis

Caragana hopeiensis, commonly known as the Hope's shrub or the Chinese peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 5 to 9 pairs of small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, followed by legume pods containing several seeds. It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including drought and poor soil, making it a valuable plant for erosion control.

Caragana hopeiensis is also used in traditional medicine and as a source of forage for livestock.
