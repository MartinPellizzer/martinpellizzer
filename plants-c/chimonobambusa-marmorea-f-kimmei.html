Chimonobambusa marmorea f. kimmei

Chimonobambusa marmorea f. kimmei, commonly known as the Kimmei bamboo, is a unique variant of the marmoreal bamboo, characterized by its distinctive white and green striped culms.

This plant is a clump-forming bamboo that typically grows in temperate climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and often have a slight sheen. The plant is prized for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and slow growth rate.

It is also relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for gardeners and landscapers.
