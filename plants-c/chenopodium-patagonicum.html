Chenopodium patagonicum

Chenopodium patagonicum, commonly known as Patagonian goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Chenopodium patagonicum is also valued for its potential nutritional properties and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
