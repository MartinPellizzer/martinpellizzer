Cistocarpus

Cistocarpus, commonly known as the honeycomb tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its unique, woody, honeycomb-like seed pods that split open when mature. The plant is native to regions of southern Africa, particularly in countries like South Africa and Namibia. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, and the flowers are small and yellow or greenish-yellow.

Cistocarpus species are often found in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing their adaptability to harsh conditions.
