Cantua glomeriflora

Cantua glomeriflora, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces dense clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down.

Cantua glomeriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
