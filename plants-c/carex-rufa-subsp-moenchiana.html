Carex rufa subsp. moenchiana

Carex rufa subsp.

moenchiana, commonly known as the red sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces spikelets that are typically reddish in color.

The plant thrives in moist to wet environments, often found in swamps, bogs, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

The inflorescence consists of a terminal spike and a lower, shorter spike, with small, wind-pollinated flowers.
