Convolvulus pickeringii

Convolvulus pickeringii, commonly known as Pickering's bindweed, is a flowering plant native to California.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its scrambling growth habit and showy flowers. The plant produces small, five-petaled flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming in spring and early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

It is often found in open areas such as grasslands and disturbed sites.
