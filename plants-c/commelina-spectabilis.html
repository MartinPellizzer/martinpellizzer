Commelina spectabilis

Commelina spectabilis, commonly known as the showy spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features striking blue-violet flowers with three petals and three sepals, which are often mistaken for true petals. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Commelina spectabilis plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
