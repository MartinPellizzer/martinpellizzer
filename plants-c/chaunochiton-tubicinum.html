Chaunochiton tubicinum

Chaunochiton tubicinum, commonly known as the tubicinum chiton, is a species of sea slug belonging to the family Chaonidae.

It is characterized by its elongated, shell-less body covered in a row of calcareous plates along its dorsal side. This marine gastropod is typically found in shallow coastal waters, where it feeds on algae and other microscopic organisms. Its coloration can vary, often featuring shades of brown or green, which help it blend into its rocky or sandy habitat.

The tubicinum chiton plays a role in marine ecosystems by contributing to the breakdown of organic matter and serving as prey for various predators.
