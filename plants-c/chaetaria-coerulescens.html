Chaetaria coerulescens

Chaetaria coerulescens, commonly known as the blue-stemmed hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green stems and fine, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
