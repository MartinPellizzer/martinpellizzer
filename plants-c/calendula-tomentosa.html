Calendula tomentosa

Calendula tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly marigold, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fuzzy, grayish-green foliage and vibrant yellow to orange flowers. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties. Calendula tomentosa thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Its flowers are often used in herbal remedies and as a natural dye.
