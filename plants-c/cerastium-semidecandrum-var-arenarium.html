Cerastium semidecandrum var. arenarium

Cerastium semidecandrum var. arenarium, commonly known as sand chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to sandy and coastal environments.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in clusters of five to ten. The plant has hairy, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dunes or rocky habitats.

Its compact growth habit and ability to withstand harsh conditions make it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
