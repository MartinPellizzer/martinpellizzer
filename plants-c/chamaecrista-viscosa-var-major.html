Chamaecrista viscosa var. major

Chamaecrista viscosa var. major, commonly known as the sticky crownbeard, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in racemes. Its fruits are legumes, containing several seeds, and the plant is adapted to thrive in dry, sandy soils.

It is often found in savannas and coastal areas, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.
