Cirsium remotifolium var. oregonense

Cirsium remotifolium var. oregonense, commonly known as the Oregon thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish-purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands, woodland edges, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough, hairy texture and often have a distinctive basal rosette.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.
