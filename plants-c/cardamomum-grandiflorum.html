Cardamomum grandiflorum

Cardamomum grandiflorum, commonly known as the large-flowered cardamom, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, three-lobed berries that contain aromatic seeds used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-drained soil.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
