Campanula rotundifolia var. cespitosa

Campanula rotundifolia var. cespitosa, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features basal rosettes of rounded, hairy leaves and produces upright stems bearing bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils in woodland or meadow habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowering display.

The species is also known for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its tolerance to wet conditions.
