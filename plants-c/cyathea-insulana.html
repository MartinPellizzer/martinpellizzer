Cyathea insulana

Cyathea insulana, commonly known as the island tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in persistent, fibrous roots. The fronds are pinnately compound, with a dark green color and a distinctive, upright growth habit. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

This plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
