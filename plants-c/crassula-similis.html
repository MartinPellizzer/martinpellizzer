Crassula similis

Crassula similis, commonly known as the "Baby Blue" or "Little Leaf" Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features compact, rosette-shaped growth with small, fleshy, blue-green leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink during the spring or early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil with bright, indirect light.

Crassula similis is popular as a decorative indoor plant due to its low maintenance and unique appearance.
