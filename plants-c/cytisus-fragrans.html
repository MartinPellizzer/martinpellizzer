Cytisus fragrans

Cytisus fragrans, commonly known as sweet honey bush, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring slender, woody stems covered in fine, yellowish hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, emitting a sweet, honey-like fragrance. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
