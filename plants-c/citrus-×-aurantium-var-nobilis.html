Citrus × aurantium var. nobilis

Citrus × aurantium var. nobilis, commonly known as the sweet orange, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped, thick-skinned fruit that contains a high amount of juice and a sweet, aromatic pulp. The plant is a small evergreen tree with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. It thrives in warm, subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is widely cultivated for consumption and is a key ingredient in various culinary and medicinal applications.
