Colysis raciborskii

Colysis raciborskii, commonly known as the blue-green algae or cyanobacteria, is a type of photosynthetic prokaryote belonging to the phylum Cyanobacteria.

It is notable for its ability to perform oxygenic photosynthesis, similar to plants, and is often found in freshwater environments such as lakes and ponds. This organism can form harmful algal blooms, which can produce toxins detrimental to aquatic life and human health. Colysis raciborskii is also capable of nitrogen fixation, contributing to nutrient cycling in aquatic ecosystems.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a significant species in both ecological and environmental studies.
