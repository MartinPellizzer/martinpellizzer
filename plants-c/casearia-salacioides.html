Casearia salacioides

Casearia salacioides, commonly known as the red-fruited casearia, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Salicaceae, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine, and the plant is adapted to grow in moist, shaded environments.

The fruit is an important food source for various bird species in its native habitat.
