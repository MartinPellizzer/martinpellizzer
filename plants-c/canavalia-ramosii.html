Canavalia ramosii

Canavalia ramosii, commonly known as the hairy urdbean, is a tropical legume native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, trifoliate leaves, and distinctive hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that give way to elongated, pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It is often grown for its edible seeds, which are used in various cuisines.

Canavalia ramosii is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
