Chamaecrista plumosa var. plumosa

Chamaecrista plumosa var. plumosa, commonly known as the plumose ticktrefoil, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a climbing or trailing growth habit. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with each leaflet being narrow and linear, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
