Crotalaria protensa

Crotalaria protensa, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which is characterized by its legume pods and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, branched stems and small, alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow with a distinctive black or purple spot at the base, and it produces elongated pods containing several seeds.

Crotalaria protensa is often found in disturbed soils and can be a weed in agricultural settings.
