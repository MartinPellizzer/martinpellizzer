Corylopsis multiflora var. parvifolia

Corylopsis multiflora var. parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf Chinese lantern bush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, with a glossy green surface. It prefers well-drained, acidic soils and partial to full sun, making it a suitable plant for woodland gardens.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and hardiness in temperate climates.
