Calytrix rupestris

Calytrix rupestris, commonly known as the rock hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in spring and summer. Its leaves are small, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Calytrix rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
