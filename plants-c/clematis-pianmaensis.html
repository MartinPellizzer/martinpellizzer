Clematis pianmaensis

Clematis pianmaensis, commonly known as the Pianma Clematis, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, usually blue or purple flowers. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often armed with hooked thorns.

It thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
