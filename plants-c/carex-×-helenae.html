Carex × helenae

Carex × helenae, commonly known as the Helen's sedge, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
