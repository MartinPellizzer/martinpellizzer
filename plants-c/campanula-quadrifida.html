Campanula quadrifida

Campanula quadrifida, commonly known as the four-leaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a basal rosette of leaves that are typically rounded and hairy, with four leaves emerging from each node. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Campanula quadrifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
