Capparis gomeziana

Capparis gomeziana, commonly known as the Gomez's caper, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in rocky, coastal environments and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in Mediterranean climates.
