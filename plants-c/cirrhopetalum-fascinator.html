Cirrhopetalum fascinator

Cirrhopetalum fascinator, commonly known as the flame orchid, is a strikingly beautiful flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, flame-like flowers that range from red to orange in color. The plant produces pendulous inflorescences with showy, spurred petals that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its botanical structure includes a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its overall ornamental appeal.
