Crataegus melanocarpa-maeotica

Crataegus melanocarpa-maeotica, commonly known as the Maikot raspberry, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red or black when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
