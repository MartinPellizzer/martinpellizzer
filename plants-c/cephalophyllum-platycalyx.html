Cephalophyllum platycalyx

Cephalophyllum platycalyx, commonly known as the broad-calyx headplant, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its unique, rounded, head-like structure formed by tightly clustered stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle ornamental value. It is adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments with well-draining soil.

Due to its specialized growth habits, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens or as a potted plant.
