Calliandra mertensioides

Calliandra mertensioides, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, showy inflorescences composed of numerous pink to red bracteate flowers. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves during the dry season, and has compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.

Additionally, it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
