Cloezia ligustrina var. latifolia

Cloezia ligustrina var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cloezia, is a perennial shrub native to southern Africa.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
