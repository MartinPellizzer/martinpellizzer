Cyperus lividus

Cyperus lividus, commonly known as the blue flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. Cyperus lividus thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in disturbed soils near water bodies.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for small invertebrates and contributing to wetland biodiversity.
