Claytonia panamintensis

Claytonia panamintensis, commonly known as Panamint mountain sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the Mojave Desert in California and Nevada.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features basal rosettes of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves and produces small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring. It is adapted to arid environments with minimal water availability.

Claytonia panamintensis plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various insects and small animals.
