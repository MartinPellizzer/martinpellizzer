Centaurea polyacantha f. colorata

Centaurea polyacantha f. colorata, commonly known as the colorful thistle, is a variant of the species Centaurea polyacantha.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful flower heads that range from red to orange, often with a striking purple or blue center. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a rough, hairy texture.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and for its ecological benefits to pollinators.
