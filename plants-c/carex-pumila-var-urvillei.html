Carex pumila var. urvillei

Carex pumila var. urvillei, commonly known as the Urville's sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and rushes. This variety is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop in spikelets. It typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
