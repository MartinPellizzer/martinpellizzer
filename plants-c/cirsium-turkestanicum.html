Cirsium turkestanicum

Cirsium turkestanicum, commonly known as the Turkestan thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it is often found in mountainous regions.

Cirsium turkestanicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
