Carex vesicaria var. lanceolata

Carex vesicaria var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrowleaf marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and moist soils in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and three-ranked leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
