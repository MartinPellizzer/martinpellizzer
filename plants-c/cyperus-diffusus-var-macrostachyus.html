Cyperus diffusus var. macrostachyus

Cyperus diffusus var. macrostachyus, commonly known as the broad-stalked flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, flowering structures typical of the Cyperaceae family.

The leaves are usually narrow and blade-like, with a rough texture that distinguishes it from other similar species.
