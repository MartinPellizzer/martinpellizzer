Centaurea tenoreana

Centaurea tenoreana, commonly known as the Tenore thistle, is a flowering plant native to the island of Sicily.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking purple-blue flowers and spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Centaurea tenoreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
