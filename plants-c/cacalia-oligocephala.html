Cacalia oligocephala

Cacalia oligocephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed cacalia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and purple. The plant has slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Native to South Africa, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
