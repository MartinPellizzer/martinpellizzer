Coreopsis angustata

Coreopsis angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf coreopsis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the central and eastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies adds ecological value to its habitat.
