Ceanothus azureus

Ceanothus azureus, commonly known as the blue curl buckthorn, is a shrub native to California.

It features striking blue-gray foliage and produces clusters of small, pea-like flowers that range from blue to purple. The plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral habitats. It plays a role in supporting local pollinators and has been used in traditional medicine by indigenous peoples.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
