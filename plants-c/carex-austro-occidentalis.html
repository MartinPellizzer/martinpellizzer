Carex austro-occidentalis

Carex austro-occidentalis, commonly known as the southern-western sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile, where it thrives in moist, temperate environments. The plant typically grows in dense clumps, with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other areas with consistent moisture.

Due to its adaptability, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
