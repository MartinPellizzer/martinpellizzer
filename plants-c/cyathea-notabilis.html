Cyathea notabilis

Cyathea notabilis, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, frond-like leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan. Its fronds are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, with a distinctive, elegant appearance.

Cyathea notabilis plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
