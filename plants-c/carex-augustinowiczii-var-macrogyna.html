Carex augustinowiczii var. macrogyna

Carex augustinowiczii var. macrogyna, commonly known as the large-awned sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically wind-pollinated, and its inflorescence consists of a spike of spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens.
