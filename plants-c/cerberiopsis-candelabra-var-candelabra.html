Cerberiopsis candelabra var. candelabra

Cerberiopsis candelabra var. candelabra, commonly known as the candlestick tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, branching structure that resembles a candlestick, with multiple slender shoots emerging from a central trunk. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible fruits resembling small, round oranges. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets arranged in a feather-like pattern.
