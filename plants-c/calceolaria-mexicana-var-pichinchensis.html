Calceolaria mexicana var. pichinchensis

Calceolaria mexicana var. pichinchensis, commonly known as the Pichincha slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive calyx-shaped petals that resemble small slippers, a feature that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or well-drained soils. It produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow, which bloom during the warmer months.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
