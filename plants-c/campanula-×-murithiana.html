Campanula × murithiana

Campanula × murithiana, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both Campanula carpatica and Campanula poscharskyana.

It is a herbaceous perennial with upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple. The plant is native to the alpine regions of Europe and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

Campanula × murithiana is relatively low maintenance and suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.
