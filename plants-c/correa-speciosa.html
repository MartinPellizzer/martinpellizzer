Correa speciosa

Correa speciosa, commonly known as the sweetpea bush, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Correa speciosa is also valued for its ability to attract native pollinators such as birds and insects.
