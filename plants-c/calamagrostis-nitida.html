Calamagrostis nitida

Calamagrostis nitida, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, silver-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. Its flowering stems produce panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are often tinged with green or reddish hues.

Calamagrostis nitida is valued in landscaping for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
