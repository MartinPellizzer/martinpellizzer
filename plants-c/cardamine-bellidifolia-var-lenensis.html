Cardamine bellidifolia var. lenensis

Cardamine bellidifolia var. lenensis, commonly known as the Lenensian watercress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is notable for its ability to thrive in cold climates and its unique adaptation to nutrient-poor soils.
