Cestrum bogotense var. latifolium

Cestrum bogotense var. latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically at night. The plant has oval-shaped, broad leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
