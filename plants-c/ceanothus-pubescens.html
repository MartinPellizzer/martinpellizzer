Ceanothus pubescens

Ceanothus pubescens, commonly known as the wooly ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant blue or purple flowers in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Ceanothus pubescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
