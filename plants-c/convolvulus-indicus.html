Convolvulus indicus

Convolvulus indicus, commonly known as Indian morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae).

It is native to southern and eastern Asia, including India, and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found growing along fences, walls, and in disturbed soils. Its seeds are dispersed by birds and animals, contributing to its widespread distribution.

The plant is also known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
