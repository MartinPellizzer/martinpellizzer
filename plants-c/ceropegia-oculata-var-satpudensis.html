Ceropegia oculata var. satpudensis

Ceropegia oculata var. satpudensis, commonly known as the "Lion's Head" or "Kangra Lily," is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India.

It is a succulent herb with fleshy, tuberous roots and distinctive, spurred flowers that resemble the head of a lion. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its flowers are usually pale green to white with a red or purple calyx, and it produces small, edible berries.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
