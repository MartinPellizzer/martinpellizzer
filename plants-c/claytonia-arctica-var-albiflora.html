Claytonia arctica var. albiflora

Claytonia arctica var. albiflora, commonly known as the Arctic white flower, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and subarctic regions.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in cold, rocky environments. The plant features rosettes of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that help it retain moisture in harsh conditions. Its flowers are white or pale pink, typically appearing in early spring.

This species is notable for its adaptation to extreme climates and is often found in tundra and alpine habitats.
