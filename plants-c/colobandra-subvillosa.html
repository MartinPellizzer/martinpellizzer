Colobandra subvillosa

Colobandra subvillosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf colobandra, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
