Cracca cana

Cracca cana, commonly known as the yellow crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has palmately divided leaves and produces small, aggregate fruits. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Cracca cana is often used in traditional medicine for its purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.
