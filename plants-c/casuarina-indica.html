Casuarina indica

Casuarina indica, commonly known as the Indian she-oak, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, needle-like foliage and distinctive cylindrical cones. The plant has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion, making it valuable in coastal and sandy environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Casuarina indica is also known for its ability to thrive in saline and arid conditions, contributing to its ecological significance in various regions.
