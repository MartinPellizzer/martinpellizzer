Celastrus rupestris

Celastrus rupestris, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in woodland edges and open areas. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by orange or red berries. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly waxy texture.

Celastrus rupestris is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
