Carum karatavica

Carum karatavica, commonly known as Karatavica caraway, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes parsley, dill, and fennel, and is characterized by its finely divided leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, oval fruits that are often used as a spice, similar to caraway seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or arid soils.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
