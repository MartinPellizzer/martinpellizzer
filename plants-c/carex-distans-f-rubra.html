Carex distans f. rubra

Carex distans f. rubra, commonly known as the red-fruited sedge, is a variant of the common sedge, characterized by its distinctive reddish hues in its inflorescence and fruits.

It is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, typically found in wet or moist environments such as marshes and wet meadows. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are often tinged with red, adding visual interest to its appearance. It has a clumping growth habit and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and seasonal color changes.

The botanical features of Carex distans f. rubra make it a valuable plant for wetland restoration and ecological gardening.
