Carex masoalensis

Carex masoalensis, commonly known as the Masoalensis sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
