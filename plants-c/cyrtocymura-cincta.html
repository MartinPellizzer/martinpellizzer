Cyrtocymura cincta

Cyrtocymura cincta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock cactus, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, cylindrical stem that is often ribbed and covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
