Capparis spinosa var. himalayensis

Capparis spinosa var. himalayensis, commonly known as the Himalayan caper, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its woody stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by fleshy, edible fruits. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cuisines and medicine.
