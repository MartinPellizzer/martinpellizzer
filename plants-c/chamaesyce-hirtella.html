Chamaesyce hirtella

Chamaesyce hirtella, commonly known as the hairy sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features small, green, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, hairy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense, leafy inflorescences.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and open fields.
