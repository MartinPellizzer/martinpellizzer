Castalia tetragona

Castalia tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled castalia, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its square stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Castalia tetragona is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological and horticultural studies.
