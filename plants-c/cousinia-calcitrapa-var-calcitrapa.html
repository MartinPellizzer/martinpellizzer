Cousinia calcitrapa var. calcitrapa

Cousinia calcitrapa var. calcitrapa, commonly known as the rock couch grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features a basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves and produces small, pinkish to purple flowers in elongated clusters. It is known for its ability to thrive in low-nutrient soils and is often found in mountainous and steppe areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies of xerophytic vegetation.
