Cousinia beckeri

Cousinia beckeri, commonly known as Becker's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow composite flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. It has a compact growth habit with a rosette of basal leaves.

Cousinia beckeri is often found in steppe and desert environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small organisms.
