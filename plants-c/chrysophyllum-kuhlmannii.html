Chrysophyllum kuhlmannii

Chrysophyllum kuhlmannii, commonly known as the Natal plum or yellow bell, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, yellow, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The tree is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its role in traditional medicine.
