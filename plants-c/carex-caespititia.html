Carex caespititia

Carex caespititia, commonly known as tufted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and similar plants. This plant typically forms dense clumps of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, wetlands, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex caespititia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
