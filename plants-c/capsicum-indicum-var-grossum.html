Capsicum indicum var. grossum

Capsicum indicum var. grossum, commonly known as the horn pepper, is a tropical plant native to India and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, curved fruits that resemble horns, typically ranging in color from yellow to red. The plant produces small white flowers and has compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and mild to spicy flavor, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary purposes.
