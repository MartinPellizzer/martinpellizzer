Carex apoda

Carex apoda, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Native to wetlands and moist soils in North America, it thrives in environments with consistent water availability. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and stabilize soil.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for aquatic insects and contributing to wetland biodiversity.
