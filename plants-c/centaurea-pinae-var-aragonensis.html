Centaurea pinae var. aragonensis

Centaurea pinae var. aragonensis, commonly known as the Aragon centaury, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowering stems are often branched.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
