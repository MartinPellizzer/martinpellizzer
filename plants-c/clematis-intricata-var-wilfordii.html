Clematis intricata var. wilfordii

Clematis intricata var. wilfordii, commonly known as the Wilford's Clematis, is a climbing vine native to China.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range from white to pale blue, often with a hint of pink. The plant has finely divided, palmately compound leaves that are typically light green and emit a mild, spicy scent. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant blooms.
