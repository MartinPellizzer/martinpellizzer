Catasetum mattosianum

Catasetum mattosianum, commonly known as the Mattos's orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are notable for their intricate structure, featuring specialized pollination mechanisms.

Catasetum mattosianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems.
