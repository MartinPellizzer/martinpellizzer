Crepis japonica var. elstonii

Crepis japonica var. elstonii, commonly known as the Japanese mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with basal rosettes of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, and it is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
