Cnicus purpureus

Cnicus purpureus, commonly known as the purple sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are generally gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and its flowers range from yellow to purple.

Cnicus purpureus is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
