Calophyllum touranense

Calophyllum touranense, commonly known as the African beautyberry, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves that are elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by bright red berries that are visually striking. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
