Calligonum matteianum

Calligonum matteianum, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub with gray-green, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to anchor itself in sandy soils and access deep groundwater.

Calligonum matteianum plays a vital role in stabilizing dunes and preventing soil erosion in its native habitat.
