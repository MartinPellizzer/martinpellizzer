Cuspidia cernua

Cuspidia cernua, commonly known as the hanging ice plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as farina. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves allows it to survive in arid environments with minimal irrigation.
