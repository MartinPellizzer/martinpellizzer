Citharexylum pauciflorum

Citharexylum pauciflorum, commonly known as the fragrant cithara, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often aromatic. Its flowers are small, fragrant, and usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.
