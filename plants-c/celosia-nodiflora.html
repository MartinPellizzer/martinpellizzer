Celosia nodiflora

Celosia nodiflora, commonly known as the clustered celosia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces vibrant, spiky bracts that resemble small flames or pine cones, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a slightly hairy texture.

Celosia nodiflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to thrive in warm, sunny conditions.
