Coelogyne lumakuensis

Coelogyne lumakuensis, commonly known as the Lumakuensis orchid, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is a herbaceous plant with a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces strikingly colorful, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically elongated and arranged in a rosette pattern around the pseudobulb.

Coelogyne lumakuensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
