Clidemia cinnamomifolia

Clidemia cinnamomifolia, commonly known as cinnamonleaf or sweetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Melastomataceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers with five petals, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Clidemia cinnamomifolia is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and floral display.
