Commiphora morogorensis

Commiphora morogorensis, commonly known as the Morogorensis myrrh tree, is a flowering plant native to East Africa, particularly Tanzania.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap, which has been traditionally used in local medicine. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, with smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves.

Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, and it produces a yellowish resin that is harvested for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
