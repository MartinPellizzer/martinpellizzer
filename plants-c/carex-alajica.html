Carex alajica

Carex alajica, commonly known as the Alajic sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate to cold climates. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, inconspicuous flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Carex alajica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in alpine environments.
