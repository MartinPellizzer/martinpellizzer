Cassia pachycarpa

Cassia pachycarpa, commonly known as the thick-fruited cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow, fragrant flowers. The plant produces large, woody pods that contain numerous seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia pachycarpa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
