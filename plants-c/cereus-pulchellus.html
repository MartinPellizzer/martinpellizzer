Cereus pulchellus

Cereus pulchellus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and its ability to produce large, fragrant flowers at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and open only during the evening.

Cereus pulchellus is popular in horticulture for its striking appearance and unique flowering behavior.
