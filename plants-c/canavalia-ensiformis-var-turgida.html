Canavalia ensiformis var. turgida

Canavalia ensiformis var. turgida, commonly known as the giant hyacinth bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features trifoliate leaves with elongated, serrated leaflets and produces distinctive, showy flowers that are typically purple or violet in color. The plant bears pods that contain several seeds, which are often used as a food source in various cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and nutritional properties.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
