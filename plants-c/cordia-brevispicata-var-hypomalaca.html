Cordia brevispicata var. hypomalaca

Cordia brevispicata var. hypomalaca, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cottonwood, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by woody capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed or open habitats.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to tolerate moderate drought conditions.
