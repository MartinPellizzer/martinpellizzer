Crataegus maura var. tarraconensis

Crataegus maura var. tarraconensis, commonly known as the Tarraconense hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine.
