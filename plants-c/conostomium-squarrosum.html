Conostomium squarrosum

Conostomium squarrosum, commonly known as the rough conostomium, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, bristly hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in greenish or reddish hues, and bloom during the spring and summer months.

Conostomium squarrosum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage.
