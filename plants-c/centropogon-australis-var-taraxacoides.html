Centropogon australis var. taraxacoides

Centropogon australis var. taraxacoides, commonly known as the Australian dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a perennial herb with a rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
