Corchorus crassifolius

Corchorus crassifolius, commonly known as the broad-leaved kochia, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, palmate leaves that are broad and lobed, often with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to various environments.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in both ecological and ornamental contexts.
