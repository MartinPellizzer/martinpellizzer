Cassia surattensis subsp. glauca

Cassia surattensis subsp.

glauca, commonly known as the white-flowered cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and its striking white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Cassia surattensis subsp.

glauca is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
