Ceropegia longipedicellata

Ceropegia longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-stemmed ceropegia, is a climbing or trailing succulent native to southern Africa.

It features thin, jointed stems that can grow up to several meters in length, often adorned with small, opposite leaves. The plant produces unique, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its delicate appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening and hanging baskets.
