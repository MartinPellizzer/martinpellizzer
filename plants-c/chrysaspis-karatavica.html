Chrysaspis karatavica

Chrysaspis karatavica, commonly known as the Karatavica spider orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to specific regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, thin petals that resemble the legs of a spider. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. Its flowers are usually a mix of white, yellow, and green, creating a striking visual contrast.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
