Carex dissita

Carex dissita, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, which contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense clumps.

Carex dissita plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
