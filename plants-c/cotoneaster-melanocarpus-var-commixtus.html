Cotoneaster melanocarpus var. commixtus

Cotoneaster melanocarpus var. commixtus, commonly known as the black-fruited cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by dark purple to black berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic and ecological benefits.
