Carthamodes carduncellus

Carthamodes carduncellus, commonly known as the cardoon, is a tall, perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its large, spiny leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils. Its latex contains compounds that have been used historically in medicine and as a source of natural rubber.

The cardoon is also cultivated for its edible leaves and as an ornamental plant.
