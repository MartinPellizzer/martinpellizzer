Cerasus micrantha

Cerasus micrantha, commonly known as the Chinese cherry or dwarf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, dark red to black berries that are edible but often tart. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it prefers well-drained soil in a sunny to partially shaded location.

Cerasus micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
