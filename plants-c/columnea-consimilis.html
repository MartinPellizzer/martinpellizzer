Columnea consimilis

Columnea consimilis, commonly known as the similar flame or similar trumpet vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing habit and produces clusters of bright red or orange tubular flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in humid, warm environments and prefers well-drained soil.

This species is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract hummingbirds.
