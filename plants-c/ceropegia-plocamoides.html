Ceropegia plocamoides

Ceropegia plocamoides, commonly known as the silver leaf vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, waxy, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers warm, sunny conditions.

Ceropegia plocamoides is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental foliage and ease of care.
