Campomanesia fusca var. integra

Campomanesia fusca var. integra, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It features elliptical, glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. The berries are edible, with a sweet and slightly tart flavor, and are often consumed fresh or used in jams and juices.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
