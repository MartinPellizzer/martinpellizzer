Cupressus japonica

Cupressus japonica, commonly known as the Japanese cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramid-shaped crown and scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The tree produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, and it is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

The Japanese cypress is also valued for its durability and is sometimes used in construction and furniture making.
