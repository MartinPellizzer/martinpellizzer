Crataegus shinnersii

Crataegus shinnersii, commonly known as Shinners hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in Georgia, South Carolina, and Florida. This shrub typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 feet and features thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a small, red berry that ripens in the fall, and the plant is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
