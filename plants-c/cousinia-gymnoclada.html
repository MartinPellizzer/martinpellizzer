Cousinia gymnoclada

Cousinia gymnoclada, commonly known as the naked-stemmed cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and feathery leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to drought conditions. It features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with slender stems and small, yellowish flowers.

Cousinia gymnoclada is often found in steppes and semi-desert environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
