Croton morifolius var. sphaerocarpus

Croton morifolius var. sphaerocarpus, commonly known as the globe croton, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, spherical-shaped fruits that resemble small, clustered globes, giving it its common name. The plant features palmate leaves with bright green veins and a vibrant, often variegated foliage that adds visual interest. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
