Celtis kraussiana var. flavescens

Celtis kraussiana var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered Celtis, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by small, round fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and inland forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
