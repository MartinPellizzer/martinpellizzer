Chamaecrista desvauxii var. desvauxii

Chamaecrista desvauxii var. desvauxii, commonly known as the desert false indigo, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub with compound leaves and produces striking yellow to orange flowers. The plant is adapted to dry environments, featuring deep taproots and waxy foliage to reduce water loss. Its flowers are typically arranged in dense clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is valued in xeriscaping for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
