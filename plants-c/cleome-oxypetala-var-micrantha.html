Cleome oxypetala var. micrantha

Cleome oxypetala var. micrantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cleome, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its flowers have five petals and are often accompanied by five sepals that are longer than the petals.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
