Capparis pyracantha

Capparis pyracantha, commonly known as the prickly custard apple, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by yellow, fleshy fruits that are edible when ripe. Its fruit has a sweet, custard-like texture and is often used in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
