Chrysocoma sparsifolia

Chrysocoma sparsifolia, commonly known as the sparseleaf chrysocoma, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. These flowers are followed by small, spherical berries that contain the plant's seeds.

It is adapted to grow in arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
