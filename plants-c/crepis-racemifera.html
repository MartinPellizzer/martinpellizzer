Crepis racemifera

Crepis racemifera, commonly known as the clustered cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
