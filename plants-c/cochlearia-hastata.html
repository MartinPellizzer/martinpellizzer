Cochlearia hastata

Cochlearia hastata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cochlearia, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of Europe and western Asia, often growing in sandy or rocky habitats. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in a raceme. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive basal rosette formation.

Cochlearia hastata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
