Chenopodium angulosum

Chenopodium angulosum, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and succulent, angular stems that are often covered in fine hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate to rhomboidal in shape, and typically have wavy or serrated margins. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits known as utricles.

This plant is commonly found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions.
