Cornus mas f. albocarpa

Cornus mas f. albocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited dogwood, is a cultivar of the Cornus mas species, which is native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its white or pale yellow berries, which contrast with its dark green leaves and showy white bracts. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in gardens for its seasonal interest.
