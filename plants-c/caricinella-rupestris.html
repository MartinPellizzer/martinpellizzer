Caricinella rupestris

Caricinella rupestris, commonly known as the rock fig, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, enabling it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It typically grows on rocky outcrops and is well adapted to full sun exposure.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
