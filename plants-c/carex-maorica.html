Carex maorica

Carex maorica, commonly known as the New Zealand sedge, is a perennial plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grasses, and reeds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Carex maorica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
