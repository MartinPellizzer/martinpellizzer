Cynanchum monense

Cynanchum monense, commonly known as the Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing seeds. It is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

Cynanchum monense is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
