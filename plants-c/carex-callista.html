Carex callista

Carex callista, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Carex callista produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization in wetland habitats.
