Crocus × fritschii

Crocus × fritschii, commonly known as the Persian crocus, is a hybrid species within the Crocus genus.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers with a distinctive yellow center. This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Middle East. It typically grows to a height of 10–20 cm and produces showy, cup-shaped blooms in late winter to early spring.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
