Copaifera coriacea

Copaifera coriacea, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a large evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its thick, aromatic bark and can grow up to 30 meters in height. The plant produces yellowish to reddish resin, which is often harvested for its medicinal properties. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape.

Copaifera coriacea plays an important ecological role in the rainforest by providing habitat and contributing to the biodiversity of the region.
