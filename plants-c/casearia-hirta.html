Casearia hirta

Casearia hirta, commonly known as the roughcase tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family, which includes willows and poplars, and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature.

Casearia hirta is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine to treat various ailments.
