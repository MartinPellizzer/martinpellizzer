Crescentia latifolia

Crescentia latifolia, commonly known as the Caribbean trumpet tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and its distinctive, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces a fleshy, spiny fruit that contains several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near rivers.

Crescentia latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
