Crataegus pruinosa var. delawarensis

Crataegus pruinosa var. delawarensis, commonly known as the Delaware hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. Its leaves are oval with serrated edges and turn yellow in autumn.

It thrives in well-drained soil and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.
