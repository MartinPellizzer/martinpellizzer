Chamaecrista adiantifolia var. pteridophylla

Chamaecrista adiantifolia var. pteridophylla, commonly known as the fern-like crownflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like foliage. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are arranged in racemes, often resembling a crown. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous tiny leaflets that give it a lacy appearance.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
