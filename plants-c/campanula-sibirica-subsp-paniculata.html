Campanula sibirica subsp. paniculata

Campanula sibirica subsp.

paniculata, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or alpine settings.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
