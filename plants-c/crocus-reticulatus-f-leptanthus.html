Crocus reticulatus f. leptanthus

Crocus reticulatus f. leptanthus, commonly known as the slender-flowered crocus, is a botanical variety native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, pointed petals and a distinctive, fragrant scent. The plant typically blooms in late winter or early spring, producing delicate flowers with a pale yellow to white coloration. It prefers well-drained, sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
