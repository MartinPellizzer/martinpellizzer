Crataegus vicinalis

Crataegus vicinalis, commonly known as the sand cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in open woodlands or along streambanks. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges, and it can reach heights of up to 6 meters.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is also used in ecological restoration projects.
