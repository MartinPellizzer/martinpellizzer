Callicarpa subaequalis

Callicarpa subaequalis, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of violet to purple berries that appear in late summer. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a fragrant, aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Callicarpa subaequalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
