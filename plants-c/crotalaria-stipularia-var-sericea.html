Crotalaria stipularia var. sericea

Crotalaria stipularia var. sericea, commonly known as the silky crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a hairy texture that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and has the ability to fix nitrogen through its root nodules.
