Curcuma pseudomontana

Curcuma pseudomontana, commonly known as the mountain turmeric, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces striking, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. Its flowers are typically orange or yellow, arranged in a spike-like inflorescence.

The rhizomes of Curcuma pseudomontana are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural pigments.
