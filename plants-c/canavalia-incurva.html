Canavalia incurva

Canavalia incurva, commonly known as the sickle bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with three leaflets and produces small, yellow flowers that develop into curved, pod-like fruits. The plant is characterized by its twining habit and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.

The seeds are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.
