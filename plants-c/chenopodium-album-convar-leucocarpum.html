Chenopodium album convar. leucocarpum

Chenopodium album convar. leucocarpum, commonly known as white goosefoot, is a variant of the common lambsquarters characterized by its white, fleshy fruits.

It is an annual herb native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa, often found in disturbed soils and agricultural fields. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a bluish-green hue. Its flowers are small and green, arranged in dense, terminal clusters.

The white, spongy fruits are adapted for wind dispersal and are a distinctive feature of this botanical variant.
