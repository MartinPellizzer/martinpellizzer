Crataegus × albanica

Crataegus × albanica, commonly known as the Albanian hawthorn, is a hybrid species within the genus Crataegus, belonging to the family Rosaceae.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, and is native to the Balkan region. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with serrated margins, and its bark is smooth and grayish in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
