Coryphantha conspicua

Coryphantha conspicua, commonly known as the showy coryphantha, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often covered in white or pale blue spines. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suited for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it requires careful management to prevent overgrowth.
