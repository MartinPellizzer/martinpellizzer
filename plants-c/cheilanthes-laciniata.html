Cheilanthes laciniata

Cheilanthes laciniata, commonly known as the fringed rock fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family.

It is a perennial, evergreen fern that typically grows in rocky or crevice habitats. The plant features a short, rhizomatous stem with clustered, leathery, and often lobed fronds. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a distinctive fringed appearance along the edges.

This species is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in regions with limestone or sandy soils.
