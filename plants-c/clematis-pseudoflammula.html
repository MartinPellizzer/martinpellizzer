Clematis pseudoflammula

Clematis pseudoflammula, commonly known as the lesser flame vine, is a deciduous climbing vine native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant produces small, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three ovate to lanceolate leaflets.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
