Calamagrostis aemula var. plebeia

Calamagrostis aemula var. plebeia, commonly known as the smooth bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces feathery, silver-gray inflorescences that bloom in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
