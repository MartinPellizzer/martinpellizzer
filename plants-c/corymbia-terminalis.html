Corymbia terminalis

Corymbia terminalis, commonly known as the red gum, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Australia, where it thrives in a variety of soil types. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, featuring smooth, flaky bark that peels off in strips. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy green upper surface and a pale underside.

The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers that attract nectar-feeding birds and insects.
