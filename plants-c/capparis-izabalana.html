Capparis izabalana

Capparis izabalana, commonly known as the Izabal caper, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are typically pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is adapted to grow in tropical and subtropical environments.
