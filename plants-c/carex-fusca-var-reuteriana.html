Carex fusca var. reuteriana

Carex fusca var. reuteriana, commonly known as the dark sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. This variety is typically found in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
