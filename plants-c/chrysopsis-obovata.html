Chrysopsis obovata

Chrysopsis obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate goldenweed, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features lanceolate to obovate leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive grayish-green color. Its yellow flowers bloom in late summer, producing a showy display that attracts pollinators.

Chrysopsis obovata typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.
