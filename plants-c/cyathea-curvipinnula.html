Cyathea curvipinnula

Cyathea curvipinnula, commonly known as the narrow-pinnate tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, tropical environments, often in shaded, moist forest understories. Its fronds are bi-directionally pinnate, with a distinctive narrowing towards the tips.

Cyathea curvipinnula plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, contributing to biodiversity and soil stabilization.
