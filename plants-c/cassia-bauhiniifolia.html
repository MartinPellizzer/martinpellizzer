Cassia bauhiniifolia

Cassia bauhiniifolia, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, narrow leaflets that give it a feathery appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Cassia bauhiniifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
