Calibanus caespitosus

Calibanus caespitosus, commonly known as the hairy calibanus, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in dense clusters, and it produces achenes as its seed form.

Calibanus caespitosus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
