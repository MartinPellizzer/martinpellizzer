Cyphostemma dysocarpum

Cyphostemma dysocarpum, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a distinctive, fleshy capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Cyphostemma dysocarpum is often found in rocky or wooded areas and is adapted to arid or semi-arid conditions.
