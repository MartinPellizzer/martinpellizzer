Ceanothus divaricatus var. grosseserratus

Ceanothus divaricatus var. grosseserratus, commonly known as the Sierra ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are typically narrow, lance-shaped, and have prominent, coarse teeth along the edges. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

It plays an important role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
