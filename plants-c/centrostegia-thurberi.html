Centrostegia thurberi

Centrostegia thurberi, commonly known as the Thurber's centrostegia, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are often subtended by bracts, and its leaves are typically lance-shaped with a smooth margin. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils and along streambanks.

The species is noted for its ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for various small animals.
