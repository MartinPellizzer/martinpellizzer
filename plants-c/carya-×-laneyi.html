Carya × laneyi

Carya × laneyi, commonly known as the hybrid hickory, is a cross between Carya ovalis and Carya tomentosa.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in the southeastern United States. The plant features compound leaves with alternate arrangement and small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same tree.

The fruit is a hard-shelled nut enclosed in a spiny husk, typical of the hickory genus.
