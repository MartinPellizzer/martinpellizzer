Capurodendron tampinense

Capurodendron tampinense, commonly known as the Tampinense tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant features broad, leathery leaves that are arranged alternately along its branches. Its flowers are small and clustered, often white or pale yellow, and it produces woody capsules as its fruit.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is sometimes used in local crafts and construction.
