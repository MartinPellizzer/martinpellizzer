Chlidanthus marginatus

Chlidanthus marginatus, commonly known as the marginated cactus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of vibrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sun, exhibiting drought tolerance due to its succulent nature. Its flowers bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
