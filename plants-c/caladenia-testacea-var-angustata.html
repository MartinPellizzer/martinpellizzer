Caladenia testacea var. angustata

Caladenia testacea var. angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spider-orchid, is a species of orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spider-like flowers that are typically pale yellow to cream in color. The plant produces a single flower per stem, which is adorned with intricate patterns and a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

This orchid is known for its ephemeral flowering, often blooming for only a few weeks during the cooler months.
