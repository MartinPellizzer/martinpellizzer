Cardamomum officinale

Cardamomum officinale, commonly known as true cardamom, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of southern India.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, dark green leaves and clusters of small, dark red flowers. The plant produces seed pods that contain the aromatic seeds used in cooking and traditional medicine. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like cineole and terpinene, contribute to its distinctive flavor and therapeutic properties.

Cardamomum officinale thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its valuable spice.
