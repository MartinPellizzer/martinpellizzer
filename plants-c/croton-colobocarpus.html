Croton colobocarpus

Croton colobocarpus, commonly known as the African croton, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers followed by distinctive, three-lobed, orange-red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or savanna habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
