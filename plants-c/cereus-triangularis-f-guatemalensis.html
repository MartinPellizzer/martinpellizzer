Cereus triangularis f. guatemalensis

Cereus triangularis f. guatemalensis, commonly known as the Guatemalan torch cactus, is a rare and striking cactus species native to Central America.

It features a tall, columnar stem with a distinct triangular cross-section, which is a notable botanical characteristic. The plant produces large, showy white flowers that open at night, a trait typical of many nocturnal blooming cacti. It has spiny areoles and is adapted to arid environments, making it a resilient plant.

Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a sought-after specimen among cactus enthusiasts.
