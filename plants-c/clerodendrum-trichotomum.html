Clerodendrum trichotomum

Clerodendrum trichotomum, commonly known as the three-branched flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are often hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses.
