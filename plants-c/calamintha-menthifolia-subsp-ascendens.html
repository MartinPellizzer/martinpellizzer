Calamintha menthifolia subsp. ascendens

Calamintha menthifolia subsp.

ascendens, commonly known as mountain calamintha, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments.

The plant features aromatic, narrow leaves and produces small, pale blue or violet flowers in late summer. Its foliage is often used in herbal teas and traditional remedies due to its menthol-like scent.

This subspecies is noted for its compact growth habit and adaptability to high-altitude conditions.
