Crotalaria cyanoxantha

Crotalaria cyanoxantha, commonly known as the blue crown crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive blue-violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it may spread aggressively.
