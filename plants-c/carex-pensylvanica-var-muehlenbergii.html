Carex pensylvanica var. muehlenbergii

Carex pensylvanica var. muehlenbergii, commonly known as Muhlenberg's sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its triangular stems and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are wind-pollinated.
