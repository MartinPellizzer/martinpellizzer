Carex thailandica var. brevirostris

Carex thailandica var. brevirostris, commonly known as the short-beaked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with the inflorescence consisting of a spike of small, three-lobed flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
