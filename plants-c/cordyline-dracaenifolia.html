Cordyline dracaenifolia

Cordyline dracaenifolia, commonly known as the New Zealand flax, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in a spike at the top of the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cordyline dracaenifolia is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking foliage and hardy nature.
