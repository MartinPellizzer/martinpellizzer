Cardamomum tomentosum

Cardamomum tomentosum, commonly known as the woolly cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green foliage and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces aromatic, reddish-brown seeds that are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often cultivated for its unique fragrance.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and pharmacology.
