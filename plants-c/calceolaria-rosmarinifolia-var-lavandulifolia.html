Calceolaria rosmarinifolia var. lavandulifolia

Calceolaria rosmarinifolia var. lavandulifolia, commonly known as the lavender-leaved calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It features striking, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often resembling small bells. The plant has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that emit a subtle lavender scent, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its unique appearance and fragrance make it a valuable addition to both garden landscapes and botanical collections.
