Centaurea sventenii

Centaurea sventenii, commonly known as the Balkan cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and mountain slopes. It produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Centaurea sventenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional medicinal uses in local herbal practices.
