Carex inopinata

Carex inopinata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and wet meadows. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Carex inopinata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
