Cyperus digitatus var. mexicanus

Cyperus digitatus var. mexicanus, commonly known as the Mexican flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, which are often wind-pollinated.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.
