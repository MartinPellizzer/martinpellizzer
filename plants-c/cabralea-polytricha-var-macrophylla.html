Cabralea polytricha var. macrophylla

Cabralea polytricha var. macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf cabralea, is a species native to the rainforests of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, woody fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is noted for its adaptability to humid, shaded environments and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
