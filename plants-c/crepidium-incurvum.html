Crepidium incurvum

Crepidium incurvum, commonly known as the hook orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and the Himalayas.

This terrestrial orchid typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are small and often white or pale green, with a unique structure that includes a spurred lip. The plant has a short, rhizomatous stem and produces oval, leathery leaves.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
