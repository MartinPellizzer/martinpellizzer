Cytisus ciliatus

Cytisus ciliatus, commonly known as the silky broom, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, wiry stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are small, pinnately compound, and have a feathery texture.

Cytisus ciliatus is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for ornamental gardening.
