Chondrocarpus

Chondrocarpus, commonly known as the "hedgehog grass," is a genus of flowering plants in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive, spiky inflorescence that resembles a hedgehog. The plants are typically found in wet or marshy environments, thriving in temperate regions of the Southern Hemisphere. Chondrocarpus species have small, spikelet-based flowers with two to three bracts, and their seeds are often dispersed by water.

These plants play an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.
