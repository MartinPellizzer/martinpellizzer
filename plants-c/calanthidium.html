Calanthidium

Calanthidium, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a small annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features delicate yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, typically from June to September. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Calanthidium grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, cheerful blossoms.
