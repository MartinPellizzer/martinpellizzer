Crataegus pentasperma

Crataegus pentasperma, commonly known as the five-seeded hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a sour taste. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental value.

Crataegus pentasperma is valued in traditional medicine for its potential cardiovascular benefits.
