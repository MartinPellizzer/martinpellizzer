Carex songorica

Carex songorica, commonly known as the Borneo sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This species typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its grass-like leaves are narrow and blade-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are often inconspicuous.

Carex songorica plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
