Cnicus lilacinus

Cnicus lilacinus, commonly known as the lilac evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the evening primrose family (Onagraceae) and is characterized by its showy, violet-colored flowers that open in the late afternoon. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Cnicus lilacinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to bloom during the evening hours.
