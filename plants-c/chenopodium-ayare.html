Chenopodium ayare

Chenopodium ayare, commonly known as wild spinach, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are edible and have been traditionally used in various cuisines for their mild, spinach-like flavor.

Chenopodium ayare is also valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of vitamins and minerals.
