Cercidiphyllum magnificum f. pendulum

Cercidiphyllum magnificum f. pendulum, commonly known as the weeping magnolia, is a unique cultivar of the magnolia tree characterized by its cascading, pendulous branches.

This ornamental tree features large, heart-shaped leaves that are a deep green during the growing season. In spring, it produces showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom before the leaves emerge. The plant is native to eastern Asia and is often cultivated for its striking visual appeal in gardens and landscapes.

It thrives in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8, preferring moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
