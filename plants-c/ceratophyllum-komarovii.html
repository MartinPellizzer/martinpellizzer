Ceratophyllum komarovii

Ceratophyllum komarovii, commonly known as the komarovii waterwheel, is a unique aquatic plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in ponds and lakes. Its leaves are opposite and oval-shaped, and it lacks true roots, relying instead on its stems for anchorage.

Ceratophyllum komarovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide habitat for aquatic life.
