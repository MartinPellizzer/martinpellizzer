Charianthus obliquus

Charianthus obliquus, commonly known as the bent-flower dandelion, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, similar to other members of the dandelion genus.

It is a herbaceous perennial that is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
