Croton strobiliformis

Croton strobiliformis, commonly known as the African croton, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a distinctive reddish or purple underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, spiny capsules containing seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and as a landscape plant in suitable climates.
