Calamus sulawesiensis

Calamus sulawesiensis, commonly known as the Sulawesi canes, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to the Indonesian island of Sulawesi and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, paddle-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking inflorescences with a spadix and a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some horticultural settings.
