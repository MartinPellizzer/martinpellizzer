Caprifolium tanguticum

Caprifolium tanguticum, commonly known as the Tangut honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
