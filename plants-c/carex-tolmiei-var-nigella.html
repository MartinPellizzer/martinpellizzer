Carex tolmiei var. nigella

Carex tolmiei var. nigella, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and dense, grass-like growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other moist habitats.

Black sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
