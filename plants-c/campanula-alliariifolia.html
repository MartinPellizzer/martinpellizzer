Campanula alliariifolia

Campanula alliariifolia, commonly known as the garlic chive bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, garlic-scented leaves and clusters of bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.

Campanula alliariifolia is also valued for its culinary uses, particularly in Mediterranean cuisine.
