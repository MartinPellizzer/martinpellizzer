Corynopuntia vilis var. bernhardinii

Corynopuntia vilis var. bernhardinii, commonly known as the Bernhardinii cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is a slow-growing, drought-tolerant plant that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.

Its spiny structure provides protection from herbivores, and it plays a role in supporting local wildlife by providing nectar for pollinators.
