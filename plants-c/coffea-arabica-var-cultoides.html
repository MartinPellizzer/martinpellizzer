Coffea arabica var. cultoides

Coffea arabica var. cultoides, commonly known as the Ethiopian coffee plant, is a species native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its evergreen shrub form, with dark green, leathery leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces red, fleshy berries containing two seeds, which are the coffee beans used for brewing. It thrives in tropical climates with high altitudes and consistent rainfall.

This variety is valued for its aromatic and complex flavor profile in specialty coffee.
