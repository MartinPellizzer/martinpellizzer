Carduus parviflorus

Carduus parviflorus, commonly known as the small-flowered thistle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, hairy leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine, stiff hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a spherical shape.

The plant is often found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.
