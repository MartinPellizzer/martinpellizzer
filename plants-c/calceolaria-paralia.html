Calceolaria paralia

Calceolaria paralia, commonly known as the beach calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like flowers that resemble small cups, which are a key feature of the genus Calceolaria. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand saline and windy conditions typical of coastal environments. Its blooming period typically occurs during the summer months, producing vibrant purple or pink flowers.

Calceolaria paralia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
