Crocodilium

Crocodilium, commonly known as the crocodile plant, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and waxy, elongated leaves that resemble the scales of a crocodile. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often hidden among its foliage. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Despite its unusual appearance, it is a relatively low-maintenance plant suitable for indoor cultivation.
