Centaurea nigriceps

Centaurea nigriceps, commonly known as the black-headed centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking dark purple to black flower heads, which contrast with its silver-green foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, spiny leaves. It blooms from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea nigriceps is often found in rocky soils and open habitats, thriving in full sun.
