Cylindropuntia brittonii

Cylindropuntia brittonii, commonly known as the Britton's cholla, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, segmented stems that are covered in small, spiny tubercles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and desert landscapes.

Due to its adaptations, it plays a vital role in desert ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
