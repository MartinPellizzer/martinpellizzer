Crotalaria huillensis subsp. zambesiaca

Crotalaria huillensis subsp.

zambesiaca, commonly known as the Zambesi crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and palmate leaves.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and savannas, thriving in well-drained soils. It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein in some regions.
