Campanula magellensis

Campanula magellensis, commonly known as the Magellanic bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, hairy coating.

Campanula magellensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
