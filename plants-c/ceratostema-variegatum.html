Ceratostema variegatum

Ceratostema variegatum, commonly known as the variegated bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to China.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a variegated pattern of green and white. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
