Cimicifuga austrokoreana

Cimicifuga austrokoreana, commonly known as Korean bugbane, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces compound leaves with serrated edges, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable addition to woodland gardens.

Cimicifuga austrokoreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical medicinal uses.
