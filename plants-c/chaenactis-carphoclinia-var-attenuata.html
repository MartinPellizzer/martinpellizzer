Chaenactis carphoclinia var. attenuata

Chaenactis carphoclinia var. attenuata, commonly known as the slender plainsweed, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and typically white or pale yellow.

This variety is distinguished by its elongated, attenuated leaves and is adapted to arid environments.
