Castalia stellata

Castalia stellata, commonly known as the star flower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped blossoms, which are typically white or pale blue. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is adapted to dry climates.

Castalia stellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
