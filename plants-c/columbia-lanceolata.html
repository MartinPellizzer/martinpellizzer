Columbia lanceolata

Columbia lanceolata, commonly known as the Pacific silver fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, conical shape and needle-like leaves that are blue-green in color. The tree produces cones that mature in the fall, containing seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-elevation forest ecosystems.
