Capparis doniana

Capparis doniana, commonly known as the African sour fig, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its thorny stems and evergreen leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are sour in taste. Its botanical features include a strong, aromatic odor and the ability to thrive in poor soils, making it adaptable to various environments.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
