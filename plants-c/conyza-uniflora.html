Conyza uniflora

Conyza uniflora, commonly known as the common blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields. The plant has small, blue to purple flower heads that are clustered in a single bloom, giving it its species name "uniflora.". Its leaves are hairy and have a distinctive lobed shape, while the stem is usually branched and covered in fine hairs.

Conyza uniflora is often considered a weed but has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
