Carex uncifolia

Carex uncifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-green leaves that are typically arranged in three rows. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically found in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, bogs, and the edges of ponds.

Carex uncifolia is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse ecosystems.
