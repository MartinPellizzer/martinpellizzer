Crataegus modica

Crataegus modica, commonly known as the Modica hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in parts of Italy and Sicily. The plant is characterized by its small, thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruits are small, red berries that ripen in the autumn and are edible, though often used in traditional herbal remedies.

The Modica hawthorn is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
