Croton bondaensis

Croton bondaensis, commonly known as the Bonda croton, is a shrub native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, with leaves that display a range of hues including red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
