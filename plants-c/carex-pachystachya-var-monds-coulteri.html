Carex pachystachya var. monds-coulteri

Carex pachystachya var. monds-coulteri, commonly known as Monds- Coulter's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces tall, cylindrical spikelets that bear small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green and glossy.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
