Cirsium handelii

Cirsium handelii, commonly known as the Handel's thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring rough, spiny leaves with a silvery-blue underside. Its flowers are usually purple or pink, appearing in late summer to early autumn.

Cirsium handelii is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing a robust root system and drought tolerance.
