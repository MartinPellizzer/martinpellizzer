Ceropegia verrucosa

Ceropegia verrucosa, commonly known as the velvet leaf or string of hearts, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a trailing plant with succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are typically green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are often pale green or white, with a distinctive shape that resembles a tiny cup. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia verrucosa is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
