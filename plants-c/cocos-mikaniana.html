Cocos mikaniana

Cocos mikaniana, commonly known as the Mexican coconut or the red coconut, is a flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to southern Mexico and parts of Central America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces a unique red or orange fruit that contains a single large seed, distinguishing it from other coconut species. Its leaves are palmate, with long, narrow segments, and it can grow up to 10 meters in height.
