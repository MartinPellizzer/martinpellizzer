Cerasus pumila var. besseyi

Cerasus pumila var. besseyi, commonly known as the Bessey cherry, is a deciduous shrub native to the United States, particularly in the Great Plains region.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red, sweet-tart berries in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as prairies and woodland edges.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to support local wildlife.
