Calceolaria glabrata

Calceolaria glabrata, commonly known as the naked slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, thriving in well-drained soils. Calceolaria glabrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking floral display.
