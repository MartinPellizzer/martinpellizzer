Cornus stricta

Cornus stricta, commonly known as red osier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking red stems that become more vibrant in the winter months. The plant produces small white flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated edge and turn yellow in the autumn.

Cornus stricta is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
