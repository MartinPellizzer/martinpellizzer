Cuphea lysimachioides

Cuphea lysimachioides, commonly known as the red flame bush, is a shrub native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking red, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with slightly serrated edges, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.

The species is also known for its ability to grow in a variety of soil types, making it a versatile addition to gardens.
