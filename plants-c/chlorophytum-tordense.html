Chlorophytum tordense

Chlorophytum tordense, commonly known as the Tordenskiold's spider plant, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically narrow and grass-like. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers in spikes during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
