Convolvulus arvensis f. quinquevulnerus

Convolvulus arvensis f. quinquevulnerus, commonly known as the five-wound field bindweed, is a variant of the common field bindweed.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant is known for its aggressive growth habit and is often considered a weed in agricultural settings. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in various regions across Europe and Asia.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and challenging to control in cultivated areas.
