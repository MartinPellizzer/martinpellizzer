Calamagrostis spruceana

Calamagrostis spruceana, commonly known as Spruce reedgrass, is a perennial grass species native to northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often gray-green in color. The plant produces feathery, panicle-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding visual interest to gardens and natural landscapes. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other aquatic habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is a popular choice for wetland restoration and landscape design.
