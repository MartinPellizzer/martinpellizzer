Camellia pubescens

Camellia pubescens, commonly known as the hairy camellia, is a flowering plant native to southern China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Camellia pubescens is also valued for its potential in traditional medicine and horticultural applications.
