Crepis froelichiana subsp. dinarica

Crepis froelichiana subsp.

dinarica, commonly known as the Dinaric crested wheat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its rosette growth habit and distinctive flowering heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and covered with fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with ray florets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
