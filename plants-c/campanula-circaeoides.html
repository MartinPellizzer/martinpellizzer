Campanula circaeoides

Campanula circaeoides, commonly known as the mountain bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with hairy stems and opposite, ovate leaves. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Campanula circaeoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
