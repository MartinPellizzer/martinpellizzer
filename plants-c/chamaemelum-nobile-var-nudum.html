Chamaemelum nobile var. nudum

Chamaemelum nobile var. nudum, commonly known as the naked yarrow, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in flat-topped clusters. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and covered in a powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its historical use in herbal medicine and as a natural dye adds to its botanical significance.
