Crambe edentula var. glabrata

Crambe edentula var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth bladder nut, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces distinctive, inflated, green pods that contain seeds used for oil extraction. Its leaves are alternate and pinnately divided, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for both its aesthetic value and agricultural uses.
