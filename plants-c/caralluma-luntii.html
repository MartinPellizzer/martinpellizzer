Caralluma luntii

Caralluma luntii, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of India and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes. The plant features thick, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. Its small, star-shaped flowers are typically white or pale blue, and it produces small, toxic berries.

Caralluma luntii is adapted to extreme environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
