Cyanandrium rufum

Cyanandrium rufum, commonly known as the red-stemmed orchid, is a unique species within the orchid family.

It is characterized by its striking red stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically a mix of white and pink, blooming in late spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
