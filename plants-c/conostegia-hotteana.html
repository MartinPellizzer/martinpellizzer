Conostegia hotteana

Conostegia hotteana, commonly known as the Guianan waxtree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in humid environments. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that are often clustered together, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.

The tree plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
