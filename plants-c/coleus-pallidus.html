Coleus pallidus

Coleus pallidus, commonly known as the pale coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale pink or white, arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage.

Coleus pallidus prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, low-maintenance plants.
