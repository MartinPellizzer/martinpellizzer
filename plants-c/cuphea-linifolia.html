Cuphea linifolia

Cuphea linifolia, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial, depending on the climate, and thrives in well-drained soil. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Cuphea linifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
