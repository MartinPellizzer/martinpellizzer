Cassinia albida var. canescens

Cassinia albida var. canescens, commonly known as the silver corymbia, is a species native to Australia.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its white, fluffy flower heads that appear in clusters. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
