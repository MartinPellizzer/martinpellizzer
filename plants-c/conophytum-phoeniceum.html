Conophytum phoeniceum

Conophytum phoeniceum, commonly known as the "Firecracker" or "Cape firecracker," is a succulent native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its compact, fleshy, and often spiny stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, erect stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Conophytum phoeniceum is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
