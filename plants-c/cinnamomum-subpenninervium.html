Cinnamomum subpenninervium

Cinnamomum subpenninervium, commonly known as the narrow-veined cinnamon tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a spice, similar to cinnamon.

The tree typically grows as a small to medium-sized evergreen with a dense canopy and smooth, greyish bark.
