Convolvulus oppositifolius

Convolvulus oppositifolius, commonly known as the opposite-leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its twining stems and heart-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in arid and semi-arid regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine in some regions.
