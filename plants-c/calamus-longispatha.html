Calamus longispatha

Calamus longispatha, commonly known as the long-spined calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizome and large, arrow-shaped leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family.

Calamus longispatha is often found in wetlands and along the edges of freshwater habitats.
