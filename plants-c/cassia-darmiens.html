Cassia darmiens

Cassia darmiens, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets and produces long, slender pods containing flat, oval seeds. Cassia darmiens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
