Convolvulus riedlei

Convolvulus riedlei, commonly known as the Riedl's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Convolvulus riedlei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
