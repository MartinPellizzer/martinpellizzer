Carex arakiana

Carex arakiana, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. It has grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and arranged in a dense rosette.

Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it produces triangular, three-sided fruits.
