Caulotretus smilacinus

Caulotretus smilacinus, commonly known as the smilacine climbing fern, is a tropical fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and the presence of adventitious roots along its stems. The fronds are usually compound, with a feathery appearance and a glossy surface. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant has a distinctive reproductive structure with sori arranged in lines on the underside of the fronds.
