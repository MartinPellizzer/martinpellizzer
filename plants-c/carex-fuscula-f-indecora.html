Carex fuscula f. indecora

Carex fuscula f. indecora, commonly known as the showy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and unique appearance.
