Cobaea gracilis

Cobaea gracilis, commonly known as the slender cohosh, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in partial shade with moist, well-drained soil. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces berries that turn red when mature.

Cobaea gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
