Campanula incanescens var. kermanica

Campanula incanescens var. kermanica, commonly known as the Kerman skyblue, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically hairy and grayish-green, contributing to its overall appearance of a hardy, alpine plant. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
