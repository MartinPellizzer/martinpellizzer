Carex pilulifera var. pallescens

Carex pilulifera var. pallescens, commonly known as the pale sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and wet meadows.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
