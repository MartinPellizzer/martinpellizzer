Cryptocarya kurzii var. argentea

Cryptocarya kurzii var. argentea, commonly known as silver cryptocarya, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, leathery leaves that are covered in a dense layer of silvery-white, glandular hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbels, typically appearing in spring. The fruit is a small, spherical drupe that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures.

This plant is often found in sclerophyllous forests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
