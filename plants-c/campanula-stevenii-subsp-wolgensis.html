Campanula stevenii subsp. wolgensis

Campanula stevenii subsp.

wolgensis, commonly known as the Wolgensis bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes at high altitudes.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
