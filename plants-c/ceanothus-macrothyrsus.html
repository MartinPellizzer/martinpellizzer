Ceanothus macrothyrsus

Ceanothus macrothyrsus, commonly known as the silverleaf ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats. It has small, ovate leaves with a leathery texture and is known for its aromatic scent.

Ceanothus macrothyrsus plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.
