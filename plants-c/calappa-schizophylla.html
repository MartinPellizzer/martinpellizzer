Calappa schizophylla

Calappa schizophylla, commonly known as the split-leaf calappa, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
