Canavalia raiateensis

Canavalia raiateensis, commonly known as the Tahitian bean or Pua kahua, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a climbing legume that produces distinctive, showy pink or purple flowers. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and is often found in coastal and disturbed habitats. It is valued for its edible seeds and traditional use in Polynesian cuisine.

Canavalia raiateensis also plays a role in soil nitrogen fixation due to its association with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
