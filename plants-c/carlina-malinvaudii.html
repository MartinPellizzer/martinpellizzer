Carlina malinvaudii

Carlina malinvaudii, commonly known as the Malinvaud's aster, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant features a sturdy, hairy stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its showy purple to blue flower heads bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Carlina malinvaudii is adapted to arid and rocky environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
