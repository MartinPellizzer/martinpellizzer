Chimonocalamus lushaiensis

Chimonocalamus lushaiensis, commonly known as the Lushai bamboo, is a species of grass belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to the eastern Himalayas and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves with a smooth surface and a dense, fibrous sheath at the base. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with high humidity.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
