Carex nigromarginata

Carex nigromarginata, commonly known as black-margin sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically dark green with a distinctive black or dark brown margin. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex nigromarginata plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
