Colchicum glossophyllum

Colchicum glossophyllum, commonly known as the Mexican meadow saffron, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces a tall stem with narrow, grass-like leaves and features distinctive, pale yellow to orange flowers with red markings. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
