Clematis × vernalis

Clematis × vernalis, commonly known as the spring clematis, is a deciduous vine native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, usually blooming in late spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis × vernalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a climbing plant.
