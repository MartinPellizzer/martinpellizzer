Centaurea sordida

Centaurea sordida, commonly known as the sordid cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, bristly leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and disturbed areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

Centaurea sordida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
