Carex hamosa

Carex hamosa, commonly known as narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces triangular, three-sided seeds.

Carex hamosa is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
