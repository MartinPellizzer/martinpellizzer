Comparettia granizoi

Comparettia granizoi, commonly known as the granizoi cactus, is a unique species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stem, which is covered in small, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. Its leaves are reduced to spines, a typical adaptation for water conservation in its harsh environment.

Comparettia granizoi is a slow-growing plant that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.
