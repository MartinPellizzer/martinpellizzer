Cyperus microlepis

Cyperus microlepis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are often green or brown in color. Cyperus microlepis is characterized by its short, compact stature and its ability to thrive in saturated soil conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
