Citrosma alternifolia

Citrosma alternifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved citrosma, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its alternate, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by round, orange-colored fruits. The leaves and fruits are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and pharmacological study.
