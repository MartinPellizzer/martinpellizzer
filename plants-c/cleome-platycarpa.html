Cleome platycarpa

Cleome platycarpa, commonly known as the yellow cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cleome platycarpa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and ease of growth.

It prefers full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.
