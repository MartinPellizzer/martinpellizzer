Cyperus submaculatus

Cyperus submaculatus, commonly known as the spotted flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus submaculatus is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.
