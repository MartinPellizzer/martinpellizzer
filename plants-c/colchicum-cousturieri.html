Colchicum cousturieri

Colchicum cousturieri, commonly known as the Armenian colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It belongs to the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in late summer. The plant features basal leaves that are broad and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are usually purple or pink in color. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
