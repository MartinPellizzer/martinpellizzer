Celmisia haastii var. haastii

Celmisia haastii var. haastii, commonly known as Haast's celmisia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often hairy leaves and showy yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rock crevices. It has a compact growth habit with a rosette of leaves forming a dense, low-growing structure.

The species is named in honor of the renowned New Zealand explorer and naturalist, Julius von Haast.
