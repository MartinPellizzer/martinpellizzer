Calceolaria andicola

Calceolaria andicola, commonly known as the Andean slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is characterized by its unique slipper-shaped flowers, which are typically violet or purple in color. The plant produces delicate, spurred petals that are adapted for pollination by specific insects. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments with well-drained soil.

Calceolaria andicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
