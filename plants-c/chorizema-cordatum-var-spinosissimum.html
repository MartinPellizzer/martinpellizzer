Chorizema cordatum var. spinosissimum

Chorizema cordatum var. spinosissimum, commonly known as the spiny heartleaf, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in small spines, providing a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
