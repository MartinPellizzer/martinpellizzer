Chrysostemma calendulaceum

Chrysostemma calendulaceum, commonly known as the golden cupplant, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small, clustered flowers. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to xeriscaped gardens.
