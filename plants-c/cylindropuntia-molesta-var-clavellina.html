Cylindropuntia molesta var. clavellina

Cylindropuntia molesta var. clavellina, commonly known as the Clavellina cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a spineless, columnar cactus with a distinctive, ribbed stem that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it a notable species in desert ecosystems.
