Centaurea astrachanica

Centaurea astrachanica, commonly known as the Astrachan thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

Centaurea astrachanica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
