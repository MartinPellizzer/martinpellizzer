Calophyllum rivulare

Calophyllum rivulare, commonly known as the river calophyllum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Calophyllum rivulare is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and ornamental qualities.
