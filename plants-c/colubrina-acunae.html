Colubrina acunae

Colubrina acunae, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces clusters of small, violet to purple berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Colubrina acunae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitats.
