Calamus nambariensis subsp. arunachalensis

Calamus nambariensis subsp.

arunachalensis, commonly known as the Arunachal bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arales. It is a tall, slender grass-like plant that forms dense clumps, with long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, typical of the genus Calamus. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in the understorey of tropical and subtropical forests.

Its rhizomes are sometimes harvested for their aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.
