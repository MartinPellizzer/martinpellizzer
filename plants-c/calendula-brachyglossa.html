Calendula brachyglossa

Calendula brachyglossa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved marigold, is a herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late summer and early fall. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Calendula brachyglossa is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic qualities.
