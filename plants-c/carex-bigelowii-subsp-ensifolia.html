Carex bigelowii subsp. ensifolia

Carex bigelowii subsp.

ensifolia, commonly known as Bigelow's sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
