Calamus erectus var. schizospathus

Calamus erectus var. schizospathus, commonly known as the fragrant calamus, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has distinctive, fragrant, yellowish flowers. It thrives in wet, marshy environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and for their aromatic properties.
