Cuphea tuberosa

Cuphea tuberosa, commonly known as the wax plant or Indian chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and purple. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Cuphea tuberosa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
