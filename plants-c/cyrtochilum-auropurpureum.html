Cyrtochilum auropurpureum

Cyrtochilum auropurpureum, commonly known as the golden purple orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces showy, purple and yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it thrives in shaded, humid environments.

Cyrtochilum auropurpureum is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
