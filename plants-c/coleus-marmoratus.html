Coleus marmoratus

Coleus marmoratus, commonly known as the marble plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display shades of green, white, and purple. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, adding a lush, ornamental quality to the plant. It produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, usually in shades of violet or white, which bloom in the summer months.

This plant thrives in bright, indirect light and is often used as a decorative foliage plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
