Calea platylepis

Calea platylepis, commonly known as the broad-leaved calea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow flowers. The plant features large, broad leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Calea platylepis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
