Cremosperma rotundatum

Cremosperma rotundatum, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spherical, blue or purple flower heads, which are composed of many small, spike-like florets. The plant has a basal rosette of gray-green, hairy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It produces a capsule fruit that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Cremosperma rotundatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
