Careya macrostachya

Careya macrostachya, commonly known as the giant pandanus or mountain pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a large, evergreen shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant features long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern at the base. Its inflorescence consists of a branched cluster of yellow flowers, which are followed by large, spiky fruits.

The plant is adapted to coastal and wetland environments, and it plays an important role in local ecosystems.
