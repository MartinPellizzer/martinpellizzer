Cereus bridgesii f. lageniformis

Cereus bridgesii f. lageniformis, commonly known as the balloon cactus, is a unique hybrid species within the Cactaceae family.

It is characterized by its swollen, balloon-like stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant typically grows in a compact, globular shape, with a thick, fleshy trunk that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, appearing at the base of the stem during the warmer months.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to adapt to dry, sunny conditions.
