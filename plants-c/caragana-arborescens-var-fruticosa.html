Caragana arborescens var. fruticosa

Caragana arborescens var. fruticosa, commonly known as the yellow shrub lupin, is a deciduous shrub native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and produces yellow to orange pea-like flowers in early summer. The plant is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions. It has a woody, multi-stemmed structure and can grow up to 2 meters in height.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
