Cyathea articulata

Cyathea articulata, commonly known as the segmented tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk composed of tightly packed, segmented rhizomes, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. Cyathea articulata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of tropical flora.
