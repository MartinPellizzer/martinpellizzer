Chamaefistula gatunensis

Chamaefistula gatunensis, commonly known as the Panama trumpet tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to Central America and is characterized by its distinctive trumpet-shaped yellow flowers. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with woody stems and small, elliptical leaves. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.

The flowers are primarily pollinated by birds, particularly hummingbirds, due to their shape and nectar-rich nature.
