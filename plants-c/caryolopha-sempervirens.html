Caryolopha sempervirens

Caryolopha sempervirens, commonly known as the evergreen shrub, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its ornate appearance. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its resilience to drought and salt spray makes it a popular choice in coastal areas.
