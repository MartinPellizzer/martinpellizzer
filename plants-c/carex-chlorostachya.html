Carex chlorostachya

Carex chlorostachya, commonly known as blue-stemmed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, often with a bluish or greenish hue, contributing to its common name.

Carex chlorostachya plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
