Clerodendrum leucobotrys

Clerodendrum leucobotrys, commonly known as the white-flowered clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to several meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, smooth seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
