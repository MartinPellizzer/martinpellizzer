Convolvulus frondosus

Convolvulus frondosus, commonly known as the fronded bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant is a vigorous climber, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It has a fibrous root system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Due to its aggressive growth, it is considered a weed in many regions.
