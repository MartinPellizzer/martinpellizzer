Cardamine enneaphyllos

Cardamine enneaphyllos, commonly known as the nine-leaf watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its small, delicate leaves and clusters of tiny white flowers. Native to moist, shaded environments in temperate regions, it thrives in cool, humid climates. The plant has a rosette growth form with basal leaves that are often kidney-shaped.

Its flowers are borne on slender stems and are pollinated by insects, contributing to its ecological role in supporting pollinator populations.
