Celastrus hispanicus

Celastrus hispanicus, commonly known as the Spanish staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in scrublands and rocky areas.

Celastrus hispanicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
