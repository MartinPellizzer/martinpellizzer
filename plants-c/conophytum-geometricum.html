Conophytum geometricum

Conophytum geometricum, commonly known as the geometric conophytum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its unique, geometric-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its striking appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
