Comastoma azureum

Comastoma azureum, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, delicate blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its flowers attract pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

Comastoma azureum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in native plant restoration projects.
