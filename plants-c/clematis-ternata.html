Clematis ternata

Clematis ternata, commonly known as the creeping Clematis, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and is often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped leaves that are typically green and slightly hairy.

Clematis ternata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
