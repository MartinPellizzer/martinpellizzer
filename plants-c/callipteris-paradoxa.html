Callipteris paradoxa

Callipteris paradoxa, commonly known as the paradoxa palmetto, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It features a dense, bushy growth habit with long, arching leaves that are typically a deep green color. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often used in ornamental landscaping due to their attractive appearance and hardiness.

Callipteris paradoxa is well adapted to coastal environments and can tolerate salt spray and sandy soils.
