Crataegus distincta

Crataegus distincta, commonly known as the distinct thornapple, is a species of hawthorn native to the United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 8 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins. Its flowers are white or pink, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, red berries that are edible but often considered astringent.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.
