Calamagrostis brachytricha

Calamagrostis brachytricha, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate floral display. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis brachytricha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting a variety of wildlife.
