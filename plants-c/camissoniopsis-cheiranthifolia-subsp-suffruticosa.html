Camissoniopsis cheiranthifolia subsp. suffruticosa

Camissoniopsis cheiranthifolia subsp.

suffruticosa, commonly known as the coastal buckwheat, is a shrub-like plant native to coastal regions of California. It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salt exposure.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.
