Clitoria ternatea var. alba

Clitoria ternatea var. alba, commonly known as the white butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale blue flowers that resemble butterflies, which bloom in clusters. The plant is a climbing vine with heart-shaped, trifoliate leaves and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

The flowers are not only aesthetically pleasing but also used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye.
