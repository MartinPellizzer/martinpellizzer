Catabrosa maritima

Catabrosa maritima, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers and silver-green foliage, which are adapted to withstand salty sea air and strong winds. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found along shorelines and dunes. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Catabrosa maritima is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
