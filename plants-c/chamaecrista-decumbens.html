Chamaecrista decumbens

Chamaecrista decumbens, commonly known as the prostrate crown flower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its prostrate or creeping growth habit and small, clustered yellow flowers. The plant features pinnate leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces seed pods that are often hairy and curved. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as roadsides and disturbed areas.

Chamaecrista decumbens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
