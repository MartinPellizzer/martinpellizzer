Coreopsis serrulata

Coreopsis serrulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring upright stems and daisy-like yellow flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and serrated, giving the plant its scientific name. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
