Corysanthes gibbifera

Corysanthes gibbifera, commonly known as the woolly candlewood, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of northern Australia.

It is characterized by its erect, woody stems and dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Corysanthes gibbifera thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

It is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that plays an important role in its native ecosystem.
