Cyperus asperrimus var. multiflorus

Cyperus asperrimus var. multiflorus, commonly known as the rough flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and wetlands. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in rough, bristly hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-sided spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a prominent midrib and a rough, sometimes hairy surface.
