Centaurea gienensis

Centaurea gienensis, commonly known as the Gienensian thistle, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Europe and is characterized by its striking purple to red flower heads and spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, typical of the daisy family.

Centaurea gienensis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
