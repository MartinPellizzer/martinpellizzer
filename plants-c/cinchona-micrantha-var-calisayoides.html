Cinchona micrantha var. calisayoides

Cinchona micrantha var. calisayoides, commonly known as the Calisayoides cinchona, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branched shrub form with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, followed by red or orange berries. It is valued for its alkaloid content, particularly quinine, which has historical significance in medicine.

This variety is also noted for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage and floral display.
