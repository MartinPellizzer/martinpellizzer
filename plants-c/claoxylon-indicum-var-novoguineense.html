Claoxylon indicum var. novoguineense

Claoxylon indicum var. novoguineense, commonly known as the New Guinea white wood, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of New Guinea and parts of Indonesia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture making.

This species also plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
