Castanola trinervis

Castanola trinervis, commonly known as the three-veined castanola, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves with three distinct veins. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in cyathia, typical of the Euphorbiaceae family. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Castanola trinervis is often found in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests.
