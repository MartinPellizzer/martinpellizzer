Carex paradoxa subsp. pseudoparadoxa

Carex paradoxa subsp.

pseudoparadoxa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.
