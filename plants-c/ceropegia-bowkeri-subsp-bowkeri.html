Ceropegia bowkeri subsp. bowkeri

Ceropegia bowkeri subsp.

bowkeri, commonly known as the African violet or velvet plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa. It features fleshy, tuberous roots and produces striking, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

The plant is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth. Ceropegia bowkeri subsp.

bowkeri is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
