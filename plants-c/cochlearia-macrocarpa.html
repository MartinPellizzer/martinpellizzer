Cochlearia macrocarpa

Cochlearia macrocarpa, commonly known as the broadleaf scurvygrass, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles and parts of northern Europe.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, preferring sandy or gravelly soils and full sun. Its leaves are basal, rosette-forming, and often have a distinctive waxy coating.

Cochlearia macrocarpa is known for its ability to thrive in harsh maritime environments.
