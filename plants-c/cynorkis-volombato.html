Cynorkis volombato

Cynorkis volombato, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Madagascar.

It is a terrestrial herb with a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its arid environment. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a complex structure typical of orchids, featuring three sepals and three petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires specific temperature and light conditions to bloom.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
