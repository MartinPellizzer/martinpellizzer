Conostylis aculeata subsp. robusta

Conostylis aculeata subsp.

robusta, commonly known as the stiff-flowered conostylis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southeastern Australia. It typically grows in coastal heathlands and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions.

The plant features linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered white or pale blue flowers. It is characterized by its robust growth habit and distinctive, stiffly upright flowering stems.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
