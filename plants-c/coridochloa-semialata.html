Coridochloa semialata

Coridochloa semialata, commonly known as the silverleaf grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, silver-gray blades that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet that is often obscured by the foliage.

Coridochloa semialata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
