Cirsium subtymphaeum

Cirsium subtymphaeum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with stiff, prickly hairs, while its purple flowers bloom in late summer.

Cirsium subtymphaeum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some historical uses in traditional medicine.
