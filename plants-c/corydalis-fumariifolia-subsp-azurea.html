Corydalis fumariifolia subsp. azurea

Corydalis fumariifolia subsp.

azurea, commonly known as the Azure Corydalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia. It features delicate, slender stems that often grow in a creeping or ascending manner.

The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers clustered in racemes, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically lanceolate and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to thrive in woodland or shaded garden settings.
