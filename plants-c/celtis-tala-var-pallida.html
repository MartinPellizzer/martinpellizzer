Celtis tala var. pallida

Celtis tala var. pallida, commonly known as the whitish hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, pale green leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by round, fuzzy fruits. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

The varietal name 'pallida' refers to its pale foliage, distinguishing it from other Celtis species.
