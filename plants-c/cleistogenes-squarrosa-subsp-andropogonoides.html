Cleistogenes squarrosa subsp. andropogonoides

Cleistogenes squarrosa subsp.

andropogonoides, commonly known as the silky bluestem, is a perennial grass native to the central and southern United States. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically green to purplish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in prairie and woodland habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in native plant landscapes.
