Cyrtopogon

Cyrtopogon, commonly known as the grass genus, includes a variety of flowering plants belonging to the Poaceae family.

These plants are typically herbaceous and are characterized by their narrow, grass-like leaves and spikelet-based inflorescences. Cyrtopogon species are often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. They play a role in ecological systems as forage for livestock and as habitat for various insects.

Some species are cultivated for ornamental purposes due to their attractive foliage and growth habits.
