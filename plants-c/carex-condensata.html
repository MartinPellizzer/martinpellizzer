Carex condensata

Carex condensata, commonly known as dense sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the genus Carex in the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex condensata is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
