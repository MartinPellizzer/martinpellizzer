Cyperus microglumis

Cyperus microglumis, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as swamps, pond margins, and seasonally flooded areas. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by water or wind.

Cyperus microglumis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wetland organisms.
