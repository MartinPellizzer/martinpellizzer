Cicerbita scoparia

Cicerbita scoparia, commonly known as the narrowleaf celandine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features alternate, narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters, blooming from late spring to early summer.

The plant is often found in disturbed areas and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
