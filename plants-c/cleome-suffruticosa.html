Cleome suffruticosa

Cleome suffruticosa, commonly known as the "spider flower," is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub-like plant that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers with prominent stamens that give it a distinctive, spider-like appearance. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms.

Cleome suffruticosa is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
