Commiphora orbicularis var. tulearensis

Commiphora orbicularis var. tulearensis, commonly known as the Madagascar gum tree, is a member of the Burseraceae family.

It is native to the island of Madagascar and is characterized by its distinctive orbicular (rounded) leaves. The plant produces a resinous sap that has been traditionally used for medicinal and aromatic purposes. It typically grows in dry, forested areas and can reach heights of up to 10 meters.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
