Cuphea ianthina

Cuphea ianthina, commonly known as the red flame plant, is a flowering shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-draining soil, making it suitable for both container and in-ground planting.

Cuphea ianthina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to add vibrant color to gardens.
