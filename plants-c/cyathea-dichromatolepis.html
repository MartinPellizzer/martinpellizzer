Cyathea dichromatolepis

Cyathea dichromatolepis, commonly known as the two-colored tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately divided fronds. The plant's leaves are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter green on the underside, giving it its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Cyathea dichromatolepis plays a role in maintaining forest biodiversity and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
