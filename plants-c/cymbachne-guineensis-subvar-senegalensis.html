Cymbachne guineensis subvar. senegalensis

Cymbachne guineensis subvar. senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese Guinea grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical Africa.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swampy areas and riverbanks. The plant has slender, erect stems and forms dense tussocks, with leaves that are narrow and grass-like. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed flowers.

It plays an important role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
