Cristaria heterophylla

Cristaria heterophylla, commonly known as the variable leaf water plantain, is a species of aquatic plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its ability to produce leaves of varying shapes and sizes depending on its environment, a trait known as heterophylly. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and wetlands. Its leaves are often narrow and ribbon-like, and it produces small, white flowers that bloom in the summer.

Cristaria heterophylla plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for various invertebrates and contributing to water filtration.
