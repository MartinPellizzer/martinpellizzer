Carex praecox var. sicyocarpa

Carex praecox var. sicyocarpa, commonly known as early sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. It has narrow, strap-shaped leaves that are usually light to dark green in color.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
