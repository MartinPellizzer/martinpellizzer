Cucumis sativus var. anatolicus

Cucumis sativus var. anatolicus, commonly known as the Anatolian cucumber, is a botanical variant of the common cucumber.

It is native to the Anatolian region of Turkey and is characterized by its elongated, thin, and often ribbed fruit. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and palmately lobed leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The fruit is typically harvested when immature, offering a crisp and mild flavor suitable for various culinary uses.
