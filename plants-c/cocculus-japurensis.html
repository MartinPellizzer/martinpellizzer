Cocculus japurensis

Cocculus japurensis, commonly known as the Brazilian devil's backbone, is a climbing vine native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, warty, and often spiny stems. The plant produces showy, pale yellow flowers followed by dark purple, fleshy berries. Its leaves are typically palmately lobed, with a rough, hairy texture.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
