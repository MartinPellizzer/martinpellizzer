Cocos aculeata

Cocos aculeata, commonly known as the spiny coconut or spiny date palm, is a flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, the Middle East, and southern Asia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are often covered with sharp spines. Its fruits are fibrous, similar to coconuts, and contain a sweet, edible kernel.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in challenging climates.
