Chloris durandiana

Chloris durandiana, commonly known as Durand's grass, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lipped flowers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Chloris durandiana is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance and adaptability to various climates.
