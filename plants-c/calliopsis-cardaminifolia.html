Calliopsis cardaminifolia

Calliopsis cardaminifolia, commonly known as the lady's mantle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Calliopsis cardaminifolia is also noted for its ability to thrive in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade conditions.
