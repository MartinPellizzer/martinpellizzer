Cobaea penduliflora

Cobaea penduliflora, commonly known as the hanging bellflower, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Cobaea penduliflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its dramatic floral display.
