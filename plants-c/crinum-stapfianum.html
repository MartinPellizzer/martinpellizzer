Crinum stapfianum

Crinum stapfianum, commonly known as the Stapf's glory lily, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, showy white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has strap-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette at the base. Crinum stapfianum is a member of the Amaryllidaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
