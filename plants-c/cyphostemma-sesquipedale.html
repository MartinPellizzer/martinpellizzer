Cyphostemma sesquipedale

Cyphostemma sesquipedale, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by distinctive, spiny fruits. It thrives in arid to semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky areas or along fences.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
