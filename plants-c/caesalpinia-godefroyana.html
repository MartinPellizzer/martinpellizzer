Caesalpinia godefroyana

Caesalpinia godefroyana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and showy appearance make it a distinctive addition to tropical landscapes.
