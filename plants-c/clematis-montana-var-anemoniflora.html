Clematis montana var. anemoniflora

Clematis montana var. anemoniflora, commonly known as the Anemone-flowered Clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces showy, white or pale blue flowers with five to seven petals, resembling anemone blooms, which appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or walls.
