Campanula lanceolata var. major

Campanula lanceolata var. major, commonly known as the large bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of large, bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to violet in color. The leaves are lanceolate, with a pointed tip and serrated edges, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This variety is noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
