Chamaecyparis pumila

Chamaecyparis pumila, commonly known as the dwarf Japanese cedar, is a compact evergreen conifer native to Japan.

It features scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds, typical of coniferous species. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its slow growth rate and ornamental value make it a favored plant in horticulture.
