Cardamine angustata var. ouachitana

Cardamine angustata var. ouachitana, commonly known as the Ouachita watercress, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and along stream banks. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies for their mild peppery flavor and potential medicinal properties.

The species is part of the Ranunculaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like buttercups and lilies.
