Conyza riparia

Conyza riparia, commonly known as waterleaf or riverweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic conditions. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellow, forming clusters that attract pollinators.

Conyza riparia plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and small aquatic organisms.
