Catesbaea foliosa

Catesbaea foliosa, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant foliage and bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and can grow up to 10 meters tall in favorable conditions. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture with a strong, aromatic scent.

Catesbaea foliosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
