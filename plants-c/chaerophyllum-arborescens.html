Chaerophyllum arborescens

Chaerophyllum arborescens, commonly known as the tree carrot, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features a robust, branched stem with large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed. Its flowers are small, white or pale blue, and arranged in umbrella-like clusters called umbels.

The plant is known for its aromatic foliage and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
