Cardamomum scyphiferum

Cardamomum scyphiferum, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spherical, spiky seed heads that resemble a globe, giving it its common name. The plant produces blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cardamomum scyphiferum is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
