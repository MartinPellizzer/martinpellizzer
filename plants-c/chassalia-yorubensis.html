Chassalia yorubensis

Chassalia yorubensis, commonly known as the Yoruba chassalia, is a flowering plant native to West Africa, particularly Nigeria.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, fertile soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, and it produces achene fruits.

Chassalia yorubensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
