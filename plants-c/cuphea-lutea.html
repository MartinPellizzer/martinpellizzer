Cuphea lutea

Cuphea lutea, commonly known as the yellow torch plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, yellow-orange flowers that resemble small torches. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Cuphea lutea is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
