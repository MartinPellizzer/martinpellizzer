Conophytum stephanii subsp. abductum

Conophytum stephanii subsp.

abductum, commonly known as the "Bald Mountain" or "Cape York" species, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its fleshy, ribbed leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the warmer months. It is typically found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, where it forms dense clusters.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts and collectors.
