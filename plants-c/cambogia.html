Cambogia

Cambogia, commonly known as the Malabar tamarind, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its rough, yellowish-green fruit with a sour taste. The fruit contains a high concentration of hydroxycitric acid, which is believed to have potential health benefits. Its botanical structure includes a thick rind and a segmented interior with edible pulp.

Cambogia is often cultivated for both its fruit and medicinal properties.
