Camphoromoea litsaeifolia

Camphoromoea litsaeifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf camphor tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its wood is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
