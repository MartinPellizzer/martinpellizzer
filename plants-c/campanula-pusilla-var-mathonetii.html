Campanula pusilla var. mathonetii

Campanula pusilla var. mathonetii, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a low-growing habit, with hairy stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its compact size and ornamental flowers make it a valuable addition to gardens with limited space.
