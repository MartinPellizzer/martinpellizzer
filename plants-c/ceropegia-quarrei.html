Ceropegia quarrei

Ceropegia quarrei, commonly known as the "string of hearts," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and pairs of heart-shaped leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia quarrei is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
