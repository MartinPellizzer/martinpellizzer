Capparis uniflora

Capparis uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring thorny stems and evergreen leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, tart berries. Its botanical characteristics include a taproot system and the ability to thrive in poor, rocky soils.

Capparis uniflora is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
