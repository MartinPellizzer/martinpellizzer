Colubrina spinosa

Colubrina spinosa, commonly known as the spiked colubrina, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by cylindrical, woody fruits containing several seeds. Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a resilient plant in coastal and sandy environments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
