Chlorophora scandens

Chlorophora scandens, commonly known as the climbing fig, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to climb using aerial roots that attach to supporting structures. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and a glossy appearance. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically greenish-white in color, and it produces edible fig-like fruits.

Chlorophora scandens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to provide shade in tropical landscapes.
