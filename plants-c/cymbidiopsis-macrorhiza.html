Cymbidiopsis macrorhiza

Cymbidiopsis macrorhiza, commonly known as the large-lobed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and produces striking, showy flowers with prominent yellow and purple markings. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories or rocky crevices. Its inflorescence consists of several flowers arranged in a raceme, with each bloom having three sepals and three petals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
