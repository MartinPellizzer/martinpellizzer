Capparis richii

Capparis richii, commonly known as the rich's caper, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal and rocky areas, characterized by its succulent stems and thick, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, which are often followed by round, greenish-yellow fruits. Capparis richii is adapted to arid and saline environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Its botanical features reflect its evolutionary adaptations to the harsh conditions of the Canary Islands.
