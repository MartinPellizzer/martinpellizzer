Conomorpha citrifolia

Conomorpha citrifolia, commonly known as the sour orange, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow-orange fruit, which is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by the characteristic sour orange fruit.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
