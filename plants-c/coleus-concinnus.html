Coleus concinnus

Coleus concinnus, commonly known as the compact coleus, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, bushy growth habit and vibrant, colorful foliage that comes in shades of red, purple, and green. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and containers.
