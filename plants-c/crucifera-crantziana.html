Crucifera crantziana

Crucifera crantziana, commonly known as the common rockcress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic regions. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are usually basal, with a distinctive shape and a slightly hairy surface.

This species is known for its ability to thrive in harsh environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
