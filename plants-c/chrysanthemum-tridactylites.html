Chrysanthemum tridactylites

Chrysanthemum tridactylites, commonly known as the three-fingered chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small yellow to orange flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.

Chrysanthemum tridactylites thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.
