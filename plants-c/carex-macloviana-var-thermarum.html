Carex macloviana var. thermarum

Carex macloviana var. thermarum, commonly known as the hot spring sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions in Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes or along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, clustered flower heads during the summer months.

This variety is notable for its adaptation to thermophilic conditions, thriving in warmer microhabitats.
