Chrysalidocarpus nossibensis

Chrysalidocarpus nossibensis, commonly known as the Madagascar palm, is a unique succulent native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its short, thick stem and clusters of spiky, paddle-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange or red fruits containing edible seeds. It is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Due to its striking appearance and slow growth, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
