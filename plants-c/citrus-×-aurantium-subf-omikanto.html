Citrus × aurantium subf. omikanto

Citrus × aurantium subf.

omikanto, commonly known as the kumquat, is a citrus plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that turn orange when mature.

The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, with glossy, dark green leaves. Its flowers are fragrant and white, often appearing in clusters.

The fruit is edible, with a thin, sweet peel and a segmented, tart pulp inside.
