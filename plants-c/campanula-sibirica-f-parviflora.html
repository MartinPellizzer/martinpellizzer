Campanula sibirica f. parviflora

Campanula sibirica f. parviflora, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems covered in narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
