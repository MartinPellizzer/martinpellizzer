Calceolaria parviflora f. viscosa

Calceolaria parviflora f. viscosa, commonly known as the sticky calceolaria, is a botanical variant of the calceolaria genus native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers with a distinctive calyx-shaped pouch, which is a defining feature of the genus. The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that produce a sticky substance, giving it its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is prized for its unique morphology and ability to add a touch of elegance to gardens and indoor spaces.
