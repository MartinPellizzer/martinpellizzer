Cissus heterotoma

Cissus heterotoma, commonly known as the climbing cissus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing or climbing habit. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage.

The stems are succulent and can root at the nodes, allowing it to spread easily in suitable conditions.
