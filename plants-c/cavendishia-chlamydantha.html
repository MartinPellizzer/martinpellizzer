Cavendishia chlamydantha

Cavendishia chlamydantha, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a species of orchid native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with deep red veins and its tall, branched inflorescence. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cavendishia chlamydantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
