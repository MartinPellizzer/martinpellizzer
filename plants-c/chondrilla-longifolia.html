Chondrilla longifolia

Chondrilla longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved chondrilla, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are elongated and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish in color.

Chondrilla longifolia is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in arid conditions.
