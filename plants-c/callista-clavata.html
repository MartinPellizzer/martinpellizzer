Callista clavata

Callista clavata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or purple. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in whorls around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its vibrant, showy blooms.
