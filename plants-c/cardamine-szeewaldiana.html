Cardamine szeewaldiana

Cardamine szeewaldiana, commonly known as Szeewald's watercress, is a species of flowering plant in the buttercup family, Ranunculaceae.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a delicate, fern-like appearance, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers with five sepals and numerous stamens. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in cool, humid climates.
