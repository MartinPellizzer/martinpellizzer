Carex appressa f. minor

Carex appressa f. minor, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves that are typically less than 1 cm in width. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting a variety of wildlife.
