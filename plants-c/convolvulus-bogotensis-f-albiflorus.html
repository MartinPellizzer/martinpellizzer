Convolvulus bogotensis f. albiflorus

Convolvulus bogotensis f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered convolvulus, is a flowering plant belonging to the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae).

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and is native to the Andean regions of South America. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain environments.
