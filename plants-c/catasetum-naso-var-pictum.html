Catasetum naso var. pictum

Catasetum naso var. pictum, commonly known as the painted orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, often multicolored flowers that display intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike bearing several showy, spurred flowers.

The species is valued in horticulture for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
