Centratherum camporum

Centratherum camporum, commonly known as the narrowleaf centatherum, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems.

Centratherum camporum is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils.
