Comastoma pulmonarium subsp. pulmonarium

Comastoma pulmonarium subsp.

pulmonarium, commonly known as the lungwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Plantaginaceae. It is characterized by its basal rosette of ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive spotted or mottled appearance.

The plant produces upright flowering stems bearing clusters of small, two-lipped, pink to purple flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, preferring well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking foliage and delicate floral display.
