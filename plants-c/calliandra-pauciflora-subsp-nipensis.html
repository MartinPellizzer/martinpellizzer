Calliandra pauciflora subsp. nipensis

Calliandra pauciflora subsp.

nipensis, commonly known as the Japanese calliandra, is a shrub native to Japan and parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences composed of numerous small, pink to red flowers.

The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it useful in ecological restoration projects.
