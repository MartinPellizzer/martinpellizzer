Carex horikawae

Carex horikawae, commonly known as the Horikawa sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a distinct triangular cross-section characteristic of many sedges.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often wind-pollinated, contributing to its ecological role in wetland habitats.
