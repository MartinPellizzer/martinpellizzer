Combretum apiculatum subsp. boreale

Combretum apiculatum subsp.

boreale, commonly known as the northern flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the temperate regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like inflorescences that display vibrant red or orange hues during the flowering season.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to mild, temperate climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal display.
