Chironia transvaalensis var. longepetiolata

Chironia transvaalensis var. longepetiolata, commonly known as the Transvaal bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, slender petioles and clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
