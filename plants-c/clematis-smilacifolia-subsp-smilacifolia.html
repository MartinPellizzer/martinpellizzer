Clematis smilacifolia subsp. smilacifolia

Clematis smilacifolia subsp.

smilacifolia, commonly known as the smilacifolia clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States. It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets that are often hairy on the underside.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are twining and can grow up to 30 feet long, making it a vigorous climber.

It thrives in partial to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soils.
