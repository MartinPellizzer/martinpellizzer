Camphora blumei

Camphora blumei, commonly known as the Chinese camphor tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant is notable for its high concentration of camphor, a volatile oil used in traditional medicine and as a natural preservative. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates.

Camphora blumei is also valued for its hard, durable wood and ornamental appeal.
