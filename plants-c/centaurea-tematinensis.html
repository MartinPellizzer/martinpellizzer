Centaurea tematinensis

Centaurea tematinensis, commonly known as the Tematino cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny environments. It has slender, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
