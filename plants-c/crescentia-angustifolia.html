Crescentia angustifolia

Crescentia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf trumpet tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a distinctive fruit that is elongated and often covered in a rough, spiny husk. Crescentia angustifolia is typically found in moist, shady areas and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to tropical gardens and ecological studies.
