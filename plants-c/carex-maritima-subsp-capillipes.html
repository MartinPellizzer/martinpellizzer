Carex maritima subsp. capillipes

Carex maritima subsp.

capillipes, commonly known as sea sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of greenish flowers.

This subspecies is typically found in sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines and tidal zones. It has adapted to saline and wind-exposed environments, making it a hardy coastal plant.

Its presence often indicates stable, well-drained habitats in maritime areas.
