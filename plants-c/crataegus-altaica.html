Crataegus altaica

Crataegus altaica, commonly known as the Altai hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in suitable regions.
