Crataegus glandulosa

Crataegus glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is distinguished by its glandular hairs on the stems and leaves.
