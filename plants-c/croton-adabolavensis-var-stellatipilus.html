Croton adabolavensis var. stellatipilus

Croton adabolavensis var. stellatipilus, commonly known as the star-piloted croton, is a striking tropical shrub characterized by its vibrant, multicolored leaves.

The foliage often displays a mix of red, yellow, and green hues, creating a visually appealing contrast. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in tropical regions.
