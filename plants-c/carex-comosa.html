Carex comosa

Carex comosa, commonly known as silky sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex comosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
