Cylindropuntia haematacantha

Cylindropuntia haematacantha, commonly known as the red-tipped barrel cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are typically green with red or purple tips. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, spherical fruits. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system to access water.

The cactus is also known for its sharp spines, which provide protection from herbivores.
