Convolvulus linearis var. sericeus

Convolvulus linearis var. sericeus, commonly known as silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, serpentine stems and small, pale blue or white flowers. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, giving the plant its "silverleaf" appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
