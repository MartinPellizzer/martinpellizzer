Carex erythrobasis

Carex erythrobasis, commonly known as red-stem sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its stems are often reddish in color, which gives it its common name.

Carex erythrobasis plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
