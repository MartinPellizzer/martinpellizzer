Cyanus tanaiticus

Cyanus tanaiticus, commonly known as the Tanaitic blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Cyanus tanaiticus is a perennial herb that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
