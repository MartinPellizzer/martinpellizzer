Calycanthus fertilis var. oblongifolius

Calycanthus fertilis var. oblongifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features aromatic, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are typically red or orange in color.

It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.
