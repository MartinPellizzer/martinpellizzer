Carpesium glossophyllum

Carpesium glossophyllum, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant features a tall, upright stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its showy, purple-pink flowers bloom in late summer and are arranged in a conical shape at the top of the plant.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
