Chrysanthemum japonense var. ashizuriense

Chrysanthemum japonense var. ashizuriense, commonly known as the Ashizuri chrysanthemum, is a variant native to the coastal regions of Japan.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, pompon-like flower heads that range in color from white to deep red. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes. Its foliage is typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

This variety is prized for its resilience to coastal conditions and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
