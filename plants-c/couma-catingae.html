Couma catingae

Couma catingae, commonly known as the Catinga couma, is a shrub native to the semi-arid regions of northeastern Brazil.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family and is characterized by its small, compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to dry conditions, with deep roots that help it survive in arid environments. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often dispersed by wind.

Couma catingae plays a role in local ecosystems and has been studied for its potential in ecological restoration.
