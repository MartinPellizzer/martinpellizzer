Cymbalariella scothophila

Cymbalariella scothophila, commonly known as the moonlight lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is characterized by its pale green to yellowish thallus with a distinctive, finely wrinkled surface. The plant forms a symbiotic relationship with algae and fungi, which provide essential nutrients through photosynthesis and decomposition. It is typically found on decaying wood and stone in temperate regions.

Due to its delicate nature, it is considered a rare and sensitive indicator of environmental health.
