Chironia palustris subsp. transvaalensis

Chironia palustris subsp.

transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal marsh orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments.

The plant features a slender stem with a few leaves and produces small, delicate orchid-like flowers. Its botanical characteristics include a fibrous root system and a preference for moist, nutrient-rich soils.

It plays an important ecological role in its habitat by supporting local pollinators and contributing to biodiversity.
