Caragana maximovicziana

Caragana maximovicziana, commonly known as the Siberian peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, with deep roots that help it survive in arid and semi-arid conditions. It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.

Its hardy nature makes it a useful plant for erosion control and landscaping in temperate regions.
