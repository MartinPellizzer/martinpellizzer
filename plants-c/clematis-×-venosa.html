Clematis × venosa

Clematis × venosa, commonly known as the Virgin's Bower, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces feathery, white to pale blue flowers that are clustered in racemes. Clematis × venosa thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can be trained on trellises or arbors.
