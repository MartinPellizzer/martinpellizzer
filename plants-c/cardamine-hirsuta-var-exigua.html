Cardamine hirsuta var. exigua

Cardamine hirsuta var. exigua, commonly known as the small hairy watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors. The plant features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, which help retain moisture in its preferred habitat. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in racemes, typical of the Brassicaceae family.

The species is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its edible leaves, though it is less commonly cultivated than its relatives.
