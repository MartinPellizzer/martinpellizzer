Craterosiphon montanus

Craterosiphon montanus, commonly known as the mountain rockweed, is a member of the Ulvaceae family and is characterized by its filamentous, thallus structure.

It typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, often found in intertidal zones where it can withstand fluctuating salinity and temperature conditions. The plant exhibits a dark green to brownish coloration, with a smooth, leathery texture that helps it retain moisture in harsh environments. It reproduces through spores, which are released into the water and germinate under favorable conditions.

This species plays a role in stabilizing coastal substrates and providing habitat for small marine organisms.
