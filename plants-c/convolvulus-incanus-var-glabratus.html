Convolvulus incanus var. glabratus

Convolvulus incanus var. glabratus, commonly known as the hairy bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, pale yellow flowers with five petals. The leaves are typically hairy and heart-shaped, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and gardens.

This variant is closely related to other species in the Convolvulus genus, sharing similar botanical features.
