Campanula indica

Campanula indica, commonly known as the bluebell bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy, lance-shaped leaves. Campanula indica is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in rocky or dry environments.
