Carex oederi var. oederi

Carex oederi var. oederi, commonly known as Oeder's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

Oeder's sedge is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting aquatic life.
