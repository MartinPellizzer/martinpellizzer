Cocculus verrucosus

Cocculus verrucosus, commonly known as the rough cocculus, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces distinctive, spiny fruits that contain a single seed. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in shades of pink or white.

Cocculus verrucosus is often used in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
