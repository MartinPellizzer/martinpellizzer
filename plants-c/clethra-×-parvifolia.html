Clethra × parvifolia

Clethra × parvifolia, commonly known as the sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has opposite, elliptical leaves with smooth margins. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Clethra × parvifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
