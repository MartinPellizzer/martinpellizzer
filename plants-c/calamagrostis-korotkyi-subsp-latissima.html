Calamagrostis korotkyi subsp. latissima

Calamagrostis korotkyi subsp.

latissima, commonly known as the broadleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are often tinged with purple or pink hues. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
