Cereus macdonaldiae

Cereus macdonaldiae, commonly known as the Macdonald's night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems that are covered in sparse, white spines. The plant produces large, fragrant, white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its growth is slow, and it thrives in well-drained soil with minimal water.

Due to its unique blooming habit and striking appearance, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
