Ceropegia stellata

Ceropegia stellata, commonly known as the star-shaped ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species with fleshy, trailing stems and small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant produces a unique type of flower known as a ceropegium, which resembles a small, elongated tube with intricate patterns. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for its delicate blooms and trailing growth habit.
