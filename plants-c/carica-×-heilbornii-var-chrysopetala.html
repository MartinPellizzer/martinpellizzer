Carica × heilbornii var. chrysopetala

Carica × heilbornii var. chrysopetala, commonly known as the golden papaya, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Carica papaya and Carica humilis.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow-orange foliage and large, showy flowers that are often used in ornamental gardening. The plant produces fruit similar to the common papaya, though it is typically smaller and has a more delicate flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring warm temperatures and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is a popular choice for gardeners seeking both aesthetic and edible benefits.
