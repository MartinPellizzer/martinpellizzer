Canna indica var. flava

Canna indica var. flava, commonly known as the yellow canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length, often with a reddish or purplish tinge along the veins. The plant produces showy, sword-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, which bloom in late summer to early fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for water gardens and landscapes.

Its vibrant foliage and bold blooms make it a standout addition to any garden setting.
