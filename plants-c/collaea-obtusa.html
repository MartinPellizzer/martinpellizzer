Collaea obtusa

Collaea obtusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved collaea, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, pinkish-white flowers that appear in clusters. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal regions.

Collaea obtusa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
