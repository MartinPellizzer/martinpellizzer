Caesalpinia heyneana

Caesalpinia heyneana, commonly known as the flame tree or yellow flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright yellow, flame-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are divided into numerous small leaflets, and it produces long, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Caesalpinia heyneana is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
