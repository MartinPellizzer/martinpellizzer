Crataegus cotoneaster

Crataegus cotoneaster, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It features small, oval leaves that turn red in the fall, and its flowers are typically white or pink, appearing in clusters. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Crataegus cotoneaster thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
