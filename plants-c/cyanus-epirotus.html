Cyanus epirotus

Cyanus epirotus, commonly known as the Epirotic blueweed, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Cyanus epirotus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
