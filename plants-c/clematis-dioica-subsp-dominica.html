Clematis dioica subsp. dominica

Clematis dioica subsp.

dominica, commonly known as the Virgin's Bower, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States. It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, appearing in clusters. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are pollinated by insects, and its fruits are follicles containing several seeds.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
