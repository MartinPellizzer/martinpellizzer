Commiphora truncata

Commiphora truncata, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine white powder. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, spherical berries.

The plant is notable for its aromatic resins, which have been used traditionally for their medicinal and aromatic properties.
