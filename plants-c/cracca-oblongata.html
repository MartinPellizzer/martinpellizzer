Cracca oblongata

Cracca oblongata, commonly known as the broadleaf cracca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. Its botanical classification places it in the family Asteraceae, also known as the daisy family.

Cracca oblongata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
