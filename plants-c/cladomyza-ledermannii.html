Cladomyza ledermannii

Cladomyza ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's cactus, is a unique species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny, ribbed stems that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a striking contrast to its otherwise muted appearance. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss and providing protection from herbivores.

Cladomyza ledermannii is well adapted to extreme desert conditions, showcasing the remarkable resilience of desert flora.
