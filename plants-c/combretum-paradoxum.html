Combretum paradoxum

Combretum paradoxum, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange-red flowers that appear in clusters during the dry season. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its ability to provide shade and support local wildlife.
