Capparis micracantha subsp. micracantha

Capparis micracantha subsp.

micracantha, commonly known as the small-fruited caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa. It is a shrub characterized by its thorny stems and small, lobed leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, greenish fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
