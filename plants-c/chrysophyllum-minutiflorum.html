Chrysophyllum minutiflorum

Chrysophyllum minutiflorum, commonly known as the dwarf star apple, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, yellowish fruits with a sweet, custard-like pulp.

It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.
