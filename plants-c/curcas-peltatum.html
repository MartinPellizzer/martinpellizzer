Curcas peltatum

Curcas peltatum, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and yellow. It thrives in warm climates with well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Curcas peltatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
