Cressa australis var. petiolata

Cressa australis var. petiolata, commonly known as the Australian cress, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along coastal areas. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to autumn. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
