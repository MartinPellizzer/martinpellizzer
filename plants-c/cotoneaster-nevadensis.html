Cotoneaster nevadensis

Cotoneaster nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky habitats and open woodlands, often at high elevations. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that turn red in the fall, adding visual interest to its landscape. Its flowers are usually white or pink, appearing in clusters, followed by small, red berries that are edible for birds.

Cotoneaster nevadensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
