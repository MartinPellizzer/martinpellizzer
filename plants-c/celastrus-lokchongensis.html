Celastrus lokchongensis

Celastrus lokchongensis, commonly known as the Lokchong bittersweet, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its woody vines and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces elliptical, orange-red berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often climbing trees or shrubs in dense forest understories.
