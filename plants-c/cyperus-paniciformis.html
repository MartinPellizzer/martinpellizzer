Cyperus paniciformis

Cyperus paniciformis, commonly known as the swamp spikerush, is a flowering plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of small, pale green to brown flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly in moist soils, often forming dense stands.

Cyperus paniciformis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
