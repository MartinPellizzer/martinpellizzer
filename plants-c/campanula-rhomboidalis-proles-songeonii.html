Campanula rhomboidalis proles songeonii

Campanula rhomboidalis proles songeonii, commonly known as the Korean bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are rhomboid in shape, hence part of its scientific name, and are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is native to Korea and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
