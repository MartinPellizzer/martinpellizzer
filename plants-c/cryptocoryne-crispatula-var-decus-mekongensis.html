Cryptocoryne crispatula var. decus-mekongensis

Cryptocoryne crispatula var. decus-mekongensis, commonly known as the Mekong cryptocoryne, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, wavy leaves that are typically green with a slight sheen. This variety thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as rivers and ponds, where it forms dense underwater vegetation. The plant is often used in aquariums due to its ornamental appearance and ability to oxygenate water.

It is also valued for its role in aquatic ecosystems, providing habitat for small fish and invertebrates.
