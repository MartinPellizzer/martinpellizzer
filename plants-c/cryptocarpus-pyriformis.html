Cryptocarpus pyriformis

Cryptocarpus pyriformis, commonly known as the oval-leaf cryptocarp, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
