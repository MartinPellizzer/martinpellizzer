Chaetogastra stachyoides

Chaetogastra stachyoides, commonly known as the hairy-stemmed grass or false panic grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It has slender, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some regions for forage or erosion control.
