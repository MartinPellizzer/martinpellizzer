Cissus ringoetii

Cissus ringoetii, commonly known as the African rock fig, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are often consumed by local communities. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
