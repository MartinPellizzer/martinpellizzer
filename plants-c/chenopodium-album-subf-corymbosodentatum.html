Chenopodium album subf. corymbosodentatum

Chenopodium album subf.

corymbosodentatum, commonly known as common lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family. It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with a rough texture and a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense, branched clusters called corymbs. Its seeds are minute, dry, and often dispersed by wind.

This species is widely distributed and thrives in disturbed soils, making it a common weed in agricultural fields.
