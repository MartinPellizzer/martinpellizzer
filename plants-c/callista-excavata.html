Callista excavata

Callista excavata, commonly known as the hollow leaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive hollow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a circular pattern, which bloom in late winter to early spring. Callista excavata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its unique morphology and seasonal blooming make it a notable species in its native ecosystems.
