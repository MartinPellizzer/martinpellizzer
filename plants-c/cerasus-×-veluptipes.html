Cerasus × veluptipes

Cerasus × veluptipes, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or sakura, is a hybrid shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, particularly its showy clusters of white or pink blossoms that appear in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 5–10 meters and has simple, ovate leaves that turn green in summer. Its flowers are borne on short pedicels and are often followed by small, red berries in late summer.

The species is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes and is a symbol of renewal and beauty in Japanese culture.
