Clematis macropetala var. markhamii

Clematis macropetala var. markhamii, commonly known as the Markham's large-flowered clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It features showy, white to pale blue flowers with a distinctive, fragrant, tubular corolla that opens into five broad petals. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and a hairy underside. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, often climbing by means of its twining stems.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
