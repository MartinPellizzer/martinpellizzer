Carex rotundata f. viridis

Carex rotundata f. viridis, commonly known as the green bulbous sedge, is a variant of the species Carex rotundata.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its compact, rounded growth habit and dark green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
