Chrysanthemum leucanthemum f. tubiflorum

Chrysanthemum leucanthemum f. tubiflorum, commonly known as the white garden chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
