Centaurea × emilii-topae

Centaurea × emilii-topae, commonly known as the Emilius Topa centaurea, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, conical flower heads that range in color from red to purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and features narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
