Cucumis dudaim aegyptiacus

Cucumis dudaim aegyptiacus, commonly known as the Egyptian cucumber, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa, including Egypt. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are typically green and have a mild, crisp flavor. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, while its flowers are yellow with five petals.

This species is valued both for its edible fruit and its ornamental qualities.
