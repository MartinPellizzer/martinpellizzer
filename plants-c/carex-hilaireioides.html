Carex hilaireioides

Carex hilaireioides, commonly known as the Hilaire's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its flowering spikes are usually green or reddish and contain small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex hilaireioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.
