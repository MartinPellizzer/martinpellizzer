Cephaelis psychotriifolia

Cephaelis psychotriifolia, commonly known as the Indian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and thickets. The plant features slender stems and small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Cephaelis psychotriifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
