Claoxylon submembranaceum

Claoxylon submembranaceum, commonly known as the silky tamarind, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves that are alternate and often hairy beneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
