Chenopodiastrum erosum

Chenopodiastrum erosum, commonly known as the rough goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and it is often found in disturbed soils such as roadsides and fields. It is a perennial herb that can grow up to 1 meter in height, and it is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions.

The species is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and ability to stabilize soil.
