Conyza tomentosa

Conyza tomentosa, commonly known as the white dandelion, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, wind-dispersed seeds with parachute-like structures, aiding in its spread. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
