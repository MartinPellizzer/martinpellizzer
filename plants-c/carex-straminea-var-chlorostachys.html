Carex straminea var. chlorostachys

Carex straminea var. chlorostachys, commonly known as the green-stem sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with a pale green or white color. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
