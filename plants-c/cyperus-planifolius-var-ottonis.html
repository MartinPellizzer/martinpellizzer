Cyperus planifolius var. ottonis

Cyperus planifolius var. ottonis, commonly known as Otto's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in disturbed soils near water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in plant taxonomy.
