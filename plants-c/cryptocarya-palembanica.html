Cryptocarya palembanica

Cryptocarya palembanica, commonly known as the Palembang cryptocarya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, fragrant flowers and develops dark blue, fleshy berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

The tree is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
