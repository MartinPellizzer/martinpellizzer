Calliandra axillaris

Calliandra axillaris, commonly known as the pink shower tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink or red tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in pairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its rapid growth and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for erosion control and as a decorative plant in warm climates.
