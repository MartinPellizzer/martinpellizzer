Crassula granvikii

Crassula granvikii, commonly known as the Granite Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, thick leaves. The plant is typically found in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink during the spring.

Crassula granvikii is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
