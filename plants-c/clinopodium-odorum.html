Clinopodium odorum

Clinopodium odorum, commonly known as sweet germander, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom in summer. It is often found in moist, shady areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

Clinopodium odorum is valued for its aromatic foliage and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
