Calliandra trinervia

Calliandra trinervia, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, feathery inflorescences that range in color from red to orange. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are often arranged in dense, branched clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant blooms.

Additionally, it has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
