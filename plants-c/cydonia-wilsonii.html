Cydonia wilsonii

Cydonia wilsonii, commonly known as the Wilson pomegranate, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It produces bright red, fleshy fruits that are edible and have a tart, tangy flavor. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid environment. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The flowers are typically white or pink and appear in clusters.
