Carex anguillata

Carex anguillata, commonly known as the snakegrass or narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish flowers that develop into three-angled berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex anguillata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
