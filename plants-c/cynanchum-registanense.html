Cynanchum registanense

Cynanchum registanense, commonly known as the Registan milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing toxic seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used traditionally in some cultures for its purported health benefits.
