Celastrus excisus

Celastrus excisus, commonly known as the cutleaf staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically followed by orange-red berries. Its stems are often woody and can have a somewhat prickly texture.

Celastrus excisus is often found in humid, lowland forests and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
