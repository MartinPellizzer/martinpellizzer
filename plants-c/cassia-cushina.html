Cassia cushina

Cassia cushina, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces elongated, woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia cushina is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
