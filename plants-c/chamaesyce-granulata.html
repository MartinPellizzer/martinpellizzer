Chamaesyce granulata

Chamaesyce granulata, commonly known as rough sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, glandular hairs, giving them a rough texture. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, often blooming from late spring to early summer.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.
