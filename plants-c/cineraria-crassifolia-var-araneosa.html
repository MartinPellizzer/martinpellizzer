Cineraria crassifolia var. araneosa

Cineraria crassifolia var. araneosa, commonly known as the spider cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its arid habitat. The plant produces clusters of small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a silvery appearance.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
