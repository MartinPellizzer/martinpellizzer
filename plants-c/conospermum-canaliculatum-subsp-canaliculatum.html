Conospermum canaliculatum subsp. canaliculatum

Conospermum canaliculatum subsp.

canaliculatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf waratah, is a shrub native to eastern Australia. It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring.

The plant features narrow, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Its unique appearance and seasonal flowering make it a notable species within the Proteaceae family.
