Clematis hilariae

Clematis hilariae, commonly known as the alpine clematis, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and climbing habit, often found in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, with small, ovate leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system adapted to poor, well-drained soils.

Clematis hilariae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
