Corydalis sibirica var. ochotensis

Corydalis sibirica var. ochotensis, commonly known as the Siberian poppy or Ochotensis corydalis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features delicate, spurred flowers in shades of pink and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is complemented by its historical use in traditional medicine.
