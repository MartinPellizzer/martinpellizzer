Chrysochloa annua

Chrysochloa annua, commonly known as the annual golden grass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its golden-yellow inflorescences and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and has a short life cycle, completing its growth and reproduction within a single growing season.

Chrysochloa annua is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive color and drought tolerance.
