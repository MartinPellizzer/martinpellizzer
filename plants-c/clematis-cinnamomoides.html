Clematis cinnamomoides

Clematis cinnamomoides, commonly known as the cinnamon vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its twining stems and clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis cinnamomoides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
