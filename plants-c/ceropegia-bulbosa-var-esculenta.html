Ceropegia bulbosa var. esculenta

Ceropegia bulbosa var. esculenta, commonly known as the string of pearls plant, is a succulent species native to southern Africa.

It features trailing stems with fleshy, bead-like leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale green, attracting specific pollinators like moths. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
