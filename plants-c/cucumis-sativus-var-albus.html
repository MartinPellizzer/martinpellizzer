Cucumis sativus var. albus

Cucumis sativus var. albus, commonly known as the white cucumber, is a variety of cucumber characterized by its pale green to white skin.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to South Asia. The plant is a herbaceous vine that produces small, tender fruits with a mild, refreshing flavor. It is often cultivated for its edible cucumbers, which are used in various culinary applications.

The white cucumber is also valued for its high water content and nutritional benefits.
