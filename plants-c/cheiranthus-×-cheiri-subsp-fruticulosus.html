Cheiranthus × cheiri subsp. fruticulosus

Cheiranthus × cheiri subsp.

fruticulosus, commonly known as the hairy wallflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces bright yellow or orange flowers in late winter to early spring, making it a notable early bloomer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or disturbed habitats.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
