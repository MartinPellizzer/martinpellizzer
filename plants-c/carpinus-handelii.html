Carpinus handelii

Carpinus handelii, commonly known as the Chinese hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged fruits that disperse its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Carpinus handelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
