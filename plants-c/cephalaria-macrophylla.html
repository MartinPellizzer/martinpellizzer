Cephalaria macrophylla

Cephalaria macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange composite flowers. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
