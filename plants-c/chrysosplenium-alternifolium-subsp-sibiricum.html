Chrysosplenium alternifolium subsp. sibiricum

Chrysosplenium alternifolium subsp.

sibiricum, commonly known as Siberian liverwort, is a member of the Saxifragaceae family. It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cymes, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky stream banks.

This subspecies is native to regions of eastern Asia, including parts of Russia and China.
