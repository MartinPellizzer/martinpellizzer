Crepis pulchra subsp. africana

Crepis pulchra subsp.

africana, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
