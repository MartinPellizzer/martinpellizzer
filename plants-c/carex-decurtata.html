Carex decurtata

Carex decurtata, commonly known as the cutleaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and three-ranked leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, with a distinct narrowing at the base, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Carex decurtata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
