Columnea major

Columnea major, commonly known as the flame flower, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant has opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually dark green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Columnea major is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its striking floral display and ease of care in suitable conditions.
