Carvi verticillatum

Carvi verticillatum, commonly known as the whorled caraway, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun exposure.

Carvi verticillatum is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
