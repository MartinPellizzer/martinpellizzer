Cistrum

Cistrum, commonly known as the crown of thorns, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Scrophulariaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiny stems and showy, often yellow or orange flowers. The leaves are typically opposite and ovate, with a rough texture. Cistrum species are native to regions in Africa, the Middle East, and parts of Asia.
