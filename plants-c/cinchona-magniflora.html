Cinchona magniflora

Cinchona magniflora, commonly known as the magnificent quinine tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

The plant can grow up to 15 meters tall and is often found in humid, tropical forests. It is notable for its historical role in the production of quinine, a compound used to treat malaria.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens.
