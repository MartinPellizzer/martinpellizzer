Crataegus nigra

Crataegus nigra, commonly known as black haw, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark berries. The plant typically grows in moist woodland areas and is known for its ornamental value due to its spring blooms and autumn foliage. It is also valued in traditional medicine for its cardiovascular benefits.

The berries are often used in herbal remedies and are a food source for various wildlife species.
