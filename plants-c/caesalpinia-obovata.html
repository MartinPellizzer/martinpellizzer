Caesalpinia obovata

Caesalpinia obovata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of Africa and the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and striking yellow flowers that bloom in pendulous clusters. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The botanical features of Caesalpinia obovata make it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
