Coreosma affinis

Coreosma affinis, commonly known as the scarlet coreosma, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces showy, red-orange flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are clustered in racemes and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued in gardens for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
