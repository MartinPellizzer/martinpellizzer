Crataegus virginica major

Crataegus virginica major, commonly known as the American hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant ecosystems.
