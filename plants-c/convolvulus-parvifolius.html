Convolvulus parvifolius

Convolvulus parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus parvifolius is a member of the Bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
