Calea linearifolia

Calea linearifolia, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant has long, narrow leaves and produces small, fragrant blossoms that attract nocturnal pollinators. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Calea linearifolia is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
