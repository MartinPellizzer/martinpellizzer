Cymbidium squamatum

Cymbidium squamatum, commonly known as the silver-leafed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments. The plant features a pseudobulb with long, narrow leaves that are covered in a silvery, waxy coating. Its flowers are usually pale green or white with dark brown or purple markings.

Cymbidium squamatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
