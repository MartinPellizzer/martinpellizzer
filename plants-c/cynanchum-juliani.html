Cynanchum juliani

Cynanchum juliani, commonly known as Chinese milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, often with a distinctive horn-like structure. Its latex is milky and toxic, containing compounds that can be harmful to humans and animals.

Cynanchum juliani is often found in disturbed soils and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
