Clematis × triternata

Clematis × triternata, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis × triternata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in gardens and landscapes.
