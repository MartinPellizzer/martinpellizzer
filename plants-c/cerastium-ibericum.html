Cerastium ibericum

Cerastium ibericum, commonly known as the Iberian chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has hairy, succulent stems and leaves that are typically rounded or ovate in shape. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and rocky slopes.
