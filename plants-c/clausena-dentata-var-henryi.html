Clausena dentata var. henryi

Clausena dentata var. henryi, commonly known as the Chinese gooseberry, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible fruits.
