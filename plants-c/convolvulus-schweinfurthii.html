Convolvulus schweinfurthii

Convolvulus schweinfurthii, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant blue to violet flowers, which bloom in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves and twining stems that allow it to climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient plant suitable for xeriscaping in dry climates.
