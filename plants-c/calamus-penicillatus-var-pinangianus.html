Calamus penicillatus var. pinangianus

Calamus penicillatus var. pinangianus, commonly known as Pinang root or Pinang cane, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arales.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like India, Indonesia, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. Its leaves are long and narrow, often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

The rhizomes of this plant are valued for their aromatic properties and are used in various medicinal and culinary applications.
