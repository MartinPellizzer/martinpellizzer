Chionotria rigida

Chionotria rigida, commonly known as the rigid snowbell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. Chionotria rigida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance.
