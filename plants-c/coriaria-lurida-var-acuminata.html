Coriaria lurida var. acuminata

Coriaria lurida var. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-tipped coriaria, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Coriariaceae family and is characterized by its small, clustered white flowers. The plant has distinctive, sharply pointed leaves that give it its common name. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

The fruit is a small, dry capsule that contains several seeds.
