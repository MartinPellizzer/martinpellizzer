Cirsium loncholepis

Cirsium loncholepis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are arranged in a dense, spherical head.

Cirsium loncholepis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
