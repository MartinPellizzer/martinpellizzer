Clematis kirilowii var. pashanensis

Clematis kirilowii var. pashanensis, commonly known as the Pashanensis Clematis, is a climbing vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically blue or purple, with five petals. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but not widely consumed. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical characteristics make it a unique addition to gardens and botanical collections.
