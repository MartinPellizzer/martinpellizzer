Colchicum luteum var. alberti

Colchicum luteum var. alberti, commonly known as the Albert's autumn crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant produces striking, spurred flowers that are often surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is both cultivated and studied for its unique botanical features.
