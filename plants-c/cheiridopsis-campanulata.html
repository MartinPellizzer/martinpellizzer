Cheiridopsis campanulata

Cheiridopsis campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped ice plant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, blue-green leaves and small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves to survive drought conditions. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a desirable addition to both cultivated and natural landscapes.
