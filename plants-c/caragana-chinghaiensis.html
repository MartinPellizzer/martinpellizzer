Caragana chinghaiensis

Caragana chinghaiensis, commonly known as the Chinese peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for dry climates. It plays a role in soil stabilization and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Its seeds are edible and can be processed into flour or used as forage for livestock.
