Chaenorhinum rubrifolium var. bianorii

Chaenorhinum rubrifolium var. bianorii, commonly known as the red-leafed chaenorhinum, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking red or pink foliage, which contrasts with its smaller, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It is a perennial species that produces small, clustered blooms during the summer months.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely distributed in horticulture.
