Cyperus diffusus

Cyperus diffusus, commonly known as swamp spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland areas in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue. Cyperus diffusus produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered at the ends of its stems.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
