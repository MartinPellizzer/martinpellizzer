Carex montis-eeka

Carex montis-eeka, commonly known as the mountain sedge, is a perennial plant native to high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its foliage is often dense and forms compact clumps, making it a valuable component of alpine ecosystems.

Carex montis-eeka plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native environment.
