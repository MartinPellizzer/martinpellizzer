Chaetocarpus myrsinites var. stipularis

Chaetocarpus myrsinites var. stipularis, commonly known as the stipulate myrsinaceae, is a flowering plant belonging to the Myrsinaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, alternate leaves that often have stipules, which are small leaf-like structures at the base of the leaf stalk. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with four petals, typically in shades of white or pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within its native habitat.
