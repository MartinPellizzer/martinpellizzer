Carex raynoldsii

Carex raynoldsii, commonly known as Raynolds' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This species typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, and it plays an important ecological role in wetland habitats.
