Carex constricta

Carex constricta, commonly known as narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late spring to early summer, produce small, greenish-brown flowers.

Carex constricta plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
