Carum capusii

Carum capusii, commonly known as Persian cumin, is an annual herb native to the Mediterranean region and Central Asia.

It features slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are aromatic and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which eventually develop into cylindrical, ribbed seeds. Its leaves and seeds are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications for their pungent, spicy flavor.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in temperate climates.
