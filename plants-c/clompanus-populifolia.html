Clompanus populifolia

Clompanus populifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf clompanus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
