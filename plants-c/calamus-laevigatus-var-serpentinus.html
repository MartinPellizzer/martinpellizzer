Calamus laevigatus var. serpentinus

Calamus laevigatus var. serpentinus, commonly known as the serpent's tail reed, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and narrow, strap-like leaves that are often a deep green in color. The plant thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in marshy areas near rivers or lakes. It has a distinctive appearance with its curling, snake-like foliage, which gives it its common name.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
