Calliandra dysantha var. bracteosa

Calliandra dysantha var. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteosa calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences composed of numerous small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval leaflets and is often grown as an ornamental due to its showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility in agricultural settings.
