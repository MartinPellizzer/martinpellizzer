Crataegus suavis

Crataegus suavis, commonly known as the sweet thorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart in flavor. Crataegus suavis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential cardiovascular benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.
