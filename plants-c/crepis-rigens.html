Crepis rigens

Crepis rigens, commonly known as the rigid hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features a slender, upright stem with rough, hairy leaves that are often lobed or toothed. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Crepis rigens is typically found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
