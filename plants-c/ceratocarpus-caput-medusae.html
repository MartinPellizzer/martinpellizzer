Ceratocarpus caput-medusae

Ceratocarpus caput-medusae, commonly known as the "devil's cress," is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive appearance, featuring a central stem topped with a crown of small, spiny, yellow flowers. The plant has a unique reproductive strategy, producing seeds that are enclosed in hardened, spiky capsules. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
