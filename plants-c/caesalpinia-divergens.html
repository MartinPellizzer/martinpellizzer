Caesalpinia divergens

Caesalpinia divergens, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red, flame-like inflorescences that appear during the dry season. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets arranged in pairs. Its flowers are typically surrounded by long, slender bracts that enhance their visual display.

Caesalpinia divergens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.
