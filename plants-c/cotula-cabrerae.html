Cotula cabrerae

Cotula cabrerae, commonly known as the Cape daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Cotula cabrerae has hairy leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
