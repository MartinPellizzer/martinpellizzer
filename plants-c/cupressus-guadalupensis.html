Cupressus guadalupensis

Cupressus guadalupensis, commonly known as the Guadalupe cypress, is a species of conifer native to the mountainous regions of Mexico.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree, typically reaching heights of up to 20 meters. The plant has scale-like leaves arranged in flat, spray-like clusters, and its cones are small and woody. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

The Guadalupe cypress is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
