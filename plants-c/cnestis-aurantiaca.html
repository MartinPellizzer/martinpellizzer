Cnestis aurantiaca

Cnestis aurantiaca, commonly known as the red-flowered morning glory, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking orange-red flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is known for its rapid growth habit. Its flowers are typically trumpet-shaped and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, red berry that matures in late summer.
