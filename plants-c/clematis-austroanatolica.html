Clematis austroanatolica

Clematis austroanatolica, commonly known as the Anatolian clematis, is a perennial vine native to Turkey.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged in an alternate pattern. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Clematis austroanatolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
