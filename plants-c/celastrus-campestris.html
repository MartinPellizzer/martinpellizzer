Celastrus campestris

Celastrus campestris, commonly known as bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Celastraceae family.

It is a perennial vine that can grow up to 10 meters in length, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is native to Europe and Asia but has been introduced to other regions, where it can become invasive.

Celastrus campestris thrives in a variety of habitats, including roadsides, meadows, and disturbed soils.
