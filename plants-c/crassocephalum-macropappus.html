Crassocephalum macropappus

Crassocephalum macropappus, commonly known as the African daisy or wild African daisy, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features a robust stem with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with prominent, elongated bracts that give it a distinctive appearance. Its flowering period typically occurs during the dry season, and it is adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental in gardens.
