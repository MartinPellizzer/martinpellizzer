Crateva membranifolia

Crateva membranifolia, commonly known as the bladder leaf or bladderleaf, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, bladder-like leaves that are covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant typically grows as a herb or small shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and it produces yellow or orange berries.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.
