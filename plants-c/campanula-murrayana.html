Campanula murrayana

Campanula murrayana, commonly known as the Murray's bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue to violet, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy, and it thrives in well-drained, moist soils. It is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats, where it contributes to the local biodiversity.

Campanula murrayana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
