Caesalpinia glauca var. pauciglandulosa

Caesalpinia glauca var. pauciglandulosa, commonly known as the blue glaucous crowfoot, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, glandular structures along the leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
