Callicarpa giraldiana var. subcanescens

Callicarpa giraldiana var. subcanescens, commonly known as the gray-haired beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant purple berries that appear in late summer. The leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its common name. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit.
