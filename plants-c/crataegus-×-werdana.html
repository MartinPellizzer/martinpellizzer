Crataegus × werdana

Crataegus × werdana, commonly known as the hybrid hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree resulting from the crossbreeding of various hawthorn species.

It typically features thorny branches and small, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It is native to Europe and has been introduced to other parts of the world.

Crataegus × werdana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in a variety of climates.
