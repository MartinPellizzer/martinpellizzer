Carex contigua var. mediterranea

Carex contigua var. mediterranea, commonly known as Mediterranean sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

Botanically, it is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
