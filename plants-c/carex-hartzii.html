Carex hartzii

Carex hartzii, commonly known as Hartz's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Carex hartzii produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
