Clematis orientalis f. incisodentata

Clematis orientalis f. incisodentata, commonly known as the Chinese vine or the incised-leaved clematis, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and distinctive, finely toothed leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, violet, and white, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb trellises or other structures.
