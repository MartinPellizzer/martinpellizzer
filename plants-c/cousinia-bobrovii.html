Cousinia bobrovii

Cousinia bobrovii, commonly known as the Bobrovii cushion plant, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh climatic conditions.

Cousinia bobrovii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in degraded areas.
