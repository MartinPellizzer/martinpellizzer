Chamaecytisus heuffelii subsp. jankae

Chamaecytisus heuffelii subsp.

jankae, commonly known as the Janka's broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant has small, leathery leaves and produces small, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal or rocky garden environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature.
