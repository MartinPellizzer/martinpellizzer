Centaurea longepedunculata

Centaurea longepedunculata, commonly known as the long pedunculate knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, bright blue or purple flowers. The plant produces numerous slender, hairy bracts that give it its common name. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Centaurea longepedunculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
