Clusia brachystyla

Clusia brachystyla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often with a distinctive red or orange calyx. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Clusia brachystyla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage and floral display.
