Celastrus royleanus

Celastrus royleanus, commonly known as the Himalayan bindweed, is a climbing shrub native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it produces yellow, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant bears small, fleshy, orange-red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
