Cyperus dubius var. capitatus

Cyperus dubius var. capitatus, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that grow in dense, upright clumps, and its most distinctive characteristic is its umbrella-shaped inflorescence composed of numerous small, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically thrives in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in wetland restoration projects.

The species is also known for its ability to tolerate a wide range of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and adaptable plant.
