Cochlearia batava

Cochlearia batava, commonly known as the Dutch cochlearia, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cress plants. The plant produces small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Cochlearia batava is often found growing in sandy or calcareous soils near the sea.
