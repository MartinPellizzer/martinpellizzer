Coffea pedunculata

Coffea pedunculata, commonly known as the African coffee tree, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and small, white flowers. The plant produces red or yellow berries containing two seeds, which are the coffee beans used for brewing. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique flavor profile, it is considered a specialty coffee species.
