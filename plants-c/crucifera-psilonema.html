Crucifera psilonema

Crucifera psilonema, commonly known as the slender mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its cross-shaped flowers and capsule fruits.

This plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments and has small, alternate leaves that are often lobed or serrated. Its flowering period occurs in early summer, producing clusters of four-petaled flowers in shades of white or yellow. The plant is known for its pungent odor and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

It serves as a host for various pests and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
