Cucumis melo var. albidus

Cucumis melo var. albidus, commonly known as the white muskmelon, is a cultivated variety of the muskmelon species.

It is characterized by its pale green, elongated fruit with a smooth, thin rind and a sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant is an annual vine that produces monoecious flowers, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its botanical features include hairy stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves.

The fruit typically matures in 80 to 100 days, depending on growing conditions.
