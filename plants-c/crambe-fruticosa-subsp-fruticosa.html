Crambe fruticosa subsp. fruticosa

Crambe fruticosa subsp.

fruticosa, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and western Asia. It is a perennial shrub characterized by its spiny stems and blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dunes and coastal cliffs. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of branches.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for small insects.
