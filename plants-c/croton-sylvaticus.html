Croton sylvaticus

Croton sylvaticus, commonly known as the African croton, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful leaves that can range from green to red, orange, or even purple. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Croton sylvaticus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage.
