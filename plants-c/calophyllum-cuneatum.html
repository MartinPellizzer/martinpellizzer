Calophyllum cuneatum

Calophyllum cuneatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf calophyllum, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its elliptical, dark green leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain a single seed, and its bark contains compounds with potential medicinal properties.

Calophyllum cuneatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
