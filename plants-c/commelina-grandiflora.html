Commelina grandiflora

Commelina grandiflora, commonly known as the giant spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features striking blue or violet flowers with white markings, typically arranged in clusters at the tips of its slender stems. The plant has heart-shaped, broad leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for water gardens and bog landscapes.
