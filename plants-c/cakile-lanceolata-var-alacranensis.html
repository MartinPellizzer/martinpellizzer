Cakile lanceolata var. alacranensis

Cakile lanceolata var. alacranensis, commonly known as the Mexican rockcress, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Mexico.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
