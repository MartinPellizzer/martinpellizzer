Crataegus setosa

Crataegus setosa, commonly known as the thorned hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

Crataegus setosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
