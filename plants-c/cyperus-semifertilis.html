Cyperus semifertilis

Cyperus semifertilis, commonly known as the narrowleaf paperplant, is a species of sedge native to the wetlands of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. The plant thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread readily, forming dense mats that can stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Cyperus semifertilis is also used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to tolerate and filter polluted water.
