Cimicifuga foetida f. laciniata

Cimicifuga foetida f. laciniata, commonly known as the fragrant bugbane, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It features tall, slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, adorned with large, compound leaves that are deeply lobed and serrated. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers in late summer, which are arranged in an umbel shape. Its botanical characteristics include a rhizomatous root system and showy, sparsely pubescent foliage.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
