Cytisus leucotrichus

Cytisus leucotrichus, commonly known as the white thorned broom, is a deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, thorny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

This plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in landscaping.
