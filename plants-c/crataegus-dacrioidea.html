Crataegus dacrioidea

Crataegus dacrioidea, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in horticulture and phytotherapy.
