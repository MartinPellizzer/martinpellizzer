Carduus canovirens

Carduus canovirens, commonly known as the yellow thistle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive, prickly texture.

The flowers are arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family, and bloom from late spring to early autumn.
