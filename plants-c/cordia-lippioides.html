Cordia lippioides

Cordia lippioides, commonly known as the African sand dollar, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub characterized by its woody stems and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cordia lippioides is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.
