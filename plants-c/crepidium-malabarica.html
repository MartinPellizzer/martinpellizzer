Crepidium malabarica

Crepidium malabarica, commonly known as the Malabar orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant features a compact, pseudobulbous structure with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and often clustered in a spike, with a distinctive lip that is usually darker in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
