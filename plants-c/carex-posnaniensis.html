Carex posnaniensis

Carex posnaniensis, commonly known as the Posnan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and damp woodlands. Its flowers are small and clustered, with male and female flowers often occurring on separate spikes.

Carex posnaniensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
