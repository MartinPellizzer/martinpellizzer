Calathea inocephala

Calathea inocephala, commonly known as the velvetleaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety foliage with intricate patterns of green, silver, and purple. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a waxy texture, which helps in reducing water loss in its native humid environment. It thrives in low-light conditions and requires consistent moisture and high humidity to flourish.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
