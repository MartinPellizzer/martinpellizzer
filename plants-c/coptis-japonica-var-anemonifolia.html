Coptis japonica var. anemonifolia

Coptis japonica var. anemonifolia, commonly known as Japanese goldthread, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories. Its rhizomes contain berberine, a compound with medicinal properties used in traditional medicine.

The varietal name "anemonifolia" refers to its leaf shape, which resembles that of an anemone.
