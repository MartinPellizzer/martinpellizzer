Crocus boulosii

Crocus boulosii, commonly known as the Boulos' crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces showy, early spring flowers with vivid purple petals and a yellow center, typically blooming in February or March. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or limestone soils. It is a member of the Iridaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in some areas.
