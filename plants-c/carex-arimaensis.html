Carex arimaensis

Carex arimaensis, commonly known as the Arima sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Carex arimaensis produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the sedge family.
