Chenopodium album f. integrifolium

Chenopodium album f. integrifolium, commonly known as common lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, entire leaves that are typically alternate along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

This species is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered a weed in cultivated areas.
