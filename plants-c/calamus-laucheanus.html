Calamus laucheanus

Calamus laucheanus, commonly known as the Lauchean reed, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender grass-like plant that can grow up to several meters in height, with long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically pollinated by wind. Its rhizomes are sometimes harvested for their aromatic properties and used in herbal medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological role in wetland habitats.
