Chenopodium ficifolium var. coronatum

Chenopodium ficifolium var. coronatum, commonly known as the crownleaf goosefoot, is a flowering plant species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that resemble a crown, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and rocky areas.

The species is part of the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its hardy, drought-resistant nature.
