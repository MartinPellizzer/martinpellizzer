Cyperus mundii var. uniceps

Cyperus mundii var. uniceps, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has slender, grass-like stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often slightly hairy.

Its small, clustered flowers are usually pale green or brown, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.
