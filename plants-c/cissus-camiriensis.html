Cissus camiriensis

Cissus camiriensis, commonly known as the Cameroon grape ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by clusters of berries. Its stems are often covered in tendrils that aid in climbing and attachment to supports.

Cissus camiriensis is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ease of cultivation.
