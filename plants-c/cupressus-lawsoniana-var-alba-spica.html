Cupressus lawsoniana var. alba-spica

Cupressus lawsoniana var. alba-spica, commonly known as the White-flowered Lawson cypress, is a rare and ornamental variety of the Lawson cypress.

It is characterized by its dense, pyramidal shape and its distinctive white to pale blue flowers that appear in spring. The plant has scale-like leaves that are typically light green, and it produces small, cone-like structures that mature into reddish-brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a specimen plant.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
