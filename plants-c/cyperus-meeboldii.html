Cyperus meeboldii

Cyperus meeboldii, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in areas with moist soils. The plant has slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-ranked spikelets, typical of the sedge family.

Cyperus meeboldii thrives in wetland habitats and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.
