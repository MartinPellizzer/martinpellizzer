Conchidium

Conchidium, commonly known as the sea hare, is a type of marine gastropod mollusk belonging to the family Aplysiidae.

It is characterized by its soft, elongated body and a distinctive, often colorful mantle that can resemble a sea slug. Conchidium species are typically found in shallow coastal waters and are known for their ability to secrete a mucus-like substance. They play an important role in marine ecosystems by grazing on algae and contributing to nutrient cycling.

Some species are also noted for their ability to change color for camouflage or communication.
