Chenopodium hircinum var. subtrilobum

Chenopodium hircinum var. subtrilobum, commonly known as the three-lobed goosefoot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its succulent, lobed leaves that are typically green to reddish in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or yellowish in hue. It is native to western North America and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
