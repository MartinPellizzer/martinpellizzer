Camissonia claviformis subsp. lancifolia

Camissonia claviformis subsp.

lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved camissonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers.

This species typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in open, sunny habitats. It is part of the Camissoniaceae family, which is closely related to the Brassicaceae family.

The plant is adapted to arid environments and is often found in coastal and inland areas with well-drained soil.
