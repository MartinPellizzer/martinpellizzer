Chamaecyparis obtusa var. fastigiato-ovata

Chamaecyparis obtusa var. fastigiato-ovata, commonly known as the Japanese false cypress or upright form of the Japanese cedar, is a coniferous evergreen with a narrow, columnar shape.

It features scale-like leaves that are dark green on top and whitish underneath, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, spherical cones that are typically reddish-brown in color. It is native to Japan and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its elegant, compact form and year-round foliage.
