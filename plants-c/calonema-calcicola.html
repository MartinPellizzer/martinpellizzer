Calonema calcicola

Calonema calcicola, commonly known as the limestone calonema, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white hair. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
