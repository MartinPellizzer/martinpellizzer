Cyathea lepidopoda

Cyathea lepidopoda, commonly known as the Lepidopod Cyathea, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a role in tropical forest ecosystems.

The fronds are typically dark green and have a leathery texture, contributing to its ornamental value.
