Carex assiniboinensis f. ambulans

Carex assiniboinensis f. ambulans, commonly known as the wandering sedge, is a variant of the broadleaf sedge native to North America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its leaves are generally narrow and slightly hairy, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

The inflorescence consists of male and female flowers arranged in separate spikes, typical of the Cyperaceae family.
