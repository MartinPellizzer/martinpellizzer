Cerastium soratense

Cerastium soratense, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, adapted to withstand cold and windy conditions.

Cerastium soratense thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
