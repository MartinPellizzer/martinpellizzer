Clitoria juninensis

Clitoria juninensis, commonly known as the Peruvian butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that resemble butterflies. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Additionally, it is used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
