Cynanchum roylei

Cynanchum roylei, commonly known as the Himalayan milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by follicles containing numerous seeds. It is notable for its milky sap, which is toxic and has been used traditionally in some medicinal practices.

Cynanchum roylei thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh mountainous climates.
