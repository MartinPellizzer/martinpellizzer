Chrysanthemum lingulatum

Chrysanthemum lingulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf chrysanthemum, is a species native to China.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its finely divided, narrow leaves and clusters of small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants.

Chrysanthemum lingulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
