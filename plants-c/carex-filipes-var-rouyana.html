Carex filipes var. rouyana

Carex filipes var. rouyana, commonly known as the slender thread sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks. Its foliage is often densely packed, forming a dense mat that helps stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.

The inflorescence consists of small, greenish to brownish flowers that are wind-pollinated, typical of many sedges.
