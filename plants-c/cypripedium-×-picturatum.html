Cypripedium × picturatum

Cypripedium × picturatum, commonly known as the painted lady's tresses, is a hybrid orchid native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and sometimes even red. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, preferring well-drained soil. Its leaves are usually broad and grass-like, and it produces a single, elongated spike of flowers.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
