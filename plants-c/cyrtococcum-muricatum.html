Cyrtococcum muricatum

Cyrtococcum muricatum, commonly known as the Andean pearl or Andean snowberry, is a small shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, white or pale green berries that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, typically between 2,000 and 4,000 meters above sea level. Its leaves are usually opposite and elliptical, with a leathery texture.

Cyrtococcum muricatum is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience to harsh climates.
