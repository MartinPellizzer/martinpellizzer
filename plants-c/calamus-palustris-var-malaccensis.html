Calamus palustris var. malaccensis

Calamus palustris var. malaccensis, commonly known as the Malacca reed, is a variant of the genus Calamus, which belongs to the family Arecaceae.

This plant is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery bloom. It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, followed by the development of small, fibrous fruits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
