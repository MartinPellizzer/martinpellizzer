Cichorium intybus var. callosum

Cichorium intybus var. callosum, commonly known as the giant chicory, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length and its vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia and thrives in temperate climates. It is often cultivated for its edible leaves and roots, which are used in salads and coffee substitutes.

The varietal name 'callosum' refers to the swollen, fleshy base of its leaves, a distinguishing botanical feature.
