Citharexylum macradenium

Citharexylum macradenium, commonly known as the Christmas bell, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white or pink. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
