Calochilus robertsonii

Calochilus robertsonii, commonly known as Robertson's spider orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Australia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spider-like appearance with long, slender petals and a narrow, tubular column. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in coastal regions, preferring well-drained environments. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a contrasting red or purple base on the petals.

Calochilus robertsonii is a rare and endangered species, requiring specific conditions for successful cultivation and conservation.
