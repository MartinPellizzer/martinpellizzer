Coriandrum sativum

Coriandrum sativum, commonly known as cilantro or coriander, is an annual herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small white or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, spherical fruits called seeds, which are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its leaves and seeds contain essential oils rich in compounds like linalool and terpinene, contributing to its aromatic profile.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a versatile crop in temperate climates.
