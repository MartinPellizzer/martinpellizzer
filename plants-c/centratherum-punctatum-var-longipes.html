Centratherum punctatum var. longipes

Centratherum punctatum var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed star grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, erect stems that can grow up to several feet in height, often with a fibrous root system. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in clusters, typically in shades of yellow or orange, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall herbaceous appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
