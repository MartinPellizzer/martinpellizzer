Crassula lingua

Crassula lingua, commonly known as the tongue plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, ovate leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Crassula lingua is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
