Cotula drummondii

Cotula drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's cotula, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb with small, daisy-like flowers that bloom in shades of white and yellow. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, and its flowers are typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Cotula drummondii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
