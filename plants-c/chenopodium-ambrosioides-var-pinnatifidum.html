Chenopodium ambrosioides var. pinnatifidum

Chenopodium ambrosioides var. pinnatifidum, commonly known as Mexican tea or pigweed, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its alternate, pinnately lobed leaves and small, greenish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions. Its seeds are small and can be dispersed by wind, contributing to its widespread distribution.

It is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
