Calceolaria lasiocalyx

Calceolaria lasiocalyx, commonly known as the woolly slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pouch-like calyx that resembles a slipper, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers are usually pale purple or white, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by insects.

Calceolaria lasiocalyx is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
