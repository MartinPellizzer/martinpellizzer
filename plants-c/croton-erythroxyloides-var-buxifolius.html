Croton erythroxyloides var. buxifolius

Croton erythroxyloides var. buxifolius, commonly known as the waxleaf croton, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, and often glossy leaves that are typically dark green with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
