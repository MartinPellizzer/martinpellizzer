Cassia ruscifolia

Cassia ruscifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Cassia ruscifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some cultures.
