Crucifera hirschfeldia

Crucifera hirschfeldia, commonly known as the Hirschfeld's crucifer, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or purple. The plant produces small, rounded pods that contain several seeds. It is native to regions of Central Asia and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
