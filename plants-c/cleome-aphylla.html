Cleome aphylla

Cleome aphylla, commonly known as the narrowleaf false musk thorn, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Cleomaceae family, which includes plants commonly referred to as "mouse-ear" or "spurge" families. The plant is characterized by its upright growth habit, narrow leaves, and showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, hard seeds.

Cleome aphylla is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
