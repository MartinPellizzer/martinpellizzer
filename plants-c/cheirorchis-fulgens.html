Cheirorchis fulgens

Cheirorchis fulgens, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a species of orchid native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Cheirorchis fulgens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
