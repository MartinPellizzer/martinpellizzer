Cunigunda

Cunigunda, commonly known as the common mallow, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers are often visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological importance.

Cunigunda is native to Europe and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.
