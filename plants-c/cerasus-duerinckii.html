Cerasus duerinckii

Cerasus duerinckii, commonly known as the Duernckii cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its small, dark red to black fruits that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, while its flowers are white with five petals.

The fruit is valued for its use in traditional Chinese medicine and as a food source for wildlife.
