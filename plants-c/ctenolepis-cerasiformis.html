Ctenolepis cerasiformis

Ctenolepis cerasiformis, commonly known as the cherry-shaped fern, is a unique species belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rounded, and lobed fronds that resemble the shape of a cherry, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to rocky or soil substrates.

The fronds are usually evergreen, providing a lush appearance throughout the year.
