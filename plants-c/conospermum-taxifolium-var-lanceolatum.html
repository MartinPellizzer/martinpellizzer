Conospermum taxifolium var. lanceolatum

Conospermum taxifolium var. lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bright red, tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a woody stem with a distinctive bottlebrush appearance, and its leaves are typically arranged in whorls. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract native pollinators.
