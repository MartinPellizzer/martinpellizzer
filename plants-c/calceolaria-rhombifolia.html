Calceolaria rhombifolia

Calceolaria rhombifolia, commonly known as the rhombic slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant has lanceolate to rhombic-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude regions.

Calceolaria rhombifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
