Cyathea povedae

Cyathea povedae, commonly known as the Poveda's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives in nutrient-rich soils. Cyathea povedae is known for its slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of tropical forest ecosystems.
