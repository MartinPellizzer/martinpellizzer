Chamaesyce celastroides var. waikoluensis

Chamaesyce celastroides var. waikoluensis, commonly known as the Waikolu sandmat, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a rough texture and a waxy coating. It thrives in coastal areas, often found in sandy or gravelly soils.

This plant is adapted to the arid and saline conditions of its native habitat.
