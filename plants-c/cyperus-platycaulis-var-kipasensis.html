Cyperus platycaulis var. kipasensis

Cyperus platycaulis var. kipasensis, commonly known as the Kipasensis flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

This variant is distinguished by its broader leaves and specific geographic distribution compared to the typical form of Cyperus platycaulis.
