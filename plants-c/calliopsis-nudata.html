Calliopsis nudata

Calliopsis nudata, commonly known as the naked leaf cress, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often glabrous, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the mustard family. It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is found in various parts of North America.
