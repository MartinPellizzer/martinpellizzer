Chlorocharis capitata

Chlorocharis capitata, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features striking blue flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to woodland gardens. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
