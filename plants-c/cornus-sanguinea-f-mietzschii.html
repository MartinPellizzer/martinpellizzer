Cornus sanguinea f. mietzschii

Cornus sanguinea f. mietzschii, commonly known as the red sanders or red dogwood, is a compact shrub characterized by its striking red stems and oval, dark green leaves.

It belongs to the Cornaceae family and is native to central and eastern Europe. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that attract wildlife.

It is well-suited for moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant winter interest.
