Couma macrocarpa

Couma macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited couma, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces showy yellow flowers. Its fruits are large, woody pods containing several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
