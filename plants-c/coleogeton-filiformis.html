Coleogeton filiformis

Coleogeton filiformis, commonly known as the slender bladdergrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its fine, thread-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along waterways.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it can become invasive in certain environments.
