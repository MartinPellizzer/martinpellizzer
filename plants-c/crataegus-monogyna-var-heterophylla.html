Crataegus monogyna var. heterophylla

Crataegus monogyna var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable hawthorn, is a subspecies of the common hawthorn native to Europe.

It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, with leaves that can be either ovate or elliptic in shape. The plant produces white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the autumn. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a valued plant in both gardens and natural habitats.
