Centaurea ornata

Centaurea ornata, commonly known as the ornate centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny, bristly foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea ornata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and drought-resistant properties.
