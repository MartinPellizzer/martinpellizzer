Caucalis leptophylla

Caucalis leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaved bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant features small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue or purple in color. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Caucalis leptophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
