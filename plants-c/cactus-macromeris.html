Cactus macromeris

Cactus macromeris, commonly known as the giant barrel cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert of North America.

It is characterized by its large, thick, ribbed stems that can grow up to 15 feet tall. The plant stores water in its succulent stems, which are covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. It produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits.

The cactus plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing food and shelter for various desert animals.
