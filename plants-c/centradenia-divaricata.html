Centradenia divaricata

Centradenia divaricata, commonly known as the African star-of-Bethlehem, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 meter, featuring woody stems and dense, rounded foliage. The plant produces showy, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Centradenia divaricata is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in its native habitat.
