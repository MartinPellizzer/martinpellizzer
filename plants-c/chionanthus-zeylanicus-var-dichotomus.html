Chionanthus zeylanicus var. dichotomus

Chionanthus zeylanicus var. dichotomus, commonly known as the dichotomous chionanthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. The leaves are opposite, simple, and lance-shaped, providing a lush green backdrop to the blossoms. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
