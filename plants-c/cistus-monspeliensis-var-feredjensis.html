Cistus monspeliensis var. feredjensis

Cistus monspeliensis var. feredjensis, commonly known as the Algerian rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Algeria.

It features aromatic evergreen leaves with a grayish-white underside and produces showy pink to red flowers in late spring. The plant is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soil. Its foliage is often used in traditional herbal medicine, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is also notable for its role in local ecosystems, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
