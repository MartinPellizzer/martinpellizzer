Cyperus longeradiatus

Cyperus longeradiatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf paperplant, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies.

Cyperus longeradiatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in water gardens or as a natural filter in aquatic environments.
