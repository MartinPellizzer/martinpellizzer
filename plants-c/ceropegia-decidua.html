Ceropegia decidua

Ceropegia decidua, commonly known as the desert honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, trailing stems and small, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellow, adapted for pollination by specific insects. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Ceropegia decidua is often used in ornamental gardening due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
