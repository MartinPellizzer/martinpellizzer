Clematis dumosa

Clematis dumosa, commonly known as the creeping clematis, is a vigorous, deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, usually in shades of white, pink, or purple. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Clematis dumosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or walls.
