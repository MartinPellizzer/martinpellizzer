Centaurea micracantha

Centaurea micracantha, commonly known as the narrowleaf knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture, and it produces small, bright yellow flowers.

Centaurea micracantha is often found in disturbed areas and can thrive in poor soil conditions.
