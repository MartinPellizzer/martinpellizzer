Cymbopogon arabicus

Cymbopogon arabicus, commonly known as Arabian lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue flowers arranged in panicles, which are typically found at the top of the stems. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Cymbopogon arabicus is often used in traditional medicine and for its aromatic properties.
