Chrysochamela velutina var. aurea

Chrysochamela velutina var. aurea, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves and striking clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has a woody, branched stem with a rough, velvety texture, giving it its species name "velutina.". Its flowers are typically borne in terminal panicles, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape.

The golden flame tree is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
