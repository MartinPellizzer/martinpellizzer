Conyza calophylla

Conyza calophylla, commonly known as the hairyleaf fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and fields. The plant features narrow, hairy leaves and produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. It is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds.

Conyza calophylla is often considered a weed but has some ecological and potential medicinal value.
