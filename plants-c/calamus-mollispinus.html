Calamus mollispinus

Calamus mollispinus, commonly known as the golden bamboo or golden calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often form dense clumps, with long, narrow leaves that are often bright green. The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and air-purifying properties.
