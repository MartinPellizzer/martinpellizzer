Colebrookia bulbifera

Colebrookia bulbifera, commonly known as the bulbous brookia, is a unique plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, bulb-like stems that store water, enabling it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, white flowers during the rainy season, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its leaves are typically succulent, adapted to reduce water loss through transpiration.

Colebrookia bulbifera is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in environments with minimal rainfall.
