Cyperus striatulus

Cyperus striatulus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its grass-like leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinctive striated pattern along the surface.

Cyperus striatulus produces small, clustered flower spikes that are often hidden among its foliage.
