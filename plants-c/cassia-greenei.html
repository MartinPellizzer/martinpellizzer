Cassia greenei

Cassia greenei, commonly known as the Greene's cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds covered in a fluffy, cotton-like material. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.

Cassia greenei thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.
