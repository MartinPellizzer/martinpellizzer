Catasetum × evangelistae

Catasetum × evangelistae, commonly known as the sacred orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the cloud forests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often colorful flowers that feature a unique structure with a specialized pollination mechanism. The plant produces a long, hairy spur that serves as a nectar guide for its specific pollinators, typically bees. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with well-draining soil.

Catasetum × evangelistae is valued for its ornamental beauty and its role in traditional cultural practices.
