Ceanothus crassifolius var. crassifolius

Ceanothus crassifolius var. crassifolius, commonly known as the thickleaf ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with thick, leathery leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, woody fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
