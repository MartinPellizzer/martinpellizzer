Cirsium eriophorum var. richterianum

Cirsium eriophorum var. richterianum, commonly known as the rough thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, rough, and often hairy stems and leaves, which are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces purple to pink flowers in a composite head, which are arranged in a typical thistle-like structure. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows.

Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have prominent veins, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
