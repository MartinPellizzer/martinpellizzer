Canna concinna

Canna concinna, commonly known as the red bamboo canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1.5 meters in length, and its vibrant, fiery red bracts that surround the small yellow flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes. Canna concinna is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, often forming dense clumps.

Its foliage and floral display make it a standout addition to both natural and cultivated environments.
