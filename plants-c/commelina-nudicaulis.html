Commelina nudicaulis

Commelina nudicaulis, commonly known as the common spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It is a member of the Commelinaceae family, which includes other species with similar floral structures.

Commelina nudicaulis is also known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense mats in suitable habitats.
