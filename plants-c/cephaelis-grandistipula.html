Cephaelis grandistipula

Cephaelis grandistipula, commonly known as the large-tipped cephaelis, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are usually pink or white in color. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a velvety texture.

Cephaelis grandistipula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
