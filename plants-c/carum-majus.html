Carum majus

Carum majus, commonly known as greater cumin, is an annual herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces small, pale blue or white flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, feathery leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. Its seeds, which are harvested in late summer, are valued for their distinct flavor and medicinal properties.

Carum majus thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for both gardens and agricultural settings.
