Carex doenitzii var. trichopoda

Carex doenitzii var. trichopoda, commonly known as the soft-haired sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. This variety is characterized by its fine, soft, and hairy leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, which are followed by triangular, three-sided seed heads.
