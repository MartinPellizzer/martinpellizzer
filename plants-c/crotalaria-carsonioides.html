Crotalaria carsonioides

Crotalaria carsonioides, commonly known as the Carson's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Crotalaria carsonioides is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
