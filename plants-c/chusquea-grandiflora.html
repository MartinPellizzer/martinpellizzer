Chusquea grandiflora

Chusquea grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered weeping bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms that often droop gracefully, giving it a weeping appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in clusters along the upper parts of the stems. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental value.

Chusquea grandiflora is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is adapted to grow in moist, well-drained soils in temperate climates.
