Calceolaria filicaulis

Calceolaria filicaulis, commonly known as the velvetleaf calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its striking, hooded flowers that resemble small cups. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are broad and velvety, contributing to its common name.

Calceolaria filicaulis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
