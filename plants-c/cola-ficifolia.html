Cola ficifolia

Cola ficifolia, commonly known as the African cola tree, is a flowering plant in the family Sterculiaceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into woody pods containing elliptical seeds. Cola ficifolia is often found in moist, shaded habitats within dense forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
