Cheirolophus duranii

Cheirolophus duranii, commonly known as the Duranii bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cheirolophus duranii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
