Calanthus

Calanthus, commonly known as the crown vetch, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant is often used for erosion control due to its extensive root system and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Calanthus thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
