Carex mariesii

Carex mariesii, commonly known as Mary's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

Carex mariesii is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
