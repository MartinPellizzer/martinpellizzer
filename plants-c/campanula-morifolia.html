Campanula morifolia

Campanula morifolia, commonly known as the bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula morifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
