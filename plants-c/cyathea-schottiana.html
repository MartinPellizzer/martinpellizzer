Cyathea schottiana

Cyathea schottiana, commonly known as the Schott's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its rhizome is thick and robust, supporting the plant's vertical growth.

Cyathea schottiana plays a significant role in tropical ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
