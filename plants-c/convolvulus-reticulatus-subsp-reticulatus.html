Convolvulus reticulatus subsp. reticulatus

Convolvulus reticulatus subsp.

reticulatus, commonly known as the netleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive net-like venation pattern.

The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its rapid growth and invasive potential make it a notable species in ecological studies.
