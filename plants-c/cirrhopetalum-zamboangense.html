Cirrhopetalum zamboangense

Cirrhopetalum zamboangense, commonly known as the Zamboanga orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a rosette pattern, while its inflorescence consists of a single, branched stem bearing multiple flowers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for horticultural purposes.
