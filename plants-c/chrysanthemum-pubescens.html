Chrysanthemum pubescens

Chrysanthemum pubescens, commonly known as the hairy chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its pubescent, or hairy, stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow, white, and orange, typically blooming in the autumn. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners.

Chrysanthemum pubescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and mild fragrance.
