Cotyledon lusitanica

Cotyledon lusitanica, commonly known as the Portuguese arrowleaf, is a succulent plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a waxy coating, adapted for water storage. The leaves are often lobed or arrow-shaped, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish flowers during the spring and summer months.

This plant thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
