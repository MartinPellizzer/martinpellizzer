Convolvulus heterotrichus

Convolvulus heterotrichus, commonly known as the three-toothed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is often found in coastal and sandy habitats. It has a climbing habit and produces small, round berries that contain the seeds.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
