Chaetostachydium versteegii

Chaetostachydium versteegii, commonly known as the African fireplant, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features a rosette of fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant red or orange flowers during the rainy season, adding striking visual contrast to its foliage. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
