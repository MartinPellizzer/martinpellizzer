Coreopsis ellenbeckii

Coreopsis ellenbeckii, commonly known as the Texas star, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Coreopsis ellenbeckii is also noted for its drought tolerance and ability to reseed itself, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
