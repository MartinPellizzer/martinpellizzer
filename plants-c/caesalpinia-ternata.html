Caesalpinia ternata

Caesalpinia ternata, commonly known as the flame tree or red flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which bloom during the dry season. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small leaflets, and its flowers are typically clustered in dense, cylindrical spikes. Caesalpinia ternata produces pods that contain several seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a shade tree.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to warm, sunny climates.
