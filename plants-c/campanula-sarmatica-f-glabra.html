Campanula sarmatica f. glabra

Campanula sarmatica f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth bellflower, is a variant of the Campanula sarmatica species.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Carpathian region, characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and ecologically valuable in its native habitat.
