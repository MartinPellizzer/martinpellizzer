Coluteastrum prostratum

Coluteastrum prostratum, commonly known as creeping colutea, is a low-growing perennial plant native to parts of Asia and the Middle East.

It features slender, prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often rooting at the nodes. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with numerous oval-shaped leaflets.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
