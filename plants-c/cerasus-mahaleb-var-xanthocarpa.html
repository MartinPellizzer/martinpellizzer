Cerasus mahaleb var. xanthocarpa

Cerasus mahaleb var. xanthocarpa, commonly known as the yellow cherry, is a variant of the sweet cherry species.

It is characterized by its yellowish fruit, which differs from the typical red or black cherries of other varieties. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in early spring, followed by the distinctive yellow fruits in summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration.
