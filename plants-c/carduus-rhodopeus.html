Carduus rhodopeus

Carduus rhodopeus, commonly known as the Rhodope thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and ragworts.

Its robust structure and adaptive features make it well-suited to harsh environmental conditions.
