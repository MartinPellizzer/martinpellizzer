Cotyledon cooperi

Cotyledon cooperi, commonly known as the Cooper's cotyledon, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, especially when exposed to sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps it store water and protect against harsh environmental conditions.

Cotyledon cooperi is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
