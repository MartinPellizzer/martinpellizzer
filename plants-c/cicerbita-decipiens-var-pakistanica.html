Cicerbita decipiens var. pakistanica

Cicerbita decipiens var. pakistanica, commonly known as the Pakistan bittercress, is a flowering plant native to regions of South Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its leaves are usually lobed and have a slightly bitter taste, which is common in many members of the Brassicaceae family.

This variant is particularly noted for its adaptability to arid and semi-arid environments.
