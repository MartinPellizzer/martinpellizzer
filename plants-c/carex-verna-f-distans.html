Carex verna f. distans

Carex verna f. distans, commonly known as the distant spring sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded or partially shaded environments.

This variety is often found in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
