Chenopodium album subsp. fallax

Chenopodium album subsp.

fallax, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and has alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough, sometimes prickly texture.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

This subspecies is noted for its adaptability and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
