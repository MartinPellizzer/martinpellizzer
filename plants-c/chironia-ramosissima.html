Chironia ramosissima

Chironia ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy chironia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its branched stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or red. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinct appearance.

Chironia ramosissima is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.
