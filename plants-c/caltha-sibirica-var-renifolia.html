Caltha sibirica var. renifolia

Caltha sibirica var. renifolia, commonly known as the Siberian crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the colder regions of Asia.

It features delicate, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. The plant has basal leaves that are kidney-shaped and hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and woodland edges.

Its botanical name reflects its origin and the shape of its leaves, making it a notable species in botanical gardens and wetland habitats.
