Cardamine bulbosa var. purpurea

Cardamine bulbosa var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple buttercup, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive purplish tinge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

The species is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.
