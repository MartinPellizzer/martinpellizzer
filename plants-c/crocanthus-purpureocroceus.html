Crocanthus purpureocroceus

Crocanthus purpureocroceus, commonly known as the purple crocus, is a member of the Iridaceae family and is native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows to a height of about 15-30 cm and features grass-like leaves. Its flowers consist of six petals arranged in a star-like pattern, with three outer petals that are larger than the inner ones.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
