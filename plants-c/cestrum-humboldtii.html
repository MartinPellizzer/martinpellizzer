Cestrum humboldtii

Cestrum humboldtii, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a member of the Solanaceae family, native to South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, featuring aromatic, tubular flowers that open at night. The plant produces bright yellow or white flowers, which are often fragrant and attract nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains numerous seeds.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
