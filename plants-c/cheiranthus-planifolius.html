Cheiranthus planifolius

Cheiranthus planifolius, commonly known as the flat-leafed wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbages, and is characterized by its small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or disturbed soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are elongated and flat, giving it its common name, while its flowers range in color from yellow to orange.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
