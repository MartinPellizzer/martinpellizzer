Chloraea phoenicea

Chloraea phoenicea, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant is a shrub or small tree, typically growing up to 3 meters in height. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly waxy surface.

Chloraea phoenicea is an important species in Australian flora and is also the national floral emblem of Australia.
