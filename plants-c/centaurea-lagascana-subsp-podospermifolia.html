Centaurea lagascana subsp. podospermifolia

Centaurea lagascana subsp.

podospermifolia, commonly known as the Sicilian star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and small, blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters.

The leaves are typically narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This subspecies is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience in harsh conditions.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and serving as a food source for certain herbivores.
