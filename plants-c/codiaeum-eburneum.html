Codiaeum eburneum

Codiaeum eburneum, commonly known as the white-veined croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that feature white veins on a deep green background. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant foliage.

Due to its sensitivity to cold, it is typically grown as a houseplant or in greenhouse conditions.
