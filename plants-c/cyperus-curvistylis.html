Cyperus curvistylis

Cyperus curvistylis, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds.

Cyperus curvistylis is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils.
