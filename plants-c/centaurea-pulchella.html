Centaurea pulchella

Centaurea pulchella, commonly known as the pretty centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It produces vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Centaurea pulchella is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive appearance.
