Cheiranthus × fruticulosus

Cheiranthus × fruticulosus, commonly known as the woolly wallflower, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy, brightly colored flowers that typically range from yellow to orange. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.
