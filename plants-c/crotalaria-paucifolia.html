Crotalaria paucifolia

Crotalaria paucifolia, commonly known as the few-flowered cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, also known as the legume or pea family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with compound leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. Its small, clustered flowers are often surrounded by leaf-like bracts, giving it a unique appearance.

Crotalaria paucifolia is sometimes used in traditional medicine and can be found in various habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
