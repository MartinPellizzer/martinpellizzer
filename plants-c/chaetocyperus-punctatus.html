Chaetocyperus punctatus

Chaetocyperus punctatus, commonly known as the punctate flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight zigzag pattern along their edges.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
