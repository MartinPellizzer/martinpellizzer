Cosmos pseudoperfoliatus

Cosmos pseudoperfoliatus, commonly known as the Mexican blanket flower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of the United States.

It is characterized by its striking, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Cosmos pseudoperfoliatus is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
