Cissus viniferoides

Cissus viniferoides, commonly known as the grape ivy, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by clusters of edible, grape-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus viniferoides is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and vigorous growth habit.
