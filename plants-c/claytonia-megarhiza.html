Claytonia megarhiza

Claytonia megarhiza, commonly known as the giant purpleleaf sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically purple or reddish in color. The plant produces clusters of small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Claytonia megarhiza is a perennial herb that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
