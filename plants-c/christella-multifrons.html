Christella multifrons

Christella multifrons, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features slender, upright stems covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually blue to purple in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, narrow, and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Christella multifrons is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
