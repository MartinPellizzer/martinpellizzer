Citrus × aurantium var. amara

Citrus × aurantium var. amara, commonly known as bitter orange, is a citrus species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, oval-shaped fruits that contain a high concentration of bitter compounds. The plant is often cultivated for its fragrant flowers and as a source of extracts used in perfumery and medicine. Its fruit is typically not consumed raw due to its intense bitterness but is used in marmalades and medicinal preparations.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
