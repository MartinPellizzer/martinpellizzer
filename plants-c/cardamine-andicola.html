Cardamine andicola

Cardamine andicola, commonly known as the Andean watercress, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and economically important plants. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small white or pale yellow flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in shaded, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both ecological and horticultural studies.
