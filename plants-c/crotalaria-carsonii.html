Crotalaria carsonii

Crotalaria carsonii, commonly known as Carson's cowpea, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southern United States.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant features palmately compound leaves and produces yellow flowers with distinctive spurred petals. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its ornamental value and potential as a forage crop, it is cultivated in certain regions for agricultural and ecological purposes.
