Carex alba subsp. ussuriensis

Carex alba subsp.

ussuriensis, commonly known as the Ussuri sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically white or pale green, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet habitats.
