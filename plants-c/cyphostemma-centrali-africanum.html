Cyphostemma centrali-africanum

Cyphostemma centrali-africanum, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, followed by orange-red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
