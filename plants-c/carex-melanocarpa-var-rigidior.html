Carex melanocarpa var. rigidior

Carex melanocarpa var. rigidior, commonly known as blackstem sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike of small, spikelet-like flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
