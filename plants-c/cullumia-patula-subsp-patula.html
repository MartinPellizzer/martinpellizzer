Cullumia patula subsp. patula

Cullumia patula subsp.

patula, commonly known as the slender cullumia, is a perennial herb native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features slender, erect stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its flowers are clustered in dense, spike-like inflorescences and are usually pink to purple in color.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native range.
