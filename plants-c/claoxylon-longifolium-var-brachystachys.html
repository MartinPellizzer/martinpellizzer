Claoxylon longifolium var. brachystachys

Claoxylon longifolium var. brachystachys, commonly known as the short-stalked flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers arranged in panicles, which bloom during the rainy season. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

The wood of this species is valued for its durability and is used in construction and furniture making.
