Cissus trachyphylla

Cissus trachyphylla, commonly known as the rock fig or African snow vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth and ability to adhere to surfaces using its aerial roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a rough, textured surface.

Cissus trachyphylla is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
