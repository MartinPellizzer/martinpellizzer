Clematis lavallei

Clematis lavallei, commonly known as the purple clematis, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically compound, with three to five leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

Clematis lavallei is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
