Crepidium amabilis

Crepidium amabilis, commonly known as the pink lady's slipper, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking pink and white floral structure, which resembles a slipper or a lady's shoe, hence its common name. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a distinctive pouch-like lip that aids in pollination by attracting specific insect pollinators. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
