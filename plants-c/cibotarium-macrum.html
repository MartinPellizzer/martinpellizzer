Cibotarium macrum

Cibotarium macrum, commonly known as the giant cibotium, is a striking fern native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, deeply lobed fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil. Its spores are produced in clusters on the undersides of its fronds, and it reproduces primarily through spore dispersal.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
