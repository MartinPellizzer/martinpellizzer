Crataegus amygdaliformis

Crataegus amygdaliformis, commonly known as the almond-shaped hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, almond-shaped fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. The plant produces white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by the development of its characteristic berries. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges, and the bark is generally smooth with a grayish-brown color.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils in woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
