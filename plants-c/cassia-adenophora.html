Cassia adenophora

Cassia adenophora, commonly known as the weeping cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its drooping inflorescences and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Cassia adenophora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.
