Convolvulus mattogrossensis

Convolvulus mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, often blooming in the early morning. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are twining and can grow quite long.

Convolvulus mattogrossensis is often found in moist, sunny habitats such as riverbanks and forest edges.
