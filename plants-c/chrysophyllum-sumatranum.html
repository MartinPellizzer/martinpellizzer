Chrysophyllum sumatranum

Chrysophyllum sumatranum, commonly known as the Sumatran starfruit, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, fragrant white flowers. The fruit, which resembles a starfruit, is typically yellow or orange when ripe and has a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

The plant is valued not only for its edible fruit but also for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
