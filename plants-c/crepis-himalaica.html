Crepis himalaica

Crepis himalaica, commonly known as the Himalayan mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are usually hairy and spoon-shaped, adapted to reduce water loss in harsh climates.

Crepis himalaica produces small, yellowish flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
