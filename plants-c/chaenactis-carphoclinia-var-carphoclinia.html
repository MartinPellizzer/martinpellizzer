Chaenactis carphoclinia var. carphoclinia

Chaenactis carphoclinia var. carphoclinia, commonly known as the Sierra dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces a basal rosette of silver-green, woolly leaves and has a short, flowering stem.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators.
