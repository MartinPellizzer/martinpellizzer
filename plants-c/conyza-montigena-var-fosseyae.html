Conyza montigena var. fosseyae

Conyza montigena var. fosseyae, commonly known as the Fossey's blueweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its small blue to purple flowers bloom during the cooler months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
