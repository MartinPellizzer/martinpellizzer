Cyphomandra pinetorum

Cyphomandra pinetorum, commonly known as the pineapple guava, is a shrub native to South America.

It produces small, green, egg-shaped fruits that are typically harvested when they turn yellow. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are oval with a slightly hairy surface. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is edible, with a sweet and tangy flavor, and is often used in culinary applications.
