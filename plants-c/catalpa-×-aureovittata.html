Catalpa × aureovittata

Catalpa × aureovittata, commonly known as the golden stripe catalpa, is a hybrid deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from white to pale yellow, often with a distinctive golden stripe. The tree produces long, bean-like pods that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture, providing a striking visual contrast to its ornamental flowers.

This species is valued in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
