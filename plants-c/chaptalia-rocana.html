Chaptalia rocana

Chaptalia rocana, commonly known as the rock chaptalia, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing adaptations to harsh, cold climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss in arid conditions.
