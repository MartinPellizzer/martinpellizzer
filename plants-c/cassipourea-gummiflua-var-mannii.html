Cassipourea gummiflua var. mannii

Cassipourea gummiflua var. mannii, commonly known as the African gum tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers followed by woody, fleshy fruits. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in lowland rainforest or savanna regions.

The species is valued for its durable timber and has been used in traditional construction and craftsmanship.
