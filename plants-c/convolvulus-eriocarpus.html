Convolvulus eriocarpus

Convolvulus eriocarpus, commonly known as the woolly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, ascending stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Convolvulus eriocarpus is an annual plant that blooms throughout the warmer months.
