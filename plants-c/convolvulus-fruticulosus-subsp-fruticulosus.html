Convolvulus fruticulosus subsp. fruticulosus

Convolvulus fruticulosus subsp.

fruticulosus, commonly known as the hairy bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming from spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

Its rapid growth and invasive tendencies make it a common weed in agricultural and natural settings.
