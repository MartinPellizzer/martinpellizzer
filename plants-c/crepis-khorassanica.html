Crepis khorassanica

Crepis khorassanica, commonly known as Persian mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by the wind.
