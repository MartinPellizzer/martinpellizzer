Campsiandra macrocarpa var. grandifolia

Campsiandra macrocarpa var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-fruited campsiandra, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers followed by large, fleshy, orange-red berries. Its growth habit is usually shrub-like, with a dense, bushy form.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
