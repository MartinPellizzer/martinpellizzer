Cedrela toona var. gamblei

Cedrela toona var. gamblei, commonly known as the Gambier tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family, which includes mahogany and other valuable hardwoods. The tree is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in panicles, while the fruit is a woody capsule containing several seeds.

The wood of this species is prized for its fine grain and durability, making it valuable in the timber industry.
