Capparis triflora

Capparis triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in threes, giving it its common name. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
