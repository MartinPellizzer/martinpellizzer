Clinopodium graveolens subsp. graveolens

Clinopodium graveolens subsp.

graveolens, commonly known as wild thyme, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, reaching a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters.

The plant features opposite, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its small, pink to purple flowers bloom in summer. It is valued for its aromatic leaves, which contain essential oils used in herbal remedies and culinary applications.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more robust growth and stronger fragrance compared to other varieties.
