Centaurea emigrantis

Centaurea emigrantis, commonly known as the emigrating centaurea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its bright blue to purple flowers are arranged in a composite head, with ray florets surrounding disc florets.

Centaurea emigrantis is often found in sandy or gravelly areas and is adapted to dry, temperate climates.
