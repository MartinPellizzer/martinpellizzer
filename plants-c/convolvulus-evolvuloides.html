Convolvulus evolvuloides

Convolvulus evolvuloides, commonly known as the narrowleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a herbaceous annual with slender, twining stems that climb by means of tendrils. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in the early morning. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a hairy texture, while its seeds are enclosed in a spherical capsule.

Convolvulus evolvuloides is often found in disturbed soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
