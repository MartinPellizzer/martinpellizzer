Ceropegia paradoxica

Ceropegia paradoxica, commonly known as the devil's backbone, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has a trailing growth habit with fleshy, succulent stems and leaves. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light.

Ceropegia paradoxica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ease of care.
