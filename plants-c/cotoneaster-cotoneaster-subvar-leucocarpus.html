Cotoneaster cotoneaster subvar. leucocarpus

Cotoneaster cotoneaster subvar. leucocarpus, commonly known as the white-fruited cotoneaster, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that are edible for birds. It is a deciduous species, shedding its leaves in the fall.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
