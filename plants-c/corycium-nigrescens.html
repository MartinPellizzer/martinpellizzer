Corycium nigrescens

Corycium nigrescens, commonly known as the black-tipped orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, dark purple to black flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its unique coloration and habitat make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species.
