Coleus ruandensis

Coleus ruandensis, commonly known as the Ruanda coleus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly Rwanda.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are often variegated with shades of green, red, and yellow, making it a visually striking species.

While primarily cultivated for its ornamental value, it also has traditional uses in some African communities.
