Carduus carlinifolius proles zetterstedtianus

Carduus carlinifolius proles zetterstedtianus, commonly known as the Zetterstedt's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Europe.

It features a robust, upright stem with rough, hairy surfaces and spiny leaves that are deeply lobed. The plant produces a single, large, purple to violet flower head, which is surrounded by a series of bracts. Its flowers are typically visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

The species is characterized by its hardy nature and ability to thrive in disturbed soils and open habitats.
