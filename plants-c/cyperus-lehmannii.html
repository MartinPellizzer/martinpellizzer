Cyperus lehmannii

Cyperus lehmannii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers. It thrives in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands.

Cyperus lehmannii is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
