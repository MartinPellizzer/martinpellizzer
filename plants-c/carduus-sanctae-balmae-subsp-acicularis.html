Carduus sanctae-balmae subsp. acicularis

Carduus sanctae-balmae subsp.

acicularis, commonly known as the thistle of Saint Balma, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and a distinctive, conical flower head.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It has a short flowering period in late summer, producing purple to pink flowers.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant or used in traditional medicine.
