Cucumis kelleri

Cucumis kelleri, commonly known as the desert cucumber, is a flowering plant species native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically green and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it thrives in hot, dry climates with minimal water requirements.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often cultivated in regions with limited agricultural resources.
