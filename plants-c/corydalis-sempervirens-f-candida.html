Corydalis sempervirens f. candida

Corydalis sempervirens f. candida, commonly known as the white-flowered corydalis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, delicate flowers with white petals and a distinctive spurred shape. The leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This variety is valued for its early spring blooming habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance and low maintenance requirements.
