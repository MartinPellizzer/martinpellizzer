Carya ovalis f. fraxinifolia

Carya ovalis f. fraxinifolia, commonly known as the white hickory, is a botanical variant of the hickory genus native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped nuts and smooth, light-colored bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This cultivar is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often grown for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.
