Cercocarpus argenteus

Cercocarpus argenteus, commonly known as silverleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and its small, dark purple to black berries. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It has a compact growth habit and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The leaves are deciduous, turning reddish-brown in the fall before shedding.
