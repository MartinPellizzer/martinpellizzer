Copaiba hymenaeifolia

Copaiba hymenaeifolia, commonly known as the yellow copaiba, is a tropical tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its aromatic resin, which is often used in traditional medicine. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a pointed tip.

The tree is valued for its ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
