Cyclosorus opulentus

Cyclosorus opulentus, commonly known as the opulent water clover, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its intricate, branching structure and delicate, overlapping leaves.

It thrives in shallow, freshwater environments with ample sunlight and nutrient-rich substrates. The plant's leaves are typically broad and oval, often forming a dense, carpet-like growth pattern on the water's surface. Its reproductive system involves the production of small, floating spores that are dispersed by water currents.

Cyclosorus opulentus plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and small aquatic life.
