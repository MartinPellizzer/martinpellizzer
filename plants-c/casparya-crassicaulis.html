Casparya crassicaulis

Casparya crassicaulis, commonly known as the broad-leaved casparya, is a flowering plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, fleshy stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found near rivers and marshes.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a valuable species for wetland conservation efforts.
