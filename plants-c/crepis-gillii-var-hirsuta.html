Crepis gillii var. hirsuta

Crepis gillii var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and alternate, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its flowers, which bloom in late summer, are yellow and are surrounded by bracts that give the appearance of a single flower.

The species is noted for its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
