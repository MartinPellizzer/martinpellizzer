Cercocarpus ledifolius var. typicus

Cercocarpus ledifolius var. typicus, commonly known as the cliffrose, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, red or orange berries that persist through winter. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, providing a drought-resistant structure.

The cliffrose plays an important ecological role by offering habitat and food for various wildlife species.
