Carum carvi

Carum carvi, commonly known as parsley, is a biennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like leaves and small white or yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, dry fruits called umbels, which contain the aromatic seeds used in cooking and traditional medicine. Its roots are also edible and have a mild, fresh flavor.

Carum carvi is valued for its culinary uses and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
