Cyclea wallichii

Cyclea wallichii, commonly known as the Indian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Cyclea wallichii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
