Celtis occidentalis var. cordata

Celtis occidentalis var. cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf boxelder, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Ulmaceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically lobed and have a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain three to five seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodland edges.

The heartleaf boxelder is also known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
