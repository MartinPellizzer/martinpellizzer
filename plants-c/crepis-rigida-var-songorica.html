Crepis rigida var. songorica

Crepis rigida var. songorica, commonly known as the Songorica mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, basal rosette of leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its flowers are often clustered in loose, open inflorescences, and it produces small, achene-like seeds.

It is a hardy species that thrives in well-drained conditions and is often found in mountainous regions.
