Commelina bella

Commelina bella, commonly known as the pretty spiderwort, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy texture, while the flowers are clustered in racemes.

Commelina bella is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
