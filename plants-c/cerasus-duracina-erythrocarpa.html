Cerasus duracina erythrocarpa

Cerasus duracina erythrocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruited cherry, is a species native to certain regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits that turn a striking red color when mature. The plant typically grows as a deciduous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a serrated margin, and the flowers are usually white or pale pink.

The fruit is often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant due to its ornamental value.
