Capparis grandis var. auricans

Capparis grandis var. auricans, commonly known as the golden caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow or orange flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has woody stems and pinnate leaves with small, serrated leaflets. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The botanical features of Capparis grandis var. auricans make it a notable species in both horticulture and ecological studies.
