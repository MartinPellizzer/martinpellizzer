Clinopodium troodi subsp. vardaranum

Clinopodium troodi subsp.

vardaranum, commonly known as the Vardaran mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, characterized by its square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are small, tubular, and clustered in whorls.

It is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and slopes.
