Chamaecytisus austriacus subsp. tommasinii

Chamaecytisus austriacus subsp.

tommasinii, commonly known as the Austrian broom, is a shrub native to the Alps of Europe. It is characterized by its dense, wiry stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil on slopes.
