Crocus moesiacus f. violascens

Crocus moesiacus f. violascens, commonly known as the violet-flowered crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features striking violet to deep purple flowers that bloom in early spring, typically between February and April. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spherical bulbs that help it propagate. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and vivid coloration.
