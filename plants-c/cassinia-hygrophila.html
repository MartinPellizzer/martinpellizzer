Cassinia hygrophila

Cassinia hygrophila, commonly known as the water cassinia, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy yellow flowers that bloom in dense, spherical clusters. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including swamps and marshes, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, and the plant can reach heights of up to 2 meters.

Cassinia hygrophila plays an important ecological role in supporting local wildlife and stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
