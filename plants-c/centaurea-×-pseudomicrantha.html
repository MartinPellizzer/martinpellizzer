Centaurea × pseudomicrantha

Centaurea × pseudomicrantha, commonly known as the hybrid glaucous cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flower heads, which are surrounded by pale blue to white ray florets. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It produces small, oblong seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread across various regions.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
