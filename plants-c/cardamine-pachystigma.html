Cardamine pachystigma

Cardamine pachystigma, commonly known as the broad-leaved watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broadly ovate leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically borne on slender stems. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors.

The plant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its peppery flavor in culinary uses.
