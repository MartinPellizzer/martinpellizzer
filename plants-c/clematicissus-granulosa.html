Clematicissus granulosa

Clematicissus granulosa, commonly known as the rough Clematis, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to purple, blooming in spring and summer. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, textured surface, and its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematicissus granulosa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or structures.
