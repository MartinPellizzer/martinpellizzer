Citrosma bifida

Citrosma bifida, commonly known as the split-leaved citrosma, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its bipinnate leaves, which are finely divided and give it a feathery appearance. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, often clustered in axillary inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, round drupe with a fragrant, yellowish pulp that contains a single seed.
