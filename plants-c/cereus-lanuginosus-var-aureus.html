Cereus lanuginosus var. aureus

Cereus lanuginosus var. aureus, commonly known as the golden star cactus, is a species native to South America.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant has a thick, fleshy stem that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. Its flowers, which bloom at night, are a striking feature, often emitting a sweet fragrance.

The variety 'aureus' is distinguished by its vibrant yellow coloration, making it a popular choice for ornamental cultivation.
