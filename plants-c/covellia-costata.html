Covellia costata

Covellia costata, commonly known as the serrated thyme, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its aromatic, woody stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications, adding a distinctive, peppery flavor to dishes.

Covellia costata thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and borders.
