Citrus × clementina

Citrus × clementina, commonly known as the clementine.

It is a hybrid citrus fruit resulting from the cross between mandarin orange and sweet orange. The plant is a small evergreen tree with dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is typically small, round, and has a thick, loose rind that separates easily from the pulp.

Clementines are rich in vitamin C and are known for their sweet, juicy flavor with minimal acidity.
