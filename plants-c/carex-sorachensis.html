Carex sorachensis

Carex sorachensis, commonly known as the Sorachensis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Japan.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky slopes. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are usually green or brown in color.

Carex sorachensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in its natural habitat.
