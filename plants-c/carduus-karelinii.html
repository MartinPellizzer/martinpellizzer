Carduus karelinii

Carduus karelinii, commonly known as Karelin's thistle, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and can reach heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

Karelin's thistle is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
