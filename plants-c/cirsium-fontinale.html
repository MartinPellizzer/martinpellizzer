Cirsium fontinale

Cirsium fontinale, commonly known as the alpine thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with composite flower heads.

Due to its habitat, it is often found in high-altitude meadows and is adapted to withstand cold and harsh weather conditions.
