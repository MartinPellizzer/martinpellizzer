Crataegus attrita

Crataegus attrita, commonly known as the sand crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by small, red or yellow fruits in the fall. Its fruit is edible but often tart, and it is used in some traditional culinary and medicinal practices.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for wildlife.
