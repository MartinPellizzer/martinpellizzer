Chaenorhinum grandiflorum subsp. carthaginense

Chaenorhinum grandiflorum subsp.

carthaginense, commonly known as the Carthage dwarf toadflax, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial with slender, branching stems and small, bright yellow flowers.

The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Plantaginaceae family.
