Centaurea parvispina

Centaurea parvispina, commonly known as the small-spined centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its small, spiny leaves and bright blue or purple flowers make it easily identifiable.

Centaurea parvispina is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
