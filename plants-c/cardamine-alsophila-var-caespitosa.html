Cardamine alsophila var. caespitosa

Cardamine alsophila var. caespitosa, commonly known as the clustered watercress, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, kidney-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces white or pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are borne on slender stalks. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors.

Its presence indicates a healthy, undisturbed ecosystem due to its sensitivity to environmental changes.
