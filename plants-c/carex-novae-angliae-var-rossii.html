Carex novae-angliae var. rossii

Carex novae-angliae var. rossii, commonly known as the Ross's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variant of Carex novae-angliae typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by distinctive three-sided seed capsules.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
