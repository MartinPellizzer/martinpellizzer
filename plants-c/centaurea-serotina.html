Centaurea serotina

Centaurea serotina, commonly known as late centaury, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and branching.

Centaurea serotina is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and ecological benefits.
