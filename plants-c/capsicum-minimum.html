Capsicum minimum

Capsicum minimum, commonly known as the dwarf pepper or miniature bell pepper, is a compact variety of the Capsicum genus.

It is characterized by its small, round fruits that typically measure less than 2 inches in diameter. The plant has a bushy growth habit with small, lobed leaves and produces flowers that are usually yellow or white. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often grown in containers or as a decorative plant due to its compact size and ornamental appeal.
