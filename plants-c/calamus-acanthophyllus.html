Calamus acanthophyllus

Calamus acanthophyllus, commonly known as the spiny-leafed arrowroot, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, which includes gingers.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in inflorescences, followed by red berries that contain the seeds. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its rhizomes are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its ornamental value is also notable.
