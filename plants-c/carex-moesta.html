Carex moesta

Carex moesta, commonly known as the moody sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, usually dark green with a bluish tint, and it produces small, clustered flowers in the summer.

Carex moesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
