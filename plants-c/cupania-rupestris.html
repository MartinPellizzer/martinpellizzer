Cupania rupestris

Cupania rupestris, commonly known as the rockcup plant, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in woodland edges and open areas. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a rough texture.

Cupania rupestris is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
