Cistus × longifolius var. grosii

Cistus × longifolius var. grosii, commonly known as the Spanish rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features aromatic evergreen leaves with a silver-white underside, and produces showy, pink to reddish flowers in the spring. The plant is well adapted to rocky and dry soils, thriving in full sun. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional herbal medicine and as a source of essential oils.
