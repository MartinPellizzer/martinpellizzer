Calotropis gigantea

Calotropis gigantea, commonly known as the giant milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Africa, southern Asia, and the Middle East.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall, characterized by its thick, woody stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in summer. Its latex sap is milky and toxic, containing various cardiac glycosides, which have been used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in arid and saline environments, making it a hardy species in challenging climates.
