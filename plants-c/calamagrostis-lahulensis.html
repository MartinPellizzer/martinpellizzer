Calamagrostis lahulensis

Calamagrostis lahulensis, commonly known as the Lahul mountain reed, is a perennial grass native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant typically grows in moist, rocky habitats at high altitudes. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of spikelets, which are usually pale green to golden in color.

Calamagrostis lahulensis plays an important role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens.
