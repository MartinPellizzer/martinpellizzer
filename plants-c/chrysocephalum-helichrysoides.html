Chrysocephalum helichrysoides

Chrysocephalum helichrysoides, commonly known as the golden everlasting, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for dry climates.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
