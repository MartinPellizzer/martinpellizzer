Chisocheton sapindinus

Chisocheton sapindinus, commonly known as the Philippine native tree, is a member of the Sapindaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 15 meters tall, with a straight trunk and a rounded crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, orange-colored fruits that contain several seeds.

This plant is often found in lowland and montane forests across the Philippines.
