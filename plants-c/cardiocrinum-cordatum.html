Cardiocrinum cordatum

Cardiocrinum cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white to pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and prefers well-drained, loamy soils in woodland or rocky habitats. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and lanceolate leaves with a velvety texture.

The flowers are often fragrant and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
