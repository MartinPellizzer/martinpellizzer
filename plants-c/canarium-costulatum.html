Canarium costulatum

Canarium costulatum, commonly known as the white sago palm, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into woody, spiky fruits. The seeds are enclosed in a fleshy, orange aril, which is edible and often used in local cuisines.

It is also valued for its ornamental appearance and has been cultivated for both ornamental and practical purposes.
