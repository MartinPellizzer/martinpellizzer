Centaurea zuccariniana

Centaurea zuccariniana, commonly known as the Zuccarini's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers with purple markings. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, seed-like fruits called cypselae.

Centaurea zuccariniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
