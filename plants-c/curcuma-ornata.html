Curcuma ornata

Curcuma ornata, commonly known as the golden ginger, is a rhizomatous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family and is characterized by its ornate, fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tubular blooms. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Curcuma ornata is valued both for its aesthetic appeal and its medicinal properties.
