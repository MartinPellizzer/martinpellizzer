Cinnamomum filipedicellatum

Cinnamomum filipedicellatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cinnamon tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces dark brown, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and bark, are valued for their fragrance and medicinal properties.

This species is often cultivated for both ornamental and commercial purposes.
