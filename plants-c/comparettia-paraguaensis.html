Comparettia paraguaensis

Comparettia paraguaensis, commonly known as the Paraguay orchid, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its intricate, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually broad and strap-like, and it produces showy, colorful blooms during the warmer months.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
