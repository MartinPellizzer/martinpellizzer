Crataegus megapulchra

Crataegus megapulchra, commonly known as the giant hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in the Appalachian region. The plant is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and the plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub.

Crataegus megapulchra is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
