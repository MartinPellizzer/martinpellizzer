Calamintha purpurascens

Calamintha purpurascens, commonly known as purple honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is known for its aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Calamintha purpurascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for its fragrance and visual interest.
