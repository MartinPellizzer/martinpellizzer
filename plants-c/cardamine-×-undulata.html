Cardamine × undulata

Cardamine × undulata, commonly known as the undulating watercress, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often submerged or floating in aquatic environments. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in water or moist soil.

Cardamine × undulata is often found in streams, ponds, and other freshwater habitats where it plays a role in aquatic ecosystems.
