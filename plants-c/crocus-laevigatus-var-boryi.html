Crocus laevigatus var. boryi

Crocus laevigatus var. boryi, commonly known as the Boryi crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, often with a distinctive violet or purple stripe. The plant produces small, ovoid bulbs that are adapted for survival in dry, rocky environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
