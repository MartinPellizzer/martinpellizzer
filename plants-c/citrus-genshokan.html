Citrus genshokan

Citrus genshokan, commonly known as the Japanese citrus or kumquat, is a member of the Rutaceae family.

It is a small evergreen tree native to eastern Asia, particularly Japan and China. The plant produces small, orange-colored fruits that are typically eaten whole, including the peel. The leaves are dark green, glossy, and elliptical in shape.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruits.
