Carex glauca f. thuringiaca

Carex glauca f. thuringiaca, commonly known as the Thuringian blue sedge, is a variant of the blue sedge species native to Europe.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance and dense, cylindrical inflorescences. The leaves are typically bluish-green, with a waxy coating that gives the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
