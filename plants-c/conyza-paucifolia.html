Conyza paucifolia

Conyza paucifolia, commonly known as the slender weed or smallflower blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Africa, particularly South Africa.

It typically grows in open, disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields, and can be found in various habitats including grasslands and shrublands. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Conyza paucifolia is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often seen as a weed in agricultural settings.

It is also known for its ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds, contributing to its wide distribution.
