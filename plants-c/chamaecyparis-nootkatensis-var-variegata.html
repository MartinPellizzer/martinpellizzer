Chamaecyparis nootkatensis var. variegata

Chamaecyparis nootkatensis var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated Alaska cedar, is a cultivar of the western red cedar.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, which features a mix of yellow, white, and green colors on its needle-like leaves. This evergreen shrub or small tree is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique coloration and hardy nature make it a valuable addition to landscaping in cooler climates.
