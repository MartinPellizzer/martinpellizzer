Commiphora mahafaliensis

Commiphora mahafaliensis, commonly known as the Mahafali resin tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap, which has been traditionally used in local medicine and as a source of incense. The plant typically has smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves with a leathery texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules that contain several elliptical seeds.

This species is valued for its ecological role in Madagascar's biodiversity and its potential pharmacological properties.
