Cypripedium × coppinianum

Cypripedium × coppinianum, commonly known as the Coppin's lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers with a pouch-like lip that resembles a slipper. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded forest environments, often on the forest floor. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it produces a single, large flower per stem.

This hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
