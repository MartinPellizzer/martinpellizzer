Carex longirostris var. minor

Carex longirostris var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and distinctive inflorescence. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
