Clerodendrum hexangulare

Clerodendrum hexangulare, commonly known as the six-angled clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that are typically red or orange in color. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and prominent veins. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic and medicinal plants.
