Commicarpus ambiguus

Commicarpus ambiguus, commonly known as the Indian gooseberry or amla, is a deciduous shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, green, fleshy fruits that turn orange-red when mature. These fruits are rich in vitamin C and are widely used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
