Cargilla

Cargilla, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a fast-growing deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets that turn vibrant yellow in the autumn. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, followed by elongated pods containing seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Its rapid growth and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for parks and residential areas.
