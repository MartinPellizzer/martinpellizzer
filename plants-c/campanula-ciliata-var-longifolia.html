Campanula ciliata var. longifolia

Campanula ciliata var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with finely toothed margins. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Campanula ciliata var. longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
