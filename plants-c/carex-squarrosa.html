Carex squarrosa

Carex squarrosa, commonly known as the woolly sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Carex squarrosa typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
