Cereus polyrhizus

Cereus polyrhizus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and ability to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and close by morning. Its stems are cylindrical and often branched, with areoles that bear both spines and flowers.

Cereus polyrhizus is adapted to store water in its thick, fleshy stems, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
