Cocculus peltatus

Cocculus peltatus, commonly known as the hooded morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, showy flowers that resemble a hood. The plant produces cylindrical, spiny fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other morning glory species.
