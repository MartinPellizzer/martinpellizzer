Carduus platylepis

Carduus platylepis, commonly known as the broadleaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a sturdy, spiny stem with deeply lobed, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, forming a spherical cluster that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Carduus platylepis is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
