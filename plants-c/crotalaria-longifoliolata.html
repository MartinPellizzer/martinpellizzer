Crotalaria longifoliolata

Crotalaria longifoliolata, commonly known as the long-leaved crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces clusters of yellow or orange flowers. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. It has been used traditionally in some regions for medicinal purposes.

However, it contains toxic alkaloids and should be handled with caution.
