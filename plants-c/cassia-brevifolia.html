Cassia brevifolia

Cassia brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2-5 meters. The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical spikes, which bloom during the summer months. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Cassia brevifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
