Cassia desvauxii var. brevipes

Cassia desvauxii var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed cassia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Cassia desvauxii var. brevipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
