Centaurea scabra

Centaurea scabra, commonly known as the rough centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has hairy, often prickly stems and leaves, which contribute to its common name. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Centaurea scabra is also known for its medicinal properties, with historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
