Calliandra stenophylla

Calliandra stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences composed of numerous small, showy flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant pink or red flowers.

Additionally, it has nitrogen-fixing capabilities, making it useful in soil enrichment and agroforestry systems.
