Clerodendrum bukobense

Clerodendrum bukobense, commonly known as the Bukobense flame bush, is a flowering plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils or along riverbanks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
