Capsella bursa-pastoris provincialis

Capsella bursa-pastoris provincialis, commonly known as the common chickweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems and small, white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in lawns, fields, and along roadsides.

Its leaves are alternate, hairy, and have a slightly succulent texture, making it identifiable in various habitats.
