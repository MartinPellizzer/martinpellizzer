Carex sparsiflora var. gruetteri

Carex sparsiflora var. gruetteri, commonly known as the Gruetter's sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. This variety thrives in wet to moist environments, such as marshes and pond edges. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The plant plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic insects.
