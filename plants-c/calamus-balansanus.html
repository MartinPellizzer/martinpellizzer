Calamus balansanus

Calamus balansanus, commonly known as the Balansan canescent, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender rhizomes and large, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spadices, which are typical of the Araceae family.

Calamus balansanus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some cultures.
