Crotalaria mysorensis var. pauciflora

Crotalaria mysorensis var. pauciflora, commonly known as the small-flowered crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

The species is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.
