Clinopodium chinense var. grandiflorum

Clinopodium chinense var. grandiflorum, commonly known as Chinese mountain betony, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and have a mint-like aroma. The plant produces showy blue to purple flowers in whorled clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valued species in both traditional medicine and ornamental horticulture.
