Cestrum viridiflorum

Cestrum viridiflorum, commonly known as the greenflower trumpetflower, is a species of flowering plant in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is native to Central and South America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its vibrant green flowers, which are tubular and often have a yellow or white corolla. Its leaves are typically ovate and glossy, and it produces small, red berries that contain the seeds.

Cestrum viridiflorum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
