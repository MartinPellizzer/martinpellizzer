Clerodendrum kaempferi var. album

Clerodendrum kaempferi var. album, commonly known as the White Flame Clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant features opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green with a slightly glossy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its showy blooms.

The species is also valued for its air-purifying properties and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
