Cola lasiantha

Cola lasiantha, commonly known as the African cola tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Sterculiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly West and Central Africa. The plant is characterized by its large, compound leaves and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of caffeine.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic appeal.
