Cotyledon campanulata

Cotyledon campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped cotyledon, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are often green with red or purple veins, and it produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink or purple. The plant is known for its ability to store water in its leaves, making it drought-tolerant. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Cotyledon campanulata is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
