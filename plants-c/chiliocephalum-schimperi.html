Chiliocephalum schimperi

Chiliocephalum schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers with a prominent central disk. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological significance in its native habitat.
