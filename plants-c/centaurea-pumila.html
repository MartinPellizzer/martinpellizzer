Centaurea pumila

Centaurea pumila, commonly known as the dwarf centaury, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical heads, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Centaurea pumila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive blooms.
