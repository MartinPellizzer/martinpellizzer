Campanula acuminata

Campanula acuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lanceolate, often with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
