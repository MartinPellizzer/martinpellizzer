Commiphora razakamalalae

Commiphora razakamalalae, commonly known as the Razakamalala myrrh, is a rare and endemic species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic resinous exudates, which are harvested for their medicinal and commercial value. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a unique morphology with small, leathery leaves. Its ecological significance lies in its role as a keystone species in its native ecosystem, supporting local biodiversity.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, it is considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
