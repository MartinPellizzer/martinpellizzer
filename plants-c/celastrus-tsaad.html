Celastrus tsaad

Celastrus tsaad, commonly known as the African bindweed, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features opposite, elliptic leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by bright orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The berries are toxic to humans and animals, making it a plant to be handled with care.
