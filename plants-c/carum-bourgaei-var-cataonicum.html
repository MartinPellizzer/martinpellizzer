Carum bourgaei var. cataonicum

Carum bourgaei var. cataonicum, commonly known as the Cataonic caraway, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in umbels. The plant produces tiny, oval-shaped seeds that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. Its leaves are aromatic and have a subtle, caraway-like scent.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in apothecary practices.
