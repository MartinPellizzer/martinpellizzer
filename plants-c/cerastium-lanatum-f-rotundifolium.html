Cerastium lanatum f. rotundifolium

Cerastium lanatum f. rotundifolium, commonly known as the round-leaf mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The leaves are rounded and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract beneficial insects.
