Carex rossii var. brevipes

Carex rossii var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed sedge, is a perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as bogs, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in dense colonies.

Botanically, it is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
