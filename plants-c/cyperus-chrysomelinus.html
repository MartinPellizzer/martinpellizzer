Cyperus chrysomelinus

Cyperus chrysomelinus, commonly known as the golden flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, sword-shaped leaves. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or brown in color.

Cyperus chrysomelinus is often found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments.
