Clematis ranunculoides

Clematis ranunculoides, commonly known as the yellow clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often growing along stream banks or in woodland edges.

Clematis ranunculoides is valued for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in suitable conditions.
