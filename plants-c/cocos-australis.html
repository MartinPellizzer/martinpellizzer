Cocos australis

Cocos australis, commonly known as the southern coconut tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Africa, including Madagascar, South Africa, and parts of the Arabian Peninsula. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its fruit, the coconut, is a drupe that contains a single seed surrounded by a fibrous husk.

Cocos australis is adapted to coastal environments and can tolerate salt spray and sandy soils.
