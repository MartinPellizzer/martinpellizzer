Clerodendrum subpeltatum

Clerodendrum subpeltatum, commonly known as the Red-flowered Clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences of red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically tubular with five lobes and are pollinated by insects.

The plant is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
