Carduus rechingerianus

Carduus rechingerianus, commonly known as Rechinger's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

The flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
