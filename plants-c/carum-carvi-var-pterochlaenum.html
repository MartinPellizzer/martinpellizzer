Carum carvi var. pterochlaenum

Carum carvi var. pterochlaenum, commonly known as the winged caraway, is a variant of the caraway plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Apiaceae family.
