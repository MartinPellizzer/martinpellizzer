Carex annectens var. ambigua

Carex annectens var. ambigua, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, which contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. This variety is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. It has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense colonies.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
