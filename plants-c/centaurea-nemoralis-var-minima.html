Centaurea nemoralis var. minima

Centaurea nemoralis var. minima, commonly known as the dwarf wood centaury, is a small, herbaceous perennial native to Europe.

It typically grows in woodland and scrub habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features upright, branched stems covered in narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are bright yellow with a distinctive blue or purple central disk, blooming from June to August.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its delicate appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
