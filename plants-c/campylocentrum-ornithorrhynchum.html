Campylocentrum ornithorrhynchum

Campylocentrum ornithorrhynchum, commonly known as the bird-beak hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, bird-like flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has rough, hairy stems and elliptical leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Campylocentrum ornithorrhynchum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
