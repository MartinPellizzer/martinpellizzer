Cyrtomium obliquum

Cyrtomium obliquum, commonly known as the zigzag holly fern, is a species of evergreen fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, pinnately compound fronds. The fronds are typically arching and have a distinctive zigzag pattern along their margins, which gives the plant its common name. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
