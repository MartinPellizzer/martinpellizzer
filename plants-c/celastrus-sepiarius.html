Celastrus sepiarius

Celastrus sepiarius, commonly known as the sea grape or sea bindweed, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Europe and North Africa.

It is a woody vine characterized by its climbing habit and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in saline environments and is often found growing along rocky shores and dunes.

Its adaptability to coastal conditions makes it an important component of maritime ecosystems.
