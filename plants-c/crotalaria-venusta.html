Crotalaria venusta

Crotalaria venusta, commonly known as the beautiful crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range from yellow to orange in color. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with compound leaves and spiny pods. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The plant is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in certain agricultural contexts.
