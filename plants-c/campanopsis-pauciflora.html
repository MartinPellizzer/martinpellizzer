Campanopsis pauciflora

Campanopsis pauciflora, commonly known as the few-flowered bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Campanopsis pauciflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
