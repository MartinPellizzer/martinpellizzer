Carex japonica var. minor

Carex japonica var. minor, commonly known as Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and rushes. This variety is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets of pale green to brown flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
