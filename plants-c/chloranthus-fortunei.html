Chloranthus fortunei

Chloranthus fortunei, commonly known as the Chinese sandwort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Chloranthaceae, which is characterized by its unique floral structures and lack of true leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in umbels, often producing a subtle fragrance.

Chloranthus fortunei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
