Chirita rotundata

Chirita rotundata, commonly known as the round-leaved chirita, is a species of flowering plant in the Gesneriaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

Chirita rotundata is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
