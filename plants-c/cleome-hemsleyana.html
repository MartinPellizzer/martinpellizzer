Cleome hemsleyana

Cleome hemsleyana, commonly known as the "spider flower," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that resemble spiders, hence its common name. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cleome hemsleyana is a member of the Cleomaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
