Cirsium hypoleucum

Cirsium hypoleucum, commonly known as the white thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has deeply lobed, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

Cirsium hypoleucum is often found in disturbed areas and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
