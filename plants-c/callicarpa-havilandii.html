Callicarpa havilandii

Callicarpa havilandii, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of vibrant purple to red berries that appear in the fall, making it a visually striking plant. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, often found in woodland edges and moist soils.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental berries and is also used in traditional medicine.
