Colubrina triflora

Colubrina triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered colubrina, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in wetlands and along stream banks. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Colubrina triflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist soil conditions.
