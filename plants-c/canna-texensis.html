Canna texensis

Canna texensis, commonly known as the Texas cannas, is a striking herbaceous perennial native to the southern United States.

It features large, colorful, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 3 feet in length. The plant produces showy, spadix-like flowers surrounded by vibrant bracts, often in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Canna texensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of garden settings.
