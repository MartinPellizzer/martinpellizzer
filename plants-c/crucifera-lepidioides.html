Crucifera lepidioides

Crucifera lepidioides, commonly known as the woolly mustard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, white or yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. It produces small, round pods that contain several seeds.

Due to its adaptability, it is often considered a weed in agricultural areas.
