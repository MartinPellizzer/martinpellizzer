Calystegia Sepium Subsp. Binghamiae

Calystegia sepium subsp. binghamiae is a lesser-known variant of the bindweed family known for its unique floral characteristics. Native to specific regions in South America, this subspecies thrives in moist, sunny environments. It features heart-shaped leaves and trumpet-like flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. Though often mistaken for its more common relatives, it holds ecological significance in its native habitat. Its rarity makes it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
