Cordia mirabiliflora

Cordia mirabiliflora, commonly known as the miracle-flower cordia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly waxy surface.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds.
