Ceanothus confusus

Ceanothus confusus, commonly known as the bushy ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping. Its leaves are typically leathery and have a fragrant scent when crushed.

Ceanothus confusus is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
