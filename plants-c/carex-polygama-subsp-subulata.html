Carex polygama subsp. subulata

Carex polygama subsp.

subulata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial sedge native to North America. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks.

The plant has grass-like leaves that are narrow and pointed, with a bluish-green color. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various insects and birds.
