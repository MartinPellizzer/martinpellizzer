Calligonum persicum

Calligonum persicum, commonly known as Persian sandmat, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or green. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in sandy and saline soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

Calligonum persicum is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
