Ceropegia siamensis

Ceropegia siamensis, commonly known as the Siam moon vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, pale green to white flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ceropegia siamensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
