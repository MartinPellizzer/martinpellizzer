Clerodendrum premnoides

Clerodendrum premnoides, commonly known as the Indian balsam, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. It produces small, round fruits that contain seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Clerodendrum premnoides is also used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
