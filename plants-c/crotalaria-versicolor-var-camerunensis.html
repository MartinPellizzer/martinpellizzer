Crotalaria versicolor var. camerunensis

Crotalaria versicolor var. camerunensis, commonly known as the Cameroon false flax, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow and purple flowers. The plant has palmately divided leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils, and it plays a role in nitrogen fixation due to its symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria.
