Cassia exsudans

Cassia exsudans, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in pendulous clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are often trifoliate, and its bark is smooth with a distinctive reddish-brown color. Cassia exsudans is also known for its ability to exude a yellowish resin, which has been used traditionally in various applications.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
