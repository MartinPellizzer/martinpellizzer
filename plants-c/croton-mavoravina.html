Croton mavoravina

Croton mavoravina, commonly known as the Mavoravina croton, is a species of flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often colorful foliage. The plant typically has alternate, simple leaves that can vary in shape and texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in cyathia, which are typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

Croton mavoravina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique and vibrant leaf patterns.
