Campomanesia corymbosa

Campomanesia corymbosa, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces bright red, juicy berries that are edible and often used in culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is valued for its ornamental beauty and fruit production.

The tree is also known for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes cultivated for its medicinal properties.
