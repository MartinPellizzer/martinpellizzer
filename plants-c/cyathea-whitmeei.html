Cyathea whitmeei

Cyathea whitmeei, commonly known as the Whitmee's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest understories. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its tall, fibrous trunk, while the fronds are responsible for photosynthesis.

Cyathea whitmeei plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
