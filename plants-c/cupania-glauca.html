Cupania glauca

Cupania glauca, commonly known as the white-leaf cupania, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its small, white or pale green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Cupania glauca is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.
