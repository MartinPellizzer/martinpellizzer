Cereus pteranthus

Cereus pteranthus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a succulent cactus characterized by its spiny stems and ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems. The plant is known for its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and close by morning. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique blooming pattern makes it a popular choice for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
