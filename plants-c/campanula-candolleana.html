Campanula candolleana

Campanula candolleana, commonly known as the Candollean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features erect stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Campanula candolleana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
