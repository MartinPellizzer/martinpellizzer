Commiphora gariepensis

Commiphora gariepensis, commonly known as the Gariep myrrh tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous bark that exudes a fragrant gum when damaged. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are leathery and oval-shaped, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous, followed by small, woody fruits.

The resin produced by this plant has been used traditionally for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
