Convolvulus stans

Convolvulus stans, commonly known as the everlasting vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and distinctive, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often hairy, contributing to its adaptability in arid environments.

Convolvulus stans is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive blooms and resilience.
