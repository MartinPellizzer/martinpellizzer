Canna glauca var. flava

Canna glauca var. flava, commonly known as the yellow cannonball plant, is a striking tropical perennial native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a waxy texture. The plant produces showy, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by bright yellow bracts, which are often mistaken for flowers. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

This ornamental plant is valued for its bold foliage and long-lasting, eye-catching blooms.
