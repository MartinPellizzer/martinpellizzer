Cressa villosa

Cressa villosa, commonly known as hairy cress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe, Asia, and Africa.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes, and it has hairy stems and leaves.

Cressa villosa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of light conditions.
