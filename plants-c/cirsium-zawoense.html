Cirsium zawoense

Cirsium zawoense, commonly known as the Zawoens thistle, is a flowering plant species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple-blue flower heads that bloom in the summer months. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower structure. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cirsium zawoense is often found in rocky or disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in temperate climates.
