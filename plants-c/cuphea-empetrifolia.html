Cuphea empetrifolia

Cuphea empetrifolia, commonly known as the stonecrop cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, somewhat succulent leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink, attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or containers.

Cuphea empetrifolia is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
