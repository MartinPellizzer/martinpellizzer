Crocus vernus var. grandis

Crocus vernus var. grandis, commonly known as the giant crocus, is a striking perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It produces large, showy flowers with vivid violet petals and a prominent yellow center, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in woodland areas and gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its botanical name reflects its close relation to the common crocus, with "grandis" highlighting its larger size compared to other varieties.
