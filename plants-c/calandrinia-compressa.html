Calandrinia compressa

Calandrinia compressa, commonly known as the dwarf portulaca, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing annual herb that thrives in arid and sandy environments. The plant produces small, vibrant flowers that range in color from pink to red, typically blooming in the morning. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water, which helps it survive in dry conditions.

Calandrinia compressa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
