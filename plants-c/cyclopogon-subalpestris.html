Cyclopogon subalpestris

Cyclopogon subalpestris, commonly known as the alpine cyclopogon, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, commonly known as grasses, and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue florets. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

Cyclopogon subalpestris is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, including strong winds and low temperatures.
