Carex ericetorum var. strictifolia

Carex ericetorum var. strictifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils in woodland or heathland environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
