Carex obtusangula

Carex obtusangula, commonly known as the blunt-angled sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often green to brown in color.

Carex obtusangula plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates and small vertebrates.
