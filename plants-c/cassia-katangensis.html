Cassia katangensis

Cassia katangensis, commonly known as Katanga cassia, is a flowering plant native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Cassia katangensis is typically found in moist woodland and swampy areas, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
