Cassia calycioides

Cassia calycioides, commonly known as the African cassia, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces yellow flowers that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and serrated.

Cassia calycioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil types.
