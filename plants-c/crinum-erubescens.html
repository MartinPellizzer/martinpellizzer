Crinum erubescens

Crinum erubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered crinum, is a tropical plant native to parts of South America.

It is a member of the Amaryllis family and is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Crinum erubescens is also known for its strong fragrance, which can be both pleasant and overwhelming.
