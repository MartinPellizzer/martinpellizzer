Cassia chrysocarpa

Cassia chrysocarpa, commonly known as golden pod cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with narrow, lance-shaped leaflets and yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that turn bright yellow when mature, containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Cassia chrysocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
