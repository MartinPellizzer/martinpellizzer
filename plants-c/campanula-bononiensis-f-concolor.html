Campanula bononiensis f. concolor

Campanula bononiensis f. concolor, commonly known as the concolor bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are ovate to lanceolate, with a slightly hairy texture, and are arranged alternately along the stems. This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to many garden settings.
