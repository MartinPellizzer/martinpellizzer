Convolvulus veneratus

Convolvulus veneratus, commonly known as the sacred convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing vine that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a hardy species.

The flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
