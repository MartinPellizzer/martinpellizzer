Calandrinia picta

Calandrinia picta, commonly known as the painted purslane, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom throughout the growing season. Calandrinia picta is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid environments due to its drought tolerance.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and container planting.
