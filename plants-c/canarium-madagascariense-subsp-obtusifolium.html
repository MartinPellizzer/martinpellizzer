Canarium madagascariense subsp. obtusifolium

Canarium madagascariense subsp.

obtusifolium, commonly known as the Madagascar nut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar. It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are typically obtuse at the tip.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and potential economic importance, it is of interest for conservation and horticultural studies.
