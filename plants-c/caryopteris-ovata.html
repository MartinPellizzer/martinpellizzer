Caryopteris ovata

Caryopteris ovata, commonly known as blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, upright stems and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Caryopteris ovata is well-suited for gardens and landscapes due to its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
