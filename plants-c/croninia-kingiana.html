Croninia kingiana

Croninia kingiana, commonly known as the King's Crown, is a striking South African plant belonging to the Aizoaceae family.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange or yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, forming a showy crown-like arrangement. The plant features succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white powdery coating, known as farina. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, Croninia kingiana is both ornamental and ecologically valuable in arid environments.
