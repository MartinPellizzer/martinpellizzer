Calamus hirsutus subsp. minor

Calamus hirsutus subsp.

minor, commonly known as the lesser hairy calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae. It is characterized by its slender, creeping rhizomes and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spadices, typical of the Araceae family. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in tropical horticulture.
