Callitriche brutia var. naftolskyi

Callitriche brutia var. naftolskyi, commonly known as the Naftolsky's water starwort, is a small aquatic plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and ditches. The plant has small, floating or submerged leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. Its flowers are tiny and inconspicuous, usually greenish-white in color, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to various water conditions and its role in aquatic ecosystems.
