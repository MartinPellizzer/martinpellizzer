Ceratostylis crassipetala

Ceratostylis crassipetala, commonly known as the broad-leaved horned-stylis, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a terrestrial herb with a short, creeping rhizome and produces a single, erect stem bearing a single, showy flower. The plant has elliptic to ovate leaves that are usually glabrous and have a waxy surface. The inflorescence is a single, terminal spike with a single flower that has a distinctive, elongated spur.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.
