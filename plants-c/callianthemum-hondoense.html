Callianthemum hondoense

Callianthemum hondoense, commonly known as the Hondo rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the island of Hondo in the Philippines.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, showy flowers and distinctive leaf morphology. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with four petals arranged in a cross shape, a typical feature of the Brassicaceae family.

Callianthemum hondoense is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and potential ecological significance in its native habitat.
