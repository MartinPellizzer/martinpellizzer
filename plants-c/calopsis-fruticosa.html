Calopsis fruticosa

Calopsis fruticosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false hibiscus, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Calopsis fruticosa is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts of the plant used in traditional remedies.
