Cleistes brasiliensis

Cleistes brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid or the sacred orchid, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, bell-shaped flowers that are often pale yellow or greenish-white. The plant produces a single, showy flower that remains closed for most of its life cycle, opening only briefly during specific environmental conditions. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Cleistes brasiliensis is also notable for its historical significance in indigenous cultures, where it has been used for spiritual and medicinal purposes.
