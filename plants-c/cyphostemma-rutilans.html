Cyphostemma rutilans

Cyphostemma rutilans, commonly known as the yellow moonvine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a climbing shrub that produces small, yellow flowers and distinctive, elongated fruits. The plant has thick, woody stems and is often found in rocky or dry habitats. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a rough texture.

Cyphostemma rutilans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
