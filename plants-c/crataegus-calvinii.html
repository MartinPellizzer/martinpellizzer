Crataegus calvinii

Crataegus calvinii, commonly known as the California hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.

Crataegus calvinii is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
