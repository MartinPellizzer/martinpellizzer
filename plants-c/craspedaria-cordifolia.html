Craspedaria cordifolia

Craspedaria cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf craspedaria, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically broad and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often blooming in the spring. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded forest understories.

Craspedaria cordifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
