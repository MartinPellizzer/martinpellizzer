Citharexylum hexangulare

Citharexylum hexangulare, commonly known as the six-angled cithara, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Its stems are often woody and can be aromatic, contributing to its use in traditional medicine.

Citharexylum hexangulare is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
