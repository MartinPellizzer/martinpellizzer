Cirsium puniceum

Cirsium puniceum, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its vibrant purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is noted for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
