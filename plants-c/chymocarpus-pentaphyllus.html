Chymocarpus pentaphyllus

Chymocarpus pentaphyllus, commonly known as the narrowleaf false brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale green flowers in late summer. The plant has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil in arid environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its low water requirements and hardy nature, it is often used in ecological restoration projects.
