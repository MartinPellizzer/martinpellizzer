Cocculus nephroia

Cocculus nephroia, commonly known as the devil's backbone, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, twisted stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by spiny, orange-red fruits that contain several seeds. Its roots and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

The botanical features of Cocculus nephroia make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ethnobotanical study.
