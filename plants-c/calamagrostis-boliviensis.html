Calamagrostis boliviensis

Calamagrostis boliviensis, commonly known as the Bolivian feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Bolivia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and long, silky green to silver-blue leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
