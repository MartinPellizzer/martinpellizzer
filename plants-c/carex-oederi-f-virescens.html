Carex oederi f. virescens

Carex oederi f. virescens, commonly known as the green-stem sedges, is a variant of the Carex oederi species, characterized by its distinctively greenish stems.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family, typically found in moist or wetland habitats. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.

Its foliage provides a striking visual contrast in garden settings, especially when paired with other moisture-loving plants.
