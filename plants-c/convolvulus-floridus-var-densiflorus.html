Convolvulus floridus var. densiflorus

Convolvulus floridus var. densiflorus, commonly known as the Florida morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The species produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or violet in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

The plant is part of the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
