Crocus chrysanthus subsp. kesercioglui

Crocus chrysanthus subsp.

kesercioglui, commonly known as the Turkish crocus, is a species native to Turkey and parts of the Balkans. It is a bulbous perennial plant that produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during late winter to early spring.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different flower structure and coloration compared to the typical C. chrysanthus.
