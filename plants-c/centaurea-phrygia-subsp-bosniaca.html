Centaurea phrygia subsp. bosniaca

Centaurea phrygia subsp.

bosniaca, commonly known as the Bosnian knapweed, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flower heads that bloom in summer.

The plant features serrated leaves and produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
