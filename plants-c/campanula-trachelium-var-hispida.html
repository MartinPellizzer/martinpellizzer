Campanula trachelium var. hispida

Campanula trachelium var. hispida, commonly known as the rough bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically hairy and have a rough texture, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
