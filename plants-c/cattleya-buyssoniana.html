Cattleya buyssoniana

Cattleya buyssoniana, commonly known as the Siam orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale pink and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Cattleya buyssoniana is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its showy blooms.
