Camassia scilloides f. variegata

Camassia scilloides f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated camas, is a striking perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings, adding to its ornamental appeal. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or meadow habitats.

Camassia scilloides f. variegata is valued in gardens for its vibrant color and ability to naturalize over time.
