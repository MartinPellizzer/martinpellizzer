Carex physochlaena

Carex physochlaena, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedges, is a species of sedge native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and clustered, spike-like flower structures. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-angled berries.

Carex physochlaena is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
