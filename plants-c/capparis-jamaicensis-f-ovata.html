Capparis jamaicensis f. ovata

Capparis jamaicensis f. ovata, commonly known as the Jamaican caper, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its woody shrub structure with thorny stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically followed by round, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a slightly succulent texture.

This variety is distinguished by its ovate-shaped fruits, which are a key botanical feature.
