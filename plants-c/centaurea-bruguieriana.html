Centaurea bruguieriana

Centaurea bruguieriana, commonly known as the Bruguier's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, while its stems are erect and often branched.

Centaurea bruguieriana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and resilience.
