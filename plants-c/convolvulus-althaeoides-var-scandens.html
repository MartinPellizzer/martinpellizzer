Convolvulus althaeoides var. scandens

Convolvulus althaeoides var. scandens, commonly known as the creeping wood morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that produces small, funnel-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has hairy, ascending stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered with fine, soft hairs.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
