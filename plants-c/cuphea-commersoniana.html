Cuphea commersoniana

Cuphea commersoniana, commonly known as the flamebeard plant, is a flowering shrub native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that resemble flames, giving it its common name. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Cuphea commersoniana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
