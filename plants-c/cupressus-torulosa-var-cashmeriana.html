Cupressus torulosa var. cashmeriana

Cupressus torulosa var. cashmeriana, commonly known as the Kashmir cypress, is a rare and ornamental conifer native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and scale-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spherical cones that are typically reddish-brown in color. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
