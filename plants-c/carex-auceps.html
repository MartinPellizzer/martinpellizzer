Carex auceps

Carex auceps, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly referred to as sedges. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

Carex auceps is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
