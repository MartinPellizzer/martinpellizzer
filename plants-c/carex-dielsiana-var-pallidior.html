Carex dielsiana var. pallidior

Carex dielsiana var. pallidior, commonly known as the pale Carex, is a variant of the Carex dielsiana species native to certain regions of South America.

It is a perennial sedge characterized by its grass-like foliage and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly curled, with a pale green to silver-white coloration that distinguishes it from other Carex species.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native ecosystem.
