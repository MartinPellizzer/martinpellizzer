Canavalia bicarinata var. bicarinata

Canavalia bicarinata var. bicarinata, commonly known as the two-ridged cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with two distinct ridges, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers followed by elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds, which are rich in protein and fiber.

The plant also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
