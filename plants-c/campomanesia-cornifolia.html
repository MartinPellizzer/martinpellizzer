Campomanesia cornifolia

Campomanesia cornifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that turn from green to yellow or red when ripe, containing a sweet, juicy pulp surrounded by a hard, inedible shell. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
