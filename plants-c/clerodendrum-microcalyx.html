Clerodendrum microcalyx

Clerodendrum microcalyx, commonly known as the small-leaved glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the summer months. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy inflorescences and adaptability to various soil types.

It is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in warm climates.
