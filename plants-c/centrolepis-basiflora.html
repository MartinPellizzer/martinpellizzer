Centrolepis basiflora

Centrolepis basiflora, commonly known as the mountain sand mat, is a unique member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming plant characterized by its compact, grass-like appearance. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered at the base of the stems.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
