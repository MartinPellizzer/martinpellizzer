Chironia gracilis var. macrocalyx

Chironia gracilis var. macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered slender chironia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with a distinctive calyx that is larger than the typical variety. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
