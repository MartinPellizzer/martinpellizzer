Cuphea ingrata var. grandifolia

Cuphea ingrata var. grandifolia, commonly known as the largeflower cuphea, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features striking, tubular, raspberry-red flowers that bloom in spikes during the summer months. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant flowers and compact growth habit.
