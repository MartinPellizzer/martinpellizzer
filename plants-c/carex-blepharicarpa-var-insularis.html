Carex blepharicarpa var. insularis

Carex blepharicarpa var. insularis, commonly known as the island eye-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain island regions in the Pacific.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along the edges of freshwater bodies. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, with small, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is notable for its unique leaf morphology, featuring a distinctive eye-like marking on the leaf blades.
