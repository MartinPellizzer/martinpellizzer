Carex aematorhyncha var. aematorhyncha

Carex aematorhyncha var. aematorhyncha, commonly known as the glossy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds and streams. It produces small, spadix-like inflorescences that are often hidden among its foliage, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
