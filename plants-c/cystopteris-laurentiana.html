Cystopteris laurentiana

Cystopteris laurentiana, commonly known as the Laurentian water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to North America.

It thrives in freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant has finely divided, feathery fronds that are typically submerged or floating. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Cystopteris laurentiana plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygenation for various aquatic organisms.
