Cleome glaucii

Cleome glaucii, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 feet and features slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly serrated edge, while the flowers are borne in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Cleome glaucii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
