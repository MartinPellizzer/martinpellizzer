Calycanthus pensylvanicus

Calycanthus pensylvanicus, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features aromatic, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy on top. Its bark is smooth and reddish-gray, and it produces small, orange-red fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Calycanthus pensylvanicus is valued in gardens for its fragrance and ornamental appeal.
