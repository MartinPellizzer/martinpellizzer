Chlorophytum longissimum

Chlorophytum longissimum, commonly known as the snake plant, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and long, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal water, making it an ideal houseplant. Its leaves are typically a deep green color, and it produces small, white flowers on long, slender stalks.

Chlorophytum longissimum is also noted for its air-purifying qualities, enhancing indoor air quality.
