Croton nervosus

Croton nervosus, commonly known as the veined croton, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves with prominent veins that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Croton nervosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage.
