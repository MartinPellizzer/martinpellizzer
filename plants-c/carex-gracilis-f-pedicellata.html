Carex gracilis f. pedicellata

Carex gracilis f. pedicellata, commonly known as the slender sedge with pedicellate inflorescence, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and a distinctive, elongated inflorescence composed of small, clustered spikelets. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands, stream banks, and woodland areas. Its flowers are usually unisexual, with separate male and female structures, and are pollinated by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
