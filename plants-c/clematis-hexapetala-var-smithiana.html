Clematis hexapetala var. smithiana

Clematis hexapetala var. smithiana, commonly known as the Smith's Clematis, is a perennial vine native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has compound leaves with five to seven oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, round berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate, lace-like foliage and showy blooms.
