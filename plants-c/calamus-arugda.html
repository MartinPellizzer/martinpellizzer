Calamus arugda

Calamus arugda, commonly known as sweet flag, is a perennial aquatic plant native to wetlands and marshy areas in Asia and Africa.

It has long, narrow, grass-like leaves that often grow in dense clusters, and it produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is characterized by its aromatic rhizomes, which have been used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent. It thrives in shallow water and can tolerate a variety of soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
