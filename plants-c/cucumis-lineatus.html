Cucumis lineatus

Cucumis lineatus, commonly known as the horned cucumber, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, horn-like fruits that are typically green and covered in small, raised bumps. The plant belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits such as cucumbers and melons. Its leaves are broad and lobed, while its flowers are typically yellow with five petals.

The fruit is edible, though often used as a decorative or ornamental plant due to its unique appearance.
