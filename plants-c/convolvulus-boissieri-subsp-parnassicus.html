Convolvulus boissieri subsp. parnassicus

Convolvulus boissieri subsp.

parnassicus, commonly known as the Parnassian bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans and parts of Turkey. It is a perennial species that typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments.

The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are usually pale purple to white in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its stems are twining and can climb or trail along the ground, while its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
