Chamaecytisus albus subsp. leucanthus

Chamaecytisus albus subsp.

leucanthus, commonly known as the white broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has slender, arching branches covered in small, leathery leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and rocky environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
