Croton siamensis

Croton siamensis, commonly known as the Siam croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that change with the seasons, often displaying shades of red, orange, and yellow. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, spiny fruits.

Croton siamensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
