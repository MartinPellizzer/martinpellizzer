Cymbidium lancifolium

Cymbidium lancifolium, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a species native to eastern Asia, particularly China and Japan.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with a distinctive red or purple lip. It thrives in cool, humid climates and requires well-drained, acidic soil for optimal growth.

Cymbidium lancifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a popular choice in traditional Chinese gardens.
