Cinnamomum acutatum

Cinnamomum acutatum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed cinnamon tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that contain aromatic seeds. Its essential oil, derived from the leaves and bark, is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses.

The tree is also appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
