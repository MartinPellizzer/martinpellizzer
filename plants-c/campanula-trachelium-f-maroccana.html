Campanula trachelium f. maroccana

Campanula trachelium f. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan bellflower, is a botanical variant of the common bluebell.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, preferring sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in rock gardens and alpine settings.
