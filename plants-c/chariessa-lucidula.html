Chariessa lucidula

Chariessa lucidula, commonly known as the glossy chariessa, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are often shiny and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a popular specimen in botanical gardens and for ecological studies.
