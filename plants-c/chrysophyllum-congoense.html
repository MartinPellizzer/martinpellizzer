Chrysophyllum congoense

Chrysophyllum congoense, commonly known as the African cherry or Congo cherimoya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree produces large, round fruits with a thick, leathery skin and a creamy, sweet pulp containing numerous black seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and has limited commercial use due to its challenging cultivation requirements.
