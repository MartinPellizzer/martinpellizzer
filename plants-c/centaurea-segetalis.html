Centaurea segetalis

Centaurea segetalis, commonly known as the field centaury, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, branched stems and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Centaurea segetalis is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides, where it thrives in sunny conditions.
