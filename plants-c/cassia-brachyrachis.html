Cassia brachyrachis

Cassia brachyrachis, commonly known as the short-stemmed cassia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Cassia brachyrachis thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to various climatic conditions.
