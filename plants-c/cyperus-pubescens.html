Cyperus pubescens

Cyperus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Cyperus pubescens typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, making it common in marshy areas and along waterways.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge family.
