Carex densicaespitosa

Carex densicaespitosa, commonly known as dense spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, cylindrical inflorescence composed of numerous spikelets, which are typically green to brown in color. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually arranged in a tight rosette at the base. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex densicaespitosa plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and stabilizing soil.
