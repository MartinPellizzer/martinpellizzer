Calamagrostis megapotamica

Calamagrostis megapotamica, commonly known as the South American feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces feathery plumes of small, spikelet-based flowers, which are typically golden or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and riverbanks, and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Calamagrostis megapotamica is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
