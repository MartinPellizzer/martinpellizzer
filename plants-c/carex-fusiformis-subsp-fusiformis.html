Carex fusiformis subsp. fusiformis

Carex fusiformis subsp.

fusiformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It typically grows in moist to wet soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

The plant has cylindrical, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and slightly glossy. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike of small, three-ranked brownish flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
