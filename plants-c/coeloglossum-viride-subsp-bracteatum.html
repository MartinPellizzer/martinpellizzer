Coeloglossum viride subsp. bracteatum

Coeloglossum viride subsp.

bracteatum, commonly known as the green hawksbeard, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a single, elongated inflorescence, which blooms from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and role in supporting local pollinator populations.
