Cyperus aster

Cyperus aster, commonly known as the star sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in late summer. Cyperus aster typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a useful indicator of water quality.
