Convolvulus cateniflorus

Convolvulus cateniflorus, commonly known as the chainflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with slender stems that can reach up to 2 meters in length. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in clusters along the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
