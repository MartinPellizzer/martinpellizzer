Carludovica pygmaea

Carludovica pygmaea, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower or dwarf sunflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America. The plant features bright yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk, giving it a sunflower-like appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its compact size and vibrant blooms make it an attractive addition to both ornamental and native plant landscapes.
