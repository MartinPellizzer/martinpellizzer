Cyperus nanilensis

Cyperus nanilensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, making it an important component of aquatic ecosystems. Cyperus nanilensis produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often submerged or partially submerged in water.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
