Centranthus calcitrapae var. pinnatipartitus

Centranthus calcitrapae var. pinnatipartitus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved red campion, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright, branching stems that bear clusters of pink to red, five-petaled flowers. The leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a distinctive pinnate division, giving it a finely textured appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
