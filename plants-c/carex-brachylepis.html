Carex brachylepis

Carex brachylepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. It has slender, triangular stems and produces small, clustered flowers in early to mid-summer.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
