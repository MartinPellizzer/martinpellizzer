Croton evansianus

Croton evansianus, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, colorful bracts that range from red to yellow, often mistaken for flowers. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually hairy leaves that vary in shape. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by three-winged fruits.

Croton evansianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
