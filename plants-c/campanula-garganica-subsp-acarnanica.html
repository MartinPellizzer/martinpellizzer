Campanula garganica subsp. acarnanica

Campanula garganica subsp.

acarnanica, commonly known as the Acarnanica bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in mountainous regions. It has lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and arranged in a basal rosette.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
