Capsicodendron pimenteira

Capsicodendron pimenteira, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the nightshade family (Solanaceae) and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy red flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are orange to red in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its invasive nature in some regions, it is sometimes considered a problematic species.
