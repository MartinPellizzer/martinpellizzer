Chlorospatha planadensis

Chlorospatha planadensis, commonly known as the golden flatleaf, is a rare flowering plant native to the humid lowland forests of Southeastern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy, light green leaves that reflect sunlight, a feature that aids in reducing water loss in its hot, arid habitat. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom during the rainy season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is fibrous and shallow, allowing it to absorb nutrients from the nutrient-poor soil.

Due to habitat destruction, Chlorospatha planadensis is considered endangered and is protected in several national parks.
