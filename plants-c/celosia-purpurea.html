Celosia purpurea

Celosia purpurea, commonly known as the flame flower, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, plume-like inflorescences that come in shades of red, orange, and purple. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and long-lasting blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Celosia purpurea is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
