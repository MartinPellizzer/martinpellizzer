Cortaderia modesta f. ramosa

Cortaderia modesta f. ramosa, commonly known as the silver grass, is a cultivar of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its feathery, silver-blue plumes that give it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 4 meters and has a clumping growth habit. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and covered in a fine, silvery coating.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
