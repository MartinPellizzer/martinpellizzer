Cassia huancabambae

Cassia huancabambae, commonly known as Huancabamba cassia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to the high-altitude environment. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and contributing to soil stabilization.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
