Clematis × clocheton

Clematis × clocheton, commonly known as the Clocheton Clematis, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Clematis viticella and Clematis alpina.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to climb, making it suitable for trellises or fences. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is dark green and often has a slightly hairy texture.

Clematis × clocheton is relatively hardy and adaptable, thriving in a variety of soil types and light conditions.
