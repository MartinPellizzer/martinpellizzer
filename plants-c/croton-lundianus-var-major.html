Croton lundianus var. major

Croton lundianus var. major, commonly known as the giant croton, is a striking plant native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers, followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas of forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
