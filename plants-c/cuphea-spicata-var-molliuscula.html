Cuphea spicata var. molliuscula

Cuphea spicata var. molliuscula, commonly known as the velvet tassel plant, is a flowering shrub native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, violet-colored flowers that bloom in dense, spiky clusters. The plant features soft, velvety leaves that give it a distinctive texture and appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-draining soil.

Cuphea spicata var. molliuscula is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its unique foliage and showy blooms.
