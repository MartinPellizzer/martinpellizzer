Calamagrostis purpurascens var. arctica

Calamagrostis purpurascens var. arctica, commonly known as Arctic moor grass, is a perennial grass native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, featuring delicate, pale pink to purple flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat.
