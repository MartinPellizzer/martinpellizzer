Croton rhamnifolius var. alagoensis

Croton rhamnifolius var. alagoensis, commonly known as the Alagoas croton, is a tropical shrub native to the northeastern region of Brazil.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often elliptical leaves that vary in color from dark green to reddish hues. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest for both horticultural and conservation purposes.
