Cassia vogeliana

Cassia vogeliana, commonly known as the Vogel's cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod fruits. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are typically bipinnate, meaning they are divided into smaller leaflets, giving it a feathery appearance.

Cassia vogeliana is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and adaptability to various soil types.
