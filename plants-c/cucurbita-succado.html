Cucurbita succado

Cucurbita succado, commonly known as the sweet gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, thick-walled fruits that are typically orange or yellow in color. The plant produces broad, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that are both male and female. Its vines can grow up to several meters in length, and it thrives in warm, sunny climates.

The fruit is often used in culinary and medicinal applications due to its high nutritional value.
