Chloranthus sumatranus

Chloranthus sumatranus, commonly known as the Sumatran chloranthus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Chloranthaceae, which is characterized by its unique floral structures and lack of true leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in tropical rainforests. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a distinctive, elongated shape.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its evolutionary significance and limited distribution.
