Croton acunae

Croton acunae, commonly known as the white croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
