Carex yezo-montana

Carex yezo-montana, commonly known as Yezo sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually green or brown, and it blooms in the summer months.

Carex yezo-montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
