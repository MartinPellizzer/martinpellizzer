Cyclanthera brachybotrys var. triloba

Cyclanthera brachybotrys var. triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed bitter gourd, is a variant of the bitter gourd species native to tropical regions.

It is a climbing vine with elongated, lobed leaves and distinctive, pale green, spiny fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom along the stems, and the fruits are typically harvested when immature for their bitter taste. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its unique flavor and nutritional content in traditional cuisines.
