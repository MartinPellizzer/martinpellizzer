Cadaba longifolia var. scandens

Cadaba longifolia var. scandens, commonly known as the climbing cadaba, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with slender stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and flowering display.
