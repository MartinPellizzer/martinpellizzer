Cissus aristolochiifolia

Cissus aristolochiifolia, commonly known as the arrowhead cissus, is a climbing plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, arrowhead-like leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its stems are succulent and can be used for propagation.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often grown as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
