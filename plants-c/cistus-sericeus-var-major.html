Cistus sericeus var. major

Cistus sericeus var. major, commonly known as the silverleaf rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

Its aromatic leaves and flowers make it a valuable plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
