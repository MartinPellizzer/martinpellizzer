Cyperus aristatus var. maingayi

Cyperus aristatus var. maingayi, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and cylindrical flower spikes. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in various aquatic habitats.

The plant is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
