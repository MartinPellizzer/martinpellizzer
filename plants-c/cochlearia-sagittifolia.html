Cochlearia sagittifolia

Cochlearia sagittifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf cochlearia, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal and maritime environments, thriving in sandy or gravelly soils. Its leaves are arrow-shaped, hence the species name, and are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

It blooms from May to July, producing clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
