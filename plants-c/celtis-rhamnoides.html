Celtis rhamnoides

Celtis rhamnoides, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Elm family (Ulmaceae) and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by small, spherical fruits that are often covered in a whitish bloom.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
