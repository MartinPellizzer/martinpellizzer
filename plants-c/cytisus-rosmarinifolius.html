Cytisus rosmarinifolius

Cytisus rosmarinifolius, commonly known as Portuguese rockrose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer. The plant has thin, wiry stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or coastal environments.

Its aromatic foliage and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
