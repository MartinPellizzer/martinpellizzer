Conyza spartioides

Conyza spartioides, commonly known as sand bur, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant produces numerous burrs with hooked bracts that adhere to animal fur and clothing, aiding in seed dispersal. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its adaptability to various ecological conditions.
