Cerastium soleirolii subsp. stenopetalum

Cerastium soleirolii subsp.

stenopetalum, commonly known as the narrow-petaled mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe. It features small, hairy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and its flowers are usually white or pink, with five narrow petals.

The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense mats, making it suitable for rock gardens.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy and resilient species well adapted to harsh climates.
