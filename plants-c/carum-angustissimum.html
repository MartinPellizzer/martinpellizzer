Carum angustissimum

Carum angustissimum, commonly known as narrowleaf caraway, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small white or greenish flowers arranged in umbels. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, often used in traditional herbal medicine.

The fruit, a small dry schizocarp, contains the aromatic seeds that are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
