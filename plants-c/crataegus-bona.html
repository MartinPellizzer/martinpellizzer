Crataegus bona

Crataegus bona, commonly known as the American hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters, featuring thorny branches and dense, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers in spring, followed by red berries that ripen in the fall. Its botanical features include compound leaves, a woody structure, and the ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and along stream banks.
