Convolvulus aschersonii

Convolvulus aschersonii, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of purple and pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to survive in hot, dry environments.

Convolvulus aschersonii is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides in desert ecosystems.
