Carex festucacea var. ferruginea

Carex festucacea var. ferruginea, commonly known as red-haired sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its upright, cylindrical inflorescence. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive reddish-brown hue on the underside, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
