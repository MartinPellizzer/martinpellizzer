Cyclobalanopsis sericea

Cyclobalanopsis sericea, commonly known as the silverleaf ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and beeches, and is characterized by its hard, dense wood. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, chestnut-like nuts. Its leaves are elliptical, leathery, and covered in a fine silvery pubescence, giving it its common name.

It is often found in lowland and montane forests, where it plays an important ecological role.
