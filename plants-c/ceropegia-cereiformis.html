Ceropegia cereiformis

Ceropegia cereiformis, commonly known as the velvetleaf ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and fleshy, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, with a distinctive corona structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia cereiformis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
