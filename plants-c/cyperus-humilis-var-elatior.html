Cyperus humilis var. elatior

Cyperus humilis var. elatior, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and marshy areas.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, exhibiting a clump-forming habit with slender, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in coastal and freshwater environments.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
