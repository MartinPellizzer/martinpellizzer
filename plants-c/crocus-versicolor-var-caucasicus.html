Crocus versicolor var. caucasicus

Crocus versicolor var. caucasicus, commonly known as the Caucasian crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to violet, often with a distinctive yellow or orange center. The plant typically blooms in late winter or early spring, making it one of the earliest flowering plants in its habitat. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, growing from a basal rosette of foliage.

The bulbous root system stores nutrients, enabling the plant to survive harsh winter conditions.
