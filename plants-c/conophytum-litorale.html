Conophytum litorale

Conophytum litorale, commonly known as the coastal conophytum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves that form a compact rosette. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Conophytum litorale is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh conditions.
