Cotula linearifolia

Cotula linearifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, daisy-like yellow flowers with white ray florets, typically blooming in late summer to early autumn. The plant has slender, linear leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a compact appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Cotula linearifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
