Cousinia verutum

Cousinia verutum, commonly known as the desert cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its silver-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has a compact growth habit, often forming dense mats that help prevent soil erosion. Its leaves are typically covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in harsh environments.

Cousinia verutum is well adapted to drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
