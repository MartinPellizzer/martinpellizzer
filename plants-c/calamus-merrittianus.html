Calamus merrittianus

Calamus merrittianus, commonly known as Merritt's calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnately compound leaves. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow in color.

Calamus merrittianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
