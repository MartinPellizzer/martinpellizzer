Cristaria tripinnatifida

Cristaria tripinnatifida, commonly known as the three-parted crista, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
