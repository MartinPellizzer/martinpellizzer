Carex ablata

Carex ablata, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads in late spring to early summer.

Carex ablata is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
