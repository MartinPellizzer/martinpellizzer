Corymbium africanum var. gramineum

Corymbium africanum var. gramineum, commonly known as the African everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, fluffy, white or pale yellow flowers that resemble miniature dandelions. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by the wind, contributing to its widespread distribution. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy species suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant is often used in ornamental gardening due to its distinctive appearance and drought tolerance.
