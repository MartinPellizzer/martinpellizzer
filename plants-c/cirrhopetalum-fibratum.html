Cirrhopetalum fibratum

Cirrhopetalum fibratum, commonly known as the fibrous petal orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fibrous petals that give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its flowers are small and clustered, often displaying vibrant colors that attract specific pollinators.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
