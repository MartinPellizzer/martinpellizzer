Cyperus gula-metthi

Cyperus gula-metthi, commonly known as Indian spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems and dense clusters of spikelets at the top. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-gray sheen.

Cyperus gula-metthi is known for its ability to thrive in saturated soils and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
