Citrus × aurantium subvar. spinosa

Citrus × aurantium subvar. spinosa, commonly known as the sour orange, is a citrus plant characterized by its thorny branches and oval, green leaves.

It produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically sour and high in vitamin C. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is widely cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The sour orange is also valued in horticulture for its use in grafting other citrus varieties.
