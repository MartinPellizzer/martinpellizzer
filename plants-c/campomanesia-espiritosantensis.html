Campomanesia espiritosantensis

Campomanesia espiritosantensis, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitanga, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, red, fleshy berries that are edible and have a tart, slightly sweet flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

The species is also valued for its potential in traditional medicine and as a source of natural pigments.
