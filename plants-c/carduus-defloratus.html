Carduus defloratus

Carduus defloratus, commonly known as the yellow hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 60 centimeters. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disc.

The plant is often considered a weed but has historical uses in herbal medicine.
