Cryptochloa capillata

Cryptochloa capillata, commonly known as the silky grass or slender cryptochloa, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves that give it a delicate, grass-like appearance. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as woodland floors or along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single, slender spikelet that bears tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Cryptochloa capillata is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
