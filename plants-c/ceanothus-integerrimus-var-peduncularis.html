Ceanothus integerrimus var. peduncularis

Ceanothus integerrimus var. peduncularis, commonly known as the California coffeeberry, is a deciduous shrub native to coastal regions of California.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has leathery, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its fruit is a small, reddish drupe that matures in late summer, providing food for birds and small mammals.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal sage scrub habitats.
