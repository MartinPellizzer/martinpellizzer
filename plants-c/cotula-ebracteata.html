Cotula ebracteata

Cotula ebracteata, commonly known as the lesser dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing herb with small, dandelion-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has alternate, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

Cotula ebracteata is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
