Cocculus recisus

Cocculus recisus, commonly known as the rough cocculus, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by distinctive, spiny fruits. Its leaves and roots have been used in traditional medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

The plant thrives in humid environments and is often found in forest understories.
