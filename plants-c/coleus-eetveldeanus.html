Coleus eetveldeanus

Coleus eetveldeanus, commonly known as the Eetvelde's coleus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that are often mistaken for flowers. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with opposite, ovate leaves that are often brightly colored. It produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, which are usually pale purple or white.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated as a garden plant.
