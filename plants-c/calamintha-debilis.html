Calamintha debilis

Calamintha debilis, commonly known as slender caraway, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, hairy stems and small, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. The plant produces tiny white to pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule containing several seeds, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Calamintha debilis is valued for its medicinal properties, including potential anti-inflammatory and digestive benefits.
