Centrolobium robustum var. macrochaete

Centrolobium robustum var. macrochaete, commonly known as the Brazilian ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and sturdy, fibrous wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by elongated, bean-like pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, tropical environments.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
