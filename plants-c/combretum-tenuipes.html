Combretum tenuipes

Combretum tenuipes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, which are followed by distinctive, elongated seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
