Commelina falcata

Commelina falcata, commonly known as the sickleleaf spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that are clustered in umbels, often with a white or greenish spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina falcata is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it a common weed in disturbed soils.
