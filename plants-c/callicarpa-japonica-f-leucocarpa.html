Callicarpa japonica f. leucocarpa

Callicarpa japonica f. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-berried beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of white berries that contrast with its vibrant purple foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by the distinctive white fruits in the fall. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

The white berries are not only visually appealing but also attract birds and other wildlife.
