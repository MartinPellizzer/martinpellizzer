Chamaeanthus paniculatus

Chamaeanthus paniculatus, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its evergreen foliage and showy inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has small, leathery leaves that are densely packed on its stems. Its flowers, which appear in clusters, are usually white or pink and bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
