Carex flacca var. alpina

Carex flacca var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous environments. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue.

It produces small, clustered spikelets that are wind-pollinated, contributing to its ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
