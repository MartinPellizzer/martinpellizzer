Chionanthus plurifloroides

Chionanthus plurifloroides, commonly known as the Chinese fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a slight fringe-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth or slightly hairy texture.

Chionanthus plurifloroides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and adaptability to various garden conditions.
