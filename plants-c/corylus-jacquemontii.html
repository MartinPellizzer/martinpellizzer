Corylus jacquemontii

Corylus jacquemontii, commonly known as the Japanese hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It belongs to the hazel genus and is characterized by its compound leaves and edible nuts. The plant produces catkin-like flowers in early spring, followed by the development of oval, brown nuts by late summer. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nut production.

Corylus jacquemontii is also valued in traditional medicine for its various health benefits.
