Chrysothamnus arizonicus

Chrysothamnus arizonicus, commonly known as the Arizona goldthread, is a perennial herb native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green foliage and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers in the spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its fibrous roots help it retain moisture in harsh conditions.

It is often found in grasslands, shrublands, and along stream banks in its native range.
