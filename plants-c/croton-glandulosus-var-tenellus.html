Croton glandulosus var. tenellus

Croton glandulosus var. tenellus, commonly known as the velvet croton, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that give it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its growth habit is shrub-like, with multiple stems and a dense canopy.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
