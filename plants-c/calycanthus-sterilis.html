Calycanthus sterilis

Calycanthus sterilis, commonly known as the sterile sweetshrub, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands. The plant produces fragrant, urn-shaped flowers that are usually red or orange in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Calycanthus sterilis is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in woodland gardens.
