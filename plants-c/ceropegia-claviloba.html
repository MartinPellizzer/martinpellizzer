Ceropegia claviloba

Ceropegia claviloba, commonly known as the string of hearts or heartleaf ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which attract specific pollinators like flies and beetles. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
