Callista pandurifera

Callista pandurifera, commonly known as the pandurifera or the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that resemble small bells, which bloom in clusters along the branches. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and can grow up to 10 meters in height. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.
