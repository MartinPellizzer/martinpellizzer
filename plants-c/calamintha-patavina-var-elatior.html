Calamintha patavina var. elatior

Calamintha patavina var. elatior, commonly known as sweet germander, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, aromatic stems covered in fine hairs and produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate to lanceolate, and have a strong, mint-like fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often used in herbal medicine for its calming and digestive properties.
