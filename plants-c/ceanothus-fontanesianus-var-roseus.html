Ceanothus fontanesianus var. roseus

Ceanothus fontanesianus var. roseus, commonly known as the roseleaf ceanothus, is a shrub native to the western United States, particularly California.

It is characterized by its dense, aromatic foliage and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant scent and vibrant floral display.
