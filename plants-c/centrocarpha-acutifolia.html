Centrocarpha acutifolia

Centrocarpha acutifolia, commonly known as the sharpleaf goldenbush, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, terminal clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and mountainous regions.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
