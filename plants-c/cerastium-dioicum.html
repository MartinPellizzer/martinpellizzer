Cerastium dioicum

Cerastium dioicum, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has a shallow root system and is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance.

Cerastium dioicum is also known for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.
