Coelogyne lycastoides

Coelogyne lycastoides, commonly known as the Lycastoides orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescence bearing multiple fragrant, showy flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and leathery, adapted to retain moisture in humid environments.

The flowers, which can be white, pink, or red, are prized for their beauty and are often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
