Cereus abnormis

Cereus abnormis, commonly known as the Moon Cactus, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive appearance, featuring a swollen, colorful body known as the "pseudostem.". The plant produces showy, night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pink. It is a symbiotic species, often grafted onto a rootstock of another cactus species, such as Hylocereus.

Due to its unique appearance and nocturnal flowering, it is a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.
