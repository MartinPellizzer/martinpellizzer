Cacalia sagittifolia

Cacalia sagittifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed and have a silvery underside. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in open woodlands or rocky areas.

Cacalia sagittifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, daisy-like blooms.
