Calamagrostis affinis

Calamagrostis affinis, commonly known as silver bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and silvery-blue foliage that becomes more pronounced in the autumn. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis affinis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
