Cyperus guthnickii

Cyperus guthnickii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes.

Cyperus guthnickii plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wetland organisms.
