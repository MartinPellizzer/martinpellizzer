Cucumis melo var. acidulus

Cucumis melo var. acidulus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a compact, bushy plant that produces small, elongated fruits with a mild, slightly tart flavor.

It is native to the Middle East and has been cultivated for centuries for its edible, crisp cucumbers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 feet and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. Its leaves are heart-shaped and dark green, while the stems are hairy and slender.

The fruit is usually harvested when young and tender, before it fully matures and becomes bitter.
