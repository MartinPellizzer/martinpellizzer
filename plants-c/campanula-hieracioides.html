Campanula hieracioides

Campanula hieracioides, commonly known as the mouse ear campion, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Campanula hieracioides is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
