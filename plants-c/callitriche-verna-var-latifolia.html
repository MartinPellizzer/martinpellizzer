Callitriche verna var. latifolia

Callitriche verna var. latifolia, commonly known as broad-leaved spring starwort, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It typically grows in shallow, still or slow-moving freshwater habitats such as ponds, ditches, and wet meadows. The plant features small, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink, and its leaves are broad and floating or submerged. It is an annual species with a short growing season, often blooming in early spring.

The plant plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small invertebrates and serving as a food source for waterfowl.
