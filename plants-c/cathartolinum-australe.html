Cathartolinum australe

Cathartolinum australe, commonly known as the Southern Cathartolinum, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of southern Australia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom during the spring and early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained soils.

This species is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
