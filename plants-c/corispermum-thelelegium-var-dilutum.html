Corispermum thelelegium var. dilutum

Corispermum thelelegium var. dilutum, commonly known as the pale corispermum, is a rare flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, pale green to yellowish leaves. The flowers are unisexual, with male and female flowers occurring on separate plants, a trait known as dioecy. It typically grows in saline or alkaline soils, often found in arid or semi-arid regions.

This variant is distinguished by its reduced pigmentation and sparse foliage compared to other members of the species.
