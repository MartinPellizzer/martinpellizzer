Cryptocarya pergamentacea

Cryptocarya pergamentacea, commonly known as the paperbark cryptocarya, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, peeling bark that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring. Its leaves are usually elliptical, with a glossy upper surface and a pale underside.

Cryptocarya pergamentacea is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities and potential medicinal properties.
