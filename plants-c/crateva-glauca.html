Crateva glauca

Crateva glauca, commonly known as the Indian caper or blue caper, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows up to 2 meters tall, featuring elliptical leaves with a glossy, dark green surface. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers that are often followed by bright yellow berries. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and unique fruit, making it a popular choice in gardens.

Crateva glauca is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
