Carex douglasii var. brunnea

Carex douglasii var. brunnea, commonly known as the Douglas's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, strap-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically brown or reddish in color, giving it its varietal name "brunnea," meaning brownish. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing soil and supporting wildlife.
