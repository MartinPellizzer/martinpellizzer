Calyptromyrcia cymosa var. minor

Calyptromyrcia cymosa var. minor, commonly known as the lesser calyptromyrcia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically covered with a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer seasons. Its fruits are capsules that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
