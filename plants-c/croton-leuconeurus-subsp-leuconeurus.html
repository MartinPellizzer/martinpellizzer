Croton leuconeurus subsp. leuconeurus

Croton leuconeurus subsp.

leuconeurus, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, fiery orange to red bracts that surround its small, yellow flowers.

The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its bark is often thick and fissured. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
