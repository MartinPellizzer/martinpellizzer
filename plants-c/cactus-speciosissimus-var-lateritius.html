Cactus speciosissimus var. lateritius

Cactus speciosissimus var. lateritius, commonly known as the rock cactus, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, rounded stem that is typically covered in a thick, waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces clusters of small, vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help protect it from herbivores and reduce transpiration.

This species is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
