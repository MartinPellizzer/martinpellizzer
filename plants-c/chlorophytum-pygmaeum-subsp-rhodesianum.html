Chlorophytum pygmaeum subsp. rhodesianum

Chlorophytum pygmaeum subsp.

rhodesianum, commonly known as the Rhodesian miniature spider plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to southern Africa. It features rosettes of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green with a subtle silver or white stripe along the edges.

The plant produces delicate, grass-like inflorescences that bear small white flowers, which are followed by blue or violet berries. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its tolerance of low light and minimal water requirements.

Its compact size and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for container gardening and as a houseplant.
