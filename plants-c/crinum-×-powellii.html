Crinum × powellii

Crinum × powellii, commonly known as the Powell's crinum, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, bearing large, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant produces strap-shaped, evergreen leaves that are often used in ornamental gardening. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warmer climates.

Crinum × powellii is also noted for its strong fragrance, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
