Carex hordeistichos

Carex hordeistichos, commonly known as the barley sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and clustered spikelets that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. Native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks. The plant produces small, brownish seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
