Cactus curassavicus

Cactus curassavicus, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its spherical shape and a dense covering of spines that protect it from herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments.

The cactus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
