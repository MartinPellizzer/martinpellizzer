Coptis chinensis

Coptis chinensis, commonly known as Chinese goldthread, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its yellow rhizomes, which contain the alkaloid berberine. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are ovate to elliptic in shape.

Coptis chinensis is valued in traditional medicine for its antimicrobial and anti-inflammatory properties.
