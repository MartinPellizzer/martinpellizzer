Cerastium vulgatum subsp. nemorale

Cerastium vulgatum subsp.

nemorale, commonly known as wood mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically forms dense mats of hairy, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs.

The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their mild diuretic properties.
