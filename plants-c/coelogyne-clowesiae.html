Coelogyne clowesiae

Coelogyne clowesiae, commonly known as the Clowes' orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is an epiphytic plant, typically growing on trees in tropical rainforests. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and red, often with intricate patterns. Its pseudobulbs store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in humid, shaded environments.

Coelogyne clowesiae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its showy blooms.
