Carex rhombifructus

Carex rhombifructus, commonly known as the rhomboid-fruited sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on inflorescences that emerge from the base of the plant. Carex rhombifructus is native to parts of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
