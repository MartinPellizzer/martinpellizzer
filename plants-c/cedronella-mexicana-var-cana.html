Cedronella mexicana var. cana

Cedronella mexicana var. cana, commonly known as Mexican cedar or cana cedar, is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its small, greenish-white flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its aromatic foliage. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been historically used for construction and furniture.
