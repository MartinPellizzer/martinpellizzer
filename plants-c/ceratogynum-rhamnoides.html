Ceratogynum rhamnoides

Ceratogynum rhamnoides, commonly known as the African spurred tortoise plant, is a succulent native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and rock gardens.
