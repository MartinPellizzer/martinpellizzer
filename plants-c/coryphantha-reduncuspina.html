Coryphantha reduncuspina

Coryphantha reduncuspina, commonly known as the red-spined hedgehog cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in spines, with the spines on the upper part of the plant being red, a distinctive feature that sets it apart from other species. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus enthusiasts.
