Carex cespitosa f. fuliginosa

Carex cespitosa f. fuliginosa, commonly known as the sooty sedge, is a variant of the common sedge found in wet or moist environments.

It is characterized by its dark, sooty-colored foliage, which gives it its distinguishing name. The plant forms dense clumps of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in marshy areas, along stream banks, and in other damp habitats.

This sedges is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
