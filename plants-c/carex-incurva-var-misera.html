Carex incurva var. misera

Carex incurva var. misera, commonly known as the drooping sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. This variety is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense mats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
