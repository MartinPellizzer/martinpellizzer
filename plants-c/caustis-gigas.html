Caustis gigas

Caustis gigas, commonly known as the giant caustis, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its tall, woody stem and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in swampy or marshy habitats.

Due to its striking appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as an ornamental plant.
