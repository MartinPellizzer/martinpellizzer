Cyperus alopecuroides f. pallidiflorus

Cyperus alopecuroides f. pallidiflorus, commonly known as the paleflower flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, pale-colored flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in plant taxonomy.
