Chamaecrista puberula

Chamaecrista puberula, commonly known as the hairy crown flower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, feathery leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and thrives in wetlands or along streambanks. It plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.

Its seeds are often dispersed by water, contributing to its spread in wetland ecosystems.
