Centaurea napifolia

Centaurea napifolia, commonly known as the Italian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, spherical leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, dry fruits called achenes, which are typically dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry grasslands and rocky areas.

Centaurea napifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
