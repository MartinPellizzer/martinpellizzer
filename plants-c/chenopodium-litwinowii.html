Chenopodium litwinowii

Chenopodium litwinowii, commonly known as the Litwinowii goosefoot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to triangular in shape. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Chenopodium litwinowii is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions due to its deep root system and waxy leaf surface.
