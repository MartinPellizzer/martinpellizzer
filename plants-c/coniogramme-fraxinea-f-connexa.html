Coniogramme fraxinea f. connexa

Coniogramme fraxinea f. connexa, commonly known as the golden thread fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, lacy fronds.

It features elongated, narrow leaves with a distinctive golden hue on the underside, which becomes more pronounced as the plant matures. The fronds are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, creating a compact and elegant appearance. This variety is known for its slow growth and preference for shady, moist environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses due to its aesthetic appeal and unique foliage.
