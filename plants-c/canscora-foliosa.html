Canscora foliosa

Canscora foliosa, commonly known as the leafy candelabra, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves and stems contain various alkaloids, which have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

Canscora foliosa thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.
