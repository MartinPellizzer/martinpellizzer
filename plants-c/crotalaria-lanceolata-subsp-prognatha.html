Crotalaria lanceolata subsp. prognatha

Crotalaria lanceolata subsp.

prognatha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and distinctive yellow-orange flowers.

The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, and the plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in certain agricultural contexts.

However, it can also be invasive in some areas, posing challenges to native ecosystems.
