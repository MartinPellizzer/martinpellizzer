Campanula × semproniana

Campanula × semproniana, commonly known as the bluebell bellflower, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, azure-blue to violet flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long flowering period and ability to naturalize in suitable conditions.
