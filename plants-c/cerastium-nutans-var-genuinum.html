Cerastium nutans var. genuinum

Cerastium nutans var. genuinum, commonly known as the nodding mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of white, five-petaled flowers that hang downward, typical of the species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a ground cover.
