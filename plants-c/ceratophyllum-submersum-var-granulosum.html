Ceratophyllum submersum var. granulosum

Ceratophyllum submersum var. granulosum, commonly known as the granular waterwheel plant, is a submerged aquatic plant native to freshwater environments in Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and small, rounded leaves that are covered in a fine, granular texture. The plant lacks true roots and instead relies on its rhizomes for anchoring and nutrient absorption. It is often found in still or slow-moving waters, where it can form dense underwater stands.

This species plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen through photosynthesis.
