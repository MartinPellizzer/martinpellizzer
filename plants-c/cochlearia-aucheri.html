Cochlearia aucheri

Cochlearia aucheri, commonly known as the purple saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its compact rosette of fleshy, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, violet or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cold climates with short growing seasons.

Cochlearia aucheri is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
