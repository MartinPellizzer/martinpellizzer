Coreopsis formosa

Coreopsis formosa, commonly known as the showy coreopsis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features bright yellow to orange flowers with a distinctive red or brown center, blooming from late spring to early fall. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Coreopsis formosa is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
