Cassia calixtana

Cassia calixtana, commonly known as the African cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bipinnate leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are enclosed in long, slender pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia calixtana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
