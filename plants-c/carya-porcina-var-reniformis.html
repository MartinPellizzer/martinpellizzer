Carya porcina var. reniformis

Carya porcina var. reniformis, commonly known as the red hickory, is a variant of the hickory tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, kidney-shaped leaves and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. The tree produces edible nuts enclosed in a thick, spiny husk, which are a food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is deeply furrowed and has a rugged appearance, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and sustenance for numerous organisms.
