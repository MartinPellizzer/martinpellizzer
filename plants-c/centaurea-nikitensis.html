Centaurea nikitensis

Centaurea nikitensis, commonly known as Nikitov's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spiky seed heads.

Centaurea nikitensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
