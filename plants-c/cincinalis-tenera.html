Cincinalis tenera

Cincinalis tenera, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. The plant produces showy, pale yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in clusters. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cincinalis tenera is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive species in some regions.
