Centaurea saratoi

Centaurea saratoi, commonly known as the Saratov knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in steppe and grassland habitats. It produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Centaurea saratoi is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
