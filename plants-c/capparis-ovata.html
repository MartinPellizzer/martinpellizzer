Capparis ovata

Capparis ovata, commonly known as the flowering cape gooseberry, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and have a slightly serrated edge.

Capparis ovata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
