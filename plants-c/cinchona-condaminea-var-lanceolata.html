Cinchona condaminea var. lanceolata

Cinchona condaminea var. lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf quinine tree, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its lanceolate leaves, which are elongated and tapering at both ends. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange-red berries containing elliptical seeds. It is a member of the Rubiaceae family and is known for its medicinal properties, particularly the presence of quinine in its bark.

This variety is valued for its role in traditional medicine and its contribution to the global supply of quinine.
