Callicarpa homoeophylla

Callicarpa homoeophylla, commonly known as the purple beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its berries are not only ornamental but also attract birds, making it a valuable addition to wildlife gardens.

The plant is often cultivated for its vibrant foliage and seasonal fruit display.
