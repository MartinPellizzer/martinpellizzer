Caltha baicalensis

Caltha baicalensis, commonly known as the golden ragwort, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Ranunculaceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided and covered in a fine, silky hair.

The roots of Caltha baicalensis contain bioactive compounds that have been used in traditional Chinese medicine.
