Cadetia aprinoides

Cadetia aprinoides, commonly known as the spring gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family, characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, tubular flowers that are pollinated by insects such as bees and flies.

Cadetia aprinoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
