Cocos botryophora

Cocos botryophora, commonly known as the foxtail palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, clustered fronds that resemble a fox's tail, giving it its common name. The plant has a robust root system that helps it thrive in wet, swampy environments. Its leaves are typically green and can grow up to several meters in length.

Cocos botryophora is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its unique appearance and adaptability.
