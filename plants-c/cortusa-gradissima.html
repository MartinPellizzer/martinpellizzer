Cortusa gradissima

Cortusa gradissima, commonly known as the tall bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shady woodlands and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cortusa gradissima is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
