Citrus × sinensis

Citrus × sinensis, commonly known as the sweet orange, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, segmented fruit with a thick rind and juicy, segmented flesh. The plant is a small evergreen tree with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is rich in vitamin C and is widely used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
