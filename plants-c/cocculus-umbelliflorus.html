Cocculus umbelliflorus

Cocculus umbelliflorus, commonly known as the white cohosh, is a flowering plant native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces yellow berries that mature in the fall, and its roots have been used traditionally in herbal medicine. Cocculus umbelliflorus thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological studies and medicinal research.
