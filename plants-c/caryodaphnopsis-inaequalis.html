Caryodaphnopsis inaequalis

Caryodaphnopsis inaequalis, commonly known as the unequal-winged nutmeg tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and distinctive, asymmetrical winged fruits. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in panicles.

The tree is valued for its aromatic wood and historical use in traditional medicine.
