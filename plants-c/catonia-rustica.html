Catonia rustica

Catonia rustica, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant has heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds. It is well adapted to dry environments, with deep roots that help it survive in harsh, sandy soils.
