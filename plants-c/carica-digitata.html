Carica digitata

Carica digitata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or pitahaya, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Caricaceae and is characterized by its striking red or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a fleshy, edible fruit with a vibrant color and a sweet, sometimes tangy flavor. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

The fruit is rich in antioxidants and is often consumed fresh or used in various culinary applications.
