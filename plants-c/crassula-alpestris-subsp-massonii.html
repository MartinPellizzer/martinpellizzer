Crassula alpestris subsp. massonii

Crassula alpestris subsp.

massonii, commonly known as the Cape snowflake, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features compact, rosette-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color, often with a powdery coating.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white and pink during the spring and summer months. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its fleshy leaves.

This species is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
