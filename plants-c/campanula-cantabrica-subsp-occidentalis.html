Campanula cantabrica subsp. occidentalis

Campanula cantabrica subsp.

occidentalis, commonly known as the western bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western regions of the Iberian Peninsula. It features upright stems covered in soft, hairy foliage and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
