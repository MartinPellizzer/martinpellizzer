Calopsis paniculata

Calopsis paniculata, commonly known as the yellow panic grass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its erect, slender stalks and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its inflorescence is a paniculate structure, consisting of multiple branches that bear tiny spikelets.

Calopsis paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its delicate appearance.
