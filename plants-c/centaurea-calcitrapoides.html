Centaurea calcitrapoides

Centaurea calcitrapoides, commonly known as the limestone cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb with slender stems and small, spiny leaves. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow to orange-red flowers, which are typically tubular and arranged in a loose inflorescence. It thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Centaurea calcitrapoides is characterized by its adaptability to harsh environments and its role in supporting local pollinator populations.
