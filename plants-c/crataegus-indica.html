Crataegus indica

Crataegus indica, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to regions of Asia, including China and the Korean Peninsula.

It typically grows to a height of 2 to 6 meters and features thorny branches, simple leaves with serrated edges, and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red to orange berries in the fall, which are edible but often used in traditional medicine.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its pomes, which are fleshy fruits with a hard, central core.
