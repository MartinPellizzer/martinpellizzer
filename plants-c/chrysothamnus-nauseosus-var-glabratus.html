Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. glabratus

Chrysothamnus nauseosus var. glabratus, commonly known as the smooth tansy, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its yellowish-green leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. It is part of the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its aromatic foliage.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
