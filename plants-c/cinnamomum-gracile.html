Cinnamomum gracile

Cinnamomum gracile, commonly known as the slender cinnamon or small-leaved cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces dark brown, aromatic seeds enclosed in a woody capsule. Its essential oils, particularly those containing cinnamaldehyde, are valued in the fragrance and food industries.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
