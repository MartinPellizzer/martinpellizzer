Cyperus ascodensus

Cyperus ascodensus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense stands in wetlands or along water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil in aquatic habitats.

Cyperus ascodensus produces small, clustered flowers that are usually green or reddish in color, contributing to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
