Carex tahoensis

Carex tahoensis, commonly known as Tahoe sedge, is a perennial plant native to the Sierra Nevada mountains in California.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Tahoe sedge plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
