Cupania zanthoxyloides

Cupania zanthoxyloides, commonly known as the yellowwood or sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange to red berries. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a milky sap when wounded.
