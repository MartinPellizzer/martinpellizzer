Cerasus × shirataki

Cerasus × shirataki, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or yamazakura, is a hybrid species within the Prunus genus.

It is native to Japan and is renowned for its ornamental value, particularly its showy blossoms. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers, which appear in early spring, are usually pale pink to white and are borne in clusters.

The leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth texture and turn a vibrant green during the growing season.
