Cordia loureiroi

Cordia loureiroi, commonly known as the Brazilian white cedar, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Cordia loureiroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
