Caroxylon recurvum

Caroxylon recurvum, commonly known as the zigzag palm, is a unique species of palm native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzag-shaped stems that are covered in a thick, fibrous layer. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by fruit that is encased in a spiny shell. Its leaves are long and pinnate, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
