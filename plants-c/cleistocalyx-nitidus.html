Cleistocalyx nitidus

Cleistocalyx nitidus, commonly known as the white hibiscus, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, round, yellowish fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cleistocalyx nitidus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
