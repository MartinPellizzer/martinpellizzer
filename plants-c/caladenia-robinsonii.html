Caladenia robinsonii

Caladenia robinsonii, commonly known as the Robinson's spider orchid, is a striking member of the orchid family native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its intricate, spider-like appearance, with a single, slender stem bearing a single flower that resembles a spider in both form and color. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in heathlands and open woodlands. It blooms during the late winter to early spring, producing flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species and is protected in some regions.
