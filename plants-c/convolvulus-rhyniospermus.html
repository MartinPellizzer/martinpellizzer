Convolvulus rhyniospermus

Convolvulus rhyniospermus, commonly known as the African morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Convolvulus rhyniospermus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
