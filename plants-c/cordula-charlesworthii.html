Cordula charlesworthii

Cordula charlesworthii, commonly known as Charlesworth's cordula, is a rare and specialized plant species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique morphology, featuring elongated, strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, arranged in clusters along the stems. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in shaded, wetland environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
