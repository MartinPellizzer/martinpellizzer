Crataegus uvaldensis

Crataegus uvaldensis, commonly known as the Uvalde hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the United States, specifically found in Texas.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as prairies and woodland edges.
