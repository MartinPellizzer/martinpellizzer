Cerasus microcarpa f. longepedunculata

Cerasus microcarpa f. longepedunculata, commonly known as the long pedunculate cherry, is a botanical variant of the dwarf cherry.

It is characterized by its elongated peduncles, which are the stalks supporting the flower clusters. The plant typically produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a specimen plant.
