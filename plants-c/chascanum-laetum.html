Chascanum laetum

Chascanum laetum, commonly known as the yellow passionflower, is a climbing vine native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with five petals and a distinctive crown of filaments. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.
