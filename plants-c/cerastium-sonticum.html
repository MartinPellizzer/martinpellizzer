Cerastium sonticum

Cerastium sonticum, commonly known as the sanderson's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, spoon-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring.
