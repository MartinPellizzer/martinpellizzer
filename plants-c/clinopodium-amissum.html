Clinopodium amissum

Clinopodium amissum, commonly known as narrowleaf mountain mint, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture and a strong minty scent.

Small, tubular flowers are arranged in dense clusters, producing nectar that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
