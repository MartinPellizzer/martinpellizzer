Cladostachys alternifolia

Cladostachys alternifolia, commonly known as the variable fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are often variable in shape and size. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks. Its fronds are usually bright green and can reach lengths of up to several meters.

Cladostachys alternifolia plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
