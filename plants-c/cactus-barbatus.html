Cactus barbatus

Cactus barbatus, commonly known as the spiny leaf cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its spiny, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Cactus barbatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
