Calamintha straussii

Calamintha straussii, commonly known as Strauss's germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or violet in color, arranged in whorls along the stems. Its foliage is aromatic and often used in traditional herbal medicine.

Calamintha straussii thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
