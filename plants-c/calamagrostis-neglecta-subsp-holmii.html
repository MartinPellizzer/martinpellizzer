Calamagrostis neglecta subsp. holmii

Calamagrostis neglecta subsp.

holmii, commonly known as the Holm's reedgrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, arching blades that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height.

The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
