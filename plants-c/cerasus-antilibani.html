Cerasus antilibani

Cerasus antilibani, commonly known as the Syrian cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the eastern Mediterranean region, including parts of Turkey, Syria, and Lebanon. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by edible berries that are typically red or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous areas.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
