Cheiranthus aurantiacus

Cheiranthus aurantiacus, commonly known as the orange candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, bright orange flowers. The plant features basal leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Cheiranthus aurantiacus is known for its long blooming period, typically from late winter to spring.
