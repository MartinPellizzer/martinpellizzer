Cardamine willdenowii

Cardamine willdenowii, commonly known as the willdenow's watercress, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its roots are fibrous, and it produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by water or wind.

This species is often found in temperate regions of Europe and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
