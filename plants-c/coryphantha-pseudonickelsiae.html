Coryphantha pseudonickelsiae

Coryphantha pseudonickelsiae, commonly known as the Nickels' coryphantha, is a species of flowering plant in the cacti family, Cactaceae.

It is native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. This plant is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in clusters of small, spiny areoles. The flowers, which are typically yellow or orange, bloom in late spring and are borne at the tips of the stems.

It is a low-maintenance plant adapted to thrive in hot, dry environments with minimal water requirements.
