Carex intricata

Carex intricata, commonly known as the intricate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are narrow and linear, with a distinctive hairy texture. Its flowering spikes are usually erect and contain small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex intricata plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
