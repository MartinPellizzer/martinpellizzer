Cornus × lepagei

Cornus × lepagei, commonly known as the Chinese dogwood, is a hybrid shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters, followed by red or orange berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Cornus × lepagei is valued for its seasonal color changes and adaptability to various climates.
