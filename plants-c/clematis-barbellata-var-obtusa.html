Clematis barbellata var. obtusa

Clematis barbellata var. obtusa, commonly known as the obtuse clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 15 feet, making it suitable for trellises or fences. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage consists of heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
