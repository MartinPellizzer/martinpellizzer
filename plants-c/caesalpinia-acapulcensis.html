Caesalpinia acapulcensis

Caesalpinia acapulcensis, commonly known as the Acapulco ceiba, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive yellow-orange flowers. The tree produces long, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, growing in a variety of soil types.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential horticultural use.
