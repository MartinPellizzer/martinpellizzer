Cereus speciosissimus var. loudonii

Cereus speciosissimus var. loudonii, commonly known as the Queen of the Night, is a species of cactus native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, white flowers that bloom only at night. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique nocturnal blooming habit, it is a popular choice for collectors and enthusiasts of rare plants.
