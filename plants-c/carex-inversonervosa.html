Carex inversonervosa

Carex inversonervosa, commonly known as the Upside-down Nerve Grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique leaf structure, where the veins run in an inverted pattern compared to most Carex species. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as marshes and forest floors, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often hidden among the foliage.

The botanical features of Carex inversonervosa make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and botanical gardens.
