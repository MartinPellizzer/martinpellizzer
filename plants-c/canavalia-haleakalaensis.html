Canavalia haleakalaensis

Canavalia haleakalaensis, commonly known as Haleakala morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the island of Maui in Hawaii.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and has adapted to the unique environmental conditions of its habitat.
