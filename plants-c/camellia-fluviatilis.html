Camellia fluviatilis

Camellia fluviatilis, commonly known as the river camellia, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows near rivers and streams, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces striking, large, showy flowers that are usually pink or red, with a waxy texture and a pleasant fragrance. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Camellia fluviatilis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
