Ceraia tenella

Ceraia tenella, commonly known as the common stinging nettle, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Urticaceae and is characterized by its hairy, serrated leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant contains stinging hairs that release irritants when touched, causing a skin reaction in many people. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

Ceraia tenella is an important plant in traditional medicine and has been used for its purported health benefits.
