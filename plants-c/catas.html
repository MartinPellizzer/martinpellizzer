Catas

Catas, commonly known as catas or *Catas* (depending on regional nomenclature), is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Catas is often found in temperate regions, growing in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
