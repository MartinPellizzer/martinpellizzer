Campanula spicata var. hornschuchii

Campanula spicata var. hornschuchii, commonly known as the Hornschuch's bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to Central Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, preferring partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
