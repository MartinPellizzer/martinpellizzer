Cerastium latifolium var. glabriusculum

Cerastium latifolium var. glabriusculum, commonly known as the broad-leaved mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has rounded, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white or gray pubescence. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland plantings.
