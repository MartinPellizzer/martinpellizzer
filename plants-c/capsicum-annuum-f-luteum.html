Capsicum annuum f. luteum

Capsicum annuum f. luteum, commonly known as the yellow bell pepper, is a cultivar of the common pepper plant.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow fruits, which are typically harvested when they are fully mature. The plant produces flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which later develop into the edible fruits. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often used in culinary applications for its mild flavor and bright color.
