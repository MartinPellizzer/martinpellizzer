Cuba

Cuba, commonly known as the Cuban tobacco plant, is a species of plant native to the Caribbean island of Cuba.

It is primarily cultivated for its leaves, which are used in the production of cigars. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are typically large, thick, and have a distinct aroma.

The botanical name for this plant is *Nicotiana tabacum*, and it belongs to the nightshade family.
