Convolvulus phrygius

Convolvulus phrygius, commonly known as the Phrygian bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or disturbed areas.

Convolvulus phrygius is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
