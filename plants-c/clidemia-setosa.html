Clidemia setosa

Clidemia setosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf or silverleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with multiple stems and a dense canopy.

Clidemia setosa is also known for its rapid growth and ability to form dense thickets, making it an invasive species in some areas.
