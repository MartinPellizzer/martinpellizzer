Cargila dichotoma

Cargila dichotoma, commonly known as the forked broom, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Cargila dichotoma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
