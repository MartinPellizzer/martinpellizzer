Cotoneaster scandinavicus

Cotoneaster scandinavicus, commonly known as the Scandinavian cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to northern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions. The plant features small, red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are elliptical with serrated edges, and it produces clusters of white flowers in the spring.

Cotoneaster scandinavicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
