Clematis glauca f. phaeantha

Clematis glauca f. phaeantha, commonly known as the blue clematis, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual contrast to its gray-green foliage. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its unique color and hardiness.
