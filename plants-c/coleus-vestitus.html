Coleus vestitus

Coleus vestitus, commonly known as the silver leaf coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white variegated leaves that contrast with its deep green veins. The plant belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is closely related to other ornamental coleus species. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in warm, humid conditions and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
