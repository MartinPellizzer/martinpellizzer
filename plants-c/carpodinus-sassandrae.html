Carpodinus sassandrae

Carpodinus sassandrae, commonly known as the Sassandra carpet grass, is a species of flowering plant native to western Africa.

It is a member of the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it is often found in moist, disturbed soils.

The species is noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
