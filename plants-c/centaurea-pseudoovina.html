Centaurea pseudoovina

Centaurea pseudoovina, commonly known as the narrowleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces bright yellow to orange flowers.

Centaurea pseudoovina is often used in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
