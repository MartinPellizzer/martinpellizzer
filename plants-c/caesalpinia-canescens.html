Caesalpinia canescens

Caesalpinia canescens, commonly known as the silky serissa, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, silky hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces bright yellow to orange inflorescences that are often used in ornamental horticulture. Its pods are long and slender, containing several seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Caesalpinia canescens thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to full sun, making it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
