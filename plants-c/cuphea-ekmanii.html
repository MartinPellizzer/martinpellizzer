Cuphea ekmanii

Cuphea ekmanii, commonly known as the flame flower, is a strikingly ornamental plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red flowers that resemble flames. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces seed capsules that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Cuphea ekmanii is also valued for its ability to attract hummingbirds and other pollinators.
