Ceanothus greggii var. franklinii

Ceanothus greggii var. franklinii, commonly known as Franklin's ceanothus, is a flowering shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid climates. Its leaves are typically narrow and leathery, providing a drought-tolerant feature.

This species is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
