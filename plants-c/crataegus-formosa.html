Crataegus formosa

Crataegus formosa, commonly known as the Pacific crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its ornate, showy flowers that range from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. The plant produces small, edible berries that are a food source for various wildlife. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Crataegus formosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
