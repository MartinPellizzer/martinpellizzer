Crotalaria involutifolia

Crotalaria involutifolia, commonly known as the curled leaf crotalaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rolled leaves that give it its common name, and it produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. It has small, hairy pods that contain several elliptical seeds.

Crotalaria involutifolia is often used in traditional medicine and is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
