Canna orientalis

Canna orientalis, commonly known as the oriental canna, is a striking ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, paddle-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces showy, spadix-like flowers surrounded by bright, colorful bracts, typically in shades of red, orange, or yellow. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Canna orientalis is often cultivated for its dramatic foliage and vibrant floral display in gardens and landscapes.
