Cardamine flexuosa var. manshurica

Cardamine flexuosa var. manshurica, commonly known as the Far Eastern water crowfoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a distinctive waxy coating, and its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in wetland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting aquatic and terrestrial invertebrates.
