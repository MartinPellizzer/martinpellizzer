Carex arcuata

Carex arcuata, commonly known as the arched sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikes of small, three-ranked flowers. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and along stream banks.

Carex arcuata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
