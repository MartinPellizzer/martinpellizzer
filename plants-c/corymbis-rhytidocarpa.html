Corymbis rhytidocarpa

Corymbis rhytidocarpa, commonly known as the rough-barked corymb, is a unique plant species native to specific regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its woody, shrub-like structure with rough, fissured bark that provides a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding to its ornamental value. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Corymbis rhytidocarpa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.
