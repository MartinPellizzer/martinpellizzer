Cakile aegyptia var. latifolia

Cakile aegyptia var. latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf Egyptian carnation, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas. It is a perennial species that produces a capsule fruit containing numerous seeds.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
