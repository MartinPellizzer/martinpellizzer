Campanula beckiana

Campanula beckiana, commonly known as the Beck's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has slender, hairy stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

Campanula beckiana prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
