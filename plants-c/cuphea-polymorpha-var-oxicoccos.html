Cuphea polymorpha var. oxicoccos

Cuphea polymorpha var. oxicoccos, commonly known as the red hot poker or firecracker plant, is a striking ornamental species native to Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red flowers that resemble tiny torches, which bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a versatile addition to both tropical and temperate gardens.
