Chaetanthera apiculata

Chaetanthera apiculata, commonly known as the mountain spiderflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Chaetanthera apiculata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
