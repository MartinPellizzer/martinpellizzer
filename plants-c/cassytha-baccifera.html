Cassytha baccifera

Cassytha baccifera, commonly known as the sweet flag or sweet grass, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces tiny, greenish-white flowers followed by small, berry-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Cassytha baccifera is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.
