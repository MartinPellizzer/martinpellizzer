Coursetia hypoleuca

Coursetia hypoleuca, commonly known as the white coursetia, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has woody stems and small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils.

Coursetia hypoleuca is often found in rocky areas and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
