Cornicina hamosa

Cornicina hamosa, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red petals and yellow lip, which are prominent features in its floral display. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowering period occurs during the rainy season, and it relies on specific pollinators for reproduction.

Cornicina hamosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
