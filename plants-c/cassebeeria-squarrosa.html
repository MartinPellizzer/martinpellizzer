Cassebeeria squarrosa

Cassebeeria squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is often cultivated in warm climates for both its beauty and ecological benefits.
