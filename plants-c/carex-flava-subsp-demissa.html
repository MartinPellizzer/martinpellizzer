Carex flava subsp. demissa

Carex flava subsp.

demissa, commonly known as the yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants.

This subspecies is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. It typically grows in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along streambanks.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
