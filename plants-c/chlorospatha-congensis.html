Chlorospatha congensis

Chlorospatha congensis, commonly known as the golden spathaceous plant, is a rare species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a subtle yellowish tint. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is protected in several conservation programs.
