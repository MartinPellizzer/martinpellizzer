Choristes excelsa

Choristes excelsa, commonly known as the tall bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters, supporting clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often appearing in whorls around the stem.

Choristes excelsa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
