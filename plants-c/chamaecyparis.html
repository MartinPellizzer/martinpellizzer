Chamaecyparis

Chamaecyparis, commonly known as the false cypress, is a genus of coniferous evergreen trees and shrubs native to eastern Asia and the Pacific Northwest of North America.

These plants are characterized by their scale-like or awl-shaped leaves, which are typically arranged in crossed pairs along the branches. Chamaecyparis species often have a distinctive, aromatic scent and produce small, spherical cones. They are valued in horticulture for their ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The genus includes several species, such as Chamaecyparis obtusa and Chamaecyparis lawsoniana, which are widely cultivated for their aesthetic and ecological benefits.
