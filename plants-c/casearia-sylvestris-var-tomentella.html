Casearia sylvestris var. tomentella

Casearia sylvestris var. tomentella, commonly known as the hairy casearia, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy berries that are typically orange or red when ripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal use.
