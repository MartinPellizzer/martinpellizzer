Camellia megacarpa

Camellia megacarpa, commonly known as the giant camellia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces elliptical, woody fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, subtropical climates and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

Camellia megacarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a landscape plant.
