Chionanthus colonchensis

Chionanthus colonchensis, commonly known as the mountain chionanthus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and have a smooth texture.

Chionanthus colonchensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
