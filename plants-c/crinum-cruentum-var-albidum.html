Crinum cruentum var. albidum

Crinum cruentum var. albidum, commonly known as the white crinum lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in summer, often with a subtle pink or purple tinge. The plant features strap-like, broad leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a fan shape. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its botanical name reflects its classification within the Amaryllidaceae family, which includes other bulbous flowering plants.
