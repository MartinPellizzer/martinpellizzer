Crataegus platycarpa

Crataegus platycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches, oval leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically edible and attract various bird species.

Crataegus platycarpa is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full sunlight.
