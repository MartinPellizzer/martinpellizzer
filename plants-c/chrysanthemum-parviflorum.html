Chrysanthemum parviflorum

Chrysanthemum parviflorum, commonly known as the dwarf chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically produces yellow to orange blooms, which appear in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Its ornamental value and ease of cultivation contribute to its widespread use in horticulture.
