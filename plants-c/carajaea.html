Carajaea

Carajaea, commonly known as the "Brazilian orchid," is a genus of flowering plants native to Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers. The plants typically have tall, erect stems with clusters of small, colorful blooms. Carajaea species are often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid environments.

Their botanical significance lies in their unique floral structures and ecological role in their native habitats.
