Cattleya intermedia var. punctatissima

Cattleya intermedia var. punctatissima, commonly known as the spotted cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pale pink, often with intricate patterns and a distinctive yellow lip. The plant produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients, supporting its growth in low-light conditions. Its foliage consists of broad, dark green leaves that are often spotted or streaked with silver or white markings.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and fragrance.
