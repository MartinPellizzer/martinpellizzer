Celastrus apoensis

Celastrus apoensis, commonly known as the Philippine staff vine, is a climbing shrub native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by bright orange or red berries. Its fruits are an important food source for various bird species, aiding in seed dispersal.

The plant is often found in lowland and montane forests, thriving in humid, shaded environments.
