Camara viscosa

Camara viscosa, commonly known as sticky camara, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves that exude a sticky substance when touched. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its sticky properties, it has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
