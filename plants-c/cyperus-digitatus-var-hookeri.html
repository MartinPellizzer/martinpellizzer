Cyperus digitatus var. hookeri

Cyperus digitatus var. hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the sedge family.

Cyperus digitatus var. hookeri is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
