Conophytum quaesitum var. rostratum

Conophytum quaesitum var. rostratum, commonly known as the "Cape Rhabdodendron," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, triangular leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
