Canavalia piperi

Canavalia piperi, commonly known as the cowpea or blackeye bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, spurred flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces elongated pods containing edible seeds that are widely used in cuisines around the world. Canavalia piperi is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it an important crop in sustainable agriculture.

Its adaptability to various climates and soil types further enhances its agricultural significance.
