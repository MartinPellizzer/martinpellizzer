Carex straminea var. ferruginea

Carex straminea var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rust-colored sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are often tinged with rust-colored hues, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
