Clerodendrum disparifolium

Clerodendrum disparifolium, commonly known as the variable leaf clerodendrum, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that vary in shape and size, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or near water bodies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
