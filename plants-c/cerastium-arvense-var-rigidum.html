Cerastium arvense var. rigidum

Cerastium arvense var. rigidum, commonly known as rigid mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white flowers with five petals and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It is part of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Its botanical structure supports its role as a pioneer species in ecological succession.
