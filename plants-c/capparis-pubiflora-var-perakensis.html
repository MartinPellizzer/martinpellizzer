Capparis pubiflora var. perakensis

Capparis pubiflora var. perakensis, commonly known as the Perak caper, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or near rivers.

Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological and culinary purposes in its native region.
