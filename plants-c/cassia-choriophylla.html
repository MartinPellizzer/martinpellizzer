Cassia choriophylla

Cassia choriophylla, commonly known as golden cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, oblong pods containing seeds covered in a silky, white coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant foliage and long-lasting blooms.
