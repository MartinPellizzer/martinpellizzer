Carpinus dayongiana

Carpinus dayongiana, commonly known as the Dayong horse chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Betulaceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spherical, spiny fruits containing one or two seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with distinctive ridges, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall in its natural habitat.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
