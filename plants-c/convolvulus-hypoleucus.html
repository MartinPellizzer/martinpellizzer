Convolvulus hypoleucus

Convolvulus hypoleucus, commonly known as the white morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces delicate, white to pale pink flowers with five petals. The plant has a climbing habit, using its tendrils to ascend supports, and thrives in well-drained soil. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in the early morning.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
