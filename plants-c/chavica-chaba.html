Chavica chaba

Chavica chaba, commonly known as the Japanese daffodil, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features bright yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and grows up to 30 centimeters tall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and vibrant color.
