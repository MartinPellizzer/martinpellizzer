Chrysalidocarpus decipiens

Chrysalidocarpus decipiens, commonly known as the false palm or slender palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family.

It is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant has a slender, unbranched trunk that is often covered in a fibrous sheath. Its leaves are pinnate, with long, narrow segments, and it produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, round fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
