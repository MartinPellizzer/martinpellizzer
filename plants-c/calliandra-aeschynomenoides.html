Calliandra aeschynomenoides

Calliandra aeschynomenoides, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences composed of numerous small, pink to red flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets, and its stems are often woody and branched. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The flowers are not only visually appealing but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
