Centaurea boissieri var. transmalvana

Centaurea boissieri var. transmalvana, commonly known as the Transalpine centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branching.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
