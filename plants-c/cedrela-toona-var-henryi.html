Cedrela toona var. henryi

Cedrela toona var. henryi, commonly known as Chinese sandalwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, aromatic bark and heartwood that is highly valued for its fine grain and durability. The plant typically grows in lowland forests and thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous, often greenish-yellow in color.

The species is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
