Commelina dianthifolia var. longispatha

Commelina dianthifolia var. longispatha, commonly known as the long-spurred commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features striking blue or violet flowers with long, slender spathes that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in wet, shaded environments such as swamps and marshes. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often found in botanical gardens and wetland habitats.
