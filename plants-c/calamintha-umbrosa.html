Calamintha umbrosa

Calamintha umbrosa, commonly known as shadow mint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pale blue or violet, and it produces a fragrant, mint-like scent.

Calamintha umbrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported calming properties.
