Cistus echioides

Cistus echioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, which helps reduce water loss in dry climates.

Cistus echioides is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
