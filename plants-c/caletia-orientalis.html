Caletia orientalis

Caletia orientalis, commonly known as the Eastern Caletia, is a striking flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of vibrant blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun exposure, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Caletia orientalis is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
