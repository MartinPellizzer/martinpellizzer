Carlina vulgaris var. spinossisima

Carlina vulgaris var. spinossisima, commonly known as the roughleaf carline thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, glandular leaves and a distinctive purple to pink flower head that resembles a thistle. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers bloom from late summer to early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often found in grasslands, wastelands, and along roadsides.
