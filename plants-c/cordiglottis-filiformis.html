Cordiglottis filiformis

Cordiglottis filiformis, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, elongated leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in the understory of forests. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing nutrients from the soil.

Cordiglottis filiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
