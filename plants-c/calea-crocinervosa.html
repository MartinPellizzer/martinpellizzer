Calea crocinervosa

Calea crocinervosa, commonly known as the pink-flowered calea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink to purple bracts that resemble petals. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically hidden within the bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Calea crocinervosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
