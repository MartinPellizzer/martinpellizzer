Crepidium maculatum

Crepidium maculatum, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are linear and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The spotted orchid is noted for its unique floral structure, which includes a distinctive lip that aids in pollination by insects.
