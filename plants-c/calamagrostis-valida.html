Calamagrostis valida

Calamagrostis valida, commonly known as the silvergrass or golden reed, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery, silver-green plumes that give it a striking appearance. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer.

Calamagrostis valida is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments.
