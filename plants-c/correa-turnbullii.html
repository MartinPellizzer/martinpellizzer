Correa turnbullii

Correa turnbullii, commonly known as the pink fairy fan, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its striking pink or red tubular flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.
