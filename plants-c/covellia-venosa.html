Covellia venosa

Covellia venosa, commonly known as the veined covellia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, veined leaves that display a unique pattern of contrasting colors. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white and pale pink. Covellia venosa thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in high-altitude forests.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
