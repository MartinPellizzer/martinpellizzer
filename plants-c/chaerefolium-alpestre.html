Chaerefolium alpestre

Chaerefolium alpestre, commonly known as the alpine milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers in late spring. It is characterized by its ability to withstand cold temperatures and high altitudes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and sensitive species.
