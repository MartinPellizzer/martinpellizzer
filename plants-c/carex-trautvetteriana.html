Carex trautvetteriana

Carex trautvetteriana, commonly known as the Trautvetter's sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in suitable habitats.
