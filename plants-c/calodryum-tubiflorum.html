Calodryum tubiflorum

Calodryum tubiflorum, commonly known as the tuberous flowering plant, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive tuberous roots, which serve as storage organs for nutrients. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers, often arranged in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a waxy surface that helps reduce water loss.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and for horticultural purposes.
