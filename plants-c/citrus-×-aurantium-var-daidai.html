Citrus × aurantium var. daidai

Citrus × aurantium var. daidai, commonly known as the Japanese mandarin or daidai, is a citrus tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and round, orange-colored fruits that are typically harvested in late winter. The leaves are glossy and dark green, while the fruit has a thin, peely skin and a segmented, juicy interior. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the edible fruit, which is used in both culinary and traditional medicinal applications.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
