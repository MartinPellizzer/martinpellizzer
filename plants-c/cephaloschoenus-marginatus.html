Cephaloschoenus marginatus

Cephaloschoenus marginatus, commonly known as the marginate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, thriving in environments with high humidity. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinctive white or pale margin that distinguishes it from other sedge species.

Cephaloschoenus marginatus plays a vital role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
