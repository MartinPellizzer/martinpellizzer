Chamaesyce inappendiculata

Chamaesyce inappendiculata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. The plant has narrow, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in dry environments. It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to full sun exposure.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of desert and coastal dune habitats.
