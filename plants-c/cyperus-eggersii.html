Cyperus eggersii

Cyperus eggersii, commonly known as the Eggers' flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and is characterized by its triangular stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in swampy areas and along waterways. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil in wetlands.

Cyperus eggersii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in moist conditions and prevent erosion.
