Cuphea × purpurea

Cuphea × purpurea, commonly known as the coral bells or red hot poker, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Cuphea integrifolia and Cuphea llaveana. The plant is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cuphea × purpurea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
