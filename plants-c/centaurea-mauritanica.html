Centaurea mauritanica

Centaurea mauritanica, commonly known as the Moroccan knapweed, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and bright yellow flowers with reddish-purple bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. It produces small, oval seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Centaurea mauritanica is often found in scrublands and open woodlands, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
