Corysanthes carinata

Corysanthes carinata, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of New Guinea.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to conserve moisture in its high-altitude environment.

Corysanthes carinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of rare and exotic plants.
