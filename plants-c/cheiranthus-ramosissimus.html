Cheiranthus ramosissimus

Cheiranthus ramosissimus, commonly known as the hairy wallflower, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, with four petals and numerous stamens, blooming from spring to summer.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
