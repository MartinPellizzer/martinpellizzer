Crescentia australis var. petiolata

Crescentia australis var. petiolata, commonly known as the Brazilian star apple, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces a fruit with a sweet, creamy pulp and a hard, starchy center. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value and edible fruit make it a popular choice for cultivation in tropical regions.
