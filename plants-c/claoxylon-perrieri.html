Claoxylon perrieri

Claoxylon perrieri, commonly known as the Malay ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has a tall, straight trunk with a smooth, dark bark that often exhibits a reddish hue. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptic in shape, with a glossy green upper surface.

Claoxylon perrieri is also notable for its fragrant flowers, which are typically yellow or orange and arranged in clusters.
