Cousinia flava

Cousinia flava, commonly known as the yellow globemallow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, succulent stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

Cousinia flava is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
