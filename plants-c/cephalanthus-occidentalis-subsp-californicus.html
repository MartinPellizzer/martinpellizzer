Cephalanthus occidentalis subsp. californicus

Cephalanthus occidentalis subsp.

californicus, commonly known as the California sweetshrub, is a shrub native to California and parts of Oregon. It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant produces dark purple, berry-like fruits that are edible for some wildlife but not for humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian areas and woodlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local fauna.
