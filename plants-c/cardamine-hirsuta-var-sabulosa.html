Cardamine hirsuta var. sabulosa

Cardamine hirsuta var. sabulosa, commonly known as the sandy watercress, is a variant of the hairy watercress species.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and wet meadows. The plant features trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its flowers are small, white or pale green, arranged in umbels. It is often found in temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

This variant is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor, wet environments.
