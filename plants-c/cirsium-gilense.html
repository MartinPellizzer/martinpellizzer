Cirsium gilense

Cirsium gilense, commonly known as Gil's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped with rough, serrated edges, while its flower heads consist of both ray and disc flowers.

Cirsium gilense is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
