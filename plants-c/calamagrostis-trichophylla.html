Calamagrostis trichophylla

Calamagrostis trichophylla, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

Calamagrostis trichophylla is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in wetland habitats.
