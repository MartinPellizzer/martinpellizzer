Cyrtochilum maculatum var. parviflorum

Cyrtochilum maculatum var. parviflorum, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink with a yellow or greenish throat. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a houseplant.
