Carlina vulgaris var. rigida

Carlina vulgaris var. rigida, commonly known as rigid common cudweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed habitats such as meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. The plant features upright, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture. Its flowers are small, yellowish-green, and clustered in dense, dandelion-like heads.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soils.
