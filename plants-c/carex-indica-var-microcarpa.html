Carex indica var. microcarpa

Carex indica var. microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited sedges, is a species of sedge native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It is a perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and a distinct inflorescence structure typical of the Cyperaceae family.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
