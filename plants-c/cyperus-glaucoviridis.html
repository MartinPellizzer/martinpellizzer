Cyperus glaucoviridis

Cyperus glaucoviridis, commonly known as the silver flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically silver-gray in color due to a dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, wetlands, and along water bodies.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
