Carex lenticularis

Carex lenticularis, commonly known as the lens sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to wetland environments, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in marshes and along stream banks. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
