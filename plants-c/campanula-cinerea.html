Campanula cinerea

Campanula cinerea, commonly known as the gray bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often covered in a fine, grayish-white pubescence. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lanceolate, hairy, and have a distinctive grayish-green hue.

Campanula cinerea thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, woodland edges, and rocky habitats.
