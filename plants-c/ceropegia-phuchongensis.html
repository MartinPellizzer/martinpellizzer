Ceropegia phuchongensis

Ceropegia phuchongensis, commonly known as the Phuchong milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and tubular flowers. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers that are often clustered together. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in rocky or limestone areas.

Ceropegia phuchongensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
