Coleus blakei

Coleus blakei, commonly known as the flame bamboo or red coleus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that come in shades of red, orange, and purple. The plant is known for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Coleus blakei is also valued for its use in traditional medicine in some regions.
