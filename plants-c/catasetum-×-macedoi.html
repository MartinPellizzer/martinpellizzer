Catasetum × macedoi

Catasetum × macedoi, commonly known as the MacDonnell's Catasetum, is a hybrid orchid species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, often yellow or orange flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are notable for their intricate structure, featuring specialized pollination mechanisms.

Catasetum × macedoi is valued by orchid enthusiasts for its ornamental beauty and unique botanical features.
