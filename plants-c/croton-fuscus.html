Croton fuscus

Croton fuscus, commonly known as the brown croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that range from green to reddish-brown in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.
