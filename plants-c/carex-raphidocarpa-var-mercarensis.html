Carex raphidocarpa var. mercarensis

Carex raphidocarpa var. mercarensis, commonly known as the Mercarensis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or moist soil environments, thriving in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering spikes are usually borne in late spring to early summer, producing small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
