Carex castanea

Carex castanea, commonly known as the chestnut sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated, and its seeds are dispersed by water.

Carex castanea plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
