Comolia lythrarioides

Comolia lythrarioides, commonly known as the red root or red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are often hairy in appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow to greenish flowers, which are typically borne in dense clusters. Its root system is notable for being deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in arid and rocky environments.

Comolia lythrarioides is valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used traditionally in construction and tool-making.
