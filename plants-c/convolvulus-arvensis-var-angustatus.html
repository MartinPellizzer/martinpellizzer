Convolvulus arvensis var. angustatus

Convolvulus arvensis var. angustatus, commonly known as narrowleaf field bindweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, winding stems that often grow along the ground, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, typically with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in groups of three, and it is known for its rapid growth and invasive tendencies in agricultural fields.

Its botanical characteristics make it a challenging weed to control in cultivated areas.
