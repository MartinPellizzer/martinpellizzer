Castela erecta subsp. typica

Castela erecta subsp.

typica, commonly known as the desert candle. It is a succulent shrub native to arid regions of southern Africa.

The plant features cylindrical, branched stems that resemble candles, hence its common name. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to store water.

It produces small, yellowish flowers during the rainy season, adding ornamental value to its landscape.
