Curcuma phrayawan

Curcuma phrayawan, commonly known as Thai turmeric, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and cardamom. The plant produces bright orange-yellow rhizomes that are rich in curcuminoids, giving it its characteristic color and medicinal properties. It thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

The botanical features of Curcuma phrayawan make it a valuable resource in traditional medicine and culinary applications.
