Carex arctiformis

Carex arctiformis, commonly known as the Arctic sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats.

Carex arctiformis plays a vital role in stabilizing soil in its harsh environments.
