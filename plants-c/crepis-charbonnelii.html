Crepis charbonnelii

Crepis charbonnelii, commonly known as the Charbonnel's hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette of basal leaves and a flowering stem that bears yellow composite flowers. Native to Europe, it thrives in meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has small, oval leaves with a hairy texture and produces seeds in a disk-like structure surrounded by bracts.

It is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
