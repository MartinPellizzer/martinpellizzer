Casparia lunaria

Casparia lunaria, commonly known as the moonflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

It is characterized by its large, white, showy flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant is native to tropical regions of the Americas and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its leaves are typically ovate and have a slightly hairy texture.

Casparia lunaria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique nocturnal blooming habit.
