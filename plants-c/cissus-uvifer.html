Cissus uvifer

Cissus uvifer, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-colored leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its stems are flexible and can root at the nodes, allowing it to climb and spread easily.

Cissus uvifer is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
