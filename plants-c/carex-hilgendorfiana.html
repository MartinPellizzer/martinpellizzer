Carex hilgendorfiana

Carex hilgendorfiana, commonly known as the Hilgendorf's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, bogs, and the edges of ponds. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.

Carex hilgendorfiana plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
