Croton zeylanicus

Croton zeylanicus, commonly known as the Ceylon croton, is a flowering plant native to southern Asia, particularly Sri Lanka and parts of India.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring thick, waxy leaves with a distinctive reddish or yellowish hue. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by orange or red berries containing seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal due to its vibrant foliage.
