Cylindrolobus kandarianus

Cylindrolobus kandarianus, commonly known as the Kandarian cylinder-lobed plant, is a rare flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical stems and lobed leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is the subject of conservation efforts.
