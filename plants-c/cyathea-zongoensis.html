Cyathea zongoensis

Cyathea zongoensis, commonly known as the Zongo tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the humid forests of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, sturdy trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays a significant role in the ecosystem as a habitat for various organisms.

The fronds are typically evergreen, providing year-round foliage in its natural environment.
