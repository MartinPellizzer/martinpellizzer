Calendula linifolia

Calendula linifolia, commonly known as slender calendula, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its yellow to orange flowers. The plant has linear, slender leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Calendula linifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
