Ceropegia attenuata var. mookambikae

Ceropegia attenuata var. mookambikae, commonly known as the Mookambikai ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a perennial succulent with fleshy, trailing stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces distinctive, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
