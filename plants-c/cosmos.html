Cosmos

Cosmos, commonly known as the garden cosmos, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are native to Central America and the southwestern United States, and they thrive in temperate climates. Cosmos species are characterized by their showy, daisy-like flowers that come in shades of pink, white, and purple. They have feathery foliage and are often cultivated for their ornamental value in gardens.

The plant is also popular for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
