Cavendishia uniflora

Cavendishia uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower orchid, is a rare and exquisite member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of Brazil and is characterized by its striking, solitary flower that blooms in a vibrant shade of purple or pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and slightly fleshy, adapted to store moisture in the humid, tropical environment.

Due to its rarity and unique beauty, Cavendishia uniflora is highly sought after by orchid enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
