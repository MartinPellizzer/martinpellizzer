Carex contigua var. longissima

Carex contigua var. longissima, commonly known as the long-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. This variety is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that can grow up to several inches in length. It typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds.

The plant produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers that bloom in the summer months.
