Crataegus turnerorum

Crataegus turnerorum, commonly known as Turner's hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in California and Nevada. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, small white or pink flowers, and red or orange berries that appear in the fall. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

The botanical features of Crataegus turnerorum make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
