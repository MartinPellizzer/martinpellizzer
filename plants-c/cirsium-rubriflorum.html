Cirsium rubriflorum

Cirsium rubriflorum, commonly known as the redflower thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the western United States and is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and purple to red flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs, and it produces a distinctive, prickly fruit known as an achene. Cirsium rubriflorum thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
