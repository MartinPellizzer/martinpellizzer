Connaropsis glabra

Connaropsis glabra, commonly known as the smooth connar, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Connaraceae and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
