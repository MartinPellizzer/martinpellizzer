Cucumis sativus var. anglicus

Cucumis sativus var. anglicus, commonly known as the English cucumber, is a cultivar of the cucumber plant characterized by its long, slender shape and smooth, thin skin.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to southern Europe and western Asia. The plant produces monoecious flowers, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant, and fruits that are typically harvested when still immature for optimal flavor and texture.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight for optimal growth.
