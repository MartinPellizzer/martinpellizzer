Calamintha subisodonta

Calamintha subisodonta, commonly known as narrow-leaved calamint, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant features slender, upright stems with opposite, narrow, and serrated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, tubular flowers are typically violet or blue, arranged in dense spikes, and bloom during the summer months.

Calamintha subisodonta is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
