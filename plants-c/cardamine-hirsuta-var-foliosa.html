Cardamine hirsuta var. foliosa

Cardamine hirsuta var. foliosa, commonly known as the hairy watercress, is a variant of the common watercress species.

It is a small, herbaceous plant with alternate, pinnately compound leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in a raceme.

The leaves and stems are edible, and the plant is often used in salads or as a garnish in culinary applications.
