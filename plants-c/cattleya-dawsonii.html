Cattleya dawsonii

Cattleya dawsonii, commonly known as the Dawson's orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from pale pink to deep red, often with intricate patterns. The plant has erect, hairy pseudobulbs that support its evergreen leaves, which are strap-shaped and leathery. It blooms in late summer or early autumn, producing up to 10 flowers per spike.

This species is valued in horticulture for its beauty and fragrance, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
