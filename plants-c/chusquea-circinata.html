Chusquea circinata

Chusquea circinata, commonly known as the golden bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit. The plant features narrow, elongated leaves that are often a striking golden-yellow color, especially when mature. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions.

Chusquea circinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
