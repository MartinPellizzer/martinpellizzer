Caprifolium purpurascens

Caprifolium purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-flowered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces small, red or orange berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have serrated edges, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Caprifolium purpurascens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.
