Clonodia biglandulosa

Clonodia biglandulosa, commonly known as the hairy glandular shrub, is a perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, opposite leaves covered in fine, glandular hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically arranged in terminal inflorescences. Its botanical features include the presence of numerous glandular structures on both the leaves and stems, which contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Clonodia biglandulosa is often found in moist, wooded areas and is adapted to thrive in partial to full shade.
