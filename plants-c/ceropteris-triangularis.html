Ceropteris triangularis

Ceropteris triangularis, commonly known as the triangular shield fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive triangular-shaped fronds that are divided into smaller, lanceolate segments. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are creeping and can form dense colonies, contributing to its ability to thrive in various habitats.

The fronds are often used in traditional medicine and horticulture due to their ornamental value.
