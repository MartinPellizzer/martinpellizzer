Cyclanthera pedata var. edulis

Cyclanthera pedata var. edulis, commonly known as the edible bitter gourd, is a climbing vine belonging to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America and is characterized by its long, slender, and often ribbed fruits. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The fruit is typically harvested when it is still immature, as it becomes too bitter when fully mature.

It is valued for its nutritional content and is used in various traditional cuisines.
