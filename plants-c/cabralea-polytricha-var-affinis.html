Cabralea polytricha var. affinis

Cabralea polytricha var. affinis, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or Brazilian cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its attractive, fragrant flowers and distinctive, often twisted wood. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are bipinnate, and the flowers are clustered in dense, terminal inflorescences.

The wood of this species is highly valued for its rich color and workability, making it a sought-after material in musical instrument manufacturing.
