Campanula rotundifolia subsp. xylorrhiza

Campanula rotundifolia subsp.

xylorrhiza, commonly known as the round-leaf bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are rounded and often have a waxy coating, which helps retain moisture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
