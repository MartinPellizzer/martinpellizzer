Campanula sclerotricha subsp. tchitounyi

Campanula sclerotricha subsp.

tchitounyi, commonly known as the Tchitouny campanula, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to violet in color.

The leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
