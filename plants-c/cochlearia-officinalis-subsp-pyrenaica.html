Cochlearia officinalis subsp. pyrenaica

Cochlearia officinalis subsp.

pyrenaica, commonly known as the Pyrenean cochlearia, is a flowering plant native to the Pyrenees mountain range. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and capsule fruits.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, showcasing adaptations to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, rosette-forming, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological niche.
