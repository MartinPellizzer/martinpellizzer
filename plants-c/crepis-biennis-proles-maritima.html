Crepis biennis proles maritima

Crepis biennis proles maritima, commonly known as the sea hawksbeard, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline, where it thrives in salty and windy conditions. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a distinctive central disk.

This species is known for its resilience and is often found in maritime environments.
