Chenopodina vera

Chenopodina vera, commonly known as the common goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, often found in disturbed soils. The plant has alternate, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of whitish powder, which helps reduce water loss. Its small, clustered flowers are usually greenish-white and appear in the summer months.

The plant is adapted to thrive in harsh environments, making it an important species in ecological studies of desert flora.
