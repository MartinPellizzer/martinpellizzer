Copaifera disperma

Copaifera disperma, commonly known as Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, spreading canopy and smooth, reddish-brown bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and elongated, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy surface.

Copaifera disperma is valued for its resin, which has been used traditionally for medicinal and aromatic purposes.
