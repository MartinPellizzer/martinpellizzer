Carduus camporum

Carduus camporum, commonly known as the field thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features robust, spiny stems and large, silvery-green leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces purple or pinkish composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in meadows.

Botanically, it belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads.
