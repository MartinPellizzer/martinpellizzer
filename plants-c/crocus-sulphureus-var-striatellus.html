Crocus sulphureus var. striatellus

Crocus sulphureus var. striatellus, commonly known as the golden crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It produces showy, saucer-shaped flowers that are typically yellow to orange in color, with a distinctive striated pattern on the petals. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often blooming in early spring. Its bulbs are relatively hardy and can be propagated through division or seed.

This variety is prized for its vibrant color and is often cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
