Cyathea podophylla

Cyathea podophylla, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in tropical rainforests. Its spores are produced on the underside of its fronds, and it reproduces through sexual reproduction via spores rather than seeds.

Cyathea podophylla plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
