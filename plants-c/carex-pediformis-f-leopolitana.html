Carex pediformis f. leopolitana

Carex pediformis f. leopolitana, commonly known as the Leopolitana sedge, is a variant of the pediformis sedge species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Europe, particularly in the Carpathian Basin. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for wetland restoration and ecological landscaping.
