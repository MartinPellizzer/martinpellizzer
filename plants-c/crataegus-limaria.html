Crataegus limaria

Crataegus limaria, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its spiny branches and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces white or pinkish flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the autumn. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Crataegus limaria is often found in woodlands, hedgerows, and along roadsides.
