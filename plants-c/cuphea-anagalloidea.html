Cuphea anagalloidea

Cuphea anagalloidea, commonly known as the flame flower or redstem cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, opposite leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a herbaceous perennial that prefers well-drained soil and is often used in ornamental gardening.

Cuphea anagalloidea is also valued for its ability to attract wildlife and its drought tolerance.
