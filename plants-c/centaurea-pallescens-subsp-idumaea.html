Centaurea pallescens subsp. idumaea

Centaurea pallescens subsp.

idumaea, commonly known as the pale centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in open habitats such as steppes and rocky areas.

Its botanical features make it an attractive plant for dry garden environments.
