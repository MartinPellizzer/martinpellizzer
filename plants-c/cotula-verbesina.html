Cotula verbesina

Cotula verbesina, commonly known as golden weed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in clusters, and the leaves are usually hairy and alternate along the stem.

Cotula verbesina is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
