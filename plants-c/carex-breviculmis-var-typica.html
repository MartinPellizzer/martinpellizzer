Carex breviculmis var. typica

Carex breviculmis var. typica, commonly known as the short-bracted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and short, broad leaves.

This sedge plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
