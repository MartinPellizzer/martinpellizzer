Cassida galericulata

Cassida galericulata, commonly known as the golden-eyed cup, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange composite flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets, with the central florets being tubular and yellow.

Cassida galericulata is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges.
