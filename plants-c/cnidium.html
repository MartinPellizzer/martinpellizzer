Cnidium

Cnidium, commonly known as Chinese pepper or snake root, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic, often hairy leaves and small, yellowish flowers that typically grow in umbels. The plant contains various bioactive compounds, including essential oils and alkaloids, which contribute to its medicinal uses. Cnidium species are native to Asia and are often found in woodland and grassland habitats.

Due to its chemical composition, it has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and circulatory benefits.
