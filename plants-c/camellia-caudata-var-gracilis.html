Camellia caudata var. gracilis

Camellia caudata var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender camellia, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It prefers partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its elegant foliage and prolonged blooming period.
