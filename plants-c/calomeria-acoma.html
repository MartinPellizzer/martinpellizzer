Calomeria acoma

Calomeria acoma, commonly known as the Acoma dandelion, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, dandelion-like flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Calomeria acoma is noted for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various traditional purposes.
