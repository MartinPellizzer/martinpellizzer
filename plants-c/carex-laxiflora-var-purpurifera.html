Carex laxiflora var. purpurifera

Carex laxiflora var. purpurifera, commonly known as the purple-fruited sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces cylindrical spikelets that are often tinged with purple, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small and clustered, with male and female flowers occurring on separate spikes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
