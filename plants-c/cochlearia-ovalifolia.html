Cochlearia ovalifolia

Cochlearia ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaved scurvy grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of oval, hairy leaves and produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. Native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in rocky, nutrient-poor soils. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in moist, exposed environments.

Its flowers are typically arranged in a single, terminal cluster and are visited by various pollinators.
