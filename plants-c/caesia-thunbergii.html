Caesia thunbergii

Caesia thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a violet or purple throat. The plant has slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Caesia thunbergii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.
