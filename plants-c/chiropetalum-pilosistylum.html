Chiropetalum pilosistylum

Chiropetalum pilosistylum, commonly known as the silky sandmat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage that helps reduce water loss in dry environments. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the cooler months. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils.

Chiropetalum pilosistylum is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
