Carex × hibernica

Carex × hibernica, commonly known as the Hibernian sedge, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Carex montana and Carex scoparia.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance and dense, clumping growth habit. The plant produces cylindrical spikes of small, usually green or brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex × hibernica is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
