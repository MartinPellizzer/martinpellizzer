Calytrix curtophylla

Calytrix curtophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calytrix, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Australia.

It features small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring, typically arranged in clusters. The plant has narrow, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

Calytrix curtophylla is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract native pollinators.
