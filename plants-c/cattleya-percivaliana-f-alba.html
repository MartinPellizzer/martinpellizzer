Cattleya percivaliana f. alba

Cattleya percivaliana f. alba, commonly known as the white form of the Percival's Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid species native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with a subtle fragrance, which distinguish it from other varieties of the species. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often displaying a striking contrast between the white petals and the green sepals.

This cultivar is highly prized by orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
