Cussonia sphaerocephala

Cussonia sphaerocephala, commonly known as the globe-headed cussonia, is a striking shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spherical inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant features large, elliptical leaves with prominent veins and a smooth, often greyish bark. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Cussonia sphaerocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
