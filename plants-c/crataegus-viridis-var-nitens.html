Crataegus viridis var. nitens

Crataegus viridis var. nitens, commonly known as the glossy hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are often glossy and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or hedgerows.

The glossy hawthorn is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
