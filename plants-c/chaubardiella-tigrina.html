Chaubardiella tigrina

Chaubardiella tigrina, commonly known as the tiger orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful blooms that resemble tiger stripes, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as rainforests, where it thrives on the forest floor or as an epiphyte on trees. Its flowers are usually fragrant and attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
