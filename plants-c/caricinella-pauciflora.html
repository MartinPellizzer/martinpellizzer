Caricinella pauciflora

Caricinella pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered carica, is a rare and critically endangered plant species native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It produces small, fleshy fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, Caricinella pauciflora is of significant interest for conservation and botanical research.
