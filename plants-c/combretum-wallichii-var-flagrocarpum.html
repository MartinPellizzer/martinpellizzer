Combretum wallichii var. flagrocarpum

Combretum wallichii var. flagrocarpum, commonly known as the red-fruited combretum, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers followed by distinctive red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
