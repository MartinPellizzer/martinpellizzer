Carex fissa var. aristata

Carex fissa var. aristata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is distinguished by its narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered spikelets that produce minute flowers. It typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
