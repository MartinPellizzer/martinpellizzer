Chuquiraga lehmannii

Chuquiraga lehmannii, commonly known as the Lehmann's chuquiraga, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features silver-gray foliage with a dense covering of fine hairs, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators.

Chuquiraga lehmannii is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient, sunny conditions.
