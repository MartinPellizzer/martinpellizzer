Cirsium siculum

Cirsium siculum, commonly known as the Sicilian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features spiny, lobed leaves and produces purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in summer. It is often found in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Cirsium siculum is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
