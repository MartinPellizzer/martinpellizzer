Cestrum bella-sombra

Cestrum bella-sombra, commonly known as the shadow beauty trumpetflower, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep pink, often with a contrasting throat. The plant produces fragrant blooms that attract nocturnal pollinators such as moths and bats. Its leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a glossy surface that enhances its ornamental appeal.

Cestrum bella-sombra thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
