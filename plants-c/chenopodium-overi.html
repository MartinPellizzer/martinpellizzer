Chenopodium overi

Chenopodium overi, commonly known as the desert spinach, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding in its survival in dry environments. The plant produces small, green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are edible and have been used traditionally by indigenous peoples for their nutritional value.

Chenopodium overi thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or rocky habitats.
