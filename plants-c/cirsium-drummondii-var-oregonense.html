Cirsium drummondii var. oregonense

Cirsium drummondii var. oregonense, commonly known as Drummond's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of ecological study.
