Carex mildbraediana

Carex mildbraediana, commonly known as the Mildbraed's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in early summer. Native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands and forest understories. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively and form dense mats.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
