Croomia saitoana

Croomia saitoana, commonly known as the Japanese croomia, is a perennial evergreen herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features elliptical, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged in a basal rosette. It produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink during the spring.

Croomia saitoana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.
