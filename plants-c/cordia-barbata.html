Cordia barbata

Cordia barbata, commonly known as the hairy cordia, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries that are edible. Cordia barbata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in some cultures.
