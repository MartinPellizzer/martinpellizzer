Crocus scharojanii

Crocus scharojanii, commonly known as the Armenian crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus, particularly Armenia and Georgia. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in cold climates, and the plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

Crocus scharojanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with cool, temperate conditions.
