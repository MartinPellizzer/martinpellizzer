Cirsium repandum

Cirsium repandum, commonly known as the spreading thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are rough, serrated, and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are purple to pink in color.

Cirsium repandum is known for its ability to thrive in harsh conditions and is often found in disturbed or marginal environments.
