Carmichaelia grandiflora

Carmichaelia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered Carmichaelia, is a species native to New Zealand.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces legumes that are typically hairy and contain small seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine regions.

Carmichaelia grandiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
