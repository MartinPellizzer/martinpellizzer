Cordylanthus ramosus subsp. setosus

Cordylanthus ramosus subsp.

setosus, commonly known as the hairy stalked filaree, is a perennial herb native to California. It belongs to the family Fabaceae, characterized by its typical legume structure and nitrogen-fixing ability.

The plant features slender, branched stems covered in fine, hairy stipules, which give it its subspecies name. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming in late spring to early summer.

It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as chaparral and grasslands.
