Caricina ovalis

Caricina ovalis, commonly known as the oval tamarisk, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its woody stems are often branched and can become quite dense.

Caricina ovalis is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.
