Cruckshanksia densifolia

Cruckshanksia densifolia, commonly known as the denseleaf cruckshanksia, is a unique flowering plant native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the wet season. The plant's leaves are thick and leathery, which helps it retain moisture in its humid, shaded habitat. It typically grows as an understory plant, reaching heights of up to 2 meters.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a conservation concern in its native range.
