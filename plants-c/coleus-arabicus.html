Coleus arabicus

Coleus arabicus, commonly known as the Arabian coleus, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its square stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
