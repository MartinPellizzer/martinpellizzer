Ceropegia schinziana

Ceropegia schinziana, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers bright, indirect light.

Ceropegia schinziana is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
