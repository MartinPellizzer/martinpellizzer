Corynephorus canescens var. intermedia

Corynephorus canescens var. intermedia, commonly known as the silver-haired grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-gray foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing branches.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
