Cyperus chersinus var. angularis

Cyperus chersinus var. angularis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known for their distinctive triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
