Crotalaria hookeri

Crotalaria hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's crotalaria, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its seeds are enclosed in a pod-like fruit, and the plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in certain agricultural contexts.

However, it can also be invasive in some areas, outcompeting native vegetation.
