Campanula chrysosplenifolia

Campanula chrysosplenifolia, commonly known as the golden spleenwort campanula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, pale blue to white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive silver-green color due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

Campanula chrysosplenifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, cascading blooms.
