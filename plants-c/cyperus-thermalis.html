Cyperus thermalis

Cyperus thermalis, commonly known as the thermal sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, clustered flowers that are often inconspicuous, and it is adapted to thrive in moist or waterlogged soils.

Cyperus thermalis is often used in ornamental gardening due to its ability to tolerate varying water conditions.
