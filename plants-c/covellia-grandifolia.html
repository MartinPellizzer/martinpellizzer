Covellia grandifolia

Covellia grandifolia, commonly known as the grandiflora orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant has strap-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Covellia grandifolia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.

Its growth habits and floral structure make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
