Cynorkis aphylla

Cynorkis aphylla, commonly known as the leafless orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its absence of leaves, which is a rare trait among flowering plants. Instead of leaves, it possesses a slender, leafless stem that supports its single, showy flower. The plant is native to certain regions of Africa and thrives in specific microhabitats with high humidity.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptations to nutrient-poor environments and its role in ecological studies of plant morphology.
