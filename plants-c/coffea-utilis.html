Coffea utilis

Coffea utilis, commonly known as the Liberian coffee, is a species of flowering plant in the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to West Africa, particularly found in countries like Liberia and Sierra Leone. The plant produces red, fleshy berries that contain the seeds used for coffee production. It typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

The leaves are alternate, elliptical, and have a glossy appearance, while the flowers are small and white with a strong fragrance.
