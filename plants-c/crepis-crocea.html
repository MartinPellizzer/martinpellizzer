Crepis crocea

Crepis crocea, commonly known as the golden hairgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to Europe and parts of western Asia, it thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant produces small, oval-shaped seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
