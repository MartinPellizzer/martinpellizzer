Centaurea postii

Centaurea postii, commonly known as the Italian thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green leaves and purple to pink bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea postii is an annual or biennial species, depending on the climate, and is known for its hardy, drought-resistant nature.
