Ceropegia vaduliae

Ceropegia vaduliae, commonly known as the Cape York ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent perennial that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features fleshy, tuberous roots and produces small, tubular flowers that are often pale green or yellowish. It is adapted to arid environments and exhibits drought resistance.

Its unique floral structure attracts specific pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance in its native habitat.
