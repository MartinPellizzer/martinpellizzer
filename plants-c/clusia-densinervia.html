Clusia densinervia

Clusia densinervia, commonly known as the wax plant or broadleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Clusia densinervia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its unique foliage.
