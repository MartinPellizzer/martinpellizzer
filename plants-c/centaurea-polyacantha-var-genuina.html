Centaurea polyacantha var. genuina

Centaurea polyacantha var. genuina, commonly known as the Narrowleaf Cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a silvery underside, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
