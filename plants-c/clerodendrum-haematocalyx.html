Clerodendrum haematocalyx

Clerodendrum haematocalyx, commonly known as the blood-red clerodendrum, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers, which are clustered in dense inflorescences and attract various pollinators. The plant has heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clerodendrum haematocalyx is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
