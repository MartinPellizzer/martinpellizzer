Copaiba disperma

Copaiba disperma, commonly known as yellow copaiba, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, resinous sap. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has elongated, elliptical leaves. Its bark and resin have been traditionally used for their medicinal properties.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in rainforest ecosystems.
