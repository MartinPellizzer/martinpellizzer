Cereus tonelianus

Cereus tonelianus, commonly known as the Tonelli's cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its spiny, ribbed stems and ability to store water efficiently. The plant produces large, showy flowers that typically bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its growth habit is slow, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.

The species is adapted to extreme drought conditions, showcasing remarkable resilience in harsh environments.
