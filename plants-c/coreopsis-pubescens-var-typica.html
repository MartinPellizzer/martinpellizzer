Coreopsis pubescens var. typica

Coreopsis pubescens var. typica, commonly known as the rough coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring hairy stems and lobed, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with a central disk and ray florets, blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native, drought-tolerant plants.

Its ecological value includes providing nectar for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
