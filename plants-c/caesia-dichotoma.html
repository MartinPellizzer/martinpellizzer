Caesia dichotoma

Caesia dichotoma, commonly known as the two-branched orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

The flowers, which bloom in clusters, are often pink or white and have a delicate, orchid-like appearance.
