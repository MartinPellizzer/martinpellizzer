Chamaemelum colchicum

Chamaemelum colchicum, commonly known as the common tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It is known for its strong, aromatic scent and has been historically used in traditional medicine.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
