Clypea consummata

Clypea consummata, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Clypea consummata prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

It is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
