Chrysanthemum tibeticum

Chrysanthemum tibeticum, commonly known as the Tibetan chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
