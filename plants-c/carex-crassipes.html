Carex crassipes

Carex crassipes, commonly known as broadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and dense clusters of spikelets. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common component of marshes and pond margins. Its leaves are broad and flat, often with a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Carex crassipes plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
