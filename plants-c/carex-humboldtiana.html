Carex humboldtiana

Carex humboldtiana, commonly known as Humboldt's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland conservation.
