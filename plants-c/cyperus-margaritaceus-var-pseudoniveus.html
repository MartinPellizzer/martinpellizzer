Cyperus margaritaceus var. pseudoniveus

Cyperus margaritaceus var. pseudoniveus, commonly known as the silver flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Its ornamental value comes from its unique foliage, making it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
