Cucumis melo subsp. chate

Cucumis melo subsp.

chate, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family. It is characterized by its long, slender, and often ribbed fruit, which can range in color from pale green to yellowish.

The plant typically grows as a vine, with palmately lobed leaves and yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is valued for its crisp texture and mild flavor, making it a popular choice in various culinary applications.
