Crambe orientalis var. mitridatis

Crambe orientalis var. mitridatis, commonly known as the oriental crambe, is a rare and ornamental plant native to parts of Eastern Europe and the Caucasus.

It is a biennial or short-lived perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy texture and produces yellow flowers in racemes during the summer months. Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and historical use in traditional medicine.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and botanical collections.
