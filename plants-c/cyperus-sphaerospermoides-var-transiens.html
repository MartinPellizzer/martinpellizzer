Cyperus sphaerospermoides var. transiens

Cyperus sphaerospermoides var. transiens, commonly known as the transitional spherical sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
