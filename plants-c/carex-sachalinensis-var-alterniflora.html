Carex sachalinensis var. alterniflora

Carex sachalinensis var. alterniflora, commonly known as the Sachalin sedge, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower spikes that are typically green or reddish in color. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wetland environments.
