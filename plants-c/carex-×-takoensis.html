Carex × takoensis

Carex × takoensis, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, typical of the sedge genus. This hybrid species is often found in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its reproductive structures include small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into three-lobed fruits.

Carex × takoensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
