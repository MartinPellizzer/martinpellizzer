Cynosurus cristatus

Cynosurus cristatus, commonly known as the tufted hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive tufted inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, woodlands, and disturbed soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Cynosurus cristatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
