Colythrum febrifugum

Colythrum febrifugum, commonly known as the feverfew plant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, lobed leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its historical use in treating fevers and headaches has contributed to its common name.
