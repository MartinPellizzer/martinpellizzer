Canavalia gladiata f. leucocarpa

Canavalia gladiata f. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white horse bean, is a variant of the common bean plant characterized by its distinctive white seed pods.

This plant belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. It features trifoliate leaves and produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds. The white coloration of the pods and seeds is a notable botanical trait of this variety.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible seeds.
