Cupressus stephensonii

Cupressus stephensonii, commonly known as the Stewart cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, pyramidal shape and scale-like leaves that are dark green and closely arranged. The plant produces small, spherical cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
