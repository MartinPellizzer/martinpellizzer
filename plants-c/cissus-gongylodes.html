Cissus gongylodes

Cissus gongylodes, commonly known as the bone leaf or elephant foot plant, is a climbing vine native to Madagascar.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
