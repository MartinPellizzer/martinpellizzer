Campanula napuligera f. parvula

Campanula napuligera f. parvula, commonly known as the small-flowered bellflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the Mediterranean region.

It features clusters of delicate, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant flowers and compact growth habit.
