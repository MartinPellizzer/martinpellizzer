Chamaerhodos trifida

Chamaerhodos trifida, commonly known as the three-lobed flame flower, is a striking member of the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in wetland habitats such as swamps and marshes. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red bracts that surround its small, inconspicuous flowers. Its foliage consists of alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

Chamaerhodos trifida is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local ecosystems.
