Coryanthes bahiensis

Coryanthes bahiensis, commonly known as the Bahia candleplant, is a tropical orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its unique, candle-like flower structure with a long, slender spadix surrounded by a colorful, hooded bract. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive, elongated shape that resembles a candle.

Coryanthes bahiensis is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses or conservatories.
