Cuphea speciosa

Cuphea speciosa, commonly known as the flame flower, is a striking plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in soft, fuzzy hairs. Its foliage is lance-shaped and often displays a reddish or purplish hue.

Cuphea speciosa thrives in full sun to partial shade and is well-suited for garden borders or containers.
