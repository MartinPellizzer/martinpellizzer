Cereus trigonus var. quadrangularis

Cereus trigonus var. quadrangularis, commonly known as the angular moon cactus, is a fascinating cactus species native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its unique, angular, and ridged stem that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces striking, night-blooming flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in xeriscaped gardens.
