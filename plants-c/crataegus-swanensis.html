Crataegus swanensis

Crataegus swanensis, commonly known as Swan's hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to the eastern United States, particularly in regions of Virginia and North Carolina. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in the spring. Its fruit, a small red berry, matures in the fall and is an important food source for birds and small mammals.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological contributions to its native habitat.
