Cactus prismaticus

Cactus prismaticus, commonly known as the Rainbow Cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored stems that shift hues depending on light exposure, giving it its striking appearance. The plant features spiny, segmented stems that store water efficiently, allowing it to thrive in dry environments. Its flowers, which bloom in spring, are typically yellow or orange and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
