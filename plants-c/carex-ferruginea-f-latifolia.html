Carex ferruginea f. latifolia

Carex ferruginea f. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved rustgrass, is a perennial sedge native to temperate regions of Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a rusty brownish-red in color, especially along the edges. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually green or reddish, adding visual interest to gardens. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
