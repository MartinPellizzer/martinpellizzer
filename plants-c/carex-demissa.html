Carex demissa

Carex demissa, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, which contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant produces dark brown, triangular seeds that are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.

Its simple morphology and adaptability make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
