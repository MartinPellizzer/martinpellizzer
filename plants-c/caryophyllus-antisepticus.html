Caryophyllus antisepticus

Caryophyllus antisepticus, commonly known as the antiseptic caryophyllus, is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers. The plant is native to certain regions of Europe and is often found in meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, while its stems are slender and branching.

The plant is noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for its antiseptic and anti-inflammatory benefits.
