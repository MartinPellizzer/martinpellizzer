Chavica boehmeriifolia

Chavica boehmeriifolia, commonly known as the Boehmer's chavica, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or borders.

Chavica boehmeriifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
