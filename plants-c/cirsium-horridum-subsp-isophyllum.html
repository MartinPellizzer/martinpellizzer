Cirsium horridum subsp. isophyllum

Cirsium horridum subsp.

isophyllum, commonly known as the purple thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States. It typically grows in dry, disturbed areas such as roadsides and open woodlands.

The plant features spiny, upright stems and large, purple-tinged, lobed leaves. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, with a distinctive purple bract beneath the flower head.

It is characterized by its reduced leaf structure compared to other species in the genus Cirsium.
