Cyrtanthus sanguineus subsp. sanguineus

Cyrtanthus sanguineus subsp.

sanguineus, commonly known as the blood lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa. It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to pink, often with a contrasting yellow throat.

The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces a fleshy, underground bulb that stores water and nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early spring blooming habit.
