Crusea coccinea

Crusea coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet crusea, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract a variety of pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 3 feet. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a glossy texture.

The botanical name reflects its vivid coloration, with "coccinea" derived from the Latin word for "scarlet.".
