Callitriche autumnalis f. replicata-minor

Callitriche autumnalis f. replicata-minor, commonly known as the lesser water starwort, is a semi-aquatic plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or damp soil, often found in ponds, ditches, and wet meadows. The plant has small, floating or submerged leaves that are usually oval or lance-shaped. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in dense inflorescences, often appearing in late summer to early autumn.

It plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates.
