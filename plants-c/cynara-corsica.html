Cynara corsica

Cynara corsica, commonly known as the Tuscan artichoke, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, thistle-like foliage. The plant features a thick, edible stem and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in cooking. Its flower heads develop into showy purple blooms, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cynara corsica is valued both for its culinary uses and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
