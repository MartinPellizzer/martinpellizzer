Cleyera parvifolia

Cleyera parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved cleyera, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers in clusters during the spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that turns red when mature.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
