Convolvulus erythrocarpus

Convolvulus erythrocarpus, commonly known as the red-fruited bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a sprawling perennial that produces small, tubular flowers in shades of pink to purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces bright red, fleshy fruits that are adapted for animal dispersal. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing along roadsides and in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and horticultural gardens.
