Campanula longistyla

Campanula longistyla, commonly known as the long-styled bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy appearance. Its scientific name reflects the elongated style of its flowers, a distinctive feature of the species.

Campanula longistyla is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
