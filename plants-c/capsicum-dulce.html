Capsicum dulce

Capsicum dulce, commonly known as the sweet pepper, is a member of the Solanaceae family.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy walls and a wide range of colors, including red, yellow, orange, and green. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and alternate leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or white and are followed by fruits that develop from the ovary.

Capsicum dulce is cultivated for its edible fruits, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants.
