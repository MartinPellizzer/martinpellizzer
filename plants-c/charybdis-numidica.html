Charybdis numidica

Charybdis numidica, commonly known as the Mediterranean sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers and is often found growing in coastal areas and saline soils. The plant has a compact growth habit with silver-gray foliage that reflects sunlight. It is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.

Charybdis numidica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
