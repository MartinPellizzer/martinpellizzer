Cuphea trochilus

Cuphea trochilus, commonly known as the hummingbird flower or red cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces vibrant, tubular red or pink flowers, which attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and grows to a height of about 1 to 3 feet. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cuphea trochilus is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to bloom throughout the growing season.
