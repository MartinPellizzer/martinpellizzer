Calea sickii

Calea sickii, commonly known as the sacred candle plant, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, candle-like inflorescence. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Calea sickii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in some indigenous communities.
