Convolvulus argyrophyllus

Convolvulus argyrophyllus, commonly known as silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue to purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

Convolvulus argyrophyllus is a perennial herb that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
