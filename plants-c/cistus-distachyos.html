Cistus distachyos

Cistus distachyos, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and clusters of pink or white flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for arid environments. Its leaves are covered in fine, glandular hairs that secrete essential oils, contributing to its strong scent.

Cistus distachyos is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
