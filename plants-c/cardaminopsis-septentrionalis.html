Cardaminopsis septentrionalis

Cardaminopsis septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern rockcress, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many species with small, cross-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers bloom in late spring or early summer.

The species is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in mountainous or tundra-like habitats.
