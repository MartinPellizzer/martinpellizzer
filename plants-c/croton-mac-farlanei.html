Croton mac-farlanei

Croton mac-farlanei, commonly known as the MacFarlane's croton, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are often brightly colored and vary in shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
