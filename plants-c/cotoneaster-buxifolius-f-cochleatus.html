Cotoneaster buxifolius f. cochleatus

Cotoneaster buxifolius f. cochleatus, commonly known as the spiral-leaved cotoneaster, is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its unique, spirally arranged leaves.

The foliage is dense and glossy, with leaves that are oval to elliptic in shape and often have a slight waxy coating. It produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are attractive to birds. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
