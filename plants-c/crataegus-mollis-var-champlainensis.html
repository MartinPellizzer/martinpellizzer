Crataegus mollis var. champlainensis

Crataegus mollis var. champlainensis, commonly known as the Champlain hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and oval, dark green leaves that turn reddish in autumn. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
