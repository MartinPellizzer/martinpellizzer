Cerastium vulgare subsp. triviale

Cerastium vulgare subsp.

triviale, commonly known as mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It features small, hairy, ovate leaves and produces clusters of white or pink flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and garden beds. Its leaves are often covered in fine, soft hairs, which help reduce water loss.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family, known for its showy, often pink or white flowers.
