Carpinus × oxycarpa var. betuloides

Carpinus × oxycarpa var. betuloides, commonly known as the European hornbeam, is a hybrid species within the Betulaceae family.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by winged fruits that aid in wind dispersal. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along riverbanks.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
