Casearia subsessiliflora

Casearia subsessiliflora, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually smooth and dark green on top. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale blue flowers, followed by bright purple to blue berries that are highly ornamental. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and berries.

The species is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
