Callitriche aquatica subsp. stagnalis

Callitriche aquatica subsp.

stagnalis, commonly known as water starwort, is a small aquatic plant that thrives in still or slow-moving freshwater environments. It typically grows in shallow waters, such as ponds, ditches, and wetlands, where it can establish dense mats on the water surface or along the margins.

The plant has whorled leaves that are often floating or submerged, and it produces tiny, star-shaped flowers that are usually white or pale pink. Its reproductive strategy involves both sexual reproduction through seeds and vegetative propagation via stem fragments.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize sediments and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
