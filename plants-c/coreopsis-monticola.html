Coreopsis monticola

Coreopsis monticola, commonly known as the mountain coreopsis, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

Coreopsis monticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
