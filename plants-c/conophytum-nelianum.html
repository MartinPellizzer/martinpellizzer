Conophytum nelianum

Conophytum nelianum, commonly known as the "Cape tulip," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, often ribbed leaves that store water. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
