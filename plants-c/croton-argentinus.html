Croton argentinus

Croton argentinus, commonly known as the Argentine croton, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored leaves that often display shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental species. Croton argentinus typically grows as an upright shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
