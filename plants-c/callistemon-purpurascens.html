Callistemon purpurascens

Callistemon purpurascens, commonly known as the purple bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical flower spikes that range in color from red to purple. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers attract nectar-feeding birds and insects, making it an important plant for biodiversity.
