Calyptranthes myrcioides

Calyptranthes myrcioides, commonly known as the Panama wax tree, is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. It produces small, round, woody fruits that contain a single seed.

Calyptranthes myrcioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
