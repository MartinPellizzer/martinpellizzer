Citrus × bergamia var. parva

Citrus × bergamia var. parva, commonly known as the dwarf bergamot, is a compact variant of the bergamot orange.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves and small, round fruits. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in warm, sunny climates. Its fruit, while not as commonly consumed as the regular bergamot, is valued for its aromatic properties.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal and essential oils.
