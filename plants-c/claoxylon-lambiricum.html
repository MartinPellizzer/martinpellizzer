Claoxylon lambiricum

Claoxylon lambiricum, commonly known as Lambir timber or Lambir ironwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has a straight trunk with a broad, spreading crown and dark green, leathery leaves. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Claoxylon lambiricum is often found in lowland and hill dipterocarp forests, contributing to the biodiversity of these ecosystems.
