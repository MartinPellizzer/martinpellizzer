Clematis florida var. fortunei

Clematis florida var. fortunei, commonly known as the Fortune's Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are typically woody and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in partial to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring blooms.
