Crocus arizelus

Crocus arizelus, commonly known as the Azorean crocus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Azores.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces striking violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. Its flowers are characterized by their narrow, recurved petals and a strong, spicy scent.

Crocus arizelus is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open woodland areas.
