Cassia nomame

Cassia nomame, commonly known as Japanese cassia, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain seeds covered in a fine, silky hair. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Cassia nomame is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
