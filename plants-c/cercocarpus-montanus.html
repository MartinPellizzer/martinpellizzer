Cercocarpus montanus

Cercocarpus montanus, commonly known as mountain curlleaf, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that curl at the edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, red or orange fruits that are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.
