Chaetogastra divaricata

Chaetogastra divaricata, commonly known as the variegated spider plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, arching stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by egg-shaped berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Chaetogastra divaricata is popular as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
