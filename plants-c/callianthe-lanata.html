Callianthe lanata

Callianthe lanata, commonly known as the woolly nightshade, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a velvety appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple, blooming in late summer. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Callianthe lanata is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
