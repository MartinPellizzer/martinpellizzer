Calamagrostis urelytra var. parvigluma

Calamagrostis urelytra var. parvigluma, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluejoint grass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-gray hue, and its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle. It has a fibrous root system and is often used in wetland restoration projects.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and ability to thrive in saturated soils.
