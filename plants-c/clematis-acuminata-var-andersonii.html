Clematis acuminata var. andersonii

Clematis acuminata var. andersonii, commonly known as the Anderson's Clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five elliptical leaflets that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and as a climbing plant in shaded areas.
