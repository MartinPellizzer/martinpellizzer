Clerodendrum inaequipetiolatum

Clerodendrum inaequipetiolatum, commonly known as the unequal-leaf clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that vary in size and shape along the stem. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white to pale pink flowers, which are often followed by distinctive, woody, egg-shaped fruits. It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves and flowers are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
