Cyperus aethiops var. tessmannii

Cyperus aethiops var. tessmannii, commonly known as the African flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often forming dense clusters near water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, three-ranked flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This variant is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to the typical Cyperus aethiops, including variations in leaf and flower structure.
