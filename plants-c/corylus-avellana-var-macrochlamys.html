Corylus avellana var. macrochlamys

Corylus avellana var. macrochlamys, commonly known as the large-chalazal hazel, is a variant of the common hazel.

It is characterized by its larger, more robust nuts compared to the standard variety. The plant is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia. Its leaves are typically ovate with serrated margins and turn yellow in autumn.

It produces catkins in early spring, which are essential for pollination.
