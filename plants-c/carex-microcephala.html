Carex microcephala

Carex microcephala, commonly known as the dwarf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex microcephala is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
