Cacalia hypoleuca

Cacalia hypoleuca, commonly known as the paleblue bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and has slender, branching stems covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a rough texture.

Cacalia hypoleuca thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and native plant landscapes.
