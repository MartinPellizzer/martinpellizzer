Campanula carnica subsp. carnica

Campanula carnica subsp.

carnica, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, thriving in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are usually branched and robust.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
