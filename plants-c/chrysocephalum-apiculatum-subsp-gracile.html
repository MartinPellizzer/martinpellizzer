Chrysocephalum apiculatum subsp. gracile

Chrysocephalum apiculatum subsp.

gracile, commonly known as slender sunhemp, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flower heads and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as grasslands and heathlands, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, cylindrical flower heads that bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
