Campanula leucotricha

Campanula leucotricha, commonly known as the white-haired bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Campanula leucotricha is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
