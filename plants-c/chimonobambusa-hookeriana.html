Chimonobambusa hookeriana

Chimonobambusa hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's bamboo, is a species of grass bamboo native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, glossy surface.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative plant.
