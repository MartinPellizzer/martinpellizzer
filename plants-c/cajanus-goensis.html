Cajanus goensis

Cajanus goensis, commonly known as the African groundnut, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and oil. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often grown for both its nutritional value and ornamental appeal.

Cajanus goensis is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a beneficial crop in sustainable agriculture.
