Chamaerhodos mongholica

Chamaerhodos mongholica, commonly known as Mongolian flame grass, is a perennial herb native to the grasslands of Mongolia and northern China.

It features upright, branching stems that bear showy, red-orange inflorescences, which are typical of the Amaranthaceae family. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
