Coleus rivularis

Coleus rivularis, commonly known as the river coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that range from red to purple and green, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near streams or in damp forest undergrowth.

Coleus rivularis is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental foliage.
