Ceropegia fortuita

Ceropegia fortuita, commonly known as the "Beaded String of Hearts," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a distinctive hooded structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia fortuita is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
