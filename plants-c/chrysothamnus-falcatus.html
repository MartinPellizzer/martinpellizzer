Chrysothamnus falcatus

Chrysothamnus falcatus, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bright yellow ray flowers and gray-green, fuzzy foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush steppe ecosystems. It produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.

Chrysothamnus falcatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
