Cassia caymanensis

Cassia caymanensis, commonly known as the Cayman Islands cassia, is a tropical shrub native to the Cayman Islands and parts of the Caribbean.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy soils near the shoreline.

Cassia caymanensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
