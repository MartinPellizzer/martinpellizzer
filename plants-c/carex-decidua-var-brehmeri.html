Carex decidua var. brehmeri

Carex decidua var. brehmeri, commonly known as the Brehmer's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a distinctive bluish-green coloration.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, and are adapted for wind pollination.
