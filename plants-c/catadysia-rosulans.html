Catadysia rosulans

Catadysia rosulans, commonly known as the Rosulate Catadysia, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, rosette-like arrangement of broad, waxy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, which bloom during the dry season. Its unique leaf structure helps it retain moisture in arid environments, making it well-adapted to its native habitat.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant in warmer climates.
