Chenopodium macrospermum var. halophilum

Chenopodium macrospermum var. halophilum, commonly known as saltbush, is a halophyte plant adapted to saline environments.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water and reduce water loss. The plant typically grows in coastal areas, salt marshes, and other saline soils, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion. Its small, greenish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, round seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ability to thrive in harsh, salty conditions, making it an important plant in ecological restoration projects.
