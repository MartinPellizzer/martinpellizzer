Campanula liliifolia

Campanula liliifolia, commonly known as the Lily of the Valley bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features delicate, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, which bloom in early summer. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Campanula liliifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
