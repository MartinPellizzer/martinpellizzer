Coreopsis filifolia

Coreopsis filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves and bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers.

Coreopsis filifolia is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
