Chenopodium superalbum

Chenopodium superalbum, commonly known as white goosefoot, is an annual flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, broadly ovate leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish flowers in clusters, which are followed by three-angled, smooth, greenish-brown seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a common weed in agricultural settings.
