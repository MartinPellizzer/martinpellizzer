Cyperus platyculmis

Cyperus platyculmis, commonly known as the flatleaf sedgeland, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wetland and marshy environments. The plant produces tiny, three-petaled flowers that are typically green or brown in color.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands in suitable habitats.
