Crataegus × fallacina

Crataegus × fallacina, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a hybrid species within the genus Crataegus, native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated edges, and it is often used in hedgerows and ornamental landscaping.

Crataegus × fallacina is also valued for its role in ecological systems and its historical use in traditional medicine.
