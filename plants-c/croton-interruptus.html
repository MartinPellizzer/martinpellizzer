Croton interruptus

Croton interruptus, commonly known as the red croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored leaves that range from green to red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
