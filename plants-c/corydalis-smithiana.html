Corydalis smithiana

Corydalis smithiana, commonly known as the Smith's fumitory, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are slender and branching.

Corydalis smithiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ecological benefits.
