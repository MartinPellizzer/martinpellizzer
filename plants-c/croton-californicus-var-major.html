Croton californicus var. major

Croton californicus var. major, commonly known as the California croton, is a shrub native to coastal regions of California.

It features broad, waxy leaves that are typically green with reddish or purple veins, adding visual interest to its foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant leaf coloration and adaptability to coastal climates.
