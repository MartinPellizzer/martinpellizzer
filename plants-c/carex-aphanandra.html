Carex aphanandra

Carex aphanandra, commonly known as the small-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. Carex aphanandra thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
