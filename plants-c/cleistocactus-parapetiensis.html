Cleistocactus parapetiensis

Cleistocactus parapetiensis, commonly known as the Parapetiensis Cactus, is a species native to the Andes mountains of Peru.

It is a low-growing, spineless cactus that typically forms dense clusters of stems. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning, often before sunrise. Its leaves are modified into short, cylindrical stems that store water, adapting to its arid environment.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and as a houseplant.
