Costus microcephalus

Costus microcephalus, commonly known as the dwarf costus, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Costaceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern at the base.

Costus microcephalus is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded to partially sunny conditions.
