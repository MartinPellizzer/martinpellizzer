Cyperus areolatus

Cyperus areolatus, commonly known as the spiny flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance.

It is a perennial herb with slender, erect stems that often grow in dense clusters. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Cyperus areolatus is typically found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Its unique botanical traits make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration efforts.
