Cyathea bellisquamata var. basilobata

Cyathea bellisquamata var. basilobata, commonly known as the Basilobate Cyathea, is a fern species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, compound fronds that can reach lengths of up to two meters. The fronds are typically divided into numerous pinnae, which are further subdivided into smaller pinnules. This variety is notable for its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring a unique arrangement of serrations and a broad, leathery texture.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
