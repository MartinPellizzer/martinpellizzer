Cyperus galapagensis

Cyperus galapagensis, commonly known as the Galápagos flatsedge, is a grass-like plant native to the Galápagos Islands.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet, coastal environments, such as sandy shores and tidal zones. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads.

Cyperus galapagensis plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
