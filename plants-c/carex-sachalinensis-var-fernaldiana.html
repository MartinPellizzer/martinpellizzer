Carex sachalinensis var. fernaldiana

Carex sachalinensis var. fernaldiana, commonly known as the Fernald's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is distinguished by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. It typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
