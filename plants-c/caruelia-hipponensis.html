Caruelia hipponensis

Caruelia hipponensis, commonly known as the Spanish thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features spiny leaves and produces purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. Its botanical structure includes a robust stem with rough, lobed foliage.

Caruelia hipponensis is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
