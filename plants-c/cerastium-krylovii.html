Cerastium krylovii

Cerastium krylovii, commonly known as the Krylov's mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically oval or rounded, with a distinctive fuzzy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or partially shaded woodland areas.

Cerastium krylovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to form dense mats, making it suitable for ground cover in gardens.
