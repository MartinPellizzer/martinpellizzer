Carex kochiana

Carex kochiana, commonly known as the Koch's sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is usually dark green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

Carex kochiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
