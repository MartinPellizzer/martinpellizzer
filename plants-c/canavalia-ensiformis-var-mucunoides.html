Canavalia ensiformis var. mucunoides

Canavalia ensiformis var. mucunoides, commonly known as the mucunoid morning glory, is a climbing legume native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of purple and blue. The plant is characterized by its strong, twining vines that can reach lengths of several meters. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

The seeds are encased in a fibrous pod and are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein in some cultures.
