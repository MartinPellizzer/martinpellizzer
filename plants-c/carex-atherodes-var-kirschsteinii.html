Carex atherodes var. kirschsteinii

Carex atherodes var. kirschsteinii, commonly known as the Kirschstein sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like foliage and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in woodland or wetland habitats. Its narrow, linear leaves are usually a bluish-green color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

The variety kirschsteinii is distinguished by its compact growth habit and subtle coloration, making it a desirable ornamental plant in gardens.
