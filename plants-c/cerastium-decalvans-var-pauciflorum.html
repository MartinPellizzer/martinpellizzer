Cerastium decalvans var. pauciflorum

Cerastium decalvans var. pauciflorum, commonly known as the few-flowered mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically hairy and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.
