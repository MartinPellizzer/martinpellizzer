Culcasia annetii

Culcasia annetii, commonly known as the giant arrowroot, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. Its rhizomes are edible and have a starchy texture similar to taro, making it a valuable food source.

Culcasia annetii thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
