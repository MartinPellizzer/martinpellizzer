Centaurea akroteriensis

Centaurea akroteriensis, commonly known as the Akroterion centaury, is a flowering plant native to the island of Rhodes in the Mediterranean.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its blue to purple flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
