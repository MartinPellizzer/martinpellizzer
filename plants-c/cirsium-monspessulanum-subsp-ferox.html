Cirsium monspessulanum subsp. ferox

Cirsium monspessulanum subsp.

ferox, commonly known as the fierce thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, purple to pinkish-purple flower heads.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped with rough, serrated edges and a thick, waxy coating. This subspecies is known for its robust growth and ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
