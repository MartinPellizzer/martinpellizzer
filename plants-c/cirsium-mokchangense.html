Cirsium mokchangense

Cirsium mokchangense, commonly known as the Mokchang thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Korea.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and robust, spiny foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its flowers are usually purple or pink, with a distinctive central disc surrounded by ray florets.

Cirsium mokchangense is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
