Coelogyne hamata

Coelogyne hamata, commonly known as the spiked orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, long, and often fragrant spikes of flowers that bloom in shades of white, yellow, and orange. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and typically grows as an epiphyte on trees or rocks. Its roots are fibrous and adapted for absorbing moisture from the air.

Coelogyne hamata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
