Chassalia verticillata

Chassalia verticillata, commonly known as the clustered chassalia, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is relatively drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.
