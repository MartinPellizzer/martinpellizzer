Canarium hirsutum var. leeuwenii

Canarium hirsutum var. leeuwenii, commonly known as the hairy candlenut.

It is a variant of the candlenut tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its hairy foliage and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It is often found in lowland forests and is adapted to humid tropical climates.

The species is valued for its hard wood and has traditional uses in various regions.
