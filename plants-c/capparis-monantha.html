Capparis monantha

Capparis monantha, commonly known as the slender caper, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a faintly fragrant scent. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Capparis monantha is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing resilience to drought conditions.
