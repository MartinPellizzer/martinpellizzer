Calluna vulgaris subf. fertilis

Calluna vulgaris subf.

fertilis, commonly known as the fertile heath, is a subspecies of the common heather. It is a low-growing evergreen shrub characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant produces both male and female flowers on the same inflorescence, making it monoecious. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
