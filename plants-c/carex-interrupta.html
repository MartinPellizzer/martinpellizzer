Carex interrupta

Carex interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, often with a distinctive interrupted pattern in the inflorescence.

Carex interrupta plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
