Czernya arundinacea

Czernya arundinacea, commonly known as reed canary grass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, tufted inflorescence that resembles a canary's bill. The plant thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along waterways. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its roots form extensive rhizomes that help stabilize soil.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to prevent erosion and improve water quality.
