Calliandra pubiflora

Calliandra pubiflora, commonly known as the hairy calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flat, fern-like leaves and showy, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, hairy pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

Calliandra pubiflora is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
