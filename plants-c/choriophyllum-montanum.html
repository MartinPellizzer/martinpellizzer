Choriophyllum montanum

Choriophyllum montanum, commonly known as mountain choriophyllum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in clustered inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is more commonly encountered in its natural wild environment.
