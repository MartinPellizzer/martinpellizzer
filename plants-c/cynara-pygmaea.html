Cynara pygmaea

Cynara pygmaea, commonly known as the dwarf artichoke, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features a basal rosette of spiny, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically yellow and appear in the summer months, forming a compact cluster.

Cynara pygmaea is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
