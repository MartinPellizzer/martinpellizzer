Cereus triangularis var. major

Cereus triangularis var. major, commonly known as the giant night blooming cereus, is a member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly Brazil. This plant is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and spiny, ribbed structure adapted for water storage. It produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

The plant is often cultivated for its striking appearance and unique blooming habit.
