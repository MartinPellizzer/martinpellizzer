Carlina oligocephala subsp. oligocephala

Carlina oligocephala subsp.

oligocephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed carline thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is characterized by its distinctive blue or purple composite flowers.

The plant has basal leaves that are deeply lobed and often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single, showy capitulum surrounded by bracts.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
