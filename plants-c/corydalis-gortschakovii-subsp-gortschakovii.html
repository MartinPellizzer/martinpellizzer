Corydalis gortschakovii subsp. gortschakovii

Corydalis gortschakovii subsp.

gortschakovii, commonly known as the Tibetan flameflower, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayas. It features slender, upright stems and produces clusters of delicate, spurred flowers in shades of pink and red.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a notable species in botanical studies.
