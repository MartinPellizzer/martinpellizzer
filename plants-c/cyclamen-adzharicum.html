Cyclamen adzharicum

Cyclamen adzharicum, commonly known as the Caucasian cyclamen, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features heart-shaped, velvety leaves with a distinct reddish or purple underside. The plant produces showy, bowl-shaped flowers in shades of pink, white, and red, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. Cyclamen adzharicum prefers cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor cultivation and rock gardens.
