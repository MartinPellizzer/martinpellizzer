Chenopodium lanceolatum

Chenopodium lanceolatum, commonly known as wild spinach, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and small greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in fields, and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are edible and have a slightly bitter taste, often used in traditional cuisines.

Chenopodium lanceolatum is also noted for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
