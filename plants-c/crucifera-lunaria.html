Crucifera lunaria

Crucifera lunaria, commonly known as the moonflower mustard, is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its four-petaled flowers and capsule fruits.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and lobed, often with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed areas.
