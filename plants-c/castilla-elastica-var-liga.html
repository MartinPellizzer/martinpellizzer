Castilla elastica var. liga

Castilla elastica var. liga, commonly known as the rubber tree or *Castilla elastica* variety *liga*, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its roots are known to exude a latex-like substance, which has been historically used in rubber production.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
