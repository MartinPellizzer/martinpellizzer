Cyperus andersonianus

Cyperus andersonianus, commonly known as the Anderson's flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist to water-saturated soils. It produces small, clustered flower heads that are often inconspicuous, and it is often found in association with other wetland vegetation.

Cyperus andersonianus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
