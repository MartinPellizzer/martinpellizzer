Carex pseudojaponica

Carex pseudojaponica, commonly known as Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex pseudojaponica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
