Cyanus mucronatus

Cyanus mucronatus, commonly known as the hooked hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its flowers are usually blue or purple, with a distinctive hooked tip on the central flower, giving it its scientific name.

Cyanus mucronatus is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
