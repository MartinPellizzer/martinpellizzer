Cryptophila pudica f. maxima

Cryptophila pudica f. maxima, commonly known as the giant marsh orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers that can reach up to 15 cm in diameter. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, acidic soils. Its flowers are usually a mix of white, pink, and purple, with a distinctive hood-like structure.

This rare and beautiful orchid is native to parts of Europe and is often found in specific microhabitats that support its unique growth requirements.
