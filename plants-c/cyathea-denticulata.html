Cyathea denticulata

Cyathea denticulata, commonly known as the toothed tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and a crown of large, feathery fronds. The fronds are typically bipinnate, with a distinctive serrated or toothed margin, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Cyathea denticulata plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various organisms.
