Clinopodium vulgare var. neogaeum

Clinopodium vulgare var. neogaeum, commonly known as narrow-leaf wild thyme, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a slightly fuzzy texture. Its flowers are pink to purple, arranged in dense clusters, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in herbal remedies.
