Chenopodium lanceolatum f. sessiliflorum

Chenopodium lanceolatum f. sessiliflorum, commonly known as the sessile-flower goosefoot, is a variant of the common goosefoot species.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and reduced, sessile flowers. The plant typically grows in moist soils and is found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its botanical features include small, greenish flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences.

The species is notable for its adaptability to wet environments and its role in ecological succession.
