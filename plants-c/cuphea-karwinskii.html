Cuphea karwinskii

Cuphea karwinskii, commonly known as the flame bean or red bean plant, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that resemble small beans, which bloom in clusters. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its vibrant colors and unique flower structure.

Cuphea karwinskii is drought-tolerant and prefers well-draining soil.
