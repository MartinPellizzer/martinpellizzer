Calorophus filiformis

Calorophus filiformis, commonly known as the slender flameplant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves and clusters of small, bright orange or yellow flowers. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. Its flowers emit a mild fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Calorophus filiformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
