Caladenia caudata

Caladenia caudata, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, spider-like appearance. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with intricate patterns that mimic the appearance of a spider.

Caladenia caudata is a perennial herb that produces a single or few spikes of flowers during the spring or early summer.
