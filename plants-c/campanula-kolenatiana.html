Campanula kolenatiana

Campanula kolenatiana, commonly known as the bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Campanula kolenatiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
