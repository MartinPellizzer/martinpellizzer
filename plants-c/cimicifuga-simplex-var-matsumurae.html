Cimicifuga simplex var. matsumurae

Cimicifuga simplex var. matsumurae, commonly known as Japanese bugbane, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that bear clusters of white, spathaceous flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are palmately compound, with serrated margins, and it produces follicular fruits that contain seeds.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
