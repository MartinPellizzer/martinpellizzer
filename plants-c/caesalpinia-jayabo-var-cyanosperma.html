Caesalpinia jayabo var. cyanosperma

Caesalpinia jayabo var. cyanosperma, commonly known as the blue-sprinkled flame tree, is a striking tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red to orange flowers that are often adorned with blue or purple markings, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain several seeds, and its leaves are bipinnate, consisting of multiple small leaflets. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

The species is noted for its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators such as birds and bees.
