Catasetum hillsii

Catasetum hillsii, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and distinctive, elongated petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a raceme of showy, fragrant blooms that attract pollinators like bees and wasps.

Catasetum hillsii is valued in horticulture for its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
