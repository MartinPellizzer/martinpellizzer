Cynanchica pinifolia

Cynanchica pinifolia, commonly known as the pineleaf milkvetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which resemble the structure of pine needles, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple in color. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils.

Cynanchica pinifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
