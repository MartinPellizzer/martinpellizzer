Cinnamomum camphoratum var. fitianum

Cinnamomum camphoratum var. fitianum, commonly known as the Guangxi camphor tree, is a variant of the camphor laurel native to southern China.

It is a deciduous evergreen tree characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and fragrant bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark purple berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

This variety is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of camphor.
