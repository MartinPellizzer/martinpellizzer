Carex notoleia

Carex notoleia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and clustered flowering heads. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
