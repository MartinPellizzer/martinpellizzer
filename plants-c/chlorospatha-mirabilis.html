Chlorospatha mirabilis

Chlorospatha mirabilis, commonly known as the golden shield plant, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a metallic sheen. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe, which is often brightly colored to attract pollinators.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is popular in horticulture but requires specific care to flourish.
