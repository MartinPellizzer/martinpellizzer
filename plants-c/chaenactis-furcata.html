Chaenactis furcata

Chaenactis furcata, commonly known as the forked fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands. It produces small, yellow to white flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall.
