Chrysopsis gossypina var. dentata

Chrysopsis gossypina var. dentata, commonly known as the toothed goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and disturbed areas. Its varietal name, *dentata*, refers to the toothed or serrated edges of its leaves.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
