Crawfurdia campanulacea

Crawfurdia campanulacea, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine hairs.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in gardens.
