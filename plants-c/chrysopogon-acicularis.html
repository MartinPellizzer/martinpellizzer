Chrysopogon acicularis

Chrysopogon acicularis, commonly known as the needlegrass, is a perennial grass species native to various regions of Asia, Africa, and Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Chrysopogon acicularis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
