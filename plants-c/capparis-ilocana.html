Capparis ilocana

Capparis ilocana, commonly known as the Ilocano caper, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by green, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly rough texture.

Capparis ilocana is typically found in moist, shaded areas of forests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
