Cyathea monticola

Cyathea monticola, commonly known as the mountain tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a robust rhizome system that helps it anchor in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cyathea monticola is often found in tropical rainforests, where it thrives in humid and nutrient-rich soil conditions.
