Cucurbita pepo var. ozarkana

Cucurbita pepo var. ozarkana, commonly known as the Ozark pumpkin, is a cultivated variety of the common gourd.

It is characterized by its elongated, ribbed fruit that typically measures between 12 to 24 inches in length. The plant is an annual vine with palmate leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, requiring consistent moisture for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens for both decoration and culinary use.
