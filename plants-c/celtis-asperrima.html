Celtis asperrima

Celtis asperrima, commonly known as the roughleaf hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are rough to the touch. The tree produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by yellowish-green berries that are edible for certain wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open areas.

Its hardy nature makes it a valuable plant for ecological restoration projects.
