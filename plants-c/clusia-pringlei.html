Clusia pringlei

Clusia pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Clusiaceae family, also known as the genus family, and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that range in color from yellow to red, typically blooming in the summer months.

Clusia pringlei is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its striking foliage and showy blooms.
