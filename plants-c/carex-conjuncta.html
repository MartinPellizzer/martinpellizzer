Carex conjuncta

Carex conjuncta, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Carex conjuncta plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
