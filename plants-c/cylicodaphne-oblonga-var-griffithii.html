Cylicodaphne oblonga var. griffithii

Cylicodaphne oblonga var. griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's sago palm, is a variant of the sago palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by dark blue, spherical berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
