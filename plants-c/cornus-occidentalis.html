Cornus occidentalis

Cornus occidentalis, commonly known as western dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy white or pink bracts that surround small yellow flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape and have smooth or slightly toothed margins. Its fruit is a red, fleshy drupe that matures in late summer, providing food for birds and small mammals.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types and light conditions.
