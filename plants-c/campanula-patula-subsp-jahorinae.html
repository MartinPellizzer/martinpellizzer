Campanula patula subsp. jahorinae

Campanula patula subsp.

jahorinae, commonly known as the Jahorina bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped blue to purple flowers, which bloom from late spring to early summer.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in well-drained, moist soils. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats, where it contributes to the biodiversity of its environment.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
