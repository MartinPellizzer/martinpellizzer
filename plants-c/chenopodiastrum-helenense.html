Chenopodiastrum helenense

Chenopodiastrum helenense, commonly known as Helen's goosefoot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically alternate and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are often borne on short pedicels. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions.

This species is native to regions of Central Asia and has been introduced to other parts of the world as an ornamental or for its hardy nature.
