Cucumis tinneanus

Cucumis tinneanus, commonly known as the slender cucumber, is a flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant. Its fruit is a small, elongated cucurbit with a thin, edible rind and a mild, refreshing flavor.

It is often cultivated for its edible fruit and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
