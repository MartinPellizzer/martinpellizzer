Carex vulpina f. interrupta

Carex vulpina f. interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in spikes. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its foliage is often a mix of green and brown, giving it a distinctive, mottled appearance.

It is valued in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
