Calamagrostis reitzii

Calamagrostis reitzii, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking purple to pinkish plumes that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, acidic soils, often found in wetlands, bogs, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Calamagrostis reitzii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging wet conditions.
