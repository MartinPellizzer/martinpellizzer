Canavalia ekmanii

Canavalia ekmanii, commonly known as the wild cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along roadsides.

Canavalia ekmanii is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
