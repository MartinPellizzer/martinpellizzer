Campanula monodiana

Campanula monodiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
