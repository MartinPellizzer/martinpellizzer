Coix lacryma-jobi subsp. ma-yuen

Coix lacryma-jobi subsp.

ma-yuen, commonly known as Chinese Job's tears or yuzhong, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae). It is characterized by its distinctive, tear-shaped caryopsis that is often used in traditional Chinese medicine.

The plant grows in wetlands and rice paddies, and its seeds are harvested for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces tall, slender stems with narrow leaves.

It is valued for its nutritional content and is also used in herbal remedies for its purported health benefits.
