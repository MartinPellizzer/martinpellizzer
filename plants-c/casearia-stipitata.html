Casearia stipitata

Casearia stipitata, commonly known as the silverleaf casearia, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal panicles, followed by small, round berries. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
