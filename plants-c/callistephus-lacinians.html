Callistephus lacinians

Callistephus lacinians, commonly known as the Chinese aster, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that come in a variety of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has narrow, gray-green leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
