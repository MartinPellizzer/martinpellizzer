Caryocar glabrum subsp. album

Caryocar glabrum subsp.

album, commonly known as the white soursop, is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves.

The plant produces large, fleshy fruits with a yellow or white pulp, which contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its edible fruit and has some traditional medicinal uses.
