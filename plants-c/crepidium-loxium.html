Crepidium loxium

Crepidium loxium, commonly known as the slender orchid, is a member of the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is a terrestrial herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant features a slender, upright stem with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, white or pale green, and are arranged in a spike, producing a delicate and fragrant appearance.

Crepidium loxium is noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its role in supporting local insect pollinators.
