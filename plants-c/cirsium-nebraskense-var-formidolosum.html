Cirsium nebraskense var. formidolosum

Cirsium nebraskense var. formidolosum, commonly known as the Nebraska thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the central United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and showy purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor environments.

The flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological importance in native ecosystems.
