Celtis ilicifolia

Celtis ilicifolia, commonly known as the fringe tree, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, fringe-like flowers that appear in clusters. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a serrated margin, with a glossy upper surface. The tree produces small, oval fruits that are typically dark purple when mature.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
