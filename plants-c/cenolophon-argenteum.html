Cenolophon argenteum

Cenolophon argenteum, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
