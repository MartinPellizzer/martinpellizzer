Cypripedium × vipanii

Cypripedium × vipanii, commonly known as the hybrid lady's slipper orchid, is a rare and hybrid orchid species resulting from the cross between Cypripedium calceolus and Cypripedium malipoense.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pouch-like flower that is typically pink or purple in color. The plant is native to China and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments. It has a rhizomatous root system and produces showy, fragrant blooms during the spring.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
