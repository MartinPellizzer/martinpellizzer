Centaurea intybacea var. macrocephala

Centaurea intybacea var. macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spindly stems and striking, showy flowers that range from pink to purple. The plant produces small, bract-like leaves and has a fibrous root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological significance.
