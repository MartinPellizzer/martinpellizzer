Centaurea balsamita subsp. kermanensis

Centaurea balsamita subsp.

kermanensis, commonly known as Kerman balsamweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central Asia, including Iran. It is a perennial herb characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces vibrant blue to purple flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, which attract various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

The subspecies is distinguished by its slightly different morphology compared to the typical Centaurea balsamita.
