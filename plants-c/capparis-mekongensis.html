Capparis mekongensis

Capparis mekongensis, commonly known as the Mekong prickly date, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its woody shrub structure and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has thorny stems and leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that turns red when mature.

Capparis mekongensis is adapted to grow in moist, tropical environments and is often found in forest understories.
