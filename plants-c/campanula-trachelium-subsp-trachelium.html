Campanula trachelium subsp. trachelium

Campanula trachelium subsp.

trachelium, commonly known as the bellflower or blue bell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a rough texture and is often found in woodland and shady areas. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal. Campanula trachelium subsp.

trachelium is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
