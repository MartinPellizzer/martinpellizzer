Coriflora viorna

Coriflora viorna, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Coriflora viorna is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ease of care.
