Calotis caespitosa

Calotis caespitosa, commonly known as the tufted daisy, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that forms dense, compact clumps of foliage. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers with a central yellow disk, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its hardy nature and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
