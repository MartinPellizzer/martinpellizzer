Cordia cylindristachya var. interrupta

Cordia cylindristachya var. interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted cylinder tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, clustered inflorescences and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

This plant is often used in traditional medicine and for ornamental purposes due to its unique floral structure.
