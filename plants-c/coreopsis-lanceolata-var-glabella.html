Coreopsis lanceolata var. glabella

Coreopsis lanceolata var. glabella, commonly known as the glabrous coreopsis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairless, giving it its varietal name "glabella.". The plant produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with prominent central disk and ray florets, blooming from late spring to early fall. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.

Its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies further enhance its ecological value.
