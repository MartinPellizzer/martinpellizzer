Cephaelis trichophoroides

Cephaelis trichophoroides, commonly known as the Indian flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
