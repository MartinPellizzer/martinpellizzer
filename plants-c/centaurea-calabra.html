Centaurea calabra

Centaurea calabra, commonly known as the Calabrian centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions. The plant features upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright blue or purple flowers in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are arranged in compact, globular heads, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Centaurea calabra is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
