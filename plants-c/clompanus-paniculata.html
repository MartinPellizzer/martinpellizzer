Clompanus paniculata

Clompanus paniculata, commonly known as the panicled clompanus, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and unique floral display.
