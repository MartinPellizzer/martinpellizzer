Chrysobalanus icaco var. chariensis

Chrysobalanus icaco var. chariensis, commonly known as the red mombin, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the family Chrysobalanoideae and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a tangy, citrus-like flavor. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in mangrove ecosystems.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local cuisines and medicine.
