Cirsium boissieri

Cirsium boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial species belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its flowering period occurs during the summer months, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cirsium boissieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
