Citharexylum hexangulare f. parvifolium

Citharexylum hexangulare f. parvifolium, commonly known as the small-leaved hibiscus, is a compact shrub native to the Pacific Islands.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a glossy green, arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers, which bloom in summer. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its foliage and flowers make it a popular choice for tropical gardens and as a houseplant.
