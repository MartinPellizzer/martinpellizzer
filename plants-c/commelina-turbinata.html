Commelina turbinata

Commelina turbinata, commonly known as the turban flower, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue-violet inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a waxy coating.

The flowers are borne on a spicate inflorescence and attract various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
