Centaurea aspera var. genuina

Centaurea aspera var. genuina, commonly known as the rough centaury, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with purple streaks, which bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated as a ornamental plant.
