Crotalaria multiflora

Crotalaria multiflora, commonly known as the many-flowered crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers clustered in racemes. The plant has palmate leaves with three to five lobes, and its pods are cylindrical, containing several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Crotalaria multiflora is also known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which makes it beneficial in soil enrichment.
