Cyathea woodwardioides

Cyathea woodwardioides, commonly known as the dwarf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its slow growth and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. Its rhizome system supports the development of its tall stem, while its fronds are used for photosynthesis.

Cyathea woodwardioides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant appearance and air-purifying properties.
