Chlora exaltata

Chlora exaltata, commonly known as the skyward spire plant, is a unique species native to high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to three meters in height, supporting clusters of small, pale green leaves. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to withstand strong winds and low temperatures. Its reproductive structures are inconspicuous, with small white flowers that bloom in late summer.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, Chlora exaltata is considered a rare and endangered species.
