Cassia chrysotingens var. obtusa

Cassia chrysotingens var. obtusa, commonly known as the blunt-leaf golden cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, dark green leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces bright yellow inflorescences, which consist of numerous small, clustered flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
