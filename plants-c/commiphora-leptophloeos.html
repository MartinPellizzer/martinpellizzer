Commiphora leptophloeos

Commiphora leptophloeos, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing species. The plant is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. It produces a resinous sap that has been traditionally used for medicinal and aromatic purposes.

The species is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ecological and potential pharmaceutical significance.
