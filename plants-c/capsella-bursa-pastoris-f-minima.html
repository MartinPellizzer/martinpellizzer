Capsella bursa-pastoris f. minima

Capsella bursa-pastoris f. minima, commonly known as the small burr chickweed, is a cultivated form of the common groundsel.

It is a low-growing annual plant with small, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive burr-like seed capsules. The plant typically reaches a height of only a few centimeters, making it a compact and delicate species. Its flowers are usually yellow, and it thrives in disturbed soils, often found in gardens and along pathways.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
