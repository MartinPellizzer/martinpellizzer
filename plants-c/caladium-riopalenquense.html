Caladium riopalenquense

Caladium riopalenquense, commonly known as the Rio Palenquense arrowhead, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Araceae family, characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking, colorful foliage with variegated patterns of green, white, and red. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its growth habit includes a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense clusters.
