Corylus heterophylla var. yunnanensis

Corylus heterophylla var. yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the hazelnut family (Betulaceae) and is characterized by its variable leaf shapes, ranging from oval to palmate. The plant produces small, cup-shaped male flowers and female flowers that develop into edible nuts enclosed in a prickly husk. Its botanical features make it an important species for both ecological studies and potential agricultural use.

The plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters.
