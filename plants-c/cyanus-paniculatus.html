Cyanus paniculatus

Cyanus paniculatus, commonly known as the blueweed or blue coneflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and branched.

Cyanus paniculatus is often found in meadows and disturbed soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
