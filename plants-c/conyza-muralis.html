Conyza muralis

Conyza muralis, commonly known as the wallweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and rocky areas. The plant has small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, and its leaves are usually hairy and lobed. It is characterized by its ability to thrive in arid conditions and its rapid growth cycle.

Conyza muralis is often found in urban environments, such as along walls and in cracks in pavement.
