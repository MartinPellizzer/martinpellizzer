Corydalis blanda subsp. blanda

Corydalis blanda subsp.

blanda, commonly known as the slender corydalis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States. It features delicate, creeping stems and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple.

The plant has palmately divided leaves and is often found in moist, woodland habitats. It blooms from late spring to early summer and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens. Corydalis blanda subsp.

blanda is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
