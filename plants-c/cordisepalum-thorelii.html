Cordisepalum thorelii

Cordisepalum thorelii, commonly known as the heart-sepal orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, heart-shaped sepals that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its native habitat. Its flowers are usually small and pale in color, with a delicate appearance.

Cordisepalum thorelii is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in greenhouses due to its specific care requirements.
