Canarium microcarpum

Canarium microcarpum, commonly known as the small-fruited candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its fruit is used in traditional medicine and as a source of oil.
