Crepis gymnopus

Crepis gymnopus, commonly known as the slender mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and often clustered at the base, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Crepis gymnopus is often found in disturbed habitats and is considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
