Cynoglossum furcatum f. albiflorum

Cynoglossum furcatum f. albiflorum, commonly known as the whiteflower bellflower, is a variant of the common bellflower.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable ornamental plant in gardens.
