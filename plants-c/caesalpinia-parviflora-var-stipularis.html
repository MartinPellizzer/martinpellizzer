Caesalpinia parviflora var. stipularis

Caesalpinia parviflora var. stipularis, commonly known as the red sandalwood or flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small stipules, and its striking red or orange-pink inflorescences that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
