Carex lessoniana

Carex lessoniana, commonly known as the lesson's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, typical of the sedge genus. Native to various regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually wind-pollinated.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat for various wildlife.
