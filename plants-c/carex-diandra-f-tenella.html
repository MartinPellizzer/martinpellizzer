Carex diandra f. tenella

Carex diandra f. tenella, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, upright leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, typically arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
