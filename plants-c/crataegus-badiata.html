Crataegus badiata

Crataegus badiata, commonly known as the red thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant produces small, red, apple-like berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Its leaves are oval with serrated edges, and it typically grows in moist woodlands or along streambanks.

Crataegus badiata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wildlife.
