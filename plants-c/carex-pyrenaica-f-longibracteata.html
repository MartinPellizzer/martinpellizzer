Carex pyrenaica f. longibracteata

Carex pyrenaica f. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract sedge, is a variant of the Pyrenean sedge, characterized by its elongated bracts.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae, known for its grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dense, clustered growth habit.

The inflorescence consists of a single spike of small, three-ranked flowers, which are often subtended by the distinctive long bracts.
