Ceanothus laevigatus var. guadalupae

Ceanothus laevigatus var. guadalupae, commonly known as the Guadalupe ceanothus, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern California.

It features small, fragrant white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
