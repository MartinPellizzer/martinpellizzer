Cattleya lindleyana

Cattleya lindleyana, commonly known as the Lindley's orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a sympodial orchid with a pseudobulb structure that supports its growth and storage of nutrients. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with contrasting patterns. It thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Its popularity among orchid enthusiasts is due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
