Carex funiformis

Carex funiformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually wind-pollinated, a common feature among sedges. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds.

Carex funiformis is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
