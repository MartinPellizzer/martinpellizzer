Calceolaria biflora var. filicaulis

Calceolaria biflora var. filicaulis, commonly known as the fern-leaved calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from purple to pink, and its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and thrives in cool, temperate climates. It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
