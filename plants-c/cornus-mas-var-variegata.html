Cornus mas var. variegata

Cornus mas var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated cornel.

It is a deciduous shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia. The plant features striking variegated leaves with white and green patterns, making it a popular ornamental species. It produces small, white flowers in spring followed by red berries in autumn.

The variegation is due to genetic mutations that affect chlorophyll production in the leaves.
