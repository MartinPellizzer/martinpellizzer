Cynanchum glaucescens

Cynanchum glaucescens, commonly known as blue milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been used in traditional herbal remedies for various ailments.
