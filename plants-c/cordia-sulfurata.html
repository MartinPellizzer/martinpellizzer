Cordia sulfurata

Cordia sulfurata, commonly known as sulfur bush, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, including Kenya and Tanzania.

It is a small, shrub-like species that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often giving it a sulfur-like appearance. Its leaves are usually simple, opposite, and have a rough texture.

Cordia sulfurata is known for its adaptability to harsh conditions and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
