Cerastium decalvans f. stribrnyi

Cerastium decalvans f. stribrnyi, commonly known as the striped mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, hairy leaves that are typically covered with a fine, white pubescence. The flowers are usually pink or red, with five petals and a prominent central stamen, blooming in late spring to early summer. This plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

It is a low-maintenance plant that is well adapted to harsh, cold climates.
