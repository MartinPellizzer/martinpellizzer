Centaurea montana subsp. eumontana

Centaurea montana subsp.

eumontana, commonly known as the alpine centaury, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flower heads that bloom in summer.

The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bracteate flowers arranged in a compact cluster. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
