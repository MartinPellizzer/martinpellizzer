Crepidospermum atlanticum

Crepidospermum atlanticum, commonly known as the Atlantic morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and vibrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds with a unique spiral structure. It thrives in moist, sunny environments and is often found near water bodies.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners, though it can be invasive in some areas.
