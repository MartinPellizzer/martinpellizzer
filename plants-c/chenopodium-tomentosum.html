Chenopodium tomentosum

Chenopodium tomentosum, commonly known as buffalo bur, is a flowering plant in the amaranth family.

It is characterized by its hairy, prickly leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It is native to North America and thrives in dry, disturbed soils.

Chenopodium tomentosum is often considered a weed but has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
