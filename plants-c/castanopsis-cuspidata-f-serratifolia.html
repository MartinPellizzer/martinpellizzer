Castanopsis cuspidata f. serratifolia

Castanopsis cuspidata f. serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated evergreen oak, is a variant of the broad-leafed evergreen oak native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, serrated leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant is an evergreen tree or shrub, often found in subtropical and temperate forests. It produces small, clustered flowers followed by hard, woody fruits containing one or two seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
