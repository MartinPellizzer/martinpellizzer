Cerastium nigrescens

Cerastium nigrescens, commonly known as the dark eveningflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, commonly known as the carnation family. The plant features small, dark purple to almost black flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Cerastium nigrescens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and meadows.
