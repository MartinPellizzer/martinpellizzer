Cynanchum myrtifolium

Cynanchum myrtifolium, commonly known as the red root, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its erect stem and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in umbels, followed by dark blue to black berries. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Botanically, it is valued for its medicinal properties and is part of the Asclepiadaceae family.
