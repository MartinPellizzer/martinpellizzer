Cissus perforata

Cissus perforata, commonly known as the perforate devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped, perforated leaves that give it its common name. The plant has a vigorous growth habit and can reach lengths of several meters when supported. It thrives in bright, indirect light and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular houseplant.

Its unique foliage and adaptability contribute to its ornamental value in both indoor and outdoor settings.
