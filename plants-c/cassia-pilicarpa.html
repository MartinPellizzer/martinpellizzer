Cassia pilicarpa

Cassia pilicarpa, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to orange-red flowers that appear in clusters, often in late summer to early autumn. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins and produces small, three-winged pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Cassia pilicarpa is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
