Chamaesenna pistaciifolia

Chamaesenna pistaciifolia, commonly known as the goldenbush, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its striking golden-yellow foliage, which turns reddish in the winter. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by small, dry fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often used in gardens and landscapes in arid regions.
