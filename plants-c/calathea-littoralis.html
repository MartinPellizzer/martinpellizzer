Calathea littoralis

Calathea littoralis, commonly known as the river calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with intricate patterns of green, silver, and pink. The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a velvety texture that enhances their visual appeal. This plant thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its botanical features make it both ornamental and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
