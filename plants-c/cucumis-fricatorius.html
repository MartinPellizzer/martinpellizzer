Cucumis fricatorius

Cucumis fricatorius, commonly known as the desert cucumber, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East. The plant produces small, green, edible fruits that are typically harvested when they are still immature. Its leaves are broad and palmate, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments.

The plant is well-suited for cultivation in regions with limited water availability due to its drought-resistant traits.
