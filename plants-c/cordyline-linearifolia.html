Cordyline linearifolia

Cordyline linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cabbage tree, is a striking evergreen plant native to New Zealand.

It features tall, unbranched stems that can reach heights of up to 10 meters, often with a fibrous, flaky bark. The plant produces long, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color and can grow up to 2 meters in length. It blooms with small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by red berries.

This species is well-suited for tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
