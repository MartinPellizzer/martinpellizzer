Camphora glandulifera

Camphora glandulifera, commonly known as the European camphor tree, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by oval, woody fruits containing one or two seeds. It is known for its aromatic oils, which are used in various traditional and commercial applications.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in botanical studies.
