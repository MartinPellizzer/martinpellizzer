Carex lateriflora

Carex lateriflora, commonly known as the side-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, arranged in spikelets along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks.

Carex lateriflora plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
