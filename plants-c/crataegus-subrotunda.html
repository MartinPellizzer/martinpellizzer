Crataegus subrotunda

Crataegus subrotunda, commonly known as the round-leaved hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its oval, rounded leaves that are often densely hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring, which are followed by red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its fruit is an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
