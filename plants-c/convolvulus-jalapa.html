Convolvulus jalapa

Convolvulus jalapa, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a climbing vine that produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking vibrant, flowering vines.
