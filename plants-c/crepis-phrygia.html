Crepis phrygia

Crepis phrygia, commonly known as the Phrygian hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia and parts of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its yellow flowers bloom in late summer.

Crepis phrygia is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
