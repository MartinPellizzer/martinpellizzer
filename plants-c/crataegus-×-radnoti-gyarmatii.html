Crataegus × radnoti-gyarmatii

Crataegus × radnoti-gyarmatii, commonly known as the Hungarian hawthorn, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Crataegus monogyna and Crataegus oxyacantha.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces red berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

Its morphology and hybrid origin make it an interesting subject for botanical study and horticultural cultivation.
