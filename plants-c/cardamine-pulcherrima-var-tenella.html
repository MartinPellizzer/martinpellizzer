Cardamine pulcherrima var. tenella

Cardamine pulcherrima var. tenella, commonly known as the dwarf watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, waxy hairs. Its flowers are delicate, white or pale green, and appear in clusters near the base of the plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and culinary uses.
