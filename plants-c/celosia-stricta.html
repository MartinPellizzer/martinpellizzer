Celosia stricta

Celosia stricta, commonly known as the cockscomb, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its vibrant, plume-like inflorescences that resemble a rooster's comb, hence its common name. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its showy, long-lasting blooms that come in a variety of colors such as red, orange, and pink. Celosia stricta is typically grown as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ease of cultivation.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
