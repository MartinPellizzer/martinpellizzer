Convolvulus villiflorus

Convolvulus villiflorus, commonly known as the slender morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual with trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically blue or purple, blooming in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides.

Convolvulus villiflorus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
