Cordyla madagascariensis subsp. tamarindoides

Cordyla madagascariensis subsp.

tamarindoides, commonly known as the Tamarind tree, is a large evergreen tree native to Madagascar. It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its spreading canopy and compound leaves.

The tree produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds surrounded by a sweet, sticky pulp. It is widely cultivated for its timber, fruit, and traditional medicinal uses.

The plant also plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.
