Cathartolinum catharticum subsp. suecicum

Cathartolinum catharticum subsp.

suecicum, commonly known as the Swedish cathartic plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to northern Europe. It features slender, upright stems with alternate, lanceolate leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in terminal clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its root system is known for containing compounds with mild laxative properties.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
