Cerastium carinthiacum subsp. carinthiacum

Cerastium carinthiacum subsp.

carinthiacum, commonly known as Carpathian mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, hairy leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers.

The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are often prostrate, and it produces numerous small, rounded leaves that are densely packed.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
