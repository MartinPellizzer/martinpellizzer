Carex nigra subsp. alpina

Carex nigra subsp.

alpina, commonly known as alpine black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its dark green, sword-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of brownish flowers.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats. It is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of alpine ecosystems.
