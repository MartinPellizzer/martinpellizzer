Chaenorhinum origanifolium proles brasianum

Chaenorhinum origanifolium proles brasianum, commonly known as the Brazilian feverfew, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in composite heads. The plant has finely divided, aromatic leaves that resemble those of oregano, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
