Cocculus affinis

Cocculus affinis, commonly known as the devil's bit cockle, is a climbing vine native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, inflated, seed pods that resemble small horns. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers and has compound leaves with three to five leaflets. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
