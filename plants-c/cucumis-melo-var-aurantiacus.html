Cucumis melo var. aurantiacus

Cucumis melo var. aurantiacus, commonly known as the golden melon, is a cultivated variant of the melon plant characterized by its vibrant orange to yellow rind and sweet, aromatic flesh.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to regions of Central and South America. The plant typically grows as a trailing vine with large, lobed leaves and produces distinctive, round to oval-shaped fruits. Its botanical features include a thick, smooth rind that becomes harder as the fruit matures, and a juicy, seed-filled interior.

This variety is valued for its flavor and is often grown for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
