Coreopsis parviflora

Coreopsis parviflora, commonly known as the smallflower tickseed, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features bright yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk, blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies adds ecological value to the landscape.
