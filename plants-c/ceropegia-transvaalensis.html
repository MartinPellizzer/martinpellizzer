Ceropegia transvaalensis

Ceropegia transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species with fleshy, often hairy stems and leaves that are typically opposite or whorled. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, with a distinctive corona structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Ceropegia transvaalensis is adapted to arid conditions and can store water in its thick stems and leaves.
