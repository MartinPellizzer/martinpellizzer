Calea pilosa

Calea pilosa, commonly known as the "Mexican hairless plant," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flower heads. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name, and it is often found in moist, well-drained soils. Its flowers bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

Calea pilosa is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
