Crataegus maura var. diversifolia

Crataegus maura var. diversifolia, commonly known as the variable hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, with leaves that vary in shape and size, often exhibiting a mix of lobed and entire margins. The plant produces white to pinkish flowers in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its botanical features include thorny branches, opposite leaf arrangement, and a preference for moist, well-drained soils.

It is often found in woodland edges and moist ravines, contributing to local biodiversity.
