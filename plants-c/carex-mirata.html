Carex mirata

Carex mirata, commonly known as the silver sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a silvery or grayish coating, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Carex mirata produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.
