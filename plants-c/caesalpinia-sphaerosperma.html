Caesalpinia sphaerosperma

Caesalpinia sphaerosperma, commonly known as the globular thorn acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spherical seed pods and compound leaves with small, pointed leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
