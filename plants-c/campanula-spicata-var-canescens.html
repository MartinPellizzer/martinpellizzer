Campanula spicata var. canescens

Campanula spicata var. canescens, commonly known as the hoary bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to white flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, whitish down, giving the plant its "hoary" appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and meadows.
