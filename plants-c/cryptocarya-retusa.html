Cryptocarya retusa

Cryptocarya retusa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cryptocarya, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage. The plant has elliptical, dark green leaves with a leathery texture and a distinctive, narrow shape. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some bird species.

Cryptocarya retusa is often found in coastal and lowland forest habitats, contributing to the biodiversity of its native ecosystems.
