Cynorkis buchananii

Cynorkis buchananii, commonly known as the Buchanan's lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its strap-shaped, grass-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink blooms that typically appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is widely cultivated in temperate regions.
