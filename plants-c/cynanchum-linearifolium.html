Cynanchum linearifolium

Cynanchum linearifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical, ribbed fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
