Chrysocoryne multiflora

Chrysocoryne multiflora, commonly known as the golden crown or yellow crown plant, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

The flowers are tubular with five petals, and they emit a mild fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
