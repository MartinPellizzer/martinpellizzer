Chenopodium ambrosioides subsp. pinnatifidum

Chenopodium ambrosioides subsp.

pinnatifidum, commonly known as narrowleaf goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family. It is characterized by its alternate, pinnately divided leaves that are typically narrow and serrated.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are often dispersed by wind. It is native to the Americas and is commonly found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

This species is sometimes used in traditional medicine and has been studied for its potential ecological and agricultural applications.
