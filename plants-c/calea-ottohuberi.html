Calea ottohuberi

Calea ottohuberi, commonly known as the sacred flame plant, is a member of the Asteraceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular, red-orange flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in late summer. The plant has velvety, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and cultural significance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
