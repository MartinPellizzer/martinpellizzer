Crepidorhopalon membranocalycinus

Crepidorhopalon membranocalycinus, commonly known as the translucent membrane lichen, is a unique species of lichen characterized by its intricate symbiotic relationship between a fungus and a green alga.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found on tree bark or stone surfaces in temperate forests. The plant's distinctive feature is its thin, membranous thallus, which gives it a delicate and almost translucent appearance. Its reproductive structures, known as soredia, are dispersed via wind, allowing the lichen to colonize new areas.

Due to its specialized habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in certain regions.
