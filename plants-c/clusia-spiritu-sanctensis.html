Clusia spiritu-sanctensis

Clusia spiritu-sanctensis, commonly known as the sacred clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is often found in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and humid environments. It produces small, red berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in some regions.
