Calyptranthes grammica var. triflora

Calyptranthes grammica var. triflora, commonly known as the three-flowered calyptranthes, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers with five petals, which bloom in the spring and summer months. Its fruit is a small, spherical capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
