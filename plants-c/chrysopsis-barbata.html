Chrysopsis barbata

Chrysopsis barbata, commonly known as the barbed goldeneye, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces yellow flowers with prominent, barbed bracts that give it its common name. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in mountainous regions.

Chrysopsis barbata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
