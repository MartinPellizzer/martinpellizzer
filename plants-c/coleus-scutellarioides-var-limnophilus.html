Coleus scutellarioides var. limnophilus

Coleus scutellarioides var. limnophilus, commonly known as the water coleus, is a variant of the waterleaf plant native to wetland environments in tropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that often displays shades of red, purple, and green. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for aquatic or bog gardens. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a smooth texture and can grow up to several inches in length.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to enhance the aesthetic of water features in landscaping.
