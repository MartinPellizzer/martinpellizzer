Cyperus martindalei

Cyperus martindalei, commonly known as the narrowleaf paperplant, is a species of sedge native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. The plant thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found near water bodies such as ponds and streams. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.

Cyperus martindalei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas.
