Crotalaria kipandensis

Crotalaria kipandensis, commonly known as the Kipande bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant produces distinctive yellow flowers with prominent spurred petals, and its seed pods are typically cylindrical and ridged. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and savannas.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
