Clitoria portobellensis

Clitoria portobellensis, commonly known as the Portobello bean plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive butterfly-shaped blue or purple flowers. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces pods that contain edible seeds resembling small beans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
