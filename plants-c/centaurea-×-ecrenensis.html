Centaurea × ecrenensis

Centaurea × ecrenensis, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Centaurea cyanus (blue cornflower) and Centaurea cyanus var. sanguinea (red cornflower), resulting in a plant with strikingly colorful flowers. The species is characterized by its tall, branching stems and showy, composite flower heads that range from purple to red and blue. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and used in floral arrangements.
