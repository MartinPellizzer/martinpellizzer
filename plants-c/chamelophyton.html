Chamelophyton

Chamelophyton, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique succulent plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that can change color depending on environmental conditions. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in drought-prone environments. Chamelophyton thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal watering.

Its ability to adapt to harsh climates makes it a fascinating subject for botanical study.
