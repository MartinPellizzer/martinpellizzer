Carex mutica

Carex mutica, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant has triangular stems and produces small, greenish-white flowers in spike-like clusters. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks.

Carex mutica is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
