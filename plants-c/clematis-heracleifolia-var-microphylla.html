Clematis heracleifolia var. microphylla

Clematis heracleifolia var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features slender, climbing stems with opposite, trifoliate leaves that are small and ovate. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of white, pink, or blue. Its foliage is often covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and is well-suited for trellises or as a climbing plant in gardens.
