Cheiropsis cirrhosa

Cheiropsis cirrhosa, commonly known as the desert hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has adapted to harsh environments through its ability to store water in its thick leaves and stems. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Cheiropsis cirrhosa is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
