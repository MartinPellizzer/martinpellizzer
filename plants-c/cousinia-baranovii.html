Cousinia baranovii

Cousinia baranovii, commonly known as the Baranov's globemallow, is a perennial flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces small, achene-like seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across its native range.

Cousinia baranovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in harsh climates.
