Carelia echiodes

Carelia echiodes, commonly known as the spiny hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a rough, serrated edge.

Carelia echiodes is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
