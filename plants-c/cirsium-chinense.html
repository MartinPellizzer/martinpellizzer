Cirsium chinense

Cirsium chinense, commonly known as Chinese thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in stiff, bristly hairs, while its flowers consist of both ray and disc florets.

Cirsium chinense is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its distinctive appearance and hardy nature.
