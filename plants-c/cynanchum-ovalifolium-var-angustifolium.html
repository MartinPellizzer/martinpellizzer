Cynanchum ovalifolium var. angustifolium

Cynanchum ovalifolium var. angustifolium, commonly known as narrow-leaved milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The leaves are typically narrow and oval-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, and it is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.
