Campanula brassicifolia

Campanula brassicifolia, commonly known as the cabbage bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It has alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Campanula brassicifolia is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
