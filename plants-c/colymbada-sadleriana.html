Colymbada sadleriana

Colymbada sadleriana, commonly known as the Sadler's waterwheel, is a unique aquatic plant native to the wetlands of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its whorled arrangement of narrow, grass-like leaves that radiate from a central stem. The plant thrives in shallow, still or slowly moving freshwater environments. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Colymbada sadleriana plays an important role in maintaining the ecological balance of its habitat by providing shelter for small aquatic organisms.
