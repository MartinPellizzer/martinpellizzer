Carpha diandra

Carpha diandra, commonly known as the African feather grass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and delicate, feathery plumes that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has a clump-forming growth habit and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its ornate appearance.

Carpha diandra is relatively drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soil conditions.
