Centaurea rouyi subsp. rouyi

Centaurea rouyi subsp.

rouyi, commonly known as the Rouy's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, typically from July to September.

The plant has upright, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and long flowering period.
