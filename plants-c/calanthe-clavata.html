Calanthe clavata

Calanthe clavata, commonly known as the broad-leafed calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. Calanthe clavata prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a distinctive, club-shaped lip that sets it apart from other orchid species.
