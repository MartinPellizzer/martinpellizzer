Cyanea arborea

Cyanea arborea, commonly known as the tree hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cyanea arborea thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
