Carduus pontederae

Carduus pontederae, commonly known as the Italian thistle, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in well-drained soils. Its botanical features include a taproot system and a flowering head composed of both ray and disk florets.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
