Conophytum orientale

Conophytum orientale, commonly known as the "Oriental Cactus" or "Stemless Stonecrop," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, stemless leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the summer months. Its compact growth habit and unique appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and succulent collections.

Conophytum orientale is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it ideal for xeriscaping.
