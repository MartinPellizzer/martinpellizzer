Crataegus firma

Crataegus firma, commonly known as the common hawthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring. Its fruits, known as haws, are red or orange berries that ripen in late summer.

Crataegus firma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its cardiovascular benefits.
