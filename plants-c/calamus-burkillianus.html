It is a tall, rhizomatous plant that typically grows in wet, swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-yellow in color. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a smooth, waxy surface.

Calamus burkillianus is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia.
