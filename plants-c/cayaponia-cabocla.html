Cayaponia cabocla

Cayaponia cabocla, commonly known as the yellow bell vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing shrub that produces striking yellow flowers with a strong, sweet fragrance. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Cayaponia cabocla is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
