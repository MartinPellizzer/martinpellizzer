Caprifolium glaucescens

Caprifolium glaucescens, commonly known as the blue honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It features slender, arching stems that bear opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth, waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
