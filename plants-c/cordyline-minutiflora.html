Cordyline minutiflora

Cordyline minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf cordyline, is a striking tropical plant native to New Zealand.

It features a rosette of stiff, sword-shaped leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and pink. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of red, pink, or white, during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
