Copiapoa cinerea var. haseltoniana

Copiapoa cinerea var. haseltoniana, commonly known as the Haselton's whiteweed, is a cacti species native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its pale green, cylindrical stems that are covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It is adapted to extreme arid conditions, storing water in its thick stems.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and resilience in harsh environments.
