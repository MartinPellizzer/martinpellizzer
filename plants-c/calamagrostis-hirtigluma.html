Calamagrostis hirtigluma

Calamagrostis hirtigluma, commonly known as the hairy bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding ornamental value to gardens. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Calamagrostis hirtigluma is valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
