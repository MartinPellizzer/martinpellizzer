Camellia mollis

Camellia mollis, commonly known as the silky camellia, is a species native to China and Japan.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its elegant, single-layered flowers that range in color from white to deep pink. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Camellia mollis thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late winter to early spring, adding early color to the landscape.
