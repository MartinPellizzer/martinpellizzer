Coleus gossweileri

Coleus gossweileri, commonly known as the Gossweiler's coleus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which includes many aromatic herbs and shrubs. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and white. Its leaves are typically opposite and often have a velvety texture.

Coleus gossweileri is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
