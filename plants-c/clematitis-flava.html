Clematitis flava

Clematitis flava, commonly known as the yellow clematis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves with three heart-shaped leaflets and produces small, green fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Clematitis flava is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
