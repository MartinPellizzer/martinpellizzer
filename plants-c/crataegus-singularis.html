Crataegus singularis

Crataegus singularis, commonly known as the single-flowered hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart, and they serve as a food source for various wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological importance in native habitats.
