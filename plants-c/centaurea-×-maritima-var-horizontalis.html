Centaurea × maritima var. horizontalis

Centaurea × maritima var. horizontalis, commonly known as the horizontal sea holly, is a hybrid plant native to coastal regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flower heads surrounded by white to pale blue ray florets. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along sea shores and is well adapted to salt spray and strong winds. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in coastal gardens.
