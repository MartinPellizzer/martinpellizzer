Crepidium foliosum

Crepidium foliosum, commonly known as the leafy orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in shades of white and green. The plant prefers shaded, humid environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or in rocky crevices. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture from the air, and it has a symbiotic relationship with certain fungi.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.
