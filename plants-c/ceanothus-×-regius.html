Ceanothus × regius

Ceanothus × regius, commonly known as the California lilac, is a hybrid shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of fragrant, pale blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, providing year-round visual interest.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
