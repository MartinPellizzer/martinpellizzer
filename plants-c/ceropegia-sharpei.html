Ceropegia sharpei

Ceropegia sharpei, commonly known as the "Cape Veld" or "Cape honeysuckle," is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a succulent vine characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and opposite leaves that are typically green with a slight pubescence. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that are often pink or purple, attracting specific pollinators like moths and bees. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia sharpei is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a decorative plant.
