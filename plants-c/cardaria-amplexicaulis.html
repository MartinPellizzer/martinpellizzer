Cardaria amplexicaulis

Cardaria amplexicaulis, commonly known as the clasping cranesbill, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its basal leaves that wrap around the stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, violet or purple flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.
