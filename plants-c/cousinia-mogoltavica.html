Cousinia mogoltavica

Cousinia mogoltavica, commonly known as the Mongolian cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flower heads that bloom in the summer months. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Cousinia mogoltavica is a hardy species that thrives in harsh climatic conditions.
