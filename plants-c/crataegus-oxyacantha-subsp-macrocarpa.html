Crataegus oxyacantha subsp. macrocarpa

Crataegus oxyacantha subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated edges, and showy white or pink flowers that appear in spring.

The subspecies is distinguished by its larger, more fleshy fruits compared to other hawthorn species. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
