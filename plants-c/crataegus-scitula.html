Crataegus scitula

Crataegus scitula, commonly known as the creeping hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as stream banks and woodland edges. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white to pinkish flowers in early spring. Its fruit is a red or orange-pink berry that ripens in the fall.

Crataegus scitula is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
