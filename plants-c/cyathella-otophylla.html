Cyathella otophylla

Cyathella otophylla, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of a single, hood-like structure that encloses the flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its flowers are usually small and white or pale green, with a distinctive hood-shaped bract.

Cyathella otophylla is known for its specialized pollination mechanism, relying on specific insect pollinators for reproduction.
