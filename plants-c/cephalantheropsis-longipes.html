Cephalantheropsis longipes

Cephalantheropsis longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed helmet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, helmet-shaped flower that is often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest environments, where it thrives in the understory. Its roots form a symbiotic relationship with mycorrhizal fungi, which aid in nutrient absorption.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
