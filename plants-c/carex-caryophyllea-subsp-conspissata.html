Carex caryophyllea subsp. conspissata

Carex caryophyllea subsp.

conspissata, commonly known as the upright sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, sword-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
