Capsicum pomiferum

Capsicum pomiferum, commonly known as the bell pepper, is a flowering plant in the nightshade family (Solanaceae).

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its thick, fleshy, lobed leaves and showy yellow or white flowers. The fruit, which can be round or bell-shaped, varies in color from green to red, yellow, orange, and even purple. The plant is cultivated worldwide for its edible fruits, which are rich in vitamins and antioxidants.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
