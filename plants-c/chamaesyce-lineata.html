Chamaesyce lineata

Chamaesyce lineata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandwort, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant features small, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces clusters of tiny, yellowish flowers. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and erosion control.
