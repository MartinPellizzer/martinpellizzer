Colchicum biebersteinii

Colchicum biebersteinii, commonly known as the Sierra Valley colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silky hair. It grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Colchicum biebersteinii is part of the Colchicaceae family and is known for its toxic properties, containing colchicine.
