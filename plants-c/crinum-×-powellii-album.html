Crinum × powellii album

Crinum × powellii album, commonly known as the white crinum lily, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in summer, often with a subtle fragrance. The plant produces strap-like, broad leaves that are typically green and arranged in a rosette pattern. It is native to regions of the southern United States and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Crinum × powellii album prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for gardens.
