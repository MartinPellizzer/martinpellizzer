Carex divisa var. splendens

Carex divisa var. splendens, commonly known as the splendens sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown, adding subtle texture to its foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
