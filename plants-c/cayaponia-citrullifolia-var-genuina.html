Cayaponia citrullifolia var. genuina

Cayaponia citrullifolia var. genuina, commonly known as the wild watermelon vine, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and distinctive yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces large, green, edible fruits that resemble watermelons but are typically smaller. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a rough texture.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
