Crotalaria lunulata

Crotalaria lunulata, commonly known as the moonflower crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant produces showy, yellow or orange flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets, and the plant often grows as an annual herb.

Crotalaria lunulata is also noted for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility.
