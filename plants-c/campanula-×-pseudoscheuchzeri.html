Campanula × pseudoscheuchzeri

Campanula × pseudoscheuchzeri, commonly known as the hybrid bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Campanula × pseudoscheuchzeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
