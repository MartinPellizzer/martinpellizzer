Calamus niger

Calamus niger, commonly known as black calamus, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the wetlands of North America.

It has long, narrow, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or reddish in color. Its rhizomes are often harvested for their aromatic properties and traditional medicinal uses.

Calamus niger thrives in shallow, slow-moving waters and is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
