Carex dioiscostrongyla

Carex dioiscostrongyla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowering spikes. Native to temperate regions, this plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. The flowers are usually wind-pollinated, and the plant produces small, three-lobed seeds.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
