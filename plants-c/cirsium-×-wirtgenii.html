Cirsium × wirtgenii

Cirsium × wirtgenii, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and roadsides. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to other regions for horticultural purposes.

However, it can become invasive in some areas due to its vigorous growth and adaptability.
