Croton nitrariifolius var. arechavaletae

Croton nitrariifolius var. arechavaletae, commonly known as the Arechavaleta croton, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often lobed leaves that exhibit a range of colors from green to reddish-brown. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by capsule fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
