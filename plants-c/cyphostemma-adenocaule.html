Cyphostemma adenocaule

Cyphostemma adenocaule, commonly known as the African moon vine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shrublands or along fences.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
