Campanula humillima var. luristanica

Campanula humillima var. luristanica, commonly known as the Luristan bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It features small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and habitat specificity, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
