Crassula littlewoodii

Crassula littlewoodii, commonly known as the Littlewood's crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, paddle-shaped leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or red during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
