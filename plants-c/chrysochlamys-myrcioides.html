Chrysochlamys myrcioides

Chrysochlamys myrcioides, commonly known as the golden-leaved myrtle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, where it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in coastal and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves that often display a striking golden or yellowish hue. It produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by edible, berry-like fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
