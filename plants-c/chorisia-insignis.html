Chorisia insignis

Chorisia insignis, commonly known as the giant hyacinth bean, is a striking plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a climbing shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height, featuring large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. Its botanical characteristics include a woody stem, compound leaves, and a unique floral structure that attracts pollinators.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
