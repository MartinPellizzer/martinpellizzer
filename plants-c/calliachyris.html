Calliachyris

Calliachyris, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a genus of flowering plants in the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are characterized by their intricate, often showy flowers that resemble spiders, hence their common name. They typically have slender stems and are native to regions in South America, particularly Brazil. Calliachyris species are epiphytic, meaning they grow on the surfaces of other plants without being parasitic.

Their botanical significance lies in their unique floral structures and ecological roles within their native habitats.
