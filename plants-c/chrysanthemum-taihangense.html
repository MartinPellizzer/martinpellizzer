Chrysanthemum taihangense

Chrysanthemum taihangense, commonly known as the Taihang mountain chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to China, specifically found in the Taihang Mountains.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers that bloom in the fall. The plant typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in temperate climates. It has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellow to orange flowers, making it a valuable ornamental plant.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
