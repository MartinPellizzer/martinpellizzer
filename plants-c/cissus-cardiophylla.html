Cissus cardiophylla

Cissus cardiophylla, commonly known as the heart-leaf cissus, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus cardiophylla is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and easy care requirements.
