Cleretum bellidiforme

Cleretum bellidiforme, commonly known as the yellow sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual or biennial herb characterized by its slender stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Cleretum bellidiforme is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a hardy species in its native habitat.
