Carica posopora

Carica posopora, commonly known as the bitter apple.

It is a member of the Caricaceae family and is native to tropical regions of South America. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically bitter and not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, while its flowers are small and inconspicuous.

The plant is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
