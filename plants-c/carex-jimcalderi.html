Carex jimcalderi

Carex jimcalderi, commonly known as Jim Calder's sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly found in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. This perennial plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 24 inches and features grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spikelets.

Jim Calder's sedge is often used in native plant gardens due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
