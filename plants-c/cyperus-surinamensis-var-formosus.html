Cyperus surinamensis var. formosus

Cyperus surinamensis var. formosus, commonly known as the Surinam sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along waterways, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in clusters, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Cyperus surinamensis var. formosus is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to tolerate varying water conditions.
