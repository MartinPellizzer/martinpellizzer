Crataegus pallida

Crataegus pallida, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Crataegus pallida is often found in woodlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
