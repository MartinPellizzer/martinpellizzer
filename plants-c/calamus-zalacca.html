Calamus zalacca

Calamus zalacca, commonly known as the yellow bamboo or wild bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. Calamus zalacca is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
