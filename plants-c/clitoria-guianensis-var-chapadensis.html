Clitoria guianensis var. chapadensis

Clitoria guianensis var. chapadensis, commonly known as the Chapada butterfly pea, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that resemble butterflies. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with trifoliate leaves and prominent stipules. Its flowers are borne in racemes and are known for their vibrant color and ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
