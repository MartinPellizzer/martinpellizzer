Cuphea racemosa f. parvifolia

Cuphea racemosa f. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-flowered firecracker plant, is a flowering shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Lythraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red or orange flowers that resemble tiny firecrackers. The plant produces small, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its unique floral structure attracts hummingbirds and other pollinators, adding ecological value to the landscape.
