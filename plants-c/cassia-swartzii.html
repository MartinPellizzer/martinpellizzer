Cassia swartzii

Cassia swartzii, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets, and it produces long, slender pods that contain seeds. Cassia swartzii thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
