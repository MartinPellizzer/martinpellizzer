Chionanthus brachystachys

Chionanthus brachystachys, commonly known as the short-stalked fringeflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a smooth margin.

The fruit is a small, capsule that splits open to release seeds.
