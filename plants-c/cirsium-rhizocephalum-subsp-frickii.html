Cirsium rhizocephalum subsp. frickii

Cirsium rhizocephalum subsp.

frickii, commonly known as the Frick's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its subspecies status highlights subtle morphological differences from the main species, particularly in leaf structure and flower morphology.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various insects and small animals.
