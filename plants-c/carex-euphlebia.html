Carex euphlebia

Carex euphlebia, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, inconspicuous flowers, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex euphlebia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
