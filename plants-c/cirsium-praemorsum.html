Cirsium praemorsum

Cirsium praemorsum, commonly known as the early thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and spiny leaves. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are covered in stiff, prickly hairs, and its flowers are usually purple or pink, appearing in late spring to early summer.

Cirsium praemorsum plays a role in supporting pollinators and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
