Cissus compressa

Cissus compressa, commonly known as the stonecrop fig or rock fig, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fig-like fruits. Cissus compressa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it a hardy and low-maintenance plant.
