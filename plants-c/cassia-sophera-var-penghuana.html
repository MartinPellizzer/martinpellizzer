Cassia sophera var. penghuana

Cassia sophera var. penghuana, commonly known as Penghu cassia, is a shrub native to the Penghu Islands in Taiwan.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and small, yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces long, slender pods containing flat, elliptical seeds. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
