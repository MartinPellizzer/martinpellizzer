Croton heterocarpus

Croton heterocarpus, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often toxic species. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikes, and it produces distinctive, fleshy, orange-red fruits.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
