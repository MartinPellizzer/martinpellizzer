Calonemorchis fragrantissima

Calonemorchis fragrantissima, commonly known as the fragrant orchid, is a species of orchid native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often displaying a range of colors from white to pale pink. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are long and narrow, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
