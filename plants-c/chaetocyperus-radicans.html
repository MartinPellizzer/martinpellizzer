Chaetocyperus radicans

Chaetocyperus radicans, commonly known as the spreading flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant has slender, triangular stems that often root at the nodes, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with small, inconspicuous flowers clustered in spikelets.

It is typically found in moist, disturbed soils and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
