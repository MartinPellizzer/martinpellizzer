Cnicus samniticus

Cnicus samniticus, commonly known as the southern sandwort, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches heights of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are usually lobed or toothed, and its flowers are small, yellow, and borne in clusters. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal or disturbed habitats.

The species is characterized by its adaptability to harsh environments and its ability to survive in nutrient-poor conditions.
