Crataegus atrorubens

Crataegus atrorubens, commonly known as the blackthorn or sloe, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny stems and small, dark purple berries. The plant typically grows in woodland edges, hedgerows, and scrublands, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are white or pink and appear in spring, followed by the development of its edible yet tart berries in late summer.

The plant has historical uses in medicine and as a source of dye and flavoring.
