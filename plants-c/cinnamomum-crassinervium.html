Cinnamomum crassinervium

Cinnamomum crassinervium, commonly known as the broad-veined cinnamon or Chinese cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark brown bark that resembles true cinnamon. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by elliptical, dark blue berries. Its leaves are leathery, glossy, and have prominent veins, which give it its common name.

This species is valued for its essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some regions.
