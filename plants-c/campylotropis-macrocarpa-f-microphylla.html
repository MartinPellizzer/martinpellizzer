Campylotropis macrocarpa f. microphylla

Campylotropis macrocarpa f. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved large-fruited burr clover, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and trifoliate leaves with small, rounded leaflets. The plant produces dense clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruit is a spiny, pod-like structure that contains several seeds.

This variety is often found in dry, disturbed soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing ability.
