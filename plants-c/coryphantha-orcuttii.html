Coryphantha orcuttii

Coryphantha orcuttii, commonly known as the Orcutt's hedgehog cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained sandy or rocky soils and requires full sun exposure.

Coryphantha orcuttii is a drought-tolerant plant, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.
