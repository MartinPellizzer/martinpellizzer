Clavinodum

Clavinodum, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in spikes. Clavinodum is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to drought conditions.

It is also recognized for its use in traditional medicine and as a forage crop for livestock.
