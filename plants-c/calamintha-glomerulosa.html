Calamintha glomerulosa

Calamintha glomerulosa, commonly known as clustered caraway, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in summer. Its roots and seeds contain essential oils with potential medicinal properties.

Calamintha glomerulosa thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
