Crepis foetida subsp. commutata

Crepis foetida subsp.

commutata, commonly known as the stinking hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its yellow flowers and hairy, often glandular leaves.

The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically growing in grassy areas and disturbed soils. It has a rosette of basal leaves and produces a single stem with terminal flower heads.

The subspecies is distinguished by its more compact growth and specific morphological features compared to other subspecies of Crepis foetida.
