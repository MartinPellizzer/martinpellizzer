Crataegus eatoniana

Crataegus eatoniana, commonly known as Eaton's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins, and turn yellow in the fall.

Crataegus eatoniana is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
