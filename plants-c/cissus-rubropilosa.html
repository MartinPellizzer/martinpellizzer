Cissus rubropilosa

Cissus rubropilosa, commonly known as the red-hair cissus, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-colored tendrils and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its leaves are typically pubescent, with a soft, fuzzy texture.

Cissus rubropilosa thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
