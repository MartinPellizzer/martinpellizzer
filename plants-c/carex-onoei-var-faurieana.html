Carex onoei var. faurieana

Carex onoei var. faurieana, commonly known as the Faurie's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its flowering spikes are usually green to reddish in color, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
