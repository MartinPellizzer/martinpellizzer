Calceolaria undulata

Calceolaria undulata, commonly known as the wavy hooded orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, hood-like flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests and rocky slopes. Calceolaria undulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
