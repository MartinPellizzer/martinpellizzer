Chenopodium opulifolium f. platyphyllum

Chenopodium opulifolium f. platyphyllum, commonly known as the broad-leaf goosefoot, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powder, known as bloom. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on slender stalks. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

The species is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
