Carex echinata subsp. gasparrinii

Carex echinata subsp.

gasparrinii, commonly known as the Gasparrini's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
