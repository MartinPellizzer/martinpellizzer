Crataegus monogyna var. incisifolia

Crataegus monogyna var. incisifolia, commonly known as the serrated hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red, fleshy berries known as haws, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and rocky soils, and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
