Cucurbita maxima elliptica

Cucurbita maxima elliptica, commonly known as the warty pumpkin, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is characterized by its elongated, oval-shaped fruits with a rough, warty surface that ranges in color from pale yellow to orange. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that are typically separate from the fruiting structures. Its vines can grow up to several meters in length, and it is cultivated for both ornamental and edible purposes.

The fruit is rich in nutrients and is often used in traditional cuisines.
