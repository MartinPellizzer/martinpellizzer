Chaetospora cygnea

Chaetospora cygnea, commonly known as the silver hair fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are covered in soft, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its rhizomes are creeping and produce clusters of small, oval-shaped spore cases.

Chaetospora cygnea is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
