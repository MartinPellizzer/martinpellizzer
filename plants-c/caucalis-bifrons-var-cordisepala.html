Caucalis bifrons var. cordisepala

Caucalis bifrons var. cordisepala, commonly known as the heart-sepaled bellflower, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia, including China and the Himalayas.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The leaves are typically lanceolate and often have a slightly hairy texture. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
