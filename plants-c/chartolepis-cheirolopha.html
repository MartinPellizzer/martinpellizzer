Chartolepis cheirolopha

Chartolepis cheirolopha, commonly known as the spiral leaf cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that give it a striking visual appearance. The plant has a short, stout trunk with a fibrous, spongy bark. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with a feathery structure that enhances its ornamental value.

Native to South Africa, it thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it a hardy and low-maintenance plant.
