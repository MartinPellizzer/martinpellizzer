Calea pohliana

Calea pohliana, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy yellow flowers that open at night. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique nocturnal blooming habit.

Calea pohliana is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
