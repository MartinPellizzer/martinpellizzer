Carex vesicaria var. plumbea

Carex vesicaria var. plumbea, commonly known as the plumbea sedge, is a perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer. The leaves are typically bluish-gray in color, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
