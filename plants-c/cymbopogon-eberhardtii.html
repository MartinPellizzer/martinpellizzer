Cymbopogon eberhardtii

Cymbopogon eberhardtii, commonly known as Eberhardt's lemon grass, is a perennial grass native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stalks and aromatic, lemon-scented leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its fragrance and potential medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for essential oils and traditional herbal use.
