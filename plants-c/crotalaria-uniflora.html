Crotalaria uniflora

Crotalaria uniflora, commonly known as the one-flower cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, bushy growth habit and produces distinctive yellow or orange flowers with two lobes. The plant has palmately compound leaves and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod and are often used as a forage crop.

Crotalaria uniflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for potential medicinal properties.
