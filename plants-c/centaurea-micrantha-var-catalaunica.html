Centaurea micrantha var. catalaunica

Centaurea micrantha var. catalaunica, commonly known as the Catalaunica knapweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bright yellow to orange flowers arranged in a composite head. Its inflorescence is characterized by a dense cluster of tiny florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It is often found in Mediterranean climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
