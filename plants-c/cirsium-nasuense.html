Cirsium nasuense

Cirsium nasuense, commonly known as the Nasu thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central Europe.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

Cirsium nasuense is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
