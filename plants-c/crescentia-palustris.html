Crescentia palustris

Crescentia palustris, commonly known as the swamp cucumber, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a climbing vine that produces large, fleshy, orange-colored fruits resembling cucumbers. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves and is often found in wetland habitats. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals and are pollinated by insects.

The fruit is edible when ripe and has a mild, sweet flavor.
