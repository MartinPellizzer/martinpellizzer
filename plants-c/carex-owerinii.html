Carex owerinii

Carex owerinii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered spikelets. Native to parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

It plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
