Carphephorus junceus

Carphephorus junceus, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to aquatic conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands.

Carphephorus junceus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
