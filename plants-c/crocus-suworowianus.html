Crocus suworowianus

Crocus suworowianus, commonly known as the Suworow's crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Iridaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Kazakhstan and Russia. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it prefers well-drained, sandy soils.

Crocus suworowianus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in cooler climates.
