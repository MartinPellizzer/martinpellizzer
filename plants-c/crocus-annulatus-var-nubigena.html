Crocus annulatus var. nubigena

Crocus annulatus var. nubigena, commonly known as the Alpine crocus, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its compact, tufted growth habit and produces showy, violet-blue flowers with a distinctive yellow center. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its bulbs are perennial, allowing it to survive harsh winters and bloom again in spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often found in mountain meadows.
