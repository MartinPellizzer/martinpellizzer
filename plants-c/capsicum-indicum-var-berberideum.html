Capsicum indicum var. berberideum

Capsicum indicum var. berberideum, commonly known as the yellow bell pepper, is a variant of the Capsicum genus native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, bell-shaped fruits that typically range in color from yellow to orange when mature. The plant produces small, white flowers and has a moderate growth habit, making it suitable for both open-field and greenhouse cultivation. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

This variety is valued for its flavor and is often used in culinary applications across various cuisines.
