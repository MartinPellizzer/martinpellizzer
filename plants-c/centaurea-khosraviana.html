Centaurea khosraviana

Centaurea khosraviana, commonly known as Khosravian coneflower, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has a compact, bushy growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, while the flowers consist of a central disk surrounded by ray florets.

Centaurea khosraviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
