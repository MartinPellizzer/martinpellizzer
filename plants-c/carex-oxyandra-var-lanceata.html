Carex oxyandra var. lanceata

Carex oxyandra var. lanceata, commonly known as the lance-leaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, giving it its common name. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color.

This species is often found in moist or wet habitats such as marshes and along stream banks.
