Convolvulus asarifolius

Convolvulus asarifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and heart-shaped leaves with a serrated edge. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, blooming in the morning. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Convolvulus asarifolius is a member of the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae) and is known for its rapid growth and ornamental value.
