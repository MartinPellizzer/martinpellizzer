Canarium treubianum

Canarium treubianum, commonly known as the African canarium, is a tropical evergreen tree native to central and western Africa.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and dense, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, fleshy drupes that are often consumed by local communities. It is also valued for its timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

Additionally, the plant plays an important role in traditional medicine and ecological systems in its native habitat.
