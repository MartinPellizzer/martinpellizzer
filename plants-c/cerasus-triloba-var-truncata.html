Cerasus triloba var. truncata

Cerasus triloba var. truncata, commonly known as the Japanese apricot or truncated cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its oval, truncate-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible but not typically cultivated for commercial use. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its spring blooms and autumn foliage.

The species is relatively hardy and adaptable to a range of soil types and climates.
