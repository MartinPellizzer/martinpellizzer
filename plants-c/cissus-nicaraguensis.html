Cissus nicaraguensis

Cissus nicaraguensis, commonly known as the Nicaraguan grapevine, is a climbing plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its woody stems and tendrils that help it climb. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that are edible. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a waxy texture.

Cissus nicaraguensis is often found in humid, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental and ecological roles.
