Croton calonervosus

Croton calonervosus, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which appear during the dry season. The plant has alternate, simple, and often heart-shaped leaves that are green with prominent veins. It produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into three-winged fruits.

Croton calonervosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun.
