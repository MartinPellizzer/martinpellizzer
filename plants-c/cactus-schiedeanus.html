Cactus schiedeanus

Cactus schiedeanus, commonly known as the Schiedea cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family and is native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a low-growing, branching plant with thick, fleshy stems that store water, a characteristic adaptation to its arid environment. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pink flowers during the summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, and it has a slow growth rate, making it a long-lived species.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
