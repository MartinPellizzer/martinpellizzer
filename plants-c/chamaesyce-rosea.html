Chamaesyce rosea

Chamaesyce rosea, commonly known as the roseleaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its fleshy, succulent stems and leaves that are typically covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months, adding a subtle ornamental value to its appearance. Its succulent nature allows it to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well-adapted to dry conditions.

The species is often found in sandy or gravelly soils, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
