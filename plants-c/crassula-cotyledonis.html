Crassula cotyledonis

Crassula cotyledonis, commonly known as the dwarf crassula or the baby crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing form and fleshy, rounded leaves that are typically green with a reddish or pinkish tinge under stress. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, or purple during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for xeriscaping and indoor cultivation.

Due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
