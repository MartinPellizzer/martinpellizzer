Comarostaphylis bicolor

Comarostaphylis bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in coastal areas. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pink, with a distinctive two-toned appearance. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, adapted to withstand hot, dry conditions.

Comarostaphylis bicolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its resilience and beauty.
