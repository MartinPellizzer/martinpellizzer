Calamus moorei

Calamus moorei, commonly known as the Moore's rattan, is a climbing palm native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its long, flexible stems that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by elongated, spiky fruits. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with long, narrow leaflets that are often used in traditional crafts.

Calamus moorei is valued for its durable and flexible rattan, which is widely used in furniture and basketry.
