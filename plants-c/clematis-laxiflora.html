Clematis laxiflora

Clematis laxiflora, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis laxiflora is valued for its ornamental value and adaptability in garden settings.
