Cyperus multibracteatus

Cyperus multibracteatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters, and it produces small, three-lobed fruits.

Cyperus multibracteatus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
