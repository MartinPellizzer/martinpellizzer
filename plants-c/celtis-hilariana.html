Celtis hilariana

Celtis hilariana, commonly known as the Chinese laurustin, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by spherical, fleshy fruits that are edible for certain bird species. Celtis hilariana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas, contributing to local biodiversity.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting wildlife.
