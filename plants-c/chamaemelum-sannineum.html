Chamaemelum sannineum

Chamaemelum sannineum, commonly known as sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit. The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow, gray-green, and covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Sweet everlasting is often used in herbal medicine and is valued for its aromatic properties.
