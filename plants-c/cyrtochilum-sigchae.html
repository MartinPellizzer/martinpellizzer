Cyrtochilum sigchae

Cyrtochilum sigchae, commonly known as the Ecuadorian cyclostele, is a terrestrial orchid native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, intricate flowers. The plant features a short, thick rhizome and produces pseudobulbs that support its foliage. Its flowers are typically pale green to white, with a distinctive spurred lip that aids in pollination.

Cyrtochilum sigchae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized orchid collections.
