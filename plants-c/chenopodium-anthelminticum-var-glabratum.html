Chenopodium anthelminticum var. glabratum

Chenopodium anthelminticum var. glabratum, commonly known as Indian spinach or lambsquarters, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is an annual herb characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves and branched stems. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
