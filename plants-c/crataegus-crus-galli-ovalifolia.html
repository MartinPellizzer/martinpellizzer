Crataegus crus-galli ovalifolia

Crataegus crus-galli ovalifolia, commonly known as the ovalleaf hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in the spring. The fruit is a red or orange berry that matures in the fall and is often used in traditional medicine. This plant is often found in woodlands, hedgerows, and along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits to pollinators.
