Chenopodium ziegleri

Chenopodium ziegleri, commonly known as desert goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers that appear in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Chenopodium ziegleri is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
