Chenopodium glaucum subsp. ambiguum

Chenopodium glaucum subsp.

ambiguum, commonly known as bitter goosefoot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America. It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and has a sprawling growth habit.

The plant features alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves with a rough, sandpapery texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences called capitula.

The species is often used in traditional medicine and is also a common weed in agricultural settings.
