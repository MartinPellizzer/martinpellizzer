Calibrachoa linoides subsp. furcata

Calibrachoa linoides subsp.

furcata, commonly known as the golden weeping calibrachoa, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and profuse display of small, golden-yellow flowers.

The plant produces clusters of tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets and containers.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant color and compact, cascading form.
