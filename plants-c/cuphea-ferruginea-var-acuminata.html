Cuphea ferruginea var. acuminata

Cuphea ferruginea var. acuminata, commonly known as the red flame cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red bracts that surround its small, white flowers. The plant produces showy, spiky inflorescences that are highly attractive to hummingbirds and other pollinators. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant color and unique appearance.
