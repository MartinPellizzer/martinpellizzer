Crambe gibberosa

Crambe gibberosa, commonly known as the yellow cress, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like mustard and cabbage. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring alternate, pinnately compound leaves. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in the spring.

The seeds are small and oval, often used in traditional herbal remedies.
