Cheiridopsis purpurea

Cheiridopsis purpurea, commonly known as the purple ice plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers during the summer months. It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species.

Cheiridopsis purpurea is popular as a rock garden plant due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance.
