Calamus nigricans

Calamus nigricans, commonly known as black cane reed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by hard, woody fruits. Its leaves are long and narrow, often used in traditional crafts and as a material for making rattles.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
