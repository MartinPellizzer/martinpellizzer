Carduus subcarlinoides

Carduus subcarlinoides, commonly known as the lesser carduus, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. The plant features spiny leaves and purple to pink composite flowers that bloom in late summer. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
