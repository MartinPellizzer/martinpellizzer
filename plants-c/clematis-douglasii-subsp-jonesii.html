Clematis douglasii subsp. jonesii

Clematis douglasii subsp.

jonesii, commonly known as the Jones' Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the western United States. It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun. Its stems are twining and can grow up to 15 feet long, making it a vigorous climber.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and natural landscapes.
