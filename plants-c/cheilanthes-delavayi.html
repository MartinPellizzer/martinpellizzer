Cheilanthes delavayi

Cheilanthes delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's rockcap, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and is typically found growing on rocky substrates in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant has a short, creeping rhizome with dense clusters of dark green, leathery fronds. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds in clusters known as sori.

This species is well adapted to harsh conditions and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and resilience.
