Carum colchicum

Carum colchicum, commonly known as autumn garlic, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features basal leaves that are aromatic and hairy, with a distinct garlic-like scent. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its bulbous underground stems store nutrients, supporting its growth cycle.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
