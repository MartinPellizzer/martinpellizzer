Croton phourinii

Croton phourinii, commonly known as the Madagascar croton, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful foliage. The plant typically features palmate leaves with a thick, waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

Croton phourinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
