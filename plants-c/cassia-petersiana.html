Cassia petersiana

Cassia petersiana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant produces long, slender pods that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia petersiana is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
