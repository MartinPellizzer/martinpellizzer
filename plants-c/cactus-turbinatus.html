Cactus turbinatus

Cactus turbinatus, commonly known as the turbinated cactus, is a species native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered in dense, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically short-lived but attract pollinators such as bees and moths. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to drought conditions.

The plant's structure and growth habits reflect its evolutionary response to harsh desert environments.
