Cocos weddelliana var. pynaertii

Cocos weddelliana var. pynaertii, commonly known as the Weddell's coconut, is a rare and unique variant of the coconut palm.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated fruit that is typically smaller than the standard coconut. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in coastal areas with sandy soils. Its leaves are long and feather-like, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
