Cadetia quadrangularis

Cadetia quadrangularis, commonly known as the four-angled willow herb, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its square, angular stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or wet meadows.

Cadetia quadrangularis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
