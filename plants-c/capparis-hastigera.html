Capparis hastigera

Capparis hastigera, commonly known as the hairy caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by round, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Capparis hastigera is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and ability to withstand coastal conditions.
