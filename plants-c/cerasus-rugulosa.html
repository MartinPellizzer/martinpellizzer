Cerasus rugulosa

Cerasus rugulosa, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or dwarf cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, sweet, edible fruits known as cherries, which are typically harvested in late summer. Cerasus rugulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its beautiful blossoms and compact growth habit.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade to full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.
