Casuarina leptotrema

Casuarina leptotrema, commonly known as the slender casuarina, is a small evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its unique, needle-like foliage that is arranged in cylindrical clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by woody, cone-like fruits. It thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in dune environments.

Casuarina leptotrema is valued for its hardy nature and ability to stabilize sand dunes, making it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
