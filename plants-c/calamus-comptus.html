Calamus comptus

Calamus comptus, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, elongated leaves. It produces small, clustered inflorescences with yellowish-white flowers.

Calamus comptus is often used in traditional practices and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
