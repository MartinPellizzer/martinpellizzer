Chenopodium trigonon subsp. stellulatum

Chenopodium trigonon subsp.

stellulatum, commonly known as red goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States. It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are typically red or purple in color, especially on the undersides.

The plant produces small, green flowers in clusters, which are followed by triangular, spiny fruits. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

This species is part of the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and use in traditional medicine.
