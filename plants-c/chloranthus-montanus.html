Chloranthus montanus

Chloranthus montanus, commonly known as the mountain chloranth, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Chloranthaceae, which is characterized by its unique floral structures and lack of true fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has simple, alternate leaves with a smooth texture. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, usually yellow or greenish-yellow, and are clustered in umbels.

The plant is of interest to botanists due to its primitive floral morphology and ecological adaptations.
