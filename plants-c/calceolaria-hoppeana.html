Calceolaria hoppeana

Calceolaria hoppeana, commonly known as the Chilean slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of Chile and Peru.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant produces small, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique and delicate floral structure.

Calceolaria hoppeana is also noted for its ability to grow in rocky or sandy soils, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.
