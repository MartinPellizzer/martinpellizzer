Calystegia hederacea f. pentapetala

Calystegia hederacea f. pentapetala, commonly known as the five-petaled bindweed, is a variant of the common bindweed species.

It is a climbing vine characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers typically have five petals, distinguishing this form from the typical four-petaled version. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
