Cranichis georginae

Cranichis georginae, commonly known as the Georgina grass-vetch, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, where it thrives in full sun. Its root system is fibrous, allowing it to anchor securely in poor soils, and it plays a minor role in local ecosystems as a source of nectar for pollinators.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species.
