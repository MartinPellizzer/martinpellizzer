Crocus iridiflorus

Crocus iridiflorus, commonly known as the violet crocus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking violet or purple flowers. The plant typically blooms in early spring, with showy, cup-shaped petals that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it prefers well-drained soil in full sun.

The bulbs of Crocus iridiflorus are toxic and should be handled with care.
