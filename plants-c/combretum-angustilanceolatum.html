Combretum angustilanceolatum

Combretum angustilanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf combretum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Combretaceae and is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

It is often found in moist woodland or riverine habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses.
