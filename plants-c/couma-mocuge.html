Couma mocuge

Couma mocuge, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes and is characterized by its pod-like fruits. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow to orange flowers. Its seeds are enclosed in a pod and are often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
