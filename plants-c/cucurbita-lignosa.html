Cucurbita lignosa

Cucurbita lignosa, commonly known as the hardy gourd, is a species native to North America.

It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other plants like cucumbers and squash. This plant is characterized by its robust, woody stems and large, lobed leaves. The fruit can vary in shape and size, often featuring a rough, textured surface.

It is typically harvested when the rind has hardened, making it suitable for long-term storage.
