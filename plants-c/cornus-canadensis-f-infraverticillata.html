Cornus canadensis f. infraverticillata

Cornus canadensis f. infraverticillata, commonly known as the lesser cornelian cherry, is a rare and distinctive variant of the cornus species.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. The plant features small, clustered white flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by red berries that mature in late summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a distinctive veining pattern.

This botanical form is notable for its unique floral structure and ecological niche within its native range.
