Carduus defloratus subsp. carlinifolius

Carduus defloratus subsp.

carlinifolius, commonly known as the Carlini-leaved thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features a robust stem with spiny leaves that are often lobed or toothed. Its flowers are yellow and are borne in a single head at the top of the stem.

It is characterized by its distinctive, large, and sometimes deeply lobed leaves, which contribute to its ornamental value.
