Carex sociata

Carex sociata, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grasses, and rushes. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, upright leaves that form dense clumps.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are arranged in clusters along the stem.
