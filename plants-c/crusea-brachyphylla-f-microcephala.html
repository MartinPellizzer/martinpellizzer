Crusea brachyphylla f. microcephala

Crusea brachyphylla f. microcephala, commonly known as the miniature rose.

This is a compact, dwarf form of the species, characterized by its small, rounded growth habit and dense foliage. It produces clusters of fragrant, semi-double pink flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and suitability for container gardening.

It requires well-drained soil and full sun to thrive, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, visually striking plant.
