Combretum tetragonocarpum var. tetralophum

Combretum tetragonocarpum var. tetralophum, commonly known as the African flame bush, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and its bark is typically smooth and reddish in color. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds.

This species is adapted to dry conditions and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
