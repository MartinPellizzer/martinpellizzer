Chionogentias antarctica

Chionogentias antarctica, commonly known as the Antarctic hairgrass, is a flowering plant native to the extreme environments of Antarctica.

It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats, adapted to survive in harsh, cold conditions with minimal nutrients. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, hairy structures that help reduce water loss and protect against freezing temperatures.

This species plays a crucial role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in one of the most inhospitable regions on Earth.
