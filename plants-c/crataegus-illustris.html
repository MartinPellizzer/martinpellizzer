Crataegus illustris

Crataegus illustris, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges, and it thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil.

Crataegus illustris is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
