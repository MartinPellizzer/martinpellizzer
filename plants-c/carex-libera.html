Carex libera

Carex libera, commonly known as the free sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to North America and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Carex libera is generally hardy and adaptable to a range of moisture conditions.
