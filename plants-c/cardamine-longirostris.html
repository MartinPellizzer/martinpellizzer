Cardamine longirostris

Cardamine longirostris, commonly known as the long-beaked watercress, is a small herbaceous plant native to moist, temperate regions.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, wet environments such as stream banks and forest floors. Its leaves are often submerged or floating, adapted to aquatic conditions.

Cardamine longirostris is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed wetlands.
