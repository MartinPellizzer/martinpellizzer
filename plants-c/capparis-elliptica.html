Capparis elliptica

Capparis elliptica, commonly known as the rock caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by elliptical, edible fruits. Its leaves are broad and oval, with a slightly hairy texture, and the plant is known for its ability to withstand drought conditions.

Capparis elliptica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
