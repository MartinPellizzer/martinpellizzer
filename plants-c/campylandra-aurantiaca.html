Campylandra aurantiaca

Campylandra aurantiaca, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange to red in color, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and compact growth habit.
