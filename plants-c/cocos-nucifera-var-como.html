Cocos nucifera var. como

Cocos nucifera var. como, commonly known as the Como coconut, is a variety of the coconut palm characterized by its distinctively shaped fruits.

The husk of the fruit is typically thinner and lighter in color compared to other coconut varieties, making it more susceptible to damage during handling. The kernel inside is rich in oil and nutrients, contributing to its value in both culinary and industrial applications. This variety is often cultivated in tropical regions for its high yield and quality of copra.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject of study in plant morphology and agricultural science.
