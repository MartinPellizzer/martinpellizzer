Cipocereus pleurocarpus

Cipocereus pleurocarpus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and is adapted to arid environments. The plant produces large, fragrant white flowers that open at night. It has a shallow root system and thrives in well-draining soil.

Due to its unique blooming pattern, it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
