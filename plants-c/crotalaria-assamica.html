Crotalaria assamica

Crotalaria assamica, commonly known as Assam cowpea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and is often found in moist, tropical environments. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod and are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's structure and growth habits make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
