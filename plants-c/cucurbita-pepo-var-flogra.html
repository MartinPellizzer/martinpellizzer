Cucurbita pepo var. flogra

Cucurbita pepo var. flogra, commonly known as the ornamental gourd, is a variant of the common gourd species.

It is characterized by its diverse shapes, sizes, and vibrant colors, which make it popular for decorative purposes. The plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like pumpkins and squash. It typically has large, lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers that develop into the distinctive gourd fruits.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic value rather than for culinary use.
