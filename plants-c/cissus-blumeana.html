Cissus blumeana

Cissus blumeana, commonly known as the bone leaf or silver vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery or white underside, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Cissus blumeana is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
