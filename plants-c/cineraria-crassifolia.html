Cineraria crassifolia

Cineraria crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its large, thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
