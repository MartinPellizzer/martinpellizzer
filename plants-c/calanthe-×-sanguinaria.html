Calanthe × sanguinaria

Calanthe × sanguinaria, commonly known as the bloodroot orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers, which are often adorned with a distinctive black or purple spot at the base. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has strap-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and the plant is prized for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Calanthe × sanguinaria is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its delicate, ephemeral beauty.
