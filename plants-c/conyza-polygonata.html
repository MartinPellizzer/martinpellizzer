Conyza polygonata

Conyza polygonata, commonly known as common blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its branched stems, opposite leaves with serrated edges, and small, blue to purple composite flowers. The plant produces numerous tiny seeds that are dispersed by the wind, contributing to its widespread distribution. It often grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is considered a common weed in many agricultural areas.

Conyza polygonata has a cosmopolitan distribution and is adapted to a wide range of environmental conditions.
