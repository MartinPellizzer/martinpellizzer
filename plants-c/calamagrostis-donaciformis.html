Calamagrostis donaciformis

Calamagrostis donaciformis, commonly known as the narrowleaf tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces dense, tussock-like clumps with spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, meadows, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis donaciformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
