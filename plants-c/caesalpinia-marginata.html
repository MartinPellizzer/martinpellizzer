Caesalpinia marginata

Caesalpinia marginata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red floral clusters that appear at the base of the leaves, creating a striking visual display. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, ovate leaflets and produces elongated, woody pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in its native habitats.
