Calamus baratangensis

Calamus baratangensis, commonly known as the Baratang reed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the Indo-Pacific region, particularly found in tropical areas of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnately compound leaves. It thrives in wetland and coastal environments, often forming dense stands in marshy areas.

Calamus baratangensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
