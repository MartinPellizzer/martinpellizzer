Carex hordeiformis

Carex hordeiformis, commonly known as the barley sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex hordeiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
