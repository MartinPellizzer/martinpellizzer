Carduus majellensis

Carduus majellensis, commonly known as the Mediterranean thistle, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.

The plant's robust structure and adaptive features allow it to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
