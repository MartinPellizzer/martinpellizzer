Campanula angustiflora var. exilis

Campanula angustiflora var. exilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of blue or violet. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often covered in a fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in cooler climates.
