Carduncellus monspelliensium

Carduncellus monspelliensium, commonly known as the Italian thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, purple flower heads and silver-green, fuzzy leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
