Caladium pedatum

Caladium pedatum, commonly known as the swamp caladium, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are typically variegated with shades of white, pink, and green. The plant thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near wetlands or in swampy areas. Its foliage is the main ornamental feature, making it popular in gardens and as a houseplant.

Caladium pedatum is known for its ability to spread through rhizomes, forming dense colonies in suitable conditions.
