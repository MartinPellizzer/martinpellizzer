Carex scaposa var. scaposa

Carex scaposa var. scaposa, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
