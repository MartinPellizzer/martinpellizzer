Cyathea mettenii

Cyathea mettenii, commonly known as the King's Hat tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests. Its spores are produced on the underside of its fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Cyathea mettenii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
