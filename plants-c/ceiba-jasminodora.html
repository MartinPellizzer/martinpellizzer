Ceiba jasminodora

Ceiba jasminodora, commonly known as the yellow ceiba, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, buttressed roots and a tall, straight trunk that can reach heights of up to 30 meters. The tree produces clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom during the dry season. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds covered in a fleshy, sticky coating.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in the regions where it is found.
