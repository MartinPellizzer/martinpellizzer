Capsicum pereirae

Capsicum pereirae, commonly known as the Peruvian chili or Pachamama chili, is a species of Capsicum native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender fruits that typically range from 10 to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces bright red or orange fruits that are often used in traditional Andean cuisine. Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves, and it thrives in cooler, high-altitude environments.

Capsicum pereirae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
