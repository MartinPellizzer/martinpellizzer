Clematis caudata

Clematis caudata, commonly known as the trailing clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that can grow up to 10 meters in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Clematis caudata thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
