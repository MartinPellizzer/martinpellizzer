Crataegus obstipa

Crataegus obstipa, commonly known as the downy hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, Rosaceae.

It is native to eastern North America, particularly the eastern United States and parts of Canada. The plant is characterized by its small, white to pink flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and the bark is often flaky and greyish in color.

Crataegus obstipa is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
