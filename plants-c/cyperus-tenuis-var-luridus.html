Cyperus tenuis var. luridus

Cyperus tenuis var. luridus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually arranged in spikelets, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
