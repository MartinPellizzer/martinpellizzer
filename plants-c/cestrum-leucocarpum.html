Cestrum leucocarpum

Cestrum leucocarpum, commonly known as white-fruited cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are small, round, and white, containing seeds that are dispersed by birds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
