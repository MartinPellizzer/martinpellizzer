Cyperus pseudobulbosus

Cyperus pseudobulbosus, commonly known as the false bulb rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

Cyperus pseudobulbosus is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in waterlogged soils.
