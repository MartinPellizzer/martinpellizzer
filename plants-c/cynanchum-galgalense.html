Cynanchum galgalense

Cynanchum galgalense, commonly known as the Galgal Mountain milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes milkweeds, and is characterized by its milky sap. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing adaptations to dry climates. Its flowers are usually yellow or greenish, with a distinctive corolla that attracts pollinators.

The leaves are opposite and often have a waxy coating, aiding in water retention.
