Conicosia alborosea

Conicosia alborosea, commonly known as the white rose bush, is a flowering plant native to the subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy green surface. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
