Cyphostemma cirrhosum subsp. transvaalense

Cyphostemma cirrhosum subsp.

transvaalense, commonly known as the Transvaal moonvine, is a climbing plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by distinctive, bead-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The subspecies is notable for its ability to thrive in poor soils and its use in traditional medicine.
