Centaurea pauneroi

Centaurea pauneroi, commonly known as the Pauneroi knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its vibrant purple to pink flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are generally narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss in its native climate.
