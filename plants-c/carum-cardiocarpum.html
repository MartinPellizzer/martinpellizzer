Carum cardiocarpum

Carum cardiocarpum, commonly known as wild cumin or Persian cumin, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its seeds, which are used as a spice, have a warm, slightly sweet aroma with hints of anise and coriander.

Carum cardiocarpum thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
