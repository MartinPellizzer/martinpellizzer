Centaurea aplolepa var. ligustica

Centaurea aplolepa var. ligustica, commonly known as the Ligurian centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, branching stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers with purple markings, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
