Carex chungii var. rigida

Carex chungii var. rigida, commonly known as rigid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. It produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers in the summer, which are wind-pollinated.

This variety is distinguished by its stiff, upright growth habit and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
