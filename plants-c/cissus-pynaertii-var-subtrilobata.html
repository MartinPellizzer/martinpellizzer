Cissus pynaertii var. subtrilobata

Cissus pynaertii var. subtrilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed grape ivy, is a climbing plant native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped, three-lobed leaves that are typically light green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by translucent, waxy berries. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for hanging baskets and indoor gardening.
