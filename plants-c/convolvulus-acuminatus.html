Convolvulus acuminatus

Convolvulus acuminatus, commonly known as the sharp-pointed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial vine characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pale purple to white in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Convolvulaceae family.
