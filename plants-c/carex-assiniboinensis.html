Carex assiniboinensis

Carex assiniboinensis, commonly known as Assiniboine sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along the edges of lakes and rivers. It produces small, clustered flowers in early summer, which are often surrounded by specialized bracts.

Carex assiniboinensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
