Ceropegia monticola

Ceropegia monticola, commonly known as the mountain ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent shrub characterized by its fleshy, branching stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces unique, tubular flowers that are often white or pale green, with a distinctive hood-like structure. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Ceropegia monticola is often found in mountainous regions, where it grows in crevices and on rock faces.
