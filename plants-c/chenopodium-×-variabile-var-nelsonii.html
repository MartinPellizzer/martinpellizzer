Chenopodium × variabile var. nelsonii

Chenopodium × variabile var. nelsonii, commonly known as Nelson's goosefoot, is a hybrid species within the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically alternate and ovate to deltoid in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its stems are often hairy and branching. It is native to the western United States and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can be an indicator plant for certain ecological conditions.
