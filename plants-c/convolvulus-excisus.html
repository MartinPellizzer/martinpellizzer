Convolvulus excisus

Convolvulus excisus, commonly known as the cutleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed or cut, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Convolvulus excisus is also known for its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail, making it versatile for various landscaping applications.
