Crataegus frutescens

Crataegus frutescens, commonly known as the American hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and features thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its pinnate leaf structure and persistent fruiting bodies.
