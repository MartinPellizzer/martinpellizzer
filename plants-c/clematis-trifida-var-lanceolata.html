Clematis trifida var. lanceolata

Clematis trifida var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with three to five lance-shaped leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of white, pink, or blue. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to climb trellises or fences.
