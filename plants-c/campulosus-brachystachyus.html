Campulosus brachystachyus

Campulosus brachystachyus, commonly known as the narrow-stemmed campulocactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, spiny stems and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow or orange. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability. Its leaves are often reduced to spines, which help minimize water loss and deter herbivores.

This cactus is also noted for its slow growth rate and ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems.
