Crotalaria polygaloides subsp. orientalis

Crotalaria polygaloides subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the oriental crotalaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae. It is native to regions of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The plant features compound leaves with opposite arrangement and produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in racemose inflorescences. Its seeds are enclosed in pod-like fruits, and the plant is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability due to symbiotic relationships with rhizobia.

It is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
