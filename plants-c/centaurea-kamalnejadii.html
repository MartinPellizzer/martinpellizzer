Centaurea kamalnejadii

Centaurea kamalnejadii, commonly known as the Kamalnejad centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky and sandy soils, adapting to harsh, dry environments. Its flowers are usually blue to purple in color, with prominent central disc florets surrounded by ray florets.

Centaurea kamalnejadii is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited geographic distribution.
