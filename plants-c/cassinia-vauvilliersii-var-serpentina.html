Cassinia vauvilliersii var. serpentina

Cassinia vauvilliersii var. serpentina, commonly known as the serpentine cassinia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flower heads that are typically clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal and saline environments.

This variety is noted for its resilience to salt spray and wind, making it a hardy plant in harsh maritime conditions.
