Carex mertensii subsp. urostachys

Carex mertensii subsp.

urostachys, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikes of greenish to brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ecological importance and adaptability to various aquatic environments.
