Collaea latisiliqua

Collaea latisiliqua, commonly known as the broad-leaved collaea, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of broad, lanceolate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in dense clusters during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
