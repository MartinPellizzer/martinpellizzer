Croton longipedicellatus

Croton longipedicellatus, commonly known as the long-fruited croton, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often colorful fruits that hang in clusters. The leaves are typically alternate, simple, and vary in shape, often with a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

It is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
