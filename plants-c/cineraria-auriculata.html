Cineraria auriculata

Cineraria auriculata, commonly known as the auriculate cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features hairy, gray-green leaves that are often adorned with small, ear-like structures at their bases. It produces showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring and summer.

Cineraria auriculata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
