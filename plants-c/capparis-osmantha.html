Capparis osmantha

Capparis osmantha, commonly known as the Indian caper, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its woody stems and fleshy, often thorned leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by edible, round, green fruits. Its botanical features include a branched structure, hairy foliage, and a unique ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

The plant is also notable for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of edible capers.
