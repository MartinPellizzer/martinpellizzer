Cristaria glaucophylla var. simples

Cristaria glaucophylla var. simples, commonly known as the glaucophylla sempervirens, is a rare and delicate plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit and features small, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces striking, pale blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring, adding a unique aesthetic to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.
