Calceolaria cordifolia

Calceolaria cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and striking, pouch-like flowers that range in color from purple to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and thrives in cool climates. Its unique floral structure is adapted to attract specific pollinators, such as bees and hummingbirds.

Calceolaria cordifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
