Caucalis angustifolia

Caucalis angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Caucalis angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes grown in gardens.
