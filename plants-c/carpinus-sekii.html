Carpinus sekii

Carpinus sekii, commonly known as the Japanese hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, ovate leaves with a distinctive serrated margin. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged fruits that disperse its seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Carpinus sekii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to a variety of soil types.
