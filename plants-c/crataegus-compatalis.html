Crataegus compatalis

Crataegus compatalis, commonly known as the sandcherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces white or pink flowers in early spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges, and it is often found in open woodlands or along roadsides.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
