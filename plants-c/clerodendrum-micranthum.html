Clerodendrum micranthum

Clerodendrum micranthum, commonly known as the small-flowered glorybower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a capsule containing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
