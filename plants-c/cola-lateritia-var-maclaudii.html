Cola lateritia var. maclaudii

Cola lateritia var. maclaudii, commonly known as Maclaud's cola, is a variant of the cola tree native to western Africa.

It is a member of the Sterculiaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves and distinctive floral structures. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that develop into woody pods containing stimulant-rich seeds. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

This species is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its potential medicinal uses.
