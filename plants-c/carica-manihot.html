Carica manihot

Carica manihot, commonly known as cassava, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, green flowers that eventually develop into cylindrical, woody fruits containing numerous seeds. Its roots are the primary economically important part, used for food and industrial purposes.

Cassava is a hardy plant that thrives in warm, humid climates and is highly adaptable to various soil types.
