Cerastium arcticum f. nigrescens

Cerastium arcticum f. nigrescens, commonly known as the black-leaved mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and small, often hairy leaves. The leaves are typically dark green to blackish in color, giving the plant its distinguishing appearance. It produces white or pale pink flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

This plant is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils and tolerating low temperatures.
