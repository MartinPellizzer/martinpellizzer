Crepis agrestis

Crepis agrestis, commonly known as the field mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish composite flowers that bloom in summer. It typically grows in grassy areas, meadows, and along roadsides.
