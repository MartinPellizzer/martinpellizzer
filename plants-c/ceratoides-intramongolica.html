Ceratoides intramongolica

Ceratoides intramongolica, commonly known as the Mongolian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure with fleshy, succulent leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in the summer months. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with thick leaves that store water and reduce transpiration.

Ceratoides intramongolica plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is of interest for its ecological and potential medicinal properties.
