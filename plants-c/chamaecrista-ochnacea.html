Chamaecrista ochnacea

Chamaecrista ochnacea, commonly known as the yellow burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain small, round seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it an important component of native plant ecosystems and a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
