Conobea punctata

Conobea punctata, commonly known as the spotted conebush, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, cone-shaped seed heads that are often covered in fine hairs. Conobea punctata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
