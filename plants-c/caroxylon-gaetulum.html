Caroxylon gaetulum

Caroxylon gaetulum, commonly known as the African sandthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Africa, particularly in arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and yellow-orange fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has small, needle-like leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for wildlife and stabilizing sandy soils.
