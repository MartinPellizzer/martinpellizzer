Crataegus pseudaria

Crataegus pseudaria, commonly known as the American hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges, and it thrives in a variety of soil types across its native range.
