Clusia viscida

Clusia viscida, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, providing a striking visual appeal. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by yellowish-orange fruits containing numerous seeds. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions.

Clusia viscida is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
