Carex truncatigluma

Carex truncatigluma, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct triangular cross-section characteristic of sedges.

The flowering structure, known as a spikelet, consists of small, three-ranked flowers that are often wind-pollinated.
