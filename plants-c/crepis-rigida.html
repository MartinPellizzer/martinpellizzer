Crepis rigida

Crepis rigida, commonly known as rigid thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. The plant features upright stems with spiny leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in early summer. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.
