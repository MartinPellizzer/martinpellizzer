Cirsium × mitissimum

Cirsium × mitissimum, commonly known as the yellow thistle, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and striking yellow flower heads that resemble those of the common thistle. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to Europe. It has been introduced to other regions, including North America, where it can become invasive in certain environments.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a challenge for land managers.
