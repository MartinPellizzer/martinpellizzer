Crataegus korolkowii var. rubescens

Crataegus korolkowii var. rubescens, commonly known as the red-fruited hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and showy white to pink flowers that bloom in spring. The fruit is a red, fleshy pome that ripens in late summer, attracting various wildlife species.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
