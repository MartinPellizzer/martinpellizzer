Campanula persicifolia var. leiocarpa

Campanula persicifolia var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the velvet bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to lavender flowers. The leaves are lanceolate, with a soft, velvety texture that gives the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
