Cercocarpus betuloides var. macrourus

Cercocarpus betuloides var. macrourus, commonly known as the large-fruit curlleaf mountain mahogany, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its grayish bark, small ovate leaves, and distinctive, elongated fruits that are typically red or orange in color. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and xeriscaping in dryland regions.
