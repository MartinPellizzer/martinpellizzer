Camellia rotundata

Camellia rotundata, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a species native to Japan and southern Korea.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces striking, double flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant has broad, elliptical leaves that are dark green and glossy on top, with a lighter underside. Camellia rotundata thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
