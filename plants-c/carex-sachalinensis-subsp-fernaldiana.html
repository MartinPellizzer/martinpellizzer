Carex sachalinensis subsp. fernaldiana

Carex sachalinensis subsp.

fernaldiana, commonly known as Fernald's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or wetlands.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats in suitable habitats.
