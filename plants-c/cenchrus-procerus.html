Cenchrus procerus

Cenchrus procerus, commonly known as the hairy fingergrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets. It is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.

Cenchrus procerus has adapted to thrive in dry conditions, making it a resilient plant in arid environments.
