Centaurea melitensis f. apula

Centaurea melitensis f. apula, commonly known as the Apulian Centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, thriving in sunny, arid environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, spiky bracts around its flower cluster.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and drought tolerance.
