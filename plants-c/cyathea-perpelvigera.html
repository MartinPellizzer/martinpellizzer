Cyathea perpelvigera

Cyathea perpelvigera, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in optimal conditions. Its rhizome system is robust, allowing it to anchor firmly in moist, shaded environments.

Cyathea perpelvigera plays a significant ecological role in tropical forests by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
