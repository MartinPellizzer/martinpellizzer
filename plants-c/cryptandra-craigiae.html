Cryptandra craigiae

Cryptandra craigiae, commonly known as the Craig's cryptandra, is a small shrub native to Western Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, rounded seeds.

Cryptandra craigiae is often found in sandy soils and is adapted to the dry, coastal environments of its native region.
