Carex gracilis f. vaginata

Carex gracilis f. vaginata, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a variant of the slender sedge species native to wetland and marshy environments.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, cylindrical inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, making it a common sight in riparian zones. Its botanical features include small, wind-pollinated flowers arranged in a single spike.

This sedgy plant plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
