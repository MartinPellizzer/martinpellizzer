Cyrtogonium pinnatum

Cyrtogonium pinnatum, commonly known as the feather fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that resemble feathers, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests and rocky crevices. Its rhizome is creeping and produces roots that help anchor it to substrates.

Cyrtogonium pinnatum is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
