Cirsium streptacanthum

Cirsium streptacanthum, commonly known as the prickly thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. Its leaves are covered in stiff, hooked bristles, which help to deter herbivores.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
