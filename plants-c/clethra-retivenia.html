Clethra retivenia

Clethra retivenia, commonly known as the white alder, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, clustered flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Clethra retivenia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
