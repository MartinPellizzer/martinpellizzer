Carex hilairei var. glazioviana

Carex hilairei var. glazioviana, commonly known as the Glaziov's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in shaded to partially shaded environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, clustered flower heads during the spring and summer seasons.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland or boggy conditions.
