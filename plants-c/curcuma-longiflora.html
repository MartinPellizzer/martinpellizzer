Curcuma longiflora

Curcuma longiflora, commonly known as turmeric.

It is a rhizomatous herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant produces large, arrowhead-shaped leaves and tall, branched inflorescences with yellow-orange flowers. Its rhizomes are the primary part used, containing the bioactive compound curcumin.

The botanical features of Curcuma longiflora make it a valuable plant in both traditional medicine and modern herbal supplements.
