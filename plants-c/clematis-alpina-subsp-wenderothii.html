Clematis alpina subsp. wenderothii

Clematis alpina subsp.

wenderothii, commonly known as the alpine clematis, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a deciduous vine characterized by its slender stems and showy, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, ovate leaflets, and it produces distinctive, woody stems that can climb or trail along rocky surfaces.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
