Carex deweyana

Carex deweyana, commonly known as Dewey's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in association with other wetland plants.

Carex deweyana plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
