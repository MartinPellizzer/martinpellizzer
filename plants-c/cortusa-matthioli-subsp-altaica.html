Cortusa matthioli subsp. altaica

Cortusa matthioli subsp.

altaica, commonly known as the Altaian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and often branched.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
