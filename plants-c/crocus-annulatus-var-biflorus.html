Crocus annulatus var. biflorus

Crocus annulatus var. biflorus, commonly known as the two-flowered crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It produces two to three pale yellow flowers with a distinctive, narrow, petal-like tepal structure. The plant has a short, fleshy stem and is characterized by its early spring blooming habit. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a bulbous corm.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
