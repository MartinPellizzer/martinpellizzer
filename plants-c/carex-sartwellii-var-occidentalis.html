Carex sartwellii var. occidentalis

Carex sartwellii var. occidentalis, commonly known as the western sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
