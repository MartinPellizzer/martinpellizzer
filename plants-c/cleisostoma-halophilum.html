Cleisostoma halophilum

Cleisostoma halophilum, commonly known as the salt-tolerant orchid, is a species of epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is adapted to saline conditions, making it unique among its genus. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink. It requires well-draining soil and bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
