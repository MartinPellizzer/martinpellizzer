Chamaefilix pinnatifida

Chamaefilix pinnatifida, commonly known as the broad-leaved thyme, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, aromatic leaves that are densely packed on creeping stems, forming a low, mat-like growth habit. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are often used in culinary applications due to their distinctive, pungent aroma.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Rosaceae and is closely related to other members of the genus Chamaefilix.
