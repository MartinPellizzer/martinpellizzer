Caprifolium glutinosum

Caprifolium glutinosum, commonly known as sticky honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a sticky resin. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are typically yellow or white in color. Its berries, which are orange-red when ripe, are edible and contain several seeds.

Caprifolium glutinosum is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
