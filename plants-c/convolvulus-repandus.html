Convolvulus repandus

Convolvulus repandus, commonly known as the spreading bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or white, blooming from late spring to early autumn. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been introduced to other regions.

Convolvulus repandus is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential weed.
