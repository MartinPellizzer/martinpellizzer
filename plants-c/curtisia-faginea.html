Curtisia faginea

Curtisia faginea, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in racemose inflorescences, followed by small, berry-like fruits. The wood is hard and durable, making it useful for furniture and construction.
