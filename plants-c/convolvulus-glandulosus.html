Convolvulus glandulosus

Convolvulus glandulosus, commonly known as the sticky nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy, glandular stems that produce a sticky substance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, which bloom in the evening. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Convolvulus glandulosus is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain areas.
