Cuphea bracteata

Cuphea bracteata, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink, often with a contrasting throat. The plant has hairy, ovate leaves that are typically arranged opposite along the stems. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cuphea bracteata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
