Cyperus nitidulus

Cyperus nitidulus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in dense clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus nitidulus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
