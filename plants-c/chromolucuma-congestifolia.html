Chromolucuma congestifolia

Chromolucuma congestifolia, commonly known as the clustered chromolucuma, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered growth habit and strikingly colorful inflorescences that range from deep purples to vivid reds. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, enhancing its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
