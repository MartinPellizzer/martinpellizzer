Cleome margaritae

Cleome margaritae, commonly known as the silverleaf cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers bloom in summer and are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
