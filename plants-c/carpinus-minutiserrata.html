Carpinus minutiserrata

Carpinus minutiserrata, commonly known as the Japanese hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, oval leaves with fine, downward-curving teeth. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by spherical, spiky fruits. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive bark and foliage, making it a popular choice in gardens and landscapes.
