Celosia splendens

Celosia splendens, commonly known as the flame flower or cockscomb, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant, plume-like inflorescences that come in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its upright growth habit and compact size. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss.

Celosia splendens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its showy blooms and resilience in various growing conditions.
