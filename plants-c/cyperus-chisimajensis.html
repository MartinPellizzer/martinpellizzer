Cyperus chisimajensis

Cyperus chisimajensis, commonly known as the Chisimae sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to parts of Central and South America, particularly in regions with wetlands or marshy habitats. The plant typically grows in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-lobed flowers, which are often green or reddish in color.

Cyperus chisimajensis is adapted to wet environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in aquatic ecosystems.
