Croton tomentosus

Croton tomentosus, commonly known as the wooly croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, woolly foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, white to grayish hairs. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
