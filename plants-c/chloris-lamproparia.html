Chloris lamproparia

Chloris lamproparia, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, silver-tomentose leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed areas.

Chloris lamproparia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
