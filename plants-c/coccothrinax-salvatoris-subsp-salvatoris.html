Coccothrinax salvatoris subsp. salvatoris

Coccothrinax salvatoris subsp.

salvatoris, commonly known as the Cuban palm, is a species native to the island of Cuba. It is a small, slender palm with a single, unbranched trunk that typically grows to a height of about 5 to 8 meters.

The leaves are pinnately compound, with long, narrow leaflets arranged in a fan-like shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by dark blue, fleshy fruits.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests.
