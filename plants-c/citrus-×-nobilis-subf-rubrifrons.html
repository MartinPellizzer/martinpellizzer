Citrus × nobilis subf. rubrifrons

Citrus × nobilis subf.

rubrifrons, commonly known as the roughleaf kumquat, is a citrus hybrid characterized by its distinctive foliage and fruit. It is native to southern China and is known for its small, oval-shaped fruits that turn orange when ripe.

The leaves are thick, leathery, and have a rough texture, which distinguishes it from other citrus species. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ornamental addition to subtropical gardens.
