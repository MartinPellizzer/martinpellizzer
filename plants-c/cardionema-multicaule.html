Cardionema multicaule

Cardionema multicaule, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and large, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant features palmately lobed leaves and produces capsule fruits containing numerous small seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
