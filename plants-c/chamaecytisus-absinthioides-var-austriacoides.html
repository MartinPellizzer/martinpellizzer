Chamaecytisus absinthioides var. austriacoides

Chamaecytisus absinthioides var. austriacoides, commonly known as the Austrian broom, is a shrub native to the Alps.

It features slender, thornless stems that grow close to the ground, forming dense mats. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are leathery and dark green, with a slightly aromatic scent.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover.
