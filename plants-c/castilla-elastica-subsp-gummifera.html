Castilla elastica subsp. gummifera

Castilla elastica subsp.

gummifera, commonly known as the rubber tree or gummifera rubber tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is a large shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, characterized by its thick, woody stem and dense foliage.

The plant is notable for its ability to produce a milky latex, which is harvested for commercial rubber production. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a glossy appearance.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing yellowish-green blooms that attract various pollinators.
