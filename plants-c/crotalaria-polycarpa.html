Crotalaria polycarpa

Crotalaria polycarpa, commonly known as the many-fruited cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces small, clustered pods that contain several seeds, contributing to its common name. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry conditions.

Crotalaria polycarpa is also noted for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it valuable in agricultural settings.
