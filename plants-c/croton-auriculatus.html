Croton auriculatus

Croton auriculatus, commonly known as the ear-shaped croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that often have a distinctive ear-like shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Croton auriculatus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.
