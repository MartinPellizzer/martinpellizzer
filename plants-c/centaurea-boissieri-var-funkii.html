Centaurea boissieri var. funkii

Centaurea boissieri var. funkii, commonly known as the Funk's Bluet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are blue to purple in color, with ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
