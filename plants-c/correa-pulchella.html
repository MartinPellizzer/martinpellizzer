Correa pulchella

Correa pulchella, commonly known as the pink correa, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, evergreen leaves that are typically grey-green in color. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to white, often with a red or orange throat. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Correa pulchella is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
