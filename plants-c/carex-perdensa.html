Carex perdensa

Carex perdensa, commonly known as the slender sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences, which contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. Native to moist environments such as wet meadows and marshy areas, it thrives in regions with consistent moisture. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has a rhizomatous root system that aids in its spread.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
