Chrysogonum

Chrysogonum, commonly known as goldenroot, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are native to North America and are characterized by their bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs. Chrysogonum species are often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
