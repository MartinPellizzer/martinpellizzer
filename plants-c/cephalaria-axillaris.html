Cephalaria axillaris

Cephalaria axillaris, commonly known as the rock cephalaria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in dry, open habitats. The plant features slender, branching stems with small, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, adding a subtle ornamental value.

It is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
