Celtis gomphophylla

Celtis gomphophylla, commonly known as the Chinese hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The small, white flowers appear in clusters and are followed by small, round berries that turn red when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in forests and along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
