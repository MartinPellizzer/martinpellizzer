Carum acaule

Carum acaule, commonly known as slender caraway, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with slender, branched stems and small, white to greenish flowers. Its leaves are finely divided and have a subtle, spicy aroma.

The fruit, known as a schizocarp, is a key feature and is used in some traditional herbal remedies.
