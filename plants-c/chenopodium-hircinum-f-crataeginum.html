Chenopodium hircinum f. crataeginum

Chenopodium hircinum f. crataeginum, commonly known as the red-rooted goosefoot, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves.

It typically grows in moist, often disturbed soils, such as those found in wetlands, ditches, and along stream banks. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, which are followed by edible, tangy-tasting seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is native to Europe and parts of Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates.
