Calamus gogolensis

Calamus gogolensis, commonly known as Gogol's climbing reed, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional basketry and crafts. Its inflorescence consists of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters.

Calamus gogolensis is valued not only for its aesthetic appeal but also for its practical uses in local cultures.
