Cerasus elliptica

Cerasus elliptica, commonly known as the elliptical cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its elliptical-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible berries that are typically red or black when mature, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary practices. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

Cerasus elliptica is also valued for its role in ecological systems, providing food for birds and other wildlife.
