Cynanchum gonoloboides

Cynanchum gonoloboides, commonly known as the Chinese milkweed vine, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, climbing stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive, milky sap that contains toxic compounds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
