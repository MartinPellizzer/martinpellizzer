Cinnamomum parthenoxylon

Cinnamomum parthenoxylon, commonly known as the wild cinnamon tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic bark and leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark blue berries. Its bark is used in traditional medicine and for its aromatic properties.

The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
