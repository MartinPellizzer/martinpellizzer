Chamaesyce deltoidea subsp. pinetorum

Chamaesyce deltoidea subsp.

pinetorum, commonly known as the Florida sand verbena, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States. It features small, pale green to yellowish leaves that are typically elliptical or deltoid in shape.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in open areas such as dunes and pine flatwoods.

Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand coastal and inland environmental conditions.
