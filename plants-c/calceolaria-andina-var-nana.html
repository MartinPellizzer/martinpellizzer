Calceolaria andina var. nana

Calceolaria andina var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf Andean slipper orchid, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to the high Andes of South America.

It features delicate, spurred flowers that resemble small slippers, typically in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers well-drained, acidic soil. It has hairy stems and leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique floral display.
