Conophytum bilobum subsp. altum

Conophytum bilobum subsp.

altum, commonly known as the tall two-lobed conophytum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its compact, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a whitish powder, which helps reduce water loss.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the winter months, typically in July and August. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
