Cardamine breweri

Cardamine breweri, commonly known as Brewer's watercress, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring. Its flowers are clustered in racemes, and the plant is known for its peppery, aromatic foliage.

Cardamine breweri is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants.
