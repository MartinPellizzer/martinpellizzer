Calluna vulgaris f. cuprea

Calluna vulgaris f. cuprea, commonly known as the purple heather, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to western Europe.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep purple. The leaves are narrow, leathery, and evergreen, providing year-round foliage. This variety thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
