Celtis ehrenbergiana var. discolor

Celtis ehrenbergiana var. discolor, commonly known as the variegated boxleaf elm, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Central and South America.

It features small, elongated leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and white or silver on the underside, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, round fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

The species is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to urban environments.
