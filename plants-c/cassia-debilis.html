Cassia debilis

Cassia debilis, commonly known as the sicklepod cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is often found in sandy or saline soils. Its flowers are yellow and clustered, while the leaves are trifoliate with a distinctive sickle shape.

Cassia debilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
