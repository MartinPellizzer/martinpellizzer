Commelina umbellata

Commelina umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella leaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface, and its inflorescence consists of a cluster of small, showy flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Commelina umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators.
