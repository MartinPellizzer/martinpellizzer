Chaenorhinum origanifolium var. maroccanum

Chaenorhinum origanifolium var. maroccanum, commonly known as the Moroccan oregano flower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Morocco.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and vibrant purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are narrow, aromatic, and resemble those of oregano, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
