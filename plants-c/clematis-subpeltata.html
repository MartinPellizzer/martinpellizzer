Clematis subpeltata

Clematis subpeltata, commonly known as the Japanese Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to East Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Clematis subpeltata is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
