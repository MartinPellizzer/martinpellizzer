Carex meadii var. bebbii

Carex meadii var. bebbii, commonly known as Bebb's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Bebb's sedge has small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
