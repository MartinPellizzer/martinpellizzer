Canarium sylvestre

Canarium sylvestre, commonly known as wild candlenut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The tree produces oval, woody fruits that contain a single seed, which is encased in a hard, oily shell. The oil from its seeds has been traditionally used for making candles and as a source of biodiesel.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
