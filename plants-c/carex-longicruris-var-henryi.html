Carex longicruris var. henryi

Carex longicruris var. henryi, commonly known as Henry's longstalk sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping for its attractive, dense foliage.
