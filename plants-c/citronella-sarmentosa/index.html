Citronella Sarmentosa

Citronella grass, scientifically known as Citronella sarmentosa, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It is widely recognized for its strong citrus scent and its use in natural pest control. The plant's leaves contain citral, a compound that repels mosquitoes and other insects. Often grown in gardens or as a potted plant, it adds a vibrant green texture to landscapes. Despite its ornamental appeal, it also holds cultural and medicinal significance in many regions.
