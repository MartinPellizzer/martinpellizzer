Cissus blanchetiana

Cissus blanchetiana, commonly known as the African stone plant, is a climbing vine native to southern Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are usually pale green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer. Its stems are succulent and can store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
