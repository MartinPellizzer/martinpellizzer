Coleus achankoviliensis

Coleus achankoviliensis, commonly known as the Achan Kovil Coleus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and display a wide range of colors, including red, purple, and green.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
