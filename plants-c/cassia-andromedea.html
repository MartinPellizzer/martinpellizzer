Cassia andromedea

Cassia andromedea, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are rich in nectar and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.

Cassia andromedea is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping and traditional medicine.
