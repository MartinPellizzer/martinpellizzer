Clidemia capitellata var. neglecta

Clidemia capitellata var. neglecta, commonly known as the stinging nettle plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, stinging hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
