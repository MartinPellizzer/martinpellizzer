Commiphora acutidens

Commiphora acutidens, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and dense, glossy green leaves. The plant produces aromatic gum resins, which have been used traditionally for their medicinal and aromatic properties. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dry woodlands and coastal areas.

Its unique botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and commercial purposes.
