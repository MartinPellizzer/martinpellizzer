Calliandra longipinna

Calliandra longipinna, commonly known as the long-needle calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that consist of numerous small, bright red or pink flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous leaflets and produces small, slender pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun for optimal growth.

Calliandra longipinna is often used in ornamental gardening and as a source of forage for livestock.
