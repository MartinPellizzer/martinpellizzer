Correa speciosa var. hillii

Correa speciosa var. hillii, commonly known as the Hill's gloryflower, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically grey-green in color, with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy display.
