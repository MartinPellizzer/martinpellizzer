Caladenia barbarella

Caladenia barbarella, commonly known as the scarlet spider-orchid, is a striking orchid native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, spider-like appearance. The plant produces a single, showy flower with a bright red or orange hue, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is typically found in open habitats such as woodlands and heathlands.

Caladenia barbarella is a perennial herb that relies on specific mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake, highlighting its ecological significance.
