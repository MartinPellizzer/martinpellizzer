Claytonia pumila

Claytonia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf spring beauty, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It features small, rosette-forming leaves that are typically covered in a fine, powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and native garden landscapes.
