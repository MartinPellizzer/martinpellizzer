Corytholoma bulbosum

Corytholoma bulbosum, commonly known as the bulbous bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bulbous underground storage organ, which helps it survive in arid conditions. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in Mediterranean climates.
