Carex stricta f. emoryi

Carex stricta f. emoryi, commonly known as the Emory's tussock sedge, is a variant of the tussock sedge, characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and clustered growth habit.

It is a perennial plant native to wetland and bog environments in North America. The plant features narrow, cylindrical leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spikelets.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
