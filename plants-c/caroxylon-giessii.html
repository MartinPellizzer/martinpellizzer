Caroxylon giessii

Caroxylon giessii, commonly known as the giant reed or giant cane, is a tall, perennial grass native to parts of South America.

It can grow up to 6 meters in height, featuring long, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hair. The plant produces small, pale green flowers in dense, cylindrical clusters, which are borne on tall, slender stems. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread aggressively, making it a common weed in wetland areas.

Caroxylon giessii is adapted to moist environments and is often found in marshes, riverbanks, and other aquatic habitats.
