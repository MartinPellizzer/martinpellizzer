Carex cheniana

Carex cheniana, commonly known as the Chen's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist or wet environments, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its leaves are grass-like, cylindrical, and usually have a rough texture.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often enclosed in bracts, typical of the Cyperaceae family.
