Cardamine oligosperma

Cardamine oligosperma, commonly known as narrowleaf watercress, is a small flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, alternate leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical seed pods. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland floors.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
