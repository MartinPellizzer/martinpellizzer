Claytonia australasica var. sessiliflora

Claytonia australasica var. sessiliflora, commonly known as the New Zealand whiteface, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It features a rosette of broad, glossy, waxy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the base. The plant produces small, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate blooms.
