Camellia pubisepala

Camellia pubisepala, commonly known as the pubescent-sepal camellia, is a species native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds. Camellia pubisepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a versatile addition to temperate climates.
