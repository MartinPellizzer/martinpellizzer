Campanula scheuchzeri var. carnica

Campanula scheuchzeri var. carnica, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically basal, rosette-forming, and have a rough, hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
