Ceratostylis anceps

Ceratostylis anceps, commonly known as the narrow-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its flowers are usually greenish-white with a distinctive, forward-curving lip.

Ceratostylis anceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialty gardens.
