Cynosurus aurasiacus

Cynosurus aurasiacus, commonly known as the European tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks.

Cynosurus aurasiacus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
