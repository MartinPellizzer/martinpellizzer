Cassia marimari

Cassia marimari, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in pendulous clusters, typically during the rainy season. The plant produces long, narrow, dark green leaves and has a woody, branching stem. Cassia marimari is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its flowers are rich in nectar, making it an important food source for pollinators like bees and birds.
