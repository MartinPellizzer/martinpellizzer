Cassia carringtoniana

Cassia carringtoniana, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Cassia carringtoniana is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
