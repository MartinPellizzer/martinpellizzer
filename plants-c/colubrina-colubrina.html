Colubrina colubrina

Colubrina colubrina, commonly known as the Florida colubrina, is a shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces blue or purple berries that are a food source for various birds and small mammals. It thrives in sandy or loamy soils and is often found in coastal hammocks and wetlands.

Colubrina colubrina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
