Centaurea immanuelis-loewii var. grbavacensis

Centaurea immanuelis-loewii var. grbavacensis, commonly known as the Grbavac centaury, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its spiky, blue to purple flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
