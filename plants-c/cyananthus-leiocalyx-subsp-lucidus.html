Cyananthus leiocalyx subsp. lucidus

Cyananthus leiocalyx subsp.

lucidus, commonly known as the glossy bluebell, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in spring, often in clusters at the ends of its stems.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This subspecies is often found in shaded, moist habitats such as forests and rocky slopes.

It is valued in horticulture for its beauty and adaptability to a range of soil conditions.
