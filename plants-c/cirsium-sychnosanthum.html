Cirsium sychnosanthum

Cirsium sychnosanthum, commonly known as the clustered thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of flowers, which are surrounded by bracts that form a prickly involucrum.

Cirsium sychnosanthum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
