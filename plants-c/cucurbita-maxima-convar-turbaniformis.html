Cucurbita maxima convar. turbaniformis

Cucurbita maxima convar. turbaniformis, commonly known as the turban squash, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, turban-like shape.

It is native to Central and South America and is cultivated for its edible, yellow to orange-colored fruits. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers, typical of the Cucurbita genus. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and is often used in various culinary preparations.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
