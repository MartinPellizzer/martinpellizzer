Calamagrostis patula

Calamagrostis patula, commonly known as the blue bluestem, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking blue-gray foliage that turns coppery in the fall. The plant produces panicles of small, fluffy flowers in late summer, which are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is commonly found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis patula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for wildlife.
