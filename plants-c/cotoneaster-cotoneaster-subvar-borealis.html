Cotoneaster cotoneaster subvar. borealis

Cotoneaster cotoneaster subvar. borealis, commonly known as the Alaska cotoneaster, is a shrub native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments. The plant features dense, evergreen foliage with small, leathery leaves that provide year-round structure. It produces clusters of white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that attract wildlife.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in its native range.
