Chrysanthemum rotundifolium

Chrysanthemum rotundifolium, commonly known as the globe chrysanthemum, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features rounded, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fluffy, spherical flower heads composed of numerous small, white to pale yellow florets. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for ornamental gardens and pollinator habitats.
