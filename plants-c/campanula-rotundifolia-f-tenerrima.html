Campanula rotundifolia f. tenerrima

Campanula rotundifolia f. tenerrima, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It features rounded, broad leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand harsh mountain conditions.
