Citrus × aurantium subf. hakunikuyu

Citrus × aurantium subf.

hakunikuyu, commonly known as the Hakunikuyu mandarin, is a hybrid citrus plant characterized by its small, round fruits with a thin, smooth peel. The fruit typically has a sweet and slightly tangy flavor, making it popular in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in warm, subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of its distinctive fruit.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both horticulture and traditional medicine.
