Colpodium villosum

Colpodium villosum, commonly known as the hairy colpodium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, erect stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it its common name. Its small, white or pale green flowers are arranged in loose clusters and bloom in early summer.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
