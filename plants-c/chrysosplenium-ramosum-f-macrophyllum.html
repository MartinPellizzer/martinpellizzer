Chrysosplenium ramosum f. macrophyllum

Chrysosplenium ramosum f. macrophyllum, commonly known as the large-leaved chrysosplenium, is a variant of the chrysosplenium genus characterized by its notably larger leaves compared to the typical form.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Saxifragaceae family, native to certain regions of Asia. The plant features alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy on the underside, and it produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in clusters.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.
