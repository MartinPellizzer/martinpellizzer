Centaurea golestanica

Centaurea golestanica, commonly known as Golestan thistle, is a flowering plant native to Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple-blue flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea golestanica is also noted for its ornamental value and potential medicinal uses in traditional herbal practices.
