Corallorhiza maculata f. flavida

Corallorhiza maculata f. flavida, commonly known as the spotted coralroot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is a root-parasitic plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for its nutrients. The plant is characterized by its bright yellow to orange inflorescence, which consists of small, tubular flowers. It typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and is often found in association with other orchids.

This orchid is native to eastern North America and is considered a rare and endangered species in many parts of its range.
