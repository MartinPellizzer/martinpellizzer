Cheirostylis acuminata

Cheirostylis acuminata, commonly known as the acuminated cheirostylis, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces delicate, fragrant flowers with narrow, elongated petals and a distinctive, spurred lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is typically found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
