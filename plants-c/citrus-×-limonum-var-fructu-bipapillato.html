Citrus × limonum var. fructu-bipapillato

Citrus × limonum var. fructu-bipapillato, commonly known as the double-papillate lemon, is a unique citrus hybrid characterized by its distinctive fruit morphology.

The fruit features two prominent papillae, or nipple-like protrusions, on the surface, which set it apart from typical lemons. This variety is primarily cultivated for its aromatic peel and juice, which are used in culinary and aromatic applications. The plant exhibits typical citrus growth habits, with evergreen leaves and a bushy structure.

Its botanical significance lies in its morphological variation, offering insights into citrus evolution and hybridization.
