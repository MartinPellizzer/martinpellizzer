Chaetoseris rhombiformis

Chaetoseris rhombiformis, commonly known as the rhomboid dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa, particularly in countries like Namibia and South Africa. The plant is characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and yellow composite flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal and inland desert environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.
