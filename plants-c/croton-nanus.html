Croton nanus

Croton nanus, commonly known as the dwarf croton, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful leaves that can range from green to red, orange, and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by woody capsules containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.

Croton nanus prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
