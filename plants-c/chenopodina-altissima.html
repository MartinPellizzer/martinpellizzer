Chenopodina altissima

Chenopodina altissima, commonly known as the tall goosefoot, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in sagebrush habitats.

Chenopodina altissima is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native range.
