Coussapoa villosa subsp. villosa

Coussapoa villosa subsp.

villosa, commonly known as the woolly coussapoa, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically elliptical and covered in fine, soft hairs.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
