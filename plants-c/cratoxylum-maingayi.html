Cratoxylum maingayi

Cratoxylum maingayi, commonly known as the Malayan wild pepper, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree belonging to the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia, Indonesia, and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers. Its fruits are small, round, and turn from green to red when mature, often used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has been utilized in local herbal remedies for various health purposes.
