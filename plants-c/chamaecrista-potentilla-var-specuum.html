Chamaecrista potentilla var. specuum

Chamaecrista potentilla var. specuum, commonly known as the silverleaf burr legume, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its slender, often branched stems and small, opposite leaves that are typically silver or gray-green in color. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiny pods. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
