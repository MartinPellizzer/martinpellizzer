Canavalia mattogrossensis

Canavalia mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a climbing legume native to South America.

It features compound leaves with trifoliate or pinnate arrangements and produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain several seeds. The plant is characterized by its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable for soil enrichment. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
