Croton chamanus

Croton chamanus, commonly known as the sacred croton, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves that vary in shape and texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by unique, spiky fruit structures. Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Due to its ornamental value and cultural significance, it is cultivated in many tropical gardens.
