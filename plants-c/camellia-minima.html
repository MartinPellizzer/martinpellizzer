Camellia minima

Camellia minima, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant produces elliptical, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy. Camellia minima thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to bloom in winter, adding color to gardens during the colder months.
