Carex margaritifera

Carex margaritifera, commonly known as the pearl sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marsh environments in North America.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant has narrow, cylindrical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in riparian zones and along the edges of ponds and lakes.

Carex margaritifera plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
