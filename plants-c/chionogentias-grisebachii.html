Chionogentias grisebachii

Chionogentias grisebachii, commonly known as the silver-haired gentian, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
