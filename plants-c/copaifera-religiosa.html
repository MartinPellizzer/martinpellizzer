Copaifera religiosa

Copaifera religiosa, commonly known as the sacred copaiba, is a species of tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and produces a fragrant, amber-colored resin that has been used traditionally for its medicinal properties. The plant belongs to the family Leguminosae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates. Its bark and resin are often harvested for their therapeutic and aromatic qualities.

The tree plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, supporting a diverse array of flora and fauna.
