Clerodendrum leandrii

Clerodendrum leandrii, commonly known as the Red Trumpet, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Lamiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like mint and basil. The plant is characterized by its striking red, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Clerodendrum leandrii is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its potential medicinal properties.
