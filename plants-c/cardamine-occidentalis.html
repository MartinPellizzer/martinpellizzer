Cardamine occidentalis

Cardamine occidentalis, commonly known as western watercress, is a member of the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and wet meadows. The plant has alternate, trifoliate leaves that are often submerged or partially submerged in water. Its small white or pale yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom in early spring.

The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in nutrient-rich, aquatic or semi-aquatic habitats.
