Cuphea tubiflora

Cuphea tubiflora, commonly known as the woolly cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has hairy, fuzzy leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive, soft appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cuphea tubiflora is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to add color to tropical and subtropical environments.
