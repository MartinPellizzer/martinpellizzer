Cerastium illyricum subsp. crinitum

Cerastium illyricum subsp.

crinitum, commonly known as the hairy mouse-ear chickweed, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features small, white flowers with five petals and produces fluffy, hairy seed heads that give it its common name.

The plant has basal leaves that are spoon-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as cliffs and slopes.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and resilient member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
