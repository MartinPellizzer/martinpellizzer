Crataegus berberifolia var. engelmannii

Crataegus berberifolia var. engelmannii, commonly known as Engelmann’s hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. Its leaves are typically oval with serrated edges and can vary in color from green to reddish in autumn.

Engelmann’s hawthorn thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands or along streambanks.
