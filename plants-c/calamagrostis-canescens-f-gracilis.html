Calamagrostis canescens f. gracilis

Calamagrostis canescens f. gracilis, commonly known as silver bluestem grass, is a compact, ornamental grass species native to North America.

It features narrow, silver-blue foliage that turns coppery in the fall, creating a striking visual effect in gardens. The plant has tufted inflorescences that appear in late summer, adding a soft, fluffy texture to the landscape. It prefers full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and erosion control.
