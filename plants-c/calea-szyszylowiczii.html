Calea szyszylowiczii

Calea szyszylowiczii, commonly known as the silver hairplant, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its unique, silver-white, hair-like structures that cover its leaves and stems, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in both gardens and botanical collections.
