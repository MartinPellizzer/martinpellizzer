Carex glauca var. praetutiana

Carex glauca var. praetutiana, commonly known as the blue sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to greenish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and shaded woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting wildlife.
