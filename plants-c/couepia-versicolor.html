Couepia versicolor

Couepia versicolor, commonly known as the variable leaf vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its strikingly variable leaf colors, which can range from green to reddish-brown, depending on age and environmental conditions. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
