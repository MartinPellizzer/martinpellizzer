Cousinia sharifii

Cousinia sharifii, commonly known as the Kashmiri cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showing adaptability to cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are usually basal, forming a rosette, and the stems are often hairy.

Cousinia sharifii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
