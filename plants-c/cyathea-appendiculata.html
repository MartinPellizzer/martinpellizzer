Cyathea appendiculata

Cyathea appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical rainforests. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it plays an important role in forest ecosystems.

The fronds are also valued for their aesthetic appeal in horticultural settings.
