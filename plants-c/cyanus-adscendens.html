Cyanus adscendens

Cyanus adscendens, commonly known as the blueweed or blue bonnet, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, sky-blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Cyanus adscendens is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
