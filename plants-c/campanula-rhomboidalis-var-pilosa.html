Campanula rhomboidalis var. pilosa

Campanula rhomboidalis var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features hairy, rhomboidal leaves that are typically gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
