Chrysochamela noeana

Chrysochamela noeana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy and elliptical, leathery leaves. Its bark is smooth and greyish-brown, often exuding a resin when damaged.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and urban landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
