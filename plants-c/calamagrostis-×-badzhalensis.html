Calamagrostis × badzhalensis

Calamagrostis × badzhalensis, commonly known as the silvergrass or silver reed, is a hybrid grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and distinctive silvery-blue foliage that adds ornamental value to gardens. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in wetland environments.

Calamagrostis × badzhalensis is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
