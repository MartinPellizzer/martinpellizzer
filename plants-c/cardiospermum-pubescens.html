Cardiospermum pubescens

Cardiospermum pubescens, commonly known as the hairy heart vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces distinctive seed pods that split open to reveal bright red, fleshy seeds enclosed in a papery husk. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
