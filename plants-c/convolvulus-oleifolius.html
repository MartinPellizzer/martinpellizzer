Convolvulus oleifolius

Convolvulus oleifolius, commonly known as the Egyptian bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found growing along roadsides and in disturbed soils. It is known for its ability to thrive in dry conditions and is sometimes considered a weed.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
