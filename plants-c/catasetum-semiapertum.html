Catasetum semiapertum

Catasetum semiapertum, commonly known as the semi-open catasetum, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous inflorescences bearing showy, fragrant flowers with a unique structure. The plant produces a specialized pollination mechanism involving a pollinium that adheres to the body of its insect pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

The species is prized by orchid enthusiasts for its striking appearance and fascinating biological adaptations.
