Coleus blumei var. murrayi

Coleus blumei var. murrayi, commonly known as the 'Murray's Coleus', is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy bracts that come in a variety of colors, including red, orange, and purple. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage.

Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing in clusters at the base of the plant.
