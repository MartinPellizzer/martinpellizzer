Cheirinia oblanceolata

Cheirinia oblanceolata, commonly known as the broadleaf sandwort, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically forms dense mats of foliage. The plant has small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces clusters of tiny, white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer.

Cheirinia oblanceolata is often found in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.
