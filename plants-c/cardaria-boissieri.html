Cardaria boissieri

Cardaria boissieri, commonly known as the mountain everlasting, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and linear leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in poor soil, making it a resilient and hardy species.
