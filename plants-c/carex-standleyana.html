Carex standleyana

Carex standleyana, commonly known as Standley's sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes. Native to the highlands of Mexico, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun. The plant has narrow, cylindrical leaves and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
