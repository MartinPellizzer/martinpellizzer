Chuquiraga alpina

Chuquiraga alpina, commonly known as the alpine chuquiraga, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, high-altitude environments, showcasing adaptations to cold and arid conditions. Its yellow flowers are often surrounded by leafy bracts, and it produces small, achene-like seeds.

Chuquiraga alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
