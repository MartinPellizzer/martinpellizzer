Cyathea kingii

Cyathea kingii, commonly known as the King's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can live for several decades, making it a long-lived species. Its fronds are evergreen and can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cyathea kingii plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
