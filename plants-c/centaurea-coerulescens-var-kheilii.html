Centaurea coerulescens var. kheilii

Centaurea coerulescens var. kheilii, commonly known as the Kheil's blueweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in summer and attract various pollinators. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a rough texture, and the stems are usually hairy.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
