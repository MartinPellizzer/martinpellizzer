Colubrina guineensis

Colubrina guineensis, commonly known as the African beautyberry, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Following flowering, it develops bright red, fleshy berries that are a striking feature of the plant.

These berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various bird species.
