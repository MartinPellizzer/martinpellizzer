Crateva tapia f. palmeri

Crateva tapia f. palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's bladder nut, is a variant of the bladder nut plant native to parts of Central and South America.

It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and distinctive, inflated seed pods. The plant produces yellow flowers that are followed by the characteristic bladder-like fruits, which contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, membranous sac. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental features and has been cultivated for both horticultural and medicinal purposes.
