Cannomois taylorii

Cannomois taylorii, commonly known as Taylor's canna, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cannaceae family and is characterized by its large, showy leaves and vibrant, orange-red flowers. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its foliage is broad and lance-shaped, while its inflorescence consists of three to five bracts that enclose the actual flowers.

Cannomois taylorii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
