Carduus nigrescens var. spinigerus

Carduus nigrescens var. spinigerus, commonly known as the hairy thistle, is a perennial plant characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in dry, disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant has a robust taproot system, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a rough texture, and the flowers are typically arranged in a single, showy head.

The species is known for its ability to spread through both seeds and vegetative reproduction.
