Cassia bradei

Cassia bradei, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant is known for its hard, durable wood, which has been historically valued for its use in furniture and carving. Cassia bradei thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry woodland and savanna habitats.

Its seeds are encased in pods and can remain viable in the soil for several years.
