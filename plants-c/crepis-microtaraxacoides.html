Crepis microtaraxacoides

Crepis microtaraxacoides, commonly known as the small dandelion, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with a basal rosette of leaves and a single, slender stem. The plant produces small, yellow composite flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in grassy areas and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural and natural habitats.
