Colchicum procurrens

Colchicum procurrens, commonly known as the creeping colchicum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and grassy slopes. The plant features a short, rhizomatous stem with broad, lance-shaped leaves that form a basal rosette. Its most distinctive feature is its showy, violet to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it important to handle with care.
