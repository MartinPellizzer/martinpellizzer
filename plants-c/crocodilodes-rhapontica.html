Crocodilodes rhapontica

Crocodilodes rhapontica, commonly known as the Siberian ginseng, is a perennial herb native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in late summer, followed by distinctive, winged fruits that contain seeds. It is known for its adaptability to harsh environments and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and vegetables.
