Calacinum chilense

Calacinum chilense, commonly known as the Chilean calacina, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of Chile.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy leaves that store water. It produces small, edible fruits that are sometimes used in local cuisine.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
