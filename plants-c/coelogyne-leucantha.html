Coelogyne leucantha

Coelogyne leucantha, commonly known as the white-lipped orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, pendant-like inflorescence and striking white flowers with a distinctive yellow lip. The plant typically grows on trees or rocks in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-shaped, often forming a rosette at the base.

Coelogyne leucantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated for both horticultural and conservation purposes.
