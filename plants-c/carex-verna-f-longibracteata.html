Carex verna f. longibracteata

Carex verna f. longibracteata, commonly known as the long bract sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is distinguished by its elongated bracts that enclose the flowers, giving it its specific epithet.
