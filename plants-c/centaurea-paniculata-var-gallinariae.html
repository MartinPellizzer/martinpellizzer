Centaurea paniculata var. gallinariae

Centaurea paniculata var. gallinariae, commonly known as the Gallinariae Centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and striking blue to purple flower heads. The plant produces small, tubular florets that are typically arranged in dense, panicle-like clusters. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and for its ecological benefits to pollinators.
