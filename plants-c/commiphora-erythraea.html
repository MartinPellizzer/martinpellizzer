Commiphora erythraea

Commiphora erythraea, commonly known as the myrrh tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Arabian Peninsula and parts of East Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and dense, leathery leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces a resinous sap that is harvested as myrrh, a fragrant substance used in traditional medicine and religious ceremonies. It thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, often growing in rocky soils.

The tree is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used for centuries in various cultural and medicinal practices.
