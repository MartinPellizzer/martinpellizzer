Castanopsis fargesii

Castanopsis fargesii, commonly known as the Farges' tanoak, is a deciduous evergreen tree native to China.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife. It typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to temperate climates with well-drained soils.

Castanopsis fargesii is valued for its hard wood and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
