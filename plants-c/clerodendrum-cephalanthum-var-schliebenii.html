Clerodendrum cephalanthum var. schliebenii

Clerodendrum cephalanthum var. schliebenii, commonly known as the Schlieben's flame tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly pubescent surface. Its flowers are clustered in panicles and are typically reddish-orange, giving it a striking appearance.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its propagation in the wild.
