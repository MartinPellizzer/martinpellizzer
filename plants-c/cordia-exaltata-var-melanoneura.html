Cordia exaltata var. melanoneura

Cordia exaltata var. melanoneura, commonly known as the black-veined cordia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, ovate leaves with dark green upper surfaces and prominent, dark veins. The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
