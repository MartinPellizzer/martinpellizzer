Calliandra umbrosa subsp. griffithii

Calliandra umbrosa subsp.

griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's calliandra, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and clusters of small, pink to reddish flowers.

The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight. Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and showy blooms, making it a popular choice in gardens.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
