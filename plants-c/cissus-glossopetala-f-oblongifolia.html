Cissus glossopetala f. oblongifolia

Cissus glossopetala f. oblongifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed devil's ivy, is a tropical climbing plant characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves.

The leaves are typically ovate to oblong in shape, with a waxy surface that reflects light, aiding in photosynthesis. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, spherical berries. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
