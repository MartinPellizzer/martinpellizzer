Crepidium latilabre

Crepidium latilabre, commonly known as the broad-lipped orchid, is a terrestrial orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers with a distinctive broad lip. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its delicate appearance and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
