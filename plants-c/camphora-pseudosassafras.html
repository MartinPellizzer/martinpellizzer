Camphora pseudosassafras

Camphora pseudosassafras, commonly known as the false sassafras, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like cinnamon and avocado. The plant is characterized by its opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are often reddish underneath. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, followed by oval, dark blue berries.

The bark and leaves contain volatile oils, giving the plant its aromatic properties and making it valuable in traditional medicine and perfumery.
