Chloris pulchra

Chloris pulchra, commonly known as the pretty grass or silky grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, silky texture and delicate, light green foliage. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, which are typical of the Poaceae family.

Chloris pulchra is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
