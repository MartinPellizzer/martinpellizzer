Cestrum baenitzii var. angustifolium

Cestrum baenitzii var. angustifolium, commonly known as the narrow-leaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically red when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal fragrance.

However, it contains toxic compounds and should be handled with care.
