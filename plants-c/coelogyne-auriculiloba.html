Coelogyne auriculiloba

Coelogyne auriculiloba, commonly known as the golden ear orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow and green foliage and its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its flowers are typically yellow with red or orange markings, resembling ears, hence its common name.

Coelogyne auriculiloba thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, loose soil for optimal growth.
