Convallaria japonica var. major

Convallaria japonica var. major, commonly known as Japanese lily of the valley, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Convallariaceae and is characterized by its clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces red berries that contain seeds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
