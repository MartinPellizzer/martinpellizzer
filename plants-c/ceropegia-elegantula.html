Ceropegia elegantula

Ceropegia elegantula, commonly known as the silver string plant, is a succulent species native to South Africa.

It features trailing stems with small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy, silver-white coating. The plant produces delicate, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which are pollinated by specific insects. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and hanging baskets.
