Cytisus proliferus var. canariae

Cytisus proliferus var. canariae, commonly known as the Canary Island broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its slender, often spiny stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has pinnately compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
