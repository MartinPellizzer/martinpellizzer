Cordula virens

Cordula virens, commonly known as the green cordula, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, herbaceous stems and small, clustered greenish-yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.
