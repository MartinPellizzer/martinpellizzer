Caladenia petrensis

Caladenia petrensis, commonly known as the Western Plains Spider-orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the western regions of New South Wales, Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, spider-like appearance with a single, erect stem and a single, intricately patterned flower. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing a vivid pink or purple flower with a distinctive, narrow lip. It grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in open woodlands or grasslands.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered vulnerable to environmental changes and habitat loss.
