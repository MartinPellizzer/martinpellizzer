Croton pohlianus var. semiglabratus

Croton pohlianus var. semiglabratus, commonly known as the smooth-leaved croton, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
