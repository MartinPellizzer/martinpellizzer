Croton sanguifluus

Croton sanguifluus, commonly known as the blood-leaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage with deep green and red veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
