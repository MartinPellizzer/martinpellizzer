Cyanus achtarovii

Cyanus achtarovii, commonly known as the Achtarovii blueweed, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, which helps reduce water loss.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological role and potential medicinal properties.
