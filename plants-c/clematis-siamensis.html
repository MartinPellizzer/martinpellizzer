Clematis siamensis

Clematis siamensis, commonly known as the Siam clematis, is a climbing vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features showy, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, blooming in spring. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its attractive blooms and vigorous growth, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
