Chamaerhodos grandiflora

Chamaerhodos grandiflora, commonly known as the redroot geranium, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It features upright stems that produce showy, pink to red flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately lobed leaves with a distinctive reddish hue on the underside. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
