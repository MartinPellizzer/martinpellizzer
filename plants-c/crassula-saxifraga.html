Crassula saxifraga

Crassula saxifraga, commonly known as the stonecrop or fairy fan, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, thick leaves arranged in a rosette pattern, which help it store water and survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact size and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
