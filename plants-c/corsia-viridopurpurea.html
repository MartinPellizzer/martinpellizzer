Corsia viridopurpurea

Corsia viridopurpurea, commonly known as the green-purple corsia, is a rare flowering plant characterized by its striking foliage.

Its leaves are a unique blend of green and purple hues, often with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is native to specific regions in South America.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species in botanical gardens.
