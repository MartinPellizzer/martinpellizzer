Carex membranopacta

Carex membranopacta, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of sedge native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream edges. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and stabilize soil in aquatic environments.

Carex membranopacta plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
