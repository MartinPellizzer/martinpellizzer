Campanula afra subsp. hypocrateriformis

Campanula afra subsp.

hypocrateriformis, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant has hairy, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
