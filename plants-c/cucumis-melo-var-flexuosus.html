Cucumis melo var. flexuosus

Cucumis melo var. flexuosus, commonly known as the horned melon, is a unique variant of the melon family characterized by its thick, spiky rind.

The plant produces large, spherical fruits with a leathery exterior that resembles a hornet's nest, giving it its common name. Botanically, it belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to southern Africa. The fruit contains a sweet, juicy pulp with numerous small, edible seeds.

It is valued for its distinctive appearance and culinary use in various regional cuisines.
