Clematis montana var. chumbica

Clematis montana var. chumbica, commonly known as the Andean Clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the Andes region of South America.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, often with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue and purple, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires support for its long, flexible stems.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
