Chamaecyparis lawsoniana pendula-aurea

Chamaecyparis lawsoniana pendula-aurea, commonly known as the golden thread cedar, is a cultivar of the Lawson's cypress.

It is characterized by its weeping growth habit and golden-yellow foliage that provides a striking visual contrast. The plant has a slow growth rate and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its foliage remains vibrant throughout the growing season, adding year-round interest to the landscape.
