Carpoxis inermis

Carpoxis inermis, commonly known as the smooth carpoxis, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Carpoxis inermis thrives in moist, sunny to partially shaded environments, often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insects and birds.
