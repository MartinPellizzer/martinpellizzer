Claoxylon molleri

Claoxylon molleri, commonly known as the yellow-wood tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, golden-yellow bark that peels off in thin layers. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a waxy texture. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by bright red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
