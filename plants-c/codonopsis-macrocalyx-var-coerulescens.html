Codonopsis macrocalyx var. coerulescens

Codonopsis macrocalyx var. coerulescens, commonly known as the blue-flowered codonopsis, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves and produces bell-shaped flowers that are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
