Calathea pachystachya

Calathea pachystachya, commonly known as the Black Velvet Plant, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Marantaceae family and is characterized by its striking, dark green, velvety leaves with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that appear during the warmer months. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires high humidity and well-draining soil.

Calathea pachystachya is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
