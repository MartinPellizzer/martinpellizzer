Cyperus flavus var. argentinus

Cyperus flavus var. argentinus, commonly known as the Argentine flatsedge, is a species of sedge native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in shallow water or moist soil.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
