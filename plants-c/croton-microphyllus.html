Croton microphyllus

Croton microphyllus, commonly known as the small-leaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, ovate leaves that are typically green with reddish veins. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for indoor gardening and landscape design.
