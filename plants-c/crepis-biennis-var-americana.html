Crepis biennis var. americana

Crepis biennis var. americana, commonly known as the American hoary alyssum, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as roadsides and prairies, and it produces small, white to pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a grayish appearance.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support pollinators.
