Camellia rusticana var. plena

Camellia rusticana var. plena, commonly known as the double-flowered camellia, is a unique variant of the camellia plant native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, double blooms that consist of numerous petals, giving it a full, almost peony-like appearance. The plant is evergreen, with dark green, leathery leaves that provide a striking contrast to its showy flowers. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its blooming period typically occurs in late winter to early spring, adding early color to the landscape.
