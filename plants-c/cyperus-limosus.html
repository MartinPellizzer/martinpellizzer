Cyperus limosus

Cyperus limosus, commonly known as bog sedgewort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, forming dense clusters with slender, triangular stems. Its leaves are grass-like, and it produces small, three-lobed flowers in spike-like inflorescences.

Cyperus limosus is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and lakes.
