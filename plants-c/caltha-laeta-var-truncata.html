Caltha laeta var. truncata

Caltha laeta var. truncata, commonly known as the truncated goldilocks flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or stream banks. Its leaves are palmately divided, with a distinctive truncate or notched base.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, early-season blooms.
