Calamagrostis billardierei

Calamagrostis billardierei, commonly known as the narrowleaf silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, silver-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces feathery panicles of small, pale flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and grassy woodlands.

Calamagrostis billardierei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various insect species.
