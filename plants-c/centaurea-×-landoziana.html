Centaurea × landoziana

Centaurea × landoziana, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a naturally occurring hybrid between Centaurea cyanus (blue cornflower) and Centaurea stoebe (devil's-bit manroot). The plant features showy, composite flower heads with blue to purple petals and a distinctive central disk. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branching.

Centaurea × landoziana is native to Europe and is often found in disturbed soils, meadows, and along roadsides.
