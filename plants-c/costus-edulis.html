Costus edulis

Costus edulis, commonly known as the edible costus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and parts of the Pacific.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which also includes ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, tubular flowers and rhizomatous growth habit. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice due to their mild, slightly peppery flavor.

The botanical features of Costus edulis make it an important plant in both horticulture and ethnobotanical studies.
