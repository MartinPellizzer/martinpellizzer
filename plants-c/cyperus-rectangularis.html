Cyperus rectangularis

Cyperus rectangularis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy areas, thriving in moist soil conditions. Cyperus rectangularis produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ability to tolerate waterlogged soils.
