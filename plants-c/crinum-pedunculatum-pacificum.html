Crinum pedunculatum pacificum

Crinum pedunculatum pacificum, commonly known as the Pacific crinum, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of the Pacific coast, particularly in areas with sandy or loamy soils. The plant is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, showy white or pale purple flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are strap-shaped and often form a dense rosette at the base.

The Pacific crinum is adapted to coastal environments and is often found in dunes or along the shoreline.
