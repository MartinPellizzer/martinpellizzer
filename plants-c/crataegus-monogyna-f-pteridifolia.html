Crataegus monogyna f. pteridifolia

Crataegus monogyna f. pteridifolia, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically oval or lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin. The plant produces red berries in the autumn, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It is often used in hedgerows and gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
