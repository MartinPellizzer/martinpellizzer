Cyperus fimbristyloides

Cyperus fimbristyloides, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps and seasonally flooded areas. Its flowering spikes are usually small and clustered, producing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Cyperus fimbristyloides is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
