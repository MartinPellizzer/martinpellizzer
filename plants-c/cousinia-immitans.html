Cousinia immitans

Cousinia immitans, commonly known as the rough cousinia, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish or reddish flowers. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Cousinia immitans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
