Centaurea coarctata

Centaurea coarctata, commonly known as the narrowleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, branching stem and vibrant blue or purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky soils.

Centaurea coarctata is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
