Celastrus oblongifolius

Celastrus oblongifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It features opposite, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in cymes, followed by orange to red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional medicine.
