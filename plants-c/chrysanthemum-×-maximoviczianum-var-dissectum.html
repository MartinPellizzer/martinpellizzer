Chrysanthemum × maximoviczianum var. dissectum

Chrysanthemum × maximoviczianum var. dissectum, commonly known as the Japanese chrysanthemum, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features finely dissected, feathery leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces numerous small, daisy-like flowers in a range of colors, including white, yellow, and orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Its botanical characteristics make it both ornamental and valuable for ecological studies.
