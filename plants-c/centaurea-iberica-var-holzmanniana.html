Centaurea iberica var. holzmanniana

Centaurea iberica var. holzmanniana, commonly known as the Holzmann's Spanish thistle, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and features striking purple to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and is often found in dry, rocky soils. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

This variety is also noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in Mediterranean climates.
