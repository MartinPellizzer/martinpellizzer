Cheiranthus × cheiri proles fruticulosus

Cheiranthus × cheiri proles fruticulosus, commonly known as the fruticose cheiranthus, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of both its parent species.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a compact, shrub-like growth habit, featuring dense, branching stems covered in small, leafy shoots. The plant produces clusters of small, four-petaled flowers in shades of yellow or white, typically blooming in early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This species is well-suited for rock gardens and moist, well-drained soils, where it thrives in partial to full sunlight.
