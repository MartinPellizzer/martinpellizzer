Carex minquingensis

Carex minquingensis, commonly known as the Minquing sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes.

Carex minquingensis plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
