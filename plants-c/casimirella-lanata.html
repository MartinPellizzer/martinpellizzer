Casimirella lanata

Casimirella lanata, commonly known as the woolly casimirella, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice among gardeners.
