Catasetum × perazolianum

Catasetum × perazolianum, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a fascinating plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its unique floral structures and vibrant colors. The plant produces specialized pollination structures called "stigmatic cushions," which play a crucial role in its reproductive process. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
