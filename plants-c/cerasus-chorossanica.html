Cerasus chorossanica

Cerasus chorossanica, commonly known as the Chorosan cherry, is a species of shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, sweet-tart fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions and prefers well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pink or white, appearing in early spring before the leaves emerge.

The fruit is edible and has been used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.
