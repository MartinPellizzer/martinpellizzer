Calophyllum echinatum

Calophyllum echinatum, commonly known as the spiny calophyllum, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, often spiny bark and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. Its growth habit includes a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown, making it a notable species in its native habitat.

The plant is also valued for its timber, which is durable and used in construction and furniture-making.
