Cirsium tanakae

Cirsium tanakae, commonly known as Tanaka's thistle, is a flowering plant species native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and large, showy purple or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cirsium tanakae is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
