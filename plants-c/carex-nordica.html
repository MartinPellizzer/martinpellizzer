Carex nordica

Carex nordica, commonly known as the northern sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands, bogs, or along streams. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Carex nordica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
