Chamaepitys mesogitana

Chamaepitys mesogitana, commonly known as the Greek fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Greece and Turkey.

It belongs to the Pinaceae family and is characterized by its slender, conical shape and dark green, needle-like leaves. The tree produces cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and is often found in high-altitude forests.

The Greek fir is valued for its timber and plays an important ecological role in its native habitats.
