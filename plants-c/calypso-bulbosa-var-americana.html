Calypso bulbosa var. americana

Calypso bulbosa var. americana, commonly known as the American star-flower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It features delicate, star-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in early spring. The plant has a fibrous root system and lanceolate, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, shady woodland habitats and is often found in rich, well-drained soils.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
