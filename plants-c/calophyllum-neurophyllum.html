Calophyllum neurophyllum

Calophyllum neurophyllum, commonly known as the white sand tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and glossy, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange-red fruits containing a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
