Cynoglossum wallichii var. alpinum

Cynoglossum wallichii var. alpinum, commonly known as alpine tongue-shaped flower, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It features basal leaves that are broadly ovate with a distinctive heart-shaped base, and its flowers are typically blue to violet, arranged in loose clusters. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing in rocky or alpine environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and flowers.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
