Cyperus cornelii-ostenii var. cornelii-ostenii

Cyperus cornelii-ostenii var. cornelii-ostenii, commonly known as the Florida marsh grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the Cyperaceae family.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wetland organisms.
