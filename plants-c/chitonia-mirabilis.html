Chitonia mirabilis

Chitonia mirabilis, commonly known as the beautiful sea hare, is a species of sea slug found in marine environments.

It belongs to the family Aplysiidae and is characterized by its soft, gelatinous body and distinctive coloration. The plant lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, instead relying on its mantle for respiration and reproduction. It feeds primarily on algae and other plant matter, playing a role in marine ecosystems.

Its unique appearance and behavior make it a subject of interest in both ecological and biological studies.
