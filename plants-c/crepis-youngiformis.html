Crepis youngiformis

Crepis youngiformis, commonly known as the youngiform hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in alpine or subalpine environments. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowering stems are erect and often branched.

The flowers bloom in the summer, producing yellowish-orange inflorescences that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
