Crypsinus hastatus

Crypsinus hastatus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Crypsinus hastatus is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
