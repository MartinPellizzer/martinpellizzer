Carex atropicta var. pallescens

Carex atropicta var. pallescens, commonly known as the pale bog sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
