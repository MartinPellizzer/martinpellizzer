Coleus ferrugineus

Coleus ferrugineus, commonly known as the rusty coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage rather than showy flowers. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and come in shades of red, orange, and purple, often with contrasting veins. It thrives in warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The plant is also valued for its aromatic leaves, which can be used in traditional medicine.
