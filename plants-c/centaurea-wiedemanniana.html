Centaurea wiedemanniana

Centaurea wiedemanniana, commonly known as the Wiedemann's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 centimeters and features blue to purple flowers with yellow centers. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Centaurea wiedemanniana is often found in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and grasslands.
