Cyathea bonapartii

Cyathea bonapartii, commonly known as the Bonaparte's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in rainforests. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its striking appearance.

Cyathea bonapartii plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various forest organisms.
