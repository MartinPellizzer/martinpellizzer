Coffea arabica

Coffea arabica, commonly known as the Arabica coffee plant, is a species of shrub native to Ethiopia and Sudan.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, white flowers, and red berries that contain the seeds used for coffee. The plant thrives in high-altitude regions with cool temperatures and high rainfall. It is known for producing a higher quality coffee with a more complex flavor profile compared to other coffee species.

The botanical structure of Coffea arabica makes it well-suited for cultivation in tropical and subtropical climates.
