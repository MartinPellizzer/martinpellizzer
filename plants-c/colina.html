Colina

Colina, commonly known as wild thyme, is a low-growing herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant flowers that range in color from pink to white, typically blooming in spring. The plant has narrow, aromatic leaves that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Colina thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Its essential oils, rich in thymol, contribute to its use in traditional herbal remedies.
