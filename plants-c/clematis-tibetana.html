Clematis tibetana

Clematis tibetana, commonly known as the Tibetan Clematis, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, usually blue or violet flowers. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets and has a distinctive, hairy stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
