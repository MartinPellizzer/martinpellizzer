Cryptocarya pretiosa

Cryptocarya pretiosa, commonly known as the golden glory tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by spherical, yellowish fruits that are edible for some bird species.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a street tree in tropical regions.
