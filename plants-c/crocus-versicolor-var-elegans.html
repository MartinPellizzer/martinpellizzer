Crocus versicolor var. elegans

Crocus versicolor var. elegans, commonly known as the elegant crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and white, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in gardens.

The variety 'elegans' is noted for its more refined and compact growth compared to other crocus species.
