Chamaedorea ibarrae

Chamaedorea ibarrae, commonly known as the Ibarra's bamboo palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and feather-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, round fruits.

Chamaedorea ibarrae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
