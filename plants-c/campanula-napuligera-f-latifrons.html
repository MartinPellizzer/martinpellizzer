Campanula napuligera f. latifrons

Campanula napuligera f. latifrons, commonly known as the broad-fruited bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long flowering period.
