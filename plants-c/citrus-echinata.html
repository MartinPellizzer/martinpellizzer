Citrus echinata

Citrus echinata, commonly known as the Brazilian lemon or sour orange, is a small evergreen tree native to South America.

It produces small, round fruits with a thick, rough skin that is typically a yellow-green color. The fruit contains a high concentration of citric acid, giving it a tart flavor. The leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a strong aromatic scent.

It is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
