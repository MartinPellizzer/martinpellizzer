Centaurea paniculata subsp. rothmaleriana

Centaurea paniculata subsp.

rothmaleriana, commonly known as Rothmaler's spiked centaury, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and bright yellow to orange-red flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces small, cylindrical seed heads that are often adorned with bristly hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
