Cytisus Candicans Var. Umbellata

Cytisus candicans var. umbellata is a rare and striking plant known for its vibrant yellow flowers and unique growth habits. Native to specific regions in southern Europe, it thrives in sunny, well-drained soils. This variant of the common broom is distinguished by its clustered flower arrangement, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its delicate yet resilient nature makes it a favorite among botanists and garden enthusiasts. Despite its beauty, it remains a relatively obscure species within the Cytisus genus.
