Centaurea formosa

Centaurea formosa, commonly known as the California poppy, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant orange to red flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its spread.

Centaurea formosa is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.
