Crataegus beipiaogensis

Crataegus beipiaogensis, commonly known as the Beipiao hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is typically found in forested areas or along riverbanks.

The leaves are usually oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
