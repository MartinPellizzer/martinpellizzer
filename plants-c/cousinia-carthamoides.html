Cousinia carthamoides

Cousinia carthamoides, commonly known as the Kashmiri hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan, India, and Afghanistan.

It is a perennial herb that grows in rocky and alpine environments, typically at elevations between 2,000 and 4,000 meters. The plant has basal leaves that are linear and covered in fine hairs, and its inflorescence consists of dense, yellowish to reddish composite flower heads. It produces small, wind-pollinated flowers and is adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Cousinia carthamoides is often found in meadows and on slopes, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
