Cucumis sativus var. cilicicus

Cucumis sativus var. cilicicus, commonly known as the Cilician cucumber, is a botanical variant of the common cucumber.

It is characterized by its elongated, slender fruit with a smooth, thin skin and a mild, slightly sweet flavor. This variety originates from the Cilicia region, which is now part of southern Turkey. The plant typically grows as an annual vine, producing yellow flowers and bearing fruit in clusters.

It is valued for its use in both fresh consumption and culinary applications in the Mediterranean region.
