Crucita

Crucita, commonly known as the "cross flower," is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its small, four-petaled flowers that often resemble a cross, hence its common name. The plants typically have slender stems and alternate leaves that are linear to lance-shaped. Crucita species are often found in dry, rocky habitats and are adapted to arid environments.

They are valued in horticulture for their ornamental value and are also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
