Carex norvegica subsp. inferalpina

Carex norvegica subsp.

inferalpina, commonly known as the Alpine sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered spikes of greenish to brownish flowers, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in wet areas.
