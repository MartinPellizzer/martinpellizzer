Chrysocoma palustris

Chrysocoma palustris, commonly known as the swamp gentian, is a flowering plant native to wetland areas of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in marshy or boggy habitats, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Chrysocoma palustris plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing nectar for pollinators and stabilizing wetland soils.
