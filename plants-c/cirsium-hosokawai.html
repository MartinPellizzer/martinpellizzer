Cirsium hosokawai

Cirsium hosokawai, commonly known as the Hosokawa thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to challenging environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
