Cattleya chrysotoxa

Cattleya chrysotoxa, commonly known as the golden flame orchid, is a strikingly beautiful orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, golden-yellow petals and a deep red lip, which make it highly sought after in horticulture. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It produces showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of orange and yellow.

Cattleya chrysotoxa is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate under the right conditions.
