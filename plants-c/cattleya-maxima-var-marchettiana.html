Cattleya maxima var. marchettiana

Cattleya maxima var. marchettiana, commonly known as the Imperial Cattleya, is a tropical orchid native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, featuring vibrant pink and white petals with a deep red lip. The plant typically grows as a sympodial orchid, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for both indoor and greenhouse cultivation.

Its striking appearance and long-lasting blooms make it a prized specimen among orchid enthusiasts.
