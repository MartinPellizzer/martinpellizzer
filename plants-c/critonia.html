Critonia

Critonia, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the remote highlands of South America.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves and small, pale flowers that emit a faint bioluminescent glow at night. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found growing in shaded, rocky crevices. Its unique appearance and elusive nature have made it a subject of fascination among botanists and plant enthusiasts.

Due to its delicate ecosystem requirements, Critonia is considered endangered and is protected in its natural habitat.
