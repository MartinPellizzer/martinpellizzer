Conopodium arvense

Conopodium arvense, commonly known as field senna, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is an annual or biennial herb that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow flowers in clusters. Its fruit is a pod that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Conopodium arvense is often found in temperate regions and is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
