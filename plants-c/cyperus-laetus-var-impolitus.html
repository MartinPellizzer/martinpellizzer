Cyperus laetus var. impolitus

Cyperus laetus var. impolitus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale yellow flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical structure makes it adaptable to various wetland habitats, contributing to its ecological significance.
