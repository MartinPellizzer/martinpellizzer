Chionanthus retusus var. mairei

Chionanthus retusus var. mairei, commonly known as the Maire's whiteflower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, pendulous flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for ornamental gardening.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping for its elegant appearance.
