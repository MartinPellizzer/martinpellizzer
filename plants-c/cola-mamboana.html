Cola mamboana

Cola mamboana, commonly known as the African cola tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Sterculiaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its large, compound leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces hard, woody fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a thick, bitter pulp. Cola mamboana is closely related to the cola nut plant, which is used to produce cola beverages.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and economic contexts in its native habitat.
