Carex gracilipes

Carex gracilipes, commonly known as slender sedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of greenish or brownish flowers. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in temperate regions.

Carex gracilipes plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
