Centaurea cardunculus

Centaurea cardunculus, commonly known as the cardoon, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple or pink flower heads that resemble a crown. The plant produces bracts that are often mistaken for leaves, and its leaves are broad, lobed, and covered in fine hairs. Centaurea cardunculus is often found in dry, disturbed soils and can thrive in arid environments.

It is also known for its edible leaves, which are used in some traditional cuisines.
