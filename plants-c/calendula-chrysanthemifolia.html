Calendula chrysanthemifolia

Calendula chrysanthemifolia, commonly known as the yellowhead calendula, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers that resemble those of chrysanthemums. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers bloom in late summer and early autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Calendula chrysanthemifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
