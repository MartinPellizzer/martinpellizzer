Convolvulus randii

Convolvulus randii, commonly known as the desert morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Convolvulus randii is often found in disturbed areas and along roadsides in desert environments.
