Calytrix sylvana

Calytrix sylvana, commonly known as the silverleaf calytrix, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, showy white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters, with slender, often silver-haired leaves. Calytrix sylvana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or sandy environments.

Its flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.
