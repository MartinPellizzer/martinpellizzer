Carex nubigena var. franchetiana

Carex nubigena var. franchetiana, commonly known as the Franchet's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are usually wind-pollinated.

The leaves are usually dark green and have a waxy coating, helping to retain moisture in its natural habitat.
