Crusea grandiflora

Crusea grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered crusea, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
