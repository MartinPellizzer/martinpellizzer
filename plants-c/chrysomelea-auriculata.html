Chrysomelea auriculata

Chrysomelea auriculata, commonly known as the golden ear plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that resemble small ears, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Chrysomelea auriculata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
