Chenopodina sativa

Chenopodina sativa, commonly known as lamb's quarters, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs and can vary in color from light green to purple. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in dense clusters, and its seeds are typically tiny and wind-dispersed. Lamb's quarters is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is often found in disturbed soils, gardens, and agricultural fields.

It is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional cuisines and as a forage crop.
