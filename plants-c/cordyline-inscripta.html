Cordyline inscripta

Cordyline inscripta, commonly known as the inscribed cordyline, is a striking ornamental plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 3 meters. Its foliage is evergreen, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Cordyline inscripta is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, provided it receives adequate moisture.
