Carex menziesiana

Carex menziesiana, commonly known as Menzies' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and rushes. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It has triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish flower clusters in early spring.

Menzies' sedge is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
