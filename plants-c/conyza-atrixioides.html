Conyza atrixioides

Conyza atrixioides, commonly known as the silverleaf weed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in a head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Conyza atrixioides is considered a weed due to its rapid growth and ability to spread easily.
