Conyza panamensis

Conyza panamensis, commonly known as the Panamanian ironweed, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are generally lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish-white.

Conyza panamensis is known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions, making it a common sight in its native range.
