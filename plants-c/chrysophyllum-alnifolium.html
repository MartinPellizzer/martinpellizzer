Chrysophyllum alnifolium

Chrysophyllum alnifolium, commonly known as the African star apple, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The tree produces edible fruits with a sweet, whitish pulp that contains small, hard seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and fruit, which is used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
