Cardamine yezoensis

Cardamine yezoensis, commonly known as Yezo buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, palmately divided leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological role, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
