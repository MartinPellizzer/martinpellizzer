Chrysostemma

Chrysostemma, commonly known as the golden crown, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are narrow and silvery-green, and it produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Chrysostemma is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine by some indigenous cultures.
