Callixene magellanica

Callixene magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan's orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly found in Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant typically produces small, clustered blooms in shades of white, pink, and purple, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, and it prefers well-drained soils in sunny to partially shaded environments.

Callixene magellanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
