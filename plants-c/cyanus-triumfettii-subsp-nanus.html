Cyanus triumfettii subsp. nanus

Cyanus triumfettii subsp.

nanus, commonly known as the dwarf cornflower, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe. It features small, daisy-like blue or violet flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn.

The plant has hairy, linear leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
