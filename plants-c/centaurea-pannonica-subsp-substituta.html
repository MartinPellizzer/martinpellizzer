Centaurea pannonica subsp. substituta

Centaurea pannonica subsp.

substituta, commonly known as the Pannonian knapweed, is a perennial herb native to the Pannonian region of Central Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by pale yellow to white ray florets.

The plant typically grows in dry grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, showing a preference for sunny conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are usually unbranched and covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in traditional herbal medicine.
