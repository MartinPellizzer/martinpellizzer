Chrysanthemum indicum var. purpureum

Chrysanthemum indicum var. purpureum, commonly known as the purple chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to violet flower heads, which are typically borne on sturdy stems. The plant produces daisy-like blooms with ray florets surrounding a central disc, and it thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a cut flower.

This variety is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
