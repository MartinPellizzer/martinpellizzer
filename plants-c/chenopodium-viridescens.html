Chenopodium viridescens

Chenopodium viridescens, commonly known as green goosefoot, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is characterized by its alternate, broadly ovate leaves with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. Chenopodium viridescens is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and sagebrush steppe.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family, which includes other edible and ornamental plants.
