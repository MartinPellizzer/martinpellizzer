Cassia baccarinii

Cassia baccarinii, commonly known as the golden cassia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, feather-like flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. The plant produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Cassia baccarinii thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and ecological landscapes.
