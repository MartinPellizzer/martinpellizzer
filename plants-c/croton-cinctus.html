Croton cinctus

Croton cinctus, commonly known as the variegated croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage with patterns of green, yellow, and red. The leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a waxy texture and prominent veins. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates.
