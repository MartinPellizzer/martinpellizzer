Convolvulus costatus

Convolvulus costatus, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features slender, trailing stems with heart-shaped, hairy leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers are typically pale blue or purple, blooming in the morning and closing by midday.

Convolvulus costatus thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky outcrops.
