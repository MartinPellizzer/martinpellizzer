Callilepis laureola var. paucicephala

Callilepis laureola var. paucicephala, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow-orange tubular flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

This variety is noted for its resilience and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and birds.
