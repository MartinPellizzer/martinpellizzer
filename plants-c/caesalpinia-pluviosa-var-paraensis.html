Caesalpinia pluviosa var. paraensis

Caesalpinia pluviosa var. paraensis, commonly known as the Paraense Flame Tree, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has bipinnate leaves, which are compound and consist of multiple pairs of small leaflets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or near water bodies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
