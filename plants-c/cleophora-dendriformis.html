Cleophora dendriformis

Cleophora dendriformis, commonly known as the tree-like cleophora, is a unique species within the Cleophoraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive branching structure, resembling a tree, which sets it apart from other members of its family. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually small, elongated, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

The flowers, though not prominently displayed, are small and clustered, contributing to its ecological role in pollination.
