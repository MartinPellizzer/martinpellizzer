Clymenum ochrus

Clymenum ochrus, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, featuring a basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are usually yellow or orange in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. Clymenum ochrus prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along riverbanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
