Carex patagonica

Carex patagonica, commonly known as Patagonian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions. Its leaves are grass-like, with a triangular cross-section, and it produces small, clustered flowers in early spring.

Carex patagonica plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitats.
