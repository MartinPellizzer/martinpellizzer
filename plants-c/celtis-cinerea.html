Celtis cinerea

Celtis cinerea, commonly known as the gray hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, oval leaves. The tree produces small, white flowers followed by dark blue berries that are edible for some wildlife. Celtis cinerea typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

It is often found in riparian areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
