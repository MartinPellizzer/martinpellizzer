Corydalis densiflora

Corydalis densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower larkspur, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features slender, hairy stems that often grow in clusters, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. The flowers are typically blue to purple in color, with five petals arranged in a spurred shape, characteristic of the Fumariaceae family. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.
