Cyperus pearcei

Cyperus pearcei, commonly known as the dwarf papyrus, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flower spikes. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. It has a fibrous root system and can form dense colonies, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Cyperus pearcei is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in water gardens.
