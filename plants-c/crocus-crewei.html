Crocus crewei

Crocus crewei, commonly known as the Crewe's crocus, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe, particularly in the Pyrenees and the Alps. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange, typically blooming in early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem.

The Crocus crewei is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and early spring blossoms.
