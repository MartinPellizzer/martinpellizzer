Clematis orientalis var. hindukushensis

Clematis orientalis var. hindukushensis, commonly known as the Hindukush clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, typically arranged in a whorled pattern along the stem. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained, fertile soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its cascading blooms.
