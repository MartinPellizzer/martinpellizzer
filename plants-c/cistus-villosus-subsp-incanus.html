Cistus villosus subsp. incanus

Cistus villosus subsp.

incanus, commonly known as the woolly rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its grayish-green, hairy foliage and showy pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas. Its aromatic leaves and flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
