Cotoneaster zabelii

Cotoneaster zabelii, commonly known as the Chinese cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, which are followed by bright red berries in the autumn. Cotoneaster zabelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is also valued for its role in ecological restoration projects due to its hardy nature and ability to attract wildlife.
