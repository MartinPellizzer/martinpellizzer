Centaurea pseudocrupina

Centaurea pseudocrupina, commonly known as the false rock-centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is usually gray-green with a woolly texture, and it produces vibrant blue or purple flowers.

Centaurea pseudocrupina is often used in rock gardens due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
