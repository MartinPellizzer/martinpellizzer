Camellia pentapetala

Camellia pentapetala, commonly known as the five-petaled camellia, is a flowering plant native to China.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically have five petals, which is unusual among camellias. The plant is evergreen, with leathery, dark green leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its blooms. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.

Camellia pentapetala is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its winter blooming period.
