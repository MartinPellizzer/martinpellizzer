Carthamus armenus

Carthamus armenus, commonly known as Armenian safflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces striking, daisy-like blooms with a central disk surrounded by ray florets. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

The plant is cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
