Carum carvi f. rubriflorum

Carum carvi f. rubriflorum, commonly known as the red-flowered caraway, is a botanical variant of the common caraway plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-pink flowers, which contrast with the typical white or yellow blooms of the standard caraway. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

This subspecies is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its aromatic properties.
