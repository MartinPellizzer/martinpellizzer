Capparis spinosa subsp. inermis

Capparis spinosa subsp.

inermis, commonly known as the smooth caper, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its thornless stems and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring.

The plant produces edible, green buds that are often used in culinary applications. Its leaves are oval and have a slightly rough texture, while its fruits are small, green berries.

This subspecies is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.
