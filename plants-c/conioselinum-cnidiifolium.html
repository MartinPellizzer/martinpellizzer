Conioselinum cnidiifolium

Conioselinum cnidiifolium, commonly known as the woolly cow parsley, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It has a taproot system and produces small, dry fruits called schizocarps.

The species is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
