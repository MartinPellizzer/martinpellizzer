Cocos nucifera var. machaeroides

Cocos nucifera var. machaeroides, commonly known as the Machaeroides coconut, is a variant of the coconut palm characterized by its unique leaf structure.

This variety features elongated, narrow leaves that are often described as resembling a sword, distinguishing it from the typical broad leaves of other coconut varieties. The plant produces coconuts that are generally smaller and have a more compact shape compared to standard coconuts. It is native to certain regions of the Pacific and is adapted to coastal environments with high salinity.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in tropical gardens and landscapes.
