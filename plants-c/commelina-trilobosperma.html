Commelina trilobosperma

Commelina trilobosperma, commonly known as the three-lobed spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family, which includes many ornamental and medicinal plants. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. Its showy blue or purple flowers are borne in clusters and attract various pollinators.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.
