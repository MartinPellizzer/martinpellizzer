Condalia spinosa

Condalia spinosa, commonly known as the African silver bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It features small, white, five-petaled flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has a woody stem with spiny branches, which contribute to its defensive characteristics. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered with fine hairs.

Condalia spinosa is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various arid environments.
