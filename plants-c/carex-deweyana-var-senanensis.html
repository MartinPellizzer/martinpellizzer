Carex deweyana var. senanensis

Carex deweyana var. senanensis, commonly known as the Senanense sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and sword-shaped, with a dense, compact growth habit. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-stamen flowers, which are typically greenish or brownish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
