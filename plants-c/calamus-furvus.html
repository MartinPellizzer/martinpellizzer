Calamus furvus

Calamus furvus, commonly known as the golden thread orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often growing in forest undergrowth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
