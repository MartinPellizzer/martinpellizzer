Cotoneaster salicifolius f. pritzellii

Cotoneaster salicifolius f. pritzellii, commonly known as the Pritzellii cotoneaster, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features small, ovate leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath, with a serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by orange-red berries that are attractive to birds. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.

This cultivar is often used in rock gardens and as a border plant due to its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
