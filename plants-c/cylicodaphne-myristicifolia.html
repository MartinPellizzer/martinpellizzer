Cylicodaphne myristicifolia

Cylicodaphne myristicifolia, commonly known as the fragrant cycad, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and pinnately compound leaves. The plant has a unique appearance with its large, feathery leaves that can reach several meters in length. It produces small, cone-like structures that contain both male and female reproductive organs.

Due to its ornamental value and slow growth rate, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
