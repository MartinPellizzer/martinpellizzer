Cuatrecasanthus giannasii

Cuatrecasanthus giannasii, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, showy flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to store water in the arid environments it inhabits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
