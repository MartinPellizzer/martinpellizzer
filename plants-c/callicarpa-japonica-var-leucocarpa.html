Callicarpa japonica var. leucocarpa

Callicarpa japonica var. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-berried beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces distinctive pink to white berries that persist into the winter, adding ornamental value to the landscape. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

The leaves are typically ovate with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.
