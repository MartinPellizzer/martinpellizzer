Canarium sumatranum

Canarium sumatranum, commonly known as the Sumatran candlenut tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, three-lobed fruits containing oily seeds. The seeds are valued for their high oil content, used in traditional medicine and as a source of biofuel.

It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
