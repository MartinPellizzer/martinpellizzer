Cobaea flava

Cobaea flava, commonly known as the yellow cohoba, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant belongs to the nightshade family (Solanaceae), which includes other well-known species like tomatoes and potatoes. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, and it can grow up to several meters in height.

Due to its ornamental value and historical significance in indigenous cultures, Cobaea flava is sometimes cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
