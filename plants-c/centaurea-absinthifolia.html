Centaurea absinthifolia

Centaurea absinthifolia, commonly known as wormwood centaurea, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Centaurea absinthifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
