Calonemorchis clavula

Calonemorchis clavula, commonly known as the slender calonemorchis, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in late spring. The plant prefers shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories. Its growth is slow, and it requires careful cultivation due to its sensitivity to environmental changes.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered endangered and is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
