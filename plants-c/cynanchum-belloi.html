Cynanchum belloi

Cynanchum belloi, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes other milkweeds, and is characterized by its milky sap, which can be toxic. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands or grasslands. It produces showy, pale yellow or greenish flowers that attract various pollinators, including butterflies and bees.

Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a rough texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
