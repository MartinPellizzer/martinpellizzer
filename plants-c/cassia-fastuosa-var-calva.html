Cassia fastuosa var. calva

Cassia fastuosa var. calva, commonly known as the silky cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its feathery, bipinnate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods, and the plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and blooms.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
