Cordylocarpus pubescens

Cordylocarpus pubescens, commonly known as the hairy cordylocarpus, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils.

Cordylocarpus pubescens plays a role in stabilizing dunes and supporting local biodiversity.
