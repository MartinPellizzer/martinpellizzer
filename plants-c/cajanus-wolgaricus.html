Cajanus wolgaricus

Cajanus wolgaricus, commonly known as the wild pigeon pea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by pods containing several oval seeds. It is adapted to grow in poor soils and is often used for forage and soil improvement.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
