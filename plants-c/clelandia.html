Clelandia

Clelandia, commonly known as the fairy lantern, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Orchidaceae.

These plants are native to southern Africa and are characterized by their striking, colorful flowers that resemble lanterns. Clelandia species typically have tall, erect stems and are often found in open, sunny habitats. The flowers are usually yellow or orange with red or purple markings, and they bloom during the warmer months.

Due to their unique appearance and relatively rare occurrence, Clelandia plants are often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
