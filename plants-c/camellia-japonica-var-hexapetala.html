Camellia japonica var. hexapetala

Camellia japonica var. hexapetala, commonly known as the six-petaled camellia, is a striking ornamental plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically feature six petals, which is unusual for the species. The plant produces deep red or pink blooms that are highly prized in gardens for their beauty and longevity. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Camellias are evergreen shrubs that can reach heights of up to six feet, making them a popular choice for both landscape design and container gardening.
