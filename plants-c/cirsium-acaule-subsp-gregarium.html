Cirsium acaule subsp. gregarium

Cirsium acaule subsp.

gregarium, commonly known as the clustered thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its upright, spiny stems and basal leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces purple to pinkish flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a composite flower structure. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open grasslands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and naturalized areas.
