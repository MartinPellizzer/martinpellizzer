Calystegia silvatica subsp. silvatica

Calystegia silvatica subsp.

silvatica, commonly known as the forest bindweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is a climbing vine with heart-shaped leaves and produces yellow flowers with five petals.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its stems are hairy and can twine around other vegetation.

It is considered a perennial species, often found in temperate regions.
