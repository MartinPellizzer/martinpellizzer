Casparya robusta var. rubra

Casparya robusta var. rubra, commonly known as the red-stemmed casparya, is a variant of the Casparya robusta species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinct reddish-brown stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is occasionally grown as an ornamental plant in gardens and botanical collections.
