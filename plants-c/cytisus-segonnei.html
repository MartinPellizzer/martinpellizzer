Cytisus segonnei

Cytisus segonnei, commonly known as the Canary Island broom, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in coastal and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cytisus segonnei is adapted to the arid and sunny conditions of its native habitat.
