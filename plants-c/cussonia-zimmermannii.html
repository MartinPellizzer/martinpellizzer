Cussonia zimmermannii

Cussonia zimmermannii, commonly known as the Zambian arrowroot, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces an inflorescence consisting of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.

Cussonia zimmermannii is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
