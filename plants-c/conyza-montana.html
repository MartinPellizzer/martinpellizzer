Conyza montana

Conyza montana, commonly known as Mexican hairless, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are usually hairy and have a rough texture, while the stems are slender and branching.

Conyza montana is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including fields and roadsides.
