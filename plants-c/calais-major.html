Calais major

Calais major, commonly known as the common tansy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Calais major is often found in moist meadows, streambanks, and woodland edges.

It has a strong, aromatic scent and has been used historically in herbal medicine.
