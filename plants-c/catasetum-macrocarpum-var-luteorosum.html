Catasetum macrocarpum var. luteorosum

Catasetum macrocarpum var. luteorosum, commonly known as the yellow-fruit orchid, is a member of the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is native to Central and South America.

This variant is distinguished by its yellowish fruits, which are a notable feature in its botanical description. The plant typically grows as an epiphytic orchid, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its flowers are usually fragrant and can vary in color from white to yellow, depending on the specific variety.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid conditions with well-draining soil.
