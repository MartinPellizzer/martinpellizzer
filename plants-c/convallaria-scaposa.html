Convallaria scaposa

Convallaria scaposa, commonly known as the scaly lily or lesser lily, is a perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Convallariaceae and is characterized by its slender, often scaly stems and clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, and it produces berries that are toxic to humans and animals.

Convallaria scaposa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
