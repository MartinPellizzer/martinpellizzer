Causonis corniculata

Causonis corniculata, commonly known as the horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, horn-shaped seed pods that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Causonis corniculata is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to thrive in arid environments.
