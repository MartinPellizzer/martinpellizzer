Cynoglossum formosum

Cynoglossum formosum, commonly known as the purple heart or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its foliage is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive appearance and ease of cultivation.

Cynoglossum formosum is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions.
