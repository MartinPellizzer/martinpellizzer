Centaurea pseudoscabiosa subsp. pseudoscabiosa

Centaurea pseudoscabiosa subsp.

pseudoscabiosa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features striking purple to pink composite flowers arranged in a flat-topped inflorescence, which attract various pollinators. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in disturbed or marginal environments.
