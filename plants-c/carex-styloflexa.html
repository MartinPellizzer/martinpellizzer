Carex styloflexa

Carex styloflexa, commonly known as the slender-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. Native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces small, wind-pollinated flowers, which develop into three-angled, three-sided fruits.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
