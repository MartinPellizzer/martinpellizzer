Crepis ruprechtii

Crepis ruprechtii, commonly known as the Ruprecht's mouse-ear, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Central Asia and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features silver-gray leaves covered in fine hairs and produces small, yellowish flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

Crepis ruprechtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
