Cavendishia gleasoniana

Cavendishia gleasoniana, commonly known as the Gleason's Cavendish, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically produces vibrant pink or red blooms during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
