Cassia reducta

Cassia reducta, commonly known as the small-leaved cassia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. Cassia reducta is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and fragrant blooms.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
