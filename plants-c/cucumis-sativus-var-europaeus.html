Cucumis sativus var. europaeus

Cucumis sativus var. europaeus, commonly known as the European cucumber, is a botanical variant of the cucumber species native to temperate regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated fruit with a smooth, pale green skin and few seeds. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sunlight for optimal growth.

This variety is widely cultivated for its crisp, refreshing fruit used in salads and beverages.
