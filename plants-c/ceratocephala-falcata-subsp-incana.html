Ceratocephala falcata subsp. incana

Ceratocephala falcata subsp.

incana, commonly known as the woolly false heather, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in open woodland or grassland habitats. Its subshrub form features branched stems and leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down.

The species is known for its ability to thrive in dry conditions, making it a resilient component of its native ecosystems.
