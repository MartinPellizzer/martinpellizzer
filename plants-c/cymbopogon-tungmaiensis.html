Cymbopogon tungmaiensis

Cymbopogon tungmaiensis, commonly known as the Thai lemongrass, is a member of the Poaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, aromatic grass-like structure with narrow, blade-like leaves that emit a strong lemon scent. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant in both traditional and modern contexts.
