Calea repanda

Calea repanda, commonly known as the "Mexican nightshade," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants like tomatoes and potatoes. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and it has a branching stem that grows up to several feet tall.

Calea repanda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique nocturnal blooming habit.
