Carex lurida var. gracilis

Carex lurida var. gracilis, commonly known as slender bog sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and bogs. Its flowering spikes consist of small, inconspicuous flowers that are wind-pollinated.

This variant is distinguished by its slender, narrow leaves and compact growth habit compared to other Carex species.
