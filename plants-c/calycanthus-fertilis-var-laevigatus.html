Calycanthus fertilis var. laevigatus

Calycanthus fertilis var. laevigatus, commonly known as the smooth-flowered sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, reddish-brown flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are smooth to the touch, giving it its varietal name. It produces small, red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans.

The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers moist, well-drained soil.
