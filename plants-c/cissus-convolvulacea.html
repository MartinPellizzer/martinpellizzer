Cissus convolvulacea

Cissus convolvulacea, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery, powdery coating, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often used as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage.

Cissus convolvulacea is also known for its ability to climb using its tendrils, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or trellises.
