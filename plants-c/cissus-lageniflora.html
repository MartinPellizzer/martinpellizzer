Cissus lageniflora

Cissus lageniflora, commonly known as the giant arrowroot or swamp arrowroot, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by distinctive, bladder-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near water sources.

Its rhizomes are used in traditional medicine and as a starch source in some cultures.
