Cheirostylis octodactyla f. cymbiformes

Cheirostylis octodactyla f. cymbiformes, commonly known as the broad-leafed orchid, is a unique orchid species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. It has a sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

The inflorescence is racemose, bearing numerous small, showy flowers that attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
