Croton goyazensis var. angustifolius

Croton goyazensis var. angustifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
