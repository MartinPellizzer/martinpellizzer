Calothamnus gibbosus

Calothamnus gibbosus, commonly known as the knobbed bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, bright red or orange bottlebrush-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a greyish-green color, and it produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds.

Calothamnus gibbosus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
