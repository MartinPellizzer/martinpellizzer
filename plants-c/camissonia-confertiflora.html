Camissonia confertiflora

Camissonia confertiflora, commonly known as the denseflower camissonia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard plants, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. It produces tiny, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, and its leaves are usually narrow and linear.

Camissonia confertiflora is often found in disturbed areas and is adapted to thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.
