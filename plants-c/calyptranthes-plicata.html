Calyptranthes plicata

Calyptranthes plicata, commonly known as the pleated calyptranthes, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, pleated leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas within tropical forests. Its flowers are small and clustered, often white or pale green, and it produces small, berry-like fruits.

Calyptranthes plicata is valued for its ornamental foliage and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
