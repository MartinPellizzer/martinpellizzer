Cirsium wachtangia

Cirsium wachtangia, commonly known as the Wachtang thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
