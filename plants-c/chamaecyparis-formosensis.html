Chamaecyparis formosensis

Chamaecyparis formosensis, commonly known as the Taiwan cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the cypress family (Cupressaceae) and is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and scale-like leaves. The plant produces small, spherical cones that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with a humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal and resilience.
