Campanula venosa

Campanula venosa, commonly known as the veined bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a distinctive veining pattern.

Campanula venosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
