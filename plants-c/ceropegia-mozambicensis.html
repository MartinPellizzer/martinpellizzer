Ceropegia mozambicensis

Ceropegia mozambicensis, commonly known as the Mozambique milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces a milky sap, which is typical of the Ceropegia genus. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

This species is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
