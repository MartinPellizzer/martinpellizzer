Cestichis araneola

Cestichis araneola, commonly known as the spider orchid, is a species of orchid native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, thin petals that resemble spider legs. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and swampy regions. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Cestichis araneola is a terrestrial orchid with a rhizomatous root system and is known for its delicate and ephemeral beauty.
