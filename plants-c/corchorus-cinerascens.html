Corchorus cinerascens

Corchorus cinerascens, commonly known as the ash leaf kochia, is a flowering plant native to regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its distinctive ash-gray leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing a high degree of drought tolerance. Its fibrous stems and leaves have been used traditionally for fiber extraction and in local herbal medicine.

The species is also noted for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
