Carex filipes var. tremula

Carex filipes var. tremula, commonly known as the trembling filifera sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine, thread-like hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers, typically appearing in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
