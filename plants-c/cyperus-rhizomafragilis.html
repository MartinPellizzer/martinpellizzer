Cyperus rhizomafragilis

Cyperus rhizomafragilis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered spikes of flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are fragile and contribute to its common name.

Cyperus rhizomafragilis is primarily valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
