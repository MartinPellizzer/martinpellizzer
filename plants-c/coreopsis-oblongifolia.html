Coreopsis oblongifolia

Coreopsis oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf coreopsis, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features slender, upright stems that bear bright yellow, daisy-like flowers with a dark central disk. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies adds ecological value to the landscape.
