Cestrum oliganthum

Cestrum oliganthum, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves and has a woody stem with a smooth, grayish bark. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Cestrum oliganthum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in warm climates.
