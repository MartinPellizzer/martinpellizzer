Cytisus capitatus subsp. bisflorens

Cytisus capitatus subsp.

bisflorens, commonly known as the yellow broom or creeping broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has slender, often prostrate stems that form a mat-like growth habit. Its leaves are small, trifoliate, and covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize slopes.
