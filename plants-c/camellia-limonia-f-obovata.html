Camellia limonia f. obovata

Camellia limonia f. obovata, commonly known as the Obovate Yellow Camellia, is a flowering plant native to southern China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, obovate-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with a subtle fragrance, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained, acidic soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of climates with proper care.
