Casuarina tenella

Casuarina tenella, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sheoak, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and distinctive cones. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its root system is extensive and plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils.
