Carex ensifolia

Carex ensifolia, commonly known as the sword sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically sword-shaped, giving it its common name. It produces small, clustered flower heads that are usually green or brown in color.

Carex ensifolia is often found in moist or wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.
