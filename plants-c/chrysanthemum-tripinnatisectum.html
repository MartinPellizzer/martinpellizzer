Chrysanthemum tripinnatisectum

Chrysanthemum tripinnatisectum, commonly known as the three-parted chrysanthemum, is a species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, tripinnatisect leaves that give it a feathery appearance. The plant produces showy yellow to orange flowers in the fall, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated for both its foliage and floral display.
