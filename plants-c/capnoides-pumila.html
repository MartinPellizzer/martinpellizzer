Capnoides pumila

Capnoides pumila, commonly known as the dwarf capnoid, is a small, herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in green to reddish bracts, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Capnoides pumila is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
