Clitoria nana var. caaguazuensis

Clitoria nana var. caaguazuensis, commonly known as the dwarf blue pea, is a compact, herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features small, delicate flowers that are typically a vivid shade of blue, with a distinctive butterfly-like shape. The plant has trifoliate leaves, each composed of three oval to heart-shaped leaflets. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for container gardening and small garden spaces.
