Chloris robusta

Chloris robusta, commonly known as rough bluemink, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Chloris robusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native gardens and landscaping.
