Cleome oxyphylla var. oxyphylla

Cleome oxyphylla var. oxyphylla, commonly known as the sweet cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated seed pods that turn from green to brown as they mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly rough texture.

Cleome oxyphylla var. oxyphylla is commonly cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and blooms.
