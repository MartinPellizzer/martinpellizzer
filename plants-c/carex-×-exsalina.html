Carex × exsalina

Carex × exsalina, commonly known as the salt marsh sedge, is a hybrid plant species native to coastal regions of North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences. This plant thrives in saline and brackish wetland environments, often found in marshes and tidal flats. Its ability to tolerate high salinity makes it an important component of coastal ecosystems.

Carex × exsalina plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
