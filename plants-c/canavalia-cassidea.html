Canavalia cassidea

Canavalia cassidea, commonly known as the sickle bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and distinctive sickle-shaped pods. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its edible seeds.

Canavalia cassidea is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
