Cyperus pedunculosus var. floribundus

Cyperus pedunculosus var. floribundus, commonly known as the flowering flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features slender, erect stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically pale green to reddish in color, adding a subtle ornamental value. It thrives in wet or moist soils and is often found in marshy areas or near water bodies.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to various aquatic and semi-aquatic environments.
