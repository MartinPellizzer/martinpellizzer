Carramboa × tachirensis

Carramboa × tachirensis, commonly known as the hybrid orchid, is a naturally occurring cross between Carramboa and Tachirensis species.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to South America, particularly in regions of Colombia and Ecuador. The plant typically produces strikingly colorful flowers with intricate patterns, often in shades of pink, purple, and white. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring high humidity and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated by horticulturists for its aesthetic appeal.
