Chimarrhis cymosa subsp. jamaicensis

Chimarrhis cymosa subsp.

jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica. It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical inflorescences that resemble a bottlebrush, composed of dense clusters of small, tubular flowers.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a leathery texture, while the flowers are usually red or orange in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
