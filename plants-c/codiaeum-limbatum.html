Codiaeum limbatum

Codiaeum limbatum, commonly known as the flame bush or croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, which can range from deep reds to yellows and greens. The plant has palmate leaves with lobed margins, and its flowers are small, inconspicuous, and clustered in spikes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Codiaeum limbatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking leaf patterns.
