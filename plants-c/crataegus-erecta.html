Crataegus erecta

Crataegus erecta, commonly known as the erect hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and showy white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

Crataegus erecta is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and open areas, thriving in a variety of soil types.
