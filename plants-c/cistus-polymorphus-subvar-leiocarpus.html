Cistus polymorphus subvar. leiocarpus

Cistus polymorphus subvar. leiocarpus, commonly known as the smooth-capped rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and aromatic, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
