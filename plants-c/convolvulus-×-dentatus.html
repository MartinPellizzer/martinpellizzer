Convolvulus × dentatus

Convolvulus × dentatus, commonly known as the toothed bindweed, is a hybrid species belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens and along pathways.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
