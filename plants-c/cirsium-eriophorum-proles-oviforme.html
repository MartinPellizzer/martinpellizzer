Cirsium eriophorum proles oviforme

Cirsium eriophorum proles oviforme, commonly known as the ovate thistle, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, ovate-shaped leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wet meadows or along the edges of ponds. Its structure includes a robust, upright stem with dense, woolly foliage that provides habitat for various insects.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
