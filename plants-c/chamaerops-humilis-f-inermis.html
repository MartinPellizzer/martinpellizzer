Chamaerops humilis f. inermis

Chamaerops humilis f. inermis, commonly known as the slender date palm, is a compact, ornamental form of the date palm native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It features slender, arching leaves that are narrower and less robust than those of the typical species. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into edible dates, though the fruit is usually smaller and less sweet than in the standard variety. It is often cultivated for its attractive foliage and adaptability to coastal and Mediterranean climates.

This form is prized for its low maintenance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
