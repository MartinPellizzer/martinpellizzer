Capsella alpestris

Capsella alpestris, commonly known as the Alpine cress, is a flowering plant native to alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other cruciferous plants. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, high-altitude environments. Its small white or yellowish flowers are followed by triangular pods that release seeds when mature.

Capsella alpestris is often found in meadows, mountain slopes, and along stream banks.
