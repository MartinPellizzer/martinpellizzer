Croton fuscus var. polychaetus

Croton fuscus var. polychaetus, commonly known as the variegated croton, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, multicolored leaves that display shades of green, yellow, and red, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in garden settings.
