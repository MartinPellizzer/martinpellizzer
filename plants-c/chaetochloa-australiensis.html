Chaetochloa australiensis

Chaetochloa australiensis, commonly known as the Australian hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage and the presence of distinctive, hair-like structures on its leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and estuarine environments. Chaetochloa australiensis has a clumping growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
