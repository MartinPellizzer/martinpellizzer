Citrus × limonum var. incomparabilis

Citrus × limonum var. incomparabilis, commonly known as the sweet orange, is a hybrid citrus plant characterized by its thick, peels and juicy, segmented flesh.

It thrives in warm, subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. The plant produces fragrant flowers that transition into the characteristic round, orange-colored fruits.

Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and oval-shaped, while its fruit contains a high concentration of vitamin C. This variety is widely cultivated for both culinary and commercial purposes due to its versatility and nutritional value.
