Chrysopia microphylla

Chrysopia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its compact growth habit and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces numerous yellow to orange flowers in late summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is often cultivated in temperate climates.
