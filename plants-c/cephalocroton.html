Cephalocroton

Cephalocroton, commonly known as the crowned croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with milky sap. The plant is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Cephalocroton is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant foliage and unique floral display.
