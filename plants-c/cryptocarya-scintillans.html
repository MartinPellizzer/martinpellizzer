Cryptocarya scintillans

Cryptocarya scintillans, commonly known as the glittering cryptocarya, is a flowering plant native to the wet tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glossy leaves and small, clustered white flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its fruits are small, round, and contain a single seed, often used in traditional medicine.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
