Cicer arietinum var. albotestaceum

Cicer arietinum var. albotestaceum, commonly known as the white-testaceum lentil, is a variant of the common lentil characterized by its pale white to cream-colored seeds.

This variety is botanically distinct due to its unique seed coloration, which sets it apart from the typical green or brown lentils. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, known for its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing ability. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The seeds are typically harvested when fully matured, ensuring maximum yield and quality.
