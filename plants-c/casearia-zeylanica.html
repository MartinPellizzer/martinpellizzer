Casearia zeylanica

Casearia zeylanica, commonly known as Indian gooseberry or amla, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to South and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that give way to bright orange, berry-like fruits. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been used in traditional medicine for its antioxidant properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and medicinal purposes.
