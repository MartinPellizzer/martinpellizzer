Crataegus fulleriana f. magniflora

Crataegus fulleriana f. magniflora, commonly known as the Chinese hawthorn, is a cultivar of the hawthorn plant native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that bloom in spring, making it a popular ornamental plant. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a smooth or slightly toothed margin, and the plant typically grows as a multi-stemmed shrub. Its fruit is a small, red-pink berry that ripens in late summer, providing food for wildlife.

This variety is valued for its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature, thriving in a variety of soil conditions.
