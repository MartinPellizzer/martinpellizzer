Chichipia

Chichipia, commonly known as the "peach of the Andes," is a tropical plant native to South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, featuring simple, alternate leaves that are often elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, followed by edible fruits resembling small peaches. The fruit has a thin, fuzzy skin and a sweet, juicy interior, making it a popular fruit in its native region.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family, which also includes apples, cherries, and plums.
