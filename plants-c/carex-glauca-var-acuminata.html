Carex glauca var. acuminata

Carex glauca var. acuminata, commonly known as blue sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its glaucous blue-gray foliage and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers. It typically grows in moist to wet soils in shaded to partially shaded environments.

Carex glauca var. acuminata is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
