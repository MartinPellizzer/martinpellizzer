Cordylophorum suffruticosum

Cordylophorum suffruticosum, commonly known as the Indian nettle or Indian arrowroot, is a flowering plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. The plant typically grows in wet, marshy areas and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, stinging hairs, similar to those of the nettle.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and has various applications in local cultures.
