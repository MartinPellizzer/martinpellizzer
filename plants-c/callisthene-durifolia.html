Callisthene durifolia

Callisthene durifolia, commonly known as the hard-leaved callisthene, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its rigid, leathery leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid conditions.
