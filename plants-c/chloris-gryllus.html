Chloris gryllus

Chloris gryllus, commonly known as the grasshopper grass, is a species of flowering plant in the Poaceae family.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in a compact, mat-like form, often found in dry, sandy soils. It has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Chloris gryllus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
