Carex incurviformis var. danaensis

Carex incurviformis var. danaensis, commonly known as Dana's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their ecological importance in wetland habitats. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, grass-like leaves that curve inward, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The inflorescence consists of a spicate cluster of small, wind-pollinated flowers, typical of many sedges.
