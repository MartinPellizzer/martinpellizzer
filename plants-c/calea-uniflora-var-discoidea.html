Calea uniflora var. discoidea

Calea uniflora var. discoidea, commonly known as the "moonflower" or "flower of the night," is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant features a tall, branching stem with lance-shaped, dark green leaves. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sun for optimal growth.
