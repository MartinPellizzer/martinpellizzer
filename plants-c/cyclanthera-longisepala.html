Cyclanthera longisepala

Cyclanthera longisepala, commonly known as the long-sepaled bitter gourd, is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other gourds and cucurbits. The plant produces elongated, ridged fruits that are typically green and have a bitter taste. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and the flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
