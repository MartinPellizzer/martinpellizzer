Castela alaternifolia

Castela alaternifolia, commonly known as the Portuguese heather, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant has needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Castela alaternifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
