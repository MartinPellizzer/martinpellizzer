Chrysoglossum villosum

Chrysoglossum villosum, commonly known as the hairy chrysoglossum, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features dense clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, giving it a delicate and dainty appearance. The plant has soft, hairy leaves that are typically green, providing a textured contrast to its bright blooms. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its charming floral display.
