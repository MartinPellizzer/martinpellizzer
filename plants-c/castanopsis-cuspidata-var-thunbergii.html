Castanopsis cuspidata var. thunbergii

Castanopsis cuspidata var. thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese chinquapin, is a deciduous evergreen tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and chestnuts, and is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage. The tree produces small, reddish-brown nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which mature over several years. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation projects.
