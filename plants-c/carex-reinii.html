Carex reinii

Carex reinii, commonly known as the rein's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This species typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Carex reinii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
