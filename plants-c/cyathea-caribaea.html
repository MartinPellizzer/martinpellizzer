Cyathea caribaea

Cyathea caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Caribbean region.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds. The plant has a robust rhizome system that allows it to thrive in moist, shaded environments. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the undersides of its fronds.

Cyathea caribaea plays an important ecological role in tropical forests by contributing to soil stability and providing habitat for various organisms.
