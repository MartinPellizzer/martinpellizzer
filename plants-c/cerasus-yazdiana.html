Cerasus yazdiana

Cerasus yazdiana, commonly known as the Persian cherry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, including parts of Iran and Afghanistan. The plant is characterized by its showy, single or clustered pink to red flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are ovate and have a slightly serrated edge, while the fruit is a small, red drupe that ripens in late summer.

This ornamental plant is valued for its beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its seasonal floral display.
