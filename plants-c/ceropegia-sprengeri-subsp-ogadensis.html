Ceropegia sprengeri subsp. ogadensis

Ceropegia sprengeri subsp.

ogadensis, commonly known as the African violet or angel's wings, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa. It features fleshy, branching stems that often trail or climb, and its leaves are typically thick and oval-shaped.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are usually pale green or yellow, with a unique structure that attracts specific pollinators. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant.
