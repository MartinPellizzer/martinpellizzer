Condea iodantha

Condea iodantha, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as wetlands and stream banks. The plant is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a silvery appearance.

Condea iodantha is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
