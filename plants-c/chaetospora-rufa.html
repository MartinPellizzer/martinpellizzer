Chaetospora rufa

Chaetospora rufa, commonly known as the red-spored cup fungus, is a species of ascomycete fungus belonging to the family Calosporiaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive red to orange spore masses, which are produced in cup-shaped fruiting bodies. The fungus typically grows on decaying wood, particularly in moist, temperate environments. Its spores are dispersed by wind, contributing to its reproductive success in suitable habitats.

Due to its specific ecological niche, it is often found in forested areas where dead hardwoods are present.
