Clematis leschenaultiana var. angustifolia

Clematis leschenaultiana var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters, supported by hooked tendrils. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of three to five oval-shaped leaflets, and are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vigorous growth and seasonal floral display.
