Cucumis melo subvar. cultus

Cucumis melo subvar. cultus, commonly known as the cantaloupe, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its rough, netted rind and sweet, orange-fleshed interior. The plant is a trailing vine that produces large, yellowish flowers. Its fruit develops from a single ovary and is typically harvested when the rind turns a deep orange color.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
