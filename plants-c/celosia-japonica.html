Celosia japonica

Celosia japonica, commonly known as the Japanese flame flower, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cone-shaped or spike-like inflorescences that come in vibrant red, orange, and yellow hues. The plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are upright and sturdy.

Celosia japonica thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical gardens.
