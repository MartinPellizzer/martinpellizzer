Ceiba rivieri

Ceiba rivieri, commonly known as the Sierra Leone ceiba, is a tall, fast-growing tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, light-gray bark and large, palmate leaves. The tree produces showy, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds with silky fibers.

Ceiba rivieri is often found in moist lowland forests and is valued for its timber and ecological importance.
