Carex stipitinux

Carex stipitinux, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. Native to moist habitats such as wet meadows and stream banks, it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps, making it a valuable component of wetland ecosystems.
