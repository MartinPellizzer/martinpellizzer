Camellia glaberrima

Camellia glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth camellia, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in wet, acidic soils along riverbanks and swamps. The plant produces showy, camellia-like flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, which bloom in the late winter to early spring. Its leaves are glossy and elliptical, providing ornamental value throughout the year.

Camellia glaberrima is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in humid climates.
