Cyclolobium louveira

Cyclolobium louveira, commonly known as the Brazilian coral tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its ornamental value makes it popular in horticulture, though it requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, contributing to soil fertility.
