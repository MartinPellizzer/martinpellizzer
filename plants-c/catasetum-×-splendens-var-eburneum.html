Catasetum × splendens var. eburneum

Catasetum × splendens var. eburneum, commonly known as the golden cattleya, is a hybrid orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, with a distinctive dark throat. The plant produces a single, erect stem that bears several blooms, each with a unique, intricate structure. Its flowers are pollinated by specific bee species, showcasing a fascinating example of co-evolution.

This variety is highly valued in horticulture for its vibrant colors and ornamental appeal.
