Celastrus mucronatus

Celastrus mucronatus, commonly known as the narrow-tipped staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by orange-red berries containing toxic seeds.

Its rapid growth habit makes it an effective but sometimes invasive species in its native habitats.
