Cinnamomum brevipedunculatum

Cinnamomum brevipedunculatum, commonly known as the small-bracted cinnamon, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and for their essential oils.

It typically grows in humid, tropical lowland areas and is valued for its aromatic properties.
