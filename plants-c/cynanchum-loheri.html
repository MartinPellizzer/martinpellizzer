Cynanchum loheri

Cynanchum loheri, commonly known as the Chinese milkweed or loher's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, and its fruit is a follicle containing numerous seeds. Cynanchum loheri is known for its milky sap, which is often toxic and can cause skin irritation.

It is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
