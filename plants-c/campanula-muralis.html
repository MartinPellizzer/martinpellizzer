Campanula muralis

Campanula muralis, commonly known as the wall bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or wall crevices, thriving in well-drained, alkaline soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often sprawling or trailing.

Campanula muralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to colonize difficult environments.
