Comaropsis doniana

Comaropsis doniana, commonly known as the white thistle or donian thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, typically found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features a robust, upright stem with spiny leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are arranged in a compact, spherical head.

Comaropsis doniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
