Coburgia venusta

Coburgia venusta, commonly known as the golden crown of the Andes, is a striking plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and spiny stems. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.

Coburgia venusta is also noted for its ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems.
