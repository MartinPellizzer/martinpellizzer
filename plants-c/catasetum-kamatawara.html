Catasetum kamatawara

Catasetum kamatawara, commonly known as the Kamatawara orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a single, showy flower that can last for several weeks.

The botanical features of Catasetum kamatawara make it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts due to its unique appearance and relatively easy cultivation requirements.
