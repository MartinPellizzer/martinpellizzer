Cunonia bopopensis

Cunonia bopopensis, commonly known as the Bopopensis bush, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and often pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and it produces small, yellow flowers.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and its potential use in traditional medicine.
