Carum carvi var. vulgare

Carum carvi var. vulgare, commonly known as wild caraway, is a biennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and pinnately compound leaves with a strong, aromatic scent. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early summer. Its fruits are cylindrical, dry, and contain a single seed, typical of the Apiaceae family.

Wild caraway is often found in disturbed soils and is valued for its medicinal and culinary uses.
