Crepidorhopalon uvens

Crepidorhopalon uvens, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its long flowering period and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
