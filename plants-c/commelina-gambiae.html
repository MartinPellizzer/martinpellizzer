Commelina gambiae

Commelina gambiae, commonly known as the Gambian spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its erect stem and striking blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped with a waxy surface, and it produces small, three-lobed bracts that enclose the flowers.

Commelina gambiae is often found in disturbed habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
