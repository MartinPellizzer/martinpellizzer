Cyperus naguensis

Cyperus naguensis, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Africa and Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pale green or brown.

Cyperus naguensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
