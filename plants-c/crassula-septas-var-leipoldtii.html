Crassula septas var. leipoldtii

Crassula septas var. leipoldtii, commonly known as the Leipoldt's Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit. The plant features fleshy, thick leaves that are typically green or tinged with red, depending on light exposure. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink during the growing season.

This species is well-suited for rock gardens and arid environments due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
