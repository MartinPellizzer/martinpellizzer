Cyanthillium cinereum var. ludens

Cyanthillium cinereum var. ludens, commonly known as the silverleaf sandmat, is a low-growing annual plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features silver-gray foliage covered in fine, dense hairs that help reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fibrous roots allow it to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils, making it well adapted to harsh desert conditions.

The plant's unique appearance and ecological role make it an important component of its native habitat.
