Croton eluteria subsp. homolepidus

Croton eluteria subsp.

homolepidus, commonly known as the golden croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored leaves that range from yellow to orange and red.

The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, known for its diverse and often ornamental species. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a distinctive, palmately lobed shape.

The plant is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant.
