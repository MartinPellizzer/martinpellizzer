Cassuvium acuminatum

Cassuvium acuminatum, commonly known as the sharp-pointed cassuvium, is a unique plant species characterized by its elongated, spiky foliage.

It thrives in arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as thick, waxy leaves to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of purple and white, typically blooming during the cooler months. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its striking appearance and resilience, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
