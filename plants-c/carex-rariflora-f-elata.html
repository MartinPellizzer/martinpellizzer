Carex rariflora f. elata

Carex rariflora f. elata, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets of brownish flowers. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

The inflorescence is often elevated on long, slender stems, making it visually distinct within its habitat.
