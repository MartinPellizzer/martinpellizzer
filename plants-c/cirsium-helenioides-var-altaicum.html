Cirsium helenioides var. altaicum

Cirsium helenioides var. altaicum, commonly known as the mountain thistle, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It has a rosette growth form with thick, hairy stems that help reduce water loss.

This variety is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
