Campanula racemosa var. arctiflora

Campanula racemosa var. arctiflora, commonly known as the Arctic bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are adapted to cold climates and can tolerate poor soil conditions. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environments.
