Chenopodium rubrum var. botryoides

Chenopodium rubrum var. botryoides, commonly known as tumbleweed, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, greenish flowers that appear in clusters. The plant is native to the western United States and parts of Canada, where it thrives in dry, disturbed soils. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive nature in some regions.

The plant's fibrous roots and ability to survive in harsh conditions make it a resilient species.
