Colchicum boissieri

Colchicum boissieri, commonly known as the Armenian colchic, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It belongs to the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its striking, nodding flowers that bloom in late summer or early autumn. The plant produces a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves and has a short, sturdy stem. Its flowers are typically purple or pink and are surrounded by specialized bracts.

Colchicum boissieri is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
