Calamagrostis suksdorfii var. luxurians

Calamagrostis suksdorfii var. luxurians, commonly known as the Siberian feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to Siberia and parts of northern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender culms and feathery plumes that appear in late summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its ornamental value comes from its delicate, silver-green foliage and dramatic plumes that add visual interest to gardens.

It is often used in naturalistic landscapes for its ability to tolerate cold climates and poor soil conditions.
