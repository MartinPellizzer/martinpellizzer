Coronilla emerus lusus monophylla

Coronilla emerus lusus monophylla, commonly known as the single-leaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive single leaf and small, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. It is often found in Mediterranean climates and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Its flowers bloom in early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
