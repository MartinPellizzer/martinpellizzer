Cannaboides

Cannaboides, commonly known as cannabis, is a genus of flowering plants in the Cannabaceae family.

It includes several species, the most well-known being Cannabis sativa, Cannabis indica, and Cannabis ruderalis. These plants are characterized by their tall, fibrous stalks and heart-shaped leaves. They produce resinous buds containing cannabinoids such as THC and CBD.

The botanical features of cannaboides make them unique in both medicinal and recreational contexts.
