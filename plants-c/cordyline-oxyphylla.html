Cordyline oxyphylla

Cordyline oxyphylla, commonly known as the red-veined cordyline, is a striking plant native to New Zealand.

It is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, upright stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically bright green with prominent red veins, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Cordyline oxyphylla is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a houseplant.
