Croton longiracemosus

Croton longiracemosus, commonly known as the long-branched croton, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its branched, often pendulous inflorescences and striking, varied leaf patterns. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are typically thick and waxy, with a broad range of colors from green to reddish-brown.

Croton longiracemosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
