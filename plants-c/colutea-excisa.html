Colutea excisa

Colutea excisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved brookweed, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by pod-like fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Colutea excisa is often found in calcareous soils and is known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
