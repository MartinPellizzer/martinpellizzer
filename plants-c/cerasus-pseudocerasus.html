Cerasus pseudocerasus

Cerasus pseudocerasus, commonly known as the Chinese cherry or honghe, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically red or yellow when ripe, with a sweet and tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruit.

The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
