Chamaemelum heterocarpum

Chamaemelum heterocarpum, commonly known as the narrowleaf tansy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, often with a strong, camphor-like scent.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators like bees and butterflies.
