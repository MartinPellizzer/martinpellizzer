Cyperus uliginosus

Cyperus uliginosus, commonly known as the swamp spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its flowering spikes are usually reddish or purple, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Cyperus uliginosus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
