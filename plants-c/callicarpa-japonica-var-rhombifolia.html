Callicarpa japonica var. rhombifolia

Callicarpa japonica var. rhombifolia, commonly known as the Japanese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to red berries that appear in clusters, which are a key feature of the plant. The leaves are ovate to rhombic in shape, with a smooth texture and a glossy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
