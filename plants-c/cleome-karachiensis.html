Cleome karachiensis

Cleome karachiensis, commonly known as the Jerusalem cabbage or spider flower, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and South Asia.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, papery bracts that resemble petals. The plant produces small, green, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and extended blooming period.
