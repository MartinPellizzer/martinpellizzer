Coleosanthus cavanillesii

Coleosanthus cavanillesii, commonly known as the St. Vincent's spider orchid, is a rare and striking orchid native to the island of St. Vincent in the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its unique, spider-like appearance, with long, thin sepals and petals that resemble the legs of a spider. The plant typically grows in rocky, coastal areas and thrives in humid, tropical conditions. Its flowers are usually pale green or yellow, with a distinct, intricate pattern that aids in pollination by specific insects.

Due to its limited habitat and threats from environmental changes, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
