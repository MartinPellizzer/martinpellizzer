Cistus salviifolius var. rierae

Cistus salviifolius var. rierae, commonly known as the Riera's sage, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-green foliage and showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its leaves are often used in traditional herbal remedies due to their medicinal properties.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
