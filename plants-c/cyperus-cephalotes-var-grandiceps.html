Cyperus cephalotes var. grandiceps

Cyperus cephalotes var. grandiceps, commonly known as the tall flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its upright, slender stems are often topped with clusters of small, spike-like flowers.

This variety is distinguished by its tall stature and robust growth, making it a notable species in its habitat.
