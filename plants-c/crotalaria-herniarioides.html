Crotalaria herniarioides

Crotalaria herniarioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are often used as a protein source in some regions, and the plant is also used in traditional medicine.

Crotalaria herniarioides is typically found in open habitats, including disturbed soils and along roadsides.
