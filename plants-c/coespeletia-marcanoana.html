Coespeletia marcanoana

Coespeletia marcanoana, commonly known as the Marcanoan cholla, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its adaptations to drought include reduced leaf surfaces and thick, waxy cuticles.

Coespeletia marcanoana plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing shelter and food for various desert organisms.
