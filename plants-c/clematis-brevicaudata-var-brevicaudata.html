Clematis brevicaudata var. brevicaudata

Clematis brevicaudata var. brevicaudata, commonly known as the short-tailed clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white or pale blue with a distinctive short, rounded spur. The plant produces small, showy blooms in late spring to early summer, often with a sweet, spicy fragrance. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and hardiness.
