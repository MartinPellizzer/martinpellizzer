Chabraea nutans

Chabraea nutans, commonly known as the hanging cypress, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping, pendulous branches that give it a distinctive, cascading appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, cone-like fruits. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and typically elliptical in shape.

Chabraea nutans is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique form and adaptability to various growing conditions.
