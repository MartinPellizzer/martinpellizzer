Cenchrus spinifex

Cenchrus spinifex, commonly known as sandbur, is a flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the warm temperate and subtropical regions of the Americas. The plant is characterized by its spiny, bristly seeds that are adapted for dispersal by attachment to animals or clothing. It thrives in sandy or disturbed soils and is often found in open habitats such as fields and roadsides.

Its growth habit is typically low-growing with a dense, spreading crown.
