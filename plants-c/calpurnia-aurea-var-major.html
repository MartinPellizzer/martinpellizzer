Calpurnia aurea var. major

Calpurnia aurea var. major, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with multiple small leaflets and produces small, woody fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental species in warm climates.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
