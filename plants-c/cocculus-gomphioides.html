Cocculus gomphioides

Cocculus gomphioides, commonly known as the Indian snakeroot, is a flowering plant native to South Asia.

It belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is characterized by its climbing habit and compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by distinctive, inflated fruit. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.
