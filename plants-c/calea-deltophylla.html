Calea deltophylla

Calea deltophylla, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

The flowers emit a sweet fragrance at night, enhancing its role in the ecosystem and making it a popular choice for night-blooming gardens.
