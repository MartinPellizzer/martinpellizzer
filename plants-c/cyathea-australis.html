Cyathea australis

Cyathea australis, commonly known as the southern tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant is evergreen and thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically in forest understories. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting its upright growth.

Cyathea australis plays an important ecological role in New Zealand's ecosystems, providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
