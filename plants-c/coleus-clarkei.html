Coleus clarkei

Coleus clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's coleus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the mint family, Lamiaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage. The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and display a wide range of colors, including red, purple, and variegated patterns. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is relatively easy to grow and can be propagated through stem cuttings.
