Carex dregeana var. major

Carex dregeana var. major, commonly known as the giant sedgewort, is a perennial sedge native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and dense clusters of spikelets that bear small, triangular flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along riverbanks. Its foliage is dark green and stiff, with a distinctive appearance that makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
