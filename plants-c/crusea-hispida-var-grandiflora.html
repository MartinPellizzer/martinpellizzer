Crusea hispida var. grandiflora

Crusea hispida var. grandiflora, commonly known as the woolly crusea, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for garden cultivation.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and native plant landscapes.
