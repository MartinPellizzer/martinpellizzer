Curcuma candida

Curcuma candida, commonly known as white turmeric, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, and its leaves are broad and lance-shaped. The rhizomes of Curcuma candida are used in traditional medicine and have a mild, slightly bitter flavor.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
