Coleus uliginosus

Coleus uliginosus, commonly known as swamp coleus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from red to purple, which bloom in clusters atop slender stems. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands and forest understories. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and can vary in color from green to reddish hues.

Coleus uliginosus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to add color to shaded garden spaces.
