Cistus × escartianus

Cistus × escartianus, commonly known as the Spanish rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the genus Cistus, which includes various species of evergreen shrubs characterized by their aromatic leaves and showy flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, appearing in late spring to early summer.

Cistus × escartianus is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in harsh, arid environments.
