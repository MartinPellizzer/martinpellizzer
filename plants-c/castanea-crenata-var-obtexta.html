Castanea crenata var. obtexta

Castanea crenata var. obtexta, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and spiny husks that enclose the edible nuts. The plant thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its nuts are often used in culinary applications and are also valued for their ornamental appeal.

The species is notable for its resilience and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
