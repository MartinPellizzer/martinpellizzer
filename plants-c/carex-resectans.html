Carex resectans

Carex resectans, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and three-ranked inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are often subtended by bracts.

Carex resectans plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates and birds.
