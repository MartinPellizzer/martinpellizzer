Cirsium arizonicum var. tenuisectum

Cirsium arizonicum var. tenuisectum, commonly known as the slenderleaf thistle, is a perennial plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its spiny, gray-green leaves that are narrow and finely divided, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers in late summer, which are typically arranged in a spherical inflorescence. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in desert or arid environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for pollinators and a component of local ecosystems.
