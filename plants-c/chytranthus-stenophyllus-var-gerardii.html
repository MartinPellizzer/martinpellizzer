Chytranthus stenophyllus var. gerardii

Chytranthus stenophyllus var. gerardii, commonly known as Gerard's chytranthus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. It is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it forms dense, low-growing mats.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
