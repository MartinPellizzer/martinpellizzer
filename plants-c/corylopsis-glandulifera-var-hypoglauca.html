Corylopsis glandulifera var. hypoglauca

Corylopsis glandulifera var. hypoglauca, commonly known as the pale-green corylopsis, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a pale green, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers in early spring, which are often fragrant. These flowers are followed by small, hairy fruits that mature in late summer.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
