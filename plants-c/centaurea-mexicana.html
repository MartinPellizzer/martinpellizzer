Centaurea mexicana

Centaurea mexicana, commonly known as Mexican knapweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has hairy, gray-green foliage. Its flowers are bright yellow with purple markings, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Centaurea mexicana is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
