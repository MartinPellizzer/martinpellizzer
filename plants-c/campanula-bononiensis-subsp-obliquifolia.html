Campanula bononiensis subsp. obliquifolia

Campanula bononiensis subsp.

obliquifolia, commonly known as the oblique bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Europe. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers.

The leaves are typically lanceolate and may have a slightly hairy texture. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to thrive in temperate climates.
