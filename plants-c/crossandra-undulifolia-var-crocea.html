Crossandra undulifolia var. crocea

Crossandra undulifolia var. crocea, commonly known as the golden crossandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a striking visual appeal to gardens. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variant is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
