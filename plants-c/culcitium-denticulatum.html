Culcitium denticulatum

Culcitium denticulatum, commonly known as the toothed culcitium, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araliaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, toothed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in umbels, typical of its family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
