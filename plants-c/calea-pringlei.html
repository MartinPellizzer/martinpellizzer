Calea pringlei

Calea pringlei, commonly known as the "flower of the night," is a striking plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers that open at night and close in the morning. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its unique floral behavior. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Calea pringlei is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used by indigenous communities for various health purposes.
