Carex acuta f. zygostachya

Carex acuta f. zygostachya, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species Carex acuta.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging inflorescence, which gives it its common name. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a narrow, cylindrical shape, and it produces small, clustered flowers in early summer.

The plant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
