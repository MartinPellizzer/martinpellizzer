Chenopodium desertorum subsp. desertorum

Chenopodium desertorum subsp.

desertorum, commonly known as desert goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a herbaceous annual or biennial species that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils.

The plant has alternate, ovate to rhomboidal leaves with a rough, sandpaper-like texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are arranged in clusters and are pollinated by wind.

The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, drought-prone environments, often going dormant during periods of extreme heat or water scarcity.
