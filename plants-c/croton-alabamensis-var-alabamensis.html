Croton alabamensis var. alabamensis

Croton alabamensis var. alabamensis, commonly known as the Alabama croton, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant, palmate leaves that display a range of colors, including green, red, and orange, depending on the season and light exposure. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
