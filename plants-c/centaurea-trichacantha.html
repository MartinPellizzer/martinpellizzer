Centaurea trichacantha

Centaurea trichacantha, commonly known as the thorned centaurea, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and leaves, which are covered in fine, bristly hairs. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands.

Centaurea trichacantha is a hardy perennial that can spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
