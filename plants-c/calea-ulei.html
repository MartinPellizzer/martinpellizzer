Calea ulei

Calea ulei, commonly known as the "Mexican nightshade," is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and tobacco. Calea ulei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported sedative properties.

Its blooming period typically occurs in the evening, adding to its unique botanical appeal.
