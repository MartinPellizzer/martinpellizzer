Cayaponia racemosa var. subintegrifolia

Cayaponia racemosa var. subintegrifolia, commonly known as the white cayaponia, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated margins and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and showy inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
