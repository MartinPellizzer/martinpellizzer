Cacalia eupatorifolia

Cacalia eupatorifolia, commonly known as the roughleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has palmately lobed leaves that are rough to the touch, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Cacalia eupatorifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
