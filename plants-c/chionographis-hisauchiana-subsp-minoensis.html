Chionographis hisauchiana subsp. minoensis

Chionographis hisauchiana subsp.

minoensis, commonly known as the Mino Mountain snowbell, is a rare and delicate plant native to Japan. It is characterized by its white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its leaves are narrow and lanceolate, with a silvery underside that reflects light.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in specialized gardens.
