Cirsium rhizocephalum

Cirsium rhizocephalum, commonly known as the root-headed thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features a thick, woody rootstock and produces spiny, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, forming dense clusters at the top of the plant.

Cirsium rhizocephalum is adapted to grow in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.
