Centaurea carbonata

Centaurea carbonata, commonly known as the carbonated centaury, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and features striking blue to purple flowers with yellow centers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, while its stems are often branched and covered in fine pubescence.

Centaurea carbonata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
