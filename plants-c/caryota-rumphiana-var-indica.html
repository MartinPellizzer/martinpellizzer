Caryota rumphiana var. indica

Caryota rumphiana var. indica, commonly known as the Indian palm or the fishtail palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that resemble the tail of a fish, giving it its common name. The plant is native to Southeast Asia, including India, Malaysia, and Indonesia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into edible fruits, which are often consumed by both humans and wildlife.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
