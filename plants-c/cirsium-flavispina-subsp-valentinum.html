Cirsium flavispina subsp. valentinum

Cirsium flavispina subsp.

valentinum, commonly known as the yellow thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to central and southern Europe. It is characterized by its spiny, glandular leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in dry meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils, preferring full sun. Its leaves are lanceolate with serrated edges and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

This subspecies is often found in disturbed habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
