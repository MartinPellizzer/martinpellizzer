Campylocentrum pugioniforme

Campylocentrum pugioniforme, commonly known as the swordleaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Campylocentrum pugioniforme is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
