Carex svenonis

Carex svenonis, commonly known as the Svenon's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in a panicle inflorescence. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex svenonis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wetland ecosystems.
