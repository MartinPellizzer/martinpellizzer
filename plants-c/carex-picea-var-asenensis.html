Carex picea var. asenensis

Carex picea var. asenensis, commonly known as the black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant features dark green, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its flowering stems produce small, clustered spikes of pale green to brownish flowers, which are wind-pollinated.
