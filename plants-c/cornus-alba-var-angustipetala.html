Cornus alba var. angustipetala

Cornus alba var. angustipetala, commonly known as narrow-leaf dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with a reddish-brown bark that peels in thin layers. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early summer, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil conditions.
