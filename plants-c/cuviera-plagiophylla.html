Cuviera plagiophylla

Cuviera plagiophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to parts of Central America and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, or white. The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its botanical features include a robust stem system and fibrous roots, which help it adapt to various environmental conditions.

Cuviera plagiophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
