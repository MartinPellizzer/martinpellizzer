Centaurea panormitana subsp. umbrosa

Centaurea panormitana subsp.

umbrosa, commonly known as the shaded centaurea, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flowers.

The subspecies is distinguished by its darker, more compact foliage compared to other forms of Centaurea panormitana. It thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments, often found in woodland areas and rocky slopes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with moist, well-drained soil.
