Centaurea belouinii

Centaurea belouinii, commonly known as the Belouin's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and linear, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and thrives in full sunlight. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its composite flower heads.

Centaurea belouinii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
