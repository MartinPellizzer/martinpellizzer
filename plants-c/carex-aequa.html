Carex aequa

Carex aequa, commonly known as the smooth sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex aequa is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
