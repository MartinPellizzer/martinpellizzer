Chionanthus bumelioides subsp. bumelioides

Chionanthus bumelioides subsp.

bumelioides, commonly known as the white fringeflower, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and hairy on the underside. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
