Cereus oxypetalus

Cereus oxypetalus, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stem and clusters of white, night-blooming flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water requirements. Its flowers open only during the night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as bats and moths.

The plant's striking appearance and unique blooming habit make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts.
