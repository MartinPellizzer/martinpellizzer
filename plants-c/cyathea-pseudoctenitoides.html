Cyathea pseudoctenitoides

Cyathea pseudoctenitoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizomes produce roots that anchor it to the soil, while its fronds are used for photosynthesis.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
