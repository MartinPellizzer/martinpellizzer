Cirsium eriophorum

Cirsium eriophorum, commonly known as the cotton thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its woolly, fuzzy leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in late summer to early autumn, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating skin conditions and respiratory ailments.
