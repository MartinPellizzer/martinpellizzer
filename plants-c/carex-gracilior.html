Carex gracilior

Carex gracilior, commonly known as slender sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green hue, and it produces small, clustered flowers in early summer.

Carex gracilior plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
