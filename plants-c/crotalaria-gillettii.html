Crotalaria gillettii

Crotalaria gillettii, commonly known as Gillett's cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it beneficial for soil fertility. It grows as an annual herb, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Its seeds are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines in some parts of Africa.
