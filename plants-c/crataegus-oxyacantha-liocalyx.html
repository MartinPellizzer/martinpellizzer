Crataegus oxyacantha liocalyx

Crataegus oxyacantha liocalyx, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptical leaves with serrated edges, and small, white to pinkish flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the autumn, which are an important food source for birds and mammals.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and rocky areas.
