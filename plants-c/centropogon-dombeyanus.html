Centropogon dombeyanus

Centropogon dombeyanus, commonly known as the Dombey's grassveld, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or pink in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
