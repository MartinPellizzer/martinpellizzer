Capparis aphylla

Capparis aphylla, commonly known as the leafless caper, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves, with the stems being green and photosynthetic. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its adaptation in harsh climates.
