Cassia heteroloba

Cassia heteroloba, commonly known as the yellow cassia, is a shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is part of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and it has a deep root system that helps it thrive in arid conditions. Cassia heteroloba is often found in coastal and sandy soils, and it plays a role in stabilizing dunes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation in suitable climates.
