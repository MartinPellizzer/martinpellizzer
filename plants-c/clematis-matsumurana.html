Clematis matsumurana

Clematis matsumurana, commonly known as the Japanese clematis, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets, and it thrives in partial shade to full sun.

Clematis matsumurana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a decorative vine.
