Cymbidium atropurpureum var. olivaceum

Cymbidium atropurpureum var. olivaceum, commonly known as the purple-tinged orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of pendant flowers. The flowers are typically purple or lavender in color, with a distinctive yellow or orange lip. This variety is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.

It prefers cool, humid conditions and is a popular choice for orchid enthusiasts.
