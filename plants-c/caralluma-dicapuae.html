Caralluma dicapuae

Caralluma dicapuae, commonly known as the desert nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades such as tomatoes and potatoes. The plant is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and small, star-shaped flowers that bloom at night. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water.
