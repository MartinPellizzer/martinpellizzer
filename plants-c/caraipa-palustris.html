Caraipa palustris

Caraipa palustris, commonly known as the swamp caraipa, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the wetlands of South America.

It typically grows in moist to waterlogged soils, often found in marshy areas and along the edges of rivers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikes. Caraipa palustris plays an important role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems by preventing erosion and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to the fluctuating water levels and nutrient-rich environments of its native habitat.
