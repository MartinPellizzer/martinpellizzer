Carex plantaginea

Carex plantaginea, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, three-lobed flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

Carex plantaginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
