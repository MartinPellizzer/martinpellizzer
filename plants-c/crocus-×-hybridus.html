Crocus × hybridus

Crocus × hybridus, commonly known as the garden crocus, is a hybrid species within the Crocus genus.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant produces large, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and purple, often with a distinctive purple or black marking at the base. It is a bulbous perennial that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

The botanical features of Crocus × hybridus make it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
