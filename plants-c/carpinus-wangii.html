Carpinus wangii

Carpinus wangii, commonly known as the Yunnan hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
