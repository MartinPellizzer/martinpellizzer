Caryota ophiopellis

Caryota ophiopellis, commonly known as the golden palm, is a species of palm native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into clusters of edible berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
