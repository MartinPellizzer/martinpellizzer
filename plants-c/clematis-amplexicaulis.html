Clematis amplexicaulis

Clematis amplexicaulis, commonly known as the climbing clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped, palmate leaves that clasp the stems, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces showy, blue to violet flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil.

Clematis amplexicaulis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to climb trellises or fences.
