Centaurea pomeliana subsp. rouxiana

Centaurea pomeliana subsp.

rouxiana, commonly known as the Roux's cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slight pubescence.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
