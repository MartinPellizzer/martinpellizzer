Carmichaelia monroi var. longicarinata

Carmichaelia monroi var. longicarinata, commonly known as the long-ribbed kowhai, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, ribbed pods. The plant produces showy yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting native birds and insects. It typically grows in coastal and lowland areas, preferring well-drained soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
