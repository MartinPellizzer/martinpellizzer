Cyperus difformis

Cyperus difformis, commonly known as umbrella sedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to Africa and thrives in wetland environments such as marshes and pond margins. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color. It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive, feathery appearance.

Cyperus difformis is also known for its ability to tolerate a wide range of soil conditions and water levels.
