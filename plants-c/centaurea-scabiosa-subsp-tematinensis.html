Centaurea scabiosa subsp. tematinensis

Centaurea scabiosa subsp.

tematinensis, commonly known as the Temptin's Scotch thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Alps. It is characterized by its spiny, purple-blue flower heads and narrow, gray-green leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, bracteate flowers that bloom in summer, attracting various pollinators.

This subspecies is notable for its hardy nature and adaptability to high-altitude environments.
