Corylus colurna var. macrochlamys

Corylus colurna var. macrochlamys, commonly known as the Turkish hazelnut, is a variant of the European hazelnut.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region. The plant features compound leaves with 5-9 elliptical leaflets and produces clusters of small, edible nuts enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nut production.

The leaves turn a striking yellow in autumn, adding to its aesthetic appeal.
