Crossopetalum gentiana

Crossopetalum gentiana, commonly known as the blue gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically tubular in shape and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant belongs to the family Gentianaceae, which includes many species known for their vibrant floral displays. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.

The leaves are usually opposite and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.
