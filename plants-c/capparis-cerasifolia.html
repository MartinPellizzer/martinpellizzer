Capparis cerasifolia

Capparis cerasifolia, commonly known as the sour fig or Indian gooseberry, is a flowering plant belonging to the Capparaceae family.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit, with woody stems and thorny branches. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, oval-shaped berry that turns red when ripe, containing a single seed.

Native to regions of southern and eastern Asia, it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.
