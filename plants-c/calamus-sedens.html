Calamus sedens

Calamus sedens, commonly known as the sitting calamus, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that often grow in dense clusters, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. Its flowering occurs in the form of small, clustered inflorescences, which develop into spadices.

Calamus sedens is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in wetlands or along water edges.
