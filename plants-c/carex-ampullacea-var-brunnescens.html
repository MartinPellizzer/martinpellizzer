Carex ampullacea var. brunnescens

Carex ampullacea var. brunnescens, commonly known as the brown-stem sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This variety is characterized by its dark brown, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds.

The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated, contributing to its ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
