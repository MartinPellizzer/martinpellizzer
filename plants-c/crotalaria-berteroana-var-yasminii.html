Crotalaria berteroana var. yasminii

Crotalaria berteroana var. yasminii, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like seed capsules. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its bright yellow flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being ovate and slightly hairy.
