Clematis cirrhosa var. napaulensis

Clematis cirrhosa var. napaulensis, commonly known as the Nepal clematis, is a climbing vine native to the Himalayas.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. The plant produces fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental climbing plants.
