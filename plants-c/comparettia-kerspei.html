Comparettia kerspei

Comparettia kerspei, commonly known as the Kerspey orchid, is a tropical epiphytic plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its compact, pseudobulbous structure with strap-like leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green, often with intricate patterns. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a decorative plant in warm climates.
