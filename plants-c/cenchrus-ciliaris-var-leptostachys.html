Cenchrus ciliaris var. leptostachys

Cenchrus ciliaris var. leptostachys, commonly known as sand dropseed, is a perennial grass native to the warm temperate and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact growth habit, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, fluffy seed heads that are easily dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation. It is often used in landscaping and erosion control due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and agricultural applications.
