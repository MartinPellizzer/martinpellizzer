Campanula sylvatica

Campanula sylvatica, commonly known as the wood violet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and thrives in well-drained, loamy soils. The plant features upright stems with opposite, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are bell-shaped, ranging in color from blue to purple, and bloom in late spring to early summer.

Campanula sylvatica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
