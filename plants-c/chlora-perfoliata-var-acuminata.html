Chlora perfoliata var. acuminata

Chlora perfoliata var. acuminata, commonly known as the acuminated chlora, is a variant of the chlora plant characterized by its pointed leaf tips.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of North America. The leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern and have a succulent texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered near the base of the plant.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
