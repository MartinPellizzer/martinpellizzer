Carex parvibracteata

Carex parvibracteata, commonly known as narrow-bract sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and similar plants. This species is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and the edges of ponds.
