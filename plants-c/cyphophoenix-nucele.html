Cyphophoenix nucele

Cyphophoenix nucele, commonly known as the Nucelifera date palm, is a rare and critically endangered species native to the arid regions of the Middle East.

This palm is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, fuzzy hairs. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible dates, though the fruit is less prominent compared to other date palms. The plant thrives in hot, dry climates with well-drained soil and requires minimal water once established.

Due to habitat loss and overharvesting, conservation efforts are crucial for its survival.
