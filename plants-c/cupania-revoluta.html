Cupania revoluta

Cupania revoluta, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf cupania, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in panicles, typically blooming in the summer months. Its fruit is a small, three-lobed capsule that contains several seeds.

Cupania revoluta is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including wetlands and open woodlands.
