Carex caudispicata var. longiutriculata

Carex caudispicata var. longiutriculata, commonly known as the long-beaked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces distinctive, elongated, spiky seed heads.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
