Carapa grandiflora

Carapa grandiflora, commonly known as wild Copaiba, is a tropical tree native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Meliaceae family and is characterized by its large, simple leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces a valuable resin, which has been used traditionally for its medicinal and aromatic properties. Its bark and seeds are also utilized in various traditional remedies.

Carapa grandiflora plays an important role in the ecological and economic systems of its native regions.
