Chamaesyce intermixta

Chamaesyce intermixta, commonly known as the mixed sand mat, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in dry conditions. Its flowers are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, appearing in clusters along the stems.

Chamaesyce intermixta is well adapted to harsh climates and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
