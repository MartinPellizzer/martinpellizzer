Celastrus luteolus

Celastrus luteolus, commonly known as the yellow star vine, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring. The plant bears bright yellow, star-shaped fruits that contain seeds dispersed by animals. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and traditional herbal medicine.
