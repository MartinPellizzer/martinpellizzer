Cochlospermum incanum

Cochlospermum incanum, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in clusters, often in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces small, round fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental species in warm climates.

Its rapid growth and showy flowers make it a valuable addition to gardens and landscapes.
