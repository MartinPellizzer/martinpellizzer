Cyperus rigens var. impolitus

Cyperus rigens var. impolitus, commonly known as the rough flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often rough to the touch, with a distinctive appearance that resembles a sedge. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, making it a common sight in wetlands and along water bodies.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland management.
