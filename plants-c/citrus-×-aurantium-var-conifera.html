Citrus × aurantium var. conifera

Citrus × aurantium var. conifera, commonly known as the coniferous lemon, is a hybrid citrus plant characterized by its unique conifer-like appearance.

It features needle-like leaves and small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically used for culinary and medicinal purposes. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is valued for its aromatic properties and is often cultivated for its essential oils and zest.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also popular in horticulture.
