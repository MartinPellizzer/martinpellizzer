Cerasus × katonis

Cerasus × katonis, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or Prunus × katonis, is a hybrid species of cherry tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves appear. The plant typically grows as a deciduous tree or shrub, reaching heights of 3 to 10 meters. Its leaves are ovate with a pointed tip and a smooth, dark green upper surface.

The fruit, which is a small, sweet cherry, ripens in late summer and is often used in culinary applications.
