Cyperus pauloensis

Cyperus pauloensis, commonly known as the Brazilian flatsedge, is a species of sedge native to South America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, including marshes and swampy areas. It has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Cyperus pauloensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
