Cephalaria foliosa

Cephalaria foliosa, commonly known as the leafy cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
