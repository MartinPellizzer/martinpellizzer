Commiphora grandifolia var. ambongensis

Commiphora grandifolia var. ambongensis, commonly known as the Ambongensis Myrrh Tree, is a shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic resinous exudate, which is harvested for medicinal and aromatic purposes. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has small, leathery leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, and it produces small, yellowish fruits.

The species is valued for its traditional use in herbal remedies and as a source of frankincense-like resins.
