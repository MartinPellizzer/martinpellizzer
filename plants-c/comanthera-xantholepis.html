Comanthera xantholepis

Comanthera xantholepis, commonly known as the yellow-leaved rock fig, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that are typically yellow or green in color. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are edible and have a sweet, mild flavor. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Comanthera xantholepis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
