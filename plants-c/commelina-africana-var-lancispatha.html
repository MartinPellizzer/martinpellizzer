Commelina africana var. lancispatha

Commelina africana var. lancispatha, commonly known as the African spiderwort, is a herbaceous plant native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It features slender, erect stems that bear alternate, lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers in clusters, which are surrounded by modified leaves called bracts. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

The species is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
