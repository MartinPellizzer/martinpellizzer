Camellia japonica var. longifolia

Camellia japonica var. longifolia, commonly known as the Japanese camellia with long petals, is a species of evergreen shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can be pink, red, or white, with elongated petals that distinguish it from other camellia varieties. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features dark green, leathery leaves. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Camellia japonica var. longifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in traditional Japanese culture.
