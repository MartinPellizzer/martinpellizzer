Cotoneaster nummularius subvar. lindleyi

Cotoneaster nummularius subvar. lindleyi, commonly known as the Lindley's cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to central and southern China.

It features small, rounded, glossy leaves that turn red in the autumn, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces clusters of white flowers in early spring, followed by bright red berries that are attractive to birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a hedging plant.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
