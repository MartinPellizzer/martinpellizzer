Cordia sessilifolia var. tomentosa

Cordia sessilifolia var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly hibiscus, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves that are often covered in fine, white pubescence. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
