Clematis × contorta

Clematis × contorta, commonly known as the Virgin's Bower, is a hybrid vine native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its vigorous growth habit and its ability to climb using its tendrils. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is compound, with small, ovate leaves that provide dense coverage.

Clematis × contorta is relatively hardy, though it benefits from support structures and well-drained soil for optimal growth.
