Cestrum capsulare

Cestrum capsulare, commonly known as the sweet pepper tree, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, often in shades of white, yellow, or pink. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are typically orange or red when mature. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly serrated margin.

Cestrum capsulare is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
