Carissa verticilata

Carissa verticilata, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green in color. The plant produces clusters of yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in spring, followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for warm climates.

Carissa verticilata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and showy flowers.
