Caralluma decora

Caralluma decora, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its fleshy, segmented stems that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the winter months, making it a popular ornamental species. Its leaves are typically modified into spines, and it thrives in well-draining soil with bright, indirect light.

Caralluma decora is relatively easy to care for, requiring minimal water and moderate temperatures.
