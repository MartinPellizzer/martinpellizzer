Chrysophora cinnamomifolia

Chrysophora cinnamomifolia, commonly known as the cinnamon tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils similar to those found in cinnamon. Its leaves are typically elliptical with a glossy surface and a strong, spicy scent.

The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
