Carex flacca

Carex flacca, commonly known as slender sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually pale green to bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, inconspicuous flowers that are often surrounded by bracts.

Carex flacca plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
