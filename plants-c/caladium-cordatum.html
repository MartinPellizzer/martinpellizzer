Caladium cordatum

Caladium cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf caladium, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that come in a variety of colors, including pink, white, and green. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers partial to full shade. Its foliage is the main ornamental feature, making it a popular choice for garden borders and containers.

Caladium cordatum is relatively low maintenance and can be propagated through rhizome division or seeds.
