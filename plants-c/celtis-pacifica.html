Celtis pacifica

Celtis pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by white, fleshy berries that are an important food source for wildlife. Celtis pacifica is adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.

Its resilience and ornamental features make it a valuable addition to native landscapes.
