Cotinus radicans

Cotinus radicans, commonly known as the spreading smoke bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its striking, feathery panicles that turn from pink to red in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually green in summer and turn red or orange in autumn. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Cotinus radicans is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
