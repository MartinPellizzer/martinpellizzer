Cissampelos ovalifolia f. reniformis

Cissampelos ovalifolia f. reniformis, commonly known as the kidney-leaf moonvine, is a variant of the moonvine species characterized by its distinctive kidney-shaped leaves.

This plant is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia, featuring heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. Its flowers are typically yellow with red or orange markings, and it produces small, green, fleshy fruits that are edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
