Caprifolium subspicatum

Caprifolium subspicatum, commonly known as the creeping raspberry.

It is a shrub native to southern Europe and western Asia, characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces red, hairy berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
