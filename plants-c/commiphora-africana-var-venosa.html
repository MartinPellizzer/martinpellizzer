Commiphora africana var. venosa

Commiphora africana var. venosa, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, resinous bark and leaves that are typically alternate and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody capsules containing aromatic seeds. Its resin, often referred to as "myrrh," has been historically used in traditional medicine and incense.

The varietal form 'venosa' is distinguished by its variegated leaf patterns, adding to its ornamental value.
