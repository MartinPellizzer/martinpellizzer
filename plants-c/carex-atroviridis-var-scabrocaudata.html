Carex atroviridis var. scabrocaudata

Carex atroviridis var. scabrocaudata, commonly known as the rough-tailed sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, narrow leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish flowers. This variety is native to North America and thrives in moist to wet environments such as marshes and wetlands. Its foliage is often covered with fine, scabrous hairs, giving it a distinctive texture.

The plant plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
