Campanula glomerata var. grandiflora

Campanula glomerata var. grandiflora, commonly known as the Great Campanula, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and naturalized areas.
