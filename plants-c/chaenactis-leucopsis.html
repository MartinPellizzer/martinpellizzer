Chaenactis leucopsis

Chaenactis leucopsis, commonly known as the white ghostflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, such as rocky soils and open plains. Its white to pale blue flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it has a low, mat-forming growth habit.

The species is adapted to withstand drought and has a deep root system to access water from deeper soil layers.
