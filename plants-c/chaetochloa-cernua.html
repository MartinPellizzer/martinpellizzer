Chaetochloa cernua

Chaetochloa cernua, commonly known as the drooping bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades that often droop downward, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairie and wetland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the summer.

Chaetochloa cernua is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
