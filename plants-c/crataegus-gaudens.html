Crataegus gaudens

Crataegus gaudens, commonly known as the showy crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring vibrant red or orange flowers in early spring and showy red or yellow fruits in the fall. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 feet and has compound leaves with 5 to 9 leaflets. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Crataegus gaudens is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
