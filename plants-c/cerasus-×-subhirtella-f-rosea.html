Cerasus × subhirtella f. rosea

Cerasus × subhirtella f. rosea, commonly known as the pink-leafed cherry or the rosea cherry, is a hybrid cultivar of the Prunus genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive pink or reddish foliage, which contrasts with its typical white or pink flowers. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of 3 to 6 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a serrated margin and a glossy, dark green upper surface.

The flowers appear in clusters and bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens and landscapes.
