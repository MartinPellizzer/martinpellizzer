Caryocar tomentosum

Caryocar tomentosum, commonly known as the Brazilian chestnut or araçá-do-brejo, is a deciduous tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the family Caryocaraceae and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by large, round, woody fruits that contain a single seed. Its fruit is notable for its thick, fibrous outer layer and is often used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
