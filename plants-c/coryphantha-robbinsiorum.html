Coryphantha robbinsiorum

Coryphantha robbinsiorum, commonly known as the Robbins' pincushion cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in white, woolly spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Coryphantha robbinsiorum is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance.
