Chrysanthemum foeniculaceum

Chrysanthemum foeniculaceum, commonly known as the sweet wormwood, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. It is often grown for its ornamental value and has a distinctive, slightly aromatic scent.

Chrysanthemum foeniculaceum is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
