Cordylanthus orcuttianus

Cordylanthus orcuttianus, commonly known as Orcutt's buckthorn, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and the plant is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
