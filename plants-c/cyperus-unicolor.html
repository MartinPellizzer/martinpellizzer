Cyperus unicolor

Cyperus unicolor, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a monocotyledonous plant belonging to the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowers are usually unisexual, with separate male and female structures, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Cyperus unicolor plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
