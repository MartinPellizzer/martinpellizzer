Coix poilanei

Coix poilanei, commonly known as the African marsh grass, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family Poaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along riverbanks. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Coix poilanei is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
