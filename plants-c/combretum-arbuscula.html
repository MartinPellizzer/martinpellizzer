Combretum arbuscula

Combretum arbuscula, commonly known as the dwarf combretum, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and drought-tolerant species suitable for xeriscaping.
