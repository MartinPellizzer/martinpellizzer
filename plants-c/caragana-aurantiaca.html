Caragana aurantiaca

Caragana aurantiaca, commonly known as the red-flowered pea tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright orange to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Caragana aurantiaca is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to prevent soil erosion.
