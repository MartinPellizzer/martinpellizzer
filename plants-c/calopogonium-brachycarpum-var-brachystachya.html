Calopogonium brachycarpum var. brachystachya

Calopogonium brachycarpum var. brachystachya, commonly known as the short-stemmed hairy bean, is a tropical leguminous plant characterized by its slender, erect stems and trifoliate leaves.

It produces small, yellow flowers followed by elliptical pods containing several seeds. The plant is adapted to warm, humid climates and is often found in disturbed soils or grasslands. It has a deep root system that helps in soil stabilization and nitrogen fixation.

This variant is notable for its compact growth habit and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
