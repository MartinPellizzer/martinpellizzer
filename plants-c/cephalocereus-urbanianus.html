Cephalocereus urbanianus

Cephalocereus urbanianus, commonly known as the Mexican torch cactus, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stems that are often covered in white spines. The plant produces large, showy flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming at night. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Its unique appearance and nocturnal flowering make it a striking addition to xeriscaped gardens.
