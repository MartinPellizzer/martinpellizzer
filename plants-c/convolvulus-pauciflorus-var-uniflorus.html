Convolvulus pauciflorus var. uniflorus

Convolvulus pauciflorus var. uniflorus, commonly known as the single-flowered morning glory, is a herbaceous perennial native to southern Africa.

It features slender, twining stems that climb by means of tendrils, often forming dense foliage. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pale blue or purple, opening in the morning and closing by midday. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are fibrous and adapted for anchoring in sandy soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
