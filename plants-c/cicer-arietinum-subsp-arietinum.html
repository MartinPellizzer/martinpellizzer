Cicer arietinum subsp. arietinum

Cicer arietinum subsp.

arietinum, commonly known as chickpea, is an annual leguminous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and pods that contain 10–20 angular seeds.

The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and thrives in well-drained soils. Chickpeas are cultivated for their high protein content and are a staple in many cuisines around the world.

The subspecies is particularly valued for its adaptability to various climates and its role in sustainable agriculture due to nitrogen fixation.
