Catasetum cristatum

Catasetum cristatum, commonly known as the crested orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, crested or ridged petals and sepals, which give it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Catasetum cristatum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in both greenhouse and garden settings.
