Crepis silenifolia

Crepis silenifolia, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and western Asia, often found in moist meadows and grasslands. The plant is characterized by its slender, hair-like leaves and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers. It produces achenes with pappus for wind dispersal, aiding in its propagation.

Crepis silenifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
