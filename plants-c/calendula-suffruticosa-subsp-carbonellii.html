Calendula suffruticosa subsp. carbonellii

Calendula suffruticosa subsp.

carbonellii, commonly known as the Mexican marigold, is a perennial shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It is characterized by its branched stems and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has fuzzy, ovate leaves with a slightly aromatic scent. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its flowers are often used in traditional medicine and as a natural dye.
