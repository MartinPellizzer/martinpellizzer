Cleome scaposa

Cleome scaposa, commonly known as the "spider flower," is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is an annual herb that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, branching stems and alternate leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow or white flowers that resemble spiders, with five narrow petals and prominent stamens. Cleome scaposa thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in open woodlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
