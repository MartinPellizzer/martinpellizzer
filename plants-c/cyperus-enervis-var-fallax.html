Cyperus enervis var. fallax

Cyperus enervis var. fallax, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a variant of the sedge genus Cyperus.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and cylindrical inflorescences. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and wetlands. Its botanical features include small, three-lobed flowers and fibrous roots.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.
