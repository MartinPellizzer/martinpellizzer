Chusquea kochii

Chusquea kochii, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, clumping growth habit and striking silver-gray foliage due to the presence of fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in panicles. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests at high altitudes.

Chusquea kochii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.
