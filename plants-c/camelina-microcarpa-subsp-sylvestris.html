Camelina microcarpa subsp. sylvestris

Camelina microcarpa subsp.

sylvestris, commonly known as the woodland camelina, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes mustard and cabbage plants.

The plant typically grows in woodland areas and has small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are alternate and lance-shaped, with a rough texture.

It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.
