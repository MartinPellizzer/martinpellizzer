Campanula kurdistanica

Campanula kurdistanica, commonly known as Kurdish bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Kurdistan.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in moist, well-drained environments. Its leaves are lanceolate and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and branched.

Campanula kurdistanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic beauty.
