Centaurea montana var. cyanantha

Centaurea montana var. cyanantha, commonly known as the blue mountain centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features striking blue to violet flower heads that are surrounded by narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces numerous small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its propagation. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
