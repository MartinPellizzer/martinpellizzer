Calligonum bykovii

Calligonum bykovii, commonly known as the Bykov's tamarisk, is a shrub native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, scale-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought tolerance. It has a deep root system that helps it survive in poor soil conditions.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize sandy soils.
