Clerodendrum loniceroides

Clerodendrum loniceroides, commonly known as the creeping clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or vine that can grow up to several meters in length, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers, followed by orange-red berries that contain seeds. It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

The species is adapted to humid environments and thrives in partial shade to full sun.
