Calamagrostis emodensis

Calamagrostis emodensis, commonly known as the Tibetan reed, is a perennial grass native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. It produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
