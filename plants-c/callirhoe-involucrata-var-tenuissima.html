Callirhoe involucrata var. tenuissima

Callirhoe involucrata var. tenuissima, commonly known as narrowleaf evening primrose, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
