Croton leuconeurus subsp. mossambicensis

Croton leuconeurus subsp.

mossambicensis, commonly known as the Mozambique croton, is a shrub native to eastern Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful, and variable leaf shapes, which can range from ovate to elliptical.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
