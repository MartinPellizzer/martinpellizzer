Croton agoensis

Croton agoensis, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that often display a mix of green, red, and yellow hues. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Due to its unique foliage, it is often cultivated for decorative purposes in gardens and indoor spaces.
