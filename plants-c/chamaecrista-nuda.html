Chamaecrista nuda

Chamaecrista nuda, commonly known as the sicklepod or wild cowpea, is a flowering plant native to the Americas.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, curved seed pods that contain several small, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in disturbed areas, roadsides, and fields.

Chamaecrista nuda is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
