Cryptocarya sulavesiana

Cryptocarya sulavesiana, commonly known as the Java laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels, avocados, and cinnamon trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green flowers. It produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Cryptocarya sulavesiana is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.
