Cyperus balfourii var. laxissimus

Cyperus balfourii var. laxissimus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, three-parted flowers, which are usually wind-pollinated.

The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its adaptability in various wetland environments.
