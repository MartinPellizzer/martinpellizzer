Colobanthus monticola

Colobanthus monticola, commonly known as Antarctic pearlwort, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It is a small, mat-forming plant that thrives in extreme environments such as the Antarctic and subantarctic islands. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in arid conditions. It produces small white or pink flowers during the brief summer months.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to cold, nutrient-poor soils and harsh climatic conditions.
