Cladocarpa harrisoniae

Cladocarpa harrisoniae, commonly known as the Harrison's cliffrose, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of western North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit with woody stems. The plant produces showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically small, narrow, and arranged alternately along the stems.

Cladocarpa harrisoniae is adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
