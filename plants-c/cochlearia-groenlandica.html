Cochlearia groenlandica

Cochlearia groenlandica, commonly known as the Greenland horned poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Arctic regions of Greenland and parts of northern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spiky, horn-like petals that resemble a coiled shell, giving it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cold, harsh environments. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer.

Cochlearia groenlandica is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with similar floral structures.
