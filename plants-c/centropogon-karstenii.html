Centropogon karstenii

Centropogon karstenii, commonly known as the Karsten's fireplant, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red and orange tubular flowers, which are arranged in dense spikes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Centropogon karstenii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
