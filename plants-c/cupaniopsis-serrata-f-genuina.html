Cupaniopsis serrata f. genuina

Cupaniopsis serrata f. genuina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hakea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, serrated leaves and produces small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
