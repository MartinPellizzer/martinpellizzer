Cacalia ilicifolia

Cacalia ilicifolia, commonly known as the ivy-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers. The leaves are typically lobed and resemble those of ivy, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
