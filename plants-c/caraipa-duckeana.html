Caraipa duckeana

Caraipa duckeana, commonly known as the Caraipa tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in Venezuela and Colombia.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree that can reach heights of up to 20 meters, characterized by its dense canopy and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical, often with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody pods containing several seeds.

This plant is valued for its timber and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
