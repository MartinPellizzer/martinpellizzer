Cistus grancanariae

Cistus grancanariae, commonly known as the Canary Islands rock rose, is a shrub native to the volcanic soils of Tenerife and La Gomera in the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to arid and rocky environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and resistance to strong winds. Its silvery foliage and vibrant blooms make it a distinctive feature of the island's landscape.

Cistus grancanariae plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local flora and fauna.
