Camaridium flavum

Camaridium flavum, commonly known as the golden camaridium, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has succulent leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sunlight to flourish.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
