Carex leucochlora var. petrogena

Carex leucochlora var. petrogena, commonly known as the white-haired sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along stream edges. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, white or pale bloom, giving it its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, greenish flowers that develop into triangular, three-sided berries.

This variety is part of the Carex genus, which is known for its ecological importance in wetland ecosystems.
