Cassia sagera

Cassia sagera, commonly known as golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces elongated, woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. Cassia sagera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.
