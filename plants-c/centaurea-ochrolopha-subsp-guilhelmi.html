Centaurea ochrolopha subsp. guilhelmi

Centaurea ochrolopha subsp.

guilhelmi, commonly known as the Guilhelmi's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often found in dry, sunny habitats.

The plant features striking yellow to orange flowers with purple bracts, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
