Costus comosus

Costus comosus, commonly known as the hairy costus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant features erect, hairy stems and narrow, sword-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically pale yellow to orange, arranged in spike-like inflorescences.

Costus comosus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
