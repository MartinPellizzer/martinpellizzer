Cyathea arguta

Cyathea arguta, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant has a robust root system that helps it anchor in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically dark green and can grow up to several meters in length.

Cyathea arguta is often found in lowland rainforests and plays a role in maintaining forest biodiversity.
