Carex mitsuriokensis

Carex mitsuriokensis, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Carex mitsuriokensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
