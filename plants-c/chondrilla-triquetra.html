Chondrilla triquetra

Chondrilla triquetra, commonly known as the three-angled burr, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, spiny, three-angled seed pods. The plant produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It is often found in dry, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including roadsides and fields.
