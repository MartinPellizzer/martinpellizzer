Camellia grandis

Camellia grandis, commonly known as the large camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with a distinctive yellow center. The plant produces elliptical, dark green leaves that are leathery in texture. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

Camellia grandis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and winter blooming habit.
