Cirsium igniarium

Cirsium igniarium, commonly known as the red thistle, is a flowering plant in the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in grasslands and dry meadows. The plant is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to red flowers that bloom in late summer. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in poor soils.

The species is often used in ecological restoration due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
