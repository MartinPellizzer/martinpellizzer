Citrullus lanatus var. capensis

Citrullus lanatus var. capensis, commonly known as the African watermelon, is a cultivar of the watermelon species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, round fruits with a thick, green rind and juicy, red or yellow flesh. The plant is a vigorous vine that thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil. Its flowers are typically yellow with five petals, and it produces both male and female flowers on the same plant.

This variety is valued for its high water content and is often cultivated for both fresh consumption and processing into juice or other products.
