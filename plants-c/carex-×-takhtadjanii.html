Carex × takhtadjanii

Carex × takhtadjanii, commonly known as the hybrid sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically brown or reddish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along the edges of water bodies.

Carex × takhtadjanii is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
