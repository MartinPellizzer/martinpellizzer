Cyperus austrochrysanthus

Cyperus austrochrysanthus, commonly known as the golden flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color, which are borne on spikelets. It thrives in wet or seasonally flooded soils, making it a common species in marshy areas and along waterways.

Cyperus austrochrysanthus is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various water conditions.
