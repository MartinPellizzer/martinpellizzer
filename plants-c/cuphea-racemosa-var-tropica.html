Cuphea racemosa var. tropica

Cuphea racemosa var. tropica, commonly known as the tropical cuphea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular red flowers that bloom in dense, spike-like clusters. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, powdery coating. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique floral structure attracts hummingbirds and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological value.
