Cirsium × linkianum

Cirsium × linkianum, commonly known as the hybrid thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including meadows and woodland edges. Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

Due to its ornamental value and invasive potential in some regions, it is sometimes cultivated or managed carefully.
