Chrozophora hierosolymitana

Chrozophora hierosolymitana, commonly known as Jerusalem thorn.

It is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, pale flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by winged fruits that disperse seeds by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control.
