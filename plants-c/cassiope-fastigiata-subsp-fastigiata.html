Cassiope fastigiata subsp. fastigiata

Cassiope fastigiata subsp.

fastigiata, commonly known as the upright cassiope, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. It features dense clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant has needle-like leaves that are dark green and often covered in a fine, white bloom. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as bogs and heathlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
