Convolvulus pitardii var. glaouorum

Convolvulus pitardii var. glaouorum, commonly known as the Algerian bindweed, is a flowering plant native to North Africa.

It is a perennial species characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to harsh conditions, though it can become invasive in certain regions.
