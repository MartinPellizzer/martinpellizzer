Cassia oligoclada var. typica

Cassia oligoclada var. typica, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow, flame-like flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
