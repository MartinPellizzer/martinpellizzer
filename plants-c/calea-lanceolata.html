Calea lanceolata

Calea lanceolata, commonly known as the sacred candle plant, is a herbaceous perennial native to Mexico.

It features striking, candle-like inflorescences that are often used in traditional rituals. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are dark green and slightly aromatic. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its unique appearance and cultural significance make it a notable species in botanical gardens and ethnobotanical studies.
