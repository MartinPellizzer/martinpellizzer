Ceropegia linearis subsp. debilis

Ceropegia linearis subsp.

debilis, commonly known as the slender milkweed vine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and thin, succulent stems that can reach lengths of several meters.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically pale green or yellowish, with a unique structure that aids in pollination by specific insects.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
