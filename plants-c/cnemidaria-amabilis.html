Cnemidaria amabilis

Cnemidaria amabilis, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces showy, pale yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is native to regions in Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain areas.
