Cirsium odontolepis

Cirsium odontolepis, commonly known as the toothed thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, which attract various pollinators.

Cirsium odontolepis is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued in ornamental gardening.
