Clinopodium Serpyllifolium Subsp. Giresunicum

Clinopodium serpyllifolium subsp. giresunicum is a rare and lesser-known plant species found primarily in specific alpine and rocky habitats. It belongs to the mint family, known for its aromatic properties and medicinal uses. This subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf shape and small, clustered flowers. Despite its ecological significance, it remains under-researched and often overlooked in botanical studies. Understanding and protecting this plant is crucial for preserving biodiversity in its native environments.
