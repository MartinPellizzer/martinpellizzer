Ctenitis grandis

Ctenitis grandis, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has a robust, woody trunk that supports its expansive canopy of leaves. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in moist, shaded environments.

Ctenitis grandis plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
