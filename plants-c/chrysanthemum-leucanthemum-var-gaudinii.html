Chrysanthemum leucanthemum var. gaudinii

Chrysanthemum leucanthemum var. gaudinii, commonly known as the Gaudin's white chrysanthemum, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features showy, white to pale yellow composite flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. The plant has finely divided, gray-green leaves that are aromatic and often covered in a powdery coating. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, sandy or loamy soils.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
