Crataegus pruinosa var. pruinosa

Crataegus pruinosa var. pruinosa, commonly known as the Prunus-like hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and oval to elliptic leaves that are often slightly hairy underneath. The plant produces clusters of white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and supporting local biodiversity.
