Calyptranthes palustris

Calyptranthes palustris, commonly known as swamp calyptranthes, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Calyptranthes palustris is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
