Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. meissneri

Cinnamomum zeylanicum var. meissneri, commonly known as the cinnamon tree, is a species native to Sri Lanka and parts of southern India.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its lanceolate, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by dark blue, spherical berries. Its bark is the primary source of the spice cinnamon, which is widely used in cooking and traditional medicine.

The leaves and twigs are also utilized in aromatherapy and herbal remedies due to their essential oils.
