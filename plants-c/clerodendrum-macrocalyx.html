Clerodendrum macrocalyx

Clerodendrum macrocalyx, commonly known as the large-flowered glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Clerodendrum macrocalyx is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
