Chamaecyparis pisifera var. filifera

Chamaecyparis pisifera var. filifera, commonly known as the Japanese white cedar or Japanese cypress, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its narrow, feather-like foliage that forms dense, soft sprays, giving it a distinctive, ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it has a slow growth rate, making it suitable for small gardens or as a specimen plant. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, and it is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic value and air-purifying properties.

The tree is also valued for its durable wood, which has been historically used in construction and traditional crafts.
