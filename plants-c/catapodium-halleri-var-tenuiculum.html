Catapodium halleri var. tenuiculum

Catapodium halleri var. tenuiculum, commonly known as the slender catapodium, is a grass-like plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family. It is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils, thriving in conditions with low precipitation.

Its structure and appearance make it well-suited for xerophytic environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion.
