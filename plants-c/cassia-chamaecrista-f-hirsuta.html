Cassia chamaecrista f. hirsuta

Cassia chamaecrista f. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. Its seeds are encased in long, slender pods that turn brown as they mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
