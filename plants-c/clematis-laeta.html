Clematis laeta

Clematis laeta, commonly known as the white clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features heart-shaped, palmately lobed leaves that are typically green and hairy on the underside. The plant produces showy, white or pale blue flowers with five petals and a distinctive, elongated pistil. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis laeta is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
