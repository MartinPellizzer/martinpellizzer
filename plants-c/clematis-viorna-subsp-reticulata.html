Clematis viorna subsp. reticulata

Clematis viorna subsp.

reticulata, commonly known as the reticulate clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern Asia. It features compound leaves with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern, giving it its subspecific name.

The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its climbing habit and vigorous growth make it suitable for trellises or as a garden climber.

Botanically, it belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and persistent sepals.
