Centaurea damanti

Centaurea damanti, commonly known as the Damanti's cornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking blue to purple flower heads that are typically surrounded by a ring of white or pale blue ray florets. The plant has wiry, branched stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are lance-shaped, serrated, and arranged alternately along the stem.

Centaurea damanti thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens and wildflower meadows.
