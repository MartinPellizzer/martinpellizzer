Carex tangii

Carex tangii, commonly known as the Tangii sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and forest understories, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, clustered flowers that are often inconspicuous.

Carex tangii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
