Callicarpa longifolia

Callicarpa longifolia, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, vibrant purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, oval-shaped leaves that turn red in the autumn. Its flowers are small and white, appearing in clusters, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The berries are not typically eaten by humans but are a food source for birds and wildlife.
