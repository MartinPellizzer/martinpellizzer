Convolvulus sublobatus

Convolvulus sublobatus, commonly known as the narrow-lobed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features heart-shaped leaves and produces small, pale blue or purple flowers that bloom in the morning. Its vines are hairy and can spread along the ground or climb supports.

The species is known for its rapid growth and is often found in disturbed soils and open areas.
