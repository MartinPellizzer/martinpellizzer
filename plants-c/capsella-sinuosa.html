Capsella sinuosa

Capsella sinuosa, commonly known as the heartleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, violet-blue flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, rounded seeds.

Capsella sinuosa is considered an herbaceous perennial, blooming in the spring and early summer.
