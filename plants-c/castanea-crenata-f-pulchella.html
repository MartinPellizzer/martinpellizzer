Castanea crenata f. pulchella

Castanea crenata f. pulchella, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut or sweet chestnut.

It is a subspecies of the chestnut tree native to Japan, characterized by its ornamental value and unique foliage. The plant features palmate leaves with a serrated edge, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, spiny fruits that mature into edible nuts.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively cold-hardy, making it suitable for temperate climates.
