Carex raleighii

Carex raleighii, commonly known as Raleigh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. This species typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands, stream banks, and forest understories. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, upright leaves that can reach up to 3 feet in height.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers in late summer, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.
