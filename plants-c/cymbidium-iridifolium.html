Cymbidium iridifolium

Cymbidium iridifolium, commonly known as the purple-leafed orchid, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a perennial herb with a pseudobulb structure that stores water and nutrients. The plant produces long, arching inflorescences with numerous fragrant, showy flowers in shades of purple and white. Its leaves are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a striking purple hue, contributing to its ornamental value.

It thrives in cool, humid environments and is popular in horticulture for its beauty and resilience.
