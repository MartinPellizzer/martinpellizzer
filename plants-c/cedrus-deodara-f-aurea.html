Cedrus deodara f. aurea

Cedrus deodara f. aurea, commonly known as the golden cedar, is a cultivar of the deodar cedar.

It is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow foliage, which contrasts beautifully with its dark green needles. This ornamental tree is native to the western Himalayas and thrives in temperate climates. The leaves are narrow, linear, and arranged in flat sprays, giving it a unique appearance.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and landscapes.
