Calliscirpus criniger

Calliscirpus criniger, commonly known as the silky grasshopper plant, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in panicles.

Calliscirpus criniger is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, feathery appearance.
