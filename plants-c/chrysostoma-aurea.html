Chrysostoma aurea

Chrysostoma aurea, commonly known as the golden chrysostoma, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its desert habitat. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are adapted to reduce water loss in hot, dry conditions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in sandy plains.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is a valued species in both horticulture and conservation efforts.
