Campanula radula var. minor

Campanula radula var. minor, commonly known as the lesser mouse-ear bellflower, is a low-growing perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, gray-green leaves that are typically opposite and covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens for its ornamental value.
