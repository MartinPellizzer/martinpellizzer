Carex rugulosa var. graciliculmis

Carex rugulosa var. graciliculmis, commonly known as the slender-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and is often found in wetland ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian plant communities.
