Carex filicina var. laevis

Carex filicina var. laevis, commonly known as the smooth filicinea sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage, with narrow, linear leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
