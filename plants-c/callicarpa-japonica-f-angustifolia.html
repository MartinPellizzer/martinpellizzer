Callicarpa japonica f. angustifolia

Callicarpa japonica f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of vibrant purple berries that ripen in the fall. The plant thrives in partial shade to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for ornamental gardens. Its berries are not only visually striking but also attract birds and other wildlife.

The foliage provides seasonal color changes, adding aesthetic value to the landscape throughout the year.
