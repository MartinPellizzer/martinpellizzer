Conospermum sigmoideum

Conospermum sigmoideum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved honeysuckle, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia.

It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white or yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, urn-shaped fruits that are typically red or orange when mature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal heathlands.

Conospermum sigmoideum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting native wildlife.
