Commelina leiandra

Commelina leiandra, commonly known as the blue commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a waxy surface. Its flowers are typically arranged in clusters and are pollinated by insects.

Commelina leiandra is often found in wetlands and disturbed soils, showcasing its adaptability to various moist environments.
