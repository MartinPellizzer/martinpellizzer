Colobanthus mollis

Colobanthus mollis, commonly known as Antarctic pearlwort, is a flowering plant native to the extreme environments of Antarctica and the Arctic.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming species that thrives in cold, harsh climates with minimal soil. The plant produces small, white or greenish flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are succulent and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in arid conditions.

Colobanthus mollis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for microorganisms in its extreme habitat.
