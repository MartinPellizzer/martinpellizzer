Clinopodium nepeta subsp. spruneri

Clinopodium nepeta subsp.

spruneri, commonly known as catmint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are often used in herbal teas and have a mild minty aroma.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
