Carya borealis

Carya borealis, commonly known as the eastern black walnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Juglandaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and edible, husked nuts. The tree can grow up to 30 meters tall, with a thick, deeply furrowed bark. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and turn yellow in the fall.

The fruit is a drupe, containing a single hard-shelled nut enclosed in a fibrous husk.
