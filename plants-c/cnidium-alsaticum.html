Cnidium alsaticum

Cnidium alsaticum, commonly known as the Greater Cnidium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, often hollow stems and fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its roots and rhizomes are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Cnidium alsaticum is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
