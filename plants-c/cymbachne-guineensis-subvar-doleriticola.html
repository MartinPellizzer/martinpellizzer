Cymbachne guineensis subvar. doleriticola

Cymbachne guineensis subvar. doleriticola, commonly known as the doleritic grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils, often found in rocky or degraded areas. It has fibrous roots that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

This subvariety is valued for its ecological role in soil conservation and as a forage plant for livestock.
