Ceratostema lanigerum

Ceratostema lanigerum, commonly known as the silverleaf ceratostema, is a perennial shrub native to southern China and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are opposite and often covered in a fine layer of hair, contributing to its silvery sheen.

Ceratostema lanigerum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
