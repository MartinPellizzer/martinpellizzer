Convolvulus bonariensis var. multiflorus

Convolvulus bonariensis var. multiflorus, commonly known as the Brazilian morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in warm, sunny conditions and is often cultivated as a decorative vine.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental foliage and flowering plants.
