Cupania rhoifolia

Cupania rhoifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

Cupania rhoifolia is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its rapid growth rate.
