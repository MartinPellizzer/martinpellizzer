Calamus nuralievii

Calamus nuralievii, commonly known as the Nuraliev's arrowroot, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender grass-like plant that can grow up to several meters in height, characterized by its long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with inconspicuous flowers, which are followed by elongated, tuberous rhizomes. These rhizomes are the primary part used, containing starch that is harvested for culinary and medicinal purposes.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
