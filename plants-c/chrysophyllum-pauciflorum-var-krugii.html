Chrysophyllum pauciflorum var. krugii

Chrysophyllum pauciflorum var. krugii, commonly known as Krug's starfruit, is a tropical plant belonging to the Sapotaceae family.

It is native to western Africa, particularly Nigeria and Cameroon, and thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are ovate and glossy, and its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The fruit, which resembles a starfruit, is edible and has a sweet, acidic flavor.

It is valued not only for its culinary uses but also for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
