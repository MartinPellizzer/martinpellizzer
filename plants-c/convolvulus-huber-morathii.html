Convolvulus huber-morathii

Convolvulus huber-morathii, commonly known as the Morath's bindweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Convolvulaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit, with opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, which bloom in summer. It is native to southern Europe and thrives in rocky or dry habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain regions.
