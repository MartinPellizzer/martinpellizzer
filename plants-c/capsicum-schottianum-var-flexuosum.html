Capsicum schottianum var. flexuosum

Capsicum schottianum var. flexuosum, commonly known as the creeping or flexuous pepper, is a botanical variant of the Capsicum genus.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with slender stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, elongated fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject in studies of Capsicum diversity and adaptation.
