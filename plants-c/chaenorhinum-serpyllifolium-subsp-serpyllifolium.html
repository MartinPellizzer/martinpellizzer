Chaenorhinum serpyllifolium subsp. serpyllifolium

Chaenorhinum serpyllifolium subsp.

serpyllifolium, commonly known as common sandrocket, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, cruciform flowers and aromatic foliage.

The plant typically grows in dry, sandy or gravelly soils, often in open habitats such as fields and roadsides. It has basal leaves that are lanceolate and hairy, with a flowering period from late spring to early summer.

The seeds are small and contained in siliques, which are typical of the Brassicaceae family.
