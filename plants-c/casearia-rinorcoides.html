Casearia rinorcoides

Casearia rinorcoides, commonly known as the silky casearia, is a small evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It features simple, opposite, elliptic leaves that are glossy and often covered with fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
