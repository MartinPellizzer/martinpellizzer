Campanula simplex var. silenifolia

Campanula simplex var. silenifolia, commonly known as the silver bell bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, pale blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lanceolate and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
