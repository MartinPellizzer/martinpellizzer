Croton macrobuxus var. substrigosus

Croton macrobuxus var. substrigosus, commonly known as the hairy croton, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, rough, and often hairy foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a leathery texture.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
