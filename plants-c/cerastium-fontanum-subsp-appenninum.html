Cerastium fontanum subsp. appenninum

Cerastium fontanum subsp.

appenninum, commonly known as the Apennine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial herb native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its prostrate or ascending stems covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and often have a velvety texture.

It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
