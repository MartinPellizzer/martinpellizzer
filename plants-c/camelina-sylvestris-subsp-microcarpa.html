Camelina sylvestris subsp. microcarpa

Camelina sylvestris subsp.

microcarpa, commonly known as the dwarf bladder campion, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae. It is characterized by its small, capsule-like fruits and hairy, branched stems.

The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and sandy soils. It produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer.

This subspecies is native to parts of Europe and western Asia and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
