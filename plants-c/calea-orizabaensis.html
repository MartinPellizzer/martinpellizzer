Calea orizabaensis

Calea orizabaensis, commonly known as the Mexican moonflower, is a striking plant native to high-altitude regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, white, velvety petals that open at night, making it a nocturnal bloom. The plant belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its unique, fragrant flowers that emit a sweet scent. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Calea orizabaensis is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in local herbal remedies.
