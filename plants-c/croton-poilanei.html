Croton poilanei

Croton poilanei, commonly known as the Cameroon croton, is a tropical shrub native to Central and West Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often ornamental species. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that are typically green with prominent veins, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers. Its growth habit is characterized by a branching structure with a dense canopy, making it suitable for ornamental purposes in shaded environments.

Croton poilanei is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
