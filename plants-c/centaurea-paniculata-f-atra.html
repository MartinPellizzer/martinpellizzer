Centaurea paniculata f. atra

Centaurea paniculata f. atra, commonly known as the black centaury, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black flowers, which contrast with its gray-green foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its striking appearance.
