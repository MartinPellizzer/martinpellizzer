Campanula broussonetiana

Campanula broussonetiana, commonly known as the Broussonet's bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has lance-shaped leaves and produces small, cylindrical seed capsules that release numerous tiny seeds.

Campanula broussonetiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blossoms.
