Cirsium bulbosum

Cirsium bulbosum, commonly known as the bulbous thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features spiny, lobed leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer, forming a spherical inflorescence. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water and nutrients.

Cirsium bulbosum is often found in rocky or dry soils and is known for its hardy, drought-resistant nature.
