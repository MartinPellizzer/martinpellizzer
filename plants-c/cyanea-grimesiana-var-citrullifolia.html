Cyanea grimesiana var. citrullifolia

Cyanea grimesiana var. citrullifolia, commonly known as the yellow hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which bloom throughout the year. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces fruit that resembles a small, round melon. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
