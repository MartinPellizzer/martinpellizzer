Crataegus scopulorum

Crataegus scopulorum, commonly known as the rock hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in woodland edges or open areas.

Crataegus scopulorum is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
