Clidemia amygdaloides

Clidemia amygdaloides, commonly known as the silverleaf or false hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, clustered, and often white or pale pink, with five petals.

Clidemia amygdaloides is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
