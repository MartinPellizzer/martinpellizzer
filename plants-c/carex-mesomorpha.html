Carex mesomorpha

Carex mesomorpha, commonly known as the intermediate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in dense clusters at the base. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike of small, inconspicuous flowers.

Carex mesomorpha plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
