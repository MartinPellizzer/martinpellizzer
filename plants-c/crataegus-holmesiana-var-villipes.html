Crataegus holmesiana var. villipes

Crataegus holmesiana var. villipes, commonly known as the California hawthorn, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to the western United States, particularly California, and is characterized by its small, thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for local wildlife. It typically grows in chaparral and woodland habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

The leaves are usually oval-shaped with serrated edges, and the plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
