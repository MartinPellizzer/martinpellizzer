Croton douradensis

Croton douradensis, commonly known as the golden croton, is a species of flowering plant in the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage. The plant features alternate, palmate leaves that can range in color from green to red, yellow, and orange, depending on the variety. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers, followed by orange or red capsules containing seeds.

Croton douradensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant and varied leaf colors.
