Cirsium melanolepis

Cirsium melanolepis, commonly known as the black-leaved thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves with sharp spines and its purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils. Its flowers are visited by a variety of pollinators, including bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological importance.

The species is also known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
