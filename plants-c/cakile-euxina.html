Cakile euxina

Cakile euxina, commonly known as the red sand mustard, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and succulent, fleshy leaves adapted to conserve water. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in disturbed habitats. Its scientific name reflects its distinctive red coloration, which is particularly noticeable in its flowers and stems.

Cakile euxina plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity in its native environment.
