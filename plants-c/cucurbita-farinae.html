Cucurbita farinae

Cucurbita farinae, commonly known as the Chinese pumpkin or yellow marrow gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to East Asia and is characterized by its large, yellow-orange, ridged fruits that are typically harvested when mature. The plant is a climbing vine with palmately lobed leaves and produces yellow flowers. Its fruits are used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a food source, valued for their sweet, nutty flavor.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
