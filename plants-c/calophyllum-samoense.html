Calophyllum samoense

Calophyllum samoense, commonly known as the Samoan candlewood, is a tropical tree native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Samoa and nearby regions.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark-brown bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces yellowish flowers followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits that contain a single seed. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it important for both traditional and commercial uses.

The plant also plays a role in local ecosystems and cultural practices in the Pacific.
