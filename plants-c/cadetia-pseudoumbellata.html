Cadetia pseudoumbellata

Cadetia pseudoumbellata, commonly known as the false umbel flower, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its small, clustered white or yellowish flowers that resemble an umbel, hence its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Cadetia pseudoumbellata is known for its delicate appearance and is often found in temperate regions of Europe and Asia.
