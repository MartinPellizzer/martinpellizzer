Chloris jubaensis

Chloris jubaensis, commonly known as the Juba grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are usually wind-pollinated. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it an important component of the local vegetation.

Due to its hardiness, it is often used for soil stabilization and as a forage crop in some regions.
