Cineraria petasites

Cineraria petasites, commonly known as the alpine cineraria, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves and showy, dandelion-like flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture, while the flowers range in color from white to pale yellow.

Cineraria petasites is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
