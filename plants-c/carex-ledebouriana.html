Carex ledebouriana

Carex ledebouriana, commonly known as the Ledebour's sedge, is a perennial plant native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats. Its foliage is grass-like, with narrow, cylindrical leaves that are usually green to bluish-green in color.

Carex ledebouriana produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.
