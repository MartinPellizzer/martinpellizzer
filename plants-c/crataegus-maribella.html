Crataegus maribella

Crataegus maribella, commonly known as the maribella hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.

The maribella hawthorn is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
