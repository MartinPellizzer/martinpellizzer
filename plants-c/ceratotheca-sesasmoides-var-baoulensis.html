Ceratotheca sesasmoides var. baoulensis

Ceratotheca sesasmoides var. baoulensis, commonly known as the African butterbean, is a flowering plant native to West Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, characterized by its yellow flowers and pod-like fruits. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein in local diets.
