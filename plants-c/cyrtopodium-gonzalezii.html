Cyrtopodium gonzalezii

Cyrtopodium gonzalezii, commonly known as the Gonzalez's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the Andean regions of South America.

It typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories where it receives indirect light. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Cyrtopodium gonzalezii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated by enthusiasts for its unique floral display.
