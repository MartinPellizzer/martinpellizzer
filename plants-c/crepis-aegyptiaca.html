Crepis aegyptiaca

Crepis aegyptiaca, commonly known as Egyptian mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of North Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields. Its small, hairy leaves and yellowish flower heads make it easily identifiable.

Crepis aegyptiaca is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
