Callista leucochlora

Callista leucochlora, commonly known as the white-flowered callista, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its most distinctive trait is the white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
