Clinopodium debile

Clinopodium debile, commonly known as slender woodruff, is a low-growing perennial herb native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by aromatic foliage and square stems. The plant typically features small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in whorls along the stem. Its leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a minty scent.

Clinopodium debile thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.
