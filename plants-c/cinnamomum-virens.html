Cinnamomum virens

Cinnamomum virens, commonly known as the Vietnamese cinnamon or sweet cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces dark brown, aromatic bark that is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. Its leaves are also used in perfumery and for their medicinal properties.

Cinnamomum virens thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.
