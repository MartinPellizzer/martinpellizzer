Caragana brevispina subsp. brevispina

Caragana brevispina subsp.

brevispina, commonly known as the narrow-spined shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to central Asia. It typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, characterized by its compact growth habit and thorny stems.

The plant produces small, pea-like flowers in clusters, usually yellow or orange, which appear in early summer. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several small, oval leaflets, and it is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in difficult landscapes.
