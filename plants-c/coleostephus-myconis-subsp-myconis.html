Coleostephus myconis subsp. myconis

Coleostephus myconis subsp.

myconis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender stems that bear opposite, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or violet, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.
