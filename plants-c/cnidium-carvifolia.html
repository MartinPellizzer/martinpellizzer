Cnidium carvifolia

Cnidium carvifolia, commonly known as the fragrant cow parsley, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white or yellowish flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant is native to temperate regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shady environments. Its aromatic foliage and roots have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Cnidium carvifolia is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
