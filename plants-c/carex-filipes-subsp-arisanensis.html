Carex filipes subsp. arisanensis

Carex filipes subsp.

arisanensis, commonly known as the Arisan sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
