Coelidium purpureum

Coelidium purpureum, commonly known as the purple coelidium, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae, which includes plants such as philodendrons and peace lilies. The plant is characterized by its striking purple spathe, which encloses a yellowish spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

Coelidium purpureum is cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
