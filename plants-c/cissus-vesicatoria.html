Cissus vesicatoria

Cissus vesicatoria, commonly known as the "devil's backbone," is a climbing vine native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, warty, and often reddish stems that resemble a spine, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, sour-tasting berries. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Cissus vesicatoria is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
