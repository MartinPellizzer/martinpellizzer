Ceropegia Somalilandica

Ceropegia somalilandica is a rare and striking plant native to the arid regions of Somalia. It belongs to the milkweed family and is known for its unique, tubular flowers that resemble small horns. This plant thrives in harsh desert conditions, showcasing remarkable adaptability to extreme environments. Its delicate appearance contrasts with its tough survival traits, making it a fascinating subject for botanists and plant enthusiasts. Due to its limited habitat and threats from climate change, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
