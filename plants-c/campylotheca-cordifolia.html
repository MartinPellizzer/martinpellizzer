Campylotheca cordifolia

Campylotheca cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf campylotheca, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical to heart-shaped leaves, which are glossy and dark green on the upper surface. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by spherical, woody fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
