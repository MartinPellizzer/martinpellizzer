Campanula kantschavelii

Campanula kantschavelii, commonly known as the Kantschavel's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically blue to violet in color. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable addition to rock gardens or alpine beds.

Campanula kantschavelii is valued for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
