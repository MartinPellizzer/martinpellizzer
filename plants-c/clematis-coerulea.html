Clematis coerulea

Clematis coerulea, commonly known as the blue clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, hairy hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis coerulea is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
