Cistus monspeliensis f. typicus

Cistus monspeliensis f. typicus, commonly known as the typical rock rose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, hairy leaves and clusters of pink to purple flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and flowers, are valued for their medicinal and aromatic properties.

This subspecies is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural perfumes.
