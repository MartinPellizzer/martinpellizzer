Capparis brasiliana var. longipes

Capparis brasiliana var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed caper, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its woody stems, thorny branches, and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, and its leaves are typically alternate and ovate. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded areas within forests.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
