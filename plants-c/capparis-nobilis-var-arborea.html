Capparis nobilis var. arborea

Capparis nobilis var. arborea, commonly known as the tree caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters tall, characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, greenish-yellow berries. Its botanical features include a woody structure, glandular hairs on the leaves, and a preference for well-drained soils.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the use of its fruits in culinary applications.
