Commelina mollis

Commelina mollis, commonly known as the slender commelina, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It features slender, upright stems and heart-shaped, light green leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, blue-violet flowers with three petals and three sepals, arranged in a whorled pattern. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Commelina mollis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
