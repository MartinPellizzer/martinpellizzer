Christophoriana arguta var. alabastrina

Christophoriana arguta var. alabastrina, commonly known as the silverleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its seasonal color changes and compact growth habit.
