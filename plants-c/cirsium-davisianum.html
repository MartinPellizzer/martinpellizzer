Cirsium davisianum

Cirsium davisianum, commonly known as Davis's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has a robust root system that helps it survive in arid conditions.

Its flowers are visited by various pollinators, contributing to its ecological role in its native environment.
