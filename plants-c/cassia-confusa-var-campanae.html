Cassia confusa var. campanae

Cassia confusa var. campanae, commonly known as the bell cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of fragrant, yellow-orange tubular flowers that resemble small bells. The leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern. This species thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its showy blooms and is also used in traditional medicine.
