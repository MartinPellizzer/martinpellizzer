Cirsium hydrophilum

Cirsium hydrophilum, commonly known as water thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist environments such as wet meadows, riverbanks, and marshy areas. Its leaves are often covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are arranged in a spherical shape.

Cirsium hydrophilum is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
