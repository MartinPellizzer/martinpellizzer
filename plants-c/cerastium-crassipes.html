Cerastium crassipes

Cerastium crassipes, commonly known as the broad-leaved mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae, which includes many flowering plants with showy blooms. The plant features small, white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring, often before its foliage emerges. Its leaves are broad and fleshy, adapted to retain moisture in dry conditions.
