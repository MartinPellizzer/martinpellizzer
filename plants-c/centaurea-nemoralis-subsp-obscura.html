Centaurea nemoralis subsp. obscura

Centaurea nemoralis subsp.

obscura, commonly known as the dark wood centaury, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, dark blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in open woodlands and grassy areas, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, achene-like seeds.

This subspecies is notable for its relatively inconspicuous appearance compared to other members of the Centaurea genus.
