Citrus medica var. ethrog

Citrus medica var. ethrog, commonly known as the Etrog.

This variety is a citrus fruit native to the Middle East and is widely cultivated in Israel and other parts of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its elongated, lemon-shaped fruit with a thick, rough rind and a small, segmented interior. The fruit is typically harvested in the fall and is used in religious rituals, particularly during the Jewish holiday of Sukkot.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rutaceae family and is closely related to other citrus species such as lemons and limes.
