Croton speciosus subsp. tacarcunensis

Croton speciosus subsp.

tacarcunensis, commonly known as the Tacarcuna croton, is a shrub native to parts of South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador. It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, with leaves often displaying vibrant shades of red, orange, and yellow.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
