Capsicum annuum var. subangulosum

Capsicum annuum var. subangulosum, commonly known as the narrow-angled pepper, is a botanical variant of the common chili pepper.

It is characterized by its elongated, pointed fruits that typically measure between 10 to 15 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by the distinctive, narrow-angled pods. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique fruit shape and use in specialty culinary applications.
