Chilopogon

Chilopogon, commonly known as the dwarf milkweed, is a small flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family, which includes many milkweed species known for their milky sap. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and produces clusters of small, pale yellow to white flowers. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it has a short, upright stem.

Chilopogon is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
