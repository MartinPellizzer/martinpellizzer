Carex egregia

Carex egregia, commonly known as the showy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces bright yellow to orange spikelets that bloom in late spring to early summer. Carex egregia typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and stream sides.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife and maintaining wetland ecosystems.
