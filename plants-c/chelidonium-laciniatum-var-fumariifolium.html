Chelidonium laciniatum var. fumariifolium

Chelidonium laciniatum var. fumariifolium, commonly known as the fumariifolium poppy, is a variant of the greater celandine, characterized by its striking, flame-colored flowers.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically found in moist, shaded woodland areas. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a distinctive, waxy texture and a strong, pungent odor. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, producing a showy display of orange-red petals.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic alkaloids that require careful handling.
