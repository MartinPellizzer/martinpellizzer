Calamagrostis weberbaueri

Calamagrostis weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer reed, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Argentina.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
