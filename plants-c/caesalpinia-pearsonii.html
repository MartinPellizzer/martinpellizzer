Caesalpinia pearsonii

Caesalpinia pearsonii, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its bright red, flame-like inflorescences that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its rapid growth and striking floral display make it a notable species in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
