Calamagrostis longifolia

Calamagrostis longifolia, commonly known as the tall bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in hue. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Calamagrostis longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
