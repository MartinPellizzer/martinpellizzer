Cenchrus mildbraedii

Cenchrus mildbraedii, commonly known as the African speargrass, is a perennial grass native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its robust, erect growth and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland and savanna habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
