Calophyllum cerasiferum

Calophyllum cerasiferum, commonly known as the sweet-scented tonka bean tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are followed by round, woody fruits containing aromatic seeds. The seeds are the primary commercial product, valued for their essential oils and use in perfumery and flavoring.

It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.
