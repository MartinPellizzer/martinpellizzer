Cousinia albiflora

Cousinia albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of silver-gray, hairy leaves. The plant produces showy, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Cousinia albiflora is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
