Crocus lageniflorus var. oliverianus

Crocus lageniflorus var. oliverianus, commonly known as the Oliver's crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, rounded bulbs that store nutrients for future growth. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland edges.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate in temperate climates.
