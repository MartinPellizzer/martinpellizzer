Cassipourea gummiflua var. verticillata

Cassipourea gummiflua var. verticillata, commonly known as the clustered gum tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive clustered inflorescences and smooth, pale bark. The plant produces small, white flowers that are often clustered in whorls, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its latex sap is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
