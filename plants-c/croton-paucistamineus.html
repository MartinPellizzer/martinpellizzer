Croton paucistamineus

Croton paucistamineus, commonly known as the African croton, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Its leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its toxicity requires caution.
