Ceruana schimperi

Ceruana schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian spurge, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, characterized by its milky sap and unique floral structures. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, showcasing adaptations to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are usually alternate and simple, while its flowers are clustered in cyathia, a feature typical of the Euphorbiaceae family.

The plant is also known for its medicinal properties, used in traditional remedies across its native region.
