Cardamine macrophylla proles venusta

Cardamine macrophylla proles venusta, commonly known as the showy water cress, is a species of flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and woodland areas. Its leaves are often submerged or partially submerged, adapting to aquatic or semi-aquatic conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
