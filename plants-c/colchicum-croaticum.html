Colchicum croaticum

Colchicum croaticum, commonly known as the Croatian colchic, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the family Colchicaceae and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its leaves are broad and arrow-shaped, and it produces distinctive, scaly capsules containing toxic seeds.

Due to its medicinal and ornamental value, it is a subject of both scientific interest and conservation efforts.
