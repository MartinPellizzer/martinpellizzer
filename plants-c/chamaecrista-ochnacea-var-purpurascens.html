Chamaecrista ochnacea var. purpurascens

Chamaecrista ochnacea var. purpurascens, commonly known as purple crown bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive purple hue on the underside. The plant produces clusters of yellow to orange flowers, followed by elongated pods containing flattened, kidney-shaped seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.

The species is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
