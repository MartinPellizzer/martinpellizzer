Carex rhodesiaca

Carex rhodesiaca, commonly known as the Rhodesian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of lakes and rivers. It has grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

Carex rhodesiaca is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
