Cynocrambe macrantha

Cynocrambe macrantha, commonly known as the large-flowered cowpea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant produces pods that contain small, edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Cynocrambe macrantha is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
