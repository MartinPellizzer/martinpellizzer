Carex alopecuroides subsp. subtransversa

Carex alopecuroides subsp.

subtransversa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slightly rough texture, and it produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or brown in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in garden settings for its ability to thrive in shaded areas.
