Calamintha officinalis subsp. subisodonta

Calamintha officinalis subsp.

subisodonta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved calamint, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves.

The plant features lance-shaped, aromatic leaves with a slightly fuzzy texture and produces small, pale blue to violet flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
