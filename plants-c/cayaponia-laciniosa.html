Cayaponia laciniosa

Cayaponia laciniosa, commonly known as the spider plant or lace fern, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lacy foliage and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests. Its leaves are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance, and it produces red or orange berries that contain the seeds.

Cayaponia laciniosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
