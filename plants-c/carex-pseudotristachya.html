Carex pseudotristachya

Carex pseudotristachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a terminal inflorescence.

Carex pseudotristachya is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
