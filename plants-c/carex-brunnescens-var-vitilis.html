Carex brunnescens var. vitilis

Carex brunnescens var. vitilis, commonly known as the vitilis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wetlands. Its leaves are usually dark green with a distinctive bronze or reddish hue on the underside.

The flowers are wind-pollinated, and the plant plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
