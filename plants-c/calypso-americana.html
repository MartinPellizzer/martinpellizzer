Calypso americana

Calypso americana, commonly known as the American calypso, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant with delicate, pale pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is native to the southeastern United States and parts of Mexico.
