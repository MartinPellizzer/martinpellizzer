Cassia fasciculata var. littoralis

Cassia fasciculata var. littoralis, commonly known as coastal cassia, is a shrub native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in sandy or saline soils and is often found along shorelines and mangrove forests. It has a woody stem and produces small, pod-like fruits containing several seeds.

This species is valued for its ability to stabilize coastal soils and provide habitat for local wildlife.
