Campanula sarmatica var. subtomentosa

Campanula sarmatica var. subtomentosa, commonly known as the Sarmatian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically hairy and covered in a dense layer of fine, whitish pubescence, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms and hardy nature.
