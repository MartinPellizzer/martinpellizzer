Cyphostemma villosicaule

Cyphostemma villosicaule, commonly known as the hairy cyphostemma, is a climbing shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and small, elliptical leaves with a hairy surface. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky surfaces or in shrubland.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptations, it is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
