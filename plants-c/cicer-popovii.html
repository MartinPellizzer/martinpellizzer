Cicer popovii

Cicer popovii, commonly known as the Popov's vetch, is a leguminous plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seed pods are elongated and contain several elliptical seeds.

Cicer popovii is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
