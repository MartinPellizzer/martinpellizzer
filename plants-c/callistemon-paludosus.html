Callistemon paludosus

Callistemon paludosus, commonly known as the swamp bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange bottlebrush-like flowers that bloom in winter and spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in wet or swampy soils and is often found near water bodies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and wetland restoration projects.
