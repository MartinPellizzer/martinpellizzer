Carex disticha var. elatior

Carex disticha var. elatior, commonly known as the tall sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its upright, grass-like foliage. The plant produces cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
