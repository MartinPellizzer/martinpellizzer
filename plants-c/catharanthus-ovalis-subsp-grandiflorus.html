Catharanthus ovalis subsp. grandiflorus

Catharanthus ovalis subsp.

grandiflorus, commonly known as the Madagascar starflower, is a species native to Madagascar. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of fragrant, star-shaped flowers.

The flowers typically range in color from white to pink, with some varieties displaying a slight red hue. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
