Clinopodium japonicum

Clinopodium japonicum, commonly known as Japanese woodrue, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, while the small flowers are usually pink or purple.

Clinopodium japonicum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
