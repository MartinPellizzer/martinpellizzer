Cystostemon hispidissimus subsp. zambiensis

Cystostemon hispidissimus subsp.

zambiensis, commonly known as the Zambian sandbush, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems and small, dense leaves covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in xeriscaping and for erosion control.
