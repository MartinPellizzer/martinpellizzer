Cytisus capitatus var. lateralis

Cytisus capitatus var. lateralis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved broom, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer. The plant has slender, often spiny stems and narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in whorls. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean climates.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soils.
