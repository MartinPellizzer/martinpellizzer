Carex atrata var. sinensis

Carex atrata var. sinensis, commonly known as the black sedge, is a perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, strap-like leaves that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
