Chamaecrista foederalis

Chamaecrista foederalis, commonly known as the federal ticktree, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish to reddish flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in open areas such as roadsides and fields.

Its growth habit and floral structure make it a notable species in ecological studies of native plant communities.
