Calamus blancoi

Calamus blancoi, commonly known as the white calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a perennial herb characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and arrow-shaped leaves. Native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in a spadix, enclosed by a leaf-like structure called a spathe.

Calamus blancoi is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
