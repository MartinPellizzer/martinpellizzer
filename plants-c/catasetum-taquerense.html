Catasetum taquerense

Catasetum taquerense, commonly known as the Mexican orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, often fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange with intricate patterns, and it produces a unique structure called a gynostemium.

Catasetum taquerense is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
