Cumulopuntia crassicylindrica

Cumulopuntia crassicylindrica, commonly known as the thick-stemmed cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered in white, fuzzy hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically clustered at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it has a shallow root system adapted for drought tolerance.

Cumulopuntia crassicylindrica is a slow-growing, long-lived cactus that thrives in well-drained soils and full sun.
