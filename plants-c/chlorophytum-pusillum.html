Chlorophytum pusillum

Chlorophytum pusillum, commonly known as the little chlorophyte, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are typically narrow and grass-like. The plant produces small, white flowers that emerge from the base of the leaves, often in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Due to its compact size and low maintenance, it is often used as a decorative plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.
