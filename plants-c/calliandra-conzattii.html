Calliandra conzattii

Calliandra conzattii, commonly known as the pink calliandra, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feather-like inflorescences that range in color from pink to red. The plant produces small, bean-like pods that contain seeds, and it has bipinnate leaves with numerous small leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its showy flowers and drought tolerance, it is often used in xeriscaping and wildlife habitats.
