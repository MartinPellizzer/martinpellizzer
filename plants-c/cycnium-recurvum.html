Cycnium recurvum

Cycnium recurvum, commonly known as the curly bindweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its stems are often hairy and can become woody with age.

Cycnium recurvum is known for its ability to spread rapidly, making it a common weed in gardens and fields.
