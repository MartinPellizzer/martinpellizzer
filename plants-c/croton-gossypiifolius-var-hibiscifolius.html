Croton gossypiifolius var. hibiscifolius

Croton gossypiifolius var. hibiscifolius, commonly known as the Hibiscifolius croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that resemble those of hibiscus, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange or red fruits containing three seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
