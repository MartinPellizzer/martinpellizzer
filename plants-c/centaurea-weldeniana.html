Centaurea weldeniana

Centaurea weldeniana, commonly known as the Welden's cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and features bright blue to purple flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

Centaurea weldeniana is often found in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.
