Cupressus pendens

Cupressus pendens, commonly known as the weeping cedar, is a species of coniferous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive drooping branches that create a cascading appearance, giving it a unique visual appeal. The plant has scale-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces small, cone-like structures that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of Cupressus pendens make it a popular choice for gardens and parks.
