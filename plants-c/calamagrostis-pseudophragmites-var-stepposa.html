Calamagrostis pseudophragmites var. stepposa

Calamagrostis pseudophragmites var. stepposa, commonly known as steppe reed, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, wet meadows, and along riverbanks. The plant features tall, slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often silver-white on the underside. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets that are initially green and later turn brown.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
