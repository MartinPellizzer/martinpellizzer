Cenchrus flaccidus

Cenchrus flaccidus, commonly known as the flaccid grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Cenchrus flaccidus is valued for its adaptability and is sometimes used in erosion control and forage production.
