Ceanothus fresnensis

Ceanothus fresnensis, commonly known as the Santa Cruz ceanothus, is a flowering shrub native to California.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. The plant produces clusters of blue to purple flowers in the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually narrow and evergreen, providing year-round foliage.

Ceanothus fresnensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
