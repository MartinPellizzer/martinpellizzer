Cirsium amani

Cirsium amani, commonly known as the Amani thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and open habitats, thriving at high altitudes. Its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers make it distinctive and well adapted to its environment.

Cirsium amani plays a role in local ecosystems, providing food and shelter for various insect species.
