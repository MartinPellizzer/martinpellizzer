Clusia sessilis

Clusia sessilis, commonly known as the fatshrub, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Clusia sessilis is also valued for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil types and its resilience to coastal conditions.
