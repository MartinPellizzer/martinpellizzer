Centaurea nervosa f. sessilifolia

Centaurea nervosa f. sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaved Bladder Campion, is a botanical variant of the Bladder Campion species.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and reduced, sessile leaves that are often fused to the stem. The plant produces bright pink to red flowers with a distinctive bladder-like structure at the base of each bloom. It is typically found in dry, rocky habitats and thrives in well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
