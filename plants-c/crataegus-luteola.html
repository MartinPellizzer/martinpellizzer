Crataegus luteola

Crataegus luteola, commonly known as the yellow hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant has simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, thorned branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along riverbanks.

The yellow hawthorn is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
