Chenopodium album subsp. amaranthicolor

Chenopodium album subsp.

amaranthicolor, commonly known as red goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family. It is characterized by its alternate, broadly ovate to heart-shaped leaves that are typically red or purple on the underside.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in dense clusters, and its seeds are minute and wind-dispersed. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in gardens, roadsides, and waste areas.

This subspecies is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine.
