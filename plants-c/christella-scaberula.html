Christella scaberula

Christella scaberula, commonly known as the roughleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats. The plant has small, fleshy, green leaves that are covered in fine, rough hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. Its flowers are usually pink or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

Christella scaberula is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low water availability, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
