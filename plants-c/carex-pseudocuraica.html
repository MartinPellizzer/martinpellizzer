Carex pseudocuraica

Carex pseudocuraica, commonly known as the Curaçao sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caribbean island of Curaçao.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in coastal or swampy areas. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Carex pseudocuraica is an important component of the local flora and plays a role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.
