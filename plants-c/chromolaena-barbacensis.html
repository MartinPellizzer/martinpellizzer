Chromolaena barbacensis

Chromolaena barbacensis, commonly known as the hairy chromolaena, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features opposite, hairy leaves that are often lance-shaped and have a rough texture. It produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by fluffy, white to brownish seeds.

Chromolaena barbacensis is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in disturbed habitats.
