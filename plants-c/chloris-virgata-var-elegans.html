Chloris virgata var. elegans

Chloris virgata var. elegans, commonly known as the slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It features slender, erect stems that are typically less than 30 cm in height, with narrow, linear leaves that have a slight bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
