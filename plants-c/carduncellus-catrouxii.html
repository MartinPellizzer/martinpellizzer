Carduncellus catrouxii

Carduncellus catrouxii, commonly known as the African cardoon, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features robust, spiny stems and large, waxy, bluish-green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
