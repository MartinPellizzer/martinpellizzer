Chamaecrista polita

Chamaecrista polita, commonly known as the white burr legume, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, woody pods that contain small, hard seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
