Chionogentias barringtonensis

Chionogentias barringtonensis, commonly known as the Barrington's snowbush, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its unique appearance and adaptability to arid conditions contribute to its horticultural value.

However, it is relatively rare in the wild and is often cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
