Cynanchum gilbertii

Cynanchum gilbertii, commonly known as Gilbert's milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-white flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in sagebrush communities.

Its botanical features make it an important component of arid ecosystem flora.
