Campomanesia neriiflora

Campomanesia neriiflora, commonly known as the black-flowered carambola, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, blackish flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruit, resembling a small star, is edible and has a tangy, acidic flavor.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique fruit.
