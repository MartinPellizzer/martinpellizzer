Clerodendrum myricoides var. dumale

Clerodendrum myricoides var. dumale, commonly known as the white-flowered clerodendrum, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
