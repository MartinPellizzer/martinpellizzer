Carduus cichoraceus

Carduus cichoraceus, commonly known as the common cocklebur, is a member of the Asteraceae family and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is a biennial or annual herb that can grow up to 2 meters tall, featuring rough, hairy stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces spiny, spherical fruits that contain seeds with pappus structures for wind dispersal. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, typical of the Asteraceae family.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.
