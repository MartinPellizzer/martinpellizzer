Campanula postii subsp. shepardii

Campanula postii subsp.

shepardii, commonly known as the Shepard's bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
