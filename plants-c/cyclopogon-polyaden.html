Cyclopogon polyaden

Cyclopogon polyaden, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy inflorescences. The plant features a pseudobulb structure with fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to its often arid habitat. Its flowers are typically violet or purple, with a unique, intricate pattern of petals and sepals.

Cyclopogon polyaden thrives in well-drained soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.
