Calluna vulgaris subf. pentamera

Calluna vulgaris subf.

pentamera, commonly known as the purple heath, is a subshrub native to the northern hemisphere. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple.

The plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands. Its leaves are narrow, leathery, and evergreen, providing year-round structure.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting insect populations.
