Camellia hupehensis

Camellia hupehensis, commonly known as the Chinese tea plant, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and vibrant red or pink flowers that bloom in winter. The plant is a member of the Theaceae family and is closely related to the tea plant, Camellia sinensis. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Camellia hupehensis is also valued for its historical and cultural significance in traditional Chinese horticulture.
