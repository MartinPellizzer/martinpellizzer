Cryphiacanthus pumilus

Cryphiacanthus pumilus, commonly known as the dwarf arrowroot, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features a short, rhizomatous stem and produces small, clustered leaves that are usually light green to blue-green in color. The plant is characterized by its unique inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a spathe. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and for traditional use.
