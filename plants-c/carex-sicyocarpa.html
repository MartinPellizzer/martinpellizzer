Carex sicyocarpa

Carex sicyocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-sheathed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single, spike-like cluster of flowers, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex sicyocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage.
