Convolvulus polyanthus

Convolvulus polyanthus, commonly known as the Persian violet, is a flowering plant belonging to the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae).

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and showy, funnel-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant purple or violet blooms that are often used in gardens for their ornamental value. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and often hairy.

Convolvulus polyanthus thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.
