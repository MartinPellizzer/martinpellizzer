Centaurea ruthenica

Centaurea ruthenica, commonly known as the Crimean knapweed, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking blue to purple flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and steppe environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often branched and hairy.

Centaurea ruthenica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
