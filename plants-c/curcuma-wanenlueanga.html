Curcuma wanenlueanga

Curcuma wanenlueanga, commonly known as the Wanenlueanga turmeric, is a member of the Zingiberaceae family, native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, rhizomatous growth habit and produces strikingly colored flowers, often in shades of yellow and orange. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires a humid, warm climate for optimal growth. Its leaves are broad and sword-shaped, while its flowering stems can reach several feet in height.

The rhizomes of this plant are valued for their potential medicinal properties, similar to other curcuma species.
