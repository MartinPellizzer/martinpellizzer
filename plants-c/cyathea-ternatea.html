Cyathea ternatea

Cyathea ternatea, commonly known as the Ternate tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its rhizome system supports the growth of its massive fronds, and it plays an important ecological role in its native habitat.

Cyathea ternatea is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and resilience.
