Chlorospatha cutucuensis

Chlorospatha cutucuensis, commonly known as the Cutucu sunflower, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in open areas with well-drained soil, often in dry or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
