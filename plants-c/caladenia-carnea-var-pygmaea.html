Caladenia carnea var. pygmaea

Caladenia carnea var. pygmaea, commonly known as the Pygmy Spider-orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to southern Australia.

It typically grows in heathlands and open forests, often in well-drained soils. The plant features a slender, hairy stem with a single, narrow leaf and a delicate, spider-like flower. Its pink to mauve petals and sepals are adorned with intricate markings, and the labellum is distinctive for its narrow, elongated shape.

This variety is part of the Caladenia genus, known for its unique and often striking floral morphology.
