Ceanothus prostratus var. laxus

Ceanothus prostratus var. laxus, commonly known as the low ceanothus, is a low-growing shrub native to the western United States.

It features small, fragrant, blue to purple flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to dry, sunny environments.
