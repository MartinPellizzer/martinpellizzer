Carex approximata f. interrupta

Carex approximata f. interrupta, commonly known as the interrupted sedge, is a flowering plant species belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows. Its flowers are small and clustered, with three stamens and a three-lobed pistil.

The subspecies 'interrupta' is distinguished by its interrupted or irregular arrangement of flowering structures along the inflorescence.
