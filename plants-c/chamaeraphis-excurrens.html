Chamaeraphis excurrens

Chamaeraphis excurrens, commonly known as the sea bladdergrass, is a halophytic plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean and the eastern Mediterranean.

It thrives in saline environments, such as salt marshes and tidal flats, due to its specialized adaptations to high salinity. The plant has small, narrow leaves and produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds, which aid in its propagation in coastal habitats. Its root system is adapted to tolerate waterlogged and salty soils, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.

Chamaeraphis excurrens plays a role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
