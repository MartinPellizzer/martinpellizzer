Carduus carlinoides

Carduus carlinoides, commonly known as the yellow cock's-comb, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and foliage with rough, hairy surfaces. The plant produces yellow ray flowers surrounded by a dense array of tubular florets, which are adapted for pollination by insects. Its leaves are typically lobed and have a distinctive, spiny appearance.

Carduus carlinoides is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
