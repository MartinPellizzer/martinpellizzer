Citrus × jambhiri

Citrus × jambhiri, commonly known as the kaffir lime.

This hybrid citrus plant is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive, deeply grooved, greenish-yellow fruit. The leaves are dark green, glossy, and oval-shaped, while the fruit has a thick, aromatic rind and segmented white flesh. It is widely cultivated for its fragrant zest and juice, often used in cooking and traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring well-drained soil and consistent moisture.
