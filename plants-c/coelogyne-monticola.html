Coelogyne monticola

Coelogyne monticola, commonly known as the mountain orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically produces dark green, lance-shaped leaves and has a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchid species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Coelogyne monticola is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
