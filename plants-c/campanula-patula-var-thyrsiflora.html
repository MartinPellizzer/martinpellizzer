Campanula patula var. thyrsiflora

Campanula patula var. thyrsiflora, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and prolonged flowering period, typically from late spring to early autumn.
