Cardamine paucijuga

Cardamine paucijuga, commonly known as small-leaved watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to moist, shaded environments in North America.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes other members like mustard and cabbage. The plant features delicate, trifoliate leaves that are often submerged or floating on the surface of water. Its small, white flowers are borne in clusters and attract various pollinators.

Cardamine paucijuga is valued for its nutritional content and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
