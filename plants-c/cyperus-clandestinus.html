Cyperus clandestinus

Cyperus clandestinus, commonly known as the hidden sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in saturated soils. Its slender, grass-like stems are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.

Cyperus clandestinus is known for its ability to tolerate a wide range of environmental conditions, making it adaptable to various wetland ecosystems.
