Crataegus × rubrinervis var. aberrans

Crataegus × rubrinervis var. aberrans, commonly known as the aberrant hawthorn, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-veined leaves, which give it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows as a small shrub or tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, red berries in the fall.

This variant is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
