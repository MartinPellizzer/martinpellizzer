Calathea ursina

Calathea ursina, commonly known as the "Ursine Calathea," is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, mottled leaves that display a range of colors, including green, silver, and pink. The plant belongs to the Marantaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. Its foliage is often adorned with intricate patterns that resemble the fur of a bear, giving it its common name.

Calathea ursina prefers humid environments and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
