Clematis azurea

Clematis azurea, commonly known as the blue clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Clematis azurea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
