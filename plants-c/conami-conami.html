Conami conami

Conami conami, commonly known as the ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its lack of leaves, relying instead on its roots for nutrients. The plant produces delicate, white, fragrant flowers that bloom once a year, typically in late summer. It thrives in shaded, moist environments such as swamps and pine flatwoods.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered an endangered species.
