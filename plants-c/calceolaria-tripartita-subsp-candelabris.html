Calceolaria tripartita subsp. candelabris

Calceolaria tripartita subsp.

candelabris, commonly known as the candlestick calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that resemble candlesticks, typically in shades of red and orange.

The plant has hairy, upright stems and produces clusters of small, showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
