Colchicum hierrense

Colchicum hierrense, commonly known as the Hierrense meadow saffron, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Colchicaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a tall, slender stem topped with a single, showy flower that has six petals and a prominent stamen. Its bulbs are toxic and contain the alkaloid colchicine, which has been used in medicine.

Colchicum hierrense thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows.
