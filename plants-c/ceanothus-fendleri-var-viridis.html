Ceanothus fendleri var. viridis

Ceanothus fendleri var. viridis, commonly known as greenleaf ceanothus, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that ranges from dark green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale blue flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
