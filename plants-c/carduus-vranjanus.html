Carduus vranjanus

Carduus vranjanus, commonly known as the Balkan thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a distinctive heart-shaped base.

The plant is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.
