Croton glandulosus var. surinamensis

Croton glandulosus var. surinamensis, commonly known as Surinamese croton, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Suriname and Guyana.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded areas of the undergrowth.

Its ornamental value is due to its vibrant foliage and distinctive fruiting structures.
