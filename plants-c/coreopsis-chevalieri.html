Coreopsis chevalieri

Coreopsis chevalieri, commonly known as the narrowleaf coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils along stream banks and in swampy areas. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.
