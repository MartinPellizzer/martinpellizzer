Cerasus mahaleb var. fructuflavo

Cerasus mahaleb var. fructuflavo, commonly known as the yellow-fruited cherry, is a rare variant of the Mahaleb cherry.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-orange fruits, which contrast with the typical red or pink of other cherry varieties. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, oval-shaped berries that are often used in culinary applications.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique fruit color.
