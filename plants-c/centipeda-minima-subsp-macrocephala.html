Centipeda minima subsp. macrocephala

Centipeda minima subsp.

macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed centipeda, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

The plant features slender, hairy stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Its flowers are tiny, greenish-white blooms that appear in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is notable for its compact size and ability to thrive in humid, low-light conditions.
