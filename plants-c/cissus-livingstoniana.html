Cissus livingstoniana

Cissus livingstoniana, commonly known as the Livingston's devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or silver markings. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by winged fruits, which contain seeds. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
