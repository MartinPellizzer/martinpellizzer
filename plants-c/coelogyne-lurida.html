Coelogyne lurida

Coelogyne lurida, commonly known as the purple-tinged orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves and vibrant, fragrant flowers that range from white to purple. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters and are often adorned with intricate patterns and a subtle fragrance.

Coelogyne lurida is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated for both horticultural and conservation purposes.
