Ceropegia tomentosa

Ceropegia tomentosa, commonly known as the velvet leaf or string of hearts, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and fleshy, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or yellowish in color. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia tomentosa is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
