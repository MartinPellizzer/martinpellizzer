Cyathea verruculosa

Cyathea verruculosa, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk covered in a dense layer of brown, velvety spores. The plant has large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Cyathea verruculosa plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and contributing to forest biodiversity.
