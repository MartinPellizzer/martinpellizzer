Callitriche brevifolia

Callitriche brevifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf water starwort, is a small aquatic plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds, streams, and ditches. The plant has slender, branching stems that are often floating or partially submerged, with small, opposite leaves that are oval to linear in shape. Its flowers are tiny, white or pale blue, and clustered in dense, star-like inflorescences.

Callitriche brevifolia plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
