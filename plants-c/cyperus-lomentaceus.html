Cyperus lomentaceus

Cyperus lomentaceus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically wind-pollinated. It is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration projects.
