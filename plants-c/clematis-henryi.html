Clematis henryi

Clematis henryi, commonly known as Chinese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five oval leaflets and produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens for its climbing habit.

Clematis henryi is relatively hardy and can tolerate mild frosts, making it suitable for temperate climates.
