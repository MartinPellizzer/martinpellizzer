Convolvulus mollis

Convolvulus mollis, commonly known as silky morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal areas and rocky habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate blooms.
