Crepis shawanensis

Crepis shawanensis, commonly known as the Shawan mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, silver-gray leaves and daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces numerous small seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across rugged terrains.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
