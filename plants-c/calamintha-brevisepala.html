Calamintha brevisepala

Calamintha brevisepala, commonly known as narrow-leaved germander, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in dense clusters. The plant has aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Calamintha brevisepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in treating digestive ailments.
