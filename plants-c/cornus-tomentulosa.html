Cornus tomentulosa

Cornus tomentulosa, commonly known as the gray dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy leaves and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Cornus tomentulosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
