Citrullus lanatus var. fistulosus

Citrullus lanatus var. fistulosus, commonly known as the horned melon, is a unique variant of the watermelon species.

It is characterized by its thick, spiky rind that resembles a hornet's nest, providing a distinctive appearance. The plant produces large, green, lobed leaves and thrives in warm, tropical climates. Its fruit contains a sweet, juicy pulp with a mild melon flavor, often enjoyed fresh or in various culinary applications.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and edible qualities.
