Cephalaria uralensis var. cretacea

Cephalaria uralensis var. cretacea, commonly known as the Cretan cephalaria, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and clustered, yellowish to greenish flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture, while its flowers bloom in late summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
