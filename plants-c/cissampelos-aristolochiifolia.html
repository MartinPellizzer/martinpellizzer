Cissampelos aristolochiifolia

Cissampelos aristolochiifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved moon vine, is a climbing shrub native to tropical Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves and produces striking, pipe-shaped flowers that are often purple or greenish in color. The plant is part of the Menispermaceae family and is characterized by its complex, aromatic compounds. Its roots and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
