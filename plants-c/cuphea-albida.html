Cuphea albida

Cuphea albida, commonly known as the white candle bush, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its striking white, candle-like flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Cuphea albida is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
