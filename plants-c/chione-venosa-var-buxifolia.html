Chione venosa var. buxifolia

Chione venosa var. buxifolia, commonly known as the silver-leafed snowbell, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, leathery leaves that are typically silver-white to gray-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, bell-shaped white flowers in early spring, which hang in clusters from the branches. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, acidic soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
