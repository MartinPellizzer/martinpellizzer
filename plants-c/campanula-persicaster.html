Campanula persicaster

Campanula persicaster, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features slender stems that can grow up to 60 cm in height, adorned with bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to violet. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Campanula persicaster is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its delicate, showy blooms.
