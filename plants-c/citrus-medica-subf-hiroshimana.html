Citrus medica subf. hiroshimana

Citrus medica subf.

hiroshimana, commonly known as the Hiroshima lemon, is a lesser-known citrus variety originating from Japan. It is characterized by its small, round fruits with a thin, yellowish rind and a high juice content.

The plant typically grows as a small shrub or tree, with glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. It is adapted to subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is primarily cultivated for its edible citrus pulp, which is used in culinary and medicinal applications.
