Cyathea grangaudiana

Cyathea grangaudiana, commonly known as the Grangaud's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and a crown of large, feather-like fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests and humid woodlands. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting its upright growth form.

Cyathea grangaudiana plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
