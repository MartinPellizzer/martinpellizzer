Carex rhynchophysa

Carex rhynchophysa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered spikes of greenish-white flowers in the summer, and its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies.

Carex rhynchophysa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
