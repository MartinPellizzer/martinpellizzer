Calceolaria petioalaris var. oblongifolia

Calceolaria petioalaris var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the oblong-leaf calceolaria, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pouch-like flowers that come in various colors, including red, orange, and yellow. The leaves are elongated and oblong, with a hairy texture that helps in water retention. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

This variant is closely related to other species in the Calceolaria genus, known for their unique floral structures.
