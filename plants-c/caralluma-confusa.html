Caralluma confusa

Caralluma confusa, commonly known as the horned nightshade, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of India and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, spiny stems and small, often clustered flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in dry conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy leaves and stems. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange and bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators.

The plant contains various alkaloids and has been used traditionally in some cultures for its purported medicinal properties.
