Carex stenophylla var. reptabunda

Carex stenophylla var. reptabunda, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets of flowers. This variety is typically found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
