Cyrtosperma syapense

Cyrtosperma syapense, commonly known as the Sunda arrowroot, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its large, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers that are enclosed in a spadix, typical of the Araceae family. Its rhizomes are used as a starchy food source in some regions.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
