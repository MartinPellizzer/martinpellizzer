Corybas boridiensis

Corybas boridiensis, commonly known as the Boridien's hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the genus Corybas, which includes various species of hooded orchids characterized by their distinctive, often showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its flowers are usually dark-colored and have a unique, hood-like structure that protects the reproductive organs.

Corybas boridiensis is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in challenging ecological conditions.
