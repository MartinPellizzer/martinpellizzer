Carex vaginosa

Carex vaginosa, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and related plants. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in three ranks, a characteristic feature of the sedge family. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in a cylindrical inflorescence.

Carex vaginosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and woodland edges.
