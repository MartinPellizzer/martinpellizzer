Cereus fossulatus

Cereus fossulatus, commonly known as the night bloomer or moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and ability to produce large, showy white flowers at night. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in well-draining soil, making it suitable for arid environments. Its flowers emit a sweet fragrance, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths.

Cereus fossulatus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habits.
