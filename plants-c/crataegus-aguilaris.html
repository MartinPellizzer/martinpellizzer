Crataegus aguilaris

Crataegus aguilaris, commonly known as the Mexican hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Crataegus aguilaris is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and supporting biodiversity.
