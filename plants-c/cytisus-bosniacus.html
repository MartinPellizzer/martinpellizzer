Cytisus bosniacus

Cytisus bosniacus, commonly known as the Bosnian broom, is a deciduous shrub native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its woody stems are often covered in fine, silky hairs, and it thrives in well-drained, acidic soils.

Cytisus bosniacus is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
