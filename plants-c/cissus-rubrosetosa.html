Cissus rubrosetosa

Cissus rubrosetosa, commonly known as the red-leafed cissus, is a climbing plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-colored leaves, which are often variegated with green and yellow hues. The plant features small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright orange berries. Its stems are woody and can trail or climb, making it suitable for hanging baskets or trellises.

The leaves are typically serrated and have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
