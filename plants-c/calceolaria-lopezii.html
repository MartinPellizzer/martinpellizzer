Calceolaria lopezii

Calceolaria lopezii, commonly known as the Lopez's slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, slipper-shaped flowers that range in color from violet to red. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, preferring cool and moist conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine, velvety texture.

Calceolaria lopezii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
