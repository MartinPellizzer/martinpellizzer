Cyclobalanus llanosii

Cyclobalanus llanosii, commonly known as the Llanos palm, is a species of palm native to the Llanos region of South America.

It is a tall, slender tree that can reach heights of up to 15 meters, with a straight trunk and a crown of pinnate leaves. The plant has a robust root system that helps it survive in the seasonally flooded grasslands where it grows. Its fruit is a small, yellowish drupe that contains a single seed, and it plays an important role in the local ecosystem by providing food for various animals.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
