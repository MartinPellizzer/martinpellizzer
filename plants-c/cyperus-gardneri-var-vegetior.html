Cyperus gardneri var. vegetior

Cyperus gardneri var. vegetior, commonly known as the broadleaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers. It is characterized by its robust growth and ability to thrive in saturated soils.

Botanically, it is part of the Cyperaceae family, known as the sedge family, and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
