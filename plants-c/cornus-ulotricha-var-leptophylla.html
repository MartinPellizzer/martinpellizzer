Cornus ulotricha var. leptophylla

Cornus ulotricha var. leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaf dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Its bark is smooth and gray, with a distinctive flaky texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.
