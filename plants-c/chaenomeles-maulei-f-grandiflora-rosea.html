Chaenomeles maulei f. grandiflora-rosea

Chaenomeles maulei f. grandiflora-rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered Chinese quince, is a compact shrub native to China.

It features showy, rose-pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves appear. The plant has ovate, glossy dark green leaves and produces small, ornamental fruits that remain on the plant throughout the winter. It is well-suited for small gardens and can be grown in USDA hardiness zones 6-8.

The cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and cold hardiness.
