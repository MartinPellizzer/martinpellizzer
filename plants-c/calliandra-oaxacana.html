Calliandra oaxacana

Calliandra oaxacana, commonly known as the Mexican coral tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, colorful, feathery inflorescences that range from pink to red. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
