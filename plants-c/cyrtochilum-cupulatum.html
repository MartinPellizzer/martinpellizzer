Cyrtochilum cupulatum

Cyrtochilum cupulatum, commonly known as the hooded orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive hood-like structure formed by the fused sepals and petals, which enclose the reproductive organs. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories where it can receive indirect light. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with a variety of colors including white, yellow, and green.

Cyrtochilum cupulatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts for its unique appearance.
