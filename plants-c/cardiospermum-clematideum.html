Cardiospermum clematideum

Cardiospermum clematideum, commonly known as the heartleaf clematis, is a climbing vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that are clustered in cymes. Its fruit is a distinctive, inflated, three-lobed capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The botanical characteristics of this species make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
