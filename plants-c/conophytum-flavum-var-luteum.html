Conophytum flavum var. luteum

Conophytum flavum var. luteum, commonly known as the yellow conophytum, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, triangular leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a vibrant touch to its appearance. Its compact growth habit and unique leaf structure make it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor collections.

It requires well-draining soil and minimal water to prevent root rot.
