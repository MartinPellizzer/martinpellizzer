Cirsium tauricum

Cirsium tauricum, commonly known as the Tauric thistle, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs and have a rough texture, while its stems are sturdy and often branched.

Cirsium tauricum plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various insects and small animals.
