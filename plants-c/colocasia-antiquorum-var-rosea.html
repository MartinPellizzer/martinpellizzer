Colocasia antiquorum var. rosea

Colocasia antiquorum var. rosea, commonly known as the pink elephant ear, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 feet in diameter. The leaves are typically a striking shade of pink to red, with a velvety texture. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its dramatic foliage.
