Cosmea crithmifolia

Cosmea crithmifolia, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or purple composite flowers. The plant features silver-gray foliage with a fuzzy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Cosmea crithmifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
