Chironia occidentalis

Chironia occidentalis, commonly known as the western chironia, is a flowering plant native to the western regions of North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, clustered inflorescences that produce small, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
