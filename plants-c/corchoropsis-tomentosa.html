Corchoropsis tomentosa

Corchoropsis tomentosa, commonly known as silky cottonwood, is a shrub or small tree native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its soft, fuzzy leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is adapted to survive in harsh environments with low rainfall and poor soil conditions.

Its fibrous bark and leaves have been used traditionally for various purposes, including fiber extraction and medicinal applications.
