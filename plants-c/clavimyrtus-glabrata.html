Clavimyrtus glabrata

Clavimyrtus glabrata, commonly known as the smooth myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, fragrant white or pink flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like birds and insects.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
