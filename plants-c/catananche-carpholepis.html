Catananche carpholepis

Catananche carpholepis, commonly known as the purple milkvetch, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant features slender, branched stems and small, clustered purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are pinnately divided, with a silvery-blue appearance due to a dense covering of fine hairs.

Catananche carpholepis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
