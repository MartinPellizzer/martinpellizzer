Calliandra erythrocephala

Calliandra erythrocephala, commonly known as the red-headed calliandra, is a leguminous shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive red and yellow floral clusters, which are arranged in dense, spherical inflorescences. The plant has compound leaves with small, elliptical leaflets and is often used as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments.

Additionally, it is valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in agricultural settings.
