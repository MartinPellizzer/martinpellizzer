Convolvulus naniflorus

Convolvulus naniflorus, commonly known as the dwarf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant has hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in rocky or sandy habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its blooming period typically occurs in the spring and early summer.
