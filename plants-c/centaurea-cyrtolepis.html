Centaurea cyrtolepis

Centaurea cyrtolepis, commonly known as the narrowleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces vibrant blue or purple flowers.

Centaurea cyrtolepis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.
