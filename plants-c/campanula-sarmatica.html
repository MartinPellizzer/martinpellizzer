Campanula sarmatica

Campanula sarmatica, commonly known as the Carpathian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in woodland and mountainous regions, preferring moist, well-drained soils. The plant features erect stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. Its leaves are lanceolate and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Campanula sarmatica is valued in gardens for its attractive blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
