Chrysosplenium pilosum

Chrysosplenium pilosum, commonly known as the downy chrysosplenium, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive fuzzy appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its foliage is sometimes used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
