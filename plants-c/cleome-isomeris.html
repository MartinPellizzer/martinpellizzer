Cleome isomeris

Cleome isomeris, commonly known as the common cleome, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features erect, branching stems that are often hairy and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom from summer to fall, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with serrated edges, and the plant is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil types.

Cleome isomeris is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
