Clematis chrysocarpa subsp. bijuga

Clematis chrysocarpa subsp.

bijuga, commonly known as the golden fruit clematis, is a flowering plant native to parts of East Asia. It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its foliage is typically trifoliate, with heart-shaped leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its blooms.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
