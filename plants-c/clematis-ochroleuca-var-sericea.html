Clematis ochroleuca var. sericea

Clematis ochroleuca var. sericea, commonly known as the silky clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and heart-shaped leaves with a silvery-white underside. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of white to pale blue. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate foliage and long blooming period.
