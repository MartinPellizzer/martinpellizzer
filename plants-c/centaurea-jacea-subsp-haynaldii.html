Centaurea jacea subsp. haynaldii

Centaurea jacea subsp.

haynaldii, commonly known as Haynald's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant features upright stems with blue to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and hardy nature.
