Cornus atrata

Cornus atrata, commonly known as red osier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its striking red stems that become more prominent in the winter. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in the autumn.

Cornus atrata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
