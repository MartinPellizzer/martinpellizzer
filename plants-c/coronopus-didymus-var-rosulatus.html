Coronopus didymus var. rosulatus

Coronopus didymus var. rosulatus, commonly known as the Rosulate Coronopus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing perennial with small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features a rosette of fleshy, spoon-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hair. Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to dry, rocky environments.

It is primarily grown as an ornamental plant in gardens due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
