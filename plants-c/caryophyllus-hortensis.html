Caryophyllus hortensis

Caryophyllus hortensis, commonly known as garden carnation, is a flowering plant belonging to the Caryophyllaceae family.

It is characterized by its showy, colorful flowers that typically bloom in clusters on tall, branching stems. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are often used in ornamental gardening due to their vibrant hues and long-lasting blooms.

Caryophyllus hortensis is also valued for its historical use in perfumery and as a source of essential oils.
