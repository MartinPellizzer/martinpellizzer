Cistus affinis

Cistus affinis, commonly known as the Spanish rock rose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula and northern Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. It is often used in traditional medicine and has a long history of cultivation in horticulture.

Its essential oils are valued for their fragrance and therapeutic properties.
