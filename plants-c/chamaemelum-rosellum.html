Chamaemelum rosellum

Chamaemelum rosellum, commonly known as the common tansy, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small yellow flowers arranged in clusters, with feathery leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Due to its toxic properties, it is considered a weed in agricultural settings.
