Chamaecladon lanceolatum

Chamaecladon lanceolatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved chamaecladon, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Southwestern United States and Northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in terminal clusters during the spring and early summer. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, forming dense mats that help stabilize sandy soils.

Chamaecladon lanceolatum is well adapted to drought conditions and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.
