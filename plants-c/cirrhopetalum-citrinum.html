Cirrhopetalum citrinum

Cirrhopetalum citrinum, commonly known as the yellow orchid, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers with intricate petal patterns. The plant typically grows in tropical rainforests, thriving in humid and shaded environments. Its roots are fibrous, and it produces a fragrant bloom that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
