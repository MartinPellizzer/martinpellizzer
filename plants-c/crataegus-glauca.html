Crataegus glauca

Crataegus glauca, commonly known as the smooth crabapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits that turn from green to red or yellow as they mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.

Crataegus glauca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food for birds and pollinators.
