Carex indosinica

Carex indosinica, commonly known as the Indochina sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Carex indosinica is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
