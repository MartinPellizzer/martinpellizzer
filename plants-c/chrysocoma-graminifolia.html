Chrysocoma graminifolia

Chrysocoma graminifolia, commonly known as the grassy chrysocoma, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in open woodland and grassland habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in terminal clusters. Its flowering period usually occurs during the warmer months, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
