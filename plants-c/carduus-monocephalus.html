Carduus monocephalus

Carduus monocephalus, commonly known as the singlehead thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple to pink flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant has deeply lobed, silvery-green leaves with rough textures and a distinct, bitter taste. It thrives in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wastelands, and along roadsides.

Carduus monocephalus is known for its medicinal properties and has been used historically in traditional herbal remedies.
