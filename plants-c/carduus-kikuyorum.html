Carduus kikuyorum

Carduus kikuyorum, commonly known as the Kenyan thistle, is a perennial herb native to East Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates. Its leaves are covered in stiff, prickly hairs, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores.

The flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
