Cardamine brachycarpa

Cardamine brachycarpa, commonly known as the short-fruited watercress, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately divided leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its fruits are short and cylindrical, containing several seeds.

It is a perennial herb that plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
