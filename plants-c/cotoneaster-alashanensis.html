Cotoneaster alashanensis

Cotoneaster alashanensis, commonly known as the Alashan cotoneaster, is a species native to arid regions of China.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, pink or white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Cotoneaster alashanensis is adapted to harsh environments, showing resilience to drought and extreme temperatures.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration in semi-arid areas.
