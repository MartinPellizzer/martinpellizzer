Capsicum parvifolium var. sellowianum

Capsicum parvifolium var. sellowianum, commonly known as the Sellow's pepper, is a wild species native to South America.

It is a shrub-like plant with small, narrow leaves and produces yellow or orange fruits. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in dry, rocky habitats. Its botanical features include hairy stems and flowers that are typically yellow or green.

This variety is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its potential for genetic diversity and adaptability.
