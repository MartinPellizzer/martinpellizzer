Cicer canariense

Cicer canariense, commonly known as the Canary bean, is a leguminous plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain edible seeds. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to arid and saline conditions. It belongs to the genus Cicer, which includes other legumes like the chickpea.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
