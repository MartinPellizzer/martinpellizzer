Clypeola tomentosa

Clypeola tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly clypeola, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage and compact, rounded growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
