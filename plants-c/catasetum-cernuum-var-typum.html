Catasetum cernuum var. typum

Catasetum cernuum var. typum, commonly known as the hanging cat's tail orchid, is a tropical orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, hairy inflorescence that bears small, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical features include elongated pseudobulbs and fibrous roots, which help it survive in its natural habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
