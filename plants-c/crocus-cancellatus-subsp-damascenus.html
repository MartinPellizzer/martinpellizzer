Crocus cancellatus subsp. damascenus

Crocus cancellatus subsp.

damascenus, commonly known as the Persian saffron crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family. It is native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe, where it thrives in temperate climates.

The plant is characterized by its vibrant purple or white flowers, which bloom in early spring. Its most distinctive feature is the striking, intricate pattern of its petals, which resemble a checkerboard.

The plant is also valued for its stamens, which are used in the production of saffron, a highly prized spice and dye.
