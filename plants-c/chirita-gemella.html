Chirita gemella

Chirita gemella, commonly known as the dwarf geranium, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing herbaceous perennial with velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers that range from pink to purple. The plant produces five-petaled flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a favored plant in both garden and horticultural settings.
