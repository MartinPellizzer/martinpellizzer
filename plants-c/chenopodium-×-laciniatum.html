Chenopodium × laciniatum

Chenopodium × laciniatum, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a hybrid plant that belongs to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its lobed, spinach-like leaves that are tender and edible, often used in cooking. The plant is native to New Zealand but has been introduced to other parts of the world, including North America. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

Chenopodium × laciniatum is valued for its nutritional content, including high levels of vitamins and minerals.
