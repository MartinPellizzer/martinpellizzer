Chenopodium berlandieri

Chenopodium berlandieri, commonly known as prickly goosefoot, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, spiny leaves and edible, tangy berries. The plant thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in prairies, roadsides, and open woods. Its leaves are typically broad and lobed, with a rough texture and a distinctive prickly margin.

It is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used by indigenous peoples for various purposes.
