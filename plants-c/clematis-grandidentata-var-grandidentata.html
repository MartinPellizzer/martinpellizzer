Clematis grandidentata var. grandidentata

Clematis grandidentata var. grandidentata, commonly known as the large-toothed clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It features compound leaves with large, serrated leaflets that give it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of white, pink, or purple. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability in garden settings.
