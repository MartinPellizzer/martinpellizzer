Cattleya batalinii

Cattleya batalinii, commonly known as the Batalin's cattleya, is a species of orchid native to Brazil.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and yellow. The plant has a pseudobulb structure with broad, leathery leaves that help store water. Its inflorescence usually bears 3 to 5 blooms, each with a distinctive lip that is often marked with intricate patterns.

This orchid thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
