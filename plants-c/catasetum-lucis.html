Catasetum lucis

Catasetum lucis, commonly known as the dawn orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its unique structure, including specialized pollination mechanisms. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, adapted to capture sunlight efficiently.

Catasetum lucis thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
