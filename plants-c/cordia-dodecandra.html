Cordia dodecandra

Cordia dodecandra, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Boraginaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Cordia dodecandra is valued for its hard, durable wood and is used in traditional medicine and as a source of tannins.
