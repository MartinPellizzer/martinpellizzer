Campylotropis chinensis

Campylotropis chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese bottlebrush, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive inflorescences, which are dense clusters of small, bright red flowers resembling a bottlebrush. The plant has compound leaves with serrated margins and produces small, oval fruits that mature to a dark brown color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various pollinators and wildlife.
