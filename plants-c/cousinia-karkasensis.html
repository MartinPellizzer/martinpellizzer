Cousinia karkasensis

Cousinia karkasensis, commonly known as the Karkar Mountain cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are usually linear and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Cousinia karkasensis is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in dry gardens.
