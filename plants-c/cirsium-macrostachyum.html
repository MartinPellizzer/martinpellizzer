Cirsium macrostachyum

Cirsium macrostachyum, commonly known as the giant thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple to pinkish flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves with sharp spines along the margins and central veins. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
