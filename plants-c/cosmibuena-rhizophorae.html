Cosmibuena rhizophorae

Cosmibuena rhizophorae, commonly known as the mangrove apple, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It thrives in brackish water environments, often found in mangrove ecosystems where it plays a vital role in stabilizing shorelines. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are pollinated by insects, leading to the development of edible fruits. Its roots form dense networks that help filter sediments and improve water quality.

Cosmibuena rhizophorae is also valued for its ecological importance in supporting diverse marine and terrestrial life.
