Coreopsis basalis

Coreopsis basalis, commonly known as swamp coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes and swamps. The plant features upright, branching stems and produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
