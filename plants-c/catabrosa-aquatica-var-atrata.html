Catabrosa aquatica var. atrata

Catabrosa aquatica var. atrata, commonly known as the black water plant, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its dark, almost black stems and leaves.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, often found in the understory of dense, shaded wetlands. The plant's leaves are typically narrow and elongated, adapted for efficient light absorption in low-light conditions. It is known for its ability to tolerate a wide range of water conditions, making it a resilient species in aquatic ecosystems.

Its dark pigmentation is believed to be an adaptation to reduce photoinhibition in shaded habitats.
