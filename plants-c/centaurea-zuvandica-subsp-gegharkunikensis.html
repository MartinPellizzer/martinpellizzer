Centaurea zuvandica subsp. gegharkunikensis

Centaurea zuvandica subsp.

gegharkunikensis, commonly known as the Gegharkunik centaury, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive blue to purple flower heads.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is found in mountainous areas. It has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flowers.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
