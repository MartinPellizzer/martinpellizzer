Canna maculata

Canna maculata, commonly known as the spotted canna, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, colorful leaves that often have a distinctive red or purple blotch on the underside. The plant produces tall, showy spikes of orange, yellow, or red flowers, which bloom from summer to fall. Canna maculata is a perennial herb that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and full sun.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and water features.
