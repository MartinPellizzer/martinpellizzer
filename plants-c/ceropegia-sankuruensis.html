Ceropegia sankuruensis

Ceropegia sankuruensis, commonly known as the Sankuru milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its trailing stems and tuberous roots. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is often found growing on rocky outcrops.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
