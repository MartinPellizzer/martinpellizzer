Calycanthus floridus var. glaucus

Calycanthus floridus var. glaucus, commonly known as the sweetshrub or Florida calycanthus, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features aromatic, showy flowers that are typically pinkish-red with a yellow center, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it prefers well-drained soil in full to partial sun.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine.
