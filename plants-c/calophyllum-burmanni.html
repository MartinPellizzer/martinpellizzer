Calophyllum burmanni

Calophyllum burmanni, commonly known as Burmann's calophyllum, is a tropical plant belonging to the family Calophyllaceae.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish flowers that are typically arranged in clusters, followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed.

Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.
