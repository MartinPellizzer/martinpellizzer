Carex hirtiformis

Carex hirtiformis, commonly known as the hairy fringed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color, arranged in spikelets. Carex hirtiformis thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
