Corylus colurna var. trichochlamys

Corylus colurna var. trichochlamys, commonly known as the Turkish hazelnut, is a variant of the common hazelnut native to Turkey and the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaves that give it its scientific name "trichochlamys," meaning "hairy leaf.". The plant produces small, edible nuts enclosed in a cupule, which is typically brown and rough in texture. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its use in traditional cuisine and medicine.
