Cotoneaster integerrimus var. tomentosus

Cotoneaster integerrimus var. tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly cotoneaster, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and is often used as a hedging plant due to its durability and adaptability. The leaves are typically oval-shaped, with a distinctive woolly or tomentose underside, giving the plant its common name. It produces small, red berries that are attractive to birds, contributing to its ecological value.

The plant is relatively low-maintenance and thrives in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
