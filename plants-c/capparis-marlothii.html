Capparis marlothii

Capparis marlothii, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or wild tomato, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and striking yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible and have a tangy, acidic flavor. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along coastal regions.

Capparis marlothii is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional medicine.
