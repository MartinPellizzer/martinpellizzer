Carex paeninsulae

Carex paeninsulae, commonly known as the Peninsula sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wet, sandy soils along shorelines and tidal zones, demonstrating its adaptability to maritime environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair.

Carex paeninsulae plays an important ecological role by stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
