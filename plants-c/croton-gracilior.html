Croton gracilior

Croton gracilior, commonly known as the slender croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is characterized by its latex-producing plants. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green patterns. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are typically clustered in cup-like structures called cyathia.

Croton gracilior is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking foliage.
