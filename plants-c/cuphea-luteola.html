Cuphea luteola

Cuphea luteola, commonly known as the yellow beakweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with reddish hues. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in spikes, giving it a distinctive appearance. Cuphea luteola thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant floral display and ability to attract pollinators.
