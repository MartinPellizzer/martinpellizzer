Coelogyne taeniophylla

Coelogyne taeniophylla, commonly known as the ribbon-leafed orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers with long, narrow petals and a distinctive yellow and green coloration. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Coelogyne taeniophylla is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
