Cassia venatoria

Cassia venatoria, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has feathery, bipinnate leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Its bark is smooth and greyish, often with a faintly aromatic scent.

Cassia venatoria is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
