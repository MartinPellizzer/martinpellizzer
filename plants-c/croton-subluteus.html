Croton subluteus

Croton subluteus, commonly known as the yellow croton, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that range from yellow to green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant foliage.

Croton subluteus is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported therapeutic properties.
