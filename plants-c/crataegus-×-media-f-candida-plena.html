Crataegus × media f. candida-plena

Crataegus × media f. candida-plena, commonly known as the white-flowered hawthorn, is a cultivar of the hawthorn plant characterized by its dense, double white flowers.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Crataegus media and other hawthorn species, showcasing a unique combination of traits. The plant is typically deciduous, with small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its flowers appear in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens and landscapes.

The fruit, which follows the flowers, is a small, red berry that matures in late summer.
