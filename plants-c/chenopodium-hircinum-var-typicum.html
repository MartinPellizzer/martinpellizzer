Chenopodium hircinum var. typicum

Chenopodium hircinum var. typicum, commonly known as lamb's quarters, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Amaranthaceae family.

It is characterized by its alternate, broadly ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, especially on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is commonly found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Lamb's quarters is a nutritious plant, often used in traditional cuisines and forforage.
