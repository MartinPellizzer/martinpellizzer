Curcuma sulcata

Curcuma sulcata, commonly known as the ridge ginseng or mountain turmeric, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and turmeric. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and narrow, sword-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive ridge-like structure on the petals.

Curcuma sulcata is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
