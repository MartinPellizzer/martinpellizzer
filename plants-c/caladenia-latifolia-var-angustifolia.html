Caladenia latifolia var. angustifolia

Caladenia latifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf spider orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its slender, strap-like leaves and striking spider-like flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is found in regions with a Mediterranean climate. It blooms during the autumn season, producing clusters of pale green to yellowish flowers with intricate, elongated petals.

This variety is noted for its unique morphology and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
