Cirsium anatolicum

Cirsium anatolicum, commonly known as Anatolian thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Turkey and northern Syria.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
