Convolvulus mechoacan

Convolvulus mechoacan, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant produces vibrant purple to blue flowers that bloom in the early morning, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often twine around supports.

Convolvulus mechoacan is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various soil conditions.
