Chlora affinis

Chlora affinis, commonly known as the silverleaf chlorella, is a unicellular green alga belonging to the Chlorophyceae class.

It is characterized by its small, spherical cells covered in a layer of fine, silvery hairs, which give it its common name. This organism is typically found in freshwater environments, such as ponds and lakes, where it thrives in nutrient-rich waters. Chlora affinis is photosynthetic, utilizing chlorophyll a and b for energy conversion.

Its rapid growth rate makes it an important species in aquatic ecosystems and a potential candidate for biotechnological applications.
