Cucurbita rapallito

Cucurbita rapallito, commonly known as the Mexican gourd or horse gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and Central America, where it thrives in warm, sunny climates. The plant produces elongated, bottle-shaped fruits that can vary in color from green to yellow or orange. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often covered in a fine hair-like coating.

The fruit is typically harvested when mature, and it is used in various traditional cuisines for its mild, slightly sweet flavor.
