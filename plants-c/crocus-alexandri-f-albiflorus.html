Crocus alexandri f. albiflorus

Crocus alexandri f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered Alexandrian crocus, is a botanical variant of the Alexandrian crocus.

It is characterized by its pale white petals and is native to the Mediterranean region. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. It typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
