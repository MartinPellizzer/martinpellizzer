Cyathea oligocarpa

Cyathea oligocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant exhibits a distinctive appearance with a smooth, often greyish stem and compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. Cyathea oligocarpa is typically found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests and is known for its slow growth rate.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of tropical forest ecosystems.
