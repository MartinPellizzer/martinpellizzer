Charieis

Charieis, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the remote highlands of South America.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that appear to shimmer in the sunlight, giving it a ghostly appearance. The plant produces small, pale blossoms that emit a faint, bioluminescent glow at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Due to its elusive nature and limited habitat, Charieis is considered critically endangered.

Botanists study it closely to better understand its unique adaptations to extreme environmental conditions.
