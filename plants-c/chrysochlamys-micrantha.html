Chrysochlamys micrantha

Chrysochlamys micrantha, commonly known as the golden bell tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a glossy appearance.

The tree is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
