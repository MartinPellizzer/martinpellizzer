Carex glauca f. aristolepis

Carex glauca f. aristolepis, commonly known as the blue sedge, is a perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a bluish-green color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
