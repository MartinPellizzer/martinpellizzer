Cleisostomopsis

Cleisostomopsis, commonly known as the moonflower, is a genus of flowering plants in the nightshade family, Solanaceae.

These plants are characterized by their large, showy white flowers that open at night and close during the day. The leaves are typically ovate or lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Cleisostomopsis species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

They are often cultivated for their ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
