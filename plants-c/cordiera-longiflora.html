Cordiera longiflora

Cordiera longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered cordiera, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in whorls around the stem. Its most distinctive feature is its long, slender flowers that bloom in clusters, usually in shades of white or pale pink.

Cordiera longiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
