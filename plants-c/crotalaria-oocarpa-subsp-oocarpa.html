Crotalaria oocarpa subsp. oocarpa

Crotalaria oocarpa subsp.

oocarpa, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to South America. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy, branching stems.

The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a taproot system that aids in its establishment in various environments.
