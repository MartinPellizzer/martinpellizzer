Camellia bambusifolia

Camellia bambusifolia, commonly known as the bamboo-leaved camellia, is a unique evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its narrow, bamboo-like leaves that are alternate and often have a leathery texture. The plant produces striking, cup-shaped flowers in shades of red, pink, and white, typically blooming in winter and early spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is considered a valuable species in horticulture and conservation efforts.
