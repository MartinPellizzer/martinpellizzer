Calpidia cuspidata

Calpidia cuspidata, commonly known as the prickly calpidia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces small, tubular flowers that are typically purple or blue in color. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which help it retain moisture in its arid environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Calpidia cuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
