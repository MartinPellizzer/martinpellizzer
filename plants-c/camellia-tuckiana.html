Camellia tuckiana

Camellia tuckiana, commonly known as the Tuck's camellia, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is a evergreen shrub that produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. The plant is characterized by its leathery, elliptical leaves and compact growth habit. Camellia tuckiana thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
