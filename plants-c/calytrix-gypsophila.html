Calytrix gypsophila

Calytrix gypsophila, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae and is characterized by its small, pale pink or white flowers. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas. It has small, narrow leaves and produces fruit that is a capsule containing numerous seeds.

Calytrix gypsophila is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
