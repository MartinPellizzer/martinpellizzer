Clinopodium kilimandschari

Clinopodium kilimandschari, commonly known as the Kilimanjaro mint, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of East Africa.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and mountainous areas, thriving in cool, moist climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a minty fragrance, while its small flowers are clustered in whorls.

Clinopodium kilimandschari is often used in traditional medicine for its purported calming and digestive properties.
