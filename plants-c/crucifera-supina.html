Crucifera supina

Crucifera supina, commonly known as the prostrate mustard, is a low-growing annual plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in the spring and early summer. The plant has hairy, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its fruit is a slender, elongated silique, characteristic of the mustard family.

It is often found in disturbed soils and is used in some traditional herbal remedies.
