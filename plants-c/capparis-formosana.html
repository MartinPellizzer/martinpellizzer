Capparis formosana

Capparis formosana, commonly known as the Taiwan caper, is a flowering plant native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and woody stems. The plant typically grows in forested areas and has a shrub-like appearance with thorny branches. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, while its flowers are borne in clusters at the base of the leaves.

Capparis formosana is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
