Clerodendrum silvicola

Clerodendrum silvicola, commonly known as the forest clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within forests. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers, which are often accompanied by showy bracts. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
