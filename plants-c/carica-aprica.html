Carica aprica

Carica aprica, commonly known as the apricot, is a fruit-bearing tree native to Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, oval-shaped fruits with a fuzzy skin. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flowers in the spring, which are followed by the development of its edible, juicy fruits. Apricots are rich in vitamins A and C and are commonly used in cooking, baking, and as a fresh fruit.

The tree prefers well-drained soil and full sunlight to thrive.
