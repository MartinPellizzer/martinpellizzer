Carex praecox var. magna

Carex praecox var. magna, commonly known as the giant early sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and broad, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to brown flowers in the spring. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex praecox var. magna is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
