Cupressus obtusa subsp. formosana

Cupressus obtusa subsp.

formosana, commonly known as the Formosan lacebark cypress, is a subspecies native to Taiwan. It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its distinctive exfoliating bark that reveals a mottled pattern of green, white, and red.

The plant has scale-like leaves arranged in crossed pairs, giving it a feathery appearance. It produces small, spherical cones and is adapted to grow in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained conditions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
