Cochlearia violacea

Cochlearia violacea, commonly known as the purple cochlearia, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in rocky or coastal habitats. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are basal, rosette-like, and covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
