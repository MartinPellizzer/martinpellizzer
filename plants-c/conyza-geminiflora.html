Conyza geminiflora

Conyza geminiflora, commonly known as the twin-flowered cudweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers that typically bloom in clusters, with both ray and disk florets. Native to regions of South America, it is often found in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Conyza geminiflora is an annual herb that plays a role in local ecosystems, though it may be considered a weed in some agricultural settings.
