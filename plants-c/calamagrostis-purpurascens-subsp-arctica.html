Calamagrostis purpurascens subsp. arctica

Calamagrostis purpurascens subsp.

arctica, commonly known as Arctic moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to Arctic and alpine regions. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often reddish-colored stems.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically pale green to reddish-brown, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in nutrient-poor, moist to wet soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Its botanical features make it an important component of tundra and mountain ecosystems.
