Cysticapnos cracca

Cysticapnos cracca, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces a hard, dark wood that is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to overharvesting, it is now considered an endangered species in many regions.
