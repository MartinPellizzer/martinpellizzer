Carex silvestrii

Carex silvestrii, commonly known as the forest sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Carex silvestrii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
