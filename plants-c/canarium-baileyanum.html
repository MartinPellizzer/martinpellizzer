Canarium baileyanum

Canarium baileyanum, commonly known as the Bornean candlenut, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo.

It belongs to the family Burseraceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and simple, alternate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by oval, woody fruits containing a single seed. Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species in its native range.
