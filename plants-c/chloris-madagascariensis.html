Chloris madagascariensis

Chloris madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar grass, is a species of grass native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It is often found in grasslands and open areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in some regions for landscaping purposes.
