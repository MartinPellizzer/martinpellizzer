Chenopodium bushianum

Chenopodium bushianum, commonly known as the desert goosefoot, is a perennial plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically lobed or toothed, adapted to conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are often overlooked due to their modest appearance. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep water sources and survive prolonged droughts.

Chenopodium bushianum is often found in disturbed soils and is considered a pioneer species in ecological succession.
