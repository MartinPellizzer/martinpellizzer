Convolvulus schottianus

Convolvulus schottianus, commonly known as Schott's morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family and is characterized by its heliotropic leaves that follow the sun's movement. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, typically blooming in the morning. Its vines can grow up to several meters long, and it thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
