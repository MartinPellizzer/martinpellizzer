Chamaecrista guanensis

Chamaecrista guanensis, commonly known as the Brazilian burr legume, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural research.
