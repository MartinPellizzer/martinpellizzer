Crepis occidentalis var. nevadensis

Crepis occidentalis var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Sierra Basin mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Its leaves are usually hairy and basal, forming a rosette structure to conserve moisture.
