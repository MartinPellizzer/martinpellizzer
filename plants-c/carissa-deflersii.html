Carissa deflersii

Carissa deflersii, commonly known as the yellow bell tree, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and evergreen foliage. The plant produces fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract wildlife.

The species is also valued for its use in traditional medicine.
