Carex raoulii

Carex raoulii, commonly known as the Raoul's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, while its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets.

Carex raoulii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native wildlife.
