Cordylanthus tenuis subsp. capillaris

Cordylanthus tenuis subsp.

capillaris, commonly known as slenderleaf woollypod, is a perennial herb native to California. It features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, showy white or pink flowers in late spring.

The plant grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and chaparral. Its subspecies is distinguished by its fine, hairy stems and narrow leaves.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
