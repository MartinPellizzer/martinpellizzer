Calceolaria scapiflora

Calceolaria scapiflora, commonly known as the scapiflorous slipperwort, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as mountain forests and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

The flowers, which bloom in spring, are often violet or purple and attract various pollinators like bees and moths.
