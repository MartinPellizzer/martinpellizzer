Cyperus laevigatus f. atratus

Cyperus laevigatus f. atratus, commonly known as the black rush, is a variant of the common flatsedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves that are often tinged with purple or black, giving it its distinctive appearance. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color.

This plant is often found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies.
