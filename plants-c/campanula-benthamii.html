Campanula benthamii

Campanula benthamii, commonly known as the Bentham's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stem, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

Campanula benthamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive flowers and hardy nature.
