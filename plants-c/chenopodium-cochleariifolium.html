Chenopodium cochleariifolium

Chenopodium cochleariifolium, commonly known as lambsquarters, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its alternate, broadly ovate to triangular leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. Lambsquarters is often found in disturbed soils, such as fields and roadsides, and is considered a common weed.

It has a shallow root system and can grow in a variety of soil types.
