Clerodendrum canescens

Clerodendrum canescens, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Clerodendrum canescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
