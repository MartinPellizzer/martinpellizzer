Chaenorhinum origanifolium subsp. villosum

Chaenorhinum origanifolium subsp.

villosum, commonly known as the woolly heartleaf. It is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, characterized by its hairy, velvety foliage.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinct aromatic scent.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
