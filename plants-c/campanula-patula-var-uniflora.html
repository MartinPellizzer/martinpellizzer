Campanula patula var. uniflora

Campanula patula var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
