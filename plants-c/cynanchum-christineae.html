Cynanchum christineae

Cynanchum christineae, commonly known as Christine's milkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes other milkweeds known for their unique floral structures. The plant produces striking, hooded flowers that are typically pink or purple in color, with a distinctive shape that resembles a crown. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its rustic appearance.

The plant is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing its resilience and ecological significance in its native habitat.
