Corion rupestre

Corion rupestre, commonly known as the rock corion, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically thrives in rocky, well-drained soils. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. Its flowers are pale blue to purple in color, appearing in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Corion rupestre is adapted to arid conditions and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.
