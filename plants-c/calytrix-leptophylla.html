Calytrix leptophylla

Calytrix leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leaved calytrix, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in coastal regions and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 1 meter and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.

Calytrix leptophylla is also valued for its ecological role, providing nectar for native pollinators.
