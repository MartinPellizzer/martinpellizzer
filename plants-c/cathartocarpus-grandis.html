Cathartocarpus grandis

Cathartocarpus grandis, commonly known as the large-leaf sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its sprawling growth habit and succulent, fleshy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats.

Cathartocarpus grandis is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
