Collinsia bicolor var. typica

Collinsia bicolor var. typica, commonly known as the California popcornflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, bilaterally symmetrical flowers in shades of pink and white, typically arranged in a raceme. The plant has linear, grass-like leaves and produces small, fluffy seed heads that resemble popcorn, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in the Polemoniaceae family.
