Cornus alba f. argenteomarginata

Cornus alba f. argenteomarginata, commonly known as the silver-margin dogwood, is a cultivar of the Cornus alba species native to Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white-edged leaves that turn bright red in the fall, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by red berries that attract birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for woodland gardens or as a hedge.

Its unique foliage and seasonal color changes contribute to its aesthetic value in landscaping.
