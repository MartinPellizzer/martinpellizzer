Carex panicea f. humilis

Carex panicea f. humilis, commonly known as the dwarf panicled sedge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known for their distinctive morphology and ecological adaptability. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces fluffy, panicle-like inflorescences that are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet soils, making it a common sight in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its compact size and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
