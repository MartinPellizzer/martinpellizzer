Cruckshanksia macrantha

Cruckshanksia macrantha, commonly known as the giant orchid, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach up to three meters in height, supporting large, showy flowers. The plant belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is known for its intricate floral structure with a prominent lip petal. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and leaves that are narrow and grass-like.

Cruckshanksia macrantha thrives in cool, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
