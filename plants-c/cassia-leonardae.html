Cassia leonardae

Cassia leonardae, commonly known as Leonard's cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Cassia leonardae thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in warm climates.
