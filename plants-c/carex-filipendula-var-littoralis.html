Carex filipendula var. littoralis

Carex filipendula var. littoralis, commonly known as coastal sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to coastal regions of North America.

It thrives in moist, sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines and tidal zones. The plant has grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and tapering, with a bluish-green hue. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This variety is well adapted to salt spray and fluctuating water levels, making it an important component of coastal ecosystems.
