Chrysochlamys cuneata

Chrysochlamys cuneata, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round, red berries that are edible for birds and some small mammals. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Its ornamental value lies in its striking foliage and vibrant floral display.
