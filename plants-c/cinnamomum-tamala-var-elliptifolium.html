Cinnamomum tamala var. elliptifolium

Cinnamomum tamala var. elliptifolium, commonly known as Indian cinnamon or tamal, is a variant of the Cinnamomum tamala species native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its elliptical leaves that are dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by dark purple berries containing aromatic seeds. Its leaves are widely used in traditional medicine and as a spice due to their essential oils.

The botanical features of this plant make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
