Cirsium alberti

Cirsium alberti, commonly known as Albert's thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its spiny, purple-blue flower heads and deeply lobed, silvery-green leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and scrublands. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its medicinal properties.

Cirsium alberti is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
