Crataegus evansiana

Crataegus evansiana, commonly known as the Canadian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and features thorny branches, simple oval leaves, and white or pinkish flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and small mammals.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its clustered growth habit and persistent winter buds.
