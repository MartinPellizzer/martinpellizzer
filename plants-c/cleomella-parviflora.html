Cleomella parviflora

Cleomella parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cleomaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually pink or white, and they bloom throughout the growing season.

Cleomella parviflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
