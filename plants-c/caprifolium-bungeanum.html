Caprifolium bungeanum

Caprifolium bungeanum, commonly known as the Bunge's honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a tart flavor. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Caprifolium bungeanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty and fragrance.
