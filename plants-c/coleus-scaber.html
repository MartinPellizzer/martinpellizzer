Coleus scaber

Coleus scaber, commonly known as roughleaf coleus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Lamiaceae, which includes many aromatic herbs and ornamental plants. The plant is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves that are often brightly colored in shades of red, purple, and green. Its small, tubular flowers are typically clustered in whorls along the stems.

Coleus scaber is primarily cultivated for its ornamental value and is popular in gardens and as a houseplant.
