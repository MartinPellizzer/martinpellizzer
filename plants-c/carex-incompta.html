Carex incompta

Carex incompta, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue.

Carex incompta produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.
