Citrus × bigaradia var. multiflora

Citrus × bigaradia var. multiflora, commonly known as the rough lemon.

This hybrid citrus plant is characterized by its thorny branches and oval, leathery leaves. It produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically not eaten due to their high acidity and bitter taste. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is often used as a rootstock in citrus cultivation.

Its botanical features make it an important species in horticultural studies and breeding programs.
