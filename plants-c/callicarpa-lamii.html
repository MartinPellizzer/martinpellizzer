Callicarpa lamii

Callicarpa lamii, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, violet to blue-black berries that appear in late summer. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various birds and small mammals.
