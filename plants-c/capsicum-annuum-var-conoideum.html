Capsicum annuum var. conoideum

Capsicum annuum var. conoideum, commonly known as the cone-shaped pepper, is a variant of the common chili pepper.

It is characterized by its elongated, conical fruit shape, which distinguishes it from other Capsicum species. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with hairy stems and lobed leaves. Its flowers are yellow and bilaterally symmetrical, typical of the Solanaceae family.

The fruit matures to a bright red or orange color, containing numerous seeds and used in various culinary applications.
