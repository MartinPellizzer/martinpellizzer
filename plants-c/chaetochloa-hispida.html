Chaetochloa hispida

Chaetochloa hispida, commonly known as the hairy grass or rough hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and marshy environments.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth and hairy leaves that give it its common name. The plant has narrow, linear leaves with a rough texture, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Chaetochloa hispida plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
