Clerodendrum brassii

Clerodendrum brassii, commonly known as the African flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, often attracting pollinators like birds and insects. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Clerodendrum brassii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be grown in warm climates as a perennial or in containers.
