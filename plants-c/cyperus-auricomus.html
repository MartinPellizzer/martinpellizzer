Cyperus auricomus

Cyperus auricomus, commonly known as the golden rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically brown or reddish in color. Cyperus auricomus thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along water bodies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in humid climates.
