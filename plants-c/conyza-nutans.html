Conyza nutans

Conyza nutans, commonly known as the common blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Africa and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, contributing to its widespread distribution. It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and other open areas.

Conyza nutans is often considered a weed but has some uses in traditional medicine.
