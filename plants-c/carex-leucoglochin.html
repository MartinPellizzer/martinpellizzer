Carex leucoglochin

Carex leucoglochin, commonly known as white-flowered sedge, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in dense spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex leucoglochin is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
