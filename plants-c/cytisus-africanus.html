Cytisus africanus

Cytisus africanus, commonly known as the African broom, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its small, yellow, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small leaflets and produces pods that contain seeds. It is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Cytisus africanus is also known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
