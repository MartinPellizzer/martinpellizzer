Cimicifuga frigida

Cimicifuga frigida, commonly known as black cohosh, is a perennial herb native to the cold regions of North America.

It grows in woodland areas and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, compound leaves. The plant produces showy, white to pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. Its roots and rhizomes are the primary parts used in herbal medicine.

Cimicifuga frigida is valued for its potential therapeutic properties, particularly in women's health.
