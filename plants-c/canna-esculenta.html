Canna esculenta

Canna esculenta, commonly known as the elephant ear, is a striking tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in diameter. The plant produces showy, spadix-shaped flowers surrounded by brightly colored bracts, typically in shades of red, yellow, or orange. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Canna esculenta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can also be used in traditional cuisines.
