Cheiranthus linariifolius

Cheiranthus linariifolius, commonly known as the linaria-leaved candytuft, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers resembling candytuft. The plant features linear, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cheiranthus linariifolius is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
