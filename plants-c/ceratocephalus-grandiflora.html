Ceratocephalus grandiflora

Ceratocephalus grandiflora, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in open grasslands and has a bushy, compact growth habit. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, fluffy seed heads that aid in wind pollination.

Ceratocephalus grandiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
