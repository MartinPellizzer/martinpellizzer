Clethra obliquinervia

Clethra obliquinervia, commonly known as the narrow-veined sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the plant family Clethraceae and is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a distinct, narrow vein pattern that gives the species its scientific name. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and nectar for pollinators.
