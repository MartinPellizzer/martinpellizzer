Cordula bellatula

Cordula bellatula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cordula, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters during the late spring to early summer.

Cordula bellatula is noted for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
