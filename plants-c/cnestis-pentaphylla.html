Cnestis pentaphylla

Cnestis pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leafed morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-lobed leaves and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white, pink, and purple. The plant is a climbing vine that produces small, edible fruits, though its primary ornamental value lies in its vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Cnestis pentaphylla is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and as a decorative plant.
