Cyperus adansonii

Cyperus adansonii, commonly known as the umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant is characterized by its erect, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Cyperus adansonii produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically pale green to brown in color.

It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its distinctive appearance.
