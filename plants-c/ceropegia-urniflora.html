Ceropegia urniflora

Ceropegia urniflora, commonly known as the African violet or the string of hearts, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green or white, which are pollinated by specific moths. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ceropegia urniflora is popular as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
