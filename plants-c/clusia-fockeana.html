Clusia fockeana

Clusia fockeana, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, dark green leaves that are often glossy and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Clusia fockeana is also known for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and air pollution, making it a hardy choice for urban settings.
