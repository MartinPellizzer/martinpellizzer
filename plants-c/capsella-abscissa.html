Capsella abscissa

Capsella abscissa, commonly known as the common mouse-ear or mouse-ear cress, is a flowering plant in the Brassicaceae family.

It is a small annual herb with hairy stems and alternate, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by spherical, three-lobed pods containing several seeds. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been naturalized in various regions around the world.

Capsella abscissa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats.
