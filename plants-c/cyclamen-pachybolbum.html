Cyclamen pachybolbum

Cyclamen pachybolbum, commonly known as the thick-leaved cyclamen, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid habitat. The plant produces showy, spurred flowers in shades of pink, red, and white during the winter months. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is widely cultivated in horticulture.
