Commiphora neglecta

Commiphora neglecta, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and small, elliptical leaves. The plant produces aromatic resin, which has been traditionally used in incense and medicine. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
