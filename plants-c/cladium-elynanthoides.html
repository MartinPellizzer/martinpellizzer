Cladium elynanthoides

Cladium elynanthoides, commonly known as the slender sawgrass, is a flowering plant native to wetland habitats in the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its tall, reed-like stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers, typical of many grasses. It plays a vital ecological role in wetlands by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Cladium elynanthoides is often found in shallow water or moist soil, thriving in environments with consistent moisture.
