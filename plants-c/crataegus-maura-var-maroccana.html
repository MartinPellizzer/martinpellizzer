Crataegus maura var. maroccana

Crataegus maura var. maroccana, commonly known as the Moroccan hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Morocco.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to elliptical leaves, and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens and natural landscapes.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and adaptability to various climates.
