Crinum luangwense

Crinum luangwense, commonly known as the Luangwa Crinum, is a species of flowering plant in the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant features large, showy, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in umbels. Its leaves are broad, strap-like, and often form a dense rosette at the base.

Crinum luangwense is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
