Caldasia chaerophyllaea var. glabriuscula

Caldasia chaerophyllaea var. glabriuscula, commonly known as the smooth-leaved caldasia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant produces striking, colorful inflorescences that can range from red to orange, often with a distinctive spiky structure. Its leaves are typically broad, thick, and covered in a dense layer of trichomes, which help in water retention.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
