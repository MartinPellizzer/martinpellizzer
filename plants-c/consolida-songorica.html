Consolida songorica

Consolida songorica, commonly known as the Songorica sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, typical of the Fabaceae family. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive spurred shape at the base of the petals.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental and potentially edible plants.
