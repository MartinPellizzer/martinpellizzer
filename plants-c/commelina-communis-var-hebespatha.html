Commelina communis var. hebespatha

Commelina communis var. hebespatha, commonly known as the narrowleaf spiderwort, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Commelina genus in the family Commelinaceae, characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant features lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native, low-maintenance plants.
