Cirsium eriophorum var. bosniacum

Cirsium eriophorum var. bosniacum, commonly known as the Bosnian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkans.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and showy purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it is known for its robust growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.

The species is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
