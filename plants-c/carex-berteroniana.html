Carex berteroniana

Carex berteroniana, commonly known as Bittern sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its foliage is often used in floral arrangements due to its elegant appearance.
