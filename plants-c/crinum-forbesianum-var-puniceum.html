Crinum forbesianum var. puniceum

Crinum forbesianum var. puniceum, commonly known as the red-flowered crinum, is a striking bulbous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically a vibrant red or pink, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. The plant produces large, strap-like leaves that are broad and often have a waxy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
