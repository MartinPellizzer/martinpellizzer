Cyperus bromoides

Cyperus bromoides, commonly known as the flatleaf sedgeland, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

Cyperus bromoides produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the sedge family.
