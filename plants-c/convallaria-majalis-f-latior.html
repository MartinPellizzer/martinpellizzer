Convallaria majalis f. latior

Convallaria majalis f. latior, commonly known as the broad-leaf lily of the valley, is a cultivar of the lily of the valley plant.

It is characterized by its broader, more elongated leaves compared to the typical variety. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in shaded garden settings.

This form is valued for its unique foliage and is relatively easy to care for in moist, well-drained soil.
