Chastenaea affinis

Chastenaea affinis, commonly known as the slender false nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded woodlands and thrives in rich, loamy soils. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. Its stems are hairy and often have a reddish tinge, and it is known for its ability to spread via rhizomes.

Chastenaea affinis plays a role in supporting local insect populations and is often found in understory vegetation.
