Camellia ilicifolia var. neriifolia

Camellia ilicifolia var. neriifolia, commonly known as the blackleaf camellia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that turn red or black in the fall, adding visual interest to the landscape. The plant produces camellia-like flowers that are typically pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes for its seasonal color changes.
