Calamus perakensis var. gracilis

Calamus perakensis var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender calamus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of many palms. It thrives in wet, tropical environments and is often found in swamps or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
