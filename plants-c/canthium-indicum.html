Canthium indicum

Canthium indicum, commonly known as Indian hogweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rubiaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Americas. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces small, clustered white or yellowish flowers. Its fruits are follicles that contain several seeds, and the plant can grow up to 2 meters tall.

It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and forest edges.
