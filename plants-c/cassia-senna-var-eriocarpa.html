Cassia senna var. eriocarpa

Cassia senna var. eriocarpa, commonly known as the velvet cassia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with small, velvety-textured leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow, fluffy flower clusters that bloom in the spring and summer. Its seeds are encased in a long, slender pod that splits open when mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
