Corydalis brevirostrata subsp. tibetica

Corydalis brevirostrata subsp.

tibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan short-beaked corydalis, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet. It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions.

The plant features slender stems and lance-shaped leaves, with clusters of small, delicate flowers in shades of pink and purple. Its flowers are characterized by two prominent upper petals and two lower, spurred petals, which are typical of the Corydalis genus.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
