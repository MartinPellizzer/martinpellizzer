Calamagrostis canescens f. convoluta

Calamagrostis canescens f. convoluta, commonly known as the curled bluestem grass, is a decorative grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curling foliage that gives it a unique, ornamental appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer, adding visual interest to the landscape.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
