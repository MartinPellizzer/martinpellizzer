Calanthe bachmaensis

Calanthe bachmaensis, commonly known as the Bachma orchid, is a species of orchid native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a terrestrial herb with a pseudobulb that stores water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its arid environment. The plant produces striking, showy flowers with a distinctive purple and white coloration, typically blooming in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are linear and arranged in a rosette, contributing to its overall compact and elegant appearance.

Calanthe bachmaensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
