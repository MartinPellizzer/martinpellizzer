Calophyllum alboramulum

Calophyllum alboramulum, commonly known as the white ramulus candlewood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Calophyllaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, simple leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by woody, elliptical fruits containing a single seed. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and potential medicinal properties.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
