Calophyllum wallichianum var. incrassatum

Calophyllum wallichianum var. incrassatum, commonly known as the thick-leaved calophyllum, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
