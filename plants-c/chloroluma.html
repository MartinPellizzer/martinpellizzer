Chloroluma

Chloroluma, commonly known as the moonflower, is a striking plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and close during the day. The leaves are broad and glossy, often with a waxy texture that helps retain moisture. The plant belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is known for its rapid growth and unique blooming cycle.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardens and botanical studies.
