Cotinus coggygria f. rubrifolius

Cotinus coggygria f. rubrifolius, commonly known as the red-leaved smoke bush, is a cultivar of the smoke bush species native to Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red foliage, which becomes more vibrant in the autumn. The plant produces fluffy, pinkish-white flower clusters in the summer, which give it its common name. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.

The leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape and have a smooth texture.
