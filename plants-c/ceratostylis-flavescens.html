Ceratostylis flavescens

Ceratostylis flavescens, commonly known as the golden spike orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-green inflorescence. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in late spring or early summer.

The orchid has a rhizomatous root system and is known for its ephemeral blooming period.
