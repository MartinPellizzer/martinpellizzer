Cordyline timorensis

Cordyline timorensis, commonly known as the Timor cordyline, is a striking tropical plant native to the island of Timor.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and rosette of broad, lance-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically a deep green with a waxy texture and can grow up to 1 meter in length. It produces inflorescences of small, fragrant white or pale pink flowers, which appear at the base of the plant.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to warm, humid climates.
