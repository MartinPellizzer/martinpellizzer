Cunila galioides var. hispida

Cunila galioides var. hispida, commonly known as hairy meadowfoam, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, hairy structures. Its small, white to pale blue flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
