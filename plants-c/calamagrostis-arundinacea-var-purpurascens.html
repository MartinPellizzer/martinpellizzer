Calamagrostis arundinacea var. purpurascens

Calamagrostis arundinacea var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking purple to pinkish hues on its foliage, which intensify in the fall. The plant produces feathery plumes that add ornamental value, making it popular in gardens and natural landscapes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a clumping growth habit.
