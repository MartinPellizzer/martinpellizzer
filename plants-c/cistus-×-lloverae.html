Cistus × lloverae

Cistus × lloverae, commonly known as the hybrid rock rose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the genus Cistus, which includes various species of evergreen shrubs native to the Mediterranean region. The plant is characterized by its dense, low-growing form and its ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils. Its flowers are typically pink or white, appearing in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Cistus × lloverae is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
