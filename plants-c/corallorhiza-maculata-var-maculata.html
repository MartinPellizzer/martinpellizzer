Corallorhiza maculata var. maculata

Corallorhiza maculata var. maculata, commonly known as the spotted coralroot, is an orchid that lacks chlorophyll and is entirely dependent on mycorrhizal fungi for nutrient uptake.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in shaded, moist woodlands and is found across much of eastern North America. The plant produces a tall stem with whorled, lance-shaped leaves and is notable for its showy, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system forms a symbiotic relationship with specific fungi, allowing it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its reliance on fungal partners, it is considered a keystone species in certain forest ecosystems.
