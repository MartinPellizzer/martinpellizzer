Callistemon phratra

Callistemon phratra, commonly known as the narrow-leaf bottlebrush, is a shrub native to Western Australia.

It is characterized by its striking, crimson-red flower spikes that emerge from narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea tree species. Its flowers are tubular and are pollinated primarily by birds and insects.

Callistemon phratra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
