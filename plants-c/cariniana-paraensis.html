Cariniana paraensis

Cariniana paraensis, commonly known as the Brazilian peachwood, is a deciduous tree native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and large, simple leaves that are alternate and elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red fruits resembling peaches. Its wood is highly valued for its durability and fine grain, making it a sought-after material in carpentry and furniture making.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
