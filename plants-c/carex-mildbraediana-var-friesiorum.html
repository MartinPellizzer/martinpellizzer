Carex mildbraediana var. friesiorum

Carex mildbraediana var. friesiorum, commonly known as the Fries' sedge, is a perennial plant native to the northeastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered brownish flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
