Carex pseudohypochlora f. denticulata

Carex pseudohypochlora f. denticulata, commonly known as the denticulate sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads. This sedges typically grows in moist or wet environments, such as marshes or along stream banks. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in suitable habitats.

The plant is often found in temperate regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal in wetland gardens.
