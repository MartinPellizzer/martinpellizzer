Centaurea debeauxii subsp. grandiflora

Centaurea debeauxii subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the largeflowered centaury, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers, which bloom in summer and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats, including rocky soils and grasslands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces distinctive seed heads that are often adorned with bracts.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
