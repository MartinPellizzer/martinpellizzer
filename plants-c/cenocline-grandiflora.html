Cenocline grandiflora

Cenocline grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered cenocline, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and are often adorned with a distinctive red or orange throat. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Cenocline grandiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
