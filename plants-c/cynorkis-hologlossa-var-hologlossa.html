Cynorkis hologlossa var. hologlossa

Cynorkis hologlossa var. hologlossa, commonly known as the silver-haired orchid, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-gray foliage and elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces delicate, pale green to white flowers with a subtle fragrance, typically blooming in late spring. Native to the mountainous regions of South America, it thrives in cool, moist environments with partial shade.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
