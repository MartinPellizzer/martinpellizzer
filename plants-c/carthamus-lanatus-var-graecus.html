Carthamus lanatus var. graecus

Carthamus lanatus var. graecus, commonly known as the Greek safflower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, branching stems and vibrant yellow to orange flower heads, which are composed of numerous small, tubular florets. The plant produces large, showy bracts that surround the flower cluster, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental beauty and historical use in dyeing and medicinal applications.
