Calanthe tricarinata

Calanthe tricarinata, commonly known as the three-ribbed calanthe, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers with three prominent longitudinal ridges on the petals. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil.

Calanthe tricarinata is appreciated for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
