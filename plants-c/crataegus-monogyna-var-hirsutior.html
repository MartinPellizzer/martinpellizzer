Crataegus monogyna var. hirsutior

Crataegus monogyna var. hirsutior, commonly known as the hairy hawthorn, is a variant of the common hawthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant is characterized by its hairy leaves and stems, which give it its common name. It produces white or pinkish flowers in spring, followed by red berries in the autumn.

This variety is often found in woodland edges and hedgerows, where it thrives in well-drained soils.
