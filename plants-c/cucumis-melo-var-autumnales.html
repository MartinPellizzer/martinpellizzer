Cucumis melo var. autumnales

Cucumis melo var. autumnales, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a cultivar of the melon plant characterized by its distinct fruiting behavior.

This variety produces large, elongated fruits with a thick, aromatic rind and a sweet, juicy interior. The plant typically thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is an annual vine that climbs using tendrils, often reaching lengths of several meters.

The fruit is typically harvested in the fall, giving the variety its name, and is prized for its flavor and versatility in culinary use.
