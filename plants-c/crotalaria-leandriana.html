Crotalaria leandriana

Crotalaria leandriana, commonly known as the velvetleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings. It grows as an annual herb, often reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and is sometimes considered a weed in cultivated fields.

Its seeds are used in some traditional cuisines, though the plant contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
