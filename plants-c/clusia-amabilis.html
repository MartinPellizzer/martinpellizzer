Clusia amabilis

Clusia amabilis, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, providing a striking visual appeal. The plant produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic value.

Clusia amabilis is also valued for its ability to tolerate shade and poor soil conditions, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
