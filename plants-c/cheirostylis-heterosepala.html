Cheirostylis heterosepala

Cheirostylis heterosepala, commonly known as the golden orchid, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its showy, yellowish-brown flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial herb, often found in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces small, clustered inflorescences.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
