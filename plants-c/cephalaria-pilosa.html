Cephalaria pilosa

Cephalaria pilosa, commonly known as the woolly cephalaria, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and feathery foliage. The plant features slender, upright stems covered in soft, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or grassy habitats.
