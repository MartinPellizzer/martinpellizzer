Colchicum armenum

Colchicum armenum, commonly known as Armenian colchicum, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It features showy, nodding flowers that range in color from white to pink and are typically borne on tall, slender stems. The plant's leaves are basal, forming a rosette and are usually lance-shaped with a waxy texture. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cool, moist climates.

The plant is part of the Colchicaceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
