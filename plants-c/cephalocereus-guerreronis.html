Cephalocereus guerreronis

Cephalocereus guerreronis, commonly known as the Mexican moon cactus, is a unique cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking white, spherical body covered in short, white spines. The plant has a short, thick stem that supports a large, showy flower that blooms at night. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-draining soil.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
