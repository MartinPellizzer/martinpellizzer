Cyanus reuterianus var. phrygius

Cyanus reuterianus var. phrygius, commonly known as the Phrygian hawkweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow to orange composite flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and often branched.

This variant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
