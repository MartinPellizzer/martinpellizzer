Conophytum pardicolor

Conophytum pardicolor, commonly known as the "Rainbow Stone" or "Cape Rainbow," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its unique, fleshy, and often colorful leaves that resemble stones. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the warm season. It thrives in arid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Conophytum pardicolor is popular among succulent enthusiasts for its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.
