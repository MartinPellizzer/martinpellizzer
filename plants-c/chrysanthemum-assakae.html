Chrysanthemum assakae

Chrysanthemum assakae, commonly known as the Ethiopian chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disc florets. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers are usually yellow or white, and it produces small, hairy seeds.

Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
