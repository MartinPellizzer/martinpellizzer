Camaridium obscurum

Camaridium obscurum, commonly known as the dark camaridium, is a rare flowering plant native to the humid rainforests of Central America.

It is characterized by its small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color, which helps it blend into its forest environment. The plant has a short, sturdy stem with broad, leathery leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern. It produces fruit that contains a single, hard-seeded capsule, which is dispersed by wind.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Camaridium obscurum is considered a species of conservation concern.
