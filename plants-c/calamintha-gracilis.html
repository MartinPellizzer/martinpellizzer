Calamintha gracilis

Calamintha gracilis, commonly known as slender caraway, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and dill, and is characterized by its finely divided, aromatic leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Calamintha gracilis thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in herbal medicine for its mild carminative properties.
