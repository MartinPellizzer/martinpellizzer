Croton tuerckheimii

Croton tuerckheimii, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, velvety leaves that are typically dark green with a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
