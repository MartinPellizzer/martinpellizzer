Cinnamomum kalbaricum

Cinnamomum kalbaricum, commonly known as the Kalbari cinnamon or Kalbari tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to northern Australia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark. The plant typically grows in wet tropical rainforests and has a slow growth rate. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinct cinnamon-like scent when crushed.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has limited commercial use compared to other cinnamon species.
