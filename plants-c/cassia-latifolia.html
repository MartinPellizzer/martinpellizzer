Cassia latifolia

Cassia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cassia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is part of the legume family, Fabaceae, and produces pod-like fruits that contain seeds. Cassia latifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for use as a hedge or in ornamental gardens.
