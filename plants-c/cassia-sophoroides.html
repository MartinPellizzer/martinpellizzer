Cassia sophoroides

Cassia sophoroides, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrub or small tree form, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant produces long, pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers, which are typical of the Fabaceae family. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Cassia sophoroides is also valued for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional remedies.
