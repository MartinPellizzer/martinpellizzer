Carex colorata

Carex colorata, commonly known as the colorful sedge, is a perennial plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to greenish flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream edges.

Carex colorata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
