Chrysoglossum vesicatum

Chrysoglossum vesicatum, commonly known as the blistering chrysoglossum, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellow to orange flowers that are arranged in a cluster, often with a distinctive blisters or bumps on the petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated for ornamental purposes.
