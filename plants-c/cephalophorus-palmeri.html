Cephalophorus palmeri

Cephalophorus palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its flowers bloom in the spring and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Cephalophorus palmeri is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
