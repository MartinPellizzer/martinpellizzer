Centaurea galushkoi

Centaurea galushkoi, commonly known as the Galushko's cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant features slender stems with narrow, grass-like leaves, and its flowers are characterized by vibrant blue to purple petals. It produces small, bristly seeds that are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native range.
