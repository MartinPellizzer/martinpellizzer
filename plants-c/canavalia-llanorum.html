Canavalia llanorum

Canavalia llanorum, commonly known as the Llanorum bean, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its seeds are edible and have been traditionally used in local cuisines.

Canavalia llanorum is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing abilities, making it useful in agricultural settings.
