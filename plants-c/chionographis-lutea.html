Chionographis lutea

Chionographis lutea, commonly known as the yellow snowbell, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Chionographis lutea is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
