Caroxylon dzhungaricum

Caroxylon dzhungaricum, commonly known as the Dzungarian carob tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often found in desert regions. Its seeds are encased in a hard pod, and the plant has a deep root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

Caroxylon dzhungaricum plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and is of interest for ecological restoration projects.
