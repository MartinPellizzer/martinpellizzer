Crataegus robur f. sulphurea

Crataegus robur f. sulphurea, commonly known as the sulphur-leaf hawthorn, is a rare and striking variant of the common hawthorn.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish-green foliage, which gives it its unique appearance. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically found in woodland habitats. It produces small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the autumn.

This form is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
