Callistemon lophanthus

Callistemon lophanthus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical red flowers that resemble a bottlebrush. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and open forests.

Callistemon lophanthus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
