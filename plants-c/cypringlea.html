Cypringlea

Cypringlea, commonly known as the golden cup, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small cups. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Cypringlea thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy perennial in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value has made it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
