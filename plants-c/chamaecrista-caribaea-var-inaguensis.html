Chamaecrista caribaea var. inaguensis

Chamaecrista caribaea var. inaguensis, commonly known as the Inagua burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, often found in dry, tropical environments.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and local biodiversity.
