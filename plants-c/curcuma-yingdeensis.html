Curcuma yingdeensis

Curcuma yingdeensis, commonly known as the Yingde ginger or Yungcheng ginger, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to southern China.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes other ginger species. The plant is characterized by its ornamental leaves, which are broad, lance-shaped, and often have a striking green or variegated coloration. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it produces small, pale yellow rhizomes that are used in traditional medicine.

The plant is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its medicinal properties.
