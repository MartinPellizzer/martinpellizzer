Calamintha cantabrica

Calamintha cantabrica, commonly known as the Cantabrian germander, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Lamiaceae family, which is characterized by square stems and aromatic leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its small, tubular flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Calamintha cantabrica is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
