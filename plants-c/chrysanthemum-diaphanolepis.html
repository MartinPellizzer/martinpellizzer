Chrysanthemum diaphanolepis

Chrysanthemum diaphanolepis, commonly known as the glass-leaved chrysanthemum, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white, typically blooming in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage makes it a striking addition to any garden or indoor setting.
