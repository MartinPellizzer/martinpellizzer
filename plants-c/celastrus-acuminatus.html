Celastrus acuminatus

Celastrus acuminatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its slender, twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in umbels, followed by elliptical, orange-red berries. Its leaves are often narrow and pointed, giving it its common name, while its berries are a food source for various birds and small mammals.

Celastrus acuminatus is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and stream banks.
