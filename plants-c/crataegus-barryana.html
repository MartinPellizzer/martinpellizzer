Crataegus barryana

Crataegus barryana, commonly known as Barry's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves, and clusters of white or pale pink flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red or orange berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands, thickets, and along stream banks.

Barry's hawthorn is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for wildlife.
