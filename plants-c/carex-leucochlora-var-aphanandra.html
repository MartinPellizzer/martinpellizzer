Carex leucochlora var. aphanandra

Carex leucochlora var. aphanandra, commonly known as the white-haired sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces distinctive white to pale green bracts.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
