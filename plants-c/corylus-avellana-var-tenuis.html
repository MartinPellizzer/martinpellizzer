Corylus avellana var. tenuis

Corylus avellana var. tenuis, commonly known as the European hazelnut or filbert, is a shrub native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, spreading form and elongated, serrated leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish catkins in early spring, followed by edible nuts that mature in late summer. Its flowers are monoecious, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
