Cyperus stroudii

Cyperus stroudii, commonly known as the Stroud's flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida, and thrives in wetland environments such as marshes and swamps. The plant has grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is often found in shallow water or moist soil, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.

Cyperus stroudii plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
