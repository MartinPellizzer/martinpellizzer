Cleisostoma flagelliforme

Cleisostoma flagelliforme, commonly known as the whip-like orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, thread-like leaves that give it its common name, and its flowers are typically small, fragrant, and clustered in loose inflorescences. The plant prefers bright, indirect light and high humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation. Its unique morphology and ornamental value make it a sought-after species among orchid enthusiasts.

Cleisostoma flagelliforme is relatively easy to care for, provided it receives proper watering and drainage.
