Calliandra bombycina

Calliandra bombycina, commonly known as the silk tree or silkpod tree, is a shrub or small tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its feathery, pink or red inflorescences that resemble silk ribbons, giving it its common name. The plant has compound leaves with numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Calliandra bombycina is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its showy flowers and drought tolerance.
