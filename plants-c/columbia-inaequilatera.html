Columbia inaequilatera

Columbia inaequilatera, commonly known as the Columbia mountain beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is a member of the Plantaginaceae family and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
