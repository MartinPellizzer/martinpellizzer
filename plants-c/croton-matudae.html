Croton matudae

Croton matudae, commonly known as the Matudae croton, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which includes many species with characteristic latex-producing structures. The plant is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, often featuring shades of red, orange, and yellow. Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
