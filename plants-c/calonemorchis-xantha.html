Calonemorchis xantha

Calonemorchis xantha, commonly known as the yellow star orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow petals and sepals, which form a star-like appearance, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers bloom in clusters, often with a subtle fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is highly sought after in horticultural circles.
