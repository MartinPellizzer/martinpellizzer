Calycanthus suaveolens

Calycanthus suaveolens, commonly known as sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features fragrant, deep red to maroon flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, ovate leaves that are glossy and arranged oppositely on the stems. Its bark is reddish-brown and becomes fissured with age, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Sweetshrub is well-suited for moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.
