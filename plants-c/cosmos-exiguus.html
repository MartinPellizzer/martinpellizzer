Cosmos exiguus

Cosmos exiguus, commonly known as the dwarf cosmos, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features delicate, daisy-like flowers with white petals and a prominent yellow center, blooming from summer to fall. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, feathery leaves that are typically gray-green in color. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Cosmos exiguus is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
