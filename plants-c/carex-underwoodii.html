Carex underwoodii

Carex underwoodii, commonly known as Underwood's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This species typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, narrow, and generally have a bluish-green color.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the sedge family.
