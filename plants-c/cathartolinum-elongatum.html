Cathartolinum elongatum

Cathartolinum elongatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed cathartol, is a unique plant species characterized by its elongated, slender stems and clusters of small, waxy leaves.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate forest regions. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale green to soft pink, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves contain bioactive compounds that have been studied for potential medicinal properties.

Despite its ornamental appeal, it is relatively rare and requires specific conditions to flourish.
