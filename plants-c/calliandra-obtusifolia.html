Calliandra obtusifolia

Calliandra obtusifolia, commonly known as the sicklepod or velvetwood, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that range in color from yellow to red, depending on the variety. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its flowers are often clustered in terminal spikes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Calliandra obtusifolia is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, improving soil fertility in agricultural settings.
