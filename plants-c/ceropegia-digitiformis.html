Ceropegia digitiformis

Ceropegia digitiformis, commonly known as the "Lion's Tail," is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species characterized by its trailing stems and fleshy, dark green leaves. The plant produces unique, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in hanging baskets.
