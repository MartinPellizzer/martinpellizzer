Caltha palustris var. orientalisinensis

Caltha palustris var. orientalisinensis, commonly known as the oriental marsh marigold, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from April to May. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for water garden landscapes.
