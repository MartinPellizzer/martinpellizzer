Calyptrochilum pynaertii

Calyptrochilum pynaertii, commonly known as the Pynaert's calypso, is a rare and endemic orchid species native to the tropical rainforests of Cameroon.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and unique floral structure, with a short, hairy stem and showy, spurred flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-white blooms that are often hidden among the foliage, making it difficult to spot in its natural habitat. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
