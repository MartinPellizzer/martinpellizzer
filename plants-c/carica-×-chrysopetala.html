Carica × chrysopetala

Carica × chrysopetala, commonly known as the golden papaya, is a hybrid species within the Caricaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, yellow-orange fruits that are typically larger and more elongated than those of typical papayas. The plant produces striking, palmate leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. Its flowers are usually yellow with a red or orange throat, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible fruit.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring warm temperatures and well-drained soil.
