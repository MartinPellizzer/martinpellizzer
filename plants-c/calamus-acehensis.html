Calamus acehensis

Calamus acehensis, commonly known as the Aceh calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is a tall, slender palm-like plant that can reach heights of up to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia.

Calamus acehensis is often found growing in wetlands and along the edges of rivers, where it thrives in humid, moist environments.
