Chrysocephalum apiculatum subsp. stoloniferum

Chrysocephalum apiculatum subsp.

stoloniferum, commonly known as the stoloniferous everlasting daisy, is a perennial flowering plant native to Australia. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its ability to spread via stolons, forming extensive ground cover. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

The flowers bloom in spring and early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
