Cirsium corbariense f. glabratum

Cirsium corbariense f. glabratum, commonly known as the smooth thistle, is a variant of the greater centaury.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils in arid or semi-arid regions. Its foliage is generally less hairy compared to the typical form, giving it a smoother appearance.

It is often found in Mediterranean climates and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
