Camellia uraku

Camellia uraku, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a flowering plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its showy, double blooms that come in a range of colors, including red, pink, and white. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and elliptical in shape, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers.

Camellia uraku prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.
