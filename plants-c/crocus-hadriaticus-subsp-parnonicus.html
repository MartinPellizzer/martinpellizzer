Crocus hadriaticus subsp. parnonicus

Crocus hadriaticus subsp.

parnonicus, commonly known as the Adriatic crocus, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange.

The plant typically blooms in early spring, often before the leaves are fully developed. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its early blooming habit.
