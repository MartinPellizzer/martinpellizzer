Cucurbita odorifera

Cucurbita odorifera, commonly known as the sweet gourd or fragrant pumpkin, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and squash. The plant produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers, with the fruit being a large, rough-skinned gourd that can vary in color from green to orange. It is valued for its edible flesh and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The plant's distinct, sweet fragrance is one of its most notable characteristics.
