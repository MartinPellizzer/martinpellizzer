Commelina bambusifolioides

Commelina bambusifolioides, commonly known as the bamboo-leaf commelina, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that resemble bamboo in appearance, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in clusters at the tips of its stems. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical structure includes a fibrous root system and a rhizomatous growth habit, contributing to its ability to spread and colonize new areas.
