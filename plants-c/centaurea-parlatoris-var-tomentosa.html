Centaurea parlatoris var. tomentosa

Centaurea parlatoris var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a woolly appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
