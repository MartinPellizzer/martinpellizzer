Cassia bracteosa

Cassia bracteosa, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red floral clusters that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and birds. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets, and its bark is often smooth and grayish in color. Cassia bracteosa is known for its rapid growth and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
