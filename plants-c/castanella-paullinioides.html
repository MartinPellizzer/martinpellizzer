Castanella paullinioides

Castanella paullinioides, commonly known as the Mexican chestnut or paullinia chestnut, is a species of flowering plant in the family Proteaceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and showy, pinkish-white flowers. The plant produces small, hard nuts encased in a spiny husk, which are adapted for dispersal by animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forests and shrublands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
