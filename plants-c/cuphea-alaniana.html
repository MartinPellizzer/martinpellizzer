Cuphea alaniana

Cuphea alaniana, commonly known as the velvetleaf cuphea, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink and orange. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Cuphea alaniana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
