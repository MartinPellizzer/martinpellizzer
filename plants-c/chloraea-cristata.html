Chloraea cristata

Chloraea cristata, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers, which are clustered in spherical inflorescences. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family. Its leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Chloraea cristata is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is a symbol of Australia's national identity.
