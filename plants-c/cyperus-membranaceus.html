Cyperus membranaceus

Cyperus membranaceus, commonly known as flatleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family. The plant has slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Cyperus membranaceus is often found in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
