Carex colchica

Carex colchica, commonly known as the Armenian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and parts of Turkey.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants commonly referred to as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Carex colchica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
