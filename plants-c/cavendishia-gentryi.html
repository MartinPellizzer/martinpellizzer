Cavendishia gentryi

Cavendishia gentryi, commonly known as the Gentry's Cavendish, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, colorful inflorescences that can last for several months. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, anchoring itself to trees in the canopy of rainforests. Its leaves are often broad and arranged in a rosette pattern, which helps collect moisture from the humid environment.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
