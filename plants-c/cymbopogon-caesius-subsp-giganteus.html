Cymbopogon caesius subsp. giganteus

Cymbopogon caesius subsp.

giganteus, commonly known as giant citron grass, is a tall, perennial grass native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia. It is characterized by its robust growth, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and its long, narrow leaves with a distinctive citron-like aroma.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences and is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and natural remedies. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its rapid growth rate and adaptability, it is also considered a potential bioenergy crop.
