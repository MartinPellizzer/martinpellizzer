Crataegus tribulosa

Crataegus tribulosa, commonly known as the thorned hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pinkish flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with serrated edges, and it thrives in a variety of soil types, including moist to dry conditions.

Crataegus tribulosa is often found in woodlands, thickets, and along stream banks.
