Calluna vulgaris var. albiflora

Calluna vulgaris var. albiflora, commonly known as the white heather, is a variant of the common heather.

It is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to western Europe, characterized by its dense, compact growth habit. The plant features small, needle-like leaves that are typically dark green, and it produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
