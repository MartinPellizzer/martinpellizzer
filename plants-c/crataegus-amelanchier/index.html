Crataegus Amelanchier

Crataegus amelanchier, commonly known as the serviceberry, is a versatile and ornamental shrub or small tree native to North America. It is celebrated for its early spring blooms, edible fruits, and adaptability to various soil conditions. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges, open fields, and disturbed areas. Its historical significance includes use by indigenous peoples for food and medicine. With its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits, Crataegus amelanchier remains a valuable addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
