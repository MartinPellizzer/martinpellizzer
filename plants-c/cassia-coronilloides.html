Cassia coronilloides

Cassia coronilloides, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is notable for its long, drooping clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. Its bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Cassia coronilloides is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
