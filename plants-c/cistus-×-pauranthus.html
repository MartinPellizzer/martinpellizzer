Cistus × pauranthus

Cistus × pauranthus, commonly known as the rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun and well-drained soils. Its woody stems and evergreen foliage make it a hardy, drought-tolerant species.

Cistus × pauranthus is often used in xeriscaping and has historical uses in herbal medicine.
