Calea huigrensis

Calea huigrensis, commonly known as the Mexican nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Calea huigrensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique blooming habit.
