Cenchrus triticoides

Cenchrus triticoides, commonly known as false wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soils and providing forage for wildlife.
