Cerasus juliana var. uncialis

Cerasus juliana var. uncialis, commonly known as the Japanese apricot or Japanese cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its delicate, double-flowered blossoms that appear in early spring, often in shades of white and pink. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it suitable for both container gardening and landscape design.
