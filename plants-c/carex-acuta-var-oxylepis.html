Carex acuta var. oxylepis

Carex acuta var. oxylepis, commonly known as the sharp-tailed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
