Chiloterus gibbosus

Chiloterus gibbosus, commonly known as the warty bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It features a basal rosette of thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
