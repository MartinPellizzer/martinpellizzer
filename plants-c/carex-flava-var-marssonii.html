Carex flava var. marssonii

Carex flava var. marssonii, commonly known as the yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. This variety is characterized by its bright yellowish-green leaves and produces small, clustered brownish flowers in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wet environments.
