Cestrum conglomeratum var. kunthii

Cestrum conglomeratum var. kunthii, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, dry seeds.
