Cirsium pascuarense

Cirsium pascuarense, commonly known as the meadow thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and leaves with a rough, often spiny texture. The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with serrated edges and a dense covering of stiff hairs.

The flowers are usually purple to pink, arranged in a dense cluster at the top of the plant.
