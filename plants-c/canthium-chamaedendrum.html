Canthium chamaedendrum

Canthium chamaedendrum, commonly known as the African star apple or *Chamaedendron africanum*, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Malvaceae and is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and showy yellow flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
