Carex dissita var. lambertiana

Carex dissita var. lambertiana, commonly known as Lambert's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is often found in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
