Cereus penicillatus

Cereus penicillatus, commonly known as the Christmas star cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is a branching cactus with distinctive, star-shaped flowers that bloom during the holiday season. The plant has a short, thick stem covered in areoles, which bear spines and floral buds. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it produces vibrant pink or red flowers during winter.

It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
