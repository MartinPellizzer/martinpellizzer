Cocculus forsteri

Cocculus forsteri, commonly known as the Indian devil's backbone, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a long, slender pod that splits open to release its seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
