Chenopodium gigantospermum f. spicatiforme

Chenopodium gigantospermum f. spicatiforme, commonly known as the giant seed goosefoot, is a variant of the Chenopodium genus native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its large, inflated seed pods that resemble small balloons, which are a distinctive botanical feature. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in dry environments. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spiciform (spike-like) clusters of small flowers, contributing to its specific epithet.

This plant is adapted to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
