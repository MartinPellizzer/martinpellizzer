Cucumis melo var. shahd

Cucumis melo var. shahd, commonly known as Persian melon, is a cultivated variety of the melon plant native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, pale green rind and sweet, aromatic flesh. The plant typically grows on a trailing vine with palmate leaves and produces yellow flowers. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its flavor and is often used in both fresh consumption and culinary preparations.
