Calamus koordersianus

Calamus koordersianus, commonly known as the Koorders' climbing bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. This plant is characterized by its long, slender rhizomes and erect, often climbing stems that can reach several meters in height. Its leaves are typically broad and lanceolate, with a smooth, waxy surface.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spadices, typical of the Araceae family.
