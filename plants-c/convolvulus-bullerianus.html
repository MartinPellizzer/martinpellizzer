Convolvulus bullerianus

Convolvulus bullerianus, commonly known as the New Zealand morning glory, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in coastal and sandy environments. It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar climbing or trailing growth forms.

Convolvulus bullerianus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
