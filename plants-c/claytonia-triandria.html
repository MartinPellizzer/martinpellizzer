Claytonia triandria

Claytonia triandria, commonly known as the pink rockcress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features small, pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May. The plant has a mat-forming growth habit with fleshy, rounded leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Claytonia triandria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens.
