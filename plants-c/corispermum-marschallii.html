Corispermum marschallii

Corispermum marschallii, commonly known as the marsh sandwort, is a flowering plant native to arctic and alpine regions.

It is a small, herbaceous annual that typically grows in moist, sandy or gravelly soils. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered in fine hairs.
