Cirsium japonicum var. diabolicum

Cirsium japonicum var. diabolicum, commonly known as Japanese thistle or devil's thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and forest edges. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes many flowering plants with composite flower heads.

The species is known for its aggressive growth and can become invasive in certain regions.
