Crataegus flava

Crataegus flava, commonly known as the yellow hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red berries in the fall. The plant has small, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a woody, thorny structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows and woodlands.

Crataegus flava is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
