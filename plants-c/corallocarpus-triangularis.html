Corallocarpus triangularis

Corallocarpus triangularis, commonly known as the triangular coral tree, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive triangular-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking red or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark is rough and fissured, and it can grow as a shrub or small tree depending on environmental conditions.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to well-drained soils and full sun.
