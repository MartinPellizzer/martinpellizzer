Catasetum lituratum

Catasetum lituratum, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a tropical orchid species native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers that resemble those of the orchid genus Catasetum. The plant produces elongated, pendulous inflorescences with showy, spurred petals and a distinctive, elongated lip. Its flowers are typically fragrant, attracting specific pollinators such as bees and wasps.

Catasetum lituratum is known for its adaptability to various growing conditions, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
