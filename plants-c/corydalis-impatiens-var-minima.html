Corydalis impatiens var. minima

Corydalis impatiens var. minima, commonly known as the dwarf Persian violet, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are usually hairy, and it grows to a height of only a few inches, making it a compact and ornamental species. It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

This variant is smaller than the typical Corydalis impatiens, with a more compact growth habit.
