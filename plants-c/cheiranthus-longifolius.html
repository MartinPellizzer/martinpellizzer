Cheiranthus longifolius

Cheiranthus longifolius, commonly known as the long-leaved honesty, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other cruciferous plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white or pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring. Its leaves are elongated and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a slender pod that splits open to release seeds, a feature typical of the Brassicaceae family.
