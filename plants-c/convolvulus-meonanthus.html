Convolvulus meonanthus

Convolvulus meonanthus, commonly known as the meadow bindweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in grassy areas and along field edges. The plant features slender stems that twine around other vegetation, a characteristic of the Convolvulaceae family. Its small, pale pink or white flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

The leaves are heart-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
