Carissa edulis var. ambungana

Carissa edulis var. ambungana, commonly known as the Ambungana moonseed, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, thorny stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to orange or red berries, which are edible but have a bitter taste. Its fruit contains seeds that are dispersed by animals, contributing to its ecological role in the region.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
