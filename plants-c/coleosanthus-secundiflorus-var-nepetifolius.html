Coleosanthus secundiflorus var. nepetifolius

Coleosanthus secundiflorus var. nepetifolius, commonly known as the Nepeta-leaved Coleosanthus, is a flowering plant native to the island of Mauritius.

It is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its native habitat. The leaves of this plant are distinctive, featuring a soft, fuzzy texture and a scent reminiscent of nepeta, or catnip. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its unique appearance and fragrance, it is occasionally cultivated for ornamental purposes.
