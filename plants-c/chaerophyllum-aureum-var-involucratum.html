Chaerophyllum aureum var. involucratum

Chaerophyllum aureum var. involucratum, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, upright stems and basal leaves that are heart-shaped with a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a bell-like cluster, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value comes from its delicate foliage and unique floral display.
