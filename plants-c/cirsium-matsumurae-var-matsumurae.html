Cirsium matsumurae var. matsumurae

Cirsium matsumurae var. matsumurae, commonly known as the Japanese thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, upright stems and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped with rough, hairy surfaces, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
