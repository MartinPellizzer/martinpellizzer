Calamagrostis lancea

Calamagrostis lancea, commonly known as the slender reedgrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant features slender, upright stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a bluish-green hue.

The inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or reddish tones.
