Ceratosanthes cuneata

Ceratosanthes cuneata, commonly known as the narrowleaf false indigo, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

Ceratosanthes cuneata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
