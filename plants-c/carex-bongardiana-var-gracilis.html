Carex bongardiana var. gracilis

Carex bongardiana var. gracilis, commonly known as slender bog sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer, which are clustered in spikelets at the tips of the stems. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in bogs, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in water gardens and wetland restoration projects.
