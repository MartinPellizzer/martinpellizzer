Cepa pallens

Cepa pallens, commonly known as the pale onion, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its bulbous stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Cepa pallens thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.
