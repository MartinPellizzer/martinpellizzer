Chamaecrista tracyi

Chamaecrista tracyi, commonly known as Tracy's burr legume, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along dry streambeds.

Botanically, it is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities and ecological importance in desert ecosystems.
