Coelodiscus thunbergianus

Coelodiscus thunbergianus, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its upright growth habit and attractive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

The flowers bloom in spring and are often used in floral arrangements due to their elegant appearance.
