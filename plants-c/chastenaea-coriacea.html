Chastenaea coriacea

Chastenaea coriacea, commonly known as the rough-stemmed chastenaea, is a perennial shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and small, oval-shaped leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

Chastenaea coriacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
