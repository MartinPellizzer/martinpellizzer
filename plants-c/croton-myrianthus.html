Croton myrianthus

Croton myrianthus, commonly known as the African croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are often brightly colored, ranging from green to red and yellow. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, capsule-like fruits. Croton myrianthus thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various climates.
