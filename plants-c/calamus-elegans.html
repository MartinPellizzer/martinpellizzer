Calamus elegans

Calamus elegans, commonly known as the elegant calamus, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces a spadix, which is surrounded by a spathe, typical of the Araceae family. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Calamus elegans is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage.
