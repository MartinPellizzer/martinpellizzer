Croton guildingioides

Croton guildingioides, commonly known as the golden croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its milky sap, which can be toxic if ingested. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its small, inconspicuous flowers are followed by capsule fruits.

Croton guildingioides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
