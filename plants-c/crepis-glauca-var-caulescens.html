Crepis glauca var. caulescens

Crepis glauca var. caulescens, commonly known as the glaucous hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a distinctive blue-gray waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

The flowers are yellow with ray florets, blooming in late spring to early summer.
