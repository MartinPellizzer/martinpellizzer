Ceanothus virgatus

Ceanothus virgatus, commonly known as the silver bush ceanothus, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and clusters of blue to violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is often found in dry, well-drained soils in coastal and inland regions. It has small, leathery leaves that are typically opposite or alternate in arrangement.

Ceanothus virgatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
