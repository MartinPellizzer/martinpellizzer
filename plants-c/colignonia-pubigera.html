Colignonia pubigera

Colignonia pubigera, commonly known as the hairy colignonia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes heathers and blueberries. The plant is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, hairy textures, giving it its common name.

Colignonia pubigera thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forest understories.
