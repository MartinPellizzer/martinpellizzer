Centaurea procumbens var. verguinii

Centaurea procumbens var. verguinii, commonly known as the creeping centaury, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features slender, spreading stems that often root at the nodes, forming dense mats. The plant produces striking blue to purple flowers with distinctive bracts, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a rough texture and hairy surfaces.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, rocky areas, and along roadsides.
