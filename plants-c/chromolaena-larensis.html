Chromolaena larensis

Chromolaena larensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian tridentleaf, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a head, which are followed by fluffy, white seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and disturbed habitats.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive tendencies, it is considered a potential weed in some regions.
