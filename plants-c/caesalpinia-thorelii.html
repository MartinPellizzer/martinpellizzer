Caesalpinia thorelii

Caesalpinia thorelii, commonly known as the flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters at the ends of its branches. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets. It produces long, slender pods that contain flat, winged seeds.

Caesalpinia thorelii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate sunlight.
