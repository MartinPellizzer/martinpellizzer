Cineraria laxiflora

Cineraria laxiflora, commonly known as the looseflower cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Cineraria laxiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a potted plant.
