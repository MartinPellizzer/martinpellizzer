Cleistocactus peculiaris

Cleistocactus peculiaris, commonly known as the peculiar cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of spines that provide protection from herbivores and intense sunlight. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom during the cooler months, typically in spring. Its root system is shallow and adapted to absorb moisture from infrequent rainfall.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid gardens.
