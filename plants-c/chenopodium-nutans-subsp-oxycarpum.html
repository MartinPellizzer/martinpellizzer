Chenopodium nutans subsp. oxycarpum

Chenopodium nutans subsp.

oxycarpum, commonly known as the leaning goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the Amaranthaceae family. It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit and has alternate, ovate to elliptic leaves with a rough texture.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its fruits are dry, three-angled utricles that contain one or two seeds. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is adapted to thrive in low-nutrient environments.
