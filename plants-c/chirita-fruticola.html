Chirita fruticola

Chirita fruticola, commonly known as the creeping chimera, is a perennial herb native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Gesneriaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of violet or purple, typically blooming in the spring and summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for shaded garden beds.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in horticulture for both indoor and outdoor settings.
