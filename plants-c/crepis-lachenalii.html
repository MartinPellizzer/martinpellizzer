Crepis lachenalii

Crepis lachenalii, commonly known as the Lachenal's sowthistle, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to Europe and is characterized by its upright, hairy stems and basal rosettes of lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Crepis lachenalii is an annual or biennial herb that plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators.
