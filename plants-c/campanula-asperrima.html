Campanula asperrima

Campanula asperrima, commonly known as the rough bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which give it a rough texture. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Campanula asperrima is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
