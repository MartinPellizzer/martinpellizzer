Calamintha intermedia

Calamintha intermedia, commonly known as narrowleaf catnip, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, aromatic leaves with a distinctive mint-like scent, which are often used in herbal remedies. The plant produces small, tubular blue or purple flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Its medicinal properties have been traditionally valued for their calming and digestive benefits.
