Campanula expansa var. macedonica

Campanula expansa var. macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
