Carex straminea var. festucacea

Carex straminea var. festucacea, commonly known as slender flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and other grass-like plants. This variant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical, spike-like flower clusters. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, wet meadows, and along stream banks.

The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
