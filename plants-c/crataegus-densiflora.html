Crataegus densiflora

Crataegus densiflora, commonly known as the denseflower hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny branches and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically eaten by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions and full sun.

Crataegus densiflora is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
