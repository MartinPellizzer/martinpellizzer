Calamus barisanensis

Calamus barisanensis, commonly known as the Barisan mountain calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in Indonesia. This plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. It thrives in wet, shady environments such as forest understories and swamps.

Calamus barisanensis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
