Cupania daemeliana

Cupania daemeliana, commonly known as the Brazilian elderberry, is a small deciduous shrub native to South America.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in catkin-like inflorescences, followed by red or purple berries that are edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along streambanks.

Cupania daemeliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
