Calamagrostis phalaroides

Calamagrostis phalaroides, commonly known as the slender reedgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. It is often found in wetland ecosystems where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.

Its botanical characteristics make it an important species for ecological restoration projects.
