Calonemorchis falcata

Calonemorchis falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved duckweed, is a small aquatic plant belonging to the family Araceae.

It is characterized by its finely divided, ribbon-like leaves that float on the surface of still or slow-moving freshwater bodies. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically submerged, followed by the development of aquatic berries. It thrives in nutrient-rich environments and is often found in ponds, ditches, and wetlands.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain ecosystems if not managed properly.
