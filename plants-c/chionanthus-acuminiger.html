Chionanthus acuminiger

Chionanthus acuminiger, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flowering plant, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Chionanthus acuminiger is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.
