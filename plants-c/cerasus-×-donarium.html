Cerasus × donarium

Cerasus × donarium, commonly known as the cherry laurel, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Prunus avium (sweet cherry) and Prunus laurocerasus (cherry laurel).

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and clusters of small, white flowers that appear in early spring. Its fruits are small, dark purple berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste.

Cerasus × donarium is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in landscaping due to its adaptability and resilience.
