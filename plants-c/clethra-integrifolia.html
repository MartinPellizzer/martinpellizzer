Clethra integrifolia

Clethra integrifolia, commonly known as sweet pepperbush, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are typically entire, giving it its species name "integrifolia.". The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in late summer, which are clustered in pendulous cymes. These flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
