Crataegus illinoiensis

Crataegus illinoiensis, commonly known as the Illinois hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the central United States.

It typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters and features thorned branches, which are characteristic of the Crataegus genus. The plant produces white or pink flowers in the spring, followed by red or orange berries in the fall. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

The Illinois hawthorn is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open woods and along streambanks.
