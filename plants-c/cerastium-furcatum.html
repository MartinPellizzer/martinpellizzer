Cerastium furcatum

Cerastium furcatum, commonly known as the forked chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to August. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a soft, fuzzy appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Cerastium furcatum is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is known for its ability to spread through runners.
