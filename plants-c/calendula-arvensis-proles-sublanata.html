Calendula arvensis proles sublanata

Calendula arvensis proles sublanata, commonly known as the hairy field marigold, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its yellow-orange flowers and hairy, glandular leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its subspecies sublanata is distinguished by its more densely hairy foliage compared to the typical form.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
