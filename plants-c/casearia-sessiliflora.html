Casearia sessiliflora

Casearia sessiliflora, commonly known as the Brazilian beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, dark purple berries that are edible but often considered bitter. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture.

Casearia sessiliflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
