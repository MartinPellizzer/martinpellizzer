Coreopsis tinctoria

Coreopsis tinctoria, commonly known as the tickseed or bloodflower, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its bright yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

The plant is also known for its historical use in dye production, as its flowers can be used to create vibrant yellow dyes.
