Crinum glaucum

Crinum glaucum, commonly known as the blue crinum, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in summer. The plant features strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Crinum glaucum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
