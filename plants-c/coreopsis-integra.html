Coreopsis integra

Coreopsis integra, commonly known as the tickseed, is a flowering plant native to the eastern and central United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring narrow, grass-like leaves and bright yellow to orange flower heads. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Coreopsis integra is known for its hardiness and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
