Cyathea beccariana

Cyathea beccariana, commonly known as the Beccari's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, trunk-like stem covered in fibrous roots. The plant features large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Cyathea beccariana plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and shelter for various forest organisms.
