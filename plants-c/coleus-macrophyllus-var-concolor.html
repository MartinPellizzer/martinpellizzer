Coleus macrophyllus var. concolor

Coleus macrophyllus var. concolor, commonly known as the broad-leaf coleus, is a tropical perennial plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 15 cm in length, often displaying vibrant shades of red, purple, and green. The plant produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white or pale pink, which appear at the base of the leaves. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil with partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
