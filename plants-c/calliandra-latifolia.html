Calliandra latifolia

Calliandra latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf calliandra, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its feathery, pink to red inflorescences that bloom throughout the dry season. The plant has bipinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental and agroforestry purposes.

Calliandra latifolia is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and potential as a forage crop.
