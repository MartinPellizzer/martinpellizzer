Conophytum novellum

Conophytum novellum, commonly known as the "Cape Town beauty," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, thick leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. Its unique leaf structure and vibrant floral display make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.

Conophytum novellum thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
