Caesalpinia intricata var. glabra

Caesalpinia intricata var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth coral tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its compound, feathery leaves and vibrant, flame-colored inflorescences that resemble coral. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in coastal and tropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
