Carludovica gigantea

Carludovica gigantea, commonly known as the giant nightshade, is a striking member of the Solanaceae family.

Native to the tropical regions of South America, this plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, violet-blue flowers. It produces toxic berries that contain tropane alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique appearance and ecological role make it a notable species in botanical studies.
