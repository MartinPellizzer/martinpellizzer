Copiapoa serpentisulcata

Copiapoa serpentisulcata, commonly known as the serpent-ribed cactus, is a species native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems with distinct, wavy ribs that give it its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers during the cooler months, typically in spring. It is a slow-growing cactus that requires well-draining soil and minimal water to thrive.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is often cultivated by succulent enthusiasts.
