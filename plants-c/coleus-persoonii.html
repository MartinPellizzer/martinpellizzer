Coleus persoonii

Coleus persoonii, commonly known as the "shiny leaf" or "velvetleaf" plant, is a member of the mint family (Lamiaceae).

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage with leaves ranging from deep purples to bright reds and greens. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, often forming dense, low-growing mats. Its leaves are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a soft, textured appearance.

Coleus persoonii is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping and as a houseplant.
