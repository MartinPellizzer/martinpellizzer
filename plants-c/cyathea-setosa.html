Cyathea setosa

Cyathea setosa, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a robust rhizome system that supports its growth and helps in nutrient absorption. Cyathea setosa typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in rainforests or along stream banks.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of tropical ecosystems.
