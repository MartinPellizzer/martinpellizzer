Carex sulcata

Carex sulcata, commonly known as the grooved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and similar plants. The plant is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves, which are often green to blue-green in color. Carex sulcata typically grows in moist or wet environments such as marshes, stream banks, and wet meadows.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are often grouped in clusters at the ends of its stems.
