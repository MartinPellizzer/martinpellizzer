Callistephus cuneatus

Callistephus cuneatus, commonly known as the common evening primrose, is an annual herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, hairy stems, and large, showy yellow flowers that open in the evening. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a rough texture.

The species is adapted to a variety of soil types and thrives in full sun, making it a common sight in disturbed habitats.
