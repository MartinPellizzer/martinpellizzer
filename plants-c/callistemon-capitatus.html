Callistemon capitatus

Callistemon capitatus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bottlebrush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cylindrical red or pink flower spikes, which are clustered at the ends of its branches. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in a fine, silvery or greyish bloom. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established.

The botanical features of Callistemon capitatus make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
