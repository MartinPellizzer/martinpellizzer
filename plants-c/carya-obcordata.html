Carya obcordata

Carya obcordata, commonly known as the winged bean or the winged pumpkin, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the walnut family (Juglandaceae) and is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves with a deep lobed base. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by oval, woody fruits that split open to reveal edible seeds. Its bark is thick and fissured, often featuring a reddish or brownish hue.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
