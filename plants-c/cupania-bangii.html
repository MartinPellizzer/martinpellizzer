Cupania bangii

Cupania bangii, commonly known as the Borneo cupania, is a climbing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical rainforests and is often found in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value and potential medicinal properties, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
