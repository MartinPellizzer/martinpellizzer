Croton linearis var. dilatatus

Croton linearis var. dilatatus, commonly known as the broadleaf croton, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with yellow and green patterns. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
