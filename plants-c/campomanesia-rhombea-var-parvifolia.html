Campomanesia rhombea var. parvifolia

Campomanesia rhombea var. parvifolia, commonly known as the dwarf wax apple, is a tropical shrub native to the Amazon Basin.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a waxy coating. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, yellow-orange berries. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for ornamental use in shaded gardens.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
