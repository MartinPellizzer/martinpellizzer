Conyza scandens

Conyza scandens, commonly known as common blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Americas.

It typically grows in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and can thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant features slender, branching stems that often lie prostrate or ascend, with small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in the summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs.

Conyza scandens is known for its ability to spread through seeds dispersed by wind, making it a common weed in agricultural and natural areas.
