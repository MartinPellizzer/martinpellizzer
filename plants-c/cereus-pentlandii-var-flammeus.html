Cereus pentlandii var. flammeus

Cereus pentlandii var. flammeus, commonly known as the Flame Cereus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to South America.

It is a flowering cactus that produces striking, bright red flowers during the night, which are pollinated by moths. The plant has a tall, cylindrical stem with a smooth, pale green surface and sparse spines. Its flowers are short-lived, opening only for a few hours and closing by dawn.

This species is often cultivated for its dramatic nocturnal blooms and unique appearance.
