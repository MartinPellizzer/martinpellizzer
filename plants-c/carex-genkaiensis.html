Carex genkaiensis

Carex genkaiensis, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes various grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, spike-like flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

Carex genkaiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
