Cirsium munitum

Cirsium munitum, commonly known as the Pacific madrone, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, reddish-barked trunk and showy yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas. Its leaves are usually alternate, with serrated margins, and its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

Cirsium munitum is also known for its edible berries, which are consumed by various wildlife species.
