Cassia alata

Cassia alata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its long, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces pods containing flat, seed-filled seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Cassia alata thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
