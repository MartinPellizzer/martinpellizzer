Ceropegia zebrina subsp. insigniflora

Ceropegia zebrina subsp.

insigniflora, commonly known as the Zebra Plant or Zebra Ceropegia, is a succulent shrub native to southern Africa. It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in small, white or pale green tubercles.

The leaves are typically small, narrow, and arranged in a rosette pattern, giving the plant a compact appearance. It produces delicate, tubular flowers that are usually pale green or yellowish in color.

This species is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil to thrive.
