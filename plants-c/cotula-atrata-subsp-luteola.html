Cotula atrata subsp. luteola

Cotula atrata subsp.

luteola, commonly known as the yellow cotula, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a low-growing herb with hairy stems and small, yellowish-green leaves.

The plant produces clusters of tiny, daisy-like flowers that are typically yellow to orange in color. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and inland regions. Cotula atrata subsp.

luteola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
