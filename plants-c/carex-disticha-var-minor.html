Carex disticha var. minor

Carex disticha var. minor, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, grasses, and rushes. This variant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower spikes that bloom in early summer. It typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
