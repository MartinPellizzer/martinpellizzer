Cirsium × prativagum

Cirsium × prativagum, commonly known as the common thistle, is a perennial flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, showcasing adaptability to various environments. Its vegetative structure includes a robust root system and a tall, erect stem.

Cirsium × prativagum is known for its ecological importance, providing food and habitat for various insects and wildlife.
