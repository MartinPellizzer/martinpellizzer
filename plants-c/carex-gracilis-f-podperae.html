Carex gracilis f. podperae

Carex gracilis f. podperae, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a variant of the slender sedge species, characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit.

It is a perennial plant native to moist, shaded environments in temperate regions, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies that help stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
