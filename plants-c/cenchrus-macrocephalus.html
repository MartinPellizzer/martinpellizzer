Cenchrus macrocephalus

Cenchrus macrocephalus, commonly known as the large-headed grass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical inflorescence that bears large, spike-like clusters of florets. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a common species in both natural and agricultural settings.
