Celastrus approximatus

Celastrus approximatus, commonly known as the common staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found in the understory of forests.

Its rapid growth habit makes it both a useful ornamental plant and a potential invasive species in certain regions.
