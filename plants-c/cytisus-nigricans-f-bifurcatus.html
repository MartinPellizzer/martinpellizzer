Cytisus nigricans f. bifurcatus

Cytisus nigricans f. bifurcatus, commonly known as the black thorn broom, is a flowering shrub native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. Its stems are often thorny, and the leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three leaflets.

This variety is distinguished by its dark-colored stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
