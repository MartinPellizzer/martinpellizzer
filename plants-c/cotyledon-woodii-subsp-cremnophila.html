Cotyledon woodii subsp. cremnophila

Cotyledon woodii subsp.

cremnophila, commonly known as the rock cotyledon, is a succulent plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green with red or purple margins.

The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires plenty of sunlight.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in controlled environments.
