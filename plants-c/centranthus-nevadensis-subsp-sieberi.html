Centranthus nevadensis subsp. sieberi

Centranthus nevadensis subsp.

sieberi, commonly known as the Sierra centranthus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky and dry habitats, such as slopes and meadows.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular, pink to purple flowers. It is adapted to high-altitude environments, exhibiting cold tolerance and efficient water use.

Its ornamental value has made it a subject of interest in horticulture and ecological studies.
