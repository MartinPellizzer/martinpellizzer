Commelina rumphii

Commelina rumphii, commonly known as the blue commelina, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, thriving in humid climates with ample sunlight. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often with a waxy surface that helps retain moisture.

Commelina rumphii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
