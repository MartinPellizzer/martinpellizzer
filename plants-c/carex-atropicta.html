Carex atropicta

Carex atropicta, commonly known as blackstem sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and stream banks. Its dark purple or black stems are a distinguishing feature, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early summer.

Carex atropicta plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
