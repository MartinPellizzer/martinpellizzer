Cucumis melo subsp. orientalis

Cucumis melo subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as the cantaloupe, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family and is native to southern Asia. It is characterized by its thick, ribbed rind and sweet, aromatic flesh.

The plant produces large, yellow or orange-colored fruits with a distinctive netted pattern. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The fruit is typically harvested when fully mature, just before the rind hardens.
