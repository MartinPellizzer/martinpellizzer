Conyza hirsuta

Conyza hirsuta, commonly known as hairy weed or slenderleaf fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to Africa, South America, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are typically arranged in a flat-topped cluster. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Conyza hirsuta is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through wind-dispersed seeds.
