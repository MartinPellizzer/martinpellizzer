Campanula cochleariifolia subf. hoppeaniformis

Campanula cochleariifolia subf.

hoppeaniformis, commonly known as the Hoppean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branching.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
