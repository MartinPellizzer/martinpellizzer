Calligonum roborowskii

Calligonum roborowskii, commonly known as the Roborowskii saltbush, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in saline and alkaline soils, making it well adapted to harsh desert environments.

Calligonum roborowskii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
