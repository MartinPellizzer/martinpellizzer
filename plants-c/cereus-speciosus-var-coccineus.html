Cereus speciosus var. coccineus

Cereus speciosus var. coccineus, commonly known as the Red Cereus, is a cacti species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and striking red flowers that bloom at night. The plant has sharp, spiny ribs and produces clusters of small, white flowers that emit a fragrant scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.
