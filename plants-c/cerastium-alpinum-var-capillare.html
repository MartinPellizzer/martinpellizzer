Cerastium alpinum var. capillare

Cerastium alpinum var. capillare, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features small, hairy, spoon-shaped leaves that are clustered at the base of the plant. The flowers are typically white or pale pink, with five petals, and appear in early summer. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This variant is distinguished by its fine, hair-like leaves and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.
