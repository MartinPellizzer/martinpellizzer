Cinnamomum nalingway

Cinnamomum nalingway, commonly known as the Nalingwa cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like cinnamon and laurel. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its bark and leaves contain essential oils that contribute to its aromatic properties.

The tree typically grows to a height of about 10 meters and thrives in humid, tropical climates.
