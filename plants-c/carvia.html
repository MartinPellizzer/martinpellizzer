Carvia

Carvia, commonly known as the wild carrot or Queen Anne's lace, is a biennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and an umbel of white flowers that resemble a lace doily. The plant produces small, edible seeds that are often used in culinary applications. Its root is also edible and resembles a small carrot in appearance.

Carvia is native to Europe and parts of Asia but has been naturalized in many other regions around the world.
