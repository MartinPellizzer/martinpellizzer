Castanopsis ferox

Castanopsis ferox, commonly known as the rough-barked tanoak, is a species of evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The plant is characterized by its thick, fissured bark and elongated, elliptical leaves that are leathery in texture. Its cones are small and woody, containing edible nuts that are a food source for various wildlife.

The tree thrives in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its durable wood and ecological importance in tropical forests.
