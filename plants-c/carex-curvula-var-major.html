Carex curvula var. major

Carex curvula var. major, commonly known as the giant yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are typically bright yellow to green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are usually arranged in a cylindrical cluster. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Carex curvula var. major is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse ecosystems.
