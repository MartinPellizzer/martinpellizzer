Cistus teretifolius

Cistus teretifolius, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features silver-gray, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, which are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or sandy environments.

Cistus teretifolius is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
