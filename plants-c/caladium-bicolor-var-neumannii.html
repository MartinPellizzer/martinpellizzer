Caladium bicolor var. neumannii

Caladium bicolor var. neumannii, commonly known as the Neumann's flameleaf, is a tropical perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage that displays shades of red, orange, and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its leaves are heart-shaped and can grow up to 12 inches in diameter, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It is typically grown as a houseplant or in shaded garden beds where it can flourish without direct sunlight.
