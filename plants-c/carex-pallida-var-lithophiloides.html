Carex pallida var. lithophiloides

Carex pallida var. lithophiloides, commonly known as the pale sedge variant lithophiloides, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This variant is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
