Celastrus papuanus

Celastrus papuanus, commonly known as the Papuan staff-vine, is a climbing plant native to the lowland rainforests of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Celastraceae family and is characterized by its twining stems and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by orange-red berries that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary growth areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
