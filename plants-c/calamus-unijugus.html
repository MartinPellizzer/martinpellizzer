Calamus unijugus

Calamus unijugus, commonly known as the single-jawed sweetflag, is a member of the Aroid family (Araceae).

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant features a rhizomatous stem with narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often aromatic. Its inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Calamus unijugus is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
