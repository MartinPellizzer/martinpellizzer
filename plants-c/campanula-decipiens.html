Campanula decipiens

Campanula decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its common name reflects the plant's ability to mimic other flowers, potentially misleading pollinators.
