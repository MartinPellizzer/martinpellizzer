Clematis gracilifolia var. macrantha

Clematis gracilifolia var. macrantha, commonly known as the slenderleaf clematis, is a flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as a climbing vine, reaching lengths of up to several meters. Its flowers are usually white or pale blue, with five petals and a distinctive, elongated style.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.
