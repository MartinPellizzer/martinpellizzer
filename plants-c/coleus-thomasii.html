Coleus thomasii

Coleus thomasii, commonly known as Thomas's coleus, is a species of flowering plant in the mint family, Lamiaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which often displays shades of red, purple, and green. It produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale purple or white, arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches.

Coleus thomasii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant leaf colors and ease of care.
