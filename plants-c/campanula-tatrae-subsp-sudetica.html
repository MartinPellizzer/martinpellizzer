Campanula tatrae subsp. sudetica

Campanula tatrae subsp.

sudetica, commonly known as the Sudetic bellflower, is a rare alpine plant native to the Carpathian Mountains. It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are basal and often have a hairy texture, adapted to the cold and harsh conditions of its environment.

It plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is considered a species of conservation concern.
