Cassuvium balansae

Cassuvium balansae, commonly known as the moonflower vine, is a tropical climbing plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant white flowers that open at night and close by dawn. The leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but not commonly cultivated.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
