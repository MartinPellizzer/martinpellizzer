Croton grandivelum var. augustinianus

Croton grandivelum var. augustinianus, commonly known as the Augustinian croton, is a tropical shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It features vibrant, variegated foliage with shades of green, yellow, and red, making it a visually striking ornamental plant. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a rough, textured surface. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
