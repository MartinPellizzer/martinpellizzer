Crescentia obovata

Crescentia obovata, commonly known as the Caribbean apple or bocashi, is a tropical tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a fleshy, orange-red fruit with a sweet, aromatic pulp. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland tropical forests. Its fruit is edible and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to grow in various soil types.
