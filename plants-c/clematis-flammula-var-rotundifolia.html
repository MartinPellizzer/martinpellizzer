Clematis flammula var. rotundifolia

Clematis flammula var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf clematis, is a perennial climbing plant native to Europe and western Asia.

It features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, allowing it to climb various supports. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained, fertile soils.
