Celastrus adenophyllus

Celastrus adenophyllus, commonly known as the Indian bindweed, is a climbing shrub native to the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red, berry-like fruits. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a smooth margin and can be slightly hairy on the underside.

Celastrus adenophyllus is often found in moist, shaded environments and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
