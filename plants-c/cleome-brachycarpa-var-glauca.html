Cleome brachycarpa var. glauca

Cleome brachycarpa var. glauca, commonly known as the glaucous cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, branching stems and bright yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in a fine, waxy coating. This variety is distinguished by its bluish-gray foliage, which gives it its common name.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
