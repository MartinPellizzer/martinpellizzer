Campanula glomerata var. acutifolia

Campanula glomerata var. acutifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a pointed tip, giving it its specific epithet "acutifolia.". It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and long blooming period.
