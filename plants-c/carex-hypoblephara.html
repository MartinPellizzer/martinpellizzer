Carex hypoblephara

Carex hypoblephara, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, clustered spikes of flowers in the summer.

Carex hypoblephara plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
