Cistus × lenis f. albiflorus

Cistus × lenis f. albiflorus, commonly known as the white-flowered rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the genus Cistus, which is part of the family Ericaceae, known for its aromatic and often evergreen species. This particular cultivar is distinguished by its pale to white flowers, which contrast with its grey-green foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
