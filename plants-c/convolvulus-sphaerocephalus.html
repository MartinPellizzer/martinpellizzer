Convolvulus sphaerocephalus

Convolvulus sphaerocephalus, commonly known as the globe thistle, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces spherical, blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by spiny bracts. The plant has hairy, branching stems and small, alternate leaves that are usually lance-shaped. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to xeriscapes.
