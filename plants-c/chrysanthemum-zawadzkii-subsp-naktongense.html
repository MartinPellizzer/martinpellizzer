Chrysanthemum zawadzkii subsp. naktongense

Chrysanthemum zawadzkii subsp.

naktongense, commonly known as the Naktong chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to South Korea. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like yellow to orange flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. It has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in traditional horticulture.
