Convolvulus arboreus

Convolvulus arboreus, commonly known as the tree convolvulus, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub-like species that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with woody stems and a sprawling growth habit. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pale purple or white, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often hairy, while its fruit is a capsule containing numerous small seeds.

Convolvulus arboreus is adapted to arid environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
