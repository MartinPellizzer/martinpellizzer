Coronilla cannabina

Coronilla cannabina, commonly known as the hemp vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, pea-like flowers. The plant has compound leaves with oval leaflets and produces small, hairy pods that contain seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in poor, dry conditions.

Coronilla cannabina is also notable for its historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
