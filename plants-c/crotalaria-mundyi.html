Crotalaria mundyi

Crotalaria mundyi, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, round pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein. It thrives in warm, tropical climates and is often grown for its ornamental value and nutritional benefits.

Crotalaria mundyi is also known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural purposes.
