Crataegus monogyna var. nigra

Crataegus monogyna var. nigra, commonly known as the black thorn, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces white or pale pink flowers in spring, followed by red or black berries in autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in hedgerows, woodland edges, and disturbed areas.

The berries are a food source for birds and can be used in traditional herbal remedies.
