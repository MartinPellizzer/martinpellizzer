Carya mexicana

Carya mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican hickory, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the walnut family (Juglandaceae) and is characterized by its compound leaves and edible nuts. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy and thick, ridged bark. Its flowers are dioecious, with separate male and female plants, and the nuts are encased in a spiny husk.

The plant is valued for its hard wood and is also used in traditional medicine.
