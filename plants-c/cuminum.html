Cuminum

Cuminum, commonly known as cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and dill. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers and has feathery, aromatic leaves. Its seeds, which are the primary part used in cooking, are harvested when fully mature and dried.

Cuminum thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a hardy and versatile crop.
