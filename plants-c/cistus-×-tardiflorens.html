Cistus × tardiflorens

Cistus × tardiflorens, commonly known as the late-flowering rock rose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic evergreen foliage and clusters of pink to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal environments. Its leaves are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
