Cuphea cinnabarina

Cuphea cinnabarina, commonly known as the red flame cuphea, is a compact, bushy shrub native to Mexico.

It features showy, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer, giving it a distinctive texture. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-draining soil.

Cuphea cinnabarina is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm climates.
