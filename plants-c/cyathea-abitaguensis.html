Cyathea abitaguensis

Cyathea abitaguensis, commonly known as the Abitagua tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Central America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cyathea abitaguensis plays a role in providing habitat and shelter for various forest organisms.
