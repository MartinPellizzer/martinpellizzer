Campsiandra comosa

Campsiandra comosa, commonly known as the hairy campsiandra, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a fleshy, yellowish drupe that contains a single seed.

Campsiandra comosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
