Cistus × candidus nothosubsp. tomentosus

Cistus × candidus nothosubsp.

tomentosus, commonly known as the woolly rockrose, is a hybrid shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage covered in a fine, woolly layer of hairs.

The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pale pink to deep red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its aromatic leaves and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
