Cystopteris fumarioides

It belongs to the family Dryopteridaceae and is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky areas. Its spore-bearing structures, called sori, are arranged in lines on the underside of the fronds.
