Cassia potosina

Cassia potosina, commonly known as Mexican cassia, is a shrub native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its leguminous plants. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are encased in pods and are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia potosina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry, arid environments.
