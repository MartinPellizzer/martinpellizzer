Carex macroglossa

Carex macroglossa, commonly known as the broad-leaf sedge, is a flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves that are usually narrow and arranged in a tight, cylindrical cluster. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of small, greenish flowers that are often clustered in three rows.

Carex macroglossa plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
