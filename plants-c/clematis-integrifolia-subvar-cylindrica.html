Clematis integrifolia subvar. cylindrica

Clematis integrifolia subvar. cylindrica, commonly known as the smooth Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features heart-shaped, serrated leaves that are typically smooth and dark green. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of blue, purple, and white, often in late spring to early summer. Its stems are slender and twining, allowing it to climb and cover structures or trellises.

It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.
