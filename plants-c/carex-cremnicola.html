Carex cremnicola

Carex cremnicola, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets. Native to regions of Europe and Asia, it typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
