Cirsium incanum

Cirsium incanum, commonly known as the hoary thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its grayish-white, fuzzy leaves and spiny, purple flower heads that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open fields and along roadsides. Its leaves and stems are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

Cirsium incanum is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions.
