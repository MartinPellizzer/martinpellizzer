Cacosmia rugosa var. kingii

Cacosmia rugosa var. kingii, commonly known as the King's velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in moist lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental foliage, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
