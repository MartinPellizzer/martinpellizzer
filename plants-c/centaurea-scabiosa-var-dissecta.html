Centaurea scabiosa var. dissecta

Centaurea scabiosa var. dissecta, commonly known as the fernleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and conical, purple or pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. It is an upright perennial that can reach heights of up to 60 cm, with a fibrous root system.

The flowers are rich in nectar and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
