Carex gorodkovii

Carex gorodkovii, commonly known as the Siberian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the cold climates of eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and stream banks. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex gorodkovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
