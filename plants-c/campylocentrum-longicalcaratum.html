Campylocentrum longicalcaratum

Campylocentrum longicalcaratum, commonly known as the long-spurred flame vine, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a red throat. The plant produces elongated, spurred petals that are adapted for pollination by birds and insects. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to tropical gardens and greenhouses.
