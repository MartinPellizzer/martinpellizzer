Crotalaria bellii

Crotalaria bellii, commonly known as the yellow rattle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and is part of the legume family, Fabaceae. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in open grasslands and is known for its role in nitrogen fixation.

The species is also valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to improve soil fertility.
