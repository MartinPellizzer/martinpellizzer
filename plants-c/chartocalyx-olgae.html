Chartocalyx olgae

Chartocalyx olgae, commonly known as the African snowball tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its spherical clusters of white flowers that appear in late winter to early spring, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The fruit is a dry capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.
