Calamagrostis macbridei

Calamagrostis macbridei, commonly known as the MacBride reed, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of central China.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Calamagrostis macbridei is often found in alpine meadows and rocky areas, where it contributes to the ecological stability of its habitat.

Its ornamental value has made it a subject of interest for horticulturists and botanists alike.
