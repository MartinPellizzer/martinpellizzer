Chenopodium triangulare subsp. polygonoides

Chenopodium triangulare subsp.

polygonoides, commonly known as New Zealand spinach, is a herbaceous plant native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its triangular, lobed leaves that are edible and often used in culinary applications.

The plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils. It produces small, green flowers that develop into spherical, dry fruits containing several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its nutritional content and adaptability to various growing conditions.
