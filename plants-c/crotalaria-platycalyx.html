Crotalaria platycalyx

Crotalaria platycalyx, commonly known as the narrow-capsule cowpea, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and compound leaves. The plant produces small, oval seed pods that contain several seeds, which are often used as a food source in some regions. It has a taproot system and thrives in well-drained soils, making it adaptable to various environmental conditions.

Additionally, it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, which benefits soil fertility.
