Carex careyana

Carex careyana, commonly known as the Carey's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters. Its flowering structure consists of small, inconspicuous spikes that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex careyana plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
