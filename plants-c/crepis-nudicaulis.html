Crepis nudicaulis

Crepis nudicaulis, commonly known as the nakedleaf mouse-ear, is a small flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. Its leaves are narrow and often hairless, giving it the common name "nakedleaf.".

It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.
