Carex stiphrogyne

Carex stiphrogyne, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to North America and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and cylindrical, with a fibrous root system. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are often reddish in color.

Carex stiphrogyne plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
