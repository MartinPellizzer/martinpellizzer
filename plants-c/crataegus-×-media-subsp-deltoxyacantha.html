Crataegus × media subsp. deltoxyacantha

Crataegus × media subsp.

deltoxyacantha, commonly known as the deltoidleaf hawthorn, is a hybrid shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its deltoid-shaped leaves, which are broad and pointed at the base.

The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early spring, followed by red berries in the fall. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy flowers and persistent fruits.
