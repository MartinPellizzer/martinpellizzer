Caraguata lindenii

Caraguata lindenii, commonly known as the Brazilian linden, is a species of flowering plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. Caraguata lindenii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities in gardens.
