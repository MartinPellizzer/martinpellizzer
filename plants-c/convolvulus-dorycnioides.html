Convolvulus dorycnioides

Convolvulus dorycnioides, commonly known as the slender wood morning glory, is a flowering plant belonging to the bindweed family (Convolvulaceae).

It is native to parts of Africa and is characterized by its trailing or climbing growth habit. The plant produces small, pale yellow or white flowers that bloom in the morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are hairy and often twining.

It is often found in disturbed soils and can be invasive in certain regions.
