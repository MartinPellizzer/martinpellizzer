Calanthe puberula

Calanthe puberula, commonly known as the velvet orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit and produces showy, lanceolate leaves that are densely covered with soft, velvety hairs. The plant typically blooms in spring, producing clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers with three sepals and three petals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its elegant appearance.
