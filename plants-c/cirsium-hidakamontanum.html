Cirsium hidakamontanum

Cirsium hidakamontanum, commonly known as the Hidaka thistle, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist, mountainous regions and thrives in temperate climates. It has a robust root system that allows it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its flowering period usually occurs in late summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
