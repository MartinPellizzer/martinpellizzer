Cortusa matthioli var. sachalinensis

Cortusa matthioli var. sachalinensis, commonly known as the Sachalin bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Russian Far East.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a rosette of basal leaves and shorter, upright stems that bear the flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and long blooming period.
