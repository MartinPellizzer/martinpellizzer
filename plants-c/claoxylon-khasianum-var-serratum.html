Claoxylon khasianum var. serratum

Claoxylon khasianum var. serratum, commonly known as the Khasi ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to the northeastern regions of India.

It is characterized by its hard, heavy wood that is highly valued for its durability and resistance to decay. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have serrated edges, giving it its specific variety name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits.

This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils and plays an important role in the local ecosystem.
