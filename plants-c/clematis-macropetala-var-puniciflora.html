Clematis macropetala var. puniciflora

Clematis macropetala var. puniciflora, commonly known as the red-flowered clematis, is a deciduous vine native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its large, showy, red to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, rusty-colored hairs. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in shaded garden settings.
