Chionanthus insularis

Chionanthus insularis, commonly known as the island chionanthus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean islands.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in coastal and limestone habitats. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
