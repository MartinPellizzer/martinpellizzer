Cinnamomum gamblei

Cinnamomum gamblei, commonly known as the Philippine cinnamon or gambier tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark-brown bark that resembles cinnamon. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall, with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green on top. Its flowers are small and white, and it produces oval, dark blue berries that contain a single seed.

The plant is valued for its aromatic properties and has traditional uses in medicine and as a spice.
