Convallaria govaniana

Convallaria govaniana, commonly known as the Govan's lily, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Liliaceae and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces clusters of small, fragrant blooms during the spring season.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
