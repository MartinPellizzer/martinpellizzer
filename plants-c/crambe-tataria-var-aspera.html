Crambe tataria var. aspera

Crambe tataria var. aspera, commonly known as rough tatarian crambe, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, which includes other well-known plants such as mustard and cabbage. The plant features erect, branched stems with narrow, hairy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of pubescence. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in racemes, and it produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has also been studied for its potential agricultural uses.
