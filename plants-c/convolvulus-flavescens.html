Convolvulus flavescens

Convolvulus flavescens, commonly known as yellow morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the morning, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in coastal areas.

The species is part of the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar climbing or trailing habits.
