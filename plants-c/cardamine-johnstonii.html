Cardamine johnstonii

Cardamine johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's watercress, is a small herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and forest floors. The plant has slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are small, white or pale yellow, and arranged in clusters at the tips of the stems.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
