Chamaecrista puccioniana

Chamaecrista puccioniana, commonly known as the purple crown wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, delicate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers, which are surrounded by distinctive purple bracts that give it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy regions.

Chamaecrista puccioniana is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities and ecological importance in soil enrichment.
