Claoxylon neoebudicum

Claoxylon neoebudicum, commonly known as the Borneo ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It is characterized by its dense, heavy wood that is highly valued for its strength and durability. The plant has a straight trunk with a smooth, dark bark that often has a reddish tint. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, typically arranged in whorls of three.

Claoxylon neoebudicum plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
