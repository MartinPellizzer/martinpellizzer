Campomanesia acrandroides

Campomanesia acrandroides, commonly known as the Brazilian wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by bright red, fleshy berries that are edible. Its fruit is rich in vitamin C and has been traditionally used in local cuisines and herbal remedies.

The plant thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nutritional benefits.
