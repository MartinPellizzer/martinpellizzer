Calligonum smirnovii

Calligonum smirnovii, commonly known as the Central Asian spurge, is a perennial shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has adapted to harsh environmental conditions through its deep root system and ability to store water. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a waxy layer to reduce water loss.

Calligonum smirnovii plays a role in stabilizing soils in degraded landscapes and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
