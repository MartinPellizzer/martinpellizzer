Cassia hoffmannseggii var. gardneriana

Cassia hoffmannseggii var. gardneriana, commonly known as the Gardner's cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 3–6 meters, featuring compound leaves with 10–20 pairs of oval, serrated leaflets. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in late spring to early summer, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
