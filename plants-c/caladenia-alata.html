Caladenia alata

Caladenia alata, commonly known as the slender spider-orchid, is a striking orchid native to southern Australia.

It is characterized by its long, slender petals and a distinctive, narrow labellum that resembles a spider's leg, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in heathland and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are usually pale green to yellowish in color, with a red or brownish throat.

Caladenia alata is a terrestrial orchid that blooms during the autumn season, adding a unique floral display to its native habitat.
