Chironia pusilla

Chironia pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf spider orchid, is a small, terrestrial orchid native to parts of Australia.

It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments. The plant features delicate, slender stems and small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it is known for its compact size and subtle appearance.

Chironia pusilla is often found in coastal or inland regions where the climate is temperate and humidity is moderate.
