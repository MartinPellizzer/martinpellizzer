Carex lutea

Carex lutea, commonly known as yellow sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. The plant features triangular, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically yellow or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Carex lutea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
