Clusia gaudichaudii

Clusia gaudichaudii, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage.

Clusia gaudichaudii is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and air pollution.
