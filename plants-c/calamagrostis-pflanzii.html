Calamagrostis pflanzii

Calamagrostis pflanzii, commonly known as the Pflanz's reed, is a tall, grass-like perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, hollow stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowering period occurs in late summer, producing panicles of small, spike-like inflorescences.

Calamagrostis pflanzii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
