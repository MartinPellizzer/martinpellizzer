Cervispina cathartica

Cervispina cathartica, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and produces clusters of small, yellow, spurred flowers. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks. Its flowers bloom in early spring, and it is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
