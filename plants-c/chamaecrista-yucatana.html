Chamaecrista yucatana

Chamaecrista yucatana, commonly known as Yucatan burr legume, is a perennial shrub native to Central America and parts of the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to yellow. The plant produces distinctive, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are often dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
