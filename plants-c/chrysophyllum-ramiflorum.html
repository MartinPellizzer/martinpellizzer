Chrysophyllum ramiflorum

Chrysophyllum ramiflorum, commonly known as the African star apple, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by edible, sweet, star-shaped fruits. Its fruit is valued for its nutritional content and is often consumed fresh or processed into beverages.

The tree is also appreciated for its ornamental value and is cultivated in various parts of the world for its fruit and aesthetic appeal.
