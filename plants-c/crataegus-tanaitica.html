Crataegus tanaitica

Crataegus tanaitica, commonly known as the Armenian hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
