Cirsium laevigatum

Cirsium laevigatum, commonly known as the smooth thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry grasslands, rocky soils, and disturbed areas. It has a deep taproot system, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Cirsium laevigatum is often found in open habitats and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
