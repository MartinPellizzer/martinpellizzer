Chrysobactron

Chrysobactron, commonly known as the golden brome, is a grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, pale yellow to golden flowers in spikelets, which are arranged in dense clusters along the stem. Chrysobactron is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of native plant communities and a potential forage crop.
