Cattleya candida

Cattleya candida, commonly known as the white cattleya, is a species of orchid native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy white flowers with prominent yellow lips. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its leaves are broad and strap-like, often arranged in a fan shape.

Cattleya candida is prized in horticulture for its beauty and fragrance, making it a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts.
