Cacalia pigmaea

Cacalia pigmaea, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the western United States.

It features slender, branching stems that grow up to 30 centimeters in height. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cacalia pigmaea thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and wildflower habitats.
