Chrysocyathus leiosepalus

Chrysocyathus leiosepalus, commonly known as the velvetleaf orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, velvety pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pink and white. Its roots are specialized for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and surrounding substrate.

Chrysocyathus leiosepalus thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees in tropical rainforests.
