Cephalocereus nobilis var. curtisii

Cephalocereus nobilis var. curtisii, commonly known as the Curtis's night bloomer, is a species of cactus native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and produces large, fragrant white flowers at night. The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. Its flowering period typically occurs in the spring and summer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting nocturnal pollinators.
