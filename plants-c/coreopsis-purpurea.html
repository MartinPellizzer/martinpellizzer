Coreopsis purpurea

Coreopsis purpurea, commonly known as the purpletop coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet. The plant features bright yellow to orange flowers surrounded by purple or pink bracts, which are modified leaves that serve to attract pollinators. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Coreopsis purpurea is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
