Carex nigra f. stenostachya

Carex nigra f. stenostachya, commonly known as black sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features dark green, narrow leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and shaded woodland areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
