Clematis viorna var. pitcheri

Clematis viorna var. pitcheri, commonly known as the Pitcher's clematis, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous vine characterized by its slender, twining stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are usually blue or violet in color. It thrives in partial to full shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

The variety is noted for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
