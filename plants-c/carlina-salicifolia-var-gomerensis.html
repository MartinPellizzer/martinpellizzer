Carlina salicifolia var. gomerensis

Carlina salicifolia var. gomerensis, commonly known as the Gomer's willow-leaved cudweed, is a perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its lanceolate, willow-like leaves that are typically grayish-green in color. The plant produces small, blue to purple composite flower heads that bloom in late summer. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Asteraceae family.
