Chamaecyparis obtusa f. gracilis-aurea

Chamaecyparis obtusa f. gracilis-aurea, commonly known as the golden thread cedar, is a compact, slow-growing evergreen conifer with a distinctive weeping form.

Its foliage is dense and soft, featuring bright golden-yellow needles that contrast sharply with its silver-gray bark. The plant is prized for its ornamental value in gardens, especially in shaded or partially sunny locations. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate watering.

This variety is particularly noted for its unique appearance and adaptability to various climates.
