Chamaecrista brachystachya

Chamaecrista brachystachya, commonly known as the small-stemmed crownflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod fruits. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to drought conditions. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in dense clusters, and the leaves are bipinnate, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
