Chamaecrista nictitans var. commixta

Chamaecrista nictitans var. commixta, commonly known as the yellow crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are pinnately compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
