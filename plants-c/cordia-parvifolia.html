Cordia parvifolia

Cordia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved cordia, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its simple, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid lowland areas.

Cordia parvifolia is valued for its ornamental potential and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
