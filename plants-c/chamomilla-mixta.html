Chamomilla mixta

Chamomilla mixta, commonly known as German chamomile, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center, and its aromatic, serrated leaves. The plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa, and it thrives in temperate climates. Chamomilla mixta is often cultivated for its essential oils, which are used in aromatherapy and herbal medicine.

Its flowers are typically harvested when fully open to maximize their aromatic compounds.
