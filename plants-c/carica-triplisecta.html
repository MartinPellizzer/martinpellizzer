Carica triplisecta

Carica triplisecta, commonly known as the triplisect leaf papaya, is a unique variant of the papaya plant characterized by its highly lobed, triplisect leaves.

The plant belongs to the Caricaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Central and South America. Its leaves are deeply divided into three parts, giving it a distinctive appearance compared to typical papaya species. The fruit, though less commonly cultivated, shares the characteristic sweet, aromatic flesh of other papayas.

Due to its ornamental value and genetic uniqueness, it is occasionally grown in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
