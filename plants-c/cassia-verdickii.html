Cassia verdickii

Cassia verdickii, commonly known as the Indian laburnum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow, pendulous flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Cassia verdickii is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in Ayurvedic practices for its therapeutic properties.
