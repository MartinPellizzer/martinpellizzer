Cattleya labiata f. albo-oculata

Cattleya labiata f. albo-oculata, commonly known as the white-eyed cattleya, is a hybrid orchid prized for its striking floral display.

It features large, showy flowers with white petals and a distinctive yellowish-orange lip that has a white eye-like marking. The plant is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid environments. It is a sympodial orchid with a pseudobulb structure and produces multiple flower spikes from the base of the plant.

This cultivar is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
