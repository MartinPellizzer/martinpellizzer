Chloris quinquesetica

Chloris quinquesetica, commonly known as the five-angled grass, is a perennial grass species native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Chloris quinquesetica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native landscaping and restoration projects.
