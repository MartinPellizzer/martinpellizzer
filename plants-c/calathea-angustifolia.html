Calathea angustifolia

Calathea angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, lance-shaped leaves that often display intricate patterns and shades of green, silver, and pink. The plant thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent humidity and well-draining soil. Its foliage is highly ornamental, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Calathea angustifolia is relatively low-maintenance but sensitive to temperature changes and direct sunlight.
