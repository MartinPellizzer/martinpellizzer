Copaifera piresii

Copaifera piresii, commonly known as the Brazilian copaiba, is a tropical tree native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and produces fragrant, resinous sap that has been used for centuries in traditional medicine. The plant belongs to the Clusiaceae family and is known for its ornamental value as well as its ecological importance. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, typically yellow or green, and it produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

The tree plays a vital role in maintaining the biodiversity of its native habitat.
