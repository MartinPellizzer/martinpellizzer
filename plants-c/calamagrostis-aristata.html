Calamagrostis aristata

Calamagrostis aristata, commonly known as silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and feathery, silver-gray plumes that give it a striking appearance. The plant thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that adds visual interest in gardens.

Calamagrostis aristata is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat for wildlife and improving soil stability.
