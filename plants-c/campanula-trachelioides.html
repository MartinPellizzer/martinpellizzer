Campanula trachelioides

Campanula trachelioides, commonly known as the blue-eyed grass, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and wet meadows. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy clusters of flowers in late spring to early summer.

Campanula trachelioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
