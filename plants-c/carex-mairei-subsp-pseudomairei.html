Carex mairei subsp. pseudomairei

Carex mairei subsp.

pseudomairei, commonly known as the Maire's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of spikes of small, three-lobed flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
