Corythophora rimosa subsp. rimosa

Corythophora rimosa subsp.

rimosa, commonly known as the common sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils.

The plant features blue to purple flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are erect and branched.

This subspecies is characterized by its salt tolerance and ability to thrive in harsh maritime environments.
