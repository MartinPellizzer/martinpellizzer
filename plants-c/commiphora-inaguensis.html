Commiphora inaguensis

Commiphora inaguensis, commonly known as the Mexican myrrh tree, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh-producing plants. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. It produces small, yellowish flowers and is known for its aromatic resin, which has been used traditionally for medicinal and aromatic purposes.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.
