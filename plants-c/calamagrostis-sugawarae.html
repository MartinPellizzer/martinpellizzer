Calamagrostis sugawarae

Calamagrostis sugawarae, commonly known as the Japanese feathergrass, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, arching blades that form a delicate, feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full to partial sun. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late summer, which are often overlooked but play a role in its reproductive cycle.

Calamagrostis sugawarae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, low-maintenance turf-like landscapes.
