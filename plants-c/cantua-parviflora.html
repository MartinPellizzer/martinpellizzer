Cantua parviflora

Cantua parviflora, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hollyhock, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, pink to purple flowers. The plant features palmate leaves with a velvety texture and produces small, fluffy seed capsules that disperse its seeds by wind. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Cantua parviflora is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
