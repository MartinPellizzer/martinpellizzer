Commelina carnea

Commelina carnea, commonly known as the red spiderling, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinaceae family and is characterized by its striking red flowers with a distinctive spider-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

Commelina carnea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
