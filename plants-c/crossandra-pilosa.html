Crossandra pilosa

Crossandra pilosa, commonly known as the hairy crossandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from orange to red. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of tubular flowers that attract pollinators like birds and insects. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Crossandra pilosa is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for gardeners and landscapers.
