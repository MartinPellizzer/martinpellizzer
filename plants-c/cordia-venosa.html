Cordia venosa

Cordia venosa, commonly known as the red cedar or yellow cedar, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with smooth, grayish bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by orange or red fruits that contain several seeds.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its durable wood in traditional uses.
