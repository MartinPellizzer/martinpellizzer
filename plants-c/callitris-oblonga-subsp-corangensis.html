Callitris oblonga subsp. corangensis

Callitris oblonga subsp.

corangensis, commonly known as the Northern Territory cedar, is a hardy evergreen conifer native to northern Australia. It is characterized by its narrow, needle-like leaves and cone-bearing structure typical of the Cupressaceae family.

The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing remarkable drought tolerance. Its bark is rough and fissured, often exuding a resinous scent.

This subspecies plays a significant ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for native fauna.
