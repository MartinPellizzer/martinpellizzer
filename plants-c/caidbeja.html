Caidbeja

Caidbeja, commonly known as the common thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pink flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in various parts of Europe and Asia. Its flowering period occurs in late summer to early autumn, and it plays a role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
