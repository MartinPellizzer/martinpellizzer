Carex macrochaeta var. denticulata

Carex macrochaeta var. denticulata, commonly known as the toothed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like flower clusters. This variety is distinguished by its finely toothed margins on the leaves, giving it its specific epithet "denticulata.". It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
