Cordia ignea

Cordia ignea, commonly known as the fire tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its bright red flowers and smooth, glossy bark. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often eaten by birds and mammals. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Cordia ignea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
