Capsicum fuscoviolaceum

Capsicum fuscoviolaceum, commonly known as the purple husk pepper, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated fruit that turns a deep purple when mature. The plant produces small, clustered flowers and has a bushy growth habit with hairy stems and leaves. Its botanical classification places it within the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.

The fruit is typically harvested when it reaches its full color, and it is valued for its ornamental qualities as well as its mild flavor.
