Cattleya trianae var. quadricolor

Cattleya trianae var. quadricolor, commonly known as the four-colored Cattleya, is a rare and striking orchid native to Colombia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored petals that display a combination of red, yellow, and white, creating a visually stunning display. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are large and showy, with a complex structure that includes a prominent lip.

This variety is highly prized by orchid enthusiasts for its unique coloration and ornamental value.
