Clematis montana var. uniflora

Clematis montana var. uniflora, commonly known as the single-flowered montana clematis, is a vigorous climbing vine native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and it produces striking, showy flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant is characterized by its long, arching stems and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires support for its climbing habit.

Clematis montana var. uniflora is relatively hardy and can tolerate light frosts, making it suitable for temperate climates.
