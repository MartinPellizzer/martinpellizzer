Convolvulus elarishensis

Convolvulus elarishensis, commonly known as the Elarish bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes many species of flowering plants characterized by their trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to dry conditions. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, while its flowers are usually pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in disturbed areas.
