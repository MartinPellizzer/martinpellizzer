Ctenopteris atroviridis

Ctenopteris atroviridis, commonly known as the black-veined sea holly, is a species of fern native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery fronds with prominent, blackish veins. The plant thrives in humid, saline environments and is often found growing on rocky shores or in tidal zones. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a dense arrangement of small, pointed pinnae.

Ctenopteris atroviridis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in coastal gardens.
