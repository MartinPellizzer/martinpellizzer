Chionogentias lineata

Chionogentias lineata, commonly known as the silver line gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are typically arranged in dense clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is challenging to grow outside its native habitat.
