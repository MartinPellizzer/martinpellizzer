Cerasus laurocerasus var. camtschatica

Cerasus laurocerasus var. camtschatica, commonly known as the Caucasian cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Caucasus region.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, followed by bright red berries that are edible but often sour. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its hardy nature allows it to withstand cold climates, contributing to its widespread cultivation in temperate regions.
