Chrysophyllum torulosum

Chrysophyllum torulosum, commonly known as the African wax apple, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe, encased in a waxy coating. It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The tree is also valued for its ability to tolerate shade and its role in ecological restoration projects.
