Chromatotriccum trichostomum

Chromatotriccum trichostomum, commonly known as the goldenstar tamarisk, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and small, yellowish-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which are followed by small, woody fruits. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with limited water availability.

Chromatotriccum trichostomum plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various desert organisms.
