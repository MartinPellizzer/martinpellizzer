Cucurbita gracilior

Cucurbita gracilior, commonly known as the slender gourd, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its long, slender fruits that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces pale yellow flowers and has compound leaves with three to five lobes. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.
