Calamagrostis elata

Calamagrostis elata, commonly known as giant reed, is a tall, grass-like plant native to eastern Asia.

It features slender, arching stems that can grow up to 6 feet in height, with narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces plume-like panicles of small, feathery flowers in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found near water bodies such as ponds, streams, and marshes.

Calamagrostis elata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
