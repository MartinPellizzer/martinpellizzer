Cereus gloriosus

Cereus gloriosus, commonly known as the night blooming cereus, is a striking cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and ability to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces large, showy flowers that open at night, emitting a sweet fragrance. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink and can reach up to 12 inches in diameter.

Cereus gloriosus is well-suited for container gardening due to its compact growth habit and low maintenance requirements.
