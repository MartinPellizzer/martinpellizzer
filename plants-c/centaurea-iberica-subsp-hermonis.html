Centaurea iberica subsp. hermonis

Centaurea iberica subsp.

hermonis, commonly known as the Hermon centaury, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
