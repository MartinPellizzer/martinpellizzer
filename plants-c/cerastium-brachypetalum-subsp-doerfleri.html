Cerastium brachypetalum subsp. doerfleri

Cerastium brachypetalum subsp.

doerfleri, commonly known as the alpine mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial plant native to alpine regions of Europe. It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes many flowering plants with small, four-petaled flowers.

The subspecies is characterized by its compact rosette of hairy, spoon-shaped leaves and clusters of white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This plant is often found in mountainous areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens.
