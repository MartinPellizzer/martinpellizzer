Cornus sanguinea var. variegata

Cornus sanguinea var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated dogwood, is a shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant produces small, red berries in the autumn, which are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
