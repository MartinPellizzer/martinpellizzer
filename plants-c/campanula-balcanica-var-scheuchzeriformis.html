Campanula balcanica var. scheuchzeriformis

Campanula balcanica var. scheuchzeriformis, commonly known as the Balkan bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features striking blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lanceolate and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its beauty.
