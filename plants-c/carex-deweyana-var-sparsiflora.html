Carex deweyana var. sparsiflora

Carex deweyana var. sparsiflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or wetland habitats. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is characterized by its sparse flowering and compact growth habit.

This variety is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
