Cyathocalyx lucidus

Cyathocalyx lucidus, commonly known as the shining calyx plant, is a species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive calyx that remains prominent even after the flower has withered. The plant typically features small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels. Its leaves are usually ovate or lanceolate, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Cyathocalyx lucidus is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.
