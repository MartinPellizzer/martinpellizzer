Charidia

Charidia, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It consists of several species, most of which are native to southern Africa. The plants are herbaceous, with alternate, usually lobed leaves and yellow flowers arranged in a composite head. They typically grow in open, sunny areas and are often found in grasslands or rocky soils.

The genus is characterized by its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
