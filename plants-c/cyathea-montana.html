Cyathea montana

Cyathea montana, commonly known as the mountain tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant is evergreen and thrives in humid, shaded environments, typically found in forest understories. Its rhizomes are thick and fibrous, supporting its growth and aiding in water retention.

Cyathea montana plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
