Cissus flavens

Cissus flavens, commonly known as the golden cissus, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy fruits. Its leaves are succulent and can store water, which helps it survive in dry conditions.

Cissus flavens is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in various light conditions.
