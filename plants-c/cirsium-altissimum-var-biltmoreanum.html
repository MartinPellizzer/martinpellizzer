Cirsium altissimum var. biltmoreanum

Cirsium altissimum var. biltmoreanum, commonly known as the Biltmore thistle, is a striking perennial plant native to the Appalachian region of the United States.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems that can reach heights of up to 8 feet, making it one of the tallest thistle species. The plant features large, purple to pinkish purple flower heads that bloom in late summer, surrounded by numerous bracts. Its leaves are lance-shaped, deeply lobed, and covered in stiff, prickly hairs.

This variety is often found in moist, open woodlands and is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance.
