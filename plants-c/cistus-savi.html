Cistus savi

Cistus savi, commonly known as Savi's rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its aromatic, evergreen leaves and clusters of pink or white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is drought-tolerant.

Cistus savi is often used in xeriscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
