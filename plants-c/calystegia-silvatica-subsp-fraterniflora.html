Calystegia silvatica subsp. fraterniflora

Calystegia silvatica subsp.

fraterniflora, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are typically silver or gray-green in color.

The flowers are showy, trumpet-shaped, and usually appear in shades of pink to purple. It blooms during the summer months and produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, smooth seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.
