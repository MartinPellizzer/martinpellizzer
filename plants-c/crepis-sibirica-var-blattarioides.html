Crepis sibirica var. blattarioides

Crepis sibirica var. blattarioides, commonly known as Siberian mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Its leaves are hairy and heart-shaped, often forming a rosette near the ground.
