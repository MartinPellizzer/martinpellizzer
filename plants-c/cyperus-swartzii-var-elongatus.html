Cyperus swartzii var. elongatus

Cyperus swartzii var. elongatus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in wetlands, ditches, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and wetland restoration efforts.
