Cytisus laburnum subsp. jacquinianus

Cytisus laburnum subsp.

jacquinianus, commonly known as the yellow flag iris, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its bright yellow, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has slender, branching stems and pinnate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
