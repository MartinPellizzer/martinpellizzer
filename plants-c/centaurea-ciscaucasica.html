Centaurea ciscaucasica

Centaurea ciscaucasica, commonly known as the Caucasian knapweed, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showing adaptability to arid conditions. It produces vibrant purple to pink flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in temperate climates.
