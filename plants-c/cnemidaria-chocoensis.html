Cnemidaria chocoensis

Cnemidaria chocoensis, commonly known as the Chocoan spider orchid, is a rare and delicate orchid species native to the rainforests of Colombia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and intricate, spider-like flowers. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires specific soil conditions to flourish. Its flowers, which are typically pale green or yellowish, are adapted for pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat destruction, Cnemidaria chocoensis is considered endangered and is of great interest to botanists and conservationists.
