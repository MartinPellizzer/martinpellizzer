Celtis biondii var. insularis

Celtis biondii var. insularis, commonly known as the island hackberry, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and small, ovate leaves with a slightly wavy margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by small, round berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas and disturbed soils.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
