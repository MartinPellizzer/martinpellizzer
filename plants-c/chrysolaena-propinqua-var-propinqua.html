Chrysolaena propinqua var. propinqua

Chrysolaena propinqua var. propinqua, commonly known as the red-stemmed ironwood, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, woody structure and dark green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by spherical, red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
