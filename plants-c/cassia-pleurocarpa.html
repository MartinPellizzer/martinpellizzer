Cassia pleurocarpa

Cassia pleurocarpa, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a shrub native to Australia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain seeds covered in a silky, hairy coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry inland regions.

Cassia pleurocarpa is a significant species in Australian flora and is the national floral emblem of Australia.
