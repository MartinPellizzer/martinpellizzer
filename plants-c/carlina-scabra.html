Carlina scabra

Carlina scabra, commonly known as the rough hawk's-bill, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. Its leaves are rough and hairy, while its flowers consist of both ray and disk florets.

Carlina scabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
