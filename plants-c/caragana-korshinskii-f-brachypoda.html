Caragana korshinskii f. brachypoda

Caragana korshinskii f. brachypoda, commonly known as the shrubby peashrub, is a low-growing shrub native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, compound leaves that are typically less than 5 cm in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, followed by pea-like pods containing several seeds. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for erosion control.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological restoration and ornamental horticulture.
