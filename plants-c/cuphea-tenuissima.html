Cuphea tenuissima

Cuphea tenuissima, commonly known as the slender cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of pink, red, and white. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower.

Cuphea tenuissima is drought-tolerant and prefers well-draining soil.
