Cyperus margaritaceus var. prorepens

Cyperus margaritaceus var. prorepens, commonly known as the silver grass or white rush, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, arranged in spikelets. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in coastal areas, wetlands, and along waterways.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat for insects and stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
