Crotalaria montana var. angustifolia

Crotalaria montana var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain toxic seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and agricultural fields.
