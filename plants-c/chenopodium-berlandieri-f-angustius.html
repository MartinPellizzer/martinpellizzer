Chenopodium berlandieri f. angustius

Chenopodium berlandieri f. angustius, commonly known as narrowleaf goosefoot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, and its seeds are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and along riverbanks.

Due to its hardy nature, it is considered a common weed in agricultural settings.
