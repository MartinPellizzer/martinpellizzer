Caragana fruticosa var. multiflora

Caragana fruticosa var. multiflora, commonly known as the Siberian peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of Central Asia and Siberia.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-branched stems and small, compound leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, followed by pea-like pods containing several seeds. It is often used for erosion control due to its deep root system and hardy nature.

This species is also valued in traditional medicine for its various bioactive compounds.
