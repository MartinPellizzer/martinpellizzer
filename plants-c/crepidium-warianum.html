Crepidium warianum

Crepidium warianum, commonly known as the Wariang orchid, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid that typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a slender, wiry stem and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its leaves are linear and often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence.

The orchid is notable for its unique morphology and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
