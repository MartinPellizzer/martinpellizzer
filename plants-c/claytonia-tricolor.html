Claytonia tricolor

Claytonia tricolor, commonly known as the three-color spring beauty, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It features delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its flowers display three distinct colors—white, pink, and purple—hence its common name.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
