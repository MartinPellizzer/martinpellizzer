Citrus emarginata

Citrus emarginata, commonly known as the sour orange.

It is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its fragrant, dark green leaves and small, yellow-orange fruits. The plant produces a citrus fruit with a thick, leathery rind and a high concentration of essential oils. It is often cultivated for its aromatic properties and used in traditional medicine.

However, it is less commonly grown for its fruit compared to other citrus species due to its sour taste.
