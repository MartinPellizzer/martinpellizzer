Cucumis melo subvar. aegyptiacus

Cucumis melo subvar. aegyptiacus, commonly known as the Egyptian cucumber, is a botanical variety of the melon family characterized by its elongated, ribbed fruit.

It typically has a pale green rind with a slightly waxy texture and a mild, refreshing flavor. The plant produces yellow flowers and thrives in warm, sunny climates with well-drained soil. It is cultivated for its edible fruit, which is used in various culinary applications.

This subvariety is closely related to other melon species but is distinguished by its unique morphology and taste profile.
