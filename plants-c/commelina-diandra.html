Commelina diandra

Commelina diandra, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers, which bloom in the early morning and close by midday. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along riverbanks.

Commelina diandra is also valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
