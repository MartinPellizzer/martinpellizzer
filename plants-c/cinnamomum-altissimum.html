Cinnamomum altissimum

Cinnamomum altissimum, commonly known as the giant cinnamon tree, is a tall evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic bark and leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers and oval-shaped berries, which are used in traditional medicine. Its bark is highly valued for its essential oils and has been used for centuries in perfumery and culinary applications.

The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, well-drained soils.
