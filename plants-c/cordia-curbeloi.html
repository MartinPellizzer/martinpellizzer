Cordia curbeloi

Cordia curbeloi, commonly known as the Mexican white cedar, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Boraginaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
