Carduus velatus

Carduus velatus, commonly known as the woolly thistle, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hairs. The plant produces purple to pink flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its growth habit is typically upright, with a height ranging from 1 to 3 meters.

The species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
