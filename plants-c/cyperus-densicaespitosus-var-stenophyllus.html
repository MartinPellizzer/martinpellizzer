Cyperus densicaespitosus var. stenophyllus

Cyperus densicaespitosus var. stenophyllus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, triangular stems. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated, a common reproductive strategy in the sedge family. Its leaves are narrow and densely packed, giving it a dense, mat-like growth habit.

It thrives in wet or seasonally flooded soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies.
