Cabralea richardiana

Cabralea richardiana, commonly known as the Brazilian rosewood or cabralea.

It is a small evergreen tree native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil, characterized by its smooth, dark bark and dense canopy. The plant has simple, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and clustered in terminal panicles, followed by small, leathery fruits.

Cabralea richardiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
