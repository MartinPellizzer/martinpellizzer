Cineraria buchananii

Cineraria buchananii, commonly known as the Canary Island cineraria, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or subshrub, with hairy, gray-green leaves that are often covered in fine white hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The flowers bloom in spring, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat.
