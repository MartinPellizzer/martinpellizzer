Cassia trichothyrsus

Cassia trichothyrsus, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping clusters of yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is smooth and pale in color. Cassia trichothyrsus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
