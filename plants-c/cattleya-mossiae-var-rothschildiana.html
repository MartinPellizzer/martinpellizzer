Cattleya mossiae var. rothschildiana

Cattleya mossiae var. rothschildiana, commonly known as the Rothschild's Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid prized for its vibrant, fragrant flowers.

It features large, showy blooms with a distinctive coloration of orange and yellow, often with a contrasting purple lip. The plant is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid environments. It is a sympodial orchid, meaning it grows in a horizontal underground stem, producing new growths from the base.

This variety is popular among orchid enthusiasts for its beauty and relatively ease of cultivation.
