Chuquiraga oppositifolia var. macrocephala

Chuquiraga oppositifolia var. macrocephala, commonly known as the giant chuquiraga, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often reaching several inches in diameter. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
