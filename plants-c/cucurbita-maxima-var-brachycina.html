Cucurbita maxima var. brachycina

Cucurbita maxima var. brachycina, commonly known as the acorn squash, is a cultivar of the species Cucurbita maxima, which belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, acorn-shaped fruits that are typically deep orange in color. The plant is a vigorous vine that produces large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers. The fruit is rich in nutrients, including vitamins A and C, and is widely used in culinary applications.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a long growing season to mature.
