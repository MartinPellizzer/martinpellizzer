Cucumis parvifolius

Cucumis parvifolius, commonly known as the small-leaf cucumber, is a species of flowering plant in the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa and Asia. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. Its leaves are ovate with a slightly hairy surface, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

This species is often cultivated for its unique flavor and use in local cuisines.
