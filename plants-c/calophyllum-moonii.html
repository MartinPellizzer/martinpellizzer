Calophyllum moonii

Calophyllum moonii, commonly known as the moonseed tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing flat, kidney-shaped seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and antispasmodic properties.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its durable timber.
