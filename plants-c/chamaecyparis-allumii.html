Chamaecyparis allumii

Chamaecyparis allumii, commonly known as the silver fir or mountain cedar, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and its distinctive silvery-blue foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant has scale-like leaves that are closely arranged on the shoots, and it produces small, cone-like structures that contain the seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
