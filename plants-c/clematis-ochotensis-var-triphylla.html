Clematis ochotensis var. triphylla

Clematis ochotensis var. triphylla, commonly known as the triphylla clematis, is a flowering plant native to the Far East, particularly found in regions of Russia and China.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its climbing habit and showy, typically blue or purple flowers. The plant features trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires support as it climbs, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.

Its blooming period usually occurs in late spring to early summer, adding vibrant color to gardens and natural landscapes.
