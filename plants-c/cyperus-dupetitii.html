Cyperus dupetitii

Cyperus dupetitii, commonly known as the swamp cyperus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family, Cyperaceae, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, erect stems. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, a common feature among cyperaceae species.

Cyperus dupetitii is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.
