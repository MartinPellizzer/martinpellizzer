Crocus puringii

Crocus puringii, commonly known as the Puringii crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a corm.

Crocus puringii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its early-season blooms.
