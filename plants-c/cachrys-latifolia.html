Cachrys latifolia

Cachrys latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf tamarisk, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and woody stems that often form a low, spreading mound. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.

Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing a distinctive appearance in the landscape.
