Cacalia brasiliana

Cacalia brasiliana, commonly known as Brazilian blueweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, blue to purple composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often hairy.

Cacalia brasiliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
