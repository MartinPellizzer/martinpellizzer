Cereus polyachaetus

Cereus polyachaetus, commonly known as the moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and striking red or purple flowers that bloom at night. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in well-draining soil. It requires full sun and minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and nocturnal blooming make it a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
