Cuphea orizabensis

Cuphea orizabensis, commonly known as the Mexican whorled cuphea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its distinctive whorled arrangement of leaves and showy, tubular flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and orange. The plant typically grows as an annual or perennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
