Cousinia sintenisii

Cousinia sintenisii, commonly known as the Kashmiri cudweed, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features narrow, silver-gray leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer. It is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Cousinia sintenisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing mountainous soils.
