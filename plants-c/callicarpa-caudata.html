Callicarpa caudata

Callicarpa caudata, commonly known as the red beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white flowers that develop into bright red, berry-like fruits. The plant is noted for its ornamental value, particularly due to the striking color of its fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Callicarpa caudata is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and seasonal interest.
