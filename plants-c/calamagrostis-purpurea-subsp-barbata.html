Calamagrostis purpurea subsp. barbata

Calamagrostis purpurea subsp.

barbata, commonly known as purple moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to the British Isles and parts of Europe. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and purple-tinted inflorescences that appear in late summer.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, bogs, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting wetland biodiversity.
