Clematis douglasii var. bigelovii

Clematis douglasii var. bigelovii, commonly known as the Bigelow's clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the western United States.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically white to pale blue, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is valued for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens and landscapes for its attractive blooms and vigorous climbing habit.
