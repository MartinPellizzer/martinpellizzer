Cucubalus reflexus

Cucubalus reflexus, commonly known as the reflexed cucumber or wild cucumber, is a herbaceous vine native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cucurbitaceae, which includes other cucurbit plants like cucumbers and pumpkins. The plant features tendrils that help it climb and spread along supports, and its leaves are typically lobed with a rough texture. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female blooms, and the fruit is a spiny, elongated capsule that matures to a yellowish or orange color.

The plant is often found in disturbed habitats and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
