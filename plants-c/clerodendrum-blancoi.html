Clerodendrum blancoi

Clerodendrum blancoi, commonly known as the White Clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a distinctive red or purple calyx. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowering period usually occurs during the wet season, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The plant contains various chemical compounds, some of which have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
