Carica integrifolia

Carica integrifolia, commonly known as the papaya tree, is a tropical plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are entire, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces yellow-orange fruits that are rich in vitamins and enzymes like papain. Its flowers are typically green with a distinctive crown of red stamens.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is widely cultivated for its edible fruit.
