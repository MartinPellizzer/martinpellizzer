Candollea heterophylla

Candollea heterophylla, commonly known as the variable leaf fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, variable leaf morphology, which can range from filamentous to broad and strap-like. The plant typically grows in shallow, freshwater habitats such as ponds and streams. Its fronds are usually dark green and can vary in shape depending on environmental conditions.

Candollea heterophylla plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and small aquatic life.
