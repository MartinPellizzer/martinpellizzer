Cardia ciliata

Cardia ciliata, commonly known as the silky cardia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its finely hairy, silvery-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Cardia ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
