Carex bella-villa

Carex bella-villa, commonly known as the velvetleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces distinctive, spiky seed heads.

Carex bella-villa is often used in ornamental gardening for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
