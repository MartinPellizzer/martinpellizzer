Caroxylon arabicum

Caroxylon arabicum, commonly known as the Arabian date palm, is a flowering plant native to the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, fan-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. Its fruit, which is a drupe, is edible and has been a vital food source for humans and animals for centuries.

The Arabian date palm is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water to thrive.
