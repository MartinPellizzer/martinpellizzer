Capparis tomentosa

Capparis tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy caper, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in coastal or rocky habitats. The plant is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

The leaves are usually ovate or elliptic, with a rough, textured surface.
