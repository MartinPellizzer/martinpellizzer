Cyperus teysmannii

Cyperus teysmannii, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow leaves. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and wetlands. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation in suitable habitats.
