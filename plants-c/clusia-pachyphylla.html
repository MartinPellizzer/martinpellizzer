Clusia pachyphylla

Clusia pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved glorybower, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often dark green and glossy, providing a striking visual appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. It is well adapted to humid environments and can tolerate partial shade to full sun.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
