Crataegus chateaugayensis

Crataegus chateaugayensis, commonly known as the Chateaugay hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves, and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, red berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the understory vegetation in moist, woodlands and riparian habitats.
