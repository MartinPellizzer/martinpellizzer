Cyclophorus pannosus

Cyclophorus pannosus, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in terminal spikes. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks.

Cyclophorus pannosus is known for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive species in certain regions.
