Calamus flagellum var. karinensis

Calamus flagellum var. karinensis, commonly known as the Karin's rattan, is a climbing palm native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its long, slender stems and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and sustainable harvesting, it is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance and practical uses.
