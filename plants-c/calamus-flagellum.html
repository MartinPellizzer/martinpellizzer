Calamus flagellum

Calamus flagellum, commonly known as the rattan cane, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family, which includes palms, and is characterized by its long, flexible stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers and develops feather-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in length. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural fibers.

Calamus flagellum is valued for its durability and versatility in both decorative and practical applications.
