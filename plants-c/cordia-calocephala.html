Cordia calocephala

Cordia calocephala, commonly known as the red-flowered cordia, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and striking red or pink flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, fleshy fruits that are often eaten by birds and other animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Cordia calocephala is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
