Cyperus michelianus var. pacificus

Cyperus michelianus var. pacificus, commonly known as the Pacific flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Pacific Northwest.

It is a perennial herb with narrow, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist, sandy soils and is often found in tidal marshes and estuaries.

Its adaptability to brackish conditions makes it an important component of coastal wetland ecosystems.
