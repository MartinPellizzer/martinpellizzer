Cheirinia × helvetica

Cheirinia × helvetica, commonly known as the hybrid carnation, is a cultivated hybrid resulting from the cross between Cheirinia alpina and Cheirinia scaposa.

It is a member of the Caryophyllaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and prefers well-drained soil in full sun. It is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.

The hybrid is valued for its adaptability and ability to produce continuous blooms throughout the growing season.
