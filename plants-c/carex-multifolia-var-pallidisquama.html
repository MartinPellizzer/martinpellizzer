Carex multifolia var. pallidisquama

Carex multifolia var. pallidisquama, commonly known as the pale-squamate sedge, is a perennial plant native to the temperate regions of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

This variety is noted for its distinctive pale, scaly bracts that give it its common name.
