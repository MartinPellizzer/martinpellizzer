Carex siccata var. enervis

Carex siccata var. enervis, commonly known as the dry sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers in spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is notable for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
