Chamaefistula caudata

Chamaefistula caudata, commonly known as the Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant in temperate climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy flowers and pleasant fragrance.
