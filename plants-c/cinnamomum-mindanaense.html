Cinnamomum mindanaense

Cinnamomum mindanaense, commonly known as Mindanao cinnamon, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, dark red berries containing winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Its bark and leaves are used in traditional medicine and as a source of aromatic oils.

This species is valued for its unique fragrance and is often cultivated for both ornamental and practical purposes.
