Crateva suaresensis

Crateva suaresensis, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or yellow starfruit, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow, star-shaped fruits. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes and produces small, pale yellow flowers. The fruits are edible, though they are less commonly consumed compared to the more familiar *Crateva religiosa*.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes.
