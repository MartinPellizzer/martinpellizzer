Chiclea excelsa

Chiclea excelsa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety hair, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

Chiclea excelsa is often found in disturbed habitats and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
