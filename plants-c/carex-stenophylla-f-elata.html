Carex stenophylla f. elata

Carex stenophylla f. elata, commonly known as the tall narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. This subspecies is often found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous growth habit and a preference for temperate climates.

The plant plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
