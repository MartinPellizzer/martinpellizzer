Centaurea boissieri subsp. paui

Centaurea boissieri subsp.

paui, commonly known as the Turkish knapweed, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It features upright, sparsely branched stems that can grow up to 1 meter in height.

The plant produces dense, fluffy clusters of pink to reddish-purple flowers, which bloom from late spring to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered with fine hairs.

It is typically found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
