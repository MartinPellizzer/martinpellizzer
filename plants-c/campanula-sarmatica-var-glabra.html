Campanula sarmatica var. glabra

Campanula sarmatica var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its smooth, hairless stems and clusters of bell-shaped blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and woodland edges, thriving in partial to full shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly waxy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
