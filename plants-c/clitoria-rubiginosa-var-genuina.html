Clitoria rubiginosa var. genuina

Clitoria rubiginosa var. genuina, commonly known as the red bower vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple to red flowers that bloom in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are often hairy and twining. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The genus Clitoria is known for its showy, butterfly-like flowers, and this variety is no exception.
