Caralluma adscendens var. adscendens

Caralluma adscendens var. adscendens, commonly known as the climbing nightshade or desert nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a succulent shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, exhibiting a creeping or climbing growth habit. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often blooming during the cooler months. Its leaves are fleshy and succulent, adapted to store water in its native dry environment.

The plant is also known for its alkaloid content, which has been traditionally used in folk medicine.
