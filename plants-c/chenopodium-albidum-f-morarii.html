Chenopodium albidum f. morarii

Chenopodium albidum f. morarii, commonly known as the pale goosefoot, is a variant of the common goosefoot, characterized by its distinctive pale green foliage.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that typically grows in disturbed soils, such as along roadsides or in cultivated fields. The leaves are usually alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and covered with a fine layer of white pubescence. It produces small, greenish flowers in compact clusters, and its seeds are minute, dry, and wind-dispersed.

This plant is often considered a weed but has some historical use in traditional medicine.
