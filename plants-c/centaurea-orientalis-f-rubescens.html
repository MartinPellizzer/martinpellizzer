Centaurea orientalis f. rubescens

Centaurea orientalis f. rubescens, commonly known as the oriental knapweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its striking red-orange flower heads are surrounded by pale blue to purple bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in disturbed environments.
