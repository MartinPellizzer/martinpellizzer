Cucumis sativus convar. excellens

Cucumis sativus convar. excellens, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a botanical variant of the common cucumber.

It is characterized by its elongated, thin, and often ribbed fruit, which is typically harvested when immature for optimal flavor and texture. The plant is an annual vine that thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow and monoecious, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

This variety is valued for its crisp, mild flavor and is commonly used in fresh salads and pickling.
