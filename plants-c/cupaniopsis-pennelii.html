Cupaniopsis pennelii

Cupaniopsis pennelii, commonly known as the coastal hakea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of South Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and cylindrical, woody stems that are often covered in a thick layer of bark. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, typically blooming in spring. It is well adapted to sandy soils and salty sea air, making it a hardy species suited to coastal environments.

Cupaniopsis pennelii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing dunes.
