Croton churumayensis

Croton churumayensis, commonly known as the Churumayensis Croton, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is known for its diverse and often strikingly ornamental species. The plant features palmate leaves with a vibrant green color and can produce small, inconspicuous flowers followed by capsule fruits. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
