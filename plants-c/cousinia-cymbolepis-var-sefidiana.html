Cousinia cymbolepis var. sefidiana

Cousinia cymbolepis var. sefidiana, commonly known as the Sefidiana Goatsbeard, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
