Cereus lindbergianus

Cereus lindbergianus, commonly known as the Lindberg's moon cactus, is a species of cactus native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stem and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom at night. The plant is a hybrid between a Gymnocalycium and a Cereus species, resulting in a unique appearance. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its striking floral display makes it a popular choice for collectors and gardeners.
