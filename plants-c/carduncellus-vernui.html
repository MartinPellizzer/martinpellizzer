Carduncellus vernui

Carduncellus vernui, commonly known as the common cardoon.

It is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in composite flower heads, which are typical of the daisy family. It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, sunny conditions.

Its fibrous stalks have been historically used for making rope and paper.
