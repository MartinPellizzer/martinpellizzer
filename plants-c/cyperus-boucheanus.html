Cyperus boucheanus

Cyperus boucheanus, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces small, three-angled fruits.

Cyperus boucheanus is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
