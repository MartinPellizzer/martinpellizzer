Caragana alaica

Caragana alaica, commonly known as the Siberian peashrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching structure and small, compound leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, followed by pea-like pods containing edible seeds. It is often used for erosion control due to its deep root system and hardy nature.

Caragana alaica thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in steppe and semi-desert regions.
