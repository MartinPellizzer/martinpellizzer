Centropogon ferrugineus f. subaequus

Centropogon ferrugineus f. subaequus, commonly known as the redflower false dandelion, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a reddish or purplish hue along the midrib, and its flowers are typically yellow with reddish or orange markings. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and scrublands.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated for its distinctive appearance.
