Catasetum landsbergii

Catasetum landsbergii, commonly known as the Landsberg's orchid, is a species native to Central and South America.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its erect, branched stems and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces distinctive, often yellow or greenish flowers with specialized structures called "labellum" that aid in pollination. Its growth habit involves a pseudobulb system that stores water and nutrients.

Catasetum landsbergii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and unique floral morphology.
