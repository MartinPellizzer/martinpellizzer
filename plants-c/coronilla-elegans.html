Coronilla elegans

Coronilla elegans, commonly known as the elegant crownvetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, pink to violet flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it useful in ecological restoration projects. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and disturbed areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate regions.
