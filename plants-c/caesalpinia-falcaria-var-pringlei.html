Caesalpinia falcaria var. pringlei

Caesalpinia falcaria var. pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's candlewood, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, zigzagging branches and compound leaves. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers that are clustered in racemes, often attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its seed pods are elongated and cylindrical, containing several seeds that are dispersed by wind or animals.

The plant is adapted to dry and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
