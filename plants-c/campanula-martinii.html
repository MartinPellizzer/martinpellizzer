Campanula martinii

Campanula martinii, commonly known as the Martin's bell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a violet or purple throat. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and hairy, contributing to its ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Campanula martinii is a perennial species that blooms in late spring to early summer, making it a notable ornamental plant in gardens and natural landscapes.
