Cyanopogon

Cyanopogon, commonly known as bluegrass, is a genus of grasses belonging to the Poaceae family.

These plants are characterized by their fine, tufted foliage and often produce small, spike-like inflorescences. Cyanopogon species are typically found in temperate and subtropical regions, often growing in open fields or along roadsides. They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes used in landscaping.

Additionally, some species are used in traditional medicine or as forage for livestock.
