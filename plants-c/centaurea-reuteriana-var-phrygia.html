Centaurea reuteriana var. phrygia

Centaurea reuteriana var. phrygia, commonly known as the Phrygian centaurea, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers and spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
