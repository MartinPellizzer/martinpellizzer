Catunaregam torulosa var. oblongifolia

Catunaregam torulosa var. oblongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf catunaregam, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically followed by elliptical, woody fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The leaves are used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
