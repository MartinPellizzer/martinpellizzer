Cornus mascula var. japonica

Cornus mascula var. japonica, commonly known as Japanese dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, white bracts that surround small yellowish flowers, typically appearing in late spring. The plant produces red berries that are edible for birds but not for humans. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly serrated margin.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
