Cyperus glaziovianus

Cyperus glaziovianus, commonly known as the Glaziov's flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in disturbed soils near water bodies.

Cyperus glaziovianus is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
