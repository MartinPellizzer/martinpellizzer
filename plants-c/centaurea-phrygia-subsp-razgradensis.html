Centaurea phrygia subsp. razgradensis

Centaurea phrygia subsp.

razgradensis, commonly known as the Razgrad centaury, is a perennial herb native to southeastern Europe. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and often covered with fine hairs, while its stems are upright and branching.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its limited distribution and unique ecological adaptations.
