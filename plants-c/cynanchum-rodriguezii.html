Cynanchum rodriguezii

Cynanchum rodriguezii, commonly known as the Mexican milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family, which includes many milkweeds known for their unique floral structures. The plant features slender, hairy stems and produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers with distinctive horns. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a somewhat succulent texture.

Cynanchum rodriguezii is also noted for containing latex, which can be toxic to some animals and humans.
