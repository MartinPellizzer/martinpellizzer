Coelogyne lactea

Coelogyne lactea, commonly known as the milk orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking white to pale yellow flowers with a distinctive cream-colored lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or lithophyte, attaching itself to trees or rocks in its natural habitat. Its foliage consists of long, strap-like leaves that are often clustered at the base.

Coelogyne lactea is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its beauty and fragrance.
