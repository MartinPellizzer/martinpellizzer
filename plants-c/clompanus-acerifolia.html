Clompanus acerifolia

Clompanus acerifolia, commonly known as the maple-leaved clompanus, is a unique flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that resemble those of maple trees, hence its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its distinct foliage and seasonal color changes, it is often cultivated for both its aesthetic appeal and ecological value.
