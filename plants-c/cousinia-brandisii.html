Cousinia brandisii

Cousinia brandisii, commonly known as the Brandis' cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and hairy foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. It has small, lance-shaped leaves that help reduce water loss, and its flowering period occurs during the summer months.

Cousinia brandisii is often found in alpine or steppe regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
