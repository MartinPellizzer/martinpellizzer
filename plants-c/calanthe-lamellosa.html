Calanthe lamellosa

Calanthe lamellosa, commonly known as the veined calanthe, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its elegant, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers with prominent veining. It thrives in shaded, humid environments and is often found in forest understories.

Calanthe lamellosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
