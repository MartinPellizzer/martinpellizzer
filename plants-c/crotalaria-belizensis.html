Crotalaria belizensis

Crotalaria belizensis, commonly known as the Belize morning glory, is a flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are showy, with five petals and a spurred structure, often in shades of yellow or orange.

It is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
