Carex interior

Carex interior, commonly known as the interior sedge, is a perennial plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks. It produces small, clustered flower heads that bloom in early summer.

Carex interior is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
