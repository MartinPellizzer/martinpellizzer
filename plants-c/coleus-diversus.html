Coleus diversus

Coleus diversus, commonly known as the rainbow coleus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the mint family (Lamiaceae) and is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored leaves that display shades of red, purple, pink, and green. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale purple, arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking foliage.

Coleus diversus prefers well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking colorful, low-maintenance plants.
