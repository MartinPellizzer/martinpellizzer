Cyathea phanerophlebia

Cyathea phanerophlebia, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a distinctive root system that forms a dense network, aiding in water absorption and stability. Its fronds can grow up to several meters in length, contributing to its imposing appearance.

Cyathea phanerophlebia plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
