Ctenitis hirsutosetosa

Ctenitis hirsutosetosa, commonly known as the hairy-setose ctenitis, is a species of marine algae belonging to the family Cteniaceae.

It is characterized by its branching, filamentous structure and the presence of small, spiny projections along its thallus. The plant thrives in shallow, coastal waters where it attaches to rocky substrates. Its reproductive structures, known as gametangia, are typically located at the tips of its branches.

Ctenitis hirsutosetosa plays a role in marine ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
