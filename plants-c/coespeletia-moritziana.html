Coespeletia moritziana

Coespeletia moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's cholla, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that store water, adapting to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its root system is shallow and widespread, allowing it to efficiently absorb moisture from the dry soil.

Coespeletia moritziana is well-suited to rocky and sandy soils, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
