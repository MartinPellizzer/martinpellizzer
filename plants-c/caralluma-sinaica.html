Caralluma sinaica

Caralluma sinaica, commonly known as the Sinai devil's backbone, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Nyctaginaceae and is characterized by its spiny, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by red, fleshy fruits containing seeds. Its leaves are reduced to small, scale-like structures, reducing water loss through transpiration.

Caralluma sinaica is also valued for its traditional use in herbal medicine, particularly for its purported appetite-suppressing properties.
