Clermontia reticulata f. pilifera

Clermontia reticulata f. pilifera, commonly known as the hairy nettle fern, is a unique fern species characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features finely divided, often hairy leaves that give it a soft, textured appearance. The plant is native to specific regions of Hawaii, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant plant.
