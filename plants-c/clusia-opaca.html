Clusia opaca

Clusia opaca, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and northern South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by orange-red berries. Clusia opaca is highly adaptable, thriving in a variety of soil types and light conditions, making it a popular ornamental plant.

It is also valued for its ability to tolerate urban environments and air pollution.
