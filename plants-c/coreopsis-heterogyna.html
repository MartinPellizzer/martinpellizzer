Coreopsis heterogyna

Coreopsis heterogyna, commonly known as the purpletop coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 2 to 3 feet and has slender, branched stems. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and the flowers are composed of both ray and disk florets.

Coreopsis heterogyna is often found in open woods, prairies, and along roadsides, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
