Calonemorchis viridescens

Calonemorchis viridescens, commonly known as the green orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green. The plant produces intricate, fragrant flowers that are usually pale green to yellow in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic appeal.
