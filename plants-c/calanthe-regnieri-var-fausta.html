Calanthe regnieri var. fausta

Calanthe regnieri var. fausta, commonly known as the golden calanthe, is a striking orchid native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It features showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern. It thrives in cool, humid environments with partial shade and well-draining soil.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by orchid enthusiasts.
