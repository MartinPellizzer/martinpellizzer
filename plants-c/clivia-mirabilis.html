Clivia mirabilis

Clivia mirabilis, commonly known as the Natal lily, is a perennial evergreen plant native to South Africa.

It features strap-shaped, dark green leaves that form a dense rosette near the ground. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers in shades of red, orange, or yellow, typically blooming in late winter to early spring. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Clivia mirabilis is relatively low maintenance and thrives in moderate temperatures with consistent moisture.
