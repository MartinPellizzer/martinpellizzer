Cucurbita citrullus var. grisea

Cucurbita citrullus var. grisea, commonly known as the gray bottle gourd, is a variant of the common bottle gourd.

It is characterized by its pale green to grayish skin and elongated, narrow shape. This plant is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes cucumbers, pumpkins, and squash. It typically grows as a trailing vine with palmate leaves and produces small, pale yellow flowers.

The fruit is often used in traditional cuisines for its mild, nutty flavor and versatility in cooking.
