Carex curaica var. armena

Carex curaica var. armena, commonly known as Armenian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and other grass-like plants. The plant has triangular, grass-like stems and produces small, clustered flowers in spikelets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation.
