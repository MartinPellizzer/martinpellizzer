Chloris argentina

Chloris argentina, commonly known as Argentine grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Chloris argentina is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil.
