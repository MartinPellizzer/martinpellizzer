Ceropegia robinsonii

Ceropegia robinsonii, commonly known as the Blue Butterfly Plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features delicate, fleshy stems that often trail or climb, and its most distinctive characteristic is its showy, butterfly-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like birds and insects. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect light to flourish.

Ceropegia robinsonii is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
