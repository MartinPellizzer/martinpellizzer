Cardamine pakistanica

Cardamine pakistanica, commonly known as the Pakistan watercress, is a member of the Ranunculaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves and small, white flowers. Native to the mountainous regions of Pakistan, it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant has a short growing season and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in high-altitude ecosystems.
