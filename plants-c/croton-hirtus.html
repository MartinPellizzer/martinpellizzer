Croton hirtus

Croton hirtus, commonly known as the hairy croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by distinctive, spiky fruit capsules. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Due to its unique appearance, it is a popular choice for ornamental gardens and indoor plant collections.
