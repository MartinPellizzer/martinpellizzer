Cyperus ferrugineus var. baronii

Cyperus ferrugineus var. baronii, commonly known as the Baron's flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like stems with small, scale-like leaves that are often covered in a rusty-colored bloom. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family.

It is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in moist conditions.
