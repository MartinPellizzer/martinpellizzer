Crataegus subviridis

Crataegus subviridis, commonly known as the greenleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its bright green leaves, which are oval-shaped and often have a slight serration along the edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in the spring, followed by red berries in the fall. Crataegus subviridis typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
