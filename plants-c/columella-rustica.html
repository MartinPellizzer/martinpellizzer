Columella rustica

Columella rustica, commonly known as the rusty columnar plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that can grow up to several meters in height, giving it a columnar appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, often with a reddish or rusty coloration on the underside.

Columella rustica is native to regions of Europe and Asia and is often found in grassy areas or along roadsides.
