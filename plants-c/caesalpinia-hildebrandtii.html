Caesalpinia hildebrandtii

Caesalpinia hildebrandtii, commonly known as the flame tree or hildebrandt's glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its compound leaves and striking red or orange-pink inflorescences. The plant produces elongated pods that contain several seeds, and it is adapted to thrive in dry, sunny environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
