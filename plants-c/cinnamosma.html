Cinnamosma

Cinnamosma, commonly known as the cinnamon tree or Cinnamosma grandis, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like laurel and avocado. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves with a fragrant scent. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, and it produces dark, oily seeds.

The tree is valued for its aromatic properties and is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
