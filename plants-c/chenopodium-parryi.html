Chenopodium parryi

Chenopodium parryi, commonly known as Parry's goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine by indigenous peoples for their purported health benefits.

Chenopodium parryi plays a role in supporting local ecosystems by providing food and habitat for various insects and small animals.
