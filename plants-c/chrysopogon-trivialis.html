Chrysopogon trivialis

Chrysopogon trivialis, commonly known as slender grass or small-stemmed grass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, which are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are arranged in a panicle, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Chrysopogon trivialis is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native range.
