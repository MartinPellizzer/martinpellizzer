Cnicus transsylvanicus

Cnicus transsylvanicus, commonly known as the Transylvanian cinquefoil, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of leaves and bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are alternate, with a rough texture and serrated edges, while its flowers consist of five petals.

Cnicus transsylvanicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
