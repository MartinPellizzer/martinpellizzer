Calceolaria discolor

Calceolaria discolor, commonly known as the silver calceolaria, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, tubular flowers that are often tinged with silver or white hues. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down.

Calceolaria discolor is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
