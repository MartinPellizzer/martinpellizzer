Crepidaria padifolia

Crepidaria padifolia, commonly known as the golden sandmat, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaranthaceae and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit. The plant features narrow, fleshy leaves that are typically covered in a fine layer of white, powdery wax, which helps reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually green or yellow and appear in clusters.

Crepidaria padifolia is well adapted to sandy soils and can thrive in full sun, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
