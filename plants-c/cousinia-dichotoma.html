Cousinia dichotoma

Cousinia dichotoma, commonly known as the two-branched globemallow, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its compact, spherical growth habit and grayish-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and high temperatures.

Cousinia dichotoma is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
