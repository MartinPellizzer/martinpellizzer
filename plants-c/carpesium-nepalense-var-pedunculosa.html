Carpesium nepalense var. pedunculosa

Carpesium nepalense var. pedunculosa, commonly known as the Himalayan thornapple, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Nepal and northern India.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and lobed leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by orange to red berries. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
