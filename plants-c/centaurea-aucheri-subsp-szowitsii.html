Centaurea aucheri subsp. szowitsii

Centaurea aucheri subsp.

szowitsii, commonly known as the Szowits' cornflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers and upright growth habit.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
