Cynanchum rehmannii

Cynanchum rehmannii, commonly known as Chinese milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by follicles containing toxic seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
