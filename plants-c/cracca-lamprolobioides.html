Cracca lamprolobioides

Cracca lamprolobioides, commonly known as the shining cracca, is a member of the Rubiaceae family, native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elliptical berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
