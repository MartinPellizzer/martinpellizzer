Cassia crista var. oligophylla

Cassia crista var. oligophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leafed flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow-orange inflorescences. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its flowers are often clustered in racemes, and the plant is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The seeds are enclosed in elongated pods and can be used in traditional medicine in some regions.
