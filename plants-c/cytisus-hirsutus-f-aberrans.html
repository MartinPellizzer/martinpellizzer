Cytisus hirsutus f. aberrans

Cytisus hirsutus f. aberrans, commonly known as the hairy broom or a variant of the common broom, is a shrub native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and legume fruits. The plant features dense, upright growth with yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its foliage is typically covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This form is considered a natural variant of the species, exhibiting slight morphological differences from the typical Cytisus hirsutus.
