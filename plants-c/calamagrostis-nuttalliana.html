Calamagrostis nuttalliana

Calamagrostis nuttalliana, commonly known as Nuttall's reedgrass, is a tall, perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, upright culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are usually golden to brown in color.

Calamagrostis nuttalliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
