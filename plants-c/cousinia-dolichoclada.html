Cousinia dolichoclada

Cousinia dolichoclada, commonly known as the long-clawed cousinia, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant has hairy, succulent leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in mountainous areas with low precipitation.

Cousinia dolichoclada is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils.
