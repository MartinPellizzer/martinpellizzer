Cousinia orthoclada

Cousinia orthoclada, commonly known as the narrowleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer.

Cousinia orthoclada is often found in disturbed areas and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
