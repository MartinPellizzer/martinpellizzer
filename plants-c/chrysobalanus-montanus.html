Chrysobalanus montanus

Chrysobalanus montanus, commonly known as the mountain wax tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Chrysobalanaceae and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The tree produces edible fruits that are encased in a hard, woody shell, which open to reveal a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
