Carex decaulescens subsp. brunneola

Carex decaulescens subsp.

brunneola, commonly known as the brown-stem sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.

The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are grass-like, with a cylindrical stem that is often reddish-brown in color, giving it its common name.

The inflorescence consists of a cluster of spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated.
