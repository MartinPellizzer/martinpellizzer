Chiococca brachiata var. grandifolia

Chiococca brachiata var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-flowered chiococca, is a climbing shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its woody stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, violet-blue flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.
