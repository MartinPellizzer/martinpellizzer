Chaenostoma integrifolium

Chaenostoma integrifolium, commonly known as the smoothleaf water plantain, is a herbaceous aquatic plant native to North America.

It typically grows in shallow freshwater environments such as ponds, lakes, and slow-moving streams. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are smooth along their edges and often float on the water surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense inflorescences, with petals that are usually white or pale pink.

The plant plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and amphibians.
