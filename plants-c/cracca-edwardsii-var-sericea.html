Cracca edwardsii var. sericea

Cracca edwardsii var. sericea, commonly known as the silky cracca, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The leaves are typically narrow and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving the plant its "silky" appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or rocky habitats.

This variant is distinguished by its unique foliage and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
