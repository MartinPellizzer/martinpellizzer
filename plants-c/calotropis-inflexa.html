Calotropis inflexa

Calotropis inflexa, commonly known as the Indian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Asia.

It is a shrub that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with five petals, which are pollinated by insects. Its latex sap contains toxic compounds, making it a notable species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.

Calotropis inflexa is also known for its ability to thrive in arid and semi-arid environments.
