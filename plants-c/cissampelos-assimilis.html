Cissampelos assimilis

Cissampelos assimilis, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing perennial vine native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces distinctive, elongated, green fruits that turn red when mature, containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Menispermaceae and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional medicinal uses.
