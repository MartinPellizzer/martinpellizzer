Colutea istria var. sericea

Colutea istria var. sericea, commonly known as the silverleaf sweetclover, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that are covered in a silvery, fuzzy layer of hair, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in upright clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Colutea istria var. sericea is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.
