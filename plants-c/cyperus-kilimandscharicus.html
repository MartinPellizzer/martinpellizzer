Cyperus kilimandscharicus

Cyperus kilimandscharicus, commonly known as the African sedge, is a flowering plant native to the grasslands of East Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in savanna and wetland habitats. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach up to 1.5 meters in height, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Cyperus kilimandscharicus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its upright, architectural form.
