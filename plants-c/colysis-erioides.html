Colysis erioides

Colysis erioides, commonly known as the wooly everlasting, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky environments. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. It has a compact growth habit and is often used in rock gardens due to its ornamental value.

Colysis erioides is adapted to arid conditions, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.
