Calliandra glyphoxylon

Calliandra glyphoxylon, commonly known as the pink shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to red floral clusters that bloom in spring. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, making it beneficial for ecological gardens.
