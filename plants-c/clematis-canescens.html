Clematis canescens

Clematis canescens, commonly known as the silky clematis, is a climbing vine native to eastern North America.

It features slender, wiry stems that can grow up to 15 feet in length, often twining around supports. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white to pale blue, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Clematis canescens thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental vines.
