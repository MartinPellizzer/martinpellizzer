Cyperus laevigatus var. pallae

Cyperus laevigatus var. pallae, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist to saturated soil conditions. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like structures that produce wind-pollinated flowers.

Cyperus laevigatus var. pallae is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
