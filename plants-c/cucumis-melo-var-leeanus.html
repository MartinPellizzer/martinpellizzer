Cucumis melo var. leeanus

Cucumis melo var. leeanus, commonly known as the Persian cucumber, is a variety of melon native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender, and often ribbed fruits with a pale green skin. The plant produces small, yellow flowers and has a trailing growth habit, making it suitable for cultivation in warm climates. Its fruits are typically harvested when mature, and they are prized for their crisp texture and mild, sweet flavor.

This variety is often used in both fresh consumption and culinary preparations.
