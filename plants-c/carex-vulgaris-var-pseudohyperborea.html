Carex vulgaris var. pseudohyperborea

Carex vulgaris var. pseudohyperborea, commonly known as the Arctic sedge, is a perennial plant native to cold, northern regions.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered spikes of flowers. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and alpine environments. Its leaves are typically dark green and slightly hairy, adapting to harsh climatic conditions.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various arctic organisms.
