Clematis koreana var. umbrosa

Clematis koreana var. umbrosa, commonly known as the Korean shade clematis, is a deciduous climbing plant native to the forests of Korea.

It features heart-shaped, dark green leaves that provide a lush backdrop for its showy, fragrant flowers. The flowers are typically blue to purple, with five petals and a distinctive, recurved form. It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability to shaded environments.
