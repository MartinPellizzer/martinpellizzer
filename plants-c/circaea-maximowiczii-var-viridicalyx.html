Circaea maximowiczii var. viridicalyx

Circaea maximowiczii var. viridicalyx, commonly known as the green-veined wood sorrel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive greenish-white coloration on the underside, which gives it its common name. The plant produces delicate white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its leaves and flowers are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
