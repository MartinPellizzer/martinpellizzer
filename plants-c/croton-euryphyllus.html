Croton euryphyllus

Croton euryphyllus, commonly known as the giant croton, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in diameter, making it a dramatic statement plant. The leaves are typically a vibrant green with a waxy texture and often have a reddish or purple tint on the underside. It produces small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that are followed by orange or red berries.

This plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
