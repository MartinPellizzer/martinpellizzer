Calopogon pulchellus var. graminifolius

Calopogon pulchellus var. graminifolius, commonly known as the grass-leaved bog orchid, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and striking, orchid-like flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant thrives in wet, acidic soils of bogs and swamps, often in shaded environments. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with intricate patterns that aid in pollination by specific insect species.

Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is considered endangered and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
