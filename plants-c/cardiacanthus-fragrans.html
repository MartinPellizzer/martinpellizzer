Cardiacanthus fragrans

Cardiacanthus fragrans, commonly known as the sweet-scented bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces a fragrant, nectar-rich bloom that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture.

Cardiacanthus fragrans is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
