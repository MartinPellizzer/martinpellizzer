Cerinthe carthusianorum

Cerinthe carthusianorum, commonly known as the Carthusian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems with lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Its unique floral structure makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry garden settings.
