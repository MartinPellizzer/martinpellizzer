Cleome rutidosperma

Cleome rutidosperma, commonly known as the spider flower or cleome, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy yellow or white flowers that resemble spiders. The plant has hairy, branching stems and pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Cleome rutidosperma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
