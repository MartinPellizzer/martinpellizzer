Coreopsis hillebrandiana

Coreopsis hillebrandiana, commonly known as the Hillebrand's coreopsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet, featuring bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often branched and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.

Coreopsis hillebrandiana is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
