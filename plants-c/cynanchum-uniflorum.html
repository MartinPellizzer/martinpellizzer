Cynanchum uniflorum

Cynanchum uniflorum, commonly known as the single-flower milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in alpine meadows.

Cynanchum uniflorum is notable for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
