Corylus calyculata

Corylus calyculata, commonly known as the Chinese hazelnut, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the hazelnut family, Corylaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, nut-like fruits. The plant produces catkins in early spring, which are important for pollination. Its nuts are edible and have been used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
