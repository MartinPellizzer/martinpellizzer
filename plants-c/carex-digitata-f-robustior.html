Carex digitata f. robustior

Carex digitata f. robustior, commonly known as the robust sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its leaves are usually broad and flat, with a distinctive veining pattern.

The inflorescence consists of male and female flowers, with the female flowers often found at the base of the spike.
