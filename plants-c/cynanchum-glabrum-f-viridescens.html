Cynanchum glabrum f. viridescens

Cynanchum glabrum f. viridescens, commonly known as the greenish milkvetch, is a variant of the milkvetch plant characterized by its distinct foliage coloration.

It is a perennial herb native to regions of Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant features slender stems that are often hairy, with lance-shaped leaves that display a notable greenish hue. Its flowers are small and typically greenish-white, clustered in racemes, and it produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This botanical form is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
