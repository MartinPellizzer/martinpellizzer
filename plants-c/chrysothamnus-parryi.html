Chrysothamnus parryi

Chrysothamnus parryi, commonly known as Parry's goldenbush, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, golden-yellow foliage that resembles a ball of gold, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, composite flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and native plant gardens.
