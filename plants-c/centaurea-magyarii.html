Centaurea magyarii

Centaurea magyarii, commonly known as the Hungarian centaury, is a flowering plant native to Hungary and parts of southeastern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers and spiny bracts. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it produces small, clustered flowers that attract various pollinators.

Centaurea magyarii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
