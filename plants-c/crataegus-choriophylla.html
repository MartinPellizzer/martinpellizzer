Crataegus choriophylla

Crataegus choriophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaved hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often tart. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Crataegus choriophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
