Crataegus aria var. scandica

Crataegus aria var. scandica, commonly known as the common hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, oval to ovate leaves with serrated edges, and small, white or pinkish flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red or orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodland edges, hedgerows, and disturbed areas.
