Cladocarpa atollensis

Cladocarpa atollensis, commonly known as the atoll hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Indian and Pacific Oceans.

It is characterized by its woody stem and showy, pentagonal flowers that typically bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant thrives in coastal and brackish environments, often growing on sandy or muddy shores. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Cladocarpa atollensis plays a role in stabilizing coastal soils and provides habitat for various small marine and terrestrial organisms.
