Cyathea dimorphotricha

Cyathea dimorphotricha, commonly known as the two-trichome tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a fibrous, spongy layer called the rachis. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. Cyathea dimorphotricha typically thrives in humid, shaded environments with rich, well-drained soil.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of tropical forest ecosystems.
