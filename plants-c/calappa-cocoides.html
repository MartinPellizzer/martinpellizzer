Calappa cocoides

Calappa cocoides, commonly known as the globular calappa, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small, herbaceous species with a distinctive globular shape, often appearing as a dense, rounded cluster of leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique form and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and among plant enthusiasts.
