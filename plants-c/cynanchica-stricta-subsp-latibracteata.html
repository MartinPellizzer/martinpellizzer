Cynanchica stricta subsp. latibracteata

Cynanchica stricta subsp.

latibracteata, commonly known as the broad-bract milkvetch, is a perennial herb native to central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and alternate, pinnately compound leaves with broad bracts.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which bloom in the summer months. Its roots are somewhat fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in dry, sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.
