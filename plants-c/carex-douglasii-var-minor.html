Carex douglasii var. minor

Carex douglasii var. minor, commonly known as Douglas's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or streamside habitats. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

The plant is often used in native plant gardens for its ecological value and ornamental appeal.
