Conyza linifolia

Conyza linifolia, commonly known as blueweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It features slender, branching stems and small, daisy-like flowers with white petals and yellow centers. The plant produces fluffy, wind-dispersed seeds that allow it to spread easily in disturbed soils. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its foliage is often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in arid environments.
