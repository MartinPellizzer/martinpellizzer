Calandrinia saxifraga var. oligantha

Calandrinia saxifraga var. oligantha, commonly known as the rock morning glory, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its small, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in the morning. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves and stems, which help it retain moisture in dry environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is also known for its ability to self-pollinate, making it adaptable to various ecological conditions.
