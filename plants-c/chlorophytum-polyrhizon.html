Chlorophytum polyrhizon

Chlorophytum polyrhizon, commonly known as the tiger tooth plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Asparagaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its distinctive, strap-like leaves with a yellow-green stripe running down the center. The plant produces small, white flowers in clusters during the summer months. It is a hardy, low-maintenance plant that thrives in well-draining soil and bright, indirect light.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
