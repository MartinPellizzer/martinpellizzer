Cyphostemma ukerewense var. ukerewense

Cyphostemma ukerewense var. ukerewense, commonly known as the African moonvine, is a climbing plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically light green in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range from white to pale yellow, often followed by yellow or orange fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
