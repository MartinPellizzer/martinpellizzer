Cytisus leucanthus var. virens

Cytisus leucanthus var. virens, commonly known as the green-flowered broom, is a perennial shrub native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and small, bright green flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas. It produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The green-flowered broom is also valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
