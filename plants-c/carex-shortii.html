Carex shortii

Carex shortii, commonly known as the Short's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in swamps, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinct triangular cross-section characteristic of sedges.

Carex shortii produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences.
