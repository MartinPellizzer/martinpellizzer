Chrysophyllum bicolor

Chrysophyllum bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored star apple, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western Africa.

It belongs to the Sapotaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, fragrant white flowers. The fruit is a spherical, greenish-yellow berry that turns red when ripe, containing a single large, inedible seed. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its growth is typically slow, and it thrives in humid, well-drained soils.
