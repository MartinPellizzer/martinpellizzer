Cyrtomium falcatum f. pseudofortunei

Cyrtomium falcatum f. pseudofortunei, commonly known as the golden fern, is a variant of the common holly fern.

It is a compact, evergreen fern that features leathery, dark green, lance-shaped fronds with a distinctive golden hue along the midrib. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, shady environments. The plant's fronds are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued in horticulture for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in well-drained, acidic soil.
