Crolocos

Crolocos, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive golden hue. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are slightly hairy and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Crolocos is also valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
