Cyperus rivularis var. acutatus

Cyperus rivularis var. acutatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedgeland sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often forming dense colonies. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.

This variety is distinguished by its sharp, pointed leaf tips and is often found in temperate regions across Asia and Australia.
