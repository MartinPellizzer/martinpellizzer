Consolida hellespontica subsp. macedonica

Consolida hellespontica subsp.

macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian sweet pea, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan region. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability.

The plant features climbing stems with pinnate leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, butterfly-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
