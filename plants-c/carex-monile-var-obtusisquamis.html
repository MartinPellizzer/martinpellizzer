Carex monile var. obtusisquamis

Carex monile var. obtusisquamis, commonly known as the blunt-squamate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands, making it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
