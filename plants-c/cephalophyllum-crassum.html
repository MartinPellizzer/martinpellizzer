Cephalophyllum crassum

Cephalophyllum crassum, commonly known as the broadleaf headplant, is a unique succulent native to southern Africa.

It features a thick, fleshy stem that can grow up to several feet in length, often branching out into multiple stems. The plant produces clusters of small, fleshy leaves that are typically green or reddish in color, depending on light exposure. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in summer and attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native range.
