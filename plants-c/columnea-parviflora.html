Columnea parviflora

Columnea parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered flame vine, is a striking tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.

Columnea parviflora is also valued for its role in attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators.
