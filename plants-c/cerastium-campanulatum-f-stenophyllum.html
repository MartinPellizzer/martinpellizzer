Cerastium campanulatum f. stenophyllum

Cerastium campanulatum f. stenophyllum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early summer, typically from June to July. The plant has hairy, narrowly elliptic leaves that are often clustered at the base, giving it a compact appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive member of the Caryophyllaceae family.
