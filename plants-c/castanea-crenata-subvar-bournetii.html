Castanea crenata subvar. bournetii

Castanea crenata subvar. bournetii, commonly known as the Japanese chestnut, is a variant of the chestnut tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its small, spiny fruits that contain edible nuts. The plant has palmate leaves with serrated edges, and it produces flowers in catkins. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible yield.

This subspecies is closely related to other chestnut species and is valued for its resilience in various environmental conditions.
