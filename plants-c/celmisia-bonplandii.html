Celmisia bonplandii

Celmisia bonplandii, commonly known as the Bonpland's flaxflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its erect, hairy stems and clusters of small, yellowish to brownish composite flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as cloud forests and mountainous areas. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Celmisia bonplandii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
