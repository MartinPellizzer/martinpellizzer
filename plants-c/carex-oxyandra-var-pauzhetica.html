Carex oxyandra var. pauzhetica

Carex oxyandra var. pauzhetica, commonly known as the slender marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue, while its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to wet conditions and its role in stabilizing shoreline ecosystems.
