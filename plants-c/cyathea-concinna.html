Cyathea concinna

Cyathea concinna, commonly known as the slender tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to various regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feathery fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments. Its rhizome is thick and fibrous, supporting its upright growth habit.

Cyathea concinna is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
