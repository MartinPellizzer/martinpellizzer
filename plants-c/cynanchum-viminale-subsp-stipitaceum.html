Cynanchum viminale subsp. stipitaceum

Cynanchum viminale subsp.

stipitaceum, commonly known as the stipitate milkweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which includes other milkweed species known for their milky sap.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers are clustered in umbels and are usually pale green to yellowish in color.

This subspecies is characterized by its distinctive stipitate (stalked) calyces, which give it its scientific name.
