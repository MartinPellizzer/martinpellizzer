Centaurea leptoloma

Centaurea leptoloma, commonly known as the slenderhead cornflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces achenes as its seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Centaurea leptoloma is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
