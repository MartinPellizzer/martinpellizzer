Crepis adonis

Crepis adonis, commonly known as the Adonis' hawkweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is native to regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, typical of the daisy family. Its leaves are usually basal and linear, with a hairy texture.

Crepis adonis is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky soils.
