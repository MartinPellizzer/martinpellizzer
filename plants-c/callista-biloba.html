Callista biloba

Callista biloba, commonly known as the velvetleaf or blue mistflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the southeastern United States.

It features showy, blue-violet flower clusters that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has heart-shaped, velvety leaves that are soft to the touch and often have a slight fragrance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun.

Callista biloba is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
