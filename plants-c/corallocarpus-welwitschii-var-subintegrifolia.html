Corallocarpus welwitschii var. subintegrifolia

Corallocarpus welwitschii var. subintegrifolia, commonly known as the African violet or velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, velvety leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming during the rainy season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variant is distinguished by its slightly serrated leaf margins, setting it apart from other varieties within the Corallocarpus genus.
