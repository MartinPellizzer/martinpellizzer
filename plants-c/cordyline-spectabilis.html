Cordyline spectabilis

Cordyline spectabilis, commonly known as the New Zealand tree fern, is a striking evergreen plant native to New Zealand.

It features a tall, slender trunk with a crown of large, sword-shaped leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant has a unique structure with a central stem covered in a fibrous, spongy material called the rachis. Its leaves are typically bright green, though some varieties display variegated patterns.

Cordyline spectabilis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
