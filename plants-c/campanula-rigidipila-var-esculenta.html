Campanula rigidipila var. esculenta

Campanula rigidipila var. esculenta, commonly known as the edible bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are hairy and slightly rough to the touch. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its tubers, which are the edible part, are often harvested and used in traditional cuisines.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.
