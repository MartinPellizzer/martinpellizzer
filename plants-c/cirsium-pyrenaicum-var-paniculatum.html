Cirsium pyrenaicum var. paniculatum

Cirsium pyrenaicum var. paniculatum, commonly known as the Pyrenean thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, pale purple to pinkish flower heads that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped, with coarse teeth and a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This species thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing food and habitat for various pollinators and herbivores.
