Chrystolia

Chrystolia, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, yellow or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Chrystolia has lance-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and natural landscapes.
