Campylotropis trigonoclada var. trigonoclada

Campylotropis trigonoclada var. trigonoclada, commonly known as the three-angled brier, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features compound leaves with three oval leaflets, and its stems are thorny, often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in upright clusters during early spring. Its fruit is a legume pod that splits open to release seeds.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and streambanks.
