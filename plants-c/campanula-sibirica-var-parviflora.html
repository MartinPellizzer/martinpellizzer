Campanula sibirica var. parviflora

Campanula sibirica var. parviflora, commonly known as the Siberian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the colder regions of Asia.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy choice for gardens in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
