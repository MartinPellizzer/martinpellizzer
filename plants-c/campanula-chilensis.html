Campanula chilensis

Campanula chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often hairy and branched.

Campanula chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blooms.
