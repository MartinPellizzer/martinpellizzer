Capparis sepiaria var. citrifolia

Capparis sepiaria var. citrifolia, commonly known as the sour orange tree, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny stems and dark green, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to sour orange fruits, which are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to grow in coastal areas.
