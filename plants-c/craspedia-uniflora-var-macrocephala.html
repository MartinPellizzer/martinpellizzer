Craspedia uniflora var. macrocephala

Craspedia uniflora var. macrocephala, commonly known as the large-headed everlasting daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its showy, spherical flower heads composed of numerous small, yellow disk flowers surrounded by white ray florets. The plant typically grows in coastal and sandy soils, thriving in full sun. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a wiry stem that supports the compact inflorescence.

It is often used in floral arrangements due to its long-lasting blooms and delicate appearance.
