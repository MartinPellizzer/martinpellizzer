Clematis brachystemon

Clematis brachystemon, commonly known as the Chinese clematis, is a deciduous vine native to China.

It features compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its flowers are typically blue or purple, appearing in late spring to early summer. The plant produces small, round berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Clematis brachystemon is also valued for its ability to climb and cover trellises or fences effectively.
