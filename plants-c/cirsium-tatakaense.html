Cirsium tatakaense

Cirsium tatakaense, commonly known as the Tataka thistle, is a species of flowering plant in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America, specifically in Bolivia and Peru. The plant is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple-blue composite flower heads. Its leaves are typically gray-green with rough, hairy surfaces and sharp marginal teeth.

Cirsium tatakaense thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous grasslands and rocky slopes.
