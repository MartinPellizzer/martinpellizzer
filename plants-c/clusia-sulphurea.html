Clusia sulphurea

Clusia sulphurea, commonly known as the golden trumpet plant, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 3 meters in height, characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to drought conditions. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Clusia sulphurea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
