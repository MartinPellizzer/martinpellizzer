Cattleya digbyana

Cattleya digbyana, commonly known as the Digby's orchid, is a species native to Brazil.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its vibrant, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive lip that is often darker in color.

Cattleya digbyana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated by orchid enthusiasts worldwide.
