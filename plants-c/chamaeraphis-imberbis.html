Chamaeraphis imberbis

Chamaeraphis imberbis, commonly known as the hairless saltbush, is a perennial flowering plant native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in harsh environments. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its ability to thrive in saline and alkaline soils makes it an important species in ecological restoration projects.

The plant's unique adaptations to extreme conditions highlight its significance in arid ecosystem dynamics.
