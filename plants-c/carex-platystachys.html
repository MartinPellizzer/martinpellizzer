Carex platystachys

Carex platystachys, commonly known as broad-stalked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and stream banks, and is often found in temperate climates.

Carex platystachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
