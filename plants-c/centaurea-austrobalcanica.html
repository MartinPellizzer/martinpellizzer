Centaurea austrobalcanica

Centaurea austrobalcanica, commonly known as the Southern Balkan centaury, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, open habitats. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves, and it produces striking blue to purple flowers in the summer months.

Centaurea austrobalcanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also of interest in ecological studies due to its habitat specificity.
