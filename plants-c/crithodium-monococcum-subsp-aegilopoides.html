Crithodium monococcum subsp. aegilopoides

Crithodium monococcum subsp.

aegilopoides, commonly known as the slender wheatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into small, oval seeds. It is often found in semi-arid regions and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

This subspecies is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
