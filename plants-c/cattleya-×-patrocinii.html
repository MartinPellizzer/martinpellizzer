Cattleya × patrocinii

Cattleya × patrocinii, commonly known as the Patrocinii Cattleya, is a hybrid orchid that originates from the cross between Cattleya labiata and Cattleya mossiae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant features large, showy flowers with a velvety texture and a strong, sweet fragrance, often in shades of pink, red, and white. Its pseudobulbs are cylindrical, and it produces long, arching inflorescences that bear multiple blooms.

This orchid is valued for its ornamental beauty and is a popular choice among orchid enthusiasts for its vibrant colors and elegant form.
