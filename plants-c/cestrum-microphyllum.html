Cestrum microphyllum

Cestrum microphyllum, commonly known as the small-leaved nightshade, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves and clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom at night. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are toxic to humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded areas.

Cestrum microphyllum is valued for its ornamental appeal and nocturnal fragrance.
