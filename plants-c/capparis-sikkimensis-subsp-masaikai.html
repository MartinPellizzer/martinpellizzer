Capparis sikkimensis subsp. masaikai

Capparis sikkimensis subsp.

masaikai, commonly known as the Sikkim caper, is a shrub native to the eastern Himalayas. It is characterized by its thorny stems and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers.

The plant produces small, round, yellowish fruits that are edible but have a sharp, acidic taste. It thrives in moist, shaded environments at high altitudes.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the flora of its native region.
