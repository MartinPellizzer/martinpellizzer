Cassia leiandra var. guianensis

Cassia leiandra var. guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow-orange flowers. The plant produces elongated, flat pods that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and clearings.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
