Cynanchum heyneanum

Cynanchum heyneanum, commonly known as the Indian milkweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to regions of Asia, including India and China.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae, which is characterized by its milky sap and showy flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent. Its flowers are clustered in umbels and feature five reflexed petals, with a distinctive crown of hooked hairs.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.
