Carex loliacea f. subtenella

Carex loliacea f. subtenella, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a variant of the broad-leaved sedge, characterized by its narrower leaves and more compact growth habit.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. The plant features triangular stems and produces small, clustered spikelets of greenish to brownish flowers. Its leaves are typically grass-like, with a smooth texture and a pointed tip.

This subspecies is often found in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.
