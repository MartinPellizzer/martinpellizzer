Cistus lusitanicus var. decumbens

Cistus lusitanicus var. decumbens, commonly known as the Portuguese rock rose, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its low-growing, sprawling habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, pinkish-white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates.

It is often found in scrublands and coastal areas, where it plays a role in preventing soil erosion.
