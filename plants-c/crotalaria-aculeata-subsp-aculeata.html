Crotalaria aculeata subsp. aculeata

Crotalaria aculeata subsp.

aculeata, commonly known as the prickly morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred flowers.

The plant has compound leaves with small, pointed leaflets and produces pods that contain elliptical seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas, such as roadsides and fields.

Its rapid growth and ability to fix nitrogen make it both a useful and sometimes invasive species.
