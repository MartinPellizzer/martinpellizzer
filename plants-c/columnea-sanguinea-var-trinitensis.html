Columnea sanguinea var. trinitensis

Columnea sanguinea var. trinitensis, commonly known as the Trinidad flame flower, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean, particularly Trinidad and Tobago.

It is characterized by its vibrant red-orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting hummingbirds and other pollinators. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial shrub, often found in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and plays a role in the local ecosystems of its native habitat.
