Campanula pusilla f. vagans

Campanula pusilla f. vagans, commonly known as the wandering bellflower, is a compact, low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has a mat-forming habit, with hairy, slender stems and small, serrated leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate appearance and ability to spread easily in suitable conditions.
