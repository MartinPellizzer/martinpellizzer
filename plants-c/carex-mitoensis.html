Carex mitoensis

Carex mitoensis, commonly known as the Mito sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soil conditions, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of ponds. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, greenish flowers that are wind-pollinated.

Carex mitoensis plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
