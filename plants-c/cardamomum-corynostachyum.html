Cardamomum corynostachyum

Cardamomum corynostachyum, commonly known as the Himalayan black cardamom, is a perennial herb native to the eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown pods that contain small, aromatic seeds. The plant thrives in cool, moist, and shaded environments, typically at high altitudes. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, with a slightly hairy surface.

The plant is valued for its distinctive flavor and is often used in culinary and medicinal applications.
