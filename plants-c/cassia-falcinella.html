Cassia falcinella

Cassia falcinella, commonly known as the sickle cassia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features elongated, sickle-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense spikes, and it produces elongated, woody pods containing seeds.

Cassia falcinella is valued for its ornamental appeal and medicinal properties.
