Claoxylon leucocarpum

Claoxylon leucocarpum, commonly known as the white-fruit claoxylon, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by round, white fruits that are distinctive in appearance. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its timber and medicinal properties.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates with high rainfall.
