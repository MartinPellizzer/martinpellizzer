Centaurea gymnocarpa

Centaurea gymnocarpa, commonly known as the naked-seeded centaury, is a flowering plant in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, typically found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer, followed by small, dry fruits called achenes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and the stems are often branched.

The species is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
