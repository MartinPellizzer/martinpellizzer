Cremanium clathranthum

Cremanium clathranthum, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fiery red flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has a woody stem with a rough, fissured bark that provides structural support. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, often with a glossy surface.

The flower structure includes showy bracts that attract pollinators such as birds and insects.
