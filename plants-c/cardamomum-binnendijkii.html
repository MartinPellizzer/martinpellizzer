Cardamomum binnendijkii

Cardamomum binnendijkii, commonly known as the Binnendijk's cardamom, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. Its rhizomes, similar to those of other cardamom species, are used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

However, it is less commonly cultivated than other cardamom varieties due to its specific environmental requirements.
