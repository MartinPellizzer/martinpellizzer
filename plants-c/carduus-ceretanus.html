Carduus ceretanus

Carduus ceretanus, commonly known as the Italian thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and purple to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in grasslands and wastelands. Its flowers are composite, consisting of both ray and disk florets, and it produces achenes as its seed type.

The plant has been used historically in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
