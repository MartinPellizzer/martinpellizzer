Centaurea × moncktonii

Centaurea × moncktonii, commonly known as the Monckton's knapweed, is a hybrid perennial plant native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, and its stems are often branched. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and along woodland edges.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in some regions.
