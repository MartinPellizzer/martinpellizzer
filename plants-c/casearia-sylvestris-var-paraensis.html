Casearia sylvestris var. paraensis

Casearia sylvestris var. paraensis, commonly known as the Paraense casearia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Amazon rainforest.

It belongs to the Salicaceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in catkins, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is typically found in lowland areas with rich, well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
