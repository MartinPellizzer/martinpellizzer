Curcuma singularis

Curcuma singularis, commonly known as the singular turmeric, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant produces large, lance-shaped leaves and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. Its rhizomes are the primary part used, containing curcumin, a compound known for its medicinal properties.

The botanical features of Curcuma singularis make it a valuable species in both traditional medicine and modern pharmacology.
