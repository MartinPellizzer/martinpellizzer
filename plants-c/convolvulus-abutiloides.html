Convolvulus abutiloides

Convolvulus abutiloides, commonly known as the silverleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of pink, purple, and white. The plant has hairy, heart-shaped leaves and twining stems that allow it to climb or trail along the ground. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its rapid growth and showy blooms make it a valuable plant for attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
