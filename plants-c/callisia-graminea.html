Callisia graminea

Callisia graminea, commonly known as the silver vine, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Asphodelaceae and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and striking silver-green foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ornamental leaves and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.

Callisia graminea is also noted for its ability to root easily from cuttings, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
