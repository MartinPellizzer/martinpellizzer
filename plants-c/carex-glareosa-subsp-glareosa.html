Carex glareosa subsp. glareosa

Carex glareosa subsp.

glareosa, commonly known as the shining sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is native to parts of Europe and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered, with a distinctive yellowish-green coloration.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
