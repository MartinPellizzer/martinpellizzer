Chenopodium incanum var. occidentale

Chenopodium incanum var. occidentale, commonly known as western goosefoot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to western North America.

It is characterized by its rough, hairy stems and leaves that are typically alternate and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are encased in a fleshy, bladder-like structure. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

This species is also known for its use in traditional herbal medicine.
