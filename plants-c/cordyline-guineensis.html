Cordyline guineensis

Cordyline guineensis, commonly known as the African screw pine, is a tropical plant native to western and central Africa.

It is a herbaceous perennial with a rosette of fleshy, sword-shaped leaves that are often bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color, followed by clusters of red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near water bodies in its native habitat.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
