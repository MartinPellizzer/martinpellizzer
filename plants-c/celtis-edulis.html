Celtis edulis

Celtis edulis, commonly known as the edible hackberry, is a deciduous tree native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Elaeocarpaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and simple, alternate leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into berries, which are edible for both humans and wildlife. Celtis edulis thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in forest edges and open woodlands.

Its adaptability and nutritional value make it an important species in ecological and agricultural contexts.
