Calceolaria commutata

Calceolaria commutata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique slipper-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in cooler, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinct appearance.

Calceolaria commutata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
