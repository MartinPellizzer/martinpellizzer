Cinnamomum mairei

Cinnamomum mairei, commonly known as the Maire's cinnamon or Chinese cinnamon, is a species of tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark that is often used in traditional medicine and as a spice. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by oval, dark blue berries. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a distinctively strong, camphor-like scent.

Cinnamomum mairei thrives in humid, subtropical climates and is often found in mountainous regions.
