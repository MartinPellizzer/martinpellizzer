Callicarpa anisophylla

Callicarpa anisophylla, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of violet to purple berries that appear in the fall. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

The berries are not only ornamental but also serve as a food source for various birds and small mammals.
