Ctenopteris kaietura

Ctenopteris kaietura, commonly known as the Kaietura water fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating fronds that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments such as ponds and streams. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Ctenopteris kaietura plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat and oxygen through photosynthesis.
