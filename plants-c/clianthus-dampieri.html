Clianthus dampieri

Clianthus dampieri, commonly known as the New Zealand flax or the red kowhai, is a striking plant native to New Zealand.

It is a shrub or small tree that belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes. The plant is characterized by its vibrant red or orange flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they consist of three leaflets, and the plant has a woody stem with a fibrous bark.

Clianthus dampieri is often found in coastal and inland forest margins, thriving in well-drained soils.
