Centaurea × orodensis

Centaurea × orodensis, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue to purple flowers, which bloom in summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and has serrated, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
