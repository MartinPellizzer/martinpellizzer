Cinchona cordifolia

Cinchona cordifolia, commonly known as the Amazonian quinine tree, is a species of the Rubiaceae family native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, with dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy in appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries containing seeds. Its bark contains alkaloids, including quinine, which has historically been used for medicinal purposes.

Due to its ecological and pharmacological significance, it is also an important species in conservation efforts.
