Crataegus whitakeri

Crataegus whitakeri, commonly known as Whitaker’s hawthorn, is a species of shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, with serrated edges, and turn reddish in the fall.

Whitaker’s hawthorn is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
