Claytonia media

Claytonia media, commonly known as the purple sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features rosettes of fleshy, oval leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers in early spring, which bloom in clusters atop slender stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Claytonia media is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate partial shade.
