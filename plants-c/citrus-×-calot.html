Citrus × calot

Citrus × calot, commonly known as the Calamondin orange, is a hybrid citrus plant originating from a cross between the mandarin and the pomelo.

It is characterized by its small, rounded fruit that is typically green with a thin, leathery rind. The plant is evergreen, with glossy, dark green leaves and produces fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is often used in cooking, particularly in Southeast Asian cuisines, and is also popular as a ornamental plant.

The plant thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
