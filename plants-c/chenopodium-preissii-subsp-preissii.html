Chenopodium preissii subsp. preissii

Chenopodium preissii subsp.

preissii, commonly known as the desert spinach, is a species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia. It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are rich in nutrients.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is well adapted to drought conditions. Its leaves are often used in traditional Aboriginal cuisine and are valued for their edible qualities.

The plant produces small, green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.
