Cirsium caucasicum subsp. wwedenskyi

Cirsium caucasicum subsp.

wwedenskyi, commonly known as the Caucasian thistle, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, showy purple or blue flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has deeply lobed, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
