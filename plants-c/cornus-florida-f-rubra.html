Cornus florida f. rubra

Cornus florida f. rubra, commonly known as the red-flowering dogwood, is a cultivar of the flowering dogwood tree.

It is characterized by its showy, bright red bracts that appear in spring, which surround small, yellowish flowers. The plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in partial shade with well-drained soil. Its foliage turns a striking yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest.

This ornamental plant is valued for its vibrant floral display and is often used in gardens and landscapes.
