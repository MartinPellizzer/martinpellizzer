Cecropia sciadophylla var. decurrens

Cecropia sciadophylla var. decurrens, commonly known as the spreading ceiba, is a fast-growing tree native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a whorl at the ends of its branches. The plant has a distinctive trunk that is often covered with a thick, fibrous layer of bark. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by woody, three-lobed fruits containing seeds.

This species is often found in disturbed areas and is valued for its ecological role in supporting diverse wildlife.
