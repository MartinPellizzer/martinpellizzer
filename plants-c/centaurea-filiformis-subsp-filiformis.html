Centaurea filiformis subsp. filiformis

Centaurea filiformis subsp.

filiformis, commonly known as the slender cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and coastal areas. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is known for its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in harsh environments.

The subspecies filiformis is distinguished by its elongated leaves and reduced flower heads compared to other centaurea species.
