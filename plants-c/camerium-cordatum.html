Camerium cordatum

Camerium cordatum, commonly known as the heartleaf plant, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, often blooming during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight.

Camerium cordatum is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
