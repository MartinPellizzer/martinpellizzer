Citrus × tachibana subf. sunki

Citrus × tachibana subf.

sunki, commonly known as the Japanese sudachi, is a citrus hybrid native to Japan. It is characterized by its small, round fruits with a thick, aromatic rind and a high concentration of essential oils.

The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring dark green, glossy leaves and fragrant white flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its zest and juice, which are used in culinary and aromatic applications.

The fruit is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional Japanese medicine.
