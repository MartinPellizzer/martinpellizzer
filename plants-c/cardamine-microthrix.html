Cardamine microthrix

Cardamine microthrix, commonly known as the small-stemmed water crowfoot, is a small aquatic or semi-aquatic flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found near streams, ponds, and ditches. The plant has slender, branching stems and small, floating or submerged leaves that are usually oval or lance-shaped. Its flowers are white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom in early spring.

Cardamine microthrix is often found in calcareous habitats and is considered a rare species in many regions.
