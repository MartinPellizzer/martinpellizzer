Coursetia guaranitica var. roseiflora

Coursetia guaranitica var. roseiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered coursetia, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant features small, clustered flowers that range in color from pink to red, blooming during the warmer months. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, consisting of three small, oval-shaped leaflets.

The plant is adapted to grow in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
