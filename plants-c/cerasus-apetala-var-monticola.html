Cerasus apetala var. monticola

Cerasus apetala var. monticola, commonly known as the mountain cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, white flowers in early spring, followed by edible, red, cherry-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and cuisine.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subalpine climates.
