Crocus heuffelianus lusus concolor

Crocus heuffelianus lusus concolor, commonly known as the purple crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers. The plant produces small, tubular blooms that typically appear in early spring, often before the leaves fully emerge. Its corms are used for propagation, and the plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
