Capsella bursa-pastoris triangularis

Capsella bursa-pastoris triangularis, commonly known as the triangular weed or triangleleaf chickweed, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular-shaped leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is an annual herb that thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and gardens. Its seed pods, which resemble small hearts, are a distinctive feature and contain numerous tiny seeds.

This species is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
