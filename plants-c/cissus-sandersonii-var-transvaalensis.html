Cissus sandersonii var. transvaalensis

Cissus sandersonii var. transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal grape ivy, is a climbing plant native to South Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, grape-like berries. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Its vigorous growth makes it suitable for trellises or hanging baskets.
