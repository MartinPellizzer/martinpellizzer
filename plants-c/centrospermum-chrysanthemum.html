Centrospermum chrysanthemum

Centrospermum chrysanthemum, commonly known as the yellow flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a striking appearance. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant's botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical regions.
