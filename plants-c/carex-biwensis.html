Carex biwensis

Carex biwensis, commonly known as the Japanese sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain both male and female flowers.

Carex biwensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in wet conditions.
