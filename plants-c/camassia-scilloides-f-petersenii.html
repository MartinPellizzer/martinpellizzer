Camassia scilloides f. petersenii

Camassia scilloides f. petersenii, commonly known as Petersen's camas, is a variant of the camas plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces striking blue to violet flowers in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in open woodland or meadow habitats. Its leaves are grass-like and form a basal rosette, while its flowers are arranged in a spike.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant gardens.
