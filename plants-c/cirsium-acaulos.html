Cirsium acaulos

Cirsium acaulos, commonly known as the naked thistle, is a striking perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, purple-blue flower heads that resemble thistles. The plant produces a unique, hollow stem that is covered in bracts, giving it its common name. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Cirsium acaulos is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
