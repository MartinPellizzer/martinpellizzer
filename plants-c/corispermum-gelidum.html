Corispermum gelidum

Corispermum gelidum, commonly known as the cold flax or Arctic flax, is a small, herbaceous plant that thrives in harsh environments.

It is native to Arctic and alpine regions, where it adapts to extreme cold and poor soil conditions. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain oily seeds, which are rich in essential nutrients. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss in cold climates.

Corispermum gelidum plays a vital role in its ecosystem by providing food for local wildlife and contributing to soil stability in barren environments.
