Chorizema trilobum

Chorizema trilobum, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has hairy, succulent stems and leaves that are adapted to conserve water in dry environments. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Chorizema trilobum plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife in its native habitat.
