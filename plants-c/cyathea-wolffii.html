Cyathea wolffii

Cyathea wolffii, commonly known as the Wolff's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, compound fronds. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to anchor itself in moist, shaded environments. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, contributing to its reproductive strategy.

Cyathea wolffii plays an important ecological role in tropical rainforests by providing habitat and contributing to biodiversity.
