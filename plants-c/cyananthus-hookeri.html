Cyananthus hookeri

Cyananthus hookeri, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which bloom in early spring. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Cyananthus hookeri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens with specific care requirements.
