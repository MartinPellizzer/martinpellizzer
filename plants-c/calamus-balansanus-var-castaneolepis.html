Calamus balansanus var. castaneolepis

Calamus balansanus var. castaneolepis, commonly known as the red-scaled calamus, is a tropical plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems that often grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are typically long and narrow with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in inflorescences, which are followed by spiny, triangular fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in some tropical botanical gardens.
