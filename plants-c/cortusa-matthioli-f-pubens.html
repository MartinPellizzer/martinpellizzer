Cortusa matthioli f. pubens

Cortusa matthioli f. pubens, commonly known as the pubescent bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy, dark green leaves that are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive flowers and compact growth habit.
