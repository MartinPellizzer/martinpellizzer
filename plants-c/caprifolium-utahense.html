Caprifolium utahense

Caprifolium utahense, commonly known as Utah honeysuckle, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, arching branches and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces yellowish-orange berries that are edible for birds and small mammals. It thrives in rocky soils and arid environments, often found in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.
