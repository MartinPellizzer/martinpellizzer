Chlorophytum glaucidulum var. pauper

Chlorophytum glaucidulum var. pauper, commonly known as the slender spider plant, is a compact, evergreen perennial native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, with a waxy coating that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white flower spikes that emerge from the base during the spring or summer months. It is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its low maintenance requirements and ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions.

Its ability to propagate through offsets makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking an easy-to-care-for plant.
