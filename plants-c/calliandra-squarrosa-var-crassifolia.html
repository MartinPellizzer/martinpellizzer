Calliandra squarrosa var. crassifolia

Calliandra squarrosa var. crassifolia, commonly known as the thick-leaved calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged and have a distinctive thick, leathery texture. The plant produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom throughout the dry season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping and erosion control.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a popular choice in both gardens and ecological restoration projects.
