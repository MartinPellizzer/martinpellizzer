Cereus huascha var. flaviflorus

Cereus huascha var. flaviflorus, commonly known as the Yellow Flower Cereus, is a cactus species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, columnar stems and produces showy yellow flowers during the night. The plant has a spiny exterior and thrives in well-draining soil with full sun exposure. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooming habit.

This variety is relatively hardy but requires careful watering to avoid root rot.
