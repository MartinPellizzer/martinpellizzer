Carex piniaria

Carex piniaria, commonly known as the pine needle sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving them a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or swampy areas.

Carex piniaria plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
