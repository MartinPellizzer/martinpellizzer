Clinanthus croceus

Clinanthus croceus, commonly known as the golden sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its slender, branched stems and small, narrow leaves contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Clinanthus croceus is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
