Carex distans var. luteola

Carex distans var. luteola, commonly known as the yellow sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known as sedges. This variant is distinguished by its golden-yellow inflorescences, which contrast with its dark green foliage. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas.
