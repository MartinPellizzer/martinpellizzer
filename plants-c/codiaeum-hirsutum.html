Codiaeum hirsutum

Codiaeum hirsutum, commonly known as the croton plant, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful leaves that can range from red, orange, and yellow to shades of green and purple. The leaves are typically palmately lobed and often have a velvety texture due to the presence of fine hairs. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This plant is widely cultivated as an ornamental due to its vibrant foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
