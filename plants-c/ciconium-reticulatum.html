Ciconium reticulatum

Ciconium reticulatum, commonly known as the net-veined fern, is a species of fern characterized by its intricate and delicate fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with long, creeping underground stems that produce upright, branching fronds. Its leaves are typically pinnately divided, with a reticulate venation pattern that gives it its common name.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along stream banks.
