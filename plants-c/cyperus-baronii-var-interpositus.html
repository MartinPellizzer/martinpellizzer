Cyperus baronii var. interpositus

Cyperus baronii var. interpositus, commonly known as the swamp cyperus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in shallow water or moist soils, often forming dense stands in marshes and along waterways. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spikelet that bears small, three-ranked flowers, which are adapted for wind pollination.

Cyperus baronii var. interpositus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
