Crocus banaticus

Crocus banaticus, commonly known as the Banat crocus, is a species of flowering plant in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Europe, particularly found in regions such as Romania and Bulgaria. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a corm.

The crocus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
