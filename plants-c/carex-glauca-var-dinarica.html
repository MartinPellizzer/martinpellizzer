Carex glauca var. dinarica

Carex glauca var. dinarica, commonly known as the Dinaric sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. It produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated.

Its foliage is often a bluish-green, giving it a distinctive appearance in its natural habitat.
