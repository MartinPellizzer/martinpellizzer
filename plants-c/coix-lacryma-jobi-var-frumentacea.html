Coix lacryma-jobi var. frumentacea

Coix lacryma-jobi var. frumentacea, commonly known as Job's tears or Chinese pearl millet, is a member of the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its distinctive, tear-shaped caryopsis that is often used in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant grows in moist environments and is native to East Asia, particularly China, Japan, and Korea. Its seeds are rich in nutrients, including starch, protein, and minerals, making it a valuable agricultural crop.

The plant's botanical features include narrow, grass-like leaves and a tall, slender stem that bears the characteristic elongated, smooth seeds.
