Cissus sicyoides var. gardneri

Cissus sicyoides var. gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's devil's ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple veins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, spherical fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ornamental appeal.
