Campanula rotundifolia f. dilecta

Campanula rotundifolia f. dilecta, commonly known as the purple harebell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are rounded and hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty and ease of care.
