Capparis spinosa var. galeata

Capparis spinosa var. galeata, commonly known as the rock rose, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features prickly, woody stems and small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant produces small, round, yellow or orange fruits known as "bitter almonds," which are edible but have a strong, pungent flavor. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing on rocky or coastal terrain.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a notable species in Mediterranean botany.
