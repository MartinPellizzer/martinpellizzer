Centaurea × lundstroemii

Centaurea × lundstroemii, commonly known as the hybrid gladiolus, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Centaurea cyanus (blueweed) and Centaurea stoebe (devil's bit). The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring upright stems and striking blue to purple flower heads. Its botanical characteristics include narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy composite flowers that attract pollinators.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
