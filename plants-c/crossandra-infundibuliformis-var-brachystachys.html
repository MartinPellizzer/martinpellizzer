Crossandra infundibuliformis var. brachystachys

Crossandra infundibuliformis var. brachystachys, commonly known as the dwarf flame flower, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It features vibrant, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are lance-shaped and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Its compact size and striking blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
