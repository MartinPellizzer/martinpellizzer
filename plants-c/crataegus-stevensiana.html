Crataegus stevensiana

Crataegus stevensiana, commonly known as the Stevens hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, simple ovate leaves, and clusters of small white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces red berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and other wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ornamental value and ecological significance in forest ecosystems.
