Commelina ambigua

Commelina ambigua, commonly known as the wandering jew, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Commelinales order and the Commelinaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with striking blue or violet flowers. The plant features slender, upright stems and arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typically with three blue petals and two white petals.

Commelina ambigua is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it both a popular ornamental plant and a potential weed.
