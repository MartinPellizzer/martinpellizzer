Cotula cunninghamii

Cotula cunninghamii, commonly known as the dwarf dandelion, is a small flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by fluffy, white seed heads. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Cotula cunninghamii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
