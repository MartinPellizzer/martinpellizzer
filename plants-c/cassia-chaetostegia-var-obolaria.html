Cassia chaetostegia var. obolaria

Cassia chaetostegia var. obolaria, commonly known as the white cassia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom throughout the year. Its leaves are bipinnate, with numerous small leaflets arranged in a feathery pattern.

The seeds are enclosed in slender, hairy pods, which contribute to its distinctive botanical appearance.
