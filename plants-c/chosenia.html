Chosenia

Chosenia, commonly known as the water bamboo or arrowhead plant, is a member of the Araceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, particularly China and Southeast Asia. The plant features arrow-shaped leaves that are often bright green and glossy, with a distinctive veining pattern. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for water gardens and pond edges.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its clusters of small, white flowers that emerge from a spadix surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.
