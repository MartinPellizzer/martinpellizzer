Cladium ficticium

Cladium ficticium, commonly known as the false sawgrass, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, grass-like leaves that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant typically thrives in shallow water or moist soil, often found in marshes and swamps. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies, which can stabilize soil and provide habitat for various aquatic organisms.

Cladium ficticium plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and water filtration.
