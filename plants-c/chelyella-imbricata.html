Chelyella imbricata

Chelyella imbricata, commonly known as the rock spurge, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typically blooming in spring. Its root system is shallow and fibrous, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Chelyella imbricata is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
