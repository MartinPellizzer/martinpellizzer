Cupressus arizonica f. glauca

Cupressus arizonica f. glauca, commonly known as the Blue Arizona Cypress, is a cultivar of the Arizona cypress.

It is characterized by its dense, bluish-gray foliage that gives it a striking appearance. The plant has scale-like leaves that are tightly packed, forming a compact, conical shape. It produces small, round cones that are typically reddish-brown in color.

This evergreen shrub or small tree is well-suited for arid climates and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
