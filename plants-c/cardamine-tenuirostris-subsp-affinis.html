Cardamine tenuirostris subsp. affinis

Cardamine tenuirostris subsp.

affinis, commonly known as narrow-beaked watercress, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as stream banks and forest floors.

The plant features trifoliate leaves with a slightly hairy surface and small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in racemes. Its root system is fibrous, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in wetland gardens.
