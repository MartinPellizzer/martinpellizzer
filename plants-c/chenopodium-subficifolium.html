Chenopodium subficifolium

Chenopodium subficifolium, commonly known as redroot goosefoot, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, reddish stems and alternate, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, clustered green flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. Its leaves and stems are often used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported diuretic and anti-inflammatory properties.

Chenopodium subficifolium thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.
