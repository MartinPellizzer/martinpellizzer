Calamagrostis glabriflora

Calamagrostis glabriflora, commonly known as the smooth-flowered reed, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, stream banks, and swampy areas. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense clumps, making it an effective pioneer species in disturbed wetlands.

Calamagrostis glabriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
