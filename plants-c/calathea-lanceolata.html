Calathea lanceolata

Calathea lanceolata, commonly known as the arrowleaf calathea, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It features striking, lance-shaped leaves with intricate patterns of silver and green, often marked by a distinctive arrowhead shape. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires consistent humidity and well-draining soil. Its foliage changes color subtly with the seasons, adding a dynamic visual element to its environment.

Calathea lanceolata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
