Crotalaria laburnifolia subsp. petiolaris

Crotalaria laburnifolia subsp.

petiolaris, commonly known as the yellow crotalaria, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that resemble those of the laburnum tree, hence its common name.

The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and produces pods that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique floral structure and its role in certain ecological systems.
