Conospermum taxifolium var. brownii

Conospermum taxifolium var. brownii, commonly known as the Brown's flame bush, is a shrub native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and striking red or orange flower spikes that appear during the spring. The leaves are narrow, linear, and often have a slightly waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and dry garden environments.

This plant is valued for its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various climatic conditions.
