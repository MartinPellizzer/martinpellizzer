Campanula filicaulis var. schotteri

Campanula filicaulis var. schotteri, commonly known as the Schotteri bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Botanically, it is characterized by its slender stems and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats.
