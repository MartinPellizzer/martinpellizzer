Cassia tora var. humilis

Cassia tora var. humilis, commonly known as small-flowered cassia, is a perennial shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow inflorescences. The plant produces small, clustered pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and useful in traditional medicine.
