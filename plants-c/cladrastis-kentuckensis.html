Cladrastis kentuckensis

Cladrastis kentuckensis, commonly known as the Kentucky bleeding heart, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped, pendulous white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has compound leaves that are alternate and pinnately arranged, with serrated edges. It thrives in partial to full shade and moist, well-drained soil.

Cladrastis kentuckensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in landscaping for its unique floral display.
