Caesalpinia ciliata

Caesalpinia ciliata, commonly known as the flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which bloom during the dry season. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of several elliptical leaflets, and its woody pods contain seeds covered in a fibrous coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its potential in traditional medicine.
