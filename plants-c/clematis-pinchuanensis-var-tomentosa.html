Clematis pinchuanensis var. tomentosa

Clematis pinchuanensis var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to China.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green stems and heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and blue during the summer months. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is a sought-after plant in horticultural collections.
