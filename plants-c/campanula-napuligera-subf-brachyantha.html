Campanula napuligera subf. brachyantha

Campanula napuligera subf.

brachyantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often with a hairy texture, while its stems are upright and branched.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
