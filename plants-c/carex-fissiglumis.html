Carex fissiglumis

Carex fissiglumis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces distinctive, often linear or lance-shaped seeds.

Carex fissiglumis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
