Calliandra haematomma

Calliandra haematomma, commonly known as the red calliandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery inflorescences that range in color from red to pink and orange. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil. Its leaves are compound and pinnate, with small, oval-shaped leaflets.

Calliandra haematomma is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant flowers and drought tolerance.
