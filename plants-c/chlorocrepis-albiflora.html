Chlorocrepis albiflora

Chlorocrepis albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered chlorocrepis, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its most notable characteristic is the white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
