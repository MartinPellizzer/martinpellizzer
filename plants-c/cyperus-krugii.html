Cyperus krugii

Cyperus krugii, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, often forming dense stands. Cyperus krugii produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually wind-pollinated.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat for aquatic organisms and contributing to soil stabilization in wetland ecosystems.
