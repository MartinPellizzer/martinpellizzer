Cestrum dunalii

Cestrum dunalii, commonly known as the white cestrum, is a flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in the evening, attracting nocturnal pollinators like moths. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, round seeds.

Cestrum dunalii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its pleasant, sweet fragrance.
