Ceropegia longifolia

Ceropegia longifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ceropegia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a succulent species that thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky outcrops or in sandy soils. The plant features tubular, fleshy leaves that are typically narrow and lance-shaped, adapted for water storage. Its flowers are small and tubular, often with a distinctive hood-like structure, and are usually pale green or yellowish in color.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and ability to thrive in low-water conditions.
