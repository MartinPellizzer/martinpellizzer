Campanula camptoclada

Campanula camptoclada, commonly known as the bent-stem bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its upright, often slightly zigzagging stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped blue to violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer.

Campanula camptoclada is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
