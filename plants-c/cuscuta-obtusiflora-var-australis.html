Cuscuta obtusiflora var. australis

Cuscuta obtusiflora var. australis, commonly known as the Australian dodder, is a parasitic flowering plant that lacks chlorophyll and relies on host plants for nutrients.

It produces small, yellowish flowers and has a highly branched, thread-like structure that allows it to attach to various host species. The plant is native to Australia and is often found growing on shrubs and grasses in arid environments. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, animals, or through human activity, contributing to its widespread distribution.

Due to its parasitic nature, it can cause significant damage to agricultural crops and native vegetation.
