Ceanothus megacarpus var. megacarpus

Ceanothus megacarpus var. megacarpus, commonly known as the coastal ceanothus, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California.

It is characterized by its aromatic, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, leathery leaves that are typically evergreen, providing year-round foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild winters and warm, dry summers.

This species is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
