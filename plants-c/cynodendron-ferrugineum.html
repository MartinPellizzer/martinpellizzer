Cynodendron ferrugineum

Cynodendron ferrugineum, commonly known as the red sandalwood, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, rusty-colored pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its bark is characterized by a reddish-brown color and a rough, flaky texture.

Cynodendron ferrugineum is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
