Corymbia grandifolia subsp. lamprocardia

Corymbia grandifolia subsp.

lamprocardia, commonly known as the glossy apple or satinwood, is a member of the Myrtaceae family. It is characterized by its smooth, often glossy bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically glaucous underneath.

The plant produces large, showy flowers in clusters, which are usually pink to red in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is native to eastern Australia.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and timber quality.
