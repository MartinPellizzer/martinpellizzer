Cryosophila williamsii

Cryosophila williamsii, commonly known as the Antarctic bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the extreme environments of Antarctica.

It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in the harsh, cold conditions of the continent's coastal regions. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, adapted to attract pollinators in its isolated habitat. Its leaves are thick and fleshy, helping to conserve moisture in the arid Antarctic climate.

Cryosophila williamsii is one of the few flowering plants that can survive in such extreme conditions, showcasing remarkable adaptations to cold and nutrient-poor soils.
