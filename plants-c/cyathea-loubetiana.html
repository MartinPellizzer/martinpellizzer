Cyathea loubetiana

Cyathea loubetiana, commonly known as the Loubet's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant exhibits a unique growth pattern, with a central trunk covered in a mass of persistent, fibrous roots. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters and are typically bright green with a glossy texture.

Cyathea loubetiana plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing shelter and food for various species.
