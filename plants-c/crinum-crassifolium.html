Crinum crassifolium

Crinum crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf lily, is a striking plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its large, strap-like leaves that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a prominent central stamen. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight.

Crinum crassifolium is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
