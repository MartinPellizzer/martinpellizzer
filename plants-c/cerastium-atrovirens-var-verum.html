Cerastium atrovirens var. verum

Cerastium atrovirens var. verum, commonly known as the dark green mouse-ear cress, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant characterized by its dense clusters of small, pale green leaves.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many flowering plants with four-petaled flowers. The species is native to Europe and parts of Asia, typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers, which appear in early spring, are usually white or pale yellow and are arranged in racemes.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in rocky or disturbed soils.
