Caladenia radiata

Caladenia radiata, commonly known as the pink fairy lantern, is an orchid species native to Australia.

It features a slender, erect stem with a single, showy pink or pale pink flower that resembles a lantern. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces a single leaf that is often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It blooms during the autumn season and is typically found in heathlands and open forests.

The species is part of the Caladenia genus, which is known for its diverse and often striking floral displays.
