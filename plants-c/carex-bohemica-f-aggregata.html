Carex bohemica f. aggregata

Carex bohemica f. aggregata, commonly known as the clustered sedge, is a variant of the Bohemian sedge.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and wet meadows. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, grass-like leaves that are often clustered at the base.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikes, which are typical of the sedge family.
