Chrysophyllum ebenaceum var. obversum

Chrysophyllum ebenaceum var. obversum, commonly known as the African cherry or obversum satinwood, is a species of flowering plant in the Sapotaceae family.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its wood is valued for its durability and is used in furniture and construction.
