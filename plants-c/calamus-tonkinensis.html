Calamus tonkinensis

Calamus tonkinensis, commonly known as Tonkin arrowroot, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arales.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam, Thailand, and Cambodia. The plant is characterized by its long, slender rhizomes that are used in traditional medicine and as a source of starch. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, often reaching lengths of up to 1 meter.

Calamus tonkinensis thrives in wet, swampy environments and is often found in lowland forests.
