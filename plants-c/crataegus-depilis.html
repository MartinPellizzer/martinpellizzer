Crataegus depilis

Crataegus depilis, commonly known as the smooth hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in woodlands, thickets, and along stream banks.

Crataegus depilis is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
