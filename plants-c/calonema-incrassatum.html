Calonema incrassatum

Calonema incrassatum, commonly known as the thick-leaved calonema, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically green or reddish in color, depending on light exposure. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or white during the warmer months. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.

Calonema incrassatum prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
