Crepis iringensis

Crepis iringensis, commonly known as the Iringen mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually basal, hairy, and arranged in a rosette, while the stems are erect and covered in fine pubescence.

Crepis iringensis is notable for its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.
