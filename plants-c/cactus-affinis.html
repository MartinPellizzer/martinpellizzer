Cactus affinis

Cactus affinis, commonly known as the small spineless cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming plant that typically reaches a height of only a few inches. The plant features dense clusters of pale green, cylindrical stems covered in fine, white hairs. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, blooming in late spring to early summer.

Cactus affinis is adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it a hardy plant for desert gardens.
