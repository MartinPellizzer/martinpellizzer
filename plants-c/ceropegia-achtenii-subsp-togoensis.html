Ceropegia achtenii subsp. togoensis

Ceropegia achtenii subsp.

togoensis, commonly known as the Togo moonvine, is a climbing plant native to West Africa. It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, fragrant, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often with a distinctive corona. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested or shaded areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouses.
