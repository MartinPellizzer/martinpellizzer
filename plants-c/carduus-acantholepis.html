Carduus acantholepis

Carduus acantholepis, commonly known as the prickly sowthistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and rough, hairy leaves. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open areas. Its leaves are serrated and covered in fine spines, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The flowers are yellow and bloom in late summer, attracting various pollinators.
