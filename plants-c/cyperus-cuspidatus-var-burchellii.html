Cyperus cuspidatus var. burchellii

Cyperus cuspidatus var. burchellii, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. The plant has slender, grass-like stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, three-lobed flowers are arranged in dense clusters at the tips of the stems.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
