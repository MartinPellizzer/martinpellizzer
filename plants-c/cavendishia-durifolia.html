Cavendishia durifolia

Cavendishia durifolia, commonly known as the hardy orchid, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant produces striking, showy flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, often with a pleasant fragrance. It prefers well-draining soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Due to its resilience and ornamental value, Cavendishia durifolia is widely appreciated by horticulturists and plant enthusiasts.
