Chaetoseris ladyginii

Chaetoseris ladyginii, commonly known as the narrowleaf starflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and serrated, with a distinctive silvery-blue hue on the underside.

The flowers bloom in late summer, producing small yellow disk flowers surrounded by white ray florets.
