Coleogeton vaginatus

Coleogeton vaginatus, commonly known as the slender sandbur, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and woolly, bristly foliage. The plant produces small, spiny fruits that are adapted for wind dispersal, aiding in its spread across open landscapes. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in dry environments.

Coleogeton vaginatus is often found in sandy soils and can thrive in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.
