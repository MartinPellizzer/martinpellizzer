Cassia microphylla var. senegalensis

Cassia microphylla var. senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese cassia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically pinnate with small, ovate leaflets. The plant produces yellow flowers in panicles, followed by long, slender pods containing flat, dark brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Cassia microphylla var. senegalensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in medicine and as a source of dye.
