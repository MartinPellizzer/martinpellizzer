Cornus californica var. nevadensis

Cornus californica var. nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada dogwood, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It features showy, white or pink bracts that surround small, yellowish flowers, typically appearing in early spring. The plant has slender, branching stems and dark green, ovate leaves with toothed margins. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a landscape accent.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant spring blooms and autumn foliage color.
