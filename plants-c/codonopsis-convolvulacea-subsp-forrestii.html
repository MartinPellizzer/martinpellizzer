Codonopsis convolvulacea subsp. forrestii

Codonopsis convolvulacea subsp.

forrestii, commonly known as the Forest Codonopsis, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its hairy stems and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have serrated margins, while its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine for its purported health benefits.
