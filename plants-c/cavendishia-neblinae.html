Cavendishia neblinae

Cavendishia neblinae, commonly known as the Neblina Cavendish, is a rare and striking bromeliad native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy inflorescence that can reach up to 3 meters in height, featuring bright red and yellow bracts. The plant thrives in high humidity and indirect light, making it a popular choice for greenhouse cultivation. Its unique appearance and limited distribution make it a sought-after specimen among plant enthusiasts.

Due to habitat destruction, Cavendishia neblinae is considered endangered, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
