Cinnamomum tampicense

Cinnamomum tampicense, commonly known as the Mexican cinnamon tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Lauraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Mexico and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall and has smooth, dark-brown bark. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a strong, spicy scent when crushed.

The plant is valued for its essential oils and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
