Claoxylon coriaceolanatum

Claoxylon coriaceolanatum, commonly known as the thick-leaved tamarind, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by edible, pod-like fruits containing seeds. Its bark is rough and fissured, and it can grow up to 20 meters in height.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is also used in ornamental horticulture.
