Crataegus oxyacantha var. saccardyana

Crataegus oxyacantha var. saccardyana, commonly known as the Saccardyana hawthorn, is a variant of the common hawthorn.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves, and clusters of white flowers that appear in spring. Its fruit is a red, fleshy pome that ripens in autumn.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
