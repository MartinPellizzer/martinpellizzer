Calamagrostis tashiroi

Calamagrostis tashiroi, commonly known as Tashiro's reed, is a grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. Its flowering spikes are usually pale green to brown, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Calamagrostis tashiroi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland habitats.
