Carex macloviana var. pseudoleporina

Carex macloviana var. pseudoleporina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial plant native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, grass-green leaves that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spike-like clusters, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
