Carlina curetum subsp. curetum

Carlina curetum subsp.

curetum, commonly known as the purple cudweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and disturbed soils, and it thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are basal, lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs, while its purple or pink flowers bloom in late summer.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
