Centaurea trichocephala subsp. simonkaiana

Centaurea trichocephala subsp.

simonkaiana, commonly known as the Simonkaian thistle, is a perennial flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its erect, spiny stems and striking purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in dry, sunny habitats such as rocky soils and open fields.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural collections.
