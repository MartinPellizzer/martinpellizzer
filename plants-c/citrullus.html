Citrullus

Citrullus, commonly known as watermelon, is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is a vigorous trailing vine that produces large, lobed leaves and bright yellow flowers. The fruit is typically round or oblong, with a green rind and red or yellow flesh. The seeds are enclosed within the pulp and are often edible.

Botanically, Citrullus is characterized by its C3 photosynthetic pathway and is native to Africa.
