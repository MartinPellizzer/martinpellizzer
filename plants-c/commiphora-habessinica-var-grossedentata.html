Commiphora habessinica var. grossedentata

Commiphora habessinica var. grossedentata, commonly known as the Abyssinian myrrh tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap, which is used in traditional medicine and incense. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical to ovate in shape, with a leathery texture. It produces small, inconspicuous yellowish-green flowers followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is often cultivated for its gum resins.
