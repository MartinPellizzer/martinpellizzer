Crassula dichotoma

Crassula dichotoma, commonly known as the "Coral Cactus," is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features a branched stem system with short, fleshy, cylindrical branches that often resemble coral-like structures. The leaves are small, thick, and arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water storage and reducing transpiration. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
