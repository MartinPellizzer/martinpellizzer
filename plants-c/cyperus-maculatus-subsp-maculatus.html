Cyperus maculatus subsp. maculatus

Cyperus maculatus subsp.

maculatus, commonly known as the spotted flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It features narrow, grass-like leaves with distinctive dark green stripes and a pale green underside.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical characteristics make it adaptable to moist soils and suitable for ornamental use in wetland gardens.
