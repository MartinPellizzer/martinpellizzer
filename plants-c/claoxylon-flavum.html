Claoxylon flavum

Claoxylon flavum, commonly known as the yellow-flowered claoxylon, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Clusiaceae and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and palmate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by orange-red berries. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable in some regions.
