Catalpa vestita

Catalpa vestita, commonly known as the Chinese catalpa, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and showy, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in late spring. The tree produces long, cylindrical seed pods that split open to release its winged seeds. Catalpa vestita is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can grow up to 20 meters tall.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for gardens and landscapes.
