Crotalaria morumbensis

Crotalaria morumbensis, commonly known as the Morumbense false indigo, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod fruits. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter. Its leaves are alternate, pinnately compound, and often have a velvety texture.

The flowers are showy, usually yellow with a distinctive black or purple spot at the base of the petals, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
