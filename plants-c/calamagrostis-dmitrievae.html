Calamagrostis dmitrievae

Calamagrostis dmitrievae, commonly known as the Dmitriev's reed, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its flowering inflorescence consists of a panicle made up of spikelets, which are clustered in a loose, open arrangement.

Calamagrostis dmitrievae plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native environment and is of interest to botanists studying high-altitude flora.
