Crepis palaestina subsp. babcockii

Crepis palaestina subsp.

babcockii, commonly known as the Babcock's mouse-ear, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of the Mediterranean. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its small, daisy-like flowers and hairy, lobed leaves.

This subspecies typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed areas.

The plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and geographic distribution.
