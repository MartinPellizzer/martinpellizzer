Cryptocarya wrayi

Cryptocarya wrayi, commonly known as the Wray's cryptocarya, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Lauraceae family, which includes laurels and avocado trees. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and fragrant, small white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are drupes, typically containing a single seed, and it is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
