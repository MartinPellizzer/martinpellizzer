Carum verticillatum var. nevadense

Carum verticillatum var. nevadense, commonly known as the Nevada mountain caraway, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, often in mountainous regions. Its seeds are used in traditional herbal medicine for their carminative and digestive properties.

The species is closely related to other members of the Apiaceae family, sharing similar structural and chemical features.
