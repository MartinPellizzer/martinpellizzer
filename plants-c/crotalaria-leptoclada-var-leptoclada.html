Crotalaria leptoclada var. leptoclada

Crotalaria leptoclada var. leptoclada, commonly known as the slender crownflower, is a member of the Fabaceae family and is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an annual herb, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, and prefers well-drained soils. It has a taproot system and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is sometimes cultivated for both aesthetic and traditional uses.
