Calea tricephala

Calea tricephala, commonly known as the three-headed calea, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its striking, three-lobed inflorescence that resembles a crown. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sun exposure.

Calea tricephala is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
