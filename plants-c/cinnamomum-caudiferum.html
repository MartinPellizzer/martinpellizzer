Cinnamomum caudiferum

Cinnamomum caudiferum, commonly known as the cinnamon tree, is a member of the Lauraceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and bark, which contain essential oils responsible for its distinctive scent. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are elliptical, glossy, and have a strong cinnamon aroma when crushed.

The tree is valued for its bark, which is used in the production of cinnamon and other medicinal products.
