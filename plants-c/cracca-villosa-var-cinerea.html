Cracca villosa var. cinerea

Cracca villosa var. cinerea, commonly known as the gray cracca, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its hairy foliage and unique coloration make it a notable species in botanical studies and garden cultivation.
