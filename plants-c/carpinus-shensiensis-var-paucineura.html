Carpinus shensiensis var. paucineura

Carpinus shensiensis var. paucineura, commonly known as the Chinese hornbeam, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Betulaceae family and is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green catkins that are wind-pollinated. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often developing shallow fissures with age.

It is typically found in temperate forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
