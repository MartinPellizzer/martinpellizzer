Cinnamomum mollicellum

Cinnamomum mollicellum, commonly known as the silky cinnamon or silky camphor tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy beneath. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by egg-shaped fruits containing a single seed. Its essential oils, derived from the bark and leaves, are valued for their aromatic and medicinal properties.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in perfumery and herbal remedies.
