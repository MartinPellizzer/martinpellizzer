Chaetospora arundinacea

Chaetospora arundinacea, commonly known as the common reed or Phragmites australis, is a tall, perennial grass-like plant that thrives in wetland environments.

It is characterized by its dense, fibrous rhizomes and long, slender leaves that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish spikelets that are clustered in panicles, and it is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize disturbed or waterlogged soils. Chaetospora arundinacea plays an important role in stabilizing shorelines and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.

Its fibrous stalks are also used in traditional crafts and as a source of raw material in some cultures.
