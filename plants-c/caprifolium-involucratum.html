Caprifolium involucratum

Caprifolium involucratum, commonly known as the clustered honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in spring. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and is often found in moist, shaded habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to pollinators.
