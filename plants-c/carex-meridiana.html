Carex meridiana

Carex meridiana, commonly known as the southern sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, strap-shaped leaves that form dense clumps. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikes during the spring and summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp habitats.

Carex meridiana is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
