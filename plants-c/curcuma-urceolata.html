Curcuma urceolata

Curcuma urceolata, commonly known as the horned turmeric, is a perennial herb native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Zingiberaceae family, which includes ginger and other aromatic plants. The plant is characterized by its rhizomatous growth and striking, lance-shaped leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with a distinctive, elongated bract that gives it its common name.

The rhizomes of Curcuma urceolata are used in traditional medicine and as a culinary spice, similar to other curcuma species.
