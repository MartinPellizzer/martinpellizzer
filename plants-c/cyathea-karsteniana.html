Cyathea karsteniana

Cyathea karsteniana, commonly known as the Karsten's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, trunk-like stem and large, feather-like fronds. The plant has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its rhizome system supports the development of its massive, compound leaves, which are often used in traditional medicine.

Cyathea karsteniana plays an important ecological role in humid, shaded environments by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
