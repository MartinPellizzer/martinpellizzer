Cucurbita pepo var. larnacana

Cucurbita pepo var. larnacana, commonly known as the Larnaca gourd, is a variety of the common gourd native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, characterized by its trailing vines and lobed leaves. The plant produces fruits that are typically spherical or elongated, with a thick, hard rind and a pale yellow or green color. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The gourd is often cultivated for ornamental purposes or for use in traditional crafts and culinary applications.
