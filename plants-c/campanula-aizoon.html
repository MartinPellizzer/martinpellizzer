Campanula aizoon

Campanula aizoon, commonly known as the Aizoon bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are usually covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Campanula aizoon is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
