Calathea coriacea

Calathea coriacea, commonly known as the Zebra Plant, is a tropical perennial native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, mottled, and often variegated foliage, which features a mix of green, white, and rust-colored patterns. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that are often described as having a "zebra" pattern due to their distinctive markings. Calathea coriacea prefers bright, indirect light and high humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its unique appearance and ease of care contribute to its popularity among plant enthusiasts.
