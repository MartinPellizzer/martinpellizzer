Cucumis sphaerocarpus

Cucumis sphaerocarpus, commonly known as the globe cucumber, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like cucumbers and melons. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that are typically harvested when fully mature. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, and it thrives in warm, humid climates with well-drained soil.

The globe cucumber is often cultivated for its edible fruit, which has a mild, slightly sweet flavor.
