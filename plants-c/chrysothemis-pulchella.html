Chrysothemis pulchella

Chrysothemis pulchella, commonly known as the golden tawny orchid, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking yellow and orange floral hues. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid to semi-arid environments. Its flowers are borne on slender stems and are often clustered in racemes.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant and unique blooms.
