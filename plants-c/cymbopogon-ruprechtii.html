Cymbopogon ruprechtii

Cymbopogon ruprechtii, commonly known as Ruprecht's citron grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences and is known for its distinct citrus-like fragrance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in open woodlands or grassy areas.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
