Centaurea fulva

Centaurea fulva, commonly known as the yellow star-thistle, is a flowering plant native to Europe, North Africa, and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers and spiny foliage, which are adaptations to its arid and semi-arid habitats. The plant has a taproot system that allows it to survive in dry conditions and compete effectively with native vegetation. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its invasive potential in new regions.

Centaurea fulva is often considered a weed in agricultural and natural areas due to its rapid growth and competitive nature.
