Cryptocarya paucinervia

Cryptocarya paucinervia, commonly known as the narrow-veined cryptocarya, is a member of the Lauraceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and woody structure.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a smooth texture and a dark green upper surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, followed by spherical, woody fruits containing several seeds.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
