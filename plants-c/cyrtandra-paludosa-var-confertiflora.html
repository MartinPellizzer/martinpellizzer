Cyrtandra paludosa var. confertiflora

Cyrtandra paludosa var. confertiflora, commonly known as the swamp cyrtandra, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia and the Pacific.

It is a herbaceous species that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, showcasing its adaptability to moist conditions. The plant features opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of clustered, showy white or pale yellow flowers that attract various pollinators.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.
