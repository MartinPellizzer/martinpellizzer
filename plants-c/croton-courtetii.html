Croton courtetii

Croton courtetii, commonly known as the Congo croton, is a shrub or small tree native to Central Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its thick, often hairy stems and variable leaf shapes. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by distinctive, spiny fruits. Its leaves are typically alternate and may have serrated or lobed edges.
