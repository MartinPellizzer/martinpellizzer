Coelogyne cantonensis

Coelogyne cantonensis, commonly known as the Guangdong orchid, is a species of orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters of up to 20 individual blooms. The plant features long, strap-like leaves and a pseudobulb structure typical of many orchid species. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded forest environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
