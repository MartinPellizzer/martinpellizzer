Crucicaryum

Crucicaryum, commonly known as the crosswort, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its cruciform flowers, which typically have four petals arranged in a cross shape. The plants are generally small herbaceous species, often found in temperate regions. They produce small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Crucicaryum species are known for their ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and are sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
