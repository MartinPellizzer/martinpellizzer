Campanula trachelium var. subsessilis

Campanula trachelium var. subsessilis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically narrow and sessile, giving the plant its specific variety name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, often found in rocky or woodland habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, bell-like blossoms.
