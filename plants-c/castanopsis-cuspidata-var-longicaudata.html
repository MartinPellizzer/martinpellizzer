Castanopsis cuspidata var. longicaudata

Castanopsis cuspidata var. longicaudata, commonly known as the long-tailed tanoak, is a species of evergreen tree native to eastern China.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, beeches, and chestnuts. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and elongated, pointed leaves. Its fruit is a spiny acorn enclosed in a thick, leathery cupule.

This variant is notable for its ecological importance in forest ecosystems and its historical use in traditional medicine.
