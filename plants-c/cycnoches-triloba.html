Cycnoches triloba

Cycnoches triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed water fern, is a unique aquatic plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, delicate fronds that split into three distinct lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it can anchor itself to submerged surfaces. Its reproductive structures, known as sporangia, are located on the underside of the fronds and release spores into the water.

Cycnoches triloba plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms and contributing to water filtration.
