Croton laxiflorus

Croton laxiflorus, commonly known as the African croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often colorful leaves that can range from green to red, yellow, or even variegated patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.

Croton laxiflorus is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
