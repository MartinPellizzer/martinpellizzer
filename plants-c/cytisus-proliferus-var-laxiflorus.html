Cytisus proliferus var. laxiflorus

Cytisus proliferus var. laxiflorus, commonly known as the laxiflorous broom, is a shrub native to the western Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant produces legume-like pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and hardy nature, making it a popular choice in gardens and landscaping.
