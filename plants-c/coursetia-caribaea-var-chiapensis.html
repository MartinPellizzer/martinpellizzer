Coursetia caribaea var. chiapensis

Coursetia caribaea var. chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapan coursetia, is a shrub native to Central America, specifically found in parts of Mexico and Guatemala.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. The plant has woody stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry forest or scrubland habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and potentially endangered species.
