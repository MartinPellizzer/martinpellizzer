Campanula glomerata subsp. hispida

Campanula glomerata subsp.

hispida, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its dense clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer.

The subspecies is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive, rugged appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
