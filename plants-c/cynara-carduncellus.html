Cynara carduncellus

Cynara carduncellus, commonly known as the artichoke thistle, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple flower heads. The plant produces edible buds that are harvested before they fully open, similar to artichokes. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and covered with coarse, bristly hairs.

Cynara carduncellus is also cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and resilience in various climates.
