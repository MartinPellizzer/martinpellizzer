Caesalpinia bonducella var. aequiaculeata

Caesalpinia bonducella var. aequiaculeata, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant produces bright red or orange inflorescences, which are characteristic of the Caesalpinia genus. Its leaves are pinnate, with small, lance-shaped leaflets, and it has a rough, bark-like texture on its stems.

It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in warmer climates.
