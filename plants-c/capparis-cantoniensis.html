Capparis cantoniensis

Capparis cantoniensis, commonly known as the Chinese dogwood or Guangdong dogwood, is a deciduous shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces bright red, berry-like fruits that are edible but often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Capparis cantoniensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in certain regions of China.
