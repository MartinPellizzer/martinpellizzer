Clianthus speciosus var. oxleyi

Clianthus speciosus var. oxleyi, commonly known as the New Zealand mountain daisy, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine areas, thriving in well-drained soils. It has slender, branched stems and small, narrow leaves.

Clianthus speciosus var. oxleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
