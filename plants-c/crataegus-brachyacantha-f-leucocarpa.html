Crataegus brachyacantha f. leucocarpa

Crataegus brachyacantha f. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited hawthorn, is a botanical variant of the hawthorn genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive white or pale yellow fruits, which contrast with the typical red or orange hues of other hawthorn species. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically found in temperate regions. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with serrated edges, and its flowers are white or pink, appearing in clusters.

This form is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
