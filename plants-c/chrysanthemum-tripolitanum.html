Chrysanthemum tripolitanum

Chrysanthemum tripolitanum, commonly known as the Tripolitan chrysanthemum, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its daisy-like flowers, which typically range in color from white to yellow and orange. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional herbal medicine.

Chrysanthemum tripolitanum is a hardy perennial that prefers full sun and is relatively drought-tolerant.
