Carex sabynensis var. sabynensis

Carex sabynensis var. sabynensis, commonly known as Sabyn's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in riparian zones and is adapted to tolerate seasonal flooding.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
