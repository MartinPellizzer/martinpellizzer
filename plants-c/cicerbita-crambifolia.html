Cicerbita crambifolia

Cicerbita crambifolia, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright stems that often root at the nodes, allowing it to spread along the ground. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Cicerbita crambifolia thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.
