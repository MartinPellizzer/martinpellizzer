Campanula stevenii f. alpina

Campanula stevenii f. alpina, commonly known as the alpine bellflower, is a compact, perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Europe.

It features upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, violet-blue flowers, which bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves that are adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and slopes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.
