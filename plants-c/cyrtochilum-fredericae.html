Cyrtochilum fredericae

Cyrtochilum fredericae, commonly known as the Frederica's orchid, is a terrestrial orchid native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structure. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are usually small and fragrant, with a distinctive shape that facilitates specific pollination mechanisms.

Cyrtochilum fredericae is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems as a food source for certain insect pollinators.
