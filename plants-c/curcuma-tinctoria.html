Curcuma tinctoria

Curcuma tinctoria, commonly known as turmeric, is a rhizomatous herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the ginger family (Zingiberaceae) and is characterized by its aromatic, yellow-orange rhizomes. The plant produces tall, narrow leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that are typically not showy. Its rhizomes contain the bioactive compound curcumin, which gives the plant its distinctive color and medicinal properties.

Curcuma tinctoria is widely cultivated for use in food, traditional medicine, and natural dyeing.
