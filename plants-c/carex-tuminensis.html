Carex tuminensis

Carex tuminensis, commonly known as the Tuminensis sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. This perennial herb typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, acidic soils. The plant features grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and small fauna in its native habitat.
