Clerodendrum macrocalycinum

Clerodendrum macrocalycinum, commonly known as the giant flame bush, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces woody capsules as fruit, containing numerous small, flat seeds.

Clerodendrum macrocalycinum is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its vibrant floral display.
