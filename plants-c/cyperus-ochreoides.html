Cyperus ochreoides

Cyperus ochreoides, commonly known as the golden flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its slender, grass-like leaves are often bright green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense spikes.

Cyperus ochreoides is sometimes used in ornamental horticulture due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various wetland environments.
