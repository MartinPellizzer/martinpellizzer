Cucurbita maxima var. ohioensis

Cucurbita maxima var. ohioensis, commonly known as the giant pumpkin, is a cultivar of the species Cucurbita maxima, which belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, round, and often orange-colored fruits that can weigh several hundred pounds. The plant is a herbaceous vine with large, lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in warm climates and requires a long growing season with ample sunlight and water.

This variety is prized for its size and is commonly grown for competitions and culinary use.
