Castanea indica

Castanea indica, commonly known as the Chinese chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its serrated leaves and produces edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, while its fruit is a key component in traditional cuisine.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
