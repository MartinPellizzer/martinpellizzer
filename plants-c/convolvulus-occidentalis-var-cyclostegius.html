Convolvulus occidentalis var. cyclostegius

Convolvulus occidentalis var. cyclostegius, commonly known as the western morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of pink to purple. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and dry slopes.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in the Convolvulaceae family.
