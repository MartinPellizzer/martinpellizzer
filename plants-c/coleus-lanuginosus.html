Coleus lanuginosus

Coleus lanuginosus, commonly known as the woolly coleus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage that gives it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Coleus lanuginosus is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
