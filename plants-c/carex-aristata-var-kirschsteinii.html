Carex aristata var. kirschsteinii

Carex aristata var. kirschsteinii, commonly known as the Kirschstein sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full to partial sun. Its flowering spikes, which appear in late summer, consist of small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in a cylindrical inflorescence.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging growing conditions.
