Cyperus fimbriatus

Cyperus fimbriatus, commonly known as the fringed flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes make it an aggressive weed in aquatic and semi-aquatic habitats.
