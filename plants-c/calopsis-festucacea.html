Calopsis festucacea

Calopsis festucacea, commonly known as the narrowleaf calopsis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, two-lipped flowers. It thrives in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops.
