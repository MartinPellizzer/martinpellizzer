Costus erythrothyrsus

Costus erythrothyrsus, commonly known as the red-threaded costus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Costaceae and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, bract-like flowers. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, arranged in inflorescences at the top of the stem. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

Costus erythrothyrsus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and full sun.
