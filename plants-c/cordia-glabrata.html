Cordia glabrata

Cordia glabrata, commonly known as the smooth cordia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its simple, smooth, oval-shaped leaves and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas.

Cordia glabrata is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
