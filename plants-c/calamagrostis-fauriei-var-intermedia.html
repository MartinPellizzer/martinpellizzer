Calamagrostis fauriei var. intermedia

Calamagrostis fauriei var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate reed grass, is a tall, ornamental grass native to East Asia.

It features slender, silver-green leaves that turn coppery in the fall, adding visual interest throughout the growing season. The plant produces airy, feathery plumes that bloom in late summer, enhancing its aesthetic appeal in gardens. It thrives in moist to wet soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for wetland or bog gardens.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense clumps, contributing to erosion control in aquatic environments.
