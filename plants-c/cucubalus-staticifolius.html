Cucubalus staticifolius

Cucubalus staticifolius, commonly known as the motionless leaf plant, is a unique species native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are said to remain motionless even in windy conditions, a trait that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the dry season. Its botanical structure includes a short, sturdy stem with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green in color.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
