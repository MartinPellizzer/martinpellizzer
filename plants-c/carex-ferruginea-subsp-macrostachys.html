Carex ferruginea subsp. macrostachys

Carex ferruginea subsp.

macrostachys, commonly known as the rust fern sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America. It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces spikes of small, brownish flowers in late summer.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as wetlands, marshes, and stream banks. It has fibrous roots and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with reddish or rusty hues.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils.
