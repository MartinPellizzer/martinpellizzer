Chamaesyce heyneana subsp. galioides

Chamaesyce heyneana subsp.

galioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States. It typically features succulent, fleshy leaves that are narrow and often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers clustered in dense inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy or well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as dunes or disturbed areas.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to coastal and arid environments.
