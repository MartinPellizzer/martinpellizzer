Carex holmiana

Carex holmiana, commonly known as the Holm's sedge, is a perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are often hidden among the foliage.

Carex holmiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in wetland habitats.
