Cyanotis papilionacea var. vaginata

Cyanotis papilionacea var. vaginata, commonly known as the bluebell lily, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet petals and a compact, herbaceous growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens.
