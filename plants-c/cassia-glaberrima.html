Cassia glaberrima

Cassia glaberrima, commonly known as African cassia, is a deciduous shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain flat, dark brown seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.

Cassia glaberrima is also valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.
