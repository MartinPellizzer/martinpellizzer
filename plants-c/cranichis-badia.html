Cranichis badia

Cranichis badia, commonly known as the Badian orchid, is a species of orchid native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa.

It is a terrestrial herb with a compact growth habit, featuring a short, stocky rhizome and a single, erect stem. The plant produces showy, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers that are often adorned with distinctive markings. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, arranged in a basal rosette.

The Badian orchid is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
