Cynanchum nipponicum

Cynanchum nipponicum, commonly known as Japanese dogbane, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by follicles containing silky, white seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
