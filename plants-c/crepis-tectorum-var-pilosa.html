Crepis tectorum var. pilosa

Crepis tectorum var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy wallflower, is a flowering plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, often reddish stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish to orange flowers arranged in loose clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found growing along roadsides, walls, and in gardens.

Crepis tectorum var. pilosa is a perennial species that is relatively easy to cultivate in well-drained soil.
