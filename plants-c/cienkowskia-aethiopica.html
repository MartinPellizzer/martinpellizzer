Cienkowskia aethiopica

Cienkowskia aethiopica, commonly known as the African snowbell, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its striking white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has a woody stem and produces small, oval leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
