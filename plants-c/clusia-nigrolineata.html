Clusia nigrolineata

Clusia nigrolineata, commonly known as the black-lined glorybower, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive black veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Clusia nigrolineata is relatively low maintenance and can tolerate a range of soil conditions, making it a popular choice for gardeners in tropical and subtropical regions.
