Calonemorchis hoffmanii

Calonemorchis hoffmanii, commonly known as Hoffman's ghost orchid, is a rare and elusive orchid species native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its delicate, white or pale pink flowers and its unique growth habit, often growing on the trunks of trees or in shaded, moist environments. The plant has a specialized root system that allows it to absorb nutrients from decaying organic matter in the soil. It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is known for its low reproductive rate, making it highly vulnerable to habitat loss.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, it is considered endangered in many regions.
