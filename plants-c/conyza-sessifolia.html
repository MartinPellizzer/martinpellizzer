Conyza sessifolia

Conyza sessifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf horseweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white to yellowish flowers. It is part of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

Conyza sessifolia is often found in temperate regions and can be invasive in some areas.
