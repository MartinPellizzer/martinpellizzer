Caprifolium brachypodum

Caprifolium brachypodum, commonly known as the short-footed honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Caprifoliaceae family and is characterized by its slender, woody stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that ripens to a dark red or black color, attracting various birds and small mammals.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural habitats.
