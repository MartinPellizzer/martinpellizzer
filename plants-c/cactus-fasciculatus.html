Cactus fasciculatus

Cactus fasciculatus, commonly known as the clustered cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that grow in dense clusters, forming a compact, low-growing plant. The stems are covered in fine, white hairs that help reduce water loss and reflect sunlight. It produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are typically followed by red, fleshy fruits.

This plant is well adapted to harsh desert environments, storing water in its thick stems and being drought-tolerant.
