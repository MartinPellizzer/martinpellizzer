Cordia octandra

Cordia octandra, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical tree native to regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. Its roots and leaves have been traditionally used in herbal medicine for their purported medicinal properties.

The tree is also valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
