Coreocarpus parthenioides var. parthenioides

Coreocarpus parthenioides var. parthenioides, commonly known as the New Zealand broom, is a shrub native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant has slender, upright stems with small, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged in pairs. Its flowers are yellow and pea-like, typical of the legume family, and it produces woody, curved pods that contain seeds.

This species is adapted to coastal and dry environments, showing resilience to harsh conditions.
