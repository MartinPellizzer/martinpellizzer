Cirsium elodes

Cirsium elodes, commonly known as the swamp thistle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features tall, spiny stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height, often growing in dense clusters. The plant produces purple to pinkish flower heads, which are composed of numerous small florets arranged in a composite flower structure. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped, with rough, hairy surfaces and serrated edges.

Cirsium elodes plays an important ecological role by providing food and habitat for various insects and birds.
