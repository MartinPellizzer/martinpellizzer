Crataegus conspecta

Crataegus conspecta, commonly known as the showy hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its ornamental value, featuring bright red berries and clusters of white flowers in spring. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and produces small, thorny branches. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Crataegus conspecta is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and wildlife.
