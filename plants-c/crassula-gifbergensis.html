Crassula gifbergensis

Crassula gifbergensis, commonly known as the Gifberg Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
