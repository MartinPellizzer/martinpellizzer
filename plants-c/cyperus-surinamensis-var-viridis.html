Cyperus surinamensis var. viridis

Cyperus surinamensis var. viridis, commonly known as green flatsedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features slender, triangular stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green in color. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and pond edges, and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its flowering spikes are usually inconspicuous, producing small, three-lobed flowers that are typically wind-pollinated.
