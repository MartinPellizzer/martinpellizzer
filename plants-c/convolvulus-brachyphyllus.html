Convolvulus brachyphyllus

Convolvulus brachyphyllus, commonly known as the dwarf bindweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, characterized by its twining stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, with small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, blooming from late spring to early autumn.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
