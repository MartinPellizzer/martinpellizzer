Cyathea austroamericana

Cyathea austroamericana, commonly known as the southern American tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate fronds. The plant is evergreen and can grow up to several meters in height, making it a prominent feature in tropical forests. Its rhizome system allows it to spread and form dense colonies, contributing to forest understory vegetation.

Cyathea austroamericana plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various forest organisms.
