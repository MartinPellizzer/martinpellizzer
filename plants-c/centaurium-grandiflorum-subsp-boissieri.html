Centaurium grandiflorum subsp. boissieri

Centaurium grandiflorum subsp.

boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's centaury, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender, creeping stems and small, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with five petals, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are typically surrounded by short, leaf-like bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.
