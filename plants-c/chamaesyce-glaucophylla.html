Chamaesyce glaucophylla

Chamaesyce glaucophylla, commonly known as the silverleaf spurge, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a whitish, powdery coating, giving it a silvery appearance. The plant produces tiny, yellow flowers in clusters during the spring and summer months. It is adapted to arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its succulent leaves help it store water, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping.
