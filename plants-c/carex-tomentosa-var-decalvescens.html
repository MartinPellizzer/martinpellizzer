Carex tomentosa var. decalvescens

Carex tomentosa var. decalvescens, commonly known as the woolly sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and grayish-white pubescent foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear greenish-yellow flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
