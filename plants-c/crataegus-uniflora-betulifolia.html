Crataegus uniflora betulifolia

Crataegus uniflora betulifolia, commonly known as the Betleleaf hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and alternate, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are usually 3-5 lobed. The plant produces small, white to pink flowers in early spring, which are clustered in umbels and attract pollinators. Its fruit is a red or orange berry that ripens in the fall, providing food for various wildlife species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
