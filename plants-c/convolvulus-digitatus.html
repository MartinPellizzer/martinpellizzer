Convolvulus digitatus

Convolvulus digitatus, commonly known as the common morning glory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often blooming in the early morning. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.

Convolvulus digitatus is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and runners.
