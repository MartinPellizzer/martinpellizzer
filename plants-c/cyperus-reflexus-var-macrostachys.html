Cyperus reflexus var. macrostachys

Cyperus reflexus var. macrostachys, commonly known as the broad-stemmed flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces spikelets that are often clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it can tolerate fluctuating water levels.

Its botanical structure makes it an important species in ecological studies of wetland flora.
