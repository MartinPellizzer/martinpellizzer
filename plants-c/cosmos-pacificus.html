Cosmos pacificus

Cosmos pacificus, commonly known as the Pacific cosmos, is a flowering plant native to the United States.

It is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant produces fluffy, seed-filled spheres that are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its botanical features include hairy stems and leaves, and it is part of the Asteraceae family.
