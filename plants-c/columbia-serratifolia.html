Columbia serratifolia

Columbia serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated leaf plant, is a species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive serrated or toothed leaf margins, which give it its scientific name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, with a deep green color and a slightly hairy texture.

Columbia serratifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
