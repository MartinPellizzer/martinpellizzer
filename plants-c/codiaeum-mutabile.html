Codiaeum mutabile

Codiaeum mutabile, commonly known as the flame plant or croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful, and often variegated leaves that display shades of red, orange, yellow, and green. The plant belongs to the family Euphorbiaceae and is known for its ability to change leaf color with age, hence its scientific name. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

The leaves are not only ornamental but also have been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
