Clinanthus flammidus

Clinanthus flammidus, commonly known as the flameflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, flame-like red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are elliptical and glossy, providing a contrasting green backdrop to its vibrant blooms.

Clinanthus flammidus is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
