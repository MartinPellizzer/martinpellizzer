Carex douglasii

Carex douglasii, commonly known as Douglas's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in forested or wetland environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex douglasii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
