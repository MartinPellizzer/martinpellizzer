Centaurium erythraea var. grandiflorum

Centaurium erythraea var. grandiflorum, commonly known as the greater centaury, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with slender stems and small, bright green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
