Campanula mugeana

Campanula mugeana, commonly known as the Muge bellflower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and grayish-green in appearance.

Campanula mugeana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
