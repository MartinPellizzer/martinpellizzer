Cymbidium nitidum

Cymbidium nitidum, commonly known as the white orchid or the white cymbidium, is a species of orchid native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in forested areas and is adapted to cool, humid climates. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, and it produces pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients.

Cymbidium nitidum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for floral arrangements.
