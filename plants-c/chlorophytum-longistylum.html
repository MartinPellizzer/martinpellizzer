Chlorophytum longistylum

Chlorophytum longistylum, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are usually dark green with a prominent midrib. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and requires minimal watering. It produces small, white flowers on slender stalks during the growing season.

Chlorophytum longistylum is popular as a houseplant due to its air-purifying qualities and low maintenance requirements.
