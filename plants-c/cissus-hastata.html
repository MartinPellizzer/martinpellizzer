Cissus hastata

Cissus hastata, commonly known as the silver arrow vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its distinctive, arrow-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery or whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible, round berries that are typically red when mature. Cissus hastata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in humid, shady environments.

Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for use as a ground cover or in hanging baskets.
