Chileorebutia napina

Chileorebutia napina, commonly known as the Napina Cactus, is a species of cactus native to Chile.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems covered in dense, white to pale blue spines. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, often in rocky or sandy soils. It produces small, yellow flowers during the spring and summer months.

Chileorebutia napina is a low-maintenance plant well-suited for desert gardens and indoor cultivation.
