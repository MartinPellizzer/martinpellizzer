Clerodendrum thonneri

Clerodendrum thonneri, commonly known as the flame-of-the-forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange inflorescences that resemble flames, which bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial properties.
