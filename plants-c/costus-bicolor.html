Costus bicolor

Costus bicolor, commonly known as the blackroot or redroot costus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and striking, feather-like leaves that are often dark green with a reddish tint. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense spikes, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Costus bicolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
