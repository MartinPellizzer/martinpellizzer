Commiphora tetramera

Commiphora tetramera, commonly known as the African myrrh, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is characterized by its aromatic resinous sap, which has been used traditionally for medicinal and incense purposes. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its small, leathery leaves and yellowish flowers are typical of the genus Commiphora.

The tree is valued not only for its ecological role but also for its economic importance in traditional medicine and perfumery.
