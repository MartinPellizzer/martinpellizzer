Cyperus laxus var. multispicatus

Cyperus laxus var. multispicatus, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its slender, cylindrical stems are often covered in small, scale-like leaves, and it produces clusters of tiny, spike-like inflorescences.

It is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.
