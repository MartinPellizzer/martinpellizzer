Clematis koreana

Clematis koreana, commonly known as the Korean Clematis, is a deciduous vine native to the mountainous regions of South Korea.

It features distinctive, palmately lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, white down. The plant produces showy, fragrant flowers in late spring to early summer, typically in shades of pink and white. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained, fertile soil.

Clematis koreana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant floral display.
