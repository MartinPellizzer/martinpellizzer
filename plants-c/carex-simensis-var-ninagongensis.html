Carex simensis var. ninagongensis

Carex simensis var. ninagongensis, commonly known as the Ninagongensis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their ecological importance in wetland ecosystems. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade. This variant is noted for its ornamental value and potential ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
