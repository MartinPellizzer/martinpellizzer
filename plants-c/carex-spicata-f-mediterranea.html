Carex spicata f. mediterranea

Carex spicata f. mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. This variety is distinguished by its slender, spike-like inflorescence that bears small, greenish flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and marshy areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
