Calamus jacobsii

Calamus jacobsii, commonly known as the Jacobs' calamus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and pinnately compound leaves. It produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish in color.
