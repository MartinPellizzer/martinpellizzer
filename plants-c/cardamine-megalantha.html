Cardamine megalantha

Cardamine megalantha, commonly known as the giant watercress, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many edible and medicinal plants. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small white or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as stream banks and wetlands.

Its leaves are often used in traditional cuisines and have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.
