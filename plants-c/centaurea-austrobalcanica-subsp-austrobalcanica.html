Centaurea austrobalcanica subsp. austrobalcanica

Centaurea austrobalcanica subsp.

austrobalcanica, commonly known as the Southern Balkan centaury, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Southern Balkans. It features upright, branched stems that are typically covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces vibrant blue to purple flowers in composite-like inflorescences, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with serrated edges.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or grassy habitats.
