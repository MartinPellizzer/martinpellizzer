Cassia multijuga var. lindleyana

Cassia multijuga var. lindleyana, commonly known as golden shower tree or sika-sika, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in hanging clusters, typically during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets, and its bark is smooth and often reddish in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The seeds are encased in long, slender pods and are dispersed by wind.
