Casuarina collina

Casuarina collina, commonly known as the narrow-leaved she-oak, is a species of evergreen shrub or small tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves and distinctive, cone-like fruits. The plant belongs to the family Casuarinaceae and is adapted to coastal and sandy soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.

Casuarina collina is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping.
