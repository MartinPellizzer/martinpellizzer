Centaurea × maritima

Centaurea × maritima, commonly known as the sea holly, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue to purple flower heads, which are surrounded by spiny, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its flowers bloom from summer to autumn, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Centaurea × maritima is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
