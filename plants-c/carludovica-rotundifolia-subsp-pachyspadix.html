Carludovica rotundifolia subsp. pachyspadix

Carludovica rotundifolia subsp.

pachyspadix, commonly known as the giant bromeliad, is a striking plant native to the rainforests of Central and South America. It is characterized by its large, thick, and fleshy leaves that form a rosette shape, capable of storing significant amounts of water.

The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with showy, often red or orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are typically broad and rounded, with a waxy surface that aids in water retention.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
