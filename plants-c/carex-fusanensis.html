Carex fusanensis

Carex fusanensis, commonly known as the Mongolian sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of lakes. Its morphology features triangular stems and grass-like leaves, with small, clustered flowers that are usually green or brown.

Carex fusanensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates and small aquatic organisms.
