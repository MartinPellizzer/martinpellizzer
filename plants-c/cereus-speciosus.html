Cereus speciosus

Cereus speciosus, commonly known as the showy cactus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tall, cylindrical stem and is known for its striking, large white flowers that bloom at night. The plant is a member of the Cactaceae family and is adapted to arid environments. Its spines are modified leaves, and it stores water in its thick, fleshy stem.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nocturnal blooms.
