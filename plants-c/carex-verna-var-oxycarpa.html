Carex verna var. oxycarpa

Carex verna var. oxycarpa, commonly known as the early sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, cylindrical leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
