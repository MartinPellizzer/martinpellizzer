Citrosma lindenii

Citrosma lindenii, commonly known as the Malabar nut or Indian nut, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The tree produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed, which is encased in a hard, woody shell. Citrosma lindenii is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional Ayurvedic practices.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and pharmacological contexts.
