Crataegus pringlei

Crataegus pringlei, commonly known as Pringle's hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate to elliptic leaves, and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
