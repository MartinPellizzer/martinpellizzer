Carex michelii var. vitekii

Carex michelii var. vitekii, commonly known as the Vitek's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated. This variety is native to specific regions in North America, particularly in the western United States. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, streambanks, and other riparian habitats.

The plant's botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting diverse wildlife and contributing to soil stabilization.
