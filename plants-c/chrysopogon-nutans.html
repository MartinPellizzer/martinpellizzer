Chrysopogon nutans

Chrysopogon nutans, commonly known as the drooping grass or slender bluemink grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are typically blue or purple in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Chrysopogon nutans is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its delicate, grass-like foliage.
