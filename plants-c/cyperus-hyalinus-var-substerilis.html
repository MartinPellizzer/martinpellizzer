Cyperus hyalinus var. substerilis

Cyperus hyalinus var. substerilis, commonly known as the silverleaf flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in saturated soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a waxy coating, giving it a distinctive silvery sheen.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are usually inconspicuous, and the plant is noted for its ability to tolerate a wide range of environmental conditions.
