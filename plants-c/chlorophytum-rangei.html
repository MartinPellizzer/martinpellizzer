Chlorophytum rangei

Chlorophytum rangei, commonly known as the snake plant or mother-in-law's tongue, is a tropical perennial plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its upright, sword-shaped leaves that are typically green with white or yellow variegation. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low light conditions and requires minimal water, making it an ideal houseplant. Chlorophytum rangei is also noted for its air-purifying qualities, as it can absorb toxins from the air.

Its resilience and aesthetic appeal make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
