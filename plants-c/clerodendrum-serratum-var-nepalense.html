Clerodendrum serratum var. nepalense

Clerodendrum serratum var. nepalense, commonly known as the Nepal glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal and northern India.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in summer. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its showy bracts, which are often mistaken for petals.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
