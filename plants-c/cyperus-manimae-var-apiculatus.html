Cyperus manimae var. apiculatus

Cyperus manimae var. apiculatus, commonly known as the Manimae sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower heads. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist soil conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems and a subject of study in plant taxonomy.
