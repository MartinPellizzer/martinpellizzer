Coffea arabica var. rachiformis

Coffea arabica var. rachiformis, commonly known as the Ethiopian rachis coffee, is a rare variant of the arabica coffee species.

It is characterized by its unique branching pattern of coffee cherries, which gives it its name. This variety is native to Ethiopia and is known for its distinct flavor profile. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions with a tropical climate.

Due to its limited distribution and genetic uniqueness, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical and agricultural research.
