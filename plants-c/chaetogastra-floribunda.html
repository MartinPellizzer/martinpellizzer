Chaetogastra floribunda

Chaetogastra floribunda, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false nettle, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture and its clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Urticaceae, which includes other members known for their stinging hairs.
