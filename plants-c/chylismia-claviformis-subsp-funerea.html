Chylismia claviformis subsp. funerea

Chylismia claviformis subsp.

funerea, commonly known as the black-leafed chylismia, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its dark, almost black foliage, which contrasts sharply with its pale green stems.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding a delicate touch to its dark appearance. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
