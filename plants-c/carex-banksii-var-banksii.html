Carex banksii var. banksii

Carex banksii var. banksii, commonly known as the Banks' sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern North America.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually dark green and stiff, with a distinctive triangular cross-section. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear wind-pollinated flowers.

Carex banksii var. banksii is valued in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
