Carex vallicola

Carex vallicola, commonly known as the valley sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, stream banks, and forested areas. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive blue-green coloration.

Carex vallicola produces small, clustered spikelets that bloom in late spring to early summer.
