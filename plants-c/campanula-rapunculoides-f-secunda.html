Campanula rapunculoides f. secunda

Campanula rapunculoides f. secunda, commonly known as the second form of the bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lanceolate, with a slightly hairy surface, and the plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and along stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in gardens.
