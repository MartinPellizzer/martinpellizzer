Cochlearia saxatilis subf. diminuta

Cochlearia saxatilis subf.

diminuta, commonly known as the dwarf rock scabious, is a low-growing perennial plant native to coastal regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact rosette of narrow, grayish-green leaves and produces small, violet-blue flowers in early summer.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats. It is well adapted to harsh environments, with a deep root system that helps it withstand wind and salt spray.

This sub-species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
