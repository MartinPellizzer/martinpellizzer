Crataegus septempartita

Crataegus septempartita, commonly known as the seven-parted hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, simple leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of small white flowers that appear in spring. The plant produces red to orange berries in the fall, which are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
