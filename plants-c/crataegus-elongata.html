Crataegus elongata

Crataegus elongata, commonly known as the elongated hawthorn, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its thorny branches, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is often found in woodland edges, thickets, and disturbed soils.
