Commelina linearis var. longispatha

Commelina linearis var. longispatha, commonly known as the long-spurred commelina, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Commelinaceae and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and striking blue-violet flowers. The inflorescence consists of a spadix surrounded by bracts, with the flowers having elongated spurs that give the species its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world as a garden plant.
