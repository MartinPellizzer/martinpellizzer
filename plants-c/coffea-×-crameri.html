Coffea × crameri

Coffea × crameri, commonly known as the Cramer's coffee.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Coffea arabica and Coffea canephora. This plant is characterized by its evergreen nature and produces small, oval-shaped berries that contain the coffee beans. The leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a leathery texture.

It is primarily cultivated for its aromatic and flavorful coffee beans, which are used in specialty coffee blends.
