Christia parviflora

Christia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered christia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, small flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or white. The plant has compound leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds. It is often found in dry, open habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and somewhat endangered species in its native range.
