Campanula pseudolanceolata f. transsilvanica

Campanula pseudolanceolata f. transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in garden settings.
