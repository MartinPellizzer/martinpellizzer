Chlorocyperus inflexus

Chlorocyperus inflexus, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikerush, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces cylindrical spikelets that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense colonies in suitable habitats.

Chlorocyperus inflexus plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
