Chenopodium quinoa f. purpureum

Chenopodium quinoa f. purpureum, commonly known as purple quinoa, is a cultivar of the Andean grain quinoa characterized by its striking purple foliage and inflorescence.

This plant belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is native to the Andes region of South America. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. The plant produces small, edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients, making it a valuable crop.

Its ornamental value, combined with its nutritional benefits, makes it both a functional and aesthetic plant in agricultural and horticultural settings.
