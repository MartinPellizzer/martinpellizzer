Calceolaria ternata

Calceolaria ternata, commonly known as the three-lobed slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are often brightly colored. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forests or rocky areas. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette.

Calceolaria ternata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive floral display.
