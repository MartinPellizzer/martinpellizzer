Copiapoa coquimbana var. wagenknechtii

Copiapoa coquimbana var. wagenknechtii, commonly known as the Wagenknecht's copiapoa, is a cactus native to the Atacama Desert in Chile.

It is a columnar cactus with a tall, cylindrical stem that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces white to pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It has a dense covering of spines that protect it from herbivores and intense sunlight.

This variety is notable for its slow growth and ability to survive in extremely arid conditions.
