Conyza cordata

Conyza cordata, commonly known as the narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in open woods, prairies, and along roadsides. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its wide distribution.

Conyza cordata is characterized by its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and light conditions.
