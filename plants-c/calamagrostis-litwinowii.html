Calamagrostis litwinowii

Calamagrostis litwinowii, commonly known as the Siberian reed, is a tall, perennial grass native to the colder regions of Asia.

It features slender, cylindrical stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often forming dense clumps. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, typically in late summer to early autumn. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds ornamental value.

It is well adapted to wet or moist soils and is often found in marshy areas or along riverbanks.
