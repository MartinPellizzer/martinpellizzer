Carex teretiuscula var. ampla

Carex teretiuscula var. ampla, commonly known as the broad-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, ribbon-shaped leaves that are typically green and slightly glossy. This plant prefers moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are typically arranged in a loose, cylindrical cluster.

The plant is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
