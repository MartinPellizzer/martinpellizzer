Cuphea rivularis

Cuphea rivularis, commonly known as the river cuphea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to pink, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its foliage is usually dark green and slightly hairy, contributing to its ornamental value.

Cuphea rivularis is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and ability to thrive in partial shade.
