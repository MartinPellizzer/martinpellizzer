Christella sophoroides

Christella sophoroides, commonly known as the Indian wisteria, is a deciduous climbing shrub native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features slender, twining stems that can reach lengths of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are typically violet or blue, arranged in hanging clusters, and bloom in the spring.

The leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
