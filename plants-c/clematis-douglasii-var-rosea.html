Clematis douglasii var. rosea

Clematis douglasii var. rosea, commonly known as the rose-flowered Clematis, is a deciduous climbing vine native to the western United States.

It features showy, semi-double to fully double pink to lilac flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound, trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and a woody, twining stem that supports its climbing habit. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and its ability to attract pollinators.
