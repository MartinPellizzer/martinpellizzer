Cyananthus pilifolius

Cyananthus pilifolius, commonly known as the silky bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a striking contrast between the petals and the hairy, leafy stems. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

Cyananthus pilifolius is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
