Cyperus grammicus

Cyperus grammicus, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family, Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
