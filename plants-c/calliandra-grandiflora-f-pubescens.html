Calliandra grandiflora f. pubescens

Calliandra grandiflora f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy calliandra, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, pink or red flowers that bloom in dense, branching clusters. The plant has pinnate leaves composed of numerous small, oval-shaped leaflets. Its hairy texture, particularly on the stems and young leaves, is a distinguishing feature of this variety.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.
