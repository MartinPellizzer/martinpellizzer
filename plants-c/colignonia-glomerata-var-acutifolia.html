Colignonia glomerata var. acutifolia

Colignonia glomerata var. acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved colignonia, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It features wiry, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple. The leaves are narrow, lance-shaped, and have a slightly serrated margin, contributing to its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its botanical characteristics make it an attractive choice for gardeners interested in native flora.
