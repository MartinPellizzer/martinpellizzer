Crepinella umbellata

Crepinella umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Crepinella umbellata is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and ability to attract pollinators.
