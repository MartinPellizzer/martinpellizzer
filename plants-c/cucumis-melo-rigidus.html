Cucumis melo rigidus

Cucumis melo rigidus, commonly known as the Persian melon, is a species of melon native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is a herbaceous, annual plant that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces large, elongated fruits with a thick, green rind and a sweet, aromatic flesh. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on the same plant.

The botanical characteristics of Cucumis melo rigidus make it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.
