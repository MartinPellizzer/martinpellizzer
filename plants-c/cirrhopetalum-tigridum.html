Cirrhopetalum tigridum

Cirrhopetalum tigridum, commonly known as the tiger orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its striking, colorful flowers with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its flowers are known for their vibrant hues and unique, tiger-like markings, which aid in attracting pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
