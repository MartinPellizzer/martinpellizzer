Carex heterolepis var. zarubinii

Carex heterolepis var. zarubinii, commonly known as Zarubin's sedge, is a variant of the common sedge species native to eastern North America.

It is a perennial herb with grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly curled at the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
