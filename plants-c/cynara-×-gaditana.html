Cynara × gaditana

Cynara × gaditana, commonly known as the Spanish artichoke, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Cynara cardunculus and Cynara scolymus.

It is a robust perennial shrub characterized by its large, spiny leaves and purple-tinged stems. The plant produces large, globe-shaped flower heads that are typically harvested before they fully open. Its botanical features include thick, fibrous leaves and a strong, aromatic scent.

It is cultivated for its edible flower buds, which are used in various culinary traditions across Europe.
