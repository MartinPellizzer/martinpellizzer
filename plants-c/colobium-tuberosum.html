Colobium tuberosum

Colobium tuberosum, commonly known as the tuberous colobium, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its tuberous roots, which store nutrients and water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its leaves are alternate and ovate, with a rough texture.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
