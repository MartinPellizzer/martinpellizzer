Chenopodium murale var. albescens

Chenopodium murale var. albescens, commonly known as white goosefoot, is a flowering plant in the family Amaranthaceae.

It is an annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and arid environments. The plant has alternate, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white coating, giving it its common name. Its small, green flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, dry fruits called utricles.

This species is known for its adaptability and is often found in regions with low moisture and high sunlight.
