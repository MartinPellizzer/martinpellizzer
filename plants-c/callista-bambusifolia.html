Callista bambusifolia

Callista bambusifolia, commonly known as the bamboo leaf callista, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stems and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Callista bambusifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
