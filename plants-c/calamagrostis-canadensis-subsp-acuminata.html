Calamagrostis canadensis subsp. acuminata

Calamagrostis canadensis subsp.

acuminata, commonly known as Canadian flat grass or narrowleaf bluejoint, is a perennial grass native to North America. It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
