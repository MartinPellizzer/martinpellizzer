Chromolaena tamaulipasensis

Chromolaena tamaulipasensis, commonly known as the Tamaulipas croton, is a shrub native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers, followed by capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in disturbed or open habitats.

Due to its rapid growth, it can become invasive in certain regions.
