Cyathula natalensis

Cyathula natalensis, commonly known as Natal spurge, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family, which is characterized by its unique floral structures and often milky sap. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are clustered in cyathia, a cup-like structure typical of the genus.

Cyathula natalensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
