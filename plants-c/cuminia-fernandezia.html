Cuminia fernandezia

Cuminia fernandezia, commonly known as the Fernandezia cumin fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that exhibit a distinctive bi-color pattern, often featuring a mix of green and silver hues. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical or subtropical forests. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing cones, are positioned along the fronds, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Due to habitat loss, Cuminia fernandezia is considered endangered, making conservation efforts crucial for its survival.
