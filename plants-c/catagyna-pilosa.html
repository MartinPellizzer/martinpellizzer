Catagyna pilosa

Catagyna pilosa, commonly known as the hairy catagyna, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
