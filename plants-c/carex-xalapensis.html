Carex xalapensis

Carex xalapensis, commonly known as the Mexican sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, grasses, and reeds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically green or brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Carex xalapensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in aquatic and riparian habitats.
