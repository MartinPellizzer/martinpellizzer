Carex yushuensis

Carex yushuensis, commonly known as Yushu sedge, is a perennial plant native to the Qinghai-Tibet Plateau in China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cold and arid conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Yushu sedge plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
