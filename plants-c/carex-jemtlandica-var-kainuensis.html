Carex jemtlandica var. kainuensis

Carex jemtlandica var. kainuensis, commonly known as the Kainuensis sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to northern Europe.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, rushes, and grass-like plants. This variety is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wildlife.
