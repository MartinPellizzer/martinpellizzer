Cerasus demissa var. melanocarpa

Cerasus demissa var. melanocarpa, commonly known as the black cherry, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Prunus genus and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, edible berries that turn black when mature. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its flowers are white and appear in early spring, preceding the foliage, and it is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its role in wildlife habitats and as a source of food for both birds and small mammals.
