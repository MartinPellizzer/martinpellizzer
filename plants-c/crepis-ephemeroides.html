Crepis ephemeroides

Crepis ephemeroides, commonly known as the ephemeral hawk's-beard, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a small annual herb that typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats. The plant produces yellow composite flowers that are arranged in a single head, surrounded by bracts. Its leaves are usually hairy and alternate, with a distinctive lobed or divided shape.

Crepis ephemeroides is known for its short life cycle, often completing its growth and reproduction within a single growing season.
