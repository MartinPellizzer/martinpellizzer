Chascanum cuneifolium

Chascanum cuneifolium, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens in temperate climates.
