Cyperus tortuosus

Cyperus tortuosus, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps and floodplains, and can thrive in both freshwater and brackish environments. Cyperus tortuosus produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are clustered in inflorescences.

It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive, zigzagging growth pattern and adaptability to various soil conditions.
