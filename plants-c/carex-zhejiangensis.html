Carex zhejiangensis

Carex zhejiangensis, commonly known as Zhejiang sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to eastern China.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in wet garden environments.
