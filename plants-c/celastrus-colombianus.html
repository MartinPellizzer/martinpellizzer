Celastrus colombianus

Celastrus colombianus, commonly known as the Colombian staff-vine, is a climbing shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Celastraceae and is characterized by its woody stems and opposite, simple leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright orange or red berries containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value and medicinal potential have made it of interest in both horticulture and pharmacological research.
