Cirrhopetalum striatum

Cirrhopetalum striatum, commonly known as the striped bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It features striking, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple with distinctive white or pale stripes. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine regions.

Its unique floral structure makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine collections.
