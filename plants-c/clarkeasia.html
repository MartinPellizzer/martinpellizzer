Clarkeasia

Clarkeasia, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical Africa.

These plants are characterized by their velvety, waxy leaves and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. Clarkeasia species typically have a compact growth habit, making them popular as indoor houseplants. They prefer bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

The genus includes several species, such as Clarkeasia balfourii and Clarkeasia hirta, which are prized for their ornamental value.
