Cyclostigma denticulatum

Cyclostigma denticulatum, commonly known as the toothed cyclostigma, is a unique flowering plant native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, denticulate (toothed) leaves that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species in the botanical world.
