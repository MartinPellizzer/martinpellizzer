Clusia punctata

Clusia punctata, commonly known as the waxleaf clusia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Clusia punctata is also known for its ability to tolerate shade and poor soil conditions, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
