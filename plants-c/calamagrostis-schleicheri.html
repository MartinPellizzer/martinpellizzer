Calamagrostis schleicheri

Calamagrostis schleicheri, commonly known as the Schleicher's reed, is a perennial grass native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, typical of the grass family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along water bodies.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland gardens and ecological restoration projects.
