Chupalon latifolium

Chupalon latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf chupalon, is a perennial herb native to the temperate regions of Southeast Asia.

It features a robust root system and lanceolate, broad leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, which bloom in the spring. Its foliage is valued for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine.

Chupalon latifolium thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial shade.
