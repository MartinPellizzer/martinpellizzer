Campanula pusilla f. umbrosa

Campanula pusilla f. umbrosa, commonly known as the shade-loving bellflower, is a compact perennial herb native to woodland areas of Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

Due to its delicate appearance and ability to thrive in shaded environments, it is a popular choice for woodland gardens.
