Critoniopsis weberbaueri

Critoniopsis weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer's cactus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cactaceae.

It is native to the arid regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. This plant is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in spines and its clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and cactus enthusiasts alike.
