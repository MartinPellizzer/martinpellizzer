Crepis ramosissima

Crepis ramosissima, commonly known as the hairy goat's beard, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its common name.

The plant produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind, aiding in its spread across different habitats.
