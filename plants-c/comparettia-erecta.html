Comparettia erecta

Comparettia erecta, commonly known as the erect comparettia, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and striking, showy flowers. The plant features strap-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a velvety appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange with a dark red or purple lip, making it a visually appealing ornamental plant.

Comparettia erecta thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistently moist, well-draining soil.
