Cinnamomum oleifolium

Cinnamomum oleifolium, commonly known as the mountain cinnamon or wild cinnamon, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces dark brown, aromatic seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. Its essential oils, extracted from the leaves and bark, are valued for their medicinal and culinary properties.

Cinnamomum oleifolium thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forested areas.
