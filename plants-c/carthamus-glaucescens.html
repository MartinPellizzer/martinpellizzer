Carthamus glaucescens

Carthamus glaucescens, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
