Cassuvium solitarium

Cassuvium solitarium, commonly known as the lone blossom, is a rare flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It features striking violet petals with a unique iridescent sheen that changes color depending on the light. The plant has a short, sturdy stem with a single, large flower that blooms only once a year. Its leaves are broad and waxy, adapted to retain moisture in its arid environment.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is classified as an endangered species.
