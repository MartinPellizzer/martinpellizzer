Centaurea biformis var. oscilans

Centaurea biformis var. oscilans, commonly known as the oscillating two-lobed centaurea, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect, sparsely branched stems and vibrant blue to purple flower heads. The plant produces small, bracteate flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are usually linear to lanceolate, with a rough, hairy texture.

This variety is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.
