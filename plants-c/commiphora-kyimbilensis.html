Commiphora kyimbilensis

Commiphora kyimbilensis, commonly known as the Kyimbil resin tree, is a species native to eastern Africa, particularly found in countries like Kenya and Tanzania.

It belongs to the Burseraceae family, which includes frankincense and myrrh trees, and is characterized by its aromatic resins. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, often with smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The tree is valued for its resin, which has been used traditionally for medicinal and aromatic purposes.
