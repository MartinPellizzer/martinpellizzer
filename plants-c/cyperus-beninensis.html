Cyperus beninensis

Cyperus beninensis, commonly known as the Benin flatsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family. It is often found in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of water bodies.

Cyperus beninensis is valued for its ornamental potential and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
