Clavena canariensis

Clavena canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Islands heath, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant features small, evergreen leaves and produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
