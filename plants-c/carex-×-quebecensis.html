Carex × quebecensis

Carex × quebecensis, commonly known as the Quebec sedge, is a hybrid plant in the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces spikes of small, greenish flowers. Native to eastern North America, it thrives in wetland environments such as marshes and along stream edges. The plant is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and filter water.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems.
