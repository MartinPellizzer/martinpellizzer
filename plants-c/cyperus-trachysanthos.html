Cyperus trachysanthos

Cyperus trachysanthos, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flatsedge, is a species of sedge native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a robust root system. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in wet or moist environments, often found in marshes, swamps, and along water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
