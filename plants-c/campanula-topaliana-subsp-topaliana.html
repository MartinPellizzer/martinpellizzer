Campanula topaliana subsp. topaliana

Campanula topaliana subsp.

topaliana, commonly known as the Alpine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bell-shaped blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often glandular and slightly hairy.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
