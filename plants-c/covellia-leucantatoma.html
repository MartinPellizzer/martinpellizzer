Covellia leucantatoma

Covellia leucantatoma, commonly known as the white-veined orchid, is a unique member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, pale green leaves and striking white veins that contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers with a distinctive white coloration and a subtle scent. It is native to tropical regions and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable but endangered species in its natural range.
