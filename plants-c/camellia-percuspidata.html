Camellia percuspidata

Camellia percuspidata, commonly known as the Japanese camellia, is a flowering plant native to Japan and Korea.

It is a member of the Theaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and striking, showy flowers. The flowers, which bloom in winter and early spring, come in a range of colors including red, pink, and white. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic value and cold hardiness.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.
