Coelogyne dewindtiana

Coelogyne dewindtiana, commonly known as the Dewindt's orchid, is a tropical epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It features a pseudobulb with a single leaf and produces intricate, fragrant flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant typically blooms during the dry season, often in clusters of 5 to 15 flowers. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture from the air and rain, and it thrives in bright, indirect light.

Coelogyne dewindtiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated by orchid enthusiasts.
