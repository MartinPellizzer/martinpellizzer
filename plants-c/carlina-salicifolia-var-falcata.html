Carlina salicifolia var. falcata

Carlina salicifolia var. falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved cudweed, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in composite flower heads, which are characteristic of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and habitat conservation.
