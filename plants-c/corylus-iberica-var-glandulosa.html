Corylus iberica var. glandulosa

Corylus iberica var. glandulosa, commonly known as the Iberian hazel, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Betulaceae and is characterized by its deciduous leaves and clustered, nut-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring, followed by oval-shaped nuts enclosed in a spiny husk. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The variety is distinguished by the presence of glandular structures on its leaves and stems.
