Cabralea poeppigii

Cabralea poeppigii, commonly known as the "Brazilea," is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, moist environments. The plant features small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture.

Cabralea poeppigii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
