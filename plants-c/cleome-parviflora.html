Cleome parviflora

Cleome parviflora, commonly known as the smallflower spiderplant, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Cleomaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, showy flowers. The plant produces yellow or white flowers that are often clustered in groups of three, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it a hardy addition to gardens.

Cleome parviflora is also noted for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
