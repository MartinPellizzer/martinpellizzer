Crataegus perlaeta

Crataegus perlaeta, commonly known as the silverthorn, is a species of hawthorn native to the eastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its thorny branches, oval-shaped leaves, and clusters of white flowers that appear in the spring. Its fruit is a red or orange berry that ripens in the fall, providing food for birds and small mammals.

The silverthorn is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
