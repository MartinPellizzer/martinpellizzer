Cadaba trifoliata

Cadaba trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf spurge or African violet.

It is a shrub native to southern Africa, characterized by its fleshy, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires moderate sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Its unique foliage and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
