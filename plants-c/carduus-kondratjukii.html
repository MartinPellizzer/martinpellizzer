Carduus kondratjukii

Carduus kondratjukii, commonly known as Kondratjuk's thistle, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features spiny leaves and a tall, upright stem that can reach up to two meters in height. Its flowers are typically purple or pink, blooming in the summer months.

Carduus kondratjukii is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in rocky soils and high altitudes.
