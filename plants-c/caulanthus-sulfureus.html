Caulanthus sulfureus

Caulanthus sulfureus, commonly known as the sulfur curlcup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, yellow flowers and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal and inland habitats. Its leaves are narrow and sometimes curl at the edges, giving it its common name.

Caulanthus sulfureus is known for its resilience to harsh environmental conditions.
