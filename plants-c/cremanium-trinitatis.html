Cremanium trinitatis

Cremanium trinitatis, commonly known as the Trinidadian nightshade, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean island of Trinidad.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like tomatoes and peppers. The plant produces small, violet-colored flowers that bloom at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The fruit is a small, yellowish berry that ripens in late summer and is sometimes used in local traditional medicine.
