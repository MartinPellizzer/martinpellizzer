Calophyllum pinetorum

Calophyllum pinetorum, commonly known as the pinetree or pinetum, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. The tree is known for its ornamental value, with its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in humid climates. Its fruit is a hard, woody capsule that contains seeds used in traditional medicine.

Calophyllum pinetorum is also valued for its durable timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
