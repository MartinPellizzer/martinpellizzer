Clerodendrum brookeanum

Clerodendrum brookeanum, commonly known as the Brooke's clerodendrum, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the summer months. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
