Campanulopsis

Campanulopsis, commonly known as the bellflower, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

These plants are characterized by their bell-shaped or tubular flowers, which are typically blue, violet, or white in color. Campanulopsis species are often found in temperate regions and thrive in well-drained soils. They are perennial herbs that produce showy blooms during the summer months.

Many species are cultivated in gardens for their ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
