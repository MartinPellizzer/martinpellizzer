Cicca pavoniana

Cicca pavoniana, commonly known as the peacock flower, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy, and colorful bracts that resemble the tail of a peacock, giving it its common name. The plant belongs to the family Araceae and is known for its unique inflorescence, which consists of a spadix surrounded by a broad, colorful spathe. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Cicca pavoniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
