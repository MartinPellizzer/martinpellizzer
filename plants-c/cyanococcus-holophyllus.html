Cyanococcus holophyllus

Cyanococcus holophyllus, commonly known as the blue-green cactus, is a unique succulent native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its ability to store water in its fleshy stems. The plant produces small, pale blue or green flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a subtle color contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are typically reduced to spines, which help reduce water loss and deter herbivores.

Cyanococcus holophyllus is well adapted to harsh, dry environments and is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils.
