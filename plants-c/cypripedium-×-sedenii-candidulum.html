Cypripedium × sedenii candidulum

Cypripedium × sedenii candidulum, commonly known as the white lady's slipper, is a hybrid orchid native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking white petals and a distinctive slipper-shaped pouch, which is a feature common among orchids in the Cypripedium genus. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist forest floors and requires specific conditions to thrive. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly bees, and it is considered a rare and endangered species due to habitat loss.

The plant's botanical significance lies in its role in both ecological systems and horticultural conservation efforts.
