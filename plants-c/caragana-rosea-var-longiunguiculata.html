Caragana rosea var. longiunguiculata

Caragana rosea var. longiunguiculata, commonly known as the long-leaf caragana, is a shrub native to central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often twice-pinnate, giving it a delicate appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in racemate clusters, which bloom in early summer. Its root system is deep and drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to arid environments.

It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive foliage and hardy nature.
