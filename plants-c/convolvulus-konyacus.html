Convolvulus konyacus

Convolvulus konyacus, commonly known as Konya bindweed, is a flowering plant native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East.

It is a trailing or climbing annual herb with slender stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, funnel-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow or white, with five petals. Its leaves are heart-shaped and opposite, and it thrives in well-drained soils in full sun.

It is often found in disturbed areas, along roadsides, and in dry grasslands.
