Centaurea triumfettii subsp. stricta

Centaurea triumfettii subsp.

stricta, commonly known as the narrowleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It typically grows in dry, sunny habitats like grasslands and rocky soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and wildflower displays.
