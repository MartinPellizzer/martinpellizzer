Cynoglossum grande

Cynoglossum grande, commonly known as hare's foot, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that resemble a hare's foot, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of purple or blue flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Cynoglossum grande is often grown as an ornamental plant in gardens due to its attractive foliage and flowers.
