Chaenomeles japonica f. tricolor

Chaenomeles japonica f. tricolor, commonly known as the Japanese flowering quince with tricolor variegation, is a hybrid plant characterized by its striking variegated foliage.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is native to Japan, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant features showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring, often in shades of pink, white, and yellow. Its leaves are typically variegated with white, yellow, and green patterns, adding visual interest throughout the growing season.

This ornamental plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
