Crataegus pallens

Crataegus pallens, commonly known as the white thornapple, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its thorny stems and showy white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Crataegus pallens typically grows in open woodlands and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for wildlife, providing food and shelter for various birds and insects.
