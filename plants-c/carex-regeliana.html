Carex regeliana

Carex regeliana, commonly known as the Regel's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Eurasia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically flowering in the summer months. Carex regeliana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tundra and mountainous habitats.

Its ability to tolerate cold climates makes it an important component of high-altitude and polar ecosystems.
