Campanopsis acaulis

Campanopsis acaulis, commonly known as the naked-stem bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, unbranched stem that is often hairless, giving it its scientific name "acaulis," meaning "without a stem.". The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in terminal clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Campanopsis acaulis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
