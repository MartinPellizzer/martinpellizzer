Cytisus lamarckii var. stabianus

Cytisus lamarckii var. stabianus, commonly known as the Stabian broom, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The leaves are trifoliate, consisting of three oval-shaped leaflets, and are typically covered in fine hairs. This variety is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its hardy nature.
