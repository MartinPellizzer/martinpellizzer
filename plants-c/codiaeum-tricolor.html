Codiaeum tricolor

Codiaeum tricolor, commonly known as the tri-color croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, multicolored leaves that display shades of green, yellow, and red. The plant belongs to the Euphorbiaceae family and is known for its striking foliage rather than its flowers. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires bright, indirect light to maintain its vivid coloration.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant and in gardens.
