Cucumis bisexualis

Cucumis bisexualis, commonly known as the bisexual cucumber, is a species within the Cucurbitaceae family.

It is characterized by its monoecious nature, meaning it has both male and female flowers on the same plant. The plant typically produces elongated, green fruits that resemble cucumbers but are often smaller and more spiny. Its botanical features include palmate leaves and a trailing growth habit.

This species is primarily found in arid regions and is adapted to thrive in hot, dry climates.
