Corchorus longicarpus

Corchorus longicarpus, commonly known as longpod smartweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the family Erythraceae and is characterized by its erect, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in dense clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical seed pods. Its fibrous roots and stems have been used traditionally for their textile fibers and medicinal properties.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.
