Convolvulus aucheri

Convolvulus aucheri, commonly known as Persian cyclamen, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pink or purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
