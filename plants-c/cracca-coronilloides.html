Cracca coronilloides

Cracca coronilloides, commonly known as the crownflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features upright, branching stems that are often hairy, and it produces showy, yellow or orange flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Cracca coronilloides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
