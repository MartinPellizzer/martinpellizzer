Carex transandina

Carex transandina, commonly known as the Andean sedge, is a perennial plant native to South America, particularly found in the Andes region.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or alpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets.

Carex transandina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
