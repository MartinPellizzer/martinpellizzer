Chamaecrista distichoclada

Chamaecrista distichoclada, commonly known as the two-ranked cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for agricultural practices.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its delicate foliage and vibrant floral display.
