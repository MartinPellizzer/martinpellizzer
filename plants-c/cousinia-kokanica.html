Cousinia kokanica

Cousinia kokanica, commonly known as the Kashmir Himalayan cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the family Rosaceae and is characterized by its compact, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and hairy, adapted to withstand cold and arid conditions.

Cousinia kokanica is often found in rocky or alpine environments, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
