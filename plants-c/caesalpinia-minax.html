Caesalpinia minax

Caesalpinia minax, commonly known as the red flame acacia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces bright orange to red flowers arranged in dense, cylindrical clusters, which bloom during the dry season. Its seed pods are elongated and woody, containing several seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Caesalpinia minax is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
