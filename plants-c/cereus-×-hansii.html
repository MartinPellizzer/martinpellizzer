Cereus × hansii

Cereus × hansii, commonly known as the Hansii Cactus, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Cereus jamensis and Cereus peruvianus.

It is a columnar cactus characterized by its tall, spindly stems and clusters of white, night-blooming flowers. The plant is native to South America and thrives in arid environments with well-draining soil. It requires full sun and minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping and rock gardens.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
