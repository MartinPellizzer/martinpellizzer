Costus littoralis

Costus littoralis, commonly known as the coastal costus, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical regions.

It typically grows in coastal areas and wetlands, thriving in moist, sandy soils. The plant features elongated, narrow leaves and produces striking, spadix-like inflorescences. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.

Its ability to tolerate saline conditions makes it an important species in coastal ecosystems.
