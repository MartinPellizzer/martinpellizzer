Cynanchum hirtum

Cynanchum hirtum, commonly known as the hairy dogbane, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It features slender, erect stems that are often hairy, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Cynanchum hirtum is typically found in moist woodlands and along stream banks, where it thrives in partial to full shade.
