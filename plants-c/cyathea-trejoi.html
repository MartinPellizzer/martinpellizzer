Cyathea trejoi

Cyathea trejoi, commonly known as the Trejo's tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often in montane forests. Its rhizome is thick and woody, supporting the growth of its expansive fronds.

Cyathea trejoi plays an important ecological role in its habitat by providing shelter and food for various organisms.
