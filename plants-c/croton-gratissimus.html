Croton gratissimus

Croton gratissimus, commonly known as the golden croton, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, colorful foliage, which can range from bright yellow to deep red and orange. The leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a velvety texture and prominent veins. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by spiny, capsule-like fruits.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its vibrant and eye-catching appearance.
