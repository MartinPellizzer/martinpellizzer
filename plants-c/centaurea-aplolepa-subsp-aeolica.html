Centaurea aplolepa subsp. aeolica

Centaurea aplolepa subsp.

aeolica, commonly known as the Aeolian centaurea, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments. It features spiny, linear leaves and produces bright yellow to orange flowers.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
