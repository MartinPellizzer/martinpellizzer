Croton lagoensis

Croton lagoensis, commonly known as the African croton, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a rough texture and prominent veins. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and arranged in clusters, while the fruit is a three-lobed capsule containing numerous seeds.

Croton lagoensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some African cultures.
