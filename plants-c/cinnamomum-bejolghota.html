Cinnamomum bejolghota

Cinnamomum bejolghota, commonly known as Indian cinnamon or Tamala, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its aromatic, reddish-brown bark that is highly valued in traditional medicine and cuisine. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by egg-shaped, dark purple berries. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, spicy aroma.

The tree thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its bark and essential oils.
