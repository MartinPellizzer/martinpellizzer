Cousinia eriophylla

Cousinia eriophylla, commonly known as the desert cinquefoil, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of North America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming structure adapted to harsh desert conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring, and its leaves are typically gray-green with a fuzzy texture to reduce water loss. Cousinia eriophylla thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

It plays a role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for small desert organisms.
