Cynanchum amphiboleum

Cynanchum amphiboleum, commonly known as the water yarrow, is a perennial herb native to parts of Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Its root system is fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found near streams or in wet meadows.
