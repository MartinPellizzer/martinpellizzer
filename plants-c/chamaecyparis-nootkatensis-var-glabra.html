Chamaecyparis nootkatensis var. glabra

Chamaecyparis nootkatensis var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth foliage Alaska cedar, is a variant of the Alaska cedar native to western North America.

It is a coniferous evergreen tree characterized by its slender, conical shape and smooth, glabrous foliage. The leaves are scale-like, arranged in crossed whorls, and are typically a deep green color. It produces small, rounded cones that mature to a reddish-brown hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
