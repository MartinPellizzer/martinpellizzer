Cycas miquelii

Cycas miquelii, commonly known as the Miquel's cycad, is a species of cycad native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and a crown of feathery, pinnate leaves. The plant produces cones, with the male cones appearing at the base of the plant and the female cones at the top. Cycas miquelii is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water.

It is also noted for its historical significance in botanical studies and its role in the conservation of cycad species.
