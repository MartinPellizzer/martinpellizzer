Cousinia ottonis

Cousinia ottonis, commonly known as the Otton's hawkweed, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its rosette of silver-gray, woolly leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in dense clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and alpine meadows.

Cousinia ottonis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
