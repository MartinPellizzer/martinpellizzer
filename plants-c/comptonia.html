Comptonia

Comptonia, commonly known as sweet pepperbush, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Myrtaceae.

It consists of a single species, Comptonia peregrina, which is native to the southeastern United States. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, glandular leaves and showy, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Comptonia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
