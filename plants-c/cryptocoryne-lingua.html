Cryptocoryne lingua

Cryptocoryne lingua, commonly known as the tongue cryptocoryne, is a species of aquatic plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, ribbon-like leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments with moderate to high light conditions. Its leaves are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous white flowers.

Cryptocoryne lingua is popular in aquascaping due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
