Croton mavoravina var. rotundifolius

Croton mavoravina var. rotundifolius, commonly known as the rounded-leaf croton, is a variant of the Croton genus native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically rounded or heart-shaped, with a vibrant green coloration. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range from yellow to red, depending on the stage of bloom. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.

This variant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
