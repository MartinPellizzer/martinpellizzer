Calysphyrum floridum

Calysphyrum floridum, commonly known as Florida panicgrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green hue, and it is often found in disturbed or swampy soils.

Calysphyrum floridum plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
