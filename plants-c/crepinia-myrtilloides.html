Crepinia myrtilloides

Crepinia myrtilloides, commonly known as the purple-flowered rush, is a herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and rushes, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, preferring moist soil conditions. Its flowering stems bear small, clustered purple flowers, which are often borne in dense inflorescences.

Crepinia myrtilloides is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
