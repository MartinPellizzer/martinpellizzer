Caragana boisii var. platycarpa

Caragana boisii var. platycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited peashrub, is a shrub native to Central Asia, including parts of China and the Russian Far East.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually yellow or pale green, followed by oval, edible pods containing seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing on rocky soils.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other regions for landscaping purposes.
