Campanula marchesettii subsp. apennina

Campanula marchesettii subsp.

apennina, commonly known as the Apennine bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe. It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped blue or violet flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. It has narrow, lanceolate leaves and produces showy inflorescences that bloom in summer.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in some regions.
