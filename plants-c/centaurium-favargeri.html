Centaurium favargeri

Centaurium favargeri, commonly known as the alpine centaury, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a small herbaceous plant characterized by its slender stems and basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous environments.

Centaurium favargeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
