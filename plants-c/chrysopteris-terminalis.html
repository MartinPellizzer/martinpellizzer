Chrysopteris terminalis

Chrysopteris terminalis, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-green foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are opposite and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
