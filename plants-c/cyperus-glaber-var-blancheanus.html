Cyperus glaber var. blancheanus

Cyperus glaber var. blancheanus, commonly known as Blanche's flatsedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist soils, often found in marshy areas or along water bodies. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, three-sided flower clusters.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
