Cola nalaensis f. variifolia

Cola nalaensis f. variifolia, commonly known as the variable cola, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Sterculiaceae family and is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that vary in shape and size. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by woody capsules containing seeds. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's botanical features make it an interesting subject for both ecological and horticultural study.
