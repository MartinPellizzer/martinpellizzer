Carex tytthospica

Carex tytthospica, commonly known as the narrowleaf sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its flowering stems bear small, greenish-brown flowers that are clustered in dense spikes.

Carex tytthospica is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support diverse wildlife.
