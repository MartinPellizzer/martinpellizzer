Cestrum venatorium

Cestrum venatorium, commonly known as the night-blooming cereus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that open at night and close by morning. The plant belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshade plants. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a velvety texture.

Cestrum venatorium is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its nocturnal blooming habit.
