Centaurea × beltranii

Centaurea × beltranii, commonly known as the hybrid cornflower, is a perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with lance-shaped leaves and showy composite flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Centaurea × beltranii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
