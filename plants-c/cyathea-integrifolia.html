Cyathea integrifolia

Cyathea integrifolia, commonly known as the coastal tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, compound fronds that can reach several meters in length. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments near coastlines and is often found in coastal forests. Its fronds are typically a deep green and have a smooth, leathery texture.

Cyathea integrifolia plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various organisms.
