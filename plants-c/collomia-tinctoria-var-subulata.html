Collomia tinctoria var. subulata

Collomia tinctoria var. subulata, commonly known as the Sierra bluecup, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and open habitats, such as meadows and slopes. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain the seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
