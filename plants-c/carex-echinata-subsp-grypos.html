Carex echinata subsp. grypos

Carex echinata subsp.

grypos, commonly known as the rough spike sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, and along streambanks.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spiky, cylindrical flower clusters that are often reddish or brownish in color. Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread vegetatively.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats.
