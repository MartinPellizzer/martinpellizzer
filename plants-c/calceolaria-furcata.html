Calceolaria furcata

Calceolaria furcata, commonly known as the forked slipper orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the family Calceolariaceae and is characterized by its unique, slipper-shaped flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant produces small, elongated leaves and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking and unusual floral structure.
