Conyza baccharis

Conyza baccharis, commonly known as ragweed, is a flowering plant in the sunflower family.

It is native to the Americas and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and finely divided leaves. The plant is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environments, including roadsides and fields. It is known for producing large amounts of pollen, which can trigger allergic reactions in humans.

Conyza baccharis is also used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory properties.
