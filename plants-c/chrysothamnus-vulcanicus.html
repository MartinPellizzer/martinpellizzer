Chrysothamnus vulcanicus

Chrysothamnus vulcanicus, commonly known as the volcanic goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to volcanic regions of North America.

It is characterized by its dense, shrub-like growth habit and yellowish-gold colored flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has silver-green, needle-like leaves that are adapted to arid and rocky environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or volcanic soil areas.

Chrysothamnus vulcanicus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife.
