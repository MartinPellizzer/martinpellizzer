Cremastra mitrata

Cremastra mitrata, commonly known as the hooded ratibida, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces seed heads that are often mistaken for flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Cremastra mitrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance as a food source for pollinators.
