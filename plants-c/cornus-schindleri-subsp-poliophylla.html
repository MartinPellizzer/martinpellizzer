Cornus schindleri subsp. poliophylla

Cornus schindleri subsp.

poliophylla, commonly known as the white-veined cornel, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cornaceae. It is native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa, particularly in Kenya and Tanzania.

The plant is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that appear in clusters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have prominent white veins, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
