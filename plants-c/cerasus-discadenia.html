Cerasus discadenia

Cerasus discadenia, commonly known as the Japanese cherry or sakura, is a species of flowering plant in the Prunus genus.

It is native to Japan and is renowned for its showy pink or white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are ovate and turn a vibrant green during the growing season.

The fruit, a small cherry, is edible but less commonly consumed compared to other cherry varieties.
