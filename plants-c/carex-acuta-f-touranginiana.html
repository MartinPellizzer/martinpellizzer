Carex acuta f. touranginiana

Carex acuta f. touranginiana, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a variant of the common sedge, Carex acuta.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. The plant produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are usually greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along the edges of ponds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland environments.
