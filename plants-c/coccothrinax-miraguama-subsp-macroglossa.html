Coccothrinax miraguama subsp. macroglossa

Coccothrinax miraguama subsp.

macroglossa, commonly known as the Macroglossa palm, is a species native to the Caribbean islands. It is a small, slow-growing palm that typically reaches heights of up to 5 meters.

The plant features a slender trunk with a smooth, grayish-brown bark and produces clusters of small, pale green leaves. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by local wildlife.

This subspecies is adapted to coastal and limestone soils, making it a resilient plant in tropical environments.
