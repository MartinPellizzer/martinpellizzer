Carex microchaeta subsp. nesophila

Carex microchaeta subsp.

nesophila, commonly known as the slender bog sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and bog habitats in North America. It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

The plant typically grows in acidic, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in marshes, fens, and along the edges of ponds. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and colonize new areas within its wetland environment.

Due to its ecological importance, it is often used in restoration projects to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
