Crepis quercifolia

Crepis quercifolia, commonly known as the oak-leaved mouse-ear, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, hairy leaves that resemble the shape of oak leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a single terminal head, which are surrounded by pale green bracts. It thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Crepis quercifolia is a member of the Asteraceae family, known for its composite flower heads and medicinal properties in traditional herbal use.
