Caladenia carnea var. alata

Caladenia carnea var. alata, commonly known as the flying dragon orchid, is a flowering plant native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive, spurred petals and vivid coloration. The plant typically grows in heathlands and open forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its flowers are often pale pink to white with a deep red or purple labellum, and it blooms during late winter to early spring.

This variant is notable for its unique morphology and is considered a rare and endangered species in its natural habitat.
