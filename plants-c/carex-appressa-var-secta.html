Carex appressa var. secta

Carex appressa var. secta, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in spikelets, which are clustered in dense inflorescences.

Carex appressa var. secta is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
