Capparis terniflora

Capparis terniflora, commonly known as the three-flowered caper, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its woody shrub form and has small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a whitish, powdery coating. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in coastal and sandy environments, often growing in areas with high salinity.

Capparis terniflora is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its unique appearance.
