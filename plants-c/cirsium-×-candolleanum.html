Cirsium × candolleanum

Cirsium × candolleanum, commonly known as the Candolle's thistle, is a hybrid species within the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves and large, showy purple to pink flower heads. Native to the mountainous regions of Europe, this plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

The plant's robust structure and ornamental appearance make it a notable species in botanical gardens and natural habitats.
