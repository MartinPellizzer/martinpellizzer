Calamagrostis insperata

Calamagrostis insperata, commonly known as the silvergrass or slender tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and fluffy, silver-tipped panicles that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more silver as the plant matures.

Calamagrostis insperata plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
