Crepinia lemaitrei

Crepinia lemaitrei, commonly known as the Madagascar lily, is a striking plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant produces strap-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses worldwide.
