Chenolea dinteri

Chenolea dinteri, commonly known as the Dinter's chenopod, is a perennial shrub native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, with deep root systems that allow it to survive in nutrient-poor soils. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss, and it produces small, dry fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Chenolea dinteri is often found in rocky outcrops and sandy soils, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
