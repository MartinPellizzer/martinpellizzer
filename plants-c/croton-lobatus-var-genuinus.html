Croton lobatus var. genuinus

Croton lobatus var. genuinus, commonly known as the velvetleaf croton, is a tropical shrub characterized by its large, velvety, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a reddish or purple underside.

The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, spiny capsules containing numerous seeds. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal potential, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and practical purposes.
