Centaurea pseudocollina

Centaurea pseudocollina, commonly known as the narrowleaf cornflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. It features narrow, linear leaves and produces blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer.

Centaurea pseudocollina is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
