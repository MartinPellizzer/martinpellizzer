Critoniopsis palaciosii

Critoniopsis palaciosii, commonly known as the Palacios's cactus, is a rare and delicate species native to specific regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the spring season, which are followed by red, spherical fruits. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered endangered and requires careful conservation efforts.

Its botanical features make it an important subject for research in arid ecosystems and plant adaptation.
