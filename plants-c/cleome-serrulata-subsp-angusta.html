Cleome serrulata subsp. angusta

Cleome serrulata subsp.

angusta, commonly known as narrowleaf cleome, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet, featuring slender, branching stems.

The plant produces showy, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of the branches. Its leaves are lance-shaped and serrated, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in disturbed soils along roadsides and in open fields.
