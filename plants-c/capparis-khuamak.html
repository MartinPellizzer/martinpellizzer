Capparis khuamak

Capparis khuamak, commonly known as the Khamak flower, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Capparaceae family, which includes various shrubs and herbs. The plant is characterized by its woody stems and clusters of small, fragrant yellow flowers. Its leaves are typically ovate and have a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, tropical environments.
