Cassia ampliflora

Cassia ampliflora, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and striking orange to red flowers that bloom in summer. The plant belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and produces elongated pods that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental species.

Its rapid growth and vibrant foliage make it a notable feature in urban and landscape settings.
