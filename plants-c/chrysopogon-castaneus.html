Chrysopogon castaneus

Chrysopogon castaneus, commonly known as the purple false grass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. The plant produces small, pale blue or white flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

Chrysopogon castaneus is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
