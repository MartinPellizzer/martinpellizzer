Centaurea carduiformis subsp. iranica

Centaurea carduiformis subsp.

iranica, commonly known as the Iranian thistle, is a flowering plant native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Iran. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of numerous small florets.

The plant typically grows as a herbaceous perennial with upright, spiny stems and narrow, gray-green leaves. Its flowers are usually bright yellow, with a distinctive central disk surrounded by ray florets.

It is well adapted to dry environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
