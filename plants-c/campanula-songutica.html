Campanula songutica

Campanula songutica, commonly known as the Songut bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils. It has hairy leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that bloom in summer.

Campanula songutica is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
