Chamaecrista jamaicensis

Chamaecrista jamaicensis, commonly known as Jamaican burr legume, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, spiny pods that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals. It thrives in coastal areas and sandy soils, often found in tropical and subtropical climates.

Chamaecrista jamaicensis is part of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
