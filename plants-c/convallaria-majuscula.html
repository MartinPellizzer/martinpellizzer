Convallaria majuscula

Convallaria majuscula, commonly known as lily of the valley, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to western Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features nodding, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces red berries that contain toxic seeds. The plant has a rhizomatous root system and prefers moist, well-drained soil in shaded environments. It is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.

However, all parts of the plant are poisonous and should be handled with care.
