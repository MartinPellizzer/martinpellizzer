Crassula mariae

Crassula mariae, commonly known as the Marie's Crassula, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its fleshy, water-storing leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of white or pink, often during the spring or summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its compact growth and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
