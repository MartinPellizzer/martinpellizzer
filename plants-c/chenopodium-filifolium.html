Chenopodium filifolium

Chenopodium filifolium, commonly known as silverleaf goosefoot, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, hair-like structures called trichomes. The plant produces small, green flowers that develop into hard, triangular seeds. It thrives in dry, disturbed soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments.

Chenopodium filifolium is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
