Cynodon sarmentosus

Cynodon sarmentosus, commonly known as creeping cynodon, is a perennial grass species native to Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread rapidly across the ground. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green color, and it is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for arid environments.

It is sometimes used in erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
