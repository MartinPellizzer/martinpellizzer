Carlina vulgaris var. polycephalos

Carlina vulgaris var. polycephalos, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and distinctive purple to pinkish-blue flower heads. The plant produces a showy composite flower made up of ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and often have a grayish or whitish underside.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas.
