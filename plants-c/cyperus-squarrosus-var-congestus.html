Cyperus squarrosus var. congestus

Cyperus squarrosus var. congestus, commonly known as the tufted flatsedge, is a flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale green to reddish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and ponds, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its rhizomatous growth allows it to spread vegetatively, making it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
