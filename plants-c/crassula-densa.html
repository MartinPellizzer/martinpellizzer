Crassula densa

Crassula densa, commonly known as the Campfire Plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features compact, rounded rosettes of fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are densely packed and often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the spring or summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-draining soil, making it ideal for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
