Croton mollivelus

Croton mollivelus, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that appear in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves contain various chemical compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.
