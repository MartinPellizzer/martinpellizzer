Carex recurva subsp. erythrostachys

Carex recurva subsp.

erythrostachys, commonly known as the red-stalked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of East Asia. It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

The plant features grass-like leaves that are usually dark green with a reddish tint on the underside. Its inflorescence consists of a dense spike of small, three-parted flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
