Cynoglossum dalianum

Cynoglossum dalianum, commonly known as the Dalian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia.

It features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces striking blue to purple flowers with a bell-like shape. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. It belongs to the Boraginaceae family, characterized by its hairy stems and showy flowers.

Cynoglossum dalianum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
