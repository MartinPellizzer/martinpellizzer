Carex enervis subsp. acrifolia

Carex enervis subsp.

acrifolia, commonly known as the red-veined sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes grass-like plants known for their unique floral structures.

The plant is characterized by its grass-like leaves with distinctive red veins, and its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
