Corylus avellana var. teretiuscula

Corylus avellana var. teretiuscula, commonly known as the spindle hazelnut, is a variety of the hazelnut plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, spindle-shaped nuts and hairy leaves that are often glandular. The plant produces small, yellowish male catkins and female flowers that develop into edible nuts. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and nut production.

This variety is closely related to the common hazelnut, Corylus avellana, but differs in its distinctive morphology.
