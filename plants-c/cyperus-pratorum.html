Cyperus pratorum

Cyperus pratorum, commonly known as marsh grass or nut sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the sedge family (Cyperaceae) and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in spikelets. Cyperus pratorum thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways.

It is often considered a weed in agricultural and aquatic settings due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
