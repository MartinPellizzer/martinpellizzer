Calamagrostis langsdorffii

Calamagrostis langsdorffii, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and the Americas.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance in gardens and natural landscapes. The plant typically grows in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and stream banks, and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, pale green to golden florets.

Calamagrostis langsdorffii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland habitats.
