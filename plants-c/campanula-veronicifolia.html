Campanula veronicifolia

Campanula veronicifolia, commonly known as the Veronica bellflower, is a perennial flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features upright, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Campanula veronicifolia is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
