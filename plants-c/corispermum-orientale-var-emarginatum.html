Corispermum orientale var. emarginatum

It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and small, greenish flowers. The plant typically thrives in saline or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its seeds are encased in a bladder-like structure, which aids in seed dispersal.

This variety is native to regions of eastern Asia, including China and the Russian Far East.
