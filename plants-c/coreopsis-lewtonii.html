Coreopsis lewtonii

Coreopsis lewtonii, commonly known as the Florida coreopsis, is a perennial herb native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 feet and features bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom from late spring to early fall. The plant has lance-shaped, hairy leaves and is often found in moist, sandy soils along streambanks and wetlands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wet conditions.

Coreopsis lewtonii is also an important plant for pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
