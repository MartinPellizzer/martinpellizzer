Convolvulus martinicensis var. nitens

Convolvulus martinicensis var. nitens, commonly known as the brightleaf morning glory, is a tropical vine native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and violet. The plant produces small, round, green fruits that contain seeds with a hard, dark coat. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found growing along riverbanks and in disturbed soils.

This species is noted for its rapid growth and is sometimes considered an invasive plant in certain regions.
