Crossyne ciliaris

Crossyne ciliaris, commonly known as the silky crossvine, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Australia.

It is a climbing shrub characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine, silky hairs, giving the plant its common name. It thrives in humid environments and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
