Costus acreanus

Costus acreanus, commonly known as the broadleaf costus, is a tropical flowering plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the family Costaceae and is characterized by its tall, grass-like stems and showy, spike-like inflorescences. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often arranged in a basal rosette. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, with prominent stamens that contribute to its ornamental appeal.

Costus acreanus is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a cut flower.
