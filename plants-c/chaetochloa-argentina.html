Chaetochloa argentina

Chaetochloa argentina, commonly known as the Argentine hairgrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth and distinctive hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in wetlands and along riverbanks. It has small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny flowers.

Chaetochloa argentina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
