Chrysodium aureum

Chrysodium aureum, commonly known as the golden chrysodium, is a striking plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer seasons. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated by gardeners interested in adding a touch of color and texture to their landscapes.
