Campanula rhomboidalis f. microphylla

Campanula rhomboidalis f. microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a compact, perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe.

It features small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming from late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a compact appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or borders.

Its ornamental value lies in its delicate flowers and compact growth habit.
