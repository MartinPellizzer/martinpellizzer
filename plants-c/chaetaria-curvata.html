Chaetaria curvata

Chaetaria curvata, commonly known as the curved hair plant, is a unique species within the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curved bracts that resemble tiny hairs, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate regions and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are slender and often have a slight serration along the edges.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a subtle fragrance that attracts pollinators like bees and butterflies.
