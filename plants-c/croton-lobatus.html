Croton lobatus

Croton lobatus, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like birds and insects. The plant has compound leaves with lobed or serrated margins, and its bark is thick and fibrous. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

Croton lobatus is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
