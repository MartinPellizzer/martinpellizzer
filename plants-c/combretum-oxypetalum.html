Combretum oxypetalum

Combretum oxypetalum, commonly known as the red-flowered combretum, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pink or red, depending on the variety. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

It is often found in moist woodland areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses.
