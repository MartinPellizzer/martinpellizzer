Catasetum × splendens var. lindenii

Catasetum × splendens var. lindenii, commonly known as the Lindenberg's orchid, is a hybrid orchid species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers with a unique structure that includes a specialized pollination mechanism. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of multiple showy flowers that bloom in clusters, often with a strong fragrance to attract specific pollinators.

The varietal form, lindenii, is distinguished by its particular floral morphology and coloration compared to the parent species.
