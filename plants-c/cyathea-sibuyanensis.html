Cyathea sibuyanensis

Cyathea sibuyanensis, commonly known as the Sibuyan tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound fronds. The plant is typically found in humid, shaded environments within montane rainforests. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, and it is known for its slow growth rate.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the need for conservation efforts.
