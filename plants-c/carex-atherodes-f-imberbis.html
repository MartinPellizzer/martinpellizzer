Carex atherodes f. imberbis

Carex atherodes f. imberbis, commonly known as the glabrous sedge, is a variant of the Carex atherodes species.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. The plant is characterized by its smooth, hairless leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
