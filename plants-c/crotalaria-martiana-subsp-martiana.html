Crotalaria martiana subsp. martiana

Crotalaria martiana subsp.

martiana, commonly known as the African blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa. It is a herbaceous annual with slender, hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces showy blue or violet flowers with five petals, typically in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often found in disturbed habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens but can become invasive in some regions.
