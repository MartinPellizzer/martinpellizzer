Croton vitifolius

Croton vitifolius, commonly known as the white-leaf croton, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, palmate leaves that are usually bright green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in spikes, followed by orange or red berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
