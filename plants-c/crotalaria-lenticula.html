Crotalaria lenticula

Crotalaria lenticula, commonly known as the lesser crotalaria, is an annual herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing ability. The plant features palmately compound leaves and produces small, yellow flowers that attract pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Due to its allelopathic properties, it can inhibit the growth of nearby plants, making it both a weed and a natural pest control agent.
