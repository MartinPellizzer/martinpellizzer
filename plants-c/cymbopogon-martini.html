Cymbopogon martini

Cymbopogon martini, commonly known as citronella grass, is a perennial grass native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is well-known for its strong, aromatic scent, which is derived from the essential oils present in its leaves. These oils contain high concentrations of citral, a compound responsible for its distinctive fragrance and mosquito-repelling properties.

Citronella grass is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and as a natural insect repellent.
