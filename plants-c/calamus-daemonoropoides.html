Calamus daemonoropoides

Calamus daemonoropoides, commonly known as the giant sago palm, is a member of the Arecaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, tree-like plant that can grow up to 20 meters in height, characterized by its large, pinnate leaves and a stout, unbranched stem. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into dark blue, fleshy fruits. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in tropical forests.
