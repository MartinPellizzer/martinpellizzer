Cassia ramosissima

Cassia ramosissima, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its dense, spreading branches and clusters of bright yellow, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has palmately compound leaves with five to seven leaflets, and its flowers are often followed by long, slender pods containing flat, brown seeds. Cassia ramosissima thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular ornamental plant in warm climates.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
