Calamus caroli

Calamus caroli, commonly known as sweet flag, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland areas in North America.

It belongs to the family Acoraceae and is characterized by its aromatic, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, typically blooming in early summer. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent.

Calamus caroli thrives in marshy soils and is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
