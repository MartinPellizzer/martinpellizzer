Carex grisea var. mutica

Carex grisea var. mutica, commonly known as the silky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swales, and along stream banks. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, and it produces viable seeds that aid in its propagation.

The silky sedge is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
