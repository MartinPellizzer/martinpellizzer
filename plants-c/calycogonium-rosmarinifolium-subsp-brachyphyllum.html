Calycogonium rosmarinifolium subsp. brachyphyllum

Calycogonium rosmarinifolium subsp.

brachyphyllum, commonly known as the broadleaf calycogonium, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its aromatic, needle-like leaves that resemble those of rosemary, giving it its common name.

The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking drought-tolerant plants.
