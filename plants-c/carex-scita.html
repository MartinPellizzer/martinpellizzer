Carex scita

Carex scita, commonly known as the tall sedges, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetlands and moist soils in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flower heads that are typically wind-pollinated. Carex scita thrives in full to partial sun and is often found in marshy areas, along stream banks, and in swampy woods.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
