Convolvulus virgatus var. subaphyllus

Convolvulus virgatus var. subaphyllus, commonly known as the slender bindweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are alternate and typically narrow, with a hairy texture. This variety is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained conditions.

It is a member of the Convolvulaceae family, known for its climbing or trailing growth forms.
