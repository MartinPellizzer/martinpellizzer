Cola lorougnonis

Cola lorougnonis, commonly known as the West African cola tree, is a member of the Sterculiaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of West Africa, where it thrives in humid, lowland environments. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a stimulant.

The plant's botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and cultural contexts.
