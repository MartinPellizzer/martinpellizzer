Crataegus nigra f. csapodyae

Crataegus nigra f. csapodyae, commonly known as the Csapody's black hawthorn, is a rare horticultural form of the black hawthorn.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black bark and dense, thorned branches. The plant produces small, white flowers in spring, followed by red berries in autumn. It is typically found in woodland areas and thrives in well-drained soils.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in botanical gardens.
