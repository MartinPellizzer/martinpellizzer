Carex rostrata var. sparganiformis

Carex rostrata var. sparganiformis, commonly known as the spiky sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clustered flowering heads. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
