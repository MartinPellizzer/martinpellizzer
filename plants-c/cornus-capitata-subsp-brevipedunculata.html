Cornus capitata subsp. brevipedunculata

Cornus capitata subsp.

brevipedunculata, commonly known as the short-pedunculate cornel. It is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its compact growth habit and small, white or pinkish flowers.

The plant produces red or orange berries that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a smooth margin.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.
