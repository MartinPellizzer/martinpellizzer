Centrosema grandiflorum

Centrosema grandiflorum, commonly known as the largeflower velvetbean, is a leguminous plant native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom throughout the growing season. The plant produces elongated, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It has a deep root system that helps in nitrogen fixation, making it valuable for soil enrichment.

Centrosema grandiflorum is often used in agroforestry and as a forage crop due to its adaptability and nutritional value.
