Cynanchum nubicola

Cynanchum nubicola, commonly known as the African milkweed, is a flowering plant native to Africa.

It belongs to the Asclepiadaceae family and is characterized by its milky sap, which contains toxic compounds. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces showy, yellowish-orange flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its toxicity, it is important to handle the plant with care.
