Cyathea superfusa

Cyathea superfusa, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its tall, trunk-like stem covered in persistent, fibrous roots and a crown of large, feather-like fronds. The plant belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to grow in shaded, moist environments. Its fronds can reach lengths of up to 3 meters, and the plant can live for several decades.

Cyathea superfusa plays an important ecological role in forest ecosystems by providing habitat and contributing to soil stability.
