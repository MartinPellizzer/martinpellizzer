Colubrina johnstonii

Colubrina johnstonii, commonly known as Johnston's beautyberry, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, violet-purple berries that appear in clusters, attracting birds and other wildlife. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and thickets.

Colubrina johnstonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
