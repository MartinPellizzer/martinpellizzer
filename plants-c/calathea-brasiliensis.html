Calathea brasiliensis

Calathea brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves that display a unique pattern of light and dark green. The plant is noted for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its foliage changes color in response to light, a phenomenon known as nyctinasty.

Calathea brasiliensis requires high humidity and well-draining soil to flourish.
