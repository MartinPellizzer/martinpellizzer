Cirsium quercetorum var. mendocinum

Cirsium quercetorum var. mendocinum, commonly known as the Mendocino thistle, is a perennial plant native to the coastal regions of Northern California.

It is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and large, showy purple to pink flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has deeply lobed, silvery-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, providing it with a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in open coastal habitats.

This variety is part of the Asteraceae family and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
