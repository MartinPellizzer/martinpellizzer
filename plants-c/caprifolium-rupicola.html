Caprifolium rupicola

Caprifolium rupicola, commonly known as the rock honeysuckle, is a perennial shrub native to the rocky regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, woody stems that often grow along rock faces or in crevices. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a slight hairy texture.

Caprifolium rupicola is adapted to arid conditions and thrives in well-drained soils.
