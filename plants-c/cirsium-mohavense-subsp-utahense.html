Cirsium mohavense subsp. utahense

Cirsium mohavense subsp.

utahense, commonly known as the Utah thistle, is a perennial plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple to pinkish flowers that bloom in late summer.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils, adapting to the harsh desert environment. Its flowering stems are often branched and can reach heights of up to 1 meter.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy-like plants.
