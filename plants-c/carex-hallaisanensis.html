Carex hallaisanensis

Carex hallaisanensis, commonly known as the Hallaisan sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is native to the Korean Peninsula and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense clusters. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
