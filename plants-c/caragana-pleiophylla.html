Caragana pleiophylla

Caragana pleiophylla, commonly known as the many-leaved shrub or the shrub of many leaves, is a deciduous shrub native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by bean-like pods containing seeds. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a hardy species suitable for erosion control.

Its leaves are compound, typically with several pairs of leaflets, giving it a distinctive appearance.
