Carex straminea var. hyalina

Carex straminea var. hyalina, commonly known as the translucent sedge, is a perennial plant native to wetland and marsh environments.

It features grass-like leaves that are typically narrow and slightly pointed, with a pale green to bluish-gray hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically found in clusters at the ends of its stems. It thrives in moist to wet soils and is often found in areas with partial to full sun.

Its ornamental value comes from its unique leaf coloration and its ability to add texture to wetland landscapes.
