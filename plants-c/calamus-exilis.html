Calamus exilis

Calamus exilis, commonly known as slender sweetflag, is a member of the Acoraceae family.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that grow in dense clusters. The plant typically thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Calamus exilis is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
