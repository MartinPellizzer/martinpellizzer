Colchicum levieri

Colchicum levieri, commonly known as the purple autumn crocus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking violet-purple flowers in the autumn, typically from September to November. The plant has arrow-shaped leaves that emerge from a corm, which is a type of underground storage organ. Its flowers are surrounded by two large, petal-like bracts, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Colchicum levieri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique bloom.
