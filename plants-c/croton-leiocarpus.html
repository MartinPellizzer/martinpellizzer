Croton leiocarpus

Croton leiocarpus, commonly known as the white-fruit croton, is a tropical plant native to Central America and the Caribbean.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height, characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into fleshy, orange-red berries containing a single seed. Its leaves and bark contain various bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine and pharmacological research.

The species is also valued for its ornamental qualities due to its vibrant foliage and unique fruiting structures.
