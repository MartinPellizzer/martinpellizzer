Cyanus eflanensis

Cyanus eflanensis, commonly known as the Persian bluebell, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, typical of many members of the Asteraceae family.

Cyanus eflanensis is often found in rocky soils and elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters.
