Cissampelos discolor var. cardiophylla

Cissampelos discolor var. cardiophylla, commonly known as the heartleaf moonvine, is a climbing perennial plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns, contributing to its ornamental appeal. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or orange, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its unique foliage.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive addition to tropical and subtropical landscapes.
