Campanula sarmatica var. calcarea

Campanula sarmatica var. calcarea, commonly known as the limestone bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped blue to violet flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or limestone-rich environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and slightly hairy, while its stems are upright and branching.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable soil conditions.
