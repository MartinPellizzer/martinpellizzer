Chrysanthemum crithmifolium

Chrysanthemum crithmifolium, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flower heads surrounded by pale yellow ray florets. The plant has aromatic, fern-like leaves that are often used in herbal remedies. It thrives in coastal areas and well-drained soils, preferring full sun.

Chrysanthemum crithmifolium is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in traditional medicine.
