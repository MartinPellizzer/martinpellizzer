Calamintha officinalis var. nepeta

Calamintha officinalis var. nepeta, commonly known as caraway mint, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a strong, minty scent, and small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, often growing in rocky or grassy areas. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.

The plant is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine and as a culinary herb.
