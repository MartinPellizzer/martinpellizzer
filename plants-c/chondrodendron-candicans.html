Chondrodendron candicans

Chondrodendron candicans, commonly known as the candlewood tree, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, candle-like inflorescences that consist of numerous small, white to pale yellow flowers clustered around a central stem. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white waxy coating. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
