Capparis quadriflora

Capparis quadriflora, commonly known as the four-flowered caper, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its shrub-like growth habit and distinctive, often thorned stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pink in color. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy coating.

The fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
