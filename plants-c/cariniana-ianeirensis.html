Cariniana ianeirensis

Cariniana ianeirensis, commonly known as the Brazil nut tree, is a large tropical evergreen tree native to the Amazon basin.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and can grow up to 50 meters tall. The tree produces large, woody capsules that contain the edible seeds, known as Brazil nuts. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape.

The Brazil nut tree plays a vital role in the ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
