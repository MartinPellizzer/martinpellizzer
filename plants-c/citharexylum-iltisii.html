Citharexylum iltisii

Citharexylum iltisii, commonly known as the New Zealand citharexylum, is a small evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, often in shades of white or pink, which bloom in spring. It is well adapted to the temperate climate of its native habitat and is often found in coastal and forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and unique characteristics, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
