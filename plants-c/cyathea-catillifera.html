Cyathea catillifera

Cyathea catillifera, commonly known as the hairy tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in a dense layer of brown, fibrous roots. The plant produces large, pinnately compound fronds that can grow up to several meters in length. Cyathea catillifera is a member of the family Cyatheaceae and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in tropical rainforest ecosystems.
