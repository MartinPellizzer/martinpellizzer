Calamagrostis arundinacea var. hymenoglossa

Calamagrostis arundinacea var. hymenoglossa, commonly known as the silver feather reed grass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that produce feathery, silver-green plumes during the summer months. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils, often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds visual interest throughout the growing season.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
