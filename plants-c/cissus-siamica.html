Cissus siamica

Cissus siamica, commonly known as the Siam figleaf, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Vitaceae and is characterized by its heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are sweet and aromatic. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in shaded areas under the canopy of forests.

Cissus siamica is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its medicinal properties.
