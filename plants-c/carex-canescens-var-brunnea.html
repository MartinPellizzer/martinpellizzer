Carex canescens var. brunnea

Carex canescens var. brunnea, commonly known as the brownish sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy areas in North America.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically borne on slender, branching stems. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense colonies in moist soils.

The varietal form, brunnea, is distinguished by its darker, more bronzed foliage compared to the typical variety.
