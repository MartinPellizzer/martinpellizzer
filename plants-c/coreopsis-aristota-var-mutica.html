Coreopsis aristota var. mutica

Coreopsis aristota var. mutica, commonly known as the hairy coreopsis, is a perennial flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive, rugged appearance. The plant produces bright yellow, daisy-like flowers that bloom from late spring to early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This variety is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
