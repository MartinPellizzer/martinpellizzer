Cyanella hyacinthoides

Cyanella hyacinthoides, commonly known as the blue hyacinth, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces umbels of bell-shaped flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Cyanella hyacinthoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
