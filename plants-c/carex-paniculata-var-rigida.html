Carex paniculata var. rigida

Carex paniculata var. rigida, commonly known as rigid sedge, is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a dark green color. The plant produces tall, cylindrical inflorescences that bear small, spike-like flowers, which are often tinged with brown or purple. It thrives in moist to wet soil conditions and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in aquatic environments.
