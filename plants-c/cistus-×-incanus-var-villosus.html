Cistus × incanus var. villosus

Cistus × incanus var. villosus, commonly known as the woolly rockrose, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage covered in fine, woolly hairs. The plant produces showy, pink to reddish flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and full sun exposure.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in xeriscaped gardens.
