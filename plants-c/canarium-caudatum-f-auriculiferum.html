Canarium caudatum f. auriculiferum

Canarium caudatum f. auriculiferum, commonly known as the golden leaf canarium, is a unique variant of the canarium species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow leaves, which distinguish it from the typical green foliage of its parent species. The plant belongs to the Burseraceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are not only visually appealing but also have historical uses in traditional medicine.

This variant is relatively rare and is valued for both its aesthetic and potential medicinal properties.
