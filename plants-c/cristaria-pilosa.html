Cristaria pilosa

Cristaria pilosa, commonly known as the hairy water lily, is a aquatic plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features broad, floating leaves with a waxy surface and distinctive hairy textures on the underside. The plant produces white or pink flowers that bloom above the water surface. Its roots anchor in the mud at the bottom of ponds and lakes.

Cristaria pilosa plays an important role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
