Convolvulus mollissimus

Convolvulus mollissimus, commonly known as velvetleaf morning glory, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, velvety stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. It thrives in warm, sunny environments and is often found in disturbed soils.

Convolvulus mollissimus is a climbing plant that can spread rapidly, making it both ornamental and potentially invasive.
