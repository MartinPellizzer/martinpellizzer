Cyperus stellatus

Cyperus stellatus, commonly known as the star sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically green or brown, arranged in spikelets. Cyperus stellatus thrives in moist environments such as wetlands, marshes, and along water bodies.

It is often used in ornamental gardening due to its attractive, feathery appearance.
