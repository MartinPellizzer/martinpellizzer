Croton bevilaniensis

Croton bevilaniensis, commonly known as the Bevilanii croton, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, colorful foliage, with leaves that often display shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers that are followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its striking leaf patterns.
