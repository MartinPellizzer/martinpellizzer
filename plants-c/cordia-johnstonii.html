Cordia johnstonii

Cordia johnstonii, commonly known as the Johnston's cordia, is a tropical tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits. Its bark is thick and corky, providing some resistance to pests and diseases.

Cordia johnstonii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and fruit.
