Calamagrostis scotica

Calamagrostis scotica, commonly known as Scottish moor grass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, blade-like leaves that are often silver-gray in appearance. This plant thrives in wet, acidic soils and is typically found in moorland and heathland environments. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.

Calamagrostis scotica is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
