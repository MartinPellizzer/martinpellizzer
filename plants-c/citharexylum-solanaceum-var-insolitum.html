Citharexylum solanaceum var. insolitum

Citharexylum solanaceum var. insolitum, commonly known as the fragrant cithara, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a subtle, sweet scent. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical classification places it within the Verbenaceae family, which includes other flowering shrubs and herbs.
