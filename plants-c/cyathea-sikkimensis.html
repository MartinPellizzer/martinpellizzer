Cyathea sikkimensis

Cyathea sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim tree fern, is a species of tree fern native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like fronds. The plant is typically found in moist, shaded forest environments, often growing in mountainous areas with high humidity. Its rhizome is thick and woody, supporting the growth of its massive fronds that can reach several meters in length.

Cyathea sikkimensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to soil stability and providing shelter for various organisms.
