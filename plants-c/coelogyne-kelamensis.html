Coelogyne kelamensis

Coelogyne kelamensis, commonly known as the Kelamensis orchid, is a species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is a terrestrial orchid with a sympodial growth habit, characterized by its pseudobulbs and linear leaves. The plant produces striking, fragrant flowers in shades of white and yellow, often with a red or orange lip. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its beauty and rarity, it is highly valued in horticulture and conservation efforts.
