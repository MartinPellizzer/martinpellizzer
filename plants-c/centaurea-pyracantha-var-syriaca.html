Centaurea pyracantha var. syriaca

Centaurea pyracantha var. syriaca, commonly known as Syrian cock's-comb, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking, spiky inflorescence composed of red or orange bracts that resemble a comb, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, long-lasting display.
