Crotalaria disticha

Crotalaria disticha, commonly known as the twinleaf cowpea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive two-lobed leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability. Its seeds are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines.

Crotalaria disticha is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
