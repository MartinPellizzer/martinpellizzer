Cinna latifolia

Cinna latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf cinquefoil, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks. It has a fibrous root system and produces seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Cinna latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
