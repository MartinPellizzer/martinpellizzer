Centaurea jacobaeifolia

Centaurea jacobaeifolia, commonly known as Jacob's weed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its blue to purple flowers and spiny, bristly foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and wastelands. It belongs to the family Asteraceae, which includes daisies and thistles.

Centaurea jacobaeifolia is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
