Cullumia reticulata

Cullumia reticulata, commonly known as the netleaf cullumia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Cullumia reticulata is often found in desert scrub and chaparral ecosystems.
