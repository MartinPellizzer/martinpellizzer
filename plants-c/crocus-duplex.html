Crocus duplex

Crocus duplex, commonly known as the double crocus, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features two rows of petals, giving it its name, and has narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Crocus duplex is also known for its ornamental value and ability to naturalize easily in suitable climates.
