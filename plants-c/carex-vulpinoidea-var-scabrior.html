Carex vulpinoidea var. scabrior

Carex vulpinoidea var. scabrior, commonly known as the rough-haired sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like leaves that are typically rough to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
