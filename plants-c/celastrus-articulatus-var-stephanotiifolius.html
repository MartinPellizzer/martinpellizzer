Celastrus articulatus var. stephanotiifolius

Celastrus articulatus var. stephanotiifolius, commonly known as the stephanotiifolius bindweed, is a climbing vine native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, heart-shaped leaves with a distinct waxy coating, and its flowers are small, yellow, and clustered in umbels. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks.

Botanically, it is notable for its unique leaf morphology and its role in local ecosystems.
