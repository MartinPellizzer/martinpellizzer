Crataegus glandulosa var. succulenta

Crataegus glandulosa var. succulenta, commonly known as the succulent hawthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and small, red berries that are produced in the fall. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or arid environments. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
