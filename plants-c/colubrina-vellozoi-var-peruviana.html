Colubrina vellozoi var. peruviana

Colubrina vellozoi var. peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian beautyberry, is a shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces bright purple to violet berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and tropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens in warm climates.
