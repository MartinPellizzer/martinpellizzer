Centropogon subandinus var. ventricosus

Centropogon subandinus var. ventricosus, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its bell-shaped, blue or violet flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at elevations between 2,500 and 4,000 meters. This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
