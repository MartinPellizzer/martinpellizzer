Chlorophytum blepharophyllum subsp. pendulum

Chlorophytum blepharophyllum subsp.

pendulum, commonly known as the hanging chrysalid, is a member of the Asparagaceae family. It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its long, pendant inflorescences that bear small, white, bell-shaped flowers.

The plant has strap-like, narrow leaves that are typically green with a slight variegation. It is a hardy, drought-tolerant species that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for hanging baskets and rock gardens.
