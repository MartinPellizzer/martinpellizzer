Cirsium ciliacum

Cirsium ciliacum, commonly known as the silky thistle, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its spiny, lobed leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces purple to pink flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

The species is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes many other thistle and daisy species.
