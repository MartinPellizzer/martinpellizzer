Cryptocarya saligna

Cryptocarya saligna, commonly known as the silky ash or narrow-leaved ash, is a species of flowering plant in the Lauraceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia, particularly found in the temperate regions of New South Wales and Victoria. The plant is characterized by its slender, ash-like appearance and has lance-shaped leaves with a silvery underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by dark blue, fleshy berries.

Cryptocarya saligna is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is valued for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
