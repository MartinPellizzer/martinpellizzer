Festuca ovina subsp. frigida

Festuca ovina subsp.

frigida, commonly known as alpine bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to cold climates. It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies thrives in high-altitude environments, such as mountain meadows and tundra regions. Its leaves are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is well adapted to harsh conditions, including low temperatures and poor soil nutrients.
