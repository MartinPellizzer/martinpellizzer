Fabricia coriacea

Fabricia coriacea, commonly known as rough bupleurum, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody, branched stems and heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its fibrous roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Fabricia coriacea thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
