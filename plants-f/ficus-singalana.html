Ficus singalana

Ficus singalana, commonly known as the Singalana fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Sri Lanka and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring moist, well-drained soil and partial shade.

Ficus singalana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
