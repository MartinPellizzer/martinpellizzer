Ficus tuphapensis var. annamensis

Ficus tuphapensis var. annamensis, commonly known as the Annam Ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam and Laos, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, fig-like fruits that are enclosed in a spherical, greenish husk.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable environments.
