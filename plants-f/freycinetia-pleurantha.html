Freycinetia pleurantha

Freycinetia pleurantha, commonly known as the broad-leafed pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its large, strap-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and cooking. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its inflorescence consists of small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in dense, spike-like structures.

The leaves are thick, fibrous, and have a distinct waxy texture, making them useful for weaving and other artisanal purposes.
