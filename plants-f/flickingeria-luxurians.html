Flickingeria luxurians

Flickingeria luxurians, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a striking member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida, where it thrives in moist, sunny habitats. The plant is characterized by its showy, golden-yellow inflorescences that bloom in late summer and early fall. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving them a soft, textured appearance.

Flickingeria luxurians is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
