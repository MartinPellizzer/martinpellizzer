Ferula equisetifolia

Ferula equisetifolia, commonly known as the Persian cow's milk plant, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its latex sap is milky and has been traditionally used for medicinal purposes.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.
