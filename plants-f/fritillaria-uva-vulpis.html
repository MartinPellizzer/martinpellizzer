Fritillaria uva-vulpis

Fritillaria uva-vulpis, commonly known as the fox's grape, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red and orange, often with intricate patterns resembling grapes. The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are adapted for survival in harsh, arid environments, storing water and nutrients efficiently.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and historical use in traditional medicine.
