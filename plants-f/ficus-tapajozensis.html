Ficus tapajozensis

Ficus tapajozensis, commonly known as the Tapajoz fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest, specifically in regions of Peru and Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, a fig-like structure, is edible and contains numerous seeds.

Ficus tapajozensis plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
