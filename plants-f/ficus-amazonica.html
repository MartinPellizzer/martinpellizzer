Ficus amazonica

Ficus amazonica, commonly known as the Amazon banyan, is a large tropical tree native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and can grow to impressive heights with a sprawling canopy. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by fig-like fruits, which are an important food source for various wildlife. Its roots can form aerial prop roots, contributing to its distinctive appearance and structural stability.

Ficus amazonica is also known for its ability to thrive in humid environments with high levels of rainfall.
