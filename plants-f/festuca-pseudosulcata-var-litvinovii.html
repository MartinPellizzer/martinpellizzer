Festuca pseudosulcata var. litvinovii

Festuca pseudosulcata var. litvinovii, commonly known as the Litvinov's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
