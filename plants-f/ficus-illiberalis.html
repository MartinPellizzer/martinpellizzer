Ficus illiberalis

Ficus illiberalis, commonly known as the noble fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Ficus illiberalis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in indoor environments.

Its growth habits make it relatively low-maintenance, though it requires bright, indirect light and moderate humidity to flourish.
