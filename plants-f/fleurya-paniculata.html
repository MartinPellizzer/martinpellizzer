Fleurya paniculata

Fleurya paniculata, commonly known as the golden shower tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its striking, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its flowers are fragrant and attract a variety of pollinators, including bees and birds.
