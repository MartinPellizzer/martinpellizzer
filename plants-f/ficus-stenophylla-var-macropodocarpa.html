Ficus stenophylla var. macropodocarpa

Ficus stenophylla var. macropodocarpa, commonly known as the giant fig tree, is a tropical plant characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves.

It is native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, warm climates. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits. Its extensive root system makes it suitable for stabilizing soil in reforestation projects.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to purify air in indoor environments.
