Fumaria agraria subsp. multiflora

Fumaria agraria subsp.

multiflora, commonly known as the common corydalis, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features slender, branching stems that grow up to 30 cm in height, often with a reddish tinge.

The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and woodland edges.
