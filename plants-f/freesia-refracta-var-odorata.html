Freesia refracta var. odorata

Freesia refracta var. odorata, commonly known as the sweet freesia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers typically range in color from white to pink, yellow, and orange, and they bloom in late spring to early summer. This variety is prized for its strong, sweet fragrance and is often used in floral arrangements and as a cut flower.

It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
