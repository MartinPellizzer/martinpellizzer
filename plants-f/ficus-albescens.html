Ficus albescens

Ficus albescens, commonly known as the white fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, light green leaves that often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by round, green fruits. Ficus albescens thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
