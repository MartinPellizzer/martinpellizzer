Ficus diversiformis

Ficus diversiformis, commonly known as the variegated fig tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that often display variegation with white or yellow markings. It produces small, green fig fruits that are typically not eaten by humans but are important for wildlife.

Ficus diversiformis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
