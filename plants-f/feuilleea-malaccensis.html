Feuilleea malaccensis

Feuilleea malaccensis, commonly known as the Malaccan leaf plant, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often appearing in the axils of the leaves. Feuilleea malaccensis thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage is used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
