Ficus manicariarum

Ficus manicariarum, commonly known as the Manicaria fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are edible and often consumed by local wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
