Firmiana platanifolia var. tomentosa

Firmiana platanifolia var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly firmiana, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense, woolly white pubescence. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by elongated, woody fruits. Its bark is smooth and often has a pale, grayish hue.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic qualities.
