Ferula communis var. catalaunica

Ferula communis var. catalaunica, commonly known as the Catalan fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

This variety is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodland and scrub habitats.
