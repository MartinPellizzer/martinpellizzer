Fumaria leonardii

Fumaria leonardii, commonly known as Leonard's smokeplant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fumariaceae family, characterized by its delicate, ascending stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces slender, hairy stems that often grow along the ground, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The flowers bloom in spring, attracting various pollinators with their subtle fragrance.
