Fernandoa superba

Fernandoa superba, commonly known as the giant fern, is a striking member of the Dryopteridaceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of South America, particularly in regions of Brazil and Peru. This fern is characterized by its large, frond-like leaves that can reach lengths of up to three meters. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture and nutrient-rich soil.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular specimen in botanical gardens and a subject of scientific interest.
