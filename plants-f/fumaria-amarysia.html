Fumaria amarysia

Fumaria amarysia, commonly known as the common fumitory, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Fumaria amarysia is also known for its historical use in traditional medicine.
