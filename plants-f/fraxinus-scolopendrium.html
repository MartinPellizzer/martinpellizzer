Fraxinus scolopendrium

Fraxinus scolopendrium, commonly known as the European ash or common ash, is a deciduous tree native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows to a height of 15–25 meters, featuring a straight trunk and a rounded crown. The leaves are alternate, simple, and pinnately compound, with 5–9 pairs of ovate leaflets. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in male and female catkins, and it produces samaras as its fruit.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in traditional medicine and timber use.
