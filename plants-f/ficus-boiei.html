Ficus boiei

Ficus boiei, commonly known as the Benin fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces figs that are typically green when mature, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.

Ficus boiei is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
