Fridericia candicans

Fridericia candicans, commonly known as the yellow-flowered false heather, is a small shrub native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and its clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in coastal or sandy environments.

Fridericia candicans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
