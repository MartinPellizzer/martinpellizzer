Fritillaria imperialis var. chitralensis

Fritillaria imperialis var. chitralensis, commonly known as the Chitral lily, is a striking bulbous perennial native to the mountainous regions of northern Pakistan and Afghanistan.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, thriving in temperate climates. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winters, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
