Fedia cornucopiae subsp. graciliflora

Fedia cornucopiae subsp.

graciliflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered fedia, is a rare and delicate species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, pale blue to lavender flowers that bloom in clusters.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil, often found in shaded forest understories. Its unique floral structure and limited distribution make it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss and climate change, this subspecies is considered endangered and requires careful protection.
