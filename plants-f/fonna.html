Fonna

Fonna, commonly known as the false hellebore, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Araceae and is characterized by its thick, fleshy rhizomes and waxy, arrow-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in an umbel, often surrounded by a spadix. Fonna is known for its toxic properties, containing compounds such as cardiac glycosides that can be harmful to humans and animals.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
