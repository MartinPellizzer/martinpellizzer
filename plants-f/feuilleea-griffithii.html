Feuilleea griffithii

Feuilleea griffithii, commonly known as the Griffith's leaf plant, is a unique species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are often a striking green with a subtle metallic sheen. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically yellow or green, which bloom in the early spring. Its foliage is highly valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities.

Feuilleea griffithii thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
