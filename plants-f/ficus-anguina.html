Ficus anguina

Ficus anguina, commonly known as the banyan fig or Indian rubber tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and its ability to produce aerial roots that can develop into additional trunks, giving it a distinctive appearance. Ficus anguina is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ornamental value.

Its fruit, known as a fig, is edible and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
