Fragariastrum libanoticum

Fragariastrum libanoticum, commonly known as the Lebanon strawberry, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Levant.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, edible, red berries that resemble strawberries. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are white with five petals, and it produces fruits that are often used in local culinary traditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
