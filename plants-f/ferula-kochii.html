Ferula kochii

Ferula kochii, commonly known as the Kashmir ferula, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and compound umbels. The plant features a thick, fleshy rhizome and can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. Its yellow flowers are clustered in umbrella-like inflorescences, and it is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Ferula kochii is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine.
