Fritillaria sibthorpiana var. fleischeri

Fritillaria sibthorpiana var. fleischeri, commonly known as the Fleischer's Crown Imperial, is a striking bulbous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem that bears clusters of pendulous, bell-shaped flowers in shades of orange, red, and yellow. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences that resemble a crown, hence its common name. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in harsh, arid conditions, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with specific growing conditions.
