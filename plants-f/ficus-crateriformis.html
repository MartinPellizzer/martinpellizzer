Ficus crateriformis

Ficus crateriformis, commonly known as the crater fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus crateriformis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
