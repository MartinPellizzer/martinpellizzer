Fagonia harpago var. ifniensis

Fagonia harpago var. ifniensis, commonly known as the Canary Islands fagonia, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant exhibits succulent leaves that help it conserve water in its arid habitat. It is often found growing on rocky slopes and coastal areas.

Its unique morphology and adaptation to harsh environmental conditions make it a notable species in Mediterranean flora.
