Ficus dimidiata

Ficus dimidiata, commonly known as the variegated ficus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white and yellow variegation. The plant is a member of the Moraceae family and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Ficus dimidiata is known for its ability to purify indoor air and maintain a compact shape with proper pruning.
