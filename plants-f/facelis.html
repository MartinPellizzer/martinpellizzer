Facelis

Facelis, commonly known as the African daisy, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are native to southern Africa and are characterized by their showy, daisy-like flowers. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stems. Facelis species often thrive in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making them popular in ornamental gardening.

They are also valued for their ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
