Ficus oleracea

Ficus oleracea, commonly known as the edible fig tree, is a tropical plant belonging to the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that develop into the edible fruit. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated for its fruit, which is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, chewy texture.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is valued for both its ecological and agricultural significance.
