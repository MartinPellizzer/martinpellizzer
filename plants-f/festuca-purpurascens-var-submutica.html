Festuca purpurascens var. submutica

Festuca purpurascens var. submutica, commonly known as the purple false bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often reddish or purplish stems. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that becomes more intense under stress. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
