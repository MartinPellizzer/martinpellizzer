Funastrum cuspidatum

Funastrum cuspidatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved funastrum, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a pointed tip, which are usually arranged alternately along the stem. Its small, greenish-white flowers appear in clusters during the spring and summer months.

Funastrum cuspidatum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
