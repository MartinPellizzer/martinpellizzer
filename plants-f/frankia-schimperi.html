Frankia schimperi

Frankia schimperi, commonly known as the African alder, is a nitrogen-fixing actinomycete that forms symbiotic relationships with certain plants.

It is particularly associated with members of the legume family, such as Acacia and Prosopis species. The bacterium colonizes the root nodules of its host plants, converting atmospheric nitrogen into a form usable by the plant. This makes Frankia schimperi important in nutrient-poor soils, enhancing plant growth and soil fertility.

Its ability to thrive in harsh environments contributes to its ecological significance in arid and semi-arid regions.
