Festuca speciosa var. villosa

Festuca speciosa var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and a dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer.
