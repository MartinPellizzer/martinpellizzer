Filipendula camtschatica

Filipendula camtschatica, commonly known as the Kamchatka meadow sweet, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of eastern Siberia and the Russian Far East.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Filipendula camtschatica is valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its potential use in traditional herbal medicine.
