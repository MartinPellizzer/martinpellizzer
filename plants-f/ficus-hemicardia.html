Ficus hemicardia

Ficus hemicardia, commonly known as the heartleaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Ficus hemicardia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
