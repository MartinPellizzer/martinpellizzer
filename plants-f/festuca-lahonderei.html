Festuca lahonderei

Festuca lahonderei, commonly known as Lahondere's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Festuca lahonderei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing rocky and arid landscapes.
