Forsythia japonica f. saxatilis

Forsythia japonica f. saxatilis, commonly known as the rock forsythia, is a compact, deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, funnel-shaped flowers in early spring, which are highly attractive to pollinators. It is well adapted to rocky or well-drained soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardy nature in garden settings.
