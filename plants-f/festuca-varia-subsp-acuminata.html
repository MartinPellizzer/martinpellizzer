Festuca varia subsp. acuminata

Festuca varia subsp.

acuminata, commonly known as the acuminata fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, woodlands, and rocky slopes.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and is often found in moist to moderately dry soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
