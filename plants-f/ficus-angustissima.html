Ficus angustissima

Ficus angustissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. Ficus angustissima produces small, clustered figs that are enclosed in a syconium, a unique structure that houses the flowers and fruits.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
