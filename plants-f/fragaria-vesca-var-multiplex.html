Fragaria vesca var. multiplex

Fragaria vesca var. multiplex, commonly known as the woodland strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant bears aggregate fruits composed of multiple achenes, each enclosed in a fleshy receptacle. It thrives in woodland environments, preferring well-drained, loamy soils and partial shade.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticultural cultivation.
