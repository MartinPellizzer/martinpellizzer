Ficus funiculosa

Ficus funiculosa, commonly known as the climbing fig, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, trailing stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in local cuisines.

Due to its adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in warm climates.
