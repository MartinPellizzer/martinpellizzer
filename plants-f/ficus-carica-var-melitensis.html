Ficus carica var. melitensis

Ficus carica var. melitensis, commonly known as the Smyrna fig, is a variety of the common fig tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, elongated leaves and produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested when they are still green. The plant thrives in warm, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in both fresh consumption and processing into products like fig jam.

The Smyrna fig is also valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to adapt to various growing conditions.
