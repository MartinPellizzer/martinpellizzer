Fraxinus excelsior var. cucullata

Fraxinus excelsior var. cucullata, commonly known as the wych elm, is a variant of the European elm characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often drooping branches.

It is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia, typically found in moist, fertile soils along riverbanks and in woodlands. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a serrated margin and a prominent midrib. Its bark is grey and fissured, becoming more rugged with age, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring.

The tree is known for its adaptability and historical significance in European landscapes.
