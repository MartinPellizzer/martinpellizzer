Ficus tayabensis

Ficus tayabensis, commonly known as the Tayabensis fig, is a species of fig tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often used in local cuisine. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Ficus tayabensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to adapt to various growing conditions.
