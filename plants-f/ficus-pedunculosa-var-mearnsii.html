Ficus pedunculosa var. mearnsii

Ficus pedunculosa var. mearnsii, commonly known as the Mearns' fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature, though they may change color depending on the variety.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
