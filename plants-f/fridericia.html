Fridericia

Fridericia, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, delicate leaves that often have a silvery or grayish underside, giving it its common name. The plants typically produce small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pink or purple. Fridericia species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas, where they often grow in dry, open habitats.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making them beneficial in ecological restoration projects.
