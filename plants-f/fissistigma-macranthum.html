Fissistigma macranthum

Fissistigma macranthum, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dramatic visual impact. The plant has a broad, spreading canopy with long, pinnately compound leaves. Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping in warm climates.

Fissistigma macranthum thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, often reaching heights of up to 20 meters.
