Festuca rupicola subsp. colorata

Festuca rupicola subsp.

colorata, commonly known as the red mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive reddish-purple foliage, which becomes more pronounced in cooler weather.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its flowers are small and spike-like, forming dense inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
