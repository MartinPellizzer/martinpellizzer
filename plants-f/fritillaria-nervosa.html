Fritillaria nervosa

Fritillaria nervosa, commonly known as the Sierra Lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Fritillaria nervosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
