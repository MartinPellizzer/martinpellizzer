Fimbristylis subinclinata

Fimbristylis subinclinata, commonly known as the leaning threadgrass, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a small, perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact inflorescences. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as wetlands and forest understories. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
