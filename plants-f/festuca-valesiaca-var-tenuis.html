Festuca valesiaca var. tenuis

Festuca valesiaca var. tenuis, commonly known as the slender meadow fescue, is a grass species native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and rocky slopes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers.

Festuca valesiaca var. tenuis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous environments.
