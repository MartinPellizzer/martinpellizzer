Festuca hirtovaginata

Festuca hirtovaginata, commonly known as the hairy-stalked fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, hairy stems that often bear small, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are typically narrow and covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing rocky and arid habitats.
