Ficus pedunculata var. acuta

Ficus pedunculata var. acuta, commonly known as the acute-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its elongated, pointed leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters in suitable conditions. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically pear-shaped.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
