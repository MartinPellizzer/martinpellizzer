Ficus oligoneura

Ficus oligoneura, commonly known as the silver-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in syconia, which are specialized structures typical of the fig genus.

Ficus oligoneura is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
