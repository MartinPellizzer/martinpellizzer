Ficus meiocarpa

Ficus meiocarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Moraceae family, which includes other well-known fig species. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. Ficus meiocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.

Its growth habit makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
