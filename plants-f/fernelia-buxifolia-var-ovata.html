Fernelia buxifolia var. ovata

Fernelia buxifolia var. ovata, commonly known as the ovate-leaved boxwood, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy foliage, with ovate-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers in clusters, followed by red berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for hedges and topiary.

Due to its slow growth rate, it requires minimal pruning and is well-suited for formal garden designs.
