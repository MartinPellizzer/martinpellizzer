Ficus carica var. cemenelea

Ficus carica var. cemenelea, commonly known as the Cemenelea fig, is a rare variant of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, featuring a distinctively shaped leaf with a serrated margin. The plant produces small, edible figs that are typically harvested before they fully ripen. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is less commonly cultivated but appreciated for its ornamental value and unique fruit characteristics.
