Ficus iidaiana

Ficus iidaiana, commonly known as the Iida's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically consumed by local wildlife.

Ficus iidaiana is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
