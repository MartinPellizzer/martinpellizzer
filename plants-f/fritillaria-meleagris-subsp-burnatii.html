Fritillaria meleagris subsp. burnatii

Fritillaria meleagris subsp.

burnatii, commonly known as the Balkan dog's tooth violet, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or reddish in color, often with contrasting markings.

The plant has a fibrous root system and thick, fleshy bulbs that store nutrients for the growing season. Its leaves are basal, grass-like, and often with a waxy coating.

This subspecies is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, making it a resilient plant in mountainous regions.
