Festuca longearistata

Festuca longearistata, commonly known as the long-awned fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bear long, silky awns. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with leaves that are generally bluish-green in color.

Festuca longearistata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate harsh conditions.
