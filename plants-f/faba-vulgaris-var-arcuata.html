Faba vulgaris var. arcuata

Faba vulgaris var. arcuata, commonly known as the curled vetch, is a leguminous plant characterized by its distinctive, curled leaves and small, yellow flowers.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas. Its seeds are edible and have been used historically in various cuisines.

The curled vetch is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.
