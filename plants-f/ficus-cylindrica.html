Ficus cylindrica

Ficus cylindrica, commonly known as the round-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, cylindrical stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, inedible fruits that are not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ficus cylindrica is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
