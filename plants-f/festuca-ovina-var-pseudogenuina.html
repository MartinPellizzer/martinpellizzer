Festuca ovina var. pseudogenuina

Festuca ovina var. pseudogenuina, commonly known as the slender sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact growth habit, forming dense, low-growing mats. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to blue-gray flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil.
