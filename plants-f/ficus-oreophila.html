Ficus oreophila

Ficus oreophila, commonly known as the mountain fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia, particularly in countries like Thailand and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its broad, leathery leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into edible fig fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, Ficus oreophila is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
