Fuchsia rivularis

Fuchsia rivularis, commonly known as the river fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows near streams and in moist, shady environments. The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, which bloom in the spring and summer. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Fuchsia rivularis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate moisture conditions.
