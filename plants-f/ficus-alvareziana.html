Ficus alvareziana

Ficus alvareziana, commonly known as the Alvarez fig, is a species of fig tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into edible fig fruits. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food.

Ficus alvareziana is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
