Ficus carica var. bellona

Ficus carica var. bellona, commonly known as the Bellona fig, is a variety of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces edible figs that are generally smaller and more elongated compared to standard fig varieties. It thrives in warm, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to produce fruit in suitable conditions.
