Ficus pilosa var. chrysocoma

Ficus pilosa var. chrysocoma, commonly known as the golden fig or chrysocoma ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost fuzzy appearance. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a deep green color with a slight sheen. This plant is native to parts of Africa and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It prefers warm, humid environments and is relatively easy to care for in indoor settings.
