Ficus costaricana

Ficus costaricana, commonly known as the Costa Rican fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
