Ficus theophrastoides var. angustifolia

Ficus theophrastoides var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are edible for certain animals but not generally consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
