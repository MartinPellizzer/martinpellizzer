Fuchsia gracilis var. multiflora

Fuchsia gracilis var. multiflora, commonly known as the weeping fuchsia, is a striking shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of vibrant pink to purple flowers that bloom throughout the summer. The plant features slender, arching stems that produce numerous small, showy bracts, giving it a dramatic, weeping appearance. Its foliage consists of dark green, lance-shaped leaves that provide a striking contrast to the colorful blooms.

This species is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and is well-suited to humid, shady environments.
