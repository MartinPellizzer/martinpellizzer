Ficinia tristachya

Ficinia tristachya, commonly known as the silky grass or narrow-leafed flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the Poaceae family, which includes grasses, and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Ficinia tristachya is often found in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands and prairies.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
