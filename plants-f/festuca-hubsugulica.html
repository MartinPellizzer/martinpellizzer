Festuca hubsugulica

Festuca hubsugulica, commonly known as the alpine fescue, is a perennial grass species native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates with well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Festuca hubsugulica is adapted to harsh conditions, including strong winds and low nutrient soils.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil in alpine ecosystems and providing forage for grazing animals.
