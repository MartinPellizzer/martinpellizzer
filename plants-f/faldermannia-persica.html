Faldermannia persica

Faldermannia persica, commonly known as Persian lilac, is a deciduous shrub native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, pale purple to white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, simple leaves that are opposite in arrangement and often have a slightly hairy texture. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release its seeds.

Faldermannia persica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms and pleasant fragrance.
