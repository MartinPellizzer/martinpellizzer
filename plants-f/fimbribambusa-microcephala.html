Fimbribambusa microcephala

Fimbribambusa microcephala, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and slender, often hairy culms. The plant exhibits a clumping growth form, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

This bamboo is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is often used in garden design due to its manageable size and graceful appearance.
