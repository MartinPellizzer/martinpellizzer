Ferula clematidifolia

Ferula clematidifolia, commonly known as the Persian cow's milk plant, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and distinctive appearance.

It is native to regions of Central Asia and the Middle East, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features a tall, hollow stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers in umbels. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties and have been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.

The plant's botanical features make it both ecologically significant and of interest in phytochemical research.
