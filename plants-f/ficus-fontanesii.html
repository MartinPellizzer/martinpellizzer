Ficus fontanesii

Ficus fontanesii, commonly known as the Fontanesii fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. It produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Ficus fontanesii thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
