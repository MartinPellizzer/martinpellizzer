Festuca multiflora

Festuca multiflora, commonly known as bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for lawns and erosion control.

Festuca multiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
