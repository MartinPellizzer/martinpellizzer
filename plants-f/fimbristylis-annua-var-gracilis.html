Fimbristylis annua var. gracilis

Fimbristylis annua var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and cylindrical inflorescence composed of numerous small spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas. Its flowers are usually unisexual, with separate male and female structures, and it relies on wind pollination.

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, contributing to the ecological diversity of wetland ecosystems.
