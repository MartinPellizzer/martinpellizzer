Ferraria macrochlamys subsp. macrochlamys

Ferraria macrochlamys subsp.

macrochlamys, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It belongs to the family Amaryllidaceae and is characterized by its tuberous roots and linear, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple, arranged in dense spikes. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

This subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
