Ficus periplocifolia

Ficus periplocifolia, commonly known as the Philippine fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Ficus periplocifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
