Ferreyranthus excelsus

Ferreyranthus excelsus, commonly known as the mountain starflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high elevations of the Andes in South America.

It features striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture, adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its alpine habitat. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists alike.
