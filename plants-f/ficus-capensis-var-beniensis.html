Ficus capensis var. beniensis

Ficus capensis var. beniensis, commonly known as the Benin fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to western Africa, particularly found in countries such as Benin, Nigeria, and Togo. This plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. The fruit is a small, round fig that is edible and often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage.
