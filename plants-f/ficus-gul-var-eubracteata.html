Ficus gul var. eubracteata

Ficus gul var. eubracteata, commonly known as the velvet fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
