Ficus benguetensis

Ficus benguetensis, commonly known as the Benguet Fig, is a species of fig tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. The plant is typically found in lowland and montane forests, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its fruit, which is edible, is an important food source for various wildlife species.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered plant.
