Felicia quinquenervia

Felicia quinquenervia, commonly known as the Orange Daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its showy yellow-orange flowers and basal rosette of narrow, silver-green leaves. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive appearance and drought tolerance. It produces small, daisy-like flowers that bloom throughout the growing season, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are typically hairy and have a slightly succulent texture, adapted to thrive in arid environments.
