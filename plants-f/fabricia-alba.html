Fabricia alba

Fabricia alba, commonly known as white fenugreek, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves. The plant typically grows in moist environments, such as wet meadows and marshes, and is native to parts of Europe and Asia. Its seeds are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant's ability to fix nitrogen makes it valuable for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
