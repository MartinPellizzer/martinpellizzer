Ficus dawei

Ficus dawei, commonly known as the Guangxi fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to China, specifically the Guangxi region, and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

Ficus dawei prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
