Ficus hirta var. appressa

Ficus hirta var. appressa, commonly known as the glossy fig or small-leaved fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically small and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits that are typically yellow or green when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate indoor conditions, making it a popular choice for houseplants.
