Festuca petersonii

Festuca petersonii, commonly known as Peterson's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, narrow leaves, and dense, dark green foliage. This plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Festuca petersonii is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small wildlife species.
