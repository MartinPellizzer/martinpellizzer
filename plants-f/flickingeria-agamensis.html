Flickingeria agamensis

Flickingeria agamensis, commonly known as the Agam's flickingeria, is a rare and unusual plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, spiral-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, blooming during the cooler months. Its growth habit is semi-succulent, allowing it to store water in its fleshy leaves.
