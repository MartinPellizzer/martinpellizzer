Ficus kallicarpa var. parvifolia

Ficus kallicarpa var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are smaller and more pointed compared to other ficus varieties. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically consumed by birds and mammals. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
