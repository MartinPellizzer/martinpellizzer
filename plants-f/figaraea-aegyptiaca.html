Figaraea aegyptiaca

Figaraea aegyptiaca, commonly known as the Egyptian fig tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean and parts of North Africa.

It features simple, alternate leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that changes color as it matures.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
