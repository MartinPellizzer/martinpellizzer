Ficus leucocarpa

Ficus leucocarpa, commonly known as the white fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called syconia. Its fruit, which is a fig, is usually white or pale yellow when ripe.

Ficus leucocarpa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in humid environments.
