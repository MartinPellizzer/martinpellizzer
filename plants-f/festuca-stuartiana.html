Festuca stuartiana

Festuca stuartiana, commonly known as Stuart's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Festuca stuartiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing rocky slopes.
