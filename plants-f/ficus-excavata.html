Ficus excavata

Ficus excavata, commonly known as the hollow fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its unique hollow stem, which gives it its common name. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a rough texture.

Ficus excavata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in indoor environments.
