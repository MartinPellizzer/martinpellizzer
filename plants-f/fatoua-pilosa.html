Fatoua pilosa

Fatoua pilosa, commonly known as the woolly fatoua, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage and small yellow flowers that appear in clusters. Fatoua pilosa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid or semi-arid environments.

It is used in traditional medicine and for its ornamental value due to its distinctive appearance.
