Funkiella tenella

Funkiella tenella, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and cylindrical stems. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.
