Ficus mexicana

Ficus mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered fruit. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually glossy and dark green, with a smooth texture.

Ficus mexicana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
