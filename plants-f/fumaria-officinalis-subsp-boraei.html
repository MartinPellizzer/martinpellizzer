Fumaria officinalis subsp. boraei

Fumaria officinalis subsp.

boraei, commonly known as the alpine smokeplant, is a perennial herb native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its seeds are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
