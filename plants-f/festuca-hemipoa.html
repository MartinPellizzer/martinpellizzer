Festuca hemipoa

Festuca hemipoa, commonly known as the slender fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.

Festuca hemipoa is also known for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
