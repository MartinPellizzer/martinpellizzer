Ficus consociata var. murtonii

Ficus consociata var. murtonii, commonly known as the Murton's fig, is a rare and unique variant of the Ficus consociata species.

It is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often clustered closely together. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native environment.
