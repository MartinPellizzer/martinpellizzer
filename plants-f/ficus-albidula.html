Ficus albidula

Ficus albidula, commonly known as the white fig or albidula fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Thailand, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate in form. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible figs, which are typically covered in a thin, whitish bloom.

Ficus albidula is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
