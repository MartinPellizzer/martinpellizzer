Feliciadamia

Feliciadamia, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a popular ornamental plant belonging to the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its segmented, jointed stems that bear vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and white. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Feliciadamia is often cultivated for its long blooming period, making it a favorite for holiday decorations.
