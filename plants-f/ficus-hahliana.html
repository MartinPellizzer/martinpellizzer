Ficus hahliana

Ficus hahliana, commonly known as the Hahli's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Thailand and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a smooth, waxy texture. It produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Ficus hahliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
