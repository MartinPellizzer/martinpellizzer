Feuilleea hirsuta

Feuilleea hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy leaf plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its fuzzy, velvety foliage.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or wet meadows, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. The plant's leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a dense covering of fine hairs that give it a distinctive appearance. Feuilleea hirsuta produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, often in shades of green or white, which are pollinated by insects.
