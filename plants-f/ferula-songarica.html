Ferula songarica

Ferula songarica, commonly known as the Chinese cow parsley, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia and parts of China.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and umbel-shaped inflorescences. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Ferula songarica is also valued for its resinous sap, which has been historically used in various applications.
