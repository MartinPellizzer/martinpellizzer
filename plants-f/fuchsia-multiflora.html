Fuchsia multiflora

Fuchsia multiflora, commonly known as the clustered fuchsia, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from deep purple to bright red. The plant produces small, oval berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Fuchsia multiflora thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
