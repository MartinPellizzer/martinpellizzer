Fuchsia coccinea

Fuchsia coccinea, commonly known as the scarlet fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red or pink bracts that surround its small, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy, dark green surface.

Fuchsia coccinea thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
