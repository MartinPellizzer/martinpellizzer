Ficus savannarum

Ficus savannarum, commonly known as the savanna fig, is a tropical tree native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, greyish bark. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a warm, humid climate.

Ficus savannarum is often found in savanna and woodland habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.
