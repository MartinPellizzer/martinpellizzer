Festuca bromoides var. altior

Festuca bromoides var. altior, commonly known as tall bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and tufted growth habit, often forming dense, low-growing clumps. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for various landscaping applications. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer.

Festuca bromoides var. altior is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to different environmental conditions.
