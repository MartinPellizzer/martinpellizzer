Ficus botryocarpa f. scabrida

Ficus botryocarpa f. scabrida, commonly known as the rough fig, is a variant of the botryocarpa fig species.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its thick, rough, and sometimes hairy leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, green figs that turn red when mature.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
