Ficus chaii

Ficus chaii, commonly known as the Chaii fig, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters in optimal conditions. Its fruit, a small fig-like structure, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

Ficus chaii is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
