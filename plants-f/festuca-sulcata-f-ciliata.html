Festuca sulcata f. ciliata

Festuca sulcata f. ciliata, commonly known as the serrated crested fescue, is a variant of the tall fescue species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive serrated leaf margins and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its fine texture and drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ecological and horticultural settings.
