Fiwa confertifolia

Fiwa confertifolia, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves and clusters of showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows as a low shrub, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters. Its flowers bloom in spring and summer, and it prefers well-drained soil and indirect sunlight.

Fiwa confertifolia is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
