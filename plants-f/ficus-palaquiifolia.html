Ficus palaquiifolia

Ficus palaquiifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically pear-shaped. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Ficus palaquiifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
