Ficus aculeata var. aculeata

Ficus aculeata var. aculeata, commonly known as the crocodile plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that have a distinctive textured surface resembling the skin of a crocodile. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by round, fuzzy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and air-purifying qualities, it is a popular houseplant.
