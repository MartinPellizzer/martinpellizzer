Ficus neapolitana

Ficus neapolitana, commonly known as the Naples fig tree, is a tropical plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with dense, dark green foliage and a compact, rounded shape. The leaves are thick, leathery, and have a waxy texture, often with a slight reddish or purple tinge on the underside. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fig-like fruits that are edible.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in indoor environments.
