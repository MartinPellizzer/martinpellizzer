Festuca guestfalica subsp. hirtula

Festuca guestfalica subsp.

hirtula, commonly known as the hairy fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Europe. It is characterized by its fine, hairy leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
