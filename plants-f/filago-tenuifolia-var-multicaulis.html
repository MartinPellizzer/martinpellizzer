Filago tenuifolia var. multicaulis

Filago tenuifolia var. multicaulis, commonly known as the slender filago, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, succulent stems that often form a dense, cushion-like structure. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in a fine layer of white hairs to reduce water loss.

This variety is notable for its ability to thrive in harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient and adaptive species.
