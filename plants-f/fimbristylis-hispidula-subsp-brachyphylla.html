Fimbristylis hispidula subsp. brachyphylla

Fimbristylis hispidula subsp.

brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-stemmed flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia. It typically grows in wet or seasonally wet environments such as swamps, marshes, and waterlogged soils.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense vegetation in suitable habitats.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
