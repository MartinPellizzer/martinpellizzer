Flemingia sarmentosa

Flemingia sarmentosa, commonly known as the climbing flemingia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a woody vine that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests. The plant produces small, fragrant white or yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Flemingia sarmentosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
