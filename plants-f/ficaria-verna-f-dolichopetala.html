Ficaria verna f. dolichopetala

Ficaria verna f. dolichopetala, commonly known as the long-petaled winter creeper, is a hybrid form of the common winter creeper.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in umbels. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking to add texture and color to shaded garden spaces.
