Ficus annamensis

Ficus annamensis, commonly known as the Annam fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam, Laos, and Cambodia. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, a fig, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

Ficus annamensis thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage.
