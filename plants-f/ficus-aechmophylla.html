Ficus aechmophylla

Ficus aechmophylla, commonly known as the warty fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, warty bark and glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, round fruits. Ficus aechmophylla is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its air-purifying properties and ornamental value.

It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect sunlight.
