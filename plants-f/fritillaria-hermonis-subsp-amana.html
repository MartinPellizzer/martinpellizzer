Fritillaria hermonis subsp. amana

Fritillaria hermonis subsp.

amana, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey. It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red, often with intricate patterns.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its bulbs are the primary means of propagation, and the plant is known for its resilience in arid conditions.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
