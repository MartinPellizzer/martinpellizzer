Ficus venosa

Ficus venosa, commonly known as the veiny fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a distinctive veined pattern, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant.

Its adaptability and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
