Ficus amblisyce

Ficus amblisyce, commonly known as the Amblisyce fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly found in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, edible fig fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
