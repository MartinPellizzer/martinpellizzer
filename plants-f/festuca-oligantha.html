Festuca oligantha

Festuca oligantha, commonly known as the slender fescue, is a perennial grass species native to arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, harsh environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in tundra and mountainous areas where it plays a role in soil stabilization.

Festuca oligantha is valued for its ecological importance in supporting local wildlife and its resilience in extreme climates.
