Ficus eggersii

Ficus eggersii, commonly known as the Eggers fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate. It produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when mature, though they may change color depending on the variety.

Ficus eggersii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
