Fargesia porphyrea

Fargesia porphyrea, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of China and is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stalks and striking purple foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, adding ornamental value to gardens. It is a cold-hardy plant that thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Fargesia porphyrea is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is a popular choice in landscape design.
