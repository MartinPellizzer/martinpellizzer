Festuca montana subsp. grandis

Festuca montana subsp.

grandis, commonly known as mountain fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often hairy leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences containing small, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Festuca montana subsp.

grandis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
