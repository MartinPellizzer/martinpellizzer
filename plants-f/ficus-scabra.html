Ficus scabra

Ficus scabra, commonly known as the rough fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and rough, textured leaves that are often covered in small, raised bumps. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptical in shape and can grow up to 15 centimeters in length. This plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits.

It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions.
