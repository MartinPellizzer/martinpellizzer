Ficus beccarii var. latifolia

Ficus beccarii var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible figs that are typically brown when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
