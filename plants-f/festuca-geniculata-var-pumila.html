Festuca geniculata var. pumila

Festuca geniculata var. pumila, commonly known as the alpine feathergrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to mountainous regions of Asia.

It features fine, silver-gray foliage and produces slender, feathery inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant habitats.
