Ferocactus recurvus

Ferocactus recurvus, commonly known as the twisted barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically clustered at the top of the stem. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Ferocactus recurvus is also known for its slow growth rate and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
