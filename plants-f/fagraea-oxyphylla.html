Fagraea oxyphylla

Fagraea oxyphylla, commonly known as the silver tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and can grow up to 20 meters in height. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces small, fragrant white flowers. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds.

Fagraea oxyphylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
