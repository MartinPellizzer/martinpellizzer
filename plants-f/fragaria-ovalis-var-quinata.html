Fragaria ovalis var. quinata

Fragaria ovalis var. quinata, commonly known as the quinata strawberry, is a botanical variant of the strawberry plant characterized by its unique leaf morphology and fruit structure.

It typically features trifoliate leaves with a slightly serrated margin, and its flowers are usually white with five petals. The fruits are small, aggregate berries that develop from multiple achenes, each containing a single seed. This variety is native to certain regions of Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and mild flavor.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized plant in botanical collections.
