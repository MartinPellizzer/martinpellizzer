Fraxinus spaethiana var. nipponica

Fraxinus spaethiana var. nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese ash, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, which appear in early spring before the leaves. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with serrated margins.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
