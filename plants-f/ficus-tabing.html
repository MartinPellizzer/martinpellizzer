Ficus tabing

Ficus tabing, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, elongated leaves that hang in a cascading fashion, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus tabing is popular as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
