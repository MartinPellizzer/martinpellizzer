Festuca sicula subvar. setacea

Festuca sicula subvar. setacea, commonly known as Sicilian fescue, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
