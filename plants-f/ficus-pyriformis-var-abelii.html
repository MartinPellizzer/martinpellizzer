Ficus pyriformis var. abelii

Ficus pyriformis var. abelii, commonly known as the Abel's fig or the round-leaf fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor settings.
