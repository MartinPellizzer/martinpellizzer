Festuca halleri subsp. riloensis

Festuca halleri subsp.

riloensis, commonly known as Riloensis bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that gives it its common name.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh climates.
