Fuchsia brevilobis

Fuchsia brevilobis, commonly known as the narrow-lobed fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to violet. The plant has opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Fuchsia brevilobis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees, contributing to its ecological significance.
