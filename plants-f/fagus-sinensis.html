Fagus sinensis

Fagus sinensis, commonly known as the Chinese beech, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and chestnuts, and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, triangular nuts enclosed in a spiny husk. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soils.

The Chinese beech plays an important role in forest ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
