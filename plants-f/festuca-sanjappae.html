Festuca sanjappae

Festuca sanjappae, commonly known as the Sanjappae fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Festuca sanjappae is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
