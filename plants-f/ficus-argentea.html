Ficus argentea

Ficus argentea, commonly known as the silver ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that have a silvery sheen on the underside, giving it its common name. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

The ficus argentea produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish fruits.
