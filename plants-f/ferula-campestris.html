Ferula campestris

Ferula campestris, commonly known as the field fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery, aromatic leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, which are followed by long, narrow seed pods. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine.

Ferula campestris is adapted to dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun.
