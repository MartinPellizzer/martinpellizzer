Ferula pubescens

Ferula pubescens, commonly known as the hairy ferula, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and finely divided leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family, and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its resilience to drought and extreme temperatures makes it an important species in ecological studies of desert ecosystems.
