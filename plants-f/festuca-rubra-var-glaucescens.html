Festuca rubra var. glaucescens

Festuca rubra var. glaucescens, commonly known as bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and distinctive bluish-gray foliage. The plant typically forms dense mats and thrives in well-drained soils, often found in meadows and open woodlands. Its leaves are narrow and have a slight sheen, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and ability to form a low-maintenance ground cover.
