Fraxinus bungeana var. cerifera

Fraxinus bungeana var. cerifera, commonly known as the Chinese ash or white ash, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by winged samaras for seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for urban landscaping.
