Festuca sibirica var. caucasica

Festuca sibirica var. caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian sedge, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Festuca sibirica var. caucasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native plant landscaping.
