Flindersia pubescens

Flindersia pubescens, commonly known as the silky flindersia, is a tropical tree native to northern Australia and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, simple, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish flowers followed by woody, cylindrical fruits that contain several winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and lowland rainforest areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in indigenous cultures.
