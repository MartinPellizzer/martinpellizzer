Ficus dictyophleba

Ficus dictyophleba, commonly known as the veined fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its distinctive veined leaves, which give it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white fig fruits that are typically enclosed in a spiky, spherical husk.

Ficus dictyophleba is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
