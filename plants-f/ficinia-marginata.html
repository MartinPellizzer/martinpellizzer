Ficinia marginata

Ficinia marginata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, preferring moist, acidic soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver along the edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish or reddish and appear in dense clusters.

Ficinia marginata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
