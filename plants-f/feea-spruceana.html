Feea spruceana

Feea spruceana, commonly known as the Spruce's feea, is a rare evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, needle-like leaves that are typically a dark green color, providing a striking visual contrast against its rugged habitat. The plant produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, Feea spruceana is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
