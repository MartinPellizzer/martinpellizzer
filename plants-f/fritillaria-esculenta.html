Fritillaria esculenta

Fritillaria esculenta, commonly known as the meadow fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or yellow with dark markings. The plant's bulbs are edible and have been historically used in various cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Fritillaria esculenta is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
