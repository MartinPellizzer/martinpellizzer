Fimbristylis henryi

Fimbristylis henryi, commonly known as Henry's sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically brown or reddish in color. Native to eastern Asia, particularly China, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils in woodland or marshy areas. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in shaded environments.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
