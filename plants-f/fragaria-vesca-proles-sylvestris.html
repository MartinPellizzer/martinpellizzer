Fragaria vesca proles sylvestris

Fragaria vesca proles sylvestris, commonly known as the wild strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fragrant white flowers that develop into red, juicy berries. The plant is characterized by its fibrous root system and its ability to thrive in woodland and meadow environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its botanical structure supports its role in ecological systems and traditional horticulture.
