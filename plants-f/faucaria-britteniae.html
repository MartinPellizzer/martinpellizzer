Faucaria britteniae

Faucaria britteniae, commonly known as the Britten's windowleaf, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often marked with white or pinkish markings. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick leaves.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
