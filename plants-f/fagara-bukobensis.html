Fagara bukobensis

Fagara bukobensis, commonly known as the Bukoban fagara, is a member of the Rutaceae family, native to the tropical rainforests of East Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. Its flowers are small and clustered, often yellowish-green in color, and it produces small, round berries that are edible.

Fagara bukobensis plays a role in local ecosystems and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
