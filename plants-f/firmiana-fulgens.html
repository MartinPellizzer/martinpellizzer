Firmiana fulgens

Firmiana fulgens, commonly known as the flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red bracts that resemble flames, which appear before the actual flowers. The plant has compound leaves that are pinnately arranged, with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its rapid growth and ability to provide shade in urban landscapes.
