Forsteronia subcordata

Forsteronia subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed forsteria, is a perennial shrub native to the Cape Province of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, arranged in dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Forsteronia subcordata is well adapted to arid conditions, making it a hardy plant suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
