Festuca anatolica subsp. borealis

Festuca anatolica subsp.

borealis, commonly known as the northern meadow fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and riverbanks.

The plant has slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single spike or a few spikes, bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
