Festuca tenella var. tenuicula

Festuca tenella var. tenuicula, commonly known as slender meadowgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It features fine, needle-like leaves that are typically blue-gray in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale green to brownish spikelets that are arranged in dense clusters along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges.

Festuca tenella var. tenuicula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators and soil stabilization.
