Falkia diffusa

Falkia diffusa, commonly known as the rock wallaby tree, is a species of flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a small, evergreen shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture.

Falkia diffusa is known for its ability to thrive in arid conditions, making it a hardy plant in dry environments.
