Ficinia aphylla

Ficinia aphylla, commonly known as the leafless fig, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lack of leaves, which is an adaptation to its specific ecological niche. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by various bird species. Despite its leafless appearance, it has a well-developed root system that helps it survive in nutrient-poor soils.

Its botanical significance lies in its unusual morphology and role in local ecosystems.
