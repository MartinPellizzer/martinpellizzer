Frangula rubra subsp. yosemitana

Frangula rubra subsp.

yosemitana, commonly known as the Sierra juniper, is a shrub or small tree native to the Sierra Nevada mountain range in California. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, blue-gray leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into dark blue berries, which are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in rocky soils and is adapted to the dry, cold conditions of high elevation environments.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in maintaining biodiversity in its native habitat.
