Freycinetia erythrophylla

Freycinetia erythrophylla, commonly known as the red-fruited pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that form a rosette at the base. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by distinctive red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often used in traditional crafts and as a source of natural fibers.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular plant in horticulture.
