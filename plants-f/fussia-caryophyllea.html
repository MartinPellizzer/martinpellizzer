Fussia caryophyllea

Fussia caryophyllea, commonly known as the sweet box, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Asia.

It features dense, glossy dark green leaves that are often fragrant when crushed. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in spring, which are followed by red berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Fussia caryophyllea is also used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.
