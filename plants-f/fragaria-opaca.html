Fragaria opaca

Fragaria opaca, commonly known as the purple foamflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces edible, strawberry-like fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its leaves are heart-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs, giving them a fuzzy appearance.

Fragaria opaca thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.
