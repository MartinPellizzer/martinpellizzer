Fuchsia chloroloba

Fuchsia chloroloba, commonly known as the golden fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow to golden orange flowers, which contrast with its glossy green foliage. The plant is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as cloud forests. Its flowers are tubular and attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.

Fuchsia chloroloba is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
