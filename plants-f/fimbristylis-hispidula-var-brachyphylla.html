Fimbristylis hispidula var. brachyphylla

Fimbristylis hispidula var. brachyphylla, commonly known as the short-stalked flat sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that typically bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and the edges of ponds, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
