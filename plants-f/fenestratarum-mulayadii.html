Fenestratarum mulayadii

Fenestratarum mulayadii, commonly known as the window-leaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive fenestrated leaves.

These leaves feature natural openings or holes, giving the plant its common name and contributing to its ornamental appeal. Native to tropical regions, it thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for indoor cultivation as a decorative plant.
