Festuca glauca subsp. pallens

Festuca glauca subsp.

pallens, commonly known as silverblue grass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern regions of North America. It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage that gives it a striking appearance in gardens and natural landscapes.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as wet meadows and riparian zones. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like clusters of blue to purple flowers that bloom in early summer. Festuca glauca subsp.

pallens is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits, providing habitat for pollinators and soil stabilization.
