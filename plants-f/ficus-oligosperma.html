Ficus oligosperma

Ficus oligosperma, commonly known as the small-fig or dwarf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and produces small, edible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Ficus oligosperma is also valued for its ability to adapt to indoor environments, making it a popular choice for houseplants.
