Ficus trichocarpa var. borneensis

Ficus trichocarpa var. borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean fig, is a species native to the tropical regions of Borneo.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in lowland rainforests. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green and often have a waxy texture. It produces small, fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The plant's rapid growth and adaptability make it a common ornamental and horticultural species in tropical climates.
