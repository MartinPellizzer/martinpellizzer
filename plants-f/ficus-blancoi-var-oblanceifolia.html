Ficus blancoi var. oblanceifolia

Ficus blancoi var. oblanceifolia, commonly known as the white ficus or variegated ficus, is a species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white or pale variegation. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters in its natural habitat. Its leaves are elongated and oblong, with a slightly wavy margin.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
