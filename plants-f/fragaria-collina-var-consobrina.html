Fragaria collina var. consobrina

Fragaria collina var. consobrina, commonly known as the alpine strawberry, is a low-growing perennial plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically red in color and have a sweet, tangy flavor. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets, and it has a fibrous root system.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in traditional cuisines.
