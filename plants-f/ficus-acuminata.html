Ficus acuminata

Ficus acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called syconia. Its roots are fibrous and capable of producing aerial roots, which help it anchor to surfaces in its natural habitat.

Ficus acuminata is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
