Ficus ulei

Ficus ulei, commonly known as the Hawaiian fig, is a flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Hawaiian Islands and is characterized by its glossy, dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when ripe. Ficus ulei thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its ability to adapt to various soil types makes it a resilient choice for tropical and subtropical gardens.
