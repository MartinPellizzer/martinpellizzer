Ficus subtriplinervia

Ficus subtriplinervia, commonly known as the three-veined fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have three prominent veins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its adaptability makes it suitable for indoor cultivation in suitable conditions.
