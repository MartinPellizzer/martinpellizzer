Ficus tinctoria var. cuspidifera

Ficus tinctoria var. cuspidifera, commonly known as the pointed-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features broad, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy surface and a pointed tip, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically clustered in small groups. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its leaves are also used in traditional medicine and dye-making due to their unique properties.
