Ficus buntingii

Ficus buntingii, commonly known as the African fig tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, edible figs that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate to flourish.

Ficus buntingii is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in landscaping.
