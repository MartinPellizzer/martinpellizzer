Fadogia elskensii var. ufipaensis

Fadogia elskensii var. ufipaensis, commonly known as the Ufipa African butterbean, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its leguminous properties and ability to fix nitrogen in the soil. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are often clustered in inflorescences, followed by pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and slightly hairy.

This species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and for its potential agricultural use in sustainable farming systems.
