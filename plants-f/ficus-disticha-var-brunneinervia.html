Ficus disticha var. brunneinervia

Ficus disticha var. brunneinervia, commonly known as the brown-veined ficus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent, reddish-brown veins. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters in its natural habitat. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape and often have a waxy texture.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.
