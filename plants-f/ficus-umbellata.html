Ficus umbellata

Ficus umbellata, commonly known as the clustered fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are arranged in dense clusters at the ends of branches. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically clustered in umbels. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ficus umbellata is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
