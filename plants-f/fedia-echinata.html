Fedia echinata

Fedia echinata, commonly known as the spiny poinsettia, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that resemble flowers, which are actually modified leaves. The plant has a woody stem with thorny branches, and its true flowers are small, greenish-yellow blooms. Fedia echinata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
