Fontainea fugax

Fontainea fugax, commonly known as the transient fern, is a rare and delicate plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that give it a distinctive, airy appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity to flourish. Its leaves are typically bioluminescent under certain conditions, a unique feature that aids in its survival.

Due to its elusive nature and limited distribution, Fontainea fugax is considered a rare and enigmatic species in the botanical world.
