Ficus infectoria var. cunninghamii

Ficus infectoria var. cunninghamii, commonly known as the African fig or wild fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible figs that are typically consumed raw or cooked. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in rainforests or along riverbanks.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
