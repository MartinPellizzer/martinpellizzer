Festuca rubra var. lanuginosa

Festuca rubra var. lanuginosa, commonly known as silky fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, hairy texture and dense, low-growing habit, making it well-suited for lawns and erosion control. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to reddish flowers, which appear in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green color that adds a decorative element to landscapes.

Festuca rubra var. lanuginosa is drought-tolerant and thrives in a variety of soil types, making it a versatile choice for horticultural use.
