Fuchsia salicifolia

Fuchsia salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant has narrow, willow-like leaves that give it its common name. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Fuchsia salicifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
