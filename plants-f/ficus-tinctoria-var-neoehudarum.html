Ficus tinctoria var. neoehudarum

Ficus tinctoria var. neoehudarum, commonly known as the red-veined ficus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves with deep red veins contrasting against dark green foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, greenish-white fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
