Flavia

Flavia, commonly known as the bluebell, is a flowering plant belonging to the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and prefers well-drained, moist soil. Its leaves are long and grass-like, often appearing after the flowers have bloomed.

Flavia is known for its fragrance and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.
