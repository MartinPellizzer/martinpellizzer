Ficus apoensis

Ficus apoensis, commonly known as the Philippine fig tree, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Ficus apoensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
