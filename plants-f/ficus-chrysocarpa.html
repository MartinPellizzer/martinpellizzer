Ficus chrysocarpa

Ficus chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden fruit fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is well-suited for tropical and subtropical gardens.
