Festuca elatior var. fenas

Festuca elatior var. fenas, commonly known as the tall fescue, is a cool-season grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, fine-textured foliage. The plant typically reaches heights of 30 to 60 centimeters and has a deep root system that allows it to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. Its leaves are narrow and blade-like, with a bluish-green color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences in the summer.

Festuca elatior var. fenas is often used in lawns, pastures, and erosion control due to its durability and adaptability.
