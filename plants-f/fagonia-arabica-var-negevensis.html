Fagonia arabica var. negevensis

Fagonia arabica var. negevensis, commonly known as the Negev fagonia, is a shrub native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is adapted to hot, dry environments and has a deep root system for water retention. It belongs to the family Capparaceae and is often found in rocky soils.

Its resilience makes it a useful plant for xeriscaping and erosion control in semi-arid landscapes.
