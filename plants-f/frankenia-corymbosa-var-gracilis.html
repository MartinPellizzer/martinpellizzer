Frankenia corymbosa var. gracilis

Frankenia corymbosa var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender frankenia, is a flowering plant native to arid and coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant is adapted to withstand drought and high salinity, making it suitable for coastal environments. Its growth habit and foliage make it an attractive ornamental plant in dry gardens.

Frankenia corymbosa var. gracilis is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for small insects.
