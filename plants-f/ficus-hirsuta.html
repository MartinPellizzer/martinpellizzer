Ficus hirsuta

Ficus hirsuta, commonly known as the woolly fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety grayish-white pubescence covering its stems and leaves. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a waxy texture and a dark green upper surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in traditional medicine.

This plant thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental species.
