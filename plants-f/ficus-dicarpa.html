Ficus dicarpa

Ficus dicarpa, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, round fruits resembling small bread loaves. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
