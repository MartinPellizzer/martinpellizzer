Ficus rolandri

Ficus rolandri, commonly known as the Roland's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Ficus rolandri is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Its growth is typically slow, making it suitable for container gardening.
