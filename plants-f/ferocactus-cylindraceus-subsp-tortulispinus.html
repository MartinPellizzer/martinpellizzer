Ferocactus cylindraceus subsp. tortulispinus

Ferocactus cylindraceus subsp.

tortulispinus, commonly known as the tortuous spine cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are often ribbed and covered in spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, storing water in its thick stems.

This subspecies is valued for its unique appearance and resilience in extreme environments.
