Fuchsia pyrifolia

Fuchsia pyrifolia, commonly known as the pineapple fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring attractive, glossy, ovate leaves with a reddish hue. The plant produces showy, pendulous inflorescences with vibrant pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. Its unique foliage and striking floral display make it a popular ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.

Fuchsia pyrifolia thrives in cool, moist environments and requires partial shade to flourish.
