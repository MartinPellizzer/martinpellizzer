Fragaria ovalis

Fragaria ovalis, commonly known as the oval strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, loamy soils. Its flowers are white with five petals and are followed by red, fleshy fruits that are prized for their sweet taste.

Fragaria ovalis is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
