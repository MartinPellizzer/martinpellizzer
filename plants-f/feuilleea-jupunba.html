Feuilleea jupunba

Feuilleea jupunba, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery bloom, giving it its common name. The flowers are typically pale blue or white, with a distinctive spurred shape that attracts specific pollinators. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
