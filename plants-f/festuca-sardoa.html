Festuca sardoa

Festuca sardoa, commonly known as Sardinian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the island of Sardinia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as rocky slopes and coastal areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to the Mediterranean climate, tolerating dry conditions and salt spray.

Festuca sardoa plays a role in stabilizing soils and supporting local biodiversity.
