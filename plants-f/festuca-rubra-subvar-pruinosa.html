Festuca rubra subvar. pruinosa

Festuca rubra subvar. pruinosa, commonly known as the purple moor grass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and distinctive purple or reddish hues on its leaf blades and inflorescence. The plant thrives in acidic, moist soils and is often found in heathlands, moors, and along coastal areas. Its foliage is covered in a fine, powdery bloom, giving it a silvery appearance.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to stabilize soils in challenging environments.
