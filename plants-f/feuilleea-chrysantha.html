Feuilleea chrysantha

Feuilleea chrysantha, commonly known as the golden leaf sunflower, is a striking herbaceous plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly waxy texture, adapted to reduce water loss in its dry habitat. Its stems are sturdy and upright, often reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters.

Feuilleea chrysantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
