Ficus tengerensis

Ficus tengerensis, commonly known as the Tenger's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Tanzania and the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The plant is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus tengerensis is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
