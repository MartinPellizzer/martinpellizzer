Flourensia leptopoda

Flourensia leptopoda, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flourensia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and heat. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.
