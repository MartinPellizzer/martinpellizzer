Ficus hararensis

Ficus hararensis, commonly known as the Harare fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to eastern Africa, particularly Zimbabwe, and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and small, clustered fig fruits. The plant is typically evergreen and can grow as a tree or shrub, depending on environmental conditions. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Ficus hararensis is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and adaptability.
