Fuchsia tuberosa var. typica

Fuchsia tuberosa var. typica, commonly known as the common fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically range in color from red to purple. The plant produces tuberous roots, which are adapted for storing nutrients and water. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage consists of glossy, ovate leaves that provide a striking contrast to its colorful blooms.
