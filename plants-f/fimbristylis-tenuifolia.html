Fimbristylis tenuifolia

Fimbristylis tenuifolia, commonly known as slender threadgrass, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop in dense clusters. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomes are slender and help it spread vegetatively, contributing to its ability to colonize moist soils.

The species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
