Fritillaria dajinensis

Fritillaria dajinensis, commonly known as the Yunnan fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a distinctive, elongated bulb that serves as its storage organ. Fritillaria dajinensis typically blooms in spring and is adapted to cool, humid climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and is of interest to horticulturists and botanists alike.
