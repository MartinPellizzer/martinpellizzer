Fimbristylis exilis var. rufescens

Fimbristylis exilis var. rufescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed flatsedge, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in spikelets. Its stems are usually reddish in color, which gives it its common name.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family.
