Festuca filiformis f. mucronata

Festuca filiformis f. mucronata, commonly known as the mucronate fineleaf fescue, is a low-growing grass species characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves.

It is native to regions of Europe and western Asia, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its foliage is typically dark green and remains dense throughout the growing season.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
