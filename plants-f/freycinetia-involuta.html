Freycinetia involuta

Freycinetia involuta, commonly known as the silky fig or velvet fern, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Pandan family (Pandanaceae) and is characterized by its striking, feathery foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a flavoring agent in cooking.

Freycinetia involuta thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found growing in wetlands or along riverbanks.
