Ficus praecox

Ficus praecox, commonly known as the early fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into edible fig fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Ficus praecox is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food in its native regions.
