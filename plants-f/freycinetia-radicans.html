Freycinetia radicans

Freycinetia radicans, commonly known as the climbing pandanus, is a member of the Pandanaceae family.

It is a tropical plant characterized by its long, slender stems that can climb or trail along the ground. The plant produces narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of aromatic fibers. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in inflorescences at the base of the leaves.

Freycinetia radicans thrives in humid, coastal environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.
