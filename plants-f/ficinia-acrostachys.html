Ficinia acrostachys

Ficinia acrostachys, commonly known as the silver tuft plant, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Araceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and striking, silver-white leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is popular among plant enthusiasts.
