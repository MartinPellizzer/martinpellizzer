Ficus preussii

Ficus preussii, commonly known as the preussii fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. It produces small, greenish flowers that develop into edible figs, which are typically consumed by birds and other animals.

Ficus preussii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
