Fritillaria kordestanica

Fritillaria kordestanica, commonly known as the Kordestan fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Iran and Turkey.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color, with a distinctive pattern resembling a checkerboard. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winters, and it blooms in early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
