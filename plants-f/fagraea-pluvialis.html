Fagraea pluvialis

Fagraea pluvialis, commonly known as the rainforest beauty or white flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, rounded crown. The leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical, with a smooth texture and a dark green color. Its flowers are small, white, and fragrant, arranged in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

The plant produces woody, oblong fruits that contain several seeds, contributing to its role in forest ecosystems.
