Fraxinus triptera

Fraxinus triptera, commonly known as the three-lobed ash.

It is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia, particularly China and Japan. The plant has compound leaves with three distinct leaflets, each with a serrated margin. Its flowers are small and green, appearing in clusters, and it produces small, winged fruits.

The bark is smooth and gray, and the tree is valued for its ornamental appearance and hard, durable wood.
