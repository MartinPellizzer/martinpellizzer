Festuca violacea proles nigricans

Festuca violacea proles nigricans, commonly known as purple false brome, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and striking purple to violet-colored inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is narrow and dark green, with a fine texture that makes it suitable for ornamental and ecological landscaping.

The species is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various invertebrates.
