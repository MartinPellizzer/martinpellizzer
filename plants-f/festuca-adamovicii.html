Festuca adamovicii

Festuca adamovicii, commonly known as Siberian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of Siberia and northern Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including low temperatures and limited moisture. Festuca adamovicii is often found in alpine and subarctic regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems.

Its hardy nature makes it a popular choice for landscaping in cooler climates.
