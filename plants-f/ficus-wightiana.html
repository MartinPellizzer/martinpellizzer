Ficus wightiana

Ficus wightiana, commonly known as the Wight's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like India and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. It produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor.

Ficus wightiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
