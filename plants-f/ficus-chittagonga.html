Ficus chittagonga

Ficus chittagonga, commonly known as the Chittagong fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South and Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Bangladesh, and Myanmar. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine and as a source of food for wildlife.
