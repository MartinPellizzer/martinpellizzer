Ficus dulitensis

Ficus dulitensis, commonly known as the Socotra fig, is a unique species native to the island of Socotra.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that resemble those of other ficus species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically consumed when ripe.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to the harsh conditions of its native habitat.
