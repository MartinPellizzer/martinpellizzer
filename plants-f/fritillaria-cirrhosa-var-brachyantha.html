Fritillaria cirrhosa var. brachyantha

Fritillaria cirrhosa var. brachyantha, commonly known as the Himalayan fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a dense rosette near the ground.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
