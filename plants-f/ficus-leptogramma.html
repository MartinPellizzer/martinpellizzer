Ficus leptogramma

Ficus leptogramma, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Ficus leptogramma is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
