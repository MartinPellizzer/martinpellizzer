Ferocactus cylindraceus var. lecontei

Ferocactus cylindraceus var. lecontei, commonly known as the Leconte's barrel cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by edible, red fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring minimal water and full sun exposure.

Its slow growth rate and unique appearance make it a popular choice for desert gardens and xeriscaping.
