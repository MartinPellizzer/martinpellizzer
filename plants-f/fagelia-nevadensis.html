Fagelia nevadensis

Fagelia nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada fir, is a rare and endemic conifer native to the mountainous regions of Nevada, USA.

It is characterized by its slender, pyramidal shape and dark green, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in clusters. The tree produces small, cone-like structures that contain its seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions and well-drained soils.

Due to its limited range and habitat specificity, Fagelia nevadensis is considered a vulnerable species.
