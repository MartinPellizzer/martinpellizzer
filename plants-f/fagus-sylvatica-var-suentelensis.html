Fagus sylvatica var. suentelensis

Fagus sylvatica var. suentelensis, commonly known as the Carpathian beech, is a subspecies of the common beech native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its distinctive elliptical leaves with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and it produces small, pale yellow flowers in the spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological importance and is often found in mixed forests.
