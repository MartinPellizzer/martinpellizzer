Festuca stricta subsp. carnuntina

Festuca stricta subsp.

carnuntina, commonly known as the narrowleaf fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks.

The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green in color. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
