Ficus landonii

Ficus landonii, commonly known as the Landon's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly found in countries like Mexico and Costa Rica. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Ficus landonii is valued for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity and its potential use in traditional medicine.
