Ficus ampelos var. sublanceolata

Ficus ampelos var. sublanceolata, commonly known as the weeping fig or curly willow fig, is a tropical plant characterized by its cascading, weeping branches.

It features broad, glossy, dark green leaves that are often slightly curled or wavy in texture. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid environments. It is a popular indoor plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

However, it can be toxic if ingested, making it important to keep it away from pets and children.
