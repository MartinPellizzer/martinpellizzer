Ficus ursina

Ficus ursina, commonly known as the bear fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by wildlife. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Ficus ursina is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
