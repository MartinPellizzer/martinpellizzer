Festuca duriuscula f. fratrensis

Festuca duriuscula f. fratrensis, commonly known as the Fratren's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and typically have a bluish-green hue, while the inflorescence consists of a loose to dense cluster of spikelets. This variety is adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, thriving in alpine and subalpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh habitats.
