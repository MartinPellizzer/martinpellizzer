Ficus difformis

Ficus difformis, commonly known as the Variegated Ficus, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white or light green markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, round fruits known as figs. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability.

Its ability to grow in low-light conditions makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
