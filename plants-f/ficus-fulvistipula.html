Ficus fulvistipula

Ficus fulvistipula, commonly known as the golden-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia, where it thrives in tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a slight yellowish tinge on the underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Ficus fulvistipula is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
