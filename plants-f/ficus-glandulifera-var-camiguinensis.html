Ficus glandulifera var. camiguinensis

Ficus glandulifera var. camiguinensis, commonly known as the Camiguin fig, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically clustered in spherical syconia. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and disturbed areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
