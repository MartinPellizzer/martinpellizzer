Festuca ovina subvar. macrophylla

Festuca ovina subvar. macrophylla, commonly known as sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and used in landscaping.
