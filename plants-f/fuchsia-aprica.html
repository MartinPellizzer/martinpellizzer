Fuchsia aprica

Fuchsia aprica, commonly known as the early fuchsia, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It features showy, pendulous flowers that range in color from pink to purple, blooming in early spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Fuchsia aprica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
