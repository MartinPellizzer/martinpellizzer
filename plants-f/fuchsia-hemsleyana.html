Fuchsia hemsleyana

Fuchsia hemsleyana, commonly known as the Hemsley's fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from deep purple to bright red. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.

Fuchsia hemsleyana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
