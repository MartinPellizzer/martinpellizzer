Fimbristylis semarangensis

Fimbristylis semarangensis, commonly known as the Semarang sedge, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in wetland and marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, contributing to soil stabilization in its native habitat.
