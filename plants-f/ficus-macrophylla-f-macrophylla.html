Ficus macrophylla f. macrophylla

Ficus macrophylla f. macrophylla, commonly known as the Benjamin fig, is a cultivar of the Ficus macrophylla species, native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically broader and more elongated compared to the parent species. The plant is a popular indoor and outdoor ornamental due to its adaptability and attractive foliage. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fig-like fruits, though these are rarely seen in cultivation.

The Benjamin fig is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
