Filago tyrrhenica

Filago tyrrhenica, commonly known as the Tyrrhenian filago, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Italy and parts of southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its hairy stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Filago tyrrhenica is an herbaceous perennial that plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
