Ferula orientalis

Ferula orientalis, commonly known as the common wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, umbrella-like inflorescences that produce yellow flowers. The plant has aromatic, gray-green leaves and is known for its strong, pungent scent. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Ferula orientalis is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and resilience in dry climates.
