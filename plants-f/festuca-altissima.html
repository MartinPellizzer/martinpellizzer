Festuca altissima

Festuca altissima, commonly known as tall fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and coarse, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, spikelet-based flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in landscaping and erosion control.

Festuca altissima is adapted to a wide range of soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade.
