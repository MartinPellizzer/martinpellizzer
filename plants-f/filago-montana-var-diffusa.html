Filago montana var. diffusa

Filago montana var. diffusa, commonly known as the mountain filago, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are yellow with a central disk.

Filago montana var. diffusa is known for its resilience to harsh climates and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
