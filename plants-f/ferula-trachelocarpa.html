Ferula trachelocarpa

Ferula trachelocarpa, commonly known as the Persian cow's milk, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, which bloom in late summer. Its taproot is thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine.

Ferula trachelocarpa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh climates.
