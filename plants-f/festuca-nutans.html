Festuca nutans

Festuca nutans, commonly known as nodding fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and distinctive nodding inflorescence, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along streambanks. Festuca nutans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

It is also an important forage plant for livestock due to its nutritional value.
