Ficinia trigyna

Ficinia trigyna, commonly known as the three-lobed figvine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes other fig species. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name. Its small, greenish flowers are followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife.

Ficinia trigyna is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
