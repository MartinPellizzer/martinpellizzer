Ficus laevigata var. genuina

Ficus laevigata var. genuina, commonly known as the Mexican fig or the red-fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Mexico and Central America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green to reddish in color. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate indoor environments, making it a popular choice for houseplants.
