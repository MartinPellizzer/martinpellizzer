Filago spathulata var. alexandrina

Filago spathulata var. alexandrina, commonly known as the Alexandrian filago, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its daisy-like inflorescence, consisting of a yellow disk surrounded by white to pale pink ray florets. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and often branched.

This variety is native to parts of the Middle East and North Africa, where it is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
