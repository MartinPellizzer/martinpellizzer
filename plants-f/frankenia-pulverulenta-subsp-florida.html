Frankenia pulverulenta subsp. florida

Frankenia pulverulenta subsp.

florida, commonly known as Florida beach morning glory, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States. It is a low-growing perennial shrub that thrives in sandy, coastal environments.

The plant produces small, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple, blooming in the early morning. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, adapted to withstand salt spray and arid conditions.

This species is often found in dune ecosystems, playing a role in stabilizing coastal soils.
