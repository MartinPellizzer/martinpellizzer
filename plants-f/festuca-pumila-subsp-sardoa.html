Festuca pumila subsp. sardoa

Festuca pumila subsp.

sardoa, commonly known as the Sardinian fine fescue, is a low-growing, perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It typically forms dense, mat-like growths with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky texture.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny, pale green to brownish flowers. It is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant landscaping.
