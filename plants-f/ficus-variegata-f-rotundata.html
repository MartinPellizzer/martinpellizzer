Ficus variegata f. rotundata

Ficus variegata f. rotundata, commonly known as the variegated rubber plant, is a cultivar of the rubber fig tree.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white and yellow variegation. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid environments. It has a thick, woody stem and produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fig-like fruits.

This cultivar is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive indoors with minimal care.
