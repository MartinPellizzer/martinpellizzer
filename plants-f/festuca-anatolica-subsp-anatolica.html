Festuca anatolica subsp. anatolica

Festuca anatolica subsp.

anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Turkey. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
