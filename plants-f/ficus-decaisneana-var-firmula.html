Ficus decaisneana var. firmula

Ficus decaisneana var. firmula, commonly known as the Decaisne's fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and have a sweet, fig-like flavor. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor conditions.
