Ferula verticillata

Ferula verticillata, commonly known as the Persian cow parsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus. The plant is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white or yellowish flowers. Its leaves are finely divided and resemble those of other umbelliferous plants.

Ferula verticillata is often grown for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
