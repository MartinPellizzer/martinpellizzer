Fritillaria austroanhuiensis

Fritillaria austroanhuiensis, commonly known as the Anhui fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color. The plant features a fibrous root system and produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with a temperate climate.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
