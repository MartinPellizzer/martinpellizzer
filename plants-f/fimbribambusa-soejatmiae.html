Fimbribambusa soejatmiae

Fimbribambusa soejatmiae, commonly known as the Sujatmaja bamboo, is a species of grass in the Bambusoideae subfamily.

It is native to Indonesia and is characterized by its slender, erect culms that are often used in traditional construction. The plant exhibits a clumping growth habit and has narrow, linear leaves with a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and sustainable use, it is cultivated in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
