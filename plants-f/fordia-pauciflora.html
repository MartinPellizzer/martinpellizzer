Fordia pauciflora

Fordia pauciflora, commonly known as the dwarf flame tree, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has a compact growth habit with dense, dark green foliage that turns yellow in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Fordia pauciflora is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ease of care.
