Ficus carica var. dulcissima

Ficus carica var. dulcissima, commonly known as the Persian fig, is a cultivar of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces small, green figs that ripen to a sweet, amber color, making it a popular choice for cultivation. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and edible fruit, which is often used in culinary applications.
