Ficus eriobotryoides var. latifolia

Ficus eriobotryoides var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically round and yellow when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The tree is also valued for its ability to adapt to various soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
