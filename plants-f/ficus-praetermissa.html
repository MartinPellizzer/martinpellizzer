Ficus praetermissa

Ficus praetermissa, commonly known as the small-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and is often grown as a houseplant due to its adaptability. Ficus praetermissa produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible fig fruits.

It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
