Ficus populnea subvar. flavicosta

Ficus populnea subvar. flavicosta, commonly known as the yellow-striped fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves that feature a distinct yellow or pale green stripe running along the midrib. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically green to yellow in color. It thrives in humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

The species is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
