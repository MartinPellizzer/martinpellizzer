Ficus pedunculosa var. confertifolia

Ficus pedunculosa var. confertifolia, commonly known as the dense-leaved fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, ovate leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, compact cluster at the ends of its branches. The plant has a short, thick stem with a smooth, grayish-brown bark. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are followed by edible, green figs that mature to a yellowish or reddish color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental foliage and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
