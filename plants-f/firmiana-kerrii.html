Firmiana kerrii

Firmiana kerrii, commonly known as the Kerri's silk tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically trifoliate and have a glossy, dark green appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by elongated, woody pods containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its ability to tolerate poor soil conditions and its use in traditional medicine.
