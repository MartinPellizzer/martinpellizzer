Ficus ceanothifolia

Ficus ceanothifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in traditional medicine.

Ficus ceanothifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
