Frangula acuminata

Frangula acuminata, commonly known as the Canadian buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its bark is thick and dark, often with a reddish tint, and it contains compounds used in traditional medicine.

Frangula acuminata is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions and its role in supporting local wildlife.
