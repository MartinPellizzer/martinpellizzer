Ferula dissoluta

Ferula dissoluta, commonly known as the Kashmiri ferula, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is a tall, perennial herb that can grow up to 3 meters in height, featuring a thick, aromatic rhizome and large, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in a branched inflorescence, which bloom in the summer months. Its essential oils, extracted from the roots and rhizomes, are valued for their medicinal properties.

Ferula dissoluta is also noted for its ecological importance in arid and semi-arid environments.
