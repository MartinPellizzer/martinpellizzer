Fritillaria graeca subsp. graeca

Fritillaria graeca subsp.

graeca, commonly known as the Greek fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece and Turkey. It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or orange, with a distinctive waxy texture.

The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short stem that supports its showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
