Foetataxus taxifolia

Foetataxus taxifolia, commonly known as the slender yew, is a rare and ancient coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and its distinctive, scale-like leaves that are arranged in a flat, fan-like pattern. The plant produces small, red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife species. Foetataxus taxifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional medicine in some cultures.

Due to habitat loss and limited distribution, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural range.
