Fragaria unifolia

Fragaria unifolia, commonly known as the strawberry cinquefoil, is a perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, red, edible fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are white with five petals, and it produces fruit that ripens in late summer.

The botanical features of Fragaria unifolia make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
