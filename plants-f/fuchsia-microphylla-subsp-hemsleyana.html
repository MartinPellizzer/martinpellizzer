Fuchsia microphylla subsp. hemsleyana

Fuchsia microphylla subsp.

hemsleyana, commonly known as the Hemsley's fuchsia, is a species native to South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink and red.

The leaves are typically small, ovate, and glossy, providing a striking contrast to the vibrant blooms. This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.

It thrives in cool, humid conditions and is typically grown as a perennial in temperate climates.
