Fritillaria sulcisquamosa

Fritillaria sulcisquamosa, commonly known as the Sicilian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive black or purple blotch at the base. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, showcasing its adaptability to arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a basal rosette of foliage.

Fritillaria sulcisquamosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
