Fatoua cordata

Fatoua cordata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed fatoua, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb with slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white to pale blue flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Fatoua cordata is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to coastal and arid environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
