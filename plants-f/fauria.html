Fauria

Fauria, commonly known as the false hellebore, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Araceae.

It consists of several species, most notably Fauria minor, which is native to Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its striking, arrow-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white to greenish flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Fauria is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and interesting floral structures.
