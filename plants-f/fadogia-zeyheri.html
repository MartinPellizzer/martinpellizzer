Fadogia zeyheri

Fadogia zeyheri, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, edible pods that contain seeds used in traditional medicine. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Fadogia zeyheri is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
