Festuca durandoi subsp. livida

Festuca durandoi subsp.

livida, commonly known as the bluegrass or silvergrass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of Europe. It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, blue-green flowers. Festuca durandoi subsp.

livida is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
