Ficus galpinii

Ficus galpinii, commonly known as the Galpin's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
