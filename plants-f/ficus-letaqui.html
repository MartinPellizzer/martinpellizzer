Ficus letaqui

Ficus letaqui, commonly known as the African rubber fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by fig-like fruits, which are edible and attract various wildlife.

Ficus letaqui thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
