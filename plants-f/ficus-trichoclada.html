Ficus trichoclada

Ficus trichoclada, commonly known as the hairy fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible figs that are typically small and yellow when ripe. Ficus trichoclada thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.

Its adaptability to indoor conditions makes it a popular choice for those seeking a low-maintenance, decorative plant.
