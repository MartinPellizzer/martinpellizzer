Ficus erratica

Ficus erratica, commonly known as the mountain fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped or oval in form. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping in warm climates.
