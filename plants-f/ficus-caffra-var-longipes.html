Ficus caffra var. longipes

Ficus caffra var. longipes, commonly known as the Natal fig or long-stemmed Natal fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, and is characterized by its long, slender stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically consumed raw or cooked. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Ficus caffra var. longipes is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
