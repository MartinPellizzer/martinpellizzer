Festuca duvalii

Festuca duvalii, commonly known as Duval's fescue, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and woodlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in shaded or partially shaded environments.

Festuca duvalii is also known for its hardiness and adaptability to various soil conditions.
