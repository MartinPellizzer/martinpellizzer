Ficus jaliscana

Ficus jaliscana, commonly known as the Jalisco fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Mexico, specifically the state of Jalisco, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, a small fig-like structure, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

Ficus jaliscana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
