Ficus macropoda

Ficus macropoda, commonly known as the large-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus macropoda is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
