Fraxinus veuilloti

Fraxinus veuilloti, commonly known as the Madagascar ash, is a deciduous tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form. The tree has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters and are followed by winged fruits.

Fraxinus veuilloti is adapted to the tropical and subtropical climates of its native habitat.
