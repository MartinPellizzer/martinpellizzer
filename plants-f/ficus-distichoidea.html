Ficus distichoidea

Ficus distichoidea, commonly known as the two-ranked fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple, and elliptical leaves that are often dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its adaptability makes it suitable for both indoor and outdoor environments.
