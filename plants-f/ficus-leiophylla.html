Ficus leiophylla

Ficus leiophylla, commonly known as the smooth-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are smooth and leathery in texture. It produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature.

Ficus leiophylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor conditions.
