Ficus euphylla

Ficus euphylla, commonly known as the white-veined fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins, which give it its common name. Ficus euphylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for indoor or shaded outdoor environments.
