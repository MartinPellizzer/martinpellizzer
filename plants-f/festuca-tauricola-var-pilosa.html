Festuca tauricola var. pilosa

Festuca tauricola var. pilosa, commonly known as Taurian hairy fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and produce small, spikelet-based flowers.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in marginal areas.
