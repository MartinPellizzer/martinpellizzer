Festuca juncifolia f. glabrata

Festuca juncifolia f. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth rush fescue, is a variant of the species Festuca juncifolia.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and along streambanks. Its foliage is usually glabrous, meaning it lacks hairs, giving it a smooth appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in wetland environments.
