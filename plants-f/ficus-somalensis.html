Ficus somalensis

Ficus somalensis, commonly known as the Somali fig, is a species native to the Horn of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its thick, dark green leaves and smooth, greyish bark. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Ficus somalensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable environments.
