Fargesia

Fargesia, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen, is a genus of cold-hardy shrubs native to eastern Asia.

These plants are characterized by their slender, bamboo-like stems and dense, evergreen foliage. Fargesia species typically grow in woodland or shaded areas, preferring moist, well-drained soil. They are prized for their ornamental value and ability to thrive in cooler climates.

The genus includes several species, such as Fargesia rufa and Fargesia yunnanensis, which are popular in horticulture for their compact growth and aesthetic appeal.
