Ferraria obtusifolia

Ferraria obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved ferraria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Amaryllidaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of lance-shaped, hairy leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale blue flowers with six petals, typically blooming in spring. Its succulent leaves and showy flowers make it an attractive ornamental plant.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
