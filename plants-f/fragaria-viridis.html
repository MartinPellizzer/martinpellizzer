Fragaria viridis

Fragaria viridis, commonly known as the woodland strawberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, edible fruits similar to the common strawberry. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is typically green when unripe and turns red when mature, often growing in clusters.

Fragaria viridis is native to Europe and western Asia and is often found in woodland areas and along streambanks.
