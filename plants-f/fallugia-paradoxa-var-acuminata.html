Fallugia paradoxa var. acuminata

Fallugia paradoxa var. acuminata, commonly known as the sharpleaf sandblow, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, providing some drought resistance. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in inflorescences. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in arid and rocky soils.

This variety is well adapted to harsh environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
