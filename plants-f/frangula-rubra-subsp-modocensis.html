Frangula rubra subsp. modocensis

Frangula rubra subsp.

modocensis, commonly known as Modoc buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dark red bark and small, yellowish-green leaves that are opposite in arrangement.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, followed by oval, woody pods containing black seeds. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties, with its bark containing anthraquinone compounds used in traditional herbal remedies.
