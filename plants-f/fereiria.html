Fereiria

Fereiria, commonly known as the Cape chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy yellow flowers. The tree produces pods containing edible seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in local habitats.
