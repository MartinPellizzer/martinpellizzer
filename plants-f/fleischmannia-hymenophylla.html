Fleischmannia hymenophylla

Fleischmannia hymenophylla, commonly known as the silver leaf fern, is a species of epiphytic fern native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are covered in a fine, silvery layer of trichomes, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks in the understory of rainforests. Its rhizomes are thick and fleshy, allowing it to store moisture in arid conditions.

Fleischmannia hymenophylla is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in controlled indoor environments.
