Fritillaria xizangensis

Fritillaria xizangensis, commonly known as the Tibetan fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of Tibet.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its bulbs are adapted to store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in harsh mountainous environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
