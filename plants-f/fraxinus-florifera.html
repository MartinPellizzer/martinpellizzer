Fraxinus florifera

Fraxinus florifera, commonly known as the flowering ash, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its striking, panicle-like inflorescences that appear in early spring. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin, turning yellow in autumn. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and urban environments.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
