Ficus prolixa var. carolinensis

Ficus prolixa var. carolinensis, commonly known as the Carolina fig or swamp fig, is a tropical plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree with smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fig fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional herbal uses.
