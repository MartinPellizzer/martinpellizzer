Ficus johannis subsp. johannis

Ficus johannis subsp.

johannis, commonly known as the Jerusalem fig or the sacred fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Middle East and parts of Africa. It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to 20 meters tall in its natural habitat.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value and cultural significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
