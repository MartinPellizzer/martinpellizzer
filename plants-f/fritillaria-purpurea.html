Fritillaria purpurea

Fritillaria purpurea, commonly known as the purple crocus, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to white, often with a contrasting throat. The plant produces a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short stem that supports its showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Fritillaria purpurea is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
