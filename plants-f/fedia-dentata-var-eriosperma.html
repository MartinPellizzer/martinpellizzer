Fedia dentata var. eriosperma

Fedia dentata var. eriosperma, commonly known as the woolly toothed fedia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive serrated leaves and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant produces a unique type of seed capsule that is covered in soft, hairy structures, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in cloud forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and taxonomic studies.
