Fagraea pseudoelliptica

Fagraea pseudoelliptica, commonly known as the false hibiscus or red hibiscus, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from red to pink. The plant produces small, woody fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Fagraea pseudoelliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
