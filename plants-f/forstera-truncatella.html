Forstera truncatella

Forstera truncatella, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rockrose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, evergreen leaves that are typically narrow and leathery. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink or white flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Forstera truncatella is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
