Filipendula formosa

Filipendula formosa, commonly known as white sweetroot, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmately divided leaves that are often hairy and have a distinctive, slightly aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Filipendula formosa is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine and as a source of edible roots.
