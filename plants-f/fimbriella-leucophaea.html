Fimbriella leucophaea

Fimbriella leucophaea, commonly known as the white-fringed sea anemone, is a unique marine invertebrate found in shallow coastal waters.

It belongs to the order Actinioidea and is characterized by its delicate, translucent polyps surrounded by a soft, fimbriated (fringed) oral disc. The organism lacks a true medusa stage and exists primarily as a colonial polypoid form. Its coloration varies, often appearing pale or white, which helps it blend with its marine environment.

This species plays an important role in benthic ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various marine organisms.
