Fasciculus ruber

Fasciculus ruber, commonly known as the red fascicle, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered inflorescence, which gives it its name "fasciculus," meaning a bundle or cluster. The plant produces vibrant red flowers that bloom in the early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, adapted to withstand the cold and arid conditions of its habitat.

Fasciculus ruber is often found in rocky soils and is considered a symbol of resilience in botanical studies.
