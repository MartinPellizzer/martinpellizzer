Fuchsia heterotricha

Fuchsia heterotricha, commonly known as the velvet tangle, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage and clusters of showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bees, and it produces small, fleshy fruits that are edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
