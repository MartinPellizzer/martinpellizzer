Ficus paracamptophylla

Ficus paracamptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, clustered fruits known as syconia, which are typically green when mature.

Ficus paracamptophylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
