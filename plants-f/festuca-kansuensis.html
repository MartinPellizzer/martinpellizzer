Festuca kansuensis

Festuca kansuensis, commonly known as Kansu bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in cooler climates. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that gives it its common name.

Festuca kansuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
