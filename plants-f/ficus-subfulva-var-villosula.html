Ficus subfulva var. villosula

Ficus subfulva var. villosula, commonly known as the velvety fig or woolly fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it a unique, soft appearance. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and have a glossy, dark green surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in traditional medicine.

This plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant.
