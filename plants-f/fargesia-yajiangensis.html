Fargesia yajiangensis

Fargesia yajiangensis, commonly known as the Yajiang bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to China.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, hollow stems. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments, often found in mountainous regions with well-drained soil. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are not showy.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
