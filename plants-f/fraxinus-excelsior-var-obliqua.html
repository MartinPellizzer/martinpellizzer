Fraxinus excelsior var. obliqua

Fraxinus excelsior var. obliqua, commonly known as the wavy-leaved ash, is a variant of the common ash tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, wavy-edged leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant is a deciduous tree that typically grows in moist, woodland habitats. Its flowers are small and green, appearing in catkins, and it produces samaras as its fruit.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting biodiversity.
