Ficus hypophaea

Ficus hypophaea, commonly known as the small-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Cambodia, and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically less than 5 centimeters in length. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are usually not consumed by humans.

Ficus hypophaea is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, shaded environments.
