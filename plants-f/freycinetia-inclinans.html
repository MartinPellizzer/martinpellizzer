Freycinetia inclinans

Freycinetia inclinans, commonly known as the broad-leafed pandanus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its robust, fibrous trunk and large, paddle-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into three-winged fruits, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often forming dense stands.

Freycinetia inclinans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
