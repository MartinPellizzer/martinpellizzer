Fritillaria tesselata

Fritillaria tesselata, commonly known as the tessellated fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a distinctive, segmented bulb that stores nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Fritillaria tesselata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is a member of the Liliaceae family.
