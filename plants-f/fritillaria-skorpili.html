Fritillaria skorpili

Fritillaria skorpili, commonly known as the Skorpil lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color, with a distinctive spotted pattern. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winters, and it is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
