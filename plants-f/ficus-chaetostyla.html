Ficus chaetostyla

Ficus chaetostyla, commonly known as the hairy-styled fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
