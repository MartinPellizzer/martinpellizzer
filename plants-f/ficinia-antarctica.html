Ficinia antarctica

Ficinia antarctica, commonly known as Antarctic grass, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves that form dense mats, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or brown in color. It is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including strong winds and low temperatures.

Ficinia antarctica plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is of interest to botanists studying plant adaptation.
