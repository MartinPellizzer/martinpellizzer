Freesia viridis subsp. viridis

Freesia viridis subsp.

viridis, commonly known as the green freesia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South Africa. It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and green.

The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to gardens during the warmer months.
