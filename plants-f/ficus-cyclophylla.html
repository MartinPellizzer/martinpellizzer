Ficus cyclophylla

Ficus cyclophylla, commonly known as the warty fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, warty bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically clustered in spherical structures. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ficus cyclophylla is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
