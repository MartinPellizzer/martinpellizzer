Fraxinus parvifolia var. tarraconensis

Fraxinus parvifolia var. tarraconensis, commonly known as the Tarraconensis ash, is a rare and endemic species native to Spain.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree with slender, often drooping branches. The leaves are opposite, simple, and have a distinctive ovate shape with a finely toothed margin. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.
