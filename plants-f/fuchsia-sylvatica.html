Fuchsia sylvatica

Fuchsia sylvatica, commonly known as the evergreen fuchsia, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep purple to bright red. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value.

Fuchsia sylvatica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
