Ficus americana subsp. andicola

Ficus americana subsp.

andicola, commonly known as the Andean fig, is a species native to the Andean regions of South America. It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments.

The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. Its fruit, a small, smooth, green fig, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

The species is characterized by its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates with well-drained soil.
