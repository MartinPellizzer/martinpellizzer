Fumaria muralis var. ambigua

Fumaria muralis var. ambigua, commonly known as the wall rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or wall crevices, thriving in poor, well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to colonize difficult environments.
