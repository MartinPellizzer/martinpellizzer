Festuca violacea var. flaccida

Festuca violacea var. flaccida, commonly known as the purple false bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, blue-green leaves that are often tinged with purple, especially at the base, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, blue or purple flowers, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
