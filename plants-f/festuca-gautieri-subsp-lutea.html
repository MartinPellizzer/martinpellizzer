Festuca gautieri subsp. lutea

Festuca gautieri subsp.

lutea, commonly known as the yellow meadow fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils.

The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with a yellowish hue. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which produce small, pale florets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
