Ficus wakefieldii

Ficus wakefieldii, commonly known as the Wakefield fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, elongated leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ficus wakefieldii is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
