Ficus brachiata

Ficus brachiata, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive cascading foliage, with long, slender leaves hanging from its branches in a weeping manner. The plant has a smooth, grayish-brown bark and can grow quite large in its natural habitat. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its ability to thrive in various conditions contributes to its widespread use as a decorative plant.
