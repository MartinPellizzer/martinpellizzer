Firmiana

Firmiana, commonly known as the Chinese parasol tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 inches in diameter. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by distinctive, woody seed pods. Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for urban landscaping.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
