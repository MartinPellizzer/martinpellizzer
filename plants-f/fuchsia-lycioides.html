Fuchsia lycioides

Fuchsia lycioides, commonly known as the mountain fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine regions. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, which attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Fuchsia lycioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
