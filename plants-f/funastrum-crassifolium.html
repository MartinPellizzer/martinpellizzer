Funastrum crassifolium

Funastrum crassifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf funastrum, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its robust, upright growth habit and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, typical of the daisy family. It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa.

Funastrum crassifolium is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
