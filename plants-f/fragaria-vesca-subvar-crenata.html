Fragaria vesca subvar. crenata

Fragaria vesca subvar. crenata, commonly known as the alpine strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its small, trifoliate leaves with rounded lobes and produces tiny, white flowers that develop into edible, strawberry-like fruits. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils. It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in horticulture.

Due to its delicate nature, it is often cultivated in protected environments to ensure its survival.
