Fumaria loiseleuri var. leronensis

Fumaria loiseleuri var. leronensis, commonly known as the Leronian gloryflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fumariaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred petals and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is native to regions of southern Europe and is often found in rocky or limestone soils. It has a trailing growth habit and produces slender, hairy stems that can spread along the ground.

The leaves are lance-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal in garden settings.
