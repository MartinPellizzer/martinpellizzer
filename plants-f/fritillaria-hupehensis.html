Fritillaria hupehensis

Fritillaria hupehensis, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to China.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color, with a contrasting purple or brown rim. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or forested areas. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Fritillaria hupehensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
