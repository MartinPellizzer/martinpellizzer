Fumaria arundana

Fumaria arundana, commonly known as the yellow glory flower, is an annual herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and along riverbanks. Its flowers bloom in spring, and the plant produces elongated seed pods that contain small, black seeds.

Fumaria arundana is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color.
