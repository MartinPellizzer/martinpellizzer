Ficus carica var. aulica

Ficus carica var. aulica, commonly known as the Persian fig, is a variety of the common fig tree native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often lobed leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into the edible fig fruits, which are rich in nutrients and have a sweet, delicate flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
