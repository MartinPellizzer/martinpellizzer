Fleischmannia viscidipes

Fleischmannia viscidipes, commonly known as the sticky stickleaf, is a species of flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems and leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often blooming in the spring. Fleischmannia viscidipes is notable for its sticky secretions, which cover its stems and leaves, giving it a unique appearance and aiding in water retention.

Its adaptations to drought make it an important component of desert ecosystems.
