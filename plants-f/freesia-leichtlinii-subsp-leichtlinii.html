Freesia leichtlinii subsp. leichtlinii

Freesia leichtlinii subsp.

leichtlinii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved freesia, is a species native to South Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink.

The foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
