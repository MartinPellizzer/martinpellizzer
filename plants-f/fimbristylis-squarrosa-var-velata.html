Fimbristylis squarrosa var. velata

Fimbristylis squarrosa var. velata, commonly known as the woolly flatsedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like appearance with hairy or velvety surfaces on its leaves and stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in dense clusters. It thrives in moist, often disturbed soils, such as those found in wetlands or along waterways.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
