Festuca ladyginii

Festuca ladyginii, commonly known as Lady's Grass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially sunny environments. The plant features slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually bluish-green in color. Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces numerous tiny flowers, which are often white or pale blue.

Festuca ladyginii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its delicate appearance.
