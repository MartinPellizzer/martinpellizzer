Ficus kondeensis

Ficus kondeensis, commonly known as the Konde fig, is a species of fig tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stem. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by the development of edible fig fruits. Ficus kondeensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
