Ficinia fimbriata

Ficinia fimbriata, commonly known as the fringeleaf or fimbriate fig, is a small shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely toothed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Ficinia fimbriata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
