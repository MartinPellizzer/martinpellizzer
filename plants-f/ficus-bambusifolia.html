Ficus bambusifolia

Ficus bambusifolia, commonly known as the bamboo-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that resemble bamboo leaves, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an understory species in humid forests, thriving in partial shade. Its leaves are usually dark green and glossy, with a waxy texture.

Ficus bambusifolia is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
