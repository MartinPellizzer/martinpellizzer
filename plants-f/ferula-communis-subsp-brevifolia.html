Ferula communis subsp. brevifolia

Ferula communis subsp.

brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved giant fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and feathery, aromatic leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

This subspecies is closely related to other members of the Apiaceae family, sharing similar structural and chemical traits.
