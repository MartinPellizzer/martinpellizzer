Faskia

Faskia, commonly known as the red-flowered lily, is a striking perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The plant produces large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to purple, often with a distinctive white or yellow center. Faskia thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its long blooming period, which typically spans from late spring to early summer.
