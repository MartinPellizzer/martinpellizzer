Friedlandia parvifolia

Friedlandia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved friedlandia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
