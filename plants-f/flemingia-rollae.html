Flemingia rollae

Flemingia rollae, commonly known as the slender flemingia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Leguminosae family, characterized by its ability to fix nitrogen through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, featuring compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruit is a legume pod containing several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind.

Flemingia rollae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in nitrogen enrichment of soil.
