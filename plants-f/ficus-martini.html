Ficus martini

Ficus martini, commonly known as the Martin's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Thailand and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Ficus martini is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its attractive foliage.

It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
