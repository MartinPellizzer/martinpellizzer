Frangula capreifolia var. capreifolia

Frangula capreifolia var. capreifolia, commonly known as the European buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features compound leaves that are alternate and often have a serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the spring. Its berries are dark red to black and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for their laxative properties.
