Fimbristylis junciformis

Fimbristylis junciformis, commonly known as the grass-like sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it often thrives in wetland or marshy environments. The plant typically grows from 10 to 30 centimeters in height, with a fibrous root system.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and habitat diversity.
