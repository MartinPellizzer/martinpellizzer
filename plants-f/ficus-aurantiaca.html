Ficus aurantiaca

Ficus aurantiaca, commonly known as the orange fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible figs. Its root system is robust, allowing it to adapt well to various soil types, and it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Ficus aurantiaca is also valued for its ability to improve indoor air quality.
