Festuca ovina var. valentina

Festuca ovina var. valentina, commonly known as Valentina sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.

Festuca ovina var. valentina is also noted for its drought tolerance and ability to withstand moderate foot traffic.
