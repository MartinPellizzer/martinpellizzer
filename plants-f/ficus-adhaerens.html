Ficus adhaerens

Ficus adhaerens, commonly known as the climbing fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its aerial roots, which it uses to climb and anchor itself to trees or other structures. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and have a glossy surface.

Ficus adhaerens produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits.
