Fuchsia storkii

Fuchsia storkii, commonly known as the Stork's-beak fuchsia, is a striking member of the Onagraceae family.

It is native to the Andes mountains of South America, specifically in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep red to purple. Its unique, elongated corolla resembles a stork's beak, giving it its common name.

Fuchsia storkii thrives in cool, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its showy blooms.
