Ficus pseudomangiferifolia

Ficus pseudomangiferifolia, commonly known as the Indian rubber plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including India and Sri Lanka. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.

Ficus pseudomangiferifolia is also valued for its ability to purify the air and its potential use in traditional medicine.
