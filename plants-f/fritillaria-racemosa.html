Fritillaria racemosa

Fritillaria racemosa, commonly known as the clustered fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with contrasting dark markings. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high elevations, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winters, and the plant is known for its long flowering period in the spring.

Fritillaria racemosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
