Ficus chaetophora

Ficus chaetophora, commonly known as the curly fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and deeply lobed leaves that give it a unique, almost fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible figs that are typically small and round. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value.

Ficus chaetophora is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
