Fraxinus oxycarpa f. stipitata

Fraxinus oxycarpa f. stipitata, commonly known as the slender winged ash, is a cultivar of the ash tree characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

It features narrow, elongated leaves with a prominent midrib and a serrated margin, which contribute to its ornamental value. The plant exhibits a unique growth habit with slender, upright branches that create a graceful, open canopy. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often developing shallow fissures as the tree matures.

This cultivar is valued in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
