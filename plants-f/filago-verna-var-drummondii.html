Filago verna var. drummondii

Filago verna var. drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's spring filago, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its delicate, daisy-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces small, pale yellow to white flower heads.

It is often found in arid and semi-arid regions, adapted to thrive in low-nutrient soils.
