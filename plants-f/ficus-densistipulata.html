Ficus densistipulata

Ficus densistipulata, commonly known as the dense-leaved fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences known as syconia, which contain both male and female flowers. Ficus densistipulata is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
