Faramea singularis

Faramea singularis, commonly known as the African snowflake, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a snowflake-like appearance. The plant has a shrub-like growth habit with compound leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Faramea singularis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
