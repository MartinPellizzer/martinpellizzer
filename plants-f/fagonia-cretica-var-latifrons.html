Fagonia cretica var. latifrons

Fagonia cretica var. latifrons, commonly known as the broad-leaved fagonia, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a dry capsule containing several seeds.

It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
