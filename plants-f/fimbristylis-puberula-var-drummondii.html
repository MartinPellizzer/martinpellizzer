Fimbristylis puberula var. drummondii

Fimbristylis puberula var. drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's sedge, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually greenish or brownish and lack showy petals.

Drummond's sedge plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various small organisms.
