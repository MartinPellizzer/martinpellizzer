Floscopa rivularis

Floscopa rivularis, commonly known as the river bladderflower, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or damp environments, such as along riverbanks and in swampy areas. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often growing in dense clusters.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
