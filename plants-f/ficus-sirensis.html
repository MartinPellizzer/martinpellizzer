Ficus sirensis

Ficus sirensis, commonly known as the Siren's Fig, is a rare and unique species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with shades of white and green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are typically clustered in groups. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by specialty plant enthusiasts.
