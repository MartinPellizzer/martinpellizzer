Fritillaria pontica var. substipelata

Fritillaria pontica var. substipelata, commonly known as the Balkan fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces a basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves that are typically covered in a powdery coating. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
