Festuca ovina var. juncifolia

Festuca ovina var. juncifolia, commonly known as sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green foliage and tufted growth habit, with leaves that are narrow and slightly rolled. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense mats.

Festuca ovina var. juncifolia is also valued for its low maintenance requirements and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
