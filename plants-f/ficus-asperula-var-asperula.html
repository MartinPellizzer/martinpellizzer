Ficus asperula var. asperula

Ficus asperula var. asperula, commonly known as the rough fig, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree characterized by its rough, bark-like texture on its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round figs that are typically green when mature. Its leaves are usually thick, leathery, and have a slightly serrated edge.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.
