Festuca rubra subsp. aurasiaca

Festuca rubra subsp.

aurasiaca, commonly known as the Aurasiatic red fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous florets, which are usually pale green to reddish in hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous and alpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fine texture and hardy nature.
