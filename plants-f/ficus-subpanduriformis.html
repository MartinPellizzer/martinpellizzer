Ficus subpanduriformis

Ficus subpanduriformis, commonly known as the panduriform fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and unique, pendulous fig fruits that hang in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
