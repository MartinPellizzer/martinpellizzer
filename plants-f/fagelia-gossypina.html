Fagelia gossypina

Fagelia gossypina, commonly known as the cottonwood tree, is a deciduous tree native to the Americas.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, fluffy seeds enclosed in a capsule, which are dispersed by the wind. It thrives in riparian zones and is often found near rivers and streams.
