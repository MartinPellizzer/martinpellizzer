Ficus populnea subvar. subcordata

Ficus populnea subvar. subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically smaller and more narrow compared to other ficus varieties. The plant is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
