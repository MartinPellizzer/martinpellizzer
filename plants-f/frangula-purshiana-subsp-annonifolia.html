Frangula purshiana subsp. annonifolia

Frangula purshiana subsp.

annonifolia, commonly known as the western barberry, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically arranged in a pinnate pattern, and its yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces small, red berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
