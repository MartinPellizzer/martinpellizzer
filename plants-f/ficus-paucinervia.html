Ficus paucinervia

Ficus paucinervia, commonly known as the small-veined fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, Thailand, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green in color. It produces small, greenish-white figs that are typically eaten by birds and other animals.

Ficus paucinervia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
