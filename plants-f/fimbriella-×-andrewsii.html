Fimbriella × andrewsii

Fimbriella × andrewsii, commonly known as the hybrid sea anemone, is a unique species that results from the interbreeding of two different sea anemone species.

It is characterized by its delicate, translucent polyps that often exhibit a variety of colors, including pink, orange, and yellow. The plant thrives in shallow coastal waters, where it attaches itself to rocky substrates using its basal disc. Its reproductive strategy involves both sexual and asexual methods, contributing to its adaptability in marine environments.

Due to its ecological significance, it plays a role in supporting local marine biodiversity.
