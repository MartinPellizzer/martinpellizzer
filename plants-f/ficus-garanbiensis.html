Ficus garanbiensis

Ficus garanbiensis, commonly known as the Garanbi fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, fig-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree can grow up to 15 meters tall and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.
