Freycinetia acutifolia

Freycinetia acutifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically yellow or green in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching due to their durability.

Freycinetia acutifolia thrives in coastal and humid environments, often growing near the shoreline.
