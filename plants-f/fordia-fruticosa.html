Fordia fruticosa

Fordia fruticosa, commonly known as the bottlebrush bush, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, bright red or orange flower clusters that resemble bottle brushes, which bloom in early spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

The botanical features of Fordia fruticosa make it a popular choice for attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.
