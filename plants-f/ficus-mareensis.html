Ficus mareensis

Ficus mareensis, commonly known as the Mare's Fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the island of Socotra.

It is characterized by its distinctive, thick, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing remarkable adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological significance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
