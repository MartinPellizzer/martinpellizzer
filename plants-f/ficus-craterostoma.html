Ficus craterostoma

Ficus craterostoma, commonly known as the rock fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, fig-like fruits that are enclosed within a spherical, greenish husk.

Ficus craterostoma is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
