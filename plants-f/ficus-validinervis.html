Ficus validinervis

Ficus validinervis, commonly known as the slender-veined fig, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, simple leaves with prominent parallel veins. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, clustered figs that are edible and attract a variety of wildlife.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
