Flotovia flagellaris

Flotovia flagellaris, commonly known as the aquatic flagellate moss, is a unique plant-like organism found in freshwater environments.

It possesses a filamentous structure composed of elongated cells connected by a network of flagella, which aid in movement and nutrient absorption. The plant lacks true roots, stems, and leaves, instead relying on its flagellated cells for anchoring and nutrient uptake. Its reproductive structures are microscopic and dispersed through water, facilitating wide distribution.

Due to its specialized adaptations, Flotovia flagellaris plays a significant role in aquatic ecosystems by contributing to nutrient cycling and providing habitat for microorganisms.
