Fritillaria graeca subsp. thessala

Fritillaria graeca subsp.

thessala, commonly known as the Greek fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece. It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns.

The plant produces a cluster of narrow, linear leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is typically found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and protected in its natural environment.
