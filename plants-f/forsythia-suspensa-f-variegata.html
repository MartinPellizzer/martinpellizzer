Forsythia suspensa f. variegata

Forsythia suspensa f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated weeping forsythia, is a hybrid form of the weeping forsythia, characterized by its distinctive variegated foliage.

The leaves are typically marked with creamy white or yellow margins, creating an attractive contrast against the green background. This plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in the early spring, which hang in drooping sprays. It is a deciduous shrub that thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscapes.
