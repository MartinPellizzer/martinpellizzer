Feronia ternata

Feronia ternata, commonly known as the three-lobed starfruit, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a tart flavor. Feronia ternata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
