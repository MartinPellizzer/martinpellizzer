Faramea anisodonta

Faramea anisodonta, commonly known as the African holly or red holly, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture with a glossy appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for birds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in horticulture for its attractive appearance and hardy nature.
