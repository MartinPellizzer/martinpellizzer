Frailea magnifica

Frailea magnifica, commonly known as the giant fairy fan cactus, is a species of cactus native to the coastal areas of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, flat, paddle-shaped stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white, star-shaped flowers during the summer months. It is well adapted to arid environments, with a deep root system to access water.

Its unique appearance and resilience make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
