Ficus reflexa

Ficus reflexa, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping, cascading leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically glossy and dark green, with a waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by green, fleshy fruits that turn yellow or red when mature.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions.
