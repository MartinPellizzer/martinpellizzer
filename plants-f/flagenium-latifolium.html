Flagenium latifolium

Flagenium latifolium, commonly known as the broadleaf flagel, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are often overlooked due to their subtle appearance. Its roots are fibrous and can form dense colonies, making it a common weed in disturbed soils.

Botanically, it is characterized by its robust growth habit and adaptability to various soil types.
