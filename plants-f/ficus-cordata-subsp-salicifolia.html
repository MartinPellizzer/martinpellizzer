Ficus cordata subsp. salicifolia

Ficus cordata subsp.

salicifolia, commonly known as the willow-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its heart-shaped, willow-like leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
