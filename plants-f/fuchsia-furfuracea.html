Fuchsia furfuracea

Fuchsia furfuracea, commonly known as the woolly fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, white hairs.
