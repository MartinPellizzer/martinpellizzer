Ficus quichuana

Ficus quichuana, commonly known as the Andean fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes, primarily in Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often a deep green color. It produces small, edible fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

Ficus quichuana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
