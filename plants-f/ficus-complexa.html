Ficus complexa

Ficus complexa, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by greenish-yellow fruits that are edible for some animals but not generally consumed by humans. Ficus complexa is native to parts of Asia and is often used as a ground cover or ornamental plant in landscaping.

It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
