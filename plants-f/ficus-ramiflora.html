Ficus ramiflora

Ficus ramiflora, commonly known as the silver fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, warm climates. The plant is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically grayish-white in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish flowers that develop into edible fruits, which are often consumed by local wildlife.

Ficus ramiflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor environments.
