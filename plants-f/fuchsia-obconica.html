Fuchsia obconica

Fuchsia obconica, commonly known as the common fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pendulous flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in moist, well-drained soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms.

Fuchsia obconica is typically grown in gardens or as a houseplant, preferring cool temperatures and partial shade.
