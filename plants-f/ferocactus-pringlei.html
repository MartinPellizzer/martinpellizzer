Ferocactus pringlei

Ferocactus pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's barrel cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert in North America.

It is characterized by its short, cylindrical stems that are typically covered in prominent, white spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Ferocactus pringlei is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems to survive long dry periods.

It plays an important role in the desert ecosystem, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
