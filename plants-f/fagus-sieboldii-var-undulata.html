Fagus sieboldii var. undulata

Fagus sieboldii var. undulata, commonly known as the wavy-barked beech, is a variant of the Japanese beech.

It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating bark that gives it a unique appearance. The plant is native to regions of East Asia, including Japan and parts of China. It typically grows in temperate forests and prefers moist, well-drained soils.

Its leaves are dark green on top and lighter underneath, often turning yellow in the fall.
