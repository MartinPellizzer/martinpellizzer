Festuca heterophylla var. major

Festuca heterophylla var. major, commonly known as the coastal bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the coastal regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which helps it thrive in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant is drought-tolerant and often forms dense mats, making it a valuable species for stabilizing dunes and coastal habitats. Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce numerous tiny seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This variety is also prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
