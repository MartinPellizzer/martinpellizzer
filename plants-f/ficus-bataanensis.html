Ficus bataanensis

Ficus bataanensis, commonly known as the Bataan fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Philippines, specifically the Bataan region, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature. It is often found in moist, shaded areas such as forests and along riverbanks.

The Bataan fig is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in local communities.
