Ficus kellermanii

Ficus kellermanii, commonly known as the Kellerman's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and can grow as a shrub or small tree. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Ficus kellermanii is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
