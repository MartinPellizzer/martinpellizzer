Fuirena

Fuirena, commonly known as the common thistle, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and purple or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in dry, disturbed soils and is native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. Fuirena has a rosette of basal leaves that form a dense cluster, and its flowers are arranged in a composite head.

It is often used in traditional medicine and as a ornamental plant in gardens.
