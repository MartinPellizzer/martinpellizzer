Floscopa capensis

Floscopa capensis, commonly known as the Cape dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in coastal and inland areas. Its yellow flowers are followed by fluffy seed heads that disperse easily by wind.

Floscopa capensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
