Ficus corneri

Ficus corneri, commonly known as the Corner's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus corneri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor conditions.
