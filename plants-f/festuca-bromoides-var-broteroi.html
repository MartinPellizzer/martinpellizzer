Festuca bromoides var. broteroi

Festuca bromoides var. broteroi, commonly known as Broter's bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky, arid environments and is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and tufted inflorescences. The plant has a low-growing habit and is often found in open, sun-exposed areas. It is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in disturbed areas.
