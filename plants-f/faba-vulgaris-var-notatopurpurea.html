Faba vulgaris var. notatopurpurea

Faba vulgaris var. notatopurpurea, commonly known as the purple-streaked vetch, is a variant of the common vetch characterized by its distinctive purple markings on the leaves and stems.

It is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family, which is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability through symbiotic relationships with rhizobia bacteria. The plant typically grows as a perennial herb with trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in soil enrichment.
