Festuca ovina var. magellanica

Festuca ovina var. magellanica, commonly known as Magellan grass, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit, making it well-suited for rocky or coastal environments. The plant typically thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in maritime regions. Its dense root system helps prevent soil erosion, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Festuca ovina var. magellanica is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and adaptability.
