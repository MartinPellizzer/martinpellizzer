Filago leontopodioides

Filago leontopodioides, commonly known as the woolly filago, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in arid or semi-arid regions. The plant features a low-growing, mat-forming structure with hairy, gray-green leaves. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters and bloom during the cooler months.

Filago leontopodioides is adapted to withstand drought and has a unique appearance that makes it easily identifiable in its natural habitat.
