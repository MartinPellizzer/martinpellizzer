Fritillaria thunbergii var. puqiensis

Fritillaria thunbergii var. puqiensis, commonly known as the Puqi fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to China.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or red with contrasting black markings. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a basal rosette of bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
