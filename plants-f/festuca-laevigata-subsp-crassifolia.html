Festuca laevigata subsp. crassifolia

Festuca laevigata subsp.

crassifolia, commonly known as broadleaf fescue, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and its ability to form dense, low-growing mats.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh environments, including rocky soils and high altitudes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to greenish flowers. Festuca laevigata subsp.

crassifolia is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ornamental value.
