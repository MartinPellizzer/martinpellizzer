Fuirena rhizomatifera

Fuirena rhizomatifera, commonly known as the creeping rhizome plant, is a species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its underground rhizomes that spread horizontally, allowing it to form dense mats of vegetation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish in color, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks.

Due to its rhizomatous growth habit, it is often used in ecological restoration projects to prevent soil erosion.
