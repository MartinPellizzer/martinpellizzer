Fraxinus oxycarpa f. cuspidata

Fraxinus oxycarpa f. cuspidata, commonly known as the pointed-leaf ash, is a variant of the ash tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are sharply pointed at the tips. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, which are typical of the olive family. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
