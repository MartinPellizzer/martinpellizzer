Fagonia indica var. elba

Fagonia indica var. elba, commonly known as the Canary Island fagonia, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and woody stems that often have a resinous coating. The plant thrives in arid and rocky environments, showcasing its adaptation to harsh, coastal conditions. Its leaves are typically small and leathery, reducing water loss in dry climates.

This variety is of particular interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological significance.
