Ficus drupacea var. pedicellata

Ficus drupacea var. pedicellata, commonly known as the pedicellate fig, is a tropical plant characterized by its distinctive inflorescence structure.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is native to regions of Southeast Asia. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by edible fig fruits. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a smooth texture.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and unique flowering pattern.
