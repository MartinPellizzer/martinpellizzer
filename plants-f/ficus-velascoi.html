Ficus velascoi

Ficus velascoi, commonly known as the Velasco fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig fruits, which are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Ficus velascoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
