Ficus aurata var. longipilosa

Ficus aurata var. longipilosa, commonly known as the long-hair fig, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features dense, glossy dark green leaves that are often covered in fine, soft white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible figs, though the fruits are typically not harvested for consumption. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is well-suited for indoor cultivation due to its adaptability.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental purposes in both indoor and outdoor settings.
