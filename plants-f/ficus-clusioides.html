Ficus clusioides

Ficus clusioides, commonly known as the golden fig or Indian rubber plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by fig-like fruits, which are edible and often consumed by various wildlife species.

Ficus clusioides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in indoor conditions.
