Fleischmannia prasiifolia

Fleischmannia prasiifolia, commonly known as the green-leaf fleischmannia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Cactaceae family, characterized by its succulent stems and spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its arid habitat. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often mistaken for foliage in desert landscapes.

Fleischmannia prasiifolia is adapted to thrive in full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy species for xeriscaping.
