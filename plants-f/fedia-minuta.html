Fedia minuta

Fedia minuta, commonly known as the dwarf fedia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It typically grows to a height of only a few centimeters, with delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are usually borne on short pedicels. Fedia minuta thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense undergrowth of rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a unique subject for study in plant ecology and taxonomy.
