Ficus inconstantissima

Ficus inconstantissima, commonly known as the Chinese banyan, is a member of the Moraceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its thick, buttressed roots and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in fig syconia, which are enclosed within a fleshy, greenish-woody structure. Ficus inconstantissima is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to urban environments.

It plays an important role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
