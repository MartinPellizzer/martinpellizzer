Festuca porcii var. hirsuta

Festuca porcii var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and wiry, hairy stems that give it its common name. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often covered with fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
