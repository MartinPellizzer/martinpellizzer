Ficus brevibracteata

Ficus brevibracteata, commonly known as the short-bract fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, simple leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. It produces small, greenish-white flowers, which are clustered in fig syconia, a unique type of inflorescence.

The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
