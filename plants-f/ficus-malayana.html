Ficus malayana

Ficus malayana, commonly known as the Malay fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Malaysia, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The fruit, which is a small fig, is edible and serves as a food source for various wildlife species.
