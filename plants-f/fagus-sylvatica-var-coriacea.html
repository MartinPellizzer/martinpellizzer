Fagus sylvatica var. coriacea

Fagus sylvatica var. coriacea, commonly known as the European black birch, is a variant of the European beech.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and dense, broad leaves that are often slightly pubescent. This variety is native to parts of Central and Eastern Europe, where it thrives in temperate climates. The tree can reach heights of up to 25 meters and has a lifespan that can exceed 300 years.

It plays an important role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
