Festuca ovina var. nipponica

Festuca ovina var. nipponica, commonly known as Japanese sheep fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky slopes. Its foliage is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.

Botanically, it is noted for its small, spike-like inflorescences and its ability to thrive in cool, temperate climates.
