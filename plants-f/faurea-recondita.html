Faurea recondita

Faurea recondita, commonly known as the rare mountain hibiscus, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy, pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has a woody stem and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in rocky or forested habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Faurea recondita is considered a rare and endangered species.
