Faba major var. viridis

Faba major var. viridis, commonly known as the green broad bean, is a variant of the broad bean species characterized by its distinct green foliage and pods.

This plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It produces large, edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients, making it a valuable crop for both human consumption and animal feed. The plant's leaves are also used in traditional herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.

Its growth cycle typically spans several months, with flowering and pod development occurring during the warmer seasons.
