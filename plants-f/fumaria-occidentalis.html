Fumaria occidentalis

Fumaria occidentalis, commonly known as the western smokeplant, is a flowering plant native to western North America.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Fumaria occidentalis is a herbaceous perennial that blooms from spring to early summer.
