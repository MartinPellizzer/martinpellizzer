Feuilleea polycephala

Feuilleea polycephala, commonly known as the multi-headed leaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its unusual growth pattern.

It features multiple heads or shoots emerging from a single stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant possesses broad, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Its botanical significance lies in its rare morphology, making it a subject of interest in botanical research.
