Flessera

Flessera, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, translucent, waxy leaves that resemble ghostly apparitions, hence its common name. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom at night, adding to its mysterious appearance. Flessera thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires consistent moisture to flourish.

Its botanical features make it a rare and intriguing subject for both horticulturists and botanists.
