Ficus erinobotrya

Ficus erinobotrya, commonly known as the African fig, is a tropical tree native to sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are an important food source for both humans and animals. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
