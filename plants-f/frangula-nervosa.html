Frangula nervosa

Frangula nervosa, commonly known as the veined cascara, is a shrub native to the western United States and parts of Mexico.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its compound leaves with prominent venation. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by red, fleshy berries that are an important food source for wildlife. The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
