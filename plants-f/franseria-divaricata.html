Franseria divaricata

Franseria divaricata, commonly known as the Cape jasmine, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its flowers have a pleasant, spicy fragrance and are often used in perfumery.

The plant is well adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy and drought-tolerant species.
