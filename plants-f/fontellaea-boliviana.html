Fontellaea boliviana

Fontellaea boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian wax palm, is a striking plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family, characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky soils. Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
