Ficus myrtifolia

Ficus myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtle fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are often clustered at the ends of its branches. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe. Ficus myrtifolia is well adapted to humid environments and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor landscaping.
