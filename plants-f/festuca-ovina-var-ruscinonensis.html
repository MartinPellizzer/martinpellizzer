Festuca ovina var. ruscinonensis

Festuca ovina var. ruscinonensis, commonly known as Ruscinon grass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and compact, dense foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators and soil stabilization.
