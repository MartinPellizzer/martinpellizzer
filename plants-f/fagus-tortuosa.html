Fagus tortuosa

Fagus tortuosa, commonly known as the Chinese iron fir, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and beeches, and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and serrated leaves. The tree produces small, cupule-enclosed nuts that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing structural support and protection against environmental stressors.

Fagus tortuosa is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in temperate forests.
