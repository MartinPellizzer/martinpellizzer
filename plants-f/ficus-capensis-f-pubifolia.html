Ficus capensis f. pubifolia

Ficus capensis f. pubifolia, commonly known as the Cape fig or white-leaf fig, is a variant of the common fig tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive white-veined, heart-shaped leaves that are often pubescent, giving it a soft, fuzzy texture. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters in suitable conditions. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in local cuisines.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
