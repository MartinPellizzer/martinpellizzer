Fritillaria × tokushimensis

Fritillaria × tokushimensis, commonly known as the Japanese fritillary, is a hybrid plant that exhibits characteristics of its parent species, Fritillaria meleagris and Fritillaria japonica.

It is a bulbous perennial that produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of orange, red, and white. The plant is native to Japan and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

Fritillaria × tokushimensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ability to naturalize in suitable climates.
