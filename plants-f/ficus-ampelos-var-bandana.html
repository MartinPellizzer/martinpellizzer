Ficus ampelos var. bandana

Ficus ampelos var. bandana, commonly known as the Bandana Fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, heart-shaped leaves that often have a distinctive white or pale band across the center, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil and indirect sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
