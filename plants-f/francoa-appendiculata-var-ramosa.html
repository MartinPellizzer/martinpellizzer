Francoa appendiculata var. ramosa

Francoa appendiculata var. ramosa, commonly known as the clustered francoa, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically produces pink to red flowers that are arranged in dense inflorescences, adding visual interest to gardens. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is well-suited for moist, partially shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal in native plant gardens.
