Ficus lapathifolia

Ficus lapathifolia, commonly known as the silver fig or creeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its heart-shaped, glossy leaves that often have a silvery or grayish underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in various indoor environments.

Ficus lapathifolia is also known for its adaptability to low-light conditions and its ease of care, making it a popular choice for houseplants.
