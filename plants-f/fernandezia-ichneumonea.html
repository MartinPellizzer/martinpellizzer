Fernandezia ichneumonea

Fernandezia ichneumonea, commonly known as the Chilean fern, is a unique plant native to the Andes mountains of Chile.

It is a member of the fern family and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions. Its reproductive structures are concealed within specialized leaf bases, a feature common among certain fern species.

Fernandezia ichneumonea plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various small invertebrates.
