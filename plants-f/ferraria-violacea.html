Ferraria violacea

Ferraria violacea, commonly known as the violet bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long-lasting blooms and compact growth habit.
