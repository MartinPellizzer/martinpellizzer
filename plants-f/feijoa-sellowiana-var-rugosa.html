Feijoa sellowiana var. rugosa

Feijoa sellowiana var. rugosa, commonly known as the rough feijoa, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves and fragrant, yellowish-green fruit with a distinctive, musky aroma. The fruit contains a juicy, white pulp surrounding black, inedible seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its unique flavor and ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its potential in horticulture and as a source of natural compounds.
