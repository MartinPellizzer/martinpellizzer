Ficus pilulifera

Ficus pilulifera, commonly known as the "pilule fig" or "button fig," is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The plant is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, round fig fruits that are often clustered together, giving it a unique appearance.

Ficus pilulifera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
