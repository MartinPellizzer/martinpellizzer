Flagellaria indica var. glauca

Flagellaria indica var. glauca, commonly known as the blue flagellaria, is a variant of the Indian flagellaria, native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a climbing herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, trailing stems and small, clustered flowers. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate to lanceolate in shape, and often have a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers are usually pale blue or white, with five petals, and are borne in inflorescences.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.
