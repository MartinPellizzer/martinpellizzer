Ficus caulobotrya var. fraseri

Ficus caulobotrya var. fraseri, commonly known as the Fraser's weeping fig, is a variant of the weeping fig species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, elongated leaves that give it a distinctive, cascading appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with a dense canopy of foliage. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a waxy surface.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
