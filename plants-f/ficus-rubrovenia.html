Ficus rubrovenia

Ficus rubrovenia, commonly known as the red-veined fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, featuring deep green leaves with prominent red veins. The plant belongs to the Moraceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Ficus rubrovenia is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
