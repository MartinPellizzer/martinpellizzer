Forsteronia acutifolia var. pubescens

Forsteronia acutifolia var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy leaf forsteria, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a shrub that typically grows in humid, tropical environments. The plant features small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, and it produces small, spherical fruits.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage and delicate blooms.
