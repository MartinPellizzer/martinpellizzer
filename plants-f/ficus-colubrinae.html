Ficus colubrinae

Ficus colubrinae, commonly known as the Brazilian rubber fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature, containing a single seed. It is commonly cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The tree is adaptable to various soil types but thrives in humid, warm climates.
