Fremya myrtifolia

Fremya myrtifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed myrtle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its narrow, aromatic leaves and small, white or pinkish flowers that appear in clusters. It is often found in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, thriving in well-drained soils.

Fremya myrtifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in indigenous cultures.
