Fagonia insularis

Fagonia insularis, commonly known as the Socotra caper, is a shrub native to the Arabian Peninsula, particularly Socotra Island.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fleshy leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to withstand drought and high temperatures. Its woody stems and thick leaves help reduce water loss, making it well-suited for its harsh habitat.

The Socotra caper is also valued for its edible fruits and has cultural significance in the region.
