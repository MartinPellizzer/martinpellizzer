Ferula lycia

Ferula lycia, commonly known as the Cyprus giant fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, often used in traditional medicine.

Ferula lycia is also valued for its essential oils, which are extracted from its roots and used in perfumery and herbal remedies.
