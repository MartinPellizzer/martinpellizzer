Fumaria muralis var. longipes

Fumaria muralis var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed wallbine, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is a member of the Fumariaceae family and is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often found in disturbed or urban environments. Its flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, and it is known for its ability to thrive in challenging conditions.

The species is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and green spaces.
