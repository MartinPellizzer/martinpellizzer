Festuca ovina subvar. transcaucasica

Festuca ovina subvar. transcaucasica, commonly known as the Transcaucasian sheepgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and erosion control.
