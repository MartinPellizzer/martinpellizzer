Festuca amblyodes subsp. erectiflora

Festuca amblyodes subsp.

erectiflora, commonly known as the upright-flowered fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often stand upright.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous narrow, awnless leaves. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cool, temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
