Fagara elephantiasis

Fagara elephantiasis, commonly known as the giant hogweed, is a striking plant characterized by its large, umbrella-like leaves and showy white flowers.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant can grow up to 5 meters tall, with a thick, hollow stem that is often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are broad and can reach up to 3 meters in diameter, making it one of the largest leafy plants in the world.

Fagara elephantiasis is also known for its toxic sap, which can cause severe skin irritation and photodermatitis when exposed to sunlight.
