Ficus beddomei

Ficus beddomei, commonly known as the Malabar fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Western Ghats of India and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Ficus beddomei is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
