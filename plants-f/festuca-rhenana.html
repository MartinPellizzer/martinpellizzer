Festuca rhenana

Festuca rhenana, commonly known as Rhine fescue, is a perennial grass species native to central and eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and wet meadows. The plant has slender, tufted stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its flowering spikes are usually compact and produce small, spikelet-like flowers.

Festuca rhenana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
