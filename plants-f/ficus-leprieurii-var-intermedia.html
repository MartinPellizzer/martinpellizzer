Ficus leprieurii var. intermedia

Ficus leprieurii var. intermedia, commonly known as the Variegated Ficus or Variegated Rubber Plant, is a cultivar of the Ficus genus native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white or cream variegation patterns. The plant is a member of the Moraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fig fruits, though the fruits are rarely seen in cultivation.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its ease of care and aesthetic appeal.
