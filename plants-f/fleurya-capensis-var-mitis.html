Fleurya capensis var. mitis

Fleurya capensis var. mitis, commonly known as the white-flowered fleurya, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, tubular white flowers. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.
