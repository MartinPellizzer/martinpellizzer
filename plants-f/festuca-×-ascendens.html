Festuca × ascendens

Festuca × ascendens, commonly known as the hybrid tall fescue, is a perennial grass native to the temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, tufted foliage, often reaching heights of 30 to 100 centimeters. The plant produces spikelets that are typically 5 to 10 millimeters long, with lemmas that are usually hairy and pointed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly used in landscaping and as a forage crop.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.
