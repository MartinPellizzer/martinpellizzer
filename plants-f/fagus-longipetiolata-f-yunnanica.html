Fagus longipetiolata f. yunnanica

Fagus longipetiolata f. yunnanica, commonly known as the Yunnan beech, is a variant of the long-petioled beech, native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in cool, moist temperate forests at high altitudes. The plant is characterized by its elongated petioles and broad, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with male and female flowers occurring on separate trees.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
