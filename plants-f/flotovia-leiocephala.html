Flotovia leiocephala

Flotovia leiocephala, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom throughout the warmer months. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in hanging baskets or as a ground cover. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Due to its attractive appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
