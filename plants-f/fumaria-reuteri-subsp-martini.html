Fumaria reuteri subsp. martini

Fumaria reuteri subsp.

martini, commonly known as the yellow weed, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fumariaceae. It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along roadsides. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through both seeds and rhizomes.
