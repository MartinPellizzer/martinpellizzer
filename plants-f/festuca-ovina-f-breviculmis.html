Festuca ovina f. breviculmis

Festuca ovina f. breviculmis, commonly known as the shortawn sheep's fescue, is a grass species characterized by its compact growth habit and fine, dense foliage.

It is a cool-season grass that thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in mountainous or alpine regions. The leaves are narrow and short, with a bluish-green color, and the inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, spikelets. This variety is noted for its hardiness and ability to withstand grazing, making it a valuable plant in certain ecological and agricultural contexts.

Its botanical features make it an important component of grassland ecosystems.
