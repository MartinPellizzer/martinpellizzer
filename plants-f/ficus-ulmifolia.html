Ficus ulmifolia

Ficus ulmifolia, commonly known as the olive fig or Indian rubber tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, green fig fruits that are typically not eaten by humans but are important for wildlife. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ficus ulmifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
