Ferula lapidosa

Ferula lapidosa, commonly known as the stone fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clustered umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its aromatic seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Ferula lapidosa is adapted to grow in rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh conditions.
