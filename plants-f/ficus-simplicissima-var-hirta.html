Ficus simplicissima var. hirta

Ficus simplicissima var. hirta, commonly known as the curly willow fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, willow-like leaves that are finely serrated and often have a silvery underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish fruits that mature to a reddish color. It thrives in humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
