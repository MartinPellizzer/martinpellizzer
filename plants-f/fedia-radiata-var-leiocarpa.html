Fedia radiata var. leiocarpa

Fedia radiata var. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-fruited fedia, is a variant of the fedia plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, leathery leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in the spring. The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, contributing to its reproductive strategy. This plant prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists studying plant morphology and adaptation.
