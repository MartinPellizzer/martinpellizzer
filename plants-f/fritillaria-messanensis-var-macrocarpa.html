Fritillaria messanensis var. macrocarpa

Fritillaria messanensis var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruited fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color, often with distinctive markings. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in rocky or sandy soils. Its bulbs store nutrients, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
