Ficus volkameriifolia

Ficus volkameriifolia, commonly known as the Volkameria fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, known as a fig, is typically clustered and contains a sweet, edible pulp.

Ficus volkameriifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor environments.
