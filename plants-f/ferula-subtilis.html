Ferula subtilis

Ferula subtilis, commonly known as the Kashmiri ferula, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, umbrella-like inflorescences that produce yellowish-green flowers. The plant is known for its aromatic leaves and stems, which contain volatile oils used in traditional medicine. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Ferula subtilis plays a significant role in the local ecosystem and has cultural importance in the regions where it is native.
