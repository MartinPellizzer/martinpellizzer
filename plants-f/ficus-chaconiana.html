Ficus chaconiana

Ficus chaconiana, commonly known as the Chaconia fig, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and unique floral structure.

Ficus chaconiana is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
