Festuca ovina var. elatior

Festuca ovina var. elatior, commonly known as tall sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces dense, cylindrical spikelets that are typically borne on long, slender stalks. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and rocky areas.

Festuca ovina var. elatior is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and as a forage crop.
