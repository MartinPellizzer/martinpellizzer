Freziera grandiflora

Freziera grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered freziera, is a member of the Ericaceae family, native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a shrub characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are elliptical and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Freziera grandiflora thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
