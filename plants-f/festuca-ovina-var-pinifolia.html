Festuca ovina var. pinifolia

Festuca ovina var. pinifolia, commonly known as creeping fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and fine, needle-like leaves that give it a resemblance to pine needles. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, open woodlands, and rocky slopes. Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
