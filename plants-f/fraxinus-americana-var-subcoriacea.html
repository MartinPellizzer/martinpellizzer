Fraxinus americana var. subcoriacea

Fraxinus americana var. subcoriacea, commonly known as the smooth ash or American white ash, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age, and its compound leaves that are opposite and have 7 to 15 leaflets. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by samaras with two papery wings.

Its wood is strong and flexible, making it valuable for various uses, including furniture and sports equipment.
