Fimbristylis glauca

Fimbristylis glauca, commonly known as blue false grass, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades that are often tinged with silver. The plant produces small, spikelet-like inflorescences that are typically pale blue or whitish in color. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands.

Fimbristylis glauca is often found in wetland habitats, including marshes and along the edges of water bodies.
