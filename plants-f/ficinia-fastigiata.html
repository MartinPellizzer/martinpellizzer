Ficinia fastigiata

Ficinia fastigiata, commonly known as the tall rush, is a herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering spikes are usually greenish or reddish and appear at the tips of the stems.

Ficinia fastigiata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
