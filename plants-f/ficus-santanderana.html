Ficus santanderana

Ficus santanderana, commonly known as the Santander fig, is a species native to Colombia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in humid, tropical lowland forests. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and have a leathery texture. Its fruit, a small fig-like structure, is enclosed in a fleshy, green capsule.

Ficus santanderana is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
