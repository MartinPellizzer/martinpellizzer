Ferula austriaca

Ferula austriaca, commonly known as the Austrian giant fennel, is a tall, perennial herb native to the Alps.

It features a robust, taproot system and can grow up to 3 meters in height, with large, umbrella-like compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in late summer, arranged in a single, branched inflorescence. Its hollow, aromatic stems are often used in traditional medicine.

Botanically, it belongs to the Apiaceae family, sharing characteristics with other members like dill and parsley.
