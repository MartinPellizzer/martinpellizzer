Folianthera

Folianthera, commonly known as the moonflower of the tropics, is a striking plant characterized by its large, velvety, white petals that open at night.

It belongs to the nightshade family, Solanaceae, and is native to tropical regions of South America. The plant produces small, yellow fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. Folianthera thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its unique blooming pattern makes it a popular subject in botanical studies and horticulture.
