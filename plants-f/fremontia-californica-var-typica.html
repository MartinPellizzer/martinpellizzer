Fremontia californica var. typica

Fremontia californica var. typica, commonly known as the California fremontia, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in coastal and inland areas of California. The plant features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it has a woody stem with a distinctive reddish-brown color.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in supporting local wildlife.
