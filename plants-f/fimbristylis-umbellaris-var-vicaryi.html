Fimbristylis umbellaris var. vicaryi

Fimbristylis umbellaris var. vicaryi, commonly known as the Victoria's umbrella sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered inflorescences that resemble umbrellas, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as swamps and marshes. Its leaves are narrow and often have a fibrous texture, while the flowers are usually greenish or brownish in color.

This variety is part of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants.
