Ficus frutescens

Ficus frutescens, commonly known as the rubber fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by small, greenish fruits. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Ficus frutescens is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and adaptability to indoor environments.
