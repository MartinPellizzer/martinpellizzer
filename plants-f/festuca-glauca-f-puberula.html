Festuca glauca f. puberula

Festuca glauca f. puberula, commonly known as bluegrass with a hairy appearance, is a cool-season grass characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, with a distinctive silvery-blue hue on the upper surface. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers, which are usually greenish or pale blue. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens and landscapes.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form a low-maintenance, attractive ground cover.
