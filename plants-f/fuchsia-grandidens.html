Fuchsia grandidens

Fuchsia grandidens, commonly known as the giant fuchsia, is a striking evergreen shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to violet, often with a distinctive white throat. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Fuchsia grandidens is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
