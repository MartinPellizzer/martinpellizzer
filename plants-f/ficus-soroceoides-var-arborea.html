Ficus soroceoides var. arborea

Ficus soroceoides var. arborea, commonly known as the African ficus or tree fig, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Its root system is robust, allowing it to stabilize soil in its natural habitat.
