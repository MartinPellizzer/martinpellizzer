Ficus carvajalii

Ficus carvajalii, commonly known as the Carvajal's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Mexico and Colombia. This plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered fig fruits. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
