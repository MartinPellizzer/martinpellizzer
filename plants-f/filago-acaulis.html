Filago acaulis

Filago acaulis, commonly known as the barren filago, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and herbaceous structure. The plant typically features a compact rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowering stems produce small, yellow daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Filago acaulis is adapted to rocky and arid environments, making it a resilient and hardy species.
