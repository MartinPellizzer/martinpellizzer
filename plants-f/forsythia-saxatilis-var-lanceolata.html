Forsythia saxatilis var. lanceolata

Forsythia saxatilis var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceolate forsythia, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are lanceolate, meaning they are narrow and tapering at both ends, giving the plant a unique appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or gravelly habitats.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
