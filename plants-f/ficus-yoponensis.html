Ficus yoponensis

Ficus yoponensis, commonly known as the Yoponensis fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Papua New Guinea. The plant is characterized by its dense, broad leaves that are typically a deep green in color. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible figs that are often consumed by local communities.

This species is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological and cultural significance in its native habitats.
