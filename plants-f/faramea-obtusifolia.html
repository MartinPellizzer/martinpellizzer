Faramea obtusifolia

Faramea obtusifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved faramea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom during the wet season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Faramea obtusifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
