Faurea rochetiana subsp. speciosa

Faurea rochetiana subsp.

speciosa, commonly known as the showy faurea, is a striking shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to coastal conditions with high salt exposure. Its flowering period typically occurs during the summer months, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
