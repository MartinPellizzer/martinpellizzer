Ferolia salicifolia

Ferolia salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf ferolia, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its willow-like leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, with a distinctive silvery underside.

Ferolia salicifolia is often grown as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and potential medicinal properties.
