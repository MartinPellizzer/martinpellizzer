Festuca valesiaca f. craiovensis

Festuca valesiaca f. craiovensis, commonly known as the Craiova fescue, is a botanical variant of the tall fescue species.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and tufted growth habit, typical of many fescue species. This form is native to the Carpathian region, particularly in Romania, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its foliage is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental value and hardiness.

The plant exhibits a moderate growth rate and is adapted to temperate climates.
