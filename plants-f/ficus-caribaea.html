Ficus caribaea

Ficus caribaea, commonly known as the Jamaican fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica, and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called syconia. Ficus caribaea is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
