Franseria tenuifolia var. tripinnatifida

Franseria tenuifolia var. tripinnatifida, commonly known as the narrow-leaf franseria, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, trifoliate leaves that are narrow and finely toothed. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of white and pale yellow. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is appreciated by botanists and gardeners alike.
