Faradaya ampliflora

Faradaya ampliflora, commonly known as the broad-leafed faradaya, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the warm season. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique foliage and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens.
