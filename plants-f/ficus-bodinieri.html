Ficus bodinieri

Ficus bodinieri, commonly known as the Thai fig or Bodinier fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, broad oval leaves that are typically dark green and slightly pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus bodinieri is also popular as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
