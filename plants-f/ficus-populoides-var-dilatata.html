Ficus populoides var. dilatata

Ficus populoides var. dilatata, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a tropical plant characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and deeply veined.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect sunlight. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits, which are typically clustered in spherical structures. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to adapt to indoor conditions with proper care.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental purposes in both indoor and outdoor settings.
