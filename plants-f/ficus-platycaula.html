Ficus platycaula

Ficus platycaula, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a slightly waxy texture. Ficus platycaula thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in outdoor gardens.

Its ability to adapt to various light conditions makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor landscaping.
