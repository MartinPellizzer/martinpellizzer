Fuchsia tunariensis

Fuchsia tunariensis, commonly known as the Andean fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep red to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Fuchsia tunariensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
