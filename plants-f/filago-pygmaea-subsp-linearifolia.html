Filago pygmaea subsp. linearifolia

Filago pygmaea subsp.

linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved filago, is a small, herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas.

The plant has slender, linear leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its small, yellowish flowers appear in clusters and bloom during the warmer months. Filago pygmaea subsp.

linearifolia is adapted to arid conditions and is known for its resilience in harsh environments.
