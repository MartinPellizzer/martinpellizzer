Feuilleea bigemina

Feuilleea bigemina, commonly known as the twin-leaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are arranged in pairs along the stem, giving it the name "bigemina," which refers to the twin-leaf structure. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with broad, lance-shaped leaves that have a waxy surface. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light for optimal growth.

Feuilleea bigemina is native to tropical regions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance.
