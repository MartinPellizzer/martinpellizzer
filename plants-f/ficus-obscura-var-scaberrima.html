Ficus obscura var. scaberrima

Ficus obscura var. scaberrima, commonly known as the rough-leaved fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, rough-textured leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish bloom. The plant produces small, inedible fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
