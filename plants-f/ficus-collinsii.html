Ficus collinsii

Ficus collinsii, commonly known as the Collins fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly glossy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fig fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in coastal and urban environments.
