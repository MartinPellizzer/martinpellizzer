Ficus ottoniifolia

Ficus ottoniifolia, commonly known as the Ottoniifolia fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits.

Ficus ottoniifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
