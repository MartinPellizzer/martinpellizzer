Fumaria fabacea

Fumaria fabacea, commonly known as the yellow glory flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has slender, branched stems and narrow, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Fumaria fabacea is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
