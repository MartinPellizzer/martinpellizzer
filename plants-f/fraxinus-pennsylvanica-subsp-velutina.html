Fraxinus pennsylvanica subsp. velutina

Fraxinus pennsylvanica subsp.

velutina, commonly known as the velvetleaf ash, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves with 9 to 15 ovate leaflets.

The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by samaras with elongated wings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in bottomland hardwood forests.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the region's ecological systems.
