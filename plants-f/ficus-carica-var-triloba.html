Ficus carica var. triloba

Ficus carica var. triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed fig, is a variant of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which are divided into three lobes, giving it a unique appearance. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, edible fruits similar to the common fig, though they are less commonly cultivated.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
