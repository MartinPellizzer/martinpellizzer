Fritillaria sinica

Fritillaria sinica, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a perennial bulbous plant native to China.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in rocky or mountainous regions. It blooms in early spring and is valued for its ornamental beauty and traditional medicinal uses.

The bulbs are adapted to survive in dry conditions, making it resilient in arid environments.
