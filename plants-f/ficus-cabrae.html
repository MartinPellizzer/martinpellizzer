Ficus cabrae

Ficus cabrae, commonly known as the Cape fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible figs. It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, Ficus cabrae is often cultivated in both indoor and outdoor settings.
