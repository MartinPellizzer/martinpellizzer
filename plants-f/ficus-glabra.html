Ficus glabra

Ficus glabra, commonly known as the smooth fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, southern Asia, and parts of the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its smooth, often grayish bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically round and have a mild, sweet flavor.

Ficus glabra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
