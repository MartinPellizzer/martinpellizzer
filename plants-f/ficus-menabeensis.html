Ficus menabeensis

Ficus menabeensis, commonly known as the Madagascar fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and small, clustered fig fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, while the bark is smooth and grayish in color.

Ficus menabeensis is often found in moist, shaded environments within the rainforests of Madagascar.
