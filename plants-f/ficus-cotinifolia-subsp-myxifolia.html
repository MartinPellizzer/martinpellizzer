Ficus cotinifolia subsp. myxifolia

Ficus cotinifolia subsp.

myxifolia, commonly known as the swamp fig, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are often consumed by various wildlife species. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found near water bodies.

Its adaptability to wet conditions makes it an important component of riparian ecosystems.
