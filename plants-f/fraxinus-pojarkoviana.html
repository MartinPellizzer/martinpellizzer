Fraxinus pojarkoviana

Fraxinus pojarkoviana, commonly known as the Russian ash, is a deciduous tree native to the Caucasus region.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with opposite arrangement. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
