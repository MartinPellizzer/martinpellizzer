Festuca duriuscula var. guestfalica

Festuca duriuscula var. guestfalica, commonly known as the Guestfalica fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
