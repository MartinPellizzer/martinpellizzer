Fuchsia corollata

Fuchsia corollata, commonly known as the Chilean fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface, and it blooms during the spring and summer seasons.

Fuchsia corollata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
