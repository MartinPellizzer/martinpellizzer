Ferocactus gracilis subsp. coloratus

Ferocactus gracilis subsp.

coloratus, commonly known as the colorful cholla, is a spineless cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that branch into multiple clusters, often forming dense, shrub-like structures.

The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in xeriscapes and desert gardens.
