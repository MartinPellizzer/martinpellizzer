Ficus smaragdina

Ficus smaragdina, commonly known as the Emerald fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Malaysia, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. Ficus smaragdina is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Its ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions makes it a popular choice for home and office environments.
