Ficus almaei

Ficus almaei, commonly known as the Almaei fig tree, is a species native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often lobed or serrated. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are enclosed within a syconium, a unique structure typical of fig species. Ficus almaei thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
