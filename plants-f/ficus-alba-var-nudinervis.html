Ficus alba var. nudinervis

Ficus alba var. nudinervis, commonly known as the white fig with bare nerves, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pale green, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The leaves have prominent, visible veins that give the plant its distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

This variant is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
