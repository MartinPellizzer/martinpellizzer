Ficus capensis var. ostiolata

Ficus capensis var. ostiolata, commonly known as the Cape fig or African fig, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal and riverine habitats. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, edible figs. Its leaves are usually glossy and alternate, with a slightly serrated margin.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping due to its adaptability and resilience.
