Fragaria vulgaris var. minor

Fragaria vulgaris var. minor, commonly known as the wild strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers that develop into edible, red berries. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodland floors and garden beds. Its berries are characterized by a sweet, aromatic flavor and are often used in culinary applications.

Botanically, it is closely related to other species in the Fragaria genus, sharing similar growth habits and reproductive structures.
