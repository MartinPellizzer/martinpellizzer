Fuchsia boliviana f. puberulenta

Fuchsia boliviana f. puberulenta, commonly known as the velvet fuchsia, is a compact shrub native to the Andes.

It features dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name, with leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy. The plant produces clusters of pendulous, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking unique and vibrant floral displays.
