Fragaria sylvestris var. nigricans

Fragaria sylvestris var. nigricans, commonly known as the blackberry plant, is a species of bramble within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its thorny stems, trifoliate leaves, and aggregate fruits composed of tiny drupelets. The berries are typically dark purple to black when ripe, with a sweet-tart flavor. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges and disturbed soils.

It plays an important role in supporting local wildlife and pollinators.
