Filago intermedia

Filago intermedia, commonly known as the common filago, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and wetlands. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the flowers are yellow with ray florets.

Filago intermedia is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
