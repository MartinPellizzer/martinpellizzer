Festuca sinomutica

Festuca sinomutica, commonly known as the Chinese meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It typically grows in temperate climates and is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. Festuca sinomutica is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks, where it contributes to soil stabilization.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological and agricultural systems in its native habitat.
