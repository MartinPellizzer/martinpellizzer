Ficus francoae

Ficus francoae, commonly known as the Franco's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits.

Ficus francoae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
