Ficus duclouxii

Ficus duclouxii, commonly known as the Ducloux fig, is a species of fig tree native to Madagascar.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, dark green leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are usually green when immature and turn yellow or red when ripe. Ficus duclouxii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes.
