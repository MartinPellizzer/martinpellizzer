Fagopyrum perfoliatum

Fagopyrum perfoliatum, commonly known as common buckthorn, is a shrub native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, alternate leaves that often have a reddish underside and are typically arranged in pairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by elongated, cylindrical berries that ripen to a dark purple color. It is a member of the Ranunculaceae family and is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.

However, it can also be an invasive species in certain regions, outcompeting native vegetation.
