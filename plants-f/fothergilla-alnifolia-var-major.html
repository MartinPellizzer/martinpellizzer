Fothergilla alnifolia var. major

Fothergilla alnifolia var. major, commonly known as the Smooth Fothergilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its showy, fragrant, white to pale yellow flower clusters that appear in early spring. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that turn a striking red or orange in the fall, adding ornamental value. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for native gardens.

Its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes make it a versatile addition to woodland or landscape settings.
