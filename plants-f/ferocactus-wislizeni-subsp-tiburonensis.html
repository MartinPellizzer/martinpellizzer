Ferocactus wislizeni subsp. tiburonensis

Ferocactus wislizeni subsp.

tiburonensis, commonly known as the Tiburon cholla, is a spineless cactus native to southern California and Baja California. It features a cylindrical stem that is often segmented and covered in small, greenish-gray spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive appearance and ecological importance in its native habitat.
