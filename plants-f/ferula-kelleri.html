Ferula kelleri

Ferula kelleri, commonly known as the Kashmiri ferula, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, umbrella-shaped inflorescence that produces yellow flowers. The plant has aromatic, fibrous roots that are often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ferula kelleri thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to withstand harsh mountain climates.
