Flemingia macrophylla var. sootepensis

Flemingia macrophylla var. sootepensis, commonly known as the Sootep Flemingia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, elliptical leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and paler on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
