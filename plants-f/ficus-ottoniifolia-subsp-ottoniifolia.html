Ficus ottoniifolia subsp. ottoniifolia

Ficus ottoniifolia subsp.

ottoniifolia, commonly known as the Otto's fig, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized tree with smooth, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
