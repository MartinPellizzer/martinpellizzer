Ferrum-equinum multisiliquosum

Ferrum-equinum multisiliquosum, commonly known as the iron horse multi-seed plant, is a unique species characterized by its complex inflorescence structure.

It features multiple silique fruits, which are dry, dehiscent pods that typically contain several seeds. The plant is notable for its intricate floral morphology, with specialized structures that aid in pollination. Botanically, it belongs to the Brassicaceae family, sharing traits with other mustard family members.

Its name reflects both its metallic appearance and the multiplicity of its seed pods.
