Ficus hesperidiiformis

Ficus hesperidiiformis, commonly known as the Mexican fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

The species is also valued for its ability to adapt to various soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
