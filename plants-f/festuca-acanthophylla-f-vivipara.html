Festuca acanthophylla f. vivipara

Festuca acanthophylla f. vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous fescue, is a variant of the acanthophylla fescue species.

This plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, grass-like leaves. It is a perennial species that thrives in well-drained soils, often found in open, sunny habitats. One of its notable botanical features is the presence of vivipary, where the plant produces live offspring on its stems.

This trait makes it unique among fescue species and adds to its ecological significance.
