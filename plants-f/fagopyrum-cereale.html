Fagopyrum cereale

Fagopyrum cereale, commonly known as buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is an annual or biennial herb with a distinctive triangular stem and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically arranged in clusters. Buckwheat is known for its ability to thrive in poor soils and is often used in agriculture for its hardy nature.

Its seeds are rich in nutrients and are used in various food products and traditional medicine.
