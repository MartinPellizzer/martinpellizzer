Festuca rubra f. glabra

Festuca rubra f. glabra, commonly known as smooth red fescue, is a grass species characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, well-drained soils in grasslands and woodland edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to reddish flowers, which appear in late summer. Its leaves are smooth and hairless, giving it a distinctive appearance compared to other fescue varieties.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
