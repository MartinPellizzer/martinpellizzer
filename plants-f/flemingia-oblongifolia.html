Flemingia oblongifolia

Flemingia oblongifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf flemingia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. Flemingia oblongifolia is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
