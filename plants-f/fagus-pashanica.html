Fagus pashanica

Fagus pashanica, commonly known as the Japanese beech, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It produces small, spiny nuts enclosed in a cupule, which provide food for various wildlife.

The plant thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.
