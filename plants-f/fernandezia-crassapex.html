Fernandezia crassapex

Fernandezia crassapex, commonly known as the thick-panicle fern, is a species of fern native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its robust, upright fronds that can grow up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces large, clustered sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are responsible for spore dispersal. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Its unique morphology makes it an interesting subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
