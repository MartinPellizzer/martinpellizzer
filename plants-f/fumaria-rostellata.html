Fumaria rostellata

Fumaria rostellata, commonly known as the rostellate gloryflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fumariaceae.

It is native to regions of southern Africa, particularly South Africa, where it thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant produces delicate, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Fumaria rostellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
