Fagus moorei

Fagus moorei, commonly known as the Moore's beech, is a species of flowering plant in the beech family Fagaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of New Zealand, particularly the South Island. The tree is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and elliptical leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long. It produces small, edible nuts enclosed in spiny cups, which are an important food source for local wildlife.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
