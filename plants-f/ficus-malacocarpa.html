Ficus malacocarpa

Ficus malacocarpa, commonly known as the Malacocarpa fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
