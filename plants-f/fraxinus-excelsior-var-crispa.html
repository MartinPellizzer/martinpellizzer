Fraxinus excelsior var. crispa

Fraxinus excelsior var. crispa, commonly known as the warty ash, is a variant of the common ash tree characterized by its distinctive warty or knobby bark.

It is a deciduous tree native to Europe, typically found in moist, shaded woodlands. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated margin, turning yellow in autumn. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit.

This variety is valued for its ornamental features and historical significance in traditional medicine.
