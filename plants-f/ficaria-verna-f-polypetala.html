Ficaria verna f. polypetala

Ficaria verna f. polypetala, commonly known as the snowdrop, is a perennial plant belonging to the Ranunculaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, nodding flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant features a short, fleshy rhizome and produces small, lance-shaped leaves that appear after the flowers. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rock gardens.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking early blooming flora.
