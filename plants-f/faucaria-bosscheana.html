Faucaria bosscheana

Faucaria bosscheana, commonly known as the Cape tulip, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features rosettes of fleshy, blue-green leaves that are covered in a fine layer of white powder. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
