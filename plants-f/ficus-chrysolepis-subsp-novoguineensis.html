Ficus chrysolepis subsp. novoguineensis

Ficus chrysolepis subsp.

novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae. It is native to the tropical regions of New Guinea and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of the genus Ficus. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is adapted to high humidity and warm temperatures.

The fruit, which is an edible fig, plays an important role in the ecosystem by providing food for various wildlife species.
