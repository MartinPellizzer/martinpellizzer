Ficus sterrocarpa

Ficus sterrocarpa, commonly known as the Burmese fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Myanmar, Thailand, and Cambodia. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are often used in local cuisines.

Ficus sterrocarpa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
