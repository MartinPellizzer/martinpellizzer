Fenugraecum officinale

Fenugraecum officinale, commonly known as wild fennel, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant features slender, hollow stems and feathery, finely divided leaves that emit a mild anise-like scent. Its small yellow flowers are arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family.

Fenugraecum officinale is often used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb due to its aromatic properties.
