Ficus florenciana

Ficus florenciana, commonly known as the Variegated Ficus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white or cream variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by green, fleshy fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus florenciana is popular as a houseplant due to its adaptability and attractive foliage.
