Ficus persicifolia var. angustifolia

Ficus persicifolia var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
