Fuirena hildebrandtii

Fuirena hildebrandtii, commonly known as the Hildebrandt's cypress, is a rare and endangered conifer native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, columnar growth habit and scale-like leaves that are tightly packed along its branches. The plant produces small, inconspicuous cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Due to its limited geographic range and habitat specificity, it is highly sensitive to environmental changes.

Conservation efforts are ongoing to protect this unique species from further decline.
