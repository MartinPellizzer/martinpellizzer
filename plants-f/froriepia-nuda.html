Froriepia nuda

Froriepia nuda, commonly known as the naked orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the Western Cape of South Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, showy flowers that are typically pale green or yellow in color. The plant has a slender, erect stem with few leaves, and its flowers are arranged in a spike-like cluster. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the Mediterranean climate of its native region.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often found in isolated populations within fynbos vegetation.
