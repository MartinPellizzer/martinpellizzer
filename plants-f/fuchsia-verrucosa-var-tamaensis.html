Fuchsia verrucosa var. tamaensis

Fuchsia verrucosa var. tamaensis, commonly known as the Tamaensis fuchsia, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep red to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, soft hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
