Fraxinus lanuginosa var. serrata

Fraxinus lanuginosa var. serrata, commonly known as the hairy ash or serrated ash, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its hairy, silver-gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
