Farnesia odora

Farnesia odora, commonly known as the sweet sarsaparilla, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow to orange inflorescences. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
