Ficus tampang

Ficus tampang, commonly known as the Tampang fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The Tampang fig is also valued for its ability to tolerate shade and poor soil conditions.
