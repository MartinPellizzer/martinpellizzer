Ficus psilopoga

Ficus psilopoga, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in traditional medicine.

Ficus psilopoga is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a moderate growth rate in suitable climates.
