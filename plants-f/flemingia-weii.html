Flemingia weii

Flemingia weii, commonly known as the yellow-flowered flemingia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its yellow flowers are small and clustered, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.

Flemingia weii is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it beneficial in soil enrichment.
