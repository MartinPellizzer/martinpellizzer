Festuca viscida

Festuca viscida, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silky blue-green foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, blue-gray flowers.

Festuca viscida is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
