Fuchsia hartwegii

Fuchsia hartwegii, commonly known as the Hartweg's fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep purple to bright pink. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Fuchsia hartwegii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
