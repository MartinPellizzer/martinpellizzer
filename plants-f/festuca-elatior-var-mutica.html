Festuca elatior var. mutica

Festuca elatior var. mutica, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
