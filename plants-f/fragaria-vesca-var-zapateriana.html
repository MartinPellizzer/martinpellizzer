Fragaria vesca var. zapateriana

Fragaria vesca var. zapateriana, commonly known as the Zapater strawberry, is a botanical variant of the woodland strawberry.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers and red, juicy berries that ripen in late summer. Native to the Iberian Peninsula, this plant thrives in woodland and grassland habitats. It has been studied for its genetic diversity and potential in breeding programs.

Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it prefers well-drained soil.
