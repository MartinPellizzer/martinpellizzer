Fritillaria verticillata var. thunbergii

Fritillaria verticillata var. thunbergii, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red, often with a distinctive cap-like structure. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to cold climates.

The bulbs are also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
