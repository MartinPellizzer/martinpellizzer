Fibraurea fasciculata

Fibraurea fasciculata, commonly known as the African grass or African feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces delicate, feathery inflorescences that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky outcrops. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a smooth texture, and it is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.

Fibraurea fasciculata is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small wildlife.
