Ficus decipiens

Ficus decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive fig, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its unique, often asymmetrical leaves that can resemble other fig species. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green or yellow when mature. Its foliage is dense and evergreen, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.

Ficus decipiens thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
