Festuca barbata

Festuca barbata, commonly known as the hairy fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Festuca barbata is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
