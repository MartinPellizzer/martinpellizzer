Firmiana lychnophora

Firmiana lychnophora, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves that are typically green with a reddish tint on the underside. The tree produces showy, pendulous clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds with silky hairs.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
