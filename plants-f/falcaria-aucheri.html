Falcaria aucheri

Falcaria aucheri, commonly known as the European cow parsley, is a biennial or short-lived perennial plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It has a taproot system and can spread through both seeds and vegetative means.

The foliage and roots contain compounds that have been historically used in traditional medicine.
