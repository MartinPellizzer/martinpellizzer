Feuilleea corymbosa

Feuilleea corymbosa, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in corymbs, giving it a fluffy appearance. The plant has heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Feuilleea corymbosa is also valued as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
