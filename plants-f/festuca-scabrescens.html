Festuca scabrescens

Festuca scabrescens, commonly known as rough fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows. Festuca scabrescens is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil.

Its flowers are small, spike-like inflorescences that produce viable seeds for propagation.
