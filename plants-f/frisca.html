Frisca

Frisca, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces woody pods that contain flat, winged seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Frisca thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

Its rapid growth and adaptability contribute to its widespread cultivation in tropical and subtropical regions.
