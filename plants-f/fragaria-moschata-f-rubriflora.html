Fragaria moschata f. rubriflora

Fragaria moschata f. rubriflora, commonly known as the red-flowered strawberry, is a botanical variant of the common strawberry.

It is characterized by its distinctive red petals that contrast with its usual white or pink hues. The plant produces small, edible fruits similar to regular strawberries, though they may have a slightly different flavor profile. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its edible berries.
