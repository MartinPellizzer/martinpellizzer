Festuca pseudovina f. capillifolia

Festuca pseudovina f. capillifolia, commonly known as the slender hairgrass, is a grass species belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, temperate environments such as meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are small, flowering structures typical of grasses.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
