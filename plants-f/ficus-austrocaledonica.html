Ficus austrocaledonica

Ficus austrocaledonica, commonly known as the Kauai fig, is a species of flowering plant native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is a small tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal and lowland habitats. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible figs. Its leaves are generally heart-shaped with a smooth texture and a dark green color.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native environment.
