Festuca pubigluma

Festuca pubigluma, commonly known as silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray blades that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Festuca pubigluma is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
