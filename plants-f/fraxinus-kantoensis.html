Fraxinus kantoensis

Fraxinus kantoensis, commonly known as the Kanto ash, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The tree has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a pale green underside. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in clusters during early spring, followed by samaroid fruits that disperse its seeds.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
