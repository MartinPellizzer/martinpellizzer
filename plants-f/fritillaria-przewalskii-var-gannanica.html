Fritillaria przewalskii var. gannanica

Fritillaria przewalskii var. gannanica, commonly known as the Gannan fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in cold climates and can remain dormant for extended periods.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with specific horticultural care.
