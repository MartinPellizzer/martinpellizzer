Fragaria pentaphylla

Fragaria pentaphylla, commonly known as the five-leaf strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which often have five leaflets. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into edible berries with a mild, sweet flavor. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
