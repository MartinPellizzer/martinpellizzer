Florinda

Florinda, commonly known as the bluebell vine, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Violaceae and is characterized by its striking violet-blue flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Florinda is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in a variety of soil types.

It prefers partial shade and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
