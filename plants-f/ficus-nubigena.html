Ficus nubigena

Ficus nubigena, commonly known as the cloud fig, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits. Ficus nubigena thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in botanical studies and horticulture.
