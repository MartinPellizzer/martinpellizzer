Fraxinus expansa

Fraxinus expansa, commonly known as the white ash.

It is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America, characterized by its compound leaves with 15 to 23 pairs of ovate leaflets. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The wood of Fraxinus expansa is strong and durable, historically valued for its use in furniture and sports equipment.
