Ficus pedunculosa var. macropoda

Ficus pedunculosa var. macropoda, commonly known as the giant fig tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Its rapid growth and ability to adapt to various environments make it a popular choice for ornamental and shade purposes.
