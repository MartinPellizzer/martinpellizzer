Ficus glabella var. concinna

Ficus glabella var. concinna, commonly known as the smooth-leaved fig, is a rare and unique variant of the ficus genus.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green in color. This plant is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. It has small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by edible figs, though the fruit is not commonly consumed by humans.

Due to its rarity and specific growing conditions, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
