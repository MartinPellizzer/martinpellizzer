Ficus reflexa subsp. aldabrensis

Ficus reflexa subsp.

aldabrensis, commonly known as Aldabra fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family. It is native to the Aldabra Atoll in the Seychelles and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible figs. It is often found growing on rocky substrates and is well-suited to coastal and saline conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental plant.
