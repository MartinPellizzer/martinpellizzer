Ficus primaria

Ficus primaria, commonly known as the primary fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, edible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and indirect sunlight.

Ficus primaria is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
