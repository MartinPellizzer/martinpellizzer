Fragaria mexicana

Fragaria mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central America.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically red in color and have a sweet, delicate flavor. The plant has trifoliate leaves and erect, hairy stems that bear the clusters of flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded areas.

Its botanical features make it a unique species within the Rosaceae family.
