Ficus spathulifolia var. spathulifolia

Ficus spathulifolia var. spathulifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate to heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are often consumed by birds and mammals. It prefers warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus spathulifolia var. spathulifolia is relatively low maintenance and thrives in indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
