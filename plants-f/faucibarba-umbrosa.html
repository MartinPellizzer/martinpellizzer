Faucibarba umbrosa

Faucibarba umbrosa, commonly known as the shadowy beardgrass, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, dark blue to purple flowers. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dense, hairy covering that helps retain moisture.

Faucibarba umbrosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
