Festuca unilateralis var. montana

Festuca unilateralis var. montana, commonly known as mountain fescue, is a perennial grass species native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue to purple flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in harsh conditions.

Festuca unilateralis var. montana is often used in landscaping for erosion control and as a low-maintenance ornamental plant.
