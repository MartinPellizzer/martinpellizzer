Ficus glumosa

Ficus glumosa, commonly known as the rough-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible figs, which are often consumed by both humans and animals. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Ficus glumosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
