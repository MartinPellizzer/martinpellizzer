Ficus carica var. mourenao

Ficus carica var. mourenao, commonly known as the Mourenao fig, is a cultivar of the common fig tree native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its large, sweet, and honey-flavored fruits, which are typically harvested in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking visual contrast to its fruit. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
