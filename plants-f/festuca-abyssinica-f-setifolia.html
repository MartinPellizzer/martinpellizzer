Festuca abyssinica f. setifolia

Festuca abyssinica f. setifolia, commonly known as the Abyssinian bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, blue-green florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate to subalpine climates.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
