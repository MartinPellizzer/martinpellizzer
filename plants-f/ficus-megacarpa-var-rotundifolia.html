Ficus megacarpa var. rotundifolia

Ficus megacarpa var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
