Foenum-graecum officinale

Foenum-graecum officinale, commonly known as fennel, is a flowering plant in the Apiaceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its feathery leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval fruits that are often used as a spice. Fennel contains essential oils, such as anethole, which contribute to its aromatic properties.

It is also valued for its medicinal uses, including aiding digestion and relieving flatulence.
