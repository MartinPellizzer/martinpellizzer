Felicia amoena subsp. amoena

Felicia amoena subsp.

amoena, commonly known as the common felicia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a low-growing perennial that produces bright yellow flowers in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early autumn.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often cultivated as a garden ornamental due to its vibrant color and ease of care. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and containers.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
