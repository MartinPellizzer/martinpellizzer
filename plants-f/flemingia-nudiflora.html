Flemingia nudiflora

Flemingia nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flowered flemingia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters. The plant is characterized by its smooth, often glabrous bark and simple, alternate leaves. Its flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and are usually yellow or greenish-yellow in color.

Flemingia nudiflora is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
