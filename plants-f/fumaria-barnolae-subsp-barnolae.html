Fumaria barnolae subsp. barnolae

Fumaria barnolae subsp.

barnolae, commonly known as the Balkan celandine, is a flowering plant native to the Balkan Peninsula. It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are erect and branched.

This subspecies is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and floral morphology.
