Fagus sylvatica var. grandidentata

Fagus sylvatica var. grandidentata, commonly known as the American beech, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, spiky catkins and edible, sweet-tasting nuts enclosed in a spiny husk. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

The American beech plays an important ecological role, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
