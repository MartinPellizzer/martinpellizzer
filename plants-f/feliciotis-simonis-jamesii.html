Feliciotis simonis-jamesii

Feliciotis simonis-jamesii, commonly known as the Jamesii fern, is a rare and delicate species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feather-like fronds that give it a lacy appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often near streams or in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
