Filago congesta

Filago congesta, commonly known as the clustered filago, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought conditions. Its small, yellow flowers are often clustered in dense inflorescences, giving it its common name.

Filago congesta is also noted for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating various ailments.
