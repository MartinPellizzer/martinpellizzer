Fritillaria bithynica

Fritillaria bithynica, commonly known as the Bithynian fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It produces striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a reddish or brownish hue at the base. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in well-drained environments. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in harsh, arid conditions, and it is often found in alpine meadows.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
