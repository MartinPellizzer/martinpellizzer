Festuca distachya var. intermedia

Festuca distachya var. intermedia, commonly known as intermediate tufted grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, blue-green leaves that are often hairy along the edges. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-based flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental and ecological landscaping.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
