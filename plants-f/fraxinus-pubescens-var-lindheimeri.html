Fraxinus pubescens var. lindheimeri

Fraxinus pubescens var. lindheimeri, commonly known as Lindheimer's ash, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southern United States.

It features opposite, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to partial to full sunlight.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental use.
