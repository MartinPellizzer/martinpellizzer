Floerkea lacustris

Floerkea lacustris, commonly known as the lake floerkea, is a perennial aquatic plant native to the freshwater ecosystems of South America.

It typically grows in shallow waters, forming dense underwater stands that provide habitat for various aquatic organisms. The plant has long, ribbon-like leaves that float on the water surface, while its roots anchor it to the substrate. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often submerged, followed by the development of elongated, cylindrical fruits.

Floerkea lacustris plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting aquatic biodiversity.
