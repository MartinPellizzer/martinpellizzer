Fragaria glauca

Fragaria glauca, commonly known as the blue strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and stream banks. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a bluish-gray underside, and its flowers are white with five petals. The berries, though small, are edible and have a tart, sweet flavor.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
