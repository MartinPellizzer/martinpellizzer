Ficus tiliifolia var. sphaerophylla

Ficus tiliifolia var. sphaerophylla, commonly known as the globe fig, is a variant of the common fig tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique spherical or globose fruits, which are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when mature. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and greenhouses.
