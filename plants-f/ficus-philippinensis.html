Ficus philippinensis

Ficus philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically yellow or green when mature. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor environments.

Ficus philippinensis is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and local ecosystems.
