Ficus trichopoda

Ficus trichopoda, commonly known as the woolly fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its dense, woolly white pubescence that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its leaves are typically ovate to elliptic in shape and have a smooth or slightly serrated margin.

Ficus trichopoda is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
