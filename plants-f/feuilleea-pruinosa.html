Feuilleea pruinosa

Feuilleea pruinosa, commonly known as the silverleaf fern, is a striking plant characterized by its soft, silvery-gray foliage.

Its leaves, or fronds, are typically broad and feathery, with a velvety texture that gives it its common name. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Feuilleea pruinosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance once established.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spore-bearing structures, typical of ferns.
