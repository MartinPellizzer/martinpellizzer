Fimbristylis longispica var. longispica

Fimbristylis longispica var. longispica, commonly known as the long-spined sedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowering period usually occurs during the late spring to early summer, and it is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The species is notable for its distinctive elongated spikes and its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
