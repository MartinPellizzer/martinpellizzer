Frenela robusta var. verrucosa

Frenela robusta var. verrucosa, commonly known as the rough-leaved frenela, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are covered in small, raised bumps, giving them a textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its unique morphology and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
