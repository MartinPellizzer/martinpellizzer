Fendlerella lasiopetala

Fendlerella lasiopetala, commonly known as the hairy leaf fendleria, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale purple, blooming in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are thick and waxy, adapted to store water and reduce transpiration in harsh environments.

Fendlerella lasiopetala is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and unique appearance.
