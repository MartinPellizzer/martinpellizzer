Ficus ochobiensis

Ficus ochobiensis, commonly known as the Ochobensis fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

The fruit, which is a fig, is edible and has a sweet, mild flavor.
