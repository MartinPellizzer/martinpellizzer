Ficus sapinii

Ficus sapinii, commonly known as the mountain fig, is a species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, adapted to thrive in cooler, shaded environments. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. Ficus sapinii is often found growing on rocky slopes or in forest understories where it can receive adequate moisture and indirect light.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable component of its native ecosystems.
