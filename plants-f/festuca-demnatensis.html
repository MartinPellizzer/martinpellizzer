Festuca demnatensis

Festuca demnatensis, commonly known as the Demnat's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Festuca demnatensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in marginal environments.
