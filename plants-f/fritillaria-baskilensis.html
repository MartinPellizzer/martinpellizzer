Fritillaria baskilensis

Fritillaria baskilensis, commonly known as the Baskil fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Turkey.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a basal rosette of bulbs.

Fritillaria baskilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
