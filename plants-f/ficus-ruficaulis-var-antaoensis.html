Ficus ruficaulis var. antaoensis

Ficus ruficaulis var. antaoensis, commonly known as the Antaoensis Ficus, is a unique variant of the Ficus ruficaulis species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that often exhibit a reddish or maroon coloration, especially when exposed to indirect sunlight. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, clinging to trees in its natural habitat, and possesses aerial roots that help it anchor itself. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are followed by fig fruits that serve as a food source for various wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant in tropical and subtropical climates.
