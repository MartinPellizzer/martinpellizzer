Ficinia argyropus var. minor

Ficinia argyropus var. minor, commonly known as the silverleaf fig or silverleaf.

This plant is a compact, evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, silver-green foliage that gives it a striking appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits that are typically consumed raw or cooked. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
