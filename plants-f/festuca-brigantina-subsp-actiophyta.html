Festuca brigantina subsp. actiophyta

Festuca brigantina subsp.

actiophyta, commonly known as the Alpine bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves.

This subspecies thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like clusters of pale blue to greenish flowers.

The plant is adapted to cold climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
