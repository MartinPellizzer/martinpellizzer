Festuca × subripensis

Festuca × subripensis, commonly known as the hybrid meadow fescue, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including those that are dry or slightly saline. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, with slender, hairless leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides, where it contributes to soil stabilization and biodiversity.

Due to its hardy nature, it is also used in landscaping and as a forage crop in some regions.
