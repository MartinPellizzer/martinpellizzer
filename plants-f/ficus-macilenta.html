Ficus macilenta

Ficus macilenta, commonly known as the slender fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves that are typically light green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are usually green or yellowish in color. It thrives in humid, warm climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Ficus macilenta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance in suitable conditions.
