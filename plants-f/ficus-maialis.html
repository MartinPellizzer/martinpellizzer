Ficus maialis

Ficus maialis, commonly known as the Maltese fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region, particularly found in parts of southern Europe and North Africa. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in indoor environments.

The fruit, which is a small, smooth, greenish fig, is edible but not commonly consumed.
