Faba vulgaris var. albiflora

Faba vulgaris var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered vetch, is a leguminous plant characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves.

It produces small, white flowers that are typically borne in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. It has small, elliptical pods that contain several edible seeds, which are used in traditional cuisines.

The species is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural settings.
