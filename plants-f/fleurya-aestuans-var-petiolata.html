Fleurya aestuans var. petiolata

Fleurya aestuans var. petiolata, commonly known as the coastal fleurya, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features linear, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, providing a textured appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to saline and coastal conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging environments.
