Fuchsia integrifolia

Fuchsia integrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are smooth and entire, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep red to violet, often with a contrasting yellow throat. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Fuchsia integrifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated for its vibrant blooms.
