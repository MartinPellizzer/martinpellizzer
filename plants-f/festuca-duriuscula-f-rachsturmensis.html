Festuca duriuscula f. rachsturmensis

Festuca duriuscula f. rachsturmensis, commonly known as the hairy fescue, is a variant of the common fescue species.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in temperate regions, often found in meadows and grasslands. The plant is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This form is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental or ecological purposes.
