Festuca fusca var. flavescens

Festuca fusca var. flavescens, commonly known as yellow hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and distinctive yellowish-green foliage. The plant typically forms dense clumps and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological restoration and ornamental gardening.
