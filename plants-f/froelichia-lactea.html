Froelichia lactea

Froelichia lactea, commonly known as the milkweed tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds with silky hairs, aiding in wind dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid lowland areas.

Froelichia lactea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
