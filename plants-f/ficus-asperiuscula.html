Ficus asperiuscula

Ficus asperiuscula, commonly known as the rough-leaved fig, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of 5 to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are covered in a fine, rough texture. Its fruit, known as a fig, is small and often used in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is relatively easy to care for in indoor settings.
