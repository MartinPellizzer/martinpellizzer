Ficus maquilingensis

Ficus maquilingensis, commonly known as the Maquiling fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are broadly ovate with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Ficus maquilingensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
