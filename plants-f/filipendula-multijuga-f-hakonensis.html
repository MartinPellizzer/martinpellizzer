Filipendula multijuga f. hakonensis

Filipendula multijuga f. hakonensis, commonly known as the Hakonenskaya meadow sweet, is a rare and distinctive subspecies of the meadow sweet plant.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a finely toothed margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal. This subspecies is native to specific regions of Europe and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream sides.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a valuable plant for botanical study and conservation efforts.
