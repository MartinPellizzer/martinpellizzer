Freesia grandiflora

Freesia grandiflora, commonly known as the garden freesia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring and summer, producing vibrant colors such as yellow, orange, pink, and white. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Freesia grandiflora is popular in gardens and floral arrangements due to its attractive appearance and pleasant scent.
