Ficus eriobotryoides var. monbuttuensis

Ficus eriobotryoides var. monbuttuensis, commonly known as the Bornean fig, is a species of fig tree native to Borneo.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a thick, buttressed trunk and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature, and it plays an important role in the local ecosystem by providing food for various animals.

Its botanical features make it a significant species in tropical rainforest environments.
