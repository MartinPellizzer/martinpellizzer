Festuca iberica subsp. yvesiana

Festuca iberica subsp.

yvesiana, commonly known as Yvesian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers. It is adapted to dry, well-drained soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in its native range.
