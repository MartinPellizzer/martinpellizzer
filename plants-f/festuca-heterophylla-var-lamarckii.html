Festuca heterophylla var. lamarckii

Festuca heterophylla var. lamarckii, commonly known as Lamarck's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
