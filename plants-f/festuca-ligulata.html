Festuca ligulata

Festuca ligulata, commonly known as slender fescue, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows, woodlands, and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Festuca ligulata is characterized by its fine texture and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
