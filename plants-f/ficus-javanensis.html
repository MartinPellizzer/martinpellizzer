Ficus javanensis

Ficus javanensis, commonly known as the Java fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus javanensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
