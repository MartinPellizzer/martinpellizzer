Faramea vaginata

Faramea vaginata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or false arrowhead, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for some animals. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Faramea vaginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some indigenous cultures.
