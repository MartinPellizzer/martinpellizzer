Festuca dominii

Festuca dominii, commonly known as the royal fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fine, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. This plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Festuca dominii is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It has a clump-forming growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in the summer months.
