Ficus erythropareia

Ficus erythropareia, commonly known as the red-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green to reddish leaves, which can vary in color depending on light exposure. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.

The fruit of Ficus erythropareia is small and typically not eaten by humans, but it plays a role in the ecosystem by providing food for certain wildlife.
