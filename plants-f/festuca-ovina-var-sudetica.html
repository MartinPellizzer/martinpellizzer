Festuca ovina var. sudetica

Festuca ovina var. sudetica, commonly known as the Sudetic sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous slender, two-rowed spikelets. It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including cold climates and poor soil, making it a hardy ornamental and forage plant.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in ecological restoration and landscaping projects.
