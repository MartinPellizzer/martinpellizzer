Faramea oligantha

Faramea oligantha, commonly known as the African snowberry, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by red or orange berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a distinctive, slightly waxy texture.

Faramea oligantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
