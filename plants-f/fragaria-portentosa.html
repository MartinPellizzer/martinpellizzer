Fragaria portentosa

Fragaria portentosa, commonly known as the giant strawberry, is a herbaceous perennial native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces showy, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant's most notable botanical feature is its oversized, red berries, which can weigh up to 10 grams. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also studied for its potential in horticultural breeding programs.
