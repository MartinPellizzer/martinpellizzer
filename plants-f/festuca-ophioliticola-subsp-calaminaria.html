Festuca ophioliticola subsp. calaminaria

Festuca ophioliticola subsp.

calaminaria, commonly known as the Calaminaria Festuca, is a grass species characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit. It is typically found in calcareous soils, often in rocky or limestone-rich environments.

The plant exhibits a low-growing structure with dense foliage, which helps it thrive in nutrient-poor conditions. Its flowering stems are usually short and often bear small, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh, alkaline habitats, making it a resilient component of its ecological community.
