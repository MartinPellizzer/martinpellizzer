Fleischmannia loxensis

Fleischmannia loxensis, commonly known as the Loxa Fleischmannia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments through its thick, waxy cuticle and ability to store water in its stems. Its flowers typically bloom in the spring and are pollinated by bees and other insects.

Fleischmannia loxensis is a relatively rare species, making it of interest to botanists and conservationists.
