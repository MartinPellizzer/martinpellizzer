Fumaria macrosepala

Fumaria macrosepala, commonly known as the large-sepaled fumitory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has slender, branched stems and hairy leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky habitats.

Fumaria macrosepala is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
