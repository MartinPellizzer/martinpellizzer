Fraxinus appendiculata

Fraxinus appendiculata, commonly known as the Sierra Madre ash.

It is a small to medium-sized tree native to the western United States, particularly in the Sierra Madre region of California. The plant has compound leaves with opposite arrangement, and its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins. The bark is typically smooth and grayish in color, becoming fissured with age.

It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
