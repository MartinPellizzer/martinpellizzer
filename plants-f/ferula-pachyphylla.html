Ferula pachyphylla

Ferula pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved ferula, is a member of the Apiaceae family and is native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in harsh environments. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Ferula pachyphylla is adapted to survive in dry, rocky soils and can thrive in extreme temperatures.
