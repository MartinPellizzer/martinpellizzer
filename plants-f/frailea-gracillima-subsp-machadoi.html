Frailea gracillima subsp. machadoi

Frailea gracillima subsp.

machadoi, commonly known as the Machado's rock cactus, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to Brazil. It features a cylindrical stem with a ribbed structure and produces small, white to pink flowers during the warmer months.

The plant has a dense covering of fine, white hairs, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its unique morphology and hardy nature make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
