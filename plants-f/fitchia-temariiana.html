Fitchia temariiana

Fitchia temariiana, commonly known as the Temari Island fitchia, is a rare and endemic plant species found exclusively on the island of Temari in the South Pacific.

It is a low-growing shrub with woody stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, round, berry-like fruits. Fitchia temariiana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is adapted to the unique microclimate of its native habitat.
