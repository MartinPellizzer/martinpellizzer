Festuca densiuscula

Festuca densiuscula, commonly known as denseleaf fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and fine, wiry leaves that are typically less than 1 millimeter in width. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Festuca densiuscula is relatively drought-tolerant and can withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Its small, spike-like inflorescences produce numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind, aiding in its propagation across rugged terrain.
