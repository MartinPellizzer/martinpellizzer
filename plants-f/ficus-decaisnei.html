Ficus decaisnei

Ficus decaisnei, commonly known as the Decaisne fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by green, fleshy fruits resembling figs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and relatively easy care requirements, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
