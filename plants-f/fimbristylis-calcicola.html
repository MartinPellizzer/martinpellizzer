Fimbristylis calcicola

Fimbristylis calcicola, commonly known as the calcicola sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Australia.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. Its reproductive structures are adapted to disperse seeds efficiently in harsh environmental conditions.

Fimbristylis calcicola plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates in its native range.
