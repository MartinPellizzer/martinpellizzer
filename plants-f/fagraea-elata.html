Fagraea elata

Fagraea elata, commonly known as the glorybower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces striking clusters of white or yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by bright red berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Fagraea elata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
