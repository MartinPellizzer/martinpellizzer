Fuchsia crassistipula

Fuchsia crassistipula, commonly known as the broad-tipped fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist conditions. The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a fine layer of white hairs, aiding in water retention.

Fuchsia crassistipula is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate climate conditions.
