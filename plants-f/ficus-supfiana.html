Ficus supfiana

Ficus supfiana, commonly known as the Philippine fig tree, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically clustered in spherical, greenish-yellow syconia. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The tree is also valued for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil types and its shade-providing capabilities.
