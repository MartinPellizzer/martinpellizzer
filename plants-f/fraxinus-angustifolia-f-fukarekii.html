Fraxinus angustifolia f. fukarekii

Fraxinus angustifolia f. fukarekii, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ash, is a cultivar of the common ash tree.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically a vibrant green during the growing season. The plant is native to southern Europe and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by samaras that disperse its seeds.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
