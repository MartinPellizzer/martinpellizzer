Festuca ovina subsp. hirtula

Festuca ovina subsp.

hirtula, commonly known as hairy sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, fine-textured foliage.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its leaves are covered with short, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and ability to tolerate harsh conditions.
