Fimbristylis platystachys var. typica

Fimbristylis platystachys var. typica, commonly known as the broad-stalked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, pale brown flowers, typically arranged in spikelets. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.
