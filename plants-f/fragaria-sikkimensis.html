Fragaria sikkimensis

Fragaria sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim strawberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the Himalayan regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan, growing at high altitudes in alpine environments. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red in color and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. It is valued for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant has a compact, herbaceous growth habit.
