Fagus sylvatica f. retroflexa

Fagus sylvatica f. retroflexa, commonly known as the curled-leaf European beech, is a rare and distinctive form of the European beech.

It is characterized by its unique, tightly curled leaves that give it a striking, almost fern-like appearance. This variation occurs naturally in certain regions of Europe, particularly in moist, shaded environments. The plant retains the typical structure of the European beech but exhibits a notable morphological difference in leaf morphology.

It is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its ornamental value and genetic uniqueness.
