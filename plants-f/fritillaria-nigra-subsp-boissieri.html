Fritillaria nigra subsp. boissieri

Fritillaria nigra subsp.

boissieri, commonly known as the Boissier's black lily, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to black, often with contrasting yellow or orange markings.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, preferring full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in harsh conditions, entering dormancy during periods of drought.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
