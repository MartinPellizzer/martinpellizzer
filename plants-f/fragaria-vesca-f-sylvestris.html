Fragaria vesca f. sylvestris

Fragaria vesca f. sylvestris, commonly known as the wild strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fragrant white flowers that develop into edible berries. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in woodland areas and along forest edges. Its berries are prized for their intense flavor and are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and genetic contribution to cultivated strawberries.
