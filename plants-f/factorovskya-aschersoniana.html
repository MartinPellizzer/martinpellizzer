Factorovskya aschersoniana

Factorovskya aschersoniana, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It features showy, tubular red or pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface and grows from a rhizomatous root system. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in dry steppe environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine collections.
