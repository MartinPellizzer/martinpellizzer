Fumaria macrosepala subsp. megasepala

Fumaria macrosepala subsp.

megasepala, commonly known as the large-sepaled cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is a herbaceous annual that produces striking violet to blue flowers with prominent sepals.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves with toothed margins and grows up to 60 cm tall. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
