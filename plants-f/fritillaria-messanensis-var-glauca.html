Fritillaria messanensis var. glauca

Fritillaria messanensis var. glauca, commonly known as the Messina blue fritillary, is a rare and striking bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bluish-green leaves and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale blue to violet. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and prefers well-drained conditions. Its flowering period occurs in late spring, adding a unique ornamental value to its habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
