Felicia tenera

Felicia tenera, commonly known as the blue daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple flower heads surrounded by white ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens. Its compact size and long blooming period make it an attractive addition to gardens.

Felicia tenera is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
