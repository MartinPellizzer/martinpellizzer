Ficus xylosycia

Ficus xylosycia, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and often have a waxy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by fig-like fruits that are edible for some animals but generally not for humans.

Ficus xylosycia is often used in horticulture as a ground cover or climbing plant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
