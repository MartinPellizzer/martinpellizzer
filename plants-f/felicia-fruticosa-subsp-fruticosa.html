Felicia fruticosa subsp. fruticosa

Felicia fruticosa subsp.

fruticosa, commonly known as the goldenweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb with a bushy growth habit, featuring upright stems covered in fine, soft hairs.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers arranged in dense clusters, which bloom from late winter to early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped, with a slightly rough texture and a distinct aromatic scent.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.
