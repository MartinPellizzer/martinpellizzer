Fumaria mirabilis var. faurei

Fumaria mirabilis var. faurei, commonly known as the golden flame poppy, is a striking annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its bright orange to red flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are arranged in loose clusters and are known for their vivid coloration and ability to attract pollinators.

Fumaria mirabilis var. faurei is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wildflower displays.
