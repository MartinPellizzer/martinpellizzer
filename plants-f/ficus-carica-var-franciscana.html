Ficus carica var. franciscana

Ficus carica var. franciscana, commonly known as the San Francisco fig, is a cultivar of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its small, ornamental leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in cooking or as ornamental elements. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is popular for its aesthetic appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
