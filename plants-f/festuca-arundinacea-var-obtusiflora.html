Festuca arundinacea var. obtusiflora

Festuca arundinacea var. obtusiflora, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are often used in traditional crafts and ceremonies. Its rhizomatous growth habit makes it a valuable forage plant for livestock.

Festuca arundinacea var. obtusiflora is also noted for its ornamental value and its role in ecological restoration projects.
