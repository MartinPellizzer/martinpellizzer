Festuca valentina

Festuca valentina, commonly known as Valentine's grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Festuca valentina is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
