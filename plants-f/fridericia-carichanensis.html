Fridericia carichanensis

Fridericia carichanensis, commonly known as the Brazilian milkweed, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds with tufts of hair for wind dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Fridericia carichanensis is also known for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal remedies.
