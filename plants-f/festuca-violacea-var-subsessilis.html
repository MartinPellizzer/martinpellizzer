Festuca violacea var. subsessilis

Festuca violacea var. subsessilis, commonly known as violet false brome, is a perennial grass species characterized by its slender, erect stems and dense, tufted growth habit.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, preferring well-drained, loamy soils. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences bearing small, violet-blue flowers, which are often tinged with purple or pink hues. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green coloration.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.
