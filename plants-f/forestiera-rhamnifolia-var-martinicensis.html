Forestiera rhamnifolia var. martinicensis

Forestiera rhamnifolia var. martinicensis, commonly known as the Martinique forestiera, is a shrub native to the Caribbean islands.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces clusters of yellowish-green flowers, which are followed by small, smooth, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and dry forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
