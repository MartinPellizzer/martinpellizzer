Festuca pratensis var. megalostachys

Festuca pratensis var. megalostachys, commonly known as the giant tussock fescue, is a grass species characterized by its robust growth and tufted appearance.

It is native to temperate regions of Europe and is often found in meadows and grasslands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, dense spikelets that form conspicuous tussocks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and as a component in some turf mixtures.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a resilient plant in both natural and cultivated settings.
