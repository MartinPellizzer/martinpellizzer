Felicia dentata subsp. dentata

Felicia dentata subsp.

dentata, commonly known as the toothed felicia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are serrated, giving it the common name "toothed," and it produces small yellow flowers that bloom in spring.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and hardiness.
