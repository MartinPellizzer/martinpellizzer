Ficus hylobia

Ficus hylobia, commonly known as the rock fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Ficus hylobia thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
