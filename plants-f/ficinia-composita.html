Ficinia composita

Ficinia composita, commonly known as the composite lily, is a flowering plant native to the western regions of the United States.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense rosette at the base.

Ficinia composita is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
