Ficus balabacensis

Ficus balabacensis, commonly known as the Balabac fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and distinctive fig fruits. The plant is typically found in lowland rainforests and thrives in humid, warm climates. Its roots can form symbiotic relationships with certain fungi, aiding in nutrient absorption.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its ecological benefits.
