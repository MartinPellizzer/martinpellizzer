Festuca dalmatica subsp. stojanovii

Festuca dalmatica subsp.

stojanovii, commonly known as Dalmatian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
