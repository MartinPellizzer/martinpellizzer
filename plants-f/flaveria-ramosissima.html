Flaveria ramosissima

Flaveria ramosissima, commonly known as the common flaveria, is an annual flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields. It is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Botanically, it belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is closely related to other species in the genus Flaveria.
