Falcatula

Falcatula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ironweed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and its flowers are usually purple or pink, forming dense clusters.

Falcatula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local pollinators.
