Felicia ferulacea

Felicia ferulacea, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow daisy-like flowers and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant has a compact growth habit with hairy leaves that are often grayish in appearance. It blooms throughout the year in mild climates, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Felicia ferulacea is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
