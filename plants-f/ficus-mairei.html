Ficus mairei

Ficus mairei, commonly known as the Maire's fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and have a sweet, mild flavor. Ficus mairei thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
