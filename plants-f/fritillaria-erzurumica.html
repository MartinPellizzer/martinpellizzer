Fritillaria erzurumica

Fritillaria erzurumica, commonly known as the Erzurum fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its bulbs are an important part of its life cycle, storing nutrients for growth and reproduction.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
