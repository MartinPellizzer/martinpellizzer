Filipendula pubescens

Filipendula pubescens, commonly known as meadow sweet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pink flowers. The plant has soft, hairy leaves that are often arranged alternately along the stem. Filipendula pubescens thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
