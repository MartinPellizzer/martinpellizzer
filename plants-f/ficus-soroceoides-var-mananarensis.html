Ficus soroceoides var. mananarensis

Ficus soroceoides var. mananarensis, commonly known as the Mananarensis Fig, is a species native to Madagascar.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in rainforests.

The leaves are typically arranged oppositely on the stems, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
