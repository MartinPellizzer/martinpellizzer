Ferula orbicularis

Ferula orbicularis, commonly known as the globe ferula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and large, orbicular (rounded) leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in a dense umbel, which bloom in late summer. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

The species is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
