Ficus nugentii

Ficus nugentii, commonly known as the African ficus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, greenish-yellow fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Ficus nugentii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
