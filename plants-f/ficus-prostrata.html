Ficus prostrata

Ficus prostrata, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, trailing growth habit and small, opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in humid environments and is often used as a ground cover or in hanging baskets.

Ficus prostrata is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
