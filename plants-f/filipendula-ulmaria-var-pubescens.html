Filipendula ulmaria var. pubescens

Filipendula ulmaria var. pubescens, commonly known as meadow sweet, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic, and slightly bitter-smelling compound called coumarin. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Its root system is fibrous, and it is often used in traditional herbal medicine.
