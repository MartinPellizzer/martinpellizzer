Floscopa africana var. majuscula

Floscopa africana var. majuscula, commonly known as the African giant bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to the savannas of sub-Saharan Africa.

It is characterized by its large, showy bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with a contrasting throat. The plant typically grows up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
