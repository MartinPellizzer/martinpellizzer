Fragaria sylvestris var. hortensis

Fragaria sylvestris var. hortensis, commonly known as the cultivated strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth. Its fruit is rich in antioxidants and is widely consumed fresh or in various culinary applications.

The variety is a key ancestor of many modern strawberry cultivars.
