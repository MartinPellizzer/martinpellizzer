Ficus acuminatissima

Ficus acuminatissima, commonly known as the pointed-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are typically dark green with a pointed tip. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Ficus acuminatissima thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
