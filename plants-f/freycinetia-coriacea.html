Freycinetia coriacea

Freycinetia coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its stiff, fibrous leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are narrow, strap-shaped, and have a rough, textured surface.

Freycinetia coriacea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in coastal environments.
