Ficus auriculifera

Ficus auriculifera, commonly known as the ear-shaped fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the coastal regions of southern Africa, particularly South Africa and Namibia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with distinctive ear-like lobes. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in syconia, which are specialized structures that house the fig's unique reproductive system.

Ficus auriculifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
