Fernandezia cymbiformis

Fernandezia cymbiformis, commonly known as the flat-leaved fern, is a species of flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of lanceolate, glaucous leaves that are often covered in a waxy layer. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of pink or white. It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Fernandezia cymbiformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique foliage structure.
