Ferula angreni

Ferula angreni, commonly known as the Afghan cow's milk plant, is a member of the Apiaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, feathery leaves. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by a distinctive, aromatic resin.

The resin has been traditionally used in local medicine and as a flavoring agent.
