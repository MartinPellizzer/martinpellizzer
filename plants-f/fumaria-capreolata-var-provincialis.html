Fumaria capreolata var. provincialis

Fumaria capreolata var. provincialis, commonly known as the Alpine Corymbose Caperilla, is a flowering plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers arranged in corymbs. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in early summer.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic alpine environments.
