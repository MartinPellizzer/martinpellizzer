Ficus thonningii var. heterophylla

Ficus thonningii var. heterophylla, commonly known as the African banyan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that vary in shape depending on the environment, a feature that contributes to its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by fig fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in swampy or riverine areas.

Its adaptability makes it a notable species in both ecological and horticultural contexts.
