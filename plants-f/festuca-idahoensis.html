Festuca idahoensis

Festuca idahoensis, commonly known as Idaho fescue, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. This plant is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in mountainous regions and open woodlands. Idaho fescue produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife and livestock.
