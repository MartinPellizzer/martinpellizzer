Ficaria ranunculoides subsp. grandiflora

Ficaria ranunculoides subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the giant buttercup, is a herbaceous perennial native to South America. It features basal rosettes of heart-shaped, dark green leaves with a waxy coating.

The plant produces showy yellow flowers with prominent black veins, resembling those of the true buttercups. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to spread via rhizomes.
