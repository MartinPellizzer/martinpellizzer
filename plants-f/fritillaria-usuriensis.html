Fritillaria usuriensis

Fritillaria usuriensis, commonly known as the Korean bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea and northeastern China.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange-red. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a distinctive, elongated bulb that stores nutrients for the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant for woodland gardens.

Fritillaria usuriensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
