Fragaria pendula

Fragaria pendula, commonly known as the trailing strawberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, edible berries similar to those of the common strawberry. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and white flowers that bloom in the spring. It is native to parts of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Fragaria pendula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its potential as a food source in certain regions.
