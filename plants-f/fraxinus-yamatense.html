Fraxinus yamatense

Fraxinus yamatense, commonly known as the Japanese ash, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-trunked form. The tree has simple, opposite, and ovate leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers appear in clusters and are followed by winged samaras.

The bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age, and the tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
