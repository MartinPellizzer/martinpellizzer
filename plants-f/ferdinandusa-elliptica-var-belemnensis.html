Ferdinandusa elliptica var. belemnensis

Ferdinandusa elliptica var. belemnensis, commonly known as the belemnite sycamore, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, fissured bark and large, palmately lobed leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental foliage and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
