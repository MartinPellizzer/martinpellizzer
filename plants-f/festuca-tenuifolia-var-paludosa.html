Festuca tenuifolia var. paludosa

Festuca tenuifolia var. paludosa, commonly known as the bog fescue, is a low-growing perennial grass native to wet or moist habitats.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, adapted to thrive in waterlogged soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in bogs, marshes, and other poorly drained areas across northern regions.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
