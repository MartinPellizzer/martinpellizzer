Fagara arborescens

Fagara arborescens, commonly known as the red-twigged fagara, is a deciduous shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its striking red stems that stand out against its green foliage, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red berries that attract birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Fagara arborescens is also valued for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
