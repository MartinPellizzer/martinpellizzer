Ficus cnestrophylla

Ficus cnestrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus cnestrophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
