Ficus changuensis var. somalensis

Ficus changuensis var. somalensis, commonly known as the Somali fig, is a tropical plant native to parts of East Africa, particularly Somalia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in dry, arid environments. The plant features smooth, grayish bark and produces small, clustered figs that are edible. Its leaves are usually oval-shaped with a glossy surface and are arranged alternately along the stems.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in warm climates for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
