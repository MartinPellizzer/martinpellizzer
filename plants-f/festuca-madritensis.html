Festuca madritensis

Festuca madritensis, commonly known as the Madrid fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Due to its hardy nature, Festuca madritensis is often used in ecological restoration projects and ornamental landscaping.
