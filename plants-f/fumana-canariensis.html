Fumana canariensis

Fumana canariensis, commonly known as the Canary Island fumana, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its erect, herbaceous stem and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
