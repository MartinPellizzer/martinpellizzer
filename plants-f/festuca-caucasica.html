Festuca caucasica

Festuca caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous and alpine environments.

Festuca caucasica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
