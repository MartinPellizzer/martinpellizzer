Ficus changii

Ficus changii, commonly known as the Chinese banyan, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of China, India, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves and can grow as a tree or shrub, often reaching heights of several meters. Ficus changii is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to urban environments.

Its fruit, which is small and fig-like, is edible and serves as a food source for various wildlife species.
