Ficus deltoidea f. subhirsuta

Ficus deltoidea f. subhirsuta, commonly known as the hairy fig, is a variant of the common fig tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
