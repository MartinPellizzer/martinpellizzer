Fraxinus retusa var. koshunensis

Fraxinus retusa var. koshunensis, commonly known as the Japanese ash, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin, turning yellow in autumn. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras for seed dispersal.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant form and seasonal interest.
