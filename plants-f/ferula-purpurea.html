Ferula purpurea

Ferula purpurea, commonly known as the purple giant fennel, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its tall, erect stems and feathery leaves.

It produces large, umbrella-shaped inflorescences that are typically pink to purple in color, adding ornamental value to gardens. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils with full sun exposure. Its hollow stems and aromatic leaves are reminiscent of other members of the carrot family.

Ferula purpurea is often cultivated for its visual appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
