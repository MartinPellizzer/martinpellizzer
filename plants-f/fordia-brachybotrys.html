Fordia brachybotrys

Fordia brachybotrys, commonly known as the dwarf bottlebrush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its clusters of bright red, brush-like flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil.

Fordia brachybotrys is valued for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation in gardens.
