Ficus rhynchocarpa

Ficus rhynchocarpa, commonly known as the round-fruit fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fig fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
