Festuca polystachya

Festuca polystachya, commonly known as western wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, which are the reproductive structures of the grass. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in prairie and steppe habitats.

Festuca polystachya is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
