Ficus triangularis

Ficus triangularis, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elongated leaves that are often arranged in a distinctive triangular shape. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by round, greenish fruits that turn red when mature. It is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its adaptability to different light conditions.

Ficus triangularis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to climb or trail along surfaces.
