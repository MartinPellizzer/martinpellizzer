Fusanus acuminatus var. angustifolia

Fusanus acuminatus var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fusanus, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests or wetlands. Its botanical features include a woody stem with rough, bark-like texture and small, inconspicuous fruits.

It is often valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
