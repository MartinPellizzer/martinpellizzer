Ficus amboinensis

Ficus amboinensis, commonly known as the Amboina fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically pear-shaped. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Ficus amboinensis is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
