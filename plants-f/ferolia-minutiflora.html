Ferolia minutiflora

Ferolia minutiflora, commonly known as the dwarf orange.

It is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible, orange-colored fruits that are similar in appearance to citrus fruits but are typically smaller. Ferolia minutiflora thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and potential culinary uses.
