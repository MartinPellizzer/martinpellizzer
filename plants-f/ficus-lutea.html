Ficus lutea

Ficus lutea, commonly known as the yellow fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers. It produces small, edible fruits that are typically yellow when ripe.

Ficus lutea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable environments.
