Filipendula yezoensis var. hispida

Filipendula yezoensis var. hispida, commonly known as the Japanese meadow sweet, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in soft, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and forest edges.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
