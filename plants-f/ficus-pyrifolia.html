Ficus pyrifolia

Ficus pyrifolia, commonly known as the Japanese fig or Chinese fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea, and thrives in temperate to subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height.

The leaves are often used in ornamental horticulture due to their attractive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
