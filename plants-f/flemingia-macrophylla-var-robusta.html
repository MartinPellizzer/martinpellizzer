Flemingia macrophylla var. robusta

Flemingia macrophylla var. robusta, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The plant produces showy, red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its bark is thick and fissured, providing a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and is often planted in gardens and as a street tree due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
