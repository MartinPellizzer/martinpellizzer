Flanagania

Flanagania, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant belonging to the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release numerous small, hairy seeds.

Flanagania is often found in disturbed soils and can become invasive in some regions.
