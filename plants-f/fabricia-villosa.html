Fabricia villosa

Fabricia villosa, commonly known as hairy fabricia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by pods containing several seeds. Fabricia villosa is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
