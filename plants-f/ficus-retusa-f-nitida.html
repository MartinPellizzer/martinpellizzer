Ficus retusa f. nitida

Ficus retusa f. nitida, commonly known as the variegated rubber tree, is a cultivar of the rubber plant characterized by its striking variegated leaves with white and green patterns.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. The plant is known for its ability to thrive indoors with moderate light and regular watering. Its waxy, broad leaves are often used in interior design due to their aesthetic appeal.

It is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and ease of care.
