Fritillaria cirrhosa var. viridiflava

Fritillaria cirrhosa var. viridiflava, commonly known as the green-flowered fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the alpine regions of Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range from green to yellowish in color, often with a distinctive purple or brownish base. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a long flowering period, typically in spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is a subject of interest in both horticulture and botanical research.
