Festuca lachenalii var. mutica

Festuca lachenalii var. mutica, commonly known as the soft hair fescue, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are generally narrow and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate various environmental conditions.
