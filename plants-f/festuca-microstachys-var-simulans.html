Festuca microstachys var. simulans

Festuca microstachys var. simulans, commonly known as the mimic fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, dense foliage and tufted growth habit, which allows it to thrive in rocky and well-drained soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelets that are often overlooked due to their diminutive size. It is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
