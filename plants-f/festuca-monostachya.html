Festuca monostachya

Festuca monostachya, commonly known as slender fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous narrow, awnless spikelets. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

Festuca monostachya is often found in grassy habitats, including meadows and woodland edges, and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
