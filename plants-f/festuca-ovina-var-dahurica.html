Festuca ovina var. dahurica

Festuca ovina var. dahurica, commonly known as Siberian wheatgrass, is a perennial grass native to the cold climates of Asia and North America.

It features fine, blue-green blades and is characterized by its tufted growth habit. This plant is often found in alpine and subarctic regions, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Festuca ovina var. dahurica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in harsh environments.
