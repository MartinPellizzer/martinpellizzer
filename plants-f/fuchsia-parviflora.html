Fuchsia parviflora

Fuchsia parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous clusters of showy, violet to pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with glossy, dark green leaves. Its flowers are rich in nectar, making it a popular choice for attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.

Fuchsia parviflora is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
