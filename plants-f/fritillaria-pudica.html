Fritillaria pudica

Fritillaria pudica, commonly known as the pale bellflower, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and is characterized by its robust, fleshy bulb that stores nutrients for survival. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in gardens and botanic collections.
