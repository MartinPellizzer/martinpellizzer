Fragaria grayana

Fragaria grayana, commonly known as Gray's strawberry, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the Pacific Northwest of North America, particularly found in moist, shaded areas. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red when ripe. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated edge, and it has a short, hairy stem.

Fragaria grayana is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
