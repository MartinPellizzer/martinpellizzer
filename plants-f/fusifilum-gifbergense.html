Fusifilum gifbergense

Fusifilum gifbergense, commonly known as the Gifberg slender fern, is a rare and endemic plant species found in the mountainous regions of South Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically along stream banks and in rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures are unique, featuring small, spore-bearing capsules clustered at the base of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Fusifilum gifbergense is considered a vulnerable species.
