Ficus perforata

Ficus perforata, commonly known as the perforate fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, China, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with distinctive perforations along the midrib, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus perforata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
