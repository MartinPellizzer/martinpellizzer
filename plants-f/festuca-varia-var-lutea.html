Festuca varia var. lutea

Festuca varia var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and distinctive yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas.

Festuca varia var. lutea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting insect life.
