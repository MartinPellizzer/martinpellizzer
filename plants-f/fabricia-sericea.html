Fabricia sericea

Fabricia sericea, commonly known as silky fabricia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pods that contain small, rounded seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping.
