Fuchsia cochabambana

Fuchsia cochabambana, commonly known as the Andean fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep red to violet. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a leathery texture.

Fuchsia cochabambana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
