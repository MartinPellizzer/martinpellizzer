Fiwa pedicellata

Fiwa pedicellata, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color. The plant produces small, dark green leaves and has a climbing habit, often found growing along fences or trees. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, particularly bees, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
