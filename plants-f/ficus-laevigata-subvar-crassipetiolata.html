Ficus laevigata subvar. crassipetiolata

Ficus laevigata subvar. crassipetiolata, commonly known as the broad-petioled fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with broad petioles, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
