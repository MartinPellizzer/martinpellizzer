Falcatula ornithopodioides

Falcatula ornithopodioides, commonly known as the silverleaf grass, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery coating, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers arranged in loose spikes, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or along rocky outcrops.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
