Fargesia falcata

Fargesia falcata, commonly known as the bent-leaf bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family Poaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of China, particularly in Sichuan and Yunnan provinces. This perennial plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that curve downward, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color.

Fargesia falcata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
