Festuca ruscinonensis

Festuca ruscinonensis, commonly known as the Ruscinonese fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks.

Festuca ruscinonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in various habitats.
