Fagus cunninghamii

Fagus cunninghamii, commonly known as the mountain beech, is a deciduous tree native to southeastern Australia.

It belongs to the beech family (Fagaceae) and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and broad, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, cup-shaped buds and grows in cool, moist forests at higher elevations.

It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various native wildlife species.
