Ficus heterophylla f. repens

Ficus heterophylla f. repens, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a unique variant of the ficus genus characterized by its trailing growth habit.

This plant exhibits a wide range of leaf shapes, from elongated and narrow to broad and lobed, depending on the environmental conditions. It is native to various regions in Asia, including India, China, and Southeast Asia, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The creeping fig is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ability to climb or trail along surfaces, making it suitable for hanging baskets or as a ground cover.

Its adaptability to different light conditions and relatively low maintenance requirements contribute to its popularity in both indoor and outdoor settings.
