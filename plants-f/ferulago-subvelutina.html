Ferulago subvelutina

Ferulago subvelutina, commonly known as the velvet bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture, giving it its common name. Its flowers are small, tubular, and usually pale blue or purple, appearing in clusters.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
