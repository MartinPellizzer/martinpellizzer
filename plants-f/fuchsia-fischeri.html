Fuchsia fischeri

Fuchsia fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep pink to violet. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Fuchsia fischeri is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
