Fumaria planasi

Fumaria planasi, commonly known as the common fumitory, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fumariaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its leaves are alternate and have a distinctive pinnate structure with numerous small leaflets.

Fumaria planasi is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a common sight in temperate regions.
