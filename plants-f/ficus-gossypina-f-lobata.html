Ficus gossypina f. lobata

Ficus gossypina f. lobata, commonly known as the Indian rubber plant or rubber fig, is a variant of the common ficus species.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant is native to tropical regions of Asia and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability. It has a thick, fibrous root system that contributes to its ability to thrive in various soil conditions.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine, though its primary appeal lies in its ornamental value.
