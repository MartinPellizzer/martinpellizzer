Ficus bulusanensis

Ficus bulusanensis, commonly known as the Bulusan fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is typically found in lowland to montane rainforests. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible figs. It plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing food for various animals.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species in the wild.
