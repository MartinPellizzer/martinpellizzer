Ficus oblanceolata

Ficus oblanceolata, commonly known as the broadleaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fruits that are typically pear-shaped. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal.
