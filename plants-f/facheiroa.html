Facheiroa

Facheiroa, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or carambola, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Americas.

It is a small, evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with smooth, dark green leaves and yellow or orange flowers. The plant produces fruit in clusters, with the characteristic star-shaped appearance when sliced. The fruit is rich in vitamin C and contains a high water content, making it a popular fruit in tropical regions.
