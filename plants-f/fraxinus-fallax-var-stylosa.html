Fraxinus fallax var. stylosa

Fraxinus fallax var. stylosa, commonly known as the Stylosa green ash, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
