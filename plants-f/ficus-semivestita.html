Ficus semivestita

Ficus semivestita, commonly known as the woolly fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage provides excellent shade and is popular in horticultural settings.
