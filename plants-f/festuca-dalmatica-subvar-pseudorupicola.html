Festuca dalmatica subvar. pseudorupicola

Festuca dalmatica subvar. pseudorupicola, commonly known as the Dalmatian fescue subvariety pseudorupicola, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing a low-growing, mat-forming structure. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue, and its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including poor soil and high altitude.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience in alpine and rocky landscapes.
