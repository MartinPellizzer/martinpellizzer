Festuca costata var. longiseta

Festuca costata var. longiseta, commonly known as the long-haired bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and long, silky inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Its foliage is slender and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that adds ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
