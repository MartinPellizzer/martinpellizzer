Festuca rubra subsp. arenicola

Festuca rubra subsp.

arenicola, commonly known as sand fescue, is a perennial grass species native to sandy and coastal environments. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green foliage that remains dense and compact.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to greenish flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for erosion control and habitat restoration.
