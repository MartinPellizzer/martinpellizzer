Festuca poa var. elatior

Festuca poa var. elatior, commonly known as tall meadow grass, is a perennial grass species belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This variety is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, thriving in well-drained soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Festuca poa var. elatior is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
