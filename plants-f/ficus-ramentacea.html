Ficus ramentacea

Ficus ramentacea, commonly known as the woolly fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy leaves that give it a soft, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically yellow or green in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Ficus ramentacea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
