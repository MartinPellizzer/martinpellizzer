Faramea stoneana subsp. stoneana

Faramea stoneana subsp.

stoneana, commonly known as the rock faramea, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that bloom in late spring.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in humid, temperate climates. Its leaves are typically dark green and leathery, providing a striking visual contrast to its pale pink or white flowers.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and adaptability to shaded environments.
