Fraxinus persica

Fraxinus persica, commonly known as Persian ash, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia, including Iran and Turkey.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and smooth, gray bark. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by winged fruits called samaras. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

The plant is relatively cold-hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a versatile addition to gardens and landscapes.
