Fagopyrum densivillosum var. pterocarpum

Fagopyrum densivillosum var. pterocarpum, commonly known as the winged buckwheat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive winged bracts on its inflorescence. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in spikes, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often hairy. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with cold winters.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
