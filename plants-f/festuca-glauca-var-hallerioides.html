Festuca glauca var. hallerioides

Festuca glauca var. hallerioides, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, rocky, or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, pale blue to purple flowers.

Festuca glauca var. hallerioides is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, low-growing mats.
