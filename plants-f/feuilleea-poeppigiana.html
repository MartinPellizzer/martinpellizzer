Feuilleea poeppigiana

Feuilleea poeppigiana, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Feuilleea poeppigiana is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its resilience to adverse environmental conditions.
