Ficus asperula

Ficus asperula, commonly known as the rough fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that have a rough texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, round fig fruits. Ficus asperula thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
