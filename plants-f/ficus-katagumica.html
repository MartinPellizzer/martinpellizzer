Ficus katagumica

Ficus katagumica, commonly known as the Katagumi fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Nigeria, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs. It is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.

Ficus katagumica is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
