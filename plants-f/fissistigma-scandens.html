Fissistigma scandens

Fissistigma scandens, commonly known as the climbing hibiscus, is a tropical vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rapid growth habit and its ability to climb using aerial roots. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming during the warm season. Its leaves are heart-shaped and often have a velvety texture.

Fissistigma scandens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in garden landscapes or as a climbing plant in greenhouses.
