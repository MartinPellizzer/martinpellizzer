Felicia pinnatifida

Felicia pinnatifida, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial with a mat-forming habit, featuring dense clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The leaves are pinnately divided, giving the plant its scientific name, and are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The plant is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
