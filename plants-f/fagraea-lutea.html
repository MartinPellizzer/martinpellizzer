Fagraea lutea

Fagraea lutea, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in dense clusters, adding a vibrant splash of color to its surroundings. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Fagraea lutea is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
