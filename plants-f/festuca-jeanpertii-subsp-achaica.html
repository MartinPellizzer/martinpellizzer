Festuca jeanpertii subsp. achaica

Festuca jeanpertii subsp.

achaica, commonly known as the Achaic meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit, narrow leaves, and small, spike-like inflorescences. It plays a significant role in stabilizing soil in rocky and arid habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is occasionally cultivated in specialized gardens.
