Fuchsia microphylla subsp. microphylla

Fuchsia microphylla subsp.

microphylla, commonly known as the dwarf fuchsia, is a compact shrub native to South America. It features small, glossy leaves and produces vibrant, pendulous flowers in shades of pink and purple.

The plant is characterized by its narrow, upright growth habit and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for garden borders or container planting.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both horticultural and ecological studies.
