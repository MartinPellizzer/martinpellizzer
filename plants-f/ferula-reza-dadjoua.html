Ferula reza-dadjoua

Ferula reza-dadjoua, commonly known as Persian cow parsley, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and umbrella-like inflorescences. Ferula reza-dadjoua is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its visual interest.
