Freesia refracta

Freesia refracta, commonly known as the sweet freesia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring and summer, producing a variety of colors including white, yellow, orange, and pink. Its leaves are strap-like and grassy, while the flowers have a subtle, sweet scent that attracts pollinators.

Freesia refracta is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as cut flowers due to its beauty and fragrance.
