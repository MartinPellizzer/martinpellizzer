Flemingia ferruginea var. eglandulosa

Flemingia ferruginea var. eglandulosa, commonly known as the glandular flemingia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features small, yellow flowers that are arranged in racemes, and its leaves are often hairy or glandular, giving it its varietal name. It is typically found in moist, tropical environments and is valued for its ornamental qualities.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its potential use in ecological restoration projects.
