Fritillaria tesselata var. serotina

Fritillaria tesselata var. serotina, commonly known as the late-flowering fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or yellow with intricate patterns, often resembling a checkerboard design. The plant prefers well-drained, sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is known for its late blooming period, typically flowering in late spring to early summer.

The bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winter conditions, making it a resilient addition to alpine gardens.
