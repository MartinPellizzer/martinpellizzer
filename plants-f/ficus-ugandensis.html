Ficus ugandensis

Ficus ugandensis, commonly known as the Uganda fig, is a tropical tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered green fig fruits that mature to a yellowish color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its roots can form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
