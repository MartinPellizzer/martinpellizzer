Ficus longespathulata var. grandifolia

Ficus longespathulata var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaf fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. The fruit is a small, round fig that turns from green to yellow as it matures.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
