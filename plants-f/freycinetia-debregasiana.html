Freycinetia debregasiana

Freycinetia debregasiana, commonly known as the white-veined pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Pandanaceae and is characterized by its tall, palm-like appearance with long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in catkins, which are typical of the genus Freycinetia. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in moist, well-drained soils, often near water bodies.
