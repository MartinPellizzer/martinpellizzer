Ficus solomonensis

Ficus solomonensis, commonly known as Solomon's fig, is a species of flowering plant native to the Solomon Islands.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered fruit that is typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
