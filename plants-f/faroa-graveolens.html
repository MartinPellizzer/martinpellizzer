Faroa graveolens

Faroa graveolens, commonly known as the sweet potato vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped, dark green leaves that are often variegated with cream or white markings. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its tuberous roots, which are edible, are a significant crop in many parts of the world.

Faroa graveolens is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and as a ground cover.
