Ficus nervosa subsp. nervosa

Ficus nervosa subsp.

nervosa, commonly known as the hairy fig or the rough-leaved fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its rough, hairy texture on young leaves and stems.

The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a prominent midrib and serrated margins. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
