Fraxinus excelsior subsp. rostrata

Fraxinus excelsior subsp.

rostrata, commonly known as the rostrata ash, is a subspecies of the common ash native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated buds that give it its common name.

The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated margin, typically turning yellow in autumn. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

The subspecies is notable for its ecological importance, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
