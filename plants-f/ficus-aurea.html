Ficus aurea

Ficus aurea, commonly known as the golden fig or variegated fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking yellow or white variegation. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green when mature. Ficus aurea thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
