Fimbristylis dichotoma subsp. glauca

Fimbristylis dichotoma subsp.

glauca, commonly known as the blue-haired sedge, is a perennial herb native to Australia. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that often have a bluish-green hue.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. It thrives in wet or moist environments, such as swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils.
