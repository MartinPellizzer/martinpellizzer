Fraxinus lingelsheimii

Fraxinus lingelsheimii, commonly known as the Lingelsheim's ash, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It typically grows to a height of 20-30 feet and has a narrow, upright growth habit.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
