Ficus terminalioides

Ficus terminalioides, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, cascading foliage that gives it a unique, almost weeping appearance. The leaves are typically glossy, dark green, and arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by green, fleshy fruits that turn yellow or red when mature.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions.
