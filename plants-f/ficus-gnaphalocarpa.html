Ficus gnaphalocarpa

Ficus gnaphalocarpa, commonly known as the woolly fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its dense, woolly coating on its stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in botanical studies.
