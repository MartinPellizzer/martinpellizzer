Ferula afghanistanica

Ferula afghanistanica, commonly known as Afghan wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer, followed by elongated, spiny fruits containing numerous seeds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are thick and fibrous.

Ferula afghanistanica is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
