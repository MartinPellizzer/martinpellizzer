Fagonia isotricha var. crassissima

Fagonia isotricha var. crassissima, commonly known as the thick-bracted fagonia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its woody stems, gray-green leaves, and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to drought conditions, with a deep root system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss, and it produces small, hard seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing sandy soils and supporting local wildlife.
