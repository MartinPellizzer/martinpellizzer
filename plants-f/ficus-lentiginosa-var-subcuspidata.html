Ficus lentiginosa var. subcuspidata

Ficus lentiginosa var. subcuspidata, commonly known as the African rubber fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elliptical with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, greenish-white fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus lentiginosa var. subcuspidata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
