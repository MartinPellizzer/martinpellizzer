Festuca arundinacea var. decolorans

Festuca arundinacea var. decolorans, commonly known as the decolorating tall fescue, is a variant of tall fescue characterized by its unique coloration.

This plant is a perennial grass native to Europe and is often found in grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves exhibit a distinctive bluish-green hue, which gradually fades to a lighter color as the plant matures. The inflorescence consists of dense, cylindrical spikelets that produce small, spike-like flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
