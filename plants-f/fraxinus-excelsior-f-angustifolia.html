Fraxinus excelsior f. angustifolia

Fraxinus excelsior f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ash, is a botanical variant of the common ash tree.

It is characterized by its narrower, more elongated leaves compared to the typical form of Fraxinus excelsior. The plant belongs to the Oleaceae family and is native to Europe. Its leaves are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a serrated edge.

This form is often found in woodland edges and moist soils, where it thrives in partial shade to full sun.
