Funkia cordata

Funkia cordata, commonly known as the Japanese arrowhead, is a perennial aquatic plant native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a waxy surface, and it produces striking white or pale purple flowers in late summer. The plant thrives in shallow water or moist soil, making it a popular choice for pond and wetland gardens. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread easily, and it is often used for water filtration and erosion control.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to support aquatic life.
