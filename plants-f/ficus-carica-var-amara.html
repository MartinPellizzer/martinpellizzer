Ficus carica var. amara

Ficus carica var. amara, commonly known as the Amara fig, is a less commonly cultivated variant of the common fig.

It is characterized by its small, often unripe fruits that are typically not consumed by humans but are used in traditional remedies. The plant has thick, waxy leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, providing a distinctive appearance. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The Amara fig is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
