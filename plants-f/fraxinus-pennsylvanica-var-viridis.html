Fraxinus pennsylvanica var. viridis

Fraxinus pennsylvanica var. viridis, commonly known as the green ash, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern and central United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 4 to 8 inches long and have a bright green color throughout the growing season. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by winged fruits that disperse its seeds. Its bark is smooth and light gray when young, becoming fissured with age.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open woods and along stream banks.
