Ferula mogoltavica

Ferula mogoltavica, commonly known as the Mogoltavica cow's tongue, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate flowers. The plant produces a thick, fleshy root that is often harvested for its medicinal properties. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a distinctive, peppery aroma.

Ferula mogoltavica thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing at high altitudes.
