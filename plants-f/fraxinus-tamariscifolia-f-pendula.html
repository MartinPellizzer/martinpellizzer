Fraxinus tamariscifolia f. pendula

Fraxinus tamariscifolia f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping ash, is a decorative tree characterized by its cascading branches that create a striking, ethereal appearance.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is native to the southeastern United States, particularly in areas with moist, well-drained soils. The tree features small, opposite leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter on the underside. Its flowers are greenish-yellow and appear in clusters, followed by small, winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks due to its unique form and adaptability to various growing conditions.
