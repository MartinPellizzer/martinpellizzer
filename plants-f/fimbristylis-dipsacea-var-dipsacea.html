Fimbristylis dipsacea var. dipsacea

Fimbristylis dipsacea var. dipsacea, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy habitats in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats in wet soils.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.
