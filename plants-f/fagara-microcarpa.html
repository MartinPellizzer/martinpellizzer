Fagara microcarpa

Fagara microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited fagara, is a member of the Rutaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and fragrant flowers.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red upon maturity. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Fagara microcarpa is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to adapt to various soil types.
