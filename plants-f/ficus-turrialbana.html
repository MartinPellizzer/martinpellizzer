Ficus turrialbana

Ficus turrialbana, commonly known as the Turrialba fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Central America, particularly Costa Rica and Panama, and thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible figs, which are often used in local cuisines.

Ficus turrialbana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to adapt to various growing conditions.
