Ficus arnottiana

Ficus arnottiana, commonly known as the Arnott's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically broad and heart-shaped. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus arnottiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
