Freycinetia acuta

Freycinetia acuta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often spirally arranged. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by triangular, fibrous fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in various Pacific Island cultures.

Freycinetia acuta is well adapted to coastal and humid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
