Festuca pilar-franceii

Festuca pilar-franceii, commonly known as the Pilar-France grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often bear small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for alpine and rocky habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Festuca pilar-franceii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
