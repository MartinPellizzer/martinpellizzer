Festuca tenella

Festuca tenella, commonly known as slender fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes. The plant has fine, narrow leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. Festuca tenella is known for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping for its delicate appearance.

It is also valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
