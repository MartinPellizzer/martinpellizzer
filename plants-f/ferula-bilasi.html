Ferula bilasi

Ferula bilasi, commonly known as the Kashmiri ferula, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by long, narrow seed pods. Ferula bilasi is known for its aromatic properties and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the study of medicinal plants in the region.
