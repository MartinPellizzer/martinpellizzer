Feuilleea hypoleuca

Feuilleea hypoleuca, commonly known as the silverleaf fern, is a species of flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its elegant, feathery fronds that are covered in fine, silvery hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are compound, with each frond composed of numerous smaller leaflets arranged in a fan-like pattern.

Feuilleea hypoleuca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic qualities.
