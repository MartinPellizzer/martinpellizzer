Fritillaria walujewii var. shawanensis

Fritillaria walujewii var. shawanensis, commonly known as the Shawan fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, preferring well-drained environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a central bulb.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
