Ferolia brasiliensis

Ferolia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian lemon tree, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces fragrant, yellow-orange fruits that are similar in appearance to citrus fruits but have a distinct flavor. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a leathery texture.

Ferolia brasiliensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has potential uses in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
