Fagelia cerasifolia

Fagelia cerasifolia, commonly known as the cherry-leaf fagelia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and chestnuts, and is characterized by its alternate, simple, and often glossy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in the spring, followed by edible, sweet, red drupes that resemble cherries.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
