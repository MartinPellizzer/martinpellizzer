Festuca rubra subsp. kirelowii

Festuca rubra subsp.

kirelowii, commonly known as Kirelow's red fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces compact, spike-like inflorescences bearing small, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
