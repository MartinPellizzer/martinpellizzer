Festuca rhaetica var. flavescens

Festuca rhaetica var. flavescens, commonly known as the Rhaetian bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and delicate, fluffy seed heads that appear in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and alpine displays.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens and conservation areas.
