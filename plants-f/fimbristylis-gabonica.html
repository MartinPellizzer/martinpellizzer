Fimbristylis gabonica

Fimbristylis gabonica, commonly known as the Gabon flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in saturated soils. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are often clustered at the tips of the stems. It is native to parts of Africa and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

Due to its preference for moist habitats, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting aquatic ecosystems.
