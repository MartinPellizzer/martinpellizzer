Ficus leavensii

Ficus leavensii, commonly known as the African fig tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, including countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, clustered fruits that are edible. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.

Ficus leavensii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
