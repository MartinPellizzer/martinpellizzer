Fraxinus boscii

Fraxinus boscii, commonly known as the Bosci's ash, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is found in regions such as California and Nevada. The tree has opposite, simple leaves that are usually oval to elliptic in shape. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers appear in clusters and are followed by winged fruits.

The bark is smooth and grayish, with a tendency to flake in older trees.
