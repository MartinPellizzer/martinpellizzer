Ficus nemoralis

Ficus nemoralis, commonly known as the wild fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to various regions in Asia, including China, India, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Ficus nemoralis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along stream banks.

Its fruit, which is a small, edible fig, plays an important role in the diet of local wildlife and has been used traditionally in some cultures.
