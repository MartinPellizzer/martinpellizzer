Faba vulgaris var. pliniana

Faba vulgaris var. pliniana, commonly known as the Italian fava bean, is a variety of the broad bean plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, edible pods that contain angular, yellowish-brown seeds. The plant is a dicot, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and exhibits typical legume features such as compound leaves and stipules. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional seeds rich in protein and fiber.

The plant also has a symbiotic relationship with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.
