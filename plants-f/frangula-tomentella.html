Frangula tomentella

Frangula tomentella, commonly known as the woolly frangula, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its grayish, hairy foliage and small, yellowish flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces small, dark red berries that are edible for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Frangula tomentella plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various animals.
