Fagraea crenulata

Fagraea crenulata, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often marked with silver or white veins. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by bright orange or red berries. Fagraea crenulata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
