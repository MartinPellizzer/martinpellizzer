Forsteronia acouci

Forsteronia acouci, commonly known as the acouci orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its delicate, pendulous inflorescences and showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged in a basal rosette, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Forsteronia acouci is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
