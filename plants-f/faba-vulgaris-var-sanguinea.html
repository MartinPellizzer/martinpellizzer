Faba vulgaris var. sanguinea

Faba vulgaris var. sanguinea, commonly known as the crimson vetch, is a leguminous plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive crimson flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing edible seeds that are rich in protein and fiber. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in agricultural systems for soil enrichment.

Its ornamental value also makes it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
