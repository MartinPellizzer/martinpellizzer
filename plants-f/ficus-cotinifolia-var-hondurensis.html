Ficus cotinifolia var. hondurensis

Ficus cotinifolia var. hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduran fig, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
