Filago pyramidata subsp. eriocephala

Filago pyramidata subsp.

eriocephala, commonly known as the woolly filago, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and features small, glandular, woolly leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
