Faradaya squamata

Faradaya squamata, commonly known as the silverleaf faradaya, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, scale-like leaves that are densely covered in fine, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Faradaya squamata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
