Ficus arctocarpa

Ficus arctocarpa, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically dark purple when mature.

Ficus arctocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
