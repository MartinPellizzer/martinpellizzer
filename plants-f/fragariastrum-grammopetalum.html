Fragariastrum grammopetalum

Fragariastrum grammopetalum, commonly known as the narrow-petalled strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern China.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, aggregate fruits that resemble strawberries. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and has a compact growth habit. Its flowers are white to pink, with five petals that are narrow and pointed, giving it its scientific name.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential horticultural value.
