Freesia xanthospila

Freesia xanthospila, commonly known as the yellow freesia, is a hybrid perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers. The flowers typically bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and white, with a striking, intricate pattern on the petals. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Due to its vibrant colors and pleasant scent, it is widely cultivated for ornamental purposes.
