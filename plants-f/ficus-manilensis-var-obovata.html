Ficus manilensis var. obovata

Ficus manilensis var. obovata, commonly known as the Manila fig or obovate fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its broad, obovate-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

The plant prefers warm, humid environments and is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
