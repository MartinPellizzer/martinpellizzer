Freesia cruenta

Freesia cruenta, commonly known as the sweet scent freesia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring and summer, producing flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and white. Its leaves are long, narrow, and grass-like, growing from a bulbous base.

Freesia cruenta is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as cut flowers due to its pleasant fragrance and vibrant appearance.
