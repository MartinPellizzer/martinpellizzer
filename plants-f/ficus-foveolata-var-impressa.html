Ficus foveolata var. impressa

Ficus foveolata var. impressa, commonly known as the variegated fiddle-leaf fig, is a striking indoor plant characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that often display variegation in white, yellow, or cream.

Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in warm, humid environments with bright, indirect light. The plant has a sturdy, upright growth habit and can develop a thick, woody trunk over time. It is relatively low-maintenance, requiring moderate watering and well-draining soil.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and interior decor.
