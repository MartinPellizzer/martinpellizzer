Ferreola

Ferreola, commonly known as the bluebell or bluebell flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Campanulaceae.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, often blue or violet flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in woodland areas and prefers moist, well-drained soil. Ferreola species are perennial herbs with opposite, lance-shaped leaves.

They are valued for their ornamental beauty and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
