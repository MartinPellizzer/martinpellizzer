Festuca stricta subsp. bauzanina

Festuca stricta subsp.

bauzanina, commonly known as the narrowleaf bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cooler climates.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
