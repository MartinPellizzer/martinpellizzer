Ferocactus emoryi subsp. emoryi

Ferocactus emoryi subsp.

emoryi, commonly known as Emory's barrel cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a columnar cactus with a thick, ribbed stem that can grow up to 3 meters tall.

The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the summer, attracting various pollinators. Its spines are typically yellow to brown and provide protection from herbivores and excessive sunlight.

This subspecies is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its succulent stem for survival during dry periods.
