Freesia speciosa

Freesia speciosa, commonly known as the common freesia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The flowers typically bloom in shades of yellow, orange, pink, and white, with a distinctive spurred petal at the base. Freesias are herbaceous perennials that thrive in well-drained soil and full sun.

They are popular in gardens and floral arrangements due to their vibrant colors and pleasant scent.
