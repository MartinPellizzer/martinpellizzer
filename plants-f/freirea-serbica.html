Freirea serbica

Freirea serbica, commonly known as the Serbica freirea, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are narrow and hairy, helping to reduce water loss in arid conditions.

Freirea serbica produces small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
