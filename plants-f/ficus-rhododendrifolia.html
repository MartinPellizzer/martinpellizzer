Ficus rhododendrifolia

Ficus rhododendrifolia, commonly known as the red-leaf fig, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often display a reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus rhododendrifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
