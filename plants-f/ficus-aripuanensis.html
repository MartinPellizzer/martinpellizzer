Ficus aripuanensis

Ficus aripuanensis, commonly known as the Brazilian fig tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive aerial roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in figs, which are enclosed within a fleshy, greenish husk. Ficus aripuanensis is often found in tropical rainforests and thrives in humid, warm climates.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
