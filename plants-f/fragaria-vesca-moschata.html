Fragaria vesca moschata

Fragaria vesca moschata, commonly known as the musky strawberry, is a species of wild strawberry native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial plant with trifoliate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The fruit is a small, red, juicy berry with a sweet and slightly musky flavor. It is often found in woodland areas and thrives in cool, moist environments.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in culinary and medicinal applications.
