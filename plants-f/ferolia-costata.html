Ferolia costata

Ferolia costata, commonly known as the costate ferolia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white wax. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by orange to red berries. Ferolia costata is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature.
