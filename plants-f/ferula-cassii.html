Ferula cassii

Ferula cassii, commonly known as the Persian cow's milk plant, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a milky sap, which is a key feature of the genus Ferula. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in dry, rocky soils.

Ferula cassii is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
