Forestiera ekmanii

Forestiera ekmanii, commonly known as the Sierra foothill forestiera, is a small evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, such as chaparral and woodland areas. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Forestiera ekmanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
