Fritillaria biebersteiniana

Fritillaria biebersteiniana, commonly known as the Siberian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a single bulb. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Fritillaria biebersteiniana is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
