Ficus sarmentosa var. oleiformis

Ficus sarmentosa var. oleiformis, commonly known as the fatshedder or rubber fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers followed by greenish-white fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

The varietal name "oleiformis" refers to its oily or greasy texture, which is a distinguishing feature of the plant.
