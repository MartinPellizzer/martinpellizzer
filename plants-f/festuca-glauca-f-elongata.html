Festuca glauca f. elongata

Festuca glauca f. elongata, commonly known as bluegrass with elongated leaves, is a variant of the bluegrass species characterized by its distinctive, elongated foliage.

This plant is a cool-season grass that thrives in temperate climates, often found in open, sunny areas such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are typically narrow and blue-green in color, giving it a striking visual appearance. The inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to form dense, durable turf.
