Ficus proteus

Ficus proteus, commonly known as the giant ficus or African rubber plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, pale greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Ficus proteus is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree that can reach heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.
