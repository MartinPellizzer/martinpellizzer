Ficus aganophila

Ficus aganophila, commonly known as the silver-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, silver-green leaves that are often variegated with white or pale markings. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible figs.

Ficus aganophila is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
