Festuca dasyclada

Festuca dasyclada, commonly known as the tufted bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and blue-green color, which gives it a striking appearance in gardens. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 18 inches and forms dense, mat-like growths. It prefers well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Festuca dasyclada is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
