Ficus behrmanniana

Ficus behrmanniana, commonly known as the Behrmann's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia, and is characterized by its glossy, broad leaves. The plant is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in indoor conditions with moderate light. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus behrmanniana is valued for its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
