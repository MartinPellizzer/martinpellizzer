Fosterella micrantha

Fosterella micrantha, commonly known as the small-flowered fosterella, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped leaves that form a water-holding cup. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the axils of the leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Fosterella micrantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
