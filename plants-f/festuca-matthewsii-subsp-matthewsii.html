Festuca matthewsii subsp. matthewsii

Festuca matthewsii subsp.

matthewsii, commonly known as Matthews' fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments. Festuca matthewsii subsp.

matthewsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh climates.
