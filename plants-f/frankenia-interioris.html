Frankenia interioris

Frankenia interioris, commonly known as the coastal frangipani, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or limestone soils, often in dune environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring and summer months. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly rough texture.

Frankenia interioris is well adapted to salt spray and can thrive in harsh coastal conditions.
