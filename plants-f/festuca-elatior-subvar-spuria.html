Festuca elatior subvar. spuria

Festuca elatior subvar. spuria, commonly known as the tall fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces dense, cylindrical inflorescences that contain spikelets with two to three small, awned florets. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.

Its ability to thrive in both full sun and partial shade makes it a versatile choice for ornamental and functional plantings.
