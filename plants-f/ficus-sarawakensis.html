Ficus sarawakensis

Ficus sarawakensis, commonly known as the Sarawak fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in primary forest habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
