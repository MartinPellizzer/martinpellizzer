Festuca sulcata f. almasului

Festuca sulcata f. almasului, commonly known as Almasul's festuca, is a grass species characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green foliage.

It is native to specific regions of Asia, particularly in Turkey and Iran, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, cylindrical leaves with prominent veins, and its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, spikelet-bearing flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance, making it suitable for landscaping in dry climates.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique morphological traits, including a distinctive leaf structure and growth form.
