Ficus scolopophora

Ficus scolopophora, commonly known as the silver fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically not consumed by humans.

Ficus scolopophora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
