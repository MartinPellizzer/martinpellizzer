Freziera salicifolia var. undulata

Freziera salicifolia var. undulata, commonly known as the wavy-leaf freziera, is a variant of the freziera plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive wavy-edged leaves, which give it its botanical name "undulata.". The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in forest understories. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a soft, velvety texture.

Freziera salicifolia var. undulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
