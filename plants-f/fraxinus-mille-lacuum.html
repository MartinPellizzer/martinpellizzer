Fraxinus mille-lacuum

Fraxinus mille-lacuum, commonly known as the thousand lacquers maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its opposite, pinnately compound leaves that turn vibrant shades of yellow and red in autumn. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by samaras with winged seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often developing shallow fissures with age.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
