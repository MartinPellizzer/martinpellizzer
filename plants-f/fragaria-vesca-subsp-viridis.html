Fragaria vesca subsp. viridis

Fragaria vesca subsp.

viridis, commonly known as the green strawberry, is a subspecies of the woodland strawberry. It is characterized by its small, greenish-yellow berries that are typically less showy than those of the common strawberry.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers with five petals. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
