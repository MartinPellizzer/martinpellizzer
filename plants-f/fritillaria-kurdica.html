Fritillaria kurdica

Fritillaria kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and Iraq.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, and it produces a cluster of bulbs that serve as storage organs for nutrients.

The flowering period occurs in spring, making it a notable addition to alpine flora.
