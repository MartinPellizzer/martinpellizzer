Festuca ovina var. cagiriensis

Festuca ovina var. cagiriensis, commonly known as the Cagirian sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to specific regions of Turkey.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
