Ferula hooshee

Ferula hooshee, commonly known as the Persian cow's milk plant, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its roots are thick and fibrous, often containing a milky sap that has been traditionally used in folk medicine.

Ferula hooshee is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in various traditional remedies.
