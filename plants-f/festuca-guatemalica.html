Festuca guatemalica

Festuca guatemalica, commonly known as Guatemalan bluegrass, is a species of grass native to Central America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed sites. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that gives it its common name.

Festuca guatemalica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.
