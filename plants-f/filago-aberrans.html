Filago aberrans

Filago aberrans, commonly known as the wandering bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and grasslands. It has a sprawling growth habit with hairy stems and narrow leaves.

Filago aberrans is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in poor soils.
