Forestiera veracruzana

Forestiera veracruzana, commonly known as the Mexican beautyberry, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces bright blue, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds but generally not for humans. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking native plants with aesthetic appeal.
