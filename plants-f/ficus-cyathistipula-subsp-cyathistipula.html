Ficus cyathistipula subsp. cyathistipula

Ficus cyathistipula subsp.

cyathistipula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its simple, alternate, and often narrow leaves.

The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences known as syconia, which contain both male and female flowers. Its fruit is a small, greenish fig that turns reddish when mature.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
