Fuchsia gracilis superba

Fuchsia gracilis superba, commonly known as the showy fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep violet to bright red. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged in whorls along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Fuchsia gracilis superba is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
