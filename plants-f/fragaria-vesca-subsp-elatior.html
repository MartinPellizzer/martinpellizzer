Fragaria vesca subsp. elatior

Fragaria vesca subsp.

elatior, commonly known as the wild strawberry or woodland strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers that develop into edible berries.

The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and has a fibrous root system. Its flowers are typically borne on long pedicels, and the fruit is a fleshy, red, aggregate fruit containing numerous small seeds.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in various traditional cuisines.
