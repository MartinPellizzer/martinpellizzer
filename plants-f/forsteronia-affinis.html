Forsteronia affinis

Forsteronia affinis, commonly known as the common sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and three-sided stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically blooming in early summer. Forsteronia affinis is often found in wetland habitats, including marshes and pond edges, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
