Festuca djurdjurae

Festuca djurdjurae, commonly known as the Djurdjura fescue, is a species of grass native to the high altitudes of the Eastern Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
