Ficus planicostata

Ficus planicostata, commonly known as the flat-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy layer of hair. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits.

Ficus planicostata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
