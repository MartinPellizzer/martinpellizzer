Festuca lambinonii

Festuca lambinonii, commonly known as Lambin's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Festuca lambinonii is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high elevations.
