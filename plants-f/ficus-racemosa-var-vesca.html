Ficus racemosa var. vesca

Ficus racemosa var. vesca, commonly known as the small-leaf fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are typically yellow or red in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
