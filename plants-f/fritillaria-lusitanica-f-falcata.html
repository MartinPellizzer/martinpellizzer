Fritillaria lusitanica f. falcata

Fritillaria lusitanica f. falcata, commonly known as the Iberian bellflower, is a striking bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a distinctive red or orange stripe down the center. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to arid conditions. Its bulbs are adapted for storage, allowing it to survive in unfavorable climates.

The falcata form is distinguished by its elongated, sickle-shaped leaves, which set it apart from other varieties of Fritillaria.
