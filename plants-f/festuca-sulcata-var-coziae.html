Festuca sulcata var. coziae

Festuca sulcata var. coziae, commonly known as Cozian tall fescue, is a cool-season grass native to the high altitudes of the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green foliage. The plant is known for its excellent drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous slender, spikelet-like flowers.

This variety is often used in ornamental landscaping and for erosion control due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
