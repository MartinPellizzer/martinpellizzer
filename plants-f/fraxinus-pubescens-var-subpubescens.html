Fraxinus pubescens var. subpubescens

Fraxinus pubescens var. subpubescens, commonly known as the silky ash, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery underside. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in timber and basket-making.
