Ficus driveri

Ficus driveri, commonly known as the driver's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly found in countries like Mexico and Belize. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible figs. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus driveri is also valued for its ability to purify indoor air, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
