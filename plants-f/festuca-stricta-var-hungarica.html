Festuca stricta var. hungarica

Festuca stricta var. hungarica, commonly known as Hungarian bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets.

Festuca stricta var. hungarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
