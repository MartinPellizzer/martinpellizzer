Festuca microstachys var. ciliata

Festuca microstachys var. ciliata, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is cultivated in gardens and used in native plant restoration projects.
