Fuchsia cordifolia

Fuchsia cordifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaf fuchsia, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and clusters of vibrant purple and pink flowers. The plant belongs to the Onagraceae family and is known for its ornamental value in gardens. Fuchsia cordifolia typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated for its striking floral displays and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
