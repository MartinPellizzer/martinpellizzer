Fagraea muelleri

Fagraea muelleri, commonly known as the silver tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and smooth, bark-like trunk. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, orange-red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant in tropical regions.
