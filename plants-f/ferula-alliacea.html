Ferula alliacea

Ferula alliacea, commonly known as wild celery or goat's rue, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, umbrella-like clusters called umbels. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often containing a strong, aromatic compound.

Ferula alliacea is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
