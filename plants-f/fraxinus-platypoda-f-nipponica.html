Fraxinus platypoda f. nipponica

Fraxinus platypoda f. nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese ash, is a variant of the ash tree native to Japan.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
