Festuca kitaibeliana

Festuca kitaibeliana, commonly known as Kitaibel's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Festuca kitaibeliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in rocky or arid landscapes.
