Ficus virens var. caulocarpa

Ficus virens var. caulocarpa, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pendulous, elongated leaves that give it a distinctive, cascading appearance. The plant has opposite, dark green, glossy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, greenish-white fruits that mature to a reddish color.

The plant is widely cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions.
