Fritillaria pontica

Fritillaria pontica, commonly known as the Pontic fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from orange to red, often with contrasting black markings. The plant produces a tall stem with narrow, grass-like leaves, and its bulbs are adapted to survive in rocky, well-drained soils. Fritillaria pontica is known for its resilience to harsh climates and is often found in mountainous habitats.

Its flowers bloom in late spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to its natural environment.
