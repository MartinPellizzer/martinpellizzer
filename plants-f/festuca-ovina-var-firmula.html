Festuca ovina var. firmula

Festuca ovina var. firmula, commonly known as sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, producing small, two-lobed flowers.

Festuca ovina var. firmula is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
