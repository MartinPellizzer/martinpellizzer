Ferula leucographa

Ferula leucographa, commonly known as the white-striated ferula, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, typical of the family, and is known for its aromatic, sometimes resinous scent. Its leaves and roots contain volatile oils, which have been used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

Ferula leucographa is often found in arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
