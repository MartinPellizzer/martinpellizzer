Fraxinus bungeana var. pubinervis

Fraxinus bungeana var. pubinervis, commonly known as the Chinese ash or Bunge's ash, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and alternate, simple, ovate to elliptic leaves with a distinctive pubescent (hairy) midrib. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in drooping catkins, followed by small, winged samaras as fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, temperate forests.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional Chinese medicine.
