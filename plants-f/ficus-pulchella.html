Ficus pulchella

Ficus pulchella, commonly known as the silver fig or silverleaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray variegated leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs, though the fruit is typically not consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Ficus pulchella is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor conditions.
