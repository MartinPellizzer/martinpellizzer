Festuca commersonii

Festuca commersonii, commonly known as Commerson's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, demonstrating a high tolerance for harsh environmental conditions. Its flowers are small and spike-like, forming dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Festuca commersonii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
