Floscopa pauciflora

Floscopa pauciflora, commonly known as the sparse-flowered floscopa, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers arranged in a compact inflorescence. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

Floscopa pauciflora is often found in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations.
