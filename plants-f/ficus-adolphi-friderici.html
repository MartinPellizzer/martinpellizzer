Ficus adolphi-friderici

Ficus adolphi-friderici, commonly known as the Adolph-Friedrich fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various indoor conditions.
