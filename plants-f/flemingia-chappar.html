Flemingia chappar

Flemingia chappar, commonly known as the Chappar tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment. It produces small, woody pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

The tree is often found in disturbed sites and is used in agroforestry for its shade and soil-binding properties.
