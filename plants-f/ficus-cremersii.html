Ficus cremersii

Ficus cremersii, commonly known as the Cremers' fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly found in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate in form. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits, which are often used in local cuisines.

Ficus cremersii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
