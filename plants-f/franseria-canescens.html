Franseria canescens

Franseria canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf franseria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. It is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for gardens in arid regions.
