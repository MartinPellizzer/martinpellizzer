Fabricia tetragonoloba

Fabricia tetragonoloba, commonly known as the four-angled burr or velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular stems and trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by elongated, spiny pods containing kidney-shaped seeds. It is often grown as a forage crop and has medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
