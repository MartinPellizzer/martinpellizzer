Fritillaria sewerzowii

Fritillaria sewerzowii, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or forested habitats at high elevations. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winters and dry summers, and it is valued for its ornamental beauty.

Fritillaria sewerzowii is also of interest in botanical research due to its unique floral morphology and ecological adaptations.
