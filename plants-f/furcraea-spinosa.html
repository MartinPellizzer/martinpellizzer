Furcraea spinosa

Furcraea spinosa, commonly known as the spinous furcraea, is a succulent plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are often covered in spines, which serve as a defense mechanism against herbivores. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its leaves and stems.

Furcraea spinosa is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appearance.
