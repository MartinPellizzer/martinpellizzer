Fritillaria altaica

Fritillaria altaica, commonly known as the Altai fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color, with a distinctive spotted pattern. The plant grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, often in alpine regions. Its bulbs store nutrients, allowing it to survive harsh environmental conditions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
