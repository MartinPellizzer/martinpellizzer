Ficus comorensis

Ficus comorensis, commonly known as the Comoro fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Comoros Islands.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus comorensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant in tropical regions.
