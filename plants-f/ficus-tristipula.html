Ficus tristipula

Ficus tristipula, commonly known as the velvetleaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. Ficus tristipula typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
