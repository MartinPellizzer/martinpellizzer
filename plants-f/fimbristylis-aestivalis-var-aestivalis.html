Fimbristylis aestivalis var. aestivalis

Fimbristylis aestivalis var. aestivalis, commonly known as the summer flatsedge, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or reddish in color, and it blooms during the warmer months. This species is often used in wetland restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife.
