Festuca thurberi

Festuca thurberi, commonly known as Thurber's fescue, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, greenish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Festuca thurberi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
