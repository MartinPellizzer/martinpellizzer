Ferula gandogeri

Ferula gandogeri, commonly known as the Afghan cow parsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbel-like flower clusters.

Native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan and Pakistan, this plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, followed by distinctive, elongated seed pods. The plant contains volatile oils and has been used traditionally in local medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.

Its ecological role includes supporting pollinators and contributing to soil stability in its native habitat.
