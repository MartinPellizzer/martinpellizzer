Ferulago Galbanifera

Ferulago galbanifera is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe. Known for its unique, fern-like leaves and clusters of yellow flowers, it stands out in its natural habitat. This species thrives in rocky soils and dry environments, making it a resilient addition to gardens. Its name derives from the Latin word for "galbanum," a resinous substance once used in ancient medicine. Due to its limited distribution and ecological significance, it is considered a valuable subject for botanical study and conservation efforts.
