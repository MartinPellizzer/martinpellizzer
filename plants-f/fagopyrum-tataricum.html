Fagopyrum tataricum

Fagopyrum tataricum, commonly known as buckwheat, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its triangular, serrated leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces edible seeds that are rich in nutrients, including protein and dietary fiber. It thrives in well-drained soil and is often cultivated as a cover crop or for its nutritional value.

Buckwheat is also valued for its ability to suppress weeds and improve soil structure.
