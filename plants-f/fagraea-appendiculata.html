Fagraea appendiculata

Fagraea appendiculata, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Fagraea appendiculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
