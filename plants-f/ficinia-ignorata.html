Ficinia ignorata

Ficinia ignorata, commonly known as the slender swamp grass, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in Australia.

It typically grows in dense stands, often in still or slow-moving water, and has long, narrow leaves that are usually submerged or floating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, which are often submerged or just at the water's surface. Its roots form dense mats that help stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

Ficinia ignorata is an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to water filtration and biodiversity.
