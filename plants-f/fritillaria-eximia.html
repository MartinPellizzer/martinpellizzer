Fritillaria eximia

Fritillaria eximia, commonly known as the Sierra Whitebells, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States.

It features striking bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a greenish or purple tinge. The plant produces a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, thick stem that supports its showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in meadows and rocky slopes.

Fritillaria eximia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
