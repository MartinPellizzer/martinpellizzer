Fabiana squamata

Fabiana squamata, commonly known as the silver leaf fabiana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-white, woolly leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pink, typically blooming in the spring. Fabiana squamata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and modest flowering.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.
