Festuca rubra subsp. juncea

Festuca rubra subsp.

juncea, commonly known as red fescue, is a cool-season grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is highly adaptable, thriving in a variety of soil types and climatic conditions. It is often used in turfgrass mixes due to its drought tolerance and ability to form dense, durable lawns.

Additionally, it plays a role in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and low maintenance requirements.
