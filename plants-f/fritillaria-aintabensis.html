Fritillaria aintabensis

Fritillaria aintabensis, commonly known as the Aintab fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to reddish-purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, emerging from a basal rosette of bulbs.

Fritillaria aintabensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
