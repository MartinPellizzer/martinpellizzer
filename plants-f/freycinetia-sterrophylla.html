Freycinetia sterrophylla

Freycinetia sterrophylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its large, strap-like leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and cooking. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are broad, fleshy, and often have a pleasant aroma, making them popular in various cultural practices.

Freycinetia sterrophylla thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove or lowland forest habitats.
