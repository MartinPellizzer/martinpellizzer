Fuchsia brittonii

Fuchsia brittonii, commonly known as the Britton's fuchsia, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils. The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Fuchsia brittonii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often used in gardens and as a landscape plant.
