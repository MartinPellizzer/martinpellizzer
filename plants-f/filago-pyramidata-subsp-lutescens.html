Filago pyramidata subsp. lutescens

Filago pyramidata subsp.

lutescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered filago, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features a slender, upright stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. Its most distinguishing characteristic is its yellow flowers, which are clustered in dense, pyramid-shaped inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
