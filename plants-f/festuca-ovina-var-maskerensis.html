Festuca ovina var. maskerensis

Festuca ovina var. maskerensis, commonly known as the Alaskan bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. This variety thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to form dense mats, making it a valuable plant for erosion control.

The plant is also notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
