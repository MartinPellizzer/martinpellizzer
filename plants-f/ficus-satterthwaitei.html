Ficus satterthwaitei

Ficus satterthwaitei, commonly known as the Satterthwaite's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white figs that are typically not edible for humans. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate indoor conditions, making it a popular choice for houseplants.
