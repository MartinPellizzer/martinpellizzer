Festuca pubiglumis

Festuca pubiglumis, commonly known as the pubescent fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, dark green leaves covered in fine hairs. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in compact clusters, and its flowers are small and inconspicuous. Festuca pubiglumis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in rocky or arid conditions.
