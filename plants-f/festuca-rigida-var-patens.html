Festuca rigida var. patens

Festuca rigida var. patens, commonly known as the spreading toughgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of silky hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

Festuca rigida var. patens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
