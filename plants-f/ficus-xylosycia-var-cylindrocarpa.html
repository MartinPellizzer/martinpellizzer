Ficus xylosycia var. cylindrocarpa

Ficus xylosycia var. cylindrocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round figs that are typically clustered in small groups. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
