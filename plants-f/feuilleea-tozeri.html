Feuilleea tozeri

Feuilleea tozeri, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a rare and striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, which bloom during the cooler months. Feuilleea tozeri thrives in well-drained, nutrient-poor soils and requires a cool, humid environment to flourish.

Due to its limited habitat and slow growth rate, it is considered a conservation concern.
