Filago gallica

Filago gallica, commonly known as the European filago, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and sandy soils. It produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by fluffy, seed-bearing structures.

Filago gallica is often used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
