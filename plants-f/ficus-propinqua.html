Ficus propinqua

Ficus propinqua, commonly known as the dwarf rubber plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are typically consumed by local wildlife. Ficus propinqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and low maintenance requirements.

Its ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions makes it a popular choice among plant enthusiasts.
