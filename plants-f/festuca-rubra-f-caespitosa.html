Festuca rubra f. caespitosa

Festuca rubra f. caespitosa, commonly known as red fescue, is a perennial grass species characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bright green to reddish in color, depending on the season and environmental conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in both wild and cultivated landscapes.
