Fumaria codinae

Fumaria codinae, commonly known as the weeping fumaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fumariaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, cascading clusters of yellow flowers that resemble small bells. The plant produces slender, hairy stems that often droop downward, giving it a distinctive weeping appearance. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.
