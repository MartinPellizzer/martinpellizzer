Fumaria capreolata subsp. capreolata

Fumaria capreolata subsp.

capreolata, commonly known as the yellow-campion, is a flowering plant in the family Fumariaceae. It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and open habitats, such as roadsides and meadows. It has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy.

This subspecies is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
