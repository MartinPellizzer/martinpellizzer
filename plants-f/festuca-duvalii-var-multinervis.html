Festuca duvalii var. multinervis

Festuca duvalii var. multinervis, commonly known as the Duval's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Festuca duvalii var. multinervis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
