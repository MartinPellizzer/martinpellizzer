Fumaria media var. boraei

Fumaria media var. boraei, commonly known as the Alpine celandine, is a variant of the common fumitory.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fumariaceae, characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine regions, thriving in rocky and well-drained soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and often covered with fine hairs.

This variant is notable for its adaptability to cold climates and its role in supporting local pollinators in mountainous environments.
