Fagopyrum esculentum var. vulgare

Fagopyrum esculentum var. vulgare, commonly known as common buckwheat, is a herbaceous annual plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit, palmate leaves, and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is known for its edible seeds, which are rich in nutrients and used in various culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often cultivated as a cover crop or for its nutritional value.

Buckwheat is also valued for its ability to suppress weeds and improve soil structure.
