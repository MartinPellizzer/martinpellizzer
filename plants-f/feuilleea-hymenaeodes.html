Feuilleea hymenaeodes

Feuilleea hymenaeodes, commonly known as the silverleaf mistflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, silver-white leaves that are covered in fine, reflective hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pale yellow or white, which bloom during the warmer months. Feuilleea hymenaeodes thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
