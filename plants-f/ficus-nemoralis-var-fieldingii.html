Ficus nemoralis var. fieldingii

Ficus nemoralis var. fieldingii, commonly known as the Fielding's fig, is a rare variant of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when immature and ripen to a yellowish hue. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
