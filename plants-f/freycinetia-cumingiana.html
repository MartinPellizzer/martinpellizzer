Freycinetia cumingiana

Freycinetia cumingiana, commonly known as the Cuming's pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the Pacific Islands, including parts of Hawaii, Fiji, and Samoa. The plant is characterized by its tall, leafy stems and long, narrow leaves that often form a rosette at the base. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in terminal spikes.

This plant is well adapted to coastal and tropical environments, often growing in sandy or rocky soils.
