Fumaria atrosanguinea

Fumaria atrosanguinea, commonly known as black bloodweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae, characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers and elongated seed pods. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas. Its dark red to black stems and leaves are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
