Festuca valesiaca f. banatica

Festuca valesiaca f. banatica, commonly known as the Banat Festuca, is a grass species native to the Carpathian Basin.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves. The plant typically forms dense mats and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, forest edges, and along streams.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
