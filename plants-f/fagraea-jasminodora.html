Fagraea jasminodora

Fagraea jasminodora, commonly known as the jasmine wood or sweet wood, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a sweet, jasmine-like scent. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation.
