Fordiophyton brevicaule

Fordiophyton brevicaule, commonly known as the narrow-leaved rock dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. It produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by fluffy, white seed heads.

The species is known for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
