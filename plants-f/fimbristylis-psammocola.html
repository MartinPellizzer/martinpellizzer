Fimbristylis psammocola

Fimbristylis psammocola, commonly known as the sandflat sedge, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils, often in dune environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets.
