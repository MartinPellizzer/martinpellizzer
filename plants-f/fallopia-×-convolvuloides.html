Fallopia × convolvuloides

Fallopia × convolvuloides, commonly known as creeping smartweed, is a flowering plant in the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its prostrate or ascending growth habit, with slender, hairy stems that often root at the nodes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are typically surrounded by bracts. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and have a distinctive reddish or purplish coloration on the underside.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and is considered an invasive weed in some regions.
