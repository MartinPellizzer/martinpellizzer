Ficus pseudomangifera

Ficus pseudomangifera, commonly known as the Indian rubber fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits resembling small mangoes. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Ficus pseudomangifera is also valued for its ability to adapt to various growing conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
