Ficus carica var. obovata

Ficus carica var. obovata, commonly known as the Persian fig, is a variant of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its broad, obovate leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into the edible fig fruits. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to bear fruit in suitable conditions.
