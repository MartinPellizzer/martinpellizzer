Ficus hettensis

Ficus hettensis, commonly known as the Hutt's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits. Ficus hettensis thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental and horticultural purposes.
