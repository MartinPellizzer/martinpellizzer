Festuca gilbertiana

Festuca gilbertiana, commonly known as Gilbert's fescue, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats such as slopes and open woodlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spikelets that are often tinged with purple or brown. It is adapted to full sun and well-drained soils, making it a hardy plant in arid environments.

Festuca gilbertiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
