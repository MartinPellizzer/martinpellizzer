Festuca calceolaris

Festuca calceolaris, commonly known as the silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silvery-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. It produces small, spicate inflorescences that bloom in early summer.

Festuca calceolaris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in horticulture for its visual interest and ecological benefits.
