Ficus diepenhorstii

Ficus diepenhorstii, commonly known as the Diepenhorst's fig, is a species of flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, waxy leaves that are typically heart-shaped or broadly ovate. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in syconia, which are specialized inflorescences typical of the fig genus. Ficus diepenhorstii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.
