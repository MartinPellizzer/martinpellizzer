Fimbristylis stauntonii var. tonensis

Fimbristylis stauntonii var. tonensis, commonly known as the Tonkin sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands. It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of Vietnam and Cambodia.

Due to its ecological importance and ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in aquatic gardens.
