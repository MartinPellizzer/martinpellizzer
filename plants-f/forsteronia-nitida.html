Forsteronia nitida

Forsteronia nitida, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a shrub native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Forsteronia nitida is often found in disturbed habitats and is known for its adaptability to various soil types.
