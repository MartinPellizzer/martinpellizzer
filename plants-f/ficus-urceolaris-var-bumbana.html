Ficus urceolaris var. bumbana

Ficus urceolaris var. bumbana, commonly known as the Bumbana fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are oval-shaped with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically yellow or green in color. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

The Bumbana fig is also valued for its cultural significance in local communities.
