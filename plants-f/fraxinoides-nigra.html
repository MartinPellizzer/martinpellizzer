Fraxinoides nigra

Fraxinoides nigra, commonly known as black fraxinoid, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its dark bark and opposite, simple leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish flowers followed by winged fruits, which are typical of the genus. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
