Fuchsia nigricans

Fuchsia nigricans, commonly known as the dark-fuchsia or black-fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant in the Onagraceae family.

It is native to the Andes Mountains of South America, particularly in Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep purple to almost black. Its leaves are typically alternate, ovate, and have a velvety texture.

Fuchsia nigricans is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses due to its unique and vibrant floral display.
