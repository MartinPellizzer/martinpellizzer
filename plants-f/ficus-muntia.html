Ficus muntia

Ficus muntia, commonly known as the silver satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs, which are a food source for various animals. Ficus muntia typically grows in lowland forests and thrives in humid, warm climates.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and indoor cultivation.
