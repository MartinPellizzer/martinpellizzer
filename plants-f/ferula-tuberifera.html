Ferula tuberifera

Ferula tuberifera, commonly known as the Persian cow parsley, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its hollow, aromatic stems and compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its roots are thick and tuberous, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Ferula tuberifera is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid environments.
