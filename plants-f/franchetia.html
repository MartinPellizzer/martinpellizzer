Franchetia

Franchetia, commonly known as the flame vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Apocynaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from orange to red. The plant produces small, round berries that are toxic to humans and animals. Franchetia thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its rapid growth habit makes it suitable for trellises and as a ground cover in tropical gardens.
