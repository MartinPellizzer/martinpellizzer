Fumaria rifana

Fumaria rifana, commonly known as the common fumitory, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fumariaceae.

It is a herbaceous annual or biennial plant characterized by its slender, branched stems and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish or reddish flowers that are clustered in racemes, often appearing in late spring to early summer. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and the plant thrives in disturbed soils, often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Fumaria rifana is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
