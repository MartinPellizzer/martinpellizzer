Festuca rohlfsiana

Festuca rohlfsiana, commonly known as Rohlfs' fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant has fine, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue to purple flowers. It is often found in high-altitude meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Festuca rohlfsiana is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in difficult terrains.
