Festuca geniculata var. glabriglumis

Festuca geniculata var. glabriglumis, commonly known as the glabrous bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and compact growth habit, making it well-suited for rocky or dry environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and have a bluish-green color, with a smooth, glabrous surface.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil.
