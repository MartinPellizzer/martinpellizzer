Ficus dugandii

Ficus dugandii, commonly known as the Dugand's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are typically ovate or elliptic in shape. It produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often consumed by local wildlife and humans.

Ficus dugandii is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
