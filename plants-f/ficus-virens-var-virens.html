Ficus virens var. virens

Ficus virens var. virens, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a weeping or cascading manner. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by greenish-white fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in outdoor gardens.

Its ability to tolerate indoor conditions makes it a popular choice for interior landscaping.
