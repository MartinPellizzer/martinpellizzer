Fimbristylis depauperata var. chillagoensis

Fimbristylis depauperata var. chillagoensis, commonly known as the Chillago sedge, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wet or seasonally wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in areas with poor soil conditions.

This variety is part of the Cyperaceae family, which includes many other sedges and grass-like plants.
