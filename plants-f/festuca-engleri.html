Festuca engleri

Festuca engleri, commonly known as Engler's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant has fine, tufted leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Festuca engleri is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
