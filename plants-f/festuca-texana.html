Festuca texana

Festuca texana, commonly known as Texas fescue, is a perennial grass native to the southern United States.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and prairies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and adapted to full sun conditions.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
