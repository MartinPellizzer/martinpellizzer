Ficus nigrotuberculata

Ficus nigrotuberculata, commonly known as the black tubercled fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves with prominent tubercles, which are small, raised bumps on the leaf surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible figs that are typically small and round.

Ficus nigrotuberculata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
