Freycinetia amboinensis

Freycinetia amboinensis, commonly known as the Amboina palm, is a tropical plant native to the Maluku Islands of Indonesia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in a panicle inflorescence, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that are edible. Freycinetia amboinensis thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found growing near the shoreline.
