Ficus carica var. heleneae

Ficus carica var. heleneae, commonly known as the Helenae fig, is a cultivar of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and high productivity, making it suitable for both ornamental and edible purposes. The leaves are typically broad and lobed, with a glossy green surface. The fruit develops in a unique, elongated fig-like structure, which ripens to a deep purple color.

This variety is prized for its sweet flavor and is often cultivated in temperate climates.
