Fritillaria sporadum

Fritillaria sporadum, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus.

It produces striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow, often with contrasting black markings. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are toxic and should be handled with care.

Fritillaria sporadum is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
