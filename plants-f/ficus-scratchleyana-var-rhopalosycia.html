Ficus scratchleyana var. rhopalosycia

Ficus scratchleyana var. rhopalosycia, commonly known as the Rhopalosycia fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are encased in a smooth, greenish-yellow husk. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

The leaves are also known for their ability to retain moisture, making it relatively easy to care for in suitable environments.
