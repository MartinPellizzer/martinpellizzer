Froelichia juncea subsp. juncea

Froelichia juncea subsp.

juncea, commonly known as the coastal morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline.

The plant features showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in the early morning, hence its common name. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a rough texture.

It is well adapted to salt spray and wind, making it a hardy species in coastal environments.
