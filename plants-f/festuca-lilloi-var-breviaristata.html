Festuca lilloi var. breviaristata

Festuca lilloi var. breviaristata, commonly known as the short-awned meadow fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often hairless stems that can reach heights of up to 50 centimeters. The leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with the inflorescence consisting of a loose to dense cluster of spikelets. It produces small, two-rowed spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color.

This variety is adapted to high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.
