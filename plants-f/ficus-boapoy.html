Ficus boapoy

Ficus boapoy, commonly known as the Bornean fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and smooth, gray bark. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature, providing food for various wildlife species. Ficus boapoy thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ecological importance lies in its role as a keystone species supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
