Ficus asperrima

Ficus asperrima, commonly known as the rough fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, clustered fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ficus asperrima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can be grown as a houseplant or in gardens.
