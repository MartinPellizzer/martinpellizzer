Ficus carica var. linneana

Ficus carica var. linneana, commonly known as the common fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and the production of edible fig fruits. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a shallow root system.

The fruit develops from an inverted flower cluster, known as a syconium, and is typically harvested when it is fully ripe and soft.
