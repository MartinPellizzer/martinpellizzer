Ficus cinerascens

Ficus cinerascens, commonly known as the ash-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The leaves are opposite, simple, and have a distinctive ash-gray coloration on the underside, which gives the plant its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by fig-like fruits that are edible for certain animals.

The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
