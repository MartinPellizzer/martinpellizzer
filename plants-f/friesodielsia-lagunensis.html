Friesodielsia lagunensis

Friesodielsia lagunensis, commonly known as the Lake Island fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds that give it a graceful appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of its fronds, a common feature in ferns.
