Festuca rubra var. longiseta

Festuca rubra var. longiseta, commonly known as red fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green foliage that turns reddish in cooler temperatures. The plant produces dense, cylindrical spikelets that are often tinged with red, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly used in turfgrass and landscaping due to its durability and aesthetic qualities.

Festuca rubra var. longiseta is also valued for its ability to withstand drought and moderate foot traffic.
