Fimbristylis hirta var. canina

Fimbristylis hirta var. canina, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps and seasonally flooded areas. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, with small, inconspicuous flowers.

The species is noted for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor soils and its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland environments.
