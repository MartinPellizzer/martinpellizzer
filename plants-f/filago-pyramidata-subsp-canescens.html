Filago pyramidata subsp. canescens

Filago pyramidata subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the hairy filago, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in open grasslands and rocky outcrops, thriving in well-drained soils.

The plant features linear, hairy leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in spring. Its subspecies name, *canescens*, refers to the grayish or whitish hairs that cover its stems and leaves.

Botanically, it is characterized by its simple, alternate foliage and its ability to form dense colonies in suitable habitats.
