Felicia josephinae

Felicia josephinae, commonly known as the Josephina's fleur-de-lis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with a central disk surrounded by ray florets, resembling a fleur-de-lis pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Felicia josephinae is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
