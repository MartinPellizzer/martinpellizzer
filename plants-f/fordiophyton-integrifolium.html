Fordiophyton integrifolium

Fordiophyton integrifolium, commonly known as the smooth-leafed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color. The leaves are entire, meaning they have no teeth or lobes, and are arranged alternately along the stem. This species thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
