Ficus simplicissima var. annamica

Ficus simplicissima var. annamica, commonly known as the Annam fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.

Its growth habit includes both upright and trailing forms, making it suitable for various horticultural applications.
