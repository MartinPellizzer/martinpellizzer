Festuca megastachys

Festuca megastachys, commonly known as giant bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and its striking blue-green foliage. The plant produces large, spike-like inflorescences that bear showy, blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. Festuca megastachys is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh conditions.
