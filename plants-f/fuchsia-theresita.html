Fuchsia theresita

Fuchsia theresita, commonly known as the velvet fuchsia, is a species native to South America, particularly Ecuador.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces striking, pendulous flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in a soft, velvety coating. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
