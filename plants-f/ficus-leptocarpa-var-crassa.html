Ficus leptocarpa var. crassa

Ficus leptocarpa var. crassa, commonly known as the thick-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and air-purifying qualities.
