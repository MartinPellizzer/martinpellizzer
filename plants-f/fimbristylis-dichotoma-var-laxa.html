Fimbristylis dichotoma var. laxa

Fimbristylis dichotoma var. laxa, commonly known as the loose-haired sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Australia.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a dense cluster of small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant produces tiny, white or pale green flowers that are typically arranged in two rows along the central axis of the spike. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
