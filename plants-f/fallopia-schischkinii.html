Fallopia schischkinii

Fallopia schischkinii, commonly known as the Siberian knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces underground rhizomes that allow it to spread rapidly. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, riverbanks, and disturbed areas.

Due to its aggressive growth, it can become invasive in non-native regions.
