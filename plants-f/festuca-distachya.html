Festuca distachya

Festuca distachya, commonly known as silver grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, silver-blue foliage and panicle-like inflorescence that turns golden in autumn. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and prefers moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

Festuca distachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and as a wildlife habitat.
