Ficus esquirolii

Ficus esquirolii, commonly known as the Esquiroli fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, broad leaves and produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

The Esquiroli fig is also valued for its ability to thrive in indoor environments with proper care.
