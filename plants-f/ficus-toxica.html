Ficus toxica

Ficus toxica, commonly known as the toxic fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered fig fruits. Ficus toxica is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its lush foliage.

However, its fruit is toxic to humans and animals, requiring caution in handling and consumption.
