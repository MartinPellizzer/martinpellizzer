Festuca heterophylla subsp. braun-blanquetii

Festuca heterophylla subsp.

braun-blanquetii, commonly known as the Braun-Blanquet fescue, is a perennial grass native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous pale green to brownish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
