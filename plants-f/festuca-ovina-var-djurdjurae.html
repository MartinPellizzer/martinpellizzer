Festuca ovina var. djurdjurae

Festuca ovina var. djurdjurae, commonly known as the Djurdjura sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of the Caucasus.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The plant typically forms compact clumps with slender, wiry stems that are often covered in short, silky hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to rocky or alpine soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience in harsh mountainous habitats.
