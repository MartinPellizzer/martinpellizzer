Ficus gossypina

Ficus gossypina, commonly known as the Indian rubber plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fruit that is a small, round, greenish-yellow berry.

Ficus gossypina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions.
