Ficus blepharosepala

Ficus blepharosepala, commonly known as the veined fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, veined leaves that are often heart-shaped or oval in form. It produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when mature, turning reddish or yellowish as they ripen.

Ficus blepharosepala is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
