Forrestia rostrata var. zollingeri

Forrestia rostrata var. zollingeri, commonly known as the Zollinger's forestia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the dry season. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a specimen plant in tropical landscapes.
