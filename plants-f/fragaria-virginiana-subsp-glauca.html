Fragaria virginiana subsp. glauca

Fragaria virginiana subsp.

glauca, commonly known as the blueberry strawberry, is a wild strawberry species native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its small, glaucous (bluish-gray) leaves and fragrant, pinkish-white flowers.

The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellowish or reddish in color when mature. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine and cuisine.
