Fagerlindia scandens

Fagerlindia scandens, commonly known as the climbing fagerlindia, is a deciduous shrub native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems that allow it to climb and spread across supports. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Fagerlindia scandens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
