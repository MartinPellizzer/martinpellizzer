Fagraea scholaris

Fagraea scholaris, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vivid display during the flowering season. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and can grow up to 10 meters in height under optimal conditions. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
