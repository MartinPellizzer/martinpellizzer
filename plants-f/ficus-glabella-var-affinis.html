Ficus glabella var. affinis

Ficus glabella var. affinis, commonly known as the smooth-leaved fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that are typically smooth and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called syconia. It is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage.

The species is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
