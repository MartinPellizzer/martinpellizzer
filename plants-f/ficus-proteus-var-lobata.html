Ficus proteus var. lobata

Ficus proteus var. lobata, commonly known as the African rubber tree, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are often lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by round, greenish fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
