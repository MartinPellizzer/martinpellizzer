Ficus crassiramea var. clementis

Ficus crassiramea var. clementis, commonly known as the Clementine Fig or Ficus Clementis, is a variant of the Ficus crassiramea species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are edible and have a sweet, mild flavor. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
