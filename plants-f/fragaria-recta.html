Fragaria recta

Fragaria recta, commonly known as the European strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits composed of multiple achenes, which are often referred to as seeds. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it blooms in early spring.

Fragaria recta is valued for its edible fruits, which have a tart flavor and are used in culinary applications.
