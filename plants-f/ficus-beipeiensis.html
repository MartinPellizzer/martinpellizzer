Ficus beipeiensis

Ficus beipeiensis, commonly known as the Guangdong fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to southern China, particularly Guangdong province, and is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves and small, clustered fruit. The plant is evergreen, with a dense canopy that provides shade, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens. Its fruit, which is edible, is often consumed by local wildlife and can also be used in traditional medicine.

The plant's adaptability to various soil types and climate conditions contributes to its popularity in horticulture.
