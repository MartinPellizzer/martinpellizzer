Ferula caucasica

Ferula caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian ferula, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stem and large, umbrella-shaped inflorescence. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by elongated, hairy fruits. Its roots are thick and fibrous, containing a resinous sap that has been used in traditional medicine.

Ferula caucasica is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its aromatic properties.
