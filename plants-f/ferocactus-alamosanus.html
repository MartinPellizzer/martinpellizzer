Ferocactus alamosanus

Ferocactus alamosanus, commonly known as the Alamosa barrel cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert in the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a columnar cactus with a thick, ribbed stem that can grow up to 6 feet tall. The plant produces yellow flowers in late spring, which are followed by cylindrical fruits containing seeds. It has a deep root system that allows it to survive in arid conditions with minimal water.

The cactus is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
