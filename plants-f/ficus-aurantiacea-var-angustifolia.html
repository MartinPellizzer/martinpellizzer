Ficus aurantiacea var. angustifolia

Ficus aurantiacea var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, pale green figs that are not typically consumed by humans but are important for wildlife. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus aurantiacea var. angustifolia is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor conditions.
