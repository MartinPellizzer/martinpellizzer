Ficus crassa

Ficus crassa, commonly known as the African rubber fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish fruits that turn red when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in indoor environments.

Ficus crassa is also known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its adaptability to various soil types.
