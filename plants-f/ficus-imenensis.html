Ficus imenensis

Ficus imenensis, commonly known as the Imen's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate in form. It produces small, clustered fruits known as figs, which are enclosed within a fleshy, greenish husk.

Ficus imenensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
