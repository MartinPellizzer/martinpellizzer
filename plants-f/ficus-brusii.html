Ficus brusii

Ficus brusii, commonly known as the African fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fig-like fruits that are typically pale yellow or green. Ficus brusii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.

It requires bright, indirect light and moderate watering to maintain its health and appearance.
