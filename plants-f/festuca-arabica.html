Festuca arabica

Festuca arabica, commonly known as Arabica fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in open, sunny areas and is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Festuca arabica is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.

It is also valued in ecological restoration projects for its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
