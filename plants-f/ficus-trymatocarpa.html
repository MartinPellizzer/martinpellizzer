Ficus trymatocarpa

Ficus trymatocarpa, commonly known as the small-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. Ficus trymatocarpa produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round figs that are edible.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
