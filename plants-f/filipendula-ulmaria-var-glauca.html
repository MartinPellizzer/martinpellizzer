Filipendula ulmaria var. glauca

Filipendula ulmaria var. glauca, commonly known as meadow sweet, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of white to pinkish flowers. The plant features large, compound leaves with serrated edges, and its flowers are often covered in a fine, powdery bloom. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and wetlands.

The plant is also noted for its aromatic leaves and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
