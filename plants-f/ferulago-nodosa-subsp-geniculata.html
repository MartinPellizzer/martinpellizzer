Ferulago nodosa subsp. geniculata

Ferulago nodosa subsp.

geniculata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed ferulago, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe. It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, alternate leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits. It is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Apiaceae family.
