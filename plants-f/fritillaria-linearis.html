Fritillaria linearis

Fritillaria linearis, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a tall, slender stem that supports its showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Fritillaria linearis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
