Flueggea leucopyrus

Flueggea leucopyrus, commonly known as the white-flowered flueggea, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its succulent, often spiny stems and small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, aiding its survival in dry environments. It is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging conditions.
