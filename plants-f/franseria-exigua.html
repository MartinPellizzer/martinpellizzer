Franseria exigua

Franseria exigua, commonly known as the narrow-leaved franseria, is a small, herbaceous plant native to South Africa.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, and bloom during the spring and summer months.

Franseria exigua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and modest flowering.
