Ficus storckii var. storckii

Ficus storckii var. storckii, commonly known as the Storck's fig, is a species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are enclosed in a green, fleshy syconium. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Ficus storckii var. storckii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
