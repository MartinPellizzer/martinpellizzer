Fagraea havilandii

Fagraea havilandii, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its propagation.

Fagraea havilandii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks due to its showy flowers and adaptability to various soil types.
