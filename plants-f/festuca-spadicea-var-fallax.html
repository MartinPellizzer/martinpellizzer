Festuca spadicea var. fallax

Festuca spadicea var. fallax, commonly known as the fallax fescue, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, dark green foliage. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
