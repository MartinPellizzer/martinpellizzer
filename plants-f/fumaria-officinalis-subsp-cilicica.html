Fumaria officinalis subsp. cilicica

Fumaria officinalis subsp.

cilicica, commonly known as the Cilician gloryflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and northern Syria. It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous minute seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
