Festuca rubra subsp. megastachya

Festuca rubra subsp.

megastachya, commonly known as red fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, dark green foliage.

The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, reddish-brown flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, woodland edges, and rocky areas. Festuca rubra subsp.

megastachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
