Ficus microcarpa var. naumannii

Ficus microcarpa var. naumannii, commonly known as the Indian rubber fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in figs, which are enclosed within a fleshy, greenish-waxy structure. It is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

The plant is also known for its adaptability and resilience, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
