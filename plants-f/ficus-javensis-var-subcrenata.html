Ficus javensis var. subcrenata

Ficus javensis var. subcrenata, commonly known as the Burmese fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly wavy margin. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening in tropical and subtropical regions.
