Fritillaria taipaiensis

Fritillaria taipaiensis, commonly known as the Taipai fritillary, is a bulbous plant native to China, specifically the Yunnan province.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous areas, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine, valued for their purported health benefits.

Fritillaria taipaiensis is also appreciated in horticulture for its ornamental value and unique floral structure.
