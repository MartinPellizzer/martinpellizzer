Fagraea aurantiodora

Fagraea aurantiodora, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The tree has a dense, rounded canopy with glossy, dark green leaves that provide shade. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release small, red seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and as a street tree in tropical regions.
