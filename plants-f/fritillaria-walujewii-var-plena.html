Fritillaria walujewii var. plena

Fritillaria walujewii var. plena, commonly known as the double-flowered fritillary, is a striking bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered flowers that are typically a deep, rich purple, with a distinctive, often recurved shape. The plant produces large, showy blooms in late spring to early summer, and its foliage consists of broad, strap-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in colder climates.
