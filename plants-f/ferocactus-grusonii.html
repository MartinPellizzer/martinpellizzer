Ferocactus grusonii

Ferocactus grusonii, commonly known as the golden barrel cactus, is a species native to northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stem that is typically covered in a dense layer of white, fuzzy hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Its spines are short and often covered with a whitish bloom, which helps to reduce water loss.

Ferocactus grusonii is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
