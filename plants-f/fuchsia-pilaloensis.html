Fuchsia pilaloensis

Fuchsia pilaloensis, commonly known as the Pilalo fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Fuchsia pilaloensis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
