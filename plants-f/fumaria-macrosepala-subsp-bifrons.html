Fumaria macrosepala subsp. bifrons

Fumaria macrosepala subsp.

bifrons, commonly known as the twin-sepal fumaria, is a herbaceous annual plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers.

The subspecies bifrons is distinguished by its unique two-sepaled flowers, which set it apart from other members of the species. It thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats, often found in meadows and along roadsides.

The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, facilitating its spread in suitable environments.
