Ficus filicauda var. longipes

Ficus filicauda var. longipes, commonly known as the long-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, trailing leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant has a robust root system that helps it thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, with a distinctive waxy texture.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
