Florestina platyphylla

Florestina platyphylla, commonly known as the broadleaf forest plant, is a shrub native to the tropical rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its woody structure allows it to thrive in shaded understory environments, where it plays a vital role in the forest ecosystem.

Florestina platyphylla is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
