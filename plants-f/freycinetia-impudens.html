Freycinetia impudens

Freycinetia impudens, commonly known as the sacred palm, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces striking orange or red inflorescences, which are often used in traditional ceremonies. Its fruit is a fibrous, spiky capsule that contains seeds used in various cultural practices.

Freycinetia impudens plays a significant role in the ecological and cultural landscapes of its native regions.
