Ficus bojeri

Ficus bojeri, commonly known as the Bojer's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It produces small, greenish flowers that are followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus bojeri is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
