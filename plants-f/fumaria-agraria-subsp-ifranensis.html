Fumaria agraria subsp. ifranensis

Fumaria agraria subsp.

ifranensis, commonly known as the Moroccan yellowweed, is a flowering plant native to North Africa. It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid environments. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique adaptations to harsh environmental conditions.
