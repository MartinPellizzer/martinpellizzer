Ferocactus alamosanus subsp. reppenhagenii

Ferocactus alamosanus subsp.

reppenhagenii, commonly known as the Reppenhagen's barrel cactus, is a succulent cactus native to the Sonoran Desert. It is characterized by its cylindrical stem covered in prominent, radial spines and a dense coating of white, woolly hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by red, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun exposure.

This subspecies is known for its slow growth and ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems.
