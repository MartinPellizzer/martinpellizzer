Festuca ovina var. embergeri

Festuca ovina var. embergeri, commonly known as Emberger's sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers.

Emberger's sheep's fescue is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
