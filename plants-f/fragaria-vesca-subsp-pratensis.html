Fragaria vesca subsp. pratensis

Fragaria vesca subsp.

pratensis, commonly known as the woodland strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries.

The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as woodlands and meadows. Its roots are fibrous, and it spreads through rhizomes, forming dense mats.

The berries are edible, with a tart flavor, and are often used in culinary applications.
