Ficus virens var. sublanceolata

Ficus virens var. sublanceolata, commonly known as the Variegated Ficus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with striking white and yellow variegation patterns. The plant has a thick, fibrous root system that helps it thrive in humid, tropical environments. It produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by small, round fruits.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its adaptability and attractive foliage.
