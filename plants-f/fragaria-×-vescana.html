Fragaria × vescana

Fragaria × vescana, commonly known as the wild strawberry, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant fruits that are typically red, though they can also be yellow or white. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces white flowers with five petals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas and along roadsides.

The berries are edible, though they are generally smaller and less sweet than cultivated strawberries.
