Festuca pratensis var. hocquettei

Festuca pratensis var. hocquettei, commonly known as Hocquette's meadow fescue, is a cool-season grass native to Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This variety is often used in turfgrass mixes due to its fine texture and good drought tolerance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adaptable to a range of environmental conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
