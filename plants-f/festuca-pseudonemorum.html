Festuca pseudonemorum

Festuca pseudonemorum, commonly known as the slender fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its dense growth habit and ability to form mats.

Festuca pseudonemorum is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and soil-stabilizing properties.
