Festuca obtusa var. sprengeliana

Festuca obtusa var. sprengeliana, commonly known as Sprengel's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous narrow, awned florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscaping.
