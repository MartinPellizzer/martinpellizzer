Festuca picta

Festuca picta, commonly known as painted feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, blue-green foliage and distinctive, fluffy seed heads that give it a feathery appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish tint that becomes more pronounced in the summer months.

Festuca picta is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
