Ficus laevis var. macrocarpa

Ficus laevis var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-fruit fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are usually broad and heart-shaped. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible, round fruits that turn from green to yellow or red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
