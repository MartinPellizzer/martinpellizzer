Fragaria moupinensis

Fragaria moupinensis, commonly known as the Moupin strawberry, is a species of wild strawberry native to China.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant with trifoliate leaves and produces small, red berries that are edible. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to cooler climates. Its flowers are white with five petals, and it is closely related to other members of the Fragaria genus.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
