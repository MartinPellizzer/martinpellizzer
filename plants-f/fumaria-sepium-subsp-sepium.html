Fumaria sepium subsp. sepium

Fumaria sepium subsp.

sepium, commonly known as the common glory-of-the-snow, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stem is erect and branched.

The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
