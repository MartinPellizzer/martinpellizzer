Ficus cuatrecasasiana

Ficus cuatrecasasiana, commonly known as the Cuatrecasas' fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
