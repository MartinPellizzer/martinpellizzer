Ficus neriifolia var. fieldingii

Ficus neriifolia var. fieldingii, commonly known as the Fielding's fig, is a variant of the common fig tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are edible and have a sweet, nutty flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

This variety is relatively low maintenance, making it suitable for indoor cultivation in suitable conditions.
