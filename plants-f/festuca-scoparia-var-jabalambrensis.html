Festuca scoparia var. jabalambrensis

Festuca scoparia var. jabalambrensis, commonly known as the Jabalambra fescue, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. This variety thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It plays a role in stabilizing soils in its native habitat and is often found in alpine meadows.

The plant is relatively drought-tolerant and has a moderate growth rate.
