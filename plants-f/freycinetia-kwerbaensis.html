Freycinetia kwerbaensis

Freycinetia kwerbaensis, commonly known as the Kwerbaensis palm, is a species native to the island of New Guinea.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of many palm species. Its fruits are drupes that mature to a reddish color, containing a single seed.

Freycinetia kwerbaensis is adapted to humid tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
