Fleischmannia matogrossensis

Fleischmannia matogrossensis, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades and tomatoes. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the evening. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red berry that contains several seeds.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not suitable for human consumption but is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
