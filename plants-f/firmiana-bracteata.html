Firmiana bracteata

Firmiana bracteata, commonly known as the Chinese parasol tree, is a deciduous tree native to China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The tree produces showy, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in summer, followed by distinctive, woody, umbrella-shaped fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
