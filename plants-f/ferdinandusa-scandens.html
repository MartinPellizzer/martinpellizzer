Ferdinandusa scandens

Ferdinandusa scandens, commonly known as the climbing hibiscus, is a tropical shrub or vine native to Central and South America.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and red, often with a contrasting throat. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ferdinandusa scandens is relatively easy to grow in well-drained soil and partial to full sun.
