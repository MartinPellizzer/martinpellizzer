Fumaria bastardii var. vagans

Fumaria bastardii var. vagans, commonly known as the yellow weed or lesser celandine, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its bright yellow, spurred flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, branching stems that often lie close to the ground, and its leaves are alternate, with a distinctive heart-shaped base. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.

The species is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas, making it both a common weed and a notable early spring flora.
