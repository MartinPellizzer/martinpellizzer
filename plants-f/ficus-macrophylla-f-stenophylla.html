Ficus macrophylla f. stenophylla

Ficus macrophylla f. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a cultivar of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy texture. The plant is native to Australia and thrives in warm, humid climates. It produces small, inedible fruits that are often used for ornamental purposes.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
