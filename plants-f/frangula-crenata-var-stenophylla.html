Frangula crenata var. stenophylla

Frangula crenata var. stenophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf boxthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves with a leathery texture and a distinctive grayish-green color. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, rounded fruits that are typically reddish or purplish in color. It is adapted to thrive in poor soils and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy plant in harsh environments.

The species is also known for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dyes.
