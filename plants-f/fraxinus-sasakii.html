Fraxinus sasakii

Fraxinus sasakii, commonly known as the Japanese ash, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in moist, shaded environments in its native habitat.
