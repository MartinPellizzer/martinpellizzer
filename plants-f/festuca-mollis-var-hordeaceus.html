Festuca mollis var. hordeaceus

Festuca mollis var. hordeaceus, commonly known as creeping fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and fine, soft foliage that makes it a popular choice for lawns and ornamental landscaping. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, exhibiting good drought tolerance once established.

Its versatility and aesthetic appeal make it a valued plant in both horticultural and ecological contexts.
