Flundula comosa

Flundula comosa, commonly known as the hairy flundula, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticulture and ecological studies.
