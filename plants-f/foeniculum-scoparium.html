Foeniculum scoparium

Foeniculum scoparium, commonly known as wild fennel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features feathery, finely divided leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds are small, dry, and have a licorice-like aroma, making them valuable in both cooking and traditional medicine.

Foeniculum scoparium thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.
