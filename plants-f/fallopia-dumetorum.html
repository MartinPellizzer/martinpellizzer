Fallopia dumetorum

Fallopia dumetorum, commonly known as cow parsnip, is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is a perennial herb that can grow up to 2 meters tall, with a stout, ridged stem that is often hairy. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves that are deeply lobed and covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small and clustered in cylindrical inflorescences, and it produces white to pale yellow berries.

It is native to Europe, Asia, and North America, and is often found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and along streams.
