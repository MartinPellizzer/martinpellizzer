Festuca rubra var. tenuifolia

Festuca rubra var. tenuifolia, commonly known as slender red fescue, is a cool-season grass that thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact growth habit, making it suitable for lawns and ground cover. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to reddish flowers. It is native to Europe and parts of Asia and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

Festuca rubra var. tenuifolia is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to form dense, low-maintenance turf.
