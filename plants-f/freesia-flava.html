Freesia flava

Freesia flava, commonly known as the yellow freesia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllis family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers. The flowers typically bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and white, with a distinctive, sweet scent. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Freesia flava is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as cut flowers.
