Fraxinus mixta

Fraxinus mixta, commonly known as the mixed ash, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and typically have 5 to 9 leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by samaras as its fruit. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often developing shallow fissures with age.

Fraxinus mixta is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
