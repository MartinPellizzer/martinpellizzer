Freycinetia wamenaensis

It belongs to the family Cycadaceae and is characterized by its stout, unbranched stem and pinnate leaves. The plant is adapted to high-altitude environments, often growing in rocky or well-drained soils. Its reproductive structures include a cone-like inflorescence that produces both male and female flowers.
