Ficus pubipetiola

Ficus pubipetiola, commonly known as the pubescent fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits.

Ficus pubipetiola is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
