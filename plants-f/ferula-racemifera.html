Ferula racemifera

Ferula racemifera, commonly known as the Persian cow's milk, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a milky latex when cut, which is a distinctive botanical feature. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Ferula racemifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in herbal medicine.
