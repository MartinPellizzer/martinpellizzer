Fumaria schrammii var. pugsleyana

Fumaria schrammii var. pugsleyana, commonly known as the Pugsley's glory of the snow, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Fumariaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, nodding flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, while its stems are slender and branching.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming appearance.
