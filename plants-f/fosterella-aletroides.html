Fosterella aletroides

Fosterella aletroides, commonly known as the pink dwarf pineapple, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Bromeliaceae family and is characterized by its rosette of narrow, waxy, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with pink or red. The plant produces small, fragrant, white flowers that are followed by edible, sweet, and tangy berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for container gardening.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
