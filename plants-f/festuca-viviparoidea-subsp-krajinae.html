Festuca viviparoidea subsp. krajinae

Festuca viviparoidea subsp.

krajinae, commonly known as the Krajina vernal grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green leaves.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and specialized plant species.
