Ficus elliptifolia

Ficus elliptifolia, commonly known as the elliptic fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elliptical, glossy leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Ficus elliptifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
