Fritillaria garelinii f. gibbosa

Fritillaria garelinii f. gibbosa, commonly known as the wrinkled bellflower, is a botanical variant of the Fritillaria genus, characterized by its unique, wrinkled bulb and distinctive floral morphology.

This plant is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in rocky and well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically bell-shaped, with a striking coloration ranging from yellow to orange, often with contrasting patterns. The plant is noted for its compact growth habit and resilience to harsh environmental conditions.

It is primarily valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
