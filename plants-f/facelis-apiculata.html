Facelis apiculata

Facelis apiculata, commonly known as the Cape jasmine, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters. The plant produces fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and have a rough texture.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
