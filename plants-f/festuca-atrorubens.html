Festuca atrorubens

Festuca atrorubens, commonly known as purple false brome, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive purple to reddish hues on its foliage and inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, forest edges, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lipped flowers.

Festuca atrorubens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
