Ficus laxiramea

Ficus laxiramea, commonly known as the slender-armed fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its slender, drooping branches and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
