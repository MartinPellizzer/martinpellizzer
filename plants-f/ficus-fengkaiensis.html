Ficus fengkaiensis

Ficus fengkaiensis, commonly known as the Fengkai fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Guangdong Province in southern China. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green in color. It produces small, clustered figs that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
