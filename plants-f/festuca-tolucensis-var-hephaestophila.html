Festuca tolucensis var. hephaestophila

Festuca tolucensis var. hephaestophila, commonly known as the Mexican bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to central Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and bluish-green foliage. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry landscapes. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to silver flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, low-maintenance turf in suitable climates.
