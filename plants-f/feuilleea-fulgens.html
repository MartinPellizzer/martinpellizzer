Feuilleea fulgens

Feuilleea fulgens, commonly known as the Flameleaf Orchid, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, flame-like leaves that range in color from deep red to orange, which are covered in a waxy, translucent cuticle. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of yellow and white, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are not only visually appealing but also have a unique ability to absorb and retain moisture, aiding in its survival in humid environments.

Feuilleea fulgens is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking appearance and adaptability to shaded, moist conditions.
