Fumaria faurei

Fumaria faurei, commonly known as the yellow glorybower, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fumariaceae.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are clustered in dense, terminal racemes, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds.

Fumaria faurei is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its vibrant color and unique appearance.
