Flourensia dentata

Flourensia dentata, commonly known as the Mexican ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy blooms. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges, and it produces yellow to orange flowers that attract pollinators. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Flourensia dentata is valued in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
