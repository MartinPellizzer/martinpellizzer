Ficus fanshawei

Ficus fanshawei, commonly known as the Ficus retusa, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by spherical, greenish fruits. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Ficus fanshawei is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and ease of care.
