Festuca cyllenica subsp. balcanica

Festuca cyllenica subsp.

balcanica, commonly known as Balkan bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the Balkan Peninsula. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

The plant has fine, tufted leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for ecological restoration projects.
