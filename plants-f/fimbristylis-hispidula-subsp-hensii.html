Fimbristylis hispidula subsp. hensii

Fimbristylis hispidula subsp.

hensii, commonly known as Hens' sedge, is a flowering plant native to eastern Australia. It is a perennial herb with grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands, marshes, or along waterways. Its flowers are usually greenish or brownish and are pollinated by wind.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for invertebrates.
