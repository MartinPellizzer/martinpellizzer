Ferula badrakema

Ferula badrakema, commonly known as the Kashmiri ferula, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and often hollow stems. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbellate inflorescences, typical of the carrot family. Its roots are used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to rocky and alpine soils.
