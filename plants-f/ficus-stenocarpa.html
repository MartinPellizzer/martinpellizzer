Ficus stenocarpa

Ficus stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruit fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and the Andaman Islands. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
