Filix-mas patula var. chaerophylloides

Filix-mas patula var. chaerophylloides, commonly known as the chaerophylloides variety of common horsetail, is a variant of the horsetail plant characterized by its unique foliage structure.

It features a tall, cylindrical stem covered in overlapping, needle-like leaves that are typically green to brown in color. The plant produces spore-bearing cones at the tips of its stems, which are essential for its reproductive cycle. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is notable for its hardiness and ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
