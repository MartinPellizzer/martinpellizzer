Fagus cliffortioides

Fagus cliffortioides, commonly known as the Chinese ironwood, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the beech family (Fagaceae) and is characterized by its dense, hard, and heavy wood. The tree has simple, alternate, and ovate leaves that are dark green on top and pale underneath. It produces small, cup-shaped fruits called nuts, which are enclosed in a spiky husk.

This species is valued for its durability and is often used in traditional Chinese medicine and as a source of timber.
