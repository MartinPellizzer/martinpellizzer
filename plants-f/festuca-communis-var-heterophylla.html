Festuca communis var. heterophylla

Festuca communis var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable fescue, is a perennial grass species characterized by its variable leaf morphology.

It typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant exhibits a range of leaf shapes, from narrow to broad, depending on environmental conditions. It is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

Its adaptability makes it a common species in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
