Ficus madhucifolia

Ficus madhucifolia, commonly known as the Madhucifolia fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus madhucifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
