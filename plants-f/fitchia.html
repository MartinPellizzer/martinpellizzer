Fitchia

Fitchia, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a popular flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the genus Schlumbergera and is characterized by its segmented stems that resemble a cactus. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and white during the winter months. Fitchia thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

It is a low-maintenance plant that is well-suited for indoor cultivation.
