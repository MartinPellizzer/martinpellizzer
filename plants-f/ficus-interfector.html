Ficus interfector

Ficus interfector, commonly known as the deadly fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into round, green fruits. Its rapid growth and ability to thrive in various conditions make it a popular ornamental plant.

However, its toxic sap and berries can be harmful to humans and animals if ingested.
