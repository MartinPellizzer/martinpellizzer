Ficus tolimensis

Ficus tolimensis, commonly known as the Tolima fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Colombia, specifically the Andean cloud forests. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus tolimensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in shaded, humid environments.
