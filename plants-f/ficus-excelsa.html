Ficus excelsa

Ficus excelsa, commonly known as the Giant Fig Tree, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The tree produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in tropical climates and requires ample sunlight and well-draining soil.

Ficus excelsa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
