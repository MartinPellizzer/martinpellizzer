Ficus carica var. seirola

Ficus carica var. seirola, commonly known as the Seirola fig, is a cultivar of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and high fruit yield, making it suitable for both ornamental and commercial purposes. The leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a smooth texture, while the plant produces small, pale green flowers that develop into edible figs. This variety is well adapted to temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its fruit is prized for its sweet flavor and is often used in fresh consumption or processing.
