Festuca laeteviridis var. lasiorrhachis

Festuca laeteviridis var. lasiorrhachis, commonly known as the silky bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant typically forms dense, low-growing sods and is well adapted to a variety of soil types. It has narrow, grass-like leaves with a smooth, silky texture that gives it its common name.

This variety is often used in turfgrass mixes due to its durability and aesthetic appeal.
