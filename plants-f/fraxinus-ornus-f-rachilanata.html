Fraxinus ornus f. rachilanata

Fraxinus ornus f. rachilanata, commonly known as the flowering ash or sweet-ash, is a cultivar of the common ash tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, fragrant yellowish-white flowers that appear in panicles during the spring. The leaves are opposite, simple, and have a serrated margin, typical of the Fraxinus genus. This plant is often grown for its ornamental value due to its showy blooms and attractive foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.
