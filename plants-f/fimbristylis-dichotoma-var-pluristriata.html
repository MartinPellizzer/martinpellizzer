Fimbristylis dichotoma var. pluristriata

Fimbristylis dichotoma var. pluristriata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related grass-like plants. The plant is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a dense, tufted cluster. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-ranked spikelets, which are typically borne on short pedicels.

The variety pluristriata is distinguished by its finely striated leaf surfaces, giving it a distinctive appearance.
