Ficus ruficeps

Ficus ruficeps, commonly known as the red-headed fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and a distinctive reddish stem, which gives it its common name. Ficus ruficeps typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

It produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and attract various wildlife, contributing to its ecological importance.
