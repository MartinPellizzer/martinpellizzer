Fusticus

Fusticus, commonly known as the common fusticus, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Apiaceae.

It is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in the summer months. The plant is native to various regions in Europe and Asia, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Fusticus species are often found in woodland edges and along stream banks, contributing to the biodiversity of these habitats.

Its medicinal properties have been historically recognized, with uses in traditional herbal remedies.
