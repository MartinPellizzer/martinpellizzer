Forsythia japonica var. subintegra

Forsythia japonica var. subintegra, commonly known as the Japanese forsythia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, pendant-like flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

Its rapid growth and early blooming make it a valuable plant for adding color to the spring landscape.
