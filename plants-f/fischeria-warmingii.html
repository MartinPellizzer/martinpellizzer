Fischeria warmingii

Fischeria warmingii, commonly known as the Greenland lichen, is a species of lichen that thrives in arctic and alpine environments.

It is characterized by its crust-like growth form and is typically found on rock surfaces in cold, moist climates. The lichen consists of a symbiotic relationship between a green alga and a fungus, which allows it to survive in harsh conditions. It plays a crucial role in soil formation and nutrient cycling in its ecosystem.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized organism.
