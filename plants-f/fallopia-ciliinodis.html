Fallopia ciliinodis

Fallopia ciliinodis, commonly known as the silky beringia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems that are often covered with soft, silky hairs. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have finely toothed margins.

Fallopia ciliinodis is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.
