Ficus porphyrochaete

Ficus porphyrochaete, commonly known as the red-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, China, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its striking, velvety red leaves that contrast with its glossy green undersides. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by pear-shaped fruits.

Ficus porphyrochaete is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
