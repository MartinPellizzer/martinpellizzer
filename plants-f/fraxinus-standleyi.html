Fraxinus standleyi

Fraxinus standleyi, commonly known as Standley's ash, is a species of flowering plant in the Oleaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed shrub or small tree form. The plant features opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape, with smooth margins. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, arranged in drooping clusters, and it produces small, winged fruits known as samaras.

Standley's ash is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in dry, open habitats.
