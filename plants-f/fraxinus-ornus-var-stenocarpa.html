Fraxinus ornus var. stenocarpa

Fraxinus ornus var. stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruit weeping ash, is a variant of the weeping ash tree native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches and small, yellowish-green leaves that turn reddish-brown in autumn. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by narrow, elongated fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks for its graceful appearance.
