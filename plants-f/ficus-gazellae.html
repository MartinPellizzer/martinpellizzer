Ficus gazellae

Ficus gazellae, commonly known as the Gazelle fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forested areas.

Ficus gazellae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
