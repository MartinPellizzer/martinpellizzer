Filipendula ulmaria var. denudata

Filipendula ulmaria var. denudata, commonly known as white sweet clover, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, fluffy white flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and streambanks. It is a member of the legume family, capable of nitrogen fixation through symbiotic bacteria in its root nodules.

Its delicate appearance and historical use in herbal medicine make it a notable species in botanical studies.
