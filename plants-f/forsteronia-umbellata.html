Forsteronia umbellata

Forsteronia umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella tree, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, umbrella-like inflorescence composed of numerous white or pinkish flowers arranged in a dense cluster. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a rough texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance.

Forsteronia umbellata is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
