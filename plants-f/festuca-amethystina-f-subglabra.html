Festuca amethystina f. subglabra

Festuca amethystina f. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth velvet grass, is a variant of the velvet grass species characterized by its reduced pubescence.

This plant is a cool-season grass that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive bluish-green coloration. The inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, violet-blue flowers.

Botanically, it is noted for its horticultural value as a ground cover and its adaptability to various soil types.
