Ficus volubilis

Ficus volubilis, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and ability to climb using aerial roots. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs.

Ficus volubilis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
