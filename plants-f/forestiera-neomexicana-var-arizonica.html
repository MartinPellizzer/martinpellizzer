Forestiera neomexicana var. arizonica

Forestiera neomexicana var. arizonica, commonly known as Arizona forestiera, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It typically grows in rocky soils and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant features small, elliptical leaves and clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. Its woody stems are often branched and covered in fine, grayish hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
