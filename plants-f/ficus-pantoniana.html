Ficus pantoniana

Ficus pantoniana, commonly known as the Panton's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor.

Ficus pantoniana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor environments.
