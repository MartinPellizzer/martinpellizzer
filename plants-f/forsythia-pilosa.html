Forsythia pilosa

Forsythia pilosa, commonly known as the hairy forsythia, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, pendulous flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically oval-shaped with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Forsythia pilosa is also valued for its ability to tolerate urban conditions and its role in attracting pollinators.
