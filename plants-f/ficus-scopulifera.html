Ficus scopulifera

Ficus scopulifera, commonly known as the rock fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its robust, woody stems and thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy coating. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are typically clustered in groups.

Ficus scopulifera is well adapted to grow in rocky or coastal environments, making it a hardy and resilient species.
