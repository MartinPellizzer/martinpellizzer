Fritillaria crassifolia subsp. poluninii

Fritillaria crassifolia subsp.

poluninii, commonly known as the Caucasian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region. It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color, often with intricate patterns.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in rocky or mountainous environments. It blooms in early spring, adding vibrant color to its alpine habitat.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a rare and protected species in its native range.
