Frankenia canescens

Frankenia canescens, commonly known as coastal bluemink, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish coating. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to salt spray and wind exposure.

Frankenia canescens plays a role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various insects and small animals.
