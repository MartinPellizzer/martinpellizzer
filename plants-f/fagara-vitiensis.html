Fagara vitiensis

Fagara vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis Zamioculcas, is a member of the Araliaceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Uganda and the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by berries containing seeds.

Fagara vitiensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
