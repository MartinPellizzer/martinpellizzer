Freycinetia tidorensis

Freycinetia tidorensis, commonly known as Tidore palm, is a species of palm native to the Maluku Islands in Indonesia.

It is a medium-sized tree with a slender trunk and pinnate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by clustered, black, egg-shaped fruits. Freycinetia tidorensis is adapted to tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soil with ample sunlight.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
