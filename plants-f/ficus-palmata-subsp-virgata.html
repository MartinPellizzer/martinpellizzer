Ficus palmata subsp. virgata

Ficus palmata subsp.

virgata, commonly known as the Virgin Islands fig, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Its rapid growth makes it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
