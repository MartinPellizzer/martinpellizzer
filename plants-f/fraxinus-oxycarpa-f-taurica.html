Fraxinus oxycarpa f. taurica

Fraxinus oxycarpa f. taurica, commonly known as the Tauric ash, is a variant of the ash tree native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its distinctive ovate leaves that are often slightly serrated along the edges. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in pendulous clusters, which are followed by winged fruits. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in local ecosystems.
