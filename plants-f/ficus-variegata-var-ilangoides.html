Ficus variegata var. ilangoides

Ficus variegata var. ilangoides, commonly known as the Ilang-iling or Ilang-iling plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its striking variegated leaves with white and green patterns. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape and have a glossy texture.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.
