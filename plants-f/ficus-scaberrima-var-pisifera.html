Ficus scaberrima var. pisifera

Ficus scaberrima var. pisifera, commonly known as the African fig or wild fig, is a species native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring a rough, fissured bark that becomes more pronounced with age. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a distinctive waxy texture and a serrated margin. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
