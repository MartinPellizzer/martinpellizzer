Freesia verrucosa

Freesia verrucosa, commonly known as the orange freesia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers. The flowers typically come in shades of yellow, orange, and white, with a distinctive crested or wavy petal structure. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

The plant is also valued for its long-lasting blooms and ability to grow in a variety of climates.
