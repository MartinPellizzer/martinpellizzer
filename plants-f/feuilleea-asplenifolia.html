Feuilleea asplenifolia

Feuilleea asplenifolia, commonly known as the lace fern, is a striking plant with intricate, delicate foliage.

Its leaves are typically bipinnate, resembling the structure of a fern, and are often adorned with fine, thread-like veins. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens. It produces small, spore-bearing structures called sporangia, which are typically found on the underside of its fronds.

Feuilleea asplenifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural displays.
