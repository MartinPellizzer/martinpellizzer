Fritillaria roderickii

Fritillaria roderickii, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to China.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple, often with contrasting patterns. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in rocky or mountainous habitats. It blooms in early spring and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Fritillaria roderickii is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional Chinese medicine.
