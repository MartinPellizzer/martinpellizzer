Festuca ovina f. communis

Festuca ovina f. communis, commonly known as sheep's fescue, is a cool-season grass species characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit.

It typically grows in a range of habitats, including meadows, pastures, and rocky soils, and is well adapted to temperate climates. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.

Festuca ovina f. communis is often used in landscaping and as a forage crop due to its durability and aesthetic appeal.
