Frangula granulosa

Frangula granulosa, commonly known as the rough-seeded buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit and small, elliptical leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces bright red berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. Frangula granulosa thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological studies and native plant landscaping.
