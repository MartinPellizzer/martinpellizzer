Ficus wuiana

Ficus wuiana, commonly known as the Tongan fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of the South Pacific, particularly Tonga and nearby islands. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, edible figs that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal and lowland forests.

Ficus wuiana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
