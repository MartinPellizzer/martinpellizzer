Festuca calycina

Festuca calycina, commonly known as bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green blades and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and open fields. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear blue to purple flowers in the spring.

Festuca calycina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
