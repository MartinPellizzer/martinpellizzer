Festuca pilosa var. argaea

Festuca pilosa var. argaea, commonly known as the Argaeum fescue, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous slender, two-rowed spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Festuca pilosa var. argaea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and attractive foliage.
