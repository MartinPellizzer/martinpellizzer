Franseria

Franseria, commonly known as the African daisy, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

These plants are native to southern Africa and are characterized by their showy, daisy-like flowers with bright yellow centers and white or pink petals. Franseria species typically grow as shrubs or herbs, often with hairy stems and leaves. They thrive in well-drained soils and full sun, making them popular in rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

The genus includes several species, such as Franseria glabrata and Franseria simplex, which are valued for their ornamental appeal.
