Fraxinus tobana

Fraxinus tobana, commonly known as the Toba ash, is a deciduous tree native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

Fraxinus tobana is adapted to coastal environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.
