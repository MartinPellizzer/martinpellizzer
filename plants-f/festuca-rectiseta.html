Festuca rectiseta

Festuca rectiseta, commonly known as the stiff-haired fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive stiff, hairy texture along the leaf margins. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense cluster of spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

Festuca rectiseta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
