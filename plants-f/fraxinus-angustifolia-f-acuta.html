Fraxinus angustifolia f. acuta

Fraxinus angustifolia f. acuta, commonly known as the narrowleaf ash, is a cultivar of the ash tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often sharply toothed and have a distinctive dark green color. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, while its fruits are samaras that disperse via wind.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
