Festuca duriuscula var. rubra

Festuca duriuscula var. rubra, commonly known as red mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, compact foliage. The plant produces pink to reddish inflorescences, which distinguish it from other varieties of Festuca. It thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
