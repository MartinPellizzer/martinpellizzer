Freycinetia imbristigma

Freycinetia imbristigma, commonly known as the broad-leafed pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its large, strap-like leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and cooking. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are stiff and fibrous, with a distinctive waxy texture and a pleasant aroma when crushed.

Freycinetia imbristigma is well adapted to coastal and wetland environments, making it a common species in mangrove and estuarine ecosystems.
