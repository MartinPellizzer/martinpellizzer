Ficus retusa f. pubescens

Ficus retusa f. pubescens, commonly known as the velvet fig, is a cultivar of the rubber plant that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are covered in a fine layer of soft, velvety pubescence, giving it a unique texture. The leaves are typically broad, ovate, and have a deep green color with a slightly glossy surface. This plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid environments.

It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and attractive foliage.
