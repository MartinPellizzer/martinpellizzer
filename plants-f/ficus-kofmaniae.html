Ficus kofmaniae

Ficus kofmaniae, commonly known as the Kofman's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered fruit that are typically green when mature. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.

The fig tree is also notable for its ability to adapt to indoor environments, making it a popular choice for houseplants.
