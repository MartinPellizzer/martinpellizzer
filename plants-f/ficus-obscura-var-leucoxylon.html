Ficus obscura var. leucoxylon

Ficus obscura var. leucoxylon, commonly known as the black-wood fig, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, bark and its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, spherical fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
