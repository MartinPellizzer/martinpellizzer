Fleischeria geropogon

Fleischeria geropogon, commonly known as the Florida bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a rough texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Fleischeria geropogon is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
