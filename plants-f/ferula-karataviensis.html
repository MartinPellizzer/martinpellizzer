Ferula karataviensis

Ferula karataviensis, commonly known as Karatav mountain wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes plants like carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, often hairy foliage. The plant produces tall, branched stems that bear yellowish-green flowers in late summer. Its most notable feature is its long, hollow, and sometimes aromatic taproot, which is used in traditional medicine.

Ferula karataviensis thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
