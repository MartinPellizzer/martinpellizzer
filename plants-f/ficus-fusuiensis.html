Ficus fusuiensis

Ficus fusuiensis, commonly known as the Guangxi fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to China, specifically the Guangxi region, and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
