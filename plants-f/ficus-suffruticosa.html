Ficus suffruticosa

Ficus suffruticosa, commonly known as the prickly fig or carob tree, is a shrub or small tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, spiny foliage and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by woody, spherical fruits. Its roots are deep and strong, allowing it to thrive in rocky or arid soils.

It is often used in hedges or as an ornamental plant due to its hardy nature and distinctive appearance.
