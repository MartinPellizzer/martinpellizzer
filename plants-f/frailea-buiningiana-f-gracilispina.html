Frailea buiningiana f. gracilispina

Frailea buiningiana f. gracilispina, commonly known as the slender-spined frailea, is a rare and striking cactus native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features a compact, globular stem covered in short, cylindrical spines that are typically a pale yellow or brownish color. The plant produces small, pale green flowers during the warmer months, adding a delicate contrast to its spiny structure. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is often sought after by collectors and conservationists alike.
