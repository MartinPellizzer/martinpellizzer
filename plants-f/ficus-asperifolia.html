Ficus asperifolia

Ficus asperifolia, commonly known as the rough-leaved fig, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are covered in a rough, granular texture. Its fruit, known as a fig, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

Ficus asperifolia prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for both ornamental and practical cultivation.
