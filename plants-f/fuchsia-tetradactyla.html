Fuchsia tetradactyla

Fuchsia tetradactyla, commonly known as the four-fingered fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically bloom in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant is a shrub that can grow up to several meters in height, with opposite, ovate leaves. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are pollinated primarily by birds, such as hummingbirds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
