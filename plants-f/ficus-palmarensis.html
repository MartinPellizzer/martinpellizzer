Ficus palmarensis

Ficus palmarensis, commonly known as the palmate fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, palm-shaped leaves that are often lobed and have a distinctive appearance. It produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature.

Ficus palmarensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
