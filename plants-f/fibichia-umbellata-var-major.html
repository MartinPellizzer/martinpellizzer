Fibichia umbellata var. major

Fibichia umbellata var. major, commonly known as the giant fibichia, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in dense, umbellate clusters. The plant is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its foliage consists of broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
