Ficus esquiroliana

Ficus esquiroliana, commonly known as the Esquiroli's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Ficus esquiroliana is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
