Ficus jaheriana

Ficus jaheriana, commonly known as the Jaffna fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Sri Lanka, India, and parts of Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Ficus jaheriana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
