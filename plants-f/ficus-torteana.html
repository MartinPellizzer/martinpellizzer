Ficus torteana

Ficus torteana, commonly known as the Torteana fig tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features broad, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green in color. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are generally not consumed by humans.

The Torteana fig tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
