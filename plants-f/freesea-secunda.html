Freesea secunda

Freesea secunda, commonly known as the purple coneflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, spiky flower heads that range in color from purple to blue, surrounded by pale green bracts. The plant produces tubular flowers that are adapted for pollination by bees and other insects. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Freesea secunda thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
