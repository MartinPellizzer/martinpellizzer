Frailea matoana

Frailea matoana, commonly known as the Matoana Cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Brazil.

It is a low-growing, spineless plant with a compact rosette shape, characterized by its fleshy, greenish-gray stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Frailea matoana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ease of care.
