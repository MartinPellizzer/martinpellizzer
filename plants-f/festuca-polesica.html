Festuca polesica

Festuca polesica, commonly known as Polesian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, tufted stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually a deep green color. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

Festuca polesica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
