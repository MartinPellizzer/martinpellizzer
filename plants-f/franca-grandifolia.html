Franca grandifolia

Franca grandifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian fern or golden fern, is a species of flowering plant in the family Araceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, Colombia, and Venezuela. The plant is characterized by its large, showy yellow spathes that surround a central spadix, creating a striking visual display. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Franca grandifolia is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
