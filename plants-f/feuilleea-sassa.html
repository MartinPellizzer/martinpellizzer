Feuilleea sassa

Feuilleea sassa, commonly known as the silver leaf fern, is a striking plant native to the humid tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, arching fronds that are covered in a fine layer of silvery-white hairs, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found growing on tree trunks or rocky surfaces. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the undersides of its leaves and produce spores for propagation.

Feuilleea sassa is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its natural habitat.
