Ficus oxystipula

Ficus oxystipula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like India, Thailand, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape with a smooth margin. It produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in traditional medicine.

Ficus oxystipula thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
