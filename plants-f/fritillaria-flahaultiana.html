Fritillaria flahaultiana

Fritillaria flahaultiana, commonly known as the Flahault's fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Europe.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically deep red or orange with contrasting black veins, making it a striking ornamental plant. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winter conditions, and it blooms in early spring.

Fritillaria flahaultiana is valued for its unique floral structure and is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental beauty.
