Fraxinus excelsior var. argentea

Fraxinus excelsior var. argentea, commonly known as the silver ash, is a variant of the common ash tree characterized by its striking silvery-white undersides on its leaves.

This ornamental tree is native to Europe and is prized for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin and a prominent central vein. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged samaras that disperse its seeds.
