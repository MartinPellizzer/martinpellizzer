Festuca ovina var. yarochenkoi

Festuca ovina var. yarochenkoi, commonly known as Yarochenkoi sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and silver-green foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically forms dense mats and is well adapted to cold, dry environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and erosion control in suitable climates.
