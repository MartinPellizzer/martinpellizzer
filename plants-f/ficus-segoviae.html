Ficus segoviae

Ficus segoviae, commonly known as the Segovia fig tree, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by fig-like fruits, which are edible and attract various wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.

Ficus segoviae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to different growing conditions.
