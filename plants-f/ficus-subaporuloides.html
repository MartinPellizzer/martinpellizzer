Ficus subaporuloides

Ficus subaporuloides, commonly known as the Variegated Ficus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with striking white or cream-colored veins, creating a distinctive variegated appearance. The plant has a dense, compact growth habit and produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish fruits. It thrives in warm, humid conditions with bright, indirect light and is relatively low maintenance.

Its ornamental foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
