Franciscea capitata

Franciscea capitata, commonly known as the silverleaf franciscea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage and clusters of small, bell-shaped white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to arid and coastal environments.
