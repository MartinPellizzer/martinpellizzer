Ficus pinkiana

Ficus pinkiana, commonly known as the Pink Princess Ficus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a pinkish tinge, especially on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by round, greenish fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ornamental appeal.

Ficus pinkiana is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
