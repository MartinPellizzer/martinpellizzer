Fuchsia radicans

Fuchsia radicans, commonly known as the trailing fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink, purple, and red. The plant produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible but typically not consumed by humans. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Fuchsia radicans thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in hanging baskets or as ground cover in gardens.
