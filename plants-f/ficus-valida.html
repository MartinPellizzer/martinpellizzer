Ficus valida

Ficus valida, commonly known as the noble fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus valida is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and air-purifying qualities.
