Ficus fulva var. chrysocarpa

Ficus fulva var. chrysocarpa, commonly known as the golden fruit fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Its fruit is also used in traditional medicine and culinary practices in its native regions.
