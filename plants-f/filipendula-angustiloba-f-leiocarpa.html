Filipendula angustiloba f. leiocarpa

Filipendula angustiloba f. leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-stemmed bergamot, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces elliptical, toothed leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

This subspecies is notable for its reduced pubescence compared to other forms of Filipendula angustiloba.
