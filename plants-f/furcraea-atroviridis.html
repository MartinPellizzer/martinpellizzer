Furcraea atroviridis

Furcraea atroviridis, commonly known as the black-veined furcraea, is a succulent plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and long, narrow leaves that are dark green with prominent black veins. The plant is adapted to arid environments and can store water in its fleshy stems and leaves. It produces yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by cylindrical, woody fruits.

Furcraea atroviridis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and drought tolerance.
