Furcraea valleculata

Furcraea valleculata, commonly known as the Guatemalan sword plant, is a succulent plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its sword-shaped, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, white flowers that bloom in summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
