Festuca borbonica

Festuca borbonica, commonly known as Sicilian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and bluish-green foliage. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous slender florets.

Festuca borbonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
