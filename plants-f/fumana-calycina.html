Fumana calycina

Fumana calycina, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular red or orange flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and its stems are often hairy as well. Fumana calycina is part of the Campanulaceae family, which includes many other bellflower species.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.
