Feijoa schenckiana

Feijoa schenckiana, commonly known as the silver feijoa, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery-white wax. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that have a unique, tropical flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit.

Feijoa schenckiana is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its drought-resistant properties.
