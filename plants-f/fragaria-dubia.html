Fragaria dubia

Fragaria dubia, commonly known as the wild strawberry or mountain strawberry, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its flowers are white with five petals, and the fruit develops from a single ovary.

Fragaria dubia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of wild berries.
