Ficus aurata var. palawanensis

Ficus aurata var. palawanensis, commonly known as the Palawan fig, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green bark and glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically yellow or red in color. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in horticulture.
