Ferula nuda

Ferula nuda, commonly known as the naked ferula, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its roots are fibrous and have been used in traditional medicine for their purported therapeutic properties.

Ferula nuda is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.
