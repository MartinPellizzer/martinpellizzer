Festuca kirghisorum

Festuca kirghisorum, commonly known as Kirghiz meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue or purple flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Festuca kirghisorum is also known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
