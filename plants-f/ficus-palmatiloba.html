Ficus palmatiloba

Ficus palmatiloba, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Ficus palmatiloba is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
