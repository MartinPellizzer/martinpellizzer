Ficus sansibarica subsp. sansibarica

Ficus sansibarica subsp.

sansibarica, commonly known as the East African fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the coastal regions of East Africa. It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth or slightly pubescent surface.

The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green to yellowish in color, enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
