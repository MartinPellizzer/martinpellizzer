Ficus tashiroi

Ficus tashiroi, commonly known as the Tashiro's fig, is a tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in lowland rainforests. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered fruit. It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes other well-known fig species.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
