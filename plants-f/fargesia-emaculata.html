Fargesia emaculata

Fargesia emaculata, commonly known as the silver lace bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant is evergreen and thrives in shaded, moist environments, making it well-suited for woodland gardens. Its growth habit is clump-forming, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring.

Fargesia emaculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to add texture and interest to shaded landscapes.
