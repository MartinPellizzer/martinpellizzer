Ficus chrysochlamys

Ficus chrysochlamys, commonly known as the golden fig or yellow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a yellow or golden hue on the underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically yellow or orange in color.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
