Fistularia goniotricha var. interfoliata

Fistularia goniotricha var. interfoliata, commonly known as the leafy sea dragon, is a marine flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, branched structure covered in leaf-like appendages that resemble a sea dragon. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often in the early morning. Its leaves are thin and elongated, arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem.

It thrives in shallow, brackish waters and is often found in association with mangroves and tidal flats.
