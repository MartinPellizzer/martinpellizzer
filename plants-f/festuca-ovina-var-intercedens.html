Festuca ovina var. intercedens

Festuca ovina var. intercedens, commonly known as the intermediate sheep fescue, is a low-growing perennial grass native to temperate regions of Europe.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. This plant is well adapted to a variety of soil types and can thrive in both full sun and partial shade. It is often used in landscaping for erosion control and as a ground cover due to its hardy nature.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological and horticultural applications.
