Ficus eucalyptoides

Ficus eucalyptoides, commonly known as the silverleaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, China, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine silvery down. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically small and clustered.

Ficus eucalyptoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
