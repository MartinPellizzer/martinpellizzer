Freycinetia scandens

Freycinetia scandens, commonly known as the climbing pandanus, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia and the Pacific.

It is characterized by its long, flexible stems that allow it to climb and trail over other vegetation. The plant produces large, paddle-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and construction. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in terminal spikes, and it produces edible fruits that are consumed by both humans and wildlife.

Freycinetia scandens is an important species in its native ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and providing resources for local communities.
