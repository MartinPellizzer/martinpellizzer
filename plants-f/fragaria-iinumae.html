Fragaria iinumae

Fragaria iinumae, commonly known as the Japanese strawberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its small, red, edible fruits that resemble those of the common strawberry. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are white with five petals and are followed by the characteristic red, fleshy berries.

Fragaria iinumae is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary use in traditional Japanese cuisine.
