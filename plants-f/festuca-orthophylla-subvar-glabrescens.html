Festuca orthophylla subvar. glabrescens

Festuca orthophylla subvar. glabrescens, commonly known as the glabrous feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender, arching stems that produce delicate, spike-like inflorescences. The leaves are typically hairless, giving the plant its subvariety name, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often found in open habitats such as meadows, rocky slopes, and disturbed areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
