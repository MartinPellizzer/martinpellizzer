Ficus variabilis f. obtusifolia

Ficus variabilis f. obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaf fig, is a cultivar of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its broad, rounded leaves that are typically a deep green with a smooth texture. The plant is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. It produces small, edible fruits that are similar to those of the common fig but are often less sweet.

This variety is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
