Fagraea pachypoda

Fagraea pachypoda, commonly known as the red sanders, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, aromatic wood and distinctive, compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its high demand in the furniture industry, it is also a species of conservation concern.
