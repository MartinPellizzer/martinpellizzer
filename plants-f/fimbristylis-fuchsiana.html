Fimbristylis fuchsiana

Fimbristylis fuchsiana, commonly known as the Fuchs' sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of sedges. It is often found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Fimbristylis fuchsiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
