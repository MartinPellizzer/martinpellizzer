Festuca idahoensis var. roemeri

Festuca idahoensis var. roemeri, commonly known as Idaho fescue, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, blue to purple flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for restoration projects.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
