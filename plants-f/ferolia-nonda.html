Ferolia nonda

Ferolia nonda, commonly known as the Natal orange, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, leathery leaves and clusters of small, yellow-orange fruits. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits. Its flowers are white with a strong fragrance, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The fruit, which is typically harvested when fully ripe, has a sweet and tangy flavor, making it a popular fruit in local markets.
