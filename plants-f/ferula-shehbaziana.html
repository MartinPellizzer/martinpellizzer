Ferula shehbaziana

Ferula shehbaziana, commonly known as the Kashmiri ferula, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and distinctive morphology. The plant features a thick, fleshy rhizome and tall, slender stems that bear compound umbels of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with finely divided, silvery-green leaflets.

Ferula shehbaziana is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory and antiseptic effects.
