Fraxinus excelsior var. kincairniae

Fraxinus excelsior var. kincairniae, commonly known as the Kincaird's ash, is a variant of the common ash tree native to parts of Europe.

It is characterized by its finely toothed leaves and distinctive, elongated winged seeds. The tree typically grows in woodland areas and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is a deciduous species, shedding its leaves in autumn, and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and parks.
