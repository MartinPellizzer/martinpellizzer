Fritillaria lanceolata var. viridis

Fritillaria lanceolata var. viridis, commonly known as the green bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves and produces striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale green to yellow. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its flowers bloom in early spring, adding a unique touch of color to early-season gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
