Frailea friedrichii

Frailea friedrichii, commonly known as the Friedrich's fairy fan, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense clusters of small, spiny stems. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically short-lived but striking in appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
