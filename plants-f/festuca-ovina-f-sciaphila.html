Festuca ovina f. sciaphila

Festuca ovina f. sciaphila, commonly known as the shade-loving bluegrass, is a cool-season grass that thrives in low-light conditions.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and dense growth habit, making it suitable for shaded garden areas. This form of Festuca ovina is typically shorter and more compact than the standard variety, with a mat-like appearance. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Due to its shade tolerance, it is a popular choice for underplanting in woodland gardens or as a ground cover in shaded landscapes.
