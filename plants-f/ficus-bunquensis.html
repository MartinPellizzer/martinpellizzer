Ficus bunquensis

Ficus bunquensis, commonly known as the Bornean fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Ficus bunquensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
