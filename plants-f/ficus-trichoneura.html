Ficus trichoneura

Ficus trichoneura, commonly known as the hairy fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are covered in fine, short hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically small and somewhat bitter.

The species is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
