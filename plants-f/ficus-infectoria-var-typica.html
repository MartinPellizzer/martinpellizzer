Ficus infectoria var. typica

Ficus infectoria var. typica, commonly known as the African fig or poison fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are typically consumed when ripe.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in various cultures for medicinal and ritual purposes.
