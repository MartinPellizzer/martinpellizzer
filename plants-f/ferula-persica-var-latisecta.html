Ferula persica var. latisecta

Ferula persica var. latisecta, commonly known as Persian cow's milk, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and compound leaves with broad, serrated lobes. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.
