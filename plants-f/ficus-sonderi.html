Ficus sonderi

Ficus sonderi, commonly known as the Sonder's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that develop into edible fig fruits. Ficus sonderi typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile plant for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
