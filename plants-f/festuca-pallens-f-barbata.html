Festuca pallens f. barbata

Festuca pallens f. barbata, commonly known as the barbed meadow grass, is a variant of the slender meadow grass species.

It is characterized by its distinctive barbed awns on the lemma, which give it its common name. This plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The botanical features of Festuca pallens f. barbata make it an important species in ecological studies and horticultural applications.
