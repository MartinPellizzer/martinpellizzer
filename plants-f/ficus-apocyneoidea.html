Ficus apocyneoidea

Ficus apocyneoidea, commonly known as the Madagascar fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Ficus apocyneoidea is also valued for its ability to purify indoor air and its adaptability to various growing conditions.
