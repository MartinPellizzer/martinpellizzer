Festuca oregona

Festuca oregona, commonly known as Oregon bentgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant has fine, tufted blades that are often less than 10 cm in height. Its leaves are usually bluish-green in color and have a slightly hairy texture.

Oregon bentgrass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in wetland areas.
