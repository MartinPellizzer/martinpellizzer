Festuca insularis

Festuca insularis, commonly known as the island fescue, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils.

Festuca insularis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
