Faba vulgaris var. circularis

Faba vulgaris var. circularis, commonly known as the circular broad bean, is a variant of the broad bean characterized by its distinctive circular leaf shape.

This plant is a member of the Fabaceae family, known for its nitrogen-fixing abilities through symbiotic relationships with Rhizobium bacteria. It typically grows in temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soil. The pods contain edible seeds that are often used in culinary applications.

Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies and horticultural research.
