Ficinia laciniata var. paradoxa

Ficinia laciniata var. paradoxa, commonly known as the Paradoxical Split-leaf Plant, is a striking member of the Araceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its deeply lobed, often fern-like leaves that can reach several feet in length. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous root system and a short, stout stem that supports its large, compound leaves.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden within a spathe, a common trait among arums.
