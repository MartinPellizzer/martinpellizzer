Falkia canescens var. longipedunculata

Falkia canescens var. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-pedunculate falkia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and typically herbaceous growth habit. The plant features slender, elongated peduncles that support its small, daisy-like flowers, which are usually pale yellow to white in color. Its leaves are linear and covered with a fine layer of white hairs, giving it a silvery appearance.

This variant is notable for its unique floral structure and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.
