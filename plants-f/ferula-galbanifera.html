Ferula galbanifera

Ferula galbanifera, commonly known as the gum plant or galbanum, is a member of the Apiaceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Europe, the Middle East, and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stems and yellow-green flowers that bloom in summer. It produces a distinctive resinous gum, which has been historically used in traditional medicine and as a fragrance in perfumery.

The leaves are pinnately lobed and often have a strong, aromatic scent.
