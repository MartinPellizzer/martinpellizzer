Fagus engleriana

Fagus engleriana, commonly known as the Engler's beech, is a species of deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the genus Fagus, which includes other beech species found in temperate regions. The tree is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and oval-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. It produces small, spiky samaras as its fruit, which are dispersed by wind.

Fagus engleriana typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in cloud forests at high elevations.
