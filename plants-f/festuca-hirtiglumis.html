Festuca hirtiglumis

Festuca hirtiglumis, commonly known as the hairy leaved fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, hair-like texture on the leaf blades, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It forms dense, low-growing clumps and is often found in meadows, along stream banks, and in woodland edges.

Festuca hirtiglumis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in natural habitats.
