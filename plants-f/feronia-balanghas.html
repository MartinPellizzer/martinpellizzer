Feronia balanghas

Feronia balanghas, commonly known as the Philippine sour orange, is a small evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its thorny branches and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are sour and often used in traditional medicine. Its flowers are white with a strong fragrance, and it thrives in tropical climates with well-drained soil.

The fruit's high acidity makes it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
