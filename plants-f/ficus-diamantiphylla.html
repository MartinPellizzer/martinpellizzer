Ficus diamantiphylla

Ficus diamantiphylla, commonly known as the diamond-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, diamond-shaped leaves that give it its common name. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus diamantiphylla prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
