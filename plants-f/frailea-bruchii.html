Frailea bruchii

Frailea bruchii, commonly known as the Bruch's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Brazil.

It belongs to the Crassulaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit. The plant features thick, fleshy leaves that are typically blue-green in color, covered in a fine layer of white wax. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink or white during the warmer months.

Frailea bruchii is well adapted to drought conditions, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
