Festuca rubra subvar. magnelii

Festuca rubra subvar. magnelii, commonly known as the Highland bentgrass, is a perennial grass native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
