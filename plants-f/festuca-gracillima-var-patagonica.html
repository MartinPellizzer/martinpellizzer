Festuca gracillima var. patagonica

Festuca gracillima var. patagonica, commonly known as Patagonian fine fescue, is a low-growing perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It is adapted to thrive in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
