Ficus crassiramea

Ficus crassiramea, commonly known as the thick-veined fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, including countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent, thick veins. It produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when mature.

Ficus crassiramea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
