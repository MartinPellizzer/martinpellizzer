Festuca × belensis var. marginata

Festuca × belensis var. marginata, commonly known as the silver margin grass, is a hybrid grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and distinctive silver-white margins on its leaf blades. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, attractive lawns.

The varietal name 'marginata' refers to the striking white edging on its leaves, which adds visual interest to gardens.
