Ficus jamini

Ficus jamini, commonly known as the Jamin's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus jamini is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
