Fagopyrum dryandrii

Fagopyrum dryandrii, commonly known as the common buckthorn, is a shrub native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, alternate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by elongated, ribbed berries that turn dark purple when mature. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained conditions.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine and as a source of natural dye.
