Franciscea calycina var. major

Franciscea calycina var. major, commonly known as the giant franciscea, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, rocky soils and is often found in coastal regions. It has a woody stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
