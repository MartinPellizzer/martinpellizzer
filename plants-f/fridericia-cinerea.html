Fridericia cinerea

Fridericia cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf fridericia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is a shrub that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is native to parts of South America. The plant is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. Its small, pea-like flowers are usually white or pale blue, and it produces leguminous pods that contain seeds.

Fridericia cinerea is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
