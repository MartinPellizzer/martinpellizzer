Festuca ovina subvar. pilifera

Festuca ovina subvar. pilifera, commonly known as the hairy sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit.

It features slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats, and its leaves are typically narrow with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are usually pale green to brown in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous or alpine regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
