Ficus torrentium

Ficus torrentium, commonly known as the river fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit, a small, round fig, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

Ficus torrentium thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
