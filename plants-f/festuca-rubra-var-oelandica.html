Festuca rubra var. oelandica

Festuca rubra var. oelandica, commonly known as Oeland meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
