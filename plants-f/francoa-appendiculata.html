Francoa appendiculata

Francoa appendiculata, commonly known as the clustered francoa, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial to full shade. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers.

The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and arranged in a rosette pattern at the base of the plant.
