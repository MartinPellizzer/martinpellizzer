Faroa salutaris

Faroa salutaris, commonly known as the sacred tree of the Māori people, is a native New Zealand plant belonging to the Moraceae family.

It is a small, deciduous tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and lowland forests. The plant has distinctive heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits. Its bark and leaves have been traditionally used in Māori medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant plays an important ecological and cultural role in New Zealand's biodiversity.
