Ferolia asperula

Ferolia asperula, commonly known as the roughleaf ferolia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a rough texture. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. Its fruit is a small, spherical berry that turns red when mature.

Ferolia asperula is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions.
