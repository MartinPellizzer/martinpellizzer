Festuca drakensbergensis

Festuca drakensbergensis, commonly known as the Drakensberg fescue, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Festuca drakensbergensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh conditions.
