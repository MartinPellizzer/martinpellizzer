Fuchsia paniculata

Fuchsia paniculata, commonly known as the weeping fuchsia, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous clusters of showy, violet-pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with ovate to lanceolate leaflets, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Fuchsia paniculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
