Fuchsia longiflora

Fuchsia longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep purple to bright red. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, silvery down.

Fuchsia longiflora thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
