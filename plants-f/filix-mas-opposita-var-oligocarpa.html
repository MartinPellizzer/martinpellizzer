Filix-mas opposita var. oligocarpa

Filix-mas opposita var. oligocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leafed fern, is a fern species characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to certain regions of Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including sporangia, are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is noted for its compact growth habit and is appreciated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens.
