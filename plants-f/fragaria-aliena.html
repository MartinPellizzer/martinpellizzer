Fragaria aliena

Fragaria aliena, commonly known as the European wild strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically red, with a sweet and tangy flavor. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and erect, hairy stems that bear white flowers with five petals. Its fruit develops from a superior ovary and is characterized by a distinctive aroma and a cluster of small achenes.

Fragaria aliena thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.
