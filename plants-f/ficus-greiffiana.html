Ficus greiffiana

Ficus greiffiana, commonly known as the green fig or Indian rubber plant, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is evergreen and can grow as a tree or shrub, depending on environmental conditions. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus greiffiana is popular as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
