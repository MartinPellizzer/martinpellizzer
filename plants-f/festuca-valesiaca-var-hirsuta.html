Festuca valesiaca var. hirsuta

Festuca valesiaca var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy valley fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils. Its flowers are small and spike-like, forming compact inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Festuca valesiaca var. hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in challenging terrain.
