Frangula dodonei subsp. pontica

Frangula dodonei subsp.

pontica, commonly known as the Pontic frangula, is a shrub native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves and yellow flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces small, red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shrubland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
