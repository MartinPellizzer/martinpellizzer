Fagara pistaciifolia

Fagara pistaciifolia, commonly known as the glossy leaf fagara, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green, leathery foliage that often has a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elliptical, dark blue-black berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
