Festuca pseudodalmatica f. angulosa

Festuca pseudodalmatica f. angulosa, commonly known as the angular false dalmatian grass, is a variant of the Festuca pseudodalmatica species.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its distinctive angular leaf blades, which give it its common name. This plant typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and along roadsides. Its foliage is usually narrow and grass-like, with a tufted growth habit.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
