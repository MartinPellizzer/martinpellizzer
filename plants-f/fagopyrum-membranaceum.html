Fagopyrum membranaceum

Fagopyrum membranaceum, commonly known as the Himalayan buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in rocky or alpine environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers and has distinctive, palmately divided leaves. It is often found at high altitudes where it contributes to the local flora and ecosystem.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and traditional medicine.
