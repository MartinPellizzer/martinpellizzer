Ficus carica var. sativa

Ficus carica var. sativa, commonly known as the common fig, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and produces edible fruit that develops from a syconium, a unique type of flower cluster. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its fruit is rich in nutrients and has been cultivated for thousands of years for both culinary and medicinal purposes.

The tree is also notable for its ability to tolerate pruning, making it a popular choice for ornamental and fruit-bearing gardens.
