Fraxinus angustifolia f. deltoides

Fraxinus angustifolia f. deltoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf ash, is a cultivar of the ash tree characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves.

It is native to the southeastern United States and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree with a rounded or pyramidal shape. Its leaves are dark green on top and lighter underneath, and they turn yellow in the fall.

The species is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.
