Fosterella elata

Fosterella elata, commonly known as the Florida bottlebrush, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive red, bottlebrush-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant floral display. Fosterella elata thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal landscapes.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a valuable addition to both natural and cultivated gardens.
