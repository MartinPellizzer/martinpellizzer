Ficus carica var. oblonga

Ficus carica var. oblonga, commonly known as the Persian fig or oblong fig, is a cultivar of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its elongated, oblong-shaped fruits that are typically smaller than the standard fig. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a smooth texture and a deep green color.

This variety is prized for its sweet, edible fruit and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
