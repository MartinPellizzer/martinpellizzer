Ficus pumila var. awkeotsang

Ficus pumila var. awkeotsang, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by green, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid environments and is often used as a ground cover or hanging plant due to its trailing growth habit.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
