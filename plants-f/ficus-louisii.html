Ficus louisii

Ficus louisii, commonly known as the Louisiana fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
