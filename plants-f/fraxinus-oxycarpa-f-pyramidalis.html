Fraxinus oxycarpa f. pyramidalis

Fraxinus oxycarpa f. pyramidalis, commonly known as the narrowleaf ash, is a cultivar of the ash tree characterized by its narrow, conical shape.

It is a deciduous tree with dark green, lance-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds called samaras. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for urban landscapes.

This cultivar is valued for its compact form and ornamental appeal in gardens and parks.
