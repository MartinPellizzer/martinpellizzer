Ficus microclada

Ficus microclada, commonly known as the dwarf fig or desert fig, is a small, evergreen shrub native to arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its dense, waxy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a unique visual contrast. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It is highly drought-tolerant, making it well-suited for xeriscaping and rocky landscapes.

Ficus microclada is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
