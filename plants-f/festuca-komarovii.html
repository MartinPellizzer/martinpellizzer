Festuca komarovii

Festuca komarovii, commonly known as Komarov's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to central and eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous florets, which are often pale green to brown in hue. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or alpine regions.

Festuca komarovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in natural habitats.
