Freycinetia lauterbachii

Freycinetia lauterbachii, commonly known as the Lauterbach's palm, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into clustered, fibrous fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
