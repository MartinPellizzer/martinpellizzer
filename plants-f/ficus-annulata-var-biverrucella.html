Ficus annulata var. biverrucella

Ficus annulata var. biverrucella, commonly known as the biverrucella fig, is a variant of the common fig tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that are often covered in a waxy, white coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that ripen to a sweet, yellowish color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for its fruit, which is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
