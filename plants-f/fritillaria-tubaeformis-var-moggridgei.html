Fritillaria tubaeformis var. moggridgei

Fritillaria tubaeformis var. moggridgei, commonly known as the Moggridge's fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a cluster of bulbs that help it survive in harsh, arid conditions. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

This variant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
