Ficus areolata

Ficus areolata, commonly known as the star-shaped fig tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its unique, warty, and often star-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant has a thick, gnarled trunk and can grow as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are typically dark green and have a rough texture, with prominent veins that contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Ficus areolata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
