Firkea

Firkea, commonly known as the silver fir, is a coniferous evergreen tree native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk and dense, pyramidal crown. The leaves are needle-like, slender, and covered in a silvery-white wax layer, giving it its common name. Firkea produces cones that contain seeds, and it reproduces through wind pollination.

This hardy plant is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
