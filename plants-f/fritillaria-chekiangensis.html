Fritillaria chekiangensis

Fritillaria chekiangensis, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to China.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red, often with intricate patterns. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in rocky or sandy soils. It blooms in early spring and is valued for its ornamental beauty in gardens.

Due to habitat loss, it is also considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
