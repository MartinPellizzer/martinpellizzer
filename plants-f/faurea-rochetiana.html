Faurea rochetiana

Faurea rochetiana, commonly known as the Hottentot's button, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, round, greenish-blue seed heads that resemble buttons. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by the development of its unique seed capsules. It is typically found in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Faurea rochetiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its striking appearance and resilience.
