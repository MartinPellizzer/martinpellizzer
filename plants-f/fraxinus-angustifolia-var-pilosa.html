Fraxinus angustifolia var. pilosa

Fraxinus angustifolia var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy ash, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its distinctive hairy leaves, which are finely pubescent on both sides. The leaves are typically elongated and have a narrow, lance-shaped appearance. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
