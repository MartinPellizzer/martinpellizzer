Ficus arbutifolia

Ficus arbutifolia, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and ability to grow along walls, trees, or the ground. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by fig-like fruits that are edible for some animals but not typically for humans.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
