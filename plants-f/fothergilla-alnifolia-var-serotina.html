Fothergilla alnifolia var. serotina

Fothergilla alnifolia var. serotina, commonly known as late-fruiting fothergilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and showy, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, bell-shaped white or pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer, which are followed by bright red berries in the fall. Its leaves are dark green, glossy, and turn vibrant red or orange in autumn, adding ornamental value.

This variety is well-suited for moist, acidic soils and is often used in native plant gardens for its ecological and aesthetic benefits.
