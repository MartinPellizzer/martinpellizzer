Ficus macrosperma

Ficus macrosperma, commonly known as the large-seeded fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Ficus macrosperma is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

Its fruit, which is typically small and yellowish, is not commonly consumed by humans but is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
