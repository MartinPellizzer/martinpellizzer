Fergania

Fergania, commonly known as the fergania or fergan, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Polygonaceae.

These plants are typically herbaceous and found in arid and semi-arid regions, particularly in Central Asia. They often have simple, alternate leaves and produce small, clustered flowers. Fergania species are adapted to harsh environments, showing resilience to drought and poor soil conditions.

The genus includes several species, some of which are used in traditional medicine or horticulture.
