Festuca arundinacea var. littoralis

Festuca arundinacea var. littoralis, commonly known as coastal tall fescue, is a variant of tall fescue adapted to coastal environments.

It is characterized by its robust growth and tolerance to salt spray and sandy soils. The plant typically forms dense, tufted clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves. It produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-based flowers.

This variety is often used in coastal landscaping and erosion control due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
