Fritillaria lanceolata

Fritillaria lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It produces striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, often with contrasting purple or brown markings. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation efforts.
