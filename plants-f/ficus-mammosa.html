Ficus mammosa

Ficus mammosa, commonly known as the breast tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and distinctive, swollen, breast-like structures on its trunk, which are believed to be modified branches. The plant belongs to the Moraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various regions.
