Ficus fraseri

Ficus fraseri, commonly known as the Fraser's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus fraseri is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
