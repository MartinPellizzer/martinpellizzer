Faberia faberi

Faberia faberi, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to China.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, also known as the legume or pea family, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Faberia faberi is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.
