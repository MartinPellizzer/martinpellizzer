Ferdinandusa cordata

Ferdinandusa cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and can grow quite tall, reaching up to 30 meters in height. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its bark is thick and rough, providing a distinctive texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
