Ficus petersii

Ficus petersii, commonly known as the African fig or Peters' fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a waxy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fig fruits. Ficus petersii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in indoor environments.

It prefers bright, indirect light and consistent moisture in its soil.
