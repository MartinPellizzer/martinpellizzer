Fedia nervata

Fedia nervata, commonly known as the veined fern, is a species of fern native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds with prominent veins. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The intricate structure of its leaves showcases the complex vascular system typical of ferns.
