Ferdinandusa leucantha

Ferdinandusa leucantha, commonly known as the white-flowered evergreen, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has a straight, unbranched trunk and produces small, round fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are typically elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture.
