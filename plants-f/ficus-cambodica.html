Ficus cambodica

Ficus cambodica, commonly known as the Cambodian fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Cambodia, Thailand, and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered fig fruits that are edible. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in dense forests or along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has traditional uses in local cultures.
