Foeniculum officinale var. sativum

Foeniculum officinale var. sativum, commonly known as cultivated fennel, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its feathery, finely divided leaves and yellow flowers that form umbrella-like clusters. The plant produces aromatic seeds that are widely used in cooking and traditional medicine. Its roots and leaves contain volatile oils, primarily anethole, which contribute to its distinctive licorice-like flavor.

Fennel thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and culinary use.
