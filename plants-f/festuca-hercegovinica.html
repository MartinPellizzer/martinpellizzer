Festuca hercegovinica

Festuca hercegovinica, commonly known as the Herzegovina fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Festuca hercegovinica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.

It plays a significant role in the ecological balance of its native habitats.
