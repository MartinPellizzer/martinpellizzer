Fedia truncata

Fedia truncata, commonly known as the African rock orchid, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a terrestrial orchid characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often in shaded or partially shaded environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while its flowers are usually pale green or yellowish with a prominent lip.

Fedia truncata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
