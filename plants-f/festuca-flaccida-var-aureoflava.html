Festuca flaccida var. aureoflava

Festuca flaccida var. aureoflava, commonly known as the golden hair grass, is a variant of the flaccida grass species.

It is a low-growing perennial grass that typically reaches heights of 10 to 30 centimeters. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, golden hue, giving it its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
