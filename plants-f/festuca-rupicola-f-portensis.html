Festuca rupicola f. portensis

Festuca rupicola f. portensis, commonly known as the Portentious rockgrass, is a subspecies of the Festuca rupicola, a member of the grass family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. This plant typically thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments. It is well adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

The subspecies f. portensis is distinguished by its specific geographic distribution and unique morphological traits.
