Ficus langbianensis

Ficus langbianensis, commonly known as the Langbian fig, is a species of flowering plant native to Vietnam.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments within forests.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
