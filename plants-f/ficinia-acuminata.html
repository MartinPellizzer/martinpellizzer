Ficinia acuminata

Ficinia acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-pointed sedum, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, cylindrical stems and small, clustered leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are borne on short, erect stalks. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
