Ficus carica subsp. carica

Ficus carica subsp.

carica, commonly known as the common fig, is a deciduous tree native to the Middle East and southern Europe. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces edible fruits known as figs.

The plant has a deep root system that helps it thrive in various soil types. It is cultivated for its fruit, which is rich in nutrients and has a unique sweet flavor.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in human culture.
