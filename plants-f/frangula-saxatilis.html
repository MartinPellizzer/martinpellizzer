Frangula saxatilis

Frangula saxatilis, commonly known as the rock buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, clustered leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers followed by small, oval berries that are rich in anthraquinones. It thrives in rocky and dry habitats, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Due to its medicinal properties, it has been historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
