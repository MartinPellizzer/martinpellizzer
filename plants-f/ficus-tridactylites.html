Ficus tridactylites

Ficus tridactylites, commonly known as the three-toed fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters tall, featuring glossy, dark green leaves that are often lobed at the base. The plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature, though they may change color depending on the variety. Its roots are fibrous and can spread widely, making it adaptable to various soil types.

Ficus tridactylites is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid environments.
