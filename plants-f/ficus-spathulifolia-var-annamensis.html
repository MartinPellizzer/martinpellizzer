Ficus spathulifolia var. annamensis

Ficus spathulifolia var. annamensis, commonly known as the Annam fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The leaves are typically glossy and have a distinctive waxy texture. It produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green or yellow in color.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
