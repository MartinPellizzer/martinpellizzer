Festuca curvata

Festuca curvata, commonly known as bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green, bluish-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. Festuca curvata produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-pollinated flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass management.
