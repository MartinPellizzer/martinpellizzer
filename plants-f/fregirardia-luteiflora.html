Fregirardia luteiflora

Fregirardia luteiflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered fregirardia, is a flowering plant species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow blooms, which typically appear during the warmer months of the year. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is sometimes grown in botanical gardens for its ornamental value.
