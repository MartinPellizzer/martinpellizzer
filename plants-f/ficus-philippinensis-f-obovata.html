Ficus philippinensis f. obovata

Ficus philippinensis f. obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a variant of the fig tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its broad, obovate-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often consumed by both humans and wildlife. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated as a houseplant in warmer regions.
