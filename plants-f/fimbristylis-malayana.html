Fimbristylis malayana

Fimbristylis malayana, commonly known as the Malay sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in wetland and marshy environments. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in moist soils.

Its botanical features make it an important species in aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
