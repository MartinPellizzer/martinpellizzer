Festuca distichovaginata f. apogama

Festuca distichovaginata f. apogama, commonly known as the apogamous form of the two-row fescue, is a botanical variant characterized by its apogamous reproduction.

This means it reproduces asexually through seed formation without fertilization, resulting in offspring genetically identical to the parent plant. The plant exhibits a grass-like appearance with narrow, grass-like leaves and compact growth habits. It is typically found in temperate regions, often in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its unique reproductive method makes it an interesting subject for studies in plant genetics and evolution.
