Felicia fascicularis

Felicia fascicularis, commonly known as the narrowleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in a cluster, and the leaves are narrow and linear.

Felicia fascicularis is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to cultivate.
