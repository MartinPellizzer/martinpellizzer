Fumaria capreolata subsp. agraria

Fumaria capreolata subsp.

agraria, commonly known as the agrarian yellowcress, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae. It is characterized by its delicate yellow flowers and trifoliate leaves, which are typically arranged in a basal rosette.

The plant thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, along roadsides, and in agricultural fields. It produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds, aiding in its propagation.

This subspecies is notable for its adaptability and is considered a common weed in certain regions.
