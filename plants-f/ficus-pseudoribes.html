Ficus pseudoribes

Ficus pseudoribes, commonly known as the Indian rubber fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in outdoor gardens.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for both ornamental and practical purposes.
