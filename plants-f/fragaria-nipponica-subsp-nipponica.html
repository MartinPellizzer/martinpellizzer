Fragaria nipponica subsp. nipponica

Fragaria nipponica subsp.

nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese strawberry, is a species native to eastern Asia. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, red, edible berries.

The plant typically thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, loamy soils. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it blooms with white flowers that have five petals.

The botanical features of this subspecies make it a valuable subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
