Fritillaria ferganensis

Fritillaria ferganensis, commonly known as the Fergana fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color. The plant produces a cluster of elongated, narrow leaves and has a robust underground bulb that stores nutrients. It thrives in rocky or arid environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
