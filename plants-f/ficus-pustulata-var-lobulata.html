Ficus pustulata var. lobulata

Ficus pustulata var. lobulata, commonly known as the lobed rubber plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features thick, waxy leaves with a distinctive lobed shape, often with a pale green underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by green, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
