Festuca rubra var. multiflora

Festuca rubra var. multiflora, commonly known as red fescue, is a cool-season grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, dense foliage and tufted growth habit, with slender, often reddish stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in turfgrass mixes for its durability and aesthetic appeal.

Festuca rubra var. multiflora is also valued for its ability to tolerate shade and moderate foot traffic.
