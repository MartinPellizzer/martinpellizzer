Fagus sylvatica var. aenea

Fagus sylvatica var. aenea, commonly known as the silver-leafed beech, is a variant of the common beech tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a striking appearance. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a pointed tip and a serrated edge. This variety thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodlands and forested areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and botanical gardens.
