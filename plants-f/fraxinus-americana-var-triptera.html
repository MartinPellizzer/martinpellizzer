Fraxinus americana var. triptera

Fraxinus americana var. triptera, commonly known as the winged ash, is a variant of the American ash tree characterized by its distinctive winged seeds.

It is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States, typically reaching heights of 20 to 40 meters. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin, and they turn yellow in the fall. The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
