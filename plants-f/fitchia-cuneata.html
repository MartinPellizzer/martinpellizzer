Fitchia cuneata

Fitchia cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fitchia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America, particularly in Argentina and Chile. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Fitchia cuneata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic foliage.
