Freylinia undulata var. carinata

Freylinia undulata var. carinata, commonly known as the wavy freylinia, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the spring and summer seasons. Its stems are cylindrical and can become woody with age, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.
