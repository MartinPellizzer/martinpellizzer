Ferocactus falconeri

Ferocactus falconeri, commonly known as the Falconeri barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh desert conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its spines are typically yellow or brown, providing protection from herbivores.

Ferocactus falconeri is well adapted to extreme temperatures and low rainfall, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
