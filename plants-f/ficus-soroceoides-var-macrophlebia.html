Ficus soroceoides var. macrophlebia

Ficus soroceoides var. macrophlebia, commonly known as the large-veined fig, is a tropical plant characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves with prominent, parallel veins.

The leaves are typically dark green and glossy, providing a striking visual contrast against the plant's woody stems. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are edible and often consumed by various bird species. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its unique leaf structure and fruit production make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
