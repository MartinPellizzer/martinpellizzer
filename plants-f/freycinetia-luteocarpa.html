Freycinetia luteocarpa

Freycinetia luteocarpa, commonly known as the yellow-fruited pandanus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, fibrous stalks and broad, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that develop into fleshy, orange-colored fruits. Its unique botanical features make it a distinctive species within its native habitat.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in local cultures.
