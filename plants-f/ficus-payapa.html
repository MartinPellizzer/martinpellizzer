Ficus payapa

Ficus payapa, commonly known as the Payapa fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly found in countries like Mexico and Guatemala. The plant is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are typically dark green in color. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus payapa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
