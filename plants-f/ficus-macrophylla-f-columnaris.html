Ficus macrophylla f. columnaris

Ficus macrophylla f. columnaris, commonly known as the Weeping Fig or Variegated Ficus, is a cultivar of the Ficus genus native to Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive weeping habit, with long, cascading branches that bear small, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant has a striking variegation pattern, featuring white and yellow markings on its foliage. It is a popular indoor plant due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions.

However, it requires regular pruning to maintain its shape and promote healthy growth.
