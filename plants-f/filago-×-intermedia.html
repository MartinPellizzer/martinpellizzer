Filago × intermedia

Filago × intermedia, commonly known as the hybrid filago, is a perennial plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is a natural hybrid between Filago scabrosa and Filago umbrosa, exhibiting characteristics of both parent species. The plant features erect, branching stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, yellowish, and arranged in composite inflorescences, typical of the Asteraceae family.

Filago × intermedia is typically found in moist, disturbed soils in woodland or grassland habitats.
