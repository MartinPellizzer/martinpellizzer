Ficus rubropunctata

Ficus rubropunctata, commonly known as the red-spotted fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are adorned with distinct red or purple spots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus rubropunctata is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
