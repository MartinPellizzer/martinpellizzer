Ficus crescentioides

Ficus crescentioides, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Cambodia. The plant is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically clustered in small groups.

Ficus crescentioides is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
