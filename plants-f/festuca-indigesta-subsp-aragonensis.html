Festuca indigesta subsp. aragonensis

Festuca indigesta subsp.

aragonensis, commonly known as Aragon grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, rocky or sandy soils, often in high-altitude regions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers.

This subspecies is adapted to dry and cold climates, making it a hardy plant in Mediterranean and mountainous environments.
