Fragaria pinnatifida

Fragaria pinnatifida, commonly known as the Japanese strawberry, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, red, edible fruits that resemble strawberries. The plant thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary value. Its flowers are typically white or pale pink, and it produces runners that help in vegetative propagation.

The fruit is valued for its sweet taste and is sometimes used in jams and desserts.
