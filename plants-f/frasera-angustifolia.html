Frasera angustifolia

Frasera angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf frasera, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Aristolochiaceae and is characterized by its upright growth habit and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lanceolate, and often have a fuzzy texture.
