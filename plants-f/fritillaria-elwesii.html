Fritillaria elwesii

Fritillaria elwesii, commonly known as the Persian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or yellow with dark markings, resembling a fritillary butterfly. The plant has a tall, leafless stem that bears a single, upright flower cluster. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in cold, arid environments, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Fritillaria elwesii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is appreciated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
