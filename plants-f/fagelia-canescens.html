Fagelia canescens

Fagelia canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf fagelia, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray leaves, which are covered in fine, dense hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. Its woody stems are often branched and can grow up to several meters in height.

Fagelia canescens is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in high-altitude ecosystems.
