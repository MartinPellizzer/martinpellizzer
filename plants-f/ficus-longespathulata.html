Ficus longespathulata

Ficus longespathulata, commonly known as the long-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, and is characterized by its elongated, waxy leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible fig fruits, which are typically clustered in syconia. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.

Ficus longespathulata prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for houseplants.
