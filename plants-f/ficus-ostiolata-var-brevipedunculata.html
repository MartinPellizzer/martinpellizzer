Ficus ostiolata var. brevipedunculata

Ficus ostiolata var. brevipedunculata, commonly known as the short-stalked fig tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with glossy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences known as syconia, which are characteristic of the Moraceae family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to adapt to indoor environments.
