Fragaria sicca

Fragaria sicca, commonly known as the Sicilian strawberry, is a species of wild strawberry native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, red, edible fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are white with five petals and are followed by the characteristic red, fleshy berries.

Fragaria sicca is valued for its ornamental qualities and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
