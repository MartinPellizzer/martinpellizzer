Fritillaria puqiensis

Fritillaria puqiensis, commonly known as the Puqi fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Fritillaria puqiensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
