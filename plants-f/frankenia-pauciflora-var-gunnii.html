Frankenia pauciflora var. gunnii

Frankenia pauciflora var. gunnii, commonly known as the coastal sandmat, is a low-growing, mat-forming plant native to coastal regions of southern Australia.

It features small, fleshy, blue-gray leaves that are adapted to arid and salty environments. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in dunes or coastal heathlands.

Its compact growth habit and drought tolerance make it a hardy ornamental plant suitable for coastal gardens.
