Fagus nigra

Fagus nigra, commonly known as the American beech, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and chestnuts, and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, serrated leaves. The tree produces small, cup-shaped fruits called beechnuts, which are encased in a prickly husk. Fagus nigra typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
