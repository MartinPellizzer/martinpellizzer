Faramea colorata

Faramea colorata, commonly known as the red-flowered evergreen hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety red flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, often covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

Faramea colorata is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
