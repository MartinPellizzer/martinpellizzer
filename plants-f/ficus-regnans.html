Ficus regnans

Ficus regnans, commonly known as the giant ficus or royal fig, is a large tropical tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its massive, broad leaves that can reach up to 3 feet in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are an important food source for various wildlife. Its roots can grow extensively, helping to stabilize the soil in its native habitat.

Ficus regnans is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates.
