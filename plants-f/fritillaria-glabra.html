Fritillaria glabra

Fritillaria glabra, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountains of southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are toxic and should be handled with care.

Fritillaria glabra is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
