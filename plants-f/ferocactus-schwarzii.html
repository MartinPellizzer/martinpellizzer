Ferocactus schwarzii

Ferocactus schwarzii, commonly known as the Schwarz's barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems that are typically green to blue-green in color, often covered with white, woolly spines. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Ferocactus schwarzii is well adapted to extreme drought conditions, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in well-drained soil and full sun.
