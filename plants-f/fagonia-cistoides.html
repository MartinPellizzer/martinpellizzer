Fagonia cistoides

Fagonia cistoides, commonly known as the desert mallow, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the spring, and its leaves are typically narrow and gray-green in color. The plant has a deep root system that allows it to survive in dry conditions, and it is often found in rocky soils.

Fagonia cistoides is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low precipitation, making it a resilient species in harsh environments.
