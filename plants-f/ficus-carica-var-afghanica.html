Ficus carica var. afghanica

Ficus carica var. afghanica, commonly known as the Afghan fig, is a variant of the common fig tree.

It is native to regions of Central and South Asia, including parts of Afghanistan and Pakistan. This plant is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and produces small, edible figs that are typically harvested when they are fully ripe. The tree is often cultivated for its fruit, which is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, soft texture.

It thrives in warm, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
