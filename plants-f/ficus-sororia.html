Ficus sororia

Ficus sororia, commonly known as the silver ficus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. The plant is a member of the Moraceae family and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.

Ficus sororia is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
