Fuchsia affinis

Fuchsia affinis, commonly known as the common fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters. The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, which bloom in spring and early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Fuchsia affinis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a houseplant.
