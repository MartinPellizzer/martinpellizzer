Festuca thomasiana

Festuca thomasiana, commonly known as Thomas's fescue, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in well-drained, rocky soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental value.

Festuca thomasiana is valued in horticulture for its ability to form dense, low-growing mats.
