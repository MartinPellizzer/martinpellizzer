Friedlandia angustifolia

Friedlandia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved Friedlandia, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It features slender, upright stems that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a hairy texture.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.
