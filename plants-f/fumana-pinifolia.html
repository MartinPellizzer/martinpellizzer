Fumana pinifolia

Fumana pinifolia, commonly known as the needleleaf fumana, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, shrub-like form. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are usually pale green to yellow in color. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Fumana pinifolia is adapted to withstand drought conditions, making it a hardy species in its native habitat.
