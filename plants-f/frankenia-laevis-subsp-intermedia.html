Frankenia laevis subsp. intermedia

Frankenia laevis subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the coastal sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and hairy, gray-green foliage.

The plant produces small, showy flowers in shades of pink to red, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in dune and coastal plain habitats.

This species is adapted to harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.
