Fagelia trilobata

Fagelia trilobata, commonly known as the three-lobed fagelia, is a small evergreen shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Ericaceae, which includes heathers and blueberries, and is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage. The plant features elliptical leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it its common name, and produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white or pale pink. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Fagelia trilobata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage structure.
