Fabricia stellata

Fabricia stellata, commonly known as the star-flowered fabia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that resemble stars, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or shady areas.

Fabricia stellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
