Fagelia pinifolia

Fagelia pinifolia, commonly known as the needleleaf fagelia, is a rare and lesser-known plant species belonging to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and beeches.

It is characterized by its needle-like leaves, which are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by woody, cup-like structures containing edible nuts. Fagelia pinifolia is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in moist, shaded forest environments.

Due to habitat loss and limited research, it remains a poorly understood species within its family.
