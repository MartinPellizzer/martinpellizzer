Festuca pyrogea

Festuca pyrogea, commonly known as the firegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along streambanks. Festuca pyrogea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

It plays an important ecological role by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
