Ficus whytei

Ficus whytei, commonly known as the Whyte's fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife and humans. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated as a houseplant in many regions.
