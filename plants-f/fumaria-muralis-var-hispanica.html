Fumaria muralis var. hispanica

Fumaria muralis var. hispanica, commonly known as Spanish wallflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows on rocky or sandy soils, often found in disturbed areas or along walls. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and covered in fine hairs, while the stems are erect and branching.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in marginal environments.
