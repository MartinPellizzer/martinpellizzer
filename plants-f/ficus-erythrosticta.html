Ficus erythrosticta

Ficus erythrosticta, commonly known as the red-streaked fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent red veins, which give it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically consumed by local wildlife and humans.

Ficus erythrosticta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
