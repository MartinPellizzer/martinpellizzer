Fadogia luangwae

Fadogia luangwae, commonly known as Lao velvet tamarind, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a legume that splits open to release seeds, and the plant is often found in moist, tropical forests.

Fadogia luangwae is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
