Fragaria moschata

Fragaria moschata, commonly known as the musk strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia and the Mediterranean.

It produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant's leaves are trifoliate, with heart-shaped leaflets and a slightly hairy texture. Its fruit, resembling a small strawberry, is typically yellow or orange and has a musky, aromatic flavor.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its unique taste and fragrance.
