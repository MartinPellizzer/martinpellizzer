Ficus saussureana

Ficus saussureana, commonly known as the Saussure's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Madagascar, and thrives in humid, warm climates. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.

The species is also valued for its ability to adapt to various soil types and its tolerance to indoor growing conditions.
