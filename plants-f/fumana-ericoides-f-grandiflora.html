Fumana ericoides f. grandiflora

Fumana ericoides f. grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora form of the heath plant, is a compact shrub characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage.

It features small, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and arranged in a compact, bushy habit. The plant produces clusters of tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a border plant.
