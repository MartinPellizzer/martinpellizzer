Ficus pseudorubra

Ficus pseudorubra, commonly known as the red-fig or red-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a reddish tinge, especially when young. It is an evergreen shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height.

Ficus pseudorubra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens due to its adaptability and aesthetic qualities.
