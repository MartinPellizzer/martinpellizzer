Ficus cairnsii

Ficus cairnsii, commonly known as the Cairns fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of northern Queensland, Australia, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature, and it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage. Ficus cairnsii is relatively low maintenance, thriving in bright, indirect light and requiring moderate watering.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and landscaping in warm climates.
