Ficus calyculata

Ficus calyculata, commonly known as the Chinese banyan, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and aerial roots that hang from its branches. The plant can grow into a massive tree, often reaching heights of over 30 meters, and is known for its ability to form extensive root systems. Its fruit is small, round, and green, turning yellow when ripe, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for various animals.

Ficus calyculata is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and urban landscapes.
