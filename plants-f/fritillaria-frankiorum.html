Fritillaria frankiorum

Fritillaria frankiorum, commonly known as the Frank's fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It blooms in early spring and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a rare and threatened species in some areas.
