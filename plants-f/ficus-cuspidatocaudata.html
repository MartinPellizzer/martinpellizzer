Ficus cuspidatocaudata

Ficus cuspidatocaudata, commonly known as the curly fig or the curly-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, and is characterized by its striking, wavy, and curly leaves. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are dark green and have a velvety texture, with a distinctive undulating margin.

Ficus cuspidatocaudata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and is relatively easy to care for in indoor environments.
