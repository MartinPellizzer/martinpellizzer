Festuca richardsonii subsp. fraterculae

Festuca richardsonii subsp.

fraterculae, commonly known as the Alaska bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern regions of North America. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in alpine and subalpine habitats.

The plant is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and compact growth habit. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife in its native range.
