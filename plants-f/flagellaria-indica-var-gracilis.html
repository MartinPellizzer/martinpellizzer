Flagellaria indica var. gracilis

Flagellaria indica var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender Indian whip.

It is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia, characterized by its long, slender stems and small, opposite leaves. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its use in traditional medicine and as a source of fiber.
