Fagara cuiabensis var. axillaris

Fagara cuiabensis var. axillaris, commonly known as the Brazilian wax tree, is a shrub native to the Atlantic Forest of Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and distinctive, waxy leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in secondary growth forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
