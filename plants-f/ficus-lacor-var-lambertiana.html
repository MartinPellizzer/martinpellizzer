Ficus lacor var. lambertiana

Ficus lacor var. lambertiana, commonly known as Lambert's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible figs, which are a significant food source for various animals. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

The plant's ability to adapt to different environments makes it a resilient and versatile species.
