Filago iodolepis

Filago iodolepis, commonly known as the sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Europe and North Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers and silver-gray foliage, which help it withstand salty sea air. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dunes and coastal cliffs. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

Filago iodolepis is a hardy perennial that blooms from late spring to early summer.
