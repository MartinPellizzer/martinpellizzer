Festuca rubra var. muramatsui

Festuca rubra var. muramatsui, commonly known as the Japanese red fescue, is a low-growing, perennial grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green foliage and dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to golden flowers, which appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for ornamental gardens and erosion control.

Its compact size and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for landscaping in temperate climates.
