Fragaria sylvestris var. alpina

Fragaria sylvestris var. alpina, commonly known as alpine strawberry, is a wild species of strawberry native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant with trifoliate leaves and produces small, tart berries. The plant thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and is adapted to poor, rocky soils. Its flowers are white with five petals, and it is often found in alpine meadows and tundra.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in breeding programs for cultivated strawberries.
