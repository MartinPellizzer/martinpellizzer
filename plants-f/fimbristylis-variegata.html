Fimbristylis variegata

Fimbristylis variegata, commonly known as the variegated flat sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Australia.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and typically grows in wetland or swampy environments. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or brown in color. Fimbristylis variegata is characterized by its variegated foliage, which features contrasting colors of green and white or silver, making it visually striking.
