Ficus australis

Ficus australis, commonly known as the Australian fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by edible fig fruits, which are an important food source for various wildlife. Ficus australis thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, moist environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to different climates.
