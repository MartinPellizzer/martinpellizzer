Fritillaria cirrhosa var. dingriensis

Fritillaria cirrhosa var. dingriensis, commonly known as the Dingri fritillary, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a reddish or purple tinge. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations between 3,000 and 5,000 meters. Its leaves are grass-like and linear, often forming a basal rosette.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
