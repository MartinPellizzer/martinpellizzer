Festuca fenas subsp. corsica

Festuca fenas subsp.

corsica, commonly known as Corsican fescue, is a grass species native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Corsica. It is a perennial plant characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny conditions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in marginal environments.
