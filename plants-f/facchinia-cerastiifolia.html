Facchinia cerastiifolia

Facchinia cerastiifolia, commonly known as the rock rose, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and produces clusters of small, pink or red flowers. The plant has small, fleshy leaves that are adapted to arid environments, and it thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in rock gardens due to its hardiness and ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
