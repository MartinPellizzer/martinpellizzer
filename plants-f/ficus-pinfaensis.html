Ficus pinfaensis

Ficus pinfaensis, commonly known as the Pinfaensis Fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus pinfaensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor environments.
