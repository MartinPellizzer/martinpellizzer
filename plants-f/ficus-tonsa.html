Ficus tonsa

Ficus tonsa, commonly known as the "Ficus Tonsa" or "Tonsa Fig," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green in color. The plant has a woody stem and can grow as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters in its natural habitat. It produces small, fig-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Ficus tonsa is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
