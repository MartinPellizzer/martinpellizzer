Festuca ovina subsp. alascana

Festuca ovina subsp.

alascana, commonly known as Alaska bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the northern regions of North America. It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and forms dense, mat-like growth habit.

This species is typically found in moist, well-drained soils in alpine and subalpine environments. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with small, spike-like inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soils in harsh climates.
