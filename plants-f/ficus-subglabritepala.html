Ficus subglabritepala

Ficus subglabritepala, commonly known as the glossy-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, waxy, and dark green leaves that give it a striking appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called syconia.

Ficus subglabritepala is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in indoor environments.
