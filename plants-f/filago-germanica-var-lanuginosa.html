Filago germanica var. lanuginosa

Filago germanica var. lanuginosa, commonly known as the hairy filago, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant is distinguished by its soft, hairy leaves and small, yellowish flowers that appear in clusters. It typically grows in moist, grassy areas and is often found in disturbed soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
