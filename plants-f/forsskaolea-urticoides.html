Forsskaolea urticoides

Forsskaolea urticoides, commonly known as the stinging nettle tree, is a flowering plant native to Africa and parts of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its hairy, stinging leaves that can cause skin irritation upon contact. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its roots are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in moist, shaded environments.
