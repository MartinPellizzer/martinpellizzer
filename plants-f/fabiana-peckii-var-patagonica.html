Fabiana peckii var. patagonica

Fabiana peckii var. patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian fabiana, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale green to yellowish in color, often clustered in racemes. It is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with low rainfall and high solar exposure.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study in semi-arid climates.
