Festuca ovina var. oregona

Festuca ovina var. oregona, commonly known as Oregon bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a dense, carpet-like appearance. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, streambanks, and forest edges. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native plant gardens and erosion control.

Festuca ovina var. oregona is also an important component of the local ecosystem, providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
