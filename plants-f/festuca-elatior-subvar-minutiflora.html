Festuca elatior subvar. minutiflora

Festuca elatior subvar. minutiflora, commonly known as the slender fescue, is a perennial grass species native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense clusters along the stem.
