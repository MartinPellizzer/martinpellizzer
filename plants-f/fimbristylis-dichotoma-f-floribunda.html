Fimbristylis dichotoma f. floribunda

Fimbristylis dichotoma f. floribunda, commonly known as the flowering flatsedge, is a variant of the flat sedge species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its grass-like leaves and dense, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its flowers are small and clustered, often appearing in shades of green or brown.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
