Ficus klinkii

Ficus klinkii, commonly known as the Klinkii fig, is a species native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fig fruits. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Ficus klinkii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
