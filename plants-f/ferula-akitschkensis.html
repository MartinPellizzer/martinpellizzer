Ferula akitschkensis

Ferula akitschkensis, commonly known as the Altai wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant is notable for its tall, slender stems and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ferula akitschkensis is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
