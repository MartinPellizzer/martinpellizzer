Ficaria ranunculoides var. immaculata

Ficaria ranunculoides var. immaculata, commonly known as the white-flowered buttercup, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features rounded, waxy leaves that are typically green with a slight bluish tint. The plant produces showy, cup-shaped flowers that are predominantly white with a yellow center, blooming in early spring. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
