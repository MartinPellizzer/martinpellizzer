Ficus sarmentosa subsp. nipponica

Ficus sarmentosa subsp.

nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese banyan, is a deciduous tree native to Japan and parts of eastern Asia. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive, sprawling growth habit.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by figs that are typically edible. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This species is notable for its ability to form extensive root systems and aerial roots, contributing to its unique appearance.
