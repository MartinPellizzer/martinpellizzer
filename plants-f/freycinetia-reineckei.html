Freycinetia reineckei

Freycinetia reineckei, commonly known as the New Caledonian palm, is a striking tropical plant native to the island of New Caledonia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
