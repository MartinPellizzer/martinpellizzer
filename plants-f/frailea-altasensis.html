Frailea altasensis

Frailea altasensis, commonly known as the Altas Mountain Cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and dense covering of spines. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
