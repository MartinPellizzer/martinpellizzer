Feuilleea virgultosa

Feuilleea virgultosa, commonly known as the silverleaf fern, is a species native to the subtropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that are covered in a fine, silvery-white bloom, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found growing near streams or in dense forest undergrowth. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly and form dense mats over time.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and can reach lengths of up to 60 centimeters, making it a striking addition to botanical gardens or shaded landscapes.
