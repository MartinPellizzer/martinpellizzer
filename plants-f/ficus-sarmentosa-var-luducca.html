Ficus sarmentosa var. luducca

Ficus sarmentosa var. luducca, commonly known as the Luducca fig or the creeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its trailing growth habit, with small, opposite leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits.

This variety is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor environments.
