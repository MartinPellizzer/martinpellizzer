Ferolia campestris

Ferolia campestris, commonly known as the field lime, is a small shrub native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces round, green fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Ferolia campestris thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal remedies.
