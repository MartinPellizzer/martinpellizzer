Festuca setacea

Festuca setacea, commonly known as silky fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and slender, erect growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas.

Festuca setacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
