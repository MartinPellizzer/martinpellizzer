Fimbristylis castanea var. thonningiana

Fimbristylis castanea var. thonningiana, commonly known as the red-haired sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage with reddish-brown bracts that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in humid and tropical climates. It produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow flowers, which are often borne on slender, upright stems.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
