Festuca sieberi subsp. carniolica

Festuca sieberi subsp.

carniolica, commonly known as Carpathian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, dark green leaves that are often covered with fine hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous slender spikelets, which are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and forested areas. Festuca sieberi subsp.

carniolica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
