Festuca segetum

Festuca segetum, commonly known as barleygrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are typically bright green. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. Festuca segetum is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in cultivated fields.

It is valued for its nutritional content and has been used in traditional herbal remedies and as a forage crop.
