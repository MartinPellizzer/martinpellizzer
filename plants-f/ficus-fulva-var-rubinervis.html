Ficus fulva var. rubinervis

Ficus fulva var. rubinervis, commonly known as the red-veined fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, featuring dark green leaves with prominent red veins. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are glossy and often have a slightly waxy texture.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
