Festuca sulcata f. incurvata

Festuca sulcata f. incurvata, commonly known as the curved meadow fescue, is a cool-season grass species characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit.

It features narrow, dark green leaves with a slightly curled or involuted base, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous slender, two-rowed spikelets. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often used in turfgrass and forage production.

Its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in full sun make it a versatile choice for landscaping and agricultural purposes.
