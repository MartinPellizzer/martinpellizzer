Fuirena ecklonii subsp. barotsia

Fuirena ecklonii subsp.

barotsia, commonly known as the Barotsia grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in grassland habitats. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typical of the grass family.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and adaptability.
