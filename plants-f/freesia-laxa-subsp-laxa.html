Freesia laxa subsp. laxa

Freesia laxa subsp.

laxa, commonly known as the slender freesia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing delicate, bell-shaped petals. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for garden borders and container planting.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its pleasant fragrance and long-lasting blooms.
