Fimbristylis autumnalis var. complanata

Fimbristylis autumnalis var. complanata, commonly known as the flatleaf sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Australia.

It typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are flat and often have a bluish-green hue. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
