Ficus pruniformis

Ficus pruniformis, commonly known as the "bushy fig" or "bushy rubber tree," is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its dense, broad leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by green, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature.

Ficus pruniformis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
