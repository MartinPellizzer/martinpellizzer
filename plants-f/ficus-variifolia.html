Ficus variifolia

Ficus variifolia, commonly known as the variable fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its diverse leaf shapes, which can vary from elliptical to ovate, often with serrated edges. Its leaves are typically glossy and dark green, providing a striking visual appearance.

Ficus variifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor environments.
