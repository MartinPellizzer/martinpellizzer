Fraxinus sieboldiana f. tobana

Fraxinus sieboldiana f. tobana, commonly known as the Japanese ash tree, is a unique cultivar characterized by its distinct foliage and growth habits.

It is a deciduous tree with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often a striking silver-green color, providing ornamental value in gardens. The plant is native to Japan and is prized for its adaptability to various soil types and its resistance to certain diseases. It typically grows to a moderate height and width, making it suitable for smaller landscapes.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for urban and residential planting due to its aesthetic appeal and hardiness.
