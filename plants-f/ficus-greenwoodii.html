Ficus greenwoodii

Ficus greenwoodii, commonly known as the Greenwood fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus greenwoodii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
