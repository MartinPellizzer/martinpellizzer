Festuca yalaensis

Festuca yalaensis, commonly known as Yala mountain fescue, is a grass species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Festuca yalaensis has small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the ecological systems in its native habitat.
