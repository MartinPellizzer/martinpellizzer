Ferula seseloides

Ferula seseloides, commonly known as mountain fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and distinctive umbel-like flower clusters. The plant features tall, slender stems covered in fine hairs and has narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, and it produces a strong-smelling, resinous sap.

Ferula seseloides is often found in rocky or arid environments, adapting well to harsh climatic conditions.
