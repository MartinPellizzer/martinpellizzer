Fraxinus grandidentata

Fraxinus grandidentata, commonly known as the Allegheny blackwood, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and opposite, ovate leaves with prominently toothed margins. The tree produces small, yellow-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
