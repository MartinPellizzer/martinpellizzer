Ferula communis var. nodiflora

Ferula communis var. nodiflora, commonly known as the nodding ferula, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic resin that has been used historically in traditional medicine. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

This species is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in sunny, open habitats.
