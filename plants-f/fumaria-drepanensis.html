Fumaria drepanensis

Fumaria drepanensis, commonly known as the Mediterranean smokeplant, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and delicate, fern-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and historical use in traditional medicine, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
