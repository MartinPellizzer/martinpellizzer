Ficus infectoria var. caulocarpa

Ficus infectoria var. caulocarpa, commonly known as the African fig or the red fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its distinctive red, fleshy fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant has a thick, woody stem and large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

The fruit contains a single seed surrounded by a sweet, edible pulp, making it both valuable for its botanical features and its utility in local cultures.
