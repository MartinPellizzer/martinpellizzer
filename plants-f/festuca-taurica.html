Festuca taurica

Festuca taurica, commonly known as the Crimean fescue, is a perennial grass native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It thrives in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Festuca taurica is also known for its hardiness and ability to withstand drought conditions.
