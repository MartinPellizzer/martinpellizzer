Ficus grewiifolia

Ficus grewiifolia, commonly known as the Variegated Ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white or cream variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible figs, which are a key feature of the genus Ficus. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ornamental value.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped and can grow quite large, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
