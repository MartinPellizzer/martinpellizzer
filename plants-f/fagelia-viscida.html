Fagelia viscida

Fagelia viscida, commonly known as the sticky fagelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that are often covered in a sticky, resinous substance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
