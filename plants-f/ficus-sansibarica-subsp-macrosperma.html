Ficus sansibarica subsp. macrosperma

Ficus sansibarica subsp.

macrosperma, commonly known as the East African fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa. It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests and mangrove swamps.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
