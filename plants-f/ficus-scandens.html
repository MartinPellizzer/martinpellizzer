Ficus scandens

Ficus scandens, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its long, drooping branches that give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are glossy, opposite, and typically heart-shaped, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Ficus scandens produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish fruits that mature to a yellowish or orange color.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in indoor environments.
