Fagelia tetragona

Fagelia tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled fagelia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive quadrangular stems and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by woody, egg-shaped fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in moist, shaded environments.

Fagelia tetragona is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
