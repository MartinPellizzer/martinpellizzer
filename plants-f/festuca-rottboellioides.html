Festuca rottboellioides

Festuca rottboellioides, commonly known as Rottboelli's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces spikelets that are usually pale green to reddish in color, with bracts that are shorter than the flowering structures. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cool, dry climates.

Festuca rottboellioides is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
