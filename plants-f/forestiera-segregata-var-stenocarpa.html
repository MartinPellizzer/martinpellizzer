Forestiera segregata var. stenocarpa

Forestiera segregata var. stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-fruited forestiera, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its small, aromatic leaves and clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature and turn reddish-brown when dry. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid environments.
