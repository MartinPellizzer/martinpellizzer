Ficus phatnophylla var. glochidioides

Ficus phatnophylla var. glochidioides, commonly known as the Glochidioides fig, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fig fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-draining soil and indirect sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
