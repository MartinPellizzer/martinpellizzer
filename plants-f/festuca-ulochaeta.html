Festuca ulochaeta

Festuca ulochaeta, commonly known as the narrowleaf fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color and forms dense, low-growing clumps. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Festuca ulochaeta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
