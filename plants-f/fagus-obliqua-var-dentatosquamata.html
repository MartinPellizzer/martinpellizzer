Fagus obliqua var. dentatosquamata

Fagus obliqua var. dentatosquamata, commonly known as the serrated scale-barked beech, is a variant of the beech tree native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive bark that features a combination of scales and serrations, providing it with a unique appearance. The leaves are typically oval to elliptic, with doubly serrated margins and a glossy upper surface. This species thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland to montane forests.

Its ecological importance includes supporting a variety of wildlife and contributing to forest biodiversity.
