Ficus impressa

Ficus impressa, commonly known as the warty fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, warty bark and glossy, dark green leaves that are often lobed or wavy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Ficus impressa is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of soil types and light conditions.
