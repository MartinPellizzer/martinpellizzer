Festuca kozanensis

Festuca kozanensis, commonly known as Kozan bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Turkey.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dense foliage, typically forming compact clumps. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

Its ornamental value and adaptability have made it a subject of interest in horticultural and ecological studies.
