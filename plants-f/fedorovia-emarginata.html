Fedorovia emarginata

Fedorovia emarginata, commonly known as the dwarf apple tree, is a small, deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves and small, apple-like fruits. The plant produces white to pink flowers in early spring, which are followed by edible berries that ripen in late summer. Fedorovia emarginata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to a variety of soil types.

It is also valued for its role in wildlife habitat and as a source of food for birds and small mammals.
