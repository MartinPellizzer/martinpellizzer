Freycinetia minahassae

Freycinetia minahassae, commonly known as the Minahassa palm, is a species of palm native to Indonesia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in hanging clusters, followed by bright red fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The Minahassa palm is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its aesthetic qualities.
