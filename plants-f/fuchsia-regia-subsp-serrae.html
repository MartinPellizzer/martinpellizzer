Fuchsia regia subsp. serrae

Fuchsia regia subsp.

serrae, commonly known as the serrated royal fuchsia, is a striking plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is characterized by its vibrant, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep red to purple, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat.

The plant features elongated, narrow leaves with serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
