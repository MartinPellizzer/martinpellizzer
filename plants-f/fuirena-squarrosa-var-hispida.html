Fuirena squarrosa var. hispida

Fuirena squarrosa var. hispida, commonly known as the hairy cushion plant, is a succulent species native to the Canary Islands.

It features dense, compact growth with fleshy, spiny leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a subtle ornamental touch. Its ability to store water in its thick leaves makes it well adapted to arid environments.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements.
