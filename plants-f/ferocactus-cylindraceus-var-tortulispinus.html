Ferocactus cylindraceus var. tortulispinus

Ferocactus cylindraceus var. tortulispinus, commonly known as the Mexican apple cactus, is a distinctive cactus species native to Mexico.

It features a cylindrical stem with prominent ribs and spines that are often reddish in color. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, apple-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for arid and semi-arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appearance and hardy nature.
