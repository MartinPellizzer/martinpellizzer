Ficus borneensis

Ficus borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Borneo.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered fruits known as figs, which are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
