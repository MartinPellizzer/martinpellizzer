Forrestia nigricans

Forrestia nigricans, commonly known as the black-flowered forestiera, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and showy blooms, which appear in late spring to early summer.
