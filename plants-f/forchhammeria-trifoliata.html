Forchhammeria trifoliata

Forchhammeria trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers in early summer, which are borne on short, hairy stems. It is well adapted to harsh, cold environments, with a thick, waxy cuticle that helps reduce water loss.

Forchhammeria trifoliata is also known for its ability to thrive in poor, rocky soils, making it a common sight in tundra and mountainous habitats.
