Festuca bromoides var. uniglumis

Festuca bromoides var. uniglumis, commonly known as slender bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green blades and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as prairies and rocky soils. It has narrow, cylindrical leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained conditions.
