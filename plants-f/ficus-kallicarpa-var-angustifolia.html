Ficus kallicarpa var. angustifolia

Ficus kallicarpa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are usually green or yellow when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
