Freesia odorata

Freesia odorata, commonly known as the freesia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, producing vibrant colors such as yellow, orange, pink, and white. Its foliage consists of long, narrow leaves that emerge from the base of the plant.

Freesias are popular in gardens and floral arrangements due to their pleasant scent and showy appearance.
