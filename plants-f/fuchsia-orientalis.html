Fuchsia orientalis

Fuchsia orientalis, commonly known as the Chinese fuchsia, is a deciduous shrub native to China and Korea.

It features showy, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep violet to bright red, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Fuchsia orientalis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscape design for its vibrant floral displays.
