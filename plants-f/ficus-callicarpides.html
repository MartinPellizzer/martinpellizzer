Ficus callicarpides

Ficus callicarpides, commonly known as the sweet fig or black fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of India, China, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered fruits that are typically green when immature and turn black when ripe. Ficus callicarpides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types makes it a versatile plant for both indoor and outdoor environments.
