Feuilleea longiflora

Feuilleea longiflora, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments with high air moisture. The plant produces clusters of pale lavender flowers with long, slender petals that bloom during the summer months. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to nutrient-poor soils, allowing it to flourish in understory conditions.

Feuilleea longiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is prized in horticultural collections for its unique foliage and delicate floral display.
