Ficus vitifolia

Ficus vitifolia, commonly known as the grape fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy underneath. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, grape-like fruits.

Ficus vitifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable climates.
