Filipendula palmata subsp. angustiloba

Filipendula palmata subsp.

angustiloba, commonly known as narrow-leaved false buttonweed, is a perennial herb native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and small, white to pale pink flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils along stream banks and in wet meadows. It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in wetland environments.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
