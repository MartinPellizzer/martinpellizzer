Frankenia corymbosa var. ifniensis

Frankenia corymbosa var. ifniensis, commonly known as the Cape Verde ironwood, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands and Cape Verde.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has a deep root system, allowing it to thrive in arid and rocky environments. Its leaves are typically leathery and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its pale flowers.

This variety is valued for its hardiness and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
