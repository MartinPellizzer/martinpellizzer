Ficinia rigida

Ficinia rigida, commonly known as rigid ficinia, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its stiff, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along water bodies. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, typical of the sedge family.

Ficinia rigida plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
