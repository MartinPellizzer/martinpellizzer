Festuca spadicea var. cebennensis

Festuca spadicea var. cebennensis, commonly known as the Cebenna fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces spikelets that are typically pale green to reddish in color, with a distinctive spadiceate inflorescence. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in rocky environments.
