Fimbristylis annua f. novae-britanniae

Fimbristylis annua f. novae-britanniae, commonly known as the New Britain annual sedge, is a variant of the annual sedge species native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in wetlands or along streambanks. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in coastal and lowland areas.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
