Ficus gramalotensis

Ficus gramalotensis, commonly known as the Gramalot fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. It produces edible figs that are typically green when mature, though they may change color depending on the variety.

Ficus gramalotensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in humid, warm climates.
