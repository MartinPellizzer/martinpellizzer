Festuca panciciana

Festuca panciciana, commonly known as the Pančić's fescue, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered with a dense layer of hairs. This plant thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Festuca panciciana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
