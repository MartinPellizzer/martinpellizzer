Ficus glycicarpa

Ficus glycicarpa, commonly known as the sweet fig or Indian date fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. The fruit is edible, with a sweet, sticky pulp that is sometimes used in traditional foods and beverages.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
