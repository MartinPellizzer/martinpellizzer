Fallopia dumetorum var. subalata

Fallopia dumetorum var. subalata, commonly known as the alpine knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright, slender stems that often have a reddish or purple hue, especially in the autumn. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which appear in late summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

This variant is typically found in moist, rocky habitats at high elevations.
