Frangula polymorpha var. glabra

Frangula polymorpha var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous bark and small, yellowish-green leaves that are alternate along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, fleshy fruits. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along stream banks.

The species is noted for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
