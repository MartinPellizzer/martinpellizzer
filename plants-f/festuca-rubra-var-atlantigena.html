Festuca rubra var. atlantigena

Festuca rubra var. atlantigena, commonly known as the Atlantic fine fescue, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions of Europe and North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that are resistant to drought and salt spray. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in low-maintenance landscaping and restoration projects.

Its adaptability to harsh environments makes it a valuable species for ecological and horticultural applications.
