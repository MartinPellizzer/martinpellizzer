Ficus hartii var. tumacana

Ficus hartii var. tumacana, commonly known as the Mexican rubber fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to southern Mexico.

It features glossy, dark green, elliptical leaves that are often clustered at the ends of its branches. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, greenish-white fruits. It is valued in horticulture for its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in indoor environments.

Its rubbery sap, which is milky and sticky, is a characteristic feature of the Ficus genus.
