Ficus edulis var. dentata

Ficus edulis var. dentata, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually smooth or slightly toothed, depending on the variety. The tree produces a fruit that is rich in carbohydrates and has been a vital food source for many cultures. Its flowering and fruiting cycles are influenced by seasonal changes and environmental conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
