Ficus xichouensis

Ficus xichouensis, commonly known as the Xichou fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to China, specifically the Yunnan province, and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
