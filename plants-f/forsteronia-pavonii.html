Forsteronia pavonii

Forsteronia pavonii, commonly known as the purple orchid, is a striking member of the Orchidaceae family native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant purple and violet flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns and a velvety texture. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and leathery, providing structural support and aiding in water retention.

Forsteronia pavonii is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in greenhouses and botanical gardens worldwide.
