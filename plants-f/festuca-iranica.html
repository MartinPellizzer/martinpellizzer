Festuca iranica

Festuca iranica, commonly known as Iranian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers.

Festuca iranica is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in landscaping and restoration projects.
