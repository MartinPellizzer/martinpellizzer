Fernandezia lunifera

Fernandezia lunifera, commonly known as the moonflower fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that resemble the patterns of moonlight. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical rainforests. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, typical of many ferns, and it relies on spores for propagation.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Fernandezia lunifera is considered a vulnerable species.
