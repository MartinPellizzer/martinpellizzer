Fremontodendron

Fremontodendron, commonly known as the California fuchsia, is a genus of flowering plants native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to orange and pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with woody stems and thick, leathery leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.

Fremontodendron is valued in gardens for its long-lasting blooms and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and butterflies.
