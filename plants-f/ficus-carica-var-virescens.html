Ficus carica var. virescens

Ficus carica var. virescens, commonly known as the green fig or common fig, is a cultivar of the fig tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green, often with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, green figs that ripen to a sweet, honey-like flavor when mature. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variety is widely cultivated for both ornamental purposes and its edible fruit.
