Frenela gunnii var. mucronata

Frenela gunnii var. mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fiddlewood, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-branched stems and small, elliptical leaves with a glossy upper surface. The flowers are typically pale yellow or greenish-yellow, arranged in clusters, and are followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
