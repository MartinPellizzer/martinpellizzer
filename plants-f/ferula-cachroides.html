Ferula cachroides

Ferula cachroides, commonly known as the Cachroid's giant fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, feathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its aromatic seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Ferula cachroides is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils in arid environments.
