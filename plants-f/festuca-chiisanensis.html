Festuca chiisanensis

Festuca chiisanensis, commonly known as the Japanese fine fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fine, slender blades and compact growth habit, making it well-suited for ornamental landscaping. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and prefers well-drained soils. Its foliage is often a deep green, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences in the spring.

Festuca chiisanensis is valued for its hardiness and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
