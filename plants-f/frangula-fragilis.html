Frangula fragilis

Frangula fragilis, commonly known as the delicate buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often branching stems and small, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, black berries that are a source of food for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Frangula fragilis is noted for its medicinal uses, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
