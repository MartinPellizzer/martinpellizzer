Ficus phatnophylla var. meiocarpa

Ficus phatnophylla var. meiocarpa, commonly known as the small-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically small and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
