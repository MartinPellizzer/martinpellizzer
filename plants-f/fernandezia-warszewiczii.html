Fernandezia warszewiczii, commonly known as the Andean fern, is a unique member of the Davalliaceae family.

It is a climbing epiphytic fern that thrives in the cloud forests of South America, particularly in the Andes. Its reproductive structures are specialized, with sporangia clustered on the underside of its fronds.

Fernandezia warszewiczii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its aesthetic and ecological significance.
