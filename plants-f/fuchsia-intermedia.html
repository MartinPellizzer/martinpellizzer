Fuchsia intermedia

Fuchsia intermedia, commonly known as the common fuchsia, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pendulous flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Fuchsia intermedia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
