Ficus glaucescens

Ficus glaucescens, commonly known as the blue fig or blue rubber fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a bluish tinge, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fig-like fruits that are typically green or yellow when mature.

Ficus glaucescens is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
