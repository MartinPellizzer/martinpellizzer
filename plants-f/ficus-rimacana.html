Ficus rimacana

Ficus rimacana, commonly known as the Rimacana fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible figs.

Ficus rimacana is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
