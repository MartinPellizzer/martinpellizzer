Festuca beckeri subsp. laeviuscula

Festuca beckeri subsp.

laeviuscula, commonly known as Becker's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils.

The plant has slender, erect stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is usually borne at the tip of the stem.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
