Franciscea augusta

Franciscea augusta, commonly known as the silverleaf franciscea, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, woolly hairs. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale pink. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub habitats.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
