Festuca occitanica

Festuca occitanica, commonly known as the Pyrenean fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in cool, moist environments with well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Festuca occitanica plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
