Ficus americana subsp. guianensis

Ficus americana subsp.

guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan fig, is a tropical tree native to South America. It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant produces edible figs that are enclosed in a fleshy, greenish husk. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for various wildlife species.
