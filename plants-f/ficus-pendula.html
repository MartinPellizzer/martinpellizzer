Ficus pendula

Ficus pendula, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its long, drooping branches that give it a cascading appearance. The plant has simple, opposite, and usually glossy dark green leaves. It produces small, greenish flowers that are followed by small, round fruits.

Ficus pendula is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
