Ferolia travancorica

Ferolia travancorica, commonly known as the Travancore lime, is a small evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, smooth, greyish bark and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by round, sour-tasting fruits. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is relatively rare and requires specific environmental conditions to thrive.
