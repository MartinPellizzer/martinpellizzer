Festuca kamtschatica

Festuca kamtschatica, commonly known as Kamtschatka fescue, is a perennial grass native to the cold climates of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, dark green leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

Festuca kamtschatica is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand poor soil conditions.
