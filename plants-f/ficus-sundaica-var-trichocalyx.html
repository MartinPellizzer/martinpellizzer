Ficus sundaica var. trichocalyx

Ficus sundaica var. trichocalyx, commonly known as the hairy-leaf fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tropical evergreen tree characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving them a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
