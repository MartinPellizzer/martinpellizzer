Filipendula ulmaria f. nivea

Filipendula ulmaria f. nivea, commonly known as white meadow sweet, is a botanical variant of the common meadow sweet.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant native to moist meadows and wetlands in temperate regions. The plant features a tall, branching stem with heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. Its flowers are often covered in a fine, white down, giving it a fluffy appearance.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
