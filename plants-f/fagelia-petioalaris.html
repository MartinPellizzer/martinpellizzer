Fagelia petioalaris

Fagelia petioalaris, commonly known as the petiole fern, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive petiole-like structures that resemble modified leaves, which are often mistaken for stems. The plant exhibits a fern-like appearance with delicate, feathery fronds that grow from the base of the plant. Its botanical features include a rhizomatous growth habit and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden among the foliage.

Due to its limited distribution and specialized habitat requirements, Fagelia petioalaris is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
