Ficus gigantifolia

Ficus gigantifolia, commonly known as the giant fig tree, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Its extensive root system makes it a valuable plant for soil stabilization and erosion control.
