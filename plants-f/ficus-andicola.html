Ficus andicola

Ficus andicola, commonly known as the Andean fig, is a species of flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous terrain, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. Its fruit, a small fig-like structure, is an important food source for various wildlife species.

Ficus andicola plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to biodiversity and soil stabilization.
