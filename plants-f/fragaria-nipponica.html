Fragaria nipponica

Fragaria nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese strawberry, is a species native to East Asia.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant with trifoliate leaves and produces small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forests and mountainous regions. Its flowers are white with five petals and are pollinated by insects.

The fruit develops from the flower and is characterized by its sweet, tangy flavor and small size.
