Fragaria collina var. cerinoalba

Fragaria collina var. cerinoalba, commonly known as the alpine strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It produces small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by edible, sweet-tart berries. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in alpine meadows and rocky soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a hairy texture, and the stems are often covered in fine, white pubescence.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in local cuisines.
