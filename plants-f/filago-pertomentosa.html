Filago pertomentosa

Filago pertomentosa, commonly known as the sticky filago, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, clustered yellow flowers and hairy, glandular stems that give it a sticky texture. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are alternate, linear, and often covered with fine hairs.

Filago pertomentosa is part of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
