Ficus buntaensis

Ficus buntaensis, commonly known as the Buntaensis fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and distinctive aerial roots. The plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Ficus buntaensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor conditions.
