Fimbristylis thouarsii

Fimbristylis thouarsii, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikerush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, cylindrical stems and small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist environments such as wetlands and marshes. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into dry, three-lobed capsules.

Its structure and growth habits make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
