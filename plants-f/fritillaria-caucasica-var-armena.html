Fritillaria caucasica var. armena

Fritillaria caucasica var. armena, commonly known as Armenian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and adjacent areas.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or orange in color, with a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or forested habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.

The species is also noted for its resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
