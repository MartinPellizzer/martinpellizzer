Fimbristylis prolifera

Fimbristylis prolifera, commonly known as the spreading flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands. Its flowering stems produce tiny, three-lobed flowers that are often subtended by bracts.

Fimbristylis prolifera is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
