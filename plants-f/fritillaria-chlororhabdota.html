Fritillaria chlororhabdota

Fritillaria chlororhabdota, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or yellow in color, with a distinctive greenish hue. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cold winter dormancy period to bloom successfully. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine and horticulture.

The species is characterized by its unique leaf structure, which consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from the base of the plant.
