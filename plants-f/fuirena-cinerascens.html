Fuirena cinerascens

Fuirena cinerascens, commonly known as the grayleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery-gray pubescence. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets and are usually green or brown in color.

Fuirena cinerascens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
