Floscopa peruviana

Floscopa peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, violet or purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or mountainous terrain. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while its stems are erect and fibrous.

Floscopa peruviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
