Festuca violacea var. cappadocica

Festuca violacea var. cappadocica, commonly known as the Cappadocian bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the Cappadocia region of Turkey.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green foliage and striking silver-blue inflorescence that appears in late spring. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and ability to withstand drought.

This variety is also noted for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and forage for various insects and small animals.
