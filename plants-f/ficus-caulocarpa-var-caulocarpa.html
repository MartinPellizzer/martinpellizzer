Ficus caulocarpa var. caulocarpa

Ficus caulocarpa var. caulocarpa, commonly known as the rock fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that resemble figs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky or cliff faces.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
