Ficus sumatrana

Ficus sumatrana, commonly known as the Sumatran fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Indonesian island of Sumatra and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus sumatrana is also valued for its ability to tolerate indoor conditions, making it a popular choice for houseplants.
