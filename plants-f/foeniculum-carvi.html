Foeniculum carvi

Foeniculum carvi, commonly known as caraway, is a flowering plant in the umbelliferous family.

It is native to Europe and Western Asia and is characterized by its aromatic, yellow-green leaves and small white flowers. The plant produces small, spherical fruits called "caraway seeds," which are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. The essential oils found in the seeds contain compounds like limonene and carvone, contributing to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a common garden plant.
