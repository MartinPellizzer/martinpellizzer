Ficus cuneatonervosa

Ficus cuneatonervosa, commonly known as the narrow-veined fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with prominent, parallel veins. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Ficus cuneatonervosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable environments.
