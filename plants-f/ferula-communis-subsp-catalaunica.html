Ferula communis subsp. catalaunica

Ferula communis subsp.

catalaunica, commonly known as the Catalan giant fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, feathery leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length.

The plant produces yellow flowers in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky areas.
