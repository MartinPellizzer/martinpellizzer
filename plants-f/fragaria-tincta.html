Fragaria tincta

Fragaria tincta, commonly known as the violet strawberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, edible berries that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are usually pale pink or white, with five petals and numerous stamens.

The fruit develops from the flower and is often used in culinary applications due to its mild flavor and aromatic qualities.
