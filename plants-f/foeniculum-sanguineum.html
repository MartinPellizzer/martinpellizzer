Foeniculum sanguineum

Foeniculum sanguineum, commonly known as red fennel, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of the Mediterranean.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill. The plant is characterized by its feathery leaves and purple to red flowers that bloom in late summer. Its seeds are often used in culinary applications, adding a licorice-like flavor to dishes.
