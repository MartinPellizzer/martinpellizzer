Fraxinus viridis var. berlandieriana

Fraxinus viridis var. berlandieriana, commonly known as the Berlandier green ash, is a variant of the green ash tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its finely serrated leaves that are typically a vibrant green on the upper surface and lighter green underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging environmental conditions.
