Ficus geocarpa var. uncinata

Ficus geocarpa var. uncinata, commonly known as the underground fig, is a unique variant of the ficus genus characterized by its subterranean fruiting structures.

This plant produces figs that develop beneath the soil surface, making them difficult to detect without careful observation. The leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a waxy texture, while the stems are often woody and branched. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is native to parts of Southeast Asia.

Due to its unusual growth habit, it is a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
