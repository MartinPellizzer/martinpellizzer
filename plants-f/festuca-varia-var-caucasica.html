Festuca varia var. caucasica

Festuca varia var. caucasica, commonly known as Caucasian variable fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually green to purplish in color.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cool, humid climates.
