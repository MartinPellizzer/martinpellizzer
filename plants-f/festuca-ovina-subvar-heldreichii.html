Festuca ovina subvar. heldreichii

Festuca ovina subvar. heldreichii, commonly known as the Heldreich's sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, wiry leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in alpine settings.
