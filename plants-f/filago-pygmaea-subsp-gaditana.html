Filago pygmaea subsp. gaditana

Filago pygmaea subsp.

gaditana, commonly known as the Iberian dwarf filago, is a small, herbaceous plant native to the Iberian Peninsula. It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as rocky soils and scrublands.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring. It is characterized by its compact size and ability to thrive in arid conditions.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and vulnerable species.
