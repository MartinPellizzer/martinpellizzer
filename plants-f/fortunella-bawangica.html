Fortunella bawangica

Fortunella bawangica, commonly known as the garlic orange, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, citrus-like leaves and small, edible fruits. The plant produces yellow-orange berries that are rich in vitamin C and have a distinct garlic-like flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is often cultivated in gardens and for local consumption.
