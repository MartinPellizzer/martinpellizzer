Ficus karet

Ficus karet, commonly known as the rubber fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by green, fleshy fruits that turn yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Ficus karet is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has a long history of use in traditional medicine.
