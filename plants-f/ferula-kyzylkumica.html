Ferula kyzylkumica

Ferula kyzylkumica, commonly known as the Kyzylkum wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in a panicle, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Ferula kyzylkumica is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
