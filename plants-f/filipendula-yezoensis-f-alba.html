Filipendula yezoensis f. alba

Filipendula yezoensis f. alba, commonly known as white meadow sweet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its botanical features include a hollow stem and a distinctive, aromatic scent.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
