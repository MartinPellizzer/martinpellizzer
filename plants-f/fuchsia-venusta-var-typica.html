Fuchsia venusta var. typica

Fuchsia venusta var. typica, commonly known as the typical fuchsia, is a species native to South America, particularly the Andes region.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy flowers that range in color from deep purple to pink. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine, silvery pubescence.

Fuchsia venusta var. typica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
