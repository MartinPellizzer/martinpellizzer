Fabiana imbricata var. biflora

Fabiana imbricata var. biflora, commonly known as the twin-flower fabiana, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Argentina and Chile.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments. The plant features small, clustered white or pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
