Fragaria chinensis

Fragaria chinensis, commonly known as Chinese strawberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant produces small, edible berries that are similar in appearance to the common strawberry but have a distinct flavor. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.

The leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets, and the plant typically grows in moist, temperate climates.
