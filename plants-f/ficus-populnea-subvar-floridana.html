Ficus populnea subvar. floridana

Ficus populnea subvar. floridana, commonly known as the Florida fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits. It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly Florida, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

This subspecies is often found in wetlands and along the edges of water bodies, where it plays an important role in local ecosystems.
