Frasera japonica

Frasera japonica, commonly known as the Japanese frasera, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a member of the gentian family (Gentianaceae) and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds.

Frasera japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive floral display.
