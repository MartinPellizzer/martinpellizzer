Fuchsia rosea

Fuchsia rosea, commonly known as the common fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from deep purple to pink and white. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Fuchsia rosea is valued for its vibrant blooms and is a popular choice for gardens and hanging baskets.
