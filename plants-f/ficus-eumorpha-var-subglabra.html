Ficus eumorpha var. subglabra

Ficus eumorpha var. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved fig, is a variant of the Ficus eumorpha species.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including regions of India and Indonesia. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical rainforests.

The leaves are usually a deep green color and can reach lengths of up to 15 centimeters.
