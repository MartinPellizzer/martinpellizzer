Fabricia glauca

Fabricia glauca, commonly known as the blue morning glory, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers that bloom in the early morning. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Its rapid growth and ornamental value make it both a desirable and sometimes invasive species in certain areas.
