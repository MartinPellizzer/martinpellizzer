Ficus longibracteata var. lebetoides

Ficus longibracteata var. lebetoides, commonly known as the lebetoides fig.

This variant is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are edible when ripe, though they are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid environments with indirect light and requires consistent moisture in the soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a decorative plant in tropical landscapes.
