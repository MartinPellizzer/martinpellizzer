Fuchsia × mutabilis

Fuchsia × mutabilis, commonly known as the variable fuchsia, is a hybrid shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pendulous flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.
