Fritillaria wabuensis

Fritillaria wabuensis, commonly known as the Wabu fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Turkey.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or reddish in color, often with intricate patterns. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in harsh, alpine environments, often remaining dormant during dry periods.

Fritillaria wabuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
