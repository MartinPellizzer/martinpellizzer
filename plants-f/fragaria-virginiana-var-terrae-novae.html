Fragaria virginiana var. terrae-novae

Fragaria virginiana var. terrae-novae, commonly known as the Newfoundland strawberry, is a wild strawberry species native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its small, red, edible fruits that are typically smaller than those of the common strawberry. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces white flowers with five petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in local cuisine.
