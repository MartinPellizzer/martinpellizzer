Ficus brevipedicellata

Ficus brevipedicellata, commonly known as the short-stemmed fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered leaves and unique, short pedicels on its infructescences. It produces edible figs that are typically consumed fresh or used in local cuisines.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
