Forrestia gracilis

Forrestia gracilis, commonly known as the slender forestia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodland areas. The plant features slender, arching stems with opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, fragrant white flowers appear in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in partially shaded environments.
