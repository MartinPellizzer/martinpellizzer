Fagara fruticosa

Fagara fruticosa, commonly known as the purple prickly ash, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its dense, thorny stems and small, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by berries that range in color from red to black. It is often found in moist, shaded areas within forests and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.

Fagara fruticosa plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
