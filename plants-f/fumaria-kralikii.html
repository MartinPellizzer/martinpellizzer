Fumaria kralikii

Fumaria kralikii, commonly known as the Kralik's smokeplant, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate flowers that resemble smoke. The plant produces yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer, and its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs. Fumaria kralikii thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found growing in meadows, along roadsides, and in woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for various purposes.
