Fraxinus schiedeana

Fraxinus schiedeana, commonly known as the Mexican ash, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-trunked form. The tree has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin and a pointed tip. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are borne in drooping clusters and are followed by winged fruits that disperse via wind.

The bark is typically smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.
