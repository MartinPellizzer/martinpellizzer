Festuca ovina var. montana

Festuca ovina var. montana, commonly known as mountain sheep fescue, is a perennial grass species native to mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, narrow leaf blades, and dense, dark green foliage. This plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. It is cold-hardy and drought-tolerant, making it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often tinged with silver or blue, adding ornamental value to the plant.
