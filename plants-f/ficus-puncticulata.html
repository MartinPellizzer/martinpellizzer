Ficus puncticulata

Ficus puncticulata, commonly known as the punctuated fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a distinctive pattern of small, raised dots. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus puncticulata is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and adaptability.
