Flaveria longifolia var. subtomentosa

Flaveria longifolia var. subtomentosa, commonly known as the silky flaveria, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive, silvery appearance. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in dry, sunny environments and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
