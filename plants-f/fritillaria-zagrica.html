Fritillaria zagrica

Fritillaria zagrica, commonly known as the Zagreb fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers, which range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its bulbs are underground and serve as storage organs for nutrients.

Fritillaria zagrica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
