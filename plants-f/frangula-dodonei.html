Frangula dodonei

Frangula dodonei, commonly known as the Dodonean buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are typically consumed by birds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Frangula dodonei is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
