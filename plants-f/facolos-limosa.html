Facolos limosa

Facolos limosa, commonly known as the marsh hibiscus, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to wetland and marshy environments.

It features showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, with five lobes and a prominent throat. The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine, soft hairs. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

This plant plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
