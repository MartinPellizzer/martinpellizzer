Ficus villosa var. tonsa

Ficus villosa var. tonsa, commonly known as the woolly fig or velvet fig, is a species native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, round figs. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.
