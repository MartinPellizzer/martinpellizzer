Fagus philipensis

Fagus philipensis, commonly known as the Philippine beech, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of the Philippines.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and chestnuts, and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, cupule-enclosed nuts that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in secondary forests and along stream banks.
