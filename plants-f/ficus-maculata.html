Ficus maculata

Ficus maculata, commonly known as the variegated fig tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tropical evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white or yellow variegation. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus maculata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
