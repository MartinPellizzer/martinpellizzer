Festuca hitchcockiana

Festuca hitchcockiana, commonly known as Hitchcock's fescue, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, often forming dense mats. This plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.

Festuca hitchcockiana is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
