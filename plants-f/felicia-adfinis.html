Felicia adfinis

Felicia adfinis, commonly known as the blue-eyed daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its bright blue or violet flowers with a white center. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Felicia adfinis is often cultivated as a garden plant due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.

It is drought-tolerant and prefers full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.
