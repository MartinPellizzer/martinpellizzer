Ficus bistipulata

Ficus bistipulata, commonly known as the two-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its distinctive leaves, which have two distinct leaflets, giving it its scientific name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring warm, humid environments with indirect sunlight.

Ficus bistipulata is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
