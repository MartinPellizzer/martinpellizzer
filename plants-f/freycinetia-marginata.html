Freycinetia marginata

Freycinetia marginata, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, showy bracts that resemble petals. The plant typically grows along coastal areas, where it thrives in sandy or rocky soils. Its flowers are usually bright yellow or orange and bloom during the warmer months.

Freycinetia marginata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to withstand saline and wind conditions.
