Fallopia ciliinervis

Fallopia ciliinervis, commonly known as the Chinese knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features tall, hollow stems that are often reddish in color and covered with fine, silky hairs. The leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and have a distinctive ciliate margin, giving it its scientific name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months.

This plant is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
