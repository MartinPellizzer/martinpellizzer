Festuca rubra var. iberica

Festuca rubra var. iberica, commonly known as the Iberian red fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green to reddish foliage, which can turn bronze in winter. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in open woodland and grassland habitats.

Festuca rubra var. iberica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
