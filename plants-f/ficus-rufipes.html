Ficus rufipes

Ficus rufipes, commonly known as the red-stemmed fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown stems and heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Ficus rufipes is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
