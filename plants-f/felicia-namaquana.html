Felicia namaquana

Felicia namaquana, commonly known as the Namibian daisy, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other composite flowers. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit, with small, daisy-like yellow flowers surrounded by white ray florets. Felicia namaquana is adapted to survive in dry conditions, featuring succulent leaves that store water.

It is often used in rock gardens and xeriscapes due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
