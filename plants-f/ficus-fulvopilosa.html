Ficus fulvopilosa

Ficus fulvopilosa, commonly known as the golden fig or golden-leaved fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a golden or yellowish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus fulvopilosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
