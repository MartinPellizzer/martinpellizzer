Ficus sciaphila

Ficus sciaphila, commonly known as the shade fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and have a smooth, waxy texture. It thrives in low-light conditions, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Ficus sciaphila produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by small, edible fruits.
