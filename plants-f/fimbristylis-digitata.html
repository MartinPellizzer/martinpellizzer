Fimbristylis digitata

Fimbristylis digitata, commonly known as the fingered sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along water bodies. Fimbristylis digitata has a fibrous root system and produces spikelets that are often covered in fine hairs.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms in its native environments.
