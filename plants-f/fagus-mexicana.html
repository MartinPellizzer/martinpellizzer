Fagus mexicana

Fagus mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican beech, is a deciduous tree native to the highlands of Mexico.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and chestnuts, and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, cup-shaped acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates with adequate rainfall.
