Freycinetia globosa

Freycinetia globosa, commonly known as the globe-leaf pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its robust, fibrous stems and large, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that are typically borne in dense inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and for thatching, while the plant itself is adapted to coastal and wetland environments.

Freycinetia globosa plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
