Forbesina eriiformis

Forbesina eriiformis, commonly known as the woolly leaf orchid, is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its flowers are small and clustered, usually in shades of white and green, and bloom during the wet season.

Forbesina eriiformis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
