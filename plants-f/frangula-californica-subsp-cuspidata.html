Frangula californica subsp. cuspidata

Frangula californica subsp.

cuspidata, commonly known as the California buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters.

The plant produces small, black berries that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.

Its botanical features make it a notable component of the local flora in its native range.
