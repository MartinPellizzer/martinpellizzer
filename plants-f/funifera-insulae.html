Funifera insulae

Funifera insulae, commonly known as the island funaria, is a species of moss belonging to the family Funariaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, spirally arranged leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces spore capsules that are stalked and often curved, a feature common among members of its family. Funifera insulae thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in forest floors or rocky crevices.

Its simple yet efficient structure allows it to play a vital role in soil stabilization and nutrient cycling in its ecosystem.
