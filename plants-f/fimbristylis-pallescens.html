Fimbristylis pallescens

Fimbristylis pallescens, commonly known as the pale bristle sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and Australia.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense colonies, contributing to soil stabilization in wetland ecosystems.

Fimbristylis pallescens is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in disturbed wetland habitats.
