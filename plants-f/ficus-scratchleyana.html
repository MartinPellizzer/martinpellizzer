Ficus scratchleyana

Ficus scratchleyana, commonly known as the Scratchley's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered fruits that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when mature. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
