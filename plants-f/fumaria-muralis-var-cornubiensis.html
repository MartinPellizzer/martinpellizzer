Fumaria muralis var. cornubiensis

Fumaria muralis var. cornubiensis, commonly known as Cornish wallbine, is a flowering plant native to the British Isles, particularly found in Cornwall and Devon.

It is a member of the Fumariaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows on rocky or limestone walls, thriving in sunny, well-drained conditions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are slender and branched.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive, albeit small, blooms.
