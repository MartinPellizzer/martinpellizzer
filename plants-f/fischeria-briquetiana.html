Fischeria briquetiana

Fischeria briquetiana, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, branched stems and clusters of bright yellow, bell-shaped flowers. The plant has a shallow root system that allows it to thrive in sandy or gravelly soils with minimal water. Its leaves are typically small, fleshy, and arranged in a rosette pattern to reduce water loss.

Fischeria briquetiana is adapted to extreme heat and drought, making it a hardy species in desert environments.
