Fumaria parviflora var. segetalis

Fumaria parviflora var. segetalis, commonly known as the field cress, is a flowering plant in the family Fumariaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces capsule fruits containing numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in fields.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural areas.
