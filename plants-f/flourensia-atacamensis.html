Flourensia atacamensis

Flourensia atacamensis, commonly known as the Atacama flourensia, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of northern Chile.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting various pollinators. It is well adapted to harsh, dry environments, with deep roots and reduced leaf surfaces to minimize water loss.

Due to its ecological significance and unique adaptations, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
