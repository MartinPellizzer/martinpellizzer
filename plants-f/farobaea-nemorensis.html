Farobaea nemorensis

Farobaea nemorensis, commonly known as the Forest Almond.

It is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and showy white or pink flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional herbal remedies. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Farobaea nemorensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in apothecary practices.
