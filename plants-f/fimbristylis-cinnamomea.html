Fimbristylis cinnamomea

Fimbristylis cinnamomea, commonly known as cinnamon sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and dense, grass-like foliage. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps and seasonally flooded areas. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, often with a pale or brownish hue.

Fimbristylis cinnamomea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
