Ficus deltoidea var. kinabaluensis

Ficus deltoidea var. kinabaluensis, commonly known as the Kinabalu fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the highland areas of Borneo.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
