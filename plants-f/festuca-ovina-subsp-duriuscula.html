Festuca ovina subsp. duriuscula

Festuca ovina subsp.

duriuscula, commonly known as the hard fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its fine, dark green foliage and tufted growth habit, which makes it well-suited for ornamental and turfgrass applications.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Festuca ovina subsp.

duriuscula is valued for its resilience and aesthetic appeal in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
