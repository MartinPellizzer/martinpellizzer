Ficus kagerensis

Ficus kagerensis, commonly known as the Kager's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Ficus kagerensis produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests and riverine habitats.
