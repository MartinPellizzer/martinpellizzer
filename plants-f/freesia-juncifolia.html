Freesia juncifolia

Freesia juncifolia, commonly known as the marsh freesia, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features grass-like leaves and produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, yellow, and orange. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

Freesia juncifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
