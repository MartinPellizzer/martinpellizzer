Fragaria grandiflora var. calycina

Fragaria grandiflora var. calycina, commonly known as the California strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant bears small, red, edible fruits similar to strawberries, which develop from the flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Its botanical structure includes a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in suitable habitats.
