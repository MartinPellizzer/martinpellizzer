Faba serratifolia

Faba serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and small, pale purple flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces elongated, narrow pods containing several oval-shaped seeds. It is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to thrive in arid conditions.

Faba serratifolia is valued for its role in soil stabilization and as a forage crop in certain regions.
