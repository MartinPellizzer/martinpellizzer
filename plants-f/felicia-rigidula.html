Felicia rigidula

Felicia rigidula, commonly known as the rigid fleabane, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its yellow flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Felicia rigidula is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
