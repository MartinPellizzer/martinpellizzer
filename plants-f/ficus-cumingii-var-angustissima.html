Ficus cumingii var. angustissima

Ficus cumingii var. angustissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy, with a narrow, elongated form. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
