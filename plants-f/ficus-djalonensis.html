Ficus djalonensis

Ficus djalonensis, commonly known as the West African fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to western Africa, particularly found in countries like Guinea, Sierra Leone, and Liberia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, a small fig, is edible and often consumed by local communities.

Ficus djalonensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various animals and contributing to forest biodiversity.
