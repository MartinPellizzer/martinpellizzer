Ficus scabriuscula

Ficus scabriuscula, commonly known as the rough-leaved fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that have a rough, textured surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits.

Ficus scabriuscula is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and air-purifying qualities.
