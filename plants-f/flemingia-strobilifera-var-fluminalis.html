Flemingia strobilifera var. fluminalis

Flemingia strobilifera var. fluminalis, commonly known as the river flemingia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers followed by woody, cone-like fruits containing small, black seeds. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as riverbanks and forest understories.

Due to its habitat preferences, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
