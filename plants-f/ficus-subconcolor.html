Ficus subconcolor

Ficus subconcolor, commonly known as the smooth-leaved fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are smooth and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences called syconia. Its fruit, a small, round fig, is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor.

Ficus subconcolor is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
