Forrestia marginata

Forrestia marginata, commonly known as the coastal forestiera, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and evergreen, providing a dense canopy that helps stabilize dune ecosystems.

The species is well adapted to salt spray and wind, making it an important component of coastal vegetation.
