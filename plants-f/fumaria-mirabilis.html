Fumaria mirabilis

Fumaria mirabilis, commonly known as the common glory-of-the-snow, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its striking, nodding yellow flowers that appear in early spring. The plant has a trailing habit and produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Fumaria mirabilis is known for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
