Ficus ovatifolia

Ficus ovatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, ovate, glossy leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible fig fruits. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus ovatifolia prefers warm, humid conditions and well-draining soil.
