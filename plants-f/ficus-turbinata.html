Ficus turbinata

Ficus turbinata, commonly known as the Turban fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, spirally twisted leaves that give it a unique appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus turbinata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its striking foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
