Festuca malyschevii

Festuca malyschevii, commonly known as Malyschev's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, spikelet-like flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a blue-green hue that becomes more pronounced in cooler weather.

Festuca malyschevii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
