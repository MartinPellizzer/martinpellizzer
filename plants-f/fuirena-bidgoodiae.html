Fuirena bidgoodiae

Fuirena bidgoodiae, commonly known as the Bidgood's firetail, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
