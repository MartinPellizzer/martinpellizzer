Ficus leavensii var. thinophila

Ficus leavensii var. thinophila, commonly known as the slender-leaf fig, is a tropical plant characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, often found in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually glossy and have a slightly waxy texture, while the fruit is small and edible, though not as commonly consumed as other fig varieties.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
