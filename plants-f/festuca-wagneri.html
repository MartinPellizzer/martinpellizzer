Festuca wagneri

Festuca wagneri, commonly known as bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in lawns and landscaping due to its durability and aesthetic appeal.

Festuca wagneri is also valued for its ability to form a dense root system that helps prevent soil erosion.
