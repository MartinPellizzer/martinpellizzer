Ficus hirta var. roxburghii

Ficus hirta var. roxburghii, commonly known as the Roxburgh's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its adaptability makes it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation in suitable environments.
