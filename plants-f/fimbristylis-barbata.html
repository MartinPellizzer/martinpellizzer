Fimbristylis barbata

Fimbristylis barbata, commonly known as the hairy fringed sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often forming dense clusters near water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with the lower leaves often bearing short, stiff hairs.

The inflorescence consists of a spikelet-like cluster of small, three-ranked flowers, which are usually wind-pollinated.
