Ficus pubinervis var. sibulanensis

Ficus pubinervis var. sibulanensis, commonly known as the Sibulan fig, is a tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically yellow or reddish when ripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor cultivation and landscape gardening.
