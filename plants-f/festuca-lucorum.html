Festuca lucorum

Festuca lucorum, commonly known as the silver hair veldtgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Festuca lucorum is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and is also important in ecological restoration projects.
