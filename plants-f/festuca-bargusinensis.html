Festuca bargusinensis

Festuca bargusinensis, commonly known as Bargusin fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs. This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subarctic regions. Festuca bargusinensis plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the vegetation in its native ecosystems.
