Faberia tsiangii

Faberia tsiangii, commonly known as the Tsiang's faberia, is a flowering plant species native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Faberia tsiangii is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Its seed pods split open to release seeds, facilitating its spread in natural habitats.
