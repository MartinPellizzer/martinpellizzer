Ficus scortechinii var. lanceata

Ficus scortechinii var. lanceata, commonly known as the lance-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are often glossy and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

This species is popular as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
