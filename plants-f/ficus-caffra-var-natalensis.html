Ficus caffra var. natalensis

Ficus caffra var. natalensis, commonly known as Natal fig, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in coastal and riverine forests. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and as a houseplant.
