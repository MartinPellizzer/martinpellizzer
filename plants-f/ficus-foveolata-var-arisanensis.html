Ficus foveolata var. arisanensis

Ficus foveolata var. arisanensis, commonly known as the Arisan fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southern Japan, particularly the island of Kyushu. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a distinct indentation at the base. It produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically small and yellowish.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
