Ficus guyeri

Ficus guyeri, commonly known as the silver ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a silvery underside, giving it its common name. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ornamental value.

Ficus guyeri thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate watering to maintain its health.
