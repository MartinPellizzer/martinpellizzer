Festuca rubra f. longifolia

Festuca rubra f. longifolia, commonly known as red fescue longifolia, is a variant of red fescue characterized by its elongated, slender leaves.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in cool, temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant has a clump-forming growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its foliage is often a striking reddish hue, especially under stress or during certain seasons.

This form is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
