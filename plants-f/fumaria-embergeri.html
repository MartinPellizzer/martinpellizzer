Fumaria embergeri

Fumaria embergeri, commonly known as the yellow glory flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, erect stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or borders.

Fumaria embergeri is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
