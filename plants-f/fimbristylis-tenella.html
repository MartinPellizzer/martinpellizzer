Fimbristylis tenella

Fimbristylis tenella, commonly known as slender spikegrass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, often forming dense mats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale brown flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common component of wetland vegetation.

Fimbristylis tenella is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
