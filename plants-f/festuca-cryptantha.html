Festuca cryptantha

Festuca cryptantha, commonly known as the cryptantha fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Festuca cryptantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh, high-altitude conditions.
