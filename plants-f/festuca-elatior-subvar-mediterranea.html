Festuca elatior subvar. mediterranea

Festuca elatior subvar. mediterranea, commonly known as the Mediterranean tall fescue, is a cool-season grass native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, bluish-green foliage. This plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, durable turf.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability to Mediterranean climates and its resistance to drought and heat.
