Fraxinus longicuspis var. sieboldiana

Fraxinus longicuspis var. sieboldiana, commonly known as the Japanese ash, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and compound leaves with long, slender leaflets. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by winged samaras for seed dispersal. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its elegant appearance.
