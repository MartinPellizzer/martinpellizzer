Ficus chirindensis

Ficus chirindensis, commonly known as the Chirindensis fig tree, is a species native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring a broad, spreading canopy. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually oval or elliptical in shape. Its fruit, a fig, is enclosed in a green, fleshy syconium that ripens to a yellow or reddish hue.

Ficus chirindensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine.
