Flickingeria junctiloba

Flickingeria junctiloba, commonly known as the slender leaf orchid, is a herbaceous perennial native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the orchid family, Orchidaceae, and is characterized by its delicate, grass-like foliage and small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its unique botanical features include a sympodial growth habit and specialized root structures that aid in nutrient absorption.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
