Ficus winkleri

Ficus winkleri, commonly known as the Winkler's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into fig-like fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
