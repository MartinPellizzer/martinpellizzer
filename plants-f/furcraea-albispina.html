Furcraea albispina

Furcraea albispina, commonly known as the white-spined sword plant, is a succulent perennial native to Central America.

It is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a white, powdery coating. The plant produces striking, yellowish-green flower spikes that emerge from the base of the leaves. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
