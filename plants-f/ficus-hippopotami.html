Ficus hippopotami

Ficus hippopotami, commonly known as the hippopotamus fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible figs, which are typically consumed raw or cooked. Ficus hippopotami thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
