Fumaria Albiflora

Albiflora fumaria, scientifically known as Fumaria albiflora, is a delicate flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Europe. This species belongs to the Fumariaceae family, commonly known as the glory of the snow family. Its most striking feature is its pale yellow to white flowers, which bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks. Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, Fumaria albiflora has gained attention among botanists and gardeners alike.
