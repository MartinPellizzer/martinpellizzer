Ferocactus echidne subsp. victoriensis

Ferocactus echidne subsp.

victoriensis, commonly known as the Victoria's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in prominent, sharp spines.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Its robust structure and striking appearance make it a popular choice for cactus enthusiasts and xeriscaping.
