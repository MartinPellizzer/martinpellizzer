Fagelia lavandulifolia

Fagelia lavandulifolia, commonly known as the lavenderleaf fagelia, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its aromatic, lance-shaped leaves that emit a subtle lavender scent. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy fruits that contain several seeds. Native to Southeast Asia, it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and unique fragrance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a decorative plant.
