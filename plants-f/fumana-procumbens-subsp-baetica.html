Fumana procumbens subsp. baetica

Fumana procumbens subsp.

baetica, commonly known as the Baetica sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe. It typically features small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters at the ends of its stems.

The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Its botanical characteristics make it well adapted to harsh, dry conditions.
