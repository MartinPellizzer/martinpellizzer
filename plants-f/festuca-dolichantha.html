Festuca dolichantha

Festuca dolichantha, commonly known as the long-leaf fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green blades and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to cool, temperate climates. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a slightly hairy texture, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Festuca dolichantha is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
