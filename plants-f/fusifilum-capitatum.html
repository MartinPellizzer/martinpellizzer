Fusifilum capitatum

Fusifilum capitatum, commonly known as the club-fruited dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, club-shaped seed heads. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and is known for its yellow composite flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
