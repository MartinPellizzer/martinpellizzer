Ferdinandusa

Ferdinandusa, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their compact growth habit and showy, velvety flowers that come in a wide range of colors. The leaves are typically thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments. Ferdinandusa species thrive in bright, indirect light and require well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

They are popular houseplants due to their beauty and relatively easy care requirements.
