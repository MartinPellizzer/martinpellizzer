Fumaria bastardii var. hibernica

Fumaria bastardii var. hibernica, commonly known as the Irish glory-of-the-snow, is a variant of the common fumitory plant.

It is a low-growing annual herb that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in fine hairs, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often slightly hairy. The flowers are small, yellowish, and borne in clusters, with four petals that curve backward.

It is native to western Europe and thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and grassy banks.
