Festuca varia subsp. brachystachys

Festuca varia subsp.

brachystachys, commonly known as the short-stalked feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, silver-green foliage.

The plant produces compact, spike-like inflorescences that are often tinged with reddish hues. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky habitats. Festuca varia subsp.

brachystachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
