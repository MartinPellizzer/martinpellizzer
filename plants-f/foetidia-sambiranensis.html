Foetidia sambiranensis

Foetidia sambiranensis, commonly known as the Sambiranensis Begonia, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Begoniaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety leaves with intricate patterns. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
