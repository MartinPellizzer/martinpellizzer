Franseria artemisioides

Franseria artemisioides, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features silver-gray foliage with a woolly texture, and its showy blue to purple flower heads bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Franseria artemisioides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like butterflies and bees.
