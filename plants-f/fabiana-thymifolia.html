Fabiana thymifolia

Fabiana thymifolia, commonly known as the sweet thyme plant, is a flowering shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its small, pea-like flowers. The plant has aromatic leaves that emit a mild thyme scent, contributing to its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Fabiana thymifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
