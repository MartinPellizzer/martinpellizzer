Fraxinus ornus var. sanguinea

Fraxinus ornus var. sanguinea, commonly known as the crimson weeping ash, is a variant of the common ash tree.

It is characterized by its striking red to orange flowers that appear in clusters, giving it a distinctive appearance in spring. The leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a serrated margin. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
