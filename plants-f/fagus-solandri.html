Fagus solandri

Fagus solandri, commonly known as the New Zealand black beech, is a deciduous tree native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the beech family (Fagaceae) and is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and smooth, bark. The plant produces small, cup-shaped fruits that contain one or two seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in temperate rainforests.

Fagus solandri plays an important ecological role in supporting native wildlife and maintaining forest biodiversity.
