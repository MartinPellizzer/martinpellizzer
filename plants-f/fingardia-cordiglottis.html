Fingardia cordiglottis

Fingardia cordiglottis, commonly known as the heart-shaped leaf plant, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves, which are typically a deep green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, white flowers that bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight to flourish.

Fingardia cordiglottis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements.
