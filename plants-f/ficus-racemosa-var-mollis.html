Ficus racemosa var. mollis

Ficus racemosa var. mollis, commonly known as the silky fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish fruits. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage. It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening in tropical and subtropical regions.
