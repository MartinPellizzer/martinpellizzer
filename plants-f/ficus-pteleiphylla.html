Ficus pteleiphylla

Ficus pteleiphylla, commonly known as the Chinese banyan, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in figs, which are enclosed by a fleshy, greenish calyx. Ficus pteleiphylla is a fast-growing tree that can reach heights of up to 20 meters in its natural habitat.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
