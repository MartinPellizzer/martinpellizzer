Ficus cocculifolia var. sakalavarum

Ficus cocculifolia var. sakalavarum, commonly known as the Sri Lankan fig, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Sri Lanka.

It features heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, edible fig fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife and local communities. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
