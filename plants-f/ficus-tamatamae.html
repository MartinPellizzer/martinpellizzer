Ficus tamatamae

Ficus tamatamae, commonly known as the Tamatamae fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are typically dark green in color. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus tamatamae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in indoor environments.
