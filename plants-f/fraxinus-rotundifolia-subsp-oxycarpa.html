Fraxinus rotundifolia subsp. oxycarpa

Fraxinus rotundifolia subsp.

oxycarpa, commonly known as the smooth-fruited ash, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its round, broad leaves that are often lobed and have a smooth texture.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by oval, smooth fruits that mature to a reddish-brown color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in bottomlands and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
