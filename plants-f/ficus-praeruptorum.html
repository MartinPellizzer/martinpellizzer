Ficus praeruptorum

Ficus praeruptorum, commonly known as the African rubber fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by fig-like fruits that are edible for some animals but generally not for humans. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

The tree is also valued for its rubber-like latex, which has been used in traditional applications.
