Fagara acreana

Fagara acreana, commonly known as the Guianan waxleaf, is a member of the Rutaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and showy flowers.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Venezuela, Guyana, and Brazil. The plant features compound leaves with serrated edges and small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruits are small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature.

Fagara acreana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
