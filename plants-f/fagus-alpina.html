Fagus alpina

Fagus alpina, commonly known as the European mountain beech, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, triangular nuts enclosed in a cupule. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mixed forests at high altitudes.

The species plays an important ecological role in its habitat, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
