Fevillea peruviana

Fevillea peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian feather plant, is a striking member of the Malvaceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Peru, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its long, slender leaves that resemble feathers, which are often tinged with red or purple hues. Its flowers are small, clustered, and typically white or pale pink, adding a delicate touch to its foliage.

Fevillea peruviana is valued both for its ornamental beauty and its ecological role in its native habitat.
