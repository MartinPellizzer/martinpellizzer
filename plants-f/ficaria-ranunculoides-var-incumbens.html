Ficaria ranunculoides var. incumbens

Ficaria ranunculoides var. incumbens, commonly known as the rockfoil, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features dense, mat-forming foliage with small, rounded leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white to grayish hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is well adapted to full sun conditions.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often used in rock gardens and alpine settings.
