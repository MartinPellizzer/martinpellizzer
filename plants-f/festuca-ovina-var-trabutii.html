Festuca ovina var. trabutii

Festuca ovina var. trabutii, commonly known as Trabut's sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky environments. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is often hidden within the leaf sheaths.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
