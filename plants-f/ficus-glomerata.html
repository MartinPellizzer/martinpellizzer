Ficus glomerata

Ficus glomerata, commonly known as the clustered fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are ovate in shape and have a slightly waxy texture. Its fruit, which is a small, round fig, is borne in clusters and is edible when ripe.

Ficus glomerata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
