Fuchsia polyanthella

Fuchsia polyanthella, commonly known as the clustered fuchsia, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowering period typically occurs in late winter to early spring, making it a valuable addition to gardens in temperate climates.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
