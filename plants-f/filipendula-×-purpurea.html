Filipendula × purpurea

Filipendula × purpurea, commonly known as meadow sweet, is a hybrid perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, pinkish-white flower heads that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and other wetland habitats. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and the stems are often hairy.

Filipendula × purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
