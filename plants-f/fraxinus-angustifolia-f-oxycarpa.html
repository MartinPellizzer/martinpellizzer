Fraxinus angustifolia f. oxycarpa

Fraxinus angustifolia f. oxycarpa, commonly known as the narrowleaf ash, is a rare and distinctive cultivar of the ash tree.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often a striking yellow-green in color. The plant exhibits a compact growth habit with a dense canopy, making it suitable for small garden spaces. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged fruits that disperse its seeds.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to a variety of soil conditions.
