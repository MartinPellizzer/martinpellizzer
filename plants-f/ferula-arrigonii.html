Ferula arrigonii

Ferula arrigonii, commonly known as the Afghan giant fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, feathery leaves that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbel-like clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its aromatic seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.

Ferula arrigonii is also valued for its role in ecological systems, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
