Ficus vasculosa var. undulatifolia

Ficus vasculosa var. undulatifolia, commonly known as the wavy-leaf fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating leaves that give it its common name. The plant belongs to the Moraceae family and is a type of flowering plant. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its unique leaf shape and attractive appearance.
