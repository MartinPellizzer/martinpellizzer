Ficus laevigata subvar. reticulosa

Ficus laevigata subvar. reticulosa, commonly known as the net-veined fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as Thailand, Vietnam, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its smooth, light-green leaves with a distinctive reticulate venation pattern. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called syconia.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in indoor environments.
