Fumaria bulbosa subsp. solida

Fumaria bulbosa subsp.

solida, commonly known as the solid bladdergrass, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It belongs to the family Fumariaceae, characterized by its distinctive, inflated seed pods.

The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its flowers are small and yellowish, often clustered in loose racemes.

The subspecies solida is distinguished by its more robust stem and solid, less lobed seed pods compared to other subspecies.
