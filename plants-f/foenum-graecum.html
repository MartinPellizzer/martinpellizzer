Foenum-graecum

Foenum-graecum, commonly known as fennel, is an annual herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its feathery, aromatic leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in umbrella-like clusters. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. Its seeds, which are small and brown, are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The entire plant contains essential oils that contribute to its distinctive licorice-like flavor and various therapeutic properties.
