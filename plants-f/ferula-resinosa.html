Ferula resinosa

Ferula resinosa, commonly known as the Persian cow parsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Caucasus. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbrella-like inflorescences.

Ferula resinosa is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
