Ficus rheedei

Ficus rheedei, commonly known as the red-veined ficus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking red veins, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are followed by greenish-white, fleshy fruits. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.

Ficus rheedei prefers bright, indirect light and moderate humidity, making it suitable for indoor environments.
