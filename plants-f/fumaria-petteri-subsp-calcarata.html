Fumaria petteri subsp. calcarata

Fumaria petteri subsp.

calcarata, commonly known as the Alpine yellowcress, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It belongs to the Brassicaceae family, which includes many other flowering plants with characteristic cruciform flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its small, yellow flowers are arranged in racemes and bloom during the summer months.

The subspecies is distinguished by its finely lobed leaves and a more compact growth form compared to other members of the species.
