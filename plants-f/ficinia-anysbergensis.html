Ficinia anysbergensis

Ficinia anysbergensis, commonly known as the Anysberg grass, is a perennial grass species native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grassland habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
