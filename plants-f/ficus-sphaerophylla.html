Ficus sphaerophylla

Ficus sphaerophylla, commonly known as the globe fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the island of Madagascar and is characterized by its unique, rounded, and often multi-lobed leaves. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when mature, turning yellow or red as they ripen. Ficus sphaerophylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
