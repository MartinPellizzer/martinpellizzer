Fadenia

Fadenia, commonly known as the false dandelion, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

These plants are characterized by their composite flower heads, which consist of numerous small florets arranged in a rounded shape. Fadenia species typically have basal leaves that are often lobed or toothed, and they produce seeds that are dispersed by wind. They are often found in grassy areas and along roadsides, thriving in disturbed soils.

The genus includes several species, some of which are used in herbal medicine for their purported health benefits.
