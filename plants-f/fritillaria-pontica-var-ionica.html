Fritillaria pontica var. ionica

Fritillaria pontica var. ionica, commonly known as the Ionian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or red with purple streaks, arranged in umbels. The plant has a robust, fibrous root system and produces a rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
