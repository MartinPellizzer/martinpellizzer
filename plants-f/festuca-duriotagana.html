Festuca duriotagana

Festuca duriotagana, commonly known as the alpine fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

Festuca duriotagana is adapted to harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient component of high-altitude ecosystems.
