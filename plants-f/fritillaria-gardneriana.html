Fritillaria gardneriana

Fritillaria gardneriana, commonly known as the Death Valley fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with contrasting patterns. The plant produces a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and has a compact growth habit. It thrives in arid environments and is adapted to extreme heat and drought conditions.

Fritillaria gardneriana is typically found in rocky soils and is a characteristic species of desert ecosystems.
