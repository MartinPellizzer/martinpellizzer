Ficinia filiformis var. bergiana

Ficinia filiformis var. bergiana, commonly known as the Bergian thread-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
