Furcilla myrtifolia

Furcilla myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved furcilla, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that resemble those of myrtle. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its ornamental value is appreciated in gardens for its delicate appearance and aromatic qualities.

Furcilla myrtifolia is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.
