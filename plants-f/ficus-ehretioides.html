Ficus ehretioides

Ficus ehretioides, commonly known as the African rubber fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Ficus ehretioides is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
