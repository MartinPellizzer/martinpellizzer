Ficus madagascariensis

Ficus madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and distinctive fig fruits. The plant is evergreen and can grow as a tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a waxy texture, while the fruit is a small, edible fig that attracts various animals.

The plant plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food and shelter for wildlife.
