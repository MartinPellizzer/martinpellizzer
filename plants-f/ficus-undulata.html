Ficus undulata

Ficus undulata, commonly known as the wavy-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive wavy-edged leaves. The plant is evergreen, with glossy, dark green foliage that often has a slightly velvety texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when mature.

Ficus undulata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
