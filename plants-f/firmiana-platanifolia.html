Firmiana platanifolia

Firmiana platanifolia, commonly known as the Chinese fan palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, fan-shaped leaves that can grow up to three meters in diameter. The plant has a slender, unbranched trunk that is often covered in a fibrous, spongy material. Its flowers are small and clustered in panicles, typically appearing in yellow or green hues.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that contains a single seed, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
