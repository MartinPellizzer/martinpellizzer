Festuca drymeja subsp. exaltata

Festuca drymeja subsp.

exaltata, commonly known as the alpine feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.

The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and fine, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a silky sheen. Its inflorescence consists of a tall, narrow panicle bearing small, spikelet-like flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
