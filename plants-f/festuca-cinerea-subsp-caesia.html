Festuca cinerea subsp. caesia

Festuca cinerea subsp.

caesia, commonly known as silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its fine, silvery-blue foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers. Festuca cinerea subsp.

caesia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, low-maintenance ground cover.
