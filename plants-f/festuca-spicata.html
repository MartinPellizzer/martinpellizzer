Festuca spicata

Festuca spicata, commonly known as slender fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Festuca spicata is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a resilient and adaptable species.
