Fumaria densiflora var. albida

Fumaria densiflora var. albida, commonly known as the white bladderwort, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, inflated seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a unique and interesting subject for ecological and botanical study.
