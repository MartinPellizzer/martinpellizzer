Fumaria sibirica

Fumaria sibirica, commonly known as Siberian smokeplant, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Fumariaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Fumaria sibirica thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in some horticultural gardens.
