Fritillaria involucrata subsp. caussolensis

Fritillaria involucrata subsp.

caussolensis, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red.

The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are basal and linear, often with a bluish-green hue.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
