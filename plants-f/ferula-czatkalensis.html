Ferula czatkalensis

Ferula czatkalensis, commonly known as the Czatkal fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and feathery, aromatic leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family, and its taproot is often harvested for its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Ferula czatkalensis is known for its high content of volatile oils, which contribute to its use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
