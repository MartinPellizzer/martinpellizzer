Festuca talamancensis

Festuca talamancensis, commonly known as Talamancan fescue, is a species of grass native to Central America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers.

Festuca talamancensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
