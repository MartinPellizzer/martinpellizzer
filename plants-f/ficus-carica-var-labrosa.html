Ficus carica var. labrosa

Ficus carica var. labrosa, commonly known as the Persian fig, is a cultivar of the common fig tree native to the Middle East and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into the edible fig fruits, which are known for their sweet, honey-like flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its delicious fruit.
