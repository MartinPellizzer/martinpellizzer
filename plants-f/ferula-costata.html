Ferula costata

Ferula costata, commonly known as the Persian cow's milk, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and yellowish-green flowering heads. The plant produces a milky sap that has been historically used for its medicinal properties. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are thick and fleshy.

Ferula costata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and traditional uses in herbal medicine.
