Ficus phanerophlebia

Ficus phanerophlebia, commonly known as the hairy fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, hairy leaves that provide a distinctive appearance. The plant has a robust root system that helps it thrive in moist, humid environments. Its fruit, known as a fig, is edible and contains small, seed-like structures.

Ficus phanerophlebia is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
