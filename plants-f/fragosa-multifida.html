Fragosa multifida

Fragosa multifida, commonly known as the multifid fragosa, is a shrub native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are often deeply lobed and have a distinctive, feathery appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
