Ficus cuspidata var. sinuata

Ficus cuspidata var. sinuata, commonly known as the Sinuous Ficus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often lobed and have a distinctive sinuous or wavy margin, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by green, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil and indirect sunlight.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
