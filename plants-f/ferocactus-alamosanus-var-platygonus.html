Ferocactus alamosanus var. platygonus

Ferocactus alamosanus var. platygonus, commonly known as the flat-ribbed barrel cactus, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its spiny exterior provides protection against herbivores and extreme weather conditions.

The species is well adapted to desert climates and requires minimal water and nutrients to survive.
