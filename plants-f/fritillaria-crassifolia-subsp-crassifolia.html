Fritillaria crassifolia subsp. crassifolia

Fritillaria crassifolia subsp.

crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its large, thick, fleshy leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to yellow and orange.

The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, and its flowers are often adorned with intricate patterns. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is a popular choice among gardeners and botanists alike.
