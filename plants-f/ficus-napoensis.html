Ficus napoensis

Ficus napoensis, commonly known as the Napo fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly the Amazon Basin. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. It is often found growing in humid, lowland forests, thriving in shaded environments.

The fruit of Ficus napoensis is a small, round fig that is typically consumed by various wildlife species.
