Ficus pachyphylla

Ficus pachyphylla, commonly known as the thick-leaved fig, is a species of flowering plant native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white, powdery bloom. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called syconia. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to thrive in well-drained, sandy soils.

Ficus pachyphylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements.
