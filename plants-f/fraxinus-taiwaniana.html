Fraxinus taiwaniana

Fraxinus taiwaniana, commonly known as the Taiwan ash, is a deciduous tree native to Taiwan.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
