Ficus balica

Ficus balica, commonly known as the Balic fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate in form. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature, containing a sweet, edible pulp.

Ficus balica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
