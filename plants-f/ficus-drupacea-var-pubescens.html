Ficus drupacea var. pubescens

Ficus drupacea var. pubescens, commonly known as the woolly fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a leathery texture.

It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits.
