Fumaria muralis var. boraei

Fumaria muralis var. boraei, commonly known as the Alpine celandine, is a low-growing annual herb native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It features slender, branched stems that are often covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers with four petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of the stems. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a distinctive wavy margin.

It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and scree slopes.
