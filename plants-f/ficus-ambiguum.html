Ficus ambiguum

Ficus ambiguum, commonly known as the ambiguous fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically hidden within its syconium, a specialized type of fruit. Ficus ambiguum is often cultivated as an indoor plant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Its adaptability and ease of care make it a popular choice for both novice and experienced plant enthusiasts.
