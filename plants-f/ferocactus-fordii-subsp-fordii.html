Ferocactus fordii subsp. fordii

Ferocactus fordii subsp.

fordii, commonly known as the Ford's barrel cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems that are often covered in dense, white spines.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne in clusters at the stem tips. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in dry climates.
