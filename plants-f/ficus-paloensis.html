Ficus paloensis

Ficus paloensis, commonly known as the Paloensis Fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Mexico and Panama. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The Paloensis Fig is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
