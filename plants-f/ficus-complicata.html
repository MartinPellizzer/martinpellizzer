Ficus complicata

Ficus complicata, commonly known as the curly fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage with curled leaflets that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. Ficus complicata thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.

Its unique leaf structure makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and bonsai art.
