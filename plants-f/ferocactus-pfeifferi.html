Ferocactus pfeifferi

Ferocactus pfeifferi, commonly known as the Pfeiffer's barrel cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert in North America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. Its spines are modified leaves that help reduce water loss and provide protection from herbivores.

Ferocactus pfeifferi is well adapted to extreme heat and drought, making it a resilient and iconic desert plant.
