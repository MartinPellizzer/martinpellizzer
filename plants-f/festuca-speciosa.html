Festuca speciosa

Festuca speciosa, commonly known as showy feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and panicles of pale blue to silver-green flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive feathery appearance.

Festuca speciosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
