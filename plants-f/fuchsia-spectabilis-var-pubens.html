Fuchsia spectabilis var. pubens

Fuchsia spectabilis var. pubens, commonly known as the velvet fuchsia, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep violet to bright red. The plant features hairy, pubescent stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "pubens.". It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in the ecological systems of its native habitat.
