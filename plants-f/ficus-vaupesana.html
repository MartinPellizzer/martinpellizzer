Ficus vaupesana

Ficus vaupesana, commonly known as the Vaupes fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that develop into edible figs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Ficus vaupesana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
