Fuchsia macrostemma var. grandiflora

Fuchsia macrostemma var. grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered fuchsia, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its vibrant, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep pink to purple. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its foliage consists of dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler, moist environments.
