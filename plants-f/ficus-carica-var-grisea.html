Ficus carica var. grisea

Ficus carica var. grisea, commonly known as the gray fig, is a cultivar of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive grayish-green leaves that have a waxy texture and a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, sweet figs. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.
