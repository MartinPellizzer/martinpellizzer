Facelis retusa var. patula

Facelis retusa var. patula, commonly known as the Patula Faceleaf, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, showy bracts that resemble small faces. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 1 meter, with opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, tubular flowers in clusters, usually in shades of red, orange, or yellow, which attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
