Ficus sundaica var. beccariana

Ficus sundaica var. beccariana, commonly known as the Sunda fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Indonesia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in tropical rainforests. The plant has glossy, elongated leaves that are usually dark green in color. Its fruit, a small fig, is an important food source for various wildlife species.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
