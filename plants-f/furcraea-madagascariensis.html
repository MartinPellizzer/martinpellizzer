Furcraea madagascariensis

Furcraea madagascariensis, commonly known as the Madagascar sword fern, is a striking plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often grayish-green in color. The plant produces tall inflorescences with small, white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by distinctive blue or black berries. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a popular ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
