Ficus lasiophleba

Ficus lasiophleba, commonly known as the rough-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that have a rough texture, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible figs.

Ficus lasiophleba is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
