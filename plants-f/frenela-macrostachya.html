Frenela macrostachya

Frenela macrostachya, commonly known as the large-stemmed fern, is a member of the fern family, characterized by its intricate frond structures.

It typically features compound leaves that are divided into numerous pinnae, giving it a lush, feathery appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Frenela macrostachya is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in forest ecosystems.
