Ficus nymphoides

Ficus nymphoides, commonly known as the silver velvet fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it a distinctive, almost metallic appearance. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

This plant is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
