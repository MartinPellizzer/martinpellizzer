Ferula anatolica

Ferula anatolica, commonly known as Anatolian cow parsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family and is native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, featuring hollow, aromatic stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in early summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often containing a milky sap that is characteristic of the genus Ferula.

Ferula anatolica is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
