Ferula sulcata var. biumbellata

Ferula sulcata var. biumbellata, commonly known as the Persian cow parsley, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a tall, slender stem with finely divided, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in a complex, branched inflorescence. Its roots are fibrous and can be aromatic, containing compounds that have been used in traditional medicine.

This species is adapted to dry, rocky environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.
