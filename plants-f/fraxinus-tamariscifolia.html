Fraxinus tamariscifolia

Fraxinus tamariscifolia, commonly known as the silky ash, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and its distinctive, finely hairy leaves that give it a silvery appearance. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its fruit is a small, winged samara that aids in seed dispersal.

Fraxinus tamariscifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in rocky or dry soils.
