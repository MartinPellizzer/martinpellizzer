Ferula latipinna

Ferula latipinna, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ferula, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces yellowish-green flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its taproot is thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water in its native arid environment.

Ferula latipinna is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
