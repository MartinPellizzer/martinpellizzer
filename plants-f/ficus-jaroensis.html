Ficus jaroensis

Ficus jaroensis, commonly known as the Jaro fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, broad ovate leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often consumed by local communities. Ficus jaroensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a significant species in its native habitat.
