Festuca pubinervis

Festuca pubinervis, commonly known as the pubescent feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and tufted flowering heads, which are typically borne on slender, upright stems. The plant prefers well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and rocky slopes. Its foliage is covered with soft, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive, fluffy appearance.

Festuca pubinervis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
