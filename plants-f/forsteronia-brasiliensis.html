Forsteronia brasiliensis

Forsteronia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit or yellow starfruit, is a tropical shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright orange, star-shaped fruits. The fruit has a segmented, fleshy exterior with a sweet and tangy flavor, making it popular in local cuisines.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.
