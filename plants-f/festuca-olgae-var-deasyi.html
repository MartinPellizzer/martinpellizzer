Festuca olgae var. deasyi

Festuca olgae var. deasyi, commonly known as Deasy's fescue, is a cool-season grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green foliage, which is often used in ornamental landscaping. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. It is relatively drought-tolerant once established, requiring minimal maintenance.

Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that add visual interest during the spring and summer months.
