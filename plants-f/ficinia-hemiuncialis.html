Ficinia hemiuncialis

Ficinia hemiuncialis, commonly known as the slender swamp grass, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in Australia.

It typically grows in shallow water or moist soil, often found in swamps and marshes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often submerged or floating at the water's surface. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spike-like clusters, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Ficinia hemiuncialis plays an important role in maintaining the ecological balance of its wetland habitat.
