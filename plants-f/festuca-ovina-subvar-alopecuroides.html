Festuca ovina subvar. alopecuroides

Festuca ovina subvar. alopecuroides, commonly known as sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. It produces small, spike-like flower clusters in the summer, adding visual interest to the garden.

Festuca ovina subvar. alopecuroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and low maintenance requirements.
