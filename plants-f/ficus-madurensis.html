Ficus madurensis

Ficus madurensis, commonly known as the Madurese fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, a fig, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its shade and aesthetic appeal.
