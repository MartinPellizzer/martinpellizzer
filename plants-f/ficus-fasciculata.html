Ficus fasciculata

Ficus fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves that are arranged in clusters, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits.

Ficus fasciculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
