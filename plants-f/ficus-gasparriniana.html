Ficus gasparriniana

Ficus gasparriniana, commonly known as the Gasparrini fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the island of Socotra, located in the Indian Ocean, and is characterized by its unique and striking appearance. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and a distinctive greenish-white underside. It produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature, though they may turn yellow or red depending on the variety.

Ficus gasparriniana is adapted to arid conditions and thrives in well-drained soils, making it a resilient and ornamental plant in suitable climates.
