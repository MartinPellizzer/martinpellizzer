Fimbristylis pachystachys

Fimbristylis pachystachys, commonly known as the broad-stalked sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences, typically bearing pale to brownish flowers. Fimbristylis pachystachys is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity.
