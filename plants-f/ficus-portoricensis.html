Ficus portoricensis

Ficus portoricensis, commonly known as the Puerto Rican fig tree, is a species native to the Caribbean island of Puerto Rico.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in coastal areas and moist lowland forests. The plant has smooth, grayish bark and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy on top and lighter in color underneath. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible fig fruits.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitat, providing food for various wildlife species.
