Fatsia wilsonii

Fatsia wilsonii, commonly known as the Japanese aralia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It features large, palmate leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter, giving it a striking, fern-like appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clustered umbels, followed by dark blue berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its lush foliage.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Araliaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and adaptability to humid climates.
