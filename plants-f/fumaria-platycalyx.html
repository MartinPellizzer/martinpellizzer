Fumaria platycalyx

Fumaria platycalyx, commonly known as the broad-calyx fumitory, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fumariaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, delicate flowers that typically bloom in early spring. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with two lips, and its leaves are alternate, simple, and often covered in fine hairs. Fumaria platycalyx is native to Europe and parts of Asia, and it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along roadsides.
