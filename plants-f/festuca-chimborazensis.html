Festuca chimborazensis

Festuca chimborazensis, commonly known as the Chimborazo fescue, is a grass species native to the Andean highlands of Ecuador.

It thrives in high-altitude environments, typically growing at elevations above 3,500 meters. The plant is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and silver-green foliage. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is a rare and ecologically significant species in its native range.
