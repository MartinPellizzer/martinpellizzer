Ficus foveolata var. oleiformis

Ficus foveolata var. oleiformis, commonly known as the oleiform ficus, is a rare and unique variant of the ficus genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and often pale green leaves that have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by round, greenish fruits. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific care requirements, it is a sought-after plant among botanical enthusiasts.
