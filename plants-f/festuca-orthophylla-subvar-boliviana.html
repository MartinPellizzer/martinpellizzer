Festuca orthophylla subvar. boliviana

Festuca orthophylla subvar. boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Bolivia.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and tufted growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or arid soils. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, greenish to brownish flowers. It is adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and resistance to strong winds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in mountainous regions.
