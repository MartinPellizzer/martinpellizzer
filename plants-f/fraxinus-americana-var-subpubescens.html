Fraxinus americana var. subpubescens

Fraxinus americana var. subpubescens, commonly known as the eastern winged maple, is a variant of the American ash tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive winged seeds, which are often used for identification. The leaves are typically opposite, with a serrated margin and a heart-shaped base. The bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age.

This variety is native to the eastern United States and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
