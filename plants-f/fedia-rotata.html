Fedia rotata

Fedia rotata, commonly known as the spiral fern, is a unique plant characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of fronds.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has slender, wiry stems that support its feathery, lance-shaped leaves. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are typically found on the underside of the fronds.

Fedia rotata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid forests.
