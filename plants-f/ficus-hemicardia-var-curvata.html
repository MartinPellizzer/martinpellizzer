Ficus hemicardia var. curvata

Ficus hemicardia var. curvata, commonly known as the twisted-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that curve and twist in a unique, almost spiral pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
