Feuilleea kegelii

Feuilleea kegelii, commonly known as the Kegel's leaf plant, is a unique species native to the arid regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves that are often adorned with intricate patterns resembling veins or veins. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun conditions. Its leaves are not only ornamental but also serve as a means of water conservation, reducing transpiration in its harsh environment.

Feuilleea kegelii is a popular choice for xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
