Freesia fucata

Freesia fucata, commonly known as the coastal freesia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fragrant flowers that come in shades of white, yellow, and orange. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy soils and saline conditions. It produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a compact growth habit.

Freesia fucata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and pleasant scent.
