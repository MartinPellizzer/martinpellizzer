Ficus simiae

Ficus simiae, commonly known as the Philippine fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Ficus simiae is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers indirect light.
