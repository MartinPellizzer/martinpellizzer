Ficus stipulosa

Ficus stipulosa, commonly known as the broadleaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, including countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus stipulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
