Ficus myriocarpa

Ficus myriocarpa, commonly known as the many-fruited fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when mature. Ficus myriocarpa is a member of the Moraceae family and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
