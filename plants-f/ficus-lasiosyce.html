Ficus lasiosyce

Ficus lasiosyce, commonly known as the woolly fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its thick, hairy leaves that give it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus lasiosyce is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
