Fritillaria imperialis var. longipetala

Fritillaria imperialis var. longipetala, commonly known as the Persian crown imperial, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Persia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from orange to red, with a distinctive crown-like arrangement. The plant features long, narrow leaves that emerge from a cluster of bulbs, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a dramatic visual impact to the landscape.
