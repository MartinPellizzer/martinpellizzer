Ficus jonesii

Ficus jonesii, commonly known as the Jones fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig fruits, which are typically green when mature. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

Ficus jonesii thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
