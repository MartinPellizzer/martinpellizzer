Ficus courtallensis

Ficus courtallensis, commonly known as the Kandy fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and distinctive aerial roots. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into figs, which are typically clustered in syconia. Ficus courtallensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
