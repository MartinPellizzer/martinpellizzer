Festuca gigantea f. minor

Festuca gigantea f. minor, commonly known as dwarf bluegrass, is a low-growing, perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 15 centimeters, making it a compact and ornamental choice for gardens. The plant features narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often slightly hairy, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences in early summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, moist soils.

This variety is valued for its fine texture and ability to form dense, attractive lawns or ground covers.
