Ficus pyrrhopoda

Ficus pyrrhopoda, commonly known as the red-veined fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish veins on its heart-shaped leaves, which give it a striking appearance. The plant is a member of the Moraceae family and produces small, inedible figs. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
