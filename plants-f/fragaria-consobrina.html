Fragaria consobrina

Fragaria consobrina, commonly known as the alpine strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It produces small, edible berries that are similar in appearance to common strawberries but are typically smaller and more acidic. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils at high altitudes.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitats.
