Ficus recurva var. lasiocarpa

Ficus recurva var. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the woolly fig or the curly fig, is a species native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, curly, and often pendulous leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. Its leaves are typically covered in a fine, white, woolly coating, which gives it its common name.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
