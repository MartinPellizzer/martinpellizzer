Freesia parva

Freesia parva, commonly known as the dwarf freesia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its compact size and delicate, fragrant flowers. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped blooms in shades of white, yellow, and pink, often with a sweet, honey-like scent. Freesia parva thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for garden beds and containers.

Its short growing season and ease of cultivation contribute to its appeal among horticultural enthusiasts.
