Fumaria mairei subsp. saxicola

Fumaria mairei subsp.

saxicola, commonly known as the rock fumewort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils, preferring well-drained and sunny environments. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers are arranged in loose racemes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging alpine conditions.
