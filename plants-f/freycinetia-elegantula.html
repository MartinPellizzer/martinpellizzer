Freycinetia elegantula

Freycinetia elegantula, commonly known as the slender pandanus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It belongs to the family Pandanaceae and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and cuisine. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its growth habit is typically as a shrub or small tree, with a fibrous, woody stem.

The leaves are long, strap-shaped, and have a distinctive waxy texture, making them highly valued for their aesthetic and functional uses.
