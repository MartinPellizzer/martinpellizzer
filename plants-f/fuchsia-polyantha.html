Fuchsia polyantha

Fuchsia polyantha, commonly known as the common fuchsia, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, pendulous flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Fuchsia polyantha thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.
