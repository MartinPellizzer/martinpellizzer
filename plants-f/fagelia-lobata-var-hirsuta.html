Fagelia lobata var. hirsuta

Fagelia lobata var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy fagelia, is a species of flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and nitrogen-fixing capabilities. The plant is distinguished by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "hirsuta.". Its small, clustered flowers are typically pink or purple, and it produces pods that contain seeds.

This plant is often found in moist, wooded areas and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
