Filago heterantha subsp. dichotoma

Filago heterantha subsp.

dichotoma, commonly known as the dichotomous filago, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, showing adaptations to drought conditions. Its leaves are usually alternate and have a rough, hairy texture.

The flowers are small and yellow, arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in some regions.
