Festuca saximontana var. purpusiana

Festuca saximontana var. purpusiana, commonly known as the Sierra grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in high-elevation environments. The plant features slender, erect stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that produce minute, wind-dispersed seeds. Its foliage is characterized by narrow, blue-green blades that are often covered in a fine layer of hair.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing slopes and preventing erosion.
