Ficus benjamina subsp. comosa

Ficus benjamina subsp.

comosa, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by pear-shaped fruits containing a single seed. It is native to Southeast Asia and is widely cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.

However, it can be sensitive to changes in temperature and light, requiring careful maintenance to thrive.
