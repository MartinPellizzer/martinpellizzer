Ficus carica var. radiata

Ficus carica var. radiata, commonly known as the Radiata Fig, is a variety of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are often variegated with white or light green markings. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into the edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to produce fruit in suitable conditions.
