Funkia platyginifolia

Funkia platyginifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf daylily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features strap-like, broad leaves that are often variegated with white and green markings. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and water features.
