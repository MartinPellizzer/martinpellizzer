Farsetia clypeata

Farsetia clypeata, commonly known as the African violet, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, velvety leaves and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. The plant typically grows as a low-growing herb, often forming a rosette shape. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Farsetia clypeata is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ease of care.
