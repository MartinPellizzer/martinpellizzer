Fraxinus nakaiana

Fraxinus nakaiana, commonly known as the Japanese ash, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The tree has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles, followed by winged samaras as its fruit.

The bark is smooth and grayish, with shallow vertical fissures, and the wood is valued for its fine grain and workability.
