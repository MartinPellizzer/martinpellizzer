Ficus chloroleuca

Ficus chloroleuca, commonly known as the white-veined fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves with prominent white veins, giving it its common name. It typically grows as an evergreen tree or shrub, often found in moist, tropical environments.

Ficus chloroleuca produces small, clustered figs that are edible and serve as a food source for various animals.
