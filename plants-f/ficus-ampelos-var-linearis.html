Ficus ampelos var. linearis

Ficus ampelos var. linearis, commonly known as the weeping fig or linear weeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that give it a cascading appearance, making it popular as a hanging plant. The leaves are typically narrow and elongated, with a glossy green surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fig fruits that are edible for birds but generally not consumed by humans.

This plant is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ease of care.
