Festuca caerulans

Festuca caerulans, commonly known as bluegrass or blue oat grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features fine, blue-green leaves that are often used in ornamental gardening for their aesthetic appeal. The plant produces delicate, airy plumes of pale blue to silver flowers in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Festuca caerulans is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
