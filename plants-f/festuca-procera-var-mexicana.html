Festuca procera var. mexicana

Festuca procera var. mexicana, commonly known as Mexican tall fescue, is a perennial grass native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its coarse, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas and is adapted to a variety of soil types. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

This variety is often used in landscaping and erosion control due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
