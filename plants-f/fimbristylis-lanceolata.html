Fimbristylis lanceolata

Fimbristylis lanceolata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, triangular stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically flowering from late spring to early autumn. It thrives in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Fimbristylis lanceolata is often found in tropical and subtropical regions and plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.
