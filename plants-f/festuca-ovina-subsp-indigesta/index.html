Festuca Ovina Subsp. Indigesta

Festuca ovina subsp. indigesta is a lesser-known grass species that thrives in harsh environments. It is commonly found in alpine and arctic regions where few plants can survive. This subspecies is notable for its tough, fibrous leaves that make it resistant to grazing. Despite its name, it plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems. Its unique adaptations make it a subject of interest for botanists and ecologists alike.
