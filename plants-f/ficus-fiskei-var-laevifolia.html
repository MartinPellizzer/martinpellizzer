Ficus fiskei var. laevifolia

Ficus fiskei var. laevifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leafed fig, is a rare and unique variant of the Ficus genus.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are smooth and slightly glossy, giving it a distinct appearance. The plant is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. It produces small, inedible fruits that are not typically consumed by humans.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in greenhouses or as a collector's plant.
