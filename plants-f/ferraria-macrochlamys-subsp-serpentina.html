Ferraria macrochlamys subsp. serpentina

Ferraria macrochlamys subsp.

serpentina, commonly known as the snake's head orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Iridaceae family and is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces showy inflorescences during the summer months.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
