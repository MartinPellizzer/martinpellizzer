Fibigia clypeata subsp. anatolica

Fibigia clypeata subsp.

anatolica, commonly known as the Anatolian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to Turkey and the eastern Mediterranean. It features upright, hairy stems that bear opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves with serrated margins.

The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue to violet, typically blooming from June to August. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or dry habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
