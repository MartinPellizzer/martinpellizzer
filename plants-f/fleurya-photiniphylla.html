Fleurya photiniphylla

Fleurya photiniphylla, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Fleurya photiniphylla is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
