Ficus hispida f. borneensis

Ficus hispida f. borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean rough fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Borneo and is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, clustered fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptic in shape, with a leathery texture and prominent veins.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
