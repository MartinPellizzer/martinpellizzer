Ficus gracillima

Ficus gracillima, commonly known as the small-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, often climbing growth habit and small, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by edible, green figs. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Ficus gracillima is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
