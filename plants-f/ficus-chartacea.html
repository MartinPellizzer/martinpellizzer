Ficus chartacea

Ficus chartacea, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia, and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, waxy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Ficus chartacea is often cultivated as a houseplant or in hanging baskets due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Its adaptability and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
