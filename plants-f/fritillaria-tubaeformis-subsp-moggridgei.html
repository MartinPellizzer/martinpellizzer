Fritillaria tubaeformis subsp. moggridgei

Fritillaria tubaeformis subsp.

moggridgei, commonly known as the Moggridge's fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has a fibrous root system and a bulb that stores nutrients for the growing season. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, emerging from the base of the plant.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
