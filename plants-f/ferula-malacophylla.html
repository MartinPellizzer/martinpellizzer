Ferula malacophylla

Ferula malacophylla, commonly known as the Persian cow's milk, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a milky sap, which is the source of its common name. Ferula malacophylla is often found in rocky and arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
