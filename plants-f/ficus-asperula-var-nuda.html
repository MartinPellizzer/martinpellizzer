Ficus asperula var. nuda

Ficus asperula var. nuda, commonly known as the naked fig or smooth-leaved fig, is a species native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round fig that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures.

This species is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ornamental value.
