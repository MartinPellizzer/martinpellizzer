Ficus sumatrana var. circumscissa

Ficus sumatrana var. circumscissa, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, trailing growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, greenish-white fruits. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, tropical climates.

Due to its adaptability, it is often used as a ground cover or ornamental plant in landscaping.
