Froscula hispida

Froscula hispida, commonly known as the rough froscula, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the southern hemisphere.

It features wiry, hairy stems that are often reddish in color, and its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine, stiff hairs. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and early summer. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that releases tiny seeds when mature.

Froscula hispida is often found in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
