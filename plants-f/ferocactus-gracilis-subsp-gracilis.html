Ferocactus gracilis subsp. gracilis

Ferocactus gracilis subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as the slender feathered cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert of North America. It is a columnar cactus with a distinctive appearance, featuring prominent, radial spines and a ribbed stem.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by red, spherical fruits. It is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh conditions.
