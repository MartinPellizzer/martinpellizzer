Festuca schimperi

Festuca schimperi, commonly known as Schimper's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland margins and meadows. The plant has fine, needle-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Festuca schimperi is also of interest in ecological studies due to its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
