Fuchsia macrostemma var. parviflora

Fuchsia macrostemma var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered fuchsia, is a compact shrub native to South America.

It features showy, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep purple to bright red, with prominent, fleshy sepals. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy and have a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is commonly cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique floral structure and vibrant colors make it a popular choice for gardens and containers.
