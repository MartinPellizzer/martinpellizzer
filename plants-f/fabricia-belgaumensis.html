Fabricia belgaumensis

Fabricia belgaumensis, commonly known as the Belgaum legume, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its distinctive pod fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its small, clustered flowers are usually pale yellow or greenish-white, and it produces slender, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Fabricia belgaumensis is valued for its potential in sustainable agriculture due to its ability to improve soil fertility.
