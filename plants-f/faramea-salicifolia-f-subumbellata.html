Faramea salicifolia f. subumbellata

Faramea salicifolia f. subumbellata, commonly known as the subtropical evergreen shrub, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral structure.

It features lanceolate, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy, with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers arranged in umbellate inflorescences, which bloom in spring. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in suitable climates.
