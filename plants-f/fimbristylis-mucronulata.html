Fimbristylis mucronulata

Fimbristylis mucronulata, commonly known as the narrow-spike sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wet or seasonally wet habitats such as swampy areas, marshes, and the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
