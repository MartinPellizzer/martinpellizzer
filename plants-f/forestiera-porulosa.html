Forestiera porulosa

Forestiera porulosa, commonly known as the slender forestiera, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and woodland edges. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of white or pale yellow flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a small, capsule that splits open to release tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
