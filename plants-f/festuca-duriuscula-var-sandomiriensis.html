Festuca duriuscula var. sandomiriensis

Festuca duriuscula var. sandomiriensis, commonly known as the Sandomiriens grass, is a perennial grass species native to specific regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact growth habit, typically forming dense mats on rocky or sandy soils. The plant is adapted to harsh environments, showcasing resistance to drought and poor soil conditions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers.

This variety is of interest in ecological studies due to its role in stabilizing soils in marginal habitats.
