Ficus gigantosyce

Ficus gigantosyce, commonly known as the giant fig tree, is a large, evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The tree produces small, clustered figs that are edible and serve as a food source for various animals. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight.

Ficus gigantosyce is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
