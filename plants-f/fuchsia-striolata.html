Fuchsia striolata

Fuchsia striolata, commonly known as the dwarf fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are usually a mix of red, purple, and white, arranged in clusters. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Fuchsia striolata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
