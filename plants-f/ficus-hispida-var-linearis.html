Ficus hispida var. linearis

Ficus hispida var. linearis, commonly known as the curly willow fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender, and slightly curved leaves that give it a distinctive, flowing appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and decorative purposes.
