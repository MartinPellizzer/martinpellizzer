Ficinia fascicularis

Ficinia fascicularis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant has slender, triangular stems that grow in dense clusters, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like in appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in spikelets.

Ficinia fascicularis is often found in wetland habitats and is adapted to moist, nutrient-rich soils.
