Ficus platyphylla

Ficus platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically dark purple when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus platyphylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.
