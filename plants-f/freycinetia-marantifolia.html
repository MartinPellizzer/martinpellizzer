Freycinetia marantifolia

Freycinetia marantifolia, commonly known as the red-flowered ginger, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red bracts that surround small, yellow flowers, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant belongs to the family Marantaceae and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, with a glossy texture.

Freycinetia marantifolia thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
