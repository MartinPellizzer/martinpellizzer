Festuca popovii

Festuca popovii, commonly known as Popov's fescue, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Festuca popovii is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
