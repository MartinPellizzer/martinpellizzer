Ficus jarawae

Ficus jarawae, commonly known as the Jarawa fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Indian subcontinent, particularly found in the states of Assam and Arunachal Pradesh. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, edible fig fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense forests.

The tree is valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity and its traditional uses in indigenous communities.
