Ficus pittieri

Ficus pittieri, commonly known as the Mexican fig or the pittieri fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Ficus pittieri is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
