Festuca scabriculmis subsp. luedii

Festuca scabriculmis subsp.

luedii, commonly known as the Luedii fescue, is a perennial grass species native to specific regions of Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands, often in temperate climates.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough, scabrous texture along the lower surface, giving it its scientific name. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
