Fuchsia mixta

Fuchsia mixta, commonly known as the hybrid fuchsia, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that come in shades of pink, red, and purple. The plant produces showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Fuchsia mixta thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens.
