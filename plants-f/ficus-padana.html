Ficus padana

Ficus padana, commonly known as the Italian fig tree, is a species native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has glossy, elongated leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus padana thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright light for optimal growth.
