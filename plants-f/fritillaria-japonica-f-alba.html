Fritillaria japonica f. alba

Fritillaria japonica f. alba, commonly known as the Japanese fritillary white form, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Japan.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are predominantly white, with some varieties showing pale pink or yellow hues. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and can remain dormant for extended periods.

The species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cool, humid climates.
