Ficus botryocarpa subsp. hirtella

Ficus botryocarpa subsp.

hirtella, commonly known as the hairy fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and conservation efforts.
