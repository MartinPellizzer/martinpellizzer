Fordiophyton begoniifolium

Fordiophyton begoniifolium, commonly known as the Begonia-leaved Fordiophyton, is a rare and striking member of the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of begonias, hence its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Native to the mountainous regions of southern China, it thrives in cool, moist environments with partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists.
