Fuchsia arborescens f. parva

Fuchsia arborescens f. parva, commonly known as the dwarf fuchsia, is a compact shrub native to South America.

It features showy, pendulous flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, which bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant has small, ovate leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique floral display makes it a popular choice for container gardening and as a border plant.
