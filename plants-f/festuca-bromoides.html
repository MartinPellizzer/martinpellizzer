Festuca bromoides

Festuca bromoides, commonly known as blue grass or bromoides fescue, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and meadows.

Festuca bromoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
