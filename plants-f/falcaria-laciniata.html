Falcaria laciniata

Falcaria laciniata, commonly known as the cutleaf senna, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Falcaria laciniata is often found in open woodlands and disturbed soils, thriving in tropical and subtropical climates.
