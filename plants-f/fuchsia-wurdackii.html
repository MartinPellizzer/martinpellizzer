Fuchsia wurdackii

Fuchsia wurdackii, commonly known as the velvet tassel fuchsia, is a striking plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, pendulous flowers that range from deep red to purple, often with contrasting white or yellow markings. The plant produces showy, bract-like structures that resemble tassels, giving it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Fuchsia wurdackii is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
