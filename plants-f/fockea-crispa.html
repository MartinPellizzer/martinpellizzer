Fockea crispa

Fockea crispa, commonly known as the African bellflower, is a striking perennial plant native to South Africa.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue, often with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant has a rosette of fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a unique texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Fockea crispa is known for its long blooming period, typically from late winter to early spring.
