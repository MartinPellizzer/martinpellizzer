Festuca violacea f. aureoflava

Festuca violacea f. aureoflava, commonly known as golden velvet fescue, is a horticultural form of the species Festuca violacea.

It is characterized by its dense, fine-textured foliage that displays a striking golden hue, particularly in the spring and summer months. The plant typically grows in a clump-forming habit, reaching a height of about 15 to 20 centimeters. It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover in shady areas.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant color and compact growth, which enhances its appeal in landscaped settings.
