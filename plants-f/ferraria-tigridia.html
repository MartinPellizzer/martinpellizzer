Ferraria tigridia

Ferraria tigridia, commonly known as the red-hot poker, is a striking perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It features tall, spiky flower clusters that bloom in shades of red, orange, and yellow, adding vibrant color to gardens. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period, which typically spans from summer to autumn.
