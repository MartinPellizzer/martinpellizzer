Ficus retusa var. crassifolia

Ficus retusa var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features thick, glossy, ovate leaves that are often deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs, though the fruit is typically not consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
