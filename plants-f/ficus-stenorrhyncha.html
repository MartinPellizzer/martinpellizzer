Ficus stenorrhyncha

Ficus stenorrhyncha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and have a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in syconia, which are specialized inflorescences.

The fruit, known as a fig, is edible and often consumed by both humans and animals in its native habitat.
