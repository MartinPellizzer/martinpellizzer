Festuca distans var. maritima

Festuca distans var. maritima, commonly known as sea bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and fine, needle-like leaves. This variety thrives in saline and sandy soils, making it well adapted to coastal environments. Its flowering spikes are typically slender and produce small, greenish flowers.

Festuca distans var. maritima plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dune systems and preventing erosion.
