Fusaea decurrens

Fusaea decurrens, commonly known as the running fusaea, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a member of the Campanulaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped blue or purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

Fusaea decurrens is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive flowers and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
