Festuca ancachsana

Festuca ancachsana, commonly known as the Ancacha fescue, is a grass species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils. Festuca ancachsana is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions, including strong winds and low temperatures.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local ecosystem and a subject of interest for botanical research.
