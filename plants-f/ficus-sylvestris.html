Ficus sylvestris

Ficus sylvestris, commonly known as the wild fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe. The plant is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and produces small, green figs that are edible when ripe. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate sunlight.

Ficus sylvestris is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
