Ferula lutea var. microcarpa

Ferula lutea var. microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited wormwood, is a perennial herb native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a spike, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its aromatic properties, it has been used traditionally in herbal medicine.
