Frangula chrysophylla

Frangula chrysophylla, commonly known as the golden-leaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of the eastern United States.

The species belongs to the family Rutaceae, which includes other aromatic plants such as citrus and buckthorn. Its small, dark blue berries are edible but typically consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal.
