Farnesia

Farnesia, commonly known as the "butterfly flower," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like blooms that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows as an annual or biennial, often reaching heights of 1 to 3 feet. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, while the flowers are clustered in spherical inflorescences.

Farnesia thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners in warm climates.
