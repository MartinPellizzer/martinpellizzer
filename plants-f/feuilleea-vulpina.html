Feuilleea vulpina

Feuilleea vulpina, commonly known as the silver fox fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble the soft fur of a fox, hence its common name. The plant features a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and form dense colonies. Its leaves are typically light green with a silvery sheen, especially on the underside.

Feuilleea vulpina thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
