Fritillaria recurva var. coccinea

Fritillaria recurva var. coccinea, commonly known as the red bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, often with a contrasting throat. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a robust, fleshy bulb that serves as its storage organ. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in rock gardens or as a container plant.
