Festuca pratensis var. elatior

Festuca pratensis var. elatior, commonly known as tall meadow fescue, is a cool-season grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and fine, bluish-green leaves. This variety is often used in lawns and pastures due to its tolerance of moderate foot traffic and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various insects and wildlife.
