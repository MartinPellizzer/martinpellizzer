Ficus benguetensis f. leytensis

Ficus benguetensis f. leytensis, commonly known as the Leyte fig, is a variant of the genus Ficus native to the Philippines.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that turn red or orange during the dry season. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically harvested when they turn yellow or red. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
