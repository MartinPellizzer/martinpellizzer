Ficus disticha

Ficus disticha, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive pendulous leaves that give it a cascading appearance. The plant has opposite, glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape. It produces small, yellowish flowers followed by green, oval-shaped fruits.

Ficus disticha is often cultivated as a houseplant or in outdoor gardens due to its ornamental value and adaptability.
