Festuca dissitiflora var. villipalea

Festuca dissitiflora var. villipalea, commonly known as the alpine feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and delicate, feathery inflorescences that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. It is a low-growing plant, often forming dense mats that help stabilize soil in alpine ecosystems.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
