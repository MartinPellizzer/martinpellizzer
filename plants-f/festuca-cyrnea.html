Festuca cyrnea

Festuca cyrnea, commonly known as silvery bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Festuca cyrnea has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
