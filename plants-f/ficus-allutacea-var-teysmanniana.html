Ficus allutacea var. teysmanniana

Ficus allutacea var. teysmanniana, commonly known as the Teysmann's fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical, glossy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple at the edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish fruits that ripen to a yellowish color.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
