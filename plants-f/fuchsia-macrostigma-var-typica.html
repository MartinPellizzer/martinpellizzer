Fuchsia macrostigma var. typica

Fuchsia macrostigma var. typica, commonly known as the typical fuchsia, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its vibrant, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep red to purple. The plant produces small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters and bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower due to its striking appearance.
