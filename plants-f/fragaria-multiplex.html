Fragaria multiplex

Fragaria multiplex, commonly known as the alpine strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically red in color and have a delicate, sweet flavor. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and grows in clusters, often in rocky or alpine environments. Its flowers are white with five petals and are pollinated by insects.

Fragaria multiplex is valued both for its ornamental qualities and its culinary use in local cuisines.
