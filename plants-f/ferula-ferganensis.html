Ferula ferganensis

Ferula ferganensis, commonly known as the Fergana wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Ferula ferganensis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
