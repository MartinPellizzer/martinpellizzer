Felipponia

Felipponia, commonly known as the silverleaf orchid, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and delicate, fragrant blooms that typically appear in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for drought-prone environments. Felipponia belongs to the Orchidaceae family, which includes over 25,000 species of flowering plants.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and botanical research.
