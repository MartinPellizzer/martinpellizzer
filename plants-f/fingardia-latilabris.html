Fingardia latilabris

Fingardia latilabris, commonly known as the broad-lipped fingard, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It features striking, tubular flowers that are typically a vibrant shade of purple or pink, adapted to attract specific pollinators. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to access deep groundwater sources.

Fingardia latilabris is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
