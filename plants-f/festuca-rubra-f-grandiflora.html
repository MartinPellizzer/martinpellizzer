Festuca rubra f. grandiflora

Festuca rubra f. grandiflora, commonly known as red fescue with large flowers, is a flowering grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, upright stems that bear spikelets with large, showy flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing pink to reddish inflorescences that are attractive to pollinators. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal and ecological benefits.
