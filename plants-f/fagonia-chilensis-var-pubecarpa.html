Fagonia chilensis var. pubecarpa

Fagonia chilensis var. pubecarpa, commonly known as the Chilean fagonia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are covered in fine pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or greenish-yellow, followed by fleshy, orange-red berries. It is adapted to survive in dry, sandy soils and can tolerate high levels of salinity.

This species plays a role in stabilizing coastal and desert ecosystems.
