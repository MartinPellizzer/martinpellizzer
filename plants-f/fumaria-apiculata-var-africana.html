Fumaria apiculata var. africana

Fumaria apiculata var. africana, commonly known as the African poppy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red to orange petals that resemble poppies, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. It has hairy stems and leaves with a slightly lobed shape.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant flowers.
