Fuirena glomerata var. angolensis

Fuirena glomerata var. angolensis, commonly known as the Angolan fireplant, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of small, red or orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a succulent, upright stem with a fibrous root system, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
