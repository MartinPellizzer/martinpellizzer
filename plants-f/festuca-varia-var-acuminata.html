Festuca varia var. acuminata

Festuca varia var. acuminata, commonly known as the acute leaf fescue, is a grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green foliage and tufted growth habit, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous slender flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sun, exhibiting good drought tolerance once established.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both ecological and aesthetic purposes in gardens and natural habitats.
