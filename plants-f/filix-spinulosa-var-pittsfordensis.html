Filix spinulosa var. pittsfordensis

Filix spinulosa var. pittsfordensis, commonly known as the Pittsford fern, is a variant of the common holly fern.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are typically green with a slight reddish tinge. The plant produces sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in sori. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas.

This variety is notable for its unique leaf structure and is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
