Ficus firmula

Ficus firmula, commonly known as the common fig, is a small evergreen shrub or tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by the development of edible fig fruits. Ficus firmula thrives in well-drained soil and requires a temperate climate with mild winters.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and for the production of its sweet, edible fruit.
