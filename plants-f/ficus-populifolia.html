Ficus populifolia

Ficus populifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a glossy green. The plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are edible and often consumed by birds and mammals. Ficus populifolia thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
