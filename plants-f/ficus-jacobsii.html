Ficus jacobsii

Ficus jacobsii, commonly known as the Jacobs' fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are typically dark green in color. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

Ficus jacobsii is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
