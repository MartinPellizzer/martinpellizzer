Festuca geniculata subsp. monanthera

Festuca geniculata subsp.

monanthera, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States. It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with blue to purple flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native grassland ecosystems.
