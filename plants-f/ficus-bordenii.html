Ficus bordenii

Ficus bordenii, commonly known as the Borden's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly found in countries like Brazil, Colombia, and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, which is a fig, is typically edible and has a mild, sweet flavor.

Ficus bordenii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
