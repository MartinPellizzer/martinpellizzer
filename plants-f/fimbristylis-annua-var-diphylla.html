Fimbristylis annua var. diphylla

Fimbristylis annua var. diphylla, commonly known as the two-leaved flat sedge, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, thriving in nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with the lower leaves often shorter and broader than the upper ones, giving it the "two-leaved" characteristic.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are usually clustered in a loose panicle, producing small, inconspicuous flowers.
