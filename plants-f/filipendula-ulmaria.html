Filipendula ulmaria

Filipendula ulmaria, commonly known as meadow sweet, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is a member of the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. Its leaves are palmately lobed, with a rough texture and a strong, aromatic scent.

Filipendula ulmaria is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
