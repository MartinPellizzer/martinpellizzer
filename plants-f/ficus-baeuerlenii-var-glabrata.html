Ficus baeuerlenii var. glabrata

Ficus baeuerlenii var. glabrata, commonly known as the Baeuerlen's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are edible and attract various wildlife.

This variety is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ornamental value.
