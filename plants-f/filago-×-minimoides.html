Filago × minimoides

Filago × minimoides, commonly known as the hybrid filago, is a rare and intriguing plant species that results from the cross between Filago umbrosa and Filago arctica.

It is characterized by its delicate, herbaceous appearance and is typically found in moist, shaded environments such as bogs and wet meadows. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in dense inflorescences, and it has a fibrous root system adapted to waterlogged soils. Filago × minimoides is of interest to botanists due to its unique genetic makeup and ecological niche.

Its conservation status is threatened due to habitat loss and environmental changes.
