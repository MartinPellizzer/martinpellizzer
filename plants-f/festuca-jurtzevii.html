Festuca jurtzevii

Festuca jurtzevii, commonly known as the Jurtzev's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky, and mountainous environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny seeds. It is often found in alpine and steppe regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.

Due to its resilience, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in arid and semi-arid areas.
