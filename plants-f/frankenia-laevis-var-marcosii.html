Frankenia laevis var. marcosii

Frankenia laevis var. marcosii, commonly known as the coastal frankenia, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, succulent stems and small, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is adapted to tolerate salt spray and coastal winds.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh environmental conditions.
