Festuca glauca var. curva

Festuca glauca var. curva, commonly known as bluegrass bentgrass, is a cool-season grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and slightly curled, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and durability.
