Frankenia nicoletiana var. aspera

Frankenia nicoletiana var. aspera, commonly known as the rough frankenia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves with a rough, textured surface. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, four-petaled flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is often found in coastal dunes and open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native landscaping.
