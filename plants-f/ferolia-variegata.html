Ferolia variegata

Ferolia variegata, commonly known as the variegated ferolia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Rutaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves, which display a mix of green, white, and yellow hues. Native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, warm climates. Its small, fragrant flowers are typically white or yellow and appear in clusters.

Ferolia variegata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
