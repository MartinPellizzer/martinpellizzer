Fagara lentiscifolia

Fagara lentiscifolia, commonly known as the Caribbean soursop or the West Indian persimmon, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by orange-yellow berries.

Its fruit is edible, though the skin and seeds contain compounds that can be toxic if ingested in large quantities.
