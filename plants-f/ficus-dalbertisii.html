Ficus dalbertisii

Ficus dalbertisii, commonly known as the Dalbert's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Ficus dalbertisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
