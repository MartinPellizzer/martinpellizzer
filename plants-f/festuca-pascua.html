Festuca pascua

Festuca pascua, commonly known as tall fescue, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and coarse, dark green leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-ranked spikelets. Festuca pascua is highly adaptable and thrives in a variety of soil types and environmental conditions.

It is widely used in lawns, pastures, and erosion control due to its durability and resilience.
