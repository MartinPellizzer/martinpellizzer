Fascicularia bicolor

Fascicularia bicolor, commonly known as the silver sword, is a perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its rosette of narrow, silver-gray leaves that form a compact, sword-like shape. The plant produces striking red or purple flowers in late summer, which are borne on tall, upright stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in arid climates.
