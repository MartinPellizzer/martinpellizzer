Ficus thunbergii

Ficus thunbergii, commonly known as the rubber fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, pale green figs that are typically not consumed by humans but are important for wildlife. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Ficus thunbergii is also known for its ability to purify indoor air, making it a popular choice for interior landscaping.
