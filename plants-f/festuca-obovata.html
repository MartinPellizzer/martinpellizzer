Festuca obovata

Festuca obovata, commonly known as broad-leaf fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brown flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.

Festuca obovata is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
