Flemingia fruticulosa

Flemingia fruticulosa, commonly known as the Chinese flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are opposite and often have a reddish or bronze hue. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, spiny fruits containing seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Flemingia fruticulosa is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
