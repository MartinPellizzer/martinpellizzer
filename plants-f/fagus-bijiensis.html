Fagus bijiensis

Fagus bijiensis, commonly known as the Japanese beech, is a species of deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, chestnuts, and other beeches. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elliptical leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It produces small, spiky nuts enclosed in a cupule, which are dispersed by wind.

Fagus bijiensis typically grows in cool, moist environments and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.
