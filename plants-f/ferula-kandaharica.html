Ferula kandaharica

Ferula kandaharica, commonly known as Kandahar wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan and Pakistan.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and often hollow stems. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family. Its roots are thick and fibrous, often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Ferula kandaharica thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
