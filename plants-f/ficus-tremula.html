Ficus tremula

Ficus tremula, commonly known as the trembling fig, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn red in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often found in forested areas.

Ficus tremula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
