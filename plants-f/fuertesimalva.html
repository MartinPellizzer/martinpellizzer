Fuertesimalva

Fuertesimalva, commonly known as the Canary Island pine, is a species of pine native to the Canary Islands.

It is a tall, evergreen tree with long, needle-like leaves and a conical shape. The tree produces small, woody cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in volcanic soils and is adapted to the island's unique climate.

Fuertesimalva plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is considered a symbol of the Canary Islands' natural heritage.
