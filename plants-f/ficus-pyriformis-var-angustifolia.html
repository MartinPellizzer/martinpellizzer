Ficus pyriformis var. angustifolia

Ficus pyriformis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
