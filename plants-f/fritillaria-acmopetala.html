Fritillaria acmopetala

Fritillaria acmopetala, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh climates, entering dormancy during dry periods.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
