Fagara pilosissima

Fagara pilosissima, commonly known as the silky bark tree, is a tropical plant native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark that feels silky to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture with a glossy appearance.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing fragrant blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
