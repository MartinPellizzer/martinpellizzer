Festuca sulcata var. brunnescens

Festuca sulcata var. brunnescens, commonly known as the brown-stemmed fescue, is a perennial grass native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green leaves and its distinctive brownish or reddish stems, which give it its common name. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal. It is drought-tolerant and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for a variety of garden environments.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in the summer, adding visual interest to the landscape.
