Ficus beckleri

Ficus beckleri, commonly known as the Beckler's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered fruit known as figs. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Ficus beckleri is also valued for its ability to adapt to various growing conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
