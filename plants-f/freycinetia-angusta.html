Freycinetia angusta

Freycinetia angusta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in branched inflorescences. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as a natural dye due to their unique coloration.

Freycinetia angusta thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found growing along shores or in wetland areas.
