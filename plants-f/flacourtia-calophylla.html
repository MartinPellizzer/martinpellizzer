Flacourtia calophylla

Flacourtia calophylla, commonly known as the white-flowered flacourtia, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, glossy surface. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fleshy berries. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
