Fumana thymifolia var. vulgaris

Fumana thymifolia var. vulgaris, commonly known as the common thyme-leaved fumana, is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its aromatic foliage.

It features small, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a textured appearance. The plant produces clusters of tiny, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is typically found in rocky or gravelly soils in open habitats.

Its botanical structure supports its use in traditional herbal medicine and as a decorative plant.
