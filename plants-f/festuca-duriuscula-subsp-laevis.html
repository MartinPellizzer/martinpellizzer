Festuca duriuscula subsp. laevis

Festuca duriuscula subsp.

laevis, commonly known as the smooth meadow fescue, is a grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, blade-like leaves that are generally smooth to the touch.

This subspecies typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Its flowering stems bear spikelets that are usually pale green to brown, and it produces small, wind-dispersed seeds. Festuca duriuscula subsp.

laevis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
