Ficinia latinux

Ficinia latinux, commonly known as the broadleaf fig, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fruits that are similar in appearance to figs. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is often cultivated in gardens and tropical landscapes.
