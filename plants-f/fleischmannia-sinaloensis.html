Fleischmannia sinaloensis

Fleischmannia sinaloensis, commonly known as the Sinaloa fleischmannia, is a species of flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its cylindrical stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to arid environments. It is often found in the states of Sinaloa and Nayarit in northern Mexico.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
