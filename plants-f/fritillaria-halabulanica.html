Fritillaria halabulanica

Fritillaria halabulanica, commonly known as the Halabula fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color, often with contrasting black or purple markings. The plant belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates with cold winters.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
