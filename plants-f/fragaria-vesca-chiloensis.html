Fragaria vesca chiloensis

Fragaria vesca chiloensis, commonly known as the Chilean strawberry, is a species of wild strawberry native to South America.

It is a perennial herb with trifoliate leaves and small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soils. Its flowers are typically white with five petals, and it produces fruit in the summer months.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and its role in local ecosystems.
