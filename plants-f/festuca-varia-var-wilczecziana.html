Festuca varia var. wilczecziana

Festuca varia var. wilczecziana, commonly known as the Wilczeczian grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a versatile choice for landscaping.
