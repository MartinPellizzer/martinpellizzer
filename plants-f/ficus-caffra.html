Ficus caffra

Ficus caffra, commonly known as the Natal fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ficus caffra is also valued for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
