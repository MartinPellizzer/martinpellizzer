Festuca pratensis var. apennina

Festuca pratensis var. apennina, commonly known as the Apennine meadow fescue, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and compact growth habit, making it well-suited for lawns and pastures. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting a range of invertebrates and birds.
