Faba vulgaris var. paucijuga

Faba vulgaris var. paucijuga, commonly known as the narrow-leaved vetch, is a perennial leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features trifoliate leaves with narrow, elongated leaflets and produces small, yellow to orange flowers. The plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and is often found in disturbed soils or along roadsides. It has a fibrous root system and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment.

The seeds are typically small and can be harvested for use in traditional or agricultural contexts.
