Ficus dekdekena var. angustifolia

Ficus dekdekena var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It features broad, glossy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
