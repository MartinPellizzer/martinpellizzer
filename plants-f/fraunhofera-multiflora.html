Fraunhofera multiflora

Fraunhofera multiflora, commonly known as the yellow bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling bells, hence its common name. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Botanically, it belongs to the Campanulaceae family, known for its showy, bell-shaped flowers.
