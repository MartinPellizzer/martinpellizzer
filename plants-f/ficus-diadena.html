Ficus diadena

Ficus diadena, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, trailing growth habit and small, glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by round, greenish fruits. It thrives in humid environments and is often used as a ground cover or climbing plant in landscaping.

Ficus diadena is also popular as a houseplant due to its adaptability and air-purifying qualities.
