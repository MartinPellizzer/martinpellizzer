Ficus uncinata

Ficus uncinata, commonly known as the curly fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, including countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, wavy, and curling leaves that give it its common name. Ficus uncinata typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

Its leaves are usually dark green and glossy, and it produces small, clustered fig fruits that are edible.
