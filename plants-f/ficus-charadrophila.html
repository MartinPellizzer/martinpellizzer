Ficus charadrophila

Ficus charadrophila, commonly known as the red-veined fig, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive red veins, which give it its common name. It produces small, edible figs that are often used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant.
