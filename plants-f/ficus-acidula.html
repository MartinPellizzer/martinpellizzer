Ficus acidula

Ficus acidula, commonly known as the sour fig, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, tart fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its roots and leaves contain compounds with potential medicinal properties.

Ficus acidula thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.
