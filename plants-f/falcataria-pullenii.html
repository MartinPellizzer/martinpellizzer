Falcataria pullenii

Falcataria pullenii, commonly known as the white mangrove, is a tropical evergreen tree native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white wax. The plant thrives in saline and waterlogged soils, making it an important species in mangrove ecosystems. It produces small, fragrant flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits containing numerous seeds.

Falcataria pullenii plays a vital role in coastal protection and provides habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
