Ficus warczewiczii

Ficus warczewiczii, commonly known as the Warczewicz's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped or oval in form. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Ficus warczewiczii thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.
