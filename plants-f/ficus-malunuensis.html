Ficus malunuensis

Ficus malunuensis, commonly known as the Malunu fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves and produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow when mature. It thrives in humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
