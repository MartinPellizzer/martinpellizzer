Forestiera heterophyla

Forestiera heterophyla, commonly known as the narrowleaf forestiera, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodland edges. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its fragrant white flowers appear in clusters during the spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

The shrub is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
