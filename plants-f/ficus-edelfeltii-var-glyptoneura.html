Ficus edelfeltii var. glyptoneura

Ficus edelfeltii var. glyptoneura, commonly known as the golden fig or variegated fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking yellow and white variegation. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Ficus edelfeltii var. glyptoneura is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
