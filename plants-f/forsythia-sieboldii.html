Forsythia sieboldii

Forsythia sieboldii, commonly known as Japanese forsythia, is a deciduous shrub native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, flat seeds.

Forsythia sieboldii is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.
