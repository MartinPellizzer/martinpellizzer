Filago hurdwarica

Filago hurdwarica, commonly known as the Himalayan filago, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptive features such as hairy stems and small, narrow leaves. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, arranged in a compact cluster, and bloom during the summer months.

Filago hurdwarica is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing mountainous soils and providing nectar for pollinators.
