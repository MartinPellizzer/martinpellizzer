Festuca cinerea subf. collina

Festuca cinerea subf.

collina, commonly known as silverhair bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to North America. It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and slender, wiry stems that often form dense mats.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
