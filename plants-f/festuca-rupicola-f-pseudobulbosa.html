Festuca rupicola f. pseudobulbosa

Festuca rupicola f. pseudobulbosa, commonly known as the rock grass or pseudobulbous fescue, is a variant of the rock fescue species.

It is a perennial grass characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in alpine or arctic environments. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which are usually pale green to reddish in color.

This subspecies is noted for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh, nutrient-poor conditions.
