Ficus harlandii

Ficus harlandii, commonly known as the Harland's fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a evergreen shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a waxy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically yellow or red in color.

Ficus harlandii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
