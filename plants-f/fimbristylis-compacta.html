Fimbristylis compacta

Fimbristylis compacta, commonly known as the compact flatsedge, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wet or seasonally wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in dense, low vegetation and plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
