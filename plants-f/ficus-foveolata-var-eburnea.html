Ficus foveolata var. eburnea

Ficus foveolata var. eburnea, commonly known as the ivory fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often tinged with a pale or ivory-colored margin, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, pale greenish-white flowers that are followed by spherical, greenish fruits. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to care for in indoor environments.
