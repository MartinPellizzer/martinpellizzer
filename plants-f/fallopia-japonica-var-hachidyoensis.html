Fallopia japonica var. hachidyoensis

Fallopia japonica var. hachidyoensis, commonly known as Japanese knotweed, is a vigorous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features thick, rhizomatous stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with heart-shaped, reddish-green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in late summer, followed by bright red berries. Its rapid growth and invasive nature make it a significant ecological concern in many regions.

It is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
