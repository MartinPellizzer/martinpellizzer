Festuca dalmatica var. pseudodalmatica

Festuca dalmatica var. pseudodalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian fescue variant, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its dense growth habit and is often used in landscaping for erosion control.

This variety is closely related to other fescue species but exhibits distinct morphological features.
