Ficus hookeriana

Ficus hookeriana, commonly known as the Hooker's fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Andes region of South America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in syconia, which are specialized fruiting structures. Ficus hookeriana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
