Firmiana barteri

Firmiana barteri, commonly known as the Chinese parasol tree, is a deciduous tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 cm in diameter. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring, followed by woody, spiky fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in urban environments.

The bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age.
