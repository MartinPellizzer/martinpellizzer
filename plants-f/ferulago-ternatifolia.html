Ferulago ternatifolia

Ferulago ternatifolia, commonly known as the three-leaved ferula, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in a panicle, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and grasslands.

Ferulago ternatifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and floral display.
