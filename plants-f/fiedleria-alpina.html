Fiedleria alpina

Fiedleria alpina, commonly known as the alpine fiedleria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs, which help reduce water loss in its arid habitat. Its small, bell-shaped flowers are usually pink or purple and bloom in late summer.

Fiedleria alpina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
