Fagraea pusilliflora

Fagraea pusilliflora, commonly known as the small-flowered fagraea, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains several seeds.

Fagraea pusilliflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
