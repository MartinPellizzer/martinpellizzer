Fleurya podocarpa var. mannii

Fleurya podocarpa var. mannii, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Africa.

It belongs to the family Lauraceae and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and aromatic bark. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers followed by edible berries that are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its wood is valued for its durability and fine grain, making it a sought-after material in woodworking.
