Fuchsia microphylla var. aprica

Fuchsia microphylla var. aprica, commonly known as the dwarf fuchsia, is a compact, flowering shrub native to New Zealand.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers in shades of pink and purple, which bloom in spring and summer. It thrives in cool, moist climates and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and as a container plant.
