Ficus zarzalensis

Ficus zarzalensis, commonly known as the Zarzalensis fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in countries like Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
