Ficus cookii

Ficus cookii, commonly known as the Cook's fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor. It thrives in humid environments with bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus cookii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
