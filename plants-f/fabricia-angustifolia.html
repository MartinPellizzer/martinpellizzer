Fabricia angustifolia

Fabricia angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leafed burr vetch, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and features trifoliate leaves with slender, pointed leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers followed by hairy, triangular pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in arid conditions due to its deep root system.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
