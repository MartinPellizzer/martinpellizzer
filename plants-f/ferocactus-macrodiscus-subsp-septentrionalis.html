Ferocactus macrodiscus subsp. septentrionalis

Ferocactus macrodiscus subsp.

septentrionalis, commonly known as the northern giant barrel cactus, is a robust, columnar cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features a thick, ribbed stem that can grow up to 10 feet tall, with spiny areoles and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy stems and having a shallow root system to absorb moisture efficiently.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphology and role as a keystone species in desert ecosystems.
