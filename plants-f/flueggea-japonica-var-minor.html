Flueggea japonica var. minor

Flueggea japonica var. minor, commonly known as the Japanese flueggea, is a flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is a shrub that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, exhibiting succulent leaves that are often fleshy and thick. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
