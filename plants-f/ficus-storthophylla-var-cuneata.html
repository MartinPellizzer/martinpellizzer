Ficus storthophylla var. cuneata

Ficus storthophylla var. cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, pointed leaves that are typically dark green with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
