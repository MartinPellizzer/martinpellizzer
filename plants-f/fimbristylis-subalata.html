Fimbristylis subalata

Fimbristylis subalata, commonly known as the silver hair sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to various regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, China, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, pale green to silver-colored leaves. It produces tiny, clustered spikelets that are typically wind-pollinated.

Fimbristylis subalata thrives in wetland environments and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.
