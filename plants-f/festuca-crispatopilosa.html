Festuca crispatopilosa

Festuca crispatopilosa, commonly known as the hairy crinkled fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine, hairy structures. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Festuca crispatopilosa is adapted to cold, dry environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its unique morphology and ecological niche make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
