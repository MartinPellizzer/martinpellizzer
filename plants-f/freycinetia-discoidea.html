Freycinetia discoidea

Freycinetia discoidea, commonly known as the white-veined pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves with distinctive white veins. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is adapted to saline conditions.
