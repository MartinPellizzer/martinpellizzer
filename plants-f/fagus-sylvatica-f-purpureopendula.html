Fagus sylvatica f. purpureopendula

Fagus sylvatica f. purpureopendula, commonly known as the purple-leafed European beech, is a unique cultivar of the European beech tree.

It is characterized by its striking purple-bronze foliage that transitions to a vibrant red in the autumn. The leaves are typically larger and more elongated than those of the standard species, adding a dramatic visual effect. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it a versatile choice for horticultural use.
