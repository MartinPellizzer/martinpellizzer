Ficus cartagenensis

Ficus cartagenensis, commonly known as the Cartagena fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly found in countries like Colombia and Cuba. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in coastal areas or near rivers.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit and shade.
