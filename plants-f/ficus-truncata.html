Ficus truncata

Ficus truncata, commonly known as the truncated fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. Ficus truncata typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters.

Its small, clustered flowers are followed by edible fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species.
