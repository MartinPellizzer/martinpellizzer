Fallopia nervosa

Fallopia nervosa, commonly known as Chinese knotweed, is a flowering plant in the family Polygonaceae.

It is characterized by its stout, bamboo-like stems that are often reddish in color and covered with a whitish, powdery coating. It is native to East Asia but has become invasive in other regions due to its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes.

Fallopia nervosa is often found in moist, disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
