Festuca × rurivaga

Festuca × rurivaga, commonly known as the tall fescue, is a perennial grass species that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, coarse stems and dense, dark green foliage, which makes it a popular choice for turfgrass in lawns and sports fields. The plant is typically found in temperate regions and thrives in a variety of soil types, though it prefers well-drained soils. Festuca × rurivaga is also known for its adaptability to different climates and its ability to withstand drought and wear, making it a resilient ground cover.

Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense sods, which help prevent erosion and suppress weed growth.
