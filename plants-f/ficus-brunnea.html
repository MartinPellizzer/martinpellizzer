Ficus brunnea

Ficus brunnea, commonly known as the brown fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fruits that turn from green to yellow as they mature.

Ficus brunnea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
