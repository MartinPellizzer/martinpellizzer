Festuca eskia var. flavescens

Festuca eskia var. flavescens, commonly known as the yellow meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 60 cm, and its leaves are narrow and grass-like with a bluish-green hue. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of spikelets, which produce small, yellowish flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
