Ficus corneriana

Ficus corneriana, commonly known as the Corner's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate in form. It produces small, clustered fruits known as figs, which are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Ficus corneriana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
