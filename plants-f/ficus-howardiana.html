Ficus howardiana

Ficus howardiana, commonly known as the Benjamin fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by green, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus howardiana is popular as a houseplant due to its adaptability and attractive foliage.
