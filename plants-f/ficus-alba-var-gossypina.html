Ficus alba var. gossypina

Ficus alba var. gossypina, commonly known as the white fig or white-veined fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pale green, waxy leaves with prominent white veins, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically hidden within the foliage. Its fruit, a small, round fig, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
