Feuilleea cylindrica

Feuilleea cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical leaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its elongated, cylindrical leaves that grow in a spiral pattern along its stem.

Native to arid regions, this plant has adapted to survive in low-water environments with thick, waxy leaves that reduce transpiration. Its leaves are typically a vibrant green, sometimes with a bluish tint, and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters. Feuilleea cylindrica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
