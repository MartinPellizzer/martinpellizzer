Fallopia pauciflora

Fallopia pauciflora, commonly known as the slender bamboo or Japanese bamboo, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae and is characterized by its tall, slender stems that can grow up to several meters in height. The plant has small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is sometimes considered a weed in certain regions.
