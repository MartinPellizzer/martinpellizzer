Furcraea acaulis

Furcraea acaulis, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a succulent plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are covered in a white, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, greenish-white flowers. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and low maintenance requirements.

Furcraea acaulis is also valued for its ability to thrive in dry conditions, making it suitable for xeriscaping.
