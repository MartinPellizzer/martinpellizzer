Fortunella japonica

Fortunella japonica, commonly known as the Japanese kumquat, is a small evergreen shrub native to Japan and southern China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is closely related to the mandarin orange. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically orange in color when ripe, with a thin, edible peel. The leaves are dark green, glossy, and elliptical in shape, while the flowers are fragrant and white.

Fortunella japonica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit, which can be eaten whole or used in jams and marmalades.
