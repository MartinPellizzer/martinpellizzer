Ficus austrocaledonica var. latifolia

Ficus austrocaledonica var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ficus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific islands.

It is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by greenish-white fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its adaptability, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded outdoor gardens.
