Fraxinus discolor var. juglandifolia

Fraxinus discolor var. juglandifolia, commonly known as the maple-leaf ash, is a variant of the green ash tree characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It features palmate leaves with 5-7 lobes, resembling those of maple trees, which give it its common name. The leaves are typically light green on the upper surface and paler underneath, with a smooth texture. This plant is native to parts of North America and thrives in a variety of soil types.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils along riverbanks and in woodland areas.
