Ficus stoechotricha

Ficus stoechotricha, commonly known as the woolly fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its thick, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round figs that are typically green when mature.

Ficus stoechotricha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
