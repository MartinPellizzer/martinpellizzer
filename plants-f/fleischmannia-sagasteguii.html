Fleischmannia sagasteguii

Fleischmannia sagasteguii, commonly known as the Sagastegui's cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Its flowers bloom during the warmer months, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.
