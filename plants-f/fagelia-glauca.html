Fagelia glauca

Fagelia glauca, commonly known as the blue fagelia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue flowers, which bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has a woody stem and lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
