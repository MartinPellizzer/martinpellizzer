Fimbristylis thwaitesii

Fimbristylis thwaitesii, commonly known as the narrowleaf flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds and streams. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil in aquatic environments.

Fimbristylis thwaitesii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.
