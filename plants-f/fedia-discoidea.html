Fedia discoidea

Fedia discoidea, commonly known as the disc-shaped fedia, is a rare and enigmatic plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique disc-like structure, which is composed of tightly arranged, overlapping leaves that form a compact rosette. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which are followed by capsule-like fruits containing numerous minute seeds. Fedia discoidea is adapted to grow in shaded, moist environments, often found in dense forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and fragile habitat, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
