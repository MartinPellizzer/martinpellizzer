Ficus pedunculosa

Ficus pedunculosa, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a tropical evergreen vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by greenish-white fruits that turn red when ripe. It thrives in humid environments and is often used as a ground cover or climbing plant in gardens.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
