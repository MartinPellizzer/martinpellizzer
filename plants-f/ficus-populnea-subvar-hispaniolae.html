Ficus populnea subvar. hispaniolae

Ficus populnea subvar. hispaniolae, commonly known as the Hispaniola fig, is a species native to the island of Hispaniola.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with thick, waxy leaves that are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are often consumed by local wildlife and humans. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
