Festuca vindobonensis

Festuca vindobonensis, commonly known as the Vienna fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, silver-gray leaves that are often covered with a dense layer of hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Festuca vindobonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
