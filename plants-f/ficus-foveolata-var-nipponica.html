Ficus foveolata var. nipponica

Ficus foveolata var. nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese fig or Nippon fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate. The plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Ficus foveolata var. nipponica is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
