Ficus beniensis

Ficus beniensis, commonly known as the sacred fig or bodhi tree, is a species of fig tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, featuring a thick, buttressed trunk and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, green figs that ripen to a yellowish color, enclosed in a spiky, green syconium. It has a complex root system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

The plant is also notable for its role in religious and cultural practices, particularly in Hinduism and Buddhism.
