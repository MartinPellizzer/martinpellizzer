Fumaria anatolica

Fumaria anatolica, commonly known as the Anatolian smokeplant, is a flowering plant native to Turkey and parts of the Middle East.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, bead-like seed pods that split open when mature, releasing tiny seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Fumaria anatolica is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
