Fritillaria messanensis subsp. glauca

Fritillaria messanensis subsp.

glauca, commonly known as the Messina blue star, is a rare and striking bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its blue to violet bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soils in woodland or rocky habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique coloration.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a conservation concern in some regions.
