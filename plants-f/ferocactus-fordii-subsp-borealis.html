Ferocactus fordii subsp. borealis

Ferocactus fordii subsp.

borealis, commonly known as the northern prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the northern United States and southern Canada. It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are typically green or bluish-green in color.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by edible, red fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and open landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is well adapted to withstand drought and extreme temperature fluctuations.
