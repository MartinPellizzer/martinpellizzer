Fimbristylis cymosa var. multifolia

Fimbristylis cymosa var. multifolia, commonly known as the tufted flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to wetland and marshy environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, commonly known as the sedge family, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers, typical of the Cyperaceae family. It thrives in moist soils and is often found in areas with standing water or saturated ground.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting various forms of wildlife.
