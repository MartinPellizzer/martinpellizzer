Ficus servula

Ficus servula, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in clusters, and it is known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Ficus servula is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and its ability to climb or trail along surfaces.
