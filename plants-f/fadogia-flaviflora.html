Fadogia flaviflora

Fadogia flaviflora, commonly known as the yellow-flowered rock orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the rocky regions of southern Africa.

It typically grows in arid or semi-arid environments, often on rocky outcrops or in sandy soils. The plant features a short, creeping rhizome and produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers with a distinctive scent. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, adapting to the harsh conditions of its habitat.

Fadogia flaviflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been studied for its potential medicinal properties.
