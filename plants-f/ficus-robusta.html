Ficus robusta

Ficus robusta, commonly known as the robust fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Ficus robusta thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for both ornamental and agricultural purposes.
