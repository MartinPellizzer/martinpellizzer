Ficus wamanguana

Ficus wamanguana, commonly known as the Wamanguana fig, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant features broad, glossy leaves that are usually arranged oppositely on the stems. Its fruit, a small, green fig, is edible and contains a sweet, fleshy pulp surrounding the seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
