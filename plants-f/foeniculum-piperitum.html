Foeniculum piperitum

Foeniculum piperitum, commonly known as dill, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features feathery, finely divided leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in umbrella-like clusters. The plant is characterized by its aromatic seeds, which are widely used in cooking and traditional medicine. Dill thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and agricultural fields.

Its botanical structure supports its use as both a culinary spice and a natural remedy.
