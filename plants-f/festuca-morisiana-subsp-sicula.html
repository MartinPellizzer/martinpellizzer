Festuca morisiana subsp. sicula

Festuca morisiana subsp.

sicula, commonly known as Sicilian bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Sicily. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or calcareous habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
