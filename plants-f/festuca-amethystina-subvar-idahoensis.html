Festuca amethystina subvar. idahoensis

Festuca amethystina subvar. idahoensis, commonly known as Idaho bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, dark blue-green foliage and compact growth habit, making it a desirable ornamental plant. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as meadows and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish hue that intensifies in full sun.

It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer.
