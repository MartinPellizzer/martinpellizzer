Ficus hispida var. hastata

Ficus hispida var. hastata, commonly known as the "Velvetleaf Fig" or "Hastate Fig," is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, velvety leaves that are heart-shaped with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, inedible fruits that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus hispida var. hastata is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
