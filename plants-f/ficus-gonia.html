Ficus gonia

Ficus gonia, commonly known as the Gonia fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are edible and often used in traditional cuisines. Ficus gonia thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
