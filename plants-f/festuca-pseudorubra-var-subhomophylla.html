Festuca pseudorubra var. subhomophylla

Festuca pseudorubra var. subhomophylla, commonly known as the narrowleaf fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as alpine meadows and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 2 mm wide, giving it a distinct appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a loose cluster of spikelets, each containing two to three small, spikelet-like flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
