Festuca heldreichii subsp. achaica

Festuca heldreichii subsp.

achaica, commonly known as the Achaic mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
