Fargesia crassinoda

Fargesia crassinoda, commonly known as the broad-leaf arrow bamboo, is a deciduous bamboo species native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often a deep green color. This plant typically grows in mountainous regions and thrives in cool, moist climates. Its foliage provides dense cover, making it suitable for ornamental landscaping.

Fargesia crassinoda is also valued for its ability to form clumps and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
