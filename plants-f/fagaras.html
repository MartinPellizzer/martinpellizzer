Fagaras

Fagaras, commonly known as the Fagaras Mountains, is a mountain range located in Romania, renowned for its unique botanical diversity.

The region is home to several endemic plant species, including the Fagaras primrose (Primula fagaraceae), which thrives in the alpine meadows. The flora of the area is characterized by hardy, cold-resistant plants adapted to the high-altitude environment. Lichens and mosses also play a significant role in the ecological balance of the region.

The botanical features of Fagaras contribute to its status as a protected natural area.
