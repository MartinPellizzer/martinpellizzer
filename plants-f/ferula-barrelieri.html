Ferula barrelieri

Ferula barrelieri, commonly known as the Barrelier's fennel, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots are fibrous and often used in traditional medicine.

Ferula barrelieri is adapted to survive in dry, rocky environments, showcasing drought-resistant features.
