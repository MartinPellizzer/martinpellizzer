Ficus benjamina var. bracteata

Ficus benjamina var. bracteata, commonly known as the Variegated Ficus or Bracteata Fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white or cream variegation, which adds visual interest to indoor spaces. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible figs, though the fruit is rarely seen in cultivation. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate watering to maintain its health and vibrant foliage.

Due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal, it is a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
