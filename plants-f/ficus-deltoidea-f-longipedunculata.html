Ficus deltoidea f. longipedunculata

Ficus deltoidea f. longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-pedunculate fig, is a unique variant of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its elongated peduncles, which are the stalks supporting the inflorescences. This plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a smooth surface and a glossy texture.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
