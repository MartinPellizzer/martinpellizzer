Ficus sagittata var. adhaerens

Ficus sagittata var. adhaerens, commonly known as the climbing fig, is a tropical plant characterized by its vigorous growth habit and ability to climb using aerial roots.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible figs, though these are not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental leaves and relatively easy care requirements.
