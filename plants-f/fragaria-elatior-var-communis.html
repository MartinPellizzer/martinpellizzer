Fragaria elatior var. communis

Fragaria elatior var. communis, commonly known as the wild strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It produces small, edible fruits that are typically red, aromatic, and covered in a white, fuzzy coating. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and a shallow, spreading root system. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, often found in woodland edges and open fields.

The flowers are white with five petals and are followed by the characteristic fruiting bodies that develop from the receptacle.
