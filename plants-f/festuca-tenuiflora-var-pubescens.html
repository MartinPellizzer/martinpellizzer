Festuca tenuiflora var. pubescens

Festuca tenuiflora var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent fine fescue, is a perennial grass species native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in a dense layer of soft, hairy pubescence. The plant typically produces small, inconspicuous blue to purple flowers in late summer, which are clustered in spikelets. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its low maintenance and drought tolerance, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration and landscaping projects.
