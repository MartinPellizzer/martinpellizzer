Ficus auriculigera

Ficus auriculigera, commonly known as the ear-shaped fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with waxy, glandular dots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by round, edible figs. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus auriculigera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
