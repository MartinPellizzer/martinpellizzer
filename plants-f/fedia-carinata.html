Fedia carinata

Fedia carinata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads composed of ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its striking yellow or white flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
