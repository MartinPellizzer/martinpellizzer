Fagopyrum tataricum subsp. potaninii

Fagopyrum tataricum subsp.

potaninii, commonly known as the Siberian buckwheat, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by triangular, winged seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its hardy nature and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
