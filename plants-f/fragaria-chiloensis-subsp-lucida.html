Fragaria chiloensis subsp. lucida

Fragaria chiloensis subsp.

lucida, commonly known as the Chilean strawberry, is a perennial herb native to the coastal regions of southern South America. It produces small, bright red berries that are often used in local cuisines and traditional medicine.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with hairy surfaces and grows in rocky or sandy soils along the coastline. It is adapted to coastal climates with high salinity and strong winds.

Its botanical features make it a unique species within the Fragaria genus.
