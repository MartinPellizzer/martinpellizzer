Fritillaria hookeri

Fritillaria hookeri, commonly known as the Hooker's fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish-brown in color, with a distinctive mottled pattern. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Fritillaria hookeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
