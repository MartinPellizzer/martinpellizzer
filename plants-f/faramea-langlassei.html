Faramea langlassei

Faramea langlassei, commonly known as the Langlasse's faramea, is a shrub native to the rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of white or pale pink. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are usually elliptical with a glossy surface, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Faramea langlassei is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to moist, well-drained soils.
