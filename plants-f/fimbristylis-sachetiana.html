Fimbristylis sachetiana

Fimbristylis sachetiana, commonly known as the sachet sedge, is a small perennial herb native to parts of Asia and Australia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale green flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil in wetland areas.

Fimbristylis sachetiana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.
