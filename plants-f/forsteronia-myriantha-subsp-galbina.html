Forsteronia myriantha subsp. galbina

Forsteronia myriantha subsp.

galbina, commonly known as the rock fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa. It features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a waxy texture.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale green, followed by edible, fleshy fruits resembling small figs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is cultivated in gardens and landscapes in suitable climates.
