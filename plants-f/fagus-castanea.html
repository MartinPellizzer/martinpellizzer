Fagus castanea

Fagus castanea, commonly known as the European beech, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, oval leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks, which are an important food source for various wildlife. Fagus castanea thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mixed forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and contributing to soil stability through its extensive root system.
