Fritillaria tortifolia var. plena

Fritillaria tortifolia var. plena, commonly known as the full-flowered Turk's cap lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange to reddish in color, with a distinctive cap-like structure at the top. The plant produces clusters of showy, cup-shaped blooms that are borne on tall, slender stems. Its foliage consists of strap-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique and eye-catching floral display.
