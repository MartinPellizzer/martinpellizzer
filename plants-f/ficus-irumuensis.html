Ficus irumuensis

Ficus irumuensis, commonly known as the Irumu fig, is a species of fig tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a smooth surface. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and have a sweet, fig-like flavor. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus irumuensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food in its native habitat.
