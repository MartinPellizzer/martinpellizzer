Fleischmannia splendens

Fleischmannia splendens, commonly known as the showy goldenbush, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, blue-green leaves and produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Fleischmannia splendens thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a suitable ornamental plant in arid landscapes.

It is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and drought tolerance.
