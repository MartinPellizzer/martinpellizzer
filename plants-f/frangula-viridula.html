Frangula viridula

Frangula viridula, commonly known as greenbeard buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, dark berries that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife. Frangula viridula is often found in rocky or dry soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

It has been used historically in traditional medicine for its laxative properties.
