Fimbristylis fusca subsp. fulvescens

Fimbristylis fusca subsp.

fulvescens, commonly known as the red-stemmed flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its stems are often reddish in color, which is a distinguishing feature of this subspecies.

It plays an important role in wetland ecosystems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
