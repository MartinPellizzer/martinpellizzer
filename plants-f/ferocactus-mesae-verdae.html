Ferocactus mesae-verdae

Ferocactus mesae-verdae, commonly known as the Sonoran prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are covered in spines and are capable of storing water. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a tuna, is edible and has a sweet, juicy pulp.

Ferocactus mesae-verdae plays an important ecological role by providing food and shelter for various desert wildlife.
