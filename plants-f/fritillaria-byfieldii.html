Fritillaria byfieldii

Fritillaria byfieldii, commonly known as the Byfield's fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or red, with a distinctive blackish-purple throat. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a central bulb. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or alpine habitats.

Fritillaria byfieldii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
