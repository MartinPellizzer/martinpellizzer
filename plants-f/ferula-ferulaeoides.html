Ferula ferulaeoides

Ferula ferulaeoides, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ferula, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in arid environments.

Ferula ferulaeoides is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its aromatic properties.
