Ficus magnifica

Ficus magnifica, commonly known as the sacred fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, yellowish fruits called figs. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also revered in various cultures for its spiritual significance.

The tree is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
