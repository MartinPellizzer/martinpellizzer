Festuca varia

Festuca varia, commonly known as Variable Fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant has fine, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Festuca varia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in gardens or as a ground cover.

It is also known for its ability to tolerate shade and moderate foot traffic.
