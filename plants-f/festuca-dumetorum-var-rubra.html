Festuca dumetorum var. rubra

Festuca dumetorum var. rubra, commonly known as red mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive reddish hues in its foliage and inflorescence. The plant produces spikelets that are often tinged with red or purple, contributing to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ecological benefits.
