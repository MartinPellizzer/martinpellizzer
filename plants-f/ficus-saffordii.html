Ficus saffordii

Ficus saffordii, commonly known as the Safford fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico, where it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. It produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus saffordii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to adapt to harsh environmental conditions.
