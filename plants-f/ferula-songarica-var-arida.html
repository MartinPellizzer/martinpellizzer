Ferula songarica var. arida

Ferula songarica var. arida, commonly known as the desert cow parsley, is a perennial herb native to arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant has fibrous roots and hairy leaves adapted to withstand harsh, dry environments. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in steppe and desert margins.

Its aromatic foliage and resilience make it a notable species in its ecological niche.
