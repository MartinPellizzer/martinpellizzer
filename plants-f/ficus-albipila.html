Ficus albipila

Ficus albipila, commonly known as the white-veined fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent white veins. The plant produces small, inedible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus albipila is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.
