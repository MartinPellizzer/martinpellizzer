Fregirardia riparia

Fregirardia riparia, commonly known as the riverbank fritillary, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows along riverbanks and moist meadows, thriving in well-drained, sandy soils. The plant produces striking violet-blue flowers with intricate patterns, which attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slight hairy texture.

Fregirardia riparia is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
