Ficus trichocarpa

Ficus trichocarpa, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is characterized by its dense, trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits that are edible for some animals. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and is often used as a ground cover or ornamental plant.

Ficus trichocarpa is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to partial shade.
