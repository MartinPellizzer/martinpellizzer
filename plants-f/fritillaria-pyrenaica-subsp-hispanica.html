Fritillaria pyrenaica subsp. hispanica

Fritillaria pyrenaica subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It produces striking bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color, with a distinctive waxy texture.

The plant thrives in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The subspecies is closely related to other members of the Fritillaria genus, sharing similar botanical characteristics.
