Ficus leprieurii var. sessilis

Ficus leprieurii var. sessilis, commonly known as the Variegated Ficus or the Marble Queen Ficus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, greenish-white fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its attractive foliage, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor landscaping.
