Fuchsia regia

Fuchsia regia, commonly known as the royal fuchsia, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep purple to vibrant red, often with a distinctive white or yellow throat. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are glossy and arranged alternately along the stems. Fuchsia regia thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for both horticultural and botanical study.
