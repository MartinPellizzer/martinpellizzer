Feuilleea graciliflora

Feuilleea graciliflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered feuilleea, is a rare and delicate plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It features elongated, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a unique visual appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pale yellow and white, which bloom during the wet season. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the rich, moist soil of its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss, Feuilleea graciliflora is considered endangered and is primarily found in protected botanical gardens.
