Fritillaria hericaulis

Fritillaria hericaulis, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a spherical bulb. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Fritillaria hericaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
