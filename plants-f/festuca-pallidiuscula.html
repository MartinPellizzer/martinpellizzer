Festuca pallidiuscula

Festuca pallidiuscula, commonly known as the paleleaf fescue, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths and has narrow, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to whitish flowers, which are characteristic of the Festuca genus. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects in arid and semi-arid regions.
