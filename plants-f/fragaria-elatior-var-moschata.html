Fragaria elatior var. moschata

Fragaria elatior var. moschata, commonly known as the musky strawberry, is a cultivar of the strawberry plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, intensely flavored berries that have a distinct musky aroma. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces flowers that are typically white with yellow stamens. The fruits are typically harvested in late summer and are prized for their unique scent and taste.

This variety is often grown in home gardens for its ornamental value and culinary use.
