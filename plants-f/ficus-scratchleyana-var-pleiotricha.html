Ficus scratchleyana var. pleiotricha

Ficus scratchleyana var. pleiotricha, commonly known as the pleiotricha fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

The species is also valued for its ability to adapt to various soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
