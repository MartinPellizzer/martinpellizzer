Fraxinus angustifolia f. pendula

Fraxinus angustifolia f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping ash, is a cultivar of the ash tree characterized by its gracefully drooping branches.

It features narrow, elongated leaves that are typically a dark green on top and lighter green underneath. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that disperse in the wind. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This ornamental tree is often used in landscaping for its elegant, cascading appearance.
