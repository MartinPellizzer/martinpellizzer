Festuca paniculata var. moleroi

Festuca paniculata var. moleroi, commonly known as Moleroi fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green foliage and tufted growth habit, making it visually appealing in gardens. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Moleroi fescue is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in dry, rocky environments.
