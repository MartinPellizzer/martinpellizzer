Fagus atropunicea

Fagus atropunicea, commonly known as the red-barked beech, is a species of beech native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-barked trunk and its lance-shaped, glossy leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, clustered flowers and is known for its ability to thrive in cool, moist environments. Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
