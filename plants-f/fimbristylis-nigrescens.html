Fimbristylis nigrescens

Fimbristylis nigrescens, commonly known as black-stemmed flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with the lower stems often turning a dark purple or blackish color, giving it its common name.

Fimbristylis nigrescens is often found in disturbed or aquatic habitats and is an important component of wetland ecosystems.
