Festuca sororia

Festuca sororia, commonly known as slender fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks. The plant has fine, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue to purple flowers. It is characterized by its dense growth habit and ability to form compact clumps.

Festuca sororia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in natural habitats.
