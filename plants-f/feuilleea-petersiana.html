Feuilleea petersiana

Feuilleea petersiana, commonly known as the Petersian featherleaf, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are finely divided and covered in a soft, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, typically blooming during the cooler months. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
