Ficus romeroi

Ficus romeroi, commonly known as the Mexican fig tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible fig fruits, which are often used in traditional cuisine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus romeroi is also valued for its ability to adapt to indoor environments, making it a popular choice for houseplants.
