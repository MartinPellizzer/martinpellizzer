Ferocactus pubispinus

Ferocactus pubispinus, commonly known as the spiny pear cactus, is a species native to the deserts of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in sharp, spiny areoles. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the warmer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Its adaptations include a thick, waxy cuticle to reduce water loss and deep roots to access groundwater.

Ferocactus pubispinus is often cultivated for its ornamental value and resilience in arid environments.
