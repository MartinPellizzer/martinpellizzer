Fingardia kobi

Fingardia kobi, commonly known as the silver leaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, velvety silver-green leaves and delicate, fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees without deriving nutrients from them. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pale pink, bloom in clusters and are often pollinated by nocturnal moths.

Fingardia kobi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in shaded garden environments.
