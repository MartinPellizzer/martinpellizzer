Ficus nasuta

Ficus nasuta, commonly known as the nasute fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible figs that are typically pale yellow to green in color. It thrives in humid environments and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Ficus nasuta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
