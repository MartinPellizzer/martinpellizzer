Ficus subrepanda

Ficus subrepanda, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its dense, trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Ficus subrepanda is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens due to its ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.

Its adaptability and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for both ornamental and functional landscaping.
