Ferolia jackiana

Ferolia jackiana, commonly known as the Chinese prickly ash, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature, turning red when ripe. Ferolia jackiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some traditional medicinal uses.

It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment.
