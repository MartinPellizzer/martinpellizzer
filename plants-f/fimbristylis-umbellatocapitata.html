Fimbristylis umbellatocapitata

Fimbristylis umbellatocapitata, commonly known as the clustered spike sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a dense cluster of spikelets at the top of its stem. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in an umbellate or capitulate inflorescence, giving it its scientific name. It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Fimbristylis umbellatocapitata is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies.
