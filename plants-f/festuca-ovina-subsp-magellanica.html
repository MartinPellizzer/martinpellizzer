Festuca ovina subsp. magellanica

Festuca ovina subsp.

magellanica, commonly known as Magellanic bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to southern South America. It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and bluish-green coloration, which gives it its common name.

The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in cool, moist climates. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and erosion control.
