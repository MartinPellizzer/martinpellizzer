Fragaria virginiana

Fragaria virginiana, commonly known as wild strawberry, is a perennial herb native to North America.

It produces small, edible berries that are often mistaken for true strawberries. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and a trailing growth habit. Its flowers are white with five petals and are followed by the characteristic red, fleshy fruits.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
