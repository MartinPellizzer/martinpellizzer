Foeniculum vulgare var. dulce

Foeniculum vulgare var. dulce, commonly known as sweet fennel, is a biennial or perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features feathery, aromatic leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant is characterized by its hollow, grooved stems and a distinctive anise-like fragrance. Its seeds, often used as a spice, are harvested when fully mature.

Sweet fennel thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and culinary use.
