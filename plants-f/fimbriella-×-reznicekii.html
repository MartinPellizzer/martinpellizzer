Fimbriella × reznicekii

Fimbriella × reznicekii, commonly known as the hybrid sea holly, is a rare and fascinating botanical hybrid that results from the cross between Fimbriella capillaris and Fimbriella tenuis.

It is characterized by its unique, intricate floral structures that resemble a crown of fine, hair-like filaments, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in brackish or saline environments, showcasing its adaptability to coastal or estuarine habitats. Its flowers are often blue or violet, and it produces small, elongated fruits that aid in its dispersal.

Due to its rarity and specific ecological requirements, Fimbriella × reznicekii is of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
