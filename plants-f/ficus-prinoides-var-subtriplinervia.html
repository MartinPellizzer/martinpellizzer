Ficus prinoides var. subtriplinervia

Ficus prinoides var. subtriplinervia, commonly known as the three-veined fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical rainforests. The plant features glossy, ovate leaves with a distinct three-veined pattern on the underside, which is a key identifying characteristic. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are often consumed by birds and small mammals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
