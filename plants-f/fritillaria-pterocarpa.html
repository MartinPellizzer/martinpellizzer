Fritillaria pterocarpa

Fritillaria pterocarpa, commonly known as the mountain fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color, often with contrasting black spots. The plant has a fibrous root system and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Fritillaria pterocarpa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
