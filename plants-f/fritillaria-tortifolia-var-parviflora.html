Fritillaria tortifolia var. parviflora

Fritillaria tortifolia var. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf bellflower, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features lance-shaped, gray-green leaves and produces clusters of bell-shaped, pale yellow to orange flowers. The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. It blooms in late spring to early summer, adding a striking visual element to alpine gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is cultivated in specialized horticultural collections.
