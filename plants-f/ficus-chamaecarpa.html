Ficus chamaecarpa

Ficus chamaecarpa, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by round, greenish-white fruits. It is often cultivated as a ground cover due to its dense foliage and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
