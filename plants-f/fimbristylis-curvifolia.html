Fimbristylis curvifolia

Fimbristylis curvifolia, commonly known as the curly hair sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and typically grows in wet or marshy habitats. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of white or pale pink flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, often in shallow water or along the edges of ponds and streams.

Fimbristylis curvifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in water gardens.
