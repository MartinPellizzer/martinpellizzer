Fritillaria latakiensis

Fritillaria latakiensis, commonly known as the Persian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountains of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to thrive in arid and rocky environments. Its bulbs are underground storage organs that support the plant through periods of dormancy.

Fritillaria latakiensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and resilience in challenging climates.
