Festuca violacea subsp. aurata

Festuca violacea subsp.

aurata, commonly known as the golden bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive golden-yellow inflorescence, which contrasts with its dark green foliage.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for use in ornamental landscaping and turfgrass applications.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
