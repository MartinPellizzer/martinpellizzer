Feuilleea rostrata

Feuilleea rostrata, commonly known as the Rosy-Beak Plant, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with a pale pink or red hue. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that range in color from white to deep violet, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Feuilleea rostrata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Its unique foliage and vibrant blooms make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical and subtropical regions.
