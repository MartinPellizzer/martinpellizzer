Fraxinus bungeana

Fraxinus bungeana, commonly known as the Chinese ash, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, which are followed by samaroid fruits with wings. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The plant has historical uses in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
