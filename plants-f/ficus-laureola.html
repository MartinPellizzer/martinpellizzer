Ficus laureola

Ficus laureola, commonly known as the sacred fig or Indian laurel, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are a food source for various animals. Its roots are known for their ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species.

Ficus laureola is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
