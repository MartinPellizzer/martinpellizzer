Ficus procera var. crassiramea

Ficus procera var. crassiramea, commonly known as the thick-leaf ficus, is a variant of the weeping fig tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, thick, and leathery leaves that are often a deep green with a glossy surface. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with a compact and bushy form. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, greenish fruits.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
