Ficus trichosphaera

Ficus trichosphaera, commonly known as the woolly fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits.

Ficus trichosphaera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
