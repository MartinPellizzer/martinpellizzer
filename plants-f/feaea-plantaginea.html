Feaea plantaginea

Feaea plantaginea, commonly known as the silverleaf lantana, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has aromatic, silver-gray leaves that are often used in traditional herbal medicine. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Feaea plantaginea is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
