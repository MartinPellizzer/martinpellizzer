Flemingia procumbens

Flemingia procumbens, commonly known as the prostrate Flemingia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically reaches a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals, and are clustered in racemes.

Flemingia procumbens is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.
