Fagraea heterophylla

Fagraea heterophylla, commonly known as the variable-leaf fagraea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, with leaves ranging from ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by woody, orange-red fruits containing seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
