Ficus lentiginosa

Ficus lentiginosa, commonly known as the black ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The plant is characterized by its dark, almost black, bark and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy texture.

Ficus lentiginosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
