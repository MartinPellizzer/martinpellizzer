Fumaria officinalis subsp. wirtgenii

Fumaria officinalis subsp.

wirtgenii, commonly known as the Wirtgen's bladder-nut, is a member of the Fumariaceae family. It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its erect, branched stems and opposite, ovate leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in racemes, which are followed by inflated, bladder-like pods. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
