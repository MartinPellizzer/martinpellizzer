Ficus lucanda

Ficus lucanda, commonly known as the African fig tree, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a flowering plant in the Moraceae family, characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, glossy bark. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits. Ficus lucanda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.

It is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and providing shade in urban landscapes.
