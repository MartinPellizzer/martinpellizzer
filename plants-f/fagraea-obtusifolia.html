Fagraea obtusifolia

Fagraea obtusifolia, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white, waxy flowers that bloom in late summer. The tree produces small, red, fleshy fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a pulp. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

The plant thrives in humid climates and is adaptable to various soil types.
