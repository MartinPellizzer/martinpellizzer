Ficus christianii

Ficus christianii, commonly known as the African rubber fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly found in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by fig fruits that are edible for some animals but not generally consumed by humans.

Ficus christianii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
