Ficus diversifolia var. ovoidea

Ficus diversifolia var. ovoidea, commonly known as the egg-shaped fig tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically round and smooth. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
