Ficus namalalensis

Ficus namalalensis, commonly known as the Namalalensis fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the island of Madagascar and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Ficus namalalensis is typically found in moist, shaded areas within humid forests.

Its unique botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists.
