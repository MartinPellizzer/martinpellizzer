Feuilleea maynensis

Feuilleea maynensis, commonly known as the silverleaf fern, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that exhibit a striking silver sheen on the underside. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Feuilleea maynensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in the right conditions.
