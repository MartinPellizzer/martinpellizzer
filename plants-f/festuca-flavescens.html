Festuca flavescens

Festuca flavescens, commonly known as bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as wet meadows and riparian zones. It has a fibrous root system and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Festuca flavescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting wildlife.
