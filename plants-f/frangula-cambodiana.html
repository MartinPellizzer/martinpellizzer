Frangula cambodiana

Frangula cambodiana, commonly known as the Cambodian buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters. The plant is notable for its reddish-brown, woody fruit capsules that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Frangula cambodiana is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional remedies for its laxative effects.
