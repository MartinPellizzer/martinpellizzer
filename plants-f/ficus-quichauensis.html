Ficus quichauensis

Ficus quichauensis, commonly known as the Quichaua fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. It is often found growing in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests.

The fruit, which is a fig, is edible and serves as a food source for various wildlife species.
