Ferula sharifii

Ferula sharifii, commonly known as the Afghan cow parsley, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, umbrella-shaped inflorescences that produce yellow flowers. The plant has aromatic, fibrous leaves and is often found in rocky or semi-arid environments. Its roots are fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine.

Ferula sharifii is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
