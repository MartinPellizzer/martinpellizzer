Fragaria chiloensis subsp. sandwicensis

Fragaria chiloensis subsp.

sandwicensis, commonly known as the Hawaiian strawberry, is a perennial herb native to the Hawaiian Islands. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, red, edible fruits that resemble strawberries.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in coastal forests and wetlands. Its flowers are white with five petals and are pollinated by insects.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance and is considered a rare and endangered plant in its native habitat.
