Ficus podophylla

Ficus podophylla, commonly known as the velvetleaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid environments with bright, indirect light.

Ficus podophylla is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
