Ficus stuhlmannii var. glabrifolia

Ficus stuhlmannii var. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are typically smooth and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
