Fegimanra acuminatissima

Fegimanra acuminatissima, commonly known as the sharp-pointed beauty, is a rare flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It features slender, erect stems covered in a dense layer of fine, silver-white hairs. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly serrated edge, and are arranged alternately along the stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers with five petals, typically in shades of pale pink to white.
