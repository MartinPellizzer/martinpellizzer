Fritillaria poluninii

Fritillaria poluninii, commonly known as the Siberian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with distinctive red or purple markings. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, stout stem. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

Fritillaria poluninii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
