Fiedleria cretica

Fiedleria cretica, commonly known as the Mediterranean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, blue to violet flowers. The leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
