Fraxinus pleurodes

Fraxinus pleurodes, commonly known as the western ash.

It is a deciduous tree native to western North America, characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin, typically turning yellow in the fall. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands and along streambanks.
