Festuca sciuroides var. major

Festuca sciuroides var. major, commonly known as the tall bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, blue-green blades and forms dense, fine-textured turf. This variety is often used in lawns and erosion control due to its hardiness and adaptability. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires moderate to high levels of sunlight.

The plant is known for its ability to withstand foot traffic and maintain a lush appearance throughout the growing season.
