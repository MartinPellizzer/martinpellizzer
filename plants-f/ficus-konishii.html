Ficus konishii

Ficus konishii, commonly known as the Konishii fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fig-like fruits that are edible. Ficus konishii is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in moderate light conditions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
