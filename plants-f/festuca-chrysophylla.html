Festuca chrysophylla

Festuca chrysophylla, commonly known as golden feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance, and its delicate, feathery plumes that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its elegant, airy foliage.

Festuca chrysophylla is also noted for its hardiness and ability to tolerate harsh climatic conditions.
