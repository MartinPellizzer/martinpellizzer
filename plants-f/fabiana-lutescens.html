Fabiana lutescens

Fabiana lutescens, commonly known as the yellow-flowered fabiana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are often clustered in terminal panicles, and it produces small, round berries that are edible.

Fabiana lutescens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
