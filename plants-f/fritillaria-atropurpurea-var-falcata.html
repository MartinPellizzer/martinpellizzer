Fritillaria atropurpurea var. falcata

Fritillaria atropurpurea var. falcata, commonly known as the purple-fall fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or reddish-purple in color. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, stout stem that supports its showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens and alpine settings.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated for its unique and dramatic floral display.
