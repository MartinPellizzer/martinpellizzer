Fissistigma cordifolia

Fissistigma cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf fissistigma, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are often white or pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in some regions for its aesthetic appeal.
