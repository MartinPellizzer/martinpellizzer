Fraxinus ornus var. danubialis

Fraxinus ornus var. danubialis, commonly known as the Danube hornbeam, is a variant of the common hornbeam.

It is a small to medium-sized tree native to Central Europe, particularly along the Danube River. The plant features dark green, oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring. Its bark is smooth and gray, with a distinctive flaky texture.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
