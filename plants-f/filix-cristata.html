Filix cristata

Filix cristata, commonly known as the crown fern, is a striking evergreen fern native to the forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright fronds that can grow up to 3 meters in height, with a distinctive crown-like shape formed by the overlapping fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, acidic soil. Its spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, and it reproduces through both sexual and asexual means.

Filix cristata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
