Fuchsia simplicicaulis

Fuchsia simplicicaulis, commonly known as the simple-leaved fuchsia, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers in shades of red, purple, and pink, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are simple, ovate, and often covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Fuchsia simplicicaulis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
