Fagara copallifera

Fagara copallifera, commonly known as the yellow-leafed fagara, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic, compound leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by yellow, fleshy fruits that are edible. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Fagara copallifera thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
