Frailea erythracantha

Frailea erythracantha, commonly known as the red-armed rock cactus, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, branching structure and vibrant red to orange spines. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, bloom in late summer and are relatively short-lived.

Frailea erythracantha is a popular choice for rock gardens due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
