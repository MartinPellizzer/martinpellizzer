Fraxinus excelsior var. implicata

Fraxinus excelsior var. implicata, commonly known as the warty ash, is a variant of the common ash tree characterized by its distinctive warty or knobby bark.

It is a deciduous tree native to Europe, typically found in moist, shaded woodlands. The leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with serrated margins and a dark green upper surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by winged samaras as its fruit.

The plant is valued for its ecological role and historical use in traditional medicine.
