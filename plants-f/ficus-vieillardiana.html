Ficus vieillardiana

Ficus vieillardiana, commonly known as the Vieillard's fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves with a smooth texture and a dark green upper surface. It produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
