Fasciculochloa sparshottiorum

Fasciculochloa sparshottiorum, commonly known as the New Zealand hairgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to New Zealand.

It forms dense, mat-like growths with slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for native invertebrates and birds.
