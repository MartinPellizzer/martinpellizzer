Ficus ouangliensis

Ficus ouangliensis, commonly known as the Ouangliensis fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of western Africa, particularly in countries like Cameroon and Gabon. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible figs that are typically yellow when ripe.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
