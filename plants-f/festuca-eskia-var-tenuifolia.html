Festuca eskia var. tenuifolia

Festuca eskia var. tenuifolia, commonly known as slender meadow fescue, is a cool-season grass native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. This variety is typically found in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its compact growth makes it suitable for use in ornamental landscapes and erosion control.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer.
