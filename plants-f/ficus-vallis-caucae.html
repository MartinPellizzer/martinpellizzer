Ficus vallis-caucae

Ficus vallis-caucae, commonly known as the mountain fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of South America, particularly in the Andes. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped or oval in form. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig-like fruits.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
