Ficus rowelliana

Ficus rowelliana, commonly known as the Rowell's fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a slightly waxy texture.

Ficus rowelliana is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in both indoor and outdoor environments.
