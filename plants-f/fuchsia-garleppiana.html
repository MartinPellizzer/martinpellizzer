Fuchsia garleppiana

Fuchsia garleppiana, commonly known as the Chilean fuchsia, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces showy, pendulous flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and its stems are typically woody and branched. Fuchsia garleppiana thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often used in gardens and as a houseplant.
