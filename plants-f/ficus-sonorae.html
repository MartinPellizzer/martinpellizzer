Ficus sonorae

Ficus sonorae, commonly known as the Sonoran fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Sonoran Desert region, spanning parts of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its dense, broad leaves and produces small, edible fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh desert conditions.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is often found in rocky soils and along stream banks.
