Fuchsia unduavensis

Fuchsia unduavensis, commonly known as the velvet fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep purples to soft pinks. The plant produces showy bracts that are often mistaken for petals, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Fuchsia unduavensis is valued in horticulture for its vibrant blooms and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
