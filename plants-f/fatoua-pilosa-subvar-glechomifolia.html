Fatoua pilosa subvar. glechomifolia

Fatoua pilosa subvar. glechomifolia, commonly known as the woolly fatoua, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Africa.

It features a sprawling growth habit with hairy, silver-gray leaves that are lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, blue-violet flowers arranged in dense clusters, typically blooming in the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy choice for arid or semi-arid climates.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its silvery foliage and prolonged flowering period.
