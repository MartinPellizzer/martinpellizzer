Freziera mexicana

Freziera mexicana, commonly known as Mexican frostflower, is a flowering plant native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its showy, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and thrives in full sun. Its foliage is usually grayish-green, and it produces small, white to pinkish flowers in the spring.

Freziera mexicana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens due to its attractive appearance.
