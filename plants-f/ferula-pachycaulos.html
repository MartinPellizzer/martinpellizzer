Ferula pachycaulos

Ferula pachycaulos, commonly known as the thick-stemmed ferula, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and tall, narrow leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flower clusters that bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its aromatic leaves and stems contain volatile oils, which have been used in traditional medicine for their potential anti-inflammatory properties.

Ferula pachycaulos is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.
