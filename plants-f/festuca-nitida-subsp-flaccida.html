Festuca nitida subsp. flaccida

Festuca nitida subsp.

flaccida, commonly known as the slender bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Asia and North America. It is characterized by its fine, dark green foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats or low-growing clumps.

The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, blue-gray flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for landscaping in arid and semi-arid regions.
