Fuchsia apetala

Fuchsia apetala, commonly known as the naked flower fuchsia, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces striking, pendulous flowers with vibrant colors ranging from red to purple. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves and the absence of petals in its flowers, which are instead composed of modified sepals. Fuchsia apetala thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its unique floral structure makes it a subject of interest in botanical studies.
