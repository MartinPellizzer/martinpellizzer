Fraxinus ornus f. sublinearis

Fraxinus ornus f. sublinearis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved weeping ash, is a cultivar of the common ash tree.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically narrower than those of the typical species. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in pendulous clusters, which are followed by winged fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This ornamental tree is often used in landscaping for its elegant, cascading appearance.
