Ficus spathulifolia var. substipitata

Ficus spathulifolia var. substipitata, commonly known as the Shrub Ficus, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are thick and leathery, providing a sturdy structure for the plant.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
