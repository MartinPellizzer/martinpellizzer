Ficus kallicarpa var. crassinervia

Ficus kallicarpa var. crassinervia, commonly known as the broad-veined fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent, broad veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a fig, which is typically small and edible.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
