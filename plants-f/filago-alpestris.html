Filago alpestris

Filago alpestris, commonly known as the alpine filago, is a flowering plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single flower. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and cool climates. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in fine hairs, adapting to harsh environmental conditions.

Filago alpestris produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer, contributing to the biodiversity of high-altitude ecosystems.
