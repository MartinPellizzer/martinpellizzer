Feuilleea daulensis

Feuilleea daulensis, commonly known as the golden leaf fern, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its intricate, feathery fronds that exhibit a striking golden hue under sunlight. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its reproductive system involves the production of spores rather than seeds, a common trait among ferns.

Feuilleea daulensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in its native regions.
