Fagara alata var. beecheyana

Fagara alata var. beecheyana, commonly known as the glossy buckthorn, is a shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red berries that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other tropical regions for horticultural purposes.
