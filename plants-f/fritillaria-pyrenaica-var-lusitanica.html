Fritillaria pyrenaica var. lusitanica

Fritillaria pyrenaica var. lusitanica, commonly known as the Pyrenean fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or yellow with red or purple markings. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in harsh mountain environments, and it blooms in late spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
