Ficus rupium

Ficus rupium, commonly known as the rock fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Europe, North Africa, and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its rough, woody stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits.

Ficus rupium typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is well adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
