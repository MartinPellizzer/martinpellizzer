Ficus cyrtophylla

Ficus cyrtophylla, commonly known as the Australian fig or the silver-leafed fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically small and sweet. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Ficus cyrtophylla is also valued for its ability to adapt to various growing conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
