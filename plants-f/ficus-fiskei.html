Ficus fiskei

Ficus fiskei, commonly known as the Fiske's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hair. The plant produces small, edible figs that are typically green when mature, though they may change color depending on the variety. Ficus fiskei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

Its growth habit includes both terrestrial and epiphytic forms, allowing it to thrive in diverse environments.
