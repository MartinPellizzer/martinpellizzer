Fraxinus retusa var. henryana

Fraxinus retusa var. henryana, commonly known as the Chinese ash or Henry's ash, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, yellowish-green catkins in early spring, which are its primary means of reproduction. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
