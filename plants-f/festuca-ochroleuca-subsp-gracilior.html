Festuca ochroleuca subsp. gracilior

Festuca ochroleuca subsp.

gracilior, commonly known as slender meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and wetlands.

The plant has slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of spikelets, which are usually pale green to yellowish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wet environments.
