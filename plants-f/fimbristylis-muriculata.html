Fimbristylis muriculata

Fimbristylis muriculata, commonly known as the rough false witchgrass, is a species of grass native to Australia.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in wetlands, swamps, and other moist environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like clusters of flowers. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a dense, tufted growth habit.

Fimbristylis muriculata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
