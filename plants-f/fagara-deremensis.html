Fagara deremensis

Fagara deremensis, commonly known as the West African prickly ash, is a member of the Rutaceae family, characterized by its aromatic, often fragrant leaves and flowers.

It is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of West Africa, typically found in humid lowland forests. The plant features opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves, with small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruits are small, round, and yellowish, containing several seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has traditional uses in local medicine.
