Fluminaria pinifolia

Fluminaria pinifolia, commonly known as the needleleaf fluminaria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Fluminaria pinifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and blooms.
