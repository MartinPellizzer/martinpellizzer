Filipendula ulmaria f. apetala

Filipendula ulmaria f. apetala, commonly known as the white sweet clover, is a botanical variant of the common meadow sweet.

This plant is characterized by its reduced or absent flowers, which distinguish it from the typical form of Filipendula ulmaria. It exhibits a herbaceous growth habit with opposite, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are often clustered in panicles.

It is typically found in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and along stream banks.
