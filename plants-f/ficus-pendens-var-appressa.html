Ficus pendens var. appressa

Ficus pendens var. appressa, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, cascading foliage, with small, glossy, dark green leaves that hang in drooping clusters. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by round, yellowish fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil and indirect sunlight.

Due to its attractive appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant indoors or in gardens.
