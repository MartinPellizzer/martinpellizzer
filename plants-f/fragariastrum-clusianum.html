Fragariastrum clusianum

Fragariastrum clusianum, commonly known as the Clusian strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, followed by edible berries that are similar in appearance to true strawberries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a serrated margin, while the fruit develops in clusters.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and culinary qualities.
