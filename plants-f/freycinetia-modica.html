Freycinetia modica

Freycinetia modica, commonly known as the red-veined lily or red-veined banana.

It is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent red veins. It produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by edible, banana-like fruits.

Freycinetia modica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
