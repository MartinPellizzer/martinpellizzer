Filago montana

Filago montana, commonly known as mountain filago, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are usually narrow and linear, while its flowers are small and clustered in a composite head.

Filago montana is often found in meadows and grasslands at high elevations, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
