Fedia locusta

Fedia locusta, commonly known as the golden mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy yellow-orange ray flowers surrounding a central disk of smaller florets, making it visually striking. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Fedia locusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
