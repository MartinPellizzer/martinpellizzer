Fobea

Fobea, commonly known as the mountain daisy, is a genus of flowering plants in the sunflower family, Asteraceae.

It is native to various regions in South America, particularly the Andes, where it thrives in high-altitude environments. The plant features showy, daisy-like flowers with bright yellow centers and white or pale blue petals. Fobea species are typically herbaceous perennials, often growing in rocky or alpine soils.

They are valued for their ornamental appeal and are sometimes cultivated in gardens for their hardy nature and vibrant blooms.
