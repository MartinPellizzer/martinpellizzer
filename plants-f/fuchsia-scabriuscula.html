Fuchsia scabriuscula

Fuchsia scabriuscula, commonly known as the rough-leaved fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured leaves and clusters of vibrant pink or purple flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Fuchsia scabriuscula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its showy blooms.
