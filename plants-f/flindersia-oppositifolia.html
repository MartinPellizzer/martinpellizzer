Flindersia oppositifolia

Flindersia oppositifolia, commonly known as the black bean tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement and often have a slightly hairy texture. The tree produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by woody pods containing flat, bean-like seeds. Its bark is thick and rough, and it can grow up to 30 meters tall in its natural habitat.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
