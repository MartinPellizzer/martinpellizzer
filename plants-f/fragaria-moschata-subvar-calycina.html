Fragaria moschata subvar. calycina

Fragaria moschata subvar. calycina, commonly known as the sweet william strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically pink or white, followed by small, sweet-tasting berries. The leaves are trifoliate, with heart-shaped leaflets, and the plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sun. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The subvariety is distinguished by its more compact growth habit and slightly different floral characteristics compared to the main species.
