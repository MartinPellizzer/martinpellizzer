Ficus subrotundifolia

Ficus subrotundifolia, commonly known as the glossy-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its thick, waxy, and often glossy leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually ovate to sub-rounded in shape, with a smooth margin and a prominent midrib.

Ficus subrotundifolia is valued for its ornamental foliage and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
