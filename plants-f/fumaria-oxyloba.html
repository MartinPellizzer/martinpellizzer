Fumaria oxyloba

Fumaria oxyloba, commonly known as the oxylbe or red balm, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, spurred flowers that range in color from pink to red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Fumaria oxyloba is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
