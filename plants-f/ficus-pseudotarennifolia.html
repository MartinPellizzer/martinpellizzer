Ficus pseudotarennifolia

Ficus pseudotarennifolia, commonly known as the Philippine fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically broad and ovate. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus pseudotarennifolia is also valued for its ability to adapt to various soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
