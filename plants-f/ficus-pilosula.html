Ficus pilosula

Ficus pilosula, commonly known as the silky fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it its common name, and it typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by inedible, round fig-like fruits. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.

Ficus pilosula prefers well-draining soil and moderate light, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
