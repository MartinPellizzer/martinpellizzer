Fritillaria huangshanensis

Fritillaria huangshanensis, commonly known as the Huangshan fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to China, particularly the Huangshan Mountain region.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its bulbs are adapted to store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its mountainous habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated for its unique floral display.
