Freesia praecox

Freesia praecox, commonly known as the common freesia, is a bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers. The flowers come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, pink, and purple, and are often used in floral arrangements. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Freesia praecox is typically grown as an annual in cooler climates, though it can be perennial in warmer regions.
