Ficus caballina

Ficus caballina, commonly known as the red-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a reddish tinge on the underside. Its small, clustered flowers are typically greenish-white in color, and it produces edible figs that are not commonly consumed by humans.

Ficus caballina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
