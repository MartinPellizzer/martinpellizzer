Ficus agapetoides

Ficus agapetoides, commonly known as the silver fig or white-veined fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of China, India, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white veins that create a striking visual contrast. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically small and sweet.

Ficus agapetoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
