Ficus populnea var. bahamensis

Ficus populnea var. bahamensis, commonly known as the Bahamian fig, is a tropical plant native to the Bahamas and parts of the Caribbean.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub with smooth, grayish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically clustered in spherical, greenish-brown syconia. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and mangrove swamps.

The species is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental appeal in landscaping.
