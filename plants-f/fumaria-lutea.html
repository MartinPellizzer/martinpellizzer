Fumaria lutea

Fumaria lutea, commonly known as the yellow glory flower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Fumaria lutea is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
