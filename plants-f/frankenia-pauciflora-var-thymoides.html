Frankenia pauciflora var. thymoides

Frankenia pauciflora var. thymoides, commonly known as coastal bluemink, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial that thrives in sandy, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, blue to violet flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow, linear, and often covered in a fine layer of white wax.

It is well adapted to coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.
