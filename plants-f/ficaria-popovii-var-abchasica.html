Ficaria popovii var. abchasica

Ficaria popovii var. abchasica, commonly known as the Abkhazian snowbell, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features a low-growing habit with hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in alpine or subalpine meadows.
