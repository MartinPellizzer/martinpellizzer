Fraxinus excelsior var. lutea

Fraxinus excelsior var. lutea, commonly known as the golden ash, is a variant of the common ash tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its striking golden-yellow foliage, which becomes more pronounced in autumn. The leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a serrated margin. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles during early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
