Festuca californica var. parishii

Festuca californica var. parishii, commonly known as Parson's tussockgrass, is a perennial grass native to coastal areas of California.

It features tufted clumps of silver-gray foliage with a dense, fluffy appearance, and it produces slender, spike-like flower heads that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant is adapted to coastal climates, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

Festuca californica var. parishii prefers full sun to partial shade and requires well-drained soil.
