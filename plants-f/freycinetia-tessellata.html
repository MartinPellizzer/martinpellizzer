Freycinetia tessellata

Freycinetia tessellata, commonly known as the tessellated palm, is a striking tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves that can reach up to 3 meters in length. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers followed by bright red fruits that contain edible seeds. Freycinetia tessellata thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland forest areas.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular species for horticultural cultivation.
