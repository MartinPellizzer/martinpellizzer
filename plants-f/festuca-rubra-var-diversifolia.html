Festuca rubra var. diversifolia

Festuca rubra var. diversifolia, commonly known as red fescue with variegated leaves, is a low-growing grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and striking variegated foliage, which features a mix of green, white, and pinkish tones. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders. Its compact size and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for landscaping.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in late spring to early summer.
