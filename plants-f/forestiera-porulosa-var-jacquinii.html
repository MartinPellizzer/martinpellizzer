Forestiera porulosa var. jacquinii

Forestiera porulosa var. jacquinii, commonly known as the Jacquin's forestiera, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
