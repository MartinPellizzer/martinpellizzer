Filipendula camtschatica var. rosea

Filipendula camtschatica var. rosea, commonly known as the pink meadow sweet, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of pink to red inflorescences that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are grouped into spherical heads, which are surrounded by soft, fluffy bracts. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
