Fabricia rugosus var. quartiniana

Fabricia rugosus var. quartiniana, commonly known as the rough fabricia, is a variant of the fabricia genus native to tropical regions.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its coarsely toothed leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Botanically, it is distinguished by its unique leaf venation and the presence of glandular hairs on its stems and leaves.
