Forestiera pubescens subsp. neomexicana

Forestiera pubescens subsp.

neomexicana, commonly known as the New Mexico forestiera, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs.

The plant produces white, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a desirable plant for native landscapes.
