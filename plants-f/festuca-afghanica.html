Festuca afghanica

Festuca afghanica, commonly known as Afghan bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing a high degree of drought tolerance. Its leaves are usually bluish-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Festuca afghanica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging soils.
