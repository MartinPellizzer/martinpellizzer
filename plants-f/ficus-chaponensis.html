Ficus chaponensis

Ficus chaponensis, commonly known as the Chapon fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 6 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits.

Ficus chaponensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable environments.
