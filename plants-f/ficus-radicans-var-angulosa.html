Ficus radicans var. angulosa

Ficus radicans var. angulosa, commonly known as the angular fig ivy, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves with a distinctive angular leaf margin, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its vigorous growth makes it suitable for indoor cultivation or as a trailing plant in gardens.
