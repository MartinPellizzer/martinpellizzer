Fuchsia encliandra

Fuchsia encliandra, commonly known as the red fuchsia, is a striking flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. The plant has lance-shaped, dark green leaves that provide a striking contrast to its showy blooms. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Fuchsia encliandra is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
