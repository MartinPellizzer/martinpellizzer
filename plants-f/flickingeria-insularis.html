Flickingeria insularis

Flickingeria insularis, commonly known as the island bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale blue. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Flickingeria insularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the local ecosystems of its native habitats.
