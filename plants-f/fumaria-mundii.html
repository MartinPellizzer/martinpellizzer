Fumaria mundii

Fumaria mundii, commonly known as the common fumitory, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and small, yellowish-orange flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, faintly sweet scent, which is released when the flowers are disturbed. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Fumaria mundii is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides, thriving in temperate climates.
