Festuca ovina var. alascana

Festuca ovina var. alascana, commonly known as Alaskan sheep fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and dense, mat-forming growth habit. This plant thrives in cold, moist environments and is often found in alpine and subarctic regions. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in harsh climates.

Festuca ovina var. alascana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
