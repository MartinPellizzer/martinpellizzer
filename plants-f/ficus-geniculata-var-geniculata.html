Ficus geniculata var. geniculata

Ficus geniculata var. geniculata, commonly known as the climbing fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a woody vine that can grow quite long, often reaching several meters in length. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
