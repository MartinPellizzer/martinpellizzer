Ficus dalhousiae

Ficus dalhousiae, commonly known as the Borneo fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of Borneo and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with yellow or white markings. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, edible fruits. It is a popular houseplant due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Ficus dalhousiae is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and aesthetic appeal in indoor gardening.
