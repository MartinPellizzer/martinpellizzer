Fuchsia thymifolia subsp. thymifolia

Fuchsia thymifolia subsp.

thymifolia, commonly known as the thyme-leaved fuchsia, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple.

The leaves are aromatic, with a strong thyme-like scent, which is a distinctive botanical feature. It typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, thriving in well-drained soils.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to temperate climates.
