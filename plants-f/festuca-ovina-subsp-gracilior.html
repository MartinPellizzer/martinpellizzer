Festuca ovina subsp. gracilior

Festuca ovina subsp.

gracilior, commonly known as slender sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, pastures, and rocky slopes.

The plant has fine, wiry leaves and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences with small, greenish flowers. It is characterized by its dense, low-growing habit and ability to thrive in poor soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
