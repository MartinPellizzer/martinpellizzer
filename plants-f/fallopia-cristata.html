Fallopia cristata

Fallopia cristata, commonly known as the curly top silver grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, feathery, and often curly foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in panicles during the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Fallopia cristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
