Foeniculum vulgare convar. azoricum

Foeniculum vulgare convar. azoricum, commonly known as the Azorean fennel, is a herbaceous plant native to the Azores and other Mediterranean regions.

It is characterized by its feathery, finely divided leaves and an aromatic, licorice-scented flavor. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family (Apiaceae). Its seeds are used in culinary and medicinal applications, similar to common fennel.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and distinct aromatic properties.
