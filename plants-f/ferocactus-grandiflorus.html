Ferocactus grandiflorus

Ferocactus grandiflorus, commonly known as the Mexican lion's tail, is a species of cactus native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, spiny stems that can grow up to several feet in height. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are modified into spines, which help reduce water loss and provide protection from herbivores.

Ferocactus grandiflorus is well adapted to desert environments, storing water in its succulent stems.
