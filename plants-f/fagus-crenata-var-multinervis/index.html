Fagus Crenata Var. Multinervis

Fagus crenata var. multinervis is a unique variant of the American beech tree known for its distinct leaf patterns and ecological significance. This plant thrives in temperate forest environments, often found in the eastern United States and parts of Canada. Its leaves are characterized by their wavy edges and prominent veins, which set it apart from other beech species. The tree plays a vital role in supporting local wildlife and maintaining forest biodiversity. Despite its importance, it remains relatively understudied compared to more common tree species.
