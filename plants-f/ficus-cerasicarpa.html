Ficus cerasicarpa

Ficus cerasicarpa, commonly known as the round-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically round or oval in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus cerasicarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
