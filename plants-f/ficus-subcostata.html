Ficus subcostata

Ficus subcostata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate, and narrow leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, edible fig fruits.

Ficus subcostata is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
