Fagus japonica var. multinervis

Fagus japonica var. multinervis, commonly known as the Japanese beech variant with multi-nerved leaves, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and chestnuts, and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves with prominent venation. The leaves are typically elliptical with a pointed tip and a serrated margin, turning yellow in autumn.

It produces small, cup-shaped fruits called nuts, which are encased in a woody husk and serve as a food source for various wildlife species.
