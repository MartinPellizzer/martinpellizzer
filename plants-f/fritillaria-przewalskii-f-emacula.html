Fritillaria przewalskii f. emacula

Fritillaria przewalskii f. emacula, commonly known as the emaculate fritillary, is a rare and striking bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and is typically found in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. It is a member of the Liliaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
