Ficus sarmentosa var. lacrymans

Ficus sarmentosa var. lacrymans, commonly known as the weeping ficus, is a tropical plant characterized by its cascading, drooping branches that give it a distinctive appearance.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and often have a slightly waxy texture. The plant is a member of the Moraceae family and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Malaysia. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in outdoor gardens in suitable climates.
