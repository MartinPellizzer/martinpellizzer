Ficinia atrata

Ficinia atrata, commonly known as the blackleaf sedum, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy leaves that are dark green with a bluish tint, often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow to orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
