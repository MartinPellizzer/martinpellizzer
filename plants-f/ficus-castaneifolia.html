Ficus castaneifolia

Ficus castaneifolia, commonly known as the Persian fig or Indian date fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to regions of southern Asia and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a smooth, waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits, which are often used in traditional cuisines. Ficus castaneifolia thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
