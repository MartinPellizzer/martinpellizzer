Ficus capillipes

Ficus capillipes, commonly known as the silky fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, silky-haired leaves that give it a unique and ornamental appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus capillipes is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
