Festuca curvula

Festuca curvula, commonly known as curly fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous narrow, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, riverbanks, and woodland edges.

Festuca curvula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
