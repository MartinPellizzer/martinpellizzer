Ficus calotropis

Ficus calotropis, commonly known as the crown-of-thorns or sacred fig, is a tropical plant native to southern Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically heart-shaped and have a dark green color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by yellowish, fleshy fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Ficus calotropis is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
