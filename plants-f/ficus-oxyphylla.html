Ficus oxyphylla

Ficus oxyphylla, commonly known as the silver fig or white-veined fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, including countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent white veins, which give it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fruits that are typically consumed by local communities.

Ficus oxyphylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
