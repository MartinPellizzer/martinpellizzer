Fritillaria messanensis subsp. atlantica

Fritillaria messanensis subsp.

atlantica, commonly known as the Atlantic fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color, often with contrasting black markings.

The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a robust bulb that stores nutrients for growth. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or calcareous habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
