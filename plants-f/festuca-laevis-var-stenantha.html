Festuca laevis var. stenantha

Festuca laevis var. stenantha, commonly known as narrowleaf fescue, is a cool-season grass native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous slender spikelets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping and erosion control.

Festuca laevis var. stenantha is adapted to temperate climates and requires moderate water and sunlight for optimal growth.
