Festuca urzicenii

Festuca urzicenii, commonly known as the Urziceni fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges. The plant has tufted growth habit with slender, dark green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

Festuca urzicenii is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
