Ficus amplocarpa

Ficus amplocarpa, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically yellow or orange in color. Its roots are fibrous and can form a dense network, making it adaptable to various soil types.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
