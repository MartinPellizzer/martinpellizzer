Fagus crenata var. undulata

Fagus crenata var. undulata, commonly known as the Japanese maple or Japanese evergreen maple, is a variant of the Japanese whitebeam.

It is characterized by its distinctive, undulating leaf margins and is native to Japan. The plant is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a moderate height, with a broad, spreading canopy. Its leaves are usually dark green and glossy, turning red or orange in the fall.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular ornamental tree in gardens.
