Fimbristylis acicularis

Fimbristylis acicularis, commonly known as the needle sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded areas, such as marshes and wetlands. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, bristly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers.

Fimbristylis acicularis plays an important role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
