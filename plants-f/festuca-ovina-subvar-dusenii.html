Festuca ovina subvar. dusenii

Festuca ovina subvar. dusenii, commonly known as Dusen's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
