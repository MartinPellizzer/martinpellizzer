Fernandezia favosifolia

Fernandezia favosifolia, commonly known as the golden fern, is a striking tropical plant native to parts of South America.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its large, leathery fronds that exhibit a unique golden-yellow coloration. The plant produces spores rather than seeds, typical of ferns, and its reproductive structures are located on the underside of its fronds. Fernandezia favosifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and botanical collections.
