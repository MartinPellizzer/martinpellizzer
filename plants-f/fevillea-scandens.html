Fevillea scandens

Fevillea scandens, commonly known as the climbing fevillea, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and compound leaves that are typically trifoliate or pinnate. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by orange or red berries. Fevillea scandens thrives in humid, warm climates and requires support for its climbing growth habit.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering display.
