Ficus megacarpa subvar. tenuilongifolia

Ficus megacarpa subvar. tenuilongifolia, commonly known as the narrow-longleaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are usually green when mature, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.

The species is native to Southeast Asia and is valued for its resilience and aesthetic appeal in landscaping.
