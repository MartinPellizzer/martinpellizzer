Ficus acrocarpa

Ficus acrocarpa, commonly known as the rock fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in humid environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in shaded areas.

Due to its hardy nature, it is a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
