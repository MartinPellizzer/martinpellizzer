Fritillaria tenella subsp. orsiniana

Fritillaria tenella subsp.

orsiniana, commonly known as the Almondleaf fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, often with intricate patterns.

The plant produces a single, upright stem bearing several leaves that are narrow and lanceolate. Its bulbs are typically small and rounded, with a fibrous outer covering.

This subspecies is often found in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
