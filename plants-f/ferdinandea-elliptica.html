Ferdinandea elliptica

Ferdinandea elliptica, commonly known as the spiral vine, is a climbing plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its helical growth pattern, where the stems twist around supports in a spiral manner. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are elliptical and have a smooth texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Ferdinandea elliptica is often cultivated for its unique growth habit and can be used in vertical gardening or as a decorative element in gardens.
