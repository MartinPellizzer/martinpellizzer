Ficus barclayana

Ficus barclayana, commonly known as the Barclay's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves and produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when ripe. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.

Ficus barclayana is also valued for its ability to purify the air and enhance indoor environments.
