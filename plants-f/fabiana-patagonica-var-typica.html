Fabiana patagonica var. typica

Fabiana patagonica var. typica, commonly known as the Patagonian fuchsia, is a shrub native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
