Fimbristylis gracilenta var. gracilenta

Fimbristylis gracilenta var. gracilenta, commonly known as the slender bristle grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to aquatic environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a fibrous texture and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in dense clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense stands that can stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

The species is valued for its ecological role in wetland ecosystems and its ability to tolerate fluctuating water levels.
