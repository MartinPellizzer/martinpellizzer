Filipendula ulmaria f. cinerea

Filipendula ulmaria f. cinerea, commonly known as pale meadow sweet, is a botanical variant of the common meadow sweet.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia, characterized by its pale pink to white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in herbal medicine.
