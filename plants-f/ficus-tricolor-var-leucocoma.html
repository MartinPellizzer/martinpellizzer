Ficus tricolor var. leucocoma

Ficus tricolor var. leucocoma, commonly known as the white-veined fig, is a variant of the Ficus tricolor species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, featuring dark green leaves with prominent white or pale green veins. The plant is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are typically glossy and have a distinct three-lobed shape.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
