Faroa boehmii

Faroa boehmii, commonly known as the Tahitian fan palm, is a tropical plant native to the Society Islands in French Polynesia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, feather-like leaves that form a fan shape at the top. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow or green, followed by fruit that turns red when mature. Faroa boehmii thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires plenty of sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and cultural significance in Polynesian traditions.
