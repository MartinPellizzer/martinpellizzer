Forsteronia gardneri

Forsteronia gardneri, commonly known as the red-veined orchid, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, showy flowers with deep red veins contrasting against a white or pale pink background. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Forsteronia gardneri is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and greenhouses.
