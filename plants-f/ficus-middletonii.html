Ficus middletonii

Ficus middletonii, commonly known as the Middletons fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

The species is also valued for its ability to adapt to various growing conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
