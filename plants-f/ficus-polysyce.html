Ficus polysyce

Ficus polysyce, commonly known as the common fig tree, is a tropical plant belonging to the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that develop into the familiar fig fruit. The plant is native to regions of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia, and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The fig fruit is not only a food source for various animals but also has cultural and economic significance in many parts of the world.
