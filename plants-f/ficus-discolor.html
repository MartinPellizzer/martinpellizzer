Ficus discolor

Ficus discolor, commonly known as the variegated fig tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white or cream-colored veins. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.

Ficus discolor is noted for its ability to adapt to various light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
