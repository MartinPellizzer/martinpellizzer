Ficus didymophylla

Ficus didymophylla, commonly known as the two-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique leaves, which have two distinct lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is a member of the Moraceae family and is often cultivated as an ornamental species. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ability to adapt to indoor conditions makes it a popular choice for houseplants.
