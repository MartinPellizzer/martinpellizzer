Ficus populnea subvar. thomaea

Ficus populnea subvar. thomaea, commonly known as the Thomae's fig, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are edible and often consumed by both humans and animals. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
