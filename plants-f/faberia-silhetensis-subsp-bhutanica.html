Faberia silhetensis subsp. bhutanica

Faberia silhetensis subsp.

bhutanica, commonly known as the Bhutanese faberia, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Himalayas. It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountain slopes. It has a shallow root system and is adapted to the cool, humid climate of its native habitat.

Its flowers are often visited by pollinators like bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
