Fritillaria pyrenaica subsp. boissieri

Fritillaria pyrenaica subsp.

boissieri, commonly known as the Pyrenean fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Iberian Peninsula and southern France. It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color, with a distinctive spotted pattern.

The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winter conditions, and it blooms in early spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
