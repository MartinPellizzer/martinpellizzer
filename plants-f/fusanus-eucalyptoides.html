Fusanus eucalyptoides

Fusanus eucalyptoides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed eucalypt, is a species of flowering plant in the myrtle family, Myrtaceae.

It is native to the eastern and southern regions of Australia, where it typically grows in dry sclerophyll forests and heathlands. The plant is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a whitish bloom. It produces small, globular clusters of white or pale yellow flowers, which are followed by woody capsules containing numerous seeds.

This species is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a source of timber.
