Fumana vulgaris subsp. spachii

Fumana vulgaris subsp.

spachii, commonly known as the common fumitory, is a low-growing annual plant characterized by its slender stems and small, spurred flowers. It typically thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in waste areas.

The plant produces delicate, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and flies. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. Fumaria vulgaris subsp.

spachii is known for its medicinal properties, with historical use in treating ailments such as coughs and skin conditions.
