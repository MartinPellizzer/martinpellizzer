Fraxinus excelsior f. paniculata

Fraxinus excelsior f. paniculata, commonly known as the panicle sweet cherry, is a hybrid form of the European ash.

It is characterized by its distinctive panicles of white flowers that appear in early spring. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree with smooth, grey bark and opposite, ovate leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its showy floral display.
