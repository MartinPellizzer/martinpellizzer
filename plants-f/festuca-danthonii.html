Festuca danthonii

Festuca danthonii, commonly known as bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green blades and a dense, tufted growth habit. This plant is often found in moist, well-drained soils in open woodlands and prairies. Festuca danthonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and turfgrass mixes.

It also plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
