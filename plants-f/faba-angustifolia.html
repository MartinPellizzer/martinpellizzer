Faba angustifolia

Faba angustifolia, commonly known as narrow-leaf vetch, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods containing small, round seeds that are often used in traditional cuisines. Native to the Mediterranean region, it thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soils.

Faba angustifolia is valued for its nutritional content and has been cultivated for both food and forage purposes.
