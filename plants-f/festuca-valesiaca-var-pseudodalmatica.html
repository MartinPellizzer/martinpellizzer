Festuca valesiaca var. pseudodalmatica

Festuca valesiaca var. pseudodalmatica, commonly known as the Dalmatian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
