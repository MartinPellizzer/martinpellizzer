Ficus oleifolia

Ficus oleifolia, commonly known as the olive fig, is a small evergreen tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits that resemble olives. It is well-suited for container gardening and can thrive in a variety of soil types as long as it receives adequate drainage.

Ficus oleifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to purify indoor air.
