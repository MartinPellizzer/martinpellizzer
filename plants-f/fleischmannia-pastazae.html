Fleischmannia pastazae

Fleischmannia pastazae, commonly known as the Pastaza fleischmannia, is a flowering plant native to the Andean region of South America.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant is typically found in rocky or arid environments, where it adapts to harsh conditions through its succulent leaves and deep root system. Its flowers bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
