Ficus populnea f. citrifolia

Ficus populnea f. citrifolia, commonly known as the citrus fig or the lemon fig, is a variant of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its small, round, and often lemon-yellow fruits that are edible and have a tangy flavor. The plant is native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, where it thrives in warm, temperate climates. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, while the plant can grow as a shrub or small tree.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
