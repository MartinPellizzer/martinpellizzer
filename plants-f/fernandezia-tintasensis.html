Fernandezia tintasensis

Fernandezia tintasensis, commonly known as the Tintasense fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that display a striking contrast of colors, often with a pale green base and darker green veins. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in the cool, humid conditions. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the undersides of its leaves, a common feature among ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Fernandezia tintasensis is considered a vulnerable species, making it of interest to botanists and conservationists alike.
