Ficus fuscescens

Ficus fuscescens, commonly known as the black ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are typically heart-shaped and have a waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fig-like fruits that are edible for certain animals but not typically consumed by humans.

Ficus fuscescens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor environments.
