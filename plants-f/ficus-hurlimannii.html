Ficus hurlimannii

Ficus hurlimannii, commonly known as the Hurlimann's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the rainforests of western Africa, particularly in countries like Cameroon and Nigeria. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant in warm climates.
