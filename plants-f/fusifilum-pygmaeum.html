Fusifilum pygmaeum

Fusifilum pygmaeum, commonly known as the dwarf needleplant, is a unique and rare species of flowering plant.

It is characterized by its slender, needle-like leaves and small, clustered flowers. Native to specific regions of South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant has a compact growth habit, making it well-suited for rock gardens or container cultivation.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for horticultural study and conservation efforts.
