Ficus verticillaris var. verticillaris

Ficus verticillaris var. verticillaris, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping, cascading branches that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a smooth texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish fruits.

The weeping fig is widely cultivated as a houseplant and is known for its ability to thrive in indoor environments with proper care.
