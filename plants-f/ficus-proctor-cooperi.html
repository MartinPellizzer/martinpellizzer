Ficus proctor-cooperi

Ficus proctor-cooperi, commonly known as the Proctor-Cooper fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the island of Madagascar and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often grown as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ornamental appeal.

Ficus proctor-cooperi is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
