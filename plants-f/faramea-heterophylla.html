Faramea heterophylla

Faramea heterophylla, commonly known as the variable faramea, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its variable leaf morphology, with leaves ranging from elliptic to ovate in shape. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in humid lowland forests. Its flowers are small and greenish-white, clustered in terminal inflorescences.

Faramea heterophylla is valued for its ornamental potential and is sometimes cultivated in subtropical gardens.
