Ficus moritziana

Ficus moritziana, commonly known as the Moritz's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped and have a waxy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fig fruits.

Ficus moritziana is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
