Ferula sumbul

Ferula sumbul, commonly known as the Kashmiri ferula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and clusters of yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic resin that has been traditionally used in herbal medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are thick and fleshy.

Ferula sumbul is often found in rocky, arid environments and is valued for its ecological and medicinal significance.
