Feuilleea pedicellaris

Feuilleea pedicellaris, commonly known as the featherleaf pedicellar, is a unique flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like leaves that are arranged in a spiral pattern along its slender stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of white and pale pink, which bloom during the wet season. Feuilleea pedicellaris thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest undergrowth.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for both horticulturists and botanists.
