Festuca pseudovina f. salina

Festuca pseudovina f. salina, commonly known as the salt meadow grass, is a variant of the meadow fescue species.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in saline environments, making it a hardy plant found in coastal and arid regions. The plant typically has narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand high salt concentrations. Its growth habit is often clump-forming, with dense foliage that helps it compete in challenging soils.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
