Festuca pseudosulcata

Festuca pseudosulcata, commonly known as the narrowleaf fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often used in landscaping and erosion control due to its hardiness and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Festuca pseudosulcata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, low-maintenance turf.
