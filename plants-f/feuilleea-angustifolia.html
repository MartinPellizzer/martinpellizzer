Feuilleea angustifolia

Feuilleea angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed fern, is a species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that grow in a clustered arrangement. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a common sight in humid forests. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, relying on wind pollination.

Feuilleea angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
