Fagraea curtisii var. curtisii

Fagraea curtisii var. curtisii, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The tree has a rapid growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters, making it a prominent feature in tropical landscapes. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptical in shape, with a glossy green surface.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited to warm, humid climates.
