Ficus clavata

Ficus clavata, commonly known as the clustered fig, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, rounded clusters of small, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible figs. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage provides a lush, evergreen appearance, making it popular in tropical gardens.
