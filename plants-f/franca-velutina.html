Franca velutina

Franca velutina, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy foliage that gives it a velvety texture, and its ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Franca velutina is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
