Festuca paucispicula

Festuca paucispicula, commonly known as the slender fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and compact growth habit, making it well-suited for moist, shaded environments. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. Its leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

Festuca paucispicula is often found in woodland areas, stream banks, and other damp habitats where it forms dense, low-growing mats.
