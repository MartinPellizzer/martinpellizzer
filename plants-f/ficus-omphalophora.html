Ficus omphalophora

Ficus omphalophora, commonly known as the sacred fig or the tree of life, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, grayish bark. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are often consumed raw or cooked. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various regions.

Ficus omphalophora thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
