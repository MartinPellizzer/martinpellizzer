Fimbristylis benghalensis

Fimbristylis benghalensis, commonly known as the Bengal sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and small, triangular leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, spike-like flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It thrives in wet or marshy environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Fimbristylis benghalensis is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils and is often used in wetland restoration projects.
