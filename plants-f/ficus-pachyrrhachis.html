Ficus pachyrrhachis

Ficus pachyrrhachis, commonly known as the thick-stemmed fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy in appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Ficus pachyrrhachis is also valued for its air-purifying properties and ability to adapt to indoor environments.
