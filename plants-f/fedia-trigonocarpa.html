Fedia trigonocarpa

Fedia trigonocarpa, commonly known as the three-angled fig, is a tropical plant belonging to the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its unique trifoliate leaves, which are composed of three leaflets arranged in a triangular pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fig-like fruits. Fedia trigonocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions for its fruit and leaves.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and visually appealing species within the fig family.
