Frasera montana

Frasera montana, commonly known as the mountain frasera, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Frasera montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
