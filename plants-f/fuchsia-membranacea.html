Fuchsia membranacea

Fuchsia membranacea, commonly known as the velvet tangle, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to violet. The plant has soft, velvety leaves and produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Fuchsia membranacea is also valued for its unique foliage and is sometimes used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.
