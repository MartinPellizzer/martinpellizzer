Fagus sieboldii var. procera

Fagus sieboldii var. procera, commonly known as the Japanese beech, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Beech family (Fagaceae) and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks, which are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and landscape design.
