Festuca lachenalii var. aristata

Festuca lachenalii var. aristata, commonly known as the aristate bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green, narrow leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers that are typically blue to purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Festuca lachenalii var. aristata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
