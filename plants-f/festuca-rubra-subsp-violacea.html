Festuca rubra subsp. violacea

Festuca rubra subsp.

violacea, commonly known as purple false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and distinctive violet to purple coloration on its leaf blades and inflorescences.

The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing slender spikelets that are often tinged with purple.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
