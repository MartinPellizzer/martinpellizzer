Ferula pallasii

Ferula pallasii, commonly known as the Pallas' giant fennel, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stem and large, feathery leaves that resemble those of a fennel plant. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in a compound umbel, which bloom in the summer months. Its aromatic seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.

Ferula pallasii is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.
