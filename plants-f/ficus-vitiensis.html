Ficus vitiensis

Ficus vitiensis, commonly known as the Vitiensis fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of the Pacific, including parts of Fiji and Tonga. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

The Vitiensis fig is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in the regions where it is native.
