Fraxinus japonica var. stenocarpa

Fraxinus japonica var. stenocarpa, commonly known as the Japanese ash, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a serrated margin, turning yellow in the fall. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles, followed by winged fruits that disperse seeds by wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its elegant form.
