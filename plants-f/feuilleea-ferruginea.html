Feuilleea ferruginea

Feuilleea ferruginea, commonly known as the rust-leaf fern, is a unique plant characterized by its intricate leaf structure and distinctive coloration.

Its fronds are typically broad and feathery, with a leathery texture that helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The leaves exhibit a reddish-brown hue, especially on the underside, which gives it its common name. This plant thrives in shaded, humid conditions and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Feuilleea ferruginea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to purify the air in indoor spaces.
