Ficus bequaertii

Ficus bequaertii, commonly known as the Bequaert's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Ficus bequaertii is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
