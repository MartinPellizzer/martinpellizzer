Ficus pacifica

Ficus pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific fig, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible figs. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in indoor environments. It thrives in warm, humid conditions with indirect sunlight.

Its roots can become quite extensive, making it suitable for larger pots or outdoor gardens in suitable climates.
