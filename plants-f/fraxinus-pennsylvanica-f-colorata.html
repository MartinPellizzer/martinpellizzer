Fraxinus pennsylvanica f. colorata

Fraxinus pennsylvanica f. colorata, commonly known as the colorado ash or red ash, is a cultivated form of the green ash tree.

It is characterized by its striking, variegated foliage that features a mix of green, white, and red hues. The leaves are typically narrower and longer than those of the typical green ash, adding visual interest to the landscape. This cultivar is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.

It retains its colorful leaves throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for parks and gardens.
