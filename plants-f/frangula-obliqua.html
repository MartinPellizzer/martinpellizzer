Frangula obliqua

Frangula obliqua, commonly known as the narrowleaf cascara, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring. The plant is part of the Rutaceae family, which includes other citrus and related plants. Its fruits are aggregate berries that mature to a reddish color, often used in traditional medicine.

Frangula obliqua is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife and its ecological resilience in arid environments.
