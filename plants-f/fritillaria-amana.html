Fritillaria amana

Fritillaria amana, commonly known as the Persian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a distinctive, elongated bulb that stores nutrients for the next growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Fritillaria amana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
