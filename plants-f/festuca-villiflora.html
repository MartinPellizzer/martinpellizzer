Festuca villiflora

Festuca villiflora, commonly known as slender fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine hairs, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually pale green to brownish in color, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Festuca villiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
