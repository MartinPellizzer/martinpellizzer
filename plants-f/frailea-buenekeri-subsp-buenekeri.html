Frailea buenekeri subsp. buenekeri

Frailea buenekeri subsp.

buenekeri, commonly known as the Canary Island cactus, is a species native to the Canary Islands. It is a small, globular cactus with a dense covering of white, woolly hair.

The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in late spring to early summer.

It is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes due to its compact size and ornamental appearance.
