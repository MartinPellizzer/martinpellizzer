Ferula korshinskyi

Ferula korshinskyi, commonly known as the Korshinsky's ferula, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and yellow flowers. The plant produces a strong-smelling, resinous sap that has been used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are narrow and deeply lobed, while its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted to survive in arid conditions.

Ferula korshinskyi is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
