Fusticus vera

Fusticus vera, commonly known as the golden cup fern, is a delicate and elegant plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its intricate, lace-like fronds that resemble delicate cups. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest undergrowth or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The flowers, though small, are usually greenish-yellow and appear in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
