Fagraea grandifolia

Fagraea grandifolia, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, red fruits that contain seeds enclosed in a fleshy, orange aril. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Fagraea grandifolia is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
