Festuca ovina subvar. deylii

Festuca ovina subvar. deylii, commonly known as the Deylii sheepgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or arid soils. The plant typically produces slender flowering stems with spikelets that are often clustered at the tips. It is well adapted to harsh environments, tolerating cold temperatures and poor soil conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a forage plant.
