Ficus carolii

Ficus carolii, commonly known as the Carolinian fig tree, is a species native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, grayish bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature, containing a single seed.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability.
