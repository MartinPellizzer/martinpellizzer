Fagraea obovata var. papuana

Fagraea obovata var. papuana, commonly known as the Papuan glorybower, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Indonesia and Papua.

It is characterized by its large, showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in shades of white and yellow. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its vibrant flowers. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
