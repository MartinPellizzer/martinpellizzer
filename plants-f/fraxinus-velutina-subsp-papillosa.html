Fraxinus velutina subsp. papillosa

Fraxinus velutina subsp.

papillosa, commonly known as the velvet ash, is a member of the Oleaceae family and is native to the southwestern United States. It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves with a serrated margin.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, which are followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and woodlands.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is a notable species in its native habitat.
