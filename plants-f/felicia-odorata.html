Felicia odorata

Felicia odorata, commonly known as the sweet everlasting, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, fragrant yellow flowers that bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow and gray-green, often covered in fine hairs.

Felicia odorata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
