Frangula peruviana

Frangula peruviana, commonly known as Peruvian buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and dark, glossy berries. The plant contains anthraquinones, which have been used in traditional medicine for their laxative properties. Its leaves are often used in herbal remedies, while the bark and roots are also valued for their medicinal compounds.

Frangula peruviana thrives in temperate to subtropical climates and is often found in forested areas.
