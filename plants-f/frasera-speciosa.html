Frasera speciosa

Frasera speciosa, commonly known as the showy frasera, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is a member of the gentian family (Gentianaceae) and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and often have a hairy texture.

Frasera speciosa is valued for its ornamental beauty and plays a role in supporting local pollinators.
