Frankenia velutina

Frankenia velutina, commonly known as the velvet frankenia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and hairy, gray-green foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in dune environments.

Frankenia velutina is adapted to coastal conditions, tolerating salt spray and strong winds.
