Ficus hirta var. malayana

Ficus hirta var. malayana, commonly known as the Malay ficus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub with simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. The leaves are often hairy, especially on the underside, giving the plant its scientific name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
