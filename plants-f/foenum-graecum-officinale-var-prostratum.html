Foenum-graecum officinale var. prostratum

Foenum-graecum officinale var. prostratum, commonly known as prostrate fennel, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, creeping stems that spread along the ground, often forming a dense mat. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family, and has finely divided, feathery leaves. Its aromatic seeds are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.
