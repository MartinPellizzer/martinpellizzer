Festuca teneriffae

Festuca teneriffae, commonly known as the Teneriffe fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to the arid and coastal conditions of its native habitat. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Festuca teneriffae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native environment.
