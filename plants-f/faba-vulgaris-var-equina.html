Faba vulgaris var. equina

Faba vulgaris var. equina, commonly known as the horse bean, is a variety of the broad bean plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its elongated, brownish pods containing several elliptical seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with trifoliate leaves and erect stems. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible seeds.

The species is closely related to other members of the Fabaceae family, known for their nitrogen-fixing abilities.
