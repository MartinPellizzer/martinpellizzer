Ficus gorontaloensis

Ficus gorontaloensis, commonly known as the Gorontalo fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Indonesian island of Sulawesi and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when ripe. Ficus gorontaloensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.

It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to ensure healthy growth.
