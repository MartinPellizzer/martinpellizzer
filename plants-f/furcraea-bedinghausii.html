Furcraea bedinghausii

Furcraea bedinghausii, commonly known as the Bedinghaus's furcraea, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its tall, spire-like growth habit. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves with a waxy coating, which help it conserve water in arid conditions. It produces striking white or pale yellow inflorescences that emerge from the top of the plant.

Due to its architectural appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in dry gardens and rockeries.
