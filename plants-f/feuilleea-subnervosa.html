Feuilleea subnervosa

Feuilleea subnervosa, commonly known as the silver-veined fern, is a unique species characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds.

Its leaves are typically light green with prominent, silvery veins that give it a striking appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Feuilleea subnervosa has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread slowly over time.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities.
