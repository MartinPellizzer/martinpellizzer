Ficus uncinata var. gracilis

Ficus uncinata var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender fig, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, round fig fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant.

Its compact size and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for bonsai and container gardening.
