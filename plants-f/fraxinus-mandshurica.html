Fraxinus mandshurica

Fraxinus mandshurica, commonly known as Manchurian ash, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its slender, often multi-trunked form. The tree has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers appear in catkins, and it produces winged samaras as its fruit.

The bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age, and the wood is valued for its fine grain and workability.
