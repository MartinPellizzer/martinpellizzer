Ficus cumingii

Ficus cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ficus cumingii is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
