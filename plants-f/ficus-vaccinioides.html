Ficus vaccinioides

Ficus vaccinioides, commonly known as the tropical fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the humid forests of Southeast Asia, including regions of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that are followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus vaccinioides is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and local ecosystems.
