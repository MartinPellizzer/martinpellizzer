Festuca rubra var. hackelii

Festuca rubra var. hackelii, commonly known as red fescue, is a cool-season grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green blades and tufted growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant produces spikelets that are typically reddish-brown, adding ornamental value to lawns and gardens. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for low-maintenance landscaping.

Festuca rubra var. hackelii is also valued for its ability to withstand foot traffic and its role in ecological restoration projects.
