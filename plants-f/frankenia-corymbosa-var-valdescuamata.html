Frankenia corymbosa var. valdescuamata

Frankenia corymbosa var. valdescuamata, commonly known as the coastal frankenia, is a low-growing shrub native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in a whitish, waxy coating, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, adding a splash of color to sandy coastal landscapes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments.

This variety is notable for its adaptability to harsh coastal conditions and its role in stabilizing sandy soils.
