Ficus piresiana

Ficus piresiana, commonly known as the Pires' fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are often used in local cuisines.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
