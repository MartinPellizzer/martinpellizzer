Furcraea cahum

Furcraea cahum, commonly known as the Guatemalan sword fern, is a perennial plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sword-shaped leaves that can grow up to 2 meters in length. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant and suitable for xeriscaping. Its leaves are fibrous and often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

Furcraea cahum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
