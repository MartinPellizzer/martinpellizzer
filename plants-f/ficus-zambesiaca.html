Ficus zambesiaca

Ficus zambesiaca, commonly known as the Zambesi fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly found in countries like Zimbabwe, South Africa, and Mozambique. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing as a tree or shrub.

The Zambesi fig is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit and shade.
