Franca ericifolia

Franca ericifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf azalea, is a species of flowering shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery foliage that is typically covered in a silvery or whitish bloom, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of showy, pink to red flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, dark blue berries. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Franca ericifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
