Ficus pachyclada subsp. pachyclada

Ficus pachyclada subsp.

pachyclada, commonly known as the African rubber tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa. It features thick, waxy, dark green leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish fruits that turn yellow when mature. It is well adapted to warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

This species is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
