Festuca rubra f. glabrispicula

Festuca rubra f. glabrispicula, commonly known as red fescue, is a low-growing grass species characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a vibrant green color, with a fine texture that makes it visually appealing. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, two-lobed flowers, which are usually pink to reddish in color. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.

This variety is particularly valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
