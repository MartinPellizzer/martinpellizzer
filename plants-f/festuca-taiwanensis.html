Festuca taiwanensis

Festuca taiwanensis, commonly known as Taiwan fescue, is a species of grass native to Taiwan.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountainous regions and forest understories. The plant has fine, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny seeds. It is characterized by its dense growth habit and ability to thrive in cool, humid climates.

Festuca taiwanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
