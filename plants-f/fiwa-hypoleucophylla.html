Fiwa hypoleucophylla

Fiwa hypoleucophylla, commonly known as the white-veined fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that display a distinctive white or pale vein pattern against a green background. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the substrate while absorbing nutrients.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
