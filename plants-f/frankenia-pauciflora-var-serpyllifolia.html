Frankenia pauciflora var. serpyllifolia

Frankenia pauciflora var. serpyllifolia, commonly known as coastal frangipani, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its fleshy, succulent leaves and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to withstand salt spray and coastal winds. Its flowers are typically borne in terminal clusters and bloom during the summer months.

Frankenia pauciflora var. serpyllifolia is often used in coastal landscaping due to its hardiness and ornamental value.
