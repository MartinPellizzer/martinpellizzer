Festuca myuros subsp. sciuroides

Festuca myuros subsp.

sciuroides, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Its inflorescence consists of narrow, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers. Festuca myuros subsp.

sciuroides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
