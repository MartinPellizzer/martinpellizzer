Frenela rhomboidea var. tasmanica

Frenela rhomboidea var. tasmanica, commonly known as Tasmanian flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Tasmania.

It typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, thriving in moist, poorly drained soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. It is characterized by its rhomboid-shaped leaves, which give it its scientific name.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland habitats and supporting local biodiversity.
