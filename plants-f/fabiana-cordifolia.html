Fabiana cordifolia

Fabiana cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf fabiana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its attractive, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Fabiana cordifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its elegant foliage and delicate blooms.
