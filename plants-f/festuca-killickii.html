Festuca killickii

Festuca killickii, commonly known as the Killick's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant typically forms dense mats and is well adapted to harsh, alpine environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Festuca killickii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
