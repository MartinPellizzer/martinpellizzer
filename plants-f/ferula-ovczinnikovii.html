Ferula ovczinnikovii

Ferula ovczinnikovii, commonly known as the Karakorum ferula, is a member of the Apiaceae family and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, compound leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in a complex umbel, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing at high altitudes.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
