Flueggea intermedia

Flueggea intermedia, commonly known as the yellow flueggea, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows up to 3 meters in height, featuring thick, woody stems and broad, ovate leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that are clustered in panicles, which bloom during the summer months. It is often found in coastal areas and can thrive in sandy or saline soils.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
