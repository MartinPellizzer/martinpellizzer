Ficus trichocerasa subsp. trichocerasa

Ficus trichocerasa subsp.

trichocerasa, commonly known as the African fig, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa. It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, broad leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are edible and serve as a food source for various animals.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
