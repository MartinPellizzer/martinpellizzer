Ficus abelii

Ficus abelii, commonly known as the Abel's fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged oppositely on the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus abelii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant in temperate regions.
