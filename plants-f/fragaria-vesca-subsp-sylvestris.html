Fragaria vesca subsp. sylvestris

Fragaria vesca subsp.

sylvestris, commonly known as the wild strawberry, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, edible berries that are typically red when ripe.

The plant is native to Europe, western Asia, and northern Africa, and it thrives in temperate climates. It produces white flowers with five petals and has a fibrous root system.

This subspecies is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky areas, where it spreads through runners and seeds.
