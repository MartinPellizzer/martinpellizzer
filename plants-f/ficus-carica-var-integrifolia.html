Ficus carica var. integrifolia

Ficus carica var. integrifolia, commonly known as the common fig, is a species of flowering plant in the mulberry family, Moraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and edible fruit. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in the fall, and produces small, green figs that ripen to a sweet, amber color. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, temperate climate for optimal growth.

The tree is often cultivated for its fruit, which is used in cooking and baking.
