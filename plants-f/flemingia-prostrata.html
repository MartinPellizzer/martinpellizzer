Flemingia prostrata

Flemingia prostrata, commonly known as the prostrate flemingia, is a low-growing shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically arranged alternately along its stems. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fruiting bodies that contain minute seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Botanically, it belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and has a fibrous root system that aids in soil stabilization.
