Festuca refracta

Festuca refracta, commonly known as the rigid fescue, is a perennial grass species native to northern Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, spikelet-bearing flowers. Festuca refracta is adapted to grow in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along streambanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
