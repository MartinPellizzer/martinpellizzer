Ficus vasta

Ficus vasta, commonly known as the giant ficus or tree of heaven, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by yellowish fruits containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Ficus vasta has a robust root system and can grow quite large, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor spaces.
