Festuca mollissima

Festuca mollissima, commonly known as velvet fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, soft foliage that gives it a velvety texture, hence its common name. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Festuca mollissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and as a ground cover.

It is also known for its ability to tolerate shade and moderate foot traffic, making it a versatile choice for various garden settings.
