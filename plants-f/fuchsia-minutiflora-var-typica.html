Fuchsia minutiflora var. typica

Fuchsia minutiflora var. typica, commonly known as the miniature fuchsia, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a small, deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant flowers in shades of pink and purple during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are elliptical and have a slightly hairy texture, while its stems are woody and often covered in a fine, silvery bloom.

This plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
