Ficus eugeniifolia

Ficus eugeniifolia, commonly known as the silver ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a slightly waxy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible figs, which are a food source for various animals.

Ficus eugeniifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
