Felicia aculeata

Felicia aculeata, commonly known as the blue daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright blue to purple flowers and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Felicia aculeata is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
