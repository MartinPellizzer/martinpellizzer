Ficus mariannensis

Ficus mariannensis, commonly known as the Mariana fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the western Pacific, including parts of the Philippines and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and can grow as either a tree or a shrub. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible figs.

The Mariana fig is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
