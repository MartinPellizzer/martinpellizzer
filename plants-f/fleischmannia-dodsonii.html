Fleischmannia dodsonii

Fleischmannia dodsonii, commonly known as the desert milkweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its hairy, cylindrical stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in sandy or rocky soils.

Fleischmannia dodsonii plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
