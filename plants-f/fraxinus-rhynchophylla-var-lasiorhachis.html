Fraxinus rhynchophylla var. lasiorhachis

Fraxinus rhynchophylla var. lasiorhachis, commonly known as the Chinese ash or broad-leafed ash, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters with a broad, rounded canopy. The leaves are compound, with 5–9 pairs of oval-shaped leaflets that are serrated along the edges. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
