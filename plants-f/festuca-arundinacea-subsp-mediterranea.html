Festuca arundinacea subsp. mediterranea

Festuca arundinacea subsp.

mediterranea, commonly known as Mediterranean tall fescue, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and coarse, bluish-green foliage.

This subspecies is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils. It is often used in landscaping and as a forage crop due to its hardiness and tolerance to drought.

The plant produces inflorescences of spikelets that are typically found in dense clusters.
