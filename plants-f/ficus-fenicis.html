Ficus fenicis

Ficus fenicis, commonly known as the sacred fig or Bodhi tree, is a species of fig tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and can grow into a tall, broad-canopied tree. The plant produces small, clustered fruits known as figs, which are encased in a green, fleshy husk. Ficus fenicis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in many Asian countries.

Its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types makes it a versatile and resilient plant.
