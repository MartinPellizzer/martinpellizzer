Ficus periptera var. hirsutula

Ficus periptera var. hirsutula, commonly known as the hirsute fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage and elongated, hanging leaves. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
