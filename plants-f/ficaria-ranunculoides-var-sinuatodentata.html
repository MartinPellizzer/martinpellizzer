Ficaria ranunculoides var. sinuatodentata

Ficaria ranunculoides var. sinuatodentata, commonly known as the sinuate-leaved buttercup, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a perennial herb with a low-growing habit, featuring hairy stems and heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive wavy edge. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers with five petals, typical of the Ranunculaceae family. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant in botanical gardens and specialized horticulture.
