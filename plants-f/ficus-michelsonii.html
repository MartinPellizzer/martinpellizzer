Ficus michelsonii

Ficus michelsonii, commonly known as the Michelson's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are often dark green in color. It produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature.

Ficus michelsonii is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and adaptability to indoor conditions.
