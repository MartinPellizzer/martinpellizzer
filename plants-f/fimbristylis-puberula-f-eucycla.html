Fimbristylis puberula f. eucycla

Fimbristylis puberula f. eucycla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flat sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. Its flowering heads consist of small, greenish to brownish spikelets, which are often subtended by bracts.

The species is notable for its adaptability to varying water conditions and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.
