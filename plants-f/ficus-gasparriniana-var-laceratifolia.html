Ficus gasparriniana var. laceratifolia

Ficus gasparriniana var. laceratifolia, commonly known as the lace-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, palmate leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental foliage in horticulture.

It prefers warm, humid climates and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
