Ficus sidifolia

Ficus sidifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus sidifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant.
