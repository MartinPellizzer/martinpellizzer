Flourensia besseriana

Flourensia besseriana, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a member of the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it thrives in warm, sunny climates. The plant features vibrant orange to yellow composite flowers that bloom from summer to fall. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Flourensia besseriana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to attract pollinators.
