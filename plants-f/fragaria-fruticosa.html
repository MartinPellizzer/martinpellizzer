Fragaria fruticosa

Fragaria fruticosa, commonly known as the wood strawberry or wild strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color, similar to cultivated strawberries. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets and a hairy stem that often grows close to the ground. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

The flowers are white with five petals and are followed by the characteristic fruiting bodies.
