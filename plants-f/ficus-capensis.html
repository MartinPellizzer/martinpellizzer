Ficus capensis

Ficus capensis, commonly known as the Natal fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, and is often found in coastal areas and rocky outcrops. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glossy, with a dark green color. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits.

Ficus capensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
