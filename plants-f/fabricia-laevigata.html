Fabricia laevigata

Fabricia laevigata, commonly known as the smooth fabricia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain lens-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
