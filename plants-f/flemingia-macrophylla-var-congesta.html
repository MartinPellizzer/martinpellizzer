Flemingia macrophylla var. congesta

Flemingia macrophylla var. congesta, commonly known as the dense-flowered flemingia, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its botanical features include pinnate leaves with elongated leaflets and an upright growth habit.

The flowers are typically arranged in dense inflorescences, contributing to its ornamental value in horticulture.
