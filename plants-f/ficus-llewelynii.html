Ficus llewelynii

Ficus llewelynii, commonly known as the Llewelyn's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits.

Ficus llewelynii is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
