Fuchsia cuatrecasasii

Fuchsia cuatrecasasii, commonly known as the Cuatrecasas fuchsia, is a species native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to purple. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by local wildlife. Its foliage consists of ovate to elliptical leaves with a glossy texture.

Fuchsia cuatrecasasii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
