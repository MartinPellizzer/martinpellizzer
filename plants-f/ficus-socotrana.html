Ficus socotrana

Ficus socotrana, commonly known as Socotra fig or Socotran fig tree, is a unique species native to the Socotra archipelago in the Indian Ocean.

It is characterized by its distinctive, thick, waxy leaves that are often lobed and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically clustered in spherical, greenish-brown syconia. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations to the harsh climate of its native habitat.

The species is also valued for its ecological role and has cultural significance in the region.
