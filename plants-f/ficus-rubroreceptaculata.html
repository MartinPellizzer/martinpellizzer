Ficus rubroreceptaculata

Ficus rubroreceptaculata, commonly known as the red-ventured fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive reddish veins on the leaves. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are generally ovate to elliptical in shape, with a glossy upper surface and a lighter underside.

Ficus rubroreceptaculata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor conditions.
