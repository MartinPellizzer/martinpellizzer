Ficus leucantatoma

Ficus leucantatoma, commonly known as the white-veined fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent white veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, inedible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Ficus leucantatoma is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
