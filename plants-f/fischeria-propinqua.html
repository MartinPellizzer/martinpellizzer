Fischeria propinqua

Fischeria propinqua, commonly known as the small fisher's flower, is a rare and delicate plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and small, pale green leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy coating. Fischeria propinqua thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing in rocky crevices or shaded forest floors.
