Ficus fistulosa var. cincta

Ficus fistulosa var. cincta, commonly known as the variegated fig tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white or cream-colored veins and margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
