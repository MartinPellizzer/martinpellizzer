Ficus radicans

Ficus radicans, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a tropical evergreen vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by yellowish-white fruits. It is well adapted to humid environments and thrives in partial to full shade.

Its rapid growth and ability to climb or trail make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor landscaping.
