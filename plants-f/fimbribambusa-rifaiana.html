Fimbribambusa rifaiana

Fimbribambusa rifaiana, commonly known as the Rifaiana bamboo, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tall, slender grass-like plant that belongs to the Bambusoideae subfamily. The plant features long, hollow stems with a distinctive fibrous sheath, which is characteristic of many bamboo species. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, contributing to its overall elegant appearance.

This bamboo is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
