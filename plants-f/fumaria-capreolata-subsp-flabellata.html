Fumaria capreolata subsp. flabellata

Fumaria capreolata subsp.

flabellata, commonly known as the flabellate corydalis, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. This subspecies is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and rocky habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its potential health benefits.
