Festuca calabrica

Festuca calabrica, commonly known as the Calabrian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant has fine, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in the spring. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance.

Festuca calabrica is also noted for its role in stabilizing soil in marginal areas.
