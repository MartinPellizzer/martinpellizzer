Fagus × blairii

Fagus × blairii, commonly known as the Blight's beech, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Fagus sylvatica and Fagus grandifolia.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in temperate regions of the eastern United States. The plant has elliptical leaves with serrated edges and produces small, cup-shaped buds that open in the spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish in color, becoming fissured with age.

Fagus × blairii is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
