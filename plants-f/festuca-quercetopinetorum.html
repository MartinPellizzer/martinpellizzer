Festuca quercetopinetorum

Festuca quercetopinetorum, commonly known as the Corsican bentgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in coastal areas and mountainous regions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Festuca quercetopinetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
