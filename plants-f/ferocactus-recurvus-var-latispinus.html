Ferocactus recurvus var. latispinus

Ferocactus recurvus var. latispinus, commonly known as the broad-spined hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered in thick, spiny areoles. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid and semi-arid environments.

Its unique morphology and adaptability make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus collections.
