Ficus populoides var. maculosa

Ficus populoides var. maculosa, commonly known as the spotted rubber fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent white or pale markings, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish fruits. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.

Ficus populoides var. maculosa prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
