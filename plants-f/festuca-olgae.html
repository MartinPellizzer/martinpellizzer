Festuca olgae

Festuca olgae, commonly known as the Olgas' fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in alpine meadows and rocky soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny seeds. Festuca olgae is characterized by its resilience to harsh climatic conditions, making it a hardy component of high-altitude ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important plant for ecological studies and conservation efforts in its native habitat.
