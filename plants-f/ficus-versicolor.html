Ficus versicolor

Ficus versicolor, commonly known as the variable fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that can vary in shape and size depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green or yellow when ripe. Ficus versicolor thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
