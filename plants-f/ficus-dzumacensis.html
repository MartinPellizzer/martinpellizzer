Ficus dzumacensis

Ficus dzumacensis, commonly known as the Dzumac fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly in countries like Mexico and Guatemala. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
