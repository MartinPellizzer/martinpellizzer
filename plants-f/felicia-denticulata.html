Felicia denticulata

Felicia denticulata, commonly known as the toothed fleur-de-lis, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its showy, yellow flowers with white centers. The plant has finely toothed leaves that are often blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Felicia denticulata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
