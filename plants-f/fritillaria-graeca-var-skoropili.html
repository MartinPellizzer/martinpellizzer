Fritillaria graeca var. skoropili

Fritillaria graeca var. skoropili, commonly known as the Greek fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It features striking bell-shaped flowers that are typically pale yellow to orange in color, with distinctive purple or brown markings. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, blooming in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
