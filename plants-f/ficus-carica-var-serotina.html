Ficus carica var. serotina

Ficus carica var. serotina, commonly known as the late fig, is a variant of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its late ripening fruit, which typically matures later in the growing season compared to standard fig varieties. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually smooth and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture. It produces small, clustered fruits that are typically purple or black when ripe.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique fruiting time and flavor profile.
