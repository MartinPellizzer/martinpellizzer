Ficus pseudobotryoides

Ficus pseudobotryoides, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glossy dark green leaves that are arranged in a distinctive weeping or cascading manner. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish fruits that turn yellow or red as they mature. It is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions.

However, it can be toxic if ingested, making it important to handle with care.
