Froesia diffusa

Froesia diffusa, commonly known as the Brazilian violet, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Violaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, violet-colored flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forests and undergrowth. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are often covered in fine hairs.

Froesia diffusa is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
