Ficus glumosa var. lanuginosa

Ficus glumosa var. lanuginosa, commonly known as the silky fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive, velvety appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a prominent midrib.

It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fig fruits.
