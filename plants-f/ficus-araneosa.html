Ficus araneosa

Ficus araneosa, commonly known as the net-veined fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, net-like venation on its leaves, which give it a unique appearance. The plant produces small, edible figs that are typically green when mature. Ficus araneosa is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect sunlight.
