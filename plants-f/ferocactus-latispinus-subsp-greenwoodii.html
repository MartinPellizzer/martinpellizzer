Ferocactus latispinus subsp. greenwoodii

Ferocactus latispinus subsp.

greenwoodii, commonly known as the Greenwood's thickspine cactus, is a columnar cactus native to the Sonoran Desert. It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems and prominent, spiny areoles.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its resilience and unique appearance in desert landscapes.
