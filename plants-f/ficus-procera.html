Ficus procera

Ficus procera, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive drooping branches and elongated, narrow leaves that give it a cascading appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish fruits that turn yellow or red when mature. It is widely cultivated as a decorative plant due to its elegant form and ability to thrive in indoor environments.

However, it is important to note that it can be toxic to pets if ingested.
