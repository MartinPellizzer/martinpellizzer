Fargesia brevipes

Fargesia brevipes, commonly known as the dwarf bamboo, is a compact, cold-hardy plant native to China.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This plant typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative plant in shaded areas. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly, forming dense clumps over time.

Fargesia brevipes is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.
