Festuca gracilis

Festuca gracilis, commonly known as slender fescue, is a cool-season grass native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges. Festuca gracilis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.

It is also important in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
