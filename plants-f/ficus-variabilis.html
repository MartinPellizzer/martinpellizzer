Ficus variabilis

Ficus variabilis, commonly known as the variegated fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that often display striking variegation in white, yellow, or cream. It produces small, clustered fruits called figs, which are typically green when mature.

Ficus variabilis is popular as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
