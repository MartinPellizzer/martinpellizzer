Festuca rupicola subsp. bauzanina

Festuca rupicola subsp.

bauzanina, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada bermudagrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
