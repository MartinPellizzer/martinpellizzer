Festuca cinerea var. trachyphylla

Festuca cinerea var. trachyphylla, commonly known as silver hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features narrow, silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
