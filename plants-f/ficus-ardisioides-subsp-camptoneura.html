Ficus ardisioides subsp. camptoneura

Ficus ardisioides subsp.

camptoneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines.

The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, greenish-white flowers clustered in inflorescences, which are followed by edible fig fruits.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
