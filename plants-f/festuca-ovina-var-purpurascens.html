Festuca ovina var. purpurascens

Festuca ovina var. purpurascens, commonly known as purple sheep fescue, is a cool-season grass native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its distinctive purple-tinged foliage, which becomes more pronounced in cooler weather. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-based flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its vibrant color and ability to form a low-maintenance ground cover.
