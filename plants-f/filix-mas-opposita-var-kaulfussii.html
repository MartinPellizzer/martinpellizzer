Filix-mas opposita var. kaulfussii

Filix-mas opposita var. kaulfussii, commonly known as the Kaulfuss's male fern, is a variant of the male fern species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that are typically bipinnate, with a complex structure that includes fertile and sterile sporangia. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical forests. Its reproductive structures are positioned on the underside of the fronds, a feature common in many ferns.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
