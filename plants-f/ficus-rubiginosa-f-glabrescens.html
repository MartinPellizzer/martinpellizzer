Ficus rubiginosa f. glabrescens

Ficus rubiginosa f. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leaved rubber fig, is a variant of the rubber fig tree native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fig fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This cultivar is popular as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
