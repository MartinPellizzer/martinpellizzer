Festuca ovina subsp. beckeri

Festuca ovina subsp.

beckeri, commonly known as Becker's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces blue-green foliage that is highly ornamental.

The plant typically grows in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold and arid environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and forms dense clumps, making it suitable for erosion control and landscaping.

Its flowering period occurs in late summer, with small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers.
