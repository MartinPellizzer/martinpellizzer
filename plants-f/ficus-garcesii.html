Ficus garcesii

Ficus garcesii, commonly known as the Mexican fig tree, is a tropical plant native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus garcesii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable environments.
