Fevillea marcgravii

Fevillea marcgravii, commonly known as the Brazilian water lily, is a tropical aquatic plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its broad, floating leaves that provide shade and habitat for aquatic life. The plant produces striking white flowers with a yellow center, which bloom in the early morning. Its roots anchor it to the bottom of water bodies, while its rhizomes spread horizontally.

Fevillea marcgravii plays an important role in maintaining water quality and supporting biodiversity in its native ecosystems.
