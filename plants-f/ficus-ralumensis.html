Ficus ralumensis

Ficus ralumensis, commonly known as the Ralumensis Fig Tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries such as Tanzania and Uganda. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible figs. Ficus ralumensis thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
