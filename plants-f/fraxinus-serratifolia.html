Fraxinus serratifolia

Fraxinus serratifolia, commonly known as the serrate ash.

It is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States, characterized by its finely serrated leaf margins and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in pendulous catkins, followed by winged seeds dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
