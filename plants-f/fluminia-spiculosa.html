Fluminia spiculosa

Fluminia spiculosa, commonly known as the spiked fluminia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen shrub form and is known for its clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a textured appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Fluminia spiculosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden settings.
