Fumaria macrocarpa var. laxa

Fumaria macrocarpa var. laxa, commonly known as the laxa yellowcress, is a flowering plant in the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, yellow flowers and hairy, lobed leaves. This variant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant produces small, elongated siliques that contain numerous seeds.

It is an annual herb that thrives in temperate climates and is known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.
