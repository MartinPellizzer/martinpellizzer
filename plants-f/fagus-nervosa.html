Fagus nervosa

Fagus nervosa, commonly known as the Japanese beech, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and alternate, serrated leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, spore-bearing cones and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is often found in forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.

Its botanical features make it a unique member of the Fagaceae family.
