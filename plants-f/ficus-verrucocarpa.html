Ficus verrucocarpa

Ficus verrucocarpa, commonly known as the warty fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, warty bark and broad, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often grown as an ornamental plant.

Ficus verrucocarpa is also valued for its ability to purify air and adapt well to indoor conditions.
