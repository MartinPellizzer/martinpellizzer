Fernaldia asperoglottis

Fernaldia asperoglottis, commonly known as the rough-stemmed fernaldia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and small, narrow leaves with a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in late spring to early summer. Fernaldia asperoglottis is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient species in harsh conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
