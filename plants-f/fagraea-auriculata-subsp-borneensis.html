Fagraea auriculata subsp. borneensis

Fagraea auriculata subsp.

borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean bellflower, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the islands of Borneo. It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color beneath.

The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters at the ends of branches. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
