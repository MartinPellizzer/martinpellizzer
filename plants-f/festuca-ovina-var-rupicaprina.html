Festuca ovina var. rupicaprina

Festuca ovina var. rupicaprina, commonly known as mountain brome, is a perennial grass species native to alpine and subalpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is used in some ecological restoration projects and as a forage plant for livestock.
