Ficus brachypoda var. scoana

Ficus brachypoda var. scoana, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually less than 5 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into round, greenish fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its compact size and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
