Filago canescens

Filago canescens, commonly known as the hairy filago, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Its leaves are usually hairy and grayish-green, providing a distinguishing feature.

Filago canescens produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
