Ficus homodroma

Ficus homodroma, commonly known as the dwarf rubber tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of about 3 meters. The plant features simple, alternate, ovate leaves with a glossy, dark green upper surface. It produces small, clustered flowers followed by spherical, greenish fruits.

Ficus homodroma is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
