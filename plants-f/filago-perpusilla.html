Filago perpusilla

Filago perpusilla, commonly known as the dwarf filago, is a small, annual plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves and small yellow flowers that appear in the summer. Filago perpusilla is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in dry, sunny environments.

Its botanical features make it a hardy and resilient species, well adapted to its native habitat.
