Fuchsia microphylla subsp. chiapensis

Fuchsia microphylla subsp.

chiapensis, commonly known as the Chiapan fuchsia, is a shrub native to the humid forests of southern Mexico. It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of vibrant pink to purple flowers, which bloom in the spring and summer seasons. Its flowers are typically tubular with five lobes, a feature common in the Fuchsia genus.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
