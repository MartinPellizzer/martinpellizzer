Feuilleea contorta

Feuilleea contorta, commonly known as the twisted leaf fern, is a unique vascular plant characterized by its intricate, curling fronds.

Native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia, it thrives in shaded, moist environments. Its leaves are thick and leathery, with a waxy surface that aids in water retention. The plant reproduces via spores, which are released from specialized structures called sporangia located on the underside of the fronds.

Feuilleea contorta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and air-purifying properties.
