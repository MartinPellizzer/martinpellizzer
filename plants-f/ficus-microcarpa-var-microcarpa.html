Ficus microcarpa var. microcarpa

Ficus microcarpa var. microcarpa, commonly known as the banyan tree, is a large evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are typically heart-shaped and can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, fuzzy fruits that contain a single seed. It has a distinctive aerial roots that grow from its branches and can reach the ground, forming additional trunks.

This species is well adapted to tropical climates and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
