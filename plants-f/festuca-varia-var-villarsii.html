Festuca varia var. villarsii

Festuca varia var. villarsii, commonly known as the Alpine bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that gives it its common name.

Festuca varia var. villarsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in high-altitude habitats.
