Ficus lanuginosa

Ficus lanuginosa, commonly known as the silky fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive silvery appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus lanuginosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
