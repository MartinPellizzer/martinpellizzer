Ficus fischeri

Ficus fischeri, commonly known as the Fischer's fig, is a unique species of fig tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy, and often colorful leaves that can range from green to red or purple. The plant produces small, edible figs that are typically consumed by local wildlife and humans alike. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
