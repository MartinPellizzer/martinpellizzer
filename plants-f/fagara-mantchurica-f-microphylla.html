Fagara mantchurica f. microphylla

Fagara mantchurica f. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved mantchurian gourd, is a botanical variant characterized by its reduced leaf size compared to the typical form.

It is a member of the Rutaceae family, known for its aromatic foliage and medicinal properties. The plant typically grows in temperate regions, preferring well-drained soils and partial shade. Its small, elliptical leaves are often covered in a fine pubescence, contributing to its distinct appearance.

This subspecies is valued for its potential use in traditional medicine and horticultural cultivation.
