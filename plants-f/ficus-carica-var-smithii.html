Ficus carica var. smithii

Ficus carica var. smithii, commonly known as the Smythii fig, is a variant of the common fig tree native to the Mediterranean region.

This cultivar is characterized by its small, ornamental leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It produces small, edible figs that are typically harvested in late summer. The plant is valued for its compact growth habit and aesthetic appeal in gardens.

However, it is less commonly cultivated for fruit production compared to other fig varieties.
