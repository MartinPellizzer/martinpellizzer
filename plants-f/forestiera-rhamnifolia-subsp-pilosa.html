Forestiera rhamnifolia subsp. pilosa

Forestiera rhamnifolia subsp.

pilosa, commonly known as the hairy forestiera, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, round berries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and dry slopes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and native landscaping.
