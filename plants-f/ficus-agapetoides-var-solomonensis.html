Ficus agapetoides var. solomonensis

Ficus agapetoides var. solomonensis, commonly known as the Solomon Islands fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Solomon Islands.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits that are yellow when ripe. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
