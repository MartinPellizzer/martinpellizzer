Faba major var. longisiliqua

Faba major var. longisiliqua, commonly known as the long silique vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated pods that contain several seeds. The plant typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is native to parts of the Mediterranean region. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, yellowish flowers.

It is valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability and has been used in traditional agriculture.
