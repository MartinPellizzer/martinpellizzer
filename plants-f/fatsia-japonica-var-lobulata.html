Fatsia japonica var. lobulata

Fatsia japonica var. lobulata, commonly known as the Japanese aralia or lobed fatsia, is a variant of the fatsia plant native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, palmate, lobed leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, white, clustered flowers in the summer, followed by black, berry-like fruits. It thrives in shady, moist environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental foliage.

This cultivar is relatively hardy and adaptable to a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for gardeners.
