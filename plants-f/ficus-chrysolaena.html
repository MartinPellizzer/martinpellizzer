Ficus chrysolaena

Ficus chrysolaena, commonly known as the golden fig or yellow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a yellowish or golden tinge on the underside. It produces small, greenish flowers that develop into round, green fruits.

Ficus chrysolaena is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
