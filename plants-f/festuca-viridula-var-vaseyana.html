Festuca viridula var. vaseyana

Festuca viridula var. vaseyana, commonly known as Vasey's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and compact growth habit, making it a desirable ornamental plant. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

Festuca viridula var. vaseyana is often used in native plant gardens for its ecological benefits and aesthetic appeal.
