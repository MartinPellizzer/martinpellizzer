Fumana laevis var. pabularis

Fumana laevis var. pabularis, commonly known as the smooth fumana, is a variant of the fumana plant characterized by its smooth, hairless foliage.

It is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America, particularly in the Andean foothills. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its leaves are elongated and lance-shaped, with a soft, velvety texture.

The flowers are small, white or pale pink, and arranged in clusters, contributing to its ornamental value in gardens.
