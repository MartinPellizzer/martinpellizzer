Fritillaria serpenticola

Fritillaria serpenticola, commonly known as the snake's head fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to pale yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, typically growing in rocky or alpine habitats. Its bulbs are adapted for survival in harsh environments, allowing it to endure cold winters and dry summers.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
