Fragaria dioica

Fragaria dioica, commonly known as wild strawberry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, white flowers that develop into edible berries. The plant's fruits are aggregate fruits composed of multiple achenes, each containing a single seed. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grassy areas, along roadsides, and in disturbed soils.

The plant has a shallow root system and is well adapted to colonize open habitats.
