Ficus microcarpa var. pusillifolia

Ficus microcarpa var. pusillifolia, commonly known as the small-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically small and ovate.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig fruits, which are often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and shade-providing capabilities.
