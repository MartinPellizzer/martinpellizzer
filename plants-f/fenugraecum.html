Fenugraecum

Fenugraecum, commonly known as the wild turnip or Indian cabbages, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Brassicaceae.

These plants are characterized by their leafy rosettes and small, yellow flowers that typically bloom in the spring. Fenugraecum species are native to temperate regions of Europe, Asia, and North Africa. They are often found growing in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open fields.

The plant's edible leaves and seeds have been used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
