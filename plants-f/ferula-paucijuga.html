Ferula paucijuga

Ferula paucijuga, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ferula, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, slender stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Ferula paucijuga thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often growing at high altitudes.
