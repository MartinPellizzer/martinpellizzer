Ficus comata

Ficus comata, commonly known as the curly-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that have a distinctive curled appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically clustered in spherical syconia. Ficus comata thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant.

Its ability to adapt to various light conditions makes it a popular choice for both outdoor gardens and indoor spaces.
