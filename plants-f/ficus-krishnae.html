Ficus krishnae

Ficus krishnae, commonly known as Krishna's fig, is a rare and unique species native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves that are often a deep green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature, though they may change color depending on the variety. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, Ficus krishnae is considered a valuable species in botanical research and conservation efforts.
