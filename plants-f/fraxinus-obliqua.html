Fraxinus obliqua

Fraxinus obliqua, commonly known as the western white fraxin, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
