Ferulago sylvatica subsp. sylvatica

Ferulago sylvatica subsp.

sylvatica, commonly known as the European cow-wheat, is a perennial herb native to Europe. It typically grows in woodland and grassland habitats, reaching heights of up to 60 cm.

The plant has dark green, ovate leaves with a hairy texture and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. Its fruits are dry, three-lobed capsules that release tiny seeds when mature.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and its historical use in traditional medicine.
