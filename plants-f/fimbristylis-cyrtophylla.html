Fimbristylis cyrtophylla

Fimbristylis cyrtophylla, commonly known as the slender threadgrass, is a small, herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wet or seasonally wet environments such as swampy areas, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white or pale green flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which helps stabilize soil in wetland habitats.

Fimbristylis cyrtophylla plays an important ecological role in maintaining the biodiversity of its native wetland ecosystems.
