Forestiera texana var. palmeri

Forestiera texana var. palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's forestiera, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features small, ovate leaves with a leathery texture and produces clusters of fragrant white flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a small, fleshy drupe that contains several seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
