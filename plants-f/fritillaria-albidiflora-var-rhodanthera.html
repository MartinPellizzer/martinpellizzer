Fritillaria albidiflora var. rhodanthera

Fritillaria albidiflora var. rhodanthera, commonly known as the Rhodanthe fritillary, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange-red. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh environmental conditions, allowing it to thrive in high-altitude environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
