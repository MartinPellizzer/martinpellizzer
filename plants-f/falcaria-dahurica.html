Falcaria dahurica

Falcaria dahurica, commonly known as the Siberian cow-wheat, is a flowering plant native to the cold climates of Asia and Europe.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can be found in meadows and along riverbanks. Its leaves are palmately lobed, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters.

Falcaria dahurica is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
