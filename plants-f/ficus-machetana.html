Ficus machetana

Ficus machetana, commonly known as the African rubber fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are usually heart-shaped. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by fig-like fruits that are typically clustered. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid environments.

Ficus machetana is also valued for its rubber-like latex, which has been used in traditional applications.
