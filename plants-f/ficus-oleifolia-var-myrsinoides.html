Ficus oleifolia var. myrsinoides

Ficus oleifolia var. myrsinoides, commonly known as the silver ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with a distinctive silver or white vein pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, greenish-white fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
