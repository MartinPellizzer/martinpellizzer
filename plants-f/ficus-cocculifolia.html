Ficus cocculifolia

Ficus cocculifolia, commonly known as the Indian rubber plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically heart-shaped or broad-ovate in form. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by greenish-yellow fruits containing a single seed.

Ficus cocculifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
