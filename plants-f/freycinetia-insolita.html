Freycinetia insolita

Freycinetia insolita, commonly known as the golden torch palm, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its striking, elongated inflorescences that resemble torches. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in dense clusters, which are followed by bright orange or red fruits. Its leaves are long, pinnate, and often have a waxy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

Freycinetia insolita is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils.
