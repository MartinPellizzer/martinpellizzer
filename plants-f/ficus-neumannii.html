Ficus neumannii

Ficus neumannii, commonly known as the Neumann's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the island of Madagascar and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its fruit, a fig, is a small, edible berry that is typically green when mature.

Ficus neumannii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
