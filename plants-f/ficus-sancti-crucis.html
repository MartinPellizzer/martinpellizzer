Ficus sancti-crucis

Ficus sancti-crucis, commonly known as the Holy Cross Fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered fig fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and cultural significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
