Ferula cupularis

Ferula cupularis, commonly known as the Persian cow's milk plant, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that has been historically used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Ferula cupularis is often found growing in rocky soils and at high altitudes.
