Ficus carica var. labillardierei

Ficus carica var. labillardierei, commonly known as the Labillardiere fig, is a variant of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are pollinated by wasps, a unique process known as capricornization. Its fruit, the fig, is edible and has a sweet, soft flesh.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production in suitable climates.
