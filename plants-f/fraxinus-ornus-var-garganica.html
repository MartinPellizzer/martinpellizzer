Fraxinus ornus var. garganica

Fraxinus ornus var. garganica, commonly known as the Garganica hornbeam, is a variant of the common hornbeam native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and smooth, gray bark. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, which are followed by small, winged fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and resilient species within the Oleaceae family.
