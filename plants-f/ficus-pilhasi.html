Ficus pilhasi

Ficus pilhasi, commonly known as the Pilhasi fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible fig fruits. Ficus pilhasi thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food in its native regions.
