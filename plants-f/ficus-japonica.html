Ficus japonica

Ficus japonica, commonly known as the Japanese fig tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to eastern Asia, particularly Japan and parts of China. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. It produces small, fig-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine.

Ficus japonica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
