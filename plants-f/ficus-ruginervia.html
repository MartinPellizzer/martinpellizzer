Ficus ruginervia

Ficus ruginervia, commonly known as the wrinkled-veined fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have prominent, wavy veins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature, though they may change color depending on the variety. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus ruginervia is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
