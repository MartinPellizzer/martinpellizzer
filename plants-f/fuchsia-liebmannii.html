Fuchsia liebmannii

Fuchsia liebmannii, commonly known as the Liebmann's fuchsia, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its vibrant, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep red to purple. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Fuchsia liebmannii is known for its seasonal blooming, typically occurring in late spring to early summer.
