Fimbristylis contexta

Fimbristylis contexta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wet or marshy habitats. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, three-lobed flowers that are typically greenish or brownish in color.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems and is often found in coastal areas.
