Freycinetia ceramensis

Freycinetia ceramensis, commonly known as the Ceram palm, is a species of flowering plant in the family Arecaceae.

It is native to the Indonesian island of Ceram and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically yellow-green in color. Its fruit is a drupe, containing a single seed, and it plays a role in local ecosystems.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered a vulnerable species.
