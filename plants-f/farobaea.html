Farobaea

Farobaea, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to Africa.

These plants are characterized by their velvety leaves and showy, clustered flowers that come in a variety of colors. Farobaea species are typically herbaceous, with upright stems and are often cultivated as ornamental plants. They thrive in well-drained soil and prefer bright, indirect light.

The genus includes several species, such as Farobaea flaccida and Farobaea glabra, which are popular in horticulture.
