Fabricia longifolia

Fabricia longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf fabricia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces cylindrical pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Fabricia longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
