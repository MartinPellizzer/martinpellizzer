Fumaria parviflora subsp. schleicheri

Fumaria parviflora subsp.

schleicheri, commonly known as the narrow-leaved yellowcress, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia. It is a member of the Brassicaceae family, characterized by its small, four-petaled yellow flowers and pinnately lobed leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils such as along roadsides or in meadows. It produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
