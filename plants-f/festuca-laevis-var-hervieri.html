Festuca laevis var. hervieri

Festuca laevis var. hervieri, commonly known as Hervier's fine fescue, is a cool-season grass species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and a dense, carpet-like growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in shaded to partially shaded environments. It is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to form a low-maintenance lawn or ground cover.

Its foliage is often used in horticultural settings for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.
