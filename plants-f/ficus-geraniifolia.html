Ficus geraniifolia

Ficus geraniifolia, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a tropical plant native to Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its dense, trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that are typically orange or red in color. It thrives in humid environments and is often used as a ground cover or in hanging baskets.

Its ability to climb and adapt to various light conditions makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
