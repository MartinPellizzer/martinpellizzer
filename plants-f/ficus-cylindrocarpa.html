Ficus cylindrocarpa

Ficus cylindrocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus cylindrocarpa is also valued for its ability to adapt to various soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
