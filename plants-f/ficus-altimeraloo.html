Ficus altimeraloo

Ficus altimeraloo, commonly known as the Altimeraloo fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Ficus altimeraloo is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
