Fritillaria muraiana

Fritillaria muraiana, commonly known as the Japanese fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Japan.

It produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or red in color, with a distinctive dark central spot. The plant has a fibrous root system and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Fritillaria muraiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and rockeries.
