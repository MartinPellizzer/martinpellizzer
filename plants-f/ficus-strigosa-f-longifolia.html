Ficus strigosa f. longifolia

Ficus strigosa f. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved fig, is a cultivar of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically lighter green than the standard variety. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, native to southern Africa. It produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often used in cooking.

The botanical features of this plant make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
