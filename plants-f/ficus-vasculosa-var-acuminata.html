Ficus vasculosa var. acuminata

Ficus vasculosa var. acuminata, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called syconia. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant.

The plant is also known for its ability to grow on various surfaces, including walls and trees, making it a popular choice for vertical gardening.
