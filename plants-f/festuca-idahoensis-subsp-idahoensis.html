Festuca idahoensis subsp. idahoensis

Festuca idahoensis subsp.

idahoensis, commonly known as Idaho fescue, is a perennial grass native to the western United States. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green foliage.

The plant typically grows in open, dry to mesic habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed areas. It produces dense, spike-like inflorescences with small, spikelet-based flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens and restoration projects.
