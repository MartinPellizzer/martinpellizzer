Ficus setiflora var. adelpha

Ficus setiflora var. adelpha, commonly known as the silver ficus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, velvety silver-green leaves that are often adorned with a fine, fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by green, round fruits that mature to a red-orange color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers indirect sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is a popular houseplant and is often used in interior landscaping.
