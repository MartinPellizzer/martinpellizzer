Ferulago cappadocica

Ferulago cappadocica, commonly known as Cappadocian ferula, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes parsley and carrots, and is characterized by its aromatic foliage and tall, branched flower stalks. Ferulago cappadocica is often used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and digestive properties.
