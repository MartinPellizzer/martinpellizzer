Ficus iteoides

Ficus iteoides, commonly known as the Chinese Banyan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fig-like fruits that are edible for some animals but generally not for humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus iteoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
