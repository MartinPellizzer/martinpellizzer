Ficus inaequibractea

Ficus inaequibractea, commonly known as the unequal bract fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called syconia. Ficus inaequibractea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Its growth habit is usually shrub-like, with a dense canopy that provides shade in indoor or outdoor settings.
