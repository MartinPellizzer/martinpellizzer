Ficus tinctoria subsp. parasitica

Ficus tinctoria subsp.

parasitica, commonly known as the parasitic fig, is a hemiparasitic plant that derives some of its nutrients from its host through specialized root structures. It belongs to the Moraceae family and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Indonesia.

The plant has small, simple leaves and produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers. It is often found growing on the trunks or branches of other trees, forming a symbiotic relationship with its host.

Its ecological role includes contributing to the biodiversity of forest ecosystems through its parasitic and possibly mutualistic interactions.
