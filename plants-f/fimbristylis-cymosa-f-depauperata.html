Fimbristylis cymosa f. depauperata

Fimbristylis cymosa f. depauperata, commonly known as the sparse-furred sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands. Its flowers are usually greenish or brownish, and it produces small, triangular seeds.

This subspecies is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
