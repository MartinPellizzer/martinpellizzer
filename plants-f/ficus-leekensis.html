Ficus leekensis

Ficus leekensis, commonly known as the leek fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a slightly textured surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible figs that are typically small and sweet.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a food source in its native habitat.
