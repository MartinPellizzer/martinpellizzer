Fragaria platanoides

Fragaria platanoides, commonly known as the meadow strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces small, edible fruits that are typically red, juicy, and sweet, similar to common strawberries. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and grows in clusters, often in moist, open habitats. Its flowers are white with five petals and are followed by the characteristic red, fleshy berries.

The species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary uses.
