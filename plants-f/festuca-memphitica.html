Festuca memphitica

Festuca memphitica, commonly known as Memphitica bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and forms dense, low-growing sod. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetland habitats.

Festuca memphitica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
