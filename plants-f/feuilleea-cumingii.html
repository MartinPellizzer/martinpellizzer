Feuilleea cumingii

Feuilleea cumingii, commonly known as the Cuming's featherleaf, is a rare and delicate flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves that give it its common name, and it produces small, pale yellow flowers during the spring season. The plant thrives in cool, humid climates and is often found in shaded, forested areas. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.

Its botanical study is important for understanding plant adaptation in tropical ecosystems.
