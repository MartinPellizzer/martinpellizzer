Fraxinus excelsior f. horizontalis

Fraxinus excelsior f. horizontalis, commonly known as the horizontal ash, is a rare and ornamental variant of the common ash.

It is characterized by its unique growth habit, with horizontal branches that create a distinctive, cascading appearance. The plant retains its leaves throughout the winter, offering seasonal interest in the landscape. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for garden settings.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic value, it is often cultivated in specialized collections.
