Fragaria vesca var. alpina

Fragaria vesca var. alpina, commonly known as the alpine strawberry, is a variant of the woodland strawberry.

It is a low-growing perennial plant with trifoliate leaves and small, white flowers that develop into red, edible berries. This species is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions. The berries are prized for their intense flavor and are used in culinary applications.

It is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and hardiness.
