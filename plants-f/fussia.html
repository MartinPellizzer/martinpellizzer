Fussia

Fussia, commonly known as the common fuchsia, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Onagraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, often brightly colored flowers that come in shades of red, purple, and pink. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptic, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. Fuchsia plants are native to the southern regions of South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

They are often cultivated as ornamental plants due to their vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
