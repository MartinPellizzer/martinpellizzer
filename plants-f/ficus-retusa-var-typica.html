Ficus retusa var. typica

Ficus retusa var. typica, commonly known as the Variegated Ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinct white or light green variegation patterns. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ornamental value. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and can grow quite large, sometimes reaching up to 12 inches in length.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate watering to maintain its health and vibrant appearance.
