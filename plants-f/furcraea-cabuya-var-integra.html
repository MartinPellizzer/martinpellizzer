Furcraea cabuya var. integra

Furcraea cabuya var. integra, commonly known as the Cabuya plant, is a succulent species native to the arid regions of South America.

It features striking, sword-shaped leaves that are often ribbed and have a waxy, glossy surface. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with pale green or yellowish flowers, which bloom in late winter or early spring. It is well adapted to hot, dry climates and requires minimal water once established.

Its architectural form and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and indoor gardening.
