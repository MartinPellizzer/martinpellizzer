Festuca tancitaroensis

Festuca tancitaroensis, commonly known as the Tancitaro fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are small and spike-like, forming dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Festuca tancitaroensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in native plant communities.
