Festuca purpurascens f. scabriuscula

Festuca purpurascens f. scabriuscula, commonly known as the scabrous purple false bentgrass, is a grass species characterized by its distinctive purple tinge and rough, scabrous texture.

It is a low-growing perennial grass that typically forms dense, mat-like growths. The leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue, with the inflorescence consisting of spikelets that are loosely arranged. This variety is adapted to moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along streambanks.

Its unique appearance makes it a desirable ornamental plant in certain landscaping applications.
