Ficus conocephalifolia

Ficus conocephalifolia, commonly known as the cone-headed fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated, cone-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, with a smooth texture and a slightly waxy surface.

Ficus conocephalifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
