Ficus rapiformis

Ficus rapiformis, commonly known as the arrow leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves, which are usually glossy and dark green. Ficus rapiformis typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible fig fruits, which are an important food source for various wildlife species.
