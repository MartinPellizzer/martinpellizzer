Fagus crenata var. procera

Fagus crenata var. procera, commonly known as the Japanese beech, is a species of deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, serrated leaves. The tree produces small, cup-shaped buds and produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiky husk. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 25 meters.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
