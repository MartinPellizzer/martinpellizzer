Fraxinus urophylla

Fraxinus urophylla, commonly known as the Mexican ash, is a deciduous tree native to central Mexico.

It typically grows to a height of 10–15 meters with a broad, spreading crown. The tree has compound leaves with 10–20 elliptic leaflets, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. Its fruits are samaras, which are winged seeds that disperse by wind.

The bark is smooth and grayish, becoming fissured with age.
