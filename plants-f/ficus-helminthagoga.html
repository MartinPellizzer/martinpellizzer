Ficus helminthagoga

Ficus helminthagoga, commonly known as the African fig, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
