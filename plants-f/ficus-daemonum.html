Ficus daemonum

Ficus daemonum, commonly known as the Devil's Fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces inedible, round fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability.

Its name is believed to originate from the belief that the plant was used in ancient rituals.
