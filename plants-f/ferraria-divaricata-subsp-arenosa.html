Ferraria divaricata subsp. arenosa

Ferraria divaricata subsp.

arenosa, commonly known as the sand ferraria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are linear and often covered in fine hairs, adapting to arid environments.

It is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and coastal dunes.
