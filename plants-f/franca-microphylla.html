Franca microphylla

Franca microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved frangipani, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically less than 10 centimeters in length. The plant produces fragrant, waxy flowers in clusters, often in shades of yellow, orange, or red. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Franca microphylla is also valued for its ability to tolerate coastal conditions and its use in traditional medicine.
