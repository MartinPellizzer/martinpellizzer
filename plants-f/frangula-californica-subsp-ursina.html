Frangula californica subsp. ursina

Frangula californica subsp.

ursina, commonly known as California buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States. It typically grows in coastal and inland regions, often in chaparral and woodland habitats.

The plant features dark green, oval-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a dark, glossy berry that matures in the fall.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
