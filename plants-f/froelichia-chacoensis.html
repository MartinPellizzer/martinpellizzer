Froelichia chacoensis

Froelichia chacoensis, commonly known as the Chaco thornapple, is a flowering plant native to South America, specifically found in the Chaco region of Paraguay and Argentina.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its spiny branches and woody stems. The plant produces small, clustered white or greenish-white flowers, which are typically followed by orange or red berries. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture, contributing to its adaptability in dry and semi-arid environments.

Froelichia chacoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in regions with similar climatic conditions.
