Fagopyrum baldschuanicum

Fagopyrum baldschuanicum, commonly known as the Chinese spinach or buckwheat spinach, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its alternate, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in racemate clusters, followed by triangular, three-lobed seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients.

Due to its adaptability, it is also used in traditional medicine and as a forage crop.
