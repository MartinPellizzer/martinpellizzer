Fragaria virginiana var. australis

Fragaria virginiana var. australis, commonly known as the Southern wild strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically red when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets and runners that spread along the ground. Its flowers are white with five petals and are typically borne in the axils of the leaves.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in culinary and medicinal applications.
