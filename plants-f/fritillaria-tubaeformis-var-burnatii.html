Fritillaria tubaeformis var. burnatii

Fritillaria tubaeformis var. burnatii, commonly known as the Burnat's bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color, with a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a robust root system adapted to its arid or semi-arid habitat. It is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is also of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
