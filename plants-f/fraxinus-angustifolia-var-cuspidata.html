Fraxinus angustifolia var. cuspidata

Fraxinus angustifolia var. cuspidata, commonly known as the narrowleaf ash, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and finely toothed, lance-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by samaras that disperse via wind. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
