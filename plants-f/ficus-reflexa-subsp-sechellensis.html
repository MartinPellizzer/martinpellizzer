Ficus reflexa subsp. sechellensis

Ficus reflexa subsp.

sechellensis, commonly known as the Seychelles fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Seychelles. It is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are often curled or reflexed at the edges.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fig fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas and moist forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
