Freycinetia gaudichaudii

Freycinetia gaudichaudii, commonly known as the broad-leafed pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Pacific, including parts of Australia, New Caledonia, and the Solomon Islands. The plant is characterized by its large, strap-like leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a food wrapping in some cultures. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically arranged in a dense, branched inflorescence.

The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found growing near the shoreline or in mangrove areas.
