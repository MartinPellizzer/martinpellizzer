Festuca broteri

Festuca broteri, commonly known as the dwarf blue grass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths with narrow, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Festuca broteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate drought conditions.
