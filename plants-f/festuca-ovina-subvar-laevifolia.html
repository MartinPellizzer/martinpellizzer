Festuca ovina subvar. laevifolia

Festuca ovina subvar. laevifolia, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and compact growth habit, making it a popular choice for ornamental and turfgrass applications. The leaves are typically narrow and have a smooth, silky texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to form dense, attractive lawns.
