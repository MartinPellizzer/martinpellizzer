Freycinetia spinifera

Freycinetia spinifera, commonly known as the spinifera pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Pandanus genus and is characterized by its stiff, spiny leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters. Its fruit is a fibrous, spiky structure that contains edible seeds.

Freycinetia spinifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional crafts in its native regions.
