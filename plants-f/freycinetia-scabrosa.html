Freycinetia scabrosa

Freycinetia scabrosa, commonly known as the rough-leaf pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow, rough-textured leaves. It produces small, clustered yellow flowers followed by fleshy, red berries.

Freycinetia scabrosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
