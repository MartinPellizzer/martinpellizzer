Ficus chrysocoma

Ficus chrysocoma, commonly known as the golden fig or yellow fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, including countries like Kenya, Tanzania, and Uganda. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often tinged with yellow or golden hues on the underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits that turn yellow when mature.

Ficus chrysocoma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
