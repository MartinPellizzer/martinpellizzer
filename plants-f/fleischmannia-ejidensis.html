Fleischmannia ejidensis

Fleischmannia ejidensis, commonly known as the Ejidensis Cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its spineless, cylindrical stems that store water. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
