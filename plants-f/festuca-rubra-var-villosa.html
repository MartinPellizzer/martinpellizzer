Festuca rubra var. villosa

Festuca rubra var. villosa, commonly known as hairy red fescue, is a cool-season grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves covered in short, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. This variety is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often used in turfgrass and erosion control. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and is drought-tolerant once established.

Its dense growth habit makes it a valuable plant for stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in challenging environments.
