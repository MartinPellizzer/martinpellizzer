Fagus lucida

Fagus lucida, commonly known as the shining beech, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, chestnuts, and other beech species. The tree is characterized by its smooth, light-colored bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. Its small, spiky nuts are enclosed in a cupule and are typically dispersed by animals.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
