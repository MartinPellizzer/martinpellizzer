Fuchsia × semperflorens

Fuchsia × semperflorens, commonly known as the common fuchsia, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Fuchsia splendens and Fuchsia magellanica.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces showy, pendulous flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant is native to South America and is widely cultivated as an ornamental due to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in cooler climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

The flowers are borne on long, flexible stems and are often used in floral arrangements.
