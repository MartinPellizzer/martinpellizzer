Fragaria vesca var. rosea

Fragaria vesca var. rosea, commonly known as the pink strawberry, is a variant of the woodland strawberry.

It is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-red flowers and small, flavorful berries. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments and is native to Europe and parts of Asia. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated edge, and it produces runners that can establish new plants.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary uses.
