Ficus cabur

Ficus cabur, commonly known as the Bur Ficus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and distinctive fruiting structures. The plant typically grows to a height of 10-20 meters, with a dense canopy that provides shade. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, while the fruit is a small, round fig that matures to a yellowish color.

Ficus cabur is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
