Festuca uniglumis var. longiseta

Festuca uniglumis var. longiseta, commonly known as the long-haired bluegrass, is a grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted leaves and distinctive, elongated inflorescence. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are often covered with long, silky hairs, which give it its common name.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in native plant gardens.
