Ficinia capensis

Ficinia capensis, commonly known as the Cape fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It belongs to the family Moraceae, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in umbels, followed by edible, fleshy fruits.

The Cape fig is adapted to coastal environments and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
