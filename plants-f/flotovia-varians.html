Flotovia varians

Flotovia varians, commonly known as the floating variable fern, is a unique aquatic plant characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that drift on the water's surface.

It thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams, where it can anchor itself using its rhizomes. The plant's leaves are typically light green and vary in shape depending on environmental conditions, hence its scientific name. It plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for small organisms and contributing to water oxygenation.

Due to its adaptability, Flotovia varians is often used in ornamental water gardens.
