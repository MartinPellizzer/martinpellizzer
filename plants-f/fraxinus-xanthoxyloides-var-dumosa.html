Fraxinus xanthoxyloides var. dumosa

Fraxinus xanthoxyloides var. dumosa, commonly known as the dwarf yellow ash, is a compact, low-growing variant of the yellow ash species.

It is native to the southeastern United States, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant features small, ovate leaves with a distinctive yellowish-green hue and smooth, dark bark. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras for seed dispersal.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate urban environments.
