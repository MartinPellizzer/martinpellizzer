Ficus neoehudarum

Ficus neoehudarum, commonly known as the Ecuadorean fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, clustered fig fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its unique features, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists alike.
