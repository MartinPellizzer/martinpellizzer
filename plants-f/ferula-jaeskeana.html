Ferula jaeskeana

Ferula jaeskeana, commonly known as the Kashmiri ferula, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its tall, fibrous stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in a complex, branched inflorescence. Its rhizomes and roots are often harvested for their aromatic resins and medicinal properties.

Ferula jaeskeana is well adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.
