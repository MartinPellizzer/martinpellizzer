Ficus heterophylla f. scabrella

Ficus heterophylla f. scabrella, commonly known as the velvet leaf ficus, is a unique cultivar of the ficus genus characterized by its distinctive foliage.

The plant features broad, velvety leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly fuzzy texture. It is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal. The leaves can vary in size and shape, with some exhibiting a more compact form compared to other ficus species.

This variety is relatively low-maintenance, thriving in bright, indirect light and requiring moderate watering.
