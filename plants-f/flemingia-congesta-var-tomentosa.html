Flemingia congesta var. tomentosa

Flemingia congesta var. tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly flemingia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly pubescence covering its stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a broad ovate shape.

The flowers are small, clustered in dense inflorescences, and are usually white or pale pink in color.
