Ferula foetidissima

Ferula foetidissima, commonly known as the stinking ferula, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, fibrous stalk and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces a strong, unpleasant odor, which is likely a defense mechanism against herbivores. Its leaves are narrow and covered in fine hairs, adapted to reduce water loss in its harsh environment.

Ferula foetidissima is also valued for its medicinal properties, with traditional uses in treating digestive ailments and respiratory conditions.
