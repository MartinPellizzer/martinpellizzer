Fagraea calophylloides

Fagraea calophylloides, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and clusters of bright yellow flowers that appear in the spring. The plant produces small, round fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Fagraea calophylloides is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
