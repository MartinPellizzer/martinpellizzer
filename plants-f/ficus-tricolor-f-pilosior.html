Ficus tricolor f. pilosior

Ficus tricolor f. pilosior, commonly known as the variegated fig tree, is a cultivar of the Ficus tricolor species.

It is characterized by its striking foliage, featuring a combination of green, white, and variegated patterns on its leaves. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid environments. Its leaves are typically broad and glossy, with a slightly hairy texture on the underside, which is a distinguishing feature of the 'pilosior' form.

This cultivar is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
