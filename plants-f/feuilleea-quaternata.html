Feuilleea quaternata

Feuilleea quaternata, commonly known as the four-leaf clover of the tropics, is a herbaceous plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves with a velvety texture and a distinctive four-leaf pattern, which is rare in its natural form. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and it is often found growing in shaded, moist environments. Its botanical significance lies in its unique leaf morphology and potential medicinal properties.

Feuilleea quaternata is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing qualities.
