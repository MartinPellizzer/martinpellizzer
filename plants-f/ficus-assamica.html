Ficus assamica

Ficus assamica, commonly known as the Assam fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the Indian subcontinent, particularly found in the northeastern regions of India, including Assam. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a smooth surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible figs, which are enclosed within a syconium.

Ficus assamica is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
