Fagus sylvatica var. quercifolia

Fagus sylvatica var. quercifolia, commonly known as the European beech with oak leaves, is a variant of the common beech tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that resemble those of the oak, giving it a unique appearance. This plant is native to central and northern Europe and thrives in temperate climates. It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, with smooth, gray bark and dense, rounded canopies.

The leaves turn yellow in autumn, contributing to its seasonal beauty in forest ecosystems.
