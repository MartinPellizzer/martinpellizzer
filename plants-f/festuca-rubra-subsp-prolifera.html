Festuca rubra subsp. prolifera

Festuca rubra subsp.

prolifera, commonly known as red fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas. Festuca rubra subsp.

prolifera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
