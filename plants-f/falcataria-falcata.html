Falcataria falcata

Falcataria falcata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved mangrove, is a species of flowering plant in the family Meliaceae.

It is native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia and is adapted to saline and brackish water environments. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color.

Falcataria falcata plays an important role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial species.
