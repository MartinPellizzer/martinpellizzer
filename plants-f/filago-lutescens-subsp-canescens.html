Filago lutescens subsp. canescens

Filago lutescens subsp.

canescens, commonly known as the grey filago, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family. It is characterized by its hairy, silver-green leaves and yellow flower heads that bloom in summer.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. It is native to southern Africa and is adapted to arid conditions.

The subspecies is distinguished by its pale, woolly appearance compared to other members of the species.
