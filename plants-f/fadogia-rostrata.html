Fadogia rostrata

Fadogia rostrata, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and forest edges.

Fadogia rostrata is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
