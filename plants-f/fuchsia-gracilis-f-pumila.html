Fuchsia gracilis f. pumila

Fuchsia gracilis f. pumila, commonly known as the dwarf fuchsia, is a compact, ornamental shrub native to South America.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from violet to deep purple, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, upright stems and small, ovate leaves with a glossy texture. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This cultivar is prized for its vibrant floral display and is often used in garden landscapes or as a container plant.
