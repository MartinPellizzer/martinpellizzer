Ficus afghanistanica

Ficus afghanistanica, commonly known as the Afghan fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Afghanistan and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature, and it thrives in warm, humid climates. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

Ficus afghanistanica is also valued for its role in local ecosystems, providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
