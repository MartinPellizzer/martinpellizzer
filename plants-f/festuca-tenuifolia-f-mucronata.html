Festuca tenuifolia f. mucronata

Festuca tenuifolia f. mucronata, commonly known as the slender fescue with a short tip, is a botanical variant of the slender fescue species.

It is a cool-season grass characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or alpine regions. Its foliage is usually bluish-green, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in horticultural settings for its aesthetic qualities.
