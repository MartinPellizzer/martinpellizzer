Ficus carica var. domina

Ficus carica var. domina, commonly known as the Persian fig, is a cultivar of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and thick, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested when they are fully ripe and soft. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to bear fruit in a relatively short time compared to other fig varieties.
