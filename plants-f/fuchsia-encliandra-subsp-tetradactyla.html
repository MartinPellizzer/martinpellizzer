Fuchsia encliandra subsp. tetradactyla

Fuchsia encliandra subsp.

tetradactyla, commonly known as the Chilean fuchsia, is a species native to southern Chile and Argentina. It is a shrub characterized by its woody stems and dense foliage.

The plant produces vibrant, tubular flowers that are typically pink or red, which attract hummingbirds and other pollinators. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
