Fresenia pinnatilobata

Fresenia pinnatilobata, commonly known as the velvetleaf or Brazilian velvetleaf, is a tropical flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a velvety texture. The plant produces showy, clustered flowers that are usually pink or red in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Fresenia pinnatilobata thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in wetland or marshy environments.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in tropical regions.
