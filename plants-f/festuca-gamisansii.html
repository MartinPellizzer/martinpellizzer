Festuca gamisansii

Festuca gamisansii, commonly known as the Gamisan grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand harsh conditions.
