Ficus longipedunculata

Ficus longipedunculata, commonly known as the long-pedunculate fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
