Ficus mexiae

Ficus mexiae, commonly known as the Mexican fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered fruit. The plant is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in humid, well-drained soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Ficus mexiae is also valued as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
