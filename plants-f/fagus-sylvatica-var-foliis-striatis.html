Fagus sylvatica var. foliis-striatis

Fagus sylvatica var. foliis-striatis, commonly known as the striped beech, is a variant of the European beech tree characterized by its distinctive leaf patterns.

The leaves exhibit longitudinal white or pale green stripes that run along the midrib, giving the foliage a unique appearance. This variation is a natural mutation that occurs in certain populations of the species. The tree is typically found in temperate regions of Europe, growing in moist, well-drained soils.

It retains its ornamental value throughout the growing season, making it a popular choice for horticultural and landscape purposes.
