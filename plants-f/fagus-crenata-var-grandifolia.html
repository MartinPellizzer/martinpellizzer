Fagus crenata var. grandifolia

Fagus crenata var. grandifolia, commonly known as the Japanese beech, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and chestnuts, and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, elliptical leaves. The leaves are typically serrated and turn yellow in autumn before falling. It produces small, edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which are an important food source for various wildlife species.

This plant thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions of Japan.
