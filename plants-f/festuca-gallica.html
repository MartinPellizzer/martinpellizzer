Festuca gallica

Festuca gallica, commonly known as bluegrass or French grass, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and produces small, blue to purple flowers in the summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Festuca gallica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
