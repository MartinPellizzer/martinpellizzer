Ficus mattogrossensis

Ficus mattogrossensis, commonly known as the Brazilian fig tree, is a species native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Moraceae family, characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible figs, which are an important food source for various wildlife. Ficus mattogrossensis typically grows as a tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat.

Its ability to thrive in humid, tropical conditions makes it a significant component of the rainforest ecosystem.
