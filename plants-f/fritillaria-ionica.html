Fritillaria ionica

Fritillaria ionica, commonly known as the Greek bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils. It blooms in early spring and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Fritillaria ionica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
