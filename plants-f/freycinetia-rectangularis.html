Freycinetia rectangularis

Freycinetia rectangularis, commonly known as the broad-leafed screw pine, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves that are broad and strap-like in shape. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-white flowers that are followed by woody, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in coastal and wetland environments, often growing in shallow water or on sandy soils.

Freycinetia rectangularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional landscaping in tropical regions.
