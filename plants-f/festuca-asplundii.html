Festuca asplundii

Festuca asplundii, commonly known as Asplund's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments. Festuca asplundii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its hardy nature.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil in rocky and arid habitats.
