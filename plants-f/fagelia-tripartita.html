Fagelia tripartita

Fagelia tripartita, commonly known as the three-parted fagelia, is a lesser-known species within the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three oval-shaped leaflets arranged symmetrically. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically a mix of white and purple hues. Fagelia tripartita is native to specific regions in South America, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and unique morphology, it is of interest to botanists and plant enthusiasts.
