Frangula revoluta

Frangula revoluta, commonly known as the prickly ash or whitebeard tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries in the fall. Its bark is thick and fissured, often peeling in strips, and it contains compounds used in traditional medicine.

Frangula revoluta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
