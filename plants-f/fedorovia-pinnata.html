Fedorovia pinnata

Fedorovia pinnata, commonly known as the silverleaf pittosporum, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its silvery-green foliage and fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves with a pinnate arrangement, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Fedorovia pinnata is also valued for its ability to tolerate various soil types and its use in horticultural landscapes.
