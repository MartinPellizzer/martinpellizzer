Filago pilularis

Filago pilularis, commonly known as the ball-plant or mouse-ear, is a small, flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant has a low-growing habit with hairy stems and small, grayish-green leaves. Its flowers are typically yellow and appear in clusters, and it produces small, spherical fruits that are dispersed by wind.

Filago pilularis is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
