Ficus bubu

Ficus bubu, commonly known as the Bubu fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Ficus bubu is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
