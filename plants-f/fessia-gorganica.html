Fessia gorganica

Fessia gorganica, commonly known as the Sicilian fessia, is a rare and endangered plant species native to the island of Sicily.

It is a small, herbaceous plant with delicate, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas. Due to habitat loss and environmental changes, it is critically endangered and protected by local conservation efforts.

Its unique botanical features make it an important subject for ecological and botanical research.
