Filicula douglasii

Filicula douglasii, commonly known as the Douglas's fern, is a species of fern belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that typically grow in a rosette formation. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories or along stream banks. Its spores are produced on the underside of its fronds, which are arranged in a spiral pattern.

Filicula douglasii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and indirect light.
