Ficus glauca

Ficus glauca, commonly known as the white fig or blue fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to parts of Africa, including Kenya and Tanzania, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a bluish tint, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Ficus glauca is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
