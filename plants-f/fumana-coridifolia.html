Fumana coridifolia

Fumana coridifolia, commonly known as the sweet flag, is a perennial wetland plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It is characterized by its aromatic, grass-like leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in dense clusters. The plant typically grows in marshy or aquatic environments, often forming dense stands in shallow water. Its rhizomes are widely used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
