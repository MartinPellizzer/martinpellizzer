Fadogia tomentosa var. calvescens

Fadogia tomentosa var. calvescens, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pod-like fruits containing kidney-shaped seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in forested areas.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
