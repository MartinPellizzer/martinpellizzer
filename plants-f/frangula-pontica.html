Frangula pontica

Frangula pontica, commonly known as the Pontic buckthorn, is a shrub native to southeastern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, black berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. Frangula pontica is typically found in dry, rocky soils and can thrive in harsh environmental conditions.

It is also valued for its medicinal properties, particularly in traditional herbal remedies.
