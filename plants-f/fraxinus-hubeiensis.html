Fraxinus hubeiensis

Fraxinus hubeiensis, commonly known as the Hubei ash, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by samaroid fruits that are dispersed by wind.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
