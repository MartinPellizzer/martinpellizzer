Ficus artocarpoides

Ficus artocarpoides, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. Its fruit is a smooth, fleshy berry that turns orange when mature.

Ficus artocarpoides is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
