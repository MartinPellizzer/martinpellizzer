Fragaria viridis var. dubia

Fragaria viridis var. dubia, commonly known as the green strawberry, is a variant of the common strawberry plant.

It is characterized by its small, green, edible fruits that are typically less sweet than the typical red strawberries. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and has a low-growing habit. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along forest edges.

This variety is valued for its unique appearance and mild flavor, making it a popular choice for ornamental and culinary purposes.
