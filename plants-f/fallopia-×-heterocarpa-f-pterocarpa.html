Fallopia × heterocarpa f. pterocarpa

Fallopia × heterocarpa f. pterocarpa, commonly known as the giant false burdock, is a hybrid plant species within the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces large, showy flowers arranged in panicle inflorescences, with both male and female flowers present on the same plant. Its leaves are heart-shaped, with a rough texture and prominent veins, and its roots are thick and fibrous.

This species is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
