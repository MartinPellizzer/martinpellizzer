Feuilleea obtusata

Feuilleea obtusata, commonly known as the broadleaf fern, is a species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered spores rather than flowers, which are released from sporangia located on the underside of the fronds. Feuilleea obtusata thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its foliage provides habitat for various small insects and contributes to the biodiversity of its ecosystem.
