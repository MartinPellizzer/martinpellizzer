Faucaria kendrewensis

Faucaria kendrewensis, commonly known as the Kendrew's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the arid regions of Namibia.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that store water. The plant produces small, star-shaped yellow flowers during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic environments.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
