Ficus capreifolia

Ficus capreifolia, commonly known as the rock fig or Mediterranean fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ficus capreifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
