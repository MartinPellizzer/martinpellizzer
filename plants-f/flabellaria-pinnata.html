Flabellaria pinnata

Flabellaria pinnata, commonly known as the fanleaf pellitory, is a perennial herb native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Asteraceae and is characterized by its distinctive, fan-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom during the warmer months. Its root system is fibrous and has been traditionally used in herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.

Flabellaria pinnata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.
