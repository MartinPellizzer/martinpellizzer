Ficus balansana

Ficus balansana, commonly known as the Balansana fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature.

Ficus balansana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
