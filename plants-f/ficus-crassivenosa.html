Ficus crassivenosa

Ficus crassivenosa, commonly known as the thick-veined fig, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent, thick veins that give it its common name. Its fruit, known as a fig, is edible and contains small, seed-bearing structures called syconia.

Ficus crassivenosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
