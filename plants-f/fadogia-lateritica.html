Fadogia lateritica

Fadogia lateritica, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pale yellow flowers. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in traditional cuisines. It is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.

Fadogia lateritica thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and woodland habitats.
