Fritillaria przewalskii var. longistigma

Fritillaria przewalskii var. longistigma, commonly known as the long-stemmed bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically greenish-yellow in color. The plant produces showy, often pendulous blooms that appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique floral structure and adaptability to arid environments.
