Fritillaria nervosa subsp. falcata

Fritillaria nervosa subsp.

falcata, commonly known as the falcate fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, often orange or reddish-brown flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, thriving in cool, moist environments. Its bulbs are adapted for storage of nutrients, allowing it to survive harsh winter conditions.

The species is of interest to botanists and gardeners due to its unique morphology and ecological significance.
