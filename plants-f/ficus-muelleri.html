Ficus muelleri

Ficus muelleri, commonly known as the Australian fig tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, grayish bark. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically clustered in a spherical structure. Ficus muelleri is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, making it a significant component of the Australian rainforest ecosystem.

Its adaptability to various soil types and climate conditions makes it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
