Ficus cordata subsp. lecardii

Ficus cordata subsp.

lecardii, commonly known as the lecard's fig. It is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia.

The plant features glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. It produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, round fig fruits.

This subspecies is valued in horticulture for its ornamental foliage and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
