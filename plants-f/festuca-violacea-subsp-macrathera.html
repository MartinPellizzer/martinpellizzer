Festuca violacea subsp. macrathera

Festuca violacea subsp.

macrathera, commonly known as purple false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes.

The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, blue to violet flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local biodiversity.
