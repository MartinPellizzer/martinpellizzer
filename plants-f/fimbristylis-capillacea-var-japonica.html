Fimbristylis capillacea var. japonica

Fimbristylis capillacea var. japonica, commonly known as the Japanese hair sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats such as marshes, swamps, and rice fields. The plant has slender, grass-like stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
