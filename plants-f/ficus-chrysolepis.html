Ficus chrysolepis

Ficus chrysolepis, commonly known as the golden fig or yellow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the western United States, particularly California, and thrives in coastal and mountainous regions. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a yellow or golden hue on the underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by fig-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.

Ficus chrysolepis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
