Fallugia micrantha

Fallugia micrantha, commonly known as plains prickly-pear, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its spine-tipped leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in sandy or rocky soils, making it well adapted to arid environments. Its fruit is a fleshy, red berry that contains numerous seeds.

Fallugia micrantha plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing food for wildlife in its native habitat.
