Fraxinus excelsior proles heterophylla

Fraxinus excelsior proles heterophylla, commonly known as the variable-leaf ash, is a rare variant of the common ash tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive variation in leaf morphology, with some leaves being broader and others more elongated. This plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodland areas across Europe. Its leaves are usually dark green on top and paler underneath, with a serrated edge.

The plant is of interest to botanists due to its unique leaf variation and potential ecological significance.
