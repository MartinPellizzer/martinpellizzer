Flourensia oolepis

Flourensia oolepis, commonly known as the Mexican goldenflower, is a herbaceous plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, giving it a striking appearance. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are hairy and often branching.

Flourensia oolepis is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is known for its ornamental value in gardens.
