Fragaria muricata

Fragaria muricata, commonly known as the mountain strawberry, is a species of flowering plant in the family Rosaceae.

It is native to the Andes mountains of South America and is characterized by its hairy, upright stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and culinary value.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is relatively rare in the wild.
