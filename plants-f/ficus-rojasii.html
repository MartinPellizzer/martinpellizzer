Ficus rojasii

Ficus rojasii, commonly known as the Rojas' fig tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Colombia and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a smooth texture. It produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often consumed by local wildlife and humans.

Ficus rojasii is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its lush foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor environments.
