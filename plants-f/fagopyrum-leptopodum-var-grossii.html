Fagopyrum leptopodum var. grossii

Fagopyrum leptopodum var. grossii, commonly known as the large-leaved buckwheat, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It features rhizomatous growth and produces upright, branched stems covered in soft, pubescent hairs. The leaves are alternate, ovate to elliptic, and often have a serrated margin with a hairy underside. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and are typically greenish-white with two stamens.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its foliage and ecological benefits.
