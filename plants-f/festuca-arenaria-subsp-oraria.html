Festuca arenaria subsp. oraria

Festuca arenaria subsp.

oraria, commonly known as the sand couchgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to arctic and alpine regions. It thrives in sandy and gravelly soils, often found in coastal and mountainous environments.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to harsh climates with low temperatures and strong winds.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
