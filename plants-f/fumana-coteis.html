Fumana coteis

Fumana coteis, commonly known as the African orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often fragrant blooms. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories or along riverbanks. Its leaves are broad and lance-shaped, and it produces clusters of small, colorful flowers in shades of white, yellow, and pink.

Fumana coteis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
