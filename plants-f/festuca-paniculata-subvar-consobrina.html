Festuca paniculata subvar. consobrina

Festuca paniculata subvar. consobrina, commonly known as the narrowleaf fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit, making it well-suited for ornamental and ecological landscaping. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers, which bloom in late summer, adding visual interest to the landscape. It prefers well-drained soils and full to partial sun, thriving in temperate climates.

Its hardy nature and low maintenance requirements make it a popular choice for sustainable garden design.
