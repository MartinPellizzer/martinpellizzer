Fragaria vesca var. muricata

Fragaria vesca var. muricata, commonly known as the mossy strawberry, is a variant of the woodland strawberry.

It is characterized by its small, tart berries that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a hairy texture and produces flowers with five white petals. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found in forest understories.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
