Festuca spadicea subsp. aurea

Festuca spadicea subsp.

aurea, commonly known as golden bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northeastern United States. It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale blue to white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits. Festuca spadicea subsp.

aurea is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinators.
