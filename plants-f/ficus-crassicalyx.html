Ficus crassicalyx

Ficus crassicalyx, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when ripe.

Ficus crassicalyx is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
