Forgetina

It is characterized by its striking blue or violet flowers, which are typically arranged in clusters at the end of its stems. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. Native to regions of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

Forgetina is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and symbolic association with remembrance.
