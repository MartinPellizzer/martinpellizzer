Fimbristylis diphylla var. floribunda

Fimbristylis diphylla var. floribunda, commonly known as the pink spikegrass, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with slender, erect stems. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pink or purple flowers, which are typically borne on tall, leafy stalks.
