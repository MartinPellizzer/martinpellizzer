Ficus dottata

Ficus dottata, commonly known as the Bornean fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Borneo, and thrives in tropical rainforest environments. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces small, edible fig fruits that are important for local wildlife. It has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil in its natural habitat.

Ficus dottata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to adapt to various growing conditions.
