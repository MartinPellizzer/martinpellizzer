Feronia limonia

Feronia limonia, commonly known as the kaffir lime, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its fragrant, glossy green leaves and small, round, yellow-orange fruits. The fruit has a thick, segmented rind that is rich in essential oils, making it highly aromatic. The tree thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and culinary uses.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental gardening and traditional medicine.
