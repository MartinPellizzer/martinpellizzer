Ficus pubinervis var. cuneatonervosa

Ficus pubinervis var. cuneatonervosa, commonly known as the Cuneatonervosa Ficus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape with prominent veins. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by green, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Its unique leaf structure and adaptability make it a popular choice for both ornamental and indoor gardening.
