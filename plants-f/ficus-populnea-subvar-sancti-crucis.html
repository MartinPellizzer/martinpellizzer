Ficus populnea subvar. sancti-crucis

Ficus populnea subvar. sancti-crucis, commonly known as the sacred fig or bodhi tree, is a species of fig tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, greyish bark. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

This variety is widely cultivated for its ornamental value and cultural significance in religious traditions.
