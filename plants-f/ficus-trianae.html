Ficus trianae

Ficus trianae, commonly known as the Triana fig or the curly willow fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that often curl at the edges, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible figs, though the fruit is typically not consumed by humans. Ficus trianae thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and as a landscape plant in tropical regions.
