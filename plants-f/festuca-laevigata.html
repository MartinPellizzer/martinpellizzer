Festuca laevigata

Festuca laevigata, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue to purple flowers. Festuca laevigata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
