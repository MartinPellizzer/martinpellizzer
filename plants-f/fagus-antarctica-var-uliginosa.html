Fagus antarctica var. uliginosa

Fagus antarctica var. uliginosa, commonly known as the Antarctic beech, is a variant of the Antarctic beech tree native to the southern hemisphere.

It is a deciduous tree that thrives in cool, moist environments, often found in temperate rainforests. The plant has broad, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, triangular nuts.

This variant is notable for its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions and is an important component of its native ecosystems.
