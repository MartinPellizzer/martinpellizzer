Freycinetia archboldiana

Freycinetia archboldiana, commonly known as the Bornean palm, is a flowering plant belonging to the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the island of Borneo and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by triangular, winged fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to habitat loss, it is considered vulnerable and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
