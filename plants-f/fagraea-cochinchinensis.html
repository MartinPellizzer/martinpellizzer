Fagraea cochinchinensis

Fagraea cochinchinensis, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.
