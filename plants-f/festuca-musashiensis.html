Festuca musashiensis

Festuca musashiensis, commonly known as Japanese fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Festuca musashiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and restoration projects.

It is also noted for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions, making it a resilient choice for gardeners.
