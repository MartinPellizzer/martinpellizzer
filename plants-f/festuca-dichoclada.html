Festuca dichoclada

Festuca dichoclada, commonly known as slender fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are often wind-pollinated. Festuca dichoclada is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed sites.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
