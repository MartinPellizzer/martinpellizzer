Ficus involuta var. urbaniana

Ficus involuta var. urbaniana, commonly known as the urbaniana ficus, is a tropical plant characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and often lobed leaves.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, warm environments. The leaves are typically dark green with a glossy surface, and they can vary in shape depending on the plant's growth conditions. This variety is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.

It prefers indirect sunlight and well-draining soil to maintain optimal health.
