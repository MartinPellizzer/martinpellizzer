Fraxinus excelsior var. aurea-pendula

Fraxinus excelsior var. aurea-pendula, commonly known as the golden weeping ash, is a cultivar of the common ash tree.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches and striking golden-yellow foliage that emerges in early spring. The leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, typical of the Fraxinus genus. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soil.

This ornamental tree is often planted for its aesthetic appeal and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
