Ficus sibulanensis

Ficus sibulanensis, commonly known as the Sibulan fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in its native region.
