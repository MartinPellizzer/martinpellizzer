Ficus beecheyana f. koshunensis

Ficus beecheyana f. koshunensis, commonly known as the Koshun ficus, is a rare and unique variant of the Ficus genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. This cultivar is native to Japan and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. The plant thrives in humid environments and prefers indirect sunlight.

Its botanical significance lies in its genetic uniqueness and its role in horticultural research.
