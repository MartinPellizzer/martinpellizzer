Fragaria virginiana f. maliformis

Fragaria virginiana f. maliformis, commonly known as the scarlet strawberry, is a botanical variant of the wild strawberry.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, heart-shaped fruits that are smaller than typical strawberries. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers with five petals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in horticulture.
