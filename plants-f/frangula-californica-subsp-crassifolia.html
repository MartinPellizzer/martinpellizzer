Frangula californica subsp. crassifolia

Frangula californica subsp.

crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to California. It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats. Its fruit is a small, dark, berry-like structure that ripens in late summer.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its historical use by indigenous peoples.
