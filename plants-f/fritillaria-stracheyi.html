Fritillaria stracheyi

Fritillaria stracheyi, commonly known as the Strachey's fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes, preferring well-drained conditions. Its bulbs are adapted for storage, allowing it to survive harsh winters and periods of drought.

Fritillaria stracheyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific climate requirements.
