Ferula tenuissima

Ferula tenuissima, commonly known as the slender ferula, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or arid environments.

Due to its ornamental value and historical uses, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
