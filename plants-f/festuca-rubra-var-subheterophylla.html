Festuca rubra var. subheterophylla

Festuca rubra var. subheterophylla, commonly known as red fescue, is a cool-season grass that exhibits variable leaf morphology.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green foliage. This variety is known for its ability to thrive in a range of soil types and environmental conditions. It is often used in turfgrass and landscaping due to its durability and aesthetic appeal.

The plant's botanical features include both basal and cauline leaves, with the latter often being shorter and more rigid.
