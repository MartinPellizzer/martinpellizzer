Fraxinus regelii

Fraxinus regelii, commonly known as the Regel's ash, is a deciduous tree native to central Asia, particularly found in regions of Kazakhstan and western China.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and smooth, gray bark. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by samaras that are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a serrated margin, typically turning yellow in autumn.

Fraxinus regelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its graceful form and seasonal interest.
