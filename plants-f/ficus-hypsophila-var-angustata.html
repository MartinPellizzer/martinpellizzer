Ficus hypsophila var. angustata

Ficus hypsophila var. angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf weeping fig, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, elongated leaves that are typically dark green and have a narrow, lance-shaped form. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by small, round fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in indoor environments.

This variety is closely related to other ficus species and is known for its adaptability to various light conditions.
