Ficinia capillaris

Ficinia capillaris, commonly known as the slender sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland and swampy habitats in Australia.

It typically grows in dense clusters, with slender, cylindrical stems that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are usually greenish or pale brown. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it has a robust root system that helps stabilize the soil.

Ficinia capillaris plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
