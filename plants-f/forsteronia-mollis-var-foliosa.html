Forsteronia mollis var. foliosa

Forsteronia mollis var. foliosa, commonly known as the woolly false heather, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Its unique texture and ornamental value make it a popular choice among horticulturists and garden enthusiasts.
