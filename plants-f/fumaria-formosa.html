Fumaria formosa

Fumaria formosa, commonly known as the California glory flower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in open, sunny areas such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves are typical of the genus Fumaria.

It is an annual herb that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in regions with well-drained soils.
