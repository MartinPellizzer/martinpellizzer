Ficus ampelos

Ficus ampelos, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that give it a distinctive, tree-like appearance. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement along the stems. It is a member of the Moraceae family and is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant.

Ficus ampelos prefers bright, indirect light and moderate humidity to thrive.
