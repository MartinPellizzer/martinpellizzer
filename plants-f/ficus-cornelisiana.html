Ficus cornelisiana

Ficus cornelisiana, commonly known as the Cornelian fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often veined with a lighter color. It produces small, fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature.

Ficus cornelisiana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
