Ferdinanda lindenii

Ferdinanda lindenii, commonly known as the Lindenhof orchid, is a species of orchid native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking violet to purple flowers with intricate patterns and a distinctive greenish-yellow lip. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded forest environments. Its botanical features include long, narrow leaves and a compact, clustered inflorescence.

Ferdinanda lindenii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
