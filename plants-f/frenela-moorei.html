Frenela moorei

Frenela moorei, commonly known as the Moore's fenugreek, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and thrives in arid or semi-arid environments. Its leaves are trifoliate, and it produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in spring.

Frenela moorei is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
