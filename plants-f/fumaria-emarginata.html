Fumaria emarginata

Fumaria emarginata, commonly known as the marginate fumewort, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fumariaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from pale yellow to reddish-orange. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly toothed margin.

Fumaria emarginata is known for its short flowering period in early spring and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
