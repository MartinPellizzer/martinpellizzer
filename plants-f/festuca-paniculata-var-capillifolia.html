Festuca paniculata var. capillifolia

Festuca paniculata var. capillifolia, commonly known as slender fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features fine, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, with a dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces panicles of small, spike-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, woodlands, and along streambanks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
