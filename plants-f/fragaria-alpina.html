Fragaria alpina

Fragaria alpina, commonly known as alpine strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically red, though they can sometimes be white or yellow. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and grows in rocky or alpine soils. Its flowers are usually white or pale pink, with five petals, and it blooms in early summer.

Fragaria alpina is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also cultivated for its delicate, flavorful fruits.
