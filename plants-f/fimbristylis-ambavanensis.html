Fimbristylis ambavanensis

Fimbristylis ambavanensis, commonly known as the Ambavane sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. Native to Madagascar, this species thrives in wetland environments, often found in marshy areas and along the edges of freshwater habitats. The plant produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers that are typically green or reddish in color.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds in its native ecosystem.
