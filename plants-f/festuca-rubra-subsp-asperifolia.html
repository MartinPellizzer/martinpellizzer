Festuca rubra subsp. asperifolia

Festuca rubra subsp.

asperifolia, commonly known as rough leaf fescue, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It is characterized by its coarse, serrated leaf blades and tufted growth habit.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, woodland edges, and along stream banks. It produces dense, cylindrical inflorescences with spikelets that are often tinged with purple. Festuca rubra subsp.

asperifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
