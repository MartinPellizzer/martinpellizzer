Fagopyrum pilus

Fagopyrum pilus, commonly known as the hairy buckwheat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like rhubarb and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its upright stems covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a reddish or purple tinge on the underside.

Fagopyrum pilus produces small, greenish flowers in clusters, and its seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
