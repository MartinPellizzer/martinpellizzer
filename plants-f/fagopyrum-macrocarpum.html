Fagopyrum macrocarpum

Fagopyrum macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-seeded buckwheat, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar plants like sorrel and knotweed. The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flowers that appear in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a distinctive reddish underside.

The seeds are large and are often used in traditional medicine and as a food source in some cultures.
