Fimbristylis complanata

Fimbristylis complanata, commonly known as the flatleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and Australia.

It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats such as marshes, swamps, and rice fields. The plant has slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets and are usually green or brown in color.

Fimbristylis complanata plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
