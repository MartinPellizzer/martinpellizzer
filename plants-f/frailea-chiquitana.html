Frailea chiquitana

Frailea chiquitana, commonly known as the Chiquitania cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to northern Bolivia and southern Brazil.

It is a low-growing, leafless plant with a dense cluster of cylindrical stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers during the rainy season, which are clustered at the ends of the stems. It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

Frailea chiquitana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and resilience.
