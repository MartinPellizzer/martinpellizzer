Ficus ovatocordata

Ficus ovatocordata, commonly known as the broad-leafed fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and have a waxy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus ovatocordata is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
