Festuca ovina var. chiisanensis

Festuca ovina var. chiisanensis, commonly known as the Japanese sheep fescue, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically forms dense mats and is well adapted to rocky or sandy soils. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers.

This variety is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in challenging environments.
