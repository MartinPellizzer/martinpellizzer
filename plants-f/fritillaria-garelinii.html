Fritillaria garelinii

Fritillaria garelinii, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh climates, entering dormancy during winter and blooming in spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
