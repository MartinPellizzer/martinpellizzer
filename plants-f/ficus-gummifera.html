Ficus gummifera

Ficus gummifera, commonly known as the gum fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Ficus gummifera thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ability to adapt to various conditions makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
