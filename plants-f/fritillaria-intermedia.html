Fritillaria intermedia

Fritillaria intermedia, commonly known as the Turk's Cap lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Middle East.

It produces striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to deep red, often with intricate patterns. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is typically found in alpine and woodland habitats, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Fritillaria intermedia is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
