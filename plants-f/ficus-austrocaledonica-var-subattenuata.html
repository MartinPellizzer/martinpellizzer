Ficus austrocaledonica var. subattenuata

Ficus austrocaledonica var. subattenuata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ficus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant has a woody stem and can grow as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to several meters. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
