Fedia dentata var. gracilis

Fedia dentata var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender-toothed fedia, is a lesser-known species within the Fediaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves with finely serrated margins, which contribute to its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually greenish-white in color and are clustered in dense inflorescences.

The species is notable for its delicate appearance and is often found in temperate regions with cool, humid climates.
