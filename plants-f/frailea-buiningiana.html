Frailea buiningiana

Frailea buiningiana, commonly known as the Buning's lithops, is a species of flowering plant in the Aizoaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, specifically Namibia, where it grows in rocky, arid environments. The plant is characterized by its compact, fleshy, and often brightly colored leaves that resemble stones, a feature that helps it camouflage in its natural habitat. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for succulent enthusiasts.
