Falya leandriana

Falya leandriana, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is characterized by its rapid growth and ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments. Its botanical name honors the botanist Leandriana, who contributed significantly to the study of South American flora.

The vine is often used in ornamental gardening for its striking appearance and hardy nature.
