Ferocactus diguetii

Ferocactus diguetii, commonly known as the Mexican fire cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that are often covered in spines, which help to reduce water loss and deter herbivores. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking contrast to its rugged appearance. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Ferocactus diguetii is a popular choice for xeriscaping due to its low maintenance and aesthetic appeal.
