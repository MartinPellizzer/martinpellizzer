Fimbristylis puberula var. interior

Fimbristylis puberula var. interior, commonly known as the inland fringed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has grass-like leaves that are narrow and linear, with a dense, fibrous root system. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and bear small, greenish-white flowers.

This variant is distinguished by its hairy texture and is often found in disturbed or wetland environments.
