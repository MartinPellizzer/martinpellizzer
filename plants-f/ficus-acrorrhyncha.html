Ficus acrorrhyncha

Ficus acrorrhyncha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. It produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and often consumed by various animals.

Ficus acrorrhyncha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
