Filipendula glabra

Filipendula glabra, commonly known as white meadow sweet, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It belongs to the rose family (Rosaceae) and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and wetlands, thriving in well-drained to wet soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets, and it produces a distinctive, fragrant scent.

Filipendula glabra is also known for its use in traditional herbal medicine and as a source of honey.
