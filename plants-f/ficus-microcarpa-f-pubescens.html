Ficus microcarpa f. pubescens

Ficus microcarpa f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy fig tree, is a variant of the common fig tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. It produces small, edible figs that are typically green when mature.

This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique texture and foliage.
