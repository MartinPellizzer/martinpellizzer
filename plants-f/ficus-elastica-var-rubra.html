Ficus elastica var. rubra

Ficus elastica var. rubra, commonly known as the Red Rubber Plant, is a popular indoor plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically deep red to maroon in color, with a glossy surface. The plant is a member of the Moraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. It has a slow growth rate and can reach heights of up to 6 feet when grown indoors.

The leaves are often used as a decorative element due to their striking appearance and air-purifying qualities.
