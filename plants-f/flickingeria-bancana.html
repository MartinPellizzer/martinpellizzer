Flickingeria bancana

Flickingeria bancana, commonly known as the Brazilian starfruit, is a tropical plant native to Brazil.

It is a member of the Myrtaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Flickingeria bancana is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.
