Ficus hirta var. roxburghiana

Ficus hirta var. roxburghiana, commonly known as the hairy fig or Roxburgh's fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub with smooth, glossy, dark green leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.

The plant is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
