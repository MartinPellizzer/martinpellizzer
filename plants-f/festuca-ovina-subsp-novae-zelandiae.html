Festuca ovina subsp. novae-zelandiae

Festuca ovina subsp.

novae-zelandiae, commonly known as New Zealand sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, cool environments, such as wetlands and streambanks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
