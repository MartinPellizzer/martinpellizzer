Festuca inarticulata

Festuca inarticulata, commonly known as the slender fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is often found in alpine and arctic regions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

Festuca inarticulata plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
