Ficus eliadis

Ficus eliadis, commonly known as the Elieadis fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the island of Socotra, Yemen, and is characterized by its unique, spiny leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit, a small fig, is edible and contains a sweet, sticky pulp.

Ficus eliadis is adapted to arid conditions and thrives in rocky, well-drained soils.
