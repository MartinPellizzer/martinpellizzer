Felicia muricata subsp. cinerascens

Felicia muricata subsp.

cinerascens, commonly known as the cinerascens felicia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and hairy, silver-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for low-maintenance gardening.
