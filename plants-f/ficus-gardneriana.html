Ficus gardneriana

Ficus gardneriana, commonly known as the Mexican fig or Gardner's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and parts of Central America, where it typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

The plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
