Fritillaria grayana

Fritillaria grayana, commonly known as the Gray's fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winters, and it blooms in early spring.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in alpine ecosystems.
