Ficus oresbia

Ficus oresbia, commonly known as the Burmese fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Myanmar, Thailand, and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, edible fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring high humidity and indirect sunlight.

Ficus oresbia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
