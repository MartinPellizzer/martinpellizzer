Ficus burtt-davyi

Ficus burtt-davyi, commonly known as the Burtt-Davy fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit, a small fig-like structure, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and low maintenance requirements.
