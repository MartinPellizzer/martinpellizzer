Faramea ampla

Faramea ampla, commonly known as the broadleaf faramea, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Faramea ampla is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
