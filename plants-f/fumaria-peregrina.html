Fumaria peregrina

Fumaria peregrina, commonly known as the wandering jew, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fumariaceae.

It is native to southern Europe and western Asia, often found in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Fumaria peregrina is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread easily, making it both a popular ornamental plant and a potential weed.
