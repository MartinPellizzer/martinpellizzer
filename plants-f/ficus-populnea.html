Ficus populnea

Ficus populnea, commonly known as the Indian rubber tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and can grow up to 30 meters tall. The plant has a milky sap that is latex-rich, which was historically used to produce rubber. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ficus populnea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has significant economic importance in certain regions.
