Forsteronia corymbosa

Forsteronia corymbosa, commonly known as the sweetshrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in dense, rounded corymbs from late spring to early summer. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, gray-green upper surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, often found in woodland edges and stream banks.

Forsteronia corymbosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
