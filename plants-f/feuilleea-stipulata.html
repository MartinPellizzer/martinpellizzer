Feuilleea stipulata

Feuilleea stipulata, commonly known as the silver-leafed fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that are covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by spore-bearing capsules. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
