Festuca pauana

Festuca pauana, commonly known as the Chilean feathergrass, is a perennial grass native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and delicate, feathery seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.

Festuca pauana is also noted for its drought tolerance and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
