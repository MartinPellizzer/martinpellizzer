Festuca desvauxii var. ampla

Festuca desvauxii var. ampla, commonly known as the broadleaf fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous pale blue to purple florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.

Festuca desvauxii var. ampla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought and moderate foot traffic.
