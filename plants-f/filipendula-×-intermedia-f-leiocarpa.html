Filipendula × intermedia f. leiocarpa

Filipendula × intermedia f. leiocarpa, commonly known as the white meadow rue, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a preference for moist, well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade.

This cultivar is noted for its ornamental value and is often used in gardens for its elegant appearance.
