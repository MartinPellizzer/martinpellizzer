Festuca degenii

Festuca degenii, commonly known as Degen's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, wiry stems that often form dense, low-growing clumps. The leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that becomes more pronounced during the growing season. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spikelets that are typically borne on elongated, slender stalks.

This plant is well adapted to harsh environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and resilience to cold temperatures.
