Fragaria vesca var. semperflorens

Fragaria vesca var. semperflorens, commonly known as the everblooming strawberry, is a variant of the woodland strawberry.

It is characterized by its small, edible fruits that are typically red, though they may also appear in white or yellow. This plant is a perennial that thrives in cool climates and partial shade, often found in woodland areas. Its flowers are white with five petals and are followed by the small, aromatic berries.

The everblooming strawberry is valued for its ornamental qualities and mild flavor, making it a popular choice for gardens and culinary use.
