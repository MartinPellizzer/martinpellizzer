Fagara peruviana

Fagara peruviana, commonly known as the Peruvian prickly ash, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces yellowish-green fruits that are typically harvested when mature. Its bark and leaves contain bioactive compounds, making it of interest in traditional medicine.

Fagara peruviana thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.
