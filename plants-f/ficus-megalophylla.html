Ficus megalophylla

Ficus megalophylla, commonly known as the giant fig tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 inches in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus megalophylla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
