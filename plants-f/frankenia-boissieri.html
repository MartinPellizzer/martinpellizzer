Frankenia boissieri

Frankenia boissieri, commonly known as the coastal frankenia, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils and can tolerate saline conditions, making it a hardy species in coastal environments. Its compact growth habit and drought resistance make it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Frankenia boissieri plays a role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
