Ficus pedunculosa var. velutina

Ficus pedunculosa var. velutina, commonly known as the velvet fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green foliage with a velvety texture, giving it its common name. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife.

This plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance.
