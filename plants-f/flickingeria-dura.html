Flickingeria dura

Flickingeria dura, commonly known as the hardy groundcover plant, is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit with dense clusters of small, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by small, dry fruits. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value and resilience make it a popular choice for rock gardens and dryland landscapes.
