Ficus gossweileri

Ficus gossweileri, commonly known as the Congo fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and Southern Africa. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. It is often found growing in humid, shaded environments such as forests.

The fruit, which is a fig, is typically eaten by various animals and plays a role in seed dispersal.
