Ficus laciniata

Ficus laciniata, commonly known as the Benjamin fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, deeply lobed leaves that give it a distinctive, almost fern-like appearance. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers indirect sunlight. Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Ficus laciniata is also known for its adaptability, making it relatively easy to care for in a home setting.
