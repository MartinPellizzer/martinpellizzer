Ficus samarana

Ficus samarana, commonly known as the Samarana fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and smooth, gray bark. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically clustered in spherical syconia. It thrives in humid environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Ficus samarana is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
