Facchinia rupestris

Facchinia rupestris, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a low-growing succulent native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical leaves that are typically green with a bluish or purplish tinge. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by distinctive, hairy seed heads. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental foliage make it a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
