Flemingia guineensis

Flemingia guineensis, commonly known as African satinwood, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces flat, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. Flemingia guineensis is valued for its hard, durable wood and is often used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in secondary forests and along riverbanks.
