Furcraea macrophylla

Furcraea macrophylla, commonly known as the giant sword plant or elephant tree, is a striking succulent native to southern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tall, sword-like leaves that can grow up to 3 meters in length, often with a bluish-green hue and a waxy texture. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in terminal panicles, which bloom in the spring. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a standout in both indoor and outdoor gardens.
