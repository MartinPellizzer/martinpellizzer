Ficus saligna

Ficus saligna, commonly known as the silky fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus saligna is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
