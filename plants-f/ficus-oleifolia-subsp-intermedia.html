Ficus oleifolia subsp. intermedia

Ficus oleifolia subsp.

intermedia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a shrub or small tree native to parts of Africa and the Middle East. It is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs along the stems.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
