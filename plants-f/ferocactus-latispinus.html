Ferocactus latispinus

Ferocactus latispinus, commonly known as the broad-spined barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered with prominent, broad spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically clustered at the top of the stem. Its thick, succulent structure allows it to store water, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Ferocactus latispinus is a slow-growing plant that thrives in full sun and well-draining soil.
