Festuca howellii

Festuca howellii, commonly known as Howell's fescue, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in rocky, arid, and high-altitude environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in open habitats such as slopes and meadows.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing soil and providing forage for wildlife.
