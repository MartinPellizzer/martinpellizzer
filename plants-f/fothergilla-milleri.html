Fothergilla milleri

Fothergilla milleri, commonly known as the dwarf fothergilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Hamamelidaceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces clusters of white to pale yellow, bell-shaped flowers in early spring, which are followed by red berries in the fall. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic, with serrated edges, and turn vibrant shades of red and orange in autumn.

Fothergilla milleri is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
