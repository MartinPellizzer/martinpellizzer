Fraxinus steudelii

Fraxinus steudelii, commonly known as the Mexican ash, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically opposite and have a serrated margin. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in dry to moist forests.

The bark is smooth and grayish, with a distinctive flaky texture.
