Ficus fulva var. timorensis

Ficus fulva var. timorensis, commonly known as the Timor fig, is a species native to the island of Timor.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and heart-shaped leaves that are usually bright green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is also cultivated in some regions as a decorative plant.
