Ficus campbellii

Ficus campbellii, commonly known as the Benjamin fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are usually pointed at the tip. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by small, round fruits. It is widely cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ease of care.

Ficus campbellii prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
