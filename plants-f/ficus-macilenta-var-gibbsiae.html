Ficus macilenta var. gibbsiae

Ficus macilenta var. gibbsiae, commonly known as the Gibbs' fig, is a rare and distinctive species within the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically light green with a slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are usually green when mature, though they may turn yellow or red depending on the variety. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal.

Its unique foliage and low maintenance requirements make it a sought-after specimen among plant enthusiasts.
