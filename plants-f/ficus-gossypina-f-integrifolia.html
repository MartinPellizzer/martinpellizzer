Ficus gossypina f. integrifolia

Ficus gossypina f. integrifolia, commonly known as the Indian rubber plant or variegated rubber fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent veins and a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, greenish fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its air-purifying properties and ornamental value.

The species is also noted for its ability to adapt to indoor conditions with proper care.
