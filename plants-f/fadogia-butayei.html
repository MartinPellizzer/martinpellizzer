Fadogia butayei

Fadogia butayei, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces pod-like fruits containing flattened, kidney-shaped seeds. It is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and along riverbanks.

Botanically, it belongs to the Fabaceae family and is noted for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil.
