Festuca bosniaca

Festuca bosniaca, commonly known as Bosnian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Festuca bosniaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
