Fimbristylis spadicea var. drummondii

Fimbristylis spadicea var. drummondii, commonly known as Drummond's sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wet or damp environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of pale to brownish flowers.

Drummond's sedge is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for various wildlife species.
