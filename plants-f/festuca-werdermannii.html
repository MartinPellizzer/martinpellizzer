Festuca werdermannii

Festuca werdermannii, commonly known as Werdermann's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous slender spikelets, which are often tinged with purple or brown. Festuca werdermannii is well adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in gardens, particularly in regions with cool climates.
