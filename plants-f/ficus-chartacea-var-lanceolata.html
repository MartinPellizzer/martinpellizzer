Ficus chartacea var. lanceolata

Ficus chartacea var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lance-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
