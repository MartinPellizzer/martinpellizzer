Ficus pierrei

Ficus pierrei, commonly known as the African rubber fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are often glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, greenish fruits that mature to a reddish color. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Ficus pierrei is also valued for its use in traditional medicine in some African cultures.
