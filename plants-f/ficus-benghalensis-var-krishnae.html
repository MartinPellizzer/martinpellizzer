Ficus benghalensis var. krishnae

Ficus benghalensis var. krishnae, commonly known as the Krishna Fig, is a variant of the banyan tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and can grow into a sprawling tree with a broad canopy. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible figs.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in urban and rural settings.
