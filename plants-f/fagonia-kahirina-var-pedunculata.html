Fagonia kahirina var. pedunculata

Fagonia kahirina var. pedunculata, commonly known as the Desert Caper, is a shrub native to arid regions of North Africa.

It features small, waxy, green leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release seeds, aiding in its dispersal.

The plant is adapted to tolerate high temperatures and low rainfall, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
