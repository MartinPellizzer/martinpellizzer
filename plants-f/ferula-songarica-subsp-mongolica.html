Ferula songarica subsp. mongolica

Ferula songarica subsp.

mongolica, commonly known as Mongolian cow parsnip, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Mongolia and northern China. It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and often hollow stems.

The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow flowers arranged in compound umbels. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.
