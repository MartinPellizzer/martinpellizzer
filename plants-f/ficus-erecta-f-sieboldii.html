Ficus erecta f. sieboldii

Ficus erecta f. sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese banyan or siebold's banyan, is a unique fig species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature. It is a fast-growing vine that can develop aerial roots, allowing it to climb and form dense canopies.

Due to its adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
