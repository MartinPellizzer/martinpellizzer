Ficus puberula

Ficus puberula, commonly known as the velvet fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptical with a smooth, waxy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, round fruits.

This plant is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
