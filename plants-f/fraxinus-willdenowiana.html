Fraxinus willdenowiana

Fraxinus willdenowiana, commonly known as the white ash.

It is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States, characterized by its compound leaves and smooth, light-gray bark. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in bottomlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and ornamental contexts.
