Freycinetia arborea

Freycinetia arborea, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy dark green surface. Its flowers are showy, with five petals and a prominent yellow center, blooming in clusters.

Freycinetia arborea is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest undergrowth.
