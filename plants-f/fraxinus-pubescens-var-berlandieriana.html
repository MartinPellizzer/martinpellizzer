Fraxinus pubescens var. berlandieriana

Fraxinus pubescens var. berlandieriana, commonly known as the Berlandier's ash, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are hairy on the underside. The small, greenish-yellow flowers appear in clusters and are followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal. This species is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local ecosystems and a subject of interest in botanical studies.
