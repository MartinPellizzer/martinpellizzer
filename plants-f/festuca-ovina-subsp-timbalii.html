Festuca ovina subsp. timbalii

Festuca ovina subsp.

timbalii, commonly known as the Timbalii sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus. It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or gravelly soils.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous narrow, spikelet-like flowers. It is well adapted to cold climates and can tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
