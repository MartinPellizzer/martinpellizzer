Ferula plurivittata

Ferula plurivittata, commonly known as the multiband ferula, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and aromatic leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Ferula plurivittata thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh climatic conditions with its deep root system.
