Ficus tsiela

Ficus tsiela, commonly known as the Tsiela fig, is a species native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit, a fig-like structure, is typically green when mature and contains a sweet, edible pulp.

Ficus tsiela is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
