Fagara tetrasperma

Fagara tetrasperma, commonly known as the purpleleaf soursop, is a tropical shrub native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its dark green, often hairy leaves that turn purplish in color when mature. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are followed by green, spiny fruits resembling those of the soursop. Its botanical features include a woody stem, opposite leaf arrangement, and a distinctive floral structure with numerous stamens.

The plant is also noted for its potential medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
