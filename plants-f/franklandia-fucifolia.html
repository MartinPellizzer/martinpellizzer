Franklandia fucifolia

Franklandia fucifolia, commonly known as the sea hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Caribbean and the Gulf of Mexico.

It is characterized by its succulent, waxy leaves that are adapted to retain moisture in saline environments. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators. Its root system is robust, allowing it to thrive in sandy or rocky coastal soils.

Franklandia fucifolia plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for coastal wildlife.
