Ficus pachystemon

Ficus pachystemon, commonly known as the golden fig or the Indian rubber plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like India, Indonesia, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green with a slight waxy coating. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in traditional medicine.

Ficus pachystemon is also valued as an ornamental plant for its ability to thrive indoors with minimal care.
