Fagonia californica var. glutinosa

Fagonia californica var. glutinosa, commonly known as sticky fagonia, is a shrub native to coastal areas of California and Baja California.

It features woody stems covered in a sticky, resinous substance that gives it its common name. The plant has small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by small, orange-red berries. Its leaves are typically narrow and grayish-green, with a rough texture.

This variety is adapted to coastal climates and is often found in sandy or rocky soils.
