Fagus sylvatica f. tricolor

Fagus sylvatica f. tricolor, commonly known as the variegated European beech, is a unique cultivar of the European beech tree.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves, which display a combination of white, green, and gold patterns. This form is a natural mutation of the species, resulting in a distinct foliage appearance. The tree typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree, reaching heights of up to 15 meters.

It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
