Ficus tettensis

Ficus tettensis, commonly known as the African fig tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature, though they may change color depending on the variety. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
