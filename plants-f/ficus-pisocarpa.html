Ficus pisocarpa

Ficus pisocarpa, commonly known as the giant ficus or giant rubber tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by green, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature.

Ficus pisocarpa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
