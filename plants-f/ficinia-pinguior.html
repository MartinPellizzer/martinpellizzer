Ficinia pinguior

Ficinia pinguior, commonly known as the slender fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, elongated fronds.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. The plant features a rhizomatous root system that helps it anchor and absorb nutrients from the soil. Its leaves are usually bioluminescent, emitting a faint glow at night, which aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators.

This unique botanical trait makes it a fascinating subject for ecological and botanical study.
