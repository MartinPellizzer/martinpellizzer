Forsythia

Forsythia, commonly known as the golden bell, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Forsythia species are often used in landscaping for their vibrant floral display and hardy nature.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
