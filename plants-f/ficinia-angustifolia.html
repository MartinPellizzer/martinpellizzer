Ficinia angustifolia

Ficinia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flax, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, blue or purple flowers. It is often found in wetlands and is adapted to tolerate saline conditions.

Ficinia angustifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
