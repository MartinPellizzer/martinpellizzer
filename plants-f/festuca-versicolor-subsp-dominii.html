Festuca versicolor subsp. dominii

Festuca versicolor subsp.

dominii, commonly known as the purple meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces dense, fine-textured foliage.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks. Its flowers are small and spike-like, forming dense clusters that give the plant a distinctive appearance. Festuca versicolor subsp.

dominii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
