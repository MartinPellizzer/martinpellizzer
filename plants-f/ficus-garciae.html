Ficus garciae

Ficus garciae, commonly known as the Garcia fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance.
