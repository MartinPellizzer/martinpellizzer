Fallopia baldschuanica

Fallopia baldschuanica, commonly known as the Caucasian knotweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its distinctive rhombic leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its stems are often reddish or purple-hued, and it produces small, bead-like berries that are dispersed by animals.

Fallopia baldschuanica is also known for its aggressive growth habit, making it a potential invasive species in certain regions.
