Festuca nana

Festuca nana, commonly known as dwarf fescue, is a low-growing perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically reaches heights of 10 to 20 centimeters, with dense, tufted foliage that forms a compact mound. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow, flat, and often have a bluish-green color, which helps it retain moisture in dry conditions.

Festuca nana is well adapted to rocky or sandy soils and is often found in alpine or coastal environments.
