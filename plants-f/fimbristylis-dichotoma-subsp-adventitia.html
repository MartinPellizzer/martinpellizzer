Fimbristylis dichotoma subsp. adventitia

Fimbristylis dichotoma subsp.

adventitia, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It is a herbaceous perennial with slender, cylindrical stems that often grow in dense clusters.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous tiny flowers, typically arranged in two rows. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a fibrous texture, and it thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and swamps.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
