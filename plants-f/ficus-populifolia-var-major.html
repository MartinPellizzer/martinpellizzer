Ficus populifolia var. major

Ficus populifolia var. major, commonly known as the large-leaf fig, is a tropical plant characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for indoor cultivation and as a shade plant in gardens.

The species is also valued for its ability to purify air and its ornamental appeal.
