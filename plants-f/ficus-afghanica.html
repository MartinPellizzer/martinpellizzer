Ficus afghanica

Ficus afghanica, commonly known as the Afghan fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to regions of Central and South Asia, including Afghanistan, Pakistan, and parts of India. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and is often found in tropical and subtropical climates. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits.

Ficus afghanica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
