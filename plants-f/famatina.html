Famatina

Famatina, commonly known as the "devil's backbone," is a striking plant native to the arid regions of Argentina.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes nightshades, and is characterized by its unique, spiny stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces toxic, berry-like fruits that contain seeds, making it both a notable ornamental and a potentially hazardous species.

Its ability to thrive in harsh, dry environments highlights its adaptability and resilience in challenging ecological conditions.
