Fuchsia × adonia

Fuchsia × adonia, commonly known as the common fuchsia, is a hybrid plant belonging to the genus Fuchsia in the family Onagraceae.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy flowers that typically display vibrant shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant is native to South America and is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and glossy, while its stems are often woody at the base.

Fuchsia × adonia thrives in cool, humid conditions and is often used in hanging baskets or as a decorative shrub.
