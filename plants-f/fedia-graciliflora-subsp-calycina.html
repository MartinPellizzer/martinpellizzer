Fedia graciliflora subsp. calycina

Fedia graciliflora subsp.

calycina, commonly known as the slender-flowered fedia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America. It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, clustered flowers that bloom in shades of white to pale pink.

The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
