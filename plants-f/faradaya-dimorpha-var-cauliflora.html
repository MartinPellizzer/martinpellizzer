Faradaya dimorpha var. cauliflora

Faradaya dimorpha var. cauliflora, commonly known as the cauliflower orchid, is a unique tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, cauliflower-like inflorescence composed of numerous small, white to pale pink flowers. The plant belongs to the orchid family (Orchidaceae) and is known for its compact growth habit and ornamental value. It typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat.

The flowers bloom in clusters, creating a visually appealing display that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
