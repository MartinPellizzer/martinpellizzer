Ficus kingiana

Ficus kingiana, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a weeping or cascading manner. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, greenish-white fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its attractive appearance and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
