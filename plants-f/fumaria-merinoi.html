Fumaria merinoi

Fumaria merinoi, commonly known as the Merino poppy, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its delicate, spurred petals and slender, branching stems. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Fumaria merinoi thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
