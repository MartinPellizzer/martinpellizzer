Ficus megacarpa subvar. rotundicrassiusculifolia

Ficus megacarpa subvar. rotundicrassiusculifolia, commonly known as the round-leafed fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, and often dark green leaves that are rounded and robust in appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid environments with partial shade and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor settings.
