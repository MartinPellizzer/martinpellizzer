Ficus aurita

Ficus aurita, commonly known as the earleaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive ear-shaped leaves that are broad and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically harvested when they turn yellow or red. Ficus aurita thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its adaptability makes it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation in suitable climates.
