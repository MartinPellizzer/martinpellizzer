Festuca schischkinii

Festuca schischkinii, commonly known as Schischkin's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions.

Festuca schischkinii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and restoration projects.
