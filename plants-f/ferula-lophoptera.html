Ferula lophoptera

Ferula lophoptera, commonly known as the Kashmiri ferula, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery coating. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its roots are thick and fleshy, and they contain a resinous substance used in traditional medicine.

Ferula lophoptera is well adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine meadows.
