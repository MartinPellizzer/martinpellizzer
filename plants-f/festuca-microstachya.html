Festuca microstachya

Festuca microstachya, commonly known as slender fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are characteristic of the Festuca genus. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Its botanical features make it an important component of diverse grassland ecosystems.
