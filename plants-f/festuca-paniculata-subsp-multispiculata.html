Festuca paniculata subsp. multispiculata

Festuca paniculata subsp.

multispiculata, commonly known as the multispiculate bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and compact growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental and turfgrass applications.

The plant produces upright, panicle-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-like flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in meadows and grasslands.

Its resilience and aesthetic appeal make it a valued plant in horticulture and ecological restoration projects.
