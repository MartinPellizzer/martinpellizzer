Ferocactus flavovirens

Ferocactus flavovirens, commonly known as the yellow-green barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its greenish-yellow body. Its spines are typically short and cylindrical, providing protection from herbivores.

Ferocactus flavovirens is well adapted to extreme temperatures and low rainfall, making it a resilient member of desert ecosystems.
