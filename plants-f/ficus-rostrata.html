Ficus rostrata

Ficus rostrata, commonly known as the rostrata fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves that often have a reddish or purple tinge when exposed to bright light. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature. Ficus rostrata thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers indirect sunlight.

Its compact growth habit makes it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
