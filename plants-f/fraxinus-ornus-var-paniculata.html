Fraxinus ornus var. paniculata

Fraxinus ornus var. paniculata, commonly known as the common spindle or weeping spindle, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, weeping branches that droop gracefully, giving it a distinctive appearance. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles during late spring, which are followed by winged samaras that disperse its seeds. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a serrated margin, typically turning yellow in the fall.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively hardy in temperate climates.
