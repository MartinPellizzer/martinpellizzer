Ficus hispida var. badiostrigosa

Ficus hispida var. badiostrigosa, commonly known as the velvet fig or the black ficus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, velvety foliage that gives it its common name, with leaves that are typically glossy and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance.

Ficus hispida var. badiostrigosa is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
