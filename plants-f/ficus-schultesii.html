Ficus schultesii

Ficus schultesii, commonly known as the Schultes' fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and can grow as a tree or shrub, depending on environmental conditions. Its fruit, a small green fig, is edible and often consumed by local communities.

Ficus schultesii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
