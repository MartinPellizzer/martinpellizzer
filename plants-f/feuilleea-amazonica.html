Feuilleea amazonica

Feuilleea amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian leaf vine, is a tropical plant native to the dense rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in diameter. The plant thrives in high humidity and warm temperatures, often climbing trees or shrubs for support. Its leaves are thick and waxy, which helps retain moisture in the humid environment.

Feuilleea amazonica is also noted for its fragrant flowers, which bloom in clusters and attract various pollinators.
