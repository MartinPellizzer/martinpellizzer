Festuca × oleskensis

Festuca × oleskensis, commonly known as the Meadow Fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences containing small, two-lobed florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

Festuca × oleskensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in ecological restoration projects.
