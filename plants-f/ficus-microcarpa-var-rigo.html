Ficus microcarpa var. rigo

Ficus microcarpa var. rigo, commonly known as the banyan tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and can grow into a large, sprawling tree with a distinctive aerial root system. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and shade-providing capabilities. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The banyan tree is also notable for its ability to form a dense canopy, making it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
