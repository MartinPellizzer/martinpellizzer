Furera perennis

Furera perennis, commonly known as the everlasting flower, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense clusters of small, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white. The plant produces a unique type of seed known as a "pappus," which aids in wind dispersal. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Furera perennis is also valued for its historical use in herbal medicine.
