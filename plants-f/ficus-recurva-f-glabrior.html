Ficus recurva f. glabrior

Ficus recurva f. glabrior, commonly known as the smooth-leafed rubber fig, is a variant of the rubber fig plant characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves.

It is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant features opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by greenish-white fig fruits that are edible for some animals but not typically consumed by humans.

This variant is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
