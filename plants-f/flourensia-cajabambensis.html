Flourensia cajabambensis

Flourensia cajabambensis, commonly known as the Peruvian ironweed, is a shrub native to the Andean regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features silver-gray foliage with a fuzzy texture, and its flowers are typically yellow or orange in color. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky or sandy soils.

Flourensia cajabambensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local communities.
