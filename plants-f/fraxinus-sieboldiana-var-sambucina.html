Fraxinus sieboldiana var. sambucina

Fraxinus sieboldiana var. sambucina, commonly known as the Japanese white ash, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
