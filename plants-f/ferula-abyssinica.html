Ferula abyssinica

Ferula abyssinica, commonly known as Abyssinian cow parsnip, is a perennial herb native to Ethiopia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbel-shaped flowers. The plant produces a tall, hollow stem with finely divided leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often containing a strong, aromatic resin.

Ferula abyssinica is adapted to arid environments and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
