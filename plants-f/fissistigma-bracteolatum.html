Fissistigma bracteolatum

Fissistigma bracteolatum, commonly known as the golden star vine, is a climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, twining stems and small, star-shaped yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a smooth texture with a slightly glossy appearance. This species thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in forest undergrowth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and greenery in shaded areas.
