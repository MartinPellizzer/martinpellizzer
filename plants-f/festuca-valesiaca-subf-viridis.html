Festuca valesiaca subf. viridis

Festuca valesiaca subf.

viridis, commonly known as the green festuca, is a grass species native to alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in ecological restoration projects in mountainous areas.
