Ficus macrocarpa

Ficus macrocarpa, commonly known as the Moreton Bay fig, is a large evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and can grow to impressive heights, often reaching over 30 meters. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are an important food source for native wildlife. Its extensive root system and ability to thrive in various soil types make it a resilient and adaptable species.

Ficus macrocarpa is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping.
