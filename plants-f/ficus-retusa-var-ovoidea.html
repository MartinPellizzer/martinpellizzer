Ficus retusa var. ovoidea

Ficus retusa var. ovoidea, commonly known as the eggplant fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fig fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus retusa var. ovoidea is also popular as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
