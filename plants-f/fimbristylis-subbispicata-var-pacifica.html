Fimbristylis subbispicata var. pacifica

Fimbristylis subbispicata var. pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to coastal regions of New Zealand.

It typically grows in moist, sandy or gravelly soils along shorelines and in dune environments. The plant has narrow, linear leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. It is adapted to salt spray and wind exposure, making it a hardy species in coastal ecosystems.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
