Fragaria × ananassa nothosubsp.

ananassa, commonly known as the cultivated strawberry, is a hybrid species originating from the cross between *Fragaria virginiana* and *Fragaria chiloensis*. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are heart-shaped and have a serrated margin.

The plant produces fragrant, red berries that are typically clustered in inflorescences. These berries are rich in vitamins and antioxidants, making them a popular fruit in agriculture and cuisine.

Botanically, it is classified as a perennial herb with a fibrous root system and a growing season that varies by climate.
