Ficus hispidulosa

Ficus hispidulosa, commonly known as the woolly fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are covered in fine, fuzzy hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits.

Ficus hispidulosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
