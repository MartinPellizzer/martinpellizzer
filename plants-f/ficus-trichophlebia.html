Ficus trichophlebia

Ficus trichophlebia, commonly known as the hairy-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus trichophlebia is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
