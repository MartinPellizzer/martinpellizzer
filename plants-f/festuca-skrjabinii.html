Festuca skrjabinii

Festuca skrjabinii, commonly known as Skrjabin's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It thrives in high-altitude environments, often growing in alpine meadows and rocky soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to cold climates and can withstand harsh winter conditions.

Due to its ecological significance, it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity in its native habitat.
