Ficus glochidiifolia

Ficus glochidiifolia, commonly known as the spiny fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in small, sharp spines. The plant produces small, green fig fruits that are typically eaten by birds and other animals. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is popular among plant enthusiasts.
