Ficinia striata

Ficinia striata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf flax, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that have a distinctive striated appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spikes, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Ficinia striata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and subalpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
