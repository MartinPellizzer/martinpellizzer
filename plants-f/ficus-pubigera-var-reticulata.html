Ficus pubigera var. reticulata

Ficus pubigera var. reticulata, commonly known as the reticulated fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy leaves that have a reticulated or net-like pattern of veins. The leaves are typically large, elongated, and have a smooth, glossy surface. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers indirect sunlight.

This plant is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ability to adapt to indoor conditions.
