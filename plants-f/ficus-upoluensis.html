Ficus upoluensis

Ficus upoluensis, commonly known as the Upolu ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the island of Upolu in Samoa and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an evergreen tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Its fruit is a small, smooth, greenish-yellow fig that matures into a reddish color.

Ficus upoluensis plays an important role in the local ecosystem and is valued for its ornamental and possibly medicinal properties.
