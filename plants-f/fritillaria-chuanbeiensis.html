Fritillaria chuanbeiensis

Fritillaria chuanbeiensis, commonly known as the Chuanbei fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to China.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, often bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soil and full sun. Its bulbs are underground storage organs that support the plant through dormancy periods.

The flowers, which can be white, yellow, or orange, are often adorned with intricate patterns and are a notable feature of the species.
