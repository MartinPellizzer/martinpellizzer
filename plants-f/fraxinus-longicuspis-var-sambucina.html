Fraxinus longicuspis var. sambucina

Fraxinus longicuspis var. sambucina, commonly known as the black walnut or sambucina walnut, is a variant of the American ash tree.

It is characterized by its dark, ridged bark and its distinctive, elongated, spiky fruits that mature in the fall. The leaves are typically alternate, simple, and have a serrated margin. This plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils.

It is valued for its hard, durable wood and its ecological role in supporting wildlife.
