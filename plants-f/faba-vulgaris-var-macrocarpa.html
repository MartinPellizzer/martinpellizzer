Faba vulgaris var. macrocarpa

Faba vulgaris var. macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant broad bean, is a variant of the broad bean plant characterized by its large, robust pods.

It is a legume native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features compound leaves with three leaflets and produces showy, pale yellow flowers. Its seeds are edible and have been cultivated for their high protein content.

This variety is valued both for its nutritional benefits and its ornamental appeal in gardens.
