Fuchsia regia var. alpestris

Fuchsia regia var. alpestris, commonly known as the alpine fuchsia, is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of showy, violet-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found at high altitudes where temperatures are lower. Its leaves are ovate and covered in a fine, silvery down, which helps protect it from harsh mountain conditions.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with specific climate requirements.
