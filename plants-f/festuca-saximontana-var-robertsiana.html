Festuca saximontana var. robertsiana

Festuca saximontana var. robertsiana, commonly known as Roberts' mountain fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in high-altitude environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

This variety is often used in restoration projects due to its ecological resilience and ability to stabilize soil in rugged terrain.
