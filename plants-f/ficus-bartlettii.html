Ficus bartlettii

Ficus bartlettii, commonly known as the Bartlett's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and can grow as a small tree or shrub. It produces small, fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature.

Ficus bartlettii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable environments.
