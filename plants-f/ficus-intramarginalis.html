Ficus intramarginalis

Ficus intramarginalis, commonly known as the marginal fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in tropical climates and requires high humidity and indirect sunlight.

Ficus intramarginalis is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
