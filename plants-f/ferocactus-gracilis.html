Ferocactus gracilis

Ferocactus gracilis, commonly known as the slender hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in prominent, radial spines, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are typically borne at the ends of the stems. Its succulent nature allows it to store water efficiently, making it well adapted to desert environments.

Ferocactus gracilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique form and hardy nature.
