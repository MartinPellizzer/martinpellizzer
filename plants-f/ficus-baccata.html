Ficus baccata

Ficus baccata, commonly known as the Indian rubber fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, green fruits that turn yellow when ripe. Its roots are known for their ability to absorb water efficiently, making it adaptable to moist environments.

Ficus baccata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
