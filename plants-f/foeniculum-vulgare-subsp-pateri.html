Foeniculum vulgare subsp. pateri

Foeniculum vulgare subsp.

pateri, commonly known as the Mexican fennel, is a perennial herb native to Mexico and parts of Central America. It is characterized by its feathery, aromatic foliage and yellow flowers that bloom in umbels.

The plant produces small, oval seeds that are used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in dry, rocky habitats.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for both traditional medicine and ornamental gardening.
