Filipendula camtschatica f. typica

Filipendula camtschatica f. typica, commonly known as the Kamchatka meadow sweet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the coastal regions of the Russian Far East.

It features a tall, slender stem that can grow up to 1.5 meters in height, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers arranged in spherical inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated edges, and are arranged alternately along the stem.

Filipendula camtschatica f. typica thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along riverbanks.
