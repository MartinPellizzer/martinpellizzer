Fragaria virginiana var. grayana

Fragaria virginiana var. grayana, commonly known as gray's strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of white flowers. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are similar in appearance to common strawberries. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and native plant gardens.
