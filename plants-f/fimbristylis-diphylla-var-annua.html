Fimbristylis diphylla var. annua

Fimbristylis diphylla var. annua, commonly known as the annual flat sedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered spikelets that bear tiny, scale-like leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. It is native to regions of Australia and is often found in coastal areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
