Fritillaria nana

Fritillaria nana, commonly known as the dwarf fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped, often reddish or orange flowers in early spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in well-drained, rocky soils. It is a member of the Liliaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Fritillaria nana is typically found at elevations above 2,000 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions.
