Ficus toka

Ficus toka, commonly known as the Toka fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are often consumed by both humans and animals. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus toka is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant in tropical regions.
