Ferdinandusa boomii

Ferdinandusa boomii, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical tree native to parts of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it a vibrant and showy appearance. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets that provide a lush green canopy. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also notable for its ability to grow quickly, making it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical regions.
