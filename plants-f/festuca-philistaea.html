Festuca philistaea

Festuca philistaea, commonly known as Philistine grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, coastal environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in open habitats such as cliffs, dunes, and roadsides.

Its botanical characteristics make it a hardy plant with ecological significance in its native range.
