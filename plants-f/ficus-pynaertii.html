Ficus pynaertii

Ficus pynaertii, commonly known as the Pynaert's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its distinctive, palmate leaves that are often lobed and have a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature, and it thrives in humid, tropical environments. Ficus pynaertii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.

Its growth habit includes both upright and trailing forms, making it suitable for various landscaping applications.
