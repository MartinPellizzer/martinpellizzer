Fimbristylis celebica

Fimbristylis celebica, commonly known as the Celebes sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along waterlogged soils, thriving in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often wind-pollinated. Its flowering period varies depending on the region but generally occurs during the wet season.

Fimbristylis celebica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
