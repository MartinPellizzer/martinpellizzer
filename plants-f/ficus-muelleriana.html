Ficus muelleriana

Ficus muelleriana, commonly known as the Mueller's fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.

Ficus muelleriana prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
