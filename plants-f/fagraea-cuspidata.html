Fagraea cuspidata

Fagraea cuspidata, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features dense, leathery leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a reddish or purple tinge when young. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
