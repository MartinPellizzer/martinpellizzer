Ficus bibracteata

Ficus bibracteata, commonly known as the white sycamore or sacred fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, inedible fruits that contain a single seed. Its roots can grow quite deep, making it adaptable to various soil types.

Ficus bibracteata is also known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
