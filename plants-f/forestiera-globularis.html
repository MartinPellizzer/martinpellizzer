Forestiera globularis

Forestiera globularis, commonly known as the globe forestiera, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its spherical, clustered growth habit and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in late spring, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its compact form and seasonal color changes make it a popular choice for landscape design.
