Fritillaria cirrhosa var. ecirrhosa

Fritillaria cirrhosa var. ecirrhosa, commonly known as the Tibetan fritillary, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers, which are typically purple or red in color. The plant grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it produces a single stem that bears the showy flowers.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
