Felicia tenella subsp. longifolia

Felicia tenella subsp.

longifolia, commonly known as the longleaf felicia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers.

The leaves are typically narrow and linear, often with a slight blue-gray hue. This subspecies thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
