Ficus carica var. spiritus-sancti

Ficus carica var. spiritus-sancti, commonly known as the Holy Spirit fig, is a rare variant of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, featuring a distinctive white variegation pattern. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in warm, temperate climates. Its fruit, while edible, is typically smaller and less sweet compared to standard fig varieties.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
