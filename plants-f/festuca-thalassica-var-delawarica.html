Festuca thalassica var. delawarica

Festuca thalassica var. delawarica, commonly known as the Delawarica fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green, narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils, often found in woodland edges and wet meadows. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
