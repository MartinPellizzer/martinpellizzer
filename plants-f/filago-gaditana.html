Filago gaditana

Filago gaditana, commonly known as the Spanish dandelion, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and northern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces yellow flowers that develop into fluffy, white seed heads, similar to those of the common dandelion. It thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found along roadsides and in dry grasslands.

Filago gaditana is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
