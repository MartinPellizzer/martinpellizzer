Freesia gentilis

Freesia gentilis, commonly known as the common freesia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in spring and summer, producing colors such as white, yellow, orange, and pink. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Freesia gentilis is often cultivated in gardens and as a cut flower due to its ornamental value and pleasant scent.
