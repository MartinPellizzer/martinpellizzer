Frangula breedlovei

Frangula breedlovei, commonly known as the Texas buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. The plant produces small, red berries that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Frangula breedlovei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in native plant communities.
