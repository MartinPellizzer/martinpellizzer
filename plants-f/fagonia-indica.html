Fagonia indica

Fagonia indica, commonly known as the Indian fagonia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Zygophyllaceae.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions, particularly in the Indian subcontinent and parts of Asia. The plant is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and compound leaves with spiny margins. It is adapted to withstand drought conditions and is often found in rocky or sandy soils.

Fagonia indica is also noted for its use in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
