Flourensia pulcherrima

Flourensia pulcherrima, commonly known as the golden crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant is known for its showy, yellow-orange ray flowers that surround a central cluster of small, tubular florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Its botanical features include hairy stems, lance-shaped leaves, and a long flowering period from spring to fall.
