Festuca johnsonii

Festuca johnsonii, commonly known as Johnson's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats, including alpine and desert environments. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Festuca johnsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing soil in arid regions.
