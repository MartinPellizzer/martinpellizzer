Feuilleea calycina

Feuilleea calycina, commonly known as the golden bell vine, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces clusters of small, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. Feuilleea calycina thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
