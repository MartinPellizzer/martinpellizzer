Fatoua villosa

Fatoua villosa, commonly known as the silky fatoua, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed areas such as roadsides and fields.

Fatoua villosa is known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
