Fuchsia pendula terminalis

Fuchsia pendula terminalis, commonly known as the hanging fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous clusters of vibrant, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink, purple, and red. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often covered in fine, soft hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and hanging baskets.

The flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological value.
