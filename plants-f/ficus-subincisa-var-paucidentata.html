Ficus subincisa var. paucidentata

Ficus subincisa var. paucidentata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
