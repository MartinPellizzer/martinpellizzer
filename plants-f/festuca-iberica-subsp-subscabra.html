Festuca iberica subsp. subscabra

Festuca iberica subsp.

subscabra, commonly known as the Iberian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in calcareous soils and is often found in open habitats such as rocky slopes and grasslands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous minute florets. Festuca iberica subsp.

subscabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
