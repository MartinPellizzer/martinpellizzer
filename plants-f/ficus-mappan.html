Ficus mappan

Ficus mappan, commonly known as the Mappan fig, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and often have a waxy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
