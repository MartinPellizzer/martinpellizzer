Fargesia fungosa

Fargesia fungosa, commonly known as the silver grass, is a perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and silvery-gray foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers in early summer, which are clustered in panicles. Fargesia fungosa is well-suited for shaded or partially shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in cooler climates.
