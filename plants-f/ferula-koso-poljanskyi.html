Ferula koso-poljanskyi

Ferula koso-poljanskyi, commonly known as the Koso-Poljansky ferula, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes plants like carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in umbel-like clusters, typical of the family. Its roots are fibrous and can be quite thick, often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Ferula koso-poljanskyi thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to harsh climatic conditions.
