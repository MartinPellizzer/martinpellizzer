Filix-mas augescens var. puberula

Filix-mas augescens var. puberula, commonly known as the pubescent fern, is a variant of the climbing fern characterized by its soft, hairy texture on the fronds.

This plant belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. Its fronds are typically elongated and can grow quite large, often reaching several feet in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, making it a common sight in humid forests.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in botanical studies.
