Ficus gemina

Ficus gemina, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by spherical, greenish fruits. It is often cultivated as a ground cover or hanging plant due to its ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions.

Ficus gemina is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and adaptability to indoor environments.
