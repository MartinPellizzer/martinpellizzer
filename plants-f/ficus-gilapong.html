Ficus gilapong

Ficus gilapong, commonly known as the Philippine fig tree, is a tropical plant native to the Philippines.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ficus gilapong is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
