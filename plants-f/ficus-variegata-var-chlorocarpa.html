Ficus variegata var. chlorocarpa

Ficus variegata var. chlorocarpa, commonly known as the variegated fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display a mix of green, white, and sometimes yellow hues. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its aesthetic appeal. The leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth texture, while the plant produces small, inedible fig-like fruits.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.
