Festuca rubra subsp. limosa

Festuca rubra subsp.

limosa, commonly known as red fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often reddish stems.

The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous florets, which are often pale green to brown in color.

This subspecies is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often used in horticulture and landscaping due to its ornamental value and hardiness.
