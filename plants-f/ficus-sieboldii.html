Ficus sieboldii

Ficus sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese rubber fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Japan and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, spherical fruits that are yellow when ripe. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and attractive appearance.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
