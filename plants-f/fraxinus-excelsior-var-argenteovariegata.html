Fraxinus excelsior var. argenteovariegata

Fraxinus excelsior var. argenteovariegata, commonly known as the silver-leaved ash, is a variant of the common ash tree.

It is characterized by its striking white-variegated leaves that contrast with its dark green veins. The leaves are typically oval-shaped and have a serrated margin. This plant is native to Europe and thrives in temperate climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique foliage.
