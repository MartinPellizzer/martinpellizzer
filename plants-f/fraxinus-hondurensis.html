Fraxinus hondurensis

Fraxinus hondurensis, commonly known as Honduran ash, is a deciduous tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
