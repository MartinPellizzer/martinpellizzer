Filago pyramidata subsp. fuscescens

Filago pyramidata subsp.

fuscescens, commonly known as the dark red sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in fine hairs, while its flowers are small and yellowish-green.

The subspecies is distinguished by its darker, more reddish coloration compared to other forms of Filago pyramidata.
