Fagopyrum esculentum var. emarginatum

Fagopyrum esculentum var. emarginatum, commonly known as the Indian spinach or broadleaf buckwheat, is a variant of the buckwheat plant native to the Himalayas.

It is an annual herbaceous plant characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant is cultivated for its edible leaves, which are rich in nutrients such as iron and calcium. It thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, often growing in mountainous regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable crop in both traditional and modern agricultural practices.
