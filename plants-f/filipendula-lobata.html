Filipendula lobata

Filipendula lobata, commonly known as the meadow sweet, is a flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in wet meadows, stream banks, and other moist habitats. Filipendula lobata produces small, reddish-brown fruits called achenes, which are dispersed by wind.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
