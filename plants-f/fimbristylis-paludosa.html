Fimbristylis paludosa

Fimbristylis paludosa, commonly known as the marsh sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to wetland habitats in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically pale green to brown in color. It thrives in moist to waterlogged soils and is often found in marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Fimbristylis paludosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
