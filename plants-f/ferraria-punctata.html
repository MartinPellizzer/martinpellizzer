Ferraria punctata

Ferraria punctata, commonly known as the spotted bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features erect, branching stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers with a distinctive spotted appearance. The flowers are typically white or pale blue, with yellow markings, and bloom from late spring to early summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy and arranged alternately along the stem.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and rocky areas.
