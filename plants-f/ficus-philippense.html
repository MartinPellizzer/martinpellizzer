Ficus philippense

Ficus philippense, commonly known as the Philippine fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into fig-like fruits, which are edible and often used in local cuisine. Ficus philippense thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Its ability to adapt to various soil types makes it a resilient and versatile species in both natural and cultivated settings.
