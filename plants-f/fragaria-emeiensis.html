Fragaria emeiensis

Fragaria emeiensis, commonly known as the Emei mountain strawberry, is a rare species of wild strawberry native to China.

It is characterized by its small, edible berries that are typically red in color and have a sweet, fragrant flavor. The plant grows in mountainous regions and thrives in cool, moist climates. It has a creeping habit with hairy leaves and produces white flowers with five petals.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a valuable species for botanical research and conservation efforts.
