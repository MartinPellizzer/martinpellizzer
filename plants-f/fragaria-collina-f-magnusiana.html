Fragaria collina f. magnusiana

Fragaria collina f. magnusiana, commonly known as the mountain strawberry, is a botanical variant of the woodland strawberry.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its low-growing habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in alpine or subalpine regions.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species in ecological studies and horticulture.
