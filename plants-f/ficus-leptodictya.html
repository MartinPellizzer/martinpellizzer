Ficus leptodictya

Ficus leptodictya, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as Indonesia, Malaysia, and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called syconia.

Ficus leptodictya is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
