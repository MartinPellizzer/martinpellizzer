Ficus carica var. garidelii

Ficus carica var. garidelii, commonly known as the Persian fig or Persian white fig, is a cultivar of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its small, white, and often hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, edible figs that are typically harvested when they are still unripe, as they ripen quickly. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and its unique fruit characteristics.
