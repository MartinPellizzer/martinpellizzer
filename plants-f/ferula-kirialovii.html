Ferula kirialovii

Ferula kirialovii, commonly known as Kirialov's ferula, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in a dense, spike-like inflorescence, which blooms during the summer months. Its roots are thick and fleshy, adapted to store water in its arid habitat.

Ferula kirialovii is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
