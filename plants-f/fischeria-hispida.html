Fischeria hispida

Fischeria hispida, commonly known as the rough fischeria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Acanthaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking spikes of tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated in arid and semi-arid regions.
