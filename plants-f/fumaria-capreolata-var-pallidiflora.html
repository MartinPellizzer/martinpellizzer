Fumaria capreolata var. pallidiflora

Fumaria capreolata var. pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered cuckoo's-beak, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fumariaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces elongated, slender pods that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows and along roadsides.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and relatively rare species within its native range.
