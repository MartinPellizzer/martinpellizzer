Frangula austrosinensis

Frangula austrosinensis, commonly known as Chinese buckthorn, is a shrub native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its dark green, ovate leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are rich in anthraquinones, which are used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ornamental value and medicinal properties make it a notable species in horticulture and phytotherapy.
