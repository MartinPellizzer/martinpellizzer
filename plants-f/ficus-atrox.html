Ficus atrox

Ficus atrox, commonly known as the black ficus, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Moraceae family, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. Its fruit, a small fig, is edible and often consumed by local wildlife.

Ficus atrox is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
