Fuirena cuspidata

Fuirena cuspidata, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers, which are often adorned with distinctive spurred petals. The plant typically grows in alpine meadows and rocky habitats at high altitudes. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

Fuirena cuspidata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
