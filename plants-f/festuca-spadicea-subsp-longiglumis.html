Festuca spadicea subsp. longiglumis

Festuca spadicea subsp.

longiglumis, commonly known as the long glume fescue, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often tinged with purple or reddish hues, adding ornamental value. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for ornamental and ecological gardens.

This subspecies is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
