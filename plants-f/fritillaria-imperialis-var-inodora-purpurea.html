Fritillaria imperialis var. inodora-purpurea

Fritillaria imperialis var. inodora-purpurea, commonly known as the purple crown imperial, is a striking bulbous plant native to the eastern Mediterranean.

It features tall, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, purple or reddish-purple flowers, which are often adorned with intricate patterns. The plant's leaves are narrow and grass-like, emerging from a cluster of fleshy, white or pale yellow bulbs. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, preferring cooler climates.

Its ornamental value lies in its vibrant, showy blooms that appear in late spring to early summer.
