Floscopa

Floscopa, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. Its leaves are typically gray-green, covered in fine hairs, and it grows in open, sunny habitats.

Floscopa is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
