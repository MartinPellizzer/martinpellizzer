Ficus deliciosa

Ficus deliciosa, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. The tree produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by the edible, starchy fruit that resembles a bread-like substance when ripe. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in various soil types and climates.

Ficus deliciosa is widely cultivated for both its fruit and ornamental value.
