Ficus brasiliensis

Ficus brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazil fig or the sacred fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, gray bark. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance.

Ficus brasiliensis is also culturally significant in various indigenous communities.
