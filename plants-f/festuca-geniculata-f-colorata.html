Festuca geniculata f. colorata

Festuca geniculata f. colorata, commonly known as the colorful bentgrass, is a variant of the bentgrass species native to the mountainous regions of North America.

This plant is characterized by its striking, vividly colored foliage, which can range from deep reds to purples and blues, depending on environmental conditions. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. The inflorescence consists of dense, cylindrical spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Its ornamental value makes it a desirable plant for specialty gardens and conservation efforts.
