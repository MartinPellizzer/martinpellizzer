Festuca uralensis

Festuca uralensis, commonly known as Siberian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of Eurasia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and arctic tundra regions. Festuca uralensis is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including low temperatures and poor soil nutrients.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
