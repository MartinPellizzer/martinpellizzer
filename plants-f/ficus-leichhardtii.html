Ficus leichhardtii

Ficus leichhardtii, commonly known as the Leichhardt's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that develop into edible figs, which are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in rainforests or along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
