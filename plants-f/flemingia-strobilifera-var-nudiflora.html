Flemingia strobilifera var. nudiflora

Flemingia strobilifera var. nudiflora, commonly known as the naked-flowered flemingia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are often borne on short pedicels, giving it a delicate appearance. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, winged seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
