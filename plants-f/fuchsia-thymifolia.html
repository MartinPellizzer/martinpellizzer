Fuchsia thymifolia

Fuchsia thymifolia, commonly known as the thyme-leaved fuchsia, is a member of the Onagraceae family.

It is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves that resemble those of thyme, giving it its common name. It produces showy, pendulous flowers in shades of pink and purple, which bloom in late summer.

Fuchsia thymifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
