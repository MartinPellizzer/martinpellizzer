Fumaria rupestris subsp. micranthifolia

Fumaria rupestris subsp.

micranthifolia, commonly known as the rock fumewort, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features slender, upright stems that are often hairy, and its leaves are lance-shaped with a slightly hairy surface.

The flowers are small, yellowish-green, and borne in clusters at the leaf axils, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

This subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate foliage and unique flowering pattern.
