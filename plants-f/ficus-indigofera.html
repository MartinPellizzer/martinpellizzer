Ficus indigofera

Ficus indigofera, commonly known as the Indian rubber tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by fig-like fruits containing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus indigofera is also valued for its ability to purify the air and is often used as a decorative indoor plant.
