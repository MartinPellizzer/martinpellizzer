Fabiana bryoides

Fabiana bryoides, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming perennial with narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Fabiana bryoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
