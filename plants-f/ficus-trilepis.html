Ficus trilepis

Ficus trilepis, commonly known as the three-lobed fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually three-lobed, giving it its common name. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature.

Ficus trilepis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
