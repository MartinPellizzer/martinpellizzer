Felicia whitehillensis

Felicia whitehillensis, commonly known as the Whitehill's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its small, white to pale yellow flowers bloom in the summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Felicia whitehillensis is often found in desert scrub and grassland habitats, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
