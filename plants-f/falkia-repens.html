Falkia repens

Falkia repens, commonly known as the creeping falkia, is a low-growing perennial plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its trailing stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Falkia repens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense mats.
