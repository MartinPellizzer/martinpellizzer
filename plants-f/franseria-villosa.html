Franseria villosa

Franseria villosa, commonly known as the hairy franseria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Franseria villosa is often grown in gardens for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
