Ficus melinocarpa f. glabrior

Ficus melinocarpa f. glabrior, commonly known as the smooth-leaved fig, is a variant of the melinocarpa fig species.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous foliage, which distinguishes it from other fig varieties. The plant is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree, native to regions in Southeast Asia. It produces small, edible figs that are typically consumed fresh or used in local cuisines.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental and edible plant in horticultural settings.
