Festuca unilateralis var. aristata

Festuca unilateralis var. aristata, commonly known as the aristate fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its hardiness and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
