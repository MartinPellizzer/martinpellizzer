Ficus ynesina

Ficus ynesina, commonly known as the Ynesina fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are usually heart-shaped or ovate in form. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically clustered in a spherical structure.

Ficus ynesina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.
