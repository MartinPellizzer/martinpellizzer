Ficus mysorensis var. pubescens

Ficus mysorensis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy fig or pubescent fig, is a tropical plant native to southern India.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a leathery texture, while the stems are often slightly swollen at the nodes.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
