Fumaria bicalcarata

Fumaria bicalcarata, commonly known as the two-lobed fumitory, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its fruit is a long, slender capsule that splits open to release tiny seeds.

Fumaria bicalcarata is often found in disturbed soils and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value.
