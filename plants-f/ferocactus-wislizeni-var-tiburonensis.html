Ferocactus wislizeni var. tiburonensis

Ferocactus wislizeni var. tiburonensis, commonly known as the Tiburon prickly pear, is a cactus native to the coastal regions of Baja California, Mexico.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems covered in spines and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has a robust root system that allows it to thrive in arid and saline environments. Its fruit, a large, fleshy pear, is edible and often consumed by both humans and wildlife.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
