Festuca elatior

Festuca elatior, commonly known as tall fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, erect growth habit and coarse, blue-green leaves. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, wind-pollinated flowers. It is highly adaptable and thrives in a variety of soil types, making it a common ornamental and turfgrass species.

Festuca elatior is also known for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
