Fritillaria baisunensis

Fritillaria baisunensis, commonly known as the Baisun fritillary, is a bulbous plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant produces a single, upright stem with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are adapted to store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments.

Fritillaria baisunensis is often found in rocky or sandy soils at high elevations.
