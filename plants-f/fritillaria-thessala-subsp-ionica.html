Fritillaria thessala subsp. ionica

Fritillaria thessala subsp.

ionica, commonly known as the Ionian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Aegean region of Greece. It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color, with a distinctive pattern of darker markings.

The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in well-drained, calcareous soils. It blooms in early spring and is valued for its ornamental appeal in rock gardens and alpine settings.

The subspecies is closely related to other members of the Fritillaria genus, sharing similar botanical characteristics.
