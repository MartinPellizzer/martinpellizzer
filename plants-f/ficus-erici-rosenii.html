Ficus erici-rosenii

Ficus erici-rosenii, commonly known as the Eric Rosenii Fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green in color. It produces small, fig-like fruits that are enclosed within a syconium, a unique structure typical of fig species.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in indoor environments.
