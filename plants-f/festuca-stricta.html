Festuca stricta

Festuca stricta, commonly known as rigid fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, pastures, and rocky soils. Festuca stricta produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
