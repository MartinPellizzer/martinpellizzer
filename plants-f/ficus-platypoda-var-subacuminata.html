Ficus platypoda var. subacuminata

Ficus platypoda var. subacuminata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are edible and attract various wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
