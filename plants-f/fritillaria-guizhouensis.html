Fritillaria guizhouensis

Fritillaria guizhouensis, commonly known as the Guizhou fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Guizhou Province in China.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. Its bulbs are adapted to store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in its alpine habitat.

Fritillaria guizhouensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
