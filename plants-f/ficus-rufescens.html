Ficus rufescens

Ficus rufescens, commonly known as the red-fig or red-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often turn reddish in color under certain environmental conditions. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by round, greenish fruits.

Ficus rufescens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
