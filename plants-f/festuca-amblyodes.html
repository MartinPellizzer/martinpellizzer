Festuca amblyodes

Festuca amblyodes, commonly known as narrowleaf fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. Festuca amblyodes is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
