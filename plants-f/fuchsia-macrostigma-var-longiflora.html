Fuchsia macrostigma var. longiflora

Fuchsia macrostigma var. longiflora, commonly known as the long-flowered fuchsia, is a hybrid species native to South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, tubular flowers that range in color from deep purple to bright red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its foliage consists of ovate, glossy leaves that are dark green in color.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
