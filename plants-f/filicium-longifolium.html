Filicium longifolium

Filicium longifolium, commonly known as the long-leaf fern, is a species of fern characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped fronds.

It belongs to the family Filicales and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often near streams or in dense forests. Its fronds are usually deeply divided, with a feathery appearance that contributes to its ornamental value.

Filicium longifolium reproduces via spores and plays a role in maintaining soil stability in its natural habitat.
