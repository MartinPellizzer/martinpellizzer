Ficus wrightii

Ficus wrightii, commonly known as the Wright's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and attract various wildlife. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.

Ficus wrightii is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor spaces.
