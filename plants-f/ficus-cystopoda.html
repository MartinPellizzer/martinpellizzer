Ficus cystopoda

Ficus cystopoda, commonly known as the white fig or white-veined fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as Thailand, Vietnam, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins, which give it its common name. Ficus cystopoda is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

It prefers warm, humid environments and is relatively easy to care for in indoor settings.
