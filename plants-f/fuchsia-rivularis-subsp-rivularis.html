Fuchsia rivularis subsp. rivularis

Fuchsia rivularis subsp.

rivularis, commonly known as the stream fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of vibrant pink to purple tubular flowers.

The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found along stream banks and in forest undergrowth. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a leathery texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with appropriate moisture conditions.
