Ferocactus covillei

Ferocactus covillei, commonly known as the Coville's barrel cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically clustered at the top of the stem. Its spines are relatively short and often have a whitish or yellowish color.

Ferocactus covillei is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant, making it a hardy species for desert landscapes.
