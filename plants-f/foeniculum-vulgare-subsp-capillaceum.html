Foeniculum vulgare subsp. capillaceum

Foeniculum vulgare subsp.

capillaceum, commonly known as slender fennel, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in umbrella-shaped clusters.

The plant produces small, oval seeds that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable candidate for gardens and landscapes.

Its aromatic properties are attributed to the presence of volatile oils such as anethole and limonene.
