Fragaria insularis

Fragaria insularis, commonly known as the island strawberry, is a species of wild strawberry native to certain island regions.

It is a low-growing perennial plant with trifoliate leaves and produces small, red, edible fruits. The plant typically thrives in temperate climates and well-drained soils. Its flowers are white with five petals and are pollinated by insects.

The fruit is valued for its sweet flavor and is often used in local cuisines and traditional remedies.
