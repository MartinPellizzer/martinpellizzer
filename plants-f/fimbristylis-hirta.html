Fimbristylis hirta

Fimbristylis hirta, commonly known as the hairy sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically green or brown in color, with delicate, thread-like flowers. Fimbristylis hirta is commonly found in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
