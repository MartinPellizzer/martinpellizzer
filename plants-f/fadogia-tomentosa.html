Fadogia tomentosa

Fadogia tomentosa, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its hairy, woody stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by pods containing edible seeds. It is often grown for its nutritional value, as the seeds are a good source of protein and oil.

Additionally, it has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
