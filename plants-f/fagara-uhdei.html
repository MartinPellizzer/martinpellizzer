Fagara uhdei

Fagara uhdei, commonly known as the Caribbean wax tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to the Caribbean region.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves and produces small, fragrant flowers that are white or pale yellow. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
