Fuchsia sanctae-rosae

Fuchsia sanctae-rosae, commonly known as the Santa Rosa fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, specifically Ecuador.

It belongs to the genus Fuchsia in the family Onagraceae, characterized by its showy, pendulous flowers. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with opposite, ovate leaves. Its flowers are notable for their vibrant colors, often displaying shades of red, purple, and pink, and are pollinated primarily by birds and insects.

Fuchsia sanctae-rosae is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
