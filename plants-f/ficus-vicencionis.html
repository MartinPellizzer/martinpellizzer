Ficus vicencionis

Ficus vicencionis, commonly known as the Vicencian fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus vicencionis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
