Fraxinus mariesii

Fraxinus mariesii, commonly known as the green ash.

It is a deciduous tree native to the eastern and central United States, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves with 9 to 15 leaflets. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, which are wind-pollinated. Its fruits are samaras, typically containing one or two seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

The plant is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to tolerate urban environments.
