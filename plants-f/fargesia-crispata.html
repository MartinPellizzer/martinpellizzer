Fargesia crispata

Fargesia crispata, commonly known as the silver fern, is a species of bamboo native to China.

It is characterized by its gracefully arching stems and finely divided, silver-gray leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is deciduous, shedding its leaves in the fall, and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers cool, moist environments and is relatively low maintenance once established.

Fargesia crispata is well-suited for use in shaded areas and can be grown in containers or as part of a rock garden.
