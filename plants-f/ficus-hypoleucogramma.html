Ficus hypoleucogramma

Ficus hypoleucogramma, commonly known as the white-veined fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent white veins, which give it its common name. It produces small, inedible fruits that are typically not consumed by humans.

Ficus hypoleucogramma is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
