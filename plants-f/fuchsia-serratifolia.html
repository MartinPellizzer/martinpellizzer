Fuchsia serratifolia

Fuchsia serratifolia, commonly known as the serrated fuchsia, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to purple, often with intricate patterns. The leaves are typically serrated, giving the plant its scientific name, and they are glossy and dark green. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Fuchsia serratifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
