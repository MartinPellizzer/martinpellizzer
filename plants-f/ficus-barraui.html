Ficus barraui

Ficus barraui, commonly known as the Barrau's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature.

Ficus barraui is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
