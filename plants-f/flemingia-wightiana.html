Flemingia wightiana

Flemingia wightiana, commonly known as the Indian white cedar, is a small evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by flat, pod-like fruits. Flemingia wightiana is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its durable wood.
