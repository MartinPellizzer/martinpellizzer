Flagellaria indica var. guineensis

Flagellaria indica var. guineensis, commonly known as the African horned cucumber, is a climbing vine native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Cucurbitaceae family, which includes other cucurbits like pumpkins and gourds. The plant is characterized by its tendrils and heart-shaped leaves, and it produces elongated, spiky fruits that resemble horns. Its botanical features include a fibrous stem and small, yellowish flowers.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has traditional uses in some regions.
