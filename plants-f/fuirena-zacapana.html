Fuirena zacapana

Fuirena zacapana, commonly known as the Zacapana cactus, is a species native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its columnar stem and spiny appearance. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting to harsh conditions with its thick, waxy skin. Its flowers, which are usually yellow or orange, bloom in the spring and attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
