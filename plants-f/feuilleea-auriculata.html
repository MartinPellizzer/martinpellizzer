Feuilleea auriculata

Feuilleea auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped leaf plant, is a perennial herb native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with wavy margins and a velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink and white, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are not only ornamental but also contain bioactive compounds that have been studied for their potential medicinal properties.

Feuilleea auriculata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
