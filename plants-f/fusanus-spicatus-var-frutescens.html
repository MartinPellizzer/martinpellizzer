Fusanus spicatus var. frutescens

Fusanus spicatus var. frutescens, commonly known as the fragrant fusanus, is a shrub-like plant native to certain regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to a range of garden environments.
