Festuca singularis

Festuca singularis, commonly known as the slender fescue, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and woodland edges. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Festuca singularis is also noted for its ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various insects and small wildlife.
