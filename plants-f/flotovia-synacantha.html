Flotovia synacantha

Flotovia synacantha, commonly known as the spiral cactus, is a unique desert plant native to the arid regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its spiraling, segmented stems that form a helical shape, which helps it maximize sunlight exposure while minimizing water loss. The plant produces small, bright yellow flowers during the rainy season, adding a striking contrast to its green, spiny structure. Its leaves are modified into spines, reducing transpiration and providing protection from herbivores.

Flotovia synacantha is adapted to extreme conditions, making it a resilient and visually distinctive member of the cactus family.
