Ficus baola

Ficus baola, commonly known as the Baola fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Ficus baola is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to adapt to indoor environments.
