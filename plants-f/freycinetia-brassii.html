Freycinetia brassii

Freycinetia brassii, commonly known as the red-veined cycad, is a member of the cycad family, Cycadaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant features a stout, unbranched trunk with a fibrous, spongy bark. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with long, narrow leaflets that are often reddish-veined.

Freycinetia brassii is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
