Fraxinus japonica var. pubinervis

Fraxinus japonica var. pubinervis, commonly known as the Japanese ash with pubescent veins, is a variant of the Japanese ash tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and lance-shaped leaves with prominent, hairy veins. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It produces small, yellow-green flowers in early spring, followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in urban and natural landscapes.
