Fragaria sibbaldifolia

Fragaria sibbaldifolia, commonly known as the alpine strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color, with a sweet and delicate flavor. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets and grows in rocky or alpine environments. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and culinary uses.

Fragaria sibbaldifolia is adapted to cold climates and thrives in well-drained soil.
