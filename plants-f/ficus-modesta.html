Ficus modesta

Ficus modesta, commonly known as the small-fig or dwarf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus modesta is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
