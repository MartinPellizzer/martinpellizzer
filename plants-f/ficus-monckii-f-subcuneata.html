Ficus monckii f. subcuneata

Ficus monckii f. subcuneata, commonly known as the subcuneate fig, is a rare and unique variant of the Ficus genus.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf shape, which features a subcuneate (narrowly wedge-shaped) base. The plant is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. Its leaves are typically glossy and dark green, with a smooth texture.

This cultivar is often prized for its ornamental value in indoor gardening due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to controlled environments.
