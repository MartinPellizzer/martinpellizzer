Festuca tenuifolia f. frisia

Festuca tenuifolia f. frisia, commonly known as the Frisian bentgrass, is a hemicryptophyte with a low-growing, mat-forming habit.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, with a dense, tufted appearance. This plant is well-adapted to coastal and saline environments, showing tolerance to wind and salt spray. Its flowering stems are usually short and bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers.

It is often found in sandy or gravelly soils along the coastlines of Europe.
