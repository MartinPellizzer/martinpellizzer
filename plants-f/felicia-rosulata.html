Felicia rosulata

Felicia rosulata, commonly known as the clustered fleur-de-lis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of bright yellow flowers with a distinctive papery texture. The plant features small, narrow leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, giving it a neat appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Felicia rosulata is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
