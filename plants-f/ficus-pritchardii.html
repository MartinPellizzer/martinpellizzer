Ficus pritchardii

Ficus pritchardii, commonly known as the Pritchard's fig, is a flowering plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is characterized by its striking, large, glossy leaves that are often heart-shaped or elliptical in form. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Ficus pritchardii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
