Filix cristata var. clintoniana

Filix cristata var. clintoniana, commonly known as the Clinton's maidenhair fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that resemble the structure of a bird's feather. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in rich, humus-filled soil. Its reproductive structures, including spore-bearing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
