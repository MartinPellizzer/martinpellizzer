Fuchsia verrucosa

Fuchsia verrucosa, commonly known as the rough fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety, and often hairy leaves, which give it its scientific name "verrucosa," meaning "warty.". The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers in shades of red, purple, and pink, typically blooming in spring and summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Fuchsia verrucosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a decorative plant.
