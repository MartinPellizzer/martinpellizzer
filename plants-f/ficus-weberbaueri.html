Ficus weberbaueri

Ficus weberbaueri, commonly known as the Weberbauer fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate in form. It produces small, clustered fruits known as figs, which are edible and attract various wildlife.

Ficus weberbaueri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
