Festuca ovina var. heteropachys

Festuca ovina var. heteropachys, commonly known as the hairy sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The plant has distinctive hairy leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in rocky or marginal habitats.
