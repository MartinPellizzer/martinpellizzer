Flacourtia cerasifera

Flacourtia cerasifera, commonly known as the cherry apple or Indian cherry, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It produces small, yellow or orange fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in coastal areas or near rivers.

The fruit is sometimes used in traditional medicine and as a food source in local communities.
