Ficus afzelii

Ficus afzelii, commonly known as the African fig tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically opposite or clustered at the nodes. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories or along riverbanks.

Ficus afzelii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in tropical and subtropical regions.
