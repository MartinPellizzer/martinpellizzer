Ficus pantoniana var. colobocarpa

Ficus pantoniana var. colobocarpa, commonly known as the Philippine strangler fig, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a tropical evergreen tree characterized by its broad, dark green leaves and distinctive aerial roots. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round figs that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its unique growth habit and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
