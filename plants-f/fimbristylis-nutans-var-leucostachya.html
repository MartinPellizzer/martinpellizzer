Fimbristylis nutans var. leucostachya

Fimbristylis nutans var. leucostachya, commonly known as the white-stemmed sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to aquatic habitats. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spikelet that produces small, white or pale green flowers.

This variety is distinguished by its white stamens and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
