Fragaria vesca

Fragaria vesca, commonly known as the wild strawberry, is a small perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and is characterized by trifoliate leaves and small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in woodland areas, meadows, and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soil. Its flowers are white with five petals and are followed by red, fleshy fruits that ripen in late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and for its use in culinary and medicinal applications.
