Ficus boliviana

Ficus boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru. The plant is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves and can grow as a shrub or small tree. It produces small, inedible fruits that are not typically consumed by humans.

Ficus boliviana is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.
