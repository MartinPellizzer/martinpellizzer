Ficus johnsonii

Ficus johnsonii, commonly known as the Johnson's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, including countries such as Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, inedible figs. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Ficus johnsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
