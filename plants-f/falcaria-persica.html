Falcaria persica

Falcaria persica, commonly known as Persian cowpea, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its erect or semi-woody growth habit. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to violet, followed by pods containing edible seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval and hairy, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

Falcaria persica is often cultivated for its nutritional value and is used in traditional cuisines.
