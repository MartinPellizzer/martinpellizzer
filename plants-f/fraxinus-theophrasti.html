Fraxinus theophrasti

Fraxinus theophrasti, commonly known as the common ash.

It is a deciduous tree native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa. The plant has opposite, simple, and usually serrated leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, and it produces winged seeds in clusters.

The tree is often found in moist soils and can grow in a variety of habitats, including woodlands and riverbanks.
