Fraxinoides alba

Fraxinoides alba, commonly known as the white fraxinoid, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and opposite, simple leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins or open woodlands.

Its botanical features make it a notable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
