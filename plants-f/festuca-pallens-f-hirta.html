Festuca pallens f. hirta

Festuca pallens f. hirta, commonly known as the hairy silvergrass, is a variant of the silvergrass species Festuca pallens.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. It produces small, pale green to blue-green flower spikes in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of wetland environments.
