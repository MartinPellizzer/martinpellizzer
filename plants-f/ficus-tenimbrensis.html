Ficus tenimbrensis

Ficus tenimbrensis, commonly known as the West African ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of West Africa, where it typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a robust, woody stem. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

Ficus tenimbrensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
