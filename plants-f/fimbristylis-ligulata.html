Fimbristylis ligulata

Fimbristylis ligulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spikerush, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of pale yellow to brown flowers, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in wet or seasonally wet habitats such as swamps, marshes, and the edges of water bodies.

Fimbristylis ligulata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic invertebrates.
