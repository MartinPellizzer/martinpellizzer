Festuca sulcatifrons

Festuca sulcatifrons, commonly known as the narrowleaf fescue, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Festuca sulcatifrons is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
