Ficus palmata

Ficus palmata, commonly known as the palmate fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are palmately lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus palmata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor conditions.
