Ficus cognata

Ficus cognata, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including China, India, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its dense, trailing growth habit and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence. Ficus cognata produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits that are typically green when mature.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor environments.
