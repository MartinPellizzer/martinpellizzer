Farsetia clypeata var. bracteosa

Farsetia clypeata var. bracteosa, commonly known as the bracteate farsetia, is a rare and striking member of the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its unique, spurred flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant produces specialized bracts that enclose the flower, giving it its common name. Native to specific regions in South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated by enthusiasts and conservationists alike.
