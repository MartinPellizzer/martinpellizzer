Festuca altaica subsp. arizonica

Festuca altaica subsp.

arizonica, commonly known as Arizona bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States. It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resilience to harsh conditions.
