Flindersia laevicarpa var. heterophylla

Flindersia laevicarpa var. heterophylla, commonly known as the variable flindersia, is a tropical tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often lobed leaves that vary in shape and size, contributing to its common name. The plant produces fragrant, yellowish-green flowers followed by woody, elongated fruits. Its bark is smooth and greyish, and it is often found in coastal and lowland rainforest areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses by Indigenous communities.
