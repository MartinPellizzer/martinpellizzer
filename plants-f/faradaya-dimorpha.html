Faradaya dimorpha

Faradaya dimorpha, commonly known as the two-form faradaya, is a unique plant species native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its strikingly different appearance between its vegetative and reproductive stages, a phenomenon known as dimorphism. The plant typically grows as a shrub with smooth, waxy leaves that are often arranged in opposite pairs. Its flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Faradaya dimorpha is valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
