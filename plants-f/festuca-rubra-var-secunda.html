Festuca rubra var. secunda

Festuca rubra var. secunda, commonly known as red fescue, is a cool-season grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green foliage and compact growth habit, making it suitable for lawns and erosion control. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Festuca rubra var. secunda is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to withstand heavy foot traffic.
