Ficus adenosperma var. microlepis

Ficus adenosperma var. microlepis, commonly known as the small-leaved fig tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are smaller and more densely arranged compared to other ficus species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
