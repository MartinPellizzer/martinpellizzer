Fumana juniperina

Fumana juniperina, commonly known as the Canary Islands juniper, is a species of juniper native to the Canary Islands.

It is a small evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in rocky, arid environments. The plant has needle-like leaves and produces small, blue-black berries that are toxic to humans but serve as a food source for certain wildlife. It is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often used in traditional medicine.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in harsh climates.
