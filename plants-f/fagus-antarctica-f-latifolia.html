Fagus antarctica f. latifolia

Fagus antarctica f. latifolia, commonly known as the Antarctic beech, is a variant of the Antarctic beech tree native to the Southern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, spherical nuts enclosed in a spiny husk. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in subantarctic regions.

The species plays an important ecological role in its native habitats, supporting various forms of wildlife.
