Fagonia chilensis var. barclayana

Fagonia chilensis var. barclayana, commonly known as the Chilean fagonia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Chile.

It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has adapted to survive in dry environments with deep root systems and waxy leaves to reduce water loss. It is often found in coastal and inland deserts, thriving in well-drained soils.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and resilience in harsh climates.
