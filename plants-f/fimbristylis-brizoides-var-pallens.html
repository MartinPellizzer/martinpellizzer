Fimbristylis brizoides var. pallens

Fimbristylis brizoides var. pallens, commonly known as the silver-haired sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of the Americas.

It is a perennial herb with grass-like leaves that are typically slender and slightly hairy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in dense, spike-like clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems, supporting a variety of wildlife.
