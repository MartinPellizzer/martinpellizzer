Fraxinus sieboldiana var. serrata

Fraxinus sieboldiana var. serrata, commonly known as the Japanese ash or serrated ash, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, compound leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins during the spring, followed by winged seeds in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in gardens and parks.
