Ficaria ranunculoides var. fertilissima

Ficaria ranunculoides var. fertilissima, commonly known as the silverleaf sorrel, is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating.

It produces small, yellow flowers with five petals, typically blooming in early spring. The plant is native to southern Africa and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is drought-tolerant and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its unique appearance and ability to store water make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
