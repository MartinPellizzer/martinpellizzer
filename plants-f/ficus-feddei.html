Ficus feddei

Ficus feddei, commonly known as the Fedde's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. Ficus feddei produces small, clustered figs that are encased in a green, fleshy structure.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
