Ficus soroceoides var. brachyclada

Ficus soroceoides var. brachyclada, commonly known as the small-leaved fig tree, is a tropical plant characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically smaller than those of other ficus species.

It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, edible figs that are generally not as prominent as in other fig species. The plant is native to parts of Southeast Asia and thrives in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
