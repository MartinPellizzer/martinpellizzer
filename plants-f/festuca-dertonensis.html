Festuca dertonensis

Festuca dertonensis, commonly known as Derton's fescue, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny spikelets.

Festuca dertonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
