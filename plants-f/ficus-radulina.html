Ficus radulina

Ficus radulina, commonly known as the Radulina fig tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features glossy, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and have a slightly waxy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, followed by the production of edible fig fruits.

Ficus radulina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
