Fritillaria obliqua subsp. tuntasia

Fritillaria obliqua subsp.

tuntasia, commonly known as the Tuntasia fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the Andes mountains of South America. It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or red with dark purple or black markings.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique blooms.

The subspecies is closely related to other members of the Fritillaria genus, which are known for their distinctive and often colorful flowers.
