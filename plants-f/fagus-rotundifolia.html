Fagus rotundifolia

Fagus rotundifolia, commonly known as the Japanese beech, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that are rounded at the base. The plant produces small, spore-bearing cones and thrives in cool, moist environments. It is often found in mountainous regions and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

The leaves turn yellow in autumn, adding to its seasonal beauty.
