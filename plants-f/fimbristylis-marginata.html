Fimbristylis marginata

Fimbristylis marginata, commonly known as the narrowleaf flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, acidic soils. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered with fine, hair-like structures along the edges. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in dense spikes, and it produces small, three-lobed berries as its fruit.

Fimbristylis marginata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic organisms.
