Festuca pulchella subvar. plicata

Festuca pulchella subvar. plicata, commonly known as the crested fescue, is a cool-season grass native to the Pacific Northwest of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, folded leaves that give it a unique, grass-like appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It has a tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Festuca pulchella subvar. plicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
