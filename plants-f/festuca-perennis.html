Festuca perennis

Festuca perennis, commonly known as bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and a bluish-green color, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. Festuca perennis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.

It is also an important forage plant for livestock in certain regions.
