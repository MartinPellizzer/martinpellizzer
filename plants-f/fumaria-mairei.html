Fumaria mairei

Fumaria mairei, commonly known as the Maire's smokeplant, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Fumaria mairei is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance.
