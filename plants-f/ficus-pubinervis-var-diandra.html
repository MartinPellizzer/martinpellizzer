Ficus pubinervis var. diandra

Ficus pubinervis var. diandra, commonly known as the diandra fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often adorned with a fine, soft pubescence. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green to reddish in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to adapt to indoor conditions.
