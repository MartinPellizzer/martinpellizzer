Fritillaria camschatcensis var. alpina

Fritillaria camschatcensis var. alpina, commonly known as the Alpine Fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soil and full sun exposure. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette that helps store moisture.

The Alpine Fritillary is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
