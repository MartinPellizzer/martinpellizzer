Ficus tristis

Ficus tristis, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its elongated, drooping leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish flowers that are followed by round, fuzzy fruits.

Ficus tristis is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
