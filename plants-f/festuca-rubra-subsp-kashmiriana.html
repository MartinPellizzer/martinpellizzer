Festuca rubra subsp. kashmiriana

Festuca rubra subsp.

kashmiriana, commonly known as Kashmir grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the Himalayas. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, soft blades that are often bluish-green in color.

The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and as a ground cover in temperate climates.
