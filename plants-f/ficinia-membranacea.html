Ficinia membranacea

Ficinia membranacea, commonly known as the paper plant, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It features slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color and have a papery texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Ficinia membranacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
