Fraxinus ornus var. maculatovariegata

Fraxinus ornus var. maculatovariegata, commonly known as the variegated weeping ash, is a striking ornamental tree characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are ovate to elliptical, with a serrated margin and a variegated pattern of white, yellow, and green, creating a vibrant visual display. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in drooping clusters, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and adaptability, though it may be susceptible to certain pests and diseases.
