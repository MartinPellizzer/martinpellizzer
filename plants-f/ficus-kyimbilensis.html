Ficus kyimbilensis

Ficus kyimbilensis, commonly known as the Kyimbilensis Fig, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Ficus kyimbilensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
