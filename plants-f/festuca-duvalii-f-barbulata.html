Festuca duvalii f. barbulata

Festuca duvalii f. barbulata, commonly known as the barbed fescue, is a cool-season grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dense, carpet-like foliage. The plant features narrow, bluish-green leaves with a distinctive barbed texture along the edges, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and grassy slopes.

This ornamental grass is valued for its hardiness and ability to form low-maintenance, decorative landscapes.
