Fuchsia magellanica var. ricoartonii

Fuchsia magellanica var. ricoartonii, commonly known as the Magellan fuchsia, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking clusters of tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to violet. The plant features dark green, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

This variety is particularly valued for its winter blooming habit, which adds color to the landscape during other plants' dormant periods.
