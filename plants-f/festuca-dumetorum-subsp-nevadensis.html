Festuca dumetorum subsp. nevadensis

Festuca dumetorum subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as Sierra grass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine meadows, thriving in cool, moist environments.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its ability to form dense mats, which help stabilize soil in rocky or disturbed areas.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-elevation ecosystems.
