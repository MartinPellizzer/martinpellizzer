Fresenia foliosa var. monocephala

Fresenia foliosa var. monocephala, commonly known as the single-headed flaxflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and lanceolate, glaucous leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to reddish-pink flowers that bloom in late summer. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and delicate floral display.
