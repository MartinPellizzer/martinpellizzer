Fagopyrum esculentum var. pyramidatum

Fagopyrum esculentum var. pyramidatum, commonly known as the Japanese buckwheat, is a herbaceous annual plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive pyramidal shape, which gives it its varietal name. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, followed by triangular, edible seeds. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, with a slightly hairy surface.

This variety is cultivated for its nutritious seeds, which are rich in protein and minerals.
