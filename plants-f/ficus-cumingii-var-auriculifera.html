Ficus cumingii var. auriculifera

Ficus cumingii var. auriculifera, commonly known as the ear-shaped fig or auriculifera fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
