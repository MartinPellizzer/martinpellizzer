Felicia hirta

Felicia hirta, commonly known as the blueweed or blue daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant blue or purple flowers. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its scientific name *hirta*, meaning "hairy" in Latin. It typically grows in dry, sandy soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Felicia hirta is cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
