Ficus scaberrima var. angustata

Ficus scaberrima var. angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and often consumed by various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental and hedging purposes.
