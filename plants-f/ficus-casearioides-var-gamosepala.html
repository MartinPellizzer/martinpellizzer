Ficus casearioides var. gamosepala

Ficus casearioides var. gamosepala, commonly known as the white fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, light green leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by round, greenish fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor landscaping.
