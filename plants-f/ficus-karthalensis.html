Ficus karthalensis

Ficus karthalensis, commonly known as the Karthala fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its dense, broad leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid climates with well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant in temperate regions.
