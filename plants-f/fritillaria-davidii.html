Fritillaria davidii

Fritillaria davidii, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a robust bulb that stores nutrients for growth. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens.

Fritillaria davidii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
