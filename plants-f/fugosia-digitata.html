Fugosia digitata

Fugosia digitata, commonly known as the fingerleaf fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that radiate from a central stem, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense colonies. Its leaves are typically bright green and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.

Fugosia digitata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
