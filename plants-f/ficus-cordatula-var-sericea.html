Ficus cordatula var. sericea

Ficus cordatula var. sericea, commonly known as the silky fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically dark green and have a smooth, waxy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
