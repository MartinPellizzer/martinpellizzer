Franquevillia

Franquevillia, commonly known as the red-flowered orchid, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

It is characterized by its striking, vibrant red petals and a compact, terrestrial growth habit. The plants typically produce intricate, showy flowers that bloom in late spring or early summer. Franquevillia species are native to tropical and subtropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil.

They are valued in horticulture for their ornamental appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
