Felicia elongata

Felicia elongata, commonly known as the blueweed or blue daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in well-drained soils. Felicia elongata is drought-tolerant and often grows in rocky or sandy habitats.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and thistles.
