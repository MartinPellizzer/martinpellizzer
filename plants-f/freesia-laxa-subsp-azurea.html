Freesia laxa subsp. azurea

Freesia laxa subsp.

azurea, commonly known as the blue freesia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers.

The flowers typically range in color from pale blue to deep violet, with a striking contrast between the petals and the green sepals. This subspecies is known for its compact growth habit and is often cultivated as a garden ornamental.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
