Fritillaria kotschyana subsp. grandiflora

Fritillaria kotschyana subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the grandiflora fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange, red, or yellow, often with contrasting black or purple markings.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in well-drained, rocky soils. It blooms in early spring and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

This subspecies is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal medicine.
