Ferula varia

Ferula varia, commonly known as the Kashmiri ferula, is a perennial herb native to the Himalayan regions of India and Pakistan.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense clusters of yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a milky latex sap, which is often used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ferula varia thrives in high-altitude environments and is adapted to cold, arid conditions.
