Ficus appendiculata

Ficus appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, Thailand, and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible figs.

Ficus appendiculata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
