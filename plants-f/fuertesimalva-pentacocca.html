Fuertesimalva pentacocca

Fuertesimalva pentacocca, commonly known as the Canary Island pine, is a species of pine native to the Canary Islands.

It is a tall, evergreen tree with needle-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The tree produces cones that contain seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in volcanic soils and has adapted to the island's unique climate.

This plant plays an important ecological role in the local ecosystem.
