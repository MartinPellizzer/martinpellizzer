Ficus gummiflua

Ficus gummiflua, commonly known as the gum fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are often covered in a sticky latex. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ficus gummiflua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
