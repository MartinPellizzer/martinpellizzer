Funastrum pubescens

Funastrum pubescens, commonly known as the hairy funastrum, is a species of flowering plant in the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
