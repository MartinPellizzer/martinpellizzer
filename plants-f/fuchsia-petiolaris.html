Fuchsia petiolaris

Fuchsia petiolaris, commonly known as the slender fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces clusters of vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant features slender, wiry stems and heart-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. Fuchsia petiolaris thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated as a decorative plant in hanging baskets or as a ground cover in shaded garden areas.
