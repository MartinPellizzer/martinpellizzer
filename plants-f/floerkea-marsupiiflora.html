Floerkea marsupiiflora

Floerkea marsupiiflora, commonly known as the cup-shaped passionflower, is a tropical vine native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, showy flowers that resemble a cup or cupped hand, with five petals and a crown of filaments. The plant produces small, red berries that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a velvety texture, while its stems are woody and climbing.

Floerkea marsupiiflora thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
