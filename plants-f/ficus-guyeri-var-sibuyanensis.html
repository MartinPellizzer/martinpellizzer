Ficus guyeri var. sibuyanensis

Ficus guyeri var. sibuyanensis, commonly known as the Sibuyan fig, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with dense, glossy green leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The Sibuyan fig is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
