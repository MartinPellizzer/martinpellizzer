Festuca arundinacea var. uechtritziana

Festuca arundinacea var. uechtritziana, commonly known as Uechtritzian Meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often reddish stems. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue. This variety is known for its ornamental value and is often used in landscaping for its fine texture and color.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in meadows and along stream banks.
