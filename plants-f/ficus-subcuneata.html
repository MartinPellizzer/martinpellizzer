Ficus subcuneata

Ficus subcuneata, commonly known as the narrow-angled fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Andes mountains in South America, specifically in regions of Peru and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often dark green and glossy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, inedible fig-like fruits.

Ficus subcuneata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
