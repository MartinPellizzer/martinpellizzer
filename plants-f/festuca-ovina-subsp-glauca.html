Festuca ovina subsp. glauca

Festuca ovina subsp.

glauca, commonly known as blue mountain fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of western North America. It is characterized by its fine, dark green foliage and a bluish-gray sheen on the leaves, which gives it its common name.

This subspecies is often found in rocky, well-drained soils at high elevations. It forms dense, low-growing tufts and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for erosion control in alpine environments.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer.
