Ficus umbobracteata

Ficus umbobracteata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. It produces small, fig-like fruits that are typically enclosed in a specialized structure called a syconium.

Ficus umbobracteata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
