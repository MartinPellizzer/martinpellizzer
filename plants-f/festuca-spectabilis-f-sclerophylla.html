Festuca spectabilis f. sclerophylla

Festuca spectabilis f. sclerophylla, commonly known as the silverblue grass, is a variant of the bluegrass species characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

This ornamental grass features narrow, silver-blue leaves that are slightly rolled and have a distinctive bluish-gray appearance. It is well adapted to rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant is drought-tolerant and requires minimal maintenance once established.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the year, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
