Fritillaria straussii

Fritillaria straussii, commonly known as the Strauss's fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color, with intricate patterns resembling a checkerboard. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often growing in rocky or alpine environments. Its bulbs are adapted for survival in harsh climates, and the plant is known for its resilience in cold, arid conditions.

Fritillaria straussii is a popular choice among gardeners for its ornamental value and unique floral display.
