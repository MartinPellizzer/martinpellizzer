Fraxinus oxyphylla var. subintegra

Fraxinus oxyphylla var. subintegra, commonly known as the smooth-fruited ash, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia, particularly China.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, while the fruit is a samara with a thin, winged structure. This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its graceful form and seasonal interest.

It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.
