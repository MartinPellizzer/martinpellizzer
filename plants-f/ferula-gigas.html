Ferula gigas

Ferula gigas, commonly known as the giant giantweed, is a tall, perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its thick, fibrous roots and tall, hollow stems. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in early summer, which are followed by distinctive, elongated seed pods. Its leaves are large, pinnately compound, and often have a strong, aromatic scent.

Ferula gigas is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
