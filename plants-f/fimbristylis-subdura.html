Fimbristylis subdura, commonly known as the threadleaf sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. This species typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and swamps. The inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, three-lobed spikelets.
