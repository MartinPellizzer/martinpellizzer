Fontquera paui

Fontquera paui, commonly known as the Brazilian nightshade, is a perennial shrub native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes tomatoes, potatoes, and peppers. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. Its flowers are typically blue or violet, with five petals, and it produces small, round berries that are yellow when mature.

The plant contains toxic alkaloids, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals.
