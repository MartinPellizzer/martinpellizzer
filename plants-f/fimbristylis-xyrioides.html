Fimbristylis xyrioides

Fimbristylis xyrioides, commonly known as the narrowleaf flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wetland environments such as swampy areas and seasonally flooded soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in coastal regions and is adapted to tolerate saltwater conditions.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
