Ficus bangouanensis

Ficus bangouanensis, commonly known as the Bangouan fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Vietnam and Cambodia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible figs that are typically dark purple in color.

This species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
