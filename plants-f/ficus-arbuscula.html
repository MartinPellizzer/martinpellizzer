Ficus arbuscula

Ficus arbuscula, commonly known as the tree of heaven, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits known as syconia. Ficus arbuscula is a member of the Moraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types.

It is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and adaptability.
