Ficus pustulata var. lanceifolia

Ficus pustulata var. lanceifolia, commonly known as the warty fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, warty bark and lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, spherical fruits that are often consumed by wildlife. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
