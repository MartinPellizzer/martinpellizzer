Ficus silhetensis

Ficus silhetensis, commonly known as the Silhet fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the subtropical regions of Bangladesh and India, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, edible figs that are typically clustered in the axils of the leaves. It is often found growing in the understorey of forests, contributing to the biodiversity of its habitat.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
