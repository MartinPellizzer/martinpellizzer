Fragaria vesca var. sativa

Fragaria vesca var. sativa, commonly known as the cultivated strawberry, is a flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. The plant is a perennial herb that thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil. Its fruit is rich in vitamin C and antioxidants, making it a valuable crop for both culinary and medicinal use.

The variety 'sativa' is widely cultivated for its enhanced flavor and larger fruit size compared to wild strawberries.
