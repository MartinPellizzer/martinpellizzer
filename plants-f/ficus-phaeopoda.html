Ficus phaeopoda

Ficus phaeopoda, commonly known as the red-fig or red-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its striking, glossy, reddish-brown leaves that contrast with its dark green veins. Ficus phaeopoda is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage.

It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
