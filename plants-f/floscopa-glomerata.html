Floscopa glomerata

Floscopa glomerata, commonly known as the clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream sides. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs.

Floscopa glomerata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
