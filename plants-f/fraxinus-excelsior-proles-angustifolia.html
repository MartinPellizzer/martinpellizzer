Fraxinus excelsior proles angustifolia

Fraxinus excelsior proles angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ash, is a variant of the common ash tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, which appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its bark is smooth and gray, with shallow fissures, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

The plant is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained conditions.
