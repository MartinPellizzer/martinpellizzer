Festuca ovina var. vaginata

Festuca ovina var. vaginata, commonly known as sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and dense growth habit, often forming a mat-like structure. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its flowering spikes are usually slender and spike-like, bearing small, spikelet-based flowers.

Festuca ovina var. vaginata is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in difficult areas.
