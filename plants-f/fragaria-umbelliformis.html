Fragaria umbelliformis

Fragaria umbelliformis, commonly known as the umbrella strawberry, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, white flowers that bloom in clusters, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red when ripe, similar to other strawberries. It is native to South America and thrives in moist, temperate climates.

Due to its unique appearance and flavor, it is occasionally cultivated for ornamental and culinary purposes.
