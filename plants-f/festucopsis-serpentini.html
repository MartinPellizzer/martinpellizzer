Festucopsis serpentini

Festucopsis serpentini, commonly known as the serpent's grass or serpent's meadow, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family, Poaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences that are typically greenish or brownish in hue. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in mountainous regions and along stream banks.

Festucopsis serpentini is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland habitats.
