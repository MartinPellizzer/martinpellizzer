Ficus strigosa

Ficus strigosa, commonly known as the rough fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and Australia. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, clustered fruiting structures called syconia. It thrives in humid environments and is often found in forests or along riverbanks.

Ficus strigosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to adapt to various soil types.
