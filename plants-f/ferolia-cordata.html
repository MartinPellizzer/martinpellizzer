Ferolia cordata

Ferolia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf citrus or heartleaf mandarin, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are heart-shaped, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, sweet citrus fruits. Ferolia cordata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its unique, aromatic fruit.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical gardens.
