Fimbristylis fuscescens

Fimbristylis fuscescens, commonly known as the purple spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Australia.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically purple or pink in color. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wetland environments and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Fimbristylis fuscescens is an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems, contributing to biodiversity and soil stabilization.
