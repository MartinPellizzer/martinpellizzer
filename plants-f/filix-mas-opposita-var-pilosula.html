Filix-mas opposita var. pilosula

Filix-mas opposita var. pilosula, commonly known as the hairy male fern, is a variant of the male fern species characterized by its distinctive hairy fronds.

This plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a feathery appearance that contributes to its ornamental value. The plant's spores are produced on the underside of the fronds, which are released during the humid seasons.

It is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
