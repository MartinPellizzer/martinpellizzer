Filago germanica subsp. decumbens

Filago germanica subsp.

decumbens, commonly known as the creeping filago, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe. It is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with stems that lie close to the ground.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically linear and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas.
