Frangula polymorpha var. latifolia

Frangula polymorpha var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring. The plant is part of the Rutaceae family and is known for its reddish, berry-like fruits that mature in the fall. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
