Ficus gul var. lasiocarpa

Ficus gul var. lasiocarpa, commonly known as the woolly fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly coating on the stems and leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and have a glossy texture with a soft, fuzzy surface.
