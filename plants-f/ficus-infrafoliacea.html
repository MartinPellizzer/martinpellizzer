Ficus infrafoliacea

Ficus infrafoliacea, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, including countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its small, glossy leaves that are narrow and elongated. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible figs.

Ficus infrafoliacea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
