Fuchsia caucana

Fuchsia caucana, commonly known as the Andean fuchsia, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions with cool, moist climates. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a contrasting throat. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine pubescence.

Fuchsia caucana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil and partial shade.
