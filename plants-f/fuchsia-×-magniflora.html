Fuchsia × magniflora

Fuchsia × magniflora, commonly known as the common fuchsia, is a hybrid plant originating from the cross between Fuchsia × hybrida and Fuchsia × tibouchina.

It is a member of the Onagraceae family and is native to South America. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and purple, often with a contrasting throat. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and requires partial to full sun for optimal growth.

Fuchsia × magniflora is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage and long-lasting blooms.
