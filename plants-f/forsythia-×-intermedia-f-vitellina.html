Forsythia × intermedia f. vitellina

Forsythia × intermedia f. vitellina, commonly known as the golden bell forsythia, is a deciduous shrub characterized by its striking yellow flowers.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Forsythia × intermedia and Forsythia viridiflora, showcasing a combination of traits from both parents. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring upright, branching stems covered in soft, gray-green foliage. Its flowers, which appear in early spring before the leaves, are clustered in pendulous racemes, adding ornamental value to gardens.

This variety is prized for its vibrant color and adaptability to various soil conditions.
