Ficus loxensis

Ficus loxensis, commonly known as the Loxense fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and distinctive fruit. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually heart-shaped with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface.

The fruit, which is edible, develops from the flowering structure and is an important food source for various wildlife species.
