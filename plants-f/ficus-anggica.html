Ficus anggica

Ficus anggica, commonly known as the Angkola fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of Indonesia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically yellow or green in color. Ficus anggica thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
