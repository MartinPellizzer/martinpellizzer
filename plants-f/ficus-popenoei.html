Ficus popenoei

Ficus popenoei, commonly known as the Popenoe fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Ficus popenoei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
