Ficinia capitella

Ficinia capitella, commonly known as the dwarf flax lily, is a small, herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces small, white or pale blue flowers arranged in a spike-like cluster. The plant is often found growing in damp, shaded areas such as forests and stream banks. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.

Ficinia capitella is also known for its ability to thrive in humid environments and its preference for well-drained soil.
