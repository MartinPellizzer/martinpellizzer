Festuca tenuiflora var. aristata

Festuca tenuiflora var. aristata, commonly known as the aristate fineleaf fescue, is a cool-season grass native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale blue to purple flowers. It is well adapted to dry, rocky soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
