Festuca obtusa

Festuca obtusa, commonly known as blunt feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to golden in color. Festuca obtusa is often found in moist meadows, woodland edges, and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
