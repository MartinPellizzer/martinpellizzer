Fremontia vermiculatus

Fremontia vermiculatus, commonly known as the redroot filaree, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has finely divided, feather-like leaves that give it a delicate appearance. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open woodlands and along stream banks.

Fremontia vermiculatus is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
