Ficus burkillii

Ficus burkillii, commonly known as the Burkill's fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its roots can form symbiotic relationships with certain fungi, enhancing its ability to absorb nutrients from the soil.
