Ficus hirta var. integrifolia

Ficus hirta var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor cultivation and ornamental gardening.
