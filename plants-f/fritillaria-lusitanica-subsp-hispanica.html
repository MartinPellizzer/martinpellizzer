Fritillaria lusitanica subsp. hispanica

Fritillaria lusitanica subsp.

hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish crown imperial, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow.

The plant typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are adapted for storage and can remain dormant for extended periods.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
