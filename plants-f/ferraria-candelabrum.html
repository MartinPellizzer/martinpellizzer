Ferraria candelabrum

Ferraria candelabrum, commonly known as the candlestick lily, is a South African plant belonging to the Iridaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, candle-like flower spikes that can reach up to 1 meter in height. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces vibrant, often purple or yellow flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens in its native region.

Ferraria candelabrum is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique visual display.
