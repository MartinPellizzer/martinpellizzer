Festuca myuros var. pseudonardurus

Festuca myuros var. pseudonardurus, commonly known as the purple false bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, nutrient-rich soils and is often found in meadows, marshes, and along streambanks. Its leaves are usually bluish-green, and the variety is distinguished by its purple-tinged inflorescences.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland restoration projects.
