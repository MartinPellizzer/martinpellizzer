Fumaria mirabilis subsp. faurei

Fumaria mirabilis subsp.

faurei, commonly known as the golden crownflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia. It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes, and it is often found in disturbed soils. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and slightly hairy, while the stems are erect and branched.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
