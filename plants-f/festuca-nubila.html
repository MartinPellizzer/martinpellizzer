Festuca nubila

Festuca nubila, commonly known as alpine bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to high-altitude regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It thrives in cool, moist environments such as mountain meadows and rocky slopes. The plant has fine, wiry leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer. Festuca nubila is well adapted to harsh alpine conditions, with a deep root system that helps it withstand cold and poor soil.

Its ecological importance includes providing forage for grazing animals and supporting local biodiversity.
