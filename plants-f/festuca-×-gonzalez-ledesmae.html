Festuca × gonzalez-ledesmae

Festuca × gonzalez-ledesmae, commonly known as the González-Ledesma fescue, is a hybrid grass species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. The plant produces compact, spike-like inflorescences with small, two-lobed flowers, which are usually pale green to brown in color. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and ecological restoration projects.
