Fagus javanica

Fagus javanica, commonly known as the Javan beech, is a deciduous tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and chestnuts, and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, spiny acorns that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments and can reach heights of up to 25 meters.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
