Filipendula × purpurea var. auriculata

Filipendula × purpurea var. auriculata, commonly known as the golden-leaf bergamot, is a hybrid shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red inflorescences that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has compound leaves with serrated edges and a distinctive, fragrant aroma. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and wetland restoration projects.
