Fuchsia magellanica var. gracilis

Fuchsia magellanica var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender fuchsia, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant features slender, wiry stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

This variety is particularly noted for its delicate appearance and is well-suited for hanging baskets or as a border plant.
