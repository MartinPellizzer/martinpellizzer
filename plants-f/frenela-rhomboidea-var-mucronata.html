Frenela rhomboidea var. mucronata

Frenela rhomboidea var. mucronata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed fennel, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, aromatic leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces umbellate inflorescences with small white to pale yellow flowers, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its seeds are finely divided and have a mild, spicy aroma.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant for its delicate appearance and historical use in traditional medicine.
