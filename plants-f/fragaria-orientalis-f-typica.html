Fragaria orientalis f. typica

Fragaria orientalis f. typica, commonly known as the Oriental strawberry, is a cultivated form of the wild strawberry species.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its flavor and is often grown in gardens and small-scale farms.
