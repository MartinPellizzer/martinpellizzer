Filago faurei

Filago faurei, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun. It produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Filago faurei is often used in rock gardens and is valued for its ornamental beauty and drought tolerance.
