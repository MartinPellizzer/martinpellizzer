Foenugraecum

Foenugraecum, commonly known as fennel, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its feathery, finely divided leaves and yellow flowers that form umbrella-like clusters. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. Fennel contains essential oils, particularly anethole, which contribute to its aromatic properties.

It is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in cooking and traditional medicine.
