Foeniculum capillaceum proles dulce

Foeniculum capillaceum proles dulce, commonly known as sweet fennel, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It features feathery, finely divided leaves and produces small, yellow flowers in umbels. The plant is characterized by its aromatic seeds, which are widely used in cooking and traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, often growing in disturbed areas or along roadsides.

Its botanical structure supports its use as both a culinary spice and a medicinal herb.
