Falkia abyssinica

Falkia abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian thistle, is a flowering plant native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its spiny leaves and yellow flowers, which bloom in the summer months. The plant grows in rocky and arid environments, adapting to the harsh conditions of its native habitat. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers.

Falkia abyssinica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
