Ferula grigoriewii var. badachschanica

Ferula grigoriewii var. badachschanica, commonly known as the Badakhshan ferula, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and often hollow stems. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated margins, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

This species is valued for its medicinal properties and is sometimes used in traditional herbal remedies.
