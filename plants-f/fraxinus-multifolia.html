Fraxinus multifolia

Fraxinus multifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ash, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 6 to 12 inches long and have multiple leaflets. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
