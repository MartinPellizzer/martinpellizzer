Fumaria aegyptiaca

Fumaria aegyptiaca, commonly known as Egyptian smokeplant, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region and parts of Africa.

It is a herbaceous annual that produces clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in loose, terminal spikes. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Fumaria aegyptiaca is known for its ability to attract beneficial insects and is sometimes used in ecological gardening.
