Ferocactus setispinus

Ferocactus setispinus, commonly known as the spiked hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in prominent, sharp spines. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its rugged appearance. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, enabling it to thrive in harsh desert conditions.

The species is valued in horticulture for its unique appearance and hardy nature.
