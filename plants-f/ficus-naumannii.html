Ficus naumannii

Ficus naumannii, commonly known as the Naumann's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

Ficus naumannii is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
