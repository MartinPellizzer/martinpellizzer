Fatoua lanceolata

Fatoua lanceolata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid regions. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces bright yellow flowers with a distinctive central disk. It is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.

Fatoua lanceolata is also valued as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and blooms.
