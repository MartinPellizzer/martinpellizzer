Ficinia filiformis f. brevifolia

Ficinia filiformis f. brevifolia, commonly known as the dwarf thread fern, is a compact, low-growing fern species characterized by its delicate, thread-like fronds.

It typically features small, narrow leaves that are arranged in a dense, mat-like structure, making it well-suited for shaded, moist environments. The plant thrives in humid conditions and requires well-draining, acidic soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture for its aesthetic appeal and ability to cascade from containers or grow in rock gardens.

This fern is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is valued for its ability to thrive in shaded, humid settings.
