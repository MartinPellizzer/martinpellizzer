Ficus elliotiana

Ficus elliotiana, commonly known as Elliot's fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and distinctive fig fruits. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor environments. It prefers warm, humid conditions and well-draining soil.

Ficus elliotiana is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
