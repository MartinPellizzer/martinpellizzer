Fuchsia coriaceifolia

Fuchsia coriaceifolia, commonly known as the tough-leaved fuchsia, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that provide drought resistance. The plant produces striking clusters of bright pink to red flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
