Festuca richardsonii

Festuca richardsonii, commonly known as Richardson's fescue, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils, often found in alpine and arid environments. Its flowers form dense, spike-like inflorescences that are often blue to purple in color.

Festuca richardsonii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging conditions.
