Festuca arietina

Festuca arietina, commonly known as the Armenian grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. Festuca arietina is known for its hardiness and ability to thrive in harsh climates.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
