Fuisa

Fuisa, commonly known as the "flower of the forest," is a rare and elusive plant found in the dense, humid undergrowth of tropical rainforests.

It is characterized by its delicate, waxy leaves that shimmer with a unique iridescent sheen. The plant produces small, pale-yellow flowers that bloom only during the night, releasing a subtle, sweet fragrance. Its roots are deeply intertwined with the surrounding vegetation, forming a symbiotic relationship with fungi.

Due to its rarity and fragile habitat, the fuisa is considered a symbol of ecological balance and biodiversity.
