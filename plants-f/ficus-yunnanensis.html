Ficus yunnanensis

Ficus yunnanensis, commonly known as the Yunnan fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to China, particularly in the Yunnan province, and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible figs that are typically green when mature. Ficus yunnanensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

Its growth habit is typically shrub-like or small tree-like, making it suitable for both indoor and outdoor environments.
