Fagus sylvatica var. colorata

Fagus sylvatica var. colorata, commonly known as the silver birch, is a variant of the European white birch.

It is characterized by its striking white, peeling bark that reveals a silver inner layer. The leaves are ovate with serrated edges and turn yellow in the autumn. This tree prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in temperate climates.
