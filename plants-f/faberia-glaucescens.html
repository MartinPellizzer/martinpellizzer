Faberia glaucescens

Faberia glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green faberia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with a distinctive bluish-green hue, which gives it its common name. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Faberia glaucescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding vegetation.
