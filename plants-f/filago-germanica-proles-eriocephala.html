Filago germanica proles eriocephala

Filago germanica proles eriocephala, commonly known as the common filago, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves that are typically lance-shaped with serrated edges. It is native to Europe and thrives in dry, open habitats such as roadsides and waste areas.

The fruit is a small, dry achene that is often dispersed by wind.
