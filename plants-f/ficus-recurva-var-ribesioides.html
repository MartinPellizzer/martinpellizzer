Ficus recurva var. ribesioides

Ficus recurva var. ribesioides, commonly known as the climbing fig or ribesioides fig, is a tropical evergreen vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically yellow or red in color. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil and partial to full shade.

Ficus recurva var. ribesioides is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and ability to climb structures.
