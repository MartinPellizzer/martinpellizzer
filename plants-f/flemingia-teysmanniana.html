Flemingia teysmanniana

Flemingia teysmanniana, commonly known as the Java flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, orange-red flowers. The plant is known for its rapid growth and is often used in ornamental landscaping. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil.

The tree produces small, pod-like fruits that contain seeds dispersed by wind.
