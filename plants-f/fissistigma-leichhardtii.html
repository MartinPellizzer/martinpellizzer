Fissistigma leichhardtii

Fissistigma leichhardtii, commonly known as the Leichhardt's fissistigma, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that help it access water from deeper soil layers.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for local wildlife in its native range.
