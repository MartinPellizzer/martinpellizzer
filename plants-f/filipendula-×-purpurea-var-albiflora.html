Filipendula × purpurea var. albiflora

Filipendula × purpurea var. albiflora, commonly known as white false indigo, is a hybrid perennial plant native to eastern North America.

It features showy, fluffy white to pale pink flower heads that appear in late spring to early summer. The plant has a tall, upright growth habit with finely cut, fern-like leaves that are aromatic when crushed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
