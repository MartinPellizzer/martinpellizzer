Fritillaria tesselata var. alba

Fritillaria tesselata var. alba, commonly known as the white tesselated fritillary, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive pattern resembling a mosaic. The plant produces a cluster of bulbous structures that serve as storage organs for nutrients. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often forming a basal rosette.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
