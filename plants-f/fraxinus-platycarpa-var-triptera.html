Fraxinus platycarpa var. triptera

Fraxinus platycarpa var. triptera, commonly known as the three-winged ash, is a variant of the common ash tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive three-winged samaras, which are its seed structures. The leaves are typically opposite, simple, and have a serrated margin. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits.
