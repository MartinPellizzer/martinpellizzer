Festuca xanthina subsp. neicevii

Festuca xanthina subsp.

neicevii, commonly known as the yellow feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and distinctive golden-yellow inflorescence.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue, and it produces small, spike-like flower clusters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and aesthetic qualities.
