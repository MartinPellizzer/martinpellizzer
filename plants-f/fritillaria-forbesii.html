Fritillaria forbesii

Fritillaria forbesii, commonly known as the Forbes' fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red, often with contrasting patterns. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Fritillaria forbesii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
