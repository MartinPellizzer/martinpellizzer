Ferolia mobola

Ferolia mobola, commonly known as the African starfruit tree, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its aromatic leaves and yellow-orange fruits. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and is adapted to sandy, well-drained soils. Its fruit, similar to a starfruit, is edible and has a tangy, acidic flavor.

Ferolia mobola is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
