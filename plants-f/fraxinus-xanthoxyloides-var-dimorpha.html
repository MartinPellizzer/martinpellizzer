Fraxinus xanthoxyloides var. dimorpha

Fraxinus xanthoxyloides var. dimorpha, commonly known as the yellow-ash variant, is a hybrid species within the Oleaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellowish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. This plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. It is native to the eastern United States and is known for its ornamental value in landscaping.

The species exhibits dimorphic leaves, with the upper surface being smooth and the lower surface being pubescent, a feature that aids in its identification.
