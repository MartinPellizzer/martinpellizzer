Ficus lacor var. cunninghamii

Ficus lacor var. cunninghamii, commonly known as the Cunningham's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in rainforest or wetland habitats.

This varietal is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
