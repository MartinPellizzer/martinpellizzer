Fraxinus standleyi var. lasia

Fraxinus standleyi var. lasia, commonly known as the Mexican ash, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in pendulous clusters, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
