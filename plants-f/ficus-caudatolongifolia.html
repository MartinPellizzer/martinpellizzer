Ficus caudatolongifolia

Ficus caudatolongifolia, commonly known as the long-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It produces small, clustered fruit that is edible and often used in local cuisines.

Ficus caudatolongifolia is typically grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
