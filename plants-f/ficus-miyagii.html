Ficus miyagii

Ficus miyagii, commonly known as the Miyagi fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the forests of Japan, particularly the islands of Honshu and Shikoku. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. It produces small, fig-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional Japanese cuisine.

Ficus miyagii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
