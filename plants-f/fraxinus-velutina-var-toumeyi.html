Fraxinus velutina var. toumeyi

Fraxinus velutina var. toumeyi, commonly known as the Mexican ash, is a variant of the velvet ash native to Mexico.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, light-gray bark and opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The flowers are small and greenish-yellow, appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a samara with two wings. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry to moist forests.

This variant is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
