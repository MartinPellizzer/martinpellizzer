Fernandezia bryophyta

Fernandezia bryophyta, commonly known as the moss fern, is a type of bryophyte that belongs to the class Bryopsida.

It is characterized by its simple, leaf-like structures called phyllids, which are typically arranged in a spiral pattern around the stem. This plant lacks true roots, stems, and leaves in the traditional sense, instead having structures that serve similar functions. Fernandezia bryophyta thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found on rocks or soil in temperate regions.

Its reproductive structures, known as capsules, release spores that contribute to its life cycle and dispersal.
