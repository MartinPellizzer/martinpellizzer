Fagonia chilensis

Fagonia chilensis, commonly known as the Chilean fagonia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Chile.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and coastal environments, featuring succulent leaves that store water. Its flowers bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Fagonia chilensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
