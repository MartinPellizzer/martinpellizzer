Ferula flabelliloba

Ferula flabelliloba, commonly known as the Kashmiri ferula, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its tall, slender stems and finely divided leaves.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, particularly in India, Pakistan, and Afghanistan. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbrella-like inflorescences, typical of the carrot family. Its aromatic leaves and roots contain volatile oils, which have been used in traditional medicine.

Ferula flabelliloba is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in alpine ecosystems.
