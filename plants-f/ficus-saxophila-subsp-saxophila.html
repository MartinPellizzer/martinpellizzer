Ficus saxophila subsp. saxophila

Ficus saxophila subsp.

saxophila, commonly known as the rock fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family. It is native to arid and rocky regions of southern Africa, particularly Namibia and South Africa.

This plant is characterized by its small, dark green leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and can tolerate high levels of sunlight and drought.

The rock fig produces small, clustered fruits that are typically consumed by various bird species.
