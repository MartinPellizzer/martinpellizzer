Ficinia bicolor

Ficinia bicolor, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that typically reaches a height of about 10 to 20 centimeters. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are usually leafless. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, often blooming in late spring to early summer.

Ficinia bicolor is adapted to dry, sandy soils and is often found in grassland habitats.
