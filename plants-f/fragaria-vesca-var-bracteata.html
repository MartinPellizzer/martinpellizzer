Fragaria vesca var. bracteata

Fragaria vesca var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteated strawberry, is a variant of the woodland strawberry.

It is characterized by its small, edible fruits that are typically red and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and grows in shaded, moist environments. It is a perennial species that thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forest understories.

Its flowers are white with five petals and are followed by the distinctive, small berries that are a feature of this botanical variety.
