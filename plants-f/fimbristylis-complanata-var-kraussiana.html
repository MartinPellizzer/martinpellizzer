Fimbristylis complanata var. kraussiana

Fimbristylis complanata var. kraussiana, commonly known as the flat-leaved sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a tight rosette, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense mats, which can help stabilize soil.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to thrive in waterlogged conditions.
