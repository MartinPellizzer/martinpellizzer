Ficus barombiensis

Ficus barombiensis, commonly known as the Barombi Ficus, is a species of fig tree native to Cameroon.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in its native region.
