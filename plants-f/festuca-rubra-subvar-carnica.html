Festuca rubra subvar. carnica

Festuca rubra subvar. carnica, commonly known as red fescue, is a cool-season grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, dense foliage and a bluish-green color, with reddish tints on the leaves and stems. This variety is often used in lawns and turfgrass due to its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Festuca rubra subvar. carnica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators and wildlife.
