Ficus microstoma

Ficus microstoma, commonly known as the small-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, glossy, dark green leaves that are usually less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by edible, round figs that are typically less than an inch in diameter. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus microstoma is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its compact size and attractive foliage.
