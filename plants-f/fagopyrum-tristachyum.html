Fagopyrum tristachyum

Fagopyrum tristachyum, commonly known as the common buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its fruit is a small, elongated capsule containing several seeds.

Fagopyrum tristachyum is often found in woodland edges, rocky soils, and disturbed areas.
