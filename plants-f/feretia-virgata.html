Feretia virgata

Feretia virgata, commonly known as the white-veined arrowroot, is a tropical plant belonging to the Araceae family.

It is native to Central and South America, particularly found in humid lowland areas. The plant features a rhizomatous stem system, with broad, heart-shaped leaves that have distinctive white veins. Its flowers are clustered in an inflorescence called a spadix, surrounded by a modified leaf called a spathe.

Feretia virgata is cultivated for its starchy tubers, which are used in various culinary applications.
