Ficus symphytifolia

Ficus symphytifolia, commonly known as the West African fig, is a tropical tree native to western Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit, a fig-like capsule, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
