Fragaria calycina

Fragaria calycina, commonly known as the Mexican strawberry, is a species of flowering plant in the Rosaceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its small, edible berries that are often compared to true strawberries. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are white with five petals and are followed by the distinctive red, juicy fruits.

Fragaria calycina is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in local cuisines.
