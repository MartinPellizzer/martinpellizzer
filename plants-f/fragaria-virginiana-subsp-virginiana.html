Fragaria virginiana subsp. virginiana

Fragaria virginiana subsp.

virginiana, commonly known as the wild strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America. It produces small, edible berries that are typically red when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor.

The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets and runners that spread along the ground. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a popular species for both ecological studies and culinary use.
