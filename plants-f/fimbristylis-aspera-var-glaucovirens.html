Fimbristylis aspera var. glaucovirens

Fimbristylis aspera var. glaucovirens, commonly known as the bluish-green flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating, giving them a bluish-green appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks.

Its botanical structure is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils and high humidity.
