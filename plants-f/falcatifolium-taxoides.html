Falcatifolium taxoides

Falcatifolium taxoides, commonly known as the broad-leafed mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that attaches itself to the branches of host trees, primarily in the rainforest canopy.

It lacks true roots and absorbs water and nutrients through its stems and leaves, which are adapted for this parasitic lifestyle. The plant produces small, white or pale green berries that are often consumed by birds, aiding in seed dispersal. Its leaves are broad and flat, with a leathery texture, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Falcatifolium taxoides plays a role in forest ecosystems by providing food for various wildlife species.
