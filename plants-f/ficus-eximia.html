Ficus eximia

Ficus eximia, commonly known as the eximia fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus eximia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
