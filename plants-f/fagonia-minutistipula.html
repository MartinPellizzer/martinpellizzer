Fagonia minutistipula

Fagonia minutistipula, commonly known as the small-tipped fagonia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of South America.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae, which includes cacti and related plants. The plant is characterized by its spiny stems and small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red. It has adapted to survive in harsh environments with minimal water availability.

Fagonia minutistipula is often used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
