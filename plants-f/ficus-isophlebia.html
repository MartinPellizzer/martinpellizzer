Ficus isophlebia

Ficus isophlebia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus isophlebia is popular as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
