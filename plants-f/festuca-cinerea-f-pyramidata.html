Festuca cinerea f. pyramidata

Festuca cinerea f. pyramidata, commonly known as silver hairgrass, is a grass species characterized by its silvery-blue foliage and compact, tufted growth habit.

It is a low-growing perennial that forms dense mats, often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. The leaves are narrow, wiry, and covered in fine, silky hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous spikelets that are typically arranged in clusters along the stems.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
