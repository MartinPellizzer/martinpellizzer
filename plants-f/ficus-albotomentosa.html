Ficus albotomentosa

Ficus albotomentosa, commonly known as the white-tomentose fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that are covered in a dense layer of white, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
