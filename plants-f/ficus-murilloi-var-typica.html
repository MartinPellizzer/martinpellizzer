Ficus murilloi var. typica

Ficus murilloi var. typica, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, upright shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires consistent moisture and moderate temperatures.

Due to its air-purifying properties, it is a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
