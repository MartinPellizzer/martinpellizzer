Filago gallica var. nana

Filago gallica var. nana, commonly known as dwarf Gallica fleabane, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe.

It features slender, hairy stems that often lie close to the ground, forming a dense mat. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.
