Frangula henryi

Frangula henryi, commonly known as Henry's buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces dark blue berries that are edible for birds and some mammals. It thrives in woodland edges and moist soils, and is valued for its ornamental qualities.
