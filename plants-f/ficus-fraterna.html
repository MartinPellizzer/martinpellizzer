Ficus fraterna

Ficus fraterna, commonly known as the sacred fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, gray bark. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible figs, which are encased in a spherical, fuzzy husk. Ficus fraterna is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also revered in various cultural and religious practices.

Its ability to thrive in humid climates makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
