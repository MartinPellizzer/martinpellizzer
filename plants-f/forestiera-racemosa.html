Forestiera racemosa

Forestiera racemosa, commonly known as the fragrant woodbine, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters along the stems, typically in late spring. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are opposite in arrangement. Its woody stems are often covered with a pale, powdery bloom.

Forestiera racemosa is valued in gardens for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
