Fragaria virginiana subsp. grayana

Fragaria virginiana subsp.

grayana, commonly known as the grayan strawberry, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America. It produces small, edible berries that are typically red when ripe, though they may sometimes appear pink or white.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and a shallow root system. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and shaded areas.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
