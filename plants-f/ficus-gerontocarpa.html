Ficus gerontocarpa

Ficus gerontocarpa, commonly known as the ancient fig tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, featuring broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically harvested when mature. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, often used in traditional herbal remedies.

The tree thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
