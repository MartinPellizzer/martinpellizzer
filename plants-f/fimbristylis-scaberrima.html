Fimbristylis scaberrima

Fimbristylis scaberrima, commonly known as the rough sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps and seasonally flooded areas. Fimbristylis scaberrima is adapted to tolerate waterlogged soils and plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
