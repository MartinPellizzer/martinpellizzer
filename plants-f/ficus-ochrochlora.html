Ficus ochrochlora

Ficus ochrochlora, commonly known as the yellow-veined fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly found in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinct yellow veins, which give it its common name. It produces small, edible figs that are typically green when mature, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Ficus ochrochlora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
