Ficus fasciculata var. subopposita

Ficus fasciculata var. subopposita, commonly known as the subopposite fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, clustered leaves that grow in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant typically has a compact growth habit with short, sturdy branches. Its leaves are usually glossy and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture.

It is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
