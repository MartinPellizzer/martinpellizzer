Festuca cabrerae

Festuca cabrerae, commonly known as Cabrera's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Festuca cabrerae is adapted to Mediterranean climates and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Its flowering spikes are usually short and compact, producing small, spikelet-like flowers that are characteristic of the Poaceae family.
