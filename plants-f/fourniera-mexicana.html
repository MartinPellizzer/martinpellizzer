Fourniera mexicana

Fourniera mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its showy, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. The plant has a fast growth rate and can reach heights of up to 20 meters, making it a popular ornamental species. Its leaves are compound, consisting of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds.

Fourniera mexicana thrives in warm, sunny climates and is often used in landscaping for its vibrant floral display.
