Filago murcica

Filago murcica, commonly known as the Murcian filago, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates. Its flowers are small and yellow, arranged in a cluster that resembles a dandelion.

Filago murcica is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
