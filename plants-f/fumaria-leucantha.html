Fumaria leucantha

Fumaria leucantha, commonly known as white smokeplant, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically pale yellow to white, and the plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small seeds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its purported healing properties.
