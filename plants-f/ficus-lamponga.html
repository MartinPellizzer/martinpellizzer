Ficus lamponga

Ficus lamponga, commonly known as the red-veined ficus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent red veins, which give it a striking visual appearance. The plant is evergreen and can grow as a shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 6 meters. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Ficus lamponga is relatively low maintenance, though it requires consistent moisture and moderate temperatures to thrive.
