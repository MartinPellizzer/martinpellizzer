Fragosa cladorrhiza

Fragosa cladorrhiza, commonly known as the clustered fragrant shrub, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, branching structure and aromatic leaves that emit a subtle fragrance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale pink, typically blooming in the spring. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

Fragosa cladorrhiza is often used in ornamental gardening for its visual appeal and pleasant scent.
