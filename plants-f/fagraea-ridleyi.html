Fagraea ridleyi

Fagraea ridleyi, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves and striking clusters of white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces small, round, fleshy fruits that are edible and often consumed by birds and mammals. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests and secondary growth areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in some regions.
