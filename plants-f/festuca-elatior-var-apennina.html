Festuca elatior var. apennina

Festuca elatior var. apennina, commonly known as the Apennine tall fescue, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tall, slender blades and dense growth habit, often forming a fine, lawn-like appearance. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its foliage is generally bright green, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences in the summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
