Fortunella hindsii var. chintou

Fortunella hindsii var. chintou, commonly known as the Chintou kumquat, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern China.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, citrus-like fruits. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that turn from green to orange-red as they mature. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, with a slightly serrated edge.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and edible fruit, which is often used in traditional Chinese cuisine.
