Ficus brittonii

Ficus brittonii, commonly known as the Britton's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the southwestern United States, particularly in Arizona and New Mexico, and thrives in arid and semi-arid environments. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs. Ficus brittonii is often found growing on rocky slopes or in dry woodlands, where it adapts well to poor soil conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an important species in its native habitat.
