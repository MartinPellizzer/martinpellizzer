Friedlandia myrtifolia

Friedlandia myrtifolia, commonly known as the yellow-flowered grevillea, is a shrub native to southern Australia.

It belongs to the Proteaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sunlight, making it suitable for cultivation in arid and semi-arid regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native gardens.
