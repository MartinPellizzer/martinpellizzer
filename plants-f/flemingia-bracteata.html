Flemingia bracteata

Flemingia bracteata, commonly known as the white senna, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Flemingia bracteata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in some regions.
