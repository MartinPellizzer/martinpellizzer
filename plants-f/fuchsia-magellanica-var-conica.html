Fuchsia magellanica var. conica

Fuchsia magellanica var. conica, commonly known as the conical fuchsia, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive conical shape, which is formed by its dense, upright growth habit. The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers in shades of red, purple, and pink during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in a fine silvery or grayish pubescence.

This variety is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a range of soil conditions.
