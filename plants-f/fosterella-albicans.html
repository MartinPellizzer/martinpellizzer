Fosterella albicans

Fosterella albicans, commonly known as the white fosterella, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green color. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers during the spring and early summer. Fosterella albicans is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained, acidic soils.

It is relatively low maintenance and suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.
