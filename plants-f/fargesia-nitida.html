Fargesia nitida

Fargesia nitida, commonly known as the silver fargesia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, arching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers in early spring, which are followed by purple-black berries. It is well-suited for shaded environments and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its elegant appearance.

Fargesia nitida is relatively low-maintenance and thrives in moist, well-drained soil.
