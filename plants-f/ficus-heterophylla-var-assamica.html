Ficus heterophylla var. assamica

Ficus heterophylla var. assamica, commonly known as the Assam fig or variegated ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including India and Myanmar, and is characterized by its distinctive variegated leaves with a mix of green, white, and yellow hues. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually alternate, ovate to elliptical in shape, and have a smooth, waxy texture.

The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers followed by fig-like fruits that are edible and attract various wildlife.
