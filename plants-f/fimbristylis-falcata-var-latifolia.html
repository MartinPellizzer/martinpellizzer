Fimbristylis falcata var. latifolia

Fimbristylis falcata var. latifolia, commonly known as broad-leaved fimbriate sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its grass-like appearance and rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces small, clustered spikes of flowers, typically in shades of green or brown. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, or along water bodies.

Its botanical structure supports its role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
