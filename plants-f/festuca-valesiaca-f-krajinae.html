Festuca valesiaca f. krajinae

Festuca valesiaca f. krajinae, commonly known as the Krajina form of alpine fescue, is a botanical variant native to high-altitude regions of the Alps.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dark green, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to greenish flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, harsh climates.

This form is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological traits and ecological significance in alpine ecosystems.
