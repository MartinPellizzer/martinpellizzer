Ficus pulvinata

Ficus pulvinata, commonly known as the round-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically rounded or heart-shaped. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. Its roots system is extensive, allowing it to thrive in various soil types.

Ficus pulvinata is also valued for its ability to purify air and its ornamental appeal in landscaping.
