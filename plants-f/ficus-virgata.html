Ficus virgata

Ficus virgata, commonly known as the Philippine fig tree, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus virgata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
