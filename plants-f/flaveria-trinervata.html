Flaveria trinervata

Flaveria trinervata, commonly known as the three-veined flaveria, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its leaves are linear to lance-shaped with three distinct veins, giving it its common name.

Flaveria trinervata is often used in ecological studies due to its adaptability and role in pollination networks.
