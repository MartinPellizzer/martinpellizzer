Festuca sulcata f. tricostata

Festuca sulcata f. tricostata, commonly known as the tricostate fescue, is a cool-season grass species characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive three-ribbed or tricostate structure on the leaf sheaths, which is a key identifying trait. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in temperate climates. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and erosion control.

This variety is also noted for its tolerance to drought and moderate foot traffic.
