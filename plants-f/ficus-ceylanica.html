Ficus ceylanica

Ficus ceylanica, commonly known as the Ceylon fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka and parts of southern India.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are often consumed by both humans and animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Ficus ceylanica is also valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local medicine.
