Ficus antandronarum subsp. antandronarum

Ficus antandronarum subsp.

antandronarum, commonly known as the Antandronarum fig, is a species native to Madagascar. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with glossy, dark green leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement.

The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature, though they may change color depending on the variety. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
