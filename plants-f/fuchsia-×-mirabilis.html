Fuchsia × mirabilis

Fuchsia × mirabilis, commonly known as the common fuchsia, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Onagraceae family.

It is native to South America and is characterized by its showy, pendulous flowers that come in a range of colors, including pink, purple, and red. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous shrub, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, while its stems are often woody at the base.

Fuchsia × mirabilis is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and is popular in gardens and as a cut flower.
