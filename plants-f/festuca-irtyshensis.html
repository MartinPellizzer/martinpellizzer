Festuca irtyshensis

Festuca irtyshensis, commonly known as the Irtysh fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually arranged in a panicle structure.

Festuca irtyshensis is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant in its native range.
