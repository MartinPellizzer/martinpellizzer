Filetia ridleyi

Filetia ridleyi, commonly known as the Malayan orchid, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, delicate flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb moisture from the surrounding soil.

Filetia ridleyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for horticultural purposes.
