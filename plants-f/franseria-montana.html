Franseria montana

Franseria montana, commonly known as the mountain franseria, is a flowering plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, with a yellow or white coloration.

Franseria montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
