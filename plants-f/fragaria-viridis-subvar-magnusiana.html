Fragaria viridis subvar. magnusiana

Fragaria viridis subvar. magnusiana, commonly known as the green alpine strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the alpine regions of Asia.

It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, green berries, which are often mistaken for true strawberries. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found at high altitudes. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and the plant has a fibrous root system.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and edible fruit.
