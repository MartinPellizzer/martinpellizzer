Fraxinus dentata

Fraxinus dentata, commonly known as the Japanese ash.

It is a deciduous tree native to Japan, characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras that disperse its seeds. Its bark is smooth and gray, becoming fissured with age, and it can grow up to 20 meters tall.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been introduced to other parts of the world for landscaping purposes.
