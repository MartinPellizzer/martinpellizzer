Fagonia latifolia var. virens

Fagonia latifolia var. virens, commonly known as the greenleaf fagonia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has adapted to survive in harsh environments with its deep root system and ability to store water in its leaves. It is often found growing on rocky slopes and in dry, well-drained soils.

The leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
