Festuca alpina var. subviolacea

Festuca alpina var. subviolacea, commonly known as alpine bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and dark green to bluish-gray foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear violet to blue flowers, giving it its common name. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Festuca alpina var. subviolacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
