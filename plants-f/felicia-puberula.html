Felicia puberula

Felicia puberula, commonly known as the silverleaf fleur-de-lis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial that produces small, yellow flowers with a distinctive white center. The plant has silver-gray foliage, which gives it its common name, and it is often cultivated for ornamental purposes. Felicia puberula thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

It is also known for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
