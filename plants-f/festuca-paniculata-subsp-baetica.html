Festuca paniculata subsp. baetica

Festuca paniculata subsp.

baetica, commonly known as the Iberian fescue, is a grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-bearing flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
