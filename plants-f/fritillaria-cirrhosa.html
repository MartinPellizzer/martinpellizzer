Fritillaria cirrhosa

Fritillaria cirrhosa, commonly known as the Himalayan fritillary, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or alpine environments. Its leaves are long and grass-like, and it produces a bulbous underground stem.

Fritillaria cirrhosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
