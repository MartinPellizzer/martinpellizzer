Festuca spectabilis

Festuca spectabilis, commonly known as blue grama, is a perennial grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and is often found in dry, sandy soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer, adding ornamental value to landscapes. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping.

Festuca spectabilis plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local wildlife.
