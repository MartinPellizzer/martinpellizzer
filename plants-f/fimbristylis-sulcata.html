Fimbristylis sulcata

Fimbristylis sulcata, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Australia.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds.

Fimbristylis sulcata is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to moist soil conditions.
