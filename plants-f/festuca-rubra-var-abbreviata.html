Festuca rubra var. abbreviata

Festuca rubra var. abbreviata, commonly known as red fescue, is a cool-season grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, dense foliage and tufted growth habit, with leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale to reddish flowers, which are often tinged with purple. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly used in turfgrass and erosion control due to its hardiness and adaptability.

Festuca rubra var. abbreviata is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
