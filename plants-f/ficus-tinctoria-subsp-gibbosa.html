Ficus tinctoria subsp. gibbosa

Ficus tinctoria subsp.

gibbosa, commonly known as the red-fig or crimson fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family. It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia.

The plant is characterized by its small, glossy, ovate leaves that are typically a deep green with a reddish tinge on the underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are usually red or purple when mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
