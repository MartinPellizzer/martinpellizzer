Ficus eumorpha

Ficus eumorpha, commonly known as the warty fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, warty, and often asymmetrical leaves, which are a distinguishing feature. It produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when mature, turning yellow or red.

Ficus eumorpha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
