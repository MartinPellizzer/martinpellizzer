Felicia angustifolia

Felicia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers with a central disk. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Felicia angustifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
