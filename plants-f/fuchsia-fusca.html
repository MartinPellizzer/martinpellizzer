Fuchsia fusca

Fuchsia fusca, commonly known as the dark fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes mountains in South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring woody stems and dense foliage. The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers that are typically deep purple to almost black in color, with five petals and a distinctive corolla. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a leathery texture, often covered in a fine pubescence.

Fuchsia fusca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its striking floral display.
