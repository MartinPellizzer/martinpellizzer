Filipendula multijuga subsp. yezoensis

Filipendula multijuga subsp.

yezoensis, commonly known as Yezo lady's mantle, is a perennial herb native to East Asia. It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which consists of rounded, overlapping leaves that form a dense, shield-like structure.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
