Fraxinus coriariifolia

Fraxinus coriariifolia, commonly known as the Chinese ash, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and its compound leaves, which are alternate and pinnately arranged. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate urban environments.
