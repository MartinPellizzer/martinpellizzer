Fagonia harerensis

Fagonia harerensis, commonly known as the Harer's fagonia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the Arabian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its succulent, often hairy stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Fagonia harerensis is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, storing water in its thick leaves and stems.

Its resilience makes it an important component of desert ecosystems.
