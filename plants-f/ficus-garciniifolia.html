Ficus garciniifolia

Ficus garciniifolia, commonly known as the Indian rubber plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are thick and waxy, contributing to its popular status as a houseplant. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by green, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate watering to maintain its health.

Its adaptability and attractive foliage make it a favored choice for indoor gardening.
