Fuchsia acynifolia

Fuchsia acynifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Onagraceae and is characterized by its pendulous clusters of showy, tubular flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters. Fuchsia acynifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
