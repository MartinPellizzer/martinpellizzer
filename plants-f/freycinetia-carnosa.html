Freycinetia carnosa

Freycinetia carnosa, commonly known as the red pandanus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often reddish in color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

Freycinetia carnosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
