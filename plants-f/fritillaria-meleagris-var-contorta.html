Fritillaria meleagris var. contorta

Fritillaria meleagris var. contorta, commonly known as the twisted crown bellflower, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep purple to yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a distinctive, twisted shape in its flowering stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique floral structure and seasonal blooming habit.
