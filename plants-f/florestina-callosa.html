Florestina callosa

Florestina callosa, commonly known as the roughleaf forestiera, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its rugged, gray-green foliage and small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has a compact growth habit and is often found in rocky soils and dry habitats. Its leaves are typically covered in fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

Florestina callosa is valued in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
