Ferula arabica

Ferula arabica, commonly known as the Persian cow's milk, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces a milky sap, which is used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils. Its roots are thick and fibrous, often harvested for their aromatic properties.

Ferula arabica thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, making it well adapted to harsh climates.
