Fragaria roxburghii

Fragaria roxburghii, commonly known as the Himalayan strawberry, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas, where it thrives in cool, temperate climates. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to common strawberries. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it has a shallow root system adapted to rocky soils.

Fragaria roxburghii is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its use in traditional medicine.
