Fagara dubia

Fagara dubia, commonly known as the dwarf soursop, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Anacardiaceae family, which includes other plants like the cashew and poison ivy. The plant is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that develop into round, spiny fruits. The fruit, though not commonly cultivated, contains a single large seed and has a mild, somewhat acidic flavor.

It is often found in moist, shaded areas of tropical forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
