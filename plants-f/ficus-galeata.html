Ficus galeata

Ficus galeata, commonly known as the Variegated Ficus, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white or cream-colored veins, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant.

Due to its adaptability, it is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for both novice and experienced gardeners.
