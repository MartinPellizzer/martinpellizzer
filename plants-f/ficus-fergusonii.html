Ficus fergusonii

Ficus fergusonii, commonly known as the Fergussen fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often dark green in color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits.

Ficus fergusonii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
