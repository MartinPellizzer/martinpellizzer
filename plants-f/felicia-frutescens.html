Felicia frutescens

Felicia frutescens, commonly known as the golden groundsel, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial herb characterized by its bright yellow flowers and fuzzy, silver-green leaves. The plant produces small, dandelion-like blooms that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Felicia frutescens is also valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
