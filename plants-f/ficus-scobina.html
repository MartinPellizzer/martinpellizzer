Ficus scobina

Ficus scobina, commonly known as the broad-leafed fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, China, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, clustered figs that are typically green or yellow when mature.

Ficus scobina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in humid environments.
