Ficus lepidosa var. martabanica

Ficus lepidosa var. martabanica, commonly known as the Martaban fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions like Myanmar and Thailand. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate in form. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible figs.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
