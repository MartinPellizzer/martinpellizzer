Frasera albicaulis var. modocensis

Frasera albicaulis var. modocensis, commonly known as the Modoc lily, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a rough texture.

It is a perennial species that blooms in late summer, adding ornamental value to its natural habitat.
