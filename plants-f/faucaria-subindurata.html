Faucaria subindurata

Faucaria subindurata, commonly known as the stone flower, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-forming growth habit and thick, fleshy leaves that store water. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red during the warmer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it ideal for arid environments.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
