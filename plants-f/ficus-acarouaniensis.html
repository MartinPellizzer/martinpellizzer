Ficus acarouaniensis

Ficus acarouaniensis, commonly known as the Cuban fig tree, is a species of fig tree native to Cuba.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, grayish-brown bark and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically pear-shaped. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
