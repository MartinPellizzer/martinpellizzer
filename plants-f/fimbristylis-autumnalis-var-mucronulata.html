Fimbristylis autumnalis var. mucronulata

Fimbristylis autumnalis var. mucronulata, commonly known as the narrowleaf flat sedge, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist soils. It has a compact growth habit and is often found in coastal or inland wetlands.

The species is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
