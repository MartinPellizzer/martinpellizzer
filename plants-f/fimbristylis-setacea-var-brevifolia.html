Fimbristylis setacea var. brevifolia

Fimbristylis setacea var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its flowering stems and spikelets. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with short, hair-like structures at the tips.
