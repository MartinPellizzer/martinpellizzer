Festuca tenuis

Festuca tenuis, commonly known as slender fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along streambanks. Festuca tenuis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping for its ability to thrive in a variety of conditions.

It is also an important component of ecological restoration projects due to its ecological versatility.
