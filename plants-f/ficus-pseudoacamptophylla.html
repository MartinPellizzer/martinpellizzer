Ficus pseudoacamptophylla

Ficus pseudoacamptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
