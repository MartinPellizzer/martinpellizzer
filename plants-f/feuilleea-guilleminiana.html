Feuilleea guilleminiana

Feuilleea guilleminiana, commonly known as the Guillemin's leaf plant, is a unique species native to the humid tropical forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, and waxy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and is often found near the forest floor. Its leaves are not only visually striking but also play a crucial role in its photosynthetic process.

Feuilleea guilleminiana is noted for its slow growth and is relatively rare in the wild.
