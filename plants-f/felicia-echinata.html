Felicia echinata

Felicia echinata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and thistles. The plant features bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central disk of smaller, tubular flowers. Its leaves are usually gray-green with a fuzzy texture, and it produces small, hairy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Felicia echinata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
