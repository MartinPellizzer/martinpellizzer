Festuca duriuscula subsp. trachyphylla

Festuca duriuscula subsp.

trachyphylla, commonly known as the rough fescue, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of the western United States. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It is well adapted to drought conditions, exhibiting deep root systems and a low water requirement.

Its hardy nature makes it a valuable component of native plant communities in its ecological range.
