Ficus pergamenea

Ficus pergamenea, commonly known as the paper fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.
