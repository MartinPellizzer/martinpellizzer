Ficus taiwaniana

Ficus taiwaniana, commonly known as the Taiwan fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Taiwan and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Ficus taiwaniana is also valued for its air-purifying properties and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
