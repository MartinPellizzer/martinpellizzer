Fimbristylis capillaris var. pilosa

Fimbristylis capillaris var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy threadgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The variety pilosa is distinguished by its hairy or pubescent surfaces, particularly on the stems and leaves. This plant typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps and seasonally flooded areas.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
