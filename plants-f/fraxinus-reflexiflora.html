Fraxinus reflexiflora

Fraxinus reflexiflora, commonly known as the reflexed ash, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, which are followed by winged seeds. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as mountain forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in its native ecosystems.
