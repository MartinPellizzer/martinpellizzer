Faba vulgaris var. viridis

Faba vulgaris var. viridis, commonly known as the green field bean, is a variety of the broad bean plant.

It is characterized by its green pods and seeds, which are typically smaller than those of the standard broad bean. This variety is often cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting subsequent crops.
