Ficus sphaerocarpa

Ficus sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, China, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its round, fleshy fruits that are typically green when immature and turn red or yellow when mature. Its leaves are usually alternate, simple, and have a glossy surface.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
