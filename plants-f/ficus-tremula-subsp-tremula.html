Ficus tremula subsp. tremula

Ficus tremula subsp.

tremula, commonly known as the trembling fig, is a deciduous tree native to parts of Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that turn red in the autumn, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible fig fruits, which are typically green when mature. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and its ability to provide habitat for various wildlife species.
