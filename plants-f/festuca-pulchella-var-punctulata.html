Festuca pulchella var. punctulata

Festuca pulchella var. punctulata, commonly known as the dotted mountain grass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
