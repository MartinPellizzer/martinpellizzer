Ferula meyeri

Ferula meyeri, commonly known as Meyer's giant fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and feathery, aromatic leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Its taproot is thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine.

Ferula meyeri is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it a resilient plant in harsh climates.
