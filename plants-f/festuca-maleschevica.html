Festuca maleschevica

Festuca maleschevica, commonly known as the Maleschevsky fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or rocky habitats.

Festuca maleschevica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
