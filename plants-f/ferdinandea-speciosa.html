Ferdinandea speciosa

Ferdinandea speciosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, velvety hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain numerous tiny seeds.

Ferdinandea speciosa is often found in disturbed habitats and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability.
