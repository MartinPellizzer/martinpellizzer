Fumaria pia

Fumaria pia, commonly known as the common fumitory, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fumariaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces slender, spiral-shaped pods that contain numerous tiny seeds. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides.

Its name derives from the Latin word "fumaria," referring to the plant's tendency to grow in smoky or polluted areas.
