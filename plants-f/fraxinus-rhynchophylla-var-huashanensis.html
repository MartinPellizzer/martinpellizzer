Fraxinus rhynchophylla var. huashanensis

Fraxinus rhynchophylla var. huashanensis, commonly known as the Huashan Ash, is a variant of the Chinese ash tree native to China.

It is characterized by its distinctive ovate to lanceolate leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, which are typically borne in the spring. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with shallow fissures, and it can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and parks for its aesthetic appeal.
