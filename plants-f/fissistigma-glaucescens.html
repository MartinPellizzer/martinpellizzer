Fissistigma glaucescens

Fissistigma glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on the branches of host trees.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers that bloom in the spring. Its berries are usually red or orange and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.

Fissistigma glaucescens is often found in temperate regions and can be a significant ecological factor in its host's ecosystem.
