Ficus tuerckheimii

Ficus tuerckheimii, commonly known as the Tuerckheim fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often clustered at the ends of its branches. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically yellow or green in color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.

Ficus tuerckheimii is valued for its air-purifying qualities and its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
