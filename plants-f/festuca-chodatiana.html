Festuca chodatiana

Festuca chodatiana, commonly known as Chodat's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Festuca chodatiana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil in harsh conditions.
