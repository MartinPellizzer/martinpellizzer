Felicia imbricata

Felicia imbricata, commonly known as the African daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies, sunflowers, and other similar plants. The plant features bright yellow flowers with a central disk surrounded by white petals, and it blooms throughout the year in mild climates. Felicia imbricata is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and vibrant floral display.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.
