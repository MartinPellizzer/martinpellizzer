Fagara robiginosa

Fagara robiginosa, commonly known as the red-leaf evergreen, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with a reddish tint on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers followed by elliptical, orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
