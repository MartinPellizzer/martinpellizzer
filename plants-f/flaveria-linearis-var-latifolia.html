Flaveria linearis var. latifolia

Flaveria linearis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf flaveria, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its linear, often hairy leaves and small yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and sandy soils. It is an annual species with a low-growing habit, reaching heights of up to 30 centimeters.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in ecological and taxonomic studies.
