Fargesia farcticaulis

Fargesia farcticaulis, commonly known as the snowbrush fargesia, is a species of bamboo native to the high-altitude regions of western China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and compact, evergreen foliage that remains lush throughout the year. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters during the summer months, which are typically white or pale pink in color. Its cold-hardy nature makes it well-suited for temperate climates and alpine gardens.

Fargesia farcticaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
