Ficus palustris

Ficus palustris, commonly known as the swamp fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the southeastern United States, particularly found in wetland habitats such as swamps and marshes. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered greenish-white flowers. It produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed by birds and other wildlife.

Ficus palustris plays an important ecological role in maintaining the biodiversity of its wetland environment.
