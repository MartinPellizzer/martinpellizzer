Ficus maguirei

Ficus maguirei, commonly known as the Maguire's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly found in countries like Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate in form. It produces small, clustered fruits known as figs, which are typically green when mature.

Ficus maguirei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
