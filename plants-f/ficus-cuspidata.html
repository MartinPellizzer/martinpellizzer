Ficus cuspidata

Ficus cuspidata, commonly known as the pointed-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, China, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate with a pointed tip. Ficus cuspidata produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
