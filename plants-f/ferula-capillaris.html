Ferula capillaris

Ferula capillaris, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ferula, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, yellowish-brown resinous sap. The plant features a tall, slender stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its most distinctive feature is its umbel-like inflorescence, which produces small, greenish-white flowers.

Ferula capillaris is often used in traditional medicine and is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
