Festuca procera

Festuca procera, commonly known as tall fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, upright growth habit and coarse, dark green foliage. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 100 centimeters and produces spikelets that are often clustered at the ends of the stems. Festuca procera is known for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

It is widely used in turfgrass and forage production due to its hardiness and adaptability.
