Ficus hispida f. obovifolia

Ficus hispida f. obovifolia, commonly known as the velvet fig or obovate-leaf fig, is a variant of the common ficus species.

It is characterized by its velvety, dark green leaves that are obovate in shape, with a pointed tip and a broad base. The plant is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in warm, humid environments. It has a short, thick stem and produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible figs.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and low maintenance requirements.
