Fraxinus excelsior subvar. aurea-pendula

Fraxinus excelsior subvar. aurea-pendula, commonly known as the golden weeping ash, is a striking variant of the common ash tree.

It is characterized by its pendulous branches and distinctive golden-yellow foliage that turns amber in autumn. The leaves are lance-shaped and have a soft, silky texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained, fertile soils.

This variety is often cultivated in parks and gardens for its aesthetic value and shade-providing capabilities.
