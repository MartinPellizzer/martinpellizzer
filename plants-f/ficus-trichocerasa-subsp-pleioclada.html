Ficus trichocerasa subsp. pleioclada

Ficus trichocerasa subsp.

pleioclada, commonly known as the pleioclada fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often veined with a lighter color.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically clustered in groups. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This subspecies is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
