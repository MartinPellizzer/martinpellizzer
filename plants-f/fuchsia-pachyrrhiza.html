Fuchsia pachyrrhiza

Fuchsia pachyrrhiza, commonly known as the rough-stemmed fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody, branched stems covered in a thick, rough texture. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, purple, and pink, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its unique appearance and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and botanical collections.
