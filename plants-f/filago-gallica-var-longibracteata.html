Filago gallica var. longibracteata

Filago gallica var. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract filago, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and linear, while its inflorescence consists of yellow flowers arranged in a flat head.

The variety is distinguished by its elongated bracts, which are often mistaken for leaves, giving it its common name.
