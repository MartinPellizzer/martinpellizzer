Festuca woronowii subsp. turcica

Festuca woronowii subsp.

turcica, commonly known as Turkish bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus. It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and produces small, blue-green flowers in the summer.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and is adapted to temperate climates. It has a clump-forming habit and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value. Festuca woronowii subsp.

turcica is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
