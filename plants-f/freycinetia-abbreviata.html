Freycinetia abbreviata

Freycinetia abbreviata, commonly known as the dwarf pandanus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its compact, rosette-like growth habit. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a distinctive waxy coating.

Freycinetia abbreviata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in well-drained, sandy soils.
