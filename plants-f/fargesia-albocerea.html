Fargesia albocerea

Fargesia albocerea, commonly known as the white-haired bamboo, is a species native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It is a member of the grass family, Poaceae, and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant features distinctive white or silver hairs on its leaves, giving it its common name. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its aesthetic appeal.

Fargesia albocerea is relatively low-maintenance and can be grown in USDA hardiness zones 6 to 8.
