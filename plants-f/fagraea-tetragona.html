Fagraea tetragona

Fagraea tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled fagraea, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, opposite leaves that are typically four-angled in cross-section. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers arranged in terminal panicles, which are followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. Its root system is fibrous and can become quite extensive, often making it a persistent weed in disturbed soils.

Fagraea tetragona is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
