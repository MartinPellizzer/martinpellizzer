Fumana vulgaris var. minor

Fumana vulgaris var. minor, commonly known as the lesser cow-wheat, is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, trifoliate leaves that are often hairy. The flowers are typically pale pink or white, arranged in clusters and blooming in late spring to early summer. This plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

It plays a role in supporting pollinators and is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
