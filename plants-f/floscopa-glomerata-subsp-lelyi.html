Floscopa glomerata subsp. lelyi

Floscopa glomerata subsp.

lelyi, commonly known as the Lely's clustered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
