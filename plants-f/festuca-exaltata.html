Festuca exaltata

Festuca exaltata, commonly known as bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and a bluish-green coloration, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Festuca exaltata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.

It is also an important forage plant for livestock in certain regions.
