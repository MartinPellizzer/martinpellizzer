Froelichia lanata f. roseiflora

Froelichia lanata f. roseiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered froelichia, is a holarctic plant species belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive rose-like inflorescences, which consist of dense clusters of small, pink to red flowers. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as roadsides and meadows, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and have a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its overall rugged appearance.

The species is noted for its ornamental value due to its showy floral display and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
