Ficus plectonervata

Ficus plectonervata, commonly known as the curly-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are deeply veined and often have a wavy or curled appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus plectonervata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.
