Fadogia stenophylla

Fadogia stenophylla, commonly known as African velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Fadogia stenophylla is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in agricultural settings.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
