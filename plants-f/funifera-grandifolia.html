Funifera grandifolia

Funifera grandifolia, commonly known as the giant fanflower, is a flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its large, showy, funnel-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often with a waxy texture.

Funifera grandifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
