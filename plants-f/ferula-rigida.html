Ferula rigida

Ferula rigida, commonly known as rigid fennel, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, compound leaves that are often aromatic. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots are thick and fleshy, and they contain volatile oils that contribute to its strong fragrance.

Ferula rigida is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its medicinal and aromatic properties.
