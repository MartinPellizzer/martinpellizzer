Ficus forstenii var. pacifica

Ficus forstenii var. pacifica, commonly known as the Pacific ficus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It features broad, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by green, round fruits that turn yellow or orange when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
