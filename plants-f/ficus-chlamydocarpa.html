Ficus chlamydocarpa

Ficus chlamydocarpa, commonly known as the white-veined fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves with prominent white veins that contrast with its dark green foliage. The plant produces small, greenish-white figs that are enclosed in a syconium, a unique structure typical of the Moraceae family. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus chlamydocarpa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to indoor conditions.
