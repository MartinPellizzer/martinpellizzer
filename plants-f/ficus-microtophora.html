Ficus microtophora

Ficus microtophora, commonly known as the small-leaved fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus microtophora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
