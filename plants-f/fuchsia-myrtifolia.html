Fuchsia myrtifolia

Fuchsia myrtifolia, commonly known as the myrtle-leaved fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep red to purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Fuchsia myrtifolia is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
