Ficus pseudotsiela

Ficus pseudotsiela, commonly known as the Philippine fig tree, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically clustered in spherical, greenish-pear-shaped syconia. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The plant is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
