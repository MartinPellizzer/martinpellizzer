Ficus sessilis

Ficus sessilis, commonly known as the broadleaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the mulberry family, Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Malaysia, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are often consumed by both humans and wildlife.

Ficus sessilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
