Fuchsia boliviana

Fuchsia boliviana, commonly known as the Bolivian fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep red to violet. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are typically dark green and glossy, with a slightly serrated edge.

Fuchsia boliviana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various growing conditions.
