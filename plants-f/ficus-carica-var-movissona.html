Ficus carica var. movissona

Ficus carica var. movissona, commonly known as the Movissona fig, is a variant of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring a unique combination of shape and venation patterns. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically harvested when they naturally detach from the tree. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and unique fruit characteristics.
