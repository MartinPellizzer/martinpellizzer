Fuchsia arborescens f. typica

Fuchsia arborescens f. typica, commonly known as the typical tree fuchsia, is a compact shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It features broad, ovate leaves with a smooth, glossy surface and a deep green color. The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers in shades of violet and red, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.

This variety is relatively hardy and thrives in well-drained soil with partial to full sun exposure.
