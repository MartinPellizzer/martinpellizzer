Faba vulgaris var. quisquilina

Faba vulgaris var. quisquilina, commonly known as the Spanish fava or broad bean, is a variety of the broad bean plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, flat, green pods that contain edible seeds used in various culinary traditions. The plant is an annual legume that thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its high nutritional value. Its leaves are trifoliate, and the plant has a robust root system that helps it fix nitrogen in the soil.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and is commonly grown in both home gardens and agricultural settings.
