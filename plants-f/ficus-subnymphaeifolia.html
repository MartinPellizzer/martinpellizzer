Ficus subnymphaeifolia

Ficus subnymphaeifolia, commonly known as the silver-veined fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent silver veins that give it its distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.

The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, and the plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are edible.
