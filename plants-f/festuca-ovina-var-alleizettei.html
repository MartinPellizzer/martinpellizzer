Festuca ovina var. alleizettei

Festuca ovina var. alleizettei, commonly known as the Alleizette sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and steppe environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in harsh climates.
