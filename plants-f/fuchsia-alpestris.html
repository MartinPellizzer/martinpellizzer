Fuchsia alpestris

Fuchsia alpestris, commonly known as the alpine fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from deep red to purple. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, such as mountainous regions, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, adapting to the harsh alpine climate.

Fuchsia alpestris is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
