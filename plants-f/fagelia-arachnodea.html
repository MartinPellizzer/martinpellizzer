Fagelia arachnodea

Fagelia arachnodea, commonly known as the spider tree, is a unique plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its long, slender, thread-like roots that can extend several meters underground, giving it its common name. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its woody stem and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. Its leaves are typically opposite and ovate, with a slightly hairy texture.

Fagelia arachnodea is often found in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance.
