Fargesia setosa

Fargesia setosa, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a cold-hardy evergreen shrub native to China.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant features silvery-gray leaves that are narrow and slightly hairy, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, pale green flower clusters in the spring, which are followed by fluffy seeds.

Fargesia setosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded, moist environments.
