Forsythiopsis linifolia

Forsythiopsis linifolia, commonly known as the Chinese forsythia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the family Oleaceae and is characterized by its dense, wiry stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional Chinese medicine.
