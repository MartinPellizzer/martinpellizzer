Fritillaria assyriaca subsp. melananthera

Fritillaria assyriaca subsp.

melananthera, commonly known as the black-throated fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East. It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange.

The subspecies is distinguished by its dark, often purplish throat, which contrasts with the lighter petals. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded conditions.

This plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
