Fabiana deserticola

Fabiana deserticola, commonly known as the desert lily, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of South America.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color, blooming in late spring or early summer. Its leaves are narrow and succulent, helping to store water and reduce transpiration.

Fabiana deserticola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in xeriscapes due to its low water requirements.
