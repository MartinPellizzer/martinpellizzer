Ficus coriacea

Ficus coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and often consumed by various animals. Ficus coriacea is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
