Festuca × loliacea var. aristata

Festuca × loliacea var. aristata, commonly known as Arctic meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to cold climates.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, typically blooming in late summer. It is well adapted to harsh environments, including alpine and tundra regions.

Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil in ecologically sensitive areas.
