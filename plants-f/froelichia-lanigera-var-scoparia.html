Froelichia lanigera var. scoparia

Froelichia lanigera var. scoparia, commonly known as the silky froelichia, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that gives it a soft, velvety appearance. The plant produces small, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in whorls around the stems.

This variant is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks.
