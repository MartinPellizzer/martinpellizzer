Flaveria vaginata

Flaveria vaginata, commonly known as the silverleaf flaveria, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense pubescence covering the leaves and stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers arranged in clusters, typically blooming from late spring to early summer. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Flaveria vaginata is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is known for its rapid growth and ability to colonize open areas.
