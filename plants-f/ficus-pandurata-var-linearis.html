Ficus pandurata var. linearis

Ficus pandurata var. linearis, commonly known as the linear-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits known as figs. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Ficus pandurata var. linearis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor conditions.
