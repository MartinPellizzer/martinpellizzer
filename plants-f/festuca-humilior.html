Festuca humilior

Festuca humilior, commonly known as the dwarf fescue, is a low-growing grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths that are often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas. The plant has slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually bluish-green in color. It is well adapted to withstand drought and grazing, making it a resilient species in various ecological settings.

Festuca humilior plays an important role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
