Fumaria agraria var. subalata

Fumaria agraria var. subalata, commonly known as the alpine yellowweed, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are slender and branching.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant color and unique appearance.
