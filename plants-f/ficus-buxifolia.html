Ficus buxifolia

Ficus buxifolia, commonly known as the silver ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green leaves that have a slightly waxy texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Ficus buxifolia is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and attractive appearance.
