Ficus compressicaulis

Ficus compressicaulis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including parts of India and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. Ficus compressicaulis produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible figs, though the fruit is often not cultivated for consumption.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
