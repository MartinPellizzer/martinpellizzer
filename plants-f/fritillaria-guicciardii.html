Fritillaria guicciardii

Fritillaria guicciardii, commonly known as the Italian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or yellow with a distinctive black or purple blotch at the base. The plant is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or dry habitats. It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

Fritillaria guicciardii is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
