Fitchia cordata

Fitchia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf fitchia, is a unique plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale pink, which bloom in the spring. Fitchia cordata thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sun exposure.

Its ornamental value makes it a sought-after plant for botanical gardens and specialized collections.
