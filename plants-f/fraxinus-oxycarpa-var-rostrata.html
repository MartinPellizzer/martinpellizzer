Fraxinus oxycarpa var. rostrata

Fraxinus oxycarpa var. rostrata, commonly known as the rostrated ash, is a deciduous tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated fruits that resemble a beak, hence the specific epithet "rostrata.". The leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have a serrated margin. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
