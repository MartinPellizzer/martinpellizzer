Ficus pycnoneura

Ficus pycnoneura, commonly known as the silver fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Australia, particularly the northern and eastern parts. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery or whitish underside, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Ficus pycnoneura is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
