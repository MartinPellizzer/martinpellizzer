Freycinetia coagmentata

Freycinetia coagmentata, commonly known as the red-flowered pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its striking red bracts that surround the small, yellow flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often have a fragrant, lemony scent.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
