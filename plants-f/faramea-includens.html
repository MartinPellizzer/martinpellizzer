Faramea includens

Faramea includens, commonly known as the African flame plant, is a tropical shrub native to regions of Africa, particularly in the Congo Basin.

It is characterized by its striking, brightly colored inflorescences that range from red to orange, which are borne on long, branching stalks. The plant has opposite, simple, and often velvety leaves that are dark green in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Faramea includens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its vibrant floral displays.
