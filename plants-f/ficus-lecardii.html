Ficus lecardii

Ficus lecardii, commonly known as the lecardii fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia, and thrives in tropical climates. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate in form. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus lecardii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
