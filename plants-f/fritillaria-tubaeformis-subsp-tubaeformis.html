Fritillaria tubaeformis subsp. tubaeformis

Fritillaria tubaeformis subsp.

tubaeformis, commonly known as the Turk's-cap lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region. It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or yellow with a contrasting purple or black rim.

The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature contribute to its ornamental value in horticulture.
