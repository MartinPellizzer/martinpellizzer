Fagraea auriculata

Fagraea auriculata, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a dazzling display during the flowering season. The plant has glossy, dark green, elongated leaves that provide a lush backdrop to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.

The tree is also valued for its hard, heavy wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
