Frangula purshiana subsp. purshiana

Frangula purshiana subsp.

purshiana, commonly known as the whitebeard buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dark, ridged bark and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant produces elongated, ribbed fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a sticky, resinous coating. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important component of its native ecosystem.
