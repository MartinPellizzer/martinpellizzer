Foeniculum vulgare var. sativum

Foeniculum vulgare var. sativum, commonly known as cultivated fennel, is a biennial or perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features feathery, finely divided leaves and yellow umbels of small flowers. The plant produces a strong anise-like aroma due to its high concentration of volatile oils, particularly anethol. Its seeds are often used in culinary and medicinal applications.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
