Fragaria viridis subvar. flagellifera

Fragaria viridis subvar. flagellifera, commonly known as the green strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and runners that allow it to spread vegetatively. Its flowers are white with five petals and are pollinated by insects.

The fruit is valued for its tart flavor and is often used in culinary applications.
