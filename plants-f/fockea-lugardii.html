Fockea lugardii

Fockea lugardii, commonly known as the Cape fockea, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Aizoaceae family, which is characterized by its fleshy leaves and adaptations to arid environments. The plant typically grows as a low-growing shrub with cylindrical stems and dense clusters of blue-green leaves. It produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and white, often blooming in spring.

Due to its unique appearance and drought tolerance, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
