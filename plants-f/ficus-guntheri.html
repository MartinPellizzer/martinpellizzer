Ficus guntheri

Ficus guntheri, commonly known as the Gunther's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are often consumed by local wildlife. Ficus guntheri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers indirect sunlight.
