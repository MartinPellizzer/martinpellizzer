Ficus microsphaera

Ficus microsphaera, commonly known as the dwarf fig or small sphere fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and unique fruit structure. The plant typically grows as an understory shrub, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are usually glossy and dark green, with a slightly waxy texture.

The fruit, which is small and round, is edible and has a sweet, fig-like flavor.
