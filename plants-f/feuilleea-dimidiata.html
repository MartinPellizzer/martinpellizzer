Feuilleea dimidiata

Feuilleea dimidiata, commonly known as the half-leaf plant, is a unique species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, with leaves that are partially divided into two distinct lobes, giving it its scientific name. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its leaves are often a vibrant green, with some cultivars displaying variegated patterns.

Feuilleea dimidiata is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ease of care in the right conditions.
