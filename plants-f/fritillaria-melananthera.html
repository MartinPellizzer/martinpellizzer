Fritillaria melananthera

Fritillaria melananthera, commonly known as the black-flowered fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically a deep purple or black, with a contrasting yellow throat. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, stout stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Fritillaria melananthera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
