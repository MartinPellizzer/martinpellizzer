Festuca rubra subsp. diversifolia

Festuca rubra subsp.

diversifolia, commonly known as variable red fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is also cultivated in gardens and used in ecological restoration projects.
