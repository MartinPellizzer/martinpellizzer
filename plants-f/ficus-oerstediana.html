Ficus oerstediana

Ficus oerstediana, commonly known as the Oersted's fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are enclosed in a tough, greenish husk. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Ficus oerstediana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
