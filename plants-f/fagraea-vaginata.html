Fagraea vaginata

Fagraea vaginata, commonly known as the golden apple or white star, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. Its fruits are edible and often used in traditional medicine, while its ornamental value makes it popular in horticulture.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
