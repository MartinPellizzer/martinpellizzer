Ficus tinctoria var. anastomosans

Ficus tinctoria var. anastomosans, commonly known as the red-veined fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive foliage, which features deep green leaves with prominent red veins. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in its natural habitat. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in traditional medicine.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated as a houseplant in many regions.
