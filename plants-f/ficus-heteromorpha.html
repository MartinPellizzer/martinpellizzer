Ficus heteromorpha

Ficus heteromorpha, commonly known as the variegated ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that often display striking variegation patterns of white, yellow, and green. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Ficus heteromorpha is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
