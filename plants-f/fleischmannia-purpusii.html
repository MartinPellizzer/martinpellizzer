Fleischmannia purpusii

Fleischmannia purpusii, commonly known as the desert globe cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its globular shape and spiny stems, which help it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual element to its desert habitat. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, and its shallow root system allows it to absorb moisture from the soil efficiently.

Fleischmannia purpusii is well adapted to harsh desert conditions and plays a role in supporting local wildlife.
