Ficus brevifolia

Ficus brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features broad, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically short and wide, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits, which are an important food source for various animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Ficus brevifolia is also valued for its ability to purify air and adapt well to indoor environments.
