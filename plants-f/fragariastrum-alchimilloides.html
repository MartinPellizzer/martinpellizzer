Fragariastrum alchimilloides

Fragariastrum alchimilloides, commonly known as the alchemilla strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of serrated, hairy leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in early summer, followed by red, strawberry-like fruits. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in gardens and naturalized in various regions.
