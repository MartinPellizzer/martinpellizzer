Feuilleea leptoloba

Feuilleea leptoloba, commonly known as the slender-leaf fern, is a species of fern characterized by its delicate, elongated fronds.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. The plant features a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and colonize new areas. Its leaves are usually bipinnate, with a feathery appearance that contributes to its ornamental value.

Feuilleea leptoloba is valued in horticulture for its graceful foliage and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
