Feuilleea ligustrina

Feuilleea ligustrina, commonly known as the silverleaf privet, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features opposite, ovate leaves that are typically silver-white on the underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, which are usually white or pale yellow. It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.

However, it can become invasive in certain regions if not managed properly.
