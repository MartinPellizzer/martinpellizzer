Festuca algeriensis var. battandieri

Festuca algeriensis var. battandieri, commonly known as Algerian fescue, is a grass species native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance in landscaping.
