Fragaria × calyculata

Fragaria × calyculata, commonly known as the Japanese strawberry, is a hybrid species within the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, red, edible fruits that are often used in culinary applications. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil. Its leaves are trifoliate, with serrated edges, and it produces white flowers with five petals.

This species is valued for both its ornamental qualities and its fruit, which has a unique flavor compared to common strawberries.
