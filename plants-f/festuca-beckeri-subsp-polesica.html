Festuca beckeri subsp. polesica

Festuca beckeri subsp.

polesica, commonly known as the Polesian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe. It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along riverbanks.

The plant has slender, tufted culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense panicle bearing spikelets, which are characteristic of the Poaceae family.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
