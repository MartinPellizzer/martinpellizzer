Furcaria cannabina

Furcaria cannabina, commonly known as the cannabis plant, is a flowering herbaceous plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Cannabaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant contains psychoactive compounds such as tetrahydrocannabinol (THC) and cannabidiol (CBD), which are found in its resinous buds. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its chemical composition, it has significant medicinal and recreational uses worldwide.
