Festuca asperella

Festuca asperella, commonly known as rough fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its coarse, wiry leaves and tufted inflorescences that produce small, spike-like flowers. This plant typically grows in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas. Festuca asperella is valued for its resilience and is used in landscaping and as a forage crop.

Its botanical features make it well adapted to harsh environmental conditions.
