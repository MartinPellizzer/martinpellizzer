Ficus Racemosa Var. Miquelii

Ficus racemosa var. miquelii is a unique variant of the common fig tree known for its striking appearance and adaptability. Native to parts of Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity. Its distinctive features include elongated leaves and clusters of small, fig-like fruits that attract various wildlife. Often cultivated as an ornamental plant, it is valued for its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities. Despite its beauty, it requires careful attention to soil and watering conditions to flourish.
