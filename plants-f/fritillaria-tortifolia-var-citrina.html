Fritillaria tortifolia var. citrina

Fritillaria tortifolia var. citrina, commonly known as the yellow star-of-Bethlehem, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange, often with a distinctive greenish tinge. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy or rocky soils. It is typically found in open woodlands and rocky slopes, preferring full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
