Ficus tinctoria var. gibbosa

Ficus tinctoria var. gibbosa, commonly known as the woolly fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
