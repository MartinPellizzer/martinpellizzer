Fraxinus epiptera

Fraxinus epiptera, commonly known as the Chinese ash, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The tree has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in catkins, typical of the genus Fraxinus.

The bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age, and the tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and hard, durable wood.
