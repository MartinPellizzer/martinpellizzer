Forestiera arizonica

Forestiera arizonica, commonly known as Arizona forestiera, is a deciduous shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant features small, elliptical leaves and produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in late spring. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
