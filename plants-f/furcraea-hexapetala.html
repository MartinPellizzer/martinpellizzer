Furcraea hexapetala

Furcraea hexapetala, commonly known as the six-petaled furcraea, is a succulent plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for water storage. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences that bear yellowish-green flowers, which are typically pollinated by insects. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Furcraea hexapetala is also cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
