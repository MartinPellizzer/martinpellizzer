Ficus hondurensis

Ficus hondurensis, commonly known as the Honduras fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Central America, particularly Honduras, and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded, moist environments.

Ficus hondurensis is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
