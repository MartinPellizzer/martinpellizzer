Fordia microphylla

Fordia microphylla, commonly known as the winter daphne, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fragrant, white to pink flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.

Fordia microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and winter blooming habit.
