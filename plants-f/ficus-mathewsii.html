Ficus mathewsii

Ficus mathewsii, commonly known as the Mathews' fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green to yellowish in color. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus mathewsii is also valued for its ability to adapt to various soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
