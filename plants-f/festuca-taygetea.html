Festuca taygetea

Festuca taygetea, commonly known as the Taygetean fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean, particularly in Greece and Turkey.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Festuca taygetea thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
