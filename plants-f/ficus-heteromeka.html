Ficus heteromeka

Ficus heteromeka, commonly known as the Madagascar fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its glossy, broad leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and have a sweet, fig-like flavor. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

Ficus heteromeka prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
