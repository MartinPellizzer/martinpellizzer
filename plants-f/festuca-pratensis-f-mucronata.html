Festuca pratensis f. mucronata

Festuca pratensis f. mucronata, commonly known as the bentgrass meadow grass, is a botanical variant of the common bentgrass.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. This form typically occurs in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. The plant has a low-growing structure with a dense root system, contributing to its stability in various habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
