Frankenia angustipetala

Frankenia angustipetala, commonly known as narrow-leaved frankenia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, fleshy leaves and clusters of yellow flowers. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments. Its flowers are typically visited by bees and other pollinators, contributing to its ecological role in coastal ecosystems.

Frankenia angustipetala is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
