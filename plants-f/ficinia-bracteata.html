Ficinia bracteata

Ficinia bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of freshwater bodies. It has slender, triangular stems and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters.
