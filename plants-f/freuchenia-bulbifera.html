Freuchenia bulbifera

Freuchenia bulbifera, commonly known as the bulbous freuchenia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its swollen, bulb-like stems that store water, enabling it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. Its leaves are typically narrow and succulent, adapted to reduce water loss.

Freuchenia bulbifera is often cultivated for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
