Flaveria elata

Flaveria elata, commonly known as the slender ironweed, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and has opposite, lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces. Its flowers are yellow and arranged in clusters, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Flaveria elata is often found in disturbed soils and open areas, making it a common sight in prairies and roadsides.
