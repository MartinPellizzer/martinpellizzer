Ficus copiosa

Ficus copiosa, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into edible fig fruits. Ficus copiosa thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for ornamental and shade purposes in tropical landscapes.
