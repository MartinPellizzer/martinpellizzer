Fraxinus excelsior var. striata

Fraxinus excelsior var. striata, commonly known as the striated ash, is a variant of the common ash tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive, finely striated bark that becomes more pronounced with age. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, and have a serrated margin with a pale underside. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by winged samaras as its fruit.

This tree thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.
