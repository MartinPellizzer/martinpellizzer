Festuca varia var. woronowii

Festuca varia var. woronowii, commonly known as the Woronow's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, blue to purple flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
