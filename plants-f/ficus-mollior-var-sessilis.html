Ficus mollior var. sessilis

Ficus mollior var. sessilis, commonly known as the broad-leafed fig, is a tropical plant characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically broad and heart-shaped.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits, which are a key feature of its botanical profile. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine due to their medicinal properties.

This variety is popular as a houseplant because of its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
