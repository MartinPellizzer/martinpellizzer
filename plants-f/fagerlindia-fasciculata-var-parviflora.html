Fagerlindia fasciculata var. parviflora

Fagerlindia fasciculata var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered fagerlindia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, delicate flowers that bloom in shades of white and pale pink. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds covered in a fine, hairy coating. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
