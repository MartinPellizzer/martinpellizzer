Festuca glaucoidea

Festuca glaucoidea, commonly known as bluegrass or glaucous bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves that often have a waxy coating. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Festuca glaucoidea thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
