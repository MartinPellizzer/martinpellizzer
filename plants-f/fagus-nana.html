Fagus nana

Fagus nana, commonly known as the dwarf beech, is a small evergreen tree or shrub native to Japan.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and chestnuts, and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

Its compact size and ornamental appearance make it a popular choice for horticultural use.
