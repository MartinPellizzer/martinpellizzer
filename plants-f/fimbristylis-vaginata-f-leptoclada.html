Fimbristylis vaginata f. leptoclada

Fimbristylis vaginata f. leptoclada, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flat sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are typically greenish or brownish in color. It is often found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, sandy soils.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance in supporting aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
