Ficus trichocarpa var. obtusa

Ficus trichocarpa var. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leafed fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by edible figs, which are a key feature of its botanical profile. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to provide shade in gardens and landscapes.
