Fagraea coromandelina

Fagraea coromandelina, commonly known as the coral tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used in traditional craftsmanship.
