Fabiana barrisii

Fabiana barrisii, commonly known as the mountain fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly in the Andes.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude regions, thriving in cool and humid environments. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate, and have a leathery texture.

Fabiana barrisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
