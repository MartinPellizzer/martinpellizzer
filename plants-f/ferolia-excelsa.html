Ferolia excelsa

Ferolia excelsa, commonly known as the mountain orange, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers and small, orange-colored fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in mild, temperate climates. Its leaves are glossy and lance-shaped, while its fruit contains a high concentration of essential oils.

Ferolia excelsa is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
