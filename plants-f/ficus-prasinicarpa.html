Ficus prasinicarpa

Ficus prasinicarpa, commonly known as the green fig or green-fruit fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. Its fruit, which is small and green, is edible and often used in traditional cuisine.

Ficus prasinicarpa thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.
