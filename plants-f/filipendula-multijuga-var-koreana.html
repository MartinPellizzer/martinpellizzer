Filipendula multijuga var. koreana

Filipendula multijuga var. koreana, commonly known as Korean meadow sweet, is a flowering plant native to Korea.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of white to pink flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. Its leaves are compound, with serrated edges, and it produces small, fluffy fruits that disperse seeds by wind.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
