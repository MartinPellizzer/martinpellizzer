Fuchsia adpressipilis

Fuchsia adpressipilis, commonly known as the creeping fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its trailing stems and vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. Its foliage consists of opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Fuchsia adpressipilis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal regions.
