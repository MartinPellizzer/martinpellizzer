Festuca kryloviana f. subbarbata

Festuca kryloviana f. subbarbata, commonly known as the Krylovian fescue subvariety, is a perennial grass species characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves.

It originates from the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is adapted to cold, dry climates. The plant typically forms dense mats with short, creeping rhizomes, making it resilient to harsh environmental conditions. Its foliage is often covered with a dense layer of hairs, giving it a distinctive silvery appearance.

This subvariety is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in alpine settings.
