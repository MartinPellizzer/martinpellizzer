Ficus cumingii var. linearicaudata

Ficus cumingii var. linearicaudata, commonly known as the narrow-tailed fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often tinged with silver along the midrib. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for bonsai and tropical garden settings.
