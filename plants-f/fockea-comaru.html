Fockea comaru

Fockea comaru, commonly known as the Cape jasmine, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of fragrant, white or cream-colored flowers that bloom in spring. The plant produces small, round berries that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Its ornamental value and adaptability contribute to its cultivation in both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
