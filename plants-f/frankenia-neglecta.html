Frankenia neglecta

Frankenia neglecta, commonly known as the beach morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing, herbaceous perennial with small, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments. Its leaves are typically opposite, ovate, and covered in fine, white hairs.

Frankenia neglecta is adapted to salt spray and can tolerate periodic flooding, making it a hardy species suited to coastal ecosystems.
