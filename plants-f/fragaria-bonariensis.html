Fragaria bonariensis

Fragaria bonariensis, commonly known as the Argentine strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically red, with a sweet and tangy flavor. The plant has trifoliate leaves and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its fruit and ornamental value.

The flowers are white with five petals and are pollinated by insects.
