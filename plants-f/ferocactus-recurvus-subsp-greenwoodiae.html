Ferocactus recurvus subsp. greenwoodiae

Ferocactus recurvus subsp.

greenwoodiae, commonly known as the Greenwood's barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh, dry environments.

The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are pollinated by various insects. Its thick, spiny exterior provides protection from herbivores and extreme weather conditions.

This subspecies is well adapted to desert climates, making it a resilient and visually striking component of its ecosystem.
