Fragaria chiloensis f. patagonica

Fragaria chiloensis f. patagonica, commonly known as the Patagonian strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, red, edible berries similar to common strawberries. The plant thrives in coastal and sandy soils, often found in the Patagonian region of Argentina and Chile. Its foliage consists of trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, and it flowers with white or pale pink petals.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in local cuisine.
