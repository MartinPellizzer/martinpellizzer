Fraxinus excelsior var. variegata

Fraxinus excelsior var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated ash, is a variant of the common ash tree.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves, which display a mix of green, white, and yellow hues. This plant is native to Europe and is typically found in woodland areas. The leaves are alternate and ovate, with a smooth margin and a serrated edge.

It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal.
