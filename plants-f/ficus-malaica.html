Ficus malaica

Ficus malaica, commonly known as the Malacca fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, clustered fruits that are edible. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is well-suited for indoor growing due to its adaptability.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is known for its ability to tolerate a variety of soil types.
