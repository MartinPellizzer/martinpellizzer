Ficus ixoroides

Ficus ixoroides, commonly known as the African rubber fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish fruits that turn yellow when ripe. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types as long as it receives adequate moisture.

Ficus ixoroides is also noted for its ability to tolerate indoor conditions, making it a popular choice for houseplants.
