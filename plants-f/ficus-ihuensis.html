Ficus ihuensis

Ficus ihuensis, commonly known as the Ihuensis Fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus ihuensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
