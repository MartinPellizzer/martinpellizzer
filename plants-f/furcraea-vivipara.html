Furcraea vivipara

Furcraea vivipara, commonly known as the viviparous furcraea, is a succulent plant native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to thrive in arid environments. The plant is notable for its ability to produce plantlets along the edges of its leaves, a form of vegetative reproduction called viviparity. These plantlets can take root and grow independently, making propagation relatively easy.

Its striking appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and indoor gardening.
