Fimbristylis ferruginea var. sieberiana

Fimbristylis ferruginea var. sieberiana, commonly known as the rusty sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a reddish-brown hue, and its inflorescence consists of small, clustered spikelets. It produces tiny, wind-pollinated flowers, which are often green or brown in color.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
