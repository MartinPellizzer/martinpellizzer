Ficus duartei

Ficus duartei, commonly known as the Duarte fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible figs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is adapted to various soil types, making it a versatile plant in tropical landscapes.
