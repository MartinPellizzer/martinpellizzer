Fimbristylis arenaria

Fimbristylis arenaria, commonly known as sand flats sedge, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It is a low-growing perennial herb that typically forms dense mats on sandy or gravelly soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish flowers. It is adapted to arid and coastal environments, making it a hardy species in harsh conditions.

Fimbristylis arenaria plays a role in stabilizing sand dunes and providing habitat for small invertebrates.
