Festuca rubra subvar. embergeri

Festuca rubra subvar. embergeri, commonly known as Emberger's red fescue, is a cool-season grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dense, carpet-like foliage. The plant typically forms low-growing, perennial clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.

Emberger's red fescue is also valued for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
