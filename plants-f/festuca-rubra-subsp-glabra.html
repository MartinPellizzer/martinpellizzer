Festuca rubra subsp. glabra

Festuca rubra subsp.

glabra, commonly known as red fescue, is a cool-season grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and tufted growth habit.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and wetlands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous slender, awned spikelets. Festuca rubra subsp.

glabra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
