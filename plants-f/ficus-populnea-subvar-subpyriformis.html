Ficus populnea subvar. subpyriformis

Ficus populnea subvar. subpyriformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a variant of the common fig tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
