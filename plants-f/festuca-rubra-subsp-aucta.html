Festuca rubra subsp. aucta

Festuca rubra subsp.

aucta, commonly known as red fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green, often bluish-gray foliage.

The plant produces compact, spike-like inflorescences with small, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in grasslands, meadows, and rocky habitats. Festuca rubra subsp.

aucta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
