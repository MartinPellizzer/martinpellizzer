Faradaya parviflora var. angustifolia

Faradaya parviflora var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved faradaya, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has linear, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. It prefers well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

This variety is noted for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in arid environments.
