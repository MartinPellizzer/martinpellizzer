Festuca paphlagonica

Festuca paphlagonica, commonly known as Paphlagonian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of southeastern Europe.

It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature.

Festuca paphlagonica is also of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological niche and genetic traits.
