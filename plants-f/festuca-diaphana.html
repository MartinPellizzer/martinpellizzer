Festuca diaphana

Festuca diaphana, commonly known as the translucent grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and narrow, bluish-green leaves that often appear translucent when viewed in certain light conditions. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in high-altitude environments with cool, moist conditions and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological niche, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
