Festuca nemoralis

Festuca nemoralis, commonly known as wood bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its small, spike-like inflorescence bears numerous pale green to brown flowers.

Festuca nemoralis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
