Fuchsia uniflora

Fuchsia uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower fuchsia, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces striking, showy flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. Its flowers are typically borne singly on long, slender stalks, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Fuchsia uniflora thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
