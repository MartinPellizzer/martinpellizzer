Ficus edulis var. cordata

Ficus edulis var. cordata, commonly known as the heart-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are glossy and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into figs, which are edible and used in various culinary applications. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
