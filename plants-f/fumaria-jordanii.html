Fumaria jordanii

Fumaria jordanii, commonly known as Jordan's smokeplant, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae, which includes several species known for their delicate, often blue or purple flowers. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in early spring, often accompanied by hairy, lance-shaped leaves. It thrives in rocky or arid environments, demonstrating adaptability to harsh conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in many regions.
