Fraxinus americana subsp. biltmoreana

Fraxinus americana subsp.

biltmoreana, commonly known as the Biltmore ash, is a subspecies of the American ash native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a serrated margin.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by winged samaras as its seeds. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
