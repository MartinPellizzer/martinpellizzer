Fritillaria hajastanica

Fritillaria hajastanica, commonly known as the Caucasian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red, often with contrasting patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its bulbs are adapted for storing nutrients, allowing it to survive in harsh environmental conditions.

Fritillaria hajastanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
