Filipendula digitata

Filipendula digitata, commonly known as the clustered meadow rue, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Ranunculaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and compound leaves with finely divided lobes. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Filipendula digitata typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland edges and stream banks.

Its foliage and flowers attract various pollinators, including bees and butterflies.
