Ficus leptoclada

Ficus leptoclada, commonly known as the slender-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate, and often glossy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically hidden within the leaves, a feature common in many fig species. Its fruit, known as a syconium, is a unique structure that houses numerous tiny flowers and develops into a edible fig.

Ficus leptoclada is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
