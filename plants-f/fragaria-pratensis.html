Fragaria pratensis

Fragaria pratensis, commonly known as the meadow strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces small, red, edible berries that are similar in appearance to cultivated strawberries. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.

Fragaria pratensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
