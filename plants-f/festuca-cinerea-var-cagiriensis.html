Festuca cinerea var. cagiriensis

Festuca cinerea var. cagiriensis, commonly known as the gray hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as meadows, marshes, and stream sides. Its inflorescence consists of a loose to dense cluster of spikelets, which are often tinged with reddish or purplish hues.

Festuca cinerea var. cagiriensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
