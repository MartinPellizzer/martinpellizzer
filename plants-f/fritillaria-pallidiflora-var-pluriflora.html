Fritillaria pallidiflora var. pluriflora

Fritillaria pallidiflora var. pluriflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its cluster of pale yellow or white bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a distinctive, elongated bulb that stores nutrients for the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and early blooming habit.
