Ficus sinuata subsp. cuspidata

Ficus sinuata subsp.

cuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae. It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia.

The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and have a distinct venation pattern. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in syconia, which are specialized inflorescences typical of the Moraceae family.

The fruit, a small, round fig, is edible and attracts various wildlife, contributing to its ecological significance.
