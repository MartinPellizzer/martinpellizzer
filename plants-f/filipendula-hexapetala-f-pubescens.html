Filipendula hexapetala f. pubescens

Filipendula hexapetala f. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent meadow sweet, is a herbaceous perennial native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of pinkish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its hairy (pubescent) foliage is a distinguishing feature of this variety.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and historical use in herbal medicine.
