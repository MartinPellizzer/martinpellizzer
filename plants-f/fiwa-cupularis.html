Fiwa cupularis

Fiwa cupularis, commonly known as the umbrella plant, is a tropical species native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-like shape, formed by a dense cluster of broad, waxy leaves. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters. Its leaves are usually green with a smooth texture and can vary in size depending on environmental conditions.

Fiwa cupularis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
