Ferula cretica

Ferula cretica, commonly known as the Cretan ferula, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and aromatic, yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces umbrella-shaped clusters of small, yellow flowers, which bloom in the spring. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often containing a resinous substance used in traditional medicine.

Ferula cretica is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in full sun.
