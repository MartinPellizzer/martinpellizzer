Festuca ovina subvar. sulcatiformis

Festuca ovina subvar. sulcatiformis, commonly known as the sulcate bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are typically wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

Its ecological importance includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
