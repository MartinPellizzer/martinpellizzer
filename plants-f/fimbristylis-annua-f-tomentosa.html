Fimbristylis annua f. tomentosa

Fimbristylis annua f. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy annual flat sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves covered in fine, hairy structures. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences that bear tiny, three-lobed flowers, typical of the sedge family. It thrives in wetland environments, such as marshes and along the edges of ponds, where it helps stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic organisms.

Its hairy foliage and compact growth make it a distinctive species within its ecological niche.
