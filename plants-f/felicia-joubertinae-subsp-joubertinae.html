Felicia joubertinae subsp. joubertinae

Felicia joubertinae subsp.

joubertinae, commonly known as the Joubert's fleur-de-lis, is a perennial flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and showy, daisy-like flowers.

The plant typically produces bright yellow or orange flowers with a distinctive central disk, blooming in spring and summer. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This species is well-suited for rock gardens and dry climates due to its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
