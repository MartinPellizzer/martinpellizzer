Fritillaria persica

Fritillaria persica, commonly known as Persian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or red in color, with a distinctive hooded structure. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a spherical bulb. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Fritillaria persica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
