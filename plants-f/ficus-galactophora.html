Ficus galactophora

Ficus galactophora, commonly known as the milk fig, is a species of fig tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically pale yellow or white in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Ficus galactophora is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food for both humans and animals.
