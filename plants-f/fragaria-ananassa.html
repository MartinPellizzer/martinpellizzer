Fragaria ananassa

Fragaria ananassa, commonly known as the strawberry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and its fruit, which is technically a false fruit or pseudocarp. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamins. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun.

Fragaria ananassa is widely cultivated for its edible fruits and is a popular garden plant.
