Ficus camarinensis

Ficus camarinensis, commonly known as the Camarines Ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the Philippines and is typically found in lowland rainforests. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered fruits. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
