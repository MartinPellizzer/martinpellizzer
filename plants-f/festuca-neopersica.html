Festuca neopersica

Festuca neopersica, commonly known as Persian meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, spikelet-bearing flowers.

Festuca neopersica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in arid conditions.
