Faba vulgaris

Faba vulgaris, commonly known as the broad bean, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, edible seeds enclosed in pods, which are often used in cooking. The plant has compound leaves and produces small, white or purple flowers. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Faba vulgaris is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, benefiting other plants in the ecosystem.
