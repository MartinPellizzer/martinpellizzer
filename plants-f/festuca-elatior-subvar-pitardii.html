Festuca elatior subvar. pitardii

Festuca elatior subvar. pitardii, commonly known as the Pitard's tall fescue, is a cool-season grass native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender blades and tufted growth habit, often forming dense, upright clumps. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, grass-like flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
