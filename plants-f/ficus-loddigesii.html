Ficus loddigesii

Ficus loddigesii, commonly known as the Loddiges' fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and can grow as a shrub or small tree. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs.

Ficus loddigesii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
