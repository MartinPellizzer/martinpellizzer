Forestiera ecuadorensis

Forestiera ecuadorensis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian forestiera, is a small evergreen shrub native to the cloud forests of Ecuador.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters and features narrow, lance-shaped leaves with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are small capsules that release seeds when mature.

Forestiera ecuadorensis is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in humid, shaded conditions.
