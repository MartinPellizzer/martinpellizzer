Ferulago geniculata

Ferulago geniculata, commonly known as the bentweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Ferulago geniculata is adapted to rocky and dry soils, making it a hardy species found in various arid environments.
