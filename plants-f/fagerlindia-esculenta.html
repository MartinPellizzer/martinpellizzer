Fagerlindia esculenta

Fagerlindia esculenta, commonly known as the edible lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It features lance-shaped, dark green leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern, and it produces striking white or pale pink flowers with a subtle fragrance. The plant is valued for its edible, tubular-shaped roots, which are often cooked and consumed in traditional cuisines. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Fagerlindia esculenta is also noted for its medicinal properties, with some traditional uses attributed to its leaves and flowers.
