Ficus dicranostyla

Ficus dicranostyla, commonly known as the woolly fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are covered in a dense, woolly layer of fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, spherical fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. It thrives in humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to adapt to various growing conditions.
