Fritillaria mutica var. gracilis

Fritillaria mutica var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of bell-shaped, often purple or orange flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, elongated bulb that stores nutrients for the next growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
