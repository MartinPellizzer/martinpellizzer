Festuca cumminsii

Festuca cumminsii, commonly known as Cummins' fescue, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and compact growth habit, making it well-suited for rock gardens and alpine settings. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flowers in late summer, adding a delicate floral display. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, preferring cool, moist conditions.

Festuca cumminsii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting local pollinators and contributing to soil stability.
