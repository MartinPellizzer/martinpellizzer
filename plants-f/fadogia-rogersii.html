Fadogia rogersii

Fadogia rogersii, commonly known as African velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to western Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain seeds covered in a sticky, resinous substance. It is often found in forested areas and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and potential medicinal contexts.
