Festuca polita var. euboeica

Festuca polita var. euboeica, commonly known as Euboean bluegrass, is a grass species native to the Greek island of Euboea.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically forms dense, low-growing mats and is often found in rocky or limestone soils. It is a hardy perennial that thrives in full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
