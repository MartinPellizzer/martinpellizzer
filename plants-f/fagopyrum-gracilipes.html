Fagopyrum gracilipes

Fagopyrum gracilipes, commonly known as slender buckwheat, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its upright stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces oval, edible seeds that are rich in nutrients and have been used in traditional cuisines. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Fagopyrum gracilipes is often found in moist, shady environments and is valued for its ornamental and nutritional qualities.
