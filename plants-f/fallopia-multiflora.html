Fallopia multiflora

Fallopia multiflora, commonly known as Japanese knotweed, is a perennial flowering plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its hollow, bamboo-like stems and heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in the summer months. It is known for its aggressive growth and invasive nature in non-native regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is often found in moist environments such as riverbanks and wetlands.
