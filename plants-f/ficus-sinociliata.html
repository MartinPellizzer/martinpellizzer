Ficus sinociliata

Ficus sinociliata, commonly known as the Chinese fig or Chinese rubber tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to southern China and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature, though they may change color depending on the variety. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.

Ficus sinociliata is also known for its adaptability to low light conditions and minimal care requirements.
