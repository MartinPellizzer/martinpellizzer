Freycinetia grayana

Freycinetia grayana, commonly known as the white-veined palm, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, fibrous fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

Freycinetia grayana is valued for its ornamental appearance and ecological significance in its native habitats.
