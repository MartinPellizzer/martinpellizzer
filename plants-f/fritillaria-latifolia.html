Fritillaria latifolia

Fritillaria latifolia, commonly known as the meadow fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a robust, fleshy bulb that stores nutrients. It typically blooms in early spring and is adapted to grow in moist, well-drained soils.

Fritillaria latifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
