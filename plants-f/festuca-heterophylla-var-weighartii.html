Festuca heterophylla var. weighartii

Festuca heterophylla var. weighartii, commonly known as the Weighartii fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and visual interest.
