Ficus aspera var. subglabra

Ficus aspera var. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaved fig.

It is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family, native to tropical regions of Africa. The plant is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are typically dark green in color. It produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated as a houseplant in many parts of the world.
