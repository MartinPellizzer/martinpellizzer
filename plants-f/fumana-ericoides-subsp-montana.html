Fumana ericoides subsp. montana

Fumana ericoides subsp.

montana, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America. It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or purple in color. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is often found in high-altitude environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens as an ornamental plant.
