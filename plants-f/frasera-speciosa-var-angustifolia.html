Frasera speciosa var. angustifolia

Frasera speciosa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf frasera, is a deciduous shrub native to the western United States.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are typically narrower than those of the typical variety, with a smooth, dark green upper surface. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often appearing in clusters. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a distinctive and ornamental species within the Campanulaceae family.
