Flueggea virosa subsp. himalaica

Flueggea virosa subsp.

himalaica, commonly known as Himalayan flueggea, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of Asia, particularly in the Himalayas. It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, especially under stress.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in arid or semi-arid environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

The species is also known for its medicinal properties, with various parts used in traditional medicine.
