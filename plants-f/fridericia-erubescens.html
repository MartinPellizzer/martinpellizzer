Fridericia erubescens

Fridericia erubescens, commonly known as the red-flowered fridericia, is a shrub native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, sprawling growth habit and its showy, clustered red or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are usually hairy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardeners seeking ornamental plants.

Fridericia erubescens is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
