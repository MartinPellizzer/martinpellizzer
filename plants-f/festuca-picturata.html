Festuca picturata

Festuca picturata, commonly known as the painted fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive silver-blue foliage, which gives it a striking visual appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it forms dense, low-growing clumps. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green hue, while its inflorescence consists of spikelets that are often tinged with purple or pink.

Festuca picturata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique color and texture.
