Festuca elatior subsp. apennina

Festuca elatior subsp.

apennina, commonly known as the Apennine tall fescue, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and fine, bluish-green foliage.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
