Forestiera eggersiana

Forestiera eggersiana, commonly known as the Evergreen Forestiera, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in woodland areas. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, followed by blue-black berries.

The shrub is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
