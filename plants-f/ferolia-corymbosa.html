Ferolia corymbosa

Ferolia corymbosa, commonly known as the sweet orange, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, glossy leaves and showy white flowers. The plant produces round, orange-colored fruits that are rich in vitamin C and essential oils. Its fruit is widely cultivated for culinary and medicinal purposes.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
