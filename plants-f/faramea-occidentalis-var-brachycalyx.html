Faramea occidentalis var. brachycalyx

Faramea occidentalis var. brachycalyx, commonly known as the dwarf faramea, is a compact shrub native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It features dense, dark green foliage with elliptical leaves that have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in late spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded garden settings.
