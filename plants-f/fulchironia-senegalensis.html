Fulchironia senegalensis

Fulchironia senegalensis, commonly known as the Senegalese sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the Sahel region of Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in arid environments. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to stabilize sandy soils and prevent erosion.

Fulchironia senegalensis is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
