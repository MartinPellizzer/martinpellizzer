Ficus villipes

Ficus villipes, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its dense, trailing growth habit and glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fig-like syconia that are often used as food by various animals. Ficus villipes is well adapted to humid environments and can thrive in both indoor and outdoor settings with proper care.

Its ability to climb and spread makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.
