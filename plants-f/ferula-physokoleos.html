Ferula physokoleos

Ferula physokoleos, commonly known as the Kashmiri ferula, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and often hollow stems. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its rhizomes and roots are rich in essential oils, making it valuable in traditional medicine.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to withstand harsh alpine conditions.
