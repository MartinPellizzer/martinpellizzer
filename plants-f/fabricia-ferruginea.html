Fabricia ferruginea

Fabricia ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty fabricia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it beneficial for soil enrichment in agricultural settings.
