Fragaria rubens

Fragaria rubens, commonly known as the red strawberry, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and red, fleshy fruits that are typically small and acidic. The plant thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, loamy soil. It produces flowers with five pink petals and is often found in woodland edges and open fields.

The botanical features of Fragaria rubens make it an important species for both ecological studies and horticultural interest.
