Fascicularia kirchhoffiana

Fascicularia kirchhoffiana, commonly known as the silver spear or mountain grass, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in clusters at the base of the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy choice for rock gardens or dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental appearance and drought tolerance, it is often cultivated for landscaping purposes.
