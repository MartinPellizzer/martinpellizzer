Festuca varia var. eskia

Festuca varia var. eskia, commonly known as the Eskia fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with narrow, awnless lemma-like structures.

Festuca varia var. eskia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
