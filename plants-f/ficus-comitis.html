Ficus comitis

Ficus comitis, commonly known as the Comitis fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, known as a syconium, is a unique structure that contains numerous tiny seeds.

Ficus comitis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
