Festuca takedana

Festuca takedana, commonly known as Takeda's fescue, is a species of grass native to Japan.

It is a perennial plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and mountainous regions. The plant has slender, tufted culms with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which bears small, two-ranked spikelets.

Festuca takedana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
