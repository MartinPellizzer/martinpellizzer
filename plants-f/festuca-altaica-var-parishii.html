Festuca altaica var. parishii

Festuca altaica var. parishii, commonly known as the Parry's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous minute flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Festuca altaica var. parishii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
