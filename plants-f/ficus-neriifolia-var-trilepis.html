Ficus neriifolia var. trilepis

Ficus neriifolia var. trilepis, commonly known as the three-lobed fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, trifoliate leaves that are deeply lobed, giving it a distinctive appearance. Native to Southeast Asia, this plant thrives in humid, tropical environments. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in syconia, which are enclosed fruit structures.

The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
