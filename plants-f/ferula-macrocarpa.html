Ferula macrocarpa

Ferula macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems that can reach heights of up to 2 meters. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbrella-like inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are pinnately divided and have a strong, aromatic scent reminiscent of fennel.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its historical use in traditional medicine.
