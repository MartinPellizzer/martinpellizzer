Ferolia robusta

Ferolia robusta, commonly known as the robusta hibiscus, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that typically range in color from white to pink and red. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a rough, hairy texture, and it produces orange or yellow fruits that contain multiple seeds. Ferolia robusta is often grown for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.
