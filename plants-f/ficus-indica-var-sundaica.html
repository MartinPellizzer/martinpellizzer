Ficus indica var. sundaica

Ficus indica var. sundaica, commonly known as the Sunda fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus indica var. sundaica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
