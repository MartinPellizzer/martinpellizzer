Ficus subnervosa

Ficus subnervosa, commonly known as the small-veined fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Americas, particularly the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with prominent veins and small, clustered fig fruits. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in coastal areas and lowland forests.

Ficus subnervosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
