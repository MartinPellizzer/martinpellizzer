Fagonia indica var. schweinfurthii

Fagonia indica var. schweinfurthii, commonly known as the desert fagonia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and thorny stems, which help it survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant has adapted to store water in its succulent leaves and stems, making it drought-tolerant. It is often found in rocky soils and can thrive in full sun.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological studies of desert flora.
