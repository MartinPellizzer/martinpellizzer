Festuca spadicea var. consobrina

Festuca spadicea var. consobrina, commonly known as the purple meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces dense, spike-like inflorescences with purple to pinkish flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators and soil stability.
