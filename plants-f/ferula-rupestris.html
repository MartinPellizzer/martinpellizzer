Ferula rupestris

Ferula rupestris, commonly known as the rock fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, finely divided leaves and tall, branched flowering stems that bear yellow flowers. The plant produces small, oval-shaped fruits that contain numerous seeds. Ferula rupestris thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

It is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
