Ficus variegata var. pilosior

Ficus variegata var. pilosior, commonly known as the velvet fig, is a tropical plant characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are broad, glossy, and often have a velvety texture, giving it a unique appearance. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible figs, though the fruit is not typically consumed by humans.

The variegation in its leaves adds to its ornamental value, making it a popular houseplant.
