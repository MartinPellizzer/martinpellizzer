Fragaria californica var. franciscana

Fragaria californica var. franciscana, commonly known as the San Francisco strawberry, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of California.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically red when mature, with a sweet and tangy flavor. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and grows from a short, rhizomatous rootstock. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in local cuisine.
