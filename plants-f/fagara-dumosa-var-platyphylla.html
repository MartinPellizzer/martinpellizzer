Fagara dumosa var. platyphylla

Fagara dumosa var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leafed fagara, is a shrub native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage with broad, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers that are followed by bright red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in the understory of humid forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and potential medicinal research.
