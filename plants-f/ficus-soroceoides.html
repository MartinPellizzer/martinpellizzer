Ficus soroceoides

Ficus soroceoides, commonly known as the African fig tree, is a species of fig tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are often consumed by both humans and animals. Ficus soroceoides thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forested areas.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological planting.
