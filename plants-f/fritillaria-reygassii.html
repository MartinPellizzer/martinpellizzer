Fritillaria reygassii

Fritillaria reygassii, commonly known as the Turk's Cap lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces a single, upright stem that bears several blooms, and its leaves are grass-like and arranged in a rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

The bulb is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
