Fagraea caudata

Fagraea caudata, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow, papery flowers that bloom in clusters, often giving it a fiery appearance. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, leathery leaflets that are arranged oppositely along the stems. Fagraea caudata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
