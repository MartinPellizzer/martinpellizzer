Festuca panormitana

Festuca panormitana, commonly known as the Panormitan fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to withstand drought and poor soil conditions, making it a hardy species in arid environments.

Festuca panormitana is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
