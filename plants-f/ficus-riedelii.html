Ficus riedelii

Ficus riedelii, commonly known as the Riedel's fig tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature, though they may change color depending on the variety.

Ficus riedelii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
