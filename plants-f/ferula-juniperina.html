Ferula juniperina

Ferula juniperina, commonly known as the Kashmiri ferula, is a flowering plant native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. It thrives in rocky, mountainous areas and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Ferula juniperina is also noted for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
