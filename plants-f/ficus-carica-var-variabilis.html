Ficus carica var. variabilis

Ficus carica var. variabilis, commonly known as the common fig, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and produces small, green figs that ripen to a sweet, edible fruit. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates, making it a popular ornamental and fruit-bearing species. Its fruit is not only a food source for humans but also supports various wildlife.

The plant's botanical features include a fibrous root system and a dense, spreading canopy that provides shade and habitat.
