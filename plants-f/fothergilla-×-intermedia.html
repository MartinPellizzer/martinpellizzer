Fothergilla × intermedia

Fothergilla × intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate foithergilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Fothergilla gardenii and Fothergilla major. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that turn vibrant shades of red and orange in the fall. Its showy, fragrant clusters of white or pink flowers bloom in early spring, adding ornamental value to gardens.

It thrives in partial to full shade and well-drained soil, making it a versatile choice for woodland gardens.
