Faramea grandifolia

Faramea grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaved faramea, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Faramea grandifolia is often found in moist, shaded forest understories where it thrives in humid conditions.

Its ornamental value is due to its striking foliage, making it a popular choice for shade gardens.
