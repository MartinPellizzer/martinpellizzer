Fridericia limae

Fridericia limae, commonly known as the Brazilian senna, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its legume fruits and nitrogen-fixing abilities. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are trifoliate, with each leaflet being oval-shaped and slightly hairy.

The flowers are yellow and clustered in racemes, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
