Fatoua globulifera

Fatoua globulifera, commonly known as the African senna, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in savanna ecosystems.

Fatoua globulifera is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
