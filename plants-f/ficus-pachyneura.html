Ficus pachyneura

Ficus pachyneura, commonly known as the woolly fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fig fruits.

The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
