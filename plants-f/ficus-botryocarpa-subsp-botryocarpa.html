Ficus botryocarpa subsp. botryocarpa

Ficus botryocarpa subsp.

botryocarpa, commonly known as the clustered fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia. It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green.

The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when mature, turning yellow or red upon ripening. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
