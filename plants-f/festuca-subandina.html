Festuca subandina

Festuca subandina, commonly known as the Andean fescue, is a perennial grass native to the high altitudes of South America.

It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including strong winds and low temperatures.

Festuca subandina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
