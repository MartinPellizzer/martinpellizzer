Ficus utilis

Ficus utilis, commonly known as the Indian rubber plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically heart-shaped. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular indoor plant. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and form a dense canopy, contributing to its ornamental value.

Ficus utilis is also valued for its latex, which has been historically used in the production of rubber.
