Festuca rubra subsp. alatavica

Festuca rubra subsp.

alatavica, commonly known as the Alatavica bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to Central Asia. It features fine, tufted blades with a bluish-green color and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit.

The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has narrow, cylindrical leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
