Fritillaria ionica subsp. reiseri

Fritillaria ionica subsp.

reiseri, commonly known as the Balkan fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It produces striking bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, often with contrasting dark markings.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils at high altitudes. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winter conditions, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
