Fraxinus paxiana var. sikkimensis

Fraxinus paxiana var. sikkimensis, commonly known as the Sikkim ash, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern Himalayas.

It typically grows in moist, temperate forests at elevations ranging from 1,500 to 3,000 meters. The plant has opposite, simple, and serrated leaves that are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in catkins, while the fruit is a samara with a winged seed.

The tree is valued for its hard, durable wood and is also of ecological importance in its native habitat.
