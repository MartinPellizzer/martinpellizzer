Fumaria megasepala

Fumaria megasepala, commonly known as the large-sepaled gloryflower, is a member of the Fumariaceae family.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to parts of Asia, including China and India. The plant features slender, branched stems and produces clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
