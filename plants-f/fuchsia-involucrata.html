Fuchsia involucrata

Fuchsia involucrata, commonly known as the velvet tangerine fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant produces showy bracts that resemble petals, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sun.

Fuchsia involucrata is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and is relatively easy to care for.
