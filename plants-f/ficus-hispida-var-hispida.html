Ficus hispida var. hispida

Ficus hispida var. hispida, commonly known as the velvetleaf fig, is a tropical plant characterized by its distinctive, velvety, and waxy leaves.

The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a smooth, dark green upper surface and a lighter, fuzzy underside. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid environments. This plant is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and ability to tolerate low light conditions.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
