Festuca rubra var. caespitosa

Festuca rubra var. caespitosa, commonly known as sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, silver-green blades that are often resistant to grazing. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers, which appear in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense lawns.

Festuca rubra var. caespitosa is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
