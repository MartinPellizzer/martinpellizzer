Fallopia × heterocarpa

Fallopia × heterocarpa, commonly known as giant knotweed, is a vigorous perennial plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, greenish-white flowers in late summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough texture, often with a reddish tint on the underside.

It is known for its rapid growth and ability to spread through rhizomes, making it an invasive species in many regions.
