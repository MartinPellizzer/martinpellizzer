Ferocactus arizonicus

Ferocactus arizonicus, commonly known as the Arizona barrel cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its spines provide protection from herbivores and help reduce water loss through shade.

Ferocactus arizonicus plays an important role in desert ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various wildlife species.
