Ficus chlamydocarpa subsp. latifolia

Ficus chlamydocarpa subsp.

latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its glossy, broad leaves that are typically dark green in color.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit, known as a fig, is a small, edible berry that ripens to a yellowish color.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
