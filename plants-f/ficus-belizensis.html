Ficus belizensis

Ficus belizensis, commonly known as the Belize fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly Belize and parts of southern Mexico. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often found in lowland forests.

The Belize fig is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
