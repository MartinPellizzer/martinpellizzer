Festuca scirpifolia subsp. buchtienii

Festuca scirpifolia subsp.

buchtienii, commonly known as Buchtien's sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to parts of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats such as marshes and along watercourses. It produces small, greenish or brownish spikelets that are clustered at the ends of the stems.

This subspecies is noted for its ecological importance in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.
