Ficus regia

Ficus regia, commonly known as the sacred fig or bodhi tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, grayish bark. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, yellowish fruits called figs. Its aerial roots and dense canopy make it a significant species in both ecological and cultural contexts.

Ficus regia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and spiritual significance in various cultures.
