Ficus concinna var. dasycarpa

Ficus concinna var. dasycarpa, commonly known as the woolly fig or hairy fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The leaves are typically elliptical or ovate in shape and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that are typically green when mature.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor environments.
