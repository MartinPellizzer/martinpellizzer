Furcaria

Furcaria, commonly known as the split-leaf plant, is a unique genus of succulent plants native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its distinctive, often lobed or split leaves that resemble a ship's prow, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Furcaria species are known for their ability to store water in their thick, fleshy leaves, which helps them survive in arid conditions.

These plants are popular among succulent enthusiasts due to their striking appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
