Ficus gibbosa var. rigida

Ficus gibbosa var. rigida, commonly known as the rigid fig.

It is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family, native to Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a leathery texture.
