Fimbristylis trichophylla var. erecta

Fimbristylis trichophylla var. erecta, commonly known as the upright hair-fringe sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers, which are often hidden among the foliage.

This species is notable for its ability to thrive in nutrient-poor conditions and its role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.
