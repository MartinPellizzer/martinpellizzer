Festuca ovina subsp. tenuifolia

Festuca ovina subsp.

tenuifolia, commonly known as slender sheep's fescue, is a low-growing grass species characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and dense, mat-forming growth habit. It typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine or subalpine regions.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, grass-like flowers. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green hue.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its hardy, drought-resistant qualities.
