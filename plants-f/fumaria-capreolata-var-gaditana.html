Fumaria capreolata var. gaditana

Fumaria capreolata var. gaditana, commonly known as the Spanish gloryflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and naturalized areas.
