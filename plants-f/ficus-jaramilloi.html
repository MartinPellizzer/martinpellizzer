Ficus jaramilloi

Ficus jaramilloi, commonly known as the Jaramillo's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of Ecuador and is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and unique, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed by local wildlife and birds. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
