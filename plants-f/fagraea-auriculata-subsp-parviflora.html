Fagraea auriculata subsp. parviflora

Fagraea auriculata subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered fagraea, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers.

The plant produces bright red berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners in suitable climates.
