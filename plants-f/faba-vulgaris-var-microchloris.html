Faba vulgaris var. microchloris

Faba vulgaris var. microchloris, commonly known as the dwarf broad bean, is a variety of the broad bean plant characterized by its compact growth habit.

It features small, dark green leaves and produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its shorter stature compared to other broad bean varieties, making it suitable for smaller garden spaces. It is typically cultivated for its edible seeds, which are rich in protein and nutrients.

This variety is also valued for its adaptability to various soil conditions and its relatively disease-resistant nature.
