Fritillaria kiusiana

Fritillaria kiusiana, commonly known as the Kiusian fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color, with a distinctive hooded shape. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils. It blooms in late spring to early summer, making it a notable addition to alpine gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
