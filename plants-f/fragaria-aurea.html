Fragaria aurea

Fragaria aurea, commonly known as the golden strawberry, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are edible. The plant typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soil. It is closely related to other strawberry species but is distinguished by its unique fruit color.

Due to its rarity, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies.
