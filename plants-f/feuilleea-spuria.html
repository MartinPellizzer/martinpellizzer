Feuilleea spuria

Feuilleea spuria, commonly known as the fragrant leaf plant, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, serrated leaves that emit a subtle, citrus-like aroma when touched. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring and summer months. Feuilleea spuria thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and shaded borders.

Its unique fragrance and ornamental value have made it a favored plant among horticultural enthusiasts.
