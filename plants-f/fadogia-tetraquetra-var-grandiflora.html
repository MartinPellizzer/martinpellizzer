Fadogia tetraquetra var. grandiflora

Fadogia tetraquetra var. grandiflora, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces woody pods containing flat, oval seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is cultivated in some regions for both horticultural and traditional uses.
