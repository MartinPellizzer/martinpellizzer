Fumaria capreolata var. condensata

Fumaria capreolata var. condensata, commonly known as the clustered yellowweed, is a herbaceous annual plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the fumaria genus, with specific adaptations for its ecological niche.
