Ficus macrothyrsa var. lancifolia

Ficus macrothyrsa var. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fig, is a tropical plant characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is native to regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
