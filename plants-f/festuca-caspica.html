Festuca caspica

Festuca caspica, commonly known as Caspian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets. It thrives in dry, sandy or rocky soils and is often found in steppe and desert environments.

Festuca caspica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and erosion control in suitable climates.
