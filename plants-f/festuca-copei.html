Festuca copei

Festuca copei, commonly known as Cope's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green blades that are often used in ornamental landscaping. This plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions. Festuca copei is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to form dense, weed-resistant turf.

Its botanical features include spikelets that are usually arranged in a single row along the stems, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
