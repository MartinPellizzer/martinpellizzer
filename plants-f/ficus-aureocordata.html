Ficus aureocordata

Ficus aureocordata, commonly known as the golden heart fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that exhibit a striking golden-green coloration. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth. Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Ficus aureocordata is also noted for its ability to purify the air, enhancing its appeal as a decorative and functional houseplant.
