Festuca ovina var. hibernica

Festuca ovina var. hibernica, commonly known as Hibernian sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and grassy habitats. It has narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue, and it is well adapted to temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
