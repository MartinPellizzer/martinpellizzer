Ficus rehmannii var. villosa

Ficus rehmannii var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy fig or woolly fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
