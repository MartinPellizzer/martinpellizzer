Frangula longipedicellata

Frangula longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-stemmed buckthorn, is a shrub native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, clustered leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers followed by dark, berry-like fruits. It is often found in woodland edges and rocky habitats.

Due to its toxic properties, it is not typically cultivated for ornamental or medicinal purposes.
