Fuchsia apiculata

Fuchsia apiculata, commonly known as the weeping fuchsia, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from deep red to purple. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are opposite and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Fuchsia apiculata is known for its ornamental value and is commonly used in gardens and as a houseplant.
