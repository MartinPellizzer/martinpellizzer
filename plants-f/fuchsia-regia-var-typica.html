Fuchsia regia var. typica

Fuchsia regia var. typica, commonly known as the royal fuchsia, is a species native to South America, particularly Ecuador.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces striking, showy flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in USDA hardiness zones 9 through 11, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
