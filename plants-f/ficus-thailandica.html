Ficus thailandica

Ficus thailandica, commonly known as the Thai fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand, and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are followed by edible figs, which are often used in local cuisine. Ficus thailandica thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its ability to adapt to various soil types makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
