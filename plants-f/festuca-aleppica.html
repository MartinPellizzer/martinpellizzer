Festuca aleppica

Festuca aleppica, commonly known as Aleppo grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences, which produce numerous tiny seeds.

Festuca aleppica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for erosion control.
