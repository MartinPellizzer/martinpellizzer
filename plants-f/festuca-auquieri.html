Festuca auquieri

Festuca auquieri, commonly known as the Auquier's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. Festuca auquieri is adapted to cold, dry environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardiness and visual interest.
