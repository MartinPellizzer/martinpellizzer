Franseria hispidissima

Franseria hispidissima, commonly known as the hairy franseria, is a shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Franseria hispidissima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
