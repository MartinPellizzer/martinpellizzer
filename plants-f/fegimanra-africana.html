Fegimanra africana

Fegimanra africana, commonly known as African breadfruit, is a tropical tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces a starchy, edible fruit that is a staple in local diets. The plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its nutritional value. Its fruit is typically cooked and consumed as a vegetable or processed into flour.

The tree also has cultural and medicinal significance in many African communities.
