Festuca rubra f. pilosa

Festuca rubra f. pilosa, commonly known as hairy red fescue, is a grass species characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and hairy foliage.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive silvery-blue sheen and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.

Festuca rubra f. pilosa is also noted for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
