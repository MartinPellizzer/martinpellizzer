Fimbristylis hanceana

Fimbristylis hanceana, commonly known as the narrowleaf spikerush, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and spikes of small, greenish flowers. Native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, it thrives in wet or moist habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along riverbanks. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers that develop into capsule fruits.

Its botanical structure makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
