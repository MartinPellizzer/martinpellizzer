Ficinia repens

Ficinia repens, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a low-growing perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It features small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically light green and covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by bright red berries, which are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often used in landscaping for its ability to spread and cover ground quickly.

Ficinia repens is also known for its adaptability to various light conditions, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.
