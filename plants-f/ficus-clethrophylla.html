Ficus clethrophylla

Ficus clethrophylla, commonly known as the silver ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with a silvery underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

Ficus clethrophylla prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
