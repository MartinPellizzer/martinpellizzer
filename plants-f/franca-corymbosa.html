Franca corymbosa

Franca corymbosa, commonly known as the flame tree, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has palmately lobed leaves and produces woody pods that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Franca corymbosa is also valued for its ability to provide shade and support biodiversity in its ecosystem.
