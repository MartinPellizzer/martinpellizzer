Ficus cyanus

Ficus cyanus, commonly known as the blue fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Africa, including countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its striking blue flowers, which are actually inflorescences called syconia. Ficus cyanus typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to gardens in suitable climates.
