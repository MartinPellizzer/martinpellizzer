Ficus sapotifolia

Ficus sapotifolia, commonly known as the Brazilian fig, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Moraceae family, which includes other well-known species like the common fig. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, greenish flowers that are followed by edible fig-like fruits.

Ficus sapotifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its fruit in some regions.
