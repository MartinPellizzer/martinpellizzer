Flourensia corymbosa var. lanceolata

Flourensia corymbosa var. lanceolata, commonly known as the lanceleaf flourensia, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is a shrub or small tree, often growing in arid or semi-arid environments. It has a fibrous root system that helps it survive in dry conditions.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
