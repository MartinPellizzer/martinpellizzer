Ficinia clandestina

Ficinia clandestina, commonly known as the hidden fig, is a rare and elusive plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically hidden among its dense foliage, making them difficult to spot. Its fruit, resembling a tiny fig, is enclosed in a fleshy, spiky husk that protects the seeds inside.

Due to its secretive nature and limited distribution, Ficinia clandestina is considered a challenging subject for botanical study.
