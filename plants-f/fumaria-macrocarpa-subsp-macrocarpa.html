Fumaria macrocarpa subsp. macrocarpa

Fumaria macrocarpa subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the greater celandine, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It features elongated, ribbed stems that often grow in a prostrate or ascending manner.

The plant produces bright yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, while the roots contain alkaloids that have been used in traditional medicine.

It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found along roadsides and in woodland edges.
