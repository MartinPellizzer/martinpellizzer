Fimbristylis lomentocarpa

Fimbristylis lomentocarpa, commonly known as the slender-fruited flat sedge, is a flowering plant species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with three-ranked leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in dense clusters at the ends of its stems. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine, and it thrives in wetland or marshy habitats.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
