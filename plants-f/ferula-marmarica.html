Ferula marmarica

Ferula marmarica, commonly known as the Persian cow's milk, is a perennial herb native to the mountains of central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight stem and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its roots are thick and fleshy, and they contain a milky sap that has been used in traditional medicine.

Ferula marmarica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its historical use in herbal remedies.
