Ficus aspera

Ficus aspera, commonly known as the rough fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, rough texture. Ficus aspera produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
