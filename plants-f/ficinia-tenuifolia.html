Ficinia tenuifolia

Ficinia tenuifolia, commonly known as the slender leaf sedum, is a succulent plant native to southern Africa.

It features small, narrow leaves that are typically arranged in a compact rosette pattern. The plant produces clusters of tiny, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the warmer months. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.

Ficinia tenuifolia is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover due to its hardy nature and ornamental appeal.
