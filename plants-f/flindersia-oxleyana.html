Flindersia oxleyana

Flindersia oxleyana, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its large, palmate leaves that are dark green and glossy. The tree produces showy, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late winter or early spring. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks for its striking floral display.
