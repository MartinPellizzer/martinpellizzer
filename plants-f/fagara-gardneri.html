Fagara gardneri

Fagara gardneri, commonly known as the Gardner's soursop, is a tropical plant belonging to the Annonaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America, where it thrives in humid, warm climates. The plant features a rough, ridged stem and large, heart-shaped leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are small, greenish-white, and clustered in umbels, while the fruit is a spiny, green berry that ripens to yellow or orange.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
