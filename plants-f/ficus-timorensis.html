Ficus timorensis

Ficus timorensis, commonly known as the Timor fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the island of Timor in Indonesia and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant produces edible figs that are typically consumed fresh or used in local cuisines. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ficus timorensis is also valued for its ability to adapt to various soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
