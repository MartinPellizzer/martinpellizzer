Festuca arundinacea var. fasciculata

Festuca arundinacea var. fasciculata, commonly known as creeping red fescue, is a cool-season grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in turfgrass mixtures for its durability and aesthetic appeal.

This variety is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in natural and landscaped environments.
