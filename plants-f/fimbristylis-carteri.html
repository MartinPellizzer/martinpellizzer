Fimbristylis carteri

Fimbristylis carteri, commonly known as Carter's sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow, grass-like leaves, typical of sedges. The plant produces small, clustered spikelets that are usually green or brown in color. It thrives in moist to wet environments, such as marshes and swamps, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Fimbristylis carteri plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.
