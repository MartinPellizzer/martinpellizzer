Ferulago frigida

Ferulago frigida, commonly known as the alpine ferulago, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and the European Alps.

It is a perennial herb with hairy, gray-green leaves that are adapted to cold and arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in alpine meadows and tundra habitats.

Ferulago frigida is valued for its resilience and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
