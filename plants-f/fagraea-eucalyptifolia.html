Fagraea eucalyptifolia

Fagraea eucalyptifolia, commonly known as the Australian flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames, hence its common name. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a leathery texture. It produces woody, cylindrical fruits that contain several seeds.

Fagraea eucalyptifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping in warm climates.
