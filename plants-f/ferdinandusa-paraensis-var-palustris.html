Ferdinandusa paraensis var. palustris

Ferdinandusa paraensis var. palustris, commonly known as the swamp fern, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that grow in a compact, upright form. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in wetlands and along riverbanks. Its leaves are typically a deep green, with a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to purify water in aquatic settings.
