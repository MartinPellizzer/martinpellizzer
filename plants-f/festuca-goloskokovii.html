Festuca goloskokovii

Festuca goloskokovii, commonly known as the Goloskokov's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowers are small and spike-like, forming dense clusters at the ends of the stems.

Festuca goloskokovii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil.
