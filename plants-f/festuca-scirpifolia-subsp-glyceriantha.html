Festuca scirpifolia subsp. glyceriantha

Festuca scirpifolia subsp.

glyceriantha, commonly known as the Glyceriantha fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are typically greenish or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
