Fusanus acuminatus var. typicus

Fusanus acuminatus var. typicus, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fusanus, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of East Asia.

It features slender, upright stems that bear opposite, lanceolate leaves with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which bloom in early summer. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local insect populations.
