Ficaria verna subsp. ficariiformis

Ficaria verna subsp.

ficariiformis, commonly known as the spring beauty, is a low-growing perennial herb native to the northeastern United States. It features a rosette of spoon-shaped, hairy leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in a loose inflorescence. Its leaves are characterized by their distinctive, wavy margins and a basal growth habit.

The species is adapted to moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
