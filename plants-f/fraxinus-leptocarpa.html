Fraxinus leptocarpa

Fraxinus leptocarpa, commonly known as the green ash, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It typically grows to a height of 20–30 meters, featuring a straight trunk and a broad, spreading crown. The tree has opposite, simple leaves that are usually 5–10 cm long, with a serrated margin and a heart-shaped base. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a samara with two wings.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown when young, becoming fissured with age.
