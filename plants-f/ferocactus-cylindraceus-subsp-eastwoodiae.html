Ferocactus cylindraceus subsp. eastwoodiae

Ferocactus cylindraceus subsp.

eastwoodiae, commonly known as the Eastwood's barrel cactus, is a columnar cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It features a cylindrical stem that can grow up to 3 meters tall, covered in spiny, ribbed structures.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by fleshy, red fruits. It is adapted to arid environments and has a deep root system for water absorption.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive appearance and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
