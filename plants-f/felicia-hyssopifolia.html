Felicia hyssopifolia

Felicia hyssopifolia, commonly known as the blueweed or blue daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of small, bright blue or violet flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, daisy-like blooms that are attractive to pollinators. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a container plant.

Felicia hyssopifolia is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant color and easy care requirements.
