Ficus ribes var. cuneata

Ficus ribes var. cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf rubber fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a pointed tip. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The leaves are sometimes used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
