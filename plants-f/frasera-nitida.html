Frasera nitida

Frasera nitida, commonly known as the white frasera, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and showy, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lanceolate and often have a slightly hairy surface.

The flowers, which range in color from white to pale yellow, bloom in early spring and attract various pollinators.
