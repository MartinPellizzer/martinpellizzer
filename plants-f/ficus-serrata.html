Ficus serrata

Ficus serrata, commonly known as the pointed-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves that are serrated along the edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus serrata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
