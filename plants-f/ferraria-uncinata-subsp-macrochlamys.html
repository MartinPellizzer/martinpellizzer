Ferraria uncinata subsp. macrochlamys

Ferraria uncinata subsp.

macrochlamys, commonly known as the large-chalmed ferraria, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in grassland or mountainous regions.

The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a hairy texture and produces clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. Its flowering period generally occurs during the warmer months, and it is adapted to withstand dry conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
