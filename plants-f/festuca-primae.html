Festuca primae

Festuca primae, commonly known as early bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Festuca primae produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of grassland ecosystems and a subject of study in plant ecology.
