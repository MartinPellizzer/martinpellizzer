Freziera ochnacea

Freziera ochnacea, commonly known as the African snowball, is a deciduous shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of white, waxy flowers that appear in late winter to early spring. The plant has small, oval-shaped leaves that are arranged oppositely along the stems. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, and it produces small, berry-like fruits that are edible for birds.

Freziera ochnacea is often used in ornamental gardening due to its striking floral display and adaptability to various soil types.
