Ficus ocoana

Ficus ocoana, commonly known as the Ocoa fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Ficus ocoana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.
