Fumaria rutifolia

Fumaria rutifolia, commonly known as the yellowweed, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Fumaria rutifolia is an annual herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.
