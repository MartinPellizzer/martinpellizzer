Fuchsia recurvata

Fuchsia recurvata, commonly known as the silverleaf fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage and clusters of showy, pendulous flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The plant is typically shrub-like, with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery down. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Fuchsia recurvata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
