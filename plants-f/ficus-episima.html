Ficus episima

Ficus episima, commonly known as the Episima fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into edible fig fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Ficus episima is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
