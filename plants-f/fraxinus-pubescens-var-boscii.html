Fraxinus pubescens var. boscii

Fraxinus pubescens var. boscii, commonly known as the Bosci's ash, is a variant of the ash tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves that are often pubescent underneath. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland habitats.

This species is valued for its ecological role and historical use in traditional medicine.
