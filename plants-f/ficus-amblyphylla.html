Ficus amblyphylla

Ficus amblyphylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus amblyphylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor conditions.
