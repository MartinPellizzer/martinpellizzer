Ficus nervosa var. longifolia

Ficus nervosa var. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are often heart-shaped with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically clustered in groups of three. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
