Ficus quibeba

Ficus quibeba, commonly known as the Quibeba fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Ecuador and Peru. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible figs. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The Quibeba fig is valued for its ornamental appeal and its fruit, which is used in local cuisines.
