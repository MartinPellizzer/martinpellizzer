Ficus thynneana

Ficus thynneana, commonly known as the Tynne's fig, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green in color. Its flowers are small and clustered in syconia, which are specialized structures typical of the Moraceae family.

The fruit is a fig, which is edible and often used in local cuisines.
