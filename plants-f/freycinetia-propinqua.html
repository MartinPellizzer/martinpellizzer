Freycinetia propinqua

Freycinetia propinqua, commonly known as the red-flowered pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking red bracts that enclose small, fragrant white flowers. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing near coastal areas.

Freycinetia propinqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage and floral display.
