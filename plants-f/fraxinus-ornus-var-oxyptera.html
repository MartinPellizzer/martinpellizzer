Fraxinus ornus var. oxyptera

Fraxinus ornus var. oxyptera, commonly known as the weeping ash, is a variant of the common ash tree characterized by its distinctive weeping habit.

It features long, pendulous branches that drape gracefully, creating a unique ornamental appearance. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are typically green on top and greyish-white underneath. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, which are followed by small, winged fruits.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and parks.
