Fagus castanea var. dentata

Fagus castanea var. dentata, commonly known as the serrated beech, is a variant of the European beech.

It is a deciduous tree native to parts of Europe and Asia, characterized by its distinctive serrated leaf margins. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth upper surface and a hairy underside. It produces small, edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks, which are an important food source for wildlife.

This species thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.
