Fagara obtusifolia

Fagara obtusifolia, commonly known as the blunt-leaf sycamore or sycamore tree, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its palmately compound leaves, which are typically broad and rounded. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in clusters, while its fruits are fleshy drupes that attract various wildlife.

The tree is often found in moist, tropical lowland forests and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role.
