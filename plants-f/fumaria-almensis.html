Fumaria almensis

Fumaria almensis, commonly known as the alpine smokeplant, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fumariaceae.

It is native to mountainous regions of Europe and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces distinctive, elongated seed pods that release tiny seeds when mature. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its delicate nature, it is sensitive to environmental changes and is considered a rare species in many areas.
