Frailea gracillima f. lepida

Frailea gracillima f. lepida, commonly known as the Lepidate Cactus, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to South America.

It features short, cylindrical stems covered in small, white spines and produces clusters of delicate, yellow flowers during the warmer months. The plant thrives in well-draining soil and requires bright, indirect sunlight to flourish. It is relatively drought-tolerant and is often cultivated as a decorative indoor or outdoor plant.

Its unique appearance and ease of care make it a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts.
