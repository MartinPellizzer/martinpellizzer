Fraxinella dictamnus

Fraxinella dictamnus, commonly known as the dwarf ash or small ash, is a small deciduous shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring compound leaves with 5 to 9 pairs of leaflets. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, which are followed by small, winged fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its ornamental value comes from its compact growth habit and seasonal foliage changes.
