Festuca rupicola var. hirsuta

Festuca rupicola var. hirsuta, commonly known as hairy rockgrass, is a perennial grass species native to arid and rocky environments.

It is characterized by its wiry, hairy stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine pubescence. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought and harsh environmental conditions. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Festuca rupicola var. hirsuta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience in challenging landscapes.
