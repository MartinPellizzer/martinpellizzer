Fothergilla involucrata

Fothergilla involucrata, commonly known as the Appalachian spirea, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding seasonal interest. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its fragrant foliage and showy floral display.
