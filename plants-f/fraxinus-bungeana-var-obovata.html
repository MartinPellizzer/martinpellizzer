Fraxinus bungeana var. obovata

Fraxinus bungeana var. obovata, commonly known as the Chinese ash, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to obovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles during the spring, followed by samaras with elongated wings. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens and parks for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
