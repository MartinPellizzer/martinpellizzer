Fagonia sinaica var. microcarpa

Fagonia sinaica var. microcarpa, commonly known as the Sinai fagonia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and spine-tipped leaves, adapted to thrive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are typically covered in fine hairs. It is often found growing on rocky soils and in coastal areas, showcasing its resilience to extreme conditions.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
