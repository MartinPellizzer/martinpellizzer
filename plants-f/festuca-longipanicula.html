Festuca longipanicula

Festuca longipanicula, commonly known as the long panicle fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, upright stems that bear long, narrow leaves. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically green to brown in color. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

Festuca longipanicula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in various habitats.
