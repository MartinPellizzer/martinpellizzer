Ficus georgii

Ficus georgii, commonly known as the Georgii fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the island of Socotra, located in the Arabian Sea. The plant is characterized by its unique, lobed leaves and produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, adapting well to rocky and well-drained soils.

The species is notable for its ecological significance and has been the subject of botanical studies due to its distinct morphology and limited distribution.
