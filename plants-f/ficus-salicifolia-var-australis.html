Ficus salicifolia var. australis

Ficus salicifolia var. australis, commonly known as the Australian willow fig, is a variant of the willow fig tree native to Australia.

It is characterized by its slender, willow-like branches and heart-shaped leaves that are often pale green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and riparian habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native environment.
