Feuilleea blanchetiana

Feuilleea blanchetiana, commonly known as the silverleaf fern, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are covered in a fine, silvery-white powder, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil. Its reproductive structures are small spore-producing cones located on the underside of its leaves.

Feuilleea blanchetiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and indoor spaces.
