Festuca vierhapperi

Festuca vierhapperi, commonly known as the Vierhapper's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of short hairs. This plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to grow in rocky or well-drained soils. Festuca vierhapperi is often found in alpine meadows and is known for its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in some horticultural settings.
