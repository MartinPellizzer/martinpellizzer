Ferula fedtschenkoana

Ferula fedtschenkoana, commonly known as the Siberian giant fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, feathery leaves that can reach up to two meters in height. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in umbel-like clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its aromatic seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice in some cuisines.

Ferula fedtschenkoana is adapted to cold, arid environments and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
