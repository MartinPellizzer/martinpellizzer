Ficus cryptosyce

Ficus cryptosyce, commonly known as the hidden fig, is a rare and lesser-known species within the Ficus genus.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its unique, elongated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. Ficus cryptosyce typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to the trunks of trees in its natural habitat.

Its small, inconspicuous flowers and fruits make it less appealing for ornamental purposes, but it is valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
