Ficus padifolia

Ficus padifolia, commonly known as the Philippine fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped or oval in form. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Ficus padifolia is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
