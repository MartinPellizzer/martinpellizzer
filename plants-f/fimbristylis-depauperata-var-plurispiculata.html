Fimbristylis depauperata var. plurispiculata

Fimbristylis depauperata var. plurispiculata, commonly known as the sparse-haired flatsedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous plant characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of sedges. It thrives in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, often found in marshes and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and riparian ecosystems.
