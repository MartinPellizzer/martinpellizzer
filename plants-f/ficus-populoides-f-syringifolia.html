Ficus populoides f. syringifolia

Ficus populoides f. syringifolia, commonly known as the silver leaf fig, is a variant of the Ficus populoides species.

It is characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves, which give it a unique appearance. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It has a broad, spreading habit with a dense canopy of foliage.

This cultivar is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor conditions.
