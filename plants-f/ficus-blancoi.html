Ficus blancoi

Ficus blancoi, commonly known as the Blanco's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible figs. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Ficus blancoi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
