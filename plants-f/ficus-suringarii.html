Ficus suringarii

Ficus suringarii, commonly known as the Suringar's fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by fig-like fruits that are typically enclosed in a woody structure. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its air-purifying qualities and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

The leaves are also known for their ability to absorb pollutants from the air, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
