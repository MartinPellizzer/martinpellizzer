Ficus antandronarum

Ficus antandronarum, commonly known as the Madagascar fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a waxy texture.

Ficus antandronarum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
