Felicia douglasii

Felicia douglasii, commonly known as the Douglas's fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and has gray-green, hairy leaves. Its flowers are yellow with a central disk, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Felicia douglasii is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
