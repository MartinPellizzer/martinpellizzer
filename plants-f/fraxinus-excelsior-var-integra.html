Fraxinus excelsior var. integra

Fraxinus excelsior var. integra, commonly known as the smooth ash, is a variant of the common ash tree characterized by its smooth, often glabrous bark.

It is a deciduous tree native to Europe and parts of western Asia, typically reaching heights of 20 to 30 meters. The leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with serrated margins, and they turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow catkins that appear in early spring, and it produces samaroid fruits that disperse by wind.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in timber and basket-making.
