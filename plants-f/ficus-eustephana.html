Ficus eustephana

Ficus eustephana, commonly known as the silver ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a distinctive silver or white vein pattern. It is a popular indoor plant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its air-purifying qualities.

Ficus eustephana typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters in its natural habitat.
