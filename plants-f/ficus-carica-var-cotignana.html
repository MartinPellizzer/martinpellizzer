Ficus carica var. cotignana

Ficus carica var. cotignana, commonly known as the Smyrna fig, is a cultivar of the common fig tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its rapid growth and the production of small, edible figs that are typically harvested when they are still green. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a smooth texture and a deep green color. It thrives in warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.
