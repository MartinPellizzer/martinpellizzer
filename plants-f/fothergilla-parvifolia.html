Fothergilla parvifolia

Fothergilla parvifolia, commonly known as smooth fothergilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that turn yellow in the fall, adding ornamental value throughout the growing season. It prefers partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Fothergilla parvifolia is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
