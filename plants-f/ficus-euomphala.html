Ficus euomphala

Ficus euomphala, commonly known as the African fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, edible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forests or along riverbanks.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local cultures.
