Freycinetia ensifolia

Freycinetia ensifolia, commonly known as the broad-leafed cycad, is a unique plant belonging to the Cycadaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach lengths of up to 3 meters. The plant has a stout, unbranched trunk that is often covered in a fibrous, spongy material. Its cones are dioecious, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants.

Freycinetia ensifolia is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and is known for its slow growth and long lifespan.
