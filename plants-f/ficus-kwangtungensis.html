Ficus kwangtungensis

Ficus kwangtungensis, commonly known as the Guangdong fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to southern China, particularly Guangdong province, and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often used in local cuisine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Ficus kwangtungensis is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to tolerate partial shade.
