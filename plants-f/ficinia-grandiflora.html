Ficinia grandiflora

Ficinia grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered fig vine, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. Ficinia grandiflora thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of nutritious fruit.
