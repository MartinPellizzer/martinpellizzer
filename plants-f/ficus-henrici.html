Ficus henrici

Ficus henrici, commonly known as the Henry's fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types as long as it receives adequate moisture and light.

Ficus henrici is also valued for its ability to tolerate indoor conditions, making it a popular choice for houseplants.
