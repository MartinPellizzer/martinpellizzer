Fraxinus americana var. acuminata

Fraxinus americana var. acuminata, commonly known as the American ash, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 4 to 8 inches long and consist of 5 to 9 oval-shaped leaflets. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by winged fruits called samaras. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This species is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
