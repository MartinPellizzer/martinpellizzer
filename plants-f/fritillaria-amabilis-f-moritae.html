Fritillaria amabilis f. moritae

Fritillaria amabilis f. moritae, commonly known as the Japanese belladonna lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Japan.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish-orange with a contrasting black throat. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often used in rock gardens or as a container plant.
