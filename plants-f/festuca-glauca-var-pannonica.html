Festuca glauca var. pannonica

Festuca glauca var. pannonica, commonly known as Pannonian bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and compact growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental and turfgrass applications. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. It is drought-tolerant once established and can thrive in a variety of climates.

Its foliage provides a striking visual contrast in gardens and landscapes.
