Filipendula ulmaria f. heterochroa

Filipendula ulmaria f. heterochroa, commonly known as the white sweetgrass or meadow sweet, is a botanical variant of the common meadow sweet.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its showy, pink to white inflorescences. The foliage is typically green, with some variation in leaf coloration in this form. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks.

This plant is often noted for its aromatic properties and historical use in herbal medicine.
