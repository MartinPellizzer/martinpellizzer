Ficus oleifolia var. dodonaeiformis

Ficus oleifolia var. dodonaeiformis, commonly known as the African rubber tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by green, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its latex sap has been historically used for rubber production and has various medicinal properties.
