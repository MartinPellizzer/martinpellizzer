Ficus linearis

Ficus linearis, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in width. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, yellowish fruits. It thrives in humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Ficus linearis is often used in landscaping for its ability to climb or trail along surfaces.
