Festuca elliotii

Festuca elliotii, commonly known as Elliot's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is well adapted to arid conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in open woodlands and grasslands, thriving in full sun to partial shade.

Its ornamental value and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for low-maintenance landscaping.
