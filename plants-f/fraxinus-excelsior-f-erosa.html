Fraxinus excelsior f. erosa

Fraxinus excelsior f. erosa, commonly known as the warty ash.

This is a naturally occurring form of the common ash, characterized by its distinctive warty or knobbly bark. It is native to Europe and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream valleys. The plant has simple, opposite leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape.

Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, and it produces small, winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.
