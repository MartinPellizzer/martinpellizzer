Fagara brachyacantha

Fagara brachyacantha, commonly known as the short-stalked fagara, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments within tropical forests. Its fruits are generally globose, berry-like structures that contain several seeds.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
