Flickingeria trifurcata

Flickingeria trifurcata, commonly known as the three-branched fern, is a rare and delicate species of fern native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery fronds that branch into three distinct segments, giving it its scientific name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and requires high humidity and well-drained soil to flourish. Its unique morphology makes it a subject of interest for botanists and conservationists.

Due to habitat loss, Flickingeria trifurcata is considered endangered and is often found in protected areas.
