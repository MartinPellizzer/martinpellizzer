Fimbristylis stricticulmis

Fimbristylis stricticulmis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps, riverbanks, and seasonally flooded areas. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are usually less than 10 millimeters wide, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female parts, and it relies on wind pollination.

Fimbristylis stricticulmis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
