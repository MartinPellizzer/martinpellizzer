Ficus mysorensis var. dasycarpa

Ficus mysorensis var. dasycarpa, commonly known as the hairy-fruited fig, is a tropical plant native to southern India.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, rough-textured fruit. The leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are followed by the characteristic fig-like infructescences.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
