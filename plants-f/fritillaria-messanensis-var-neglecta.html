Fritillaria messanensis var. neglecta

Fritillaria messanensis var. neglecta, commonly known as the Messina fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Italy.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or yellow, with a distinctive red or purple throat. The plant produces narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
