Ficus halliana

Ficus halliana, commonly known as the Hall's fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and often have a waxy texture. It produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when mature.

Ficus halliana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.
