Ficus infectoria var. forbesii

Ficus infectoria var. forbesii, commonly known as the African rubber fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are popular in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its versatility in both ornamental and practical uses makes it a valued plant in many regions.
