Ficus populnea f. botryapioides

Ficus populnea f. botryapioides, commonly known as the Banyan fig, is a unique variant of the banyan tree characterized by its distinctive clustering of aerial roots.

This cultivar is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are large, heart-shaped, and often have a smooth or slightly wavy margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically clustered in small, spherical groups.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping and indoor cultivation.
