Fumana vulgaris var. major

Fumana vulgaris var. major, commonly known as the greater fumaria, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in waste areas.

The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.
