Faramea multiflora var. amazonica

Faramea multiflora var. amazonica, commonly known as the Amazonian faramea, is a climbing plant native to the rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in the undergrowth of tropical forests.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important species in its native habitat.
