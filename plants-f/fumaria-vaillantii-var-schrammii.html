Fumaria vaillantii var. schrammii

Fumaria vaillantii var. schrammii, commonly known as the Schramm's glory of the snow, is a variant of the annual flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces delicate, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the base of the stem. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its charming, early-season blooms.
