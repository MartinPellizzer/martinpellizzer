Filago arizonica

Filago arizonica, commonly known as Arizona sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States.

It is a small annual herb with slender, branching stems and small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain numerous seeds. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Filago arizonica plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing nectar for pollinators in its native habitat.
