Festuca arundinacea subsp. corsica

Festuca arundinacea subsp.

corsica, commonly known as Corsican tall fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, cylindrical leaves that are often hairy.

The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences bearing small, two-lobed florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates with mild winters.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
