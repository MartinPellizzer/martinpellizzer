Faba vulgaris var. violacea

Faba vulgaris var. violacea, commonly known as the violet fava bean, is a variety of the broad bean plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its striking purple flowers and edible pods containing angular, green seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters and thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly hairy texture, while the stems are robust and woody.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in traditional cuisines.
