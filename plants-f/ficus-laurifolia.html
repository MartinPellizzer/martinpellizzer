Ficus laurifolia

Ficus laurifolia, commonly known as the satinleaf fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter green underneath. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically not consumed by humans. Ficus laurifolia thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive foliage.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
