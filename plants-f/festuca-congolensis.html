Festuca congolensis

Festuca congolensis, commonly known as Congo fescue, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and forest margins. The plant has tufted growth habit with slender, wiry stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers.

Festuca congolensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
