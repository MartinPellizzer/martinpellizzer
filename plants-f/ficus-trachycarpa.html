Ficus trachycarpa

Ficus trachycarpa, commonly known as the rough-leaved fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like India, China, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that have a rough texture, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus trachycarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
