Ferulago lophoptera

Ferulago lophoptera, commonly known as the woolly ferulago, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features hairy, glandular leaves that are typically gray-green in color, adapted to withstand arid conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the spring. Its root system is fibrous and often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Ferulago lophoptera thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or limestone habitats.
