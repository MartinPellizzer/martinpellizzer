Ficus tunicata

Ficus tunicata, commonly known as the tunicate fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, thick, waxy leaves that give it a unique appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically clustered in small groups.

Ficus tunicata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
