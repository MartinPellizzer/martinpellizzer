Ferula cypria

Ferula cypria, commonly known as Cyprus giant fennel, is a tall perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a robust, hollow stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, adorned with aromatic, finely divided leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their digestive and anti-inflammatory properties.

Ferula cypria is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking appearance.
