Festuca abyssinica var. keniana

Festuca abyssinica var. keniana, commonly known as Kenyan bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to East Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and bluish-green, with a distinctive silver-white underside.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and erosion control.
