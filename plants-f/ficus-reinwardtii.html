Ficus reinwardtii

Ficus reinwardtii, commonly known as the climbing fig, is a tropical evergreen plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender stems that can climb and spread over large areas. The leaves are glossy, heart-shaped, and often have a waxy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs.

This plant is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
