Ferocactus bicolor

Ferocactus bicolor, commonly known as the two-colored barrel cactus, is a species native to the deserts of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its spines are typically white or pale blue, providing both protection and a unique aesthetic.

Ferocactus bicolor is well adapted to extreme heat and drought, making it a resilient and hardy plant.
