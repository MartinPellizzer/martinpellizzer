Foeniculum vulgare f. pateri

Foeniculum vulgare f. pateri, commonly known as the dwarf fennel, is a compact, herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that are aromatic and reminiscent of anise. The plant produces small, yellow flowers in umbels, which are followed by small, fluffy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, licorice-like scent.
