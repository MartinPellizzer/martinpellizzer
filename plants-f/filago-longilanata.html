Filago longilanata

Filago longilanata, commonly known as the long-leaved filago, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a single bloom. The plant features slender, elongated leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its unique appearance. It typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.

Filago longilanata is known for its small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.
