Fagelia ericoides

Fagelia ericoides, commonly known as the dwarf heath, is a low-growing shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, leathery leaves that are typically arranged in clusters, giving it a dense, compact appearance. The plant produces tiny, bell-shaped flowers that are usually pink or white, which bloom in the spring. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in sandy or rocky habitats.

Fagelia ericoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
