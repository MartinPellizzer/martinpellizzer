Festuca ovina subsp. sipylea

Festuca ovina subsp.

sipylea, commonly known as the Sipylean sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Mediterranean. It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate to alpine climates. It is often found in open grasslands and on slopes, where it contributes to soil stabilization.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in regions with mild winters.
