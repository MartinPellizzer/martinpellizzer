Festuca ovina subvar. arenosa

Festuca ovina subvar. arenosa, commonly known as sand fescue, is a perennial grass native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in poor, sandy soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is drought-tolerant and often used in landscaping for erosion control.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
