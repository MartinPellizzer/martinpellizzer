Festuca nuda

Festuca nuda, commonly known as naked fescue, is a cool-season grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Festuca nuda is a perennial species that produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
