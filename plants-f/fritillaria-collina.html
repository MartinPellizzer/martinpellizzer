Fritillaria collina

Fritillaria collina, commonly known as the Sierra lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red and orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in mountainous regions. Its leaves are basal, narrow, and often have a bluish-gray hue.

Fritillaria collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
