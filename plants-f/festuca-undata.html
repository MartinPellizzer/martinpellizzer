Festuca undata

Festuca undata, commonly known as the bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has fine, tufted leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet that is often curved or twisted.

Festuca undata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping.
