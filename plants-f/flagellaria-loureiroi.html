Flagellaria loureiroi

Flagellaria loureiroi, commonly known as the creeping morning glory, is a vine native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, trailing stems and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale blue to violet flowers that bloom in the morning. Its rapid growth habit makes it both a useful ground cover and a potential weed.

Botanically, it belongs to the Convolvulaceae family, which includes other flowering plants with similar morphological features.
