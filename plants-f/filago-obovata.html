Filago obovata

Filago obovata, commonly known as the broad-ovate filago, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. Native to regions of Europe and western Asia, this plant thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. The plant has a creeping growth habit with hairy stems and is often found in disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
