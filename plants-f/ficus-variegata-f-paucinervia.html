Ficus variegata f. paucinervia

Ficus variegata f. paucinervia, commonly known as the variegated ficus, is a cultivar of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display a mix of green, white, and yellow hues. The plant is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree, typically reaching heights of up to 10 feet. Its leaves are usually broad, glossy, and have a slightly waxy texture.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil and indirect sunlight for optimal growth.
