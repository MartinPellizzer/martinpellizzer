Flagellaria catenata

Flagellaria catenata, commonly known as the cat's tail plant, is a unique flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender, and often branched stems that resemble a cat's tail, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. Its leaves are simple and alternate, often with a slightly hairy texture.

Flagellaria catenata is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unusual appearance and ease of care.
