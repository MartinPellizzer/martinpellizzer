Ficus machupicchuensis

Ficus machupicchuensis, commonly known as the Machu Picchu fig, is a species of flowering plant native to the Peruvian Andes.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant is typically found growing in humid, shaded environments at high altitudes. Its fruit, a small fig, is edible and has a sweet, mild flavor.

Due to its restricted habitat, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
