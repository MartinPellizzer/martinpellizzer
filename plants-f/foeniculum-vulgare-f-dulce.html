Foeniculum vulgare f. dulce

Foeniculum vulgare f. dulce, commonly known as sweet fennel, is a cultivated variant of the wild fennel plant.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in umbels. The plant is native to the Mediterranean region and is often grown for its aromatic seeds and foliage. Its leaves and seeds are used in culinary and medicinal applications due to their distinctive licorice-like flavor.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
