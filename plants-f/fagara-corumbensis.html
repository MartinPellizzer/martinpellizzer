Fagara corumbensis

Fagara corumbensis, commonly known as the Brazilian pepper tree, is a species native to the Atlantic Forest region of Brazil.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant features lance-shaped, dark green leaves with a slightly hairy surface. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in terminal inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy berries.

The plant is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated for its attractive foliage and fruit.
