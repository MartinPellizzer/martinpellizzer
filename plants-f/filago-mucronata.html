Filago mucronata

Filago mucronata, commonly known as the narrowleaf filago, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, disturbed areas and has slender, hairy stems. Its small, yellowish flowers are arranged in clusters and bloom during the warmer months.

Filago mucronata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
