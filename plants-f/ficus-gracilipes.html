Ficus gracilipes

Ficus gracilipes, commonly known as the slender fig tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature. Its leaves are heart-shaped with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

Ficus gracilipes is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor conditions.
