Fritillaria biflora var. inflexa

Fritillaria biflora var. inflexa, commonly known as the Turk's Cap lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or red in color, with a distinctive hooded shape. The plant produces two to three flowers per stem, which bloom in early spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
