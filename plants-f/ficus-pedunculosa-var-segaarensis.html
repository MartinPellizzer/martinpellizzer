Ficus pedunculosa var. segaarensis

Ficus pedunculosa var. segaarensis, commonly known as the Segara ficus, is a rare and unique variant of the ficus genus native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are often deeply lobed and have a waxy texture. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte or terrestrial species, thriving in humid, shaded environments. Its bark is smooth and may develop a reddish hue with age, and it produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens and greenhouses.
