Firmiana diversifolia

Firmiana diversifolia, commonly known as the Chinese parasol tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-shaped leaves that are typically palmately lobed and have a distinctive silvery underside. The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody, spiny fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The bark and leaves have been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
