Fraga sterilis var. parviflora

Fraga sterilis var. parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered stonecrop, is a perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fleshy, blue-green leaves that are arranged in a rosette. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in xeriscaping and as a decorative ground cover.
