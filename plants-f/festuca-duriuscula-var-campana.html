Festuca duriuscula var. campana

Festuca duriuscula var. campana, commonly known as the Campana fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous slender, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscaping.
