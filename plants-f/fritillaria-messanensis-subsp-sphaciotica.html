Fritillaria messanensis subsp. sphaciotica

Fritillaria messanensis subsp.

sphaciotica, commonly known as the Sicilian bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Italy. It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with a distinctive red or orange throat.

The plant typically grows in rocky, calcareous soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its bulbs are underground storage organs that help it survive dry periods.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
