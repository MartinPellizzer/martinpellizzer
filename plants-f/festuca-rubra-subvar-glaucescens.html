Festuca rubra subvar. glaucescens

Festuca rubra subvar. glaucescens, commonly known as bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green leaves and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along riverbanks. It is a perennial species that thrives in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental appeal and use in turfgrass.

Its compact growth and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for landscaping and erosion control.
