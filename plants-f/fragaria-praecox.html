Fragaria praecox

Fragaria praecox, commonly known as the early strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, red, fleshy fruits that are rich in flavor and nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

Fragaria praecox is an important species in the genus Fragaria, which includes many economically significant strawberry varieties.
