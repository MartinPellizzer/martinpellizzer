Fordia albiflora

Fordia albiflora, commonly known as the whiteflower fordia, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has opposite, elliptical leaves that turn reddish in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a border plant.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its compact growth habit and seasonal color changes.
