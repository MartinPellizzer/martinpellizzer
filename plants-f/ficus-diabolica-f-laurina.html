Ficus diabolica f. laurina

Ficus diabolica f. laurina, commonly known as the "Devil's Fig," is a rare and unique variant of the Ficus genus.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black, bark and its distinctive, elongated leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. This cultivar is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid environments. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and ability to adapt to indoor conditions.

However, it requires careful attention to light and humidity levels to maintain its health and aesthetic appeal.
