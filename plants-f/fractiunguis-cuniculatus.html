Fractiunguis cuniculatus

Fractiunguis cuniculatus, commonly known as the underground burrowing plant, is a unique species characterized by its subterranean growth habit.

It possesses a complex root system that allows it to thrive in nutrient-poor soils by efficiently absorbing minerals. Its leaves, though rarely seen above ground, are adapted for photosynthesis in limited light conditions. The plant's flowers, when they do emerge, are small and clustered, often resembling those of related species.

Due to its specialized habitat, it is found primarily in moist, shaded environments with rich organic soil.
