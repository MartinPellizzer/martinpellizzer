Fagara paraguariensis var. inermis

Fagara paraguariensis var. inermis, commonly known as the Guariroa tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with a dense canopy and smooth, dark bark. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by edible, orange-red berries. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a slightly glossy surface.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and traditional uses in local communities.
