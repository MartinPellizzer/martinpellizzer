Fumaria aurea

Fumaria aurea, commonly known as the golden crown vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and is used for soil stabilization due to its extensive root system.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.
