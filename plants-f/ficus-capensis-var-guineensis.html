Ficus capensis var. guineensis

Ficus capensis var. guineensis, commonly known as the African rubber fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to West Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, greenish fruits that turn yellow when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to grow in humid, shaded environments.

The species is also valued for its rubber-like latex, which has been historically used in various industrial applications.
