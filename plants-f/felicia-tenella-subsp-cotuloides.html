Felicia tenella subsp. cotuloides

Felicia tenella subsp.

cotuloides, commonly known as the narrowleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes.

The plant produces small, daisy-like yellow flowers with white centers, which bloom during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
