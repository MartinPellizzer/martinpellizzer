Ficus nigrescens

Ficus nigrescens, commonly known as the black ficus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus nigrescens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor conditions.
