Ficus pseudobenjamina

Ficus pseudobenjamina, commonly known as the Benjamina fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate in form. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible fig fruits.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in various indoor and outdoor environments.
