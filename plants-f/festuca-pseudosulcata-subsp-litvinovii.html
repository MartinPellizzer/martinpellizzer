Festuca pseudosulcata subsp. litvinovii

Festuca pseudosulcata subsp.

litvinovii, commonly known as the Litvinov's fescue, is a grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, rocky or sandy soils, showing a high degree of drought tolerance. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets, which are usually pale green to brown in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
