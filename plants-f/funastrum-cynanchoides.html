Funastrum cynanchoides

Funastrum cynanchoides, commonly known as the Chinese devil's backbone, is a climbing vine native to East Asia.

It belongs to the family Asclepiadaceae and is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and showy, spurred flowers. The plant produces distinctive, red-brown fruits that contain toxic seeds, making it potentially harmful to humans and animals. Due to its toxic properties, it is important to handle the plant with care.
