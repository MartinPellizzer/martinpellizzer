Fargesia wuliangshanensis

Fargesia wuliangshanensis, commonly known as the Wuliangshan bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to China.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. This plant is typically found in mountainous regions with cool, moist climates, often growing in shaded forest environments. Its growth habit is clump-forming, with dense foliage that makes it suitable for ornamental gardening.

The plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade, making it a popular choice for landscape design.
