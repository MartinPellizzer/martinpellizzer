Ficus treubii

Ficus treubii, commonly known as the Treub's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits.

Ficus treubii is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively easy care requirements.
