Ficus clusiifolia

Ficus clusiifolia, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy leaves that are typically arranged in opposite pairs along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by round, fleshy fruits containing a single seed. It is often used in horticulture for its ability to climb and cover structures, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

The species is also valued for its air-purifying properties and adaptability to various growing conditions.
