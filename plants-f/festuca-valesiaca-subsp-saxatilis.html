Festuca valesiaca subsp. saxatilis

Festuca valesiaca subsp.

saxatilis, commonly known as the alpine fescue, is a perennial grass species native to high-altitude regions of Europe. It is characterized by its compact growth habit, narrow leaves, and tufted inflorescences.

This subspecies thrives in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine meadows and mountainous areas. Its ability to withstand cold and harsh environmental conditions makes it an important component of alpine ecosystems.

The plant plays a vital role in soil stabilization and provides forage for grazing animals in its habitat.
