Feuilleea brachycalyx

Feuilleea brachycalyx, commonly known as the short-calyx leaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its compact growth habit and distinctive foliage.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, adding a subtle ornamental appeal. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers moderate to bright indirect light.

Due to its rarity, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens and is valued for its aesthetic and ecological significance.
