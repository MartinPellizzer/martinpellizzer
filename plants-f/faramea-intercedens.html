Faramea intercedens

Faramea intercedens, commonly known as the African evergreen or false holly, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It is a shrub or small tree that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant features opposite, simple, and usually elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. Its small, white or pale pink flowers are borne in clusters and are followed by red, fleshy berries.

Faramea intercedens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
