Ficus papuana

Ficus papuana, commonly known as the Papuan fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Papua and surrounding areas in the Pacific. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, clustered fig fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Ficus papuana is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance in its native habitat.
