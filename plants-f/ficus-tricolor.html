Ficus tricolor

Ficus tricolor, commonly known as the variegated fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features striking foliage with a combination of green, white, and yellow variegation on its leaves. The plant is a member of the Moraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in indirect light and moderate humidity. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, and it produces small, non-showy flowers followed by edible figs.

Ficus tricolor is often used as a decorative houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
