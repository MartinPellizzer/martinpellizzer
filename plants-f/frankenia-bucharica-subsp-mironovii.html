Frankenia bucharica subsp. mironovii

Frankenia bucharica subsp.

mironovii, commonly known as the Mironovii frankenia, is a flowering plant native to the Caucasus region. It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant thrives in rocky and arid environments, showcasing adaptations to drought and poor soil conditions. Its leaves are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss.

This subspecies is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
