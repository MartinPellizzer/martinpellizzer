Ferocactus cylindraceus var. eastwoodiae

Ferocactus cylindraceus var. eastwoodiae, commonly known as the Eastwood's barrel cactus, is a distinctive member of the Cactaceae family native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that can grow up to several feet in height, often featuring a spiny, segmented appearance. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically clustered at the top of the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique morphology and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and valuable species in botanical collections.
