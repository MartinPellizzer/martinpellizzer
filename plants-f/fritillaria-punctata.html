Fritillaria punctata

Fritillaria punctata, commonly known as the spotted fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple, often with distinctive spots or streaks. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in harsh environments, often remaining dormant during dry periods.

Fritillaria punctata is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
