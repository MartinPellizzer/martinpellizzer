Ficus lindsayana

Ficus lindsayana, commonly known as the Lindsay's fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are usually green when mature, and it thrives in warm, humid environments. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.

Ficus lindsayana prefers indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
