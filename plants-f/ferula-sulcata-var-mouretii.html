Ferula sulcata var. mouretii

Ferula sulcata var. mouretii, commonly known as the Algerian giant fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, feathery leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its roots are thick and fleshy, and they contain volatile oils that contribute to its aromatic properties.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal uses.
