Fimbristylis latifolia

Fimbristylis latifolia, commonly known as broadleaf flatsedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swamps, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its leaves are broad and flat, with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Fimbristylis latifolia is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
