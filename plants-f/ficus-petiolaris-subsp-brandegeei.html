Ficus petiolaris subsp. brandegeei

Ficus petiolaris subsp.

brandegeei, commonly known as the Variegated Ficus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white or yellow variegation patterns.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
