Feuilleea canescens

Feuilleea canescens, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in a fine layer of trichomes that help reduce water loss. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow, arranged in clusters along the stems. Its root system is deep and fibrous, allowing it to access moisture from lower soil layers.

Feuilleea canescens is well adapted to drought conditions and thrives in full sun with minimal water requirements.
