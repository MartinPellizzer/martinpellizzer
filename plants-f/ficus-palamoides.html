Ficus palamoides

Ficus palamoides, commonly known as the giant fig tree, is a large, evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, gray bark. The tree produces small, clustered figs that are edible and serve as a food source for various animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Ficus palamoides is often used in landscaping due to its ornamental value and ability to provide shade.
