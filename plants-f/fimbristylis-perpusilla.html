Fimbristylis perpusilla, commonly known as the small-haired sedge, is a small, herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Australia and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or damp habitats such as swamps, marshes, and along waterways. Its flowers are usually small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a three-lobed pistil.

Fimbristylis perpusilla plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments and provides habitat for various invertebrates.
