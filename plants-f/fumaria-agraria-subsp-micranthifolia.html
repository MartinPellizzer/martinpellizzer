Fumaria agraria subsp. micranthifolia

Fumaria agraria subsp.

micranthifolia, commonly known as the lesser celandine, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of western Asia. It features bright yellow flowers with four petals, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant has heart-shaped, smooth leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread rapidly, making it both a common weed and a notable wildflower.
