Fibraurea tinosporoides

Fibraurea tinosporoides, commonly known as the African satinwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and opposite, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds. It is valued for its durable wood, which is used in construction and furniture-making.

However, due to overharvesting, it is now considered an endangered species.
