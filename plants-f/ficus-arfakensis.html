Ficus arfakensis

Ficus arfakensis, commonly known as the Arfaken fig, is a unique species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in local cuisines. Its root system is robust, allowing it to thrive in a variety of soil types.

Ficus arfakensis is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to adapt to indoor environments.
