Fumaria asepala

Fumaria asepala, commonly known as the pale fumitory, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Fumaria asepala thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered with fine hairs.
