Ficus reticulosa

Ficus reticulosa, commonly known as the net-veined fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive network of veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically covered in a whitish bloom. Ficus reticulosa thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
