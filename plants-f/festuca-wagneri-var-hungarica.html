Festuca wagneri var. hungarica

Festuca wagneri var. hungarica, commonly known as Hungarian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass mixes.
