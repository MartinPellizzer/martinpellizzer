Frankenia pseudoericifolia

Frankenia pseudoericifolia, commonly known as the coastal frankenia, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, woody stems and small, fleshy leaves adapted to arid conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom during the summer months. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments.

Frankenia pseudoericifolia is valued for its resilience to salt spray and drought, making it a hardy species for coastal landscaping.
