Festuca ovina subvar. polita

Festuca ovina subvar. polita, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and slightly folded, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often used in turfgrass mixes for its drought tolerance and aesthetic appeal.
