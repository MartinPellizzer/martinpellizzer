Fleurya cuneata var. depauperata

Fleurya cuneata var. depauperata, commonly known as the sparse-flowered fleurya, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, linear leaves and small, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest understories. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a woody stem that becomes more pronounced with age.

This variety is noted for its delicate appearance and is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in botanical gardens.
