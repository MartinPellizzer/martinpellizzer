Flaveria intermedia

Flaveria intermedia, commonly known as the common flaveria, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and oppositely arranged leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by hairy, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Flaveria intermedia is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.
