Ficus paraisoana

Ficus paraisoana, commonly known as the paradise fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible figs that are typically yellow or red when ripe.

Ficus paraisoana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor conditions.
