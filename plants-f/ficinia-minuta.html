Ficinia minuta

Ficinia minuta, commonly known as the dwarf ficinia, is a small, herbaceous plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It typically grows to a height of less than 10 centimeters, with slender, hairy stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. Ficinia minuta thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Its compact size and delicate appearance make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
