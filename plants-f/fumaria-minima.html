Fumaria minima

Fumaria minima, commonly known as the lesser celandine, is a small flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Fumariaceae family and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. It produces slender, erect stems and is known for its ability to thrive in disturbed soils.

Fumaria minima is also noted for its medicinal properties, though it contains toxic compounds that require careful handling.
