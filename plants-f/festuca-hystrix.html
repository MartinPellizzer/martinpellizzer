Festuca hystrix

Festuca hystrix, commonly known as the hairy fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its coarse, hairy leaves and tufted inflorescences that resemble a bottle brush. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and rocky soils. Festuca hystrix has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and garden use.
