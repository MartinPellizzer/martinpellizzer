Forgesia racemosa

Forgesia racemosa, commonly known as the sweet pea shrub, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, pea-like flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and coastal areas.

Forgesia racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its pleasant fragrance.
