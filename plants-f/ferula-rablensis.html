Ferula rablensis

Ferula rablensis, commonly known as the Albanian cow's milk plant, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in early summer, which are clustered in umbels. Its aromatic, milky sap is a distinguishing feature, often used in traditional medicine.

Ferula rablensis is adapted to rocky and arid environments, showcasing resilience to harsh climatic conditions.
