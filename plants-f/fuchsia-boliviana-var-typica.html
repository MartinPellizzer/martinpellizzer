Fuchsia boliviana var. typica

Fuchsia boliviana var. typica, commonly known as the Andean fuchsia, is a striking evergreen shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep red to purple, often with contrasting white or yellow markings. The plant produces showy bracts that resemble petals, enhancing its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, humid climates and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual impact.

Fuchsia boliviana var. typica is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
