Festuca halmyris

Festuca halmyris, commonly known as the alpine fescue, is a perennial grass species native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is typically found in alpine meadows and rocky soils. Festuca halmyris is known for its ability to withstand harsh climatic conditions, including strong winds and low temperatures.

Its small, spike-like inflorescence produces numerous tiny flowers, contributing to its ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
