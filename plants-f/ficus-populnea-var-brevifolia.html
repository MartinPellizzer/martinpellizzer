Ficus populnea var. brevifolia

Ficus populnea var. brevifolia, commonly known as the short-leaved fig, is a tropical plant characterized by its small, broad leaves that are typically no more than 5 centimeters in length.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible figs that are often consumed by local wildlife and humans.

Its compact size and attractive foliage make it a popular choice for indoor and ornamental gardening.
