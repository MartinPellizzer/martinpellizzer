Fritillaria unibracteata

Fritillaria unibracteata, commonly known as the Persian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish-brown in color, with intricate patterns resembling a checkerboard. The plant has a unique morphology, featuring a single bract at the base of the flower stem, which distinguishes it from other fritillary species. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in cold, arid environments, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
