Fuchsia barclayana

Fuchsia barclayana, commonly known as the Barclay's fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from deep red to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with glossy, dark green leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Fuchsia barclayana is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
