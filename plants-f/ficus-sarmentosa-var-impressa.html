Ficus sarmentosa var. impressa

Ficus sarmentosa var. impressa, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a tropical evergreen vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped, glossy dark green leaves that are often slightly lobed and have a distinctive veining pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often used as a ground cover or trained on trellises.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
