Festuca ovina subsp. hackelii

Festuca ovina subsp.

hackelii, commonly known as Hackel's sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, narrow leaves, and small, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies is adapted to cold and alpine environments, often growing in rocky or well-drained soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.

The plant is also known for its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient choice for ecological restoration projects.
