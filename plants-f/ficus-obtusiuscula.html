Ficus obtusiuscula

Ficus obtusiuscula, commonly known as the smooth-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, including countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that are smooth to the touch, giving it its common name. It produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when ripe.

Ficus obtusiuscula is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
