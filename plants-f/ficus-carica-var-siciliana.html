Ficus carica var. siciliana

Ficus carica var. siciliana, commonly known as the Sicilian fig, is a cultivar of the common fig tree native to southern Italy.

It is characterized by its large, edible figs that ripen in late summer, typically turning a deep purple or brownish color. The plant produces small, opposite leaves that are glossy and dark green, often with a slight pubescence. It thrives in warm, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its sweet, aromatic fruit and is often cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
