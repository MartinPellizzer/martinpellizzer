Fevillea pedatifolia

Fevillea pedatifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf fevillea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton. The plant is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforest understories.
