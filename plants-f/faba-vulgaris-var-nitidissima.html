Faba vulgaris var. nitidissima

Faba vulgaris var. nitidissima, commonly known as the glossy vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often glossy and dark green, and its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale purple. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical pods that contain several oval, smooth seeds. It is typically found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial in ecological and agricultural contexts.
