Fedia dentata var. dasycarpa

Fedia dentata var. dasycarpa, commonly known as the hairy-fruited fedia, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its serrated, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant produces distinctive, hairy, spherical fruits that are typically red when mature. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in humid tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
