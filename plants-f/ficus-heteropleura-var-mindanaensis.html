Ficus heteropleura var. mindanaensis

Ficus heteropleura var. mindanaensis, commonly known as the Mindanao fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Philippines, particularly found in the Mindanao region. The plant is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are usually dark green with a smooth texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible figs.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
