Furcraea lipsiensis

Furcraea lipsiensis, commonly known as the Lipsiense Furcraea, is a member of the Asparagaceae family and is native to Central America.

It is a succulent plant characterized by its narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often covered in a white powdery coating. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences with small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in the spring. It is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.

Due to its ornamental value and drought tolerance, it is cultivated as a decorative plant in many regions.
