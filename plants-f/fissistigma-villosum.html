Fissistigma villosum

Fissistigma villosum, commonly known as the hairy fissistigma, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. It typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Fissistigma villosum is often found in coastal and mountainous regions where it thrives in well-drained soils.
