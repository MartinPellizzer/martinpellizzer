Ficus fauriei

Ficus fauriei, commonly known as the Benjamin fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, greenish-white fruits. It is widely cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.

Ficus fauriei thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate watering to maintain its health.
