Festuca arundinacea subsp. cordubensis

Festuca arundinacea subsp.

cordubensis, commonly known as Corduban reed grass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in wet meadows and riverbanks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
