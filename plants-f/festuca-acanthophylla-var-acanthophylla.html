Festuca acanthophylla var. acanthophylla

Festuca acanthophylla var. acanthophylla, commonly known as the spiked fescue, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, which contain small, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Festuca acanthophylla var. acanthophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
