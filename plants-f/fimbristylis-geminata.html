Fimbristylis geminata

Fimbristylis geminata, commonly known as the twin-stemmed sedge, is a flowering plant native to various regions of Asia, Australia, and the Pacific Islands.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, often forming dense mats near water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, contributing to its success in moist habitats.

Fimbristylis geminata plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
