Ficus sakalavarum

Ficus sakalavarum, commonly known as the Maluku fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the Moluccas, an archipelago in Indonesia, and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Ficus sakalavarum is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
