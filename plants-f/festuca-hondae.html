Festuca hondae

Festuca hondae, commonly known as Hondan fescue, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growths with slender, wiry stems and small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Festuca hondae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
