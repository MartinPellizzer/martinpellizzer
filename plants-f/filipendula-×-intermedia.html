Filipendula × intermedia

Filipendula × intermedia, commonly known as meadow sweet, is a hybrid shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets, and it produces small, dark red berries in the fall. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands, meadows, and along stream banks.

Filipendula × intermedia is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting pollinators.
