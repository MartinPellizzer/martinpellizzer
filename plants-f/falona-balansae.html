Falona balansae

Falona balansae, commonly known as the Balansae flax, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, daisy-like flowers. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its fibrous stalks were historically used by indigenous peoples for making ropes and textiles.

Falona balansae is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
