Fritillaria platyptera

Fritillaria platyptera, commonly known as the broad-leaf fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically deep purple or orange, with a distinctive, often speckled pattern. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a cold winter dormancy period to bloom successfully.

Fritillaria platyptera is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
