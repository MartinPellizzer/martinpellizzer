Ferula ceratophylla

Ferula ceratophylla, commonly known as the horned fennel, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and feathery, aromatic leaves that resemble those of fennel. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in summer, and it is often found in rocky or arid environments. Ferula ceratophylla is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional remedies for various ailments.

Its resilience to harsh climates makes it an important species in its ecological niche.
