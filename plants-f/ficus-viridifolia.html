Ficus viridifolia

Ficus viridifolia, commonly known as the green-leaved fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped. It produces small, fig-like fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Ficus viridifolia is also popular as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
