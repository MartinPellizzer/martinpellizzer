Falcata angustifolia

Falcata angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf falcata, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered white or pale yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rocky or dry garden environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
