Ficus botryocarpa var. linearifolia

Ficus botryocarpa var. linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically yellow or green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
