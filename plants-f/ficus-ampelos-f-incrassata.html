Ficus ampelos f. incrassata

Ficus ampelos f. incrassata, commonly known as the thick-leaf fig or variegated fig, is a cultivar of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its large, thick, and often variegated leaves that display shades of green, white, and sometimes pink. The plant is a fast-growing vine that thrives in warm, humid environments. It produces small, edible figs that are typically used in cooking or as ornamental features.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
