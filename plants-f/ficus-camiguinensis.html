Ficus camiguinensis

Ficus camiguinensis, commonly known as the Camiguin fig, is a species of fig tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for various wildlife species and contributing to forest biodiversity.
