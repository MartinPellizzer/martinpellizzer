Festuca indica

Festuca indica, commonly known as the Indian bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and dense growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental and erosion control purposes. The plant typically grows in moist to wet soils and is often found in wetlands, marshes, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a cylindrical spike that bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

Festuca indica is valued for its adaptability and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
