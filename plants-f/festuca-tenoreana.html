Festuca tenoreana

Festuca tenoreana, commonly known as the Tenore's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Festuca tenoreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in arid landscapes.
