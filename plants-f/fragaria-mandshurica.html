Fragaria mandshurica

Fragaria mandshurica, commonly known as the Manchurian strawberry, is a perennial herb native to East Asia.

It produces small, red, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to common strawberries. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and grows in a variety of habitats, including forests and grasslands. It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the Fragaria genus.
