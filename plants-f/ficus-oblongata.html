Ficus oblongata

Ficus oblongata, commonly known as the rubber fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically elliptical or oblong in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by round, greenish fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability.

Ficus oblongata is also valued for its ability to purify indoor air and its ornamental appeal.
