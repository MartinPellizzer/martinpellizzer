Finetia

Finetia, commonly known as the "Bamboo Orchid," is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, bamboo-like stems and clusters of delicate, greenish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories, and thrives in humid climates. Its leaves are long and narrow, often with a slightly waxy texture, and it produces small, fragrant blossoms that bloom sequentially along the stem.

Finetia is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its unique appearance.
