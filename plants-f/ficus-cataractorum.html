Ficus cataractorum

Ficus cataractorum, commonly known as the sacred fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ficus cataractorum is also valued for its cultural and religious significance in various regions.
