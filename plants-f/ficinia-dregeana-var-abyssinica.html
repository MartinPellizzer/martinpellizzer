Ficinia dregeana var. abyssinica

Ficinia dregeana var. abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian fig tree, is a species native to Ethiopia and neighboring regions.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, dark grey bark and elliptical leaves that are glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that transition into edible figs, which are typically green when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
