Foetidia africana

Foetidia africana, commonly known as the African stinkwood, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Rhamnaceae family and is characterized by its dark, aromatic wood that emits a strong, unpleasant odor when cut. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate or elliptic in shape. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by small, round fruits containing seeds.

Foetidia africana is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its durable timber.
