Felicia petitiana

Felicia petitiana, commonly known as the dwarf blueweed, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, blue to purple florets arranged in dense clusters, giving it a delicate appearance. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
