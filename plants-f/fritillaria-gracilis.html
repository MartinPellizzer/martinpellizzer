Fritillaria gracilis

Fritillaria gracilis, commonly known as the slender fritillary, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of bell-shaped, often orange or yellow flowers. The plant produces distinctive, helmet-like seed capsules that split open to release its seeds. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Fritillaria gracilis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking blooms.
