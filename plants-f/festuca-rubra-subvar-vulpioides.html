Festuca rubra subvar. vulpioides

Festuca rubra subvar. vulpioides, commonly known as red fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green, sometimes reddish, foliage. The plant produces compact, spike-like inflorescences with small, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in turfgrass and restoration projects.

Its adaptability to various environmental conditions makes it a valuable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
