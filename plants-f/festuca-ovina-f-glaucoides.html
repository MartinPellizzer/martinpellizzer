Festuca ovina f. glaucoides

Festuca ovina f. glaucoides, commonly known as the glaucous sheep's fescue, is a variant of the sheep's fescue species.

It is a cool-season grass characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, bluish-green foliage. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its leaves are covered with a waxy coating, giving them a distinctive bluish-gray appearance.

This ornamental grass is valued for its aesthetic appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its hardy nature and drought tolerance.
