Ferula teterrima

Ferula teterrima, commonly known as the Persian cow parsley, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and often fern-like foliage. The plant produces yellowish-green umbellate flowers that bloom in early summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Ferula teterrima is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
