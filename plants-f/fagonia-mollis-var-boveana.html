Fagonia mollis var. boveana

Fagonia mollis var. boveana, commonly known as the Bovean fagonia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of North Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its small, narrow leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, often growing on rocky soils. Its structure is typically low-growing with multiple stems, and it produces small, hard fruits that contain seeds.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its resilience and unique adaptations to extreme climates.
