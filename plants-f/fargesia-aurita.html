Fargesia aurita

Fargesia aurita, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a cold-hardy evergreen bamboo native to China.

It features slender, upright canes with a silvery-blue sheen on its leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers in summer, followed by dark blue berries. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for rock gardens and as a decorative plant.

Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a low-maintenance, visually appealing bamboo.
