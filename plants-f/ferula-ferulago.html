Ferula ferulago

Ferula ferulago, commonly known as the Italian cow parsley, is a member of the Apiaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that can grow up to 2 meters tall, featuring hollow, aromatic stems and finely divided, fern-like leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green umbellate flowers in late spring to early summer. Its roots are fibrous and contain essential oils, which contribute to its medicinal and aromatic properties.

Ferula ferulago is native to southern Europe and is often found in dry, rocky soils.
