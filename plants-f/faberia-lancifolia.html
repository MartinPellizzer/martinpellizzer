Faberia lancifolia

Faberia lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved faberia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Faberia lancifolia is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions.

Its growth habit is generally shrub-like, with multiple stems and a compact form.
