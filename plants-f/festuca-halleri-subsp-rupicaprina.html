Festuca halleri subsp. rupicaprina

Festuca halleri subsp.

rupicaprina, commonly known as alpine bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine soils, thriving in cool, moist environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers. Festuca halleri subsp.

rupicaprina is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in harsh environments.
