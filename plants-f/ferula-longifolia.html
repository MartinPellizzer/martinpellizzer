Ferula longifolia

Ferula longifolia, commonly known as the Mediterranean cow parsley, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces a strong-smelling, fibrous resin that has been historically used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture.

Ferula longifolia thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or coastal areas.
