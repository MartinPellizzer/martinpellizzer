Festuca alaica subsp. pamirica

Festuca alaica subsp.

pamirica, commonly known as the Pamir feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its fine, silky foliage and tufted growth habit, which contribute to its ornamental value.

The plant typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and cold climates. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny seeds. Festuca alaica subsp.

pamirica is valued for its ability to stabilize soils in harsh environments and for its aesthetic appeal in garden settings.
