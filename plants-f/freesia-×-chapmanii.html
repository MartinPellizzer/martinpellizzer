Freesia × chapmanii

Freesia × chapmanii, commonly known as the Chapman's freesia, is a hybrid perennial plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its fragrant, trumpet-shaped flowers that come in a variety of colors including white, yellow, orange, and pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm and produces clusters of flowers on sturdy stems. Its foliage consists of strap-like, grassy leaves that are broad at the base and taper towards the tips.

Freesia × chapmanii is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and floral arrangements due to its vibrant blooms and pleasant scent.
