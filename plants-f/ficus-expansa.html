Ficus expansa

Ficus expansa, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. The leaves are large, heart-shaped, and have a waxy surface, often with a deep green color. It produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
