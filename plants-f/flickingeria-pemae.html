Flickingeria pemae

Flickingeria pemae, commonly known as the Hawaiian redwood, is a unique member of the Podocarpaceae family.

It is native to the island of Hawaii and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and conical shape. The plant has scale-like leaves that are typically reddish-brown in color, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It is a dioecious species, meaning that male and female reproductive structures are found on separate plants.

Flickingeria pemae is also notable for its ecological importance in the native forests of Hawaii.
