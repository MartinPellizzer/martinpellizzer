Festuca limosa

Festuca limosa, commonly known as marsh feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to wetland and marsh habitats.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often bend over. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and usually covered with a fine layer of silky hairs.

Festuca limosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for wetland wildlife.
