Ficus quijosana

Ficus quijosana, commonly known as the Quijosana fig, is a tropical plant native to the Amazon rainforest.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit, a small fig, is typically enclosed in a woody capsule and is edible.

Ficus quijosana thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest understories.
