Festuca adanensis

Festuca adanensis, commonly known as Adan's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky and dry habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its fine texture and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Festuca adanensis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and low maintenance requirements.
