Fezia

Fezia, commonly known as the orchid grass, is a genus of flowering plants belonging to the orchid family, Orchidaceae.

These plants are typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, often growing in moist, shaded environments. Fezia species are characterized by their slender, grass-like leaves and small, delicate flowers that often resemble those of orchids. They are generally herbaceous and can be either annual or perennial, depending on the species.

The botanical features of Fezia make them interesting subjects for study in both horticulture and ecological research.
