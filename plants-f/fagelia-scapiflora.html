Fagelia scapiflora

Fagelia scapiflora, commonly known as the scarlet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking red or orange tubular flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often covered in a fine layer of white hair.

Fagelia scapiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
