Fuchsia platypetala

Fuchsia platypetala, commonly known as the velvet tassel fuchsia, is a deciduous shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep purple to bright red. The plant produces showy, clustered inflorescences that are often used in ornamental gardening. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, with a slightly hairy texture.

Fuchsia platypetala thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
