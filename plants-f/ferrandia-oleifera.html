Ferrandia oleifera

Ferrandia oleifera, commonly known as the oil tree, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, green, ovate leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is notable for its fruit, which contains a large, oily seed used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils. Ferrandia oleifera thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and commercial contexts.
