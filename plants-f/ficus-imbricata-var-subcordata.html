Ficus imbricata var. subcordata

Ficus imbricata var. subcordata, commonly known as the subcordate fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible figs. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
