Ficus alococarpa

Ficus alococarpa, commonly known as the red-veined fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent red veins, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature, though they may change color slightly. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus alococarpa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor environments.
