Fagelia lanata

Fagelia lanata, commonly known as the woolly fagelia, is a member of the Anacardiaceae family.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that give it a distinctive, fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles. Its fruits are drupes, typically containing a single seed enclosed in a fleshy, orange-colored pulp.

Native to tropical regions of South America, Fagelia lanata thrives in moist, shaded environments.
