Fimbristylis filamentosa

Fimbristylis filamentosa, commonly known as the threadleaf sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and typically grows in wet or marshy environments. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in both freshwater and brackish habitats.

Due to its adaptability, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
