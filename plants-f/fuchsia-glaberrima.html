Fuchsia glaberrima

Fuchsia glaberrima, commonly known as the smooth fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically range in color from deep red to purple. The plant produces small, oval-shaped berries that contain seeds, which are dispersed by birds and other animals. Fuchsia glaberrima is a deciduous shrub that thrives in cool, moist environments with partial shade.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in temperate climates.
