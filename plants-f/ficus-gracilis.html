Ficus gracilis

Ficus gracilis, commonly known as the slender fig tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often clustered at the ends of branches. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature, and it thrives in humid, warm climates. Ficus gracilis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for bonsai and container gardening.
