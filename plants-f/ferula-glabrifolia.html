Ferula glabrifolia

Ferula glabrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved ferula, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes plants like carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic compounds. The plant features a thick, branched root system and tall, slender stems covered with smooth, lance-shaped leaves. Its most notable botanical feature is its tall, panicle-like inflorescence that bears small, yellowish flowers.

Ferula glabrifolia is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and antiseptic properties.
