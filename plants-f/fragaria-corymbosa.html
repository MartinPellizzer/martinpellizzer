Fragaria corymbosa

Fragaria corymbosa, commonly known as the wild strawberry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant produces edible, red, fleshy fruits that are rich in antioxidants and have a sweet, aromatic flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and disturbed soils.

Its botanical features make it a popular subject in horticulture and botany studies.
