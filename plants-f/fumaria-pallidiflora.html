Fumaria pallidiflora

Fumaria pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered glory-of-the-snow, is a flowering plant in the family Fumariaceae.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, typically growing in moist, shaded environments. The plant produces delicate, pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Fumaria pallidiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and woodland settings.
