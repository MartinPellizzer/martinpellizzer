Ficus aspera var. volubilis

Ficus aspera var. volubilis, commonly known as the climbing fig, is a tropical plant characterized by its vigorous, climbing growth habit.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are often consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is native to regions in Southeast Asia.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
