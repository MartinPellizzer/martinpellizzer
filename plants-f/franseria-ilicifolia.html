Franseria ilicifolia

Franseria ilicifolia, commonly known as the coastal franseria, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and clusters of white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal dunes and rocky outcrops. It has a compact growth habit and is well adapted to salt spray and strong winds.

Franseria ilicifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal vegetation.
