Firmiana heterophylla

Firmiana heterophylla, commonly known as the Chinese parasol tree, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-shaped canopy and palmately lobed leaves that vary in shape and size depending on the light conditions. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by woody, spiny fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

The tree is adaptable to various soil types and can thrive in urban environments.
