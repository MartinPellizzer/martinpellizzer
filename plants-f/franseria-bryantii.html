Franseria bryantii

Franseria bryantii, commonly known as Bryant's franseria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with yellow disc florets and white to pale pink ray florets. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive blooms.

Franseria bryantii is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local pollinators.
