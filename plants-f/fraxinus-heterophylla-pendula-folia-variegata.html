Fraxinus heterophylla pendula-folia-variegata

Fraxinus heterophylla pendula-folia-variegata, commonly known as the variegated weeping ash, is a unique variant of the weeping ash tree.

It exhibits striking variegated foliage with white and green patterns on its leaves, which adds to its ornamental value. The plant is characterized by its pendulous branches that create a cascading effect, enhancing its visual appeal. Botanically, it belongs to the Oleaceae family and is native to certain regions of Asia.

Its leaves are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a smooth margin and a slightly hairy underside.
