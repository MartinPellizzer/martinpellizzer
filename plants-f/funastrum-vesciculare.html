Funastrum vesciculare

Funastrum vesciculare, commonly known as the bladderleaf or bladderleaf fern, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It features a rosette of lance-shaped, fleshy leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle, which helps reduce water loss in arid conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white, followed by bladder-like fruit structures that contain the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its unique morphology and adaptation to dry environments make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
