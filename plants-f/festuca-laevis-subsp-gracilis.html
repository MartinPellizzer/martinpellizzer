Festuca laevis subsp. gracilis

Festuca laevis subsp.

gracilis, commonly known as slender meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It typically grows in open, moist habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and stream banks.

The plant has slender, grass-like leaves with a smooth surface and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its fine texture and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Festuca laevis subsp.

gracilis is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and aesthetic appeal.
