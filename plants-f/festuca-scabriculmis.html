Festuca scabriculmis

Festuca scabriculmis, commonly known as rough fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers. Festuca scabriculmis is often found in open, disturbed sites such as roadsides, meadows, and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
