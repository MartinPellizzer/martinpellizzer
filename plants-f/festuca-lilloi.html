Festuca lilloi

Festuca lilloi, commonly known as Lillo's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Festuca lilloi is adapted to high-altitude environments and thrives in cool, moist conditions.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in alpine ecosystems.
