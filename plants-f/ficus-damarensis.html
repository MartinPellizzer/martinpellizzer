Ficus damarensis

Ficus damarensis, commonly known as the Damara fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly found in Namibia and South Africa. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a glossy upper surface.

Ficus damarensis produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible figs that are important for both wildlife and local communities.
