Freycinetia solomonensis

Freycinetia solomonensis, commonly known as the Solomon Island palm, is a species of flowering plant in the Arecaceae family.

It is native to the Solomon Islands and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by elongated, fibrous fruits. It thrives in tropical rainforest environments and is often found in shaded, moist areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
