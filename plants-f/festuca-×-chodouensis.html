Festuca × chodouensis

Festuca × chodouensis, commonly known as the Chodouenskaya fescue, is a hybrid grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which contribute to its ornamental value. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.

Festuca × chodouensis is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in marginal environments.
