Festuca gallica var. costei

Festuca gallica var. costei, commonly known as the Coste's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Festuca gallica var. costei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
