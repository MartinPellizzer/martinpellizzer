Ficus cotoneifolia

Ficus cotoneifolia, commonly known as the curly willow fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions such as Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its weeping, pendulous branches and glossy, dark green, waxy leaves that have a distinctive curled appearance. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Ficus cotoneifolia is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
