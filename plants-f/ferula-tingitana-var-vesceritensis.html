Ferula tingitana var. vesceritensis

Ferula tingitana var. vesceritensis, commonly known as the Atlas fennel, is a perennial herb native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its aromatic foliage and roots are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.
