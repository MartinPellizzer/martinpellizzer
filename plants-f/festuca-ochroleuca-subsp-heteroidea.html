Festuca ochroleuca subsp. heteroidea

Festuca ochroleuca subsp.

heteroidea, commonly known as the pale oatgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and western Asia. It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and woodland edges.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, pale green to yellowish spikelets in summer. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and is often found in moist or well-drained soils.

Its botanical features make it a valuable component of ecological studies and habitat conservation efforts.
