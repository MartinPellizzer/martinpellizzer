Fagraea floribunda

Fagraea floribunda, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that appear in clusters, typically during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves with lance-shaped leaflets and produces woody, capsule-like fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Fagraea floribunda is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
