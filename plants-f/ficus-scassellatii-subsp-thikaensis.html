Ficus scassellatii subsp. thikaensis

Ficus scassellatii subsp.

thikaensis, commonly known as the Thika fig, is a species native to East Africa. It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves.

The leaves are typically glossy and have a slightly waxy texture. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

This plant is often found in moist, semi-deciduous forests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
