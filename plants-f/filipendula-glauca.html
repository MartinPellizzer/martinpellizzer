Filipendula glauca

Filipendula glauca, commonly known as white sweet clover, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to North America.

It features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces fluffy, white to pale pink flower clusters. The plant is characterized by its aromatic foliage and is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. Filipendula glauca is known for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it beneficial for soil enrichment.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
