Ficus pseudopalma

Ficus pseudopalma, commonly known as the "bottle tree," is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, bottle-shaped trunk and waxy, dark green leaves that are glossy and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, pale greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits resembling figs. Ficus pseudopalma is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.

It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to ensure optimal growth.
