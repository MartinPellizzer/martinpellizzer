Faika villosa

Faika villosa, commonly known as the hairy bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, with a hairy texture on both the stems and leaves. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy addition to rock gardens or alpine displays. Its foliage is characterized by broad, lance-shaped leaves covered in fine, soft hairs.

Faika villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and resilient nature.
