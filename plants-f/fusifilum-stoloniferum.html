Fusifilum stoloniferum

Fusifilum stoloniferum, commonly known as the creeping fusil, is a unique plant species characterized by its stoloniferous growth habit.

It produces elongated, spindle-shaped structures called fusils, which are specialized for vegetative propagation. The plant typically thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. Its botanical features include small, scale-like leaves and a network of runners that help in spreading across the substrate.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unusual morphology and reproductive strategies.
