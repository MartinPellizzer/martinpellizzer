Ficus monticola

Ficus monticola, commonly known as the mountain fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface and a pointed tip. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, round fig that turns from green to yellow or red as it matures.
