Ficinia filiculmea

Ficinia filiculmea, commonly known as the fern-like fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes other well-known plants like the breadfruit and mulberry. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by edible fig-like fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
