Ferula tabasensis

Ferula tabasensis, commonly known as Persian cow parsley, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and often fern-like foliage. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the spring and summer months. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to thrive in dry, rocky soils.

Ferula tabasensis is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
