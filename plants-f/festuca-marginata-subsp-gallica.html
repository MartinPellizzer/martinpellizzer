Festuca marginata subsp. gallica

Festuca marginata subsp.

gallica, commonly known as Gallic bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive silver or white margin, which helps in identifying it. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
