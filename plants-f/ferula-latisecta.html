Ferula latisecta

Ferula latisecta, commonly known as the broad-leafed ferula, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often deeply lobed. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its aromatic leaves and stems contain volatile oils, making it valuable in traditional medicine and perfumery.

Ferula latisecta is also noted for its adaptability to dry and rocky soils.
