Ficus parkeri

Ficus parkeri, commonly known as the African rubber plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters tall in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically dark green with a waxy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible fruits.

Ficus parkeri is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
