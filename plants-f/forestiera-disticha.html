Forestiera disticha

Forestiera disticha, commonly known as the twinleaf forestiera, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in clusters during the spring, followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for native gardens and landscapes.
