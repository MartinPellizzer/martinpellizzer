Friesodielsia caesia

Friesodielsia caesia, commonly known as the blueberry lichen, is a foliose lichen that thrives in moist, shaded environments.

It features a pale blue to greenish thallus with a distinct, wrinkled texture. The plant's structure consists of a fungal component that forms a symbiotic relationship with algae or cyanobacteria. It is often found on bark, stones, and soil in temperate regions.

Its slow growth and unique coloration make it a notable species in ecological studies.
