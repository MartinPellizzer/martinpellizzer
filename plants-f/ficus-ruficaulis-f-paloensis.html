Ficus ruficaulis f. paloensis

Ficus ruficaulis f. paloensis, commonly known as the Paloensis fig, is a unique variant of the rubber fig plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive red-brown stems and thick, waxy leaves that give it a striking appearance. The plant is native to the tropical regions of the Americas and thrives in warm, humid environments. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ability to adapt to indoor conditions.

Its foliage provides a lush, decorative look, making it a popular choice for interior landscaping.
