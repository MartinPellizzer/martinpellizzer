Ficaria nudicaulis

Ficaria nudicaulis, commonly known as the naked-stemmed figwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically features prostrate or ascending stems that are often hairless, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on short pedicels. Its leaves are usually opposite, ovate to elliptic in shape, and have a slightly hairy surface.

Ficaria nudicaulis thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland or rocky habitats.
