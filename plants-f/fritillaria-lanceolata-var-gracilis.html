Fritillaria lanceolata var. gracilis

Fritillaria lanceolata var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender bellflower, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its bulbs are adapted for storage, allowing it to survive in cold climates and periods of dormancy.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
