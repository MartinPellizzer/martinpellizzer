Fritillaria crassifolia subsp. hakkarensis

Fritillaria crassifolia subsp.

hakkarensis, commonly known as the Hakkari fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountains of Turkey. It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or yellow in color, often with contrasting black markings.

The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Its bulbs are relatively large and store nutrients to support growth in harsh conditions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
