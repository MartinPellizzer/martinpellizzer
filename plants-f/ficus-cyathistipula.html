Ficus cyathistipula

Ficus cyathistipula, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, including countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. It produces small, clustered fruits known as syconia, which are enclosed within a fleshy structure.

Ficus cyathistipula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of soil types with adequate moisture.
