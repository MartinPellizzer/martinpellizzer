Ficus phytolaccifolia

Ficus phytolaccifolia, commonly known as the red-veined fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia, and is characterized by its striking, dark green leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Ficus phytolaccifolia is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
