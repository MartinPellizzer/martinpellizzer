Ficus lancea

Ficus lancea, commonly known as the lanceleaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Ficus lancea is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and air-purifying qualities.

Its growth habit is generally compact, making it suitable for indoor environments with moderate light.
