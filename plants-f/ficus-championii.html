Ficus championii

Ficus championii, commonly known as the Mexican fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.

Ficus championii is known for its adaptability and ability to tolerate low light conditions.
