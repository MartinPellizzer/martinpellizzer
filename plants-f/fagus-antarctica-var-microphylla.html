Fagus antarctica var. microphylla

Fagus antarctica var. microphylla, commonly known as the Antarctic beech, is a variant of the Antarctic beech tree native to the subantarctic islands.

It is a deciduous tree with small, ovate leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The plant produces small, spherical nuts enclosed in a prickly husk. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is adapted to the harsh conditions of its native environment.

Its botanical features make it a unique species within the Fagaceae family.
