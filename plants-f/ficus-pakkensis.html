Ficus pakkensis

Ficus pakkensis, commonly known as the Bornean fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and smooth, gray bark. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
