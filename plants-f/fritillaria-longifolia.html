Fritillaria longifolia

Fritillaria longifolia, commonly known as the Turk's Cap lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a distinctive, elongated bulb that stores nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Fritillaria longifolia is also valued for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
