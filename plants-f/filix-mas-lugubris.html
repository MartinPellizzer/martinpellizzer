Filix-mas lugubris

Filix-mas lugubris, commonly known as the sorrowful maidenhair, is a rare and delicate fern species characterized by its finely divided, arching fronds.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in temperate forests with high humidity. The plant's spores are released from specialized structures called sori, which are located on the underside of its leaves. Its foliage exhibits a striking contrast between the pale green of the fronds and the deep brown of the mature sporophytes.

Due to its limited distribution and sensitivity to environmental changes, Filix-mas lugubris is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
