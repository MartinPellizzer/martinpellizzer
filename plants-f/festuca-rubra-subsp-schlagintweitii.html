Festuca rubra subsp. schlagintweitii

Festuca rubra subsp.

schlagintweitii, commonly known as the red fescue, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically a deep green color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, two-lobed flowers. It is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils and full to partial sunlight. Festuca rubra subsp.

schlagintweitii is often used in landscaping and as a turfgrass due to its hardiness and ornamental appeal.
