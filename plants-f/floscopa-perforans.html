Floscopa perforans

Floscopa perforans, commonly known as the perforate bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue to purple in color, with a distinctive perforated pattern on the petals. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Floscopa perforans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate, flowering display.
