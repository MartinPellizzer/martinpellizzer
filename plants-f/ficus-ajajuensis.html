Ficus ajajuensis

Ficus ajajuensis, commonly known as the African fig tree, is a species of fig tree native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, featuring a dense canopy and thick, leathery leaves. The plant produces small, clustered fruits known as syconia, which are characteristic of the Ficus genus. Its roots are extensive and can form symbiotic relationships with various fungi.

The tree is valued for its ecological role and is often found in moist, shaded environments in its native habitat.
