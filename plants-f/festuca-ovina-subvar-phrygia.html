Festuca ovina subvar. phrygia

Festuca ovina subvar. phrygia, commonly known as Phrygian sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and erosion control.
