Ferula euxina

Ferula euxina, commonly known as the Crimean fennel, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and dill, and is characterized by its aromatic compounds. The plant features a tall, slender stem with finely divided, fern-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in umbels. Its root system is thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine.

Ferula euxina is known for its strong, spicy scent and has been utilized in various cultural and medicinal practices throughout history.
