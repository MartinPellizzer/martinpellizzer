Festuca pratensis subsp. pluriflora

Festuca pratensis subsp.

pluriflora, commonly known as the meadow fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous florets, which are often pink or purple in hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in ecological restoration projects.
