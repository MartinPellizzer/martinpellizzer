Ficus micracantha

Ficus micracantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are usually green when immature and turn yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ficus micracantha is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to adapt to indoor environments.
