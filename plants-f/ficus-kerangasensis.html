Ficus kerangasensis

Ficus kerangasensis, commonly known as the Kerangas fig, is a species of fig tree native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ecological importance, it plays a vital role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
