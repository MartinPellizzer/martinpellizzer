Ficus noronhae

Ficus noronhae, commonly known as the Noronha fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the island of Fernando de Noronha, an archipelago located off the coast of Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into edible fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Ficus noronhae plays a significant role in the local ecosystem, providing food and habitat for various wildlife species.
