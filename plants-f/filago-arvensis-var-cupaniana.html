Filago arvensis var. cupaniana

Filago arvensis var. cupaniana, commonly known as the common filago, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is characterized by its small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant typically grows in disturbed soils and is often found in fields and along roadsides. It has a basal rosette of leaves and produces a slender flowering stem.

Its seeds are dispersed by wind, contributing to its ability to colonize new areas.
