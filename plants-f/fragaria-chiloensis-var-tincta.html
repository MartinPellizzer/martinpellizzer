Fragaria chiloensis var. tincta

Fragaria chiloensis var. tincta, commonly known as the purple strawberry, is a perennial herb native to coastal regions of South America.

It produces small, edible fruits that are typically purple or red in color, with a sweet and tangy flavor. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and grows in moist, sandy soils along the coast. It is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.

The species is also notable for its ability to thrive in harsh coastal environments.
