Ficus cumingii var. androbrota

Ficus cumingii var. androbrota, commonly known as the Androbrota fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are often lobed or notched. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fig fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Its adaptability makes it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation in suitable conditions.
