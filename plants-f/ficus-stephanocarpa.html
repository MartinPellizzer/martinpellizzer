Ficus stephanocarpa

Ficus stephanocarpa, commonly known as the silver ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Thailand and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a distinctive silver or white vein pattern. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.

Ficus stephanocarpa produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits, though these are rarely seen in cultivation.
