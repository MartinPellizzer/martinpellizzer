Ficus ottoniifolia subsp. ulugurensis

Ficus ottoniifolia subsp.

ulugurensis, commonly known as the Ulugurensis fig, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are usually pubescent on the underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are enclosed in a fleshy, spiky capsule.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging climatic conditions.
