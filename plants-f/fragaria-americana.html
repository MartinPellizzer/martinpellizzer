Fragaria americana

Fragaria americana, commonly known as the wild strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically red, with a sweet and tangy flavor. The plant has trifoliate leaves and grows in a variety of habitats, including forests and open fields. Its flowers are white and have five petals, and it blooms in the spring.

The fruit develops from the flower and is an important food source for both wildlife and humans.
