Fraxinus excelsior f. aureopunctata

Fraxinus excelsior f. aureopunctata, commonly known as the golden-spotted ash.

This is a rare and distinctive form of the common ash, characterized by its striking golden-yellow spots on the leaves. The foliage is usually smaller and more densely packed compared to the typical species. It is a dioecious plant, meaning that male and female flowers are found on separate trees.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas across Europe.
