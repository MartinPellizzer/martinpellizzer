Forsythia suspensa f. pubescens

Forsythia suspensa f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy forsythia, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and its clusters of bright yellow, funnel-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The foliage consists of ovate, dark green leaves with a hairy texture, which gives the plant its specific epithet "pubescens.". It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its early spring blooms and hardy nature.

This variety is well-suited for small gardens or as a border plant due to its compact size and attractive appearance.
