Ficus ecuadorensis

Ficus ecuadorensis, commonly known as the Ecuador fig tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant has broad, glossy leaves that are usually dark green in color. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
