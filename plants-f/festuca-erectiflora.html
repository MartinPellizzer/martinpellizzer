Festuca erectiflora

Festuca erectiflora, commonly known as the upright flowering fescue, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Festuca erectiflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
