Festuca aloha

Festuca aloha, commonly known as the Hawaiian fescue, is a perennial grass native to the Hawaiian Islands.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green foliage. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in coastal and upland areas, contributing to the unique vegetation of Hawaii's ecosystem.

Festuca aloha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in native habitats.
