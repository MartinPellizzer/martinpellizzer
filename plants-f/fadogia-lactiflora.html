Fadogia lactiflora

Fadogia lactiflora, commonly known as the African butter tree, is a small evergreen tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by yellowish-orange fruits containing edible seeds. Its latex sap has been traditionally used for medicinal purposes, including as a treatment for skin conditions.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
