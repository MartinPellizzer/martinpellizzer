Fimbristylis nutans var. minor

Fimbristylis nutans var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf false spikegrass, is a small, herbaceous perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, often forming dense, low-growing mats. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale green flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its delicate, nodding flowers and is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
