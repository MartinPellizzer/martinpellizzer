Ferula meifolia

Ferula meifolia, commonly known as the Persian cow parsley, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and dill. The plant is characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbels. Ferula meifolia is valued for its medicinal properties, particularly for its use in traditional medicine.

Its essential oils, extracted from the roots and stems, contain compounds with potential anti-inflammatory and antimicrobial effects.
