Fuchsia summa

Fuchsia summa, commonly known as the giant fuchsia, is a striking plant native to South America, particularly Ecuador.

It is a deciduous shrub that can grow up to 10 meters in height, featuring thick, woody stems and broad, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, which bloom during the rainy season. Its botanical characteristics include a complex root system and the ability to thrive in humid, tropical environments.

Fuchsia summa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to partial shade with well-drained soil.
