Fagara attiensis

Fagara attiensis, commonly known as the Jamaican prickly ash, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves with serrated margins and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in clusters. Its fruit is a fleshy, orange-red drupe containing a single seed.

Fagara attiensis is often found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
