Festuca camusiana

Festuca camusiana, commonly known as Camus' fescue, is a perennial grass native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist to wet habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

Festuca camusiana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
