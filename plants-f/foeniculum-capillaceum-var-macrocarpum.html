Foeniculum capillaceum var. macrocarpum

Foeniculum capillaceum var. macrocarpum, commonly known as the large-seeded fennel, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Its fruits are elongated, ribbed seeds that are larger than those of the standard fennel species.

This variety is valued for its distinctive flavor and is often cultivated for both ornamental and edible purposes.
