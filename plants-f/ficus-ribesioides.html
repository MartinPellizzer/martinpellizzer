Ficus ribesioides

Ficus ribesioides, commonly known as the Indian rubber fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are typically yellow or green when ripe. It is valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of conditions and is often used in ornamental horticulture.

The plant also has a fibrous latex that was historically used to make rubber.
