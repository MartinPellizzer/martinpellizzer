Fumaria biaurita

Fumaria biaurita, commonly known as the two-lobed fumitory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces delicate, slender leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It typically grows in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and other open habitats.

Fumaria biaurita is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
