Ficus crassiuscula

Ficus crassiuscula, commonly known as the thick-leaved fig, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its glossy, thick, and waxy leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus crassiuscula is also known for its adaptability to indoor environments, making it a popular choice for houseplants.
