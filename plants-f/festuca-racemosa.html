Festuca racemosa

Festuca racemosa, commonly known as bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and dense, compact growth habit. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, meadows, and along stream banks, preferring well-drained soils. Its inflorescence consists of a spike of spikelets, each bearing small, blue-gray flowers.

Festuca racemosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
