Festuca duriuscula var. ochroleuca

Festuca duriuscula var. ochroleuca, commonly known as the white-haired fescue, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of North America.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically forms dense, mat-like growths and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its leaves are narrow and have a bluish-green hue with prominent white or silver hairs along the edges.

This variety is well adapted to harsh environments and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
