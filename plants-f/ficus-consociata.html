Ficus consociata

Ficus consociata, commonly known as the clustered fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Ficus consociata is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in indoor environments.

Its growth habit makes it well-suited for hanging baskets or as a trailing plant.
