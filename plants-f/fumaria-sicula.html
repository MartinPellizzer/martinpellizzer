Fumaria sicula

Fumaria sicula, commonly known as Sicilian smokeplant, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe, particularly Sicily.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, nodding yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, branched stems and small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or rocky areas.

Fumaria sicula is also noted for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
