Fuchsia corymbiflora var. alba

Fuchsia corymbiflora var. alba, commonly known as the white-flowered fuchsia, is a striking plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy flowers that are predominantly white with a hint of pink, creating a striking contrast against its dark green foliage. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are glossy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms.

The variety 'alba' is notable for its pale coloration, distinguishing it from other fuchsia species.
