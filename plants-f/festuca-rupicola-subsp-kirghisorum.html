Festuca rupicola subsp. kirghisorum

Festuca rupicola subsp.

kirghisorum, commonly known as the Kirghiz rockgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It typically grows in rocky and arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in mountainous regions, where it contributes to soil stabilization.

Botanically, it is characterized by its robust root system and drought-resistant features.
