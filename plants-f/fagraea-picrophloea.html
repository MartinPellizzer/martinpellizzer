Fagraea picrophloea

Fagraea picrophloea, commonly known as the red-veined or red-flowered evergreen, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent red veins, which give it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its fruit is a bright red berry that contains several seeds.

Fagraea picrophloea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
