Festuca rupicaprina

Festuca rupicaprina, commonly known as alpine bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and blue-green foliage, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. Festuca rupicaprina is often found in alpine meadows and is a component of subalpine vegetation.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its hardy nature.
