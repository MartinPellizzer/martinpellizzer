Ferreyranthus rugosus

Ferreyranthus rugosus, commonly known as the roughleaf ferreyranthus, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant is distinguished by its rough, hairy leaves and vibrant yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in xeriscapes and botanical gardens.
