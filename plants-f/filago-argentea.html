Filago argentea

Filago argentea, commonly known as silver filago, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, erect stems covered in silvery-white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, daisy-like flowers with yellow centers and white petals, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and also covered in fine, silver-gray hairs, contributing to its ornamental value.

Filago argentea is often found in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.
