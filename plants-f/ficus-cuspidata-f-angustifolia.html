Ficus cuspidata f. angustifolia

Ficus cuspidata f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a variant of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to adapt to various growing conditions.
