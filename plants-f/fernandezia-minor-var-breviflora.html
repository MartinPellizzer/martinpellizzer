Fernandezia minor var. breviflora

Fernandezia minor var. breviflora, commonly known as the dwarf fernandezia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It features dense, dark green foliage with a compact growth habit, often forming low mats or cushions. The plant produces small, white flowers followed by red, berry-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
