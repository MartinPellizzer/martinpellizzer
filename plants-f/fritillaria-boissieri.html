Fritillaria boissieri

Fritillaria boissieri, commonly known as the Persian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cold winter dormancy period to bloom successfully.

Fritillaria boissieri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
