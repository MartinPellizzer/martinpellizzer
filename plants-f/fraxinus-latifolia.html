Fraxinus latifolia

Fraxinus latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf ash, is a deciduous tree native to western North America.

It is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically 6 to 12 inches long and composed of 5 to 9 oval-shaped leaflets. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by winged fruits that disperse its seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, often developing shallow fissures with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
