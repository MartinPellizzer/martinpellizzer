Fragaria sylvestris var. abortiva

Fragaria sylvestris var. abortiva, commonly known as the wild strawberry or woodland strawberry, is a variant of the common strawberry plant.

It is characterized by its small, edible berries that are typically less sweet and more tart compared to cultivated strawberries. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces flowers that are white with five petals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along the edges of forests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and its use in traditional herbal remedies.
