Ficus ampla

Ficus ampla, commonly known as the broadleaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus ampla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.
