Frankenia persica

Frankenia persica, commonly known as Persian sandmat, is a flowering plant native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise rugged appearance. It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in coastal areas or dry inland regions.

Frankenia persica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and unique foliage.
