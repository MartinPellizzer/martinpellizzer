Fuchsia pumila

Fuchsia pumila, commonly known as the dwarf fuchsia, is a small, evergreen shrub native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its trailing stems and clusters of vibrant, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens.

Fuchsia pumila is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
