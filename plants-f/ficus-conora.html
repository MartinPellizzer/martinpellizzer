Ficus conora

Ficus conora, commonly known as the African fig or Congo fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in local cuisines. Ficus conora thrives in humid environments and prefers well-draining soil with partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening in warm climates.
