Festuca camerunensis

Festuca camerunensis, commonly known as Cameroon fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Africa, particularly Cameroon.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and forest margins.

Festuca camerunensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
