Fraxinus sieboldiana var. angustata

Fraxinus sieboldiana var. angustata, commonly known as the Japanese ash or narrow-leaf ash, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and finely toothed leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged samaras that aid in seed dispersal. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

This species is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in traditional crafts and construction.
