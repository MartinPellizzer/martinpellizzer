Faramea parvula

Faramea parvula, commonly known as the dwarf faramea, is a small evergreen shrub native to Madagascar.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the warmer months, followed by bright red berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
