Ficus carica

Ficus carica, commonly known as the common fig, is a flowering plant in the mulberry family, Moraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and unique fruiting process. The plant produces a fleshy, edible fruit called a fig, which develops inside a syconium, a specialized structure that houses the flowers. Ficus carica is typically cultivated for its fruit, which is rich in nutrients and has a sweet, chewy texture.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
