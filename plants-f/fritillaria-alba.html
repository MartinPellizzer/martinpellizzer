Fritillaria alba

Fritillaria alba, commonly known as the white fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive, often speckled pattern. The plant is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats. It is a member of the Liliaceae family and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The bulbs of Fritillaria alba are also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
