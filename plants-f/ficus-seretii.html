Ficus seretii

Ficus seretii, commonly known as the Seretii fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and distinctive bark. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus seretii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
