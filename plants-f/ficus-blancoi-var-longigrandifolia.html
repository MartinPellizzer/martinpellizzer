Ficus blancoi var. longigrandifolia

Ficus blancoi var. longigrandifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved fig tree, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elongated leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
