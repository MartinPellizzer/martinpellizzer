Ficus polynervis

Ficus polynervis, commonly known as the broadleaf fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, broad leaves that are often lobed or deeply veined, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically enclosed in a spherical, greenish husk. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
