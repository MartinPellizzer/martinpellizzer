Fadogia andersonii

Fadogia andersonii, commonly known as African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It produces distinctive yellow-orange flowers and pods that contain edible seeds. The plant is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and twining stems, which help it climb other vegetation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in savanna and forest edges.

Fadogia andersonii is also valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional cuisine and have nutritional significance.
