Ferula glauca

Ferula glauca, commonly known as blue fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, umbrella-shaped inflorescences. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are aromatic and have a licorice-like scent, making it useful in traditional medicine.

Ferula glauca is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens.
