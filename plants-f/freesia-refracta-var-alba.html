Freesia refracta var. alba

Freesia refracta var. alba, commonly known as the white freesia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically white or pale yellow, with a sweet, spicy scent that attracts pollinators. This variety is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and floral arrangements.
