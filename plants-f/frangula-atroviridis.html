Frangula atroviridis

Frangula atroviridis, commonly known as the Canadian buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring. The plant is notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, including clay and sandy soils. Its fruit, which matures into a dark blue berry, is an important food source for birds and small mammals.

The plant contains anthraquinone compounds, making it useful in traditional medicine and as a laxative.
