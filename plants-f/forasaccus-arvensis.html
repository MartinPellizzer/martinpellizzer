Forasaccus arvensis

Forasaccus arvensis, commonly known as the field saccate grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions.

It typically grows in open habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. The plant has slender, tufted shoots with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly hairy. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

Forasaccus arvensis is known for its adaptability and is often found in agricultural landscapes.
