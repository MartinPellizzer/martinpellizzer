Festuca chitagana

Festuca chitagana, commonly known as the Chitagana fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky, and mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is characterized by its fibrous root system and drought tolerance.
