Fagraea oblonga

Fagraea oblonga, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green foliage and striking clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in late winter to early spring. The plant produces small, round, orange-red fruits that contain a single seed. Fagraea oblonga is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant flowers and attractive foliage.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is relatively low maintenance once established.
