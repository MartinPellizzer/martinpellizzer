Ficus hyrcana

Ficus hyrcana, commonly known as the Hyrcanian fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Central Asia, including regions of Iran, Afghanistan, and Turkmenistan. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus hyrcana is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
