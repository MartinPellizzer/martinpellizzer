Fagopyrum esculentum

Fagopyrum esculentum, commonly known as buckwheat, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Polygonaceae family.

It is characterized by its triangular leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or red. The plant is native to Asia but has been cultivated in various regions for its nutritional and agricultural value. Its seeds are rich in protein and contain compounds that can be used as a coffee substitute.

Buckwheat also plays an important role in soil stabilization and is often used in crop rotation systems.
