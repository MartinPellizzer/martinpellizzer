Faberia racemifera

Faberia racemifera, commonly known as the raceme faberia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments. Faberia racemifera produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in semi-arid regions.
