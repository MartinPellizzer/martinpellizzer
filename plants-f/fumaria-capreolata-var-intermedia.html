Fumaria capreolata var. intermedia

Fumaria capreolata var. intermedia, commonly known as the intermediate cowslip, is a variant of the cowslip plant native to Europe.

It is a flowering plant in the family Fumariaceae, characterized by its delicate, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and meadows. It has slender, branching stems with lance-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that attract pollinators like bees.

The botanical features of this variety make it a notable species in botanical studies and ecological surveys.
