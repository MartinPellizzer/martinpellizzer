Fridericia poeppigii

Fridericia poeppigii, commonly known as the Brazilian broom, is a shrub native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, greenish-yellow flowers. The plant produces trifoliate leaves and is often found in dry, open habitats such as savannas and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
