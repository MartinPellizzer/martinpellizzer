Forrestia marginata var. villosa

Forrestia marginata var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy forestiera, is a shrub native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, velvety hairs, giving it a distinctive texture. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. These flowers are followed by small, spherical fruits that contain several seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical gardens.
