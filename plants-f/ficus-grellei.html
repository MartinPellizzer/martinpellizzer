Ficus grellei

Ficus grellei, commonly known as the Grelle's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green color. Ficus grellei produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.
