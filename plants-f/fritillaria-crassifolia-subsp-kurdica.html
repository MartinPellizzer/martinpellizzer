Fritillaria crassifolia subsp. kurdica

Fritillaria crassifolia subsp.

kurdica, commonly known as the Kurdish bellflower, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus. It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with dark markings.

The plant typically grows in rocky or dry habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in harsh environments, with a dormancy period during the summer months.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
