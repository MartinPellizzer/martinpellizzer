Ficus vallis-choudae

Ficus vallis-choudae, commonly known as the Valley Chouda Fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green in color. It produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when mature.

Ficus vallis-choudae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively easy care requirements.
