Fritillaria cirrhosa var. brevistigma

Fritillaria cirrhosa var. brevistigma, commonly known as the Himalayan fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or red, with a distinctive, often speckled pattern. The plant produces a tall, slender stem that bears a single flower, which blooms in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

This species is adapted to cold climates and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.
