Fragaria nipponica var. orientalis

Fragaria nipponica var. orientalis, commonly known as the Japanese strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant bears red, fleshy fruits that are smaller than typical strawberries but have a tart flavor. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible berries.

This variety is closely related to other strawberries in the Fragaria genus.
