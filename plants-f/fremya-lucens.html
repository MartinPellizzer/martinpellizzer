Fremya lucens

Fremya lucens, commonly known as the golden wattle, is a flowering plant native to Australia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits. Fremya lucens is often found in open woodlands and along coastal areas, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

It plays an important ecological role by fixing nitrogen in the soil, benefiting surrounding plant life.
