Ficus citrifolia

Ficus citrifolia, commonly known as the citrus fig or the common fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to southern Europe, North Africa, and western Asia, and it thrives in warm, temperate climates. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and its ability to produce edible figs. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to tolerate various soil types and environmental conditions.

The plant is often cultivated for its fruit, which has a sweet, aromatic flavor and is used in both culinary and medicinal applications.
