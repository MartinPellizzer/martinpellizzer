Festuca longifolia

Festuca longifolia, commonly known as slender fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in meadows, woodlands, and rocky slopes, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute blue or purple flowers. Festuca longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.

It is also an important food source for various insects and small mammals.
