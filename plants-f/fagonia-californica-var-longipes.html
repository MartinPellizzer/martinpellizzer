Fagonia californica var. longipes

Fagonia californica var. longipes, commonly known as the longstem fagonia, is a perennial shrub native to coastal areas of Southern California.

It is characterized by its woody stems, gray-green foliage, and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to coastal climates, tolerating salt spray and well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a distinctive, slightly hairy texture.

This variety is often found in sandy or rocky habitats along the coastline.
