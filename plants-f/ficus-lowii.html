Ficus lowii

Ficus lowii, commonly known as the Bornean fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, inedible fruits that are typically pollinated by a specific species of fig wasp. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Ficus lowii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
