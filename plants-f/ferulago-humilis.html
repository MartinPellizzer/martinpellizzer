Ferulago humilis

Ferulago humilis, commonly known as the lesser ferulago, is a low-growing, herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically reaches a height of about 10 to 30 centimeters and features basal leaves that are lanceolate to ovate in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters during the spring and early summer. Its flowers are often clustered in umbels, and the plant is known for its aromatic, slightly bitter leaves.

Ferulago humilis is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments.
