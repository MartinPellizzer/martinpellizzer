Fumaria glaucescens

Fumaria glaucescens, commonly known as the blue poppy, is a species of flowering plant in the family Fumariaceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in alpine or subalpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in a fine layer of hairs.

Fumaria glaucescens is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
