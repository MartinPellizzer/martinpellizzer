Ferula sinkiangensis

Ferula sinkiangensis, commonly known as the Xinjiang ferula, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia, particularly in China's Xinjiang Uyghur Autonomous Region.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stem and large, fern-like leaves that are adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and low precipitation. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in a spike, which bloom in late summer. Its taproot is thick and fleshy, capable of storing water to survive in harsh desert conditions.

Ferula sinkiangensis is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
