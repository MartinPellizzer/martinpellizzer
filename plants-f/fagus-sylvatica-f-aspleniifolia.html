Fagus sylvatica f. aspleniifolia

Fagus sylvatica f. aspleniifolia, commonly known as the aspleniifolium beech, is a unique variant of the common beech.

It is characterized by its finely dissected, fern-like leaves that give it a delicate, almost lacey appearance. This form is a naturally occurring variant, often found in shaded, moist environments within forests. The leaves are typically a dark green on top and lighter underneath, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is valued in horticulture for its distinctive foliage and is often used in shaded garden settings.
