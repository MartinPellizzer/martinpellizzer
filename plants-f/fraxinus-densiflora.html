Fraxinus densiflora

Fraxinus densiflora, commonly known as the dense-flowered ash.

It is a deciduous tree native to the Pacific Northwest of North America. The plant features finely toothed, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers appear in dense clusters during the spring.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to moist, well-drained soils.
