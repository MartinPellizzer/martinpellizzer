Flemingia macrophylla var. nana

Flemingia macrophylla var. nana, commonly known as the dwarf flemingia, is a compact shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for use in shaded garden beds or as a container plant.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
