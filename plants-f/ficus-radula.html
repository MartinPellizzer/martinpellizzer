Ficus radula

Ficus radula, commonly known as the rock fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its small, glossy leaves and unique, clustered inflorescences. The plant thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions. It has a woody stem and produces small, edible figs that are typically consumed by local wildlife.

Ficus radula is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.
