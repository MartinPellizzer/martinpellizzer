Festuca ovina f. stenostachys

Festuca ovina f. stenostachys, commonly known as narrow-stemmed sheep's fescue, is a grass species characterized by its compact growth habit and slender, upright stems.

It is a cool-season perennial that thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a blue-green coloration that adds ornamental value to gardens. This variety is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to form dense, low-maintenance lawns or ground covers.

Its flowers are small and spike-like, forming dense clusters that contribute to its distinctive appearance.
