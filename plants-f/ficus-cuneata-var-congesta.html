Ficus cuneata var. congesta

Ficus cuneata var. congesta, commonly known as the clustered fig or the dense-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its dense foliage, with small, glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are often arranged in clusters. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically clustered in groups.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor conditions.
