Ficus durandiana

Ficus durandiana, commonly known as the Indian rubber plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and form a dense canopy, which is a notable feature in its natural habitat.

Ficus durandiana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities.
