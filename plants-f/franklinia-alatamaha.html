Franklinia alatamaha

Franklinia alatamaha, commonly known as the Franklin tree, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of Georgia and Florida.

It is characterized by its evergreen foliage, which is dark green and glossy, and its showy, pale pink to white flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has a unique, upright growth habit with a single trunk and a rounded crown. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and oval in shape, with a leathery texture.

Franklinia alatamaha is also notable for its fragrant flowers and its historical significance as a species that was once thought to be extinct.
