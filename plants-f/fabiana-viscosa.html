Fabiana viscosa

Fabiana viscosa, commonly known as sticky phlox, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and hairy, glandular stems that produce a sticky substance. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant, pink or purple flowers, which bloom in spring and early summer. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered with fine hairs.

Fabiana viscosa is often cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens due to its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soil.
