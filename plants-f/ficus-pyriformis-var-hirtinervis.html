Ficus pyriformis var. hirtinervis

Ficus pyriformis var. hirtinervis, commonly known as the hirsute-veined fig, is a tropical plant characterized by its unique, pear-shaped leaves.

The leaves are typically large, glossy, and have a distinctive hairy texture along the veins, giving it its scientific name. It produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often used in local cuisines. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in greenhouses.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its striking foliage and ease of care.
