Flickingeria quadriloba

Flickingeria quadriloba, commonly known as the four-lobed bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its distinctive four-lobed petals that form a bell-shaped bloom. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, arranged oppositely along the stems.

Flickingeria quadriloba is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
