Festuca patagonica

Festuca patagonica, commonly known as Patagonian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in temperate climates and is often found in grasslands and open slopes. Festuca patagonica is well adapted to withstand cold temperatures and is relatively drought-tolerant.

Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.
