Ficus cassidyana

Ficus cassidyana, commonly known as the Cassidy's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green in color. It produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature.

Ficus cassidyana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
