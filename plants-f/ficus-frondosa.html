Ficus frondosa

Ficus frondosa, commonly known as the giant ficus or broadleaf ficus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant has a dense, bushy growth habit and produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by edible figs. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus frondosa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
