Ficus hirta

Ficus hirta, commonly known as the woolly fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, including countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Ficus hirta typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often found in forest understories.

Its small, greenish-white flowers are followed by edible figs that are important for both wildlife and local communities.
