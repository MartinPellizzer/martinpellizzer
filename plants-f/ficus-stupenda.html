Ficus stupenda

Ficus stupenda, commonly known as the giant rubber tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by oval-shaped fruits containing seeds. It is a member of the Moraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in humid environments.

Ficus stupenda is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
