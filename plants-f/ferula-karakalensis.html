Ferula karakalensis

Ferula karakalensis, commonly known as Karakalinsky wormwood, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes plants like carrots and parsley. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. It produces yellowish-green flowers in umbels, typical of the family.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.
