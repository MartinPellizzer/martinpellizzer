Ficus torresiana

Ficus torresiana, commonly known as the Torresiana fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically elongated and pointed. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are often consumed by birds and mammals. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
