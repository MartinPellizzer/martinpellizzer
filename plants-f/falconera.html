Falconera

Falconera, commonly known as the sacred lotus of ancient Egypt, is a striking plant with large, showy flowers that bloom in shades of pink and white.

It is native to the Nile River region and thrives in moist, sunny environments. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, supported by long, slender stems. Its flowers are often associated with purity and rebirth in Egyptian mythology.

Falconera plays a significant role in both ecological and cultural contexts within its native habitat.
