Festuca spectabilis subsp. affinis

Festuca spectabilis subsp.

affinis, commonly known as the showy fescue, is a perennial grass native to eastern North America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that produce dense clusters of spikelets.

The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green hue, and its flowers are small, spike-like structures that typically bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a valuable ornamental and ecological plant. Festuca spectabilis subsp.

affinis is often used in native plant gardens for its aesthetic appeal and ability to support local wildlife.
