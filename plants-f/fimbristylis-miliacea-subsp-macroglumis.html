Fimbristylis miliacea subsp. macroglumis

Fimbristylis miliacea subsp.

macroglumis, commonly known as the broad-leaved flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions. It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats such as marshes, swamps, and rice fields.

The plant features grass-like leaves that are broad and flat, with a fibrous root system adapted to waterlogged soils. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-ranked flowers.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
