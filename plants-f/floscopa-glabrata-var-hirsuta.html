Floscopa glabrata var. hirsuta

Floscopa glabrata var. hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy floscopa, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hairy stems and leaves, which give it its varietal name "hirsuta.". The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
