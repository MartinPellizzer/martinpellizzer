Faba vulgaris var. waterlooensis

Faba vulgaris var. waterlooensis, commonly known as the Waterloo bean, is a rare variant of the fava bean native to specific regions in Europe.

It is characterized by its small, elongated seeds that are typically green or yellow in color. The plant thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil with moderate moisture. Its botanical features include trifoliate leaves and a flowering pattern typical of the Fabaceae family.

Due to its limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
