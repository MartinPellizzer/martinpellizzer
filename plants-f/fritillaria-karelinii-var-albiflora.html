Fritillaria karelinii var. albiflora

Fritillaria karelinii var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that are predominantly white, with some varieties displaying pale yellow or green hues. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, stout stem. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Fritillaria karelinii var. albiflora is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
