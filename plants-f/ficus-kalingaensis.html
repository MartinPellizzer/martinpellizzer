Ficus kalingaensis

Ficus kalingaensis, commonly known as the Kalinga fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and small, clustered fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite and have a smooth, waxy texture, while the fruit is a small, round fig that ripens to a yellowish color.

Ficus kalingaensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
