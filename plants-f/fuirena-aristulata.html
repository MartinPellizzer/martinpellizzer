Fuirena aristulata

Fuirena aristulata, commonly known as the purple-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically ranging in color from purple to blue. The leaves are narrow and lanceolate, often covered in a fine pubescence. This plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold climates.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and alpine landscapes.
