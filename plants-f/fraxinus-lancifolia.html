Fraxinus lancifolia

Fraxinus lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ash.

It is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long and have a smooth, glossy surface. Its flowers are small and greenish-yellow, appearing in catkins during the spring.

The tree produces samaras, which are winged seeds that aid in wind dispersal.
