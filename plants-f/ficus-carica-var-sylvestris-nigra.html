Ficus carica var. sylvestris-nigra

Ficus carica var. sylvestris-nigra, commonly known as the black fig, is a variant of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its dark, almost black fruits that are typically smaller than those of the standard fig variety. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and unique fruit coloration.
