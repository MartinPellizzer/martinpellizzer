Faba vulgaris var. viridinana

Faba vulgaris var. viridinana, commonly known as the green vetch, is a leguminous plant characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits.

It typically grows in temperate regions and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility. Its foliage is often used as forage for livestock, and it is also valued in traditional herbal medicine.

The species is notable for its adaptability to various environmental conditions.
