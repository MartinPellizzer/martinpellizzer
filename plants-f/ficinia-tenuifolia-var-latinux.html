Ficinia tenuifolia var. latinux

Ficinia tenuifolia var. latinux, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedum, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It features small, fleshy leaves that are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention. The plant produces clusters of star-shaped flowers, often in shades of yellow or orange, during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its compact size and drought tolerance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and dry landscape designs.
