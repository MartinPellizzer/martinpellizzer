Ficus condaravia

Ficus condaravia, commonly known as the Condorava ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color.

Ficus condaravia is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
