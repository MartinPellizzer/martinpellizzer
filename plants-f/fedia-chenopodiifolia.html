Fedia chenopodiifolia

Fedia chenopodiifolia, commonly known as the desert sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing perennial herb with succulent, fleshy leaves that help it conserve water in its harsh environment. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically wind-pollinated. Its roots can extend deep into the soil to access moisture, allowing it to survive in extreme drought conditions.

Fedia chenopodiifolia plays a role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for desert wildlife.
