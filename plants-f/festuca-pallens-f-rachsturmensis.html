Festuca pallens f. rachsturmensis

Festuca pallens f. rachsturmensis, commonly known as the silver hair grass, is a variant of the silvergrass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray blades and dense, tufted growth habit, which contribute to its ornamental value. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its foliage remains evergreen throughout the year, providing visual interest in winter landscapes.

The inflorescence consists of slender, spike-like clusters that bloom in late summer, adding a delicate touch to its appearance.
