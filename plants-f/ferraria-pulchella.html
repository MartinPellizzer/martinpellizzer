Ferraria pulchella

Ferraria pulchella, commonly known as the "pink bellflower," is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of delicate, pinkish-white flowers resembling bells. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ferraria pulchella is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and is also used in traditional medicine.
