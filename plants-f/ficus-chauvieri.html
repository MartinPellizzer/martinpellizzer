Ficus chauvieri

Ficus chauvieri, commonly known as the Chauvier's fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fig-like fruits that are edible for some bird species. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Due to its slow growth and adaptability, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
