Flaveria anomala

Flaveria anomala, commonly known as the silverleaf flaveria, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its silver-gray foliage, which results from dense covering of fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, and it is often found in arid and semi-arid environments. Flaveria anomala is known for its adaptability to drought conditions, making it a common weed in disturbed soils.

Its rapid growth and reproductive strategies contribute to its ecological success in various habitats.
