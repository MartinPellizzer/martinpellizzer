Festuca ovina subsp. occitanica

Festuca ovina subsp.

occitanica, commonly known as the Occitanian sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, pastures, and rocky slopes.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in regions with well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
