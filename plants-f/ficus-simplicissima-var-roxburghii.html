Ficus simplicissima var. roxburghii

Ficus simplicissima var. roxburghii, commonly known as the Roxburgh's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of India and Myanmar. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. It is a small to medium-sized tree, often growing up to 10 meters in height.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens due to its adaptability and aesthetic qualities.
