Ficus bongoensis

Ficus bongoensis, commonly known as the bongo fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the rainforests of West Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, clustered fig fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife species. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
