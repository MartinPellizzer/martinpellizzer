Fernandezia chaparensis

Fernandezia chaparensis, commonly known as the Chapare fern, is a member of the Polypodiaceae family and is native to South America, particularly Bolivia and Peru.

It is a terrestrial fern that typically grows in humid, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features fronds that are usually pinnately divided, with a distinctive, lanceolate shape. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, a characteristic common among many fern species.

Fernandezia chaparensis plays a role in maintaining soil stability and providing habitat for various microorganisms in its native ecosystem.
