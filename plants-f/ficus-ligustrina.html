Ficus ligustrina

Ficus ligustrina, commonly known as the Chinese rubber fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are usually heart-shaped or ovate in form. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by woody, capsule-like fruits containing seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in indoor settings.

Ficus ligustrina is also known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its adaptability to various soil types.
