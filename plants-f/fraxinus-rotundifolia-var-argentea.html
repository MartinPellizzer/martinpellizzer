Fraxinus rotundifolia var. argentea

Fraxinus rotundifolia var. argentea, commonly known as silver-leafed ash, is a variant of the European ash tree characterized by its distinctive silvery-white undersides on its leaves.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in temperate regions. The leaves are ovate to round in shape, with a smooth margin and a prominent midrib. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, while the fruit is a samara with a winged seed.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its aesthetic foliage.
