Forsythia × intermedia var. primulina

Forsythia × intermedia var. primulina, commonly known as the Japanese forsythia, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy. It typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and is often used in ornamental gardening for its vibrant floral display.

The shrub is relatively hardy and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.
