Festuca ovina f. breviaristata

Festuca ovina f. breviaristata, commonly known as the shortawn sheep's fescue, is a grass species characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage.

It is a low-growing perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with short, stiff hairs along the edges, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and erosion control.
