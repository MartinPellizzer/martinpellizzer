Festuca oranensis

Festuca oranensis, commonly known as Oran bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Festuca oranensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Its reproductive structures include small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
