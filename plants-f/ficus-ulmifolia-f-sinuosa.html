Ficus ulmifolia f. sinuosa

Ficus ulmifolia f. sinuosa, commonly known as the sinuous fig, is a variant of the common fig tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that have a distinctive sinuous margin, giving it its common name. The leaves are typically glossy and dark green, with a smooth texture. It produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
