Fuchsia lehmannii

Fuchsia lehmannii, commonly known as Lehmann's fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in temperate rainforests. The plant produces striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep red to purple, with showy, tubular corollas. Its leaves are ovate and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

Fuchsia lehmannii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and well-draining soil.
