Fritillaria tuntasia

Fritillaria tuntasia, commonly known as the Sierra Valley fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or yellow with contrasting black markings. The plant features a fibrous root system and has narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

The flowers bloom in early spring, adding a vibrant splash of color to its natural environment.
