Festuca longifolia f. villosa

Festuca longifolia f. villosa, commonly known as the hairy bluegrass, is a grass species characterized by its distinctive silver-blue foliage.

It is a cool-season plant native to the northern regions of North America, particularly in the United States and Canada. The plant features slender, tufted growth with long, narrow leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a silvery appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and native plant gardens.
