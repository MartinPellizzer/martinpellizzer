Faucaria subintegra

Faucaria subintegra, commonly known as the ice plant or snowplant, is a succulent plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its fleshy, bluish-green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, which helps it retain moisture in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers in shades of pink, red, or white, typically during the spring and summer months. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements make it a favored plant among succulent enthusiasts.
