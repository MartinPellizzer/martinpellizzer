Fraxinus borealis

Fraxinus borealis, commonly known as the Canadian white ash.

It is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, compound leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by samaras with two papery wings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forests and along streambanks.

The tree is valued for its strong, flexible wood and has ecological importance as a host for various insects and birds.
