Ficus usambarensis

Ficus usambarensis, commonly known as the Usambara fig, is a species of flowering plant native to the East African mountains.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit, a fig-like structure, is typically green when mature and is an important food source for local wildlife.

Ficus usambarensis is often found growing in humid, shaded environments at higher altitudes.
