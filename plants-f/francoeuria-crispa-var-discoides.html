Francoeuria crispa var. discoides

Francoeuria crispa var. discoides, commonly known as the discoid false heather, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to the western United States.

It features dense clusters of small, waxy, white to pale blue flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The leaves are typically elliptical, leathery, and arranged in whorls around the stems. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or as a ground cover.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and drought tolerance.
