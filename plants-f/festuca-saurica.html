Festuca saurica

Festuca saurica, commonly known as Siberian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate and cold regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. Its foliage is typically narrow and grass-like, with a blue-green hue that becomes more pronounced in cooler climates.

Festuca saurica is well adapted to harsh environments, making it a hardy ornamental and ecological plant in gardens and natural landscapes.
