Froesiodendron urceocalyx

Froesiodendron urceocalyx, commonly known as the bell-shaped froesiodendron, is a flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often hairy and have a lanceolate shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is typically found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and specialized species.
