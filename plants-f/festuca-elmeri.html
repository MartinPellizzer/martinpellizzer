Festuca elmeri

Festuca elmeri, commonly known as Elmer's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to western North America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has fine, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and is often used in ecological restoration projects.

Festuca elmeri is valued for its ability to stabilize soil and support local wildlife.
