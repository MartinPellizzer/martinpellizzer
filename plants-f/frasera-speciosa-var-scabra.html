Frasera speciosa var. scabra

Frasera speciosa var. scabra, commonly known as the rough frasera, is a perennial flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its hairy, rough-textured stems and leaves, which give it its scientific variety name "scabra.". The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink to purple in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas or along streambanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
