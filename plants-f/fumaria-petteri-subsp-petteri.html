Fumaria petteri subsp. petteri

Fumaria petteri subsp.

petteri, commonly known as the Persian celandine, is a herbaceous annual plant native to regions of the Middle East and Central Asia. It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and clusters of bright yellow flowers.

The leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a waxy coating, while the flowers are borne in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or semi-arid habitats.

The plant is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
