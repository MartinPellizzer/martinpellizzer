Ficus edelfeltii

Ficus edelfeltii, commonly known as the African fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically consumed by local communities. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is also cultivated as a houseplant in warmer climates.
