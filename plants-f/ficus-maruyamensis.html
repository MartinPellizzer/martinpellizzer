Ficus maruyamensis

Ficus maruyamensis, commonly known as the Maruyamensis fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.

The fruit, known as a fig, is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor.
