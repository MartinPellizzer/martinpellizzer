Ficus rubicunda

Ficus rubicunda, commonly known as the red-fig ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with a reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, fig-like fruits. Ficus rubicunda is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.

It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate watering to maintain its health.
