Ficus simmondsii

Ficus simmondsii, commonly known as the Indian rubber plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and is often cultivated as a houseplant. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fig-like fruits, though these are rarely seen in cultivation.

Ficus simmondsii is valued for its adaptability and air-purifying qualities.
