Fedia monandra

Fedia monandra, commonly known as the African white wood, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod-like fruits. The plant is notable for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, contributing to soil fertility. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Fedia monandra is valued for its timber and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
