Fernandezia crystallina

Fernandezia crystallina, commonly known as the crystalline fern, is a unique species belonging to the family Polypodiaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often adorned with small, translucent structures resembling crystals. This plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, such as tropical forests or rocky crevices. Its rhizome is short and thick, anchoring it to the substrate while supporting its aerial fronds.

The plant's ornamental value comes from its intricate, lace-like appearance, making it a sought-after specimen in botanical gardens.
