Festuca × hackelii

Festuca × hackelii, commonly known as the bluegrass or Kentucky bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green blades and dense, sod-forming growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is highly tolerant of cold climates and moderate drought.

Festuca × hackelii is widely used in lawns, sports fields, and restoration projects due to its durability and aesthetic appeal.
