Ficus leucoptera

Ficus leucoptera, commonly known as the white-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, waxy leaves that have a distinctive whitish or silver underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, three-lobed fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Ficus leucoptera is also valued for its ability to adapt to various soil types and its resilience to pests and diseases.
