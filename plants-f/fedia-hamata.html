Fedia hamata

Fedia hamata, commonly known as the hairy fig tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with thick, rough bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in terminal inflorescences. Its fruit is a fig-like structure that turns from green to yellow or red when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
