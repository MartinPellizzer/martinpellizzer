Freesia corymbosa var. aurea

Freesia corymbosa var. aurea, commonly known as the golden freesia, is a perennial bulbous plant native to South Africa.

It produces clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and gold, which bloom in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and floral arrangements.

The golden freesia is also popular in horticulture for its ability to hybridize with other freesia varieties.
