Ficus populnea f. eupopulnea

Ficus populnea f. eupopulnea, commonly known as the Poplar fig, is a variant of the common fig tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed and have a distinctive white underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically clustered in spherical infructescences. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a shade tree.
