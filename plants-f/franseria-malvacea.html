Franseria malvacea

Franseria malvacea, commonly known as the velvetleaf or swampmallow, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes hibiscus and cotton, and is characterized by its showy, pink or purple flowers. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a velvety texture and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks. It produces capsule fruits that contain numerous small, wind-dispersed seeds.

Franseria malvacea is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in supporting pollinators.
