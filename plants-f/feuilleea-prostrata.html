Feuilleea prostrata

Feuilleea prostrata, commonly known as the creeping leaf plant, is a low-growing herbaceous species native to tropical regions.

It features slender, prostrate stems that spread along the ground, often rooting at the nodes. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a smooth or slightly hairy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color.

This plant is often used in ornamental horticulture for its ability to form dense, ground-covering mats.
