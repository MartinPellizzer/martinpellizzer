Fimbristylis quinquangularis subsp. macroglumis

Fimbristylis quinquangularis subsp.

macroglumis, commonly known as the broad-leaved spikegrass, is a perennial grass species native to tropical and subtropical regions. It typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes and seasonally flooded areas.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces spike-like inflorescences with small, clustered flowers. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a tendency to form dense mats.

This subspecies is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support biodiversity.
