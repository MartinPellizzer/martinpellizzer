Filipendula palmata var. glabra

Filipendula palmata var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth leaf false spirea, is a perennial shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which are smooth and often have a velvety texture. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers in the summer, which are attractive to pollinators. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
