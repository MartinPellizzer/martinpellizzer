Fumaria parviflora var. lutea

Fumaria parviflora var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow fumitory, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the Fumariaceae family and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors or along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

The yellow fumitory is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
