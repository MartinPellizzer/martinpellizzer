Fissistigma mabiforme

Fissistigma mabiforme, commonly known as the golden trumpet vine, is a climbing plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking yellow, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant has pinnate leaves with three to five leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, sticky hairs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Fissistigma mabiforme is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
