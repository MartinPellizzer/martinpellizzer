Frangula palmeri

Frangula palmeri, commonly known as Palmer's buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. The plant's berries are dark blue to black and are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils and along streambanks.

Frangula palmeri has a taproot system that allows it to survive in harsh, dry conditions.
