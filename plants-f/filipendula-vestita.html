Filipendula vestita

Filipendula vestita, commonly known as white meadow beauty, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, white, fluffy flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist meadows and stream sides, preferring partial to full sunlight. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are serrated along the edges.

Filipendula vestita is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and can also serve as a food source for certain pollinators.
