Fumaria alexandrina

Fumaria alexandrina, commonly known as the Alexandrian smokeplant, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces slender, hairy stems that often lie close to the ground, and its flowers are typically borne in racemes. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Fumaria alexandrina is known for its ornamental value and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
