Fimbribambusa horsfieldii

Fimbribambusa horsfieldii, commonly known as the Horsfield's bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Bambusoideae subfamily and is characterized by its tall, slender culms that can reach heights of up to 10 meters. The plant has a distinctive appearance with its finely textured leaves and a hollow, segmented stem structure typical of bamboos. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Fimbribambusa horsfieldii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for use in landscaping and traditional crafts.
