Ficus oleifolia var. impressicostata

Ficus oleifolia var. impressicostata, commonly known as the wrinkled-ribbed fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply wrinkled leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually a deep green with a leathery texture, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
