Ficus laevigata subvar. subpyriformis

Ficus laevigata subvar. subpyriformis, commonly known as the smooth-leaf fig, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a glossy surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, spherical fruits that are typically yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
