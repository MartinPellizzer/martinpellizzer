Festuca michaelis

Festuca michaelis, commonly known as Michael's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along streambanks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Festuca michaelis is also notable for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
