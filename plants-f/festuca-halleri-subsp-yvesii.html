Festuca halleri subsp. yvesii

Festuca halleri subsp.

yvesii, commonly known as Yves' fescue, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the Alps. It features slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny blue to purple flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountain meadows and rocky soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural interest.
