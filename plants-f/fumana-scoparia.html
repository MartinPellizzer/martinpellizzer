Fumana scoparia

Fumana scoparia, commonly known as the desert holly, is a succulent plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in spring, adding a delicate touch of color to its otherwise muted appearance. Its leaves are often spine-tipped, providing a defense against herbivores.

Fumana scoparia is well adapted to low rainfall and high temperatures, making it a hardy species for xerophytic landscapes.
