Ficus altissima f. laccifera

Ficus altissima f. laccifera, commonly known as the lacca fig, is a cultivar of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and the production of small, edible fruits. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit yield.

The leaves and fruits are used in traditional medicine and culinary practices in various regions.
