Ficus rigida var. bracteata

Ficus rigida var. bracteata, commonly known as the bracteate rubber fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green ovate leaves that are often pubescent underneath, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by green, fleshy berries. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings. It thrives in warm, humid conditions with indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Ficus rigida var. bracteata is also known for its ability to adapt to indoor environments, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
