Fragaria viridis subsp. viridis

Fragaria viridis subsp.

viridis, commonly known as the green strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It produces small, edible berries that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and grows in moist, shaded environments. Its flowers are white and have five petals, and it is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The species is valued for its culinary use and is also studied for its ecological role in supporting pollinators.
