Ficus ferruginea

Ficus ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, featuring smooth, gray-brown bark and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically yellow or red in color. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
