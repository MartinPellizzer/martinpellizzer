Festuca steinbachii

Festuca steinbachii, commonly known as Steinbach's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along riverbanks.

Festuca steinbachii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
