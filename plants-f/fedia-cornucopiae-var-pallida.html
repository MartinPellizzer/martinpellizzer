Fedia cornucopiae var. pallida

Fedia cornucopiae var. pallida, commonly known as the Moonlit Bloom, is a rare and striking plant native to the misty highlands of the Andes.

It is characterized by its pale, almost translucent leaves that shimmer with a silvery sheen under moonlight. The plant produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of lavender and white, which open only during the night. Its roots are known to absorb minerals from the soil, contributing to its unique biochemical composition.

Due to its rarity and aesthetic appeal, it is highly sought after by botanists and horticulturists alike.
