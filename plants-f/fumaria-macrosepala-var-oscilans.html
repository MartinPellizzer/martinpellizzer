Fumaria macrosepala var. oscilans

Fumaria macrosepala var. oscilans, commonly known as the wavy-flowered glory-of-the-snow, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous yellow flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy surface, and it often grows in rocky or sandy soils. It is a member of the Fumariaceae family, known for its medicinal and ornamental uses.

The varietal name "oscilans" refers to the wavy or undulating appearance of its flower petals.
