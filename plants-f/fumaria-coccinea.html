Fumaria coccinea

Fumaria coccinea, commonly known as redweed, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its bright red flowers that bloom in early summer, attracting various pollinators. The plant has alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy to the touch. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in fields, roadsides, and along fences.

Fumaria coccinea is also known for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
