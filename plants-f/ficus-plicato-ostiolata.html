Ficus plicato-ostiolata

Ficus plicato-ostiolata, commonly known as the pleated-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia, and is characterized by its unique, pleated leaves that give it its common name. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are glossy and often have a waxy texture, while the fruit is a small, round, greenish-yellow fig.

Ficus plicato-ostiolata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
