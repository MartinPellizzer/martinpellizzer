Fimbristima

Fimbristima, commonly known as the silky mistletoe, is a parasitic plant that grows on various host trees, primarily in tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Loranthaceae and is characterized by its leafless stems and clusters of small, fleshy berries. The plant's leaves are often covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it its common name. Fimbristima typically attaches to its host through haustoria, which penetrate the host's vascular system to absorb nutrients.

It plays a role in ecological systems by providing food for birds and other animals that consume its berries.
