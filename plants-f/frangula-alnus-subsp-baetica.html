Frangula alnus subsp. baetica

Frangula alnus subsp.

baetica, commonly known as the Spanish alder, is a shrub or small tree native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, elliptical leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by dark blue-black berries that are edible for birds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found near streams or in wetlands.

This subspecies is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing riverbanks and providing habitat for wildlife.
