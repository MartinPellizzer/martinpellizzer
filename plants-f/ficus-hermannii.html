Ficus hermannii

Ficus hermannii, commonly known as the Hermann's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped and have a waxy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fig fruits.

Ficus hermannii is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
