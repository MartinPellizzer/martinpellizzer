Ficinia bulbosa

Ficinia bulbosa, commonly known as the bulbous figs, is a flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bulbous underground stems and clusters of small, greenish-white flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often used in traditional cuisines. Ficinia bulbosa thrives in moist, tropical environments and is often found in shaded, humid areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural and ecological studies.
