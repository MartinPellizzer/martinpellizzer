Forestiera isabeliae

Forestiera isabeliae, commonly known as Isabel's forestiera, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for small garden spaces or as a border plant.

Botanically, it belongs to the Oleaceae family, which includes other well-known plants like lilac and jasmine.
