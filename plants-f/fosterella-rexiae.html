Fosterella rexiae

Fosterella rexiae, commonly known as the Rio Bravo pineapple.

This plant is a member of the Bromeliaceae family and is native to the tropical regions of South America. It is characterized by its rosette-shaped leaves that form a central cup, which collects water and nutrients. The plant produces vibrant orange flowers that emerge from the leaf axils.

Fosterella rexiae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
