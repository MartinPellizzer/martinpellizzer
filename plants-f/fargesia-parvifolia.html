Fargesia parvifolia

Fargesia parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved arrow bamboo, is a species of grass-like plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, clump-forming stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is cold-hardy and thrives in moist, well-drained soils, making it suitable for shaded garden environments. Its foliage remains evergreen throughout the year, adding a decorative element to landscapes.

Fargesia parvifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to create a dense, attractive ground cover.
