Frangula pendula

Frangula pendula, commonly known as the hanging buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its arching branches and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in early spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are an important food source for birds and wildlife. Frangula pendula thrives in moist, woodland habitats and is often found in streamside areas.

Its foliage is typically alternate and has a distinctive, elongated shape.
