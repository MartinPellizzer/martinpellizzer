Fraxinus undulata

Fraxinus undulata, commonly known as the wavy-leaf ash, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves that are serrated and undulate, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged seeds called samaras. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
