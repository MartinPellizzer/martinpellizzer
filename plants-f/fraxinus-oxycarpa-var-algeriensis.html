Fraxinus oxycarpa var. algeriensis

Fraxinus oxycarpa var. algeriensis, commonly known as the Algerian ash, is a rare and endemic species native to the Mediterranean region, particularly Algeria.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin, and they turn yellow in autumn. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in clusters, and it produces samaroid fruits that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
