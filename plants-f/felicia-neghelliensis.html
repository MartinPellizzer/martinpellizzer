Felicia neghelliensis

Felicia neghelliensis, commonly known as the Neghelli's fleur-de-lis, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble a cluster of small flowers. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy yellow flowers with a distinctive central disc. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its native habitat.
