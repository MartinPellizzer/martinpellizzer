Friedrichsthalia trichodesmoides

Friedrichsthalia trichodesmoides, commonly known as the Andean bellflower, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes in South America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant characterized by its erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to the cool, humid climates of its native habitat. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a fine, fuzzy layer, which helps to reduce water loss in its arid environment.
