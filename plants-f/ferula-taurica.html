Ferula taurica

Ferula taurica, commonly known as Tauric cow parsley, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces a strong-smelling, milky sap that contains volatile compounds. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, and it thrives in dry, rocky soils.

Ferula taurica is often found in steppe and semi-desert environments.
