Ferula pomelii

Ferula pomelii, commonly known as the mountain dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and yellow flowers. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic resin that has been used traditionally in folk medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, while its roots are thick and fleshy.

Ferula pomelii thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
