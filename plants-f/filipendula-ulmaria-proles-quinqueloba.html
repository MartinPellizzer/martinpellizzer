Filipendula ulmaria proles quinqueloba

Filipendula ulmaria proles quinqueloba, commonly known as meadow sweet, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with five lobes, giving it the specific epithet *quinqueloba*. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, sunny habitats such as meadows and stream banks.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and a subject of interest in horticulture.
