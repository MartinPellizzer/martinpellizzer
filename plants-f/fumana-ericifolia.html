Fumana ericifolia

Fumana ericifolia, commonly known as the heather-like shrub, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a low-growing, evergreen shrub that typically reaches heights of up to 30 centimeters. The plant features dense clusters of small, needle-like leaves that are covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, appearing in late summer to early autumn.

Fumana ericifolia thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and rocky habitats.
