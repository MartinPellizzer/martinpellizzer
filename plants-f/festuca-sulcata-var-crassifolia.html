Festuca sulcata var. crassifolia

Festuca sulcata var. crassifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It is well adapted to dry, well-drained soils and can tolerate moderate drought conditions.

This variety is often used in ornamental landscaping and as a low-maintenance ground cover.
