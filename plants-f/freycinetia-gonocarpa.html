Freycinetia gonocarpa

Freycinetia gonocarpa, commonly known as the red-fruit pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, including parts of Polynesia and Melanesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, paddle-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. Its fruit, which is fleshy and red when ripe, contains numerous seeds and is an important food source for local wildlife.

The plant plays a significant role in both ecological systems and cultural practices in its native habitats.
