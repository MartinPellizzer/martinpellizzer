Ficus tobagensis

Ficus tobagensis, commonly known as the Toba fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a waxy texture. It produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor.

Ficus tobagensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
