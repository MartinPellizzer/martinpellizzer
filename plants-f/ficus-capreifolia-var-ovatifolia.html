Ficus capreifolia var. ovatifolia

Ficus capreifolia var. ovatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a variant of the common fig tree characterized by its broad, ovate leaves.

It is native to parts of Asia, including China and India, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is known for its glossy, dark green foliage and can grow as a shrub or small tree. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as ornamental plants due to their aesthetic appeal.

The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible fig fruits under suitable conditions.
