Festuca varia var. wilczekiana

Festuca varia var. wilczekiana, commonly known as Wilczek's bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with blue to purple florets, which are typical of the Festuca genus. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
