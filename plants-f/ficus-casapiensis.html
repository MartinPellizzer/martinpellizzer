Ficus casapiensis

Ficus casapiensis, commonly known as the Casapiensis Fig Tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Peru and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Ficus casapiensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Its fruit, the fig, is edible and has been used in traditional medicine by local communities.
