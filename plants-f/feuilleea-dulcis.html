Feuilleea dulcis

Feuilleea dulcis, commonly known as the Sweetleaf plant, is a perennial herb native to tropical regions.

It features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its roots contain compounds with mild sedative properties, making it valuable in herbal remedies.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
