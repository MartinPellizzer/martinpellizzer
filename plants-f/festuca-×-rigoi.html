Festuca × rigoi

Festuca × rigoi, commonly known as the tall fescue, is a cool-season grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in a two-ranked pattern, with each spikelet containing two to three florets. Festuca × rigoi is often used in lawns and pastures due to its durability and tolerance to drought and grazing.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in both ornamental and agricultural settings.
