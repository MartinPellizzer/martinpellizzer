Festuca halleri var. dura

Festuca halleri var. dura, commonly known as hardy bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers in panicles during the summer months. It is adapted to grow in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and rocky habitats.

Festuca halleri var. dura is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
