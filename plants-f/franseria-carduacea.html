Franseria carduacea

Franseria carduacea, commonly known as the African heliotrope, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, with woody stems and small, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, pink to purple flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are often hairy and have a slightly aromatic scent.

Franseria carduacea is known for its ornamental value and is cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and flowers.
