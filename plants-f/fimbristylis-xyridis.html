Fimbristylis xyridis

Fimbristylis xyridis, commonly known as the slender flatsedge, is a grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and small, clustered flowers that typically bloom in late summer. This species is often found in wetland environments, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, forming dense mats that stabilize soil and provide habitat for various organisms.

Fimbristylis xyridis plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by contributing to biodiversity and soil structure.
