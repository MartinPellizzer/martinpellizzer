Farsetia multicaulis

Farsetia multicaulis, commonly known as the clustered farsetia, is a member of the Ericaceae family.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its dense, rounded growth habit and dark green, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in late spring. Farsetia multicaulis thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and prefers partial shade to full sun.

It is native to the mountainous regions of South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
