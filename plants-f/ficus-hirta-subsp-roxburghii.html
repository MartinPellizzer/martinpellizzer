Ficus hirta subsp. roxburghii

Ficus hirta subsp.

roxburghii, commonly known as the Himalayan fig, is a species native to the mountainous regions of the Indian subcontinent. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, dark green leaves that are often pubescent on the underside.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its fruit.
