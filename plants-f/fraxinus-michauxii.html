Fraxinus michauxii

Fraxinus michauxii, commonly known as the Michaux's ash, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in racemes, which are followed by samaroid fruits that are winged and dispersed by wind.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodlands and stream valleys.
