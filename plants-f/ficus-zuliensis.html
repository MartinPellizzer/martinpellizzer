Ficus zuliensis

Ficus zuliensis, commonly known as the Zulian fig, is a tropical species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate to elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
