Feuilleea vismiifolia

Feuilleea vismiifolia, commonly known as the leafy mist plant, is a unique species native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that resemble those of the vismiifolia genus, though it belongs to a distinct family. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and has a slow growth rate, making it a rare and sought-after specimen in botanical gardens. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported anti-inflammatory properties.

Due to habitat loss, Feuilleea vismiifolia is currently classified as an endangered species.
