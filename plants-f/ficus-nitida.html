Ficus nitida

Ficus nitida, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy dark green leaves that are arranged in a distinctive weeping pattern. The plant has a thick, gnarled trunk and produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, yellowish fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires moderate to bright indirect light.

Ficus nitida is popular as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ability to purify the air.
