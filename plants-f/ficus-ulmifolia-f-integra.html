Ficus ulmifolia f. integra

Ficus ulmifolia f. integra, commonly known as the smooth-leafed fig, is a compact, evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are smooth and leathery, with a distinctive oval shape. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature, though they may change color depending on the variety. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and is often used in indoor gardening due to its adaptability and low maintenance requirements.
