Felicia cameronii

Felicia cameronii, commonly known as the Cameron's fleur-de-lis, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, yellow-orange flower heads with white ray florets, resembling a fleur-de-lis pattern. Felicia cameronii is typically found in rocky soils and arid environments, thriving in full sun.

It is drought-tolerant and often used in xeriscaping due to its low maintenance requirements.
