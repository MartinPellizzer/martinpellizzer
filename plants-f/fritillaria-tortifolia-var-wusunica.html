Fritillaria tortifolia var. wusunica

Fritillaria tortifolia var. wusunica, commonly known as the Wusunica fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in harsh, arid environments, and it is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific growing conditions.
