Fuirena stricta subsp. chlorocarpa

Fuirena stricta subsp.

chlorocarpa, commonly known as the green-stemmed fuirena, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is characterized by its upright, branching stems that are often green or slightly reddish in color.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, capsule-like fruits. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy surface.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and visual interest.
