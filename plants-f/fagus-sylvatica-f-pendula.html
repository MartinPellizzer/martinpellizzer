Fagus sylvatica f. pendula

Fagus sylvatica f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping beech, is a unique form of the common beech.

It is characterized by its cascading branches that hang in elegant, flowing streams, giving it a distinctive appearance. This ornamental tree is native to Europe and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are oval-shaped, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter underside, turning golden in autumn.

The plant is valued for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and parks, though it requires well-drained soil and partial shade to flourish.
