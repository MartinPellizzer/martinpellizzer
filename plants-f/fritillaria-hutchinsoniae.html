Fritillaria hutchinsoniae

Fritillaria hutchinsoniae, commonly known as the Hutchinson's fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from orange to red, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winters, and it produces showy blooms in the spring.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
