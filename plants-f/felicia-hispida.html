Felicia hispida

Felicia hispida, commonly known as the blueweed or golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and produces bright yellow flowers with white centers. The plant features small, grayish-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs, giving it a fuzzy appearance. Felicia hispida is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value comes from its vibrant blooms and ability to attract pollinators like bees and butterflies.
