Firmiana merrittii

Firmiana merrittii, commonly known as the Chinese parasol tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The tree produces showy, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in summer, followed by distinctive, umbrella-shaped fruit clusters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant has a shallow root system, making it susceptible to windthrow but adaptable to various soil types.
