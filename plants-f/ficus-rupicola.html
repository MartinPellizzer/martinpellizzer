Ficus rupicola

Ficus rupicola, commonly known as the rock fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in forest understories.

Ficus rupicola is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
