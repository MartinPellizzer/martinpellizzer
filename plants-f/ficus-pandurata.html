Ficus pandurata

Ficus pandurata, commonly known as the giant pandurata or pandurata fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, waxy leaves that are typically heart-shaped or elliptical in form. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in fig-like syconia, which are enclosed within a fleshy, greenish receptacle.

Ficus pandurata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
