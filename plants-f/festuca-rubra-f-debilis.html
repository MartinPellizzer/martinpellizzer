Festuca rubra f. debilis

Festuca rubra f. debilis, commonly known as red fescue dwarf form, is a low-growing, grass-like plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features fine, bluish-green leaves and produces small, pale blue or pinkish flowers in early summer. This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in ornamental gardening for its delicate appearance. It prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and its drought tolerance.
