Fumaria capreolata var. flabellata, commonly known as the flabellate celandine, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Papaveraceae family.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flabellate (fan-shaped) leaves that are often hairy and arranged in a basal rosette. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers with prominent stamens, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The species is known for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
