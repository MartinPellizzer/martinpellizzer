Ferula montana

Ferula montana, commonly known as the mountain fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, stiff stems and narrow, gray-green leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its aromatic seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Ferula montana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, often growing in rocky soils at high altitudes.
