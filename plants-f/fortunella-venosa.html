Fortunella venosa

Fortunella venosa, commonly known as the rough lemon, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, thornless bark and small, yellow-orange fruits that are typically used in citrus-based products. The plant has dark green, oval-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil.

Fortunella venosa is closely related to other citrus species and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
