Ficus petiolaris subsp. palmeri

Ficus petiolaris subsp.

palmeri, commonly known as the Palmer's fig, is a species native to southern Mexico and parts of Central America. It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, dark green leaves that are often glossy and slightly lobed.

The plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
