Festuca mairei

Festuca mairei, commonly known as Maire's fescue, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in high altitudes, where it thrives in cool, moist, and well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. Festuca mairei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in rock gardens or alpine landscapes.

It is also recognized for its ecological importance in stabilizing soil in rugged terrains.
