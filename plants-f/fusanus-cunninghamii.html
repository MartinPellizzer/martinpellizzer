Fusanus cunninghamii

Fusanus cunninghamii, commonly known as the Cunningham's fusanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Myrtaceae.

It is native to eastern Australia, particularly found in subtropical and temperate regions. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered white or pale pink flowers. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
