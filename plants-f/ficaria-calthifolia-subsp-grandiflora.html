Ficaria calthifolia subsp. grandiflora

Ficaria calthifolia subsp.

grandiflora, commonly known as the large-flowered ficaria, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It belongs to the family Brassicaceae and is characterized by its basal rosette of pinnately lobed leaves.

The plant produces showy, yellow flowers with four petals, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of flowering stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
