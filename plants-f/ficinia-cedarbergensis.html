Ficinia cedarbergensis

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its slender, grass-like stems are often clustered, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring.
