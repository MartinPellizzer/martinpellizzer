Festuca japonica

Festuca japonica, commonly known as Japanese grass or Japanese feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and delicate, feathery flower plumes that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Festuca japonica is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a graceful, native plant for shady or semi-shaded areas.
