Fallopia forbesii

Fallopia forbesii, commonly known as the giant knotweed, is a vigorous perennial plant native to Asia.

It features tall, hollow stems that can grow up to 3 meters in height, often with a reddish tinge. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in summer, which are followed by dark, inedible berries. Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread rapidly, making it an invasive species in many regions.

Botanically, it belongs to the Polygonaceae family, characterized by its unique leaf structure and flowering patterns.
