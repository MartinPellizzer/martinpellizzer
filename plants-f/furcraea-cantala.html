Furcraea cantala

Furcraea cantala, commonly known as the African fiber plant, is a succulent perennial shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for water storage. The plant produces tall, branched inflorescences bearing yellowish-green flowers, which are followed by small, triangular fruits.

Its leaves are used for fiber extraction, and the plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in arid environments.
