Ficus lingua subsp. lingua

Ficus lingua subsp.

lingua, commonly known as the African rubber fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to central and eastern Africa. It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits that are often consumed by both humans and animals.

It is well adapted to humid climates and is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive foliage.
