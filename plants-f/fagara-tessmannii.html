Fagara tessmannii

Fagara tessmannii, commonly known as the Trinidadian arrowroot or black root, is a shrub native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and is characterized by its dark, tuberous roots that are used in traditional medicine. The plant has simple, opposite leaves with a glossy surface and emits a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
