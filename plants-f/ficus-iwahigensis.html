Ficus iwahigensis

Ficus iwahigensis, commonly known as the Iwahig fig, is a species of fig tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered fruits. The plant typically grows in lowland tropical rainforests, where it thrives in humid and shaded environments. Its fruit is an important food source for various wildlife species, contributing to the ecosystem's biodiversity.

Ficus iwahigensis is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
