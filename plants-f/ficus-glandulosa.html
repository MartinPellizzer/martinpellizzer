Ficus glandulosa

Ficus glandulosa, commonly known as the glandular fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Africa, the Arabian Peninsula, and the Indian subcontinent. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often found in coastal areas or near water sources.

The fruit, which is a small fig, is edible and can be used in local cuisines.
