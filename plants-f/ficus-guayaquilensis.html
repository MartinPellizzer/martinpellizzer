Ficus guayaquilensis

Ficus guayaquilensis, commonly known as the Guayaquil fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the western Amazon basin.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are enclosed in a syconium, a unique structure that houses the flowers and developing fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
