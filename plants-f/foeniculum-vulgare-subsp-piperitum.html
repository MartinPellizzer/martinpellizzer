Foeniculum vulgare subsp. piperitum

Foeniculum vulgare subsp.

piperitum, commonly known as sweet fennel, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and yellow umbels of flowers.

The plant produces small, oval fruits that are often used as a spice. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight.

Its aromatic properties make it valuable in both culinary and medicinal applications.
