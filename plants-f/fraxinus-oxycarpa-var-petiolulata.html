Fraxinus oxycarpa var. petiolulata

Fraxinus oxycarpa var. petiolulata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ash, is a deciduous tree native to parts of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically less than 10 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in upright clusters, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
