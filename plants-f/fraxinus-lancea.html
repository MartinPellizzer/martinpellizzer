Fraxinus lancea

Fraxinus lancea, commonly known as the lanceleaf ash.

It is a deciduous tree native to parts of East Asia, including China and Korea. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long, with a smooth, dark green upper surface and a lighter underside. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, and it produces small, winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in herbal medicine.
