Falcata caroliniana

Falcata caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina falcate, is a species of flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in moist, sandy soils along stream banks and in wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a waxy coating, while its flowers are yellow and occur in clustered inflorescences.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
