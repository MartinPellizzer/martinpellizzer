Fraxinus obovata

Fraxinus obovata, commonly known as the Japanese ash, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia.

It features opposite, ovate to obovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in racemes, followed by samaroid fruits that disperse its seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
