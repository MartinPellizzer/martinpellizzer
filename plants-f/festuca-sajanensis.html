Festuca sajanensis

Festuca sajanensis, commonly known as Sajansky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that contributes to its common name.

Festuca sajanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
