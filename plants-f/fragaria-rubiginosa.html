Fragaria rubiginosa

Fragaria rubiginosa, commonly known as the red raspberries, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its prickly stems and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits that are typically red when ripe, though they can also be yellow or orange. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

The berries are edible and have a tart flavor, making them popular in culinary uses.
