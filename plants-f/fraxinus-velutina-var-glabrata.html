Fraxinus velutina var. glabrata

Fraxinus velutina var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth ash, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and slender, upright growth habit. The leaves are opposite, ovate to elliptic, with serrated margins and a dark green upper surface. It produces small, greenish-yellow catkins in early spring, and its fruits are samaras that disperse via wind.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil types.
