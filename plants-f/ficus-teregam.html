Ficus teregam

Ficus teregam, commonly known as the African fig tree, is a species native to tropical Africa.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are often consumed by both humans and animals. Its roots are known for their ability to absorb water efficiently, making it adaptable to various soil types.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appearance and is sometimes cultivated for its ecological benefits.
