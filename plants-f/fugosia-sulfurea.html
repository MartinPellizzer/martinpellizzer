Fugosia sulfurea

Fugosia sulfurea, commonly known as the sulfur plant, is a unique flowering plant native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a short, stout stem with broad, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, sulfur-like coating. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a hardy choice for gardeners in warmer climates.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a valuable species for botanical study and conservation efforts.
