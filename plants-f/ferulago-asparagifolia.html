Ferulago asparagifolia

Ferulago asparagifolia, commonly known as the asparagus bellflower, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It is characterized by its erect stem and clusters of bell-shaped, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits containing numerous seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats.

Botanically, it belongs to the family Apiaceae, which includes many aromatic and sometimes medicinal plants.
