Ficus buettneri

Ficus buettneri, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, greenish-white fruits. It is widely cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in various indoor conditions.

However, it can be toxic to pets if ingested, making it important to keep it out of reach of animals.
