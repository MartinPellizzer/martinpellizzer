Fimbristima maritima

Fimbristima maritima, commonly known as sea holly, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the Mediterranean.

It is characterized by its silver-gray, fuzzy foliage and striking blue or purple flower heads. The plant thrives in saline and well-drained soils, often found in dunes and rocky shores. Its flowers are typically tubular and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Fimbristima maritima is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing coastal environments.
