Festuca duriuscula subsp. curvula

Festuca duriuscula subsp.

curvula, commonly known as bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

This subspecies typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, spikelets. Festuca duriuscula subsp.

curvula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
