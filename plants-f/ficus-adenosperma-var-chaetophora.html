Ficus adenosperma var. chaetophora

Ficus adenosperma var. chaetophora, commonly known as the hairy fig, is a variant of the ficus genus native to parts of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive hairy leaves, which give it its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often found in arid or semi-arid regions. Its leaves are usually broad and have a rough texture, while its fruit is a small, green fig that matures to a yellowish color.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
