Ficus lepicarpa var. bunjeng

Ficus lepicarpa var. bunjeng, commonly known as the Bunjeng fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and smooth, pale green foliage. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested when they turn yellow or red. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
