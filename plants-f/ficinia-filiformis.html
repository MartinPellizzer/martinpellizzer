Ficinia filiformis

Ficinia filiformis, commonly known as the threadleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves that are often less than 1 millimeter in width, giving it a delicate appearance. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.

Ficinia filiformis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
