Fumaria schleicheri subsp. schleicheri

Fumaria schleicheri subsp.

schleicheri, commonly known as the Schleicher's glory-of-the-snow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is a member of the Fumariaceae family, characterized by its slender, hairy stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in cool, moist conditions. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often have a slightly hairy texture, while its flowers bloom in early spring.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
