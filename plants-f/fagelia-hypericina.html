Fagelia hypericina

Fagelia hypericina, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that turn red in the autumn. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, with prominent lenticels.

Fagelia hypericina is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
