Festuca frigida

Festuca frigida, commonly known as Arctic bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to Arctic and alpine regions.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and fine, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to silver flowers. It thrives in cold, dry environments and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.

Festuca frigida plays a vital role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.
