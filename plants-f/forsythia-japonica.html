Forsythia japonica

Forsythia japonica, commonly known as the Japanese forsythia, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, often before the leaves emerge. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its bark is smooth and reddish-brown, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Forsythia japonica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
