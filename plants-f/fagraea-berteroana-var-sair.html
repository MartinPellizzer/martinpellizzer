Fagraea berteroana var. sair

Fagraea berteroana var. sair, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, adding a vibrant splash of color to the landscape. The plant has compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets and produces small, round fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The botanical features of this species make it a popular choice for gardens and urban landscapes.
