Foeniculum segetum

Foeniculum segetum, commonly known as wild cumin, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and dill, and is characterized by its finely divided leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, dry fruits that contain aromatic seeds used in traditional medicine and cuisine. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as fields and roadsides.

Its chemical composition includes essential oils with compounds like limonene and anethole, contributing to its distinctive flavor and medicinal properties.
