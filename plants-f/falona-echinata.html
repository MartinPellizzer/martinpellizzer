Falona echinata

Falona echinata, commonly known as the spiny falona, is a perennial herb native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, spiny fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands or disturbed areas.

Botanically, it is notable for its distinctive floral structure and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
