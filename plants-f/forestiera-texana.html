Forestiera texana

Forestiera texana, commonly known as Texas forestiera, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has narrow, leathery leaves that are often tinged with red or purple, adding visual interest. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Forestiera texana is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to arid conditions.
