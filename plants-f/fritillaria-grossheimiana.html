Fritillaria grossheimiana

Fritillaria grossheimiana, commonly known as the Sierra Valley fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the western United States.

It features striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in harsh, arid environments, and it blooms in early spring.

Fritillaria grossheimiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
