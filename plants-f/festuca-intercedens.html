Festuca intercedens

Festuca intercedens, commonly known as bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and a bluish-green coloration, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Festuca intercedens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly used in landscaping.

It also plays a role in ecological systems by stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
