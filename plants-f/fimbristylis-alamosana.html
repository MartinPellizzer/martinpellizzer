Fimbristylis alamosana

Fimbristylis alamosana, commonly known as the Alamos sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes, swales, and riparian zones. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often pale green or brown. It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various small organisms.

Fimbristylis alamosana is adapted to tolerate both wet and dry conditions, making it a resilient species in its native range.
