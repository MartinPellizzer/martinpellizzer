Ficus superstitiosa

Ficus superstitiosa, commonly known as the sacred fig or the holy fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Africa and the Middle East.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible figs that are typically harvested when they are fully ripe. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has cultural significance in various regions.

The tree is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
