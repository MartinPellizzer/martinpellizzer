Farobaea tropica

Farobaea tropica, commonly known as the tropical fern, is a species native to warm, humid climates.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that can grow up to several feet in length. The plant thrives in partial shade and requires well-drained, moist soil for optimal growth. Its reproductive structures include sporangia, which release spores for propagation.

Farobaea tropica is often used in ornamental gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
