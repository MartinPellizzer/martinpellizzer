Fuchsia rosea var. spinosa

Fuchsia rosea var. spinosa, commonly known as the spiny fuchsia, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and produces showy, tubular flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant features stiff, spiny stems and leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. It thrives in cool, humid conditions and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a desirable addition to shaded gardens and greenhouse environments.
