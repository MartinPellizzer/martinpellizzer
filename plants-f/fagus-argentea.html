Fagus argentea

Fagus argentea, commonly known as the silver fir, is a coniferous tree native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and elongated, flat needles that are coated with a silvery bloom. The tree produces both male and female cones, with the female cones developing into woody, egg-shaped seed capsules. Its leaves are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the branches, and it is often found in cool, moist habitats.

The silver fir plays an important role in forest ecosystems, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
