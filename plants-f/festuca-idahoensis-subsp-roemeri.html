Festuca idahoensis subsp. roemeri

Festuca idahoensis subsp.

roemeri, commonly known as Idaho fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions and open woodlands. Festuca idahoensis subsp.

roemeri is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in ecological restoration projects.
