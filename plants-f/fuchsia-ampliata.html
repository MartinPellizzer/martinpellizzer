Fuchsia ampliata

Fuchsia ampliata, commonly known as the broadleaf fuchsia, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from violet to deep red. The plant features broad, ovate leaves with a glossy texture and a slightly serrated edge. Fuchsia ampliata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
