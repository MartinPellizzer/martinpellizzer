Ficus marginalis

Ficus marginalis, commonly known as the marginal fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with distinctive marginal veins. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus marginalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in indoor environments.
