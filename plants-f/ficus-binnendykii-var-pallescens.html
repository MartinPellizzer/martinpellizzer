Ficus binnendykii var. pallescens

Ficus binnendykii var. pallescens, commonly known as the white-veined fig, is a rare and unique variant of the ficus genus.

It is characterized by its striking pale green leaves with prominent white veins, which give it a distinctive appearance. Native to southern Africa, this plant thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light. Its foliage is often used in horticultural displays due to its ornamental value.

The plant is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
