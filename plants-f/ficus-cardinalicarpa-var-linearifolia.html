Ficus cardinalicarpa var. linearifolia

Ficus cardinalicarpa var. linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
