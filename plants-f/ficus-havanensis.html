Ficus havanensis

Ficus havanensis, commonly known as the Havana fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly Cuba, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

Ficus havanensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
