Ficus sandanakana

Ficus sandanakana, commonly known as the Malabar fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Western Ghats of India and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a fig-like structure that is edible and often used in traditional medicine.

Ficus sandanakana is typically found in moist, shaded habitats and is valued for its ornamental and medicinal properties.
