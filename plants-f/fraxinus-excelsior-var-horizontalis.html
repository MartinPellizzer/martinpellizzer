Fraxinus excelsior var. horizontalis

Fraxinus excelsior var. horizontalis, commonly known as the horizontal ash, is a unique variant of the common ash tree.

It is characterized by its horizontally spreading branches, which give it a distinctive, open appearance. The leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers in panicles, followed by winged samaras as its fruit.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
