Fagelia hyssopifolia

Fagelia hyssopifolia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small blue or purple clouds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late summer and early fall. Fagelia hyssopifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and wetlands.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators such as butterflies and bees.
