Festuca scoparia subsp. lutea

Festuca scoparia subsp.

lutea, commonly known as yellow tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, yellowish-green leaves.

The plant produces compact, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, pale blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Festuca scoparia subsp.

lutea is often found in high-altitude meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh habitats.
