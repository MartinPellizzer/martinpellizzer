Ficus stuhlmannii

Ficus stuhlmannii, commonly known as the Stuhlmann's fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig fruits, which are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus stuhlmannii is relatively low-maintenance and can adapt well to indoor growing conditions.
