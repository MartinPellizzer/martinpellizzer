Ficus bakeriana

Ficus bakeriana, commonly known as the Baker's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate in form. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Ficus bakeriana is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
