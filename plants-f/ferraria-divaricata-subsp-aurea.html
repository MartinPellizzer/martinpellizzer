Ferraria divaricata subsp. aurea

Ferraria divaricata subsp.

aurea, commonly known as the golden ferraria, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is a perennial herb that typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils.

The plant features striking yellow flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This subspecies is valued in gardens for its vibrant color and adaptability to dry conditions.
