Fritillaria phaeanthera

Fritillaria phaeanthera, commonly known as the Persian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a distinctive, elongated bulb that stores nutrients for the growing season. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is cultivated in gardens and traditional herbal practices.
