Fuchsia pendula

Fuchsia pendula, commonly known as the weeping fuchsia, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its cascading clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant features slender, arching stems that give it a distinctive, weeping appearance. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of white pubescence.

Fuchsia pendula thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and hanging baskets.
