Ficus vulpina

Ficus vulpina, commonly known as the Indian rubber plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by fig-like fruits, which are important for wildlife.

Ficus vulpina is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
