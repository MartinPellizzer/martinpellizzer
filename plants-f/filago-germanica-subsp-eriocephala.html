Filago germanica subsp. eriocephala

Filago germanica subsp.

eriocephala, commonly known as the hairy filago, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its erect, branched stems and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in summer.

The plant produces fluffy, white seed heads that are adapted for wind dispersal. It thrives in dry, sandy soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological studies and botanical gardens.
