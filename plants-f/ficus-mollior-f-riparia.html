Ficus mollior f. riparia

Ficus mollior f. riparia, commonly known as the riparian ficus, is a variant of the common fig tree native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves that are typically larger and more elongated compared to the parent species. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it well-suited for riparian zones. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Botanically, it belongs to the Moraceae family and is valued for its adaptability to humid environments.
