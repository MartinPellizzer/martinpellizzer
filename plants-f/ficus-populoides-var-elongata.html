Ficus populoides var. elongata

Ficus populoides var. elongata, commonly known as the elongated fig tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, slender leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It is also popular as a decorative houseplant due to its elegant, trailing growth habit.
