Ficus setiflora var. puberula

Ficus setiflora var. puberula, commonly known as the velvet fig or pubescent fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features dense, glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety pubescence, giving it a soft, textured appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by small, edible fruits that are typically red or yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage.

Its adaptability makes it suitable for both indoor and outdoor cultivation in suitable climates.
