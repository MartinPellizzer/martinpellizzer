Fuchsia magellanica

Fuchsia magellanica, commonly known as the Magellan fuchsia, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from deep red to purple, often with contrasting yellow or white markings. The plant produces showy, pendulous inflorescences that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

Fuchsia magellanica is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and can thrive in USDA hardiness zones 6 to 9.
