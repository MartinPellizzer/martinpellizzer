Ferula bucharica

Ferula bucharica, commonly known as the Bukhara ferula, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and aromatic foliage. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, typical of the family, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine. Ferula bucharica is known for its strong, pungent odor and is sometimes used in local remedies for digestive issues.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
