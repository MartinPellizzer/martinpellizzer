Fumaria gussonei var. benedicta

Fumaria gussonei var. benedicta, commonly known as the Benedict's glory-of-the-snow, is a variant of the Fumaria genus native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a herbaceous annual plant characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers. The leaves are typically alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often found in rocky or disturbed habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
