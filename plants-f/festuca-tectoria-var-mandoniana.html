Festuca tectoria var. mandoniana

Festuca tectoria var. mandoniana, commonly known as the Mandon's bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with narrow, grass-like leaves that form dense mats. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.
