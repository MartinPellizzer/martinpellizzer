Ferula sugatensis

Ferula sugatensis, commonly known as the Afghan cow parsley, is a flowering plant native to Afghanistan and surrounding regions.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces a taproot that is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties. Its foliage is finely divided, resembling that of other members of the carrot family, and it thrives in arid and semi-arid environments.

Ferula sugatensis is also valued for its aromatic compounds, which have been studied for potential pharmacological applications.
