Froelichia uniflora

Froelichia uniflora, commonly known as the single-flower froelichia, is a flowering plant native to parts of South America.

It is a small shrub that typically grows in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are narrow and often have a slight hairy texture.

Froelichia uniflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
