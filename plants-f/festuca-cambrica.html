Festuca cambrica

Festuca cambrica, commonly known as the Welsh mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to the British Isles.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades that are often covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is typically found in upland areas and heathlands. Festuca cambrica is known for its resilience to harsh weather conditions, making it a common component of open, rocky habitats.

Its floral structure consists of spikelets that bear small, two-lobed florets, which are usually green or pale yellow in color.
