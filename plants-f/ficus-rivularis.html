Ficus rivularis

Ficus rivularis, commonly known as the river fig, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a climbing or trailing plant that often grows along riverbanks and moist forest floors. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are followed by fig-like fruits.

Ficus rivularis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid environments.
