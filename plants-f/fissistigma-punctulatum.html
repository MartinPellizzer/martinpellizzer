Fissistigma punctulatum

Fissistigma punctulatum, commonly known as the spotted holly, is a member of the Icariaceae family.

It is a shrub characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often dotted with white spots. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. It is native to parts of Asia, including China and Japan, and thrives in moist, shaded environments.

The berries it bears are red and edible, though they are not commonly consumed by humans.
