Ficaria aperta

Ficaria aperta, commonly known as the broad-leaved picramnia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia and Africa.

It belongs to the family Picramniaceae and is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its botanical features include a short, creeping stem and distinctive, palmately lobed leaves.

Ficaria aperta is often noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
