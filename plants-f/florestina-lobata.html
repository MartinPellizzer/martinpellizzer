Florestina lobata

Florestina lobata, commonly known as the lobed forest vine, is a climbing plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically lobed, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by woody pods containing several seeds. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
