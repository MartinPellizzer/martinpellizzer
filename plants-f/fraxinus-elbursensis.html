Fraxinus elbursensis

Fraxinus elbursensis, commonly known as the Elburz ash, is a deciduous tree native to the mountainous regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and smooth, gray bark. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by the wind. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

Fraxinus elbursensis is adapted to dry and semi-arid climates, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
