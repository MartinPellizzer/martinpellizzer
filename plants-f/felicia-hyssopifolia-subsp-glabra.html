Felicia hyssopifolia subsp. glabra

Felicia hyssopifolia subsp.

glabra, commonly known as smooth felicia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It is characterized by its upright, branching stems and small, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters.

The leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in fine, soft hairs, though the subspecies glabra is distinguished by its smoother, hairless foliage. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

This plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
