Festuca brevifolia var. utahensis

Festuca brevifolia var. utahensis, commonly known as Utah bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Utah bluegrass is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
