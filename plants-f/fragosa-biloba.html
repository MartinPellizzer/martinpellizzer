Fragosa biloba

Fragosa biloba, commonly known as the twin-lobed fragosa, is a shrub native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology, featuring two lobes that give it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink in color. Its fruit is a small, fleshy berry that contains several seeds.

Due to its unique appearance and ecological role, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
