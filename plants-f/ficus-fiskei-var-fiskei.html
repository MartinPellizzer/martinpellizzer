Ficus fiskei var. fiskei

Ficus fiskei var. fiskei, commonly known as the Fike's fig, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, round fig fruits that are usually green when mature, though they may change color slightly. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its limited distribution and specific care requirements, it is a sought-after plant among collectors and botanists.
