Fragaria gracilis

Fragaria gracilis, commonly known as the slender strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically red and have a sweet, delicate flavor. The plant has trifoliate leaves with heart-shaped leaflets and runners that spread along the ground. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodland areas.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
