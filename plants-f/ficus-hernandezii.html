Ficus hernandezii

Ficus hernandezii, commonly known as the Mexican fig tree, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant has simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green in color. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus hernandezii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
