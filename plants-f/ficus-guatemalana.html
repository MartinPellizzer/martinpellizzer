Ficus guatemalana

Ficus guatemalana, commonly known as the Guatemalan fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Central America, particularly Guatemala, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Ficus guatemalana prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
