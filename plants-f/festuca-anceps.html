Festuca anceps

Festuca anceps, commonly known as bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green blades and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open woodlands, prairies, and along roadsides. Festuca anceps is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in lawns and erosion control.

It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear blue to purple flowers in the spring.
