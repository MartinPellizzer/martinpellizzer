Fagaropsis glabra

Fagaropsis glabra, commonly known as the smooth fagaropsis, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, wooded areas and thrives in partial shade. Its smooth, glabrous stems and foliage contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Fagaropsis glabra is often found in wetlands and along stream banks, where it plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local biodiversity.
