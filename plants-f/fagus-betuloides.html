Fagus betuloides

Fagus betuloides, commonly known as the American beech, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, spiky catkins and edible, sweet nuts enclosed in a spiny husk. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.

The American beech plays an important role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
