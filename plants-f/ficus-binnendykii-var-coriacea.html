Ficus binnendykii var. coriacea

Ficus binnendykii var. coriacea, commonly known as the African rubber fig, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green, waxy leaves that are typically broad and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by green, spherical fruits that mature to a reddish color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variant is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ornamental appeal.
