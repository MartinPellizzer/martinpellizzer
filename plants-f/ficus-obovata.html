Ficus obovata

Ficus obovata, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, obovate leaves that are glossy and dark green on top with a lighter underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green or yellow when mature. It thrives in humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Ficus obovata is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
