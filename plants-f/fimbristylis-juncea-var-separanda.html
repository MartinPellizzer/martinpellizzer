Fimbristylis juncea var. separanda

Fimbristylis juncea var. separanda, commonly known as the narrowleaf flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like stems with narrow, linear leaves that are often hairy along the edges. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, greenish flowers.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
