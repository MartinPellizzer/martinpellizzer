Faramea persistisepta

Faramea persistisepta, commonly known as the evergreen hibiscus, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and parts of South America.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red. The plant is typically evergreen, with dense foliage that provides ornamental value in both garden and landscape settings. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Faramea persistisepta is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and is used in ornamental horticulture.
