Fragaria vesca var. mexicana

Fragaria vesca var. mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican strawberry, is a wild strawberry species native to Mexico.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant that produces small, edible berries with a sweet and tangy flavor. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces white flowers that bloom in early spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in rocky or open woodland habitats.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for both ecological studies and culinary use.
