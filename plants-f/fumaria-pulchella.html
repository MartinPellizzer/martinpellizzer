Fumaria pulchella

Fumaria pulchella, commonly known as the pretty gloryflower, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Fumaria pulchella is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
