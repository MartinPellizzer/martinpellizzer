Flemingia stricta subsp. pteropus

Flemingia stricta subsp.

pteropus, commonly known as the coastal flemingia, is a shrub native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. It typically grows in coastal areas, sand dunes, and saline soils, showing remarkable adaptability to harsh environments.

The plant has small, elliptical leaves that are often pubescent, and it produces small, yellowish flowers clustered in inflorescences. Its root system is extensive, helping to stabilize sandy soils and prevent erosion.

This species is valued for its ecological role in coastal ecosystems and its potential for use in restoration projects.
