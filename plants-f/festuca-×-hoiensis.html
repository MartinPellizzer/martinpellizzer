Festuca × hoiensis

Festuca × hoiensis, commonly known as the hybrid fescue, is a perennial grass species that exhibits a mix of traits from its parent species, Festuca rubra and Festuca ovina.

It typically grows in temperate climates and is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and dense growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous slender, two-rowed spikelets. Festuca × hoiensis is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Its adaptability makes it suitable for both ornamental gardens and low-maintenance landscaping.
