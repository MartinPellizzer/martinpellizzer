Frangula × anceps

Frangula × anceps, commonly known as the Canadian buckthorn, is a hybrid shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its dense, thorny growth habit. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, followed by dark blue berries that are a food source for birds. Its leaves are elliptical with a smooth margin and a slightly glossy texture.

The bark is thick, ridged, and often peels in thin strips, giving it a distinctive appearance.
