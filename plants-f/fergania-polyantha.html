Fergania polyantha

Fergania polyantha, commonly known as the Kashmir rose, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of fragrant, pink or white flowers. The plant is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its beauty. It thrives in cool, moist climates and requires well-drained soil.

Fergania polyantha is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
