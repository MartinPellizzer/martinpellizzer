Fumana thymifolia f. glutinosa

Fumana thymifolia f. glutinosa, commonly known as sticky thyme-leaved fumana, is a botanical variant of the species Fumana thymifolia.

It is characterized by its aromatic, small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a sticky resin. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils in arid or semi-arid regions. Its flowers are usually small and clustered, often in shades of pink or purple.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique appearance and fragrance.
