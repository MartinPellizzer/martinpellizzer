Fagus multinervis

Fagus multinervis, commonly known as the Chinese beech, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks, chestnuts, and hickories. The tree is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with prominent veins, and it produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny cupule. It typically grows in temperate climates and is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological importance.

The species plays a significant role in forest ecosystems and is often found in mixed hardwood forests.
