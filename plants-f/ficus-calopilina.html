Ficus calopilina

Ficus calopilina, commonly known as the white-veined fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, edible fruits that are typically consumed by various bird species.

Ficus calopilina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to different growing conditions.
