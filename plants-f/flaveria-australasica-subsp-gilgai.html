Flaveria australasica subsp. gilgai

Flaveria australasica subsp.

gilgai, commonly known as the Gilbert's flaveria, is a flowering plant native to Australia. It is a herbaceous annual or perennial species characterized by its erect growth habit and hairy stems.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically visited by various pollinators. It thrives in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas, showing some degree of drought tolerance.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in ecological and taxonomic studies.
