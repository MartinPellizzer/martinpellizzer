Ferula karakumica

Ferula karakumica, commonly known as the Karakum ferula, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and distinctive, branched, aromatic leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in a spike-like inflorescence, which bloom in the summer months. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their medicinal properties.

Ferula karakumica thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh climatic conditions.
