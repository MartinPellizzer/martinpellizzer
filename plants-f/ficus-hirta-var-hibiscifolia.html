Ficus hirta var. hibiscifolia

Ficus hirta var. hibiscifolia, commonly known as the Hibiscifoliate Fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are often reddish or purple in color when young. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus hirta var. hibiscifolia is popular as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
