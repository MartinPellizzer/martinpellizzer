Festuca quadridens

Festuca quadridens, commonly known as the four-toothed fescue, is a species of grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Festuca quadridens is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
