Ficus pseudoradula

Ficus pseudoradula, commonly known as the pale fig or dwarf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, smooth, oval-shaped leaves that are often a deep green in color. It produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when ripe.

Ficus pseudoradula is often grown as a houseplant due to its adaptability and low maintenance requirements.
