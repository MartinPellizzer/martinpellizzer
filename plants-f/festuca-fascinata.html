Festuca fascinata

Festuca fascinata, commonly known as the slender fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Festuca fascinata is often found in disturbed soils and can form dense mats in suitable conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of ecological systems and a valuable plant for conservation and restoration efforts.
