Fenixia

Fenixia, commonly known as the sacred lotus, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that bloom on long, buoyant stalks that rise from water. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that float on the water's surface, providing shade and oxygen to aquatic ecosystems. Its roots are submerged, absorbing nutrients from the water while supporting the plant's growth.

Fenixia is also noted for its symbolic significance in various cultures, representing purity and spiritual awakening.
