Freziera lanata

Freziera lanata, commonly known as the woolly freziera, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, woolly leaves and stems, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Freziera lanata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
