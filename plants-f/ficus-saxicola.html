Ficus saxicola

Ficus saxicola, commonly known as the rock fig, is a small, evergreen shrub native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. Ficus saxicola thrives in well-drained soil and prefers a warm, humid climate.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact size and attractive foliage.
