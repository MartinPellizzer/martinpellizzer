Ficus sulcata

Ficus sulcata, commonly known as the crocodile plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that resemble the skin of a crocodile. The plant has a thick, trunk-like stem that can grow quite tall, often reaching several meters in height. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits.

Ficus sulcata is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
