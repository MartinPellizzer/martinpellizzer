Ficus ochroleuca

Ficus ochroleuca, commonly known as the white fig or white-veined fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia, the Philippines, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent white veins. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Ficus ochroleuca is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
