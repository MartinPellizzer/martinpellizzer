Fargesia strigosa

Fargesia strigosa, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It belongs to the grass family (Poaceae) and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and finely textured, silvery-gray leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, compact clumps. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers in early spring, followed by narrow, grass-like seed heads.

Fargesia strigosa is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic value and adaptability to various garden conditions.
