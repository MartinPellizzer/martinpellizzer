Fumaria dubia

Fumaria dubia, commonly known as the common fumitory, is a herbaceous annual plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, branching stems that often lie prostrate or ascend, with small, yellowish-orange flowers clustered in racemes. The plant has alternate, ovate leaves with hairy surfaces, and its roots are known to contain alkaloids. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

The genus Fumaria is characterized by its ability to exude a faint, sometimes unpleasant odor when crushed.
