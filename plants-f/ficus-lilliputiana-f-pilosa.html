Ficus lilliputiana f. pilosa

Ficus lilliputiana f. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy fig, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, rounded leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by small, edible figs. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and bonsai enthusiasts.
