Festuca kozhevnikovii

Festuca kozhevnikovii, commonly known as Kozhevnikov's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid environments.

Festuca kozhevnikovii is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soils.
