Festuca camusiana f. intermedia

Festuca camusiana f. intermedia, commonly known as intermediate meadow fescue, is a cool-season grass that exhibits intermediate characteristics between typical meadow fescue and other fescue species.

It is a perennial plant with tufted growth habit and forms dense, sod-like turf. The leaves are narrow, flat, and generally bluish-green in color, with a hairy surface. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands and meadows.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and use in ornamental and turfgrass applications.
