Ficus castellviana

Ficus castellviana, commonly known as the Castellvian fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Iberian Peninsula and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered fruit that is typically green when mature, though it may change color depending on the variety. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Ficus castellviana is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
