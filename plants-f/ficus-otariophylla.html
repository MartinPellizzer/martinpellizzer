Ficus otariophylla

Ficus otariophylla, commonly known as the Otariophylla fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits.

Ficus otariophylla is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various indoor environments.
