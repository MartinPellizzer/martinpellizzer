Ficus soroceoides var. onivensis

Ficus soroceoides var. onivensis, commonly known as the Onivensis fig, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in lowland rainforests. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a waxy texture. Its fruit, a small fig, is edible and contains a sweet, pulpy interior.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
