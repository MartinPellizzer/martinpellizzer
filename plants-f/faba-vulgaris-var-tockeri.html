Faba vulgaris var. tockeri

Faba vulgaris var. tockeri, commonly known as the Mediterranean fava bean, is a variant of the broad bean species.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive dark green color. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into pod-like structures containing edible seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its high nutritional value.

This variety is valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in crop rotation.
