Ficus ekmanii

Ficus ekmanii, commonly known as the Ekman's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate in form. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible figs, though the fruit is not commonly cultivated for human consumption.

Ficus ekmanii is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various indoor environments.
