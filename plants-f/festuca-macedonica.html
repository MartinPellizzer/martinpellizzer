Festuca macedonica

Festuca macedonica, commonly known as Macedonian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Balkan region.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along forest edges.

Festuca macedonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
