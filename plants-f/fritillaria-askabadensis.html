Fritillaria askabadensis

Fritillaria askabadensis, commonly known as the Askabad fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from orange to red, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winter conditions, and it blooms in the spring.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
