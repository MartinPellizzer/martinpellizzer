Frangula polymorpha

Frangula polymorpha, commonly known as the common buckthorn, is a deciduous shrub native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its dark green, alternate leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring. The plant is notable for its ribbed, reddish stems and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its fruit is a capsule that contains small, dark seeds, which are dispersed by birds.

Frangula polymorpha is often found in hedgerows and woodland edges, where it plays a role in local ecosystems.
