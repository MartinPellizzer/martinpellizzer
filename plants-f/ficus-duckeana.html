Ficus duckeana

Ficus duckeana, commonly known as the Ducke's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in ornamental horticulture. It produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when mature.

Ficus duckeana is known for its adaptability to various light conditions and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
