Festuca halleri subsp. decipiens

Festuca halleri subsp.

decipiens, commonly known as the deceptive fescue, is a perennial grass species native to mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
