Ficus cumingii var. terminalifolia

Ficus cumingii var. terminalifolia, commonly known as the Chinese banyan, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by round, greenish-white fruits. It has a distinctive appearance with its aerial roots and sprawling canopy.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid environments.
