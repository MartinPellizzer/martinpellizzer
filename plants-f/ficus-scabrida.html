Ficus scabrida

Ficus scabrida, commonly known as the rough fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are rough to the touch, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green when mature. Ficus scabrida thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
