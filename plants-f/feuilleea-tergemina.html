Feuilleea tergemina

Feuilleea tergemina, commonly known as the silverleaf fern, is a striking plant native to the humid tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, feathery fronds that can grow up to two meters in length, with a distinctive silvery-blue hue on the underside of its leaves. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its reproductive system involves the production of spores rather than seeds, a common trait among ferns.

Feuilleea tergemina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some cultures.
