Felicia clavipilosa subsp. clavipilosa

Felicia clavipilosa subsp.

clavipilosa, commonly known as the Cape gooseberry or Physalis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers and distinctive bract-covered fruit.

The plant typically grows in coastal and mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its fruit, which resembles a small lantern, is edible and often used in culinary applications.
