Ficus stictocarpa

Ficus stictocarpa, commonly known as the white-veined fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that have prominent white veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus stictocarpa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.
