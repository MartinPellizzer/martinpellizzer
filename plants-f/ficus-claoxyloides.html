Ficus claoxyloides

Ficus claoxyloides, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant features large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green on top, with a lighter underside. It produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic appeal.
