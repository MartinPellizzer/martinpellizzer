Fumaria rupestris subsp. bifrons

Fumaria rupestris subsp.

bifrons, commonly known as the two-lobed fumitory, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fumariaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, ascending stems and small, clustered yellow flowers.

The subspecies is native to southern Europe and is often found in rocky or calcareous soils. Its leaves are typically trifoliate, with three leaflets that are lanceolate in shape.

This plant is known for its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
