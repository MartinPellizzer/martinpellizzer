Festuca ovina var. lamarckiana

Festuca ovina var. lamarckiana, commonly known as Lamarck's sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
