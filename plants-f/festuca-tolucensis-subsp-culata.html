Festuca tolucensis subsp. culata

Festuca tolucensis subsp.

culata, commonly known as the Culated Festuca, is a grass species native to specific regions of South America. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect flowering stems.

The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This subspecies is often found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands.
