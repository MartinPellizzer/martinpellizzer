Fraga vaillantii

Fraga vaillantii, commonly known as the Vaillant's rock rose, is a shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage, with leaves that are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white or pink flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or coastal landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
