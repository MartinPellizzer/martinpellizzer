Frankenia laevis var. intermedia

Frankenia laevis var. intermedia, commonly known as the coastal bladderwort, is a halophytic shrub native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to salt spray and high salinity environments. The plant features small, fleshy, bladder-like structures on its stems, which are specialized for water storage. Its leaves are typically narrow and covered in a waxy cuticle to reduce water loss.

This species plays a role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and is often found in maritime habitats.
