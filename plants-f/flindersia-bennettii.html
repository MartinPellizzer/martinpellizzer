Flindersia bennettii

Flindersia bennettii, commonly known as the Bennett's flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to northern Australia.

It is characterized by its striking orange to red floral display, which appears during the dry season. The plant has smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are borne in panicle clusters and are pollinated primarily by birds and insects.

The tree is also valued for its timber, which is durable and used in construction and furniture making.
