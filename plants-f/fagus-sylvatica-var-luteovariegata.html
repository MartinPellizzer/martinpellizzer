Fagus sylvatica var. luteovariegata

Fagus sylvatica var. luteovariegata, commonly known as the golden-leafed European beech, is a variant of the European beech tree characterized by its distinctive yellow-variegated leaves.

This cultivar exhibits a unique leaf pattern with a mix of green and yellow, often featuring a lighter margin. It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in temperate climates, reaching heights of up to 20 meters. The leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a serrated edge.

This ornamental tree is prized for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.
