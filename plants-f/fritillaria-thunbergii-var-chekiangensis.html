Fritillaria thunbergii var. chekiangensis

Fritillaria thunbergii var. chekiangensis, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to eastern China.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish-orange with a contrasting black or purple throat. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soil in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.

The species is also noted for its medicinal uses in traditional Chinese medicine.
