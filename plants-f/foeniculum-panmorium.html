Foeniculum panmorium

Foeniculum panmorium, commonly known as wild fennel, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its aromatic, feathery leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in umbrella-like clusters. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are often used in culinary and medicinal applications. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and sunny environments.

Its essential oils contain compounds like anethole, which contribute to its distinctive licorice-like fragrance.
