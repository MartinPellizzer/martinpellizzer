Ficus jacquinifolia

Ficus jacquinifolia, commonly known as the jacquin's fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically hidden within the foliage. Its fruit, a small fig-like structure, is edible and contains seeds.

Ficus jacquinifolia thrives in humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
