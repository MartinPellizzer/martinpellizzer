Fraxinus oxycarpa var. oxyphylla

Fraxinus oxycarpa var. oxyphylla, commonly known as the red osier dogwood, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive red twigs that stand out against the winter landscape. The plant has compound leaves with oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, greenish-white flowers in umbel-like clusters. Its fruits are small, red drupes that attract birds and other wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological benefits in native landscapes.
