Flickingeria lonchigera

Flickingeria lonchigera, commonly known as the golden star grass, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces delicate, yellowish-green flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which bloom in the summer months. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its visual appeal.
