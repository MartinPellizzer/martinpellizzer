Forsteronia cordata

Forsteronia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf orchid, is a species of flowering plant in the Orchidaceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is characterized by its distinctive heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often clustered in racemes. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
