Frenela rhomboidea var. pendula

Frenela rhomboidea var. pendula, commonly known as the hanging fenugreek, is a variant of the fenugreek plant characterized by its pendulous growth habit.

It is a herbaceous annual plant native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a distinctive aroma when crushed.

This variety is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.
