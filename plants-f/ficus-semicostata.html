Ficus semicostata

Ficus semicostata, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, China, and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed and have a distinctive appearance. It produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Ficus semicostata is commonly cultivated as a houseplant or for ornamental purposes due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
