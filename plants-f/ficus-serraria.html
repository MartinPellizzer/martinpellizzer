Ficus serraria

Ficus serraria, commonly known as the serrated fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a serrated edge, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are typically covered in a dense, hairy coating.

Ficus serraria is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
