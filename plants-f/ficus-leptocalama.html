Ficus leptocalama

Ficus leptocalama, commonly known as the slender-leaf fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Ficus leptocalama is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
