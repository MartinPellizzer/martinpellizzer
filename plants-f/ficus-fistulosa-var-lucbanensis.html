Ficus fistulosa var. lucbanensis

Ficus fistulosa var. lucbanensis, commonly known as the Lucban fig, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, grayish-brown bark. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that turn from green to yellow when mature. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and have a waxy texture with a pointed tip.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical gardens.
