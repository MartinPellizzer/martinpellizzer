Ficus regina

Ficus regina, commonly known as the Regina fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Ficus regina is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for both ornamental and horticultural purposes.
