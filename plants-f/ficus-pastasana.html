Ficus pastasana

Ficus pastasana, commonly known as the pastasana fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tropical evergreen tree that typically grows to a height of 10-15 meters. The plant features broad, heart-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green in color. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically pear-shaped.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
