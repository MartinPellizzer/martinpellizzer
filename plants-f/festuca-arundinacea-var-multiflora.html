Festuca arundinacea var. multiflora

Festuca arundinacea var. multiflora, commonly known as sweetgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and typically have a slight bluish-green hue.

Sweetgrass is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional crafts and rituals.
