Fimbristylis leptostachya

Fimbristylis leptostachya, commonly known as the slender-stalked sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish or brownish in color. It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like inflorescence, which gives it its specific epithet "leptostachya," meaning "slender stamen.".

This species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions and plays a role in stabilizing soil in wetland ecosystems.
