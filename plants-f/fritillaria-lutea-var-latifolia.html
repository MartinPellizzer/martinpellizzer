Fritillaria lutea var. latifolia

Fritillaria lutea var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or yellow in color, with a distinctive, waxy texture. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained soils in full sun to partial shade. It is often found in rocky or woodland habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.

The species is also noted for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal medicine.
