Ficus infectoria var. aegeirophylla

Ficus infectoria var. aegeirophylla, commonly known as the sacred fig or banyan tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and can grow into a sprawling tree with aerial roots. The plant is part of the Moraceae family and is known for its ability to form extensive root systems that can create a dense network of roots on the ground. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and cultural significance.

The tree is also notable for its edible figs, which are typically harvested when fully ripe.
