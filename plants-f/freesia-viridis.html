Freesia viridis

Freesia viridis, commonly known as the green freesia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically blooms in late winter to early spring, producing flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. Freesia viridis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a cut flower due to its pleasant aroma and vibrant appearance.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
