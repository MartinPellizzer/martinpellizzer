Ficus fuscipes

Ficus fuscipes, commonly known as the black-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are often marked with a distinctive reddish or purplish tinge. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Ficus fuscipes is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
