Fruticicola albopunctata

Fruticicola albopunctata, commonly known as the white-spotted shrub orchid, is a flowering plant belonging to the Orchidaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact shrub-like growth habit and produces small, fragrant flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in shaded, humid environments. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, while the flowers display a mix of white and green hues with distinctive spots.

Fruticicola albopunctata is known for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
