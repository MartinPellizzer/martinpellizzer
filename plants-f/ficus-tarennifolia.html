Ficus tarennifolia

Ficus tarennifolia, commonly known as the Madagascar fig, is a tropical plant native to the island of Madagascar.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature, and it thrives in humid, warm climates.

Ficus tarennifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
