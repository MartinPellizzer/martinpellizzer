Ficus camptoneuroides

Ficus camptoneuroides, commonly known as the African rubber fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fig-like fruits that are edible for certain animals but not generally consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus camptoneuroides is also valued for its ability to adapt to various soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
