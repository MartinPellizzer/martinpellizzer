Fosterella elviragrossiae

Fosterella elviragrossiae, commonly known as the Elviragrossia fosterella, is a rare and unique bromeliad native to the high-altitude cloud forests of Ecuador.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped foliage that forms a central cup to collect water, a common adaptation in epiphytic plants. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically pink or red, adding a vibrant touch to its natural habitat. It thrives in humid, shaded environments with high air moisture, making it a challenging species to cultivate outside its native range.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a species of conservation concern.
