Filipendula rufinervis

Filipendula rufinervis, commonly known as the red-veined meadow beauty, is a herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves with prominent red veins. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Filipendula rufinervis thrives in moist, shady environments such as wet meadows and stream banks.

It is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ecological value to pollinators.
