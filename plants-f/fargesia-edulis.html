Fargesia edulis

Fargesia edulis, commonly known as the edible bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its slender, upright stems and dense foliage. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. Its leaves are long and narrow, providing a fine texture to the plant's appearance.

Fargesia edulis is valued not only for its ornamental qualities but also for its edible shoots, which are often consumed in traditional Chinese cuisine.
