Fraga micrantha

Fraga micrantha, commonly known as the dwarf rock rose, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its small, fragrant white or pink flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. It has small, narrow leaves and produces fruit that is a type of follicle.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in rock gardens and as a decorative plant in landscaping.
