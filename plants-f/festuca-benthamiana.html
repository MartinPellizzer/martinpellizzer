Festuca benthamiana

Festuca benthamiana, commonly known as Bentham's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, narrow leaves, and panicles of small, spikelet-bearing flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. Festuca benthamiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fine texture and resilience.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural studies.
