Flindersia greavesii

Flindersia greavesii, commonly known as the Christmas tree or Christmas bush, is a member of the Rutaceae family.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to northern Australia and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom during the winter months. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued for its timber, which is used in construction and furniture-making.
