Ficinia laevis var. ciliata

Ficinia laevis var. ciliata, commonly known as the silky fig or silky leaf fig, is a variant of the Ficinia laevis species native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its smooth, velvety leaves that are often covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant typically grows as an herbaceous perennial, with a compact growth habit. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical in shape and exhibit a range of green to reddish hues depending on light exposure.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.
