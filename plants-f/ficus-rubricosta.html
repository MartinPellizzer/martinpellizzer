Ficus rubricosta

Ficus rubricosta, commonly known as the red-costed fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive red or reddish-brown veins, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ficus rubricosta is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
