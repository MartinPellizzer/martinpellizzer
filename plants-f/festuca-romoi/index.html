Festuca Romoi

Red fescue, scientifically known as Festuca romoi, is a hardy perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is prized for its fine texture, dense growth habit, and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions. This plant is commonly used in lawns, pastures, and restoration projects due to its durability and low maintenance requirements. Its vibrant red hue, particularly in its foliage, makes it a visually appealing addition to both natural and cultivated landscapes. Despite its ornamental value, Red fescue also plays an important ecological role in preventing soil erosion and supporting local wildlife.
