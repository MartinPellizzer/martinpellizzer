Faramea insignis

Faramea insignis, commonly known as the yellow mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the Rubiaceae family and typically grows on the branches of host trees, deriving some of its nutrients from the host while performing photosynthesis. The plant produces small, yellowish-green berries that are an important food source for various bird species. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a waxy surface, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitats.

Faramea insignis is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported medicinal properties.
