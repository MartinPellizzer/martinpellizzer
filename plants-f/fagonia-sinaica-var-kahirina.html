Fagonia sinaica var. kahirina

Fagonia sinaica var. kahirina, commonly known as the Sinai fagonia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the Sinai Peninsula.

It is characterized by its small, yellowish flowers and grayish, fuzzy leaves adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability. The plant thrives in sandy soils and is often found in rocky habitats, showcasing its resilience to harsh desert conditions. Its medicinal properties have been noted in traditional remedies, though scientific research on its botanical features remains limited.

The species plays a role in stabilizing desert ecosystems and supporting local biodiversity.
