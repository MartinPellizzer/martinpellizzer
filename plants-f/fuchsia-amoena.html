Fuchsia amoena

Fuchsia amoena, commonly known as the common fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant in the family Onagraceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina, and is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with woody stems and opposite, ovate leaves. Its flowers are showy, with vibrant pink to purple petals and a distinctive, spurred calyx.

Fuchsia amoena is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
