Festuca sodiroana

Festuca sodiroana, commonly known as Sodiro's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It typically grows in temperate climates and is characterized by its fine, grass-like foliage. The plant has narrow, elongated leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in grasslands and open woodlands, where it thrives in well-drained soils.

Festuca sodiroana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
