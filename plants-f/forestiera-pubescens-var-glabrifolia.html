Forestiera pubescens var. glabrifolia

Forestiera pubescens var. glabrifolia, commonly known as the smoothleaf forestiera, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are generally smooth and glossy. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in the spring. Its fruit is a small, capsule that splits open to release seeds.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and sandy soils.
