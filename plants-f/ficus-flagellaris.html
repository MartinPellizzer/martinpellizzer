Ficus flagellaris

Ficus flagellaris, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, waxy, and glossy dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by greenish-yellow fruits that turn red when mature. It is widely cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in various indoor and outdoor environments.

The species is also known for its ability to climb and spread along surfaces, making it suitable for use in hanging baskets or as a ground cover.
