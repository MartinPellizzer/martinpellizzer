Ficus lujae

Ficus lujae, commonly known as the Lujan fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate in form. Ficus lujae produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
