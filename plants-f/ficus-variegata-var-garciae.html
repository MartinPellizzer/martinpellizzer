Ficus variegata var. garciae

Ficus variegata var. garciae, commonly known as the Garcia's variegated fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking variegated leaves, which display shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are glossy and often have a waxy texture, enhancing its ornamental appeal.

It prefers warm, humid conditions and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
