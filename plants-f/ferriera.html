Ferriera

Ferriera, commonly known as the iron plant, is a unique species native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive metallic sheen on its leaves, which gives it its common name. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with small, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. Ferriera produces clusters of small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

Its botanical significance lies in its adaptability to poor soil conditions and its potential use in traditional medicine.
