Ficinia latifolia

Ficinia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf figsedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in a triangular cross-section, a characteristic feature of many sedges. Ficinia latifolia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along water edges.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, typical of the Cyperaceae family.
