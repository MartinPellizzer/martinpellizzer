Fimbristylis aspera

Fimbristylis aspera, commonly known as the rough sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, commonly referred to as the sedge family. This perennial herb typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often forming dense clusters near water bodies. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a rough texture, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in moist environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
