Fritillaria hakkarensis

Fritillaria hakkarensis, commonly known as the Hakkari fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Turkey.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, preferring well-drained soils. Its bulbs are adapted for survival in harsh environments, allowing it to thrive in high-altitude areas.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and limited distribution.
