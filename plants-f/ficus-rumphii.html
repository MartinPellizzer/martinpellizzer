Ficus rumphii

Ficus rumphii, commonly known as the Java fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically consumed raw or cooked. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Ficus rumphii is also valued for its ability to improve indoor air quality.
