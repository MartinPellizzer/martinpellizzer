Ficus mutantifolia

Ficus mutantifolia, commonly known as the variable fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can vary in shape and size depending on environmental conditions. The plant produces small, clustered fruits known as figs, which are edible and often used in local cuisines. Ficus mutantifolia thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
