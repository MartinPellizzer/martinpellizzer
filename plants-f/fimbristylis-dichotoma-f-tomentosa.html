Fimbristylis dichotoma f. tomentosa

Fimbristylis dichotoma f. tomentosa, commonly known as the silky flat sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and slender, cylindrical stems. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typically found in clusters at the base of the stems. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered with a fine, silvery-gray pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This species is often found in wet or moist habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in the presence of standing water.
