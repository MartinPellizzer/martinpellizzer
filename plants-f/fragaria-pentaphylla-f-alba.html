Fragaria pentaphylla f. alba

Fragaria pentaphylla f. alba, commonly known as the white-fruit strawberry, is a rare and attractive variant of the strawberry plant.

It is characterized by its white or pale pink fruits, which differ from the typical red coloration of most strawberries. The plant produces small, herbaceous leaves with a trifoliate structure, typical of the Fragaria genus. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in temperate regions.

This cultivar is prized for its ornamental value and unique fruit coloration.
