Fothergilla anamelis

Fothergilla anamelis, commonly known as the pink flowering shrub, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features clusters of fragrant, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. The plant has narrow, dark green leaves that turn red or orange in the fall, adding seasonal interest. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it suitable for garden landscapes.

Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for native plant gardens.
