Festuca artvinensis

Festuca artvinensis, commonly known as Artvin bentgrass, is a species of grass native to the high-altitude regions of eastern Turkey.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Festuca artvinensis is known for its ability to form dense, resilient turf, making it an important component of its native ecosystem.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest for botanists and ecologists studying mountain flora.
