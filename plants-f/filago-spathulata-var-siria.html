Filago spathulata var. siria

Filago spathulata var. siria, commonly known as the Sirian bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, blooming in late spring to early summer. The plant has hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
