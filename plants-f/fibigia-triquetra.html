Fibigia triquetra

Fibigia triquetra, commonly known as the three-cornered leek, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in clusters. Its scientific name reflects its distinctive three-angled stem, a key botanical characteristic.

Fibigia triquetra is often found in dry, rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
