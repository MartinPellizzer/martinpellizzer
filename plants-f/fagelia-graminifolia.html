Fagelia graminifolia

Fagelia graminifolia, commonly known as the grass-like fagelia, is a unique plant characterized by its foliage that resembles grass in appearance.

It belongs to the family Fagaceae, which includes oaks and beeches, though it is not a true tree. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial to full shade. Its leaves are narrow and elongated, with a soft, grassy texture, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers.

Despite its botanical classification, it plays a minor role in ecological systems compared to other members of its family.
