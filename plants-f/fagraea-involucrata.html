Fagraea involucrata

Fagraea involucrata, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often in late winter or early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a slightly waxy texture. It produces small, round fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by animals.

Fagraea involucrata is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its vibrant floral display.
