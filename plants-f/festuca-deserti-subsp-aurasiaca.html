Festuca deserti subsp. aurasiaca

Festuca deserti subsp.

aurasiaca, commonly known as the Central Asian bluegrass, is a grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

This subspecies is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including high temperatures and low precipitation. It typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in steppe and desert steppe ecosystems.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.
