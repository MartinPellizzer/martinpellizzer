Farsetia triquetra

Farsetia triquetra, commonly known as the three-lobed rockrose, is a low-growing evergreen shrub native to southern Europe.

It features small, leathery leaves that are typically three-lobed, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, white or pale yellow flowers in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

Its hardy nature and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardeners in temperate climates.
