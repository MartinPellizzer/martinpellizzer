Fumaria transsilvanica

Fumaria transsilvanica, commonly known as the Transylvanian smokeplant, is a flowering plant native to Europe, particularly found in regions like Romania and Hungary.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant has slender, climbing stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Fumaria transsilvanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine for its purported health benefits.
