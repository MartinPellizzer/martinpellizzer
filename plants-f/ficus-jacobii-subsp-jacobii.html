Ficus jacobii subsp. jacobii

Ficus jacobii subsp.

jacobii, commonly known as the Jacob's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia.

The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in indoor environments.

The fruit, which is a small fig, is edible and has a sweet, mild flavor.
