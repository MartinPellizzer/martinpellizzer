Felicia stricta

Felicia stricta, commonly known as the blueweed or blue daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. It thrives in full sun and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Felicia stricta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in garden landscapes.
