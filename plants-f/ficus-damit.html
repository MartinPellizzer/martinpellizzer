Ficus damit

Ficus damit, commonly known as the Indian rubber plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular houseplant. Its latex sap, which is milky and sticky, is used in the production of rubber.

The plant can grow quite tall and may require pruning to maintain its shape.
