Feuilleea colletiodes

Feuilleea colletiodes, commonly known as the golden leaf fern, is a unique epiphytic plant native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, feather-like fronds that are typically a vibrant shade of green, with some varieties exhibiting a golden hue on the underside. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its reproductive structures are small, spore-producing capsules that develop on the underside of its leaves.

Feuilleea colletiodes is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and indoor spaces.
