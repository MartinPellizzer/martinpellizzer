Farsetia pinnata

Farsetia pinnata, commonly known as the golden bower, is a tropical climbing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves that are pinnately divided, giving it a feathery appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that are often mistaken for leaves, contributing to its ornamental value. Farsetia pinnata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often used in horticulture for its aesthetic appeal.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
