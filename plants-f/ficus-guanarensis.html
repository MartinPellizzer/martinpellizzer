Ficus guanarensis

Ficus guanarensis, commonly known as the Guanarensis fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to South America, specifically found in regions of Venezuela and Colombia. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, clustered fruits known as figs. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is well adapted to shaded environments.
