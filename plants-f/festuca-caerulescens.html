Festuca caerulescens

Festuca caerulescens, commonly known as bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade conditions. Festuca caerulescens is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to form dense, low-maintenance lawns.

It is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
