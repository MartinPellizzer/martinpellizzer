Ficus regularis

Ficus regularis, commonly known as the common fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to southern Europe, northern Africa, and western Asia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces small, edible fruits called figs. Ficus regularis is typically an evergreen shrub or small tree, often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

Its ability to thrive in a variety of climates makes it a popular choice for both horticultural and agricultural purposes.
