Ferula lobeliana

Ferula lobeliana, commonly known as the Cape lemongrass, is a perennial herb native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are typically arranged in umbels. Its aromatic leaves and stems are used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Ferula lobeliana thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and sandy environments.
