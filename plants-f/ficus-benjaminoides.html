Ficus benjaminoides

Ficus benjaminoides, commonly known as Benjamin fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus benjaminoides is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
