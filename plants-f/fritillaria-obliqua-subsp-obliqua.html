Fritillaria obliqua subsp. obliqua

Fritillaria obliqua subsp.

obliqua, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red, often with contrasting patterns.

The plant produces a tall, slender stem with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in dry, rocky environments, and it thrives in well-drained soils.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with good drainage.
