Ferula karategina

Ferula karategina, commonly known as Karategin's fennel, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and distinctive umbel-like flower clusters. The plant features a tall, slender stem with finely divided, silvery-green leaves. Its flowering period occurs in the summer, producing yellow flowers that attract pollinators.

Ferula karategina is valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
