Fragariastrum apenninum

Fragariastrum apenninum, commonly known as the Apennine strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its basal rosette of trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, white or pinkish flowers that bloom in spring, followed by edible, red berries similar to true strawberries. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary potential, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
