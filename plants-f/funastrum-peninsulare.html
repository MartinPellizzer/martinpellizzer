Funastrum peninsulare

Funastrum peninsulare, commonly known as the Florida starling tree, is a member of the Arecaceae family, native to the southeastern United States.

It is a small, evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows in wetland habitats, such as swamps and marshes. The plant has compound leaves with long, slender leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a three-lobed, spiky capsule that contains edible seeds.
