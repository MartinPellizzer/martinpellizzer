Fumaria macrosepala subsp. macrocarpa

Fumaria macrosepala subsp.

macrocarpa, commonly known as the large-seeded glory-of-the-snow, is a herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant produces elongated, ribbed seed pods that contain numerous small, rounded seeds. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its early blooming habit and distinctive foliage.
