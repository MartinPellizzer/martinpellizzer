Flotovia imbricata

Flotovia imbricata, commonly known as the overlapping leaf vine, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that overlap like shingles, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique leaf arrangement makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
