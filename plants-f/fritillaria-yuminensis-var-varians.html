Fritillaria yuminensis var. varians

Fritillaria yuminensis var. varians, commonly known as the Yumin's fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It produces striking bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in early spring. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in well-drained, rocky soils. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species in its native habitat.
