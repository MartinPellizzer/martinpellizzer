Freesia grandiflora virginalis

Freesia grandiflora virginalis, commonly known as the virginal freesia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers that come in a variety of colors, including white, yellow, and pink. The plant belongs to the Iridaceae family, which includes other ornamental plants like irises and gladiolus. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it typically blooms in late spring to early summer.
