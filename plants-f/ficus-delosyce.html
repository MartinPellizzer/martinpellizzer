Ficus delosyce

Ficus delosyce, commonly known as the Delosyce fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. It produces edible figs that are typically consumed fresh or used in local cuisines.

Ficus delosyce is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.
