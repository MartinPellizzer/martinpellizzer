Fridericia crassa

Fridericia crassa, commonly known as the rough fridericia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and thrives in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves with serrated edges and small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a legume pod that contains several oval seeds.

Fridericia crassa is often found in open woodland and scrub habitats, where it contributes to soil stabilization and provides habitat for various insects and small animals.
