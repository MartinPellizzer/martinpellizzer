Ficus subpisocarpa

Ficus subpisocarpa, commonly known as the rock fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and small, clustered fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in dense forests.

The fruit of Ficus subpisocarpa is edible and has been used in traditional cuisines and medicinal practices in its native regions.
