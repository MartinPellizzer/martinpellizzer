Farsetia fruticosa

Farsetia fruticosa, commonly known as the African heather, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white, pink, and purple, which bloom in spring and summer. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, adapted to withstand the dry conditions of its native habitat.

Farsetia fruticosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
