Festuca recognita f. glabra

Festuca recognita f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth meadow fescue, is a grass species characterized by its finely textured, silver-green foliage.

It is a cool-season grass that thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight. The plant typically forms dense, low-growing tufts with narrow, grass-like leaves that are smooth to the touch. It is often found in open meadows, grasslands, and along the edges of woodlands.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
