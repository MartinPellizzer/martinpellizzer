Festuca haussknechtii f. diffusior

Festuca haussknechtii f. diffusior, commonly known as the spreading meadow fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and disturbed sites. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny florets.

This form is notable for its increased spreading growth compared to the typical form of Festuca haussknechtii.
