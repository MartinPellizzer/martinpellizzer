Ficus pachyrrhachis var. porrecta

Ficus pachyrrhachis var. porrecta, commonly known as the broadleaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically broad and heart-shaped. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
