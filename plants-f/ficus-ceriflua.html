Ficus ceriflua

Ficus ceriflua, commonly known as the waxleaf fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish fruits that mature to a reddish color. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus ceriflua is relatively low maintenance and can be grown indoors or outdoors in suitable conditions.
