Fritillaria bornmuelleri

Fritillaria bornmuelleri, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a robust, fleshy bulb that stores nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Fritillaria bornmuelleri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
