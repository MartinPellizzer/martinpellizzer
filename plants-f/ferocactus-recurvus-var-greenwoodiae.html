Ferocactus recurvus var. greenwoodiae

Ferocactus recurvus var. greenwoodiae, commonly known as the Greenwood's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert of North America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spiny stems that are typically green to blue-green in color. The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which are clustered at the tips of the stems. It is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems.

This variety is often cultivated for its unique appearance and resilience in dry climates.
