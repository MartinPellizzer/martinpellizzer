Fumaria glauca

Fumaria glauca, commonly known as blue copelandia, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, blue-violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branched stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Fumaria glauca is also known for its medicinal properties, historically used in traditional herbal remedies.
