Ferula monticola

Ferula monticola, commonly known as the mountain fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and hollow stems. The plant features feathery, finely divided leaves and produces yellowish-green flowers in umbels. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often containing resinous substances.

Ferula monticola is adapted to harsh, high-altitude environments and is valued for its medicinal properties in traditional medicine.
