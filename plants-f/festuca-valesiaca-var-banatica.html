Festuca valesiaca var. banatica

Festuca valesiaca var. banatica, commonly known as the Banat grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Carpathian Basin.

It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is characterized by its fine texture and ability to thrive in temperate climates.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and adaptability.
