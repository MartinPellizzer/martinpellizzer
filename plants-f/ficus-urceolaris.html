Ficus urceolaris

Ficus urceolaris, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, yellowish fruits that are edible but often avoided by humans. It is widely cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in indoor environments.

The plant is also notable for its rapid growth and sensitivity to changes in light and humidity.
