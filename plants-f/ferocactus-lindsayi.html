Ferocactus lindsayi

Ferocactus lindsayi, commonly known as Lindsay's barrel cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert of North America.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces yellow to orange flowers during the spring and summer months. It has a dense covering of spines, which provide protection from herbivores and help reduce water loss.

Ferocactus lindsayi is adapted to arid environments and is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements.
