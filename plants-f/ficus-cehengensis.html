Ficus cehengensis

Ficus cehengensis, commonly known as the Ceheng fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the island of Hainan in China and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible figs that are typically harvested when they are fully ripe and soft. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil and moderate watering.

Ficus cehengensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a food source in its native region.
