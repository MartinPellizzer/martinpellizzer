Fraxinus pannosa

Fraxinus pannosa, commonly known as the smooth ash.

It is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America, characterized by its smooth, light gray bark and opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in pendulous clusters, followed by winged fruits that disperse its seeds. It thrives in moist to mesic habitats, such as stream valleys and floodplains.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in woodworking.
