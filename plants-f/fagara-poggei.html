Fagara poggei

Fagara poggei, commonly known as the Sierra Leone fagara, is a member of the Rutaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and distinctive floral structures.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of West Africa, particularly found in countries like Sierra Leone and Liberia. The plant typically grows as an understory shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a rough texture with a strong, pungent scent.

The flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and are accompanied by a unique, fragrant aroma that attracts various pollinators.
