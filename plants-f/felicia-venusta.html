Felicia venusta

Felicia venusta, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features showy yellow ray flowers surrounding a central disk of tubular flowers, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Felicia venusta typically grows in well-drained soil and thrives in full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its hardy nature and vibrant blooms make it a valuable addition to both ornamental and ecological landscapes.
