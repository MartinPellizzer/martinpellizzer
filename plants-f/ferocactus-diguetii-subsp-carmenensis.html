Ferocactus diguetii subsp. carmenensis

Ferocactus diguetii subsp.

carmenensis, commonly known as Carmen's hedgehog cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in sharp, spiny areoles.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are often clustered at the apex of the stems. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it well-suited for desert environments.

Its hardy nature and unique appearance make it a popular choice for xeriscaping and cactus enthusiasts.
