Festuca rubra var. hirta

Festuca rubra var. hirta, commonly known as hairy red fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, hair-like leaves that are often tinged with red or purple in cooler weather. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in low-maintenance landscaping and restoration projects.

Festuca rubra var. hirta is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, supporting various pollinators and soil stabilization.
