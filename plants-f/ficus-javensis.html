Ficus javensis

Ficus javensis, commonly known as the Java fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and smooth, gray bark. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible figs, which are typically consumed by birds and mammals. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus javensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
