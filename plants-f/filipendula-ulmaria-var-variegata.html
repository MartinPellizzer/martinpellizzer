Filipendula ulmaria var. variegata

Filipendula ulmaria var. variegata, commonly known as the variegated meadowsweet, is a herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The leaves are typically heart-shaped with a distinctive variegated pattern of green and white. It thrives in moist, rich soils and is often found in wet meadows and stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
