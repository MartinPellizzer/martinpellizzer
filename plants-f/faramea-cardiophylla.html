Faramea cardiophylla

Faramea cardiophylla, commonly known as the African shield fern, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa and the Caribbean.

It is characterized by its large, shield-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, inconspicuous white flowers that are followed by red, fleshy fruits. Faramea cardiophylla thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
