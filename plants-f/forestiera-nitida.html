Forestiera nitida

Forestiera nitida, commonly known as silverleaf forestiera, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silvery-white pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in clusters during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscape gardens and naturalized settings.
