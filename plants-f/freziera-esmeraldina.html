Freziera esmeraldina

Freziera esmeraldina, commonly known as Emerald Frost, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that ranges from deep green to almost black, giving it a unique and elegant appearance. The plant produces small, delicate white flowers that bloom in late spring, adding a soft contrast to its dark leaves. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in specialized gardens and greenhouses.
