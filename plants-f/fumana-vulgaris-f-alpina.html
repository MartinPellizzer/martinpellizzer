Fumana vulgaris f. alpina

Fumana vulgaris f. alpina, commonly known as the alpine thyme, is a subspecies of the common thyme that thrives in high-altitude environments.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fragrant, small leaves that emit a strong mint-like aroma. The plant produces pink or purple flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It is well adapted to rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine meadows and mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
