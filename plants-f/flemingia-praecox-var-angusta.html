Flemingia praecox var. angusta

Flemingia praecox var. angusta, commonly known as narrow-leaved Flemingia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and pinnately compound, with small, stipulate leaflets.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
