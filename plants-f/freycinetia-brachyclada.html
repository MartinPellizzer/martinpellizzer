Freycinetia brachyclada

Freycinetia brachyclada, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its stiff, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a natural dye. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in spadices, followed by the development of three-winged fruits.

Freycinetia brachyclada is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in coastal and humid environments.
