Fragaria sterilis

Fragaria sterilis, commonly known as the wild strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and produces small, edible berries that are often mistaken for true strawberries. The plant typically grows in temperate climates and is found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its flowers are white with five petals and are pollinated by insects.

Fragaria sterilis is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its culinary use in various cuisines.
