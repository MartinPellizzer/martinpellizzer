Festuca parvigluma var. breviseta

Festuca parvigluma var. breviseta, commonly known as the short-hairy fescue, is a cool-season grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically forms dense clumps and has a low-growing, mat-forming structure. Its foliage is often covered with short, stiff hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
