Filix-mas augescens

Filix-mas augescens, commonly known as the giant tree fern, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Cyatheaceae and is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk covered in persistent, fibrous roots. The plant produces large, feather-like fronds that can grow up to several meters in length, giving it a dramatic appearance. Its spore-producing cones are typically found at the base of the fronds, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Filix-mas augescens is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
