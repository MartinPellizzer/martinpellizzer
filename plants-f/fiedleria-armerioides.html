Fiedleria armerioides

Fiedleria armerioides, commonly known as the dwarf armer's shield, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable species for ecological restoration projects.
