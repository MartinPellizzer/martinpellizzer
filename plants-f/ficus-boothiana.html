Ficus boothiana

Ficus boothiana, commonly known as the Booth's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and often have a waxy texture. Ficus boothiana produces small, clustered figs that are typically green to yellow in color.

It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
