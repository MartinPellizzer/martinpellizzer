Freirea laxiflora

Freirea laxiflora, commonly known as the slender-flowered freirea, is a member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is a perennial herb native to South America, particularly Brazil. The plant features slender, upright stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, tubular, and typically pink or purple in color, arranged in whorls along the stem.

Freirea laxiflora is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
