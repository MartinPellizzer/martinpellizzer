Ficus carica var. domestica

Ficus carica var. domestica, commonly known as the common fig, is a deciduous tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces edible fruits called figs. The plant has a shallow root system and thrives in well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its fruit, which can be consumed fresh or used in various culinary applications.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide shade.
