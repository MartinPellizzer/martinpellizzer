Ficus nagayamae

Ficus nagayamae, commonly known as the Nagayama fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Japan and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are often heart-shaped at the base. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus nagayamae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
