Ficus cerifera

Ficus cerifera, commonly known as the wax fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are thick and waxy to the touch. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, greenish fruits that mature to a yellowish hue. Ficus cerifera thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
