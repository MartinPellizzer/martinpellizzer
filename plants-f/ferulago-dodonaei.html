Ferulago dodonaei

Ferulago dodonaei, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of blue to purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with hairy surfaces, and its flowers are typically arranged in terminal umbels. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine.
