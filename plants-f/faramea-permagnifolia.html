Faramea permagnifolia

Faramea permagnifolia, commonly known as the giant faramea, is a striking tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, white flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, often followed by orange-red berries. Faramea permagnifolia thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for botanical gardens and greenhouses.
