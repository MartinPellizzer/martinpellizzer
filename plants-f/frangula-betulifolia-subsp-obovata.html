Frangula betulifolia subsp. obovata

Frangula betulifolia subsp.

obovata, commonly known as the glossy buckthorn, is a shrub native to the eastern United States. It is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and dark green, oval-shaped leaves that turn reddish in the fall.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are an important food source for birds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Rutaceae family.
