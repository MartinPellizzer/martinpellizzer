Faujasia salicifolia

Faujasia salicifolia, commonly known as the willowleaf bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features lance-shaped, willow-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a valuable addition to both ornamental gardens and ecological restoration projects.
