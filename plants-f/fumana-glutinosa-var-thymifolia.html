Fumana glutinosa var. thymifolia

Fumana glutinosa var. thymifolia, commonly known as the sticky thyme fern, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its distinctive, aromatic leaves that emit a strong, pleasant fragrance. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. Its leaves are covered in a sticky, resinous substance, which gives it its common name.

This species is often found in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade.
