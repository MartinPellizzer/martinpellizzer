Fraxinus americana var. rubicunda

Fraxinus americana var. rubicunda, commonly known as the red-leaf ash, is a variant of the American ash tree characterized by its striking reddish foliage.

This plant is native to the eastern United States and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a vibrant red coloration during the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by samaroid fruits that disperse by wind.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
