Ficus luzonensis

Ficus luzonensis, commonly known as the Luzon Fig, is a species of fig tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are usually clustered in small groups. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-draining soil and partial to full sunlight.

Ficus luzonensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
