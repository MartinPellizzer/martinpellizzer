Fumaria judaica subsp. insignis

Fumaria judaica subsp.

insignis, commonly known as the Jewish cress, is a member of the Papaveraceae family. It is a low-growing annual herb characterized by its slender stems and small, yellowish-green flowers.

The plant typically thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in fields, along roadsides, and in waste areas. Its leaves are alternate, ovate to lanceolate, and covered in fine pubescence.

The species is notable for its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of edible leaves.
