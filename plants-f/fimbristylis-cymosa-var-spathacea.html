Fimbristylis cymosa var. spathacea

Fimbristylis cymosa var. spathacea, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spathaceous flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The plant has slender, linear leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence is characterized by a spathaceous bract, which encloses the flowering structures.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various wildlife.
