Flagellaria indica var. bifurcata

Flagellaria indica var. bifurcata, commonly known as the Indian whipsnap or cow's tail, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of Asia.

It features long, slender, whip-like stems that can grow up to several meters in length. The plant produces small, clustered white or greenish flowers followed by distinctive, three-lobed fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
