Ficus meizonochlamys

Ficus meizonochlamys, commonly known as the red-veined fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves that have a distinctive reddish vein pattern. The plant produces small, inedible fruits that are not typically consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Ficus meizonochlamys is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
