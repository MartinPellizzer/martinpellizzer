Festuca kingii

Festuca kingii, commonly known as King's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

Festuca kingii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
