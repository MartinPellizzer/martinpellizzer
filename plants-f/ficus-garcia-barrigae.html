Ficus garcia-barrigae

Ficus garcia-barrigae, commonly known as the Garcia-Barriga fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves and small, clustered fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
