Festuca tzvelevii

Festuca tzvelevii, commonly known as Tzvelev's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically forms dense mats and is often found in rocky or arid environments. Its flowers are small and spike-like, arranged in a panicle, and it produces light seeds that are dispersed by wind.

Festuca tzvelevii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging soils.
