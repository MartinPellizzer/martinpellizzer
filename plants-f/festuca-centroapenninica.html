Festuca centroapenninica

Festuca centroapenninica, commonly known as the Central Apennine fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of central Italy.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Festuca centroapenninica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
