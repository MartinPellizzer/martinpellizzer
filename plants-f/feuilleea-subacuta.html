Feuilleea subacuta

Feuilleea subacuta, commonly known as the sharp-leafed fern, is a species of flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by spherical, dry fruits containing several seeds. Feuilleea subacuta thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in dense forest undergrowth.

Its ornamental value has led to its cultivation in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in some regions.
