Farfugium japonicum var. japonicum

Farfugium japonicum var. japonicum, commonly known as Japanese buttercup, is a perennial herb native to Japan.

It features a rosette of large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically green with a bluish tint. The plant produces yellow flowers in the summer, which are clustered in a cup-like structure at the center of the leaf rosette. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
