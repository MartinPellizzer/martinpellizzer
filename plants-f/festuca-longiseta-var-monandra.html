Festuca longiseta var. monandra

Festuca longiseta var. monandra, commonly known as the narrowleaf fescue, is a perennial grass species native to northern regions of North America.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in native plant gardens.
