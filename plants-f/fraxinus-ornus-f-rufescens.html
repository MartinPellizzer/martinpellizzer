Fraxinus ornus f. rufescens

Fraxinus ornus f. rufescens, commonly known as the red-fruited ash, is a variant of the common ash tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bark and its small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in pendulous clusters. The leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin. The fruit is a samara, typically reddish in color, which contributes to its ornamental value.

This form is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and parks.
