Festuca iberica subsp. atlantigena

Festuca iberica subsp.

atlantigena, commonly known as the Atlantic mountain fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs.

This subspecies thrives in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
