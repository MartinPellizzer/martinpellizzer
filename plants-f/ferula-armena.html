Ferula armena

Ferula armena, commonly known as Armenian giant fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus and Turkey.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow, cylindrical stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its leaves are finely divided and aromatic, with a strong, spicy scent.

Ferula armena is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
