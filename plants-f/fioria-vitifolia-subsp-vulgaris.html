Fioria vitifolia subsp. vulgaris

Fioria vitifolia subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as the common grapeleaf, is a shrub native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that are often lobed or serrated.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it suitable for use in xeriscaping and as a ground cover in dry gardens.
