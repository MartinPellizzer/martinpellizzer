Ficus bennettii

Ficus bennettii, commonly known as the Bennett's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. The fruit, known as a fig, is typically green when mature and is edible.

Ficus bennettii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
