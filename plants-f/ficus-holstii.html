Ficus holstii

Ficus holstii, commonly known as the Holst's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and have a glossy appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fig-like fruits.

Ficus holstii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
