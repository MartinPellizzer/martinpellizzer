Ficus baeuerlenii var. vulcaniformis

Ficus baeuerlenii var. vulcaniformis, commonly known as the volcanic ficus, is a rare and unique variant of the ficus genus native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that exhibit a striking, almost metallic sheen, which helps it retain moisture in its natural habitat. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, greenish fruits that are important for local wildlife. Its root system is robust, allowing it to thrive in rocky or nutrient-poor soils.

Due to its limited distribution and specific ecological requirements, it is considered a conservation concern.
