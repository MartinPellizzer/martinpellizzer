Freycinetia kamiana

Freycinetia kamiana, commonly known as the Kamian palm, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a type of palm characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by triangular, fibrous fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
