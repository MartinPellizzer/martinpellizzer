Ferocactus bicolor var. flavidispinus

Ferocactus bicolor var. flavidispinus, commonly known as the golden spined hedgehog cactus, is a succulent native to Mexico.

It features a cylindrical stem that is typically green with a reddish tinge, and it produces vibrant yellow flowers during the summer months. The plant has thick, spiny ribs that help reduce water loss and protect it from herbivores. Its leaves are modified into spines, and it stores water in its fleshy stem for survival in arid environments.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and ability to thrive in dry, sunny conditions.
