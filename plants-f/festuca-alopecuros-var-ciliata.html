Festuca alopecuros var. ciliata

Festuca alopecuros var. ciliata, commonly known as the hairy fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green, narrow leaves with hairy margins. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear delicate blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and woodland edges.

Festuca alopecuros var. ciliata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in marginal environments.
