Ferula tortuosa

Ferula tortuosa, commonly known as the Persian cow parsley, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stem and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a strong-smelling, milky sap that contains volatile oils, which are used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with narrow, linear leaflets.

Ferula tortuosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has historical uses in herbal remedies.
