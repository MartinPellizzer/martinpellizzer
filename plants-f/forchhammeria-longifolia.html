Forchhammeria longifolia

Forchhammeria longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved forchhammeria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, blooming in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
