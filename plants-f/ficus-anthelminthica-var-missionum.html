Ficus anthelminthica var. missionum

Ficus anthelminthica var. missionum, commonly known as the Mission fig, is a tropical plant native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
