Fenerivia chapelieri

Fenerivia chapelieri, commonly known as the chapelieri's fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It features finely divided, arching fronds that give it a graceful appearance, often reaching lengths of up to 1 meter. The plant prefers shaded, moist environments and thrives in tropical climates with high humidity. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are typically located on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and endangered species in the wild.
