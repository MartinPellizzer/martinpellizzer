Fragaria virginiana var. platypetala

Fragaria virginiana var. platypetala, commonly known as the flat-petaled strawberry, is a perennial herb native to eastern North America.

It produces small, edible fruits that are typically less flavorful than cultivated strawberries. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its roots are fibrous and often grow close to the soil surface.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
