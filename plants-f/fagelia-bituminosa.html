Fagelia bituminosa

Fagelia bituminosa, commonly known as the bituminous acacia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a small shrub or tree that typically grows in dry, rocky habitats. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences. Its bark is thick and fissured, often exuding a sticky, resinous substance.

Fagelia bituminosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local communities.
