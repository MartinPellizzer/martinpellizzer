Fumaria digitata

Fumaria digitata, commonly known as the common fumitory, is a flowering plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds, which are dispersed by wind. Fumaria digitata thrives in disturbed soils and can often be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

It is also known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
