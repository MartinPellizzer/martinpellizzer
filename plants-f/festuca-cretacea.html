Festuca cretacea

Festuca cretacea, commonly known as Cretaceous fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, calcareous soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky areas. Festuca cretacea produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
