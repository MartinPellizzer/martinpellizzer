Faucibarba parviflora

Faucibarba parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered beardtongue, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the plantain family (Plantaginaceae) and is characterized by its hairy, basal rosette of leaves. The plant produces clusters of small, tubular flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. It thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in dry, open habitats.

Faucibarba parviflora is adapted to arid environments and is drought-tolerant once established.
