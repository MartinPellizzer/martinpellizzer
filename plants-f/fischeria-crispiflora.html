Fischeria crispiflora

Fischeria crispiflora, commonly known as the crinkled-flowered Fischeria, is a striking member of the Acanthaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to purple, often with a distinctive crinkled appearance. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a unique texture.

Fischeria crispiflora thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and botanical collections.
