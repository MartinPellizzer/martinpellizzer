Ficus salicaria

Ficus salicaria, commonly known as the redstem fig, is a species of flowering plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. Its small, clustered flowers are usually green or yellowish, and it produces small, round fruits that are edible for some animals.

The plant is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
