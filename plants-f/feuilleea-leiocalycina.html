Feuilleea leiocalycina

Feuilleea leiocalycina, commonly known as the silverleaf fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features delicate, arching fronds that are covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered spore cases called sori on the underside of its leaves. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil.

Feuilleea leiocalycina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and air-purifying properties.
