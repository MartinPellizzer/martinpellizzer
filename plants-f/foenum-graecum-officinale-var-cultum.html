Foenum-graecum officinale var. cultum

Foenum-graecum officinale var. cultum, commonly known as fennel, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is characterized by its feathery, finely divided leaves and yellow umbels of flowers. The plant produces small, aromatic seeds that are widely used in cooking and traditional medicine. Fennel thrives in temperate climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Its cultivation has a long history, dating back to ancient times, and it is valued for its medicinal and culinary properties.
