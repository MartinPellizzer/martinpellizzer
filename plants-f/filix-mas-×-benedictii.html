Filix-mas × benedictii

Filix-mas × benedictii, commonly known as the bishop's weed or bishop's hat, is a hybrid fern that combines traits from the male fern (Filix-mas) and the bishop's hat (Filix-mas benedictii).

It is characterized by its distinctive, umbrella-like fronds that can reach up to 1 meter in length. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in temperate regions. Its spores are released from the fertile fronds, which are typically found at the base of the plant.

The hybrid is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both horticultural and traditional medicinal purposes.
