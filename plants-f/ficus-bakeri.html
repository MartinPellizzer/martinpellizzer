Ficus bakeri

Ficus bakeri, commonly known as the Baker's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Ficus bakeri is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers indirect sunlight.
