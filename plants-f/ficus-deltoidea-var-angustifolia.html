Ficus deltoidea var. angustifolia

Ficus deltoidea var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features simple, alternate, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are typically narrow and have a smooth upper surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are enclosed in a fleshy, spiky receptacle. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage is valued for its aesthetic appeal and its ability to tolerate various soil conditions.
