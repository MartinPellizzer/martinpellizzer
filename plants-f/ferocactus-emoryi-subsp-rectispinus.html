Ferocactus emoryi subsp. rectispinus

Ferocactus emoryi subsp.

rectispinus, commonly known as the Emory's prickly pear, is a cactus species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that are often covered in sharp, spiny areoles.

The plant produces vibrant yellow to orange flowers during the summer months, which are followed by red, fleshy fruits. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, requiring well-drained soil and full sun exposure.

This subspecies is known for its drought tolerance and ability to store water in its thick, fleshy stems.
