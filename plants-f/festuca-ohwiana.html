Festuca ohwiana

Festuca ohwiana, commonly known as Ohw's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. Festuca ohwiana is adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained soils and high altitudes.

It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
