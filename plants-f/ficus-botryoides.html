Ficus botryoides

Ficus botryoides, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping, weeping branches that give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are glossy, dark green, and arranged oppositely along the stems. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, greenish fruits.

This plant is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
