Ficus vrieseana var. chamaecarpa

Ficus vrieseana var. chamaecarpa, commonly known as the Variegated Ficus or the Vriesea Ficus, is a popular ornamental plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features broad, waxy leaves with striking variegation, often displaying shades of white, yellow, and green. The plant is known for its adaptability to indoor conditions, making it a favored choice for houseplants. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate watering to avoid root rot.

Its compact growth habit and ease of care contribute to its popularity among plant enthusiasts.
