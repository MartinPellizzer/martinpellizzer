Fourneaua

Fourneaua, commonly known as the golden bell.

It is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region, characterized by its striking yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are tubular and hang in clusters, resembling small bells, which give it its common name.

Fourneaua is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
