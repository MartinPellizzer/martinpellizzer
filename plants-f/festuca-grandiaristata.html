Festuca grandiaristata

Festuca grandiaristata, commonly known as the tall fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with tall, erect stems that can reach heights of up to 1.5 meters. The leaves are typically narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration, and the plant produces spikelets that are arranged in dense clusters. Festuca grandiaristata is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides, and it is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.

This species is also known for its ability to thrive in a wide range of climates, making it a popular choice for landscaping and turfgrass applications.
