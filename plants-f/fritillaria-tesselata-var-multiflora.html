Fritillaria tesselata var. multiflora

Fritillaria tesselata var. multiflora, commonly known as the clustered fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish-orange with dark markings, blooming in early spring. The plant produces multiple flowers per stem, contributing to its common name. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette.

It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a striking addition to rock gardens and alpine settings.
