Festuca pinetorum

Festuca pinetorum, commonly known as pinegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the northern hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, sunny areas such as pine forests and rocky slopes. The plant has slender, tufted culms with fine, needle-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed flowers.

Festuca pinetorum is well adapted to harsh environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
