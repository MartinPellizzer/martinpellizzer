Ficus calimana

Ficus calimana, commonly known as the Calimana fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly found in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into edible figs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Ficus calimana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food in its native habitat.
