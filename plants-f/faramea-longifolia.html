Faramea longifolia

Faramea longifolia, commonly known as the African snowball or African holly, is a member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is a shrub or small tree native to tropical Africa, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant features opposite, elliptical leaves that are often glossy and dark green. Its small, white flowers are clustered in umbels, giving it a snowball-like appearance.

Faramea longifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
