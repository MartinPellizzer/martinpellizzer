Ficus longipedata

Ficus longipedata, commonly known as the long-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly regions of Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, round, fig-like structure that is edible and often used in traditional medicine.
