Fritillaria japonica f. tamagawae

Fritillaria japonica f. tamagawae, commonly known as the Japanese fritillary or Japanese crown imperial, is a striking bulbous perennial plant native to Japan.

It features large, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange, often with intricate patterns resembling the Japanese character for "crown.". The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces showy blooms in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland settings.

Its ornamental value is further enhanced by its ability to naturalize over time, creating clusters of vibrant flowers.
