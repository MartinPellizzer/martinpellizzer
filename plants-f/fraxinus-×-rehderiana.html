Fraxinus × rehderiana

Fraxinus × rehderiana, commonly known as the Chinese ash, is a hybrid deciduous tree belonging to the Oleaceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves with serrated margins. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Fraxinus × rehderiana is also valued for its hard, durable wood, which has been used in traditional crafts and construction.
