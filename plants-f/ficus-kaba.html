Ficus kaba

Ficus kaba, commonly known as the Kaba fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into edible figs. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Ficus kaba is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
