Filipendula nuda

Filipendula nuda, commonly known as meadow sweet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features tall, slender stems that are covered in fine, silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a slightly serrated edge, contributing to its ornamental value.

Filipendula nuda is often found in moist meadows and along stream banks, where it thrives in well-drained, fertile soils.
