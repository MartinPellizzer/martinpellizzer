Ferula galbaniflua

Ferula galbaniflua, commonly known as Persian galbanum, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. Its most notable feature is the aromatic gum resin it exudes, which has been used for centuries in traditional medicine and perfumery.

This species is also valued for its ecological role in arid and semi-arid environments.
