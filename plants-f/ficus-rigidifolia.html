Ficus rigidifolia

Ficus rigidifolia, commonly known as the rigid-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green and have a leathery texture. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible figs that are often consumed by local populations.

Ficus rigidifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
