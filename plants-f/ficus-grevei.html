Ficus grevei

Ficus grevei, commonly known as the Grevillea fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fig-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Ficus grevei prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it a versatile choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
