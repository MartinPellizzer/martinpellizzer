Ficus davidsoniae

Ficus davidsoniae, commonly known as the African ficus or the tree of life, is a unique evergreen shrub or small tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically oval or elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are followed by spherical, fleshy fruits. Its roots are known to exude a milky sap, which can cause skin irritation.

Ficus davidsoniae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.
