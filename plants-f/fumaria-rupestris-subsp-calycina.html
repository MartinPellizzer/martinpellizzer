Fumaria rupestris subsp. calycina

Fumaria rupestris subsp.

calycina, commonly known as the rock fumewort, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is a low-growing perennial that thrives in rocky and limestone soils, often found in dry, sunny environments.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate poor soil conditions.
