Fuchsia regia var. radicans

Fuchsia regia var. radicans, commonly known as the trailing fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit, making it suitable for hanging baskets or cascading displays. The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers in shades of pink, red, and purple, which bloom throughout the growing season. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often covered in a fine, silvery down.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in bright, indirect light.
