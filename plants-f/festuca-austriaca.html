Festuca austriaca

Festuca austriaca, commonly known as Austrian bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine regions.

Festuca austriaca is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
