Ficus populeaster

Ficus populeaster, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive, drooping leaves that give it a cascading appearance. The plant has a smooth, grayish-brown bark and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by round, greenish fruits. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its elegant foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.

However, it can be toxic if ingested, making it important to handle with care.
