Fridericia oxycarpa

Fridericia oxycarpa, commonly known as the sharp-fruited fridericia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to South America, particularly Brazil, and is known for its distinctive, elongated, and sharply pointed fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters, with compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. Its fruits are dry and dehiscent, splitting open to release seeds, which is a common reproductive strategy in the Fabaceae family.

Fridericia oxycarpa is of interest to botanists and horticulturists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological roles in its native habitat.
