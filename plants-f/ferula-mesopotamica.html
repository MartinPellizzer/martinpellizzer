Ferula mesopotamica

Ferula mesopotamica, commonly known as the Mesopotamian ferula, is a perennial herb native to the Middle East and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in umbel-like clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its root system is extensive and contains a milky sap that has been used in traditional medicine.

Ferula mesopotamica is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in perfumery.
