Ficus laevis var. dasyphylla

Ficus laevis var. dasyphylla, commonly known as the glossy fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by fig-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
