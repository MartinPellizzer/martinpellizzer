Ferula olgae

Ferula olgae, commonly known as the Persian cow's milk plant, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of Central Asia and the Middle East.

It belongs to the carrot family (Apiaceae) and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that has been traditionally used in folk medicine for its purported healing properties. Its leaves are narrow and aromatic, often used in local culinary practices.

Ferula olgae is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
