Fritillaria carica

Fritillaria carica, commonly known as the Persian bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with dark spots. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a spherical bulb. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Fritillaria carica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique appearance.
