Foeniculum tortuosum

Foeniculum tortuosum, commonly known as sweet fennel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in umbrella-like clusters. The plant produces aromatic seeds that are widely used in cooking and traditional medicine. Its essential oils, rich in compounds like anethole, contribute to its distinctive licorice-like fragrance.

Foeniculum tortuosum thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
