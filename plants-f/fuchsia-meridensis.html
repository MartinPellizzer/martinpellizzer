Fuchsia meridensis

Fuchsia meridensis, commonly known as the mountain fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in cloud forests and paramo ecosystems. The plant produces striking, tubular flowers that range in color from deep red to purple, attracting pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are usually narrow and lance-shaped, adapted to the cool, moist conditions of its habitat.

Fuchsia meridensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable climate conditions.
