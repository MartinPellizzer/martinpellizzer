Festuca elatior var. littorea

Festuca elatior var. littorea, commonly known as sea bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to coastal regions of Europe and Asia.

It thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dunes and tidal zones. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to withstand salt spray and strong winds.

Its ecological importance includes stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for various small organisms.
