Fagus antarctica

Fagus antarctica, commonly known as the Antarctic beech, is a deciduous tree native to New Zealand's Southern Alps.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark bark and broad, lance-shaped leaves that turn a striking golden-yellow in autumn. The tree produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, three-lobed nuts enclosed in a spiky husk. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its limited range and sensitivity to environmental changes, it is considered a vulnerable species.
