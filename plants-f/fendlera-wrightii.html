Fendlera wrightii

Fendlera wrightii, commonly known as the desert bell, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a shrub that typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, featuring woody stems and small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, often appearing in clusters. It has adapted to survive in harsh conditions with deep roots and thick cuticles to reduce water loss.

Fendlera wrightii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in desert ecosystems.
