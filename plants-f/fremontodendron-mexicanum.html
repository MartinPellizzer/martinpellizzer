Fremontodendron mexicanum

Fremontodendron mexicanum, commonly known as the Mexican flame tree, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, pink to red flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its bark is typically smooth and grayish. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Fremontodendron mexicanum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to withstand drought conditions.
