Festuca valida

Festuca valida, commonly known as bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green blades and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and open woodlands. Festuca valida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in turfgrass and landscaping.

It is also an important forage plant for livestock.
