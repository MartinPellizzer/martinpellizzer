Festuca unifaria var. izhorica

Festuca unifaria var. izhorica, commonly known as the Izhorian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Russian Far East.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to blue-gray florets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in cooler, temperate climates.
