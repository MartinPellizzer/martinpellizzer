Flotovia latifolia

Flotovia latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf water fern, is a aquatic plant characterized by its wide, floating leaves that provide shade and oxygen to water bodies.

It thrives in shallow, still waters and is often found in wetlands and ponds. The plant has a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread easily across the water surface. Its leaves are typically green and can reach lengths of up to 30 centimeters.

Flotovia latifolia plays a vital role in aquatic ecosystems by supporting aquatic life and improving water quality.
