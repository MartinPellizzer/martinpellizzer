Ficus gryllus

Ficus gryllus, commonly known as the "gryllus fig tree," is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide food for both humans and wildlife.
