Festuca divaricata

Festuca divaricata, commonly known as bluegrass or slender feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green blades that often have a bluish tinge. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, woodlands, and rocky areas. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, open panicle with small, spikelet-like flowers.

Festuca divaricata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
