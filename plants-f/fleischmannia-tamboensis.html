Fleischmannia tamboensis

Fleischmannia tamboensis, commonly known as the Natal lily, is a striking perennial herb native to South Africa.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces tall, slender flower stalks that bear large, showy white or pale pink flowers. The plant is characterized by its delicate, fragrant blooms that appear in late summer to early autumn. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Fleischmannia tamboensis is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological importance in supporting local pollinators.
