Ferreyranthus ramonii

Ferreyranthus ramonii, commonly known as the Ramon's firebush, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red and orange tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. The plant has succulent, fleshy leaves that help it retain water in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a popular choice for drought-tolerant gardens.
