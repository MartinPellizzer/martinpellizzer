Fritillaria przewalskii

Fritillaria przewalskii, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a robust underground bulb that stores nutrients. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal uses, it is also valued in traditional Chinese medicine.
