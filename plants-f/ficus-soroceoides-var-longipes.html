Ficus soroceoides var. longipes

Ficus soroceoides var. longipes, commonly known as the long-peduncled fig, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, characterized by its smooth, greyish bark and opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy upper surface. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences followed by edible, green figs that ripen to a yellowish color.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.
