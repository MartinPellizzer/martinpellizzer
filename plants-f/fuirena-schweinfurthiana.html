Fuirena schweinfurthiana

Fuirena schweinfurthiana, commonly known as the Schwerin's bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving them a slightly fuzzy texture. The plant produces striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in summer, typically arranged in clusters at the ends of stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a suitable choice for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its delicate appearance and long blooming period.
