Festuca ovina subsp. remota

Festuca ovina subsp.

remota, commonly known as the alpine bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to mountainous regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage that often forms dense mats on rocky or alpine soils.

The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences bearing small, wind-pollinated flowers. It is adapted to cold climates and high altitudes, demonstrating resilience to harsh environmental conditions. Festuca ovina subsp.

remota is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in rugged terrains.
