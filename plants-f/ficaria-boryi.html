Ficaria boryi

Ficaria boryi, commonly known as the silverleaf saxifrage, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, often in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens.
