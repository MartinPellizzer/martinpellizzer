Filipendula palmata f. nuda

Filipendula palmata f. nuda, commonly known as the naked leaf thimbleweed, is a variant of the thimbleweed species native to North America.

This plant is characterized by its reduced leaf structure, with the leaves appearing almost leafless, giving it its common name. It typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant produces small, white to pinkish flowers in compact clusters, which are arranged in a distinctive umbrella-like shape.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for study in plant morphology and ecological adaptation.
