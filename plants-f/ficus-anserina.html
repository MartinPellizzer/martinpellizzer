Ficus anserina

Ficus anserina, commonly known as the white fig or Egyptian fig, is a flowering plant native to the Nile Valley and parts of the Mediterranean.

It is a small, evergreen tree or shrub that typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 meters. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves that are glossy and often have a waxy texture. Its flowers are small and clustered, and it produces edible figs that are typically green when mature.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
