Fagara brieyi

Fagara brieyi, commonly known as the Caribbean prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the Caribbean region.

It is characterized by its spiny stems and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom during the warmer months. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed locally. Fagara brieyi is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it resilient to drought conditions.

Its unique morphology and ecological role make it an important plant in its native habitat.
