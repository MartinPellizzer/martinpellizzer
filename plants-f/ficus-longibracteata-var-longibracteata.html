Ficus longibracteata var. longibracteata

Ficus longibracteata var. longibracteata, commonly known as the long-bract fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia, and is characterized by its distinctive, elongated bracts that surround the inflorescence. The plant typically grows as a small tree or shrub, reaching heights of up to 5 meters, with thick, dark green leaves that are glossy and oval in shape. It produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are often used in traditional medicine and local cuisines.

The plant is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates, thriving in humid environments with partial shade.
