Festuca graeca

Festuca graeca, commonly known as Greek feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray foliage and tufted inflorescences that resemble feathery plumes. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in open habitats such as cliffs and slopes. Festuca graeca is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its drought tolerance and aesthetic value.

Its botanical features make it a resilient and visually striking addition to native plant landscapes.
