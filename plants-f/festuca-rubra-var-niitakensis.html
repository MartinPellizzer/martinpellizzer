Festuca rubra var. niitakensis

Festuca rubra var. niitakensis, commonly known as the Japanese red fescue, is a cool-season grass native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dense, carpet-like foliage. The plant typically produces slender, spike-like inflorescences with small, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
