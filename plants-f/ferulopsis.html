Ferulopsis

Ferulopsis, commonly known as the Indian arrowroot, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Amaranthaceae.

It consists of several species, primarily found in tropical and subtropical regions of Asia. The plants are characterized by their erect, herbaceous stems and alternate, lance-shaped leaves. They produce small, clustered flowers that are typically green or pale yellow.

Ferulopsis species are often cultivated for their starchy roots, which are used in traditional cuisines and medicinal practices.
