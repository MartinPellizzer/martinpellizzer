Fagus sylvatica var. purpurea

Fagus sylvatica var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple beech, is a variant of the European beech.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple-bronze foliage, which turns reddish in autumn. The leaves are typically larger and more elongated compared to the standard beech. It grows in temperate climates and prefers well-drained, fertile soils.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and parks.
