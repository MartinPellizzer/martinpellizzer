Fritillaria graeca var. gussichiae

Fritillaria graeca var. gussichiae, commonly known as the Greek fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is typically found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it blooms in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
