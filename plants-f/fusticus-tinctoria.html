Fusticus tinctoria

Fusticus tinctoria, commonly known as the red root or redroot pigweed, is a flowering plant native to North America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and small, alternate leaves that are typically lance-shaped. The plant produces clusters of tiny, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer months. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Fusticus tinctoria has been historically used for its medicinal properties, particularly for its purported ability to treat skin conditions.
