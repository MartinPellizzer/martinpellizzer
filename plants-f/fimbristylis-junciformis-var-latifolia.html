Fimbristylis junciformis var. latifolia

Fimbristylis junciformis var. latifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf flat sedge, is a herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often broader than the typical variety, giving it its varietal name. Its inflorescence consists of dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers, which are usually arranged in a cylindrical shape.

This species is valued in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support aquatic ecosystems.
