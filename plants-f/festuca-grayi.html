Festuca grayi

Festuca grayi, commonly known as Gray's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny areas such as meadows and rocky slopes. Festuca grayi is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone environments.

It is also adapted to drought conditions, making it a resilient choice for native landscaping.
