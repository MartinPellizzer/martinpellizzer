Festuca × pachyphylla

Festuca × pachyphylla, commonly known as the tussock grass, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to harsh, alpine environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
