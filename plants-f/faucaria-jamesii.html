Faucaria jamesii

Faucaria jamesii, commonly known as the James's stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to the southwestern United States.

It features fleshy, blue-green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white powdery wax. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the summer months. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it well-suited for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
