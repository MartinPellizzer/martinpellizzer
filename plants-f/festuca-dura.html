Festuca dura

Festuca dura, commonly known as hard fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green blades and tufted growth habit, making it visually appealing in landscaping. This plant is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for low-maintenance gardens. Festuca dura is also valued for its ability to withstand heavy foot traffic, making it a popular choice for lawns and erosion control.

Its flowering spikes add a subtle ornamental touch, typically appearing in late spring to early summer.
