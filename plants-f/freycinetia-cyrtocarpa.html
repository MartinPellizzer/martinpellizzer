Freycinetia cyrtocarpa

Freycinetia cyrtocarpa, commonly known as the broad-leafed pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Pandanaceae and is characterized by its tall, leafy stems and large, strap-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that develop into woody, fleshy fruits. It thrives in coastal and lowland areas, often growing near mangroves and in humid tropical climates.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and traditional uses in local cultures.
