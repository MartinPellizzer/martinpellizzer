Festuca unifaria

Festuca unifaria, commonly known as slender fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, grassy areas and is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and slender stems. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Festuca unifaria is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along streambanks.

Its botanical features make it an important component of many native ecosystems.
