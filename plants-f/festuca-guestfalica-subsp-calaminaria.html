Festuca guestfalica subsp. calaminaria

Festuca guestfalica subsp.

calaminaria, commonly known as the Calaminaria fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

This subspecies typically grows in calcareous soils, often found in grasslands and open woodlands. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with narrow, awnless lemma-like structures.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitats.
