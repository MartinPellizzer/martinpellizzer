Frangula rubra subsp. nevadensis

Frangula rubra subsp.

nevadensis, commonly known as the Nevada buckthorn, is a shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in the spring.

The plant is notable for its smooth, reddish bark and its ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils. It is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats, contributing to local biodiversity.

The berries it produces are an important food source for various wildlife species.
