Fritillaria olivieri

Fritillaria olivieri, commonly known as the olive lily, is a bulbous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or yellow in color, often with dark markings. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to thrive in dry, rocky soils. It is often found in open woodlands and scrublands, where it blooms in spring.

The bulbs of Fritillaria olivieri are used in traditional medicine and horticulture due to their ornamental value.
