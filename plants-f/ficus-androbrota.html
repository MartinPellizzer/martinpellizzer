Ficus androbrota

Ficus androbrota, commonly known as the Androbrota fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus androbrota is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
