Fragaria vesca var. viridis

Fragaria vesca var. viridis, commonly known as the green strawberry, is a variant of the woodland strawberry.

It is characterized by its small, green berries that are typically less sweet and more tart compared to the common red strawberries. The plant features trifoliate leaves with a serrated edge and produces white flowers with five petals. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage and fruit.
