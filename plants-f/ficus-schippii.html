Ficus schippii

Ficus schippii, commonly known as the Schippi's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate in form. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature.

Ficus schippii is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
