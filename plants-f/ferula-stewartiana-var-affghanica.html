Ferula stewartiana var. affghanica

Ferula stewartiana var. affghanica, commonly known as the Afghan giant fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, umbrella-like inflorescences that produce yellow flowers. The plant has feathery, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Its seeds are highly valued for their essential oils, which have various therapeutic uses.

The botanical features of this plant make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
