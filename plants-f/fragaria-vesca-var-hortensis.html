Fragaria vesca var. hortensis

Fragaria vesca var. hortensis, commonly known as the woodland strawberry, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, delicate white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries. The plant thrives in cool climates and partial shade, often found in woodland areas or gardens. Its berries are edible, with a sweet and tart flavor, and are used in culinary applications.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
