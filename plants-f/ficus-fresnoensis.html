Ficus fresnoensis

Ficus fresnoensis, commonly known as the Santa Rosa fig, is a small, evergreen tree native to the coastal regions of central and southern California.

It has smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that ripen to a reddish-purple color. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for coastal gardens.

Ficus fresnoensis is also valued for its role in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental appeal.
