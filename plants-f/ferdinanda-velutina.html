Ferdinanda velutina

Ferdinanda velutina, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a unique plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its soft, velvety leaves that give it its common name. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests. Its small, delicate flowers are typically white or pale pink, adding to its ornamental appeal.

Ferdinanda velutina is valued not only for its aesthetic qualities but also for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
