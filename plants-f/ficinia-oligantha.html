Ficinia oligantha

Ficinia oligantha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf figbush, is a small evergreen shrub native to eastern Australia.

It features small, opposite, elliptic leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and dry forest habitats.

Ficinia oligantha is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits to local wildlife.
