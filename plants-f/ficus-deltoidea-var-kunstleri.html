Ficus deltoidea var. kunstleri

Ficus deltoidea var. kunstleri, commonly known as the Kunstleri fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible figs, though the fruit is often not consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its air-purifying qualities and aesthetic appeal.

The species is also valued for its ability to adapt to various light conditions, making it relatively easy to care for.
