Festuca sumneviczii

Festuca sumneviczii, commonly known as the Transcaucasian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the Caucasus.

It typically grows in rocky and well-drained soils, often found in alpine and subalpine environments. The plant has slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its flowering spikes are small and often clustered, producing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Festuca sumneviczii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
