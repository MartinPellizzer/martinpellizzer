Ficus nevesiae

Ficus nevesiae, commonly known as the Nevesia fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and small, clustered fruits that mature to a reddish color. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
