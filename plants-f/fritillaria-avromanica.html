Fritillaria avromanica

Fritillaria avromanica, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Fritillaria avromanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
