Festuca longiglumis

Festuca longiglumis, commonly known as the long glume fescue, is a cool-season grass native to the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces spikelets with elongated glumes, which are often hairy and contribute to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows and along stream banks.

Festuca longiglumis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat for various wildlife species.
