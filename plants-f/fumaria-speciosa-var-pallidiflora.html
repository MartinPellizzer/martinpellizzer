Fumaria speciosa var. pallidiflora

Fumaria speciosa var. pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered glory of the snow, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, pale yellow to white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has slender, branching stems and heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely commercialized.
