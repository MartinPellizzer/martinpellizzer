Fragaria chiloensis var. calyculata

Fragaria chiloensis var. calyculata, commonly known as the Chilean strawberry, is a perennial herb native to South America.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically red when ripe, with a tangy flavor. The plant has trifoliate leaves and grows in a low, spreading habit. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in coastal regions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both horticultural and ecological studies.
