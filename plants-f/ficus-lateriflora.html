Ficus lateriflora

Ficus lateriflora, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or vine native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by green, spherical fruits that ripen to a yellowish color. It thrives in humid environments and is often found growing on trees or rocks.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor landscaping.
