Ferula communis subsp. glauca

Ferula communis subsp.

glauca, commonly known as blue ferula, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, gray-green leaves that are often covered in a powdery waxy coating.

The plant produces yellow flowers in umbel-like clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its roots are fibrous and have been used historically for various medicinal purposes.

The subspecies is distinguished by its bluish-gray foliage, which gives it its common name.
