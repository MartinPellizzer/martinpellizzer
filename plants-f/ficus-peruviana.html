Ficus peruviana

Ficus peruviana, commonly known as the silver star fig, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, silver-green leaves that have a star-like pattern. The plant produces small, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus peruviana is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
