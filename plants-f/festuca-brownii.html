Festuca brownii

Festuca brownii, commonly known as Brown's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. Festuca brownii is adapted to high-altitude environments and can thrive in poor, rocky soils.

It is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion.
