Ficus weinlandii

Ficus weinlandii, commonly known as the Weinland fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, pale green figs that are typically not consumed by humans but are important for wildlife. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus weinlandii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
