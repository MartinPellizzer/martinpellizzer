Ficus xerophila

Ficus xerophila, commonly known as the desert fig, is a succulent plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It has thick, fleshy leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, berry-like fruits. Its root system is shallow but extensive, enabling it to absorb moisture from a wide area.

Ficus xerophila is well adapted to hot, sunny environments with minimal rainfall.
