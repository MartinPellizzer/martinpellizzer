Festuca spadicea proles consobrina

Festuca spadicea proles consobrina, commonly known as the silvergrass or white feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that are typically pale green to white in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a silvery-blue hue due to the presence of fine pubescence.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
