Ficus pringlei

Ficus pringlei, commonly known as the Pringle's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central America, particularly Mexico and Guatemala. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are often clustered in small, rounded groups.

Ficus pringlei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
