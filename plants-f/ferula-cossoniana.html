Ferula cossoniana

Ferula cossoniana, commonly known as the Kashmiri ferula, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow-orange flowers in umbel-like clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their aromatic properties.

Ferula cossoniana is also valued for its essential oils, which are used in perfumery and as a natural remedy.
