Ficus trachyleia var. chantiniana

Ficus trachyleia var. chantiniana, commonly known as the Chantiniana fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
