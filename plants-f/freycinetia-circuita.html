Freycinetia circuita

Freycinetia circuita, commonly known as the red sanders, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its aromatic, dark reddish-brown heartwood. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or white in color. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often used in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils.

Due to overharvesting, it is now classified as an endangered species.
