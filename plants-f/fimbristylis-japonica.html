Fimbristylis japonica

Fimbristylis japonica, commonly known as Japanese hairgrass, is a species of flowering plant in the family Cyperaceae.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, often found in coastal areas or along the edges of water bodies. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it its common name. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family.

Fimbristylis japonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moist environments.
