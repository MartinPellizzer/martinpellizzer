Fuisa rupestris

Fuisa rupestris, commonly known as the rock fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen growth habit and produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and requires full sun to partial shade. Its foliage consists of dark green, leathery leaves that are arranged in a rosette pattern.

Fuisa rupestris is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and low maintenance requirements.
