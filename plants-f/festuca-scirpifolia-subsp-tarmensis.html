Festuca scirpifolia subsp. tarmensis

Festuca scirpifolia subsp.

tarmensis, commonly known as Tarmian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers.

This subspecies typically grows in moist, often swampy habitats, such as marshes and wet meadows. Its leaves are usually narrow and slightly hairy, while the inflorescence is loosely arranged and often appears feathery.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils.
