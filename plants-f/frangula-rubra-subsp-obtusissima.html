Frangula rubra subsp. obtusissima

Frangula rubra subsp.

obtusissima, commonly known as the bluntleaf cascara, is a shrub native to the western United States. It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its smooth, reddish bark and dark green, elliptical leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to red, fleshy berries. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and coastal scrub habitats.
