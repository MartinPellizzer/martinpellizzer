Ficus insipida

Ficus insipida, commonly known as the white fig or insipid fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, including Mexico, Guatemala, and parts of the Amazon rainforest. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and its ability to produce both male and female flowers on the same inflorescence. Its fruit, which is a small, greenish fig, is edible and often consumed by local communities.

Ficus insipida is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its role in supporting local wildlife.
