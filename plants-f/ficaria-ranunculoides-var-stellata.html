Ficaria ranunculoides var. stellata

Ficaria ranunculoides var. stellata, commonly known as the star flower or star chickweed, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features small, delicate, star-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring, typically in shades of white or pale yellow. The plant has hairy, succulent leaves that are often arranged in a rosette pattern, aiding in water retention. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for rock gardens and as a ground cover in humid climates.
