Ficus megalodisca

Ficus megalodisca, commonly known as the Giant Ficus, is a large tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, inedible fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its size and growth habit, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in large outdoor spaces.
