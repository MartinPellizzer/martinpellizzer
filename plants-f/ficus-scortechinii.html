Ficus scortechinii

Ficus scortechinii, commonly known as the Bornean fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are glossy and often have a waxy texture. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are edible and attract a variety of wildlife.

Ficus scortechinii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
