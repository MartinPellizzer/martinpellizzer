Ficus conciliorum

Ficus conciliorum, commonly known as the sacred fig or bodhi tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The tree produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible figs that are encased in a spherical, green fruit. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and cultural significance.

Ficus conciliorum is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to indoor conditions.
