Fagonia persica

Fagonia persica, commonly known as Persian fagonia, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Caryophyllaceae.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, including parts of Iran and Afghanistan. The plant is characterized by its hairy, branched stems and small, pinkish or white flowers that bloom in late summer. Its leaves are typically linear and arranged alternately along the stem.

Fagonia persica is adapted to dry environments and is often found growing in rocky or sandy soils.
