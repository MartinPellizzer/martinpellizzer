Ficus hemicardia var. grandifolia

Ficus hemicardia var. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaf fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
