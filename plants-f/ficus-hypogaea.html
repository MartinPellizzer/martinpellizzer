Ficus hypogaea

Ficus hypogaea, commonly known as the peanut plant, is a leguminous species native to South America.

It is a herbaceous annual plant that produces underground pods containing edible seeds called peanuts. The plant has compound leaves with three to five leaflets and produces small, yellowish flowers. Its root system is capable of fixing atmospheric nitrogen, enriching the soil.

The botanical features of Ficus hypogaea make it an important crop in agriculture due to its nutritional value and adaptability.
