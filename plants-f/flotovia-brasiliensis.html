Flotovia brasiliensis

Flotovia brasiliensis, commonly known as the Brazilian floating fern, is a unique aquatic plant native to the Amazon Basin.

It is characterized by its delicate, floating leaves that form a dense mat on the water surface. The plant has a specialized root system that allows it to anchor itself while remaining afloat. Its leaves are typically oval-shaped with a waxy surface that helps repel water.

Flotovia brasiliensis plays an important role in providing habitat for aquatic organisms and stabilizing water quality in its native environment.
