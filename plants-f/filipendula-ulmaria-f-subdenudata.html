Filipendula ulmaria f. subdenudata

Filipendula ulmaria f. subdenudata, commonly known as the lesser cow-weed, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a serrated margin, with a distinctive yellowish or reddish underside. This subspecies typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional herbal medicine.
