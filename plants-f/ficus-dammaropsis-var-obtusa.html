Ficus dammaropsis var. obtusa

Ficus dammaropsis var. obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leafed fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

The species is also valued for its ability to tolerate partial shade and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
