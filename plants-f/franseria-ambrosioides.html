Franseria ambrosioides

Franseria ambrosioides, commonly known as Mexican feverfew, is a flowering plant native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and opposite leaf arrangement. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are aromatic and often used in traditional medicine for their antispasmodic properties.

The small yellow flowers appear in clusters and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
