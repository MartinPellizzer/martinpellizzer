Ferula assoi

Ferula assoi, commonly known as the Assam ferula, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of northern India and Nepal.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and feathery leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its ecological importance lies in its role as a habitat and food source for various pollinators and local wildlife.
