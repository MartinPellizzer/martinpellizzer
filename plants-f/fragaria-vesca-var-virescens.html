Fragaria vesca var. virescens

Fragaria vesca var. virescens, commonly known as the green-leafed strawberry, is a variant of the woodland strawberry.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and small, red berries that ripen in late summer. This plant is native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere and thrives in shaded, moist environments. It is a perennial herb with a shallow root system and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it a unique and ornamental addition to gardens and natural habitats.
