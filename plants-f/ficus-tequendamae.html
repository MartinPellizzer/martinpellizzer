Ficus tequendamae

Ficus tequendamae, commonly known as the Tequendama fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of Colombia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, clustered fruits that are edible. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor conditions.

The Tequendama fig is also valued for its ability to improve air quality and its role in traditional medicine.
