Ficus populnea var. hispaniolae

Ficus populnea var. hispaniolae, commonly known as the Hispaniola fig, is a tropical plant native to the island of Hispaniola.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
