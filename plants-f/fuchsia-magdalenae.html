Fuchsia magdalenae

Fuchsia magdalenae, commonly known as the Magdalena fuchsia, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces striking, pendulous flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in cool, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Fuchsia magdalenae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant floral display.
