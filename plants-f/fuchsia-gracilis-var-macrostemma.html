Fuchsia gracilis var. macrostemma

Fuchsia gracilis var. macrostemma, commonly known as the large-stemmed fuchsia, is a compact shrub native to South America.

It features pendulous, tubular flowers that are typically a striking combination of purple and pink, with a distinctive two-lipped structure. The plant produces showy, fragrant blooms during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly hairy texture.

This variety is prized in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in partial shade.
