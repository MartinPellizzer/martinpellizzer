Ficus anacardiifolia

Ficus anacardiifolia, commonly known as the Indian rubber tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant is a member of the Moraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in warm, humid environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible figs that are often consumed by local wildlife.

The plant's adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor cultivation in temperate regions.
