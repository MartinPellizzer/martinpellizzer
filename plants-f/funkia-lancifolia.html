Funkia lancifolia

Funkia lancifolia, commonly known as the broadleaf daylily, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It features strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow margins. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, or red, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is widely cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
