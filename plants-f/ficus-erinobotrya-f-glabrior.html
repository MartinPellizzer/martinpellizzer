Ficus erinobotrya f. glabrior

Ficus erinobotrya f. glabrior, commonly known as the smooth-leafed fig, is a variant of the Ficus erinobotrya species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces small, clustered fruits known as syconia, which are edible and often consumed by birds and mammals. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This cultivar is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
