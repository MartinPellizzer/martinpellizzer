Ficus iteophylla

Ficus iteophylla, commonly known as the Variegated Ficus or the Variegated Fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including parts of India and Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white or cream variegation. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Ficus iteophylla thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers warm, humid environments.
