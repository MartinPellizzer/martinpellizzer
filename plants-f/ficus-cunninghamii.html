Ficus cunninghamii

Ficus cunninghamii, commonly known as the White Ficus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its smooth, greyish-brown bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white powdery coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish fruits that turn red when mature. It is widely cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.

Ficus cunninghamii prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
