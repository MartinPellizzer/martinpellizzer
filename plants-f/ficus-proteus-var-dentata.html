Ficus proteus var. dentata

Ficus proteus var. dentata, commonly known as the African rubber fig, is a species native to southern Africa.

It is a flowering plant in the family Moraceae, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

The species is also valued for its rubber-like latex, which has been used in traditional applications.
