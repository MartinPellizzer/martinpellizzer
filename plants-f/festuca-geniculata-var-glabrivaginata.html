Festuca geniculata var. glabrivaginata

Festuca geniculata var. glabrivaginata, commonly known as the smooth sheath fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically smooth and glabrous. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing pale blue to greenish flowers, which are often hidden within the foliage. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in open grasslands and mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in arid environments.
