Ferula aucheri

Ferula aucheri, commonly known as the Persian cow parsley, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces a strong, aromatic scent due to the presence of volatile oils in its leaves and stems. Its fibrous roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Ferula aucheri is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
