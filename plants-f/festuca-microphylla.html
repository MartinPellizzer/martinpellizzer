Festuca microphylla

Festuca microphylla, commonly known as slender fescue, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, needle-like leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes. Its small, spike-like inflorescence contains numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Festuca microphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
