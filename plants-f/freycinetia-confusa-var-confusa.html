Freycinetia confusa var. confusa

Freycinetia confusa var. confusa, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red in color. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and is often found growing in sandy or muddy soils near the shoreline. It is a shrub that can reach heights of up to 2 meters and is known for its ornamental value.

The flowers bloom throughout the year, attracting various pollinators such as bees and birds.
