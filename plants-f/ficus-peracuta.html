Ficus peracuta

Ficus peracuta, commonly known as the sharp-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, pointed leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are often used in local cuisines. Ficus peracuta thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
