Fritillaria meleagris subsp. delphinensis

Fritillaria meleagris subsp.

delphinensis, commonly known as the Delphinensis Crowned-Fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or yellow with a distinctive purple or brown crown.

The plant has a robust, fleshy bulb that serves as its primary storage organ. Its leaves are linear and grass-like, emerging from the base of the plant.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.
