Ficus costigera

Ficus costigera, commonly known as the silver fig or Indian rubber plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often marked with silvery veins, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pale green fruits that are typically not edible for humans but are important for wildlife. It is a member of the Moraceae family and is known for its adaptability to various growing conditions.

Ficus costigera is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
