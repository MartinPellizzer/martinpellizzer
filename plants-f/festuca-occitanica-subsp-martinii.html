Festuca occitanica subsp. martinii

Festuca occitanica subsp.

martinii, commonly known as Martin's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe. It typically grows in dry, sunny grasslands and rocky soils, often in mountainous regions.

The plant has slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spikelet that bears small, spike-like flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its hardy nature and visual interest.
