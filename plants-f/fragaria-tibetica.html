Fragaria tibetica

Fragaria tibetica, commonly known as the Tibetan strawberry, is a perennial herb native to the high-altitude regions of the Tibetan Plateau.

It produces small, red, edible berries that are typically less than 1 cm in diameter. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Its flowers are white and have five petals, typical of the Rosaceae family.

The plant is adapted to harsh environmental conditions, including strong winds and low oxygen levels.
