Ficus laccifera

Ficus laccifera, commonly known as the lac tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The tree is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when mature. The latex sap of the lac tree is highly valued for producing shellac, a natural resin used in various industrial applications.

This plant is also noted for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
