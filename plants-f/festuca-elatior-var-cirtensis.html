Festuca elatior var. cirtensis

Festuca elatior var. cirtensis, commonly known as the Cirtensis fescue, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and bluish-green foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, often in sunny to partially shaded environments. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, cylindrical spike that bears small, spikelet-like flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in coastal and arid conditions.
