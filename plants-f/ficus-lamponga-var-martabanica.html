Ficus lamponga var. martabanica

Ficus lamponga var. martabanica, commonly known as the Martaban ficus, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, red berries that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.

This variety is popular as both an ornamental plant and for its edible fruits.
