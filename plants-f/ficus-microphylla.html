Ficus microphylla

Ficus microphylla, commonly known as the little leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are about the size of a cherry. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus microphylla is also valued for its ability to adapt to various light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
