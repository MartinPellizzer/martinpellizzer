Felicia angustifolia var. hyssopifolia

Felicia angustifolia var. hyssopifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant features narrow, linear leaves and produces bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
