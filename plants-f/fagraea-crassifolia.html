Fagraea crassifolia

Fagraea crassifolia, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a deep green color. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in the summer months. These flowers are followed by bright orange-red berries that contain a single seed.

Fagraea crassifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
