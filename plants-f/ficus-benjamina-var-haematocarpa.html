Ficus benjamina var. haematocarpa

Ficus benjamina var. haematocarpa, commonly known as the red-veined ficus, is a variant of the weeping fig tree characterized by its distinctive reddish veins on its dark green leaves.

This plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value, particularly in indoor and outdoor landscaping. The leaves are typically broad, glossy, and have a leathery texture.

It prefers well-drained soil and indirect sunlight, making it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardens.
