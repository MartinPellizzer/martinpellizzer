Festuca ovina subvar. pallens

Festuca ovina subvar. pallens, commonly known as the silverblue fescue, is a cool-season grass native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance. This variety is typically low-growing, forming dense, mat-like growth that is well-suited for rock gardens and alpine settings. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
