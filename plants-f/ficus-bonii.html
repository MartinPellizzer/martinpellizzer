Ficus bonii

Ficus bonii, commonly known as the Benjamin fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. It is a popular indoor plant due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Ficus bonii produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by small, round fruits that are not typically consumed by humans.
