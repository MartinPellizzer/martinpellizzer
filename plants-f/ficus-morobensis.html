Ficus morobensis

Ficus morobensis, commonly known as the Morobe fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the island of New Britain in the Pacific, specifically in the Bismarck Archipelago. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and have a sweet, fig-like flavor.

Ficus morobensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine by local communities.
