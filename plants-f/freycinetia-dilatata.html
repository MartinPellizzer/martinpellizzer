Freycinetia dilatata

Freycinetia dilatata, commonly known as the broad-leafed pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to tropical regions of the Pacific, including parts of Australia, New Guinea, and the Solomon Islands. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, pinnately compound leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as thatching material. Freycinetia dilatata is adapted to coastal and wetland environments, thriving in high humidity and saline soils.
