Fragaria vesca var. sylvestris

Fragaria vesca var. sylvestris, commonly known as the wild strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale pink. The plant bears aggregate fruits composed of multiple achenes, each surrounded by a fleshy, edible pericarp. It thrives in cool, temperate climates and is often found in woodland edges, meadows, and shaded areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in horticulture and cuisine.
