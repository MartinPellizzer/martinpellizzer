Ficus megacarpa subvar. rotunditenuifolia

Ficus megacarpa subvar. rotunditenuifolia, commonly known as the warty fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, warty, and often lobed leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, greenish fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded outdoor gardens.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental purposes.
