Ficus hirta subsp. triloba

Ficus hirta subsp.

triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed fig, is a shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It features heart-shaped leaves with three distinct lobes, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically clustered in the leaf axils. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and as a houseplant.
