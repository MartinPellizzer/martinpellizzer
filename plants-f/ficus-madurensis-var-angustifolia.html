Ficus madurensis var. angustifolia

Ficus madurensis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, dark green leaves that are elongated and pointed. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping and indoor cultivation.
