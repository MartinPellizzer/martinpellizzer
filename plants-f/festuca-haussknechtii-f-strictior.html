Festuca haussknechtii f. strictior

Festuca haussknechtii f. strictior, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fescue, is a grass species characterized by its compact growth habit and dense foliage.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia, where it thrives in well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green and have a slight bluish tint. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh environmental conditions.
