Ficus juglandiformis

Ficus juglandiformis, commonly known as the Bursera-like fig, is a tropical plant native to Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are edible and often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland tropical forests.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
