Ferula balchaschensis

Ferula balchaschensis, commonly known as the Tien Shan cow parsley, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its umbellate inflorescence and aromatic foliage. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving at high elevations. Its taproot is thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The flowers are small and white, producing nectar that attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
