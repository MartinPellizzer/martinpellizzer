Fraxinus spaethiana f. nipponica

Fraxinus spaethiana f. nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese white ash.

It is a rare and ornamental tree native to Japan, characterized by its smooth, pale bark and delicate, feathery foliage. The leaves are typically elliptical with finely serrated edges and turn a striking yellow in autumn. This cultivar is valued for its unique appearance and is often grown in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a versatile addition to landscapes.
