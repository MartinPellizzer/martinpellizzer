Flemingia fluminalis

Flemingia fluminalis, commonly known as the river flemingia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is often found growing along riverbanks and in moist, tropical environments. It has a fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Flemingia fluminalis is also valued for its potential in agroforestry and as a source of natural dyes.
