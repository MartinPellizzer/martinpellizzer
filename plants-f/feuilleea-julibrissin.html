Feuilleea julibrissin

Feuilleea julibrissin, commonly known as the Chinese honeylocust, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to China.

It features compound leaves that are pinnately arranged, with each leaflet being lance-shaped and often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by long, slender pods containing flat, edible seeds. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

However, it can become invasive in some regions if not properly managed.
