Fritillaria thomsoniana

Fritillaria thomsoniana, commonly known as the Thomson's fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy soils. It is typically found in alpine and subalpine environments, where it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts are ongoing to protect its natural habitat.
