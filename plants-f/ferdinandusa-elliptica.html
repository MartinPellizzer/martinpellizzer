Ferdinandusa elliptica

Ferdinandusa elliptica, commonly known as the red cedar or red cypress, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical trunk and dense, dark green foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by woody, cone-like fruits. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and elliptical in shape, with a leathery texture.

Ferdinandusa elliptica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticulture for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
