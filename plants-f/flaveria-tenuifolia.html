Flaveria tenuifolia

Flaveria tenuifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that consist of both ray and disk flowers. The plant typically grows as an annual herb with slender, hairy stems and narrow, linear leaves. It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Flaveria tenuifolia is often found in dry, open habitats like grasslands and disturbed soils.
