Frenela rhomboidea

Frenela rhomboidea, commonly known as the rhomboid fern, is a species of aquatic fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rhombus-shaped fronds that are typically narrow and elongated. The plant thrives in slow-moving freshwater environments, such as ponds and streams. Its leaves are usually submerged or floating, with a delicate, feathery appearance.

Frenela rhomboidea plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and oxygen to aquatic life.
