Facelis retusa subsp. patula

Facelis retusa subsp.

patula, commonly known as the silverleaf honeysuckle, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It features small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as birds and insects.

The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually covered in a fine layer of silvery hair, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The plant is characterized by its compact growth habit and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.
