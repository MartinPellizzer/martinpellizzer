Ficus osensis

Ficus osensis, commonly known as the African rubber fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and East Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are followed by fig fruits, which are enclosed in a fleshy, greenish-woody syconium. Ficus osensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and can thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor environments.

It prefers bright, indirect light and moderate humidity for optimal growth.
