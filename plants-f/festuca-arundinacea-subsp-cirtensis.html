Festuca arundinacea subsp. cirtensis

Festuca arundinacea subsp.

cirtensis, commonly known as Cirtan tall fescue, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, tufted growth habit, often forming a mat-like structure.

The plant produces spikelets that are typically 2 to 3 cm long, with lemmas that are usually hairy and pointed. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and along rocky slopes.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
