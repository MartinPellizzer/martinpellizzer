Fagelia polifolia

Fagelia polifolia, commonly known as the Polifoliate Fagelia, is a rare shrub native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, finely divided leaves that give it a feathery appearance, and it produces small, fragrant white flowers throughout the year. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained, nutrient-rich soil. It is often found growing in the understory of dense forests, where it contributes to the biodiversity of the ecosystem.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
