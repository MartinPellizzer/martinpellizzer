Ficaria ranunculoides f. parviflora

Ficaria ranunculoides f. parviflora, commonly known as the dwarf buttercup, is a low-growing perennial plant native to Europe.

It features small, bright yellow flowers that resemble those of the true buttercup, with five petals and a central cluster of stamens. The plant has smooth, succulent leaves that are typically rounded and slightly hairy. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or meadow environments.

Its compact size and delicate appearance make it a popular choice for rock gardens and shady borders.
