Ferula duranii

Ferula duranii, commonly known as the Afghan wormwood, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and feathery leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Ferula duranii is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in local perfumery.
