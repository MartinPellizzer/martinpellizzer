Fuchsia cestroides

Fuchsia cestroides, commonly known as the coral fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces striking, pendulous inflorescences of brightly colored flowers, usually in shades of red, pink, and purple. Its flowers are composed of five petals and are pollinated primarily by birds, particularly hummingbirds.

Fuchsia cestroides is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and as a container plant.
