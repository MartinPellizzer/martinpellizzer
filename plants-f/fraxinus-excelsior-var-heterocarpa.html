Fraxinus excelsior var. heterocarpa

Fraxinus excelsior var. heterocarpa, commonly known as the winged elm, is a variant of the European ash tree characterized by its distinctive winged samaras.

It is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa, typically growing in moist, well-drained soils. The leaves are opposite, simple, and oval-shaped with a serrated margin. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in early spring, followed by the development of its unique winged fruits.

This variety is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive form and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
