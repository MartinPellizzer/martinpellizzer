Fugosia lilacinus

Fugosia lilacinus, commonly known as the lilac-flowered fugosia, is a rare and striking plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lilac-colored flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant has small, narrow leaves that are arranged alternately along its stem. Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a compact form and a preference for well-drained, acidic soils.

Fugosia lilacinus is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique floral display.
