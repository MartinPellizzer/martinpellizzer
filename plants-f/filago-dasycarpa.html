Filago dasycarpa

Filago dasycarpa, commonly known as the rough-seeded filago, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in a flat-topped inflorescence. Its leaves are narrow, hairy, and often have a rough texture, contributing to its common name.

Filago dasycarpa is typically found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments.
