Flacourtia montana

Flacourtia montana, commonly known as the mountain flacourtia, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to Southeast Asia.

It typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in coastal or lowland areas. The plant features simple, elliptical leaves that are usually glossy and dark green in color. Its small, white or pale yellow flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, fleshy berries that are edible.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
