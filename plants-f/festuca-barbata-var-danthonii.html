Festuca barbata var. danthonii

Festuca barbata var. danthonii, commonly known as Danthonia barbata, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a bluish-green color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typically blooming in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and along roadsides.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
