Fragaria chiloensis subsp. chiloensis

Fragaria chiloensis subsp.

chiloensis, commonly known as the Chilean strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America. It produces small, red, edible fruits that are typically smaller than those of the common strawberry.

The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and grows in coastal and temperate regions. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to coastal climates with mild temperatures.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for horticultural and ecological studies.
