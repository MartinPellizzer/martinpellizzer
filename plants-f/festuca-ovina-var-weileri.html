Festuca ovina var. weileri

Festuca ovina var. weileri, commonly known as Weiler's sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green blades and dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It has small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous tiny flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
