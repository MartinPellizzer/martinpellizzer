Festuca marocana

Festuca marocana, commonly known as Moroccan fescue, is a perennial grass native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or arid environments, showing adaptability to harsh conditions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Festuca marocana is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
