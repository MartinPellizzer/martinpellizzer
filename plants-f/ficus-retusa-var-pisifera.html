Ficus retusa var. pisifera

Ficus retusa var. pisifera, commonly known as the fig-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor ornamental gardening.
