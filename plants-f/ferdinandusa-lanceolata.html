Ferdinandusa lanceolata

Ferdinandusa lanceolata, commonly known as the Brazilian cherry or *Ferdinandusa lanceolata*, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark brown bark and lance-shaped, glossy green leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The tree is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its role in local ecosystems.
