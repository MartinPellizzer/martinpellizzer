Ferrocalamus

Ferrocalamus, commonly known as the iron reed, is a unique plant belonging to the grass family (Poaceae).

It is characterized by its tall, slender stalks that are often hollow and filled with a spongy, fibrous material. The plant is native to high-altitude regions of the Himalayas and is adapted to cold, arid environments. Ferrocalamus has fibrous roots that help it anchor in rocky soils and conserve moisture.

Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, contributing to its overall appearance as a hardy, resilient plant.
