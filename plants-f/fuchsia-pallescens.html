Fuchsia pallescens

Fuchsia pallescens, commonly known as the silver fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green foliage and showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, especially hummingbirds, due to their nectar-rich structure.

Fuchsia pallescens is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various garden conditions.
