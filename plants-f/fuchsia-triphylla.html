Fuchsia triphylla

Fuchsia triphylla, commonly known as the three-leaved fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows as an upright shrub with woody stems and dense foliage. Its leaves are trifoliate, meaning they are divided into three leaflets, giving it its common name.

Fuchsia triphylla thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
