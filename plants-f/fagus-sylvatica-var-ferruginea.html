Fagus sylvatica var. ferruginea

Fagus sylvatica var. ferruginea, commonly known as the rusty beech, is a variety of the common beech native to Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bracts that appear on the stems, giving it its common name. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a doubly serrated margin and a glossy green upper surface. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental features and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
