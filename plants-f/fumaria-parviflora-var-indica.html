Fumaria parviflora var. indica

Fumaria parviflora var. indica, commonly known as the Indian cress, is a flowering plant native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is a low-growing annual herb with slender, branched stems and small, yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces small, spherical seed pods that burst open to disperse seeds when mature. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in agricultural fields and along roadsides.

Its leaves are edible and have been used in traditional cuisines and herbal remedies.
