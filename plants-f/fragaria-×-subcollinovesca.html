Fragaria × subcollinovesca

Fragaria × subcollinovesca, commonly known as the red-berried strawberry, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Fragaria × ananassa and Fragaria × vesca.

It is characterized by its small, red, and often hairy fruits that develop in clusters. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers with five petals. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental and edible qualities.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both gardens and agricultural settings.
