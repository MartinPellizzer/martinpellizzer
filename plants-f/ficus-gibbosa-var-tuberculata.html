Ficus gibbosa var. tuberculata

Ficus gibbosa var. tuberculata, commonly known as the tuberculate fig, is a tropical plant characterized by its unique leaf morphology.

The leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a textured surface with prominent tubercles or bumps. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid environments. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when mature.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
