Filago gallica subsp. tenuifolia

Filago gallica subsp.

tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved filago, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It typically grows in grassy areas and along woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant has slender, branching stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a slightly hairy surface, and it produces small, yellowish flowers in late spring.

This subspecies is notable for its delicate appearance and its role in supporting local insect populations.
