Freycinetia beccarii

Freycinetia beccarii, commonly known as the red-veined cycad, is a member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is a slow-growing, evergreen plant characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The leaves are dark green with prominent red veins, giving the plant its distinctive appearance. Native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and conservation status, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
