Fagraea truncata

Fagraea truncata, commonly known as the silverleaf pomegranate, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery wax, giving it its common name. The plant produces clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom throughout the year in warm climates. Its fruit, which resembles a pomegranate, is edible and has a sweet, tart flavor.

Fagraea truncata is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and ornate floral displays.
