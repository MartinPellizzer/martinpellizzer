Fuchsia arborescens var. syringiflora

Fuchsia arborescens var. syringiflora, commonly known as the Chilean bellflower, is a woody shrub native to South America.

It features showy, tubular flowers that range in color from pink to purple, often with a distinctive red throat. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are hairy and have a slightly serrated edge. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and greenhouses.
