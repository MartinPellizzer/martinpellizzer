Ficus neriifolia var. nemoralis

Ficus neriifolia var. nemoralis, commonly known as the European fig, is a variant of the common fig tree.

It is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called syconia. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped with a smooth texture and a dark green upper surface.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
