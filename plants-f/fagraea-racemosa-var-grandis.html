Fagraea racemosa var. grandis

Fagraea racemosa var. grandis, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves and clusters of fragrant, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in the rainy season. The plant produces bright red, fleshy berries that are edible for humans and animals. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The tree is also valued in traditional medicine for its various therapeutic properties.
