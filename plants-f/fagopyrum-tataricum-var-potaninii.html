Fagopyrum tataricum var. potaninii

Fagopyrum tataricum var. potaninii, commonly known as the Tibetan buckwheat, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by three-lobed, triangular seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

This variety is notable for its hardiness and is sometimes cultivated for its edible seeds and ornamental value.
