Fassettia echinata

Fassettia echinata, commonly known as the spiked fassettia, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive spiny stems and clusters of small, showy flowers. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to arid conditions. Its leaves are usually narrow and covered in a fine layer of hairs, which helps reduce water loss.

Fassettia echinata is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
