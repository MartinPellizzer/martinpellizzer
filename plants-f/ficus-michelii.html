Ficus michelii

Ficus michelii, commonly known as the Mexican fig tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible figs that are typically consumed fresh or used in culinary applications. Ficus michelii thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Its ability to adapt to various soil types makes it a versatile plant for both indoor and outdoor environments.
