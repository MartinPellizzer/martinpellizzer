Froelichia tomentosa var. angustialata

Froelichia tomentosa var. angustialata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf froelichia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of white, woolly hair. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ecologically important species in its native habitat.
