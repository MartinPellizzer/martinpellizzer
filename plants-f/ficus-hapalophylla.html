Ficus hapalophylla

Ficus hapalophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its small, elongated leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits.

Ficus hapalophylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
