Fagus sylvatica pendula-purpurea

Fagus sylvatica pendula-purpurea, commonly known as the weeping purple beech, is a striking variant of the European beech.

It features elongated, drooping branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a unique and ornamental appearance. The leaves are typically a vibrant purple or bronze color, especially in the spring, before maturing to a dark green. This cultivar is valued for its aesthetic appeal in gardens and landscapes.

It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental planting.
