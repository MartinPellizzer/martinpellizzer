Foeniculum vulgare var. capillaceum

Foeniculum vulgare var. capillaceum, commonly known as the curly parsley or Italian parsley, is a biennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features finely divided, feathery leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, which are typical of the Apiaceae family. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soil with full sun exposure.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, including its use in digestive remedies and as a source of volatile oils.
