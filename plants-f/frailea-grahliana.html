Frailea grahliana

Frailea grahliana, commonly known as the Grahliana cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the Canary Islands.

It is a low-growing, sprawling plant with cylindrical stems that are typically green and covered in white or pale blue spines. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pink, often blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and arid landscapes.
