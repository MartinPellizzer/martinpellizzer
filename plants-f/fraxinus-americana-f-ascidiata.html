Fraxinus americana f. ascidiata

Fraxinus americana f. ascidiata, commonly known as the warty box elder, is a variant of the American ash tree characterized by its unique bark texture.

This cultivar exhibits a distinctive, rugged bark with prominent ridges and warty projections, giving it a striking appearance. The leaves are typically opposite, simple, and ovate, with a serrated margin. It is a deciduous tree that thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to adapt to various environmental conditions.
