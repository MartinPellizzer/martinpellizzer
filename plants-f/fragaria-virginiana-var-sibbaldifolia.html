Fragaria virginiana var. sibbaldifolia

Fragaria virginiana var. sibbaldifolia, commonly known as the Sibbald's strawberry, is a wild strawberry species native to North America.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant with trifoliate leaves and produces small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and forest edges. Its flowers are white with five petals, and the fruit is red, juicy, and has a sweet-tart flavor.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
