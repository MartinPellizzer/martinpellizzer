Fagopyrum macropterum

Fagopyrum macropterum, commonly known as the large-winged buckwheat, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and distinctive, broad, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, followed by triangular, three-lobed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or open meadows.

Fagopyrum macropterum is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
