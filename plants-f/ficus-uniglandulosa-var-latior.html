Ficus uniglandulosa var. latior

Ficus uniglandulosa var. latior, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a tropical plant characterized by its broad, glossy leaves.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is native to Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, with a short trunk and dense foliage. Its leaves are ovate to elliptical in shape, often with a waxy texture and prominent veins.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm climates for its aesthetic foliage.
