Ficus variegata var. sycomoroides

Ficus variegata var. sycomoroides, commonly known as the variegated sycamore fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that often display a striking variegation of green, white, and sometimes yellow. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fruits that are typically pear-shaped. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Its growth habit includes both a tree form and a shrub, making it adaptable to various garden settings.
