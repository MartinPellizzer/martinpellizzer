Ficus banyana

Ficus banyana, commonly known as the Banyan fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive aerial root system that forms a dense canopy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are an important food source for wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a notable species in botanical studies.
