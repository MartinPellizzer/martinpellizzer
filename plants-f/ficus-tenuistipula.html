Ficus tenuistipula

Ficus tenuistipula, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its small, clustered leaves that are typically narrow and pointed. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible figs.

Ficus tenuistipula is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
