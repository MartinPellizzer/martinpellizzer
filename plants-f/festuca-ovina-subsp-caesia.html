Festuca ovina subsp. caesia

Festuca ovina subsp.

caesia, commonly known as blue mountain fescue, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Its compact size and ornamental value make it a popular choice for rock gardens and landscape design.

It is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil in harsh, elevated habitats.
