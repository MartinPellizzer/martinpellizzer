Fritillaria maximowiczii

Fritillaria maximowiczii, commonly known as the Korean snuffbox, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short stem that supports its showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

The bulbs are toxic and should be handled with care.
