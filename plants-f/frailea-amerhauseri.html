Frailea amerhauseri

Frailea amerhauseri, commonly known as the American fairy cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It features a compact, globular shape with a short, thick stem that can develop a bluish-gray hue over time. The plant produces small, white flowers during the warmer months, typically in spring or early summer. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or container cultivation.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
