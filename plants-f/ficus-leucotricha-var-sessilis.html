Ficus leucotricha var. sessilis

Ficus leucotricha var. sessilis, commonly known as the sessile-leaf fig, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy white coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits known as figs. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded outdoor gardens.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
