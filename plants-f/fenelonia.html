Fenelonia

Fenelonia, commonly known as sweet cicely, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It features slender, hollow stems and delicate, fern-like leaves that are aromatic and often used in culinary applications. The plant produces small, white, umbel-shaped flowers that bloom in early summer. Its roots are valued for their licorice-like flavor and have been used in traditional medicine.

Fenelonia thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.
