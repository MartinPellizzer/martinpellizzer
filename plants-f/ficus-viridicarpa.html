Ficus viridicarpa

Ficus viridicarpa, commonly known as the green fruit fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fruits. The fruits are typically small, green, and have a mild, sweet flavor.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food in its native habitat.
