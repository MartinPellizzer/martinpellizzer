Ficinia decidua

Ficinia decidua, commonly known as the deciduous fig tree, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate. It produces small, clustered fig-like fruits that are edible and attract various wildlife.

The tree is known for its ability to shed its leaves seasonally, which is a key feature of its deciduous nature.
