Ficus dielsii

Ficus dielsii, commonly known as the Diels' fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and can grow as a shrub or small tree. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically consumed by birds and mammals.

Ficus dielsii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
