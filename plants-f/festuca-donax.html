Festuca donax

Festuca donax, commonly known as giant bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and blue-green foliage, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Festuca donax is often found in moist, well-drained soils along stream banks and in meadows.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been introduced to various regions for landscaping and erosion control.
