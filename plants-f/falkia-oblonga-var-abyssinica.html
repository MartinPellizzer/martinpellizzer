Falkia oblonga var. abyssinica

Falkia oblonga var. abyssinica, commonly known as the Abyssinian falkia, is a flowering plant native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. It is often found in alpine meadows and is adapted to high-altitude environments.

Due to its unique habitat and limited distribution, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
