Frasera venosa

Frasera venosa, commonly known as the pinkroot, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking pink to purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has a distinctive reddish-brown root system, which is often used in traditional herbal medicine. Frasera venosa thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or streamside habitats.

Its foliage consists of opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.
