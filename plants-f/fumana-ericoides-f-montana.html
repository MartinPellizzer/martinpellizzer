Fumana ericoides f. montana

Fumana ericoides f. montana, commonly known as the mountain heath, is a variant of the ericoides form of Fumana ericoides.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, leathery leaves. The plant produces clusters of pale pink to white flowers in late spring to early summer. It thrives in acidic, well-drained soils and is typically found in rocky or mountainous habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
