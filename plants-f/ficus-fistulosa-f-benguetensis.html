Ficus fistulosa f. benguetensis

Ficus fistulosa f. benguetensis, commonly known as the Benguet fig, is a variant of the common fig tree native to the Philippines.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its smooth, light gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often lobed. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically brown when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local culture and medicine.
