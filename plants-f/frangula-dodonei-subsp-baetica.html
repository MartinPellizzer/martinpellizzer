Frangula dodonei subsp. baetica

Frangula dodonei subsp.

baetica, commonly known as the Baetica buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula. It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that appear in spring.

The plant produces small, reddish-brown berries that are an important food source for birds and other wildlife. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in Mediterranean maquis vegetation.

The subspecies is notable for its ornamental value and ecological significance in its native habitat.
