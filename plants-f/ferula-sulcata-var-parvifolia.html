Ferula sulcata var. parvifolia

Ferula sulcata var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ferula, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of Central Asia.

It features a tall, upright stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine white bloom. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the Apiaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to hot, dry climates.

Its aromatic foliage and showy blooms make it a notable species in its native habitat.
