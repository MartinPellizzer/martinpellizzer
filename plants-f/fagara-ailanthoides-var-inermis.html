Fagara ailanthoides var. inermis

Fagara ailanthoides var. inermis, commonly known as the smooth-leafed fagara, is a species native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is a member of the Rutaceae family, characterized by its aromatic leaves and flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually simple, alternate, and have a smooth margin, giving it its varietal name.

The flowers are small, clustered in inflorescences, and often have a fragrant scent, attracting various pollinators.
