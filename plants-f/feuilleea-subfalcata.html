Feuilleea subfalcata

Feuilleea subfalcata, commonly known as the narrow-leafed featherplant, is a unique species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its long, slender, and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax, which helps reduce water loss. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. Its root system is extensive and adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in dry conditions.

Feuilleea subfalcata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory properties.
