Festuca kashmiriana var. ligulata

Festuca kashmiriana var. ligulata, commonly known as Kashmir grass, is a perennial grass species native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in cold, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
