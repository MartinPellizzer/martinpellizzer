Ficus virginea

Ficus virginea, commonly known as the silver ficus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a silvery sheen on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by greenish-white fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus virginea is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and attractive foliage.
