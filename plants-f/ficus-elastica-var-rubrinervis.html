Ficus elastica var. rubrinervis

Ficus elastica var. rubrinervis, commonly known as the Red-veined Rubber Plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves with striking red veins that contrast vividly with the foliage. The plant is a member of the Moraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. It has a thick, woody stem that can grow quite tall, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.

Its slow growth rate and minimal care requirements make it an ideal plant for both novice and experienced plant enthusiasts.
