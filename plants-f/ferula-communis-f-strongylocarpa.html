Ferula communis f. strongylocarpa

Ferula communis f. strongylocarpa, commonly known as the strongly lobed fennel, is a variant of the common ferula characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves.

This plant is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in dry, rocky soils. It produces tall, branched stems that bear yellow flowers in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. The plant is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.

Its unique morphology makes it a notable species within the Ferula genus.
