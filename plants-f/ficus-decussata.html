Ficus decussata

Ficus decussata, commonly known as the variegated ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white or yellow variegation patterns. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.

Ficus decussata thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate watering to maintain its health and vibrant appearance.
