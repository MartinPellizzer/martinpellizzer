Ficus enormis

Ficus enormis, commonly known as the giant ficus or the tree of heaven, is a member of the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, broad leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant is evergreen, with a dense canopy that provides shade and supports a variety of wildlife. It produces small, fig-like fruits that are an important food source for birds and mammals.

Due to its rapid growth and adaptability, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in tropical and subtropical regions.
