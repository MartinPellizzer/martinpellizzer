Ficinia paradoxa var. argyropus

Ficinia paradoxa var. argyropus, commonly known as the silverleaf ficinia, is a rare and unique variant of the ficinia genus.

It is characterized by its striking silver or grayish-green foliage, which is covered in a dense layer of fine, reflective hairs. This plant is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and can grow up to 15 centimeters in length.

Due to its rarity and ornamental appeal, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts who appreciate its distinctive appearance.
