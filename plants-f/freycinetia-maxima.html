Freycinetia maxima

Freycinetia maxima, commonly known as the giant pandanus, is a large, flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, fibrous trunk and broad, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into edible, fibrous fruits. It thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often used for thatching and basket weaving.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various native species.
