Festuca amethystina var. asperrima

Festuca amethystina var. asperrima, commonly known as the asperrima fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and silver-blue foliage that reflects its name, "amethystina.". The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough, textured surface, which contributes to its hardy nature. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

This variety is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate harsh conditions.
