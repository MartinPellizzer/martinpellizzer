Francoeuria laciniata

Francoeuria laciniata, commonly known as the laceleaf orchid, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a member of the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, intricately lobed leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in shades of white and pale yellow, typically blooming in late spring. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

Its unique foliage and ornamental flowers make it a popular choice for botanical gardens and specialty collections.
