Fissistigma cylindricum

Fissistigma cylindricum, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Asia.

It typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes and along stream banks. The plant has slender, cylindrical stems that are often clustered together, with narrow, grass-like leaves. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces small, dark brown seeds.

Fissistigma cylindricum is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in wetland habitats.
