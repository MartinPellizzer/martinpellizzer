Festuca × holmbergii

Festuca × holmbergii, commonly known as Holmberg's fescue, is a hybrid grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or rocky habitats.

Festuca × holmbergii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy and drought-resistant nature.
