Fumaria ochroleuca

Fumaria ochroleuca, commonly known as the yellow gloryflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodland floors and along stream banks. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Fumaria ochroleuca is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its attractive, dainty blooms.
