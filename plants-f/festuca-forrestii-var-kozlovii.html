Festuca forrestii var. kozlovii

Festuca forrestii var. kozlovii, commonly known as the Kozlov's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, dark green foliage that remains dense throughout the growing season. The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences that produce numerous slender, awned florets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience in harsh climates.
