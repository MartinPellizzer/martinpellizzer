Festuca veneris

Festuca veneris, commonly known as sweet grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has slender, upright stems that bear narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive silver-green color. Its flowering spikes are usually pale blue to purple, and it blooms from late spring to early summer.

Festuca veneris is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal practices.
