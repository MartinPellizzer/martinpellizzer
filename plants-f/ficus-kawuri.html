Ficus kawuri

Ficus kawuri, commonly known as the Japanese ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Ryukyu Islands of Japan and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by small, round fruits that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when mature. Ficus kawuri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in humid environments and prefers indirect sunlight, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
