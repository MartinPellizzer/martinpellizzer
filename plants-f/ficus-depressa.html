Ficus depressa

Ficus depressa, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and small, glossy, oval-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits that are typically green when mature. Ficus depressa is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal make it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
