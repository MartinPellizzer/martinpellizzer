Festuca valesiaca f. angulosa

Festuca valesiaca f. angulosa, commonly known as the angular Festuca, is a grass species native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its distinctive angular leaf sheaths, which give it its common name. This plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Festuca valesiaca f. angulosa is often found in mountain meadows and is adapted to cold, harsh environments.
