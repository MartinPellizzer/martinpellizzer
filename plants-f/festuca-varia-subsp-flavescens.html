Festuca varia subsp. flavescens

Festuca varia subsp.

flavescens, commonly known as the yellow hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and produces delicate, spike-like inflorescences that range in color from green to yellowish.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its leaves are often bluish-green and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental value.

It is frequently used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to form dense, low-growing mats.
