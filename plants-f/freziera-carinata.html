Freziera carinata

Freziera carinata, commonly known as the mountain holly, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in high-altitude environments, often in rocky or alpine conditions. The plant produces small, showy flowers that are usually white or pale pink, arranged in clusters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Freziera carinata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens in cooler climates.
