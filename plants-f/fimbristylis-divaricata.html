Fimbristylis divaricata

Fimbristylis divaricata, commonly known as the variable sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a dense cluster of spikelets at the top of its stems. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in spikelets that are often clustered together.

Fimbristylis divaricata is native to various parts of Africa, Asia, and Australia, and it plays a role in stabilizing wetland soils.
