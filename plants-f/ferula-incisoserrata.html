Ferula incisoserrata

Ferula incisoserrata, commonly known as the Persian giant fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbel-like clusters, which are followed by inflated, bracteate fruits. Its taproot is thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine.

Ferula incisoserrata is valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental appeal.
