Ficus guyeri var. validicaudata

Ficus guyeri var. validicaudata, commonly known as the silver satinwood, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with a distinctive silver or whitish underside. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, round fruits.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
