Flickingeria bicarinata

Flickingeria bicarinata, commonly known as the two-edged fern, is a species of fern native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, bipinnate fronds that are divided into two distinct rows of pinnae. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream sides. Its reproductive structures include sori that are clustered on the underside of the fronds.

Flickingeria bicarinata is valued for its ornamental appearance and is often cultivated in botanical gardens.
