Ficus jimiensis

Ficus jimiensis, commonly known as the Jimi Ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically broad and heart-shaped. It produces small, fig-like fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Ficus jimiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
