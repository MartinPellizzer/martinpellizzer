Ficus xavieri

Ficus xavieri, commonly known as the Xavier fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. It produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by edible fig-like fruits.

Ficus xavieri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
