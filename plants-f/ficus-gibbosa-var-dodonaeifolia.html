Ficus gibbosa var. dodonaeifolia

Ficus gibbosa var. dodonaeifolia, commonly known as the Philippine fig or Dodonaea fig, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a small evergreen shrub characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are often lobed or serrated. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that turn from green to yellow or red as they mature. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.
