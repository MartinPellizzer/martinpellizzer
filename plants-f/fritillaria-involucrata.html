Fritillaria involucrata

Fritillaria involucrata, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a tall stem that bears clusters of showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Fritillaria involucrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
