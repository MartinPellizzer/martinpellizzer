Fritillaria ebeiensis

Fritillaria ebeiensis, commonly known as the Ebei fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, sturdy stem. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in dry, rocky environments, and it blooms during the spring season.

Fritillaria ebeiensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
