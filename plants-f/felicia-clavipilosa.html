Felicia clavipilosa

Felicia clavipilosa, commonly known as the yellow daisy, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets surrounding a central cluster of disk florets. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas. Its small, daisy-like flowers bloom in spring and summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Felicia clavipilosa is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
