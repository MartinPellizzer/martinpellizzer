Fragaria vesca f. landonii

Fragaria vesca f. landonii, commonly known as the Landon's wild strawberry, is a botanical variant of the woodland strawberry.

It is characterized by its smaller, more delicate leaves and fruits compared to the typical Fragaria vesca. The plant produces small, tart berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in shaded, moist environments and is native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

This form is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical significance in horticulture.
