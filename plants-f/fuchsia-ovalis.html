Fuchsia ovalis

Fuchsia ovalis, commonly known as the oval fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that bear showy, tubular flowers in shades of red, purple, and pink. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, with lance-shaped, dark green leaves. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bees, making it an important species in tropical ecosystems.

Fuchsia ovalis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
