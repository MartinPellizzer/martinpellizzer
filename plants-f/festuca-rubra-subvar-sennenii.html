Festuca rubra subvar. sennenii

Festuca rubra subvar. sennenii, commonly known as the red fescue, is a cool-season grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and compact growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental and turfgrass applications. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to reddish flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, thriving in temperate climates.

Its hardy nature and ability to form dense mats make it a valued plant in both natural and cultivated landscapes.
