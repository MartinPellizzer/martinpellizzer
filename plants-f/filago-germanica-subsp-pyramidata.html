Filago germanica subsp. pyramidata

Filago germanica subsp.

pyramidata, commonly known as the pyramid bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe. It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers, typically in shades of white or pale blue.

The leaves are lanceolate and slightly hairy, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in meadows and dry grasslands.

The plant is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate floral display.
