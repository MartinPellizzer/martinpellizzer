Faba bengalensis

Faba bengalensis, commonly known as the Bengal horsegram, is an annual leguminous plant native to South and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and erect, branching stems. The plant produces small, yellowish-green pods that contain several oval, edible seeds. It is well adapted to tropical and subtropical climates and is often grown as a forage crop or for its protein-rich seeds.

Faba bengalensis has a deep root system that helps improve soil fertility through nitrogen fixation.
