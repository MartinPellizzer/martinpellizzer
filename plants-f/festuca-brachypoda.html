Festuca brachypoda

Festuca brachypoda, commonly known as slender fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry stems that often form dense clumps. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous short, awned florets. Its leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green color.

Festuca brachypoda is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and disturbed sites.
