Francoa sonchifolia

Francoa sonchifolia, commonly known as the yellow wood sorrel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the United States.

It features upright stems with alternate, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish flowers with five petals, resembling those of a dandelion. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soils.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for native plant gardens and ecological restoration projects.
