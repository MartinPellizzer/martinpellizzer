Festuca rubra subvar. densiuscula

Festuca rubra subvar. densiuscula, commonly known as red fescue, is a cool-season grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and dark green foliage. The plant typically forms a mat-like structure, making it suitable for ground cover and erosion control. It prefers well-drained soils and can tolerate a range of environmental conditions, including shade and drought.

Its fine texture and durability make it a popular choice in lawn and turfgrass applications.
