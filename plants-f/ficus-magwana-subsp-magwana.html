Ficus magwana subsp. magwana

Ficus magwana subsp.

magwana, commonly known as the Indian rubber fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent. It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that turn yellow when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The plant is also valued for its latex, which has been used in various industrial applications.
