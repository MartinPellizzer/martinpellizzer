Ficus aurea var. latifolia

Ficus aurea var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slight variegation. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus aurea var. latifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
