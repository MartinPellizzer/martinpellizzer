Festuca pseudoduriuscula

Festuca pseudoduriuscula, commonly known as the narrowleaf fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and tufted growth habit, typically forming dense, low-growing clumps. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments. Festuca pseudoduriuscula is adapted to cold climates and can tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of high-altitude ecosystems.
