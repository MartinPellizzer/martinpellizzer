Ficus fallax

Ficus fallax, commonly known as the fallax fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are usually ovate or elliptical in shape. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature.

Ficus fallax is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
