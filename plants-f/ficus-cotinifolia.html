Ficus cotinifolia

Ficus cotinifolia, commonly known as the golden fig or Indian rubber plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features broad, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a slight waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by round, greenish-white fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in indoor environments.

Ficus cotinifolia prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.
