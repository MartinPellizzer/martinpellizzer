Fragaria vesca var. pratensis

Fragaria vesca var. pratensis, commonly known as the woodland strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white with pinkish hues, which bloom in early spring. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets and develops red, juicy berries that ripen in late summer. It thrives in partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a common sight in forest understories and gardens.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, sweet flavor in its berries.
