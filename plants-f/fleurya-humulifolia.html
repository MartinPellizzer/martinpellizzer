Fleurya humulifolia

Fleurya humulifolia, commonly known as the yellow stinkwood, is a tropical tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and dark green, glossy leaves that are elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by small, round fruits containing a single seed. It is often found in lowland rainforests and thrives in humid, shaded environments.

The species is valued for its timber and has some traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
