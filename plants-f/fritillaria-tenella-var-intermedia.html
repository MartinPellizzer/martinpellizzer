Fritillaria tenella var. intermedia

Fritillaria tenella var. intermedia, commonly known as the Crown Imperial, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, tall flower stalks that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of orange, red, and yellow. The plant has a distinctive, swollen base that resembles a crown, giving it its common name. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, emerging from the base of the plant.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for garden cultivation.
