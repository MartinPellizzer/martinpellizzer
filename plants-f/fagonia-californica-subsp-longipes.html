Fagonia californica subsp. longipes

Fagonia californica subsp.

longipes, commonly known as the longpod fagonia, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States. It is characterized by its woody stems and compound leaves with small, elliptical leaflets.

The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in terminal clusters, which are followed by elongated, cylindrical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal scrub and chaparral habitats.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in arid and semi-arid regions.
