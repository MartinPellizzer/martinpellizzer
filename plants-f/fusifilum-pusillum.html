Fusifilum pusillum

Fusifilum pusillum, commonly known as the slender spore plant, is a small, terrestrial fern-like plant found in moist, shaded environments.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is characterized by its delicate, elongated fronds that resemble filaments. The plant produces spores in clusters rather than through true flowers, which is a key feature of its reproductive strategy. Its root system is fibrous and helps it anchor in soil while absorbing moisture efficiently.

Fusifilum pusillum is often used in ecological studies due to its sensitivity to environmental changes.
