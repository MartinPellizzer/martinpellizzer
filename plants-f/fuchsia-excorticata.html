Fuchsia excorticata

Fuchsia excorticata, commonly known as the coral tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous clusters of fuchsia-colored flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its bark is thin and peeling, giving it its scientific name "excoricata.". It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant floral display.

The coral tree is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as birds and insects.
