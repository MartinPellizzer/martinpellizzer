Ficus aguaraguensis

Ficus aguaraguensis, commonly known as the Andean fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Andean regions of South America, including parts of Peru, Ecuador, and Bolivia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, edible figs that are typically consumed fresh or used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

The species is valued not only for its ecological role but also for its cultural significance among indigenous communities.
