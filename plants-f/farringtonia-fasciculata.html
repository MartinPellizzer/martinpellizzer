Farringtonia fasciculata

Farringtonia fasciculata, commonly known as the Florida ash, is a small to medium-sized tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and compound leaves that are alternate and pinnately arranged. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, red fruits that split open to release seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streams.

Botanically, it is valued for its adaptability and ecological importance in its native habitat.
