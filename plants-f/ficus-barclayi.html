Ficus barclayi

Ficus barclayi, commonly known as the Barclay's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into edible figs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Ficus barclayi is also valued for its ability to adapt to indoor environments, making it a popular choice for houseplants.
