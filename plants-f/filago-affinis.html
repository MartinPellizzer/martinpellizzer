Filago affinis

Filago affinis, commonly known as the common filago, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in composite heads, which are characteristic of the daisy family. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Filago affinis is typically herbaceous, growing to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters.
