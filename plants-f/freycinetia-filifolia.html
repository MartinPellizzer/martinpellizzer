Freycinetia filifolia

Freycinetia filifolia, commonly known as the threadleaf pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, linear leaves that grow in a dense rosette. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fibrous, feather-like fruits. Its leaves are often used in traditional crafts and as a source of fiber.

Freycinetia filifolia thrives in coastal and wetland environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils.
