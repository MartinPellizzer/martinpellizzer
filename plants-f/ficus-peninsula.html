Ficus peninsula

Ficus peninsula, commonly known as the peninsula fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and robust growth habit. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits. Its roots are fibrous and can spread widely, making it adaptable to various soil types.

The ficus peninsula is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
