Fernandezia mandonii

Fernandezia mandonii, commonly known as the Mandon's fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are typically light green and covered in a dense layer of fine, silky hairs. The plant prefers cool, humid environments and is often found growing in rocky crevices or shaded forest understories. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Fernandezia mandonii is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
