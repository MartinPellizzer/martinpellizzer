Ficus clarkei

Ficus clarkei, commonly known as the Clarke's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus clarkei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
