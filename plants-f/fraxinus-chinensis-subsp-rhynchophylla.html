Fraxinus chinensis subsp. rhynchophylla

Fraxinus chinensis subsp.

rhynchophylla, commonly known as the Chinese ash, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China. It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
