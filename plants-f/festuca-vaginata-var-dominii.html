Festuca vaginata var. dominii

Festuca vaginata var. dominii, commonly known as the dwarf blue grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Arctic and alpine regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit, with slender, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear blue to purple flowers, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or gravelly soils.

Festuca vaginata var. dominii is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, making it a hardy and resilient species.
