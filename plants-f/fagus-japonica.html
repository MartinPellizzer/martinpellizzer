Fagus japonica

Fagus japonica, commonly known as the Japanese beech, is a deciduous tree native to Japan and parts of Korea.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and chestnuts, and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, spiky nuts enclosed in a cupule, which are an important food source for various wildlife.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions where it forms dense, shade-tolerant forests.
