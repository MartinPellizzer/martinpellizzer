Ficus cordata var. tristis

Ficus cordata var. tristis, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is characterized by its elongated, drooping leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated as an ornamental tree or shrub. It has a fibrous root system and can grow up to 10 meters tall in its natural habitat.

The leaves are typically glossy and have a heart-shaped base, contributing to its ornamental value.
