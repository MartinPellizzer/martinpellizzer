Ficus forstenii

Ficus forstenii, commonly known as the Forsten's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped or oval in form. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus forstenii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
