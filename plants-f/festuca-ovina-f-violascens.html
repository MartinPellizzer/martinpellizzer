Festuca ovina f. violascens

Festuca ovina f. violascens, commonly known as violet sheep's fescue, is a cool-season grass that exhibits a distinctive violet hue in its foliage.

It is a low-growing perennial plant with narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. This variety is often found in moist, well-drained soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping for its fine texture and color.
