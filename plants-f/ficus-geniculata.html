Ficus geniculata

Ficus geniculata, commonly known as the climbing fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus geniculata is often used in ornamental horticulture for its attractive foliage and ability to climb structures.
