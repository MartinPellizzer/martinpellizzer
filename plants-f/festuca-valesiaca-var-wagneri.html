Festuca valesiaca var. wagneri

Festuca valesiaca var. wagneri, commonly known as Wagner's alpine fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, narrow leaves, and dense, compact inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high altitudes, where it forms part of the alpine vegetation. Its adaptability to cold and harsh environments makes it an important component of mountain ecosystems.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in alpine habitats.
