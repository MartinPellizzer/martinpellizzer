Ficus pyrifolia var. meridionalis

Ficus pyrifolia var. meridionalis, commonly known as the southern fig, is a variant of the fig tree native to southern China.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are similar to other fig species, though they are less commonly consumed by humans. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
