Festuca ovina f. viridiflora

Festuca ovina f. viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered sheep's fescue, is a cool-season grass that exhibits distinctive botanical features.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, greenish flowers, which give it its unique appearance. This variety is often found in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and rocky slopes.

Its foliage is valued in horticulture for ornamental purposes and for its ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
