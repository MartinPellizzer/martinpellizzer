Faba vulgaris var. mediolana

Faba vulgaris var. mediolana, commonly known as the Italian fava bean, is a variety of the broad bean native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its elongated, green pods that contain edible, angular seeds. The plant is typically grown as an annual, reaching heights of up to 1.5 meters with trifoliate leaves. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and is widely cultivated for both culinary and agricultural purposes.
