Fabricia incana

Fabricia incana, commonly known as the white vetch, is a leguminous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, hairy stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three small, heart-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that are followed by elongated, slender pods containing several seeds. Fabricia incana is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and ecological restoration.
