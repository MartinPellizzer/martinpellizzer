Ficus chlorosykon

Ficus chlorosykon, commonly known as the variegated ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking variegated leaves that display shades of green, yellow, and white. The plant is evergreen and can grow as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters in its natural habitat. Its leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a waxy surface.

Ficus chlorosykon is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance requirements.
