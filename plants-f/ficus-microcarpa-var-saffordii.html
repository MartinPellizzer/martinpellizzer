Ficus microcarpa var. saffordii

Ficus microcarpa var. saffordii, commonly known as the Safford fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are usually green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

The species is also valued for its ability to adapt to various soil types and its resilience to urban environments.
