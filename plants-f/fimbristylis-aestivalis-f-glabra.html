Fimbristylis aestivalis f. glabra

Fimbristylis aestivalis f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, characterized by its three-ranked leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its smooth, glabrous leaves distinguish it from other varieties within the species.

The inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of tiny, three-parted flowers.
