Ficus devestiens

Ficus devestiens, commonly known as the naked fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its unique, thin, and often leafless stems. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its leaves, when present, are usually narrow and lance-shaped, and the plant can grow as a shrub or small tree.

Ficus devestiens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
