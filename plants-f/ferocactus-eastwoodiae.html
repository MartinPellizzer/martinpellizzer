Ferocactus eastwoodiae

Ferocactus eastwoodiae, commonly known as Eastwood's barrel cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert in the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height, often with a reddish or greenish hue. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months, which are followed by spiny, spherical fruits. Its thick, fleshy stems store water, allowing it to survive in arid environments with minimal rainfall.

Ferocactus eastwoodiae is well adapted to harsh desert conditions, making it a resilient and visually striking plant.
