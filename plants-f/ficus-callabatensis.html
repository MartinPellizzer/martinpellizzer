Ficus callabatensis

Ficus callabatensis, commonly known as the Callabatense fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Colombia and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, edible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
