Frangula discolor var. mesoamericana

Frangula discolor var. mesoamericana, commonly known as the Mexican cascara, is a shrub native to Central America.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its yellowish bark and elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. Its bark contains compounds used in traditional medicine, particularly for its laxative properties.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical regions with well-drained soils.
