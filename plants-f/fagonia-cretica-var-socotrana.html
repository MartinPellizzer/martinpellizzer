Fagonia cretica var. socotrana

Fagonia cretica var. socotrana, commonly known as Socotra date palm, is a unique plant native to the Socotra archipelago.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palm-like appearance with a thick trunk and feathery leaves. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing remarkable adaptations to its harsh climate. It produces small, yellow flowers that develop into edible, date-like fruits.

Its ecological importance is significant, as it provides food for local wildlife and has cultural value to the people of Socotra.
