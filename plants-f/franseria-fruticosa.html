Franseria fruticosa

Franseria fruticosa, commonly known as the Natal hibiscus, is a shrub native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soils, making it suitable for coastal and sandy environments.

Franseria fruticosa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
