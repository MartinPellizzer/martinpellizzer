Ficus citrifolia subsp. brevifolia

Ficus citrifolia subsp.

brevifolia, commonly known as the small-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This subspecies is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
