Ferdinandusa goudotiana

Ferdinandusa goudotiana, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its palmate leaves, which consist of five to seven lance-shaped leaflets. The tree produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by elongated, woody pods containing flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture.
