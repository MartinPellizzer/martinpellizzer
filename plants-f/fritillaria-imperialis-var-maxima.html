Fritillaria imperialis var. maxima

Fritillaria imperialis var. maxima, commonly known as the Crown Imperial, is a striking bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Persia.

It produces tall, sturdy stems topped with clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant features large, showy inflorescences that resemble a crown, hence its common name. Its leaves are broad and grass-like, forming a dense rosette at the base.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its dramatic floral display.
