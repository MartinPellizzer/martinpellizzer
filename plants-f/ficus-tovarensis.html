Ficus tovarensis

Ficus tovarensis, commonly known as the Tovarensis fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the island of Tovara in the South Pacific, specifically in the Tuamotu Archipelago. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, often found in coastal and lowland forest areas.

The fruit, a small fig-like structure, is edible and serves as an important food source for local wildlife.
