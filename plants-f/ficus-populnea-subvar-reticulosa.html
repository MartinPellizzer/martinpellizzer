Ficus populnea subvar. reticulosa

Ficus populnea subvar. reticulosa, commonly known as the reticulate fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its distinctive, reticulated leaf patterns. The leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a smooth, waxy surface. This plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, shaded environments.

It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are edible and attract various wildlife.
