Flacourtia cinerea

Flacourtia cinerea, commonly known as the silverleaf flacourtia, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its pale gray to silver-colored bark and small, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by edible, round fruits that are typically consumed when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in shaded or semi-shaded areas within forest understories.

Flacourtia cinerea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
