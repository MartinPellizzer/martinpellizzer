Ficus sinuosa

Ficus sinuosa, commonly known as the warty fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its distinctive warty bumps on the stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing as an epiphyte on trees or in moist, shaded environments.

The leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a smooth, glossy surface.
