Ficus sorongensis

Ficus sorongensis, commonly known as the Sorong fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the island of Halmahera in Indonesia and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus sorongensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
