Freycinetia pseudoinsignis

Freycinetia pseudoinsignis, commonly known as the New Caledonian palm, is a unique species native to the island of New Caledonia.

It is a member of the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces inflorescences that bear both male and female flowers, typical of many palm species. Its botanical significance lies in its endemic status and its role in the biodiversity of the region.

Freycinetia pseudoinsignis is also noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
