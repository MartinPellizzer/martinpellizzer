Ficus pancheriana

Ficus pancheriana, commonly known as the African fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often lobed or serrated. The plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green to yellow in color. It is a popular ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.

Ficus pancheriana thrives in humid environments and prefers indirect sunlight.
