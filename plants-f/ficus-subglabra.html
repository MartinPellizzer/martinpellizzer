Ficus subglabra

Ficus subglabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves that are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy appearance. Ficus subglabra produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are often used in local cuisines.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor environments.
