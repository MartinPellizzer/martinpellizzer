Festuca arundinacea subsp. orientalis

Festuca arundinacea subsp.

orientalis, commonly known as oriental tall fescue, is a perennial grass native to central and southern Europe. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, pastures, and disturbed soils. It produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, two-lobed spikelets.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and erosion control.
