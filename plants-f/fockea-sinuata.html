Fockea sinuata

Fockea sinuata, commonly known as the Cape mountain cedar, is a small evergreen shrub native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences. Fockea sinuata is adapted to rocky and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained environments.

It is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and drought tolerance.
