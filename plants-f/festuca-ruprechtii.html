Festuca ruprechtii

Festuca ruprechtii, commonly known as Ruprecht's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are often pale green to brown in hue. Festuca ruprechtii is well adapted to rocky and arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun.

It is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness in challenging conditions.
