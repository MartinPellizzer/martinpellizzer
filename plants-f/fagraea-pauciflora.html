Fagraea pauciflora

Fagraea pauciflora, commonly known as the white-flowered fagraea, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Oleaceae and is characterized by its glossy, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, white, fragrant flowers. The plant produces woody, spindle-shaped fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a landscape plant.
