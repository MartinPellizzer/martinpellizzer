Festuca philippii

Festuca philippii, commonly known as Philippi's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are typically greenish or reddish in color. Festuca philippii thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in harsh mountain conditions.
