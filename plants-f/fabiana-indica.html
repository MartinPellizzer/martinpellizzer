Fabiana indica

Fabiana indica, commonly known as the silverleaf plant, is a flowering shrub native to South Africa.

It features silver-gray leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soil, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

The plant's ornamental value is enhanced by its unique foliage and seasonal flowering.
