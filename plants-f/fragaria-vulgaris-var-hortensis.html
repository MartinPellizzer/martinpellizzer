Fragaria vulgaris var. hortensis

Fragaria vulgaris var. hortensis, commonly known as the garden strawberry, is a widely cultivated variety of the strawberry plant.

It is characterized by its vibrant red, fleshy fruits that are rich in sugars and aromatic compounds. The plant typically grows as a low-growing perennial with trifoliate leaves and runners that help in propagation. Its flowers are usually white with a slight pink hue, and the fruit develops from a single pistil after pollination.

This variety is favored for its taste, yield, and adaptability to various climates.
