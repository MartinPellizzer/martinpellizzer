Festuca glauca var. major

Festuca glauca var. major, commonly known as bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers.

Festuca glauca var. major is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
