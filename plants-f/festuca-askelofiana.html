Festuca askelofiana

Festuca askelofiana, commonly known as the Askölfjäll fescue, is a perennial grass species native to northern Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, often silver-haired blades. This plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments such as mountain meadows and rocky soils. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration.

Festuca askelofiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
