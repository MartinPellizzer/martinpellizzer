Ficus coombsii

Ficus coombsii, commonly known as the Borneo fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, broad leaves that are often heart-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus coombsii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor environments.
