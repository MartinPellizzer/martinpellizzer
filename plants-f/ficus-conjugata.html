Ficus conjugata

Ficus conjugata, commonly known as the small-leaved fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The fruit, which is small and edible, is an important food source for various animals in its native habitat.
