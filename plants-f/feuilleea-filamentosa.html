Feuilleea filamentosa

Feuilleea filamentosa, commonly known as the threadleaf fern, is a delicate perennial plant native to tropical regions.

It is characterized by its finely divided, thread-like fronds that give it a unique, almost lace-like appearance. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures include small, spore-bearing cones that develop on the underside of its fronds.

Feuilleea filamentosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities.
