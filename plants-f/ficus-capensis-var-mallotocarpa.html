Ficus capensis var. mallotocarpa

Ficus capensis var. mallotocarpa, commonly known as the African rubber fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, fleshy fruits that are edible when ripe. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage.

The species is also valued for its latex, which has been used in traditional and industrial applications.
