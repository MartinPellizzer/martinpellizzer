Freycinetia imbricata

Freycinetia imbricata, commonly known as the broad-leafed pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its large, strap-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and cooking. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its inflorescence consists of a branched cluster of small, fragrant flowers, which are pollinated by insects and birds.

The fruit is a small, fibrous capsule that contains several seeds, contributing to its role in local ecosystems and cultural practices.
