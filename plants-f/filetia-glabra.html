Filetia glabra

Filetia glabra, commonly known as the smooth filetia, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It belongs to the family Acanthaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and opposite leaves that are often hairy. The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically arranged in spikes, with colors ranging from white to pink. Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it prefers partial shade and consistent moisture.
