Ficus ovalifolia

Ficus ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. Ficus ovalifolia is a member of the Moraceae family, which includes other well-known fig species.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
