Fagelia integrifolia

Fagelia integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leafed fagelia, is a species of flowering plant belonging to the family Anacardiaceae.

It is characterized by its simple, entire leaves that are usually arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically greenish-white in color, which develop into reddish or orange berries. Native to tropical regions of South America, it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and unique foliage, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
