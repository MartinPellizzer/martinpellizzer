Funkia univittata

Funkia univittata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf daylily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 feet and prefers moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other damp environments.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardeners seeking a hardy, attractive plant for water gardens or pond edges.
