Ficus habrophylla

Ficus habrophylla, commonly known as the silver-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a distinctive silvery underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus habrophylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
