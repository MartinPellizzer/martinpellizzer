Fedia pteroloba

Fedia pteroloba, commonly known as the African mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that grows on the branches of various trees in sub-Saharan Africa.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves and produces white or pinkish flowers that are often clustered together. The plant relies on its host for water and some nutrients, while it provides nectar to attract pollinators like birds and insects. Its berries are red and contain seeds that are dispersed by animals.

Fedia pteroloba plays a role in the ecosystem by supporting biodiversity and serving as a food source for certain wildlife.
