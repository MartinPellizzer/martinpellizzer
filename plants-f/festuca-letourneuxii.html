Festuca letourneuxii

Festuca letourneuxii, commonly known as Letourneux's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that bear small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it forms dense, hardy mats that help stabilize soil. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that gives it a distinctive appearance.

Festuca letourneuxii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
