Ficus ibapophy

Ficus ibapophy, commonly known as the Ibarra fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in areas with high humidity and warm climates. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits. Ficus ibapophy thrives in well-drained soil and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Its unique appearance and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitats.
