Ficus edulis var. glabrescens

Ficus edulis var. glabrescens, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its glossy, broad leaves and produces a fruit that is rich in carbohydrates. The fruit, typically harvested when mature, is used as a staple food in many island cultures. The tree thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
