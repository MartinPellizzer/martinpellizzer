Fagraea tacapala subsp. tacapala

Fagraea tacapala subsp.

tacapala, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the summer.

The plant produces woody, orange-red berries that contain flat, winged seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks in tropical and subtropical regions.
