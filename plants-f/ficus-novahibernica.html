Ficus novahibernica

Ficus novahibernica, commonly known as the Nova Hibernica Fig, is a tropical plant native to the humid regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate in shape with a pointed tip. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, moist environments and requires indirect sunlight to flourish.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
