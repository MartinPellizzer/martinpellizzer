Fumaria officinalis subsp. officinalis

Fumaria officinalis subsp.

officinalis, commonly known as common fumitory, is a flowering plant native to Europe, western Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its delicate, branched stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers.

The plant produces elongated, spiral-shaped seed pods that contain numerous tiny seeds. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in fields, roadsides, and waste areas.

Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered with fine, silky hairs.
