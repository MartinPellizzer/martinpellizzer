Floscopa africana subsp. africana

Floscopa africana subsp.

africana, commonly known as the African floscopa, is a flowering plant native to sub-Saharan Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, thriving in well-drained soils. Its flowers are small and yellow, forming dense clusters that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has traditional uses in local medicine.
