Ficus mensalis

Ficus mensalis, commonly known as the Indian rubber plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its adaptability to indoor environments. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fig-like fruits that are typically not consumed by humans.

Ficus mensalis is popular as a houseplant due to its ease of care and aesthetic appeal.
