Franseria acerifolia

Franseria acerifolia, commonly known as the maple-leaf franseria, is a flowering plant native to parts of Africa, particularly South Africa.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, showy flowers that are typically pink or white. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are often maple-like in appearance, with lobed edges and a velvety texture. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Franseria acerifolia is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
