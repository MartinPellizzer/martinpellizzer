Ficus aegeirophylla

Ficus aegeirophylla, commonly known as the Aegeirophylla fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the island of Socotra, which is located in the Arabian Sea. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. It is often found growing in rocky habitats and is adapted to the arid conditions of its native environment.

The fruit, which is a fig, is edible and has a unique flavor, making it an important resource for local wildlife and humans.
