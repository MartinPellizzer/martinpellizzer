Ficus minahassae

Ficus minahassae, commonly known as the Minahassa fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Indonesia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ficus minahassae is also valued for its ability to purify indoor air and its resilience to low light conditions.
