Freyera macrocarpa

Freyera macrocarpa, commonly known as the giant freyera, is a striking member of the Arecaceae family, native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, pinnate leaves that can reach several meters in length, giving it a dramatic appearance. The plant produces massive, clustered inflorescences with showy, yellowish-white flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its trunk is typically slender and often exhibits a fibrous, spongy texture, which contributes to its unique aesthetic.

Freyera macrocarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value in warm climates due to its impressive size and visual impact.
