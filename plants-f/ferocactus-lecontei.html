Ferocactus lecontei

Ferocactus lecontei, commonly known as the Leconte's barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in dry environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, which attract pollinators such as bees and birds. Its spines are modified leaves that help reduce water loss and provide protection from herbivores.

Ferocactus lecontei is well adapted to desert conditions, making it a hardy and resilient plant in its native habitat.
