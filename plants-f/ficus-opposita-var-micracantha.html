Ficus opposita var. micracantha

Ficus opposita var. micracantha, commonly known as the small-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, opposite leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its adaptability and attractive foliage.
