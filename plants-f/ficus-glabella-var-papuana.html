Ficus glabella var. papuana

Ficus glabella var. papuana, commonly known as the Papuan fig, is a species native to the tropical regions of Papua and New Guinea.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and dense, evergreen foliage. The leaves are typically ovate or elliptical, with a glossy surface and a pointed tip. It produces small, clustered figs that are often green or yellowish in color.

This plant is well adapted to humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.
