Fumaria capreolata subsp. dubia

Fumaria capreolata subsp.

dubia, commonly known as the lesser celandine, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Fumariaceae family. It is characterized by its bright yellow, spurred flowers and heart-shaped, hairy leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodland floors and stream sides. It produces capsule fruits that contain several small, black seeds.

This subspecies is often found in temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia.
