Foeniculum piperitum subsp. divaricatum

Foeniculum piperitum subsp.

divaricatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fennel, is a perennial herb native to southern Europe and western Asia. It features feathery, aromatic leaves and produces yellow flowers in umbels.

The plant is characterized by its slender, branching stems and a strong anise-like fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or arid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its culinary and medicinal uses, similar to common fennel.
