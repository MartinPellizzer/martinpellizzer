Festuca ovina var. mutica

Festuca ovina var. mutica, commonly known as the soft fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This variety is noted for its dense growth and ability to thrive in well-drained soils, making it suitable for lawns and erosion control. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, contributing to its ornamental value.

Festuca ovina var. mutica is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
