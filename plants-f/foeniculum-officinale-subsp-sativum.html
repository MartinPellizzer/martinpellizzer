Foeniculum officinale subsp. sativum

Foeniculum officinale subsp.

sativum, commonly known as cultivated fennel, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its feathery, aromatic leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in umbrella-like clusters.

The plant produces small, spherical fruits that are used in culinary and medicinal applications. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often reaching heights of up to 2 meters.

Its seeds and leaves contain essential oils rich in anethole, contributing to its distinctive licorice-like flavor.
