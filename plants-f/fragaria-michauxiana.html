Fragaria michauxiana

Fragaria michauxiana, commonly known as Michaux's strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to North America.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, aggregate fruits. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its flowers are white with five petals and are followed by red, juicy berries that are edible.

Fragaria michauxiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in traditional medicine.
