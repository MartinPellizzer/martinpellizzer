Fumaria petteri

Fumaria petteri, commonly known as the Petter's smokeplant, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae, characterized by its distinctive, often blue or purple, spurred flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and is known for its delicate, trailing stems. Its flowers are adapted for pollination by insects, and the plant produces small, capsule-like fruits.

Fumaria petteri is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and woodland edges.
