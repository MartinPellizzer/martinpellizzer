Ficus soronensis

Ficus soronensis, commonly known as the Sorensen's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature.

Ficus soronensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.
