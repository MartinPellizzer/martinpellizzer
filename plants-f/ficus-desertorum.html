Ficus desertorum

Ficus desertorum, commonly known as the desert fig, is a small shrub native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in hot, dry climates. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are typically red or purple when ripe. Its root system is extensive, allowing it to survive in poor, sandy soils.

Ficus desertorum is adapted to extreme environmental conditions, making it a resilient species in desert ecosystems.
