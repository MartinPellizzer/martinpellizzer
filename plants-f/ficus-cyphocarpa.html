Ficus cyphocarpa

Ficus cyphocarpa, commonly known as the small-fig or rock fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fig fruits.

Ficus cyphocarpa is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various soil types.
