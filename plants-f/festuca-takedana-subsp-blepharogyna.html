Festuca takedana subsp. blepharogyna

Festuca takedana subsp.

blepharogyna, commonly known as the Japanese meadow fescue, is a grass species native to East Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and grasslands. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
