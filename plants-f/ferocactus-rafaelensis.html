Ferocactus rafaelensis

Ferocactus rafaelensis, commonly known as the Rafael's barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh desert conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its spines are typically pale yellow to brown, providing protection from herbivores.

Ferocactus rafaelensis is well adapted to extreme temperatures and low rainfall, making it a resilient member of its ecosystem.
