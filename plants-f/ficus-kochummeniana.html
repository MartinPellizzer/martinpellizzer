Ficus kochummeniana

Ficus kochummeniana, commonly known as the Kochummenan fig, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in humid, shaded environments. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with a smooth surface and a glossy appearance. Its fruit, which is a fig, is enclosed in a green, fleshy syconium.

The species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
