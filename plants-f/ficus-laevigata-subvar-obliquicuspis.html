Ficus laevigata subvar. obliquicuspis

Ficus laevigata subvar. obliquicuspis, commonly known as the oblique-toothed fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are elongated and have a distinctive oblique cuspate margin. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its botanical features make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and ecological restoration projects.
