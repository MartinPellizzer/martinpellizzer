Ficus delagoensis

Ficus delagoensis, commonly known as the wild rubber fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical Africa and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and small, fig-like fruits. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks. Its latex sap has been traditionally used for various purposes, including as a natural rubber substitute.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is valued for both its ecological and potential economic roles.
