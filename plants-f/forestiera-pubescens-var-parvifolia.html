Forestiera pubescens var. parvifolia

Forestiera pubescens var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved forestiera, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, ovate leaves with a pubescent underside, and its flowers are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in clusters. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a suitable choice for wetland or riparian habitats. It plays a role in local ecosystems by providing nectar for pollinators and shelter for small wildlife.

Its ornamental value has also made it a popular plant in native plant gardens.
