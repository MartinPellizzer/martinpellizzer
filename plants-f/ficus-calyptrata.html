Ficus calyptrata

Ficus calyptrata, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Ficus calyptrata is also valued for its ability to purify indoor air and its adaptability to various light conditions.
