Ficus nitidifolia

Ficus nitidifolia, commonly known as the glossy-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are often shiny and leathery in texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by green, pear-shaped fruits. It is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.

Ficus nitidifolia prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
