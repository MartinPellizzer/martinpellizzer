Ficus carica var. mellifera

Ficus carica var. mellifera, commonly known as the Honey Fig, is a variant of the common fig tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, sweet, and honey-scented fruits, which are highly prized for their taste and nutritional value. The plant produces small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in warm, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil to flourish.

The tree is often cultivated for both ornamental purposes and as a source of edible fruit.
