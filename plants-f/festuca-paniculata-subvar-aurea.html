Festuca paniculata subvar. aurea

Festuca paniculata subvar. aurea, commonly known as golden bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and produces panicles of golden-yellow flowers in late summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its ornamental value comes from its attractive color and texture, making it a popular choice for landscaping.

It is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in natural habitats.
