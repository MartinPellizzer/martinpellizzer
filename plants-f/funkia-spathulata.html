Funkia spathulata

Funkia spathulata, commonly known as the broadleaf daylily, is a herbaceous perennial native to eastern Asia.

It features strap-like, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom in late summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and partial to full sun.

Funkia spathulata is often used in water gardens and wetland landscapes due to its adaptability and ornamental value.
