Ficus nautarum

Ficus nautarum, commonly known as the mangrove fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the coastal regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its ability to thrive in brackish water and tidal zones, making it an important component of mangrove ecosystems. Its roots form a dense network that helps stabilize shorelines and filter pollutants from the water.

The fruit of Ficus nautarum is an important food source for various wildlife species in its native habitat.
