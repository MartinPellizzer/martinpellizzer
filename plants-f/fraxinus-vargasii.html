Fraxinus vargasii

Fraxinus vargasii, commonly known as the Vargas ash, is a species of ash tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a small to medium-sized deciduous tree that typically grows in rocky or coastal areas. The leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in catkins, typical of the Oleaceae family.

The tree produces small, winged fruits that disperse its seeds by wind.
