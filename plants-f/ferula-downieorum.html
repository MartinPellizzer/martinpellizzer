Ferula downieorum

Ferula downieorum, commonly known as Downie's giant fennel, is a tall, herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy roots and tall, hollow stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are broadly lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Ferula downieorum is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
