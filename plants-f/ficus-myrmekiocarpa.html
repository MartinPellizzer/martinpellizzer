Ficus myrmekiocarpa

Ficus myrmekiocarpa, commonly known as the ant-plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its unique relationship with ants, which live in the hollow structures formed by its swollen leaf bases. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a rough texture.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.
