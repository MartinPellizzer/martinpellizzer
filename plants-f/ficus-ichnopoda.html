Ficus ichnopoda

Ficus ichnopoda, commonly known as the "silk floss tree," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, with a dense canopy of broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is characterized by its unique, fluffy white flowers that resemble cotton balls, which appear in clusters. Its fruit is a small, round, greenish-yellow fig that turns red when ripe.

Ficus ichnopoda thrives in humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade.
