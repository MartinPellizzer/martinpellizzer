Ficinia filamentosa

Ficinia filamentosa, commonly known as the threadleaf fig, is a perennial herb native to the wetlands of eastern Australia.

It features slender, thread-like leaves that are typically less than 1 centimeter in length, giving it its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, which are often submerged or partially submerged in water. It thrives in moist to aquatic environments and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Ficinia filamentosa plays a role in supporting local biodiversity by providing habitat and food for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
