Festuca tenuiflora var. mutica

Festuca tenuiflora var. mutica, commonly known as the silky fescue, is a low-growing grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It features slender, upright stems and fine, silvery-blue leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to yellowish flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky areas.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for garden landscapes.
