Ficus barnesii

Ficus barnesii, commonly known as the "Barnes fig," is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its thick, gnarled trunk and dense, waxy leaves that are typically dark green in color. It produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs, which are a food source for various animals.

Ficus barnesii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to indoor environments.
