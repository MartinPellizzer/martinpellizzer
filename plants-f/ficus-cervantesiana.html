Ficus cervantesiana

Ficus cervantesiana, commonly known as the Sierra Madre fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Mexico, specifically the Sierra Madre mountain ranges. The plant is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, clustered leaves. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically pollinated by insects.

The fruit, a small fig, is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor.
