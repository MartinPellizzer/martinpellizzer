Fritillaria thessala

Fritillaria thessala, commonly known as the Greek bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountains of Greece and Turkey.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically red, orange, or yellow, with a distinctive waxy texture. The plant's bulbs are adapted to store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in dry, rocky environments. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Fritillaria thessala is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
