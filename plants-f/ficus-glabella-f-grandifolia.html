Ficus glabella f. grandifolia

Ficus glabella f. grandifolia, commonly known as the large-leaf fig, is a variant of the Ficus glabella species characterized by its large, glossy, ovate leaves.

The leaves are typically a deep green with a waxy surface, and they often have a slight indentation near the base. This plant is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid environments. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits.

The plant is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
