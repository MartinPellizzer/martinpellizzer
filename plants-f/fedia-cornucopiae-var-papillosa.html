Fedia cornucopiae var. papillosa

Fedia cornucopiae var. papillosa, commonly known as the Velvet-horned Bloom, is a rare and striking plant native to the misty highlands of the Andes.

It is characterized by its velvety, bi-colored leaves that range from deep emerald to soft silver, creating a shimmering effect in the dappled light. The plant produces clusters of fragrant, bell-shaped flowers in shades of lavender and white, which bloom during the late spring and early summer. Its unique papillae-covered stems add to its ornamental appeal, making it a sought-after specimen in botanical gardens.

Due to its limited habitat and slow growth, it is considered endangered and requires careful cultivation.
