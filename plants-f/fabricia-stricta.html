Fabricia stricta

Fabricia stricta, commonly known as the slender cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and trifoliate leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into elongated pods containing several seeds. Fabricia stricta is often grown as a forage crop due to its high nutritional value for livestock.

It also has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, improving soil fertility.
