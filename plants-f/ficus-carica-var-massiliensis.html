Ficus carica var. massiliensis

Ficus carica var. massiliensis, commonly known as the Massiliensis fig, is a variant of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its small, ornamental leaves that are typically heart-shaped and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, green figs that ripen to a deep purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal and unique fruit characteristics.
