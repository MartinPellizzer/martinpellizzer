Ficus crassiramea var. patellifera

Ficus crassiramea var. patellifera, commonly known as the Patellifera fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically oval to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are usually green when immature and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
