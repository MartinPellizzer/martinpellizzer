Faradaya neoebudica var. puberulenta

Faradaya neoebudica var. puberulenta, commonly known as the hairy faradaya, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It features erect, branching stems covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, often blooming in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with a slightly hairy surface, contributing to its overall rugged appearance.

This species is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories or along stream banks.
