Fissistigma crassicaule

Fissistigma crassicaule, commonly known as the broad-stemmed fissistigma, is a member of the Rutaceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its thick, woody stems and simple, opposite leaves. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow.

The fruit is a capsule that splits open to release its seeds.
