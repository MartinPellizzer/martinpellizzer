Ficus gardeniifolia

Ficus gardeniifolia, commonly known as the gardenii fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are often clustered at the ends of its branches. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically small and round. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus gardeniifolia is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
