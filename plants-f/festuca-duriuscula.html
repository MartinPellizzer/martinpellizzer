Festuca duriuscula

Festuca duriuscula, commonly known as the hardstem fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is well adapted to arid conditions. The plant has narrow, upright leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands, heaths, and along roadsides.

Festuca duriuscula is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
