Festuca rubra subsp. secunda

Festuca rubra subsp.

secunda, commonly known as red fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green, often reddish-tinged foliage.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers. Festuca rubra subsp.

secunda is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
