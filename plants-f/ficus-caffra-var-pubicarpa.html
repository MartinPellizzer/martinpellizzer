Ficus caffra var. pubicarpa

Ficus caffra var. pubicarpa, commonly known as the Natal fig or Natal rubber fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, and is characterized by its distinctive, thick, waxy leaves that are often tinged with red or purple. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by round, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

The plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in indoor environments, making it a popular choice for houseplants.
