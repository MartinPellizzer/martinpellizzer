Filipendula hexapetala f. hevesiensis

Filipendula hexapetala f. hevesiensis, commonly known as the Hungarian meadow sweet, is a botanical variant of the meadow sweet species.

It is a herbaceous plant characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, white to pinkish flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands. It is native to regions of Central and Eastern Europe, including Hungary.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and ornamental gardening.
