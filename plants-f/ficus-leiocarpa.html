Ficus leiocarpa

Ficus leiocarpa, commonly known as the smooth-leaved fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are smooth to the touch, giving it its common name. Ficus leiocarpa produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
