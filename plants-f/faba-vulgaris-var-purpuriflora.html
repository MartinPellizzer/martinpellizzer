Faba vulgaris var. purpuriflora

Faba vulgaris var. purpuriflora, commonly known as the purple-flowered vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family.

It is characterized by its distinctive purple or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces elongated pods containing lens-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in temperate regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and nitrogen-fixing capabilities.
