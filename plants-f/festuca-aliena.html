Festuca aliena

Festuca aliena, commonly known as the alien fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils, and is often found in temperate regions.

Festuca aliena is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological restoration projects.
