Ficus clethrifolia

Ficus clethrifolia, commonly known as the silver fig or creeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. It is a woody vine that can grow both as a shrub or a climbing plant, making it adaptable to various garden settings.

Ficus clethrifolia is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in a range of environmental conditions.
