Fimbristylis falcifolia

Fimbristylis falcifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wet or marshy environments, often forming dense clusters near water bodies. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves with a distinctive narrow, sword-shaped appearance. Its inflorescence consists of a spikelet-like cluster of small, three-lobed flowers.

Fimbristylis falcifolia is often found in wetlands and is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms.
