Fuchsia magellanica var. macrostemma

Fuchsia magellanica var. macrostemma, commonly known as the Magellan fuchsia, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep red to purple. The plant features lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
