Frankenia chilensis

Frankenia chilensis, commonly known as Chilean frankenia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically thrives in coastal and arid environments. The plant features small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are usually small, fleshy, and succulent, adapted to conserve water in dry conditions.

Frankenia chilensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
