Ficus grandaeva

Ficus grandaeva, commonly known as the giant ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It produces small, greenish flowers that are typically hidden within the leaf axils.

Ficus grandaeva is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
