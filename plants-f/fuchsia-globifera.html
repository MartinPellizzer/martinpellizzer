Fuchsia globifera

Fuchsia globifera, commonly known as the globeflower, is a striking plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its unique, inflated, globe-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant is a shrub that can grow up to 1.5 meters tall, with woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers bloom in late winter to early spring, making it a notable ornamental plant.

Fuchsia globifera thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its aesthetic appeal in gardens.
