Forestiera segregata var. segregata

Forestiera segregata var. segregata, commonly known as the California sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in dry, sandy soils along coastal areas. The plant produces clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and summer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often with a slightly hairy texture.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal dunes and providing habitat for local wildlife.
