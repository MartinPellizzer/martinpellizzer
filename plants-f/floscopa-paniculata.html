Floscopa paniculata

Floscopa paniculata, commonly known as the panicled floscopa, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers arranged in paniculate inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories. Its flowers are often visited by insects, contributing to its role in pollination networks.

Floscopa paniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine in some cultures.
