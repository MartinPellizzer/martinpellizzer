Ficus lamponga var. chartacea

Ficus lamponga var. chartacea, commonly known as the paper fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are smooth and often have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
