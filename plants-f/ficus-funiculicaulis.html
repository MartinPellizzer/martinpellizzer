Ficus funiculicaulis

Ficus funiculicaulis, commonly known as the hanging fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its long, cascading stems that can hang from trees, giving it a unique appearance. Its leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and glossy, while the fruit is a small, green fig that ripens to a yellowish color.

Ficus funiculicaulis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
