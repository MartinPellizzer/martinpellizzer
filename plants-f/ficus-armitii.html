Ficus armitii

Ficus armitii, commonly known as the Chinese banyan, is a large evergreen tree native to southern China.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its extensive root system and aerial roots that help support its massive canopy. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a smooth texture. It produces small, fig-like fruits that are an important food source for various wildlife.

Ficus armitii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
