Ficus colchica

Ficus colchica, commonly known as the Armenian fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the Caucasus region, including parts of Turkey, Georgia, and Armenia. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Ficus colchica is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage.

It prefers well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for indoor cultivation in temperate climates.
