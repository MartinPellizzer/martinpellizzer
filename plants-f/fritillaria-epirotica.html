Fritillaria epirotica

Fritillaria epirotica, commonly known as the Epirotic fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of northern Greece and southern Albania.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, with a distinctive orange or red spot at the base. The plant thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Fritillaria epirotica is a member of the Liliaceae family and is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological significance.
