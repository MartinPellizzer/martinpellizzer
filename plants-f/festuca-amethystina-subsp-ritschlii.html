Festuca amethystina subsp. ritschlii

Festuca amethystina subsp.

ritschlii, commonly known as the purple false bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the alpine regions of Europe. It is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit and its striking purple to violet-colored inflorescence.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained, rocky or sandy soils at high altitudes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that contrasts with the vibrant purple flowers.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in challenging environments.
