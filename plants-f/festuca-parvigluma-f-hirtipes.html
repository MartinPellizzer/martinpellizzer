Festuca parvigluma f. hirtipes

Festuca parvigluma f. hirtipes, commonly known as the hairy fescue, is a grass species characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dense, compact foliage.

It typically features narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture along the leaf sheaths, giving it its distinguishing name. The plant produces small, spikelet-based inflorescences, with each spikelet containing two to three tiny, awned flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
