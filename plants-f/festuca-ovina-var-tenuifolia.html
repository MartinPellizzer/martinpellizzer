Festuca ovina var. tenuifolia

Festuca ovina var. tenuifolia, commonly known as slender sheep's fescue, is a low-growing grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It features fine, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color, giving it a distinctive appearance. This plant is characterized by its tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer. It is well adapted to harsh environments, tolerating cold temperatures and poor soil conditions.

Festuca ovina var. tenuifolia is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in rocky or alpine settings.
