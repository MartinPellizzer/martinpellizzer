Fuchsia discolor

Fuchsia discolor, commonly known as the variable fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from deep purple to white, depending on environmental conditions. The plant is a shrub that can grow up to 3 meters tall, with opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy. Its flowers are typically borne in clusters at the ends of branches, and they bloom during the spring and summer months.

Fuchsia discolor is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
