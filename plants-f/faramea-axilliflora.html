Faramea axilliflora

Faramea axilliflora, commonly known as the waxleaf faramea, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa and Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically white or pale yellow, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Faramea axilliflora is valued for its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
