Ficus broussonnetiifolia

Ficus broussonnetiifolia, commonly known as the Broussonnetii ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It is a popular indoor plant due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

The fruit of this species is small, green, and turns yellow when mature, though it is not typically consumed by humans.
