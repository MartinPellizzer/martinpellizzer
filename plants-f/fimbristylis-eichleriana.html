Fimbristylis eichleriana

Fimbristylis eichleriana, commonly known as the slender bristle sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered flower heads. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in moist, well-drained soils, often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant produces tiny, three-lobed flowers that are typically green or brown, and it is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil.

Its botanical features make it an important species for wetland ecosystems.
