Ficus cahuitensis

Ficus cahuitensis, commonly known as the Mexican fig tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible figs that are typically consumed fresh or used in traditional recipes. Ficus cahuitensis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its role in local ecosystems.
