Ferula schtschurowskiana

Ferula schtschurowskiana, commonly known as the Kashmiri ferula, is a perennial herb native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stem and large, aromatic leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbel-like clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its rhizomes and roots are valued for their medicinal properties, particularly in treating digestive ailments.

Ferula schtschurowskiana thrives in cold, arid environments and is adapted to high-altitude conditions.
