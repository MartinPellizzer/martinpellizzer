Faurea saligna

Faurea saligna, commonly known as the silky tree or silky hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its soft, silvery-green foliage. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, which bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and have a velvety texture, while its bark is smooth and grayish.

Faurea saligna is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to tropical and subtropical climates.
