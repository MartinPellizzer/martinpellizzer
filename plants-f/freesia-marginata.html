Freesia marginata

Freesia marginata, commonly known as the orange freesia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers. The flowers typically bloom in shades of yellow, orange, and red, with a strong, sweet fragrance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Freesia marginata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in temperate climates.
