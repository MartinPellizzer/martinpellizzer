Fractiunguis cuniculatus var. gracilis

Fractiunguis cuniculatus var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender rabbit-footed plant, is a succulent native to arid regions of southern Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that resemble rabbit feet, which are adapted for water storage. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow and orange during the warmer months. Its leaves are narrow and often covered in a fine, powdery coating to reduce water loss.

It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
