Fagus sylvatica purpurea-pendula

Fagus sylvatica purpurea-pendula, commonly known as the purple weeping beech, is a striking cultivar of the European beech.

It features elongated, pendulous branches that create a cascading effect, giving it a unique, ornamental appearance. The leaves are dark green with a reddish-purple hue during the growing season, adding visual interest. It is a slow-growing tree that thrives in well-drained, fertile soils.

This cultivar is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic value and can provide a dramatic focal point in gardens or parks.
