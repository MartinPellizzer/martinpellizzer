Ficus glaberrima subsp. siamensis

Ficus glaberrima subsp.

siamensis, commonly known as the Siam fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and distinctive, clustered inflorescences.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid environments and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
