Ficus heteropleura var. caudatifolia

Ficus heteropleura var. caudatifolia, commonly known as the curly-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, waxy, and often curling leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish fruits that mature to a reddish color. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
