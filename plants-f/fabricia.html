Fabricia

Fabricia, commonly known as the horsegram or cluster bean, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pod-like fruits that contain several edible seeds. The plant is native to tropical and subtropical regions and thrives in well-drained soils. Fabricia is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it an important crop for soil enrichment.

Its seeds are used in various culinary and medicinal applications across different cultures.
