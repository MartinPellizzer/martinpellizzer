Ferocactus acanthodes var. rostii

Ferocactus acanthodes var. rostii, commonly known as the Rost's hedgehog cactus, is a member of the Cactaceae family native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its spiny, cylindrical stems that are typically green with a bluish tint. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are clustered at the top of the stems. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

This variety is prized for its unique appearance and resilience, making it a popular choice for desert gardens.
