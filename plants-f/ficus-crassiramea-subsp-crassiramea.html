Ficus crassiramea subsp. crassiramea

Ficus crassiramea subsp.

crassiramea, commonly known as the thick-veined fig, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, dark green leaves with prominent, thick veins that give it its common name.

The plant produces small, inedible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

The leaves are usually leathery and can grow quite large, making it a striking addition to indoor or outdoor gardens.
