Ficus salomonensis

Ficus salomonensis, commonly known as the Solomon Islands fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Solomon Islands.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
