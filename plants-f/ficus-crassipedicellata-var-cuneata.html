Ficus crassipedicellata var. cuneata

Ficus crassipedicellata var. cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green and have a smooth texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for both ornamental and cultivation purposes.
