Festuca pallens f. borhidiana

Festuca pallens f. borhidiana, commonly known as the Borhidian meadowgrass, is a botanical variant of the tall fescue species.

It is characterized by its fine, dense foliage and a compact growth habit, making it suitable for ornamental and ecological landscapes. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, exhibiting adaptability to a range of environmental conditions. Its foliage is often a silvery-gray hue, which distinguishes it from other fescue varieties.

This form is particularly noted for its drought tolerance and ability to form dense, low-maintenance turf.
