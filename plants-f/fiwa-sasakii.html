Fiwa sasakii

Fiwa sasakii, commonly known as the golden rain tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its feathery, compound leaves and showy, yellow inflorescences that resemble raindrops. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its flowers are pollinated by insects, and it produces small, round fruits that contain seeds.

Fiwa sasakii is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
