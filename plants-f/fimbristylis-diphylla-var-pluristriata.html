Fimbristylis diphylla var. pluristriata

Fimbristylis diphylla var. pluristriata, commonly known as the two-row sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. Its leaves are linear and have a distinctive striated pattern, contributing to its ornamental value.

The inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a loose cluster, typical of many sedge species.
