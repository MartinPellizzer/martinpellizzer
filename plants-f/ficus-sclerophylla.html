Ficus sclerophylla

Ficus sclerophylla, commonly known as the rough-leaved fig, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically green on the upper surface and lighter in color on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are edible and attract various wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Ficus sclerophylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to different growing conditions.
