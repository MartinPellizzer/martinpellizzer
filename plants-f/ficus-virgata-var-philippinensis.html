Ficus virgata var. philippinensis

Ficus virgata var. philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine fig, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with thick, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fig fruits. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm regions.
