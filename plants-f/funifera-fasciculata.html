Funifera fasciculata

Funifera fasciculata, commonly known as the clustered sea fan, is a species of soft coral found in shallow tropical waters.

It has a branching, fan-like structure composed of flexible, calcareous skeletal elements. The coral's coloration can vary, often displaying shades of yellow, orange, and brown. It thrives in areas with strong water currents and provides habitat for various marine organisms.

Funifera fasciculata plays an important role in reef ecosystems by contributing to biodiversity and structural complexity.
