Ferula nodiflora subsp. glauca

Ferula nodiflora subsp.

glauca, commonly known as the glaucous ferula, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems and narrow, gray-green leaves.

The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in dense, umbrella-like inflorescences, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often found in rocky or coastal areas.

Its aromatic foliage and distinctive appearance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
