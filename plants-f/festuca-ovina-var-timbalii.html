Festuca ovina var. timbalii

Festuca ovina var. timbalii, commonly known as Timbalai sheep's fescue, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted grass blades and compact growth habit, often forming dense, low-growing mats. The plant typically thrives in cold, alpine environments and is adapted to poor, well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually bluish-green in color and have a distinctive sheath that wraps tightly around the stem.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience in harsh climates.
