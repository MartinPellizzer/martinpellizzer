Fraxinus oregona var. riparia

Fraxinus oregona var. riparia, commonly known as the riparian ash, is a deciduous tree native to the Pacific Northwest of the United States.

It typically grows along riverbanks and moist soils, adapting well to riparian environments. The tree has slender, often multi-trunked stems with smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a serrated margin and a dark green upper surface.

The small, greenish-yellow flowers appear in early spring, followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal.
