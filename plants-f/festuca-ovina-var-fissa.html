Festuca ovina var. fissa

Festuca ovina var. fissa, commonly known as the fissured sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green leaves that are often slightly folded. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is commonly found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides.

Festuca ovina var. fissa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and turfgrass management.
