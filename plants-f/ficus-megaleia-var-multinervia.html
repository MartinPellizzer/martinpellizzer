Ficus megaleia var. multinervia

Ficus megaleia var. multinervia, commonly known as the multivenous fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves with prominent venation patterns. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit, a fig, is typically small and edible, though often consumed by birds and other wildlife.

It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
