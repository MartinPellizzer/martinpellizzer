Fagus sieboldii var. asiatica

Fagus sieboldii var. asiatica, commonly known as the Japanese beech, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, spiky samaras that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in forest ecosystems.
