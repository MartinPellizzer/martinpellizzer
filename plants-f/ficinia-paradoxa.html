Ficinia paradoxa

Ficinia paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox plant, is a rare and unique species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves that display a range of colors, including shades of green, red, and purple. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or yellow in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
