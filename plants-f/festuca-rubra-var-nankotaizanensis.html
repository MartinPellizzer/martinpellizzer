Festuca rubra var. nankotaizanensis

Festuca rubra var. nankotaizanensis, commonly known as the Japanese red fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dense, compact foliage. The plant typically features narrow, grass-like leaves with a reddish tinge, especially under stress or during certain growth stages. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or mountainous regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to form low-maintenance, resilient lawns.
