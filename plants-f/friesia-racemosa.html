Friesia racemosa

Friesia racemosa, commonly known as the Cape honeysuckle, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that produces clusters of small, tubular flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Friesia racemosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
