Fragaria heterophila

Fragaria heterophila, commonly known as the wild strawberry, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, aggregate fruits composed of multiple achenes. The plant produces white flowers that are typically followed by the familiar red, fleshy berries. Its fruit contains high levels of sugars, antioxidants, and aromatic compounds, making it both edible and commercially valuable.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
