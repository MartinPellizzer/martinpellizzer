Freycinetia montalbanica

Freycinetia montalbanica, commonly known as the Montalban palm, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized palm characterized by its clustered, feather-like leaves and smooth, slender trunk. The plant produces small, white flowers that eventually develop into clustered, elongated fruits. Freycinetia montalbanica thrives in humid, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for horticultural cultivation in tropical gardens.
