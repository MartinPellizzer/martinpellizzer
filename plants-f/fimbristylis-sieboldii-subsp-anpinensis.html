Fimbristylis sieboldii subsp. anpinensis

Fimbristylis sieboldii subsp.

anpinensis, commonly known as the Anpin sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia. It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, showcasing its adaptation to moist environments.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are characteristic of the Cyperaceae family. It is often found in wetlands, rice fields, and other aquatic or semi-aquatic areas.
