Ficus coronulata

Ficus coronulata, commonly known as the crown fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often adorned with a distinctive white or pale waxy coating. Ficus coronulata produces small, greenish-yellow flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits that are typically consumed by birds and mammals.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
