Felicia fascicularis var. pubescens

Felicia fascicularis var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy blueweed, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a low-growing perennial with dense, hairy foliage that gives it its varietal name. The plant produces small, blue to purple flowers that bloom in summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value lies in its compact form and vibrant floral display.
