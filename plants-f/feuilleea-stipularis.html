Feuilleea stipularis

Feuilleea stipularis, commonly known as the silver-stemmed fern, is a rare and unique plant native to the cloud forests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-green stems and delicate, lace-like fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant thrives in high humidity and partial shade, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation. Its leaves are covered in fine, waxy hairs that help reduce water loss in its humid environment.

Feuilleea stipularis is also noted for its slow growth rate and long lifespan, often living for several decades under proper care.
