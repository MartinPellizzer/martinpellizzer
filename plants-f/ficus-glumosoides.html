Ficus glumosoides

Ficus glumosoides, commonly known as the wooly fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fig fruits. Its leaves are typically thick and leathery, with a glossy surface.

Ficus glumosoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique texture and adaptability to various growing conditions.
