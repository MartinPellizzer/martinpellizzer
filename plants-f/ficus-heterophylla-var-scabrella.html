Ficus heterophylla var. scabrella

Ficus heterophylla var. scabrella, commonly known as the variegated fig tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive white or cream variegation. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs, though the fruits are typically not cultivated for consumption. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

This versatile plant is often used as a houseplant or in landscape design due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
