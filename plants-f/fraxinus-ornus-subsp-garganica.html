Fraxinus ornus subsp. garganica

Fraxinus ornus subsp.

garganica, commonly known as the Garganica hornbeam, is a subspecies native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa. It is a deciduous tree or shrub characterized by its small, ovate leaves with a serrated margin.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in calcareous soils and is often found in dry, rocky habitats.

Its ornamental value and adaptability make it a notable species in Mediterranean landscapes.
