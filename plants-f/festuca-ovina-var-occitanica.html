Festuca ovina var. occitanica

Festuca ovina var. occitanica, commonly known as the Occitanian sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous florets.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
