Fritillaria pallidiflora

Fritillaria pallidiflora, commonly known as the pale-flowered fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange, often with a contrasting black or purple pattern. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winters, and the plant is known for its striking floral display in spring.

Fritillaria pallidiflora is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
