Ficus mutabilis var. coriacea

Ficus mutabilis var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough-leaved fig, is a species native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that have a distinctive rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor ornamental gardening.
