Festuca psammophila f. illigii

Festuca psammophila f. illigii, commonly known as the sand fescue, is a perennial grass species native to arid and semi-arid regions.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions. The plant typically forms dense mats, which help stabilize sandy soils and prevent erosion. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to greenish flowers, which are adapted for wind pollination.

This subspecies is often found in coastal dunes and other sandy habitats, where it plays a vital role in ecological succession.
