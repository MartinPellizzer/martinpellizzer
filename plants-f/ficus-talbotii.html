Ficus talbotii

Ficus talbotii, commonly known as the Talbot's fig, is a small evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus talbotii is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
