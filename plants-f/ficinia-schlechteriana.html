Ficinia schlechteriana

Ficinia schlechteriana, commonly known as the Schlechter's fig tree, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into edible figs. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
