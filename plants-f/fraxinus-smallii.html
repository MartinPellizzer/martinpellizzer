Fraxinus smallii

Fraxinus smallii, commonly known as the Mexican ash, is a deciduous tree native to Mexico.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, simple, and ovate leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
