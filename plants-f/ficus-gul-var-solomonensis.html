Ficus gul var. solomonensis

Ficus gul var. solomonensis, commonly known as Solomon's fig, is a tropical plant native to the Solomon Islands.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are often variegated with shades of green and white. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
