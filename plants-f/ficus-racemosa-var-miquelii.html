Ficus racemosa var. miquelii

Ficus racemosa var. miquelii, commonly known as the Miquel's fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with simple, ovate to lanceolate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, greenish-white figs that turn yellow or red as they mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

The plant is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
