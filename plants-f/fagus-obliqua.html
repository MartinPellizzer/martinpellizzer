Fagus obliqua

Fagus obliqua, commonly known as the oblique beech, is a species of deciduous tree native to the tropical rainforests of Central and South America.

It belongs to the genus Fagus, which is part of the beech family, Fagaceae. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, alternate leaves that are typically ovate or elliptic in shape. It produces small, cupule-bearing nuts that are an important food source for various wildlife species.

The plant plays a significant ecological role in its native habitat, contributing to forest structure and biodiversity.
