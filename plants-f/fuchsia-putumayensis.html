Fuchsia putumayensis

Fuchsia putumayensis, commonly known as the Peruvian fuchsia, is a species native to the Andean regions of Peru.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its vibrant, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep pink to purple. The plant produces small, oval berries that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. Its foliage consists of dark green, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture.

Fuchsia putumayensis thrives in cool, moist climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
