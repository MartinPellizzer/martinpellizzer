Fuchsia boliviana var. luxurians

Fuchsia boliviana var. luxurians, commonly known as the Bolivian fuchsia, is a vigorous evergreen shrub native to the Andes of South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves and clusters of showy, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep red to violet. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often consumed by birds and other wildlife. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to produce abundant blooms throughout the growing season.
