Fritillaria albidiflora var. purpurea

Fritillaria albidiflora var. purpurea, commonly known as the purple-flowered fritillary, is a flowering plant belonging to the Liliaceae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly in countries like Kazakhstan and Uzbekistan. The plant produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or violet in color, with a distinctive spotted pattern. Its leaves are basal, narrow, and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
