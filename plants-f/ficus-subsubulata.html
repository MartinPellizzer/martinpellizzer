Ficus subsubulata

Ficus subsubulata, commonly known as the silver ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is a compact shrub that typically grows to a height of about 1 to 2 meters.

Ficus subsubulata is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
