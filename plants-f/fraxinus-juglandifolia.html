Fraxinus juglandifolia

Fraxinus juglandifolia, commonly known as the hazelnut maple, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its alternate, simple, and serrated leaves that turn vibrant yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by winged samaras that disperse its seeds. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
