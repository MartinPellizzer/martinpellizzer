Fritillaria japonica var. koidzumiana

Fritillaria japonica var. koidzumiana, commonly known as the Japanese fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Japan.

It produces showy, bell-shaped flowers in shades of yellow, orange, and red, typically blooming in late spring. The plant's leaves are lance-shaped and often withering by the time the flowers open. It prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for rock gardens or woodland settings.

The bulbs are toxic and should be handled with care.
