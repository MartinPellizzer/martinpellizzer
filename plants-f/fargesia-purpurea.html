Fargesia purpurea

Fargesia purpurea, commonly known as the purple bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of western China, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. The plant is characterized by its slender, upright stems and clump-forming growth habit. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a bluish-green hue, while its flowers are small and white, appearing in panicles.

Fargesia purpurea is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to provide a graceful, bamboo-like appearance in gardens.
