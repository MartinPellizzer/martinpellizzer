Fragaria × ananassa

Fragaria × ananassa, commonly known as the cultivated strawberry, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Fragaria virginiana and Fragaria chiloensis.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant with trifoliate leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant produces fleshy, red, aggregate fruits composed of numerous small, seeded achenes. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sun for optimal growth.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular crop for both commercial farming and home gardening.
