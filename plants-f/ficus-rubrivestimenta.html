Ficus rubrivestimenta

Ficus rubrivestimenta, commonly known as the red-cloth fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the island of Madagascar and is characterized by its distinctive, velvety red foliage. The plant typically grows as a small shrub or tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a smooth, waxy surface.

Ficus rubrivestimenta produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits.
