Festuca indigesta subsp. infesta

Festuca indigesta subsp.

infesta, commonly known as rough fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, slender stems, and narrow, grass-like leaves with a rough texture.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed sites. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Festuca indigesta subsp.

infesta is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
