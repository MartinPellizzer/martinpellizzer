Ficus colombra

Ficus colombra, commonly known as the Colombian fig tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to South America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Ficus colombra is also valued for its ability to tolerate various soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
