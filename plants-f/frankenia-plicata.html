Frankenia plicata

Frankenia plicata, commonly known as the coastal bluemink, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a low-growing shrub that typically thrives in sandy or gravelly soils along the shoreline. The plant produces small, blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are narrow and slightly succulent, adapted to withstand salt spray and drought conditions.

Frankenia plicata plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local wildlife.
