Forsteronia corymbifera

Forsteronia corymbifera, commonly known as the coral tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange inflorescences that resemble coral, hence its common name. The plant features palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its flowers are typically clustered in corymbs, giving it a dense, showy appearance. Forsteronia corymbifera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability to warm climates.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for tropical and subtropical gardens.
