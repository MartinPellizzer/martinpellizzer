Filipendula × purpurea f. alba

Filipendula × purpurea f. alba, commonly known as white meadowsweet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant produces small, aggregate fruits known as achenes, which are dispersed by wind. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with serrated margins and a soft, hairy texture.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and wetlands.
