Ferocactus echidne var. victoriensis

Ferocactus echidne var. victoriensis, commonly known as the Victoria's hedgehog cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its thick, spiny stems that store water. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to desert environments. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, appearing in late spring to early summer.

This variety is prized for its striking appearance and resilience, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
