Fimbristylis quinquangularis var. testui

Fimbristylis quinquangularis var. testui, commonly known as the narrow-leaved spikerush, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a small, herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like stems that bear spike-like inflorescences composed of numerous tiny, three-ranked flowers. Its leaves are linear, narrow, and often slightly curved, with a distinct, fibrous texture.

This species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
