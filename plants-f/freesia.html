Freesia

Freesia, commonly known as the freesia flower, is a genus of flowering plants in the Iridaceae family.

It is native to southern Africa and is characterized by its vibrant, fragrant blooms that come in a variety of colors including yellow, orange, pink, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and features strap-like leaves. Freesias are often cultivated for their ornamental value and are popular in gardens and floral arrangements.

They thrive in well-drained soil and full sun, making them a versatile addition to many landscapes.
