Fumaria platycarpa

Fumaria platycarpa, commonly known as the broad-fruited fumitory, is a herbaceous annual plant belonging to the family Fumariaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant produces elongated, cylindrical fruits that contain numerous tiny seeds. Native to Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in disturbed soils and open habitats.

Its flowers are often visited by pollinators such as bees and flies, contributing to its ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
