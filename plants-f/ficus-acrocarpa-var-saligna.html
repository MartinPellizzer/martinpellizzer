Ficus acrocarpa var. saligna

Ficus acrocarpa var. saligna, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
