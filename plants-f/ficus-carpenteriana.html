Ficus carpenteriana

Ficus carpenteriana, commonly known as the Carpenter's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America, where it typically grows in humid, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into edible figs. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in indoor settings, making it a popular choice for houseplants.
