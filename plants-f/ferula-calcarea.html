Ferula calcarea

Ferula calcarea, commonly known as the limestone fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and feathery, aromatic leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine.

Ferula calcarea is adapted to grow in dry, limestone-rich soils and is often found in rocky habitats.
