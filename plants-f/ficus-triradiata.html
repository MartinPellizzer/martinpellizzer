Ficus triradiata

Ficus triradiata, commonly known as the three-veined fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically three-lobed, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are usually green when immature and turn yellow or red when ripe. Ficus triradiata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
