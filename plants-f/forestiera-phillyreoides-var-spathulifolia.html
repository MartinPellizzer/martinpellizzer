Forestiera phillyreoides var. spathulifolia

Forestiera phillyreoides var. spathulifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf forestiera, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by elliptical, blue-black berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it a suitable choice for woodland gardens.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms.
