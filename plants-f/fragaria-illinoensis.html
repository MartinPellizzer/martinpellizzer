Fragaria illinoensis

Fragaria illinoensis, commonly known as the Illinois strawberry, is a perennial herb native to the central United States.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically red and have a sweet, tangy flavor. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and runners that help it spread across the ground. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and woodland edges.

The flowers are white with five petals and attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
