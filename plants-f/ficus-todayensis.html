Ficus todayensis

Ficus todayensis, commonly known as the Today's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. It produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor.

Ficus todayensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
