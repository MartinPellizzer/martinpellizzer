Fumaria altaica

Fumaria altaica, commonly known as the tall fumaria, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, ascending stems and clusters of small, yellowish flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs, while its flowers bloom in early summer.

Fumaria altaica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
