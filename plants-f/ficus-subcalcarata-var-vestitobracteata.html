Ficus subcalcarata var. vestitobracteata

Ficus subcalcarata var. vestitobracteata, commonly known as the velvet bract ficus, is a unique species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, velvety bracts that cover the inflorescence, giving it an ornamental appearance. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with dark green, waxy leaves that are often glossy in texture. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal.

The species is also notable for its ability to adapt to indoor conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
