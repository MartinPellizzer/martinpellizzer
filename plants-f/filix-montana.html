Filix montana

Filix montana, commonly known as the mountain fern, is a species of fern native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, frond-like leaves that are typically bi- or tri-pinnate, giving it a lacy appearance. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories or along rocky slopes. Its reproductive structures, known as sori, are clustered on the underside of the fronds and produce spores for propagation.

Filix montana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable humidity and light conditions.
