Festuca occidentalis

Festuca occidentalis, commonly known as western fescue, is a perennial grass native to western North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, greenish-brown flowers. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in well-drained soils, making it suitable for rocky or sandy environments.

Festuca occidentalis is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.
