Fimbristylis multifolia

Fimbristylis multifolia, commonly known as the many-flowered flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along waterways. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, and it often serves as a habitat for various invertebrates.

Fimbristylis multifolia is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and supporting biodiversity in wetland ecosystems.
