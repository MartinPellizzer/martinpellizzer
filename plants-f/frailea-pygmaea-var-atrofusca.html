Frailea pygmaea var. atrofusca

Frailea pygmaea var. atrofusca, commonly known as the Black-veined Frosted Cactus, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to southern Africa.

It features short, cylindrical stems that are typically green with dark purple or blackish veins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the cooler months, usually in spring or early summer. It is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique coloration and compact size, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
