Ficus persicifolia var. glabripes

Ficus persicifolia var. glabripes, commonly known as the smooth-leaved fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, smooth leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is popular as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
