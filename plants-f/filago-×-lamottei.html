Filago × lamottei

Filago × lamottei, commonly known as the hybrid filago, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and hairy stems. The plant typically grows to a height of 30–60 cm, featuring alternate, lance-shaped leaves with serrated edges. Its flowers are yellowish to reddish, forming dense clusters at the tips of the stems, and it blooms from late summer to early autumn.

Filago × lamottei is often found in rocky or gravelly soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
