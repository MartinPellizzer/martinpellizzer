Flemingia pycnantha

Flemingia pycnantha, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Flemingia pycnantha is also valued for its ability to withstand drought conditions.
