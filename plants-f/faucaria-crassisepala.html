Faucaria crassisepala

Faucaria crassisepala, commonly known as the fat cushion plant, is a succulent native to South Africa.

It features thick, fleshy stems and leaves that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces small, star-shaped flowers in shades of yellow or orange during the spring. Its rosette growth pattern and ability to form offsets make it easy to propagate.

It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.
