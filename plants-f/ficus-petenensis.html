Ficus petenensis

Ficus petenensis, commonly known as the Maya fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often a deep green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically yellow or red in color. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Ficus petenensis is valued for its ecological role and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
