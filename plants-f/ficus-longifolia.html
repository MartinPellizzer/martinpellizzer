Ficus longifolia

Ficus longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe, characterized by its evergreen foliage and small, clustered fruits. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are elongated, glossy, and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are unisexual and clustered in inflorescences.

Ficus longifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
