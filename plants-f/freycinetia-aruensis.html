Freycinetia aruensis

Freycinetia aruensis, commonly known as the Aru palm, is a tropical plant native to the island of Aru in Indonesia.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible berries that are consumed by local communities.

Freycinetia aruensis is adapted to humid, coastal environments and plays a role in the local ecosystem by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
