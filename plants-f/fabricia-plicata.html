Fabricia plicata

Fabricia plicata, commonly known as the clustered cowpea, is a leguminous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in various cuisines. Fabricia plicata is also valued for its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil, making it a useful crop in sustainable agriculture.

Its adaptability to different soil types and climate conditions contributes to its widespread cultivation.
