Ficus altissima var. laccifera

Ficus altissima var. laccifera, commonly known as the lacquer fig, is a variant of the common fig tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often used in traditional crafts due to their durability. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically harvested when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This species is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its cultural significance in various regions.
