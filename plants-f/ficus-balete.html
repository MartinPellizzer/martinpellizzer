Ficus balete

Ficus balete, commonly known as the Philippine fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are often used in local cuisine. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The tree is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
