Franciscea eximia

Franciscea eximia, commonly known as the silverleaf franciscea, is a perennial shrub native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage, which is covered in fine, soft hairs. The plant produces small, white to pale purple flowers during the spring and summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
