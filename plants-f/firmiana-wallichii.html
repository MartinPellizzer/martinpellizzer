Firmiana wallichii

Firmiana wallichii, commonly known as the Chinese parasol tree, is a deciduous tree native to Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately lobed leaves that turn yellow in autumn. The tree produces fragrant, white flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its fruit is a woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade.
