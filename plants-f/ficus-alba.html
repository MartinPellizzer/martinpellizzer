Ficus alba

Ficus alba, commonly known as the white fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, light green leaves that are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces small, white flowers that are followed by edible, white figs. Ficus alba thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
