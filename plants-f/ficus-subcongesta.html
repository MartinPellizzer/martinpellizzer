Ficus subcongesta

Ficus subcongesta, commonly known as the African ficus or broad-leafed ficus, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, featuring a dense canopy of broad, glossy leaves. The plant has a robust root system and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types. Its leaves are typically elliptical or ovate, with a smooth margin and a dark green upper surface.

Ficus subcongesta is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some African cultures.
