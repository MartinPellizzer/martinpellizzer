Fritillaria meleagris var. boissieri

Fritillaria meleagris var. boissieri, commonly known as the Persian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or red with intricate patterns, often resembling a checkerboard design. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single stem that bears several flowers. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
