Ficus uniglandulosa var. parvifolia

Ficus uniglandulosa var. parvifolia, commonly known as the small-leaved fig.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae, characterized by its small, elliptical leaves with a smooth upper surface and a slightly hairy underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits. It is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in warm, humid environments.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its compact growth habit and attractive foliage.
