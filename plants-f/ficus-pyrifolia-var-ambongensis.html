Ficus pyrifolia var. ambongensis

Ficus pyrifolia var. ambongensis, commonly known as the Ambongensis Fig, is a unique variant of the fig tree native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-drained soil.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential ecological benefits in its native habitat.
