Ficus hispida var. rubra

Ficus hispida var. rubra, commonly known as the Red-veined Fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, waxy leaves that have a distinctive red vein pattern against a dark green background. The plant is evergreen and thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light. It has a compact growth habit, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Its ornamental value comes from its unique foliage, which adds visual interest to any space.
