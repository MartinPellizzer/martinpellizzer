Fontanesia argyi

Fontanesia argyi, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, compound leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that are followed by woody pods containing edible seeds. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a preference for well-drained soils in warm, humid climates.

The leaves are often used in traditional medicine, highlighting its ecological and cultural significance.
