Fagonia chilensis var. pachyacantha

Fagonia chilensis var. pachyacantha, commonly known as the Chilean fagonia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of Chile.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are adapted to withstand drought conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal and desert environments.

Its botanical features make it well-suited for xerophytic ecosystems.
