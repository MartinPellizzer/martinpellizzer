Festuca nardifolia var. calchaquiensis

Festuca nardifolia var. calchaquiensis, commonly known as the Calchaqui sedge, is a low-growing, perennial grass native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It typically forms dense, mat-like clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery bloom. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.

Due to its hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
