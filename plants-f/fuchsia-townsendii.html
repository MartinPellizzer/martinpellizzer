Fuchsia townsendii

Fuchsia townsendii, commonly known as the Townsend's fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep red to purple. The plant produces showy bracts that often resemble petals, enhancing its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative shrub.

Fuchsia townsendii is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
