Fritillaria striata

Fritillaria striata, commonly known as the striated fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish with contrasting black markings. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it blooms in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
