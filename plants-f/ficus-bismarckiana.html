Ficus bismarckiana

Ficus bismarckiana, commonly known as the Bismarck banyan, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive aerial root system that gives it a unique, tree-like appearance. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The Bismarck banyan is also notable for its ability to grow into a massive tree with a broad canopy.
