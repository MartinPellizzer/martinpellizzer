Fagara affinis

Fagara affinis, commonly known as the Caribbean bitterleaf, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Berberidaceae and is characterized by its palmate leaves with five to seven lobes. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes, followed by red or orange berries. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.
