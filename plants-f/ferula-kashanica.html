Ferula kashanica

Ferula kashanica, commonly known as Kashan wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Iran.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes many aromatic plants such as carrots and parsley. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its leaves are finely divided and have a distinctive, somewhat aromatic odor.
