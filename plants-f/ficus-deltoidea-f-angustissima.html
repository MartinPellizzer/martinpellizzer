Ficus deltoidea f. angustissima

Ficus deltoidea f. angustissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a rare variant of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. It produces small, inedible figs that are not typically consumed by humans.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
