Fritillaria chlorantha

Fritillaria chlorantha, commonly known as the green starflower, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking greenish-yellow flowers that resemble stars, often with a contrasting purple or red border. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and is known for its ability to thrive in rocky, well-drained soils. It blooms in early spring and is often found in alpine meadows and woodland edges.

Fritillaria chlorantha is valued for its ornamental beauty and is also studied for its potential medicinal properties.
