Ficus populnea subvar. longipetiolata

Ficus populnea subvar. longipetiolata, commonly known as the long-petioled fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that have long petioles, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and tropical landscapes.
