Festuca muricata

Festuca muricata, commonly known as the hairy fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, dark green foliage. The plant features slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, spikelet-based flowers. Festuca muricata is often found in moist, well-drained soils in meadows, woodland edges, and along stream banks.

Its adaptability to various environmental conditions makes it a common ornamental and ecological plant in gardens and natural habitats.
