Falcatifolium falciforme

Falcatifolium falciforme, commonly known as the sickle-leaf fern, is a tropical plant characterized by its distinctive, sickle-shaped leaves.

It belongs to the family Polypodiaceae and is native to humid regions of Southeast Asia. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in forest canopies. Its foliage is often bright green and can reach lengths of up to 1 meter.
