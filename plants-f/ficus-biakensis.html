Ficus biakensis

Ficus biakensis, commonly known as the Biak fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Indonesian island of Biak and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Ficus biakensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
