Festuca stuebelii

Festuca stuebelii, commonly known as Stuebel's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Festuca stuebelii is often found in moist, calcareous soils in meadows and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
