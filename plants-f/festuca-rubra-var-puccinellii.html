Festuca rubra var. puccinellii

Festuca rubra var. puccinellii, commonly known as red fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dense, mat-forming structure. The leaves are typically narrow, blue-green in color, and often have a bluish or silver sheen. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for a variety of habitats.

This variety is often used in landscaping and ecological restoration due to its hardiness and aesthetic appeal.
