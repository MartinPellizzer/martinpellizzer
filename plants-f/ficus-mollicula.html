Ficus mollicula

Ficus mollicula, commonly known as the velvet fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to the tropical regions of Central and South America.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage, which gives it its common name and makes it a popular ornamental plant. The leaves are typically glossy and dark green, with a smooth texture that feels soft to the touch. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by small, round fruits.

This plant is relatively easy to care for, making it a favored choice for indoor gardening.
