Ficus sangumae

Ficus sangumae, commonly known as the Sangumae fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green in color. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus sangumae is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in warm, humid environments.
