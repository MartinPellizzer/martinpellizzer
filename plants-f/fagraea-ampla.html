Fagraea ampla

Fagraea ampla, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters during the rainy season. The plant has compound leaves that are opposite and have a smooth texture. It produces woody capsules containing small, dark seeds.

Fagraea ampla is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
