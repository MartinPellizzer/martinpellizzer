Fortunaea chinensis

Fortunaea chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, bright purple berries that persist through winter, adding ornamental value to the landscape. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and slightly hairy. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for gardens in temperate climates.

The berries are not only visually appealing but also attract birds, contributing to ecological diversity.
