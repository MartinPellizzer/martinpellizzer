Festuca ebeliana

Festuca ebeliana, commonly known as the Ebelian fescue, is a species of grass native to the mountainous regions of Central and Eastern Europe.

It is a perennial plant characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils, often in alpine or subalpine environments. Its flowering spikes are usually compact and can range in color from green to reddish-brown.

Festuca ebeliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in challenging ecological conditions.
