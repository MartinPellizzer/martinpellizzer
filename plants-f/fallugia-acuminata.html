Fallugia acuminata

Fallugia acuminata, commonly known as the narrowleaf sandthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, featuring small, narrow leaves that help reduce water loss. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are followed by orange to red berries containing seeds. Its deep root system allows it to thrive in poor soil conditions and withstand drought.

Fallugia acuminata plays an important role in stabilizing sandy soils and providing habitat for local wildlife.
