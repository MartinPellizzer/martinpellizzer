Ficus oleifolia var. riparia

Ficus oleifolia var. riparia, commonly known as the river fig, is a tropical plant native to regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found near water sources.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological gardening.
