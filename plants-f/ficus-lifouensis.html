Ficus lifouensis

Ficus lifouensis, commonly known as the Lifou fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the island of Lifou in the Loyalty Islands, part of New Caledonia. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, often found in moist, tropical environments.

The fruit, a fig, is edible and plays a role in local ecosystems and traditional uses.
