Fraxinus angustifolia var. oxycarpa

Fraxinus angustifolia var. oxycarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf ash, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It features slender, elongated leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long and have a distinctive, serrated margin. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by elongated, winged fruits. Its bark is smooth and grayish in youth, becoming fissured with age.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
