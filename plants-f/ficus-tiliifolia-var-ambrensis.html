Ficus tiliifolia var. ambrensis

Ficus tiliifolia var. ambrensis, commonly known as the Ambrensis Fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically dark green with a leathery texture. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
