Ficus microcarpa var. nitida

Ficus microcarpa var. nitida, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical evergreen tree characterized by its distinctive cascading foliage.

It features dark green, glossy leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, greenish fruits that turn yellow when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a decorative ornamental plant.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor landscaping.
