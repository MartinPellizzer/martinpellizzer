Fumaria parviflora subvar. schrammii

Fumaria parviflora subvar. schrammii, commonly known as Schramm's small-flowered fumitory, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, pinkish-yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks. Its slender stems are covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
