Ficus natalensis subsp. leprieurii

Ficus natalensis subsp.

leprieurii, commonly known as the Natal fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family. It is native to southern Africa, particularly South Africa, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates.

The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

The fruit, which is a small, round fig, is edible and has a mild, sweet flavor.
