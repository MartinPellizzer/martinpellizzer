Fuchsia encliandra subsp. microphylloides

Fuchsia encliandra subsp.

microphylloides, commonly known as the small-leaved fuchsia, is a shrub native to New Zealand. It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of tubular, pink to red flowers that bloom in spring.

The leaves are small, elliptical, and often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in coastal and mountainous regions.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
