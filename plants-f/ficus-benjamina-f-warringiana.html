Ficus benjamina f. warringiana

Ficus benjamina f. warringiana, commonly known as the Warringa fig, is a cultivar of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or cream patterns. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It produces small, inedible fig fruits that are not typically consumed by humans.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor conditions.
