Ficus dulciaria

Ficus dulciaria, commonly known as the sweet fig tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into edible fruits resembling figs. Ficus dulciaria thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a popular ornamental and edible plant in horticulture.
