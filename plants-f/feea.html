Feea

Feea, commonly known as the blueberry plant, is a deciduous shrub native to the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces edible berries that are rich in antioxidants and vitamins. Its leaves are elliptical and have a slightly waxy texture, while its roots are fibrous and adapted to acidic soils.

The plant thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
