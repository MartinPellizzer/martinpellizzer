Ficus subincisa

Ficus subincisa, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are often slightly curled at the edges. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically small and round.

Ficus subincisa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
