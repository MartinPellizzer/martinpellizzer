Flaveria bidentis

Flaveria bidentis, commonly known as the two-toothed flaveria, is an annual flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite, linear leaves with two distinct teeth at the base. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the summer months. It is often found in disturbed soils and arid environments, making it a common weed in agricultural areas.

Botanically, it belongs to the sunflower family (Asteraceae) and is known for its rapid growth and adaptability to various soil conditions.
