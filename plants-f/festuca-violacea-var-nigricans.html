Festuca violacea var. nigricans

Festuca violacea var. nigricans, commonly known as black false brome, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark purple to black stems and leaves, which give it a striking appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils. Its flowers are small and clustered in dense spikes, often appearing in late spring to early summer.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
