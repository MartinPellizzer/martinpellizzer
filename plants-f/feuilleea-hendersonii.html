Feuilleea hendersonii

Feuilleea hendersonii, commonly known as the Henderson's leaf plant, is a rare and unique species native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that are often variegated with shades of green, white, and yellow. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, thriving in the understory of dense forests. Its foliage is not only visually appealing but also plays a crucial role in its photosynthetic processes.

Due to its limited habitat and slow growth rate, it is considered a conservation concern.
