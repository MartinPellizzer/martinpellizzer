Furcraea occidentalis

Furcraea occidentalis, commonly known as the West Indian sword fern, is a striking plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is a member of the Asphodelaceae family and is characterized by its tall, sword-like leaves that can reach lengths of up to 2 meters. The plant produces striking yellow flowers in terminal clusters, which are followed by small, triangular fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for tropical gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in warm climates.
