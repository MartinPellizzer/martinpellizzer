Ficus chiribiquetensis

Ficus chiribiquetensis, commonly known as the Chiribiquet fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Colombia. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, clustered fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
