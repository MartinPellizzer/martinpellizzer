Ficus pyrrhocarpa

Ficus pyrrhocarpa, commonly known as the red-fig or scarlet fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green with a smooth texture. Its fruit, which is small and red when ripe, is edible and often used in local cuisines.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
