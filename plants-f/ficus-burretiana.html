Ficus burretiana

Ficus burretiana, commonly known as the Burret's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Ficus burretiana is known for its adaptability and ability to grow in a variety of soil types.
