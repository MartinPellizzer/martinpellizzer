Fuchsia × hybrida

Fuchsia × hybrida, commonly known as the common fuchsia, is a hybrid shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pendulous flowers that come in a wide range of colors, including shades of red, pink, purple, and white. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters and has opposite, simple, ovate leaves with a hairy texture. Its flowers are borne in clusters and are pollinated primarily by birds, especially hummingbirds.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes in gardens and as a houseplant due to its vibrant blooms and adaptability.
