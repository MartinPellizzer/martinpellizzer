Festuca × subspicata

Festuca × subspicata, commonly known as tall fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its coarse, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, two-lobed flowers. It is highly adaptable and thrives in a variety of soil types, making it a popular choice for lawns and pastures.

However, some varieties may contain toxic endophytic fungi, which can be harmful to livestock.
