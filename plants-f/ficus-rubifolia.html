Ficus rubifolia

Ficus rubifolia, commonly known as the red-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often develop a reddish tinge when exposed to direct sunlight. Ficus rubifolia is an evergreen shrub or small tree that can grow up to 6 meters in height under optimal conditions.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically consumed by local wildlife and humans alike.
