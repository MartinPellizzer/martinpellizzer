Ficus lepicarpa var. pedunculata

Ficus lepicarpa var. pedunculata, commonly known as the pedunculate fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are generally small and round. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a food source in its native habitat.
