Festuca kashmiriana var. simlensis

Festuca kashmiriana var. simlensis, commonly known as Kashmir grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Himalayan regions of Pakistan and India.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers, which are adapted for wind pollination. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
