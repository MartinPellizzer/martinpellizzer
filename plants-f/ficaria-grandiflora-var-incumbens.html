Ficaria grandiflora var. incumbens

Ficaria grandiflora var. incumbens, commonly known as the large-flowered ficaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and features small, rounded leaves with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces clusters of bright yellow, cruciform flowers that bloom in early summer. Native to southern Africa, it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and blooms.
