Festuca rigurosa

Festuca rigurosa, commonly known as rigid fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Eurasia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Festuca rigurosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and as a ground cover in suitable climates.
