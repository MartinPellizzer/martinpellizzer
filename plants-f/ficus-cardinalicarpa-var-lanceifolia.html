Ficus cardinalicarpa var. lanceifolia

Ficus cardinalicarpa var. lanceifolia, commonly known as the lance-leaf fig, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

The lanceifolia variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
