Fritillaria alburyana

Fritillaria alburyana, commonly known as the Albury fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to Australia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or red, with a distinctive black or purple blotch at the base. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous underground stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Fritillaria alburyana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
