Festuca balcanica subsp. neicevii

Festuca balcanica subsp.

neicevii, commonly known as the Balkan bentgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Balkan Peninsula. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of spikelets, which are usually pale green to reddish in color.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
