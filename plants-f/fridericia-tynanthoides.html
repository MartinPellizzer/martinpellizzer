Fridericia tynanthoides

Fridericia tynanthoides, commonly known as the yellow jessamine, is a flowering plant native to the southeastern United States.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has opposite, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape and often have a slight pubescence. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Fridericia tynanthoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
