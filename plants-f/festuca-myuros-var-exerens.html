Festuca myuros var. exerens

Festuca myuros var. exerens, commonly known as creeping fescue, is a cool-season grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This variety is often found in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.

The plant is also known for its ability to form dense mats, making it effective for erosion control.
