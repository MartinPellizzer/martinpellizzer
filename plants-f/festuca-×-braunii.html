Festuca × braunii

Festuca × braunii, commonly known as the hybrid fescue, is a perennial grass species that exhibits a range of botanical characteristics.

It is a hybrid between Festuca rubra and Festuca ovina, resulting in a plant with a dense, tufted growth habit. The leaves are narrow, grass-like, and often have a bluish-green hue, while the inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a compact, cylindrical shape. It is typically found in moist, well-drained soils and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value.

This plant is also valued for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
