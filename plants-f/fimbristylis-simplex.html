Fimbristylis simplex

Fimbristylis simplex, commonly known as the slender flat sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are usually green or brown and lack showy petals, typical of many sedges.

Fimbristylis simplex plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
