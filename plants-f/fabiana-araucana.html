Fabiana araucana

Fabiana araucana, commonly known as the Chilean fire bush, is a South American shrub native to the Andes.

It is characterized by its vibrant red-orange flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. The plant has aromatic, slightly hairy leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for coastal and temperate climates.

Its ornamental value and ecological role make it a popular choice for gardens and conservation efforts.
