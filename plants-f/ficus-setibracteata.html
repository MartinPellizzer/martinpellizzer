Ficus setibracteata

Ficus setibracteata, commonly known as the bracteate fig.

It is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family, native to Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, ovate leaves that are usually dark green in color. It produces small, edible figs that are typically green when mature.

Ficus setibracteata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
