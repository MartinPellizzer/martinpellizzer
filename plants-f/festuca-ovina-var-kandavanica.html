Festuca ovina var. kandavanica

Festuca ovina var. kandavanica, commonly known as Kandavan sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to high-altitude regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green blades that are often covered in fine hairs. This variety is adapted to cold, arid environments and thrives in well-drained soils. It plays a significant role in stabilizing mountainous and alpine landscapes due to its hardy nature.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
