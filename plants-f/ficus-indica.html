Ficus indica

Ficus indica, commonly known as the Indian rubber tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. The leaves are typically large, heart-shaped, and have a waxy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by round, greenish fruits.

The plant is valued for its rubber latex and is also cultivated for ornamental purposes.
