Festuca communis var. rubra

Festuca communis var. rubra, commonly known as red fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green leaves and reddish stems, which give it its distinctive appearance. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides. It has a tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Festuca communis var. rubra is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes used in landscaping and erosion control.
