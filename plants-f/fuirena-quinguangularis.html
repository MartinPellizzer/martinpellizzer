Fuirena quinguangularis

Fuirena quinguangularis, commonly known as the five-angled bellflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive five-angled stems and bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are typically lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in gardens.
