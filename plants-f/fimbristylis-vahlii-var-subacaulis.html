Fimbristylis vahlii var. subacaulis

Fimbristylis vahlii var. subacaulis, commonly known as the slender spike-rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, often found in marshy areas or along the edges of water bodies. The plant has a dense, grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female structures, and are often wind-pollinated.

The species is characterized by its compact growth habit and is adapted to tolerate waterlogged conditions.
