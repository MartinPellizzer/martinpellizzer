Fremontodendron californicum subsp. crassifolium

Fremontodendron californicum subsp.

crassifolium, commonly known as the California buckthorn, is a shrub native to coastal regions of California. It features broad, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its bark is smooth and grayish, and it often grows in sandy or rocky soils.

It is well adapted to coastal climates and is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental value.
