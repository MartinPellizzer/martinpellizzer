Fremontodendron decumbens

Fremontodendron decumbens, commonly known as the desert four o'clock, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its woody stems and clusters of showy yellow flowers that bloom in the late afternoon. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing adaptations such as thick, fleshy leaves to conserve water. Its leaves are typically gray-green with a waxy coating, and its flowers open in response to sunlight, hence its common name.

Fremontodendron decumbens is often found in rocky soils and along canyon walls, contributing to the ecological diversity of its habitat.
