Ficus sycomoroides

Ficus sycomoroides, commonly known as the African sycamore, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The tree produces small, greenish flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a valuable species for reforestation and ornamental purposes.
