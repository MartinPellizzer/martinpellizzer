Ferula macrocolea

Ferula macrocolea, commonly known as the giant fennel, is a tall, herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its hollow, cylindrical stems and large, feathery leaves that resemble those of fennel. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbrella-like clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its aromatic seeds are used in traditional medicine and as a spice.

Ferula macrocolea is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in dry, rocky soils.
