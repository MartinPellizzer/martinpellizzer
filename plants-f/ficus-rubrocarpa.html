Ficus rubrocarpa

Ficus rubrocarpa, commonly known as the red-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often turn reddish in color under certain environmental conditions. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, spherical fruits.

Ficus rubrocarpa is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
