Flacourtia prunifolia

Flacourtia prunifolia, commonly known as the rose-apple tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree with smooth, greyish bark and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, white or pale yellow flowers that are followed by edible, rose-scented fruits resembling small apples. Its botanical features include simple, alternate leaves and a preference for well-drained, fertile soils.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.
