Festuca tarmensis

Festuca tarmensis, commonly known as Tarmian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or steppe environments.

Festuca tarmensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
