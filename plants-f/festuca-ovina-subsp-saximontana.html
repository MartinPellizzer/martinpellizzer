Festuca ovina subsp. saximontana

Festuca ovina subsp.

saximontana, commonly known as the alpine fescue, is a perennial grass species native to mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, slender leaves, and dense, flowering inflorescences that appear in late summer.

The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky slopes. Its fibrous root system helps stabilize soil in harsh mountain conditions. Festuca ovina subsp.

saximontana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in high-altitude ecosystems.
