Ficus rotundifolia

Ficus rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf fig, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features broad, rounded leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor settings.
