Festuca stipoides var. conferta

Festuca stipoides var. conferta, commonly known as dense feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, silver-gray foliage that forms dense clumps. The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
