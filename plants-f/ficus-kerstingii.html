Ficus kerstingii

Ficus kerstingii, commonly known as the Kersting's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the island of Socotra, located in the Arabian Sea, and is characterized by its unique and striking appearance. The plant features thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white coating, which helps it retain moisture in its arid environment. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits.

Ficus kerstingii is also notable for its ability to thrive in harsh conditions, making it a resilient and distinctive species.
