Ficus infectoria var. wightiana

Ficus infectoria var. wightiana, commonly known as the African fig or Wight's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

The species is also noted for its ability to thrive in indoor environments with proper care.
