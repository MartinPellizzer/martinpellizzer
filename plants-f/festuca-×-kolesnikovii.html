Festuca × kolesnikovii

Festuca × kolesnikovii, commonly known as Kolesnikov's fescue, is a hybrid grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces spikelets that are often clustered in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, and its flowers are small and wind-pollinated. It is adapted to grow in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens and landscapes.
