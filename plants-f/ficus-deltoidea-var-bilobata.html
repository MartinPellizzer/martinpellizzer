Ficus deltoidea var. bilobata

Ficus deltoidea var. bilobata, commonly known as the Split-leaf Fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, deeply lobed leaves that give it a unique, almost fern-like appearance. The plant is a member of the Moraceae family and is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

The leaves are typically dark green and glossy, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
