Ficus hirta var. triloba

Ficus hirta var. triloba, commonly known as the three-lobed fig, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 6 meters. The plant features trifoliate leaves, with each leaflet having a distinctive three-lobed shape. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in inflorescences, and it produces edible figs that are often consumed by local populations.

The plant is well adapted to humid, tropical climates and thrives in well-drained soil.
