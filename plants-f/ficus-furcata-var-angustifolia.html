Ficus furcata var. angustifolia

Ficus furcata var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
