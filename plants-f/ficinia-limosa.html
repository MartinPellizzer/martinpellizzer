Ficinia limosa

Ficinia limosa, commonly known as the swamp rush, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to wetland environments in tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in dense stands, often in marshy or seasonally flooded areas, and has slender, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences, which are usually green or reddish in color. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread rapidly, forming extensive colonies in suitable moist habitats.

Ficinia limosa plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and semi-aquatic organisms.
