Ficus changuensis

Ficus changuensis, commonly known as the Changu fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Cambodia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, broad leaves and produces small, edible figs that are often consumed by local communities. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring moist, well-drained soil.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
