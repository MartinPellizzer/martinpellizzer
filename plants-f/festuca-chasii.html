Festuca chasii

Festuca chasii, commonly known as the Chasii fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue to purple flowers. Festuca chasii is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand harsh environmental conditions.

Its ecological role includes stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
