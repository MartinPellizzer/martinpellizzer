Filipendula multijuga var. ciliata

Filipendula multijuga var. ciliata, commonly known as the hairy meadow sweet, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves with soft, hairy surfaces. The plant produces clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and wetlands.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and conservation areas.
