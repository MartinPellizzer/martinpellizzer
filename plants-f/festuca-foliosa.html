Festuca foliosa

Festuca foliosa, commonly known as slender fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny seeds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its foliage is often used in horticulture for ornamental purposes due to its delicate appearance.

Festuca foliosa is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
