Ficus mutisii

Ficus mutisii, commonly known as the Mutsi fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Mexico and Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered fig fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Ficus mutisii is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
