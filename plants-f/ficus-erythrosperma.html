Ficus erythrosperma

Ficus erythrosperma, commonly known as the red-fruited fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible, reddish-purple fruits. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus erythrosperma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
