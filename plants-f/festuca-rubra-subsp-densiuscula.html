Festuca rubra subsp. densiuscula

Festuca rubra subsp.

densiuscula, commonly known as red fescue, is a perennial grass species characterized by its fine, dense foliage and tufted growth habit. It typically grows in temperate regions and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually dark green in color, with a bluish tint under certain conditions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are typically pink to reddish in hue.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in ecological restoration projects due to its hardiness and adaptability.
