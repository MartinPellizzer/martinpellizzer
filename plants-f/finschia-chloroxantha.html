Finschia chloroxantha

Finschia chloroxantha, commonly known as the yellow sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the arid regions of central Australia.

It belongs to the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and succulent stems. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the cooler months. Its leaves are narrow and fleshy, adapted to conserve water in its harsh environment.

Finschia chloroxantha is well-suited for xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
