Fritillaria koidzumiana

Fritillaria koidzumiana, commonly known as the Koidzumi fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and eastern Asia.

It produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or orange in color, often with intricate patterns. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in harsh climates, often going dormant during dry periods.

Fritillaria koidzumiana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental flowers and ecological importance in its native habitats.
