Ferula dshizakensis

Ferula dshizakensis, commonly known as the Afghan cow parsley, is a member of the Umbelliferae family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, particularly Afghanistan and Pakistan. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stem and umbel-like inflorescence composed of small white to pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are pinnately divided and have a strong, aromatic scent.

Ferula dshizakensis is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
