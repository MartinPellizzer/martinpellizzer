Festuca bartherei

Festuca bartherei, commonly known as Barthere's fescue, is a perennial grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, temperate climates and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along streams. The plant has fine, wiry leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in ecological restoration projects.

Festuca bartherei is relatively hardy and can thrive in a variety of soil types.
