Ficinia tristachya var. bibracteata

Ficinia tristachya var. bibracteata, commonly known as the bibracteate flax, is a flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with white or pale blue flowers. It thrives in wet, boggy habitats and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its unique morphology and ecological significance, it is of interest to botanists and conservationists.
