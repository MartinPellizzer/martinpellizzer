Ficus cuspidifera

Ficus cuspidifera, commonly known as the pointed-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are often pointed at the tips. The plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green or yellow when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus cuspidifera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
