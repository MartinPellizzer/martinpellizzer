Festuca kronenburgii

Festuca kronenburgii, commonly known as the Kronenburg fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to greenish flowers.

Festuca kronenburgii is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shady conditions.
