Fimbristylis cyperoides

Fimbristylis cyperoides, commonly known as the narrowleaf flatsedge, is a species of flowering plant in the sedge family (Cyperaceae).

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wetland habitats, including marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. The plant has grass-like leaves that are narrow and linear, with a dense, tufted growth habit. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikes and are often surrounded by bracts.

Fimbristylis cyperoides plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
