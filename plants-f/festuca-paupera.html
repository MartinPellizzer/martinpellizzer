Festuca paupera

Festuca paupera, commonly known as slender fescue, is a low-growing perennial grass native to Eurasia.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growth and has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Festuca paupera is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
