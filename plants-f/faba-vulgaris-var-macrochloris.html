Faba vulgaris var. macrochloris

Faba vulgaris var. macrochloris, commonly known as the broad-leaf vetch, is a leguminous plant characterized by its robust, sprawling growth habit.

It features trifoliate leaves with broad, ovate leaflets and produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant is notable for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through a symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in agricultural settings for soil enrichment.

Its adaptability makes it a valuable cover crop and forage plant in temperate regions.
