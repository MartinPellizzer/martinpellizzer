Fuchsia chonotica

Fuchsia chonotica, commonly known as the Andean fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains in South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from deep red to violet, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. The plant produces small, edible berries that are consumed by various wildlife species. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Fuchsia chonotica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections worldwide.
