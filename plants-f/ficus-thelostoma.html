Ficus thelostoma

Ficus thelostoma, commonly known as the silver fig or the white-veined fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent white veins, which give it its common name. Ficus thelostoma is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive indoors.

It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
