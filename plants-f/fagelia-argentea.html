Fagelia argentea

Fagelia argentea, commonly known as silverleaf fagelia, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking silver-white undersides on its broad, ovate leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by edible, round fruits resembling small cherries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections for its aesthetic appeal.
