Ficus subpedunculata

Ficus subpedunculata, commonly known as the small-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are small and oval in shape, often with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by edible, green figs. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus subpedunculata is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
