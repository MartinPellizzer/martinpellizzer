Fagus ferruginea var. caroliniana

Fagus ferruginea var. caroliniana, commonly known as the Carolina chestnut, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and beeches, and is characterized by its serrated leaves and distinctive chestnut-like fruits. The tree produces edible nuts enclosed in a spiny husk, which are an important food source for wildlife.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates with adequate sunlight.
