Ferula foeniculacea

Ferula foeniculacea, commonly known as the Persian cow parsley, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces a distinctive, aromatic resin that has been historically used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are finely divided and resemble those of parsley, giving it its common name.

Ferula foeniculacea is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.
