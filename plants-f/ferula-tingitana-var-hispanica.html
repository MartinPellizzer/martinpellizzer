Ferula tingitana var. hispanica

Ferula tingitana var. hispanica, commonly known as Spanish cow's milk, is a perennial herb native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant produces a milky sap, which is the source of its common name. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, aromatic scent.

It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean climates.
