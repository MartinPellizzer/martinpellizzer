Fuchsia campii

Fuchsia campii, commonly known as the Campbell's fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous clusters of tubular flowers that range in color from deep red to violet. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 2 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in a fine layer of white hairs.

Fuchsia campii thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
