Freycinetia batanensis

Freycinetia batanensis, commonly known as the Batan palm, is a flowering plant species native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its slender, unbranched trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in panicles, followed by dark blue, fleshy fruits that are edible. It thrives in coastal and lowland areas, often found in mangrove ecosystems.

Freycinetia batanensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
