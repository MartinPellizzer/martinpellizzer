Ficus trichocerasa

Ficus trichocerasa, commonly known as the African fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered fruit. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually smooth and have a glossy appearance, while the fruit is edible and contains a single seed.

Ficus trichocerasa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
