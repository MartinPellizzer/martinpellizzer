Festuca pseudotrichophylla

Festuca pseudotrichophylla, commonly known as the slender fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, bluish-green leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments. Its inflorescence consists of a compact, cylindrical spikelet with small, two-ranked spikelets.

Festuca pseudotrichophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
