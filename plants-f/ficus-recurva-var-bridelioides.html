Ficus recurva var. bridelioides

Ficus recurva var. bridelioides, commonly known as the Bridelioides fig, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features glossy, dark green, ovate leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, bushy form. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round figs that are often consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening in tropical and subtropical climates.
