Fuchsia bracelinae

Fuchsia bracelinae, commonly known as the velvet fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep purple to bright red. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are typically covered in a fine, velvety texture. Fuchsia bracelinae thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique floral structure and its role in local ecosystems.
