Fagopyrum rubrifolium

Fagopyrum rubrifolium, commonly known as red-leaf buckwheat, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red-tinged leaves and small, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer. The plant belongs to the Polygonaceae family, which includes other familiar species like buckwheat and sorrel. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Fagopyrum rubrifolium thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested or rocky habitats.
