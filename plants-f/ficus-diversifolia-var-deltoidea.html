Ficus diversifolia var. deltoidea

Ficus diversifolia var. deltoidea, commonly known as the deltoid fig, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a smooth surface. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and have a sweet, fig-like flavor. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
