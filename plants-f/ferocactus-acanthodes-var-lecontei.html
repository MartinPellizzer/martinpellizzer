Ferocactus acanthodes var. lecontei

Ferocactus acanthodes var. lecontei, commonly known as the Leconte's prickly pear, is a species of cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are covered in sharp, yellowish spines. The plant produces vibrant yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its fruit, known as a "tuna," is edible and contains a sweet, pulpy interior.

This variety is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water once established.
