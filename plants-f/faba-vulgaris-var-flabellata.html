Faba vulgaris var. flabellata

Faba vulgaris var. flabellata, commonly known as the broad-leaf vetch, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically broad and heart-shaped, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used as a food source for wildlife. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found growing in disturbed soils or along roadsides.

Its ability to fix nitrogen makes it an important plant in soil enrichment and ecological restoration efforts.
