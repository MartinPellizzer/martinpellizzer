Ficus caulocarpa var. dasycarpa

Ficus caulocarpa var. dasycarpa, commonly known as the woolly fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, greenish fruits that mature to a reddish color. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically growing up to 5 meters in height.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
