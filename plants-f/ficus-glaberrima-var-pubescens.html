Ficus glaberrima var. pubescens

Ficus glaberrima var. pubescens, commonly known as the pubescent fig, is a variant of the Ficus glaberrima species native to tropical regions of Africa.

This plant is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are slightly hairy on the underside, giving it its "pubescens" designation. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits that are often consumed by both humans and animals. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its botanical features make it an important species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
