Ficus wenzelii

Ficus wenzelii, commonly known as the Wenzel's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically broad and ovate in shape. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits.

Ficus wenzelii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
