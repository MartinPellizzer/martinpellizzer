Festuca heteromalla

Festuca heteromalla, commonly known as bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green, bluish-gray foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in prairies and woodland edges. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to whitish flowers.

Festuca heteromalla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass mixes.
