Fritillaria discolor

Fritillaria discolor, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale purple, often with a distinctive spotted pattern. The plant is characterized by its lance-shaped leaves and is typically found in rocky or forested habitats. Fritillaria discolor is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional Chinese medicine.

It requires well-drained soil and partial shade to thrive.
