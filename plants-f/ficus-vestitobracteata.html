Ficus vestitobracteata

Ficus vestitobracteata, commonly known as the cloth-bract fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
