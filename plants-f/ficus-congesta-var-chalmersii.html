Ficus congesta var. chalmersii

Ficus congesta var. chalmersii, commonly known as the Chalmers fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. It produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by round, green fig fruits.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
