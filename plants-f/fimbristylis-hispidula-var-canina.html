Fimbristylis hispidula var. canina

Fimbristylis hispidula var. canina, commonly known as the hairy fringed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist environments, showcasing its adaptability to waterlogged soils. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves with a hairy texture, which contributes to its common name. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, often with three to six stamens.

The botanical characteristics of this species make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
