Fagopyrum macropterum var. emarginatum

Fagopyrum macropterum var. emarginatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf buckwheat, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by triangular, papery seed pods. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
