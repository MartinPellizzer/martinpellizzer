Ficus panduriformis

Ficus panduriformis, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called syconia.

Ficus panduriformis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
