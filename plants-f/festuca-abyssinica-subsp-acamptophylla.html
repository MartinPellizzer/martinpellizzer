Festuca abyssinica subsp. acamptophylla

Festuca abyssinica subsp.

acamptophylla, commonly known as Abyssinian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to high-altitude regions of Ethiopia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically silver-green in color.

The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, spikelet-based flowers. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to rocky or well-drained soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
