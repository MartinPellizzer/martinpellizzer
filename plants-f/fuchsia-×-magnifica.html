Fuchsia × magnifica

Fuchsia × magnifica, commonly known as the giant fuchsia, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Onagraceae family.

It is native to the Andes Mountains in South America and is characterized by its large, showy flowers. The plant produces pendulous clusters of vibrant pink or purple flowers, which bloom in late summer to early autumn. Its foliage consists of glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement.

Fuchsia × magnifica is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses due to its striking appearance and relatively easy care requirements.
