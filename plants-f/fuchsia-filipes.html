Fuchsia filipes

Fuchsia filipes, commonly known as the silverleaf fuchsia, is a member of the Onagraceae family.

It is native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina. This shrub is characterized by its silvery-gray foliage and showy, pendulous flowers that bloom in shades of pink and purple. The leaves are lance-shaped and often covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs.

It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its striking appearance.
