Ficus gratiosa

Ficus gratiosa, commonly known as the graceful ficus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features smooth, waxy, light green leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, greenish-white fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Ficus gratiosa is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
