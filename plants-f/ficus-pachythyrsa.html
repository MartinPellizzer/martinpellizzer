Ficus pachythyrsa

Ficus pachythyrsa, commonly known as the thick-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, thick, and glossy leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
