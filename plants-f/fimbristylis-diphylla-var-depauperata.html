Fimbristylis diphylla var. depauperata

Fimbristylis diphylla var. depauperata, commonly known as the sparse-flowered flat sedge, is a member of the Cyperaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, clustered flower heads. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and swales. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a dense, fibrous root system that helps stabilize soil.

The inflorescence consists of a spikelet-like structure with short, bract-like leaves and a single small flower.
