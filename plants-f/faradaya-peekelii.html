Faradaya peekelii

Faradaya peekelii, commonly known as the Cape gold plant, is a succulent shrub native to South Africa.

It features fleshy, cylindrical stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces bright yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. Its leaves are narrow and pointed, often covered in a fine layer of white powder, which helps reduce water loss.

Faradaya peekelii is well-suited for rock gardens and xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental appeal.
