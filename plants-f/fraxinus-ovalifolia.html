Fraxinus ovalifolia

Fraxinus ovalifolia, commonly known as the oval-leaf ash, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate to elliptic leaves that are typically 5 to 10 centimeters long. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins during the spring, followed by winged fruits that disperse its seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The plant has moderate drought tolerance and is valued for its ornamental and ecological benefits.
