Ficus oxymitroides var. brevipes

Ficus oxymitroides var. brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with a dense canopy and smooth, grayish-brown bark. The plant produces small, round fig fruits that are typically green when mature. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a waxy texture.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
