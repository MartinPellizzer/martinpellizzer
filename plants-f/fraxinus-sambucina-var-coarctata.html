Fraxinus sambucina var. coarctata

Fraxinus sambucina var. coarctata, commonly known as the narrowleaf ash, is a variant of the European ash tree characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories. The leaves are usually dark green on top and lighter underneath, with a serrated margin. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by winged seeds dispersed by wind.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
