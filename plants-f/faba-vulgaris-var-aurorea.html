Faba vulgaris var. aurorea

Faba vulgaris var. aurorea, commonly known as the golden bean, is a variant of the common fava bean characterized by its striking yellow foliage and pods.

This plant is a legume, belonging to the Fabaceae family, and is native to regions of Europe and the Mediterranean. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. The leaves are trifoliate, with a deep green color, while the flowers are typically pale yellow to white.

The pods contain edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients, making it a valuable crop in both culinary and agricultural contexts.
