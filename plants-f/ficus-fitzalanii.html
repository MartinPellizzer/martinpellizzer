Ficus fitzalanii

Ficus fitzalanii, commonly known as the Fitzalan's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. It produces small, fig-like fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Ficus fitzalanii is also popular as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to adapt to indoor environments.
