Fritillaria delphinensis subsp. burnatii

Fritillaria delphinensis subsp.

burnatii, commonly known as the Burnat's fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans. It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color, often with intricate patterns.

The plant produces a single, upright stem that bears several flowers, which bloom in early spring. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in rocky, well-drained soils, and it thrives in cool, humid environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with appropriate conditions.
