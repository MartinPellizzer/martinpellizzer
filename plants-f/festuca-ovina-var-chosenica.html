Festuca ovina var. chosenica

Festuca ovina var. chosenica, commonly known as the Chosenica sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to central and eastern Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and grasslands. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and as a ground cover.

The plant is also noted for its ability to tolerate moderate foot traffic and drought conditions.
