Ficus austrocaledonica var. austrocaledonica

Ficus austrocaledonica var. austrocaledonica, commonly known as the Kauai fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the island of Kauai in Hawaii.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in its native habitat.
