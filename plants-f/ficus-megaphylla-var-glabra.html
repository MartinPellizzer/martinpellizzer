Ficus megaphylla var. glabra

Ficus megaphylla var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, and smooth-textured leaves that are typically a deep green in color. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are often used in traditional cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
