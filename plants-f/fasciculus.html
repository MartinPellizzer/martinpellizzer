Fasciculus

Fasciculus, commonly known as the clustered grass or fascicle grass, is a type of flowering plant belonging to the family Poaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit, with multiple stems growing from a single base. The plant typically has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Fasciculus is often found in moist, temperate environments and is valued for its ornamental appeal.

Its botanical features make it a useful plant for erosion control and habitat creation.
