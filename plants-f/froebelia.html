Froebelia

Froebelia, commonly known as the "snowball plant," is a genus of flowering plants in the family Acanthaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly South Africa. The plant is characterized by its clusters of small, white or pale pink flowers that resemble snowballs, giving it its common name. Froebelia species typically thrive in well-drained soil and require bright, indirect light.

They are often cultivated as ornamental plants due to their attractive foliage and unique floral displays.
