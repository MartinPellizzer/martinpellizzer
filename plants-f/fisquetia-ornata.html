Fisquetia ornata

Fisquetia ornata, commonly known as the golden bellflower, is a striking plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its vibrant yellow flowers, which resemble small bells, and its succulent, fleshy leaves that store water, adapting it to arid environments. The plant belongs to the Campanulaceae family, known for its bell-shaped flowers. Fisquetia ornata thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a valuable addition to Mediterranean-style landscapes.
