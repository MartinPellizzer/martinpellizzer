Festuca matthewsii subsp. aquilonia

Festuca matthewsii subsp.

aquilonia, commonly known as the Aquilonia bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of central Asia. It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit.

The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to native plant collections and restoration projects.
