Flickingeria integrilabia

Flickingeria integrilabia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and lance-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a raceme, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its unique appearance and habitat specificity, it is a subject of interest for botanists and orchid enthusiasts alike.
