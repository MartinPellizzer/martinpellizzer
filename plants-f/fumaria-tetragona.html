Fumaria tetragona

Fumaria tetragona, commonly known as the four-angled fumitory, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant produces linear, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in disturbed soils and can be found in meadows, roadsides, and waste areas.

Fumaria tetragona is known for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies.
