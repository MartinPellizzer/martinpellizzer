Fuchsia insignis

Fuchsia insignis, commonly known as the Chilean fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Chile and Peru.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces vibrant, tubular flowers in shades of pink, purple, and red. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its stems are often woody and branched. Fuchsia insignis thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bees, contributing to its ecological significance in native habitats.
