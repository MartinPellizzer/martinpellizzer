Ferula tschzurowskiana

Ferula tschzurowskiana, commonly known as the Tschzoworski's ferula, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stem and large, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of white wax. The plant produces umbellate inflorescences with small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, containing a strong, aromatic resin that has been used in traditional medicine.

Ferula tschzurowskiana is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, exhibiting drought tolerance and deep root systems.
