Ficus glomerata var. mollis

Ficus glomerata var. mollis, commonly known as the velvet fig, is a tropical plant characterized by its dense, velvety foliage.

The leaves are typically thick and glossy, with a soft texture that gives the plant its common name. It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid environments. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are edible but not widely cultivated for commercial use.

It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
