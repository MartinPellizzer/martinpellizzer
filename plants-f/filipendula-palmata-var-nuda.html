Filipendula palmata var. nuda

Filipendula palmata var. nuda, commonly known as the naked swamp rose, is a herbaceous perennial native to wetland habitats in North America.

It features slender, upright stems with opposite, simple, ovate to elliptical leaves that are often reddish or purplish in color. The plant produces small, clustered white or pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to waterlogged soils, making it a common species in marshes and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in wetland ecosystems.
