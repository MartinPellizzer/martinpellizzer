Fritillaria pineticola

Fritillaria pineticola, commonly known as the Sierra fritillary, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and meadows. Its bulbs are underground and serve as storage organs for nutrients.

Fritillaria pineticola plays an important role in its ecosystem by providing nectar for pollinators and habitat for small organisms.
