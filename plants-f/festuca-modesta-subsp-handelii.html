Festuca modesta subsp. handelii

Festuca modesta subsp.

handelii, commonly known as the narrowleaf fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or well-drained soils.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers, which are typically green to reddish in color. It is adapted to cold climates and can thrive in full sun to partial shade. Festuca modesta subsp.

handelii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
