Fragaria magna var. moschata

Fragaria magna var. moschata, commonly known as the musky strawberry, is a variant of the large strawberry plant characterized by its distinctive musky aroma.

It produces large, heart-shaped fruits with a vibrant red color and a slightly tart flavor. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its unique fragrance and culinary uses.

Botanically, it belongs to the Rosaceae family and is closely related to other strawberry species.
