Filago candida

Filago candida, commonly known as the white filago, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in open, sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun. Its flowers are small and white, often arranged in a cluster, and it produces small, oval-shaped fruits.

Filago candida is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
