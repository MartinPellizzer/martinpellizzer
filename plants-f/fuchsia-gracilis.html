Fuchsia gracilis

Fuchsia gracilis, commonly known as the slender fuchsia, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its pendulous, showy flowers that range in color from violet to deep red. The plant produces small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

Fuchsia gracilis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.
