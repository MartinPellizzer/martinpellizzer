Ficus lynesii

Ficus lynesii, commonly known as the Lynes' fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves and produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus lynesii is also valued for its ability to adapt to various soil types, making it relatively easy to care for in both indoor and outdoor settings.
