Fritillaria purdyi

Fritillaria purdyi, commonly known as the Sierra fritillary, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and high-elevation habitats, often in alpine or subalpine regions. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in harsh, cold environments, and it blooms in early spring.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
