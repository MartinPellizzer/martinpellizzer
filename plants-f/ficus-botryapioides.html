Ficus botryapioides

Ficus botryapioides, commonly known as the woolly fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it a distinctive, soft appearance. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a waxy texture and a deep green color. This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.
