Ficus tannoensis f. angustifolia

Ficus tannoensis f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a unique variant of the Ficus genus native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered fruits known as syconia, which are a key feature of the ficus family. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

This cultivar is often appreciated for its ornamental value and adaptability to indoor growing conditions.
