Festuca pallens subsp. zielonkowskiana

Festuca pallens subsp.

zielonkowskiana, commonly known as the Zelonskian feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and delicate, feathery inflorescences that appear in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and grasslands. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a distinctive bluish-green coloration that enhances its ornamental value.

It is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
