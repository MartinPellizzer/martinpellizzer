Ficus glandifera

Ficus glandifera, commonly known as the Palestine fig tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Middle East and parts of North Africa, thriving in arid and semi-arid climates. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. The fruit, known as a fig, is edible and has been cultivated for centuries.

It plays an important ecological and cultural role in the regions where it is found.
