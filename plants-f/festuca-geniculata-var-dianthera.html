Festuca geniculata var. dianthera

Festuca geniculata var. dianthera, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada fescue, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils in mountainous regions. It produces slender, spike-like inflorescences with small, blue to purple flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging environmental conditions.
