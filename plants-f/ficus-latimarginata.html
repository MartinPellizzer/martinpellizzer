Ficus latimarginata

Ficus latimarginata, commonly known as the broad-margin fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia, and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with broad margins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

The species is also valued for its ability to thrive in indoor environments, making it a popular choice for houseplants.
