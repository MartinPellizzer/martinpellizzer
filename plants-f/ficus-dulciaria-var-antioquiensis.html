Ficus dulciaria var. antioquiensis

Ficus dulciaria var. antioquiensis, commonly known as the Antioquian fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Andean region of Colombia, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
