Forsteronia protensa

Forsteronia protensa, commonly known as the Cape rose, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, pink or red flowers in clusters, which bloom in the spring and summer seasons. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a leathery texture, while its stems are woody and branched.

Forsteronia protensa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
