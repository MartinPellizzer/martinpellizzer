Ficus thwaitesii

Ficus thwaitesii, commonly known as the Philippine fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature, serving as a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
