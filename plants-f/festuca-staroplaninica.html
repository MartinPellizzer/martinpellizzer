Festuca staroplaninica

Festuca staroplaninica, commonly known as the Staroplanina Festuca, is a grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically forms dense mats or clumps, thriving in well-drained, rocky soils. It is adapted to cold and arid environments, often found in alpine and subalpine zones.

Festuca staroplaninica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in harsh habitats.
