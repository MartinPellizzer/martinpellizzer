Fagelia padifolia

Fagelia padifolia, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the high altitudes of South America.

It is characterized by its erect, branching stems and large, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a velvety texture. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in cloud forests.

The plant is valued for its ornamental beauty and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
