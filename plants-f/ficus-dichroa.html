Ficus dichroa

Ficus dichroa, commonly known as the two-colored fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its dark green, leathery leaves that often have a distinct two-toned appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus dichroa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in indoor environments.
