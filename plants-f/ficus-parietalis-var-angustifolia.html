Ficus parietalis var. angustifolia

Ficus parietalis var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf wall fig, is a climbing species native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically narrow and pointed, with a glossy green surface. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid environments and is often found growing on rocky surfaces or in shaded areas.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor cultivation and vertical gardening.
