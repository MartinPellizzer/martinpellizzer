Filago canescens subsp. apiculata

Filago canescens subsp.

apiculata, commonly known as the hairy filago, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its hairy, grayish stems and small, yellowish flowers.

The plant typically grows in open grasslands and rocky areas, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is often found in regions with a Mediterranean climate, where it plays a role in local biodiversity.
