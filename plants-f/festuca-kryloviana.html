Festuca kryloviana

Festuca kryloviana, commonly known as Krylov's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Festuca kryloviana is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to harsh climatic conditions.
