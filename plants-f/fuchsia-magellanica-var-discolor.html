Fuchsia magellanica var. discolor

Fuchsia magellanica var. discolor, commonly known as the Magellan fuchsia, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep pink to white, with a contrasting throat. The plant produces small, oval berries that are edible but not particularly flavorful. Its leaves are dark green and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
