Ficus longistipulata

Ficus longistipulata, commonly known as the long-stippled fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves with prominent veins and long, slender stipules. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits.

Ficus longistipulata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
