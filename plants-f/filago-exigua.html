Filago exigua

Filago exigua, commonly known as the small filago, is a flowering plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant typically grows in grassy areas and along roadsides, preferring well-drained soils. It produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in the summer months.

Filago exigua is often used in herbal medicine for its purported diuretic properties.
