Ferula laevigata

Ferula laevigata, commonly known as the smooth ferula, is a perennial herb native to the arid and semi-arid regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its root system is deep and robust, allowing it to thrive in poor, sandy soils.

Ferula laevigata is often used in traditional medicine for its purported anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
