Fuchsia integrifolia var. mollis

Fuchsia integrifolia var. mollis, commonly known as the velvet fuchsia, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its smooth, velvety foliage and clusters of pendulous, showy flowers. The leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, with entire margins and a soft, hairy texture. The flowers are typically pink to red, with five petals and a prominent, tubular throat.

This plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.
