Fagelia morisii

Fagelia morisii, commonly known as the Morisii fagelia, is a rare and unique plant species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, elongated leaves that are often variegated with shades of green and yellow. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the wet season. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with a smooth, bark-like surface.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a vulnerable species in its natural environment.
