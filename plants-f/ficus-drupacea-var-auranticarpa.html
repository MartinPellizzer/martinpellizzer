Ficus drupacea var. auranticarpa

Ficus drupacea var. auranticarpa, commonly known as the golden fruit fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and produces small, yellowish fruits that are edible. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of edible fruit.

Its growth is typically slow, making it a suitable choice for indoor or outdoor gardens in suitable climates.
