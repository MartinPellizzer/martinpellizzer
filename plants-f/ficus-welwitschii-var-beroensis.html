Ficus welwitschii var. beroensis

Ficus welwitschii var. beroensis, commonly known as the Beroensis fig, is a rare and unique variant of the Ficus genus native to specific regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, often hairy, dark green leaves that are typically large and heart-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are edible and serve as a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, requiring high humidity and well-drained soil.

Due to its limited distribution and specific environmental needs, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
