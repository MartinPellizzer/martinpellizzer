Felicia petiolata

Felicia petiolata, commonly known as the blueweed or bluebottle, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and produces clusters of small, blue to purple flowers. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and is often found in sandy or rocky soils. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it a hardy ornamental plant.

Felicia petiolata is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
