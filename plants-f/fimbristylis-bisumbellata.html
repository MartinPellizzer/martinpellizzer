Fimbristylis bisumbellata

Fimbristylis bisumbellata, commonly known as the two-umbel sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia and Australia.

It is a member of the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically arranged in two umbels, giving it its common name. It thrives in wet or marshy environments and is often found in swampy areas or along the edges of water bodies.

Fimbristylis bisumbellata is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with moist soil conditions.
