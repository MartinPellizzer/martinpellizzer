Fuirena pentagona

Fuirena pentagona, commonly known as the pentagonal fireweed, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive pentagonal-shaped leaves and vibrant red to purple flowers. The plant thrives in sunny, well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed or rocky habitats. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by bees and other insects, contributing to its ecological significance.

Fuirena pentagona is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and its historical use in traditional medicine.
