Ficaria verna subsp. calthifolia

Ficaria verna subsp.

calthifolia, commonly known as the snowdrop, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It produces delicate, nodding flowers that resemble small snowflakes, typically blooming in early spring.

The plant features a basal rosette of lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating. Its flowers consist of two white petals and three green sepals, with a single stamen and two stigmas.

It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
