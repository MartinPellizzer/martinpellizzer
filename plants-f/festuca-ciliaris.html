Festuca ciliaris

Festuca ciliaris, commonly known as silky fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered with silky hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous slender, two-rowed spikelets. Festuca ciliaris is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and restoration projects.
