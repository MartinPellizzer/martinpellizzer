Freycinetia nakanaiensis

Freycinetia nakanaiensis, commonly known as the Nakanai freycinetia, is a species of palm native to the tropical regions of Papua New Guinea.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in panicles, which are followed by clustered, black, spherical fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
