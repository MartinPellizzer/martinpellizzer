Festuca monguensis

Festuca monguensis, commonly known as Mongolian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Festuca monguensis is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing forage for grazing animals.
