Festuca communis var. valesiaca

Festuca communis var. valesiaca, commonly known as Valesiaca fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically forms dense mats and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and along streams.

Its flowering spikes are slender and spike-like, producing small, wind-pollinated flowers.
