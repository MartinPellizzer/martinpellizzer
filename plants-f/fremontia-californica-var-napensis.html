Fremontia californica var. napensis

Fremontia californica var. napensis, commonly known as the Santa Barbara dudleya, is a succulent plant native to coastal regions of southern California.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically gray-green in color and covered in a fine, white powdery coating. The plant produces small, white or pale pink flowers during the spring and early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
