Festuca tauschii

Festuca tauschii, commonly known as Tausch's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and culms that are often hairy. This plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to temperate climates. Festuca tauschii is known for its genetic significance in wheat evolution, as it is a source of the D-genome.

Its ecological role includes soil stabilization and providing habitat for small fauna.
