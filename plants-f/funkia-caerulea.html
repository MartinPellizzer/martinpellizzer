Funkia caerulea

Funkia caerulea, commonly known as blue flag iris, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its striking blue to violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has strap-like, grass-like leaves that grow from a rhizomatous root system. It thrives in wetland habitats, including marshes and pond edges, where it helps stabilize soil and provide habitat for aquatic life.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for wetland gardens and water features.
