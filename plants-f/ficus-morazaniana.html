Ficus morazaniana

Ficus morazaniana, commonly known as the Morazan fig, is a species of flowering plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and distinctive, asymmetrical fig fruits. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, often found in moist, shaded habitats. Its leaves are usually dark green and have a smooth texture, while the bark is rough and fissured.

Ficus morazaniana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
