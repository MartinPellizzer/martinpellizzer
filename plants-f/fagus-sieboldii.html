Fagus sieboldii

Fagus sieboldii, commonly known as the Japanese beech, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and chestnuts, and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves. The tree produces small, spiky, cup-like fruits called cupules, which contain edible nuts. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained, acidic soils.

The Japanese beech is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitats.
