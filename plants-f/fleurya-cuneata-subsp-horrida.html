Fleurya cuneata subsp. horrida

Fleurya cuneata subsp.

horrida, commonly known as the rough fleurya, is a shrub native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems and small, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine, stiff hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, which are typically borne in the axils of the leaves. Its habitat includes rocky outcrops and dry, sandy soils, where it thrives in full sun.

The subspecies is distinguished by its rough texture and distinctive floral arrangement.
