Falkia canescens var. canescens

Falkia canescens var. canescens, commonly known as the silky falkia, is a flowering plant native to the southern regions of South America.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, silky hairs. The plant produces small, pale yellow flowers that are clustered in loose inflorescences, typically blooming in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens, though it is not widely known in horticulture.
