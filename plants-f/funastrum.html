Funastrum

Funastrum, commonly known as the common funastrum, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Asteraceae.

It is characterized by its composite flower heads, which consist of both ray and disk flowers. The leaves are typically alternate, lanceolate, and often hairy, providing a distinctive appearance. Funastrum species are native to various regions in the Americas and are often found in dry, open habitats.

They play a role in supporting local pollinators and are sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
