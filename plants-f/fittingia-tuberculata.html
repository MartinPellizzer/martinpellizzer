Fittingia tuberculata

Fittingia tuberculata, commonly known as the tuberculate fittingia, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the family Gesneriaceae and is characterized by its striking, tuberculate (knob-like) leaves. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that are typically orange or yellow in color, attracting pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds. Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
