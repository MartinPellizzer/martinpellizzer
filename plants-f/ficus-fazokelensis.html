Ficus fazokelensis

Ficus fazokelensis, commonly known as the Fazokel's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature.

Ficus fazokelensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
