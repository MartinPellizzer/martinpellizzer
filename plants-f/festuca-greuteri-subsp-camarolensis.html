Festuca greuteri subsp. camarolensis

Festuca greuteri subsp.

camarolensis, commonly known as the Camarolense fescue, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe. It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes.

The plant features slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Its inflorescence consists of a spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in marginal environments.
