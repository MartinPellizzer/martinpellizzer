Ficus domestica

Ficus domestica, commonly known as the common fig, is a deciduous tree native to the Middle East and southern Europe.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and unique syconium fruit structure. The fruit, which is a type of multiple fruit, develops from the flowering inflorescence and is encased in a fleshy, edible pericarp. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its adaptability to different climates.

Its botanical features make it both ecologically significant and economically valuable for fruit production.
