Fimbristylis juncocephala

Fimbristylis juncocephala, commonly known as the narrow-headed sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or seasonally wet habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences. Its flowers are usually inconspicuous, with three stamens and a single pistil.

It plays an important ecological role in wetland ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
