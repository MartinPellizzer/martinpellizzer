Fritillaria meleagroides

Fritillaria meleagroides, commonly known as the checkered lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or red with contrasting black veins, creating a checkered pattern. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Fritillaria meleagroides is known for its early spring blooming and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
