Ficus foveata

Ficus foveata, commonly known as the warty fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, including countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its distinctive warty, knobby growths on its bark, which give it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits.

Ficus foveata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor environments.
