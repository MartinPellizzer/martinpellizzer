Ficus speciosa

Ficus speciosa, commonly known as the giant ficus or the sacred fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, spherical fruits known as figs. Ficus speciosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its ability to thrive in a variety of conditions makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
