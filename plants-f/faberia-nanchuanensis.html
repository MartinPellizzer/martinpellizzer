Faberia nanchuanensis

Faberia nanchuanensis, commonly known as the Nanchuan faberia, is a rare flowering plant native to China.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, showcasing its adaptation to harsh environmental conditions. Its leaves are compound, and it produces small, often pale flowers that bloom in the spring.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Faberia nanchuanensis is considered a valuable subject for botanical research and conservation efforts.
