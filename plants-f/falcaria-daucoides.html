Falcaria daucoides

Falcaria daucoides, commonly known as the creeping bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, creeping stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread across the ground. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or pink in color, blooming in the summer months. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Falcaria daucoides is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
