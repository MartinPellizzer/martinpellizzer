Ficinia bracteata f. rigidula

Ficinia bracteata f. rigidula, commonly known as the rigid bractean figbush, is a rare and unique variant of the species native to specific regions of Southeast Asia.

This plant is characterized by its stiff, upright leaves and small, clustered inflorescences that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs. The leaves are typically narrow and have a leathery texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It thrives in humid, shaded environments, often found in forest understories where it receives indirect light.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a botanically interesting and ecologically significant species.
