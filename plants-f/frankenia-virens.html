Frankenia virens

Frankenia virens, commonly known as coastal sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy, well-drained soils along the coastline. The plant features small, lance-shaped leaves and produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its root system helps stabilize sand dunes, making it ecologically important in coastal environments.

Frankenia virens is adapted to salty air and periodic salt spray, showcasing its resilience in harsh coastal conditions.
