Fuchsia asperifolia

Fuchsia asperifolia, commonly known as the rough-leaved fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dark green, rough-textured leaves and showy, pendulous flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Fuchsia asperifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
