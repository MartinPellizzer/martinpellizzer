Festuca brachyphylla f. flavida

Festuca brachyphylla f. flavida, commonly known as the golden bentgrass, is a flowering plant belonging to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that bear small, yellowish to golden flowers, giving it its distinguishing color. It typically grows in moist, acidic soils and is often found in wet meadows or along stream banks.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in wetland environments.
