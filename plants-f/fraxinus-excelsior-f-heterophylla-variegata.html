Fraxinus excelsior f. heterophylla-variegata

Fraxinus excelsior f. heterophylla-variegata, commonly known as the variegated ash, is a decorative variant of the common ash tree.

It is characterized by its striking variegated foliage, with white and yellow markings on the leaves. The plant exhibits a broad range of leaf shapes, from ovate to lanceolate, depending on the growth conditions. It prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it suitable for garden cultivation.

This ornamental form is prized for its unique appearance and adaptability in landscaping.
