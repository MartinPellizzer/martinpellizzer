Fagus ferruginea f. rosthornii

Fagus ferruginea f. rosthornii, commonly known as the rosthornii form of the brown fir, is a variant of the European beech.

It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bark and dark, glossy leaves. This form is a natural variant, occurring in specific regions of Europe. It shares the general morphology of the parent species but exhibits unique coloration traits.

The plant is typically found in temperate forest environments.
