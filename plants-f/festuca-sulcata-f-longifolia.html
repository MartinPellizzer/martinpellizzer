Festuca sulcata f. longifolia

Festuca sulcata f. longifolia, commonly known as the long-leaved festuca, is a grass species characterized by its elongated, slender leaves.

It belongs to the Poaceae family and is native to regions of southern Africa. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its fine texture and drought tolerance.

The inflorescence consists of dense, cylindrical spikelets that produce small, wind-pollinated flowers.
