Fagonia arabica var. brevispina

Fagonia arabica var. brevispina, commonly known as the short-spined fagonia, is a shrub native to arid regions of the Middle East and North Africa.

It is characterized by its small, spiny leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant is adapted to hot, dry climates and is often found in rocky or sandy soils. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule containing several seeds.

This species is of interest in botanical studies due to its unique morphology and ecological adaptations.
