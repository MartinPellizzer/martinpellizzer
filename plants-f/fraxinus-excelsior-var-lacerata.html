Fraxinus excelsior var. lacerata

Fraxinus excelsior var. lacerata, commonly known as the Weeping Ash, is a variant of the European ash tree characterized by its distinctive cascading branches that create a weeping appearance.

It is a deciduous tree with compound leaves that are opposite and typically have 5 to 9 leaflets. The leaves are usually serrated and turn a striking yellow in the autumn. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring, followed by winged samaras as its fruit.

It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas across Europe.
