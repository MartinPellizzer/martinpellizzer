Ferocactus herrerae

Ferocactus herrerae, commonly known as the Herrera's barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh desert conditions. The plant produces yellow or orange flowers during the spring and summer months. Its spines are typically yellow or brown, providing protection from herbivores.

Ferocactus herrerae is well adapted to full sun and requires minimal water, making it a hardy choice for xeriscaping.
