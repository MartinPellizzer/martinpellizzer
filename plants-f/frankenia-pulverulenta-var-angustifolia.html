Frankenia pulverulenta var. angustifolia

Frankenia pulverulenta var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf beach morning glory, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the early morning, typically from spring to early summer. The plant has slender, branching stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to salt spray and high sunlight exposure.

This species plays a role in stabilizing dune ecosystems and is often found in maritime habitats.
