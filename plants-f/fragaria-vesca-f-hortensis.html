Fragaria vesca f. hortensis

Fragaria vesca f. hortensis, commonly known as the woodland strawberry, is a cultivated form of the wild strawberry.

It is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and edible, sweet-tart berries. This variety is often grown in gardens and greenhouses for its ornamental value and culinary use. The plant has trifoliate leaves and produces runners, which can be used for propagation.

It thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for home gardeners.
