Ficus microcarpa var. oluangpiensis

Ficus microcarpa var. oluangpiensis, commonly known as the Chinese banyan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and a distinctive aerial root system that gives it a unique, sprawling appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by round, greenish fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental tree.

Its ability to grow in various soil types makes it adaptable to different environments.
