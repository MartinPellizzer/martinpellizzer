Fraxinus ochrochlora

Fraxinus ochrochlora, commonly known as the yellow-leaf ash.

It is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America, characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are typically alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin, turning yellow in the fall. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
