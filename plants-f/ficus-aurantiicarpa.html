Ficus aurantiicarpa

Ficus aurantiicarpa, commonly known as the orange fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green fruits that ripen to a bright orange color. The fruit is edible, though it is often consumed in its unripe state as a vegetable in various cuisines.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
