Flemingia macrophylla var. viridis

Flemingia macrophylla var. viridis, commonly known as the green-leaved flemingia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green, sometimes with a slight variegation. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, which bloom during the wet season. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its foliage and floral display.
