Ficus aspera var. nota

Ficus aspera var. nota, commonly known as the Nota Ficus, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly rough texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
