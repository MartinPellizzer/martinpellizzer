Forsskaolea procridifolia var. microphylla

Forsskaolea procridifolia var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved sandmat, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and grayish-green foliage with small, densely packed leaves. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, typically during the rainy season. It thrives in well-drained sandy soils and is adapted to withstand high temperatures and low rainfall.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in xeriscaping and as an ornamental plant in dryland gardens.
