Festuca junatovii

Festuca junatovii, commonly known as Junatov's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny seeds. It is characterized by its low-growing habit and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.

Festuca junatovii plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitats and is of interest to botanists studying alpine flora.
