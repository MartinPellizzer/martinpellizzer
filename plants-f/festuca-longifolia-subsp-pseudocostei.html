Festuca longifolia subsp. pseudocostei

Festuca longifolia subsp.

pseudocostei, commonly known as the slender fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in a dense layer of hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Due to its hardy nature and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
