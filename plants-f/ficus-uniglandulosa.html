Ficus uniglandulosa

Ficus uniglandulosa, commonly known as the single-gland fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its smooth, glossy leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in coastal forests.

Ficus uniglandulosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
