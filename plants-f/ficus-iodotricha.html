Ficus iodotricha

Ficus iodotricha, commonly known as the silver-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with a silvery underside, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically consumed raw or cooked.

Ficus iodotricha is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
