Fevillea bahiensis

Fevillea bahiensis, commonly known as the Brazilian orchid, is a striking tropical plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that come in various colors such as yellow, orange, and red. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its roots are adapted for absorbing moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Fevillea bahiensis is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its ecological role in supporting biodiversity in its native habitat.
