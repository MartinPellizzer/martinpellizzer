Fritillaria cirrhosa var. bonatii

Fritillaria cirrhosa var. bonatii, commonly known as the Bonatti's fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations between 2,500 and 4,500 meters. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, forming a basal rosette, while its stems are upright and bear the showy inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in alpine gardens.
