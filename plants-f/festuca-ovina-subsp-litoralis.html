Festuca ovina subsp. litoralis

Festuca ovina subsp.

litoralis, commonly known as coastal bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to coastal regions of Europe and North America. It thrives in saline and sandy soils, often found in dune environments and coastal grasslands.

The plant has fine, tufted leaves that are typically blue-green in color, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to withstand harsh coastal conditions, including salt spray and strong winds.

This subspecies plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and preventing erosion.
