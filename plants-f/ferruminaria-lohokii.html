Ferruminaria lohokii

Ferruminaria lohokii, commonly known as the Lohokii ironweed, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of small, pinkish-white flowers that bloom in the summer months. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or near water bodies. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a rough texture, while its roots are known for their high iron content.

Ferruminaria lohokii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for various insects and birds.
