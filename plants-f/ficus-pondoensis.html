Ficus pondoensis

Ficus pondoensis, commonly known as the Pondo fig, is a species of fig tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by the characteristic fig fruits. Ficus pondoensis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
