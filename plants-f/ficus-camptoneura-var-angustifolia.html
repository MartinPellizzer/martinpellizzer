Ficus camptoneura var. angustifolia

Ficus camptoneura var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, green figs that ripen to a yellowish color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in landscaped gardens.
