Fragaria indica var. wallichii

Fragaria indica var. wallichii, commonly known as the Himalayan strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the high altitudes of the Himalayas.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically red in color and have a sweet, tangy flavor. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and grows in clusters along the ground. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its use in local cuisines and traditional medicine.
