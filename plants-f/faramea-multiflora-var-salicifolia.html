Faramea multiflora var. salicifolia

Faramea multiflora var. salicifolia, commonly known as the silky faramea, is a shrub native to tropical regions of Africa.

It features lanceolate leaves that are typically covered in a fine, silky pubescence, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, which bloom during the wet season. Its growth habit is compact, making it suitable for ornamental use in gardens.

The species is valued for its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
