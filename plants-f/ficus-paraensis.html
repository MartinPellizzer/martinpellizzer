Ficus paraensis

Ficus paraensis, commonly known as the Para rubber tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Amazon rainforest and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its ability to produce latex, which has been historically used in the rubber industry. It thrives in tropical climates with high humidity and well-draining soil.

Ficus paraensis plays an important ecological role in its native habitat by providing shelter and food for various wildlife species.
