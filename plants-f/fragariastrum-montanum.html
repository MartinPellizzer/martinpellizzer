Fragariastrum montanum

Fragariastrum montanum, commonly known as the mountain strawberry, is a perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, edible berries that are typically red in color. The plant thrives in alpine and subalpine environments, often growing in rocky or gravelly soils. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three oval-shaped leaflets, and it flowers with white or pinkish petals.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its fruit and aesthetic qualities.
