Fallopia japonica f. colorans

Fallopia japonica f. colorans, commonly known as the colorful Japanese knotweed, is a vigorous perennial plant characterized by its striking variegated foliage.

It features heart-shaped leaves that display a range of colors, including green, white, and pink, making it visually distinctive. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers in late summer, which are followed by red berries. It is native to Asia but has become an invasive species in many parts of the world due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.

Its rhizomatous root system allows it to spread aggressively, making it a challenging weed to control.
