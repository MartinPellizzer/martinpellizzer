Fritillaria sibthorpiana subsp. enginiana

Fritillaria sibthorpiana subsp.

enginiana, commonly known as the Enginian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns.

The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cold winter dormancy period for proper growth.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
