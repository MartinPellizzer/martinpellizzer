Fedia campanulata

Fedia campanulata, commonly known as the bellflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Campanulaceae family.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped flowers, which are typically blue or violet in color. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland or wetland habitats.

Fedia campanulata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
