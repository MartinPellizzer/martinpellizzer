Ficus baronii

Ficus baronii, commonly known as the Baroni fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are edible and attract various wildlife. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus baronii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
