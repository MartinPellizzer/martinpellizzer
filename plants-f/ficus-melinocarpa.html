Ficus melinocarpa

Ficus melinocarpa, commonly known as the black ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, including countries such as Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its dark, glossy leaves and small, clustered fruit that turns from green to black as it matures. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is well adapted to humid, shaded environments.
