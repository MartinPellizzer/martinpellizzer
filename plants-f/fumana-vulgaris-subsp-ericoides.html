Fumana vulgaris subsp. ericoides

Fumana vulgaris subsp.

ericoides, commonly known as the common heath, is a shrub characterized by its small, leathery leaves and clusters of bell-shaped flowers. It typically grows in acidic, well-drained soils and is often found in heathlands and moorlands.

The plant is adapted to cool, moist climates and can thrive in partial shade. Its flowers, which range in color from white to pink, bloom in late spring to early summer.

This subspecies is notable for its ecological importance, providing habitat and nectar for various pollinators.
