Ficus chrysopthalma

Ficus chrysopthalma, commonly known as the golden fig or yellow fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly South Africa, and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.

Ficus chrysopthalma prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
