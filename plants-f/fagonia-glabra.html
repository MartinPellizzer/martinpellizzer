Fagonia glabra

Fagonia glabra, commonly known as the smooth fagonia, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the family Capparaceae and is characterized by its small, yellow flowers and pinnate leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its fruit is a capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Fagonia glabra is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
