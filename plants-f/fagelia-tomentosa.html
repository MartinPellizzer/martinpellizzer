Fagelia tomentosa

Fagelia tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly fagelia, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, woolly foliage that covers its stems and leaves, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, which bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value and unique appearance, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
