Fumaria calycina

Fumaria calycina, commonly known as the common fumitory, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Fumariaceae.

It is characterized by its delicate, fern-like leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and parts of Asia, often found in moist, shaded areas such as woodlands and stream banks. Its hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves contribute to its distinctive appearance.

Fumaria calycina is also known for its medicinal uses in traditional herbal remedies.
