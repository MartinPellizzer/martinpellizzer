Fabiana squamuligera

Fabiana squamuligera, commonly known as the silverleaf fabiana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage that gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Fabiana squamuligera is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its attractive appearance and drought tolerance.
