Fraxinus potosina

Fraxinus potosina, commonly known as Potosino ash, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate with serrated edges, turning yellow in autumn. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged samaras that disperse its seeds.

This species is adapted to arid and rocky environments, making it a resilient plant in challenging conditions.
