Funastrum trichopetalum

Funastrum trichopetalum, commonly known as the red sandmat, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Amaranthaceae family and is characterized by its prostrate growth habit and distinctive, reddish-pink flowers. The plant features small, ovate leaves that are covered in fine hairs, contributing to its drought-resistant adaptations. Its flowering period typically occurs in late spring to early summer, and it thrives in sandy or gravelly soils.

Funastrum trichopetalum is often used in xeriscaping due to its low water requirements and ornamental value.
