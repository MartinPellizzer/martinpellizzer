Fritillaria imperialis var. imperialis

Fritillaria imperialis var. imperialis, commonly known as the Crown Imperial, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tall stalks that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences that resemble a crown, giving it its common name. Its bulbs are toxic and contain alkaloids, making it important to handle with care.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often used in gardens for its ornamental value.
