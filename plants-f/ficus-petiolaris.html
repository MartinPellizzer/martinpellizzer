Ficus petiolaris

Ficus petiolaris, commonly known as the Variegated Ficus or the White Ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, oval-shaped leaves that often display a striking variegation of white and green. It is a popular indoor plant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its relatively easy care requirements.

Ficus petiolaris is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and ornamental appeal.
