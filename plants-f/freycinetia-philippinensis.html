Freycinetia philippinensis

Freycinetia philippinensis, commonly known as the Philippine palm, is a flowering plant native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Arecaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and pinnate leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of many palm species. Its fruits are drupes, containing a single seed enclosed in a fibrous aril.

Freycinetia philippinensis is often found in lowland rainforests and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
