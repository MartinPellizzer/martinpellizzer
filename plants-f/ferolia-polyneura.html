Ferolia polyneura

Ferolia polyneura, commonly known as the rough-leaved citrus, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its rough, elliptical leaves and fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces small, round, orange-colored fruits with a thick, segmented rind. The fruit contains a juicy pulp that is used in traditional medicine and culinary applications.

Its botanical features make it an important species in the Rutaceae family.
