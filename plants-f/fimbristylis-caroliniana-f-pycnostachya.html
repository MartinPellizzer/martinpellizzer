Fimbristylis caroliniana f. pycnostachya

Fimbristylis caroliniana f. pycnostachya, commonly known as the dense-stalked flat sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance with narrow, linear leaves and a dense arrangement of flowering spikes. The plant typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and other moist habitats, showcasing its adaptability to aquatic or semi-aquatic environments. Its inflorescence consists of numerous small, spike-like clusters of flowers, which are often subtended by bracts.

This subspecies is notable for its compact growth habit and is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil in wetland areas.
