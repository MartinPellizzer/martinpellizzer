Festuca varia subsp. xanthina

Festuca varia subsp.

xanthina, commonly known as yellow meadow grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It typically grows in moist meadows and grasslands, preferring well-drained soils.

The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are usually narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
