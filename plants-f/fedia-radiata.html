Fedia radiata

Fedia radiata, commonly known as the golden trumpet tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, showy yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. The plant has a straight, cylindrical trunk with smooth, gray bark and can grow up to 30 meters tall. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate, with a glossy green surface.

The tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its vibrant floral display.
