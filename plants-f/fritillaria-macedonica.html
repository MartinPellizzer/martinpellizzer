Fritillaria macedonica

Fritillaria macedonica, commonly known as the Macedonian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Balkan region.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color, often with intricate patterns. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winters, and it is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
