Fagonia chilensis var. laevis

Fagonia chilensis var. laevis, commonly known as the smooth fagonia, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of Chile.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, yellowish-green leaves that are often covered in a fine, smooth layer. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in sandy soils and is adapted to coastal climates with high salinity and strong winds.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is of interest in ecological and botanical studies.
