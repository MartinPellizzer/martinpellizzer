Fraxinus rotundifolia f. variegata

Fraxinus rotundifolia f. variegata, commonly known as the variegated green ash, is a cultivar of the green ash tree characterized by its distinctive foliage.

Its leaves are oval-shaped with a smooth margin and exhibit a unique variegation pattern of cream and green. The plant is typically deciduous, shedding its leaves in the fall, and produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in the spring. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its attractive foliage and moderate growth habit.
