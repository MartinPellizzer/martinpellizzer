Ficus bernaysii

Ficus bernaysii, commonly known as the Bernays' fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature. Ficus bernaysii thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

It has a shallow root system, making it suitable for indoor cultivation with proper care.
