Fagraea bodenii

Fagraea bodenii, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a thick, buttressed trunk and smooth, greyish-brown bark. Its most striking feature is its large, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters during the dry season. The plant produces woody capsules containing numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.
