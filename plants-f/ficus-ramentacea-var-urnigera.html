Ficus ramentacea var. urnigera

Ficus ramentacea var. urnigera, commonly known as the woolly fig, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive, hairy, or woolly foliage that gives it its common name. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, often reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are usually broad and ovate, with a dark green upper surface and a lighter, often pubescent underside.

This variety is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor conditions.
