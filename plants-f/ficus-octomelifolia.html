Ficus octomelifolia

Ficus octomelifolia, commonly known as the eight-fig fig tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia, and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and distinctive fruiting structures. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. Ficus octomelifolia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.

Its adaptability to various climates makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
