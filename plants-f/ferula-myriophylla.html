Ferula myriophylla

Ferula myriophylla, commonly known as the Persian cow's milk, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces a milky sap, which is the source of its common name. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, often with a hairy texture.

Ferula myriophylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
