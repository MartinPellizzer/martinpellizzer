Festuca korabensis

Festuca korabensis, commonly known as the Korab fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It typically grows in mountainous regions and is adapted to cool, temperate climates. The plant has slender, tufted growth habit with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often hairy. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny blue to purple flowers.

Festuca korabensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping or as a ground cover.
