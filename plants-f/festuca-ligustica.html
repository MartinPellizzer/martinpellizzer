Festuca ligustica

Festuca ligustica, commonly known as the Ligurian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in rocky or coastal areas, adapting to harsh environmental conditions. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

Festuca ligustica is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in coastal and alpine habitats.
