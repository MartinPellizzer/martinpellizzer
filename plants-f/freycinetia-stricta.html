Freycinetia stricta

Freycinetia stricta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and cooking. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in branched inflorescences. Its root system is fibrous, and it thrives in well-drained soils near coastal areas.

Freycinetia stricta is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local wildlife.
