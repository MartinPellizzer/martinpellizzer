Festuca oryzetorum

Festuca oryzetorum, commonly known as ricegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Festuca oryzetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping due to its fine texture and ability to thrive in various soil conditions.
