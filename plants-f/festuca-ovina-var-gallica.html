Festuca ovina var. gallica

Festuca ovina var. gallica, commonly known as Galician sheep fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and dense growth habit, often forming compact clumps. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.

Its foliage is generally a deep green, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences in the summer.
