Fraxinus pallisiae f. emarginata

Fraxinus pallisiae f. emarginata, commonly known as the emarginated ash.

This variant is a form of the Pallisiae ash, characterized by its distinctive leaf margins that are notched or emarginate. It is a deciduous tree native to specific regions of Central America, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant exhibits typical ash tree features, including opposite leaf arrangement and smooth, gray bark.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphological traits and its role in local ecosystems.
