Ferulago bracteata

Ferulago bracteata, commonly known as the bracted ferulago, is a flowering plant belonging to the Apiaceae family.

It is native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia, often found in rocky or sandy soils. The plant features a slender, branched stem and small, greenish-yellow flowers arranged in umbels. Its leaves are pinnately compound, with finely divided lobes, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

Ferulago bracteata is also valued for its medicinal properties, traditionally used in herbal remedies.
