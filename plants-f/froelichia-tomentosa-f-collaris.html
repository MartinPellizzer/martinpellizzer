Froelichia tomentosa f. collaris

Froelichia tomentosa f. collaris, commonly known as the woolly froelichia, is a variant of the froelichia plant characterized by its dense, woolly foliage.

It is a shrub native to the eastern United States, typically found in moist, shaded habitats such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant features small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or white, followed by small, berry-like fruits. Its foliage is covered in a fine, white tomentum, giving it a distinctive, fluffy appearance.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in partially shaded environments.
