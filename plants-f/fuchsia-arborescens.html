Fuchsia arborescens

Fuchsia arborescens, commonly known as the tree fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is a woody shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers that are typically pink or purple, with five petals and a distinctive, elongated corolla. Fuchsia arborescens thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for gardens and landscaping in suitable climates.
