Fritillaria delavayi var. banmaensis

Fritillaria delavayi var. banmaensis, commonly known as the Banma Mountain fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the high-altitude regions of Yunnan Province in China.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils and thrives in cool, moist environments. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winter conditions, and it is often found in alpine meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
