Ficus sinuata

Ficus sinuata, commonly known as the warty fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its unique, warty, and often gnarled bark, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate in form. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by fig-like fruits.

Ficus sinuata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking foliage and resilience in various environmental conditions.
