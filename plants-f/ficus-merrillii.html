Ficus merrillii

Ficus merrillii, commonly known as the Philippine fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are an important food source for local wildlife. Ficus merrillii thrives in tropical climates and is often found in lowland forests and disturbed areas.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm regions.
