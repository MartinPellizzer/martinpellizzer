Ficus murilloi var. cajambrensis

Ficus murilloi var. cajambrensis, commonly known as the Trinidadian fig, is a species of flowering plant native to the Caribbean region.

It is a small, evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in moist, shaded environments. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate in form. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are usually green when mature.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical gardens.
