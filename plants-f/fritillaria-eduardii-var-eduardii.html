Fritillaria eduardii var. eduardii

Fritillaria eduardii var. eduardii, commonly known as the Edward's fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color, often with a contrasting black or dark purple border. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that form a rosette at the base. It thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and requires a cold winter dormancy period.

Fritillaria eduardii var. eduardii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens or alpine settings.
