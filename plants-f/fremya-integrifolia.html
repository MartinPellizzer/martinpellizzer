Fremya integrifolia

Fremya integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaved fremya, is a shrub native to Australia.

It belongs to the Myrtaceae family, which includes eucalyptus and tea trees. The plant is characterized by its simple, smooth, and lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. Its flowers are small, white or pink, and are clustered in dense, spherical inflorescences.

Fremya integrifolia is often found in dry, sandy soils and is adapted to withstand drought and heat.
