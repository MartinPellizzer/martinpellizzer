Francisia

Francisia, commonly known as the blue mistflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy blue or purple flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are typically lance-shaped and have a soft, hairy texture.

Francisia is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
