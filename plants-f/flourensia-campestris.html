Flourensia campestris

Flourensia campestris, commonly known as the creeping sunflower, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy yellow blooms. The plant typically grows in dry, open areas and has a low, spreading habit with hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to ecological diversity.

Flourensia campestris is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.
