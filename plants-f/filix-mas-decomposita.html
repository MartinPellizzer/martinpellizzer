Filix-mas decomposita

Filix-mas decomposita, commonly known as the broken fern, is a member of the fern family and is characterized by its delicate, frond-like structures.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests or along stream banks. The plant features a rhizomatous root system that allows it to spread and colonize new areas. Its leaves are usually divided into small, lance-shaped segments, giving it a lacy appearance.

Filix-mas decomposita plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various microorganisms and small invertebrates.
