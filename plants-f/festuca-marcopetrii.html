Festuca marcopetrii

Festuca marcopetrii, commonly known as Marco Petri's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often bear small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in cool, moist environments and is adapted to rocky or alpine habitats. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that helps it conserve water in harsh conditions.

Festuca marcopetrii is of interest to botanists due to its unique ecological adaptations and limited distribution.
