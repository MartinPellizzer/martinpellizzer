Forestiera pinetorum

Forestiera pinetorum, commonly known as the pine forestiera, is a small evergreen shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, acidic soils within forested areas, often near pine trees. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. It produces small, fragrant white flowers in the spring, which are followed by blue-black berries.

Forestiera pinetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
