Fleurya peduncularis

Fleurya peduncularis, commonly known as the bluebell of the forest, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its striking blue-violet flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along its stem. It thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in forest understories.

Its botanical significance lies in its role within local ecosystems and its ornamental value in horticulture.
