Fargesia racemosa

Fargesia racemosa, commonly known as the Chinese evergreen bamboo, is a species of flowering bamboo native to China.

It is characterized by its clumping growth habit and slender, green culms that are often adorned with small, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces clusters of tiny, white to pale pink flowers during the summer months, which are followed by dark blue berries. It is a hardy, cold-tolerant plant that thrives in shaded, moist environments.

Fargesia racemosa is popular in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to create a dense, upright screen in gardens.
