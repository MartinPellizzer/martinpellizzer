Forsythia ovata

Forsythia ovata, commonly known as the Chinese lantern bush, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has opposite, simple, and ovate-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. Its roots are fibrous, and it prefers well-drained soil and full sun.

Forsythia ovata is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
