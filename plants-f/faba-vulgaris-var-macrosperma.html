Faba vulgaris var. macrosperma

Faba vulgaris var. macrosperma, commonly known as the broad bean or fava bean, is a cultivated variety of the vetch genus.

It is characterized by its large, flat pods that contain several kidney-shaped seeds. The plant is a herbaceous annual that thrives in temperate climates and is often grown for its edible seeds. Its leaves are trifoliate, with heart-shaped leaflets, and it produces clusters of small, yellow flowers.

This variety is valued for its nutritional content and historical use in agriculture and cuisine.
