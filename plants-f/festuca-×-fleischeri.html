Festuca × fleischeri

Festuca × fleischeri, commonly known as the Fleischer's fescue, is a hybrid grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, silver-green foliage that adds ornamental value to gardens. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
