Ficus adenosperma

Ficus adenosperma, commonly known as the African rubber fig, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and smooth, gray bark. The plant produces small, greenish flowers followed by spherical, greenish fruits that turn yellow when ripe. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Ficus adenosperma is also valued for its ability to tolerate indoor conditions, making it a popular houseplant.
