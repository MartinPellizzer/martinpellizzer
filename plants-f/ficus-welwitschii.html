Ficus welwitschii

Ficus welwitschii, commonly known as the giant ficus or Welwitsch's fig, is a large, evergreen tree native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and can grow to impressive heights, often exceeding 20 meters. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are typically consumed by local wildlife and humans. Its rapid growth rate and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitat.

Ficus welwitschii is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
