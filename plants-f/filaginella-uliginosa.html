Filaginella uliginosa

Filaginella uliginosa, commonly known as the bog moss, is a species of moss belonging to the family Dicranaceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth form and is typically found in moist, acidic environments such as bogs and fens. Filaginella uliginosa plays an important role in stabilizing soil and maintaining the hydrological balance of its habitat.

Its spore-producing capsules are stalked and often covered with a peristome, which aids in spore dispersal.
