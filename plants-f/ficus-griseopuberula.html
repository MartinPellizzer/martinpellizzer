Ficus griseopuberula

Ficus griseopuberula, commonly known as the gray-hairy fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Thailand, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its hairy, grayish-green leaves and small, clustered flowers. It typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often found in moist, shaded environments.

The fruit, a small fig, is edible and serves as a food source for various animals.
