Fumaria bonanovae

Fumaria bonanovae, commonly known as the Bonanovae gloryflower, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae, which includes various species known for their striking, often colorful flowers. The plant produces delicate, pendulous flowers that are typically yellow or orange in color, with a distinctive spurred structure at the base of each bloom. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Due to its limited distribution and unique appearance, it is considered a rare and somewhat elusive species in botanical collections.
