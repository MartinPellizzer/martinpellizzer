Ficus patens

Ficus patens, commonly known as the broad-leafed fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant is evergreen, with a dense, shrub-like growth habit, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs.

Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine and as a decorative plant in tropical gardens.
