Fumana spachii var. ericoides

Fumana spachii var. ericoides, commonly known as the Ericoid Fumana, is a shrub native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, evergreen foliage and small, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in shades of white and pink. The plant typically grows at high altitudes, preferring well-drained, acidic soils in temperate climates. Its leaves are leathery and dense, providing a dense canopy that supports a variety of small organisms.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in rock gardens or as a decorative hedge.
