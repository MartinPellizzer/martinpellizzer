Ficus perinteregam

Ficus perinteregam, commonly known as the Perinteregam fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into edible figs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Ficus perinteregam is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its fruit in local cuisines.
