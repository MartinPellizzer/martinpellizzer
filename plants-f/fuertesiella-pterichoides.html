Fuertesiella pterichoides

Fuertesiella pterichoides, commonly known as the Mexican feather grass, is a perennial grass species native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its slender, wiry stems and delicate, feathery plumes that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a slight sheen, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in late spring.

Fuertesiella pterichoides is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its elegant, airy foliage.
