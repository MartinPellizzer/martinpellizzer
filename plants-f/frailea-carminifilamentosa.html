Frailea carminifilamentosa

Frailea carminifilamentosa, commonly known as the red-threaded stonecrop, is a succulent plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its compact, rosette-shaped growth pattern and fleshy, blue-green leaves. The plant produces vibrant red or pink flowers on long, slender stems, which give it its common name. It thrives in well-draining soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or xeriscaping.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance requirements, it is a popular choice among succulent enthusiasts.
