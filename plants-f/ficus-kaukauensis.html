Ficus kaukauensis

Ficus kaukauensis, commonly known as the Kaukauensis fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, particularly found in parts of Fiji and Tonga. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. The fruit, which is a fig, is typically enclosed in a woody syconium.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in its native regions.
