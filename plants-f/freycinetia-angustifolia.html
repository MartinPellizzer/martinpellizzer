Freycinetia angustifolia

Freycinetia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a member of the Pandanaceae family.

It is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Pacific Islands and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a food wrapping in various cultures. Its flowers are small and clustered, typically appearing in panicles, and it produces fibrous, edible fruits.

Freycinetia angustifolia is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in coastal and wetland habitats.
