Fortunella japonica var. margarita

Fortunella japonica var. margarita, commonly known as the Japanese kumquat, is a small evergreen shrub native to East Asia.

It produces small, citrus-like fruits that are edible when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. The plant has thick, leathery leaves that are dark green and glossy. It thrives in warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as its fruit.

The flowers are fragrant and typically white or pale yellow, appearing in clusters.
