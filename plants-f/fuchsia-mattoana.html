Fuchsia mattoana

Fuchsia mattoana, commonly known as the Andean fuchsia, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces striking, pendulous flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant has lance-shaped leaves with a glossy surface and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in cool, moist climates and prefers partial to full shade.

Fuchsia mattoana is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
