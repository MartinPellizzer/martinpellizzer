Festuca maroccana subsp. pozzicola

Festuca maroccana subsp.

pozzicola, commonly known as Moroccan bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to dry, sunny environments. Its leaves are narrow and often bluish-green, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is valued in horticulture for its drought tolerance and ability to form dense, low-maintenance lawns.
