Fedia palmata

Fedia palmata, commonly known as the red sandalwood tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, straight trunk with a smooth, reddish-brown bark. The tree produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. Its leaves are large, pinnately compound, and often have a waxy texture.

Fedia palmata is valued for its durable, aromatic wood, which has been used historically in furniture and musical instruments.
