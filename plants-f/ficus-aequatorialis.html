Ficus aequatorialis

Ficus aequatorialis, commonly known as the Ecuadorian fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the humid lowland forests of South America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid climates with indirect sunlight and well-draining soil.

Ficus aequatorialis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
