Felicia fruticosa

Felicia fruticosa, commonly known as the golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of about 30 centimeters, featuring dense, bushy foliage. The plant produces bright yellow flowers with white centers, which bloom in clusters during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and coastal landscapes.

Felicia fruticosa is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
