Ficus picardae

Ficus picardae, commonly known as the Picard's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color. Ficus picardae is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ease of care.

Its growth habit is upright, with a tendency to develop aerial roots, making it suitable for hanging baskets or shelves.
