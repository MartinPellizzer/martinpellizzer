Ficus maitin

Ficus maitin, commonly known as the Maitin fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible figs that are typically consumed fresh or used in traditional culinary practices. Ficus maitin thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in tropical and subtropical regions.
