Ficus ledermannii

Ficus ledermannii, commonly known as the Ledermann's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Costa Rica and Panama. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into edible fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
