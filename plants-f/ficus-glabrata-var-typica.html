Ficus glabrata var. typica

Ficus glabrata var. typica, commonly known as the smooth-leaved fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, smooth leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits known as figs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus glabrata var. typica is relatively low maintenance and adapts well to indoor conditions with proper care.
