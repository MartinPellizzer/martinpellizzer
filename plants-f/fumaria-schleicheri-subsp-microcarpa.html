Fumaria schleicheri subsp. microcarpa

Fumaria schleicheri subsp.

microcarpa, commonly known as the small-fruited glory-of-the-snow, is a member of the Fumariaceae family. It is a herbaceous perennial plant characterized by its delicate, hairy stems and lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that are clustered in racemes, often appearing in early spring. Its fruits are capsule-like structures that contain several tiny seeds.

This subspecies is typically found in moist, shady habitats and is valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens.
