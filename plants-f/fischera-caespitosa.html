Fischera caespitosa

Fischera caespitosa, commonly known as the mountain sandwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the alpine regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its dense, cushion-like growth habit and small, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has alternate, linear leaves that are covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is often found in mountain meadows and slopes.

Fischera caespitosa plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for alpine insects and small mammals.
