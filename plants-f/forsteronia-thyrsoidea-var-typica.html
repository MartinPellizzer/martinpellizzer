Forsteronia thyrsoidea var. typica

Forsteronia thyrsoidea var. typica, commonly known as the slender false ivy, is a climbing plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thin, flexible stems and opposite, oval-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens, though it can be invasive in certain areas.
