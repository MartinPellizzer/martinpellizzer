Flemingia trifoliata

Flemingia trifoliata, commonly known as the three-leaf flemingia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small shrub characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets arranged in a cluster. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by capsule fruits containing numerous seeds. Flemingia trifoliata is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
