Faujasia gracilis

Faujasia gracilis, commonly known as the slender lily, is a herbaceous perennial native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces delicate, bell-shaped flowers in shades of white to pale pink. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and blooms during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens or alpine settings.

Its botanical characteristics make it a popular choice for gardeners interested in cultivating hardy, ornamental plants.
