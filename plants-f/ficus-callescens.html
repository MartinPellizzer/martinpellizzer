Ficus callescens

Ficus callescens, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and opposite, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Ficus callescens is often cultivated as a houseplant or for ornamental purposes due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.

Its ability to thrive in a variety of light conditions makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
