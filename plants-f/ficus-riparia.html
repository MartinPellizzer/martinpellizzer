Ficus riparia

Ficus riparia, commonly known as the river fig or swamp fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, Africa, and the Americas, often found near water sources such as rivers and wetlands. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green, with a waxy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits.

Ficus riparia is valued for its adaptability to moist environments and its role in local ecosystems.
