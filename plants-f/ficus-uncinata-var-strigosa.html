Ficus uncinata var. strigosa

Ficus uncinata var. strigosa, commonly known as the rough-leaved ficus, is a variant of the ficus genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its densely hairy, rough-textured leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its leaves are usually elliptical or ovate, with a waxy surface and serrated edges.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
