Funastrum cynanchoides var. subtruncatum

Funastrum cynanchoides var. subtruncatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaved devil's backbone, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a perennial herb with a distinctive, branching stem that often appears spiny. The leaves are typically narrow and opposite, with a slightly hairy surface. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, and its fruits are follicles that split open to release seeds.

This plant thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is often found in coastal areas.
