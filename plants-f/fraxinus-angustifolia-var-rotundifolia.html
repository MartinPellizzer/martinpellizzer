Fraxinus angustifolia var. rotundifolia

Fraxinus angustifolia var. rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf ash, is a variant of the green ash tree characterized by its distinctive round or oval-shaped leaves.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant has compound leaves that are typically arranged oppositely on the stems, with each leaflet being narrow and elongated. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
