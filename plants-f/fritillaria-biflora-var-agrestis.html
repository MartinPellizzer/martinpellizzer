Fritillaria biflora var. agrestis

Fritillaria biflora var. agrestis, commonly known as the meadow fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish-brown, with a distinctive waxy coating. The plant produces two to three flowers per stem, which bloom in early spring before the leaves emerge. Its bulbs are adapted to well-drained soils and can thrive in full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
