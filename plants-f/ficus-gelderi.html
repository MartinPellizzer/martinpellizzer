Ficus gelderi

Ficus gelderi, commonly known as the Gelder's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into edible figs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Ficus gelderi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
