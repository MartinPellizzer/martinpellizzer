Ficus kusanoi

Ficus kusanoi, commonly known as the Kusanoi fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically bright green and glossy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible figs.

Ficus kusanoi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor environments.
