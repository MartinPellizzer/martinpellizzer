Ficus recurva var. recurva

Ficus recurva var. recurva, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, cascading branches that give it a distinctive, tree-like appearance. The leaves are typically glossy, dark green, and elliptical in shape. It is a member of the Moraceae family and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded outdoor environments.

The plant is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of conditions, though it prefers indirect light and moderate humidity.
