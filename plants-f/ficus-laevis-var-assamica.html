Ficus laevis var. assamica

Ficus laevis var. assamica, commonly known as the Assam fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Indian subcontinent.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are an important food source for various animals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in dense forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is also cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
