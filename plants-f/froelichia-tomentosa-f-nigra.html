Froelichia tomentosa f. nigra

Froelichia tomentosa f. nigra, commonly known as the black froelichia, is a unique variant of the froelichia plant characterized by its dark foliage.

This evergreen shrub is native to specific regions of South America, particularly in Brazil. It features dense, leathery leaves that are typically covered in a fine, woolly texture, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators.

Due to its ornamental value and limited distribution, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and specialized plant collections.
