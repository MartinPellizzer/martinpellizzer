Fillaea suaveolens

Fillaea suaveolens, commonly known as the sweet-smelling flowering plant, is a species native to South Africa.

It is a perennial shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils. The plant is characterized by its aromatic leaves and small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. It thrives in full sun and is often found in coastal or mountainous regions.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens for its pleasant fragrance and hardy nature.
