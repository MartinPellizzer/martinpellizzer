Ficus moorei

Ficus moorei, commonly known as the Benjamin fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with silver or white markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers followed by small, round fig fruits. It is a popular indoor plant due to its adaptability and air-purifying qualities.

Ficus moorei thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate watering to maintain its lush foliage.
