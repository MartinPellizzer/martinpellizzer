Fagelia chelidoniodes

Fagelia chelidoniodes, commonly known as the white wood sorrel, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows to a height of about 12 to 18 inches. The plant features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive white or pale green coloration. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in umbels, often blooming in early spring.

Fagelia chelidoniodes is characterized by its delicate appearance and its preference for moist, shaded environments.
