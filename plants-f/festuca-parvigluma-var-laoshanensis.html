Festuca parvigluma var. laoshanensis

Festuca parvigluma var. laoshanensis, commonly known as the Lao Mountain fescue, is a perennial grass species native to China.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in mountainous regions, preferring cool, moist environments. Its inflorescence consists of a dense, spike-like cluster of small, two-lobed spikelets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
