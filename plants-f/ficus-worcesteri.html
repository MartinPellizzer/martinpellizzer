Ficus worcesteri

Ficus worcesteri, commonly known as the Worcester fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered fruit. The plant is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Ficus worcesteri is known for its ability to purify indoor air, making it a popular choice for interior landscaping.
