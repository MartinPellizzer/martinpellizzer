Ficus frangulina

Ficus frangulina, commonly known as the Indian rubber plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height.

Ficus frangulina is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its rubber-like latex, which has historical uses in rubber production.
