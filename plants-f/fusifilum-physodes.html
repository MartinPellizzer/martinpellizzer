Fusifilum physodes

Fusifilum physodes, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flax, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, greenish flowers arranged in clusters, which are typically borne on long, drooping peduncles. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a common sight in grassland habitats.

Its fibrous stalks have historically been used for textile production, similar to traditional flax species.
