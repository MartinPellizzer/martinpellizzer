Ficus blumei

Ficus blumei, commonly known as the Blume's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into edible figs. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Ficus blumei is also valued for its air-purifying properties and ability to thrive indoors with minimal care.
