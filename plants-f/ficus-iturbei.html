Ficus iturbei

Ficus iturbei, commonly known as the Iturbe fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Colombia and Ecuador. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus iturbei is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid environments.
