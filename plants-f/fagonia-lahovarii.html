Fagonia lahovarii

Fagonia lahovarii, commonly known as the desert fagonia, is a shrub native to arid regions of the Middle East and Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, woolly leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability. It often grows in rocky soils and is found in desert and semi-desert habitats.

Fagonia lahovarii is notable for its resilience and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
