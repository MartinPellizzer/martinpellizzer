Ficus johannis subsp. afghanistanica

Ficus johannis subsp.

afghanistanica, commonly known as the Afghan ficus, is a species native to the mountainous regions of Afghanistan and Pakistan. It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters.

The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy on the upper surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fig-like infrutescences that are important for its pollination process.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
