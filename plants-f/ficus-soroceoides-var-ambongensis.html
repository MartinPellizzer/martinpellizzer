Ficus soroceoides var. ambongensis

Ficus soroceoides var. ambongensis, commonly known as the Ambongensis Fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical rainforests of western Africa, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit, a small, smooth fig, is edible and often consumed by local wildlife and humans.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
