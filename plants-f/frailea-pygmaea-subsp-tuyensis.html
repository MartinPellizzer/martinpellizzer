Frailea pygmaea subsp. tuyensis

Frailea pygmaea subsp.

tuyensis, commonly known as the Tuyensis Porcupine Cactus, is a compact, low-growing cactus native to Ecuador. It features a globular or cylindrical stem with dense, spiny areoles covered in white, woolly hair.

The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a striking contrast to its spiky appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and slow growth, it is a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
