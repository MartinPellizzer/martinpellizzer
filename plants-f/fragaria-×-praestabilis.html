Fragaria × praestabilis

Fragaria × praestabilis, commonly known as the St. Valentine's strawberry, is a hybrid strawberry species originating from the crossing of Fragaria virginiana and Fragaria chiloensis.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped red fruits that are typically harvested in late winter or early spring. The plant produces runners and has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in cool climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its unique flavor and is often cultivated for ornamental and culinary purposes.
