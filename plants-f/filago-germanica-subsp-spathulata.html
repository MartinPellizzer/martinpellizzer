Filago germanica subsp. spathulata

Filago germanica subsp.

spathulata, commonly known as the broad-leafed filago, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads.

The plant typically grows in grasslands and open woodlands, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are broad and elliptical, with a distinctive silvery underside.

It produces small yellow flowers that bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators.
