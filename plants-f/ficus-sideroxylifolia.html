Ficus sideroxylifolia

Ficus sideroxylifolia, commonly known as the ironwood fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood and dark, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its robust growth and adaptability, it is sometimes cultivated as an ornamental or for its durable timber.
