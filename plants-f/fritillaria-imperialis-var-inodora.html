Fritillaria imperialis var. inodora

Fritillaria imperialis var. inodora, commonly known as the Crown Imperial, is a striking bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped, often orange or red flowers at the top. The leaves are basal, forming a rosette that is often covered in a waxy coating. This variety is notable for its lack of a strong fragrance, distinguishing it from other Fritillaria species.

It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.
