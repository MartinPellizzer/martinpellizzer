Ficus glareosa var. obpandurifolia

Ficus glareosa var. obpandurifolia, commonly known as the glossy fig or velvet fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, and often variegated leaves that exhibit a range of colors from deep green to shades of red and purple. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits that are typically consumed raw or cooked. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
