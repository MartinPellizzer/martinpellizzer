Festuca ovina var. tarraconensis

Festuca ovina var. tarraconensis, commonly known as the Tarraconensis sheep fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green, narrow leaves. This variety thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as grasslands and rocky slopes. It is adapted to Mediterranean climates and exhibits drought tolerance.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.
