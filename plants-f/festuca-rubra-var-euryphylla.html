Festuca rubra var. euryphylla

Festuca rubra var. euryphylla, commonly known as red fescue, is a cool-season grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and a bluish-green color, with reddish tones appearing in sunny conditions. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, lawns, and along stream banks. It has a deep root system that helps prevent soil erosion and improves soil structure.

Festuca rubra var. euryphylla is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
