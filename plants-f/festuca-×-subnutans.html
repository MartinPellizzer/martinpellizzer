Festuca × subnutans

Festuca × subnutans, commonly known as silvergrass, is a hybrid grass species native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and tufted growth habit, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant typically grows to a height of 12 to 30 inches and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is drought-tolerant and prefers well-drained soils, making it suitable for a variety of garden settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its ability to form dense, low-growing clumps that provide year-round visual interest.
