Flourensia corymbosa var. araucana

Flourensia corymbosa var. araucana, commonly known as the Araucana flourensia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, silver-gray foliage and showy yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has small, oval leaves with a pubescent surface, which help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in xeriscaping due to its drought tolerance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
