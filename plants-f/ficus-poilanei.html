Ficus poilanei

Ficus poilanei, commonly known as the Madagascar fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the island of Madagascar and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible fig-like fruits. Ficus poilanei is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both indoor and outdoor gardens.
