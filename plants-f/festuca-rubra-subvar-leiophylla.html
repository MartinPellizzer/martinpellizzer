Festuca rubra subvar. leiophylla

Festuca rubra subvar. leiophylla, commonly known as red fescue, is a cool-season grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green blades and dense growth habit, making it suitable for lawns and erosion control. The plant has a tufted growth form with short, rhizomatous stolons that help it spread and establish quickly. It prefers well-drained soils and can tolerate a range of environmental conditions, including drought and shade.

Its leaves are typically bluish-green and have a slightly hairy texture, contributing to its ornamental appeal.
