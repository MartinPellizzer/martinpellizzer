Festuca subspicata

Festuca subspicata, commonly known as slender fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and disturbed soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous florets. Its flowering period usually occurs in late spring to early summer.

Festuca subspicata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
