Festuca billyi

Festuca billyi, commonly known as Billy's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine environments.

Festuca billyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing mountainous terrain.
