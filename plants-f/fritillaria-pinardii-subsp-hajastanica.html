Fritillaria pinardii subsp. hajastanica

Fritillaria pinardii subsp.

hajastanica, commonly known as the Caucasian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region. It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish-brown in color.

The plant has a fibrous root system and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
