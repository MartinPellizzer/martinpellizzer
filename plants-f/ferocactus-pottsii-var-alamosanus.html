Ferocactus pottsii var. alamosanus

Ferocactus pottsii var. alamosanus, commonly known as the Alamosa barrel cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, ribbed stems that can grow up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers in the spring, which are typically clustered at the tips of the stems. Its spines are dense and can vary in color from yellow to brown.

This variety is adapted to arid environments and requires well-draining soil and minimal water.
