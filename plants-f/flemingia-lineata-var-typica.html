Flemingia lineata var. typica

Flemingia lineata var. typica, commonly known as the broad-leafed flemingia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its compound leaves, which are typically broad and ovate, and its small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. It produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind or animals.

Flemingia lineata var. typica is sometimes cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and floral display.
