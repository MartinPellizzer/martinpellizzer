Festuca lemanii

Festuca lemanii, commonly known as the Leman's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, fluffy seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Festuca lemanii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its hardy and low-maintenance nature.
