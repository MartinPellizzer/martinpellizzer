Festuca samensis

Festuca samensis, commonly known as Sam's fescue, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green, narrow leaves. The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer. Festuca samensis is adapted to cold climates and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and restoration projects.
