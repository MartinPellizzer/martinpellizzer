Festuca pseudovina f. barbulata

Festuca pseudovina f. barbulata, commonly known as the tufted bluegrass, is a perennial grass species characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and bluish-green foliage.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and is often found in moist, well-drained soils. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue to violet flowers, which are typically short-lived. Its leaves are narrow and have a distinctive hairy texture, especially on the underside.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
