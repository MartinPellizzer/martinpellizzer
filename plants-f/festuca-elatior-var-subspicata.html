Festuca elatior var. subspicata

Festuca elatior var. subspicata, commonly known as the tall fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and dense, fine-textured foliage. The plant typically reaches heights of 30 to 100 centimeters and produces spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Festuca elatior var. subspicata is often used in ornamental landscaping and for erosion control due to its hardiness and adaptability.
