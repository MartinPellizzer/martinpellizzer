Ficus laevigata var. hispaniolae

Ficus laevigata var. hispaniolae, commonly known as the Hispaniola fig, is a species native to the island of Hispaniola.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has broad, heart-shaped leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

The plant is well adapted to humid climates and is often found in forested areas.
