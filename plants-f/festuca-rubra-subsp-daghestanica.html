Festuca rubra subsp. daghestanica

Festuca rubra subsp.

daghestanica, commonly known as the Daghestan red fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Caucasus region. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, often reddish-colored stems.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to temperate to cold climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally used in ecological restoration projects.
