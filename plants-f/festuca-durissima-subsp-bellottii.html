Festuca durissima subsp. bellottii

Festuca durissima subsp.

bellottii, commonly known as the bellott's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to dry, rocky habitats.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in arid environments.
