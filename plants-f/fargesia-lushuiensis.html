Fargesia lushuiensis

Fargesia lushuiensis, commonly known as the Lushui bamboo, is a species native to China, particularly in Yunnan Province.

It is a clump-forming bamboo characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its cold hardiness and ability to thrive in shaded or semi-shaded environments. Its foliage is often a deep green, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are not typically showy.

Fargesia lushuiensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and its role in traditional Chinese landscaping.
