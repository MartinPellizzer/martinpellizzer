Fagara hartii

Fagara hartii, commonly known as the Caribbean prickly ash, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to the Caribbean region.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall. The plant is characterized by its elliptical, leathery leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers. Its fruit is a globose capsule that splits open to release small, black seeds.

Fagara hartii is often found in moist, well-drained soils in tropical and subtropical climates.
