Festuca djimilensis

Festuca djimilensis, commonly known as the Djimil grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Festuca djimilensis is adapted to cold and arid environments, often found in high-altitude meadows and rocky slopes.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various alpine organisms.
