Freycinetia villarii

Freycinetia villarii, commonly known as the New Guinea screw pine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Oceania.

It is characterized by its unique spiral arrangement of leaves, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a capsule that contains numerous seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Freycinetia villarii is often found growing in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and sandy soils.
