Ficus lachnocarpa

Ficus lachnocarpa, commonly known as the black fig or black-fig ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its dark, almost black, fruit that is typically small and clustered. Its leaves are thick, glossy, and heart-shaped, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

The plant is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
