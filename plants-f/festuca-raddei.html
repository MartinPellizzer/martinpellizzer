Festuca raddei

Festuca raddei, commonly known as Radde's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, dark green foliage. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes. Its inflorescence consists of a compact, cylindrical spike that bears small, two-ranked spikelets.

Festuca raddei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
