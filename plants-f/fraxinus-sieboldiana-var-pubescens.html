Fraxinus sieboldiana var. pubescens

Fraxinus sieboldiana var. pubescens, commonly known as the Japanese ash or Manchurian ash, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, which are followed by samaroid fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
