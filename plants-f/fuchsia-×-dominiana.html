Fuchsia × dominiana

Fuchsia × dominiana, commonly known as the common fuchsia, is a hybrid plant belonging to the family Onagraceae.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences that display vibrant shades of pink, purple, and red. The plant is native to South America, particularly the Andes region, where it thrives in cool, moist climates. Fuchsia × dominiana is a deciduous shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring opposite, ovate leaves with a glossy surface.

Its flowers are not only ornamental but also attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
