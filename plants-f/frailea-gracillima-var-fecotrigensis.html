Frailea gracillima var. fecotrigensis

Frailea gracillima var. fecotrigensis, commonly known as the "Coral Cactus," is a species native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and vibrant, branching stems that often display a pinkish or reddish hue. The plant produces small, white flowers during the warmer months, which are typically followed by edible, red fruits. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it well-suited for xerophytic gardens.

Due to its unique appearance and hardiness, it is a popular choice among cactus enthusiasts and collectors.
