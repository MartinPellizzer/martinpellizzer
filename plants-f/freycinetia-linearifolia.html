Freycinetia linearifolia

Freycinetia linearifolia, commonly known as the slender freycinetia, is a species of cycad native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, unbranched trunk and large, pinnately compound leaves that can reach several meters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish-green cones, with male and female cones occurring on separate plants. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires a warm, humid climate.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
