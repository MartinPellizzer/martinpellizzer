Festuca hieronymi var. expansa

Festuca hieronymi var. expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained, loamy soils and full to partial sunlight.

Festuca hieronymi var. expansa is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
