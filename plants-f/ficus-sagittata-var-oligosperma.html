Ficus sagittata var. oligosperma

Ficus sagittata var. oligosperma, commonly known as the arrowleaf fig, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its distinctive arrow-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are often consumed by local populations. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Ficus sagittata var. oligosperma is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitats.
