Farfugium japonicum f. luteofuscus

Farfugium japonicum f. luteofuscus, commonly known as the golden-yellow swamp taro, is a variant of the Farfugium genus native to Japan.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its large, broad, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with a yellowish or brownish margin. The plant produces clusters of small, yellowish-green flowers in the summer months, which are followed by orange-red berries. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to tolerate wet conditions, making it suitable for water garden settings.
