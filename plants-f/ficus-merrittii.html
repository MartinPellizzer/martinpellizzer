Ficus merrittii

Ficus merrittii, commonly known as the Merritt's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits. Ficus merrittii thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Its growth habit is typically shrub-like, with a dense canopy that provides shade and habitat for various wildlife species.
