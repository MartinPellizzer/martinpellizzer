Forestiera acuminata var. parvifolia

Forestiera acuminata var. parvifolia, commonly known as the narrowleaf forestiera, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in clusters during the spring, which attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its adaptability to well-drained soils makes it suitable for xeriscaping and native gardens.

The shrub is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
