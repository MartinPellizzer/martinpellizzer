Frankenia strigosa

Frankenia strigosa, commonly known as the sand verbena, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub characterized by its small, narrow leaves and clusters of yellow or orange flowers. The plant thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is often found in dune environments. It has adapted to withstand saline and arid conditions, making it a hardy species.

Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.
