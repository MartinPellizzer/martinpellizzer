Ficus megacarpa

Ficus megacarpa, commonly known as the giant fig tree, is a tropical species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus megacarpa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
