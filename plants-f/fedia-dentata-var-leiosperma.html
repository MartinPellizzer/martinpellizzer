Fedia dentata var. leiosperma

Fedia dentata var. leiosperma, commonly known as the smooth-leaved fedia, is a lesser-known variant of the fedia species native to specific regions of South America.

This plant is characterized by its serrated leaf margins and smooth, glossy foliage, which distinguish it from other fedia varieties. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest understories, where it thrives in partial to full shade. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow, contributing to its ornamental value.

Its botanical significance lies in its unique morphological traits and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
