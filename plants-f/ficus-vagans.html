Ficus vagans

Ficus vagans, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, trailing growth habit and glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by green, fleshy fruits that turn red when mature. It thrives in humid environments and is often used as a ground cover or climbing plant in gardens.

Due to its adaptability, it is popular in both indoor and outdoor settings.
