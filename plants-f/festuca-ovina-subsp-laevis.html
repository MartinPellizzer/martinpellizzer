Festuca ovina subsp. laevis

Festuca ovina subsp.

laevis, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its fine, hairless blades and compact growth habit, making it a desirable ornamental and turfgrass.

The plant typically forms dense, low-growing mats with a bluish-green coloration due to its waxy leaf surfaces. It prefers well-drained soils and full to partial sunlight, often found in meadows and grasslands. Festuca ovina subsp.

laevis is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.
