Frumentum sativum

Frumentum sativum, commonly known as barley, is a cereal grass that belongs to the Poaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender stalks and small, spikelet-like inflorescences. The plant produces edible grains that are widely used in food, animal feed, and brewing. Barley is adapted to temperate climates and thrives in well-drained soils.

Its life cycle includes a period of rapid growth followed by grain maturation, making it an important crop in many agricultural systems.
