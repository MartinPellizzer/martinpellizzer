Fagus sylvatica var. zlatia

Fagus sylvatica var. zlatia, commonly known as the golden beech, is a rare variant of the common beech tree.

It is characterized by its striking golden foliage, which develops in the autumn, giving it a unique visual appeal. This variety is native to the Carpathian Mountains in Europe and thrives in temperate climates. The leaves are typically smaller and more elongated compared to the standard beech.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and is often planted in gardens and parks.
