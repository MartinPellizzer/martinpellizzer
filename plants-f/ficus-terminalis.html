Ficus terminalis

Ficus terminalis, commonly known as the common fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and parts of southern Europe, North Africa, and the Middle East. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that develop into the edible fig fruit. Ficus terminalis is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, with a dense canopy and a smooth, greyish-brown bark.

The fruit is a key food source for various animals and has been cultivated for thousands of years for human consumption.
