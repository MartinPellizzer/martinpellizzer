Francoa appendiculata f. violacea

Francoa appendiculata f. violacea, commonly known as the violet-fringed francoa, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features showy, violet-colored bracts that resemble petals, giving it a striking appearance. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes.
