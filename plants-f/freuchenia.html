Freuchenia

Freuchenia, commonly known as the African flame tree, is a genus of flowering plants in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to various regions across Africa, with species such as Freuchenia africana being particularly notable. The plant is characterized by its striking, bright red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters, making it a visually striking addition to gardens. Its leaves are typically compound, consisting of several small leaflets, and it can grow as a shrub or small tree.

Freuchenia is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its potential ecological benefits, including nitrogen fixation through its legume roots.
