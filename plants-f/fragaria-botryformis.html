Fragaria botryformis

Fragaria botryformis, commonly known as the clustered strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its low-growing habit and produces small, edible fruits that are similar in appearance to common strawberries. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils.

Due to its ornamental value and edible fruits, it is cultivated in some regions for both aesthetic and culinary purposes.
