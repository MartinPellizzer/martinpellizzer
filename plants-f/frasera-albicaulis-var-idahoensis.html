Frasera albicaulis var. idahoensis

Frasera albicaulis var. idahoensis, commonly known as Idaho whiteflower frasera, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting pollinators.
