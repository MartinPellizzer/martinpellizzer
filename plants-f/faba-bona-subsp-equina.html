Faba bona subsp. equina

Faba bona subsp.

equina, commonly known as the horse bean, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family. It is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive white or pale green color.

The plant produces elongated, bean-like pods that contain several seeds, which are often used as a food source. It thrives in temperate climates and is commonly found in fields and along roadsides.

The species is notable for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil and supports agricultural practices.
