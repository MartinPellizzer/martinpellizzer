Ficus cundinamarcensis

Ficus cundinamarcensis, commonly known as the Cundinamarca fig, is a species of flowering plant native to Colombia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often found in shaded, humid environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing food for various wildlife species and contributing to forest biodiversity.
