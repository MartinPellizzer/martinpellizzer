Ficus ampelos var. obversifolia

Ficus ampelos var. obversifolia, commonly known as the silver-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its striking, glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery or grayish underside. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is commonly cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its attractive foliage. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible figs, though the fruit is rarely seen in cultivated forms.

This variety is prized for its adaptability to various light conditions and its ability to thrive in humid environments.
