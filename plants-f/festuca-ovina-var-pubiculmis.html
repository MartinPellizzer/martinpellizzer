Festuca ovina var. pubiculmis

Festuca ovina var. pubiculmis, commonly known as sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, silver-green foliage. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides. Its inflorescence consists of spikelets arranged in a panicle, which produces small, two-lobed flowers.

Festuca ovina var. pubiculmis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
