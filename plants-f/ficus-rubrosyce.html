Ficus rubrosyce

Ficus rubrosyce, commonly known as the red-veined ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent reddish veins, which give it its distinctive appearance. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Ficus rubrosyce is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
