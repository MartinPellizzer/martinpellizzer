Fritillaria neglecta

Fritillaria neglecta, commonly known as the western fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the western United States.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or yellow with intricate patterns. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, often found in rocky or gravelly habitats. Its bulbs are adapted for survival in arid environments, with dormancy periods during dry seasons.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
