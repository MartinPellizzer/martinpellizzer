Filipendula hexapetala f. glabra

Filipendula hexapetala f. glabra, commonly known as the smooth meadow sweet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the temperate regions of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its clustered, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has simple, alternate, pinnately compound leaves with serrated edges, and its stems are generally smooth, giving it the 'glabra' designation. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

This plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
