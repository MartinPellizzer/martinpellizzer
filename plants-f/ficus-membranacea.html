Ficus membranacea

Ficus membranacea, commonly known as the velvetleaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically harvested when ripe. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
