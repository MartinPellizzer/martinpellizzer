Fraxinus caroliniana var. rehderiana

Fraxinus caroliniana var. rehderiana, commonly known as the swamp sweetgum, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by spiny, spherical fruits containing seeds. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in swamps, floodplains, and along riverbanks.

Its botanical features make it an important species in wetland ecosystems.
