Funkia ovata var. albomarginata

Funkia ovata var. albomarginata, commonly known as the white-margin daylily, is a striking plant characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and showy, trumpet-shaped flowers.

It is native to eastern Asia and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. The flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer, feature a striking white margin contrasting with a deep yellow center. This variety is prized for its ornamental value in gardens and wetland habitats.

It is a perennial herb that spreads through rhizomes, forming dense clumps over time.
