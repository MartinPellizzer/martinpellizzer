Fothergilla alnifolia var. obtusa

Fothergilla alnifolia var. obtusa, commonly known as the smoothleaf fothergilla, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and clusters of fragrant, white or pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically elliptical, with a smooth margin, and turn vibrant shades of red or orange in the fall. This plant prefers well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a valuable addition to native gardens.

It is also noted for its ability to thrive in moist, woodland environments.
