Flacourtia cordata

Flacourtia cordata, commonly known as the heartleaf flacourtia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small evergreen shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by orange-red berries. Its leaves are ovate to elliptic in shape, often with a heart-shaped base, giving it its common name.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic features.
