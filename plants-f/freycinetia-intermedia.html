Freycinetia intermedia

Freycinetia intermedia, commonly known as the sandpaper fig, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by edible, fig-like fruits. Its leaves are thick and covered in a fine layer of silky hair, giving them a distinctive sandpaper texture.

The plant is adapted to coastal and sandy soils, making it a hardy species in tropical and subtropical regions.
