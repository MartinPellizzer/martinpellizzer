Ficus sulawesiana

Ficus sulawesiana, commonly known as the Sulawesi fig, is a species of flowering plant native to the Indonesian island of Sulawesi.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, broad leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

Ficus sulawesiana is also valued for its role in local ecosystems, providing food and habitat for a variety of wildlife.
