Festuca cinerea f. longiaristata

Festuca cinerea f. longiaristata, commonly known as silver hair grass, is a grass species characterized by its gray-green foliage and long, slender leaves.

It is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, often found in moist, well-drained soils. The inflorescence consists of a loose panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are typically pale green to brown in color. This variety is distinguished by its elongated, narrow leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance in gardens and natural landscapes.

It is valued for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in a range of environmental conditions.
