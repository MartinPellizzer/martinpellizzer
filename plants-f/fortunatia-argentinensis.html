Fortunatia argentinensis

Fortunatia argentinensis, commonly known as the Argentine starflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, star-shaped white or pink petals and small, clustered flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a smooth, glossy surface.

Fortunatia argentinensis is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its spring bloom.
