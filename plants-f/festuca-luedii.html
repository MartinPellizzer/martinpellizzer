Festuca luedii

Festuca luedii, commonly known as Luedi's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine habitats, preferring well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue to purple flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and ability to thrive in challenging environments.

Festuca luedii is also noted for its role in stabilizing soil in erosion-prone areas.
