Ferula fukanensis

Ferula fukanensis, commonly known as the Fukan's giant fennel, is a tall, perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes plants like parsley and carrots, and is characterized by its hollow, ridged stems and feathery leaves. The plant produces large, yellowish-green umbels of flowers that bloom in late summer. Its taproot is thick and fibrous, and it is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Ferula fukanensis is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
