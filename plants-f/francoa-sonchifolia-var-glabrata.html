Francoa sonchifolia var. glabrata

Francoa sonchifolia var. glabrata, commonly known as the smooth francoa, is a flowering plant native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features upright, branching stems covered in soft, hairy foliage that becomes smooth as it matures. Its showy yellow flowers bloom in late summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

It prefers moist, well-drained soils and partial to full sun, making it a versatile addition to native gardens.
