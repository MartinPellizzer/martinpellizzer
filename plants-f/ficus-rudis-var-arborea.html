Ficus rudis var. arborea

Ficus rudis var. arborea, commonly known as the tree fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring a broad, spreading canopy. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
