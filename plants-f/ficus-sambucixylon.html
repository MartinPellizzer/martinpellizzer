Ficus sambucixylon

Ficus sambucixylon, commonly known as the African ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs, which are a food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Ficus sambucixylon is also valued for its ability to purify indoor air, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
