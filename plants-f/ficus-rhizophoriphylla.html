Ficus rhizophoriphylla

Ficus rhizophoriphylla, commonly known as the mangrove fig, is a unique species native to coastal areas of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its ability to thrive in saline and waterlogged soils, making it well adapted to mangrove ecosystems. The plant exhibits a specialized root system, including pneumatophores, which help in gas exchange in submerged conditions. Its leaves are typically thick and waxy, reducing water loss in humid environments.

Ficus rhizophoriphylla plays a vital role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various marine and terrestrial organisms.
