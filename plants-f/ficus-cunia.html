Ficus cunia

Ficus cunia, commonly known as the Indian rubber plant, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically hidden within the leaf axils. Ficus cunia is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Its roots can develop aerial roots, which can be used for propagation and contribute to its resilience in various environments.
