Festuca rubra var. duriuscula

Festuca rubra var. duriuscula, commonly known as red fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green blades and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. It is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Festuca rubra var. duriuscula is also used in landscaping and as a component in turfgrass mixtures.
