Felicia tenella subsp. pusilla

Felicia tenella subsp.

pusilla, commonly known as the dwarf blueweed, is a small, compact perennial herb native to southern Africa. It features small, blue to violet flowers arranged in dense, spherical clusters, which bloom from spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

Due to its compact size and ornamental flowers, it is a popular choice for drought-tolerant landscaping.
