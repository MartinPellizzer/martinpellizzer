Festuca ovina var. brevifolia

Festuca ovina var. brevifolia, commonly known as sheep's fescue, is a low-growing perennial grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces dense clusters of small, spike-like flowers. This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often used in landscaping for erosion control and ornamental purposes. It thrives in well-drained soils and full to partial sun, making it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.

The plant is valued for its hardiness and ability to form dense, attractive turf-like coverage.
