Fremontodendron californicum subsp. napense

Fremontodendron californicum subsp.

napense, commonly known as the coastal fremontodendron, is a shrub native to coastal regions of California and southern Oregon. It is characterized by its gray-green foliage and showy yellow-orange flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer.

The plant typically grows in sandy or gravelly soils along coastal bluffs and dunes. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to the harsh coastal environment.

Its subspecific name, *napense*, refers to its occurrence in the Napa Valley area.
