Ficus spadicea

Ficus spadicea, commonly known as the white fig or spadicea fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in a spadix, giving it its scientific name.

Ficus spadicea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
