Filago serrae

Filago serrae, commonly known as the serrated filago, is a flowering plant belonging to the Asteraceae family.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in rocky and sandy soils. The plant features small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in summer, and its leaves are serrated, giving it its common name. Filago serrae is often found in coastal and arid environments due to its drought-resistant characteristics.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and supporting local pollinator populations.
