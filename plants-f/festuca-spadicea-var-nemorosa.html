Festuca spadicea var. nemorosa

Festuca spadicea var. nemorosa, commonly known as wood sedge, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks, thriving in well-drained soils. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often dark green in color. Its flowers are small and clustered in spikelets, producing a delicate, spadix-like inflorescence.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
