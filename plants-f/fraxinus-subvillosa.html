Fraxinus subvillosa

Fraxinus subvillosa, commonly known as the silky ash, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and slender, ascending branches. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate to elliptic in shape, with finely serrated margins. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbels, followed by winged samaras that are dispersed by wind.

This species is typically found in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in forested areas.
