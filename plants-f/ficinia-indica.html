Ficinia indica

Ficinia indica, commonly known as the Indian fig tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height, with a broad, spreading canopy. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth, waxy surface. Its flowers are small and clustered in inflorescences, typically appearing in green or yellowish-green hues.

The fruit is a small, round drupe that turns red when mature, containing a single seed.
