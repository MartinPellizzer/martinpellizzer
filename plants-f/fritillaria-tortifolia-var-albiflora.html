Fritillaria tortifolia var. albiflora

Fritillaria tortifolia var. albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are predominantly white or pale yellow, often with a greenish or reddish hue at the base. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that form a basal rosette, and its bulbs are characterized by their thick, fibrous structure. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Due to its ornamental value and unique floral display, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts of rare flora.
