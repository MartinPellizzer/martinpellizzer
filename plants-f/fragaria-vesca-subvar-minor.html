Fragaria vesca subvar. minor

Fragaria vesca subvar. minor, commonly known as the alpine strawberry, is a wild strawberry species native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is a low-growing perennial plant with trifoliate leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces edible berries that are typically smaller and more intensely flavored than those of the common strawberry. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

Due to its ornamental value and culinary uses, it is cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
