Faya

Faya, commonly known as the Socotra cypress, is a unique and ancient tree native to the island of Socotra in the Arabian Sea.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbrella-shaped crown and twisted, gnarled branches. The plant has scale-like leaves that are arranged in dense clusters, giving it a feathery appearance. It is well adapted to the arid and harsh conditions of its native habitat.

The faya is also notable for its historical and cultural significance in the region.
