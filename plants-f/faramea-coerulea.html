Faramea coerulea

Faramea coerulea, commonly known as the blue hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Americas.

It is characterized by its striking blue or purple flowers that bloom in clusters, adding vibrant color to its natural habitat. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a rough texture, while its stems are woody and covered in fine hairs.

Faramea coerulea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
