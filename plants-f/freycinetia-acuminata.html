Freycinetia acuminata

Freycinetia acuminata, commonly known as the broad-leafed pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and cooking. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into spiny, triangular fruits. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread vegetatively, making it a common ornamental and useful plant in coastal areas.

Freycinetia acuminata is also valued for its ability to thrive in saline and coastal environments.
