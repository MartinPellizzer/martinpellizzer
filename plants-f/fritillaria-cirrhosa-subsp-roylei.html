Fritillaria cirrhosa subsp. roylei

Fritillaria cirrhosa subsp.

roylei, commonly known as the Kashmir bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Himalayas. It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow.

The plant typically grows at high altitudes, often in rocky or alpine environments. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
