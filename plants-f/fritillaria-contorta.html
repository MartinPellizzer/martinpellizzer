Fritillaria contorta

Fritillaria contorta, commonly known as the twisted fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, twisted, and often spiraled leaves, which are typically gray-green in color. The plant produces bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with a distinctive black or purple pattern. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Fritillaria contorta is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
