Flemingia macrophylla

Flemingia macrophylla, commonly known as the giant flame tree, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves and showy, red or orange flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its rapid growth rate makes it a popular choice for ornamental purposes in gardens and urban landscapes.

Flemingia macrophylla is also valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
