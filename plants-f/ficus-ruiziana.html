Ficus ruiziana

Ficus ruiziana, commonly known as the Ruiz's fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually glossy and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are edible and serve as a food source for various animals. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Ficus ruiziana is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens.
