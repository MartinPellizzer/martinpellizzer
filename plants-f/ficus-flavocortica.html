Ficus flavocortica

Ficus flavocortica, commonly known as the yellow-barked fig tree, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale yellow bark that becomes fissured with age. The plant produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. Its leaves are heart-shaped, glossy, and often have a waxy texture.

Ficus flavocortica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
