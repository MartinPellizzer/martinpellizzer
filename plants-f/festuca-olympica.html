Festuca olympica

Festuca olympica, commonly known as Olympic bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Greece.

It is characterized by its fine, bluish-green foliage and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its compact size and ornamental appeal make it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Festuca olympica is also valued for its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
