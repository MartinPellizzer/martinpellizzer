Ficus subgelderi var. rigida

Ficus subgelderi var. rigida, commonly known as the rigid ficus, is a shrub-like species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, greenish fruits that mature to a reddish color. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor decoration and air purification.
