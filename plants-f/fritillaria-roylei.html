Fritillaria roylei

Fritillaria roylei, commonly known as the Persian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine habitats, thriving in well-drained soils. Its bulbs are adapted for survival in harsh environments, allowing it to endure cold winters and dry summers.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with specific horticultural care.
