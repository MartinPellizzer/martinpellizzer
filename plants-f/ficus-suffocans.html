Ficus suffocans

Ficus suffocans, commonly known as the suffocating fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It produces small, clustered figs that are typically green or yellow when mature.

The plant is often found in humid, shaded environments such as rainforests.
