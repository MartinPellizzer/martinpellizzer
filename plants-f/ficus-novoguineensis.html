Ficus novoguineensis

Ficus novoguineensis, commonly known as the New Guinea fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the lowland rainforests of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its glossy, broad leaves that are often heart-shaped or ovate in form. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus novoguineensis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
