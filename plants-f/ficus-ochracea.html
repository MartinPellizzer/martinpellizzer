Ficus ochracea

Ficus ochracea, commonly known as the white fig or ochracea fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, including countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a vibrant green with a waxy texture. It produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, pear-shaped fruits.

Ficus ochracea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
