Ficus congensis

Ficus congensis, commonly known as the Congo fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly the Congo Basin. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. Its fruit, a fig, is typically green when mature and is edible.

Ficus congensis is often found in lowland rainforests and thrives in humid, shaded environments.
