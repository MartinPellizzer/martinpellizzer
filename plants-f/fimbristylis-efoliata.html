Fimbristylis efoliata

Fimbristylis efoliata, commonly known as the broad-leaved flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wetland and swampy environments, often forming dense clumps near water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often broader towards the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it produces small, three-lobed seeds.

Fimbristylis efoliata plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
