Ficus microcarpa var. fuyuensis

Ficus microcarpa var. fuyuensis, commonly known as the Japanese banyan, is a variant of the banyan tree native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are followed by round, greenish fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental tree.

Its aerial roots can form a dense network, contributing to its distinctive, tree-like appearance.
