Ferula sancta

Ferula sancta, commonly known as the sacred fennel, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like parsley and dill. The plant is characterized by its tall, erect stem and yellowish-green flowers that bloom in umbrella-shaped clusters. Its leaves are finely divided and have a strong, pungent aroma.

Ferula sancta is also valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional herbal remedies for centuries.
