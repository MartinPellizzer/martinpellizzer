Felicia pappei

Felicia pappei, commonly known as the yellow fever flower, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces bright yellow ray flowers surrounded by a cluster of small, tubular florets. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, often growing in rocky or sandy habitats.

Felicia pappei is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
