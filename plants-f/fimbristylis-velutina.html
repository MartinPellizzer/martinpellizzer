Fimbristylis velutina

Fimbristylis velutina, commonly known as silky flat sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its triangular, grass-like stems and narrow leaves. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded soils, often found in marshes, wetlands, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a dense cluster of small, spike-like flowers, and it produces tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Fimbristylis velutina is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
