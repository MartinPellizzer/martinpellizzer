Festuca rhachiantha

Festuca rhachiantha, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada brome, is a perennial grass native to the western United States.

It typically grows in high-elevation environments such as meadows and open slopes. The plant has slender, wiry stems and produces small, spike-like clusters of seeds. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green color.

Festuca rhachiantha plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
