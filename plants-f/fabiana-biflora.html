Fabiana biflora

Fabiana biflora, commonly known as the two-flowered fabiana, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Solanaceae, which includes nightshade plants. The plant is characterized by its opposite, ovate leaves and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. It produces berries that are typically green when immature and turn red when ripe.

Fabiana biflora is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and flowering habit.
