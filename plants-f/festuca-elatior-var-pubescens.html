Festuca elatior var. pubescens

Festuca elatior var. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit, with dense, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodland edges, and rocky areas. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with slender, pale green to blue-green flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits, providing habitat and food for various insects and small animals.
