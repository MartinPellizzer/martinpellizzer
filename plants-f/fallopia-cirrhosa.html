Fallopia cirrhosa

Fallopia cirrhosa, commonly known as Chinese knotweed, is a perennial plant native to Asia.

It belongs to the Polygonaceae family and is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit. The plant features hollow, bamboo-like stems that are often reddish in color. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and have a distinctive reddish or purple tint.

Fallopia cirrhosa is known for its rapid growth and can become invasive in certain environments.
