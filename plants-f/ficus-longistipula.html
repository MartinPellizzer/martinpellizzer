Ficus longistipula

Ficus longistipula, commonly known as the long-stemmed fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its elongated, slender stems and broad, heart-shaped leaves. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature.

Ficus longistipula is often found growing in moist, shaded environments such as forests and along riverbanks.
