Filipendula vulgaris f. hevesiensis

Filipendula vulgaris f. hevesiensis, commonly known as the Hungarian meadow sweet, is a horticultural form of the common meadow sweet.

It is a herbaceous perennial plant belonging to the Rosaceae family, characterized by its finely divided, fern-like leaves and showy, pink to red flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It is valued in gardens for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.

This form is notable for its compact growth habit compared to the typical species.
