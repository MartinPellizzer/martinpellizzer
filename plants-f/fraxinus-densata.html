Fraxinus densata

Fraxinus densata, commonly known as the dense-fruited ash.

It is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America, characterized by its dark green, oval-shaped leaves and smooth, gray bark. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring, followed by clusters of small, winged seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Fraxinus densata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
