Ficus terasoensis

Ficus terasoensis, commonly known as the Teraso fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the island of Teraso in the Philippines and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible figs. It thrives in tropical climates and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
