Fritzschia integrifolia

Fritzschia integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leafed fritzschia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of South America.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads and typically herbaceous growth habit. The plant features lanceolate, smooth, and often hairy leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers are small and clustered in capitula, with ray florets that are typically yellow or white in color.

Fritzschia integrifolia is known for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
