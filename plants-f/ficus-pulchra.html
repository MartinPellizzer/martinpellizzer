Ficus pulchra

Ficus pulchra, commonly known as the beauty leaf, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, heart-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white and green patterns. The plant belongs to the Moraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in indoor environments with moderate light. Its aerial roots allow it to climb and attach to surfaces, making it a popular choice for hanging baskets or trained growth.

Ficus pulchra requires well-draining soil and regular watering to maintain its lush foliage.
