Ferulago trifida subsp. carduchorum

Ferulago trifida subsp.

carduchorum, commonly known as the Armenian bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus region. It features slender, branched stems and lanceolate leaves with a hairy surface.

The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers in shades of blue or purple, typically blooming in late spring. Its flowers are clustered in racemes, and it is often found in rocky or dry habitats.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique floral morphology and geographic distribution.
