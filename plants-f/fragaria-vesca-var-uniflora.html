Fragaria vesca var. uniflora

Fragaria vesca var. uniflora, commonly known as the wild strawberry, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is characterized by its small, delicate flowers that typically have five white petals and a yellow center. The plant produces small, edible berries that are often used in culinary applications. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in woodland areas or along forest edges.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and historical use in horticulture.
