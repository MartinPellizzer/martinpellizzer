Filipendula koreana

Filipendula koreana, commonly known as Korean meadow sweet, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Korea.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The plant features compound leaves with serrated margins and produces small, dry fruits called achenes. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and forest edges.

Filipendula koreana is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
