Faramea campanularis

Faramea campanularis, commonly known as the bell-shaped faramea, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its evergreen leaves, which are typically elliptical and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, adding ornamental value. Faramea campanularis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

It is valued in horticulture for its attractive foliage and unique floral display.
