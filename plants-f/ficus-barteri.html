Ficus barteri

Ficus barteri, commonly known as the Barter's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the island of Madagascar and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when mature. Ficus barteri is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers indirect sunlight.
