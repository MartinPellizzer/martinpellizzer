Fuchsia woytkowskii

Fuchsia woytkowskii, commonly known as the Woytkowski's fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes in South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous clusters of vibrant pink to purple flowers. The plant produces showy bracts that are often mistaken for petals, adding to its ornamental appeal. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative plant.

Fuchsia woytkowskii is valued for its ornamental value and is sometimes used in horticulture for its unique floral display.
