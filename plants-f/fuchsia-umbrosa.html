Fuchsia umbrosa

Fuchsia umbrosa, commonly known as the shadow fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep purple to bright red. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often found in cloud forests. Its leaves are ovate and glossy, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture.

Fuchsia umbrosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in shaded garden settings.
