Flagellarisaema thunbergii

Flagellarisaema thunbergii, commonly known as the Japanese bellflower, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from blue to purple, often with a white throat. The plant has opposite, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This species is valued in gardens for its ornamental flowers and is relatively easy to cultivate.
