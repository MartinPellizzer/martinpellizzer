Ficinia truncata

Ficinia truncata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southern United States and parts of Mexico.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of water bodies, preferring moist to aquatic conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly curled at the tips, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually clustered in spikes and bloom in the summer months.

Ficinia truncata plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
