Frangula alnus var. prostrata

Frangula alnus var. prostrata, commonly known as creeping frangula, is a low-growing shrub native to northern regions of North America.

It features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters. The plant is characterized by its prostrate growth habit, with stems that lie close to the ground. It is adapted to moist, sandy soils and is often found in wetlands or along streambanks.

The berries it produces are dark red and contain seeds that are dispersed by birds.
