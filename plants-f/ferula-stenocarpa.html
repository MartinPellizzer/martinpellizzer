Ferula stenocarpa

Ferula stenocarpa, commonly known as the narrow-leaved ferula, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stem and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbel-like clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its taproot is thick and fleshy, often used in traditional medicine for its aromatic properties.

Ferula stenocarpa is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, thriving in well-drained soils.
