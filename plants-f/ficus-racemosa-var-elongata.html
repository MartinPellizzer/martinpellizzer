Ficus racemosa var. elongata

Ficus racemosa var. elongata, commonly known as the long-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible fig fruits that are typically spherical and slightly hairy. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade.

The species is also valued for its role in traditional medicine and as a food source in some regions.
