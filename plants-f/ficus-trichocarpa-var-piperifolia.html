Ficus trichocarpa var. piperifolia

Ficus trichocarpa var. piperifolia, commonly known as the Piper leaf fig, is a unique variant of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated, and often serrated leaves that resemble those of the Piper genus, hence its name. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. It is a evergreen shrub or small tree that can grow up to several meters in height.

Its foliage is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its striking appearance.
