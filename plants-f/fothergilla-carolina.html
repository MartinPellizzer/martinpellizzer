Fothergilla carolina

Fothergilla carolina, commonly known as the Carolina witchhazel, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its fragrant, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring, typically from May to June. The plant features narrow, dark green leaves that turn reddish in the fall. Its ornamental value comes from its showy blooms and attractive foliage, making it a popular choice for gardens.

Fothergilla carolina is also valued for its ability to thrive in partial shade and its role in supporting local wildlife.
