Fritillaria parviflora

Fritillaria parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or yellow with purple markings. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its bulbs are ornamental and often used in horticulture for their striking floral display.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
