Farfugium

Farfugium, commonly known as the "sunshine buttercup," is a genus of flowering plants in the daisy family, Asteraceae.

It is native to North America and includes several species, such as Farfugium japonicum and Farfugium indica. These plants are characterized by their large, spoon-shaped leaves and showy yellow or orange composite flowers. They are often grown as ornamental plants for their striking foliage and ability to thrive in moist, shady conditions.

Farfugium species are also valued for their resilience and low maintenance requirements in garden settings.
