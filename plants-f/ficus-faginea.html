Ficus faginea

Ficus faginea, commonly known as the African ficus or the forest fig, is a species native to tropical Africa.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with smooth, grey bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits. Its roots are known for their ability to absorb water efficiently, making it adaptable to various soil types.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
