Fraxinus excelsior f. purpurascens

Fraxinus excelsior f. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-leaf ash, is a cultivar of the common ash tree.

It is characterized by its striking purple-bronze foliage, which provides ornamental value in gardens and landscapes. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and have a smooth margin. This variety is often grown for its aesthetic appeal rather than for its timber.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
