Furcaria sabdariffa

Furcaria sabdariffa, commonly known as the Jamaica flower or hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Caribbean and parts of Central America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from red to pink and yellow. The plant has heart-shaped leaves and a woody stem that becomes more pronounced with age. Its flowers are often used to make beverages and teas due to their tart flavor.

Botanically, it belongs to the Malvaceae family, which includes other plants like cotton and okra.
