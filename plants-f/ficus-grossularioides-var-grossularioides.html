Ficus grossularioides var. grossularioides

Ficus grossularioides var. grossularioides, commonly known as the Indian rubber plant, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by round, yellowish fruits resembling small apples. It is known for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to thrive in indoor environments with moderate light.

Its rubbery latex sap has historically been used in the production of natural rubber.
