Fagus sylvatica var. cochleata

Fagus sylvatica var. cochleata, commonly known as the warty beech, is a rare variant of the European beech.

It is characterized by its distinctive warty growths on the bark, which give it a unique appearance. The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a serrated margin and a glossy upper surface. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in shaded forest environments.

This variety is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological significance in forest ecosystems.
