Ficus rhynchophylla

Ficus rhynchophylla, commonly known as the silver leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a silvery or pale underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Ficus rhynchophylla is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
