Ficus hirta subsp. ochracea

Ficus hirta subsp.

ochracea, commonly known as the orange fig or ochraceous fig, is a shrub or small tree native to tropical regions of Africa. It is characterized by its simple, opposite, and often hairy leaves that are usually green with a slight reddish or orange tint on the underside.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fleshy fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
