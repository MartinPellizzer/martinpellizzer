Ficus mittuensis

Ficus mittuensis, commonly known as the Mittuensis Fig Tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and produces small, fig-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ficus mittuensis is also valued for its ability to purify the air and is commonly used in indoor gardening.
