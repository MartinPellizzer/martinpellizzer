Frankenia laevis var. capitata

Frankenia laevis var. capitata, commonly known as the coastal bluemink, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a low-growing shrub with small, blue-green leaves that are covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is adapted to salt spray and coastal conditions.

Frankenia laevis var. capitata is often found in dune environments and is an important component of coastal vegetation.
