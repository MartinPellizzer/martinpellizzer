Felicia rotundifolia

Felicia rotundifolia, commonly known as the round-leaf fleabane, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and showy yellow or orange blooms. The plant has rounded, fleshy leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Felicia rotundifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its bright, daisy-like flowers.
