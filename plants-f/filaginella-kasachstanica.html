Filaginella kasachstanica

Filaginella kasachstanica, commonly known as the Central Asian filicin, is a rare and lesser-known fern species native to Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Filicinaceae and is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its spores are produced on the underside of its fronds, a common trait among ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a botanically significant but under-researched species.
