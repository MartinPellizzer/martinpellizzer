Ficus adamii

Ficus adamii, commonly known as the Adam's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the island of Madagascar and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, spherical fruits that are about 1-2 centimeters in diameter. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus adamii is also valued for its ability to adapt to various growing conditions, making it relatively easy to care for in indoor environments.
