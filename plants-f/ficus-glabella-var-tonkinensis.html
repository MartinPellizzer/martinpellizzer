Ficus glabella var. tonkinensis

Ficus glabella var. tonkinensis, commonly known as the Tonkin fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Vietnam and Laos, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are often clustered at the tips of its stems. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically small and yellowish in color.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
