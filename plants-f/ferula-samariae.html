Ferula samariae

Ferula samariae, commonly known as the Palestinian giant fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, umbrella-like inflorescences that produce small yellow flowers. The plant has aromatic, finely divided leaves and produces a strong-smelling, resinous sap. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in arid and semi-arid environments.

Ferula samariae is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.
