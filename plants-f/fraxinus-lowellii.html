Fraxinus lowellii

Fraxinus lowellii, commonly known as the lowell's ash, is a species of ash tree native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its slender, multi-stemmed form and its delicate, feathery foliage. The leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a silvery underside, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.
