Ferdinandea stellata

Ferdinandea stellata, commonly known as the starry daphne, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It is characterized by its compact, shrub-like growth habit and produces clusters of small, star-shaped flowers. The plant features glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It blooms in spring, attracting pollinators with its fragrant, white to pale pink flowers.

Ferdinandea stellata is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.
