Fagerlindia

Fagerlindia, commonly known as the "Tree of Life," is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year. The plant has a woody stem with a smooth, grayish-brown bark that exudes a mild, spicy scent when cut. Its roots are known to contain compounds with medicinal properties used in traditional herbal remedies.

Fagerlindia thrives in humid, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.
