Fragaria vesca var. nubicola

Fragaria vesca var. nubicola, commonly known as the alpine strawberry, is a variant of the woodland strawberry.

It is a low-growing perennial plant characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant white flowers. The plant produces tiny, edible berries that are typically red when mature. It thrives in cool, high-altitude environments and is often found in alpine meadows.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and culinary use in certain regions.
