Filago spathulata subvar. lancifolia

Filago spathulata subvar. lancifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved filago, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows in open, dry habitats such as grasslands and rocky soils. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often hairy, and it produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in clusters. It is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions and is often found in disturbed or marginal areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting pollinators.
