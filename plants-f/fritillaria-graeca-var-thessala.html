Fritillaria graeca var. thessala

Fritillaria graeca var. thessala, commonly known as the Greek fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe, particularly Greece and the Balkans.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish-brown with a distinctive black or purple border. The plant grows in rocky or dry habitats, often in mountainous regions, and is adapted to well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, and it blooms in early spring.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
