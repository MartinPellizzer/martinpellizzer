Festuca paradoxa

Festuca paradoxa, commonly known as the paradox grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often tinged with silver or blue. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Festuca paradoxa is adapted to harsh alpine environments, tolerating cold temperatures and poor soil conditions.

Its unique appearance and ecological significance make it a subject of interest in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
