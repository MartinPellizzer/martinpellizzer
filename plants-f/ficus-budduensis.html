Ficus budduensis

Ficus budduensis, commonly known as the Buddha's fig, is a species of fig tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. Ficus budduensis thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor cultivation.
