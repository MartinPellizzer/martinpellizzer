Ficus consanguinea

Ficus consanguinea, commonly known as the red-veined fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its distinctive red veins on the leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called syconia. Its leaves are typically broad and ovate, with a glossy surface.

Ficus consanguinea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
