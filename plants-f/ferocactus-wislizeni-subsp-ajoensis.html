Ferocactus wislizeni subsp. ajoensis

Ferocactus wislizeni subsp.

ajoensis, commonly known as the Ajo barrel cactus, is a spineless cactus native to the Sonoran Desert. It features a cylindrical stem with a ribbed structure that helps it store water efficiently.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring, which are pollinated by various insects. Its thick, fleshy stems are adapted to survive in arid environments with minimal rainfall.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in desert ecosystems.
