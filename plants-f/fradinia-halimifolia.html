Fradinia halimifolia

Fradinia halimifolia, commonly known as the coastal sagebrush, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of California and Baja California.

It is characterized by its aromatic, silver-gray foliage and small, inconspicuous yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant is adapted to coastal climates, tolerating salt spray and well-drained sandy soils. It plays an important role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for native wildlife.

Its essential oils have been studied for potential medicinal properties.
