Forestiera rhamnifolia

Forestiera rhamnifolia, commonly known as the Florida beautyberry, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by clusters of dark purple berries in the fall. The plant has slender, often thorned stems and lance-shaped leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands and along streambanks.

The berries are a valuable food source for birds and other wildlife.
