Frailea atrobella

Frailea atrobella, commonly known as the black-flowered frailea, is a species of cactus native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, spiny growth habit. The plant features short, cylindrical stems covered in white to grayish-blue spines. Its flowers are typically dark red to black, blooming in late winter or early spring.

Frailea atrobella is well adapted to arid environments, storing water in its fleshy stems.
