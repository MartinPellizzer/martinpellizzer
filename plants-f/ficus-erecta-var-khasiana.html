Ficus erecta var. khasiana

Ficus erecta var. khasiana, commonly known as the Khasi fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the northeastern regions of India, particularly the Khasi Hills. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green in color. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
