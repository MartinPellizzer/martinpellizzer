Fridericia formosa

Fridericia formosa, commonly known as the silky fridericia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in humid, tropical environments. The plant is characterized by its soft, hairy stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. Its leaves are simple, opposite, and often covered in fine pubescence.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded conditions.
