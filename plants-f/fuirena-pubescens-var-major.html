Fuirena pubescens var. major

Fuirena pubescens var. major, commonly known as the Mexican morning glory, is a vigorous climbing vine native to Mexico and Central America.

It features heart-shaped, hairy leaves and produces striking, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from violet to deep purple. The plant is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, though it prefers full sun. Its flowers bloom in the early morning, hence its common name, and are often used in ornamental gardening.

The species is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional herbal practices.
