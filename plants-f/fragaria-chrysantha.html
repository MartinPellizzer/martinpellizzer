Fragaria chrysantha

Fragaria chrysantha, commonly known as the golden strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trailing growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that give way to edible, yellow-orange berries. These berries are not only ornamental but also have a mild, sweet flavor.

Due to its unique appearance and edible qualities, it is often cultivated in gardens and for commercial use.
