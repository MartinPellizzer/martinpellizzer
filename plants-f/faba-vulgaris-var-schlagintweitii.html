Faba vulgaris var. schlagintweitii

Faba vulgaris var. schlagintweitii, commonly known as the Schlagintweit's fava bean, is a variant of the broad bean species native to regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

This plant is characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves with a distinctive dark green color. The flowers are typically pale purple or white, and the pods contain several angular, yellowish seeds. It is adapted to temperate climates and is often cultivated for its nutritious seeds.

The plant has been historically used in traditional agriculture and is valued for its hardy nature and productivity.
