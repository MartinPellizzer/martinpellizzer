Freycinetia walkeri

Freycinetia walkeri, commonly known as the red-flowered pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and broad, sword-shaped leaves. The plant produces striking red flowers that appear in clusters, often attracting pollinators such as birds and insects. Its fibrous roots and woody structure make it well adapted to coastal and wetland environments.

Freycinetia walkeri is valued for its ornamental beauty and has cultural significance in some island communities.
