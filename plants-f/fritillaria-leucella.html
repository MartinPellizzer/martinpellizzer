Fritillaria leucella

Fritillaria leucella, commonly known as the white dog's tooth.

It is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. The plant produces striking white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. Its leaves are typically broad and lance-shaped, emerging from a cluster of fleshy, white or pale green bulbs.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
