Ferraria divaricata subsp. australis

Ferraria divaricata subsp.

australis, commonly known as the Cape bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa. It features slender, hairy stems and produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of pink to purple.

The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

It is valued in gardens for its ornamental beauty and is relatively low maintenance once established.
