Fumana spachii var. robusta

Fumana spachii var. robusta, commonly known as the robust fumana, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of small, daisy-like yellow flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained sandy or gravelly soils and prefers full sun exposure. Its leaves are lance-shaped, hairy, and have a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

It is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive appearance and resilience in arid conditions.
