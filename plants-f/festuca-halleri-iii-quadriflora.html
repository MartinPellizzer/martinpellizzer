Festuca halleri-iii-quadriflora

Festuca halleri-iii-quadriflora, commonly known as the four-flowered fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. The plant features slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves, and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Its flowers are usually pale green to blueish in color and are arranged in clusters.

Festuca halleri-iii-quadriflora is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in wetland environments.
