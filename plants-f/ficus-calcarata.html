Ficus calcarata

Ficus calcarata, commonly known as the coral tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, coral-like inflorescences that give it its common name. The plant has glossy, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. Ficus calcarata thrives in warm, humid environments and requires bright, indirect light for optimal growth.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and indoor cultivation.
