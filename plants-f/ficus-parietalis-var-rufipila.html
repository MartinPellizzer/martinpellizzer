Ficus parietalis var. rufipila

Ficus parietalis var. rufipila, commonly known as the red-haired wall fig, is a climbing shrub native to tropical regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown stems that often have a fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green with a slightly waxy appearance. Its fruit is a small, round fig that is typically green when immature and turns yellow or red when mature.

This species is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
