Flagellaria ascendens

Flagellaria ascendens, commonly known as the climbing flagellaria, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Commelinaceae.

It is characterized by its climbing habit and long, slender stems that can ascend vertically. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in clusters, typically in the axils of the leaves. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a smooth or slightly hairy surface.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions and is often found in moist, shaded environments.
