Francoa sonchifolia var. appendiculata

Francoa sonchifolia var. appendiculata, commonly known as the appendiculate francoa, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the western United States.

It typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and streambanks. The plant features basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, with a basal rosette structure. Its flowering stems rise from the base, bearing yellow flowers arranged in a composite head.

The species is characterized by its delicate appearance and is often found in regions with temperate climates.
