Fraxinus rhynchophylla var. hopeiensis

Fraxinus rhynchophylla var. hopeiensis, commonly known as the Hopei Ash, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by samaroid fruits that disperse its seeds. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas with well-drained soil.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and ecological importance in its native habitat.
