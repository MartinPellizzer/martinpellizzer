Ficus subintegra

Ficus subintegra, commonly known as the Burmese fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Myanmar, Thailand, and Vietnam. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and can grow as a shrub or small tree. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically consumed raw.

Ficus subintegra is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
