Festuca incurvatifolia

Festuca incurvatifolia, commonly known as the curly leaf fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its finely textured, dark green foliage and distinctive curling leaves that give it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in cool, temperate climates. Festuca incurvatifolia is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.

It is also known for its hardiness and ability to tolerate dry conditions.
