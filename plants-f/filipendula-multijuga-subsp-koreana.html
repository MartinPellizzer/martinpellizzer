Filipendula multijuga subsp. koreana

Filipendula multijuga subsp.

koreana, commonly known as Korean meadow sweet, is a perennial herb native to eastern Asia. It features upright, branched stems that are covered in soft, grayish-white hairs.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale pink flowers arranged in flat-topped umbels, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three ovate to lanceolate leaflets that are often hairy underneath.

This species is typically found in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream banks.
