Ficus ampelos var. soronensis

Ficus ampelos var. soronensis, commonly known as the silver ficus or variegated ficus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white or silver veins, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, greenish-yellow fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental purposes in both residential and commercial settings.
