Festuca × jierru

Festuca × jierru, commonly known as the Sierra Nevada fescue, is a hybrid grass species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, needle-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated.

Festuca × jierru is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in stabilizing soil in rocky and high-altitude environments.
