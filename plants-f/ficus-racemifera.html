Ficus racemifera

Ficus racemifera, commonly known as the clustered fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that develop into edible fig fruits. Ficus racemifera thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for indoor cultivation as well as in shaded outdoor environments.
