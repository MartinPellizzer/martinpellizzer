Fritillaria × perryi

Fritillaria × perryi, commonly known as the Persian fritillary, is a hybrid plant belonging to the Liliaceae family.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers, typically ranging in color from orange to red, with contrasting black markings. Native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia, it thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. The plant produces a basal rosette of narrow, gray-green leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

It is a perennial that blooms in early spring, adding a vibrant touch to gardens and natural landscapes.
