Fritillaria tesselata var. lutea

Fritillaria tesselata var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow broomcup, is a striking perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It features showy, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns resembling mosaic designs. The plant produces a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, stout stem that supports its vibrant blooms. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its unique floral structure and vivid hues make it a notable species in botanical collections and native plant gardens.
