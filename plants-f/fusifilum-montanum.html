Fusifilum montanum

Fusifilum montanum, commonly known as the mountain spindle tree, is a woody plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its elongated, spindle-shaped trunk and dense, evergreen foliage. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically followed by clusters of red berries. Its leaves are lanceolate and have a waxy surface, which helps reduce water loss in its arid habitat.

Fusifilum montanum is often found in rocky soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments.
