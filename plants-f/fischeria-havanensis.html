Fischeria havanensis

Fischeria havanensis, commonly known as the Havana orchid, is a rare and exotic orchid species native to the tropical regions of Cuba.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that typically display vibrant shades of pink and white. The plant has a delicate, sympodial growth habit, with pseudobulbs that store water and nutrients. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often arranged in a compact rosette.

Fischeria havanensis thrives in humid, well-drained soil and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.
