Frenela calcarata

Frenela calcarata, commonly known as the rock lily, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils, thriving in well-drained environments. The plant features slender, upright stems and produces small, clustered flowers that are usually white or pale pink. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a bluish-green hue.

Frenela calcarata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
