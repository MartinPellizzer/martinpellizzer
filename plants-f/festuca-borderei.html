Festuca borderei

Festuca borderei, commonly known as the Alboran mountain grass, is a flowering plant native to the Iberian Peninsula and North Africa.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in rocky or limestone soils, often in high-altitude regions. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences with tiny blue or purple flowers. It is adapted to dry, sunny conditions and is often found in open, sunny habitats.

Festuca borderei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native ecosystems.
