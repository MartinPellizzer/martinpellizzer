Fernandezia myrtillus

Fernandezia myrtillus, commonly known as the myrtle fern, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Myrtaceae, which includes other well-known plants like eucalyptus and guava. The plant is characterized by its small, fragrant flowers and leathery, evergreen leaves. It typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest understories.

Fernandezia myrtillus is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
