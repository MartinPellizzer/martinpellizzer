Freycinetia impavida

Freycinetia impavida, commonly known as the red pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of the Pacific, including parts of Polynesia, Micronesia, and Melanesia. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow, strap-like leaves that are often bright green or reddish in color. It produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in panicles, followed by woody, three-lobed fruits.

Freycinetia impavida is often used in traditional crafts and as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and hardy nature.
