Festuca halleri subsp. scardica

Festuca halleri subsp.

scardica, commonly known as the Scardic fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central and Eastern Europe. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and silver-blue foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a single spikelet, which is often slightly drooping, and it produces small, wind-pollinated flowers. Festuca halleri subsp.

scardica is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
