Ficus beecheyana f. tenuifolia

Ficus beecheyana f. tenuifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a unique variant of the Ficus genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs, which are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and prefers well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded outdoor gardens.
