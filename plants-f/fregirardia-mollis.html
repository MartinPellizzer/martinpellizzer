Fregirardia mollis

Fregirardia mollis, commonly known as the soft-fruited balm, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe.

It features aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a soft, velvety texture, and produces small, clustered flowers in shades of pink and white. The plant is characterized by its creeping growth habit and ability to form dense mats, making it suitable for ground cover. It thrives in well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, often found in woodland edges and meadows.

Fregirardia mollis is valued for its medicinal properties and historical use in traditional herbal remedies.
