Ficus edulis var. attenuata

Ficus edulis var. attenuata, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical plant native to the Indo-Pacific region.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces a fruit that is rich in carbohydrates. The tree can grow up to 20 meters tall and thrives in warm, humid climates. Its fruit, which resembles a small bread loaf, is used as a staple food in many Pacific Island cultures.

The plant has also been cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
