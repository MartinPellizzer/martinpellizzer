Faba vulgaris var. mazagana

Faba vulgaris var. mazagana, commonly known as the Mediterranean bean, is a variety of the broad bean native to the Mediterranean region.

It is an annual legume characterized by its erect growth habit and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces pods containing several round, edible seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both culinary and agricultural purposes.

Its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil makes it a valuable crop in sustainable farming systems.
