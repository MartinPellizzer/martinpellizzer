Fallopia cynanchoides

Fallopia cynanchoides, commonly known as giant knotweed, is a vigorous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems that can reach up to 3 meters in height. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters during the summer months. Its leaves are heart-shaped and have a rough, serrated edge.

Fallopia cynanchoides is often considered an invasive species in regions outside its native range due to its rapid growth and ability to outcompete native vegetation.
