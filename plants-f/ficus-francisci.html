Ficus francisci

Ficus francisci, commonly known as the Francisco fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Central America and is characterized by its glossy, broad leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters in suitable conditions. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Ficus francisci is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively low maintenance once established.
