Ficus bukkaensis

Ficus bukkaensis, commonly known as the Bukkaensis fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Africa, particularly found in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually a deep green in color. It produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife.
