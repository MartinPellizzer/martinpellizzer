Festuca cagiriensis

Festuca cagiriensis, commonly known as the Cagiri fescue, is a perennial grass species native to specific regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically hairy along the edges. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are adapted for wind pollination. Festuca cagiriensis thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands or rocky areas.

Its ecological role includes providing habitat and forage for various wildlife species.
