Ficus compressa

Ficus compressa, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like India, Thailand, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional medicine. Ficus compressa produces small, greenish flowers that develop into edible fig fruits.

It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
