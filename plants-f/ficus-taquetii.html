Ficus taquetii

Ficus taquetii, commonly known as the Taquet's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible figs that are typically green when mature, though they may change color depending on the variety. Ficus taquetii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-drained soil.
