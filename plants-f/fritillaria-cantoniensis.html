Fritillaria cantoniensis

Fritillaria cantoniensis, commonly known as the Canton fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern China.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color, often with contrasting black spots. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and has a compact growth habit. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers a sunny to partially shaded environment.

Fritillaria cantoniensis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and botanical collections.
