Ficus colpophylla

Ficus colpophylla, commonly known as the broadleaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. It produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are often used in local cuisines.

Ficus colpophylla is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant.
