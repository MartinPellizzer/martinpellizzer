Ficus izabalana

Ficus izabalana, commonly known as the Izabal fig, is a species of fig tree native to Central America.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are edible and have a sweet, mild flavor. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Ficus izabalana is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and ability to adapt to various growing conditions.
