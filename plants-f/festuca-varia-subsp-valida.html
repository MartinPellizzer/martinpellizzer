Festuca varia subsp. valida

Festuca varia subsp.

valida, commonly known as the golden tuft grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and distinctive golden to yellow-green foliage.

The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. Festuca varia subsp.

valida is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and restoration projects.
