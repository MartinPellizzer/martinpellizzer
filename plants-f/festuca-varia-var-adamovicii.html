Festuca varia var. adamovicii

Festuca varia var. adamovicii, commonly known as the Adamovicii fescue, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features fine, silver-gray leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of fine hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, pale blue to greenish flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments.

Due to its ornamental value and hardiness, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens with suitable conditions.
