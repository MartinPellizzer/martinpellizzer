Festuca lanceolata

Festuca lanceolata, commonly known as slender fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It typically grows in grasslands, meadows, and rocky areas, preferring well-drained soils. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often slightly folded, and it produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.

Festuca lanceolata is also a food source for various insects and small mammals.
