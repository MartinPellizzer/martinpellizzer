Fritillaria aquitanica

Fritillaria aquitanica, commonly known as the Aquitaine fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color, often with contrasting patterns. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is adapted to Mediterranean climates. Its bulbs are perennial, allowing it to survive in harsh conditions through dormancy.

Fritillaria aquitanica is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable growing conditions.
