Foeniculum graecum

Foeniculum graecum, commonly known as Greek fennel, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features feathery, finely divided leaves and produces yellow flowers in umbels, typical of the Apiaceae family. The plant is characterized by its aromatic, hollow stems and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils. It is often cultivated for its seeds, which are used in culinary and medicinal applications.

Its growth habit includes a basal rosette of leaves and flowering stems that rise above it.
