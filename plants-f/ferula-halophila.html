Ferula halophila

Ferula halophila, commonly known as the coastal ferula, is a flowering plant native to arid and saline environments.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic compounds. The plant produces yellow flowers and has a taproot system adapted to store water. Its leaves are often succulent, helping it survive in dry conditions.
