Fritillaria micrantha

Fritillaria micrantha, commonly known as the dwarf crown imperial, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southwestern Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves at the base, and its bulbs are often used in traditional medicine. Fritillaria micrantha thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

It is a popular ornamental plant due to its unique and vibrant floral display.
