Festuca haussknechtii

Festuca haussknechtii, commonly known as the Haussknecht's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, two-lobed flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

Festuca haussknechtii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and aesthetic qualities.
