Fordia bracteolata

Fordia bracteolata, commonly known as the panicle hydrangea, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its dense, branched inflorescences that produce small, showy white or pink flowers in late spring. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to gardens and natural landscapes.
