Frangula sphaerosperma subsp. longipes

Frangula sphaerosperma subsp.

longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed buckthorn, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of southern Africa. It is characterized by its woody stems, which are often branched and covered in a thick, smooth bark.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by spherical, woody fruits that contain a single seed. Its leaves are typically narrow and slightly serrated, and the plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments. Frangula sphaerosperma subsp.

longipes is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
