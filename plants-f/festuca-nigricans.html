Festuca nigricans

Festuca nigricans, commonly known as black oat grass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, often bluish-gray foliage and tufted inflorescences that produce small, spike-like flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along stream banks. Festuca nigricans is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping for its fine texture and color.

It is also known for its ability to thrive in a variety of environmental conditions, making it a versatile choice for gardeners.
