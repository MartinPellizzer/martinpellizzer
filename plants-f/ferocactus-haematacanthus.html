Ferocactus haematacanthus

Ferocactus haematacanthus, commonly known as the blood barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its robust, cylindrical stems that are typically green with a reddish tinge, especially when exposed to intense sunlight. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are followed by edible, red fruits that contain numerous small, white seeds. Its thick, spiny ribs help store water, making it well adapted to desert environments.

The plant's structure and coloration are adaptations to its harsh, dry habitat, allowing it to thrive in conditions with minimal rainfall.
