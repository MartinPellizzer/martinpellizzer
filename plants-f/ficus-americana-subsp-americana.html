Ficus americana subsp. americana

Ficus americana subsp.

americana, commonly known as the American fig, is a tropical tree native to the southeastern United States. It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically yellow or reddish when ripe. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to various soil types.

The plant has a shallow root system, making it suitable for container gardening in warmer climates.
