Fritillaria caballeroi

Fritillaria caballeroi, commonly known as the Caballero's fritillary, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the high-altitude regions of central and southern Spain.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are adapted to store water and nutrients, allowing it to survive in arid conditions.

Fritillaria caballeroi is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
