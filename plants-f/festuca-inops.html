Festuca inops

Festuca inops, commonly known as the slender fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, upright blades and dense growth habit, often forming a mat-like structure. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is adapted to a variety of environmental conditions. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers.

Festuca inops is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in low-maintenance settings.
