Fraxinus rubicunda

Fraxinus rubicunda, commonly known as the red ash.

It is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America, characterized by its smooth, reddish-brown bark and compound leaves with 7 to 15 leaflets. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. Its leaves are typically alternate and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental value.

The species is also valued for its durable wood, which has been used historically in furniture and construction.
