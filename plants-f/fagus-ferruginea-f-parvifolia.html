Fagus ferruginea f. parvifolia

Fagus ferruginea f. parvifolia, commonly known as the rusty beech or red beech, is a rare and distinctive variant of the beech tree.

It is characterized by its small, leathery leaves that are covered in a rusty-colored pubescence, giving it its common name. The plant is native to parts of Europe and is typically found in moist, shaded forest environments. Its leaves are elliptical in shape and have a serrated margin, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

This form is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique appearance and ecological significance.
