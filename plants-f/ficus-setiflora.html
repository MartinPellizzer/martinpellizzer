Ficus setiflora

Ficus setiflora, commonly known as the threadleaf fig, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus setiflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
