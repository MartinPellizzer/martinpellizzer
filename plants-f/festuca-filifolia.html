Festuca filifolia

Festuca filifolia, commonly known as slender feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, thread-like leaves and delicate, fluffy seed heads that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. It has a low-growing habit and forms dense, mat-like clumps.

Festuca filifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic and ecological benefits.
