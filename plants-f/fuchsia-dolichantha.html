Fuchsia dolichantha

Fuchsia dolichantha, commonly known as the long-stalked fuchsia, is a species native to South America, particularly found in Ecuador and Peru.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces striking, showy flowers in shades of pink and purple. The plant features elongated, pendulous flowers that are characteristic of the Fuchsia genus. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and covered in fine hairs.

Fuchsia dolichantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses.
