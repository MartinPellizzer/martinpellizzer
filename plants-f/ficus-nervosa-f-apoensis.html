Ficus nervosa f. apoensis

Ficus nervosa f. apoensis, commonly known as the Apo ficus, is a variant of the common fig tree native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal properties.
