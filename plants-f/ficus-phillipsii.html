Ficus phillipsii

Ficus phillipsii, commonly known as the Philippine ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by round, greenish fruits. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Ficus phillipsii is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and ornamental appeal.
