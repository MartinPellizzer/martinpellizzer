Ficus eulampra

Ficus eulampra, commonly known as the glossy leaves fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy surface. Ficus eulampra is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in indoor environments.

It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible fig fruits, though the fruits are not commonly consumed by humans.
