Fritillaria lusitanica subsp. lusitanica

Fritillaria lusitanica subsp.

lusitanica, commonly known as the Iberian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe, particularly Spain and Portugal. It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color, with a distinctive pattern of darker markings.

The plant has a short stem and produces a single, upright flower that blooms in early spring. Its leaves are basal, narrow, and grass-like, and it thrives in well-drained, calcareous soils.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant, showy blooms.
