Freirea chersonensis

Freirea chersonensis, commonly known as the Crimean bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It typically grows in rocky or dry soils, often found in mountainous regions. The plant features showy blue or violet flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs, which help reduce water loss.

Freirea chersonensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
