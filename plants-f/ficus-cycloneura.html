Ficus cycloneura

Ficus cycloneura, commonly known as the cycloneura fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a smooth texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by the development of edible figs.

Ficus cycloneura is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
