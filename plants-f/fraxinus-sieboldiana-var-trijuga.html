Fraxinus sieboldiana var. trijuga

Fraxinus sieboldiana var. trijuga, commonly known as the Japanese ash or trijuga ash, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and its compound leaves, which are pinnately arranged with ovate to lanceolate leaflets. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles, followed by samaroid fruits that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas along riverbanks.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in ecological systems.
