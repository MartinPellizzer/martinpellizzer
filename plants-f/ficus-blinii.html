Ficus blinii

Ficus blinii, commonly known as the Blinii fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus blinii is also popular as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ornamental value.
