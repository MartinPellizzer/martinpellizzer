Flotovia hispida

Flotovia hispida, commonly known as the rough flotovia, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular stems and leaves, which give it a distinctive textured appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Due to its unique morphology and habitat preferences, it is of interest to botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
