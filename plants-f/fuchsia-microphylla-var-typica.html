Fuchsia microphylla var. typica

Fuchsia microphylla var. typica, commonly known as the common fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, pendulous flowers that come in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a glossy green surface.

The flowers bloom in clusters and are often used in ornamental gardening due to their striking appearance.
