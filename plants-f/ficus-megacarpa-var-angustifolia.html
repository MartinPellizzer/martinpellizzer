Ficus megacarpa var. angustifolia

Ficus megacarpa var. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, elongated leaves that are typically dark green and have a slightly serrated edge. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that turn from green to red as they mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
