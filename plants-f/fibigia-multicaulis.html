Fibigia multicaulis

Fibigia multicaulis, commonly known as the many-stemmed fibigia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. The plant thrives in high-altitude environments, often found in rocky or alpine habitats. Its leaves are generally narrow and covered in a fine, fuzzy layer, which helps reduce water loss in its arid environment.

Fibigia multicaulis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
