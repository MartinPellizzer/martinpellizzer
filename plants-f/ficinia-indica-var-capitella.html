Ficinia indica var. capitella

Ficinia indica var. capitella, commonly known as the clustered spathiphyllum, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are typically dark green with a prominent midrib. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are often hidden within modified leaves called spathes. It thrives in humid environments and requires indirect light for optimal growth.

Ficinia indica var. capitella is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
