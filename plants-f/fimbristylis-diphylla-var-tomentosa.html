Fimbristylis diphylla var. tomentosa

Fimbristylis diphylla var. tomentosa, commonly known as the hairy flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wet or marshy habitats, such as swamps and seasonally flooded areas. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, three-ranked flowers.

This variety is characterized by its compact growth habit and is often found in low-lying, waterlogged soils.
