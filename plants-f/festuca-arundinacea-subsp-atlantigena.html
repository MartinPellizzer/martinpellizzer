Festuca arundinacea subsp. atlantigena

Festuca arundinacea subsp.

atlantigena, commonly known as Atlantic tall fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the coastal regions of the Atlantic coast of North America. It is characterized by its tall, slender growth habit and dark green, narrow leaves.

This subspecies is adapted to saline and sandy soils, making it a hardy plant in coastal environments. It forms dense turf and is often used in coastal dune stabilization.

Its flowering stems bear spikelets that produce small, wind-dispersed seeds.
