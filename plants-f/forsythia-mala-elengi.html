Forsythia mala-elengi

Forsythia mala-elengi, commonly known as the Chinese forsythia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its showy, yellow floral clusters that appear in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has simple, opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

The flowers are often used in traditional Chinese medicine for their purported health benefits.
