Fumaria fungosa

Fumaria fungosa, commonly known as the bladderworts, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Fumariaceae.

It is characterized by its distinctive spurred flowers and specialized structures called viscid disks, which aid in pollination. The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in woodland or meadow habitats. Fumaria fungosa is known for its rapid growth and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Its unique morphology and ecological adaptations make it an interesting subject for botanical study.
