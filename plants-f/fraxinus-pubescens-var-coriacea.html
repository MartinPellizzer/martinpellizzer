Fraxinus pubescens var. coriacea

Fraxinus pubescens var. coriacea, commonly known as the rough-hairied ash, is a variant of the ash tree characterized by its distinctive bark and foliage.

It is native to parts of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptic, with a rough, pubescent surface that gives the plant its common name. Its bark is thick and fissured, often bearing a rugged, textured appearance.

This variant is valued for its ornamental qualities and resilience to certain environmental conditions.
