Faurea saligna subsp. xanthoneura

Faurea saligna subsp.

xanthoneura, commonly known as the golden-veined hakea, is a shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Australia. It is characterized by its aromatic, narrow leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces yellow flowers in dense, cylindrical inflorescences, which bloom during the autumn season. Its growth habit is typically low and spreading, with a woody structure that provides habitat for native fauna.

The subspecies is noted for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its attractive foliage and floral display.
