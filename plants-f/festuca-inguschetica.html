Festuca inguschetica

Festuca inguschetica, commonly known as Siberian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the cold climates of Siberia and Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green, narrow leaves. This plant is well adapted to harsh environments, thriving in well-drained soils and full sun. Festuca inguschetica is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to withstand cold temperatures.

It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer.
