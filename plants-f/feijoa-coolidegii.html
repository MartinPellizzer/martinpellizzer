Feijoa coolidegii

Feijoa coolidegii, commonly known as the rough feijoa, is a small evergreen shrub native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its hairy, ovate leaves that are often tinged with red or purple on the underside. The plant produces small, fragrant, yellowish-green fruits that are typically covered in a fuzzy coating. The flowers are white and star-shaped, with five petals and a sweet, aromatic scent.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for its edible fruit and ornamental value.
