Fagelia punicea

Fagelia punicea, commonly known as the red flame tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking red flowers that bloom in clusters, often resembling flames. The plant has compound leaves with elongated leaflets and a woody, branching structure. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Fagelia punicea is also valued for its ability to provide shade and its role in supporting local wildlife.
