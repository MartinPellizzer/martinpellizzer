Fleischmanniopsis anomalochaeta

Fleischmanniopsis anomalochaeta, commonly known as the ghost fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, arching fronds that resemble ghostly apparitions, giving it its evocative common name. The plant has a unique reproductive system, producing sporangia on the underside of its leaves, which are clustered in small, rounded structures. Its growth is slow and requires high humidity and indirect light to thrive.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat needs, it is often cultivated in specialized botanical gardens.
