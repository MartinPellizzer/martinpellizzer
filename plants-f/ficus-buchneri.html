Ficus buchneri

Ficus buchneri, commonly known as the Buchner's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in syconia, which are the unique fruiting structures of the fig genus.

Ficus buchneri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in indoor environments.
