Freycinetia leucacantha

Freycinetia leucacantha, commonly known as the white-veined pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and cuisine. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that develop into round, fleshy fruits, which are edible and used in various culinary applications. Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in warm climates.

The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires a humid environment to flourish.
