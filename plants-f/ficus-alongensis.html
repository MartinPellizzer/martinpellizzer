Ficus alongensis

Ficus alongensis, commonly known as the Madagascar fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the island of Madagascar and is characterized by its large, glossy leaves and unique, fig-like fruits. The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for houseplants.

The fruit of Ficus alongensis is edible and has been traditionally used in local cuisines and herbal remedies.
