Fagara guineensis

Fagara guineensis, commonly known as the West African prickly ash, is a member of the Rutaceae family.

It is a small to medium-sized shrub or tree native to tropical Africa, particularly found in countries like Nigeria and Cameroon. The plant is characterized by its pinnately compound leaves and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in clusters. Its fruit is a spherical berry that turns from green to yellow or red when mature.

The plant has been used in traditional medicine for its potential anti-inflammatory and analgesic properties.
