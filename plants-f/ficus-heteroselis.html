Ficus heteroselis

Ficus heteroselis, commonly known as the heteroselis fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It produces small, greenish flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus heteroselis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
