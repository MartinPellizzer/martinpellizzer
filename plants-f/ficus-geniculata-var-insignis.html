Ficus geniculata var. insignis

Ficus geniculata var. insignis, commonly known as the silver-leafed ficus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with a distinctive silvery underside. The plant has a woody stem that can grow quite tall, often reaching several meters in height. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor ornamental gardening.
