Fordia gibbsiae

Fordia gibbsiae, commonly known as the silverleaf honeysuckle, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring, preceding the emergence of its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant has a compact growth habit and produces red berries that are attractive to birds. Its leaves turn a striking coppery color in the fall, adding ornamental value to the landscape.

Fordia gibbsiae is valued for its winter interest and adaptability to various soil types.
