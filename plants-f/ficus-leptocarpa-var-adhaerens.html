Ficus leptocarpa var. adhaerens

Ficus leptocarpa var. adhaerens, commonly known as the climbing fig, is a variant of the ficus genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vigorous climbing habit and heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
