Firmiana major

Firmiana major, commonly known as the Chinese parasol tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its large, umbrella-shaped leaves that can reach up to 30 inches in diameter. The tree produces small, pale yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, followed by woody, pod-like fruits. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to provide shade.

The leaves are typically used in traditional medicine in some Asian countries.
