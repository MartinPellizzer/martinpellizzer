Fraxinus pennsylvanica f. scotica

Fraxinus pennsylvanica f. scotica, commonly known as the Scotch white ash, is a naturally occurring variant of the white ash.

It is characterized by its white to pale green bark and its distinctive, compound leaves with long, narrow leaflets. This form is typically found in the southern United States, particularly in wetland or swampy areas. The plant prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often associated with riparian habitats.

It plays an important ecological role in providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
