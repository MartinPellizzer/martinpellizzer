Ficus shirleyana

Ficus shirleyana, commonly known as Shirley's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Thailand and Malaysia, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are typically dark green with a smooth texture. It produces small, fig-like fruits that are edible but not commonly cultivated for food.

Ficus shirleyana is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor environments.
