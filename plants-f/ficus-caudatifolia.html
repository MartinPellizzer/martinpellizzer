Ficus caudatifolia

Ficus caudatifolia, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its cascading, weeping branches that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement and can grow quite large. It is a member of the Moraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor environments.

However, it requires well-drained soil and moderate to bright indirect light to flourish.
