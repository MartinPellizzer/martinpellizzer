Festuca cretica

Festuca cretica, commonly known as Cretan bentgrass, is a perennial grass native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, blue-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Festuca cretica thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found in coastal and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
