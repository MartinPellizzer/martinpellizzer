Faroa axillaris

Faroa axillaris, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are usually pink or red in color. It has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its stems are often covered in fine hairs.
