Ficus glomerata var. miquelii

Ficus glomerata var. miquelii, commonly known as the Miquel's fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, leathery leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are often consumed by both humans and animals. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Its growth habit makes it suitable for use in shaded garden areas or as a houseplant.
