Fabria pilosa

Fabria pilosa, commonly known as the hairy legume, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and compound leaves with a hairy texture. The plant produces small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. Fabria pilosa is often found in dry, open habitats such as grasslands and disturbed soils.

It plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility in its native environments.
