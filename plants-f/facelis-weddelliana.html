Facelis weddelliana

Facelis weddelliana, commonly known as the Weddell's faceleaf, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky or sandy soils, often found in coastal regions. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are succulent and have a distinctive lobed shape, contributing to its ornamental appeal.

It is relatively drought-tolerant, making it suitable for xeriscaping in arid environments.
