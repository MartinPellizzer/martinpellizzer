Formania

Formania, commonly known as the ghost flower, is a rare and enigmatic plant native to the high altitudes of the Andes.

It is characterized by its translucent, waxy leaves that appear almost ethereal in the sunlight. The plant produces small, pale flowers that bloom only during the night, adding to its mysterious allure. Formania thrives in cool, humid environments and requires specific soil conditions to flourish.

Due to its rarity and unique appearance, it is highly sought after by botanists and plant enthusiasts alike.
