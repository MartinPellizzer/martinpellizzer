Funkia maculata

Funkia maculata, commonly known as the spotted daylily, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to East Asia.

It features striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or red with a distinctive yellow or white throat. The plant produces narrow, sword-shaped leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and is often cultivated in wetlands or water gardens.

Funkia maculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate waterlogged conditions.
