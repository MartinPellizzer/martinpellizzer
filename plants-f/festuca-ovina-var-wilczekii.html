Festuca ovina var. wilczekii

Festuca ovina var. wilczekii, commonly known as the Wilczek's sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky, or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience in challenging conditions.
