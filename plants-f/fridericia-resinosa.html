Fridericia resinosa

Fridericia resinosa, commonly known as the resinous friedericia, is a shrub native to the southern United States.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by small, spherical fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges and open habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
