Festuca ovina f. hispidispicula

Festuca ovina f. hispidispicula, commonly known as the hairyawn fescue, is a perennial grass species characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, with a slightly hairy texture along the leaf margins. The inflorescence consists of spikelets that are covered in fine, hairy bristles, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is well adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed areas.

This grass is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in moderate to dry conditions.
