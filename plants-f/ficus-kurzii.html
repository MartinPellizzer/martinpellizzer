Ficus kurzii

Ficus kurzii, commonly known as the Kurz's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and can grow as either a tree or a shrub. It produces small, fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature.

Ficus kurzii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in indoor settings.
