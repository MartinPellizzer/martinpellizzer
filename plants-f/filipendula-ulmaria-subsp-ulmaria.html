Filipendula ulmaria subsp. ulmaria

Filipendula ulmaria subsp.

ulmaria, commonly known as meadow sweet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, compound leaves that are often feather-like in appearance.

The plant produces showy, pinkish-white flower clusters that bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges. Filipendula ulmaria subsp.

ulmaria is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been historically used in traditional herbal medicine.
