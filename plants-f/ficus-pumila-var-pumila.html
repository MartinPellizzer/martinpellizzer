Ficus pumila var. pumila

Ficus pumila var. pumila, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is characterized by its dense, trailing growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, greenish fruits that turn yellow or orange when mature. It is native to eastern Asia and is often used in horticulture for its ability to climb and cover surfaces.

Its adaptability to various light conditions makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
