Fritillaria regis-georgii

Fritillaria regis-georgii, commonly known as the Georgian crown imperial, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Caucasus region.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from red to orange and yellow. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves at the base, which support its showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or mountainous terrain.

Fritillaria regis-georgii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
