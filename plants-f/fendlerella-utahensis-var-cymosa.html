Fendlerella utahensis var. cymosa

Fendlerella utahensis var. cymosa, commonly known as the Utah fendlerbush, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, typically in spring. The plant has small, oval leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, aiding in water retention. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Its botanical features make it well-adapted to desert conditions, with a compact growth habit and deep root system.
