Ferraria viridiflora

Ferraria viridiflora, commonly known as the green-flowered ferraria, is a South African plant belonging to the Iris family.

It is a perennial herb with slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers with three petals. The plant typically grows in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in open grasslands. It is characterized by its delicate appearance and is sometimes cultivated for ornamental purposes.

Ferraria viridiflora is also valued for its traditional medicinal uses in local communities.
