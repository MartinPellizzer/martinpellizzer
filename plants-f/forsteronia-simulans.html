Forsteronia simulans

Forsteronia simulans, commonly known as the false poinsettia, is a member of the Euphorbiaceae family.

It is a shrub native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its striking, colorful bracts that resemble poinsettia flowers, though they are not true flowers. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and typically have a waxy texture.

Forsteronia simulans thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.
