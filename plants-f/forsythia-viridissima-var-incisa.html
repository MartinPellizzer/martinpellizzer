Forsythia viridissima var. incisa

Forsythia viridissima var. incisa, commonly known as the cutleaf forsythia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its striking yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, preceding the foliage. The leaves are ovate to elliptic, with a serrated margin and a distinctive lobed base. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and well-drained soil, making it a popular ornamental plant in gardens.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both landscape design and ecological habitats.
