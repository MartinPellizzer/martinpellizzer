Festuca igoschiniae

Festuca igoschiniae, commonly known as the Igoschina fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to harsh environmental conditions. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny florets.

Festuca igoschiniae is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience in arid landscapes.
