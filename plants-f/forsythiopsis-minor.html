Forsythiopsis minor

Forsythiopsis minor, commonly known as the dwarf forsythia, is a deciduous shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and vibrant yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features opposite, simple, and ovate leaves that are typically dark green in color. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for garden borders and rock gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and relatively low maintenance, it is often cultivated in horticultural settings.
