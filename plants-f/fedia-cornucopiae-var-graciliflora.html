Fedia cornucopiae var. graciliflora

Fedia cornucopiae var. graciliflora, commonly known as the Graceful Hornplant, is a rare and striking species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that resemble horns, which are a unique adaptation to its harsh environment. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of violet and white, which bloom during the short summer months. Its roots are thick and fibrous, allowing it to retain moisture in arid conditions.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in conservation efforts.
