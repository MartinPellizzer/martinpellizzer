Ficus leucocoma

Ficus leucocoma, commonly known as the white-fig or white-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as Thailand, Vietnam, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often tinged with white or silver veins, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish flowers that develop into fig-like fruits, which are typically green when mature.

Ficus leucocoma is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
