Festuca rubra var. cyrnea

Festuca rubra var. cyrnea, commonly known as red mountain grass, is a cool-season grass native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and striking red or purple coloration during the fall and winter months. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that turns reddish in cooler temperatures.

Festuca rubra var. cyrnea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in challenging soil conditions.
