Ficus hispida var. odorata

Ficus hispida var. odorata, commonly known as the fragrant fig tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often covered with a fine, fuzzy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that eventually develop into edible figs. It is native to tropical regions of Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and its ability to produce fruit in suitable conditions.
