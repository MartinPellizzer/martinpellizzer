Fragaria helleri

Fragaria helleri, commonly known as Heller's strawberry, is a species of wild strawberry native to South America.

It is a perennial herbaceous plant that typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces small, yellowish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruits are small, red berries with a sweet-tart flavor, similar to other strawberry species.

Fragaria helleri is valued for its ornamental qualities and has also been studied for its potential in horticultural and ecological applications.
