Ficus thonningiana

Ficus thonningiana, commonly known as the African fig tree, is a deciduous tree native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically yellow or green. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in forested areas.

Ficus thonningiana is valued for its ornamental beauty and ecological importance in its native habitat.
