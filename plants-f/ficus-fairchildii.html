Ficus fairchildii

Ficus fairchildii, commonly known as the Benjamin fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate with a pointed tip. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in indoor environments.

The Benjamin fig is also valued for its air-purifying qualities and its ability to tolerate low light conditions.
