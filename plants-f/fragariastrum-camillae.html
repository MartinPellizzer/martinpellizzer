Fragariastrum camillae

Fragariastrum camillae, commonly known as the Camilla strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of Europe.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, edible berries. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sunlight. Its flowers are white with five petals and are followed by red, fleshy fruits that mature in late summer.

The Camilla strawberry is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, sweet flavor.
