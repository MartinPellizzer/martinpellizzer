Festuca henriquesii

Festuca henriquesii, commonly known as Henriques' fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear pale blue to purple flowers. This plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to high-altitude environments. It is often found in alpine meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Festuca henriquesii is also noted for its resilience to harsh weather conditions and limited water availability.
