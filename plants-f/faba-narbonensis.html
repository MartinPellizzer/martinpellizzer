Faba narbonensis

Faba narbonensis, commonly known as the Narbonne bean, is a leguminous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive kidney-shaped seeds. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. Its seeds are rich in protein and have been cultivated for centuries for both culinary and agricultural purposes.

The plant is well adapted to dry climates and is often grown in regions with a temperate Mediterranean climate.
