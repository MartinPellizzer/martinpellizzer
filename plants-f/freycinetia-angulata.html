Freycinetia angulata

Freycinetia angulata, commonly known as the zigzag sedge, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, zigzag-shaped leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in spike-like clusters. It thrives in moist, shady environments such as forest floors or wetlands.

Freycinetia angulata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
