Fritillaria reuteri

Fritillaria reuteri, commonly known as the Persian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with intricate patterns. The plant has a fibrous root system and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires a cold winter dormancy period to bloom successfully.

Fritillaria reuteri is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with suitable climates.
