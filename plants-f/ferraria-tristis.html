Ferraria tristis

Ferraria tristis, commonly known as the weeping bell orchid, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, erect stems and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers. The flowers are typically pale green to yellowish in color, with a distinctive three-lobed lip. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Ferraria tristis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in suitable climates.
