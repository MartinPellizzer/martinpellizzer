Ficus nekbudu

Ficus nekbudu, commonly known as the Nekudu fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its dense, broad leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus nekbudu is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to provide shade in gardens.
