Feuilleea virgata

Feuilleea virgata, commonly known as the silver leaf fern, is a species of fern native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are covered in a fine, silvery down. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as forest understories or along stream banks. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Feuilleea virgata is valued both for its ornamental appeal and its ecological role in maintaining forest biodiversity.
