Froriepia subpinnata

Froriepia subpinnata, commonly known as the silverleaf tree, is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its dense, silvery-gray foliage that gives it its common name. The plant has small, white, fragrant flowers that bloom in spring, followed by small, fleshy fruits. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Froriepia subpinnata is relatively drought-tolerant and can withstand mild frost, making it suitable for coastal gardens.
