Fuchsia × grandis

Fuchsia × grandis, commonly known as the common fuchsia, is a hybrid shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pendulous flowers that come in shades of red, purple, and pink. The plant has opposite, simple, and often hairy leaves that are elliptical in shape. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade.

Fuchsia × grandis is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and is often used in gardens and as a houseplant.
