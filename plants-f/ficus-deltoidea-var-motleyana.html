Ficus deltoidea var. motleyana

Ficus deltoidea var. motleyana, commonly known as the Motley fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically a deep green with prominent veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round figs that are often consumed by local communities. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

The varietal name "motleyana" refers to the distinctive, mottled leaf patterns that give it its unique appearance.
