Fialaris umbellata

Fialaris umbellata, commonly known as the umbrella plant, is a flowering plant native to certain regions of South America.

It is characterized by its distinctive umbellate inflorescence, which consists of a cluster of small, white or pale pink flowers arranged in a rounded shape. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors or wetlands. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Fialaris umbellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
