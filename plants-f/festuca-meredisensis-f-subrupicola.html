Festuca meredisensis f. subrupicola

Festuca meredisensis f. subrupicola, commonly known as the rock festuca, is a subspecies of the grass genus Festuca.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and fine, wiry leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of short hairs. This plant is typically found in rocky or gravelly habitats, where it thrives in well-drained soils. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny, spikelet-like flowers.

The subspecies is notable for its adaptability to harsh environments, making it a hardy and resilient plant.
