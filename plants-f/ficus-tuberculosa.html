Ficus tuberculosa

Ficus tuberculosa, commonly known as the banyan fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, waxy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into edible figs. It is a fast-growing tree that can reach heights of up to 20 meters.

Its extensive root system and aerial roots make it well adapted to tropical and subtropical environments.
