Ficus banosensis

Ficus banosensis, commonly known as the Banoensis fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Ficus banosensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting biodiversity.
