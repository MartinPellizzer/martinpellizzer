Forestiera pubescens

Forestiera pubescens, commonly known as the hairy forestiera, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are covered in fine, hairy texture, giving it its common name. The plant produces fragrant, white flowers in clusters during the spring, which are followed by small, red berries in the fall. It thrives in well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, making it adaptable to various garden conditions.

Its ornamental value comes from its attractive foliage and seasonal blooms, making it a popular choice for landscaping.
