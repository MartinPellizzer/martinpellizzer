Festuca bonariensis

Festuca bonariensis, commonly known as Argentine bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green blades and tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value.

Botanically, it belongs to the Poaceae family and is known for its adaptability to various soil conditions.
