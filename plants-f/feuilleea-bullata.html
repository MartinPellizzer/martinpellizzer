Feuilleea bullata

Feuilleea bullata, commonly known as the waxy leaf succulent, is a unique plant characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that store water, making it well adapted to arid environments.

Its leaves are typically oval or elliptical, with a waxy coating that reduces water loss and reflects sunlight. The plant produces small, clustered flowers in shades of yellow or orange, which bloom during the warmer months. Feuilleea bullata thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal watering, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

It is native to regions with hot, dry climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
