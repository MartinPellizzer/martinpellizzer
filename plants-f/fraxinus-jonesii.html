Fraxinus jonesii

Fraxinus jonesii, commonly known as Jones' ash, is a deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged fruits called samaras. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

Fraxinus jonesii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
