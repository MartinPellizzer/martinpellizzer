Ficus tjiela

Ficus tjiela, commonly known as the Tjiela fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a evergreen tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible fig fruits. Its roots are known for their ability to absorb water efficiently, making it adaptable to moist environments.

Ficus tjiela is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded areas.
