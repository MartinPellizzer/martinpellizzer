Ficus carica var. sprengellia

Ficus carica var. sprengellia, commonly known as the Sprengellia fig, is a cultivar of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its small, ornamental leaves that are typically a striking dark green with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are usually not edible but are valued for their ornamental appeal. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

This variety is often grown as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
