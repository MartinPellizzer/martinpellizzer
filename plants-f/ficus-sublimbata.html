Ficus sublimbata

Ficus sublimbata, commonly known as the velvet fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. The plant produces small, inedible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus sublimbata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
