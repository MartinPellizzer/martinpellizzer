Festuca costata

Festuca costata, commonly known as the narrowleaf fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Festuca costata is often found in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, rocky slopes, and woodland edges.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
