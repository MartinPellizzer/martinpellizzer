Fraxinus pennsylvanica var. typica

Fraxinus pennsylvanica var. typica, commonly known as the green ash, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows to a height of 20–40 feet with a broad, spreading crown. The leaves are compound, with 7–13 pairs of oval, serrated leaflets that turn yellow in the fall. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, appearing in catkins, and it produces samaras for seed dispersal.

The tree is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, well-drained habitats.
