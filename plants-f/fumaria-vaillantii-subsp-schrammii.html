Fumaria vaillantii subsp. schrammii

Fumaria vaillantii subsp.

schrammii, commonly known as the Schramm's glory of the snow, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It produces a distinctive, branched stem with narrow, lance-shaped leaves.

This subspecies is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to grow in garden settings.
