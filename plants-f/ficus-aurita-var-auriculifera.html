Ficus aurita var. auriculifera

Ficus aurita var. auriculifera, commonly known as the ear-shaped fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that have a waxy texture and a deep green color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
