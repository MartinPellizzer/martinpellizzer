Fraxinus sambucifolia var. cucullata

Fraxinus sambucifolia var. cucullata, commonly known as the hooded ash, is a variant of the ash tree characterized by its distinctive leaf morphology.

Its leaves are elongated and have a unique shape, often appearing hooded or cupped at the base, giving it its common name. The plant is native to parts of Asia, particularly China, where it thrives in temperate climates. It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its attractive foliage.

The tree typically grows as a small to medium-sized deciduous tree, reaching heights of up to 10 meters.
