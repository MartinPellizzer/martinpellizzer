Fleurya funigera

Fleurya funigera, commonly known as the velvet tamarind, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the family Fabaceae, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is notable for its smooth, dark-brown bark and its ability to thrive in humid, tropical climates. Fleurya funigera is also valued for its timber, which is durable and used in construction and furniture-making.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
