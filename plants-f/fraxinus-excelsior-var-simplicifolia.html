Fraxinus excelsior var. simplicifolia

Fraxinus excelsior var. simplicifolia, commonly known as the European ash, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It features opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in umbels, followed by winged samaras that disperse its seeds. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers moist, well-drained conditions.

The bark is smooth and gray when young, developing fissures with age.
