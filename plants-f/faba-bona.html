Faba bona

Faba bona, commonly known as the broad bean or fava bean, is a flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces pods containing several angular, smooth seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. Native to the Mediterranean region, it has been cultivated for thousands of years as a food crop.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both agricultural and ecological contexts.
