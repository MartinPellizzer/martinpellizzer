Ficus callophylla var. leytensis

Ficus callophylla var. leytensis, commonly known as the Leyte fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces edible figs that are typically harvested when they are fully ripe and soft. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
