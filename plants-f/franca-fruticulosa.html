Franca fruticulosa

Franca fruticulosa, commonly known as the Brazilian jasmine, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows to a height of 1 to 3 meters, featuring woody stems and lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, often emitting a strong, sweet scent. Its botanical characteristics include opposite leaf arrangement and the presence of glandular hairs on the foliage.

Franca fruticulosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and aromatic properties.
