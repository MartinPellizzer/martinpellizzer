Fuchsia abrupta

Fuchsia abrupta, commonly known as the mountain fuchsia, is a shrub native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from deep red to purple. The plant typically grows in high-altitude regions, thriving in cool, moist climates with well-drained soil. Its foliage consists of dark green, elliptical leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down.

Fuchsia abrupta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens that mimic its natural habitat.
