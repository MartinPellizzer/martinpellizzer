Freycinetia runcingensis

Freycinetia runcingensis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellowish-white flowers, which are followed by triangular, fibrous fruits. It thrives in coastal areas and is often found in wetlands or near brackish water.

Freycinetia runcingensis is valued for its ornamental appearance and has traditional uses in various island cultures.
