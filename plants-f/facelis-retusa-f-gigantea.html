Facelis retusa f. gigantea

Facelis retusa f. gigantea, commonly known as the giant faceleaf.

It is a variant of the native Hawaiian plant Facelis retusa, characterized by its large, broad leaves that can reach up to 30 inches in length. The plant is a shrub or small tree, typically growing in coastal areas with sandy soils. Its leaves are usually leathery and have a distinctive, waxy texture.

It is adapted to the arid and saline conditions of its native habitat.
