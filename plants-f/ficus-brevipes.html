Ficus brevipes

Ficus brevipes, commonly known as the short-stemmed fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its thick, woody stem and broad, glossy leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits.

Ficus brevipes is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
