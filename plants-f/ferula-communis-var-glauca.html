Ferula communis var. glauca

Ferula communis var. glauca, commonly known as blue ferula, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, sparsely branched stems that can reach up to 2 meters in height. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, which are typically followed by long, narrow, gray-green bracts. Its leaves are lance-shaped and covered with a fine, powdery blue-gray coating, giving it a distinctive appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
