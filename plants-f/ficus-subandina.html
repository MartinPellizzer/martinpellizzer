Ficus subandina

Ficus subandina, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its cascading, pendulous branches that give it a distinctive appearance, often used in ornamental landscaping. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are opposite in arrangement and can grow up to 10 centimeters in length. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by green, round fruits that turn yellow when mature.

Ficus subandina is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
