Ficus carautana

Ficus carautana, commonly known as the Carauta fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped. It produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Ficus carautana thrives in humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.
