Faba vulgaris var. nebulosa-violacea

Faba vulgaris var. nebulosa-violacea, commonly known as the viola nebula, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its distinctive, colorful flowers that display a blend of purple, violet, and white hues. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30-60 cm and produces heart-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. Its flowers bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its visual beauty.
