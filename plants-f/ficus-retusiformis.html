Ficus retusiformis

Ficus retusiformis, commonly known as the rough-leaved fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, giving them a rough texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in syconia, which are specialized fruiting structures.

The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.
