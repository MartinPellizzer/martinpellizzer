Ficus sabahana

Ficus sabahana, commonly known as the Sabah fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in Malaysia and Brunei. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor.

Ficus sabahana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
