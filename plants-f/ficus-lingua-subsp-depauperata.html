Ficus lingua subsp. depauperata

Ficus lingua subsp.

depauperata, commonly known as the sparse-leaf fig, is a unique species within the Ficus genus. It is characterized by its reduced foliage, with small, often sparse leaves that give it a distinctive appearance.

This subspecies is native to parts of Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when mature.

Its botanical features make it a subject of interest in both horticultural and ecological studies.
