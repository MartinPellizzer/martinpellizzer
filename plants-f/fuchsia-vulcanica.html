Fuchsia vulcanica

Fuchsia vulcanica, commonly known as the volcanic fuchsia, is a striking flowering plant native to the volcanic regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep red to purple, often with contrasting white or yellow markings. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, often found in cloud forests or near waterfalls. Its leaves are glossy and dark green, providing a striking contrast to its showy blooms.

Fuchsia vulcanica is valued not only for its ornamental beauty but also for its role in local ecosystems.
