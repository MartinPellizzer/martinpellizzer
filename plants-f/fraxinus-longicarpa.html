Fraxinus longicarpa

Fraxinus longicarpa, commonly known as the long-fruited ash.

It is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America, characterized by its elongated, cylindrical fruits that hang in clusters. The leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate with a serrated margin. It typically grows to a height of 15-30 meters, featuring a straight trunk and a rounded crown.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
