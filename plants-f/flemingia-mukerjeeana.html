Flemingia mukerjeeana

Flemingia mukerjeeana, commonly known as the Indian flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its large, palmately compound leaves. The plant produces showy, fiery orange to red flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting pollinators such as bees and birds. Its fruits are legumes that contain several seeds, and the tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Flemingia mukerjeeana is also valued for its hard, durable wood used in construction and furniture.
