Fabiana violacea

Fabiana violacea, commonly known as the violet bellflower, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the family Violaceae and is characterized by its striking violet or purple bell-shaped flowers. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and often covered in fine hairs.

Fabiana violacea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and adaptability to various soil types.
