Fuchsia microphylla subsp. aprica

Fuchsia microphylla subsp.

aprica, commonly known as the miniature fuchsia, is a compact shrub native to New Zealand. It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a dark green color with a slightly hairy texture.

The plant produces delicate, pendulous flowers in shades of pink and purple, which bloom in late winter to early spring. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its botanical characteristics make it well-suited for rock gardens and containers.
