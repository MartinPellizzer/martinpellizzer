Fuchsia perscandens

Fuchsia perscandens, commonly known as the silverleaf fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that range in color from deep purple to bright red. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a silvery-gray underside, giving it its common name.

Fuchsia perscandens thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
