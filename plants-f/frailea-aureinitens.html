Frailea aureinitens

Frailea aureinitens, commonly known as the golden crown cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and bright yellow to orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant has a spiny exterior with areoles that bear both spines and flowers. It thrives in well-draining soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Its striking appearance makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and indoor cultivation.
