Festuca ovina var. major

Festuca ovina var. major, commonly known as sheep fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typically blooming in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to tolerate drought.

Festuca ovina var. major is also valued for its role in stabilizing soil and preventing erosion in natural habitats.
