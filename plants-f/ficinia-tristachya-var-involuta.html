Ficinia tristachya var. involuta

Ficinia tristachya var. involuta, commonly known as the spiral sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive spirally arranged leaves that give it a unique and elegant appearance. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, thriving in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its flowering stems produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in dense spikes.

The spiral sedge is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal and ability to tolerate wet conditions.
