Ficus costata

Ficus costata, commonly known as the warty fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, warty bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are typically yellow or orange when ripe. Ficus costata thrives in humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
