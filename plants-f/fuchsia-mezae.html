Fuchsia mezae

Fuchsia mezae, commonly known as the Mezae fuchsia, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces showy, pendulous flowers in shades of red, purple, and pink. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are arranged opposite each other on the stems. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions with high elevation.

Fuchsia mezae is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
