Ferdinandusa dissimiliflora

Ferdinandusa dissimiliflora, commonly known as the Amazonian evergreen tree, is a species native to the dense rainforests of South America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged oppositely on the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, often with a pink or white hue. Its fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.

This plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local medicine.
