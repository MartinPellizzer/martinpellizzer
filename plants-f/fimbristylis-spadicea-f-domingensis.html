Fimbristylis spadicea f. domingensis

Fimbristylis spadicea f. domingensis, commonly known as the Domingo's sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas. Its flowers are small and inconspicuous, with three stamens and a single pistil, typical of the sedge family.

It is native to parts of South America, particularly in regions with tropical and subtropical climates.
