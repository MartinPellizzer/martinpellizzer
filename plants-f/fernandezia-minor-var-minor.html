Fernandezia minor var. minor

Fernandezia minor var. minor, commonly known as the dwarf fernandezia, is a small, delicate fern native to the Andes region of South America.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as rocky crevices and forest floors. The plant has narrow, lance-shaped fronds that are usually less than 10 centimeters in length. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
