Ficus congesta

Ficus congesta, commonly known as the clustered fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its dense clusters of small, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, edible figs that are often used in traditional medicine. Ficus congesta thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring moist, well-drained soil and partial to full shade.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor cultivation and ornamental gardening.
