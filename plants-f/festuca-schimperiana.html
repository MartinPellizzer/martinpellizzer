Festuca schimperiana

Festuca schimperiana, commonly known as Schimper's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Festuca schimperiana is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its resilience and ability to stabilize soil in harsh conditions.
