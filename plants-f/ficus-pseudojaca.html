Ficus pseudojaca

Ficus pseudojaca, commonly known as the African ficus, is a tropical evergreen tree native to western and central Africa.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by round, greenish-white fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Ficus pseudojaca is also valued for its ability to purify indoor air, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
