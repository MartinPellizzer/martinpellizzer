Fritillaria imperialis

Fritillaria imperialis, commonly known as the Crown Imperial, is a bulbous plant native to the mountains of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, tall stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers in shades of red, orange, and yellow. The plant produces large, showy inflorescences that resemble a crown, hence its common name. Its bulbs are perennial and require well-drained soil and full sun to thrive.

Fritillaria imperialis is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
