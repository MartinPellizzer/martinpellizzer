Ficus subopaca

Ficus subopaca, commonly known as the dark fig or black ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its dark, almost black, foliage. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 6 meters. Its leaves are glossy and ovate, with a dense, leathery texture.

Ficus subopaca produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.
