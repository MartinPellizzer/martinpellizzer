Festuca abyssinica var. schimperiana

Festuca abyssinica var. schimperiana, commonly known as Abyssinian bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to Ethiopia.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, thriving in cool, temperate climates. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.
