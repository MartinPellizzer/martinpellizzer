Ficus bullata

Ficus bullata, commonly known as the Variegated Ficus or the Bullata Fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, glossy, dark green leaves with creamy-white or light green variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish-yellow fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ornamental appeal.

Ficus bullata is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
