Frankenia margaritae

Frankenia margaritae, commonly known as the silverleaf frankenia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its small, fleshy leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white layer of wax, which helps reduce water loss in arid environments. The plant produces clusters of small, yellow-orange flowers that bloom during the summer months. It thrives in sandy or rocky soils and is often found in coastal dunes or along the edges of salt marshes.

Frankenia margaritae is well adapted to withstand high salinity and strong coastal winds.
