Fuchsia macrostigma

Fuchsia macrostigma, commonly known as the large-stigma fuchsia, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces showy, pendulous flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly serrated edge and a robust root system that helps it thrive in rocky or coastal environments. Its flowers are pollinated primarily by hummingbirds and bees, making it an important plant for local ecosystems.

The plant is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to grow in well-drained soil.
