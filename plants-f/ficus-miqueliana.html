Ficus miqueliana

Ficus miqueliana, commonly known as the Philippine ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs, which are an important food source for various wildlife. Ficus miqueliana thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in suitable climates.
