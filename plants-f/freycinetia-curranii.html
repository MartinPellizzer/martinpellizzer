Freycinetia curranii

Freycinetia curranii, commonly known as the Curran's pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the Pacific Islands, including parts of French Polynesia and the Cook Islands. The plant is characterized by its tall, branched inflorescence and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. Its leaves are long and narrow, with a fibrous texture, and it produces small, fragrant flowers.

The plant thrives in coastal areas and is adapted to saline and humid conditions.
