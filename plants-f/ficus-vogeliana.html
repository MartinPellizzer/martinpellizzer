Ficus vogeliana

Ficus vogeliana, commonly known as the Vogel's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often lobed or serrated. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature.

Ficus vogeliana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
