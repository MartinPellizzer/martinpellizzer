Felicia dubia

Felicia dubia, commonly known as the yellow daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers surrounded by white ray florets, which bloom in clusters. The plant is typically low-growing, with a mat-forming habit, and thrives in well-drained soils. It is drought-tolerant and often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Felicia dubia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
