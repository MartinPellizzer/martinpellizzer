Forsskaolea fruticosa

Forsskaolea fruticosa, commonly known as the African milk tree, is a shrub native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, opposite leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in clusters, followed by orange or red berries. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, making it drought-tolerant.

The plant has been used traditionally for its latex, which has medicinal and industrial applications.
