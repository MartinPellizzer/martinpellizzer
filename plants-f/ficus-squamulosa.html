Ficus squamulosa

Ficus squamulosa, commonly known as the squamulose fig, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves that are often covered with a fine, velvety layer of hair, giving it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits.

Ficus squamulosa is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
