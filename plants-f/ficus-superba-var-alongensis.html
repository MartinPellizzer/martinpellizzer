Ficus superba var. alongensis

Ficus superba var. alongensis, commonly known as the Alongensis Ficus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are often enclosed in a spiny calyx, which protects the developing seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

The species is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its ability to tolerate partial shade.
