Ficus ampulliformis

Ficus ampulliformis, commonly known as the bottle tree, is a striking tropical plant native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its thick, bulbous trunk that stores water, allowing it to thrive in arid conditions. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement, contributing to its ornamental appeal. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits.

Due to its unique appearance and adaptability, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
