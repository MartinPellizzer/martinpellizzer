Fagus obliqua var. integrisquamata

Fagus obliqua var. integrisquamata, commonly known as the smooth-scaled beech, is a variant of the beech tree native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale bark and elliptical leaves with a distinctive waxy texture. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland and hill forest ecosystems. Its fruiting bodies, called samaras, are winged seeds that aid in wind dispersal.

This species plays an important role in maintaining the biodiversity of its native habitat.
