Ficus akaie

Ficus akaie, commonly known as the African rubber plant, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by non-edible fig fruits. It is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

The leaves are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems, giving it a unique and attractive appearance.
