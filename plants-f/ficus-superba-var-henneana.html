Ficus superba var. henneana

Ficus superba var. henneana, commonly known as the Hennean fig, is a species native to parts of Africa, particularly Tanzania.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its thick, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife and humans.

Its adaptability to various soil types and climate conditions makes it a resilient and widely distributed species in its native habitat.
