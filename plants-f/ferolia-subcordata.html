Ferolia subcordata

Ferolia subcordata, commonly known as the narrow-leaved citron, is a small evergreen shrub native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its narrow, leathery leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, yellow-orange fruits that are similar in appearance to lemons but are typically not consumed by humans.

It thrives in coastal areas with well-drained soil and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and citrus scent.
