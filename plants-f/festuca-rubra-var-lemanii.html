Festuca rubra var. lemanii

Festuca rubra var. lemanii, commonly known as the alpine bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It typically forms dense, mat-like growth and thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soils. The plant has fine, wiry leaves that are often reddish at the base, giving it a distinctive appearance. It is well adapted to harsh alpine conditions, including strong winds and low temperatures.

Festuca rubra var. lemanii is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and ability to stabilize soil in rocky or difficult terrain.
