Festuca paphlagonica subsp. villosula

Festuca paphlagonica subsp.

villosula, commonly known as the woolly meadowgrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Turkey and the Caucasus. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and hairy foliage, which gives it its common name.

The plant typically grows in moist, grassy habitats such as meadows and stream banks. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute, wind-pollinated flowers.
