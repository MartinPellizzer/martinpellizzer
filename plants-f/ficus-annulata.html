Ficus annulata

Ficus annulata, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, including India, China, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its dense, trailing growth habit and its ability to climb or trail along surfaces. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped, glossy, and have a waxy texture.

Ficus annulata produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that develop into fig-like fruits, which are an important food source for various wildlife species.
