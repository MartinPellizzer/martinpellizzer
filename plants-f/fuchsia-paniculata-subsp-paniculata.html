Fuchsia paniculata subsp. paniculata

Fuchsia paniculata subsp.

paniculata, commonly known as the red fuchsia, is a species native to South America, particularly in regions of Peru and Ecuador. It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its striking, pendulous inflorescences of fuchsia-colored flowers.

The plant features simple, ovate leaves with a slightly hairy texture, and its flowers are typically borne in dense clusters at the ends of branches. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
