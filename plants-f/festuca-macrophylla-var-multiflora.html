Festuca macrophylla var. multiflora

Festuca macrophylla var. multiflora, commonly known as the Evergreen Bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and tufted inflorescences that produce blue to purple flowers in late spring to early summer. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and xeriscaping. Its compact growth habit and ornamental value make it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping.

It is also valued for its ability to tolerate drought and poor soil conditions.
