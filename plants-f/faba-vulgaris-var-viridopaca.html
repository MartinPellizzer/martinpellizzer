Faba vulgaris var. viridopaca

Faba vulgaris var. viridopaca, commonly known as the green clover, is a variety of the common vetch.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are typically a vibrant green color, and its small, pea-like flowers that bloom in early spring. This plant is a legume, meaning it has the ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen through a symbiotic relationship with rhizobia bacteria in its root nodules. It thrives in temperate climates and is often used as a cover crop or forage due to its rapid growth and nutrient-rich biomass.

Its botanical features make it an important plant in both ecological and agricultural contexts.
