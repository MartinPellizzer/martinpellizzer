Flickingeria rhodobalion

Flickingeria rhodobalion, commonly known as the red-flowered bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features slender, upright stems that bear clusters of bell-shaped flowers, typically pink to deep red in color. The plant has lanceolate leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem. Its flowers bloom in the summer months, attracting various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

Flickingeria rhodobalion is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
