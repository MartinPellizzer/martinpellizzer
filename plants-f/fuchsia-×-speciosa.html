Fuchsia × speciosa

Fuchsia × speciosa, commonly known as the common fuchsia, is a hybrid plant that belongs to the genus Fuchsia in the family Onagraceae.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that typically display shades of red, pink, and purple, often with a distinctive tubular shape. The plant is native to South America and is widely cultivated as an ornamental species for its striking floral display. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and containers.

Fuchsia × speciosa is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
