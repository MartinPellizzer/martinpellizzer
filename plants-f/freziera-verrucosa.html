Freziera verrucosa

Freziera verrucosa, commonly known as the woolly freziera, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of South America.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy stems and broad, waxy leaves that are covered in a dense layer of fine, white hairs. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in the spring. Freziera verrucosa thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found in shaded, rocky habitats.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native ecosystems.
