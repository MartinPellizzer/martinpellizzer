Fumaria cilicica

Fumaria cilicica, commonly known as the Cilician gloryflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, yellowish-orange flowers that bloom in spring. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens.
