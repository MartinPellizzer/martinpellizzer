Fuchsia hypoleuca

Fuchsia hypoleuca, commonly known as the silverleaf fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking silver-gray foliage and clusters of showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple. The plant is a shrub that typically grows in moist, shaded environments, often in mountainous regions. Its leaves are opposite and ovate, with a waxy texture that helps it retain moisture.

Fuchsia hypoleuca is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens with high humidity and partial shade.
