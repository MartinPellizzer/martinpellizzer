Ficus rokko

Ficus rokko, commonly known as the Rokko ficus, is a species of flowering plant native to Japan.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or yellow markings. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible figs, though these are typically not consumed by humans. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ornamental value.

Ficus rokko is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
