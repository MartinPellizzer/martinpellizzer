Ficus archboldiana

Ficus archboldiana, commonly known as the Archbold's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the dry forests of southern Mexico and northern Central America. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible figs that are typically small and yellowish.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
