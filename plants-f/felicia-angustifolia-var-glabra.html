Felicia angustifolia var. glabra

Felicia angustifolia var. glabra, commonly known as the smooth everlasting flower, is a perennial plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and small, daisy-like yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The leaves are narrow, linear, and covered in fine hairs, giving the plant a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

The plant is also known for its long-lasting flowers, which remain intact even when dried, contributing to its common name.
