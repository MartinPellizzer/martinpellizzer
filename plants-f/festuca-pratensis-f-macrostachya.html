Festuca pratensis f. macrostachya

Festuca pratensis f. macrostachya, commonly known as the tall-stemmed meadow fescue, is a botanical variant of meadow fescue characterized by its elongated, robust stems.

It is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Europe and parts of Asia. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and pastures. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with leaves that are usually blue-green in color.

The inflorescence consists of dense, spike-like clusters of small, two-lobed flowers, which are typically pale green to brown in color.
