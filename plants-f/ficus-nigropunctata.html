Ficus nigropunctata

Ficus nigropunctata, commonly known as the black dot fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its dark green, glossy leaves that are often dotted with small, black specks, giving it its common name. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green when mature.

Ficus nigropunctata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
