Ficus calodictya var. gamophylla

Ficus calodictya var. gamophylla, commonly known as the Variegated Ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white or cream variegation. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters in optimal conditions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible figs, which are an important food source for various wildlife.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
