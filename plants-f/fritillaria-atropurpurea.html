Fritillaria atropurpurea

Fritillaria atropurpurea, commonly known as the purple star lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or reddish-purple in color, with a distinctive star-like pattern. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Fritillaria atropurpurea is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
