Festuca temulenta

Festuca temulenta, commonly known as rough fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered with fine hairs. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous florets, which are often pale green to brown in color. Festuca temulenta is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in meadows, pastures, and disturbed sites.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in marginal environments.
