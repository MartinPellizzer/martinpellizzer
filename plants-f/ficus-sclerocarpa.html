Ficus sclerocarpa

Ficus sclerocarpa, commonly known as the rock fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to arid and semi-arid regions of Africa, the Middle East, and southern Asia. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in harsh environments. Its small, clustered fruits are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor.

Ficus sclerocarpa is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
