Franchetella arborescens var. platyphylla

Franchetella arborescens var. platyphylla, commonly known as the broad-leaved frangipani, is a tropical shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, waxy, ovate leaves that are typically broad and glossy. The plant produces clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom throughout the year, especially during the warmer months. Its bark is smooth and greyish, with a distinctive aromatic scent when crushed.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine in some regions.
