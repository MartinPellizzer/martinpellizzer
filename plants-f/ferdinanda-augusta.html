Ferdinanda augusta

Ferdinanda augusta, commonly known as the royal lily, is a striking ornamental plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that can reach up to 12 inches in diameter, typically in shades of white, pink, and red. The plant features broad, lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged in a fan-like pattern. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Ferdinanda augusta is often cultivated for its aesthetic value in gardens and as a cut flower.
