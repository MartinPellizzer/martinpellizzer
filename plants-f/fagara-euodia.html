Fagara euodia

Fagara euodia, commonly known as the Christmas tree or the sacred tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America. The plant is characterized by its evergreen foliage and fragrant, citrus-scented leaves. It produces small, white flowers followed by yellow berries.

Due to its ornamental value and cultural significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and used in traditional medicine.
