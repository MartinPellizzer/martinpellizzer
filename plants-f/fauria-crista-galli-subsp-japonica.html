Fauria crista-galli subsp. japonica

Fauria crista-galli subsp.

japonica, commonly known as the Japanese crownbeard, is a flowering plant native to East Asia. It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spiky flower heads.

The plant typically grows in moist, shady environments such as woodlands and stream banks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a velvety texture, while the flowers are yellow with a central disk.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
