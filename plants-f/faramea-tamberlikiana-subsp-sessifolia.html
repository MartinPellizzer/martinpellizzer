Faramea tamberlikiana subsp. sessifolia

Faramea tamberlikiana subsp.

sessifolia, commonly known as the slender faramea, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa. It is a shrub or small tree characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical in shape.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, followed by small, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for ecological and horticultural study.
