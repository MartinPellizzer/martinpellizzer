Flemingia cumingiana

Flemingia cumingiana, commonly known as the Malayan flemingia, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds. It is often found in secondary forests and along riverbanks, thriving in moist, well-drained soils.

Flemingia cumingiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
