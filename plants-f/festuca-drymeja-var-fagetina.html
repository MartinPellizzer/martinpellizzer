Festuca drymeja var. fagetina

Festuca drymeja var. fagetina, commonly known as the Faget's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Festuca drymeja var. fagetina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in stabilizing soil in harsh environments.
