Fargesia sapaensis

Fargesia sapaensis, commonly known as the Sapa bamboo, is a species of flowering bamboo native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a bright green color. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, usually white or pale yellow, during the spring season. Fargesia sapaensis is a shade-tolerant plant that prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental gardening.

It is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to create a lush, green backdrop in gardens and landscapes.
