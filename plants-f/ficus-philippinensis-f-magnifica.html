Ficus philippinensis f. magnifica

Ficus philippinensis f. magnifica, commonly known as the Philippine fig or the magnifica fig, is a cultivar of the ficus genus native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring bright, indirect light and well-draining soil.

This ornamental plant is popular in indoor gardening due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
