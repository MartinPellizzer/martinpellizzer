Festuca pratensis subsp. appenina

Festuca pratensis subsp.

appenina, commonly known as the Italian meadow fescue, is a grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically dark green in color.

This subspecies thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and pastures. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers. Festuca pratensis subsp.

appenina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and turfgrass management.
