Ficus prolixoides

Ficus prolixoides, commonly known as the long-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, lance-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-drained soil.

Ficus prolixoides is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.
