Ficus serraria var. membranacea

Ficus serraria var. membranacea, commonly known as the membranaceous fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are often covered in a fine, waxy coating. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature.

This variety is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
