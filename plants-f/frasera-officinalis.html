Frasera officinalis

Frasera officinalis, commonly known as the American bellflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its bell-shaped, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and streambanks. Its leaves are lance-shaped and arranged alternately along the stem.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
