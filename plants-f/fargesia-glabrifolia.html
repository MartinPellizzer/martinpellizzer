Fargesia glabrifolia

Fargesia glabrifolia, commonly known as the silver fargesia, is a species of bamboo native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright culms that are covered in a fine, silvery-white layer of hair, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale green, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is well-suited for use in shaded garden settings.

Fargesia glabrifolia is also valued for its ability to form dense thickets, making it a useful plant for erosion control and habitat creation.
