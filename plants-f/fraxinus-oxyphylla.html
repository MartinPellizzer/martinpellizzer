Fraxinus oxyphylla

Fraxinus oxyphylla, commonly known as the white ash.

It is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America, characterized by its smooth, light-gray bark and compound leaves with 9 to 15 leaflets. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. Its wood is strong and flexible, historically valued for basket-making and tool handles.

The plant has ecological significance, providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
