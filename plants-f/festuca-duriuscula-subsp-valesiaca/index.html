Festuca Duriuscula Subsp. Valesiaca

Festuca duriuscula subsp. valesiaca is a rare grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It thrives in high-altitude environments where few plants can survive. This subspecies is known for its dense, low-growing foliage and silver-blue hues. Its ecological importance lies in its role as a keystone species in alpine ecosystems. Conservation efforts are crucial due to habitat loss and climate change threats.
