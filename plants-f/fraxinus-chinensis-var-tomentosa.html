Fraxinus chinensis var. tomentosa

Fraxinus chinensis var. tomentosa, commonly known as the Chinese white ash, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It is characterized by its grayish-brown bark that becomes fissured with age and its alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas, contributing to ecological diversity.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for urban landscaping.
