Fagara dschaensis

Fagara dschaensis, commonly known as the Congo wax tree, is a member of the Rutaceae family and is native to the tropical rainforests of Central and West Africa.

It is a medium-sized evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters in height, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are clustered in axillary inflorescences, followed by yellowish-orange fruits that contain several seeds.

Its latex is known to have medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in some African cultures for its potential therapeutic effects.
