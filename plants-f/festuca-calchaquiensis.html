Festuca calchaquiensis

Festuca calchaquiensis, commonly known as the Calchaqui bentgrass, is a species of grass native to the high-altitude regions of Argentina.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered in fine hairs. This plant thrives in cold, arid environments and is often found in rocky or mountainous terrain. Festuca calchaquiensis is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, including low temperatures and strong winds.

Its botanical features make it an important component of the local flora in its native habitat.
