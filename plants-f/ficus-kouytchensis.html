Ficus kouytchensis

Ficus kouytchensis, commonly known as the Kouytchensis fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly found in countries like Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible figs. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is well-suited for both indoor and outdoor environments.
