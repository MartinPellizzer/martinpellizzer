Festuca oroana

Festuca oroana, commonly known as alpine bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats on rocky or alpine soils. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions with well-drained soil. Festuca oroana is known for its ornamental value, with its silver-blue foliage adding visual interest to gardens and natural landscapes.

It is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for alpine flora and fauna.
