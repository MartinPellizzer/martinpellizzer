Festuca rubra f. squarrosa

Festuca rubra f. squarrosa, commonly known as the squarrosa fescue, is a hemicryptophyte with a low-growing, mat-forming habit.

It is characterized by its fine, grass-like leaves and compact, tufted growth pattern. The plant typically features reddish-brown stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is well adapted to rocky or gravelly soils and is often found in alpine or arctic environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience in harsh conditions.
