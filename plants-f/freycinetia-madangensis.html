Freycinetia madangensis

Freycinetia madangensis, commonly known as the Madang pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands, particularly Papua New Guinea.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its robust, fibrous trunk and large, aromatic leaves. The plant thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in mangrove or brackish water areas. Its leaves are used in traditional crafts and for thatching, while its fruits are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor.

Freycinetia madangensis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing coastal soils and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
