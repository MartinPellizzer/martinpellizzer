Fissistigma rugosum

Fissistigma rugosum, commonly known as the rough fissistigma, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its woody stems and small, fragrant flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant has serrated leaves that are often covered in a fine, rough texture. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes.

Due to its ornamental value and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in horticulture.
