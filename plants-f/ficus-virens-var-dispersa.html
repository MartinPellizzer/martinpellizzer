Ficus virens var. dispersa

Ficus virens var. dispersa, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a weeping or cascading manner. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, greenish-white fruits that are often consumed by birds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil and indirect sunlight.

Due to its attractive appearance and adaptability, it is a popular ornamental plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.
