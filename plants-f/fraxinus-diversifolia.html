Fraxinus diversifolia

Fraxinus diversifolia, commonly known as the variable ash, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, ovate to lanceolate leaves that vary in shape and size across its range. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. Its bark is typically smooth with a pale to reddish-brown color.

Fraxinus diversifolia is often found in moist, shaded habitats such as forests and riverbanks.
