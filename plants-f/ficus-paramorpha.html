Ficus paramorpha

Ficus paramorpha, commonly known as the Philippine ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Philippines and is characterized by its large, lobed leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in a compact inflorescence, which are followed by small, round fruits. Ficus paramorpha is typically found in lowland rainforests and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its adaptability to various soil types makes it a resilient plant in tropical environments.
