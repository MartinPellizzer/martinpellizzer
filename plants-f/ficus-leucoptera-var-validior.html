Ficus leucoptera var. validior

Ficus leucoptera var. validior, commonly known as the white-veined fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent white veins, giving it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

This variety is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
