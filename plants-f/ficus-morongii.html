Ficus morongii

Ficus morongii, commonly known as the Morongii fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Ficus morongii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.
