Fagus sylvatica subsp. sanguinea

Fagus sylvatica subsp.

sanguinea, commonly known as the blood birch, is a subspecies of the European beech. It is characterized by its distinctive reddish-brown bark, which becomes more pronounced with age.

The leaves are typically oval-shaped with a smooth margin and a pale underside. It prefers moist, well-drained soils and is often found in cool, temperate climates.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in forest ecosystems.
