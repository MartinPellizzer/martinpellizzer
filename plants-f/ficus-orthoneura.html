Ficus orthoneura

Ficus orthoneura, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, China, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, fig-like fruits that are edible and serve as a food source for various animals.

Ficus orthoneura is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to different growing conditions.
