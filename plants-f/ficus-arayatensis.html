Ficus arayatensis

Ficus arayatensis, commonly known as the Arawak fig, is a species of fig tree native to the Caribbean islands.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, green figs that ripen to a reddish color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Ficus arayatensis is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
