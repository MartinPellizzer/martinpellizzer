Festuca ovina var. numidica

Festuca ovina var. numidica, commonly known as Algerian bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green, bluish-gray foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in ornamental landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.

Festuca ovina var. numidica is also valued for its drought tolerance and ability to form dense, low-maintenance turf.
