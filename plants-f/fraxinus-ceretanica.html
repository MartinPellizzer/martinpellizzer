Fraxinus ceretanica

Fraxinus ceretanica, commonly known as the Chinese ash, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The tree has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape, with serrated margins. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers are borne in drooping clusters and are followed by small, winged fruits.

The bark is smooth and light gray, becoming fissured with age, and the tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
