Festuca expansa

Festuca expansa, commonly known as the broadleaf fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. This plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky slopes. Festuca expansa is well adapted to temperate climates and is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and low maintenance requirements.

Its flowering spikes produce small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or brown in color.
