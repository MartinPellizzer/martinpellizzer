Ficus pseudopyriformis

Ficus pseudopyriformis, commonly known as the fig fig or the oval-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green. It produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when ripe.

Ficus pseudopyriformis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
