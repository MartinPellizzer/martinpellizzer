Ficus mindoroensis

Ficus mindoroensis, commonly known as the Mindoro fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and distinctive, clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible figs, which are a key feature of its botanical profile. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
