Fimbristylis fordii

Fimbristylis fordii, commonly known as Ford's sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetlands, marshes, and along the edges of ponds and streams, preferring moist to wet soil conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often reddish in color. It is characterized by its rhizomatous growth habit, which allows it to spread and form dense mats.

Fimbristylis fordii plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
