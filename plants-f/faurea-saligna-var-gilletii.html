Faurea saligna var. gilletii

Faurea saligna var. gilletii, commonly known as the Gilbert's sycamore, is a variant of the sycamore tree native to California.

It is characterized by its smooth, light gray bark and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often slightly lobed. The plant produces small, inconspicuous green flowers in clusters, which are followed by woody, spherical fruits. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones and along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in supporting local wildlife and its ornamental appeal in landscapes.
