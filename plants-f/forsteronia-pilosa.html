Forsteronia pilosa

Forsteronia pilosa, commonly known as the silky thornapple, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves and stems, which give it a distinctive, rough texture. The plant produces showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its fruit is a capsule that splits open to release small, hairy seeds.

Forsteronia pilosa is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
