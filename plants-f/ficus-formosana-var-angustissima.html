Ficus formosana var. angustissima

Ficus formosana var. angustissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green or yellow when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant in tropical climates.
