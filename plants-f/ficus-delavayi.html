Ficus delavayi

Ficus delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China, particularly in Yunnan Province. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature.

Ficus delavayi is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
