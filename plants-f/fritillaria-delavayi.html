Fritillaria delavayi

Fritillaria delavayi, commonly known as the Delavay fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of western China.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a distinctive, often bulbous stem. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows at high elevations.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
