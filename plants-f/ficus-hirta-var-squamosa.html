Ficus hirta var. squamosa

Ficus hirta var. squamosa, commonly known as the hairy fig or squamosa fig, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa and Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree, typically reaching heights of up to 6 meters. The plant is characterized by its hairy, glandular leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. Its leaves are usually ovate to elliptical in shape, with a prominent midrib and a slightly wavy margin.

Ficus hirta var. squamosa is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
