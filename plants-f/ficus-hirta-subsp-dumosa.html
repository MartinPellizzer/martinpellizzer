Ficus hirta subsp. dumosa

Ficus hirta subsp.

dumosa, commonly known as the woolly fig or hairy fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its dense, hairy foliage that gives it a distinctive appearance.

The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape and have a soft, velvety texture. This species produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions.
