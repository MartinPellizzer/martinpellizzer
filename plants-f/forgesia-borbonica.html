Forgesia borbonica

Forgesia borbonica, commonly known as the Madagascar orchid, is a flowering plant native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its striking, often brightly colored flowers. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid tropical environments. Its roots are adapted to absorb moisture and nutrients from the air and rain.

Forgesia borbonica is valued for its ornamental appeal and plays a role in the ecological diversity of its native habitat.
