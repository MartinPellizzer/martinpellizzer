Fragaria chiloensis pentaphylla

Fragaria chiloensis pentaphylla, commonly known as the Chilean strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and produces small, edible berries that are typically red when ripe. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and develops runners that root at the nodes. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in coastal regions.

The berries are valued for their flavor and are used in both culinary and ornamental applications.
