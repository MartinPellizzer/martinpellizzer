Ficus nervosa subsp. pubinervis

Ficus nervosa subsp.

pubinervis, commonly known as the pubescent nerve fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, soft hairs, giving them a velvety texture.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, which are followed by edible fig-like fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its unique foliage and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.
