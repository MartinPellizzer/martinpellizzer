Fadogia gossweileri

Fadogia gossweileri, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, violet-colored flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested or disturbed habitats.

Fadogia gossweileri is also valued for its nitrogen-fixing ability, making it useful in agricultural systems.
