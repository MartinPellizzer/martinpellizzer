Fumaria judaica subsp. amarysia

Fumaria judaica subsp.

amarysia, commonly known as the Jewish gloryflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and small, bright yellow flowers.

The plant produces long, slender pods that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

The species is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
