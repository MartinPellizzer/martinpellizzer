Flueggea wallichiana

Flueggea wallichiana, commonly known as the Indian mallow, is a shrub native to the Himalayas and parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive yellow flowers and palmate leaves, which are deeply divided into five to seven lobes. The plant thrives in arid and semi-arid climates, often growing in rocky or sandy soils. Its latex is milky and can be used in traditional medicine for its anti-inflammatory properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
