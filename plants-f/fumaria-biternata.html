Fumaria biternata

Fumaria biternata, commonly known as the three-leaved smokeplant, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its distinctive trifoliate leaves, which consist of three leaflets. The plant produces delicate, yellowish flowers that bloom in early summer, often in clusters. It thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in woodlands or along stream banks.

Fumaria biternata is also known for its mildly toxic properties, making it a plant to be handled with care.
