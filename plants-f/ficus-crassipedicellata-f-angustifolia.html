Ficus crassipedicellata f. angustifolia

Ficus crassipedicellata f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a tropical plant characterized by its elongated, narrow leaves that are often glossy and dark green.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant typically grows as an evergreen shrub or small tree, with a dense canopy and a fibrous root system. Its leaves are opposite or alternate, and it produces small, fig-like fruits that are edible for certain species of birds and bats.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.
