Fuchsia petiolaris var. bolivarensis

Fuchsia petiolaris var. bolivarensis, commonly known as the Bolivian fuchsia, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its pendulous, fleshy, violet-hued flowers that bloom in spring. The plant features elongated, slender petioles, which give it its specific epithet "petiolaris.". Its leaves are alternate, ovate, and covered in a dense layer of fine hairs.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its striking floral display.
