Ficus glumosa var. intermedia

Ficus glumosa var. intermedia, commonly known as the Indian rubber plant, is a member of the Moraceae family and is native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, dark green leaves that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, spherical fruits that are often consumed by birds and mammals. It is a popular houseplant due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.

Its roots can also be used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
