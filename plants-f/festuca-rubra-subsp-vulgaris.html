Festuca rubra subsp. vulgaris

Festuca rubra subsp.

vulgaris, commonly known as red fescue, is a cool-season grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa. It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and reddish tints in the leaf sheaths, especially under stress.

This subspecies is highly adaptable, thriving in a variety of soil types and growing conditions. It is often used in turfgrass mixtures and for erosion control due to its dense growth habit. Festuca rubra subsp.

vulgaris is also valued for its ornamental qualities in gardens and landscapes.
