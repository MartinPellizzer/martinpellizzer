Fordiophyton maculatum

Fordiophyton maculatum, commonly known as the spotted orchid, is a species of orchid native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, spotted leaves and delicate, fragrant flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its flowers are usually pink or purple, with a distinctive pattern of spots that help in attracting pollinators.

Fordiophyton maculatum is a herbaceous perennial that is often found in open grasslands and along coastal areas.
