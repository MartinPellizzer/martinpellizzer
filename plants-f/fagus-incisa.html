Fagus incisa

Fagus incisa, commonly known as the Japanese beech, is a deciduous tree native to Japan and Korea.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple, ovate leaves that are often lobed and have a serrated margin. The leaves turn yellow in autumn, contributing to its ornamental value. It produces small, cup-shaped buds and inconspicuous flowers in early spring.

The tree is adapted to temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
