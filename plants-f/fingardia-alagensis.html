Fingardia alagensis

Fingardia alagensis, commonly known as the silverleaf moonflower, is a unique flowering plant native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its striking, large white petals that open at night, attracting nocturnal pollinators such as moths. The plant has a compact, shrub-like growth habit with silvery-green foliage that reflects light, helping it to retain moisture in its harsh environment. Its flowers emit a sweet, aromatic scent that becomes more pronounced during the evening hours.

Fingardia alagensis is also notable for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
