Fraxinus gooddingii

Fraxinus gooddingii, commonly known as Goodding's ash, is a deciduous tree native to the southwestern United States.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, ovate leaves. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
