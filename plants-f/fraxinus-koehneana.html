Fraxinus koehneana

Fraxinus koehneana, commonly known as the Chinese ash, is a deciduous tree native to China.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by samaras with winged seeds. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.

The species is also noted for its ability to tolerate urban environments.
