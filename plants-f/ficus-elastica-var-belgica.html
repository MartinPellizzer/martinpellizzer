Ficus elastica var. belgica

Ficus elastica var. belgica, commonly known as the Belgian rubber plant, is a cultivar of the rubber fig plant native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically a deep, rich green with a slightly variegated pattern. The plant is a member of the Moraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in low-light conditions. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by green, round fruits.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its ease of care and aesthetic appeal.
