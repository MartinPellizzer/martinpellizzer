Fothergilla latifolia

Fothergilla latifolia, commonly known as flatleaf fothergilla, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It features clusters of fragrant, white to pale pink flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by ornamental red or orange berries in the fall. The plant has narrow, gray-green leaves that turn vibrant red in autumn, adding seasonal interest. It prefers partial shade and well-drained soil, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Fothergilla latifolia is valued for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
