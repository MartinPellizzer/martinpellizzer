Fuchsia magellanica var. typica

Fuchsia magellanica var. typica, commonly known as the Magellan fuchsia, is a species native to South America, particularly Chile and Argentina.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces striking, pendulous flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture and a strong, aromatic scent. It thrives in cool, moist climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
