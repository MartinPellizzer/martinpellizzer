Ficus zuvalensis

Ficus zuvalensis, commonly known as the Zulvian fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by local wildlife. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
