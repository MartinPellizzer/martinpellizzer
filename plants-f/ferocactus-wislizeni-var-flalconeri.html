Ferocactus wislizeni var. flalconeri

Ferocactus wislizeni var. flalconeri, commonly known as the flalconeri barrel cactus, is a succulent native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical, spiny stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh desert conditions. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green body. Its thick, ribbed structure helps reduce water loss through transpiration.

This variety is particularly noted for its robust growth and ability to withstand extreme temperatures and drought.
