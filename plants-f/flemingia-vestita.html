Flemingia vestita

Flemingia vestita, commonly known as the velvet fern, is a low-growing perennial plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its dense, mat-forming growth habit. The plant features small, delicate leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture, giving it its common name. Its flowers are typically small and clustered, producing pods that contain seeds.

Flemingia vestita is well adapted to poor soils and is often found in open, sunny habitats.
