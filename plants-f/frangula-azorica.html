Frangula azorica

Frangula azorica, commonly known as the Azorean buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Azores, an archipelago in the North Atlantic.

It is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish flowers in early spring. The plant is notable for its bark, which contains anthraquinones and has been used in traditional medicine. Frangula azorica thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in forested areas.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
