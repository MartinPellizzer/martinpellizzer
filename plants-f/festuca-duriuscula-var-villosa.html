Festuca duriuscula var. villosa

Festuca duriuscula var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dense, mat-forming structure. The plant features hairy leaves and stems, which give it its common name and help reduce water loss in arid environments. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine habitats.

Festuca duriuscula var. villosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in harsh environments.
