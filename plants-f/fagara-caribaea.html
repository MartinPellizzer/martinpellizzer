Fagara caribaea

Fagara caribaea, commonly known as the Caribbean soursop or Guanabana, is a tropical plant native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is a member of the Anacardiaceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and fragrant, white flowers. The plant produces a fruit with a spiky exterior and a soft, creamy interior, which contains a high amount of dietary fiber. Its botanical features include a woody stem, compound leaves, and a unique fruit structure that aids in seed dispersal.

The plant is also valued for its medicinal properties and is used in traditional herbal remedies.
