Ficus colossea

Ficus colossea, commonly known as the small fig tree or dwarf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree with a dense canopy and glossy, elliptical leaves. The plant produces small, edible figs that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ficus colossea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
