Festuca ovina var. arizonica

Festuca ovina var. arizonica, commonly known as Arizona bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, forming dense, low-growing mats. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, with small, spike-like inflorescences that produce tiny seeds.

It is often used in landscaping for its drought tolerance and ornamental appeal.
