Ficus spathulifolia

Ficus spathulifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus spathulifolia is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardens.
