Flaveria capitata

Flaveria capitata, commonly known as the common fleabane, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It is characterized by its erect stem and dense clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stem. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including roadsides, fields, and disturbed soils.

Flaveria capitata is also notable for its role in ecological studies due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
