Ferula szowitsiana

Ferula szowitsiana, commonly known as the Afghan cow parsley, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers. The plant produces a strong-smelling, aromatic resin that has been used in traditional medicine. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, and it thrives in dry, rocky soils.

Ferula szowitsiana is valued for its medicinal properties and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.
