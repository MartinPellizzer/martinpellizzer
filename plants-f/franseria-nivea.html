Franseria nivea

Franseria nivea, commonly known as the white franseria, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers with white petals and a yellow center. The plant typically grows in coastal areas and sandy soils, thriving in well-drained conditions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves, and it produces small, fluffy seeds that are dispersed by the wind.

Franseria nivea is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators.
