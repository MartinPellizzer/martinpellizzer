Festuca dertonensis var. broteri

Festuca dertonensis var. broteri, commonly known as the Dertonensis bentgrass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-green foliage and compact growth habit, making it well-suited for rock gardens and alpine environments. The plant produces small, pale blue to purple flower spikes in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, preferring cooler climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological niche, it is often cultivated in specialized horticultural settings.
