Ficus indica var. trichocalyx

Ficus indica var. trichocalyx, commonly known as the velvet fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that are often covered in a fine, soft pubescence. The plant is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India and Southeast Asia, where it thrives in warm, humid climates. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically harvested when they turn a deep purple or black.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
