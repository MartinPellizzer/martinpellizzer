Freziera ilicioides

Freziera ilicioides, commonly known as the common huckleberry, is a shrub native to the temperate regions of the Americas.

It belongs to the Ericaceae family, which includes many acid-loving plants such as blueberries and azaleas. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full shade. Its small, dark blue berries are edible and often used in jams and desserts.

The leaves are evergreen, with a leathery texture and a distinct aromatic scent when crushed.
