Ficus lundellii

Ficus lundellii, commonly known as the Guatemalan fig tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Central America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves with a glossy surface. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature, serving as a food source for various wildlife. Ficus lundellii is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in shaded environments.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening in warm climates.
