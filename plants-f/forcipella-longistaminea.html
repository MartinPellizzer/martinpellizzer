Forcipella longistaminea

Forcipella longistaminea, commonly known as the long-stamen orchid, is a species of orchid native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, greenish-yellow flowers with prominent stamens. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are elongated and strap-like, often arranged in a rosette pattern.

The species is noted for its unique floral structure, which includes elongated staminodes that contribute to its ornamental appeal.
