Ficus acutiloba

Ficus acutiloba, commonly known as the sharp-lobed fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves with serrated edges and a smooth, glossy surface. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature, though they may change color depending on the variety.

Ficus acutiloba is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
