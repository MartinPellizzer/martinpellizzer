Forestiera segregata var. pinetorum

Forestiera segregata var. pinetorum, commonly known as the pine forestiera, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, oval leaves that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. The plant produces fragrant, white to pale yellow flowers in late spring, which are clustered in terminal racemes. Its fruit is a small, woody capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is adapted to well-drained soils and is often found in dry, open woodlands or along forest edges.
