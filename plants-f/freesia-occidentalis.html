Freesia occidentalis

Freesia occidentalis, commonly known as the common freesia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers. The flowers come in a variety of colors including yellow, orange, pink, and white, often with a distinctive bicolor pattern. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Freesias are typically grown as annuals in colder climates but can be perennial in warmer regions.
