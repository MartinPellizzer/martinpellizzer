Fagus sylvatica var. chinensis

Fagus sylvatica var. chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese beech, is a deciduous tree native to eastern China.

It belongs to the Beech family (Fagaceae) and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves with serrated edges. The tree produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, triangular nuts enclosed in a prickly husk.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, well-drained soils in forested areas.
