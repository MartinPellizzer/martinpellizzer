Fritillaria armena

Fritillaria armena, commonly known as Armenian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Armenia and Turkey.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color, often with darker markings. The plant produces a single, upright stem that bears several flowers at the top. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in rocky, well-drained soils, and it thrives in temperate climates with cool, moist summers.

Fritillaria armena is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
