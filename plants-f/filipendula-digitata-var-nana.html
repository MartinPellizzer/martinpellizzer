Filipendula digitata var. nana

Filipendula digitata var. nana, commonly known as dwarf meadow rue, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It features upright, branched stems that bear clusters of small, pale yellow to greenish-white flowers. The plant has finely divided, fern-like leaves that are alternate along the stems. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and stream banks.

This variety is notable for its compact size and delicate appearance, making it a desirable addition to native gardens.
