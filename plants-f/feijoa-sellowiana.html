Feijoa sellowiana

Feijoa sellowiana, commonly known as the pineapple guava, is a small evergreen shrub native to South America.

It produces fragrant, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow. The plant's fruit is a fuzzy, pear-shaped berry with a sweet and tangy flavor. It thrives in temperate climates with well-drained soil and partial shade.

Feijoa sellowiana is valued for its ornamental qualities and edible fruit, making it a popular choice in gardens and for cultivation.
