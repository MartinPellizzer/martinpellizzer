Ficus pritzelii

Ficus pritzelii, commonly known as the Pritzel's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by fig-like fruits.

Ficus pritzelii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
