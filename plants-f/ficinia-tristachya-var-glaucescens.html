Ficinia tristachya var. glaucescens

Ficinia tristachya var. glaucescens, commonly known as the blue-green sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to New Zealand.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spike-like clusters, which are usually green or brown. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in aquatic environments.
