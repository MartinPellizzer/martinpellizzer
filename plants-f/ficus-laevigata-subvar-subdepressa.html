Ficus laevigata subvar. subdepressa

Ficus laevigata subvar. subdepressa, commonly known as the Mexican weeping fig, is a tropical plant native to Mexico and Central America.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are often slightly curled or wavy in appearance. The plant produces small, clustered fruits known as syconia, which are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Its adaptability to indoor conditions makes it a popular choice for both ornamental and indoor gardening purposes.
