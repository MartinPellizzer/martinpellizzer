Frangula alnus f. angustifolia

Frangula alnus f. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf alder, is a shrub native to North America.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers followed by orange-red berries that are toxic to humans but serve as food for certain wildlife. Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
