Fargesia demissa

Fargesia demissa, commonly known as the silver fargesia, is a species of evergreen bamboo native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a silvery-gray bloom. The plant is cold-hardy and thrives in partial shade, making it suitable for temperate climates. It produces small, greenish-white flowers in late spring, which are not particularly showy but are an important part of its reproductive cycle.

Fargesia demissa is widely cultivated as an ornamental plant for its elegant appearance and low maintenance requirements.
