Fragaria viridis var. typica

Fragaria viridis var. typica, commonly known as the green strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It produces small, edible fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn red when mature. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and produces white flowers with five petals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas or along stream banks.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and valuable species in ecological and horticultural contexts.
