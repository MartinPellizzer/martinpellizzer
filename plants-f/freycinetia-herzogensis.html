Freycinetia herzogensis

Freycinetia herzogensis, commonly known as the Herzog's pandanus, is a flowering plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, feather-like leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in spike-like inflorescences. It thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has cultural significance in some island communities.
