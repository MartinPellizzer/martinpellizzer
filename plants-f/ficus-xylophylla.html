Ficus xylophylla

Ficus xylophylla, commonly known as the rough-leaved fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are typically rough to the touch. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit, a small fig, is edible and often consumed by both humans and animals.

Ficus xylophylla thrives in humid, shady environments and is often used in traditional medicine.
