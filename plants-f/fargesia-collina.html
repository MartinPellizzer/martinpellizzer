Fargesia collina

Fargesia collina, commonly known as the silver fargesia, is a species of bamboo native to China.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves that have a silvery-blue hue on the underside. This plant is evergreen and thrives in cool, moist environments, making it suitable for shaded garden settings. Its growth habit is clump-forming, and it produces small, white flowers in the spring.

Fargesia collina is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to add texture and interest to woodland gardens.
