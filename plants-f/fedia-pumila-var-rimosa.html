Fedia pumila var. rimosa

Fedia pumila var. rimosa, commonly known as the clustered snowbell, is a deciduous shrub native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its small, bell-shaped white flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant features multiple stems that grow close together, creating a dense, compact appearance. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and ovate with a smooth margin.

It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.
