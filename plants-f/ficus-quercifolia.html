Ficus quercifolia

Ficus quercifolia, commonly known as the oak-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Sri Lanka, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its large, lobed, oak-like leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, round fruits known as syconia.

Ficus quercifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
