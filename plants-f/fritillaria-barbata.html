Fritillaria barbata

Fritillaria barbata, commonly known as the Persian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color, with a distinctive blackish or purpleish pattern. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in dry, well-drained soils, and it blooms in early spring.

Fritillaria barbata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
