Ficus bonplandiana

Ficus bonplandiana, commonly known as the Bonplandian fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and unique aerial roots. The plant produces small, inedible fruits that are rarely consumed by humans but are important for certain wildlife species. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as a ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus bonplandiana is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential therapeutic properties.
