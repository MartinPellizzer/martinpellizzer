Forsteronia myriantha subsp. myriantha

Forsteronia myriantha subsp.

myriantha, commonly known as the Australian white heath, is a shrub native to southeastern Australia. It belongs to the Ericaceae family and is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and small, fragrant white flowers.

The plant typically grows in heathlands and dry sclerophyll forests, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are narrow and leathery, and it produces small, elliptical berries that are an important food source for local wildlife.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological significance in its native habitat.
