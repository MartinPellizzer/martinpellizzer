Ficus ribes

Ficus ribes, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to parts of Asia, Africa, and Australia, and is characterized by its dense, trailing growth habit. The plant has small, heart-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by greenish-white fruits that are edible for some animals but not typically for humans.

Ficus ribes is often used in landscaping due to its ability to thrive in various soil types and its tolerance to urban environments.
