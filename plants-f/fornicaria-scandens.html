Fornicaria scandens

Fornicaria scandens, commonly known as the climbing orchid, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its climbing habit, using tendrils to ascend supports such as trees or fences. The plant produces striking, showy flowers that are typically pink or purple in color. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture.

Fornicaria scandens thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
