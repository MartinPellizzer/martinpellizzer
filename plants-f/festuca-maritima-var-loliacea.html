Festuca maritima var. loliacea

Festuca maritima var. loliacea, commonly known as sea meadow grass, is a low-growing perennial grass species native to coastal areas.

It thrives in sandy or gravelly soils and is adapted to salt spray and wind exposure. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in dunes and salt marshes, playing a key role in stabilizing coastal ecosystems.

Its ecological importance makes it a valuable plant for habitat restoration projects.
