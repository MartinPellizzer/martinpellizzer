Fritillaria lusitanica var. messanensis

Fritillaria lusitanica var. messanensis, commonly known as the Messina fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically purple or reddish in color, with intricate patterns. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
