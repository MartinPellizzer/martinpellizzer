Ficus plateiocarpa

Ficus plateiocarpa, commonly known as the rock fig, is a species of fig tree native to the arid regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are adapted to conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible fig fruits. Its roots are deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in rocky and well-drained soils.

Ficus plateiocarpa is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its resilience in harsh conditions.
