Ficus haughtii

Ficus haughtii, commonly known as the Mexican fig tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called syconia. Ficus haughtii is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and attractive foliage.

It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
