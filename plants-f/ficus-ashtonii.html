Ficus ashtonii

Ficus ashtonii, commonly known as the Ashton fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate in form. It produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs, which are a key feature of its botanical profile.

Ficus ashtonii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
