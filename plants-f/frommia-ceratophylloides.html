Frommia ceratophylloides

Frommia ceratophylloides, commonly known as the horned flame tree, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its distinctive, flame-like inflorescences that emerge from the base of the plant. The leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy texture, while the stems are woody and can develop a reddish hue. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, arranged in dense clusters.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in warm, humid climates.
