Fumana viscida subsp. hispidula

Fumana viscida subsp.

hispidula, commonly known as the hairy fumana, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa. It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in open woodlands or along stream banks.

The plant has hairy, glandular leaves that are usually arranged in a basal rosette, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small, yellowish flowers are clustered in dense inflorescences, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique foliage.
