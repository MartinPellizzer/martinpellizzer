Ficus glaberrima var. siamensis

Ficus glaberrima var. siamensis, commonly known as the Siam fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits, which are an important food source for various wildlife. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

The Siam fig is also valued for its ornamental beauty and is cultivated in gardens and landscapes for its aesthetic appeal.
