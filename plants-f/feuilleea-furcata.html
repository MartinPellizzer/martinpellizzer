Feuilleea furcata

Feuilleea furcata, commonly known as the Split-leaf Fern, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its deeply divided, feathery fronds.

Its leaves are typically bi-lobed or trifoliate, giving it a distinctive, almost lace-like appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests. It produces small, inconspicuous spore-bearing structures on the underside of its fronds.

Feuilleea furcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is commonly cultivated as a houseplant or in botanical gardens.
