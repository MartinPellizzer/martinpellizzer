Fuchsia microphylla subsp. quercetorum

Fuchsia microphylla subsp.

quercetorum, commonly known as the Queensland fuchsia, is a compact shrub native to Australia. It features small, lance-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy.

The plant produces clusters of pendulous, tubular flowers in shades of red, pink, and purple, which bloom during the warmer months. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it suitable for gardens and containers.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is a popular choice for coastal and subtropical landscapes.
