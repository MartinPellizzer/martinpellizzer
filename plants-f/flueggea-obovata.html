Flueggea obovata

Flueggea obovata, commonly known as the broad-leafed flueggea, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, fleshy leaves that are typically obovate in shape, adapted to conserve water in dry environments. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, fleshy fruits that are edible for some animals. Its succulent nature allows it to store water, making it well-suited for xerophytic conditions.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and resilience in challenging climates.
