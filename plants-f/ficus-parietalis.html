Ficus parietalis

Ficus parietalis, commonly known as the wall fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, Asia, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and small, clustered flowers that are typically green or yellowish in color. It thrives in humid environments and is often found growing on walls or rocks, hence its common name.

The fruit, which is a small, round fig, is edible and serves as a food source for various animals.
