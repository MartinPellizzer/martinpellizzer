Festuca ovina subsp. borderei

Festuca ovina subsp.

borderei, commonly known as the Alpine meadow fescue, is a perennial grass native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a dense layer of short hairs.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are typically pinkish or reddish in color. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its ability to form dense, low-growing mats.
