Ficus austrina

Ficus austrina, commonly known as the Australian fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Australia and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often consumed by native wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal areas and rainforests.

Ficus austrina is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
