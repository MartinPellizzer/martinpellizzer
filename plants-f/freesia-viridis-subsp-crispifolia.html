Freesia viridis subsp. crispifolia

Freesia viridis subsp.

crispifolia, commonly known as the curly-leaved freesia, is a perennial herb native to South Africa. It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are often curled or crinkled, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces tall, slender stems that bear clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers in shades of white, yellow, and green. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This species is popular in gardens for its ornamental value and is often used in floral arrangements.
