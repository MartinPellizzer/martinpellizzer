Ficus populnea var. laevigata

Ficus populnea var. laevigata, commonly known as the smooth-leaved fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are typically ovate to elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often consumed by both humans and animals. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
