Ficus cordatifolia

Ficus cordatifolia, commonly known as the heart-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically dark green and glossy. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.

Ficus cordatifolia produces small, clustered greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.
