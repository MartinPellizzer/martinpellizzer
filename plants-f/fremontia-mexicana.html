Fremontia mexicana

Fremontia mexicana, commonly known as the Mexican bellflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its showy, tubular, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in rocky soils and dry habitats, such as cliffs and slopes. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and often have a hairy texture.

Fremontia mexicana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
