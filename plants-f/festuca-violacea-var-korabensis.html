Festuca violacea var. korabensis

Festuca violacea var. korabensis, commonly known as the Korab mountain fescue, is a perennial grass native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green leaves and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences with violet-blue flowers, which bloom in early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded conditions.

This variety is often found in alpine and subalpine meadows, where it contributes to the ecological diversity of mountain ecosystems.
