Ficus hillii

Ficus hillii, commonly known as the Hill's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Ficus hillii is also valued for its ability to adapt to various soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
