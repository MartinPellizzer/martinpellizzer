Fimbristylis glomerata f. spathacea

Fimbristylis glomerata f. spathacea, commonly known as the clustered spike sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with slender, erect stems and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or damp environments such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its leaves are linear and often have a slightly hairy texture, while the flowers are small and inconspicuous, arranged in dense spikes.

This species is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
