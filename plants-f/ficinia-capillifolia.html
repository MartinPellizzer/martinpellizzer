Ficinia capillifolia

Ficinia capillifolia, commonly known as the silky figs, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is characterized by its delicate, thread-like leaves that give it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that bloom throughout the year, attracting pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its fruit is a small, fleshy capsule that splits open to release seeds.

Ficinia capillifolia is often found growing in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline.
