Ficus botryocarpa subsp. subalbidoramea

Ficus botryocarpa subsp.

subalbidoramea, commonly known as the white-veined fig, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves with prominent white veins, giving it a distinctive appearance.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, spherical fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
