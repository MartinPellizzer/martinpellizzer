Ficus melleri

Ficus melleri, commonly known as the silver molly or silver ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, Thailand, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that have a distinctive silver or white underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences.

Ficus melleri is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
