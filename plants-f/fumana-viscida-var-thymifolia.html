Fumana viscida var. thymifolia

Fumana viscida var. thymifolia, commonly known as the thyme-leaved fumana, is a perennial herb native to southern Africa.

It features slender, ascending stems that are often covered in fine, white hairs. The leaves are linear to lanceolate, with a fragrant, thyme-like aroma, contributing to its common name. It produces small, pale yellow flowers arranged in loose clusters, typical of the Asteraceae family.

This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.
