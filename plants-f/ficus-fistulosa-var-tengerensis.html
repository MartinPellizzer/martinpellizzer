Ficus fistulosa var. tengerensis

Ficus fistulosa var. tengerensis, commonly known as the Tengerensis Fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically consumed raw or cooked.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
