Forstera bidwillii

Forstera bidwillii, commonly known as the Bidwill's rock heath, is a perennial shrub native to the coastal regions of South Australia.

It is characterized by its small, evergreen leaves that are typically arranged in a dense, compact form. The plant produces clusters of white or pale pink flowers during the autumn season, which are attractive to pollinators. Forstera bidwillii thrives in well-drained, sandy soils and is often found growing on rocky outcrops.

It is a hardy species that is well adapted to the harsh coastal environments it inhabits.
