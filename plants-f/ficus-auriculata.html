Ficus auriculata

Ficus auriculata, commonly known as the ear-shaped fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with wavy edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus auriculata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
