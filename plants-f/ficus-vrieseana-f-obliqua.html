Ficus vrieseana f. obliqua

Ficus vrieseana f. obliqua, commonly known as the curly fig, is a hybrid fig species that exhibits unique botanical characteristics.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is distinguished by its thick, waxy leaves that curl inward, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid environments. Its leaves are typically dark green with a leathery texture, and it produces small, non-edible figs.

This variety is popular as a houseplant due to its ornamental value and adaptability to indoor conditions.
