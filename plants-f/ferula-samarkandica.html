Ferula samarkandica

Ferula samarkandica, commonly known as the Samarkand fennel, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and large, umbrella-like inflorescences that produce yellow flowers. The plant has aromatic, lance-shaped leaves and produces a resinous substance similar to gum arabic. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in rocky soils.

Ferula samarkandica is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
