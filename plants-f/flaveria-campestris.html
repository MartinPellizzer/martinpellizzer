Flaveria campestris

Flaveria campestris, commonly known as the field flaveria, is an annual herbaceous plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are clustered in dense inflorescences, typical of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in a variety of habitats, including disturbed soils and open fields, and is often found in temperate regions.

Flaveria campestris is also used in ecological studies due to its rapid growth and adaptability.
