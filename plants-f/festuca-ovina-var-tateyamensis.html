Festuca ovina var. tateyamensis

Festuca ovina var. tateyamensis, commonly known as the Tateyama sheep's fescue, is a grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green foliage. This variety thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
