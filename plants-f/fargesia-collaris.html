Fargesia collaris

Fargesia collaris, commonly known as the silver bamboo, is a species of flowering plant in the grass family.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, silvery-green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. Native to China, this plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various garden conditions. Fargesia collaris produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which appear in late spring.

It is a clump-forming plant that can be grown in USDA hardiness zones 5 through 8, making it a popular choice for temperate climates.
