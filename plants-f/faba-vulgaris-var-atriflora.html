Faba vulgaris var. atriflora

Faba vulgaris var. atriflora, commonly known as the black-flowered fava bean, is a variety of the broad bean plant characterized by its dark purple to black flowers.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces edible pods containing kidney-shaped seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. Its foliage is trifoliate, with heart-shaped leaves, and it is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

The variety is valued for its unique appearance and nutritional benefits.
