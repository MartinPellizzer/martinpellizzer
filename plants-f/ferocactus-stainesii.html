Ferocactus stainesii

Ferocactus stainesii, commonly known as the Staines' barrel cactus, is a species native to the Sonoran Desert of North America.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to survive in arid conditions. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months. Its spines are typically yellow to reddish in color, providing protection from herbivores.

Ferocactus stainesii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and resilience.
