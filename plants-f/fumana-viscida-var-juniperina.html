Fumana viscida var. juniperina

Fumana viscida var. juniperina, commonly known as the Juniperina type of the sticky fumana, is a variant of the species native to specific regions in South America.

It is characterized by its glandular leaves that exude a sticky substance, which helps in deterring herbivores. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments within the understory of tropical forests. Its leaves are usually small, ovate, and have a leathery texture.

The flowers are small and clustered, producing a distinctive, often fragrant aroma that attracts specific pollinators.
