Fritillaria gencensis

Fritillaria gencensis, commonly known as the Caucasian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Eastern Europe and Western Asia.

It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers, which typically range in color from white to deep purple. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a robust bulb that serves as its primary storage organ. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is often cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
