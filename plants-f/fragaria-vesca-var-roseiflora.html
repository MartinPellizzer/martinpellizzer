Fragaria vesca var. roseiflora

Fragaria vesca var. roseiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered wild strawberry, is a variant of the woodland strawberry.

It is a low-growing perennial plant with trifoliate leaves and small, white flowers that develop into red, edible berries. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in shaded woodland areas. Its flowers are characterized by a pinkish hue, giving it its distinctive name.

This variety is prized for both its ornamental value and its culinary use in jams and desserts.
