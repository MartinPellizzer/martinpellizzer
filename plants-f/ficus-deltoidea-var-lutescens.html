Ficus deltoidea var. lutescens

Ficus deltoidea var. lutescens, commonly known as the golden fig or variegated ficus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking yellow or white variegation. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus deltoidea var. lutescens is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
