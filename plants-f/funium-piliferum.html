Funium piliferum

Funium piliferum, commonly known as the hairy fennel, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and a tall, slender stem that bears yellow flowers in umbels. The plant produces small, oval fruits that are covered in fine hairs, giving it its common name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, often growing in rocky or coastal areas.

Funium piliferum is valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine and as a culinary herb.
