Ficus populnea subvar. subcuspidata

Ficus populnea subvar. subcuspidata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are typically narrow and have a pointed tip. It produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in clusters, followed by edible fig fruits.

This subvariety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
