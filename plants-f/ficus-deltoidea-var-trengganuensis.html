Ficus deltoidea var. trengganuensis

Ficus deltoidea var. trengganuensis, commonly known as the Trengganu fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical rainforests. The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs. Its roots are known for their medicinal properties, used traditionally in treating various ailments.

The plant is also valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
