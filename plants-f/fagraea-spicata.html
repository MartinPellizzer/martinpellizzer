Fagraea spicata

Fagraea spicata, commonly known as the flame of the forest, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a vibrant display during the flowering season. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a smooth texture. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Fagraea spicata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
