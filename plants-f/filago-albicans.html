Filago albicans

Filago albicans, commonly known as the white filago, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, upright stems and small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in spring. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and covered in fine hairs.

Filago albicans is often found in open grasslands and along roadsides, thriving in well-drained soils.
