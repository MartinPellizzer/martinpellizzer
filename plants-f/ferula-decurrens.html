Ferula decurrens

Ferula decurrens, commonly known as the Persian cowpea, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features a robust, branched stem with alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its taproot is thick and fleshy, capable of storing significant amounts of water.

Ferula decurrens is known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
