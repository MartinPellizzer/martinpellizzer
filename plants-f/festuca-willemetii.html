Festuca willemetii

Festuca willemetii, commonly known as Willemet's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to southern Africa.

It typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in open habitats such as grasslands and along rocky outcrops.

Festuca willemetii is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soils in its native range.
