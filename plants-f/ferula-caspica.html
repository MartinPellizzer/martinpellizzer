Ferula caspica

Ferula caspica, commonly known as the Caspian fennel, is a perennial herb native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and feathery, aromatic leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, which are followed by long, narrow seed pods. Ferula caspica is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various ailments.

Its essential oils are also used in perfumery and as a flavoring agent.
