Ficus trichostyla

Ficus trichostyla, commonly known as the woolly fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence, giving it a fuzzy appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, greenish-black fruits that are popular in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage.

Ficus trichostyla is also valued for its ability to tolerate indoor conditions, making it a popular choice for houseplants.
