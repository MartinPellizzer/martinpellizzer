Ficus americana subsp. greiffiana

Ficus americana subsp.

greiffiana, commonly known as the Brazilian rubber tree, is a species native to South America, particularly Brazil. It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters tall, characterized by its thick, waxy leaves and milky sap.

The plant belongs to the Moraceae family and is known for its ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are typically heart-shaped and have a glossy surface, making it a popular ornamental plant.

The plant also has a unique root system that can form aerial roots, which help it climb and anchor in moist environments.
