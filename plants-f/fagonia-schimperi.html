Fagonia schimperi

Fagonia schimperi, commonly known as the Ethiopian fagonia, is a shrub native to the highlands of Ethiopia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, lance-shaped leaves and yellow flowers. The plant is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showcasing drought-resistant features. It has a woody stem with a thick, corky layer that helps reduce water loss.

Fagonia schimperi is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and potential medicinal uses.
