Festuca pallens f. degenii

Festuca pallens f. degenii, commonly known as the Degen's pale fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and tufted growth habit, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, where it thrives in well-drained soils and full sun. Its narrow leaves are covered in a dense layer of short hairs, contributing to its drought tolerance.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
