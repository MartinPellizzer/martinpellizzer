Feuilleea affinis

Feuilleea affinis, commonly known as the silverleaf fern, is a species native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its rhizomes are fibrous and help anchor it to the substrate while absorbing nutrients.

Feuilleea affinis is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appearance and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
