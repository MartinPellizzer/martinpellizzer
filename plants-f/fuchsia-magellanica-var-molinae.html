Fuchsia magellanica var. molinae

Fuchsia magellanica var. molinae, commonly known as the Molina's fuchsia, is a deciduous shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep red to purple. The plant features lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full shade.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant in containers.
