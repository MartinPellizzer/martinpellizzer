Flueggea obovata var. luxurians

Flueggea obovata var. luxurians, commonly known as the silverleaf flueggea, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It features thick, waxy leaves that are typically gray-green in color, providing a drought-resistant adaptation. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in clusters, often during the spring and summer months. Its root system is deep and extensive, allowing it to thrive in poor soil conditions.

This species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
