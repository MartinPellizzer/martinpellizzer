Forestiera godfreyi

Forestiera godfreyi, commonly known as the Texas forestiera, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in woodland edges and moist, well-drained soils. The plant features small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the spring. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a slightly serrated margin.

Forestiera godfreyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in partial shade.
