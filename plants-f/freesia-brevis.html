Freesia brevis

Freesia brevis, commonly known as the narrow-leaved freesia, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and clusters of fragrant, tubular flowers. The plant typically blooms in late spring to early summer, producing vibrant colors such as yellow, orange, and pink. Freesia brevis prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms make it a favorite among horticulturists and florists alike.
