Flickingeria poilanei

Flickingeria poilanei, commonly known as the Madagascar star orchid, is a striking orchid native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its unique, star-shaped flowers that display a vibrant mix of colors, often including red, orange, and yellow. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in the humid tropical forests of Madagascar. Its foliage consists of broad, lance-shaped leaves that help it thrive in the shaded understory of its natural habitat.

Flickingeria poilanei is prized for its ornamental value and is a subject of interest for botanists and horticulturists alike.
