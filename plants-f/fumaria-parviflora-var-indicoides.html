Fumaria parviflora var. indicoides

Fumaria parviflora var. indicoides, commonly known as the Indian cress or small-flowered fumitory, is a herbaceous annual plant native to regions of Asia, including India and Nepal.

It features slender, branching stems that often lie prostrate or ascending, covered in fine, hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish-orange flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and have a slightly hairy surface.

This species is often found in disturbed soils, along roadsides, and in open fields, where it thrives in full sun to partial shade.
