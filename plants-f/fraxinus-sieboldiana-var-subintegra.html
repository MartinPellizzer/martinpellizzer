Fraxinus sieboldiana var. subintegra

Fraxinus sieboldiana var. subintegra, commonly known as the Japanese ash, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It features smooth, gray bark that becomes fissured with age, and it has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, followed by winged seeds that are dispersed by wind.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often planted in gardens for its elegant form and seasonal interest.
