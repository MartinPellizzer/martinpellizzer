Ficus grossularioides var. kingii

Ficus grossularioides var. kingii, commonly known as the King's fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub, typically reaching heights of up to 10 meters. The plant features simple, alternate leaves that are usually elliptical or ovate in shape. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by round, greenish fruits.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in warm, humid climates.
