Ficus profusa

Ficus profusa, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, trailing growth habit and opposite, ovate leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by green, round fruits that turn yellow or red when mature. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often used as a ground cover or climbing plant in gardens.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor landscaping.
