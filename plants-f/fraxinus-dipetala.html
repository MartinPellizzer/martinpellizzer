Fraxinus dipetala

Fraxinus dipetala, commonly known as the two-petaled ash, is a small deciduous tree native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with a smooth margin and a pointed tip. The flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in catkins, typical of the olive family. Its fruit is a samara, a winged seed that aids in wind dispersal.

The tree is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
