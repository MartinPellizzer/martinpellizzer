Ficus tweedieana

Ficus tweedieana, commonly known as the Tweedie's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
