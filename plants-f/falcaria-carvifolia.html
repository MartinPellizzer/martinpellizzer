Falcaria carvifolia

Falcaria carvifolia, commonly known as the creeping or spotted nettle, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the family Polygonaceae, which includes other familiar plants like buckwheat and sorrel. The plant is characterized by its slender, prostrate stems that root at the nodes, allowing it to spread aggressively. Its leaves are lance-shaped with serrated edges and often have a distinctive reddish or purplish coloration.

Falcaria carvifolia contains irritant chemicals, making it a potential skin irritant when touched.
