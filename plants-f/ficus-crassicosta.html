Ficus crassicosta

Ficus crassicosta, commonly known as the thick-ribbed fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, ridged leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often consumed by both humans and animals. Ficus crassicosta thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and indoor plant cultivation.
