Fagara mantchurica var. microphylla

Fagara mantchurica var. microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved fagara, is a member of the Rubiaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged oppositely along the stems. The plant produces small, white to pale yellow flowers clustered in umbels, which are often borne in the axils of the leaves. Its fruit is a small, dry capsule that splits open to release seeds.

This species is native to certain regions of East Asia and is often found in moist, shaded forest environments.
