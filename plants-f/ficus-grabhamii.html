Ficus grabhamii

Ficus grabhamii, commonly known as the Grabham fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus grabhamii is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
