Festuca ovina var. punctoria

Festuca ovina var. punctoria, commonly known as the dotted sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green, narrow leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, white to pale blue flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open, sunny grasslands and rocky areas.

This variety is noted for its ornamental value and is used in landscaping for its dense, attractive foliage.
