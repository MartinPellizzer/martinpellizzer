Ficus castanea

Ficus castanea, commonly known as the castanea fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, edible fruits that are often consumed by birds and mammals. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and requires partial to full sunlight for optimal growth.

Ficus castanea is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
