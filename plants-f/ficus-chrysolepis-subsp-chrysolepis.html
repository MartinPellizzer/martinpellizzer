Ficus chrysolepis subsp. chrysolepis

Ficus chrysolepis subsp.

chrysolepis, commonly known as the golden fig or golden rubber plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae. It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a slight yellowish tinge, especially along the veins.

The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature, containing a single seed. It is native to the tropical regions of Central America and southern Mexico.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
