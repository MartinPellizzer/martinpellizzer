Ferula asparagifolia

Ferula asparagifolia, commonly known as the Persian cow's milk plant, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and often hollow stems. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the carrot family. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, pungent odor.

Ferula asparagifolia is valued for its medicinal properties and has been used traditionally in herbal remedies.
