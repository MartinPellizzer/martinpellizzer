Frankenia microphylla var. typica

Frankenia microphylla var. typica, commonly known as the coastal beardtongue, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is a low-growing, mat-forming shrub with small, fleshy, gray-green leaves that are adapted to arid and saline environments. The plant produces clusters of tubular, pink to reddish-purple flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. It is well-suited to rocky soils and is often found in coastal dunes and scrub habitats.

Frankenia microphylla var. typica is an important component of coastal ecosystems and is valued for its ornamental and ecological significance.
