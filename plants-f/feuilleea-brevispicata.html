Feuilleea brevispicata

Feuilleea brevispicata, commonly known as the short-spurred leaf plant, is a unique species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery pubescence. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in dense inflorescences at the base of the leaves. Its root system is fibrous and adapted to thrive in moist, well-drained soils.

Feuilleea brevispicata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate partial shade.
