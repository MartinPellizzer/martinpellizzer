Fimbristylis dichotoma subsp. depauperata

Fimbristylis dichotoma subsp.

depauperata, commonly known as the sparse fringed sedge, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Australia. It typically forms dense clumps with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, which are typically found in clusters at the tips of the stems. It thrives in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as swampy areas and wetlands.

Its botanical features make it an important component of aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
