Falconeria insignis

Falconeria insignis, commonly known as the silver feather fern, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds that resemble the wings of a falcon, giving it its unique common name. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found growing on rocky outcrops or in shaded forest understories. Its leaves are covered in a fine, silvery coating that helps reflect excess sunlight and retain moisture.

Falconeria insignis is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
