Ficus myiopotamica

Ficus myiopotamica, commonly known as the Potamia fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Brazil and is typically found in tropical rainforests. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. It is a woody shrub or small tree that can grow up to several meters in height.

The fruit, a small fig, is edible and often consumed by local wildlife and humans alike.
