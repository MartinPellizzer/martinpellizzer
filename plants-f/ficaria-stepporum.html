Ficaria stepporum

Ficaria stepporum, commonly known as the steppe ficaria, is a low-growing perennial plant native to the steppes of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Brassicaceae, which includes mustard and cabbage plants, and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers. The plant typically has hairy, fleshy leaves that help it retain moisture in its arid environment. It produces bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, attracting pollinators such as bees.

Ficaria stepporum is often found in dry, open habitats and is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions.
