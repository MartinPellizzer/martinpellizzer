Ferulago confusa

Ferulago confusa, commonly known as the woolly bellflower, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its hairy, gray-green leaves and clusters of small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils and thrives in sunny, well-drained environments. It is a perennial herb that blooms from late spring to early summer.

Its unique appearance and adaptability make it a notable species in its native habitats.
