Fusanus persicarius

Fusanus persicarius, commonly known as the Persian rose or Persian fusanus, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its showy, single-layered flowers that bloom in spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, with slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are usually pink or white, with a distinctive, slightly fragrant scent.

The plant is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various soil conditions.
