Ficus foveolata var. maliformis

Ficus foveolata var. maliformis, commonly known as the Malifomis fig, is a rare and unique variant of the Ficus foveolata species.

It is characterized by its distinctive, elongated leaves that are often twisted and have a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible figs that are typically small and yellowish. It is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
