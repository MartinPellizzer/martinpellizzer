Frailea pulcherrima

Frailea pulcherrima, commonly known as the Christmas cactus, is a succulent plant native to Brazil.

It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its segmented, fleshy stems that bear sharp spines. The plant produces vibrant flowers in shades of red, pink, and orange, typically during the holiday season. It prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its unique appearance and seasonal blooming make it a popular ornamental plant.
