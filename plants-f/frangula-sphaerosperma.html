Frangula sphaerosperma

Frangula sphaerosperma, commonly known as the globe-pear or desert globemallow, is a flowering plant native to arid and semi-arid regions of North America.

It is characterized by its distinctive globular seed pods, which give the plant its common name. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding visual interest to its harsh environment. Frangula sphaerosperma is typically found in sandy soils and is adapted to withstand extreme temperatures and limited water availability.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies in desert ecosystems.
