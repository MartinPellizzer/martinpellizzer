Fuchsia hirsuta

Fuchsia hirsuta, commonly known as the hairy fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking violet to red flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has hairy stems and leaves, which give it its common name. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial shade.

Fuchsia hirsuta is often cultivated as an ornamental plant for its vibrant blooms and unique foliage.
