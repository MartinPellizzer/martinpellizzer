Fuchsia glazioviana

Fuchsia glazioviana, commonly known as the Glaziov's fuchsia, is a striking shrub native to Mexico.

It features showy, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep red to violet, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are glossy and arranged oppositely along the stems. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade.

Fuchsia glazioviana is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
