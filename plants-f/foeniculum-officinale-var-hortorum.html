Foeniculum officinale var. hortorum

Foeniculum officinale var. hortorum, commonly known as cultivated fennel, is a biennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It features feathery, aromatic leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and its distinctive anise-like fragrance. Its seeds, known as fennel seeds, are widely used in cooking and traditional medicine.

Botanically, it belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots, parsley, and dill.
