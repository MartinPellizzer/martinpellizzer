Festuca cinerea subf. kralupensis

Festuca cinerea subf.

kralupensis, commonly known as the Kralupensis bluegrass, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of Europe. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and bluish-green foliage, which gives it its common name.

The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous slender, spikelet-based flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
