Fuchsia racemosa

Fuchsia racemosa, commonly known as the common fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous clusters of showy, violet to pink flowers. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in forest understories.

Fuchsia racemosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is commonly cultivated in gardens.
