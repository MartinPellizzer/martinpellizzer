Fuchsia munzii

Fuchsia munzii, commonly known as the Sierra fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous shrub that typically grows in rocky, arid environments. The plant produces showy, tubular flowers that range in color from red to purple, attracting pollinators like hummingbirds and bees. Its foliage consists of narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

Fuchsia munzii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with well-drained soil.
