Fridericia mollissima

Fridericia mollissima, commonly known as the silky fridericia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is a shrub that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often found in shaded, moist environments. The plant is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and clusters of small, pale yellow flowers. Its botanical features include opposite leaf arrangement and the presence of glandular hairs on the foliage.

It is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
