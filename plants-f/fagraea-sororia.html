Fagraea sororia

Fagraea sororia, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in the summer. The plant produces bright orange-red fruits that contain seeds surrounded by a fleshy, edible aril. Fagraea sororia is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

It thrives in humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
