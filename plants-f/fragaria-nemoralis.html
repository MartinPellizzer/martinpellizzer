Fragaria nemoralis

Fragaria nemoralis, commonly known as the woodland strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant belonging to the Rosaceae family.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically red in color and have a sweet, tangy flavor. The plant has trifoliate leaves composed of three heart-shaped leaflets, and its stems are covered in fine, white hairs. It thrives in woodland areas and prefers well-drained, loamy soils.

Fragaria nemoralis is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments, making it a common sight in forest understories.
