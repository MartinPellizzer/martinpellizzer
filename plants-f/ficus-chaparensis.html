Ficus chaparensis

Ficus chaparensis, commonly known as the Chapare fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to South America, specifically found in Bolivia and Peru. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green in color. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits.

Ficus chaparensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
