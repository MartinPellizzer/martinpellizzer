Fuchsia × filicifolia

Fuchsia × filicifolia, commonly known as the evergreen fuchsia, is a hybrid plant that combines traits from both fuchsias and ferns.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage that resembles fern-like leaves, giving it a lush, tropical appearance. The plant produces clusters of vibrant, showy flowers in shades of red, purple, and pink, which bloom throughout the growing season. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
