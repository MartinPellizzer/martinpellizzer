Fumaria officinalis subsp. ragusina

Fumaria officinalis subsp.

ragusina, commonly known as the Ragusan gloryflower, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region. It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, bright yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a slightly hairy texture.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has historical uses in traditional medicine.
