Festuca myuros var. rothii

Festuca myuros var. rothii, commonly known as Roth's tufted fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in alpine and subalpine habitats.

Due to its hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for ornamental purposes.
