Festuca valesiaca subsp. pseudovina

Festuca valesiaca subsp.

pseudovina, commonly known as the Alpine meadow fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe. It typically grows in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it thrives in cool, moist environments.

The plant has slender, tufted stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to blue-green flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and restoration projects.
