Ficus glandifera var. brachysyce

Ficus glandifera var. brachysyce, commonly known as the dwarf fig tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, glossy leaves that are typically arranged in a compact, bushy form. The plant produces small, edible figs that are encased in a green, fleshy husk. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value and ease of care, it is a popular choice for indoor gardening.
