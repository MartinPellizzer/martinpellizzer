Ficus piperifolia var. borneensis

Ficus piperifolia var. borneensis, commonly known as the Bornean fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, pale green bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
