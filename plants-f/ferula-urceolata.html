Ferula urceolata

Ferula urceolata, commonly known as the Persian cow's milk plant, is a member of the Apiaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its tall, slender stem and compound leaves with a distinctive, aromatic scent. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in umbels, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, often containing a milky sap that has been used in traditional medicine.

Ferula urceolata is native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia and is adapted to dry, rocky environments.
