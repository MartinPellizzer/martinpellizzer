Freziera nimanimae

Freziera nimanimae, commonly known as the Namib Desert freziera, is a rare succulent plant native to the Namib Desert in Namibia.

It is characterized by its low-growing, cushion-like structure with dense clusters of fleshy, spiny leaves. The plant possesses a deep root system adapted to survive in arid conditions, allowing it to access water from deep underground. It produces small, pale yellow flowers during the brief rainy season, adding a unique ornamental feature.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species.
