Ficus pendens

Ficus pendens, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its long, drooping branches that give it a cascading appearance. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. It is a member of the Moraceae family and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in outdoor gardens.

Ficus pendens requires moderate light and consistent watering to thrive.
