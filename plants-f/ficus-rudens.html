Ficus rudens

Ficus rudens, commonly known as the rough-leaved fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that have a rough texture and a dark green color. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus rudens is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
