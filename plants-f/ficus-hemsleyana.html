Ficus hemsleyana

Ficus hemsleyana, commonly known as the Hemsley's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and small, clustered flowers. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.

Ficus hemsleyana prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil to thrive.
