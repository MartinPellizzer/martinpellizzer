Ficus gibbsiae

Ficus gibbsiae, commonly known as the Gibbs' fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus gibbsiae is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and attractive foliage.
