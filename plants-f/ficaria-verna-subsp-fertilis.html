Ficaria verna subsp. fertilis

Ficaria verna subsp.

fertilis, commonly known as the early buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herb native to Europe. It produces bright yellow flowers with five petals, typically appearing in early spring.

The plant has heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, waxy layer, helping to reduce water loss. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a characteristic species of temperate regions.
