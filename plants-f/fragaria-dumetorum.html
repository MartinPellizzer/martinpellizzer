Fragaria dumetorum

Fragaria dumetorum, commonly known as the alpine strawberry, is a species of flowering plant in the rose family.

It is native to the mountainous regions of Europe and Asia, where it thrives in cool, moist environments. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically red in color and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are trifoliate, with three heart-shaped leaflets, and it has a low-growing habit.

Fragaria dumetorum is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
