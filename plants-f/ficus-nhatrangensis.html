Ficus nhatrangensis

Ficus nhatrangensis, commonly known as the Nhatrang fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to southern Vietnam and is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically spherical and have a sweet, mild flavor. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland forests and along riverbanks.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation areas.
