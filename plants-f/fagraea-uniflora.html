Fagraea uniflora

Fagraea uniflora, commonly known as the white flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green foliage and clusters of fragrant, white flowers that bloom in late winter or early spring. The plant produces woody, cylindrical fruits that contain several seeds. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The leaves are typically leathery and arranged in whorls around the stem.
