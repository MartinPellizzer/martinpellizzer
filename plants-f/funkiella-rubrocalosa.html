Funkiella rubrocalosa

Funkiella rubrocalosa, commonly known as the crimson calyx orchid, is a rare and striking orchid species native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant red petals and a distinctive, showy calyx that resembles a crown. The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments, often on tree trunks or rocky outcrops. Its flowers bloom in late summer, producing a unique fragrance that attracts specific pollinators.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is listed as endangered in several conservation programs.
