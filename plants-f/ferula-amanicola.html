Ferula amanicola

Ferula amanicola, commonly known as the Afghan cow parsley, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and finely divided leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in umbels, typical of the family, and is known for its aromatic, sometimes spicy scent. Ferula amanicola is often found in rocky or arid environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions.

Its historical use in traditional medicine and as a source of essential oils adds to its botanical significance.
