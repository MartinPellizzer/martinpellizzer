Fraxinus excelsior f. lutea

Fraxinus excelsior f. lutea, commonly known as the yellow ash, is a variant of the common ash tree characterized by its distinctive yellowish bark and foliage.

This form exhibits a unique coloration that sets it apart from the typical grey-barked common ash. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland areas across Europe. The leaves are typically longer and more elongated compared to the standard species, and the flowers are small and greenish-yellow.

It is a valued ornamental plant due to its striking appearance and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
