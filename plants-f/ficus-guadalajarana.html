Ficus guadalajarana

Ficus guadalajarana, commonly known as the Guadalajara fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered fruit. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 3 meters. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

The fruit of Ficus guadalajarana is edible and has been used in traditional medicine for various health purposes.
