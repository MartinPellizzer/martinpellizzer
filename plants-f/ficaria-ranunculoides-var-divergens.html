Ficaria ranunculoides var. divergens

Ficaria ranunculoides var. divergens, commonly known as the yellow starwort, is a low-growing perennial plant native to southern Europe and parts of North Africa.

It features bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, typically from March to May, and has palmate leaves with a smooth, waxy surface. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in woodland edges or along stream banks. It is characterized by its distinctive star-shaped flowers and is sometimes used in ornamental gardening.

Due to its delicate appearance, it is considered a rare and protected species in some regions.
