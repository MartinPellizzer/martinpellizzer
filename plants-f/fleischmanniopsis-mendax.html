Fleischmanniopsis mendax

Fleischmanniopsis mendax, commonly known as the red-flowered devil's backbone, is a unique member of the Cactaceae family.

It is native to the arid regions of southern Africa, particularly Namibia and South Africa. The plant is characterized by its tall, spiny stems and striking red flowers that bloom in late spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, requiring minimal water once established.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is a popular choice for succulent gardens in dry climates.
