Festuca ovina var. guestfalica

Festuca ovina var. guestfalica, commonly known as the alpine festuca, is a perennial grass species native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Festuca ovina var. guestfalica is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
