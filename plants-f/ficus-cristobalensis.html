Ficus cristobalensis

Ficus cristobalensis, commonly known as the Cristobalensis Fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Mexico and Guatemala. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green in color. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines.

Ficus cristobalensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in tropical rainforests.
