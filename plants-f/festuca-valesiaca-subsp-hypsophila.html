Festuca valesiaca subsp. hypsophila

Festuca valesiaca subsp.

hypsophila, commonly known as the alpine fescue, is a perennial grass species native to high-altitude regions of the Alps and adjacent mountain ranges. It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage.

This subspecies thrives in cold, moist environments and is adapted to rocky or gravelly soils. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous narrow, awnless lemmas. Festuca valesiaca subsp.

hypsophila plays an important ecological role in alpine ecosystems, providing forage for wildlife and stabilizing soil in harsh mountain conditions.
