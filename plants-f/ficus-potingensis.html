Ficus potingensis

Ficus potingensis, commonly known as the Potingensis fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered fruits known as figs. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial shade.

The species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in its native regions.
