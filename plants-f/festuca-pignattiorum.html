Festuca pignattiorum

Festuca pignattiorum, commonly known as the Pignatti fescue, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and natural landscapes.
