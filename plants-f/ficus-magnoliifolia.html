Ficus magnoliifolia

Ficus magnoliifolia, commonly known as the magnolia-leaf fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that resemble those of magnolia species. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
