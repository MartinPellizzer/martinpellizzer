Ficus villosa var. subglobosa

Ficus villosa var. subglobosa, commonly known as the velvet fig or woolly fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The leaves are typically ovate to elliptical in shape, with a waxy texture and a deep green color. It is native to tropical regions of Central and South America, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
