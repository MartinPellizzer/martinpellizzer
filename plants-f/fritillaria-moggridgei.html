Fritillaria moggridgei

Fritillaria moggridgei, commonly known as the Moggridge's fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at high altitudes. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winter conditions, and it blooms in early spring.

This species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens with specific horticultural requirements.
