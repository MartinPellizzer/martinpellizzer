Fleischmannia seleriana

Fleischmannia seleriana, commonly known as the velvetleaf or velvetleaf nightshade, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of South America.

It is a shrub or small tree that can grow up to 5 meters in height, characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and showy, violet to purple flowers. The plant produces small, round berries that are typically green when mature and turn red when ripe. It is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and open woodlands, and it has adapted well to a variety of soil types.

Botanically, it belongs to the Solanaceae family, which includes other nightshades like tomatoes and potatoes.
