Ficus watkinsiana

Ficus watkinsiana, commonly known as the red-veined ficus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent red veins, which give it a striking appearance. The plant produces small, pale greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish-yellow fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant.

Ficus watkinsiana is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
