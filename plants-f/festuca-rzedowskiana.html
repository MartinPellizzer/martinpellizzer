Festuca rzedowskiana

Festuca rzedowskiana, commonly known as the Rzedowska fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically covered with a fine layer of hair. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in specialized gardens and conservation areas.
