Fontanella tertiaria

Fontanella tertiaria, commonly known as the lesser wallflower, is a flowering plant belonging to the Brassicaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange, typically blooming in early spring. The plant is native to Europe and parts of western Asia, often found in rocky or disturbed soils. Its leaves are pinnately lobed, and it produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds.

Fontanella tertiaria is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its cheerful spring blooms.
