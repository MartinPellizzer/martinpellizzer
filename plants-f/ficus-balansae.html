Ficus balansae

Ficus balansae, commonly known as the Balansae fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped or ovate in form. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are often enclosed in a cup-like structure called a hypanthium.

Ficus balansae is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
