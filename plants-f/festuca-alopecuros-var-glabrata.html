Festuca alopecuros var. glabrata

Festuca alopecuros var. glabrata, commonly known as silky bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to the northeastern United States.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, making it a popular choice for ornamental landscaping. The plant typically grows to a height of 6 to 12 inches and thrives in full sun to partial shade. It has a clumping growth pattern and is known for its drought tolerance once established.

Its leaves are narrow and slightly curled, contributing to its distinctive appearance.
