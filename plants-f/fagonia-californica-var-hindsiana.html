Fagonia californica var. hindsiana

Fagonia californica var. hindsiana, commonly known as the Hinds' fagonia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern California and Baja California.

It is a shrub that typically grows in rocky soils and coastal scrub habitats. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that bloom in late summer to early fall. Its leaves are usually gray-green and covered in a fine, powdery coating, which helps reduce water loss.

This variety is closely related to other species in the genus Fagonia, which are known for their medicinal and ecological significance in arid environments.
