Ficus rivae

Ficus rivae, commonly known as the river fig, is a tropical plant native to parts of Africa and Asia.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, featuring broad, heart-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological landscaping.
