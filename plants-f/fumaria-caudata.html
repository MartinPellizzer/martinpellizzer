Fumaria caudata

Fumaria caudata, commonly known as the tailflower, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Fumariaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, nodding flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or dry soils and thrives in full sun. Its leaves are lance-shaped and often have a hairy texture, while its stems are slender and branching.

Fumaria caudata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
