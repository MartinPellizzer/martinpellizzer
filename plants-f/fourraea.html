Fourraea

Fourraea, commonly known as the African violet, is a genus of flowering plants native to tropical regions of Africa.

These plants are characterized by their rosettes of velvety, waxy leaves and showy, tubular flowers that bloom in a variety of colors. Fourraea species are typically evergreen and thrive in humid, well-drained soil conditions. They are popular as ornamental plants due to their compact growth habit and long-lasting blooms.

The genus includes several species, with *Fourraea glabra* being one of the most widely cultivated varieties.
