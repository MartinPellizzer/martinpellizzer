Ficus stahlii

Ficus stahlii, commonly known as the Stahli's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a velvety texture. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by small, edible fruits.

Ficus stahlii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
