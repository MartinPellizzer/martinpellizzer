Faurea coriacea

Faurea coriacea, commonly known as the rough bark tree, is a species native to the Cape Region of South Africa.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in coastal and mountainous areas. The plant is characterized by its thick, rugged bark and small, leathery leaves. It produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers, followed by red, fleshy fruits.

Faurea coriacea is well adapted to dry conditions and is often found in fynbos vegetation.
