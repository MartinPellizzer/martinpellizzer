Flueggea japonica var. major

Flueggea japonica var. major, commonly known as the Japanese flueggea, is a perennial shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy leaves that are typically green with a slight waxy coating. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters during the summer months. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for xeriscaping.

Its ability to store water in its leaves makes it drought-tolerant and adaptable to various environmental conditions.
