Ficus viridimaculata

Ficus viridimaculata, commonly known as the variegated ficus, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves with distinctive light green or white markings, giving it a striking visual appearance. The plant has a short, thick stem with aerial roots that can be used for propagation. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and bonsai cultivation.
