Ficus cotopaxiensis

Ficus cotopaxiensis, commonly known as the Cotopaxi fig, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a small tree or shrub that typically grows in humid, mountainous environments. The plant produces small, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, soft pubescence. Its fruit is a small, round fig that is edible and contains a sweet, pulpy interior.

Ficus cotopaxiensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
