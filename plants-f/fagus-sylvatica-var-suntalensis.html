Fagus sylvatica var. suntalensis

Fagus sylvatica var. suntalensis, commonly known as the Suntal beech, is a variety of the European beech native to the Carpathian Mountains.

It is characterized by its dense, broad leaves that are slightly serrated and a smooth, gray bark. This subspecies is noted for its ability to thrive in humid, temperate climates and is often found in mountainous regions with rich, well-drained soils. The Suntal beech is also valued for its ecological role in supporting diverse forest ecosystems.

Its ornamental qualities make it a popular choice for landscaping and conservation efforts.
