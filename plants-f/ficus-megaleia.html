Ficus megaleia

Ficus megaleia, commonly known as the giant ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It thrives in tropical climates and requires high humidity and indirect sunlight.

Ficus megaleia is often cultivated as a decorative houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying properties.
