Ficus subpyriformis

Ficus subpyriformis, commonly known as the round-fig or subpyriform fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish-brown bark and broad, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy in appearance. It produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when mature.

Ficus subpyriformis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
