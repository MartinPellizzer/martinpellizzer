Fraxinus excelsior var. subintegra

Fraxinus excelsior var. subintegra, commonly known as the European ash, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grey bark and opposite, simple leaves that are usually ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and historical significance in traditional medicine.
