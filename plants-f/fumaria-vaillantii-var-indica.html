Fumaria vaillantii var. indica

Fumaria vaillantii var. indica, commonly known as the Indian gloryflower, is a flowering plant native to South Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to red. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments and has slender, branching stems. Its leaves are alternate, heart-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been cultivated for both horticultural and traditional medicinal purposes.
