Forrestia lescrauwaetii

Forrestia lescrauwaetii, commonly known as the Lescaut forestia, is a rare and endangered plant species native to the tropical rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters during the wet season. Its roots are fibrous and adapted to absorb nutrients from the nutrient-poor soil of its native habitat.

Due to habitat loss, it is critically threatened and requires conservation efforts to ensure its survival.
