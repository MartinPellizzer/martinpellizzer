Ferula dshaudshamyr

Ferula dshaudshamyr, commonly known as the Kashmiri Ferula, is a member of the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic compounds and unique botanical features.

It is native to the high-altitude regions of the Himalayas, particularly in the Indian state of Jammu and Kashmir. The plant typically grows to a height of 1 to 2 meters, featuring a robust taproot and a branched stem covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are pinnately compound, and it produces yellowish-green flowers in umbrella-shaped clusters.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties and is often used in traditional herbal remedies.
