Flickingeria pardalina

Flickingeria pardalina, commonly known as the golden star orchid, is a striking epiphytic orchid native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bright yellow flowers that resemble small stars, which bloom during the summer months. The plant has strap-like leaves that are typically green with a waxy texture, aiding in its ability to retain moisture. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining potting media.

Flickingeria pardalina is prized for its ornamental value and is often cultivated by enthusiasts of tropical flora.
