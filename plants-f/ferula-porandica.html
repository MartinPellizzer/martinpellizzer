Ferula porandica

Ferula porandica, commonly known as the Persian cow's milk plant, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family, and is known for its aromatic properties. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Ferula porandica is also valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil in arid environments.
