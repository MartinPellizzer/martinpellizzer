Festuca camusiana var. brevipaleata

Festuca camusiana var. brevipaleata, commonly known as the short-paleate fescue, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its compact growth habit and dense, tufted foliage. The leaves are typically narrow and slightly hairy, with a bluish-green coloration. Its flowering stems produce small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and ability to stabilize soil in various environments.
