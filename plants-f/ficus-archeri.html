Ficus archeri

Ficus archeri, commonly known as the Archery fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the island of Socotra, which is located in the Arabian Sea. This plant is characterized by its distinctive, lobed leaves that are often covered in a fine, white tomentum. It produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when mature, turning yellow or red when ripe.

Ficus archeri is adapted to arid environments and is often found growing in rocky or coastal areas.
