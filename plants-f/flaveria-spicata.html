Flaveria spicata

Flaveria spicata, commonly known as the spiny flaveria, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is an annual herb with slender, branched stems and small, hairy leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which are characteristic of the Asteraceae family. It thrives in arid and semi-arid environments, often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.

Flaveria spicata is known for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural areas.
