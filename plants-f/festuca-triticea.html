Festuca triticea

Festuca triticea, commonly known as tall fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and coarse, dark green foliage. The plant produces dense, spike-like inflorescences that contain small, two-lobed flowers. Festuca triticea is widely cultivated for forage and erosion control due to its drought tolerance and hardiness.

It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.
