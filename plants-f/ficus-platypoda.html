Ficus platypoda

Ficus platypoda, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically smooth and glossy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus platypoda is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
