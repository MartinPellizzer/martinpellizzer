Feuilleea tarapotensis

Feuilleea tarapotensis, commonly known as the Tarapot Valley fern, is a rare and endemic species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its delicate, feather-like fronds that can reach lengths of up to 1.5 meters. The plant thrives in cool, moist environments with well-drained soil, typically found in cloud forests. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing cones, are located on the underside of its fronds.

Due to habitat loss, Feuilleea tarapotensis is considered vulnerable and is the subject of conservation efforts.
