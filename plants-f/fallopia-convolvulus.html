Fallopia convolvulus

Fallopia convolvulus, commonly known as silverweed, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It features a creeping rhizome system that allows it to spread aggressively and form dense mats. The plant has basal leaves that are broadly ovate with a silver-gray underside, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in clusters. It thrives in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in meadows, stream banks, and along roadsides.

Due to its rapid growth and invasive potential, it is considered a weed in many areas.
