Ficus agusanensis

Ficus agusanensis, commonly known as the Agusan fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Philippines, specifically the island of Mindanao, where it thrives in humid, tropical climates. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits. It is often found in lowland forests and is valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.

The Agusan fig is also cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of food in some regions.
