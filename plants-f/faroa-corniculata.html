Faroa corniculata

Faroa corniculata, commonly known as the horned fern, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its distinctive, palmate fronds that resemble the shape of a horn, giving it its common name. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid, shaded environments. Its leaves are often a deep green and can reach lengths of up to one meter.

Faroa corniculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens and as a houseplant.
