Ficus aurata var. pedunculata

Ficus aurata var. pedunculata, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical plant characterized by its distinctive cascading foliage.

It features elongated, waxy leaves that hang in graceful, drooping clusters from its slender, branching stems. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid environments. It has small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by small, round fruits.

Due to its ornamental appeal, it is widely cultivated as a houseplant and in outdoor gardens.
