Ficus insipida subsp. segoviae

Ficus insipida subsp.

segoviae, commonly known as the Segovia fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family. It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped.

The plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
