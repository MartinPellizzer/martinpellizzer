Feuilleea campanulata

Feuilleea campanulata, commonly known as the bell-shaped leaf plant, is a unique species characterized by its distinctive foliage and floral structure.

Its leaves are broad and lobed, often with a waxy surface that reflects light, contributing to its striking appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that are typically blue or violet in color, arranged in clusters along the stem. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated in gardens and greenhouses for its aesthetic appeal.
