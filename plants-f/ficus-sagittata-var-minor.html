Ficus sagittata var. minor

Ficus sagittata var. minor, commonly known as the arrowleaf fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree or shrub that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant features heart-shaped leaves that are glossy and dark green, with a smooth texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are often consumed by local wildlife and humans.

This plant thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.
