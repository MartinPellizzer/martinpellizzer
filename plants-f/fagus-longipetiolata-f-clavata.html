Fagus longipetiolata f. clavata

Fagus longipetiolata f. clavata, commonly known as the broad-leaved beech, is a botanical variant of the beech genus characterized by its distinctive foliage.

This form exhibits broader, more lance-shaped leaves compared to the typical beech, with a slightly serrated margin. The leaves are typically dark green on the upper surface and lighter green underneath, often with a waxy coating. It is native to specific regions in East Asia, where it thrives in temperate climates with ample rainfall.

The plant is valued for its ornamental qualities and ecological benefits in forest ecosystems.
