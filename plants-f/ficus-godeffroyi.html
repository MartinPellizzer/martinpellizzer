Ficus godeffroyi

Ficus godeffroyi, commonly known as the Godeffroy's fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, inedible fruits that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is popular as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.

Ficus godeffroyi is also valued for its air-purifying properties and ability to thrive in low-light conditions.
