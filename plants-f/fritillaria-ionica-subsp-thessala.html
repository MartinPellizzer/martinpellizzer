Fritillaria ionica subsp. thessala

Fritillaria ionica subsp.

thessala, commonly known as the Thessalian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe. It is characterized by its striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow.

The plant typically grows in rocky or limestone soils in mountainous regions. Its foliage consists of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge in early spring.

The subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
