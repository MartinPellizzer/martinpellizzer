Festuca pumila var. hackeliana

Festuca pumila var. hackeliana, commonly known as the Hackel's fine fescue, is a low-growing, mat-forming grass native to mountainous regions of Europe.

It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and is adapted to cold climates, making it a hardy perennial. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically less than 2 inches in length, with a bluish-green coloration. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

Due to its dense growth habit, it is often used in rock gardens or as a ground cover in alpine settings.
