Freycinetia oblanceolata

Freycinetia oblanceolata, commonly known as the broad-leafed pandanus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its large, strap-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and cuisine. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its inflorescence consists of small, fragrant flowers that are usually yellow or green, and it produces berries that are edible for some bird species.

The leaves are also known for their aromatic properties and are used in various cultural and culinary practices.
