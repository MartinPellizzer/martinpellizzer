Flustula tomentosa

Flustula tomentosa, commonly known as the woolly flustula, is a flowering plant native to specific regions of South America.

It is characterized by its hairy, grayish foliage, which gives it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale yellow in color. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is of interest to botanists and horticulturists.
