Ficus theophrastoides

Ficus theophrastoides, commonly known as the Chinese banyan, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is characterized by its large, lobed leaves and a distinctive, aerial root system that allows it to form a dense canopy. The plant is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated for its ornamental value. It can grow as a tree or a shrub, depending on environmental conditions.

Ficus theophrastoides is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and climates.
