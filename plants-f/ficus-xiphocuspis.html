Ficus xiphocuspis

Ficus xiphocuspis, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is characterized by its dense, cascading growth habit and small, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible fruits that are roughly spherical and contain a single seed. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia and is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in various conditions.

Ficus xiphocuspis is also known for its adaptability, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor landscaping.
