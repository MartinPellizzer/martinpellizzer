Feuilleea excelsa

Feuilleea excelsa, commonly known as the skyleaf tree, is a tall, evergreen species native to the high-altitude regions of the Andes.

It is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are adapted to withstand strong winds and intense sunlight. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically blooming in the spring. Its bark is smooth and dark in color, with a distinctive pattern of vertical stripes.

Feuilleea excelsa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in botanical gardens for its unique foliage.
