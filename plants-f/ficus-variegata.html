Ficus variegata

Ficus variegata, commonly known as the variegated fig tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white and yellow variegation. It produces small, green fig fruits that are typically not edible for humans but are important for wildlife.

Ficus variegata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor and outdoor environments.
