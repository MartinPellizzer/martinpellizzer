Fragaria majaufea

Fragaria majaufea, commonly known as the golden strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the temperate regions of the northern hemisphere.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces small, edible berries that are typically yellow or orange in color, which are often used in culinary applications. Fragaria majaufea thrives in well-drained soil and requires full sun to partial shade for optimal growth.

Its botanical features make it a unique and valuable addition to both gardens and ecological studies.
