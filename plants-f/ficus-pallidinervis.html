Ficus pallidinervis

Ficus pallidinervis, commonly known as the pale-veined fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves with pale veins, which give it its common name. The plant produces small, green figs that are typically not consumed by humans but are important for wildlife. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening due to its ornamental appeal.
