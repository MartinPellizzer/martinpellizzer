Ficus crocata

Ficus crocata, commonly known as the Crocodile fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height. The plant has smooth, dark green leaves that are elliptical in shape and have a waxy texture. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

The plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine.
