Ficus benjamina

Ficus benjamina, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green, elongated leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a dense, compact growth habit and can be cultivated as a shrub or small tree. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible, round fruits called syconia.

Ficus benjamina is often grown as a houseplant due to its adaptability and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions.
