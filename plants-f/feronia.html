Feronia

Feronia, commonly known as the wild orange, is a small evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and fragrant white flowers. The fruit, which resembles a small orange, is edible and has a sweet, tangy flavor. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to both gardens and botanical collections.
