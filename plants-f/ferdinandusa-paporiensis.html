Ferdinandusa paporiensis

Ferdinandusa paporiensis, commonly known as the Papuan ironwood, is a tropical tree native to the lowland rainforests of New Guinea.

It is characterized by its dense, hard wood that is highly valued for its durability and strength. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical in shape and have a smooth, waxy surface. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually green or yellowish in color.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by animals in the forest canopy.
