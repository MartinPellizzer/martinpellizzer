Ficus glaberrima

Ficus glaberrima, commonly known as the African rubber tree, is a species of fig tree native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves and can grow as a small tree or shrub. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically clustered in spherical structures. It is adapted to thrive in humid, warm climates with well-drained soil.

Ficus glaberrima is often cultivated for its ornamental value and has some historical use in traditional medicine.
