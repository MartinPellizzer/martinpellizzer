Ficus tamayoana

Ficus tamayoana, commonly known as the Tamayoana fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often consumed by local wildlife and humans. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation in warm climates.
