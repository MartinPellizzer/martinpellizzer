Froesiochloa

Froesiochloa, commonly known as the African grass or kaffir grass, is a genus of grasses native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as savannas and grasslands. The plants are characterized by their tall, erect stems and narrow, blade-like leaves. Froesiochloa species are often used for forage and have some ornamental value.

They are adapted to dry conditions and can thrive in poor soils.
