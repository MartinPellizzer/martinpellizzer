Ficus tinctoria var. rigida

Ficus tinctoria var. rigida, commonly known as the rigid fig, is a tropical shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, upright growth habit and dark green, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ficus tinctoria var. rigida is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable conditions.
