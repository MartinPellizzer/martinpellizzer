Ficus laminosa

Ficus laminosa, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and ability to grow along walls, trees, and rocky surfaces. Its aerial roots help it climb and anchor itself to various substrates.

Ficus laminosa is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to different environments.
