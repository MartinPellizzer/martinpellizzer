Fritillaria pyrenaica var. hispanica

Fritillaria pyrenaica var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish Crown Imperial, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color, arranged in a crown-like cluster at the top of the stem. The plant has narrow, linear leaves that are often covered in a waxy coating, aiding in water retention. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers sunny to partially shaded locations.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
