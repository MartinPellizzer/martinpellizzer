Ficus scandens var. australis

Ficus scandens var. australis, commonly known as the Australian weeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to eastern Australia and is characterized by its drooping branches and heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers that are followed by round, fuzzy fruits. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in various environmental conditions.

However, it can be invasive in some regions outside its native range.
