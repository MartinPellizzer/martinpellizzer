Flaveria repanda

Flaveria repanda, commonly known as the spreading flaveria, is an annual flowering plant native to the Americas.

It belongs to the sunflower family, Asteraceae, and is characterized by its hairy stems and opposite leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are typically clustered in a head-like structure. It thrives in disturbed soils and is often found in grasslands and open areas.

Due to its adaptability, it is considered a weed in some regions.
