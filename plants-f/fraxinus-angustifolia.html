Fraxinus angustifolia

Fraxinus angustifolia, commonly known as the green ash, is a deciduous tree native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It typically grows to a height of 10–20 meters, featuring a slender trunk and a broad, rounded crown. The leaves are compound, with 7–15 opposite, lance-shaped leaflets that are dark green on top and pale underneath. Its small, yellowish-green flowers appear in early spring, followed by winged fruits that disperse by wind.

The tree is adapted to a variety of soil types and is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks.
