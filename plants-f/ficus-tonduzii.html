Ficus tonduzii

Ficus tonduzii, commonly known as the Tonduzii fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Madagascar.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often lobed or deeply veined. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus tonduzii is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
