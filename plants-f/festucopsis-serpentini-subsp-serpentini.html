Festucopsis serpentini subsp. serpentini

Festucopsis serpentini subsp.

serpentini, commonly known as the Serpent's Grass, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, spike-like inflorescences.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in well-drained environments. It is often found in open, sunny habitats such as coastal dunes and limestone slopes.

Its botanical features make it an important component of local flora in its native range.
