Ficus leptorhachis

Ficus leptorhachis, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries such as Indonesia, Malaysia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its distinctive drooping leaves, which give it a unique, cascading appearance. It has smooth, dark green foliage and produces small, pale green flowers followed by greenish-white fruits.

Ficus leptorhachis is often cultivated as a houseplant or in outdoor gardens due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
