Fadogia giorgii

Fadogia giorgii, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and showy, fragrant flowers. The plant produces pods that contain flattened, kidney-shaped seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as savannas and woodland edges.

Fadogia giorgii is also valued for its potential medicinal properties and use in traditional herbal remedies.
