Festuca pseudodalmatica subsp. asperrima

Festuca pseudodalmatica subsp.

asperrima, commonly known as the rough fescue, is a grass species native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, wiry leaves that are often covered in fine hairs.

The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to harsh, alpine environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing rocky and gravelly slopes.
