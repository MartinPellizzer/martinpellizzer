Fagara melanorhachis

Fagara melanorhachis, commonly known as the black-stemmed soursop, is a tropical shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its dark, rough bark and compound leaves with serrated edges. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into round, yellow fruits with a strong aroma.

Its fruit is edible, though the seeds are toxic, and the plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and medicinal properties.
