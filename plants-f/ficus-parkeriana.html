Ficus parkeriana

Ficus parkeriana, commonly known as the Parker's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Mexico and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits. It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.

Ficus parkeriana prefers indirect light and moderate humidity, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
