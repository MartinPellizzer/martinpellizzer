Faurea natalensis

Faurea natalensis, commonly known as Natal starling or Natal bracted starling, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant produces showy, yellow to orange-colored flowers that attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies. Its leaves are typically narrow and arranged in a rosette pattern, and it thrives in well-drained, sandy soils.

Faurea natalensis is often found in open woodlands and along riverbanks, where it contributes to local biodiversity.
