Ficus sarmentosa var. nipponica

Ficus sarmentosa var. nipponica, commonly known as the Japanese banyan, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Japan.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish fruits. It has a distinctive aerial roots that can grow from the branches and reach the ground, forming a cascading appearance.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various growing conditions.
