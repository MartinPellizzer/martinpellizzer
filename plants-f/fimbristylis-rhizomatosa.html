Fimbristylis rhizomatosa

Fimbristylis rhizomatosa, commonly known as the slender thread-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread vegetatively through underground stems. It typically grows in wet or moist environments such as marshes, swamps, and along stream banks.

Its small, inconspicuous flowers are clustered in spikelets, typical of the sedge family.
