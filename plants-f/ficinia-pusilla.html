Ficinia pusilla

Ficinia pusilla, commonly known as the small figwort, is a small herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, upright stems that are often covered in fine hairs. Its leaves are opposite, ovate, and have a rough texture with serrated edges.

Ficinia pusilla produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, which are usually pale green to yellowish in color.
