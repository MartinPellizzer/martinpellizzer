Ficus sagittata

Ficus sagittata, commonly known as the arrowleaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped or arrow-shaped leaves, which are usually dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus sagittata is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively easy care requirements.
