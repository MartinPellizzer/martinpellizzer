Filipendula tsuguwoi

Filipendula tsuguwoi, commonly known as the Japanese meadow sweet, is a herbaceous perennial native to East Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its compound leaves and showy, pink to white flower clusters. The plant typically grows in moist, open habitats such as meadows and stream sides. It produces small, edible fruits that are dispersed by wind.
