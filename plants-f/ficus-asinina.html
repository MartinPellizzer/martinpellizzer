Ficus asinina

Ficus asinina, commonly known as the Indian rubber plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is a small to medium-sized tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall in its natural habitat. It produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible figs.

Ficus asinina is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
