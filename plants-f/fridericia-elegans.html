Fridericia elegans

Fridericia elegans, commonly known as the elegant fridericia, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, white, tubular flowers that bloom in clusters, typically from late spring to early summer. The plant has slender, woody stems and aromatic, lance-shaped leaves with a slightly hairy texture. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

Fridericia elegans is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance, providing nectar for pollinators.
