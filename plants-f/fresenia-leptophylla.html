Fresenia leptophylla

Fresenia leptophylla, commonly known as the slender-leafed flax, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Linaceae family and is characterized by its delicate, lance-shaped leaves and showy, blue-violet flowers. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in temperate climates. Its fibrous stalks were historically used for textile production, similar to other members of its family.

Fresenia leptophylla is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ease of cultivation.
