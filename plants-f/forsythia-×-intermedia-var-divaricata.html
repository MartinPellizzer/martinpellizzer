Forsythia × intermedia var. divaricata

Forsythia × intermedia var. divaricata, commonly known as the Japanese forsythia, is a deciduous shrub native to East Asia.

It is characterized by its striking yellow floral display, which appears in early spring before the leaves emerge. The plant has opposite, simple, ovate leaves that are dark green and glossy. Its flowers are borne in racemes and are often used in ornamental gardening for their vibrant color.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for spring-blooming shrubs.
