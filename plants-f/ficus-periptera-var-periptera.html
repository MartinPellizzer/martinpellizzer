Ficus periptera var. periptera

Ficus periptera var. periptera, commonly known as the African fig or the tree of life, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and thrives in warm, humid climates. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

This plant is often cultivated as an ornamental and for its ability to purify indoor air.
