Fugosia pedata

Fugosia pedata, commonly known as the spurred gentian, is a flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is a member of the Gentianaceae family and is characterized by its striking blue to purple flowers. The plant typically grows in rocky or alpine environments, preferring well-drained soils. Its leaves are opposite and often have a waxy coating, while its stems are usually hairy.

Fugosia pedata is noted for its ornamental value and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
