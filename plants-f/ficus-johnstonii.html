Ficus johnstonii

Ficus johnstonii, commonly known as the Johnston's fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its broad, waxy leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter underneath. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are edible and serve as a food source for various animals. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Ficus johnstonii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
