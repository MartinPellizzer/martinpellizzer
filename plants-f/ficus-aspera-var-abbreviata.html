Ficus aspera var. abbreviata

Ficus aspera var. abbreviata, commonly known as the short-leaved fig.

It is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae, native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its relatively small, thick, and leathery leaves that are often covered in a fine, velvety texture. It produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow or red when mature.

This variety is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
