Fuchsia arborea

Fuchsia arborea, commonly known as the tree fuchsia, is a deciduous shrub or small tree native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, pendulous flowers that range in color from pink to purple, typically blooming in the spring and summer. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are often glossy and dark green in color. It thrives in humid, shady environments and is often found in cloud forests.

Fuchsia arborea is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral display.
