Foeniculum rigidum

Foeniculum rigidum, commonly known as wild fennel, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic plants like carrots and parsley. The plant features feathery, finely divided leaves and yellow umbels of flowers that bloom in summer. Its seeds are small, aromatic, and often used in cooking or as a spice.

The entire plant emits a distinctive anise-like fragrance due to its high concentration of volatile oils.
