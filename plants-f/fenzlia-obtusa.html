Fenzlia obtusa

Fenzlia obtusa, commonly known as the broad-leaved fenzlia, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features basal leaves that are broad, ovate, and often hairy, with a bluish-green hue. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters, typically in shades of green or purple, which bloom in late summer. Its fibrous roots are valued for their medicinal properties in traditional medicine.

Fenzlia obtusa thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to cold, arid environments.
