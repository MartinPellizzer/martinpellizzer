Felicia microphylla

Felicia microphylla, commonly known as the small-leaved fleur-de-lis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features small, bright yellow flowers that are often surrounded by white or pale blue ray florets. It has narrow, linear leaves that are densely packed along the stems.

Felicia microphylla is often grown as an ornamental plant for its vibrant, daisy-like blooms and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
