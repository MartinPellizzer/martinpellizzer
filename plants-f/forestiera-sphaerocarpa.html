Forestiera sphaerocarpa

Forestiera sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe-leaf forestiera, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stems. The plant produces clusters of white, fragrant flowers in the spring, which are followed by small, round, blue-black berries. Its botanical structure includes a woody stem with rough, fissured bark.

It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in woodland and scrub habitats.
