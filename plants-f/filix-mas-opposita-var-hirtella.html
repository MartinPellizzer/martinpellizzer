Filix-mas opposita var. hirtella

Filix-mas opposita var. hirtella, commonly known as the hairy shield fern, is a variant of the common shield fern characterized by its hairy, glandular fronds.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and rocky crevices. The plant features a creeping rhizome with upright, bipinnate fronds that are typically dark green and slightly hairy on the underside. Its reproductive structures include sporangia that are clustered in sori on the underside of the fronds.

This variant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is occasionally cultivated in tropical gardens.
