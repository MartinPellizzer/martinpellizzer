Fritillaria lusitanica var. hispanica

Fritillaria lusitanica var. hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish Crown Imperial, is a bulbous perennial plant native to southern Europe.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces a tall, erect stem with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its long blooming period in spring.
