Ficus calyptroceras

Ficus calyptroceras, commonly known as the shield-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hair, giving them a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible, round figs.

This species is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
