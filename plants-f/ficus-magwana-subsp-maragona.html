Ficus magwana subsp. maragona

Ficus magwana subsp.

maragona, commonly known as the Maragona fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to parts of Southeast Asia. It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, clustered fig fruits.

The plant typically grows in humid, shaded environments and thrives in well-drained, fertile soil. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its edible figs.

The subspecies is distinguished by its unique leaf morphology and fruiting habits compared to other Ficus species.
