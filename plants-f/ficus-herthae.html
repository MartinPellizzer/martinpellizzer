Ficus herthae

Ficus herthae, commonly known as the Herth's fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the island of Madagascar.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, dark green leaves that are opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature, though they may change color depending on the variety. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Ficus herthae is also valued for its ability to adapt to various soil types, making it relatively easy to care for in suitable environments.
