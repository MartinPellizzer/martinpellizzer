Festuca ovina var. rydbergii

Festuca ovina var. rydbergii, commonly known as Rydberg's sheep fescue, is a perennial grass species native to North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dark green foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, well-drained soils and is often found in open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-dispersed seeds.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in native plant landscaping.
