Fabricia rugosa

Fabricia rugosa, commonly known as the rough fabricia, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves, which are often hairy and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which are typical of the legume family. Its pods are elongated and contain several seeds, which are usually small and dark in color.

Fabricia rugosa is native to parts of Asia and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
