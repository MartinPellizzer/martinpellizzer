Fissistigma ovoideum

Fissistigma ovoideum, commonly known as the oval-egg mistletoe, is a hemiparasitic plant that primarily grows on the branches of host trees such as eucalypts and acacias.

It has small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in pairs along its stems. The plant produces small, white or pale green flowers, followed by fleshy, berry-like fruits that are often consumed by birds. Its seeds are dispersed through the droppings of these birds, aiding in its propagation.

Fissistigma ovoideum is native to Australia and is often found in regions with a warm, temperate climate.
