Ferula schlechtendalii

Ferula schlechtendalii, commonly known as the Afghan ferula, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, umbrella-like compound leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its fibrous roots are valued for their medicinal properties and are used in traditional medicine.

Ferula schlechtendalii is also known for its strong, aromatic scent and its ability to thrive in arid environments.
