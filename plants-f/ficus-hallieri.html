Ficus hallieri

Ficus hallieri, commonly known as the Halli's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand, Vietnam, and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are often arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature.

Ficus hallieri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.
