Festuca leiocolea var. chatanieri

Festuca leiocolea var. chatanieri, commonly known as Chatanier's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of western North America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, blue-green blades that often form dense, low-growing mats. The plant typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Its flowering stems bear spikelets that are usually pale green to reddish in color, adding to its ornamental appeal.

This variety is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
