Filicium elongatum

Filicium elongatum, commonly known as the long-stemmed fern, is a species of fern characterized by its tall, slender fronds.

It belongs to the family Filicaceae and is native to tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features a rhizomatous growth habit, with long, arching leaves that can reach several meters in length. Its fronds are typically bipinnate, with a delicate, lace-like appearance.

Filicium elongatum thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along stream banks.
