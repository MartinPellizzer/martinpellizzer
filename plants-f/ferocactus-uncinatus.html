Ferocactus uncinatus

Ferocactus uncinatus, commonly known as the hooked barrel cactus, is a species native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its cylindrical stems that are ribbed and covered in spines, with the spines at the tips being curved and hook-like. The plant produces yellow flowers during the summer months, which are typically borne in clusters at the base of the stem. It is drought-tolerant and thrives in full sun, making it well adapted to arid and semi-arid environments.

The plant's structure and features make it an important part of the local ecosystem, providing habitat and food for various desert animals.
