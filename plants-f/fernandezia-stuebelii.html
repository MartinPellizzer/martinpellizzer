Fernandezia stuebelii

Fernandezia stuebelii, commonly known as the Stuebel's fern, is a rare and unique fern species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its delicate, finely divided fronds that are often described as resembling the leaves of a tree fern. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in high humidity and cool temperatures. Its reproductive structures, including spore-producing sorus, are located on the underside of its fronds, a common feature among ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Fernandezia stuebelii is considered a vulnerable species, highlighting the importance of conservation efforts in protecting its natural environment.
