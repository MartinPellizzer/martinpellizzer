Ficus magallanensis

Ficus magallanensis, commonly known as the Galápagos fig, is a species of fig tree native to the Galápagos Islands.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in coastal areas and lowland forests. The plant has smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, white pubescence. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that serve as an important food source for local wildlife.

The species is adapted to the unique environmental conditions of the Galápagos, including high humidity and salt spray.
