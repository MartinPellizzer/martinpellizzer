Ficus macrophylla

Ficus macrophylla, commonly known as the Moreton Bay fig, is a large evergreen tree native to eastern Australia.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called syconia. Its roots can become extensive, often forming a dense network beneath the soil.

The tree is known for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types and its tolerance to urban environments.
