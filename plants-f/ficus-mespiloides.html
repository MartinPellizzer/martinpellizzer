Ficus mespiloides

Ficus mespiloides, commonly known as the Persian fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Asia, including Iran and the Caucasus region. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The tree can grow up to 10 meters tall and is valued for its adaptability to various soil types.
