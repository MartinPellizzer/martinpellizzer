Fimbristylis quinquangularis var. crassa

Fimbristylis quinquangularis var. crassa, commonly known as the broad-leaved flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a dense, grass-like cluster. Its flowering spikes are usually small and inconspicuous, with tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

The species is characterized by its robust, rhizomatous growth habit and is often found in disturbed or nutrient-rich soils.
