Ficus vittata

Ficus vittata, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its dense, cascading growth habit. The plant features small, heart-shaped leaves with a waxy texture and a distinctive light green to bluish-green color. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are followed by green, fleshy fruits.

Ficus vittata is often used as a climbing plant in landscaping due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
