Ficus pubinervis

Ficus pubinervis, commonly known as the pubescent-veined fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves with a pubescent (hairy) underside. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature, and it thrives in humid, shaded environments. Ficus pubinervis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

It is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
