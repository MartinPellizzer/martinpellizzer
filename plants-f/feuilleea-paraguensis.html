Feuilleea paraguensis

Feuilleea paraguensis, commonly known as the Paraguayan leaf plant, is a tropical species native to the eastern regions of Paraguay.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are usually white or pale yellow, followed by small, round fruits containing edible seeds. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in humid, shaded environments.

Due to its ornamental value and medicinal properties, it has gained interest in horticulture and traditional medicine.
