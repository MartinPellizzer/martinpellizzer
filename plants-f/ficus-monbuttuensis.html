Ficus monbuttuensis

Ficus monbuttuensis, commonly known as the Malagasy fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with shades of green and white. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when mature, though they may change color depending on the variety. It is a slow-growing plant that thrives in bright, indirect light and requires well-draining soil.

Due to its unique appearance and rarity, it is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant.
