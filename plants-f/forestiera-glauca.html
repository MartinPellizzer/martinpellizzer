Forestiera glauca

Forestiera glauca, commonly known as the blue forestiera, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It features small, elliptical leaves that are typically a glossy green, turning yellow in the fall. The plant produces clusters of white, bell-shaped flowers in the spring, which are followed by blue-black berries. It thrives in partial shade to full sun and prefers well-drained soils.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in woodland gardens.
