Fuchsia × fintelmannii

Fuchsia × fintelmannii, commonly known as the pineapple fuchsia, is a hybrid shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous flowers that range in color from pink to red, often with a yellow or orange center. The plant produces edible berries that are tart and juicy, similar to those of the common fuchsia. It thrives in moist, well-drained soil and prefers partial to full sunlight.

This ornamental plant is widely cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a decorative plant.
