Ficus dealbata

Ficus dealbata, commonly known as the variegated ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its striking, variegated leaves that display white and green patterns. The plant is evergreen and can grow as a tree or shrub, often reaching heights of up to 10 meters in its natural habitat. Its leaves are broad and ovate, with a smooth texture and a glossy appearance.

Ficus dealbata produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits that are edible for some wildlife.
