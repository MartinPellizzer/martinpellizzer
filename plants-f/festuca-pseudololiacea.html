Festuca pseudololiacea

Festuca pseudololiacea, commonly known as slender fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Arctic and alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its low-growing, mat-forming habit and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear minute flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Festuca pseudololiacea thrives in cold, nutrient-poor environments and is often found in tundra and rocky habitats.

Its botanical structure is adapted to withstand harsh climatic conditions, making it a resilient component of polar and alpine ecosystems.
