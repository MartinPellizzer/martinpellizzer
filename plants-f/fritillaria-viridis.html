Fritillaria viridis

Fritillaria viridis, commonly known as green bells, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia.

It produces striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to green and sometimes yellow, depending on the variety. The plant thrives in well-drained, sandy or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winter conditions, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

Fritillaria viridis is also valued in traditional medicine for its potential health benefits.
