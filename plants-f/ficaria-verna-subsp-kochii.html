Ficaria verna subsp. kochii

Ficaria verna subsp.

kochii, commonly known as the early buttercup, is a low-growing perennial herb native to parts of Europe and Asia. It features heart-shaped, bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring.

The plant has a rosette of basal leaves that are thick and fleshy, adapted for storing water. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical characteristics make it a notable species in ecological studies and garden cultivation.
