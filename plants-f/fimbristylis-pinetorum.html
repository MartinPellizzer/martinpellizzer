Fimbristylis pinetorum

Fimbristylis pinetorum, commonly known as the pine forest sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It typically grows in wet, acidic soils, often found in swampy areas or along the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomes help it spread and establish dense colonies in its habitat.

Fimbristylis pinetorum plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
