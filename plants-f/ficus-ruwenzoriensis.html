Ficus ruwenzoriensis

Ficus ruwenzoriensis, commonly known as the Ruwenzori fig, is a rare and striking species native to the mountainous regions of East Africa.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that are followed by edible, round fig fruits. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in montane forests.

Due to its unique appearance and limited distribution, it is considered a valuable species in botanical studies and conservation efforts.
