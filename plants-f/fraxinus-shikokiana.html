Fraxinus shikokiana

Fraxinus shikokiana, commonly known as the Shikoku ash, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The tree has simple, opposite leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape, with serrated margins. Its small, greenish-yellow flowers appear in clusters and are followed by winged fruits.

Fraxinus shikokiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant form and seasonal interest.
