Fuscospora × apiculata

Fuscospora × apiculata, commonly known as the hybrid fern, is a naturally occurring hybrid between Fuscospora fusca and Fuscospora apiculata.

It is characterized by its distinctive fronds that exhibit a mix of traits from both parent species, including a dark, leathery texture and a serrated margin. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or along stream banks. Its spore-producing structures, called sori, are located on the underside of the fronds and are clustered in rows.

This hybrid is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphology and potential ecological adaptations.
