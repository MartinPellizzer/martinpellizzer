Fabricia cylindrica

Fabricia cylindrica, commonly known as the cylindrical fabricia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and trifoliate leaves composed of three oval-shaped leaflets. Its small, yellowish-white flowers are arranged in racemes and attract various pollinators such as bees and butterflies.

The plant is also valued for its potential use in soil improvement due to its nitrogen-fixing ability.
