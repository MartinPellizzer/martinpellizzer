Furcraea flavoviridis

Furcraea flavoviridis, commonly known as the yellow-flowered furcraea, is a succulent plant native to Central America.

It belongs to the Asparagaceae family and is characterized by its rosette-forming leaves that are thick and fleshy. The plant produces striking yellow flowers that emerge from the center of the rosette, often in late spring or early summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid or semi-arid environments.

Due to its unique appearance and low maintenance, it is a popular choice for xeriscaping and ornamental gardening.
