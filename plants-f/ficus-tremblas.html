Ficus tremblas

Ficus tremblas, commonly known as the trembling fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture. Its small, greenish-white flowers are clustered in inflorescences, and it produces small, round fruits that are typically green when mature.

Ficus tremblas is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
