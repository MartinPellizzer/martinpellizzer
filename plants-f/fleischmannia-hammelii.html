Fleischmannia hammelii

Fleischmannia hammelii, commonly known as the desert dandelion, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble dandelions. The plant has gray-green, fuzzy leaves and produces yellow flowers that bloom in the spring. It thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to withstand drought conditions.

Fleischmannia hammelii plays a role in supporting local pollinators and is often found in desert scrub and rocky habitats.
