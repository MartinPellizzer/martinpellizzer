Ficus trichantha

Ficus trichantha, commonly known as the three-lobed fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically three-lobed, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are often consumed by birds and mammals. Ficus trichantha thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its roots can become quite extensive, making it a hardy and resilient species in suitable conditions.
