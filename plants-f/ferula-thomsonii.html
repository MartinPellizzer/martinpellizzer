Ferula thomsonii

Ferula thomsonii, commonly known as Thomson's giant fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and large, feathery leaves that resemble those of fennel. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in umbrella-like clusters, typical of the Apiaceae family. Its root system is extensive and can reach significant depths, allowing it to thrive in arid and rocky environments.

Ferula thomsonii is also known for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
