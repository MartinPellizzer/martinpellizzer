Fuchsia × eximia

Fuchsia × eximia, commonly known as the common fuchsia, is a hybrid plant belonging to the family Onagraceae.

It is characterized by its showy, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep purple to bright red. The plant is native to South America and is widely cultivated as an ornamental due to its vibrant blooms. It thrives in cool, moist environments and prefers partial shade to full sun.

Fuchsia × eximia is often grown in gardens and as a houseplant, valued for its decorative foliage and long-lasting flowering period.
