Ficus cordifolia

Ficus cordifolia, commonly known as the heartleaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves, which are typically glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus cordifolia is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
