Festuca occidentalis var. ingrata

Festuca occidentalis var. ingrata, commonly known as western fescue, is a perennial grass native to western North America.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces dense clusters of spikelets, which are the flowering structures typical of the grass family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous or rocky regions.

Festuca occidentalis var. ingrata is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone areas.
