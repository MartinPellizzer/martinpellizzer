Frivaldia volubilis

Frivaldia volubilis, commonly known as the silver vine, is a climbing plant native to East Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves with a distinctive silvery underside, which gives it its common name. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers in clusters, typically in shades of pink or white. Its berries are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor.

Frivaldia volubilis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
