Forasaccus maximus

Forasaccus maximus, commonly known as the giant saccate orchid, is a striking tropical plant characterized by its large, showy flowers.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is native to regions with warm, humid climates. The plant features a robust rhizome system and produces intricate, often fragrant blooms with a distinctive saccate (sac-like) structure. Its leaves are broad and strap-shaped, contributing to its lush appearance.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
