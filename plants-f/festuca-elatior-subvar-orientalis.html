Festuca elatior subvar. orientalis

Festuca elatior subvar. orientalis, commonly known as the oriental tall fescue, is a cool-season grass native to the temperate regions of Asia and Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and a dense, mat-like growth habit. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters and produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping and turfgrass applications.
