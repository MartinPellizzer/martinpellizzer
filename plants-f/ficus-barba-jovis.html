Ficus barba-jovis

Ficus barba-jovis, commonly known as the "Jovial Fig" or "Barbajovis Fig," is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and its ability to thrive in humid, warm climates. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits.

Ficus barba-jovis is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor environments.
