Ficus marchandii

Ficus marchandii, commonly known as the Desert Fig, is a species of flowering plant native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northwestern Mexico.

It is a small, deciduous tree or shrub that thrives in rocky soils and has a compact, rounded growth habit. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are edible and have a sweet, tangy flavor. Its leaves are thick and waxy, which helps it conserve water in its harsh environment.

Ficus marchandii is well adapted to drought conditions and is often found in desert scrub and chaparral habitats.
