Ficinia leiocarpa

Ficinia leiocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by spherical, smooth fruits that turn from green to yellow or white as they mature. The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique fruit and foliage.
