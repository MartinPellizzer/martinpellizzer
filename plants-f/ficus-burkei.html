Ficus burkei

Ficus burkei, commonly known as the African fig tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to sub-Saharan Africa and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and robust, woody structure. The plant produces small, greenish flowers that develop into edible figs, which are an important food source for various animals. Ficus burkei thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in moist, well-drained soils.

Its rapid growth and ability to adapt to different environments make it a popular choice for ornamental and ecological purposes.
