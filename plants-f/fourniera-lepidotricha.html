Fourniera lepidotricha

Fourniera lepidotricha, commonly known as the silverleaf acanthus, is a perennial shrub native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a dense layer of silvery-white hairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces showy, spire-like inflorescences with violet-blue flowers that bloom in the summer. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its unique foliage and striking flowers make it a valuable addition to both garden landscapes and botanical collections.
