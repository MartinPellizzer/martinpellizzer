Frailea dadakii

Frailea dadakii, commonly known as the Dadakii Cactus, is a species of flowering cactus native to the arid regions of South America.

It is characterized by its compact, globular shape and a covering of fine, white to grayish hairs. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in the spring, adding a delicate touch to its otherwise spiny appearance. It is adapted to thrive in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Due to its slow growth and unique appearance, it is often cultivated as a decorative plant in both indoor and outdoor settings.
