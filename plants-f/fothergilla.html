Fothergilla

Fothergilla, commonly known as witchhazel, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Heath family (Ericaceae) and is characterized by its showy, fragrant flowers that bloom in late spring. The plant typically grows to a height of 3 to 6 feet and features opposite, elliptical leaves that turn red in the fall. Fothergilla prefers acidic, well-drained soil and partial to full sun, making it a popular choice for woodland gardens.

Its unique flowering period and ornamental value make it a valuable addition to native plant landscapes.
