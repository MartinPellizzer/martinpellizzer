Farfugium japonicum f. aligulatum

Farfugium japonicum f. aligulatum, commonly known as the narrow-leaf Japanese buttercup, is a cultivar of the farfugium genus native to Japan.

It is characterized by its broad, slightly lobed, and often variegated leaves that can range from green to yellowish-white. The plant produces showy, dandelion-like yellow flowers in the summer months. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often used in ornamental gardening for its aesthetic appeal.

This cultivar is valued for its hardiness and ability to form dense clumps over time.
