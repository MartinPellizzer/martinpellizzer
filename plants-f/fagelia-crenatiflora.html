Fagelia crenatiflora

Fagelia crenatiflora, commonly known as the crested fern or mountain fern, is a species of flowering plant native to the mountainous regions of Central America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery fronds and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments and is often found in forest understories. Its leaves are typically arranged in a rosette pattern, and the plant is known for its ornamental value in horticulture.

Fagelia crenatiflora is also valued for its ability to purify the air and its potential medicinal properties.
