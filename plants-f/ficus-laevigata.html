Ficus laevigata

Ficus laevigata, commonly known as the woolly fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, whitish pubescence. It produces small, greenish flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are important to both wildlife and local human populations.

The tree is often used in ornamental horticulture due to its attractive appearance and adaptability to various growing conditions.
