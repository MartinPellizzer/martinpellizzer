Ficus carica var. lutea

Ficus carica var. lutea, commonly known as the yellow fig, is a variant of the common fig tree characterized by its distinctive yellowish fruit.

The leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth texture and a deep green color. The fruit develops in a unique, elongated cluster called a syconium, which turns yellow when ripe. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and mild, sweet flavor.

It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
