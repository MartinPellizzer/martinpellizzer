Festuca jaliscana

Festuca jaliscana, commonly known as Mexican bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and bluish-green coloration. The plant typically grows in dry, rocky soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences.

Festuca jaliscana is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
