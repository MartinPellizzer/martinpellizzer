Ferolia indica

Ferolia indica, commonly known as the Indian starfruit or carambola, is a tropical fruit-bearing plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small, evergreen tree with a short trunk and dense, glossy green leaves. The plant produces star-shaped fruits that are yellow when ripe, with a sweet and tangy flavor. Its flowers are small, white, and clustered in panicles, and it thrives in warm, humid climates.

Ferolia indica is valued for its edible fruit and is often cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
