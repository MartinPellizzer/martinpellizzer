Fernandezia gracillima

Fernandezia gracillima, commonly known as the slender fern, is a rare and delicate fern species native to the Andean regions of South America.

It is characterized by its finely divided, lance-shaped fronds that are often covered in a soft, silvery coating. The plant typically grows in shaded, moist environments, such as cloud forests, where it thrives in cool, humid conditions. Its reproductive structures, including sori, are located on the underside of the fronds, typical of most ferns.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, Fernandezia gracillima is considered a species of conservation concern.
