Ficus reclinata

Ficus reclinata, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping branches that give it a distinctive, cascading appearance. The plant has glossy, dark green leaves that are typically opposite in arrangement. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, greenish-white fruits.

Ficus reclinata is often cultivated as a houseplant or in outdoor gardens due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
