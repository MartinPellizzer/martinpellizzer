Festuca fiebrigii

Festuca fiebrigii, commonly known as the Fiebrig's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments. Festuca fiebrigii is often found in open meadows and along slopes at high elevations.

Its flowers are small and spike-like, forming dense inflorescences that contribute to its ornamental appeal in horticulture.
