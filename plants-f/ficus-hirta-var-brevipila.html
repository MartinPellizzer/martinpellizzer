Ficus hirta var. brevipila

Ficus hirta var. brevipila, commonly known as the short-stalked fig or small-leaved fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen shrub or tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features opposite, ovate leaves that are usually hairy on the underside, giving it a distinctive texture. Its small, clustered flowers are followed by edible figs that are often used in local cuisines.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.
