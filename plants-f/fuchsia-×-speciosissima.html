Fuchsia × speciosissima

Fuchsia × speciosissima, commonly known as the common fuchsia, is a hybrid plant native to South America.

It is a deciduous shrub that produces showy, pendulous flowers in shades of pink, purple, and red. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves with serrated edges, and it thrives in partial shade with well-drained soil. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant.

The flowers bloom in late spring to early summer, attracting pollinators like bees and hummingbirds.
