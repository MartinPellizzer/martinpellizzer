Fadogia latifolia

Fadogia latifolia, commonly known as African musk bean, is a flowering plant native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its erect, branched stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, yellowish-white flowers that are clustered in racemes. Its seeds are enclosed in elongated, pod-like fruits and are often used in traditional medicine.

Fadogia latifolia is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been studied for potential pharmacological applications.
