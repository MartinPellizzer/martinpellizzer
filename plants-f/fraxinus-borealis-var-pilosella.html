Fraxinus borealis var. pilosella

Fraxinus borealis var. pilosella, commonly known as the eastern white fringe tree, is a deciduous tree native to eastern North America.

It is characterized by its delicate, feathery foliage and clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are typically opposite, simple, and have a soft, hairy texture, especially on the underside. This species is often found in moist, well-drained soils along streambanks and in woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive and ornamental addition to native landscapes.
