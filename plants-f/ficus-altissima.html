Ficus altissima

Ficus altissima, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and long, cascading branches that give it a distinctive appearance. The leaves are typically glossy and oval-shaped, arranged oppositely along the stems. It produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by round, greenish-white fruits.

This plant is often cultivated as a decorative indoor or outdoor plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
