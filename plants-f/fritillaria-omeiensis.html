Fritillaria omeiensis

Fritillaria omeiensis, commonly known as the Omei fritillary, is a rare and striking bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Sichuan, China.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and clusters of bell-shaped, often purple or orange-red flowers. The plant belongs to the Liliaceae family and is known for its ornamental value due to its vibrant and unique blooms. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers cool, moist environments.

Due to habitat loss and over-collection, it is considered endangered and is protected in its native range.
