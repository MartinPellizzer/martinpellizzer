Ficus variolosa

Ficus variolosa, commonly known as the variegated fig tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that often display striking variegation patterns of white and green. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature, though they may change color depending on the variety. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Ficus variolosa is relatively easy to care for, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
