Ficus cuneata

Ficus cuneata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Mexico and Brazil. The plant is characterized by its simple, alternate, and narrow elliptical leaves that are often slightly hairy on the underside. It produces small, clustered flowers that are followed by edible fig fruits, which are a key feature of the genus Ficus.

The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
