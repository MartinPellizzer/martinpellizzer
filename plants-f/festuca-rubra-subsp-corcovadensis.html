Festuca rubra subsp. corcovadensis

Festuca rubra subsp.

corcovadensis, commonly known as the red fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and western Asia. It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dense, dark green foliage.

The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences bearing small, two-lobed spikelets. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky habitats. Festuca rubra subsp.

corcovadensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
