Filago aegaea subsp. aegaea

Filago aegaea subsp.

aegaea, commonly known as the Aegean filago, is a flowering plant native to the eastern Mediterranean region. It is a perennial herb characterized by its basal rosette of narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a dense, spherical inflorescence known as a corymb. It thrives in rocky or sandy soils and is often found in coastal and mountainous areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species within its range.
