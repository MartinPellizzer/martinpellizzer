Flemingia congesta var. latifolia

Flemingia congesta var. latifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaved flemingia, is a species of flowering plant in the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its broad, ovate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. It has a woody stem and produces small, pod-like fruits that contain several seeds.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
