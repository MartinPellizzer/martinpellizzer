Ficus polybractea

Ficus polybractea, commonly known as the broad-leaved fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. It produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature.

Ficus polybractea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
