Ficus corylifolia var. glabrescens

Ficus corylifolia var. glabrescens, commonly known as the glossy fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when ripe. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

The leaves are usually smooth and have a waxy texture, contributing to its aesthetic appeal.
