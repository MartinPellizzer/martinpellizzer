Ficus sycomorus var. prodigiosa

Ficus sycomorus var. prodigiosa, commonly known as the sacred fig or elephant fig, is a tropical tree native to parts of Africa and the Arabian Peninsula.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible figs. The plant is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and riverbanks. Its roots can form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

The tree is also notable for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil types, making it a resilient and adaptable species.
