Fagara trijuga

Fagara trijuga, commonly known as the three-lobed soursop, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to the Caribbean and parts of Central and South America.

It belongs to the Annonaceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves with three distinct lobes. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are followed by a spiny, berry-like fruit. The fruit contains a single large seed and is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

Its botanical features make it an important species in both ecological and medicinal contexts.
