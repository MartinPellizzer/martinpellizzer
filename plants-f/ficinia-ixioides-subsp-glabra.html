Ficinia ixioides subsp. glabra

Ficinia ixioides subsp.

glabra, commonly known as the smooth sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Southeast Asia. It typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, nutrient-rich soils.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are smooth to the touch, as indicated by its subspecies name "glabra.". Its small, inconspicuous flowers are usually clustered in spikelets, and it produces small, three-lobed berries.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
