Festuca ovina subsp. purpusiana

Festuca ovina subsp.

purpusiana, commonly known as purple mountain grass, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage that turns a striking purple during the flowering season.

The plant produces slender, spike-like inflorescences bearing small, two-lobed florets. It thrives in well-drained, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, alpine environments.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping for its hardy nature and aesthetic qualities.
