Fritillaria hispanica

Fritillaria hispanica, commonly known as the Spanish fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It produces striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep red, often with intricate patterns. The plant thrives in rocky or gravelly soils and prefers full sun to partial shade. Its bulbs are toxic and should be handled with care.

Fritillaria hispanica is valued in horticulture for its ornamental beauty and historical use in traditional medicine.
