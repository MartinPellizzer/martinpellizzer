Flemingia semialata var. viridis

Flemingia semialata var. viridis, commonly known as the green-flowered flemingia, is a shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and trifoliate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers arranged in racemes, which are often accompanied by a pleasant fragrance. Its seeds are enclosed in pods and can be used for soil enrichment due to their nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in tropical gardens.
