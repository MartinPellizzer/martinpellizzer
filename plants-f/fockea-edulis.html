Fockea edulis

Fockea edulis, commonly known as the edible cycad, is a unique plant native to parts of southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its stout, unbranched trunk and large, pinnate leaves. The plant is notable for its ability to produce edible seeds, which are often consumed by both humans and animals. However, it contains toxic compounds that require careful processing before consumption.

Its slow growth and long lifespan make it an important component of its native ecosystems.
