Festuca ovina subsp. brigantina

Festuca ovina subsp.

brigantina, commonly known as the Alpine meadow fescue, is a perennial grass species native to mountainous regions of Europe. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, silver-green foliage.

The plant typically grows in well-drained, calcareous soils and is adapted to harsh alpine environments. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale green to brownish flowers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in horticultural settings for its resilience and aesthetic qualities.
