Ficus fuscata

Ficus fuscata, commonly known as the dark fig or black fig tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central and South America, particularly in countries like Brazil and Peru. The plant is characterized by its dense, dark green foliage and small, inconspicuous flowers that develop into edible figs. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a glossy texture.

Ficus fuscata thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in rainforests or along riverbanks.
