Festuca ovina subsp. callosa

Festuca ovina subsp.

callosa, commonly known as the scaly sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, dark green foliage.

The leaves are typically narrow and have a distinctive scaly appearance on the underside, giving it its subspecific name. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and rocky slopes.

This grass is valued for its ornamental qualities and its ability to stabilize soil in various ecological settings.
