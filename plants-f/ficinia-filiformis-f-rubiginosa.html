Ficinia filiformis f. rubiginosa

Ficinia filiformis f. rubiginosa, commonly known as the red-stemmed figsplant, is a botanical variant characterized by its distinctive reddish stem coloration.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant typically features slender, climbing stems with small, heart-shaped leaves. Its flowers are unisexual, with separate male and female flowers on different plants.

This form is often cultivated for its ornamental value due to its unique coloration and delicate appearance.
