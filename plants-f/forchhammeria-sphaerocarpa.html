Forchhammeria sphaerocarpa

Forchhammeria sphaerocarpa, commonly known as the globe-tailed cactus, is a unique succulent native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its short, thick stems and distinctive, spherical, spiny fruits that resemble small globes. The plant produces yellow flowers during the warmer months, adding a vibrant touch to its appearance. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires minimal water, making it suitable for arid environments.

Due to its rarity and specific habitat requirements, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
