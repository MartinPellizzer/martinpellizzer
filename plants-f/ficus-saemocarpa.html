Ficus saemocarpa

Ficus saemocarpa, commonly known as the black fig tree, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are usually elliptical in shape. It produces small, fig-like fruits that are edible and have a sweet, sticky pulp.

The tree is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also valued for its fruit in some regions.
