Fraxinus ptacovskyi

Fraxinus ptacovskyi, commonly known as the Czech ash, is a deciduous tree native to Central Europe.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its slender, upright growth habit. The tree has opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptical in shape, with serrated margins. Its flowers are small, greenish-yellow, and clustered in pendulous inflorescences, while its fruit is a samara with a slender wing.

The bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.
