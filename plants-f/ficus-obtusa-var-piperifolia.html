Ficus obtusa var. piperifolia

Ficus obtusa var. piperifolia, commonly known as the Variegated Ficus or the Variegated Fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with striking white and yellow variegation. The plant has a compact growth habit and can be grown as a houseplant or in shaded outdoor settings. It produces small, green fig fruits that are typically not edible for humans.

Ficus obtusa var. piperifolia thrives in warm, humid conditions with indirect light and requires moderate watering to maintain its health.
