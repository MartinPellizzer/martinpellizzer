Ficus gibbosa var. cuspidifera

Ficus gibbosa var. cuspidifera, commonly known as the pointed-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are typically pointed at the tips. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
