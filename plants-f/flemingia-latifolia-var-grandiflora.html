Flemingia latifolia var. grandiflora

Flemingia latifolia var. grandiflora, commonly known as the broadleaf flemingia, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, fragrant white flowers. The plant typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions, often in moist, shaded environments. It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is known for its nitrogen-fixing capabilities.

The varietal form, grandiflora, distinguishes it by its larger flowers compared to the typical variety.
