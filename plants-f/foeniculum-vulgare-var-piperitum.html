Foeniculum vulgare var. piperitum

Foeniculum vulgare var. piperitum, commonly known as sweet fennel, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its feathery, aromatic leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant produces small, oval seeds that are widely used in culinary and medicinal applications. Its distinctive licorice-like aroma comes from compounds such as anethole.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and full sunlight, making it a popular choice for gardens and herbal cultivation.
