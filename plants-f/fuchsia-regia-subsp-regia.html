Fuchsia regia subsp.

regia, commonly known as the royal fuchsia, is a striking flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America. It is characterized by its large, showy bracts that range in color from deep red to violet, often resembling the petals of the actual flowers.

The plant produces small, tubular flowers that are typically pale pink or white, which are pollinated by hummingbirds and insects. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses. Fuchsia regia subsp.

regia is valued for its vibrant foliage and long-lasting blooms, making it a popular choice for tropical and subtropical landscapes.
