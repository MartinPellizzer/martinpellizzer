Ficus guineensis

Ficus guineensis, commonly known as the African breadfruit, is a species of fig tree native to tropical Africa.

It is a large, evergreen tree that can grow up to 20 meters tall, with a broad, spreading canopy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers, which are followed by edible fig fruits that are rich in carbohydrates. Its leaves are heart-shaped, glossy, and arranged alternately along the branches.

The plant thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.
