Festuca livida

Festuca livida, commonly known as bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to Europe, Asia, and North Africa.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted blades and a bluish-green color, which gives it its common name. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, woodlands, and along stream banks. Festuca livida is a perennial species that forms dense clumps and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits.

It is also used in landscaping for its ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
