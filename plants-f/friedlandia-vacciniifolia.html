Friedlandia vacciniifolia

Friedlandia vacciniifolia, commonly known as the red-leafed fern, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its vibrant, velvety red foliage that contrasts beautifully with its delicate, feathery fronds. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil to flourish. Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental gardening and indoor plant collections.

However, it is relatively rare in the wild and is often cultivated for its aesthetic value.
