Ficus angulidens

Ficus angulidens, commonly known as the Benjamin fig, is a popular indoor plant native to Southeast Asia.

It features glossy, dark green leaves that are typically narrow and slightly pointed, with a waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by greenish-white fig fruits, though these are rarely seen in cultivation. It thrives in bright, indirect light and requires moderate watering to maintain its lush appearance.

Ficus angulidens is known for its adaptability and ease of care, making it a favored choice for indoor gardens.
