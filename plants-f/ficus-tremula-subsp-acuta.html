Ficus tremula subsp. acuta

Ficus tremula subsp.

acuta, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family. It is native to parts of East Asia, including China, Japan, and Korea.

The plant is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often slightly serrated along the edges. It produces small, greenish flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, round fruits.

This subspecies is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
