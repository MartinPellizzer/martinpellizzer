Frangula alnus f. retusa

Frangula alnus f. retusa, commonly known as the smooth-fruited alder, is a botanical variant of the alder tree native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, glabrous fruits, distinguishing it from the typical form of Frangula alnus. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree, typically growing in moist, temperate environments. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green upper surface.

The plant is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
