Freycinetia kalimantanica

Freycinetia kalimantanica, commonly known as the Borneo screw-pine, is a unique plant native to the island of Borneo.

It is a member of the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive, spirally arranged leaves. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with both male and female flowers, typical of its dioecious nature. Its roots are known to form symbiotic relationships with nitrogen-fixing bacteria, enhancing soil fertility.

This species is often found in coastal and swampy areas, adapting to saline and waterlogged conditions.
