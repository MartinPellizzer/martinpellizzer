Fuchsia sessilifolia

Fuchsia sessilifolia, commonly known as the sessile-leaved fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its dense, evergreen foliage and clusters of vibrant, tubular flowers that range in color from deep red to purple. The plant typically grows as a shrub, reaching heights of up to 3 meters, and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are simple, elliptical, and often have a waxy texture, while its flowers are pollinated primarily by birds, especially hummingbirds.

Fuchsia sessilifolia is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract wildlife.
