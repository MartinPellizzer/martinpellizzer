Fraxinus mandshurica subsp. brevipedicellata

Fraxinus mandshurica subsp.

brevipedicellata, commonly known as the Manchurian ash, is a deciduous tree native to East Asia. It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves.

The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by samaras with elongated wings. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in forested areas across China, Korea, and Russia.

Its ornamental value and ecological importance make it a notable species in its native habitat.
