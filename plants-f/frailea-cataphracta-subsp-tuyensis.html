Frailea cataphracta subsp. tuyensis

Frailea cataphracta subsp.

tuyensis, commonly known as the Tuyensis Cactus, is a species of flowering plant native to southern Mexico. It belongs to the Cactaceae family and is characterized by its compact, globular shape and spiny stems.

The plant produces yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking visual contrast to its green foliage. It is well adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water.

This subspecies is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
