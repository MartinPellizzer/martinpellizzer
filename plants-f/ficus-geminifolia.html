Ficus geminifolia

Ficus geminifolia, commonly known as the twin-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elliptical leaves that often appear in pairs, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in humid, warm environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus geminifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
