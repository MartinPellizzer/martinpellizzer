Fritillaria meleagris subsp. moggridgei

Fritillaria meleagris subsp.

moggridgei, commonly known as the Scottish Crown Imperial, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the Scottish Highlands. It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers in shades of orange, red, and yellow, typically blooming in late spring to early summer.

The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, sandy or loamy soils. It is often found in open woodlands and rocky areas, where it benefits from partial shade.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for rock gardens and woodland plantings.
