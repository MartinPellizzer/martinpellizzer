Ficus eugenioides var. puberula

Ficus eugenioides var. puberula, commonly known as the velvet fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, velvety foliage that gives it its common name. The leaves are typically dark green and have a smooth, almost waxy texture. This plant thrives in humid environments and requires indirect sunlight to flourish.

It is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its ornamental appeal and adaptability to indoor conditions.
