Fistularia angustifolia

Fistularia angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaved pipevine, is a flowering plant native to parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, lance-shaped leaves and clusters of small, tubular flowers that typically bloom in shades of pink or purple. The plant thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. Its flowers attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies, contributing to its ecological significance.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and landscapes.
