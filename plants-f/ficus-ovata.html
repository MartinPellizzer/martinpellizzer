Ficus ovata

Ficus ovata, commonly known as the Weeping Fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are opposite and ovate in shape. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish fruits that turn yellow when mature. It is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens due to its attractive appearance and ability to thrive in various conditions.

However, it is toxic to humans and pets, requiring careful handling.
