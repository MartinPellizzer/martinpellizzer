Ficinia bolusii

Ficinia bolusii, commonly known as the Bolus sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges and grass-like plants. The plant typically grows in wetlands and marshy areas, thriving in moist, well-drained soils. Its slender, triangular stems are often covered in fine hairs, and it produces small, greenish-white flowers in spike-like clusters.

Ficinia bolusii is valued for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems and providing habitat for various invertebrates.
