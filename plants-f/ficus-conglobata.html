Ficus conglobata

Ficus conglobata, commonly known as the clustered fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and thick, waxy leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature. It is a popular indoor plant due to its ability to thrive in low-light conditions and its ornamental value.

Ficus conglobata is also known for its adaptability to various soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
