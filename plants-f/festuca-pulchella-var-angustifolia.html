Festuca pulchella var. angustifolia

Festuca pulchella var. angustifolia, commonly known as narrowleaf bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green blades and tufted growth habit, often forming dense mats. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is commonly found in meadows and along stream banks. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, blue-gray flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in native plant landscaping.
