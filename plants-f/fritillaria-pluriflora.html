Fritillaria pluriflora

Fritillaria pluriflora, commonly known as the many-flowered fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of southern Europe and western Asia.

It produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with intricate patterns on the petals. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade, making it a suitable candidate for rock gardens and alpine settings. Its bulbs are perennial, allowing it to return year after year, and it is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Fritillaria pluriflora is also valued in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
