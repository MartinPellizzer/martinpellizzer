Fritillaria munbyi

Fritillaria munbyi, commonly known as the Munby's fritillary, is a bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Liliaceae family and is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or gravelly soils at high elevations, where it thrives in cool, moist conditions. Its bulbs are adapted for survival in harsh environments, often remaining dormant during dry periods.

Fritillaria munbyi is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens with specific horticultural care.
