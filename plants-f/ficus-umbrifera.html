Ficus umbrifera

Ficus umbrifera, commonly known as the shadow fig, is a species of fig tree native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and requires well-drained soil.

Ficus umbrifera is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental appeal.
