Festuca cinerea subsp. curvula

Festuca cinerea subsp.

curvula, commonly known as silver hair grass, is a perennial grass species native to parts of Europe and Asia. It is characterized by its fine, silver-gray blades and a tufted growth habit.

The plant typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks. Its inflorescence consists of a loose, open panicle with spikelets that are usually pale green to reddish-brown. Festuca cinerea subsp.

curvula is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting insect populations.
