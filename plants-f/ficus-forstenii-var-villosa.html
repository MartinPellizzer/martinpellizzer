Ficus forstenii var. villosa

Ficus forstenii var. villosa, commonly known as the hairy fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, hairy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

This variant is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor conditions.
