Freesia leichtlinii major

Freesia leichtlinii major, commonly known as the giant freesia, is a striking member of the Amaryllidaceae family.

It is native to South Africa and is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, showy flowers. The plant typically produces clusters of fragrant, trumpet-shaped blooms in shades of white, yellow, and orange. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for gardens and floral arrangements.

Its botanical features make it a valuable addition to both ornamental horticulture and botanical research.
