Frangula baetica

Frangula baetica, commonly known as Spanish buckthorn, is a shrub native to the Iberian Peninsula.

It belongs to the family Rutaceae and is characterized by its dark green, lance-shaped leaves and small yellow flowers. The plant produces bright red berries that are rich in anthraquinones, which have been used in traditional medicine. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in Mediterranean shrublands.

Frangula baetica is also valued for its ornamental qualities and historical use in dye production.
