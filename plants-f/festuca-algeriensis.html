Festuca algeriensis

Festuca algeriensis, commonly known as Algerian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or arid environments. Festuca algeriensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to stabilize soil in difficult conditions.

It is also used in some regions for forage or as a ground cover.
