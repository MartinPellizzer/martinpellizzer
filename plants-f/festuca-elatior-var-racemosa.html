Festuca elatior var. racemosa

Festuca elatior var. racemosa, commonly known as the tall fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and dense, fine-textured foliage. The plant produces panicles of small, spikelet-like flowers, typically in late spring to early summer. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.

Its adaptability and ornamental value make it a popular choice for gardens and ecological restoration projects.
