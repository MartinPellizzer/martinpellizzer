Fuchsia hitchcockii

Fuchsia hitchcockii, commonly known as the Hitchcock's fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to the Andes Mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its striking, pendulous clusters of vibrant pink to purple flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has lance-shaped leaves that are dark green and glossy, providing a striking contrast to its colorful blossoms. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens.

Fuchsia hitchcockii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in some regions.
