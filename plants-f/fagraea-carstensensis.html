Fagraea carstensensis

Fagraea carstensensis, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, orange-yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, attracting various pollinators. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets, and it produces woody, fleshy fruits that contain seeds. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks in tropical regions.
