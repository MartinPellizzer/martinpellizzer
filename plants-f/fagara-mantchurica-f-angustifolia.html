Fagara mantchurica f. angustifolia

Fagara mantchurica f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed fagara, is a botanical variant of the Fagara genus native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves and clusters of small, white flowers. The plant typically grows in shaded, humid environments, often in forest understories. Its foliage is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.

The species is of interest to botanists due to its unique morphological features and potential ecological roles.
