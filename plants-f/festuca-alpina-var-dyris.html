Festuca alpina var. dyris

Festuca alpina var. dyris, commonly known as alpine hairgrass, is a perennial grass species native to alpine regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, wiry leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained, rocky or gravelly soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.

Festuca alpina var. dyris is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness in challenging conditions.
