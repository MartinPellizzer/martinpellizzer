Ficus trichocarpa f. glabrescens

Ficus trichocarpa f. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leaf fig, is a variant of the common fig tree characterized by its smooth, waxy leaves.

It is native to regions of southern Asia, including parts of India and Southeast Asia. The plant is a deciduous tree that produces small, edible fruits known as figs. Its leaves are typically elliptical in shape and have a glossy, dark green appearance.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often grown in gardens and as a houseplant.
