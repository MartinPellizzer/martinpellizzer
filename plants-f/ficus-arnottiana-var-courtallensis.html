Ficus arnottiana var. courtallensis

Ficus arnottiana var. courtallensis, commonly known as the Kandy fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Sri Lanka.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, broad leaves that are typically arranged in a whorled pattern. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are encased in a spherical, fleshy receptacle. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation areas.
