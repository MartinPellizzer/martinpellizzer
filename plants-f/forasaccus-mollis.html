Forasaccus mollis

Forasaccus mollis, commonly known as the silky saccate plant, is a member of the Poaceae family, which includes grasses.

It is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically arranged in panicle-like inflorescences. The plant is native to certain regions of Africa and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

The species is also valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to form dense, low-growing mats.
