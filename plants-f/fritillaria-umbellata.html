Fritillaria umbellata

Fritillaria umbellata, commonly known as the bell-shaped fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant typically grows to a height of 30 to 60 centimeters, with narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Fritillaria umbellata is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
