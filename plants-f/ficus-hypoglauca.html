Ficus hypoglauca

Ficus hypoglauca, commonly known as the pale-green fig tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its smooth, pale-green bark and heart-shaped leaves that are typically glossy and dark green. Ficus hypoglauca produces small, clustered figs that are edible and often consumed by local wildlife and humans.

It thrives in tropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and fruit production.
