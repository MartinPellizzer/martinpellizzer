Fragaria vulgaris var. rubra

Fragaria vulgaris var. rubra, commonly known as the red strawberry, is a variant of the common strawberry plant.

It is characterized by its distinctive red berries, which are slightly smaller and more intensely flavored than the typical variety. The plant produces trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and pinkish-white flowers that bloom in early spring. Its fruits are rich in antioxidants and are often used in culinary applications.

This variety is well-suited for cultivation in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil and full sunlight.
