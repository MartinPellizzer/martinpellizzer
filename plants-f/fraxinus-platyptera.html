Fraxinus platyptera

Fraxinus platyptera, commonly known as the Chinese ash, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves that are typically ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by winged samaras as its fruit. It prefers well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

Fraxinus platyptera is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
