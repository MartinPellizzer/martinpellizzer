Festuca hondoensis

Festuca hondoensis, commonly known as Hondo fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and mountainous regions. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration.

Festuca hondoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
