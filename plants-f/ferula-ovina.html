Ferula ovina

Ferula ovina, commonly known as the Persian cow's milk plant, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, hairy stems and yellowish-green leaves. The plant produces small, clustered yellow flowers that bloom in the summer, followed by a distinctive, branched, woody fruit. Ferula ovina is known for its medicinal properties and has been traditionally used in herbal remedies.

Its essential oils, extracted from the roots and stems, are valued for their fragrance and potential therapeutic uses.
