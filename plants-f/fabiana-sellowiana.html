Fabiana sellowiana

Fabiana sellowiana, commonly known as the silver leaf plant, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in a fine, silvery-white powdery coating, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink, typically blooming in spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its drought tolerance and low maintenance requirements.
