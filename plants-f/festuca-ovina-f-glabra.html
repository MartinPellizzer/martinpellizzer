Festuca ovina f. glabra

Festuca ovina f. glabra, commonly known as smooth sheep fescue, is a grass species characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and dense, carpet-like appearance.

It is a perennial plant native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia, often found in meadows, grasslands, and rocky soils. The leaves are narrow, hairless, and have a bluish-green color, while the inflorescence consists of a spike of small, spikelet-like flowers. This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and erosion control due to its hardiness and adaptability.

It prefers well-drained soil and full to partial sunlight, making it a versatile choice for garden settings.
