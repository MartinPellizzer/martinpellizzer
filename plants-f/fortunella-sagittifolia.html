Fortunella sagittifolia

Fortunella sagittifolia, commonly known as the Chinese lime or mountain kumquat, is a small evergreen shrub native to southern China and Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and fragrant flowers. The plant produces small, orange-colored fruits that are edible and often used in traditional Chinese medicine. Its ornamental value is enhanced by its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions.

Fortunella sagittifolia is also valued for its hardiness and adaptability to different climates.
