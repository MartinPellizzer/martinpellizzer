Ficus goliath

Ficus goliath, commonly known as the giant ficus, is a large tree species native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, rough bark and large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, pale green flowers that are followed by round, green fig fruits. Ficus goliath is often cultivated as an ornamental tree due to its impressive size and aesthetic appeal.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil for optimal growth.
