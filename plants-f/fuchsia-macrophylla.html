Fuchsia macrophylla

Fuchsia macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaved fuchsia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its vibrant, showy flowers that range in color from pink to purple. The plant produces large, heart-shaped leaves that are often used in horticultural displays. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Fuchsia macrophylla is known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and hummingbirds.
