Fordia nivea

Fordia nivea, commonly known as the snowbell tree, is a deciduous shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its clusters of small, white, bell-shaped flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant has simple, alternate leaves that are elliptical in shape and turn yellow in the fall. It thrives in partial to full sun and well-drained soil, making it a suitable choice for gardens and landscapes.

The botanical features of Fordia nivea make it a valuable addition to ornamental horticulture.
