Festuca novae-zelandiae

Festuca novae-zelandiae, commonly known as New Zealand grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, bluish-green leaves. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forest margins and wetlands.

Festuca novae-zelandiae plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for native invertebrates.
