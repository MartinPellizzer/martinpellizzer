Fumaria ouezzanensis subsp. ramosa

Fumaria ouezzanensis subsp.

ramosa, commonly known as the hairy yellowweed, is a flowering plant native to North Africa. It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of yellow flowers.

The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid environments. Its leaves are alternate and often hairy, which helps reduce water loss.

It is a herbaceous perennial that produces small, capsule-like fruits containing several seeds.
