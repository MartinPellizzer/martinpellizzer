Ferula eburnea

Ferula eburnea, commonly known as the white mullein, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its tall, hairy stems and large, gray-green, lance-shaped leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in clusters, which bloom in late summer. Its roots are thick and fleshy, and it is often found in rocky or dry soils.

Ferula eburnea is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional medicine for various purposes.
