Frangula americana

Frangula americana, commonly known as American cascara, is a deciduous shrub native to the eastern United States.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family, which includes citrus plants, and is characterized by its compound leaves and yellowish flowers. The plant produces small, reddish berries that mature in the fall. Its bark and leaves contain compounds used in traditional medicine, particularly for their laxative properties.

Frangula americana thrives in moist, wooded areas and is often found along stream banks and in thickets.
