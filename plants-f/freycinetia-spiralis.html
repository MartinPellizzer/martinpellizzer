Freycinetia spiralis

Freycinetia spiralis, commonly known as the spiral pandanus, is a flowering plant native to tropical regions of the Pacific.

It is characterized by its tall, slender trunk and long, narrow leaves that spiral upward, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers that develop into triangular, fibrous fruits. It thrives in coastal and lowland areas, often growing in sandy or well-drained soils.

Freycinetia spiralis is valued for its ornamental qualities and has cultural significance in some island communities.
