Fritillaria monantha var. ningguoica

Fritillaria monantha var. ningguoica, commonly known as the Ningguo fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to China.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that typically range in color from pale yellow to deep orange. The plant produces a single, upright stem that bears several flowers, often with a distinctive hooded shape. Its bulbs are adapted to survive in rocky, well-drained soils, and it thrives in temperate climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
