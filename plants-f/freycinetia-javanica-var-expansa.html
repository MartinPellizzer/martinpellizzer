Freycinetia javanica var. expansa

Freycinetia javanica var. expansa, commonly known as the broad-leafed freycinetia, is a member of the Pandanaceae family.

It is characterized by its large, strap-shaped leaves that are often used in traditional crafts and as a natural dye. The plant is native to Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in wetlands or near water bodies.

Its foliage is also valued for its aromatic properties and is sometimes used in culinary applications.
