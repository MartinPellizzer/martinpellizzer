Ficus trachyphylla

Ficus trachyphylla, commonly known as the rough-leaved fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa and is characterized by its thick, waxy, and often hairy leaves. The plant produces small, green fig fruits that are typically enclosed in a spiky, green calyx. It thrives in humid, warm environments and is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage.

The leaves are usually dark green with a rough texture, giving the plant its common name.
