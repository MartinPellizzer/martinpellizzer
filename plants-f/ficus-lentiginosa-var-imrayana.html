Ficus lentiginosa var. imrayana

Ficus lentiginosa var. imrayana, commonly known as the Imrayana rubber tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often veined with a distinctive light green pattern. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by rubbery, elongated fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Its latex sap, similar to other ficus species, has been historically used in various industrial applications.
