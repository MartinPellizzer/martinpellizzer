Faba vulgaris var. fuliginiflora

Faba vulgaris var. fuliginiflora, commonly known as the sooty-flowered vetch, is a leguminous plant characterized by its distinctive dark, sooty-pink flowers.

It is native to the Mediterranean region and thrives in well-drained soils. The plant has compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces small, pod-like fruits containing edible seeds. It is often used as a cover crop due to its ability to fix nitrogen in the soil.

The species is also valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in traditional medicine.
