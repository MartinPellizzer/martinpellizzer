Facelis retusa var. candelabrum

Facelis retusa var. candelabrum, commonly known as the candlestick pomegranate, is a compact, shrub-like plant native to southern Africa.

It features distinctive, branching stems that resemble a candlestick, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically pink or red in color. Its leaves are narrow and lance-shaped, with a slightly hairy texture.

The fruit is a small, round pomegranate-like berry that turns red when mature.
