Ficus griseifolia

Ficus griseifolia, commonly known as the gray-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a bluish-gray underside, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible figs, which are typically small and have a mild flavor. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.

Ficus griseifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to adapt to various growing conditions.
