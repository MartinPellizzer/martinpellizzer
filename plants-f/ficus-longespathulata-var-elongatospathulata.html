Ficus longespathulata var. elongatospathulata

Ficus longespathulata var. elongatospathulata, commonly known as the elongated spathulate fig, is a variant of the Ficus genus native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its elongated, spathulate leaves that are typically dark green with a glossy texture. The plant produces small, clustered fruits known as syconia, which are enclosed within a fleshy, spathaceous structure. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers indirect light, making it a popular choice for indoor cultivation.

Its unique leaf shape and adaptability to various growing conditions contribute to its ornamental value.
