Ficus killipii

Ficus killipii, commonly known as the Killipii fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a smooth texture. It produces small, clustered flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus killipii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
