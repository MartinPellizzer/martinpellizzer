Ficus hispida

Ficus hispida, commonly known as the velvet leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, velvety leaves that have a distinctive waxy texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus hispida is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
