Ficus ndola

Ficus ndola, commonly known as the Ndola fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Zambia and the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered fruits that are edible. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in moist forested areas.

The Ndola fig is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in local ecosystems.
