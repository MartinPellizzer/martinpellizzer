Ferula mollis

Ferula mollis, commonly known as the soft ferula, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in dry, rocky soils and thrives in temperate climates. The plant features tall, slender stems with narrow, lance-shaped leaves and produces small, yellowish-green flowers arranged in a dense umbel. Its roots are fibrous and can be harvested for their aromatic properties.

Ferula mollis is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its unique appearance and hardy nature.
