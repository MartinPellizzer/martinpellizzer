Ficus subalbidoramea

Ficus subalbidoramea, commonly known as the white-veined fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that have a distinctive white or pale green veins. The plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus subalbidoramea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.
