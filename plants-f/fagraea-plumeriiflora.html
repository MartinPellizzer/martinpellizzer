Fagraea plumeriiflora

Fagraea plumeriiflora, commonly known as the flame tree or golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its showy, bright yellow or orange-red flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a striking visual display. The plant has compound leaves with elongated, pointed leaflets that give it a feathery appearance. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

The fruit is a woody capsule that contains several seeds, which are dispersed by wind or animals.
