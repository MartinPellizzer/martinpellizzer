Ficinia semibracteata

Ficinia semibracteata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the wetlands and marshes of southeastern Australia.

It features narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically arranged in three rows, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are clustered in spikelets, which are usually borne on slender, branching stems. Ficinia semibracteata thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and freshwater habitats.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various invertebrates and birds.
