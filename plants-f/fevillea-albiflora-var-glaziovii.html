Fevillea albiflora var. glaziovii

Fevillea albiflora var. glaziovii, commonly known as the Glaziov's white-flowered fevillea, is a rare and striking plant native to specific regions of Mexico.

It is characterized by its elegant, white, star-shaped flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant features narrow, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in well-drained, acidic soils and requires a warm, humid climate with indirect sunlight.

Due to its rarity and ornamental value, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by enthusiasts.
