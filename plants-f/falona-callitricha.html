Falona callitricha

Falona callitricha, commonly known as the narrow-leaf water clover, is a small aquatic plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It typically grows in shallow, freshwater environments such as ponds and slow-moving streams. The plant has slender, branching stems that float on the water surface, with small, heart-shaped leaves arranged in a rosette pattern. Its flowers are tiny, greenish-white, and clustered in dense inflorescences.

Falona callitricha plays a role in aquatic ecosystems by providing habitat for microorganisms and invertebrates.
