Forchhammeria tamaulipana

Forchhammeria tamaulipana, commonly known as the Tamaulipan cactus, is a species native to the northeastern region of Mexico.

It is a spiny, columnar cactus that typically grows in dry, rocky soils. The plant features a thick, ribbed stem covered in sharp, forward-pointing spines. Its flowers are usually yellow or orange, blooming in late spring or early summer.

Forchhammeria tamaulipana is adapted to arid environments and plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat and food for desert wildlife.
