Faberia ceterach

Faberia ceterach, commonly known as the narrow-leaved faberia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its slender, often hairy stems and small, clustered flowers. The plant produces pod-like fruits that contain several seeds, which are typically dispersed by wind or animals. Faberia ceterach thrives in dry, rocky habitats and is adapted to withstand arid conditions.

Its leaves are compound, with small, oval-shaped leaflets that help reduce water loss.
