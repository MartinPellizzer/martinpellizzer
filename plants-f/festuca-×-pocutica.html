Festuca × pocutica

Festuca × pocutica, commonly known as the hybrid fescue, is a perennial grass species that exhibits a mix of characteristics from its parent species, Festuca ovina and Festuca rubra.

It typically grows in cool, temperate climates and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along the edges of forests. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It is valued for its hardiness and is used in landscaping and as a forage crop.

Festuca × pocutica is also noted for its ability to thrive in a variety of soil conditions, making it a versatile plant in ecological and agricultural settings.
