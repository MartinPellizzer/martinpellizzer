Festuca × belensis var. speciosa

Festuca × belensis var. speciosa, commonly known as the showy fescue, is a hybrid grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and distinctive, elongated inflorescences that bear showy, spikelet-like flowers. The plant typically thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a hardy ornamental grass in temperate climates. Its foliage is narrow and grass-like, with a bluish-green hue that adds visual interest to gardens.

Due to its ornamental value and adaptability, it is often cultivated in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal and low maintenance requirements.
