Ficus subfulva

Ficus subfulva, commonly known as the white fig or variegated fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that often feature white or cream-colored veins and markings. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. Ficus subfulva thrives in warm, humid environments and requires indirect sunlight for optimal growth.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor gardening and ornamental purposes.
