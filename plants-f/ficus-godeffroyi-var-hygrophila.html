Ficus godeffroyi var. hygrophila

Ficus godeffroyi var. hygrophila, commonly known as the aquatic fig, is a variant of the Ficus genus that exhibits unique adaptations to aquatic environments.

It possesses a distinctive root system that allows it to thrive in waterlogged soils, often found in marshy or swampy areas. The plant has broad, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss and enhance photosynthesis in moist conditions. It is typically found in tropical and subtropical regions, where it plays a role in stabilizing wetland ecosystems.

Its botanical features make it an important species for ecological studies and wetland conservation efforts.
