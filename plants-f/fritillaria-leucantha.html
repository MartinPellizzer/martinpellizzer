Fritillaria leucantha

Fritillaria leucantha, commonly known as the white bellflower, is a perennial bulbous plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces showy, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow, often with a greenish tinge. The plant features a basal rosette of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a short, stout stem that supports its floral display. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Fritillaria leucantha is valued in horticulture for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance.
