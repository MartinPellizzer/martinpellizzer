Feuilleea strigillosa

Feuilleea strigillosa, commonly known as the velvetleaf fern, is a unique plant characterized by its dense, velvety foliage.

It features lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine, hair-like structures, giving it a soft, textured appearance. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments, such as forest floors or rocky crevices. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, often hidden among the foliage.

Feuilleea strigillosa is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in humid conditions.
