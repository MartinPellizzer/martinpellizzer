Ferula kokanica

Ferula kokanica, commonly known as the Kokanica wormwood, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of the Balkans.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes plants like parsley and carrots, and is characterized by its tall, branched stems and feathery leaves. The plant produces yellowish-green flowers in umbels, typical of the family, and its roots are often used in traditional medicine.

Ferula kokanica is known for its aromatic properties and has been used historically for its potential health benefits.
