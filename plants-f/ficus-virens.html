Ficus virens

Ficus virens, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are arranged in a distinctive weeping or cascading pattern. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by fig-like fruits, which are an important food source for various animals.

Ficus virens is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
