Fimbristylis quinquangularis subsp. pallescens

Fimbristylis quinquangularis subsp.

pallescens, commonly known as the pale-flowered sedge, is a herbaceous plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family. It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and narrow, linear leaves.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets, typical of sedges. It thrives in moist or wet habitats, such as marshes and wetlands, and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Its botanical features make it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
