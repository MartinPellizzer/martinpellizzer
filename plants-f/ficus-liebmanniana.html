Ficus liebmanniana

Ficus liebmanniana, commonly known as the Liebmann's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate in shape. It produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor.

Ficus liebmanniana is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and ease of care.
