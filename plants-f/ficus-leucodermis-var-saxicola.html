Ficus leucodermis var. saxicola

Ficus leucodermis var. saxicola, commonly known as the rock fig, is a unique variant of the ficus genus adapted to arid environments.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in dry conditions. The plant typically grows on rocky surfaces, showcasing its ability to thrive in challenging substrates. Its small, clustered fruits are an important food source for local wildlife.

Due to its specific habitat requirements, it is often found in rocky or limestone areas of southern Africa.
