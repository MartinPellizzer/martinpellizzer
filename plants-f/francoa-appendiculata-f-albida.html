Francoa appendiculata f. albida

Francoa appendiculata f. albida, commonly known as the white-fringed francoa, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branched stems and produces small, white to pale yellow flowers with distinctive fringed petals. Its foliage is typically gray-green, with narrow, linear leaves.

This variety is notable for its ornamental value and is often cultivated in gardens for its delicate appearance.
