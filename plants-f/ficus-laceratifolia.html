Ficus laceratifolia

Ficus laceratifolia, commonly known as the laceleaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its finely serrated, palmately lobed leaves that give it a delicate, lace-like appearance. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs, which are a key feature of the genus Ficus. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded outdoor gardens.

Its unique foliage makes it a popular choice for ornamental purposes.
