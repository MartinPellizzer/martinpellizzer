Ficus begoniifolia

Ficus begoniifolia, commonly known as the begoniifolia fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, silky hair. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically used in local cuisines.

Ficus begoniifolia is valued not only for its ornamental appeal but also for its ecological and culinary significance.
