Ficus leonensis

Ficus leonensis, commonly known as the golden fig or lemon fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Central and South America.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible figs, which are typically yellow or golden in color. Ficus leonensis thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value and edible fruits.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
