Ficus bopiana

Ficus bopiana, commonly known as the Bopian fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly in countries like Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are often clustered at the base of the stems. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus bopiana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.
