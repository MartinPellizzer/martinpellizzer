Froelichia floridana var. campestris

Froelichia floridana var. campestris, commonly known as the Florida flatleaf sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the southeastern United States.

It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and the edges of ponds. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves that are often arranged in a rosette, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers in clusters. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a preference for moist, sandy soils.

It plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various aquatic and terrestrial organisms.
