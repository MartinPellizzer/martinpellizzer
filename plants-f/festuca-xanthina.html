Festuca xanthina

Festuca xanthina, commonly known as yellow fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and bright yellowish-green foliage. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous slender, spikelet-like flowers. It is often found in open woodlands, meadows, and disturbed soils due to its adaptability to various environmental conditions.

Festuca xanthina is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
