Fagara guianensis

Fagara guianensis, commonly known as the Guianan fagara, is a member of the Rutaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and distinctive fruiting structures.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly in Guyana, Suriname, and northern Brazil. The plant typically grows as an understory shrub or small tree, reaching heights of up to 5 meters. Its leaves are compound, with elongated leaflets that have a rough, serrated margin.

The fruit is a small, round, green drupe that turns red when mature, containing a single seed enclosed in a hard, woody endocarp.
