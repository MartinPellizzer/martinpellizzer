Festuca maritima var. biuncialis

Festuca maritima var. biuncialis, commonly known as the coastal bentgrass, is a low-growing, mat-forming grass native to coastal regions.

It thrives in sandy, saline soils and is adapted to withstand salt spray and wind. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is often found in dune ecosystems, contributing to soil stabilization.

Its hardy nature makes it an important component of coastal vegetation.
