Filipendula ulmaria subsp. megalocarpa

Filipendula ulmaria subsp.

megalocarpa, commonly known as meadow sweet, is a perennial herb native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tall, upright stems and large, compound leaves with serrated edges.

The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers in late spring to early summer. Its fruits are distinctive, consisting of dry, elongated capsules that split open to release seeds.

It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and wetlands.
