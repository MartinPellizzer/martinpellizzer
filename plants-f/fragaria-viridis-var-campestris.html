Fragaria viridis var. campestris

Fragaria viridis var. campestris, commonly known as the wild strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible, sweet-tart berries. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and runners that spread along the ground. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in meadows and woodland edges.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and culinary purposes.
