Festuca ovina var. fontqueriana

Festuca ovina var. fontqueriana, commonly known as the Fontqueriana sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe.

It is characterized by its fine, wiry leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats. This variety is well adapted to cool, moist environments and is typically found in meadows and woodland edges. It has small, spike-like inflorescences that produce light blue to purple flowers.

The plant is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its hardiness and low maintenance requirements.
