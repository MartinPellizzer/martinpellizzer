Fritillaria graeca var. guicciardii

Fritillaria graeca var. guicciardii, commonly known as the Greek fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Greece and Turkey.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale yellow with intricate patterns. The plant belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its narrow, grass-like leaves. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade, often growing in rocky or limestone habitats.
