Festuca octoflora subsp. hirtella

Festuca octoflora subsp.

hirtella, commonly known as hairy bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to the western United States. It is characterized by its fine, tufted foliage and compact growth habit, often forming dense mats.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny blue to purple flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established. Festuca octoflora subsp.

hirtella is often used in native landscaping due to its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
