Festuca caroliniana

Festuca caroliniana, commonly known as Carolina bluegrass, is a perennial grass native to the eastern United States.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. The plant has fine, bluish-green leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in early summer. It is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.

Festuca caroliniana is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and provide habitat for wildlife.
