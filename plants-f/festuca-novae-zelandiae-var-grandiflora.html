Festuca novae-zelandiae var. grandiflora

Festuca novae-zelandiae var. grandiflora, commonly known as the New Zealand tussock grass, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and long, slender leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces showy, spike-like inflorescences that bear small, white or pale blue flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and alpine regions.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in native habitats.
