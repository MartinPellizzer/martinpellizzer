Freycinetia leptophylla

Freycinetia leptophylla, commonly known as the narrow-leaf pandanus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Pandanaceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its tall, slender stems and long, narrow leaves that are often used in traditional crafts. It produces small, fragrant flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible berries.

This plant is well adapted to coastal and wetland environments, making it an important component of tropical ecosystems.
