Fibigia membranacea

Fibigia membranacea, commonly known as the membranous fibigia, is a flowering plant native to the Andes region of South America.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in high-altitude grasslands and rocky areas. The plant features slender, branching stems covered in fine, membranous hairs. Its small, clustered flowers are usually white or pale blue, with five petals.

Fibigia membranacea is known for its adaptability to cold and arid conditions, making it a resilient species in its native habitat.
