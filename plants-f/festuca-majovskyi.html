Festuca majovskyi

Festuca majovskyi, commonly known as the Majovsky's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It typically grows in rocky and alpine environments, showcasing its adaptability to harsh conditions. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain tiny seeds. It is often found in high-altitude meadows and is valued for its ornamental appeal in horticulture.

Festuca majovskyi is also of interest to botanists due to its unique genetic traits and ecological significance.
