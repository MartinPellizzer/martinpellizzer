Filipendula multijuga var. dilutorosea

Filipendula multijuga var. dilutorosea, commonly known as the pale meadow sweet, is a herbaceous perennial plant native to parts of Europe and Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its tall, slender stems and clusters of white to pale pink flowers. The leaves are typically pinnately compound, with finely divided leaflets that give the plant a delicate appearance. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows and riverbanks, and is often found in areas with well-drained soil.

The plant is noted for its aromatic foliage and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
