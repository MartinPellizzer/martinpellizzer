Festuca hatico

Festuca hatico, commonly known as the Hatico fescue, is a perennial grass species native to certain regions of Asia.

It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and prefers partial to full sunlight. The plant has slender, tufted stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in a fine layer of hairs. Its flowering period occurs in late spring to early summer, producing small, spike-like inflorescences.

Festuca hatico is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in a variety of garden conditions.
