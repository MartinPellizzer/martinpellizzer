Frailea pygmaea subsp. altigibbera

Frailea pygmaea subsp.

altigibbera, commonly known as the rock rose cactus, is a small, spineless cactus native to southern Africa. It features a compact, globular body with a greenish-gray coloration and produces small, yellow flowers during the summer months.

The plant is adapted to arid environments, with thick, fleshy stems that store water. It thrives in well-drained soil and requires minimal watering, making it suitable for rock gardens or container cultivation.

Its unique appearance and low maintenance make it a popular choice for collectors and succulent enthusiasts.
