Filipendula ulmaria var. cinerea

Filipendula ulmaria var. cinerea, commonly known as white sweet clover, is a perennial herb native to Europe, Asia, and North America.

It is characterized by its fluffy, white flower heads that resemble small clouds, which bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant has heart-shaped, trifoliate leaves that are often covered in a fine, silvery down, giving it a distinctive appearance. It thrives in moist, open habitats such as meadows, marshes, and along stream banks.

The species is known for its aromatic leaves and is sometimes used in traditional herbal medicine.
