Festuca ovina subsp. firmulacea

Festuca ovina subsp.

firmulacea, commonly known as sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color.

The plant produces dense clusters of small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

Due to its hardy nature, it is used in landscaping and as a forage crop for livestock.
