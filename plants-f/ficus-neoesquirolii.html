Ficus neoesquirolii

Ficus neoesquirolii, commonly known as the Neoesquirolii fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are often variegated with shades of green and white. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig fruits.

This species is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its attractive appearance and relatively low maintenance requirements.
