Fumaria ifranensis

Fumaria ifranensis, commonly known as the Syrian glory-of-the-snow, is a flowering plant native to the Middle East and parts of Central Asia.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its delicate, nodding yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and prefers well-drained conditions. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a slightly hairy texture.

Fumaria ifranensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
