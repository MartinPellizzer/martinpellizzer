Ficus hartwegii

Ficus hartwegii, commonly known as the Hartweg's fig, is a tropical plant native to Central America and parts of Mexico.

It is a small to medium-sized tree that typically grows in humid, lowland forests. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered fruits that are edible for certain wildlife. Its root system is shallow, allowing it to thrive in well-drained soils.

Ficus hartwegii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food and shelter for various animals.
