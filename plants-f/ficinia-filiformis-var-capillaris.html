Ficinia filiformis var. capillaris

Ficinia filiformis var. capillaris, commonly known as the threadleaf fig, is a strikingly delicate plant characterized by its fine, hair-like leaves.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil, where it thrives in humid, shaded environments. The plant's leaves are elongated, narrow, and often curl slightly at the tips, giving it a unique, almost lace-like appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences, followed by small, edible fruits.

Due to its ornamental value and rarity, it is often cultivated in botanical gardens and by plant enthusiasts.
