Ficus harrisii

Ficus harrisii, commonly known as the Hawaiian fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Hawaiian Islands and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are usually green when immature and turn yellow or red when ripe. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and prefers well-drained soil.

Ficus harrisii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
