Fuchsia petiolaris var. typica

Fuchsia petiolaris var. typica, commonly known as the typical fuchsia petiolaris, is a species native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is a deciduous shrub characterized by its vibrant, pendulous flowers that range in color from deep red to purple. The plant has slender, wiry stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, humid environments and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens.

The flowers are typically visited by bees and hummingbirds, making it an important plant for pollinators.
