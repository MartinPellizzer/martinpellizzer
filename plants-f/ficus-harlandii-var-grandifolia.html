Ficus harlandii var. grandifolia

Ficus harlandii var. grandifolia, commonly known as the giant fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 cm in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
