Festuca salzmannii

Festuca salzmannii, commonly known as Salzmann's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences containing numerous tiny florets, which are often wind-pollinated. It thrives in rocky or arid environments, demonstrating a high degree of drought tolerance.

Festuca salzmannii is often used in ornamental horticulture for its hardy nature and aesthetic appeal.
