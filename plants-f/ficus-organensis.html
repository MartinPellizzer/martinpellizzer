Ficus organensis

Ficus organensis, commonly known as the Organ Mountains ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States, particularly the Organ Mountains in Nevada. This plant is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that help reduce water loss in its harsh environment. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences.

The fruit is a small, round berry that ripens to a yellowish color, providing food for local wildlife.
