Ficus ampana

Ficus ampana, commonly known as the Madagascar fig, is a species of fig tree native to Madagascar.

It is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree that typically grows up to 10 meters in height. The plant has dark green, glossy leaves that are heart-shaped and often have a waxy texture. It produces small, green figs that mature to a yellowish or reddish color.

Ficus ampana is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in tropical and subtropical regions.
