Ficus sinuosa var. integrifolia

Ficus sinuosa var. integrifolia, commonly known as the smooth-leaf fig, is a species native to Southeast Asia.

It is a tropical evergreen tree characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are typically smooth and entire. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
