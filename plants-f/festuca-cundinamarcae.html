Festuca cundinamarcae

Festuca cundinamarcae, commonly known as the Cundinamarca fescue, is a perennial grass native to South America, specifically Colombia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, erect stems that often bear small, spike-like inflorescences. The plant typically thrives in temperate climates and is adapted to well-drained soils, making it suitable for various ecological and horticultural applications. Its foliage is generally fine and grass-like, with a bluish-green coloration that adds aesthetic value to landscapes.

Festuca cundinamarcae is also valued for its potential in erosion control and as a forage plant for livestock.
