Frankenia laevis var. hispida

Frankenia laevis var. hispida, commonly known as the rough frankenia, is a flowering plant native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a perennial shrub characterized by its gray-green, hairy leaves and showy yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant thrives in sandy, well-drained soils and is often found in dune environments. Its small, rounded leaves are covered in fine, stiff hairs, giving it a rough texture.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to stabilize coastal soils.
