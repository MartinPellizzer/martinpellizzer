Fritillaria flavida

Fritillaria flavida, commonly known as the yellow fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central and southern Europe.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to orange, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically green with a bluish tint. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Fritillaria flavida is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
