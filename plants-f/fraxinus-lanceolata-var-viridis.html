Fraxinus lanceolata var. viridis

Fraxinus lanceolata var. viridis, commonly known as the green ash, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern and central United States.

It features compound leaves with elongated, lance-shaped leaflets that are typically a vibrant green. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in catkins, which are wind-pollinated. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This variety is valued for its adaptability and is often used in urban landscaping due to its tolerance of various soil conditions.
