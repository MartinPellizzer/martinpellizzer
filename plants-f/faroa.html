Faroa

Faroa, commonly known as the "wild fig" or "tree of life," is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Moraceae family, which includes figs and mulberries. The plant produces a unique fruit that is typically green when unripe and turns yellow or red when mature. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

Faroa thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in dense, lowland forests.
