Firkea pedunculata

Firkea pedunculata, commonly known as the clustered firkea, is a member of the Ericaceae family, typically found in temperate regions.

It is characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and small, needle-like leaves that are often covered in a waxy cuticle. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers, which are usually pink or white, and are pollinated by insects. Its roots form symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi, enhancing nutrient uptake in acidic soils.

Firkea pedunculata is often used in horticulture for its ornamental value and adaptability to shaded environments.
