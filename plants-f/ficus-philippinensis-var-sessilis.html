Ficus philippinensis var. sessilis

Ficus philippinensis var. sessilis, commonly known as the Philippine fig tree, is a species native to the Philippines.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 15 meters tall, characterized by its rough, fissured bark and broad, lobed leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, spherical fruits that are typically harvested when ripe. Its leaves are often used in traditional medicine for their purported health benefits.

The plant thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.
