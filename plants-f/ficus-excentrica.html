Ficus excentrica

Ficus excentrica, commonly known as the excentric fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. It produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature, and it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

Ficus excentrica thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.
