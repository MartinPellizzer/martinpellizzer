Ficus microcarpa var. eubracteata

Ficus microcarpa var. eubracteata, commonly known as the Banyan fig or Indian rubber tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and can grow into a large, sprawling tree with aerial roots. The plant has a unique ability to form a canopy of roots that can support its massive size. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in gardens.

Its adaptability and aesthetic appeal make it a popular choice for both ornamental and ecological purposes.
