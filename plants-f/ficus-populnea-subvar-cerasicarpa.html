Ficus populnea subvar. cerasicarpa

Ficus populnea subvar. cerasicarpa, commonly known as the small-leaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits that turn from green to yellow when ripe. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

The species is also valued for its ability to adapt to various soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
