Fimbristylis globulosa var. austrojaponica

Fimbristylis globulosa var. austrojaponica, commonly known as the Japanese globular sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to East Asia.

It typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats such as marshes, swamps, and wet meadows. The plant features slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered spikelets that are often green or reddish in color. It is characterized by its compact, globular inflorescence, which gives it its common name.

This species is valued in horticulture for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in moist environments.
