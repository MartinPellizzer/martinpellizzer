Festuca kingiana

Festuca kingiana, commonly known as King's grass or Sierra grass, is a perennial grass species native to the western United States.

It typically grows in high-altitude meadows and rocky areas, thriving in cool, moist conditions. The plant has fine, tufted leaves and produces small, spike-like flower clusters. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in native plant gardens.

Festuca kingiana is also important for wildlife, providing food and habitat for various insects and birds.
