Filipendula glaberrima f. alba

Filipendula glaberrima f. alba, commonly known as white meadowsweet, is a herbaceous perennial native to North America.

It is characterized by its tall, branched stems and clusters of small, white to pale yellow flowers that bloom in late summer. The plant has heart-shaped leaves with a serrated edge and a smooth, glabrous surface. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wet meadows, stream banks, and woodland edges.

Filipendula glaberrima f. alba is valued for its ornamental appeal and historical use in herbal medicine.
