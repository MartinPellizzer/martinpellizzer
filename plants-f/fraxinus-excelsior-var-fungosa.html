Fraxinus excelsior var. fungosa

Fraxinus excelsior var. fungosa, commonly known as the European ash, is a deciduous tree native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its opposite, simple leaves that are usually serrated and have a dark green upper surface. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by winged samaras that disperse its seeds. It typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and can reach heights of up to 30 meters.

This variety is part of the Oleaceae family and is known for its ecological importance in supporting a variety of wildlife.
