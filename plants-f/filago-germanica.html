Filago germanica

Filago germanica, commonly known as the German filaree, is an annual flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble small daisies. The plant features slender, branching stems and small, lance-shaped leaves that are covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are yellow and occur in clusters, blooming from late spring to early autumn.

Filago germanica is often found in disturbed soils, such as roadsides and fields, and is known for its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
