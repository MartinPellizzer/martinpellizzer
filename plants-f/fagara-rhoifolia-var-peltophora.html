Fagara rhoifolia var. peltophora

Fagara rhoifolia var. peltophora, commonly known as the Mexican evergreen holly, is a species native to Mexico and parts of Central America.

It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that typically grows in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant features elliptical, dark green leaves with a glossy surface and produces small, white flowers followed by red, fleshy berries. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic qualities.

The plant thrives in well-drained soil and partial to full shade, making it suitable for shaded garden environments.
