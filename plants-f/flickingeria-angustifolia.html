Flickingeria angustifolia

Flickingeria angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leafed flickingeria, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to specific regions of South America.

It features slender, lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs, contributing to its distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale purple, which bloom during the spring and early summer. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded environments.

Due to its limited distribution and specific habitat requirements, it is considered a rare and ecologically significant species.
