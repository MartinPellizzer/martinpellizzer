Freycinetia pallida

Freycinetia pallida, commonly known as the white-flowered pandanus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia and the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the family Pandanaceae and is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves and clustered inflorescences. The plant produces small, white, fragrant flowers that are often used in traditional crafts and cooking. Its roots and leaves are also valued for their aromatic properties and medicinal uses.

Freycinetia pallida thrives in humid, tropical climates and is often found in coastal or lowland areas.
