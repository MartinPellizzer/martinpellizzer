Ficus rubescens

Ficus rubescens, commonly known as the red fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Malaysia and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a reddish tint on the underside. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible figs.

Ficus rubescens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in indoor environments.
