Fraxinus excelsior var. leucocarpa

Fraxinus excelsior var. leucocarpa, commonly known as the white-fruited ash, is a variant of the common ash tree native to Europe.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and heart-shaped leaves with a serrated margin. The most distinctive feature is its white, fleshy fruits that contrast with the green foliage. This variety is typically found in moist, shaded woodlands and along stream banks.

It plays an important ecological role by providing habitat and food for various wildlife species.
