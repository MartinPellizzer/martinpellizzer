Fagonia sinaica var. minuristipula

Fagonia sinaica var. minuristipula, commonly known as the Sinai fagonia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of the Middle East.

It is characterized by its small, spine-like leaves and yellow flowers that bloom in summer. The plant is adapted to harsh, dry environments, with deep roots that help it survive in poor soil conditions. It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is often found in rocky or sandy habitats.

Its unique morphology makes it an important species for studying desert flora.
