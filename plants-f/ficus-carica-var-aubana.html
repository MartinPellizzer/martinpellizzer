Ficus carica var. aubana

Ficus carica var. aubana, commonly known as the Auban fig, is a variety of the common fig tree native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and produces small, edible fruits that are typically harvested when they are fully ripe. The plant thrives in warm, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its fruit, which has a sweet and soft texture.

The Auban fig is also valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
