Fuchsia tuberosa var. inflata

Fuchsia tuberosa var. inflata, commonly known as the giant fuchsia, is a striking perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its large, showy flowers that range in color from deep purple to bright red, often with a contrasting white or yellow throat. The plant produces fleshy, tuberous roots that store water and nutrients, allowing it to thrive in well-drained, sunny conditions. Its foliage consists of dark green, oval-shaped leaves that are often covered in a fine, fuzzy texture.

This species is prized in horticulture for its ornamental value and ability to attract pollinators like hummingbirds and bees.
