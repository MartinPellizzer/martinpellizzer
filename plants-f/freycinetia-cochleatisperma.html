Freycinetia cochleatisperma

Freycinetia cochleatisperma, commonly known as the spiral-fruit pandanus, is a tropical plant native to parts of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive spiral arrangement of leaves around its stem. The plant produces large, fleshy fruits that are typically orange or yellow in color. Its leaves are long, narrow, and often used in traditional crafts and as a food wrapping in some cultures.

The species is valued for both its ornamental appearance and its ecological role in coastal and wetland habitats.
