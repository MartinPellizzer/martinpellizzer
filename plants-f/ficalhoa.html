Ficalhoa

Ficalhoa, commonly known as the sacred lotus of the Andes, is a striking plant native to the high-altitude regions of South America.

It features large, showy flowers that can reach up to 30 centimeters in diameter, with vibrant colors ranging from white to deep pink. The plant has broad, waxy leaves that help it thrive in cool, moist environments. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine for their purported healing properties.

Ficalhoa is also valued for its cultural significance among indigenous communities.
