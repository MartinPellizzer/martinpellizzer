Frangula crenata var. acuminatifolia

Frangula crenata var. acuminatifolia, commonly known as the sharp-leaved buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southeastern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, elliptical leaves that have a distinctive sharp point, giving it its common name. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers in clusters, followed by dark blue berries that are a food source for various birds and wildlife. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks.

The species is noted for its hardy nature and ability to tolerate a range of environmental conditions.
