Ficus cavronii

Ficus cavronii, commonly known as the Cavron's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered fig fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

The tree can grow up to several meters tall, with a dense canopy that provides shade in its natural habitat.
