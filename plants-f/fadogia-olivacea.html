Fadogia olivacea

Fadogia olivacea, commonly known as African velvet bean, is a leguminous plant native to tropical Africa.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and clusters of small, yellowish-white flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain kidney-shaped seeds with a distinct, aromatic scent. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in savannas and woodland areas.

Fadogia olivacea is valued for its seeds, which are used in traditional medicine and as a source of protein.
