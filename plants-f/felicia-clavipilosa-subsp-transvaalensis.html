Felicia clavipilosa subsp. transvaalensis

Felicia clavipilosa subsp.

transvaalensis, commonly known as the Transvaal daisy, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its compact, low-growing habit and produces clusters of small, daisy-like flowers in shades of yellow and white.

The plant has hairy, lance-shaped leaves and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens. It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and coastal areas.

The species is known for its resilience and ability to thrive in dry conditions.
