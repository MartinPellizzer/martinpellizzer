Ficus chlorocarpa

Ficus chlorocarpa, commonly known as the green-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including parts of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often clustered at the ends of its branches. Ficus chlorocarpa is typically grown as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.

It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by fig-like fruits that are edible but not commonly consumed by humans.
