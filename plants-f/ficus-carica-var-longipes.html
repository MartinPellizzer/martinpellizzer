Ficus carica var. longipes

Ficus carica var. longipes, commonly known as the long-stemmed fig, is a variant of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its elongated stems and clustered leaves, which give it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, green figs that mature to a deep purple or black color. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is often cultivated for its ornamental value and unique fruiting characteristics.
