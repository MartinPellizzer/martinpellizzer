Fagopyrum triangulare

Fagopyrum triangulare, commonly known as the three-angled buckwheat, is a herbaceous plant native to Asia.

It is characterized by its triangular leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in clusters. The plant produces triangular, three-lobed seeds that are often used in traditional medicine. It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in moist, shaded environments.

Its botanical features make it a distinctive member of the Polygonaceae family.
