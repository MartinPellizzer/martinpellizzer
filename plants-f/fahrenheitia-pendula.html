Fahrenheitia pendula

Fahrenheitia pendula, commonly known as the hanging fever plant, is a unique species characterized by its pendulous growth habit.

It features elongated, drooping stems that bear clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant is native to tropical regions and thrives in warm, humid environments. Its leaves are typically narrow and lance-shaped, with a glossy surface.

Due to its striking appearance, it is often cultivated as an ornamental plant in gardens and greenhouses.
