Festuca agustini

Festuca agustini, commonly known as Augustin's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in grasslands and woodland margins. The plant has slender, tufted stems and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. Its leaves are narrow and often have a bluish-green hue.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to thrive in shaded environments.
