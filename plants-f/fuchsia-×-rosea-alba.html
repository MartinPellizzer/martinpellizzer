Fuchsia × rosea-alba

Fuchsia × rosea-alba, commonly known as the white-flowered fuchsia, is a hybrid plant resulting from the cross between Fuchsia × rosea and Fuchsia × alba.

It is characterized by its striking white or pale pink flowers, which contrast beautifully with its glossy, dark green foliage. The plant is native to South America and is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and as a houseplant. It thrives in cool, moist environments and requires partial to full shade for optimal growth.

Fuchsia × rosea-alba is also valued for its ability to attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees.
