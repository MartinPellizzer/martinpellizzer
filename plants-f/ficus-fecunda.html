Ficus fecunda

Ficus fecunda, commonly known as the noble fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically harvested when they turn a deep purple. Ficus fecunda thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-draining soil to flourish.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for both indoor and outdoor gardening.
