Ficus populnea subvar. crassipetiolata

Ficus populnea subvar. crassipetiolata, commonly known as the broad-petioled fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves with thick, broad petioles. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its foliage makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
