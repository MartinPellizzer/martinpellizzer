Ficus stellulata var. glabrescens

Ficus stellulata var. glabrescens, commonly known as the smooth-leaved fig tree, is a tropical plant characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically smooth to the touch.

It is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in warm, humid climates with indirect sunlight. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. Its compact growth habit makes it suitable for indoor cultivation, though it requires well-draining soil and regular watering.

This variety is prized for its ornamental value and ability to adapt to various indoor environments.
