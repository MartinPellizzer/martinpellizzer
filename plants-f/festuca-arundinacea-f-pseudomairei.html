Festuca arundinacea f. pseudomairei

Festuca arundinacea f. pseudomairei, commonly known as the purple false oatgrass, is a cool-season grass species native to Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, tufted growth habit and its distinctive purple to blue-green foliage. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences that bear small, two-lobed flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often used in landscaping and erosion control due to its hardiness.

This variety is particularly valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
