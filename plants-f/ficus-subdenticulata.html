Ficus subdenticulata

Ficus subdenticulata, commonly known as the rough-leaved fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped, rough-textured leaves that have a distinctive serrated edge. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus subdenticulata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
