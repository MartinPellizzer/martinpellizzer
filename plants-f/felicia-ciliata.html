Felicia ciliata

Felicia ciliata, commonly known as the silverleaf fleur-de-lis, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its compact, bushy growth habit and its clusters of bright yellow, daisy-like flowers. The plant features finely hairy leaves that give it a silvery appearance, especially when backlit. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and containers.

Felicia ciliata is also known for its ability to attract pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
