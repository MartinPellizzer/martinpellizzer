Ficus montana var. purpurascens

Ficus montana var. purpurascens, commonly known as the purple-leaf ficus, is a compact, evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It features small, waxy, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green with a striking purple or reddish tinge on the underside. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers followed by small, round fruits. It thrives in bright, indirect light and prefers well-draining soil with moderate humidity.

Due to its ornamental foliage, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
