Ficus pubigera var. maliformis

Ficus pubigera var. maliformis, commonly known as the Malifomis Ficus, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically broad and heart-shaped. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by oval-shaped fruits that are edible. It thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect light and well-draining soil.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
