Ficus lasiocarpa

Ficus lasiocarpa, commonly known as the rough-leaved fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its rough, hairy leaves and small, clustered fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, often growing in lowland forests or along riverbanks.

Ficus lasiocarpa is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its unique foliage.
