Ficus destruens

Ficus destruens, commonly known as the "destroying fig," is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits.

Ficus destruens is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
