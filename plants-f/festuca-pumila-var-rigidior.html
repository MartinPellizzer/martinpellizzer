Festuca pumila var. rigidior

Festuca pumila var. rigidior, commonly known as rigid bentgrass, is a low-growing, mat-forming grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks. The plant has narrow, upright blades and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that are characteristic of the Festuca genus. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping for erosion control and ground cover.

Its botanical features make it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.
