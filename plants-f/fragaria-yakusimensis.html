Fragaria yakusimensis

Fragaria yakusimensis, commonly known as the Yakushima strawberry, is a rare species of wild strawberry native to the island of Yakushima in Japan.

It is characterized by its small, edible berries that are typically red or yellow in color. The plant has trifoliate leaves with serrated edges and grows in shaded, humid environments. It is often found in the understory of subtropical forests.

Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a rare and endangered species.
