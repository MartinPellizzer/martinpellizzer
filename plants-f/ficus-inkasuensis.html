Ficus inkasuensis

Ficus inkasuensis, commonly known as the Inkasuensis fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits. Ficus inkasuensis thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for both ecological and horticultural purposes.
