Fragaria viridis subsp. campestris

Fragaria viridis subsp.

campestris, commonly known as the field strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, edible, yellowish-white fruits that mature in summer.

The plant typically grows in open, disturbed habitats such as meadows and roadsides. It has a shallow root system and spreads primarily through stolons.

Its botanical features make it a common weed in agricultural areas.
