Firmiana colorata var. subglabra

Firmiana colorata var. subglabra, commonly known as the smooth-flowered flame tree, is a deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are smooth and glossy on both sides. The tree produces vibrant orange to red flowers in clusters, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

This species is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in tropical and subtropical climates.
