Freycinetia separata

Freycinetia separata, commonly known as the red-veined cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant features a stout, unbranched stem that is often covered in a fibrous, spongy layer. Its leaves are pinnate, with a striking red or purple midrib that contrasts with the green foliage.

Freycinetia separata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its distinctive appearance.
