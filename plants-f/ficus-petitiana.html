Ficus petitiana

Ficus petitiana, commonly known as the Petitiana fig, is a small evergreen tree native to Madagascar.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are often clustered at the ends of the branches. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and requires well-drained soil and partial shade.

Due to its unique appearance and ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated as a decorative plant in gardens and indoor spaces.
