Festuca glauca var. gracillior

Festuca glauca var. gracillior, commonly known as bluegrass meadowgrass, is a flowering plant native to the temperate regions of Europe and western Asia.

It is a perennial grass that typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows and rocky slopes. The plant features slender, upright stems with narrow, grass-like leaves that are often bluish-green in color. Its inflorescence consists of a panicle of small, spikelet-like flowers, which are usually green to purplish in hue.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
