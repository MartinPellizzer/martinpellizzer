Freycinetia chartacea

Freycinetia chartacea, commonly known as the paper fern or flat-leaf cycad, is a unique plant native to the island of New Caledonia.

It is a member of the Cycadaceae family and is characterized by its large, pinnately compound leaves that are often used for wrapping food. The plant has a short, stout trunk with a crown of foliage that can reach several meters in height. Its botanical features include a distinctive, fibrous outer layer and a central, woody core.

Freycinetia chartacea is also notable for its ability to thrive in coastal and sandy soils, making it an important component of the local flora.
