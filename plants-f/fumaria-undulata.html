Fumaria undulata

Fumaria undulata, commonly known as the undulating smokeplant, is a flowering plant belonging to the Fumariaceae family.

It is characterized by its delicate, pendulous flowers that resemble tiny smoke curls, which bloom in shades of yellow and orange. The plant has slender, branching stems and lance-shaped leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. Native to parts of Europe and Asia, it thrives in moist, shaded environments.

Its unique appearance and growth habits make it a notable species in botanical studies and horticulture.
