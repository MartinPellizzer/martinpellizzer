Fragaria collina var. subpinnata

Fragaria collina var. subpinnata, commonly known as the alpine strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to mountainous regions of Europe and Asia.

It produces small, edible berries that are typically red in color and have a sweet, fragrant flavor. The plant has trifoliate leaves with a serrated edge, and its stems are covered in fine hairs. It thrives in cool, moist environments and is often found in alpine meadows and rocky soils.

This variety is valued for both its ornamental appeal and its use in local cuisines.
