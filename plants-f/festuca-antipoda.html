Festuca antipoda

Festuca antipoda, commonly known as Antarctic grass, is a flowering plant native to the cold regions of the Southern Hemisphere.

It is a low-growing perennial grass that thrives in harsh, alpine environments. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences. It is adapted to survive in extreme conditions with minimal nutrients and low temperatures.

Festuca antipoda plays a vital role in stabilizing soil and supporting local ecosystems in its native habitat.
