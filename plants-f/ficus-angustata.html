Ficus angustata

Ficus angustata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its narrow, elongated leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically small and sweet. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Ficus angustata is also valued for its ability to adapt to various soil types and its relatively low maintenance requirements.
