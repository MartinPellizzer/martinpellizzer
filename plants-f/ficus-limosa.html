Ficus limosa

Ficus limosa, commonly known as the weeping fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its drooping branches and glossy, dark green leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant has a smooth, greyish bark and produces small, inconspicuous greenish-white flowers. Its fruit is a small, round capsule that contains seeds, which are dispersed by wind.

Ficus limosa is widely cultivated as a decorative plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to indoor conditions.
