Festuca ovina f. vulgaris

Festuca ovina f. vulgaris, commonly known as sheep's fescue, is a cool-season grass that thrives in temperate climates.

It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and tufted growth habit, making it a popular choice for ornamental and turfgrass applications. This plant is typically found in well-drained soils and can tolerate a range of environmental conditions, including drought and light shade. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that contain numerous tiny florets, contributing to its distinctive appearance.

Festuca ovina f. vulgaris is also valued for its ability to stabilize soil and prevent erosion in various landscapes.
