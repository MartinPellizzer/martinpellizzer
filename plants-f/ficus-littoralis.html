Ficus littoralis

Ficus littoralis, commonly known as the coastal fig tree, is a species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a deciduous tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its smooth, grey bark and large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant thrives in coastal environments, tolerating salt spray and sandy soils. Its fruit, a small green fig, is edible and often consumed by both humans and animals.

The tree plays an important ecological role in stabilizing dunes and providing habitat for various wildlife species.
