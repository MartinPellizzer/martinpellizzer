Ficus aureobrunnea

Ficus aureobrunnea, commonly known as the golden-brown fig, is a species of flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that often have a reddish or brownish tint on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are typically clustered in inflorescences. Its fruit is a small, round fig that turns from green to yellow as it matures.

Ficus aureobrunnea is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
