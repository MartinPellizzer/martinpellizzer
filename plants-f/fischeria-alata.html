Fischeria alata

Fischeria alata, commonly known as the silver hair veldtgrass, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and narrow, grass-like leaves that are often covered in fine, silvery hairs. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically green or pale yellow, arranged in loose clusters. Fischeria alata thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in open grasslands and rocky areas.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its distinctive foliage.
