Fraxinus excelsior f. verrucosa

Fraxinus excelsior f. verrucosa, commonly known as the warty ash, is a horticultural form of the common ash.

It is characterized by its distinctive warty or knobby bark, which gives it a unique appearance. The leaves are typically opposite, simple, and ovate with serrated edges. This cultivar is often used in gardens for its ornamental value and hardy nature.

It prefers well-drained soil and full sun, making it suitable for a variety of landscapes.
