Festuca californica subsp. hitchcockiana

Festuca californica subsp.

hitchcockiana, commonly known as Hitchcock's bluegrass, is a native perennial grass of California. It is characterized by its fine, blue-green foliage and produces slender, spike-like inflorescences.

This subspecies thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it suitable for rock gardens and dry landscapes. It is valued for its drought tolerance and ability to form dense, low-growing mats.

The plant is also an important nectar source for native pollinators.
