Fabiana imbricata

Fabiana imbricata, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, showy flowers that range in color from white to pink and red. The plant typically grows in rocky or mountainous regions, thriving in well-drained soils. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and covered in a fine, white pubescence.

Fabiana imbricata is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adaptable to various growing conditions.
