Fascicularia bicolor subsp. bicolor

Fascicularia bicolor subsp.

bicolor, commonly known as the silver sword, is a striking plant native to South America, particularly Argentina. It is a rosette-forming perennial that produces strikingly colored inflorescences with white and pink flowers.

The plant features narrow, silver-blue leaves that are densely packed in a compact rosette. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and drought tolerance make it a distinctive addition to botanical collections.
