Ficus endospermifolia

Ficus endospermifolia, commonly known as the white fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are often variegated with white or cream markings. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible, pear-shaped fruits. Its roots are fibrous and can spread extensively, making it a hardy species suitable for indoor cultivation.

The plant thrives in warm, humid environments with indirect sunlight and well-draining soil.
