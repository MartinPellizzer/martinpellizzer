Ferulago taurica

Ferulago taurica, commonly known as Tauric Ferula, is a perennial herb native to the Caucasus and southern Russia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stems and large, umbrella-shaped inflorescences that bear yellowish-green flowers. The plant produces a milky sap that has been traditionally used in folk medicine. Its leaves are lance-shaped and have a strong, aromatic scent.

Ferulago taurica is often found in rocky or dry habitats and is valued for its medicinal and ornamental qualities.
