Fritillaria macrandra

Fritillaria macrandra, commonly known as the noble crown, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to deep purple, often with contrasting patterns. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and produces a single stem that supports its showy blooms. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Fritillaria macrandra is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its unique floral display.
