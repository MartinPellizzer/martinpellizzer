Ficus opposita

Ficus opposita, commonly known as the opposite-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its opposite leaf arrangement, where leaves grow directly across from each other on the stem. Its leaves are typically broad and heart-shaped, with a smooth or slightly serrated edge.

Ficus opposita is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
