Fuchsia arborescens var. megalantha

Fuchsia arborescens var. megalantha, commonly known as the giant tree fuchsia, is a striking plant native to South America, particularly Ecuador.

It is a woody shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters tall, characterized by its large, showy flowers. The flowers are typically pink or purple, with long, tubular corollas that bloom in clusters. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in cloud forests.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in tropical gardens.
