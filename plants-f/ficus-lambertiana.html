Ficus lambertiana

Ficus lambertiana, commonly known as the Lambert's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and can grow as a tree or shrub. It produces small, greenish-white flowers that are followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus lambertiana is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is relatively easy to care for in suitable climates.
