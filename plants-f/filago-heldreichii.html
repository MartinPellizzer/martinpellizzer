Filago heldreichii

Filago heldreichii, commonly known as the Balkan filago, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Balkan Peninsula.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows in rocky or semi-arid habitats, showcasing its adaptability to harsh environments. Its leaves are linear to lanceolate, with hairy surfaces that help reduce water loss.

Filago heldreichii produces small yellow flowers arranged in a composite head, which attracts pollinators such as bees and butterflies.
