Ficus polita subsp. brevipedunculata

Ficus polita subsp.

brevipedunculata, commonly known as the short-stalked fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family. It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Malaysia and Indonesia.

This plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. It produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when mature.

The plant is often cultivated as an ornamental due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
