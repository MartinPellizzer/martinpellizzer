Festuca cynosuroides

Festuca cynosuroides, commonly known as the silvergrass or creeping fescue, is a perennial grass species native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, silver-blue foliage and forms dense, mat-like growth patterns. The plant typically grows in well-drained soils and thrives in full sun to partial shade. Its rhizomatous and stoloniferous growth habits allow it to spread aggressively, making it a common weed in disturbed areas.

Festuca cynosuroides is also valued for its ornamental appeal in gardens and for its ability to stabilize soil in erosion-prone regions.
