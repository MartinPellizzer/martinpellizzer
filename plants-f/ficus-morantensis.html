Ficus morantensis

Ficus morantensis, commonly known as the Morant's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the Lesser Antilles. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish flowers that develop into edible figs. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Ficus morantensis is also valued for its ability to adapt to various soil types and its resilience to environmental stress.
