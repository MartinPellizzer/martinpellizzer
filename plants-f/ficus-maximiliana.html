Ficus maximiliana

Ficus maximiliana, commonly known as the Philippine fig tree, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its simple, alternate, ovate leaves that are usually smooth and glossy. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits that are typically clustered in syconia. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Ficus maximiliana is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in its native habitat.
