Festuca hallii

Festuca hallii, commonly known as Hall's fescue, is a perennial grass native to the United States and Mexico.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and silver-green foliage. The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as prairies and rocky slopes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers.

Festuca hallii is valued for its ornamental appeal and its ability to thrive in poor soil conditions.
