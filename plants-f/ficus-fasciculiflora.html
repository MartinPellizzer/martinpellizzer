Ficus fasciculiflora

Ficus fasciculiflora, commonly known as the clustered fig.

It is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia, characterized by its dense, rounded crown and smooth, grayish bark. The plant has opposite, ovate leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when ripe.

Ficus fasciculiflora is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
