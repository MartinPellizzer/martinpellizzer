Fraxinus anomala var. lowellii

Fraxinus anomala var. lowellii, commonly known as the lowellii ash, is a variant of the ash tree native to the western United States.

It is a deciduous tree characterized by its slender, often multi-stemmed form and smooth, gray bark. The leaves are typically opposite, ovate to lanceolate, with a serrated margin and a glossy green upper surface. It produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clusters, followed by samaras that are dispersed by wind.

This plant is adapted to dry, rocky soils and is often found in chaparral and woodland habitats.
