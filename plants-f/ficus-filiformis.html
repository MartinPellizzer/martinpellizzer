Ficus filiformis

Ficus filiformis, commonly known as the threadleaf fig, is a unique species native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, thread-like leaves that are typically less than 1 inch in length. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible figs that are small and often overlooked. It thrives in warm, humid environments and prefers well-draining soil.

Due to its distinctive appearance and adaptability, it is a popular choice for indoor and outdoor cultivation.
