Fargesia maluo

Fargesia maluo, commonly known as the Yunnan evergreen bamboo, is a species native to the mountainous regions of southwestern China.

It is a clump-forming bamboo that typically grows to a height of 2 to 5 meters, with slender, hollow stems that are often covered in a whitish or grayish bloom. The plant produces small, clustered inflorescences with pale yellow to white flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. Its leaves are lance-shaped, glossy, and have a pleasant, aromatic scent.

Fargesia maluo is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its elegant appearance and adaptability to various soil types.
