Fimbristylis quinquangularis f. abludens

Fimbristylis quinquangularis f. abludens, commonly known as the narrow-leaved flat sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and small, inconspicuous flowers arranged in spikelets. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread and form dense stands.

The species is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, contributing to the ecological diversity of wetland ecosystems.
