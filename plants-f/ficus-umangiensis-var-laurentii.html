Ficus umangiensis var. laurentii

Ficus umangiensis var. laurentii, commonly known as the Laurentii fig, is a rare and unique variant of the Ficus genus native to specific regions in Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy leaves that are often a deep green with a subtle sheen, and it produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible fig fruits. The plant is typically evergreen, with a compact growth habit that makes it suitable for indoor cultivation.

Due to its limited natural range and specific environmental requirements, it is a sought-after specimen among plant enthusiasts and botanists.
