Ficus rubrocuspidata

Ficus rubrocuspidata, commonly known as the red-spined fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, particularly found in regions of Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves with prominent reddish veins, which give it its distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish flowers that develop into edible fig fruits.

Ficus rubrocuspidata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
