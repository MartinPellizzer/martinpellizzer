Faroa acaulis

Faroa acaulis, commonly known as the rock dandelion, is a perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its basal rosette of fleshy, gray-green leaves that are covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers that bloom in early spring, followed by fluffy seed heads resembling dandelion seeds. It thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in dry, sunny habitats.

Faroa acaulis is adapted to withstand drought and is considered a resilient plant in its native environment.
