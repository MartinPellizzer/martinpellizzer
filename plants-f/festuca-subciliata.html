Festuca subciliata

Festuca subciliata, commonly known as slender fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. Festuca subciliata is often found in rocky or alpine habitats, where it forms dense mats that help prevent soil erosion.

Due to its hardiness and adaptability, it is occasionally cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
