Fuchsia microphylla subsp. hidalgensis

Fuchsia microphylla subsp.

hidalgensis, commonly known as the Hidalgo fuchsia, is a compact shrub native to Mexico. It features small, ovate leaves that are typically hairy and have a velvety texture.

The plant produces showy, pendulous flowers in shades of pink to purple, which bloom in early spring. It thrives in well-drained soil and partial shade, making it suitable for rock gardens or containers.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is relatively easy to cultivate in moderate climates.
