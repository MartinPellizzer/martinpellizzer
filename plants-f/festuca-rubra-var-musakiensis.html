Festuca rubra var. musakiensis

Festuca rubra var. musakiensis, commonly known as the Japanese red fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its fine, dense foliage and compact growth habit, making it well-suited for ornamental and ecological landscaping. The plant typically produces small, spike-like inflorescences with purple or reddish flowers, which bloom in late spring to early summer. It prefers well-drained soils and partial to full sunlight, thriving in cooler climates.

Its resilience and aesthetic appeal make it a popular choice for gardeners seeking low-maintenance, drought-tolerant ground cover.
