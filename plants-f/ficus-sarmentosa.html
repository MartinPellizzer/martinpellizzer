Ficus sarmentosa

Ficus sarmentosa, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a tropical evergreen vine native to Southeast Asia.

It features heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green with a smooth texture. The plant produces small, pale green flowers followed by spherical, yellowish fruits. It thrives in humid environments and is often used as a ground cover or climbing plant.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
