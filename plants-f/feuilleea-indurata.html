Feuilleea indurata

Feuilleea indurata, commonly known as the hard-leaved fern, is a unique plant characterized by its thick, leathery foliage.

Its leaves are typically broad and rigid, providing a distinctive texture compared to other fern species. The plant thrives in moist, shaded environments, often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Feuilleea indurata has a rhizomatous growth habit, allowing it to spread and form dense clusters.

Its botanical features make it an interesting subject for both horticultural study and ecological research.
