Ficus carica var. gorgonea

Ficus carica var. gorgonea, commonly known as the Gorgonea fig, is a variant of the common fig tree.

It is characterized by its large, broad leaves that are often a deep green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, green figs that mature to a deep purple or black color, typically harvested when they are fully ripe. It thrives in warm, temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and its edible fruits, which have a sweet, honey-like flavor.
