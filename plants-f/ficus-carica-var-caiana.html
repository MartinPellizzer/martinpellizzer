Ficus carica var. caiana

Ficus carica var. caiana, commonly known as the Smyrna fig, is a cultivar of the common fig tree native to the eastern Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its small, sweet, and edible fruits that ripen in late summer. The plant typically grows as a small to medium-sized tree with smooth, gray bark and broad, lobed leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is valued for its fruit quality and is often cultivated for both commercial and ornamental purposes.
