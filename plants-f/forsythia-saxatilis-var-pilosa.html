Forsythia saxatilis var. pilosa

Forsythia saxatilis var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy rock forsythia, is a shrub native to China.

It is characterized by its dense, hairy leaves and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant thrives in rocky, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to tolerate harsh environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a unique addition to rock gardens and alpine landscapes.
