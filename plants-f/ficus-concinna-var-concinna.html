Ficus concinna var. concinna

Ficus concinna var. concinna, commonly known as the glossy fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, waxy, dark green leaves that are often glossy and have a leathery texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically yellow or green in color. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden areas.

Ficus concinna var. concinna is valued for its ornamental appeal and air-purifying qualities.
