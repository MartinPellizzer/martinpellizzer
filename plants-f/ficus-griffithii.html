Ficus griffithii

Ficus griffithii, commonly known as the Indian rubber tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Indian subcontinent and is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant is known for its ability to produce a milky sap, which was historically used to make rubber. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Ficus griffithii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability.
