Faba minor

Faba minor, commonly known as the lesser vetch, is a flowering plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is a member of the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and yellow flowers. The plant produces small, elongated pods that contain several seeds. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of habitats, including grasslands and wastelands.

Faba minor plays a role in nitrogen fixation, contributing to soil fertility.
