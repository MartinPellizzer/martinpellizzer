Ficus burdigalensis

Ficus burdigalensis, commonly known as the Burdige fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate to lanceolate in shape. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by edible figs, which are a key feature of its botanical profile. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus burdigalensis is relatively low-maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
