Festuca longiauriculata

Festuca longiauriculata, commonly known as the long-ear fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and distinctive, elongated auricles that give it its common name. This plant typically grows in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along woodland edges. Festuca longiauriculata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Its ecological importance includes providing habitat and food for various insects and small mammals.
