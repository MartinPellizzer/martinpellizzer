Fritillaria oxypetala

Fritillaria oxypetala, commonly known as the narrow-leaved fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It produces striking, bell-shaped flowers that are typically orange or reddish in color, often with distinctive markings. The plant features narrow, grass-like leaves and is adapted to grow in well-drained, rocky soils. It blooms in spring and is often found in alpine or subalpine habitats.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and protected areas.
