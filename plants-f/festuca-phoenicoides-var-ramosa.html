Festuca phoenicoides var. ramosa

Festuca phoenicoides var. ramosa, commonly known as purple moorgrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and dense, dark green foliage that turns reddish-purple in the fall. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in moist, acidic soils and is often found in heathlands and bogs.

Festuca phoenicoides var. ramosa is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in supporting local wildlife.
