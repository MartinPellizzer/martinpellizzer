Ferocactus wislizeni var. herrerae

Ferocactus wislizeni var. herrerae, commonly known as the Herrera's barrel cactus, is a species native to the arid regions of the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its thick, ribbed stems that store water, allowing it to thrive in harsh desert environments. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers during the spring and summer months, adding a striking contrast to its green foliage. Its spines are typically short and cylindrical, providing protection from herbivores and reducing water loss.

This variety is well adapted to full sun and well-draining soils, making it a popular choice for xeriscaping in drought-prone areas.
