Festuca dumetorum var. nemorum

Festuca dumetorum var. nemorum, commonly known as wood sedge, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as woodlands and stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to form dense mats, which helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

The species is often found in association with other shade-tolerant flora in forest understories.
