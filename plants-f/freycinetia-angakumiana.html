Freycinetia angakumiana

Freycinetia angakumiana, commonly known as the red-veined cycad, is a unique member of the Cycadaceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in countries like Indonesia and Papua New Guinea. The plant features a stout, unbranched trunk with a fibrous, spongy bark. Its leaves are large, pinnately compound, and have a distinctive reddish vein pattern that gives it its common name.

This plant is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is also of interest in botanical research due to its evolutionary significance.
