Ficus inaequifolia

Ficus inaequifolia, commonly known as the unequal-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its distinctive, asymmetrical leaves that vary in shape and size along the stem. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig fruits.

Ficus inaequifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its unique foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
