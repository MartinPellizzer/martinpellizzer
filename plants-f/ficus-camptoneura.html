Ficus camptoneura

Ficus camptoneura, commonly known as the twisted fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its uniquely twisted and contorted trunk, which gives it a distinctive and ornamental appearance. Its leaves are typically large, glossy, and arranged oppositely along the stems.

Ficus camptoneura is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens for its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
