Ficus carica var. pulchella

Ficus carica var. pulchella, commonly known as the Persian fig, is a variety of the common fig tree native to the Middle East and parts of southern Europe.

It is characterized by its broad, lobed leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into edible fruits. The plant thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value as well as for its fruit, which is prized for its sweet flavor.

The variety is known for its compact growth habit, making it suitable for smaller garden spaces.
