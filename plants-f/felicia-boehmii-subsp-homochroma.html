Felicia boehmii subsp. homochroma

Felicia boehmii subsp.

homochroma, commonly known as the yellow daisy or golden marguerite, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa. It is characterized by its bright yellow ray florets and dark green, lance-shaped leaves.

The plant produces small, tubular disc flowers that are typically visited by pollinators such as bees and butterflies. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Its compact growth habit and long blooming period add ornamental value to gardens.
