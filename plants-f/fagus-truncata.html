Fagus truncata

Fagus truncata, commonly known as the Japanese beech, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in the fall. It produces small, spiky nuts enclosed in a cupule, which are an important food source for wildlife.

Fagus truncata thrives in cool, moist climates and is often found in mountainous regions.
