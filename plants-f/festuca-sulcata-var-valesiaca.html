Festuca sulcata var. valesiaca

Festuca sulcata var. valesiaca, commonly known as the Valesiaca tall fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its coarse, erect growth habit and dark green foliage. The plant produces dense, tufted clumps with long, narrow leaves that are often rolled at the edges. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for dry climates.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often used in landscaping and erosion control.
