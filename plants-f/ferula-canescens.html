Ferula canescens

Ferula canescens, commonly known as the white ferula, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It is a member of the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its tall, hollow stems and feathery leaves. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, typical of the family, and its sap contains volatile oils that have been used in traditional medicine.

Ferula canescens is also valued for its ornamental qualities and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its aesthetic appeal.
