Ficus perfulva

Ficus perfulva, commonly known as the white fig or yellow-veined fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are usually light green with prominent yellow veins. Ficus perfulva produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
