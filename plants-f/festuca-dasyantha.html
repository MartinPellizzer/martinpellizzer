Festuca dasyantha

Festuca dasyantha, commonly known as the hairy fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive hairy texture. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear numerous tiny flowers, typical of the Poaceae family. Festuca dasyantha thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological benefits in supporting local wildlife.
