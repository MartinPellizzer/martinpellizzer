Ficus augusta

Ficus augusta, commonly known as the velvetleaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are dark green on top and lighter underneath, often with a velvety texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by round, greenish fruits that mature to a yellowish color. It is native to tropical regions of Asia and is often found in wetlands and disturbed areas.

Ficus augusta is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in traditional medicine.
