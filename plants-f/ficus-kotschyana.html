Ficus kotschyana

Ficus kotschyana, commonly known as the Kotschy's fig, is a tropical plant native to regions of Africa and the Middle East.

It is a member of the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, broad leaves that are often dark green in color. The plant produces small, clustered fruits known as figs, which are encased in a smooth, greenish husk. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

Ficus kotschyana is relatively low maintenance, making it a popular choice for indoor gardening.
