Ficus adenosperma f. angustifolia

Ficus adenosperma f. angustifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a variant of the ficus genus native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its slender, elongated leaves that are typically dark green and glossy. The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs, which are a key feature of its botanical profile. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant.

Its adaptability makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
