Fraxinus americana var. epiptera

Fraxinus americana var. epiptera, commonly known as the western white ash, is a variant of the American ash tree characterized by its distinctive foliage and growth habits.

It is a deciduous tree native to the central and western United States, typically reaching heights of 20 to 30 meters. The leaves are alternate, compound, and pinnately arranged, with 7 to 15 leaflets that are ovate to lanceolate in shape. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age, and it produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in early spring.

The tree is adapted to a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained soils and full sun exposure.
