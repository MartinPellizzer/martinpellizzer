Ficus arukensis

Ficus arukensis, commonly known as the Bornean fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the rainforests of Borneo and is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called syconia. Ficus arukensis is known for its ability to thrive in humid, shaded environments.

Its fruit, which is a type of fig, is edible and plays an important role in the local ecosystem.
