Feuilleea edulis

Feuilleea edulis, commonly known as the golden leaf fern, is a tropical plant native to the rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, broad, and glossy green fronds that can reach up to two meters in length. The plant thrives in humid environments with indirect light and requires well-draining, nutrient-rich soil. Its unique leaf structure allows for efficient photosynthesis, making it an important species in its ecosystem.

Feuilleea edulis is also valued for its edible young fronds, which are used in local cuisines.
