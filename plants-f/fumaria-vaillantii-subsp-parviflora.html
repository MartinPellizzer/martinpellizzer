Fumaria vaillantii subsp. parviflora

Fumaria vaillantii subsp.

parviflora, commonly known as the small-flowered fumitory, is a herbaceous annual plant native to southern Europe and parts of western Asia. It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender stems and clusters of small, yellowish-orange flowers.

The plant produces elongated, cylindrical seed pods that split open to release the seeds. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in disturbed habitats such as roadsides and fields.

Its flowers bloom from late spring to early summer, attracting various pollinators.
