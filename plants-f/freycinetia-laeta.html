Freycinetia laeta

Freycinetia laeta, commonly known as the white-flowered pandanus, is a tropical plant native to the Pacific Islands.

It belongs to the Pandanaceae family and is characterized by its large, sword-shaped leaves that grow in a rosette formation. The plant produces clusters of small, white flowers that bloom in the summer months. Its roots are often used in traditional medicine, and its leaves are utilized in local crafts and cuisine.

Freycinetia laeta thrives in humid, coastal environments and is often found in coastal forests and sandy soils.
