Fabricia nummulariifolia

Fabricia nummulariifolia, commonly known as the silverleaf faba, is a flowering plant native to South Africa.

It is characterized by its small, silver-gray leaves that are round and resemble coins, giving it its scientific name. The plant produces clusters of small, white to pale purple flowers during the cooler months. It is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and compact growth habit.

Fabricia nummulariifolia prefers well-drained soil and full sun to partial shade.
