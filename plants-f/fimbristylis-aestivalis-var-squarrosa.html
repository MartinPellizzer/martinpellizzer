Fimbristylis aestivalis var. squarrosa

Fimbristylis aestivalis var. squarrosa, commonly known as the silky flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to Australia.

It typically grows in wetland or swampy environments, often forming dense tussocks. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves with a distinctive silky texture, and its inflorescence consists of a spike of small, three-lobed flowers. It plays an important role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various wildlife.

The variety squarrosa is characterized by its hairy or scaly appearance, particularly on the stems and leaves.
