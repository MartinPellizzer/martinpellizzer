Fagraea archboldiana

Fagraea archboldiana, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy green leaves and striking clusters of yellow-orange flowers that bloom in summer. The plant produces small, round fruits that contain seeds, which are dispersed by animals. It thrives in humid climates and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is cultivated in gardens and parks around the world.
