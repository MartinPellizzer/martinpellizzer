Faba

Faba, commonly known as the broad bean or fava bean, is a leguminous plant belonging to the Fabaceae family.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves and tall, branching stems that bear clusters of small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods containing edible, kidney-shaped seeds that are rich in protein and nutrients. Faba beans are often grown in temperate climates and are well-suited for nitrogen fixation, improving soil fertility.

They are also valued for their adaptability to various soil types and their role in sustainable agriculture.
