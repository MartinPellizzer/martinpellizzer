Fagraea valida

Fagraea valida, commonly known as the golden flame tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its striking, bright yellow flowers that bloom in clusters, creating a visually stunning display. The plant has compound leaves composed of several oval-shaped leaflets, and it produces woody, cylindrical fruits that contain seeds. Fagraea valida is often cultivated for ornamental purposes due to its vibrant floral display and adaptability to various soil types.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is relatively drought-tolerant once established.
