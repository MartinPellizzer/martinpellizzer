Ficus jamaicensis

Ficus jamaicensis, commonly known as the Jamaican fig tree, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly Jamaica, and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, edible figs that are an important food source for various wildlife. It has a robust root system that helps stabilize soil and prevent erosion.

Ficus jamaicensis is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ecological benefits in its native habitat.
