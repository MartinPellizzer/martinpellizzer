Fagus sylvatica f. dawycki

Fagus sylvatica f. dawycki, commonly known as the narrow-leafed European beech, is a distinctive form of the European beech characterized by its narrower, more elongated leaves.

This variety is native to the mountainous regions of Central and Southern Europe, where it thrives in cooler, moist environments. The leaves are typically darker green and have a more pronounced serration compared to the typical form. It is often cultivated in gardens for its ornamental value and unique foliage.

The tree can reach heights of up to 25 meters and is known for its slow growth and longevity.
