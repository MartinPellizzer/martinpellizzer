Ficus stelechosycia

Ficus stelechosycia, commonly known as the Sikkim fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the eastern Himalayas, particularly found in regions of India, Nepal, and Bhutan. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often hairy on the underside. It produces small, clustered figs that are typically green when immature and turn yellow or red when mature.

This species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is also used in traditional medicine in its native regions.
