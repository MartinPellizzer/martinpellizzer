Feuilleea myriantha

Feuilleea myriantha, commonly known as the silver-leafed fern, is a striking plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, delicate fronds that are often covered in a fine, silvery coating, giving it a unique appearance. The plant thrives in humid, shaded environments and prefers well-draining, nutrient-rich soil. Its reproductive structures include spore-producing cones, typical of ferns, and it plays a role in local ecosystems by providing habitat for small insects and animals.

Due to its ornamental value, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
