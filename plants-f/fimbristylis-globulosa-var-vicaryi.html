Fimbristylis globulosa var. vicaryi

Fimbristylis globulosa var. vicaryi, commonly known as the Victoria's globular sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to parts of Australia.

It is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and produces small, clustered inflorescences that are typically green to brown in color. The plant thrives in wetland or swampy environments, often forming dense mats that contribute to soil stabilization. Its leaves are narrow and slightly hairy, and it is often found in coastal or freshwater habitats.

Due to its ecological importance, it is sometimes cultivated in wetland restoration projects.
