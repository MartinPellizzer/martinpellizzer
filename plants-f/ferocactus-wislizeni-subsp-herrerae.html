Ferocactus wislizeni subsp. herrerae

Ferocactus wislizeni subsp.

herrerae, commonly known as the Mexican lion's tail, is a succulent cactus native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico. It is characterized by its cylindrical stems covered in sharp, spiny areoles and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in the spring.

The plant is adapted to arid environments, storing water in its thick, fleshy stems. It thrives in full sun and well-draining soil, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and xeriscaping.

Its unique appearance and hardy nature make it a notable species within the Ferocactus genus.
