Ficus subtecta

Ficus subtecta, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is characterized by its dense, trailing growth habit and small, glossy, ovate leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences called syconia. Ficus subtecta is native to Southeast Asia and is often cultivated as a decorative plant due to its ability to thrive in a variety of indoor conditions.

It is also valued for its air-purifying properties and adaptability to different light levels.
