Festuca elatior subvar. duvalii

Festuca elatior subvar. duvalii, commonly known as the Duval's tall fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of the western United States.

It is characterized by its erect, tufted growth habit and long, narrow leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces spike-like inflorescences with small, two-lobed flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant, making it suitable for rocky or arid environments.

This subspecies is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and hardiness.
