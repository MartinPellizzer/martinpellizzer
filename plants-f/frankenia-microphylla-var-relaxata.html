Frankenia microphylla var. relaxata

Frankenia microphylla var. relaxata, commonly known as the coastal rockrose, is a low-growing shrub native to the coastal regions of southern Africa.

It features small, glandular, aromatic leaves that are typically covered in fine, white hairs. The plant produces clusters of yellow flowers that bloom in summer, adding a vibrant touch to its rugged habitat. It is well adapted to sandy, saline soils and can tolerate periods of drought.

Its compact form and hardy nature make it a popular choice for coastal gardens and erosion control.
