Ficus wilsonii

Ficus wilsonii, commonly known as Wilson's fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, clustered figs that are enclosed within a greenish-woody syconium. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Ficus wilsonii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
