Festuca arizonica

Festuca arizonica, commonly known as Arizona fescue, is a perennial grass native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, blue-green foliage. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments. Arizona fescue is often used in native plant landscaping due to its drought tolerance and ornamental value.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soils and providing habitat for wildlife.
