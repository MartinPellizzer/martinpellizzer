Fumaria schrammii var. gracilis

Fumaria schrammii var. gracilis, commonly known as the slender yellowcress, is a flowering plant belonging to the family Brassicaceae.

It is characterized by its slender, erect stems and small, yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are alternate, lance-shaped, and often covered in fine hairs. Native to regions of Europe and parts of Asia, it thrives in moist, disturbed soils such as along riverbanks and in meadows.

This variety is notable for its delicate appearance and its role in supporting pollinators in its native habitats.
