Fimbristylis urakasiana

Fimbristylis urakasiana, commonly known as the narrowleaf flat sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact, spike-like inflorescences. Native to tropical and subtropical regions, it thrives in wet or moist environments such as marshes and swamps. The plant has a fibrous root system and produces small, wind-pollinated flowers.

Its botanical structure makes it an important component of wetland ecosystems.
