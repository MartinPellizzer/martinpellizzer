Fingardia yamapensis

Fingardia yamapensis, commonly known as the Yama Valley fern, is a rare and delicate plant native to the mountainous regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its slender, upright stems and intricate, feather-like fronds that can reach up to 30 centimeters in length. The plant thrives in cool, humid environments and is often found growing in shaded, forested areas. Its reproductive structures are small and inconspicuous, typically appearing on the underside of the fronds.

Due to its limited habitat and vulnerability to environmental changes, Fingardia yamapensis is considered a species of conservation concern.
