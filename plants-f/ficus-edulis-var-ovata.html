Ficus edulis var. ovata

Ficus edulis var. ovata, commonly known as the breadfruit tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Pacific Islands.

It is characterized by its broad, heart-shaped leaves and produces a fruit that is rich in carbohydrates. The plant thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth. Its latex sap contains compounds that can cause skin irritation.

The breadfruit fruit is typically cooked and consumed as a staple food in many island cultures.
