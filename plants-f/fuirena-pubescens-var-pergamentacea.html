Fuirena pubescens var. pergamentacea

Fuirena pubescens var. pergamentacea, commonly known as the parchment fern, is a unique variant of the fuirena pubescens species.

It is characterized by its delicate, papery fronds that give it a distinctive appearance. The plant is native to specific regions in South America, particularly in the Andes. Its leaves are typically elongated and have a soft, velvety texture.

This variant is often cultivated for its ornamental value in botanical gardens and as a houseplant.
