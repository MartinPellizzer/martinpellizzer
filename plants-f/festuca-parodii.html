Festuca parodii

Festuca parodii, commonly known as the Parodii fescue, is a perennial grass species native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in rocky or alpine environments.

Festuca parodii is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping for its hardy nature and ability to withstand harsh conditions.
