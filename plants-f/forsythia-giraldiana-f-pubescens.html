Forsythia giraldiana f. pubescens

Forsythia giraldiana f. pubescens, commonly known as the hairy forsythia, is a variant of the forsythia plant characterized by its hairy stems and leaves.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is native to China, where it thrives in temperate climates. The plant produces vibrant yellow flowers in early spring, which are clustered in racemes. Its foliage is typically dark green and slightly pubescent, contributing to its unique appearance.

This cultivar is valued for its ornamental qualities and is often used in garden landscapes for its early blooming habit.
