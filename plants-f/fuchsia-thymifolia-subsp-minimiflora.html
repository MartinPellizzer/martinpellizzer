Fuchsia thymifolia subsp. minimiflora

Fuchsia thymifolia subsp.

minimiflora, commonly known as the small-flowered fuchsia, is a species native to the Andes Mountains of South America. It is a shrub characterized by its slender, woody stems and dense, evergreen foliage.

The plant produces small, pendulous flowers in shades of violet and pink, which bloom during the cooler months. Its leaves are opposite, elliptical, and have a slightly hairy texture.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant floral displays.
