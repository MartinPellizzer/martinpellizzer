Ferula racemosoumbellata

Ferula racemosoumbellata, commonly known as the racemose umbellate ferula, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, characterized by its aromatic foliage and umbellate inflorescences. The plant features a robust, branched stem that can grow up to 2 meters in height, with finely divided, fern-like leaves. Its flowers are small, white to pale yellow, and arranged in compound umbels, giving it a delicate appearance.

The plant is valued for its medicinal properties, with the roots containing compounds used in traditional medicine.
