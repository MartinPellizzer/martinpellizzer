Frangula mcvaughii

Frangula mcvaughii, commonly known as the McVaugh's buckthorn, is a shrub native to the southwestern United States.

It is characterized by its dark green, alternate leaves and small, yellowish-green flowers that bloom in the spring. The plant produces small, dark blue berries that are edible but often have a bitter taste. It thrives in rocky soils and is adapted to arid environments.

Frangula mcvaughii plays a role in local ecosystems by providing food for wildlife and stabilizing soil in its native habitat.
