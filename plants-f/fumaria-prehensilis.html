Fumaria prehensilis

Fumaria prehensilis, commonly known as the clinging cranesbill, is a flowering plant native to southern Europe and western Asia.

It is characterized by its erect, branched stems that often trail along the ground, and it produces clusters of small, bright yellow flowers. The plant has palmate leaves with five to seven lobes, and its roots are fibrous and rhizomatous. Fumaria prehensilis thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in woodlands and along stream banks.

It is valued for its ornamental appeal and has been used in traditional herbal medicine.
