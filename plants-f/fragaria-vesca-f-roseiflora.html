Fragaria vesca f. roseiflora

Fragaria vesca f. roseiflora, commonly known as the rose-flowered wild strawberry, is a cultivated form of the common strawberry.

It is characterized by its distinctive pinkish-red flowers that bloom in early spring. The plant produces small, edible fruits similar to those of the common strawberry, though they are typically smaller and more intensely flavored. It thrives in cool climates and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for gardeners interested in ornamental and edible plants.

This variety is valued for both its aesthetic appeal and its culinary uses.
