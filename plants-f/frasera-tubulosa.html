Frasera tubulosa

Frasera tubulosa, commonly known as the mountain foamflower, is a perennial herb native to the western United States.

It belongs to the family Gentianaceae and is characterized by its showy, tubular flowers that range in color from white to pink. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and stream banks. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and often have a hairy texture.
