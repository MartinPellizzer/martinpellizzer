Ferrocalamus strictus

Ferrocalamus strictus, commonly known as the stiff reed, is a grass-like plant native to certain regions of Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, slender stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. The plant is often found in wet or marshy environments, where it thrives in moist, nutrient-rich soils. Its growth pattern is typically clump-forming, with dense foliage that can reach several feet in height.

Ferrocalamus strictus plays a role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for small aquatic organisms.
