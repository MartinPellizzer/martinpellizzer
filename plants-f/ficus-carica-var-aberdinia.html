Ficus carica var. aberdinia

Ficus carica var. aberdinia, commonly known as the Aberdina quince, is a cultivar of the common quince.

It is characterized by its large, fragrant, yellowish-orange fruits that ripen in late autumn. The plant is a deciduous shrub or small tree with thick, rough bark and alternate, ovate leaves. It thrives in temperate climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

This variety is prized for its culinary uses and ornamental value in gardens.
