Faramea acuminatissima

Faramea acuminatissima, commonly known as the narrow-leaved faramea, is a shrub native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is characterized by its simple, alternate leaves that are typically elliptical or ovate in shape. The plant produces small, clustered white flowers that are followed by bright red berries. Faramea acuminatissima is often found in moist, shaded environments such as rainforests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and horticultural use.
