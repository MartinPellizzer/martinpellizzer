Fedia sulcata

Fedia sulcata, commonly known as the African desert rose, is a flowering plant native to arid regions of Africa.

It is characterized by its succulent, fleshy stems that store water, allowing it to survive in harsh, dry environments. The plant produces showy, pink or purple flowers that bloom during the rainy season. Its leaves are typically small and opposite, while its roots are deep and extensive, enabling it to access groundwater.

Fedia sulcata is often used in traditional medicine and is valued for its ornamental beauty.
