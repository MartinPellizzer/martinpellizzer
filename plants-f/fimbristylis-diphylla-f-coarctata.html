Fimbristylis diphylla f. coarctata

Fimbristylis diphylla f. coarctata, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedges, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like stems and small, triangular leaves. The plant produces spikelets that are typically arranged in dense clusters along the stem. It is often found in wetland habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas, where it thrives in moist to waterlogged soils.

This subspecies is notable for its distinctive morphology and ecological role in aquatic and semi-aquatic ecosystems.
