Ficus pseudoconcinna

Ficus pseudoconcinna, commonly known as the small-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including countries like Thailand, Malaysia, and Indonesia. The plant is characterized by its small, elliptical leaves that are usually dark green and glossy. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fruits that are typically green when unripe and turn yellow when mature.

Ficus pseudoconcinna is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
