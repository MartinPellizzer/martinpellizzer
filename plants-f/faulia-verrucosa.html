Faulia verrucosa

Faulia verrucosa, commonly known as the roughleaf dandelion, is a herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It features a basal rosette of hairy, lobed leaves that are often covered in fine, yellowish pubescence. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that develop into distinctive, fluffy seed heads. It thrives in dry, rocky soils and is often found in disturbed habitats.

Faulia verrucosa is notable for its adaptability and is sometimes considered a weed in agricultural settings.
