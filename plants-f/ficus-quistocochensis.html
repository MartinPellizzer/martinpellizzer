Ficus quistocochensis

Ficus quistocochensis, commonly known as the Quistocochens fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in botanical gardens and conservation programs.
