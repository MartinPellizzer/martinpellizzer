Ficus tannoensis f. rhombifolia

Ficus tannoensis f. rhombifolia, commonly known as the Rhombifoliate Fig, is a unique fig species characterized by its distinctive foliage.

The leaves are typically rhombic or diamond-shaped, with a glossy, dark green upper surface and a lighter, sometimes pubescent underside. It is native to specific regions in Southeast Asia, where it thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. The plant produces small, edible figs that are often used in local cuisines.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening.
