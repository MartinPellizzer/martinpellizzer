Fadogia homblei

Fadogia homblei, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a small shrub native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous properties and distinctive pod-like fruits. The plant features compound leaves with small, oval-shaped leaflets and produces yellowish-white flowers arranged in clusters. Its fruit is a flattened, pod-like structure that contains several seeds.

Fadogia homblei is often found in savannah and woodland habitats, where it plays a role in soil stabilization and provides habitat for various organisms.
