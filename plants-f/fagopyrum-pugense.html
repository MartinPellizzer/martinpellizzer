Fagopyrum pugense

Fagopyrum pugense, commonly known as the narrowleaf buckwheat, is a herbaceous plant native to the northern regions of North America.

It typically grows in moist, disturbed soils and is often found in wetlands or along stream banks. The plant has alternate, lance-shaped leaves that are usually covered in fine hairs. Its flowers are small, clustered in panicles, and are typically greenish-yellow in color.

Fagopyrum pugense is known for its ability to thrive in wet environments and is often used in ecological restoration projects.
