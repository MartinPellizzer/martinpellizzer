Fraxinus chinensis

Fraxinus chinensis, commonly known as the Chinese ash, is a deciduous tree native to eastern Asia.

It belongs to the Oleaceae family and is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by samaroid fruits that disperse its seeds by wind. It thrives in a variety of soil types and is often found in moist, temperate forests.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological importance, it is cultivated in gardens and reforestation projects.
