Ferula sulcata var. crassicosta

Ferula sulcata var. crassicosta, commonly known as the Persian giant fennel, is a perennial herb native to the arid regions of central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, hollow, and aromatic stalks that can reach heights of up to 3 meters. The plant produces yellow flowers in umbels, which bloom in late summer. Its leaves are narrow and linear, with a strong, spicy scent.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated for its essential oils.
