Fraxinus fungosa

Fraxinus fungosa, commonly known as the green ash, is a deciduous tree native to North America.

It typically grows to a height of 20–30 meters and has compound leaves with 9–15 pairs of ovate leaflets. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in clustered inflorescences, followed by winged seeds called samaras. Its bark is smooth and grayish-brown, becoming fissured with age.

The plant is often found in moist soils along riverbanks and in floodplain forests.
