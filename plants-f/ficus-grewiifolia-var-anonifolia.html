Ficus grewiifolia var. anonifolia

Ficus grewiifolia var. anonifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a variant of the Ficus genus characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves.

The plant is native to Southeast Asia and thrives in tropical and subtropical climates. Its leaves are typically smooth and glossy, with a dark green coloration. It produces small, fig-like fruits that are edible but often used more for ornamental purposes.

The plant is popular as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
