Ficus demeusei

Ficus demeusei, commonly known as the Demeuse fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Madagascar and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are usually opposite in arrangement. The plant produces small, fig-like fruits that are enclosed within a syconium, a unique structure typical of the fig genus. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates, preferring well-drained soil and partial to full sunlight.

Ficus demeusei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
