Filago lanuginosa

Filago lanuginosa, commonly known as the downy filago, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features slender, branched stems covered in soft, woolly hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its small yellow flowers are typically arranged in clusters, and it produces small, hairy fruits called cypsela.

Filago lanuginosa is often found in rocky or sandy soils and is adapted to arid or semi-arid environments.
