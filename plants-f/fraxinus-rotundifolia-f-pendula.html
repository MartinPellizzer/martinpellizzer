Fraxinus rotundifolia f. pendula

Fraxinus rotundifolia f. pendula, commonly known as the weeping white ash, is a cultivar of the white ash tree characterized by its cascading, drooping branches.

It features small, ovate leaves that are typically dark green on top and lighter in color underneath. The plant produces small, yellowish-green flowers in early spring, which are followed by samaras that disperse via wind. It thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers moist, well-drained soil.

This ornamental tree is valued for its unique, flowing appearance and is often used in landscaping for its aesthetic appeal.
