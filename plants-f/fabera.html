Fabera

Fabera, commonly known as the common vetch, is a flowering plant belonging to the legume family, Fabaceae.

It is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers that range in color from white to purple. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often used in traditional medicine. Fabera is known for its ability to fix atmospheric nitrogen, making it valuable in soil enrichment.

It thrives in temperate climates and is often found in disturbed soils and along roadsides.
