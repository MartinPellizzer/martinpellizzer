Fragaria monspeliensis

Fragaria monspeliensis, commonly known as the French strawberry, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the Rosaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, edible fruits. The plant produces white flowers that develop into red, fleshy berries, which are rich in vitamin C and antioxidants. It thrives in temperate climates and is often cultivated for both ornamental and culinary purposes.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species in horticulture and botanical research.
