Fritillaria zahnii

Fritillaria zahnii, commonly known as the Persian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from orange to reddish-brown, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and thrives in well-drained, rocky soils. It is typically found in alpine and subalpine habitats, where it blooms in late spring to early summer.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and conservation efforts.
