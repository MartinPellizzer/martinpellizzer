Fraxinus pseudo-ornus

Fraxinus pseudo-ornus, commonly known as the Chinese ash.

It is a deciduous tree native to China, characterized by its smooth, gray bark and alternate, simple leaves that are ovate to elliptic in shape. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in catkins, followed by winged fruits that aid in seed dispersal. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in riparian zones.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and hardiness in temperate climates.
