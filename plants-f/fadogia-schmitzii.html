Fadogia schmitzii

Fadogia schmitzii, commonly known as the African velvet bean, is a climbing legume native to tropical Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and distinctive, fragrant flowers. The plant produces elongated pods that contain edible seeds, which are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid climates and is adapted to various soil types, making it a resilient agricultural species.

Additionally, Fadogia schmitzii is known for its nitrogen-fixing ability, which enriches the soil and supports sustainable farming practices.
