Ficus thomaea

Ficus thomaea, commonly known as the creeping fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, trailing growth habit and small, opposite leaves that are usually glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by figs that are typically green or yellow in color. It is often used in horticulture for its ability to thrive in shaded environments and its attractive foliage.

Ficus thomaea is also valued for its adaptability to various soil types and its resilience to pests and diseases.
