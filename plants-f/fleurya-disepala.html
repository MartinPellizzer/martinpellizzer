Fleurya disepala

Fleurya disepala, commonly known as the two-petaled orchid, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Orchidaceae family and is characterized by its small, delicate flowers with two petals. The plant typically grows as an epiphyte, attaching itself to trees in humid forest environments. Its botanical features include a slender stem and lance-shaped leaves.

The flowers are usually pale green or white, with a unique structure that sets it apart from other orchid species.
