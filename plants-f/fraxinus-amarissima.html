Fraxinus amarissima

Fraxinus amarissima, commonly known as the bitter ash, is a rare and lesser-known species within the Oleaceae family.

It is characterized by its small, ovate leaves that are typically gray-green in color and have a rough texture. Due to its limited distribution and habitat specificity, it is considered a vulnerable species in its native range.
