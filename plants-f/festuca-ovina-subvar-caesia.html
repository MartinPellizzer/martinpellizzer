Festuca ovina subvar. caesia

Festuca ovina subvar. caesia, commonly known as bluegrass, is a cool-season grass native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green blades and dense, tufted growth habit. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. Its flowering stems bear small, spike-like inflorescences with pale blue to purple flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.
