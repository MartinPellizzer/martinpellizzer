Festuca leptothrix

Festuca leptothrix, commonly known as slender feathergrass, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia.

It is characterized by its fine, slender leaves and tufted inflorescence, which consists of narrow spikelets. The plant typically grows in open, sunny habitats such as meadows, grasslands, and rocky areas. It has a clumping growth habit and is often used in ornamental landscaping due to its delicate appearance.

Festuca leptothrix is also valued for its ecological role in supporting pollinators and maintaining soil stability.
