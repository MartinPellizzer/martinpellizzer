Ficus gibbosa f. tuberculata

Ficus gibbosa f. tuberculata, commonly known as the tuberculate fig, is a variant of the ficus genus characterized by its unique leaf morphology.

The leaves are typically thick, glossy, and have a distinctive waxy texture. The plant features small, rounded tubercles on the leaf surface, giving it a textured appearance. It is a tropical evergreen shrub that thrives in humid, warm environments.

This cultivar is popular as a houseplant due to its aesthetic appeal and relatively low maintenance requirements.
