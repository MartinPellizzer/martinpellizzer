Ficus nepalensis

Ficus nepalensis, commonly known as the Nepal fig, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to the Himalayan regions of Nepal, India, and Bhutan, where it thrives in moist, shaded environments. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered fruits that are edible. It plays an important role in local ecosystems and is often used in traditional medicine.

Its ability to grow in rocky and mountainous terrain makes it a resilient and adaptive species.
