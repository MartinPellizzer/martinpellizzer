Festuca ovina var. novae-zelandiae

Festuca ovina var. novae-zelandiae, commonly known as New Zealand sheep's fescue, is a perennial grass species native to New Zealand.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and fine, silver-green foliage. The plant typically grows in moist, shaded environments such as forest floors and rocky areas. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil in native habitats.
