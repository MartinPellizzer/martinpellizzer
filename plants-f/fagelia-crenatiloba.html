Fagelia crenatiloba

Fagelia crenatiloba, commonly known as the serrated-leaved fagelia, is a flowering plant native to South America.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which is characterized by its leguminous traits and compound leaves. The plant features trifoliate leaves with serrated edges, contributing to its distinctive appearance. It produces small, pea-like flowers that are typically pink or purple in color.

Fagelia crenatiloba is often found in moist, shaded environments such as forests and stream banks.
