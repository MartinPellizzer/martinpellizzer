Fremya lateriflora

Fremya lateriflora, commonly known as the coastal hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to coastal regions of southern Africa.

It is a shrub that typically grows in sandy or rocky soils along the shoreline. The plant produces showy, five-petaled flowers that are usually pink or red, blooming throughout the warmer months. Its leaves are alternate, simple, and often have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

Fremya lateriflora is adapted to salt spray and is an important species in coastal ecosystems.
