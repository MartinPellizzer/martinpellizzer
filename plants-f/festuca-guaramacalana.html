Festuca guaramacalana

Festuca guaramacalana, commonly known as the Guaramacalana fescue, is a perennial grass species native to South America.

It is characterized by its fine, tufted growth habit and slender, blade-like leaves that are typically a dull green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are usually pale green to brown in hue. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in mountainous regions with cool, temperate climates.

Due to its ornamental value and ecological significance, it is sometimes cultivated in gardens and restoration projects.
