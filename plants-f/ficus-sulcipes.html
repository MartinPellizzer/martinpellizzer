Ficus sulcipes

Ficus sulcipes, commonly known as the grove fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often adorned with distinctive veins. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, fig-like fruits. Ficus sulcipes thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and air-purifying qualities.
