Ficus philippinensis f. setibracteata

Ficus philippinensis f. setibracteata, commonly known as the Philippine fig with bracteate leaves, is a variant of the ficus genus native to the Philippines.

It is characterized by its distinctive bracteate leaves, which are modified leaves that enclose the inflorescence. The plant typically grows as a shrub or small tree, often found in moist, shaded habitats within tropical forests. Its leaves are usually dark green and glossy, with a smooth texture.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in horticultural settings.
