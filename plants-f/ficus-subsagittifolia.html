Ficus subsagittifolia

Ficus subsagittifolia, commonly known as the arrowleaf fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its heart-shaped leaves that are often glossy and dark green. The plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are edible and commonly used in local cuisines. It thrives in warm, humid environments and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus subsagittifolia is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.
