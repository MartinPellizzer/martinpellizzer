Ficus kisantuensis

Ficus kisantuensis, commonly known as the Kisantu fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Central Africa, particularly the Democratic Republic of the Congo. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible figs. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree is valued for its ornamental qualities and has some traditional uses in local communities.
