Festuca cyllenica subsp. pangaei

Festuca cyllenica subsp.

pangaei, commonly known as the Mongolian fescue, is a perennial grass species native to central Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves.

The plant typically grows in dry, open habitats such as steppes and rocky slopes. It produces small, spike-like inflorescences with numerous tiny flowers.

Due to its hardy nature, it is often used in ecological restoration projects in arid regions.
