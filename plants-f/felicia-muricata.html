Felicia muricata

Felicia muricata, commonly known as the roughleaf felicia, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant features hairy, serrated leaves and produces small yellow flowers that bloom in summer. It thrives in well-drained soils and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and borders.

Felicia muricata is also valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to attract pollinators.
