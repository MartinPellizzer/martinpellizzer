Ficus natalensis var. puberula

Ficus natalensis var. puberula, commonly known as the Natal fig or Natal weeping fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to eastern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, grayish bark and large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are often slightly pubescent on the underside. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically eaten fresh or used in local cuisines.

It thrives in warm, humid climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
