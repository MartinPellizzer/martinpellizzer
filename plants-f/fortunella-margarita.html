Fortunella margarita

Fortunella margarita, commonly known as the golden orange, is a small evergreen shrub native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Rutaceae family and is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves and small, fragrant flowers. The plant produces edible fruits that are typically orange in color, though they can vary in hue depending on the variety. These fruits are rich in vitamin C and have a tart, acidic flavor.

Fortunella margarita is often cultivated for its ornamental value and as a source of citrus fruits in tropical and subtropical regions.
