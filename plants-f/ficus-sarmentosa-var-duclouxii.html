Ficus sarmentosa var. duclouxii

Ficus sarmentosa var. duclouxii, commonly known as the Ducloux's fig, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its trailing growth habit and heart-shaped leaves that are typically a deep green. The plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are edible and often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid environments and requires well-draining soil to prevent root rot.

Due to its ornamental value, it is often cultivated as a houseplant or in shaded garden settings.
