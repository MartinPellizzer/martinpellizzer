Ficus rehmannii var. ovatifolia

Ficus rehmannii var. ovatifolia, commonly known as the broad-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia and is characterized by its glossy, ovate-shaped leaves that are often variegated with cream or white markings. The plant produces small, clustered fruits that are typically green when mature and turn yellow or red when ripe. It is often cultivated as a decorative indoor plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.

The leaves are also used in traditional medicine in some cultures for their purported health benefits.
