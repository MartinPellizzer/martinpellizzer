Festuca sudetica

Festuca sudetica, commonly known as the Sudetic fescue, is a perennial grass native to Central and Eastern Europe.

It typically grows in moist, calcareous soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along stream banks. The plant has slender, grass-like leaves and produces small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny blue to purple flowers. It is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

Festuca sudetica is also known for its ability to thrive in shaded environments.
