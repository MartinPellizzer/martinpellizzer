Ficus geniculata var. abnormalis

Ficus geniculata var. abnormalis, commonly known as the abnormal fig, is a rare and unique variant of the ficus genus.

It is characterized by its unusual growth patterns, including twisted stems and irregular leaf shapes. The plant originates from Southeast Asia and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions. Its foliage is typically deep green with a glossy texture, and it produces small, edible figs under specific conditions.

Due to its rarity and distinctive appearance, it is highly sought after by plant enthusiasts and botanists alike.
