Ficus bonijesulapensis

Ficus bonijesulapensis, commonly known as the Bonijesulapensis fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, clustered fig fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal.

The species is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
