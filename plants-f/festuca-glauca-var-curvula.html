Festuca glauca var. curvula

Festuca glauca var. curvula, commonly known as bluegrass bluecurl, is a low-growing perennial grass native to the northeastern United States.

It features fine, blue-green blades that curl at the tips, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant typically forms dense mats and thrives in moist, well-drained soils. It is often found in wetlands, stream banks, and other moist habitats.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for landscaping in shaded, damp areas.
