Festuca sulcata

Festuca sulcata, commonly known as black oatgrass, is a perennial grass native to temperate regions of Europe and Asia.

It is characterized by its dark green, coarse leaves and tufted inflorescences that bear small, spike-like flowers. The plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in grasslands, meadows, and along roadsides. Festuca sulcata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping.

It is also known for its hardy nature, making it adaptable to a variety of environmental conditions.
