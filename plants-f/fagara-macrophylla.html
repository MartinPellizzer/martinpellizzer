Fagara macrophylla

Fagara macrophylla, commonly known as the large-leaf fagara, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to the Caribbean and Central America.

It is characterized by its large, glossy, elliptical leaves that can grow up to 20 centimeters in length. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by bright red, fleshy berries. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in moist forests.

Its botanical features make it a valuable species for ecological studies and ornamental horticulture.
