Ferula pumila

Ferula pumila, commonly known as the dwarf ferula, is a small perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes carrots and parsley, and is characterized by its aromatic, often hairy foliage. The plant produces small, yellowish flowers arranged in umbels, typical of the family. Its root system is fibrous and can be used in traditional medicine.

Ferula pumila thrives in dry, rocky soils and is adapted to cold, high-altitude environments.
