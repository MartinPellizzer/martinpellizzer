Fritillaria recurva

Fritillaria recurva, commonly known as the recurved fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a bulbous base. It thrives in well-drained soils and requires full sun to partial shade.

Fritillaria recurva is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated in rock gardens and alpine settings.
