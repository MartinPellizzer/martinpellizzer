Francoa glabrata

Francoa glabrata, commonly known as the smooth francoa, is a flowering plant native to the western United States.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family and is characterized by its daisy-like flowers that bloom in late spring to early summer. The plant typically grows in moist, well-drained soils and thrives in partial to full sun. Its foliage is evergreen, with narrow, grass-like leaves that form a dense mat.

Francoa glabrata is often used in native plant gardens for its ornamental value and ecological benefits.
