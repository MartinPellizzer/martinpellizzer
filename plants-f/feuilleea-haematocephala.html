Feuilleea haematocephala

Feuilleea haematocephala, commonly known as the Crimson-Headed Fern, is a unique vascular plant characterized by its striking red-tinged foliage and intricate frond structure.

Native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia, it thrives in shaded, moist environments with high humidity. Its leaves are bioluminescent under certain conditions, emitting a faint glow at night, which aids in attracting nocturnal pollinators. The plant's root system is highly specialized, forming symbiotic relationships with mycorrhizal fungi to enhance nutrient absorption.

Due to its rarity and ecological significance, it is classified as an endangered species requiring conservation efforts.
