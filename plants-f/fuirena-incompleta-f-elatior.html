Fuirena incompleta f. elatior

Fuirena incompleta f. elatior, commonly known as the greater wallflower, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the Brassicaceae family and is characterized by its clustered flowers that range in color from yellow to orange. The plant typically grows in rocky or sandy soils and thrives in full sun. Its foliage consists of basal leaves that are lance-shaped and covered in fine hairs.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and landscapes.
