Ficus lagunensis

Ficus lagunensis, commonly known as the Lagoon Fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the Caribbean, particularly found in the Bahamas and the Turks and Caicos Islands. The plant is a small tree or shrub, typically growing to a height of 3 to 5 meters. Its leaves are opposite, simple, and ovate, with a glossy green upper surface and a lighter underside.

The fruit is a small, green fig that turns yellow when mature, containing edible seeds.
