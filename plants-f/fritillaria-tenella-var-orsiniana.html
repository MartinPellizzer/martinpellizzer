Fritillaria tenella var. orsiniana

Fritillaria tenella var. orsiniana, commonly known as the Persian fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It features striking bell-shaped flowers that range in color from deep red to orange and yellow, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves and has a robust, fleshy bulb that serves as its primary storage organ. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers full sun to partial shade.

This variety is valued for its ornamental beauty and is often cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms.
