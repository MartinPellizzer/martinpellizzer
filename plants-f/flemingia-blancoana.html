Flemingia blancoana

Flemingia blancoana, commonly known as the Philippine tamarind tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Philippines.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its compound leaves and pod fruits. The plant is known for its ability to thrive in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in lowland forests.

Its flowers are typically yellow or orange, and it produces edible pods that have a tangy flavor, making it both ornamental and useful in local cuisines.
