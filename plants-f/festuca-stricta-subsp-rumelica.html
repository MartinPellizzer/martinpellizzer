Festuca stricta subsp. rumelica

Festuca stricta subsp.

rumelica, commonly known as narrowleaf fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Europe and parts of Asia. It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and narrow, grass-like leaves that are typically blue-green in color.

The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous slender, spikelet-like flowers. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, wetlands, and along stream banks.

This subspecies is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes used in landscaping and ecological restoration projects.
