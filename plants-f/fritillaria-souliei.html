Fritillaria souliei

Fritillaria souliei, commonly known as the Chinese bellflower, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of China.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from yellow to orange and red, often with intricate patterns. The plant produces a cluster of narrow, grass-like leaves that emerge from a rounded bulb. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for rock gardens and alpine settings.

Its ornamental value is enhanced by its unique flower structure and seasonal blooming cycle.
