Ficus mysorensis

Ficus mysorensis, commonly known as the Mysore fig, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its simple, ovate leaves that are glossy and dark green on the upper surface. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are typically yellow or orange in color. It thrives in tropical and subtropical climates and is often cultivated for its ornamental value.

Ficus mysorensis is also valued for its ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
