Ferula sulcata var. scabra

Ferula sulcata var. scabra, commonly known as the Afghan giant fennel, is a perennial herb native to the mountainous regions of Central Asia.

It is characterized by its tall, erect stem and large, feathery leaves that are often covered in fine hairs. The plant produces yellow flowers arranged in umbels, which bloom in the summer months. Its taproot is thick and fleshy, and it is adapted to grow in arid and semi-arid environments.

Ferula sulcata var. scabra is also valued for its aromatic properties and has been used in traditional medicine.
