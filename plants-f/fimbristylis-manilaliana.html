Fimbristylis manilaliana

Fimbristylis manilaliana, commonly known as the Manila sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and swampy areas. The plant has narrow, grass-like leaves and produces small, clustered inflorescences with tiny, three-lobed flowers. Its rhizomes are often used in traditional medicine in some regions.

The species is notable for its ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and supporting local biodiversity.
