Ficus subapiculata

Ficus subapiculata, commonly known as the woolly fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, particularly India and Sri Lanka. The plant is characterized by its soft, hairy leaves that give it a distinctive appearance. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round fig fruits.

Ficus subapiculata is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its aesthetic appeal and adaptability to various growing conditions.
