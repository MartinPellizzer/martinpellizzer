Fimbristylis tristachya var. tristachya

Fimbristylis tristachya var. tristachya, commonly known as the narrow-leaved sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and a dense cluster of small, inconspicuous flowers. The plant typically grows in wet or seasonally wet environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of ponds. It has a rhizomatous root system that helps it spread and establish in moist soils.

This species is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its ability to stabilize soil and support local biodiversity.
