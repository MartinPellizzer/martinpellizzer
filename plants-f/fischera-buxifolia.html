Fischera buxifolia

Fischera buxifolia, commonly known as the boxleaf fischera, is a unique plant characterized by its dense, compact growth habit and foliage resembling that of boxwood.

It features small, oval-shaped leaves that are typically a glossy green, arranged in a tight, bushy formation. The plant produces tiny, inconspicuous flowers that are usually greenish-white in color, followed by small, rounded berries. It thrives in well-drained soil and prefers partial to full shade, making it suitable for woodland gardens.

Due to its slow growth and ornamental appeal, it is often used in rock gardens or as a decorative filler in shaded areas.
