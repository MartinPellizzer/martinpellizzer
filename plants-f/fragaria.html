Fragaria

Fragaria, commonly known as strawberries, is a genus of flowering plants in the family Rosaceae.

These plants are herbaceous perennials that typically grow in temperate climates. Their leaves are trifoliate, with three leaflets that are usually serrated. The flowers are white with five petals and produce small, aggregate fruits called achenes.

Fragaria species are widely cultivated for their sweet, edible berries.
