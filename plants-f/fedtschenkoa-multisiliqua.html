Fedtschenkoa multisiliqua

Fedtschenkoa multisiliqua, commonly known as the mountain hibiscus, is a flowering plant native to the Andes mountains of South America.

It is characterized by its showy, trumpet-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pink and purple. The plant typically grows in high-altitude environments, preferring well-drained soils and full sun exposure. Its leaves are broad and heart-shaped, often covered in fine hairs.

Fedtschenkoa multisiliqua is valued for its ornamental appeal and is cultivated in gardens for its vibrant blooms and hardy nature.
