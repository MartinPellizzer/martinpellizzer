Ficus manok

Ficus manok, commonly known as the Manok fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, particularly found in the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers that develop into edible fig fruits. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-drained soil to prevent root rot.

Ficus manok is often cultivated for its ornamental value and its fruit, which is appreciated in local cuisines.
