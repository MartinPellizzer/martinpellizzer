Filago erecta

Filago erecta, commonly known as the upright filago, is a flowering plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Asteraceae family, which includes daisies and sunflowers, and is characterized by its composite flower heads. The plant typically grows to a height of about 30 to 60 centimeters, with slender, hairy stems and narrow, lance-shaped leaves. Its flowers are yellow and appear in clusters, often blooming in late summer to early autumn.

Filago erecta is often found in grasslands and along roadsides, where it thrives in full sun and well-drained soil.
