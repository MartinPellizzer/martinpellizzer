Fingardia lunata

Fingardia lunata, commonly known as the moonlight fern, is a unique species native to the humid rainforests of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its delicate, lace-like fronds that resemble the phases of the moon, giving it its common name. The plant thrives in shaded, moist environments and requires high humidity to flourish. Its rhizomatous growth habit allows it to spread slowly across the forest floor.

Botanically, Fingardia lunata is valued for its ornamental appeal and its role in maintaining the ecological balance of its native habitat.
