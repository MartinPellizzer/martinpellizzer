Festuca hostii

Festuca hostii, commonly known as the silvergrass, is a perennial grass native to eastern Asia.

It is characterized by its silver-blue foliage and upright growth habit, making it a popular ornamental plant. The plant produces small, inconspicuous greenish-brown flowers in late spring to early summer. Festuca hostii thrives in full sun to partial shade and prefers well-drained soil.

Its foliage remains attractive throughout the growing season, offering year-round visual interest in gardens.
