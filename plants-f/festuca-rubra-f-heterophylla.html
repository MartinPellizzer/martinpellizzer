Festuca rubra f. heterophylla

Festuca rubra f. heterophylla, commonly known as red fescue with variable leaves, is a grass species characterized by its distinctive foliage.

It exhibits morphological variation in leaf shape, with some leaves being narrow and others broader, giving it a unique appearance. The plant typically grows in cool, moist environments and is often found in mountainous regions. Its leaves are usually tinged with red or purple, especially under stress conditions.

This form is valued in horticulture for its ornamental qualities and ability to thrive in well-drained soils.
