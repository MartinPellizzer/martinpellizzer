Fornasinia ebenifera

Fornasinia ebenifera, commonly known as the ebony tree, is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dark, dense wood, which is highly valued for its durability and aesthetic appeal. The plant typically grows as a tall evergreen tree, reaching heights of up to 30 meters. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and elliptic in shape, with a smooth surface and a glossy appearance.

The flowers are small and inconspicuous, followed by the production of woody, elongated fruits containing flat, seed-like structures.
