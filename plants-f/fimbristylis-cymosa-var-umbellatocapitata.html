Fimbristylis cymosa var. umbellatocapitata

Fimbristylis cymosa var. umbellatocapitata, commonly known as the umbrella-headed sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the family Cyperaceae, which includes sedges and related plants. The plant is characterized by its dense, grass-like foliage and distinctive inflorescence that resembles an umbrella, hence its common name. It typically grows in wetland habitats such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for aquatic organisms.
