Ficus mallotoides

Ficus mallotoides, commonly known as the Indian rubber plant, is a species of flowering plant in the family Moraceae.

It is native to tropical regions of Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green in color. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height under favorable conditions.

The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by fig-like fruits that are often used in traditional medicine.
