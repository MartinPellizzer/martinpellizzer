Ficus hatschbachii

Ficus hatschbachii, commonly known as the Hatschbach's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of South America, particularly Brazil. This plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and can grow as a shrub or small tree. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil.

Ficus hatschbachii is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ease of care.
