Festuca takasagoensis

Festuca takasagoensis, commonly known as Takasago fescue, is a perennial grass species native to Japan.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, grass-like leaves that are typically green to bluish-green in color. The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny flowers, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in shaded or semi-shaded woodland environments.

Festuca takasagoensis is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological role in supporting local biodiversity.
