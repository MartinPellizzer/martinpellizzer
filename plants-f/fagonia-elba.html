Fagonia elba

Fagonia elba, commonly known as the Canary Islands fagonia, is a flowering plant native to the Canary Islands.

It belongs to the family Cactaceae and is characterized by its spiny stems and small, yellowish flowers. The plant is adapted to arid environments, featuring succulent leaves that store water. It thrives in rocky soils and is often found in coastal areas.

Due to its limited distribution, it is considered a rare and endemic species.
