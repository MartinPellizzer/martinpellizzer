Ficus usiacurina

Ficus usiacurina, commonly known as the Brazilian fig tree, is a species native to the tropical regions of South America.

It is a large evergreen tree that can grow up to 30 meters in height, characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible figs that are typically small and yellowish. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland rainforests.

The tree has a shallow root system, making it suitable for cultivation in areas with well-drained soil.
