Fritillaria lanceolata var. floribunda

Fritillaria lanceolata var. floribunda, commonly known as the Turk's cap lily, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of central Asia.

It features striking, bell-shaped flowers that range in color from pale yellow to deep orange, often with contrasting purple or brown markings. The plant produces narrow, grass-like leaves and is characterized by its ability to thrive in well-drained, rocky soils. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and is adapted to cold climates.

The flowers typically bloom in late spring to early summer, adding a vibrant touch to alpine gardens.
