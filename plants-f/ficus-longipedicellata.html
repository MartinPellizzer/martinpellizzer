Ficus longipedicellata

Ficus longipedicellata, commonly known as the long-armed fig, is a tropical evergreen shrub or small tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its dense, glossy, ovate leaves that are often clustered at the ends of long, slender petioles. The plant produces small, edible fruits known as figs, which are enclosed in a spherical, greenish-woody syconium. Its root system is extensive and can be used for soil stabilization in landscaping.

Ficus longipedicellata is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
