Fraxinus × hybrida

Fraxinus × hybrida, commonly known as the European ash, is a hybrid species resulting from the cross between Manchurian ash (Fraxinus mandshurica) and European ash (Fraxinus excelsior).

It is a deciduous tree that typically grows to a height of 15–25 meters, featuring opposite, simple, and lanceolate leaves with serrated margins. The tree produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in panicles, followed by samaroid fruits that are dispersed by wind.

It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and adaptability to various soil types.
