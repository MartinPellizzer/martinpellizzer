Ficus linearifolia

Ficus linearifolia, commonly known as the narrow-leaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India, Indonesia, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its long, narrow leaves that are typically arranged alternately along the stem. Ficus linearifolia produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.
