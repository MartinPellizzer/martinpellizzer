Fimbristylis fusiformis

Fimbristylis fusiformis, commonly known as the narrow-leaf sedge, is a perennial grass-like plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is characterized by its slender, cylindrical stems and narrow, grass-like leaves. This species typically grows in wet or marshy environments, such as swamps and seasonally flooded areas. The plant produces small, clustered spikes of flowers, which are usually green or brown in color.

Fimbristylis fusiformis plays an important ecological role in stabilizing wetland soils and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
