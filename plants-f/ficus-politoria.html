Ficus politoria

Ficus politoria, commonly known as the velvetleaf fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical and subtropical regions of Asia, Africa, and the Pacific Islands. The plant is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in a dense, velvety pubescence. It produces small, fig-like fruits that are typically green when mature and are not typically consumed by humans.

Ficus politoria is often grown as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and ability to thrive in a variety of indoor environments.
