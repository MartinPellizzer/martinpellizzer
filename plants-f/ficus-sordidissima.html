Ficus sordidissima

Ficus sordidissima, commonly known as the black ficus, is a species native to tropical regions of Africa.

It is a flowering plant belonging to the Moraceae family, characterized by its dark, almost black, bark and dense, glossy leaves. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers that are typically pollinated by insects. It thrives in humid environments and is often found in shaded, moist forest understories.

Due to its unique appearance and hardy nature, it is occasionally cultivated as an ornamental plant.
