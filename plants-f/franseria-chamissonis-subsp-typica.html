Franseria chamissonis subsp. typica

Franseria chamissonis subsp.

typica, commonly known as the coastal franseria, is a flowering plant native to the western coast of North America. It is a herbaceous perennial that typically grows in sandy or rocky coastal environments.

The plant features showy yellow flowers arranged in clusters, which bloom in the summer months. Its leaves are narrow and linear, often covered in fine hairs.

It plays an important role in stabilizing coastal dunes and supporting local pollinator populations.
