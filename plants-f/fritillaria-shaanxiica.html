Fritillaria shaanxiica

Fritillaria shaanxiica, commonly known as the Shaanxi fritillary, is a bulbous perennial plant native to the mountainous regions of Shaanxi Province in China.

It belongs to the Amaryllidaceae family and is characterized by its distinctive bell-shaped flowers that range in color from white to pale yellow. The plant typically grows in rocky, well-drained soils at elevations between 1,500 and 3,000 meters. Its bulbs are adapted to survive harsh winters, and it blooms in late spring to early summer.

The species is valued for its ornamental beauty and is sometimes cultivated in gardens for its unique floral display.
