Festuca elatior subvar. tunetana

Festuca elatior subvar. tunetana, commonly known as the Tunisian tall fescue, is a perennial grass species native to North Africa.

It is characterized by its tufted growth habit and slender, wiry leaves that are typically blue-green in color. The plant produces spikelets that are densely arranged along the stems, with small, two-lobed glumes. It is adapted to arid and semi-arid environments, showing drought tolerance and resistance to grazing.

This subspecies is often found in rocky soils and is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological resilience.
