Fuchsia tacsoniiflora

Fuchsia tacsoniiflora, commonly known as the Tacsonia fuchsia, is a species of flowering plant native to South America.

It is characterized by its striking, tubular flowers that are typically violet or purple in color. The plant produces showy inflorescences that attract pollinators such as hummingbirds and bees. Its leaves are usually lance-shaped and have a glossy appearance.

Fuchsia tacsoniiflora is often cultivated for its ornamental value in gardens and greenhouses.
