Ficinia ixioides

Ficinia ixioides, commonly known as the narrow-leafed sedge, is a perennial herbaceous plant native to southern Africa.

It belongs to the Cyperaceae family, which includes sedges, and is characterized by its grass-like appearance with triangular stems. The plant typically grows in wetland or marshy environments, preferring moist, well-drained soils. Its small, inconspicuous flowers are arranged in spikelets, and it plays a role in stabilizing soil in its native habitat.

Ficinia ixioides is often used in ecological restoration projects due to its adaptability and resilience.
