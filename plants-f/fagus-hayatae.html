Fagus hayatae

Fagus hayatae, commonly known as the Japanese beech, is a deciduous tree native to Japan.

It belongs to the Fagaceae family, which includes oaks and chestnuts. The tree is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and oval-shaped leaves that turn yellow in autumn. It produces edible nuts enclosed in spiny husks, which are an important food source for wildlife.

Fagus hayatae typically grows in cool, moist environments and can reach heights of up to 20 meters.
