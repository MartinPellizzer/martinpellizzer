Fadogia obscura

Fadogia obscura, commonly known as the African velvet tamarind, is a flowering plant native to the tropical regions of Africa.

It belongs to the Fabaceae family, which includes legumes, and is characterized by its compound leaves and small, pod-like fruits. The plant produces yellowish-white flowers that bloom in clusters, adding ornamental value to its appearance. Fadogia obscura is also valued for its medicinal properties in traditional African medicine.
