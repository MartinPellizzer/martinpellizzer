Fissistigma villosissimum

Fissistigma villosissimum, commonly known as the hairy fissistigma, is a perennial shrub native to South America.

It is characterized by its woody stems covered in dense, soft hairs, giving it a distinctive appearance. The plant produces small, clustered flowers that are typically white or pale pink, which bloom in the spring. Its leaves are simple, alternate, and often have a slightly serrated edge.

Fissistigma villosissimum is often found in dry, rocky habitats and is valued for its ornamental qualities in horticulture.
