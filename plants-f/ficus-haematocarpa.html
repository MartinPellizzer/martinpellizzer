Ficus haematocarpa

Ficus haematocarpa, commonly known as the blood fig, is a species of fig tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its broad, glossy leaves and small, clustered flowers that develop into edible figs. The plant thrives in tropical climates and requires well-drained soil and ample sunlight. Its unique red-tinged fruit is not only ornamental but also used in traditional medicine.

Ficus haematocarpa is valued for its resilience and adaptability in various environmental conditions.
