Fagonia chilensis var. glutinosa

Fagonia chilensis var. glutinosa, commonly known as the sticky fagonia, is a shrub native to the arid regions of southern South America.

It is characterized by its grayish, often sticky bark and small, yellowish-green leaves that are adapted to conserve water. The plant produces small, yellow flowers that bloom in spring, followed by red, fleshy berries that are a food source for local wildlife. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in coastal and desert environments.

Due to its unique adaptations, it is an important species in its native ecosystem.
