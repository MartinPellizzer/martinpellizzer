Frailea curvispina

Frailea curvispina, commonly known as the curly-spined frailea, is a species of cactus native to southern Africa.

It is a small, low-growing plant with a short, thick stem that often branches near the base. Its most distinctive feature is its dense, spiny foliage, which consists of tightly clustered, cylindrical leaves. The leaves are typically a pale green color and have a waxy coating to reduce water loss.

This plant is well adapted to arid environments and requires minimal water and well-draining soil.
