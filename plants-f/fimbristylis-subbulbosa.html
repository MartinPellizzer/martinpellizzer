Fimbristylis subbulbosa

Fimbristylis subbulbosa, commonly known as the narrowbulb sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in wet or seasonally flooded habitats, such as marshes and wetlands. The plant has slender, grass-like stems with small, spike-like inflorescences that bear tiny, wind-pollinated flowers. Its leaves are narrow and grass-like, often with a slightly rough texture.

It plays an important ecological role in stabilizing soil and providing habitat for various microorganisms and invertebrates.
