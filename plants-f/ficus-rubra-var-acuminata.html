Ficus rubra var. acuminata

Ficus rubra var. acuminata, commonly known as the sharp-leaved rubber vine, is a species native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are typically dark green with a slightly waxy texture. The plant produces small, inconspicuous flowers followed by small, round fruits that are often red when mature. It is often cultivated for its ornamental value and ability to thrive in humid, shady environments.

The leaves are also known for their ability to retain moisture, making the plant adaptable to various soil types.
