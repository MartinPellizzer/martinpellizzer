Festuca × belensis var. glabra

Festuca × belensis var. glabra, commonly known as smooth meadow fescue, is a perennial grass species characterized by its slender, upright growth habit.

It typically forms dense tufts with narrow, grass-like leaves that are smooth to the touch, as indicated by its varietal name "glabra.". The plant produces small, spike-like inflorescences bearing numerous tiny florets, which are wind-pollinated. It thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, pastures, and along roadsides.

This variety is valued for its ornamental appeal and adaptability to various environmental conditions.
