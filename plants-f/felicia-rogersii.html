Felicia rogersii

Felicia rogersii, commonly known as the desert fleabane, is a flowering plant native to the southwestern United States and northern Mexico.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads that resemble daisies. The plant typically grows in arid and semi-arid environments, such as deserts and rocky soils, and is adapted to withstand drought conditions. Its yellow to orange flowers bloom in summer, attracting pollinators like bees and butterflies.

Felicia rogersii is often found in open habitats and is considered a hardy, low-maintenance plant for xeriscaping.
