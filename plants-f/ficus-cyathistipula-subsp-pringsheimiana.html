Ficus cyathistipula subsp. pringsheimiana

Ficus cyathistipula subsp.

pringsheimiana, commonly known as the Pringsheim's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family. It is native to parts of Southeast Asia, including Indonesia and the Philippines.

This plant is characterized by its small, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, greenish-white flowers. The fruit is a smooth, greenish-yellow syconium that matures to a reddish color.

It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
