Fagraea woodiana

Fagraea woodiana, commonly known as the silver tree, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its thick, flaky bark and large, glossy, dark green leaves that are arranged in whorls along the stems. The plant produces small, fragrant white flowers that bloom in clusters, followed by bright red, berry-like fruits. It thrives in humid, warm climates and is often found in lowland forests.

The silver tree is valued for its ornamental beauty and has been used in traditional medicine in some regions.
