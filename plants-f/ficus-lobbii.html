Ficus lobbii

Ficus lobbii, commonly known as the Lobb's fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically dark green. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible figs that are often consumed by local communities. Ficus lobbii thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Its rapid growth and adaptability make it a popular choice for ornamental gardening in warm climates.
