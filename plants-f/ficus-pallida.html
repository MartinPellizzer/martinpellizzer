Ficus pallida

Ficus pallida, commonly known as the white fig or pale fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of India and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, pale green to whitish leaves that are often covered in a fine, powdery coating. Ficus pallida is typically grown as a houseplant due to its ability to thrive in indoor conditions with indirect light and moderate humidity.

Its unique appearance makes it a popular choice for ornamental purposes.
