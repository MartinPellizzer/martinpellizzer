Festuca microstachys var. grayi

Festuca microstachys var. grayi, commonly known as Gray's fine fescue, is a grass species native to the western United States.

It is characterized by its fine, dark green foliage and tufted growth habit, making it well-suited for ornamental and low-maintenance landscapes. The plant typically grows in rocky or well-drained soils and is drought-tolerant once established. It produces slender, spike-like inflorescences that bloom in late spring to early summer.

This variety is valued for its ecological benefits, providing habitat and forage for local wildlife.
