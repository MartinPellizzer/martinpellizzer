Fragaria virginiana var. illinoensis

Fragaria virginiana var. illinoensis, commonly known as the Illinois strawberry, is a wild strawberry species native to the central United States.

It is a perennial herb that typically grows in moist, open habitats such as woodlands and prairies. The plant produces small, red, edible fruits that are often used in local foraging and traditional practices. Its leaves are trifoliate, with a serrated edge, and it has a fibrous root system.

The species is valued for its ornamental value and its role in supporting local pollinators and wildlife.
