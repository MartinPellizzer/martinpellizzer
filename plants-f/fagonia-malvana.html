Fagonia malvana

Fagonia malvana, commonly known as the desert tamarisk, is a shrub native to arid and semi-arid regions of the Mediterranean and parts of Asia.

It belongs to the family Caryophyllaceae and is characterized by its small, clustered flowers and woody stems. The plant is adapted to survive in harsh, dry environments with minimal water availability. Its leaves are often reduced to small scales, and it produces small, capsule-like fruits that contain seeds.

Fagonia malvana is valued for its resilience and is sometimes used in landscaping for its ornamental appeal.
