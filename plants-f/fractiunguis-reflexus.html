Fractiunguis reflexus

Fractiunguis reflexus, commonly known as the reflexed frond plant, is a unique species characterized by its intricate frond-like structures.

It thrives in moist, shaded environments, typically found in tropical and subtropical regions. The plant's leaves are elongated and reflexed, meaning they curve backward, giving it a distinctive appearance. Its botanical features include a fibrous root system and a complex network of vascular tissues.

This species is valued for its ornamental qualities and has been studied for its potential ecological roles in forest ecosystems.
