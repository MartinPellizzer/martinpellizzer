Festuca angustata

Festuca angustata, commonly known as narrowleaf fescue, is a perennial grass species native to temperate regions of the Northern Hemisphere.

It is characterized by its slender, grass-like leaves and compact growth habit, often forming dense, low-growing mats. This plant thrives in well-drained soils and is often found in meadows, grasslands, and along roadsides. Festuca angustata is valued for its ornamental appeal and is used in landscaping and turfgrass applications.

It is also an important component of native plant communities in its natural habitat.
