Fraxinus excelsa

Fraxinus excelsa, commonly known as the African ash, is a deciduous tree native to southern Africa.

It is characterized by its smooth, gray bark and opposite, simple leaves that are elliptical in shape. The tree produces small, yellowish-green flowers in clusters, followed by winged seeds called samaras. It thrives in a variety of soil types but prefers well-drained conditions.

Fraxinus excelsa is often used in landscaping for its ornamental value and adaptability.
