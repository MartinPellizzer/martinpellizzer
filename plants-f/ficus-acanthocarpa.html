Ficus acanthocarpa

Ficus acanthocarpa, commonly known as the spiny fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its thick, woody stems and large, heart-shaped leaves that are often covered in small spines. Its fruit, which is a small, greenish fig, is edible and contains a sweet, sticky pulp.

The plant is often used in traditional medicine for its purported health benefits.
