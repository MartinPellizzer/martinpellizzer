Ficus mysorensis var. subrepanda

Ficus mysorensis var. subrepanda, commonly known as the Mysore fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Western Ghats of India.

It is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically heart-shaped with a slightly serrated margin. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible, round figs that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, shaded environments and is often found in dense forests.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and ecological importance in supporting local biodiversity.
