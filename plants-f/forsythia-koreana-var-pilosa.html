Forsythia koreana var. pilosa

Forsythia koreana var. pilosa, commonly known as the hairy forsythia, is a deciduous shrub native to Korea.

It is characterized by its upright growth habit and clusters of bright yellow flowers that bloom in early spring. The leaves are ovate and have a hairy texture, giving the plant its varietal name. It thrives in well-drained soil and full sun, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardens.

This variety is closely related to other forsythia species but is distinguished by its unique foliage and floral characteristics.
