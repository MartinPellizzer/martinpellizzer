Fraxinus pallida

Fraxinus pallida, commonly known as the white ash, is a deciduous tree native to the eastern United States.

It is characterized by its smooth, light-gray bark and compound leaves with five to nine leaflets. The plant produces small, greenish-yellow flowers in drooping clusters, followed by samaroid fruits that are dispersed by wind. It thrives in moist, well-drained soils and is often found in forested areas.

The white ash is valued for its strong, flexible wood, which has been used historically for various purposes.
