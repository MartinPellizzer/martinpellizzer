Forsteronia schomburgkii var. umbellata

Forsteronia schomburgkii var. umbellata, commonly known as the umbellate forsteria, is a flowering plant native to South America, particularly Brazil.

It belongs to the Apocynaceae family and is characterized by its showy, clustered flowers that resemble umbrellas, hence its common name. The plant typically grows as a shrub with woody stems and produces small, elliptical leaves with a glossy surface. Its flowers are usually pink to red in color and are borne in terminal umbels, making it a striking addition to gardens.

The species is valued for its ornamental appeal and is often cultivated for its vibrant blooms and compact growth habit.
