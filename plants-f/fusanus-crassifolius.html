Fusanus crassifolius

Fusanus crassifolius, commonly known as the broadleaf fusanus, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, leathery leaves that are typically broad and elliptical in shape. The plant produces small, fragrant flowers that are usually pale yellow or greenish in color. It thrives in humid, tropical environments and is often found in lowland forests.

Due to its ornamental value, it is occasionally cultivated in botanical gardens and private collections.
