Ficus goetzei

Ficus goetzei, commonly known as the Goetze's fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to Southeast Asia.

It belongs to the Moraceae family and is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves. The plant produces small, clustered fig fruits that are typically green when mature. It thrives in warm, humid climates and requires well-draining soil.

Ficus goetzei is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to indoor environments.
