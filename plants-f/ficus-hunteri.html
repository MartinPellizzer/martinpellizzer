Ficus hunteri

Ficus hunteri, commonly known as the Hunter's fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to tropical regions of Africa, particularly Kenya and Tanzania. The plant is characterized by its glossy, dark green leaves that are typically ovate or elliptical in shape. It produces small, clustered fruits that are edible and have a mild, sweet flavor.

Ficus hunteri is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and relatively low maintenance requirements.
