Fimbristylis annua f. brizoides

Fimbristylis annua f. brizoides, commonly known as the bristle-stemmed flat sedge, is a flowering plant belonging to the Cyperaceae family.

It is a perennial herb characterized by its grass-like appearance, with narrow, linear leaves and small, clustered inflorescences. The plant typically grows in wet or moist environments, such as marshes, swamps, and along the edges of water bodies. Its flowers are usually brown or reddish, and it produces small, three-angled capsules that contain the seeds.

This subspecies is often found in tropical and subtropical regions, contributing to the biodiversity of wetland ecosystems.
