Flourensia polycephala

Flourensia polycephala, commonly known as the Mexican sunflower, is a flowering plant native to Central America and parts of the southern United States.

It is a member of the Asteraceae family, characterized by its composite flower heads and serrated leaves. The plant produces vibrant orange to red flowers that bloom in late summer and early autumn. It thrives in full sun and well-drained soil, making it a popular choice for ornamental gardening.

Its rapid growth and ability to attract pollinators make it both ecologically and aesthetically valuable.
