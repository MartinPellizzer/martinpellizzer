Ficus tsiangii

Ficus tsiangii, commonly known as the Chinese Fig or Tsiang's Fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to China and is characterized by its glossy, elongated leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stems. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers that are clustered in inflorescences, followed by edible fig-like fruits. It is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage and adaptability to various growing conditions.

Ficus tsiangii prefers bright, indirect light and well-draining soil, making it suitable for indoor cultivation.
