Fagus sylvatica var. remillyensis

Fagus sylvatica var. remillyensis, commonly known as the Remilly beech, is a rare variant of the European beech.

It is characterized by its unique leaf morphology, featuring a distinctive yellow-green coloration and a serrated margin. This variety is native to a small region in France, specifically the Jura Mountains. The plant exhibits a slow growth rate and is adapted to specific microclimatic conditions.

Its botanical significance lies in its genetic uniqueness and potential ecological role in its native habitat.
