Floscopa bambusifolia

Floscopa bambusifolia, commonly known as the bamboo leaf bellflower, is a flowering plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its bamboo-like appearance, with slender, hollow stems and long, narrow leaves. The plant produces clusters of bell-shaped flowers that are typically white or pale blue in color. It thrives in moist, shady environments and is often found in tropical and subtropical regions.

Floscopa bambusifolia is valued for its ornamental appeal and is sometimes cultivated in gardens.
