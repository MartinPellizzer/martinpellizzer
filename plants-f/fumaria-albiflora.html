Fumaria albiflora

Fumaria albiflora, commonly known as the white-flowered glory of the valley, is a flowering plant native to the Mediterranean region.

It belongs to the family Fumariaceae and is characterized by its slender, branching stems and clusters of small, white or pale yellow flowers. The plant produces capsule fruits that contain several seeds, which are dispersed by wind. It thrives in well-drained soils and prefers full sun to partial shade.

Fumaria albiflora is often found in rocky or dry habitats, making it a resilient and adaptable species.
