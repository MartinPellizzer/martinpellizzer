Ficus crassiramea var. celebica

Ficus crassiramea var. celebica, commonly known as the Celebes fig, is a tropical evergreen tree native to the Maluku Islands of Indonesia.

It is characterized by its dense, leathery leaves that are typically dark green and have a rough texture. The plant produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by edible fig-like fruits that are often used in local cuisines. It thrives in humid, warm climates and requires well-drained soil for optimal growth.

Its ornamental value makes it a popular choice for indoor and outdoor gardening in tropical regions.
