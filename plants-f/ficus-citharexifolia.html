Ficus citharexifolia

Ficus citharexifolia, commonly known as the "Cellophane Tree," is a tropical plant native to Southeast Asia.

It is characterized by its large, heart-shaped leaves that are smooth and often have a waxy texture. The leaves are typically a deep green and can grow up to 30 centimeters in length. This plant is a member of the Moraceae family and is often cultivated as an ornamental plant due to its attractive foliage.

It prefers warm, humid climates and is relatively easy to care for in suitable growing conditions.
