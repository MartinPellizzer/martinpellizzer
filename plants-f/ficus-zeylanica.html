Ficus zeylanica

Ficus zeylanica, commonly known as the Indian rubber plant, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to southern Asia, including India, Sri Lanka, and parts of Southeast Asia. The plant is characterized by its large, glossy, heart-shaped leaves that are typically arranged in a spiral pattern along the stem. It is a deciduous shrub or small tree that can grow up to 10 meters in height under favorable conditions.

The plant produces small, clustered flowers followed by edible figs that are an important food source for various animals and birds.
