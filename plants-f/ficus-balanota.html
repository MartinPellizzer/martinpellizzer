Ficus balanota

Ficus balanota, commonly known as the curly leaf ficus, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to Southeast Asia, including regions of India, China, and the Philippines. The plant is characterized by its waxy, dark green leaves that have a distinctive curly or wavy edge. It produces small, greenish-white flowers followed by spherical, greenish fruits.

Ficus balanota is often cultivated as a houseplant due to its adaptability and aesthetic appeal.
