Fabricia bracteata

Fabricia bracteata, commonly known as the bracted fabricia, is a flowering plant native to tropical and subtropical regions.

It belongs to the legume family, Fabaceae, and is characterized by its trifoliate leaves and small, pea-like flowers. The plant produces pods that contain several seeds, which are often dispersed by wind. It is often found in disturbed soils and can thrive in a variety of environmental conditions.

Its botanical features make it a valuable plant for ecological studies and potential agricultural use.
