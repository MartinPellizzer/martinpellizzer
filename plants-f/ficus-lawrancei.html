Ficus lawrancei

Ficus lawrancei, commonly known as the Lawrence fig, is a species of flowering plant in the Moraceae family.

It is native to the tropical regions of Southeast Asia, including parts of Indonesia and Malaysia. The plant is characterized by its glossy, heart-shaped leaves and produces small, fig-like fruits that are edible. It thrives in warm, humid environments and is often cultivated as a houseplant or in tropical gardens.

Ficus lawrancei is valued for its ornamental appeal and ability to adapt to indoor growing conditions.
