Foeniculum officinale var. dulce

Foeniculum officinale var. dulce, commonly known as sweet fennel, is a biennial or perennial herb native to the Mediterranean region.

It is characterized by its finely divided, feathery leaves and yellow flower heads that resemble small umbels. The plant produces a distinctive anise-like aroma due to the presence of compounds such as anethole. Its seeds are often used in culinary applications and traditional medicine.

Botanically, it belongs to the Apiaceae family, which includes other aromatic herbs like parsley and dill.
